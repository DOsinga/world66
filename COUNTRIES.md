# Country Pages — Guidelines

Country pages are the main planning tool for travelers. They should answer the practical questions (how do I get there, what do I need to know) and the inspirational ones (why should I go, what will I experience). This document defines what a good country page looks like.

## The overview page

The overview is the most important page. It should make someone want to visit — or at least understand the place. See STYLE.md for detailed guidance, but in short:

1. Open with what makes this country distinctive
2. Walk through the regions and highlights, linking to cities and places
3. Be honest — if infrastructure is rough or safety is a concern, say so
4. Be opinionated — if something is great, say it's great

## Required sections

Every country should have these five sections. They answer the questions every traveler asks first.

### Getting There (`getting_there.md`)

How do you arrive? Major international airports and which cities they serve, hub airlines, overland options from neighboring countries, ferries if relevant. Which entry points are cheapest or most convenient. Keep it practical.

### Getting Around (`getting_around.md`)

How do you travel within the country? Trains, buses, domestic flights, car rental, ferries. What's reliable, what's rough. Is driving practical or terrifying? Are there passes or cards worth buying? Give the big picture so a traveler can plan their route.

### Practical Information (`practical_informat.md`)

The nuts and bolts:

- **Visas** — what do most travelers need, how to get one, how long can you stay
- **Money** — currency, ATM availability, card vs. cash, tipping customs
- **Language** — what's spoken, how far English gets you, useful phrases
- **Safety** — honest assessment, common scams, areas to avoid
- **Connectivity** — SIM cards, Wi-Fi, mobile payments

### When to Go (`when_to_go.md`)

Climate and seasons. When is the weather best for different parts of the country? When are the crowds? When is it cheapest? Any events or natural phenomena worth timing a visit around (migrations, festivals, blooming seasons)?

### Highlights (`highlights.md`)

The big reasons to visit this country — the experiences, places, and activities that make it distinctive. This is not an exhaustive list of sights. It's a curated, opinionated overview of what makes the country worth the trip. Mix different types: nature, history, food, adventure, culture, cities. A traveler reading this should come away thinking "I want to do that."

This replaces both "sights" and "things to do" at the country level. One section, focused on why you'd go.

## Optional sections

These add depth and are worth having where they fit, but not every country needs all of them.

### Food (`food.md`)

What do people eat here? Describe the cuisine, name the signature dishes, explain what makes the food culture distinctive. Every country has a food story worth telling — even where the cuisine isn't famous, travelers eat every day and want to know what to expect. A country where the food is bland or repetitive is worth saying so honestly, along with what to seek out instead.

Don't list specific restaurants here — that belongs on city pages. This is about the cuisine itself.

### People (`people.md`)

The human landscape. Ethnic and cultural diversity, religions, social customs a traveler will notice. How do people greet each other? What's considered polite or rude? What will surprise you? Focus on what a traveler will experience, not demographics.

### Festivals (`festivals.md`)

Events worth timing a visit around. Major national celebrations, religious holidays that affect travel, cultural events with dates and locations. A few well-described festivals are better than a long calendar.

### Books (`books.md`)

Reading recommendations — fiction, travel writing, history. A few well-described picks that help a traveler understand the place. Say what each book is about and why it's worth reading. Write recommendations inline, don't create a subdirectory with POI entries.

### Health (`health.md`)

Only where health is a real planning concern — tropical countries, places with altitude, countries with specific vaccination requirements. Don't create this for western Europe or Japan. Do create it for countries with malaria risk, yellow fever requirements, serious altitude, or unreliable medical facilities.

### Top 5 Must Do's (`top_5_must_dos.md`)

Our most opinionated page. Five experiences, numbered, each with a substantial paragraph. Mix different types of activity. See STYLE.md for how to write these.

## Sections that belong on city pages only

Do not create these at the country level. They are too specific for a whole country and belong on individual city or region pages:

- **Eating Out** — specific restaurants
- **Bars & Cafes** — specific bars and cafes
- **Nightlife & Entertainment** — specific venues
- **Shopping** — specific shops and markets
- **Museums** — specific museums
- **Day Trips** — excursions from a specific base
- **Beaches** — specific beaches (cover the best ones in Highlights instead)

## Sections to delete

These are legacy sections from the original World66 that no longer add value. Delete them when encountered:

- `tours_and_excursio.md` — almost always spam or empty
- `webcams.md` — obsolete
- `budget_travel_idea.md` — almost always empty
- `family_travel_idea.md` — almost always empty
- `senior_travel_idea.md` — almost always empty

## Principles

- **The overview is king.** A great overview with no sections is better than a thin overview with ten empty sections.
- **Delete empty sections.** A page that says "We currently have no X" is worse than no page at all. If there's nothing to say, don't have the section.
- **Quality over completeness.** A country with a good overview, solid practical information, and a vivid highlights page is well-served. Don't create stub sections just to fill the list.
- **Link generously.** Every city, region, or place mentioned should link to its page if one exists.
- **Be honest.** If a country is difficult, expensive, dangerous, or boring, say so. Travelers trust a guide that tells the truth.
