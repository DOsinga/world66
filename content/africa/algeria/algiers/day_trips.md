**Monument Hopping**

Algiers is interspersed with several adroit and
skillfully erected monuments. The best way to savor the historical
fragrance of this city is to make a day trip to its charming
ancient structures. Start your visit with a journey to the classic
Monument des Martyrs, an emblematic monument erected to commemorate the
nation’s struggle for independence. Another quaint Moorish-style
architecture is conspicuous in Algiers’s Grand Post Office, a heritage
structure that is located in the heart of the city and gives a
fantastic vista of the city center. A superb masterpiece of
contemporary architectural elegance is the National Library, located in
El Hamma. Visitors can also include the Place of Emir Abdelkaer, built
in the memory of the celebrated leader Abd El-Kader.

**The Famous Mosques**

Algiers is famous for its neo-classic mosques that are a brilliant
depiction of the city’s tryst with refined Islamic culture. The most
celebrated work of Muslim workmanship is the Ketchaoua Mosque that is
located near the old Casbah region. The structure boasts of fine marble
columns and a portico embellishment. There are rows of arcades along
with several chapels and a gigantic tomb of San Geronimo. The great
Mosque of Algiers situated at Rue de la Marine was constructed during
the rule of Sultan Yusuf ibn Tashfin and is a great heritage edifice to
view. Other mosques worth viewing are the El Djedid Mosque (erected by
the Turks), the El Kebir Mosque and the New Mosque.

**Botanical Discoveries **

A day trip to the Jarndin d’essai is an excellent idea for botany
aficionados. The place houses over 2500 varieties of the most exotic
shrubs and plant species and is spread across 200 acres of lush
greenery. Include the Villa Abd-el-hair in the trip as well to get a
snapshot of the artistic legacy of the Price abd-el-Hair prize winning
artists. The villa is located just above the garden and offers stunning
view of the ecology below.

**Casbah**

Visit the old quarter of the town for its charming mosques with
minarets, delightful street markets, historical maze of narrow by lanes
and petite ‘old worldly’ houses.

Algiers is a striking city of an indigenously bred civilization and
a labyrinth of other beguiling cross-cultural influences that has each
lent its nuance to this fantastic cultural rainbow.
