Algiers has a few good museums that show artifacts from its long history: roman times, arab rulers of the middle ages and ottoman and french times.

**Museums**

**Museum of Antiquities**

Antique mosaics, Roman glass work and sculptures and Islamic art are on display.

 Parc de la Liberté  Hours: 9am-12 noon & 2pm-5pm daily except Friday morning and Saturday  Admission: Free  

**Bardo Museum**

This impressive collection of indigenous artefacts from various regions of Algeria is housed in a beautiful Moorish style mansion. The museum features exhibits on prehistoric findings and ethnography as well as regional jewellery, costumes, leatherwork and weaponry.

 3 avenue Franklin Rooseve [more..](/africa/algeria/algiers/museums/bardomuseum)

**Museum of Popular Arts**

Indigenous arts and crafts, including carpets, jewellery, ceramics and furniture are housed in an Ottoman palace built in 1570 and later used by the French as a town hall. The palace is as interesting as the collection it contains.

 Off rue Hadj Omar on the left  Hours: 10am-12 noon & 2pm-5p [more..](/africa/algeria/algiers/museums/museumofpopulararts)
