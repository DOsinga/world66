Bou Saâda is an oasis town at the edge of the Sahara, about 250 km south of Algiers. It sits in a valley where the Tell Atlas gives way to the desert, and the light here is famous — Étienne Dinet, the French Orientalist painter, spent decades in Bou Saâda and is buried in the local cemetery after converting to Islam. A small museum dedicated to his work sits in the old town.

The palm groves along the Oued Bou Saâda are pleasant for walking, and the town retains a traditional Saharan character with narrow streets and flat-roofed houses. The Moulin Ferrero, an old watermill in the gorge above town, is a popular picnic spot. Beyond the town, the landscape transitions rapidly into rocky desert and sand dunes — a taste of the deep Sahara without the long drive south.
