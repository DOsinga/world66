Djanet is one of the most extraordinary places in Algeria -- a Saharan oasis town sitting at the foot of the Tassili n'Ajjer plateau, surrounded by some of the most dramatic desert landscapes on the continent. If you have any interest in the deep Sahara, this is where you come.

The town itself is small and quiet, a collection of low buildings and palm gardens fed by springs at the edge of the sand sea. The population is mostly Tuareg, and the pace of life here is unhurried in a way that feels genuine rather than posed. Djanet serves as the gateway to Tassili n'Ajjer National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that preserves both an otherworldly sandstone landscape and one of the most important collections of prehistoric rock art anywhere in the world. The paintings and engravings, some dating back 8,000 years, depict a time when this desert was green savanna teeming with cattle, giraffes, and hippos.

Getting to Djanet requires commitment. There are flights from Algiers, but they are infrequent and sometimes cancelled. The overland route from the north is long and passes through genuinely remote territory. Once here, exploring the Tassili plateau requires a local guide and permits arranged through a tour agency in town -- you cannot go independently. Multi-day treks into the plateau are the main draw, sleeping under the stars among rock formations that look like they belong on another planet.

The best time to visit is between October and March, when daytime temperatures are manageable. Summers are brutally hot. Come prepared for basic facilities -- Djanet has guesthouses and small restaurants, but this is not a place with tourist infrastructure. That is part of its appeal.
