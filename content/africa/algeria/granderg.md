The Grand Erg Oriental is one of the world's great sand seas — a vast ocean of dunes covering around 192,000 square kilometres across eastern Algeria and into southern Tunisia. This is the Sahara at its most cinematic: enormous undulating dunes, some reaching 150 metres high, that change colour through the day from gold to orange to deep red as the light shifts. At the fringes of the erg, the dunes thin out into hammada (rocky desert) and the occasional oasis town.

The main entry points for travelers are from the Algerian side via the oasis towns of [Touggourt](/africa/algeria/touggourt) and [El Oued](/africa/algeria/eloued) — the latter known as the "City of a Thousand Domes" for its distinctive domed architecture, built to keep sand out of living spaces. The Tunisian side is more accessible, with the oasis village of Douz serving as the main gateway to the erg from that direction. Douz markets itself as the "Gateway to the Sahara" and has a well-developed infrastructure of camel treks, 4WD excursions, and desert camps.

Travel here requires planning and, on the Algerian side, some patience with bureaucracy. But the Grand Erg Oriental offers the full Saharan experience — vast silence, spectacular sunsets, and the disorienting sensation of being surrounded by an ocean with no water in it.
