---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Fort of Santa Cruz, perched on the summit of Jebel Murdjadjo above the city, is Oran's most visible landmark — a 16th-century Spanish fortification now reached by a winding road with sweeping views of the Mediterranean. Next to it stands the 19th-century chapel and a statue of the Virgin of Santa Cruz, a pilgrimage site for Algerian Christians.

Down in the city, the Place du 1er Novembre (the old Place d'Armes) is the heart of the colonial-era quarter, surrounded by Haussmannian buildings and the ornate Mairie. The Great Synagogue, converted into the Abdellah Ben Salem Mosque, is a striking 19th-century building. The Sidi El Houari quarter, the old Ottoman medina below the fort, is gradually being restored and offers the most atmospheric walking in the city.
