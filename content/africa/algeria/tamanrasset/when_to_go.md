---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Nomad Festival takes place every year in the village of Tazrouk, about 160 km from Tamanrasset. It is held on the first Wednesday of August and lasts two days. Tuareg and nomadic communities travel from across the Sahara — from In Salah, Djanet, Adrar, In Guezzam, and even from Ghat in Libya and Tamasna in Niger — for camel races, traditional music, and dancing in full ceremonial dress.

Tazrouk sits at 1,900 metres, making it the highest village in Algeria. It is surrounded by gardens producing grapes, apricots, melons, and apples. You will need a local guide and a 4x4 to get there.
