A small fishing and beach town on the Kabyle coast, Tigzirt sits about 40 km north of Tizi Ouzou. The setting is striking — rocky headlands, a stony beach, and the ruins of an ancient town just visible above the waterline. Algerians from the Kabylie region have long come here for summer weekends, though it has never developed the infrastructure of a major resort.

The most striking sight in town is a large early Christian basilica, one of the best-preserved in North Africa. Built around the 5th century on the ruins of a Roman settlement, it stands in the middle of town and gives a sense of how much has layered itself here over the centuries. The ruins of the Roman city of Iomnium, of which Tigzirt is the successor, extend along the coast.

Tigzirt is best reached by car or louage (shared taxi) from Tizi Ouzou.
