---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Fortaleza de São Miguel, a 17th-century Portuguese fort on the hill above the bay, is Luanda's most visible landmark and houses a military museum with colonial-era cannons and artifacts. The National Slavery Museum (Museu Nacional da Escravatura), a former church overlooking the Atlantic, documents the history of the slave trade that passed through this port. The Marginal promenade along the waterfront is a pleasant walk with views of the bay. Kissama National Park, 70 km south, is Angola's main wildlife reserve and a meaningful day trip for elephant and antelope sightings.
