---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Getting around Benin is straightforward if you are patient and flexible. The main north-south highway from [Cotonou](/africa/benin/cotonou) to [Parakou](/africa/benin/parakou) and beyond to the Niger border is paved and in reasonable condition. The coastal road linking Cotonou to [Grand Popo](/africa/benin/grandpopo) and the Togo border to the west, and to [Porto Novo](/africa/benin/portonovo) and the Nigeria border to the east, is also decent.

**Bush taxis and minibuses** are the main form of public transport. They run between all major towns and leave when full -- which can mean waiting an hour or more. [Cotonou](/africa/benin/cotonou) to [Abomey](/africa/benin/abomey) takes about two hours, Cotonou to Parakou about six. Fares are low. Conditions range from acceptable to seriously overcrowded.

**Zemidjans** (motorcycle taxis) are everywhere and are the standard way to get around within cities and towns. Agree on a price before you get on. They are cheap, fast, and slightly terrifying -- helmets are theoretically required but rarely provided. In [Cotonou](/africa/benin/cotonou), they are the only practical way to navigate the traffic.

**Car rental with driver** is worth considering if you plan to cover a lot of ground, especially in the north where public transport becomes sparse. Agencies in Cotonou can arrange this, and a driver solves both the navigation and the language problem. Driving yourself is possible but not recommended unless you are experienced with West African roads and traffic.

**There is no rail service** for passengers. Benin once had a railway line from Cotonou to Parakou, but it has not operated reliably in years.

For [Ganvie](/africa/benin/ganvie), you take a pirogue (motorized canoe) from the town of Abomey-Calavi, about 15 km north of Cotonou. Boats can be hired at the lakeside for a round trip.

Road quality drops significantly on secondary routes, especially in the north and during the rainy season. If you are heading to the [Somba villages](/africa/benin/sombavillages) or Pendjari National Park, allow extra time and ideally travel in a 4x4.
