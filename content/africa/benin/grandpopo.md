Grand-Popo is a quiet beach town on Benin's coast, close to the Togolese border. The beach is long and wild — crashing Atlantic surf, coconut palms, and almost no one on it. Swimming can be dangerous because of the undertow, but the atmosphere is wonderful.

The town was once a prosperous colonial trading post, and a few crumbling old buildings remain. The real draw is the voodoo culture that is still alive here. Grand-Popo sits in the heartland of West African vodun, and visitors can arrange to meet local practitioners and attend ceremonies — though this should be done respectfully and through a local guide. The Bouche du Roy, where the Mono river meets the sea, is a wetland area with good birdwatching and fishing villages accessible by pirogue.

Grand-Popo is an easy day trip or weekend escape from Cotonou or Lomé.
