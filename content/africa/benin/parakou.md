Parakou is Benin's second largest city and the main hub of the north, sitting at the end of the railway line from Cotonou and at the crossroads of routes heading to Natitingou, Djougou, and the Niger border. If you are heading to the Atakora region or [Pendjari National Park](/africa/benin/pendjari), you will almost certainly pass through here.

The city has a busy, sprawling feel — a commercial centre for the surrounding cotton-growing region rather than a tourist destination. The grand marche (central market) is one of the largest in northern Benin and worth a wander, particularly for its textiles and the bustle of the transport hub around it. The Gaani festival, held annually in September, is the city's biggest cultural event, a celebration rooted in the traditions of the Bariba and Dendi peoples with horse parades, drumming, and dancing.

Parakou is functional rather than charming, but it has decent enough facilities for an overnight stop — a handful of restaurants serving local dishes like igname pilee (pounded yam) and wagashi cheese, and a few reasonable places to stay. Most travellers use it as a staging point: stock up on supplies here before heading into the parks or the Atakora hills to the northwest.
