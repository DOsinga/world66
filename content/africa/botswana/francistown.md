Francistown is Botswana's second largest city and its oldest urban settlement, located over 400 km north of [Gaborone](/africa/botswana/gaborone). The city grew around a gold rush in the late 19th century — named after Daniel Francis, who held the first mining concession — and its history is reflected in a small museum near the centre.

As a destination in itself, Francistown is modest. The market is lively and worth a look, and the pace is unhurried. Most travellers use it as a gateway: it sits conveniently between the Zimbabwean border at Plumtree and the routes north to the [Okavango Delta](/africa/botswana/okavangodelta), [Moremi Game Reserve](/africa/botswana/moremigamereserve), and [Chobe National Park](/africa/botswana/chobenationalpark). The borders with [Zambia](/africa/zambia) and [Zimbabwe](/africa/zimbabwe) are both within a few hours' drive.
