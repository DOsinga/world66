Ghanzi is a small ranching town in the central Kalahari of Botswana, the kind of place where cattle outnumber people and the horizon is flat in every direction. The town itself is basic — a few shops, a fuel station, somewhere to eat — but it is the gateway to San (Bushman) cultural experiences that offer a window into one of the oldest continuous cultures on earth.

Several community-run lodges and campsites around Ghanzi offer guided walks with San trackers, who demonstrate hunting techniques, plant identification, and fire-making skills. The experiences vary in authenticity, but at their best they are genuinely illuminating. The D'Kar settlement nearby is a centre for San art, with a gallery selling paintings and crafts.

Ghanzi sits on the A3 highway between Windhoek and Maun, making it a natural overnight stop on the long drive into the Okavango Delta region. The sunsets over the Kalahari bush are extraordinary.
