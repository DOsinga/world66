A UNESCO World Heritage Site northwest of the delta, the Tsodilo Hills contain over 4,500 rock paintings — one of the highest concentrations of rock art in the world. The San people consider these hills sacred, calling them the "Mountains of the Gods." Four main hills rise abruptly from the flat Kalahari scrub, and guided walking trails lead past the most significant panels. The paintings span thousands of years and depict animals, humans, and geometric patterns. Allow a full day for the visit, and bring plenty of water.
