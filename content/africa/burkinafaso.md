Burkina Faso — "the land of upright people" — is one of West Africa's most rewarding and least visited countries. Landlocked between Mali, Niger, Ghana, and Ivory Coast, it has none of the obvious drawcards that pull tourists to its neighbors: no coast, no Saharan dunes, no famous game reserves. What it has instead is an extraordinary cultural richness, warm and genuinely welcoming people, and landscapes that surprise anyone who imagines the Sahel as featureless scrub.

The south and west hold the most for travelers. [Bobo-Dioulasso](/africa/burkinafaso/bobodioulasso), the country's relaxed second city, has one of West Africa's most beautiful mud-brick mosques and a lively music scene. From there it is an easy trip to [Banfora](/africa/burkinafaso/banfora), where you can swim below the Karfiguela waterfalls, hike through the otherworldly Sindou Peaks, and drink fresh-pressed sugarcane juice at the roadside. Further south, Nazinga Game Ranch offers walking safaris among elephants in open savanna — low-key, affordable, and nothing like the manicured parks of East Africa.

[Ouagadougou](/africa/burkinafaso/ouagadougou), the capital, is the cultural engine. Every two years it hosts FESPACO, Africa's largest and oldest film festival, which draws filmmakers and cinephiles from across the continent. Even in off years the city has good live music, busy markets, and a street-food scene built around poulet bicyclette — free-range chicken grilled over charcoal — and cold bottles of Brakina beer. In the far south, [Tiebele](/africa/burkinafaso/tibl) is home to the Kassena people, whose women paint elaborate geometric patterns on the walls of their mud-brick compounds — one of the most striking visual traditions in Africa.

Honesty requires a serious note. Since 2015, and particularly since the military coups of 2022, security has deteriorated badly. Jihadist groups operate across the north and east, and most Western governments advise against all travel to the country. The southwest — Bobo-Dioulasso, Banfora, Nazinga — has remained calmer, but the situation is volatile and checking current advisories before any trip is essential. This is not a country to visit casually right now. But it is a country worth knowing about, and one that many travelers hope to return to.
