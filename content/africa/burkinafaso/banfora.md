Banfora is a small city in southwestern Burkina Faso, and it is surrounded by the best natural scenery in the country. The Cascades de Karfiguéla, about 12 km from town, are a series of waterfalls tumbling through lush vegetation — a rare sight in this part of the Sahel, and a popular swimming spot. The Dômes de Fabédougou, bizarre rock formations eroded into rounded pillars and mushroom shapes, are nearby and well worth the detour.

Lac de Téngrela, a shallow lake about 7 km west of town, has a resident population of hippos that can be observed from pirogues — one of the more surreal wildlife experiences in West Africa. The surrounding area is sugarcane country, greener and more fertile than the arid north, with mango trees and baobabs lining the roads.

Banfora is reached by train or bus from Ouagadougou (about 8 hours) or from Bobo-Dioulasso (about 2 hours). The town itself is quiet and pleasant, with good street food and a couple of decent places to stay.
