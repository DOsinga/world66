Gaoua is the capital of Poni Province in the far southwest of Burkina Faso, about 430 km from Ouagadougou and close to the borders with Ghana and Côte d'Ivoire. Most travellers who make the effort to get here come for one reason: the Musée de Poni, one of the best ethnographic museums in West Africa. It is absolutely worth the journey.

The town sits in Lobi country. The Lobi are one of the least assimilated peoples in Burkina Faso — historically resistant to outside authority, they still live in their traditional fortified mud compounds (called soukala), which look like nothing else in the region. The museum makes those compounds accessible and understandable to visitors.

Gaoua itself is a small, quiet town with a market and the usual amenities for a provincial capital. There is not much else to detain you here, but the museum alone earns its place on a southwest Burkina itinerary.
