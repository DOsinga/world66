title: "Highlights"

---

**FESPACO, Ouagadougou.** Africa's oldest and largest film festival takes over [Ouagadougou](/africa/burkinafaso/ouagadougou) every two years (odd years, usually late February). For a week the capital fills with screenings, outdoor projections, and parties. Even if you are not a cinephile, the energy is infectious — this is Burkina Faso at its most cosmopolitan and proud. Founded in 1969, FESPACO has launched careers across the continent and remains the single most important event in African cinema.

**The Sindou Peaks.** About 50 km west of [Banfora](/africa/burkinafaso/banfora), a forest of slender sandstone pillars rises from the bush like something from another planet. You can hike through narrow corridors between the formations, some over 100 meters tall, with local guides who know the trails and the legends attached to each rock. Late afternoon light turns them orange and gold. It is one of the most photogenic landscapes in West Africa, and you will likely have it nearly to yourself.

**Nazinga Game Ranch.** In the southern savanna near the Ghanaian border, this 94,000-hectare reserve is home to one of West Africa's largest elephant populations — around 600 at last count — along with antelope, monkeys, warthogs, and over 270 bird species. The experience is nothing like an East African safari: you drive quiet dirt tracks with a guide, stopping when elephants appear at a waterhole, and the lodge is simple. What makes Nazinga special is the intimacy. It is affordable, uncrowded, and genuinely wild.

**Karfiguela Waterfalls, Banfora.** A short ride from [Banfora](/africa/burkinafaso/banfora), the Karfiguela cascades tumble over stepped sandstone ledges into natural pools where you can swim. The surrounding landscape is green and lush — mango trees, sugarcane fields — a world away from the dusty Sahel further north. Combine a visit with the nearby Tengrela hippo lake, where local fishermen paddle dugout canoes among resident hippos.

**Tiebele painted houses.** The Kassena people of [Tiebele](/africa/burkinafaso/tibl), about 150 km south of Ouagadougou, are known for their extraordinary decorated compounds. Women paint the mud-brick walls with geometric patterns in black, white, and terracotta — designs that encode clan identity and history. Visits are arranged through the local community association and include a guided walk through the royal compound. It is a candidate for UNESCO World Heritage status and one of the most striking things you will see anywhere in West Africa.

**Bobo-Dioulasso's Grand Mosque.** The mud-brick mosque in [Bobo-Dioulasso](/africa/burkinafaso/bobodioulasso) dates from 1893 and is one of the finest examples of Sudano-Sahelian architecture outside of Djenne. Wooden beams protrude from its walls — used as scaffolding during annual repairs — and the interior is a cool, quiet forest of pillars. Non-Muslims can sometimes visit outside prayer times with a guide from the mosque. The surrounding old quarter is worth exploring on foot, with traditional Bobo houses, dye pits, and a fetish market.
