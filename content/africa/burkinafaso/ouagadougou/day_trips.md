---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Ouagadougou sits in the middle of the Sahel, and the surrounding countryside has more to offer than first impressions suggest. Wildlife, sacred sites, and ancient villages are all within reach.

**Sabou** — About 90 kilometres west of the city, Sabou is known for its sacred crocodile pond. The crocodiles are considered sacred and are remarkably placid — locals will let you get very close for a small fee.

**Ranch de Nazinga** — South of Ouagadougou near Po, this game reserve has a large population of elephants, along with antelopes, monkeys, baboons, and warthogs. It is one of the best places in West Africa to see elephants in the wild.

**La Mare aux Poissons Sacrés de Dafra** — A scenic sacred fish pond about 8 kilometres southeast of the city, where large catfish are considered sacred.

**Mare aux Hippopotames** — About 66 kilometres northeast, where visitors can take a pirogue out to view hippos at close range for a small fee.

**Pabre** — An ancient Mossi village a short distance north of the city, near a large reservoir.
