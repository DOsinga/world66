Ouahigouya is the capital of the Yatenga province in northern Burkina Faso, and the third-largest city in the country. It was once the seat of a powerful Mossi kingdom, and the Naaba (chief) still holds court and performs traditional ceremonies. The city sits in the dry Sahel zone, flat and dusty, with a large market that draws traders from the surrounding villages and from across the border in Mali.

Ouahigouya is not on most tourist itineraries, but for travellers exploring Burkina Faso beyond [Ouagadougou](/africa/burkinafaso/ouagadougou) and [Bobo-Dioulasso](/africa/burkinafaso/bobodioulasso), it offers a glimpse of Sahel town life and Mossi culture. The market is lively and colourful, the people are welcoming, and the surrounding countryside — eroded laterite hills dotted with baobabs — has a stark beauty.
