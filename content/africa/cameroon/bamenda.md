Bamenda is the main city of the anglophone Northwest Region of [Cameroon](/africa/cameroon), set among the hills of the Bamenda Highlands at around 1,600 metres elevation. The altitude gives it one of the most pleasant climates in West Africa — cool, green and often misty.

The countryside around the city is spectacular — rolling hills covered in tea plantations and grasslands, dotted with traditional Tikar and Bamiléké chiefdoms. The surrounding villages have ornately decorated palaces and fon (chief) compounds that welcome visitors. **Lake Bambuluwe**, about half an hour's drive from the city, makes a pleasant day trip.

Bamenda is reached by road from [Yaoundé](/africa/cameroon/yaound) (a long, rough drive). The city has an airport, but flights are rare and irregular. The city's main attraction is as a base for exploring the highlands and the Ring Road circuit — a loop through the surrounding mountains, villages and crater lakes that is one of Cameroon's great drives.
