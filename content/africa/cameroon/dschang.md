Dschang is a university town and hill station in western Cameroon, sitting at around 1,400 metres above sea level in the Menoua region. The altitude keeps temperatures mild and the surrounding landscape green — rolling hills, tea plantations, and forest patches that feel very different from the heat of the lowland cities.

The town has long been a retreat from the heat of the coast and larger cities like Douala, and the cooler climate gives it a pleasant atmosphere. The Museum of Civilisations in Dschang documents the art and culture of the western Cameroonian kingdoms. The area is well suited to hiking, and the local countryside rewards exploration on foot or by bicycle.

A visit to Dschang combines well with a trip to [Foumban](/africa/cameroon/foumban), the historic seat of the Bamoun kingdom, and with hiking in the [Bamenda](/africa/cameroon/bamenda) highlands to the north.
