Foumban is the cultural capital of the Bamoun people and one of Cameroon's most rewarding destinations for visitors interested in history and traditional craft. The city sits in the West Region, about 70 kilometres east of Bafoussam, and has been the seat of the Bamoun sultanate for more than six centuries.

The Royal Palace is the reason to come to Foumban. The current building — a large, German-colonial-era structure built around 1917 — houses the personal possessions of 18 Bamoun sultans, accumulated over dynasties going back to the 14th century. The palace museum contains thrones, masks, clothing, weapons, and ceremonial objects that tell the story of one of West Africa's most sophisticated pre-colonial kingdoms. The museum's collection is genuine and well-presented, and the sultan's family still lives in part of the complex.

Beyond the palace, Foumban is famous throughout Cameroon for its artisans. The streets around the palace are lined with workshops producing bronze sculptures, carved wooden masks, embroidered textiles, and pottery — much of it made by craftspeople who have inherited techniques across generations. The quality is uneven, but the best work is excellent. The town's art market is one of the most authentic in the country, without the aggressive tourist-trap atmosphere of coastal market towns.

Foumban makes a good day trip from Bafoussam, but staying overnight lets you see the market at its most active in the morning and take time with the palace museum properly.
