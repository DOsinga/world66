Travel Guide

Fajã d’Agua is a small village on the west coast of Brava. It is claimed to be the most scenic village in Cape Verde. The houses are arranged in a sickle-formed row along the shore of a somewhat sheltered bay of the sea. Yachters can anchor in the bay. The village is a good starting point for walks along the road towards Vila Nova Sintra, on the old bridle path to Nossa Senhora do Monte or along the coast to the sheltered bay at Porto do Portete.
