Praia is one of the most relaxed capital cities in Africa. With around 160,000 inhabitants it is small by capital standards, spread across a flat-topped plateau that drops sharply to the sea. There are not many conventional sights to tick off, but that is rather the point — the city rewards aimless wandering, good food, and cold Strela beer.

The nightlife scene is genuinely excellent for a city this size, drawing on Cape Verde's rich musical heritage of morna and funaná. Beaches right in the city are decent; better ones, including Praia de Quebra Canela and the more remote stretches to the north, are within 10 to 15 kilometres.
