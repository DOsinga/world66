São Vicente is a small, dry, mountainous island whose entire personality is concentrated in its capital, [Mindelo](/africa/capeverde/mindelo). Outside the city, the landscape is stark — volcanic peaks, barren hillsides, and a handful of small settlements. Monte Verde, at 774 metres, is the highest point and offers views across to [Santo Antão](/africa/capeverde/santo_antao) on a clear day.

The bay of Mindelo, Porto Grande, is one of the finest natural harbours in the Atlantic and was historically a major coaling station for ships crossing between Europe, Africa, and South America. That maritime heritage gave Mindelo its cosmopolitan character — British, Portuguese, and African influences all layered in this compact port town.

Baia das Gatas, a natural rock pool beach on the eastern coast, is good for swimming and hosts an outdoor music festival every August that draws visitors from across the archipelago. The beach at Praia de Laginha is within walking distance of central Mindelo.

Most travellers use São Vicente as a base for exploring [Mindelo](/africa/capeverde/mindelo) and as a jumping-off point for the ferry to [Santo Antão](/africa/capeverde/santo_antao).
