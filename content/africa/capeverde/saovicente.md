Sao Vicente is a small, dry, mountainous island whose entire personality is concentrated in its capital, [Mindelo](/africa/capeverde/mindelo). Outside the city, the landscape is stark -- volcanic peaks, barren hillsides, and a handful of small settlements. Monte Verde, at 774 meters, is the highest point and offers views across to [Santo Antao](/africa/capeverde/santo_antao) on a clear day.

The bay of Mindelo, Porto Grande, is one of the finest natural harbors in the Atlantic and was historically an important coaling station for ships crossing between Europe, Africa, and South America. That maritime heritage gave Mindelo its cosmopolitan character -- British, Portuguese, and African influences all mixed in this small port town.

Sao Baia das Gatas, a natural rock pool beach on the eastern coast, is pleasant for swimming and hosts an annual music festival in August. The beach at Praia de Laginha, within walking distance of central Mindelo, is a good spot for a swim.

Most travelers use Sao Vicente as a base for [Mindelo](/africa/capeverde/mindelo) and as a jumping-off point for the ferry to [Santo Antao](/africa/capeverde/santo_antao).
