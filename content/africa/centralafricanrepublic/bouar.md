Located close to the major border crossing with Cameroon, Bouar is a common stop for overland travellers passing through the [Central African Republic](/africa/centralafricanrepublic). The town is a bit run down, but thanks to its altitude — around 1,000 metres — the climate is more pleasant than in the lowlands.

The main reason to linger is the megaliths. Scattered across the surrounding grasslands are curious granite standing stones weighing up to four tonnes, some dating back over 5,000 years. The site at Bouar is one of the most significant megalithic complexes in Central Africa, though it remains almost entirely unvisited.
