---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

N'Djamena's nightlife is small but spirited. On weekends, a handful of clubs come alive with Chadian, Congolese, and West African music. The crowd is a mix of locals, aid workers, and diplomats. Things don't get going until late, often past midnight.

**Ma Carriere** is one of the better-known spots for a night out, with live music on some weekends. Bars along the main avenues serve cold beer and brochettes (grilled meat skewers). The atmosphere is friendly and relaxed, though you should take the usual precautions and arrange transport home in advance. Walking back to your hotel at night is not advisable.
