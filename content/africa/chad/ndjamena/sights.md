N'Djamena is not a city of grand monuments, but a few places are worth your time.

**National Museum** — Reopened after being looted during the civil wars, the museum houses artifacts from the Sao civilization, traditional Chadian crafts, and some archaeological finds from the Sahara. The collection is modest but gives useful context for the rest of your trip through [Chad](/africa/chad).

The city's markets are its real attraction. The **Grand Marche** is sprawling and chaotic, a good place to browse textiles, spices, and leather goods. Smaller neighborhood markets offer a quieter look at daily life. The banks of the **Chari River**, which forms the border with Cameroon, are worth a walk in the cooler hours.
