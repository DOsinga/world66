Wour is the border post on the route from Libya's al-Qatrun to the Tibesti Mountains, one of the most remote and least-traveled trans-Saharan crossings. It is not a destination in itself, but rather a necessary stop for paperwork and supplies on the way into the Tibesti.

The settlement is tiny. You can pick up basic provisions here, but do not count on it for anything beyond the essentials. Be prepared for lengthy formalities at the border post, and expect to negotiate if your documents are not perfectly in order, which they rarely are. Patience and a sense of humor help.

From Wour, tracks lead south into the heart of the Tibesti toward [Emi Koussi](/africa/chad/emikoussi) and the volcanic landscapes of the range. This is genuinely frontier territory, and the security situation should be checked carefully before attempting any crossing here.
