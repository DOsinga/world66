The Democratic Republic of the Congo is the largest country in sub-Saharan Africa and one of the most difficult to travel in. It is also, for the right kind of traveler, one of the most extraordinary. This is a country of superlatives: the Congo River is the deepest in the world, Virunga is Africa's oldest national park, and the forests of the Congo Basin are second only to the Amazon in scale. Nowhere else can you peer into a volcanic lava lake, trek with mountain gorillas, and dance to live rumba -- all in the same country.

Let's be direct about what you're getting into. The DRC has been through decades of conflict, misrule, and neglect. Infrastructure is minimal outside the major cities. Roads barely exist in much of the country. The east has been wracked by armed conflict for years, and as of early 2026, the security situation in North and South Kivu remains volatile, with M23 rebels and Rwandan forces controlling [Goma](/africa/congokinshasa/goma) and [Bukavu](/africa/congokinshasa/bukavu). Most governments advise against all travel to the eastern provinces. Even [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa), which is relatively stable, requires vigilance -- street crime is common and protests can turn violent without warning.

That said, the DRC rewards those who make the effort. [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa) is one of Africa's great cities -- chaotic, loud, and bursting with music and energy. The Congolese invented rumba and soukous, and live music still pulses through the Matonge district every weekend. The Congo River remains a working highway, with barges and pirogues linking communities that have no roads between them. In the east, when peace allows, Virunga National Park offers gorilla trekking at a fraction of Rwanda's prices, and Nyiragongo volcano has one of the most otherworldly landscapes on earth.

The country is vast -- roughly the size of Western Europe -- and getting around is a logistical challenge. Domestic flights are the only practical way to cover long distances, and even those are unreliable. River travel is slow but possible on some routes. Overland travel outside the main corridors requires serious planning, a sturdy vehicle, and patience.

The Congolese themselves are famously warm and sociable, with a deep love of music, fashion, and conversation. French is the official language, and it will get you everywhere. The food is hearty and cassava-based, the beer is cold, and the welcome is genuine even in the most unexpected places.

## Destinations

- [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa) -- the capital, a sprawling megacity of over 17 million on the Congo River
- [Lubumbashi](/africa/congokinshasa/lubumbashi) -- the mining capital in the southeast, closer to Zambia than to Kinshasa
- [Goma](/africa/congokinshasa/goma) -- gateway to Virunga, currently affected by conflict
- [Bukavu](/africa/congokinshasa/bukavu) -- on Lake Kivu, near Kahuzi-Biega National Park
- [Kisangani](/africa/congokinshasa/kisangani) -- the old Stanleyville, deep in the interior on the Congo River
- [Mbandaka](/africa/congokinshasa/mbandaka) -- a river town on the Equator
- [Epulu](/africa/congokinshasa/epulu) -- home to the Okapi Wildlife Reserve
- [Kolwezi](/africa/congokinshasa/kolwezi) -- mining town in Katanga province
- [Moanda](/africa/congokinshasa/moanda) -- the DRC's only coastal town, on a short stretch of Atlantic shoreline
