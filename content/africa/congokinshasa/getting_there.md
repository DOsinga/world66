---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Most visitors arrive by air through **N'djili International Airport** in [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa). It is the country's main hub, with connections to Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines), Nairobi (Kenya Airways), Johannesburg (South African Airways), Paris (Air France), and Brussels (Brussels Airlines). There are no direct flights from North America. The airport is chaotic -- expect lengthy immigration queues, baggage checks, and officials who may ask for unofficial "fees." Keep your patience and your documents in order.

[Lubumbashi](/africa/congokinshasa/lubumbashi) also has international flights, mainly from Johannesburg and Addis Ababa, and is a more manageable entry point if you are heading to Katanga province.

Overland entry is possible from several neighboring countries, though border crossings range from straightforward to grueling. The crossing from [Brazzaville](/africa/congobrazzaville/brazzaville) to [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa) is the easiest -- a short ferry ride across the Congo River between the two capitals. It operates daily and takes about 20 minutes, but the formalities on both sides can take hours.

From [Rwanda](/africa/rwanda), you can cross at the Petite Barriere border between Gisenyi and [Goma](/africa/congokinshasa/goma), or at Rusizi/Bukavu. These crossings have historically been popular for gorilla trekking trips, but as of early 2026 the eastern borders are affected by the M23 conflict and commercial crossings may be restricted.

From [Zambia](/africa/zambia), the Kasumbalesa crossing south of [Lubumbashi](/africa/congokinshasa/lubumbashi) is well-used and relatively efficient by DRC standards. Crossings from [Uganda](/africa/uganda), [Tanzania](/africa/tanzania), and [Angola](/africa/angola) exist but tend to be remote and slow.

**Visas**: Almost everyone needs a visa. The DRC introduced an e-visa system in early 2025, which simplifies the process somewhat. Apply at least two weeks before travel. The standard tourist e-visa is valid for a single entry and a stay of up to 7 days, though longer visas are available through embassies. Your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond your arrival date.

A departure tax of $55 is charged on international flights and $10 on domestic flights, payable at check-in.
