title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**The Congo River** -- The deepest river in the world and the DRC's lifeline. In a country where roads barely exist, the Congo and its tributaries are the transport network. Taking a barge from [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa) to [Kisangani](/africa/congokinshasa/kisangani) is one of Africa's great journeys -- slow, unpredictable, and completely immersive. You travel for days through dense forest, stopping at riverside villages where traders paddle out in pirogues to sell fish, fruit, and bushmeat. It is uncomfortable, fascinating, and unlike anything else you will do.

**Virunga National Park and Mountain Gorillas** -- Africa's oldest national park, established in 1925, is home to around a quarter of the world's mountain gorillas. When the park is open -- and this depends entirely on the security situation in North Kivu -- gorilla trekking permits cost $400, a fraction of Rwanda's $1,500. The park sits in spectacular volcanic terrain along the Albertine Rift, with lush montane forest and views across Lake Kivu. Rangers lead small groups to habituated gorilla families, and the encounter, when it happens, is profoundly moving. As of early 2026, Virunga is closed due to the M23 conflict. Check the park's official site before planning any visit.

**Nyiragongo Volcano** -- Rising above [Goma](/africa/congokinshasa/goma), Nyiragongo contains one of the world's few permanent lava lakes -- a churning cauldron of molten rock visible from the crater rim. The overnight trek to the summit is steep and physically demanding, climbing through lava fields and cloud forest to reach cabins at the top. At night, the crater glows red against the dark sky. The volcano last erupted in 2021, destroying parts of Goma. Like Virunga, access depends on security conditions and the volcano's activity level.

**Kahuzi-Biega National Park** -- Near [Bukavu](/africa/congokinshasa/bukavu), this UNESCO World Heritage site is the only place to trek with eastern lowland gorillas (Grauer's gorillas), a distinct subspecies larger than their mountain cousins. The park has two habituated families, and the trekking experience is more intimate and less crowded than in Rwanda or Uganda. Access is from Bukavu, about 50 km away. Permits are $400. The park has suffered from armed group activity, so check conditions carefully.

**Kinshasa's Music Scene** -- [Kinshasa](/africa/congokinshasa/kinshasa) is the birthplace of Congolese rumba, a genre so significant it was inscribed on UNESCO's intangible cultural heritage list in 2021. From the 1940s onward, the city's bars and dancehalls produced some of Africa's most influential music -- Franco, Tabu Ley Rochereau, Papa Wemba, Koffi Olomide. The tradition is alive. Head to the Matonge district on a weekend night and you will find live bands playing rumba and ndombolo, musicians in sharp suits, and dancing that goes until dawn. Music is not a sideshow in Kinshasa -- it is the heart of the city's identity.

**The Okapi Wildlife Reserve** -- Deep in the Ituri Forest near [Epulu](/africa/congokinshasa/epulu), this reserve protects the okapi, a forest-dwelling relative of the giraffe found nowhere else on earth. The reserve is also home to forest elephants, chimpanzees, and Mbuti pygmy communities who have lived in these forests for millennia. Getting here is an expedition in itself -- the roads from [Kisangani](/africa/congokinshasa/kisangani) are rough and unreliable -- but the Ituri Forest is one of Africa's last great wilderness areas.
