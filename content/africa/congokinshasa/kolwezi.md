Kolwezi is a mining city of roughly 450,000 in the southern Katanga province of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and its story is inseparable from what lies beneath its red soil. The city sits on some of the richest cobalt and copper deposits on the planet, and mining — industrial and artisanal — dominates everything here. It is not a tourist destination in any conventional sense, but for those with a reason to be in this part of the DRC, it offers a raw look at one of the world's most strategically important mining regions.

The city itself is functional rather than attractive, with the usual mix of colonial-era infrastructure and newer Chinese-built roads connecting the various mining concessions. Security conditions fluctuate, and you should get current advice before traveling. Infrastructure is basic — there are a handful of guesthouses catering mainly to mining company visitors, and a small commercial airport with connections to Lubumbashi. The surrounding landscape, beyond the mine tailings, is surprisingly green savanna, and Lake Nzilo to the north offers a rare escape from the dust and trucks of the mining zone.
