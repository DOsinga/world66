Mbandaka sits almost exactly on the equator, on the banks of the Congo River in the northwestern DRC. It is one of the few Congolese cities accessible by river, and the journey upriver from Kinshasa, while slow and uncomfortable, is one of the great African travel experiences for those with the time and temperament for it.

The city itself is quiet by Congolese standards, with wide colonial-era boulevards slowly being reclaimed by tropical vegetation. The surrounding Equateur province is densely forested, and Mbandaka serves as a jumping-off point for visits to the bonobo habitat in the Salonga National Park, though organizing such trips requires serious advance planning and local contacts. The central market is lively and worth exploring, and the views across the vast Congo River at sunset are unforgettable.
