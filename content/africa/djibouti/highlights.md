**Lake Assal — Africa's lowest point**

[Lake Assal](/africa/djibouti/lakeassal) sits 155 meters below sea level in a volcanic crater, making it the lowest point on the African continent and the third lowest on earth. The water is ten times saltier than the ocean — you float effortlessly, like the Dead Sea but without the crowds. The shore is crusted in thick white salt, and Afar salt caravans still cut blocks here to trade in [Ethiopia](/africa/ethiopia). The drive from Djibouti City passes through black lava fields and the turquoise inlet of Ghoubet el Kharab, a collapsed volcanic caldera that locals call the Devil's Cauldron. The whole landscape feels geological in a way few places on earth do.

**Lac Abbe's moonscape**

On the Ethiopian border, 140 kilometers southwest of the capital, [Lac Abbe](/africa/djibouti/lakeabbe) is a dried-out salt lake studded with travertine chimneys — some over 50 meters tall — that vent sulfurous steam into the desert air. At dawn, when flamingos pick through the shallow pools and the chimneys glow in the low light, it looks genuinely otherworldly. The legend that the 1968 *Planet of the Apes* was filmed here is persistent but false — that was Arizona. The reality is strange enough without Hollywood. Getting here requires a 4WD and ideally an overnight camp, but the effort is the point.

**Whale shark diving in the Gulf of Tadjoura**

Between November and February, juvenile whale sharks gather in the Gulf of Tadjoura in remarkable numbers to feed on seasonal plankton blooms. You can snorkel or dive with them from boats out of [Djibouti City](/africa/djibouti/djibouticity) or [Tadjoura](/africa/djibouti/tadjoura) — this is one of the most reliable and accessible whale shark encounters anywhere in the world. Scientists believe the gulf may be a nursery for the species. Manta rays often appear in the same season.

**The Moucha Islands**

A thirty-minute boat ride from Djibouti City, the Moucha and Maskali islands sit in the Gulf of Tadjoura surrounded by healthy coral reefs. The snorkeling is excellent — parrotfish, angelfish, sea turtles, and the occasional ray — and the white-sand beaches make a welcome contrast to the arid mainland. Moucha also has the largest protected mangrove forest in the country. Easy to visit as a day trip; boats leave from the port.

**The Djibouti-Addis Ababa railway**

The modern electrified railway from Djibouti to [Addis Ababa](/africa/ethiopia/addisababa) covers 750 kilometers in about twelve hours, climbing from the scorching coast to the Ethiopian highlands above 2,000 meters. It opened in 2018 as East Africa's first electric railway, replacing the old French-built line that had been running (barely) since 1917. The journey is dramatic — desert gives way to green mountains — and it is one of the great train rides on the continent. Online ticketing was introduced in late 2025.
