Asyut is Egypt's largest city in Upper Egypt outside of Luxor, sitting on the west bank of the Nile about 380 km south of Cairo. It's primarily a transport and commercial hub rather than a tourist destination, but it does have some points of interest for those passing through.

The city is home to Asyut University, one of Egypt's oldest, which gives it a young, relatively lively atmosphere. The nearby Deir el-Muharraq monastery, located about 12 km north, is a significant Coptic Christian pilgrimage site believed to mark a resting place of the Holy Family during their flight into Egypt.

The area around Asyut has ancient roots — the nearby necropolis of Meir contains rock-cut tombs from the Old and Middle Kingdom periods, and the ancient city of Lycopolis once stood here. Most travellers stop only briefly, but Asyut is a reasonable overnight break on the long journey along the Nile valley.
