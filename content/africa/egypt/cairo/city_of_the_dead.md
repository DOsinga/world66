This vast necropolis on Cairo's eastern edge — officially the Qarafa cemetery — stretches for kilometres and contains some of the city's most elaborate funerary architecture, including the mausoleums of Mamluk sultans. A large population of squatters has long made their homes here among the tombs. It's worth a daytime visit for the architecture; don't go after dark.
