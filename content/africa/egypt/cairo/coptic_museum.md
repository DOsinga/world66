In the heart of Old Cairo, the Coptic Museum houses a rare collection of early Christian relics — architectural fragments, textiles, icons, and manuscripts — tracing the history of Egypt's Christian community from the Roman period through the Islamic conquest. The building itself, with its mashrabiya screens and carved ceilings, is worth the visit alone.
