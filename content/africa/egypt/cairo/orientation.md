---
title: "Orientation"
type: section
---

Most of Cairo sits on the east bank of the Nile. Downtown — the European-grid neighbourhood around Midan Tahrir — is the commercial and transport hub. Islamic Cairo stretches northeast toward the Citadel. Old (Coptic) Cairo is to the south, accessible by metro (Mar Girgis station). Zamalek, an upscale residential island in the Nile, has most of the better bars and international restaurants. The Pyramids and Sphinx are on the west bank, in Giza — technically a separate city, but effectively part of greater Cairo.
