Saqqara, about 30 km south of Cairo, is the site of the Step Pyramid of Zoser — the world's first pyramid, built around 2650 BC. The tombs here are older than Giza and often more interesting inside; a doctor's tomb contains depictions of surgical operations. Combine it with a visit to the ruins of Memphis, once the greatest city of the ancient world, where a colossal statue of Ramses II still lies in an open-air museum.
