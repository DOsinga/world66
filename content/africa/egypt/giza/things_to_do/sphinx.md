Carved from a single outcrop of limestone, the Sphinx has the body of a lion and, traditionally, the face of pharaoh Khafre — though arguments still rage over whether the head was re-carved from an older, larger original. The nose was lost centuries before Napoleon's troops got the blame for it. Best viewed from the causeway in front of Khafre's pyramid, or at sunset from the Sound and Light Show seating area east of the plateau.
