---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Hurghada"
type: section
---

Though Hurghada is a popular tourism destination in Egypt, visitors
may find the lack of amenities and entertainment here a bit
disheartening, especially if they are coming from larger cities like
Sinai or Cairo. Here the resorts and all-inclusive properties take up
much of the landscape and having a budget trip is a bit more
challenging than in those locations. However, it can be quite rewarding
if you can arrange one and have some room to negotiate in terms of your
budget.

**Budget Activities in Hurghada**

The main source of entertainment in Hurghada is the water. Lined
with beaches, Hurghada is a destination for water sport lovers
interesting in scuba diving, snorkeling, windsurfing and more. While
most of these sports cost a bit of money to participate in, there are
several snorkeling outfits that offer inexpensive lifts to the reefs
and afternoons of snorkeling as part of a larger group. Inquire at your
accommodation for budget water sports if you are interested, and have
some money to spare.

Otherwise, Hurghada is a great place to unwind and spend a few days
on the beach or strolling through its wide boulevards. When combined
with another city tour elsewhere, Hurghada is often a great place to
just kick back and relax. If that is the case, you should have no
problem sticking to your budget, as you will only have to spend money
on food and drinks. Beyond the beach, most activities here require
money – some popular Hurghada activities include camel rides, an
aquarium, bowling alley and tennis.

**Budget Accommodations in Hurghada**

Though the coast here is dominated by resorts, the city has several
low budget options available for lodging. It is always the tourist
season here, but the Christmas and Easter holidays, as well as the
Russian vacation time of August and September, are high times for
tourism. For budget travel, the winter is when you have to plan
for an influx in tourism. Backpackers come from the Nile Valley on
their way to Sinai. If you will be traveling during the winter and
plan to stay at budget accommodations, be sure to book your hotel well
in advance.

**Budget Eating and Shopping in Hurghada**

For the best value on food, head to the downtown area, or El Dahar,
to find a wide selection of Egyptian dining, including small street
stall and other inexpensive restaurants. There are many international
fast food chains here as well.

There is a traditional Egyptian bazaar in the old downtown area, with
many shops selling inexpensive souvenirs. Be prepared to bargain here
for good prices. Some popular tourist items from this area include
papyrus and shisha pipes.

Though the resort atmosphere here makes a budget traveler have to
work a little harder for a deal, savings can be had in Hurghada by
planning ahead and exploring some of the inexpensive dining and
entertainment options available.
