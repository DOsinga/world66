Port Safaga lies 53 km south of Hurghada on the Red Sea coast and has a completely different character from its busier neighbour. The town grew up around a commercial port and phosphate export facilities, but it has quietly developed a devoted following among windsurfers and kitesurfers, drawn by the strong, consistent northerly winds that funnel through the bay. The 1993 Red Sea World Windsurfing Championships were held here and the sport never really left.

The black-sand beaches are unlike anything at Hurghada and the mineral-rich water has attracted visitors looking for therapeutic benefits alongside the sports crowd. Diving is excellent too, with the towering walls of Panorama and Abu Qifan and the chain reefs of Tobia Arbaa offering encounters with tuna, sharks, and occasional mantas.

Safaga makes a good base for a day trip to [Luxor](/africa/egypt/luxor), about 220 km away across the Eastern Desert, and is also a starting point for exploring the ancient Roman quarries at Mons Claudianus.
