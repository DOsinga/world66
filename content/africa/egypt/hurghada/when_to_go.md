---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Hurghada has a desert climate — hot, dry, and sunny almost year-round — moderated by breezes off the Red Sea. The water temperature never drops below 20°C, which makes diving and snorkeling viable in any month.

**October to April** is the most comfortable season for most visitors. Daytime temperatures range from 20–28°C, evenings can be surprisingly cool (10–15°C), and the sea is still warm enough for swimming. European package tourism peaks in winter, so expect busy beaches and higher hotel prices around Christmas and New Year.

**May to September** is hot — consistently 35°C or above — but sea breezes keep the resort strip bearable and the water temperature is ideal. This is low season for European visitors, which means cheaper accommodation and quieter dive sites.

**Wind** is a significant factor, particularly in spring and autumn. Strong northerly winds can stir up sand and make sea conditions choppy, occasionally closing dive sites for a day or two. Windsurfers, on the other hand, time their visits specifically for the windy periods.

**Ramadan** affects opening hours and the general atmosphere — restaurants may be closed or reduced during daylight hours, but evenings become more lively with iftar celebrations.
