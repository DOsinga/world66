The house where Louis IX of France was imprisoned in 1250 after the Seventh Crusade was crushed at the battle of Mansoura. Nearly 800 years later it still stands, now a small museum with the chains, coins, weapons and personal effects of the captured king and his court. A curious footnote of medieval history in a city almost no tourist visits.
