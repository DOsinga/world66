---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Sharm el-Sheikh has a surprisingly diverse food scene for a beach resort. Na'ama Bay's promenade is lined with restaurants serving everything from Egyptian seafood and grilled meats to Indian, Thai, Chinese, Italian, and Lebanese cuisine. Quality varies — the best restaurants are genuinely good, the worst are tourist traps serving overpriced mediocrity.

**Na'ama Bay**

Little Buddha in Na'ama Bay is one of the better options for Asian food, mixing Japanese, Thai, and Chinese dishes in a stylish setting. For seafood, the restaurants along the waterfront display the day's catch at the entrance so you can point at what you want before it's cooked — a system that usually produces good results. Abou El Sid serves traditional Egyptian food including mezze, grilled meats, and slow-cooked dishes at reasonable prices (note that prices on the menu sometimes exclude service and tax). Hard Rock Café is reliable for a casual meal and draws a lively crowd.

**The Old Market**

The street-food stalls in the Old Market (Sharm el-Maya) offer the cheapest and most local eating in Sharm. A meal of grilled fish, rice, and salad for two costs a few dollars. The atmosphere is lively if you can navigate the shopkeepers, and the food is often better than anything you'll find in the tourist restaurants.

**International Fast Food**

Pizza Hut, KFC, and McDonald's are all present in Na'ama Bay for those in need of a fallback. They're not recommended when local alternatives are nearby and inexpensive, but they are there.
