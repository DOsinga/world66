---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Sharm el-Sheikh sits at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, connected to the rest of Egypt and the world by air, sea, and road.

**By Air**

Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport (SSH) receives direct flights from European cities — particularly the UK, Germany, and Russia — as well as from Cairo, Hurghada, and other Egyptian cities. If you are flying from the Americas, Asia, or Australasia, you will almost certainly connect through Cairo. EgyptAir and several charter carriers serve the Cairo route with multiple daily departures.

**By Sea**

International Fast Ferries operates a high-speed catamaran service between Hurghada and Sharm el-Sheikh, crossing the Red Sea in roughly 90 minutes. The service runs several times a week but is not always reliable — check current schedules and be prepared for a bumpy crossing. A one-way ticket costs around E£250.

**By Land**

Buses connect Sharm el-Sheikh to Cairo (around 6–8 hours), Dahab (90 minutes), and other Sinai towns. The main terminal is in the Old Market area. The journey crosses multiple military checkpoints — carry your passport and allow extra time. The road through the southern Sinai is spectacular, passing through mountains and desert valleys. Keep your belongings close at bus terminals and watch for pickpockets.
