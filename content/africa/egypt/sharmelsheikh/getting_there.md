---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Sharm el Sheikh is located on the Southern tip of the Sinai
Peninsula in Egypt. Sharm el Sheikh is a bustling tourist city that
most refer to as "Sharm". Many tourists and travelers flock to Sharm
for its proximity to the Red Sea, which if often referred to as one of
the best scuba dive sites in the world due to it's over 200 different
types of coral reefs, over 1000 different species of fish, and it's
calm waters.

Getting to Sharm el Sheikh is done one of three ways:

**By Air**

Probably the quickest and easiest way to arrive in Sharm is through
Sharm el Sheikh's Ophira International Airport. The Sharm airport
services many airlines, with most flights arriving from Europe and the
Middle East. If you are traveling from the America's, Asia, or South
Pacific you may find yourself flying via Cairo, and then onto Sharm.
There are many domestic airlines that run services daily from Cairo to
Sharm.

**By Sea**

There is a ferry company called International Fast Ferries that
operates high speed ferry service from Hurghada on the Red Sea Coast.
This line runs four times per week and isn't the most reliable. The
ride is approximately 90 minutes, and very bumpy.

**By Land**

Sharm el Sheikh can be reached by car from most parts of Egypt, and
there are daily buses that operates between most Egyptian cities to
Sharm. Traveling by land can be a bit of a hassle due to the many
checkpoints, but the drive is very scenic and well worth it. Be very
careful in the main bus terminals, keep you belongings close to you at
all times, and watch for pickpockets.
