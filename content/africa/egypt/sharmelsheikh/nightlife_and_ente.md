---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

Although nightlife activity throughout Egypt has become increasingly
more appealing, the city of Sharm el Sheikh holds the reputation for
providing the most diverse variety of lounges, clubs, and bars.
Travelers come from all parts of the world to enjoy the luxurious
resorts, restaurants, and beaches that adorn Sharm el Sheikh. The
surrounding areas of Sharm allow for beautiful weather and an endless
amount of daytime activities, but the nightlife in Sharm has recently
drawn more attention than expected.

**Legal Drinking Age**

Similar to laws in the United States, the legal drinking age in
Sharm el Sheikh is 21. Clubs, bars, and evening functions that serve
alcohol do require legal picture identification before allowing entry.
However, this law is in no way discouraging to travelers under the age
of 21; there is plenty to enjoy during evening hours in town.

**Beach Parties**

Beach parties start at sunset at Terrazinna beach near the Iberotel
Palace. There is a D.J. on the beach and mesmerizing fire shows are
exhibited on Tuesdays and Fridays. The beaches along Sharm and Naama
Bay are quite lively throughout the night. Most beachside resorts also
cater to nightlife seekers by offering casino and bar services. The
Ritz-Carlton, The Hyatt Regency, and Four Seasons are located in Sharm.
Other internationally popular spots include Planet Hollywood and Hard
Rock Café.

**Casinos**

Sharm is home to one of the largest casinos in the Middle East-Sinai
Grand Casino. The hours of operation are from 6 pm until 6 am. Sinai
Grand Casino is known for serving outstanding entrees and providing a
plethora of live shows to guests-from belly dancing to jazz concerts.
Not to mention, there are over 200 slot machines.

**Night Clubs**

If travelers are searching for a nightclub atmosphere, the Bus Stop
is a popular place to go. It is attached to the Sanafir Hotel in a
landmark structured red bus. Visitors cannot miss the entrance or the
disco themed music that can be heard from outside of the club. Within
the Sanafir Hotel, there are Thursday night parties. Dancing takes
place in the courtyard of the hotel as the D.J. mixes techno music for
the crowd. Fashion shows are frequently held along with ethnic dancing
shows. The courtyard of Sanafir Hotel is often compared to the
nightclubs of Ibiza.

**Dinner and Dancing**

For a cozier and more extravagant experience, visitors should plan
an evening at Little Buddha. The exotic ambiance is a result of Chinese
and Japanese art décor throughout the premises. The special cuisine is
a fusion of Japanese and French gourmet dishes, which are freshly
prepared by world-renowned chefs. Once dinnertime has passed, the
upstairs level of Little Buddha transforms into a dance floor.
Delicious martinis are served into the late hours of the evening.

**Sports Bars**

Sports fanatics can stay updated on daily programs at bars such as
Bianco’s, Tavern, and Camel Bar. These sports bars are fully equipped
with large flat screen televisions, basic meals, and a variety of
beers. Similar to typical nightclubs within Sharm, most of the sports
bars are open until 2 am.
