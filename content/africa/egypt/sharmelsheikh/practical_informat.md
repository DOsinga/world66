**Visas**

Most visitors require a tourist visa for Egypt, obtainable on arrival at Cairo Airport for USD 25. However, if you fly directly into Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport and plan to stay within the Sinai region only, you can obtain a free 15-day entry stamp at the airport without a full visa. If you intend to travel to Cairo or elsewhere in Egypt, you need the full visa.

**Currency**

Egypt uses the Egyptian Pound (E£). ATMs are plentiful in Na'ama Bay and accept Visa, Mastercard, Cirrus, and Plus. Most hotels and larger restaurants accept credit cards. When changing money, ask for small denominations — getting change can be difficult, and it is easy to confuse denominations (the 50-piastre note and the 50-pound note can look deceptively similar).

**Language**

Arabic is the official language. In the tourist areas of Sharm, English is widely spoken, as are some Russian and German. Arabic phrases are warmly received.

**Tipping**

Tipping (baksheesh) is standard and expected. Tip private drivers E£5–20 depending on the journey. Hotel porters, restaurant servers, and guides all expect small tips. Carry small bills — a handful of 5-pound notes is useful for day-to-day tips.

**Health**

Sharm el-Sheikh International Hospital is on Na'ama Bay Road, reachable at +20 69 366 0893. The Sharm el-Sheikh Hyperbaric Medical Center (for diving-related injuries) can be reached at +20 69 366 0922 or on their 24-hour emergency line at +20 12 212 4292. Many common medications are available over the counter at local pharmacies.

**Safety**

In an emergency dial 122 (police). Sharm el-Sheikh is generally safe for tourists, but petty theft and scams targeting visitors do occur. Keep belongings close in markets and bus terminals; negotiate taxi fares before boarding.

**Electricity**

220V/50Hz. Two round pin sockets (European type C/F).

**Time Zone**

GMT +2 (Egypt Standard Time; Egypt does not currently observe daylight saving time).
