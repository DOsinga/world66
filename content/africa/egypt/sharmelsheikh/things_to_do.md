---

Sharm's position on the Red Sea makes it one of the world's premier destinations for diving and snorkelling, but there is plenty to do on land too — from jeep safaris through the Sinai desert to camel rides with Bedouin guides.

**Diving**

The reefs around Sharm el-Sheikh are exceptional. Ras Mohammed National Park, accessible by boat from the Na'ama Bay marina, protects some of the most diverse coral in the world. The Shark and Jolanda Reefs are famous dive sites, as is the Thistlegorm wreck — a British WWII supply ship sunk in 1941 that now sits at 30 metres, intact and encrusted with coral. Dive centres line the beach in Na'ama Bay and sharks Bay; reputable operations include Sinai Divers, Camel Dive Club, and Aquavision. All offer PADI courses for beginners as well as guided dives for the experienced.

**Snorkelling**

You do not need to dive to see the Red Sea's reefs. Many of the best snorkelling spots are accessible from the beach or by short boat trip. Hire gear at your hotel or from beachside vendors, or join a boat trip to Ras Mohammed for a guided snorkel.

**Parasailing and Watersports**

Operators on the beach in Na'ama Bay run parasailing, wakeboarding, and jet-ski rentals. Safety standards vary — observe before you book.

**Desert Safaris and Bedouin Camping**

The desert surrounding Sharm is extraordinary — granite mountains, sand-filled valleys, and almost total silence once you leave the coast. Jeep and quad-bike safaris head into the Sinai interior, often stopping at Bedouin settlements for tea. Overnight camping trips include a fire-cooked dinner and a sky full of stars; bring warm layers, as desert nights are cold even in summer. Book through your hotel or at any of the operators in Na'ama Bay.

**Golf**

The Jolie Ville Golf Resort has an 18-hole course set against the desert mountains. Contact them at +20 69 3600 100.
