---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

**By air:** Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport receives direct charter and scheduled flights from Europe and the Middle East, making it the easiest entry point for most international visitors. Taba International Airport (near the Israeli border in the north) handles some charter traffic. Cairo is the main hub for onward connections within Egypt.

**By road from Cairo:** Buses run from Cairo to Sharm el-Sheikh (roughly 6–7 hours), Dahab (7–8 hours), and Taba. East Delta Bus Company operates the main routes. The road crosses the Suez Canal via the Ahmed Hamdi Tunnel before entering the Sinai.

**From Israel:** The Taba border crossing (Menachem Begin Crossing) connects Eilat in Israel with Taba in Egypt. It is open 24 hours. Travellers entering from Israel can obtain a Sinai-only entry stamp (free) that allows access to the Sinai Peninsula as far as Sharm el-Sheikh but not the rest of Egypt. A full Egyptian visa is required to travel beyond the Sinai.

**By ferry:** High-speed ferries connect Aqaba in Jordan with Nuweiba on the Sinai coast, taking about 1–1.5 hours. Slow ferries make the same crossing in 3–4 hours. This is a useful route for travellers crossing between Jordan and Egypt overland.
