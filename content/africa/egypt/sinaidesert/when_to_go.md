---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Sinai has a desert climate — dry year-round, with extreme summer heat and surprisingly cold winter nights in the mountains. The best times to visit depend on what you plan to do.

**October to April** is the best season for trekking, climbing, and sightseeing. Daytime temperatures in the St Katherine highlands range from 15–25°C, and the mountain nights are cold but manageable with proper layers. The summit of Mount Sinai can dip below freezing between December and February — the blanket vendors at the top are there for good reason.

**November to March** offers the clearest water for diving and the best visibility at Ras Mohammed and in the Gulf of Aqaba. Sea temperatures drop to around 21°C, which is comfortable in a 3mm wetsuit.

**June to August** is peak beach season in Sharm and Dahab. It is also extremely hot — daytime air temperatures reach 40°C or more, and the interior desert is brutal. Most serious trekking in the St Katherine area shuts down. Diving remains good in the mornings before the heat sets in.

**Ramadan** shifts the rhythm of daily life significantly: restaurants close during daylight hours, and the atmosphere in Egyptian towns changes. It can be a fascinating time to visit or an inconvenient one, depending on your priorities.
