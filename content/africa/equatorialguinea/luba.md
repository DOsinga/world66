Less than 50 km from the capital [Malabo](/africa/equatorialguinea/malabo), Luba is the second-largest town on Bioko Island and a useful base for exploring the island's southern coast. The town itself is a quiet port with a small fishing community, but its surroundings are the real draw.

The beaches near Luba are among the finest in Equatorial Guinea. Arena Blanca is a particular highlight — a stretch of white sand that sees few visitors. Several small fishing villages dot the coastline nearby, and two hiking trails head into the forest, offering a taste of Bioko's dense tropical interior. Taxis and minibuses connect Luba to Malabo regularly, making day trips easy in either direction.
