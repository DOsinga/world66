---
title: "People"
type: section
---

Keren is one of Eritrea's most ethnically diverse cities. The Bilen are the largest group and the most closely associated with the town historically, but significant communities of Tigre, Tigrinya, and Saho people all live here. Most residents are Muslim — in contrast to the predominantly Christian highlands further east — and the presence of several mosques gives the city a different rhythm and feel to Asmara.

The Bilen speak a Cushitic language related to Agaw, distinct from the Semitic languages that dominate much of the rest of Eritrea. You'll hear it in the market alongside Tigre and Tigrinya, and sometimes Arabic.

The town has not had an easy history. During the Italian colonial period it was developed as an administrative and commercial center. In World War II it was fiercely contested — the Battle of Keren in early 1941 lasted 53 days before the Italians surrendered to British and Commonwealth forces. During the Eritrean War of Independence it was again a front-line town, changing hands and suffering heavily before Eritrea's liberation in 1991.

Despite all that, the people of Keren are known for being welcoming to outsiders. The pace of life is noticeably slower than Asmara.
