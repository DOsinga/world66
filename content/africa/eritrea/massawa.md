Massawa is Eritrea's main port and one of the most atmospheric towns on the Red Sea coast. The city is spread across two islands and a strip of mainland, connected by causeways. Before Eritrean independence it was the headquarters of the Ethiopian navy, and the war of independence left it badly damaged — bombed-out buildings still stand alongside functioning ones. But the town has great charm, especially at night when the narrow alleyways between the Ottoman-Egyptian coral-block houses and crumbling Italian colonial facades come alive with people.

Taulud Island has the main sights: the old railway station (trains once climbed from here up to [Asmara](/africa/eritrea/asmara)), St Mariam's Cathedral, and the Imperial Palace. Cross the second causeway and you reach the old port area, the most characterful part of town — faded Italian architecture, fish restaurants, and a waterfront that has not changed much in decades.

Massawa is the starting point for trips to the Dahlak Archipelago, a scattering of islands with excellent diving and virtually no other tourists. Sheikh Said Island (Green Island), just opposite Taulud, makes an easy day trip. North of town, the white sand beach at Gurgussum is clean and uncrowded — good for swimming, snorkelling, and doing not much at all. Be warned: Massawa is one of the hottest cities on earth, regularly exceeding 40°C in summer.
