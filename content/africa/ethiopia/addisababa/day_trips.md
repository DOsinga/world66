---
title: "Day Trips in Addis Ababa"
type: section
---

Addis Ababa is many cities cast into one and a stroke of sheer cultural and artistic genius. This guide
offers visitors information on the best day trip options to enjoy the
magnificence of the surroundings of this quintessentially African land.

**A Slice of Pre-Historic Life**

Melka Kunture (50 kilometers from Addis Ababa) has an interesting
pre-historical legacy that manifests itself in the form of several
stone tools and other rudimentary relics dating back to millions of
years, which have been discovered here over a period of time. Witness
snapshots of the ingenious and self-sufficient life of the ancient man
in the form of fossils and remains of innate settlements. A little
further from Melka Kuntare is the rare and must see Adadi Mariam Cave
Church, touted to have been in existence since the 13 th
century. The destination offers brilliant vistas of the rock-hewn
religious edifices and the sheer architectural genius of the era.
Approximately 35 kilometers from Adadi Mariam is Tiya Stelae Fields
that boasts of innumerable prehistoric monoliths on the lines of the
Axum Stelae. The drive through these destinations is pleasant and
offers panoramic views of the rustic charm of Africa.

**Blue Nile Gorges **

Visitors can take a day trip to Blue Nile Gorge located
approximately 200 kilometers to the north of the city. The region is
interspersed with quaint, photogenic villages and the celebrated 13 th
century Debre Libanos monastery that is dramatically nestled in a high
canyon. There is also a Portuguese Bridge and a few rugged walking
trails that offer a nice adventurous trek through the wilderness of the
region. Watch several species of ingenious birds and mammals while
wandering through the paths of Blue Nile. Drive further through the
hills of the area to enjoy the striking scenery from the tip of the
gorge.

**Sodore**

Located approximately 126 kilometers to the south of Addis Ababa is
a beautiful natural resort of the fascinating towns of Akaki, Zeit,
Mojo and Kaliti. There is a large swimming pool with warm and
rejuvenating mineral water that helps visitors distress while watching
amusing monkeys and an array of bird species perched on the trees
adorning the adjacent river side. If you want to explore the region a
little, spend a few hours at Debre Zeit (on the way back to Addis
Ababa) for its pristine crater lakes.

**Ankober**

The drive to Ankober offers visitors many breathtaking natural
visuals of rough gravel roads and lush green hills to reach the
stunning Kundi Mountain preening over the Great Rift Valley. Enjoy the
trek through the surrounding Eucalyptus forests.

Addis Ababa’s surrounding regions are a bagful of rustic charm and
ancient memories that have been brilliantly captured in its
architectural structures, pre historic remains and wild natural appeal.
