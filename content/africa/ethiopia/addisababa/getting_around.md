---
title: "Getting Around in Addis Ababa"
type: section
---

Addis Ababa is a modern city in Ethiopia and **Addis Ababa Transportation** is reliable and safe. Most travelers arrive in Addis Ababa by air. The airport offers a free shuttle service that will take you into town. Once you get into town you will find that there are several methods of transportation.

**Walking**

Walking in Addis Ababa is one method of transportation. You should know that the sidewalks are not in the best of shape. During the rainy season most of the sidewalks turn into mud puddles. The rainy season in Addis Ababa is June to September. Walking at night is as safe as it is in any major city. You should always try walking with at least one other person and stay away from unlit streets.

**Taxis **

Taxis are a popular method of transportation in Addis Ababa. There are plenty of taxis and the prices are reasonable. While the taxis are normally metered you should still inquire about the price before getting in. Taking a taxi in Addis Ababa is a safe and reliable method of transportation.

**Public Bus**

The public bus system in Addis Ababa is the method of transportation most locals use. The public buses are white and blue and they travel throughout most of the city. Taking the public bus is a cheap and easy way to get around in Addis Ababa. The buses tend to get crowded and can get very hot. Carry change with you as the drivers do not like to give change.
