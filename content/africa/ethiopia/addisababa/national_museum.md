The National Museum's star attraction is Lucy, the 3.2-million-year-old hominid skeleton discovered in the Afar region in 1974. The actual remains are kept in secure archives, but the replica on display is excellent. The museum also holds archaeological finds, Ethiopian art, and a collection of royal regalia. It is small enough to see in an hour or two, and worth every minute.
