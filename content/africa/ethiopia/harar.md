The old walled city of Harar used to be strictly off limits for non-Muslims. It was one of the most holy cities in the country and remains the fourth holiest city in Islam.

In the west, Harar is also famous for the fact that the French poet Arthur Rimbaud lived here for a while. His house, now a museum, is still one of the most important sights of the city. Other sights include the Friday Mosque and the old city walls — Harar's Jugol, the fortified historic town, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with 82 mosques and over 100 shrines packed inside its five gates.

Harar is on the train line from [Addis Ababa](/africa/ethiopia/addisababa) to [Djibouti](/africa/djibouti). This line was built by the French, and there is still a French-language girls' school in the town — so if you happen to speak French you may find it easier to get around than with Arabic.
