Mekele is the capital of the Tigray region in northern Ethiopia, sitting at about 2,000 metres above sea level on a plateau east of the Ethiopian Highlands. It's a city shaped by imperial history — Emperor Yohannes IV made it his capital in the 1870s — and more recently by conflict; the city was at the centre of the Tigray war that devastated the region from 2020 to 2022.

For travellers, Mekele is primarily a base for ventures into some of Ethiopia's most extreme landscapes. The Danakil Depression — one of the lowest, hottest, and most geologically active places on earth — lies to the northeast, and organised tours depart from here to the sulphurous Dallol hot springs and the permanently active Erta Ale volcano. The rock-hewn churches of Tigray, scattered through the surrounding mountains, are also accessible from Mekele.

The city itself has the Emperor's 19th-century palace, now a museum, and a covered market that serves the surrounding farming communities. Check current security conditions before travelling to the region.
