Emperor Yohannes IV chose Mekele as the seat of his government and built this graceful stone palace in the 1870s. After his death in battle against the Mahdists in 1889, the palace was preserved and is now a museum. Inside you can see the Emperor's throne, royal bed, ceremonial dress, rifles, and church collections including illuminated manuscripts, processional crosses, and paintings.
