Ambo is a spa town in the Mirab Shewa Zone of Ethiopia's Oromia Region, sitting at 2,101 metres above sea level about 115 kilometres west of Addis Ababa. It is best known across the country for its mineral water — bottled just outside town, the Ambo brand is arguably Ethiopia's most recognised sparkling water. Beyond the springs, the town has a relaxed highland character, a busy Saturday market, and serves as a practical base for exploring the surrounding countryside.

The main draw in the area is Mount Wenchi, a dormant volcano to the south whose caldera contains a crater lake and monastery. The Guder and Huluka waterfalls are also within reach. Ambo has had a long association with agriculture — an agricultural research station has operated here since the 1970s, and there is a college of agriculture that gives the town a younger, student population.

Historically, Ambo has seen more than its share of drama. Lij Iyasu, the deposed heir to the Ethiopian throne, was briefly held here after his capture. During the Italian occupation in the late 1930s, the town gained a post office, telephone service, and a clinic. When Allied forces arrived in early 1941, they evacuated a panicked Italian garrison without a fight. The town eventually fell to EPRDF forces in April 1991 in the final weeks of the civil war, as part of a strategy to encircle Addis Ababa rather than assault it directly.
