---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Libreville has the best restaurant scene in Gabon, though that is a low bar. French cuisine dominates the upscale end, while street food and local joints serve Gabonese staples at more reasonable prices.

**Le Pescadou** on Rue Lafond is one of the city's top seafood restaurants. The fish is fresh and the preparation is French-influenced. Not cheap, but reliable.

**La Dorade** at the Hotel Alize offers a good mix of Gabonese and French dishes in a comfortable setting. A solid choice if you want to try local cuisine in an accessible format.

**La Paillote** on Boulevard de l'Independance serves well-prepared seafood and grilled meats with a view of the waterfront. Popular with expats and visiting business travelers.

For more affordable eating, head to the area around Marche du Mont-Bouet, where street vendors sell brochettes (grilled meat skewers), fried plantains, and poulet nyembwe. The food is good, the portions are generous, and a full meal costs a fraction of what you would pay in the sit-down restaurants.

Lebanese restaurants are scattered around the city and offer some of the best value for a proper sit-down meal, with shawarma, grills, and mezze widely available.
