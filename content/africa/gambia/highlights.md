**Birdwatching at Abuko Nature Reserve**

Just 25 minutes from the coast, Abuko is a small but remarkably rich reserve that serves as the perfect introduction to Gambian birdlife. Over 270 species have been recorded in this pocket of gallery forest and savanna. You will see everything from kingfishers and bee-eaters to raptors and weavers, often at close range. Crocodiles and monkeys are a bonus. A local guide at the gate costs very little and will spot birds you would walk straight past.

**Kunta Kinteh Island and Juffure**

A river trip to [Juffure](/africa/gambia/juffure) and Kunta Kinteh Island (formerly James Island) is the most historically significant excursion in The Gambia. The island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was a key staging post in the transatlantic slave trade. The ruins are small but the setting is powerful. In Juffure, you can visit the village that Alex Haley identified as his ancestral home in *Roots*. The boat trip itself, through mangrove-lined creeks, is beautiful.

**A river cruise upstream**

The Gambia River is the country's spine, and a cruise upstream reveals a side of the country most visitors never see. Hippos surface in the shallows, crocodiles bask on sandbanks, and the birdlife along the mangrove creeks is extraordinary. The popular 'Birds and Breakfast' dawn cruises from the coast are a good introduction, but for a deeper experience, take an overnight trip further upriver toward Janjanbureh (Georgetown).

**The Atlantic beaches**

The stretch of coast from [Bakau](/africa/gambia/bakau) through Fajara, Kotu, and Kololi offers wide, sandy beaches with warm water year-round. Kotu Beach is quieter and popular with birdwatchers thanks to the adjacent Kotu Stream, while Kololi (the Senegambia strip) has more bars and restaurants. The beaches are not pristine -- erosion is an ongoing problem and hawkers can be persistent -- but the sunsets are magnificent and the swimming is good.

**Makasutu Culture Forest**

This privately managed forest reserve on a tributary of the Gambia River combines nature walks, river kayaking, and cultural experiences. The sacred forest is home to monkeys, monitor lizards, and an impressive array of birds. It is well run and offers a concentrated taste of what makes The Gambia special -- wildlife, river, and warm hospitality in one place.
