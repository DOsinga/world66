Accra is a sprawling, energetic capital of around four million people, spread along the southern coast. It is chaotic, noisy, and fascinating -- a city where modern skyscrapers and air-conditioned malls sit alongside open sewers and crowded market stalls. Love it or endure it, you will almost certainly pass through.

Start at the **National Museum**, which has one of the best collections in West Africa and deserves at least two hours. Nearby is a good craft market for paintings, prints, and postcards. **Black Star Square** (Independence Square) is the vast ceremonial plaza where Ghana celebrated its independence in 1957 -- the Kwame Nkrumah Mausoleum and memorial park are nearby. **Makola Market** is the city's beating commercial heart, a dense maze of stalls selling everything imaginable.

The **Jamestown** and **Usshertown** neighbourhoods are the oldest parts of Accra, full of colonial-era buildings, fishing communities, and increasingly, street art and creative spaces. The **W.E.B. Du Bois Center** honours the American civil rights leader who spent his last years in Accra. The **Art Centre** in the city centre is the best place for souvenirs and cultural events.

Osu, along Oxford Street, is the main eating and nightlife district, with restaurants, bars, and shops catering to every budget. The Accra Mall and other modern shopping centres are in the east of the city.
