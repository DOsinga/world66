The capital of Ghana Accra is a pleasant city (pop. 1 100 000) on the southern coast. Start your sightseeing at the Perpetual Flame at the Cenotaph in Revolution Square then go on to the National Museum (one of the best collections in West Africa—allow at least two hours). Next to the museum is a good craft market which sells paintings prints and postcards. The city’s main sites are the Makola Market (the city’s central market) Black Star Square (memorial to three Ghanaians who were shot during colonial times) the tropical gardens the Art Centre (for shopping and cultural events) and the grave of W. E. B. Du Bois (the American-born civil rights leader who spent his last years in Accra).
