---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

**National Museum** is one of the best collections in West Africa -- allow at least two hours. The archaeology and ethnography galleries are the highlights, covering Ghanaian history from prehistoric times through the Ashanti kingdom to independence.

**Kwame Nkrumah Mausoleum and Memorial Park** honours Ghana's first president. The mausoleum sits in lush gardens on the spot where Nkrumah declared independence in 1957. A small museum behind it displays personal belongings, photographs, and publications.

**Black Star Square** (Independence Square) is the vast ceremonial plaza where independence was celebrated. The black star atop the Independence Arch is Ghana's national symbol. The square is most impressive during national events, but worth seeing any time.

**Jamestown Lighthouse** sits in the heart of the old Ga quarter, surrounded by colourful fishing boats and colonial-era buildings. Climb to the top for views over the harbour and the neighbourhood's vibrant street life.

**W.E.B. Du Bois Centre** is the former home and final resting place of the American civil rights leader, who became a Ghanaian citizen and lived in Accra until his death in 1963. A small but thoughtful museum.

**The Art Centre** on 28th February Road is the main craft market and cultural events venue. Good for souvenirs, paintings, and woodcarvings -- be prepared to bargain.
