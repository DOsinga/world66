Built by the Danes in 1661 as Christiansborg, this whitewashed seafront castle was a slave-trading post, a colonial governor's residence, and after independence the seat of Ghana's government for half a century. The presidency moved out in 2013 and the castle is now occasionally open for guided tours; check ahead before turning up. Comparable in history, if not in setting, to the larger castles at [Elmina](/africa/ghana/elmina) and [Cape Coast](/africa/ghana/capecoast).
