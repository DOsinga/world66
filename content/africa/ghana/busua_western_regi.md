Busua is a small fishing village on Ghana's western coast, about 30 kilometres from Takoradi. It has one of the best beaches in the country — a long sweep of golden sand sheltered by a rocky headland, with waves strong enough for surfing but calm enough for swimming closer to shore.

The village itself is relaxed and unhurried. A handful of guesthouses and beach bars line the sand, and fishermen still launch painted wooden boats through the surf each morning. From Busua you can walk west along the coast to Butre, another quiet village with a ruined Dutch fort on the hill above it, or take a boat to the tiny offshore island of Princess Town.

If you are looking for a beach break away from Accra's bustle, Busua is one of the best options in Ghana — genuine, affordable, and far enough off the main tourist trail to feel like a discovery.
