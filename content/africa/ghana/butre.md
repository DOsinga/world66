Butre is a small fishing village on Ghana's Western Region coast, tucked between forest hills and the Atlantic. It's quiet, genuinely off the beaten track, and rewards travelers who make the detour from the Cape Three Points road.

The village sits on the west bank of the Butre River, and to reach the beach and the guesthouse you cross the river by dugout canoe — one of those small moments that makes the place feel pleasantly remote. On the hill above the village stands Fort Batenstein, a Dutch slave fort built in 1652 and now in atmospheric ruins. The views over the coastline from up there are worth the climb.

Butre beach itself is beautiful and usually uncrowded. Ellis Hideout, the guesthouse on the beach side of the river, is the main place to stay and eat. It's a relaxed spot, popular with travelers doing the coastal circuit between [Takoradi](/africa/ghana/takoradi) and [Dixcove](/africa/ghana/dixcove).
