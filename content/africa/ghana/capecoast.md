Cape Coast is the historical heart of Ghana and the place most visitors find hardest to forget. The town is dominated by Cape Coast Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that served as one of the main holding points for enslaved Africans before they were shipped across the Atlantic. A guided tour through the dungeons and out through the "Door of No Return" is devastating and essential.

The town itself is a busy, compact place with a lively fishing harbour, a university, and good restaurants and bars catering to both locals and visitors. It is smaller and more manageable than Accra, and many travelers prefer to base themselves here while exploring the coast.

Kakum National Park, with its famous canopy walkway, is just a 30-minute drive north. [Elmina](/africa/ghana/elmina) and its castle are 15 km to the west. You could spend several days in Cape Coast without running out of things to see.
