Dixcove is a small fishing village on Ghana's southwest coast, about 30 km west of Takoradi. It sits on a rocky headland around a natural harbour, with fishing canoes pulled up on the beach and the smell of the sea everywhere. It's a quiet, unpretentious place — not a resort, just a working village that happens to have one of the most impressive forts on the coast and easy access to one of Ghana's best beaches.

Fort Metal Cross dominates the headland above the harbour. Built by the English in 1691, it served as a trading post and later as a transit point in the slave trade. You can walk up to it, look out over the bay, and buy fresh fish from the market below — the contrast is uncomfortable and worth sitting with. The fort is in reasonable condition and gives a good sense of how these coastal fortifications worked.

Busua Beach is a 10-minute walk or short taxi ride from Dixcove, through a shaded bush path. The beach itself is wide, white-sanded, and backed by palms — genuinely beautiful. The sea is calmer here than on many stretches of the Gulf of Guinea coast, making it one of the better spots for swimming in the region.

The harbour market is worth a wander. Fresh shark, swordfish, and lobster come off the boats, and prices are low if you bargain directly with the fishermen.
