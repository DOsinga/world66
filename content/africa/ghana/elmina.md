Elmina is a small, colourful fishing town 15 km west of [Cape Coast](/africa/ghana/capecoast), and home to St George's Castle -- the oldest European building in sub-Saharan Africa, built by the Portuguese in 1482. Like Cape Coast Castle, it was used in the transatlantic slave trade, and tours of the dungeons and trading halls are deeply moving.

The town is worth exploring beyond the castle. The fishing harbour is one of the most photogenic sights in Ghana -- hundreds of brightly painted wooden boats crowded into the lagoon below Fort St Jago, which sits on the hill above. The daily fish market is noisy and colourful. Elmina is a good base for visiting both castles and Kakum National Park, and it is quieter and more relaxed than Cape Coast itself.

The Bakatue festival, held in early July, marks the beginning of the fishing season with canoe races, drumming, and celebrations along the lagoon.
