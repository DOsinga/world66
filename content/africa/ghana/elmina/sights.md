---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

**St George's Castle (Elmina Castle)** is the oldest European building in sub-Saharan Africa, built by the Portuguese in 1482 and later expanded by the Dutch. Originally a gold trading post, it became a major holding point for enslaved Africans. The guided tour through the dungeons, the auction hall, and the "Door of No Return" is harrowing and essential. The castle grounds also include a chapel, living quarters, and an extensive courtyard. Allow at least 90 minutes.

**Fort St Jago** sits on the hill above the castle. The Dutch built it in 1666 after capturing Elmina by bombarding the castle from this very hilltop -- they made sure no one could do the same to them. It was used purely as a military garrison, with quarters for officers and soldiers on rotation from the castle below. The views over the town, lagoon, and coastline are excellent.

**The Dutch Cemetery** in the old town is a quiet, overgrown reminder of the European presence. Many of the tombstones date from the 17th and 18th centuries.

**The fishing harbour** below the castle is one of the most photogenic spots in Ghana -- hundreds of brightly painted wooden pirogues crowded into the Benya lagoon, with the castle rising behind them. Best seen in the early morning when the boats come in.
