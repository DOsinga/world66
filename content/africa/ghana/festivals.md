---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Ghana celebrates dozens of traditional festivals throughout the year, and catching one is one of the best reasons to visit. These are not tourist performances -- they are living cultural events with deep roots, and you are welcome to attend and watch.

**Homowo** is the harvest festival of the Ga people in [Accra](/africa/ghana/accra), held in August and September. The name means "hooting at hunger" and commemorates a famine overcome. Chiefs and elders parade through the streets, kpokpoi (a fermented corn meal) is sprinkled for the ancestors, and the whole city celebrates. It is Accra's biggest traditional event.

**Aboakyere** is the deer-hunting festival in [Winneba](/africa/ghana/winneba), held on the first Saturday in May. Two rival companies of warriors compete to catch a live bushbuck with their bare hands and bring it back to the chief. It is dramatic, noisy, and unlike anything else you will see in West Africa.

**Damba** is the major festival of the Dagomba and other northern peoples, celebrated in [Tamale](/africa/ghana/tamale) and towns across the three northern regions in July and August. It marks the birth of the Prophet Mohammed with drumming, dancing, horse riding, and elaborate chief processions. The fire festival (Bugum) precedes it.

**Chale Wote** is a contemporary street art festival held in the Jamestown neighbourhood of Accra every August. It features murals, installations, live music, performance art, and fashion -- a showcase for Ghana's thriving creative scene.

**Panafest** (Pan-African Historical Theatre Festival) and **Emancipation Day** are held biennially in [Cape Coast](/africa/ghana/capecoast) and [Elmina](/africa/ghana/elmina), drawing visitors from across the African diaspora for performances, rituals, and remembrance at the slave castles. **Akwasidae** is not a single festival but a recurring ceremony held every six weeks at the Manhyia Palace in [Kumasi](/africa/ghana/kumasi), when the Ashanti king receives homage from his sub-chiefs. It is open to visitors and gives a vivid sense of the living Ashanti chieftaincy tradition. Check locally for the next date.
