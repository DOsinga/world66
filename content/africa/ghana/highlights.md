---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Ghana rewards travelers who are willing to move beyond the beach. The country's highlights span history, nature, and culture in ways that few West African destinations can match.

**Cape Coast Castle and Elmina Castle** are the most visited sites in the country, and for good reason. These UNESCO World Heritage slave forts are devastating and essential. Walking through the dungeons where enslaved people were held before being shipped across the Atlantic, then stepping through the "Door of No Return" to the crashing ocean beyond, is an experience that stays with you. Visit both -- they are only 15 km apart and each tells a slightly different story.

**Kakum National Park** sits just north of Cape Coast and is famous for its canopy walkway -- a series of seven suspension bridges strung 30 metres above the rainforest floor. The walk is thrilling and the birdlife from up there is remarkable, with over 300 species recorded in the park. Go early in the morning for the best wildlife sightings and fewer crowds.

**Mole National Park** in the north is Ghana's premier wildlife destination. Unlike most African parks, Mole offers walking safaris led by armed rangers, which means you can get genuinely close to elephants, baboons, antelope, and warthogs on foot. The dry season between December and March is the best time to visit, when animals congregate around waterholes.

**Kumasi and the Ashanti Kingdom** offer a deep dive into one of West Africa's most powerful cultural traditions. The Manhyia Palace Museum tells the story of the Ashanti kings, and Kejetia Market -- one of the largest open-air markets in West Africa -- is a sensory overload of colour, noise, and commerce. Nearby Bonwire village is where authentic kente cloth is woven on hand looms.

**The Volta Region** in the east is Ghana's most scenic area: lush green hills, waterfalls, and quiet villages. Wli Waterfalls, the highest in West Africa, and Mount Afadjato, Ghana's tallest peak, are both accessible as day hikes. The region also has the stilt village of Tafi Atome, where sacred mona monkeys roam freely.

**Lake Volta**, the world's largest man-made lake by surface area, dominates the eastern half of the country. A slow ferry ride from Akosombo to Yeji takes about 24 hours and passes through beautiful, remote scenery -- one of Ghana's great off-the-beaten-track experiences.
