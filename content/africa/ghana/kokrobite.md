Kokrobite is the closest beach escape from [Accra](/africa/ghana/accra), about 30 km west of the capital along the coast road. It is a fishing village that has quietly become the place Accra residents and backpackers come to decompress — wide sandy beach, cold beer, and drumming into the night.

The Academy of African Music and Arts (AAMA), founded by master drummer Mustapha Tettey Addy, is the cultural anchor of the village. It runs drumming and dance workshops for all levels, and performances happen regularly. Big Milly's Backyard, right on the beach, has long been the social hub — a sprawling compound with simple rooms, a bar, and a fire pit that tends to attract an interesting mix of travellers. The beach itself is wide and pleasant, but the currents can be strong, so swim with care.

Kokrobite works as a day trip or a relaxed overnight. Tro-tros run from Kaneshie station in Accra and drop you at the village junction, a short walk from the beach.
