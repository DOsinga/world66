Kumasi is the heart of the Ashanti kingdom and Ghana's second city. It is sprawling, busy, and culturally rich -- a place where traditional chieftaincy, gold, and kente cloth are not museum exhibits but part of everyday life.

The **Manhyia Palace Museum** tells the story of the Ashanti kings and is the main tourist attraction. The current Asantehene (king) still holds court here, and the Akwasidae ceremony held every six weeks is a spectacular display of traditional authority. **Kejetia Market** -- one of the largest open-air markets in West Africa -- is a chaotic, colourful sprawl where you can buy everything from yams to car parts. The **Prempeh II Jubilee Museum** and the old British fort are also worth visiting.

The village of **Bonwire**, about 30 minutes from the city, is where authentic kente cloth is woven on hand looms. You can watch the weavers at work and buy directly. **Lake Bosumtwi**, a crater lake 30 km southeast, is sacred to the Ashanti and a good spot for a quiet swim.

Kumasi is also an excellent transport hub. It sits at the junction of routes to [Accra](/africa/ghana/accra), [Tamale](/africa/ghana/tamale), [Cape Coast](/africa/ghana/capecoast), and the Brong Ahafo Region. STC and VIP buses connect to all major cities.
