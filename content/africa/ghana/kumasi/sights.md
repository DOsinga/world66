---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

**Manhyia Palace Museum** is the main tourist attraction in Kumasi. The old palace of the Ashanti kings has been turned into a museum stuffed with historical objects, paintings, and photographs of the Ashanti rulers. A guided tour gives a fascinating insight into the power and traditions of the Ashanti kingdom. The current Asantehene still holds court in the adjacent modern palace.

**Kejetia Market** is one of the largest open-air markets in West Africa -- a sprawling maze of stalls selling everything from yams and textiles to car parts and herbal medicines. It is chaotic, colourful, and worth at least an hour of wandering. Keep your valuables secure.

**Prempeh II Jubilee Museum** near the palace houses Ashanti royal regalia, war trophies, and cultural artefacts. Small but worth a quick visit.

**Fort Kumasi** was built by the British after the 1896 conquest and now houses a military museum. The Ashanti had burned down the previous British fort during the wars of resistance.

**Bonwire** village, about 30 minutes from Kumasi, is the home of kente cloth weaving. Watch weavers working on hand looms and buy authentic cloth directly from the artisans.

**Lake Bosumtwi**, a crater lake about 30 km southeast of Kumasi, is sacred to the Ashanti people. It is a peaceful spot for swimming, surrounded by green hills. Traditionally, fishing here is done only with wooden planks, not boats -- though that rule has relaxed somewhat.
