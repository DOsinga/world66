Paga is a small town in Ghana's Upper East Region, right on the border with Burkina Faso. It is famous across Ghana for its sacred crocodile ponds, where large Nile crocodiles live in shallow pools and are considered sacred by the local community. For a small fee, a guide will summon a crocodile from the water with a live chicken — visitors can sit on its back for photographs. The tradition dates back centuries and the crocodiles, well-fed and habituated to people, are remarkably docile.

Across from the pond, a small museum demonstrates traditional northern Ghanaian building techniques — the round, flat-roofed mud compounds characteristic of the region — and sells local crafts. Paga is also known for its distinctive Navrongo-style painted houses, with geometric patterns adorning the exterior walls. The border crossing into Burkina Faso is within walking distance.
