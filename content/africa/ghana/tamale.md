Call it "tah-mah-LAY," not "ta-mal-EY" -- the latter is a Mexican food. Tamale is the capital of the Northern Region, a hot, dusty, and predominantly Muslim city that feels like a different country from the lush south. It is the main transport hub for northern Ghana and the gateway to Mole National Park, about 150 km to the west.

The city itself is not a major tourist destination, but it has a good market, interesting mosques, and a lively atmosphere during the Damba and fire (Bugum) festivals. Nearby [Larabanga](/africa/ghana/larabanja) has one of the oldest mosques in West Africa. If you are heading to Mole, you will almost certainly pass through Tamale -- stock up on supplies and catch your onward transport here.

Domestic flights from [Accra](/africa/ghana/accra) to Tamale save a gruelling 12-hour bus journey and are worth considering.
