---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Ghana is a tropical country and it is hot year-round. The main factor in choosing when to visit is the rain.

**The dry season (November to March)** is the most popular time to travel. Humidity drops, skies are clearer, and roads are in better condition. December and January bring the Harmattan, a dry wind from the Sahara that carries dust and haze -- it makes the air feel cooler but visibility can be poor, especially in the north. Temperatures inland regularly hit 35°C and higher.

**The wet season (April to October)** brings heavy but usually short afternoon downpours. The south gets the most rain, with a peak around June. The north has a single rainy season from May to September. Travel is still perfectly possible during the rains -- roads can be muddy on dirt tracks, but main routes stay open. The landscape is lush and green, and tourist sites are less crowded.

**For wildlife**, the dry season is best. Animals in Mole National Park gather around waterholes between December and March, making them much easier to spot. The park is accessible year-round but walking safaris in the wet season can be challenging.

**For beaches**, the dry season is more comfortable, though the coast stays warm and swimmable all year. Sea conditions can be rough during the rainy months.

**For festivals**, the calendar is packed. The Homowo festival in Accra happens in August and September. The Chale Wote street art festival in Jamestown, Accra, takes place in August. Aboakyere, the deer-hunting festival in Winneba, falls in May. The Damba festival in Tamale and the northern regions is celebrated in July and August.

**Budget travelers** will find better deals during the wet season, when flights and accommodation are cheaper and there is less competition for services.
