Bafata is Guinea-Bissau's second-largest town, though that says more about the country's modest urban landscape than about the size of Bafata itself. It sits on the Geba River along the main road from [Bissau](/africa/guineabissau/bissau) to the Guinea (Conakry) border, and most travelers pass through rather than linger.

There is not much to see or do here, but the town has a pleasant enough atmosphere and a lively market. Bafata was the birthplace of Amilcar Cabral, the independence leader, and his legacy is felt here. A few basic places to eat and sleep are available, but keep your expectations modest. If you are heading overland to or from Guinea, you will likely stop here for a meal or to break the journey.

Be aware that landmines from past conflicts remain a risk in the Bafata region. Stick to well-traveled roads and paths.
