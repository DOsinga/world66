---
title: 'Festivals'
type: section
---

**Carnival** is the big one. Held every February in [Bissau](/africa/guineabissau/bissau), it is the most important cultural event in the country and one of the most impressive carnivals in Africa. Every ethnic group in Guinea-Bissau sends representatives who parade through the streets in elaborate costumes and masks -- sacred masks, animal masks, warrior costumes in crocodile skin. Drums and balafons set the rhythm, and the dancing goes on for days. Each performance tells a story: harvest celebrations, rites of passage, historical events. It is a genuinely African carnival, mixing animist, Muslim, and Christian traditions in a way that is unique to Guinea-Bissau.

The carnival also extends to the [Bijagos Islands](/africa/guineabissau/bijagosislands), where the celebrations take on a more traditional character, with Bijago initiation dances and ceremonies that outsiders rarely see at other times of year.

**Independence Day** on September 24 commemorates the declaration of independence from Portugal in 1973. It is marked with official ceremonies, speeches, and cultural performances, particularly in Bissau.

Traditional **initiation ceremonies** among various ethnic groups happen throughout the year, though they are not public events for tourists. On the Bijagos Islands, the fanado -- a coming-of-age ritual for young men -- is a significant event that involves weeks of seclusion and ceremony. If you happen to be on the islands during such a period, you may notice the village atmosphere change, with certain areas becoming off-limits. Respect these boundaries.

The **cashew harvest** (March to June) is not a festival exactly, but it shapes the country's social calendar. Rural communities gather for the harvest, and cashew wine flows freely. It is a good time to experience village life.
