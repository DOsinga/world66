---
title: 'Getting Around'
type: section
---

Getting around Guinea-Bissau requires flexibility and low expectations. There is no rail network, domestic flights are rare, and the road system is poor.

**Bush taxis** are the main form of transport between towns. They are shared minibuses or sept-places (seven-seat Peugeots) that leave when full. The main routes from [Bissau](/africa/guineabissau/bissau) to [Bafata](/africa/guineabissau/bafata) and the Senegalese border are served regularly. Elsewhere, waits can be long.

**Roads** are paved between Bissau and a few major towns, but much of the country's road network is unpaved. In the rainy season (June to October), many roads become impassable -- even the paved ones can be rough. If you are driving, a 4x4 is essential outside Bissau.

**Taxis** operate within Bissau. They are cheap and identifiable by their blue color. Agree on a fare before getting in.

**Boats** are the only way to reach the [Bijagos Islands](/africa/guineabissau/bijagosislands). A weekly ferry and occasional fast boats depart from Bissau port. Within the archipelago, motorized canoes are the standard transport between islands.

**Car hire** is possible in Bissau but expensive and rarely worth it unless you are an experienced West African driver. Hiring a car with a driver is a better option for exploring beyond the capital.

There is no Uber or ride-hailing service. Distances are short -- the whole country is only about 350 kilometers across -- but travel times are long relative to distance because of road conditions.
