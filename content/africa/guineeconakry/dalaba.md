Dalaba is one of the best bases for hiking in the Fouta Djallon and one of the most pleasant towns in Guinea. Set in the highlands at around 1,200 meters, it is noticeably cooler than the coast -- a relief after the sweltering heat of [Conakry](/africa/guineeconakry/conakry).

The town itself is small and quiet. The **Centre d'Accueil**, built by the French in the colonial era, sits on the outskirts and can provide information on hiking routes and guides. The nearby **Ditinn Falls** drop 80 meters and are one of the most impressive waterfalls in the Fouta Djallon, best visited during or just after the rainy season when the water is at full force.

From Dalaba, you can arrange multi-day treks through the surrounding highlands, passing Fulani villages, terraced hillsides, and river gorges. A local guide is strongly recommended -- trails are unmarked and navigation without one is difficult.
