The Iles de Los are a small archipelago just south of [Conakry](/africa/guineeconakry/conakry), reachable by pirogue in about 30-45 minutes. The three main islands -- Tamara, Kassa, and Roume -- offer a welcome escape from the capital's noise and traffic.

**Ile de Kassa** is the most popular with Guineans, especially on weekends, and has a lively atmosphere with beach bars and grilled fish. **Ile de Roume** is quieter and favored by expats looking for peace. It has a more interesting history too -- the island was used during the slave trade era and is said to have inspired Robert Louis Stevenson.

Boats leave from the beach near the Novotel in Conakry. You can hire a private pirogue or take one of the regular boats that run to Soro village on Kassa. Agree on your fare and return time before departing -- being stranded overnight without planning for it is not ideal. Bring food, water, and sun protection, as facilities on the islands are basic.
