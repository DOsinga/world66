Nzerekore is the largest city in Guinea's forested southeast, a region that feels quite different from the rest of the country. The landscape here is dense tropical forest and rolling hills, and the ethnic groups -- Manon, Kono, and Guerze -- have distinct cultures from the Malinke and Fulani of the north.

The town itself is a busy market center with a frontier feel. The main reason to come is access to **Mount Nimba**, the 1,752-meter peak on the border with Liberia and Ivory Coast. Mount Nimba is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of West Africa's most important ecological zones, home to endemic species including the viviparous Nimba toad and wild chimpanzees.

Getting to Nzerekore from [Conakry](/africa/guineeconakry/conakry) is a serious undertaking -- the road is long and rough, especially south of Kissidougou. But the forest region rewards those who make the effort with some of the most spectacular and least-visited wilderness in West Africa.
