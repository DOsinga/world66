---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Abidjan"
type: section
---

Abidjan is often endearingly referred to as the ‘’Paris of West
Africa” for its stately sixties lifestyle and a classy cultural
ambiance. The **Abidjan Budget Travel** guide offers visitors super budget ideas to make the most of their trip to this Ivory Coast charmer.

**Moving Around**

The smartest way to move around in Abidjan on a budget is to hire a
orange taxi and fix on a price beforehand as the drivers are sometimes
known to play truant and overcharge tourists. The best way to learn the
normal rates is by checking with your hotel staff or the friendly
locals. A trip from downtown Plateau should set you back by CFA 3000.
For visitors on an extremely terse budget, there are the petite white
vans that run through different localities and neighborhoods and cost
no more than CFA 200-300. There are other large fixed route public
transport buses that will take you through the town at dirt cheap
prices, however they are the favorite pick pocketing zones and require
visitors to be highly vigilant.

**Sightseeing**

Abidjan is not exactly a storehouse of the most attractive tourist
destinations, though the city zoo is a nice and cheap place to visit.
The park is surprisingly well-maintained and at CFA 200 is a good value
for money visit. Tourists can also visit Bassam, the most popular beach
in town, which is a great place to just lie back and do nothing but
soak in the natural glory of the sun-smacked sands and warm sea water.

**Food and Accommodation**

The best way to save money on meals is to eat at the sidewalks or
road-side terraces of Abidjan that serve delicious Ivorian food. To
avoid haggling after post meal overcharges, confirm the prices before
meals are ordered and consumed. The cheapest food in the region is the
staple rice, yam and cassava, with bread acting as an accompaniment.
Seafood is also very cheap in the town and several lip-smacking
variations are available at humble prices.

For budget accommodation, Hotel Banfoura located in Adjame next to
the bus stand is a popular choice. The property offers neat rooms with
a good city view at $ 10/ per night. The staff is also friendly and the
service impeccable. Another smart budget pick is Hotel du Nord in
Adjame again. Visitors should avoid hotels in Yamoussoukroas as they
are notorious crime hubs, though there are a few good cheap options
sprinkled around the area as well.

Abidjan is an economy tourist’s haven and lets you discover the exotic glory of West Africa on a shoe string budget.
