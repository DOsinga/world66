---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Côte d'Ivoire has a continuous Atlantic coastline stretching nearly 500 km, and much of it is backed by palm-fringed sand. The sea can be rough — Atlantic rollers and strong currents are the norm, so swimming requires care.

The easiest beach day from [Abidjan](/africa/ivorycoast/abidjan) is to [Grand Bassam](/africa/ivorycoast/grandbassam), 40 km east — a UNESCO-listed colonial town with a long beach and good seafood restaurants. On weekends it gets busy with Abidjanais; on weekdays it's quiet. Push another 40 km east to Assinie for some of the best beaches in the country: long, white, and much less visited.

To the west, [San-Pédro](/africa/ivorycoast/sanpedro) is a working port town but has beaches nearby and is a gateway to the relatively undeveloped coastline around Sassandra. The further you go from Abidjan, the fewer people you will find on the sand.
