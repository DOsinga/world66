---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Kenya's coastline runs about 480km along the Indian Ocean, from the Tanzanian border in the south to the Somali border in the north. The water is warm year-round, the beaches are mostly white sand fringed with coral reefs, and the Swahili culture of the coastal towns gives the whole stretch a character entirely distinct from the safari interior.

**Diani Beach**, south of Mombasa, is the most developed stretch — 10km of white sand with a long string of resorts, beach bars, water sports operators, and kitesurfing outfitters. The reef is just offshore and snorkeling is straightforward from the beach. The Colobus Conservation centre here is worth visiting, protecting the endangered Angola colobus monkeys that live in the coastal forest.

**Watamu and Malindi**, 120km north of Mombasa, offer a slightly quieter scene. Watamu is a small village with excellent snorkeling in the Watamu Marine National Park, and whale shark sightings between October and March are reasonably reliable. Malindi has a longer history of Italian and European tourism and the facilities to match, plus the ruins of the Gedi Swahili settlement nearby — a 12th-century walled town abandoned mysteriously in the 17th century.

**Lamu Archipelago** is the highlight of the Kenyan coast for many travelers. The island of Lamu town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site: a medieval Swahili town of narrow alleys, carved wooden doors, and donkeys — no cars. Shela Beach, a 20-minute walk from town, is one of the finest beaches in East Africa, backed by sand dunes and fronted by very few people. Getting here requires a flight to Lamu airport or a long bus journey from Mombasa — neither discourages the right kind of visitor.

**Tiwi Beach**, just south of Diani, is quieter and cheaper than its neighbour, with a collection of self-catering cottages and a more relaxed vibe. Good for budget travelers who want the coast without the resort scene.

The best time for beaches is from **December to March** (dry, calm, hot) and **July to August** (cooler, drier). The long rains from April to June make the coast less appealing. Water temperature hovers between 24°C and 28°C year-round.
