Meru is a substantial market town on the northeastern slopes of Mount Kenya, home to the Meru people — close relatives of the Kikuyu and Embu, with their own distinct traditions and language.

Most travelers pass through rather than linger, but Meru rewards a stop. The town is famous throughout East Africa for *miraa* — the twigs of the *Catha edulis* shrub, chewed for their stimulant properties. Grown legally here in large quantities, the miraa trade is the economic heartbeat of the region. If you want to try it, you won't find it fresher anywhere in the world: chew the bark of the young twigs for an hour and you'll feel the effect of a couple of strong coffees.

The Meru Museum is worth an hour if you have time — it covers the natural and cultural history of the region and gives useful context for the broader area, including Meru National Park to the east.
