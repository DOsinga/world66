Nyeri is a highland town in central Kenya, tucked between the slopes of Mount Kenya and the forested ridges of the Aberdare Range. At around 1,750 meters, the climate is cool and green year-round, and the surrounding countryside is some of Kenya's most productive farmland — coffee and tea estates stretch in every direction.

The town serves as the capital of Nyeri County and a gateway to [Aberdare National Park](/africa/kenya/centralhighlands/nyeri/things_to_do), one of Kenya's most atmospheric reserves. Most visitors pass through on their way to the park or to Mount Kenya, but Nyeri itself has a quiet charm. The compact center along Gakere Road has everything a traveler needs, and the pace of life is unhurried.

Nyeri holds a small place in world history as the final home of Robert Baden-Powell, founder of the Scout movement, who retired here in 1938 and is buried in the grounds of St. Peter's Church. His cottage at the Outspan Hotel is now a small museum.

You can reach Nyeri by matatu from Nairobi (about three hours), Nakuru, Nanyuki, or Nyahururu. In Nairobi, the stage for Nyeri and most Central Province towns is on Accra Road near the "Tea Room" stage, just off Tom Mboya Street.
