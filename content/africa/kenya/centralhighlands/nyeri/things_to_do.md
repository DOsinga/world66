---
title: "Things to Do in Nyeri"
type: section
---

Nyeri's main draw is its proximity to two of Kenya's great natural attractions — the Aberdare Range and Mount Kenya — but the town itself has a few sights worth a stop.

**Aberdare National Park** is the big one. The park covers the eastern slopes of the Aberdare Range and is home to elephants, buffalo, leopards, and black rhinos. The landscape is dramatic: bamboo forest gives way to moorland and waterfalls at higher elevations. The park also contains the famous Treetops Lodge, where Princess Elizabeth learned she had become Queen in 1952.

**Baden-Powell's Grave** at St. Peter's Church is a pilgrimage site for Scouts from around the world. The grave of the movement's founder and his wife sits in a quiet churchyard with views toward Mount Kenya. His cottage at the nearby Outspan Hotel has been preserved as a small museum.

**Gakere Road** is the main commercial street running from the bus park into the center of town. It is where you will find restaurants, supermarkets, bookshops, and internet cafes — and it gives a good feel for everyday life in a Kenyan highland town.
