The Festival of the Dhow Countries
is now the largest annual cultural event in East Africa, and among the eight
major festivals in sub-Saharan Africa.
It is scheduled annually around the first two weeks in July. The
festival celebrates the arts and cultures of the African Continent, India,
Pakistan, Gulf States, Iran and the Indian Ocean islands. It features an
international film and video competition, music, theatre and performing arts,
workshops, seminars, conferences and other related arts and cultural
programmes. The festival programme is centred in a variety of magnificent
venues near the seafront in the historic Stone Town; with a Village Panorama
that extends the festival to rural communities; Women’s Panorama, which
provides a focus for women’s issues; Children’s Panorama, which provides for
the participation of children and youths.
