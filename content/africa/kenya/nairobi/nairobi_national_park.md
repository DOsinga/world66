Nairobi National Park is the only major wildlife reserve inside a capital city anywhere in the world. Just 7 km from the city centre, its 117 square kilometres of open grassland, forest, and riverine habitat support over 100 mammal species, including lion, leopard, cheetah, buffalo, and both black and white rhino — four of the Big Five in all. The Nairobi skyline is visible from the plains.

The park is best visited in the early morning when animals are most active and the light is best for photography. A half-day loop by vehicle takes in most of the main viewing areas. Self-drive is permitted with your own 4WD; the park also has several tour operators running shared-vehicle morning drives from city hotels.

The main entrance gate is on Langata Road, about 20 minutes by taxi from downtown. Entrance fees for foreign visitors are set by Kenya Wildlife Service and should be checked in advance — they are significantly higher than for Kenyan nationals.
