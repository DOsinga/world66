Amani ya Juu (meaning “higher peace” in Swahili) is a Fair Trade, Non-Profit Organization in Nairobi, Kenya. Amani is a project started in 1996 with the purpose of helping marginalized women find a higher peace that transcends ethnic differences and improve their living conditions by a steady, reasonable stream of income generated from the women's sewing projects. Today, Amani itself portrays a unique picture of diversity with over 80 women coming from Rwanda, Burundi, Congo, Uganda, Sudan, Kenya, Ethiopia, Somalia as well as other African countries.

Amani ya Juu is located in the neighborhood of Riverside on Riverside Mews (just off Riverside Drive). It is situated in a lush garden featuring aloe vera plants, bird of paradise, bougainvillea, bottle brush trees, double hibiscus. In addition to the shop and main production workshop and warehouse (located in the beautiful pamoja house), there is a cafe with muffins, salads, soup, and sandwiches. The cafe has, of course, wonderful coffee, espresso, and tea. Another factor that adds to the charm of Amani is that it has a playground. Many mothers and caretakers bring their young children to this peaceful location because it is one of the few playgrounds Nairobi has to offer.
