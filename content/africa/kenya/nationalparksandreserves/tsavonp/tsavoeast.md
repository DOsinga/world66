The vast northern two-thirds of Tsavo East is closed to the public while the Kenyan Government works to eliminate poaching in the region. There is still much to see south of the Galana River, however. The **Kanderi Swamp** draws large numbers of elephants and buffalo, especially in the dry season, while the **Aruba Dam** is a reliable spot for hippos, crocodiles, and waterbirds. The flat, semi-arid plains here are classic safari country -- big skies, red earth, and large herds visible across long distances.

There are campsites near most gates to Tsavo East, as well as one close to the Kanderi Swamp. Several lodges and tented camps operate inside the park, with Voi Safari Lodge just east of Voi being one of the most established.
