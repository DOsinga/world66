Most of the sights in Tsavo West centre around the watering holes near the Kilaguni and Ngulia lodges. **Mzima Springs**, next to Kilaguni Lodge, has plenty of hippos and crocodiles and even an underwater viewing chamber where you can watch the aquatic life in the crystal-clear spring water. North of the lodges lies the **Shetani lava flow and caves**, a dramatic black volcanic landscape that looks like it belongs on another planet. The **Chaimu Crater** just south of Kilaguni Lodge is worth the climb for the views, and not far from Ngulia Lodge you will find the **Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary**, where up to 30 black rhinos roam inside a 70 square kilometre fenced area.

Tsavo West has several campsites near its three main gates and some affordable self-catering bandas near the lodges at Ngulia Safari Camp and Kitani Safari Camp. For more comfort, the Kilaguni Lodge and Ngulia Lodge both overlook watering holes that attract wildlife every night -- sitting on the terrace with a drink while elephants come in to drink is one of the great Tsavo experiences.
