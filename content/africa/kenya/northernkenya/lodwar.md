Lodwar is the main town in Turkana County and the gateway to Lake Turkana from the west. It's a small, poor, dusty place — temperatures regularly exceed 40°C — but it's the essential overnight stop if you're heading to the lake. Expect persistent attention from vendors and children; it comes with the territory.

From Lodwar, the lake is about 70 kilometres to the east. The shoreline around Ferguson's Gulf offers camping and the chance to see the extraordinary jade-green water up close. The lake is the world's largest permanent desert lake and one of the oldest in Africa; the flamingos and Nile crocodiles around the southern shore are just the visible tip of its biodiversity.

Getting here from Kitale involves a long, often rough road. A 4WD is advisable in wet conditions. The road from Lodwar to the lake is passable in a standard vehicle when dry.
