title: "Safaris"

---

A Kenya safari is one of the great travel experiences, and the country has the infrastructure to do it well across a wide range of budgets. The [National Parks and Reserves](/africa/kenya/nationalparksandreserves) provide all the framework, and the concentration of wildlife in the major reserves is hard to match anywhere.

**Maasai Mara National Reserve** is the flagship. The rolling grasslands of the southwest hold year-round populations of lions, elephants, cheetahs, leopards, and hippos. From July to October, the Great Migration sweeps through from the Serengeti — over a million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles making their annual circular journey, with river crossings that attract the best wildlife photographers in the world.

**Amboseli National Park**, near the Tanzanian border, is famous for its large elephant herds and unobstructed views of Mt Kilimanjaro across the border on clear mornings. It can be muddy in the wet season but is spectacular when dry.

**Tsavo** — split into East and West by the main Nairobi–Mombasa road — is Kenya's largest park. It's wilder and less visited than the Mara, with red-dust-stained elephants, lions, and the Yatta Plateau. Good for those wanting a more remote feel.

**Samburu National Reserve** in the north holds species unique to arid northeast Africa: reticulated giraffe, Grevy's zebra, Somali ostrich, and gerenuk. A worthwhile addition to any safari, particularly combined with a Mara trip.

**Nairobi National Park** is the world's only national park within a capital city. Lions and rhinos roam the grasslands with the city skyline visible in the background. Easy to visit on a half-day from Nairobi.

Safari logistics range from budget camping (sharing a vehicle with strangers, cooking your own food) to private fly-in camps with drinks included and a guide to yourself. For most travelers, a mid-range tented camp with a shared vehicle and knowledgeable driver-guide is the sweet spot. Book through a licensed operator — the Kenya Association of Tour Operators (KATO) maintains a list of reputable members.
