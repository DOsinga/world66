Kilifi is a small town on the Kenyan coast about halfway between Mombasa and Malindi, set around Kilifi Creek where the inlet creates a natural harbour and a pleasant waterfront. It is quieter than either Mombasa or Malindi and has a more relaxed, community atmosphere.

The main sight is the **Mnarani ruins** on the south bank of the creek — the remains of a 14th-century Swahili town including mosques and pillar tombs, with good views over the creek. Kilifi's beach is pleasant, though getting to it requires some local knowledge. The creek itself is good for boat trips and dhow sailing. The region has a significant presence of marine research scientists, drawn by the coral ecosystems offshore.

Getting there from Mombasa: about 60 km on the A7 coast road; matatus and buses run regularly.
