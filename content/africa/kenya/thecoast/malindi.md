Malindi is a coastal town about 120 km north of Mombasa on Kenya's Indian Ocean coast, and one of the oldest trading settlements in East Africa — Vasco da Gama stopped here in 1498 and erected a pillar that still stands. It is popular with Italian and European visitors for its beaches, marine park, and water sports.

The **Malindi Marine National Park** protects some of the best coral reefs on the East African coast, with clear water and abundant fish life. Snorkelling, diving, deep-sea fishing, and glass-bottomed boat trips are all available from the beachfront. The **Gedi Ruins**, about 12 km south near Watamu, are the remains of a 13th-century Swahili trading town abandoned mysteriously in the 17th century — one of the most evocative archaeological sites in Kenya.

Getting there from Mombasa: matatus (minibuses) run frequently from Abdel Nassy Road and the journey takes about two hours.
