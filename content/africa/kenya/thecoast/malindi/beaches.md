Malindi is a fantastic Kenyan beach resort that features a host of
pristine coasts, exciting deep sea diving activities and charming
beachside restaurants serving lip smacking seafood. The Malindi Beaches Guide offers visitors information on the beach scene in this popular coastal tourist getaway.

**Introduction **

Malindi is a serene beach town located on the Malindi Bay at the tip
of River Galana that lies on the Indian Ocean Kenyan coast. The town is
located approximately 120 kilometers to the northeastern region of
Mombasa and is a very popular beach hideaway highly frequented
by Italian tourists. The region nestles tranquil beaches that are ideal
for a castaway, peaceful holiday and action-packed centers that play
host to umpteen water sports activities. Malindi also houses a famous
marine national park that boasts of a rich aquatic ecology and several
heritage ruins that present brilliant illustrations of classic Swahili
culture.

**Attractions and Activities **

Malindi Beach is a super diving destination that offers its visitors
fantastic previews of a rich coral life and varied species of sea
animals. The deserted sandy beaches of Malindi provide a tranquil
backdrop for idyllic leisure and provides innumerable opportunities for
viewing the historical ruins of the region. The interesting Gedi ruins
can be spotted from the main Malindi Beach. This is one of the most
breathtaking depictions of an erstwhile Arab beach settlement. Deep sea
fishing is an immensely celebrated activity in the region for its
marlins, sharks and sail fish. The waters of Malindi are ideal for
spotting the rare mako shark and hammerhead. Malindi also features
numerous five star hotel properties that are located on the sea-front
and offer their guests stunning beach vistas. For deep sea fishing
Malindi Sea Fishing Club is a popular private diving center. They offer
intensive instructional sessions on waterskiing, snorkeling, diving and
deep sea fishing. Kingfisher Sport Fishing is another good center for
exciting deep sea fishing. Malindi houses a superb marine national park
that is great for surfing, snorkeling, big game angling and diving.
Visitors can also rent glass bottom boats from hotels for a trip to
view the region’s majestic corals. The beach front of Malindi has an
eclectic line-up of restaurants and cafes that serve delicious food.

Malindi is a inviting potpourri of the most attractive beaches in
the country and a bevy of water sports activities that have burgeoned
as an aftermath of the rich marine life of the region.
