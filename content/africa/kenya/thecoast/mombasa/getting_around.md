---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Mombasa runs on **tuk-tuks** — three-wheeled rickshaws that are everywhere on the island and the cheapest way to get around the old town and downtown. Agree the fare before getting in. **Matatus** (minibus taxis) cover the longer routes to the North and South Coast beaches and to the mainland suburbs; they are cheap, chaotic, and not particularly comfortable. For trips between the city and the main beach hotels at Diani Beach, the **Likoni Ferry** (free for foot passengers) crosses the harbour to the south coast every few minutes — expect crowds and a slow, colourful experience. **Taxis** and Uber are reliable if pricier. For longer-distance travel, the **SGR** standard-gauge railway runs from Mombasa's Miritini terminus to Nairobi in about five hours — a smooth, modern ride, and much more pleasant than the old overnight train it replaced.
