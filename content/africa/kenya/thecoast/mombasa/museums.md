While Mombasa does not have a lot of typical museums, its open-air
history is long and deep and offers an interesting look into the city’s
past and present. Here are some of the top museums and historical sites
of Mombasa.

**The Fort Jesus**

One of the city’s largest attractions, this fort was built in 1593
by the Portuguese as a way to keep local enemies and Turkish warships
at bay. Today the fort’s remains set the stage for a great tour that
includes a small museum, as well as a look at prison cells and torture
rooms that slaves were held in before their trade. There are numerous
gun turrets and houses within the walls of the fort that make for an
enjoyable afternoon of exploration.

If you want to learn more about the fort, there is an interesting
light and sound show offered 3 nights per week that uses actors, sound
effects and lights to tell the fort’s storied history. The show
includes a candlelight dinner in the fort’s courtyard.

**Gedi Ruins**
