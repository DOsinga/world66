*When you arrive on the Dhow you are given a welcome drink called a 'Dawa' (swahili for medicine) this is delicious it's a combination of lime/honey and vodka on ice and is definately 'moreish'.*

*Once everyone is onboard you set off for a short sail down the river, under 'Nyali bridge', the boat the moors while the food is eaten, there is also a live band (which has been excellent evertime I've been) there is also a small dance floor.*

*Going to the toilet is fun as it's down inside the dhow and is very small, so try and go before you embark on the evenings festivities!!!*

*Once you have enjoyed the food and music the dhow takes a slow jaunt back to 'The Tamarind' restaurant, (a quick trip to the loo is recommended, the hand basin is the best I've ever seen!!!)*

*A coach awaits you at the restaurant to take you back to your hotel.*

*If you don't fancy the Dhow you can eat at the Tamarind, the menu is extensive and the food magnificent.*

*If anybody from The Tamarind reads this... bring back the coconut and prawn 'bitings'*
