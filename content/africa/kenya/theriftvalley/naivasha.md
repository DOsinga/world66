Naivasha is a moderately sized town about 100 km northwest of [Nairobi](/africa/kenya/nairobi), at the foot of the Great Rift Valley escarpment. It is the gateway to several of [Kenya's](/africa/kenya) most accessible natural attractions.

**Lake Naivasha** is a freshwater lake fringed with papyrus and home to hippos, fish eagles, and enormous flocks of waterfowl. Boat trips on the lake are the main activity. **Hell's Gate National Park**, one of the few parks in Kenya where you can walk and cycle, lies just south of the lake — its dramatic cliffs and gorges are well worth a day. **Mount Longonot**, a dormant volcano with a crater-rim hike offering panoramic views of the Rift Valley, is another popular day trip from town.

Naivasha sits on the main road between Nairobi and [Nakuru](/africa/kenya/theriftvalley/nakuru) and is easily reached by matatu or bus.
