#### The Unique Blend of Nakuru Bars and Cafes

**Nakuru bars and cafes** offer the best of African hospitality. Whether you are in search of vibrant, exciting entertainment with sports and music as the attraction, or just a quiet place to have a drink and chat, Nakuru bars and cafes have it. There is no shortage of places to grab tasty, culturally diverse meals in the city either, reflecting the rich heritage of the area.

#### Chilled Nakuru Bars and Cafes

Of all the Nakuru bars and cafes, Taidy&rsquo;s literally stands out among the choices. It&rsquo;s a large building with three levels and an encircling balcony. It is constantly screening sports, usually football, inside on its televisions while offering decent food and cold Tusker beer. Naturally, Taidy&rsquo;s doubles as a nightclub with a good, friendly atmosphere. Locals regard Taidy&rsquo;s quite highly as it offers most of what one looks for on a night out, making this an ideal spot for tourists to convene and experience the Nakuru culture.

For enjoying a quieter drink, hotels seem to offer the best bars in town, including the Cathay and Waterbuck. Both are located in the heart of town and tend to attract a clientele seeking a more relaxed atmosphere for socializing.

#### Homely Culture of Nakuru Bars and Cafes

If cafe culture is more your thing when searching for Nakuru spots to relax, Cafe Lemon Tart on the corner of Moi Road and Kenyatta Avenue is an excellent first choice, serving from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. and offering light snacks and freshly-ground coffee. Their breakfasts are highly recommended, and the cafe is known for being one of the best places in the city to grab a meal.

If you&rsquo;d rather make your way into downtown Nakuru, Cafe Sweet Mart on Gusii Road is a popular destination with tourists. Excellent food and friendly, helpful staff make it an ideal stop when exploring the city. It doubles as a bakery as well, selling a variety of tasty snacks from Indian samosas and Masala burgers to French bread and puff pastries.

There is something for everyone in Nakuru when you want to visit a bar or cafe. Cafe culture, African style, offers a variety of scenes to enjoy and certainly more than you would have expected in the great African Rift Valley.
