---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Nakuru sits at 1,850 metres on the Rift Valley floor, so the climate is mild year-round — daytime temperatures usually 20–28°C, cooler at night. The two rainy seasons run roughly March to May (the long rains) and October to November (the short rains); roads in and around the parks can get slippery during the wettest weeks. **The best months to visit are June through September and January through February**, when skies are clearer, wildlife viewing in Lake Nakuru National Park is easiest, and the flamingo numbers tend to be highest. The annual **Rhino Charge**, an off-road fundraising rally for fencing the country's rhino sanctuaries, takes place near Nakuru in late May or June and is worth timing a trip around if you can find tickets.
