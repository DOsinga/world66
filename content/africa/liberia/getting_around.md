---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Getting around Liberia takes patience. The road network is limited and in poor shape outside Monrovia, and the rainy season turns many dirt roads into mud.

## Bush Taxis and Minibuses

The standard way to travel between towns is by **bush taxi** — shared cars (usually saloons crammed with more passengers than seats) that leave once full and stop frequently. Minibus taxis cover some of the same routes and are slightly more comfortable. Neither follows a timetable. You wait until the vehicle fills, then go.

Within Monrovia, shared taxis and motorcycle taxis (**okadas**) are everywhere and cheap. Pay the full fare and ride alone if you want to go directly to your destination rather than stopping repeatedly along the route.

## Roads

The road from Monrovia to Buchanan (about 180 km southeast) is the best-maintained paved route in the country. The highway to the Sierra Leone border has also been improved. Most other roads are unpaved and deteriorate badly during the rainy season (May–October). A 4WD vehicle is strongly recommended for travel outside the main corridors.

Driving yourself is possible but challenging — traffic regulations are loosely observed, vehicles are often poorly maintained, and road conditions can change quickly after heavy rain.

## Domestic Flights

Charter flights connect Monrovia with some regional towns. This is the only realistic way to reach remote areas quickly, and the cost is substantial. Organizations working in the country use air charters routinely. Ask locally about options.

## River Transport

Boats on rivers provide access to some villages not reachable by road, particularly in the southeast near Sapo National Park. Arrangements are made locally.
