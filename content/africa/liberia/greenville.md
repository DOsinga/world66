Greenville is the capital of Sinoe County in southeastern [Liberia](/africa/liberia), about 350 km from Monrovia — the first 100 km paved, the rest dirt road. The journey is an adventure in itself and can take the better part of a day.

The town was founded as a settlement for freed American slaves in the 1830s, and some of the original colonial-era buildings still stand. The population is predominantly Kru and Bassa, and the town serves as a small port and regional hub. Greenville is remote and facilities are basic, but it offers a glimpse of a part of Liberia that few outsiders see.
