## Sapo National Park

Liberia's largest protected area and one of the most significant remaining tracts of primary rainforest in West Africa. Sapo is home to forest elephants, pygmy hippos, leopards, chimpanzees, and an extraordinary range of birds. Getting there takes effort — it's about five hours from Monrovia with the last stretch on rough roads — but that difficulty is also what keeps it extraordinary. Guided walks through the forest are the main activity; basic accommodation is available at the park station near Juarzon. Go in the dry season.

## Robertsport

A small fishing town at the end of a peninsula in Grand Cape Mount County, about two hours northwest of Monrovia. Robertsport has become one of West Africa's better-known surfing destinations, with consistent Atlantic swells and several reef and beach breaks. Non-surfers come for the beaches, the relaxed pace, and the handful of small lodges. The surrounding area also has the ruins of a colonial-era hotel and a lake good for kayaking and paddling.

## Kpatawee Waterfall

Near Gbarnga in Bong County, about two hours from Monrovia. The waterfall drops around 20 meters into green plunge pools through a landscape of bamboo and forest. It's a short walk from the road and one of the most accessible natural attractions in the country. The pools are good for swimming. After heavy rain it's spectacular.

## Marshall Wetlands and Chimpanzee Island

About 30 km east of Monrovia, Marshall is a coastal area where rivers meet the ocean through mangrove forest. The wetlands are a Ramsar-designated site of international importance for migratory birds. Just offshore, Chimpanzee Island is home to chimpanzees rescued from medical research, now living a semi-wild life. Local rangers take visitors out by boat to watch feeding time — a genuinely moving experience.

## Monrovia's Waterside Market

The main market in central Monrovia is a deep dive into Liberian commerce. Traders sell everything from fresh produce and dried fish to fabric, electronics, and traditional medicine. Loud, crowded, and very alive. The area along the waterfront (though gentrifying slowly) still has some of the texture of old Monrovia.
