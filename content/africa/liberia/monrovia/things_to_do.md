---
title: "Things to do"
type: section
---

Monrovia's most evocative site is the **Ducor Palace Hotel**, the decaying hulk of a 1960s luxury hotel that dominates the hilltop above the city — tours of the ruins can be arranged informally. **Providence Island**, at the mouth of the Mesurado River, is the landing point of the first freed American settlers in 1822; a short boat trip reaches the remains of Fort Norris and a small memorial. In the city centre, **Waterside Market** is the busiest market in the country — a dense, noisy, colourful maze worth an hour of wandering.

The **National Museum of Liberia** holds artefacts and documents tracing the country's history from the indigenous peoples through the settler period to independence. **Cape Mesurado** at the tip of the peninsula offers views over the ocean and back across the city.
