## Visa

Most visitors require a visa. Apply at the nearest Liberian embassy before travel — a tourist visa is straightforward to obtain. Your passport must be valid for at least six months from the date of entry. A yellow fever vaccination certificate is required for entry; you will be asked for it at the airport.

## Money

The official currency is the **Liberian Dollar (LRD)**. US dollars are widely accepted and often preferred, particularly in Monrovia and for larger transactions. Bring enough US dollars in cash to cover your stay — Liberia is a cash economy. ATMs exist in Monrovia but are unreliable; do not count on them. Small bills are useful, as change can be hard to find.

## Language

English is the official language and is used in business, government, and education. Day-to-day spoken English in Liberia has its own cadence and some distinct vocabulary — it takes an ear to follow at first. Around 30 indigenous languages are also spoken, including Kpelle, Bassa, Grebo, Kru, and Mande. Outside towns, many people speak only their local language.

## Communications

Mobile phone coverage is reasonable in Monrovia and major towns, thinner in rural areas. Local SIM cards are cheap and available at the airport. Internet access is available in Monrovia via mobile data; elsewhere it's limited.

## Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Power cuts are frequent even in Monrovia. A voltage adapter may be needed depending on your equipment.

## Safety

Monrovia is generally navigable during daylight hours, but petty crime is common and you should take the usual precautions. Avoid traveling between towns after dark — roads are unlit, poorly surfaced, and accidents are common. The US State Department advises increased caution due to crime and unrest; check current advisories before travel.

## Time Zone

GMT / UTC+0. Liberia does not observe daylight saving time.
