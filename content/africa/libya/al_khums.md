Al Khums is a small coastal town about 130 km east of [Tripoli](/africa/libya/tripoli), and for most visitors it exists for one reason: [Leptis Magna](/africa/libya/leptismagna), the spectacular Roman city just 3 km to the east. As the nearest town to arguably the best-preserved Roman ruins in the Mediterranean, Al Khums serves as the practical base for anyone visiting the site.

The town itself is modest — a working harbour, a grid of low-rise streets, and a handful of simple restaurants serving fresh fish and Libyan staples like couscous with lamb. There is not much to detain you beyond the ruins, but the harbour area has a certain quiet charm, and the seafront corniche makes for a pleasant walk in the evening. The town also sits near the remains of a smaller Roman villa district that most visitors overlook entirely.

Al Khums is reached by road from Tripoli, a drive of about two hours along the coast. Shared taxis and minibuses make the trip regularly. If you are visiting Leptis Magna, plan to arrive early in the morning to have the ruins in good light and to beat the midday heat, which can be fierce in summer.
