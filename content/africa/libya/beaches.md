---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Libya's Mediterranean coastline stretches over 1,700 km and includes some genuinely beautiful beaches — white or cream sand, clear blue-green water — that see almost no visitors. Before 2011, Libyans would drive out of Tripoli in summer to swim at beaches west of the city, and the water near Sabratha and Zuwara was popular.

Tileel public beach, about 11 km west of Sabratha, has powder-fine sand and clean water that would be unremarkable on a Mediterranean resort coast but is startling in Libya. It's a reasonable stop if you're visiting the Sabratha ruins. Follow the coast road west from Sabratha and look for the turnoff past the petrol station.

The beaches east of Tripoli near Janzour (Zanzur) are closer to the city but more developed. Those further west, approaching the Tunisian border, are less visited and tend to be cleaner.

Given the current security situation, beach tourism is not a realistic goal in itself. But if you're in the Tripoli or Sabratha area, an hour at the coast is a worthwhile detour.
