Misurata is Libya's third largest city, with a population of around 300,000. It sits on the site of an ancient Phoenician trading post, some 200 km east of [Tripoli](/africa/libya/tripoli) and 825 km west of [Benghazi](/africa/libya/benghazi).

The city was one of the main battlegrounds of the 2011 revolution — a prolonged and bloody siege left parts of it heavily damaged. It has since been substantially rebuilt and is a busy commercial hub for western Libya. There is little specifically for tourists here, but it is a natural stopping point on the coastal road between the two main cities.
