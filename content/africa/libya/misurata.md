Travel Guide

Misurata City is situated on the location of an old trade centers that the Phoenicians founded in the tenth century BC . It is some 200 km East of [Tripoli](/africa/libya/tripoli) and 825 km west of [Benghazi](/africa/libya/benghazi).

Misurata is Libya's third largest city, with a population of around 300,000. It was one of the main battlegrounds of the 2011 revolution — a prolonged and bloody siege left parts of the city heavily damaged. It has since been substantially rebuilt and is a commercial hub for western Libya. There is little for tourists here, but it's a natural stopping point on the coastal road between Tripoli and Benghazi.
