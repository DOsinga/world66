The Karamanli Mosque is probably the most splendid mosque in Tripoli. Built in 1738 by Ahmed Karamanli, the semi-autonomous Ottoman governor, it sits near the castle at the entrance to the main souq. The interior is lavishly decorated with imported Italian tiles, carved stucco, and multiple domes. The octagonal minaret is a local landmark. Ahmed Karamanli and several of his descendants are buried in a mausoleum attached to the mosque.
