Anakao is a small Vezo fishing village on Madagascar's southwest coast, about 45 kilometres south of [Toliara](/africa/madagascar/toliara). It sits on a long white sand beach facing a turquoise lagoon protected by a coral reef — one of the most beautiful stretches of coast on the island.

The village is a base for visiting Nosy Ve, a tiny offshore island where red-tailed tropicbirds nest and the snorkelling over the reef is excellent. Between July and September, humpback whales pass close to shore on their annual migration, and pirogue trips to see them are easily arranged. The Vezo fishing community itself is fascinating — watch the outrigger pirogues come in with the morning catch and buy seafood directly on the beach.

Getting here is part of the adventure. The most common route from Toliara involves a bus south, a boat crossing, and a truck ride along the coast. The whole journey can be organised in Toliara. Accommodation is simple — a handful of beachside bungalow operations, some with solar power, none with pretensions beyond fresh fish and cold beer.
