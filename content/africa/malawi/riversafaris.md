---
title: "River Safaris"
type: section
---

The **Shire River** flows south from Lake Malawi, passing through Liwonde National Park before crossing into Mozambique and joining the Zambezi. The section through Liwonde is one of the best wildlife boat safari experiences in Africa, and it is genuinely underrated.

The river here is wide and slow, fringed with dense riverine vegetation. Hippo pods are large and numerous — sometimes several hundred animals in a stretch of a few kilometres. Crocodiles are everywhere. Elephant come to the bank to drink and occasionally swim across. Liwonde is one of Malawi's most successful conservation areas, with African Parks having reintroduced lion, cheetah, and wild dog since taking over management in 2015. Birding is exceptional: kingfishers, bee-eaters, fish eagles, and herons in extraordinary variety.

Boat safaris on the Shire are offered by lodges within and around Liwonde National Park. The standard trip is 2–3 hours at dusk or dawn. This is the single activity in Malawi most likely to exceed expectations — if you visit Liwonde, don't skip the river.

**Majete Wildlife Reserve**, further south on the Shire, is another African Parks-managed area and offers walking safaris and game drives alongside river viewing. It is Malawi's only Big Five reserve following the reintroduction of black rhino.
