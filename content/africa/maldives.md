The Maldives is an archipelago of 26 atolls and roughly 1,200 coral islands scattered across the Indian Ocean southwest of India. Only about 200 islands are inhabited. The rest are either uninhabited wilderness, private resort islands, or just sandbanks. It is one of the flattest countries on earth: the highest natural point sits around 2.4 meters above sea level.

That geography defines everything here. The water is everything — turquoise lagoons, coral reefs, whale sharks, manta rays, and the kind of underwater visibility that divers travel across the world to find. Above water, the landscape is simple almost to the point of abstraction: white sand, palm trees, ocean horizon.

Tourism arrived in 1972 with two resorts. Today the Maldives receives around 1.8 million visitors a year and tourism accounts for the majority of GDP. The model has long been the "one island, one resort" system, with luxury resorts on uninhabited islands, entirely separate from local life. That is changing: guesthouses on local islands have grown rapidly since they were legalized in 2010, opening up a genuinely affordable way to visit.

## The existential threat

The Maldives faces a crisis that no amount of careful management can fully solve. Around 80 percent of the land area sits less than one meter above sea level. As sea levels rise — projections suggest anywhere from 30 centimeters to one meter by 2100 — large portions of the country will become uninhabitable, not through sudden flooding but through saltwater intrusion into freshwater supplies, erosion of beaches, and increasingly frequent storm surges. The government has explored buying land in other countries as a contingency plan for relocation.

This is not a distant hypothetical. Some islands are already losing ground. The government is investing in land reclamation and coastal protection, but these are delaying measures. The Maldives is on the front line of climate change in the most literal sense, and visiting with awareness of that is more honest than treating it purely as a backdrop for holiday photographs.
