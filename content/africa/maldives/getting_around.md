---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Inter-island transport runs on speedboats, ferries, domestic flights, and seaplanes. The choice depends on where you are going and how much you want to spend.

**Speedboats** are the standard transfer from Malé airport to resort islands and nearby local islands. Most resorts within a couple of hours of Malé use speedboats; journeys typically take 30 minutes to 2 hours.

**Seaplanes** reach more distant resorts in roughly 30–45 minutes of spectacular low-altitude flying over atolls. They are expensive — transfers can cost USD 300–600 each way — and only operate in daylight, which sometimes means an overnight stay in Malé for late-arriving flights.

**Domestic flights** with Maldivian and Manta Air connect Malé to airstrips on larger inhabited islands in the outer atolls. This is the most practical option for reaching islands in Addu, Fuvahmulah, Haa Alif, or Laamu atolls.

**Public ferries** run from Malé to inhabited islands in the central atolls on a fixed schedule. They are slow but very cheap — a useful option for budget travelers staying on local islands close to the capital. The ferry from Malé to Maafushi, one of the most popular budget-travel islands, takes about 1.5 hours and costs around USD 2.

**The dhoni** — a traditional wooden vessel now usually diesel-powered — remains the workhorse of inter-island transport for locals and is widely used on liveaboard diving trips. Larger vedis handle cargo and longer crossings.

Within individual islands, distances are almost never a problem. Most inhabited islands are small enough to walk across in under ten minutes. Malé is an exception: taxis, motorcycles, and bicycles are all available, though the city is compact enough that walking is often fastest given the traffic.
