---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency:** The Maldivian rufiyaa (MVR) is the local currency. US dollars are widely accepted at resorts and guesthouses and often preferred. The exchange rate is around MVR 15–16 to USD 1. Note that the rufiyaa is a non-convertible currency — convert any remaining MVR back to dollars before leaving, as you cannot exchange it abroad. ATMs are available in Malé and on some larger local islands but charge significant fees (around USD 6–7 per withdrawal); bring cash or make one large withdrawal.

**Costs:** The Maldives has a wide cost range. A luxury resort can easily cost USD 500–1,000 per night per person. A guesthouse on a local island runs USD 50–150 per night. Budget travelers staying on local islands can get by on USD 60–80 per day including accommodation, meals, and basic activities.

**Taxes:** A 16% GST applies to most goods and services. A Green Tax of USD 6 per night is charged at most accommodation. Prices should include these taxes but always confirm — some places list pre-tax rates.

**Dress and conduct:** The Maldives is an Islamic republic. On inhabited local islands, modest dress is required: shoulders and knees covered for both men and women. Alcohol is not available on local islands; it is only served at resorts and liveaboards. Swimwear is restricted to designated "bikini beaches" on local islands. Public displays of affection are frowned upon. Pork is not served outside of resorts.

**Electricity:** 230V, 50Hz. UK-style three-pin plugs are most common, though adapters are widely available.

**Health:** No vaccinations are specifically required, though keeping routine vaccinations current is advisable. The water in Malé is desalinated tap water; bottled water is the norm on islands. Sunscreen is essential — reef-safe formulas are strongly recommended to avoid contributing to coral bleaching.

**Communications:** Mobile coverage (4G) is reliable on inhabited islands and in the central atolls. Ooredoo and Dhiraagu are the main providers. Tourist SIM cards are available at the airport. Resort islands generally have Wi-Fi, though speeds vary. Many outer islands have limited connectivity.

**Safety:** The Maldives is generally a very safe destination with low crime. Ocean safety is a more realistic concern: currents can be strong in channels between atolls, and not all beaches are safe for swimming. Ask locally before swimming in unfamiliar spots.
