---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Maldives has two seasons governed by the monsoon: the dry northeast monsoon (November to April) and the wet southwest monsoon (May to October).

**December to March** is peak season: clear skies, calm seas, excellent visibility for diving and snorkeling, and reliable sunshine. It is also the most expensive and crowded period. Book accommodation well ahead.

**April** is a transition month — still largely dry but with the first occasional showers. It offers a reasonable compromise between weather and cost.

**May to October** is the wet season. This does not mean constant rain; showers tend to be heavy but short, and many days are sunny. September and October are typically the wettest and stormiest months. The southwest monsoon also brings increased plankton and stirred-up water, which reduces visibility in some areas — but it is the best time for manta rays and whale sharks, which are drawn by the plankton bloom. Baa Atoll's Hanifaru Bay sees its peak manta aggregations between June and November.

**Budget travelers** should look at May, June, and late October to November: the weather is imperfect but manageable, and resort and guesthouse rates drop substantially — sometimes 30–50 percent compared to peak season.

The water temperature stays between 26°C and 30°C year-round. There is no bad time to dive from a thermal comfort standpoint.
