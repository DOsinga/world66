Bankas sits at the southern end of the Bandiagara Escarpment and is one of the better starting points for a trek into [Dogon Country](/africa/mali/dogoncountry). It is smaller and considerably less touristed than [Bandiagara](/africa/mali/dogoncountry/bandiagara), which means the guides here tend to be friendlier and less pushy — a real advantage when you are negotiating a multi-day trek through the cliff villages.

From Bankas, trekking routes head north along the base of the escarpment, passing through traditional Dogon villages built into the cliff face. The villages around Bankas see fewer visitors than those near Bandiagara, and the experience feels more genuine as a result. You will still need a local guide, which can be arranged in town. Market day is Monday, and it is worth timing your arrival to see it — traders come in from surrounding villages and the market gives a good sense of daily life in this part of Mali.

The town is most easily reached from [Mopti](/africa/mali/mopti) by road, a journey of about five hours depending on conditions. Transport is by bush taxi or minibus, and departures are most frequent in the morning. Accommodation in Bankas is basic — expect simple guesthouses with shared facilities — but the warm hospitality of the Dogon people more than makes up for the lack of comfort.
