Djiguibombo is a Dogon village at the base of the Bandiagara Escarpment, easily reached from [Bandiagara](/africa/mali/dogoncountry/bandiagara) and frequently used as a starting point for trekking along the cliff face. The village has basic guesthouses, and the surrounding area is among the most rewarding walking terrain in West Africa.

From Djiguibombo, well-worn paths lead to the escarpment villages of **Ende**, **Teli**, and **Kani Kombole** — each clinging to the cliff or sheltering in its shadow, with ancient granaries built into the rockface above. All have simple accommodation where you can spend the night. Teli in particular has striking cliff-face architecture and a dramatic setting.

To get here from Bandiagara, hire a mobylette (moped) or negotiate a lift with a local driver. Some trekkers walk the escarpment; the terrain is manageable but hot, so start early and carry plenty of water.
