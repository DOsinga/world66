Kayes holds the unenviable distinction of being one of the hottest inhabited cities on Earth, with temperatures regularly exceeding 45 degrees Celsius in the dry season. The city sits on the Senegal River in western Mali, near the Senegalese border, and served as the colonial capital of French Sudan before Bamako took over. The old colonial buildings, including the train station and governor's residence, still stand, though in various states of decay.

The Senegal River is the lifeblood of the region, and the area around Kayes offers some striking natural scenery, including the Chutes du Felou, a set of waterfalls about fifteen kilometers from town. The train line connecting Kayes to Bamako and to Dakar was once a major engineering achievement, though service has become increasingly unreliable. If you do visit, come between November and February when temperatures are merely very hot rather than completely unbearable.
