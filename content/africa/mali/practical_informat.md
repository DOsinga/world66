## Currency

The currency is the **West African CFA franc (XOF)**, shared with Senegal, Burkina Faso, Côte d'Ivoire, Guinea-Bissau, Niger, Togo, and Benin. It is pegged to the euro at a fixed rate (655.957 XOF per euro). Cash is king; credit cards are accepted only at a few hotels and restaurants in Bamako and are unreliable. ATMs exist in Bamako and some regional capitals but can be out of service. Bring enough euros or US dollars to change as needed. Bureaux de change operate at the international airport and in city centres.

## Visas

Visas are required for most nationalities and must be obtained before arrival — Mali does not issue visas on arrival. Apply at a Malian embassy or consulate. The political situation since the 2021 coup has affected Mali's diplomatic relationships; verify that your country has a functioning embassy relationship with Mali before assuming visa processing will be straightforward.

## Health

**Yellow fever vaccination is mandatory.** Carry your vaccination certificate — it will be checked at the airport. Mali is a high-risk malaria zone with chloroquine-resistant strains; take appropriate prophylaxis and use mosquito nets and repellent. Hepatitis A, typhoid, meningococcal meningitis, and rabies vaccinations are recommended. Do not drink tap water; bottled or treated water only.

Bamako has adequate private medical facilities by West African standards, but serious illness or injury will require medical evacuation. Ensure your travel insurance covers this.

## Safety

Read the current travel advisory from your government before travelling. As of early 2026, the situation is serious across most of the country. See the security section of the main [Mali](/africa/mali) page.

If you do travel: register with your embassy on arrival, share your itinerary with someone at home, keep a low profile, avoid large crowds, and have a plan for emergency evacuation.

## Language

**French** is the official language. **Bambara** (Bamanankan) is the national lingua franca, spoken as a first or second language by around 80% of the population. Many other languages are spoken regionally: Fulani, Songhai, Tamasheq (Tuareg), Soninke, and more. A few words of Bambara go a long way.

## Climate

Mali is hot. The south has a rainy season from June to September; the north receives little or no rain at all. Temperatures regularly exceed 40°C before the rains in April–May. The coolest and most pleasant time is November to February, when daytime temperatures in the south are 25–32°C and nights are cool. See [When to Go](/africa/mali/when_to_go) for more detail.

## Religion

Mali is around 90% Muslim, predominantly Sufi in tradition. Dress modestly, particularly outside Bamako — cover shoulders and knees. Friday prayers are important; expect businesses to close or quieten on Friday afternoons. Greetings are taken seriously; it is rude to rush straight to business without the proper exchange of hellos and enquiries about health and family. Learn a few words of Arabic greeting — it is genuinely appreciated.

## Electricity

220V, 50Hz. French-style two-round-pin plugs (Type C and E). Power cuts are frequent; a power bank for devices is essential.

## Communications

Mobile coverage exists in [Bamako](/africa/mali/bamako) and along major routes; it becomes patchy quickly outside cities. Orange Mali and Malitel are the main providers. International SIM cards are expensive to use; buy a local SIM at the airport or in the city. Internet is available in Bamako but speeds are slow and connectivity unreliable outside the capital.
