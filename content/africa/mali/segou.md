Segou is the historic capital of the Bambara people, a calm, tree-lined city of about 65,000 on the banks of the Niger River, some 235 kilometres northeast of [Bamako](/africa/mali/bamako). After the dust and congestion of the capital, Segou feels like a deep breath — wide streets, friendly people, and a pace of life that invites you to slow down.

The city is best known through Maryse Condé's historical novel *Segu*, which chronicles the Bambara kingdom. The remains of Mbella, the ancient Bambara capital, lie nearby. Monday is market day at the Grand Marché, and it is the best time to find the bright hand-woven fabrics for which Segou is famous.

A rewarding day trip runs to [Niono](/africa/mali/niono), locally known as the Venice of Mali. Its mud mosque rivals the larger and more famous one in Djenné, and it is generally easier to get inside.
