---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Getting there is half the fun. Timbuktu did not earn its reputation for being at the end of the world for nothing. You have three options: air, road, and river.

**By air**

There are flights from [Bamako](/africa/mali/bamako) and from [Mopti](/africa/mali/mopti). Schedules change frequently, so confirm before you plan around them. A bus runs from the airport to town for about 1,000 CFA.

**By road**

Not easy, but possible in the dry season. You can get here by 4WD or by bus from Bamako via Segou -- count on at least two hard days of driving. In the wet season the roads become impassable, and a boat is the better option.

**By boat**

The most scenic and most memorable way to arrive. Riverboats run from [Mopti](/africa/mali/mopti) (about three days) and from [Gao](/africa/mali/gao) (three to four days), with stops at villages along the way. You will see good birdlife and, on the Gao route, possibly hippos. Chartering a pinasse from Mopti costs around 300,000 CFA and technically seats twenty, though more than six people will be cramped. The boat docks at Kabara, about ten kilometers from town.
