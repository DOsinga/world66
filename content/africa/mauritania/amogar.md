The Amogjar Pass is the dramatic mountain road connecting [Atar](/africa/mauritania/atar) to [Chinguetti](/africa/mauritania/chinguetti), cutting through the Adrar Plateau in central [Mauritania](/africa/mauritania). The pass itself is one of the most scenic stretches of road in the country, winding through rocky gorges and across a stark, beautiful landscape of sandstone cliffs and desert scrub.

Near the top of the pass, ancient rock paintings and engravings are found on sheltered cliff faces — some depicting cattle, giraffes, and human figures from a time when the Sahara was green and fertile. The art dates back thousands of years and offers a striking reminder of how dramatically this landscape has changed. The paintings are not fenced or ticketed; you simply walk up to them, which makes the experience all the more affecting.

The road through the pass is paved in parts but deteriorates in others, and after heavy rains sections can become difficult. A 4x4 is strongly recommended. The drive from Atar to Chinguetti takes roughly two to three hours, and it is worth stopping several times along the way to take in the views and explore the rock art sites. If you are making the journey to Chinguetti's ancient libraries, the pass is half the reason to go.
