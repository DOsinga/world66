Atar is the capital of the Adrar region and the main gateway to one of Mauritania's most rewarding corners. Sitting at the foot of the Adrar plateau, roughly 450 kilometres northeast of Nouakchott, it is the largest town for a very long distance in any direction — a working market town that happens to be the best base for exploring the Saharan landscapes and ancient caravan cities nearby.

The town itself is not the destination; the region is. Within a day's drive you can reach the [Terjit oasis](/africa/mauritania/terjit), where spring-fed pools lie in a narrow palm-lined gorge, and [Chinguetti](/africa/mauritania/chinguetti), the holy city of the Sahara with its crumbling library towers and medieval mosque. For centuries Chinguetti was more important than Atar — a stopping point for pilgrims crossing the desert — but Atar has grown into the administrative and commercial hub, and that suits the traveller well.

The market is worth a wander: livestock, vegetables, and the kind of general goods a desert town depends on. Prices for food and supplies are lower here than in Chinguetti or out in the dunes. A handful of hotels and small restaurants serve the traveller trade, basic but adequate. Do sort out your money before arriving — there is no reliable banking in Atar, so come with enough ouguiya from Nouakchott.
