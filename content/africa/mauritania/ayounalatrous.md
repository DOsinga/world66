Ayoun el-Atrous is a dusty market town in southeastern Mauritania, sitting at the crossroads of the long trans-desert run between Nouakchott and Mali. It is known locally for its trade in plastic beads — not the most compelling reason to detour, but the lively market gives you a genuine slice of Sahelian commerce. At 800 kilometres from the capital, it is a natural place to break the journey, rest, and take stock before the road pushes on into harder territory. From here, Nema is another 350 kilometres east — a better-equipped base for exploring the surrounding desert or crossing into Mali.
