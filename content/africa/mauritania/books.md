Most serious books about Mauritania are in French. English-language options are limited but a few are worth knowing about.

**Two Rivers: Travels in West Africa** by Peter Hudson — funny, honest travel writing covering West Africa including Mauritania.

**Impossible Journey** by Michael Asher — Asher and his wife spent months at Chinguetti preparing for a camel crossing of the Sahara from west to east. One of the best accounts of the Mauritanian desert.

**Historical Dictionary of Mauritania** by Alfred G. Gerteiny — the standard reference work for those wanting historical depth.

**The Children of Mauritania** by Lauren Goodsmith — a photographic and cultural portrait.

For broader context, the Rough Guide to West Africa covers Mauritania adequately, and the Wikivoyage entry is one of the more reliable free online resources.
