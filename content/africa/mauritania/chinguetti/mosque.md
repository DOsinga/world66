The 13th-century mosque is Chinguetti's most iconic structure — its square minaret topped with ostrich eggs is the image that appears on Mauritanian currency. Non-Muslims cannot enter, but the exterior and the surrounding old quarter are well worth exploring. The mud-brick architecture here represents one of the best-preserved examples of Saharan vernacular building in West Africa.
