---
title: "North and South"
type: location
---

The mixed population of Mauritania has caused quite a few problems in the past and still continues to do so. In the North the dominating Arabic speaking tribes of Berber stock rule. In the south different Black-African people live, mostly in the fertile regions along the banks of the Senegal river.

 The different groups do not really get along well, but right now there are no big problems. As you go from a predominantly arabic to a Black-African town, you will notice the differences immediately. The Black-African population ahs a strong focus on life in Senegal, plays Sengelase music and revers Senegalese Islamic Saints and Wise men. The Berber/Arabic people are mostly nomads, and prefer a typical arabic lifestyle. They eat couscous, play Oum Kilthoum music and drink sweet mint tea.
