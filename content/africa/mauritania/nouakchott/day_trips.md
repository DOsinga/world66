Nouakchott sits on the coast, with the Sahara beginning almost immediately to the east. Most excursions from the capital involve long desert drives and are better thought of as multi-day trips than true day trips — [Chinguetti](/africa/mauritania/chinguetti), the ancient caravan city with its mud-brick libraries, is around 700 km away. The nearest worthwhile destination for a shorter outing is the coast itself: the beach north of the city, where fishing pirogues bring in their catch in the late afternoon, is as close to a day trip as Nouakchott offers.

For those with more time, [Atar](/africa/mauritania/atar) is the gateway to the Adrar plateau and its extraordinary desert landscapes, carved canyons, and ancient settlements. From Atar you can continue to Chinguetti and Ouadane. These journeys require a 4x4 and typically a local guide.
