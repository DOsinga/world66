There is not much to do in Rosso itself, but this border town on the Senegal River has a character of its own. It sits in Mauritania's fertile southern strip where the country's crops are grown, and the drive from Nouakchott is pleasant, passing small villages, interesting people and sand-coloured rock buildings.

Rosso has a large community of Black Mauritanians who feel closer to their relatives across the river in Senegal than to the Berber and Arab populations of the north. The ferry landing where the bac arrives from Senegal is the liveliest spot in town — there is always something going on. If you are heading to Senegal, Rosso is the crossing point; from the other side it is about an hour's drive to Saint-Louis, with its colonial architecture and better accommodation options.
