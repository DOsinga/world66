Slavery was abolished officially in 1970. Before that the French had tried to abolish it about four times, but every-day practices were stronger than their laws.

 Most people tend to think that Slavery in Mauritania was about Arabs holding Black Africans as slaves. This image needs some nuance. All Black groups in Mauritania had slavery practices. Rich Halpular, Soninke and Wolof all had big households where slaves did the hard labor.

 The question if slavery still exists in Mauritania is a hard one. There are still people living in a social condition not very different from slavery. But there are no slave markets, you will not see Black Slaves with iron rings around their neck being sold ten at a time.

 Mauritania is one of the poorest countries in the world with a very fragile social balance. To deal with a situation centuries of slavery has produced, is one of the major challenges the governement faces. There are many who criticize the governement for not doing enough. On the other hand, you will see that in the government there are ex-slaves who have made it to become minister.
