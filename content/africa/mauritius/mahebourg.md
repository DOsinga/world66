Mahébourg is a fishing town on the southwest shore of Grand Port Bay, close to the international airport. It's one of the more authentic towns on the island — not heavily touristed, with a lively waterfront market and a real neighbourhood feel.

The National History Museum is the main attraction: it's housed in a French colonial mansion where the wounded commanders from the 1810 Battle of Grand Port (the only naval victory Napoleon's forces achieved over the British) were brought to recover. The collection includes cannon, armour, furniture, and maps from the colonial era. Free entry.

Blue Bay Marine Park, a short drive south, is the best snorkelling site on the east coast — a protected lagoon with exceptional coral and fish diversity. Most hotels and guesthouses in the area offer snorkelling and kayak rentals.

Mahébourg is a practical base if you're arriving or departing late from the airport, and a good base for exploring the south coast.
