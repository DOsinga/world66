Mauritius is a small volcanic island in the Indian Ocean, roughly 900 kilometres east of Madagascar. It's one of Africa's wealthiest and most stable countries, and it shows: the roads are good, the food is excellent, and the infrastructure built for tourism is among the best on the continent.

The island's appeal is obvious — turquoise lagoons, white sand beaches, dramatic mountains, and a warm multicultural society that blends Indian, African, Chinese, and French influences. But Mauritius is more than a beach destination. The interior offers hiking, waterfalls, tea plantations, volcanic craters, and Hindu temples surrounded by sugar cane fields.

At roughly 65 kilometres by 45 kilometres, the island is small enough to circle by car in a day, though you won't want to rush it. [Port Louis](/africa/mauritius/portlouis) is the capital and commercial hub, while the north coast around [Grand Baie](/africa/mauritius/grandbaie) is the main tourist strip. The south is wilder and less developed — better beaches, fewer crowds, more honest prices.

[Rodrigues](/africa/mauritius/rodrigues), 560 kilometres to the northeast, is a quieter dependency worth the trip if you have time.
