Amizmiz is a small Berber town about 55 kilometres south of Marrakech, sitting at the foot of the High Atlas mountains where the flat Haouz plain meets the first serious slopes. The population is predominantly Chleuh Berber, speaking Tashelhiyt, and the town serves as a gateway to the mountain villages above it.

The main reason to come here is the Tuesday souk, one of the largest and most authentic weekly markets in the Marrakech region. Berbers from the surrounding mountain villages descend to trade alongside local vendors, and the atmosphere is lively and entirely untouristic. Beyond market day, Amizmiz is a starting point for hikes into the High Atlas, where small Berber villages — some still without electricity or running water — offer a glimpse of a way of life that has changed remarkably little over centuries.
