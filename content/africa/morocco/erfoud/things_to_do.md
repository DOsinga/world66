---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Erfoud's appeal is less about the town itself and more about what surrounds it. The main square where Avenue Hassan II meets Avenue Mohammed V is the social hub — a good place to sit with a mint tea and watch the world go by. From the center, short walks lead to a couple of nearby kasbahs, and you can rent a bike to explore the palm groves and earthen fortresses that dot the surrounding oasis.

The big draw for most visitors is the nearby Erg Chebbi dunes at [Merzouga](/africa/morocco/merzouga), about 50 km south. Erfoud is also known for its marble-like fossil rock — the dark stone quarried here is packed with ancient marine fossils and sold in workshops and shops throughout town.
