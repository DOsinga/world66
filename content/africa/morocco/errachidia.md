Errachidia is a garrison town on the edge of the Sahara in eastern [Morocco](/africa/morocco), serving as the gateway to the Ziz Valley and the desert regions beyond. Most travellers pass through on their way to the dunes at Merzouga or Erg Chebbi, but the town itself has a certain austere charm and makes a practical overnight stop.

The Ziz Gorge, just north of town, is the main attraction in the immediate area — a dramatic canyon carved by the Ziz River through red and ochre rock, lined with date palm oases at its base. The views from the road above are impressive, particularly in the late afternoon light. South of Errachidia, the Ziz Valley opens into a string of ksour (fortified villages) and palmeries that stretch toward [Erfoud](/africa/morocco/erfoud) and the desert.

Errachidia has a functional centre with banks, a covered market, and several simple restaurants serving tagine and grilled meats. The town was built as a French military outpost and still has a garrison, which gives it a more orderly layout than most Moroccan towns of similar size. It is well connected by road and has a small airport with occasional domestic flights. If you are driving the route from [Fes](/africa/morocco/fes) to the Sahara, Errachidia is the natural place to break the journey.
