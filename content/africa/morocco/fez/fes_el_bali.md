The medieval medina, founded in 789, walled, and almost entirely closed to motor traffic — donkeys still do the heavy lifting on its 9,000-odd lanes. This is where the great monuments live (Al-Qarawiyyin, Bou Inania, the tanneries) but the experience is the labyrinth itself: blacksmiths and dyers and metal-beaters working in tiny shopfronts, food stalls steaming with harira and brochettes, hidden riads behind unmarked doors. Allow at least two full days. The main gates to enter by are **Bab Boujeloud** (the blue gate, the most famous) and **Bab Rcif**.
