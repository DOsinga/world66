Housed in the magnificently restored *Fondouk Nejjarine*, an eighteenth-century caravanserai for travelling merchants, this small museum is worth visiting as much for the building as for the collection of carved cedar doors, ploughs, musical instruments and Quranic stands. The rooftop café gives you a good view across the medina rooftops. On the small Place Nejjarine, in the carpenters' quarter.
