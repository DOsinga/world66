Imilchil is a remote Berber village in the High Atlas at about 2,200 metres, surrounded by some of the most dramatic mountain scenery in Morocco. It sits on a high plateau between the lakes of Isli and Tislit — two crater lakes associated with a local Romeo-and-Juliet legend of star-crossed lovers whose tears formed the waters.

The village is best known for the Moussem des Fiançailles, a betrothal festival held in late August or September in the nearby village of Ait Amer, about 20 km away. Berber families from across the region gather for several days of music, trading, and marriage negotiations. The festival has become increasingly commercialised and now overlaps with a mountain music festival, but it remains one of the most distinctive cultural events in Morocco.

Getting here is half the adventure. All routes to Imilchil involve long stretches of unpaved mountain road, and the village feels genuinely remote — bring enough cash, as facilities are minimal. Market day is Saturday, and that is when the village comes alive.
