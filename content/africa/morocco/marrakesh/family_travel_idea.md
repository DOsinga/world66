---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Marrakesh"
type: section
---

Marrakesh is a land of an interesting and deeply-fusionist culture that
manifests itself in the form of its architecture, religious structures, old
souks, ancient ruins, modern buildings, traditional medina squares and a range
of other attractions depicting the glory of the exotic Morocco. The
family-friendly African hotspot is a nice place to introduce the kids to a
‘Europe meets Africa’ cultural marriage. Here are some great ideas to enjoy a
family vacation in Marrakesh.

**Majorelle Gardens**

The lovely French-style botanical gardens, situated at Gueliz , are a
peaceful destination to explore a vast collection of rare and intriguing
botanical life from all over the planet. The place seems to house every variety
of cacti known to mankind. The pleasant tapestry of flowers and an education
center chronicling the evolution and facts about various species is a must see.
There is also a Museum of Islamic Art in the premises, which houses several
artifacts and items from the Arabic culture, ranging from religious scriptures
and paintings to local costumes and carved wooden handicrafts.

**White Water Rafting **

This is fantastic idea for families looking to go canoeing in the Atlas
Mountains. There are several trips organized by tour groups that leave daily for
fun-filled activities like canoeing, white water rafting, speed boating and a
couple of other adventure activities.

**Marrakesh Ciel d’Afrique Hot Air Ballooning**

Children will enjoy a hot air balloon ride to catch
breathtaking sky vistas of Marrakesh and its surrounding regions. The flight
transcends vast deserts, tiny local hamlets and old historical streets. Visitors
can enjoy a refreshing mint tea and nice homemade bread at a village after the
balloon ride.

**Ethno Art Shop**

This is like entering the magical treasure cave of Ali Baba from the Arabian
Nights. The Ethno Art Shop is great for art-oriented families. This is like a
museum showcasing several products from the artistic and cultural legacy of
Morocco. There are old carpets, rare paintings and wooden artifacts and--here’s
the best part--visitors can buy any of genuine items at unbeatable prices. The
sales people are extremely cordial and knowledgeable and will help you buy
rather than selling aggressively.

Marrakesh is an attractive, family-friendly bouquet of cultural attractions,
adventure sports, parks and art shops, all representng its diverse ethnic
spirit.
