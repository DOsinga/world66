A botanical garden designed by French painter Jacques Majorelle in the 1920s and 1930s, famous for its intense cobalt blue buildings (Majorelle blue) and vivid collection of cacti, bamboo, and exotic plants. Yves Saint Laurent and Pierre Bergé purchased the garden in 1980, restored it, and it is now one of Marrakesh's most visited sites. The garden houses the Berber Museum with a collection of traditional North African artefacts. Entrance fee applies; worth it.
