M'Hamid el Ghizlane is the last village before the Sahara on the Draa Valley road, about 100 km south of Zagora. The drive to get here is remarkable: you pass through Zagora (with its famous sign counting down the days to Timbuktu by camel), round the Jebel Zagora, follow the Draa River through a corridor of palm groves, ancient kasbahs, and ksour villages, and arrive at a small adobe settlement half-swallowed by sand.

The village has a Monday souk and a few basic guesthouses. From M'Hamid you can access the open desert on foot, by camel, or by 4WD. Overnight camel trips to dune bivouacs in Erg Chigaga — about 50 km west — are the main activity, and several local operators arrange them. The dunes at Erg Chigaga are larger and less visited than those at Merzouga.

The experience here is genuinely remote. Beyond M'Hamid the road ends, and the piste into the desert requires local knowledge or a guide.
