Mohammedia is a coastal city midway between [Casablanca](/africa/morocco/casablanca) (30 km south) and [Rabat](/africa/morocco/rabat) (60 km north). It began as a small Portuguese trading post and was known as Fedala until Moroccan independence. Today it serves largely as a beach getaway for Casablanca residents, with a long sandy coastline and a more relaxed pace than its larger neighbour.

The town has a pleasant corniche, a small kasbah near the port, and one of Morocco's few golf courses. The beach is popular but can be rough — the Atlantic swells here are strong. Mohammedia is also home to Morocco's largest oil refinery, which sits at the edge of town and is hard to miss.

For travellers, Mohammedia is more of a lunch stop or half-day detour than a destination in its own right, but the seafood restaurants along the port are good and considerably cheaper than Casablanca.
