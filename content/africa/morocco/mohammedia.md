Mohammedia is a mid-sized coastal city on the Atlantic between [Casablanca](/africa/morocco/casablanca) and [Rabat](/africa/morocco/rabat), about 30 kilometres north of Casablanca along the main coastal road and rail line. Once a sleepy fishing port called Fedala, it grew quickly in the 20th century around Morocco's main oil refinery and port, and today it's a mix of industrial outskirts and a genuinely pleasant seaside centre.

For most visitors, the draw is the beach. The long sweep of sand in front of the town is one of the most popular weekend escapes for Casablancans, and the seafront is lined with cafes, grilled-fish restaurants, and the local golf club. The tidy old medina and the small kasbah behind the port are worth a wander. Mohammedia isn't a place you cross Morocco to see, but if you're travelling between Casablanca and Rabat and want a breath of sea air, it's an easy half-day stop.
