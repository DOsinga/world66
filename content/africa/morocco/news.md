In Morocco French Newspapers are for sale everywhere, but it is not hard to get an English onbe either. The news is also broadcasted in French and Spanish so you may be able to get some of that as well. 

The Economist, Herald Tribune and many other good sources of news can be bought at the train station in Rabat and Casablanca.
 

The biggest news in Morocco this year-and perhaps this millenium- was the death of His Majesty the King Hassan II and the optimistic atmosphere for change brought on by the young, King Mohammed VI (who is still seen jet skiing down the Bougreg River on weekends). Here is an excerpt from his revolutionary speech promoting women from 8/20/99:
 

"...how can we experience complete development when our rural areas contend with problems forcing the inhabitants to abandon the land, which God has made a source of abundance, to go to the cities?
 ...How can we hope to achieve progress and prosperity when women, who constitute half of our population, see their interests scorned, without taking into account the rights which our noble religion has granted them along with men?
 ...We do not have a magic wand with which we can pretend to resolve all these problems and others, but we are firmly determined to face them..."
