The Kasbah Taourirt was the principal residence of the Glaoui clan, the powerful Berber family who controlled much of southern Morocco in the 19th and early 20th centuries. The great earthen fortress — a labyrinth of towers, courtyards, and living quarters built from pisé (rammed earth) — was partially restored with UNESCO assistance and is the most significant historic monument in Ouarzazate. A guided tour takes visitors through the decorated interior rooms. The Centre Artisanal opposite the kasbah sells local crafts.
