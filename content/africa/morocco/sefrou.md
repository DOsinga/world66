Sefrou is a small ancient walled town at the foothills of the Middle Atlas, 28 kilometres south of Fes. Most travellers pass it by — which is exactly what makes it worth stopping for. The medina is well-preserved and almost entirely free of tourist shops, giving you a genuine feel for a working Moroccan town.

The town has an unusually deep history. Long before the Arab conquest, Sefrou was home to a Berber-Jewish community. By the early 20th century, Jews made up roughly a third of the population. The Mellah — the Jewish quarter — survives with its distinctive tall houses and covered alleyways, though the community itself has largely gone.

Summers here are noticeably cooler than Fes, and the surrounding countryside is pleasant for walking. Springs and seasonal waterfalls sit just above the town. In June, Sefrou holds a cherry festival that draws Moroccans from across the region — if you happen to be passing through, it's worth timing your visit around it.
