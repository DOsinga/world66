Skoura is a small oasis town in the Draa Valley, about 40 km east of Ouarzazate, famous for its dense palm grove and the ancient kasbahs scattered within it. The town itself is unremarkable, but the palm grove that surrounds it is the point — a labyrinth of irrigation channels, mud-walled kasbahs, and shaded paths that rewards unhurried exploration.

The two most celebrated kasbahs are Amerhidil and Dar Ait Sidi El Mati, both on the north bank of the Dades river. They are not easy to find on your own, which is part of the appeal. Hiring a local guide for a half-day walk through the grove is genuinely worthwhile — not a tourist performance, but a practical necessity in a place where every path looks like every other path. Guides can be arranged informally in town.

The drive from Ouarzazate is mostly flat and undistinguished, but about 19 km out you pass Restaurant du Lac, where the road skirts close to the Dades and the views across the lake toward a half-submerged kasbah are worth a stop (open May to September).
