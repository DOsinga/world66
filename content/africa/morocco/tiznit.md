Tiznit is a walled town in southern Morocco, known for its ochre ramparts, silver jewellery, and relaxed atmosphere. It sits at a crossroads between the Souss plain and the Anti-Atlas, making it a natural stopping point on the way south from [Agadir](/africa/morocco/agadir).

The medina is compact and unhurried. The market square, where intercity buses pull in, is the centre of daily life. Inside the walls you will find silversmiths — Tiznit has been Morocco's centre for Berber silver jewellery for centuries — along with a historic spring (the Source Bleue) and a mosque whose minaret owes more to sub-Saharan West African styles than to typical Moroccan architecture.

From here you can head southeast to [Tafraoute](/africa/morocco/tafraoute) in the Anti-Atlas, west to the beach at Aglou Plage, south to Sidi Ifni on the coast, or continue further south towards Guelmim and the Western Sahara.
