The Todra Gorge cuts through the eastern edge of the High Atlas mountains near the town of Tinghir, its walls rising nearly 300 metres above a narrow river bed. At the tightest point the canyon is only ten metres wide, with sheer red and orange limestone faces pressing in on either side. It's one of the most dramatic landscapes in Morocco, and deservedly popular.

Most visitors stay at one of the small hotels clustered at the mouth of the gorge — there are plenty of options ranging from basic to comfortable, with a handful of restaurants nearby. If you're a rock climber, Todra is one of the best spots in North Africa: the walls are studded with pre-anchored routes at all grades, and the setting is extraordinary. Even non-climbers will enjoy walking the road through the canyon itself, watching the light shift across the rock faces as the day progresses.
