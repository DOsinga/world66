---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

**Independence Day (June 25)** is the biggest national celebration. Mozambique won independence from Portugal in 1975 after a decade-long armed struggle, and the day is marked with pride — military parades, music, and public gatherings in cities and towns across the country. In Maputo, concerts run into the night at the national stadium.

**National Day (October 7)** commemorates the start of the armed struggle for independence in 1964. A quieter public holiday but still widely observed.

**Marrabenta Festival** celebrates Mozambique's own musical genre — marrabenta, a rolling, syncopated guitar sound that developed in Maputo's townships under colonial rule. The festival typically runs in Maputo in late January or February and draws performers from across the country. It's the best introduction to Mozambican music you'll find.

**Mozambique Jazz Festival** has been held in Maputo in April, featuring both local and international acts. The venue and dates shift year to year — check local listings closer to your visit.

**Chopi Timbila Festival** in Inhambane province celebrates the xylophone orchestras of the Chopi people, whose music is on UNESCO's intangible heritage list. The timbila orchestras perform complex compositions with up to 50 players — a genuinely remarkable sound. Ask locally for timing, as the festival doesn't run on a fixed international calendar.

**New Year's Eve** is celebrated with real enthusiasm on the beach in [Tofo](/africa/mozambique/tofo) and along Maputo's waterfront, with beach parties running through the night. It's a major domestic holiday and resorts fill up — book well ahead.
