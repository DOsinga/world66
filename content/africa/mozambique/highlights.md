---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Mozambique is southern Africa's great coastal secret — a 2,500 km shoreline of white-sand beaches, warm Indian Ocean water, and coral reefs that still feel genuinely unspoiled. It gets a fraction of the visitors that Tanzania or Kenya draw, which means you can have beaches to yourself that would be packed elsewhere.

**Diving and marine life** are the primary reasons most people come. The waters off [Tofo](/africa/mozambique/tofo) near [Inhambane](/africa/mozambique/inhambane) are among the best places on earth to swim with whale sharks — from October to March, groups of 30 or more gather in the bay, feeding on plankton at the surface. Humpback whales migrate through from June to November. The Bazaruto Archipelago offers world-class reef diving with potato bass, manta rays, turtles, and one of the last healthy dugong populations in the Indian Ocean.

**The Bazaruto Archipelago** — a chain of five protected islands off [Vilanculos](/africa/mozambique/vilanculos) — is Mozambique's most visited destination and still manages to feel remote. Two Mile Reef between Bazaruto and Benguerra islands is exceptional diving. The islands are accessible by light aircraft or boat from Vilanculos.

**Gorongosa National Park** is one of Africa's great conservation comeback stories. Devastated by the civil war (1977–1992), the park has been painstakingly restored with international support and now has thriving populations of elephant, lion, buffalo, hippo, and a remarkable range of bird life. It's a genuine wilderness experience without the vast crowds of East African parks.

**Ilha de Mozambique (Mozambique Island)** in the north is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — a tiny coral island connected to the mainland by a causeway, with a perfectly preserved Portuguese colonial town: mosques, churches, fortresses, and painted merchant houses layered on top of each other over five centuries. It's unlike anywhere else in Africa.

**[Maputo](/africa/mozambique/maputo)** is a real city with good food, lively music, and an easy confidence that comes from being Africa's southernmost capital. The Portuguese tile work, the harbour promenade, the Mercado Central, the late-night bars — it's a good place to start or end a trip.

**The Quirimbas Archipelago** in the far north, around [Pemba](/africa/mozambique/pemba), is harder to reach but rewards the effort with pristine reefs, remote dhow sailing, and a Swahili culture quite different from the south. Note that parts of Cabo Delgado province have seen security problems since 2017 — check current travel advisories before heading north of Pemba.

Mozambique isn't a destination for everyone. Roads are rough, infrastructure is basic outside major lodges, and the distances are punishing. But if you want stunning coastline, excellent diving, and a country that hasn't yet been smoothed out for mass tourism, it's hard to beat.
