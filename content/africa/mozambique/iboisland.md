Ibo is one of the original Swahili trading settlements of the East African coast, now a quiet, crumbling jewel in the Quirimbas Archipelago. The island has barely 3,000 inhabitants, a handful of beautifully restored colonial mansions repurposed as lodges, and the ruins of three Portuguese forts including the large Fort São João, built in 1791.

Walking Ibo is the point. Silver jewellery has been crafted here for centuries — the island's silversmiths work with scrap metal and old coins, creating intricate pieces using techniques passed down through generations. The mangrove channels surrounding the island make for excellent kayaking and dhow sailing, and the birdlife is exceptional.

Getting here requires effort: fly to [Pemba](/africa/mozambique/pemba), then take a small boat or charter plane to the island. Most visitors stay at Ibo Island Lodge or the boutique Ibo Safari Lodge — both occupying beautifully restored colonial buildings — and this is very much a destination for those who've planned it in advance. Note that parts of Cabo Delgado province have seen security issues since 2017; check current travel advisories before visiting.
