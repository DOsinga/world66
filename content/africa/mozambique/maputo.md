**Maputo** is the capital and largest city of **Mozambique**, set on the shore of **Maputo Bay** in the far south of the country, close to the borders of South Africa and Eswatini (Swaziland). It was known until 1976 as **Lourenço Marques**, named after the 16th-century Portuguese explorer who first landed here, and grew up in the 19th century as a port for exporting goods from the South African Rand. The Portuguese turned it into their colonial capital, leaving a grid of wide tree-lined avenues, Art Deco apartment blocks, and the unmistakable architecture of Portuguese Africa; the long civil war that followed independence in 1975 devastated the economy and the city, but the past two decades have seen a slow, uneven recovery. Maputo is still the most cosmopolitan and Westernised city in the country, and for most visitors the only one they pass through.

The city's signature sight is the **Railway Station** (Estação Central dos Caminhos de Ferro), designed in part by the firm of **Gustave Eiffel** and completed in 1910, often cited as one of the most beautiful railway stations in the world — a wedding-cake confection of pale green domes, wrought iron and marble that is now used as much as a filming location and wedding venue as a functioning station. The nearby **Praça da Independência** holds the **Maputo City Hall**, the **Maputo Cathedral** (a striking 1944 Art Deco building), and the pink-and-white **Iron House** (*Casa de Ferro*), a prefabricated iron house that was supposed to be the governor-general's residence and turned out to be uninhabitable in the African heat. The **Museu Nacional de Arte** has a good collection of 20th-century Mozambican painting, including works by **Malangatana Ngwenya**, the country's most famous artist. And the sprawling **Mercado Central** is the best place in the city for fish, fruit, and atmosphere.

Beyond the city itself, **Inhaca Island** — a two-hour ferry ride across Maputo Bay — has good beaches, a marine reserve, and a handful of small resorts, and makes a pleasant overnight escape. For longer trips, Maputo is the entry point for the spectacular southern Mozambique coast: **Ponta do Ouro**, the dolphin-diving village on the South African border, and the beaches around **Inhambane** further north. Mozambican **piri-piri prawns** are legendary, and eating them with a cold **Laurentina** beer at one of the open-air grills along the Costa do Sol is the essential Maputo experience.
