---
title: "Top 5 Must-Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Swim with whale sharks at Tofo**

The waters off [Tofo Beach](/africa/mozambique/tofo) near [Inhambane](/africa/mozambique/inhambane) are one of the world's best places to encounter whale sharks. From October to March, seasonal plankton blooms bring dozens — sometimes over 50 — of these enormous, gentle fish to the surface. Multiple dive centres in Tofo run snorkelling excursions, no scuba experience needed. Even without whale sharks, the diving here is outstanding: manta ray cleaning stations, humpback whales (June to November), and healthy reef systems.

**2. Island-hop the Bazaruto Archipelago**

The five islands of the Bazaruto National Park, accessible from [Vilanculos](/africa/mozambique/vilanculos), are the most visited part of Mozambique for good reason. Two Mile Reef between Bazaruto and Benguerra Islands offers exceptional diving with dugongs, reef sharks, manta rays, and dense coral. Dhow sailing between islands over turquoise water is one of those experiences that stays with you. Most lodges here are in the luxury bracket, but the setting justifies it.

**3. Visit Ilha de Mozambique**

Mozambique Island in the north is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and genuinely one of Africa's most extraordinary places. This tiny coral island — just 3 km long — holds five centuries of layered history: a Portuguese fort from 1507, mosques, Hindu temples, a colonial governor's palace, and crumbling merchant houses. It was the capital of Portuguese East Africa for over 300 years. Wander without a plan — every street has something unexpected.

**4. Safari in Gorongosa National Park**

Gorongosa was one of Africa's finest game parks before the civil war turned it into a battlefield. The restoration effort, backed by the Carr Foundation and the Mozambican government, has been remarkable. Elephant, lion, hippo, buffalo, wild dog, and over 500 bird species now thrive in the park. It doesn't have the famous density of East Africa's parks, but it's a genuinely wild, immersive experience — and you'll share it with far fewer tourists.

**5. Eat peri-peri prawns by the ocean**

Mozambique's signature dish — huge prawns grilled over charcoal and basted with peri-peri sauce — is best eaten at a simple beachside restaurant with your feet in the sand and a cold Laurentina in hand. [Maputo](/africa/mozambique/maputo)'s seafood restaurants serve excellent versions, but so does almost any coastal town. Don't leave without eating this multiple times.
