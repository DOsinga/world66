Vilanculos is the main gateway to the Bazaruto Archipelago and, for many visitors, the highlight of a Mozambique trip. It's a small, relaxed coastal town that has grown considerably with tourism without losing its easy character. The beach in town isn't the finest — you're here to reach the islands — but the sunsets over the bay are spectacular.

Most travellers pass through Vilanculos on the way to Bazaruto or Benguerra Island, staying a night or two to sort logistics. The town has a good spread of accommodation, from backpacker lodges to mid-range hotels, plus restaurants serving excellent fresh seafood. The local market is worth a wander.

**Getting there**: SA Airlink flies direct from Johannesburg to Vilanculos Airport, a short distance from town. From Maputo, it's a long drive up the EN1 or a connection via Johannesburg. Charter flights also operate from Inhambane.

**To the islands**: Boats and light aircraft to the Bazaruto Archipelago are arranged through lodges and local operators. Most island lodges handle transfers as part of their package — sort it before you arrive.
