---
title: "Food and Drink"
type: section
---

Namibian food is shaped by three distinct traditions: the meat-heavy braai culture shared with southern Africa, German colonial baking and brewing, and indigenous staples that go back centuries.

**Meat** is central. Namibian beef is rightly famous — cattle graze on open savannah without antibiotics or hormones, and the quality shows. Braai (barbecue) is the national pastime. Game meat — kudu, oryx, springbok, warthog — is widely available at lodges and restaurants, often as grilled steaks or potjiekos (slow-cooked stew in a cast-iron pot over an open fire). Biltong, dried and spiced game or beef strips, is sold everywhere and makes excellent road-trip food.

**Kapana** is the street food of [Windhoek](/africa/namibia/windhoek): grilled beef chopped on a wooden block, seasoned with chilli and salt, eaten standing up at a market stall. The main kapana market is in the Katutura township. It's cheap, delicious, and a genuine local experience.

**German heritage** is everywhere in the towns. Swakopmund in particular is full of bakeries producing excellent Brötchen, Schwarzbrot, and pastries. The coffee is reliably good. Several places still serve Kaffee und Kuchen (coffee and cake) in the European tradition. The German influence also left Namibia with a brewing tradition — Windhoek Lager is a clean, Czech-style pilsner brewed to the German Reinheitsgebot purity law and widely available.

**Oysters from Lüderitz** are a highlight. Farmed in the cold, clean waters of the Atlantic, Lüderitz oysters are excellent and often available in Windhoek and Swakopmund restaurants. If you're passing through [Lüderitz](/africa/namibia/luderitz), eat them straight from the source.

**Pap** (mealie porridge) with a stew of meat, tomato and onion is the everyday meal for most Namibians. In northern areas, oshifima — a stiffer millet porridge — is the staple, served with spinach, game, or dried fish.

**Mopane worms** — caterpillars from mopane trees, dried or fried — are a protein-rich snack popular in the north. They taste like crunchy, earthy chips. Worth trying once.

Seafood along the coast is good and underrated. Hake, kabeljou, and kingklip are the main fish; crayfish (rock lobster) and line fish are served at coastal restaurants in Walvis Bay and Swakopmund.
