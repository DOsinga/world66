Outjo is a small, dusty town in northern [Namibia](/africa/namibia) that serves as the main gateway to [Etosha National Park](/africa/namibia/etosha). Most travellers pass through on their way to the park's Anderson Gate, about 100 kilometres to the north, but Outjo is worth a stop in its own right — if only to stock up on supplies and get a feel for small-town Namibian life.

The town has a Germanic colonial character typical of northern Namibia, with a few well-preserved buildings from the early 1900s and a small but friendly main street. The Naua Naua Game Lodge on the outskirts and several guesthouses provide comfortable bases for Etosha-bound travellers. The town's bakeries are well known locally — the Bäckerei Müller has been baking German-style bread and pastries since the colonial era.

If you are driving the classic Namibian circuit from [Windhoek](/africa/namibia/windhoek) north to Etosha, Outjo sits at a natural stopping point on the B1/C38 route. Stock up on water, fuel, and food here — facilities inside Etosha are limited and expensive.
