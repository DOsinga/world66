Tsumeb is the main town in northern Namibia and the practical gateway to Etosha National Park, roughly 70km to the west. It's a working mining town — copper, lead, zinc, and silver have been extracted here since German colonial times — and a useful stop for fuel, supplies, and a night's rest before or after Etosha.

The **Tsumeb Museum** is worth a visit, housed in the old German private school built in 1915. It covers the town's mining history and features one of the world's best collections of Tsumeb minerals — the mine has produced over 220 different mineral species, many found nowhere else. German colonial artifacts from the 1904-08 period are also displayed.

**Oshikoto Lake**, 24km south of town, is a circular collapse lake in the dolomite — unusual enough to warrant a quick stop. German colonial forces dumped weapons into it during World War I, and some have been recovered and are displayed at the museum.

Tsumeb has a handful of hotels and guesthouses, several restaurants, and good supermarkets. Fill up your fuel tank here before heading into Etosha.
