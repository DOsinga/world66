---

Housed in the old German private school building from 1915, this is a surprisingly good museum for a small mining town. The main draw is the mineralogy collection: dazzling specimens of dioptase, tsumebite, and dozens of other rare minerals extracted from the mine over a century of operation. A separate section covers German colonial history in the region, including artifacts from the 1904–08 Herero and Nama genocide period. Weapons recovered from Oshikoto Lake — dumped there by retreating German forces in World War I — are also on display.
