Walvis Bay is Namibia's main port and the only deep-water harbour on the country's coastline. The town sits on a sheltered lagoon that attracts enormous flocks of flamingos — sometimes tens of thousands at a time — along with pelicans, terns and other seabirds. The lagoon and the adjacent salt pans are a Ramsar wetland site and one of the most important coastal bird habitats in southern Africa.

The town itself is functional rather than charming, built around the harbour and the fishing industry. But it makes a good base for exploring the surrounding coast: Sandwich Harbour, a remote lagoon where towering dunes meet the Atlantic surf, is accessible by 4x4 from here, and kayaking trips among the seals and dolphins in the bay are popular. [Swakopmund](/africa/namibia/swakopmund), with more to offer in terms of restaurants and atmosphere, is just 30 km to the north.
