---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Walvis Bay's main attraction is its lagoon — a shallow, sheltered estuary that is one of the most important wetlands in sub-Saharan Africa. The lagoon holds enormous flocks of flamingos year-round (sometimes over 50,000 birds), along with pelicans, cormorants, and scores of wader species. The walkway along the waterfront is one of the best bird-watching spots in Namibia.

Several local operators run morning catamaran or ski-boat tours on the lagoon. You'll encounter colonies of Cape fur seals, bottlenose dolphins are common, and humpback and southern right whales pass through from August to October. Most tours end with fresh oysters and sparkling wine — Lüderitz oysters farmed in these cold Atlantic waters are excellent.

The salt pans south of town produce much of southern Africa's salt and are worth a look from the road — pink with halophytic algae and often covered in wading birds.

**Dune 7**, just east of town, is one of the highest dunes accessible by standard vehicle in Namibia and offers sweeping views over the lagoon and town. It's a popular spot for sandboarding.
