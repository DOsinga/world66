title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Namibia can be visited year-round, but the dry season — **May to October** — is the best time for most travellers.

## Dry Season (May–October)

This is winter in the southern hemisphere, and Namibia's cooler, cloudless months. Days are warm and sunny (15–25°C), nights can be cold, and in the desert it gets genuinely freezing after dark — pack layers even for a June or July trip.

Wildlife viewing is outstanding from June through October. As water sources dry up, animals concentrate around the permanent waterholes in Etosha. Vegetation is sparse, making game easier to spot. The light is also extraordinary — clear skies and low humidity are perfect for photography.

August is the peak tourist month. Book accommodation well ahead, especially at Etosha rest camps and Sesriem for Sossusvlei.

## Green Season (November–April)

Summer brings heat and afternoon thunderstorms, mostly in the north and east. The Namib Desert and Atlantic coast remain relatively dry year-round. Rain transforms the landscape dramatically — the parched brown plains turn green, migrant birds arrive in enormous numbers, and the dunes at Sossusvlei take on an even more intense colour after rain.

Wildlife is harder to spot (thick vegetation, animals dispersed), but the scenery is beautiful and prices are lower. November and April are shoulder months with reasonable weather and fewer visitors.

The Kalahari in the east is striking in green season, with desert-adapted wildlife and wildflowers after rain.

## The Namib Desert

The Namib is uniquely moderated by Atlantic fog and upwellings — temperatures at the coast (Swakopmund, Walvis Bay) are cool and overcast year-round, rarely exceeding 20°C. A sweater is always needed. Inland in the dune sea, temperatures climb sharply in summer; visit the dunes at Sossusvlei in the cooler months for more comfortable conditions, though they're accessible all year.

## Fish River Canyon

The famous multi-day hiking trail in Fish River Canyon is only open **May to September** — the river is impassable in the summer rains and extreme heat makes hiking dangerous. Viewpoint visits are possible year-round.
