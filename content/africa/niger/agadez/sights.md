---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Agadez rewards a few days of wandering. The main sights are compact enough to explore on foot, and the city's atmosphere — desert light, mud-brick architecture, turbaned Tuareg in the alleys — is itself the main attraction.

The towering minaret of the Grande Mosquée is the city's most recognizable landmark, visible from almost anywhere. The camel market operates in the early morning and is best before 11am, when it winds down. The old city's narrow streets reward aimless exploration, and the silversmith workshops near the market are where you'll find authentic Croix d'Agadez jewelry — one of the most distinctive crafts of the Sahara.
