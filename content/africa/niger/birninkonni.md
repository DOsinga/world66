Birnin Konni is a busy market town in southern [Niger](/africa/niger), sitting right on the crossroads where the road from [Agadez](/africa/niger/agadez) to [Sokoto](/africa/nigeria/sokoto) meets the east-west route between [Zinder](/africa/niger/zinder) and [Niamey](/africa/niger/niamey). Its position near the [Nigerian](/africa/nigeria) border gives it a lively, trader-town energy.

The town does not have many sights, but that is not the point. Birnin Konni is an easy-going place to break a journey, stock up on supplies and watch the flow of cross-border commerce. Petrol is cheap here -- smuggled across from Nigeria, it sells at some of the lowest prices in Niger. The market is worth a wander for everyday goods that become harder to find further north in the desert.
