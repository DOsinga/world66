Maradi is Niger's third-largest city, located in the fertile south near the Nigerian border. It's primarily an agricultural and trading centre — groundnut farming made it one of the more prosperous cities in colonial times, and cross-border trade with Nigeria continues to drive its economy.

As a destination, Maradi is a stopover rather than a destination. The market is lively and has cheap goods smuggled in from Nigeria. The Maison des Chefs is a reasonable example of Hausa architecture, but if that's what you're after, [Zinder](/africa/niger/zinder) has far finer examples and is worth the detour.
