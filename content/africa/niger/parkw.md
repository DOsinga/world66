W National Park — named for the double bend the Niger River makes through it — is one of West Africa's finest wildlife reserves and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It straddles three countries: [Niger](/africa/niger), [Burkina Faso](/africa/burkinafaso), and [Benin](/africa/benin), with the Niger side offering the most accessible entry point and some of the wildest terrain.

The park is home to lions, leopards, cheetahs, elephants, hippos, giraffes, and buffalo, along with an exceptional array of antelope species and over 350 recorded bird species. The landscape shifts between dense gallery forest along the river, open savanna, and laterite plateaus — a variety that makes it compelling even outside the main game-viewing hours.

The best time to visit is March to May, at the end of the dry season, when animals concentrate around shrinking waterholes and are easy to spot in the thinning vegetation. Avoid the rainy season (June to September), when many tracks become impassable and the park is effectively closed to visitors.

Tours and vehicle hire can be arranged from [Niamey](/africa/niger/niamey), around 150 km to the northwest. The park headquarters are at Tapoa, where there is a small guesthouse. Bring everything you need — supplies are minimal inside the park.
