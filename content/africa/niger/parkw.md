W National Park (Parc du Double Vé) straddles three countries — Niger, [Burkina Faso](/africa/burkinafaso), and [Benin](/africa/benin) — and is most easily accessed from the Niger side. It's one of the finest wildlife parks in West Africa, home to lions, elephants, hippos, giraffes, buffalo, and a remarkable variety of antelope and birdlife.

The best time to visit is March to May at the end of the dry season, when the animals concentrate around shrinking waterholes and are easier to spot. The park is largely deserted in the rainy season (June-September) when roads become impassable.

Tours can be arranged from [Niamey](/africa/niger/niamey), which is around 150 km to the northwest. The park headquarters are at Tapoa.
