---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The best time to visit Niger is the cool dry season from November to February. Days are warm and sunny (25–30°C), nights can be genuinely cold in the desert north, and the dust that blankets everything in other seasons is at its least oppressive. This is when travel on desert roads is easiest and when the Air Mountains and Agadez are at their most accessible.

December and January are the prime months. If you want to visit [Agadez](/africa/niger/agadez) or do any desert travel in the [Tenere](/africa/niger/teneredesert) or [Air Mountains](/africa/niger/airmountains), plan around these months.

## The Hot Season (March–May)

Avoid it if you can. Temperatures climb above 40°C by March and can hit 48°C in April and May. The harmattan wind picks up, filling the air with fine Saharan dust that reduces visibility to near zero and covers everything in a gritty film. Travel is exhausting and dehydration is a constant risk.

## The Rainy Season (June–September)

Rain transforms the south. The Sahel turns briefly green and the landscape becomes genuinely beautiful. However, many roads — particularly in rural areas — become impassable. Flooding is common. The rainy season also coincides with peak malaria transmission.

The one reason to consider coming in September is the Cure Salée festival at [Ingal](/africa/niger/ingal), where the Tuareg and Wodaabe gather at the end of the rains for camel races, the Gerewol courtship ceremony, and days of celebration. It's one of the most remarkable cultural events in all of Africa, and worth planning a trip around.

## October

October is a pleasant shoulder month — the rains have ended, the bush is still green, and the heat hasn't fully returned. Roads are drying out but often still muddy in places.
