Asaba is the capital of Delta State, perched on the western bank of the River Niger in southern Nigeria. The Niger Bridge connecting Asaba to Onitsha on the eastern bank is one of Nigeria's most important crossings — a landmark that links the west and east of the country.

Since becoming the state capital, Asaba has grown rapidly to over two million people, with modern developments, hotels, and commercial activity transforming what was once a quiet riverine town. The city is a useful stopover for travelers crossing between western and eastern Nigeria, and the riverside setting has a certain appeal despite the traffic and bustle.
