---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The dry season (November to March) is the most comfortable time to visit, avoiding the heavy rains that peak from June to September.

Asaba has a rich calendar of traditional festivals. **Ulo**, held every two years in July, is a cleansing ceremony to drive out evil spirits and misfortune. **Aja** ("sacrifice") takes place annually in August to mark the beginning of the harvest. **Iwaji** in September celebrates the new yam harvest. **Ine** (or Ekwensu) is a five-day celebration with one day for each of Asaba's five villages, featuring military-style parades and war dances. **Oli Oma** closes the harvest season with family sacrifices honoring deceased mothers.
