---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Benin City is not a major shopping destination, but its markets are lively and worth exploring. Most trade happens in open-air markets selling food, household goods, and everyday essentials. For visitors, the main draw is local craftsmanship — particularly bronze casting, woodcarving, and traditional beadwork that carry on techniques from the Benin Empire.

The area around Igun Street is famous for its bronze casters, many of whom belong to guilds that trace their lineage back centuries. You can watch artisans at work and buy pieces directly. Prices are negotiable and quality varies, so take your time. Oba Market and New Benin Market are the largest general markets, bustling and chaotic, where you will find everything from foodstuffs to fabric.
