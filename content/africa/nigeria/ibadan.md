Ibadan is the capital of Oyo State and one of the largest cities in West Africa. Built across a rippling plain of eight rocky hills, it sits about 100 kilometers northeast of Lagos in southwestern Nigeria. The city is a striking study in contrasts: one side of a road can be home to modern concrete buildings, while the other is a dense cluster of traditional mud houses accessible only by footpaths.

For visitors who want to experience how traditional Yoruba culture coexists with modern Nigerian life, Ibadan delivers. The ancient quarters feel like a different world from the newer parts of town, yet they exist side by side. The University of Ibadan, the country's first university and alma mater of Nobel laureate Wole Soyinka, anchors the city's intellectual life. Cocoa House, once the tallest building in tropical Africa, remains a landmark of the skyline.

The city is becoming more cosmopolitan, with Lebanese and Asian business communities adding to the mix, but it still moves at a slower pace than Lagos. What Ibadan lacks in flash it makes up for in hospitality and character. If you want to understand Nigeria beyond the chaos of Lagos, this is a good place to start.
