---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

The main attraction of Ibadan is the whole city and the locals. Of course there are particular places to visit such as the palace of the **Olubadan** (the King) of Ibadan and **University of Ibadan** which has a *zoo*, but this is more due to their historical and academic significance than anything else.

  Ibadan is still struggling with itself as to whether to be modern and cosmopolitan or ancient and conservative. As a result the city's attempts to bring about modern attractions that abound in Lagos are hardly patronized by the locals and found to be desolate by foreigners.

  Other sights worth visiting are **Agodi Zoological Gardens** and the **International Institute of Tropical Agriculture**.

  For those who wish to mix with the indigenes during their festivities they should visit **Cocoa House**, which also has a nice restaurant and a dancing hall for when musicians come to play, **Liberty Stadium** for those interested in fooball, **Ibadan Recreation Centre** and **Gbagi Market**.
