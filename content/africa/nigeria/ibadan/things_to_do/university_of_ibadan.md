Nigeria's first university and one of the most important in West Africa. The campus is worth visiting for its Institute of African Studies, which houses a museum of bronze carvings, statues, and other African artifacts (open Monday to Friday, 10am to 3:30pm). The university's zoological garden showcases native plants and animals. Nobel laureate Wole Soyinka studied here.
