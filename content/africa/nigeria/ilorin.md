Ilorin is the capital of Kwara State, sitting at the crossroads between Nigeria's predominantly Muslim north and its Christian south. That position has given the city a distinctive character -- the Emir's palace anchors a traditional Yoruba-Islamic culture, while the University of Ilorin campus brings a younger, more cosmopolitan energy.

The city sprawls across a gently rolling landscape on the southern edge of the savanna. The central market is vast and chaotic, worth a visit for the textiles alone. Ilorin is not a typical tourist destination, but it is a genuine window into middle-belt Nigerian life -- hospitable, affordable, and far from the pressures of Lagos.
