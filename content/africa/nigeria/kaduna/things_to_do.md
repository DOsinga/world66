---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Kaduna is not overflowing with conventional sights, but the city has a few spots worth seeking out, mostly tied to its colonial and river history.

**River Gardens** sit along the Kaduna River and make for a pleasant afternoon. The old iron footbridge built by Lord Lugard in 1882 is still standing, and there is a swimming pool. **Lugard Hall**, the former seat of the Northern Nigerian legislature, is an imposing colonial-era building that speaks to the city's political past.

If you want to burn off energy, the **Kaduna Club** has tennis courts and sits next to a polo field where you can arrange horseback riding even if polo is not your thing. Membership is technically required, but in practice a bit of friendliness and some naira will get you in.
