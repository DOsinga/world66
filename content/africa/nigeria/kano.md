Kano is the largest city in northern Nigeria and the capital of Kano State — a Hausa trading city that has been a major commercial crossroads since at least the tenth century. It sits at the edge of the Sahel, with historical and cultural ties reaching across the Sahara as far as North Africa and the Middle East. If you are visiting Nigeria and have any interest in Islamic culture, history, or traditional craft, Kano is the most rewarding city in the country.

The Old City is the heart of the visit. Its ancient earthen walls still stand in places, pierced by 16 gates, and within them you find the Emir’s Palace, the Central Mosque, the Gidan Makama Museum, and the vast Kurmi Market — one of the oldest in sub-Saharan Africa. The Kofar Mata dye pits, where indigo cloth has been dyed for centuries, are an extraordinary sight still very much in use today. The Gidan Dan Hausa, an old colonial governor’s mansion, shows the striking fusion of Hausa and Islamic architectural styles.

Day excursions from Kano can be made to Katsina, the traditional Hausa town of Daura, the rock paintings at Birnin Kudu, and [Sokoto](/africa/nigeria/sokoto), which has a sultan’s palace and good market for leather goods. Kano is 835 km northeast of [Lagos](/africa/nigeria/lagos).

Kano is one of the most rewarding cities to visit in Nigeria. It has been one of the most important trading towns for the Hausa, who built a powerful empire in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries and remain one of the most influential peoples in the country. On the edge of the Sahel, Kano has deep cultural and historical ties to the communities of the southern Sahara.

It is the third-largest city in Nigeria and has grown at a frenzied pace, but despite the noise and crowds it is far more bearable than [Lagos](/africa/nigeria/lagos). The **Old City** is the heart of Kano, still ringed by ancient walls with sixteen gates. Inside, the massive **Kurmi Market** sprawls through the streets — one of the great markets of West Africa. The **Central Mosque**, near the market, is closed to non-Muslim visitors but worth seeing from outside, especially during Friday prayers. Across from the mosque stands the **Emir's Palace**, which you cannot enter without a personal invitation, though watching the local dignitaries arrive is entertainment enough.

The **Gidan Makama Museum**, opposite the palace, has fine historical and craft exhibits and helpful staff. At the **Kofar Mata dye pits**, near the old city gate, indigo cloth has been dyed for centuries — these are said to be the oldest dye pits in Africa. The **Gidan Dan Hausa**, an old colonial governor's mansion, is worth a visit for its unusual blend of Hausa and Islamic architecture.

Excursions from Kano can take you to Katsina, Daura (a traditional Hausa town), Birnin Kudu (rock paintings), and [Sokoto](/africa/nigeria/sokoto), which has a sultan's palace and a good market for leather goods. Kano lies 835 km northeast of Lagos.
