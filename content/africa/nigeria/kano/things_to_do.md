---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Kano's most compelling attractions are concentrated in the Old City, where you can walk through ancient city gates, watch indigo cloth being dyed in pits that have been operating for centuries, and observe courtly life around the Emir's Palace.

**Kofar Mata Dye Pits** are the oldest in Africa and still working. Located at the Kofar Mata Gate at the edge of the old walled city, you can watch cloth being dyed in vivid indigo using techniques unchanged for hundreds of years.

**Gidan Makama Museum**, across the street from the Emir's Palace, is one of the better museums in West Africa. It holds historic and craft exhibits on Hausa culture. Staff are genuinely helpful and a tip at the end is expected.

**Emir's Palace** is not normally open to visitors, but you can request permission from the office on the far side of the main square. Even without access, watching the local dignitaries arrive to pay court is entertaining in itself.

**Central Mosque**, a large modern structure near the Palace, is off-limits to non-Muslims but worth seeing from the outside, especially during the Friday service.

**Kurmi Market**, within the old walls, is one of the oldest and largest markets in sub-Saharan Africa — leather goods, cloth, spices, and crafts sold in a warren of stalls.
