---
title: "Shopping in Lagos"
type: section
---

**Lagos shopping** includes large malls and markets. Buy souvenirs for home range from local artistry to modern gadgets for purchase. Visit the mega malls in Lagos for all day entertainment and shopping. The outlets of Park ‘N’ Shop offer many low priced deals in food and hard goods. If looking for imported items, these are more expensive than what it costs in other countries.

**The Palms Shopping Centre**

Visit a multi-store mall in Lekki with over 60 stores to shop. It is the largest mall in Nigeria and has a 6-screen cinema to catch a movie after shopping. Buy African fashion, purchase electronics, or see the latest in lighting and furniture items. Much of the fashion in Nigeria is based on Western design with jeans and tops.

**Park ‘N’ Shop**

Located on Victoria Island, Park ‘n’ Shop has just about everything in the outlets. Pick up a bottle of wine to bring back home or enjoy while visiting Nigeria. Purchase travel gear, fabrics, furniture, or lighting. Park ‘N’ Shop offers it all and can take a day to look through the stores that are situated there.

**Yaba Market**

Buy local food items at Yaba Market. Stalls line the market area, located on Tejuosho Street. The market is on the mainland and is a central location for many visitors to buy food items and other things. Browse through the household items, hair accessories, and electronics.

**Lagos City Mall**

With over 50 shops, Lagos City Mall has a lot for visitors to browse. The mall has jewelry, furniture, children’s clothes, and several other shops. Entertainment is available with a cinema. Book stores are also located in this mall to pick up a magazine or book when going to the beach. Buy souvenirs here with glass gifts and electronics.

**La Pointe Supermarket**

The La Pointe supermarket carries many Nigerian products including fresh milk. Food items include French and Lebanese cheese, canned goods, and cold meats. Pick up wine for the evening at the La Pointe to enjoy while visiting Nigeria. Some imported items are stocked on the shelves at La Pointe.

**Goodies Supermarket**

The Goodies supermarket houses a few imported items but be prepared to pay a high dollar for these. The shop has a bakery to select fresh baked goods instead of the high priced imports. Clothing can be purchased here and jewelry. This theme based supermarket has a lot to offer tourists and locals.
