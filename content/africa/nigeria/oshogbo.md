Oshogbo is the art capital of Nigeria. The city is filled with art studios that produce some of Africa's most inspiring works. The Oja Oba market is a good place to shop for souvenirs, especially if you're into juju. The market is across the street from the Oba's palace.

The highlight of Oshogbo is the Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove, a UNESCO World Heritage Site on the outskirts of town. Austrian artist Suzanne Wenger spent decades restoring and creating monumental sculptures among the traditional Yoruba shrines here. The annual Osun festival in August draws huge crowds.
