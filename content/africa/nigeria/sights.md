---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Nigeria's most remarkable sights span several thousand years of history and several very different landscapes.

**Kano Old City** — The *birni* (old walled city) of [Kano](/africa/nigeria/kano) is one of the most intact historic urban centres in West Africa. The Emir's palace, the Kurmi Market (trading continuously for over four centuries), and the indigo dye pits are all within walking distance of each other. The dye pits — great circular wells in the ground, their rims darkened from centuries of use — are among the most vivid images in Nigeria.

**Benin Royal Palace and Bronzes** — [Benin City](/africa/nigeria/benincity) was the capital of the Benin Empire, which cast some of the finest bronze and brass sculptures in the world between the 13th and 17th centuries. The royal palace is still active and the Oba still reigns. You can visit the palace grounds, and several workshops in the city continue the bronze-casting tradition. Most of the original bronzes were looted by Britain in 1897 and remain in Western museums — this context hangs over every visit.

**Oshogbo Sacred Grove** — A UNESCO World Heritage Site on the edge of [Oshogbo](/africa/nigeria/oshogbo): a forested grove along the Oshun River containing shrines, sculptures, and sanctuaries dedicated to Yoruba deities. The Austrian artist Suzanne Wenger worked here from the 1950s onwards with local artists to restore and expand the site. The result is unlike anything else: baroque religious imagery in dense tropical forest. Entry involves a guided tour through the grove.

**Yankari National Park** — The flagship wildlife reserve, in Bauchi State. Elephants are the draw, and the park has a healthy population. You're also likely to see baboons, warthogs, waterbuck, and many bird species. The **Wikki Warm Spring** at the centre of the park is a genuinely wonderful place — clear, 31°C water in a forested setting. The park has basic lodges and a campsite.

**Zuma Rock** — A dramatic granite monolith visible from the main road between [Abuja](/africa/nigeria/abuja) and [Kaduna](/africa/nigeria/kaduna). At 725 metres above the surrounding plain, it dominates the landscape. It has a face-like feature eroded into one side that has given rise to various legends. You can stop and walk around the base easily on a drive north from Abuja.

**National Museum Lagos** — On Lagos Island, the National Museum houses bronzes, terracotta sculptures, textiles, and historical artefacts. The collection of Benin bronzes and Nok terracottas is significant. The building is ageing, but the objects are extraordinary.

**Sukur Cultural Landscape (Adamawa State)** — A UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mandara Mountains: a hilltop chieftaincy with an intact historic landscape of terraced fields, iron-smelting sites, and traditional architecture. Remote and difficult to reach, but rewarding for those who make the effort. Note the current security situation in Adamawa before planning a visit.
