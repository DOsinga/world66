---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

The local cuisine of Warri and the Delta State is among the most flavour-forward in Nigeria. Banga soup — made from palm nut oil with dry fish, stockfish, crayfish, and spices — is the signature dish of the region. Pounded yam served with banga or egusi (melon seed) soup is ubiquitous. Suya (grilled spiced meat skewers) is available from roadside vendors in the evenings. The Efferun Market and Uwie area have the best concentration of cheap market food with authentic local flavour. Palm wine — tapped from oil palms and drunk fresh — is the traditional drink.
