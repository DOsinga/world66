Yola is the capital of Adamawa State in northeastern Nigeria, sitting along the Benue River where the Mandara Mountains rise to the east toward the Cameroonian border. It's a quiet administrative city — unhurried by Nigerian standards — with a predominantly Muslim population and a character shaped by the Fulani and Kanuri cultures that dominate this corner of the country.

The city splits into two parts: old Yola, the original settlement with its traditional market and older buildings, and Jimeta, the newer commercial district a few kilometres to the north that handles most of the city's business. Travellers passing through tend to use Yola as a stopover on routes toward Cameroon or as a base for exploring Adamawa's highlands and the Gashaka-Gumti National Park to the south — one of Nigeria's largest and wildest protected areas.

Security conditions in the broader region have been volatile in recent years due to activity from armed groups in the Lake Chad basin. Check current travel advisories before visiting.
