Hong is a small town in Adamawa State, northeast [Nigeria](/africa/nigeria), roughly 120 km north of [Yola](/africa/nigeria/yola). It sits on a plateau at the edge of the Mandara Mountains, surrounded by rocky hills and farmland, and serves as the headquarters of Hong Local Government Area.

The town is known for its striking granite inselbergs — massive rounded rock formations that rise abruptly from the surrounding plains. The Hong Hills, as they are collectively known, offer excellent hiking for those willing to make the effort, with panoramic views across the savanna toward Cameroon. The area is home to the Kilba people, who have a rich tradition of weaving and metalwork.

Hong is reached by road from Yola, a journey of about two to three hours depending on road conditions. Public transport runs along the route in the form of minibuses and shared taxis. Facilities in town are basic — there is a small market, a few provisions shops, and simple eateries. Visitors are rare, and you will likely attract friendly curiosity. The security situation in northeast Nigeria should be checked before travelling, as conditions can change.
