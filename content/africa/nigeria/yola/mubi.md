Mubi is the second largest city in Adamawa State, northeast [Nigeria](/africa/nigeria), sitting close to the Cameroon border at the foot of the Mandara Mountains. It is a busy commercial centre and one of the main crossing points into Cameroon, which gives it an energy and diversity that belies its modest size.

The city's large daily market is the main draw for the surrounding region, attracting traders from both sides of the border. You will find everything from livestock and grain to textiles and electronics. The Mandara Mountains, rising to the east, offer dramatic scenery — terraced hillsides, traditional hilltop villages of the Higi and Marghi peoples, and a rugged landscape that rewards exploration on foot.

Mubi is reached by road from [Yola](/africa/nigeria/yola), about three to four hours to the south, or via the border crossing from Cameroon. The road from Yola passes through rolling savanna and is generally in reasonable condition. The city has basic facilities including banks, fuel stations, and simple restaurants. As with all of northeast Nigeria, it is essential to check the current security situation before travelling — the region has experienced instability, and conditions can change rapidly.
