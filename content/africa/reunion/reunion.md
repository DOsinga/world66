*Africa > Réunion*

Réunion is a French overseas department sitting in the Indian Ocean, roughly 700 kilometres east of Madagascar and 170 kilometres southwest of Mauritius. It is France transplanted to the tropics — the currency is the euro, the roads are French-quality, and the healthcare system is French — yet the landscape and culture are entirely its own.

The island is dominated by two volcanic massifs. The older, extinct Piton des Neiges rises to 3,071 metres at the centre of the island and is the highest point in the Indian Ocean. Around it are three extraordinary cirques: Mafate, Salazie, and Cilaos — deep, cliff-walled natural amphitheatres carved by erosion, each with its own character and its own way of life. On the southeastern flank, the active Piton de la Fournaise erupts several times a year and is one of the most accessible volcanoes in the world.

Most visitors come for the hiking, and the island delivers. The GR R1 and GR R2 trails cross the mountains and cirques; the Cirque de Mafate is only accessible on foot or by helicopter; and the route to the rim of Piton de la Fournaise is a day hike that should not be missed. But Réunion is not only a hiking destination. The west coast has calm lagoons and decent beaches. The Creole food is genuinely good. And the mix of French, African, Indian, and Chinese influences gives the island a cultural texture you will not find anywhere else.

Réunion is not a cheap destination by the standards of the Indian Ocean. As French territory, costs are closer to southern Europe than to neighbouring Mauritius or Madagascar. That said, the island is easy to travel independently, infrastructure is reliable, and the combination of volcanic scenery, warm water, and French food is one of the better travel propositions in this part of the world.

## Destinations

- [Cirque de Salazie](/africa/reunion/cirquedesalazie) — the greenest and most accessible of the three cirques, reached by a dramatic mountain road from Saint-André
- Saint-Denis — the capital, on the northern coast, with 150,000 people and an appealing Creole centre
- Cilaos — a mountain village in its own cirque, known for lentils, wine, and thermal baths
- Saint-Pierre — the main town of the south, with a relaxed atmosphere and a better beach than Saint-Denis
