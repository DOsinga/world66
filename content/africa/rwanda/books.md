**We Wish to Inform You That Tomorrow We Will Be Killed With Our Families** by Philip Gourevitch (1998) is the essential account of the 1994 genocide — how it happened, why the world failed to act, and what Rwanda looked like in its aftermath. Unflinching and very well written.

**A Sunday at the Pool in Kigali** by Gil Courtemanche (2000) is a novel set in Kigali in the months before the genocide, based on real events. It captures the atmosphere of that period in a way that straight journalism cannot.

**The Antelope's Strategy** by Jean Hatzfeld (2009) is the third in his trilogy on the genocide, focusing on perpetrators and survivors attempting to live alongside each other in the same villages. The earlier volumes, *Into the Quick of Life* and *Machete Season*, are equally important.

**Land of a Thousand Hills** by Rosamond Halsey Carr (1999) is a memoir by an American woman who ran a flower farm in Rwanda for decades and stayed through the genocide. Warm and illuminating about life in colonial and post-colonial Rwanda.

**Gorillas in the Mist** by Dian Fossey (1983) remains the definitive account of mountain gorilla conservation and the years Fossey spent studying them in the Virunga volcanoes. Essential reading before a gorilla trek.
