Rwandan food is simple, filling, and built around what grows at altitude: beans, plantains, sweet potatoes, cassava, rice, and maize. Meat is present but not dominant — beef, goat, and pork appear as accompaniments rather than centrepieces for most people.

The most characteristic meal is a spread of dishes served at lunch, the main meal of the day: a starchy staple (rice, ugali, or potatoes), beans cooked with onion and tomato, steamed plantains, and perhaps a small meat stew. This combination is found everywhere, from local roadside restaurants to mid-range places, and usually costs a few hundred francs.

**Dishes to try**

*Isombe* — mashed cassava leaves cooked with groundnuts and palm oil, sometimes with dried fish. Rich and earthy.

*Agatogo* — a stew of green plantains with beef or goat, slow-cooked with spices until the plantain absorbs the meat's flavour.

*Brochettes* — grilled meat skewers over charcoal, the default street food and social eating experience. Beef, goat, pork, and fish versions are all common.

*Matoke* — green bananas steamed in their leaves and served as a soft, starchy accompaniment.

*Mizuzu* — fried ripe plantain slices. Sweet, simple, and very good with brochettes.

**Eating in Kigali**

[Kigali](/africa/rwanda/kigali) has a developed restaurant scene with solid Indian, Ethiopian, Lebanese, and international options alongside Rwandan food. The Kimironko and Kacyiru neighbourhoods have good local places; the city centre has more upmarket options. Supermarkets stock imported goods if you're self-catering.

Outside Kigali, options narrow quickly. Near Volcanoes National Park and Lake Kivu, most lodges provide meals. In small towns, stick to the local buffet places — they're reliable and cheap.

**Drink**

Primus and Mutzig are the dominant local beers, both lagers and both drinkable. *Urwagwa* is a traditional sorghum or banana beer; the banana version is mildly sweet and not very strong. Rwandan coffee and tea are excellent — the country produces high-quality arabica coffee that has found a following internationally.
