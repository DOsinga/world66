Kigali is one of the cleanest, safest, and most surprising capital cities in Africa. Spread across a series of lush hills and valleys, it has transformed itself remarkably since the 1994 genocide. The streets are spotless (plastic bags are banned nationwide), the roads are good, and the city hums with quiet ambition. It is not a place you pass through — it deserves a few days of your time.

The Kigali Genocide Memorial is the essential visit, a devastating and beautifully done tribute to the estimated one million people killed in 1994. Beyond that, the city rewards walking: the busy streets of Nyamirambo, the craft shops and galleries of Kiyovu, the views from Mount Kigali. The restaurant scene is better than you might expect, with solid Ethiopian, Indian, and local food alongside the inevitable expat-oriented places.

Kigali also serves as the gateway to Rwanda's national parks. The [Volcanoes National Park](/africa/rwanda/volcanoesnationalpark) for gorilla trekking, the [Akagera National Park](/africa/rwanda/akageranationalpark) for savanna wildlife, and [Nyungwe Forest](/africa/rwanda/nyungweforest) for chimpanzee tracking are all reachable as day trips or overnights. The Rwanda Development Board (RDB) in Kigali handles all park permits — book gorilla permits well in advance, as they sell out months ahead.
