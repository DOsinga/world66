---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Kigali has two tiers of eating: international restaurants catering mostly to foreigners and expats, and local places serving brochettes and buffets. Street food is virtually nonexistent.

The international restaurants are some of the best in the region, but you will pay for it — expect around 10 USD per main dish. Local places are much cheaper (lunch buffets downtown run about 3 USD), though service can be slow. Indian and Chinese restaurants tend to be the fastest options if you are short on time.

**Beijing Tangren Restaurant** — The most popular Chinese restaurant in town, with faster service than most Kigali places. On the airport road in Remera, near the RDB offices.

**Khana Khazana** — Widely considered the best Indian restaurant in Kigali. More expensive than the competition, but the food justifies it. In Kiyovu, a five-minute walk from Hotel des Mille Collines.

**Zaafran** — Excellent Indian food at friendlier prices than Khana Khazana, also in Kiyovu. On Rue de l'Akagera near the Canadian Embassy road. A good lunch option thanks to quick service.

**Lalibela** — Excellent Ethiopian food at a decent price. Best shared with someone so you can split a platter. In Kimihurura, a short walk from the Papyrus area.

**Sol e Luna** — One of the contenders for best pizza in Kigali, with a great view and good service. Monday nights feature a popular quiz at 8:30 — join any table with fewer than seven players. On the airport road before the Remera roundabout.
