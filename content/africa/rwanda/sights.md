Rwanda's most visited sights divide into two categories: wildlife and nature experiences, and genocide memorials. The contrast is part of what makes the country so affecting to visit.

**National parks and wildlife**

The main wildlife draw is [Volcanoes National Park](/africa/rwanda/volcanoesnationalpark) in the northwest, where mountain gorilla tracking takes place. Akagera National Park in the east offers traditional savanna wildlife including elephant, giraffe, hippo, and — since recent reintroductions — lion and black rhino. Nyungwe National Park in the southwest is the place for chimpanzees and colobus monkeys in ancient rainforest.

**Genocide memorials**

Several of Rwanda's most powerful sites are memorials to the 1994 genocide. The Kigali Genocide Memorial in the capital holds the remains of over 250,000 victims. Outside the capital, the churches at Nyamata and Ntarama (south of Kigali) and the Murambi Genocide Memorial near Gikongoro are stark, deeply disturbing, and essential for anyone trying to understand what happened here. They are not easy visits, but Rwanda does not want you to look away.

**Towns and lake**

[Kigali](/africa/rwanda/kigali) is worth time in itself — better than its reputation suggests. Lake Kivu on the western border offers scenery, water activities, and a relaxed pace, with [Gisenyi](/africa/rwanda/gisenyi) (Rubavu) and [Cyangugu](/africa/rwanda/chyangugu) (Rusizi) as bases.
