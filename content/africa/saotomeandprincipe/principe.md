Príncipe is the smaller and wilder of the two islands that make up São Tomé and Príncipe. Only about 160 sq km in size, it sits roughly 150 km north of São Tomé, cloaked in dense rainforest that tumbles down to some of the least-visited beaches in the Atlantic. The pace here is genuinely slow — there is one main town, Santo António, and life moves accordingly.

The island is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, and the forest is the reason to come. Birdwatchers make the trip specifically for endemic species found nowhere else on earth, including the Príncipe thrush and the Príncipe sunbird. Parrots are conspicuous and easy to spot even for casual visitors. The beaches — Praia Banana in particular — rank among the finest in West Africa, with calm, warm water and virtually no one on them.
