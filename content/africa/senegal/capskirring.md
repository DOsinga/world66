Cap Skirring is the main beach resort in Senegal's Casamance region, at the southern tip of the country near the Guinea-Bissau border. It has some of the best beaches in West Africa — long, wide stretches of golden sand backed by palm trees, with warm water and reliable sunshine.

The village itself is small and relaxed, with a handful of restaurants and a local market. The Casamance region has a lush, tropical character quite different from the dry north of Senegal, and the Diola culture here is distinctive. Pirogue trips through the mangrove-lined bolongs (creeks) to traditional villages are the highlight of any visit.

Cap Skirring has a small airport with seasonal flights from Dakar. Overland, the journey from Dakar is long and can involve a ferry crossing at Ziguinchor.
