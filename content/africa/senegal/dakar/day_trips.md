---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Lac Retba — **Pink Lake** — is the obvious day trip, an hour's drive northeast, where a cyanobacterium turns the water a bubblegum pink in the dry season. Salt collectors work the lake in wooden pirogues. Closer to Dakar, the fishing village of Yoff has a Layène Sufi shrine and a working beach. Further out, Keur Moussa Monastery holds Sunday mass set to kora music — an unexpected and beautiful thing. Cap Almadies, the westernmost point of mainland Africa, sits at the tip of the peninsula.
