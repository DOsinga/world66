Saly is Senegal's main package-holiday resort, about ninety minutes south of [Dakar](/africa/senegal/dakar) on the Petite Côte. A string of hotels and apartment complexes lines a long sandy beach, backed by gardens of baobab and eucalyptus. It is a purpose-built resort rather than a town — the atmosphere is closer to a Mediterranean beach complex than to the rest of Senegal.

The beach is the main draw: warm water, gentle waves, and a range of watersports including windsurfing, jet skiing, and snorkelling. Deep-sea fishing is excellent off this stretch of coast — blue marlin, sailfish, and swordfish are all caught regularly. There is also an 18-hole golf course and horse riding along the beach.

Saly is comfortable and easy, but visitors looking for a more authentic Senegalese experience will want to base themselves elsewhere. The nearby fishing village of Mbour, just a few kilometres south, has a spectacular morning fish market and a very different feel.
