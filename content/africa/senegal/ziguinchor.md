Ziguinchor is the main town of the Casamance region, sitting on the south bank of the Casamance River about 70 km from the Atlantic coast. It is the obvious base for exploring the lush forests, mangroves, and villages of southern Senegal.

The town has a pleasantly drowsy atmosphere compared to Dakar. The influence of the Diola people is evident in the culture and architecture, and the surrounding landscape — rice paddies, oil palms, tall ceiba trees — looks nothing like the sandy north. The local market is animated and well-stocked. A few good guesthouses and restaurants cater to travellers passing through on their way to Cap Skirring and the coast.

From Ziguinchor, pirogues carry passengers up the river to villages and the remarkable mangrove forests of the delta. The town also serves as the ferry terminus for the overnight service from Dakar — a 12-hour journey that is the most atmospheric way to arrive.
