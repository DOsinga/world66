Aride Island is the northernmost of the granitic Seychelles and arguably the most important seabird nesting site in the western Indian Ocean after Aldabra. The island is managed by the Island Conservation Society as a nature reserve and is open to visitors year-round, weather permitting.

The numbers are staggering: over a million seabirds breed here, including the world's largest colonies of lesser noddies and roseate terns. Wright's gardenia, found nowhere else on earth, grows on the island's granite slopes. The island is also free of rats and cats, which makes it a rare sanctuary for native species.

Visits are arranged as half-day trips from Praslin (about 15 minutes by boat). A warden guides visitors along a trail to the summit, where the views and the sheer density of birdlife overhead are unforgettable. Landing depends on sea conditions and is not always possible, so build flexibility into your plans.
