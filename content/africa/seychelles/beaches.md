---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The Seychelles does beaches exceptionally well. What sets them apart isn't just the white sand and warm clear water — it's the enormous granite boulders that spill down to the shore on many of the granitic islands, creating sheltered coves and unusual backdrops you won't find anywhere else.

**Anse Source d'Argent, La Digue** is the most photographed beach in the Seychelles and arguably the world. Pink-tinged sand, shallow turquoise water, and giant smoothed granite rocks make it genuinely as striking as it looks in pictures. It's protected by a reef, so swimming is calm. The access path runs through a working coconut plantation — there's a small entry fee. Go early to beat the crowds.

**Anse Lazio, Praslin** consistently ranks among the world's best beaches. Wide arc of pale sand, clear blue water, good snorkelling on the reef, and the added bonus of shade from takamaka trees. A restaurant at the north end serves grilled fish.

**Beau Vallon, Mahé** is the main social beach — large, easily accessible from Victoria, with watersports, vendors, and a laid-back vibe. Not the most beautiful, but good for families and for people-watching. Wednesday evening beach barbecues draw a local crowd.

**Anse Intendance, Mahé** is a wilder, more dramatic beach on the southwest coast. Strong currents make it better for watching than swimming much of the year — but at low tide in the right conditions it's magnificent. Leatherback turtles nest here between October and February.

**Anse Cocos, La Digue** requires a hike to reach, which keeps the numbers down. The reward is one of the most beautiful and deserted beaches on the island.
