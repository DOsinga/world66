---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Seychellois cultural life revolves around its Creole heritage, and the main festivals reflect that — music, food, dance, and a mix of African, French, and Indian influences.

**Festival Kreol** (late October) is the most important cultural event of the year, running for about a week across the main islands. It celebrates Seychellois Creole culture in all its forms: traditional music and dance, Creole cuisine, crafts, storytelling, and costume. The highlight is the parade through Victoria with music groups and dancers from Seychelles and sometimes from other Creole nations across the Indian Ocean. If you're visiting in late October, this is worth timing your trip around.

**FetAFrik** (late June) celebrates Seychelles' African roots. It's a more recent addition to the calendar but has become a well-attended celebration of African music, dance, and food.

**Carnival of Victoria** (February or March) is a street carnival through the capital with floats, music, and performers — similar in spirit to carnivals elsewhere in the former French colonial world, though on a modest scale. It draws some international dance troupes.

**Festival de la Digue** (15 August) marks the Feast of the Assumption on [La Digue](/africa/seychelles/ladigue) — a Catholic feast day with particular local significance. The small island comes alive with music and celebration.

**Semaine de la Francophonie** (March) reflects the island's French heritage — art, literature, music, and food with a French cultural flavour.

Public holidays include Liberation Day (5 June), National Day (18 June), and Independence Day (29 June) — all of which prompt local gatherings, particularly in Victoria.
