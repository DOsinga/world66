La Digue's high point at 333 metres rewards the climb with views across the island and out to [Praslin](/africa/seychelles/praslin), [Mahe](/africa/seychelles/mahe), and the smaller islands scattered between them. The path up through the forest is rough in places — sturdy footwear and water are essential — but it takes only about 45 minutes from the road.

The forest around Nid d'Aigle is one of the best places on the island to spot the Seychelles black paradise flycatcher, a striking bird with an iridescent black body and long trailing tail feathers. The male is unmistakable; the female is chestnut-coloured. There are thought to be a few hundred left, mostly on La Digue.
