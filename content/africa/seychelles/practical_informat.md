## Money

The local currency is the Seychelles Rupee (SCR). Most tourist expenses — accommodation, car hire, national park entry, diving — are quoted and payable in US dollars or euros. Local restaurants and buses use rupees. ATMs are plentiful on Mahé and available on Praslin; bring cash to La Digue and smaller islands. Visa and Mastercard are widely accepted at larger establishments.

## Visa

No advance visa is required, but all visitors must obtain a Travel Authorization (TA) before arrival — apply online at the Seychelles Electronic Border System for around €11. You'll need confirmed accommodation and a return or onward ticket.

## Health

There is no malaria risk in Seychelles. Tap water meets WHO standards and is safe to drink. Healthcare is reasonable on Mahé and basic on the outer islands; travel insurance with medical evacuation cover is strongly recommended.

## Language

Three official languages: Seychellois Creole (spoken natively by around 90% of the population), English, and French. English is widely understood in hotels, restaurants, and tourist facilities.

## Safety

Seychelles is one of the safer destinations in the Indian Ocean region. Petty theft exists but violent crime against tourists is rare. The sea can be hazardous — some beaches have strong currents and no lifeguards. If a beach has warning flags, pay attention to them.

## Climate and Electricity

Tropical throughout the year, with temperatures between 24°C and 32°C. Electricity is 240V, 50Hz with UK-style three-pin plugs.
