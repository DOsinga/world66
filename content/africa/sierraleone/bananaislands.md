The Banana Islands — Dublin, Ricketts, and the uninhabited Mes-Meheux — lie about 25 km south of Freetown, a short boat ride from the fishing village of Kent. They were a significant base for the Atlantic slave trade in the 18th century, and the ruins of slave trader compounds and an old British fort are still visible on Dublin Island. The islands' difficult history sits alongside genuinely beautiful beaches, clear water, and an unhurried pace that makes them one of the most rewarding escapes from the capital.

Dublin Island has the only significant settlement, a village of fishermen and a handful of guesthouses. The beaches are quiet and largely uncommercialised. Snorkelling in the surrounding waters is good — the sea is warm and visibility can be excellent in the dry season. The journey from Freetown to Kent takes about two hours by road or poda-poda, followed by a 20-minute pirogue crossing. Come with cash and basic supplies; facilities are minimal.
