Bo is Sierra Leone's second largest city and the capital of the Southern Province, a sprawling commercial hub about four hours east of Freetown by road. It functions as the main market town for the south and east of the country, and its busy central market is the economic heart of the region. Bo was one of the first towns in Sierra Leone to have a secondary school -- the Bo Government Secondary School, founded in 1906 -- and it retains a reputation as an education center.

The city itself is not a tourist destination in the conventional sense, but it is a practical base if you are heading to the Gola Rainforest or the diamond-mining areas of Kenema and Kono. The atmosphere is lively and welcoming, and you will find basic but decent accommodation and food. Infrastructure is improving but still rough -- expect power outages and unpaved roads in many neighborhoods. Bo was heavily affected by the civil war but has recovered with considerable energy, and the warmth of the people here is one of the best things about the place.
