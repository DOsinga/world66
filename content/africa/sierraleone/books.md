title: "Books"
type: section
---

**White Man's Grave** by Richard Dooling — A novel set in Sierra Leone that captures the country's atmosphere, superstitions, and bush culture better than most non-fiction. Published in 1994, it remains the most evocative English-language fiction set in the country.

**A Long Way Gone: Memoirs of a Boy Soldier** by Ishmael Beah — A memoir by a Sierra Leonean author about his experience as a child soldier during the civil war. Essential reading for understanding what happened.

**Fighting for the Rain Forest: War, Youth and Resources in Sierra Leone** by Paul Richards — Academic but accessible analysis of the causes of the civil war. Argues it was less about ethnicity and more about frustrated youth, land rights, and resource struggles.

**The Devil That Danced on the Water** by Aminatta Forna — A Sierra Leonean-British writer's investigation into the killing of her father, a Sierra Leonean politician, under the Stevens regime in 1975. Part memoir, part political history. Excellent.
