Freetown is the capital and by far the largest city, home to about a third of Sierra Leone's population. It's dense, noisy, and congested — built on steep hills running down to the Atlantic, with a geography that makes traffic a constant ordeal. It's not a city you'll fall in love with immediately, but it has real energy and a distinct Krio character that sets it apart from other West African capitals.

The historic center has the Cotton Tree, a giant silk-cotton tree that has stood at the intersection of Siaka Stevens Street since before the city was founded — it's the symbolic heart of Krio heritage. The National Museum on Tower Hill is small but gives useful context on the country's history. The Aberdeen and Lumley neighborhoods, on the west side of the peninsula, are where most travelers end up: this is where the better restaurants, bars, and guesthouses are concentrated, and where Lumley Beach stretches north toward Aberdeen.

The Lebanese community runs many of the better restaurants. You'll find good grilled fish at the beach shacks near Aberdeen. The nightlife scene in Aberdeen has grown significantly in recent years.

Freetown is also the base for day trips down the peninsula to the better beaches — Tokeh, Bureh, River No. 2. Budget about an hour or two each way on variable roads.
