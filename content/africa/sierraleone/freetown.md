Freetown is the capital and by far the largest city in [Sierra Leone](/africa/sierraleone), home to about a third of the country's population. It is not a city you will fall in love with immediately — it is dense, noisy, and congested, built on steep hills running down to the Atlantic, with a geography that makes traffic a constant ordeal. But it has real energy and a distinct Krio character that sets it apart from other West African capitals.

The historic center clusters around the Cotton Tree, a giant silk-cotton tree that has stood at the intersection of Siaka Stevens Street since before the city was founded. It is the symbolic heart of Krio heritage and the one landmark every visitor sees. Nearby, the National Museum on Tower Hill is small but gives useful context on the country's history, from the slave trade through the civil war. King's Gate, the old city gate built by freed slaves in the early 1800s, still stands on the hillside above.

The Aberdeen and Lumley neighborhoods, on the west side of the peninsula, are where most travelers end up. This is where the better restaurants, bars, and guesthouses cluster, and where Lumley Beach stretches north toward Aberdeen — a long, busy strip of sand that fills up on weekends. The Lebanese community runs many of the better restaurants, and you will find good grilled fish at the beach shacks near Aberdeen.

Freetown is also the base for day trips down the peninsula to some of West Africa's best beaches — [Lakka](/africa/sierraleone/freetown/beaches/lakka_beach), Tokeh, Bureh, and [River No. 2](/africa/sierraleone/freetown/beaches/river_no_2). Budget an hour or two each way on variable roads, but the white sand and jungle backdrops are worth the effort.
