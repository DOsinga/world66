Kabala is the main town in the Koinadugu district of northern [Sierra Leone](/africa/sierraleone), sitting at the foot of the Wara Wara Mountains close to the Guinean border. It is one of the highest towns in the country and the air is noticeably cooler than on the coast — a relief after the humidity of [Freetown](/africa/sierraleone/freetown).

The town itself is small and quiet, but the surrounding landscape is the draw. Mount Bintumani (Loma Mansa), at 1,945 metres the highest peak in West Africa west of Cameroon, is accessible from Kabala on a multi-day hike through the Loma Mountains National Park. The trek is challenging — no marked trails, basic camping, and a guide is essential — but the montane forest and grassland at the summit are home to chimpanzees, pygmy hippos, and some of the most intact highland ecosystem left in West Africa.

Getting to Kabala requires patience. The road from Freetown takes eight to ten hours in good conditions, longer in the rains when the laterite roads turn to mud. There are basic guesthouses in town. Come in the dry season (November to April) if you plan to hike.
