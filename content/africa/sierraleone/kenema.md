Kenema is the third-largest city in [Sierra Leone](/africa/sierraleone), capital of the Eastern Province, and the gateway to the country's diamond country. Sitting at the edge of the Gola Rainforest, it has a different feel from the coastal capital [Freetown](/africa/sierraleone/freetown) — greener, quieter, and more deeply connected to the interior.

The city grew around the diamond trade and still carries that frontier energy. The central market is busy and colourful, and the surrounding hills offer good walking. Kenema is the logical base for visiting the Gola Rainforest National Park, one of the last significant patches of Upper Guinean rainforest in West Africa, home to pygmy hippos, chimpanzees, and forest elephants.

Most travellers pass through on the way to or from the Liberian border, but the town rewards a short stop. The people are welcoming, the food is good, and the forest is close.
