Somalia occupies the Horn of Africa, a dramatic peninsula where the Gulf of Aden meets the Indian Ocean. It has the longest coastline on the African mainland — some 3,025 km of largely untouched shore — and a landscape that shifts from mountains in the north to semi-arid scrubland in the interior to the river valleys of the south.

Be honest with yourself before planning a trip here. Most of Somalia remains extremely dangerous. Al-Shabaab launches regular attacks in Mogadishu and the south. Armed robbery, kidnapping, and carjacking are common in all regions outside Somaliland. The federal government controls limited territory, and road travel between cities is genuinely life-threatening. Every major government travel advisory rates Somalia as "do not travel." That assessment is warranted.

The partial exception is [Somaliland](/africa/somaliland), the self-declared republic in the northwest, which has its own functioning government, relative stability, and a small but growing tourism scene. Somaliland is a separate travel destination and has its own page on this site. The notes below concern the rest of Somalia — primarily Puntland in the northeast and the federal government territories in the south.

A small number of journalists, aid workers, business travelers, and determined adventurers do visit Mogadishu and Puntland. If you are one of them, go in with a local fixer, security briefings, and realistic expectations. The city and the coast are genuinely fascinating; the risks are genuinely severe.

## Geography

Somalia is bounded by Djibouti and the Gulf of Aden to the north, the Indian Ocean to the east, Kenya to the southwest, and Ethiopia to the west. The northern highlands reach elevations of over 2,000 m. The south is defined by two major rivers, the Jubba and the Shabeelle, which support the country's most productive farmland. The center is largely semi-arid Ogaden plateau.

The climate is hot and dry, moderated in the north by altitude. Two monsoon seasons bring the main rains: the Gu (April–June) and the shorter Dayr (October–November). Temperatures along the coast can exceed 40°C in the hottest months.
