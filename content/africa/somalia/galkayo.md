Galkayo is a divided city straddling the administrative boundary between Puntland in the north and the Galmudug region in the south. The two halves operate under different administrations and have at times been separated by checkpoints and inter-clan tension. The northern, Puntland-administered half is the more stable side.

It is a significant trading hub for central Somalia, driven by the livestock trade and remittances from the Somali diaspora. The city has grown rapidly since the 1990s and has functioning schools, clinics, and mobile phone coverage.

For travelers, Galkayo is primarily a transit point on the route between Mogadishu and Bossaso. Jubba Airways and other small carriers operate connecting flights. The security situation is better than in the south but has seen periodic inter-clan fighting.
