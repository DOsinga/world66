Garowe is the capital of Puntland, Somalia's semi-autonomous northeastern region. It sits in the Nugal valley at around 500 m elevation, which makes it cooler than the coastal cities. The city has grown from a small settlement into a functioning regional capital since Puntland was established in 1998, and it serves as the seat of the Puntland government.

Garowe has banks, hotels, a university, and regular air connections to Mogadishu and Bossaso. By Somali standards it is relatively orderly and stable, though kidnapping risk and general insecurity still apply. It is the administrative base for many NGOs and international organizations working in the Puntland region.

There is not much for a visitor to see specifically in Garowe, but the surrounding Nugal valley is striking — semi-arid terrain that turns green after the rains, with scattered frankincense and acacia trees.
