Kismayo (also spelled Kismaayo) is a port city in the far south of Somalia, near the Kenyan border. It is the country's second-largest port and the administrative center of Jubaland state. Historically it was a significant trading hub and had beautiful beaches, but it has been at the center of conflict for most of the past three decades.

The city changed hands multiple times between Somali factions, al-Shabaab (which controlled it from 2008 to 2012), and Kenyan and African Union forces. Al-Shabaab retains a strong presence in the surrounding Jubba valley region and continues to stage attacks in and around the city.

Kismayo is not safe for travel. Do not visit without compelling professional reasons and robust security arrangements. There is no tourist infrastructure of any kind.
