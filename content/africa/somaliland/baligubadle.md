Baligubadle is a small town in the Woqooyi Galbeed region of [Somaliland](/africa/somaliland), lying south of [Hargeisa](/africa/somaliland/hargeisa) near the Ethiopian border. The area is notable for the Laas Geel cave paintings, some of the best-preserved Neolithic rock art in Africa, located in the limestone outcrops nearby. The paintings, depicting cattle, herders, and wild animals, are estimated to be between 5,000 and 11,000 years old.

The town itself is a modest settlement, but its position on a route between Hargeisa and the Ethiopian border gives it some passing traffic.
