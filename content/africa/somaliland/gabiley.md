Gabiley is an agricultural town 54 kilometres west of Hargeisa, the capital of Somaliland. It sits in the fertile "Dhul-beereed" region and is known as the breadbasket of the country — the farms here produce corn, maize, beans, groundnuts, and fruit including papaya, oranges, and watermelon.

The town itself is modest and not a typical tourist stop, but travellers passing through between Hargeisa and the Ethiopian border at Togwajale will find a bustling market town at 1,450 metres elevation with a moderate climate year-round. The surrounding countryside is one of the most densely settled parts of Somaliland, with dozens of small townships scattered across the farmland.
