Xiis (also spelled Heis) is a small coastal town in Somaliland's Sanaag region, perched on the Gulf of Aden shore where the dry mountains drop down to the sea. The town is ancient — archaeological sites in the area include cairns, ruins, and rock art that point to centuries of trade connections across the Gulf.

The coastline here is dramatic: rocky headlands, clear water, and very few people. Xiis is remote even by Somaliland standards, reached by rough roads from Erigavo through mountain passes with spectacular views. The journey is part of the experience — the Sanaag highlands are some of the most beautiful and least-visited landscapes in the Horn of Africa.

Facilities are minimal. There is no formal accommodation, and you will need your own transport and supplies. But for travellers willing to make the effort, Xiis offers a glimpse of a coast that few outsiders have seen.
