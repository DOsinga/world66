---
title: "Surfing"
type: section
---

Cape Town has many excellent surf spots — about 48 lie within an hour's drive of the city. Most are on the west side of the Cape Peninsula, facing the Atlantic, but the east side facing the Indian Ocean also has its breaks.

**Glen Beach** — One of the most established surf spots in Cape Town. Many South African top surfers are locals here. It is a shortish ride but can deliver superlative form when the banks and conditions are right. Works best at 2–5 feet and is sheltered from the southeasterly gales that blow down from the Twelve Apostles above.

**Llandudno** — One of Cape Town's most popular spots. Depending on the shifting sandbanks, Llandudno can deliver terrific barrels. The Gat, named for its spitting tubes, is a thick wedge that breaks near the boulders to the right of the bay. Often crowded even though the water in summer can drop to 9 degrees — but thawing out in the hot sun afterwards is half the pleasure.

**Thermopylae** — This wave breaks at the sunken remains of the ship Thermopylae in Mouille Point. It takes a serious swell before this spot begins to work, and be aware of water quality from the nearby outfall.
