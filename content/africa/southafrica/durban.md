Durban is South Africa's third-largest city and its warmest, sprawling along the Indian Ocean in the subtropical heart of KwaZulu-Natal. It's a beach town and a busy industrial port at the same time, and a place where Zulu, Indian, English and Afrikaans cultures live on top of one another in a way you don't quite see anywhere else in the country.

The defining stretch is the **Golden Mile**, a long arc of sand backed by promenade, hotels and apartment blocks. The water is warm enough to swim year-round, the surf is good, and on weekends the whole beachfront fills up with families, swimmers and surfers. At the southern end, **uShaka Marine World** combines an aquarium, water park and dolphin shows in a half-day's worth of distraction. North of the centre, **Umhlanga** is the fancier seaside suburb, with calmer beaches and a famous lighthouse.

Inland from the beach lies the legacy of the indentured labourers brought from India in the 1860s to work the sugar fields. Durban's Indian community is the largest outside India, and you feel it everywhere. Around **Grey Street** (now Yusuf Dadoo) and the **Victoria Street Market**, the air smells of cardamom and roasted chickpeas, the Juma Masjid is the largest mosque in the southern hemisphere, and you can eat **bunny chow** — a hollowed-out half-loaf of bread filled with curry, invented here — for next to nothing. The curries in Durban are the best argument for visiting on their own.

Up the hill, the residential **Berea** has Victorian villas and jacarandas, while the CBD is a working African downtown — chaotic, lively, and not somewhere to wander after dark. Durban's reputation for street crime is real; stick to the beachfront, the Berea suburbs, Florida Road in Morningside, and the northern beaches, and you will be fine. Get out of town for a day to the **Valley of a Thousand Hills**, the Drakensberg, or the wildlife of **Hluhluwe-iMfolozi**, and you have one of the best bases for exploring KwaZulu-Natal.
