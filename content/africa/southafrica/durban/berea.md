The leafy ridge above the city centre, lined with Victorian and Edwardian villas and some of the city's grander 20th-century apartment blocks. The Berea was Durban's first wealthy suburb and still has the feel of one — mature jacarandas and flame trees, quiet streets, and views down over the harbour and the beachfront. Wander Musgrave Road for the architecture, or duck into the **Durban Botanic Gardens** at the bottom of the hill, the oldest surviving botanic garden in Africa, founded in 1849.
