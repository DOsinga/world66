---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

South Africa has a lively festival calendar that spans music, arts, culture, and wildlife.

**Cape Town International Jazz Festival** (March/April) is sub-Saharan Africa's largest music event, filling five stages at the Cape Town International Convention Centre over two days with around 37,000 people. The lineup always mixes South African jazz legends with international names; it's a chance to hear music you would not easily encounter elsewhere.

**Klein Karoo Nasionale Kunstefees (KKNK)** (March/April, Oudtshoorn) is the premier Afrikaans arts festival, drawing writers, theatre makers, musicians, and comedians to the dusty Karoo town of [Oudtshoorn](/africa/southafrica/oudtshoorn) for a week. The festival is performed almost entirely in Afrikaans, but the atmosphere is inclusive and the fringe scene especially worth exploring.

**National Arts Festival** (June/July, Makhanda/Grahamstown) is the country's largest and most eclectic arts gathering — over ten days of theatre, dance, visual art, film, and music. The fringe is enormous and includes both polished productions and experimental work.

**Hermanus Whale Festival** (late September/early October) marks the arrival of southern right whales who come to calve in Walker Bay. The whale watching from the cliff paths around Hermanus is genuinely world-class, and the festival weekend adds live music, food markets, and an eco-marine exhibition. Entrance to most events is free.

**Oppikoppi** (August, near Northam, Limpopo) is South Africa's long-running rock and alternative music festival, held in the dusty bushveld. It is rough and fun in equal measure — camping, stages, no showers worth mentioning, and a loyal crowd who return year after year.

**Rocking the Daisies** (October, Cape Town area) is the city's leading electronic music and lifestyle festival, set in a wine farm outside Cape Town. More polished than Oppikoppi, with a strong environmental ethos.
