Magaliesburg is a small town in the Magaliesberg mountain range about 70 kilometres west of Johannesburg, making it the most popular weekend escape for Gauteng residents. The mountains are ancient — among the oldest on earth — and the country around them is beautiful: broad valleys, rocky kloofs, and rivers shaded by fig trees and willows. After the noise and concrete of the city, the contrast is immediate.

The town itself is modest, little more than a main street with a few shops and restaurants. The appeal is the surroundings rather than any urban life. Hiking and mountain biking trails wind through the range, the Skeerpoort and Magalies rivers are good for swimming in summer, and several working farms in the valley have opened as guesthouses or activity centres. Canopy tours and hot-air ballooning are popular, though both book up quickly on weekends.

If you are based in Johannesburg and want a single night away in proper countryside, Magaliesburg is the practical choice. It's close enough to reach after work on a Friday and far enough to feel genuinely removed from the city.
