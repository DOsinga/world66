## By Air

South Africa has three main international airports. **O.R. Tambo International Airport (JNB)**, between Johannesburg and Pretoria, is the country's busiest and your most likely point of entry. **Cape Town International Airport (CPT)** is about 20 km from the city centre. **King Shaka International Airport (DUR)** serves Durban and KwaZulu-Natal.

From London, flying time is around 11-12 hours to Johannesburg, slightly longer to Cape Town. From New York, expect roughly 15-16 hours non-stop. From Los Angeles there is no direct service — you will connect through Johannesburg or a European hub. South African Airways operates international routes, as do British Airways, Emirates, Ethiopian Airlines, Qatar Airways, and Lufthansa.

Nelspruit's Kruger Mpumalanga International Airport (MQP) is a useful option if you are heading directly to the Kruger National Park, with connections via Johannesburg.

## By Land

South Africa shares land borders with Namibia, Botswana, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Lesotho, and Eswatini. The most-used overland crossing from Zimbabwe is **Beit Bridge** on the N1; expect delays, especially on weekends and public holidays. From Mozambique, the Komatipoort/Ressano Garcia crossing connects to the Mozambican capital Maputo. Borders with Lesotho and Eswatini are straightforward and popular with travellers on road trips.

Long-distance coaches operated by Greyhound, Intercape, and Translux connect South Africa to Harare, Maputo, Windhoek, and other regional cities.

## By Sea

[Cape Town](/africa/southafrica/capetown) and [Durban](/africa/southafrica/durban) are both cruise ports. The V&A Waterfront in Cape Town handles most large cruise ships, and several operators include South Africa on southern Africa itineraries.

## Departure Tax

International departure tax is included in the price of airline tickets.
