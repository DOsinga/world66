Kimberley sits in the middle of South Africa, a long way from anything — [Johannesburg](/africa/southafrica/johannesburg) is 500 km away, [Cape Town](/africa/southafrica/capetown) nearly 1,000, [Durban](/africa/southafrica/durban) over 800. It exists because of diamonds. In 1871, the discovery of a massive diamond-bearing kimberlite pipe triggered a rush that brought tens of thousands of prospectors to the dry Northern Cape, and within a few years the chaotic tent city had become the diamond capital of the world.

The Big Hole is the main attraction — the largest hand-dug excavation on earth, an enormous pit that drops 215 metres from the surface. The adjacent open-air museum reconstructs the original rush town with period buildings, mining equipment, and exhibits on the diamond industry. It is genuinely well done and surprisingly atmospheric.

The modern city retains a scattering of Victorian and Edwardian buildings from the boom years. The William Humphreys Art Gallery, one of the best in South Africa outside the major cities, is worth a visit. The McGregor Museum, housed in a former sanatorium that was used as the British headquarters during the Siege of Kimberley in 1900, covers natural and cultural history.
