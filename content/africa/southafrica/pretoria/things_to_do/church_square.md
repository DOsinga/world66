Church Square (Kerkplein) is the historic heart of Pretoria, a large open plaza that has been the civic centre of the city since its founding in 1855. In the middle stands the famous statue of **Paul Kruger** by Anton van Wouw, guarded by four bronze burghers of the old South African Republic. The square is ringed by some of Pretoria's most important buildings: the sandstone **Palace of Justice** (where Mandela was tried in the Rivonia Trial in 1963–64, and where the modernist trials of the 1990s took place), the **Raadzaal** (the old Transvaal parliament), the Ou Kapitol and the old Reserve Bank. **Church Street**, which runs across the square, is the main axis of old Pretoria and one of the longest straight streets in the world at over 26 km.
