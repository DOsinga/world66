The modest single-storey house where the Boer president **Paul Kruger** lived from 1883 until he fled into European exile in 1900, a short walk from Church Square. Kruger was a man who famously refused to live in the presidential palace the Transvaal government built for him, preferring his own unpretentious home. Inside you can see his desks, pipes, the armchair he sat in for most of his official portraits, and the *Vierkleur* republican flag that flew over Pretoria on the day he left for Europe, never to return. In the back garden his **state coach** and his **private railway carriage** (built in the Netherlands) are on display. The Dutch Reformed Church where he worshipped and preached stands directly opposite.
