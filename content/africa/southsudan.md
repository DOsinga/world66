---
title: "South Sudan"
type: location
---

South Sudan is the world's youngest country, having gained independence from [Sudan](/africa/sudan) in 2011 after decades of civil war. It is also one of the least visited and most difficult countries in Africa for travelers. Civil conflict has continued since independence, and large parts of the country remain unsafe.

The capital [Juba](/africa/southsudan/juba), on the White Nile, is the main point of entry and the only city with anything resembling tourist infrastructure. It is chaotic, expensive, and fascinating in its rawness — a boomtown that has been through multiple cycles of hope and collapse. Outside Juba, travel is extremely challenging. Roads are mostly unpaved and many become impassable in the rainy season. There is very little public transport.

South Sudan has enormous natural potential — vast wetlands in the Sudd, savanna wildlife, and the culture of the Dinka, Nuer, and other Nilotic peoples. Boma National Park and Bandingilo National Park once hosted one of Africa's great wildlife migrations. Whether any of this is accessible depends entirely on the security situation, which changes unpredictably. Check current conditions very carefully before planning a trip, and consider whether your presence is helpful or just risky.
