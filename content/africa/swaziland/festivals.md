Eswatini's two great ceremonies are among the most remarkable cultural events in southern Africa. Both are living royal rituals, not tourist performances, and attendance comes with a responsibility to behave respectfully.

**Incwala (December–January)**

The Incwala is the most sacred of all Swazi ceremonies — a first-fruits ritual and celebration of kingship that renews the strength of the king and the nation. It is held around the time of the summer solstice, on dates set by the position of the moon. The ceremony unfolds over several days, with the culminating Big Incwala day involving song, dance, and the ritual tasting of the first harvest foods by the king.

Foreigners may attend parts of the ceremony, but dress and behaviour are taken seriously: modest clothing, no photography unless explicitly permitted, and absolute respect for instructions from royal officials. The setting at [Lobamba](/africa/swaziland/lobamba) is extraordinary. Check in advance whether public attendance is being welcomed in a given year.

**Umhlanga (Reed Dance, late August or early September)**

The Umhlanga draws tens of thousands of unmarried women and girls from across the country to [Lobamba](/africa/swaziland/lobamba). Over several days they travel to cut reeds, bring them to the Queen Mother's village to repair the windbreaks, and then on the final day perform a traditional dance before the king and the court. The spectacle of thousands of women in traditional dress — bare-chested, wearing beaded skirts — is extraordinary, though the ceremony has attracted criticism over the years for the practice of the king selecting additional wives from among the participants.

The event is open to visitors. Arrive early for good viewing; the main dancing day draws very large crowds. Dress respectfully.

**Other Events**

The MTN Bushfire Festival, held in late May at the House on Fire venue near [Manzini](/africa/swaziland/manzini), is an internationally recognised music festival celebrating African and world music. It draws a mixed local and international crowd and is one of the best music events in the region.
