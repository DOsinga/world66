title: "Sights"

---

Eswatini punches well above its size when it comes to things to see. The country is small enough to visit several of its highlights in a few days, yet varied enough — highland scenery, lowland bushveld, cultural sites, wildlife reserves — that it rewards a longer stay.

**Hlane Royal National Park**

Hlane is Eswatini's largest protected area and its only true national park. Located in the Lubombo region in the east, it is home to the country's only lion and elephant populations, as well as white rhino (Eswatini has one of the highest rhino densities in southern Africa), and large numbers of vultures and other raptors. Game drives, guided walks, and night drives are all available. The birdlife is exceptional. It is a straightforward three-hour drive from Johannesburg, making it an underrated alternative to the far busier Kruger.

**Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary**

The country's oldest conservation area sits in the [Ezulwini Valley](/africa/swaziland/ezulwinivalley), just a short drive from [Mbabane](/africa/swaziland/mbabane). It is a more relaxed experience than Hlane — no predators, which means you can walk, cycle, or ride horses through the reserve without a guide. Warthogs wander freely through the rest camp. It is a good first stop for those arriving from Johannesburg, and the accommodation options are some of the best value in the country.

**Mkhaya Game Reserve**

The most exclusive of the Big Game Parks properties, Mkhaya is a private reserve focused on conservation of black rhino and other rare species. Access is by guided vehicle only, and visitor numbers are strictly limited — book well in advance. The open Land Rover drives bring you remarkably close to the animals.

**Malolotja Nature Reserve**

In the northwestern highlands, Malolotja is the place to come for dramatic scenery and serious hiking. The reserve encompasses rocky gorges, waterfalls, and some of the oldest geological formations on earth. A canopy tour — zip lines and suspension bridges through a forested gorge — has become a major attraction. The wildflower meadows in spring (September–October) are outstanding.

**Mantenga Cultural Village and Falls**

In the Ezulwini Valley, Mantenga is both a living cultural village — where traditional Swazi homestead life is demonstrated — and the site of Mantenga Falls, the country's most photogenic waterfall. The cultural village is frankly a bit touristy, but it is well run and gives genuine insight into traditional Swazi architecture and customs. The falls themselves are worth the visit on their own.

**Lobamba**

[Lobamba](/africa/swaziland/lobamba) is the royal and legislative capital, a few kilometres south of Mbabane. The National Museum and the Somhlolo National Stadium are both here, and this is where the Incwala and Umhlanga ceremonies take place. It is not much to look at day-to-day, but at the right time of year — particularly for Umhlanga in late August — it is unforgettable.
