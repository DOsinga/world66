Tanzania is one of the great travel destinations of Africa. It has the continent's highest mountain, its most famous wildlife migration, its most storied spice island, and a coastline that has drawn traders and explorers for over a thousand years. Most visitors come for the safari parks — and they are extraordinary — but the country rewards those who look further.

The north is where most tourists go: the Serengeti, the Ngorongoro Crater, Kilimanjaro, and the gateway city of [Arusha](/africa/tanzania/arusha). The south and west have wilder parks — Ruaha, Selous, Mahale — that see far fewer visitors. [Zanzibar](/africa/tanzania/zanzibar) sits offshore with its own distinct culture, food, and history. [Dar es Salaam](/africa/tanzania/daressalaam) is the commercial hub, sprawling and chaotic, but worth a day or two.

Tanzania shares borders with [Kenya](/africa/kenya) and [Uganda](/africa/uganda) to the north, [Rwanda](/africa/rwanda), [Burundi](/africa/burundi), and the Democratic Republic of Congo to the west, and [Zambia](/africa/zambia), [Malawi](/africa/malawi), and [Mozambique](/africa/mozambique) to the south. The Indian Ocean defines its eastern edge.

The country is safe for travel by East African standards, genuinely friendly, and increasingly well set up for visitors. Costs are higher than you might expect — park fees alone can run to $70 or more per person per day — but independent travel is perfectly feasible outside the northern safari circuit.
