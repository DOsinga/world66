---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The best times for safari on the northern circuit are the dry seasons: June to October (long dry) and January to February (short dry). Wildlife viewing is best during the dry season, when animals congregate around water sources. The wildebeest migration in the Serengeti peaks between December and July, moving in a great arc.

The long rains (March to May) make some roads difficult and game harder to spot, but lodges are cheaper and the landscape is green. Arusha itself is pleasant year-round given its elevation; temperatures rarely exceed 28°C or drop below 10°C.
