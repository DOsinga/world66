Bagamoyo is a historic coastal town about 75 km north of [Dar es Salaam](/africa/tanzania/daressalaam). Founded in the late 1700s by families related to the ruler of [Oman](/asia/oman), it grew into one of the most important towns on the East African coast during the 19th century as the terminus of the slave and ivory trade route from Lake Tanganyika.

Catholic missionaries were allowed to settle in 1868, and their legacy can be explored in the Catholic Museum, one of the best small museums in Tanzania. By the late 19th century Bagamoyo was the German colonial capital, though they soon moved operations to Dar es Salaam and the town quietly declined.

A few kilometres south are the Kaole ruins — two medieval mosques and 13th-century tombs, evidence of the much older Swahili trading settlement that preceded the town. Several German-era stone buildings have been restored, and there is a German burial ground.

David Livingstone's body was carried through Bagamoyo on its way to [Zanzibar](/africa/tanzania/zanzibar) and eventually London. A good tarmac road from Dar es Salaam makes the town an easy day trip, and a handful of tourist hotels have opened in recent years.
