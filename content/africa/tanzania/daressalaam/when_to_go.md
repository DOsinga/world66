---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Dar es Salaam sits just below the equator on a humid coast, so it is warm year-round — daily highs hover in the high 20s to low 30s Celsius and the sea breeze rarely stops. The best months are the cool dry season from June to October: clear skies, lower humidity, and comfortable evenings. The short rains arrive in November and December as afternoon downpours that pass quickly. The long rains, from March through May, bring heavier and more sustained downpours and are worth avoiding if you can — roads flood and ferries to Zanzibar are sometimes cancelled. January and February are hot and sticky but mostly dry, and a good time to combine a city visit with the start of the safari season inland.
