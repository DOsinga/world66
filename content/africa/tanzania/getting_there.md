## By Air

Tanzania has three main international airports. Julius Nyerere International Airport (DAR) in [Dar es Salaam](/africa/tanzania/daressalaam) is the country's largest, handling most long-haul traffic. Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO), between [Arusha](/africa/tanzania/arusha) and [Moshi](/africa/tanzania/moshi), is the preferred entry point for northern safari circuits. Zanzibar's Abeid Amani Karume International Airport (ZNZ) receives direct flights from several East African hubs.

Direct or one-stop services operate from Europe, the Middle East, and elsewhere in Africa. Kenya Airways via Nairobi, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, and Qatar Airways via Doha are among the most reliable connections. Emirates, Turkish Airlines, and KLM also serve Dar es Salaam.

## Visas

As of 2025, Tanzania requires all visitors to obtain an eVisa before arrival — visa on arrival is no longer available. Apply at the official government portal (visa.immigration.go.tz). A single-entry tourist visa costs $50; multiple-entry is $100. Processing usually takes 7–10 working days; apply at least three weeks ahead during peak season (June–October, December–January).

Citizens of some East African Community and SADC member states are exempt. Check current requirements before travel, as rules have changed frequently in recent years.

Yellow fever vaccination is required if arriving from a yellow fever-endemic country, and you must carry your vaccination certificate. Malaria prophylaxis is strongly recommended throughout the country.

## Overland from Kenya

The main crossing is at Namanga, on the Arusha–Nairobi road, which sees frequent bus services in both directions. The journey from Nairobi to Arusha takes around five to six hours depending on border queues. Isebania/Sirare is the crossing used for travel between Kisumu and Mwanza. Both borders are busy and relatively straightforward.

## By Boat

Ferries connect [Zanzibar](/africa/tanzania/zanzibar) and [Pemba](/africa/tanzania/pemba) to Dar es Salaam. Zanzibar is also reachable by dhow from Mombasa, though this takes considerably longer than the ferry. Visitors to Zanzibar must purchase mandatory travel insurance from the Zanzibar Insurance Corporation on arrival.
