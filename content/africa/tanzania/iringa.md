Iringa is a provincial capital in the Tanzanian Southern Highlands, picturesquely situated on an escarpment at 1,600 metres. It is a peaceful town with surprisingly few tourists and an excellent base for exploring the southern part of the country. Within easy reach are the **Isimila Stone Age site**, [Ruaha National Park](/africa/tanzania/ruaha_national_par), and the western parts of [Udzungwa National Park](/africa/tanzania/udzungwa_national).

In town, **Neema Crafts** is a workshop and cafe that employs disabled artisans making paper from elephant dung, weaving, candlemaking, and recycling glass bottles into beaded jewellery. The cafe serves fresh-ground coffee, proper ice cream, and good light lunches. **Gangilonga Rock** is worth the climb for the views, but go in a group. The **market** is one of the best in Tanzania — look for the traditional Iringa baskets in the basket market.
