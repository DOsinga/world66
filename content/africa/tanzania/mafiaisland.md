Mafia Island sits about 130 km south of Dar es Salaam in the Indian Ocean, and it is deliberately, pleasantly off the beaten track. There are no big resort hotels, no nightclubs, and very little tourist infrastructure — which is exactly the point. People come here for the water.

Chole Bay, Mafia's deep-water anchorage, forms the heart of the Mafia Island Marine Park. Its natural horseshoe shape shelters calm beaches and a lagoon that fills with whale sharks between October and March. Beyond the bay, the reefs open up into some of East Africa's best diving: coral gardens, dramatic walls, and shelves teeming with fish that have never seen much pressure from divers. The visibility is often exceptional.

On land, the island is quiet. The village of Kilindoni handles most of the logistics — a small market, a handful of guesthouses, a dhow harbour. The ruins of an Arab-Swahili settlement at Kua on the neighbouring island of Juani are worth the short boat ride for anyone interested in the coast's medieval trading history.
