Moshi is the gateway to [Mount Kilimanjaro](/africa/kenya/mtkilimanjaro), a busy little town set where the plains begin to climb towards Africa's highest peak. On this southern side the mountain hides behind cloud for most of the day — the snow-capped summit only reveals itself in the early morning and late evening, if you are lucky. Moshi means "smoke" in Swahili, a fitting name for a place perpetually in Kilimanjaro's shadow.

The town is the starting point for most Kilimanjaro treks, and dozens of safari operators are based here, offering trips to the northern circuit parks — Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, and Tarangire. Kilimanjaro International Airport lies between Moshi and [Arusha](/africa/tanzania/arusha) to the west. Even if you are not climbing, Moshi makes a pleasant stop: the market is lively, the coffee grown on the mountain slopes is excellent, and the surrounding Chagga villages offer walks through banana plantations and waterfalls.
