Mwanza is Tanzania's second-largest city, spread along the southern shore of Lake Victoria among dramatic granite boulders. It is a busy, sprawling port town — not conventionally pretty, but with a character quite different from the tourist trail further east.

The main reason travellers pass through is proximity to the Serengeti. Mwanza's Ndabaka Gate provides access to the western corridor of the park, which sees far fewer visitors than the routes from [Arusha](/africa/tanzania/arusha). The Saanane Island National Park, a tiny reserve on an island in the lake just off the city centre, is worth a few hours for its views and wildlife.

The city has flights to [Dar es Salaam](/africa/tanzania/daressalaam), [Arusha](/africa/tanzania/arusha), and [Bukoba](/africa/tanzania/bukoba) on the western shore of the lake. The ferry across Lake Victoria no longer operates regularly, but the MV Victoria boat service runs on some routes.
