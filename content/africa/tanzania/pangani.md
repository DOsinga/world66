Pangani sits at the mouth of the Pangani River, about 50 km south of [Tanga](/africa/tanzania/tanga) on Tanzania's northeastern coast. It is a small, quiet town with a history that punches well above its weight — for centuries it served as an Arab trading settlement and slave port, and traces of that past are still visible in its carved doors, crumbling German colonial buildings, and old mosques.

Think of it as a mainland echo of [Zanzibar](/africa/tanzania/zanzibar), but without the crowds or the tourist infrastructure. The beaches north and south of town are largely empty, the coral reefs offshore are home to sea turtles, and the river itself winds through vast coconut plantations where you can watch the harvest and processing that supplies over half of Tanzania's coconuts.

Pangani is not a place you stumble on by accident. Getting here requires some effort, and once you arrive there is not much in the way of organized entertainment. That is precisely the appeal. Town elders double as walking tour guides, fishermen will take you out on the water, and the pace of life along the river is about as far from a resort experience as coastal Tanzania gets.
