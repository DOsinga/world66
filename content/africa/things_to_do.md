---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Africa is the continent where the natural world still runs the show. No amount of reading prepares you for the first time a herd of elephants crosses the road in front of your vehicle, or for the silence of a desert night with more stars than you thought existed. The range of experiences here is staggering, from snow-capped equatorial peaks to coral reefs to some of the oldest civilizations on earth.

The classic African safari remains one of the greatest travel experiences anywhere. The Serengeti in [Tanzania](/africa/tanzania) and the Masai Mara in [Kenya](/africa/kenya) deliver the big migration — over a million wildebeest on the move, trailed by predators. Kruger National Park in [South Africa](/africa/southafrica) is enormous and well-organized, good for self-drive trips. The Okavango Delta in [Botswana](/africa/botswana) is something else entirely: a vast inland delta where you glide through channels in a mokoro canoe with hippos nearby. Etosha in [Namibia](/africa/namibia) centers on a huge salt pan where animals gather at floodlit waterholes at night. Each park has a different character — don't assume one safari is like another.

Gorilla trekking in [Rwanda](/africa/rwanda) and [Uganda](/africa/uganda) is expensive and worth every dollar. You hike through dense mountain forest with a small group until you're sitting a few meters from a silverback and his family. Rwanda's Volcanoes National Park is the more polished experience; Uganda's Bwindi Impenetrable Forest is rougher, cheaper, and arguably more dramatic. Chimpanzee tracking in Uganda's Kibale Forest or Tanzania's Mahale Mountains is the less famous cousin and just as rewarding.

[Tanzania](/africa/tanzania)'s Mount Kilimanjaro is the big one — the highest point in Africa and climbable without technical gear, though altitude makes it genuinely tough. The Simien Mountains in [Ethiopia](/africa/ethiopia) offer multi-day treks along dramatic escarpments with endemic gelada baboons for company. [Morocco](/africa/morocco)'s High Atlas has good day hikes and longer routes through Berber villages. The Drakensberg in [South Africa](/africa/southafrica) is superb for walking, with San rock art tucked into caves along the trails.

The Sahara in [Morocco](/africa/morocco) and the Namib in [Namibia](/africa/namibia) are two very different desert experiences. In Morocco, camel treks from Merzouga to the Erg Chebbi dunes are a well-worn classic — spend a night in a desert camp and wake up for sunrise over the sand. Namibia's Sossusvlei has the tallest dunes on earth, bright orange against white clay pans. The Kalahari in [Botswana](/africa/botswana) is less dramatic but rich with wildlife and San Bushmen culture.

Africa has deep history that predates and extends well beyond the colonial period. The pyramids and temples of [Egypt](/africa/egypt) hardly need introduction, but the rock-hewn churches of Lalibela in [Ethiopia](/africa/ethiopia) are just as extraordinary and far less crowded. Great Zimbabwe's stone ruins rewrite assumptions about medieval African civilization. In West Africa, [Ghana](/africa/ghana)'s Cape Coast Castle and Elmina are essential and sobering visits for understanding the Atlantic slave trade. The island of Goree off Dakar carries similar weight. Timbuktu in [Mali](/africa/mali), once the richest city in the world, still has its ancient mosques and libraries.

The Red Sea coast of [Egypt](/africa/egypt) has some of the best wall diving on the planet, with visibility that seems almost unfair. Zanzibar off [Tanzania](/africa/tanzania) and the Quirimbas Archipelago in Mozambique offer warm Indian Ocean diving and snorkeling without the crowds. Cape Verde has solid surf. But Africa's cities deserve time too — Marrakech's souks in [Morocco](/africa/morocco), the live music scene in Bamako and Accra, the jazz clubs of Johannesburg, the markets of Lagos. African city life is loud, fast, and fascinating if you show up ready for it.
