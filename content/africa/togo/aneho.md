Aneho sits on a narrow sand spit between a lagoon and the Gulf of Guinea, 45 kilometres east of [Lomé](/africa/togo/lome) along the coastal road. It was the capital of Togo during the German colonial period until 1920, and the faded colonial architecture from that era still defines the town's character. The location near the border with [Benin](/africa/benin) makes it a natural stopping point for travellers crossing between the two countries.

The town offers three things: crumbling old buildings from the colonial era, a market that serves the surrounding area, and the beach along the Gulf of Guinea. It is a quiet, atmospheric place that sees few tourists — most people pass through rather than linger, but it rewards a few hours of wandering.
