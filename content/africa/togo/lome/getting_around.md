---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Getting around Lomé requires some patience. The city has no public bus system to speak of, so you are left with two options: motorcycle taxis (motos) and shared taxis. Motos are everywhere and cheap, but helmets for passengers are rare, speedometers usually do not work, and the driving is aggressive. Negotiate the fare before you get on. Shared taxis follow fixed routes and are safer, though drivers will not know street names — give landmarks instead. Neither motos nor taxis have meters, and foreigners will be quoted higher prices, so agree on a fare in advance. French is essential for both. The city center is compact enough to walk during cooler hours.
