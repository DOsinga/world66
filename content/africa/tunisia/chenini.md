Chenini is a hilltop Berber village in southern Tunisia, its ancient stone dwellings carved into the rock face and cascading down a steep ridge. A white mosque perches at the top, visible for miles across the dry plain below. It is one of the most striking villages in the country and one of the last places where the indigenous Berber language is still spoken in Tunisia.

The village is largely abandoned now — most residents have moved to the new town at the base of the hill — but the old granaries (ghorfas) and cave dwellings remain, and a few families still live among them. The views from the ridge are vast: endless arid landscape stretching toward the Sahara.

Chenini is usually visited as a day trip from [Tataouine](/africa/tunisia/tataouine), about 18 kilometres to the north. It can be combined with nearby Douiret, another spectacular abandoned hilltop settlement.
