---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Tunisia has a good festival calendar spread across the year, with the biggest events concentrated in summer when European tourists are around and the ancient amphitheatres come into their own.

**Festival of Carthage** (July-August) is the biggest cultural event in the country. International and Arab musicians, theatre companies and dance troupes perform in the ancient Roman amphitheatre near [Tunis](/africa/tunisia/tunis). The setting is spectacular and the programming genuinely eclectic.

**International Jazz Festival of Tabarka** (July) draws acts from across Africa and Europe to the northern coastal town of [Tabarka](/africa/tunisia/tabarka). The festival has run since 1973 and retains a good reputation.

**International Festival of Hammamet** (July-August) brings music, theatre and dance to an open-air amphitheatre right on the sea in [Hammamet](/africa/tunisia/hammamet). More accessible than Carthage for visitors staying on the coast.

**La Ghriba Festival** (May or early June, 33 days after the start of Passover) is a Jewish pilgrimage to the ancient El Ghriba synagogue on [Jerba](/africa/tunisia/jerba), drawing thousands of Jewish visitors from Tunisia's diaspora in France, Israel and elsewhere. It is one of the most striking events in the Tunisian calendar and open to non-Jewish visitors.

**International Festival of the Sahara** (December) takes place in [Douz](/africa/tunisia/douz) on the edge of the desert. Camel races, Saluki dog races, horse-riding displays, traditional music and poetry. A good reason to visit the south in winter.

**Tozeur International Oasis Festival** (December) celebrates desert culture with music, storytelling, camel and horse races. Combines well with the Sahara Festival if you're travelling the south in December.
