Entebbe is a quiet lakeside town on the northern shore of Lake Victoria, 37 kilometres southwest of Kampala. It serves as Uganda's main international gateway — Entebbe International Airport handles virtually all international flights — and many travellers pass through on their way to Kampala and the national parks. Those who stay longer find a genuinely pleasant town: cooler than Kampala, green, unhurried, and set amid tropical gardens and lake beaches.

The Entebbe Botanical Gardens, 100 acres of landscaped grounds bordering a kilometre of Lake Victoria shoreline, are one of the most beautiful green spaces in East Africa. They contain over 300 plant species and were used as a filming location for the original Tarzan films. The Uganda Wildlife Education Centre nearby is a rehabilitation zoo with over 200 animals — shoebill storks, chimpanzees, lions, and more — in spacious enclosures, accessible for a few dollars.

Lido Beach is the most popular of Entebbe's lake beaches; Aero Beach is scattered with retired aircraft, making it an unusual spot. Several resort hotels allow non-guests to use their pools and beach facilities for a small fee. Boats make day and overnight trips to the Ssese Islands archipelago and to Ngamba Island, a chimpanzee sanctuary run by the Uganda Wildlife Conservation Trust.
