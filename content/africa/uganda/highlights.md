---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**Gorilla trekking in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest.** This is the experience that puts Uganda on the map. Half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas live in Bwindi's dense, steep, mist-shrouded forest in the southwest. You hike for one to six hours through thick vegetation until your guide finds a habituated family, and then you sit with them for one hour. Watching a silverback feed, a mother nurse her infant, or juveniles wrestle in the undergrowth is overwhelming and humbling. Permits cost $700 and must be booked well in advance through the Uganda Wildlife Authority.

**The source of the Nile at [Jinja](/africa/uganda/jinja).** The world's longest river begins its 6,650-kilometer journey to the Mediterranean here, flowing out of Lake Victoria. [Jinja](/africa/uganda/jinja) has built an entire adventure industry around this stretch of river -- world-class white-water rafting, kayaking, bungee jumping, and stand-up paddleboarding. Even if adrenaline is not your thing, a sunset boat cruise to the source is a beautiful, contemplative experience.

**[Murchison Falls](/africa/uganda/murchison_falls_na).** The most powerful waterfall in the world, measured by volume per meter width. The entire Nile squeezes through a gap just six meters wide and explodes into the gorge below. You can hike to the top and stand at the edge feeling the spray on your face, or take a boat cruise up the river from Paraa and watch hippos, crocodiles, elephants, and buffalo along the banks. The park itself is Uganda's largest and home to recently reintroduced Rothschild's giraffes.

**Chimpanzee tracking in Kibale Forest.** Kibale has the highest density of primates in Africa -- 13 species, including around 1,500 chimpanzees. Guided tracking walks find habituated chimp communities almost every day. The forest is stunning, and you will likely see red colobus monkeys, grey-cheeked mangabeys, and L'Hoest's monkeys along the way.

**Queen Elizabeth National Park.** This park in western Uganda is remarkably varied -- savannah, wetlands, crater lakes, and tropical forest all within one park. The Kazinga Channel boat safari is one of the best in Africa, with elephants and buffalo coming to drink at the water's edge. In the southern Ishasha sector, tree-climbing lions drape themselves over fig tree branches -- a sight found almost nowhere else.

**Kidepo Valley National Park.** If you want to see Africa as it was before the tourists arrived, go to Kidepo. In the far northeast, bordering South Sudan and Kenya, this remote park sees only a handful of visitors per week. The landscape is dramatic -- wide valleys ringed by mountains -- and the wildlife includes species found nowhere else in Uganda: cheetah, ostrich, and greater kudu. Getting there is an adventure in itself, but that is the point.

**Lake Bunyonyi.** Tucked into the green hills near the Rwandan border, this deep, terraced lake is one of the most beautiful spots in Uganda. The water is bilharzia-free (rare in Africa), so you can swim. Dugout canoes connect the islands, the birdlife is exceptional, and the pace of life slows to something approaching zero. Many travelers stop here to decompress after gorilla trekking.

**The Rwenzori Mountains.** The legendary "Mountains of the Moon" rise to 5,109 meters at Margherita Peak, the third-highest point in Africa. The multi-day trek through surreal Afro-alpine vegetation -- giant lobelias, tree heathers draped in moss, and groundsels that look like something from another planet -- is unlike any mountain experience anywhere. This is serious trekking in wet, challenging conditions, but the reward is extraordinary.
