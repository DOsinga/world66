Zambia stretches from the southern shore of Lake Tanganika to the shores of Lake Kariba. It is bordered by [Malawi](/africa/malawi) and [Mozambique](/africa/mozambique) on the east, [Zimbabwe](/africa/zimbabwe) to the south, [Namibia](/africa/namibia) and [Angola](/africa/angola) to the west and Congo and Tanzania to the north. A large portion of the country is given over to wildlife with 19 national parks and 31 game management areas. The prime wildlife areas are the Luangwa valley and the South Luangwa National Park, Kafue National Park and the Lower Zambezi National Park.
