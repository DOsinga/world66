---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

A Zambian will tell you they haven't eaten unless they've had nshima, the stiff porridge made from mealie meal (corn meal). It's a great comfort food, usually eaten with meat and a variety of bean and vegetable dishes. Also recommended are roasted mealie cobs, boiled groundnuts, and the local Mosi beer, which is a little lighter than Castle.

If you're not eating in someone's home you're more likely to find western-style food, or Indian, Chinese, or a kebab.
