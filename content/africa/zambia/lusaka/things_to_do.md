---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Lusaka's main attractions are its museums, markets, and craft centres. You won't need more than a day or two to see the highlights, but the city's markets alone can absorb a good few hours.

The Lusaka National Museum is one of the most attractive public buildings in the city. Its four exhibit sections -- ethnography, witchcraft, history, and contemporary art -- tell the story of Zambia from independence in 1964 to the present day, with paintings, sculpture, and ceramics. Nearby, the Political Museum provides a compelling look at the political history of the country and of Africa as a whole.

The Lusaka City Market, a huge single-roof marketplace west of Cairo Road, houses over 4,000 stalls selling clothes, local art, jewellery, and food. For crafts and gifts, head to Kabwata Cultural Village, the Sunday craft market at Arcades Shopping Centre, Kubu Crafts at Manda Hill, or Abbabas House. The Moore Pottery Factory on the eastern side of Kabelenga Road features beautiful ceramics made by local village artists. The last Saturday of every month there's a craft market in the Kabulonga suburb at the Dutch Reform Church grounds.
