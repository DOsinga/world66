Bulawayo is Zimbabwe's second largest city, with a population of around 700,000, and the economic and cultural capital of Matabeleland. It's a slower, more relaxed city than Harare — the wide tree-lined streets were laid out on a generous colonial grid, and the pace of life in the city centre has a certain unhurried quality that Harare lost some time ago. The city was founded in 1894 on the site of King Lobengula's royal kraal after the defeat of the Matabele kingdom, and the National Museum of Zimbabwe here has strong collections covering the archaeology, natural history, and history of Zimbabwe's peoples.

Bulawayo is primarily used by travelers as a base for visiting Matobo National Park, about 30 kilometres south of the city — a remarkable landscape of granite kopjes, caves, and rock art, where Cecil Rhodes is buried at a spot he called "View of the World." The park also has significant concentrations of rhino. The city is on the main rail line from Victoria Falls to South Africa, which makes it a natural transit stop for travelers doing a southern Africa circuit.

The city has a proud railway heritage — the Bulawayo Railway Museum has an excellent collection of vintage steam locomotives — and the National Gallery has a small but interesting collection of Zimbabwean contemporary art.
