## Buses and minibuses

Long-distance buses connect the major cities. Companies like Intercity Express, Munhenzva and Pioneer run scheduled services between [Harare](/africa/zimbabwe/harare), [Bulawayo](/africa/zimbabwe/bulawayo) and [Victoria Falls](/africa/zimbabwe/victoriafalls). The Harare-Bulawayo run takes about five hours, Harare to Victoria Falls around 12 hours. Kombi minibuses fill the gaps, running fixed routes between smaller towns — they leave when full, not on a schedule, and driving standards are poor.

## Domestic flights

Fastjet and local charter operators fly between Harare, Bulawayo, Victoria Falls and Kariba. Flights are relatively affordable and save enormous amounts of time on the longer routes. Bush flights to remote safari camps can be arranged through tour operators.

## Self-driving

Driving is on the left. The main highways between Harare, Bulawayo and Victoria Falls are tarred and in reasonable condition, though potholes appear without warning. Secondary roads deteriorate quickly, especially in the rainy season — a 4x4 is essential for national parks and rural areas. Fuel availability has improved but can still be patchy outside major towns, so fill up whenever you can. Driving at night is strongly discouraged due to unlit vehicles, pedestrians and animals on the road.

## Rail

Zimbabwe's rail network exists but is extremely slow and unreliable. The overnight train between Harare and Bulawayo is an experience in itself — if it runs. Do not depend on trains for time-sensitive travel.

## Tour operators

For national parks and safari areas, most visitors use organized tours or self-drive with pre-booked accommodation. Tour operators in Victoria Falls and Harare can arrange transfers, guided safaris and multi-day itineraries. This is often the most practical way to reach places like [Mana Pools](/africa/zimbabwe/manapools) or [Gonarezhou](/africa/zimbabwe/gonarezhou).
