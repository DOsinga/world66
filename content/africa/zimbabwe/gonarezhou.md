Gonarezhou — the name means "place of many elephants" in Shona — is Zimbabwe's second largest national park and arguably its wildest. Sprawling across 5,000 square kilometres of the southeastern lowveld, it sits at the junction of Zimbabwe, Mozambique, and South Africa, forming part of the vast Great Limpopo Transfrontier Park. It sees a fraction of the visitors that Hwange attracts, which is precisely its appeal.

The landscape is dramatic and varied: sandstone cliffs glow red above the Runde River, mopane woodland stretches for miles, and the Runde and Save rivers carve their way through country that feels genuinely remote. The Chilojo Cliffs — towering red sandstone bluffs rising above a bend in the Runde — are the park's iconic sight, best seen from the Chipinda Pools area at sunset when elephant herds come down to drink.

Wildlife is exceptional and less habituated to vehicles than in more visited parks. Gonarezhou has large elephant populations, lion, leopard, wild dog, cheetah, buffalo, and an important population of nyala. Birding is outstanding, with species from both the Zambezian and Afrotropical biomes.

Access requires a 4WD and some advance planning. The closest upmarket option is the Malilangwe Wildlife Reserve, which adjoins the park and is home to Pamushana Lodge and Nduna Lodge. Basic camping is available at Chipinda Pools and along the Runde River — remote, unfenced, and genuinely wild.
