Harare is Zimbabwe’s capital and largest city, set at about 1,500 metres on the central plateau. It has been through very difficult times — hyperinflation, political repression, economic collapse — and the city shows the scars. But it remains a surprisingly green and pleasant place, with wide tree-lined avenues, parks, and a more relaxed atmosphere than you might expect.

The National Gallery is one of the best in southern Africa, with an excellent collection of Zimbabwean stone sculpture. The Harare Gardens next door make for a pleasant stroll. The Queen Victoria Museum covers natural history. For crafts and art, the open-air markets and the Chapungu Kraal sculpture park on the outskirts are worth visiting.

Day trips from Harare include the Lion and Cheetah Park, Lake Chivero Game Park, and the Tengenenge Sculpture Community — a remarkable open-air workshop where hundreds of sculptors work in stone.
