Afghanistan is one of the most beautiful and least accessible countries on earth. The Hindu Kush mountains cut through its heart, rising above 7,000 meters. The landscape ranges from high alpine valleys to scorching deserts, and the history runs deeper than almost anywhere in Central Asia. Alexander the Great marched through here. The Silk Road crossed it. Genghis Khan destroyed its greatest cities, and they were rebuilt, and destroyed again.

Since the Taliban's return to power in August 2021, Afghanistan is effectively closed to conventional tourism. Most embassies advise against all travel. There are no tourist visas in any normal sense, women face severe restrictions on movement and dress, and the security situation remains unpredictable. This is not a country you can casually visit.

That said, a small number of adventurous travelers do still enter, and what they find is extraordinary. The turquoise lakes of [Band-e-Amir](/asia/afghanistan/bamian) sit in the barren highlands of Bamyan province like something from another planet. The remains of the Bamiyan Buddhas -- destroyed by the Taliban in 2001 -- still draw visitors to their empty niches carved into sandstone cliffs. [Herat](/asia/afghanistan/herat)'s Friday Mosque is one of the finest pieces of Islamic architecture in the world. The Wakhan Corridor, a narrow strip of land reaching toward China, offers some of the most remote trekking on earth.

Afghan culture is defined by hospitality. The Pashtun code of Pashtunwali demands that guests be protected and honored, and travelers who have been to Afghanistan almost universally describe the warmth of the people as the most memorable part of the experience. Tea will be offered everywhere. Conversation is valued. Poetry -- particularly the works of Rumi, who was born in [Balkh](/asia/afghanistan/balkh) -- runs through daily life.

The country's cuisine is underrated: kabuli pulao (rice with lamb, raisins and carrots), mantu (dumplings), fresh naan baked in tandoor ovens. The bazaars of [Kabul](/asia/afghanistan/kabul) and [Mazar-i-Sharif](/asia/afghanistan/mazarisharif) are chaotic and vivid, selling everything from carpets to lapis lazuli.

Afghanistan demands respect, preparation, and a clear-eyed understanding of the risks. It is not for everyone. But for those drawn to places where history, landscape and human resilience intersect, there is nowhere quite like it.

## Destinations

- [Kabul](/asia/afghanistan/kabul) -- the capital, battered but alive
- [Herat](/asia/afghanistan/herat) -- western Afghanistan, close to the Iranian border
- [Mazar-i-Sharif](/asia/afghanistan/mazarisharif) -- the Blue Mosque and the north
- [Kandahar](/asia/afghanistan/kandahar) -- the Taliban's spiritual heartland
- [Bamian](/asia/afghanistan/bamian) -- the Buddhas, Band-e-Amir lakes
- [Balkh](/asia/afghanistan/balkh) -- ancient "Mother of Cities"
- [Ghazni](/asia/afghanistan/ghazni) -- former capital of a great empire
- [Jalalabad](/asia/afghanistan/jalalabad) -- gateway to the Khyber Pass
- [Kunduz](/asia/afghanistan/kunduz) -- northern crossroads
- [Maimana](/asia/afghanistan/maimana) -- remote Faryab province
