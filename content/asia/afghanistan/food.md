---
title: "Food"
type: section
---

Afghan cuisine is hearty, aromatic and built around rice, bread and grilled meat. It shares DNA with Persian, Central Asian and Indian cooking but has its own distinct character.

**Kabuli pulao** is the national dish -- long-grain rice cooked with lamb, raisins, carrots and almonds, often flavored with cardamom. Every family has its own recipe, and it is the centerpiece of any celebration.

**Mantu** are steamed dumplings filled with spiced ground meat and onions, topped with a tomato-based sauce and yogurt. They are labor-intensive to make and usually reserved for special occasions or guests.

**Ashak** are similar to mantu but filled with leeks and served with meat sauce and yogurt. A specialty of [Kabul](/asia/afghanistan/kabul).

**Bolani** is a stuffed flatbread filled with potatoes, leeks or pumpkin, fried crisp on a griddle. It is street food at its best -- cheap, filling and available everywhere.

**Kebabs** are grilled over charcoal in every bazaar and roadside stall. Lamb and chicken are most common. Chapli kebab, a spiced minced meat patty originally from the Pashtun regions, is particularly good.

**Bread** is essential to every meal. Afghan naan -- large, oblong flatbreads baked in tandoor ovens -- is freshly made throughout the day. Tearing off pieces to scoop up food is the standard way to eat.

**Tea** is served constantly. Green tea is preferred in the north and [Kabul](/asia/afghanistan/kabul), black tea in the south and east. It comes sweet and strong, often flavored with cardamom.

**Fruit** is Afghanistan's glory. The pomegranates, grapes, melons, mulberries and apricots are among the best in the world. Dried fruits and nuts -- almonds, pistachios, raisins -- are served with tea and sold in every bazaar.
