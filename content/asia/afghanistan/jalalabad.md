Jalalabad sits in eastern Afghanistan on the main road from [Kabul](/asia/afghanistan/kabul) to the Khyber Pass and on to [Pakistan](/asia/pakistan). It is the capital of Nangarhar province, with a population of around 200,000, and commands the entrances to the Laghman and Kunar valleys.

The city has a subtropical climate -- warm and green compared to the rest of Afghanistan -- and was once popular as a winter retreat. In the 1970s it was a regular stopover for overlanders traveling from London to Kathmandu. The orange groves and gardens that surround the city give it a lush character unusual for Afghanistan.

Jalalabad's historical roots run deep. It was founded by the Mughal emperor Akbar in the 16th century and named after his son Jalal. The old city retains some of its Mughal-era character, though decades of conflict have taken their toll. The road from Kabul to Jalalabad, winding through the Kabul Gorge, is one of the most dramatic drives in the country -- and one of the most dangerous.
