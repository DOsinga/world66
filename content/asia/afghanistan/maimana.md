Maimana (also spelled Maymana) is the capital of Faryab Province in northwestern Afghanistan, a predominantly Uzbek and Turkmen city set in a fertile valley between dry hills. It sits well off the main routes through the country and is rarely visited by outsiders, which gives it a character untouched by the tourism infrastructure found elsewhere in Afghanistan.

The surrounding region is known for its carpet weaving. Faryab produces some of Afghanistan's finest traditional rugs — deep reds, rich blues, and tight geometric patterns that have been traded on the Silk Road for centuries. The bazaar in the centre of town is a working market, not a showcase, and the pace of life here follows rural Afghan rhythms.

Travel to Maimana is difficult and, as of the mid-2020s, effectively impossible for most foreign visitors due to the security situation and near-total absence of foreign travel infrastructure throughout Afghanistan. The province has seen sustained conflict over many decades. Those with a specific reason to be here — journalists, aid workers — should check current conditions carefully before making any plans.
