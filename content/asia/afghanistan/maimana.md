Maimana is the main town of Faryab province in northwestern Afghanistan. It is a remote, predominantly Uzbek and Turkmen city, far from the usual routes and rarely visited by outsiders. The surrounding countryside is known for its carpet weaving -- Faryab produces some of Afghanistan's finest traditional rugs, with deep reds and geometric patterns.
