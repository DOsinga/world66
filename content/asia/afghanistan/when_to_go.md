---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Afghanistan has a continental climate with extreme variations. Summers are scorching in the lowlands (40C+ in [Jalalabad](/asia/afghanistan/jalalabad) and [Kandahar](/asia/afghanistan/kandahar)) and pleasant in the mountains. Winters bring heavy snow to the Hindu Kush, closing mountain passes and making travel in the highlands impossible.

**Spring (April-May)** is the best time. Temperatures are mild, the landscape turns green, and mountain passes begin to open. The almond blossoms around Jalalabad are famous.

**Autumn (September-October)** is equally good. Clear skies, warm days and cool nights. The harvest season means fresh fruit everywhere -- Afghanistan's pomegranates, grapes and melons are superb.

**Summer (June-August)** is brutally hot in the south and east, though the highlands around [Bamian](/asia/afghanistan/bamian) and the Wakhan Corridor remain comfortable. Dust storms are common in the lowlands.

**Winter (November-March)** is harsh. The Salang Pass closes regularly, cutting [Kabul](/asia/afghanistan/kabul) off from the north. Temperatures in Kabul drop well below freezing. The central highlands are snowbound. Only the lowlands around Jalalabad remain mild.

Ramadan affects travel significantly -- restaurants close during daylight hours, and the pace of life slows. The dates shift annually according to the Islamic calendar.
