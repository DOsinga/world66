**Looking for Dilmun** by Geoffrey Bibby. The classic account of archaeological excavations in Bahrain and the Gulf, tracing the lost Dilmun civilization. Written with real flair -- part adventure story, part scholarly detective work. Essential reading if you are interested in Bahrain's ancient history.

**First Light: Modern Bahrain and Its Heritage** by Hamad Bin Al Khalifah. A richly illustrated overview of the country's history and culture from a member of the ruling family. Useful for understanding how Bahrain sees itself.

**The Making of the Modern Gulf States** by Rosemarie Said Zahlan. Covers the political history of Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, the UAE, and Oman. Good context for understanding how these small states navigated between larger powers.

**Arabian Adventure: A Dream Achieved** by Gifford S. Rossi. A personal account of life and culture in the Gulf region, with insights into the rapid modernization of countries like Bahrain.

**Bahrain: A Travel Guide** by Philip Ward. An older guidebook but still valued for its detailed descriptions of historical sites and architecture across the islands.
