---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Manama's key sights are spread out — plan on using taxis or a rental car. The Bahrain National Museum on the waterfront is the essential first stop for context; next door, the small but striking Beit al-Qur'an holds a fine collection of illuminated Qur'ans. The Bab al-Bahrain gate marks the entrance to the old souq, where the gold and spice stalls are busy in the evenings.

Out of the city centre, Qal'at al-Bahrain (Bahrain Fort) covers thousands of years of occupation and is best visited in late afternoon. The UNESCO-listed Muharraq Pearling Path, a 3.5-kilometre walking route past restored merchants' houses, is a different angle on the country's pre-oil history. A trip south of the city to the "Tree of Life" — a single large mesquite standing improbably in empty desert — is an easy day out.
