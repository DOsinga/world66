---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Bahrain has a hot desert climate, and timing your visit matters. The best months are **November through March**, when temperatures are a pleasant 20-25°C and the humidity drops to bearable levels. This is peak tourist season and when outdoor activities are most enjoyable.

**Summer (June through September)** is brutal. Temperatures regularly exceed 40°C with suffocating humidity, and even short walks outdoors become unpleasant. If you visit in summer, expect to spend most of your time in air-conditioned spaces. Hotel rates drop considerably, which is the one upside.

**Spring and autumn** (April-May, October) are transitional -- warm but not yet unbearable, with occasional sandstorms in spring.

The **Formula 1 Bahrain Grand Prix**, usually held in March, is the biggest event on the calendar and draws crowds from around the Gulf. Book accommodation well in advance if you are visiting during race weekend.

**Ramadan** shifts dates each year (moving roughly 11 days earlier annually). During Ramadan, the pace of daily life changes -- restaurants are closed during the day, and nightlife pauses. The evenings come alive after iftar, though, and it can be a fascinating time to experience local culture. Just be prepared to adjust your own eating and drinking habits during daylight hours.
