---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Most international visitors arrive by air at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport (DAC) in [Dhaka](/asia/bangladesh/dhaka). It has direct connections to major hubs across Asia and the Middle East, including Dubai, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok, Doha, and Kolkata. Airlines serving Dhaka include Biman Bangladesh Airlines, Emirates, Singapore Airlines, Qatar Airways, and several others. Shah Amanat International Airport in [Chittagong](/asia/bangladesh/chittagong) handles a smaller number of international flights, mainly to Middle Eastern destinations.

Citizens of many countries can obtain a visa on arrival at Bangladeshi airports for US$50, valid for 30 days. The process takes anywhere from 20 minutes to two hours depending on how many flights are on the ground. Check the Bangladesh e-visa portal at visa.gov.bd before departure to see if you qualify, or apply in advance to avoid the queue. Some nationalities still require a visa sticker obtained at an embassy before travel.

**Overland from India**, there are several land border crossings. The busiest is Benapole-Petrapole on the Dhaka-Kolkata route, with regular bus services on both sides. Hili (near [Rajshahi](/asia/bangladesh/rajshahi)), Tamabil (near [Sylhet](/asia/bangladesh/sylhet)), and Burimari (in the north) are other options. Crossing procedures can be slow and bureaucratic -- allow a full day for any land border crossing. Direct Dhaka-Kolkata bus services run by Shyamoli, Green Line, and others take around 12 hours including the border stop.

**From Myanmar**, the Teknaf-Maungdaw border crossing in the southeast has historically been restricted to locals and is not a reliable entry point for international travelers.
