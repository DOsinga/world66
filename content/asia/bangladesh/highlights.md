---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**The Sundarbans.** The world's largest mangrove forest, shared with India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the last refuge of the Bengal tiger. Multi-day boat trips from Khulna or Mongla take you deep into the tidal waterways, where you will see spotted deer, crocodiles, river dolphins, and an extraordinary variety of birds. Tiger sightings are rare but possible. Even without one, the sheer scale and silence of the mangroves are unforgettable.

**Cox's Bazar beach.** At 120 kilometers, it is the longest unbroken sandy beach in the world, and it remains remarkably free of mass tourism. The golden sand stretches south from [Cox's Bazar](/asia/bangladesh/coxsbazar) town toward Teknaf, backed by green hills. Sunsets here are spectacular. Further south, [St. Martin's Island](/asia/bangladesh/coxsbazar/sights/st_martins) is Bangladesh's only coral island.

**Old Dhaka.** The historic heart of [Dhaka](/asia/bangladesh/dhaka) is a sensory overload in the best possible way. Lalbagh Fort, the pink palace of Ahsan Manzil, the Star Mosque with its mosaic decoration, and the chaos of Sadarghat river terminal -- where hundreds of ferries jostle for space on the Buriganga River -- are all packed into a few square kilometers.

**The tea gardens of Sylhet.** The rolling hills around [Sylhet](/asia/bangladesh/sylhet) and Srimangal are covered in manicured tea plantations that feel like a different country from the flat delta. Srimangal, known as the tea capital of Bangladesh, is a peaceful base for walks through the gardens and visits to the Lawachara National Park, home to the endangered hoolock gibbon.

**River journeys.** Bangladesh is the land of rivers, and traveling by water is both practical and magical. The overnight launch from Dhaka to Barisal passes through a landscape of river islands, fishing boats, and enormous skies. The Rocket paddle steamer, one of the last in the world, runs the same route when it is operational.

**Sonargaon and Panam City.** Just 30 kilometers from Dhaka, the ruined merchant quarter of Panam City is a haunting collection of crumbling colonial mansions lining a single street. The adjacent Folk Art Museum displays a rich collection of traditional crafts, textiles, and boats.
