---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The best time to visit Bangladesh is during the cool, dry winter from **November to February**. Temperatures are pleasant (20-28°C), skies are clear, and the countryside is lush and green from the receding monsoon. This is peak season for the Sundarbans, [Cox's Bazar](/asia/bangladesh/coxsbazar), and [Sylhet](/asia/bangladesh/sylhet).

**March to May** is brutally hot and humid, with temperatures climbing above 35°C. April and May also bring the first pre-monsoon cyclone risk in the Bay of Bengal. This is the least comfortable time to travel.

**June to October** is the monsoon season. Rain is torrential and relentless, especially in the northeast around Sylhet, which is one of the wettest places on earth. Flooding is widespread -- roads become impassable, ferries stop running, and large parts of the country go underwater. The monsoon is dramatic and beautiful if you are prepared for it, but it makes practical travel very difficult. Cyclones are a serious risk in the Bay of Bengal from late April through November, with peaks in May-June and October-November.

If you can only choose one month, **December** or **January** offer the most reliably pleasant weather across the whole country.
