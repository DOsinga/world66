Phuntsholing is the main gateway to [Bhutan](/asia/bhutan) from India, sitting on the southwest border where the Himalayan foothills meet the Indian plains. The town faces Jaigaon on the Indian side, and the border crossing between them is one of the busiest in the country — a constant flow of shoppers, traders, and travellers moving back and forth.

From here, the only road north climbs steeply through dramatic mountain scenery to [Thimphu](/asia/bhutan/thimphu), about 180 kilometres and seven hours by bus. The drive is spectacular but exhausting. An immigration checkpoint at Kharbandi, just above town, is where Indian nationals show their permits; other foreigners will have arranged their Bhutanese visa in advance through a tour operator.

Phuntsholing is Bhutan's second-largest town and has a different feel from the highland cities — warmer, more hectic, and noticeably cheaper for food and clothing thanks to the open border with India. It is not a destination in itself, but a necessary transit point for overland travellers entering from the south.
