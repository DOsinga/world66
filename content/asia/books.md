---
title: "Books"
type: section
---

Asia has inspired some of the best travel writing and fiction of the last century. These are worth reading before you go — or carrying with you.

**The Great Railway Bazaar** by Paul Theroux is the book that set the standard for Asian travel writing. Theroux takes trains from London through Turkey, Iran, Afghanistan, India, and Southeast Asia, and is funny, honest, and occasionally brutal about what he finds. His later *Riding the Iron Rooster* covers a year of train travel through China and is equally good.

**The Quiet American** by Graham Greene is set in 1950s Vietnam during the dying days of French colonialism. Short, precise, and prescient about American involvement in Asia — it reads like it was written last year.

**Shantaram** by Gregory David Roberts is a long, sprawling novel set in the Mumbai underworld. It polarizes readers, but no other book captures the overwhelming quality of India quite as vividly. Good preparation for a first visit.

**First They Killed My Father** by Loung Ung is a survivor's account of growing up under the Khmer Rouge in Cambodia. Essential reading before visiting Angkor Wat or the Killing Fields.

**The Beach** by Alex Garland was the book that defined Southeast Asian backpacker culture in the 1990s. It holds up as a novel about the dark side of paradise-seeking, and Thailand's islands as a setting.

**In Xanadu** by William Dalrymple traces the route of Marco Polo across the Middle East and Central Asia to China. Dalrymple's later *City of Djinns*, about Delhi, and *The Anarchy*, about the East India Company's conquest of India, are also outstanding.

**Oracle Bones** by Peter Hessler is the best book about modern China for a traveler — intimate, grounded, and attentive to ordinary lives rather than political abstractions. His *River Town*, about living in a small Chinese city, is equally recommended.

**Silk Road** by Colin Thubron is a spare, moving account of traveling through Central Asia and western China. Thubron walks carefully through countries still figuring out what they are after the Soviet collapse.

**A Fortune-Teller Told Me** by Tiziano Terzani is the account of a year spent traveling through Southeast Asia by every means except air, after a fortune-teller warned him not to fly. Part travel book, part philosophical meditation — unusual and very good.
