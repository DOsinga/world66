Bandar
Seri Begawan is the capital of the Sultanate of Burnei in Southeast
Asia, and offers a unique and lasting experience for travelers who
hunger for something different than the norm. Here the globe traveler
can find a variety of attractions and experiences beyond the normative
Asian experience.

**What to Do **

Bandar Seri Begawan offers numerous
open-air markets, mosques and temples, and water villages. Guided
private tours are available, spanning from the typical city photo tour
to an “Eco-Tour” through the surrounding jungle wetlands. Bandar Seri
Begawan also offers majestic river tours via “water taxi” through the
quaint water villages surrounding the area. A must-see for
visitors to the area is the Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque, a stunning and
beautiful active mosque open to visitors through much of the day.

Anyone could easy spend a full day or more in the exotic open air
markets, taking in the scents and sights in the bustle of a true Asian
open air market. Additionally, any visitor to the area would be remiss
in not visiting Makam Di-raja, or the Royal Mausoleum. This tomb has
been used for generations of Sultans and offers a portrait on the rich
history and culture of the region.

**Where to Stay **

Focusing on budget options, KH
Soon Resthouse, LeGallery Hotel and the Brunei Hotel all offer
affordable stays within the city. Bunei Hotel offers a hotel experience
likely to be close to the standard Western hotel stay. Further from the
city limits would be APEK Utama Hotel, offering a quaint and affordable
stay at the cost of distance from downtown Bandar Seri Begawan. If
budget is the primary concern, the Brunei International Airport offers
the standard budget airport hotels, though any visitor
should keep in mind that this area is quite some distance from Bandar
Seri Begawan itself. Bus trips in and out of the city add to the cost
of the stay, and should be budgeted in accordingly.

**Where to Eat **

What
Bandar Seri Begawan may be lacking in eatery variety, it more than
makes up in quality. By far the most popular restaurant with travelers,
Aminah Arif Restaurant offers a selection of Bruneian fare as well as
dishes that may be more familiar to travelers. Aminah Arif
Restaurant’s menu is very reasonably priced, so experiencing the local
fare is made even more enjoyable by not breaking the wallet.
