---
title: 'Highlights'
type: section
---

**Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque** -- The most iconic building in Brunei sits on an artificial lagoon in the heart of [Bandar Seri Begawan](/asia/brunei/bandarseribegawan). Built in 1958, it combines Mughal and Italian Renaissance architecture with a golden dome, marble walls, and stained glass from England. A full-size royal barge floats in the lagoon. Visit at dusk when the mosque is beautifully lit against the darkening sky.

**Kampong Ayer** -- The world's largest water village is home to around 30,000 people living in houses on stilts over the Brunei River. It has its own schools, mosques, fire station, and police post. Take a water taxi (BND2-5) from the waterfront and wander the wooden boardwalks. Many residents welcome visitors into their homes.

**Ulu Temburong National Park** -- Brunei's "Green Jewel" is 550 square kilometers of pristine primary rainforest in the Temburong District. The highlight is the canopy walkway, a metal structure rising 50 meters above the forest floor that gives breathtaking views across unbroken jungle. Getting there is half the adventure: a speedboat ride from BSB to [Bangar](/asia/brunei/bangar), then a longboat up the Temburong River. Day trips can be arranged through local tour operators.

**Istana Nurul Iman** -- The official residence of the Sultan of Brunei is the largest residential palace in the world, with 1,788 rooms and 257 bathrooms. It is closed to the public except during the three-day Hari Raya Aidilfitri celebration at the end of Ramadan, when the Sultan opens the palace gates and personally greets visitors. Even outside this period, the palace is impressive viewed from across the river.

**Gadong Night Market** -- Every evening from around 4pm, this open-air market comes alive with stalls selling grilled fish, satay, nasi katok, and dozens of other local dishes at rock-bottom prices. Because Brunei sees relatively few tourists, this remains a thoroughly local experience.

**Jame'Asr Hassanil Bolkiah Mosque** -- Brunei's largest mosque, built to commemorate the Sultan's 25th year of reign. Its 29 golden domes are spectacular, and the interior is opulent with Italian marble and English chandeliers. It is open to non-Muslim visitors outside prayer times.

**Mangrove River Cruise** -- A boat trip along the Brunei River into the mangrove forests is one of the most rewarding nature experiences in the country. You are likely to spot proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, monitor lizards, and a variety of birdlife. Cruises are best done in the late afternoon.
