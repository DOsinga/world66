---
title: 'When to Go'
type: section
---

Brunei has a tropical equatorial climate, meaning it is hot and humid year-round. Temperatures hover between 24-32°C (75-90°F) regardless of the month, and humidity rarely drops below 75%.

The wettest months are September through January, with the heaviest rainfall typically in November and December. Rain usually comes in intense afternoon downpours that clear within an hour or two, so it rarely ruins a full day of sightseeing. February and March tend to be the driest and most pleasant months.

The best time to visit is between February and October, with March through May being ideal -- less rain, slightly cooler temperatures, and comfortable conditions for exploring the rainforest.

Keep Ramadan in mind when planning your trip. During the fasting month, many restaurants close during daylight hours. On the other hand, Hari Raya Aidilfitri (the celebration at the end of Ramadan) is an extraordinary time to visit -- the Sultan opens Istana Nurul Iman to the public for three days. Ramadan dates shift by about 11 days each year.

The Sultan's birthday is celebrated for two weeks beginning July 15, with fireworks, parades, and cultural performances in [Bandar Seri Begawan](/asia/brunei/bandarseribegawan).
