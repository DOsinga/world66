---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

No trip to Cambodia starts anywhere but [Angkor Wat](/asia/cambodia/angkorwat). The sheer scale of the temple complex is hard to overstate — you could spend three days here and still miss entire sections. Get up before dawn for sunrise over the main temple, then lose yourself in the jungle-strangled ruins of Ta Prohm and the eerie stone faces of Bayon.

In [Phnom Penh](/asia/cambodia/phnompenh), the Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda are dazzling, all gold spires and glittering floors. But the city demands something harder of you too. The Killing Fields at Choeung Ek and the Tuol Sleng genocide museum are devastating, and they should be. You cannot understand modern Cambodia without confronting what happened here. Go early, bring water, and give yourself time to sit quietly afterward.

[Tonle Sap](/asia/cambodia/siemreap) is Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake, and the floating villages are genuinely surreal — entire communities of houses, schools, and shops bobbing on the water. Visit during the wet season when the lake swells to five times its dry-season size.

Down south, [Sihanoukville](/asia/cambodia/sihanoukville) has grown hectic in recent years, but the islands are worth the boat ride. Koh Rong and Koh Rong Samloem still have stretches of white sand that feel properly remote. For something quieter on the mainland, head to [Kep](/asia/cambodia/kep) and eat pepper crab by the water.

[Battambang](/asia/cambodia/battambang) is the Cambodia that package tourists miss — a languid riverside town surrounded by rice paddies, hilltop temples, and villages where the bamboo train still rattles along its single track. Nearby [Kampot](/asia/cambodia/kep) is equally charming, known for its pepper plantations, French colonial architecture, and the kind of slow pace that makes you extend your stay.
