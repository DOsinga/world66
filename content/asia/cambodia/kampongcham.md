Kampong Cham is a port city on the banks of the Mekong River, about 120 kilometres northeast of [Phnom Penh](/asia/cambodia/phnompenh). It is Cambodia's third-largest city and one of the more pleasant provincial towns to spend a day or two — relaxed, walkable, and less visited than the Angkor circuit.

The town's most significant sight is Wat Nokor, a well-preserved 13th-century Khmer temple complex a short tuk-tuk ride from the centre. Nearby, the hill of Phnom Pros is topped by a stupa and offers views over the Mekong plain. A sobering reminder of the Khmer Rouge era, mass graves lie close to town — sites that draw visitors seeking to understand Cambodia's difficult recent history.

The Mekong waterfront is the heart of local life, with a bamboo bridge connecting the town to the island of Koh Paen during the dry season — a remarkable feat of annual reconstruction by local villagers.
