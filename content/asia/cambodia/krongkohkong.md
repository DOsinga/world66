Krong Koh Kong is a small coastal town in Cambodia's far southwest, tucked between the Cardamom Mountains and the Gulf of Thailand. It sits just 80 km from Thailand's [Koh Chang](/asia/thailand/kohchang), and the border crossing at Cham Yeam makes it a common entry point for travellers coming overland from the Thai side.

The town itself is quiet and unpolished — a handful of guesthouses, a riverside market, and not much else. The real draw is what surrounds it: the largely untouched Cardamom Mountains to the north, mangrove estuaries along the coast, and Koh Kong Island offshore, a large and almost empty island with long stretches of white sand. Boat trips to the island and jungle treks into the Cardamoms can be arranged locally. There are flights from [Phnom Penh](/asia/cambodia/phnompenh) and the town is also reachable by road from [Sihanoukville](/asia/cambodia/sihanoukville).
