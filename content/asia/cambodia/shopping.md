---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Cambodia produces some genuinely beautiful things and shopping here can mean supporting traditions that were nearly wiped out during the Khmer Rouge years.

**Silk and textiles** are among the best buys. Traditional Khmer silk weaving — nearly destroyed under the Khmer Rouge — has been painstakingly revived. Look for ikat-woven pieces (hol) in deep jewel colors, and the simpler cotton krama, the checkered scarf that every Cambodian uses for everything from a head covering to a baby carrier. Silk pieces with gold and silver thread embroidery are also available.

**Silver work** is another traditional craft: bowls, trays, and figurines in classical Khmer and Chinese styles. The craftsmanship can be excellent; buy from reputable shops rather than the most tourist-facing stalls.

**Stone and wood carvings** — replicas of Angkor figures, apsara dancers, Buddhas — range from excellent to mediocre. The better pieces come from workshops that employ trained artisans. In Siem Reap, look for the work associated with **Artisans d'Angkor**, a social enterprise that trains young Cambodians in traditional crafts and sells through its own shops. Prices are higher than the market stalls but quality and ethical sourcing are reliable.

**Gemstones** — Cambodia has deposits of rubies, sapphires, and other stones, particularly near Pailin. Unless you know what you're doing, approach gems with caution: authenticity is hard to verify and cutting is often poor by international standards.

**Kampot pepper** is one of Cambodia's finest products — complex and aromatic, with genuine provenance. Buy it at markets or direct from the farms around Kampot. Much of what's sold at tourist stalls in Siem Reap is not the real thing.

Markets are the best and cheapest places to shop. In Phnom Penh, Psar Thmei (Central Market) and Psar Tuol Tom Poung (Russian Market) are both excellent. In Siem Reap, the Old Market (Psar Chas) is central and well-stocked.
