Stung Treng is a small provincial capital on the Mekong in northeastern Cambodia, close to the Lao border. Most travellers pass through on their way between Laos and the rest of Cambodia, but it is worth a stop.

The Mekong Blue silk weaving project, which trains vulnerable women in traditional weaving skills, produces beautiful silk scarves and textiles — a good place to pick up a distinctive souvenir. The town sits at the confluence of the Mekong and Sekong rivers, and the surrounding area has some of the best-preserved flooded forest and wetlands in the country. Boat trips along the Mekong north toward the Lao border pass through stretches where Irrawaddy dolphins are sometimes spotted.
