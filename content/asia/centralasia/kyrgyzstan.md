Kyrgyzstan calls itself the Switzerland of Central Asia. In some sense this epitheton is more deserved than in others. It is quite off the mark when it comes to the level of income, but very justifiable when it comes to the beauty of the mountainous landscape.

 Compared with the dry and hot plains of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan it is quite refreshing to go into the high mountains of Kyrgystan. Kyrgyzstan may not have a series of medieval towns, but it has all the natural beauty to make up for that. Furthermore the poeple are very friendly and helpful. This makes traveling in Kyrgyzstan relatively easy.

 Compared with its neighbours, Kyrgyzstan might lack in cultural heritage, but the natural beauty can be overwhelming. In Soviet times much of the country was closed to visitors because of its strategic importance, closeness to China and the Uranium mines in the Tian Shan mountains. Now it is the most of open of the Central Asian states.

 [Bishkek](/asia/centralasia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek) the capital is a green and friendly city. It is the travel hub and the best starting place to explore the country. You can visit the [Ala archa gorge](/asia/centralasia/kyrgyzstan/alaarchagorge) as an easy day trip from there. [Osh](/asia/centralasia/kyrgyzstan/osh) and Naryn are further away.
