China is one of the most varied countries on earth. Within its borders you get the Tibetan Plateau, the Gobi Desert, the karst mountains of Guilin, the subtropical forests of Yunnan, and some of the world's most dynamic cities. The sheer scale of the place means no single trip covers it all — and that's part of the appeal.

The country has been transformed by four decades of rapid development. Cities that were provincial backwaters in the 1990s now have metro systems, high-speed rail connections, and skylines to rival anywhere. But step an hour outside the big cities and you'll find villages where life moves at a completely different pace, where minority cultures have their own festivals, languages, and ways of doing things.

Travelers come for many reasons: the imperial monuments of [Beijing](/asia/china/beijing), the terracotta warriors at [Xi'an](/asia/china/xian), the cosmopolitan energy of [Shanghai](/asia/china/shanghai), the rice-paddy landscapes around [Guilin](/asia/china/guilin), the old trade routes of the Silk Road through [Dunhuang](/asia/china/dunhuang) and into Xinjiang. [Tibet](/asia/china/tibet) remains one of the most spiritually charged landscapes anywhere — if you can get the permits. [Hong Kong](/asia/china/hongkong) operates under its own rules and is worth a trip in itself.

Traveling here takes a little preparation. English is spoken in tourist areas but disappears quickly once you go off the beaten track. The internet is filtered, which affects apps and services you rely on at home. Payments are mostly digital — WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate — and foreign cards can be tricky. None of this is insurmountable, but arriving unprepared makes things harder.

## Destinations

- [Beijing](/asia/china/beijing) — the capital, with the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, and several sections of the Great Wall within reach
- [Shanghai](/asia/china/shanghai) — China's financial center, a fascinating collision of Art Deco Bund architecture and ultramodern Pudong
- [Xi'an](/asia/china/xian) — home of the Terracotta Army and a well-preserved city wall; starting point for Silk Road journeys
- [Guilin](/asia/china/guilin) and [Yangshuo](/asia/china/guilin/yangshuo) — the karst landscape that appears in every Chinese painting; take the Li River cruise
- [Chengdu](/asia/china/sichuanprovince/chengdu) — relaxed, food-obsessed Sichuan capital; giant panda research base nearby
- [Hong Kong](/asia/china/hongkong) — its own world: dense, vertical, efficient, and great for eating
- [Macau](/asia/china/macau) — Portuguese colonial architecture meets casino sprawl; a short ferry from Hong Kong
- [Dunhuang](/asia/china/dunhuang) — desert oasis on the Silk Road; Mogao Caves are extraordinary
- [Hangzhou](/asia/china/hangzhou) — West Lake, green tea, and canals; a beautiful city Marco Polo called the finest in the world
- [Harbin](/asia/china/harbin) — the far northeast; famous for its ice sculpture festival in January
- [Suzhou](/asia/china/suzhou) — classical gardens and silk; manageable as a day trip from Shanghai
- [Tibet](/asia/china/tibet) — requires a permit; [Lhasa](/asia/china/tibet/lhasa) and the monasteries reward the effort
- [Yunnan Province](/asia/china/yunnanprovince) — the most ethnically diverse region; [Lijiang](/asia/china/yunnanprovince/lijiang) and Shangri-La are the main draws
- [Xinjiang Province](/asia/china/xinjiangprovince) — the far west; Silk Road towns, Uyghur culture, and the Taklamakan Desert
