## Travel Writing

**River Town** by Peter Hessler (2001) — Hessler spent two years as a Peace Corps English teacher in the small Sichuan city of Fuling in the mid-1990s, just as the Three Gorges Dam was about to flood it. A patient, deeply observed account of a China that was already disappearing. His follow-up **Oracle Bones** covers history and modernity with equal intelligence, and **Country Driving** follows the car boom of the 2000s.

**Shark's Fin and Sichuan Pepper** by Fuchsia Dunlop (2008) — Dunlop went to Chengdu to learn to cook and wrote a memoir that is equal parts food writing, travel, and cultural immersion. The best introduction to why Sichuan food is so extraordinary.

**Mr. China** by Tim Clissold (2004) — A British investment banker's account of trying to do business in China in the 1990s. Funny and honest about how spectacularly wrong things can go.

## History

**Wild Swans** by Jung Chang (1991) — Three generations of women in a single Chinese family, from the last years of the warlords through the Cultural Revolution. One of the most read books about modern China, and deservedly so.

**The Rape of Nanking** by Iris Chang (1997) — A thorough and unflinching account of the 1937 Japanese massacre in Nanjing. Essential context if you visit the city.

**The Search for Modern China** by Jonathan Spence (1990) — The standard scholarly history from the late Ming dynasty to the present. Dense but authoritative.

## Fiction

**Balzac and the Little Chinese Seamstress** by Dai Sijie (2000) — Two young men are sent to re-education in a remote mountain village during the Cultural Revolution and discover a suitcase of Western novels. Short, elegant, and moving.

**Wolf Totem** by Jiang Rong (2004) — A young man sent to Inner Mongolia during the Cultural Revolution becomes fascinated by the wolves and the Mongol nomads who live alongside them. A meditation on nature, civilization, and what China has lost.

**Beijing Coma** by Ma Jian (2008) — A student activist shot during the Tiananmen Square massacre lies in a coma and relives the events of 1989. Harrowing and important.
