Datong sits on the dry, windy plateau of northern Shanxi, close to the Mongolian border and far from the polished tourist circuits of eastern China. For centuries it was a frontier garrison town, and the landscape still has that stark, edge-of-empire feel.

The reason to come here is the Yungang Grottoes, a complex of 53 cave temples carved into a sandstone cliff in the 5th century. The sheer scale of the carvings — some Buddha figures stand over 15 metres tall — makes this one of China's most impressive ancient sites. The old walled city centre has been extensively restored, with the Nine Dragon Screen and the Huayan and Shanhua temples worth a visit. Beyond town, the Hanging Monastery at Hengshan clings improbably to a cliff face and can be combined with a day trip to the sacred peak itself.
