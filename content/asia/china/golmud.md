Golmud (also known as Geermu) is the second city of Qinghai province — a sparse, dusty town whose main attraction is as a jumping-off point for Tibet. Route 109 runs buses up onto the Tibetan Plateau, and the Qinghai-Tibet Railway makes Golmud the last major stop before Lhasa.

Once part of Tibetan Amdo, Qinghai now owes its existence mainly to mining, and the largest ethnic group is Han Chinese. Golmud is easy to navigate — the streets are laid out in a grid. Kunlun Lu is the main commercial street in the centre.

The town has few attractions of its own. If you find yourself with time to kill, take advantage of the cheap shopping — this may be your last opportunity before Tibet. Come prepared: information is sparse, few people speak English, and a good phrase book will serve you well.
