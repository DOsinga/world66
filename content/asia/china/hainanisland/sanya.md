Sanya is China's answer to a tropical beach resort — a city on the southern tip of Hainan Island where the air is warm year-round, coconut palms line the shore, and the pace of life is noticeably slower than on the mainland. It has been heavily developed into a domestic tourism destination, but there is genuine appeal here: white sand beaches, warm clear water, fresh seafood, and a cultural diversity that includes the Hui Muslim minority community on the western edge of town.

The beaches are the main attraction and they are properly good. Yalong Bay, 35 km from the airport, is the resort strip — manicured and quiet, with turquoise water. Dadonghai, closer to the city centre, has more local life and better eating options nearby. Sanya Bay is the longest stretch and the most accessible. All three offer different experiences, and it is worth visiting more than one.

Beyond the beaches, Nanwan Monkey Island (reached by cable car) and the Binlang Ethnic Li Village give a different character to the place. The market at Sanya sells tropical produce — mangoes, dragonfruit, jackfruit — that can go straight into a bag. Prices are generally lower than on the mainland for comparable quality. Sanya has an international airport with flights from many Chinese cities and some international routes.
