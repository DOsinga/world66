Xinglong is a small town on Hainan's eastern coast, known across China for its natural hot springs and tropical produce. The mineral-rich spring water is piped directly into hotel rooms at a regulated temperature, making it a popular destination for Chinese tourists seeking a warm soak. Rubber and coffee are the main cash crops here — the local Xinglong coffee has a devoted following and offers a curious counterpoint in the world's largest tea-drinking country.

Near town is the Indonesian Village, a cultural attraction that draws tour groups with performances of Indonesian singing, dancing, and snake acts, rounded off with a complimentary cup of the famously sweet local coffee. Xinglong has a sizeable community of ethnic Chinese who returned from Indonesia and other Southeast Asian countries in the 1950s and 1960s, which gives the town a mild tropical flavour unlike anywhere else on the island.
