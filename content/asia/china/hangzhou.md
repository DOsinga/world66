Hangzhou sits at the southern end of the Yangtze Delta, about 180 km southwest of Shanghai, and has been considered one of China's most beautiful cities for a thousand years. Marco Polo visited in the 13th century and called it "the finest and most splendid city in the world." The claim still has some force.

The centre of everything is West Lake (Xihu), a large freshwater lake bordered on three sides by wooded hills. Its shores and causeways are planted with willows and peach trees, its islands dotted with pavilions, and the surrounding hills hold temples, pagodas, and the terraced tea plantations that produce Longjing — China's most prized green tea. The lake and its landscape were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2011.

Hangzhou is also China's silk capital. The trade has been here since at least the Song dynasty, and the city remains one of the best places in the country to buy silk fabric. Combined with the tea, the lake, and a handful of excellent temple complexes, Hangzhou makes a very good two- or three-day stop, and is easy to reach from Shanghai by high-speed train in under an hour.
