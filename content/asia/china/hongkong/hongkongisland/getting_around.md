---
title: "Getting Around Hong Kong Island"
type: section
---

Hong Kong Island's north shore is well-served by the MTR Island Line, running from Kennedy Town in the west through Central, Wan Chai, and Causeway Bay to Chai Wan in the east. The line runs frequently and connects to the rest of the network at multiple interchanges.

The **trams** — double-decker, green and cream — run the same east-west corridor along the north shore from Kennedy Town to Shau Kei Wan, passing through Sheung Wan, Central, Wan Chai, and Causeway Bay. Slower than the MTR but cheap and good for short hops or sightseeing; the front seat of the upper deck has the best view.

**Buses** serve the south side of the island, which the MTR doesn't reach. The 6, 6A, 6X, and 260 routes connect Central to Aberdeen, Repulse Bay, and Stanley. Bus 15C connects Edinburgh Place in Central to the Peak Tram lower terminus. Route information is available on the Citybus/NWFB apps or at bus stops.

**Central-Mid-Levels Escalator** is the world's longest outdoor covered escalator system, running from Central Market up through SoHo and the Mid-Levels residential district. It runs downhill in the morning (until 10am) and uphill the rest of the day. Useful for getting from Central to SoHo without the climb.

Taxis on Hong Kong Island are red with a silver roof. They can be flagged on the street but do not stop at bus stops.
