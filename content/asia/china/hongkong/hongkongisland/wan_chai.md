Immediately east of Central, Wan Chai is where the old and new Hong Kong collide most visibly — wet markets and tong lau tenements along Stone Nullah Lane and Cross Street sit a block from the convention centre and the glass towers along the harbour. Once famous as the setting for Richard Mason's *The World of Suzie Wong*, the neighbourhood has gentrified but kept its edge: cha chaan tengs, old temples (Hung Shing, Pak Tai), tailors, and a dense cluster of restaurants and bars on Lockhart Road and Johnston Road. Wan Chai MTR puts you in the middle of it.
