---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Hong Kong Island covers the full range from three-Michelin-star restaurants in Central to cheap lunch counters in Sheung Wan.

**Central and Soho** are the most concentrated areas for restaurants. Soho (along Staunton Street, Elgin Street, and the streets connected by the Mid-Levels escalator) has dense competition and good variety — Italian, Japanese, French, and Cantonese side by side. Prices are moderate to high. The restaurants in IFC Mall and Pacific Place cater to a business lunch crowd.

**Wan Chai** offers better value. Johnston Road and the streets north of it have Chinese restaurants, noodle shops, and a few older-style Cantonese places that haven't yet been converted to something trendier. Fook Lam Moon on Johnston Road is a long-established Cantonese restaurant known for expensive traditional dishes; the lunch dim sum is less prohibitive.

**Causeway Bay** is dense with Japanese and Korean restaurants — the area has a large Japanese residential community — as well as everything else. Times Square food court covers many budgets.

**Aberdeen and Stanley** on the south side offer seafood restaurants with a more relaxed waterfront atmosphere. The floating restaurants in Aberdeen are tourist-oriented and not particularly good value; the seafood at the working jetty restaurants is better.

For cheap and authentic, any cha chaan teng (Cantonese tea restaurant) in the mid-levels or Western District serves milk tea, congee, and wonton soup for very little. Tai Cheong Bakery in Central is the most famous source of egg tarts on the island.
