title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Victoria Peak is the obvious starting point for any visit to Hong Kong Island — the view over the harbour from the top is the defining image of the city, and the Peak Tram ride itself is part of the attraction. The circular Lugard Road walk around the Peak takes about 45 minutes and is free.

[Hong Kong Park](/asia/china/hongkong/hongkongisland/things_to_do/hong_kong_park) in Central is an excellent urban park with a walk-in aviary and the Museum of Tea Ware in the Flagstaff House. [Man Mo Temple](/asia/china/hongkong/hongkongisland/things_to_do/man_mo_temple) on Hollywood Road is one of the oldest temples on the island, atmospheric and free.

The SoHo arts district around Hollywood Road and Peel Street is good for an afternoon walk — antique shops, galleries, and restaurants, connected to Lan Kwai Fong by the outdoor escalator. [Fringe Club](/asia/china/hongkong/hongkongisland/things_to_do/fringe_club) on Lower Albert Road hosts live music and events in a heritage building.

Ocean Park on the south side of the island suits a full day — theme park rides, a large aquarium, and a giant panda exhibit. The Dragon's Back Trail, accessible from Shau Kei Wan MTR, is the best urban hike on the island. Most hikers finish at Big Wave Bay beach.

The south side generally rewards exploration: the fishing village-turned-restaurant district of Aberdeen, the beach at Repulse Bay, and the colonial-era Stanley Market are all within 30 minutes of Central by bus.
