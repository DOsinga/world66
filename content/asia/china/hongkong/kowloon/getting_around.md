---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Tsim Sha Tsui, Yau Ma Tei, and Mong Kok are all walkable from one another, and the MTR covers the rest of Kowloon efficiently. The Tsuen Wan Line (red) and Kwun Tong Line (green) run through the main tourist and commercial districts. The East Rail Line (blue) heads north through the New Territories from Hung Hom.

The Star Ferry from Tsim Sha Tsui to Central (or Wan Chai) on Hong Kong Island runs every few minutes from early morning until late evening. The Cross-Harbour Tunnel carries buses (KMB and various cross-harbour routes) and taxis under the harbour; the harbour taxi surcharge applies.

Taxis in Kowloon and urban Hong Kong are red. They do not stop at bus stops and cannot cross to the New Territories without the passenger paying a return surcharge. Most drivers know Nathan Road and the main hotels; having your destination written in Chinese characters helps for anything off the main tourist routes.

The Kowloon-Canton Railway (now the East Rail Line) runs from Hung Hom station north to the mainland border at Lo Wu and Lok Ma Chau. Hung Hom is also where high-speed trains to Guangzhou and Beijing depart from the West Kowloon terminus (a few stations west).
