title: "Shopping"

---

Kowloon is where most of Hong Kong's street-level shopping happens. Nathan Road — the "Golden Mile" — runs north from the Star Ferry terminal through Tsim Sha Tsui, lined with camera shops, electronics, jewellery, and fashion. Prices are negotiable in smaller shops. Be cautious of electronics shops on Nathan Road that quote aggressive prices — check receipts carefully.

[Harbour City](/asia/china/hongkong/kowloon/shopping/harbourcity) on Canton Road is one of Asia's largest malls, with 700+ shops across several interconnected buildings.

**Mong Kok** has Kowloon's best concentrated street shopping: Ladies' Market on Tung Choi Street (clothing and accessories, noon to midnight), the [Flower Market](/asia/china/hongkong/kowloon/shopping/flowermarket) on Flower Market Road, the Goldfish Market nearby, and the Yuen Po Street Bird Garden — all within walking distance of Prince Edward or Mong Kok MTR stations.

The **Jade Market** under the flyover at Kansu Street in Yau Ma Tei is worth a browse — hundreds of stalls selling jade, beads, and Chinese ornaments. Buying genuine jade requires knowledge; the atmosphere is worth experiencing regardless.

**Sham Shui Po** (MTR Sham Shui Po station) is where locals buy electronics and computer parts at wholesale prices. The Golden Computer Arcade and Apliu Street electronics flea market are genuinely useful if you know what you want.

Chungking Mansions on Nathan Road is a labyrinth of budget guesthouses, foreign exchange booths, Indian and Pakistani restaurants, and small trade shops — chaotic, fascinating, and worth a wander even if you don't need anything.
