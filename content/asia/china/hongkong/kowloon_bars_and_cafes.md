---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Kowloon's bar scene is centred around Tsim Sha Tsui, particularly Knutsford Terrace and Ashley Road — a cluster of outdoor tables and bar fronts that gets lively after 9pm. The crowd is mixed: tourists, expats, and locals.

**Knutsford Terrace** (off Kimberley Road) is the main outdoor bar street in Kowloon, with a dozen or so venues competing for the same stretch. More relaxed than Lan Kwai Fong on the Hong Kong Island side, and slightly cheaper.

The lobby bar at the **Peninsula Hotel** on Salisbury Road is worth experiencing at least once — the formal tea service from 2 to 7pm is an institution, the string quartet plays in the high-ceilinged lobby, and the prices are exactly what you'd expect from one of Asia's grand old hotels.

For karaoke, the private-room style (KTV) is found throughout Kowloon in various price brackets. Red Box Karaoke on Observatory Road is a reliable choice that has been around long enough to develop a following.

Mong Kok and Yau Ma Tei have local-style cafes and cha chaan teng at street level — good for milk tea and cheap lunches but not for evening drinks in the Western sense.
