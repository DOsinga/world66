---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Kowloon offers the full range from street-level noodle shops to rooftop destination restaurants with harbour views. Nathan Road itself is mainly tourist-trap restaurants; the better eating is in the side streets.

**Tsim Sha Tsui** has a concentration of good restaurants in the streets between Nathan Road and Canton Road. The newer development at One Peking Road has several high-end restaurants with exceptional harbour views — Aqua Roma on the 29th floor combines Italian and Japanese cuisine with a view of the Island skyline.

**Mong Kok** is the best place in Kowloon for cheap, authentic eating. The streets around Sai Yeung Choi Street South and Fa Yuen Street have dense concentrations of noodle shops, roast meat restaurants, and dai pai dong. Prices are low and the food is genuine.

The **Peninsula Hotel** on Salisbury Road offers formal Cantonese dining at Spring Moon and formal high tea in the lobby from 2pm daily. The lobby tea is an institution that's worth the price once.

**Yau Ma Tei** around Temple Street has street food stalls from late afternoon, serving seafood, noodles, and local dishes at straightforward prices.
