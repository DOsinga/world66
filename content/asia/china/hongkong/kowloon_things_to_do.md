title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Kowloon's main attractions are spread across the peninsula, from the waterfront district of Tsim Sha Tsui in the south to the temple and market areas of Yau Ma Tei and Mong Kok further north.

The **Tsim Sha Tsui waterfront promenade** is the best place to see the Hong Kong Island skyline, particularly in the evening when the Symphony of Lights runs at 8pm (a daily laser and light show across the harbour). The [Clock Tower](/asia/china/hongkong/kowloon/things_to_do/clock_tower) at the Star Ferry terminal is a heritage landmark from the old Kowloon-Canton Railway.

[Kowloon Park](/asia/china/hongkong/kowloon/things_to_do/kowloon_park) in Tsim Sha Tsui is a good open space with a bird lake, sculpture walk, and swimming pool. The **Hong Kong Museum of History** in Tsim Sha Tsui East has an excellent permanent exhibition on Hong Kong's history from prehistoric times through the 1997 handover — well worth two hours. The adjacent **Hong Kong Science Museum** suits children.

Further north, [Wong Tai Sin Temple](/asia/china/hongkong/kowloon/things_to_do/wong_tai_sin_temple) is Hong Kong's most active Taoist temple and one of the most interesting places in the territory to observe religious practice. The **Temple Street Night Market** in Yau Ma Tei and the **Ladies' Market** on Tung Choi Street in Mong Kok are both worth an evening or afternoon.

Mong Kok is also where to come for the **Flower Market** (Prince Edward Road West), the **Goldfish Market** (Tung Choi Street, north of Ladies' Market), and the **Bird Garden** (Yuen Po Street) — three specialised street markets within walking distance of each other.
