---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Lantau is connected to the rest of Hong Kong by ferry and by the MTR Tung Chung Line. The MTR terminates at Tung Chung in the northeast, near the airport and the base of the Ngong Ping 360 gondola. From Tung Chung, buses reach most parts of the island.

The main bus routes are: NLB 23 (Tung Chung to Ngong Ping, for the Big Buddha and Po Lin Monastery), NLB 3M (Tung Chung to Mui Wo), and various routes along the south coast to Cheung Sha beach. Bus frequency drops in the evening.

Ferries connect Mui Wo on the east coast to Central Pier 6 (roughly 35 minutes fast ferry, 55 minutes ordinary). Ferries also serve Discovery Bay, a residential area on the northeast coast.

Cycling is popular on the flat land around Mui Wo. Bicycle hire is available in Mui Wo village. The north Lantau expressway is off-limits to cyclists, but the country roads in the centre and south of the island are manageable. The Lantau Trail (70km) circles the island and is the primary long-distance hiking route.
