---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The New Territories rewards those who make the effort to get out of the urban core. Hiking, heritage villages, and temples are the main draws, alongside the Sha Tin Racecourse for something more local.

The **MacLehose Trail** (100km, east to west across the territory) is Hong Kong's most celebrated long-distance route. Most people hike individual sections rather than the whole thing. Stage 2 from Pak Tam Chung to Long Ke offers the finest coastal scenery. Stage 1 crosses the Sai Kung Country Park with views over the High Island Reservoir.

**Sha Tin Racecourse** is one of the world's largest, with seating for 85,000 and computerised totalisator boards. Meetings are held on Saturdays and Sundays from September to June. The atmosphere — the scale of the crowds, the noise, the genuine passion for the sport — is worth experiencing even if you don't bet.

For heritage, the **Tsang Tai Uk** walled village in Sha Tin is the best surviving example of a fortified clan village in the territory. The **Ten Thousand Buddhas Monastery** above Sha Tin (more than 13,000 Buddha statues line the walls) requires a climb of 400 steps but is extraordinary. **Che Kung Temple** in Sha Tin is an active Taoist temple dedicated to a deified general; it draws huge crowds at Lunar New Year.

The **Sai Kung** fishing town on the eastern peninsula has a harbour full of sampans and a row of seafood restaurants where you choose your dinner from tanks outside the door. Ferries from the pier take you to otherwise inaccessible beaches on the outer peninsula.
