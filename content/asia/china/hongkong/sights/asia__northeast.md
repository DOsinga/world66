The highest point in Hong Kong, Victoria Peak, is visited by 6 million
tourists each year. You should not miss this
popular tourist sight and its panoramic views of Hong Kong. This is a
great first or last stop on your tour of Hong Kong. It is a perfect
first stop because as you look out over Hong Kong from Victoria Peak
you can get a feel for the layout of the city and see from above the
famous tourist sights you will soon see on the ground. Just as perfect
for a farewell to Hong Kong, looking out at the vast city and reliving
the new memories made on your trip.

**Victoria Peak at Night**

Most people view Victoria Peak during the day but the view at night
is just as spectacular and much more romantic. To get to the top of
Victoria Peak most people take the Peak Tram, which is much like an old
time trolley car. The Peak Tram is an eight minute ride. There is
usually a long line to take the Peak Tram from the bottom to the top,
but a small line to take the Peak Tram from the top to the bottom.
There is a double-decker public bus available that will take you up or
down. The front seat on the top level is the best seat on the bus for
the view.

**View of Hong Kong's Landmarks**

From Victoria Peak you can see the bustling harbor and all the
commercial and recreational boats in the harbor below. Several
architectural landmarks can be seen including the 72 story Bank of
China building and the 78 story Central Plaza.

**Take Photos**

Once at the top, on Victoria Peak, you will want to take a series of
pictures, but there are other activities as well. You can take more
photos at Madame Tussauds Hong Kong. There are over one hundred wax
replicas of celebrities including many famous locals. There is also a
Ripley’s Believe It or Not Odditorium housed on the second and third
floors of the Peak Tower.

**Peak Tower Mall**

Not surprisingly, there is a large shopping mall at Victoria Peak.
The mall is in the Peak Tower and there is plenty of fancy shopping and
over priced restaurants. The most economical snacks and drinks are from
the convenience store that is across from the Peak Tower. For
additional views from Victoria Peak stroll the lovely nature trails and
botanical gardens.

The view is free at Victoria Peak and the Peak Tram is only a few U.S. dollars.
