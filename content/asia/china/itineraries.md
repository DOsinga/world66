China is enormous and no single trip covers it all. The best approach is to pick one region and go deep rather than rushing between famous sights. Here are a few suggestions.

## Classic Highlights — 3 Weeks

[Beijing](/asia/china/beijing) (4 nights) → [Xi'an](/asia/china/xian) (2 nights) → [Guilin](/asia/china/guilin) and Yangshuo (3 nights) → [Shanghai](/asia/china/shanghai) (3 nights). This is the standard circuit and it's standard for good reason: it covers imperial history, the terracotta warriors, the karst landscape, and the most modern city in China. All connections are easy by high-speed train or flight.

## Shanghai and the East — 10 Days

Start in [Shanghai](/asia/china/shanghai) (3 nights), then take the high-speed train to [Hangzhou](/asia/china/hangzhou) (2 nights) for West Lake and green tea. From there, go to [Suzhou](/asia/china/suzhou) for a day — the classical gardens are compact and beautiful — then to [Nanjing](/asia/china/nanjing) (2 nights) for its history and the city walls. Return to Shanghai by train.

## Silk Road — 2 Weeks

Fly to [Xi'an](/asia/china/xian) (2 nights), then take the train or fly west to Dunhuang ([Dunhuang](/asia/china/dunhuang), 2 nights) for the Mogao Caves and the singing sands. Continue into Xinjiang to Turpan ([Turpan](/asia/china/xinjiangprovince/turpan), 1 night) for the desert ruins and Uyghur culture, then [Urumqi](/asia/china/xinjiangprovince/urumqi) (1 night) as a transit hub, and finally [Kashgar](/asia/china/xinjiangprovince/kashgar) (3 nights) for the old town and the Sunday bazaar. This route requires planning — some areas have restricted access.

## Southwest China — 2 Weeks

Fly into [Kunming](/asia/china/yunnanprovince) (2 nights), then head north to [Lijiang](/asia/china/yunnanprovince/lijiang) (3 nights) for the old town and Tiger Leaping Gorge. Continue to Shangri-La (2 nights) if altitude permits. Alternatively, head east from Kunming into Guizhou to see the Miao minority villages around Kaili (2 nights). The southwest is the most ethnically diverse part of China and rewards slow travel.

## Tibet — 10 Days Minimum

Allow at least 10 days for [Tibet](/asia/china/tibet). Fly into [Lhasa](/asia/china/tibet/lhasa) (5–6 nights) and acclimatize properly before doing anything strenuous. Day trips from Lhasa cover the Potala Palace, Jokhang Temple, Drepung Monastery, and Sera Monastery. From Lhasa, a longer circuit west can take in Shigatse and Gyantse. Note: Tibet requires a special permit in addition to a China visa.
