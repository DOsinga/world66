---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan) is Nanjing's great green lung and holds most of the city's most important historical sights clustered together. The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, with its long marble staircase up the hillside, is the most visited. Nearby stand the Ming Xiaoling tombs — the founder of the Ming dynasty rests here, approached along a spirit road lined with massive stone animals — and the Linggu Temple pagoda.

In the city itself, the Ming city walls encircle the old centre and you can walk long stretches of them. The Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall and the Confucius Temple area along the Qinhuai River are the other essential stops. The Qinhuai at night, with its lantern-lit pleasure boats, is where Nanjing most feels like a poem from the Tang dynasty.
