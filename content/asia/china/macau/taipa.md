Taipa is one of the two main islands that make up Macau's territory alongside the peninsula. It is now largely continuous with Macau city via the Cotai Strip — the reclaimed land between Taipa and [Coloane](/asia/china/macau/coloane) that has become a dense concentration of casino resorts. The original village of Taipa, however, retains a pleasant Portuguese colonial character that is worth seeking out.

The Taipa House Museum (Casas-Museu da Taipa) is a row of five restored Portuguese villas overlooking a garden, offering a glimpse of how Macau's Portuguese community lived in the early 20th century. Nearby, the Church of Our Lady of Carmel and several Tin Hau temples reflect the island's layered religious heritage. The village lanes have a good selection of traditional Macanese restaurants, making it a welcome contrast to the casino strip.
