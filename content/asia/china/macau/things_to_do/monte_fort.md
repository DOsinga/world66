---

A 17th-century Jesuit fortress on the hill above the Ruins of São Paulo, famous for the day in 1622 when its cannons repelled a Dutch naval invasion and secured Portuguese rule over Macau. The ramparts now house the **Macau Museum**, which tells the city's story from both Chinese and Portuguese perspectives, and the view from the top is one of the best in the city.
