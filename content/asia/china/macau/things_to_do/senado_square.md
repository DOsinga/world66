The heart of the old city — a long open square paved in a wave pattern of black-and-white Portuguese *calçada* tiles, lined with pastel colonial buildings housing banks, churches and cafes. The 18th-century **Leal Senado** (Loyal Senate) building on the south side served as the colonial city hall and still houses local government. From here, pedestrian lanes run north to the Ruins of São Paulo.
