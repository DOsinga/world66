A good place to sample local specialties is the Jiangsu Restaurant. Nanjing pressed, salted duck is slathered with roasted salt, steeped in clear brine, baked dry and then kept under cover for some time; the finished product should have a creamy-colored skin and red, tender flesh.   The vegetarian restaurant at Jiming Temple is worth visiting for view more than for its food. High on the hill overlooking Xuanwu Lake Park, it's very cheap and opens daily from 8am. to 5:30pm.  

 If you look for cheaper food, you should go to the Ninghai Rd, Street stalls and restaurants there serve dumplings and steamed buns or you can get a bowl of noodles or Chinese pastries just out of oven.   Like other big cities, you'll always see KFC and McDonald's outlets all over the town.  

**Restaurants**

**Sichuan Restaurant**

A famous Nanjing restaurant, as the name suggests, the specialty is Sichuan cuisine, but other regional cuisines are catered for. 

address: 171 Taiping Lu

tel: 025-4402038

**Dechangxing Restaurant**

The best jiaozi (dumpling) restaurant in Nanjing with several varieties of this popular eastern Chinese staple. 

address: 38 Hunan Lu

tel: 025-3619879

**Dingshan Meishi Cang**

One of Nanjing's more up-market restaurants. The restaurant is built in traditional style, and serves cuisine from the local area of Huainan. 

address: 2 Changhan Lu

tel: 025-8805931

**Piano Cafe**

A nice restaurant serving Western dishes at reasonable prices. 

address: 121 Shigu Lu

tel: 025-4204252
