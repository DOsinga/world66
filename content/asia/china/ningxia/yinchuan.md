Yinchuan is the capital of the Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, a city of around a million people on the edge of the Yellow River with the Helan Mountains rising to the west. Most travellers heading between Lanzhou and Inner Mongolia pass straight through — which is a mistake. Spend a few days and Yinchuan earns its keep.

The city splits into an old and a new district. The new city clusters around the train station; the old city, a short bus ride away, is the more interesting half. Life there centres on the pedestrian street opposite the Drum Tower, an atmospheric spot lined with restaurants and market stalls. The surrounding streets reward a wander — Yinchuan has a strong Hui Muslim character, with mosques and halal food throughout.

About 60 kilometres west of the city, beyond the village of Gunzhongzou, a gorge in the Helan Mountains contains ancient rock carvings dating back some 10,000 years. The images depict hunting scenes, human faces, and various animals; one is claimed to resemble an extraterrestrial. There is no public transport — a taxi from the train station runs to around 200 yuan — but the gorge makes an excellent day out, with good walking through dramatic scenery.
