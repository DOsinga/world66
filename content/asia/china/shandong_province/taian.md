Tai'an exists for one reason: Mount Tai. Taishan is the most revered of China's Five Sacred Mountains and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the city at its base has served pilgrims, emperors, and travelers for thousands of years. The climb begins at Dai Temple in the center of town, a sprawling complex that is itself worth a visit before you start the ascent.

The mountain trail runs about 7,000 steps to the summit, lined with inscriptions carved into the rock by emperors and poets over the centuries. You can take a bus partway and a cable car to the top, but the walk is the point — generations of Chinese have made this climb as an act of devotion or ambition. Confucius climbed it. Mao climbed it. The sunrise from the peak is famous across China. The city below is unremarkable on its own, but as a gateway to Taishan it has all the hotels, restaurants, and transport connections you need.
