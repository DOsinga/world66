---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Shanghai has two airports. **Pudong International (PVG)** handles the long-haul flights and is around 45 km east of the centre, connected by the **Maglev** train (350 km/h, 8 minutes to Longyang Road) and Metro Line 2 (cheaper, around an hour). **Hongqiao Airport (SHA)**, on the western edge, mostly handles domestic and East Asian routes and connects directly to the high-speed rail station. The **Hongqiao high-speed rail terminal** is one of the world's largest and runs trains to Beijing (4.5 hours), Hangzhou (45 minutes), Suzhou (25 minutes), Nanjing (1 hour) and most major Chinese cities. Long-distance buses are cheap but slow.
