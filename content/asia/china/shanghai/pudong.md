Until 1990 this was farmland and warehouses across the river from old Shanghai. Today it is the city's financial showpiece, anchored by Lujiazui — the cluster of supertall towers visible from the Bund. The Shanghai Tower (632 m), Shanghai World Financial Center (the "bottle opener") and Jin Mao Tower stand shoulder to shoulder; all three have observation decks, and the Shanghai Tower's is the highest in the world. Below them, the elevated walkways of Lujiazui make a pleasant if surreal stroll. Pudong also holds the Shanghai Disneyland resort and the Pudong international airport.

story: "When Deng Xiaoping designated Pudong a Special Economic Zone in 1990, the entire area east of the Huangpu had no skyline to speak of and a population mostly engaged in agriculture and dockwork. The Oriental Pearl tower, completed in 1994, was the first major building of the new district."
