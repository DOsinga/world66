---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Shanghai has four real seasons but none of them are gentle. **Spring (April-May)** and **autumn (September-October)** are the best times: warm days, manageable humidity, blossom or autumn light. **Summer** is brutal — June through August averages above 30°C with crushing humidity, and late August to September is typhoon season. **Winter** (December-February) drops to a few degrees above freezing and feels colder because of the damp; it almost never snows but a heavy coat is essential.

The biggest event is **Chinese New Year** (late January or February), which empties the city as everyone travels home and shuts most non-tourist businesses for a week — atmospheric but inconvenient. Other festivals worth timing for: the **Longhua Temple Fair** in March, the **Shanghai International Film Festival** in June, the **Shanghai Biennale** (autumn of even years), and the **Mid-Autumn Festival** in September-October. Avoid the **National Day** week (1-7 October) when the rest of China descends on the city's sights.
