Shannan — known in Tibetan as Lhoka — is considered the cradle of Tibetan civilization. This prefecture-level city in southern Tibet, sitting along the Yarlung Tsangpo River valley at around 3,600 metres, is where the first Tibetan kings ruled, where Tibet's first monastery was built, and where the mythic origins of the Tibetan people are said to lie. It is one of the most historically resonant corners of the plateau.

The **Yumbulagang Palace**, perched on a narrow rocky ridge above the Yarlung valley, is Tibet's oldest building — a watchtower-turned-palace said to date to the 2nd century BC. The **Chonggye Tombs**, a short drive away, are the burial mounds of Tibet's early kings, including Songtsen Gampo, the 7th-century emperor who unified Tibet and brought Buddhism from India. **Samye Monastery**, across the Yarlung Tsangpo to the northwest, was Tibet's first monastery, founded in 779 AD — a mandala-shaped complex that still draws pilgrims and scholars alike.

The valley itself is greener and more temperate than [Lhasa](/asia/china/tibet/lhasa), sheltered by mountains and productive enough to be one of Tibet's main agricultural zones. Barley fields line the valley floor; the hills above are austere and treeless.

Getting here from Lhasa involves a drive of around three hours south along the Yarlung Tsangpo valley. Foreign visitors to Tibet require both a Chinese visa and a Tibet Travel Permit, arranged through a licensed tour agency.
