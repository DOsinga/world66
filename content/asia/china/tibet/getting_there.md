---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Travel into Tibet is tightly controlled. Foreign visitors need a **Tibet Travel Permit** (TTP) in addition to a Chinese visa — apply through a licensed Chinese tour operator at least 20 days before arrival, since the permit must be in hand before you can board a flight or train into the region.

**By air**, Lhasa Gonggar Airport has direct flights from Chengdu (the main gateway, around 2 hours), Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an, Kunming, Chongqing and Kathmandu. Flying is the easiest option but gives you no altitude acclimatisation — plan to rest for two days on arrival. **By train**, the **Qinghai–Tibet Railway** from Xining to Lhasa is one of the great train journeys of the world: 22 hours across the plateau, crossing passes over 5,000 m with piped oxygen in the carriages. Trains also run through from Beijing (40 hours), Shanghai (47 hours) and Chengdu (36 hours). **By road**, overland access from Nepal (via Zhangmu and Gyirong) is occasionally possible but has been closed for long periods since the 2015 earthquake.
