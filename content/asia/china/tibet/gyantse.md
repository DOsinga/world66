Gyantse is a small agricultural town at nearly 4,000 metres in southern [Tibet](/asia/china/tibet), one of the least changed towns on the Friendship Highway between Lhasa and the Nepali border. It was once the third-largest town in Tibet, a major wool-trading centre on the route to India, and it still has a languid, end-of-the-road feel.

The highlight is the **Pelkor Chöde Monastery**, built in 1427, and its extraordinary **Kumbum** — a nine-tiered stupa containing 108 chapels arranged in a three-dimensional mandala. The murals inside span Tibetan, Nepalese, and Chinese artistic traditions and are among the finest in Tibet. Above the monastery, the ruins of the **Gyantse Dzong** (fortress) command views over the valley and the barley fields below. Gyantse is typically visited as a stop on the overland journey between Lhasa and Shigatse.
