---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Xi'an was China's capital for over a thousand years and the eastern terminus of the Silk Road. The Terracotta Army is the obvious reason to visit, but the city itself rewards time — especially the Muslim Quarter around the Great Mosque, where the city's substantial Hui Muslim population has maintained a distinct food culture for over a millennium. The Tang Dynasty-era city walls, remarkably intact, can be cycled in about two hours.
