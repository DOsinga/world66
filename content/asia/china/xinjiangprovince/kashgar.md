The oasis of Kashgar, with a view of the last peaks of the western Himalayas, is the last stop in China on the Silk Road. From here you can cross to [Pakistan](/asia/pakistan) over the Karakoram Highway or to [Kyrgyzstan](/asia/kyrgyzstan) over the Torugart Pass.

Kashgar is mainly interesting for its Sunday market. It is big — no, it is huge. You will feel like you are back in the heyday of the Silk trade. Everywhere, donkeys are sold and taken for a test drive, sheep are bought and put on the back of a bicycle to take them home. Fur hats, silver daggers, and ancient Korans are on display in small shops. Men and women shout as they haggle over prices.

The old town, centred on the Id Kah Mosque, is one of the few remaining Uyghur neighbourhoods in Xinjiang that still has something of its original character, though much has been rebuilt. Getting something to eat is simple: there are food stalls all over doing shish kebab, lagman noodles, and samsa pastries. Rent a bike and ride out into the oasis — the landscape is flat and green, the people friendly, and there are a few saints' tombs to visit along the way.
