is an eastern coastal province of the People's Republic of China. The word *Zhejiang* (crooked river) was the old name of the Qianang River which passes through Hangzhou, the provincial capital. The name of the province is often abbreviated to "Zhe" (浙).

Zhejiang borders Jianqsu province and Shanghai municipality to the north, Anhui province to the northwest, Jianqxi province to the west, and Fujian province to the south; to the east is the East China Sea, beyond which lie the of Ryukyu Island of Japan
