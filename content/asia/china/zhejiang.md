Zhejiang is one of [China](/asia/china)'s smallest but wealthiest provinces, stretching along the eastern coast south of [Shanghai](/asia/china/shanghai). The provincial capital, [Hangzhou](/asia/china/zhejiang/hangzhou), has been famous for centuries — Marco Polo called it the finest city in the world — and its West Lake remains one of China's most celebrated landscapes.

Beyond Hangzhou, Zhejiang is a province of green hills, tea plantations, and a long, deeply indented coastline. The Thousand Islands Lake (Qiandao Hu), created by a dam in the 1950s, drowned two ancient cities and scattered over a thousand forested islands across an emerald reservoir. The coastal cities of [Wenzhou](/asia/china/zhejiang/wenzhou) and Ningbo are major commercial centres. The province is also home to some of China's best-preserved water towns, including Wuzhen and Nanxun.
