Hangzhou is one of China's most celebrated cities, famous since Marco Polo reportedly called it the finest city in the world. The claim still has some force: West Lake, right at the city's centre, is a genuinely beautiful expanse of water ringed by pagodas, willow-lined causeways and wooded hills. It's been a place of literary and artistic pilgrimage for a thousand years, and even with the crowds it manages to feel serene in the early morning.

The lake's surroundings reward slow exploration on foot or by bike. The Su Causeway, Broken Bridge and Leifeng Pagoda are the classic sights, but the gardens and tea plantations in the hills above the western shore are just as good. The Longjing (Dragon Well) tea grown here is among the finest green tea in China — a walk through the terraced fields and a cup at a farmhouse teahouse is something you won't want to skip.

Beyond the lake, Hangzhou has a strong old-town area around the Hefang Street pedestrian zone, the impressive Song Dynasty ruins at Liuhe Pagoda, and a well-regarded local cuisine built around river fish and the local tea. It's a two-hour high-speed train ride from Shanghai, which makes it the most popular day trip from that city — aim to stay overnight if you can to see it without the worst of the crowds.
