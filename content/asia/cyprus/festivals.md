---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Cyprus celebrates Greek Orthodox religious holidays alongside a calendar of cultural and folk events. The following are the most notable for visitors.

**Limassol Carnival** (February/March) is one of the liveliest pre-Lenten carnivals in the eastern Mediterranean — ten days of parades, costumes, street parties, and music leading up to Clean Monday. Dates shift each year in line with Orthodox Easter.

**Anthestiria (Flower Festival)** takes place in May in several towns, most visibly in Limassol. Floral floats and decorated vehicles parade through the streets; the tradition dates to ancient Greek celebrations of spring.

**Kataklysmos (Festival of the Flood)** falls on the Monday after Orthodox Pentecost — typically in June. It is uniquely Cypriot, combining Christian observance with ancient water-related traditions: people gather at the sea or riversides and throw water on each other. Larnaca holds the largest celebration.

**Limassol Wine Festival** (September) is a week-long event in the Municipal Gardens, where visitors can taste wines from Cyprus's main producers for a modest entrance fee. There is live music and traditional food. Cyprus's wine culture is ancient — the island claims to produce Commandaria, possibly the world's oldest named wine.

**Kypria International Festival** (September–October) brings theatre, dance, and classical music performances to venues across the island, including ancient sites like Kourion and the Paphos Odeon.

**Christmas and Orthodox Easter** are the two biggest religious celebrations. Orthodox Easter (Pascha) follows the Julian calendar and usually falls one to several weeks after Western Easter. The midnight Resurrection service is worth attending — churches spill onto the streets, candles are lit from a central flame, and the atmosphere is genuinely moving.
