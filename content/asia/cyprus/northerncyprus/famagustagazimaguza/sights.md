**Venetian Walls**

The so-called 'Venetian Walls' belong, like the St. Nicholas cathedral, to the de Lusignan period. They are 2,5 miles long and enclose the entire old city. The walls were originally more than 6 feet thick, and were equipped with several turrets. **Church of Sts. Peter and Paul**

Built in 1358, this church became the Sinan Pasa Mosque during the Ottoman period. Celebi Mehmet Efendi, a Turkish diplomat of the 18th century (died in 1732), is buried in its court.
