---
title: "When to Go in Troodos mountains"
type: section
---

Creating a **Troodos Mountains when to go** strategy is an unfortunate necessity. Those seeking to visit this Cyprus mountain range (which offers the famed Mount Olympus) cannot simply assume all seasons will appeal. Each season provides its own advantages and possible concerns.

**Spring**

Temperate in climate, spring can grant relief to those longing for the Mediterranean. Trekking through the peaks and discovering their unexpected attractions (like the churches of Panayia tou Araka and Ayios Nikolaos tis Stegis, both offering magnificent copper work and historical significance) will be easy. Guests should note, however, that storms are prevalent. Plan well.

**Summer**

With high temperatures and days without rain, summer becomes a perfect time to visit the Troodos Moutains. Visitors should indulge in such attractions as the resort-town of Platres and even Mount Olympus (which provides all things mythic and modern, with its collection of hiking trails, archaeological sites and even an amusement park). Travelers beware: many villages are filled beyond their capacity at this time. All reservations must be made in advance.

**Autumn**

Exploring the high peaks of Troodos is essential; exploring them while facing stifling climates is not. For those cautious of the heat, the fall is best. Storms are rare and days remain at a comfortable 68 degrees Fahrenheit.

**Winter**

Though the winter may seem an unlikely choice for a Troodos Mountains getaway, it's instead ideal: guests are encouraged to visit the village of Prodromos. This resort offers skiing, sledding and more. Those travelers looking for sports instead of mere relaxation will find it perfect. Exploring neighboring towns will be difficult, though, due to the weather.
