Timor-Leste — officially the Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste, still widely called East Timor — is one of the world's youngest nations, having gained independence in 2002 after a brutal 24-year Indonesian occupation. It sits at the eastern end of the island of Timor, between Indonesia and Australia, covering about 14,874 sq km of mountains, coast, and tropical forest.

The country's story is inseparable from its suffering. Portugal colonised Timor for four centuries, leaving behind a language, a religion (the country is over 97% Catholic), and a handful of crumbling buildings. Indonesia invaded in 1975 and ruled for 24 years with considerable violence — an estimated 100,000 to 180,000 people died from conflict, famine, and disease. A 1999 UN-supervised referendum produced an overwhelming vote for independence, but Indonesian-backed militias responded by destroying much of the country's infrastructure before an international force intervened. The last UN peacekeeping mission left in 2012.

What draws travellers here — and it is still relatively few — is a combination of extraordinary marine life, raw mountain scenery, and a country that remains largely unspoiled by mass tourism. The coral reefs offshore are part of the Coral Triangle, considered one of the richest marine environments on earth. The coffee is world-class. The people are warm and genuinely pleased to see visitors.

The capital [Dili](/asia/easttimor/dili) has the feel of a small, relaxed city with some Portuguese colonial atmosphere, a lively waterfront, and good diving from the shore. Outside the capital, the landscape becomes rugged quickly — terraced hillsides, highland forests, and a coast that alternates between dramatic cliffs and white-sand beaches. Tourist infrastructure is limited but functional for independent travellers.

## Destinations

- [Dili](/asia/easttimor/dili) — the capital; starting point for most visitors, good diving and colonial remnants
- Atauro Island — volcanic island 25 km offshore, outstanding diving and snorkelling
- Jaco Island — uninhabited protected island at the eastern tip, some of the country's best beaches
- Baucau — the second city, Portuguese-era architecture and a hilltop plateau
- Mount Ramelau (Tatamailau) — the highest point in the country at 2,963 m, popular sunrise hike
- Maubisse — cool highland town, Portuguese-era pousada, good base for hiking
