---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Getting around Timor-Leste takes patience. The road network is improving — the government has been investing steadily since independence — but outside the main Dili-Baucau coastal road, conditions range from rough to impassable during the wet season.

## In Dili

**Microlets** are the city's main public transport: small numbered and colour-coded minibuses that run set routes across Dili. There are no fixed stops — wave one down, pay 25 cents (US), and tap a coin on the metal railing when you want to get off. They run during daylight hours with no fixed schedule but usually pass every 10–20 minutes on busy routes. The system is easy to use and cheap.

**Taxis** are plentiful in Dili. Negotiate the fare before you get in — there are no meters. A trip within the central area typically costs $1–3. Apps like Letefoho and local ride-hailing services operate in Dili.

## Between Towns

**Microlet and bus**: Shared minibuses (called mikrolet or oto) run between most towns, departing when full from the central market or bus terminal in Dili. These are cheap but slow and can be crowded. The Dili–Baucau run (around 2.5 hours on a good road day) is the most reliable inter-city route.

**Renting a vehicle**: A 4WD with driver is the most practical option for exploring the interior. Roads to highland areas like Ermera, Maubisse, and Same require a vehicle with ground clearance, especially outside the dry season. Many guesthouses and tour operators in Dili can arrange this; expect to pay $80–150 per day including driver.

Self-drive rental is theoretically possible but roads, signage, and local driving conditions make a local driver worth the cost. Fuel (petrol and diesel) is available in towns but scarce in rural areas; carry extra if going off the main routes.

## To Atauro Island

Passenger ferries run between Dili's main wharf and Atauro Island approximately three times a week (roughly 2 hours). Private speedboat transfers can be arranged through dive operators and take around 45 minutes. Schedules vary; check locally.

## Road Conditions

The north coast road from Dili to Baucau and on toward Lospalos is the country's best highway. The south coast road and mountain interior roads are often rough, sometimes washed out, and can be slow going. In the wet season (December–April), some highland roads close entirely. Allow significantly more time than distances on a map suggest — 100 km in the interior can easily take 4–5 hours.
