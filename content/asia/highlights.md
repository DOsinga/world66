title: "Highlights"

---

With a continent this large, any highlights list is a argument waiting to happen. These are not rankings — they are a starting point.

**Angkor Wat, Cambodia.** The temple complex at Angkor is the largest religious monument ever built, and it earns the hyperbole. Arrive before dawn to watch the towers emerge from darkness and reflection, then spend the next two days exploring the surrounding jungle temples. [Cambodia](/asia/cambodia) is the reason to be here, not just Angkor.

**Kyoto, Japan.** The city that kept its temples and neighborhoods when the rest of Japan was rebuilding from scratch. In spring the cherry blossoms in the Maruyama and Philosopher's Path are genuinely as beautiful as the photographs suggest. Visit in November for autumn foliage with fewer crowds.

**The Himalayas, Nepal.** The Annapurna Circuit and the route to Everest Base Camp are two of the world's great long-distance hikes. Even if trekking is not your thing, the view of the high peaks from Nagarkot or Pokhara is enough to make the journey worthwhile.

**Varanasi, India.** The oldest continuously inhabited city on earth sits on the banks of the Ganges. The morning boat ride past the ghats — cremation fires, pilgrims bathing, priests performing puja — is unlike anything else in the world. Difficult and overwhelming for many visitors. Worth it anyway.

**Bagan, Myanmar.** Over two thousand Buddhist temples spread across a dusty plain, lit at dawn and dusk by extraordinary light. Less visited than Angkor, and in some ways more affecting. The political situation in [Myanmar](/asia/myanmar) is complex — check current conditions before planning a visit.

**Tokyo, Japan.** Hyper-efficient, deeply strange in the best ways, and relentlessly interesting. The food alone — Tsukiji fish market, ramen alleys, department store basement food halls — justifies the trip. Combine with Kyoto and you have the essential Japan circuit.

**Ha Long Bay, Vietnam.** Thousands of limestone karsts rising from green water. The overnight cruise from Hanoi has become heavily commercialized — choose your boat carefully — but the landscape is genuinely spectacular.

**Petra, Jordan.** The rose-red rock city carved by the Nabataeans more than two thousand years ago. Approach via the Siq, the long narrow canyon, and the moment the Treasury materializes in front of you is as theatrical as architecture gets. [Jordan](/asia/jordan) is one of the most visitor-friendly countries in the Middle East.

**Borobudur, Indonesia.** The world's largest Buddhist monument sits in central Java, surrounded by volcanoes. Less famous internationally than Angkor but equally impressive in scale and detail. Combine with a visit to nearby Yogyakarta and the Hindu temples of Prambanan.

**Samarkand, Uzbekistan.** The great Silk Road city, with its tiled mosques and madrasas, is one of the most beautiful places in Central Asia. [Uzbekistan](/asia/uzbekistan) has become easier to visit in recent years, and few travelers regret making the detour.

**The Mekong, Laos.** Taking the slow boat from Huay Xai to Luang Prabang — two days on the river through forest and hill-tribe villages — is one of Southeast Asia's classic journeys. Luang Prabang itself is the most graceful small city in the region.

**The Kerala Backwaters, India.** A network of lagoons, canals, and lakes running parallel to the Arabian Sea coast. A houseboat overnight on the backwaters is deservedly popular — book well ahead in season.
