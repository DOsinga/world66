Radhanagar Beach on Havelock Island (officially Swaraj Dweep) is the Andamans' most famous stretch of sand — a wide crescent of white powder backed by tropical forest. The water is clear and calm enough for swimming most of the year, and the sunsets here are spectacular.

Getting to Havelock takes about 1.5 hours by government ferry or private speedboat from Port Blair. The beach itself is on the western side of the island, roughly a 20-minute auto-rickshaw ride from the jetty. Go early or late in the day to avoid the midday heat and the tour groups. Basic changing facilities and a few food stalls are available at the entrance.
