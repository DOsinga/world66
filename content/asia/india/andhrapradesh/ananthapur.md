Anantapur is the administrative capital of Anantapur district in the Rayalaseema region of Andhra Pradesh, one of the driest districts in India. It sits on the Deccan plateau at around 350m elevation, about 200km north of Bangalore and 500km from Hyderabad.

The district is known for groundnut cultivation and is a major producer in India. For travellers, the area's main points of interest are the Gooty Fort — an ancient hill fort 52km from town — and the Puttaparthi ashram of Sathya Sai Baba (70km), which draws large numbers of devotees. Lepakshi, about 15km east of Hindupur, has some of the finest Vijayanagara-period temple sculpture in Andhra Pradesh.

Anantapur is connected by road and rail to Hyderabad and Bangalore. The nearest airports are Puttaparthi (70km) and Bangalore (200km).
