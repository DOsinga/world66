---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

The Bhadrachalam Temple is the town's center of gravity. Built in the 17th century by Kancherla Gopanna (known as Bhakta Ramadasu), the temple complex includes several smaller shrines and a museum with artifacts and painted portraits related to the Rama story. Outside the temple, prasadam (food offered to the deity) is sold and makes for an authentic snack. The Parnasala village, 35 km away, is traditionally associated with Rama's forest exile and is a pleasant half-day excursion through tribal settlements along the Godavari.
