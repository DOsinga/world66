---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Cuddapah food leans heavily on Rayalaseema spicing — expect heat. The local specialty is *sangati*, a thick finger-millet porridge served with spiced mutton gravies like *boti* (offal) and *paya* (trotters). Non-vegetarian and vegetarian restaurants cluster around the old bus stand, YV Street, and Madras Road.

**Manasa Hotel** on Madras Road (near YV Street) is the go-to for non-vegetarian cooking — one of the better places in the Rayalaseema region. It serves a wide range of dishes including Chinese and seafood such as prawns, at reasonable prices.

**Mayura** (next to Saibaba Theatre, about 200m from the old bus stand) is the best spot for chaat, ice creams, and snacks — pav bhaji, cutlets, samosas, belpuri. The place has some greenery inside, which is unusual. Avoid Sunday afternoons when it gets crowded.

**Near the old bus stand** is where you find the most atmospheric street eating. Opposite Laxmiranga Theatre you can get kushka and parottas; *sangati* with boti and paya is available from stalls nearby. **Nagaraju Hotel** in this area does ragi sangati, natukodi (country chicken) and mutton in classic Rayalaseema style. Most items here run Rs 10–100.

**Ashoka Hotel** (not to be confused with a luxury chain) is an old-fashioned pure-vegetarian restaurant known for cleanliness and consistent home-style cooking.

**Vijaya Hotel** is another well-regarded pure-veg option, located in the clock-tower building at the Seven Roads junction — a good orientation point in itself.
