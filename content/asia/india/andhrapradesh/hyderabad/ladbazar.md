The bangle alley running west from Charminar — a narrow street densely packed with shops selling lacquer bangles studded with coloured stones, pearls (Hyderabad has been a pearl-trading centre for centuries), silks, and wedding finery. Best early evening, when the lights come on and the shops fill with brides-to-be shopping for their weddings. The name "Laad" comes from "laad," meaning lacquer, and the tradition here goes back centuries to the Qutb Shahi era. Even if you are not buying, the street is a sensory overload worth experiencing — glittering glass, the smell of attar perfume, and vendors calling out from every doorway.
