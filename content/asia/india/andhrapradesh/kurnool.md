Kurnool is a city in the Rayalaseema region of Andhra Pradesh, on the Tungabhadra River. It was the state capital of Andhra State from 1953 to 1956, before the capital was moved to Hyderabad. Today it is a busy commercial and administrative centre, but for most travellers it serves primarily as a base for exploring the surrounding region — which contains some of the most significant natural and religious sites in Andhra Pradesh.

The Belum Caves, about 100 kilometres south of Kurnool, are among the largest and longest cave systems in the Indian subcontinent, with formations of stalactites, stalagmites, and sinkholes that have been developed for visitors by the Andhra Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation. The Mahanandi temple, set in the Nallamalai hills of the Eastern Ghats, is one of the most sacred Shiva shrines in the region. Srisailam, a major pilgrimage destination, is also within reach.
