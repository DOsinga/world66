Machilipatnam (also known as Masulipatnam or Bandar) is a port city in Krishna district of Andhra Pradesh, situated on the Bay of Bengal about 50 km from Vijayawada. It is one of the oldest port towns on the eastern coast of India, with a history stretching back to the 2nd century BCE. During the colonial era it was a significant trading post — the Dutch, British, and French all had factories here at various times in the 17th century.

The town is known for its distinctive Kalamkari textile tradition — hand-painted or block-printed cotton cloth with intricate patterns drawn from Hindu mythology and nature. The craft is still practiced in the surrounding villages, and Machilipatnam is one of the best places to buy authentic Kalamkari work directly from artisans.

The harbour and the beach at Bandar are pleasant in the evenings. The town is primarily a functional stop rather than a major tourist destination, but for those interested in the Kalamkari tradition or the layered colonial history of the coast, it rewards a visit.
