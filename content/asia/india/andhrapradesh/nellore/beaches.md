Nellore district has over 100 km of coastline, most of it undeveloped and blissfully uncrowded. Mypadu, Katepalli, and Kotha Koduru are the easiest to reach from the city — all around 20 km out. There are no water sports facilities to speak of, which is either a drawback or the whole point depending on who you ask. APSRTC buses run to some of these beaches, particularly on weekends.

### [Katepalli Beach](/asia/india/andhrapradesh/nellore/beaches/katepalli_beach)

A quiet stretch of sand about 20 km from Nellore that sees relatively few visitors. Worth the trip if you want to get away from the city without a crowd.

### [Mypadu Beach](/asia/india/andhrapradesh/nellore/beaches/mypadu_beach)

One of the better-known beaches near Nellore, about 20 km from the city centre. Regular bus connections from Nellore, especially on Sundays when it gets busy with local families.

### [Kotha Koduru Beach](/asia/india/andhrapradesh/nellore/beaches/kotha_koduru_beach)

A beach village around 20 km from Nellore with an old Velankani Church that draws pilgrims alongside beach visitors. Buses run from Nellore on Sundays.

### [Thupilipalyem Beach](/asia/india/andhrapradesh/nellore/beaches/thupilipalyem_beach)

About 12 km from Vakadu, this beach has a guest house with basic rooms and boating facilities — unusual for this stretch of coast. Surrounded by trees, it's greener than most beaches in the district. Buses run from Kota.
