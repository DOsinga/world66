---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Prakasham district's sights are mostly temples, reservoirs, and stretches of coastline. Most are easily reached by road from Ongole.

**Sai Baba Temple** — Locally known as the "Second Shirdi," this temple draws large numbers of devotees, particularly on Thursdays and festival days.

**Gundlakamma Reservoir and Venkateswara Temple** — The reservoir on the Gundlakamma River is both a scenic spot and a pilgrimage site, with a Venkateswara temple on its banks that sees steady foot traffic from the surrounding villages.

**Srigiri Hills — Venkateswara Swamy Temple** — A hilltop Venkateswara shrine above the plains, popular for the views as much as the worship.

**Kothapatnam Beach** — A stretch of Bay of Bengal coastline with a Shivalayam and Ramalayam nearby. Quieter than the beaches further north, it draws local families on weekends.

**Chadalavada Temple and Valluramma Temple** — Two well-regarded local shrines, each with its own festival calendar and following among pilgrims from across the district.

**Raja Rajeswara Swamy Temple** — A notable temple with traditional Dravidian architecture, associated with the region's Telugu heritage.

**Rangarayudu Water Tank** — A large tank (reservoir) in the area, used historically for irrigation and now a quiet landmark.

**Vijaya (Prakasham) Milk Dairy** — Once claimed as Asia's second largest dairy cooperative, it reflects the district's strong agricultural economy and is worth a passing glance.
