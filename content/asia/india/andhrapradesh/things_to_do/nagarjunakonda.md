Nagarjunakonda sits on an island in a reservoir about 150km east of Hyderabad, preserving one of the most significant Buddhist archaeological sites in India. When a dam was proposed in the 1960s, the Archaeological Survey of India undertook extensive excavations, uncovering over 1,000 sites of archaeological interest before the valley was flooded.

Thirty Buddhist monasteries and stupas have been found here, showing the evolution of Buddhism across Mahayana and Hinayana strands, with remains dating mainly from 200 BC to 900 AD. The reconstructed sites and museum on the island — accessible by boat from Nagarjun resort — display findings from four distinct Buddhist sects. Amaravati and other monastic sites lie nearby along the coastal belt of Andhra Pradesh.
