Vijayawada sits on the Krishna River in the heart of Andhra Pradesh, squeezed between granite hills and the broad plains of the delta. It is one of the busiest railway junctions in south India — nearly every train between the north and the south passes through — which means most travelers blow through without stopping. That is their loss.

The city has a genuine character: loud, commercial, and proudly Telugu. The Kanaka Durga Temple on Indrakeeladri Hill watches over the river from the city's most recognizable vantage point, drawing pilgrims from across the state. Below it, the Prakasam Barrage stretches more than a kilometre across the Krishna, with the low green hills of the opposite bank reflected in the water. The whole scene, especially at dusk, is worth an evening.

Vijayawada is not a polished tourist city. But it has a strong local food culture — particularly its pickles (avakaya, the raw mango version, is famous across Andhra Pradesh) — and an energy that rewards a short stay between longer journeys.
