Dirang is a small valley town in western Arunachal Pradesh, sitting at about 1,500 metres between Bomdila and Tawang on the road that climbs toward the Tibetan border. Most travelers pass through on their way to Tawang, but Dirang deserves more than a fuel stop.

The valley is known for its kiwi orchards and apple farms, and the Yak Research Centre on the outskirts is worth a visit. A few kilometres away, the village of Sangti sits in a wide, open valley where migratory black-necked cranes arrive from Siberia each winter — a quiet spectacle best seen between November and February. The Sangti Valley also has a sheep breeding farm and some pleasant walks through rice paddies.

Dirang Dzong, the old stone fortification in the centre of town, is a lived-in medieval settlement where Monpa families still occupy traditional houses. Hot springs nearby provide a welcome soak after the long mountain drive. The town is small and quiet, but the setting — steep hillsides, terraced fields, clear river — makes it one of the most pleasant stops on the Tawang circuit.
