Itanagar is the capital of Arunachal Pradesh, India's wildest and least-visited northeastern state. The city sits in the foothills of the eastern Himalayas, surrounded by subtropical forest and low mountains. It is small, unhurried, and genuinely off the tourist circuit.

The main historical draw is Ita Fort, a 14th-century brick fortification that gives the city its name. The ruins are scattered across a forested hillside and worth an hour of exploration. Nearby, the Ganga Lake (also called Gekar Sinyi) is a pretty spot for a walk.

Itanagar has a handful of Buddhist monasteries, including the yellow-roofed Itafort Buddhist Temple, which reflect the Tibetan cultural influence that runs through much of the state. The Jawaharlal Nehru State Museum offers a decent introduction to the tribal cultures of Arunachal Pradesh.

Foreign visitors and even Indian nationals from outside the state need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to enter Arunachal Pradesh. The permit is straightforward to obtain online but you need to sort it out before arrival.
