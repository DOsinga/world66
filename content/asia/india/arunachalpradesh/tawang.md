Situated at over 3,000 metres in the far northwest of Arunachal Pradesh, Tawang is renowned for its 400-year-old Buddhist monastery — the largest in India and second only to the Potala Palace in Lhasa. The Tawang Monastery, also known as Galden Namgyal Lhatse, was founded by Mera Lama, a contemporary of the fifth Dalai Lama. The sixth Dalai Lama was born here.

The Parkhang hall houses a remarkable library: rare handwritten and block-printed thankas, 108 gold-embossed manuscripts of the teachings of Lord Buddha, and 225 volumes wrapped in moth-proof silk adorned with paintings. The monastery complex includes a towering gilded Buddha statue and a craft centre producing fine woollen carpets.

Getting here is part of the experience — the road from Tezpur crosses the Sela Pass at over 4,100 metres, often snowbound in winter, with views of high-altitude lakes and rhododendron forests. Tawang is also a base for trekking in the surrounding valleys.
