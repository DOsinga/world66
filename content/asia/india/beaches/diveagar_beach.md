Diveagar sits about 170 km south of Mumbai along the Konkan coast, reachable from the Mumbai-Goa highway via Kolad or Karnala. The beach stretches roughly six kilometers, backed by dense casuarina (suru) groves and belu trees that are otherwise uncommon in the area.

The northern end has a small fishing settlement where a stream meets the sea, along with some paddy farming. The southern end is a sanctuary for migratory seabirds. Sand-bubble crabs are a common sight, and sea turtles come ashore to lay eggs.

Diveagar is quieter than neighboring beach towns Shrivardhan and Harihareshwar, making it a good pick if you want a low-key stretch of sand. Local eateries serve fresh seafood — head to the nearby fishing village of Bharadkhol for the best catch.
