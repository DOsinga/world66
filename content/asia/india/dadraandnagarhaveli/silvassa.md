Silvassa is the capital of the Indian union territory of Dadra and Nagar Haveli, tucked into the foothills of the Western Ghats between Gujarat and Maharashtra. A former Portuguese colony until 1954, the town still carries traces of that era alongside a strong tribal culture rooted in the Warli, Kokna, and Koli communities.

The real draw here is nature. Almost 40 percent of the territory is forest, and the surrounding landscape is a patchwork of gardens, lakes, rivers, and small waterfalls. Silvassa itself is compact and easy to explore, with most attractions within a short drive. Van Ganga Lake and the Island Garden are the scenic centrepieces, while the Tribal Cultural Museum offers a window into the indigenous heritage that defines this region.

Silvassa is not a big tourist destination, and that is part of its appeal. It works well as a quiet weekend break from Mumbai (about 180 km south) or as a stopover between Gujarat and Goa. The pace is slow, the costs are low, and the forest trails are largely yours.
