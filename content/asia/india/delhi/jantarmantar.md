An open-air collection of enormous geometric instruments built in 1724 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II of Jaipur, who was obsessed with astronomy and dissatisfied with the brass instruments of his day. The Delhi Jantar Mantar is the oldest and largest of the five observatories he built across northern India.

The centrepiece is the Samrat Yantra, a massive triangular sundial that can tell the time to within half a second. Around it stand structures for tracking the positions of stars, measuring declination, and predicting eclipses -- all built in masonry at monumental scale because Jai Singh believed bigger instruments would give more accurate readings. He was right.

The site sits just off Connaught Place, surrounded by modern Delhi. It is a surprisingly quick visit -- half an hour is enough to walk the grounds and read the explanatory plaques. Go in the morning when the shadows are sharp and the instruments are actually doing what they were designed to do.
