### Navratri

For nine nights, Amdavadis celebrate with traditional Garba and Raas dances. Superb folk music, often mixed with pop rhythms, and colourful ethnic dresses combine to create an enchanting experience. Navratri falls in the Hindu month of Ashvin, typically in October. Ahmedabad hosts some of the largest Navratri gatherings in India, with venues accommodating thousands of dancers.

### Makar Sankranti

Every January 14th, the city erupts into a massive kite-flying festival. Shops close, rooftops fill with families, and the sky becomes a battlefield of colourful kites. The competition is fierce -- cutting your opponent's string with your own is the goal. After dark, the sky fills with illuminated kites and paper lanterns.
