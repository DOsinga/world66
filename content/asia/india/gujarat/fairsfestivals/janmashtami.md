The festival Janmashtami is celebrated every year on a very large scale in the city Dwarka. This ‘City of Gold’ is one of the abodes of Lord Krishna and the festival belongs to him as well. It is believed that Lord Krishna settled here 5000 years ago after fleeing from Mathura. Janmashtami, which is a very enjoyable and loveable festival, is not only celebrated in Dwarka, but also in the other districts of Gujarat, in Maharashtra, as well as in other parts of the rest of India.


 
 The story behind the breaking of ‘Dahi Handi’ is known to everyone. A group of more than 50, sometimes even up to 500, people goes into town. These ‘Govindas’, Govinda being another name of Lord Krishna, gather at one place, where a ‘Handi’, or a pot made of clay, is hanging between two buildings on a height of 30 to 40 feet high. This pot, which is known as the ‘Dahi Handi’ is filled with turmeric powder, made of haldi, milk, yoghurt, cucumber and bananas, and a certain amount of coins and bank notes. The group of Govindas tries to reach this Dahi Handi by forming a human pyramid, climbing one on another. Whoever manages to break this Handi gets all the money. In the mean time, however, bystanders try to prevent the Govindas from breaking the Handi by throwing balloons, filled with plain or coloured water, which complicates the job.


 
 The expected date is the 23rd of August in 2000, the 12th of August in 2001 and the 31st of August in 2002.
