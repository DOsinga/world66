### Navratri

The biggest festival in Gujarat, and the one most worth timing your visit around. For nine nights, usually in October, the entire state dances. The Garba and Raas dances are performed at every level of society, from small neighbourhood circles to massive commercial grounds that hold thousands. [Ahmedabad](/asia/india/gujarat/ahmedabad) and [Vadodara](/asia/india/gujarat/vadodara) host the largest celebrations, but even small towns come alive. The colourful ethnic dresses, the rhythmic folk music, and the sheer energy make Navratri in Gujarat unlike anything else in India.

### Makar Sankranti (Uttarayan)

On January 14th, Gujarat hosts one of the world's great kite festivals. The skies above [Ahmedabad](/asia/india/gujarat/ahmedabad) fill with thousands of kites as families take to the rooftops. The competition to cut rival kite strings is fierce and joyful. After dark, illuminated kites and paper lanterns take over.

### Janmashtami

The birth of Lord Krishna is celebrated across Gujarat, but Dwarka -- believed to be Krishna's ancient kingdom -- is the epicentre. The festival features elaborate temple ceremonies and the tradition of Dahi Handi, where groups form human pyramids to reach and break a clay pot hung high between buildings.

### Kutch Mahotsav

Held in late February or early March in [Kutch](/asia/india/gujarat/kutch), this festival celebrates the region's distinctive culture with folk music, traditional crafts, and tours of the surrounding desert landscape and salt marshes.

### Dangs Darbar

A vibrant tribal fair held a few days before Holi in Ahwa, in the hilly Dangs region near [Saputara](/asia/india/gujarat/saputara). Tribal Adivasi people from the surrounding hills gather in colourful dress, beating drums and playing shehnai. The highlight is the folk dancing.
