Gujarati cuisine is a vegetarian's delight. The cooking balances sweet, sour, bitter, salty, and spicy flavours in a way that is distinctly different from the rest of India -- a touch of sugar or jaggery finds its way into most dishes, even the savoury ones.

The classic Gujarati thali is an experience in itself: a large round plate with a dozen or more small bowls of different vegetables, dals, chutneys, pickles, rice, rotli, and something sweet, all served unlimited. **Pakwan Dining Hall** in [Ahmedabad](/asia/india/gujarat/ahmedabad) and countless other thali restaurants across the state serve this format.

Must-try dishes include **undhiyu** (a slow-cooked mixed vegetable dish, best in winter), **dhokla** and **khandvi** (steamed savoury snacks), **fafda-jalebi** (a Sunday morning tradition), **khichdi-kadhi** (rice and lentils with a tangy yogurt sauce), **thepla** (spiced flatbread, the Gujarati traveller's essential snack), and **shrikhand** (sweetened strained yogurt with cardamom and saffron).

Street food culture is strong, especially in [Ahmedabad](/asia/india/gujarat/ahmedabad). The stalls around Manek Chowk come alive after dark with everything from pav bhaji to kulfi.
