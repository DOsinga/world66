Gandhidham is a planned city in [Kutch](/asia/india/gujarat/kutch), built in the 1950s to resettle refugees from Sindh after Partition. Unlike the ancient towns of the region, Gandhidham was designed on a grid and has a distinctly modern, commercial feel. It is the main railway junction for Kutch and the economic engine of the district, handling much of the trade flowing through the nearby port of [Kandla](/asia/india/gujarat/kutch/kandla).

Most travellers pass through Gandhidham on their way to [Bhuj](/asia/india/gujarat/kutch/bhuj) or the Rann of Kutch, and there is not a great deal to detain you here. The Sindhi refugee heritage is visible in the food — look for Sindhi-style dal pakwan at breakfast stalls near the railway station — and in the temples built by the resettled community. The Swaminarayan Temple and the Hathidhar Jain Temple are the most visited.

If you find yourself with time between connections, the Kutch Museum in the centre of town has a small collection covering local history, crafts, and the geology of the Rann. Gandhidham is well connected by train to Ahmedabad, Mumbai, and Delhi, and the bus stand has frequent services to Bhuj (about an hour away). The town is functional rather than charming, but it does what it needs to do as a gateway to one of India's most distinctive landscapes.
