story: >
  The Machhu river looks peaceful from this bridge, but it was the site of one of
  India's worst industrial disasters. On 11 August 1979, the Machhu II dam upstream
  collapsed after heavy monsoon rains, sending a wall of water through Morbi that
  killed an estimated 2,000 people (some estimates run far higher). The disaster
  was hushed up by state authorities for years.
---

The Hanging Bridge over the Machhu river is Morbi's most recognisable landmark — a Victorian-era suspension bridge that has become a symbol of the city. It offers good views over the river and the old town. The bridge was rebuilt after being damaged in the 1979 flood.
