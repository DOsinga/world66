Patan was the ancient capital of Gujarat, and its main attraction is the stunning **Rani ki Vav** (Queen's Stepwell), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 11th century, this intricately carved stepwell descends seven stories underground, with over 800 sculptures depicting Hindu deities, apsaras, and scenes from mythology. It is one of the finest examples of stepwell architecture in India.

The **Sahasralinga Talav** (Lake of a Thousand Lingas) is nearby, though largely in ruins. A small section of the old city fort is still intact and visible on the road between the new city and the stepwell.

Patan is also famous for its **Patola** saris -- double ikat silk saris that take months to weave and are among the most expensive textiles in India. A few families in Patan still practise this craft, and some welcome visitors to watch the weaving process.

Patan is easily combined with [Modhera](/asia/india/gujarat/modhera) and [Siddhpur](/asia/india/gujarat/siddhpur) in a day trip from [Ahmedabad](/asia/india/gujarat/ahmedabad), about 130 km to the south.
