Somnath is one of India's most sacred pilgrimage sites, home to the Somnath Temple, one of the twelve jyotirlingas (shrines of Shiva). The present temple, rebuilt in 1951, stands dramatically on the Arabian Sea coast. The original temple was legendarily wealthy and was destroyed and rebuilt several times over the centuries. The evening light-and-sound show at the temple tells this turbulent history against the backdrop of crashing waves.

The town is small and quiet outside pilgrimage season. The nearby beach at Veraval, 7 km away, is where the fishing boats land their catch.
