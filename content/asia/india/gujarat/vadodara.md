Vadodara -- still commonly called Baroda -- is Gujarat's third-largest city and one of its most pleasant. The legacy of the Gaekwad dynasty, who ruled the princely state of Baroda for over two centuries, is everywhere: grand palaces, wide tree-lined avenues, and a genuine emphasis on education and the arts that persists to this day.

The **Laxmi Vilas Palace**, the Gaekwad family's main residence, is one of the largest private residences in the world and is still partly occupied by the family. The **Baroda Museum and Picture Gallery** in Sayaji Baug has a fine collection that includes European paintings, Mughal miniatures, and Egyptian and Tibetan artefacts. Sayaji Baug itself, the large public park that contains the museum, is a good place for an evening walk.

The **Maharaja Fateh Singh Museum** in the palace grounds has a remarkable collection of European and Indian art, including works by Murillo, Raphael, and Titian, alongside paintings by Raja Ravi Varma. The city is well positioned for day trips to [Champaner-Pavagadh](/asia/india/gujarat/champaner), a UNESCO World Heritage Site about 50 km away.
