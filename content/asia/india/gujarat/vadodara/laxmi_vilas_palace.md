Built in 1890 for Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III by the British architect Major Charles Mant, this is reportedly the largest private residence in India and one of the largest in the world. The Indo-Saracenic exterior is a riot of domes, towers, and arches; inside, the Durbar Hall is the showpiece, with Belgian stained glass, Italian Venetian mosaic floors, and a gilded ceiling. The Gaekwad family still lives in part of the palace, but the public can tour the main halls daily except Mondays. Allow at least an hour and arrive early to avoid coach tours.
