Himachal Pradesh is a Himalayan state in northern India, carved from Punjab in 1966. Despite its small size and low population density, it draws visitors year-round with hill stations, ancient temples, adventure sports, and the living heritage of Tibetan culture.

[Shimla](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/shimla), the state capital and former summer capital of British India, still carries traces of the Raj in its colonial architecture and Mall Road promenades. [Dharamsala](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/dharamsala) and its upper town [McLeodganj](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/mcleodganj) are home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government in exile, earning the nickname 'Little Lhasa.'

The [Kullu](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/kullu) Valley and neighbouring [Manali](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/manali) sit among pine-covered hills and lush meadows. [Bir Billing](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/bir_billing) is one of the world's top paragliding sites. Further north, the remote valleys of Lahaul and Spiti, accessible via [Keylong](/asia/india/himachalpradesh/keylong), reward trekkers with stark high-altitude landscapes and ancient Buddhist monasteries.

The state is an ideal base for treks and walks. Popular routes include circuits around Shimla (Mashobra, Kufri, Naldehra, Chail), the Kullu-Manali area (Manikaran, Naggar, Brighu Lake), and the Dhauladhar range above Dharamsala.
