Bir is a small village in the Kangra valley of Himachal Pradesh, set among tea gardens and monasteries with the snow wall of the Dhauladhar range rising directly behind it. Half an hour's drive up the mountain, a meadow at 2,400 metres called **Billing** is one of the world's great paragliding launch sites — and the two names have become inseparable. Pilots come here from all over the world, especially in the autumn season, and the village below has grown up around the sport.

The setting is extraordinary. From Billing a pilot can soar for more than 200 km along the Dhauladhar ridge, flying ridge-to-ridge all the way to Dharamsala or Mandi, often in the company of Himalayan griffon vultures. The short "top-to-bottom" flight down to the landing field at Bir takes about half an hour and is the standard tandem ride that most visitors come for. In 2015 the Paragliding World Cup was held here, cementing Bir's reputation as the best site in India.

Bir is more than just paragliding, though. The lower village is home to a large **Tibetan colony** founded by refugees who followed the Dalai Lama into exile in 1959; the nearby **Sherab Ling monastery**, seat of the 12th Tai Situpa, houses a magnificent golden Maitreya and a lively community of young monks. Above the village, tea gardens climb the lower slopes of the Dhauladhar, and short walks lead up to the river and the meditation hermitages in the forest. The place has the quiet, cosmopolitan feel you sometimes get in Himalayan towns where climbers, meditators and tea farmers coexist happily.
