---
title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

Bir Billing is one of the world's top paragliding destinations, but there's plenty more to explore on the ground.

**Bir Tibetan Colony**

Sherab Ling monastery sits in the woods and houses a huge golden Maitreya statue in its main temple room. Don't miss the smaller Tara shrine on the first floor. You can walk there in about ninety minutes or take a cab. A beautiful row of stupas lines the road behind the monastery toward Baijnath. There's a coffee shop near the stupas, and you can walk onward to Baijnath to see the ancient Shiva temple, grab fresh orange juice by the bus station, and catch a bus back to Bir.

**Upper Bir**

Continue along the main road onto a path uphill. After a few minutes, walk down to the river and cool off in one of the natural pools.

**Billing**

Head to the Billing launch site for stunning mountain-top views and to watch paragliders take off. Share a cab with paragliders from the Tibetan Colony around 9:30 a.m. from Emaho Cafe for the one-hour climb to Billing. An old couple living on the mountain often serves tea, chapati, and omelets. You can stay overnight, walk back down in a few hours, or take a cab.
