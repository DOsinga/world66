---
title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

Dharamsala and its surroundings offer a rich mix of Buddhist heritage, colonial history, Hindu temples, and natural beauty.

**McLeod Ganj**

The heart of 'Little Lhasa in India.' The Buddha temple sits opposite the residence of His Holiness the Dalai Lama and is well worth a visit. The Tibetan Institute of Performing Arts (TIPA), a one-kilometre walk from McLeod Ganj, preserves Tibetan musical, dance, and theatrical traditions and holds a ten-day folk opera each April. A Tibetan handicraft centre and Sunday market are also nearby.

**Bhagsunag Falls**

Eleven kilometres from Lower Dharamsala, Bhagsunag has an old temple, a freshwater spring, and a restaurant. A further two-kilometre walk leads to the beautiful Bhagsunag waterfall.

**St. John's Church**

The Church of St. John in the Wilderness lies on the road between McLeod Ganj and Forsythe Ganj, eight kilometres from Lower Dharamsala. It contains a monument to Lord Elgin, a Viceroy of India who died in Dharamsala and was buried here in 1863.

**Dal Lake**

Nestled among hills and tall fir trees, eleven kilometres from Lower Dharamsala. It's the starting point for excursions and treks and sits next to the Tibetan Children's Village.

**Dharamkot**

A picnic spot on the crest of a hill, eleven kilometres from Dharamsala, with panoramic views of the Kangra Valley, Pong Dam Lake, and the Dhauladhar ranges.

**Triund**

At 2,975 metres and twenty kilometres from Dharamsala, Triund sits at the foot of the perpetually snow-clad Dhauladhar. The snow line starts at Ilaqa, five kilometres beyond. A popular trekking and camping destination with a Forest Department rest house, though water must be fetched from about two kilometres away.

**Jwalamukhi Temple**

Thirty kilometres from Kangra and fifty-six from Dharamsala. Dedicated to the Goddess of Light, this is one of northern India's most popular Hindu temples. There is no idol; instead, an eternally burning blue flame emanates from the rock sanctum. The golden dome was a gift from Emperor Akbar. Major fairs are held during the Navratras in April and October.

**Masroor Rock Temples**

Forty-eight kilometres from Dharamsala, these fifteen monolithic rock-cut temples in Indo-Aryan style bear a striking resemblance to the Kailash Temple at Ellora. The main temple is dedicated to Lord Rama, Lakshman, and Sita.
