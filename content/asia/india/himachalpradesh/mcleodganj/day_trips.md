If
you are inclined towards Buddhism or are a Himalayan Traveler then a visit to
McLeodganj in India
is a must. This beautiful Himalayan town is located in the upper part of
Dharamshala in the state of Himachal Pradesh in India.
It was established as British settlement during British Rule in India
and named after David McLeod. Now it is the headquarters of Tibetan government
in exile and the residence of the Dalai Lama. Because of its Tibetan culture
this hill station is also called “Little Lhasa.” Being in the lap of Himalayas
this small town offer many activities for its visitors.

**Cycling
to Palampur**

For
those adventure types, cycling to Palampur is really an amazing experience
which will always be fresh in your memory. Located about 24 kilometers from
McLeodganj amid the Dhauladhar Ranges
in the Kangra Valley,
Palampur is also known as the tea city of the Northern India
because of its tea gardens. Cycling past the tea garden along the zigzag
mountain road is an experience of a lifetime. It is the romantic destination of
the Kangra Valley
with its Pines and Deodars woods fragrance in the atmosphere.

**Trekking
in Kangra**

Kangra
is located just 19 kilometers from McLeodganj but is the ultimate destination
for those interested in trekking in the Himalayan
Ranges. There are many lush green
valleys, springs and mountain ranges in Kangra. Most of these trekking
expeditions start from McLeodganj or Dharmshala. Spending time overcoming the challenges
of The Mighty Himalayan Ranges and discovering some of the hidden beauty of
Mother Nature is truly mesmerizing in the trekking.

**Norbulingka
Institute**

This
is the best way to understand the Tibetan art and culture. The institute is
situated about 15 kilometers on the outskirts of McLeodganj and was established
by Dalai Lama. It is the center for continuation and training of Tibet
art and craft. Here you will also get a chance to interact with the crafts
person who are busy weaving clothes or in woodwork. You can also see the
photographic exhibition of the establishment of this institute in one of the
building. However more amazing is the Losel
Doll Museum
which is pretty dolls dressed up in traditional dress of Tibet
depicting life of people in each region of Tibet
as well as their dance and festivals.
