McLeodganj packs a remarkable amount of culture, nature, and spirituality into a small Himalayan town.

**Tsuglagkhang Temple**

Home to the Dalai Lama, the Tsuglagkhang Temple is the largest Tibetan temple outside Tibet. The walk from McLeodganj's centre takes about fifteen minutes along a trail with gorgeous mountain and stream views. The temple complex includes a large meditation hall and the Tibet Museum, which documents the Chinese occupation and the Tibetan exodus through photographs and videos (open Tuesday to Sunday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.).

**Yoga and Meditation Centre of Vipassana**

A few kilometres from town, this centre offers meditation classes led by long-practising Buddhists with years of training in meditation, breathing, yoga, and pilates.

**Nowroji's Market**

Excellent shopping including discounted Tibetan rugs, woodcarved masks, paper artwork, glass beads, and jewellery. Prices are mostly fixed, though you can barter when buying multiple items.

**Dhauladhar Range Treks**

Those staying a few days will love hiking the Dhauladhar Range, featuring some of the most stunning mountain scenery in the world. You can hike in shorts while photographing snow-capped peaks just a few hundred metres away. Local guides can be found at the coffee shops in town.

**Tibetan Children's Village**

A craft museum selling paper crafts and artwork created by local youngsters. Nearby, the Tibetan Medical and Astrological Institute is worth a wander.

**Library of Tibetan Works and Archives**

An important repository of Tibetan literature, manuscripts, and cultural artefacts.

**Gu Chu Sum Monument Gallery**

Created with involvement from former political prisoners, this gallery documents the political oppression suffered by Tibetans through countless photographs.

**Environmental Education Centre**

Run by the Tibetan Welfare Office, this centre addresses environmental issues affecting the area and explains why protecting McLeodganj's natural beauty matters.
