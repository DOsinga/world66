Pong Dam Lake is a serious angling destination, though fishing here requires a boat and some patience. Some days the water boils with jumping fish but not a single bite, while other days the catch is relentless -- the record stands at thirty-four fish in a morning on two rods, averaging about eight pounds each.

Good game includes Rohu (*Labeo Rohita*), Singhada (*Sperata Aor*), Sol (*Channa Micropeltes*), and Mully (*Wallagoo Attu*). Rohu only takes bait during the monsoons. Singhada responds to dead or live bait, or a Mepps No. 3/4 reeled in slowly parallel to the bank. Mully and Sol require live bait, though Sol occasionally takes a slow-moving plug or spinner.
