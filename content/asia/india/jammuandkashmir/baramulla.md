Baramulla sits on the Jhelum River in the western part of the Kashmir Valley, about 60 km from [Srinagar](/asia/india/jammuandkashmir/srinagar). For centuries it was the gateway to Kashmir, the point where the old Mughal road from the plains entered the valley, and it retains something of that crossroads character. The old town along the river has wooden houses with carved balconies in the traditional Kashmiri style, though many have suffered from decades of neglect and conflict.

The town itself is not a major destination, but it serves as a useful base for exploring the western valley. Gulmarg, one of India's premier ski resorts and a spectacular meadow in summer, is about 35 km to the south. The road from Baramulla to Gulmarg climbs through pine forests with views across the Pir Panjal range. To the north, the road continues toward Uri and the Line of Control.

Baramulla has a railway station on the Srinagar-Baramulla line and regular bus connections. The security situation in the Kashmir Valley can change rapidly — check conditions before travelling and be prepared for checkpoints on the roads. When things are calm, this is a beautiful stretch of country, with the river winding through orchards of apple and walnut trees below snow-capped peaks.
