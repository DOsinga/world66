---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The main gateway to the Kashmir Valley is Srinagar Airport, which has frequent daily flights from Delhi (about 1.5 hours) and connections to Mumbai, Chandigarh, Jammu, and other Indian cities. IndiGo, Air India, and SpiceJet all serve the route. Flying is by far the easiest option — the approach over the mountains is spectacular in itself.

By road, the standard route is via Jammu: take a train or bus from Delhi to Jammu (around 10 hours), then continue 290 km to Srinagar by bus or shared Sumo taxi (8 hours or more, with security checkpoints). The Banihal tunnel keeps this route open year-round, though heavy snowfall can cause delays in winter. Shared Sumos from Jammu's bus stand are faster than government buses.

There is no railway to Srinagar; the nearest railhead is at Banihal, from which buses and taxis cover the remaining distance.

The alternative road approach is from Leh via the Srinagar–Leh Highway, open roughly June to October. This route crosses the Zoji La pass (3,528 m) and is only for those already in Ladakh heading west.
