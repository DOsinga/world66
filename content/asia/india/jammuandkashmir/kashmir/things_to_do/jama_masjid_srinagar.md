Srinagar's Jama Masjid is one of the most impressive mosques in India, built almost entirely from deodar cedar — a wood so durable the original 14th-century structure has survived multiple destructions and rebuildings. The current building dates from 1674 under Aurangzeb. Its forest of 378 wooden pillars and the distinctive square minarets rising at each corner give it a character very different from the marble mosques of Delhi or Agra. The surrounding old city lanes, with their wooden-fronted shops and neighbourhood shrines, are worth an hour's wandering.
