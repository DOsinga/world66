[Pahalgam](/asia/india/jammuandkashmir/pahalgam), 96 km east of Srinagar, is the main trekking base in the Kashmir Valley. At 2,130 m at the confluence of the Lidder and Sheshnag rivers, it is ringed by pine forests and alpine meadows with peaks rising on all sides. The trek to Kolahoi Glacier — the source of the Lidder river — is a classic two-day route. The longer loop via Aru and Lidderwat to Sheshnag Lake is one of the finest walks in Kashmir. For those who prefer water at speed, the Lidder also offers Grade II–III whitewater rafting from March to September.
