---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Kashmir Valley is accessible year-round but has a clear best season. April and May are the most spectacular months: tulips and irises bloom in the Mughal Gardens, cherry blossoms line the roads, and the snow on the surrounding peaks is still fresh. The light is excellent and the crowds are lighter than summer.

June to August is peak season. Indian domestic tourists fill Srinagar, houseboats are booked out, and prices are at their highest. The weather is warm rather than hot, and evenings are cool. Advance booking is essential in July and August.

September and October are many experienced travellers' preference. The summer rush subsides, the light turns golden, and the chinar trees around Dal Lake go brilliant orange and red. The Tulip Festival in late March or early April — when over a million tulips bloom at the Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden, the largest such garden in Asia — is worth timing a trip around if you can.

November to March is cold and often grey. Most houseboats close for winter. The valley's main draw in this season is skiing at [Gulmarg](/asia/india/jammuandkashmir/gulmarg), which operates from December to March on some of the longest runs in Asia.

Migratory birds arrive at Hokersar Wetland (15 km from Srinagar) in September and October — a quiet morning excursion for birdwatchers.
