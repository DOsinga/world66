Kulgam is a small town in the southern Kashmir Valley, sitting at 1,739 metres on the banks of the river Veshaw with the foothills of the Pir Panjal mountains rising across the valley. It is the district headquarters and known locally as the rice bowl of Kashmir, surrounded by rice paddies and apple orchards.

The main draw for visitors is Aharbal, a spectacular waterfall in the Kulgam district where the river Veshaw drops off a cliff edge. The falls are fed by Konsarnag Lake, a high-altitude glacial lake that makes a rewarding multi-day trek from the valley floor. The scenery along the Veshaw valley is quintessential Kashmir — green, lush, and backed by snow-capped peaks.

In Kulgam town, the shrine of Syed Simnan Sahib — a 14th-century mystic from Iran who settled here — is an important pilgrimage site with a handsome deodar wood structure overlooking the river.
