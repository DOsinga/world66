title: "Manali - Leh"

A trip on the road from Manali to Leh, which passes over a number of the highest passes in the world, has to be one of the highlights of any trip to the Himalaya. Before the road was built, these passes were traversed by traders who plied between Central Asia and India.
The road, which was planned in the 1960’s but only completed in 1987, was built as a vitally important access route for the Indian Army to the disputed Indian - Pakastani border area of Kashmir. Due to the severe weather conditions experienced, the road can only be kept open for between 3 and 4 months each year.
The journey begins in Manali, a hill station situated in the southern foothills of the Himalayas. It then winds its way across the main Himalayan range, crossing four passes in transit from the alpine scenery around Manali to the high altitude desert scenery of the Tibetan plateau, finally ending 500km away in Leh. The highest pass on the route, the Taglang-La , is over 5,300 metres above sea level.
There are a number of options for the journey, but either way it’s a two-day drive. A-class bus (Rs 1000) halt overnight at the tented camps at Sarchu, while the B-class bus (Rs 400) stops overnight at Keylong. A further alternative is to hire a 4WD for up to Rs 12,000 one way. Finally many people tackle the route on motorbikes and a few hardened adventurers tackle the arduous route on bicycles.
However you tackle this route, remember that the roads and conditions can be extreme, and there is very little fuel (Tandi is the last fuel stop). Hotel accommodation is limited to Keylong and Darcha, after that there are a few tented camps.
