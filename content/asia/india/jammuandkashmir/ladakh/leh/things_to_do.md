---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Leh is primarily a base for exploring the wider Ladakh region, but there is plenty to occupy several days in and around the town.

**Monastery visits** are the main draw. The Indus Valley stretching east and west of Leh holds some of the finest Buddhist architecture in Asia. Hemis, Thiksey, Shey, Alchi, and Lamayuru are the highlights — see the Sights section for detail on each.

**Trekking** from Leh ranges from half-day walks to multi-week expeditions. The Markha Valley Trek (6–8 days, crossing two passes above 5,000 m) is the classic. Shorter options include the Stok Kangri summit (strenuous, 6,150 m), the walk to Hemis via the Indus villages, and day hikes to various monasteries. Permit requirements vary by area; check current rules before setting out.

**River rafting** on the Indus is a highlight for many visitors. The standard day trip from Spituk to Karu covers Grade II–III rapids; the longer Zanskar River section (entering at Nimu) is more serious. Dozens of operators in Leh arrange half-day to multi-day trips from June to September.

**Jeep expeditions** into the high valleys — Nubra Valley (via 5,359 m Khardung La), Pangong Lake, Tso Moriri, and the Zanskar Valley — are the way to see the remoter parts of Ladakh. Most visitors hire a jeep and driver for 2–5 day trips out of Leh. Nubra (dunes, Bactrian camels, Diskit Monastery) and Pangong (the extraordinary blue lake on the India-China border) are the two most popular.

**Polo and archery** are traditional Ladakhi sports; competitions are held in summer in Leh's polo ground.

Altitude sickness affects most visitors for the first 24–48 hours. Plan to rest on arrival, drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol the first night, and don't rush into strenuous activity.
