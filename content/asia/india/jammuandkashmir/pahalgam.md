Pahalgam sits at the confluence of the Lidder and Sheshnag rivers at 2,130 m, about 96 km east of Srinagar. It is the main trekking base in the Kashmir Valley and the starting point for the annual Amarnath pilgrimage, one of the most significant Hindu pilgrimages in India.

The setting is beautiful — pine forests climb the valley walls, alpine meadows rise above, and the Lidder river rushes through town. Aru, a small village 12 km upstream, is a quieter alternative base with better mountain views. The trek from Pahalgam to Kolahoi Glacier (source of the Lidder) is a classic 2-day route; the longer trek via Aru to Lidderwat and Sheshnag Lake is one of the finest walks in Kashmir.

Pahalgam is also the base for whitewater rafting on the Lidder (March to September, Grade II–III — good for beginners and intermediate paddlers) and for horse trekking into the surrounding hills.

The town has expanded with tourist development and can feel congested in peak summer. September and early October offer the best weather and smaller crowds. Accommodation ranges from riverside guesthouses to mid-range hotels.
