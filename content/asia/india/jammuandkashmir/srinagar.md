Srinagar is the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, a city built around lakes and waterways in a broad mountain valley at 1,585 m. It has been called "Paradise on Earth" since the Mughal emperors chose it as their summer retreat, and the combination of Dal Lake, the old city's carved wooden mosques, and the snow-capped peaks visible from almost everywhere does justify the reputation.

The old city on the western bank of the Jhelum River is compact and walkable, its streets a warren of wooden buildings and neighbourhood shrines. The main Mughal gardens — Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, Chashma Shahi — line the eastern shore of Dal Lake. The lake itself is the city's heart: houseboats moored at its edges, shikaras (traditional wooden boats) ferrying produce and tourists alike across its surface, and the Zabarwan hills rising behind.

Srinagar has had a difficult few decades. The insurgency of the 1990s devastated tourism, and the political situation has remained tense. But tourists have returned in large numbers since the 2000s, and the city now feels far more relaxed. That said, travel advisories should be checked before visiting — things can change quickly.

The city makes an excellent base for exploring the wider valley. [Gulmarg](/asia/india/jammuandkashmir/gulmarg) is an hour's drive for skiing or the Gondola. [Pahalgam](/asia/india/jammuandkashmir/pahalgam) is a 2-hour drive for trekking and river rafting. The day-trip options are extensive.
