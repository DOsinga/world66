---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Srinagar's festivals reflect the city's mixed heritage, with Islamic, Hindu, and Sikh celebrations all observed.

**Lohri** (13 January) marks the end of winter with bonfires and communal dancing. It is particularly significant for newly married couples and families expecting children.

**Navroz** is the Persian/Shia New Year, celebrated over nine days in spring. Shia Muslims gather in mosques, visit relatives, and prepare special foods including biriyani, kebabs, and phirni.

**Urs festivals** mark the death anniversaries of Sufi and Islamic saints throughout the year. The Urs at the Hazratbal Shrine draws large crowds and is attended by both Muslims and Hindus. Unusually, both communities often avoid meat during Urs observances.

**Baisakhi** (April) is a harvest festival marked with fairs across northern India. In Kashmir it is also considered an auspicious day for weddings.

**Ramzan** (dates vary, Islamic calendar) sees the city slow through the days but animate in the evenings. The month ends with the celebrations of Eid ul-Fitr.

**Tulip Festival** (usually late March to early April) coincides with the blooming of the Mughal Gardens, when over a million tulips are in flower at Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden — the largest tulip garden in Asia.
