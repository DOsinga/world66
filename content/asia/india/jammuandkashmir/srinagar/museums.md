**Sri Pratap Singh Museum**

The main museum of Kashmir, located at Lal Mandi, Srinagar. Originally the summer palace of the Maharajas, it became a museum in 1898. The collection spans Kashmiri handicrafts, pashmina shawls, textiles, paintings, arms and armour, metal utensils, ornaments, and carved woodwork. The "toshkhana" collection includes a remarkable needlework map of Kashmir embroidered on a shawl, and some fine stone sculptures from the medieval period. Good for a couple of hours; closed Mondays.

**Kashmir University Museum**

Inside the university campus, this smaller museum holds ethnographic and archaeological collections from different periods of the valley's civilisation, including stone sculptures and folk artefacts. Open Monday to Saturday, 10am–4pm; closed Sundays and university holidays.
