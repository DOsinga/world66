Vaishno Devi is one of the most visited Hindu pilgrimage sites in India, drawing millions of devotees each year to a sacred cave shrine in the Trikuta Mountains, 61 km north of Jammu at 1,560 m elevation. The cave is said to house the three forms of the goddess — Maha Kali, Maha Lakshmi, and Maha Saraswati — as natural rock formations (pindies) rather than sculpted images.

The pilgrimage begins at Katra, the base town 1–1.5 hours from Jammu by road. All pilgrims must register and collect a Yatra Slip at the Tourism Reception Centre in Katra — it is free but mandatory, and you will not be allowed past the Banganga checkpoint without it.

The trek from Katra to the Bhawan shrine is 13 km and takes 4–6 hours. The path is well maintained with rest stops throughout. Ponies and pithus (porters carrying people in chairs) are available for those unable to walk. Starting at 4am is recommended to reach the shrine by morning and avoid the midday heat and longest queues.

The route passes through Banganga, Charan Paduka, and Adhkunwari before reaching the Bhawan. Cameras and leather items are not permitted inside the cave. After the cave darshan, visitors continue 2.5 km to Bhairav Temple — considered essential for completing the pilgrimage.

The shrine is most crowded during Navratri (October and March/April). Outside these periods it is considerably calmer.
