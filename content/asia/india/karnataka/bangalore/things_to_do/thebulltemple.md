In this temple built by Kempe Gowda the object of veneration is a magnificent Nandi (statue of a bull), 15 feet tall and over 20 feet long. It is carved out of a single block of granite. In Nov/Dec every year, when the groundnuts have been harvested, a groundnut fair is held near the temple. The first groundnuts are offered by the farmers to the sacred bull.
