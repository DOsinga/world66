Bijapur, now officially known as Vijayapura, is a historic city in northern Karnataka that was once the capital of the Adil Shahi sultanate. Between the 16th and 17th centuries the city accumulated some of the finest Islamic architecture in India, and what remains today is remarkable — a skyline of domes, minarets, and monumental gateways rising from the Deccan plain.

The Gol Gumbaz is the centrepiece: a massive tomb whose central dome, when completed in 1656, was the second largest in the world. The interior is famous for its Whispering Gallery — a circular balcony where the slightest sound travels the full circumference of the dome and echoes back with uncanny clarity. The Ibrahim Rouza, built a generation earlier, is smaller but more beautiful — its minarets and fine carvings influenced the design of the Taj Mahal. The Citadel at the city centre once contained palaces and mosques, most now in ruins but still atmospheric, and the Jama Masjid is one of the largest mosques in the country.

Bijapur is an easy day trip or overnight from Badami or Hampi, though it rewards spending a full day if you want to explore the dozens of lesser-known mosques, tombs, and tanks scattered across the old city.
