**Useful Information for Visitors to Gokarna**

You may find the following **Gokarna practical information**
useful if you're staying at this colorful religious town in Karnataka,
as it will surely help you feel more confident and comfortable when
communicating with the local people.

**Demographics **

Gokarna has a population of over 25,000 residents, but this number
grows rapidly due to the pilgrims coming from all over India to visit
the numerous impressive Hindu temples or to attend the Maha Shivaratri
festival. The local people use mainly their native Kannada language,
which is one of the classical languages of India. English is spoken by
about one third of the locals, but it is spoken at an average level. You
may experience some communication difficulties, and it is a good idea
to have someone with you who knows the language.

**Currency **

The currency used in Gokarna, as in the rest of India, is the Indian
rupee. One US dollar is worth about 47 rupees in summer 2010. All
payments are made in cash, so you will surely need to exchange some
money. You can do this at the Om Hotel, Kiran's Internet or at the Pai
STD. The latter gives the best exchange rates and is the only officially
licensed money exchange booth in Gokarna. If you have a VISA card, you
can also use the ATM at the Karnataka Bank on Bus Stand Road, but keep
in mind the ATM will charge you a small withdrawal fee.
