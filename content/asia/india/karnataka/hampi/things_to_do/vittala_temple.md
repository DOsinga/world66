The most magnificent temple in Hampi. Built in the 15th and 16th centuries, it is famous for its 56 musical pillars — striking them with knuckles or a wooden stick produces tones resembling classical Indian instruments. The stone chariot in the courtyard, its wheels once able to turn, is an icon of Vijayanagara architecture. Entry fee Rs 10 for Indians, USD 5 for foreign tourists. Open weekends at night when the complex is lit.
