Ullal is a small coastal town about 10 km south of Mangalore, where the Netravathi and Gurpur rivers meet the Arabian Sea. It is still primarily a fishing village — boats go out at night, the catch comes in at dawn — but it sits close enough to Mangalore to draw visitors looking for a quieter stretch of coast.

The town carries the memory of Abbakka Rani, a 16th-century Jain queen who resisted Portuguese expansion along the Konkan coast with remarkable tenacity. She repulsed multiple Portuguese expeditions, once surrounding a frigate at night with her Mogaveera warriors and routing the crew. When a larger armada was finally sent under the Portuguese Governor himself, she was betrayed — legend says for a casket of silver. The ruins of her temple still stand, and locals speak of her with genuine pride. A small moat to the south marks what may have been her palace.

The beach at Ullal runs south from the town for several kilometres, backed by casuarina trees. The bar where the rivers meet the sea — with Mangalore visible across the water and the Western Ghats in the distance — is one of the better views in the region. Cycling along the backwaters and joining fishermen on their trips out to sea are the main reasons to linger.
