Murudeshwar sits on a small peninsula jutting into the Arabian Sea in Uttara Kannada district, dominated by what was once claimed to be the world's tallest Shiva statue — a 37-metre figure that glitters in the direct sun. The Murudeshwara Temple at its base is built in traditional style and draws pilgrims year-round.

The town is easy to reach on the Konkan Railway, which hugs the coast through spectacular scenery. Most visitors combine it with a stop at the Netrani Island (also called Pigeon Island), one of Karnataka's better diving and snorkelling spots, about 14 km offshore. The beach in front of the temple is busy but the views of the statue and the two gopurams framing the sea behind it are hard to beat.
