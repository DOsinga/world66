Sravanabelagola is home to the Gommateshwara statue, a 17-metre monolithic figure of Lord Bahubali carved in 981 AD and widely considered the tallest freestanding monolithic statue in the world. It stands on top of Vindhyagiri Hill and can be seen for miles across the surrounding plains. Climbing the 614 rock-cut steps to the top is part of the experience.

The town is one of the most sacred Jain pilgrimage sites in India, and every 12 years hosts the Mahamastakabhisheka — an anointing ceremony in which the statue is bathed in milk, curds, saffron, and gold coins from scaffolding erected for the occasion. It draws hundreds of thousands of people. Located on the Bangalore–Hassan highway, it makes an easy stop on the way to Hassan or Belur.
