A pilgrimage town that puts its money where its mouth is on the subject of unity in diversity. The temple is dedicated to Shiva, the priests are Vaishnavites, and the management is by a Jain family — the Heggades — who have run it for centuries. Free meals are served daily to all visitors regardless of religion or caste. The attached museum of antiques collected by Dr Veerendra Hegde — swords, cameras, typewriters, old kitchen equipment — is worth an hour of anyone's time.
