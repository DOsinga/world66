One of the most important Naga (serpent deity) shrines in India, set deep in the Western Ghats on the banks of the Kumaradhara river. The surrounding forest is dense enough that the approach feels genuinely wild, and bathing in the river before visiting is both customary and refreshing. Pilgrims come specifically to perform the Sarpa Dosha ritual here.
