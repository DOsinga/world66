The centrepiece of Udupi and one of the most visited Vaishnava shrines in South India. The deity — Krishna as a young child — is viewed through a window of nine holes in a wooden screen, the Kanakana Kindi, named after the 15th-century devotee-poet Kanakadasa who reportedly caused the wall to open so he could see the Lord. The surrounding mathas (monasteries) keep the tradition of Vedic learning alive; you can hear Sanskrit recitation echoing through the lanes at dawn.
