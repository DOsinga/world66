Located 70 km north of [Thiruvananthapuram](/asia/india/kerala/thiruvananthapuram), Kollam is one of the oldest ports on the Malabar coast and the cashew capital of India. It is also the southern gateway to Kerala's backwaters.

The eight-hour boat trip between Kollam and [Alappuzha](/asia/india/kerala/alappuzha) is the longest and most enchanting backwater journey in Kerala. The public ferry leaves each morning and winds through narrow canals, past coconut groves, rice paddies and small villages where daily life unfolds right at the water's edge. It is one of those trips you remember for years.

The town itself is a pleasant, unhurried place with a few colonial-era buildings around the old harbour. Ashtamudi Lake, just south of town, is a beautiful expanse of water where you can arrange shorter houseboat trips if the full journey to Alappuzha feels too long.
