Kumarakom is a cluster of small islands on the eastern shore of Vembanad Lake, about 16 km from [Kottayam](/asia/india/kerala/kottayam). It is one of the most popular backwater destinations in Kerala, and for good reason: the setting is beautiful, with quiet lagoons, rice paddies and coconut groves all around.

The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, spread across the grounds of a former rubber estate, is home to egrets, herons, cormorants and, if you are lucky, migratory Siberian storks between November and February. Early morning is the best time to visit.

Most visitors come for the houseboat cruises on Vembanad Lake. You can arrange anything from a few hours to an overnight trip, drifting through the backwaters with freshly cooked fish curry served on board. The lake is also the venue for several of Kerala's famous snake boat races.
