Payyanur is a small town in the Kannur district of northern Kerala, set in the valley of the Ezhimala Hills. Its name derives from the Sri Subrahmanya Swami Temple here, "Payyan" being an appellation of Lord Subrahmanya.

The town has a long history. The Ezhimala port was described by travellers including Ibn Battuta in 1342 and Marco Polo in 1293, both of whom noted its importance as a trading hub. Archaeological finds in the area confirm the existence of a significant settlement here centuries ago.

Today Payyanur is best known as a centre for theyyam, the extraordinary ritual dance form of northern Kerala. The town is also known for Pavithra Mothiram (sacred ring-making) and traditional astrology. If you are in northern Kerala during the theyyam season (roughly October to May), Payyanur and the surrounding villages are some of the best places to witness performances.
