---
title: 'Beaches'
type: section
---

**Papanasam Beach** is the main beach, stretching below the famous red cliffs. The name means "Destroyer of Sins," and the Janardhana Swamy temple sits at its southern end. Natural springs emerge from the cliff face, and pilgrims come here to perform religious rites. The beach is backed by the cliffside path with its restaurants, shops and guesthouses. Dress modestly near the temple.

**Black Beach** is adjacent to the southern end of Papanasam Beach. The sand here is dark, the crowd is thinner, and steps from the cliff provide easy access. Mineral springs nearby are said to have healing properties. This is the quieter option.

**North Cliff Beach** stretches north of the main cliff area and tends to be less crowded. It is a good spot for sunbathing and swimming, though the currents can be strong.

The best months for the beaches are October to March, when the weather is sunny and the sea is relatively calm. During the monsoon season (June to August) the waves are rough and swimming is dangerous. Even in good weather, be cautious of the currents, which can be powerful.
