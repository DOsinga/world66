---
title: 'Budget Travel Ideas'
type: section
---

Varkala is one of Kerala's best budget destinations. Accommodation is cheap, food is inexpensive, and the main attractions cost little or nothing.

**Getting there** is easy and cheap. From Trivandrum, take the bus (about an hour and a half) or the train. Both cost next to nothing. Varkala has its own railway station.

**Getting around** on a rented scooter is the cheapest option for exploring beyond the cliff area. Auto-rickshaws are available but agree on the fare before you get in, as drivers tend to hike prices in tourist zones.

**Yoga classes** are widely available and affordably priced. The Baby House offers reasonably priced morning and evening sessions without the inflated tourist-area prices.

**The Janardhana Swami Temple** is free to visit and over 2,000 years old. The sacred springs and beach beside it are one of Varkala's highlights.

**Ayurvedic massages** are cheaper along the main Varkala road than on the cliffside. You will also find people offering foot and back massages on the beach at bargain prices.

**Eating cheaply** is easy. The small restaurants away from the cliff are significantly cheaper than the tourist places, and the food is often better. Look for local places serving thalis and fish curry.
