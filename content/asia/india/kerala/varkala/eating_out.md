---
title: 'Eating Out'
type: section
---

The cliffside restaurants in Varkala all serve a similar menu of fresh seafood, Indian dishes and a smattering of Western food. The fish is the star: grilled marlin, butterfish, shark curried in coconut milk, and whatever else the fishermen brought in that morning.

**Cafe Del Mar** offers Indian, Italian and Mexican dishes alongside the seafood. The Badam Halwa (a dessert made from almonds, sugar, ghee and milk) is excellent. Beer and wine are served discreetly.

For authentic Kerala food, seek out the smaller restaurants away from the cliff. A proper fish curry with rice, sambar and a couple of vegetable sides costs a fraction of what you would pay at the tourist places and is usually better. Try appam (fermented rice pancakes) with fish curry for breakfast.

The local desserts are worth trying. Fruit Kheer, made from banana, yoghurt, sugar and saffron, is a Varkala favourite. Nendranga chips (banana chips fried in coconut oil) are sold everywhere and make a good snack.
