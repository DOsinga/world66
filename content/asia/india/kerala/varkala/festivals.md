---
title: 'Festivals'
type: section
---

**Onam** (August/September) is the biggest festival in Varkala, as it is across all of Kerala. Families lay intricate flower patterns (pookalam) at their doorsteps and gather for the grand sadya feast. If you are in Varkala during Onam, expect a festive atmosphere and many businesses to close for the main day.

**Vallamkali** is a boat race festival held in August with religious significance tied to Sri Krishna. Even if the religious aspect does not interest you, the snake boat races are exciting to watch.

**Navaratri** ("nine nights") is celebrated in September or October. On the final day, Vijayadashami, children who are about to begin school draw their first letter in rice with their finger, honouring the goddess of wisdom Saraswathi.

**Chandanakudam at Beemapally** is the most colourful Muslim festival in the area, honouring the pilgrim Beema Beevi who travelled from Mecca. It begins in October and lasts ten days. Pilgrims walk in procession around the tomb carrying sandalwood-smeared clay pots filled with monetary offerings.

The **Sivagiri Pilgrimage** in late December draws thousands of followers of Sree Narayana Guru to the ashram on the hilltop above Varkala. It is a unique event that combines social reform philosophy with religious devotion.
