Mandsaur is a district town in western Madhya Pradesh with a surprisingly rich archaeological heritage. The main attraction is the temple of Lord Pashupatinath on the bank of the Shivna River — its idol is said to have a parallel only in the famous Pashupatinath temple in Kathmandu.

The town sits in the Malwa plateau region, where the local language is Malwi, a blend of Rajasthani and Hindi. The surrounding area is one of India's major opium-producing regions, cultivated legally under government license for the pharmaceutical industry. Ancient stone inscriptions and ruins scattered around the district point to a long history of settlement stretching back to the Gupta period.
