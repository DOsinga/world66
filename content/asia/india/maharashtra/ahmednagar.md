Ahmednagar — usually just called Nagar — sits about 120 km northeast of [Pune](/asia/india/maharashtra/pune) in the dry Deccan plateau. Founded in 1494 by Ahmad Nizam Shah I after his victory over the Bahmani Sultanate, the city was once the capital of a powerful medieval kingdom. Today it is a mid-sized Maharashtrian city surrounded by farmland, unremarkable at first glance but with enough history layered into its streets to reward a short stop.

The star attraction is Ahmednagar Fort, a massive stone fortification ringed by a moat that has survived five centuries of sieges. The British used it as a prison during the independence movement — Jawaharlal Nehru wrote his famous "The Discovery of India" while imprisoned here. The fort is now maintained by the Indian Army and open to visitors.

The city draws a steady stream of pilgrims to Meherabad, the ashram and tomb-shrine of the spiritual teacher Meher Baba, about 10 km south of town. Even if you have no particular interest in Meher Baba, the hilltop setting is peaceful and the community welcoming. Meherazad, his later residence 14 km to the north, is also open for visits on set days.

Beyond these draws, Ahmednagar is an authentic slice of small-city Maharashtra — busy bazaars, decent street food, and the kind of place where rickshaw drivers will want to know where you are from and why you came.
