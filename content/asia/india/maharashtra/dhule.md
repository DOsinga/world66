Dhule is a city in the northern part of Maharashtra, about 340km north-east of Mumbai, close to the border with Gujarat. It sits at the convergence of three major national highways — the junction of NH-3 (Mumbai–Agra), NH-6 (Surat–Nagpur), and NH-211 — which has historically made it an important commercial hub. The climate is hot and dry, with temperatures reaching 45°C in summer.

The city itself is a working district headquarters rather than a tourism destination, but the surrounding area has interest. The Sarangkheda pilgrimage site on the Tapi river draws large numbers of visitors during its annual fair on Margashirsha Pournima, and Sarangkheda's horse fair in November–December is one of the largest in India. Nearby forests once sheltered large numbers of wildlife; today the area is known for bauxite deposits and cotton production.
