Konkan is the coastal strip of Maharashtra between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea — a long, lush, largely undeveloped stretch of beach towns, fishing villages, and sea-forts that feels remote despite being only a few hours from Mumbai. The Konkan Railway, completed in the 1990s, opened the region to visitors without destroying its character; most of the coast still lacks the commercial infrastructure of Goa to the south.

The beaches are the main draw: long, often deserted, with the kind of clear water and white sand that used to be found all along India's west coast before mass tourism arrived. Tarkarli in the south, with accessible coral reefs and some of the clearest water on the coast, is the best-known. Kashid, Diveagar, and Ganapatipule are others worth considering. The Maratha sea-forts — Janjira, Sindhudurg, Vijaydurg — are extraordinary, built on rocky islands in the sea and never taken by the Mughals or British.

Konkan food is one of the finest regional cuisines in India. Malwani cooking, from the south of the region, makes use of kokum, fresh coconut, and an abundance of seafood: crab, pomfret, squid, surmai. Eat at local restaurants and in family homes wherever possible.

The best time to visit is October to March; the monsoon (June–September) brings heavy rain, rough seas, and closed ferry services, though the landscape turns extraordinarily green.
