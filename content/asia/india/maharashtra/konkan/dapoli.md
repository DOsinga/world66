Dapoli is a small coastal town in the Ratnagiri district of Maharashtra, in the heart of the Konkan coast. Sitting at about 300 metres above sea level yet only a few kilometres from the sea, it has a pleasant climate year-round — cooler than the coast but with easy access to beaches.

The area around Dapoli has several good beaches — Murud, Karde, and Anjarle are the closest — that remain far less crowded than the beaches near Mumbai or Goa. The town itself is historic: it was a British cantonment in the colonial era and the birthplace of a number of Maharashtra's important reformers and mathematicians. The surroundings are lush, green Konkan countryside, with mango and cashew orchards and a generally unhurried pace.

Dapoli is about 215 km from Mumbai and 180 km from Pune — accessible as a weekend escape but still off the tourist circuit.
