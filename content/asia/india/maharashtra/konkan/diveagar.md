Diveagar is a quiet beach village on the Konkan coast of Maharashtra, about 165 km south of Mumbai. It sits on a stretch of coast known for its clean, uncrowded beaches, coconut groves, and the easy pace of village life that has largely been pushed out of the more popular Goa resorts.

The beach at Diveagar is long and sandy, backed by casuarina trees and coconut palms. The sea here can be rough during the monsoon, but from October to May it is calm enough for swimming. Nearby, the Harihareshwar temple complex makes it a pilgrimage destination as much as a beach retreat, drawing Hindu devotees alongside holidaymakers from Pune and Mumbai.

The village is small, and that is the point. There is no nightlife, no beach bar scene, and almost no tourist infrastructure beyond a handful of guesthouses and homestays. If you want a weekend away from the city without the crowds of Alibaug or the distance of Goa, Diveagar delivers.
