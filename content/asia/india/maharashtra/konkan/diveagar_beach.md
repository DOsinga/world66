A long, uncrowded stretch of white sand about 200 km south of Mumbai on the Konkan coast. The beach runs for roughly six kilometres, with a fishing settlement at one end and a creek at the other that attracts migratory seagulls. Casuarina trees line the shore and the access road passes through dense vegetation.

The village itself became locally famous after a gold Ganesh idol was discovered in an areca nut garden. It's a genuinely quiet place — the village has local food but little else in the way of tourist infrastructure, which is much of its appeal. The drive from Mangaon on the Mumbai-Goa highway takes about an hour on a decent road.
