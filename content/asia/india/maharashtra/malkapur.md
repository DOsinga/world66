Malkapur is a small town in the Buldhana district of Maharashtra, on the Central Railway line between Bhusawal and Akola. The town itself is unremarkable, but it serves as a base for visiting the Lonar crater — one of only five hypervelocity meteorite impact craters on earth, and the only one formed in basalt rock. The crater is roughly 40,000 years old and contains a saline lake surrounded by ruins of Hemadanti-style temples and a surprising amount of wildlife, including peafowl, monitor lizards, and langurs.
