---
title: "Eating Out in Goa"
type: section
---

Goa is an area in India with a lot of area on the beach. Because of
its popularity with beachfront resorts, food dishes in the region are versatile, differing greatly from one another. Goan food is a mix of
Portuguese, Continental and Indian techniques and flavors. Seafood and
other fish dishes are popular since it’s so close to the sea. Common
dishes served in Goa include things like xacuti (curry made in Goa) and
cafreal (spicy chicken). Since Goa has become a destination for
tourists, the area is riddled with fast food and chain restaurants with
more western flavors and Chinese food, but that doesn’t mean that you
can’t find restaurants that serve a wide variety of flavors and
cuisines.

**Indian and Regional Cuisine **

When you visit a different area you’ll always want to try and sample
the native foods of the area. Indian foods are often rich,
flavorful and very spicy. If you can’t handle a lot of spice most
places will let the chef know and they don’t mind dropping down the
heat level. There are many great places to get these local flavors all
around the beaches in Goa. One place is called Souza Lobo located in
Calangute. The restaurant has a lovely view of the beach and an
extensive menu to serve any pallet while sticking to mostly local food.
Other places to see for Indian and regional food are Shiva’s Café, Jay
Jay’s, Xavier’s and Tequila Restaurant.

**Mixed Cuisine**

Goa has really adapted to life as a tourist destination. There
are many resorts and hotels that act as home for countless travelers
who come to enjoy the beaches and Goa attractions. Because of this, many
restaurants have a diverse menu or take on fusion dishes to add to
their restaurants. This makes it easier for even the pickiest
travelers to find something they’d like to eat. For the most part
you’ll find Asian cuisine like Chinese and Thai but you’ll there are
also Italian restaurants and English themed places. One of the
Italian restaurants in the area is J & A’s Little Italy. Here they
serve a lot of traditional Italian foods such as antipasti, pizzas and
innovative pasta dishes that incorporate local ingredients.

Prices for the restaurants in the area vary by cuisine but
for the most part are middle range. Fine dining restaurants are harder
to find and if you want something cheap, there is a lot of fast food.
