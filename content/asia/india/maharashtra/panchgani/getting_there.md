title: "Getting There in Panchgani"
type: section
---

Nestled
between 5 mountains in the Satara district in Maharashtra, India the **Panchgani Getting there **experience can be quite thrilling.
Panchgani, along with Matheran and Mahabaleshwar, are a few popular
hill stations in Maharashtra.

**
Distance from Major Cities**

Panchgani,
is 285 km from Mumbai, 100 km from Pune and 18
km from Mahabaleshwar. These are the prominent areas around
Panchgani.

**By Air**

To
reach Panchgani, you could either take a flight to Pune or to Mumbai.
Pune has the
closest airport and there are direct flight to Pune from most of the
tourist destinations in India like, Goa and Bangalore as well as Mumbai.
From Pune airport you can travel to Panchgani either by a taxi or by bus. The other option is to land in Mumbai airport then proceed to
Panchgani.

**By Car**

If
you are in a mood to take a road trip and enjoy the sights while
traveling, you can travel to Panchgani by road. Landing in Mumbai
would be the best bet. You can hire taxis from the international or
the domestic airports in Mumbai. The taxi will take you directly to
Panchgani. The cost is between $55 and $80, depending on the car
that you hire. Traveling by road will take anywhere
between 3 and 4 hours, depending on the speed at which you
drive, the weather and the traffic blocks, if any. You can also take a
taxi from Pune to Panchgani, which would cost you approximately INR 2100 or
$46.

**By Bus**

You
can experience the public transport of India by taking a bus to
Panchgani. You can take a bus either from Pune or from Mumbai. There are state run buses and
private players as well who shuttle between Pune and Panchgani and
Mumbai and Panchgani. A bus ride from Pune will cost you around INR
300 or $7. The rates for the route from Mumbai to Panchgani will be a bit higher. The travel time is approximately 4 hours.

**By Rail**

Find
out about the functioning of the rail system introduced in India by the
British. You can take a train ride from Mumbai to Pune. Panchgani,
unfortunately does not have a train station. In Mumbai, there are 3 boarding stations for the trains to Pune. You can either take
the train departing from Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST), Lokmanya
Tilak Terminus (LTT) or the Dadar Terminus (DR).
