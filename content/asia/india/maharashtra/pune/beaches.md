---
title: 'Beaches near Pune'
type: section
---

Pune has no coastline, but the Konkan coast is only three to four hours away by car, making weekend beach trips a Pune tradition.

**Kashid** is the most popular beach getaway from Pune, about 165 km to the southwest. The beach has clean white sand and relatively clear water by Arabian Sea standards. It gets crowded on weekends and holidays, but during the week you can have long stretches to yourself. Be careful around noon when the tide comes in strong. The drive through the ghats and the coastal lowlands is scenic on its own.

**[Alibaug](/asia/india/maharashtra/alibag)** is less about the beach itself and more about the historic Kolaba Fort, which stands just offshore and can be reached on foot at low tide. Timing is everything -- the window is about an hour before and after low tide. Alibaug is well connected to Mumbai by ferry and to Pune by road (about 140 km).

**Murud** is a quiet fishing town further south along the coast, known for the dramatic Janjira Fort sitting on an island in the bay. The beach is pleasant but the real draw is the fort and the quiet, unhurried pace of the town. The wooden cottages and views from the Dattatreya temple are worth the trip.

**Harihareshwar** is known as the "house of God" -- a temple town at the tip of a peninsula where three rivers meet the sea. The beaches are clean and uncrowded, and the rocky coastline makes for good exploring. It is about 200 km from Pune and works best as an overnight trip.

**Dapoli** and **Ganpatipule** are further afield (four to five hours) but worth the extra drive for cleaner, less crowded beaches. Ganpatipule has a famous Ganesh temple right on the beach, and Dapoli offers dolphin-spotting boat trips.
