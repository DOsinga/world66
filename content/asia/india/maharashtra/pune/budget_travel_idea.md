---
title: 'Budget Travel Ideas in Pune'
type: section
---

Pune is one of the more affordable major cities in India, and you can have a full experience without spending much.

**Accommodation** ranges from basic guesthouses near the railway station (from around 500-800 rupees a night) to decent budget hotels in the 1,500-2,500 rupee range. The areas around Shivajinagar and Deccan Gymkhana offer good value with easy access to the main sights.

**Street food** is where Pune really shines on a budget. A plate of misal pav -- the fiery sprouted lentil curry that is Pune's signature dish -- costs 50 to 80 rupees at most local joints. Bedekar Misal near Narayan Peth is legendary. Vada pav is everywhere for 15 to 30 rupees. The area around FC Road and JM Road is packed with student-friendly eateries serving thalis for under 150 rupees.

**Forts** are free to visit and make for excellent day outings. Sinhagad, just 25 km away, is the easiest and cheapest day trip -- take a local bus to the base village and trek up. The buttermilk and bhakri lunch at the top costs almost nothing. Rajgad and Torna are free alternatives for more adventurous hikers.

**City sights** are inexpensive. Entry to the Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum is nominal, and Shaniwar Wada charges a small fee. The Pataleshwar Caves, Aga Khan Palace, and most temples are free. Walking through the old city around Kasba Peth and Shaniwar Peth costs nothing and gives you a real feel for Pune's character.

**Transport** on local PMPML buses is extremely cheap, though routes can be confusing. The new metro is affordable and efficient on its operating corridors. Auto-rickshaws are reasonable for short hops -- insist on the meter.

**Markets** like Tulsi Baug and Laxmi Road are free entertainment even if you do not buy anything. The crowds, colors, and energy are part of the Pune experience.
