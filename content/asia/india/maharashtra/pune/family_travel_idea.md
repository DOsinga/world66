---
title: 'Family Travel Ideas in Pune'
type: section
---

Pune is a manageable city for families -- less chaotic than Mumbai, with good infrastructure and plenty to keep children interested.

**Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park** in Katraj is home to a decent collection of Indian wildlife, including a snake park that kids tend to love. The zoo is reasonably well maintained and has enough to fill a morning. The adjacent Katraj Lake is pleasant for a stroll.

**Osho Teerth Park** (also called Nullah Park) in Koregaon Park is a beautifully landscaped garden built around a reclaimed stream. The bamboo groves, stone pathways, and koi ponds make it a peaceful escape from the city noise. Children enjoy the stepping stones and fish.

**Sinhagad Fort** works well with older children who can handle a moderate trek. The climb takes about an hour, and the reward at the top is wide views, ruined fortifications to scramble around, and fresh buttermilk. Drive up if the kids are younger -- the road goes most of the way.

**Parvati Hill** is a gentler climb in the city itself -- 108 steps to the hilltop temple, with panoramic views of Pune from the top. The Parvati Museum at the summit has Peshwa-era portraits and coins that give older children a tangible connection to the history they have been hearing about.

**Karla and Bhaja Caves** combine a short trek with the excitement of exploring rock-cut caves from the second century BC. The scale of the columns and carved figures impresses children, and the forested hillside around the caves is good for running around.

**Aga Khan Palace** has spacious lawns where kids can play while parents explore the museum dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi and Kasturba Gandhi, who were interned here during the Quit India movement. The Italian arches and well-maintained gardens make it one of the more pleasant historic sites in the city.
