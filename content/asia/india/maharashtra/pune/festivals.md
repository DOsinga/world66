---
title: 'Festivals in Pune'
type: section
---

Pune celebrates festivals with a fervor that rivals any city in India. If you can time your visit to coincide with one, you will see the city at its most alive.

**Ganesh Chaturthi** (August or September) is the big one. Pune claims to have started the public celebration of this festival -- Lokmanya Tilak transformed it from a private household ritual into a mass civic event in 1893, using it to build national unity. For ten days, enormous painted clay idols of Ganesh sit in elaborately decorated pandals across the city. On the final day, processions carry the idols through the streets to the river for immersion, accompanied by drumming, dancing, and enormous crowds. The five most famous pandals -- the Manache Ganpati -- are paraded in a fixed order and the procession can last well into the next morning. Book accommodation early if you visit during this period.

**Diwali** (October or November) lights up the city for five nights with oil lamps, candles, and fireworks. Markets overflow with sweets, new clothes, and rangoli materials. The effect is magical, especially in the older parts of town.

**Janmashtami** (August or September) celebrates the birth of Krishna. The highlight is the dahi-handi competition, where teams of young men form human pyramids to reach a clay pot of curd suspended high above the street -- a reenactment of young Krishna stealing butter. Prizes attract serious competitors and the atmosphere is electric.

**Navratri** (September or October) brings nine nights of worship of Goddess Durga. In Pune this means garba and dandiya dancing at organized venues across the city, with the Bengali community hosting traditional Durga Puja celebrations at Congress House Ground. The temples of Tambdi Jogeshwari and Chatushrungi see the heaviest crowds.

**Pune Festival** takes place at the tail end of Ganesh Chaturthi and runs for about a month. Organized by the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation, it features traditional sports competitions, classical music and dance performances, and auto rallies.

**Sawai Gandharva Bhimsen Festival** (December) is one of India's premier classical music festivals, held over several days in the open air. If you have any interest in Hindustani classical music, this alone is worth planning a trip around.
