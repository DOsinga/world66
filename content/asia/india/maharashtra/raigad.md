Raigad Fort, in the Sahyadri ranges of the Western Ghats, was the capital of the Maratha warrior king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Perched on a sheer-sided plateau roughly 820 m above the surrounding valley, it was one of the most formidable fortresses of 17th-century India. Shivaji was crowned here in 1674 and died here in 1680.

The fort is now largely ruined — the British destroyed much of it after the collapse of Maratha power — but the scale of the original construction remains impressive. A ropeway (cable car) connects the base to the top; the walk up takes about two hours through forest. The iconic equestrian statue of Shivaji, the ruins of the market area (Maha Darwaja), the Jagdishwar temple, and the tomb of Shivaji are all within the fort complex.

The views of the Konkan coast and the surrounding Sahyadri ranges are spectacular on a clear day. Raigad is about 120 km from Mumbai and can be visited as a day trip.
