Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, sits at around 1,500 metres in the Khasi Hills. The British called it the "Scotland of the East" for its pine-covered hills, cool climate, and colonial-era architecture. Today it is a lively university town known for its music scene, vibrant markets, and as a gateway to the living root bridges and waterfalls of the surrounding countryside.
