The Second World War Battle of Kohima in 1944 was one of the turning points of the war in Asia — a two-week fight over a tennis court at the Deputy Commissioner's bungalow, where Allied forces halted the Japanese advance into India. The Commonwealth War Graves Commission cemetery here is immaculate, set on a hillside with rows of white headstones. The inscription on the memorial stone — a couplet by John Maxwell Edmonds — is among the most quoted of the war.
