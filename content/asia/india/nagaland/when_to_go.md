---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

October to March is the best window: dry, relatively clear, and cool at altitude. November is the peak month — the Hornbill Festival runs for ten days from 1 December at the Kisama Heritage Village near Kohima, bringing together dance performances, traditional games, music, and crafts from all the major Naga tribes. It is not a tourist fabrication; it is a genuine inter-tribal gathering that happens to be open to visitors, and it is the single best opportunity to see tribal culture in concentrated form.

Avoid June to September if you can: the monsoon makes the mountain roads treacherous and the trails muddy. The Dzukou Valley flowers in July and August, which is the one strong reason to visit during the wet months.

Winters (December–February) are cold at Kohima — temperatures can drop near freezing at night — so bring layers. Dimapur, down in the valley, stays warmer year-round.
