Chhatia, also known as Chhatiabata, is a small pilgrimage town about 30 km from Cuttack in Odisha. It is best known for its shrine of Lord Jagannath, a modern example of Orissan temple architecture. The surroundings also contain the archaeological remains of Amravati-Kataka, one of the five major forts built by the medieval king Chodaganga Deva. The forested hill of Dhania provides a scenic backdrop to the ruins.

Most visitors come on a day trip from Cuttack or as a stop en route to Puri.
