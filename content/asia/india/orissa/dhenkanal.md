Dhenkanal is a district town in central [Odisha](/asia/india/orissa), once the seat of a princely state and now a quiet administrative centre surrounded by forested hills. The town itself is modest, but the district holds some of the state's most rewarding detours for travellers willing to get off the main highways.

The Kapilash Temple, dedicated to Shiva, sits atop a hill about 25 km from town, reached by a steep climb through dense sal and teak forest. The temple is an important pilgrimage site and the surrounding Kapilash Wildlife Sanctuary is home to elephants, leopards, and a rich variety of birdlife. The Satakosia Gorge on the Mahanadi River, partly within Dhenkanal district, is one of Odisha's most dramatic landscapes — the river cuts through a narrow canyon flanked by steep, forested hillsides, and boat rides through the gorge are possible during the dry season.

Dhenkanal district is also known for its dhokra metal castings, a traditional tribal craft made using the lost-wax method. The artisan villages around Sadeibareni produce distinctive figurines and ornaments that make for excellent souvenirs. The town is on the rail line between [Bhubaneswar](/asia/india/orissa/bhubaneswar) and Sambalpur, and regular buses connect it to the state capital about three hours to the southeast.
