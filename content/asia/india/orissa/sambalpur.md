Sambalpur is a connecting point between Madhya Pradesh and Odisha, set on the banks of the Mahanadi River. Once known as a diamond trading centre, today the city is recognised for its handloom textiles — Sambalpuri saris and fabrics, woven with traditional ikat patterns, are prized across India.

The surrounding forests still harbour wildlife, and the Badrama Wildlife Sanctuary is the best-known spot for a day trip into the hills. The Hirakud Dam, one of the longest earthen dams in the world, is about 15 kilometres from town and can be visited easily. The Samaleswari Temple, dedicated to the presiding goddess of the region, is the most important religious site in the district.
