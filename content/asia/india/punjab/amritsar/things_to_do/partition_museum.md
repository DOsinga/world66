Opened in 2017 in the Town Hall building near the Golden Temple, this is the world's first museum dedicated to the 1947 Partition of British India — an event that displaced over 10 million people and killed up to two million in communal violence. The museum is built around personal testimonies: letters, photographs, oral histories, and artifacts from families on both sides of the newly drawn border.

It is sobering and carefully curated. Amritsar was one of the cities most directly torn apart by Partition, and the museum puts that trauma into human scale. Allow two hours.
