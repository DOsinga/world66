Gurdaspur is a border district in northern Punjab, sitting close to the Pakistani frontier and serving historically as a gateway to Kashmir. The district's history runs deep — from ancient habitation through Mughal rule to the Sikh kingdoms and the British Raj.

The main towns of interest are Batala, known for machine tool manufacturing, and Pathankot, a transit point for travellers heading to Dharamsala, Dalhousie and the Himalayan hill stations. Dera Baba Nanak and Sri Hargobindpur are important Sikh pilgrimage sites. The landscape is classic Punjab — flat agricultural plains watered by the Ravi and Beas rivers, green in the growing season and golden at harvest.
