Jalandhar is one of the oldest cities in Punjab, named — depending on who you ask — either after a mythological demon king who lived in water, or simply for its location between two rivers (jal meaning water). It served as the capital of Punjab until 1953, when Chandigarh took over, and today has an urban population of about a million.

The city sits roughly halfway between Delhi and [Amritsar](/asia/india/punjab/amritsar), making it a natural stop on the way to the Golden Temple. Jalandhar itself is known more as a commercial centre — particularly for sports equipment manufacturing — than as a tourist destination, but it has lively bazaars and a handful of old temples and gurudwaras worth visiting.

The best time to visit is January to March, when the weather is pleasant and you can catch festivals like Lohri (bonfires and dancing to mark the winter solstice) and Basant Panchami (kite flying to welcome spring). Summers are brutally hot from April through July, followed by monsoon rains through September.
