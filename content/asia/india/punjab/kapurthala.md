Kapurthala is a small Punjabi city with an outsized architectural legacy. The Ahluwalia dynasty rulers were besotted with European design, and the results are scattered across town. The Jagatjit Palace, modeled after Versailles, dominates the skyline with its Moorish-Indo-Saracenic domes and arches. The Panch Mandir and Moorish Mosque further reflect the eclectic tastes of its former maharajas. Walking through Kapurthala feels like flipping through an architectural scrapbook — French facades next to Sikh gurdwaras next to Mughal-inspired gardens.

The city sits about 20 kilometers from [Jalandhar](/asia/india/punjab/jalandhar), making it an easy day trip. Come for the palaces and the oddly European streetscapes, stay for the Sikh heritage sites and the unhurried pace of a town that once punched well above its weight.
