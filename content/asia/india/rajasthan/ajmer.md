Ajmer is one of India's most important Muslim pilgrimage cities, home to the Dargah Sharif of the Sufi saint Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti. The shrine draws millions of devotees each year, and the streets around it pulse with a energy quite different from the Hindu-dominated cities elsewhere in Rajasthan.

Beyond the Dargah, Ajmer has a few worthwhile stops. Ana Sagar Lake, created in the 12th century, is a pleasant place for an evening walk. The Nasiyan (Red) Jain Temple has an extraordinary gilded hall depicting the Jain universe. The town also serves as the gateway to [Pushkar](/asia/india/rajasthan/pushkar), just 15 km away over the Nag Pahar hills.
