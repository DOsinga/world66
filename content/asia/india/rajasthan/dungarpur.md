Dungarpur is a small town in southern Rajasthan, close to the Gujarat border and about 110 km from [Udaipur](/asia/india/rajasthan/udaipur). It sees very few tourists, which is part of its appeal. The town was founded in 1335 and retains a sleepy, old-world atmosphere.

The main attraction is the Udai Bilas Palace, a striking lakeside building mixing Rajput and Victorian architecture. The Juna Mahal (Old Palace) in town has remarkably well-preserved miniature paintings and mirror work. In the surrounding countryside, the temples at Baroda village and the Beneshwar shrine at the confluence of the Som and Mahi rivers are worth the trip, especially during the annual tribal fair in February.
