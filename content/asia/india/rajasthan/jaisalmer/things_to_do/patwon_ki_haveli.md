The largest and most elaborate of Jaisalmer's merchant mansions, Patwon ki Haveli is actually a cluster of five connected havelis built in the early 19th century by a wealthy brocade merchant and his five sons. The facades are covered in extraordinarily detailed jali (latticed stonework) — the craftsmen who built this spent years on carving alone. Two of the five sections are now museums; the others are still privately owned. Worth at least an hour.
