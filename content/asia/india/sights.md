India's sights range from the iconic — the Taj Mahal at dawn, the ghats of Varanasi — to landscapes and living cultures that are harder to categorize. Below are some highlights at the national level; individual state and city pages have more detail.

**Taj Mahal, Agra** — The most famous building in India, and it earns the reputation. A Mughal emperor built it as a tomb for his wife; it took 22 years and 20,000 workers. Go at dawn to beat the crowds and see the marble shift from grey to rose to white. Agra is in [Uttar Pradesh](/asia/india/uttarpradesh).

**The Ghats of Varanasi** — One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on earth, Varanasi sits on the Ganges and is the holiest city in Hinduism. The ritual life along the riverbank — cremation fires at Manikarnika Ghat, the evening aarti ceremony, pilgrims bathing at first light — is unlike anything in the world.

**Hampi, Karnataka** — The ruins of the Vijayanagara empire, scattered across a landscape of enormous boulders and rice paddies. The empire was one of the greatest in Indian history before its destruction in 1565. Wandering between the temples and royal enclosures, with monkeys everywhere, takes at least two or three days.

**The Temples of Rajasthan** — The carved temples of Khajuraho (Madhya Pradesh) are famous for their erotic sculpture, but the overall quality of the stone carving is what is truly extraordinary. In Rajasthan itself, the temples at Ranakpur and Mount Abu are the finest examples of Jain temple architecture.

**The Himalayas** — The mountain scenery of [Himachal Pradesh](/asia/india/himachalpradesh), [Uttaranchal](/asia/india/uttaranchal), and [Jammu and Kashmir](/asia/india/jammuandkashmir) is among the most dramatic on earth. Manali, Dharamsala (home of the Dalai Lama's exile government), and Leh in Ladakh are all excellent bases for trekking and mountain exploration.

**Backwaters of Kerala** — A network of lagoons, canals, and lakes running behind the coastline in [Kerala](/asia/india/kerala). Spending a night on a houseboat drifting through the palm-fringed waterways is one of the more genuinely relaxing experiences India offers.

**Ajanta and Ellora Caves, Maharashtra** — Near [Aurangabad](/asia/india/maharashtra/aurangabad), the Ajanta caves contain extraordinary Buddhist frescoes dating from the 2nd century BCE to the 5th century CE. Ellora has Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain temples carved directly into the rock, including the massive Kailasa temple — the largest rock-cut structure in the world.
