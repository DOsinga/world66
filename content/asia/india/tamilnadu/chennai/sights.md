---
title: "Sights in Chennai"
type: section
---

Chennai's sights are spread across the city but several are concentrated in the older parts near the seafront.

**Fort St George**, built by the British East India Company in 1644, is the oldest surviving English fort in India. The fort houses the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, so access is restricted to parts of it, but the **Fort Museum** inside is open and has a good collection of colonial-era artefacts, paintings, and memorabilia from the early British period in India.

**Kapaleeswarar Temple** in Mylapore is the city's main Hindu temple, a Dravidian-style Shiva temple rebuilt in the 16th century. The gopuram is 37 metres tall and the temple tank is a centre of activity during festival seasons.

**Marina Beach** is one of the longest urban beaches in the world — 13 km from Fort St George to Besant Nagar. It's not a swimming beach (currents are dangerous) but an evening walk is a Chennai institution. The beach road has statues of Tamil leaders and is busy with snack sellers and families from late afternoon onwards.

**Government Museum** (Egmore) is one of the better museums in south India, with strong archaeological and natural history collections and a good gallery of Chola bronzes.
