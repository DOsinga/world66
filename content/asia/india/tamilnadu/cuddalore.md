Cuddalore is an industrial port town on the Bay of Bengal, about 20 km south of Pondicherry. It has a long history — the Old Town traded with Rome 2,000 years ago, and the town was among the first in southern India to fall under direct European control, passing through Dutch, Portuguese, French, and British hands before independence. The British built Fort St David here, though little of it survives.

The main reason to stop in Cuddalore is **Pichavaram**, a few kilometres north, where one of the world's largest mangrove forests spreads across a tidal estuary. Boats can be hired to navigate through the narrow waterways between the mangroves; the density of the forest and the channels running through it create an unusual and peaceful environment. It's busiest on weekends.

**Silver Beach** on the seafront gets crowded on weekends and holidays but is pleasant enough outside those times.

Cuddalore is also a convenient base for visiting Chidambaram (43 km north), site of the Nataraja Temple — one of the most important Shiva temples in India and the only one where the deity is worshipped as the cosmic dancer in his form as Akasha (ether).
