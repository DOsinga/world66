---
title: "Things to do"
type: section
---

The Nilgiri Hills offer good trekking, mostly under-visited outside the main Ooty-Coonoor area.

**Mukurti National Park**, west of Ooty, protects the high plateau of the Nilgiris — rolling grasslands and shola forests at 1,800-2,600 metres. The **Mukurti trek** gives good views of Mukurti Lake and a chance of seeing Nilgiri thar (a mountain goat unique to the Western Ghats). October, after the southwest monsoon, is the best month when the grass is green and the weather clear. Access requires a forest permit and overnight stays need advance arrangement.

**Dodda Betta** (2,637 m), the highest point in the Nilgiris, has a short walking trail to the summit with views over the hills and, on clear days, to Coimbatore on the plains below.

**Coonoor** (18 km south of Ooty, accessible by Nilgiri Railway) is the better base for plantation walks. Several tea estates around Coonoor allow visitors during the picking season; Highfield and Korakundah are well-known ones. The **Sim's Park** botanical garden at Coonoor is a quieter alternative to the Ooty version.
