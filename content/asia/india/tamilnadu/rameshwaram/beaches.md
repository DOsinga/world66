---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The beaches around Rameshwaram are shaped by the island’s geography: shallow, warm water on both the north (Bay of Bengal) and south (Gulf of Mannar) sides.

**Agnitheertham**, directly in front of Ramanathaswamy Temple, is where the ritual dawn bathing happens. It’s a wide sandy beach, but very much a place of pilgrimage rather than leisure. Arriving before sunrise you’ll find thousands of pilgrims already in the water.

The beach at **Dhanushkodi**, 20 km east at the island’s tip, is the one worth visiting in its own right — long, clean, almost deserted, with a surreal quality from the ruined ghost town directly behind it. The water is shallow and calm on the bay side.

The **Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park** covers the surrounding waters, protecting coral reefs and seagrass beds with marine life including dugongs, sea turtles, and dolphins. Glass-bottom boat tours operate from the main beach area. January to March is the best period when the sea is calmest and clearest.
