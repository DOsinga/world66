Sivakasi is an industrial town in the Virudhunagar district of southern Tamil Nadu, about 60 km north of Tirunelveli. It has an unlikely claim to fame: it produces roughly 90% of India's fireworks and matches, and is also one of the country's major offset printing centres.

The fireworks connection is visible everywhere — warehouses and small factories line the roads in and out of town, and the atmosphere during Diwali season has a charged, smoky quality. The industry is significant but the working conditions have historically been poor, with child labour a longstanding issue that government and NGO pressure has worked to address.

There's not much conventional tourism here, but the town offers an unusual industrial-India experience that's quite different from the temple circuit. Sivakasi is on the rail line between Madurai and Tirunelveli.
