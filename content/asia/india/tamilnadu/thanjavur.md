Thanjavur (Tanjore) was the capital of the Chola empire, and the Brihadeeswara Temple at its centre is the dynasty's greatest surviving monument. Built by Raja Raja Chola I around 1010 CE, the temple's vimana tower rises 66 metres above the surrounding streets — one of the tallest temple structures in India — and the whole complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The scale and precision of the stonework, the Chola bronzes in the inner corridors, and the massive Nandi bull carved from a single rock are worth a long visit.

Beyond the temple, Thanjavur has the Royal Palace complex, a sprawling compound of courtyards, halls, and a bell tower that houses the Saraswathi Mahal Library, one of the oldest libraries in Asia, with a collection of palm-leaf manuscripts. The Art Gallery next door holds an excellent collection of Chola bronzes, including some of the finest Nataraja figures in existence.

Thanjavur sits in the heart of the Cauvery delta, Tamil Nadu's rice bowl, surrounded by flat, green farmland. The town is compact and manageable on foot or by auto-rickshaw. It makes a natural stop on a route through Tamil Nadu's temple towns, between [Trichy](/asia/india/tamilnadu/trichy) and Kumbakonam.
