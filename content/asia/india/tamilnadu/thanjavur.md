Thanjavur (Tanjore) was the capital of the Chola empire at its height in the 10th to 12th centuries, and the city's legacy from that period is remarkable. The **Brihadeeswarar Temple** (also called the Big Temple or Peruvudaiyar Kovil) at its centre is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most significant achievements in Indian architecture. Built by Raja Raja Chola I around 1010 AD, its 66-metre vimana (tower) was the tallest structure in India when it was built and is still imposing today. The entire complex is made of granite, largely quarried from a site 60 km away, and the engineering precision of the stonework remains extraordinary. The frescoes inside the corridor date from the Chola and later Nayak periods.

Beyond the big temple, Thanjavur has the **Thanjavur Palace** (Maratha and Nayak period) with an art gallery that holds a significant collection of Chola bronzes, stone sculpture, and paintings. The Saraswathi Mahal Library next door contains thousands of rare manuscripts in Tamil, Sanskrit, Telugu, and Marathi.

The town is the centre of the Carnatic music tradition and the Bharatanatyam dance form — the classical arts that spread from the Chola court. Music and dance performances happen regularly; the town's position as a cultural centre in south India is genuine rather than manufactured for tourism.

Thanjavur is 55 km from Kumbakonam and about 350 km from Chennai by road. It makes a good base for exploring the Cauvery Delta temple circuit, which includes Kumbakonam, Darasuram, Gangaikonda Cholapuram, and Swamimalai.
