Auli is a high-altitude ski resort in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, sitting at around 2,500 metres and offering some of the most dramatic mountain scenery in India. The slopes are managed by the state-owned Garhwal Mandal Vikas Nigam (GMVN), which keeps prices low enough to make it one of the most affordable ski destinations in the world — a welcome contrast to the glossy resorts of Europe or North America.

The skiing season runs from January to March, when the slopes are reliably blanketed in snow. Beyond the runs themselves, the views are extraordinary: on a clear day you can see the peaks of Nanda Devi, Kamet, and Mana Parvat stretching across the horizon. The resort is connected by a gondola to the town of Joshimath below, which is also the base for the trek to the Valley of Flowers and the Char Dham pilgrimage circuit.

Getting here requires effort — Auli is connected by road to [Haridwar](/asia/india/uttaranchal/haridwar), a full day's bus journey away, with the last stretch a steep climb through mountain villages. That distance keeps the crowds thin, and in summer the meadows around Auli attract trekkers rather than skiers.
