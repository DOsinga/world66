Pauri is the administrative headquarters of Pauri Garhwal district, perched at around 1,650 metres on a ridge in the lower Himalayas. It is primarily a government town — the kind of place where the district collector's office and the court complex are the main landmarks — but the setting is spectacular. From the ridge, you get panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Chaukhamba, and these are among the finest mountain vistas accessible by road in Garhwal.

The Kandoliya Temple above the town is the best viewpoint, especially at dawn when the peaks catch the first light. The surrounding forests of oak and deodar are pleasant for walking, and the Khirsu hill station about 19 km away offers even quieter surroundings with apple orchards and more mountain views. Pauri is also a starting point for treks into the Garhwal hills, though facilities for trekkers are basic compared to more established bases like Joshimath or Uttarkashi.

The town is connected by road to Rishikesh (about five hours) and [Karanprayag](/asia/india/uttaranchal/karanprayag) on the Alaknanda. Buses run regularly, though the mountain roads are winding and slow. The climate is pleasant from March to June and September to November; winters bring occasional snowfall and biting cold. Pauri sees very few foreign travellers, which is part of its appeal — it offers an unvarnished glimpse of small-town life in the Garhwal hills.
