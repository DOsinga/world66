Nainital is a hill station in the Kumaon hills of [Uttarakhand](/asia/india/uttaranchal), built around a crescent-shaped lake at about 2,000 metres. It was developed as a summer retreat by the British in the 1840s and still has that hill-station character — colonial-era buildings, a promenade along the lake, and cool air even when the plains below are baking.

The town is a popular weekend escape from [Delhi](/asia/india/delhi), about six hours away by road. The lake itself is the centrepiece, ringed by forested hills and busy with rowing boats. Beyond Nainital, the Kumaon lake district includes Bhimtal, Naukuchiatal, and Sattal — quieter alternatives that make good day trips.
