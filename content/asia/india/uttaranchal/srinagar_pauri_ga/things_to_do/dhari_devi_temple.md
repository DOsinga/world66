The Dhari Devi Temple stands on a 20-metre high rock above the Alaknanda River, 19 km from Srinagar Garhwal on the highway to Badrinath. The temple houses only the upper portion of the goddess Dhari — the lower part is said to reside at Kalimath in Rudraprayag district. According to local tradition, the idol was washed downstream in a flood and a divine voice instructed that it be installed under the open sky on the spot where it was found; it remains there to this day. Thousands of pilgrims travelling to Badrinath stop here to pay their respects. Photography of the idol is strictly prohibited. A hanging bridge over the Alaknanda connects the temple rock to Dhari Village.
