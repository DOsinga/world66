The Agra Fort is one of India's most important Mughal monuments and deserves as much time as the Taj Mahal itself. Built primarily in red sandstone, the fort began as a military structure before becoming the lavish palace of emperors Akbar, Jahangir, and Shah Jahan.

Inside the walls you'll find a series of grand courtyards, the Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience), the delicate white marble Moti Masjid mosque, and the Musamman Burj — the octagonal tower where Shah Jahan spent his final years imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb, gazing across the river at the Taj Mahal he'd built for his wife.

The fort sits on the bank of the Yamuna River, about 2.5 km northwest of the Taj. Early morning is the best time to visit, before the tour groups arrive. Allow at least two hours.
