Extraordinary mausoleum built by Shah Jahan for his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in childbirth in 1631. Take your time to see the gardens, the minarets, the symmetry of the place and then walk the marble steps of the Taj itself. Look closely at the inlaid precious stones and take in the views of the Yamuna river and the Red Fort.

Do not hesitate to visit more than once: the Taj is a different building at dawn, in the blistering midday heat, on a rainy afternoon, and at sunset. Entry is 1,100 rupees for foreigners (as of 2024), and the queues at the west gate are shorter than the main south gate. Friday is closed to tourists.
