Aligarh is a city in western Uttar Pradesh, about 130 km southeast of Delhi and an easy side trip from [Agra](/asia/india/uttarpradesh/agra). It is best known as the home of **Aligarh Muslim University**, one of the most important educational institutions in South Asia, founded in 1875 by Sir Syed Ahmad Khan. The campus is worth visiting for its red-brick Moorish-style buildings and the beautiful Jama Masjid at its centre.

Beyond the university, Aligarh is an industrial city known for its lock and hardware manufacturing — it produces the majority of locks made in India. The Aligarh Fort, dating from the 18th century, is in a somewhat neglected state but retains some interest. The city is not a major tourist destination, but travellers passing between Delhi and Agra may find it a worthwhile detour.
