Ghaziabad is one of the fastest-growing cities in the Delhi National Capital Region, sprawling east of the Yamuna River in Uttar Pradesh. It is primarily an industrial and residential satellite city, and most of its nearly two million residents commute to Delhi or work in local manufacturing. The city is connected to Delhi by the Metro, which has made it an increasingly popular place to live for people priced out of the capital.

There is not much here in the way of traditional tourist attractions. The Swarna Jayanti Park, also known as Indirapuram Park, is the largest green space and offers some relief from the concrete. The ISKCON temple in Indirapuram draws devotees and curious visitors. The Shipra Mall and Opulent Mall in Indirapuram serve as the main commercial and entertainment hubs. Ghaziabad's street food, particularly around Turab Nagar and Navyug Market, is solid and cheap, with the usual North Indian roster of chaat, paranthas, and kebabs done well.

For travelers, Ghaziabad is a place you may pass through or stay in for budget reasons, but Delhi's attractions are a Metro ride away. The city is honest about what it is: a working Indian city, not a tourist destination, but one that functions as a practical base if you need affordable access to the NCR.
