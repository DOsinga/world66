Gorakhpur is an important railway junction in eastern Uttar Pradesh, close to the Nepal border. Most travellers pass through on their way to the border crossing at Sunauli, but the city has a few draws of its own if you are stopping.

The Gorakhnath Temple, dedicated to the medieval ascetic Guru Gorakshnath who popularised Hath Yoga, is the city's main landmark and an active pilgrimage site. The Gita Press, one of the world's largest publishers of Hindu religious texts, has been based here since 1923 and its bookshop is worth a browse. The Ramgarh Tal lake on the outskirts offers a pleasant escape from the city's noise.

Gorakhpur is the headquarters of the North Eastern Railway, and the city's enormous railway station — which held the Guinness record for the world's longest platform — reflects its importance as a transit hub. Connections run to Varanasi, Lucknow, Delhi and Kolkata.
