Haridwar is one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism, sitting at the point where the Ganges descends from the Himalayan foothills onto the plains of Uttar Pradesh. Its name means "Gateway to the Gods" — it is the last major town before the road divides toward Rishikesh and the high mountains of the Char Dham pilgrimage. Every evening, the Ganga Aarti ceremony at Har Ki Pauri ghat draws thousands of pilgrims: oil lamps are set afloat on the river in a ritual that has been performed here for centuries.

Haridwar is also the site of the Kumbh Mela, held once every twelve years — the largest religious gathering on earth. Even outside festival times, the town is perpetually busy with pilgrims passing through; the ghats, the temples, and the devotional bazaars are active at all hours.

The nearest airport is at Jolly Grant, Dehradun (41 km). Haridwar has its own railway station, well connected to Delhi and other major cities. Rishikesh is 24 km further up the river and is the more relaxed, yoga-focused complement to Haridwar's intense pilgrimage energy.
