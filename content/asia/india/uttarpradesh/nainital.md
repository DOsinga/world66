Nainital is a hill station in Uttarakhand perched at nearly 2,000 metres in the Kumaon Hills, built around a crescent-shaped lake that gives the town its name. The British established it as a summer retreat in the 1840s, and the colonial-era architecture — churches, boarding schools, a band stand on the Mall Road — still sets the tone.

The lake is the centre of everything. A walk along the Mall Road that lines its northern shore is the default evening activity, and brightly painted rowing boats bob on the water during the day. Naina Devi Temple sits at the northern end, and Snow View Point above the town offers panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks on clear days. A cable car runs up to the viewpoint, or you can walk.

Beyond the lake, the surrounding hills are covered in oak and pine forest. Tiffin Top (Dorothy's Seat) and Land's End are popular short hikes. The town gets crowded during the Indian summer holidays from April to June and again around Diwali, but during the shoulder months it is pleasantly quiet. Winters are cold and sometimes bring snow.
