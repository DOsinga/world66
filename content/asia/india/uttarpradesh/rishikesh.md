Rishikesh sits at the foothills of the Himalayas where the Ganges emerges from the mountains into the plains, about 25 kilometres north of Haridwar. It is one of the most important pilgrimage centres in India and has also become one of the world's most popular destinations for yoga and meditation — the Beatles stayed here in 1968 at the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's ashram, an act that cemented the town's global reputation.

The two famous suspension bridges — Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula — cross the Ganges between the eastern and western banks. The ghats along the river are busy with sadhus, pilgrims, tourists, and the evening Ganga Aarti ceremony, which is worth attending. The river here runs cold and fast from the mountains, and white-water rafting on the rapids between Rishikesh and Haridwar is popular.

Yoga and meditation centres are everywhere, ranging from serious ashrams with structured programmes to less rigorous operations aimed at tourists. If you are coming specifically for yoga, research in advance — quality varies enormously.
