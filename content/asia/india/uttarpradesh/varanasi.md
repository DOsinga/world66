Varanasi is one of the oldest continually inhabited cities in the world and the spiritual heart of Hinduism. Situated on the west bank of the Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, it is a city where death and life exist side by side with a directness that can be overwhelming — cremation ghats burn day and night, pilgrims bathe at dawn, and sadhus sit in meditation while children fly kites overhead.

The ghats are everything. More than 80 stone stairways descend to the river along a five-kilometre stretch, and the best way to see them is from a boat at sunrise. Dashashwamedh Ghat is the most famous, host to the nightly Ganga Aarti ceremony — an elaborate ritual of fire, chanting, and incense that draws enormous crowds. Manikarnika Ghat is the principal cremation ground, sacred and intense.

Away from the river, the old city is a maze of narrow lanes packed with temples, silk shops, and street food stalls. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple — the Golden Temple — is Varanasi's holiest Hindu shrine. Sarnath, just 10 km north, is where the Buddha gave his first sermon and is a peaceful counterpoint to the intensity of the city.

Varanasi is also known for its silk. Banarasi saris, woven with gold and silver thread, are among India's finest textiles, and the weavers' workshops in the old city are worth seeking out.
