---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Calcutta"
type: section
---

Calcutta has a distinct café and bar culture rooted in its history as a cosmopolitan colonial city. The old institutions have hung on longer here than in most Indian cities.

**Coffee houses:** The Indian Coffee House on College Street is the most famous café in the city — a first-floor room with ceiling fans and marble-topped tables where students, academics, and intellectuals have argued about politics and literature for decades. The food is secondary; come for the atmosphere. It opens early and fills up by mid-morning.

**The Park Hotel cluster:** The Park Hotel on Park Street houses four notable venues. Someplace Else is modelled on a British-style pub and is generally regarded as the best bar in the city — it has live music most evenings and a relaxed crowd. Tantra is the high-energy nightclub option, with pumping music and a good dance floor. Roxy is a cocktail bar. Aqua is the al-fresco option.

**Flury's:** More tearoom than bar, but a Calcutta institution since 1927. Park Street address, afternoon tea with pastries, and the kind of faded elegance that the city does well.

**College Street:** The lane behind Presidency University is lined with secondhand book stalls and small tea shops. The tea here is plain chai served in clay cups, and the conversation ranges from exams to existentialism. This is the intellectual heart of the city on a small budget.
