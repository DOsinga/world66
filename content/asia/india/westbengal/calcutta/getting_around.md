---
title: "Getting Around in Calcutta"
type: section
---

Calcutta is a large city and getting around takes planning. The metro is the fastest option for north-south travel; for everything else, you'll be navigating a combination of buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws.

**Metro:** The Kolkata Metro was the first in India, opened in 1984, and runs north-south from Dakshineswar to Kavi Subhas. It is reliable, air-conditioned, and far quicker than road transport for the main tourist corridor (Howrah to Esplanade to Park Street to Kalighat). The network has been expanded with new lines in recent years.

**Taxis:** Yellow Ambassador taxis are metered and plentiful in central Kolkata. App cabs (Ola, Uber) are widely available and often easier to navigate for visitors. For airport runs, app cabs are the most straightforward option.

**Buses:** The city bus network is extensive but crowded and confusing for newcomers. Minibuses serve many routes and are cheaper than taxis.

**Auto-rickshaws:** Common in the northern and southern suburbs where taxis are less prevalent. Fares are fixed on most routes.

**Trams:** Kolkata is the only Indian city still running trams. The network is limited and slow, but riding the tram is worth doing once for the experience. Routes run along major arteries including Esplanade and Shyambazar.

**Ferries:** Country boats and launches cross the Hooghly between various ghats (Chandpal, Bagbazar, Armenian) and Howrah. Fares are cheap — a few rupees — and the crossing is one of the great Kolkata experiences. Useful for reaching the Botanic Gardens at Shibpur.

**Rickshaws:** Hand-pulled rickshaws are still found in parts of north Kolkata. Hire one for short distances; the pullers are licensed.
