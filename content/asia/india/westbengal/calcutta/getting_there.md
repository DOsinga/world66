---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Calcutta is well connected to the rest of India and to Southeast Asia.

**By air:** Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport (CCU) is in Dum Dum, about 17 km north of the city centre. Domestic flights connect Kolkata with Delhi (about 2 hours), Mumbai (2.5 hours), Chennai, Bangalore, and other major cities on IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet, and Vistara. International connections include Singapore (Singapore Airlines, IndiGo), Bangkok (Thai Airways, IndiGo), Dhaka (Biman), Kuala Lumpur (AirAsia), and various Middle Eastern hubs. A pre-paid taxi counter at the arrivals hall offers fixed-price metered rides to the city; the app cabs also work from the terminal.

**By train:** Calcutta has two major railway stations. Howrah (on the west bank of the Hooghly) and Sealdah (on the east side) handle trains to different parts of India. The Rajdhani Express to Delhi departs from Howrah and takes 17 hours. The Coromandel Express to Chennai is around 27 hours. Long-distance trains need booking in advance; the Indian Railways online reservation system or the station reservation offices handle this.

**By bus:** Interstate buses arrive at Kolkata's Esplanade bus stand or the Tenghara stand. Services connect Kolkata with towns across West Bengal, and with Dhaka in Bangladesh via the Petrapole-Benapole border crossing.

**From Bangladesh:** The Maitree Express train runs between Kolkata (Chitpur station) and Dhaka on certain days of the week. The Petrapole road crossing is a common overland option, with buses on both sides of the border.
