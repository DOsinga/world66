The Indian Botanic Garden at Shibpur in Howrah is the largest in South Asia, spread over 270 acres on the western bank of the Hooghly. Its centrepiece is the Great Banyan Tree, estimated to be over 250 years old, with a canopy circumference of around 486 metres — one of the widest trees in the world. The main trunk was removed after it was struck by lightning, but the aerial roots have formed a self-supporting grove of their own. Reach the gardens by ferry across the Hooghly from Bagbazar or Chandpal Ghat, or by bus from Howrah station.
