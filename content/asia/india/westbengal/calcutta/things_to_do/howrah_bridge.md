The Howrah Bridge — formally the Rabindra Setu — is the defining symbol of Kolkata. This cantilever bridge across the Hooghly, opened in 1943, carries an estimated 100,000 vehicles and countless pedestrians every day. It was covered with camouflage netting during World War II to protect it from Japanese bombing raids. Walking across it early in the morning, when flower sellers are setting up at the Mullik Ghat flower market on the Kolkata side and the light is coming in from the east, is one of the city's better experiences. Don't miss the view back toward the bridge from the ghats below.
