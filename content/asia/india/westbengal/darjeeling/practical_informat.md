---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Getting there:** The nearest railhead is New Jalpaiguri (NJP) near Siliguri, connected to Kolkata by overnight trains (the Darjeeling Mail is the classic option, around 12 hours). From NJP, shared jeeps to Darjeeling take 3–4 hours on the hill road; the toy train takes 7–8 hours and needs to be booked separately. Bagdogra Airport near Siliguri receives flights from Kolkata, Delhi, and Bangalore.

**Accommodation:** Darjeeling has a wide range of options from basic guesthouses to colonial-era hotels like the Windamere and the Elgin. Book ahead for October–November and March–May when demand is high.

**Permits:** No inner-line permit is needed for Darjeeling town or the surrounding hills, but you will need one for restricted areas of Sikkim if your route takes you that way.

**Altitude:** At 2,050 metres, altitude is not a serious concern, but take it easy for the first day if you've come directly from sea-level Kolkata.

**Climate:** October–November and March–May are the best months: clear skies, pleasant temperatures (daytime 10–18°C in October). December–February is cold (near freezing at night). June–September is monsoon season — the mountains are hidden in cloud and leeches appear on the trails.

**Money:** There are ATMs in the main bazaar. Mobile connectivity is good; most carriers have signal in town, though it drops in remote valleys.
