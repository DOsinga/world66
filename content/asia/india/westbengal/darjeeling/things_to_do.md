---
title: "Things to Do in Darjeeling"
type: section
---

Darjeeling's pleasures are largely outdoor and unhurried. The light on the mountains, the smell of tea gardens, and the winding toy train are as much the point as any individual sight.

[Tiger Hill](/asia/india/westbengal/darjeeling/things_to_do/tiger_hill) is the classic sunrise excursion — a pre-dawn jeep ride to watch the light hit Kanchenjunga and, on clear days, glimpse Everest. [Happy Valley Tea Estate](/asia/india/westbengal/darjeeling/things_to_do/happy_valley_tea_estate) is the easiest place to see how Darjeeling tea is made and to buy directly from the source. The [Batasia Loop](/asia/india/westbengal/darjeeling/things_to_do/batasla_loop) on the toy train line is a short drive or ride from town — the spiral of track in a garden setting, with Kanchenjunga visible on clear days, is one of the most photographed spots in the hills.

[The town itself](/asia/india/westbengal/darjeeling/things_to_do/darjeeling_town) rewards slow walking: Chowrasta at dawn, the Padmaja Naidu Zoo for snow leopards and red pandas, the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute's Everest museum, and the Buddhist monasteries of Bhutia Busty and Ghoom.

A trip on the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, even for a single section, is worth doing for the engineering alone. The steam service between Darjeeling and Ghoom is the most manageable.
