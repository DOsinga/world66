---
title: "Things to Do in Durgapur"
type: section
---

Durgapur is primarily an industrial city — it was developed in the 1950s around the Durgapur Steel Plant and has a distinctly planned, functional character. Visitors rarely come specifically for tourism, but those passing through or staying for business will find a lively enough city.

[City Center](/asia/india/westbengal/durgapur/things_to_do/city_center) is the main commercial hub, with malls and restaurants where the city's young crowd gathers in the evenings. The older Benachity Bazaar nearby has better food and a more characterful atmosphere. Durgapur Club on Bengal Chemical Road retains some of its colonial-era club feel with a swimming pool and grounds, though access is member-focused.

The national highway dhabas on the Grand Trunk Road on the outskirts serve excellent mutton roti — honest roadside food at low prices and worth stopping for if you're passing through by car.
