---
title: "Things to Do in Kalimpong"
type: section
---

Kalimpong has three important monasteries worth visiting. The **Tharpa Choeling Gompa** (1922), on the Yellow Hat Gelukpa tradition, is the largest and easiest to find. The **Tongsa Gompa** is the oldest in Kalimpong, founded in 1692, with the current building over the ruins of the original. The **Zong Dog Palri Fo-Brang Gompa** on Durpin Hill, built in the mid-1970s and consecrated by the Dalai Lama, is known for its traditional Tibetan wall paintings and has excellent views across the valley.

Kalimpong is one of India's most important flower-producing areas — 80 percent of the country's gladioli come from here. Several nurseries around town are open to visitors and have remarkable collections of orchids, cacti, and Himalayan flora.

The **Sericulture Research Institute** and **Dr Graham's Home** (a school on the slopes of Deolo Hill, with a chapel) are interesting stops. The **Nature Interpretation Centre** on Rinkingpong Road has exhibits on the Himalayan ecosystem.

**Teesta Bazaar**, 16 km from town, is the whitewater rafting base on the Teesta River. The rafting season runs mid-November to mid-February. The **Samco Ropeway** across the Teesta on the Siliguri road is worth stopping for on the way in or out.

It is possible to cross to Phuntsholing in Bhutan from Kalimpong without a visa for day visits — check current rules before planning this.
