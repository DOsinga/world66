Pulau Weh — known locally as Sabang Island after its main town — is a small volcanic island about an hour's ferry ride from Banda Aceh. It sits at the northernmost tip of Sumatra and, by extension, of Indonesia, a fact announced by a concrete monument on its western cape.

The island has a well-deserved reputation among divers and snorkellers. The waters around it are rich with coral and marine life, and visibility is typically excellent. If you're certified, dive operators in Sabang town rent equipment and run trips at reasonable rates. For snorkellers, the reef just off the beach at Iboih on the western coast is accessible without a boat.

Life on Pulau Weh is more relaxed than on the Aceh mainland — alcohol is easier to come by and the atmosphere is notably less conservative. The island's small size means you can get around on a rented motorbike. Sabang town itself has some attractive colonial-era buildings from the Dutch period, when the island served as a coaling station.
