Tranquil and tropical Bali ,
Indonesia is
one of the most beautiful destinations in the world. This spiritual Hindu Island
is so small that everything is a day trip. Bali
is one of those fantastic destinations where luxury and low prices are
synonymous.

**Renting a Motorbike **

Renting a motorbike in Bali is the best way
to get around. It may be a little intimidating at first, but adventurous budget
travelers will save money and increase their independence by renting a
motorbike. It’s really cheap and so is gasoline. The Balinese ride all over the
island on their motorbikes and you can too. Riding in the cities of Legian and
Denpasar is a hectic rushm, but once you are out in the countryside, it’s a
relaxing cruise. Outside the city and in between gas stations gasoline is sold
in huts on the side of the road in liquor bottles. Don’t worry, most of the
time it’s clean.

**Ubud**

The town of Ubud in Bali, Indonesia
is the bohemian epicenter on the island. Park your motorbike for the day, hang
your helmets on your handlebars and walk around. You will stumble upon many
mystical Hindu temples and statues of Hindu deities around town. Ubud has
reasonably priced boutique stores and a much cheaper but also far more chaotic
buyers market. Bargain down to at least half the given price. There are many
great restaurants here and they are fancy and cheap. There are also used book
stores, internet cafes, vegetarian restaurants and a Monkey
Forest. Things to buy here are purses, silk scarves, comforters and journals.

**Legian – Kuta **

Legian and Kuta are next to each other outside of Denpasar in Bali, Indonesia. You won’t notice when you pass from one
to the other. Vendors are the most aggressive in this area. The beaches here
are huge and there are surfing lessons available. On the beach you can get a
massage or play a game of chess. The sunsets in Bali
are spectacular, both locals and tourists gather together in drum circles to
honor it every night. Don’t be surprised when everyone around you applauds when
the sun drops below the horizon. Things to buy here are the jewelry and drums
and just about everything.

**Blue Lagoon **

For spectacular snorkeling and scuba diving take a day trip to Blue Lagoon. You
can go in a group or hire your own private guide. You can also learn to skin
dive which is a happy medium between snorkeling and scuba. Blue Lagoon also has
many delicious restaurants. Any place that is offering a fresh catch of the day
is the best place to eat. If you forgot your beach sarongs don’t worry there
are plenty for sale in this town.
