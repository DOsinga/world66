Denpasar is the capital of **Bali** and its largest city, a busy, traffic-choked metropolis of around 750,000 people in the south of the island. For most travellers it is the place you transit through rather than stay in — the airport (now officially called Ngurah Rai International) is technically in neighbouring Tuban, and the backpacker district of **Kuta** and the upmarket resorts of **Seminyak**, **Canggu** and **Sanur** are a short drive away. Denpasar itself has none of the rice-paddy charm that people come to Bali for, and few visitors give it more than a couple of hours.

That said, the city is the heart of modern Balinese cultural life, and has the best museums and cultural institutions on the island. The **Bali Museum**, on the main square, has the most serious collection of Balinese art, religious objects and historical exhibits in the country and is housed in a building that is itself a catalogue of traditional Balinese architecture. Next door, the **Pura Jagatnatha** is Denpasar's main Hindu temple, dedicated to the supreme god Sang Hyang Widhi Wasa; it is quiet during the day and spectacular on full-moon festival nights. The annual **Bali Arts Festival**, held at the **Taman Werdhi Budaya** cultural centre every June and July, is a month-long programme of traditional music, dance, wayang kulit shadow puppetry, and theatre from every corner of the island — by far the best time to be in the city. The sprawling **Pasar Badung**, Bali's largest traditional market, is another good reason to stop, especially early in the morning when the flower sellers arrive. Beyond that, the city is most useful as a base for administrative errands and air-conditioned shopping at the modern malls around Jalan Sunset and Jalan Diponegoro.
