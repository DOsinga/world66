Sanur is one of [Bali](/asia/indonesia/bali)'s oldest resort areas, stretching along the island's southeastern coast about 20 minutes south of [Denpasar](/asia/indonesia/bali/denpasar). Unlike the frenetic energy of [Kuta](/asia/indonesia/bali/kuta) to the south, Sanur moves at a gentler pace. The beach is calm and sheltered by a reef, making it good for swimming and safe for children. At low tide the reef sits exposed and the water recedes some distance — the beach is best at high tide.

A paved beachfront promenade runs the full length of Sanur, lined with cafes, warungs, and surf shops. Accommodation is mostly mid-range, and the atmosphere is laid-back rather than party-driven. Sanur is also the departure point for boats to [Nusa Lembongan](/asia/indonesia/bali/nusalembongan) and [Nusa Penida](/asia/indonesia/bali/nusapenida), making it a useful base if you're planning day trips to those islands.

The town has a small but charming museum of Balinese art, the Museum Le Mayeur, housed in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur de Merprès, who settled here in the 1930s and married a celebrated Balinese dancer.
