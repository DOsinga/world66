Tulamben is one of the world's great shore dives. The wreck of the USAT Liberty, a US Army cargo ship torpedoed by a Japanese submarine in 1942 and dragged onto the beach after the 1963 eruption of Mount Agung, lies just 30 metres from shore in 3 to 29 metres of water. The wreck is encrusted with coral and swarming with hundreds of fish species, making it accessible to divers of almost any level.

The village itself is small and unpretentious, strung along the northeast coast road. Most visitors come purely for the diving and stay in one of the modest dive resorts clustered near the beach. Dawn dives are popular — the Liberty wreck is at its quietest and most photogenic before the day-trip boats arrive from Amed and Candidasa.

Beyond the Liberty, Tulamben has several other good dive sites including the Drop Off, a vertical wall starting at 3 metres and plunging to 40, and the Coral Garden, a pleasant reef for snorkellers and beginner divers.
