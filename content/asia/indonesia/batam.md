Batam is an industrial island in the Riau Archipelago, just 20 kilometres south of Singapore and reachable by ferry in about an hour. It was developed as a special economic zone in the 1990s and remains primarily a business and manufacturing hub.

For travellers, Batam functions mainly as a stopover — either as a cheap base for visiting Singapore or as a transit point into Indonesia's Riau Islands. It has some beach resorts on its northern and western coasts, and golf courses geared toward Singaporean weekend visitors. The nightlife is lively in parts and has a reputation as a red-light district, which is worth knowing before you arrive.

Most visitors to the Riau Islands are better served by heading straight to [Bintan](/asia/indonesia/bintan), which has better beaches and a more relaxed atmosphere.
