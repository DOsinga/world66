---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Batam is hot, humid and tropical year-round, with daytime temperatures hovering around 28–32°C. The driest months — and the best for beach trips and bridge drives — are roughly **May to September**. The wet season runs **November to March**, with heavier downpours in December and January; ferry crossings can get rough. Avoid the worst of the haze if you can — smoke from forest fires in Sumatra sometimes drifts over between July and October.

Worth timing your visit around: the **Nongsa Regatta** in January between Singaporean and Batam sailors at Nongsa Point Marina; the **Mid-Autumn (Mooncake) Festival** in September, which the local Chinese community celebrates with lanterns and pastries; and **Hari Raya Puasa** at the end of Ramadan, when the island's Muslim majority puts on family feasts and prayers.
