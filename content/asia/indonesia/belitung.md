Belitung (in English, Billiton) is an island in the Java Sea off the eastern coast of Sumatra, about 400 kilometres northeast of Jakarta. Long known for tin and pepper, it has more recently gained attention as a beach destination — its granite boulders, crystal-clear turquoise water, and relative lack of development make for a compelling combination.

The island's main town is **Tanjung Pandan**, where most accommodation and transport connections are based. The most photographed spot is **Tanjung Kelayang**, where improbably large granite formations rise from the sea and the water runs a vivid blue-green. Island-hopping trips to the surrounding islets — particularly **Lengkuas Island** with its Dutch lighthouse — are the main tourist activity.

Belitung came to wider attention as the setting of the novel and film *Laskar Pelangi* (*Rainbow Troops*), a beloved story of schoolchildren in a tin-mining village. The shooting locations have since become tourist attractions in themselves.

The island was under British control briefly after 1812 before being ceded to the Dutch under the Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1824.
