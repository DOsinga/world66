---
title: "Festivals in Tanjung Pinang"
type: section
---

Many countries throughout Indonesia have unique and entertaining festivals throughout the year and the **Tanjung Pinang Festivals **are
no exception. Tanjung Pinang is located on the Bintan Island, which is
one of the islands that makes up the Riau Island chain of Indonesia. In
addition, Tanjung Pinang is the capital of Bintan Island. The beautiful
city of Tanjung Pinang is quite renowned for stage performances of both
Malay dances and music. There are recurring music and dance festivals
throughout the year. But this is one of many festivals held in Tanjung
Pinang throughout the year. Here are some other important and special
events, which take place in this vibrant and traditional city.

**New Year's Day**

Celebrated on the first of January is the celebration of New Year’s
Day. This is considered to be a public holiday and most of the
celebration takes place on New Year’s Eve and lasts into the early
morning hours of January 1. There are many celebrations,
which take place in neighborhood streets and accented by colorful
firework displays. Special shows and entertainments are typically
planned for the New Year's as well. Many of the children like to ring in
the New Year with noise makers and paper horns.

**Chinese New Year**

Similar to the New Year’s Day festivity, Tanjung Pinang celebrates
the Chinese New Year on February 7. The Chinese New Year
is also a public holiday and is observed throughout Indonesia. This
occasion includes many celebrations and festivals that encompass
Chinese culture. There are traditional Chinese performances, inclusive
of dances and musical displays. Also many fairs and booths are set up
for people to purchase Chinese goods. Finally, the beautiful street parades
consisting of brightly colored dragons and lions wind their way down
the parade route.

**Independence Day**

The Indonesia National Independence Day is celebrated annually
throughout Indonesia on August 7. Many communities,
including Tanjung Pinang, celebrate their independence with proud
displays, depicting their heritage and culture. Traditional carnivals,
sporting events and cultural performances contribute to the festivities
of Indonesia. At the Merdeka Presidential Palace a flag-raising
ceremony takes places as well.

**Tanjung Pinang International Dragon Boat Festival**

Finally, on October 1, the world famous Dragon Boat Festival takes place in Tanjung Pinang every year. Since 1979 the
Pinang International Dragon Boat Festival has been held on the Tanjung
Pinang seaside, which faces the Penyengat Island. The vibrant and
gorgeous boats seen at this festival are just breathtaking. Tourists
also have the opportunity to not only view these attractive boats, but
see traditional cultural performances, participate in canoe races,
diving and swimming contests, as well.
