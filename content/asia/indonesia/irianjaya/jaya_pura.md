Jayapura is the capital of Papua province, a bustling city of around 250,000 people spread along the shores of Yos Sudarso Bay (formerly Humboldt Bay). The city has had many names: Hollandia under the Dutch from 1910, then Kotabaru and Sukarnopura after Indonesian integration, before finally becoming Jayapura.

The city serves as the main gateway for travel into the Papuan interior. Most people heading to the [Baliem Valley](/asia/indonesia/irianjaya/baliemvalley) pass through here. While Jayapura itself is not a destination you would linger in, there are a few things worth seeing.

Sentani Lake, near the airport about 30 km west of the city, is a beautiful freshwater lake known for its annual festival of traditional arts. The museum in Jayapura has a fascinating collection of Asmat carvings and other Papuan art. Scattered around the bay are remnants of World War II, when Jayapura served as General MacArthur's headquarters for the Pacific campaign.
