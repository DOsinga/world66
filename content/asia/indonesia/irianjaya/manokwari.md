Manokwari is the capital of West Papua province, set on a bay surrounded by forested hills at the northern edge of the Bird's Head Peninsula. It is a small, pleasant city that sees few tourists but rewards those who come.

The main attraction near town is Gunung Meja Nature Park, a forested hill with trails and views over the city and bay. Amban Beach is a short ride from the centre. For a longer trip, the twin Anggi Lakes sit high in the Arfak Mountains to the south, surrounded by cool cloud forest — a striking contrast to the tropical coast.

Manokwari has historical significance as the place where Christianity was first introduced to Papua, in 1855, via the island of Mansinam just offshore. The island is still a pilgrimage site and easy to reach by boat from the harbour.
