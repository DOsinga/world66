The old Dutch town hall on Fatahillah Square, built in 1710, is the centrepiece of the Kota district. Its cool, high-ceilinged rooms display furniture, maps, portraits and artefacts spanning Jakarta's history from its founding as Sunda Kelapa through the VOC era to Indonesian independence. The square outside — cobblestoned and flanked by heritage buildings — is one of the most atmospheric spaces in the city.
