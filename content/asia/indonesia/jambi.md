Jambi is the capital of Jambi Province on the eastern coast of [Sumatra](/asia/indonesia/sumatra), straddling the broad Batang Hari River as it winds toward the Strait of Malacca. It is not on most travellers' itineraries, but the city serves as the gateway to one of Sumatra's most important archaeological sites and some of the island's last remaining lowland rainforest.

The Muaro Jambi temple complex, about 25 kilometres east of the city, is the largest Buddhist archaeological site in Southeast Asia. The brick-and-stone ruins of an 8th- to 13th-century Malay kingdom sprawl across several square kilometres of riverside forest, largely unrestored and blissfully free of crowds. A visit here gives a sense of discovery that Borobudur no longer can.

The city itself is a mid-sized Sumatran trading town — busy, hot, and centred on the river. The pasar (market) along the waterfront is the liveliest spot, with boats loading and unloading goods alongside stalls selling fresh fish and local snacks. Jambi is also a jumping-off point for Kerinci Seblat National Park to the west, one of the best places to encounter Sumatran wildlife in the wild.
