---
title: 'Day Trips'
type: section
---

The volcanic landscape around Bandung is the real draw. Tangkuban Perahu, the most-visited destination, is 30km north — an active volcano whose main crater you can reach by road and peer into from the rim, with sulphur vents and a lunar landscape at 2,084 metres. From there it's worth continuing to the Ciater hot spring resort, where natural thermal pools sit in a tea plantation. The scenery on the road through the tea country is excellent.

To the south, Kawah Putih (White Crater) is a striking turquoise sulphuric lake set in a collapsed caldera near Ciwidey, 46km from Bandung. The same area has strawberry farms you can pick from, and the Situ Patengan lake nearby is a peaceful stop. These southern destinations are best combined into a full-day loop.
