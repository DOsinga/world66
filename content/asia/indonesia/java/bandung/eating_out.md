---
title: 'Eating Out'
type: section
---

Sundanese food is the reason many Indonesians make the trip to Bandung. The cuisine relies on fresh vegetables, light broths, and a restrained use of chilli compared with Padang or Javanese cooking. The classic format is *nasi timbel* — steamed rice wrapped in banana leaf, served with grilled chicken or fish, tofu, tempeh, *lalapan* (raw vegetables), and *sambal*. Eaten with your hands, ideally at a table overlooking a rice field or koi pond.

Street food is strong here too: *batagor* (fried fish dumplings with peanut sauce), *siomay* (steamed dumplings), and *mie kocok* (beef noodle soup with kikil tripe) are Bandung inventions that have spread across Indonesia but taste best at the source.
