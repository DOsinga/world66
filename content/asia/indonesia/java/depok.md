Depok is a sprawling satellite city wedged between [Jakarta](/asia/indonesia/java/jakarta) to the north and [Bogor](/asia/indonesia/java/bogor) to the south. It is primarily known as the home of the University of Indonesia (UI), whose large, leafy campus with its lake and surrounding parkland is the most pleasant green space in the area. Most travellers pass through on the KRL commuter train that runs between Jakarta and Bogor's famous Botanical Gardens.

There is little conventional tourist interest in Depok, but the UI campus is worth a stop if you are on the commuter line — the lake area is popular with joggers and picnickers, and the campus has a notably peaceful atmosphere compared to the surrounding urban density. The city also has a growing cafe and street food scene around the Margonda Road corridor, fuelled by the student population.

Depok is essentially part of the Jabodetabek metropolitan sprawl and functions as a bedroom community for Jakarta commuters. If you find yourself here, the commuter train connects you quickly to central Jakarta (about 45 minutes) or to Bogor (about 30 minutes), making it an easy transit point rather than a destination in its own right.
