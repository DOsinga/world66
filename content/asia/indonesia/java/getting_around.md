---
title: 'Getting Around'
type: section
---

Java has good transport connections from end to end, and you rarely need to fly unless you are short on time.

**Trains** are the most comfortable way to travel between major cities. Two main lines run between Jakarta and Surabaya -- one via [Yogyakarta](/asia/indonesia/java/yogyakarta) and [Solo](/asia/indonesia/java/solo), the other via [Semarang](/asia/indonesia/java/semarang). Executive-class carriages have air conditioning and assigned seats, and are worth the modest premium. In the east, trains connect to the ferry at Ketapang for [Bali](/asia/indonesia/bali); in the west, to the ferry at Merak for Sumatra.

**Buses** are the main form of inter-city transport and range from cramped public services to comfortable air-conditioned express coaches. Small **minibuses** cover shorter routes and backroads. Door-to-door minibus services are surprisingly convenient for mid-range trips.

**Hiring a car with a driver** is affordable and far less stressful than driving yourself. Java's roads are busy and the driving style takes some getting used to -- drive on the left and yield to anything bigger than you, more or less sums it up. **Motorbikes** are available for hire everywhere but demand real skill in traffic. **Bicycles** are fine for getting around town but not practical for long distances.
