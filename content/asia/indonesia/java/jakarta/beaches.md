---
title: Beaches
type: section
---

Jakarta is not a beach city -- the urban shoreline is industrial and the water is not for swimming. The escape is the **Thousand Islands** (Kepulauan Seribu), a chain of small coral islands strung out into the Java Sea north of the city.

Boats leave from Marina Ancol. **Pulau Bidadari** and **Pulau Onrust** are the closest, an hour out, and have colonial Dutch ruins as well as beaches. **Pulau Pramuka** has cheap homestays and is the easiest base for snorkelling. **Pulau Macan** and **Pulau Kotok** are further out, more pristine, and the diving and snorkelling improve sharply once you get past the first ring of islands. **Pulau Pantara** and **Pulau Putri**, two hours out by speedboat, are the top end -- resort accommodation and the clearest water of the chain.

Visibility and water quality drop noticeably during the rainy season (November to March). Weekends fill up with day-trippers from Jakarta; go midweek if you can.
