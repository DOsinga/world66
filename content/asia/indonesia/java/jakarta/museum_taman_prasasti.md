An open-air museum of tombstones and wooden funeral carriages. The site was originally an 18th-century Dutch cemetery for prominent residents. When it filled up, the graves were relocated but the tombstones were kept. Among the most notable are those of Lady Marianne Raffles (wife of Governor Raffles) and several Dutch Governors-General.
