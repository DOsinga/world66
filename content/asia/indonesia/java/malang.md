Malang is a highland city in East [Java](/asia/indonesia/java) that offers cooler temperatures, cleaner air, and a more relaxed pace than [Surabaya](/asia/indonesia/java/surabaya), about two hours to the north. Set at around 500 metres, the city has an attractive compact centre with Dutch colonial architecture — wide boulevards lined with art deco buildings, a leafy town square (Alun-Alun), and a sense of faded elegance that rewards a day or two of wandering.

The old town around Jalan Ijen is the most handsome quarter, with grand colonial villas shaded by mature trees. The Tugu Hotel, a beautifully restored heritage property, anchors this neighbourhood. Malang is also known for its street food, particularly bakso (meatball soup) and the local variant of rawon (black beef soup). The Pasar Besar traditional market is lively and worth a visit for spices, batik, and cheap eats.

Beyond the city, Malang serves as the gateway to some of East Java's most spectacular landscapes. Mount Semeru, Java's highest peak, is accessed via Tumpang to the east. The Bromo-Tengger-Semeru National Park can be reached from the southern side through Malang, avoiding the more crowded northern approach from Probolinggo. The Coban Rondo waterfall and the apple orchards around Batu, about 20 km northwest, make for an easy half-day trip. Malang's train station has direct connections to Surabaya, [Yogyakarta](/asia/indonesia/java/yogyakarta), and Jakarta.
