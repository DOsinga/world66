---

The name Dieng comes from the Sanskrit *Di-Hyang*, meaning 'Abode of the Gods.' At 2,093 meters above sea level, this plateau is an eerie landscape of sulphur craters, a colored lake (Telaga Warna), and scattered Hindu temple complexes named after heroes of Javanese mythology -- Bima, Arjuna, and Gatutkaca. It is the second most-visited tourist destination in Central Java after Borobudur. Bring warm clothes -- it gets cold up here.
