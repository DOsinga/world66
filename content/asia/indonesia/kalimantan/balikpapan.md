Balikpapan is East Kalimantan's main port and air hub, a prosperous oil city with better infrastructure than most of Borneo. Most travelers pass through on the way to [Samarinda](/asia/indonesia/kalimantan/samarinda) and the Mahakam River, but the city itself is a comfortable place to rest and restock before heading into the interior.

The city has a reputation for cleanliness unusual in Indonesian cities of its size, and the oil wealth has produced decent roads and facilities. The surrounding coast has some accessible diving and snorkeling. Balikpapan Bay, visible from the hilltops above the city, is a fine sight. With the new Indonesian capital Nusantara being built just north of the city, Balikpapan is growing fast — construction workers and government contractors have added to its already international character.
