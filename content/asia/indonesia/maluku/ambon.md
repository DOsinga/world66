Ambon is the largest city in Maluku and the capital of South Maluku province, situated on the island of the same name in eastern Indonesia. Its transport connections — a busy airport, regular ferries, and fast boat services — make it the natural gateway to the wider Maluku archipelago.

The city itself has few grand sights, but the island surrounding it has considerable appeal: colonial Dutch forts, clear diving waters, and quiet villages with strong spice-trade histories. Ambon was one of the most bitterly contested prizes of the 17th-century spice trade — the Dutch massacred English merchants here in 1623 and held the clove and nutmeg monopoly for over a century. **Fort Victoria** in the city centre survives from that era.

The coastline around Ambon Bay is fringed with good beaches and excellent snorkelling reefs. Hulaliu and Tulehu on the eastern peninsula are worth visiting on a half-day trip from the city.
