Tucked away in remote southeast Maluku, the Kei Islands are famous for having some of the finest unspoilt white-sand beaches in all of Indonesia. Pasir Panjang on Kei Kecil is the standout — a long arc of powder-white sand fringed by turquoise water, with only basic accommodation nearby.

The regional capital Tual, on the narrow strait between Kei Kecil and Kei Besar, has better facilities but few attractions of its own. It is well connected to Ambon by air and sea. Kei Besar, the larger and more mountainous island, is less visited and wilder, with traditional villages perched on hillsides above the coast. Getting around the islands requires patience and a willingness to negotiate with boat owners, but the rewards — empty beaches, warm water, and a genuine sense of remoteness — are considerable.
