South Nias (Nias Selatan) is the most visited part of Nias Island, off the west coast of Sumatra. The region draws surfers to the powerful waves at **Lagundri Bay** (Sorake Beach), widely considered one of the best right-handers in Indonesia. But South Nias has more to offer than surf.

The traditional Nias culture is among the most distinctive in Indonesia. The village of **Bawömataluo**, perched on a hilltop, is famous for its megalithic stone-jumping tradition (*fahombo*) — young men leap over tall stone columns as a rite of passage. The traditional houses here, with their massive wooden frames and soaring roofs, are architectural marvels. Other traditional villages at Hilisimaetano and Gomo are also worth visiting.

The capital of the regency is [Teluk Dalam](/asia/indonesia/nias_selatan/teluk_dalam), a small town that serves as the base for exploring the south. The island's main town, [Gunungsitoli](/asia/indonesia/nias_selatan/gunungsitoli) in the north, is the gateway for flights and ferries from the mainland.
