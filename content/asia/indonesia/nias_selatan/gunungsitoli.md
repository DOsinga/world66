Gunungsitoli is the capital of Nias Island, sitting on the northeast coast where ferries arrive from Sibolga on the Sumatran mainland. The town was badly damaged by the March 2005 earthquake and tsunami, but has been rebuilt with wider roads and sturdier buildings. It is not a destination in itself, but virtually every visitor to Nias passes through here.

The waterfront area along the harbour is the liveliest part of town, with a handful of warungs serving fresh fish and basic Indonesian fare. The Nias Heritage Museum (Museum Pusaka Nias) is worth a stop — it houses an impressive collection of traditional stone carvings, ancestor figures, and war shields from the island's megalithic culture. The building itself, a reconstruction of a traditional Nias chief's house, is as interesting as the exhibits inside.

Most travellers spend a night here before heading south to [Teluk Dalam](/asia/indonesia/nias_selatan/teluk_dalam) and the surf breaks at Lagundri Bay, a journey of around four hours by road. Binaka Airport, a few kilometres south of the centre, has flights to Medan. The main ferry terminal connects to Sibolga, with boats departing several times a week — the crossing takes around ten hours and can be rough in monsoon season.
