Teluk Dalam is the capital of South Nias and the starting point for everything that makes the island worth visiting: world-class surf, ancient megalithic villages, and empty beaches. The town itself is small, dusty, and functional — a single main street with shops, a market, and a few basic losmen — but you are not here for the town.

Lagundri Bay, about 12 kilometres southwest, draws surfers from around the world with a powerful right-hand reef break known as The Point. The wave is most consistent from June to October and can hold well overhead. Even if you do not surf, the bay is a beautiful crescent of sand backed by coconut palms, and the handful of guesthouses along the beach make for a peaceful base.

The hilltop village of Bawömataluo, about 14 kilometres from Teluk Dalam, is the most impressive of Nias's traditional villages. Reached by climbing a long stone staircase, the village is arranged around a central courtyard lined with massive wooden chiefs' houses raised on stilts. Stone-jumping — young men leaping over a two-metre stone column — was traditionally a rite of passage and is still performed for visitors. The village is a UNESCO tentative site and one of the most remarkable pieces of living architecture in Southeast Asia.

Getting here from [Gunungsitoli](/asia/indonesia/nias_selatan/gunungsitoli) takes about four hours by road. Shared transport runs through the day but dries up by late afternoon, so plan accordingly.
