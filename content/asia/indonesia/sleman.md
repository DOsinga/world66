Sleman is a regency on the northwest border of the Yogyakarta Special Region, stretching from the urban fringes of [Yogyakarta](/asia/indonesia/yogyakarta) up to the slopes of Mount Merapi. The volcano dominates the northern skyline and shapes daily life — its fertile ash soils support the surrounding rice paddies and villages.

Most visitors pass through Sleman on the way to or from Yogyakarta's Adisucipto International Airport, but the regency has its own appeal. Several traditional Javanese villages have opened their doors to visitors, offering a glimpse of rural life — rice planting, livestock tending, and craft-making — with homestay accommodation in traditional joglo houses. The foothills of Merapi make for good day walks, and the volcano museum offers a sobering look at the mountain's destructive power.
