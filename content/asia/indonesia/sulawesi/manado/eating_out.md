---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Manadonese food is famous throughout Indonesia for two things: the Minahasa eat almost anything, and it is very, very spicy. Sugar barely features; flavour comes from lemon and herbs. Being predominantly Christian, the Minahasa also marinate meats in alcohol and favour pork. More exotic options include bat, field rat and dog — the last a traditional centrepiece at weddings and Christmas feasts.

Classic dishes:

- *ayam rica-rica* — grilled chicken smothered in a fiery red chili-and-onion paste
- *ikan woku* — fish simmered with turmeric, lemongrass and basil
- *babi tore* — pork fried until crunchy with lots of chilli
- *perkedel milu* — corn fritters
- *tinutuan* (bubur manado) — a thick porridge of rice, pumpkin, noodles and vegetables; one of the few local dishes that isn't fiercely spicy

Dutch colonial history also left its mark:

- *breneibon* — a rich kidney bean soup
- *klappertart* — a coconut custard cake with a distinctly European feel
