Jambi is the capital of Jambi province on Sumatra's east coast, a sprawling river city on the Batang Hari. Most travellers skip it, but the city has an important archaeological site: Muaro Jambi, about 26 km downstream, is the largest ancient temple complex in Sumatra — a cluster of brick Buddhist and Hindu temples from the Srivijaya and Melayu kingdoms (7th–13th centuries) spread through the forest along the riverbank. It is far less visited than the comparable sites in Java.

The city itself is a typical Sumatran provincial capital — busy, hot, and not particularly charming, but with good street food and a lively riverfront market. Jambi sits on the Trans-Sumatra highway between Palembang and Padang, making it a natural overnight stop on the long cross-island journey.
