Medan is the gateway to northern Sumatra. Indonesia's fourth-largest city (population around 2.5 million) is loud, sprawling and chaotic, but most travellers pass through on their way to [Lake Toba](/asia/indonesia/sumatra/laketoba), the immense volcanic lake a few hours south. Give Medan a day before you move on -- it has more character than it gets credit for.

The old colonial quarter around Jalan Ahmad Yani rewards a morning's walk. The Maimun Palace, built in 1888 for the Sultan of Deli, mixes Malay, Mughal and European styles and is still partly occupied by the royal family. Nearby, the Grand Mosque (Masjid Raya Al Mashun) is an impressive black-domed landmark from the same era. Further out, the crumbling Dutch fort and the Chinese temples of Kampung Keling add to the city's layered history.

Medan is 875 km northwest of [Jakarta](/asia/indonesia/java/jakarta). Kualanamu International Airport has domestic connections across Indonesia and some international flights to Malaysia and Singapore. From the airport, it is about an hour's drive into the city centre.
