Samosir is a large island sitting within the caldera of Lake Toba, itself a supervolcanic lake in the highlands of North Sumatra. The combination — an island within a lake within a volcanic caldera — is geologically remarkable, and the scenery is extraordinary: steep green cliffs dropping to dark water, with the island's plateau rising in the centre.

The island is the heartland of the Batak Toba people, and their culture is strongly present here. Traditional Batak houses, with their distinctive curved rooflines and carved facades, stand in the villages along the shore. The town of Tomok has a royal cemetery and ancient stone carvings, and Simanindo on the northern tip has a well-preserved traditional house complex with performances of Batak music and dance.

Most travellers base themselves at Tuk Tuk, a small peninsula on the east coast with guesthouses, restaurants, and easy swimming from the lakeshore. The island is big enough to explore properly by scooter, which can be hired locally; the roads that circle the interior give views over the caldera and pass through Batak villages that see relatively few visitors.
