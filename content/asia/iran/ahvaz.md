Ahvaz is a large industrial city in Khuzestan province, in southwestern Iran near the Iraqi border. It sits on the Karun River and serves as the main hub for Iran's oil industry. Heavily bombed during the Iran-Iraq War, reconstruction has left the city functional rather than beautiful, and summer temperatures regularly exceed 45°C, making it one of the hottest cities on earth.

Most travelers pass through rather than linger, but the city makes a practical base for visiting some outstanding historic sites nearby: the Elamite ruins at Chogha Zanbil (a remarkably well-preserved ziggurat, a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the ancient mounds at Haft Tappeh, and the ruins of Susa (Shush), one of the oldest cities in the world.
