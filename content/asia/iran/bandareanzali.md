Bandar-e Anzali is a port city on the Caspian Sea coast of Iran's Gilan province, about 40 km northwest of Rasht. The town sits on a spit of land between the Caspian and the Mordab lagoon, a large freshwater wetland rich in birdlife and connected to the sea by a narrow channel.

The old bridge connecting Anzali to the Ghazian neighbourhood spans the lagoon channel and is a pleasant walk. The former Shah's palace overlooks the harbour. The real attraction, though, is the setting — the Caspian coast here is green and humid, a world away from Iran's desert interior, and the lagoon is beautiful, especially at dawn and dusk when fishermen are out on the water.

The Shanbeh Bazar (Saturday Bazaar) is a weekly open-air market in the town centre where you can buy fresh fish, produce, clothing, and household goods. It has been running for generations and remains the liveliest day of the week in Anzali.
