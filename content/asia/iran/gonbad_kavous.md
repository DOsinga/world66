Gonbad-e Kavus is a city in northeastern Iran's Golestan province, close to the Turkmenistan border. The majority of the population is Turkmen, giving the city a distinctly different character from most Iranian cities. Its remarkable tower — the Gonbad-e Qabus — is a UNESCO World Heritage Site: an extraordinary mud-brick minaret built in 1006 AD, 72 metres tall, and considered by many engineers to be the world's tallest brick tower. The tower tapers to a conical point and is visible for miles across the flat plain.
