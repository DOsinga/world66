*Asia > Iran*

Iran is one of the most rewarding and least understood destinations in the world. Few countries carry such a gap between reputation and reality. Travelers who make it here invariably return astonished — by the hospitality, by the depth of history, by cities that have been continuously inhabited for three or four thousand years. The welcome from ordinary Iranians tends to be extraordinary, often embarrassingly so.

The country sits on the Iranian Plateau, flanked by the Caspian Sea in the north and the Persian Gulf in the south. It is large — nearly four times the size of France — and varied, from the snowcapped Alborz mountains ringing Tehran to the vast central deserts, from the subtropical forests of Gilan to the parched Baluchestan borderlands. The highest peak, Mount Damavand (5,610 m), is a dormant volcano that dominates the Tehran skyline on clear days.

The arc from [Tehran](/asia/iran/tehran) south through [Kashan](/asia/iran/kashan) to [Isfahan](/asia/iran/isfahan), [Yazd](/asia/iran/yazd), and [Shiraz](/asia/iran/shiraz) is the classic route, and for good reason — it encompasses some of the finest Islamic architecture in the world alongside the ruins of the pre-Islamic Persian empire. Near Shiraz, [Persepolis](/asia/iran/persepolis) stands on a vast stone terrace, its columns still casting sharp shadows in the desert light after 2,500 years. Isfahan's Naqsh-e Jahan square, laid out in the seventeenth century under Shah Abbas, is a masterpiece of urban design that still functions as a gathering place for families in the evening. Yazd, built from the desert itself, is a city of wind towers and underground water channels that has been continuously inhabited longer than almost anywhere on earth.

Outside the classic route, there is plenty. [Tabriz](/asia/iran/tabriz) in the northwest has one of the greatest covered bazaars in the Islamic world, a UNESCO site in its own right. [Mashhad](/asia/iran/mashhad) in the northeast is one of Islam's holiest cities, centered on the shrine of Imam Reza, and draws millions of pilgrims a year. [Kerman](/asia/iran/kerman) is the gateway to the dramatic Lut Desert, a UNESCO natural site. The Caspian coast is green and humid in a way that surprises people who picture Iran as all desert.

The people are the other great reason to go. Iranians have a deep tradition of hospitality — ta'arof, the elaborate Persian etiquette of offering and declining, can bewilder newcomers, but genuine warmth underpins it. Invitations to homes are real. Conversations with strangers are common. Many Iranians, particularly in cities, speak English and are curious about the world.

## Destinations

- [Tehran](/asia/iran/tehran) — the busy, sprawling capital; museums, mountains, good food
- [Isfahan](/asia/iran/isfahan) — the architectural crown jewel; Naqsh-e Jahan Square, bridges, bazaar
- [Shiraz](/asia/iran/shiraz) — city of poets, roses, and the gateway to Persepolis
- [Yazd](/asia/iran/yazd) — ancient desert city; wind towers, Zoroastrian fire temple, mud-brick lanes
- [Kashan](/asia/iran/kashan) — historic houses, rose water, a quieter alternative to Isfahan
- [Mashhad](/asia/iran/mashhad) — holiest city in Iran; immense shrine complex
- [Tabriz](/asia/iran/tabriz) — great bazaar, gateway to northwest Iran
- [Kerman](/asia/iran/kerman) — gateway to the Lut Desert; fine mosques and bazaar
- [Persepolis](/asia/iran/persepolis) — the ancient Achaemenid capital; Iran's most dramatic ruin
- [Pasargadae](/asia/iran/pasargadae) — tomb of Cyrus the Great; older and quieter than Persepolis
- [Hamadan](/asia/iran/hamadan) — ancient Ecbatana; cave at Ali Sadr, tomb of Avicenna
- [Bandar-e Abbas](/asia/iran/bandar_e_abbas) — southern port, ferry point for Hormuz Island
- [Kish Island](/asia/iran/kishisland) — free-trade island in the Persian Gulf; beaches, diving
- [Hormuz Island](/asia/iran/hormuz_island) — small island with a ruined Portuguese castle
- [Ardabil](/asia/iran/ardabil) — UNESCO-listed Sheikh Safi shrine; Caspian gateway
- [Gonbad-e Kavus](/asia/iran/gonbad_kavous) — the world's tallest brick tower
