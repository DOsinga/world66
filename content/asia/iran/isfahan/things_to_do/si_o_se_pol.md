The "Bridge of 33 Arches" is the longest of Isfahan's historic bridges and one of the finest examples of Safavid bridge-building. Completed in 1602 under Shah Abbas I, it stretches 297 metres across the Zayandeh River on 33 spans. The bridge functions as both a dam and a thoroughfare: at river level, tea houses occupy the arched alcoves, and in the evenings Isfahanis gather here to stroll, sit, and sing — a practice that feels entirely natural in a structure designed to be lingered in rather than merely crossed.
