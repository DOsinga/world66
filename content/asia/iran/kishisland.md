Kish is Iran's answer to a beach resort — a small coral island in the Persian Gulf, about 20 kilometres off the southern coast, that operates as a free-trade zone and visa-free destination. That last detail matters: you can visit Kish without an Iranian visa, which makes it accessible to travelers who might otherwise find Iran's entry requirements daunting.

The island draws a mix of Iranian holidaymakers and Gulf-region visitors. The beaches are the main draw, and they're genuinely good — clear turquoise water, soft sand, warm temperatures most of the year. Women have designated stretches where they can swim without a hijab, which is unusual for Iran and much appreciated.

Beyond the beaches, Kish has a Greek shipwreck rusting picturesquely in the shallows, a historic underground aqueduct (the Karizak), and an old town with a Portuguese fort. The free-trade status means shopping malls and duty-free goods, which is what brings many Iranian visitors — but for foreign travelers the island's real appeal is simpler: a rare slice of Iranian coastline that's easy to reach and genuinely relaxing.

The best time to visit is October to April, when temperatures are pleasant. Summer is brutally hot and humid.
