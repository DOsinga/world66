---
title: "Things to do"
type: section
---

Kish punches above its weight for a small island. The Greek ship Kourosh, a rusted cargo vessel stranded in the shallows off the north coast since 1966, is one of the more atmospheric sights — photogenic at sunset and popular with snorkellers. The Karizak underground aqueduct is genuinely impressive: a 2,500-year-old water system that you can walk through, cool and dimly lit, a contrast to the glare outside. The old quarter of Harireh has the remains of a 1,000-year-old city with wind towers, a Portuguese fort, and a sense of history that the rest of the island's malls lack.

Scuba diving and snorkelling are worthwhile — the Persian Gulf has decent coral, and the wreck makes for an easy dive. Dolphin-watching boat trips run from the harbour. The beaches themselves, particularly on the north coast, are the island's main attraction and reason enough to visit.
