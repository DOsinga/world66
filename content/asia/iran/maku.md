Maku is a town in northwestern Iran, some 22 km from the Turkish border. There is not much to see in Maku itself, but it is a useful overnight stop if you are crossing to or from [Doğubayazıt](/asia/turkey/dogubeyazit) in Turkey.

The town is set in a spectacular mountain gorge, and the surrounding hills are good for hiking. Be careful, though — you are close to the border, and wandering across it by accident would be a serious problem.

About 10 km west of town is the small Urartian citadel of Sangar. In town you can visit the Maku Palace, a 19th-century Qajar-era building worth a look if you have time to spare.
