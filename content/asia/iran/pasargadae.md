Pasargadae was the first capital of the Achaemenid Empire, built by Cyrus the Great in the 6th century BCE after his victories over the Medes and Lydians. It sits on a high, windswept plain in Fars province, about 130 km north of Shiraz — a stark, open landscape that makes the scattered ruins feel ancient in a way that more crowded sites don't.

The site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and compact enough to walk in a few hours. The centrepiece is the Tomb of Cyrus, one of the oldest and best-preserved royal tombs in the world. Nearby are the remains of his audience hall, residential palace, and a fortified citadel. There's no city here — just ruins on a plain, which is part of the appeal.

Most visitors come as a day trip from Shiraz, often combined with Persepolis (about 70 km away). The site is open year-round; spring and autumn are the most pleasant times to visit.
