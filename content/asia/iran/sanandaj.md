Sanandaj — historically known as Senna — is the capital of Kurdistan Province in western Iran, set in a mountain valley at about 1,500 metres elevation. The city is the cultural heart of Iranian Kurdistan, and Kurdish language, music, and traditions are everywhere: in the bazaar, in the teahouses, and in the sound of the tanbur lute drifting from doorways in the old quarter.

The city has a scattering of historical sites — the Asef Vaziri mansion, the old bazaar, and several mosques — but the real appeal is the surrounding landscape. The Abidar Park on the edge of town offers views across the city and the mountains beyond. The road south to Marivan and Lake Zarivar passes through some of the most beautiful highland scenery in Iran. Sanandaj is also a good base for exploring the Kurdish villages of the Hawraman region, where stepped stone houses cling to steep valley walls.
