Saadi (c.1210–1291) spent decades travelling before returning to Shiraz, where he wrote the Gulistan and Bustan — works that shaped Persian prose and poetry for centuries. His mausoleum, in gardens slightly north of the city centre, has a quieter atmosphere than the Hafez tomb. Many Iranians briefly touch the tombstone as they pass.

Saadi reportedly asked for this inscription on his tomb: "From the tomb of Saadi, son of Shiraz — the perfume of love escapes — thou shalt smell it still one thousand years after his death."
