title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Tehran"
type: section
---

Tehran is a backpacker’s delight and a haven for budget tourists looking to
savor the spirit of the city. Here is a comprehensive guide
to some super budget travel ideas in Tehran.

**Transport**

For budget travelers the most economical way to travel within the city is to
take the well-connected local bus network that provides frequent links to almost any point in Tehran. Visitors need to study the routes and line
numbers in Persian beforehand, to ensure a hassle-free travel. The metro is a
sparkling new transport system that has become instantly popular in Tehran and is speedy, inexpensive and convenient for traveling within city limits.

**Budget Eating **

Visitors can enjoy a king’s meal in the city for almost nothing. Tehran has several eateries serving the piping hot local delicacy--fefafel--and a range of
other meat stews and kabobs. Khordad Avenue is one of the best
places to relish robust meals at street-side stalls. The several ghavekhunehs
dotted around the city that offer nice lamb stew. SFC, the Iranian version of KFC, serves hearty chicken burgers and
rolls. The Iranian Traditional Restaurant located on Keshavarz Boulevard serves
full-bodied fare at very inexpensive prices. There are also several other roadside
stalls that sell yummy meat sandwiches and delicately spiced minced meat kabobs
that are extremely appetizing and appeal to any budget.

**Budget Accommodations**

There is a bevy of inexpensive accommodation options huddled around Amir Kabir
Street. The area is far from polished or elegant but is decent enough to stay
put for a few days for budget travelers. Single rooms are priced at
approximately $8 and the area is accessible to the metro rail and local bus
network, making the region extremely well-connected. Hotel Kahzar Sea is a nice,
informal property that has a friendly staff and airy rooms. Rooms are priced at about 60 to 90 Rials. Mashhad Hostel, located on West
Amir Kabir Avenue, is a no-frills destination that has basic amenities like
an internet connection and hot water. Dorm beds are available for as little as 50
Rials. Firouzeh Hotel, on Dolat Abadi Alley, is a good functional property
serving delicious breakfast and snacks.

Tehran is a culturally and historically rich city that sports a rugged charm and is a paradise, offering budget travelers a variety of choices.
