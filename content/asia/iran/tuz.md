Tus (also spelled Tous) is an ancient city near [Mashhad](/asia/iran/mashhad) in northeastern Iran, easily visited as a day trip. The main reason to come is the tomb of Ferdowsi, author of the Shahnameh (Book of Kings) — the national epic of the Persian-speaking world, written around 1000 AD. The mausoleum, rebuilt in the 1930s in a style evoking ancient Persian architecture, sits in a garden and is a place of genuine reverence for Iranians.

Tus was once a major city — it was the birthplace of both Ferdowsi and the Sufi poet Ghazali — but was destroyed by the Mongols in the thirteenth century and never recovered. Little remains of the old city beyond some walls and a citadel mound, but the Ferdowsi tomb alone justifies the trip.
