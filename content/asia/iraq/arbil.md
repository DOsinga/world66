Erbil (also spelled Arbil or Irbil) is the capital of the Kurdistan Region of Iraq and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on earth. The great tell at its center — the Erbil Citadel — has been occupied for at least six thousand years, a fact recognized by its UNESCO World Heritage listing in 2014. From the street below, the citadel mound rises dramatically above the modern city like a ship above a harbor.

The citadel itself is mostly unoccupied and awaiting restoration, but you can walk the narrow lanes of the old quarter and look across the rooftops. The mudbrick facades are extraordinary, even in decay. The small Textile Museum inside the citadel is worth a visit.

Below the citadel, Erbil is a prosperous, lively Kurdish city. The bazaar district around the citadel base is a working market where you can find spices, cloth, tea, and the everyday goods of Kurdish commercial life. Shar Park, the central garden, is busy with families in the evenings. Restaurants serving grilled meats, dolma, and Kurdish rice dishes line the streets around the park.

Erbil functions as the main base for travelers visiting the Kurdistan Region. It has good hotels (including international chains), functioning ATMs that accept foreign cards, and a genuine tourist infrastructure. It is also considerably more relaxed in terms of dress and atmosphere than Arab Iraq.
