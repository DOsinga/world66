Tikrit is a city on the Tigris River in Saladin Governorate, roughly 160 km north of Baghdad. It was the birthplace of Saddam Hussein and is associated with the Sunni Arab community that dominated Iraqi politics under his rule.

The city was held by ISIS from 2014 until March 2015, when it was retaken by Iraqi forces and Shia militias in one of the first major Iraqi military victories of that conflict. It has been rebuilding since then.

There is little specifically to draw tourists to Tikrit, though the area was historically significant in the early Islamic period. The city lies on the route between Baghdad and Mosul, and the landscape along the Tigris in this stretch is attractive.
