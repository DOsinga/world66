Acre (Akko) is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, and its old city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site of remarkable density: Crusader vaults, Ottoman mosques, Bahá’í gardens, and a working Arab fishing harbour all coexist within the old walls. It is about 20 km north of [Haifa](/asia/israel/haifa) and 130 km from [Jerusalem](/asia/israel/jerusalem).

The old city is built on a promontory that juts into the sea. Beneath the Ottoman-era buildings lies an almost complete Crusader city — the underground halls, refectories, and passages of the Crusader fortress are astonishingly well preserved and can be visited. The Knights Hospitallers’ quarter is among the most evocative surviving Crusader structures anywhere. Above ground, the 18th-century **Al-Jazzar Mosque** with its green dome dominates the skyline. The adjacent Ottoman bathhouse houses the Municipal Museum with strong archaeological collections.

Napoleon besieged Acre in 1799 but failed to take it — the defenders held out for 61 days. The walls he attacked still stand. The old Khan el-Umdan caravanserai near the harbour and the atmospheric covered market are both worth exploring. The beaches between Acre and Haifa are good.
