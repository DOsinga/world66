---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Israel punches above its weight as a beach destination. The Mediterranean coast stretches roughly 200 km from the Lebanese border down to the Gaza border, with clean sandy beaches accessible from most coastal cities. The Red Sea coast at Eilat adds a very different experience — clear water, coral reefs, and desert mountains as a backdrop.

[Tel Aviv](/asia/israel/telaviv) has Israel's most famous urban beach scene. A string of named beaches runs along the promenade (the Tayelet) from the renovated Tel Aviv Port in the north down to Jaffa. Each has its own character: some have beach bars with chairs in the sand, one caters specifically to the LGBT community, another has a dog-friendly zone. Café Hafuch (a kind of cappuccino, literally "upside-down coffee") at a waterfront café is a classic Tel Aviv experience. The scene is at its liveliest on long summer evenings.

For quieter beaches, head north toward Haifa, [Acre](/asia/israel/acre), and Nahariya, or south past Ashkelon. The Carmel coast between Herzliya and Haifa has several beaches backed by ancient ruins. In the far north, the beaches near Rosh Hanikra and Achziv are beautiful and rarely crowded.

The unmistakable beach sport is matkot: two paddles and a small rubber ball, the object being to hit the ball back and forth indefinitely — no scoring, no losing. You'll see it on every Israeli beach. It sounds tranquil but produces considerable shouting.

The Dead Sea is in a category of its own. Technically a lake (and the lowest point on Earth), its extraordinarily salty water makes floating effortless — you cannot sink. The mud is rich in minerals and much sought after for skin treatments. The experience is strange and worth having at least once. Ein Bokek on the Israeli side has hotels and public access beaches; Ein Gedi further north is wilder and backed by a nature reserve with hiking trails and waterfalls.

Eilat on the Red Sea has good snorkeling and diving directly off the shore at the Coral Beach Nature Reserve. The water is warm, clear, and rich in marine life. The town itself is a package-holiday resort with the heat and prices to match, but the reef is genuinely good.
