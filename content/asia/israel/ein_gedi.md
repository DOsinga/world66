Ein Gedi is a lush oasis nature reserve clinging to the cliffs above the Dead Sea, about 90 km southeast of Jerusalem. The contrast with the surrounding desert is dramatic: springs feed waterfalls, ibex pick their way along rocky ledges, and hyraxes sun themselves on the boulders.

The most popular trail follows Wadi David to David's Waterfall — an easy hour's walk with a rewarding payoff. A second trail through Wadi Arugot reaches a larger falls in a narrower canyon. Both trails are well marked. Arrive early; the reserve gets crowded mid-morning and can be very hot by midday.

Ein Gedi Spa on the Dead Sea shore (a short drive below the nature reserve) offers a public beach, swimming in the Dead Sea, and mud treatment facilities. The beach here is considerably quieter than the resort strip at Ein Bokek further south.

The kibbutz at Ein Gedi has a botanical garden and a guesthouse. There is no bus service to the spa itself; the reserve and kibbutz are served by the Dead Sea bus route.
