Ein Hod is a small artists' village tucked into the Carmel mountains south of [Haifa](/asia/israel/haifa). Founded as an artists' colony in 1953 by the Dadaist Marcel Janco, the village is home to painters, sculptors, and craftspeople who live and work in stone houses along winding hillside lanes. Many have their studios open to visitors, and the whole village feels like an open-air gallery.

The atmosphere is relaxed and welcoming — artists are happy to show their work and talk about it. A walk through the village takes you past murals, sculptures, and unexpected installations tucked between the houses. There is a pleasant outdoor cafe with views over the hills.

About a mile further up the hill is Ein Hud, a small Arab village that until recently was unrecognised and off the map. It now has a well-regarded Middle Eastern restaurant run by a local family, worth the short detour.

Ein Hod has no public transport. You will need a car to get here.
