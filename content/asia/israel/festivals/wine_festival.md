The Jerusalem Wine Festival, held each summer at the Israel Museum, is the most prominent annual wine event in the country. Local wineries from the Galilee, Golan Heights, Judean Hills, and Negev highlands bring bottles for tasting on the museum's sculpture terrace, which is lit up for the occasion. Tickets cover an evening session with unlimited tastings; food stalls and live music run alongside.

Israel's wine industry has grown significantly since the 1980s, when Californian-trained winemaker Golan Heights Winery transformed expectations for what Israeli wine could be. The Judean Hills appellations around Jerusalem now produce internationally recognized reds, and boutique wineries dot the Galilee and Negev.

If you're travelling through wine country, the Judean Hills and Ella Valley have passable winery routes with tastings available on weekends — no festival required.
