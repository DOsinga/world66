The best-preserved of the Nabataean UNESCO cities in the Negev. Mamshit's streets, houses, churches, and a stable for the horse trade are more intact than those at Avdat — partly because the site was abandoned suddenly and never reoccupied. Two Byzantine churches retain mosaic floors. Smaller and less visited than Avdat, it rewards a careful walk through its compact ruins.
