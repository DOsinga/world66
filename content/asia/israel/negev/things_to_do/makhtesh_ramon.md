The world's largest erosion crater — 40 km long, 10 km wide, and up to 500 m deep. Unlike a meteor crater or volcanic caldera, a makhtesh is formed by millennia of erosion cutting through a dome of rock; the result is a landscape of multicolored sandstone walls, flat crater floor, and eerie quiet. The town of Mitzpe Ramon sits on the northern rim and serves as the base for hiking, jeep tours, and stargazing. Spring wildflowers across the crater floor make March and April the best time to visit.
