Netanya is Israel's largest coastal resort city, perched on sandstone cliffs above a long stretch of Mediterranean beaches. Sitting roughly halfway between [Tel Aviv](/asia/israel/telaviv) and [Haifa](/asia/israel/haifa), it draws both Israeli weekenders and international visitors who want sun and sand without Tel Aviv's intensity.

The city's centerpiece is the cliff-top promenade, a paved walkway that runs high above the sea with views stretching to the horizon. Elevators and stairways cut through the cliffs to connect the promenade to the beaches below. The main beach, Sironit, is wide and well-maintained, with lifeguards, cafes, and calm water that makes it popular with families. Further south, Poleg Beach attracts surfers.

Downtown Netanya is built around Independence Square (Kikar Ha'Atzmaut), a pedestrianized plaza ringed with restaurants and cafes. The atmosphere is relaxed and unhurried, a world away from Tel Aviv's constant buzz. On warm evenings the square fills with families, street performers, and couples lingering over coffee. The city has a significant French-speaking community, and you will hear as much French on the streets as Hebrew.

Beyond the beaches and the square, Netanya is a practical base for exploring the Sharon Plain. The ancient Roman city of [Caesarea](/asia/israel/caesarea) is a short drive north, and the nature reserves along the Alexander River offer easy walks through coastal wetlands. The city itself is not architecturally remarkable, but what it offers — affordable seaside living, good weather from April to November, and a genuinely laid-back pace — it does well.
