title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Tel Aviv"
type: section
---

**Holiday Apartments**

Tel Aviv has an array of clean,
well-maintained budget holiday apartments apart from the many hotels nestled around the streets of this bustling beach town. Holiday apartments turn
out to be cheaper for a longer stay for big groups and allow visitors to cook
their own meals to save costs. The Haavakook Apartment Hotel located near a
beach in the northern region of the city is a good bet for small albeit neat
apartments that boast of a functional pool and gymnasium. Priced at approximately $ 45 per night.
Another practical pick in the same range is The Home Apartments located at the
corner of Frishman Street. Visitors can pick from a spectrum of well-equipped
apartments at comfortable prices.**

Hostels and Hotels**

Tel Aviv is filled with cheap
hostels and modest resorts that redefine the experience of free-spirited and
adventure travel- the stuff that nomadic travelogues are made of. A majority of
the hostels in the city have dormitories where visitors can catch up with
fellow travelers and share stories. The two most popular hostels in Tel Aviv
frequented by budget travelers are The Gordon Inn located at Gordon Street and
the conventionally charming Old Jaffa Hostel which has a friendly, relaxed and
informal countenance and gives a stunning view of the sea beneath. Good option
for families. The dormitory bed is priced at around $ 8 and the single rooms
are priced $ 30 per night. Check out Hayarkon 48 Hostel that boasts of a good
location and serves the most delicious breakfast in town. The staff is friendly
and there is an in-house indoor games center and rooftop bar. Dorms cost $ 10
per night. A caution for visitors-There are many cheap sleep-ins crammed all
over the city however many of them are shady, unclean and dingy black holes
that offer to rent rooms by the hour. It is wise to check the accommodation in
advance before making any payment.

**Eating Out**

Sample full-bodied local fare at
Al Haderech, an unassuming eatery that deceptively hides its genius behind a
plain façade. This place serves lip-smacking falafel and flavorsome chicken
breast. The service is great and the fresh salads here are to die for. Eat
sumptuous Eastern European cuisine at Bebale, an old-Jewish eatery that whips
up great meatballs and gefilte fish. Expect generous portions at moderate
prices.

**Transportation**

There are several budget car
rental companies that are ideal for travelling. Some of the most
well-known are Budget Car Rentals and
Rhino Car Hire. Public buses and sheruts (mini-buses) are other cheap travel
options.

Tel Aviv is a budget traveler’s delight and promises great beach-side
fun and true adventure at a price that doesn’t hurt.
