title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Japan is not primarily a beach destination, and much of its coastline is rocky, industrial, or covered in the grey concrete wave-breakers that the Japanese government has installed with extraordinary enthusiasm along most of its shores. That said, there are good beaches — you just have to know where to look.

**Okinawa and the Ryukyu Islands**

The [Ryukyu Islands](/asia/japan/ryukyuislands) in the far southwest — including Okinawa, Miyako, and the Yaeyama group — have the best beaches in Japan: white sand, clear turquoise water, and year-round warmth. Ishigaki and Iriomote offer excellent diving among coral reefs. The water is swimmable from March through November. This is genuine tropical Japan, with a distinct culture and history distinct from the mainland.

**Shirahama (Wakayama)**

A few hours south of Osaka on the Kii Peninsula, Shirahama has wide white sand and unusually clear water for mainland Japan. The beach itself is good; the surrounding area has hot springs and is popular with Japanese day-trippers and weekenders.

**Shonan Coast (Kanagawa)**

The Shonan coast — including the beaches at Kamakura, Zushi, and Enoshima — is the beach for Tokyo residents. Within an hour of the capital, these beaches get crowded in summer but have a lively atmosphere, surf culture, and plenty of food and drink nearby. The water quality is reasonable. Kamakura itself, with its giant Buddha and network of temples and hiking trails, is the main draw for day-trippers.

**Shikinejima Island**

One of the Izu Islands south of Tokyo, reachable by overnight ferry or day-trip jet boat, Shikinejima is small, quiet, and has several attractive coves with clear water. The island feels genuinely remote compared to the mainland beaches.

**A word of warning**

Summer weekends on popular mainland beaches can be extremely crowded. Many beaches also prohibit swimming in specific zones and take the rules seriously. Jellyfish can be a hazard in mid-summer.
