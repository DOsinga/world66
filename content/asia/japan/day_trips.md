---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Japan's efficient rail network makes day trips genuinely easy from most major cities.

**From Tokyo**

**Nikko** (2 hours by Tobu or JR) holds the elaborate mausolea of the Tokugawa shoguns — gilded, carved, and dripping with colour in a forested mountain setting. The contrast with austere Zen aesthetics elsewhere in Japan is striking.

**Kamakura** (1 hour south) is a pleasant seaside town with a famous 13-metre bronze Buddha (Kotoku-in) and a dozen temples and shrines connected by hiking trails through the hills. Combine it with a walk along the coast to Enoshima for a full day.

**Hakone** (90 minutes) offers mountain scenery, hot springs, and views of Mount Fuji — best in winter and spring when the air is clear. The Hakone Open Air Museum has good sculpture in a well-designed outdoor setting.

**Yokohama** (30 minutes) is Japan's second city, with Chinatown, a well-preserved waterfront, and the Sankeien garden. Easy to combine with the rest of Tokyo on a single day.

**From Kyoto**

**Nara** (45 minutes) is essential: the deer, the Todai-ji temple with its giant Buddha, and the quieter lanes behind the park are all worth the trip. Arrive early.

**Osaka** (15 minutes by Shinkansen, 30 minutes by local train) is worth a full day — or several. The street food alone (takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu) justifies the trip.

**From Osaka/Kyoto**

**Hiroshima and Miyajima**: the Shinkansen reaches [Hiroshima](/asia/japan/honshu/hiroshima) in under an hour from Shin-Osaka. The Peace Memorial Park is profound; Miyajima island with its famous gate is an easy ferry ride away. Can be done as a long day trip but deserves an overnight.
