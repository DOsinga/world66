Amanohashidate — the "Bridge to Heaven" — is one of the Nihon Sankei, the Three Great Views of [Japan](/asia/japan). It is a 3.6 km pine-covered sand spit stretching across Miyazu Bay on the northern coast of Kyoto Prefecture.

The traditional way to appreciate the view is to turn your back to it, bend over, and look at the sandbar upside down between your legs — framed against the sky, it is said to resemble a bridge ascending to heaven. Chairlifts at each end of the bay take you to elevated viewpoints where you can do exactly this.

Walking or cycling along the spit itself is pleasant, with over 5,000 pine trees providing shade. There are several small shrines along the way. A boat service crosses the bay if you prefer not to walk back. The area around Amanohashidate station has ryokan and onsen, and the seafood — especially crab in winter — is excellent.
