Honshu is Japan's main island and home to most of what visitors come to see. It stretches over 1,300 kilometers from the subtropical south to the snowy north, and contains the country's biggest cities, most sacred temples, and finest landscapes.

The cultural heart lies in the Kansai region. [Kyoto](/asia/japan/honshu/kyoto) has over a thousand temples and shrines, and walking its backstreets you can still encounter geisha on their way to appointments. [Osaka](/asia/japan/honshu/osaka) is Japan's kitchen, a loud, friendly city obsessed with food, where takoyaki and okonomiyaki are practically religions. [Nara](/asia/japan/honshu/nara) is smaller and older, with sacred deer wandering among eighth-century temples. [Kobe](/asia/japan/honshu/kobe) adds port-city cosmopolitanism, sake breweries, and the famous beef.

Central Honshu is dominated by the Japanese Alps. [Takayama](/asia/japan/honshu/takayama) is a beautifully preserved mountain town with traditional inns and sake breweries. [Matsumoto](/asia/japan/honshu/matsumoto) has one of Japan's finest original castles and serves as a base for hiking in Kamikochi. [Nagoya](/asia/japan/honshu/nagoya) is the industrial powerhouse between Tokyo and Osaka, with Nagoya Castle and easy day trips to the castle town of [Inuyama](/asia/japan/honshu/inuyama).

The western end of Honshu holds [Hiroshima](/asia/japan/honshu/hiroshima), whose Peace Memorial Park is one of the most moving places on earth. Nearby Miyajima island, with its floating torii gate, is one of Japan's most photographed sights. [Himeji](/asia/japan/honshu/himeji) has the finest surviving castle in the country. Further north, [Sendai](/asia/japan/honshu/sendai) is the gateway to the scenic Tohoku region, while [Yokohama](/asia/japan/honshu/yokohama), just thirty minutes from Tokyo, is Japan's second city with a beautiful waterfront and vibrant Chinatown.

Honshu's train network is superb. The shinkansen bullet trains connect the major cities at speeds up to 320 km/h, and the Japan Rail Pass makes unlimited travel remarkably affordable.
