Kawasaki sits between [Tokyo](/asia/japan/honshu/tokyo) and [Yokohama](/asia/japan/honshu/yokohama) along the western shore of Tokyo Bay. With over 1.5 million residents it is one of Japan's largest cities, though most visitors pass through it without stopping. That is a mistake if you are curious about the less polished side of the Kanto region.

The main draw is the Kawasaki Daishi temple complex, one of the most visited Buddhist temples in Japan, particularly packed during New Year. The industrial waterfront district of Keihin has been partly reinvented, and the warehouse areas along the canal make for an interesting evening walk when the factories light up. Kawasaki is also home to the Fujiko F. Fujio Museum, dedicated to the creator of Doraemon, and the open-air Nihon Minka-en folk house museum, where two dozen traditional farmhouses from across Japan have been reassembled in a hillside park.
