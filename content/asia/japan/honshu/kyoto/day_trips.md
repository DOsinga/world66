Kyoto is in the middle of the Kansai region, which means nearly every important historic city in western Japan is within day-trip range by train. **Nara**, an hour away on the Kintetsu line, was the capital before Kyoto and is home to the colossal bronze Buddha at Tōdai-ji and a deer park where the tame deer bow to visitors for biscuits. **Osaka**, 15 minutes on the shinkansen, is Japan's great food city and a total change of pace from Kyoto. **Himeji**, an hour the other way, has the country's most spectacular surviving castle. Further afield, **Hiroshima** and **Miyajima** are reachable on the shinkansen (about 100 minutes) for a long but entirely feasible day. And the quiet castle town of **Hikone** and the pine-covered sandbar of **Amanohashidate** on the Sea of Japan coast are pleasant alternatives to the better-known sights.
