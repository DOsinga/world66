---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Kyoto is flat, the grid is easy, and the city runs on an excellent bus network that connects almost every major temple. **Buses** are the default way to get between sights — a one-day unlimited pass costs around ¥700, and individual fares are flat. Board at the rear and pay at the front when you get off. The **subway** has only two lines and covers fewer places of interest than the buses, but is useful for getting north-south along Karasuma or east-west on the Tozai line. A combined one-day bus and subway pass (around ¥1,100) is good value if you plan to use both. **Taxis** are plentiful and honest but expensive. Perhaps the best way to see Kyoto is by **bicycle** — the city is gently sloped, distances are walkable to cyclable, and rentals are cheap (around ¥1,000 a day). Keep to the bike lanes where marked; otherwise, cyclists share sidewalks with pedestrians.
