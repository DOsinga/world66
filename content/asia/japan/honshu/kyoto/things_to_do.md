---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Kyoto's problem is not finding things to do but narrowing them down. Seventeen sites in the city are inscribed together as a single UNESCO World Heritage entry, and you could spend a week on those alone without seeing everything. A sensible first trip would include **Kinkaku-ji** (the Golden Pavilion), **Ginkaku-ji**, **Ryōan-ji** with its famous dry stone garden, **Kiyomizu-dera** on its hillside platform, and the vermilion-gate tunnels of **Fushimi Inari**. The east side of the city — **Higashiyama** — is best explored on foot: the lantern-lit lanes of Ninen-zaka and Sannen-zaka leading up to Kiyomizu, and the bamboo grove of **Arashiyama** on the western outskirts. **Nijō Castle**, with its famous nightingale floors, is the main surviving Tokugawa-era residence. For a quieter day, the **Philosopher's Path** walks you between temples along a canal lined with cherry trees. After dark, the best thing to do is simply to wander the back streets of **Gion** and watch the lights come on in the wooden teahouses.
