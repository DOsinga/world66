Kinkaku-ji — the Golden Pavilion — is the most famous sight in Kyoto and the one that appears on every postcard and tourism poster: a three-storey wooden retirement villa sheathed in gold leaf, mirrored perfectly in the still pond in front of it. It was built in 1397 by Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu and converted to a Rinzai Zen temple after his death. Come as early as you can (the gates open at 9 am), or on a rainy day when the crowds thin and the gold reads more vividly against grey sky. The visit itself is short — a single one-way path around the pond — but the image will stay with you. Raku Bus 101 from Kyoto Station runs directly here.
