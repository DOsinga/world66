title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Matsumoto-jo or Matsumoto castle, is one of the best preserved castles
in Japan. Unlike many other remaining castles, it is an example of a
plains-castle (ie. it is not built on a hill). There is a moat
surrounding the castle, which was used to bury the dead. The castle is
mainly painted black, which earned it the nickname "crow castle".
