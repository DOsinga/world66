Made up of many smaller parks, Hisaya-odori Park, the swath of green running through Sakae is very popular as a place to stroll or sit and relax in the middle of the hectic city. There are monuments commemorating Nagoya's sister city relationships with Los Angeles, Mexico City, Sydney, and Nanjing in the "River Park" area. Different festive events take place here throughout the year. 


 The first multi-purpose television transmission tower built in Japan in 1954 is also located in the park. This 180-meter high tower in Central Park has long been a city landmark. From the observation deck on clear days there are spectacular views of Central Japan Alps to the north and Ise Bay to the south.
