Kansai (also called Kinki) is the cultural heartland of Japan — the region that gave the country most of its history, religion, and aesthetic tradition. [Kyoto](/asia/japan/honshu/kyoto) was the imperial capital for over a thousand years. [Osaka](/asia/japan/honshu/osaka) is the great merchant city, louder and more direct in manner than Tokyo, famous for its food. [Nara](/asia/japan/honshu/nara) was the capital before Kyoto. [Kobe](/asia/japan/honshu/kobe) has the country's most cosmopolitan port history.

Kansai International Airport (KIX), built on a artificial island in Osaka Bay, is one of the main international gateways to Japan. Transit through the airport is efficient — most visitors clear customs and are on a train within 45 minutes of landing. The Haruka express to Kyoto takes around 75 minutes; the Nankai Line to Osaka's Namba district takes 29 minutes.

The Kansai accent (Kansai-ben) is distinct from Tokyo Japanese and a source of regional pride. Osaka residents are known for a directness and a sense of humour that Tokyoites sometimes consider blunt.
