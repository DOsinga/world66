Miyako Island has some of the most beautiful beaches in Japan — white coral sand, turquoise water so clear you can see the bottom from the bridges that connect the island to its smaller neighbours. The island sits about 300 kilometres southwest of Okinawa's main island, far enough south that the water stays warm well into autumn.

The main beaches are Yonaha Maehama on the southwest coast (a long, wide arc of sand) and Yoshino Beach on the east (better for snorkelling over the shallow reef). Three long bridges connect Miyako to Irabu, Kurima, and Ikema islands, each worth a drive across for the views alone. Irabu's Toguchi-no-Hama beach is quieter and excellent for swimming.

Miyako is flat — unusual for a Japanese island — so cycling is a good way to get around. The island's culture retains traces of the old Ryukyu Kingdom, visible in the traditional stone walls and the annual Paantu festival, where mud-covered figures chase villagers through the streets to ward off evil spirits.
