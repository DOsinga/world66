Tokashiki is the largest of the Kerama Islands, a chain of small islands about 30 kilometres west of Naha in Okinawa. The island is known for its clear water, coral reefs, and white-sand beaches — Aharen Beach on the south coast and Tokashiku Beach on the west are the main draws.

In winter (January through March), humpback whales migrate to the warm waters around the Kerama Islands for breeding. Whale-watching boat trips run from the island and from Naha, and sightings are reliable during peak season. The rest of the year, the main activities are snorkelling, diving, and simply sitting on some of the best beaches in Okinawa.

The island is small and quiet, with only a few hundred permanent residents. There is a campsite behind Aharen Beach for budget travellers, and several minshuku (family-run guesthouses) in both Aharen and Tokashiki village.
