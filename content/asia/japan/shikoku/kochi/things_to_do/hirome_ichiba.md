Hirome Market is Kōchi's most enjoyable gathering place — a covered market hall just below the castle with dozens of food and drink stalls arranged around communal tables. It is less a tourist attraction than a place where locals actually eat and drink, and at lunchtime or early evening it fills up quickly with salarymen, families, and students. Order *katsuo no tataki* from one of the fish stalls, grab a beer or a glass of local sake, and find yourself a space at the long shared tables. The atmosphere is lively and unpretentious. Go hungry.
