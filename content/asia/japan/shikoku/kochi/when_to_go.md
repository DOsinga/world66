---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Kōchi's Pacific location gives it a warmer, wetter climate than the rest of Shikoku. The city receives more rainfall than almost anywhere else in Japan — the southern mountains wring moisture from the typhoon-season air — and summer humidity is high. The upside is mild winters and an early spring.

**Spring (March–April)** is the best time to visit. Cherry blossoms at Kōchi Castle typically peak in late March, slightly earlier than the rest of Japan. Temperatures are pleasant (15–22°C) and the days are clear before the rains arrive.

**May–June** brings the rainy season (*tsuyu*), with overcast skies and frequent downpours. Not miserable, but not ideal either.

**Summer (July–August)** is hot, humid, and typhoon-prone. The **Yosakoi Festival** runs on August 9–12, filling the city with dancing teams and live music. It is spectacular if chaotic, and accommodation books up weeks in advance.

**Autumn (October–November)** is the second-best season: clear skies, cooler temperatures, and good river conditions for rafting in the Niyodo and Shimanto valleys.

**Winter (December–February)** is mild and quiet. The coast rarely freezes; snow in the city is unusual, though the mountains see snow regularly.
