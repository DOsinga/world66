Built in 1835, Kanamaru-za is Japan's oldest surviving kabuki theatre and still functions as a working performance venue. The interior — trap doors, revolving stage, oil lamp holders — is a window into Edo-period theatre craft. It opens to visitors outside the performance season, and every April hosts a major kabuki festival that draws actors and audiences from across Japan.
