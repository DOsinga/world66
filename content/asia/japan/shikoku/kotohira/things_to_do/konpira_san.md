One of Japan's most famous Shinto shrines, Konpira-san sits atop Mount Zozu and has been a pilgrimage destination for centuries. The climb — 785 steps to the main hall, 583 more to the inner sanctuary — passes stone lanterns, vendor stalls, and increasingly dramatic views over the Sanuki plain. The main hall itself is modest in scale but moving in atmosphere, especially in the early morning before the tour groups arrive.
