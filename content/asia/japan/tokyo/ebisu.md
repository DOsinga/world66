Ebisu is one of Tokyo's most likeable neighbourhoods — upscale but not flashy, with excellent restaurants and a relaxed atmosphere that sets it apart from the sensory overload of nearby [Shibuya](/asia/japan/tokyo/shibuya). The district takes its name from the Shinto god of fishermen, but the more relevant origin story is the Yebisu beer brewery that operated here until 1988.

The brewery's legacy lives on at Yebisu Garden Place, a plaza at the east exit of the station that houses the Museum of Yebisu Beer, a Mitsukoshi department store, and the Tokyo Photographic Art Museum. The surrounding streets are packed with izakayas, ramen shops, and small French and Italian restaurants — Ebisu has one of the highest concentrations of good mid-range dining in the city.

The narrow lanes west of the station feel almost village-like by Tokyo standards. This is a neighbourhood for eating and drinking well, not for sightseeing, and that is precisely the point.
