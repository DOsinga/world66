---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Shibuya scramble crossing — five streams of pedestrians crossing at once when all the traffic lights turn red — is the defining experience. Do it once from street level, then take the second-floor Starbucks overlooking Hachiko Plaza for the aerial view.

Meiji Shrine, a short walk through Yoyogi Park to the north, is the other must-see: 70 hectares of forest in the middle of Tokyo, built around an emperor's shrine. Between the two, you have the neighbourhood of Harajuku, the vintage and craft lanes of Cat Street, and the design-led shops around Omotesando. Shibuya is where to eat late, drink, and shop; Meiji Jingu is where to slow back down.
