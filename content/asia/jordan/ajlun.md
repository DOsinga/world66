Ajlun is a small town in the highlands of northern Jordan, set among forested hills that are unusually green for the region. It sits about 75 km north of Amman and makes an easy half-day or full-day excursion from [Jerash](/asia/jordan/jerash), which lies just to the east.

The main draw is Ajlun Castle (Qalat er-Rabad), a 12th-century Ayyubid fortress built by one of Saladin's generals to defend against Crusader incursions. The castle commands a sweeping view over the Jordan Valley and three wadis — on a clear day you can see all the way to Palestine. It remains one of the best-preserved Islamic military fortresses in the Levant.

Beyond the castle, the Ajlun Nature Reserve protects a stretch of oak, carob, and pistachio woodland — habitat rare in the Middle East. The RSCN (Royal Society for the Conservation of Nature) maintains a network of walking trails here, ranging from short strolls to full-day hikes through villages and orchards. Nearby [Anjara](/asia/jordan/anjara) has an old church worth a look.
