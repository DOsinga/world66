The national museum, opened in 2014, and one of the best in the Middle East. The first gallery alone — holding the **Ain Ghazal statues**, nine thousand years old and among the oldest large-scale human figures ever made — is worth the visit. Other highlights include several of the **Dead Sea Scrolls** (on rotation), the Copper Scroll, Nabataean inscriptions, Umayyad frescoes and an excellent overview of Jordanian history from the Palaeolithic onwards. Skip it at your peril if you are heading out to Petra.
