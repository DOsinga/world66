---
title: "Amman Citadel"
type: poi
category: "Sight"
latitude: 31.9548
longitude: 35.9342
---

The highest hill in the old city, inhabited for more than seven thousand years and still topped with the ruins of successive civilisations stacked on top of one another: the six standing columns of the **Roman Temple of Hercules**, a 6th-century **Byzantine church**, and the extraordinary vaulted reception hall of the 8th-century **Umayyad Palace**, whose wooden dome was reconstructed by archaeologists in 1998. The small on-site museum holds the 9,500-year-old Ain Ghazal figures. Come at sunset for the view over the old city and the Roman Theatre below.
