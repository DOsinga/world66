The Ottoman-era fort at the south end of the main street, built on Mamluk foundations from the 13th century. The fort was used as a caravanserai by the Mamluk and Ottoman sultans and sustained significant damage from British naval bombardment in 1917 during the Arab Revolt (Lawrence of Arabia's campaign). After Jordanian independence the fort was restored and is now a museum with exhibits covering the city's history from the Nabataean and Roman periods through the Islamic era. The Hashemite coat of arms above the main gate is original. The large flagpole in the plaza outside flies one of the world's largest Jordanian flags.
