**Lawrence in Arabia** by Scott Anderson (2013) is the most readable account of T.E. Lawrence and the Arab Revolt. Anderson places Lawrence alongside three other Westerners operating in the region during World War One, giving the book genuine historical sweep. Essential reading before visiting [Wadi Rum](/asia/jordan/wadirum) or [Aqaba](/asia/jordan/aqaba).

**Seven Pillars of Wisdom** by T.E. Lawrence (1926) is Lawrence's own account of the Arab campaign — sprawling, beautiful, occasionally unreliable, and one of the great travel-war memoirs in English. The sections on Wadi Rum are unforgettable. If you find the full text daunting, the abridged *Revolt in the Desert* covers the essentials.

**Married to a Bedouin** by Marguerite van Geldermalsen (2006) is the memoir of a New Zealand woman who fell in love with a Bedouin man while passing through [Petra](/asia/jordan/petra) in 1978 and moved into his cave home. It is an intimate portrait of Petra and Bedouin life before mass tourism arrived.

**The Language of Baklava** by Diana Abu-Jaber (2005) is part memoir, part food writing, about an Arab-American family moving between Jordan and the United States. It captures something essential about Jordanian hospitality and the role food plays in social life.

**A Leap of Faith** by Queen Noor (2003) is the memoir of Lisa Halaby, an American who married King Hussein and became Queen of Jordan. It is candid about life inside the Hashemite court and provides real context for Jordan's modern political history.

**Appointment with Death** by Agatha Christie (1938) is a Hercule Poirot mystery set largely in Petra. It is not a serious guide, but it is a pleasure to read on-site and captures something of Petra's drama and strangeness.
