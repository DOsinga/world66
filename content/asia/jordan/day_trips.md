---
title: "Day Trips in Jordan"
type: section
---

Historically, Jordan has held a very important position,
being located near the merging landscape of 3 major
continents—Africa, Asia and Europe. Undertaking **Jordan Day Trips**
is an excellent way to understand how varying cultural influences have
become a part of Jordan’s remarkable history. Following are some
noteworthy suggestions for planning Jordan day trips:

**Visiting Mount Nebo**

Unlike some of the mountain peaks popular for their altitude or
scenic beauty, Mount Nebo is renowned for its religious significance. It
is visited by thousands of people from the surrounding countries of the
Middle East. Mount Nebo is actually a ridge located in the western part
of Jordan. While some people say that Mount Nebo is the site where
Moses first had a glimpse of the Promised Land, some believe that Moses
was buried beneath this ridge. The mount offers a unique view of the
Holy Land, making it a pilgrimage-like site for many people. The summit
of the ridge offers a combined view of the cities of Jerusalem and
Jericho. The view might be a bit hazy on some days. This is why visiting
Mount Nebo around the noon is recommended. Some parts of the River
Jordan valley can also be seen on clear days. Another site worth noting
here is the serpentine sculpture at the summit that was carved by an
Italian artist. This is why Mount Nebo is often called the Serpent
Mountain.
- **Directions: **10 km from Madaba. Mount Nebo is best traveled
from the small town of Madaba where local taxis can be hired for a
day trip.

**Visiting Qusayr Amra**

Qusayr Amra is among the most scared of Jordan’s castles. The castle
is remarkably well-preserved considering it was built in the 8th
century. The external facades might suggest that the castle has begun
to deteriorate but the insides, particularly the exotic frescoes on the
ceilings, don’t appear to have aged. The castle serves as an excellent resource
to understand the early, Persian architectural styles and some typical
Roman influences. The castle consists of a massive bath-house that is
bordered by unending halls on either side. The castle showcases varying
influences in its drawings, particularly those of the caliphs who ruled
over Jordan. Certain sections of the wall have a religious significance
since they depict the advent of Islam in this part of the world.
- **Directions: **Reaching** **Qusayr Amra is easy since it is a
national landmark. Local taxis can be hired for a trip to the castle. It
is located about 30 km from the city of Azraq, along Highway 40.

**Visiting the Roman Army and Chariot Show**

To showcase the cultural influence of the Roman Empire across Jordan,
an international show is frequently arranged in the city of Jerash. The
entire show is dedicated to the Roman tradition of holding gladiator
shows. This show has attained global fame and has become an integral
part of most Jordan travel packages. The gladiators are adorned with
heavy armors and the fight depictions are amazingly realistic.
- **Directions: **This show is held at the Jerash Hippodrome, in
Jerash. This facility was recently renovated and some people still call
it the Circus Gerasa. The Hippodrome can be easily accessed from the capital
city of Amman by taxi or a bus-ride.- **Hours: **There are 2 shows in a day, during the daytime only. Saturday
to Thursday: 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. There is only 1 show on Friday
at 10 a.m. No shows are held on Tuesdays.
