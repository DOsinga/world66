Ma'in is a valley of hot springs and waterfalls about 60 km southwest of Amman, tucked into the dramatic basalt escarpment above the Dead Sea. The springs are fed by rain that seeps through the highland plateau and emerges here at temperatures ranging from 40°C to nearly 60°C, heavy with minerals and sulphur. The result is a string of cascading falls that pour steaming water into pools below — startling and beautiful in the middle of desert rock.

The drive from [Madaba](/asia/jordan/madaba) takes about 30 minutes along a road that drops steeply into the Wadi Zarqa Ma'in gorge. On clear days the Dead Sea glitters in the distance, and if the haze is light you can make out the hills of the West Bank across the water. Most visitors hire a taxi from Madaba for the day.

The springs sit at the bottom of the gorge, where a waterfall tumbles directly into a pool. The water directly beneath the falls is scalding — you approach it from the sides, where the temperature settles to something you can tolerate. A soak here, with the sound of falling water and the mineral warmth working into your muscles, is one of the more restorative experiences in the region.

Note that the site is managed by the Evason Ma'In Hot Springs resort, which charges an entry fee for day visitors to access the main waterfall pool and changing facilities. Budget travelers sometimes skip the resort entrance and explore the lower canyon area independently, though facilities outside the resort are minimal.
