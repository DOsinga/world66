## By Air

Kazakhstan's two main international airports are in [Almaty](/asia/kazakhstan/almaty) (ALA) and [Astana](/asia/kazakhstan/astana) (NQZ). Both have direct connections to major European, Middle Eastern, and Asian hubs. Air Astana is the national carrier and has a solid safety record; it flies to London, Frankfurt, Amsterdam, Istanbul, Dubai, Beijing, and a number of other destinations.

From Europe, common routes are via Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), Frankfurt (Lufthansa), or through a Central Asian hub. From the UK, the most direct option is often Turkish Airlines through Istanbul. From North America, the practical choice is a connection through a European or Middle Eastern hub — there are no direct transatlantic flights.

Within the region, there are good connections to Bishkek, Tashkent, Baku, and other Central Asian cities.

Almaty airport (ALA) is 15 km northeast of the city center. Bus 101 connects it to the center. Use a metered or app-based taxi rather than the unofficial drivers who approach arrivals.

## By Rail

International train services connect Kazakhstan with Russia, Uzbekistan, and China. The Moscow–Almaty train runs several times a week and takes around 65 hours — a real Trans-Eurasian journey if you have the time. There are also services from Tashkent and Bishkek.

The Almaty–Urumqi (China) service is twice-weekly and crosses some dramatic steppe and mountain scenery.

## By Road

Road crossings into Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Russia are generally straightforward. The crossing between Shymkent and Tashkent is one of the busiest in Central Asia, with buses running throughout the day. Shared taxis (marshrutka) serve the main border towns on both sides.

The China crossing at Khorgos (near Almaty) is the major land link to Xinjiang and can be slow during busy periods.

## Visa

Citizens of over 80 countries — including the EU, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and Japan — can visit Kazakhstan visa-free for up to 30 days. Most other nationalities can apply online for an e-Visa. Check the current rules before you travel, as Kazakhstan has expanded its visa-free list in recent years.

## Customs

Declare all electronic devices, valuables, and significant amounts of cash on your customs form on entry. Keep the form — you hand it in at departure. Customs officers occasionally ask for informal payments; asking for a name and badge number is usually enough to end that conversation.
