Air:

Kazakhstan's national airline is *Kazakhstan Airlines* *(K4)*. Almaty is rapidly gaining importance as an international hub. It has air links with 63 cities in the CIS. *Kazakhstan Airlines* flies to Almaty from Frankfurt/M on Wednesday and Saturday and from Vienna (via Ataran on the Caspian) on Monday. From London the best connections are via Tashkent on *Uzbekistan Airways*; from Frankfurt/M on* Lufthansa *(twice a week); from Moscow on *British Airways *or *Aeroflot*; and from Istanbul on *Turkish Airways *or* Aeroflot.* There are no direct flights from the USA or Australia. Further connections are offered by *CAAC* and *Aeroflot* from Almaty to Urumchi in China from where there are connections to Beijing. Charter flights are also available to Cairo and Alexandria in Egypt; Dubai in the UAE; Islamabad and Karachi in Pakistan; Delhi and Mumbai in India; and Tehran in Iran. There are also flights to Novosibirsk and Irkutsk in the Russian Federation Bishkek in Kyrgyzstan Ashkhabad in

Turkmenistan and Samarkand in Uzbekistan. New direct routes from Almaty to Tehran Delhi and Tel Aviv are planned.

Approximate flight times:

From *London* to Almaty (via Moscow) is 8 hours not including stopover time in Moscow; from *Istanbul* is 5 hours 30 minutes from *Ulgi* (Mongolia) is 4 hours and from *Hanover* and *Frankfurt/M* is 7 hours.

International airports:

*Almaty (ALA) *is located 15km (9.5 miles) northeast of the city. Bus no. 101 connects the airport with the city centre (travel time – 20 minutes). Taxis are also available at the airport for transport into the city centre. Airport facilities include car rental duty-free shops restaurant and post office.

Sea

Freight is carried on the Caspian Sea to Russia and Iran.

Rail:

There are international rail connections with the Russian Federation Uzbekistan Turkmenistan and China. Services run from Almaty to Urumchi in China twice-weekly and daily to Moscow (the journey takes three days) and connect with the entire Russian Federation railway network. Trains leave Almaty Bishkek and Tashkent twice a day for Moscow. The Tashkent–Novosibirsk express passes through Almaty each day in both directions. The lines from Almaty in the north connect with the *Trans-Siberian Railway* running west to Chimkent and finally to Orenburg in the Russian Federation. A new rail line is being built to connect Kazakhstan to Iran and Turkey.

Road:

There are good road connections into Russia the other Central Asian states and China.

Bus

Buses leave Chimkent for Tashkent every 25 minutes 0700-1925. The journey is 160km (100 miles) and takes three hours. There are also buses from Chimkent to Bishkek.

  ** More Information **

 Air

 If you are going to Kazakhstan, consider using Lufthansa or Turkish Airlines if you are flying from the United States. Currently, no US aircrafts land in Kazakhstan. If you are flying from other parts of the world, you might consider your national carrier. Try to avoid Kazakhstani airlines as long as possible – you might experience quite a shock when you realize that someone is still using aircrafts that were manufactured back in the 70s. 

 Lufthansa (you can find more info at www.lufthansa.com) provides excellent service to Kazakhstan. Their crew is very sensitive culturally and will speak to you in German, English or even sometimes Russian (if you wish so). 

 You will also enjoy your flight with Turkish airlines if you speak Turkish. Unfortunately, their knowledge of foreign languages and their desire to know them is not strong enough to compel them to learn several useful foreign words. Therefore, a few Turkish words might be useful during this flight. Your Turkish vocabulary might also help you to find your gate in Istanbul airport.

 Customs

 When you get to Kazakhstan, please read the signs carefully. Please don’t forget to fill out your declaration (your carrier will usually distribute blank copies). Please read the declaration carefully and declare everything listed (jewelry, computers, cellular phones) except books and videotapes (according to an article in Almaty Herald). 

 There were cases when the customs officers asked for a bribe. In that case, try to avoid panicking. Just ask them their names and tell them that you will call your embassy. They say that is usually enough.

 In the city

 Now, that you are out of the customs, try to avoid taking local taxi from an international airport. It is the best choice to arrange that someone meet you at the airport, should it your friends or a company that invited you.
