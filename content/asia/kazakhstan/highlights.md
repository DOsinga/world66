## Sharyn Canyon

About 200 km east of Almaty, Sharyn Canyon stretches 90 km through the steppe, with walls dropping up to 300 meters. The most dramatic section is the Valley of Castles, where erosion has carved the red sandstone into towers, battlements, and spires. It looks and feels like a scaled-down Grand Canyon — which means it's still extraordinary. Go early in the morning before it gets hot; the colors are better in the low light. You need your own transport or a tour from Almaty.

## Almaty's Mountains

The Tien Shan rises almost immediately behind [Almaty](/asia/kazakhstan/almaty). Within an hour of the city center you can be at Medeu, a high-altitude speed skating rink, and from there a cable car takes you to the Shymbulak ski resort at 2,200 meters. In summer, these same slopes are hiking territory, with routes ranging from easy valley walks to serious multi-day mountain treks. The combination of a cosmopolitan city and real wilderness at the doorstep is unusual anywhere in the world.

## Astana's Architecture

[Astana](/asia/kazakhstan/astana) is an acquired taste, but it is genuinely unlike anywhere else. Designed in large part by Norman Foster and a roster of international architects, the city is an exercise in petrodollar ambition — a glass-and-steel capital dropped onto the open steppe. The Bayterek Tower, the Palace of Peace and Reconciliation, the Khan Shatyr entertainment center (a giant translucent tent), and the EXPO 2017 grounds are all worth seeing for the sheer strangeness of the project.

## Baikonur Cosmodrome

The world's first and largest space launch facility, deep in the steppe south of Kyzylorda. Sputnik 1 launched from here in 1957. Yuri Gagarin lifted off from here in 1961. Rocket launches still happen regularly and can be watched by visitors on organized tours. Access requires advance booking through a tour operator and approval from the Russian space agency (which jointly manages the site). An unforgettable experience for anyone with the slightest interest in space history.

## Aksu-Zhabagly Nature Reserve

The oldest nature reserve in Central Asia, at the foot of the western Tien Shan. The reserve protects snow leopards, ibex, and more than 250 bird species. The community-based tourism network in nearby Zhabagly village offers homestays and guided treks into the reserve — this is one of the most well-organized ecotourism setups in Kazakhstan. The tulip bloom in late April is spectacular.

## Turkestan

An ancient city on the Silk Road, [Turkestan](/asia/kazakhstan/turkestan) was an important spiritual center for the Turkic world for centuries. The massive Yasawi Mausoleum — commissioned by Timur (Tamerlane) in the 14th century, never quite completed — is the most significant historical monument in Kazakhstan. The turquoise dome dominates the flat landscape for miles around.

## Semipalatinsk Nuclear Test Site

The Soviet Union detonated 456 nuclear devices at Semipalatinsk between 1949 and 1989 — above ground, underground, and in the atmosphere. The human cost to the surrounding population was severe, with elevated cancer rates and genetic effects that persist today. The Polygon, as the test site is known, is now partially accessible. It's a sobering visit: the scorched earth, the deformed test structures, and the memorials to the affected communities make for one of the most haunting sites in Central Asia. Tours depart from [Semey (Semipalatinsk)](/asia/kazakhstan/semipalatinsk).
