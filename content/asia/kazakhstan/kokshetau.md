Kokshetau is the administrative capital of Akmola Region in northern [Kazakhstan](/asia/kazakhstan), a quiet provincial city of about 150,000 people on the edge of the Kazakh steppe. The city itself is unremarkable, but its setting is the draw: the Burabay (Borovoe) lake and mountain district lies about 70 kilometres to the south and is one of Kazakhstan's most popular domestic holiday destinations.

The Burabay area — sometimes called "the Pearl of Kazakhstan" — is a cluster of lakes surrounded by forested granite hills, a startling contrast to the flat steppe that stretches in every direction. Kazakhs come here to swim, hike, and breathe pine-scented air. The area has sanatoriums and resort hotels dating from the Soviet era alongside newer developments. From Kokshetau, marshrutkas (minibuses) run to Burabay regularly.

The city centre has a handful of Soviet-era parks and a regional museum. Kokshetau is most easily reached by train from [Astana](/asia/kazakhstan/astana), about four hours to the south.
