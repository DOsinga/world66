Turkestan is the spiritual heart of Kazakhstan and one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Central Asia. The city's centrepiece is the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, a massive, unfinished 14th-century structure commissioned by Timur himself. It is Kazakhstan's first UNESCO World Heritage Site and draws pilgrims from across the Turkic world — three visits here are said to equal one hajj to Mecca.

Beyond the mausoleum, the old town has been extensively rebuilt in recent years with a new amphitheatre, caravanserai complex, and museums. The results are mixed — impressive but sometimes theme-park-like — but the mausoleum itself remains genuinely awe-inspiring.

Turkestan sits in the dry steppe of southern Kazakhstan, about 3.5 hours' drive from [Kyzylorda](/asia/kazakhstan/kyzylorda) and reachable by train from Almaty or Shymkent.
