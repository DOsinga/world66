Ust-Kamenogorsk is also known as Oskemen in Russian, and it offers many **Ust-Kamenogorsk sights**. It is the capital city of East Kazakhstan province. Founded way back in the 1790&rsquo;s as a Russian fort, the city lies at the meeting point of the Irtysh and Ulba rivers. The city is served by the Oskemen Airport, also known as the Ust-Kamenogorsk Airport. Given its ancient monuments and sights, several tourists travel to Ust-Kamenogorsk every year. Given below are some of the Ust-Kamenogorsk Sights.

**Ethnographical Museum**

The Ethnographical Museum had once housed the Town&rsquo;s college. This museum was created in 1968 as a school museum, and then was awarded the status of the regional Ethnographic museum. The museum itself is housed in a historical building which depicts the Kazakhstan architecture. There are several landscaped zones for tourists to relax and a zoological par and children&rsquo;s play area located near the museum on the banks of Irtysh River. In the activity area, you will find all the details on the history of Kazakhstan, the cultural heritage and exhibits showing the sights through centuries in Russia. It would be best if you were a part of the excursion group that will take you through the museum and explain the art and details on display. You can also take a moment and enjoy the various ethnographic performances inside and outside the museums, and take part in the quizzes and competitions. All in all, this is a must visit sight in Ust-Kamenogorsk if you want to know more abut the Kazakhstan culture.

**Maxim Gorky Street**

This is a must-visit street in Ust-Kamenogorsk and is named after a famous writer, Maxim Gorky. The street was earlier called the Fire bystreet, taking its name from the fire tower at the in the city square which rises above the city. This street has several shops where you can indulge in some shopping, and also has some fine residences of the rich in Ust-Kamenogorsk. Walk along the street while admiring the fine architecture. These buildings were homes to some of the richest people in the city, which after the revolution became the property of the government. Most of these houses today are art centers or museums.

**Monuments in Ust-Kamenogorsk**

One thing that you will notice in Ust-Kamenogorsk is the monuments. The city is full of these monuments. This is another popular sight in Ust-Kamenogorsk for tourists. Some of the famous monuments, which were placed in the honor of famous writers, politicians and other notable personalities, are monuments to Abai, Rabindranatu Tagoru, A.S. Pushkin, S. Amanzholov and Y.V. Ushanov.
