Ahmadi is Kuwait's oil town, built by the Kuwait Oil Company in the 1940s and named after the ruling Emir at the time. It sits about 40 km south of [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity) and feels like a different world -- tree-lined avenues, small houses with gardens, and a quiet, almost suburban atmosphere that is rare in Kuwait.

The town was designed by British planners and still has a distinctly un-Kuwaiti feel, with its orderly layout and green spaces. The **Oil Display Centre** offers a surprisingly interesting look at Kuwait's petroleum industry and its history. The surrounding area is home to much of Kuwait's oil infrastructure, and driving south from Ahmadi you pass through the Burgan oil field, one of the largest in the world.
