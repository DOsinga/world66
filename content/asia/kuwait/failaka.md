Failaka is an island about 20 km off the coast of [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity), and it is the most historically significant site in the country. The island has been inhabited since the Bronze Age, and archaeological excavations have uncovered Greek temples from the time of Alexander the Great, when the island was known as Ikaros.

Before the 1990 Iraqi invasion, Failaka was home to a small Kuwaiti community of fishermen and farmers. The Iraqi forces occupied the island, deported the residents, and used it as a military base. The abandoned village remains largely as it was left, a haunting collection of empty houses and rusted military equipment that has become an unofficial memorial to the occupation.

Today the island is being slowly developed for tourism. Ferries run from the mainland (check current schedules with the Kuwait Public Transport Company from Ras Salmiya), and you can explore the archaeological sites, walk through the abandoned village, and enjoy quiet beaches that feel a world away from the city. It makes for an excellent day trip, particularly in the cooler months.
