---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**Kuwait Towers** -- The country's most iconic landmark, three blue-and-green striped towers on a promontory in [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity). The main tower has a revolving observation deck and restaurant with panoramic views of the city and the Gulf. Come at sunset for the best light.

**Souk Al-Mubarakiya** -- The oldest market in Kuwait, right in the heart of the city. Spice stalls, gold shops, traditional clothing, and some of the best street food in the country. This is where you eat machboos from a plastic plate while sitting on a bench, surrounded by the smell of cardamom and grilled meat.

**The Grand Mosque** -- The largest mosque in Kuwait and one of the largest in the world. Free guided tours are available and provide a genuine introduction to Islamic architecture and practice. The gardens around it make for a pleasant stroll.

**Al Shaheed Park** -- A rare expanse of greenery in a dusty city, with walking trails, sculptures, water features, and two excellent museums. It is where Kuwaitis go to escape the concrete, and it is lovely in the cooler months.

**Failaka Island** -- A short ferry ride from [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity), this island has Greek ruins from the time of Alexander the Great, remnants of a Bronze Age settlement, and abandoned buildings from the Iraqi occupation. It is eerily beautiful and largely untouched by modern development.

**The Scientific Centre** -- In [Salmiya](/asia/kuwait/salmiya), this waterfront complex houses the largest aquarium in the Middle East, a discovery centre, and an IMAX cinema. Particularly good if you are traveling with children.

**Friday Market (Souk Al-Juma'a)** -- A sprawling open-air market that takes place every Friday morning. Everything from rugs and antiques to live animals and second-hand electronics. It is chaotic, colourful, and thoroughly Kuwaiti.

**The Corniche** -- The long waterfront promenade stretching from the Kuwait Towers south through the city. Perfect for an evening walk, with views of the Gulf and the city skyline lit up at night.
