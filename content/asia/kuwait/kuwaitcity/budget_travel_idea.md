---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

Kuwait is not a cheap destination, but it is possible to visit without spending a fortune if you make smart choices.

**Getting around**: Walking is free and feasible in central areas, especially around the souk and waterfront. Buses are the cheapest transport option. When you do need a taxi, negotiate the fare upfront -- and consider using ride-hailing apps, which tend to be cheaper and more transparent than street taxis.

**Eating**: Skip the hotel restaurants and eat where Kuwaitis eat. Souk Al-Mubarakiya has excellent traditional food at very low prices. Shawarma stands and small Indian restaurants in Salmiya and Hawalli offer filling meals for 1-2 KWD. Fast food chains are everywhere if you need a cheap fallback.

**Sightseeing**: The Grand Mosque tours are free. The Tareq Rajab Museum has no admission fee. Walking the Corniche and exploring the souk costs nothing. Al Shaheed Park is free and pleasant.

**Shopping**: The Friday Market (Souk Al-Juma'a) has the best prices on everything from rugs to electronics. Bargain firmly but politely.

**Timing**: Come in winter to avoid the temptation of expensive air-conditioned escapes from the heat. The cooler months make outdoor activities practical, which are generally cheaper than indoor entertainment.

**Water fun**: The Aqua Park near the Kuwait Towers is reasonably priced for a full day of entertainment, especially compared to hotel beach clubs.
