---
title: "Family Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

Kuwait City is a surprisingly good destination for families. The city is very safe, and there are plenty of activities that work for all ages.

**Kuwait Scientific Centre** in [Salmiya](/asia/kuwait/salmiya) is the standout family attraction. The aquarium, discovery centre, and IMAX cinema can fill a full day. Children love the marine life exhibits, and the building itself -- with its Arabic calligraphy-decorated exterior -- is impressive.

**Aqua Park** on Arab Gulf Street near the Kuwait Towers has pools, slides, a man-made river, go-karts, and a paintball field. Open April to October. It is the best way to beat the summer heat with children.

**Entertainment City** is Kuwait's largest amusement park, combining three themed areas: the Arab World, the International World, and the Future World. Rides, live shows, and the Ali Baba and Sinbad section for younger children. Affordable at around 3.5 KWD per person. Closed Saturdays; Mondays are ladies-only.

**Green Island** is a pleasant half-day outing with a children's castle, swimming pool, and open spaces for running around. The observation tower and amphitheatre add interest for older kids.

**The Grand Mosque** offers free tours and is a good educational experience for older children. Proper dress is available to borrow for those who need it. The gardens outside are good for a picnic.

**Practical note**: Kuwait is very respectful of families and children are welcome everywhere. Remember to dress modestly, especially when visiting cultural and religious sites. The heat can be dangerous for young children in summer -- carry water and seek shade.
