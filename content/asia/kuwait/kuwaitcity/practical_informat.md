**Climate**: Kuwait City has an arid climate. Summers (May-September) are extremely hot, regularly exceeding 45C, with high humidity and occasional dust storms. Winters (December-February) are mild, with daytime temperatures around 18-22C and cool nights. January sees the most rainfall, but annual precipitation is only about 77mm.

**Currency**: The Kuwaiti Dinar (KWD), divided into 1,000 fils. One dinar is worth roughly $3.25 USD. ATMs and money changers are widely available.

**Transport**: Taxis are the most practical option. Agree on fares beforehand -- meters are rarely used. Call taxis (white) are more reliable than street taxis (orange). Ride-hailing apps work well. Buses are cheap but schedules are erratic.

**Safety**: Kuwait City is very safe, with low crime rates. The biggest risk is the traffic -- drive defensively. Gulf War-era minefields have been cleared, but stick to marked paths in the desert.

**Culture**: Kuwait City is predominantly Muslim. Dress modestly, especially around mosques. During Ramadan, eating, drinking, and smoking in public during daylight hours is illegal. The weekend is Friday-Saturday, with many shops closed on Friday until evening.

**Dialing code**: +965

**Time zone**: GMT+3 (no daylight saving)

**Electricity**: 240V AC, British-style three-pin plugs (Type G)

**Government**: Constitutional monarchy with an elected parliament
