---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Kuwait City's sights are concentrated along the waterfront corniche. The Kuwait Towers are the unmistakable landmark — their observation deck gives the best view of the city. The Souk Al-Mubarakiya, a covered market near the old centre, is the most atmospheric place in the city for a morning wander: spice merchants, gold dealers, and food stalls in a compact, genuinely lived-in setting. The Tareq Rajab Museum holds one of the finest private collections of Islamic art in the world, and is free. For a sense of Kuwait before oil, Sadu House and Bayt Al-Badr offer glimpses of the pre-oil townscape. Al Shaheed Park provides welcome green space with two small museums.
