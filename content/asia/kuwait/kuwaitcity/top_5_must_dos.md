---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Kuwait Towers at sunset** -- Take the elevator up to the revolving observation deck of the main tower and watch the city light up as the sun drops into the Gulf. The view is spectacular and the experience is quintessentially Kuwaiti. Have dinner at the restaurant inside the tower to make an evening of it.

**2. Souk Al-Mubarakiya** -- Lose yourself in Kuwait's oldest market. The spice stalls, gold shops, and traditional food stands are a sensory overload in the best possible way. Eat machboos at one of the small eateries, haggle for gold jewellery, and pick up saffron and cardamom to take home. Come in the evening when the souk is at its liveliest.

**3. Failaka Island** -- Take the ferry to this island 20 km offshore and explore Greek ruins from the era of Alexander the Great alongside the haunting abandoned village from the 1990 Iraqi invasion. It is the most historically layered place in Kuwait and one of the most memorable day trips in the Gulf.

**4. The Grand Mosque** -- One of the largest mosques in the world, with free guided tours that are genuinely informative and welcoming. Even if you have no particular interest in Islam, the architecture is stunning and the guides are happy to answer questions. The surrounding gardens make an excellent picnic spot.

**5. National Museum** -- The As-Sabah collection of Islamic art is world-class, and the exhibit on the Iraqi invasion and liberation is moving and well-presented. The museum tells the story of Kuwait from its pearl-diving past to its oil-fuelled present, and it does it well.
