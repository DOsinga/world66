Salmiya is Kuwait's second city in all but name -- a densely populated district about 12 km south of central [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity) that serves as the country's main commercial and entertainment hub. Over 90% of its 150,000 residents are expatriates, giving it a cosmopolitan, multicultural atmosphere.

The **Salem Al-Mubarak Street** is the main shopping drag, lined with malls, restaurants, and a mix of modern stores and older shops that retain something of Kuwait's traditional souk character. The **Marina Mall**, housed in a striking glass pyramid, and the surrounding **Marina Crescent** area offer waterfront dining and pleasant evening strolls.

Salmiya is home to the **Kuwait Scientific Centre**, one of the best attractions in the country. This waterfront complex houses the largest aquarium in the Middle East, with over 100 species, plus an IMAX cinema and a discovery centre. It is well worth a half day, especially with children.

The district has several beaches along its shoreline, and the **Marina Bay** area is attractive at night when the lights reflect off the water. For nightlife -- or what passes for it in a country without alcohol -- Salmiya has cafes, restaurants, and the Hard Rock Cafe that stay open late. **Cozmo Bowling** and the **Al-Shaab Gardens** amusement park offer evening entertainment for families.

Salmiya is walkable in its central area, and taxis to Kuwait City take only about 10 minutes during the day. It makes a practical base for exploring the country, with more dining options and a livelier street scene than central Kuwait City.
