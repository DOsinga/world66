---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Salmiya is primarily a residential and commercial district, so the sightseeing is modest compared to [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity). But there are a few worthwhile stops.

**Kuwait Scientific Centre** -- The standout attraction. This waterfront complex houses the largest aquarium in the Middle East (over 100 species), a discovery centre, and an IMAX cinema. The building itself is impressive, with Arabic calligraphy designs on the exterior. Allow half a day.

**Salmiya Centre (Salem Al-Mubarak Street)** -- The largest and oldest shopping area in town, covering the outer blocks of the district. It retains some of the feel of Kuwait's traditional souk bazaars, with gold and fabric shops mixed in among modern stores. Worth exploring on foot.

**St. Therese Parish** -- A church in the centre of Salmiya, established as a chapel in 1974. The interior is more impressive than the modest exterior suggests. A reminder that Kuwait is more religiously tolerant than many of its neighbours.

**Marina World** -- The second-largest shopping and entertainment complex, situated on the beachfront. The Marina Mall, Marina Crescent (with its waterfront restaurants), and the Marina Waves walkway combine for a pleasant area to spend an evening.

**Salmiya Beach** -- On the shore of Marina Bay, this modern beach is particularly attractive at night when the lighting transforms the waterfront. The dome and palm trees create a picturesque scene, and the sunset views are excellent.

For more substantial sightseeing, the Kuwait Towers and Souk Al-Mubarakiya in [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity) are only a 10-minute drive away.
