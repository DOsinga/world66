---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Kuwait Scientific Centre** -- The largest aquarium in the Middle East, plus a discovery centre and IMAX cinema. The aquarium alone has over 100 species and is beautifully designed. Allow half a day and grab coffee at one of the on-site cafes afterwards. Look for the Arabic calligraphy and the history of Kuwait depicted on the building's exterior.

**2. Salem Al-Mubarak Shopping District** -- Walk through the oldest shopping area in Kuwait, where traditional souk character survives alongside modern stores. The gold and fabric shops in the old souk area are remnants of the days when Kuwait's bazaars were known for pearls and jewellery. Explore on foot and bargain confidently.

**3. Al-Shaab Gardens** -- One of the most popular amusement parks in Kuwait, with rides, ice skating, adventure sports, restaurants, and landscaped gardens. Good for families and open until midnight. A pleasant way to spend an evening.

**4. Marina World** -- Stroll the Marina Crescent waterfront, eat at one of the world-class restaurants, and browse the Marina Mall. The Spanish-influenced design of the mall is attractive, and the waterfront views are excellent, especially at night.

**5. Salmiya Beach at sunset** -- End the day watching the sun go down from the shore of Marina Bay. The lighting transforms the beach and the waterfront into something quite beautiful. Grab a coffee or shisha at a nearby cafe and watch the city glow.
