---

Salmiya is only about 12 blocks across but it packs in more activity than most of Kuwait. Over 90% of its 150,000 residents are expatriates, giving it a diverse, cosmopolitan atmosphere that feels quite different from central [Kuwait City](/asia/kuwait/kuwaitcity).

**Getting around**: The central area is compact and walkable. Salem Al-Mubarak Street is the main artery, with shops, restaurants, and hotels along its length. For the Scientific Centre or the Marina, you may want a short taxi ride (about 20-25 minutes on foot). Taxis to Kuwait City take about 10 minutes during the day.

**Eating**: Salmiya has an excellent range of restaurants at every price point. For local food, try **Fatatri Al Omda** or **Khasad Khair** where the fatayer (stuffed pastries) cost about 1 KWD each. The **Chocolate Bar Restaurant** is popular for its dessert fondue. For familiar comfort food, McDonald's and other chains are well represented.

**Shopping**: Salem Al-Mubarak Street is the main shopping strip, with everything from traditional gold shops to modern boutiques. The **Marina Mall** and the surrounding Marina World complex offer upscale shopping and waterfront dining. **Villa Moda** is the newest high-end mall.

**Entertainment**: The Kuwait Scientific Centre is the star attraction. Bowling at Cozmo, the Al-Shaab Gardens amusement park, and the Marina Crescent cafes provide evening options. Expect most places to close by midnight.

Salmiya makes a practical base for exploring Kuwait. It has more dining options and street life than the city centre, and everything in the country is within easy reach.
