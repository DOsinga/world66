Ala-Archa National Park is a mountain gorge 40 kilometers south of [Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek), and the easiest way to get a taste of Kyrgyzstan's mountain scenery without leaving the capital region. The park climbs from around 1,600 meters at the entrance to glacier-topped peaks above 4,000 meters.

The most popular walk follows the river up through a forest of juniper and birch to a viewpoint overlooking the Ak-Sai glacier and waterfall, about a two-hour round trip on a well-marked trail. More ambitious hikers can continue to the Ratsek Hut, a Soviet-era mountaineering base at 3,350 meters, which takes a full day and offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks.

Getting here on your own requires a taxi from Bishkek (about 1,000-1,500 KGS one way). Infrequent marshrutkas run to the park gate, but it is still several kilometers from the gate to the trailheads, so a taxi is more practical. Agree on a return time or arrange a pickup. The park entrance fee is nominal.

Ala-Archa is a good day trip from Bishkek and a useful acclimatization hike if you are heading to higher altitudes later. Weekends can be busy with local families; weekdays are quieter.
