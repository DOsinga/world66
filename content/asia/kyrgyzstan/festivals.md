## Nooruz (March 21)

The Central Asian new year, celebrated on the spring equinox. Nooruz is the biggest holiday in Kyrgyzstan and the streets fill with music, dancing and the smell of sumalak, a sweet wheat pudding that takes 24 hours to prepare. Families gather, yurts are erected in public parks, and traditional games are played. It is a pre-Islamic festival with deep roots in Zoroastrian tradition, and Kyrgyzstan celebrates it with genuine enthusiasm.

## World Nomad Games

Held every two years (most recently in 2024), this is Kyrgyzstan's showcase event. Athletes from dozens of countries compete in traditional nomadic sports: kok-boru (a brutal form of polo played with a goat carcass), er enish (horseback wrestling), eagle hunting, archery and many others. The games take place around Lake Issyk-Kul and draw huge crowds. Even if you miss the competition itself, the cultural program of yurt camps, craft demonstrations and traditional music is spectacular.

## At-Chabysh (Horse Racing)

Horse racing is woven into Kyrgyz life, and races take place at festivals and celebrations throughout the year. The long-distance races, where horses gallop across open steppe for kilometers, are thrilling to watch. Major races coincide with national holidays.

## Independence Day (August 31)

Parades, concerts and celebrations in [Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek) mark the anniversary of independence from the Soviet Union in 1991. Ala-Too Square is the center of the action. It is a good day to be in the capital.

## Harvest Festivals (September-October)

In autumn, communities across the country celebrate the harvest and the return of livestock from the summer pastures. These are local, informal events -- not on any tourist calendar -- but if you happen upon one, you will be welcomed with food, drink and hospitality.
