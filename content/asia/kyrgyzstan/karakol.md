Karakol is the main town on the eastern shore of [Lake Issyk-Kul](/asia/kyrgyzstan/lakeissykkul) and the trekking capital of Kyrgyzstan. With a population of around 80,000, it is a quiet, leafy town that serves as the base for some of the country's best multi-day hikes, including the Ala-Kul trek, Altyn-Arashan hot springs and the Jeti Oguz Valley.

The town itself has a few worthwhile sights. The wooden Dungan Mosque, built in 1910 without a single nail and looking more like a Chinese pagoda than a mosque, is unique in Central Asia. The Russian Orthodox Holy Trinity Cathedral, a wooden church surrounded by gardens, is also worth a visit. The small but interesting Przhevalsky Museum commemorates the Russian explorer who died here in 1888.

Karakol has a significant Dungan (Chinese Muslim) population, and the food reflects this. The town is the best place in Kyrgyzstan to try ashlyamfu (cold spicy noodles) and Dungan-style laghman. The Sunday animal market on the edge of town, where livestock is traded in chaotic fashion, is one of the most authentic market experiences in Central Asia.

The town's airport reopened in late 2024 with seasonal flights from [Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek). Otherwise, shared taxis and marshrutkas from Bishkek take four to five hours along the northern shore of the lake.
