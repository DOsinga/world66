Kyrgyzstan is Central Asia's most accessible adventure. A small, mountainous country wedged between China, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan and Tajikistan, it offers some of the most dramatic alpine scenery on earth, a living nomadic culture and a visa-free entry policy that makes it the easiest of the 'stans to visit.

More than ninety percent of the country lies above 1,500 meters, and the Tian Shan range dominates everything. The landscapes shift from the grassy high pastures called jailoo, where herders still live in yurts through the summer months, to deep gorges, glacial lakes and peaks topping 7,000 meters. [Issyk-Kul](/asia/kyrgyzstan/lakeissykkul), the world's second-largest alpine lake, sits in a basin ringed by snow-capped mountains and never freezes despite the altitude.

The country's nomadic heritage is not a museum piece. In summer, families move their livestock to high pastures and set up yurt camps exactly as their ancestors did. You can stay in these yurts, drink fermented mare's milk (kymyz), eat beshbarmak with your hands and ride horses across valleys that feel like the edge of the world. The World Nomad Games, held every two years, showcase traditional sports like horseback wrestling and eagle hunting.

[Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek), the capital, is a Soviet-planned city of wide boulevards and shady parks that serves as the gateway for most visitors. From there, roads fan out toward the mountains. [Karakol](/asia/kyrgyzstan/karakol), on the eastern shore of Issyk-Kul, is the base for serious trekking. [Osh](/asia/kyrgyzstan/osh), in the south, is one of Central Asia's oldest cities, with a Silk Road bazaar that has been trading for over two thousand years.

Kyrgyzstan is not a polished destination. Roads are rough, infrastructure is basic outside the cities, and getting to the best places requires patience and a tolerance for shared taxis that leave when full. That is part of the appeal. This is a country for travelers who want to feel genuinely remote, where the reward for a long drive over a mountain pass is a lake or valley with nobody else in sight.

## Destinations

- [Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek) - the capital and main travel hub
- [Karakol](/asia/kyrgyzstan/karakol) - trekking base on Lake Issyk-Kul
- [Issyk-Kul](/asia/kyrgyzstan/lakeissykkul) - vast alpine lake and resort area
- [Osh](/asia/kyrgyzstan/osh) - ancient Silk Road city in the south
- [Uzgen](/asia/kyrgyzstan/uzgen) - small town with 12th-century Karakhanid monuments
- [Ala-Archa](/asia/kyrgyzstan/alaarchagorge) - mountain gorge near Bishkek
- [Norus](/asia/kyrgyzstan/norus) - ski area an hour from Bishkek
