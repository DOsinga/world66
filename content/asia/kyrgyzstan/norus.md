Norus is a small ski area about 35 kilometers from [Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek), making it the most convenient option for a day on the slopes if you are based in the capital. It is not world-class skiing by any measure, but it is easy to reach and surprisingly enjoyable for what it is.

The resort has a couple of drag lifts and groomed runs suitable for beginners and intermediates. The scenery is lovely, with views across the mountains on clear days. There is a lodge with a restaurant serving shashlik and other Kyrgyz staples, and the food is decent.

Getting here by taxi from Bishkek costs around 2,000-3,000 KGS. Make sure the driver has snow chains in winter. The season runs from roughly January to early March, depending on snowfall. There is no ski or snowboard rental on site, so you will need to bring or rent equipment in Bishkek.

For better skiing with more terrain and proper chairlifts, head to the ski base above [Karakol](/asia/kyrgyzstan/karakol), a five-hour drive from Bishkek.
