title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Kyrgyzstan's seasons are dramatic. The country sits high and continental -- summers are warm and dry, winters are long and bitterly cold, and the mountains dictate what is accessible and when.

## Summer (June-September)

This is when Kyrgyzstan comes alive. Mountain passes open, herders move their yurts to the high pastures, and trekking routes become accessible. July and August are the warmest months, with temperatures reaching 30-35C in the valleys and comfortable 15-25C at altitude. This is peak season for trekking, horse riding and visiting Son-Kul and other high-altitude lakes. September is arguably the best month: warm but not hot, fewer visitors, and the landscapes take on golden autumn tones.

## Shoulder Seasons (April-May, October)

Spring brings wildflowers to the lower valleys and foothills, but high passes remain snow-blocked until June. April and May are good for visiting [Bishkek](/asia/kyrgyzstan/bishkek), [Osh](/asia/kyrgyzstan/osh) and the lower shores of [Issyk-Kul](/asia/kyrgyzstan/lakeissykkul). October can be beautiful at lower elevations but unpredictable -- snow can arrive early in the mountains.

## Winter (November-March)

Cold, seriously cold. Temperatures in Bishkek drop to -5C or lower, and mountain areas are buried in snow. High passes close, and many rural areas become unreachable. However, this is ski season. [Norus](/asia/kyrgyzstan/norus) near Bishkek offers basic skiing, and Karakol's ski base on the slopes above town has better terrain. Heli-skiing is available for those with the budget. The best snow falls between January and early March.

## What to Pack

Layer everything. Even in July, mountain nights are cold. A warm fleece, rain jacket and sun protection are essential for any trek. In winter, bring serious cold-weather gear. Good walking boots are a must year-round.
