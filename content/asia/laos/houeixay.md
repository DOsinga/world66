Huay Xai (also spelled Houeixay or Houei Sai) sits on the Mekong in the far northwest of Laos, directly opposite Chiang Khong in [Thailand](/asia/thailand). For most travelers, it serves one purpose: as the starting point for the two-day slow boat journey down the Mekong to [Luang Prabang](/asia/laos/luangprabang), one of Southeast Asia's classic river trips.

The slow boats leave each morning and take two days, overnighting in the riverside town of Pak Beng. The scenery is wonderful -- limestone cliffs, dense jungle, and small villages along the riverbanks. Bring snacks, a cushion, and something to read.

The Gibbon Experience, a zipline and treehouse eco-adventure in the Bokeo Nature Reserve, is based near Huay Xai and is one of the more unusual things you can do in Laos. You sleep in treehouses high in the forest canopy and travel between them on ziplines, with a chance of spotting black-crested gibbons.

The town itself is small and functional. The Thai and Chinese influence is visible in the architecture and food. The Laos-China Railway has a station at Boten, about 200 kilometres to the northeast, which has provided an alternative route into this part of northern Laos.
