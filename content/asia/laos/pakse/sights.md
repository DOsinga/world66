---
title: 'Sights'
type: section
---

The main sights around Pakse lie outside the city, in the surrounding Champasak province.

**Wat Phu** is the headline attraction -- a UNESCO-listed pre-Angkorian temple complex at the base of Phu Kao mountain near Champasak town, about 45 kilometres south. The oldest structures date from the 6th century. Set on a hillside with flowering trees and wide views over the Mekong plain, it is atmospheric and far less crowded than Angkor. Look for the carved crocodile and elephant near the top, and the holy spring. The small museum has artifacts with English explanations. Open 8am to 4.30pm.

**Bolaven Plateau** rises east of Pakse, offering cooler temperatures, coffee plantations, and dramatic waterfalls. Tad Fane, Tad Yuang, and Tad Lo are the most visited falls. A motorbike loop around the plateau is one of the best riding routes in Laos.

**Si Phan Don (4,000 Islands)** lies further south, where the Mekong broadens into a vast network of islands and waterfalls near the Cambodian border. Buses and boats from Pakse reach the main traveler islands of Don Det and Don Khone in a few hours.
