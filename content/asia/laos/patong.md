Patong is a small village on Route 13 between [Vientiane](/asia/laos/vientiane) and [Luang Prabang](/asia/laos/luangprabang), north of [Vang Vieng](/asia/laos/vangvieng). Not to be confused with the beach resort in Thailand, this Patong is a quiet roadside settlement in the Lao mountains where the bus might stop long enough for you to buy a bag of sticky rice and stretch your legs.

The village sits in a narrow valley surrounded by karst limestone hills covered in dense vegetation. There is no particular reason to stay here unless your transport breaks down, but the scenery along this stretch of road is some of the best in Laos — jagged peaks rising from morning mist, terraced hillsides, and the occasional water buffalo blocking the road. If you are driving yourself, the section between Vang Vieng and Luang Prabang is now fully paved but still winding and slow.

The few noodle shops in the village serve basic Lao fare: foe (noodle soup), grilled meats, and Beer Lao. It is the kind of place where the journey is the point, not the destination.
