---
title: 'Practical Information'
type: section
---

**Visas.** Most nationalities can get a 30-day visa on arrival at international airports and major land borders for $30-50 USD (depending on nationality), payable in cash. You will need a passport photo. An e-visa is also available for $50 including processing fees, but is only valid at specific entry points. ASEAN nationals enter visa-free for 30 days. Since 2025, Laos requires a digital arrival and departure card (LDIF) to be completed online before entering or leaving the country.

**Money.** The currency is the Lao kip (LAK), but Thai baht and US dollars are widely accepted in tourist areas. ATMs are common in [Vientiane](/asia/laos/vientiane), [Luang Prabang](/asia/laos/luangprabang), and [Pakse](/asia/laos/pakse), but scarce in smaller towns -- carry cash when heading off the beaten track. Credit cards are accepted at upscale restaurants and hotels in the main cities, but not much elsewhere.

**Budget.** Laos is very affordable. Budget travelers can manage on $25-35 per day, covering simple guesthouse accommodation, local food, transport, and activities. Mid-range travelers spending $50-80 per day will be comfortable.

**Language.** The official language is Lao, which is closely related to Thai. Younger Lao people in tourist areas speak reasonable English. Older people sometimes speak French, a legacy of colonial rule. Outside the main towns, communication can be a challenge -- a few words of Lao go a long way and are always appreciated. The Lao greeting is 'sabaidee.'

**Safety.** Laos is generally very safe for travelers. Petty theft can occur in tourist areas, so take normal precautions. The most serious risk is unexploded ordnance (UXO) in rural areas, a legacy of the Vietnam War-era bombing campaign. Stick to marked paths in the countryside, particularly around the Plain of Jars and the Ho Chi Minh Trail areas. Never touch anything that looks like a bomb or shell.

**Electricity.** 230V, 50Hz. Plug types A, B, C, E, and F are all found. Bring a universal adapter.

**Internet.** Wi-Fi is available in guesthouses and cafes in all tourist towns. A local SIM card with data is cheap and easy to get at the airports or phone shops in any town.

**Health.** Tap water is not safe to drink -- stick to bottled or filtered water. Mosquito-borne diseases including dengue and malaria are present, particularly in rural areas. Use repellent and sleep under a net where possible. Medical facilities are limited outside Vientiane; for anything serious, most travelers cross to Thailand.
