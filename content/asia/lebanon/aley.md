Aley is a mountain town perched above [Beirut](/asia/lebanon/beirut) in the Mount Lebanon range, popular as a summer retreat when the coastal heat becomes oppressive. The cool mountain air, traditional stone houses and hillside restaurants make it a pleasant escape from the city, just 30 minutes up the winding mountain road.

The town has a long history as a resort -- Ottoman-era Lebanese and foreign residents fled here in summer, and the tradition continues today. You will find good restaurants with terraces overlooking the valley, some surviving traditional Lebanese architecture, and a relaxed pace that contrasts sharply with Beirut's intensity.

Aley is also a useful base for exploring the Chouf Mountains and visiting [Beiteddine](/asia/lebanon/beiteddine) and [Deir el Qamar](/asia/lebanon/deirelqamar), both of which are within easy reach to the south.
