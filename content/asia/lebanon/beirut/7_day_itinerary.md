---
title: "7 Day Itinerary"
type: section
---

**Day 1: Arrive and explore downtown.** Get your bearings with a walk through the rebuilt city centre. Visit Martyrs' Square, the Omari Mosque, Nejmeh Square and the Roman ruins scattered between the modern buildings. Grab a ka'ek (a sesame-crusted bread ring) from a street vendor. In the evening, have your first meal in Gemmayzeh or Mar Mikhael.

**Day 2: The National Museum and Gemmayzeh.** Spend the morning at the National Museum of Beirut, then walk east into Gemmayzeh to explore the street art, galleries and shops along Gouraud Street. Have lunch at one of the neighbourhood restaurants and spend the afternoon wandering.

**Day 3: Day trip to Jeita Grotto and [Byblos](/asia/lebanon/jbeilbyblos).** Head north to the Jeita Grotto caves in the morning (about 30 minutes from Beirut), then continue to Byblos for lunch at the old harbour and an afternoon exploring the Crusader castle, Phoenician excavations and the souk.

**Day 4: The Corniche and the coast.** Walk the Corniche from Ain el Mreisseh to Raouche, stopping for coffee overlooking the Pigeon Rocks. Spend the afternoon at a beach club or head south to Ramlet al-Bayda. Watch the sunset from one of the Raouche cafes.

**Day 5: Day trip to [Beiteddine](/asia/lebanon/beiteddine) and [Deir el Qamar](/asia/lebanon/deirelqamar).** A full day in the Chouf Mountains. The drive is scenic, Deir el Qamar is charming, and the Palace of Beiteddine is magnificent. Have lunch in the mountains and return to Beirut in the evening.

**Day 6: Day trip to [Baalbek](/asia/lebanon/baalbek).** An early start for the 1.5-hour drive across the mountains to the Bekaa Valley. Spend the morning at the Roman temples -- allow at least 3-4 hours. Stop at a winery on the way back (Chateau Ksara or Chateau Kefraya both offer tours).

**Day 7: Shopping, food and nightlife.** Visit Souk el Tayeb (if it is Saturday), browse the shops in Hamra or downtown, and eat your way through a final Lebanese mezze. Save your energy for a last night out in Beirut -- you will not regret it.
