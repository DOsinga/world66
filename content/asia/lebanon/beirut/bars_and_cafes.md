---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Beirut's bar and cafe scene is one of the best in the Middle East, ranging from traditional Arabic coffeehouses to sleek cocktail lounges.

**Gemmayzeh and Mar Mikhael** are the main bar districts. Gouraud Street in Gemmayzeh is lined with bars and cafes in restored Ottoman-era buildings. Mar Mikhael, the next neighbourhood east, has a more alternative, artsy vibe with rooftop bars and craft cocktail spots. Both areas were damaged in the 2020 explosion but have largely bounced back.

**Torino Express** in Mar Mikhael is a tiny, standing-room-only bar that is always packed. Good cocktails, great atmosphere, no pretension.

**Internazionale** -- A popular Mar Mikhael spot with a lively crowd and good music. Gets busy late.

**Blue Note Cafe** on Makhoul Street near AUB is one of the few jazz-oriented spots in Beirut. Relaxed vibe, live local bands, unpretentious crowd. Open Monday to Saturday.

**The Gemmayze Cafe (Ahwet L'Zeiz)** is a traditional Lebanese cafe where locals are the main clientele. A good place to sit with a coffee or an arak and watch the neighbourhood go by.

**Paul Cafe** on Gouraud Street brings French cafe culture to Beirut, with a pleasant garden terrace.

For a drink with a view, the **rooftop bars** along the Corniche and in the downtown area offer cocktails overlooking the Mediterranean. Prices in Beirut bars range from $5-8 for a beer to $12-18 for cocktails, though upscale venues charge more.

**Coffee culture** is strong in Beirut. You will find both traditional Arabic coffee (strong, cardamom-scented, served in small cups) and excellent modern espresso bars throughout the city.
