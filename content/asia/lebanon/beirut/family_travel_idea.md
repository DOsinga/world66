---
title: "Family Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

Beirut and its surroundings offer plenty for families with children.

**Jounieh cable car (Telepherique).** The steep cable car ride from Jounieh Bay up to Harissa is thrilling for children and adults alike. At the top, a funicular takes you to the statue of Our Lady of Lebanon, with panoramic views over the bay. Jounieh is about 20 minutes north of Beirut by car or taxi.

**Jeita Grotto.** These limestone caves in the Nahr al-Kalb valley are genuinely impressive -- massive cathedral-sized chambers with giant stalactites and stalagmites. The upper grotto is explored on foot; the lower grotto by boat on an underground river. Children love it. Open daily except Mondays, 9 AM to 6 PM (5 PM in winter). About 20 km north of Beirut.

**The Corniche.** Beirut's seafront promenade is a good place for a family walk or bike ride. Children can watch fishermen, look at the Pigeon Rocks, and stop for fresh juice from the waterfront vendors.

**Beach clubs.** Several of Beirut's private beach clubs are family-friendly, with pools, playgrounds and calm swimming areas. Long Beach and Sporting Club are both good options.

**The National Museum.** Older children will be interested in the archaeological collection, which covers Phoenician, Roman and Byzantine periods. The museum is compact enough for shorter attention spans.

**Day trip to Byblos.** The Crusader castle, the old harbour and the excavations are interesting for children, and lunch by the harbour makes it a pleasant family outing.
