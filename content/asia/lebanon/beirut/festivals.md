---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Beirut hosts a rich calendar of festivals and cultural events throughout the year.

**Beirut International Film Festival** takes place in October, screening independent documentaries, features and shorts from Lebanon and around the world. A good opportunity to see contemporary Lebanese cinema.

**Beirut Spring Festival** is held in June, with free concerts, theatre performances and cultural events at venues across the city. The festival is dedicated to the memory of journalist Samir Kassir and features a mix of opera, contemporary music and spoken word.

**Beirut International Jazz Festival** brings local and international jazz musicians to the city in October. Performances take place at various venues, often with food and art markets alongside.

**Salsa Festival** draws dancers from across the region every June, with workshops, parties and social dancing over several days.

**Ramadan and Eid al-Fitr** transform the city. During Ramadan, the rhythm of daily life shifts around the fast, and the evening iftar meals are social events. Eid al-Fitr, marking the end of Ramadan, brings three days of celebration with fireworks, family gatherings and festive decorations.

**Independence Day** on November 22 features a military parade through central Beirut.

For events outside the capital, the **Baalbek International Festival** (July-August) and the **Beiteddine Art Festival** (summer) are Lebanon's premier cultural events, featuring international performers in extraordinary ancient and historical settings.
