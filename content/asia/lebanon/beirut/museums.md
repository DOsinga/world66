---
title: "Museums"
type: section
---

**National Museum of Beirut** -- The most important museum in Lebanon, housing a remarkable collection spanning prehistoric times through the Ottoman period. Located on the former Green Line, the building itself was damaged during the civil war, but the collection was heroically preserved by museum staff who encased the larger pieces in concrete. The museum underwent a major renovation and is well worth a visit. Admission is 5,000 LBP.

**Sursock Museum** -- A beautiful 19th-century villa in Achrafieh that serves as Beirut's principal modern and contemporary art museum. The building alone is worth seeing, with its blend of Ottoman and Venetian architecture. It was damaged in the 2020 port explosion and has been undergoing restoration.

**MIM (Mineral Museum)** -- A surprisingly impressive collection of minerals from around the world, housed at Saint Joseph University. Well presented and a good option for a rainy afternoon.

**Beirut Art Center** -- A non-profit contemporary art space in the city's industrial zone, hosting rotating exhibitions by Lebanese and international artists. Free admission.

**Archaeological sites downtown** -- Extensive Roman-era ruins are visible at several points in central Beirut, including Roman baths behind Bank Street and ruins exposed during the Solidere reconstruction. You can see some of these just by walking around downtown.
