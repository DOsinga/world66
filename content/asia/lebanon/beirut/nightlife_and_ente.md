---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Beirut"
type: section
---

**Beirut Nightlife** has a lot to offer with Beirut being among
Lebanon’s most vivacious cities, renowned for its parties and the
vibrant, clubbing options. Beirut offers entertainment options of every
kind, ranging
from loud hardcore party joints to some exotically-themed options.
Following is a list of Beirut nightlife
and entertainment options that are seriously recommended:

**Music Hall**

The Music Hall is a bit difficult to describe, being somewhat of a
traditional Lebanese cabaret house blended with a contemporary pub-like
ambiance. However, the resulting mix is perhaps the best in Beirut,
offering a huge dance floor, often sprinkled with exotic dancers in the
background. The music has no set theme and it ranges from the gypsy-like
local bands to Arabic rock, often peppered with Asian fusion.

- **Address:** Along the main entrance of Starco Building in downtown Beirut- **Hours:** Thursday to Saturday, from 10 p.m. to the early morning
hours, often until 4 a.m.

**B018**

This party destination has been mentioned in many lifestyle magazines
across the globe. The B018 is a bar-cum-lounge and the most
sought-after Beirut nightlife hotspot. The landscape of B018 makes it
very unusual, located in a remote parking lot. Its external frame is
made of raw steel that dazzles in the moonlight. Often called ‘the lot,’
B018 was once a quarantine center that hosted many refugees. The top
ceiling opens up late in the night, allowing dancers to enjoy the view
of a star-lit sky. The music is a mix of trance and the latest numbers
that are maneuvered by an in-house DJ.

- **Address:** Lot 317, La Quarantine (near the main Beirut highway,
near a turnaround at Forum De Beyrouth)- **Hours: **7 p.m. onwards, until early morning hours

**EOM**

Not the most popular of clubs in Beirut but it is often recommended
to folks searching for a late-night dancing jig. The crowd is very young
and theme nights are also arranged.

- **Address: **Sin el Fil, located opposite to the Rotana Academy- **Hours: **11 p.m. onwards** **on Thursday, Friday, Saturday
and Sunday

**Gotha**

The name hints at it and the ambiance shouts it loud, this is among
Beirut’s handful of Gothic-styled, vampire-themed nightspots. The entire
place looks like a mythological chateau adorned with seductive lights.
Some parts of the Gotha are very European in nature and the restaurant
exudes a distinct French, colonial aura. The DJ plays everything from
Arabic numbers to global lounge hits, hip-hop and other genres of world
music.

- **Address:** Rue University, St. Joseph, Achrafieh--the
fourth building from the left turn at Rue University- **Hours:** Monday to Saturday
