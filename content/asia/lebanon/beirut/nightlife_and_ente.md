---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

Beirut's nightlife is legendary and has survived war, economic collapse and explosions. This is a city that goes out late and stays out later -- most clubs do not get going until midnight and run until dawn.

**B018** is Beirut's most famous club, located in a bunker-like structure in the Karantina district whose roof opens to reveal the night sky. The music ranges from electronic to Arabic pop. It is a unique experience and has been written up in lifestyle magazines worldwide.

**Music Hall** in downtown Beirut is part cabaret, part concert venue, part nightclub. Live performers play everything from Arabic classical to gypsy music to rock. The dance floor fills up late. Open Thursday to Saturday.

**The Grand Factory** is a large-scale nightlife venue with multiple rooms and DJs playing electronic, house and techno. Popular with a younger crowd.

**Gemmayzeh and Mar Mikhael** are where most of the bar-hopping happens. Start with drinks at one of the many bars along Gouraud Street or Armenia Street, then move to wherever the night takes you. The vibe shifts constantly -- ask locals what is good right now.

**Live music** is a strong part of Beirut's entertainment scene. Look for events at Metro al Madina, a theatre and live music venue that hosts Arabic music, comedy and cabaret shows.

**Be aware** that Beirut nightlife is not cheap by regional standards. Cover charges at clubs range from $10-30, and drinks inside are priced accordingly. The upside is the quality and energy -- few cities in the world match Beirut for sheer commitment to a good night out.
