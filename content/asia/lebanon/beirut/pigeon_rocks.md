Two massive natural stone arches rising from the sea at Raouche, at the western end of the Corniche. One of Beirut's iconic landmarks and a popular spot at sunset, when the cafes on the cliffs above fill up with locals and visitors ordering coffee, juice, or a nargileh. The rocks are best seen from the cliff-top promenade or from a boat.
