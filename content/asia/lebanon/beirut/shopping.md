---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Beirut offers a wide range of shopping, from traditional souks to designer boutiques.

**Beirut Souks** downtown is a large modern shopping complex built on the site of the original Ottoman-era markets. It houses international brands, local designers, restaurants and cinemas. Clean and air-conditioned, though it lacks the atmosphere of a traditional market.

**Hamra** is a more eclectic shopping street with bookshops, clothing stores, and cafes. It has a neighbourhood feel and is good for browsing without the high-end prices of downtown.

**Verdun** is Beirut's upmarket shopping district, with designer boutiques and high-end fashion outlets. **Aishti**, on El Moutrane Street downtown, is the flagship store for international luxury brands including Chloe, Jimmy Choo and Gucci.

**Souk el Tayeb** is a weekly farmers' market held on Saturdays, selling organic produce, artisan cheese, honey, bread and traditional Lebanese products. It is the best place to buy food souvenirs and taste regional specialties.

**Artisan du Liban** at Ain Mreisseh sells traditional Lebanese crafts, fabrics and contemporary designs inspired by local heritage. Good for gifts and household items.

**The Sunday market** at Jisr el-Wati in Sin el-Fil is Beirut's biggest flea market -- a sprawling affair where you can find everything from antiques to second-hand clothes. Arrive early for the best picks and be prepared to bargain.

For **books**, Librairie Antoine has several branches across Beirut with good selections in English, French and Arabic.
