---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

**Walk the Corniche.** Beirut's seaside promenade runs along the western coast, popular with joggers, families and fishermen. Walk south to Raouche to see the Pigeon Rocks -- two massive natural stone arches rising from the sea. Best at sunset.

**Visit the National Museum.** Lebanon's premier museum covers millennia of history, from the Bronze Age through the Roman, Byzantine and Mamluk periods. The Sarcophagus of Ahiram, dating to the 10th century BC, is a highlight. Admission is very cheap.

**Explore Gemmayzeh and Mar Mikhael.** These east Beirut neighbourhoods are the centre of the city's creative life -- street art, independent shops, galleries, and some of the best restaurants and bars. Walk Armenia Street and Gouraud Street.

**See the Cathedral of St. George.** The Maronite cathedral near Martyrs' Square was built in 1767, severely damaged during the civil war, and beautifully restored by 2000. Archaeological excavations underneath reveal layers of Roman, Byzantine and medieval remains.

**Wander downtown.** The rebuilt city centre is a mix of gleaming new construction, restored Ottoman architecture and Roman ruins visible at street level. Nejmeh Square with its clock tower, the Ottoman-era Parliament building, and the nearby Greek Orthodox churches are all worth seeing.

**Scuba diving.** Beirut's coast has surprisingly good diving, with shipwrecks, caves, and marine life including rays. Several operators offer lessons and guided dives year-round.

**Catch the sunset at Raouche.** The cafes overlooking the Pigeon Rocks fill up in the late afternoon. Order a coffee or a nargileh (water pipe) and watch the sun drop into the Mediterranean.

**Visit the Hippodrome.** Arabian thoroughbreds race at the Beirut Hippodrome on Sundays. Admission is free and it is a fun, lively afternoon activity. The track dates to 1885.
