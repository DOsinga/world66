Think of a Lebanese Monte Carlo and you are close. Jounieh is the resort town where Beirutis go to have fun -- casinos, beach clubs, restaurants and nightlife, all set against one of the most beautiful bays on the Lebanese coast, just 20 minutes north of [Beirut](/asia/lebanon/beirut).

The town's setting is stunning: a crescent-shaped bay backed by steep mountains. The main attraction is the **Telepherique**, a cable car that climbs steeply from the bay up to Harissa. At the top, you transfer to a funicular that takes you to the lookout point surrounding the shiny white statue of Our Lady of Lebanon. If you want to go still higher, you can climb the ramp around the statue's base -- the closer you come to her outstretched hands, the narrower the ramp becomes and the pushier the people get. The view is spectacular, and the Virgin seen from close up has a sweet, sad charm.

Jounieh also has a lively waterfront promenade, and the bay area comes alive at night with restaurants and bars. A ferry service to Larnaca, Cyprus has been announced, which would make Jounieh a port of entry as well.
