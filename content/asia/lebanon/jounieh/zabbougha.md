Zabbougha is a tiny village of about 300 people in the Lebanese mountains, roughly 51 km from [Beirut](/asia/lebanon/beirut). It sits at 950 metres altitude at the foot of Mount Sannine, in the governmental district of Baskinta.

The village is Maronite Christian and has a history tied to Lebanon's 19th-century silk industry -- it once had two silk-unwinding factories, though neither survives today. Zabbougha is reachable by several winding mountain roads through Bikfaya, Bteghrine and Kfar Aaqab, or via Balloune and Daraya. It is a quiet spot with mountain views, far from the tourist trail.
