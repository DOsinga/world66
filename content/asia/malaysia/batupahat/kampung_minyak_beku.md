A coastal kampung (village) southwest of the town centre, notable for an ancient well said to have been chiselled by Siamese craftsmen — from which the district's name (Batu Pahat: "chiselled stone") is traditionally derived. The well is a modest attraction but is central to local history and gives the town its identity. The village sits on the coast with views across the Strait of Malacca.
