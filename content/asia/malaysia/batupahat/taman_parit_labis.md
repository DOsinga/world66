A wetland area on the edge of town notable for its population of Lesser Adjutant storks (burung botak locally) — large wading birds, endangered and rarely seen in Malaysia, that use Batu Pahat as a roosting and feeding ground. The sight of these massive birds — nearly the height of a person — is striking. Bird watchers visit early morning for the best chance of seeing the storks active along the waterways.
