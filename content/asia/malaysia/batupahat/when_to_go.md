**Planning a Vacation in Batu Pahat**

You may be wondering as you're planning a visit to **Batu Pahat**, **when to go** to the city. The main factor that you should take in consideration is the climate. Batu Pahat experiences a tropical climate, without extremely high temperatures, with hot days and cooler nights. It rains a lot throughout the whole year, so make sure to pack some suitable light clothes (due to the high temperatures) and a raincoat (due to the excessive amounts of rain), and avoid visiting the city during the Monsoon Season.

**The Monsoon Season**

The Monsoon Season lasts from November to February. It is characterized by heavy rain and wind storms, with a total of 15 to 20 days of precipitation per month. The humidity, especially in the morning, reaches values of 95 percent, which causes a major discomfort combined with the temperatures of 31 degrees Celsius (88 degrees Fahrenheit). Typhoons and floods are very likely to occur during the Monsoon Season, so avoid visiting Batu Pahat at this time of the year for your own good.

**Rest of the Year**

In general, there is no significant change in the weather during the rest of the year. Precipitation stays high and again the months have 10 to 20 rainy days. Average temperatures are 30 degrees Celsius (86 degrees Fahrenheit) during the day and 20 degrees Celsius (68 degrees Fahrenheit) during the night. The only plus is that the risk of floods and typhoons is significantly lower, and knowing this you can feel much safer.
