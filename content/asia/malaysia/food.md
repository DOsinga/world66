Malaysian food is extraordinary. The convergence of Malay, Chinese, Indian and Peranakan cooking traditions, amplified by tropical ingredients and a culture that takes eating very seriously, has produced one of the world's great cuisines. Eating is the national pastime, and Malaysians will drive hours for the right bowl of laksa.

**The essentials**

**Nasi lemak** is the national dish -- coconut rice with sambal, fried anchovies, peanuts, cucumber and a hard-boiled egg, often with rendang or fried chicken on the side. It is eaten at breakfast, lunch, dinner and midnight. The sambal makes or breaks it.

**Char kway teow** is flat rice noodles stir-fried with prawns, cockles, Chinese sausage, egg and bean sprouts over fierce heat. [Penang](/asia/malaysia/penang) claims the best version, and the best hawkers have queues to prove it.

**Laksa** comes in many forms. Penang assam laksa (sour, fish-based, tamarind-spiked) and Sarawak laksa (coconut-based, with prawns and shredded chicken) are the standouts. They taste nothing alike and both are superb.

**Roti canai** is the Indian-influenced flatbread served with dhal or curry at mamak stalls. It is the default late-night snack across the country. Order it plain, stuffed with egg (roti telur), with onion (roti bawang) or with banana (roti pisang).

**Rendang** is meat -- usually beef or chicken -- slow-cooked in coconut milk with a complex paste of lemongrass, galangal, turmeric and chilies until the sauce reduces to a dry, intense coating. A celebration dish that finds its way into everyday meals.

**Satay** -- skewers of marinated meat grilled over charcoal and served with peanut sauce, compressed rice cakes and a cucumber-onion relish. Kajang, south of KL, claims satay supremacy.

**Where to eat**

The best food in Malaysia is not in restaurants -- it is at hawker centers, kopitiam (traditional coffee shops) and roadside stalls. These are where Malaysians eat, and the quality is remarkable for the price. A meal at a hawker stall costs RM5-15.

[Penang](/asia/malaysia/penang) is widely considered the food capital, with legendary hawker spots like Gurney Drive, New Lane and Air Itam. [Kuala Lumpur](/asia/malaysia/kualalumpur) counters with Jalan Alor, Petaling Street and the mamak stalls of Bangsar. [Ipoh](/asia/malaysia/ipoh) is famous for its bean sprout chicken, white coffee and dim sum. [Melaka](/asia/malaysia/melaka) has Peranakan specialties like chicken rice balls and nyonya laksa.

**Drinks**

**Teh tarik** (pulled tea) is sweet, milky tea poured dramatically between two cups to create a frothy top. It is the national drink. **Kopi** at a kopitiam means strong coffee roasted with butter and sugar, served with condensed milk. Fresh fruit juices are everywhere and cheap. Alcohol is available but pricey due to tax -- a beer at a bar runs RM15-25.
