Fraser's Hill is a quiet hill station in the mountains of Pahang, about two hours' drive north of Kuala Lumpur. At around 1,500 metres, it is noticeably cooler than the lowlands — evenings can be genuinely chilly, so bring a sweater.

The area is one of the best birdwatching spots in Peninsular Malaysia, with over 260 recorded species including several highland endemics. Walking trails of varying difficulty wind through the surrounding montane forest, from easy strolls around the golf course to steeper jungle paths. The atmosphere is unhurried and slightly old-fashioned — stone bungalows, mist rolling through the trees, and far fewer visitors than the Cameron Highlands to the north.
