Also known as Coronation Park, this large green space in the centre of Ipoh is a good place to take a breather. It holds one of the last surviving specimens of the *ipoh* tree from which the city takes its name (the tree's sap was once used by the Orang Asli to poison their blowpipe darts), plus an artificial lake with feedable fish, a small Japanese garden with koi ponds, and a children's playground with a miniature railway. The park is named after a popular Tamil-Malaysian politician of the 1960s.
