title: Eating Out
type: section
---

Kluang has good local food. The railway station coffee shop is the most famous, but the town has several strong options for traditional Malaysian and Hainanese cuisine.

**Kluang Railway Station Coffee Shop** — Inside the railway station. Packed at breakfast with locals and visitors alike. Toasted bread with kaya (coconut jam), Mee-Siam, Nasi Lemak, and local coffee served Malaysian-style. Does not serve lunch.

**Yean Kee Beef Noodles** — Handmade rice noodles in pure beef stock, with peanuts, lime, and chili sauce. Available dry or in soup.

**Hoover Restaurant** — Taman Sayang, Kluang Baru. Hainanese chicken rice and local Malaysian street food staples.

**Yong Tau Fu Restaurant** — Jalan Haji Manan. Bean curd, fish paste, fish balls, eggplant, and bitter gourd in the classic Hakka style.
