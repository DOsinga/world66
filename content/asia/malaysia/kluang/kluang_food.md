Kluang punches above its size for food. The town is best known for Kluang Rail Coffee (inside the railway station), but there are stronger options for those who explore further.

**Chinese restaurants** worth seeking out include Chuan Mei (seafood, lunch and dinner), Cin Cin (popular for wedding banquets but open to all), and Lerk Foo Lian Seng (open 8 pm to 3 am — good for a late supper).

**Mamak food**: Warung Kak Esah in Taman Intan is the local favourite — the mee goreng and roti are standouts, open until 2 am. Another reliable mamak is in Kluang Baru at the cross junction near the old Paris block.

**Nasi lemak**: Bob's has two stalls, one in Taman Intan opposite 7-Eleven and one in Kluang Baru opposite Myone.

**Curry mee**: Botak has two outlets — one in Chinatown and one in Kluang Baru. Yong He along Jalan Niyor is another solid option.

**Kopitiam**: Beyond Kluang Rail, Old Town in Kluang Parade and the kopitiam near Rex Cinema are both decent.
