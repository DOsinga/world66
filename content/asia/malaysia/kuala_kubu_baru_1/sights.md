---
title: "Sights in Kuala Kubu Baru"
type: section
---

A visit to the famous town of Malaysia would be incomplete without
experiencing the best **Kuala Kubu Baru Sights**. Malaysia, one of
the most renowned tourist spots in South East Asia, boasts of its
redeemed town named Kuala Kubu Baru, or simply KKB. When a great flood
struck this town in 1883, continuous reconstruction and growth have been
apparent. Its carefully maintained history in certain landmarks and
artefacts attracts tourists most. Here are some suggestions of places to
go to for you.

**Kuala Kubu Baru Rest House**

If you
already have an accommodation somewhere else and is still in great
curiosity of how the townspeople believed the flood came to be, you must
pay a visit to the KKB Rest House and see for yourself the grave of Sir
Cecil Ranking’s hand. It was said that he caused the flood by killing a
white crocodile. To know more about it, you should go check this site
out.

**The Broken Dam**

What better way to know a town
is to catch a glimpse of what caused it to be. The Broken Dam, or Ampang
Pecah in Malay, brought about the great tragedy of KKB. Nonetheless, if
you want to understand more of how this town started to flourish, you
must not miss out on the one thing that motivated it to do so.

**Indian
Village Restaurant**

Yes, you read it right. The Indian
Village Restaurant is the place to be if you want to try some different
cuisine out while on vacation. This restaurant offers a delectable menu
with their mouth-watering and spicy dishes. They do serve authentic
Indian cuisine; so it’s best if you are in for a nice treat with a
little bit of spice.

**Chiling Waterfalls**

Considered
as the most beautiful waterfalls in Selangor, the Chiling Waterfalls is
one site you must not miss out on your itinerary. With only a few miles
of driving and a little walking pass the bridge, you get to see a
breathtaking scenery of nature at its best. The trekking might cost you
some sweat, but it sure is worth it once you get a view of the
waterfalls.

**Old Railway Station**

The Old Railway
Station is famous among photographers who want to get a shot of the
town’s life and history. Not only does the architecture of this site
bring story to their photos, it also inspires a splendid work of art
that continuously attracts tourist to visit. Don’t get left behind and
take your own pictures.
