Kuala Terengganu is the capital of Terengganu, one of Malaysia's east coast states, standing on the banks of the Terengganu River. It is a quiet, traditional Malay city that serves as the gateway to some of Southeast Asia's best islands — Redang and Perhentian, with their crystal-clear waters and coral gardens, are the main draws.

The city itself has a relaxed, unhurried character. Chinatown along the rivermouth dates back to the fifteenth century, when Chinese traders arrived after Admiral Zheng He's voyages. The Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah, about 4.5 kilometres outside town, appears to float on water — a striking modern mosque combining traditional and contemporary design.

Beyond the islands, Lake Kenyir (55 km from the city) is the largest man-made lake in Southeast Asia, good for fishing, canoeing, and jungle trekking. Rantau Abang, 70 kilometres south, is one of the world's few nesting grounds for leatherback turtles, though their numbers have declined dramatically and turtle sanctuaries are working to reverse this.
