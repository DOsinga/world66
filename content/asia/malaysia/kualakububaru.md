Kuala Kubu Baru — KKB to everyone who knows it — is a small town an hour north of Kuala Lumpur, in the foothills of the Titiwangsa range. Almost nobody stops in the town itself, but it is the jumping-off point for some of the best outdoor adventures in central Malaysia. The old 1920s shophouse-lined main street has a faded, colonial-era charm: this was the administrative centre of the district after the original Kuala Kubu was wiped out by floods in 1883, and the new town was laid out on a grid with broad streets and a maidan, which is unusual for the region.

The real draw is the surrounding rainforest. The Chiling Waterfall, a half-hour's drive from town, is a classic jungle trek — you wade the river five times along the way and come out at a pool beneath a tall, cool cascade. The Selangor River (Sungai Selangor) is the main white-water rafting river in peninsular Malaysia: guided half-day trips run from KKB through a series of grade two and three rapids and are genuinely fun. Fraser's Hill, an old British hill station famous for its birdwatching, is an hour's drive into the mountains above town. And the Sungai Sendat waterfall and the hot springs at Ulu Kalong are both easy half-day excursions.

It is a short commuter-train ride from Kuala Lumpur (Komuter line, about 90 minutes), which makes it a good day or overnight trip if you want to swap concrete for jungle without hiring a driver. Bring proper shoes and insect repellent.
