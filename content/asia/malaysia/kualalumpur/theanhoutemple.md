One of Southeast Asia's largest Chinese temples, Thean Hou sits on a hilltop in Robson Heights with sweeping views of the KL skyline. Built by the Hainanese community and completed in 1989, the six-tiered structure blends Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian elements under a cascade of red-and-gold rooflines, dragon pillars, and ornate carvings.

The temple is dedicated to Tian Hou, the goddess of the sea, and draws worshippers and visitors alike — particularly during Chinese New Year and the Nine Emperor Gods Festival, when it's illuminated with thousands of lanterns. The upper decks offer some of the best free panoramic views in the city. A medicinal herb garden and a tortoise pond occupy the lower terraces.

Entry is free. It's best reached by taxi or ride-hail, as the hilltop location isn't well served by public transport.
