---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Kuching has one of the best food scenes in Malaysian Borneo, with Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous Sarawakian food all represented. The city's signature dishes include kolo mee (dry noodles with minced pork and spring onion), laksa Sarawak (a rich coconut and tamarind broth quite different from the Peninsula version), and midin (wild jungle fern stir-fried with belacan or garlic). The cheapest and most rewarding eating is at roadside stalls and coffee shops in the Padungan and Carpenter Street areas. The Jalan Song area, further from the centre, has a strong concentration of good local restaurants popular with Kuching residents.
