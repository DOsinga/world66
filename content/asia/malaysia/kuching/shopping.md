---
title: "Shopping in Kuching"
type: section
---

Located on the panoramic island of Borneo, the city often referred to as the Garden City happens to be a paradise for local **Kuching shopping**. In Malay the name stands for "cat." The rambling city is also popularly acknowledged as the most charming destination in South East Asia. The city holds a rich historical legacy of which the Chinese and Indian trade and the swashbuckling pirates are an integral part. Kuching has been the official capital of Sarawak state since 1963. The ambiance of the township, its inhabitants and multi-hued Chinese or Malay traditions make it a favorite tourist destination. It's a great place to collect South East Asian artifacts and collectibles.

**Main Bazaar**

If you are up to some souvenir shopping, Main Bazaar is the ultimate destination for sure. Located right on the opposite side of Kuching Waterfront near the Sarawak Tourism Complex, this place is also termed as the Antique Arcade. This is an old-fashioned two-story construction housing the richest shops of handicrafts and artifacts. There are several Chinese families that have been trading in this market for generations now. You can find some of the most splendid local handcrafted stuffs and exquisite jewelry here. Put some effort in bargaining to fetch a profitable purchase on interesting goods.

**The Spring **

The Spring is a contemporary lifestyle shopping arcade in Kuching which is the second largest mall in the entire state. The market covers about 42,000 square feet, accommodating prestigious shops like the Ta Kiong Supermarket, Parkson Department Store and MBO Cinemas. You can find branded shoes, perfumes, watches, garments and wide variety of gourmet foods here.

** Boulevard Shopping Complex**

Boulevard Shopping Complex is near the 4th Mile region. It covers about 720,000 square feet and houses some of the most popular brands out there. The major tenant at this mall is obviously Boulevard Department Store and Hypermarket. Other stores like Hang Ten, Converse, Body Glove, Kenny Roasters, Guardian and Popular Bookstore are must-visits for all shopaholics. Food is available from Pizza Hut, Sushi King, KFC and many more.

**Sarawak Plaza**

Sarawak Plaza is one of the oldest malls in Kuching, located at the heart of the city's business area. The arcade features four shopping levels and holds a three-story parking area with nominal hourly charges. You can find supermarkets selling all types of lifestyle products, money-exchangers, boutique-made exclusive clothing and sports equipment here.

**Green Heights Mall**

Green Heights Mall is probably the first small shopping mall in the neighborhood suburbs of Kuching. You can buy some great quality food products from Darling Bakes, The Pan Tree, home accessories from Kartini, computers and IT products and much more.
