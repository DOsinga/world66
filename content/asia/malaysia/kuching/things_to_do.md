---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Kuching Waterfront is the natural starting point — an esplanade along the Sarawak River with views across to the Astana and Fort Margherita, lined with food stalls and shade trees. The Sarawak Museum is one of Southeast Asia's best ethnographic collections, covering the indigenous cultures of Borneo with genuine depth. Semenggoh Wildlife Centre, 25 km south, offers reliable sightings of semi-wild orangutans. The Satok weekend market on Saturday evenings draws locals in force and gives a better sense of the city than any tourist attraction. Annah Rais longhouse, 65 km out, is the most accessible traditional Bidayuh community in the region. Bako National Park — proboscis monkeys, bearded pigs, and remarkable forest diversity — is the essential day trip.
