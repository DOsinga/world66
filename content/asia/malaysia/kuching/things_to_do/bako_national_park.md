Sarawak's oldest national park, about 37 km from Kuching, is reached by road to Kampung Bako and then a short boat ride across the estuary. Despite its small size, Bako holds an exceptional variety of forest types — from mangroves and beach forest to heath forest and cliff vegetation. The wildlife is remarkable: proboscis monkeys, bearded pigs, long-tailed macaques, and silvered langurs are commonly seen. Multiple trails of varying difficulty fan out from the park headquarters. Overnight stays are possible; booking in advance is essential in high season.
