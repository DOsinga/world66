One of the best natural history and ethnographic museums in Southeast Asia, founded in 1891 and still housed in a Victorian colonial building on Jalan Tun Abang Haji Openg. The collection covers Sarawak's indigenous peoples — their crafts, rituals, weapons, and daily life — alongside natural history exhibits including wildlife and geology. The Islamic Heritage Museum and the Textile Museum in adjacent buildings are part of the same complex. Free entry.
