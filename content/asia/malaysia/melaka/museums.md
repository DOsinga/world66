---
title: "Museums in Melaka"
type: section
---

Melaka has many museums depicting art, history and the cultural heritage
of Malaysia. Baba-Nonya, Stadthuys, Kite, Villa Sentosa and Beauty
Museum are the major attractions.

**Villa Sentosa Museum**

The Villa Sentosa Museum is also known as Malay Living museum.
Situated in Kampung Morten along the Melaka River in Melaka, this is a
small museum that depicts the life of an old Malaysian family. Villa
Sentosa is actually a Malay House of 1920s. This house museum has two
cats, a beautiful garden and other old living items. The entry to the museum is
free and it opens daily from 9 am to 1 pm and 2 pm to 5 pm except
Fridays. On Friday, timing is from 2:45 pm to 5 pm.

**Baba-Nonya Heritage Museum**

Located on Ji Tun Tan Cheng Lock, adjacent to Jonker Street in
Melaka, Baba-Nonya Heritage museum has various Malaysian art and
cultural items on display. It shows the wealthy lifestyle of the
Penakaran family. Antique crafted furniture, Chinese art paintings,
traditional costumes, ceramic dinner items and many historical pieces
of Baba and Nonya can be found here. This museum is open from 10 am to
12 pm and 2 to 4:30 pm daily. The entry ticket used to be 8 RM but now is
free.

**People’s and Beauty Museum**

Also known as Muzium Rakyat Dan, this beauty museum is located in
Jalan Kota in Melaka. There are three floors each depicting different
aspects of local development. The first floor is about Malacca culture with
many pictures. The top floor is like a sauna bath. But it is the middle
floor that is the major attraction displaying all forms of body
beautification and various kites. Entry fee to this museum is 2 RM.

Melaka indeed has amazing museums depicting the various lifestyles of
different times.
