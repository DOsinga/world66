The drinking scene in [Melaka](/asia/malaysia/melaka) is modest but pleasant, and it suits the city's pace. Most of the action is along the river and in the streets around Jonker Walk, where old shophouses have been converted into bars and cafes.

The riverfront is the nicest place to have a drink in the evening. Several bars occupy restored buildings along the Melaka River, and the river cruise boats passing by with their coloured lights add to the atmosphere. Discovery Cafe and Lounge on Jalan Bunga Raya is a reliable spot for cocktails and a mixed crowd of locals and visitors.

Melaka shuts down earlier than [Kuala Lumpur](/asia/malaysia/kuala_lumpur) or [Georgetown](/asia/malaysia/georgetown), and on weeknights you may find yourself one of a few patrons. The weekend is livelier, especially when the Jonker Walk Night Market is running and the surrounding streets fill with people. If you want anything resembling a club, you will need to head to the newer part of town around Mahkota Parade, though expectations should be kept in check.
