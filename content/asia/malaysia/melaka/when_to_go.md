---
title: "When to Go in Melaka"
type: section
---

When planning your vacation to** Melaka, when to go** is an
important choice to make. Malaysia has nearly 10 months of monsoon
season and an average rainfall of 2000 mm (79 inches) a year which is
approximately double the rainfall of temperate climates. The southwest
monsoon season starts in late May to September and the northeast
monsoon, which is the stronger of the 2, occurs from November to
March. However, Melaka's diverse demographics mean whenever you visit
you will be witness to many local festivals and celebrations. It is
important to remember to dress modestly when attending local religious
festivals--if you are unsure, ask a hotel clerk or tour guide.

**Southwest Monsoon Season**

Because of the heat and humidity year round in Melaka it is important
to dress in light clothes but be prepared for rain. On June 6 the
city celebrates the birthday of Malaysia's King Yang di Pertuan
Agong with parades and celebrations. The Malacca
Carnival is held for an entire month, displaying traditional Malaysian
dances and showcasing local tourism, art and industry.

**Northeast Monsoon Season**

November and December bring 2 very special occasions in Melaka,
Diwali and Christmas. During Diwali the Hindu quarter of Melaka
celebrates their Festival of Lights in style while Christmas fills the
streets with carols and yuletide revelry. January or February, depending
on the year, is when the large Chinese population celebrates Chinese New
Year in Melaka. And, the very best time for tourists to visit comes in
March with the Melaka Tourism Week where the hotels throw large
publicity stunts with gourmet food and competitions.

**Non-Monsoon Season**

Consisting of October, April and most of May, this is the best time to
visit Melaka. Specifically when to go this time of year depends on what your
particular tastes are. Easter Sunday is joyous occasion in Melaka, as a
procession is held near St. Peters Church and a mass is held that
brings in Catholics from all over the surrounding countryside. The
beaches are calm and the jungles and countryside surrounding Melaka are in full bloom
this time of year. There is much see for any nature lover. It is still
important to dress for humidity this time of year, but chances of rain
are slim and it is the best time to go for a hike in Melaka. When to go
to religious services and celebrations always be respectful of the people and their culture and dress modestly.
