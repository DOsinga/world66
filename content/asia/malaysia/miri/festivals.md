title: "Festivals in Miri"
type: section
---

There are several **Miri festivals **visitors will enjoy.
The dates of all festivals, except Christmas, are calculated based
on the Islamic lunar calendar or the Chinese calendar. To obtain the
exact dates, you should contact the Miri Tourism Office.

**Chinese New Year Festival**

The Chinese New Year Festival begins
on the first day of the first month in the Chinese calendar and ends
on the fifteenth day. These days usually fall near the end of January
or the beginning of February. Each year in the Chinese calendar is
named after 1 of the 12 animals of the Chinese calendar. On New
Year's Eve, every member of a household attends a reunion dinner and
temple visits are made the next day. This festival lasts for 15 days
and you can attend several festivities and exhibitions as well as yam
seng (bottoms up) parties and feasts.

**Festival of the Patriotic Poet**

This festival isalso known as the Dumpling Festival and begins on the fifth day of the
fifth lunar month (late May). This festival honors Chu Yuan Wa who
was a minister of the State of Chu until corruption forced him out.
For the next 20 years of his life, he traveled around the country and
wrote poems against corruption. To honor him, fishermen row boats to
the middle of rivers and throw rice dumplings into the water. You can
attend Dragon Boat Races in Penang.

**Gawai Dayak**

It is celebrated in
early June to mark the end of the padi planting season and the
beginning of a new season. A day before the celebration, every member
of a family will attend a reunion ceremony where the youngest family
member will offer a plate of specially prepared food to his parents.
During the festival, the Dayaks are decked in their finest
traditional costumes and Tuak (rice wine) is served. They also
sacrifice livestock for a good harvest.

**Hari Raya Puasa Festival**

This festival is held the
first day of Ramadhan, which is the ninth month in the Islamic lunar
calendar, near the beginning of August. Muslims decorate the insides
and outsides of their houses with lamps and colorful lights, wear new
clothes and prepare a large variety of cakes, meats, curries and
ketupat (rice boil in pandan leaves).

**Moon Cake Festival **

This festival takes place
at the beginning of August and commemorates the Chinese victory
against the Mongols. The Chinese defeated the Mongols by passing
messages hidden in cakes under the noses of their captors. For this
festival, various sizes and shapes of moon cakes stores are are sold
in stores erected along a city center street. These moon cakes are
stuffed with lotus seeds, pork, egg and bean paste (both sweet and
sour varieties). You can also attend lantern processions.
