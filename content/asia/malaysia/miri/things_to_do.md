---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Most visitors to Miri are passing through to Mulu or Niah, and the city itself is a one-night stopover rather than a destination. That said, Canada Hill (Bukit Kanaan) is worth an hour for the views over the city and sea, and the small Petroleum Museum at the site of Malaysia's first oil well gives context to the city's history. The Waterfront Commercial Centre along the river has seafood restaurants and evening hawker stalls. The Tamu open market, busiest on weekends, sells jungle produce and local goods. The diving off Miri is genuinely good — Miri-Sibuti Coral Reef National Park protects a series of offshore reefs with turtles, large schools of fish, and good visibility between March and September.
