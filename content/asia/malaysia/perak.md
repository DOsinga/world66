Perak is [Malaysia's](/asia/malaysia) third-largest state, stretching from the jungle-covered mountains of the Main Range down to the mangrove coast of the Strait of Malacca. The name means "silver" in Malay — a reference to the tin that once made this one of the wealthiest states in Southeast Asia. The tin boom built the capital [Ipoh](/asia/malaysia/perak/ipoh) into a grand colonial city with ornate shophouses, cave temples, and some of the best street food in Malaysia.

The Cameron Highlands, in the eastern part of the state, offer cool mountain air, tea plantations, and jungle walks — a welcome escape from the lowland heat. The Royal Belum State Park in the north is one of the oldest rainforests in the world, older than the Amazon, and home to tigers, elephants, and the enormous Rafflesia flower. Pangkor Island off the west coast has quiet beaches and fishing villages.

Perak is less visited than Penang or the east coast, but it rewards the detour. Ipoh alone — with its white coffee, its bean sprout chicken, and its limestone cave temples — is worth a stop on any trip through peninsular Malaysia.
