Sitiawan is a quiet Fuzhou Chinese settlement town in the Manjung district of Perak, about three hours north of Kuala Lumpur. Founded by Chinese immigrants from Fujian province in the early 1900s, it retains a strong Fuzhou cultural identity that sets it apart from most Malaysian towns. The dialect, the food, and the architecture all carry traces of that heritage.

The town itself is not flashy. There are no grand monuments or resort beaches. What draws visitors is the food — Sitiawan's Fuzhou cuisine is famous across Malaysia. Kong piang buns baked in clay ovens, sweet and sour fish maw soup, and fish balls stuffed with minced pork are local specialties you won't find done this well anywhere else. The food courts stay busy late into the evening.

Beyond eating, there is the small but well-kept Settlement Museum chronicling the Fuzhou pioneers, colonial-era buildings including a century-old Chinese Methodist church, and easy access to the beaches and islands of the Perak coast. [Pangkor Island](/asia/malaysia/sitiawan) is a short ferry ride from nearby Lumut. Sitiawan works best as a base for exploring this stretch of coast, or as a detour for anyone curious about Malaysia's Chinese immigrant history.
