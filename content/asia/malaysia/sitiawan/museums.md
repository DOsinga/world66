**Sitiawan Museums**

If you are holidaying in Sitiawan, **Sitiawan museums** will help you to understand and appreciate the many wonderful Chinese influences which are evident in this beautiful part of the world.

Your first stop should be the much-loved Sitiawan Settlement Museum. It is housed in a 70-year-old parsonage. A feeling of nostalgia engulfs you as you enter the museum, and you soon become aware of an atmosphere of respect and solemnity.

#### **Chinese Influence**

The Sitiawan Settlement Museum represents a proud legacy and heritage in the development of Sitiawan. The town's original residents were Chinese and escaped the poverty in their own country. Exhibits in the Sitiawan Settlement Museum include some authentic tools used for farming and forestry, early furniture, jars and bottles, period costumes and clothing, musical instruments, cameras and a photographic record of the area of Sitiawan, coal irons, educational material and church items. Such a wide and diversified collection typifies the life of an early settler. All of the items on display form an informative look into the lifestyle of the early Chinese settlers in Sitiawan, some of which is still evident in this stunning area of Malaysia. Understanding the pioneering early days of this area is important to understanding the residents today.

#### **People Power**

It is advisable to plan your visit before arriving on the doorstep of the Sitiawan Settlement Museum, as it is under the care of a volunteer organization. It was private funding that originally organized the museum, which is part of a complex offering a senior citizen&rsquo;s center, a multi-purpose hall and beautiful gardens. It is a tribute to the dedication and commitment of around 300 church parishioners.

Other sights worth seeing are the Chinese Methodist Church Building, which burned to the ground after a fire and was rebuilt in the shape of a cross and is still in use today. The Well of Salvation in Lin Chen Mei Gardens is one of the antique wells, with an ever-flowing water supply, and used to be the only source of drinking water for a period of 60 years. As the population increased, the actual number of wells also increased to cater to the needs of the community.

There are few museums available in Sitiawan.

### [Sitiawan Settlement Museum](/asia/malaysia/sitiawan/museums/sitiawan_settlemen)

**Opened in 2003, the Sitiawan
Settlement Museum was established to preserve the history and artifacts
of the Fuzhou settlers. It is housed in a converted 70-year-old church
parsonage. Out the back is the well dug by the early pioneers for their
water supply (now disused). Next door is the Chinese Methodist Church,
a small white building in the shape of a cross. Originally built in
1905, it was later rebuilt and renovated after a fire.
