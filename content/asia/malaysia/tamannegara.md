Taman Negara is [Malaysia](/asia/malaysia)'s oldest and largest national park, covering over 4,000 square kilometres of rainforest that is estimated to be 130 million years old — making it one of the oldest tropical forests on earth. The park straddles three states in the interior of Peninsular Malaysia, about four hours northeast of [Kuala Lumpur](/asia/malaysia/kualalumpur).

The main entry point is the riverside settlement of Kuala Tahan, where most accommodation and the park headquarters are located. From here, trails lead into the forest to a canopy walkway (one of the longest in the world at 530 metres), rapids for swimming, and hides where you can spend the night watching for tapir, deer and elephants at salt licks. Longer treks to Gunung Tahan, the highest peak in Peninsular Malaysia at 2,187 metres, take about a week and require a guide.

The traditional way to arrive is by boat: drive to Jerantut, then take a three-hour longboat ride upstream on the Tembeling River through the forest — a journey that is an attraction in itself. Road access to Kuala Tahan is also possible for those short on time.
