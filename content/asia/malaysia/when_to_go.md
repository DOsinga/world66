title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Malaysia is a year-round destination, but timing matters depending on where you want to go.

**Climate basics**

Malaysia is tropical -- hot, humid and wet throughout the year. Temperatures in the lowlands hover around 30-33°C during the day and rarely drop below 24°C at night. Humidity is consistently high. Rain falls year-round, typically in short, heavy afternoon downpours rather than all-day drizzle. The Cameron Highlands and hill stations are the exception, with daytime temperatures around 18-25°C.

**The monsoons**

The northeast monsoon (November to March) hits the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia hardest. During this period, most east coast islands -- Tioman, the Perhentians, Redang -- effectively close. Ferries stop running, resorts shut down, and the sea is too rough for diving or snorkeling. If beaches and islands are your priority, visit the east coast between April and October.

The west coast and Malaysian Borneo receive rain from the southwest monsoon (May to September), but the effect is much milder. [Kuala Lumpur](/asia/malaysia/kualalumpur), [Penang](/asia/malaysia/penang), [Langkawi](/asia/malaysia/langkawi) and the cities of Borneo can be visited year-round without major weather disruptions.

**Peak seasons**

The busiest periods are December through January (Christmas, New Year, Chinese New Year) and June through August (European and Middle Eastern summer holidays). During these times, popular beach resorts and highland retreats fill up and prices rise. Malaysian school holidays in March, May and August also bring domestic crowds.

The end of Ramadan (Hari Raya Aidilfitri) triggers the biggest domestic travel surge of the year. Transport and accommodation across the country book out as Malaysians travel home. Check the dates and plan accordingly.

**Best time overall**

For the most flexibility, visit between March and early June or September and October. You will have access to both coasts, reasonable weather across the country, and fewer crowds than the peak seasons.
