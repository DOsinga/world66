Rasdhoo is a small inhabited island at the northern tip of Ari Atoll, about 56 kilometres west of [Malé](/asia/maldives/male). Unlike the resort islands nearby, Rasdhoo is a local island — you stay in guesthouses rather than resorts, eat at local cafés, and get a sense of everyday Maldivian life.

The island is tiny — you can walk around it in twenty minutes — but the diving and snorkelling are outstanding. The Rasdhoo Channel is famous for hammerhead shark sightings on early-morning dives, and the house reef is reachable by a short swim from the beach. A sandbank appears at low tide just off the island, and excursions to nearby uninhabited islands and resort reefs are easy to arrange.

Rasdhoo is one of the more accessible local islands for independent travellers, with regular speedboat and ferry connections from Malé. Several guesthouses offer dive packages. It is a good choice if you want the Maldives experience — white sand, warm water, spectacular marine life — without the resort price tag.
