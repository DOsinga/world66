The Maldives is like nowhere else on earth — 1,190 coral islands scattered across 90,000 square kilometres of the Indian Ocean, so flat that even a modest sea-level rise could claim much of the country within decades. The highest point in the entire nation barely clears two metres above sea level. That fragility is part of what gives the place its particular intensity: this beauty feels borrowed.

Most visitors come for the water. The Indian Ocean here is warm, clear, and stacked with coral reefs, sharks, rays, and more species of fish than you'll have names for. Diving and snorkelling are world-class at every atoll. Overwater bungalows perched above the lagoon have become the defining image of the place — and the reality does not disappoint.

The country divides neatly into two worlds. Resort islands operate as self-contained bubbles where alcohol flows, swimwear is fine, and everything is included in a price that would pay for a month in Southeast Asia. Local islands — home to the 500,000 or so Maldivians who aren't in the tourism trade — are conservative Muslim communities where modest dress is expected, alcohol is absent, and the rhythms of daily life have little to do with the reef.

The capital [Malé](/asia/maldives/male_atoll/male) is one of the most densely populated cities on earth, crammed onto an island barely two square kilometres in size. It's worth a day or two: good food, a lively waterfront, and the Grand Friday Mosque. From here, seaplanes and speedboats fan out to resorts and guesthouses across the atolls.

The Maldives is not a budget destination, but it has become more accessible in recent years as guesthouse tourism on local islands has grown. Visiting an inhabited island and staying in a locally run guesthouse gives you a very different and arguably more honest experience than a week in a resort bubble — and costs a fraction of the price.

## Destinations

[Malé Atoll](/asia/maldives/male_atoll) — the capital atoll, home to [Malé](/asia/maldives/male_atoll/male) and many resorts within easy reach of the airport.

[Ari Atoll](/asia/maldives/ari_atoll) — one of the most popular dive destinations, known for whale sharks and manta rays year-round.

[South Malé Atoll](/asia/maldives/south_male_atoll) — close to the capital, quieter, with some excellent house reefs.
