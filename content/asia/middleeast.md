The Middle East is the cradle of civilization. The people who lived in the valley of the Tigris and Eufrates and in the Levant were the first to use the alphabet.


 The most visited countries in the region are probably [Turkey](/asia/middleeast/turkey) with its palaces and mosques in [Istanbul](/asia/middleeast/turkey/istanbul) and its beaches on the Mediterranean and Agean coast and [Israel](/asia/middleeast/israel) with its good tourist infrastructure, the city of [Jerusalem](/asia/middleeast/israel/jerusalem) and the Dead Sea.


 [Jordan](/asia/middleeast/jordan), [Syria](/asia/middleeast/syria) and [Lebanon](/asia/middleeast/lebanon) are easy to visit as well. They are compact countries with lots of sights on a small surface and a friednly population.


 The countries of the Gulf are somewhat less visited. [Oman](/asia/middleeast/oman), [United Arab Emirates](/asia/middleeast/unitedarabemirates), [Qatar](/asia/middleeast/qatar), Bahrein, [Kuwait](/asia/middleeast/kuwait) and [Saudi Arabia](/asia/middleeast/saudiarabia) have all benefited from the oil boom. Their societiesare still pretty conservative though.


 [Yemen](/asia/middleeast/yemen) is located at the southern tip of the Arabian peninsula. The country is extremely beautiful, travel can be a bit tough, however.


 [Iran](/asia/middleeast/iran) has started to open up and is rapidly becoming one of the most interesting detsinations in the regions. [Isfahan](/asia/middleeast/iran/isfahan), [Tabriz](/asia/middleeast/iran/tabriz) and [Shiraz](/asia/middleeast/iran/shiraz) are all very old cities with beautiful old centres.


 [Iraq](/asia/middleeast/iraq) is hard to get to and due to the political climate you should ask yourself twice if you really want to. It does have many interesting historical sights, however.
