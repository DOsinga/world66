Cyprus, in the Eyewitness Travel Guides series by Paphos
Books, offers a decent guide book for a trip to Cyprus. The Eyewitness
Travel Guide series specializes in a colorful and bright format that
features lots of pictures, old and new, and maps to give you an idea of
what traveling there is like. You could argue that it's a
better companion for the armchair traveler than the one who actually
visits places like Cyprus. However, the book still has a lot of
worthwhile information, so don't throw it out offhand.

**Contents**

The
guide starts out with a history of Cyprus, then breaks it down region
by region, beginning with a brief overview before getting slightly more
in-depth, breaking it down into west Cyprus, which covers southern
Cyprus and the Troodos
Mountains, central Cyprus, north Cyprus and South Nicosia. The next
major section is devoted to where to stay and what to do during your
stay in Cyprus. The Cyprus Eyewitness Travel Guide offers tips on where
to eat, where to shop, entertainment and outdoor activities. The most
useful section might be at the end of the book, which offers up the
practical and travel information. Also included at the end is a handy
phrase book, which, although it is probably worthwhile to invest in a
more substantive phrasebook, will do in a pinch.Taken as a whole, this Cyprus travel guide is useful as an overview of what you should know if you're going to visit Cyprus, or as a good way to decide before you leave whether you really want to travel there. At $20 list price, it's not a substantial investment, and the pictures alone make it entertaining to flip through.
