This **Troodos Mountains practical information **guide can help give you an understanding of what you should know before visiting this mountain range located in the center of Cyprus. The Troodos Mountains are the largest mountain chain in Cyprus. Rising to just over 1,900 meters, Mount Olympus is the highest peak.

**Getting Here**

To navigate to the foot of the mountains, you'll need to first fly into Cyprus. Most major carriers offer service here. It is about an hour drive from the airport to the Troodos. You can make this drive by hiring a private driver or by booking a ride on the public bus line.You can hike the trails anytime of the year, as the region tends to have a dry and sunny climate year round, but avoiding the rainy season of November to March may give you a better experience.

**Currency
**

The Euro is the formal form of currency. The Euro tends to be just a bit stronger than the dollar, as it takes roughly $1.23 US dollars to get one Euro.

** Language**

Modern Greek is the official language, while English is the unofficial second language, and many locals strive to perfect their English. This makes getting around simple for English speaking tourists.

**Safety**

You'll want to avoid taking photos in places where "No Photography" signs are posted. Police will be nearby, and this law is strictly enforced. Though crime here is low, you should avoid "cabarets," as these are seedy establishments, which also have a history of inflating bar tabs and threatening violence if the patron refuses to pay.
