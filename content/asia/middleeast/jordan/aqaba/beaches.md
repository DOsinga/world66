There is a wide mix of **Aqaba Beaches**, ranging from
the snobbish, private beach-spots to the more accessible public beaches.
Aqaba is Jordan’s only city with a substantial coastline. Hence,
thousands of native visitors apart from the foreign visitors can be
found soaking-up the fun at Aqaba beaches.

**Overview of Aqaba Public Beaches**

Most of the public beaches are found along Aqaba’s south coast. This
is an easy-to-reach area, located at a distance of few minutes from the
popular downtown area. All the local beaches are under the jurisdiction
of the Aqaba Marine Park. All the beaches in Aqaba can be broadly
divided into 3, main beach zones—north beach zone, middle beach zone
and south beach zone. The most popular beaches across these zones
include:

**Internationally Popular Beaches: Barracuda Beach**

This is one of Aqaba’s most popular and affordable diving centers.
The Barracuda Diving Club is administered by professional divers. The
diving community here consists of native and international divers. The
diving gear and diving instructors are easily available. This is
probably the safest spot in Aqaba for learning the basics of deep-sea
diving. It also consists of a luxurious beach-house where entry is
strictly through a personal recommendation from the existing members.
The Barracuda is also popular for its globally-renowned snorkeling
facilities. Many times, local snorkeling events are held that often
engage international participation. The sandy spread is maintained in
the most hygienic manner. However, the rush can be maddening at times.
Those looking for a more peaceful spot can rent a hammock or enter the
lounge area. Other easily-available rentals include banana-shaped boat
buggies. Visitors can also catch a game of beach volleyball.
- **Location: **southern beach zone, Aqaba, Jordan 77110.

**Recently-opened Beaches: Palm Beach**

The most recently-opened public beach-spot is the Al-Hafayer Beach.
It was previously called the Al-Ghandoor Beach. This is the only
beachfront in Aqaba that is located along the interior of the city. Many
locals refer to it as the Palm Beach also. This beach isn't the most
suitable location for international travelers. It is administered by
some conservative rules. Women in swimming gear are not allowed.
This is the most unusually-located beachfront in Aqaba, being surrounded
by historical building on all sides. It provides an amazing view of
historic spots such as the Great Arab Revolt Square. Toward the east
lies the crop gardens that develop into an exotic, bright-green shade during
the growing season.
- **Direction:** east from the Big Flag, near the Royal Yacht
Club.

**Best of Family Beaches: Tala Bay**

This beach-spot is preferred by families vacationing in Aqaba. It is
located in the vicinity of some affordable, accommodation facilities.
The beachfront is massive and hence, the usual jostling-for-space is
less prevalent. There is a golf-course along the southern-end of the
Tala. Part of the Tala Bay complex consists of a marina that serves as
the entry point to Jordan’s mainland. This spot consists of some
road-side shopping options that are worth a visit, offering some
amazingly affordable deals.
- **Location: **South Beach Road, southern beach zone, Aqaba.
