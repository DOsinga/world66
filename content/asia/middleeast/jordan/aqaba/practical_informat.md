This **Aqaba practical information **is very useful when you are going to visit the only seaport in Jordan. The city boasts great diving and noteworthy beach resorts. There's much to see in this town, but knowing more about the place makes your stay worthwhile. Here are a few important things to be familiar with that are practical when you stay in this town.

**Visa Requirements**

In order to get to Aqaba, you must be a holder of a visa valid in Jordan. Apart from this, you must have a valid passport in order to obtain a Jordanian visa. You may opt to get an advance visa from the nearest consulate in your area and you need to fill in an application form and submit a photo. You can also opt to get the visa at the border entering Jordan, which is much easier, wherein you don't need to fill up a form or submit a photo. A single entry visa would cost around $15, and for multiple entries, around $30. You have to take note that border visas are not available at the Allenby/King Hussein Bridge crossing with Israel. Thus if you are traveling by this route, you need to have a visa beforehand.

**Basic Facts**

Aqaba has two different time zones being followed, depending on the time of year. During winter time, October to March time zone is GMT+2, while in the summer, April to September time zone is GMT+3. The currency of Aqaba is the Jordanian Dinar or JD. One JD is equal to $1.409 or $1 is equivalent to 0.7095 JD.
