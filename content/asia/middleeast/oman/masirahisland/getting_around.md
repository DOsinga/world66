---
title: "Getting Around in Masirah Island"
type: section
---

From the lush beaches of Salalah to the rocky shores of Masirah
Island, Oman has begun to be noticed as one of the Middle East's great
destinations for tourists and travelers. Located on the Southwest tip
of the Arabian Peninsula, Oman's coastline reaps the benefits of the
breezes blowing off of the Arabian Sea and the Gulf of Oman, a welcome
respite from the otherwise hot and arid region.

If you're going to fly into Oman chances are you will fly into Seeb
International Airport in Muscat, the capitol city. Although you can fly
into Salhalah, Muscat is actually easier because they are more
accustomed to handling tourists and visas. Once you are there you have
a few options to travel around the country. Oman Air is the national
airline and travels regularly between Muscat and Salhalah. Since
Masirah Island is located closer to Muscat, however, it makes more
sense to just travel by land. A bus service connects all the major
destinations in Oman. Other options include renting a car or taking a
taxi.

To actually get to Masirah Island you have to take the ferry. It
runs fairly regularly and will drop you off by the town of Hilf. Most
of the Hotels on the Island are located near the ferry, which is very
nice. There are paved roads on the island, but if you really want to be
able to explore it is worthwhile to invest in renting some kind of
4-wheel drive vehicle.
