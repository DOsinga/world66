There are really no major festivals that take place on the Musandam
peninsula. The people, of course, celebrate the major Islamic and national
holidays, but if you're looking for a big event you would be better
served by making the trek south to other parts of Oman. The peninsula is
part of Oman, but geographically it is attached to the United Arab
Emirates. The main attraction of the Musandam peninsula is its coastline. The movement of the Earth's crust has created a network of fjords on the Musandam peninsula that resemble the fjord's of Northern Europe. If you are vacationing on the Musandam peninsula, however, you may want to explore some of the festivals in other cities of Oman, which are fairly easily accessible from the peninsula.

**Muscat Festival**

The easiest place to get to from the Musandam peninsula is Muscat. It is connected to Khasab, the capital city of the Musandam
peninsula, by the world's fastest diesel powered ferry service, that
can make the 200 nautical mile journey in as little as 6 hours. The
Muscat Festival is the city's highlight of the year and occurs annually at the beginning of each year. The Muscat
Festival is a great place to absorb the culture of the region, as you
soak up local art, food and music. Other major events in the capital
city include the Shopping Festival, held annually in September and the
birthday of Sultan Qaboos, which is a big celebration featuring music
and camel races. The birthday of the Sultan, or National Day, occurs in
November. Holidays to avoid, unless you are celebrating them for religious
purposes, are the Islamic New Year and the month of Ramadan, although
there is a national holiday at the end of Ramadan.

**Khareef Festival**

While Salalah is definitely more of a trek than Muscat if you are vacationing on the Musandam
peninsula, this southern city in Oman is home to one of nature's own
festivals--and the city knows how to celebrate it. Every year the
monsoon season soaks Salalah from late June until the beginning of
September, and by the end of the season this desert city is the closest thing
to the tropics that the region will ever see. A month-long festival,
attracting people from all over the area, celebrates the natural beauty
brought on by the monsoons. The festival features shopping, music, arts
and more, making it well worth the journey from the Musandam.

### [Martin Hill](/asia/middleeast/oman/musandampeninsula/festivals/martin_hill)

**The Sohar Music Festival **is held during 3 weekends in late Oct./early November. It provides a platform for both local and international musicians performing music from a variety of genres. Further details are available from the festival's website.

Sohar is located on the coast, midway between Dubai and Muscat.
