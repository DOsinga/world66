---
title: "When to Go in Doha"
type: section
---

Doha has a primarily arid climate since it is located in the Arabian Peninsula
desert region, and the temperature sometimes soars to over 40 degrees C in
scorching summers that have fluctuating levels of humidity. The city also has an
interesting line-up of festivals and shopping events, enticing tourists to come here to
enjoy the spirit of the Middle East. Here is a guide to the best time to visit
this buzzing Qatar Capital.

**Climate **

From May to September, which are the peak summer months in Doha, the average
temperature races to over 40 degrees C. The arid and humid weather also
makes it extremely tiring and stressful to venture outdoors during summer months.
There is next to no precipitation in summers in Doha and about less than an inch
during the other seasons. If you are making your decision of when to visit based on climate, the best time to visit the city is from
October to May, when Doha experiences pleasant winters characterized by
occasional, scarce showers. The average rainfall in Doha is approximately 70 mm
per year. The temperature on wintery nights rarely plunges below 7 degrees, so
it’s not freezing cold for tourists to move outdoors.

**Ramadan **

The holy fasting month of Ramadan is a great time to visit Doha. Though
shops, restaurants, bars and other entertainment zones may be shut or have
limited hours of operation, this is indeed a great time to witness the religious and cultural
customs of the city. The month-long fasting season gives Doha a look of
festivity, enthusiasm and brotherhood and you can spot several locals sharing
delicious hot meals at dusk, when the day long fast ends. The streets of the
city waffle with an unmistaken aroma of meat kabobs, local bread and traditional
desserts. The exact month of the festival varies every year according to the
Lunar Calendar; however it generally falls between September and December.

**Shopping Events**

The International Trade Fair (ITF) is the largest and most widely attended
shopping festival in Doha. The hugely successful consumer event sees various
brands from different niches coming together under one roof at the Qatar
International Exhibition Center. Exhibits include electronics, house hold items,
apparel, furniture, luggage and host of other international products offered at heavily
discounted prices and, sometimes, bargain deals are offered. The annual event is generally held in the months
of January or February.

**Emir GCC Camels Race**

The annual Camels Race, held in April, is an exciting sporting event in the
country and sees several well-heeled Arab camel owners participate in the event
and enthusiastically cheer for their pets. Visitors all over the world come to
the Middle East to see the famous camel races.

The best time to visit Doha is during its pleasant winters when visitors can enjoy and observe shopping events,
religious festivals and traditional sporting events that are famous all over the
world.
