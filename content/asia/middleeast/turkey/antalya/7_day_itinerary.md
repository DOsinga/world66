This **Antalya Itinerary** guide gives you a day by day plan for
an entire week when visiting the city of Antalya that lies on the
Mediterranean coast of Turkey. It is a well-known tourist hub as there
is much to see and do in Antalya ranging from nights out at bars and
restaurants to days filled with visits to shopping zones, monuments and
pristine beaches!

**Day 1- Sample Turkish Fare**

A not-to-be-skipped activity in Antalya is the satiating of your
taste buds with Turkey’s delectable local delights such as ‘meze,’ which
is something like an entrée and includes spicy salsas and dips that are
eaten along with crusty baguettes and seafood. Other local delicacies
include the famous Turkish kebabs, available in fresh fish, lamb and
other meats. The restaurant Villa Perla offers all of the above along
with a good dose of Turkish desserts and drinks to wash all that food
down.

**Day 2- Hadrian’s Gate**

Visit the famous Hadrian’s Gate, which is a monumental arch built as a
tribute to the Roman emperor Hadrianus. Comprised of 3 arched gates,
this architectural beauty has a very interesting story behind it. You
can learn all about it on your visit here.

**Day 3- Hit the Beach**

Visit the Side Beach for a day out in the sun and a whole lot of
powdery white sand that stretches for over 2 km. The beaches are full of
sea-facing restaurants and cafes, where you’ll find every cuisine to
suit your taste. Plus, you’ll also find a good number of water sports
here. While in the area, a must visit are the Roman and Greek ruins

**Day 4- Go Uphill**

Visit Tunek Tepe, a well-known hillock region on the western side of
Antalya. It is 618 meters high, and at the peak you will find a fabulous
rotating restaurant and bar called Doner Gazino. The view from the
restaurant is spectacular as one is able to see the Antalya Gulf and the
surrounding mountainous area.

**Day 5- Go Diving**

If you haven’t had enough of Antalya’s beaches, visit the Turkish
Riviera where you can go for an underwater scuba dive. You will be able
to check out age old sea wrecks, caves and many interesting under water
creatures here.

**Day 6- Visit a Holy Place**

The Yivli Minare Mosque in Antalya is known for its incredible
architecture. The mosque that was built in 1230 has a fluted minaret
that is adorned with gleaming blue tiles.

**Day 7- Kaleici**

Located at the center of Antalya City, Kaleici is a great place for
shopping and houses superb restaurants and bars. It’s no wonder that
this area has become a haven for tourists.
