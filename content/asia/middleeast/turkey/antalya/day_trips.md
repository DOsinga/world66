---
title: "Day Trips in Antalya"
type: section
---

There are so many worthy **Antalya Day Trips**, that it may be
really hard for you to choose one. Antalya is a city surrounded by a
number of interesting historical places and there is never enough time
to see them all. That's why you'd better look for some combined day trip
offers, which cover more than one location. Here are some of the
must-sees:

**Demre**

Demre is the contemporary name of the ancient Lycian town of Myra. It
is located west of Antalya and is easily accessible by car. This place
is very popular with its rock-cut tombs, part of an ancient Lycian
Necropolis. The antique Roman Theatre and Roman Baths are also an
impressive sight. You should see the church of St. Nicholas. He was the
bishop of Myra in the 4th century, who is said to be the real Santa
Claus.

**Kekova**** ****Island**

If you're not tired, you can go to the harbor of Demre and hire a
boat to take you to Kekova Island. On the way to the island you can dive
and see the ruins of the sunken city of Simena. After that, you can
take a look at the Lycian Necropolis Teimiussa. It is a good idea to
combine visiting Demre and Kekova, but it may seem a bit hasty for some
tourists, so if you feel that way, just spend two days on viewing them
instead of one.

**Termessos**

The ruins of the ancient town of Termessos are another good day trip
choice. They are located just 30km northwest of Antalya, high in the
mountains within the Güllük Dagi National Park. You'll have to pay a
small fee to enter. After viewing the ruins, you can visit the small
museum with ancient artifacts from the site. You can also travel to the
nearby Karain Cave.

**Perge**

Perge, often called Perga, is one of the major tourist attractions.
An impressive example of an ancient metropolitan city, Perge is said to
be the oldest settlement found in the region. The ruins on the site are
from the Hellenistic and Roman eras. It is a great place to visit.

**Boat ****Tours**

If you're not interested in all these ruins at all, you can always go
to the seaside of Antalya and hire a boat to take you on tour. There is
a wide variety of tour choices: diving, shark fishing, swimming or just
sailing on the boat. Whatever you choose, it surely won't be boring.
