**Welcome to Izmir**

Izmir is the third most populated city in Turkey and it rivals
Istanbul as the major port city of the country. Izmir is a city where
the new and old coexist happily and the Eastern and Western cultures
meet together in a relaxed beach setting. Izmir is well known for the
international arts festival that take place in the summer.

**Getting There by Plane**

The Adnan Menderes International Airport (IATA airport code: ADB)
serves Izmir. Several international and local airline companies offer
flights to and from Izmir. British Airways, Monarch Airlines and
Thomson Airways offer direct flights to and from England. Thomas Cook
Airlines offers direct flights to and from Brussels in Belgium.
Germanwings, Hamburg International, Lufthansa, Pegasus, SunExpress, and
Turkish Airlines offer direct flights to and from Germany. Also, there
are several local airlines such as Atlasjet that connect Izmir with
other cities in Turkey. After you arrive to Izmir, you have the option
to take one of the several shuttle buses that make several stops at
different key locations in Izmir. There are also taxis available but
they are quite expensive.

**Getting There by Bus**

Located in the Altindaq borough, Otogar is the bus terminal that
serves the city of Izmir. Across Turkey there are several local bus
companies that offer trips to and from Izmir. It is important that at
the time of booking you inquire about the stops that the bus will make
once it arrives at Izmir because it is common that bus companies have
several branch offices across the city.
