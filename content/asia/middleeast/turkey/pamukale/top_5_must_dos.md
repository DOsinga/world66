---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Pamukale"
type: section
---

In this natural site of Turkey, there are loads of **Pamukale Must Do&rsquo;s.** In fact, Pamukale, which means "cotton castle" in Turkish. It lies in the Turkish province of Denizli. Tourists come here to enjoy a variety of activities like sightseeing and shopping and also for the good climate. Here are a list of top 5 must-do&rsquo;s when visiting the natural Turkish site of Pamukale.

**1. Hot Springs**

A visit to the hot springs of Pamukale is a must when visiting Turkey. The water in the springs of Pamukale contains traces of calcium bicarbonate. Further, these thermal springs are naturally heated at 33 degrees C. This offers a relaxing and therapeutic experience to those who immerse themselves in the hot springs. These hot springs are believed to have healing properties, since in ancient times, people came from all over to bathe in these waters which are rich in minerals. The hot spring water is said to cure diseases and ailments.

**2. Hierapolis**

Visit the remnants of an ancient Turkish city called Hierapolis. Interestingly, Hierapolis has been named as a World Heritage Site and is frequented by a good number of tourists. You will be able to see ruins of beautiful pillars and columns as well as a thermal pool which contains artifacts from the time Hierapolis was a full-fledged city

**3. The Necropolis**

Visit the largest and best preserved graveyards in Anatolia known as the Necropolis. It consists of around 1,200-odd graves. You will notice that a good number of grave stones have carved inscriptions as well as decorative designs, depicting the person who passed away.

**4. Hierapolis Museum**

Visit the Hierapolis Archaeology Museum that was built in 1984. It is houses in the former Roman Bath zone of the city of Hierapolis. You will get to observe wonderful historical artifacts from the time of Hierapolis, as well as antiquities from other ancient cities like Tripolis and Colossae. Further, the Hierapolis Museum also houses some great specimens of arts and crafts from the time of the Bronze Age.

**5. Laodikya**

Visit Laodikya which is a site that has been mentioned in the Bible. It lies on the Denizli Road. In the Bible, Laodikya was noted as one of the 7 churches of the Revelations. But, be prepared, because Laodikya has not been maintained very well nor has it been reconstructed. However, from here you will be able to enjoy a beautiful view of the ruins and have a great photographic opportunity.
