---
title: "Beaches in Side"
type: section
---

Side is a pleasant Turkish town with an incredible mix of ancient
ruins, and important historic places.** Side beaches **include modern beach resort amenities
like bars, restaurants, and discos. The town is lined on both sides by
wide, white sandy beaches which are perfect for sunbathing, making
sandcastles and doing a variety of fun water sports. The great thing
about Side is that you can spend all morning relaxing at the fantastic
beaches in a posh, resort setting, and then in the afternoon, you can
head inland and enjoy all of the fascinating ancient Roman and Greek
features that made the town such an important one in the past.
Basically, there are only two main beaches, both of which are very big
and picturesque, the East Beach and the West Beach.

**The East Beach**

The East Beach is a long, wide, sandy beach that runs along the
coast of Side until the inlet of the Manavgat River. There are plenty
of amenities including umbrellas and lounge chairs to make your day at
the beach as enjoyable as possible. The water is warm, but a bit rougher
than that of the western beach, but water sports such as fishing,
water skiing, and parasailing are still popular with tourists. Be
aware that during the tourist season, which occurs during July and
August, this beach can get crowded, and it is not as nice as it
is during other months.

**The West Beach**

This 30 kilometer stretch of sand goes from Side to Colaki. It is
lined by great little restaurants and cafes, and is the more popular of
the two beaches. The water is warm and shallow, and because of this,
the West Beach is the best beach to bring young children as it is
safer. Water sports at this beach include swimming, diving, snorkeling,
water skiing and para sailing. There are also some great boat tours
that travel around Side. Much of the year this beach is crowded,
especially during tourist season, but if you get away from the crowds
you can often find some nice, secluded spots, some of which are
actually turtle beaches.

**When To Go**

Since so many people flood the area in July and August, it is better
to visit the beaches of Side either before the tourist season begins,
or after it ends. Until June, the weather and the water can be
cold, so the best months to go are either June, September or October.
