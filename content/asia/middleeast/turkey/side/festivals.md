title: "Festivals in Side"
type: section
---

Istanbul is the major city in Turkey that hosts almost all of the
international festivals, including **Side Festivals**. However, the small town of Side does indeed
have one very important festival that is celebrated every year. It is
called the Side Culture and Art Festival. Sponsored by the many luxury
resorts, shops, restaurants, and cultural organizations in the area,
this lively and colorful festival is a hit with locals and tourists
every year. The festival takes place in September, usually the
beginning of September, and features a huge variety of concerts by
classical orchestras, chamber groups, jazz bands, and piano soloists,
as well as folk music and dance of Turkey. In addition, many art
collections are displayed. Some are meant purely for public viewing,
while others display pieces that can be purchased. There is local
Turkish art on display as well as international works. The Side Culture
and Art Festival is the main festival in the town, but there are other
entertaining festivals in nearby towns that are worth a visit.

**Nearby Festivals**

In Aspendos, there is an annual international festival of opera,
ballet and classical music that takes place in the months of June and
July. The main events of the festival take place in the Aspendos
Theater, which is the best preserved Roman amphitheater in all of
Europe. This festival has been going on since 1994. In the beach side
town of Alanya, there is another International Culture and Art Festival
that occurs from October 3rd to the 5th. It is very similar to the Side
Culture and Art Festival.

**Antalya **

Antalya is another town located right on the coast and has plenty
of festivals all year round. In the beginning of May, there is the
Antalya Music Festival, which is basically for the wealthy, vacationing
foreigners to enjoy. It goes on for a week and features tons of artists
from the United Kingdom and a few from Istanbul as well. It seems at
times like a private event, as it is still not very popular and there
are not huge crowds of people. Many times people who attend the
festival will get a chance to talk personally with the artists. The
last week of September, Antalya also holds the Antalya Golden Orange
Film Festival, which is Turkey's biggest national film festival. The
last week of Augustis the Antalya International Folk Music and Dance
Festival and Competition, while in May, there is the annual Flower
Festival.
