---
title: "Shopping in Abu dhabi"
type: section
---

Abu Dhabi has a stunning confluence of traditional and contemporary
shopping destinations that cater to a cosmopolitan tourist and
expatriate crowd. Visitors can pick up anything from exquisite antique
carpets from a local bazaar to designer wear at the swanky boutiques
housed in plush shopping malls. Most high-end international brands have
their outlets in Abu Dhabi and there are also several markets selling
locally crafted artifacts. The duty free shopping helps visitors bag
high-end stuff at some of the lowest prices and bargain offers. Here is
a comprehensive guide to buying in this buzzing UAE capital.

**Local Souks**

Souks are traditional Arabic markets that are housed in the narrow
by lanes and alleys of Abu Dhabi. The colorful and vibrant ‘local
bazaar’ ambiance is accentuated by the vivid artifacts, spices, local
fabric, antiques, carpets, ceramic figures and a host of other
hand-crafted and locally produced items. One of the most frequented
souks in Abu Dhabi is the beautiful Iranian Souk, which is a must visit
more for the regional and cultural experience than the goods on sale.
The Al Ain Souk sports a run down and rugged appeal, which makes it
highly exotic and endearing. For the best local carpets in the city,
visit the Al Meena Souk.

**Marina Mall**

Marina is a popular mall located near Emirates Palace on the
Corniche Breakwater and houses several world-class outlets including
Versace, Louis Vuitton, Gucci, Channel, Yves Saint Lurent, IKEA and
host of other top international brands. Carrefour-a major hypermarket
in Abu Dhabi is also located inside the mall. There is a nine-screen
multiplex and an array of global fast food chains and local restaurants.

**The Abu Dhabi Mall**

Located in a popular spot near the Rotana Hotel, The Abu Dhabi Mall
is one of the most crowded malls in the city and features over 230
stores that include brands like Espirit, Tommy Hilfiger, Body Shop,
Betty Barclay, Virgin Megastore and many more. There are over 50
well-known global food chains and regional eateries along with a
six-screen multiplex and a huge hypermarket. There is also an
action-packed entertainment center and a bowling alley.

**Al Ain Mall**

The Al Ain Mall houses a large number of international brands along
with more than 30 food outlets. The mall is popular due of its ice
skating rink and a sprawling bowling center.

In addition to these there are other shopping destinations like Al
Jimi Mall, Al Ain City Center, Lulu Hypermarket, Lamcy Plaza, City
Center, Liwa Center, Gold Center and a host of other malls, shopping
centers and hypermarkets.
