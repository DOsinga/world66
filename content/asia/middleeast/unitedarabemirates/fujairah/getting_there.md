---
title: "Getting There in Fujairah"
type: section
---

The small emirate of Fujairah is a bustling business center. Not meant
for tourists and sightseeing, the primary purpose of this city is for
corporate meetings and conventions. Traveling to and around Fujairah
can be done in a number of ways depending on your preference. When in
Fujairah, keep in mind that the majority of the city center consists of
roads--and pedestrian and bike traffic is kept at a minimum.

**Traveling by Plane

**Fujairah does have a small airport but is currently closed to
commercial and passenger aviation. If traveling to Fujairah by plane,
you will need to book a flight into 1 of the surrounding emirates and
plan a short ride into Fujairah by bus or train.

**Traveling by Bus

**Buses shuttle tourists from other emirates to Fujairah on a daily
basis. Buses also run on a regular basis within the city. Affordable
and comfortable, this is the way to go if planning a short trip to the
United Arab Emirates as travel is punctual and easy.

**Traveling by Taxi

**Taxis run all over Fujairah, shuttling locals and tourists all over
the city as well as to and from neighboring emirates. More expensive
than taking a bus, a taxi drive from Fujairah to Dubai will cost an
average of $30. Sharing a taxi is definitely the way to go
to split up the cost.

**Traveling by Car

**Fujairah is only a 2-hour drive from Dubai, so if you're flying into this
city and you would rather have your own mode of transportation, renting
a car in Dubai might be the solution for you. Keep in mind that if you
plan on traveling outside the UAE to Oman or any other neighboring
country, you will need a separate visa when crossing the border.
