Khovd (also Hovd) is the largest city in western [Mongolia](/asia/mongolia), a dusty administrative capital on the Buyant River with the snow-capped Altai Mountains rising to the south and west. It is the main gateway for travellers heading into the Mongolian Altai — one of the most remote and spectacular mountain ranges in Central Asia.

The city itself is functional rather than beautiful: a Soviet-era grid of apartment blocks and government buildings, a lively market, and a small museum. But the surroundings are extraordinary. Eagle hunters of the Kazakh minority practise their ancient art in the valleys south of town. The Altai Tavan Bogd National Park, with Mongolia's highest peak and ancient Turkic petroglyphs, is reachable from here.

Getting to Khovd involves either a long flight from [Ulaanbaatar](/asia/mongolia/ulaanbaatar) or an even longer drive across the steppe. Once here, you are genuinely remote — and that is the point.
