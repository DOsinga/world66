Amarapura -- the “City of Immortals” -- was the royal capital of Burma from 1783, home to 200,000 people at its peak. Today fewer than 10,000 remain, most of them cotton and silk weavers working at looms in workshops you can visit along the main road.

The main draw is U Bein Bridge, a 1.2-kilometre teak footbridge built around 1850 that stretches across Taungthaman Lake. At sunset, when monks and locals cross in silhouette against the water, it is one of the most photographed scenes in Myanmar. Get there early or late to avoid the tour groups. Scattered palace ruins and several gilded pagodas fill out a half-day visit. Amarapura sits about 10 km south of [Mandalay](/asia/myanmar/mandalay) and is easily combined with the other ancient capitals of Inwa and Sagaing.
