Kalaw is a former British hill station perched on the rim of the Shan Plateau at about 1,320 metres elevation, in a landscape of pine forests and terrace-farmed valleys. The British are said to have been so fond of it that they declared "If we could take Kalaw with us, we would!" when leaving Myanmar. The mock-Tudor colonial bungalows and cooler temperatures still give it a different character from most Burmese towns.

The main reason travellers come today is trekking. Kalaw is the most popular starting point for the two-to-three-day walk to Inle Lake, passing through villages of Danu, Palaung, Pa-O, and other ethnic minority communities. Guides can be arranged easily in town. The surrounding countryside also has good day-hike options through the pine forests.

The town itself is pleasant to wander. The market draws traders from surrounding hill villages and is at its most colourful early in the morning.
