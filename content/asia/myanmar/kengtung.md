Kengtung (Chiang Tung in Shan) is a highland town in eastern Shan State, set in a broad valley surrounded by hills and accessible overland from the Thai border crossing at Tachilek. For years it was closed to foreigners, and it retains a feel of isolation — this is far from the main tourist circuits of Myanmar.

The town is a treasure trove of traditional Shan architecture, with old wooden houses featuring intricately carved balconies lining quiet lanes. The most sacred site is the Maha Myat Muni Buddha image, housed in a temple complex in the centre of town. Other important monasteries include Sun Sali, Sun Taung, and Sun Lwe, each worth visiting for their traditional Shan-style wooden construction. The central market draws hill tribe villagers from the surrounding mountains and is one of the most colourful in the region.

Kengtung is a base for trekking to Akha, Lahu, and Ann hill tribe villages in the surrounding hills, though a licensed guide is required. The town is connected to Tachilek by a winding mountain road that takes several hours.
