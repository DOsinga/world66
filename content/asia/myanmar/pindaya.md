**Pindaya** is a small, unhurried town in the Shan Hills of central Myanmar, set around a lake called Nattamiekan (Angels Lake). It is worth a stop on the route between [Bagan](/asia/myanmar/bagan) or [Mandalay](/asia/myanmar/mandalay) and [Inle Lake](/asia/myanmar/inlelake), and many travellers pass through in a day — though the town rewards an overnight stay.

The main attraction is the Shwe Oo Min Paya cave complex, which burrows into a limestone cliff above the lake. Inside, more than 8,000 Buddha images have been placed over the centuries by pilgrims and donors. They range from tiny gilded figures to large marble statues, filling every chamber and alcove in an effect that is genuinely extraordinary. A covered stairway leads up to the cave entrance; a mechanical lift is also available.

The town itself is a centre for Shan umbrella-making, and you can watch craftspeople produce the lacquered paper parasols in workshops along the main street.
