**Taunggyi** is the administrative capital of Shan State and Myanmar's fourth-largest city, after Yangon, Mandalay, and Mawlamyine. The name means "big hill" — an understatement for the dramatic scarp slope on which the town perches, looking out over the Shan Plateau.

The market is the highlight. Colourfully dressed ethnic groups from across the Shan hills converge here, and on a good morning it feels like a walking museum of Shan culture. Taunggyi is also home to some of Myanmar's finest cheroot factories, if you want to see how those distinctive green cigars are rolled.

The **Shan State Museum** (also called the Taunggyi Museum) has a good collection of tribal costumes, weapons, and artefacts, including antique opium weights and musical instruments — look for the snakeskin guitar. The town also serves as a base for trips to Inle Lake, about an hour's drive to the southwest.
