---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Street food is the most rewarding way to eat in Yangon. In the evenings, vendors spread out along most major downtown streets selling Burmese noodle dishes, Indian-style curries, tea-leaf salads, and grilled skewers. A full meal rarely costs more than a dollar or two. Anawratha Street between 31st and 34th is a reliable strip for both Burmese and Indian food.

Burmese cuisine centres on rice and a rotating selection of slow-cooked curries, typically served with multiple side dishes and a clear soup. Indian food is also widely available downtown, a legacy of the large Indian community that settled here under British rule. Mohinga — a fish-based noodle soup eaten for breakfast — is the national dish and worth tracking down in the morning.
