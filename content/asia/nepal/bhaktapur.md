Bhaktapur is one of the three royal cities of the Kathmandu Valley and arguably the best preserved. It lies about 13 km east of Kathmandu and can be reached by bus or by bicycle — the road is busy but the approach gives a sense of leaving the capital behind as the air clears and rice paddies appear between houses.

The city was the capital of the valley before Kathmandu took that role at the beginning of the 17th century. Its medieval core, largely closed to motor traffic, revolves around two main squares: **Bhaktapur Durbar Square** and **Taumadhi Square**. The **Nyatapola Temple** (1702) in Taumadhi Square rises five tiers above the city and is the tallest pagoda in Nepal. The **Golden Gate** and the **55-Window Palace** in Durbar Square are extraordinary examples of Newari craftsmanship in gilded metal and carved wood. Bhaktapur is also the pottery centre of the Kathmandu Valley — **Pottery Square** is a working demonstration of traditional craft.

Bhaktapur charges a substantial entry fee (around US$15 for foreigners) that funds maintenance of the historic structures. Most visitors come for the day, but staying overnight is worthwhile — the squares are quiet in the early morning and evening.
