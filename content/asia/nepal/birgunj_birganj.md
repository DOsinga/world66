Birgunj is the main border crossing point between Nepal and India, paired with Raxaul on the Indian side. Most travellers pass through rather than stay, but if you are crossing the border late in the day you may need to spend a night. The town is hot, flat, and busy — part of the Terai lowlands rather than the Nepal most visitors imagine.

If you need to stay, the Hotel Vishuwa is the best option in town — it is just north of the bus park, a big green building with air conditioning, a pool, and a decent restaurant. Rooms run around US$50. For something cheaper, head to the cluster of hotels, airline offices, and money changers along the main road north of the border. Hotel Kailash is the budget pick, while Hotel Makalu and Heera Plaza offer middle-ground options. Internet cafes are plentiful in this area.
