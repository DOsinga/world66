Butwal is a busy commercial city in the western Terai, the flat lowland strip along Nepal's southern border with India. It serves as the main transport hub for western Nepal and is the closest significant city to Lumbini, the birthplace of the Buddha, about 95 km to the southwest.

The city itself is functional rather than attractive — a transit point where the roads from the hills meet the east-west highway. But the Siddha Baba temple on the Tinau River gorge at the edge of town is a dramatic spot, and the surrounding area marks the transition from the flat Gangetic plain to the first Himalayan foothills. Most travellers pass through Butwal on the way to Lumbini, Pokhara (via the Siddhartha Highway), or the Palpa district hill towns.
