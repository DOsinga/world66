---
title: 'Getting There'
type: section
---

Most trekkers fly from Kathmandu to Lukla (about 30 minutes), one of the world's most dramatic airport landings on a short, sloped runway surrounded by mountains. Flights are weather-dependent and cancellations are common, so build buffer days into your itinerary. The alternative is a longer approach trek from Jiri (about a week to reach Namche Bazaar), which avoids the Lukla bottleneck and lets you acclimatise gradually. All trekkers need a Sagarmatha National Park entry permit and a TIMS card, both obtainable in Kathmandu or at checkpoints along the trail.
