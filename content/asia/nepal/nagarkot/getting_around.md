---
title: "Getting Around in Nagarkot"
type: section
---

Getting around in Nagarkot, Nepal, provides you with a few options in **Nagarkot transportation**. The main forms of getting around in and around the city include the tourist buses, city buses, taxis, walking, and biking. The best kind of transportation depends on where you are going to be staying, what you are going to be doing, and how much you want to see.

#### Tourist Buses

If you want to find a good way to get around some of the attractions in the city, you can take one of the tourist buses that usually depart from the major hotels. There are travel agents around the town where you can buy your bus tickets and the buses leave from the north end of Kantipath in Thamel and return to Katmandu for traveling in that region.

#### City Buses

There are bus services available that can take tourists to a number of places to see. The buses do tend to be crowded and many local people use them, so be prepared for a crowded ride and it can also take a long time to get to where you are going.

#### Taxis

You can take a taxi service that can take you on a round trip ride to where you are going to be visiting and staying and most of the major hotels will help you arrange the taxi services. You can book afternoon tours from Nagarkot transportation but the services are more expensive that taking the buses.

#### Walking

If you would like to see the area more and you do not wish to take a taxi or a bus, you can enjoy one of the many paths in Nagarkot that you can take to see some of the main attractions in the area. It can take a while to travel on foot, and be sure that you have the right kind of shoes and clothing to make your walks more comfortable.

#### Biking

The terrain can be steep in some areas around Nagarkot, so if you are going to be biking in the area, you do want to use caution and you may want to park your bike along the way and venture the rest of the path out on foot. There are areas in the city that are easier to get around by bike, so you may want to use one for shorter distances when you are traveling in the area.
