Phidim is a small hill town in eastern Nepal, perched in the foothills below Kanchenjunga — the world's third highest mountain. It's the district headquarters of Panchthar, a practical stop for trekkers heading toward the Kanchenjunga Conservation Area, and a place that rewards a slower look.

Two rivers — the Phewa and the Hewa — run through the town, which spreads across terraced hills in the usual Nepali mix of tin-roofed houses and concrete blocks. The setting is genuinely lovely: green valleys, cloud-draped ridges, and on clear days the white mass of Kanchenjunga rising to the north.

The population is ethnically mixed — Limbu, Magar, Rai, Tamang, Brahmin, Chhetri — making it one of the more diverse towns in the region. That diversity shows in the markets and festivals if you're passing through at the right time.

Getting here means a six-hour drive from Charaali in Jhapa on the Mechi Highway, winding up through dense forest and terraced farmland. The road trip itself is one of the better ones in eastern Nepal — slow, winding, and worth it.
