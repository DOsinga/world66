---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Nepal"
type: section
---

**1. Walk the streets of Bhaktapur at dawn.** The medieval Newari city in the Kathmandu Valley is the best-preserved of the three ancient kingdoms and genuinely beautiful in the early morning before the day-trippers arrive. The Durbar Square, the potters' quarter, and the back alleys around Taumadhi Tole are all worth losing yourself in. See [Bhaktapur](/asia/nepal/bhaktapur).

**2. Trek to Annapurna Base Camp or the Everest region.** You don't have to go all the way to Base Camp — just getting into the mountains and walking for a week through Sherpa or Gurung villages and high alpine country is genuinely transformative. The trail system is well developed, teahouses feed you generously, and the scenery is extraordinary. See [Everest Region](/asia/nepal/everest_region) or use [Pokhara](/asia/nepal/pokhara) as your base for Annapurna.

**3. Sit at Boudhanath stupa at dusk.** The great white stupa in Kathmandu's Boudha neighbourhood is the spiritual heart of Tibetan Buddhism in exile. In the late afternoon, monks and pilgrims make their circumambulations, butter-lamp smoke drifts up from the shrines, and the surrounding courtyards fill with chanting. One of the most peaceful hours you can spend in Asia.

**4. Take a canoe trip in Chitwan National Park.** The Terai is a different Nepal — flat, warm, and full of wildlife. Floating down the Rapti River in a dugout canoe, watching gharial crocodiles and wading birds, is a good counterpoint to days at altitude. One-horned rhinos graze openly in the park grasslands. See [Royal Chitwan](/asia/nepal/royalchitwannationalpark).

**5. Experience a festival in the Kathmandu Valley.** If your visit coincides with Dashain, Tihar, Indra Jatra, or Holi, rearrange your plans to be in the Valley. These are not tourist events — they are living celebrations that animate the city's medieval squares with processions, music, and rituals that have continued for centuries. Ask your guesthouse what is happening during your stay.
