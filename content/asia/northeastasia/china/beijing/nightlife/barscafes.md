The bar scene in Beijing rivals that of any large city. From mellow, artsy bars to raucous sports bars to traditional European pubs, you will find something to suit your taste.

**A local drink** in Beijing is *ergoutou*. Averaging at 104 proof and Y7 a liter, this is powerful stuff. Buy a recommended brand; locals warn that some off-brands can make you blind. Ergoutou has a smooth taste, but the alcohol doesn't hit full force until much later, unlike other hard drinks. Beijingers brag that this is the perfect Chinese drink - tasty, strong, and slow-acting, so you save face by getting home before it *really* hits you.

**New Bar Street**

In Beijing, there is "New Bar Street" and "Old Bar Street". New Bar Street is along the edges of Beihai Park (Northwest of the Forbidden City). These bars are where the hip young locals go for a night on the town, whereas the Old Bar Street is a bit more for the tourist clientele.
