type: section

**Hainan Festivals** are filled with tradition and folk culture.
They are also overflowing in the Chinese Lunar Calendar. Beginning with
New Years Day on January 1, the celebrations continue with the Spring
Festival, Hainian International Coconut Festival, Changjian Mango
Festival, Tianya Jaijiao, Longevity Cultural Festival, Dongjiao
Festival, Jaijiao Festival, Tianya Jaijiao International Wedding
Celebration and Huanle Festival, just to name a few. With so many to
choose from, which ones match your vacation appeal? After all, what
better time to see Hainan than in the midst of a cultural celebration.

**Huanhua Festival**

Based on the Chinese Lunar Calendar, the Huanhua Festival is known
as the flower exchanging festival. Each year, locals exchange flowers
with one another in prayer for a happy life in the coming year. The
rose is both the most popular, as well as the most common flower traded
during the day. Held in Qiongshan city, the festival focuses on
friendship, wellness, and happiness.

**Hainan International Coconut Festival**

The Hainan International Coconut Festival origins began in early
April of 1992. The idea of the festival was to promote tourism and open
arms to all cultures. Hainan has been known as Coconut Island from
early days, and centers on teaching this deep history and culture of
Hainan while learning and absorbing that of other nations. The
spotlight of the festival combines physical sports, touring, culture,
customs, and trade.

**Tianya Haijiao Joy Festival/Danzhou Folk Song Festival**

A wonderful family Festival is the** **Tianya Haijiao Joy
Festival. Held in Autumn, the festival is celebrated in the Tianya
Haijiao Sightseeing Zone. Throughout the day, those rejoicing enjoy
dance performances, moon cakes, folk lantern displays, and mountain
songs all while observing the moon. The Danzhou Folk Song Festival is
very similar. Also held in Autumn, the festival focuses around the
moon, and includes lighting lanterns and shooting fireworks.

**The Third Day of the Third Lunar Month**

The Third Day of the Third Lunar Month is a festival where people
indulge in campfire songs, dragon boat racing, and is an opportunity
for young Li men and women to court one another in attempt to find love
and happiness. Held in late March, it is a** **sizeable event that includes sports, culture, trade, and tourism. A joyful celebration for everyone to take pleasure in.

**Hainan Joy Festival**

Taking place in late November, the Hainan Joy Festival is all about
joyfulness. Taking place in Danzhou City, the festival has been
observed for nine years by the Hainan community. An elaborate opening
ceremony kicks off the festivity. The performance includes over 10 drum
performances such as dragon, elephant feet, and steel. The show is
followed by a parade, fireworks and one heck of a good party.
