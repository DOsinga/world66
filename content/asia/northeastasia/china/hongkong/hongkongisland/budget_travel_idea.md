**Saving Money with Hostelling International**

The perfect start to plan your **Hong Kong Island Budget Travel** is to visit your local office of Hostelling International. Having a membership card from Hostelling International (the cards are also known as HI ID or HI card) allows you not only to receive discounts at any of the 8 hostels in Hong Kong, but also in many restaurants, bars, night clubs and retail shops in all of Hong Kong. Make sure to check out the Hong Kong Youth Hostels Association in the Fu Cheong Shopping Center of Kowloon to get the most updated booklet that indicates all places in Hong Kong Island that provide discounts and special offers to HI card holders.

**The Jockey Club Mount Davis Youth Hostel**

Specifically in Hong Kong Island, you can find the Jockey Club Mount Davis Youth Hostel, located on the northeast region. The hostel's address is 123 Mount Davis Path, Western District, Hong Kong and its telephone for reservations is: +852-2817-5715. This hostel is only available to registered members of Hostelling International with valid cards. Prices range from HK$70 to HK$600 depending on whether you would like to book a bed in a shared room or your own private room.

It is important that you check in as soon as you get to Hong Kong because the allocation of beds is dependent upon when you check in. The earlier you check in, the higher your chance is of receiving a better spot. There are rooms for 2, 4 and 6 people. All rooms are are divided into all male and all female dorms. All information is available at the hostel's website athttp://www.yha.org.hk. The hostel reception desk operates daily from to 7:00 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. and from 3:00 to 11:00 p.m.

**Getting Around Hong Kong Island on a Budget**

If you do decide to stay at the Jockey Club Mount Davis Youth Hostel, you will be glad to hear that there is a free shuttle bus from the ShunTak Center at Shueng Wan (the bus stop by the Macau Ferry Pier).

The cheapest way to get around is through the bus system. Even getting to the airport is affordable by using the airport bus service that stops every 10 to 20 minutes at major hotels in Central District and Causeway Bay (onHong Kong Island) and at Tsimshatsui in Hong Kong. Some hotels are reluctant to dispense airport bus information because it is the cheapest way of getting to the airport and it would interfere with their own paid transportation services. On airport buses, however, there are no individual luggage racks (just a communal baggage bay) and the buses' steps are really steep. Travelers with heavy bags should take a cab instead. Routes A2 and A3 serve the Hong Kong Island routes towards the airport. Please carry exact change when traveling by bus as bus operators cannot provide change.
