**Lantau Island**
To
talk about the Tian Tan Buddha, you need to talk about the Po
Lin Monastery, and to talk about the Po Lin Monastery, you need to talk
about the island of Lantau. Of all the outlying islands from Hong Kong,
the greatest in size is Lantau with a land area twice that of Hong
Kong. The people of Lantau follow a very rural lifestyle, almost
untouched by time, that has endured the fall of emperors, the British
colonization and the decay of empires. Despite being larger than Hong
Kong, this island has less population and provides a sense of peace.
Therefore, it is no surprise that many Christian and Buddhist
monasteries have set Lantau as their home.
**

Getting to Lantau Island**

To get
to Lantau Island you will first need to get to Mui Wo on Lantau via
ferry through the Silver Mine Bay from any pier on either Hong Kong or
other outlying islands. Mui Wo is located on the eastern shore of
center Lantau. Another option is to get to Tung Chung station via MTR.
Once in Lantau, you can grab the NLB number 2 from Mui Wo to Ngong Ping
or the NLB number 23 from Tung Chung to Ngong Ping.

**Tian Tan Buddha**
Once
you arrive to Ngon Ping you will be able to find several signs to guide
you to the Po Lin Monastery, where eternally seated in its ground is
one of the world's tallest bronze statues of Buddha, the Tian Tan
Buddha. Erected in 1989, its official opening for viewing was delayed
until late 1991, after the single lane road to the Po Lin Monastery had
been widened.
The
Tian Tan Buddha is also known in English as The Big Buddha. It is a
model of the Altear of Heaven of Tian Tan in Beijing and one of the
five largest Buddha statues in China. The Tian Tan Budha statue stands
tall at 34 meters (110 feet) and weights almost 250 metric tons (280
short tons). The Tian Tan Buddha statue is so massive that on a clear
day, it can be seen from Macau.
Plan
your trip ahead because about 1,000 daily visitors head to the Po Lin
Monastery, all walking on steep hill north of the Shek Pik reservoir.
After the long hike, you can enjoy a vegetarian meal and even an
overnight stay in the small guest bungalows (if space and conditions
allow).
