China's South West - the provinces including Hunnan, Guizhou, Yunnan, Chongqing, Sichuan, Tibet (and, at a stretch, Qinghai) - are among the most fascinating in China.

(The other two scenically delightful ones are China's North East - including provinces Heilongjiang and Jilin - and the North West - which largely means Xinjiang province.)

The South West is perhaps the most beautiful because it is not only scenically stunning, but also because it is home to the majority of China's 56 ethnic minorities. (See Paul Noll's site - http://www.paulnoll.com/China/Minorities/index.html - for a complete list of them.)

Within China's South West, perhaps the loveliest is the region which takes its name from James Hilton's novel *Lost Horizon*. Even though Hilton's "Shangri-La" is fictional, there are probably only a few areas that Hilton could have meant. One of them straddles Sichuan and Yunnan.

A great itinerary is to start in Chengdu and travel west to Litang (officially the world's highest town), then to turn south into the incredibly beautiful Yading Nature Reserve and from there to proceed into north-west Yunnan (Deqin, Zhongdian and Lijiang).

Since these regions are very remote, a good way (the best way?) is to travel by private car (unless you want to be cramped into choke-full buses). How long does it take?

12 days are good enough. Any less is too rushed since the distances are great. Any more might eat too much into the budget.
