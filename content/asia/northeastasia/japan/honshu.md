If you stick to Honshu on your trip to Japan, you're not shortchanging yourself. Honshu has all the fun and all the attractions.

 [Tokyo](/asia/northeastasia/japan/tokyo) and [Kyoto](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/kyoto), the imperial cities are both on Honshu. Other big cities with a long history and many sights such as [Yokohama](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/yokohama), [Kobe](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/kobe) and [Osaka](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/osaka) are on Honshu too. The holy mountain of Mr. Fuji (for Japanese it's not Fuji-yama, meaning Mount Fuiji, but Fuji-san, Mr. Fuji) is on the island as well. 

 [Nikko](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/nikko) in northern Honshu is definately worth a visit. It's one of the most historic towns of Japan and the old city centre is still very much in use.

 In central Honshu, the area around Furukawa is pretty and the Japanese alps are great for hiking in summer and winter sports and activities in winter. Matsumo and [Takayama](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/takayama) are good places for exploring the alps.

 In the west Kurashiki is a very charming town. [Hiroshima](/asia/northeastasia/japan/honshu/hiroshima) is famous for being bombed in the second world war and a visit to the Peace park is impressive. Although the Seto Naikai or "inland sea" is no longer lined with traditional fishing villages it's still a nice area to spend some time. Pick an island and come to rest in the beautiful surroundings.
