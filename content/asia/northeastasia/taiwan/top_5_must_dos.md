title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Taiwan"
type: section
---

If you are creating a list of things to do in Taiwan, then do not forget to put the following activities in your **Taiwan must do's** list. Taiwan is a filled with natural wonders, temples, shrines and historical destinations.

#### 1. Explore the Taroko Gorge

The Taroko Gorge is situated in the Taroko National Park. The surrounding area, as well as the Taroko Gorge, is well-known for its abundant supply of marble. The gorge itself is carved from marble by the Liwu River. Aside from marble, jade can also be found in this National Park. There are also several caves, cliffs and hiking trails in Taroko Gorge, making it an ideal destination for adventure travelers.

#### 2. See 100 Temples in Tainan

The oldest city in Taiwan, Tainan is a worthy destination to go to featuring over 220 temples. Tainan is also called &lsquo;City of 100 Temples&rsquo;; this destination has different temples that have a variety of architectural designs. You can also see excellent architecture in various temples.

#### 3. Visit the Museum of Marine Biology and Aquarium

The Museum of Marine Biology and Aquarium in Taiwan showcases some of the local marine life. The museum also features a penguin breeding program, as well as a wide collection of marine life like whale sharks, belugas and lion fish. The Museum of Marine Biology and Aquarium also have several types of corals on display. As the largest marine life museum in Asia, this museum features 3 main exhibit halls namely Coral Kingdom Pavilion, Waters of Taiwan and the Waters of the World. Each of the halls houses different species of marine life.

#### 4. Check Out the National Palace Museum in Taipei

Taipei&rsquo;s National Palace Museum houses the world&rsquo;s largest collection of Chinese artifacts. The Museum features a wide array of Asian art including bronze, jade, paintings, rare books, calligraphy, ceramics and pottery. The collection in the Museum encompasses over 8,000 years of Chinese history, starting from the Neolithic age up until the late Qing Dynasty.

#### 5. Explore the Chiang Kai-Shek Memorial Hall

This famous monument built for Taiwan&rsquo;s most famous leader, Chiang Kai-Shek, is an imposing tomb located in Taipei. The Chiang Kai-Shek Memorial Hall is surrounded by a park that houses the National Theater on the north and the National Concert Hall on the south. This imposing structure features a 70 meter octagonal roof covered with blue glazed tiles. Dominating the hall is a large bronze statue of Chiang Kai-Shek.
