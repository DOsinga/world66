Wonsan is a port city on the east coast, facing the Sea of Japan, and has traditionally been one of the few places outside Pyongyang on standard North Korea tours. The beach and coastal setting offer a change of pace from monument-heavy city itineraries.

The **Wonsan-Kalma Coastal Tourist Zone**, opened in June 2025, is the most ambitious tourism development in North Korean history: a 2.5km stretch of beach on the Kalma Peninsula backed by around 400 buildings including high-rise hotels, villas, a water park, concert hall, department store, and restaurants. The zone was designed for 20,000 guests at capacity. Its first foreign visitors were Russian tour groups in summer 2025. Whether and when it opens to Western visitors depends on North Korean policy decisions that remain unpredictable.

The city has a fishing harbour, a naval base, and the kind of low-key seaside atmosphere that feels genuinely different from Pyongyang. The **Songdowon International Children's Camp** nearby is a standard tour inclusion — a showcase facility for children's activities and exchange programmes.

Wonsan is roughly three hours from Pyongyang by road, or reachable by Air Koryo domestic flight from Sunan.
