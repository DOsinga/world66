Barka is a small coastal town on the Batinah plain, about 80km west of Muscat and easily done as a day trip. The fort is well-preserved and sits right on the waterfront, which makes it more photogenic than many of Oman's inland fortifications. The sea is close enough that you can walk between the fort and the shore in a few minutes.

Barka is also known for bullfighting — the Omani style, which involves two bulls pushing against each other until one backs down, rather than any matador theatrics. Bouts happen on Friday mornings in season, usually between October and April, and attract local crowds rather than tourist coaches.

There isn't much else to keep you in Barka beyond the fort and, if you time it right, the bullfighting. But as a half-day excursion from Muscat, or a stop along the route west toward Sohar, it's a pleasant one.
