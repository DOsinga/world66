---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Muscat is a good base for exploring the rest of Oman. [Nizwa](/asia/oman/nizwa), the old imperial capital in the interior, is about a 90-minute drive inland — a huge circular fort, a Friday livestock market, and a proper old souk. The mountain villages of Jebel Akhdar (the Green Mountain) are higher and cooler than the coast, with terraced pomegranate orchards and rose-water distilleries. South along the coast, Sur is a shipbuilding town still making traditional wooden dhows; just beyond it, Ras al-Jinz is one of the world's most important nesting sites for green turtles.

For a wilder day out, the Wahiba Sands (Sharqiya Sands) offer classic red-dune desert scenery about two and a half hours south of the city; desert camps make the overnight version more comfortable than driving back the same day. The Bimmah Sinkhole on the coast road to Sur is a short, photogenic stop.
