Salalah is the capital of Oman's Dhofar region and one of the Arabian Peninsula's great surprises. From June through September, the khareef monsoon rolls in off the Indian Ocean and transforms the surrounding mountains from parched brown to vivid green — a phenomenon that draws visitors from across the Gulf who come purely for the cool air and the spectacle of grass and waterfalls appearing in the desert.

Outside monsoon season the city is hot and unhurried, with a coastline of near-empty beaches, a UNESCO-listed archaeological park, and frankincense trees growing in the hills. Salalah was the home of the late Sultan Qaboos and carries a quiet sense of importance for Omanis.
