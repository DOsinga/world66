A UNESCO World Heritage Site on the eastern edge of Salalah, Al-Baleed preserves the ruins of a medieval port city that thrived on the frankincense trade from the 12th to 15th centuries. The sprawling excavated site includes mosques, palaces, and warehouses. The Museum of the Frankincense Land on the grounds provides essential context with exhibits on Dhofar's history, natural environment, and the ancient trade routes. Free entry; open Saturday–Thursday.
