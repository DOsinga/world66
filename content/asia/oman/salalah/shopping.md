---
title: "Shopping in Salalah"
type: section
---

Salalah’s shopping scene is as much characterized by its quaint street-side
markets or traditional Arabic souks than by its plush new malls that are slowly
sketching a modern identity for the town. Salalah is known as the ‘Perfume
Capital of the Middle East' and visitors can lay their hands on the finest
fragrances in the region. Here is a comprehensive guide to shopping in
Salalah.

**Haffa Souk **

Trips to the Middle East are never consummated without a trip to
their famous regional bazaars--the souks. The street-side flea markets are a
cultural experience and are packed with tourists who come here to
savor the local culture and lifestyle in a colorful setting. Haffa Souk is the
largest and oldest souk in Salalah and tourists can pick up a range of eclectic
items from the seemingly never-ending stretch of stalls. Travelers can buy the
celebrated Frankincense souvenir here along with a string of other items like
kaftans, woollen caps, perfumes, jewelry, lanterns, ceramic figurines, bells and
wooden handicrafts.

**Lulu Shopping Center**

Lulu is a known brand of super markets having outlets all around the Middle
East and is very popular with western tourists for its electronics and crockery
items. This is one of the best places to buy imported goods, dry fruits, fine
coffee beans and freshly baked-bread. Lulu is well-stocked with perfumes, cosmetics, artifacts,
toys, upholstery, and a host of other items and offers
quality merchandise at great bargain prices.

**Al Husn Souk**

Al Husn Souk is very popular for its trademark Omani silver shops, ancient
silver weapons and jewelry. Visitors can bargain for decent prices and pick up
traditional silverware items including kohl containers and incense holders.
There are also some good perfume shops and textile merchants in the souk. Watch
out for the local goldsmiths who craft lovely made-to-order jewelry pieces that
can beat any international brand hands down for inventiveness and attention
to detail.

**Centrepoint Shopping Mall**

This is a sprawling, modern local mall featuring mostly high-end apparel
brands, accessories and home décor stores. Several international fashion labels
have their outlets in the mall, which also houses a multi-cuisine food-court and
an electronic games play-zone for children.

Salalah isn’t as much of a shopper’s paradise as other Middle East
destinations; however it does have a decent mix of local bazaars, super markets
and swanky malls to choose from.
