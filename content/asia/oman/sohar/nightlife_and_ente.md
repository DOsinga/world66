title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Sohar"

---

If you want to explore the **Sohar nightlife** and entertainment options in Oman, you might be in for a little surprise. Sohar is still a developing city, and although you will find plenty of restaurants that cater to every taste bud, you won&rsquo;t find many clubs, bars and casinos in this area. But that shouldn&rsquo;t stop you from exploring other parts of Oman or Muscat.

#### Nightlife in Muscat

Copacabana is one of Muscat&rsquo;s most popular nightclubs. The interiors have a more festive, carnival-like atmosphere. This is where Muscat&rsquo;s top DJs spin good tracks to get you to let your hair down and have a good time. One thing you need to know when visiting Oman for the first time is that you&rsquo;re allowed to consume alcohol only if you buy drinks from licensed bars and restaurants. If you want to pick up a bottle of wine or beer for consumption at your premise, you are required to obtain a permit from your embassy. Most other nightclubs are located within 5-star hotels, so if you head to the Grand Hyatt Muscat or the Shangri-La&rsquo;s Barr Al Jissah Resort & Spa, be prepared to be entertained. The Xyro Nightclub within the Shangri-La&rsquo;s Barr Al Jissah Resort offers you live entertainment, with performances by international singers and music groups. You will also find plenty of bars and restaurants at these hotels that are sure to please your palate.

#### Bars within Hotels

If you wander into the Al Wadi Hotel in Sohar, you will have plenty of entertainment options to choose from. This hotel features a sports bar named Al Majaz Club, the Al Waqhbah bar with a live Indian band, the Oasis restaurant with an Arabic band, and the Al Hibi Pub, which also has a live band playing every night. But if you want to be entertained all night long with some enthralling music and live performances, visit the Sheraton Oman Hotel to have access to their nightclub, popularly known as the "Saba Discotheque." It&rsquo;s here that you will find DJs spinning popular Arabic music and dancers entertaining a mixed audience with their live music performances.
