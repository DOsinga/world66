Since Abbottabad is surrounded by the Sarban Hills on all four sides, there are none **Abbottabad beaches** located nearby. At the same time, you can find various water bodies around the city. Abbottabad serves as an important junction in Pakistan where you can access some of the different tourist destinations in the country, making it easier to get to the different tourist locations of Pakistan.

#### Haro River

Haro River is a river in the Abbottabad district. This river starts at the southern end of Dongagali range, and it flows through 2 separate streams in the district of Abbottabad. The upper stream or northern stream is called the Karlal Haro. The stream on the southern end or the lower section of the district is referred to as Dhund Haro. However, both streams flow back into each other into the Khanpur Lake.

#### Fresh Water Spring in Ilyasi Mosque

There is a fresh water spring in Ilyasi Mosque that is said to have healing properties particularly for people with arthritis. The mosque is built over this stream that comes from the mountain. Because of the stream and its famed healing properties, the spring in Ilyasi Mosque is a frequently-visited destination in Abbottabad. A pond-like structure with water from the mountain spring is also available where people can ride paddle driven boats.

#### Khanpur Lake

Located 2.5 hours from Abbottabad is the city of Islamabad where the Khanpur Lake is located. The lake is situated near the Taxila-Haripur Road and is favorite among the locals for day trips and other leisure activities including picnicking, angling, boating and, during winter, bird watching.

#### Tarbela Dam

From Abbottabad, travel about 40 miles to get to Rawalpindi, which will lead you to Tarbela Dam. Tarbela Dam is approximately 100 kilometers from the city of Rawalpindi. It was completed in 1976 and is the 3rd biggest earth-filled dam in the world. It is also located on Indus River which is an important river in the world. Tarbela Dam is a hydel power station with a reservoir of 97 kilometers long and 137 meters in depth. The entire lake, however, is 260 square kilometers. You can check out the Tarbela Dam, but you do need to acquire a permit to do so. You can get the permit from the Public Relations Officer of the Water and Power Development Authority. Aside from this permit, you will also need to get a No-Objection Certificate at the Ministry of Interior in Islamabad.
