Pind Gali is a small settlement near [Abbottabad](/asia/pakistan/abbottabad) in the Hazara division of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The area sits in the foothills leading up toward the higher ranges of the Karakoram Highway region, at an elevation that keeps temperatures comfortable even in summer.

The village is typical of the Hazara hill towns — terraced fields, pine-covered slopes, and a close-knit community where hospitality toward visitors is deeply felt. There is no formal tourism infrastructure here, but the surrounding countryside is good for walking, with paths leading through forests and farmland to neighbouring villages.

Abbottabad itself, the nearest town of any size, has a bazaar, restaurants, and transport connections onward to Nathia Gali, the Kaghan Valley, and eventually northward toward Gilgit and the Karakoram.
