Nestled into a valley, Abbottabad is considered one of the most beautiful cities in Pakistan. Knowing some basic **Abbottabad practical information **will enhance your visit even more and generate a deeper appreciation for this place.

**Getting Here**

The city can be reached in about 3 hours if traveling from nearby Islamabad. There is a bus service which makes daily runs. Once inside the city, you'll find taxis and motorized Suzukis. The city does not have a railway. The nearest station is about 30 minutes away in Havalian. **
**

**Language**

The official language is Hindko. English speakers will fit in well here though, as English is widely used as a means of education and in the business world. Given that tourism is such a large economic basis here, you should have no problem finding an English speaker so long as you don't go off of the beaten path.

**Currency**

The official currency is the Pakistani Rupee. You'll find that the American dollar is extraordinarily strong here, with a typical exchange rate getting you bout 85 Rupees per dollar.

**Climate**

Thanks to the valley location, Abbottabad is known for it's mild winters and cold winters. There is a humid season in June and July, but even then temperatures rarely reach over 90 degrees Fahrenheit. Winter temperatures rarely dip below 30 degrees Fahrenheit. Despite this, snowfall is common. There is also a monsoon season from May to August when rains are common and flooding is normal.

**Safety**

Take general precautions here. Try to travel in well lit areas and in groups. Given that the tourist dollar is widely sought, the area is generally tourist-friendly.
