It is imperative to make a list of **Abbottabad Things to Do** before visiting the place because of the many places to visit and many things to do around there. Here are some of the various things to do in the town.

**Skiing **

Being a mountainous area, it is no wonder that Abbottabad is a place where trekking, climbing and hiking are part of the daily lives of people. Visitors are welcome to join any of these activities, but tourists generally visit the area for skiing, where it is cheaper than most of the other skiing destinations in the world. Thandiani is a hill station in the mountain areas, and is a popular starting point for ski enthusiasts. For those who are in for the whole package, the Nathia Gali is the place to go. The mountain resort town is found about a 40-minute drive away from the main town of Abbottabad, and is a great skiing destination for both beginners and experts alike in the winter.

**Admiring the View**

Nathia Gali in the summer and spring is the perfect place to be for tourists who are visiting Abbottabad. The remote location of the place means that the view is unspoiled and the traffic promises to be fairly good at all times. The town is known for its scenery with lush meadows and beautiful pine and oak forests. The Hunza Valley is another popular spot, particularly in the winter, where the large sheets of show and ice cover the valley, creating a pristine image of the mountains in the distance. The Valley is famous for the capital Karimabad, which offers a view of the mountains in the moonlight, glittering as the light of the moon bounces off the snowflakes that form the white of the mountains.

**Tasting the Food**

The food culture in Abbottabad is vibrant and colorful, mixing traditional Pakistani fare with the influences of the nearby China and India. The Chapli Kebab is a large part of the diet in the North-West Frontier Province, where Abbottabad is located, made out of minced beef or mutton and wrapped in a white kebab wrap. The town is also marked with a wonderful snack known as Pakora, a deep fried vegetable such as eggplant, onion or spinach dipped in a gram flour batter and eaten with chilies and pepper. All of the food here is traditionally washed down with tea, known locally as Qehwa, a green tea that is native to the highland regions of India and Pakistan and drunk with spices and honey.
