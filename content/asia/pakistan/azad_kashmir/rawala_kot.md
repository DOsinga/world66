Rawalakot is the district headquarters of Poonch in Azad Kashmir, sitting in a saucer-shaped valley at around 1,600 meters. The town itself is small and functional, but the surrounding landscape is the real draw — green ridges, pine forests, and mountain views in every direction.

The valley is 76 kilometers from Kohalla and connected to Rawalpindi and Islamabad via Azad Pattan. In summer the hillsides fill with wildflowers and the temperature stays pleasant while the plains below bake. The town has a few bazaars covering everyday needs, but you come here for the mountains, not the shopping.

Rawalakot works well as a base for exploring the wider Poonch district. [Banjosa Lake](/asia/pakistan/azad_kashmir/rawala_kot/things_to_do/banjosa) is a short drive away, [Toli Pir](/asia/pakistan/azad_kashmir/rawala_kot/things_to_do/toli_pir) offers panoramic views from the highest point in the area, and the hot springs at [Tatta Pani](/asia/pakistan/azad_kashmir/rawala_kot/things_to_do/tatta_pani) make for an unusual day out.
