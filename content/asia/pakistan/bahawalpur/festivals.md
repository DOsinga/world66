---
title: "Festivals in Bahawalpur"
type: section
---

Owing to its rich culture, there are several **Bahawalpur**** festivals** that are celebrated each year. Bahawalpur, the "Princely State," is located on the province of Punjab in Pakistan. It is called the Princely State because it was the erstwhile home to several Nawabs (rulers). Given its royal residents, Bahawalpur has a rich heritage and culture and has several tourist attractions like its famous palaces, forts, monuments and national parks. The city of Bahawalpur, which is the capital of the state of Bahawalpur, is located close to the ancient cities of Uch and Harappa.

**Channan Pir Festival**

Channan Pir is a small village in the district of Bahawalpur, which takes its name from a famous Sufi Saint, Channan Pir and contains his tomb. Every year the death anniversary or the urs of the Sufi saint is held at his tomb. The Channan Pir festival, or the Channan Mela, is held on seven consecutive Thursdays starting from mid February onwards. This festival attracts followers from all over Pakistan. Colorfully dressed men, women and children gather at the shine to pay their respects. The fifth Thursday is most awaited by the followers of Chanan Pir.

You can witness several camel caravans, snake charmers, camel dance trainers and desert folk with colorful attire filling the area with color and vibrancy. There are several cultural and folk dances and songs, camel races and other competitions that you can see at Channan Pir. As the custom goes, the men folk dominate the festivals and the women are required to cover their faces. Devotees come from all over Pakistan to pray for their families, and women pray especially for their daughters' matrimonial status and their sons' health. This is a very interesting festival to attend and a place where you can witness the true Pakistan.

**Fair at Shrine of Muluk Shah **

The Shrine of Muluk Shah, another popular saint during his time, is located in the heart of Bahawalpur city. This is another famous religious spot in Bahawalpur. The shrine is visited by several devotees and tourists every day to pay homage to the great saint. Every year a small fair is held at the Muluk Shah shrine. Special prayers are offered during the fair and the men and women from all over Pakistan travel to seek the blessings of Allah and the saint during this day.
