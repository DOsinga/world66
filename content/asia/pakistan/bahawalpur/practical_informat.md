You may want to learn some **Bahawalpur Practical Information**, since Bahawalpur is one of the most notable cities in the Punjab region. So, if that is truly the case, the next few lines are for you.**
**

**Population**

Bahawalpur is the 12th largest city in Pakistan, with a population of around 800,000 citizens. They belong mainly to the Jat and Baluchi ethnic groups. Bahawalpur is the city with the second biggest birth rate in Pakistan, right after Islamabad, with a birth rate of almost 5 percent. Of course, this results in an increasing population density as well, although the number is relatively small at around 838 people on a square kilometer.

**Language**

Although the official languages in Pakistan are Urdu and English, most of the people in Bahawalpur speak Saraiki (more specifically Multani dialect). Punjabi is widely spoken and understood too, but Punjabi and Saraiki are used mainly in everyday conversations, while all the official institutions, like banks and government services, use English and Urdu.

**Currency**

The official currency used here, as in the rest of the country, is Pakistan rupee. The word "rupee" originates from the Sanskrit word for silver. In each rupee, there are 100 paisas. An interesting fact is that new paise coins haven&rsquo;t been manufactured since 1994. It is good to know that $1 equals approximately to 85 Pakistan rupees.

**Climate
**

When visiting the city be aware of the climate. Bahawalpur is located in a desert environment and the usual temperatures in the summer are above 40 degrees C. In addition the air is very dry and rainfall is a rare thing. In the winter, the average temperatures fall no lower than 18 degrees C. So pack some lighter clothes and drink lots of water to avoid dehydration.

**Economy
**

The city has a developed economy, as it is located on the way of a major trade route. Bahawalpur is most famous with its cotton manufacture. Except, cotton fabrics, cotton oil and cotton cake are produced as well. Different local industries manufacture items like soap, silk, carpets and embroidery, too. A serious number of the local residents are engaged in agriculture and trade. Bahawalpur is an important marketing center for wheat, millet and rice and although located near the desert, the city has the most fertile lands in the region.
