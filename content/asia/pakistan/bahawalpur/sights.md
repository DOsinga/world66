A visit to **Bahawalpur sights** is not complete without a trip to the famous Cholistan Desert in the south of Punjab, Pakistan. Bahawalpur, one of the largest cities of Pakistan, has a rich heritage and is very important archeologically as well historically. Cotton, silk, carpets, delicate pottery, hand embroidery, and other handicrafts are unique to this region.

Bahawalpur has many famous palaces, mausoleums and other attractions for the tourists to see. Indus Valley civilization site of Harappa is located nearby. There are ancient and modern sights of interest.

**Bahawalpur Museum:**

With exhibits like artifacts from Mohenjo-Daro, Harappa and Cholistan, there are many sections in this museum, like the Fabrics Gallery, Manuscripts and Calligraphy Gallery, Coins and Medals Gallery, Islamic Art Gallery, and many more.

**Markets:**

There are many shopping centres like Shahi Bazaar, Machli Bazaar, the mall, and Farid Gare selling items like flassi, Gindi or Rilly, changaries, Khaltis, and other artwork unique to Bahawalpur.

**The Bahawalpur Wildlife Zoo:**

This huge zoo is home to a variety of animal species like Asiatic lions, hyenas, leopards, Bengal tigers, and the animals unique to the Cholistan region. There is also an aquarium and a zoological museum here.

**Lal Suhanra National Park**: This is an important wildlife safari park, situated 35 km east of the city. It houses lions, wild boars, chinkara, gazelle, and rhinoceros. The park was established in 1972.
