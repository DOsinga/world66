Once your accommodation is sorted out, you will want to find **Bahawalpur ****things to do** and see. You can travel by train or bus, but a rented car or scooter is the most convenient way to travel around to the most interesting sites that this city has to offer.

**Things to Do**** Outdoors**

A perfect outing for the entire family would be to the Bahawalpur Zoo. This zoo is well-known throughout Pakistan for containing the most diverse species of animals including tigers and lions. Most students studying zoology and wildlife head to this zoo to complete their research.

If the outdoors is where you like to be, a short 23-mile drive outside town will take you to Lal Suhanra National Park. There are camping facilities with essential guided tours, and with rare Chinkara gazelles and a myriad of bird life, this will be a truly wonderful experience.

**Top Historic ****Things to Do**

The historic fort of Derawar is situated about 65 miles from Bahawalpur and is sought after by most tourists and historians. With a fascinating history, it makes for one of the top Bahawalpur things to do. There have recently, however, been reports detailing the deterioration of the fort&rsquo;s walls, which suggest that it may not last very much longer.

**Visiting Palaces, Temples, and Religious Shrines**

Just 30 miles from the Bahawalpur is Dera Nawab Sahib which is home to the beautiful Sadiqgarh Palace, otherwise known as the Sleeping Beauty Castle. Even though it is sealed to visitors by order of the government, it is still a fantastic building steeped in history, and it is well worth seeing.

Religion is very important in Bahawalpur, as it is all over Pakistan, so to visit the many mosques, temples and shrines is something that one must do. You will be able to experience some of the most wonderful buildings, lavish d&eacute;cor and rich colors in celebration of their religious heritage. To the east of Bahawalpur is the Shrine of Makhdum Bilawal, a real treasure in the desert.

Other popular sights to see are the various palaces in Bahawalpur including the Sadiq Gahr Palace and the famous Noor Mahal, which is closed to the public. The palace has been restored, somewhat, to its original condition, as it was when Nawab Sadiq Mohammed Kahn IV had it built in 1872.
