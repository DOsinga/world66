Bhalwal City is located roughly at mid-point of Pakistan capital Islamabad and provioncial Punjab capital Lahore. Formed by the British with the arrival of the railway link from Lala-Musa to Sargodha, in the 1920s, it is essentially an agricultural city. The city is served by the loere-Jhelum Canal and is a major producer of Sugar Cane, Oranges, Cotton and Basmati Rice crops.  

  

The biggest employer in town is the Noon sugar mills corporation.
