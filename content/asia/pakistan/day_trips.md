title: "Day Trips"

---

**From Islamabad / Rawalpindi**

[Taxila](/asia/pakistan/taxila) is the obvious choice — 35 km away and one of the great archaeological sites in Asia. A half-day gets you through the main museum and the monastery ruins at Dharmarajika; a full day lets you see Jaulian, Mohra Muradu, and more. Most guesthouses in Islamabad can arrange transport.

The Margalla Hills, on Islamabad's northern edge, offer straightforward hiking with good views over the capital. Trail 3 and Trail 5 are well-marked and popular with morning joggers and families.

**From Lahore**

[Harappa](/asia/pakistan/harappa), 250 km southwest, is the other great Indus Valley site after Moenjodaro — a long day trip or an overnight. The site is less dramatically preserved than Moenjodaro but the museum is informative.

[Hassan Abdal](/asia/pakistan/hassan_abdal), 45 km from Lahore, holds Panja Sahib, one of the most important Sikh shrines in the world. The town is also historically significant for Mughal-era gardens and the tomb of Hasan Abdal. A manageable half-day.

**From Peshawar**

The Khyber Pass road is an exceptional excursion if you can get the required permit (arrange through tour operators in Peshawar). The pass itself is 45 km from the city; the fortified villages, abandoned British-era forts, and the Pakistani military's elaborate infrastructure along the route make it one of the most striking drives in the country.

**From Gilgit**

The drive up to [Hunza](/asia/pakistan/hunzavalley) — 100 km along the Karakoram Highway — is an obvious day trip or overnight. Altit and Baltit forts, the Attabad Lake (created by a 2010 landslide that blocked the Hunza River), and the Eagle's Nest viewpoint above Karimabad are all manageable in a day with an early start.
