## By Air

Domestic flights are the only practical option for reaching the north quickly. PIA, Airblue, and SereneAir all operate routes between the major cities. Flights to Gilgit, Skardu, and Chitral are spectacular but highly weather-dependent — the northern airports are high-altitude and frequently shut by cloud, fog, or snow. Budget a few extra days when flying to or from the mountains; delays and cancellations are common in winter and during the July–September monsoon.

## By Train

Pakistan Railways runs an extensive network connecting [Karachi](/asia/pakistan/karachi) to [Peshawar](/asia/pakistan/peshawar) via [Lahore](/asia/pakistan/lahore) on the Main Line-1 route. Trains are slow but cheap, and the sleeper services are a reasonable overnight option for long journeys. The Lahore–Karachi express is a classic route; book in advance for the better berths. The rail network does not reach the north.

## By Bus

Intercity buses range from cramped local coaches to the comfortable air-conditioned services run by Daewoo Express, Faisal Movers, and similar operators. The private companies are reliable, affordable, and connect most major cities. For travel in Gilgit-Baltistan, the Northern Areas Transport Corporation (NATCO) runs buses along the Karakoram Highway and onward into the valleys — a scenic if slow journey.

## By Ride-Hailing and Taxi

Uber and Careem operate in Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, and other major cities and are the easiest way to get around without negotiating fares. inDrive is also popular and lets you haggle directly. In smaller towns, rickshaws and local taxis are plentiful — agree on a fare before you get in.

## Driving

Renting a car with a driver is a practical option for reaching sites off the main routes. Self-drive rentals are available but road conditions and traffic in cities can be challenging. The Karakoram Highway is one of the great road journeys in the world, but requires a sturdy vehicle for side roads.
