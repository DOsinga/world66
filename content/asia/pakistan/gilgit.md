Gilgit is the capital of **Gilgit-Baltistan**, the administrative region covering Pakistan's northern highlands, and the main transport hub for the Karakoram and the Hindu Kush. The town itself sits in a broad, dry river valley at about 1,500 metres, ringed on all sides by enormous brown peaks; it is unremarkable in itself — dusty, bazaar-centred, with a permanent population of around 200,000 — but it is the place nearly every traveller to the mountains passes through. Gilgit has been a crossroads for as long as anything here has been written down: the old caravan routes from Kashgar over the Khunjerab, from Ladakh via Skardu, and from Chitral over the Shandur all met here.

The main sight in town is a pair of rock-cut **standing Buddhas** at **Kargah Nala**, about 10 km south-west of the city — 7th-century Gandharan carvings that are a reminder of how Buddhist this region once was. The **Gilgit Bridge** (the old suspension bridge across the Gilgit River) and the **polo ground** at the edge of town are the other small landmarks. The polo here is the real game — rough, fast, unlimited-player village polo, which the locals claim is the original form of the sport. A much larger version is played every July at **Shandur Pass**, at 3,700 metres between Gilgit and Chitral, for the famous **Shandur Polo Festival**.

Most travellers use Gilgit as a base for trips further into the mountains. **[Hunza Valley](/asia/pakistan/hunzavalley)** and Karimabad are 2–3 hours north on the **Karakoram Highway**. **Skardu** (and from there, the trekking routes to the great 8,000-metre peaks — K2, Broad Peak, the Gasherbrums) is a full day's drive east. The trek to **Rakaposhi Base Camp**, on the flanks of the 7,788-metre giant that looms over the Hunza valley, is a popular three- or four-day outing. **Fairy Meadows** and **Nanga Parbat** Base Camp are south. **Gilgit Airport** has occasional flights from Islamabad, weather permitting — the 40-minute flight through the mountains is spectacular, but is also cancelled often and without warning, so most visitors come overland on the 20-hour bus journey up the Karakoram Highway from Islamabad.
