Hassan Abdal is a quiet historical town 50 km west of [Rawalpindi](/asia/pakistan/rawalpindi), worth a stop on the road to [Peshawar](/asia/pakistan/peshawar) or Abbottabad.

The town's most famous landmark is the Gurdwara Panja Sahib, one of the holiest Sikh shrines, drawing pilgrims from around the world twice a year. Nearby stand two Mughal-era structures: Muqbara Hakeeman and the so-called tomb of Lala Rukh, located just opposite the Gurdwara.

On the hill above town — at 714 metres — is a meditation chamber associated with the 15th-century Muslim saint Baba Wali Qandhari, after whom the town is named. Climb the hill for a panoramic view over Hassan Abdal.
