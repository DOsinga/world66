Hussaini is a small village in the Gojal region of Upper Hunza, 145 km north of Gilgit and about 45 km from Aliabad in Central Hunza. The Karakoram Highway (KKH) passes to its west while the Hunza River runs to the east, at an altitude of around 2,556 metres.

The village sits in dramatic mountain scenery. Gulmit, headquarters of the Gojal tehsil, lies to the south; Ghulkin is to the southwest; Passu is in the north, with the famous Batura Glacier. The Hussaini Glacier lies to the west beneath Shisper Peak (7,611 m).

Hussaini is perhaps best known for its famously precarious suspension bridge across the Hunza River — a sequence of wooden planks strung on wire cables over a roaring gorge, frequently photographed by travellers on the KKH.
