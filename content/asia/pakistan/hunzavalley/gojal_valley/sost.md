Sost is the last town in Pakistan on the Karakoram Highway before the Chinese border at the Khunjerab Pass (4,693 metres). It sits in the Gojal Valley of Gilgit-Baltistan, a narrow settlement stretched along the road in a dramatic canyon landscape of rock and snow.

The town exists largely because of the border — Pakistani immigration and customs are based here, and all passenger and cargo traffic between Pakistan and China passes through. For travellers on the Karakoram Highway, Sost is either the last stop before crossing into China or the first taste of Pakistan coming the other way. There are a handful of basic guesthouses, restaurants, and shops catering to transit travellers.

The surrounding Gojal region is spectacular, with glaciated peaks on all sides and the Karakoram Highway winding through gorges of almost absurd drama. The Khunjerab Pass, about 85 kilometres north, is one of the highest paved international border crossings in the world and open roughly May to November depending on conditions.
