title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Kaghan"
type: section
---

Consider some of these **Kaghan**** ****Valley**** Must Do&rsquo;s** when going to this attractive tourist destination in Pakistan. The Kaghan Valley is famous with its high mountains, clear lakes and beautiful nature.

**Visit Shogran**

Shogran is usually the first place to visit when in the Kaghan Valley. It is a small village resort located just 10km away from Kiwai, on the road to Naran. Shogran is surrounded by beautiful pine forests and offers an incomparable mountain panorama. Once there, make sure to visit the Pine Park gardens. Later on, you can hire a jeep to take you to the plateau of Sri Paya and enjoy some more breathtaking views.

**Stay at Naran**

Naran should be the next stop of your journey. This small town is the tourist center of the region, with over 100 hotels you can stay at, plus many restaurants and shops. Naran is a starting point of many climbing and hiking trips to popular destinations like Lulusar Lake, Dudipatsar Lake, Babusar Pass, the famous Saiful Muluk Lake, and many more. Fishing, climbing and pony riding are only a small part of the activities Naran could offer.

**Visit the Saiful ****Muluk**** ****Lake******

Just 10km east of Naran, visiting this place is a definite must-do. The locals believe that, attracted by its beauty, fairies come to the lake every full moon. There&rsquo;s even a popular legend about the lake that tells the story of a prince who fell in love with a fairy. Saiful Muluk impresses with its crystal clear water, a result of the surrounding glaciers, and with a stunning view to the Malika Parbat Mountain in the background.

**Climb the Babusar Top**

Babusar Top is located at the Babusar Pass at a height of 4173m, roughly 80km away from Naran. Keep in mind that the road from Naran to Babusar is closed from October to June due to the excessive amounts of snow. On a clear day, you get an incredible view of the surrounding area, and if you&rsquo;re lucky enough, you may even be able to see the Nanga Parbat in the distance--that&rsquo;s the 9th highest peak on Earth, with a height of 8125m.

**Go Fishing**

Fishing is one of the main activities the Kaghan Valley could offer. You can practically go fishing at any of the lakes in the area, but consider going to the Kunhar River. The river is very famous with its trout and mahasher. Don&rsquo;t forget to get a permit from the Fishery Department at Naran or Shinu.
