Kamra is a small town in Attock District of Punjab province, about 70 kilometres northwest of Islamabad along the Grand Trunk Road. The town is best known as the site of the Pakistan Aeronautical Complex (PAC), one of the country's main military aviation facilities, which means parts of the area are restricted and photography can attract unwanted attention.

For travellers, Kamra is a place you pass through rather than visit. The Grand Trunk Road runs right through it, and the town functions as a transit point between Islamabad and Peshawar. The old road here follows the same route that armies, traders, and travellers have used for centuries — Attock Fort, where the Indus River is crossed, is about 20 kilometres further west and worth a stop for its Mughal-era fortifications and the dramatic confluence of the Indus and Haro rivers.

The surrounding Attock District has a dry, rocky landscape that contrasts sharply with the green Pothohar Plateau to the east. Roadside dhabas (truck-stop restaurants) along the GT Road serve solid Punjabi food — daal, roti, and karahi — at low prices.
