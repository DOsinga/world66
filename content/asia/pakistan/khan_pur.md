Khanpur is a town in southern Punjab's Rahim Yar Khan district, sitting on the main Karachi-Peshawar railway line near the edge of the Cholistan Desert. Before Partition it served as a district headquarters in the Bahawalpur princely state, and that older importance still shows in the density of forts and historic sites scattered across the surrounding desert.

The main draw here is Cholistan itself. Pakistan's largest desert stretches south and east of town, dotted with the ruins of forts, ancient settlements, and traces of the long-vanished Hakra River, a branch of the old Sarasvati system. The [Bhong Mosque](/asia/pakistan/khan_pur/things_to_do/bhongmosque), about 50 kilometers away, won an Aga Khan Award for Architecture and is the area's most celebrated building.

Khanpur is not a tourist town. You come here as a base for exploring the Cholistan sites or because the railway brought you. Urdu, Punjabi, and Saraiki are all spoken. The pace is slow, the hospitality is genuine, and the desert light at dusk makes the ruined forts worth the dusty drive.
