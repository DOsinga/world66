Built over several decades by local landlord Rais Ghazi Muhammad, the Bhong Mosque is an extraordinary piece of folk architecture. The interior is covered in marble inlay, mirror work, carved wood, and calligraphy in a dizzying mix of styles. It won the Aga Khan Award for Architecture in 1986, bringing international attention to a village mosque in rural Sindh-Punjab borderlands. The mosque sits in the village of Bhong, about 50 kilometers from Khanpur.
