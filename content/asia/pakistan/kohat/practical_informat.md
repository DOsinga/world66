Armed with this **Kohat practical information **you will have a better chance to explore this destination. This town is located in the Khyber-Pakhtunkhwa Province in Pakistan and has a colorful history as well as old buildings that are relics from the past that abound the town. Here is some of the practical information that you need to know about Kohat.

**The History of Kohat**

The earliest history of Kohat is vague since there are no exact recordings when and who established the town. In fact, the only history that has been recorded for this town dates back to the 18th century when Kohat was under control of the Sikhs. The occupancy of Sikhs in Kohat is one of the darkest events that ever happened to this town. Following the rule of the Sikhs, Kohat came under British dominion during 1849 which was also short-lived since locals went on an uprising shortly after being held under the British rule.

**Religion in Kohat**

Contrary to what most people think, Kohat&rsquo;s main religion is not Hinduism. In fact, a large population of Muslims resides in Kohat. Further, there are two types of Muslim population in the town these are the Sunni and Shias. The Islam community has been founded during the Sikhs rule. Aside from Islam, the British rule during the 1800s also established several Christian families who worked in the Municipal Government then. Now, these families live together with the Hindus that live in the Cantonment area where the old municipal government in Kohat is found.
