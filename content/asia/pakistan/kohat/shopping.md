type: section
---

The region of Kohat is located the northern part of Pakistan. **Kohat Shopping **includes small shops and bazaars. This former British fort is renowned for its eclectic finds.

**Anarkali Bazaar**

The Anarkali Bazaar is one of the oldest marketplaces in the nation. For history buffs the bazaar was named after a slave girl named Anarkali. According to legend this girl was found guilty for having an illicit relationship with the emperor&rsquo;s son and was buried alive in the mausoleum that is next to the market. The market itself is filled with items made by local artisans. There are very few things that cannot be found in the Anarkali Market. The tapestries, food, books, spices, and other items are just some of the finds here in this market. People shopping will find handmade carpets and blankets that have traditional Pakistani designs. The only limitation will be the amount of luggage allowed back home. There are several tea houses in the vicinity for people to take a break and enjoy the local culture. This bazaar is over 200 years old and is one of oldest surviving markets in Central Asia.

**Fortress Stadium**

Fortress Stadium is an open air-market that is especially popular with the Pakistani youth. There are number of food and shopping areas for people to check out. For parents with small children there a small amusement park on-site. This center is known for selling women and children&rsquo;s clothes and DVDs. There are a number of stores on this site and it is a great place to meet local people.

** Al Falah Square**

Al Falah Square is one of the most popular markets in Lahore, a neighboring city of Kohat. This marketplace is more similar to a Western style mall. Located in a modernized this building this marketplace is especially popular with native Pakistanis. The building is modernized with western chain stores that carry the latest in Pakistani fashion. For women interested in a designer sari, this is the place to go. There are also several art galleries specializing in local artists. For shoppers looking for an unique painting and a little local culture, Al Falah Square is the place to find them.
