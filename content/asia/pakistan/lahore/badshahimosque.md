The Great Mughal Mosque, commissioned by Emperor Aurangzeb in 1671 and completed in just over two years. For 313 years the largest mosque in the world; today it still holds 100,000 worshippers. The red sandstone and marble facade is one of the great pieces of Mughal architecture. Stands beside Lahore Fort at the north-west corner of the Old City — the pair are the defining image of historical Lahore.
