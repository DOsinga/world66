One of the oldest and largest museums in South Asia, established in 1894 on the Mall Road. Rudyard Kipling's father, John Lockwood Kipling, was one of the early curators; the museum and its surroundings feature in the opening chapters of *Kim*. The collection is strong on Gandhara Buddhist sculpture, Mughal miniatures, and Indus Valley artefacts. The Zamzama ("Kim's Gun") cannon stands outside the main entrance.
