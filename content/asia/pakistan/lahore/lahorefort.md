The UNESCO-listed Mughal citadel at the north-west corner of the Old City, built and rebuilt by successive emperors — Akbar, Jahangir, Shah Jahan, Aurangzeb — each adding their own marble and inlay-stone contributions. The **Sheesh Mahal** (Palace of Mirrors) with its inlaid-mica ceiling is the highlight, along with the **Diwan-i-Khas** and the **Naulakha Pavilion**. A sound and light show runs on Monday, Thursday, and Saturday evenings.
