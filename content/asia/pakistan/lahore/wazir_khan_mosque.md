A 17th-century Mughal mosque in the Walled City, completed in 1634 and celebrated for having some of the finest tile-mosaic (*kashi-kari*) work in South Asia. The entrance, just inside the Delhi Gate of the old city, opens through a small bazaar; a small tip to the chowkidar will let you climb one of the minarets for a view over the Old City rooftops.
