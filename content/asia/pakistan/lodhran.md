Lodhran is a small district town in southern Punjab, Pakistan, sitting near the Sutlej River in the heart of the cotton belt. It's a working agricultural town rather than a tourist destination — the surrounding district is known for cotton, wheat, and handicrafts.

Travelers passing through the region will find Lodhran a functional stopover between Multan (about 80 km to the northeast) and Bahawalpur to the south. There isn't much here to detain you, but the town has a busy bazaar and the flat Punjab landscape stretching to the horizon has a quiet character of its own.
