---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Multan is a city of shrines. The most rewarding way to spend a day here is to walk between the great Sufi tombs of the old fort area — Shah Rukn-e-Alam, Bahauddin Zakariya, Shah Shams Tabrez — stopping for tea and street food along the way, and then dive into the crowded lanes of the old bazaar around Haram Gate and Bohar Gate for pottery, *khussa* leather slippers, and the famous Multani *sohan halwa*. Most of the sights are concentrated in and around the raised mound of the old Multan Fort; ask a local for the direction of each shrine and you will rarely be more than a few minutes' walk from the next one.
