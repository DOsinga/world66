**Murree practical information **will lead you to one of the favorite summer destinations of tourists who visit Pakistan because of its cool and invigorating surroundings. About 7,500 feet above sea level, Murree provides visitors with a variety of outdoor experiences that only Mother Nature can offer, like the picturesque mountain scenes, sparkling clean streams, and the incredible flora and fauna. If you want a peaceful and quiet, relaxed vacation in communion with nature, then Murree is the paradise for you.

**Visa and Passports**

You need to have a visa and passport to get in and out of Pakistan. Within the country, you can use your passport. Some establishments demand both though, so you must have alternative plans in case this happens. Since Pakistan locals are teetotalers, you will have to present your foreign passport if you want some hard liquor. There are no government-licensed bars and cocktail lounges in the country.

**Language Spoken**

Locals speak in the Paharai dialect, which is a mixture of the language spoken from Kashmir and Hazara. Because of British influence when it was a British Hill, Murree is one of the highly literate areas of Pakistan, so most locals have ample knowledge of the English language. There are several educational institutions in the area.

**Local Currency**

The local currency is Pakistan Rupee or PKR. The currency conversion of $1 is 85.33 PKR or Rs 85.33. You can convert your dollars to Rs at the airport or in your hotel. Do not attempt to do so in the black market; it may be higher, but dangerous.

**Climate and Clothing**

Murree has 4 seasons: spring, summer, autumn and winter. Summer is the best time to visit starting from the month of May to August where temperatures range from 13 degrees Celsius, (55.4 degrees Fahrenheit) to 29 degrees Celsius (84 degrees Fahrenheit). You will have to bring summer clothes to wear during the day and light sweaters for the evenings as they can become chilly. During spring, autumn and winter, you will have to bring thicker clothing as the temperatures are colder than summer. If you plan to visit during winter, then bring thick winter clothes as this season brings snowfall and hailstorms.

**Safety Concerns**

The Taliban movement has been an alleged constant threat to tourists in Pakistan. If the weather is good, tourists are often advised to fly by plane to Islamabad the capital of Pakistan and then drive to the beautiful town of Murree on a guided tour. Driving to Murree is an extraordinary adventure with the awesome and breathtaking scenery. Joining a group tour or having a legitimate and trusted local guide is the best option.
