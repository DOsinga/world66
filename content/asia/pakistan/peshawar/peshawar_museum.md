Housed in an imposing 1906 British-era building, the Peshawar Museum holds one of the world's finest collections of Gandharan Buddhist sculpture — the Greek-influenced Buddhist art produced in this region between the 1st and 5th centuries. Also sections on Muslim-era calligraphy, tribal life, and ethnography.
