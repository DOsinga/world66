**Peshawar Shopping** gives tourists lots of excitement.

**Jans Shopping Mall**

Jans is one of the biggest shopping malls in Peshawar. People can find fine apparel, lingerie, designer shoes and traditional clothing. The mall is full during the weekends when teenagers and students from the local university flock to the mall in order to buy the latest fashions and to spend time together.

**Ander Shahar**

Glittering gold shops can be seen in Ander Shahar to the west of Chowk Yadgar. People can be seen competing for the best bargains in Afghan-run shops full of antiques.

**Chowk Yadgar**

Chowk Yadgar is in the heart of old city that has been reconstructed to be a plaza. The money exchangers exhibit their currencies along the west side. Private moneychangers are also seen on Saddar Roadd near Green&rsquo;s Hotel.

The banks like Habib Bank on Saddar Road, National Bank Bajori Road, near Rampur Gate, Saddar Road, Standard Chartered Bank on Islamia Road do service for travellers cheques. Meena Bazaar is a haven of shops for women.

**Mochi Lara**

Mochi Lara is near Qissa Khawani, which sells Pashtun chappals and leather bags at low price. At the end of the Qissa Khawani Bazaar, take a sharp left turn that leads to a bazaar full of coppersmiths. They are highly skilled and have international standards.

Work by a popular crafstman named Ali also known as "Poor Honest Ali" is sold at Mochi Lara. Most of this popular craftsman's work has been gifted to many important political figures and even to Queen Elizabeth II of Great Britain. Peshawar Shopping cannot be complete without visiting the fine jewelry markets that have ornaments made out of silver and gold.

One can also find a big market for Peshawar shopping of leather goods selling Peshawari chappals, belts, hostlers and robust suitcases called Yakdhaan. One cannot forget to visit Peshawar's market where they sell traditinal food such as Tikkas and Kabbab.
