The list of **Peshawar things to do** given here will help you decide on how best to tour this beautiful, frontier town of Pakistan. It is an ancient town that saw the likes of Alexander the Great and Babur in scribe history. It was the pathway for many great civilizations like the Aryans, Persians, Greeks, Mughals and the Mongols.

#### Visit the Famous Khyber Pass

Khyber Pass, the most legendary pass of the ancient business world, is located at a close 16km from Peshawar. It is rich with historical stories of trade, invasion and migration. Your tour of the Pass will start with the Bab-e-Khyber, a gate built in 1964 marking the entry point. The Pass extends from the foot of the Suleiman Range of hills to the border of Pakistan at Torkham.

Tourists are allowed to go up to the Michni Checkpost. A half hour journey from this point leads you to Afghanistan. The most fascinating aspect of this place is the fact that you are in tribal land after this point. Here Pakistani laws no longer apply, and give way to tribal laws of the Khyber Agency. It is one of the 7 agencies that constitute the tribal area. The tribal people here make their own weapons. You will see signboards on the roadside, asking you not to wander off the main highway. As a strong precautionary measure, stay close to the armed escorts that accompany visitors at all times.

Protect yourself from the forces of nature with appropriate clothing and accessories.

#### Enjoy Strolling in the Qisa Khawani Bazaar

The Qisa Khawani Bazaar, or the &ldquo;Street of story-tellers,&rdquo; extends right across the city from the west to the east. During earlier times, it was the camping ground for the military and traveling caravans. The market place gets its mystic name from the presence of professional storytellers entertained soldiers and travelers with tales of war and love. The storytellers are gone now, but the atmosphere is reminiscent with them. Sip on the green tea that is available in plenty over here. The streets are crowded, but it is possible to still take in the historic feel of the place, where things are still the same even after 1000 years.

A point to be noted is that Peshawar is the land of Pathans, a male dominated society. Dress conservatively so as to not cause any offense.

#### Revisit History in the Peshawar Museum

The Peshawar Museum was built in 1905 during the British colonialist regime. During this time, it was called the Victoria Memorial Hall. It houses many ancient relics and historic objects of ancient civilizations. The most important object to see here is the Kanishka casket. It is said to have contained 3 fragments of the bone of Lord Buddha when it was archeologically excavated in 1908. The bones were given to the Buddhist Society in Burma by the British, and the casket is on display in Peshawar.

You need to pay a moderate entry fee to tour the museum.
