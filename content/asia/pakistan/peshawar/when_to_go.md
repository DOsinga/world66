**Choosing When to Visit ****Peshawar******

If you are planning to visit **Peshawar****. when to go** is a decision you will have to carefully make. The area experiences continental climate and each of the 3 seasons has its own specifics.

#### **Cool Season**

The cool season lasts from October to February, when average daily temperatures are about 20 degrees Celsius (68 degrees Fahrenheit). This provides an excellent opportunity for hiking, and you can visit all of the nearby historic sights without worrying about the burning sun.

#### **Hot Season**

The hot season lasts from March to June. This is the greatest time to visit Peshawar if you dislike cool weather. Temperatures are usually over 30 degrees Celsius (86 degrees Fahrenheit) but never above 40 degrees Celsius (104 degrees Fahrenheit). The heat is bearable, and you can still walk around the city untroubled.

#### **Wet Season**

The wet season lasts from July to September. This is when the monsoon strikes, bringing heavy thunderstorms in the whole country. However, Peshawar isn&rsquo;t affected as much as the rest of Pakistan, so you can still visit the city during the wet season and enjoy pleasant weather. When it rains, the weather causes serious discomfort due to the high humidity levels combined with temperatures of over 35 degrees Celsius (95 degrees Fahrenheit).

#### **Tourist**** ****Peak**

Keep in mind that although the wet season strikes at this time of the year, there is a tourist peak between June and August, and you may experience difficulties finding accommodation. Prices tend to be a little higher. Many tourists aren&rsquo;t aware and choose to visit Peshawar during the wet season without booking a hotel, because they expect the weather will put off most of the visitors. This is not the case here, so always make reservations to be sure you will find accommodation.

#### **Ramadan**

Visiting the city during the Ramadan may also be challenging. Ramadan&rsquo;s date isn&rsquo;t steady, but the Ramadan month is usually in August and September. This is an important religious holiday for all Muslims, affecting the business hours of most local establishments which usually results in all banks, restaurants and shops closing earlier.
