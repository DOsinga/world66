---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Quetta is connected to the rest of Pakistan by road, rail and air, though all three approaches are slow. PIA flies daily from [Karachi](/asia/pakistan/karachi), Lahore and Islamabad into Quetta International Airport. The RCD Highway runs south to Karachi and west via Koh-e-Taftan into Iran (Tehran is 1,435 km away); the road via Sibi connects to Punjab and upper Sindh; the road via Loralai, Fort Munro and DG Khan is the shorter route to [Multan](/asia/pakistan/multan); and the Chaman Road is the link to the Afghan border, four hours away.

Pakistan Railways runs the famous Bolan Mail and Jaffar Express from Karachi up the Bolan Pass — a long, slow journey across the desert and then a steep climb into the mountains, but a memorable one. Foreign travellers should expect to need a No Objection Certificate and may be required to take a police escort on arrival.
