---
title: "Festivals in Rahim Yar Khan"
type: section
---

Many **Rahim Yar Kahn festivals, **like the rest of Pakistani festivals, are religious festivals. However, there are a few others that memorialize national heroes and special political events in history. The Muslim festivals will occur ten days earlier each year than the year before due to the Muslim lunar calendar. Some festivals in Pakistan are listed below.

**Shab-e-Barat**

This is a religious festival that is celebrated in the 8th Islamic calendar month. It offers the exchange of sweets to relatives and friends, along with some fireworks and prayers.

**Eid-ul-Fitr**

This religious festival takes place on the first day of the 10th month on the Islamic calendar. It is to celebrate the end of a month of fasting.

**May Day**

This event is celebrated throughout the entire country and is marked by parades and meetings. It is celebrated on May 1st.

**Pakistan**** Day**

This day is in memory of the Pakistan Resolution that was passed by the Muslims of South Asia in 1940. It is held at Lahore Peshawar, the main feature being a big parade at Islamabad. This celebration takes place on March 23rd.

**Lok mela**

This is a folk festival held at Islamabad that showcases music contests, folk dances, craftsmen showing off their skills, and sales of crafts. It is a great place to go to witness the culture of the entire country in one location. It lasts for 1 week during the first week of October.

**Quaid-e-Azam**

The 11th of September is the anniversary of the death of Quaid-e-Azam, Mohammad Ali Jinnah. He was the father of the nation.
