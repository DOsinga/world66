---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Rahim Yar Khan"
type: section
---

If you are expecting **Rahim Yar Khan nightlife** to be anything like it is in the West you'll be in for a disappointment. However there are fun things to do in the evening that don't have anything to do with alcohol or partying in the Western sense.

**Street Life**

Whereas the nightlife in the West really happens in bars and pubs, in Rahim Yar Khan people are more likely to go walking around with their families on the streets perhaps looking into the public park which isn't in a great condition these days. Men will tend to sit outside their homes having heated discussions about politicians, the army or public policies. In the summer, the vogue is to head down to the canals for a picnic. The best picnic spot is arguably "Head Adam Sahaba" which is about eight km from the city center.

**Hotels and Restaurants**

The nearest Rahim Yar Khan nightlife gets to Western-style nightlife is in its hotels and restaurants where you may not be able to find much in the way of alcohol but you can find great food, conversation and social pleasures. Tiki Raam is a favorite restaurant of many, serving its specialty of daal lentil soup, capati bread and makhani. The Quetta Hotel, about 50 km from Rahim Yar Khan, close to Zahir Peer serves great traditional Pakistani food especially mutton rosh. In other ways this isn't a standout venue in Rahim Yar Khan nightlife as the facilities aren't great but it isn't a bad place to hang out.

**Variety is the Spice of Life**

Other nightspots in the city offer a great range of cuisines. For the outdoor barbecue experience, try Cafe Teria, located in the city center at the Noshehra Minor Canal. Other restaurants in this mode are Al-Kabab and Club N Cafe.

**Getting Modern**

If your notion of Rahim Yar Khan nightlife revolves around a sophisticated contemporary restaurant, then you could go to Cafe Lamis and Diwan-E-Khas which both serve English, Continental and Chinese dishes. The surroundings are chic and the clientele are pleasantly amiable. The food is first-class and cooked by master chefs. If none of the above suit your nightlife tastes then Rahim Yar Khan residents would recommend perhaps another 20 first-class cafes and restaurants that are more popular with locals than travelers.
