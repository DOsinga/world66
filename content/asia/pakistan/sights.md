---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Pakistan's sights span 5,000 years of continuous history and some of the most extreme landscapes on earth. The challenge is that the best of them are spread across a country the size of France and Spain combined.

**Moenjodaro** (Sindh) is the most remarkable archaeological site in South Asia and one of the most important in the world. Founded around 2500 BC, this city of the Indus Valley Civilization had a grid layout, covered sewers, and public baths at a time when most of Europe was still in the Stone Age. It sits 400 km north of [Karachi](/asia/pakistan/karachi), reached by air or overnight train to Larkana. See [Moenjodaro](/asia/pakistan/moenjodaro) for details.

**Lahore's Mughal core** is extraordinary. The Badshahi Mosque (1673) is one of the largest mosques in the world and still startling in its scale and refinement. The Lahore Fort, the Shalimar Gardens, and the Wazir Khan Mosque round out a UNESCO-listed ensemble that rewards a full day of unhurried walking. The old city bazaars — food, spices, fabric — are equally worth exploring.

**Taxila**, 35 km from [Islamabad](/asia/pakistan/islamabad), is a UNESCO World Heritage Site covering several ancient cities and Buddhist monasteries spanning 1,000 years of history, from the 5th century BC to the 2nd century AD. The Taxila Museum holds the best of what's been excavated. The site itself is peaceful and rarely crowded.

**The Karakoram Highway** is less a sight than an experience: the world's highest paved international road, running 1,300 km from [Islamabad](/asia/pakistan/islamabad) to the Chinese border at Khunjerab Pass. The scenery along the final stretch through [Hunza](/asia/pakistan/hunzavalley) is extraordinary. See [Karakoram Highway](/asia/pakistan/karakoram_highway).

**Baltit Fort** and **Altit Fort** in the Hunza Valley are ancient fortresses perched on cliffs above the valley floor. Baltit Fort has been well restored by the Aga Khan Cultural Service and gives a vivid picture of how Hunza's rulers lived. Both are worth half a day each.

**The Khyber Pass** has been a strategic crossing between Central Asia and the subcontinent for millennia. The road through it from [Peshawar](/asia/pakistan/peshawar) is accessible to foreigners with a permit (arrange through local tour operators) and the landscape alone justifies the trip. See [Khyber Pass](/asia/pakistan/khyberpass).

**Peshawar's old city** — particularly the Qissa Khwani Bazaar (Street of Storytellers) — is one of the most atmospheric urban spaces in Pakistan. The traffic and noise are part of the experience.

**Thatta** (Sindh), 98 km east of Karachi, holds the Shah Jahan Mosque — a 17th-century Mughal mosque with spectacular blue tilework — and the vast Makli Necropolis, one of the largest funerary sites in the world with over a million tombs. Both are UNESCO-listed and badly undervisited.
