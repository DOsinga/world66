Originally called *Fong-Khar* — "Palace on the Rock" in Balti — this 400-year-old fort-palace sits on an outcrop above the Shigar Valley. It was the seat of the Raja of Shigar, and the Aga Khan Cultural Service Pakistan spent six years restoring it, reopening it in 2005. The restoration is careful and thorough: carved wooden ceilings, a traditional garden, and the original structure's relationship with the surrounding rock have all been preserved. The fort now operates as a small heritage hotel, which means visitors can stay overnight in one of the restored rooms — one of the more memorable places to sleep in northern Pakistan. Shigar town itself is a quiet base for the Karakoram and Baltoro Glacier approaches.
