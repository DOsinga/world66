Khaplu sits at the confluence of the Hushe and Shyok rivers in Baltistan, a green oasis in an otherwise stark mountain landscape. It was once the capital of a small Balti kingdom, and the legacy shows in the beautifully restored Chaqchan Mosque — one of the oldest in the region, dating to the 14th century — and the Khaplu Palace, a grand timber-and-stone residence perched above the town with views across the valley to the Karakoram peaks.

The town is quieter and less visited than nearby [Skardu](/asia/pakistan/skardu), which makes it a good base for trekkers heading into the Hushe Valley toward Masherbrum and the Charakusa Glacier. Even if you are not trekking, the drive from Skardu along the Shyok River is spectacular, and Khaplu itself rewards a day or two of wandering — the old bazaar, the apricot orchards in season, and the views from the palace grounds are reason enough to come.
