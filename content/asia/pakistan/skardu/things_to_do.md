**general**

**Kharfocho Fort**

The fort was probably built by Ali Sher Khan Anchan. It offers nice views over the Valley. Ali Sher Khan ruled till the end of the 16th century and conquered Ladakh.  

**Mindoq Khar**

There are some remains of the 'Flower Palace'. The Palace was destroyed by the troops of the Sikh ruler of Kashmir, Maharaja Gulab Singh, when he invaded Skardu in 1840 AD.  

**Hilal Bagh**

Just below the Mindoq Khar or Flower Palace, there was a terraced garden with fountains built in marble. This royal garden covered the areas from Mindoq Khan to the present bazaar at Skardu where the newly constructed road crosses the channel.  

**Satpara Lake**

Some 10 km south of Skardu, lies the Satpara Lake. Surrounded by high glacial mountains, this lake has an island in the middle of its clear waters, which can be reached, by boat. The lake is considered ideal for fishing.  

**Kachura Lake**

About 32 kms from Skardu lie the shimmering waters of the Kachura Lake. The lake offers opportunities for trout fishing.  

**Shigar Valley**

Shigar Valley is about 30 km from Skardu over bad roads / tracks. Count on 2 Hours by jeep. It forms the gateway to the great mountain peaks of the Karakoram, including Mount K-2.  **hendrickx :** 

**Khaplu Valley**

More than 100 km fro Skardu is the Khaplu valley. The valley offers sights on the Masherbrum, K-6, K-7, Sherpi Kangh, Sia Kangri and Saltoro Kangri.  

**Buddhist Rock**

There is only one surviving Buddhist Rock with rock carvings in the Skardu Valley located on Satpara Road.
