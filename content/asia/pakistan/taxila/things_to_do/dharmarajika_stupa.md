---
title: "Dharmarajika Stupa"
type: poi
category: "Sight"
latitude: 33.7536
longitude: 72.8514
---

The oldest and most venerated Buddhist monument in Taxila, believed to have been founded by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BC to enshrine relics of the Buddha. The main stupa rises from a circular base surrounded by a ring of smaller votive stupas, chapels, and monastery cells added over the centuries. The site gives a powerful sense of the scale of Buddhist devotion here -- at its peak, monks from across Asia came to study and worship at this spot.
