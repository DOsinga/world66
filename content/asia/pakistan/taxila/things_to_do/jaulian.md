A hilltop Buddhist monastery and stupa complex from the 2nd to 5th century AD, perched above the valley with fine views. Jaulian is particularly worth visiting for its well-preserved stucco sculptures -- Buddha figures, attendants, and decorative panels that still retain remarkable detail after fifteen centuries. The monastery's cells, courtyard, and assembly hall give a clear picture of monastic life. It is a short but steep climb from the road, and the effort is rewarded by both the art and the panorama.
