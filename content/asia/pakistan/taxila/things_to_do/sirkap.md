The second city of Taxila, built by the Bactrian Greeks in the 2nd century BC on a neat Hippodamian grid plan that still shows clearly in the excavated ruins. The main street runs arrow-straight with side streets branching at right angles, lined with the foundations of shops, houses, and temples. The most famous find here is the Double-Headed Eagle Stupa, a small shrine decorated with Classical, Hindu, and Buddhist motifs side by side -- a perfect symbol of Taxila's cultural crossroads. Walking through Sirkap gives you a vivid feel for daily life in an ancient Gandharan city.
