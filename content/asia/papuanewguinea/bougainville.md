**Bougainville** is the largest island in the Solomon Islands chain, though politically it belongs to [Papua New Guinea](/asia/papuanewguinea) as an autonomous region. It is a lush, mountainous island with dense tropical rainforest, active volcanoes, and a complex recent history.

The island was devastated by a civil war from 1988 to 1998, triggered by disputes over the Panguna copper mine — one of the largest in the world. The mine remains closed and the island is still recovering, but Bougainville voted overwhelmingly for independence in a 2019 referendum. The political situation continues to evolve.

Visiting Bougainville requires planning. Infrastructure is basic, accommodation limited, and travel is mostly by small boat or on rough roads. But for those who make the effort, the island offers pristine reefs, WWII wreck sites, and a glimpse of a society rebuilding itself. Buka, the main town on the smaller northern island, is the usual arrival point by air from Port Moresby.
