---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Port Moresby"
type: section
---

**Port Moresby must do's** include visiting gardens, museums and markets.
Port Moresby is a large bustling city, and is the capital of Papua New
Guinea. The tourism industry in this area is growing and there are many
attractions
to visit. Visitors can enjoy the variety of shopping options or sample
some of the delicious local food. Jacksons International Airport offers
easy access to Port Moresby making it a convenient place to visit.

**Botanical Gardens**

These gardens offer an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
Families can enjoy walking through the gardens and admiring the
beautiful scenery. The Botanical Gardens are located on the University
of Papa New Guinea
campus. The gardens were established in 1971. Inside the well kept
gardens are a mini-rain forest, palms, bamboos and an orchid garden. Within
the gardens you can see different types of Papua New Guinea wildlife.
For bird enthusiasts there is a variety of birds to see, and some birds are kept in
cages for visitors to view.
The Botanical Gardens are also used as a venue for weddings. If you
happen to be there at the right time you will be able to get a glimpse
of the wedding ceremony. Take mosquito spray with you to avoid being
bitten by mosquitoes.

**Ela Beach Craft Market**

Mingle with the locals and find hidden treasures amongst the market
stalls. At the market you can get lost among the colors, sounds and
mass of people. Carvings and handwoven baskets are among the goods on
offer at this market. The market is overseen by the Ela Murray
International School. It takes place on the last Saturday of every
month. Local goods are brought from all over Papa New Guinea to be sold
at this market.

**Moitaka Wildlife Sanctuary**

Moitaka Wildlife Sanctuary is located on the outskirts of Port Moresby.
At this sanctuary you can see crocodiles and a variety of bird species. Research is carried out on crocodiles at the sanctuary. There is a show
once a week when the crocodiles are fed.

**Scuba Diving**

There are many places to go scuba diving in the areas that surround
Port Moresby. Places that offer scuba diving include diving shops and
resorts. There are scuba diving conditions to
suit all levels of experience. While scuba diving you can see beautiful
coral reefs as well as a variety of fish species. There are also wrecks
which offer exciting areas to explore. Conditions are good for
underwater photography and there are many opportunities to take
pictures.

**Port Moresby National Museum**

Port Moresby National Museum is located on Independence Hill near next
to the Parliament building. A visit to the museum will give you insight
into the regions history and cultural traditions. Exhibitions at the museum include pottery and art displays.
