---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

PNG produces some of the finest traditional art in the Pacific. The best place to buy is directly from artisans at village markets or through reputable craft shops, which avoids intermediaries and puts money in the right hands.

Sepik carvings are the most celebrated: ancestor figures, crocodile sculptures, spirit masks, and carved sago storage hooks. Quality varies enormously — much of what is sold in tourist shops is mass-produced. If you want a genuine piece, buy in the Sepik region itself, from the villages where the tradition is alive.

In the Highlands, look for intricately woven bilum bags (netted string bags made from bark fibre or wool, used to carry everything from vegetables to babies), carved bows and arrows, kina shell ornaments, and Huli wigs. Bilums are genuinely useful and come in every size and colour.

In Port Moresby, the NCD Craft Market at Ela Beach operates on weekends and has a good selection. The PNG Arts and Artefacts shop in Waigani sells better-quality pieces. Most large hotels have small craft shops. The Boroko area has general shops and a covered market.

PNG coffee is an excellent purchase: tightly regulated Arabica from the Highlands, exported under regional certifications including Organic and Fair Trade. Look for it at the airport or at specialty food shops in Port Moresby. Packaged locally, it travels well.
