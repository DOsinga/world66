Rabaul was once the most beautiful city in Papua New Guinea, a harbour town ringed by volcanic cones on the Gazelle Peninsula of East New Britain. Twin eruptions of Tavurvur and Vulcan in 1994 buried much of the old town under ash, and the provincial capital shifted to Kokopo, 20 km away. What remains has a haunting, post-apocalyptic charm — half-buried buildings, ash plains, and the still-smoking Tavurvur visible across the harbour.

The real draw is underwater. Japanese warships, aircraft, and submarines sunk during World War II litter the harbour floor, making Rabaul one of the Pacific's premier wreck-diving destinations. On land, you can explore Japanese tunnel networks carved into the hillsides and the well-preserved war memorial at Bita Paka. The nearby Duke of York Islands offer a quieter side trip with good snorkelling and traditional village life.
