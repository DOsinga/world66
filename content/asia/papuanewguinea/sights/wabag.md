Wabag is the capital of Enga Province, sitting at around 1,900 metres in the central highlands of Papua New Guinea. The real draw is the journey itself: the road from Mount Hagen winds through some of the most dramatic mountain scenery in the country, climbing to passes above 3,000 metres with views over deeply furrowed ranges. The town is small and quiet, serving mainly as an administrative centre for the surrounding Enga-speaking communities. A modest local market and a few basic guesthouses are the main facilities. Wabag also hosts a small art scene, with young artists creating and displaying work in local galleries.
