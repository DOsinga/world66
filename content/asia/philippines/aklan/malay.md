Malay is a municipality on the northwestern tip of Panay Island in Aklan province, and for most travellers it means one thing: the gateway to Boracay. The Caticlan Jetty Port, where boats depart for the ten-minute crossing to the island, is in Malay's Caticlan barangay.

If your flight arrives at Caticlan Airport (officially Godofredo P. Ramos Airport), you are already in Malay -- the airport is a short tricycle ride from the jetty. Travellers arriving by bus from Kalibo or other parts of Panay will be dropped at the Caticlan transport terminal.

Beyond being a transit point, Malay has its own quiet appeal. The mainland coast has some undeveloped beaches, and the surrounding countryside is green and hilly. But most visitors pass through quickly, eyes fixed on the white sand across the water.
