Alaminos City in [Pangasinan](/asia/philippines/pangasinan) province is the gateway to the Hundred Islands National Park, a scattering of 124 mushroom-shaped islands and islets in the Lingayen Gulf. Most visitors come for the island-hopping — you hire a boat from Lucap Wharf and spend the day snorkelling, kayaking and jumping off limestone cliffs into clear water.

Governor Island, Quezon Island and Children's Island are the most developed, with basic facilities, but the real appeal is the quieter islands where you can have a beach to yourself. The town itself is a practical jumping-off point rather than a destination, though the seafood market near the wharf is worth a stop.
