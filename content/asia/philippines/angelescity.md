Angeles City sits in the heart of Pampanga province on the island of Luzon, about 80 kilometers north of Manila. For decades it was known primarily for Clark Air Base and the entertainment strip that grew up around it, but the city has been steadily reinventing itself since the Americans left in 1991.

The eruption of Mount Pinatubo that same year devastated the region, yet today the volcano is one of the area's biggest draws — you can trek to its crater lake or fly over it in a small plane from Clark Field. The former air base has become the Clark Freeport Zone, a sprawling economic hub with duty-free shopping, casinos, golf courses, and an international airport that brings in direct flights from across Asia.

Angeles City is also the culinary capital of Pampanga, a province famous across the Philippines for its food. Sisig — the sizzling dish of chopped pig face seasoned with calamansi and chili — was invented here. You will find it everywhere, from street-side stalls to proper restaurants, alongside a surprisingly diverse international dining scene.

The city makes a good base for exploring Central Luzon. Mount Arayat, the beaches of Subic Bay, and the historic town of San Fernando are all easy day trips, and the jeepneys and tricycles that crowd the streets will get you around cheaply if not always comfortably.
