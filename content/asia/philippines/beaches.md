---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The Philippines has some of the best beaches in Southeast Asia, and the country is large enough that you can almost always find a quiet one if you're willing to travel.

**[Palawan](/asia/philippines/palawanisland)** consistently tops lists of the world's best islands, and the beaches around El Nido and Coron justify the reputation. Limestone karst formations rise from turquoise lagoons, the snorkelling is excellent, and the crowds, while growing, remain manageable outside peak season. The Underground River at Sabang is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and easily combined with a few days on the beach.

**[Boracay](/asia/philippines/boracay)** is the country's most developed beach — White Beach is a long sweep of powdery sand lined with bars and restaurants, busy from November through April. The island shut down entirely in 2018 for an environmental rehabilitation program and emerged noticeably cleaner. It remains the place for nightlife and resort-style comfort.

**Siargao** in the northeastern Mindanao region is the surfing capital of the Philippines. Cloud 9 is the famous wave, but there is also island hopping to pristine sandbars, paddle-boarding in the Sugba Lagoon, and a generally laid-back scene that has attracted a permanent community of long-term travelers.

**[Bohol](/asia/philippines/bohol)** and the nearby Panglao Island offer white sand beaches and some of the best diving in the Visayas, including the Balicasag marine sanctuary. Combine with a trip to see the Chocolate Hills and the Philippine tarsier.

The **Batanes** islands, at the northern tip of the archipelago, have a completely different feel — dramatic cliffs, rolling grasslands, and stone-walled houses built to withstand typhoons. The beaches are not tropical-postcard material, but the landscape is unlike anywhere else in the Philippines.
