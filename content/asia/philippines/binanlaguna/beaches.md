title: "Beaches"

---

Binan and Laguna province have no ocean beaches, but the lakes and hot springs more than compensate. If you are after water, you will not be disappointed.

#### Laguna de Bay

Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake borders Binan to the north and east. The shoreline areas near the city are popular with locals for swimming, especially schoolchildren cooling off in the afternoon heat. Some parts of the lake are deep and currents can be unpredictable, so exercise caution, particularly with children.

#### Lake Caliraya

An enclosed lake elsewhere in Laguna that feels like a beach minus the sand. Popular for speed boating, water skiing, and jet ski lessons. You can rent a jet ski and zip around the lake, which is one of the genuine highlights of being in Laguna. Snorkelling is possible though the water can be murky at certain times of year.

#### Hidden Valley Springs

Laguna's most famous hot springs, tucked away in the town of Alaminos. Run as a resort and spa, the springs sit in a secluded valley. The sulphur-rich water is known for its skin benefits. A good half-day escape if you want to soak and decompress.
