A list of **Binan Laguna things to do** includes several activities from exploring the town center to swimming in a hot spring.

#### Exploring the Binan Town Center

In Binan&rsquo;s town proper, there are several notable historical landmarks that visitors should go to including the century old infrastructures scattered around the town. There are no fees to see these landmarks. Some of the most noteworthy places include the Albert House which was built in 1575. Visitors can also go to the Roman Catholic Church where they will find several historical landmarks nearby, including the Stone Steeple and the Meditation Garden.

#### Visiting the Different Falls

There are two major waterfalls near the town of Binan, one of which is the Buruwisan Falls in Siniloan. The Buruwisan Falls is one of the five falls in the Sierra Madre Mountain Ranges, and it is the most accessible. It has a 50-meter drop and features cool and clear water. The vegetation around the falls is unexplored, and this is a popular camping site. Aside from swimming in the waterfalls or camping beside them, mountain climbing is another popular activity in Buruwisan Falls. There is no entrance fee to get to the falls.

Across the Buruwisan Falls is anther popular waterfall, the Lanzones Falls. The river that is formed by both the Lanzones and Buruwisan Falls create the much bigger Binaytuan Falls which has approximately a 200-meter drop.

Visitors wanting more adventure can go to Pagsanjan Falls in Cavinti. To get to the falls, visitors need to travel the Pagsanjan River which is already an adventure. The river is known for its strong currents through 16 rapids. The boat ride is approximately 3 hours round trip. Expect to pay a nominal fee for the boat rental which will cover the entire trip.

#### Mountain Climbing

Mountain climbing is another popular activity in Binan. There are several locations for both beginners and professional hikers. One of the most popular areas is Mount Makiling that rises 1,109 meters above sea level. The mountain is actually an inactive volcano which accounts for the rich variety of plant species.

Mount Makiling has 2 major trails, one of which is suitable for beginners while the other is more suitable for professional hikers. Getting to Mount Makiling can be rigorous since the road is winding and steep. There is also no public transportation that travels directly to the area so it is advisable to hire a private car or jeep. The rate for transportation is generally between P100 and P300 in summer 2010. All visitors are expected to register at the San Bartolome Barangay Hall prior to entering Mount Makiling. An entrance fee of about P5 should also be expected.
