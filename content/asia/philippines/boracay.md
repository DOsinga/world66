Boracay is a seven kilometre slip of an island off the northern tip of Panay, and it has spent the last few decades on every "world's best beach" list ever written. The reason is **White Beach**, a four kilometre arc of powder-soft sand so fine it squeaks under your feet, washed by absurdly turquoise water and lined end to end with bars, dive shops, and barefoot restaurants.

The island is divided up by **boat stations**: Station 1 is the upmarket end with the wider beach and the smarter resorts, Station 2 is the busy middle where the party happens (D'Mall, the bars, the loud crowd), and Station 3 is the quieter, scruffier southern end where backpackers and long-stayers settle in. Walk the length of White Beach at sunset at least once — it is the island's defining ritual, with paraw sailboats tacking back to shore against an orange sky.

Boracay is more than a sunbed, though. The eastern side, **Bulabog Beach**, is one of Asia's premier kitesurfing and windsurfing destinations from November through April, when the *amihan* trade winds blow steadily across the reef. Divers come for the wall dives at **Yapak** and the wreck and coral sites scattered around the island. Walkers and bikers can trek north to **Puka Beach** for shells and quiet, or up **Mount Luho** for the view back over the whole island.

Be honest with yourself about what you want. If you came for tranquil, untouched paradise, you arrived twenty years too late — the centre of White Beach is loud, built up, and at peak season uncomfortably crowded. If you came for a tropical beach with great food, easy diving, fierce sunsets, and a party that goes until sunrise, this is exactly the place. The shoulder months of June and November tend to give the best balance of weather and breathing room.

Boracay was famously closed for six months in 2018 for an environmental clean-up, and it has come back tidier — fewer fly-by-night beach bars, cleaner water, and a (loosely enforced) limit on construction. It is still recovering its reputation, which is the traveller's gain.
