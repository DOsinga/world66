---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

**White Beach** on the western shore is the famous one — four kilometres of fine, almost cool-to-the-touch white sand washed by calm turquoise water. It is divided into Stations 1 (north, smarter), 2 (middle, busy) and 3 (south, mellower). On the eastern side, **Bulabog Beach** has rougher, shallower water perfect for kitesurfing and windsurfing during the *amihan* season (November through April).

For something quieter, head north to **Puka Beach** at the top of the island, where the crowds thin out and you can still find the puka shells the beach is named after. **Diniwid**, just around the corner from Station 1, is a smaller, mellower stretch reachable by a footpath cut into the cliff. **Ilig-Iligan** on the eastern side and **Baling Hai** on the northwest are tucked-away coves worth the boat ride.
