---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

You can eat very well on Boracay. The island runs the full range from cheap *carinderias* serving grilled chicken, *adobo* and rice, to beachfront seafood barbecues where you pick your fish at the market and have it cooked next door, to seriously good Italian, Spanish and Greek restaurants opened by the long-stay foreign community. **D'Talipapa** wet market near Station 2 is the place for fresh fish at sensible prices — buy it there, hand it to one of the surrounding *paluto* restaurants, and they will grill it for a small fee. Don't leave without trying *kalamansi* juice and a Boracay sunset cocktail.
