---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Boracay isn't a shopping destination, but you can pick up the usual beach-trip souvenirs. **D'Mall** behind Station 2 is the island's main shopping cluster — open-air, packed with stalls and small shops selling sarongs, swimwear, sunglasses, flip-flops, and cheap T-shirts, plus a small supermarket and a few real shops.

For local crafts, head to the **Plazoleta** at White Beach, where you'll find handmade leather bags from the T'boli people, beaded jewellery, sarongs, drums and other instruments. **D'Talipapa** market sells fresh seafood downstairs and trinkets, pearls and clothing upstairs — bargain hard. The stalls along the **White Beach pathway** are good for inexpensive sarongs and shell jewellery, and you can pick up a henna tattoo while you wait.
