title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Boracay has two seasons. The **dry season** runs roughly from late October to May, when the *amihan* trade winds blow steadily from the northeast. White Beach (on the western, leeward side) is calm and glassy, while the eastern Bulabog Beach gets the wind and turns into a kitesurfing playground. Temperatures sit comfortably between 24 and 32 degrees. This is peak season — December and January are the busiest and most expensive months, with Holy Week (March or April) close behind.

The **wet season** runs from June through September, when the *habagat* southwest monsoon brings rain, occasional typhoons, and rougher seas on the western side. Many beach businesses board up, prices drop sharply, and the island is much quieter — but some days are washed out entirely. The **shoulder months** of late May/early June and late October/early November are the sweet spot: warm, mostly dry, and noticeably less crowded.

## Festivals

The biggest event in the wider region is the **Ati-Atihan Festival** in nearby **Kalibo**, held in the third week of January in honour of the Santo Niño — a four-day street parade with drummers, dancers in tribal paint, and plenty of rum. On Boracay itself, the **Boracay International Funboard Cup** in mid-January draws professional kitesurfers and windsurfers to Bulabog. The **Boracay International Dragon Boat Festival** runs in April or May with races and beach parties along White Beach.
