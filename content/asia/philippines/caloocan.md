Caloocan is one of the 16 cities that make up Metro Manila, occupying the northern end of the metropolis along the boundary with Bulacan province. It is split into two non-contiguous sections by Malabon and Valenzuela: the southern part (around the old city centre) and the northern Camarin district.

The city is densely populated and primarily residential and industrial — it is not a tourist destination in its own right. Most visitors pass through it on the way between central Manila and the provinces north of the capital. The **Caloocan City Hall** area near the old centre and the **Grace Park** commercial district are the main reference points.

For travellers, Caloocan is mainly relevant as a transit point: the Monumento LRT station (Line 1 northern terminus, named for the Andrés Bonifacio monument at the EDSA–Rizal Avenue intersection) is a major transport hub connecting the city to the rest of Metro Manila.
