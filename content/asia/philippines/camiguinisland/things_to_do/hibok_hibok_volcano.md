Camiguin's highest and most active volcano at 1,332 metres, Mt Hibok-Hibok last erupted in 1951 and remains closely monitored. The two-day climb to the crater — with an overnight camp at the crater rim — is one of the more demanding volcano treks in the Philippines, rewarded by views across Mindanao and the Bohol Sea. Registered guides are mandatory and can be arranged through the tourism office in Mambajao. A permit is required.
