A small, uninhabited sandbar about a kilometre offshore from Agoho, White Island is a crescent of powdery white sand that appears and disappears with the tides. The view back to the mainland — with the cone of Mt Hibok-Hibok rising directly behind — is one of the most photographed scenes in the Philippines. Outrigger bancas from Agoho make the crossing in ten minutes. There is no shade and no vendors, so bring water, food and sun protection.
