The best way to spend a vacation in the Philippines is to take on a few **Camiguin Island tours and excursions.** Camiguin is an island off the northern portion of the island of Mindanao. It is a favorite tourist destination among local and foreign tourists alike. Here are some of the places that you need to visit during your excursion in this scenic island of the Philippines.

**Ardent Hot Spring**

This is a quaint hot spring resort which is located two kilometers from the capital of the island. The resort is frequented by a lot of people who seek the therapeutic effects of bathing in the 40 degree Celsius warm waters of the resort. The resort also has its own restaurant that serves Filipino foods. The entrance to the hot spring costs only $2.

**PHIVOLCS Observatory**

The PHIVOLCS Observatory is an observation center that monitors the volcanic activity of the island&rsquo;s volcano called Mount Hibok-Hibok. You can get yourself educated on volcanology as well as take a look at the breathtaking panoramic view of the island&rsquo;s capital. If you are lucky enough, at night you can get to see a warm glow emanating from the crater of the island&rsquo;s active volcano.

**White Island**

This is a sandbar located off the coast of Barangay Agoho and Yumbing. To get here, you need to hire a small motorboat that will cost you $10 for a one-way ride for a group of 6 to 8 individuals. You can bathe in the lagoon that is located in the sandbar or you can just take in the breathtaking panoramic view of the entire island as you bask in the sun.

**Katibawasan Falls**

This impressive waterfall is accessible from the capital of the island, Mambajao. The waterfalls offer a refreshing pool underneath them. Getting to this place is very easy; therefore, this a popular place for tourists to swim and take pictures. Entrance to this place is cheap, at less than $1.

**Camiguin Giant Clams and White Bach**

Located in a cove within an island, this beach takes pride on its white sand beach. Moreover, it also has a nursery for giant clams that is owned by the local government to increase the number of this endangered mollusk in the wild. You can reach this place by renting a vehicle. However, visiting this place can cost you about $10 to $50, since the place is a bit far from the capital.
