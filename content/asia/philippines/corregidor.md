Corregidor is a small tadpole-shaped island at the mouth of Manila Bay, 48 kilometres west of Manila. It is one of the most significant World War II sites in Southeast Asia: in the spring of 1942, Filipino and American forces held the island against Japanese assault for months after the fall of Bataan, until the garrison finally surrendered in May. General Douglas MacArthur commanded from the island's underground Malinta Tunnel before being ordered to Australia, famously vowing "I shall return."

The island is now an open-air war museum administered as a national park. The remains are extensive and atmospheric: ruined barracks and officers' quarters, gun batteries still in their emplacements, the Malinta Tunnel network, Japanese and Filipino memorials, the Pacific War Memorial at the island's summit, and a Spanish-era lighthouse that predates all of it. The vegetation has reclaimed much of what was once a garrisoned military post, giving the ruins a jungle-overgrown quality.

Most visitors come on a day cruise from Manila, run by Sun Cruises from the CCP harbor on Roxas Boulevard. The trip takes about an hour each way. On the island, a guided tramvia circuit covers the main sites; the guides are generally informative and the narration adds significant context to what would otherwise be hard to interpret ruins.
