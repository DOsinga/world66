**Dagupan City practical information** is necessary for any traveler to this metropolis of the Philippines. The city is located on the Lingayan gulf and has a population of about 150,000. The city is host to some popular festivals in the Philippines and is known for its bangus or milk fish.

**Basic Information**

The Philippine peso is used nationwide, with the exchange rate hovering anywhere between 40 and 47 Philippine pesos to 1 U.S. dollar in 2010.

Dagupan City is one of the major cities in Pangasinan, and Ilocano is the local dialect. This dialect is one of the chief dialects in this country, with about 10 to 15 million speakers nationwide. However, the people here do also speak Tagalog, the main Filipino language. English is an official language of the Philippines, with a great number of the fluent speakers residing in the major cities such as Dagupan.

Of the all hospitals in Pangasinan, the most advanced medical centers and clinics are in Dagupan. Moreover, major broadcasting and telecommunication networks reach the city. As one of the main cities in the entire country, physical and IT infrastructure are reliable, and transportation is efficient, abundant and economical.

#### **Cultural Notes**

A festive city with celebrations occurring almost quarterly, tourism is a budding industry in Dagupan City. The city and its people are highly accommodating to foreigners.

A large majority of the population are Roman Catholics, as is with the rest of the country.
