---
title: "Sights in Dagupan City"
type: section
---

There are lots of** Dagupan City sights **that will surely fascinate a great number of tourists. Located in Pangasinan, Dagupan City faces the South China Sea which makes it a perfect haven for beach combers. The city also plays a major role in the Philippine history, as there are many World War II relics found in Dagupan City. Here are some of the sights that people will truly enjoy in Dagupan City.

**Bonuan Blue Beach**

This public beach is not just an ordinary beach. It was the place where the Liberation Forces of the American Troops landed during the onset of World War II in the Asia Pacific Region. Now, the Bonuan Beach is a developed public beach where tourists can relax and mingle with the locals. The beach also has a nearby park called the MacArthur Landing Marker and Japanese Park that showcases statues of MacArthur and Japanese troops in the park itself.

**Old St. John&rsquo;s Cathedral**

This old cathedral was built during the Spanish regime and is considered the oldest church in Dagupan. The church also witnessed the surrender of the Spanish army to the Filipino armed forces during the late 1800s.

**Limahong Channel**

This is a man-made channel located in the outskirts of Dagupan City, which ends up in the shore of Lingayen. This is a historic channel that was made by a Chinese corsair named Limahong as a way to escape from Spanish pursuers after failing the attempt to establish a Chinese colony in Manila. Trips going to and from the channel are arranged by the local tourism board of the city.

**Villa Verde Trail**

The place is ideal for those wanting to learn history, as well as to those who want to do some trekking and have a hiking adventure. The trail is rich with lush vegetation. The tourists can also see the Red Arrow Monument, which marks the spot where the U.S. Forces was about to drive Japanese Corps away from Pangasinan.

**San Fabian Beach**

A white, sandy beach located in the town called San Fabian, this beach is preferred by a lot of vacationing tourists who want to escape the hustle and bustle of Dagupan City. The place is perfect for beach combers who want to sunbathe and try water sports such as kayaking, scuba diving and swimming. Not only that, the San Fabian Beach is also a perfect place to learn history, as this site was considered to be a battle ground between American and Japanese troops during World War II.
