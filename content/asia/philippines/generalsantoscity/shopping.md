---
title: "Shopping in General Santos City"
type: section
---

The city will offer you everything that you need, right from street shops to huge supermarkets; from tribal clothing to world famous designer labels. Here is what all you can do to shop till you drop!

**KCC Mall

**The city’s largest mall would be the fully air conditioned, three storied KCC mall. The department store here has a wide array of clothing for all ages that are reasonable priced. The mall also has luxury shops for the fashion conscious, right from Guess to Bench. Other than these KCC has watch shops, jewelry stores and bookstores. The tech freaks can also feel at home here with mobile stores, hardware stores and computer shops. You can visit this mall from 9 am until midnight. It has free parking on the 3rd floor, and is considered safe. Weekdays are recommended as they would certainly be crowd free. If you are on a tight budget you can pick up some low priced local clothing brands or some souvenir shirts that will cost you around $3. A lot of items are sold at a discount, like the new cell phone models, Filipino crafts and factory surpluses.

**Gaisano Mall**

Another popular mall would be the Gaisano Mall which houses the country's top designer shops and fast food outlets.

**
The Robinson Place**

The Robinson place is the latest addition to the city’s malls. With over 200 stores and parking for about 2000 people, this mall will surely be a hit. A huge department store and movie theatre along with a supermarket, appliance center is what you will find here. For the children it has come up with an amusement center as well.

**Cagampang Street
**
There is a row of Maranaw stores along the Cagampang Street right opposite the city's public market. Imported goods from Indonesia, Malaysia and Thailand flood this street. Local artists and craftsmen also sell their famous sarongs made of Batik or Thai silk. All the items are considerably cheaper than sold in other nearby markets. Another very popular set of shopping items would be the native Tiboli tribe items such as their cloths, artifacts, jewelry etc. With a budget of $25 you will be able to buy sufficient amount of apparel.

General Santos City has much on offer for hardcore shopping enthusiasts including local artifacts, designer stuff and high-end electronics.
