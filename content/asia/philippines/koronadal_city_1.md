Koronadal, also known as Marbel, is the capital of South Cotabato in southern Mindanao. The name comes from two B'laan words — *kolon* (cogon grass) and *nadal* (plain) — an accurate description of what was here before the post-war settlers arrived. Today it is a busy provincial city of a few hundred thousand people, built on a grid of wide streets and surrounded by rice fields and the foothills of the Quezon range.

There isn't a great deal to see in Koronadal itself. The city is the administrative and commercial centre for the province and most visitors pass through on the way to somewhere more interesting — **Lake Sebu** in the T'boli highlands, the waterfalls and forests of **Surallah**, or the coast at **General Santos**. If you find yourself here with an afternoon to spare, the public market is worth a wander and the small Cultural Center Museum has modest displays on T'boli weaving and traditional crafts.
