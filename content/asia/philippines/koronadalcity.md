Koronadal City, also known as Marbel, is the capital of South Cotabato province in the Soccsksargen region of Mindanao, southern Philippines. It sits at the foothills of the Roxas and Quezon mountain ranges, about 50km from General Santos City.

The city is a regional administrative and commercial centre rather than a tourist destination, but it has a lively food scene and is a reasonable base for exploring South Cotabato. The annual Tinalak Festival in July celebrates the T'nalak cloth of the B'laan people — a traditional hand-woven fabric with geometric patterns. The city is typhoon-free, making it a more reliable destination year-round than much of the Philippines.
