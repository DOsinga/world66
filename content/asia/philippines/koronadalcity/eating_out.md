The **Koronadal City Restaurants** are varied in number and offer a peek into the local eating habits. Located in the province of South Cotabato, this city is a well known tourist destination in Philippines. Read on to find out more.

**Lantaw Marbel**

This restaurant is located on the hill in Koronadal. Lantaw Marbel takes dining to a whole new level. This restaurant serves the most delicious local cuisine. You can sample the suckling pig, roasted to perfection. This is the most sought after dish at this restaurant. Another must try is the stuffed chicken and the milkfish. The view that this restaurant offers is out of this world. You will get an eagle&rsquo;s eye view of the Koronadal city from the restaurant's main dining area.

**Sabalai**

Sabalai is a famous restaurant located right in the heart of the city. This is one of the most preferred restaurants in town. This is more like a fine dining restaurant. The chef experiments with various cooking techniques to present the guests with a unique dish. The menu changes on a daily basis, but there are a few dishes that stay constant. The honey beef, spicy pork, and chicken in coconut curry are some of the star attractions on the menu.

**Lechon Manok**

The restaurant has a very calm and relaxing feel about it. This is another restaurant that specializes in the local cuisine. The restaurant is open for lunch and dinner. It is located in the city center and is well known amongst the locals. The fried tofu stuffed with pork is served with a garlic sauce and makes for a great appetizer. This restaurant is known for its grilled food. The grilled prawns glazed with peanut sauce and the grilled pork are a must try. The grilled chicken breast is stuffed with a coconut and garlic mixture.

**Food Harbor**

Located very close to the bus station this restaurant is a classic family restaurant in Koronadal City. The restaurant has a reputation of serving delicious food with affordable rates. You can find international dishes like hamburgers and fish and chips here, but these dishes are given a local twist. Try the pig&rsquo;s ear, which is a local specialty.

**Bay Walk**

Ask any local for Bay Walk and they will direct you to this street. The Bay Walk is a street in Koronadal City well known for its food stalls. This street is a food lover&rsquo;s paradise, as you have a wide variety of dishes to choose from. The food here is fresh and prepared with a lot of love. There are 25 barbecue stalls on this street. From seafood to chicken, pork, and lamb, you can find them all here. Also, unusual dishes like pig intestine and chicken blood soup are served here.
