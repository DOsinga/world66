The walled Spanish colonial core of Manila. The walls were 3 kilometres long, 13 metres thick, and 6 metres high; little else survived the 1945 Battle of Manila, when the Japanese occupying force and American bombing between them flattened what had been one of the great Spanish cities of Asia. Today the walls have been partly rebuilt, San Agustin Church (1607) survived the war almost intact, and horse-drawn *kalesa* ply the tourist loop. Go early; the heat gets heavy by mid-morning.
