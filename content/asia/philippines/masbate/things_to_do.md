---
title: "Things to Do in Masbate"
type: section
---

Your **Masbate things to do** list should include plenty of activities involving nature trips. Masbate is located in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. The island province is blessed with beaches and pristine white islands located near it, perfect for beach combers and island hoppers. Aside from island hopping, here are some of the things that you need to include in your things to do list.

**Enjoy the Rich Marine Life**

Since Masbate is surrounded by the sea, there are heaps of marine sanctuaries in the island. Places you might want to visit are the Bugsayon Marine Sanctuary and the Buntod Reef Sanctuary located in Barangay B. Titong and Barangay Buntod respectively. The sanctuaries can be reached easily by motorized boat. However, permission from the local office needs to be acquired before entering these areas.

**Bird Watching**

Bird watchers will surely love watching birds in the Pawa Mangrove Ecosystem and Wildlife Park. This natural park is located in Barangay Pawa and has a 1.3 kilometer wooden bridge that connects one town to the other. This is the perfect place to see kingfishers, herons and other estuarine birds.

**Dining in the Floating Restaurant**

The perfect way to enjoy the view of the Masbate Bay is by riding and dining in the infamous Masbate Floating Restaurant. The restaurant offers fresh Filipino cuisines as well as entertainment coming from the live band aboard the restaurant too.

**Enjoy the Festivals**

Masbate has a lot of festivals held annually. One of the famous festivals that they have is the Rodeo Filipino held every May. Local cowboys from all over the country display their prowess over untamed bulls and cattle. Another interesting festival is the Corn Festival, which is held in the town of San Ramon. This festival is infamous for their local dancers dancing on top of glowing embers. Tourists too are encouraged to join.

**Explore the Caves**

Masbate is also home to many natural caves, perfect for those who love adventure. There are several caves found in the island such as Bat-ongan Caves, Claveria Cave and Mata Cave. The Bat-ongan and Mata Cave are perfect for tourists who have never explored a cave before, since its trail is easily accessible. The Claveria Cave is an underwater cave with a cavity inside which is infamous for having old wooden caskets belonging to the ancient tribes that used to dwell in the island.
