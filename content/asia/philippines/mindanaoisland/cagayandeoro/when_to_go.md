---
title: "When to Go in Cagayan de Oro"
type: section
---

Cagayan de Oro has a tropical climate with temperatures that are warm the whole year through. Although there are separate wet and dry seasons, rainfall is fairly common throughout the entire year. There are pros and cons to both seasons which should be taken into consideration when planning when to go to Cagayan de Oro, and you should also consider the various festivals and peak travel times when arranging your visit to this beautiful city.

**Wet Season**

The wet season lasts from June through October and is characterized by frequent rains. Although Cagayan de Oro is outside of the Pacific Typhoon Belt, there are still occasions when heavy rains disrupt travel by any means - land, air or sea. July is typically the wettest month, with June and October coming in a close second. However, rain is usually limited to the afternoons, with mornings being either sunny or cloudy and dry. If you mind the rain less than you mind large crowds, the wet season is definitely the time to go. Prices are lower, the weather is cooler and the crowds are significantly smaller. Just keep in mind that the occasional ferry or flight may be canceled.

**Dry Season**

From November through May is the dry season, although rain is still a common occurrence. Occasional rainfall still tends to be limited to the afternoons, but it's usually a much lighter rain. In between are hot, sunny days. April is generally the driest month of the year, with April, May and August being the hottest. January and February are the coolest months of the year. Still, yearly temperatures tend to average around 28 degrees C, with occasional reaches to 35 degrees C. March through May is the peak tourist season, when prices go up and crowds swell. The best time to visit is between December and March, when temperatures are cooler, precipitation is fairly low and tourists are few in number. You should still bring an umbrella, as it's useful against both the rain and the sun.

**[Festivals](/asia/philippines/mindanaoisland/cagayandeoro/festivals)**

Cagayan de Oro's largest festival, Kagay-an, is held at the end of August and includes parades, dancing, shows, concerts, river activities and competitions. It's preceded by the annual culinary festival and followed by the annual boat race. If you want to experience true Mindanao culture, then August is the time to visit Cagayan de Oro. If you want to take part in any religious practices, you can visit during Holy Week (March or April) to take part in the Way of the Cross and Easter celebrations.
