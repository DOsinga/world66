Naga City sits in the Bicol Region of Luzon, about 440 km south of Manila, and is the cultural and commercial heart of the Camarines Sur province. It's a lively mid-sized city with a strong university presence and a distinct Bicolano identity — you'll notice the local cuisine here, hotter than most Filipino food, built on coconut milk and chillies.

The city's defining event is the **Peñafrancia Festival** every September, one of the largest Marian celebrations in Asia. A massive fluvial procession carries the image of Our Lady of Peñafrancia along the Naga River, with hundreds of boats and crowds estimated in the millions lining the banks. It's a spectacle unlike anything else in the Philippines. Outside of festival season, Naga is a comfortable base for trips to the nearby Caramoan Peninsula, known for its limestone islands and beaches made famous by various *Survivor* productions.
