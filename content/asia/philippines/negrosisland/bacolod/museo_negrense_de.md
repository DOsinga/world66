A small museum on the University of Saint La Salle campus housing artefacts from prominent Negrense families. The collection leans toward religious items -- santos, vestments, liturgical silver -- alongside period furniture and personal effects that sketch out the sugar-baron era on Negros Island. A few pre-colonial pieces round out the displays. It is a quiet stop and rarely crowded, worth a visit if you are interested in the island's colonial and cultural history.
