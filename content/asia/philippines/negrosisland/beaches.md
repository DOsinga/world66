Like elsewhere in the Philippines, **Negros Island beaches** are
picture postcard gorgeous, boasting lovely white sand and a laidback
ambience. In just a brief time th casual traveller can explore all these
delightful destinations and really soak up what the Philippines has to
offer. Negros is known today as the 'deep south' of the Philippines,
offering travellers a taste of unspoilt paradise, where both the nature
and culture has been largely untouched by the overdevelopment in other
parts of the nation.

**Sugar Beach, Sipalay**

Probably the best known and
most attractice of the Negros island beaches, Sugar Beach is lined with
various resorts from the native-inspired Driftwood Village to the late
night drinking at Sulu Sunset. From these establishments you can book
boat rides, snorkelling trips and diving sojourns. The food is generally
excellent, with the pizza at Takatuka Beach and Dive Resort the
standout dish.

**Punta Ballo**

Just southeast of Sipalay
City is the seemingly neverending white strip of sand kown as Punta
Ballo. Diving is one of the favourite activities of travellers to this
area, with the best operators including Artistic Diving and Easy Diving
which both offer mindblowing trips into the Sulu Sea where all manner of
tropical fish, molluscs and reef can be glimpsed. The best food and
drink is served at the German-owned Robinson Crusoe Resort and Natasaan,
the latter featuring some pleasant accommodation too.

**Escalante**

Bonista
Beach Resort has it all: superb deluxe rooms, a swimming pool that
makes a great alternative to swimming in the sea and fishermen who
arrive with the freshest fish for your dinner. Nearby White Island
boasts Jomabo Beach resort which is about 30 minutes' travel from
Escalante. The island is only 1 km long and affords great ocean kayaking
opportunities. The beach and the surrounding mangroves make this one of
the best Negros Island beaches.

**Before You Go to the Beach**It's
important to be aware of the 'point of departure' for all the Negros
Island beaches: Bacolod City. This is a low-key and pleasant city as
compared to busy and polluted Filipino urban centres such as Manila and
Cebu. If you do spend some time here you'd be advised to stay somewhere
clean and of a high standard. The Sylvia Manor in San Juan Street is
Bacolod's only boutique hotel famed across the country and beyond for
its contemporary amenities, hospitality and perfect location. You can
avail yourself of the Wi-Fi access and pick from over 50 well-looked
after rooms.
