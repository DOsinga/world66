Sipalay sits on the southwest coast of Negros, about three to four hours by road from [Bacolod](/asia/philippines/negrosisland/bacolod). It is one of the Philippines' best-kept beach and diving destinations -- still developing, still uncrowded, and all the better for it.

Sugar Beach is the headline attraction, a strip of white sand lined with small resorts where the pace of life barely registers. Punta Ballo draws serious divers to its clear waters and healthy reefs, and Campomanes Bay is a protected marine reserve with calm water for kayaking, diving, and water sports. Offshore, Danjugan Island is a protected reserve with lagoons, mangroves, and the endangered white-breasted sea eagle.

The diving here is outstanding. Over 40 dive sites range from shallow house reefs to deep walls, World War II wrecks, and underwater caves. The water is warm, visibility is generally good, and the marine life is abundant. Artistic Diving and Easy Diving are the main operators at Punta Ballo.
