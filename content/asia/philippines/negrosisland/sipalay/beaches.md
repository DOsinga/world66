---
title: 'Beaches'
type: section
---

Sipalay's beaches are the main draw. **Sugar Beach**, a 15-minute boat ride from the city proper, is the star -- fine white sand lined with small resorts run by a mix of German, Swiss, and Filipino owners. Driftwood Village has a native vibe, Takatuka has great pizza, and Sulu Sunset is the place for late-night drinks.

**Punta Ballo** is a long white sand stretch southeast of Sipalay, popular with divers. Artistic Diving and Easy Diving both operate from here with trips into the Sulu Sea. Not far away, **Tinagong Dagat** (the 'hidden sea') is a sheltered lagoon sprinkled with small islets, old-growth mangroves, and sea grass beds.

**Campomanes Bay** is excellent for water sports of all kinds. The enclosed shape of the bay keeps the water calm even in rough weather, and the entrance is a wide expanse of coral garden. The bay has port facilities and serves as a marine reserve.

**Danjugan Island**, off the coast, is a protected marine and terrestrial reserve with beautiful lagoons and diverse wildlife, including the endangered white-breasted sea eagle.
