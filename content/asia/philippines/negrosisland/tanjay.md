Tanjay is a small city on the northeastern coast of [Negros Island](/asia/philippines/negrosisland), one of the oldest settlements in Negros Oriental. Founded by Spanish colonists in 1580, it served as the administrative centre for a large stretch of the eastern coast long before [Dumaguete](/asia/philippines/negrosisland/dumaguete) grew to prominence.

Today Tanjay is a quiet, friendly place that most travellers pass through on the way between Dumaguete and points north. The town centre has a handsome Spanish-era church and a compact plaza worth a short stop. The coastline offers views across the Tañon Strait to Cebu, and the surrounding countryside is green sugar-cane and rice land backed by the central mountains of Negros.

Tanjay makes a reasonable base for exploring the less-visited northern half of Negros Oriental. The city is connected by frequent buses and jeepneys along the main coastal road.
