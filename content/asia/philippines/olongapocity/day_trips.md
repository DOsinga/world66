---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

**Mt Pinatubo** is 50–60km east — one of the most dramatic day trips in Luzon. The 1991 eruption was the second-largest of the 20th century and left a crater lake of striking turquoise. The hike to the crater (typically via a 4WD transfer to the trailhead) takes around 2 hours each way and is well worth the effort.

**Pundaquit and San Antonio**, south of Olongapo, are quieter fishing villages with access to diving, surfing, and offshore islands including Capones Island and its lighthouse. Significantly less developed than the Subic Bay freeport.

**Bataan Peninsula**, across the bay, requires a ferry crossing but offers historic WWII sites including the Death March memorials and Mt Samat with its giant cross visible from Subic Bay.
