---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Olongapo has a good range of restaurants for a city of its size, with seafood the obvious speciality given the bay. The Subic Bay Freeport Zone has several options including Pier One on the waterfront, which has live music most evenings. In the city proper, the wet market area and the streets near the city hall have cheap local canteens serving Filipino standards — adobo, sinigang, and grilled fish. Mango's Seaside Grill by the bay is popular for its outdoor setting and fresh catch.
