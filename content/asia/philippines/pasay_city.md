Pasay is the city most visitors to Manila see first without realising it — Ninoy Aquino International Airport sits within its borders, and many of Metro Manila's major transport hubs are here. The city stretches along Manila Bay south of the capital proper, bordered by Makati to the northeast and Parañaque to the south.

For travellers, Pasay's main draw is the Entertainment City complex along the bay, a cluster of large casino-resorts that has become Manila's answer to Macau. The Cultural Center of the Philippines, designed by Leandro Locsin, hosts concerts, theatre, and art exhibitions, and the adjacent Coconut Palace (built for a papal visit that never happened) is a curious piece of Marcos-era excess. The Manila Bay sunset, viewed from the CCP Complex or the nearby Seaside area, remains one of the city's simple pleasures.

Pasay is not a destination in itself so much as a place you pass through — but it has enough going on to reward a stop, especially if you are arriving or departing from NAIA and have a few hours to spare.
