Lucena is the capital of Quezon Province and the main gateway to the southern Luzon region of the [Philippines](/asia/philippines). It is a busy, mid-sized city that most travellers pass through rather than linger in — the Grand Central Terminal here is the jumping-off point for buses heading south to the Bicol region and for boats to Marinduque island.

The city has a lively market scene and some good local food — try the pancit habhab (stir-fried noodles served on a banana leaf) and the longganisa Lucena, the local sweet sausage. The Pasayahan sa Lucena festival in May fills the streets with parades and street dancing. For most visitors, though, Lucena works best as a transit stop on the way to the beaches and islands further south.
