Calbayog is the largest city on Samar Island and the gateway for most visitors to the western coast. Known locally as the "City of Waterfalls," the surrounding area is packed with natural attractions -- Bangon-Bugtong Falls with its swimmable pool, the misty Pan-as Falls, Tabokno Falls, and several more cascading through the forested interior.

The city itself is functional rather than beautiful, but Malajog Beach offers good swimming and floating cottage rentals, and the Mawacat natural water slide -- a 50-metre mossy rock formation angled at 45 degrees into a cool pool below -- is genuinely fun. The Guinogo-an and Danao caves provide underground adventure for the more intrepid. The Samar Archaeological Museum at Christ the King College has a modest but interesting collection of pre-colonial artefacts.

Calbayog is reachable by air from Manila (three flights weekly), by ferry from Cebu (about 12 hours overnight), or by bus and jeepney from elsewhere on the island. Within the city, tricycles and habal-habals are cheap and plentiful.
