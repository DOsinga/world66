You might want to get your **Calbayog things to do** list ready
before arriving here for a holiday. Here are a few recommendations for
you.

**Beach Hopping**

There are lots of great beaches in Calbayog. You can visit one beach
every day or if you don’t have the time to do that, you can try beach
hopping for a single day. Great beaches you can go to are Malajog, Lo-ok
and Bagacay. Malajog beach is a 200-meter gray sand beachfront property
that has crystal blue waters and wonderful rock formations. Lo-ok beach
is situated just 10-minutes from Tinambacan district. Lo-ok beach is a
white sand beach and is surrounded by towering coconut trees and huge
rocks. The water here is crystal clear and very cold. The beach also has
a “South Sea Island” style rest-house where you can stay. Bagacay beach
on the other hand is situated 10-minuites away from the city center.
This beach also has white sand, cold crystal blue water and towering
coconut trees.

**Visit the Cathedral**

You might also want to visit the largest and oldest cathedral within
the province of Samar. St Peter and St. Paul’s Cathedral is an old
Spanish architecture situated right in the heart of Calbayog City. It
has great dome ceilings, spire, thick brick walls and colorful
interiors.

**Trekking**

One of the things to do while in Calbayog is trekking. You can go
trekking and discover the hidden riches of the forest. Within the forest
of Oquendo District you can find the Mapaso Hot Spring. This is a
mid-size spring that is surrounded with coconut trees and is a great
place to relax after a trek. You can also go to Baranggay Tinaplacan for
a 45-minute trek to the Bangon-Bugtong Falls. This is Calbayog’s most
beautiful waterfalls and it has a 30-meter (in diameter) crystal clear
pond.

**Explore the Caves**

You can also explore the natural caves in Calbayog. Well-known caves
you can go to are the Guinogo-An and the Danao caves. The Danao cave is
located in the Tinambacan District of Calbayog. It has 7 chambers that
serves as home to wild animals like bats and snake and has rich deposit
of guano. The Guinogo-An on the other hand is situated in Oquendo
District, just minutes away from the Mapaso hot spring and has a
cathedral-like façade.

**Mawacat Slide**

If you want some natural adventure, try the Mawacat Slide. This is a
nature-made slide that is shaped like a spoon has a 45 degree incline.
Ice cold water flows through this 50-meter long land formation. You can
slide smoothly through this mossy lane straight to the inviting water
pond at the end of your slide.
