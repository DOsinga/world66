Catarman is the capital of Northern Samar, a modest provincial city that most visitors pass through on their way to Biri Island — and then wish they had stayed a little longer. It sits on the northern coast of Samar, far enough from the main tourist circuits to feel genuinely local. The streets run slow, the mornings smell of fresh tamalos from street stalls, and the University of Eastern Philippines — the first state university in the Visayas — gives the town an unexpected academic energy.

The city's main draw is as a base for exploring Northern Samar's natural landscape. Pinipisakan Falls, a multi-tiered cascade surrounded by limestone and forest, is one of the better waterfalls on the island. Adjacent Sulpan Cave has winding chambers and a subterranean river running through it. The coast within a couple of kilometres of town has a beach passable for swimming.

The real highlight is a day trip to Biri Island, about an hour northeast by jeepney to Lavezares and then a ferry crossing. Biri's rock formations — carved by Pacific swells over millennia into arches, channels, and tidal pools — are among the most dramatic coastal geology in the Philippines. Magasang and Bel-at are the most visited formations; getting between them requires a bangka tour of the island's perimeter.

The Our Lady of the Annunciation Cathedral anchors the town centre. The pinyato sold at roadside stalls — a local take on peanut brittle made with carabao peanuts — is the thing to bring home.
