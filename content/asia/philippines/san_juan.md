San Juan is Metro Manila's smallest city by area, wedged between Mandaluyong, Quezon City, and [Manila](/asia/philippines/manila) itself. Most visitors pass through without stopping, which is their loss — this compact city punches above its weight in historical significance and has a livelier street scene than its modest size suggests.

The battle that started the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule was fought here on 26 August 1896, when Andres Bonifacio and the Katipunan launched their uprising in San Juan del Monte. The site is marked and the story is worth knowing before you arrive: it was ordinary people — workers, farmers, craftsmen — who turned up with bolos and old firearms against a colonial garrison, and lost that first battle badly, yet started something that would end three centuries of Spanish rule within two years.

Today San Juan is best known among Filipinos for Greenhills Shopping Center, a multi-level complex famous for its pearl and jewelry market, where you can spend an afternoon negotiating over cultured pearl strands and loose stones. The city also sits close to the Ortigas business district and draws a mix of residents who prefer its quieter streets to the density of Makati or Quezon City.

Getting to San Juan is straightforward — jeepneys and taxis connect it to the rest of Metro Manila, and the area around Ortigas on its border is served by the MRT. It is not a destination in itself so much as a neighbourhood city that rewards those who take the time to look at what happened here.
