**Enjoy the Sun and Beaches in San Pablo

**Tourists looking to visit **San Pablo City beaches** will have to do a little more traveling than expected, as most of the beaches are located some distance away. Here are some of the beaches that tourists can visit if they are looking for some sun, sand and surf activities in San Pablo City.

**Punta**** Sur Beach

**Located about 40 km to the south of San Pablo City is Punta Sur Beach. This beach is located in the ecological preserve, Punta Sur National Park. This is a beautiful white sand beach that opens up to the Caribbean Sea. Visitors to this beach can choose from a number of exciting activities like swimming and snorkeling in the stunning blue-green water. Tourists can also enjoy windsurfing at this beach. Those looking for something more adventurous can opt to climb the lighthouse for spectacular views of the sea.

**La Luz Beach

**Tourists looking for a quieter beach to visit must try La Luz beach, which is situated about 45 km to the south. When traveling by road, you will have to pass San Juan. The beach is located along the Naic Road. It is at the end of the Laiya beach stretch. Although it isn&rsquo;t all that sandy, the beach stretch has fine white sand and crystal clear waters that offer good swimming opportunities. Visitors who want to spend more than a day here can check into the La Luz Beach Resort, which is located right on the beach front. La Luz beach is ideal for relaxing and soaking up the sun.

**Anilao**** Beach

**If you are looking for beach activities that are more thrilling, then you must make a trip to Anilao Beach. It is located about 55 km from San Pablo city, south of Bantagas City. Tourists planning to visit this beach should set out early in the morning if they want to make the most of their trip. Anilao beach doesn&rsquo;t have sand, but it does have numerous wonderful diving spots. This remote area boasts of rich underwater assets like tropical reef fish, coral, crabs, sharks, crayfish, mantas and even sea wrecks. The clean blue waters allow visitors to enjoy a number of other water sporting activities like swimming, sailing, windsurfing, sport fishing and snorkeling. There are many small resorts where tourists can eat, drink and relax as well.
