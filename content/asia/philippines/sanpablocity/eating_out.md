title: "Eating Out in San Pablo City"
type: section
---

Although the Philippines is often criticized for its unadventurous cuisine, if you're shrewd you can find a great variety of food options and **San Pablo City restaurants**, offering everything from traditional local dishes to the Filipino-loved American junk food to Chinese and Mexican.

**Palmeras Garden Restaurant**

For less than US $10, you can eat delicious a feast of grilled tilapia fish, tempura shrimp, chicharon buklaklak (fried pig pancreas) or beef shank soup at this favourite local eatery. It's so well-regarded that even the mayor swings by on a regular basis. Palmeras is also a fun hangout aside from the superb food with great covers bands playing songs by 50 Cent, Ja Rule, etc., for the young, cool clientele, as well as musicians catering to the older generation with 60s classics by the Eagles and Cat Stevens. An added attraction is the freshwater pond and brooks in the garden where your fish will be caught and cooked to order. To wash it all down, a bottle of San Miguel will cost you about $1.

**Coco Palace Hotel Restaurant**

If you're craving Chinese food, then this is the only place in town serving it. The restaurant is located inside a 3 star hotel and is enjoying a boom in popularity. The dishes cost around $5-6 each, and the specialty is probably the bird's nest soup, although some patrons have criticized the lack of choice at the Coco Palace and the high level of MSG present within the food.

**Palaisdaan**

In the 1990s in San Pablo City, there was a vogue for venues that served fish freshly caught from a system of small lakes in between the huts that are rented by the resort that the restaurant is attached to. Because of the resort facilities offered, the selection of dishes is not great - you can basically choose from a few different kinds of fish: tilapia, catfish, mud fish and carp grilled or fried to perfection.
