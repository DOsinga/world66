This **San Pablo City travel guide** will show you all things you need to know about this bustling city in Laguna, Philippines.

**Getting In and Around**

San Pablo City is accessible through the South Luzon Expressway. If you are coming in from Manila, all you have to do is ride a bus to Lucena City or Sto Tomas. The journey will take two to three hours and it will cost you about $5.

Getting around the city is a bit complicated because the streets are somewhat narrow and there is always a traffic jam. However, you can ride the jeepney or tricycle to go around the city. Regular jeepney fares cost $0.25 while the tricycle fares depend on your destination, but will mostly likely start at $0.20.

**Accommodations**

Finding a nice place to stay and sleep is not a problem. There are several good hotels, inns and resort in and around San Pablo City and you can choose according to your budget. Popular establishments are Rockpoint Hotel, Casa San Pablo, Tahanan ni Aling Meding, Villa Escudero, Bukid Garden, Tierra Monde and Dioko Resort. All of these hotels offer comfy yet affordable, accommodations. Average rooms cost about $25 per night.

**Attractions**

San Pablo City is known for its seven lakes as well as its beautiful mountain ranges. There are different places to visit and things to can do around the city. You will never have a dull moment while holidaying in San Pablo City. Great places to visit are Casa San Pablo, Villa Escudero, Sampaloc Lake, Bato Spring and the twin lakes of Yambo and Pandin. You also wouldn&rsquo;t want to miss the Coco Festival in San Pablo City.

**Eating Out**

Your journey to San Pablo wouldn&rsquo;t be complete without tasting the delicious Buko Pie. This is one of the most famous in Laguna. You can pick up some of the best Buko Pies at Collette&rsquo;s or Letty&rsquo;s which have outlets abound the city. If you want try the local cuisine, Kamayan sa Palaisdaan Restaurant, Clydenjosh Restaurant, Palmeras Garden Restaurant and Sulyap Gallery Caf&eacute; are the best places to enjoy a good local meal.

**Nightlife and Entertainment**

Nightlife in San Pablo is as vibrant as the day time. There are several bars around the city which offers live band performances, affordable ice cold beers and mouthwatering pica-picas. Night spots you don&rsquo;t want to miss are the La Pizzeria, Coco Place, Red Chili Resto Bar, ZYPP Zone and Q5 Corner Stage.
