A **Tacloban City Travel Guide** is essentially about the amazing, historic and cultural places-of-interest that the city of Tacloban has to offer. Tacloban is the capital city of Leyte, located along its north-eastern edge. Leyte is among the bigger islands of Philippines. In fact, it is regarded as the most commercially-progressive island in the Eastern Visayas part of Philippines. Tacloban has evolved into a major tourist attraction. Most of Tacloban&rsquo;s visitors descend from the capital of Philippines, Manila—situated about 200 miles north of Tacloban.

**History of Tacloban**

Tacloban boasts of an interesting history. It was called &lsquo;Kankabatok&rsquo; for many centuries. Most people believe that this name was coined by its first settlers—the Kabatok people. Even now, some descendants of the Kabatok tribe claim themselves to be the true rulers of Tacloban. However, the Kabatok aren&rsquo;t the only tribes that inhabited Tacloban. Many other south Asian communities like the Gumoda and the Huraw also settled here. Later, Tacloban was ruled by many colonial powers. Among these, the Spanish were the city&rsquo;s most authoritative rulers.

**People of Tacloban**

Today, families of many Iberian settlers and those with a Spanish-Filipino bloodline form the core of Tacloban&rsquo;s inhabitants. The people of Tacloban have their own dialect that is distinct from the more cosmopolitan culture of Manila. This dialect is often referred to as the &lsquo;Leyte-Samarnon&rsquo; dialect.

**Overview of Popular Sites of Tacloban**

Among the more popular buildings is the Leyte Provincial Capitol. This place served as Philippine&rsquo;s office for its Commonwealth operations. This building is also important from a historic perspective. It gives an insight into the struggle of popular national leaders who fought the colonial powers. Due to the varied cultural influences, many religious faiths co-exist in Tacloban. The popular places of worship include the Ni&ntilde;o Church that houses the popular ivory image of Child Jesus. The Ni&ntilde;o Shrine also has a popular Heritage Museum. Other public buildings like the Adjacent People&acute;s Library provide recorded history of the nation&rsquo;s ethnic groups. The most popular statue in Tacloban is the 18-feet tall statue of Sacred Hearth of Jesus. Popular, family-visit sites in Tacloban include the Lake Danao National Park and the Tongonan Hot Spring National Park.

**What to expect in Tacloban?**

Traveling in and around Tacloban isn't difficult. There are plenty of public transport options. The most common mode of transport is the &lsquo;jeepneys&rsquo; or the PUJs. Visitors can also rent a car for a few days at very affordable rates. Tourists who plan to visit Tacloban in June can look forth to catching a glimpse of the annual, Tacloban Festival. This is the city&rsquo;s most sought-after celebration of sports. Here, the &lsquo;Subiran Regatta&rsquo; or the sailboat race engages participants from Philippines and the neighboring, island nations.
