Tuguegarao City is the capital of Cagayan province in the Cagayan Valley, the northernmost region of Luzon. While it's primarily a commercial and administrative hub rather than a tourist destination, it serves as the main gateway for travellers heading to the Callao Caves and the outdoor adventures of the Cagayan Valley.

The city itself is busy and congested in the centre, with a lively market district and the prominent Saints Peter and Paul Cathedral — an impressive colonial church with a tall belfry modelled on 18th-century European ecclesiastical architecture. The Cagayan Valley Museum and Research Center is worth a visit for context on the region's history and indigenous cultures.

The real draws are outside the city. Callao Caves, about 30 kilometres away in Peñablanca, is a complex of seven chambers with impressive stalactites and a natural chapel in the seventh chamber. The Pinacanauan River alongside the caves is excellent for kayaking. Deeper in the Peñablanca Protected Landscape is Sierra Cave, considered one of the largest cave systems in Asia.

The city can be reached by bus from Manila (9–12 hours overnight) or by air via Tuguegarao Airport. Jeepneys and tricycles are the main local transport.
