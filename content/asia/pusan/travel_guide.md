---
title: "Travel Guide in Pusan"
type: section
---

Any **Pusan travel guide** makes the city out as a bustling metropolis that, with nearly 4 million people, holds its own as the second largest city in South Korea. The nightlife makes it a hive of activity for young people looking for a good time, but there are also plenty of attractions that will keep families entertained and older visitors busy. So whether you're looking for some action and adventure or for a more laid-back city visit, Pusan is the place to go. Take a look at this Pusan travel guide to get some ideas for your trip.

**Nightlife**

If you're interested in the nightlife more than the daily goings-on of Pusan, why not head toward Haeundae Beach. The beach itself is a beautiful strip of white sand with fantastic views, but what interests the younger crowd is the surrounding strip of restaurants, bars, casinos and hotels. Accommodations in the area run between 5,000 and 500,000 KRW, as in the rest of the city. In the summer Haeundae becomes overcrowded, being the most popular beach in Korea, but at any time of year, it makes for a great night out on the town. There are even a few excellent spas in the area, allowing for a break from the frenzied pace.

**Temples**

Many who come to South Korea are looking for more insight into the Buddhist culture, and a temple stay in Pusan will offer just that. Two of the best examples of Buddhist temples are located in Pusan: Haedong Yonggung Temple on the seaside and Samgwangsa Temple at the foot of the mountains. Both are scenic and serene, and if you're interested in more than just sight-seeing, you can sign up for a temple stay at Beomeosa Temple, where you live like the monks for a day or two.

**Museums**

History buffs will love Pusan's abundance of museums. Busan Museum showcases the history and archaeology of the area, including a touchable exhibit. Donsam-dong Shell Midden Museum and Bokcheon Museum also display the history and archaeology of Pusan, but the former focuses on the Neolithic period while the latter focuses on the Gaya Kingdom. There is also the Modern History Museum and the Busan Marine Natural History Museum. The Busan Museum of Modern Art is great for those who want to explore the creative side of Pusan, and young and old will appreciate the delicacies of the Korean Tea Museum.

**Attractions**

There are plenty of other top attractions in Pusan. This travel guide suggests a tour of Geumjeong Sanseong Mountain Fortress or the shopping and leisure mecca that is in Shinsegae Centum City. If you want a thrill, you can try diving with the sharks at Busan Aquarium. And, of course, Pusan is known as the jumping off point for trips to beautiful Jeju Island. No matter what your interests, Pusan is sure to have something wonderful to offer you!
