Al Wakrah is a fishing town about 15 km south of central Doha — close enough to reach easily, different enough to feel like somewhere else. For most of Qatar's oil-era growth it was ignored, but the old waterfront quarter has been carefully restored and now has a small, pleasant souq that most Doha tourists miss entirely.

The **Al Wakrah Souq** is smaller and quieter than Souq Waqif in Doha, which makes it more enjoyable in some ways. The restored coral-and-gypsum buildings house craft shops and a few restaurants, and the harbor behind them still has working dhows. It is a good evening destination.

The town also has a public beach, one of the few genuinely accessible ones near Doha, and a small museum with local archaeological and maritime artifacts.

Al Wakrah sits on the Red Line of the Doha Metro, making it straightforward to reach without a car.
