title: "Highlights"
type: section

**Museum of Islamic Art**

The MIA is a genuinely great museum — not just by Gulf standards, but by any standards. I.M. Pei designed the building as his final major commission, and the collection of Islamic art from the 7th to 19th centuries is comprehensive and beautifully displayed. Spend a morning here before the afternoon heat sets in. The views from the waterfront park back toward Doha's skyline are worth the visit alone.

**Khor Al Udeid — the Inland Sea**

At the far southern tip of Qatar, the sea breaks through the dunes and creates a calm inland lagoon surrounded by some of the tallest free-moving dunes in the country. Access requires a 4WD. You can either rent one, go with an organized tour, or find a local who makes this run on weekends. Camp overnight in winter if you can — the silence and the stars are unlike anything else in the country.

**Souq Waqif at night**

Doha's restored old market comes alive after dark when the temperature drops. The falcon souq (where birds change hands for thousands of dollars), the spice stalls, the tea houses where men play backgammon and smoke shisha — none of it is entirely authentic in the oldest sense, but it captures something real about Gulf commercial culture. Have dinner here rather than at a hotel restaurant.

**Zubarah Fort and ruins**

The drive alone — 90 minutes northwest through flat desert scrub — is a useful reminder of how small and bare most of Qatar actually is. The UNESCO fort and the adjacent ruins of the 18th-century trading town are genuinely interesting, and the fishing villages along the nearby coast are largely unchanged. You will almost certainly have the place to yourself on a weekday.

**The 2022 World Cup stadiums**

Eight stadiums were built or completely refurbished for the tournament, all within about 55 km of each other — something that no previous World Cup had managed. The most impressive is Lusail Stadium, a golden lattice bowl in the purpose-built city of Lusail. Tours run regularly, and league and cup matches are played there year-round. If you care even slightly about football, it is worth seeing one game.
