Saudi Arabia has around 2,500km of coastline split between two very different bodies of water. The Red Sea in the west is warm, brilliantly clear, and shelters some of the world's most biodiverse coral reefs. The Arabian Gulf in the east is shallower and sandier, with calmer water and long, flat beaches.

Beach access has become considerably more relaxed since 2019. Women can swim in mixed public areas, and the dress code is more like the broader Gulf region than the stricter norms of a decade ago. That said, modest swimwear is the norm outside private hotel pools.

**Around Jeddah**

The Corniche in [Jeddah](/asia/saudiarabia/jeddah) stretches 30km along the Red Sea and is the city's social spine — lined with parks, sculptures, and seafood restaurants. The offshore reef is the real draw; diving and snorkelling operators run trips out to it from the Corniche. Durat Al-Arus, north of Jeddah, is a quieter coastal resort area with a good beach and cleaner water than the city shoreline.

**The Eastern Province**

Half Moon Bay (Khawr Dawhat Salwa), south of [Khobar](/asia/saudiarabia/khobar), is perhaps the most celebrated beach on the Gulf side: a crescent of pale sand and calm, shallow water popular with families and weekend campers from across the province.

**Diving and Snorkelling**

The reef systems off [Jeddah](/asia/saudiarabia/jeddah) and [Yanbu](/asia/saudiarabia/yanbu) are genuinely world-class — colourful, fish-rich, and largely undiscovered by international divers. Several dive centres operate in both cities. Visibility is excellent from October through May.
