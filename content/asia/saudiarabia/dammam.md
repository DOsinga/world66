Dammam is the capital of Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province and one of the country's major urban centres, sitting on the western shore of the Arabian Gulf. The city grew rapidly with the oil industry after the discovery of petroleum in 1938 and today forms part of a large conurbation with the neighbouring cities of Khobar and Dhahran. Dammam is the main port of the Eastern Province and an important commercial hub, with significant Indian, Pakistani, and other expatriate communities.

The city has a long Corniche along the Gulf waterfront with parks and walkways, and the traditional Souk al-Manaah offers a contrast to the modern shopping centres. The Heritage Village in the old part of the city preserves examples of traditional Arabian architecture and holds collections of jewellery, weapons, and old photographs. The King Fahd Causeway linking Saudi Arabia to Bahrain begins from just south of the city near Khobar. Coral Island, reachable by boat from the Corniche, is a popular spot for fishing and swimming.

Dammam is notably more conservative than Saudi Arabia's larger cities. There are no bars or alcohol, and public entertainment was for many years heavily restricted, though the country has been opening up cultural and sporting venues since the Vision 2030 reform programme began in 2016.
