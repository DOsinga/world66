Dir’iyah sits on the outskirts of [Riyadh](/asia/saudiarabia/riyadh) in the Wadi Hanifa valley, and it is where the story of modern Saudi Arabia begins. This was the ancestral home of the Al Saud family and the capital of the First Saudi State in the 18th century, before the Ottomans destroyed it in 1818.

The At-Turaif district, the ruined mud-brick heart of the old city, is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most impressive historical monuments in the Gulf. The distinctive Najdi architecture — thick earthen walls, narrow alleys, tall wind towers — has been extensively restored. Walking through the site gives a vivid sense of the scale and sophistication of what was here before the destruction.

The surrounding area has been developed into a broad cultural quarter (the Dir’iyah Gate Development) with restaurants, galleries, and events spaces. The result is a mix of genuine history and polished tourism infrastructure. Go for the UNESCO site, which justifies the visit entirely on its own terms.
