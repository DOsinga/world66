title: "Food"
type: section
---

Saudi food is built around rice, meat, and bread, slow-cooked and generously spiced with cardamom, turmeric, cloves, and dried lemon. The cuisine is not particularly spicy by the standards of the wider region, but it is aromatic and satisfying.

**Kabsa** is the dish most Saudis would name as their national food. Long-grain rice cooked with meat — usually chicken or lamb — tomato, dried fruit, and a blend of spices. The dish varies by region: in the Hejaz it tends towards sweeter, in Najd towards more savory. You will find kabsa everywhere, from roadside canteens serving plastic plates of it for a few riyals to restaurants in [Riyadh](/asia/saudiarabia/riyadh) making an elaborate event of it.

**Mandi** is older and simpler: meat cooked low and slow in an underground pit (or in modern versions, a sealed oven), then served over rice that has absorbed the smoke and fat. The result is extraordinarily tender. Mandi houses are easy to spot — look for the clay ovens or the signs advertising whole roasted lamb. A good mandi is one of the great eating experiences the country offers.

**Jareesh** is cracked wheat cooked down slowly into a porridge-like dish, often topped with chicken or lamb and a ladleful of clarified butter. It looks modest and tastes excellent.

**Saleeg** is a white rice dish cooked in broth and milk until creamy and soft, typically served with roasted chicken and a spicy tomato sauce. It is a speciality of the Hejaz region, particularly around [Jeddah](/asia/saudiarabia/jeddah).

**Harees** — wheat and meat pounded together into a smooth porridge — is found across the Gulf and is particularly associated with Ramadan and Eid celebrations.

**Saudi coffee (qahwa)** is very different from espresso: brewed light, flavoured with cardamom and sometimes saffron or rose water, and served in small cups. Refusing coffee is considered impolite. Dates are always offered alongside.

Saudi cities have a full range of international restaurants, from fast food chains to Japanese and Italian restaurants in [Riyadh](/asia/saudiarabia/riyadh) and [Jeddah](/asia/saudiarabia/jeddah). The local food is better and cheaper.
