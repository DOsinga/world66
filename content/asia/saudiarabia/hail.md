Ha'il occupies a striking position in the Saudi landscape, sitting between the Great Nafud desert to the north and the Aja and Salma mountain ranges that frame the city. For centuries it served as a crossroads for pilgrims, traders, and Bedouin tribes moving across the Arabian interior. The Rashidi dynasty ruled from here in the 19th century, and the city retains a proud sense of its own identity, distinct from the Hejaz to the west and the Najd heartland to the south. Visitors who make the effort to reach Ha'il find a city that feels authentically Saudi in ways that the more cosmopolitan coastal cities do not.

The ancient rock art sites around Ha'il are the region's headline attraction, and deservedly so. The petroglyphs at Jubbah and Shuwaymis, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage list in 2015, span thousands of years and depict cattle, ostriches, human figures, and hunting scenes that record a time when this desert was green savanna. The carvings at Jubbah are set against dramatic sandstone outcrops at the edge of the Nafud, and the site has a visitor center that provides useful context. Shuwaymis, further south, is more remote and requires a guide, but the density and quality of the rock art there is extraordinary.

In the city itself, the A'arif Fort sits on a rocky hill overlooking the old town, its mud-brick walls offering panoramic views across the modern city and the mountains beyond. The traditional souq below the fort is worth a wander, particularly during the cooler months when Ha'il hosts its annual desert festival. The region is famous for its hospitality -- Ha'il calls itself the capital of Arabian generosity, and you will find the claim is not empty.

Ha'il also works as a base for exploring the northern desert. The Nafud's red sand dunes begin just outside the city, and day trips into the desert landscape are straightforward to arrange. The Aja mountains to the west offer hiking with views across the surrounding plains. If you are driving between Riyadh and the northern borders, or heading toward Tabuk and AlUla, Ha'il makes a natural and rewarding stopover.
