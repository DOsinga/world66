Medina is the second holiest city in Islam, after [Mecca](/asia/saudiarabia/mecca) but before [Jerusalem](/asia/israel/jerusalem). It lies in the Hejaz region of western Saudi Arabia, about 200 kilometres north of Mecca and close to the harbour town of Yanbu. The name literally means "the city" — short for the City of the Prophet.

When Muhammad left Mecca during the Hijra, the citizens of Medina invited him to become their leader, and from here Islam began its spread across the world. Medina is not a tourist city. It is a holy place, strictly forbidden to non-Muslims, and most foreign visitors come on pilgrimage — either the Hajj or the Umra. A stop in Medina is not technically required, but many pilgrims make the journey to visit the tomb of the Prophet.

The Mosque of the Prophet, with its iconic green dome, is enormous and sumptuously decorated. Inside lies the tomb of Muhammad. The Qiblatayn Mosque, where Muhammad decreed that prayer should be directed toward Mecca rather than Jerusalem, is another significant site. Baqi cemetery, said to be the largest in the world, houses the family and grandchildren of the Prophet, over twenty thousand of his companions, and many great scholars and residents of Medina.
