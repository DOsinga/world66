Qatif is one of the oldest continuously inhabited places in the Eastern Province, with a history of settlement going back several thousand years. It sits on the Arabian Gulf coast north of [Khobar](/asia/saudiarabia/khobar) and has historically been a centre of pearl diving and trade.

The old city has a traditional market and the ruins of the Portuguese fort (Qal'at Qatif), which dates to the 16th century. The city is predominantly Shia Muslim, which makes it culturally distinct from much of the rest of Saudi Arabia. It is a fairly low-key destination — not much visited by tourists — but worth a stop if you are exploring the Eastern Province.
