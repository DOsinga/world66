The mud-brick fortress that Abdul Aziz ibn Saud and his followers captured in 1902, the event that marks the beginning of modern Saudi Arabia. The courtyard contains a well that is still functional, surrounded by painted wooden doors opening into bare rooms. Upstairs, carved walls and wooden columns support the roof. A well-restored traditional majlis sits on the ground floor. Open Saturday to Wednesday, 7:30am–1:30pm; a permit from the nearby museum may be required.
