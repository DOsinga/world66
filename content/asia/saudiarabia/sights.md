title: "Sights"

---

Saudi Arabia's most important sights fall into a few categories: ancient pre-Islamic sites, Islamic heritage, and the natural landscape. The country's decision to open up to tourism has been accompanied by significant investment in making these places accessible.

**Hegra (Mada'in Saleh), AlUla region** — Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site. Over 100 Nabataean rock-cut tombs from around the 1st century BCE to the 1st century CE, carved into sandstone formations in the northwest desert. The site and the surrounding AlUla landscape are extraordinary.

**At-Turaif District, Dir'iyah** — The mud-brick ruins of the first Saudi capital, on the outskirts of [Riyadh](/asia/saudiarabia/riyadh). Also a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The architecture is distinctively Najdi, and the scale of the site is impressive. See the [Dir'iyah](/asia/saudiarabia/diriyah) page for more.

**Masmak Fortress, Riyadh** — The 19th-century mud-brick fort where the young Abdulaziz ibn Saud stormed the Riyadh garrison in 1902, beginning his campaign to unite the Arabian Peninsula. Now a well-presented heritage museum in the heart of the old city.

**National Museum, Riyadh** — Eight galleries covering the natural and human history of the Arabian Peninsula, from prehistoric rock art through to the present. The best single introduction to the country.

**Al-Balad (Old Jeddah)** — The historic core of [Jeddah](/asia/saudiarabia/jeddah), a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2014. Coral-built merchants' houses with intricately carved wooden screens (rawasheen), narrow alleys, and old mosques. The Naseef House is particularly striking.

**Edge of the World (Jebel Fihrayn)** — An escarpment about 90km northwest of Riyadh where the limestone plateau drops dramatically into the desert lowlands. The view is one of the most impressive natural sights near the capital. Accessible by 4WD.

**Rijal Alma Heritage Village, Asir** — A collection of centuries-old stone houses in the Asir mountains, many with brightly painted interiors. One of the most distinctive architectural sites in the country, and still partially inhabited.

**Al-Ahsa Oasis, Eastern Province** — The world's largest oasis, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. A cluster of ancient forts, traditional crafts, and millions of date palms. See the [Al-Ahsa](/asia/saudiarabia/alhasa) page.
