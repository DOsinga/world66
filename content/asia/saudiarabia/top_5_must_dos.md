title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Saudi Arabia"
type: section
---

## 1. Hegra (Mada'in Saleh)

The Nabataean tombs at Hegra are Saudi Arabia's most spectacular ancient site and its first UNESCO World Heritage listing. More than 100 monumental tombs are cut directly into sandstone outcrops across a desert plain — the same culture that built Petra in Jordan, but here with far fewer visitors and an eerie, wide-open grandeur. The facades are carved in extraordinary detail, and many still bear Nabataean inscriptions naming the dead. The site is near the oasis town of AlUla, and the whole region — ochre cliffs, narrow canyons, and rock art — rewards several days of exploration.

## 2. Dir'iyah

On the outskirts of [Riyadh](/asia/saudiarabia/riyadh), Dir'iyah is the ancestral home of the House of Saud and the birthplace of the first Saudi state in the 18th century. The At-Turaif district — a large complex of mud-brick palaces and mosques in the distinctive Najdi architectural style — is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It has been extensively restored and is one of the most impressive historical sites in the Gulf. The surrounding area has been developed into a cultural quarter with restaurants and galleries, but the old city itself is the draw.

## 3. The Asir Mountains

Few visitors expect Saudi Arabia to have a region of cool green mountains, cloud forests, and centuries-old stone villages perched on clifftops — but the Asir delivers exactly that. The area around Abha, in the southwest near the Yemen border, is a world apart from the desert interior. The villages of the Rijal Alma heritage area are particularly striking: multi-story stone houses with colourful painted interiors, still inhabited. Come in summer if you want an escape from the heat elsewhere.

## 4. The Red Sea

The Red Sea coast is one of the world's great diving destinations. The reefs off [Jeddah](/asia/saudiarabia/jeddah) and [Yanbu](/asia/saudiarabia/yanbu) are remarkably unspoiled given their proximity to major cities. Coral coverage is high, visibility is excellent, and the water is warm year-round. The coastline remains relatively quiet — the reefs are often shared only with Saudis who have been diving them for decades.

## 5. The National Museum, Riyadh

The National Museum in [Riyadh](/asia/saudiarabia/riyadh), adjacent to the historic Masmak Fortress, is the best single place to understand the country you're traveling through. Eight galleries take you from the geological origins of the Arabian Peninsula through ancient civilizations, the coming of Islam, and the emergence of the Saudi state. The scale models of the Grand Mosque and the Hajj rituals are particularly impressive. Allow at least two hours, and combine it with a walk through the old Masmak district.
