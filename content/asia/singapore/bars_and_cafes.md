---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Drinking in Singapore is not cheap. Beer in a bar typically costs S$10–15, cocktails more. There is also an alcohol curfew: retail shops cannot sell alcohol after 10:30pm (earlier in some zones). Bars and licensed restaurants are exempt, but the rule was introduced to limit late-night drinking in public spaces and has shaped where nightlife happens.

The most interesting areas for bars and cafes are:

**Keong Saik Road and Duxton Hill** — a row of restored shophouses in Tanjong Pagar that has become the best stretch of bars and restaurants in the city. Good cocktail bars, wine bars, and some of the more interesting small restaurants. Less touristy than Clarke Quay and more worth your time.

**Clarke Quay** — the tourist-facing riverfront entertainment district in former godowns along the Singapore River. Lively and loud on weekends; a reasonable place to start a night if you are new to the city, though not where Singaporeans go if they have a choice.

**Ann Siang Hill and Club Street** — another shophouse neighbourhood adjacent to Chinatown, with wine bars, craft beer spots, and cafes. Quieter and more pleasant than Clarke Quay.

**Haji Lane (Kampong Glam)** — the narrow street in the Arab Quarter is lined with independent cafes and boutiques in narrow shophouses. A good area for coffee and lunch.

The **kopitiam** — the traditional coffeeshop — is worth understanding. Singapore's kopi is brewed strong through a flannel sock-filter, typically served with evaporated or condensed milk. Order it hot or iced. Teh is tea. Add "O" for black (no milk), "C" for evaporated milk only, "kosong" for no sugar. It is very good, very cheap, and available everywhere from 6am onwards. Any neighbourhood kopitiam is better value than a branded cafe chain.
