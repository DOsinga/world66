---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Bicycles are the obvious choice and the rental shops at the pier will fit you out for S$5–10 per day depending on the bike. Mountain bikes are worth the extra dollar or two if you plan to go off the main tracks. The ride from the village to Chek Jawa at the eastern tip takes about 30–40 minutes on good tracks.

A handful of trishaws (bicycle taxis) are also available at the pier for those who would rather be pedalled. Rates are negotiated directly with the driver; agree on a price before you set off. Private vehicles are not allowed on the island except for residents and park vehicles.

Walking is perfectly feasible for shorter distances around the village and the western part of the island, though the full length of the island on foot is a serious undertaking.
