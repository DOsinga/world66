Pulau Hantu is a small pair of islands linked by a narrow seawall, lying about 6 km south of mainland [Singapore](/asia/singapore). The name means "Ghost Island" in Malay, a reference to an old legend about two warriors who fought to the death here and were transformed into the two islands by the sea god. Despite the eerie name, it is one of the most peaceful spots in Singapore's waters.

The islands are popular with snorkelers and anglers. The sheltered lagoon between the two islands has surprisingly decent coral growth for Singaporean waters, and at low tide you can explore rock pools teeming with sea urchins, anemones, and small reef fish. Visibility is not world-class — this is still the Strait of Singapore — but it is good enough to be rewarding, especially on calm days.

There is no ferry service to Pulau Hantu, so you will need to charter a bumboat from Marina South Pier or the West Coast Pier. Most visitors come for day trips, bringing their own food and water since there are no facilities on the island. A basic shelter and toilet block exist on the larger island, but that is about it. The best conditions for snorkeling are during the northeast monsoon season from November to March, when the waters are calmer.
