Pulau Sekang is one of Singapore's southern islands, a tiny speck in the strait between Sentosa and the Indonesian Riau Islands. Historically a Malay fishing village on stilts — the kind of settlement that existed across Singapore before rapid development transformed the mainland — the island offered a glimpse of a pre-modern way of life, with wooden houses, sturdy boats, and a quiet menagerie of birds, monkeys, and goats.

Most of Singapore's southern islands are now restricted or undergoing reclamation. Access to Pulau Sekang is limited, and the village that once stood here has largely disappeared. The island is of more historical than practical interest today, a reminder of the kampung life that Singapore has otherwise built over entirely.
