---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

Singapore has a real nightlife, though it is expensive and increasingly concentrated in a few areas. The city is safe at night, well-connected by late MRT and night buses, and the main question is what you want to spend.

**Clubs.** The large clubs are mostly around Clarke Quay and the Marina Bay area. Zouk, in its current location at Clarke Quay, is the city's long-standing institution for electronic music. Cover charges of S$30–50 are common at the bigger venues on weekends. The scene leans mainstream.

**Live music.** The Esplanade (Singapore's main performing arts centre on the waterfront) has free outdoor performances almost every evening on its covered outdoor stage — worth checking what is on. The Singapore Symphony Orchestra performs at the Victoria Concert Hall. Jazz, folk, and indie acts play at smaller venues scattered across the city; check the Bandwagon or Timeout Singapore listings.

**Night Safari.** Singapore Zoo's Night Safari is a genuinely impressive attraction: a tram ride and walking trails through a wildlife park at night, with animals in naturalistic enclosures. Admission is around S$55 for adults. Not nightlife in the usual sense, but unusual and well executed.

**Geylang.** Singapore's red-light district is a twenty-minute MRT ride east of the centre. It is legal, regulated, and unlike the rest of Singapore in feel — older shophouses, open-air eating at all hours, durian stalls, and a noticeable lack of the usual Singapore polish. The best late-night food in the city is here: frog porridge, durian, and cheap zi char (Chinese stir-fry) restaurants open past midnight. Safe to walk around; the area is busy and well-lit.

**Rooftop bars.** The SkyPark bar at Marina Bay Sands is the obvious one: expensive, crowded with tourists, but the view is genuinely extraordinary. The bars at 1-Altitude (at One Raffles Place) and LeVeL33 (a microbrewery on the 33rd floor of a Marina Bay tower) are alternatives with fewer selfie-sticks.
