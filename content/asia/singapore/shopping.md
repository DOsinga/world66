---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Singapore is a serious shopping city. Electronics, fashion, and luxury goods are what draw most shoppers; the ethnic districts offer more interesting and less predictable buying.

**Orchard Road** is the main shopping belt — a two-kilometre stretch of connected malls running from Dhoby Ghaut to Tanglin. ION Orchard, Paragon, and Ngee Ann City (which contains Takashimaya) are the anchors. The range is comprehensive but the experience is generic. Useful for electronics, international fashion, and anything standardised.

**Mustafa Centre** (145 Syed Alwi Road, Little India) is something else entirely. Open 24 hours a day, seven days a week, it is a vast, slightly chaotic emporium selling electronics, jewellery, saris, groceries, currency, and an improbable range of other goods across several floors. Currency exchange rates here are among the best in the city. Worth visiting even if you are not buying anything.

**Little India** (Serangoon Road and surrounding streets) sells textiles, saris, flowers, spices, temple offerings, and Bollywood memorabilia. The Tekka Centre wet market is one of the most vivid food markets in the city. The fabric shops around Campbell Lane and Dunlop Street are good for cheap Indian and Malay textiles.

**Chinatown** (Pagoda, Trengganu, and Smith Streets) has a street market running most days with souvenirs, dried goods, tea, and crafts. More touristy than Little India but still with genuine shops selling items people actually use. The People's Park Complex nearby is the place for cheap electronics and mobile phone accessories.

**Haji Lane and Arab Street** are good for small independent boutiques, batik fabric, and rattan goods.

**Bugis Street** was historically Singapore's most famous street market; it is now a covered complex selling cheap clothing and accessories, popular with teenagers. Not as interesting as its history suggests.

GST (Goods and Services Tax, currently 9%) is refundable for tourists on purchases over S$100 at participating retailers through the Tourist Refund Scheme — claim at the airport before departure.
