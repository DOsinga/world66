---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Singapore is a small island, but there is more to do outdoors than its urban density suggests. The parks and nature reserves are genuinely good, and the surrounding sea offers options for water-based activities.

**Hiking and cycling.** The Southern Ridges is a 10-kilometre trail linking four parks — Mount Faber, Telok Blangah Hill, Kent Ridge Park, and Labrador Nature Reserve — via elevated walkways and forest paths. A good half-day walk with city views. Bukit Timah Nature Reserve has trails through primary rainforest; macaques and flying lemurs are common. The park connector network around the island is good for cycling; bicycles can be rented at many points.

**Night Safari.** The Singapore Zoo's night zoo on the northern edge of the island is one of the best wildlife attractions in Southeast Asia. A tram ride through naturalistic enclosures reveals civets, fishing cats, and nocturnal deer in near-darkness. Walking trails extend the visit. Admission around S$55 for adults; book in advance on weekends.

**Singapore Zoo and River Wonders.** The main zoo is very good — large naturalistic enclosures, a strong focus on Southeast Asian biodiversity. Adjacent River Wonders (formerly River Safari) covers freshwater ecosystems around the world and includes a giant panda exhibit.

**Gardens by the Bay.** Beyond the Supertrees and conservatories, the gardens have good walking paths along the Marina waterfront, free outdoor music performances, and changing horticultural displays. Worth half a day. The adjacent Marina Barrage has a rooftop park popular for kite flying on weekends.

**Watersports.** East Coast Park rents equipment for kayaking, windsurfing, and cycling along the coast. Sentosa Island has cable skiing, parasailing, and a surf machine at Siloso Beach. For open-water diving and snorkelling, the southern islands — Kusu, St John's, Sisters' Islands — have reasonable coral, though visibility varies.

**Spectator sports.** The Singapore F1 Grand Prix is held on a night street circuit around Marina Bay in September; tickets sell out early and the city fills with visitors. The Singapore Premier League runs a domestic football season from February to November.
