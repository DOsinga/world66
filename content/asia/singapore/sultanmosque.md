The Sultan Mosque (Masjid Sultan) is the focal point of the Kampong Glam historic district. The current building dates from 1928, designed by Denis Santry of Swan and Maclaren in a Saracenic Revival style, and replaced an earlier mosque built in 1824 with funding from the East India Company. The gold onion dome is the most recognizable feature; look closely at its base and you will see it is ringed with glass bottle ends, said to have been donated by poorer members of the congregation who could not afford other building materials.

The prayer hall is vast and can hold up to 5,000 worshippers. Non-Muslims are welcome to visit outside prayer times, but dress modestly and remove shoes before entering. The surrounding streets of Arab Street and Haji Lane are worth exploring for textiles, perfumes, and some of the best Middle Eastern food in Singapore.
