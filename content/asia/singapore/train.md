Singapore's Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) is the backbone of the city's public transport network — fast, air-conditioned, and cheap. The system covers the whole island with five main lines (North-South, East-West, North-East, Circle, and Downtown), plus two Light Rapid Transit (LRT) feeders serving the northeastern suburbs of Sengkang and Punggol.

Trains run from around 5:30am to midnight, with peak-hour frequency of two to three minutes on major lines. Fares are distance-based and paid with an EZ-Link card (sold at any station), which is also valid on buses. Single-trip tickets are available but cost more. A tourist day pass provides unlimited rides for a flat fee and is worth it for heavy sightseeing days.

The network map is clear and station names are announced in English. Platform screen doors keep stations cool and quiet. Most transfers between lines involve a short walk within the same station. Changi Airport is connected directly by the East-West Line extension, making arrival by rail straightforward.
