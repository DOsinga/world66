---
title: "Beaches in Batu Pahat"
type: section
---

Batu Pahat is a town located in the State of Johor, in Malaysia. Not at all famous for its beaches, the town still has a lot of hidden surprises for any visitor to uncover. Although water sports are not popular in conservative Malaysia, those who love water sports and activities need not fret, since there are many of these to do. Here are some **Batu Pahat Beaches**.

**Tasik Y**

Not quite a beach but more of a large body of water, the Tasik Y is a man-made lake constructed by the state government in response to the need for more recreational areas around the town. Tasik Y, as a whole, consists of the lake and the surrounding park around it, and it provides a good way to spend an afternoon, particularly when the weather gets too warm indoors in the humid Malaysian climate. Take a stroll along the lakeside in the evening, have a picnic in the afternoon. Those with children will always find that this is good place to bring them – the park has a large playground consisting of swings, seesaws and climbing frames for children to enjoy.

**Minyak Beku**

The seaside of Batu Pahat is where the name of city originates, according to the elders in the town. There is a lighthouse nearby, which is open to the public to view the sea. Those looking for a slightly more interesting way to spend an afternoon can opt to go for a coffee at one of nearby coffee shops – an activity very popular among the local people. Stand by the seaside and enjoy the wonderful sea breeze as it sweeps over you, or watch the seagulls in a distance in the evenings, against the backdrop of a sunset for a wonderful way to end a day.

**Wet World**

Another of Batu Pahat&rsquo;s popular establishments, the Wet World, is not a beach but more of a place to enjoy water activities and sports in a hot climate like Malaysia. The park is not found in the city itself, but in the outskirts of the town on the famous Merdeka Lane. Tourists pay a small fee to enter, but this is all definitely worth it, as the only water park in all of Johor unfurls before your eyes. The 44-acre establishment consists of water slides, pools and tubes where the family can spend an afternoon of endless fun in the sun – very much akin to an afternoon at the beach.
