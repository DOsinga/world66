**Visit the Impressive City of ****Johor Bahru******

A popular option among the various **Batu Pahat day trips **is an excursion to the impressive city of Johor Bahru. Johor Bahru is an important commercial and industrial hub in Malaysia, but it also attracts the attention of many tourists, with its big size and interesting sights, and due to the fact that it is located within walking distance of Singapore. Here are some tips as to how to reach the city and what to see once you're there.

**Getting There**

The distance by road from Batu Pahat to Johor Bahru is about 110km. The road connection between both cities is good, so you can count on a fast trip. If you don&rsquo;t have a private car, there are several transportation options. The most common choice is taking a bus. It will cost you about $4 and a good number of the buses are well-maintained and have air conditioning. There are also long-distance taxis available, but they are more expensive and not much faster.

**Sights**

Johor Bahru offers many interesting sights. The Sultan Abu Bakar Mosque is one of them. Unfortunately, non-Muslims are not allowed inside, but everyone can admire its beautiful Victorian architecture. The top sight of Batu Pahat is the Sultan Abu Bakar Royal Palace, which nowadays functions as a museum. It is open to tourists from Saturday to Thursday from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. and shows the high living standards of the former royal family of Johor. The admission fee is about $7.
