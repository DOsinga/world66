---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Batu Pahat"
type: section
---

Here are some **Batu Pahat Nightlife** highlights. Nights in Batu Pahat are somewhat different from other towns, and with its large Chinese population, it is different from other parts of Malaysia.

**Pasar Malam**

The famous night market in the regions of Singapore and Malaysia is immensely popular in Batu Pahat, and even though the culture of Pasar Malam (literally &ldquo;market at night&rdquo;) is beginning to die out in more developed cities such as Singapore and Kuala Lumpur, the markets are still very common in small town areas such as Batu Pahat. The markets sell a variety of items – from clothes and bags, to street foods and little snacks that Malaysia is so famous for. Be sure to try some of the classic favorites, such as the Nasi Biryani Batu Pahat, yellow long-grained rice with spicy curry, or Kueh Tutu, a rice flour-based sweet with a brown-sugared center. Although piracy is not encouraged, be sure to look through the dozens and dozens of little stalls peddling illegal DVDs – such culture is a large part of the Malaysian Pasar Malam. The night markets here are rotated, setting up at various locations everyday. The Friday markets at Taman Sri Jaya and the Saturday ones at Kampung Kenangan Dato Onn are particularly busy, and very worth visiting for the complete Pasar Malam experience.

**Laser**

Batu Pahat, like every small town in Malaysia with a large Chinese population, has a strip of shops located in the city center that caters to the younger crowd. These are usually marked with colorful neon lights, signaling the presence of a nightspot in the town. The Laser is located in such a strip in Batu Pahat – it is totally unmistakable as it is located smack in the middle of the city center. With its neon lights, the Laser is a typical nightclub that caters to a select group of the younger Chinese in the town. Clubbing is not a typical social activity among the local youths, so be prepared to meet some of those who are part of the street gangs or those who are simply out to have serious fun.

**De Palais**

The De Palais is a pub spot in the town, and unlike the clubs, the pubs are a lot more subdued – consisting mostly of an older clientele who are in for a late beer or a swig of the cognac on the shelf. The De Palais is somewhat of a European-style pub, located along the river with a nice al fresco ambience. The pub can be very crowded on weekend nights, and serves up some wonderful pub snacks in the form of spicy calamari rings or deep fried wedges. The beer here is very cheap, which explains the number of people in the place.
