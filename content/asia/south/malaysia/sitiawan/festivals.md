---
title: "Festivals in Sitiawan"
type: section
---

**Sitiawan festivals **put forth an enjoyable way of exploring the various cultural influences that define Sitiawan—one of the most important tourist destinations in the Manjung District of Malaysia. Sitiawan is home to people belonging to various religious faiths. For instance, it has a distinct Chinese influence in its local customs. This is because Chinese immigrants settled in Malaysia many centuries ago. Similarly, some Hindu festivals are also celebrated since Hinduism was the most predominant religion in ancient Malaysia. The most popular of Sitiawan festivals include:

**Hindu Festival of Thaipusam (January)**

This festival is usually celebrated in the last week of January. Though it has a distinct aura of Hinduism to it, the Thaipusam has now become a popular regional festival. Thaipusam is celebrated more enthusiastically in the neighboring city of Pangkor, owing to its larger Hindu population. However, Sitiawan too celebrates Thaipusam with great zeal. Sometimes, the festival is celebrated in the first week of February also. Special feasts are prepared and offered to the idol of Lord Subramanya—the son of Lord Shiva.

**Chinese Festival of Ching Ming (April)**

Ching Ming is celebrated in the first week of April wherein its exact date is based upon the calculations of the lunar calendar. This festival is celebrated in many parts of south-east Asia, including China. It is often referred to as the Spring-Remembrance Day or the Grave-Sweeping Day. This festival is essentially about remembering and paying respects to the ancestors and elders of the family. The idea is also to ensure a place in Heaven, i.e. to make an offering and pray for a peaceful afterlife. Native families can be seen offering paper money to the graves of their beloved. After coming back from the cemeteries, people celebrate the festival with fruits and wine.
