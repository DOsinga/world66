---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Sitiawan"
type: section
---

Although **Sitiawan nightlife** is a little bit hamstrung by the high price of Malaysian beer and that country's more conservative attitudes (as compared to Thailand and Vietnam), you can have a pretty good time in the town if you know where to go. Don't expect too many traditional bars and nightclubs though.

**Sampling the Street Food**

One of the main attractions of Sitiawan nightlife is the food court that is open until quite late. It's a vibrant, happening place where you can meet both travelers and local people and enjoy such Malaysian delicacies as 'yau char queh', a deep fried flatbread. You can also enjoy 'kong peah', a type of Chinese bun stuffed with onions and lard and cooked in a clay oven. This particular dish you can only find in Sitiawan and its satellite towns. Other dishes worth checking out are 'oh chien' (oyster omelet), 'loh mee' (noodles and bamboo shoots) and 'ang chio mee sua' (red rice wine noodles). On a hot night you can also enjoy the scrumptious Indian dessert 'cendol'.

**Bread Forest Bakery**

Sitiawan nightlife may not be hugely alcohol-fueled but you can wile the evening away in the lovely, atmospheric Bread Forest Bakery on Jalan Sejahtera. The upper floor cafe is decorated with petal styles and furnished with real rope swings. The green-dressed waitresses can be a little slow with the service but the quality of the food is excellent. The colorful cakes and pastries served are a particular delight.
