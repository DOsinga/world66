When you plan to visit this Malaysian city, here are some **Sitiawan things to do**. Sitiawan is a region in the Manjung district in Malaysia. As of the year two thousand, this area&rsquo;s population has grown to 95,992. As of late, this has gone from a relatively desolate place to a booming place for tourism.

**Nightlife**

When it comes to the nightlife in the area, one of the places that you&rsquo;re going to want to visit is a place called &ldquo;night time serenades.&rdquo; This bar is the perfect place to be when the sun sinks into the China sea. This bar is right next to the ocean, which allows for a spectacular view of the sunset while you let the breeze and the cocktails wash your cares away.

**Shopping**

Until recently, a shop called &ldquo;Dawn&rdquo; was the premiere shopping mall in the area, but thanks to the recent wealth and economic** **growth of the area, there are now far more options for your shopping experience. One of these is Econ market. Econ market is a super market that opened in 2006. This particular super store allows you to find just about anything you would find in a western super market, as well as things that are native to the area. Unfortunately, this area isn&rsquo;t one of the best when it comes to tourist shopping and souvenirs, but you&rsquo;re still likely to find some interesting local treats while shopping in the area.

**Attractions**

Attractions are few and far between in the area, but there are some that you&rsquo;re not going to want to miss out on. The museum opened it&rsquo;s doors in the year 2003, and was established to preserve the artifacts and history of the Fuzhou settlers that made their home in the area. It&rsquo;s housed in an old converted church parsonage. Behind the museum is a well that was dug by the early pioneers in order to create their water supply. Some other attractions of the area are the old buildings, one of which is the Sitiawan Chinese Methodist church. This church is a white building in the shape of a cross. This building was originally built in the year 1905, but was renovated after a fire.

**Food**

The food in the area is based around kong piang buns and sweet and sour fish maw soup. There are restaurants that serve different kinds of foods, but this is the food that is native and traditional to the area.
