**Experience the Beauty and History of Sitiawan**

The following **Sitiawan must do's **will open your eyes to the endless possibilities of holidaying in small towns. Sitiawan might not be among the top traveling destinations of Malaysia, but this scenic and idyllic town is definitely worth visiting.

Beautiful landscapes, lush green scenery, delicious cuisine and a variety of modern and ancient festivals is what visitors find in Sitiawan. This city offers a perfect mixture of stimulation and relaxation with its serene coastline and the hustle and bustle of street life. While the spectacular beaches offer the perfect seclusion to get closer to the nature, its open-air markets give the feeling of big-city life. And like any other Malaysian city, Sitiawan city streets offer a religious flavor of having Hindu and Buddhist temples standing along with the Islamic mosques.

In addition to the following must do's, visitors may want to visit Pangkor Island, Kampung Sitiawan River or the rubber estates.

#### **Sin Busu Hill**

At almost 1,000 feet high, Sin Busu Hill is considered the highest peak in the whole district. The hill is actually located in the neighborhood Lumut. Many travellers prefer hiking on the hill. While climbing past the green foliage, it&rsquo;s hard to miss the picturesque sights of Sitiawan, the nearby sea, Pangkor Island or even the Lumut Naval Base.

#### **Lumut Waterfront**

Get enchanted with the spectacular view of the Lumut Waterfront. The stunning sunset is something you should not miss. Situated just 12 kilometers from Sitiawan, the waterfront offers a serene yet trendy atmosphere thanks to the recent renovation. Today there are retail stores, cafes and children's amusement parks in and around the waterfront area.

#### **Teluk Batik and Teluk Rubiah Beach **

Located between Sitiawan and Lumut, these two unspoiled, quieter beaches offer the perfect ambiance for recreation. Go fishing or shell-collecting or even take a quiet stroll along the beach.

#### **Sitiawan Settlement Museum
**

The Settlement Museum in Sitiawan is located in a 70-year-old grand church parsonage. History and artifacts of the Fuzhou settlers have been preserved nicely in this museum. The museum has been in existence since 2003 and attracts a throng of visitors every day.

#### **Colonial Style Architecture**

Another major tourist destination are the colonial buildings. Even today, you will come across many traditional Chinese-style buildings which were set up during 1927 but remain fully functional. The Anglo-Chinese School is one such popular colonial building.
