---
title: "Shopping in Kathmandu"
type: section
---

Over decades mountaineers and adventurers flocked to Kathmandu for hiking to the highest point on earth, Mt. Everest. In recent years Kathmandu has become more popular among the vacationers for its natural beauty. Kathmandu now has evolved into a city capable of catering to the needs of all types of tourists. Kathmandu shopping might not give you a modern upscale experience of shopping in posh malls but it will definitely give you a vastly different and unique experience. **Kathmandu Shopping** is famous for the types of things that are exclusively available there.

**Pasmina**

The big brothers of Nepal, India and Pakistan have banned Pashmina trading and this has made Kathmandu the hub of Pashmina of the Indian peninsula. Pashmina shawls woven by the refugees who stay near Patan are the best in terms of quality. Pashmina shawls and scarves are available at Kathmandu at much lower prices than anywhere in the world. However, you ought to be cautious because there are imitations. It is recommended that you do your shopping from the government approved shops.

**Clothes**

Kathmandu is famous for woolen clothes and fancy dresses. New Road, Thamel, Durbar Square and Indira Chowk are the best places to buy clothes. A wide variety of clothes are available here. All types of vibrant colorful dresses will definitely catch your attention. If you want to shop for different kinds of items, then you can look out for saffron monk robes, hippie gauzes and funky colorful elegant silks.

**Electronic Items**

Kathmandu has no entry or sales tax on any of the imported electronic items because the government wants to maximize the sale of electronic gadgets in the country. This policy has made Kathmandu an ideal place for shopping cheap electronic goods. You will find all kinds of electronic items produced in China, Japan and Korea here. You are likely to be surprised when you see a fully-automated remote-controlled toy car being sold for just $1 or a wall clock for 40 cents.

**Specialty Shopping**

If you visit Nepal for the first time and you end up looking for something that is very unique to this place, then the first thing that you will draw your admiration is the Kukuri knife. You will find these well-decorated small arc-shaped knives almost in all markets. You will find some shops in Thamel and Khichapokhri selling some of the very traditional Nepalese musical instruments like bamboo flutes and peddle sarangi. You will also find a lot of Tibetan curios near Basantpur Square.
