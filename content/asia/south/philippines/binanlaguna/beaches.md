---
title: "Beaches in Binan Laguna"
type: section
---

The province of Laguna and the city of Binan have barely any beaches, but they make up for it with their numerous lakes. Here are some **Binan Laguna Beaches.**

**Lake**** Caliraya**

The Lake Caliraya occupies a special place in Laguna for being an enclosed lake where one can enjoy the thrills of a beach. Except for the sand, the lake is everything that a beach can be, and is a popular place among the locals for various kinds of water activities. In particular, the activities on the surface are very well developed, and tourists can try anything from speed boating to water skiing. The highlight here are the Jet Ski lessons that anyone can take, and zipping around the lake on a rented Jet Ski is one of the greatest pleasures of being in Laguna. Lake Caliraya is also teeming with wildlife, and the more adventurous can try snorkeling, although the lake can be rather muddy at certain times of the year. All in all, the place makes for a great family vacation.

**Laguna de Bay**

The Laguna de Bay is Southeast Asia&rsquo;s biggest freshwater lake, and it has several islands on the lake itself. The province of Laguna surrounds the lake, and as such, the place is easy to locate. With its size and area, it is no wonder that different activities can be found around the place. Nearer to the city of Binan, the area of the lake is suited for swimming, and local children can often be found there taking reprieve from the humid Filipino sun after school gets out. Those looking to experience a little slice of the Philippines in this manner are welcome to join them, and the cooling waters provide a wonderful way of escaping the heat of the afternoon. Be very careful, though, particularly for those with children, as some parts of the lake can be deep and there have been cases of drowning.

**Hidden Valley Springs**

The Hidden Valley Springs are the most prominent of Laguna&rsquo;s many hot springs, and is popular among the tourists visiting Laguna, particularly those who are here for the first time. As its name suggests, the Hidden Valley Springs are hidden away in a secluded location – a little area tucked away in the town of Alaminos. Take a dip in the springs, run by a resort and spa, and enjoy a little time on your own as the hot water steams your skin. The water is full of sulphur, which is known for its medicinal qualities and skin benefits – so be sure to take a good dip in it.
