You might want to use this **Binan Laguna day trips** guide to pick up some ideas for a nice excursion. From the famous water parks of Binan, Laguna, there are many sights and sounds in the surrounding areas which can capture the interest of this area.

**Muntinlupa City**

Although a mere 6.6 kilometers from Binan, Muntinlupa city is an interesting mix of agricultural land and city proper. If you&rsquo;re up for some shopping, check out the Alabang Town Center, Festival Supermall and Metropolis Mall. If some retail therapy really isn&rsquo;t considered therapy on your part, then check out more natural sights, such as Jamboree Lake, considered the smallest lake in the Philippines. Like so many towns and provinces in the Philippines, Muntinlupa takes part in many colorful festivals all-year long such as the Banhayan Festival in September and the Bulaklakan Festival in May. Several high-end country clubs, such as the Ayala Alabang Country Club and Palms Country Club, are also found here.

**Calamba City**

Calamba, Laguna is about 2 hours away from Binan. It&rsquo;s ideal for those who want to relax especially since it&rsquo;s considered as the hot spring capital of the Philippines. Some of the options are Palmridge Private Resort, C3Y Villa Private Hot Spring Resort, Villafranca Resort and Batu-Bato Mountain Resort. For those who want a historic perspective of the city, the country&rsquo;s National Hero, Jose Rizal was born here. His house and other childhood places are easily traced by asking the Tourism Board of the city. Other heroes from the Philippine Revolution which were born or raised in this city are Teodora Alonzo, Jose Paciano Rizal, Vicente Lim and Geronimo Aclan.
