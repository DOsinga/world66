---
title: "Sights in Binan Laguna"
type: section
---

There are so many **Binan Laguna sights** visitors can enjoy, from the cultural to the historical. The different sights in this city cater to all sorts of travelers, from those wanting to know more about how the locals live to those interested in learning the colorful past of Binan.

#### Casa Parroquial

More popularly known as The Convent, or Bahay Pari, this former church was first constructed in 1848 and was completed in 1851. A strong earthquake destroyed the building a year later, but it was immediately renovated. Before it was fully completed, The Convent was converted to a school by a group of nuns. When the nuns left, the building was left in ruins until it was restored to its former glory. Bad luck struck the building again when it was consumed by fire. Now, this centuries-old building is an interesting attraction because of its history as well as the fact that is now known as Cursillo House.

#### Kampanaryong Bato (Stone Steeple)

The first steeple built in Binan was made from pure stone. It was headed by Reverendo Padre Jose Simeos de Carreira in 1840. When the reverend passed away, the project to build this tall tower was abandoned until another priest headed the project. The tower was destroyed by an earthquake in 1870 and was rebuilt with wood instead. When the tall tower slowly deteriorated because of the materials used, the city decided to rebuild it using cement. This decades old tall tower is till existing and can be located in front of the Catholic Church.

#### Casa Real

Another popular historical landmark is the Casa Real, more popularly referred to by the locals as Hacienda House. This house used to be the residence of the Dominican priests during the Spanish occupation and was where taxes were hidden. This huge house is made from stone and hard wood. In 1942, Casa Real became the property of the city of Binan.

#### Meditation Garden

Next to the Roman Catholic Church is the Meditation Garden. The garden is a popular spot for both locals and visitors because of the 14 Stations of the Cross or the Via Crucis. The Meditation Garden is frequently visited, particularly during the Holy Week when many devotees visit the place for the Stations of the Cross.

#### Mount Makiling

Traveling by bus or jeepney, visitors can go to Mount Makiling in Los Banos. This mystical mountain is an inactive volcano that is a popular destination for mountain climbers. This mountain rises about 1,109 meters above sea level and is home to over 2,048 different species of plants. Aside from mountain climbing, Mount Makiling is also a popular spot for bird watching, trekking and butterfly watching.

#### Seven Lakes

Situated in San Pablo City is the Seven Lakes. These seven lakes are nestled in a depression created by volcanic activity long ago. The Seven Lakes are popular vacation spots among locals. Sampaloc Lake, which is the biggest among the seven, is a fishing farm for tilapia, a local freshwater carp.
