Binan is one of the three main cities in Laguna and there is important information visitors will find helpful in this **Binan Laguna travel guide**. The city of Bi&ntilde;an is located in the province of Laguna in the Republic of the Philippines. Over the years, it has become a residential suburban community as well as the largest industrial export processing zones in the country.

#### History

The city of Binan was founded in 1571 by Captain San Juan de Salcedo a month after Manila was established. The history behind the name Binan is a little vague but it was said that when Catholic priests were celebrating a Thanksgiving Mass after the city was discovered, curious natives gathered around and the priests baptized each of the natives which the natives referred to as &ldquo;binyagan.&rdquo; The priests were not able to pronounce the word properly and can only pronounce Binan, hence the name.

#### Geography

The city of Binan has a total area of 4,350

hectares which is 2.5 percent of the province of Laguna. It is approximately 40 kilometers south from the city of Manila. On its northern and eastern borders is Laguna de Bay, one of the biggest bays in the country.

Binan is generally a plain field where most of the 24 barangays are situated. Since most of the land area in Binan is level to nearly level, the city is a prime location for urban development.

#### Weather

Just like the entire country of the Philippines, Binan is dominated by 2 pronounced seasons – wet and dry. Visitors travelling to Binan from November to April can expect the dry season making it the best time to travel to this city. On the other hand, the rest of the months are considered the wet months.

During this time, travelers should expect some slight showers to heavy rain fall. However, Binan is generally protected by the mountains surrounding the region so visitors can expect some cooler climate.

#### Main Attractions of Binan

Most of the attractions in Binan are related to its history and discovery including the Casa Tribunal which was built in 1872. The house was made from stone and tiles but was destroyed by an earthquake in 1894. In 1921, it was developed to be part of the town&rsquo;s center and is now used as part of the town&rsquo;s plaza.

The Monument of Dr. Jose Rizal is another notable attraction in Binan. The monument in Binan is said to be the second biggest monument of the National Hero in the country, the biggest being in Luneta Park in Manila.
