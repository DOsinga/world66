---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Boracay"
type: section
---

The list of **Boracay Must Do’s** offers an exciting proposition across every age-group. Boracay Island isn't your typical tropical paradise. Unlike other, popular island destinations, it isn't overwhelmed by tourist guides and other commercial establishments. Despite hosting a large number of tourists every year, the island has retained its natural charm. Further, the island offers many escapades that require a bit of self-exploring. This makes Boracay ideal for people searching for an adrenaline rush without having to take upon too many risks. Among the vast number of vacationing options that Boracay offers, the following five Boracay must-do’s are simply inescapable:

**1. Boat Riding with "Flying Fish"**

This is the most unusual boat ride. For starters, it is just about 20 minutes in duration. Second, for most of the year, the charges for this ride are very high. Third, the name of this boat ride is misleading. Yes, the use of the term "Flying Fish" doesn’t refer to the scope of spotting any kind of fish! The term refers to a native method of referring to the hovercraft. Also known as air-cushion all-terrain vehicles, these hovercrafts are commonly used in some parts of the world, like Florida.

However, apart from Boracay, hovercrafts are rarely used in any other part of Asia. Further, the hovercrafts used here don’t present the usual, mechanical perfection that is associated with such specialized vehicles. Most of them are damaged-beyond-use in most parts and are moved along by an additional, motorized vehicle. The result is simply unbelievable—the smallest duration spent on this boat-ride makes for a truly memorable occasion. This is also the most extreme form of adventure sports that visitors can expect in Boracay.

**2. Snorkeling and Island-hopping at Crocodile Island**

Crocodile Island can be understood as the snorkeling capital of Philippines. Though there are many other spots along Philippines’ extensive coastline, none have been able to engage international attention like the Crocodile Island. The name stems from the fact that the entire island is in the shape of the upper half of a crocodile. However, most visitors cannot visualize this.

Thus, local tour guides carry aerial shots of the island that helps the tourist to understand the reptilian shape of this island. The island is frequented by
adventure-seekers, particularly from Australia and Japan. All kinds of
water-gear are available to rent. Another popular activity here is
island-hopping. This refers to renting a small boats, called "bancas,"
and visiting other smaller, uninhabited islands in the vicinity.

**3. Ariel's Point Trip**

Ariel’s Point has become the most unique trip on Boracay.
Guests are brought to a small cove a half hour from the White Beach. The boat
trip alone is already a refreshing experience seeing the beautiful tiny islands
around Boracay. Soon after you arrive at Ariel's Point, you are served a
sumptuous, fresh-cooked, barbecue, buffet lunch. Their grilled whole fish
stands-out in an all-around excellent meal. Unlimited drinks (beers, soda,
juices, rum, etc.) are also included during the whole trip. You can swim, snorkel
a bit around the area and see the beautiful marine life and jump or dive from
different points off the cliff - which is the highlight of the trip! And, of
course, enjoy the serenity and the unique beauty of the location, as well as
the breathtaking views of the ocean out on the horizon.

**4. Visiting Greenyard Center**

Greenyard Funboard Center is among the most sought-after family destinations in Boracay. This place is popular for its safely-conducted kite-surfing and wind-surfing events. The complex has a number of tour guides and professional experts to ensure that children are not exposed to any risk. Children can enroll for windsurfing lessons, too. For adults, there is a multitude of other activities including speed boat-racing and spot-diving.

**5. Kite-boarding at Habagat**

Kite-boarding and kite-surfing are endemic to south-east Asia and Habagat is regarded as the regional capital of these activities. This place is also referred to as the Bulabug Beach Kite-boarding Center. Visitors can enroll for short-term courses in kite-boarding and even earn a graded certificate. Visitors can taste the local delicacies at the Gaastra—the in-house restaurant. The Habagat Center is accessible from the Bulabog Beach and the Pacific Patio.

**6. Watching the Boracay Sunset **

The perfect way to end the day in Boracay is to spend a few minutes observing the beautiful sunset. It is widely believed that the sunset view from White Beach is perhaps the best on the island. The sunset also signals the opening of street-side stalls. Lights from the dancing bars and nearby clubs start to light-up the sky that has a dull shade of orange due to the setting sun. This is the perfect opportunity to take a picture and save it for memories.
