---
title: "Eating Out in Butuan"
type: section
---

Several fine **Butuan restaurants** dot this regional center in the CARAGA region of the Philippines. Butuan is bounded by the Agusan Valley, Agusan del Norte and the Butuan Bay. The place is well-endowed with forestland and swampland, thus there is much agricultural food. Aside from this, the province is endowed with two fishing grounds that fill the people&rsquo;s needs for fresh seafood. Here are some restaurants in Butuan.

**Weegols Grillhaus**

Weegol Grillhaus is located in Montilla Boulevard. It is proudly a native Filipino restaurant where instead of eating on plates, diners will be eating on banana leaf resting on a weave basket plate. Patrons will also be amazed and satisfied with local concoctions as a form of condiments. The specialty of the house is grilled chicken, locally known as Chicken Inasal, and the best way to eat chicken here is by bare hands. Other specialties include lumpia shanghai, calamari, seskabichi, kinilaw and pancit canton. The cost of a meal for a group of 3 people is just below $10.

**Jonies Flavored Chicken**

As its name implies, this restaurant only offers chicken products, fish and dessert. However, there are many chicken dishes to choose from. Aside from this, the chicken preparation meets the Halal standard; that is why this restaurant is a favorite dining place for Muslim customers. The must try dish is Jonies chicken, which costs $1.5 for a cup of rice and a slice of Jonies chicken. This Filipino restaurant is found inside Butuan&rsquo;s mall, Gaisano Mall.
