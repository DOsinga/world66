Guests can enjoy to the fullest their **Camiguin Island Day Trips**. Camiguin is an indescribable place for them. It is a small island with verdant forests, magnificent volcanoes, everlasting hot and cold natural springs, exotic aquatic life, fantastic mountains, and idyllic surroundings and lifestyles.

Camiguin Island is shaped like a pear volcanic island. It is called &ldquo;Born of Fire&rdquo; because it has seven volcanoes within the island. According to the Philippine Department of Tourism, Camigion belongs to the top 25 tourist destinations and is among the top 10 diving spots in the world.

**Historical Places**

Visitors can visit the historical places on the island which includes the Catarman Church Ruins, Sunken Cemetery, Old Sto. Rosario Church, Via Cruzes, Ancestral House, and Centennial House.

**Nature**

Camiguin is blessed with wonderful nature. The guests can take pleasure in going around the island to have a closer encounter of the falls, hot and cold springs, volcanoes, mountains, islands, lagoons, and trees.

**Beach Site**

Cabua-an Beach and Agohoy Beach are the two beaches in the island. Cabuaan Beach is the nearest beach in Mambajao proper. This is where the annual &ldquo;San Juan sa Hibok-Hibok Festival&rdquo; is held. Agohoy Beach is located in Barangay Agoho, which is 7 km away from Mambajao proper.

**Dive Site**

There are five main diving spots in the island. These are the Cabu-an Point, Jicdup Shoal, Burias Shoal, Old Volcano, and Sunken Cemetery. Cabu-an Point and Jicdup Shoal are two of the diving sites on the island that are ideal for underwater photographers and for beginner divers.
