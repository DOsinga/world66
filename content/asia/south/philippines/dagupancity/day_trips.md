**Dagupan City day trips** include visits to the locations which had a historical impact in the Philippine history. Just outside of Dagupan at Bonuan Blue Beach was an important landing spot during World War II.

#### **Gen. MacArthur&rsquo;s Refuge**

What is now an elementary school in Dagupan City was once the headquarters of General Douglas MacArthur during World War II. Gen. MacArthur. The General stayed in a house near the school. It is a historical place for the Filipino people, especially the people of Dagupan. If you visit on a weekday, you will be warmly welcomed by the District Supervisor of West Elementary School.

#### **MacArthur&rsquo;s Mark**

Bonuan Blue Beach is located in Dagupan City. This beach is where the Liberation Forces landed on the January 9, 1945. The Bonuan Blue Beach is now built with parks and the famous McArthur Landing Marker. This marker is a statue of Gen. Douglas MacArthur, the U.S. war general. The landing is also marked with a plaque.

Today, the famous restaurants in Dagupan are found in Bonuan Blue Beach. The highest government official of the Philippines and the other famous officers and celebrities head their way to Bonuan Blue Beach to enjoy a sumptuous meal and to taste the famous bangus. After you appreciate the historical significance of the area, you can enjoy a locally prepared meal.
