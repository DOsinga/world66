---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Dagupan City"
type: section
---

About a 6 hour drive from Manila, the City of Dagupan is small but it has a lot to offer to its visitors and many **Dagupan City must do&rsquo;s**. Bounded on the North by the Lingayen Gulf, this place is famous for its seafood and beautiful beaches. To enjoy your stay in the city, here are 5 things you must do while in Dagupan City. **
**

#### 1. Attend the &ldquo;Bangus&rdquo; Festival

The Bangus (milkfish) festival, which takes place during the last week of April, is one of the most important events in the city. Organized to help promote the &ldquo;bangus&rdquo; industry in the City, this festival attracts thousands of people year after year. Highlights of the festival include a street parade, trade exhibits, cultural shows and the food fair. If you want to experience the real Dagupan, you should come to this place during the festival and learn how to cook the &ldquo;bangus&rdquo; or simply eat the delicious seafood cooked by the local chefs.

#### 2. Have Fun in the Water

You do not need to spend a lot of money to enjoy the beautiful beaches in Dagupan. The Bonuan Blue Beach is a well maintained public beach where you can bask under the sun and mingle with the locals. If you do not like the crowded public beach, you can always go the Leisure Coast Resort just 10 minutes drive from the heart of the city. Nestled in the middle of lush vegetation, this place is the perfect getaway for people who want to relax and have fun with their families. If you have kids, they will surely love the three story water slides of the water park.

#### 3. Visit the Old Cathedral

The old St. John&rsquo;s Cathedral located in the heart of the city is considered as the oldest church in Dagupan. Although this old cathedral is not as big or as grand as the other cathedrals in Manila, this place has a great history attached to it. In the late 1800s, the Spanish General Caballos and his troupes surrendered to the Filipino army inside this church which signaled the end of the war between the Spanish colonizers and the natives.

#### 4. Navigate the Limahong Channel

This channel was created in the later part of the 1500s by a Chinese corsair known as Limahong, to escape the Spanish forces. After being locked down inside his fortress for almost 8 months, Limahong and his troupes were able to breakout by creating a channel from the Agno River to the Gulf of Lingayen.

#### 5. Sample the Food

You should never leave Dagupan City without sampling its local cuisine. To savor the delicious food of Dagupan, go to the Bonuan-Tondaligan area. The restaurants located at Galvan and Herrero-Perez Streets serve authentic Dagupan dishes.
