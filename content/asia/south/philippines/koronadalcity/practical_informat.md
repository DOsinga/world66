**Koronadal City practical information** will provide you with a better understanding of the way of life for the people in the area. Koronadal City, a 2nd class city in the Philippines, is the capital of South Cotabato province. It is also the center of Region XII. Through the Republic Act 8803 on October 8, 2000, Koronadal has become a part of the city of South Cotabato. The city of Koronadal has received several awards and distinctions in the Philippines. It was awarded the &ldquo;Most Competitive City&rdquo; in the small-city category in the Philippines in the year 2003 and 2005. The city also received an award in the year 2005 and 2006 as the &ldquo;Most Business Friendly City&rdquo; in Mindanao.

**Population**

The population of Koronadal has reached 157,870, according to the record of the National Statistics Office. More than forty-six percent of its population resides in the urban area, while over fifty percent dwell in the rural area. Koronadal City is composed of 27 barangays. G. P. Santos is the highest populated barangay in the urban area, while the Topland tops the most populated barangay in the rural part of Koronadal.
**
Currency**

The monetary unit that is used in Koronadal City is the Philippine peso. There are money changers in Koronadal. However, to make sure that you will have enough pesos to spend in the city, it is better if you are going to have your money exchanged at the airport before you reach Koronadal City. One US dollar is equivalent to 46 Php. Be reminded that the exchange rate varies sometimes. It will be of help to you to check for the exchange rate before having your money exchanged.

**Language Spoken**

Hiligaynon is the main dialect used by the people of Koronadal City. This is because the Ilonggos from the Visayas are the first dwellers of this place. Next to Hiligaynon, people also used the dialects of Cebuano and Ilocano in communicating with the local residents. The Ilonggo, Ilocano, and Cebuano dialects are some of the many dialects in the Philippines that are used by the people of Koronadal City to interact with their fellows. Aside from these regional dialects, the residents of Koronadal City also know how to speak Filipino, the National Language of the Philippines. Moreover, they can also communicate to foreigners by using the English language as a medium of interaction.

**Security and Safety**

Koronadal City is part of Mindanao, and some parts of Mindanao are not that safe for travel. However, to be sure that you will have safe travels, it is better if you are going to accompany a Filipino with you in your travel to Koronadal City.
