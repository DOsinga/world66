type: section
---

There are numerous things that you can include in your **Tacloban City things to do** list. Tacloban City is a port city that is not only famous for its scenic nature views, but also known for its rich history and culture. Here are some of the things that you really need to include in your to do list in Tacloban City.

**Visit the Historical Sites**

Tacloban has rich stories to tell regarding World War II. There are many sites in Tacloban City that involves World War II. For instance, you can go to the San Pedro Bay to see the Japanese Imperial Navy, the Red Beach which has the large statue of MacArthur depicting his infamous landing in the Philippines and the Redona Residence where Sergio Osmena Sr., one of the former presidents of the Philippines had taken refuge during World War II.

**Traverse the Longest Bridge **

Connecting the islands of Samar and Leyte, the San Juanico Bridge is the longest bridge in the Philippines and the pride of the Philippine nation. While you are traversing the bridge, do not forget to stop and take pictures as you can see the scenic view of both islands of Samar and Leyte.

**Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival**

Held on the 29th of June every year, the Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival is a colorful and fun-filled festival which showcases the ritual dances of Samar Island. As the name suggest, the word pintados means &ldquo;painted&rdquo; thus you will see most of the locals parading and showing off their tattoos which is a symbol of heroism in the old Filipino culture.

**Visit Kanhuraw Hill**

Nestled on Kanhuraw Hill is where you can see Tacloban&rsquo;s city hall. The hill overlooks the entire city and this is the point where you can see San Pedro Bay, Philippine-Japanese Memorial and other popular landmarks in Tacloban City. Moreover, the hill also boasts of beautiful landscaped gardens and concrete chairs where families and visitors can enjoy a picnic.

**Swimming in the Beach**

There are many beaches in Tacloban City that are open to the public. One of them is Red Beach, this is where General MacArthur landed. This particular beach comes complete with a secured beach front, swimming pool, restaurant, kiosks and water sports facilities. The other public beaches, although devoid of pools and other amenities, are also a great venue for swimming as they are free and have modest cottages and kiosk stands.
