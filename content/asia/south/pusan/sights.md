---
title: "Sights in Pusan"
type: section
---

Pusan sights include everything from beaches to museums to national landmarks. Here are some of the** Pusan sights** that you can fit into your itinerary when traveling to this seaside city.

**Beaches**

Being a city on the coast, Pusan sights naturally include some beautiful beaches. The most popular beach in Korea is found in Pusan: Haeundae Beach. It becomes packed with people every summer, but the views are fantastic. Gwangalli Beach is also a lovely white sand beach, with plenty of restaurants that specialize in sashimi and sushi. Songjeong Beach is less crowded than the other two, and if you want even more seclusion, you can head to Dadaepo Beach.

**Museums**

Busan Museum is filled with the history and archaeology of Pusan and its surrounding areas. A big draw of the museum is the touchable exhibit, where you can get hands-on with the artifacts. Dongsam-dong Shell Midden Museum is also centered around archaeology, but specifically details the Neolithic Period. Bokcheon Museum, on the other hand, displays the Gaya Kingdom as shown through the artifacts found in the Bokcheon tombs. Delve into more recent times with a visit to the Modern History Museum. If you prefer to explore the art of Pusan and the east, why not visit the Busan Museum of Art or the Busan Museum of Modern Art? Other museums include the Korean Tea Museum, Fisheries Science Museum and Busan Marine Natural History Museum.

**Landmarks**

No matter where you are in South Korea, temples are a big draw for visitors. Pusan sights include two incredible temples. Beomeosa Temple is built on the rocky shoreline, giving it fantastic sea views. Samgwangsu Temple was constructed at the foot of BaekYang Mountain and is surrounded by a beautiful forest. There are plenty of other sights to see as well. Geumjeong Sanseong Mountain Fortress was built in medieval times, and was the largest fort in Korea. Today you can hike up the surrounding mountains (or take the cable car for a quicker trip). Visit Taejongdae Resort Park to see the rock formations and hear the legends that go with them. Yeongdo Bridge is not just the connection between Pusan and Yeongdo Island, it was also the meeting point for refugees during the Korean War and has a history that stretches back to Japanese occupation of Korea. Finally, you'll want to make a stop at Busan Tower. The tower itself isn't much to see, but it still offers panoramic views of Pusan, a sight you won't want to miss.
