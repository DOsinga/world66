Below is a brief **Colombo tr****ansportation** guide that lists out the most convenient ways to explore the city. You can move around in Colombo either by tri-Shaw, taxi or a bus. Each of these has their own unique advantage.

**By Tri-Shaw

**Tri-Shaws or tuk-tuks, as they are commonly known, are the preferred mode of transport in Colombo. Negotiate the fares with the driver, as these tri-shaws do not have installed meters. Longer trips spanning a distance of 8 km will cost you around Rs.250, while shorter trips will cost Rs. 50 for a distance of 1 to 2 miles. You can also take the tuk-tuk, which is operated by the Sri Lankan Tourist Development Authority. These tuk-tuks are metered, and tourists can know the exact fares.

**By Taxi

**Taxis are another convenient mode of transportation in Colombo. The taxi fares are reasonable, and the meters start at Rs. 40. You will be charged around Rs. 120 per mile. Taxi fares rarely go beyond Rs. 500 while traveling in Colombo. Traveling in a taxi is a much safer and convenient option than the tuk-tuks. Taxis are also equipped with air-conditioners. Do not commence the journey before deciding the fare.

**By Bus

**Traveling by bus is a viable option for budget conscious tourists. Bus stations are situated in southern Pettah. Buses are available for all the major destinations in Colombo. Private bus services are also available to Jaffna, while tourist buses run from the Central Station from Colombo. Tourists can get information on bus timings at the bus stations. However, there is a possibility that the buses might get delayed, as late departures are the norm.
