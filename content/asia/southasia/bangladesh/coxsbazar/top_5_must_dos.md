---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Cox's bazar"
type: section
---

Cox's Bazar is located in South Eastern Bangladesh. It is also known as Panowa, which means "yellow flower." Cox's Bazar is famous for it's magnificent beaches; however, there are many other attractions in and around Cox's Bazar. Cox's Bazar is attracting more tourists every year from regions of India as well as international tourists. The variety of accommodation options and resorts offer the perfect base to explore the surrounding attractions and beaches.**
**

**Aggmeda Khyang**

Agghmeda Kehyanh is a magnificent Buddhist monastery visited by
thousands of Buddhists from the surrounding areas. There is a prayer
chamber, assembly hall, Buddha images and manuscripts. The main chamber is supported by timber
columns. The Aggmeda
Khyang is located by the entrance to the town. The majority of the Buddha
images originate from Burma. Aggmeda Khyang is at the bottom of a hill and is
nestled in between trees.

**Ramu**

Just 10 kilometers outside Cox's Bazar is a village called Ramu. The
population is predominantly Buddhist and there are many monasteries and
pagodas. In the village you can see many handicrafts and sample the village's
famous cigars. Weavers display their work outside their workshops and
people make cigars in their houses. There is a fascinating temple on the
Baghkhali river that has Burmese crafts and a huge statue of Buddha.
Woodcarving is another specialty of the village. Ramu can be reached
by a 30 minute bus ride. Other transport options are taxi and
minivan.

**Dulhazra Safari Park**

The Safari Park is part of an animal sanctuary and is home to many
native elephants. Visitors can also go for elephant rides. Other
animals include lions, crocodiles and Bengal tigers. The park is host
to a variety of bird and monkey species

**Beaches**

The beach at Cox's Bazar is breath taking as the sandy beach
stretches for 120 km. While sitting on the golden sand you can
enjoy the expanse of ocean in front of you while behind you towers
hills and forests. This is the perfect backdrop to watch the sunset.
Conditions are often suitable for surfing. The water is warm and therefore it is pleasant to go swimming.

**Moheshkhali**

This island is located off the coast of Cox's Bazar. The island is
268 square kilometers. It takes 15 minutes to reach by speed boat and
30 minutes to reach by trawler. Boats leave regularly. The island has
beautiful hills that are covered in forests. There are temples and
Buddhist Pagodas to visit on the island as well as the shrine of Adinath. The fishing villages are good places to see how local fishermen live.
