title: "Getting Around in Bhadrachalam"
type: section
---

If you are vacationing in** Bhadrachalam getting around** shouldn't be a big problem. Bhadrachalam
is a tiny town in southern India that is centered around the temple
dedicated to the Hindu god Lord Ram. Because it is such a small town,
you can frequently walk to places you need to go. The other main
sources of transportation are t3 wheeled taxis, which resemble a
covered go-cart. If you want to visit some of the towns or tourist
attractions outside of Bhadrachalam, there is a good system of trains and buses that connect the area.

**Hopping a Ride on a Rickshaw**

If
you are going to catch a ride with the 3 wheeled taxis, also known as
auto rickshaws, the most important thing to remember is that they don't
carry meters. Make sure you clearly agree on the price before you get
in. Prices vary by the distance you are going to travel, and can vary
widely. If you don't mind doing a little bargaining, however, these
little taxis can be a convenient way to get around.

**On the River** If you want to see the nearby Papikondalu
Hills, a beautiful tourist attraction, leave land behind and travel on
one of the steam boats. Rides on the steam boats typically include your
meals, and the tour guides will help you to understand the significance of what you are seeing. In addition to the beautiful scenery, the Papikondalu hills contain a small temple to Shiva which is worth a visit.

**Bhadrachalam**

Bhadrachalam is one of the most mystical places in India. Most of
India’s history mingles with religion. Most of the important cities or places
were built around places of worship, like temples, and those places bear
the names of important shrines, saints and Hindu
deities. Bhadrachalam is one of the places that is full of mysticism. This
place is geographically located on the banks of the Godavari River of
India. This site is a prime destination for those people that want to
embark on a journey and to see the glory and the architecture of
India’s earliest inhabitants.

**How to Reach Bhadrachalam**

The most important place is Bhadrachalam, the holy site that is
believed to be the holy home of Lord Rama. This makes this place one of
the most significant destinations in India. Although the place is hard to
reach, you can find many inexpensive ways to visit Bhadrachalam. You can use a boat or a ferry to
reach this pilgrimage site. You can also reach this place traveling by
train. There is a train route, the Machilipatnam / Manuguru Express, and the
number of this train is 7050. You can also take different bus routes to
travel to Bhadrachalam.

When traveling by road you can use one of the many buses provided by
the tourism department. The good thing about these buses is that they will not stop along the way. They make a direct nonstop trip to
the pilgrimage site. However, you have to be ready for a long 7 hour
ride.

**When to Visit Bhadrachalam**

During the months of October and November there are many different
celebrations when pilgrims take a dip in the cold waters of the
Godavari River. The celebrations are made mainly in the temple’s
grounds and many people flock for these holidays. There are many
different accommodations that the temple and the government offer to pilgrims and travelers alike.

**Where to Stay When Visiting**

There are many places where travelers could find lodging that is
virtually inexpensive. It should be noted that it is best to visit this
location during the week. There are many people visiting during the
weekends, and it is virtually impossible to find lodging during the
weekends. The prices of the lodgings vary from RP$20 to RP$200. Of
course the price of the room varies according to the style and the
commodity of the place.
