---
title: "Chitra-Vichitra Fair"
type: location
---

This fair is held in the village Gunbhakhari in the Sabarkantha District, nearby the border of Rajasthan. The Chitra-Vichitra fair is held once every year and an exciting fair not to be missed. The name of this fair is taken from Chitravirya and Vichitravirya, the two sons of king Shantanu, who are believed to have lived in the village where they were cured from their diseases.

  The fair attracts a maximum number of Adivasis and people from the communities of Bhills and Garasias. The Adivasis are dressed colourfully and the women appear, from head to toe, in traditional costumes. Every group that comes here brings its own drums. The women members of the groups sing folk songs and the other members dance until they nearly drop dead.

  There are plenty of food and handicrafts stalls for the visitor to be found. The expected fair date is the 2nd of March in 2001 and the 11th of April in 2002.
