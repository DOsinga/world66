---
title: "Dangs Darbar"
type: location
---

Adjoining the border of Maharashtra and located in the Saputara Hills, lies one of the most beautiful and delightful areas of Gujarat, better known as ‘the Dangs’. With its cool and pleasant climate and surrounded by thick and dense forests of ‘Sahyadris’, it is the hometown of the tribal Adivasi people.

  The fair, which is called Dangs Darbar, is held at a place known as Ahwa, situated 2000 feet above sea level. It takes place every year, a few days before ‘Holi’. It attracts many tribal people, both from within the town as well as from the surrounding hills. They gather in Ahwa, dressed colourfully, and beat the drums and play the shehnai.  

The main attraction is the folk dancing performed by the local tribal people. Besides folk dancing, the Government of the Gujarat Education Department organises, together with the District Collector, the performances of garbas, songs and dramas. All together, they turn this fair into a lively event not to be missed. The assumed dates for this fair are from the 9th until the 13th of March 2001 and from the 28th of March until the 1st of April 2002.
