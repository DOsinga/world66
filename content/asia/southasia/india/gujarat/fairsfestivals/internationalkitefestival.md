The International Kite Festival, which coincides with the Festival of Uttarayan, is held at Ahmedabad in January and lasts for three days. The Kite Festivals take place in the rest of Gujarat and India too. Ahmedabad, however, is especially selected for the International Kite Festival.

  It is enjoyed by young and old and it makes the people go mad -literally. They have to close their businesses for at least three to four days after the last day of the festival. You will find all sorts of people, who dwell in Gujarat, enjoying the spectacle from the ‘agasis’, i.e. terraces of buildings. Accompanied by music and traditional food, such as Laddu, sugarcane and Bor, this is a very unique and great experience to watch.

  The international kite flyers and the local champions bring along kites in shapes and sizes one cannot even think about. It is totally amazing. Sometimes, the kite flyer needs help from three or four persons to fly the kite. The foreign kite flyers are eagerly awaiting these festival days in order to show off their talents. 

The Gujarat Tourist Office organises the International Kite Festival and the place of venue is the Sardar Patel Stadium or the Police Stadium. The dates are fixed on the 13th, 14th and 15th of January 2001 and on the 13th, 14th and 15th of January 2002.
