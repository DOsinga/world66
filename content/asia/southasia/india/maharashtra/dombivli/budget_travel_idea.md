**Traveling on a Budget in Dombivli**

You might want to use this **Dombivli budget travel** guide to
pick up a few budget travel tips while traveling. In Dombivli, most of
the attractions are for public access and are free. The shopping,
however, is pricey, so make sure to put a strict budget to anything.
Otherwise, it is an easily survivable trip for your finances.
**Walk, Walk, Walk!**

Dombivli is blessed with different forms of transportation. The train
can take you to daytrip destinations like Thane or Mumbai, and the
buses and rickshaws are also accessible for in-city destinations. But
the best and most affordable way to get around is by walking. If the
destination is a reachable area by foot, get a little exercise in the
process. Buses also have fixed routes, so this may not be much help when
going around the different destinations.
**Affordable Shopping Destinations**

Kelkar Road is probably the most popular destination for shopping. It
has high-end shops that range from moderate to the expensive. So when
you’re saving a buck or two, skip Kelkar Road as well as Dadai and
Mumbai for the same reason. Try to go to more affordable shopping areas
for saris and authentic Indian clothes. The malls, Citi Mall and Empress
Mall, have department store brands that are cheaper alternatives to the
pricier Kelkar Road items.
