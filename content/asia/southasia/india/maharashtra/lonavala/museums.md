---
title: "Museums in Lonavala"
type: section
---

Visiting **Lonavala museums** and
historical sites is commonly suggested to tourists for understanding the
history of Maharashtra. Besides the usually-visited museums, visitors
should also visit the slightly eccentric Lonavala museums. One such
museum is the Celebrity Wax Museum, which is the first of its kind in
India.

**What the Celebrity Wax Museum** **Offers**

Also called the Wax Museum, this is among the most contemporary and
perhaps the most unique of Lonavala Museums. Located in Varsoli, in
Lonavala near the Toll Gate that oversees Pune, this is also India’s
first wax museum. The museum opened in 2005. It is largely perceived as a
rip-off of the Madam Tussaud Museum in London, and yes, the museum
doesn’t deny the unmistakable similarities.

The museum’s collection is now growing with every passing week, with
waxed impressions of Indian and international celebrities being
regularly added. The most popular wax statues are those of Rajiv Gandhi,
Michael Jackson and Adolf Hitler. The current collection seems to be
more inclined towards stars from the Indian film industry (Bollywood).

**Reaching Celebrity Wax Museum**

The Wax Museum is located just about 2 km from the main railway
junction of Lonavala. Visitors can simply walk to the museum or get
aboard any of the rickshaws for a slightly discomforting but very cheap
ride.

**Timings**

The museum usually remains open on all days between 8.30 a.m. and 9.30 p.m., except on national holidays.

**Tickets**

The tickets are nominally priced across various age-groups, though there are no discounts for families or bigger groups.
