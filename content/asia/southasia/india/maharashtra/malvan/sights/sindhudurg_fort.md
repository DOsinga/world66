Constructed by Shivaji in 1664, at a site personally selected by him.
The construction of a sea fort is a stupendous task, and at Sindhudurg
no efforts were spared. Over 2000 khandis (4000 mounds) of iron were
used for casting and the foundation stones were laid down firmly in
lead.

Even today, as one approaches the fort past a rocky
reef, navigable through a narrow channel, one marvels at the
transportation of such heavy material through such choppy waters.
Within its precincts are temples holding the shrines of Maruti,
Bhavani, Mahadeo, Jarimai, Mahapurush and also of Shivaji -- the only
such shrine in the country.
