Mumbai is famous for its festivals like Ganapati Utsav and Dahihandi. The Ganapati Festival was started by *Lokamanya Tilak *to spread awareness among the people about freedom struggle. Mumbai has a number of local Ganapati Mandals who celebrate this 10-day festival in great devotion and enthusiasm. Huge Ganapati idols are worshipped during this festival. On the 10th day, Ganapati idols are immersed in the sea water. The immersion procession is one of the most happening occasions in Mumbai. The processions come from the various parts of the city and head to the nearby beach to immerse the idols. The processions are escorted by the enthusiastic Mandal activists and traditional band experts. The participants perform several cultural programmes. They dance to the tune of the music. They throw the pink-colored powder gulal in the air.

Dahihandi is the celebration of the festival called Gokulashtami. The festival is celebrated to mark Lord Krishna's birth day. Since Lord Krishna, according to the mythology, used to break the earthern pots and steal butter from them, the people who participate in Dahihandi break the earthern pots kept hanging at a height of around 50-100 feet and win the handsome amount of money as the prize.
