---
title: "Getting Around in Jaipur"
type: section
---

India is a large and busy, vibrant country, and there are hundreds
of unique cities and sites to explore throughout the area; if you find
yourself in Rajasthan, one thing that you'll likely need to consider is
**Jaipur transportation**. Jaipur is one of the more popular tourist
destinations in Rajasthan, and it's helpful to decide how you plan to
get around in this area before you arrive so that you maximize the
amount of time that you'll have for sightseeing.

**Rickshaws**

One of the most traditional and common ways for tourists to get
around in the Rajasthan province is by rickshaw. These carts, pulled by
Indian men on bicycles or on wheels themselves, are inexpensive and a
wonderful way to view the city and the sites around you.

**Cars**

Cars are few and far between in Jaipur, and it makes very little
sense for a traveler to rent one. They tend to be overly expensive and
inefficient, not to mention that they are oftentimes in poor condition,
and it can be very difficult to find gas stations in the area.

**Bicycles**

Bicycles are also occasionally seen on the streets of Jaipur. These
are decent modes of transportation, but they can be inefficient during
busy times of day or on busy days of the week.

**On Foot**

One of the best ways to see Jaipur is simply by walking. You'll have
plenty of chances to view different attractions, restaurants and other
places of interest along the way.

If you have any other questions, speak with a representative from your hotel for more information.
