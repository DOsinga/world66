---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Rameshwaram"
type: section
---

This **Rameshwaram must do’s** list is
filled with the temples and pilgrimages the city is known for. This
city, situated in Tamil Nadu in India, is considered one of the holiest
places for Hindus in the country.
1. Visit the Ramanathaswamy Temple

This 17th century temple is one of the most visited temples in India.
This temple is devoted to the Hindu god Shiva, and is one of the
holiest Char Dham shrines in Hindu. It is said that all Hindus must
visit the Ramanathaswamy Temple at least once in their lifetimes.

The temple is surrounded by an 865 foot wall from east to west, and a
657 foot furlong from north to south. Upon entering the temple,
visitors are greeted by a striking corridor between huge colonnades on
top of platforms at least 5 feet high.

Inside the Ramanathaswamy Temple are series of huge, magnificent
corridors and shrines including the Chokkattan Madapam, which is a
structure in the form of a chess board.
2. See the Floating Stone Bridge

The mystery behind the Floating Stone Bridge has brought many
visitors, local and tourists alike, to the city of Rameshwaram. Aside
from this, the place is another holy destination for Hindus in India,
since it is said that God Ram built the bridge himself.
3. Explore the Hanuman Temple

The Hanuman Temple is one of the most popular temples in the city.
This five-faceted temple features the five profile faces of Hanuman. The
statues of Hanuman in the temple are made from wood and are said to
move like puppets. The statues also depict the incidence of Ramayana. As
it is dedicated to the most renowned deity in India, the Hanuman Temple
receives many visitors each year.
4. Travel to Agnitheertham

Agnitheertham is another religious destination that is near the
Ramanathaswamy Temple. This body of water is also considered a holy
place in India, and many tourists flock the beach to cleanse themselves
here. Prior to entering the Ramanathaswamy Temple, Hindus usually take a
dip in the Agnitheertham to wash away their sins.

According to Indian epic, Agnitheertham is where Lord Rama went to
worship Shiva to absolve himself from the sin of killing a Brahman.
5. Discover the Gandhamadana Parvatham

Located just 3 kilometers from Ramanathaswamy Temple is the
Gandhamadana Parvatham. On top of this hill are two mandapam, wherein
one can find footprints said to be from Rama. This two-storey temple is
dedicated solely for the holy footprints of Rama which can be found on
the ground floor. On the second floor, visitors have a view of the
Ramanathaswamy Temple and the northern part of Sri Lanka.
