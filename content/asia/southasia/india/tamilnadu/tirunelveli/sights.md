---
title: "Sights in Tirunelveli"
type: section
---

Nestled amidst Cardamom Hills, **Tirunelveli Sights** present a
completely relaxed holiday retreat. There are pristine green woods, palm
and coconut groves and historic temples. Looking back to the past,
Tirunelveli was once the kingdom of Pandyas. Though not too popular, it
is a beautiful holiday spot in Tamil Nadu. Tirunelveli** **has
several breathtaking sights** **that visitors will enjoy exploring.
Discover the meadows dotted with flowers and trees. There is no dearth
of hotels and you can get plenty of cheap and affordable accommodations
here. Shopping in Tirunelveli can be a pleasant experience. You can buy
palm leaf handicrafts, seashells and trinkets as souvenirs here.

**Major Architectural Attractions**

The Nalli Temple is famous for its gigantic gopurams. The temple has
long corridors and minutely crafted pillars. These pillars are specimens
of great architectural work. Every day, thousands of pilgrims come to
visit. Another worth visiting place is Maharaja Temple. Devotees come to
this shrine to spend some time in serenity. You can hear birds chirping
and enjoy the sweet fragrance of flowers along the shrine.

**Forest Reserve **

The Kalikad Tiger Reserve is home to tigers and elephants. Wild dogs,
Nilgiri languor, panther, lion-tailed monkey, leopard, bonnet macaque,
Sambar, snakes, chital, sloth bear and tuskers can also be spotted. The
Kunthakulam Bird Sanctuary is located in Nanguneri Taluk. It is 33 km
away from Tirunelveli. Those who love bird watching must visit this
reserve, as more than 10,000 migratory birds come here from different
countries like Australia, Pakistan and Sri Lanka. Another renowned bird
sanctuary is located in Ariyakulam.

**Museum**

The Tirunelveli State Museum exhibits artifacts, coins, war
equipment, bronze items, potteries made by celebrated artisans and
statues made up of stones and wood. The Regional Science Centre is also
worth a visit. If you have children accompanying you, show them the
uniquely designed telescope. In the adjoining garden, you can find
guinea pigs and rabbits roaming about playfully.

**Waterfall**

The Coutrallam Falls must be visited, as the water has curative
properties. Coutrallam has emerged as a health resort. Tourists enjoy
swimming and boat rides here. Other famous falls include Manimathther
Falls and Papanasam Falls. These cascading waterfalls will leave you
awestruck for a moment. Papanasam Falls is situated amidst dense
forests.

**Temples**

The Sankara Narayanan Temple in Sankarankovil is worth visiting.
Tourists from around the world flock to this temple to watch Car
festival in June and July. Pilgrims offer prayers to Lord Muruga at
Thirumalai during their visit to Tirunelveli. This temple is nestled on a
hillock at Panpozhil.
