---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Tirunelveli"
type: section
---

Tourists should check out the list of **Tirunelveli Must Do's**
before planning vacation to this place. These exciting activities
include shopping, boating, fishing, canoeing and parasailing. You can go
for a trekking expedition at the hilly areas, too. Check out the local
shopping areas and handicraft emporiums for buying souvenirs such as
jewelry boxes, wood carved handicrafts and so on. Taste the Tirunelveli
and special South India cuisines like idli, masala dosa and uppma. Don't
miss sipping the fresh coconut water. Tirunelveli has two theaters as
well; so you can watch movies and dance performances here in the
evening.

**Pristine Beaches**

Located in the state of Tamil Nadu, this beautiful destination has
tranquil beaches. During your trip you can experience boating and enjoy
the scenic ride while rowing. You can find tourists strolling along the
beach. Another thrilling water sport you can try is canoeing. Fishing at
the pristine beaches can be even more interesting. You can soothe your
mind and soul with an Ayurvedic massage. If you love nature, you must
not miss the panoramic coastline encircled with coconut and palm trees.
You will be mesmerized by this spectacular scenery.

**Courtallam**

This must-visit site is located 60 km away from Tirunelveli. This
waterfall is very popular and the water holds healing properties.
Tourists from all parts of the world visit this picnic spot. The
cascading waterfall's water is used in curing physical ailments. It has
emerged as a health resort and is comprised of 9 waterfalls. You can
also see old mural paintings of gods and goddesses in the Chithra Sabha
in Courtallam. Make a visit to Lord Shiva temple here.

**Papanasam**

This is a Lord Shiva temple. The Chitrai Vishu festival is organized
here in April. Another festival called Adi Ammavasai draws a large
number of pilgrims from far off places. You must visit this place while
you are in Tirunelveli.

**Tiruchendur**

Spending a few moments in this shore temple can give you peace. This
is a Lord Subramanya temple and according to the myths, Lord Subramanya
in this very place worshiped Lord Shiva, his father, after killing demon
Surapadma. If you are planning your trip in November, don't miss the
Skandasashti festival.

**Manappad**

Located on the shore and 71 km from Tirunelveli, the Holy Cross
Church is worth visiting here. You can also find remains of the
purported Real Cross, which is said to have been brought from Jerusalem.
