---
title: "Shopping in Srinagar (Pauri Garhwal)"
type: section
---

The summer capital of the state of Jammu and
Kashmir in northern India is famous for beautiful scenery and cheap
buys, and here are some **Srinagar shopping** areas.

**Lal Chowk**

The Lal Chowk and the Polo View in Srinagar is the best place for art
lovers to go. Here, you can find Indian art at its finest, with some of
the youngest and most promising artists showcased in these galleries.
Indian art, in itself, is very different from a lot of the mainstream
art in the world today. Kashmiri art is more distinguished that many
other art forms, shaped by the ongoing conflict that remains in the
background of Kashmir today. Browse through the traditional Kashmiri
paintings that are not so different from the ones found back in the
olden days, and admire the artistry and skill of the painters, sculptors
or wood carvers as they capture a piece of Kashmiri culture. Those
looking for something a little more modern will not be left out here –
the new wave of art has caught on in Srinagar, and many of the pieces
showcase the creativity and vibrancy of the young generation with hope
in their hearts for a good future.

**Gulmarg**

The famous ski resort and skiing area is popular among tourists for
its rolling white slopes and the view of the majestic Mount Everest in
the background during the winter season. Shopping is not left out in
such a highly tourist populated area, and this is the cheapest place to
pick up souvenirs to bring home. Postcards and key rings are popular and
common, but those looking for something a little more exotic can bring
home little handicrafts – particularly those with papier mache that the
Kashmiri people are so proud of. Woven bracelets and bookmarks are
popular among the ladies, and can be purchased at the gift shop located
at the Gulmarg.

**Markets **

Markets are everywhere in Srinagar, particular during the warmer days
of winter. With fresh produce and color everywhere, the markets are not
to be missed by any visitor to this region. Take in the sights and
smells of an outdoor market – stalls lined alongside a muddy road, with
people trudging across the area looking for a bargain. The markets are
the best places to taste some of the Kashmiri food, and the sweets here
come very highly recommended. The water markets are good places for
tourists to venture, with hawkers floating around the river in their
little boats. Bargaining is encouraged here, and the price of goods can
go down to as low as 10 percent of the asked price.
