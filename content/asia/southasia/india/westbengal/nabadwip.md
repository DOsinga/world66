**Nabadwip** (নবদ্বীপ) (also Navadwip, Navadvipa or Nabadwipdham) is a city and a municipality in Nadia district in the Indian state of West Bengal. Its name means "9 islands" in the Bengali language. The islands are named Antardwip (Sri Mayapur), Simantadwip, Rudradwip, Madhyadwip, Godrumdwip, Ritudwip, Jahnudwip, Modadrumdwip, and Koladwip. It is a place of many lilas (or "pastimes of God" in Hinduism) of Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu. Many pilgrims from origins worldwide adhering to Gaudiya Vaishnavism come to Navadwip every year on pilgrimage and for festivals like Sri Navadvipa-mandala Parikrama and holidays like Gaura Purnima.[[1]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-0)

History

Nabadwip was the capital of Ballal Sen and later Lakshman Sen, the famous rulers of the Sena Empire, who ruled from 1159 to 1206. [[2]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-1) In 1202, Nabadwip was captured by Bakhtiyar Khilji. This victory paved the way for Muslim rule in Bengal.[[3]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-2) Nabadwip and Nadia were great centres of learning and intellectual prowess. For five centuries, it was referred to as “Oxford of Bengal”. [[4]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-3) Chaitanyadeb (1486-1533) was born and spent many years at Nabadwip. Geography

Nabadwip is located at 23°25′N 88°22′E﻿ / ﻿23.42°N 88.37°E﻿ / 23.42; 88.37[[7]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-6). It has an average elevation of 14 m (46 ft). It is located at the confluence of Bhagirathi and Jalangi rivers.[[8]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-7) The Bhagirathi has shifted course. Earlier, Nabadwip was on its east bank with the rest of Nadia district, but now it is on its west bank. There also is a dispute regarding Sri Chaitnaya’s birthplace.[[9]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-8)
Demographics

As of 2001[update] India census[[10]](/asia/southasia/india/westbengal#cite_note-9), Nabadwip had a population of 115,036. Males constitute 51% of the population and females 49%. Nabadwip has an average literacy rate of 75%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 80%, and female literacy is 70%. In Nabadwip, 9% of the population is under 6 years of age.
