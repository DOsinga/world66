---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Bandar Seri Begawan"
type: section
---

Bandar Seri Begawan, the capital of Brunei in Southeast Asia offers many interesting places for the entire family to enjoy. There are beaches, places of historical interest, and a water village as well as an amusement park for the children. One can also take in the natural parks of the region.
Kampong Ayer
Kampong Ayer is the water village near Brunei. It is best to take a water taxi to this place. It offers a beautiful sight as one can see hundreds of houses that appear to float on water. The people of the water village are very friendly and many of them open their homes to visitors all year around.
Royal Regalia
This museum houses the best gifts received by the sultan. It is near the city center and is definitely worth the visit. The children too will enjoy seeing the miniature figurines and toys received by the sultan.
The Royal Ceremonial Hall or Lapau
Situated in Jalan Kianggeh, the Lapau (Royal Ceremonial Hall) is used for traditional royal ceremonies. The interior of the Lapau and the Sultan's throne are decorated in exquisite gold. You will also find the Dewan Majlis here. This is the place where the legislative assembly used to sit. One requires official permission to enter this building.
Jerudong Playground
Jerudong Playground is built on the lines of Disneyworld. Earlier the entry was free however they now charge an admission fee. The amusement park is located in a suburb and you will have to go there by taxi. The children are sure to enjoy some inventive theme rides, small toy shops and an array of stalls selling chocolates and candy floss.
Taman Peranginan Tasek
Take your children for a picnic lunch here. One can also walk and jog along the trails and parks. There is a waterfall and reservoir that is especially good during the monsoon season. One can swim in the reservoir, but women and girls must wear a t-shirt and shorts.
If your children are older, say teenagers, they will also enjoy a trip to Bangar to see the rain forest. A visit to the tropical rain forests of Southeast Asia will be enjoyable for the entire family. There are some easy treks that one can take and see the flora and fauna of the place.
Ulu Temburong National Park
This should be on the must visit list of all families that visit Brunei. You get a magnificent view of the rain forests and can see some of the most unique monkeys of this region.
All in all, try to include a bit of everything – a museum, a village, an amusement park, and some nature trails so that the children will not be bored by repetitive sights.
