Cambodia, and Phnom Penh and Siem Reap in particular, is home to a huge
range of restaurants geared to teh English-speaking visitor. The
capital, Phnom Penh, for example has well over 200 cafes, restaurants
and bistros, not to mention literally hundreds of local restaurants and
foodstalls.

Anyone visiting Phnom Penh or Siem Reap should pick up a copy of the
Eating & Drinking guides published by Cambodia Pocket Guide, for
really up-to-date information on the cities' food and drink scenes.
Articles, detailed directory listings, maps and advice will help
navigate through teh many options that each destination has to offer.

The guides are available free at over 250 locations, and all the content can be viewed online at www.cambodiapocketguide.com

### [Restaurant frizz

Restaurant frizz (yes, no capital letter) at Phnom Penh's riverside
is a good location to try and taste Cambodian food. Their objective is
to spread the knowledge and appreciation of traditionally prepared
Khmer food. Many people think it's inferior to Thai food, frizz is
trying to proof they are wrong. The restaurant is nicknamed 'The home
of the Volcano Pot', which is a kind of BBQ Cambodian-style. Other
specialties include Amok (fish curry), Saich Moan Ang (charcoal roasted
chicken) and Luk Lak (beef in Khmer sauce).
