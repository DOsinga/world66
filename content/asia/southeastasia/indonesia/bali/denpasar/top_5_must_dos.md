---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Denpasar"
type: section
---

This list of **Denpasar Must Do's**
provides an exciting range of options. Being the largest city of Bali as
well as its administrative capital, Denpasar is a typical metropolis.
It is endowed with many historic buildings, and is surrounded by some
beautiful exotic locations. Everyone who visits Bali should pursue the
following as a part of their Denpasar Must Do's list:

**1. Cycling, Watersports at Sanur**

Sanur is small, coastal town located along the southern end of Bali.
Sanur is seriously recommended to those who like clean, tranquil
beaches, and it is is just about an hour away from Denpasar via taxi.
Sanur is one of the few places in Indonesia that provide well maintained
cycle paths. This five-kilometer cycling zone around the beachfront is
located near the Jalan Mertasari area of Sanur. The Sanur beach is lined
with tents of instructors who can familiarize visitors with
windsurfing, kite-surfing, paragliding and wake-boarding.

**2. Partying at Seminyak & Elysian**

Just a few minutes from the main Denpasar commercial zone, Seminyak
is a globally recognized site of fashionable residences. Though gaining
entry in most of the private clubs here is difficult, visitors can look
forward to some chic partying options. Near Seminyak is Elysian—regarded
as one of the most upmarket area in southern Bali. Visitors can
recuperate from the partying at the all-day spas of Elysian.

**3. Visiting Ulun Danu**

Lake Beratan boasts the iconic Ulun Danu temple. It is a sacred place
for the natives, since it is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Dewi Danu.
This sacred spot attracts Hindu worshipers from across Asia. Visitors
can look forth to something special if they visit Danu around the New
Year period. This is when the native Hindu festival of Nyepi is
celebrated. The lead-up to the Nyepi is an amalgamation of many local
festivities. Visitors can take part in processions wherein small statues
of gods are carried to the river and immersed in the water.

**4. Exploring Ubud Caves **

Ubud is a small town located at a drivable distance from the northern
end of Denpasar. The entire setting exudes a rustic charm. Ubud is
looked upon as the cultural hub of Bali. The most famous site here is
that of the Ubud Caves. These caves are also called Goa Gajah, or
Elephant Caves. Located in the Bedulu village of Ubud, these caves make
for a fantastic adventure outing. The caves' walls have drawings that
are supposed to have been engraved in 9th century A.D.

**5. Surfing at Kuta**

Just about 15 minutes away from the main streets of Denpasar lies
Kuta. The surfing facilities manage to attract professional surfers from
many surrounding nations. Kuta has some of Bali’s most recognized
surfing schools. Visitors can enroll in short courses for two to three
days and earn watersport certifications.
