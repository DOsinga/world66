Manadonese food is famous throughout Indonesia for two things: the Minahasa eat **absolutely anything**, and the flavor of choice is **very, very spicy**.
Sugar is used little if at all, with flavor added through lemon and
herbs. Being Christian, the Minahasa also often marinate meat in
alcohol and favor pork. More exotic protein options include bat, field
rat and dog, the last of which is the obligatory centerpiece of any
wedding ceremony or Christmas feast. Classic dishes include:

*ayam rica-rica*, grilled chicken topped with a fiery red chili-and-onion paste
*ikan woku*, fish simmered in herbs
*babi tore*, fried pork until crunch with a lot of chilli
*perkedel milu*, corn fritters
*tinutuan* (or *bubur manado*), a goopy porridge of
rice, pumpkin, noodles and random vegetables — doesn't look very
attractive, but it can be tasty (and, for once, not spicy)

Dutch settlement here has also brought in a few surprising influences, notably:

*breneibon*, a rich kidney bean soup
*klappertart*, a Western-style coconut cake Susen and Lumpia, also famous with ice kacang.
