Before traveling to Kluang, Malaysia, it is important that
you get some **Kluang Practical Information **that can help you
enjoy your holiday.

**Passport and Visas**

If you plan to visit Kluang you must possess a valid passport and travel
documents that are valid for at least 6 months from the date of your
intended journey. With regards to visa, most of nationalities are
allowed to enter Malaysia without any visa. Malaysia requires visitors
from a few countries to obtain a visa prior to arrival. You can check
with your travel agent whether you need a visa or not.

**Climate and Time Zone**

Kluang belongs to the (Greenwich Mean Time) GMT+8 time zone. The
official time of this town is 13 hours ahead of the Eastern Standard
Time (EST).

Belonging to the tropical zone, Kluang has 2 major climates--the
dry season (summer) and the wet season (rainy). The dry summer is from
May to September while the wet season is from November to March. The
best time to visit Kluang is during summer when there is no rain.

**Taxes**

Tourists who are staying in Kluang for 72 hours or more are exempted
from paying taxes for the following merchandise: 1 quart of liquor,
souvenirs and gifts worth not more than RM 200, 50 cigars or
cigarettes, 225 grams of tobacco, electrical and/or battery-operated gadgets. Duty free item purchases can be made throughout Malaysia and include perfume, cosmetics, watches, lighters, cameras and pens.

**Currency**

The official currency used in Kluang is Ringgit Malaysia (RM). Just
like any other Asian country, Malaysia strictly follows their Currency
Exchange Control Order. So if you are a non-resident traveler, you are
not allowed to import currency of more than RM 1,000. Importing a total
amount of more than $10,000 must be declared and registered using the
IMM26 form. You can change your local currency into Ringgit Malaysia at
the authorized money changers or at banks. Most places will accept
credit cards for payments.
