---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Kuching"
type: section
---

**Kuching nightlife** has an eclectic mix of entertainment to suit
all tastes. Options range from slow-paced evenings strolling the
waterfront to adrenaline-pumping nights dancing at local nightspots.

**Waterfront**

Take a relaxing stroll and enjoy the Kuching Waterfront during the
nighttime. It's a relaxing part of town where families, couples and
young people can enjoy a pleasant night out. If you are feeling
peckish, choose from the many restaurants open for dinner along the Main
Bazaar of the waterfront.

**Bars**

If you love partying the night away, then head to the Travillion Pub
Centre in Petanak. The Travillion is a popular hangout for the young at
heart. It offers an extensive number of bars and lounges where you can
dance till the early hours of the morning. Drinking dens include the
Check-In, Paragon, Barz!ng, Pavillion, and the Core Concept Bar. As well
as house music and techno, you can also dance to the beat of Chinese
pop. Or, if karaoke is more your style, drop in to the Crown Karaoke Pub
to sing your heart out.

There are some good bars congregated
around the Bukit Mata area. Affectionately known to the locals as St
Pica, the Piccadilly's Music Cafe is a lively place where friends can
meet up over a beer or two. While in the neighborhood, check out the
Cottage for its special themed nights or the Eagle's Nest for its
spacious, relaxed atmosphere.
**
Restaurants**

Eating out is a favorite pastime among locals, and family and friends
can often be found catching up over a tasty meal at a favorite
restaurant. Kuching restaurants offer a tantalizing array of cuisines to
choose from including Malay, Chinese, Indian, Thai, and Western food.

Open
air dining, Kuching style, is an experience not to be missed. Try an
open-air restaurant to share a piping hot steamboat with friends, or
sample local delights at the open-air markets opposite the Electra House
Shopping Centre in the city.
**
Tribal Dance Evenings**

Large hotels such as the Holiday Inn and the Hilton hold regular Dyak
tribal dance evenings to showcase talented local performers. It makes
for an entertaining night, as the dancers perform lively routines to the
accompaniment of orchestral music. An evening of cultural delights can
be nicely finished off with a nightcap at Cats City or Latino, Taman
Sri Sarawak, opposite the Hilton.
