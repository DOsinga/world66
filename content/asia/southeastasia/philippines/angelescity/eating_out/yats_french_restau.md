Angeles City may have carried a heavy stigma of being a red-light district for the longest time, probably not totally unfair to say that this is a remnant of US-foreign air-base era in the 70s. Now-a-days, it is anything but a cheap sleazy town although it should be conceded that there are plenty of cheap joints especially along the Clarkview Road a.k.a. Fields Avenue.

The re-opening of the Clark Internationla Airport (DMIA) brought in some heavy-weight players and serious future stake-holders. These are international businessmen and investment strategists who are rather spoiled and very discerning with how they spend their precious few hours after work. Cheap beers and cheap entertainment aren't how these people got to where they are today. It's more like Dom Perignon, Foie Gras, Chateau Margaux and Nespresso Coffee.

While old establishments in Angeles City are trying to re-engineer themselves, high-end luxurious restaurants begin to appear. Among them, is the fine-dining Yats Restaurant and Wine Bar. This restaurant is not like anything we've ever seen in these parts. This is 5-star in every respect. The menu is world-class. The 2700-line award-winning wine list may be the best in Asia and can stand up to just about anything in the international arena.

The web site is www.YatsRestaurant.com. I admit I love my food and my wine. Not everyone is as fastitious as I but then if I think about life being far too limited in length, I seriously doubt I need to force myself to consider anything less.

Try these: Kangaroo Loin, Atlantic Mackerel, Cog Au Vin or better still, ask for their classic French Escoffier set dinner and a bottle of Morey-St.-Denis (or a 20-year-old St. Emilion/Pomerol).
