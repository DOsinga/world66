If you are visiting Camiguin Island, you can make use of
these **Camiguin Island budget travel
ideas**. Located in Mindanao in the Philippines, this is an excellent summer
vacation spot if you happen to be on a shoe-string budget.

**Getting there**

There are only a few seasonal flights to Camiguin Island.
However, you can take a low cost airline to Cagayan de Oro. Once you have
reached Cagayan De Oro by your flight of choice, you’ll have to head to the
Balingoan Port, which is a two hour bus ride. From here you can take one of the
ferries to get to Camiguin Island.

**Where to Stay**

Camiguin Island offers a good many budget-friendly beach
resorts and guest lodges. Tia’s Beach Resort and Cottages will cost you less
than $30 a night. But if you happen to be looking for a bit of luxury and yet
want to be stringent on those purse strings, then try out the Secret Cove beach
resort which houses rooms that have an air conditioner, fridge and cable TV.
Plus, they also have a bar/restaurant with a lounge area, a pool table and a
dive shop. All this for just $30 per night!

**Day Tours**

Hooking yourself up with a local guide on Camiguin Island is
no problem at all. The real challenge lies in your bargaining skills. A
benchmark value of a day tour can be estimated at around 1000 Pesos (less than $30),
so use those haggling skills to the hilt! On a usual round trip of the island,
visitors can expect to see the spooky Sunken Cemetery, Guiob Church ruins, St. Nino
Cold Springs, the amazing Katibawasan Falls, the Ardent Hot Springs and the
Fishnet restaurant on a lagoon near Benoni.

**Water Sports**

Camiguin Island offers a host of water sports for the
adrenaline pumped adventurist. From rappelling to cannoning to mountain biking to
water skiing to snorkeling and scuba diving right to fishing opportunities in
the area, you’ll never find a bored day on this island. And the best part is
that you’ll get yourself a good price (approximately $35) per sport if you
spend enough time bargaining.

**Eating Out**

Eating out on the Camiguin Islands is not a big deal at all.
This is because the island is dotted with a large number of cafes and
restaurants to keep up with the influx of tourists. Most of the restaurants
serve the freshest sea food available at cheap rates, so this will be your best
option.
