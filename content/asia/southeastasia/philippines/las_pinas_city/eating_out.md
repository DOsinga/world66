---
title: "Eating Out in Las Pinas City"
type: section
---

**Las Pinas City - eating out** is made very convenient by the
fact that most of the best restaurants are gathered together at certain
points in the city and are within walking distance of one another. This
pleasant destination already has much to recommend it
culturally and historically. You'll be pleased to hear that its
culinary excellence is of a high standard also. You'll be able to eat
Filipino, Chinese, Japanese, American, Italian and Mexican.

**SM SouthMall**

In and around this major shopping mall are classic Filipino eateries
such as Kamay Kainan and Barrio Fiesta, Chinese-style Mongkok Dim Sum
and Noodles, Japanese places Tokyo Tokyo and Fukuya, US-style pizza
parlours such as Shakey's, Pizza Hut, Yellow Cab Pizza, Pizza! Pizza!
and Little Caesar's, the famous Mexican fast-food chain Taco Bell and
such international eateries as Cafe Elysee and Almon Marina. For great
coffee go no farther than The Coffee Experience. Las Pinas City -
eating out could not be more enjoyable.

**BF Resort Village**

Around the plush district of BF Resort Village you'll also be
spoiled for luscious cuisine. Rosalyn's Place Garden and Restaurant is
renowned as being a top place to eat and is located at the corner of
Jose Ma. and Buenvenido Streets. On BF Resort Drive you can sate your
appetite at Grill and Restaurant, Tambayan Bar and Tarragon Grill and
Restaurant. The Foodlane features the excellent Dexter's Pizza, just
nearby the Elizabeth Seaton School is Royal Caribbean Jamaican Patties
(for something a little bit exotic), and for a superlative cup of
coffee you have to go to Mocha Blends on the corner of Editha Vital and
Gloria Diaz streets.

**Resto-Bars**

If you fancy having a drink with your meal then there are several
establishments that could be considered resto-bars, even if they are
not quite as hip and happening as their counterparts in other major
Filipino cities such as Manila. Las Pinas City - eating out is possible
with a drink in certain establishments that are more spread out than
grouped together. Even so, people go to them
and these places are rarely quiet. The most popular include the Star
Mall's Dencio Bar and Mark's Sports Bar, while Alabang-Zapote Road can
boast the excellent Z-Bar Cowboy Grill. CV Star Avenue in Pamplona
has Cuba Libre Bar & KTV where you can enjoy yourself
singing badly in front of your friends. You're sure to enjoy that!
