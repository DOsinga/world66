The city of Olongopo
is situated in the Zambales province of the Philippines. Olongopo is a city full
of tradition that is found near Bataan and Pampamga. It has a bay and it has
wonderful weather. It was not until 1959 that the United
States granted independence to the city of Olongopo and made it a municipality
of the Philippines. Today we are fortunate to have a place where we can learn
about the history of this city and its struggles in the past, thanks to the
Olongopo City Museum.

**City of Olongopo
Museum**

The museum of the city
of Olomgapo has many different exhibits representing the important events in the
history of the city. It also has statues of the famous patriarch
of Olongopo Ulo Ng Apo. Father Apo, as people would always call him, was known
for his fairness and his ability to resolve conflict. One of the most
imposing items in the museum is the gate that the Spaniards built to fortify
their arsenal. You can also see scenes of the tragedies that happened at
Olongopo during WWII which help us understand how Olongopo became a well-respected city
of the Philippines.

Olongopo has received
an award by UNESCO for being a city of peace. This city will continue to grow
and is an example of efficiency to the rest of the region.
