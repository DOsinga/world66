---
title: "Things to do in Quezon Province"
type: section
---

You must have your **Quezon Province things to do** list ready if
you are visiting this remarkable destination. Formerly known as
Tayabas, the Quezon Province is home to the noteworthy landmarks that
played numerous roles in the Philippine history. This amazing place
also has an isolated white sand beach situated at Puting Bato where
visitors can unwind and enjoy the pristine waters in the area.

**Snorkeling at Pagbilao Grande**

Located in Tayabas Bay, the Pagbilao Grande is a collection of large
islands that is comparable to those situated in the province of
Pangasinan. This is the perfect place where you can swim through
Quezon’s clear waters and enjoy the sunshine in its white sand beaches.
You can view the beautiful aquatic life by means of snorkeling in its
lucid seawater, or do some island hopping with your friends or family.
If you are lucky enough to visit this place during low tide, you can go
to the Tulay Buhangin, a group of sand bridges that join the islets of
Padre Burgos. This is a perfect place to laze around, swim and enjoy
the day.

**Walk Around the Quezon National Park**

Covering about 9 million hectares in terms of land area, the Quezon
National Park is like a portrait of waterfalls and springs, with a rich
surrounding flora and different kinds of birds flying over the waters.
Walking around the park with your kids or loved one is one of the best
bonding moments you can have in this area. It is under the protection of
the government, so the picturesque scenery is expected to remain for
many years to come. Remember to take your digital camera along to
capture some extraordinary pictures.

**Bird Viewing in Minasawa Bird Sanctuary**

Minasawa is an island in Quezon where the outstanding bird sanctuary
is located. Here you can find about a hundred different species of
birds and you can observe how the avian life thrives in this safe
haven. From pigeons to herons, Minasawa Bird Sanctuary has got almost
anything you need for a great bird viewing experience. Keep your
binoculars and digital camera handy to enjoy your visit to the fullest.

**Hiking at Mt. Banahaw**

Located at the boundaries of Quezon Province and Laguna, Mt. Banahaw
is a splendid mountain that has remained an active volcano throughout
the years. You can hike your way to the summit while taking some
pictures of the dark caves, exceptional rock formations and gorgeous
waterfalls.
