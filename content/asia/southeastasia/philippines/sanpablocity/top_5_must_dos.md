---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in San Pablo City"
type: section
---

If you plan to visit and explore this city, these are the top five **San
Pablo City must do's**.

**Explore the Seven Lakes**

There are seven lakes all over the city, namely Bunot, Calibato,
Mohicap, Palakpakin, Pandin, Sampaloc and Yamboo. One of the top things
to do here is to go visit the seven lakes. You can go boating, fishing,
biking and strolling around these wonderful fresh water lakes. The most
famous among these lakes is the Sampaloc Lake; this is the largest of
all the other lakes. You will surely enjoy the natural beauty as you
explore these lakes.

**Coco Festival**

Coco Festival is a week-long celebration that takes place on January
15 each year. This festival takes place one week before the actual city
fiesta of San Pablo. This event showcases the city’s main product which
is coconut. You can take part in the celebrations and enjoy the colorful
parade, street dancing competition and fireworks display as well as the
“Lakan at Mutyang San Pablo” beauty pageant.

**Casa San Pablo**

Enjoy a unique Filipino experience at Casa San Pablo. This is one of
the most popular tourist destinations in San Pablo City. Casa San Pablo
is a rustic country inn that offers traditional Filipino living and
accommodations right in the heart of the city. You can take advantage of
the serene atmosphere, wonderful amenities and artistic displays of
Casa San Pablo. It is also part of the famous Viaje Del Sol culture and
culinary tour. Casa San Pablo is located at Barangay 1-A, within the Kay
Inay Resort premises.

**Villa Escudero**

Take a trip to Villa Escudero. The villa is situated in the
boundaries of San Pablo City and Dolores, Quezon. This used to be a
coconut and sugarcane plantation and was transformed into a nice
native-themed resort. Villa Escudero is known for its rural offerings
where you can enjoy carabao rides, stay in nipa huts and enjoy the
cultural shows. You will definitely enjoy the rural village tour,
waterfall restaurant and the sports and recreational facilities
available at Villa Escudero.

**Good Friday Procession**

Most of the Filipinos are Catholic, so Lenten season is a big thing
in this country and San Pablo is not an exception. If you happen to drop
by during this season you don’t want to miss out on the Good Friday
procession. Here you can see beautifully adorned Karosas carrying
life-sized statues of saints. These statues are taken around town as
people pray and sing the popular hymn “pasyon” as the procession passes
through the streets.
