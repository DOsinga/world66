---
title: "Sights in Tacloban City"
type: section
---

You don’t want to miss a single thing when you are on a travel
itinerary and see the beautiful **Tacloban City** **sights**.
Tacloban City is the capital of Leyte Province in the Eastern Visayas
Region of the Philippines. It is a coastal town that keeps a rich record
of history to tell, and the offers the hospitality that every Filipino
is known for.

**General MacArthur Landing Memorial**

You might want to start off by visiting the General MacArthur Landing
Memorial. This is where the famous war-time general returned to turn
the tides of war on his side against the Japanese in the final months of
the World War Two. Today, sculpted figures of men, including Macarthur
himself, stand as a reminder of that glorious comeback.

**The San Juanico Bridge**

Go and see the San Juanico Bridge. This is the longest bridge in the
Philippines, and links the port city of Tacloban to neighboring island
province of Samar. The bridge was built during the time of the late
Ferdinand Marcos. It was formerly known as the Marcosias Bridge in honor
of Marcos himself. The arch-shaped truss bridge stands over a number of
islets that traverse the sea, separating the Leyte province from Samar.

**Price Mansion**

Visit the Price Mansion with your family while vacationing in
Tacloban City. The mansion was once the colonial house for American
dignitaries and soldiers during the war. Today, it is an attractive safe
for historical events and equipments left from the war. The museum
houses several artifacts preserved from the time of war.

**Santo Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum**

The Santo Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum is another place to visit
in Tacloban City. The museum houses religious grottos and statues that
reflect the devout Catholicism of Tacloban. Priceless sets of antique
furniture are also in display, some of which date back to the Spanish
colonization era. The museum also houses several are pieces of art,
antique porcelain sets, and ivory, which are believed to have been
brought about by the Spanish.

**Madonna Shrine of Peace**

Tacloban City also heralds its own valley of tranquility, where
tourists can take a well deserved respite from a long day’s journey of
sight-seeing. Here, people flock for prayer and meditation. The Shrine
statue is made out of a rare miyagi stone created by an unnamed Japanese
sculptor.
