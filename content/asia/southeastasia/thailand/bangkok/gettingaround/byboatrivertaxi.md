The most interesting alternative to get around is the fleet of canal (klong) boats and river taxis on the Chao Phraya River. They are inexpensive and during day-time often one of the speediest ways to get to your destination. As the river taxi drivers do not stop for long you should jump aboard quickly. Occasionally it happens that you get a little bit wet - so make sure you do not wear a white wedding dress! 

**Points of Interest**

**Chao Phraya Express Boat**

These boats run up and down the river and they only stop at certain piers. Make sure your boat stops at the desired destination by pointing on a bilingual map or asking the locals or fellow travellers if you are not sure. **Klong Saen Sap**

My favourite route goes from the near the Democracy Monument all the way down to Ramkhamhaeng Open University (and beyond) in the east of Bangkok. The trip takes about 25 minutes, but in comparison with the bus, the boat is much faster and there is no Skytrain going in this area of town. **Klong Banglampoo Boat **

Approx. every twenty minutes until 7 pm there are small boats going from Klong Banglampoo near Khao San Road to Hualompong Railway Station (Central Station) and back. This is a nice way to catch your train if you do not have too much luggage and stay in the Khao San Rd. area. 5 Baht only.
