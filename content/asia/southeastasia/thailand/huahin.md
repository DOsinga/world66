Hua Hin is located approx. 190 km south of Bangkok and 92 kilometres north of Prachuab Khiri Khan, its  provincial chief town and it is a coastal, well known resort town, set on the west coast of the Gulf of Thailand, Hua Hin has a population of around 60,000 people, the King of Thailand has his summer palace adjoining the beach and almost every year spends a few months in Hua Hin.

 Hua Hin has one of the lowest rain falls in Thailand and being a coastal town gets that lovely sea breeze, so Hua Hin can be visited all year round. Hua Hin possesses a tropical climate with a rather high degree of humidity. There are three seasons, the hot season (March-May), the rainy season (June-October) and the cool season (November-February). 

 Among the most important sights to visit is Klai Kang Won Palace, a royal summer residence built by  King Rama VII. The major nightlife attraction which is very popular with both Thai and foreign tourists is the night market.
