Nakhon Pathom is a small province located just 56 km from Bangkok. It
is located in the alluvial plain of central Thailand. It is drained by
the Tha Chin River (also called Nakhon Chaisi river), a distributor of the Chao Phraya river. The urban area of Bangkok has already grown until the provincial borders to Nakhon Pathom. The province features an ancient religious structure called “Phra
Pathom Chedi”, the first religious landmark that signified the influx
of Buddhism into Thailand. Nakhon Pathom is also renowned for its
abundant fruits varieties and famous dishes.
