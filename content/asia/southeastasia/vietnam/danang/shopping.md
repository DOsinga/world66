Da Nang is the fourth
largest city in Vietnam, and is the perfect place for a **Da Nang
shopping** experience. It is one of those cities whose culture clearly
reflects the marriage of East and West. Da Nang used to be occupied by
Americans and French decades ago. There are many notable sights in the
city, but if you are looking to purchase local goods and products, Da
Nang abounds with the most diverse markets and shopping centers where
you can find just about anything. Your Da Nang shopping experience will
be truly one of a kind.
Da Nang Supermarket

This shopping hot spot is located right in the heart of the city,
close to the railway station. It is easy to find, and you can easily get
lost in its colorful vendor-filled streets. Vietnam is home to
top-calibre silk and hand-woven fabrics. Aside from this, they also make
great export-quality lacquer ware, ceramics, bamboo artifacts and other
handicrafts worth buying to decorate your Western home. Da Nang
Supermarket is the perfect place to shop for these, and more. You will
find friendly vendors who sell jewelry, fabrics, clothing, shoes, books,
mechanical and electrical appliances, and intricate designs and
decorations for your home.
X Q Da Nang Embroidered Art

This company sells hand embroidered silk pictures. These portraits
are elaborate scenes of Vietnamese life, landscapes and flowers and can
be unframed and framed. Tourists visit this center mainly to buy
embroidered pictures to take home as souvenirs.
Da Nang Marble Mountain

Fall in love with marble structures of all shapes and sizes. Da Nang
Marble Mountain is popular for its quality marble products that are used
widely for home design and decorations. The marbles are hand-carved by
artistic craftsmen.

**Other Shopping Sites**

Da Nang is an upper class city, so the economy here is
well-maintained. You will see many local shopping centers and markets
that sell both traditional and modern products. Markets are filled with
name-brand products that boast of good quality minus the price. There’s
even a French store named Big C, which sells various goods and is open
until 10 p.m.. Other markets worth visiting are Hung Vuong, Hoang Dieu,
Phan Chu Trinh, where you can find DVDs, clothes, bags, shoes and just
about anything you may need, including house wares. Finally, ask around
the city and locals will point you to the best tailor shops, silk shops
and stone carving shops.
