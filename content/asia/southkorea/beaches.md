---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Three of South Korea's four borders meet the sea, and the beaches vary considerably by coast.

**East Coast**

The east coast, facing the Sea of Japan, has the cleanest and clearest water. Pine forests come down to the shore in places, and the beaches are generally long and sandy. Sokcho beach, near [Seoraksan](/asia/southkorea/sights) National Park, is the most convenient for combining mountain hiking with a swim. Samcheok further south has several good beaches surrounded by pine trees. These beaches are quieter than the Busan strip and popular with Korean families.

**Southeast (Busan)**

Haeundae and Gwangalli in [Busan](/asia/southkorea/pusan) are the most visited urban beaches in the country, and it shows in July and August when the sands are completely carpeted with beach umbrellas. Gwangalli has the better atmosphere: a long strip of restaurants and bars facing the water and the illuminated Gwangan Bridge, with the beach as a backdrop rather than the point. Worth visiting even off-season for the seafood restaurants.

**West Coast**

The Yellow Sea side has tidal flats rather than surf, and the water is murkier, but the beaches have their advocates. Taean on the Taean Peninsula has around 15 beaches; Mallipo is the most famous, known for soft sand and calm waters. Boryeong's Daecheon beach is where the annual Mud Festival is held every July, when the mineral-rich tidal mud becomes the subject of intense recreational enthusiasm.

**Jeju Island**

[Jeju](/asia/southkorea/chejuisland) has the warmest and clearest water of any Korean beach. Jungmun on the south coast is the most developed, with surf and good conditions for water sports. Hamdeok in the northeast has shallow turquoise water popular with families. Udo Island, just off the northeast corner of Jeju, has a white coral-sand beach that looks implausibly tropical for a Korean island. Jeju beaches are at their best in August and September.
