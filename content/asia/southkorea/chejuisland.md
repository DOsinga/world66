Jeju (historically Cheju) is South Korea's largest island and its most popular domestic holiday destination, sitting 90 km off the southern coast of the peninsula. It has a mild subtropical climate, dramatic volcanic scenery built around **Hallasan** (1,950 m, the highest peak in South Korea), and a UNESCO World Heritage designation covering the mountain, the lava tube caves, and the coastal basalt columns.

The island has a distinct culture and history — for centuries the inhabitants spoke a dialect so different from mainland Korean that it functioned almost as a separate language. The **haenyeo** (sea women), female divers who free-dive for abalone, urchin, and other seafood without equipment, are one of the island's most famous traditions and have their own UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage designation.

Getting there: hourly flights from Seoul (one hour) and from most major Korean cities; overnight ferries from Busan (about 14 hours) and from other southern ports. The island has a good bus network, but a rental car or scooter is the most flexible option.
