Hallasan (1,950 m) is the highest mountain in South Korea and a dormant volcano that forms the core of Hallasan National Park. Its slopes support distinct vegetation zones from subtropical coast to alpine summit. The **Baengnokdam** crater lake at the top is one of the most scenic views in Korea. Several trails lead to the summit, with the Seongpanak and Eorimok routes being the most popular; the summit trail requires an early start to beat crowds and the noon cutoff.
