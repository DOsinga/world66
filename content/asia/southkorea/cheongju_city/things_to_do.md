Cheongju punches above its weight for history and culture. The city's most famous landmark is the Cheongju Early Printing Museum, dedicated to Jikji — the world's oldest surviving book printed with movable metal type, predating Gutenberg by decades. The book itself is held in Paris, but the museum brings to life the remarkable printing process that created it.

Near the Cheongju Department Store stands an iron flagpole that has endured for centuries, the last remnant of a Buddhist temple long since vanished. Locals say the area around it has the best tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes) in Korea. The traditional covered market downtown is a sensory maze of stalls selling everything from deboned pig faces to live eels to plastic toys — the kind of market that rewards slow wandering. Every other year the city hosts the Cheongju International Craft Biennale, a major showcase of folk crafts and contemporary design from across Asia and beyond.
