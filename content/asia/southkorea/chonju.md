Chonju (Jeonju) is one of South Korea's most rewarding cities for travelers — the birthplace of bibimbap, the country's most famous rice dish, and home to a beautifully preserved Hanok Village where hundreds of traditional tile-roofed houses have survived intact.

The Hanok Village sits in the heart of the old city, a rare pocket of pre-modern Korea where you can stay in a traditional guesthouse, eat well, and wander stone alleyways without feeling like you're in a theme park. The village is genuinely lived-in, which is what makes it work.

Beyond the city itself, the region rewards exploration. About an hour east, Mt. Maisan is known for its twin rocky peaks and the extraordinary stone pagodas built in its valleys — especially striking in spring and autumn. Kumsansa (Gold Mountain) Temple, dating to the 6th century and housing a 12-metre-high golden Buddha, is one of the largest temple complexes in the country. Deogyu-san National Park and the ancient Mireuksa stone pagoda are both within reach for day trips.
