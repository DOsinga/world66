Insadong is Seoul's traditional culture street, a long pedestrian-friendly lane lined with tea houses, calligraphy shops, antique stores, and small galleries that make it the city's best area for Korean art and craft. Ssamziegil, a quirky open-air shopping complex, spirals upward with independent artisan shops on every level. The tea houses are a highlight — sit on floor cushions drinking omija-cha or ssanghwa-tang in centuries-old hanok buildings. Just north, Bukchon Hanok Village preserves hundreds of traditional tile-roofed houses along narrow hillside lanes, offering views down toward Gyeongbokgung Palace. Insadong can feel touristy on weekends, but duck into the side alleys and you'll find working artists' studios and tiny galleries showing contemporary Korean work. It's the right starting point if you want to understand what Korean culture looks like beyond K-pop.
