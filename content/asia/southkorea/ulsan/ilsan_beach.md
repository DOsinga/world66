Ulsan's most accessible beach stretches along the east coast of the city, backed by a boardwalk lined with seafood restaurants, cafes, and the usual Korean beachside amusements. It's not the prettiest stretch of sand in Korea, but it's where Ulsan goes to unwind on summer evenings.

The beachfront restaurants specialize in fresh fish — several have outdoor terraces overlooking the water that make for a good sunset spot. Cafes near the steps leading up to Ulgi Park have swinging benches and decent coffee. The park itself is worth the short climb for coastal views.

**Getting there:** A taxi from anywhere in the city is inexpensive. From Ulsan Airport, take bus 112 or 102. From Ulsan Station or the Intercity Bus Terminal, take bus 1114, 1401, or 401.
