The Esala Perahera is Sri Lanka's grandest festival — ten nights of processions through the streets of Kandy in July or August, culminating in an enormous final parade. It honours the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha, housed in the Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa), and has been held almost without interruption for over two centuries.

The procession is spectacular. Dozens of elaborately decorated elephants walk through the streets, the lead elephant carrying a replica of the golden casket that holds the relic. Dancers, drummers, fire-breathers, and whip-crackers fill the gaps between the elephant trains. The sound of the Kandyan drums alone is worth the trip.

The festival builds over ten nights. The first five are the Kumbal Perahera, smaller and quieter. The last five are the Randoli Perahera, with the full contingent of elephants and performers. The final night is the biggest — tens of thousands of people line the streets, and the procession can last five or six hours.

Book accommodation in Kandy well in advance if you plan to visit during Perahera. The town fills up completely. Seats along the route can be reserved through hotels and guesthouses, but standing in the crowd is the more immersive experience.
