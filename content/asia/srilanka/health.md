---
title: "Health"
type: section
---

Sri Lanka was certified malaria-free by the WHO in 2016, which simplifies planning considerably. You do not need malaria prophylaxis.

**Recommended vaccinations** before visiting include Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and routine immunizations (tetanus, MMR). For longer stays or travel to rural areas, consider Hepatitis B and Rabies. Consult a travel health clinic at least four to six weeks before departure.

**Dengue fever** is a real concern. It is transmitted by mosquitoes that bite during the day, and cases are reported year-round across the country. Use repellent with DEET, wear long sleeves and trousers in the early morning and late afternoon, and sleep under a mosquito net if your accommodation does not have screened windows. If you develop a high fever within two weeks of arriving, see a doctor.

**Water** from taps is not safe to drink. Stick to bottled or filtered water. Most guesthouses provide filtered water for free or for a small charge; ask before buying. The same applies to ice — be cautious in local restaurants, fine in tourist-oriented places.

**Medical facilities** are reasonable in [Colombo](/asia/srilanka/colombo) and major cities. Nawaloka and Asiri hospitals in Colombo treat foreigners and have English-speaking staff. Outside the cities, facilities are limited. Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage is strongly recommended.

**Heat and sun** are serious risks in the lowlands and at archaeological sites like Sigiriya, where you will be climbing exposed rock. Start early, carry water, and cover up.

**Pharmacies** are well-stocked in cities and most towns, and many medications available only on prescription in the West can be bought over the counter at low cost.
