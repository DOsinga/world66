Jaffna is the cultural capital of Tamil Sri Lanka, a city that spent decades cut off by civil war but has roared back to life since 2009. The northern peninsula feels distinctly different from the rest of the island -- the food is spicier, the temples more ornate, and the pace of life has its own rhythm. You will find a city proud of its Tamil heritage and genuinely happy to see visitors.

The Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil is the spiritual heart of Jaffna, a towering Hindu temple where elaborate festivals draw thousands, especially during the annual July-August festival season. The Dutch-built Jaffna Fort, one of the best-preserved colonial forts in Asia, sits near the waterfront and is worth a slow wander. The rebuilt Jaffna Public Library, once one of the largest in South Asia before its destruction in 1981, stands as a powerful symbol of the city's resilience.

Jaffna's food scene alone justifies the trip north. The crab curry here is legendary, the dosai are thinner and crispier than anywhere in the south, and the local seafood -- cooked in fiery Tamil style with plenty of chilli and curry leaf -- is outstanding. Try the woodapple juice if you are feeling adventurous.

Beyond the city, the Jaffna peninsula's islands are a highlight. Nainativu has an ancient Hindu temple and a Buddhist vihara sitting side by side. Delft Island, the most remote, feels like stepping back in time with its wild horses and coral-stone ruins. The islands are reached by short ferry rides from Kurikattuwan jetty, and the journey through shallow turquoise waters is half the fun.
