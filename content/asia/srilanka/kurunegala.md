Kurunegala is a mid-sized city in the North Western Province of Sri Lanka, about 100 kilometres north of Colombo. It was a medieval capital of the Sinhalese kingdom in the 13th and 14th centuries, and the large rocky outcrops that surround the town were given the names of animals — Elephant Rock, Tortoise Rock — that still serve as local landmarks.

The town sits at a crossroads between Colombo and the Cultural Triangle (Kandy, Sigiriya, Anuradhapura), and most travellers pass through rather than stay. For those who do stop, the **Ibbagala** (Tortoise Rock) can be climbed for views over the lake and the surrounding plains, and there are a handful of Buddhist temples in the area.

The surrounding district is productive agricultural land — coconut plantations and paddy fields stretch in most directions — and the town itself is a busy commercial hub with good transport connections to the main cultural sites of the interior.
