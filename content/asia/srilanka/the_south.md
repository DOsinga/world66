---
title: "The South"
type: location
---

The southern region of Sri Lanka has excellent beach resorts — Tangalle, Hambantota, and Weligama among them — but it is more than a beach destination. The area is also home to Kataragama, one of Sri Lanka's most sacred pilgrimage sites, shared by Buddhists, Hindus, and Muslims, where the god Kataragama (an incarnation of the war god Skanda) is venerated. Near [Matara](/asia/srilanka/matara) the Werehana temple has a remarkable five-storey structure enclosing a Buddha figure, its walls covered in paintings illustrating scenes from the Buddha's life.
