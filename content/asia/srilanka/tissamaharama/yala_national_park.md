Yala is Sri Lanka's most visited national park and one of its wildest corners. The park stretches across nearly 1,300 square kilometres of dry monsoon forest, scrubland, and lagoons along the island's southeast coast, and it holds one of the highest densities of leopards anywhere in the world.

Most visitors explore Block 1, the oldest and most wildlife-rich section, where elephant herds, crocodiles, sloth bears, and dozens of bird species share the landscape with those elusive leopards. The best time to visit is between February and July, when water holes shrink and animals congregate around them. Early-morning jeep safaris from [Tissamaharama](/asia/srilanka/tissamaharama) are the standard way in — the park entrance at Palatupana is about 20 kilometres from town.
