---
title: "Getting Around in Damascus"
type: section
---

There is a good choice of **Damascus transportation** options in this Syrian city. Damascus is richly steeped in history. You can get around by foot in Damascus. However, if you have to go a long distance, there are other modes of transport. Here are the ways you can move around in Damascus.

**Public Transport**

The cheapest and most convenient mode of transport in Damascus is traveling by minibuses. These buses can accommodate around 12 people and run around the city on pre-determined routes. You may need the help of a local to read details of bus routes, since this is written in front of the bus in Arabic. Private air-conditioned buses are also easily available in Damascus, if you want to get out of the city. You should try and book in advance because these bus schedules change very frequently. Another mode of transport is by train. Damascus has 2 main railway stations, one of which is currently being renovated. Since trains are a bit slow here, they can often be an unreliable mode of transport within the city.

**Taxis and Private Cars**

Damascus' yellow-colored taxis are also a great way to travel within the city. You should remember to tell the taxi driver to put the meter on, so as to avoid getting cheated on the rate. A taxi from the airport to the city of Damascus costs around $17. Taxi rates can get a bit expensive especially during the night. Private cars are also available if you want to travel to another city from Damascus. This will cost you anywhere between $10 and $25.
