---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Taiwan is an island, and its coastline runs to over 1,500km, but the beach scene is a secondary attraction compared with the mountains and cities. The best beaches are concentrated in the south and northeast.

**Kenting** at the southern tip of the island has the longest-established beach culture. The Kenting National Park area has multiple bays — Baisha (White Sand) Beach is the widest — and the area is very popular with Taiwanese on weekends. The water is warm enough to swim from April through October. It can get crowded and resort-heavy in peak season; come in April or late October for quieter conditions.

**Fulong Beach** on the northeast coast, about 90 minutes from [Taipei](/asia/taiwan/taipei) by train, is the nearest decent beach to the capital. It hosts a major sand sculpture festival in spring and an annual beach volleyball competition. The beach itself is backed by a river lagoon, which is good for kayaking.

**Jinshan Beach** (also written Chinshan) on the north coast, about an hour from Taipei, is popular with surfers. The nearby Jinshan township has hot spring hotels and a good fish market.

**Green Island (Ludao)**, off the east coast near Taitung, offers coral reefs, snorkelling, and some of the best diving in Taiwan. It's also notable for a former political prison used by the Nationalist government. Accessible by ferry or short flight from Taitung.

**Penghu Islands** in the Taiwan Strait have a different character altogether — windswept basalt coastlines, shallow turquoise bays, and a strong fishing culture. The water is clear and the snorkelling is good. Accessible by flight or ferry from Taipei, Taichung, or Kaohsiung.
