Kaohsiung is Taiwan's second city and its main port, sitting at the southern end of the island where the climate is truly tropical. It spent most of the 20th century as a heavy industry hub — steel, shipbuilding, petrochemicals — and is still reinventing itself, but the transformation is well underway.

The Love River, once badly polluted, has been cleaned up and is now lined with cafes and walkways. The harbour front has been redeveloped into the Pier-2 Art Center, a cluster of former warehouses converted into galleries, studios, and performance spaces that has become one of the more interesting cultural destinations in Taiwan. The nearby Cijin Island, reached by a short ferry ride, has a long beach on one side and a seafood street on the other.

Fo Guang Shan, about 30km east of the city centre, is one of the largest Buddhist monastery complexes in Asia, with a 120-metre-tall golden Buddha visible from some distance. Worth the trip for the scale of the place alone.

Kaohsiung has its own MRT system and is the southern terminus of the High-Speed Rail. It's also a logical base for visiting [Kenting](/asia/taiwan/kenting) National Park at the island's tip.
