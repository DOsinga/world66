Keelung is a working port city on Taiwan's northeast coast, about 30 minutes from [Taipei](/asia/taiwan/taipei) by train. It's often drizzly — Keelung is one of the rainiest cities in Taiwan — and emphatically not a polished tourist destination. That's part of its appeal.

The Miaokou Night Market, clustered around the Diangan Temple near the harbour, is one of the best night markets in Taiwan and less well known to foreign visitors than the Taipei markets. Grilled corn, braised pork rice, herbal duck soup, and crab congee are among the specialities. The market runs every evening and is at its best after dark.

The waterfront area has been cleaned up and has decent seafood restaurants. The Zhongzheng Park on the hillside has a large white Guanyin statue visible from the port. Keelung is also a departure point for the ferry to Matsu islands.
