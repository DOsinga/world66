---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Taipei has one of the best public transport systems in Asia. The **MRT** (Mass Rapid Transit) is clean, cheap, air-conditioned, and reaches nearly everywhere a visitor wants to go — five main lines and several branches cover the city and its suburbs. Buy a rechargeable **EasyCard** at any station on arrival; it works on the MRT, buses, the airport express, and most convenience stores. **Buses** fill in the gaps to places the MRT does not reach (notably Yangmingshan National Park) and are also covered by the EasyCard. **Taxis** and Uber are plentiful and cheap by international standards. **YouBike** is the city's bike-share scheme, with docks every few hundred metres and an excellent network of riverside bike paths. Walking is easy and pleasant; most sidewalks are wide and shaded by covered arcades.
