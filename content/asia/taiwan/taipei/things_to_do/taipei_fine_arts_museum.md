Taiwan's first and most important museum of contemporary and modern art, opened in 1983 in a striking glass-and-white modernist building on the north side of the city. The collection focuses on Taiwanese artists of the 20th century and is particularly strong on the **Nihonga** painters who trained during the Japanese colonial period and the later modernist and contemporary schools. TFAM also hosts the **Taipei Biennial** in odd-numbered years, one of the most important contemporary art events in East Asia. The building itself — a cascade of rectangular white forms with wrap-around glass walls — is almost as interesting as the contents. Closed Mondays.
