---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Taiwan"
type: section
---

#### 1. Walk into Taroko Gorge

The Taroko Gorge is one of those places that exceeds every photograph of it. The Liwu River has cut through solid marble for millennia, creating a canyon of white and grey walls that seem to glow in the right light. Walk the Shakadang Trail along the river, or take the longer Zhuilu Old Trail for vertiginous views from the cliff face. The Eternal Spring Shrine — a red pavilion set over a waterfall in a side gorge — is the most beautiful small monument in Taiwan. Get to Hualien by the eastbound TRA train and stay at least two nights.

#### 2. Spend a Night in a Taipei Night Market

Taipei's night markets are not tourist traps — they're where the city actually eats. Shilin, Raohe, and Ningxia are the most famous, but any neighbourhood market on a warm evening will do. Try stinky tofu (it smells far worse than it tastes), oyster vermicelli noodles, scallion pancakes, and tang yuan (glutinous rice balls in sweet broth). Go hungry and go late — things only get better after 9pm.

#### 3. Take the Mountain Railway to Alishan

The Alishan Forest Railway is a narrow-gauge line built by the Japanese in the early 20th century to log the ancient cypress forests. The trees are still standing — the logging was stopped — and the train now winds up through three climate zones on a journey that is slow, beautiful, and completely unlike anything else in Taiwan. The highest station sits at over 2,400 metres. If you time it right, the sunrise over a sea of cloud is one of the great travel experiences in Asia.

#### 4. Explore Tainan's Temples and Street Food

Taiwan's oldest city has more temples per capita than anywhere else on the island, and arguably the best food. The Chihkan Tower and Anping Fort give you the history; the lanes around Shennong Street give you the present. Tainan's local specialities — coffin bread, milkfish congee, shallow-fried shrimp rolls, and the sweetest beef soup in Taiwan — justify the trip on their own. Don't rush: this is a city for wandering.

#### 5. Cycle Around Sun Moon Lake

The 30km cycling path that circles Sun Moon Lake runs along the shore and through the surrounding tea-covered hills. It's gentle enough for anyone, beautiful at dawn when mist hangs over the water, and dotted with temples, pagodas, and Aboriginal cultural sites. Rent a bike at the main dock in Shuishe and take the ferry across to Ita Thao village, home to the Thao indigenous people, for lunch and a walk before cycling back. It's one of the most satisfying day trips in Taiwan.
