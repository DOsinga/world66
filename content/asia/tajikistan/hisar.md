Hisar (also spelled Hissar or Gissar) is a small town about 30 km west of Dushanbe, best known for its 18th-century fortress. The site was once the residence of the Bukharan regional governor and served as the administrative center of this part of Tajikistan until the Soviet period.

The fortress itself is largely ruined, but two well-preserved madrasas (one old, one newer) and a caravanserai remain on site. A small museum covers the history of the region. The approach is through a park-like setting and the views back toward the mountains are good.

Hisar makes an easy half-day trip from Dushanbe. Shared taxis run from the Dushanbe bus station, and the site is accessible by bus. Combine it with Ajina-Tepe (Buddhist monastery ruins a few km further) to make a full day of it.
