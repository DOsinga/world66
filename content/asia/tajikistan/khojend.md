Khujand (also spelled Khojend or Khudjand) is Tajikistan's second city and the capital of the northern Sughd province. It sits in the Fergana Valley, a fertile lowland shared with Uzbekistan and Kyrgyzstan, and has a more relaxed and prosperous feel than Dushanbe. The population is around 180,000.

The city has an ancient history — it was refounded by Alexander the Great as Alexandria Eschate ("Alexandria the Farthest") around 329 BCE and has been an important trading center on the Silk Road ever since. The old fortress at the city center dates to this period, though what stands today is a 20th-century reconstruction on the original site.

The covered bazaar (Panjshanbe Bazaar) is one of the largest in Central Asia and worth a few hours on its own — spices, dried fruit, textiles, and produce, with a high Soviet-era roof overhead. The fortress houses a small historical museum. The Kayrakkum reservoir to the east offers swimming beaches in summer; locals call it the "Tajik sea."

Khujand is geographically separated from the rest of Tajikistan by the Turkestan Range. The easiest connections are to Tashkent (3 hours by road) and by the domestic Khujand–Dushanbe flight. The overland route to Dushanbe goes through the Anzob Tunnel, an infamous stretch of unlit, flooded Soviet-era tunneling, though conditions improve year by year.
