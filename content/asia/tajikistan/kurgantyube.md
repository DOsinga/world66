Kurgan-Tyube (now officially renamed Bokhtar) is the administrative center of Khatlon province in southern Tajikistan, about 100 km south of Dushanbe. It is a mid-sized city of roughly 100,000 people in a hot, flat agricultural plain near the confluence of the Vakhsh and Panj rivers.

The city itself has little to draw travelers. Its main role is as a transit point for the Afghan border crossing at Nizhny Pyanj (for those with the necessary permits), and as the starting point for visits to the Tigrovaya Balka Nature Reserve — one of the few remaining habitats for Bactrian deer and Central Asian wildlife in the region. The reserve is remote and requires advance organization to visit.

The south of Tajikistan around Kurgan-Tyube bears more scars from the civil war of the 1990s than most parts of the country. Recovery has been steady but the region remains poorer than the north.

There is an archaeologically significant site nearby at Ajina-Tepe, where a 7th–8th century Buddhist monastery yielded a 13-meter reclining Buddha (now in the National Museum in Dushanbe). The site itself has little remaining to see.
