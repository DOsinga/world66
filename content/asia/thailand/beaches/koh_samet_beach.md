Koh Samet is a small island off the coast of Rayong province, about three and a half hours southeast of Bangkok by bus and ferry. That proximity makes it the nearest proper beach escape from the capital, and it gets busy on weekends.

The island's main draw is Hat Sai Kaew (Diamond Beach) on the northeast coast — a long strip of fine white sand. Further south, the beaches get quieter: Ao Phai and Ao Vong Duen are good middle-ground choices, while Ao Wai at the southern tip is genuinely peaceful on weekdays. The whole island sits inside a national park, so there's a 200-baht entry fee for foreigners.

Ferries leave from the pier at Ban Phe. The crossing takes about 40 minutes.
