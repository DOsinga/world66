The Krabi coastline offers some of the most dramatic beach scenery in Southeast Asia. Towering limestone karsts rise straight out of turquoise water, and the variety of beaches means you can find something whether you want a party scene or solitude.

Railay Beach is the standout -- only accessible by boat, hemmed in by sheer cliffs that double as world-class rock climbing routes. Ao Nang serves as the main tourist hub with plenty of restaurants and tour operators, though the beach itself is ordinary; long-tail boats leave from here for the better sand at Railay, Tonsai, and the offshore islands. Further south, Koh Lanta has a more laid-back feel with long, quiet stretches of sand.

The Phi Phi Islands draw huge day-trip crowds, but staying overnight lets you enjoy them after the boats leave. The smaller islands around Krabi -- Koh Poda, Chicken Island, Tup Island -- are worth a long-tail trip for snorkeling and empty beaches.
