The "Sunrise Coast" runs south from Bangkok along the Gulf of Thailand, stretching from Cha Am through Hua Hin and down past Chumphon. It's mainland Thailand's most accessible beach stretch -- no flights or ferries needed, just a few hours by train or car from the capital.

Hua Hin and neighboring Khao Takiab are the busiest stops, with seafood restaurants, night markets, and long sandy beaches popular with Thai weekenders. Further south, Pranburi and Dolphin Bay get progressively quieter. The Sam Roi Yot beaches sit below dramatic limestone cliffs and feel genuinely remote despite being only a short drive from Hua Hin.

Keep going south and you reach Baan Krut and Bang Saphan, where tourism thins out to almost nothing -- simple guesthouses, empty sand, and local fishing villages. Koh Talu, a small island offshore from Bang Saphan, offers good snorkeling. The highway and railway both run along the coast, making it easy to hop between towns.
