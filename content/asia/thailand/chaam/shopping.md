---
title: "Shopping in Cha Am"
type: section
---

The **Cha Am Shopping** experience is truly comprehensive, offering a rare blend of authentic and contemporary offerings. Cha Am has turned into a prominent shopping locale of Thailand. Cha Am serves as the supply center for many of Bangkok&rsquo;s popular stores. Further, it is located just about 170 km south of Bangkok. This distance is easily commutable via a shared taxi. As a result, a number of shoppers converge at Cha Am for their shopping needs. Since Bangkok has turned into an internationally-themed metropolis, those searching for an authentic, Thai shopping experience should head towards Cha Am.

**Night Market**

The night market is popularly referred to as the &lsquo;Talat And&rsquo;. The night market is organized every day, except for Mondays and Wednesdays. This market offers a mind-boggling range of affordable clothing and home-d&eacute;cor accessories. Visitors should be guarded against the spurious brands that are sold here. It is recommended to buy the local offerings rather than the branded goods. Haggling for prices is common. Stretched between the Chao Lay road and the Narathip Road, this market attracts native shoppers from many neighboring towns. The night market is perfect to buy souvenirs. Visitors can find everything, ranging from sea-shell garlands to golf shirts and local handicrafts. Other popular items include handbags and various kinds of headgears. Many items offered here are factory-seconds and are sold at shockingly low prices. The handicrafts of Cho Am are renowned for their authenticity, being sourced from the nearby Hup Kapong village.

**Mini-Markets**

Apart from the night market, some shops have been operational for many years and are widely regarded as dependable retailers. This includes the Seven Eleven stores and the series of Family Marts that are sprinkled across the beachfront. The original Family Mart is located opposite to the Cha Am Municipality. Another dependable option is the Tesco Lotus Express, located along the nearby Narathip Road. For buying the usual stuff like packaged foods, milk products and branded confectionery items, the Okay Supermarket on Narathip Road is recommended. It isn't a real supermarket by any stretch of imagination, but it stocks most of the branded eatables. Another shop with a similar range of goods is the Cha-Am Memory.

**Other Shopping**

Besides these stores, there is a paucity of big brand or authentic, luxury brands in Cha Am. However, visitors can make a trip to the nearby Hua Hin or the Phetburi for visiting a supermarket or a major, luxury retailer. These are the two locations where expats and foreign visitors usually head to when seasonal discounts are announced.
