Chantaburi is a prosperous provincial capital on Thailand's eastern seaboard, about 245 km southeast of Bangkok. The city sits at the mouth of the Chantaburi River and has long been famous for two things: rubies and sapphires. The surrounding hills produce some of the finest colored gemstones in Southeast Asia, and the weekend gem market draws traders from across the world.

Beyond the gem trade, Chantaburi has a distinct character shaped by centuries of migration. The old town along the river is lined with shophouses built by Vietnamese and Chinese settlers, and the Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception — the largest Catholic church in Thailand — has stood here since the 19th century, a legacy of French occupation and Vietnamese Catholic refugees.

The surrounding province is lush and fertile. Durian, rambutan, and mangosteen grow in abundance, and the fruit markets during season are a highlight in themselves.
