Chiang Dao sits about 70 kilometres north of Chiang Mai, in a broad valley beneath Doi Luang Chiang Dao — Thailand's third-highest peak and a UNESCO World Biosphere Reserve. The name means "city of stars," and on clear nights in the dry season you'll understand why: the darkness is real and the sky is full.

This is not a beach town or a party destination. It's a place to slow down — hike through mist-covered jungle, soak in hot springs, wander a weekly hill tribe market, and eat very well for very little. A growing community of artists, expats, and slow travellers has settled here without overwhelming the valley's quiet character.

The cave temple at the base of the mountain is the best-known attraction and worth the trip on its own. Beyond that, the appeal is diffuse: good coffee, mountain trails, rice paddy views, and the general pleasure of a town that hasn't been optimised for tourists. Most people come for a night and stay three.

The best time to visit is November through February — cool, dry, and clear. Avoid February to May (burning season, heavy smoke) and June to October (heavy rain, some trails close).
