How often did I hear people moaning after three consecutive days in the capital, that they need to get out of Bangkok to avoid a nervous breakdown? Well, the choice of escape is there be it in form of taking the Death Railway across the River Kwae bridge, visiting museums, trips to waterfalls, seaside resorts along the east and west coast, national parks, cultural shows or floating markets Bangkok’s surroundings are awash with beautiful places that make great escape routes.

One of the highlights in the vicinity of Bangkok is undoubtedly Ayutthaya, the former capital of Siam is around 80km north of Bangkok and during its heydays it had a larger population than Paris or London at that time. The Burmese razed Ayutthaya to the ground and it vanished in the dust of history. Today many tour operators offer tours to Ayutthaya whereby you’re offered guidance from one sightseeing to the other by minivan or tour bus.

How can you experience it?

We’re not big fans of repeatedly getting on and getting off the bus whilst being timed throughout the entire tour but prefer to cruise independently between the temple ruins and explore anything that Ayutthaya has to offer and invite people to join us in the future

For sporty people there’s the option to travel the entire distance from Bangkok to Ayutthaya on a bike. The route runs parallel to the river and leads through endless rice fields and canals. Alternatively you can just load your bikes on a train and go round-trip by train.

Which ever way of traveling you prefer, bike, train, organized or rather independent, you should not miss out on Ayutthaya.
