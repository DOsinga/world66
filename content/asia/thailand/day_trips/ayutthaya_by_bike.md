Ayutthaya, the former capital of Siam, sits about 80 kilometres north of Bangkok and is one of the best day trips from the city. The ruins of temples and palaces spread across an island formed by three rivers, and a bicycle is the ideal way to get between them at your own pace.

You can take the train from Bangkok's Hua Lamphong or Bang Sue Grand stations (about two hours, cheap and scenic) and rent a bike near the Ayutthaya station for around 50 baht a day. The flat terrain makes cycling easy, and the distances between major temples are short enough that you can cover the highlights in a single day.

Start at Wat Mahathat, famous for the Buddha head entwined in tree roots, then ride to Wat Phra Si Sanphet with its three iconic chedis. Cross the river to visit Wat Chaiwatthanaram, one of the most photogenic temple complexes. In between, stop at the Ayutthaya Historical Study Centre for context on the city that once rivalled London and Paris in size. Bring plenty of water — shade is scarce among the ruins and the heat can be fierce.
