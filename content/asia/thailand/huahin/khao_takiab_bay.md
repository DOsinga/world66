Khao Takiab Bay stretches south from the foot of Khao Takiab (Chopstick Mountain), where a large golden Buddha overlooks the Gulf of Thailand. The beach is wide, the sand is firm, and the water stays calm — waves rarely amount to more than ripples, making it good for swimming.

It's quieter than the main Hua Hin beach further north. Beach chairs and umbrellas are available, and vendors sell food and drinks along the sand. A green songthaew (shared pickup truck) runs from Hua Hin market to Khao Takiab; from the drop-off point, follow the road past the temples and you're there.

Boat trips to Koh Singto and other small islands offshore can be arranged with the fishing boats at the southern end of the bay.
