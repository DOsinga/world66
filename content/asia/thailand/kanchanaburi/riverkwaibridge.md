The iron bridge over the Khwae Yai river is Kanchanaburi's most visited landmark. The Japanese army brought the steel spans from Indonesia and Allied prisoners of war reassembled them as part of the Death Railway linking Thailand with Burma. The bridge was bombed repeatedly by Allied aircraft in 1945 — the curved replacement spans are still visible among the originals. Today the bridge is still used by local trains and is open to pedestrians; a small railway museum on the eastern approach holds WWII-vintage steam locomotives and a hybrid road-railcar from the same era. The area around the bridge is busy with riverside restaurants, souvenir shops and boat tours.
