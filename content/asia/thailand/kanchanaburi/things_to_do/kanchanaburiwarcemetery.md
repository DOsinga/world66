The main Allied war cemetery, opposite the Kanchanaburi railway station on Thanon Saeng Chuto, is maintained in immaculate condition by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission. It holds the remains of 6,982 Allied prisoners of war — British, Australian, Dutch and other nationalities — who died during construction of the Death Railway. Rows of low headstones under mango trees make for a quiet, deeply affecting place. An estimated 16,000 Allied POWs and 49,000 Asian labourers died in total during the railway's construction; this cemetery represents only a fraction of those lost.
