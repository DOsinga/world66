Koh Phayam is a small island in the Andaman Sea off the coast of Ranong province, in southern [Thailand](/asia/thailand). It is less developed and far quieter than the big-name Thai islands — no ATMs, limited electricity until recently, and a pace of life set by the tides and the cashew harvest.

The island is known for its long, fine white-sand beaches — Ao Yai (Long Beach) on the west side gets the sunsets — and for the cashew nuts grown in the interior. A small community of Chao Le (sea gypsies) lives on the island's eastern shore, fishing the traditional way.

Getting here involves a boat from Ranong, either a speedboat (about 45 minutes) or a slow boat (two hours). There are bungalows and guesthouses scattered along the beaches, most of them simple. The island is small enough to explore by motorbike or bicycle, and the best season to visit is November through April, when the seas are calm and the weather dry.
