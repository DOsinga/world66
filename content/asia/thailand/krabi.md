Krabi is a province on the Andaman coast of southern Thailand, roughly opposite Phuket across the bay, where limestone karsts rise straight out of the sea and a scattering of beaches, islands and mangrove estuaries make up some of the most photographed landscapes in South-east Asia. The name refers to a whole province — the small provincial capital, **Krabi Town**, is unremarkable — but in traveller's shorthand "Krabi" almost always means the beach resorts and islands strung along the coast nearby.

The centre of that tourist coastline is **Ao Nang**, a long palm-fringed beach that is now the most developed base in the area, backed by a strip of hotels, dive shops and travel agents. A short long-tail ride south brings you to the **Railay peninsula**, cut off from the mainland by a wall of limestone cliffs and reachable only by boat — the setting is dramatic, the beaches some of the best in Thailand, and the cliffs are a world-famous sport-climbing destination. Offshore lie the **Phi Phi Islands** (shared with Phuket), busy day-trip favourites, and the quieter, larger island of **Koh Lanta**, further south. Inland the forested hills contain cave temples, hot springs, and the famous **Emerald Pool** at Khao Phra Bang Khram.

Come for the beaches, the rock climbing, and the sea kayaking through the mangrove-lined inlets around Ao Thalane and Ao Luk. Avoid if you are looking for a quiet, untouristed corner of Thailand — those days are long gone in Krabi — but accept the crowds and it still delivers one of the most beautiful coastal landscapes anywhere.
