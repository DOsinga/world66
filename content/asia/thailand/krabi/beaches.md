---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Krabi's beaches are the reason most people visit — a long Andaman coastline of white sand backed by limestone karsts and fringed by islands. The most developed base is **Ao Nang**, long and palm-fringed, lined with hotels, dive shops, and seafood restaurants; from there, long-tail boats serve the spectacular **Railay peninsula**, a cluster of beaches cut off from the mainland by cliffs and only reachable by boat. **Hat Noppharat Thara**, next door to Ao Nang within the national park, is a quieter stretch of casuarinas and tide pools. Further south, **Had Yao (Long Beach)** in Khlong Thom district is a rural escape that most of the bus tours miss. And offshore, the usual cast of islands — **Phi Phi**, **Koh Lanta**, **Koh Jum**, **Koh Hong** — each offers its own take on the Andaman formula of sand, karst and clear water.
