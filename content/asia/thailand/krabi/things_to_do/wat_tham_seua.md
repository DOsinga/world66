About 5 km north of Krabi Town, Wat Tham Seua — the Tiger Cave Temple — is a working forest monastery built into a long shallow limestone cave, with a meditation centre and a scattering of smaller caves in the surrounding karst. The real draw, though, is the absurdly steep **1,237-step staircase** that climbs a neighbouring limestone tower to a gold Buddha on the summit and one of the finest views in southern Thailand — a 360° panorama over Krabi, the sea, and the jungle-covered cliffs. Start early, bring water, and don't underestimate the heat; the climb takes most people well over half an hour.
