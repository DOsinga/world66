Mae Sariang is the southernmost district of Mae Hong Son province, sitting in a broad river valley ringed by forested hills near the Myanmar border. It gets a fraction of the tourists that Pai or the provincial capital attract, which is part of its appeal. The town itself is small and unhurried — Burmese-style teak temples line the main street, longboats idle at the riverside pier, and the pace of life is genuinely slow. Day trips into the surrounding national parks and down the Salawin River are the main draws, and they’re worth the detour.
