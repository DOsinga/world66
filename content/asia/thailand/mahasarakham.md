Mahasarakham is an Isan college town that few foreign visitors see. With seven institutions of higher learning — led by Mahasarakham University and its 30,000 students — it has the energy and cheap prices that come with a young population, without any of the tourist infrastructure. If you want to see a real central Isan town rather than a polished destination, this is a good place to do it.

The Museum of Northeastern Isan Culture on the MSU downtown campus is worth a visit for its overview of the region's history and traditions. The university's second campus at Khamriang, nine kilometres north of town, is striking in itself — modern brick buildings set against rice paddies where farmers still plough with buffalo.

The fresh food market near the central clock tower is the best window into daily life. Restaurants and bars cluster near the university campuses, lively and inexpensive. The best time to visit is November through January, when the weather is cool and dry.
