The Sanctuary of Truth is a massive structure — 105 metres tall — built entirely from teakwood and covered in hand-carved mythological and religious figures from Hindu and Buddhist iconography. Construction began in 1981 and is still ongoing; the wood requires constant maintenance and replacement. The effect is overwhelming: every surface is carved, and the scale of the project is hard to comprehend until you are standing in front of it. Admission includes a short Thai cultural performance. The site is north of the city centre on the cape, fronted by its own small beach.
