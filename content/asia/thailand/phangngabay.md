Phang Nga Bay is one of the most dramatic seascapes in Southeast Asia: dozens of sheer limestone karst towers rise straight out of calm, shallow emerald water between [Phuket](/asia/thailand/phuket) and [Krabi](/asia/thailand/krabi). The bay is part of a national marine park, and most visitors explore it by longtail boat or sea kayak.

The most famous formation is Koh Phing Kan, better known as James Bond Island after its appearance in "The Man with the Golden Gun." It draws big crowds, but paddle past it and you will find sea caves, hidden lagoons, and mangrove channels that feel genuinely remote. The fishing village of Koh Panyi, built entirely on stilts, makes a popular lunch stop. The small provincial capital of Phang Nga town, set among its own limestone crags on the mainland, serves as the usual jumping-off point.
