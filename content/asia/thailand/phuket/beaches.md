---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Phuket's beaches run down the west coast of the island in a string of sandy bays separated by rocky headlands. Best swimming and weather is **November to April**; the southwest monsoon from May to October brings rough seas and strong currents — pay attention to red flags, drowning incidents are common in the wet season.

**Patong** is the busiest, with full tourist infrastructure, jet-skis, parasailing, and the bar strip of Bangla Road inland. **Karon** and **Kata**, just south, are the calmer family alternatives — wide beaches, waves big enough to bodyboard, plenty of restaurants without the Patong intensity. **Nai Harn** at the southern end of the island is one of the prettiest beaches, sheltered between two headlands and not (yet) over-developed.

North of Patong, **Kamala** and **Surin** are upmarket and quieter, with several big resorts. **Bang Tao**, **Layan** and **Nai Yang** further north are even less developed; Nai Yang is part of Sirinat National Park and has a turtle conservation centre. **Mai Khao** at the very northern tip is the longest and emptiest beach on the island — and right beside the airport runway, a popular spot to watch planes land just metres above the sand.
