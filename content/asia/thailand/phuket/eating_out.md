---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Phuket's food culture is a mix of southern Thai (heavy on seafood, hot curries, fresh herbs), Hokkien Chinese (the legacy of the tin-mining migrants), Muslim southern Thai (rotis, beef rendangs), and the international resort circuit of Italian, French and Japanese restaurants in the beach towns.

For local food, **Phuket Old Town** is by far the most rewarding area. Look for *moo hong* (slow-braised pork belly with star anise and pepper), *mee Hokkien* (yellow noodles in a thick savoury gravy), *o-tao* (oyster omelette), and the colourful Nyonya-style sweets. The **Sunday Walking Street** along Thalang Road is the easiest way to graze through everything in one evening.

The **night markets** (Chillva Market in Phuket City, Phuket Indy Market) and the **roadside stalls** in any town are typically excellent and very cheap. Beach-area restaurants in Patong, Karon and Kata cover the spectrum from cheap Thai cafes to international fine dining; quality varies widely. **Kopitiam by Wilai** on Thalang Road is one of the best-known sit-down places for Phuket-style dishes.
