Karon is [Phuket](/asia/thailand/phuket)'s second-largest beach after Patong, stretching about three kilometres along the Andaman Sea coast. It sits between [Kata](/asia/thailand/phuket/kata) to the south and Patong to the north, and strikes a middle ground between the two — livelier than Kata, calmer than Patong.

The beach itself is broad and the sand is notably yellow and grainy, with a coarser texture than the fine white sand further north at Mai Khao. Development is spread out along the bay rather than packed tight, which means that even in high season you can find a quiet stretch. There is almost no natural shade, so bring strong sunscreen or hire a deckchair and umbrella. In the low season (roughly May to October) the surf gets rough and the undertow can be dangerous — flags are posted and should be taken seriously.

Karon Town, the strip of restaurants, bars, and shops running parallel to the beach, is functional rather than charming. Eating and drinking here is inexpensive by Thai resort standards, and the range is wide enough that you can eat well without going far.
