A grid of streets in central Phuket City lined with Sino-Portuguese shophouses painted in pastel pinks, blues and yellows — the architectural legacy of the Hokkien Chinese tin-mining boom of the late 19th century. Thalang Road and Soi Romanee are the most photogenic stretches. Increasingly crowded with hipster cafes, design shops and small boutique hotels. The Sunday Walking Street (along Thalang Road) closes the road to traffic and fills with food and craft stalls.
