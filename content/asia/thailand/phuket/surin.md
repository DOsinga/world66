Surin Beach is one of [Phuket](/asia/thailand/phuket)'s quieter stretches of sand, a bay lined with tall casuarina trees that offer welcome shade from the tropical sun. It is a favourite beach for locals, who flock here on weekends and holidays to eat seafood at the beachside restaurants.

Unlike most Phuket beaches, Surin has a steep sand shelf, which makes it less suitable for swimming. Big waves are common during the monsoon season and can create dangerous undertow conditions. That same swell has made surfing increasingly popular here, and the beach offers decent snorkelling at both ends of the bay when the water is calm — though visibility drops considerably during the rainy season.
