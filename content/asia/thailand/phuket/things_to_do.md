---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The headline is the beach, but Phuket has more to it once you step away from the resort strips. **Phuket Old Town** repays half a day's wander — the Sino-Portuguese shophouses on Thalang Road, the Sunday walking street, and a string of independent cafes have made the town quietly fashionable.

The **Big Buddha** on Nakkerd Hill above Chalong (45m of white Burmese marble; visible from much of the south island) and **Wat Chalong** at its base are the main religious sites. **Sirinat National Park** at the north end of the island has the quietest beaches and a turtle conservation centre.

For the active, **diving** is the obvious draw — day trips and multi-day liveaboards run to the Similan Islands, Phi Phi and Racha Yai. **Snorkelling**, **kayaking** through the limestone caves of Phang Nga Bay, **kitesurfing** off Nai Yang in the right season, and **Muay Thai training** at one of the dozens of gyms around Chalong all draw repeat visitors. **Cooking classes** are widely available and a good rainy-day option.

A typical first-time visitor combines beach days with a James Bond Island day trip and an evening exploring Phuket Old Town.
