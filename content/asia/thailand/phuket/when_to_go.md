---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The dry season runs **November to April** and is the prime time for the beach — sunny days, calm seas, water temperatures around 28°C. December and January are peak season; book accommodation well ahead and expect higher prices. **March and April** get hot — high 30s by mid-afternoon — but the sea is still flat.

The southwest **monsoon** runs **May to October**. It rarely rains all day but afternoon downpours are frequent, the sea gets rough with strong rip currents (red flags posted; treat them as binding), and many smaller beach businesses close. The trade-off is significantly cheaper rooms and a much quieter island.

The major event to time a visit around is the **Vegetarian Festival** in September or October (the first nine days of the ninth lunar month of the Chinese calendar), centred on Phuket Old Town. Devotees dressed in white process through the streets in trance states, piercing their cheeks with skewers, walking on hot coals and climbing ladders of upturned blades — startling, intense and crowded. Earplugs help; the firecrackers are constant.
