Founded in the 14th century and sacked by the Burmese in 1767, Ayutthaya was the capital of the Siamese kingdom for over four hundred years. Today the ruins of palaces, monasteries, and enormous Buddha statues are scattered across an island at the confluence of three rivers, all designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Explore by bicycle or on foot. Highlights include Wat Mahathat, where a Buddha head is entwined in tree roots, the towering prang of Wat Ratchaburana, and the reclining Buddha at Wat Lokayasutharam. The site is an easy day trip from Bangkok — about 80 km north — by train, minibus, or boat.
