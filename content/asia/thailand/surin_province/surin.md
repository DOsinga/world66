Surin is a medium-sized city in the Isan region of northeastern [Thailand](/asia/thailand), and the capital of the province that bears its name. It sits on the flat, dry plateau of lower Isan, closer to the Cambodian border than to Bangkok.

The city is best known for the annual Elephant Round-Up, held every November, which draws large crowds to see elephant shows and processions. The surrounding area is home to the Suay people, traditional elephant keepers whose culture is distinct from mainstream Thai society.

Surin is also a centre for the jasmine rice trade — the fragrant rice grown in this region is considered among the finest in Thailand. The Khmer heritage of the area is visible in several ancient temple ruins nearby, including Prasat Sikhoraphum, a well-preserved 12th-century Khmer sanctuary about 30km east of town.

The city itself is quiet and sees relatively few foreign tourists. It makes a practical base for visiting the Khmer ruins of the region, and for crossing into [Cambodia](/asia/cambodia) via the Chong Chom border checkpoint, about 70km to the south.
