Bodrum sits on the Aegean coast where the ancient city of Halicarnassus once stood — home to one of the seven wonders of the ancient world. Most visitors come for the beaches and the nightlife, but the town rewards anyone willing to look past the bars. The Castle of St Peter, built by the Knights of St John, dominates the harbour and houses an excellent Museum of Underwater Archaeology. The remains of the Mausoleum of Halicarnassus are a short walk inland.

During the day the harbour is the centre of activity — gulet boats line up offering day trips to secluded bays and neighbouring islands for swimming and snorkelling. The bazaar area at the foot of the castle is packed with small shops, and the pedestrian streets make for good wandering. For a quieter meal, head to the fishing village of Gümüslük, where simple restaurants serve fresh fish overlooking the water.

At night, Bodrum's bar street comes alive. The town has been Turkey's party capital for decades, and while some of the old megaclubs have faded, the waterfront strip still draws crowds well into the early hours.
