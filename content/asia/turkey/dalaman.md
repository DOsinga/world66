Dalaman is a small farming town on Turkey's southwestern Mediterranean coast, known almost entirely for its international airport. Charter flights from across Europe land here daily, funneling visitors toward the spectacular destinations nearby — [Fethiye](/asia/turkey/fethiye), [Dalyan](/asia/turkey/dalyan), and Marmaris among them.

The town itself is unremarkable, and few visitors linger longer than it takes to catch a bus or pick up a rental car. But the surrounding region is genuinely beautiful: a stretch of coast where ancient Lycian ruins meet turquoise water, loggerhead turtles nest on protected beaches, and the Dalaman River cuts through forested hills ideal for rafting.

If you do find yourself with time in Dalaman, there are a handful of decent restaurants and cafes near the centre of town. The real draws, though, are all within easy reach — and that is precisely the point of being here.
