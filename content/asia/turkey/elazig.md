Elazığ is a city in eastern Turkey, sitting at around 1,000m altitude in the upper Euphrates basin. It is the gateway to Keban Dam reservoir — one of the largest in Turkey — which flooded much of the old Harput valley when it was built in the 1970s. The city itself is modern and somewhat unremarkable, but it sits in a region with deep history and dramatic landscape.

The old town of Harput, 5km northeast of the city centre, is where most visitors head. It has a ruined Seljuk castle, a medieval mosque (the Ulu Cami), and sweeping views over the plain. Elazığ is also known for its wine — the local Öküzgözü grape produces some of Turkey's better reds, and the area around the city has a long winemaking tradition. The city functions mainly as a base for the surrounding region rather than a destination in itself.
