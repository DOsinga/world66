The largest theatre in the ancient world, with a seating capacity of about 25,000, built into the slope of Mount Panayır. Originally constructed in the Hellenistic period and substantially rebuilt under several Roman emperors, the theatre looks down the full length of the Arcadian Way — the marble street that once ran from the theatre to the ancient harbour. The Apostle Paul is said to have preached here, provoking a riot against the silversmiths who made statues of Artemis.
