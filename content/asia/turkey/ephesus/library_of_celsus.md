The two-storey façade of the Library of Celsus is the most photographed structure at Ephesus. Built around AD 117 as a mausoleum and library for the Roman governor Celsus, it originally held around 12,000 scrolls in niches between its columns. The facade was reconstructed in the 1970s from original fragments. The four statues in the niches represent Wisdom, Knowledge, Intelligence, and Virtue.
