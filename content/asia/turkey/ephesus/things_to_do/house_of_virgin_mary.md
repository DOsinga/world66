A small stone structure on Mount Koressos, about 7 km from the main Ephesus site, traditionally identified as the house where the Virgin Mary lived out her later years after leaving Jerusalem with the Apostle John. The site has been a place of Christian pilgrimage since the 19th century and has been visited by multiple popes. The identification is disputed historically, but the setting — a wooded hillside — is peaceful, and the site draws visitors of many faiths. Access is by a winding road or a short walk from the upper Ephesus gate.
