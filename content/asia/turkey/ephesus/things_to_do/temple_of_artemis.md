One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus was in its final form one of the largest buildings of the ancient world — more than four times larger than the Parthenon in Athens. Almost nothing remains above ground today except a single reconstructed column standing in a field just outside Selçuk. The Ephesus Museum in Selçuk holds statues and fragments from the temple and is essential context for understanding what once stood here.
