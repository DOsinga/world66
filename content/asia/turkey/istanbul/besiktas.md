Besiktas sits on the Bosphorus waterfront between Kabatas and Ortakoy, anchored by the absurdly opulent Dolmabahce Palace — the Ottoman sultans' 19th-century attempt to out-Versailles Versailles. The neighbourhood itself is grittier and more alive than the palace suggests, with a busy fish market, tea gardens along the shore, and the passionate roar of Besiktas football fans on match days. The nearby Ortakoy Mosque, framed against the Bosphorus Bridge, is one of Istanbul's most iconic images. Ciragan Palace, now a Kempinski hotel, adds more waterfront grandeur. The ferry terminal connects you quickly to the Asian side, and the steep streets climbing uphill toward Yildiz Park offer green escape from the city. This is a working Istanbul neighbourhood that happens to have world-class landmarks on its doorstep.
