The Yerebatan Sarayı — Underground Palace — is a 6th-century cistern the size of a cathedral, with 336 columns rising from the water. Built under Justinian to supply the Great Palace, it's cool, dim, and quietly spectacular. Two columns in the far corner stand on Medusa heads, repurposed from older Roman buildings. Open daily from 9am.
