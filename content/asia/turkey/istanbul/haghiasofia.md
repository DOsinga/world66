The outside underwhelms; the inside is one of the most astonishing spaces ever built. Justinian's 6th-century church — converted to a mosque in 1453, museum in 1934, mosque again in 2020 — has a dome 56 metres high that appears to float on a ring of light. The surviving Byzantine mosaics, partly revealed through the plaster that once covered them, are extraordinary. Get there when it opens to beat the tour groups.
