Take the ferry from Eminonu to Kadikoy and you cross to the Asian side of Istanbul — a different rhythm entirely. The waterfront market area is one of the city's best for produce, fish, pickles, and street food, and the surrounding streets have an independent, lived-in character that the tourist districts lack. Walk south into Moda, a leafy residential neighbourhood with a coastal promenade, vintage cafes, and views back toward the Old City skyline. Kadikoy has a thriving bar and restaurant scene that caters to locals rather than visitors, with craft beer spots and innovative Turkish kitchens. The Süreyya Opera House adds unexpected cultural weight. Most visitors skip the Asian side entirely, which is precisely why you should go — this is where Istanbulites actually live and eat.
