Rize sits on the Black Sea coast at the foot of the Kaçkar Mountains, about 70 km east of Trabzon airport and 90 km from the Georgian border. It is the heart of [Turkey](/asia/turkey)'s tea country — the steep green hillsides above the town are covered in tea plantations, and the drink is a matter of fierce local pride. You will be offered tea constantly, and it would be rude to refuse. Rize serves as the main gateway to the northern approaches of the [Kaçkar](/asia/turkey/kackar) range.

The town itself is not beautiful — it is a functional Black Sea port with concrete buildings climbing the hillside — but the setting is spectacular. The mountains rise sharply behind the coast, and on clear days you can see the snow-capped Kaçkar peaks from the waterfront. The Ziraat Tea Garden above town offers panoramic views over the coastline and the tea plantations, and is the best place to understand why this strip of coast is so green: it rains here more than almost anywhere else in Turkey.

From Rize, minibuses run south into the mountains toward Çamlıhemşin and the Ayder Plateau, which are the main jumping-off points for trekking in the Kaçkar range. The mountain villages above Rize are home to the Hemşin people, who have their own distinct culture and cuisine — try the local muhlama, a bubbling dish of melted cheese, butter, and cornmeal that is the perfect fuel before a mountain hike.
