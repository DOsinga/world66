---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

The seafront and marina are wall-to-wall restaurants, most of them cheerful tourist places serving a mix of Turkish grills, fish and pasta. For something more local, walk five minutes inland from the old town, where the *lokantas* around the bazaar do proper Turkish home cooking — *kebabs*, *meze*, grilled fish straight off the boat — at half the price. Look out for the local speciality, pine honey (*çam balı*), drizzled over kaymak and yoghurt. As a rough rule, anywhere with a menu in six languages and a waiter pulling tourists in off the street is to be avoided.
