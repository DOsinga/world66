Yaşar Kemal's *Memed My Hawk* (1955) is the most celebrated Turkish novel of the twentieth century and one of the great outlaw epics of world literature. Set in the Taurus Mountains of southern Turkey, it tells the story of Memed, a young peasant who becomes a brigand after fleeing the brutal landlord who controls his village.

The book captures the rhythms of Turkish rural life — the feudal grip of the agha class, the solidarity and fatalism of mountain communities, the landscape of the Çukurova plain — with a storyteller's directness. Kemal wrote it in the tradition of the Turkish ashik (wandering bard), and the prose carries that oral quality throughout. It's a useful companion to any journey through rural Anatolia or the Mediterranean coast of Turkey, providing the human and historical context that guidebooks rarely manage.
