Olympos is a backpacker favourite on the Lycian coast of [Turkey](/asia/turkey), set in a forested valley that runs down to a long pebble beach. The ruins of ancient Olympos — Lycian tombs, a Roman theatre, bath houses — are scattered through the undergrowth along the stream, giving the place an Indiana Jones atmosphere.

The famous tree houses (bungalows built on stilts among the trees) are the signature accommodation, though standards vary widely. The beach itself is beautiful, backed by steep wooded cliffs on both sides. At night, many visitors hike up to the Chimaera — natural gas flames that have burned from the mountainside since antiquity.

Olympos is about 80 km southwest of [Antalya](/asia/turkey/antalya) and can be reached by minibus from the main coast road.
