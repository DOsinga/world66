Tekirdağ is a port city on the northern shore of the Sea of Marmara, about 150 km west of Istanbul in Thrace (European Turkey). It is best known in Turkey for two things: **Tekirdağ Rakısı**, a nationally celebrated brand of rakı (anise-flavoured spirit, 45% alcohol, served diluted with cold water), and **Tekirdağ köfte**, the local style of meatball that has been exported to restaurants across Turkey.

The city itself has a pleasant seafront promenade and a modest historic centre. The **Namık Kemal Museum**, in the house where the Ottoman poet and nationalist was imprisoned in 1873, is the main cultural sight. The old Greek neighbourhood has characteristic architecture from the Ottoman period.

For a more scenic experience, the coastal road south of Tekirdağ runs through Kumbağ and along the shore toward Saros Bay, passing through wine-producing villages (Mürefte, Şarköy) and vineyard-covered hills. The Saros Bay area has clear water and is popular with Turkish summer visitors.

Tekirdağ is an easy day trip from Istanbul or a worthwhile stop on the way to Gallipoli and the Dardanelles.
