Chardzhou — now officially known as Türkmenabat — is [Turkmenistan](/asia/turkmenistan)'s second-largest city, sitting on the banks of the Amu Darya river close to the Uzbekistan border. With a population of around 250,000, it is the main urban centre of eastern Turkmenistan and the country's most important border crossing point.

Most travelers encounter Chardzhou as a transit stop on the journey between Turkmenistan and [Uzbekistan](/asia/uzbekistan). The Trans-Caspian Railway passes through on its way to Tashkent, and the Farap border crossing to the south handles most overland traffic. The train journey from [Ashgabat](/asia/turkmenistan/ashgabat) takes about 10 hours across the Karakum Desert, passing through some of Central Asia's most desolate landscape.

The city itself is a typical Soviet-era Central Asian town — wide boulevards, concrete apartment blocks, and a large bazaar that is the liveliest spot in town. The Amu Darya riverbank is pleasant for a stroll in the cooler months, and the covered bazaar sells carpets, dried fruit, and melons from the surrounding Lebap province. As with all of Turkmenistan, independent travel requires a guide and a letter of invitation, so your time here will likely be structured by your tour operator.
