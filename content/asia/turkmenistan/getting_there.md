---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Getting into Turkmenistan requires planning well in advance. Virtually all nationalities need a visa. Until recently that meant obtaining a letter of invitation from a licensed Turkmen travel agency — which in practice meant booking a tour. Since April 2025 an e-visa system has been introduced that allows travelers to apply online without an invitation letter, but most visitors still work through a registered agency to satisfy requirements around pre-arranged accommodation and transport. Check current conditions before you go; the rules have changed often.

Standard visa processing takes 10–14 working days; express processing is 3–7 days. Your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond your planned departure date. Restricted zones — border areas and much of the Caspian coast — require additional permits.

**By air**

Ashgabat Airport (ASB) is the main international gateway, about 4 km north of the city center. Turkmenistan Airlines operates international routes to Istanbul, Moscow, Frankfurt, Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and a handful of other cities. Turkish Airlines also flies to Ashgabat from Istanbul. From London the flight takes about six and a half hours; from Istanbul, two and a half hours; from Dubai, around two hours.

**By sea**

Ferries cross the Caspian between [Turkmenbashi](/asia/turkmenistan/turkmenbashi) and Baku in Azerbaijan. The crossing is notoriously irregular — boats leave when they are ready, which can mean waiting 24–48 hours at the port. Budget time generously. It remains a classic Central Asian adventure.

**By rail**

The Trans-Caspian Railway enters Turkmenistan at [Turkmenbashi](/asia/turkmenistan/turkmenbashi) and runs east through [Ashgabat](/asia/turkmenistan/ashgabat) to [Chardzhou](/asia/turkmenistan/chardzhou), where it crosses into Uzbekistan. A connection to the Iranian rail network was completed in 1996, making overland travel toward Turkey possible in principle.

**By road**

Road crossings exist into Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, and Iran. There is also a road south from [Mary](/asia/turkmenistan/mary) toward Herat in Afghanistan, though the Afghan border situation changes frequently. Verify current conditions for all border crossings before departure.
