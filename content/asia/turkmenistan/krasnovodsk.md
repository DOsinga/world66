Krasnovodsk is the former Soviet-era name for the Caspian port city now called [Turkmenbashi](/asia/turkmenistan/turkmenbashi), located on [Turkmenistan](/asia/turkmenistan)'s western coast. The city was renamed in 1993 when President Saparmurat Niyazov gave it his own title, "Turkmenbashi" — Leader of the Turkmens.

Founded in 1869 as a Russian military outpost, Krasnovodsk served as the western terminus of the Trans-Caspian Railway and the main Caspian Sea port for Central Asia. The railway connection to the port made it strategically important throughout the Soviet period, and ferries still connect the city to Baku in [Azerbaijan](/asia/caucasus/azerbaijan) across the Caspian. The old Russian fort and the 19th-century town layout near the harbour are remnants of the city's imperial origins.

Today the city is better known under its modern name. See [Turkmenbashi](/asia/turkmenistan/turkmenbashi) for current practical information on visiting.
