**Visas**

All nationalities require a visa. Since April 2025 an e-visa system allows online applications for stays up to 30 days. Previously, a letter of invitation from a licensed Turkmen agency was required. In either case, most independent travelers arrange their trip through a local tour operator who handles visa sponsorship, accommodation, and a guide. Truly independent travel — showing up and making it up as you go — is not permitted.

**Currency**

The currency is the Turkmenistan Manat (TMT). One manat divides into 100 tenge. The official exchange rate is fixed at approximately 3.5 TMT to 1 US dollar, but a parallel market exists with a significantly better rate. Exchange at Ashgabat airport, banks, or larger hotels. Cash is the only practical payment method — credit and debit cards are rarely accepted anywhere.

Bring clean, undamaged US dollar bills printed after 2009. Smaller denominations are useful as change can be hard to get.

**Safety**

Turkmenistan is, in terms of street crime, one of the safer places in Central Asia. The risk to travelers is more bureaucratic than physical: photographing government buildings, military installations, or anything the authorities deem sensitive can cause serious trouble. Err heavily on the side of caution with a camera. Keep copies of your documents with you at all times.

**Health**

Tap water is not reliable. Drink bottled water. Medical facilities outside Ashgabat are basic. Take out comprehensive travel insurance with medical evacuation cover.

**Communications**

Internet access is heavily filtered and slow. A VPN is advisable but technically restricted. Mobile coverage is limited outside major cities. SIM cards are available for foreigners at Ashgabat airport.

**Language**

Turkmen is the official language. Russian remains widely understood, especially among older residents and in urban areas. English is rare outside Ashgabat hotels and tour agencies.
