---

Turkmenistan's most extraordinary sight is also its most remote. The **Darvaza Gas Crater** — known informally as the "Gate to Hell" — sits in the Karakum Desert about 260 km north of Ashgabat. In the early 1970s a Soviet drilling operation broke through into a natural gas cavern; the ground collapsed, and engineers set the escaping gas alight to prevent dangerous build-up. It was supposed to burn out within weeks. Over fifty years later it is still burning, a pit about 70 meters across glowing orange in the dark desert night. Most visitors camp on the rim. The drive through the desert is itself memorable.

In [Mary](/asia/turkmenistan/mary), the ancient city of **Merv** is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the great archaeological landscapes of Central Asia. What was once one of the largest cities in the medieval world — a great Silk Road hub that Genghis Khan destroyed in 1221 — is now a vast expanse of mud-brick walls, fortresses, and mausoleums rising from the plain. The **Sultan Sanjar Mausoleum** (12th century, with its distinctive turquoise dome) is the best-preserved structure and worth the journey alone. Entry is inexpensive and the site is rarely crowded.

**Kunya-Urgench** in the far north was the capital of the Khwarazmian Empire and met the same fate as Merv at Mongol hands. Its surviving monuments — including the **Kutlug-Timur Minaret**, probably the tallest medieval minaret in Central Asia at 60 meters — stand in a flat desert landscape that amplifies their isolation and scale. Also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

[Ashgabat](/asia/turkmenistan/ashgabat) repays a day or two even if marble boulevards and gold statues are not your usual thing. The **Turkmenbashi Ruhy Mosque** is the largest in Central Asia and genuinely impressive in scale. The **National Carpet Museum** houses the world's largest hand-woven carpet (over 300 square meters). The **National Museum of History** covers Silk Road and pre-Soviet history. The city's monumental axis — especially at dusk, when the white marble turns pink — is something you will not see anywhere else.
