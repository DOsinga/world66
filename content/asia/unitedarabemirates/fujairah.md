Fujairah is the UAE's quiet outlier. While Dubai and Abu Dhabi chase skylines and superlatives, this small emirate on the Gulf of Oman coast goes about its business against a backdrop of the rugged Hajar Mountains. It is the only emirate that sits entirely on the eastern seaboard, which means Indian Ocean waters instead of the shallow Persian Gulf — clearer, deeper, and better for diving.

The city itself is compact and commercial, built around a modest centre of government buildings and souqs. But the real draw is what surrounds it: rocky wadis that snake into the mountains, long stretches of beach backed by resort hotels, and a coastline rich with marine life. The 360-year-old Fujairah Fort watches over it all from its hilltop perch, a reminder that this was a fishing settlement long before oil money reshaped the region.

Fujairah works well as a weekend escape from Dubai — just two hours by car — or as a base for diving, hiking, and beach days. It does not have the nightlife or shopping of the bigger emirates, but that is exactly the point. Come here to slow down, snorkel off Snoopy Island, hike a wadi at dawn, and eat fresh seafood while the sun drops behind the mountains.
