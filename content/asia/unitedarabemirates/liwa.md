Liwa is a string of oasis settlements along the northern edge of the Rub al Khali — the Empty Quarter — in the Western Region of Abu Dhabi. It is the ancestral homeland of the ruling families of both Abu Dhabi and Dubai, and the landscape is as dramatic as anywhere in the [UAE](/asia/unitedarabemirates): enormous orange sand dunes rising to over 300 metres, date palm plantations in the valleys between them, and very little else.

The main reason to come here is the desert itself. The dunes around Moreeb Hill (Tal Moreeb), one of the tallest in the world, are popular for dune bashing and sandboarding. The Liwa area also hosts a major date festival each summer. The Qasr Al Sarab resort, built to look like a desert fortress, sits among the dunes south of town and is worth seeing even if you are not staying there.

Liwa is about 300 kilometres southwest of Abu Dhabi city, roughly a three-hour drive. There is no public transport — you will need a car, preferably a 4x4 if you plan to go off-road. Bring water and fuel; services are sparse once you leave the main road.
