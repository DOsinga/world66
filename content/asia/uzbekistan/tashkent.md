Tashkent is the capital of Uzbekistan but lacks the headline attractions of [Samarkand](/asia/uzbekistan/samarkand) and [Bukhara](/asia/uzbekistan/bukhara). A devastating earthquake in 1966 levelled much of the old city, and the Soviets seized the moment — calling in volunteer workers from across the USSR to rebuild. Thousands came and stayed, turning the ancient Silk Road city into a modern, Soviet, and rather Russian town.

The result is not unpleasant. There may not be many historical sights, but Tashkent has a few genuinely good museums, wide tree-lined boulevards, parks with fountains, and imposing Stalinist architecture that is impressive on its own terms. The metro stations alone are worth a visit, decorated with marble, chandeliers, and ornate mosaics.

Tashkent is also an important transport hub for Central Asia. There are internal flights to Khiva, Bukhara, and Samarkand, and overland routes into Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan pass through here.
