Uzbekistan's southernmost city, sitting right on the Afghan border where the Amu Darya river marks the line between the two countries. Termez is one of the hottest cities in Central Asia — summer temperatures hit 50°C — and one of the least visited by travellers, which is a shame.

The area has been inhabited since the Bactrian period in the first century BC, and the remains of a 9th-century fortress and Buddhist pottery finds point to a layered history. Termez served as a major transit point during the Soviet occupation of Afghanistan and still has a frontier feel. The highway between Russia and Afghanistan passes through the city.
