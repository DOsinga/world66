Ba Be National Park, in Bac Kan province about 250 km north of Hanoi, is centred on Ba Be Lake — the name means "three lakes", though the lake is a single continuous body of water 8 km long and up to 800 m wide. At 178 m above sea level, it is Vietnam's only significant natural mountain lake. The park covers 7,610 ha of limestone karst landscape, with peaks rising to over 1,000 m, deep river valleys, and dozens of caves, the largest being Phuong Cave, through which the Nang river flows.

The forest is a mix of limestone karst forest and lowland evergreen forest. The park supports several globally threatened species, including Owston's Civet and Francois's Leaf Monkey. Ba Be is also notable for its freshwater biodiversity, with a number of endemic fish species.

Boat trips on the lake and along the Nang river are the main activity for visitors, passing through limestone scenery and calling at the Dau Dang waterfall. Trekking trails lead into the forest and to the villages of the Tay, Dao, and H'Mong minority peoples who live in and around the park. Most visitors stay in the small settlement at Cho Ra or in homestays in Pac Ngoi village on the lakeside. Ba Be is a full-day journey from Hanoi by bus, or quicker by private vehicle.
