---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Vietnam's coastline stretches for over 3,000 kilometers from the Gulf of Tonkin to the Gulf of Thailand, and beach quality improves dramatically as you head south. The northern beaches are functional rather than beautiful; the central and southern coasts have some of the best in Southeast Asia.

**Halong Bay** offers small beaches on the islands, best reached by kayak from a junk boat. The scenery of limestone karsts rising from the water is exceptional; the beaches themselves are secondary to the landscape.

**My Khe (China Beach)** near Da Nang has good surf, especially in November when waves can reach two meters. It's a working local beach — Vietnamese families swim and play football here — rather than a resort scene, though that's changing with development.

**Lang Co** is a sliver of sand on a peninsula between Da Nang and Hue, backed by a lagoon and framed by mountains. One of the most dramatically situated beaches in the country and often overlooked.

**An Bang and Cua Dai** near Hoi An are calmer and less developed than the Da Nang beaches, and benefit from the charm of being close to the ancient town. Note that erosion has taken a toll on Cua Dai in recent years.

**Doc Let**, about 50km north of Nha Trang, has shallow turquoise water and a fraction of the crowds. It's the kind of beach Nha Trang used to be before the development arrived.

**Nha Trang** is Vietnam's most developed beach resort city. Busy, commercial, and backed by a strip of large hotels. The bay is beautiful and the islands offshore offer snorkeling and diving. Good if you want facilities and nightlife; not the place for seclusion.

**Mui Ne** has gone through cycles of development. Windsurfing and kitesurfing are the main draws (the steady winds from November to May are some of the best in Asia). The famous red and white sand dunes are nearby. The beach town itself has stretched into a long resort strip; it's more relaxed than Nha Trang.

**Phu Quoc**, Vietnam's largest island in the Gulf of Thailand, has the best beaches in the country: fine white sand, warm clear water, and spectacular sunsets (the only spot in Vietnam where you can watch the sun set over the sea). Development has accelerated significantly since an international airport opened, but the west coast beaches around Ong Lang and the north remain quieter.
