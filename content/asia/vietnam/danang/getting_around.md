As the third largest city in Vietnam, **Da Nang transportation** options are wide and varied. If, like most travelers, you have a limited amount of time to devote to seeing the various sites and attractions in and around Da Nang, you'll be well served to research the various transportation modes that are available to you in order to find the most efficient, cheapest and safest options. Read on for a brief overview.

**Taxis**

The most common way of getting around if you're a traveler in Da Nang is by taxi. Taxis are officially licensed by the government, and a certificate should be readily visible. You may run into problems with some taxi drivers who are reluctant to use a meter. These drivers know that you're likely to take the easier choice, even if it means that you'll end up paying more. Insist on having the meter run while you are driving; if the driver refuses, simply find another taxi.

**Buses**

Buses are dirty, poorly managed and generally not reliable in Vietnam. Avoid them if possible.

**Scooters and Motorbikes**

The least safe option for traveling as a tourist is by scooter or motorbike. Many Da Nang residents use these as a mode of transportation (in fact, it's one of the most distinctive and recognizable parts of Vietnamese city life), but the traffic rules are nonexistent, and it's very dangerous for a tourist to attempt to drive alongside other scooters.

For more information about the transportation options in Da Nang, speak with a representative at your local hotel or tourism board.
