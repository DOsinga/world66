title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Vietnam's major cities all have worthwhile day trips nearby. A few of the most popular:

## From Hanoi

**Halong Bay** is the classic, though it's better done as an overnight or two-night trip rather than a frantic day excursion. For those short on time, Ninh Binh (2–3 hours south) offers similar karst limestone scenery on water — the Tam Coc boat trip through rice paddies is beautiful, and Trang An is a UNESCO-listed cave and valley system. Hoa Lu, the ancient capital, is an easy add-on.

**Bat Trang** (20km from Hanoi) is a pottery village that has been producing ceramics for 700 years, accessible by local bus or motorbike.

## From Ho Chi Minh City

**Cu Chi Tunnels** — 200km of underground tunnels used by Viet Cong fighters during the American war. The site is heavily touristed but genuinely moving. The Ben Dinh section (slightly smaller) is less crowded than Ben Duoc.

**Tay Ninh and the Cao Dai Temple** — the Great Caodai Temple is one of the most architecturally singular buildings in Southeast Asia, a flamboyant synthesis of Buddhist, Taoist, Catholic, and other influences. The noon ceremony (11:45am) is open to visitors. About 100km northwest of the city.

**Mekong Delta** — dozens of tour operators run boat trips through the canals and waterways around My Tho, Ben Tre, and Vinh Long. The more time you have, the further you can get from the day-trip crowds. An overnight stay in Can Tho (the delta's main city) gives a much better sense of the region than a rushed day trip.
