---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Hanoi"
type: section
---

Although **Hanoi bars and cafes** are a far cry from the hustle and bustle of Ho Chi Minh City's nightlife and locals tend to get to bed early, there are still some great spots where you can eat, drink, be merry or simply chill out.

**Noc Gau**

One of the real highlights is late-night street cafes serving 'noc gau' which is a delicious non-alcoholic beverage made from rice and which tastes strangely like sweet peanut butter. It was invented by a veteran of the Vietnam War and is an excuse for young Hanoians to gather together after hours.

**Something Stronger**

For something a little stronger, try great bars like the Polite Pub which plays Western music on its stereo, serves endless supplies of roasted peanuts with its great selection of local and foreign beers and offers free pool - arguably Vietnam's national sport. If you fancy a cocktail then the barmaid - who also happens to be a professional women's pool player - can make a great Long Island Ice Tea and Margarita.

**Ecelecticism**

Lac Viet Cafe is surely the most intriguing of all Hanoi bars and cafes. A cross between a library, cafe and a movie theater, patrons can borrow books by everyone from Toni Morrison to Robin Cook and feast on delicious coffees, bagels, ice cream and sandwiches. The location is fabulous too: on the edges of Hoan Kiem Lake. Of all Hanoi bars and cafes this is a must-see and a must-do.
