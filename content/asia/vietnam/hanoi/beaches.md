---
title: "Beaches in Hanoi"
type: section
---

The bustling, yet historical city of Hanoi offers some great beaches in north Vietnam. Beware that between May and September, the weather is very hot and sticky, so make sure to use plenty of sun screen and drink lots of water when visiting **Hanoi beaches**.

**Tra Co Beach**

One of your best options for seaside fun and easily accessible from Hanoi, Tra Co is beautifully sandy and features shallow water great for swimming. It is 17km long, but is still relatively small-scale as a resort. Some travelers have begun to complain that Tra Co has fallen victim to big-scale developments, and the massive influx of Chinese and Vietnamese tourists between May and August has created a slightly sleazy environment of massage parlors. However, these are avoidable if they aren't your sort of thing, and you can just enjoy a level of peace and tranquility rare amongst Hanoi beaches.

**Mong Cai Beach
**

Slightly busier and more rock n roll than Tra Co, Mong Cai boasts romantic sand dunes and a fascinating fishermen's village. It is also a compelling center of cultural exchange with a mixture of Chinese and Vietnamese cuisine, customs, attitudes and architecture. Indeed, this most delightful of Hanoi beaches also serves as a border crossing into China. The theme of Mong Cai city could be termed as 'international business', as you can find almost any commodity you want there.

**Bai Chay Beach**

Geographically the nearest beach to Hanoi, Bai Chay is extremely popular with local and foreign tourists at the weekends and in the summer holidays. It may not be as stunning as some of its south Vietnamese counterparts, but it certainly scores high in the cuisine stakes, with delicious local seafood a highlight. Don't bother coming here off-season, though, as temperatures can sink to as low as 15C.

**Halong Bay**

Another geographically closer option is the stunning Halong Bay, where you can find dozens of smaller beaches and a fair amount of solitude and relaxation. The pick of them is located on the islands in the center of the bay, although most of these islands are too jagged and inaccessible to really serve as good beaches. Your best bet is to visit such places by kayak - especially if you are feeling rugged and adventurous - as the bigger touring boats are too large to approach them, running the risk of running aground.
