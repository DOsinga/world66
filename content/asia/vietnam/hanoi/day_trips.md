Hanoi is the launching pad for most of northern Vietnam. **Halong Bay** and its limestone seascape is the classic trip — realistically an overnight cruise rather than a day trip. **Ninh Binh** (nicknamed "inland Halong") is easier, closer, and can be done in a long day: karst towers rising out of rice paddies, with rowing-boat trips through the caves at Tam Coc or Trang An. **Sapa** in the northern mountains takes longer to reach — overnight train to Lao Cai, then a winding road up — but rewards you with trekking and visits to Hmong, Dzao, and Tay villages.
