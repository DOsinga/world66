---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Eating in Hanoi is mostly about the street. The city's signature dishes — **pho bo** (beef noodle soup), **bun cha** (grilled pork with rice noodles), **banh cuon** (steamed rice rolls), **cha ca** (turmeric-marinated fish with dill) — are almost always better from a specialist street stall than from a sit-down restaurant. Most places open for one meal a day and close when they run out.

A few streets and institutions to know. **Cha Ca La Vong** on Cha Ca Street (Old Quarter) has been serving its one dish, turmeric fish, since the 19th century. **Bun Cha Huong Lien** is where Obama and Bourdain ate; the resulting "combo Obama" is a reliable tourist magnet but the place is legitimately good. **Pho Thin** on Lo Duc is the classic old-school pho counter. **Banh Mi 25** is the go-to for the Hanoi take on the French-Vietnamese sandwich.

For a proper sit-down dinner, La Badiane, Gia Nguyen, and Home Hanoi (all in or near the French Quarter) do elevated Vietnamese.
