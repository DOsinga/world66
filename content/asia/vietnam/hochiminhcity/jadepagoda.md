The Jade Emperor Pagoda (Chua Ngoc Hoang) is one of the most atmospheric temples in Ho Chi Minh City. Built by the Cantonese community in 1909, the pagoda is dedicated to the Jade Emperor, the supreme Taoist god. Inside, the air is thick with incense smoke and the rooms are packed with an elaborate collection of gilded wooden statues, papier-mache figures, and carved panels depicting Buddhist and Taoist divinities. The tortoise pool in the courtyard is considered good luck. Look for the striking Hall of Ten Hells, with its dioramas showing the punishments awaiting sinners in the afterlife. The pagoda is on Mai Thi Luu street in District 1, a short taxi ride from the city center.
