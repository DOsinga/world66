The heart of Hue. The Citadel is a massive walled fortress built by Emperor Gia Long in 1805, surrounded by a wide moat and 2.5 kilometres of walls. Inside lies the Imperial Enclosure, where the emperor conducted official business, and within that the Forbidden Purple City — once reserved for the emperor alone. Much was destroyed during the Tet Offensive in 1968, but the Ngo Mon Gate, Thai Hoa Palace, and the Nine Dynastic Urns survive intact. Ongoing restoration is slowly bringing other structures back.
