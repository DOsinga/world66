Hue Citadel was built by King Gia Long in 1805 and then upgraded by King Minh Mang in 1832. The citadel is surrounded by 2.5 km long wall and encircled by a large moat. The citadel can be entered through any of ten fortified gates each with their own bridge across the moat. Inside the citadel lies the Imperial Enclosure where the Emperor carried out his official functions. Out of the Imperial Enclosure's four gates, the most famous and the largest is the Ngo Mon gate which served as the main entrance. Within the Imperial Enclosure lies the Forbidden Purple city which was exclusively reserved for the Emperor.
