title: Thien Mu Pagoda
type: poi

The most iconic image of Hue — a seven-story octagonal tower overlooking the south bank of the Perfume River, about 4 kilometres upstream from the Citadel. Founded in 1601, it is the oldest pagoda in the city. Behind the tower is a garden and the monastery where Thich Quang Duc lived before his famous self-immolation in Saigon in 1963; the car he drove to the protest is on display here.
