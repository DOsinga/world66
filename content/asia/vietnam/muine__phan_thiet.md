Mui Ne Beach stretches along the coast about 300 km northeast of [Ho Chi Minh City](/asia/vietnam/ho_chi_minh_city), reachable only by road. A long strip of soft grey sand backed by low-rise hotels, bamboo bungalows, and coconut palms, it has been developed with more restraint than most Vietnamese beach resorts — good gardens and tree screening give each place a sense of seclusion.

The real character of Mui Ne comes from the contrast between the beach resort strip and the working fishing village at the far end. The small harbour is picturesque, and few tourists venture past the famous red and white sand dunes that roll for hectares behind the village. There is an interesting pagoda where followers of Vietnam's whale cult hold services and store skeletons of the massive sea mammals.

Mui Ne is one of the best kitesurfing and windsurfing spots in Southeast Asia. From November to May, strong north-northeast winds reach 30 mph and sometimes gust higher — serious conditions for experienced riders. The rest of the year brings a gentler southwest breeze, ideal for beginners. Hon Rom beach, 10 km away, offers bigger waves during the windy season.

Between July and October, the Phan Thiet fishing boats go out at night and form a magnificent display of lights visible from shore. The local seafood is excellent — the restaurants lining the inland side of the main road serve Vietnamese and international menus at a range of prices.
