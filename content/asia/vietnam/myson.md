My Son is the most important Cham archaeological site in Vietnam — a cluster of red-brick temple towers set in a narrow valley surrounded by jungle-covered mountains, about 40 kilometres southwest of [Hoi An](/asia/vietnam/hoian). Built between the 4th and 13th centuries as the religious and political capital of the Champa kingdom, My Son was the equivalent of [Angkor](/asia/cambodia/angkorwat) for the Cham civilisation.

The towers and sanctuaries are Hindu temples, mostly dedicated to Shiva, and the stone carvings that survive are remarkably detailed despite centuries of weather and war. American bombing during the Vietnam War destroyed many of the structures, but what remains is still striking — particularly Groups B, C, and D, where you can see carved lintels, mythological reliefs, and the distinctive Cham tower silhouette.

Most visitors come on a half-day trip from Hoi An. The site opens early, and the first hours of the morning, before the tour buses arrive, are the best time to explore. The drive through the countryside and river valley is part of the experience.
