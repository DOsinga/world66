Thanh Hoa is a large province in north-central Vietnam, bordering Laos to the west and the South China Sea to the east. It sits between the Red River Delta and the Central Highlands, a transitional zone of mountains, river valleys, and coastal lowland. The province is home to around 3.5 million people from nine ethnic groups, including Kinh, Thai, Muong, and H'mong communities concentrated in the highland districts.

The provincial capital, also called Thanh Hoa, is 153 km south of Hanoi on National Highway 1 and served by the main north-south rail line. Sam Son Beach, 16 km to the east, is the province's most visited destination — a long stretch of sand popular with summer crowds from Hanoi.

The mountain interior holds more for travellers willing to go further. Pu Luong Nature Reserve in the north-west protects limestone karst and primary forest with some of the best trekking in the region. The economy of the province runs on forestry, marine resources, and minerals.
