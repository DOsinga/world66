The Mekong Delta starts about two hours southwest of Ho Chi Minh City and feels like a different country — flat, green, crisscrossed by canals, and oriented entirely around water. Most day trips head to either My Tho or Cai Be, both reachable in a morning.

My Tho is the closer option. Tours typically include a boat ride through narrow palm-lined canals, stops at fruit orchards and coconut candy workshops, and lunch on one of the small islands in the Tien River. Cai Be, a bit further out, is known for its floating market where vendors sell produce directly from their boats, though the market has shrunk as road transport has improved.

Organised tours from HCMC are cheap and easy to book from any hotel or travel office in District 1. If you'd rather go independently, buses to My Tho leave from the Mien Tay bus station and take around ninety minutes. For a deeper experience, consider an overnight trip that continues to Can Tho, which has the delta's largest and most photogenic floating market at Cai Rang.
