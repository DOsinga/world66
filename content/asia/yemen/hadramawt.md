Hadramawt is a vast wadi region in eastern Yemen, stretching about 560 kilometres from the Arabian Sea to the Saudi border. It is one of the most spectacular landscapes in the Arabian Peninsula — a deep canyon cut through a limestone plateau, with ancient mud-brick tower cities perched along its walls.

The most remarkable of these is [Shibam](/asia/yemen/shibam), a 16th-century walled city of skyscraper-like mud towers that has been called the "Manhattan of the desert." Seyun has a white-washed sultan's palace that rises incongruously from the flat valley floor, now a museum. Tarim is known for its hundreds of mosques and its traditional Hadrami merchant architecture. Given the ongoing conflict in Yemen, travel to this region requires careful assessment of current security conditions.
