About forty minutes by car from Sana'a, the Rock Palace of Wadi Dhahr is the most photographed image in Yemen — an imam's summer residence built directly on and around a pinnacle of rock, rising from the valley floor as if growing out of it. The building is five storeys and was constructed in the 1930s on the foundations of a much older structure. The valley below is green and terraced, a striking contrast to the arid plateau. The interior is open to visitors and gives a vivid sense of how Yemeni elite architecture looked at its most theatrical.
