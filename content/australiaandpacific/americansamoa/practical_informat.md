---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency:** American Samoa uses the US dollar. Cash is king — ATMs exist but are limited to a few locations on Tutuila. The Territorial Bank of American Samoa (TBAS) has branches in Utulei and Tafuna with ATMs. Bring enough cash, especially if you're heading to the Manu'a Islands.

**Entry requirements:** US citizens and nationals enter freely. Citizens of Australia, New Zealand, the UK, and most other developed nations receive a 30-day permit on arrival. Other nationalities should check visa requirements before traveling. All visitors need a valid passport, a return or onward ticket, and sufficient funds. A health declaration form may be required on arrival.

**Phone and internet:** The territory uses US phone infrastructure — US SIM cards work without roaming charges for American visitors. Internet access exists but is limited outside Pago Pago.

**Electricity:** Same as the US: 120V, 60Hz, standard American plugs.

**Time zone:** UTC-11.

**Language:** English and Samoan are both official languages. Most people speak both. English is understood everywhere.

**Health:** No vaccinations are required for entry. The standard of medical care on Tutuila is adequate for basic needs. Bring any prescription medication you need, as supplies may be limited. Tap water is generally safe to drink in Pago Pago.

**Customs and respect:** Fa'a Samoa — the Samoan way — is not just a phrase. Dress modestly, especially in villages and when visiting churches. Avoid entering a village on a Sunday without an invitation. Remove shoes before entering a home. Ask permission before photographing people. When walking through a village during evening prayer (sa), stop and be still until it ends.
