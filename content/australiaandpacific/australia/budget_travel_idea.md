---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Australia"
type: section
---

Australia is not a cheap country. Cities are expensive, fuel is expensive, and the sheer size of the place means you can spend a lot just getting from one thing to another. But there are ways to travel it on a limited budget.

**Working Holiday visas** are the most popular mechanism for stretching time in Australia. Citizens of around 40 countries (including the UK, Ireland, Germany, France, Japan, and Canada) aged under 30 can get a one-year working holiday visa. A second and third year are available if you complete regional agricultural work. This allows travellers to fund a long trip with work in hostels, restaurants, farms, or whatever comes up.

**Hostels** are excellent and widespread in Australia, with a strong backpacker network along the east coast. YHA properties are reliable; smaller independents vary. Dorm beds in Sydney or Melbourne run AUD 30–45/night; in smaller towns less.

**Campervan or camping:** National parks have excellent campgrounds, often in spectacular settings, with fees of AUD 10–30/night. Camping is also free in some state forests and on Crown land. A second-hand campervan is the classic budget option for long trips; the backpacker network in Sydney (particularly at King's Cross and Ultimo) has a second-hand vehicle market.

**The east coast** from Melbourne to Cairns is the classic backpacker route and has the best infrastructure for budget travel — plentiful hostels, bus passes (Greyhound), and enough people doing the same trip that you'll find company and shared transport costs easily.

**Couchsurfing and WWOOFing** (Willing Workers on Organic Farms) provide free accommodation in exchange for a few hours of daily work, and are a useful way to spend time in rural Australia without spending much money.

**Food:** Supermarkets (Woolworths and Coles are the main chains) are well-stocked. Markets and cheap Asian or Vietnamese restaurants in city suburbs offer excellent cheap meals — pho or banh mi in Sydney's Cabramatta or Melbourne's Richmond can cost AUD 10–12. Avoid tourist-strip restaurants in city centres.
