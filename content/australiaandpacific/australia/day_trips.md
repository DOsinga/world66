---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Given Australia's scale, "day trips" typically means excursions from the major cities. Each state capital has good options within a couple of hours.

**From Sydney:** The Blue Mountains are 90 minutes by train from Central Station. The Three Sisters rock formation at Katoomba is the famous viewpoint, but the real appeal is the walking tracks that drop into the valley — forested gorges, waterfalls, and a genuine sense of wilderness surprisingly close to a major city. The Hunter Valley wine region is a two-hour drive north; cellar doors open to visitors most days. The Royal National Park to the south has clifftop walks and isolated beaches reachable by train and foot.

**From Melbourne:** The Yarra Valley, an hour east of the city, has wineries, the Healesville wildlife sanctuary, and good food. The Mornington Peninsula (also about an hour) offers hot springs, ocean beaches, and more wineries. The Dandenong Ranges have rainforest walks and the charming Puffing Billy steam railway.

**From Brisbane:** The Gold Coast is 45 minutes south by train — theme parks, surf beaches, and busy restaurant strips. The Sunshine Coast (north) is quieter and more pleasant in many ways; Noosa has good surf, national park walks, and a relaxed atmosphere. The Glass House Mountains, a cluster of volcanic plugs rising from farmland north of Brisbane, can be seen in a half day.

**From Adelaide:** The Barossa Valley (an hour north) is one of Australia's most famous wine regions, with Shiraz its signature variety. McLaren Vale (45 minutes south) is a more intimate wine region with beautiful coastal views. The Adelaide Hills have walking trails, small towns, and the excellent Hahndorf — an old German settlement with good food.

**From Perth:** Fremantle is essentially part of Perth now but retains its own port-city character — the markets, the cappuccino strip, and the historic prison are all worth a half day. The Swan Valley wine region is 30 minutes east. Margaret River, a world-class wine and surf region, is three hours south and better suited to a weekend.

**From Darwin:** Litchfield National Park (1.5 hours south) has accessible waterfalls and swimming holes, including the excellent Florence and Wangi Falls. Unlike Kakadu, Litchfield doesn't require permits and is easier to visit independently on a day trip.
