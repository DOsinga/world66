---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Australia"
type: section
---

Australia works well for family travel. It's safe, English-speaking, has excellent infrastructure in cities, and the wildlife — kangaroos, koalas, wombats, echidnas — is a genuine draw for children who have grown up on documentaries.

**Wildlife parks and sanctuaries** are everywhere and generally excellent. Healesville Sanctuary in Victoria's Yarra Valley specialises in native species and has well-designed enclosures. Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary near Brisbane allows you to hold a koala and hand-feed kangaroos. Taronga Zoo in Sydney has harbour views and a good collection. Philip Island (Victoria) has the famous Little Penguin parade — hundreds of small penguins waddling ashore at dusk — which children love.

**Beaches** are central to Australian family holidays. Patrolled beaches with lifesavers present mean children can swim safely. The east coast has dozens of suitable options; Airlie Beach, the Gold Coast, and Noosa are family-friendly. For warmer water, try the Queensland coast from October to May (but note stinger risks in the far north).

**The interactive museums** in major cities are well suited to children. Scienceworks in Melbourne, the Australian Museum in Sydney, and Questacon (National Science and Technology Centre) in Canberra all have hands-on exhibits. MONA in Hobart has some content unsuitable for very young children — check the room descriptions.

**Self-driving** with children is entirely feasible and gives flexibility. Car seats are required by law; rental companies can provide them. Caravan parks (often called "holiday parks") have family cabins, playgrounds, and pools throughout the country.

**Long flights** are the main challenge of Australian family travel. From Europe or the US, even the shortest route involves 14+ hours in the air. Flying to Perth first (less than 2 hours from Singapore, shorter than Sydney from the US West Coast) can reduce flight fatigue. Direct flights from the UK to Perth take about 17 hours. Most airlines offer children's meals and entertainment; book bassinet seats well in advance for infants.
