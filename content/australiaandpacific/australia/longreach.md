Longreach is a small outback town in central Queensland, about 700 km west of Rockhampton. It sits at the edge of the channel country — flat, dry grassland country crossed by river channels that flood dramatically in wet years and shrink to nothing in dry ones.

The town is best known as the birthplace of Qantas. In 1921 a group of graziers and businessmen registered the Queensland and Northern Territory Aerial Service in Winton (nearby), and the airline's first purpose-built hangar was constructed here in Longreach. The **Qantas Founders Museum** is the main attraction — it houses restored historic aircraft including a Boeing 747, a 707, and a Constellation, and the guided tours are genuinely interesting.

The **Australian Stockman's Hall of Fame and Outback Heritage Centre** is a large heritage museum dedicated to the drovers, jackaroos, and Indigenous stockmen who worked the inland cattle stations. It's well-done and gives real context to the outback pastoral history.

Longreach makes a reasonable base for exploring the channel country and surrounding outback. The town has a small airport with connections to Brisbane.
