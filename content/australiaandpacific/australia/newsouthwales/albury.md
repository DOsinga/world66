Albury sits on the Murray River at the New South Wales–Victoria border, paired with its twin city Wodonga on the opposite bank. It is a common overnight stop on the highway between Sydney and Melbourne, about halfway along the Hume Motorway, but there is enough here to justify staying longer.

The town has a pleasant riverside setting, with walking and cycling trails along the Murray and through the Nail Can Hill bushland reserve on the edge of town. The Murray Art Museum Albury (MAMA) is one of the better regional galleries in New South Wales, and Noreuil Park along the riverbank is a good spot for a swim in summer. The surrounding region produces excellent wine, particularly around Rutherglen across the border in Victoria.
