Australia's easternmost point and one of its most famous beach destinations, Byron Bay has been pulling surfers, backpackers, and free spirits since the 1970s. The town sits at the base of a volcanic headland and is surrounded by some of the finest beaches on the New South Wales coast — Main Beach for swimming, Wategos for longboarding, and the breaks around the Cape for serious surfers.

The Cape Byron lighthouse walk is the thing to do: a 3.7 km coastal path around the headland with views up and down the coast, and near-certainty of spotting dolphins in the water below. Humpback whales pass by on migration in June and July.

The town itself is compact, with good cafes, a good Saturday market, and a lively but not overwhelming bar scene. It gets crowded in summer and over Easter. Come in autumn or early winter for uncrowded beaches and warm enough water to swim.
