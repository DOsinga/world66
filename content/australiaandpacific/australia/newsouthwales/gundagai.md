Gundagai is a small historic town on the Murrumbidgee River, about halfway between Sydney and Melbourne on the Hume Highway. It sits at the foot of Mount Parnassus, 387 km south-west of Sydney, and has been a stopping point for travelers since the days of bullock teams hauling wool across the continent.

The town is best known to Australians through folklore — the Dog on the Tuckerbox monument, five miles out of town, is one of those peculiarly Australian icons that every schoolchild knows. Beyond the legend, Gundagai has genuine historic character: a handsome courthouse, a prince's bridge, and the memory of one of Australia's deadliest floods.

It's a natural stop if you're driving between Sydney and Melbourne and want somewhere with more soul than a highway service centre.
