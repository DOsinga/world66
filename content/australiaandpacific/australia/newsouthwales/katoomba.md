Katoomba is the gateway to the Blue Mountains, perched on the edge of the Jamison Valley about 100 km west of [Sydney](/australiaandpacific/australia/newsouthwales/sydney). The town itself has a faded Art Deco charm — old cinemas, antique shops and cafes lining the main street — but the main attraction is the landscape that drops away dramatically at the town's edge.

**Echo Point** is the most visited viewpoint, looking across to the **Three Sisters**, three sandstone pillars that are the most photographed natural feature in [New South Wales](/australiaandpacific/australia/newsouthwales). From here, walking tracks descend into the valley through temperate rainforest. The **Giant Stairway** (800 steps) takes you down to the valley floor, and the Scenic Railway — the steepest incline railway in the world — brings you back up.

The Blue Mountains get their name from the blue haze created by oil vapour released by the eucalyptus forests. On a clear day the views from the cliff edges are extraordinary. Most visitors come as a day trip from Sydney, but staying a night or two lets you explore the quieter trails and enjoy the town's restaurants and galleries.
