Narooma sits on a narrow finger of land almost entirely surrounded by the Wagonga River estuary and the Pacific Ocean, on the New South Wales Sapphire Coast about 300 km south of Sydney. The Aboriginal name means "clear blue water," and the description holds: the waterways here are strikingly beautiful.

Fishing is the town's great preoccupation — commercial boats work the estuary while anglers line the banks for bream, flathead, and trevally. Big game fishing charters operate offshore. The calm waters of the inlet are also popular for kayaking, swimming, and stand-up paddleboarding. Offshore, Montague Island is a protected nature reserve with Australia's largest colony of little penguins and thousands of nesting seabirds; boat tours run from the wharf.

Narooma is a genuinely relaxed destination with few pretensions, and the drive along the coast highway through here — with ocean views and small fishing towns — is one of the pleasanter stretches of road on the south coast.
