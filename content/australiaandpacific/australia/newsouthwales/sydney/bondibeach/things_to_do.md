---
title: "Things to do in Bondi Beach"
type: section
---

One of Australia's most famous beaches, Bondi Beach is a little less
than a mile long (or approximately a kilometer). Bondi Beach is located
on the East Coast of Australia and is on the outskirts of Sydney.
Easily accessible by public transport, and heavily monitored by
lifeguards, Bondi Beach is the place to be seen.

Aside from the obvious sun, surf, swim and sand, Bondi Beach has a
lovely promenade connecting all the beaches. This walkway is ideal for
an evening stroll or early morning jog.

**The Iceberg Pub**

An icon of Bondi Beach is the Iceberg club. The Iceberg Club is
located at the South end of Bondi Beach, and above the Bondi Baths
swimming pool. The Iceberg club is a licensed swimming club with a
fabulous view of the beach.It's ideal for visitors looking for a dip in
the water during the winter season (June - August) when the ocean
temperature is just too frigid. You can swim year round at Icebergs.
You will also find gym, yoga, and massage services here. Entrance is $4.

**Pubs and Bars**

Bondi Beach is littered with pubs and bars, there is a little
something here for everyone. B B's Wine Bar is a great spot for nice
glass of Chardonnay on a hot day. Bungabar is a lively Mexican/Latin
bar with a little flair. The Rum Diaries is a great spot for a cold
drink and delicious tapas. If you are looking for a yummy pizza, then
Gelbison Pizzeria is your stop.

The Bondi Beach Markets are held every Sunday from 10am to 5pm at
the local public school on Campbell Parade, one block north of the main
shopping center, a good spot for the treasure hunters. You can expect
to find anything from arts and crafts, to second hand clothes.

**Walk from Bondi to Coogee Beach**

Take a coastal walk if you have time, estimated 4 hour walk. The
walk from Bondi to Coogee is a classic stroll. You will encounter
stunning views, white sand beaches, cliffs, and the old Waverley
Cemetary. Take a break at Tamarama along the walk for a cold beverage
along the water. Some refer to Coogee Beach as the backpacker capital
of the world, so be prepared! If you have time and the place isn't jam
packed, try to make your way to the top of the Beach Palace Hotel and
enjoy the view from the veranda.
