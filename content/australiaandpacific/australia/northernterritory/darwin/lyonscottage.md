The oldest surviving stone building in Darwin, Lyons Cottage was built in 1925 by the British Australian Telegraph Company as a residence for one of its managers. It now serves as a small local history museum, with displays on Darwin's colonial era and the telegraph communications that once connected Australia to the rest of the world. The cottage sits on the Esplanade overlooking the harbour. Entry is free, and there's a pleasant garden out front.
