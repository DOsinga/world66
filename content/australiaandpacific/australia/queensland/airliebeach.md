Airlie Beach is the gateway to the Whitsunday Islands, a small coastal town in tropical North Queensland that exists almost entirely to get people onto boats. The main strip is a short stretch of bars, tour booking offices, backpacker hostels, and dive shops, with a man-made lagoon pool on the foreshore that compensates for the stinger-season swimming restrictions.

The town itself is not the attraction — the Whitsundays are. Day trips and overnight sailing tours depart daily to Whitehaven Beach (consistently rated among the world's best), the fringing reefs of the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park, and the 74 islands of the archipelago. Most visitors spend a night or two in Airlie Beach before and after their island time.

The atmosphere is young, relaxed, and firmly backpacker-oriented, though the marina development at Abel Point has brought some upmarket dining and accommodation. The surrounding area offers bushwalks through Conway National Park and views over the Coral Sea from the hilltops above town.
