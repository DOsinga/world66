The Gold Coast is Queensland's beach capital: fifty kilometres of unbroken surf beach stretching from South Stradbroke Island down to the New South Wales border, backed by a glittering skyline that feels more Miami than Australia.

Surfers Paradise is the brash heart of it — high-rise towers, buzzing Cavill Avenue, and a beach wide enough to lose yourself on. Head south to Broadbeach for better restaurants and a calmer vibe, or keep going to Burleigh Heads, where a headland reserve separates two beaches and the surf is reliably good. Palm Beach and Currumbin are quieter still, popular with locals.

The hinterland is worth a day trip: the Gold Coast Hinterland rises sharply behind the strip, with Lamington National Park and its ancient rainforest just forty minutes inland. The contrast with the neon coast is striking.
