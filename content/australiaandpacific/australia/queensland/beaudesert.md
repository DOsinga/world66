Beaudesert is a small rural town in the **Scenic Rim**, the arc of mountain country south-west of Brisbane that traces the New South Wales border. It sits in the Logan River valley with a working cattle-and-dairy hinterland on one side and the basalt walls of the McPherson Range on the other — a landscape of green pastures, fig-lined creeks and old timber farmhouses.

For travellers it is mainly a base or a turn-off. The town is the obvious gateway to **Lamington National Park** at Binna Burra and O'Reilly's, where ancient Antarctic beech and Gondwanan rainforest cover the rim of an extinct volcano, and to the lakes and waterfalls of **Mount Barney National Park** further south. The town itself is a single main street with the usual country-town mix of pubs, a heritage museum, the Saturday morning farmers' market, and not much else. Stay if you want a quiet base for hiking the Scenic Rim; otherwise drive on up into the hills.
