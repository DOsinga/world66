Bundaberg sits on the Burnett River in southern Queensland, best known to the world for its dark rum — the Bundaberg Distillery has been producing it here since 1888, and the famous polar bear still graces every bottle. The city itself is a working agricultural hub, surrounded by sugarcane fields and macadamia orchards, with a relaxed pace that suits a day or two of exploration.

The real draw is what lies just offshore. Mon Repos Beach, about 15 kilometres east of the city centre, is one of Australia's most significant marine turtle nesting sites. Between November and March, loggerhead turtles haul themselves up the beach after dark to lay eggs, and rangers run nightly tours that get you up close without disturbing the animals. It is one of the most affecting wildlife experiences in Australia.

The Bundaberg Region also serves as a launching point for [Lady Elliot Island](/australiaandpacific/australia/queensland), the southernmost coral cay of the Great Barrier Reef. Day trips by light aircraft are available from the local airport. For the reef without the flight, [Bargara](/australiaandpacific/australia/queensland) and its surrounds offer accessible snorkelling and diving along the Coral Sea coast.
