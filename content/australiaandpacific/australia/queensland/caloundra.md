Caloundra sits at the southern end of Queensland's Sunshine Coast, where the Pumicestone Passage separates the mainland from Bribie Island. It is a relaxed seaside town with a string of beaches — from the calm swimming waters of Bulcock Beach in the town centre to the surf breaks at Kings Beach, Dicky Beach, and Moffat Beach further north.

The town is popular with families from Brisbane, about an hour's drive south, and has a quieter, less developed feel than the resort strips further up the coast at Mooloolaba and Noosa. The coastal boardwalk connecting Kings Beach to Caloundra Head is one of the best short walks in southeast Queensland, with humpback whale sightings common during the migration season from June to November.
