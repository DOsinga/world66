The Gold Coast stretches roughly 40 kilometres of beach from Coolangatta in the south to the outskirts of Brisbane in the north, with the high-rise towers of **Surfers Paradise** at its commercial centre. It has been Australia's premier domestic beach resort since the mid-20th century, and it shows: the infrastructure is dense, the beaches are genuinely outstanding, and the place makes no apologies for its purpose.

The waves are the main draw for many visitors. **Burleigh Heads** is the classic surf break, with a rainforest headland park behind the beach and some of the Gold Coast's best cafés and restaurants at its base. **Rainbow Bay** in Coolangatta is calmer and better for swimming. Away from the beach, theme parks cluster around the Coomera area — **Warner Bros. Movie World**, **Sea World**, and **Wet'n'Wild** are the main ones.

Day trips into the hinterland are worthwhile. **Springbrook National Park**, about 40 minutes inland, has ancient rainforest, waterfalls, and at night a remarkable natural phenomenon: thousands of bioluminescent glow-worms visible in rock overhangs. The drive up through the Tamborine Mountain area passes through fine scenery.
