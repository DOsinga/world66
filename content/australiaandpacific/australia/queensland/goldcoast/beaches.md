---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The Gold Coast's subtropical climate and near-constant sunshine make it one of Australia's best beach destinations. The coastline runs from the quiet bays around Coolangatta in the south to the bustling high-rise strip at Surfers Paradise and beyond.

**Rainbow Bay** in Coolangatta is protected from the prevailing trade winds, making it popular with both surfers and swimmers. The town is just five minutes from the airport and has a relaxed feel. The headland walks in this area offer spectacular coastal views.

**Burleigh Heads** is a rainforest headland jutting into the Pacific that has been a famous surfing spot since the 1960s. The point break is world-class, and the adjacent national park has a short but beautiful rainforest walk. Good cafes and restaurants overlook the break.

**Surfers Paradise** is the tourist hub — high-rises, nightlife, and crowds. Locals tend to avoid it in peak season, but the beach itself is long enough to absorb the numbers, and it remains one of Australia's premier nightlife strips.
