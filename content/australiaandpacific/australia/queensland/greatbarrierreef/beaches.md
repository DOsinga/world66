---
title: "Beaches in Great Barrier Reef"
type: section
---

The Great Barrier Reef stretches from the northern most tip of
Queensland, Cape York, down 3000 kilometres to Bundaberg. The reef
consists of 2,900 individual reefs and over 900 islands. This
magnificent part of the world also has some of the most spectacular
beaches seen anywhere. Protected by the outer reef, most of the beaches
north of Frazer Island in Queensland have little to no waves, which
mean perfect swimming and snorkelling conditions year round. The warm
turquoise waters of the Coral Sea rarely drop below 22 degrees Celsius
and sun tanning can be enjoyed 365 days a year. The only problem
with the beaches in North Queensland is the various seasonal stingers.
These can be extremely dangerous and in many cases even fatal. Many
people choose to swim with stinger suits to be on the safe side;
however this is much less of a problem if you are on the outer islands
of the reef.

**Whitehaven Beach**

Whitehaven Beach is located on an island in a group of islands
called the Whitsunday’s. The Whitsunday’s has 74 islands of which a few
have world-class resorts. The town on the mainland, which is renowned
among international backpackers is called Airlie Beach and is just over
an hour’s flight from Queensland’s capital, Brisbane. This beach has
some of the most beautiful sand and blue water in the world. Getting to
Whitehaven will consist of either a privately chartered boat or group
tour, which can be done from many of the islands and the mainland. This is a
must see for the whole family.

**Cape Tribulation**

Cape Tribulation is the only place in the world where two World
Heritage Sites meet. The greenery of the Daintree Rainforest hits the
beautiful waters of the Greet Barrier Reef. Cape Tribulation can be
reached from a day trip out of Cairns and will certainly be worth it
once you get here. Here you can snorkel the shallow reefs or waste the
day away reading on the on the white sandy beach.

**Four-Mile Beach**

Four Mile Beach is located in North Queensland’s resort town of Port
Douglas. This four-mile stretch of sandy beach is laced with tall palms
and crystal clear water. It also enjoys no development pressure and
gives a feeling of isolation. Not many amenities on this beach although
it is a very romantic way to spend a day with that special someone.
