Gympie is a gold-rush town in the hinterland of Queensland's Sunshine Coast, about 170 km north of Brisbane. The town owes its existence to gold discovered here in 1867 by James Nash — the find arguably saved the then-struggling colony of Queensland from bankruptcy. Gold mining continued until the mid-20th century, and Gympie still celebrates its heritage at the local gold mining museum.

Today Gympie is a service town for the surrounding agricultural region, known for its dairy farming and subtropical fruit growing. The annual Gympie Muster music festival, held in August, draws large crowds and is one of Queensland's major country music events. The town is a convenient stop on the Bruce Highway for travellers heading north to Noosa, the Sunshine Coast hinterland, or the Fraser Coast.
