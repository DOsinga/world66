Longreach sits in the red dust of outback Queensland, about 1,200 km west of the coast. It is a quintessential Australian bush town — wide streets, low buildings, enormous skies — and the birthplace of Qantas, which started here in 1920 as a two-plane mail service before becoming Australia's national airline.

The **Australian Stockman's Hall of Fame** is the town's main attraction, a museum celebrating the drovers, shearers, and station workers who shaped the outback. It is well done and worth several hours. Nearby, the **Qantas Founders Museum** has a retired 747 you can walk through, plus the original Qantas hangar.

Longreach is also a departure point for the Thomson River sunset cruises, where you drift past river red gums while watching the outback sky turn orange. The town makes a good overnight stop on the long drive between the coast and the Channel Country further west.
