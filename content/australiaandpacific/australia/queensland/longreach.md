Longreach is a classic Queensland outback town, about 1,200 kilometres northwest of [Brisbane](/australiaandpacific/australia/queensland/brisbane) on the banks of the Thomson River. It built its fortune on wool and, later, on the fact that Qantas was founded here in 1920 and based the airline in town through the 1920s. Today Longreach lives partly on cattle and sheep, partly on travellers driving the Matilda Highway.

The Qantas Founders Museum sits next to the original hangar at the airport and is genuinely worth the stop — the collection includes a Boeing 747 and a 707 that you can walk through, parked out in the red dirt. Across town, the Australian Stockman's Hall of Fame tells the story of the drovers, shearers, and station hands who built the outback pastoral industry. Sunset cruises on the Thomson River and bush-poetry dinners under the stars are the classic local outings.

The best time to visit is the cooler dry season from April to September. In summer, daytime temperatures routinely top 40°C and most travellers stay away.
