Palm Cove is a small beach village about 25 kilometres north of Cairns, strung along one of Queensland's most attractive stretches of coastline. A long esplanade shaded by ancient melaleuca trees runs beside a calm, usually swimmable beach — an unusual asset this far north, where stingers and crocodiles make many beaches off-limits for much of the year.

The village is compact and deliberately low-key. The main strip has a handful of good restaurants, a few bars, and some boutique shops, all within easy walking distance of the beach. It's a quieter alternative to Cairns for those who want a beachside base, and popular with couples and families alike.

From Palm Cove, the Great Barrier Reef is easily accessible by boat — several operators run day trips to the outer reef and to nearby Upolu Cay. The Daintree Rainforest is about an hour's drive north, making this a good base for exploring both of the region's World Heritage areas in a single trip.
