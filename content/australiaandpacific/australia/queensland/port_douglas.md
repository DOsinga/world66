Port Douglas is a small resort town on the Coral Sea coast, about 70 km north of Cairns along the Captain Cook Highway. The drive up is one of the best in Australia — the road hugs the coast with rainforest on one side and the sea on the other, passing through country that looks largely as it must have before Europeans arrived.

The town itself sits on a low peninsula between four-mile beach and Dickson Inlet. It is upmarket and relaxed, more so than Cairns, with good restaurants and a quieter pace. The main drag, Macrossan Street, fills up in season but never overwhelms.

Port Douglas is well-placed for two World Heritage areas. Reef trips to the outer Great Barrier Reef run daily from the marina — conditions are generally better here than at Cairns, as the continental shelf is closer. The Daintree Rainforest, the oldest tropical rainforest in the world, begins about 30 km north. Most visitors use Port Douglas as a base to explore both.
