Rockhampton sits on the Fitzroy River in central Queensland, roughly halfway up the Queensland coast between Brisbane and Cairns. It lies almost exactly on the Tropic of Capricorn — a fact marked by several roadside signs on the highway — and the climate has the tropical warmth of the north without quite reaching its humidity.

The city is the commercial centre of central Queensland's beef cattle industry, and it leans into this: the streets are lined with statues of Brahman bulls and the region produces a large share of Australia's beef. The Caves district to the north has several limestone cave systems open to visitors, including Capricorn Caves, which are known for their cathedral-like chambers.

Rockhampton serves as a practical base for visiting Great Keppel Island, accessible by ferry from Rosslyn Bay. The island has white-sand beaches and snorkelling over fringing reefs, and is quieter than better-known Queensland island resorts.
