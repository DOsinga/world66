Townsville is North Queensland's largest city and one of the main gateways to the Great Barrier Reef. It sits on the coast beneath the granite bulk of Castle Hill, with Magnetic Island — a national-park island with koalas, bays, and walking trails — visible just offshore and reachable by a 20-minute ferry.

The city itself is low-key but pleasant: the Strand is a long palm-lined waterfront promenade with a swimming lagoon, and Reef HQ is the world's largest living coral reef aquarium, useful if the weather is not cooperating for a reef trip. Townsville averages over 300 days of sunshine a year, making it one of the driest spots on the tropical Queensland coast.
