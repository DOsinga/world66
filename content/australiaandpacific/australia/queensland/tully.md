Tully is a small town in Queensland's Wet Tropics, about 140 km south of Cairns, and claims the title of Australia's wettest town — annual rainfall regularly exceeds 4 metres. The surrounding country is almost entirely sugarcane and banana plantations, which is the economic backbone of the area.

For travelers, the main draw is the Tully River. White-water rafting here is among the best in Queensland, with grade three and four rapids running through rainforest gorges. Day trips run from Cairns. The town itself has little to detain you, but the setting in the rainforest-clad hills is scenic and the drive south toward Mission Beach passes through beautiful country.
