Australia's headline attractions are spread across a continent, which means no single trip covers everything. Most visitors pick a coast (usually the east), a reef, and possibly the Red Centre.

**Uluru** (Ayers Rock) rises from the flat red desert of the Northern Territory with an abruptness that still surprises people who've seen photographs. The sandstone monolith is sacred to the Anangu people, who ask that visitors do not climb it — the climbing route has been permanently closed since 2019. Walking the base (10.6 km) at sunrise or sunset, when the colour shifts through orange, purple and crimson, is the right way to see it. The nearby Kata Tjuta domes are less famous but equally impressive. See: [Northern Territory](/australiaandpacific/australia/northernterritory).

**The Great Barrier Reef** is the world's largest coral system — 2,300 km of reef, islands, and marine park off the Queensland coast. Cairns is the main gateway; Airlie Beach (for the Whitsundays) is another. Snorkelling and diving are extraordinary, though coral bleaching from warming ocean temperatures has damaged parts of the reef significantly. What remains is still spectacular. See: [Queensland](/australiaandpacific/australia/queensland).

**Sydney** is the city most visitors picture when they think of Australia: the Opera House sails and Harbour Bridge framing one of the world's great urban waterfronts. The Rocks (the original 1788 settlement), Bondi Beach, the Royal Botanic Gardens, and the ferry rides across the harbour are all worth your time. See: [Sydney](/australiaandpacific/australia/newsouthwales/sydney).

**Kakadu National Park** in the Northern Territory is a UNESCO World Heritage site combining extraordinary Aboriginal rock art — some dating back 20,000 years — with wetlands full of birds and crocodiles, cascading waterfalls, and a raw sense of ancient landscape. Best visited in the dry season (May–September). See: [Northern Territory](/australiaandpacific/australia/northernterritory).

**The Great Ocean Road** in Victoria runs 243 km along a dramatic southern coastline, past the Twelve Apostles limestone stacks, Loch Ard Gorge, and forest-covered ranges. Koalas are common in the roadside trees near Kennett River. Drive it west from Torquay to Warrnambool, or east from Portland. See: [Victoria](/australiaandpacific/australia/victoria).

**Tasmania** is Australia's island state, with wilderness that rivals anything in the Southern Hemisphere — ancient rainforest, dolerite peaks, pristine rivers, and beaches of chalk-white sand. The Overland Track through Cradle Mountain is one of Australia's great multi-day walks. MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) in Hobart is the most provocative private museum in the country. See: [Tasmania](/australiaandpacific/australia/tasmania).

**The Daintree Rainforest** near Cairns is the world's oldest tropical rainforest, thought to be 180 million years old — older than the Amazon. It runs almost to the edge of the Coral Sea. Crocodiles inhabit the Daintree River.

**The Whitsunday Islands** offer 74 islands, most uninhabited, in the heart of the Barrier Reef. Whitehaven Beach — white silica sand, turquoise shallows — is consistently rated among the world's best beaches.
