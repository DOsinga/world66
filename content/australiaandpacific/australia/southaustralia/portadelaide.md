Port Adelaide is the historic maritime quarter of Adelaide, sitting about fourteen kilometers northwest of the city center on the Port River. Once the gateway for immigrants arriving in South Australia and the hub of the state's shipping trade, it fell into decline in the late twentieth century but has been reviving steadily, with old wool stores and warehouses being converted into apartments, cafes, and galleries while retaining genuine character. It feels grittier and more interesting than much of suburban Adelaide.

The South Australian Maritime Museum, housed in a beautifully restored bond store, is the highlight — it tells the story of the ships, settlers, and dock workers who built this port, with excellent exhibits and a lighthouse you can climb. The Port River is home to a resident pod of bottlenose dolphins, and dolphin-watching cruises run regularly from the wharf. The inner harbor area has a growing cluster of restaurants, and the Port Admiral Hotel, one of South Australia's oldest pubs, is a fine place for a beer. On weekends, the Fishermen's Wharf Market adds some bustle, and the nearby Semaphore beach and its old-fashioned seaside esplanade are a short walk or tram ride away.
