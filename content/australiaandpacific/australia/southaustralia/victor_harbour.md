Victor Harbor is the main holiday town on South Australia's Fleurieu Peninsula, about an hour's drive south of [Adelaide](/australiaandpacific/australia/southaustralia/adelaide). The town sits on Encounter Bay, where Matthew Flinders and Nicolas Baudin famously met in 1802, and looks out toward Granite Island — connected to the mainland by a wooden causeway that you can cross on foot or by horse-drawn tram.

Between June and October, southern right whales come into Encounter Bay to calve, and the Bluff headland is one of the best land-based whale-watching spots in Australia. The South Australian Whale Centre on Railway Terrace has exhibits and real-time sighting updates. Outside whale season, the town is a pleasant base for exploring the peninsula: the Cockle Train steam railway runs along the coast to Goolwa, and the wine regions of McLaren Vale and Langhorne Creek are within easy reach.

Victor Harbor (the town dropped the "u" from Harbour decades ago, though both spellings persist) has a relaxed, old-fashioned seaside feel — fish and chips on the foreshore, ice cream shops, and families on the beach. It is not glamorous, but it is genuine.
