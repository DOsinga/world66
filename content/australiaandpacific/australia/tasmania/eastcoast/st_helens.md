St Helens is the largest town on Tasmania's east coast, sitting at the head of Georges Bay where the Blue Derby mountain bike trails end and the Bay of Fires begins. It functions as the practical hub for the whole region — fuel, food, accommodation — and makes a good base whether you're coming off the trails or heading north along the coast.

The town itself is modest: a main street, a handful of cafes, a working fishing harbour. The draw is what surrounds it. Fifteen kilometres north, the Bay of Fires stretches up toward Eddystone Point — a long arc of white sand and turquoise water, its granite boulders stained vivid orange by lichen. On a clear day it's genuinely one of the more beautiful stretches of coastline in Australia. The northern end, accessible only on foot or by sea, is less visited and often deserted.

Blue Derby, a short drive inland into the Pyengana Valley, has become one of the most celebrated mountain bike destinations in Australia, with trails ranging from long cross-country loops to technical descents. The trails are well-built and well-signed; even less experienced riders can manage the blue-grade runs.

The fishing in Georges Bay is good — flathead and bream in the bay itself, with charter boats offering offshore trips. The town hosts the St Helens Game Fishing Classic each summer, which draws boats from across the state.
