Penguin is a small town on Tasmania's northwest coast, about 20 kilometres west of [Devonport](/australiaandpacific/australia/tasmania/northwest/devonport) and its Bass Strait ferry terminal. The town takes its name from the little penguins that nest along the foreshore — you can see them at dusk in season.

It's a good-looking place: a main street of cafes and craft shops facing the beach, a Sunday market popular with locals, and a lush forested hinterland that rises steeply behind the town. The surrounding countryside is dairy and vegetable farming country, and the drive inland through the Dial Range offers a quieter version of Tasmania's mountain scenery.

For those arriving by ferry at Devonport, Penguin makes a pleasant first stop on a northwest Tasmania loop rather than heading straight inland.
