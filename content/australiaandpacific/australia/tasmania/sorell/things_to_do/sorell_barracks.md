title: "Sorell Barracks"
type: poi
category: Architecture
latitude: -42.7885
longitude: 147.6665
---

Built in 1827 to house a garrison tasked with suppressing bushranging in the district, the barracks is one of the best-preserved examples of colonial military architecture in southern Tasmania. The building is on the National Estate register and still stands in reasonable condition on Walker Street. It's not a museum — you view it from outside — but the structure itself is worth a look as you walk through the old town precinct.
