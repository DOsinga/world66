Bruny Island is accessible as a day trip from Hobart, but to do the island justice you should take a weekend. The northern half is mostly grazing land, with a few small towns and good swimming beaches. The hike to Elizabeth Bay is well worth the effort.

The southern half has more for visitors. The narrow isthmus connecting the two halves is home to roosting muttonbirds and a beautiful ocean beach. From Adventure Bay, hike to Fluted Cape or climb Mt Mangana. Drive to the southern tip to see the old lighthouse and Cloudy Bay.

To get there, drive south from Hobart on the Southern Outlet, take the road to Margate, and follow it to Kettering. Ferries run every one to two hours.
