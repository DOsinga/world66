---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Tasmania's main attractions are outdoors. The **Overland Track** from Cradle Mountain to Lake St Clair is the great six-day walk; the alpine plateau, glacier-carved lakes and dolerite peaks make it one of Australia's classic hikes. Shorter wilderness experiences include **Freycinet National Park** (the Wineglass Bay walk), **Bay of Fires** on the east coast (white-sand beaches and orange-lichened granite), and **Mount Field** with its waterfalls and tall swamp gum forests.

In and around [Hobart](/australiaandpacific/australia/tasmania/hobart), **MONA** (Museum of Old and New Art) is the standout — a provocative private museum built underground into sandstone cliffs, reachable by a fast catamaran from the central pier. The 1830s convict ruins at **Port Arthur** on the Tasman Peninsula are about 90 minutes from Hobart and remain the best-preserved convict site in Australia. **Mount Wellington** rises 1,270m directly behind the city, with a road and walking tracks to the summit.

For wildlife, Maria Island and Bonorong Sanctuary near Hobart are the easiest places to see Tasmanian devils, wombats and pademelons. **Cradle Mountain** has reliable wombat and platypus sightings at dawn and dusk. The whisky scene around Hobart and the Tamar Valley wineries north of [Launceston](/australiaandpacific/australia/tasmania/launceston) make for excellent slow-paced day trips.
