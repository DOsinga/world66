---
title: "Tours and Excursions"
type: section
---

Independent travel in Australia is straightforward in cities and along the east coast, but organised tours make sense for remote areas where distances are large, roads are rough, and navigating without local knowledge is genuinely difficult.

**The Red Centre** (Uluru, Kata Tjuta, Kings Canyon) is best approached with a tour or a hire car and solid preparation. Campervan tours and small-group guided trips run from Alice Springs. Operators like APT and Intrepid run multi-day itineraries through the national park. The park entry fee is included in most tours.

**Kakadu and Arnhem Land** essentially require a guided tour for Arnhem Land (a permit is needed to enter, and independent access is difficult). Kakadu can be done independently in dry season, but guided tours add cultural context — particularly for the rock art sites — that self-guided visits cannot replicate.

**Sailing the Whitsundays:** Bareboat charters (no skipper required if you have a competency licence) are available from Airlie Beach and allow you to island-hop independently. For those without sailing experience, crewed charter yachts or group sailing tours cover similar territory. The Whitsunday sailing scene is well organised and ranges from budget three-day trips on older boats to week-long liveaboards on well-equipped yachts.

**The Nullarbor crossing:** The highway from Perth to Adelaide runs across the Nullarbor Plain — famously flat, treeless, and remote. The Eyre Highway stretches 1,000 km between Norseman in WA and Port Augusta in SA, with very little in between. Most people drive it in a campervan or rental car over three to four days. There are basic roadhouses and campgrounds along the way; fuel prices rise significantly in the remote stretches. It is a long, hypnotic, somewhat demanding drive — and one of the great Australian overland experiences.

**Diving the Great Barrier Reef:** Liveaboard dive trips from Cairns or Port Douglas put you on the outer reef, far from the day-trip crowds. Mike Ball Dive Expeditions and Spirit of Freedom are well-regarded operators. A basic open water certification takes 3–4 days and can be completed in Cairns before boarding.
