Bendigo is one of Victoria's great gold rush towns, about two hours northwest of [Melbourne](/australiaandpacific/australia/victoria/melbourne) by car or a comfortable train ride on the V/Line. Like [Ballarat](/australiaandpacific/australia/victoria/ballarat), it boomed in the 1850s when gold was found at Bendigo Creek, and the wealth still shows in the elaborate Victorian architecture lining Pall Mall and View Street.

The Central Deborah Gold Mine lets you descend 60 metres underground into the tunnels where miners once worked by candlelight. The Golden Dragon Museum tells the story of the Chinese miners who shaped the town's character and houses Sun Loong, one of the world's longest imperial dragons. Bendigo's Easter Festival, running since 1871, is one of Australia's oldest and features the dragon in a parade through the centre of town.

Beyond the gold rush heritage, Bendigo has reinvented itself as a regional arts centre. The Bendigo Art Gallery is one of the oldest and largest in regional Australia, regularly hosting major touring exhibitions. The town's food and wine scene draws on the surrounding Goldfields region, with local producers supplying a growing number of good restaurants and cafes along the main streets.
