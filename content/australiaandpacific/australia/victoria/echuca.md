Echuca is a Victorian river town on the Murray that wears its history proudly. In the 1870s it was Australia's largest inland port, its wharves stacked with wool and timber hauled down from the hinterland on the backs of paddlesteamers. That era is gone but not forgotten — the restored historic port precinct is one of the best-preserved examples of 19th-century river trade in the country, and working paddlesteamers still churn up and down the Murray most days.

The town sits on the New South Wales border, which here runs along the centre of the Murray. Cross the bridge and you are in Moama, a quieter satellite town that shares Echuca's river frontage. Together they make a pleasant base for a few days: swimming and kayaking in the river, cycling the flat tracks through red gum forests, and eating well at the cafes and restaurants that have followed the tourism trade.

Echuca is about three hours north of Melbourne on the Hume Highway, making it a popular weekend escape for city dwellers. It is best in spring and autumn when the river is running well and the heat is manageable.
