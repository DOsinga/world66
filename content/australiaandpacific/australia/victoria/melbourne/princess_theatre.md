One of Melbourne's grandest Victorian-era theatres, the Princess Theatre has been staging performances since 1854. The ornate Second Empire facade and lavish interior make it worth seeing even if you are not catching a show. It is reputedly haunted by the ghost of Frederick Baker, a baritone who died on stage during a performance of Faust in 1888. Theatre staff still leave a seat empty for him on opening nights.
