Morwell sits in the Latrobe Valley in Gippsland, the heart of Victoria's brown coal country. It is not a tourist destination in the conventional sense — the open-cut mines and power stations that dominate the valley are a stark reminder of industrial Australia — but the town has an unexpected natural asset on its doorstep.

Morwell National Park, on the town's southern edge, protects a stretch of tall eucalypt forest and is home to a resident koala colony. The Billys Creek walk winds through the forest past fern gullies and a waterfall, and it is one of the few places in Gippsland where you can reliably spot koalas in the wild without a guide.

If you are passing through the Latrobe Valley on your way to the Gippsland Lakes or Wilsons Promontory, the national park makes a worthwhile stop.
