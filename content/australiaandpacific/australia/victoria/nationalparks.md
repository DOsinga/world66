The best known national park in Victoria is probably Grampians State Forest. In this enormous park, extending for hectares, you will see eucalypts, streams, rare birds and animals, authentic vegetation and endless walking trails. However, The Grampians is one of the nearly 75 national parks and although it is the largest in the area, the smaller ones have also a lot to offer. Take for instance the Port Campbell National Park at the coast that offers some suburb views of cliffs, rocks and the sea.
