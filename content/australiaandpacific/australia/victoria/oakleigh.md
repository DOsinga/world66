Oakleigh is a suburb in southeastern [Melbourne](/australiaandpacific/australia/victoria/melbourne) and the undisputed heart of the city's Greek community. If you want the best Greek food in Australia, this is where you come. Eaton Mall, the pedestrianised strip at the centre of the suburb, is packed with Greek bakeries, tavernas, souvlaki bars, and cafes that spill onto the pavement with tables and conversation that gets louder as the evening goes on.

The food is the main reason to visit. Oakleigh Souvlaki is a local institution, Vanilla Lounge does excellent loukoumades (Greek doughnuts drenched in honey and cinnamon), and the bakeries sell spanakopita and galaktoboureko that rival anything in Athens. On weekend evenings, Eaton Mall fills up with families and groups, and the atmosphere is genuinely festive. Melbourne has the largest Greek diaspora population of any city outside Greece, and Oakleigh is where that community feels most concentrated and alive.

The suburb is easy to reach on the Pakenham or Cranbourne train line, about 20 minutes from the city centre. Beyond the food, there is not much reason to linger, but a dinner trip to Oakleigh is one of Melbourne's most distinctive suburban experiences.
