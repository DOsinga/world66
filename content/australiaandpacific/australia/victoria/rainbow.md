Rainbow is a small wheat-belt town in Victoria's remote northwest Wimmera region, sitting on the edge of vast golden plains about 365 kilometres from Melbourne. It's a genuine outback town with a strong agricultural character, and a useful gateway to the nearby Wyperfeld National Park.

Wyperfeld is one of Victoria's largest and least-visited protected areas — mallee scrubland, dry lakes, and long walking trails with almost no one on them. The park is particularly good for birdwatching, with mallee fowl, regent parrots, and Major Mitchell's cockatoos among the species regularly sighted. The road to the park entrance passes through typical Wimmera landscape of red dirt and dry saltbush.
