Yackandandah — "Yack" to the locals — is a tiny gold rush town tucked into the hills of northeast Victoria. Its main street has barely changed in a century: wide verandahs, weatherboard shopfronts, and a scatter of antique stores that reward a slow afternoon of browsing.

The town punches well above its weight for a place this small. A strong creative community keeps the shopfronts interesting, the local bakery draws people from surrounding towns, and there's a community-owned solar grid that powers most of the place. The surrounding bushland is laced with mountain bike trails and old mining tracks.

If you're driving between Beechworth and Wodonga, the short detour is well worth it. Even half an hour wandering High Street is enough to get the idea.
