Geraldton sits 400 kilometres north of Perth on the Batavia Coast, and its beaches are some of the best in Western Australia for water sports. The strong, reliable winds make it one of the world's top spots for windsurfing and kiteboarding, while the coastal reefs and breaks draw surfers from across the state.

Town Beach and Back Beach are the easiest to reach from the centre, with calm swimming at the former and better surf at the latter. Further out, the beaches around Coronation and Sunset are quieter and good for long walks. The Abrolhos Islands, 60 kilometres offshore, offer world-class diving and snorkelling over coral reefs shaped by the warm Leeuwin Current, the same current that supports Geraldton's famous rock lobster industry.

The wildflower season from July to October blankets the coastal scrubland with colour, making beach walks particularly rewarding during that stretch.
