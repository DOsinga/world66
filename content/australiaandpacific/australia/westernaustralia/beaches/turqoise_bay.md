A small, pristine beach about 60 kilometres south of Exmouth in the Ningaloo Marine Park, inside Cape Range National Park. Ningaloo is one of the largest fringing coral reefs in Australia and one of the few in the world close enough to shore that you can walk straight in and start snorkelling.

The bay is sheltered by reef about a kilometre offshore, keeping the water calm and shallow -- around two metres deep within 10 to 30 metres of the beach. A gentle current runs through two gaps in the reef, creating perfect drift snorkelling conditions. Walk south along the beach, wade in, swim out as far as you like, and let the current carry you back along the coastline. Exit before the sandbar at the north end, where the current strengthens.

Expect tropical fish, clownfish, turtles, and the occasional reef shark (rangers say they are harmless, but use common sense). Bring your own snorkelling gear, food, and water -- the nearest supplies are a 15-minute drive away. There are toilets at the car park but nothing else.
