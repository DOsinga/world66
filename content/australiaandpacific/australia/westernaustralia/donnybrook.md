Donnybrook is a small country town in Western Australia's south-west, about thirty kilometres inland from [Bunbury](/australiaandpacific/australia/westernaustralia/bunbury) and surrounded by orchards. It is the centre of WA's apple-growing country — the harvest in autumn is the town's busy season, when backpackers arrive for fruit-picking work — and the giant Apple Fun Park in the centre of town, one of the largest free playgrounds in the southern hemisphere, is a reliable stop for families breaking the drive south. Beyond the orchards, Donnybrook makes a quiet base for visiting the wineries and tall-tree forests of the Geographe and Margaret River regions further south.
