Donnybrook is a small country town in the South West of [Western Australia](/australiaandpacific/australia/westernaustralia), about 35 kilometres inland from Bunbury. It is best known as the **apple capital of WA** — orchards line the Preston River valley and the harvest from February through May draws backpackers looking for fruit-picking work. There is a year-round farmgate trade in apples, pears and stone fruit, and a couple of cider makers worth seeking out.

The town itself is small and quiet. The main street has a few cafes and the giant fibreglass apple that is required of any self-respecting Australian fruit town. Most travellers pass through on the way to the Margaret River wine country to the south, or stop briefly to load up on fresh fruit at one of the orchard stands along the South Western Highway.
