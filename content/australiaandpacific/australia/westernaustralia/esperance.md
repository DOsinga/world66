Esperance sits on the southern coast of Western Australia, a long way from anywhere — about 700 km southeast of Perth. The reward for getting here is some of the most beautiful coastline in the country. The beaches along the Great Ocean Drive are startlingly white, the water an intense, almost unreal turquoise, and in the right season you will have long stretches entirely to yourself.

The town itself is a quiet, friendly place that serves as a base for exploring the coast and the national parks nearby. Cape Le Grand National Park, about 50 km east, has the famous Lucky Bay — regularly cited as Australia's whitest beach, where kangaroos lounge on the sand. Nearby Thistle Cove and Hellfire Bay are equally stunning and less visited.

The drive to Esperance from Perth is long but spectacular, passing through the wheat belt and the goldfields. Many visitors combine it with a trip to the wave-shaped rock formations at Hyden or the pink waters of Lake Hillier on Middle Island in the Recherche Archipelago.
