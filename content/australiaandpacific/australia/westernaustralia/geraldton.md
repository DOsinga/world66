Geraldton is the major regional city of Western Australia's Midwest, situated 400 km north of [Perth](/australiaandpacific/australia/westernaustralia/perth) on the Batavia Coast. It serves as the hub for the fishing, farming, and mining industries of the surrounding region and has the only deep-water port for hundreds of kilometres.

The surrounding waters, fed by the warm Leeuwin Current, are renowned for rock lobster fishing — Geraldton is considered a world leader in the industry. The same reefs and islands make for excellent diving, and the city's famously strong winds have made it one of the best locations in the world for windsurfing and kiteboarding. Surfers will find good breaks along the coast in either direction.

Geraldton was surveyed in 1850 and began as a convict settlement. The 1890s Murchison gold rush brought rapid growth, and the town is named after Charles Fitzgerald, governor of the state at the time. The **HMAS Sydney II Memorial** on the hill overlooking the city commemorates the warship lost with all 645 crew in 1941.

The annual wildflower season (roughly August to October) draws large crowds to the surrounding countryside, where the diversity of Western Australian flora is on spectacular display.
