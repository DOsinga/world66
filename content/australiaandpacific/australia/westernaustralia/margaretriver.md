Margaret River is Western Australia's premier wine region, a stretch of coast and hinterland between two capes where over 200 wineries produce some of Australia's best cabernet sauvignon and chardonnay. But wine is only part of the story. The coastline here is stunning — powerful surf breaks, limestone caves, and tall karri forests that run almost to the shore.

The town itself is small and relaxed, with good restaurants and a Saturday farmers' market that draws half the region. Most visitors use it as a base for exploring the surrounding area: surfing at Prevelly, walking through the Boranup karri forest, or tasting their way along Caves Road. The caves — Mammoth, Lake, and Jewel — are worth the detour, especially Jewel Cave with its enormous underground chambers.

From June to September, humpback whales pass close to the coast at Cape Naturaliste and Cape Leeuwin. The latter is where the Indian and Southern Oceans meet, and the lighthouse there marks the south-western tip of Australia.
