Scarborough is a beachside suburb of Perth about 14 km north of the city centre, built around one of the most popular beaches in Western Australia. The beach is broad, the surf is reliable, and the foreshore has been significantly redeveloped in recent years with a new promenade, food outlets, and a renovated outdoor pool complex. It is a good spot for a day at the beach without straying too far from Perth.

The surf is consistent enough to support a small surfing culture, and the Scarborough Beach Pool — an outdoor 50-metre lap pool right on the foreshore — is a genuine attraction. The suburb itself is not particularly charming, but the ocean views and the walk north or south along the coast path make it worth the trip from the city. The Scarborough Beach Road strip has the usual mix of cafes, fish and chip shops, and casual restaurants.
