Aitutaki is the Cook Islands' second most visited island, and for many travellers the highlight of the whole country. The island itself is pleasant — small, green, quiet — but the real draw is the lagoon: a vast, shallow expanse of turquoise water ringed by fifteen small motus (islets), with white sand and coral outcrops scattered across it. It is genuinely one of the most beautiful lagoons in the Pacific.

Most visitors come on a day trip from Rarotonga by Air Rarotonga (about 45 minutes). Those who stay longer find a relaxed pace, good snorkelling, and accommodation ranging from budget guesthouses to the Aitutaki Lagoon Private Island Resort — the only overwater bungalow resort in the Cook Islands.

The lagoon tour is the essential activity: a day-long boat cruise with snorkelling stops at coral heads, a BBQ lunch on a sandbank, and a visit to One Foot Island (Tapuaetai) where the tiny post office will stamp your passport. Kitesurfing is also popular — the sheltered lagoon provides ideal conditions.
