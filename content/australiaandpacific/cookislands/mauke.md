Mauke is one of the three small islands — along with Mitiaro and Atiu — that form the southern Cook Islands' inner cluster. Like its neighbours, it is ringed by makatea (raised limestone), giving it a rugged coastal character but limiting beach access. The interior is fertile, growing arrowroot, citrus, and taro.

The island has a small population living in three villages: Ngatiarua, Areora, and Kimiangatau. The main attraction for the few visitors who come is the Divided Church in Areora, a striking building split down the middle — the two halves constructed separately by rival missionaries who later agreed to share the space. It is an unusual piece of Pacific ecclesiastical history.

Mauke is connected to Rarotonga by Air Rarotonga flights a few times per week. Accommodation is limited to small guesthouses; meals are provided by your hosts.
