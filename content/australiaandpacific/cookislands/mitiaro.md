Mitiaro is a small, low-lying island sitting between Atiu and Mauke in the southern Cook Islands. Like its neighbours, it is encircled by makatea (raised coral limestone), and the interior consists of a fertile taro swamp plateau. The population of a few hundred lives in a single village, Mangarei.

The island has two freshwater lakes, Rotonui and Rotoiti, and a cave system including Vai Nauri — a freshwater cave pool used for swimming, where small eels live peacefully alongside bathers. The lakes attract a range of bird species.

Mitiaro receives very few visitors. Getting there means a small Air Rarotonga flight from Rarotonga, and accommodation is limited to basic guesthouses. It is the kind of place where a stranger's arrival is a notable event and hospitality is accordingly generous.
