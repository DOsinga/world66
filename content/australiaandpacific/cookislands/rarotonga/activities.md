---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The lagoon is the main attraction: snorkelling, scuba diving, kayaking, and glass-bottom boat trips are all popular. The reef is intact and marine life is abundant. Inland, the cross-island track (Te Rua Manga) is a two-hour guided walk that crosses the central ridge, passing through cloud forest with plant species found nowhere else in the Cooks — the inimitable local guide Pa has been leading it for decades. A four-wheel drive mountain safari is a gentler option for the same scenery. The Takitumu Conservation Area in the southern mountains runs guided walks specifically to see the endangered kakerori flycatcher, one of the rarest birds in the Pacific. Cultural shows featuring traditional Polynesian dance and drumming are performed at several resorts most evenings.
