---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency:** The Fijian dollar (FJD). ATMs are available at the airports, in Nadi, Suva, and at larger resort areas, but can be scarce on outer islands — carry cash if you're heading somewhere remote. Visa and Mastercard are widely accepted at hotels and larger restaurants; elsewhere, cash is essential.

**Health:** Drink bottled or boiled water — tap water is not reliably safe outside major urban centres. Fiji has mosquitoes and the occasional dengue fever outbreak, so use repellent. Standard travel vaccinations (Hepatitis A, typhoid, routine boosters) are recommended. There is no malaria in Fiji.

**Safety:** Fiji is a safe destination by most measures. Petty theft occurs in urban areas, particularly around Suva and Nadi, so take the usual precautions with valuables. Violent crime targeting tourists is rare. Swimming at unpatrolled beaches and reef edges carries real risks — strong currents, urchins, and occasional sharks. Ask locally before going in.

**Customs and etiquette:** When visiting a village, it is customary to bring a sevusevu — a gift of kava root (yaqona) presented to the chief or village elder before you are welcomed in. Dress modestly when entering villages or churches. Remove shoes before entering a home. Don't wear a hat in a village — it's considered disrespectful.

**Kava:** Fiji's national drink is made from the crushed root of the yaqona plant. It has a mild sedative effect. You will almost certainly be offered it. Accept — declining is rude. Clap once before receiving the bowl, drink it in one go, then clap three times.

**Communications:** Mobile coverage is reasonable on Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. Outer islands vary — some resorts have satellite internet, others offer a welcome break from connectivity. Vodafone Fiji and Digicel both sell local SIMs at the airport.

**Electricity:** 240V/50Hz, Australian-style plugs (Type I). Bring an adapter if coming from North America or Europe.
