Suva is the capital of Fiji and, by Pacific standards, a proper city — busy, ethnically mixed, and with enough going on to keep you occupied for a couple of days. It sits on a peninsula on Viti Levu's southeastern coast, hemmed in by hills and the harbour, and gets considerably more rain than the tourist resorts on the Coral Coast, which keeps it green but gives it a different, more working-city atmosphere.

The downtown area has an almost colonial feel: the Government Buildings, the old Parliament, and Thurston Gardens belong to another era, while the covered Municipal Market — a maze of root vegetables, tropical fruit, kava, and homemade sauces — belongs very much to this one. It's one of the best markets in the Pacific for just wandering.

The Fiji Museum, inside the Thurston Gardens, is genuinely good. The collection covers traditional Fijian culture, the cannibal period, the Indo-Fijian experience, and the wreck of the *Bounty* mutineers' ship. Give it an hour or two.

Suva is also where you'll feel Fiji's ethnic complexity most directly. About half the population is Indo-Fijian, descendants of indentured labourers brought by the British in the nineteenth century, and the city's restaurants, shops, and street life reflect that mix. The tension between these communities is real and worth understanding before you visit.
