Taveuni is Fiji's "Garden Island," the third-largest island in the archipelago and one of its most beautiful. The volcanic soil and heavy rainfall produce an almost absurdly lush landscape: dense tropical forest, cascading waterfalls, and hillsides thick with coconut palms and tropical flowers. The 180th meridian runs through the island, which means you can technically stand with one foot in today and one foot in yesterday, though the International Date Line itself was long ago bent around Fiji for practical reasons.

The island's star attraction on land is Bouma National Heritage Park, where a series of waterfalls tumble through pristine rainforest and well-maintained trails lead through some of the best-preserved native forest in Fiji. Offshore, the diving is world-class: the Rainbow Reef, in the Somosomo Strait between Taveuni and Vanua Levu, is home to stunning soft coral walls and the famous Great White Wall, a vertical drop covered in white soft coral.

Taveuni moves at a pace even slower than the rest of Fiji. There is one main road, a handful of small villages, and a scattering of low-key resorts and guesthouses. If you have come to Fiji looking for something beyond the resort beaches of the Mamanucas, this is the place.
