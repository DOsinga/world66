Viti Levu is the largest island in Fiji and the one most visitors see. The international airport sits on the west coast at [Nadi](/australiaandpacific/fiji/vitilevu/nadi), and the capital [Suva](/australiaandpacific/fiji/vitilevu/suva) is on the wetter east side. In between you get the full range of Fijian landscapes — lush rainforest interior, coral reefs, sugarcane plantations, and the famous [Coral Coast](/australiaandpacific/fiji/vitilevu/coral_coast) strip of beach resorts along the southern shore.

Most travellers land in Nadi and head straight for the smaller outer islands — the Mamanucas or Yasawas — without seeing much of Viti Levu itself. That's a missed opportunity. The island is big enough to take a week to circumnavigate at a gentle pace, and the interior is wilder and more interesting than the postcards suggest.

The Kings Road loops around the north and east of the island, a quieter route than the Queens Road down the south. The eastern half is rain forest, rivers suitable for whitewater rafting, and traditional villages where staying with a family and drinking kava is still common. [Lautoka](/australiaandpacific/fiji/vitilevu/lautoka), Fiji's second city, sits 30 minutes north of Nadi and is the departure point for most trips to the Yasawa islands.

Getting around is straightforward: buses run both the Queens and Kings Roads, rental cars are available in Nadi, and the coastal towns are linked by regular minibus services.
