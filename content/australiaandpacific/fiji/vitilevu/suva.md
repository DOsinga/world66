Suva is the capital of Fiji and the largest city in the South Pacific outside of Australia and New Zealand. It sits on a hilly peninsula on the southeast coast of Viti Levu, surrounded by lush green hills that catch a lot of rain — this is not a beach destination, but a real working city with more character than the resort strips along the western coast.

The centre has a pleasantly scruffy charm: colonial-era buildings, busy markets, and streets full of Indian and Fijian shops. The Municipal Market is worth a wander for its produce, kava, and seafood. Thurston Gardens, next to Government House, is a beautiful botanical garden and home to the Fiji Museum.

Suva is the best place in Fiji to arrange onward travel to the outer islands. It is also where you will find the country's best restaurants, its university, and whatever nightlife Fiji has to offer — which, on a Friday night, is more than you might expect.
