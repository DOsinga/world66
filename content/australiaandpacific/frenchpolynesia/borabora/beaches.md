---
title: "Beaches in Bora bora"
type: section
---

Most visitors who come for a beach vacation to Bora Bora are
somewhat surprised to learn that it is a lagoon destination with very
few beaches. Getting around the island is a little tricky with only one
bus, but bicycles and golf carts are available for hire if you are
looking for transportation to get you to one of the few beaches.

**Matira Beach, **located on the southern tip of Bora,** **is
the only public beach on the island. This beach can get very busy on
weekends and public holidays. This beautiful white sand beach is
littered with jet ski rentals, hotels, and a couple restaurants. Matira
has been named one of the Top Ten Beaches in the World! The aquamarine
waters are warm and inviting, and the beach is the perfect spot to
enjoy one of the numerous water activities offered. A few boat
excursions can be taken from Matira Beach, around the island. Most
visitors come to Matira Beach to laze the day away, and fall privy to
one of the most amazing sunsets in the world! The beach also offers a
few hiking trails, several boutique shops, and the infamous Ben's Place
snack bar. The lagoon here is a little deeper and makes its ideal for
swimming. At the far end of Matira Beach is the Hotel Matira,
behind which you can climb the hill to the American Guns for a
spectacular ocean and island view. The snorkeling off the the northern
point of Matira is amongst the best on the island due to its calmer
waters and optimal visibility.

North of Matira Beach along the east coast is the island's only other decent stretch of sandy beach alongside **Pofia Bay**.
This crescent shaped bay is one half of the collapsed volcanic crater
and here are some of the best panorama shots of Bora's magnificent
hills. Pofai Bay is far less commercial than Matira with only a few
guesthouses, and much more secluded beach with little to no crowd.

**Intercontinental Hotel **offers private beach access at the
southern point of Matira. The lagoon here is very shallow, but the
turquoise water is extremely tempting. Although too shallow for
swimming, there is decent snorkeling just further out. The beach
extends along the East coast of Matira Point for less than mile where
the newly renovated **Sofitel Marara **now enjoys the best of the beach front. **Maitai Polynesia **offers a wide range of accommodation including bungalows on the beach.
