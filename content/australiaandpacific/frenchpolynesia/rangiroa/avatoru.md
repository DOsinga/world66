More a sleepy hamlet than a settlement, Avatoru is a series of islets connected by concrete bridges. It and neighbouring Tiputa are the only populated areas of the Rangiroa atoll, aside from the boat-accessible resort across the main lagoon.

Avatoru is long and narrow with a single paved road from end to end. A bike is the right vehicle: lagoon on one side, open ocean on the other. The airport is at one end — a single open-sided room with a hand-painted TAPU sign keeping people off the runway.

The village has a couple of dive shops, a handful of pensions (guesthouses), and the Kia Ora resort hotel. There are no bars or clubs; evenings are quiet. The small graveyard makes interesting use of coral and shells. In June–August the sun sets early and the nights are cool for the tropics, but the stars are spectacular once the light goes.

Bring cash. The ATM at Banque Socredo is the only one that reliably works with foreign cards, and it is not always working. Two banks sit next to each other near the centre of the strip.
