---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Rangiroa is an atoll, so forget powdery white sand — cyclones washed most of it away long ago. What you get instead are coral beaches with extraordinary colours and coconut-tree shade. Bring a towel or blanket, settle in, and the lagoon views will do the rest. The water is warm and calm on the lagoon side, perfect for swimming.

Snorkelling is excellent right off any beach. Bring water shoes — you will be walking over coral pieces and rocks to get in. Expect to see sharks and rays on every outing, along with schools of colourful tropical fish. Locals say the lagoon is safe to swim in despite the sharks, but they are emphatic that swimming on the ocean side is dangerous. The currents through the passes are powerful and unpredictable.

For a day trip, **Lagon Bleu** is spectacular — about an hour by boat from Avatoru, with schools of baby blacktip sharks in crystal-clear shallows. Ask your pension owner which boat operators are running trips. **Lagon Vert** is smaller but equally pretty; there is a fish park where you can swim with large tame sharks and rays.
