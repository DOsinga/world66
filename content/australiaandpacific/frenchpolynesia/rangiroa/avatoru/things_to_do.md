---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

More affordable and far less developed than the Society Islands — Tahiti, Bora Bora, Moorea — Avatoru is quiet by design. The main draws are underwater, but there is enough above the surface to keep you occupied.

The two passes (Avatoru Pass and Tiputa Pass) are the headline: strong tidal currents draw in sharks, rays, barracuda, dolphins, and every kind of reef fish. Drift diving and drift snorkelling here rank among the best in the Pacific. Underwater photographers will find no shortage of subjects — grey reef sharks in clear water, manta rays, the full spectrum of tropical fish. Several dive shops in the village run daily trips to both passes.

The community graveyard is small and worth a quiet look — coral and shell decorations, local names, simple wooden crosses. Rangiroa is geologically old and the ocean is slowly reclaiming it. You can see the erosion clearly on both the lagoon side and the ocean side; some old bungalows are collapsing into the water.

After dark, Avatoru is genuinely dark — too small for street lighting to matter. The Milky Way is bright on clear nights, and you understand viscerally how Polynesian navigators found their way across the Pacific.
