Tahiti is the largest island in French Polynesia, covering 1,042 km², and is divided into a larger northwestern lobe (Tahiti Nui) and a smaller southeastern peninsula (Tahiti Iti), joined by an isthmus. Most of the population lives on the northwest coast around the capital [Papeete](/australiaandpacific/frenchpolynesia/tahiti/papeete). The island's interior is dominated by dramatic volcanic peaks, and the coastal road — 114 km around the main island — passes waterfalls, blowhole, lava tubes, and archaeological sites.

The island is more than just a transit point for the outer atolls. The Arahoho Blowhole and Faarumai Waterfalls are on the east coast. Point Venus, where Captain Cook observed the transit of Venus in 1769, is a historically significant beach. Marae Arahuruhu near Paea preserves restored stone temples (marae), tikis, and petroglyphs from pre-European Polynesian culture. In the mountainous interior, the Fautaua Valley trail leads to a large waterfall on a half-day walk.

On the southeast peninsula, Teahupo'o is one of the most famous big-wave surf breaks in the world. The currency is the Pacific Franc (XPF/CFP), fixed to the Euro at 119 CFP per euro.
