Herman Melville's trio of early novels draws on his own experiences as a young sailor in the South Pacific during the 1840s. *Typee* recounts his time living among the Taipi people in the Marquesas Islands, *Omoo* follows his wanderings through Tahiti and Moorea, and *Mardi* ventures into more allegorical territory across a fictional Polynesian archipelago. Together they offer a vivid, sometimes romanticized portrait of island life before tourism reshaped these places. If you are heading to French Polynesia, Melville gives you a lens into what these islands looked and felt like nearly two centuries ago.
