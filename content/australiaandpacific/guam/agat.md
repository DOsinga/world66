Agat is a village on the southwestern coast of Guam, best known as one of the two landing beaches where US Marines came ashore in July 1944 during the Battle of Guam to retake the island from Japanese occupation. The War in the Pacific National Historical Park has a unit at Agat — Gaan Point — where you can see a preserved tank and read about the assault. It is a quiet, sobering place with good views across the water.

The village itself is a small Chamorro community with a pleasant beach and calmer waters than the east coast. It is a short drive from the main resort area of Tumon Bay but feels entirely different in character — unhurried and local. The reef offshore is good for snorkelling.
