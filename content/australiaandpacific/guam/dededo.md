Dededo is the most populous village in Guam, occupying the northern part of the island above the tourist corridor of Tumon Bay. It is primarily a residential area, home to a large proportion of Guam's population including many of the military families associated with the US bases on the island.

For visitors, Dededo's main draw is the Chamorro Village Night Market, a weekly open-air market where local food, crafts, and traditional Chamorro dance performances make for an authentic alternative to the resort strip. The northern tip of the island, accessible from Dededo, offers dramatic coastline scenery at Ritidian Point, part of the Guam National Wildlife Refuge.
