Mangilao is a village on the east coast of **Guam**, midway up the island and home to the main campus of the **University of Guam** — the largest higher-education institution in Micronesia — along with its associated **Richard F. Taitano Micronesian Area Research Center**, which holds the most important collection of Chamorro and Micronesian ethnographic and historical material in the region. Beyond the university, Mangilao is mostly a quiet residential area; it is also the unlikely location of Guam's famous **Latte Stone Park** on the clifftop above Pago Bay, where several of the mushroom-shaped coral pillars carved by ancient Chamorro are on display.
