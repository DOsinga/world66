Merizo is a small village at the southern tip of Guam, one of the most traditional Chamorro communities on the island. It sits on a calm lagoon protected by a reef, with Cocos Island visible just offshore. The pace of life here is noticeably slower than in the tourist areas to the north.

Cocos Island, accessible by a short boat ride from Merizo, has a good beach and reef snorkelling. The village itself has a 17th-century Spanish church, Dulce Nombre de Maria, which is one of the oldest buildings still standing on Guam. Merizo hosts a lively village fiesta each year that draws Chamorro families from across the island.
