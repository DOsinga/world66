Talofofo Bay is a long curved bay on the southeast coast of [Guam](/australiaandpacific/guam), where the island's biggest river, the Talofofo, empties into the Pacific. The beach sweeps around beneath red cliffs and is popular with local families and surfers — the river-mouth break is one of the more reliable on the island. A nearby waterfall and a small theme park, Talofofo Falls Park, sit up the river valley; the park includes a replica of the cave where Shoichi Yokoi, a Japanese soldier who refused to surrender at the end of the Second World War, hid out in the jungle until he was discovered in 1972.

The area is quiet and rural by Guam standards, a complete change of pace from the hotels and shopping of Tumon Bay on the opposite side of the island. A rental car is the only practical way to get here.
