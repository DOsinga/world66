Umatac is a historic village on the southwestern coast of Guam, set in a sheltered bay below steep green hills. It is the site where Ferdinand Magellan landed in March 1521, the first European contact with Guam, and the village celebrates this with a re-enactment festival each year in early March.

The bay is one of the most scenic on the island, lined with Spanish-era ruins and an old fort. Umatac is quiet and largely undeveloped, making it a sharp contrast to the resort hotels of Tumon Bay. The drive south along the coast from Agana through Umatac is one of the most scenic routes on Guam, passing through several traditional villages and offering views over the Pacific.
