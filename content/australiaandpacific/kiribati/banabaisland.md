Banaba — also called Ocean Island — is a solitary raised coral island sitting well to the west of the rest of Kiribati, closer to Nauru than to Tarawa. For most of the twentieth century it was stripped of its phosphate by British, Australian, and New Zealand companies, leaving a moonscape of pinnacled rock where plantations and villages once stood. Most of the Banabans were resettled on Rabi Island in Fiji in 1945 and the island now has only a few hundred residents.

There is essentially no tourism. The island has no airstrip; the only way to reach it is by cargo boat from Tarawa, and the schedule is measured in weeks or months rather than days. Those who do make it find rusting colonial-era machinery, a small cluster of villages, and a bitter local memory of what extraction did to the land.
