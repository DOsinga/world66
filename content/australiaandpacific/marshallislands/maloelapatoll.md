Maloelap is a ring of 71 small islands in the Ratak Chain of the Marshall Islands, enclosing a wide turquoise lagoon about 160 kilometres east of Majuro. Fewer than a thousand people live here, mostly on Airok and Taroa, and getting out this far takes a seat on the small domestic flight or a slow copra boat.

The draw, for the few travellers who make it, is the wreckage of a Japanese airbase on Taroa — the second-largest Japanese outpost in the Marshalls during the Second World War. American bombers left it a graveyard of rusting Zeros, twin-engine bombers, anti-aircraft guns and concrete bunkers, much of it still sitting where it fell in the undergrowth and the shallows. Offshore, divers can swim through a handful of sunken aircraft and ships.

Beyond the war relics, Maloelap is pure atoll life: coconut palms, white sand, reef fish, and almost no infrastructure. Bring everything you need.
