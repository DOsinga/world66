---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency and money.** The US dollar is the official currency. Most guesthouses in Majuro accept credit cards, but take cash for anything outside the capital. ATMs exist in Majuro but are unreliable; bring enough dollars to cover your stay.

**Visas.** US citizens do not need a visa under the Compact of Free Association — you can enter and stay without limit. Citizens of most other countries can get a 30-day entry permit on arrival, extendable to 90 days. You will need an onward or return ticket and evidence of sufficient funds.

**Health.** Tap water is not safe to drink anywhere in the Marshalls; buy or filter. Typhoid and hepatitis A vaccinations are recommended. Visitors between 6 months and 62 years of age must show proof of measles vaccination before boarding vessels. Medical care in Majuro is adequate for minor problems; anything serious requires evacuation to Honolulu or Guam, so travel insurance with evacuation cover is essential.

**Climate and clothing.** The climate is tropical, hot and humid year-round, with average temperatures around 29°C. Rainfall varies across the atolls. Dress casually but modestly outside beach areas — shorts are not appropriate street wear. Lightweight cotton clothing is best.

**Communications.** Mobile coverage (NTA Mobile) exists in Majuro and on Ebeye. The outer islands have no mobile service; they use HF radio. Internet is available in Majuro but slow by most standards.

**Time zone.** The Marshall Islands is UTC+12, meaning it is on the same calendar day as eastern Australia but 21 hours ahead of New York.

**Safety.** The Marshall Islands has a low crime rate. Petty theft can occur in Majuro; take normal precautions. Typhoon season runs from November through April, though direct hits are rare.
