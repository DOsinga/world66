Ujelang is a small, lozenge-shaped atoll at the far western edge of the Marshall Islands, one of the remotest inhabited places in the chain — and, for most of its recent history, a sad one. The original population of nearby [Enewetak](/australiaandpacific/marshallislands/enewetakatoll) was relocated here in 1947 so that the United States could use Enewetak for nuclear weapons testing, and the Enewetakese then spent more than thirty years on Ujelang in severe hardship before being allowed back in 1980. After that, Ujelang itself was largely abandoned.

There is no airstrip and no regular boat service. The atoll is effectively off-limits to travellers today — included here for completeness rather than as a destination.
