Pohnpei is the largest and tallest island in the Federated States of Micronesia, and one of the wettest places on earth — its peaks collect enough rain to feed over 40 rivers and waterfalls that cascade through dense tropical forest. The island is the seat of the national capital, Palikir, but feels anything but urban. This is a place for hikers, divers and anyone drawn to the genuinely remote.

The island's greatest mystery is **Nan Madol**, sometimes called the Venice of the Pacific — a ruined city of artificial islets linked by canals, built from massive basalt columns on a reef flat off the southeast coast. It once housed a thriving royal civilisation, and much about its construction remains unexplained. A knowledgeable local guide is essential for a visit.

The diving around Pohnpei is superb. The lagoon is rich with nutrients carried by mangrove channels, attracting sharks, sea turtles, manta rays and dense coral gardens. The nearby atolls of **Ant** and **Pakin**, reached by a short boat ride, offer pristine wall diving and pass drifts in near-total solitude.

On land, Pohnpei is famous for **sakau**, a kava-like drink pounded from wild pepper root and served at communal ceremonies. Watching it being prepared — the rhythmic pounding, the earthy liquid squeezed through hibiscus bark — is an experience unique to the island. The energetic traditional dances are another highlight, performed at community gatherings and cultural events.
