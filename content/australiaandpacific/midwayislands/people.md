---
title: "People"
type: section
---

Midway has no permanent civilian population. The islands are staffed by a small rotating crew of US Fish & Wildlife Service personnel, along with occasional contractors and volunteers. There are no indigenous inhabitants and never have been — the atoll was uninhabited when American whalers first landed in the mid-19th century.

At peak, the US Navy stationed several thousand personnel on Sand Island. Today the year-round population is a handful of people, vastly outnumbered by the millions of seabirds that claim the islands as their own.
