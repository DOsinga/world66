---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Nauru's coastline is mostly rocky reef and ironshore, with only limited stretches of sandy beach. The best of them is at **Anibare Bay** on the eastern side of the island — a long, calm bay with white sand, clear water, and a reef edge suitable for snorkelling. It's quiet, clean, and rarely crowded.

Near the Menen Hotel on the western coast there is a narrow sandy stretch that is pleasant for swimming in calm conditions. The reef here can be rough in rougher weather.

The reef encircles the entire island and is exposed at low tide. It is worth walking out across it at low water to look at the marine life in the pools, but take care — the ironshore is sharp and the reef drops steeply on the ocean side.

For serious diving and snorkelling, Anibare Bay remains the main destination. The underwater visibility is excellent and the marine life, while not as dense as some more celebrated Pacific destinations, is undisturbed.
