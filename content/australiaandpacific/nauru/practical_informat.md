## Money

The Australian Dollar (AUD) is the official currency. Cash is king — credit and debit cards are rarely accepted anywhere on the island. There is one ATM, located at the Capelle & Partner store. Bring enough Australian dollars in cash to cover your entire stay, including unexpected delays. There are no exchange offices on the island.

## Getting Connected

Mobile coverage exists on the island. Digicel operates the main network. Wi-fi is available at some hotels and restaurants but is slow and intermittent.

## Health

Nauru has a government hospital but medical facilities are limited. For anything serious, medical evacuation to Australia is required. Bring adequate supplies of any prescription medication. Travel insurance with medical evacuation cover is strongly recommended.

Tap water is not reliably safe to drink. Buy bottled water or treat your own.

## Food Supply

Most food in Nauru is imported from Australia and arrives by ship or air, sometimes only every six to eight weeks. Stock at shops and restaurants can run low between deliveries. Expect limited choice and occasional shortages. The main hotel restaurants are the most reliable option.

## Alcohol

Alcohol is available in hotels and restaurants. There are no significant restrictions on tourists, but be aware that Nauru has a strong Christian culture and modesty is appreciated in public settings.

## Departure Tax

A departure tax applies to all passengers leaving by air. As of the most recent information available, this was around A$10–25 per person; confirm the current amount when you book.

## Emergency Contacts

Police and emergency services can be reached through the hotel or guesthouse. The island is small enough that help arrives quickly.
