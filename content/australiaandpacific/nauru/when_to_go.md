---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Nauru sits almost exactly on the Equator, so temperatures barely vary year-round. Expect highs around 32°C (90°F) and lows around 26°C (79°F) whatever month you arrive. The difference between seasons is mostly about rain.

The dry season runs roughly March to October. This is the better time to visit — more sunshine, less rain, and more manageable humidity. Outdoor activities like snorkelling at Anibare Bay or hiking to Command Ridge are more enjoyable in these conditions.

The wet season runs November to February. Full cyclones are rare this close to the Equator, but expect heavy rain, persistent cloud, and frequent thunderstorms. Travel can be disrupted and the landscape becomes muddier and less inviting.

Given the infrequency of flights, your visit dates will largely be dictated by the airline schedule rather than ideal weather windows. Check Nauru Airlines for current routes and pick the closest dry-season window that works for your connections.
