New Caledonia is a French special collectivity in the southwest Pacific, roughly 1,500 km east of Australia. It consists of one large island — Grande Terre — and several smaller island groups: the Loyalty Islands (Lifou, Maré, Ouvéa), the Isle of Pines, and a handful of remote atolls. The total population is around 265,000.

The main island, Grande Terre, is dominated by a central mountain range that runs its length, separating a dry, savanna-like west coast from a lush, wetter east coast. The lagoon encircling the archipelago is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — one of the largest coral lagoons in the world, with extraordinary marine diversity and visibility.

[Noumea](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/noumea), the capital, is a genuinely French city, with good restaurants, a lively waterfront, and the excellent Tjibaou Cultural Centre. Beyond Noumea, the island rewards slow travel: the east coast road through [Hienghene](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/hienghene) and [Poindimie](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/poindimie) is one of the most scenic drives in the Pacific.

## Political context

New Caledonia has been living with a deep political fracture for decades. The indigenous Kanak people — roughly 41% of the population — have long sought independence from France, while European settlers and other immigrant communities generally favor remaining French. This tension produced the deadly conflicts of the 1980s and was partially addressed by the 1998 Nouméa Accord, which promised a gradual transfer of powers and three independence referendums (all voted no, though the third in 2021 was boycotted by pro-independence groups).

In May 2024, France's attempt to expand the provincial electoral roll sparked the most serious violence since the 1980s. Riots across the territory left at least 13 dead and caused enormous damage to infrastructure and businesses, particularly in Noumea's suburbs. A state of emergency was declared and thousands of security forces were deployed. The overnight curfew was lifted in December 2024, and by mid-2025 tourist advisory levels had been downgraded across the board.

The situation today is broadly stable but politically unresolved. A new framework — the Bougival Accord — proposed creating a "State of New Caledonia" with its own nationality within the French Republic, but it does not guarantee full independence and remains contested. Demonstrations, road blockades, and flare-ups can still occur with little warning. Travelers should stay informed.

## Destinations in New Caledonia

- [Noumea](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/noumea) — the capital, with the best restaurants, museums, and nightlife
- [Ile des Pins](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/iledespins) — crystal lagoons, natural swimming pools, and extraordinary beaches
- [Lifou](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/lifou) — the largest Loyalty Island, with impressive sea cliffs and a laid-back atmosphere
- [Ouvea](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/ouvea) — a coral atoll with a stunning 25-km white beach; deeply scarred by the 1988 hostage crisis
- [Hienghene](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/hienghene) — dramatic black rock formations on the northeast coast
- [Poindimie](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/poindimie) — a quiet east coast base for rainforest and river exploration
- [Ile Ouen](/australiaandpacific/newcaledonia/ileouen) — a day-trip island south of Noumea, good for snorkeling
