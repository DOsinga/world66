story: "The cave takes its name from Queen Hortense — born Kanedjo — who took refuge here during the tribal wars of succession in 1855 and 1856. She was the last queen of the island's Kanak chiefs, and the cave became associated with her memory after her death."
---

A limestone cave set in lush vegetation, reached by a short trail from the road. Inside: giant stalactites, banyan roots working through the ceiling, and resident bats. It's not a grand cave system, but the setting is atmospheric and the Kanak historical connection gives it weight.

Entry fee XPF 300 per person. A guide from the local clan usually accompanies visits and explains the cave's history.
