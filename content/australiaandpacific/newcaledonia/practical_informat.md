---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency.** The CFP franc (XPF or FCFP) is the only accepted currency. It is pegged to the euro. Cards (Visa and Mastercard) are widely accepted in Noumea and tourist areas; carry cash when heading to rural Grande Terre or the outer islands, where ATMs are scarce. ATMs in Noumea accept most international cards but fees can be high.

**Language.** French is the official language and the only one you can reliably count on across the territory. English is spoken in some tourist-facing businesses in Noumea but don't assume it outside the capital. Learning a few French phrases will go a long way. Kanak languages are spoken in many communities; local greetings are warmly received.

**Visas.** New Caledonia is a French collectivity. European Union citizens do not need a visa. Citizens of most other Western countries (including Australia, New Zealand, USA, Canada) can enter visa-free for stays of up to 90 days. Check French government guidance for current entry requirements.

**Safety.** The situation in 2025-2026 is broadly stable but has not returned to pre-2024 normal. Noumea functions normally; tourist areas and the outer islands are welcoming travelers without incident. However, political tensions remain, and protests, road blockades, and isolated outbreaks of violence can occur with little warning, particularly in the suburbs of Noumea (e.g., Dumbea, Paita, Mont-Dore) and on rural Grande Terre. Avoid large gatherings and roadblocks, and check current travel advisories from your government before you go. The US State Department maintained a Level 3 advisory into 2025; Australia and Canada had eased to Level 2 by mid-2025.

**Health.** No vaccinations are required for entry, though being up to date on routine immunizations is sensible. Dengue fever is present; use insect repellent. Medical care in Noumea is of a reasonable standard; the outer islands have limited facilities. Travel insurance with medical evacuation cover is strongly recommended.

**Electricity.** 220V, 50Hz. Australian/New Zealand-style plugs (type I) are standard.

**Time zone.** UTC+11 year-round (no daylight saving time).

**Internet.** Mobile data and WiFi are good in Noumea and most towns. Coverage can be patchy in the interior and on some islands.

**Opening hours.** Shops in Noumea often close for a lunch break (around 12:00-14:00) and may close on Sunday afternoon. Outside the capital, hours can be unpredictable — don't count on anything being open on Sunday.
