Te Puia is Rotorua's largest geothermal park and the site of Pohutu Geyser, which erupts up to 20 times a day and reaches heights of 30 metres — one of the most active geysers in the Southern Hemisphere. The park also contains the New Zealand Māori Arts and Crafts Institute, where you can watch carvers and weavers working in traditional styles. The combination of hissing vents, boiling mud pools, and the smell of sulphur is genuinely impressive and not easily forgotten.
