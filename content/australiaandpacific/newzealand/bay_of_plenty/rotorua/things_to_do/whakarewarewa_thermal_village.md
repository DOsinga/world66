Adjacent to Te Puia but run separately by the Tūhourangi Ngāti Wāhiao people who actually live here, Whakarewarewa is a working village built around geothermal vents. Residents still cook in the boiling pools, and the village has been continuously inhabited for centuries. Guided tours are led by village members, which gives the experience a grounded quality that the more polished cultural shows in town lack. You'll see geysers, silica terraces, and a Māori meeting house — and talk to people who call this extraordinary place home.
