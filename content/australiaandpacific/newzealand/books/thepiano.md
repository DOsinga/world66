Jane Campion and Kate Pullinger's novelisation of the 1993 film follows Ada McGrath, a mute Scottish woman who arrives in mid-nineteenth-century New Zealand with her young daughter and her beloved piano for an arranged marriage. The story unfolds on the wild black-sand beaches of the west coast, where the collision between European settlers and Maori, between duty and desire, plays out against a landscape that feels both beautiful and threatening.

The film was shot primarily at Karekare Beach near Auckland, and if you visit you'll recognise the brooding coastline immediately. The novel adds interior depth that the film can only hint at, making it a fine companion read before or after a trip to New Zealand's west coast.
