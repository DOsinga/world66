Temuka is a small town in South Canterbury, set on the rich farmland between the Rangitata and Opihi rivers. It is best known to the outside world as the birthplace of Richard Pearse, the shy farmer-inventor who may have achieved powered flight in 1903 or 1904 — around the same time as the Wright Brothers, though without their financial backing or engineering resources. Debate over the exact date continues, but local pride is fierce and the story is worth knowing before you arrive.

The town's main street preserves picturesque Victorian and Edwardian facades, and the surrounding landscape is fishing country: the Rangitata and Opihi rivers are renowned for quinnat salmon (running from November to April) and trout. Anglers come specifically for the area, and experienced guides are available. The lakes of the Mackenzie Country to the south and the Rakaia River to the north extend the options considerably.

Temuka also makes a practical base for exploring the broader Canterbury region, with the coast, the Southern Alps, and the Mackenzie Basin all within reach.
