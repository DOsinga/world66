---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The Coromandel has more coastline than most visitors manage to cover, split between the sheltered western shore and the Pacific-facing east. The eastern beaches are generally the better ones for swimming and surfing.

**Hahei** is a small settlement with a good beach and easy access to Cathedral Cove. **Hot Water Beach**, a short drive south, is famous for its thermal springs accessible at low tide. **New Chums Beach**, north of Whangapoua, regularly appears on New Zealand's best-beach lists — it's a 30-minute walk each way along a coastal track with no road access, which keeps it quiet. **Whangamatā** on the east coast has a long beach popular with surfers. On the west coast, the beaches around Coromandel Town and further north are sheltered and calm, better for swimming with children than for surf.
