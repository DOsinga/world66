---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Most visitors arrive by car from Auckland, which takes around 90 minutes to Thames at the base of the peninsula via State Highway 2 through the Bombay Hills. The road up the western coast (SH25) continues to Coromandel Town in another hour; reaching the eastern coast at Whitianga takes roughly two hours from Auckland via SH2 and the Kopu–Hikuai saddle road. The road over the Coromandel Range connecting the two coasts (SH25A via Tairua) is winding and scenic.

A passenger ferry runs between Auckland and Coromandel Town seasonally, a useful option if you want to avoid driving — the crossing takes around two hours. Check current operators as services change year to year.

Thames is the main gateway town at the base of the peninsula and has bus connections from Auckland. Getting further around the peninsula without a car is difficult; the towns are spread out and local bus services are limited.
