New Zealand's only narrow-gauge mountain railway, built by potter Barry Brickell entirely by hand starting in the 1970s. The 3km track climbs through regenerating kauri forest on the hills above Coromandel Town, passing through tunnels and across viaducts to a viewpoint at the top called the Eyefull Tower. The 50-minute return trip is part heritage curiosity, part native bush walk by rail. It doubles as a kauri reforestation project — Brickell planted thousands of kauri seedlings along the route over the decades.
