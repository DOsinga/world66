The most photographed building in New Zealand, and for good reason. Sir George Troup's 1906 Flemish Renaissance station is all dark basalt and Oamaru limestone, topped with a dome and decorated with Royal Doulton mosaic floors inside. Troup earned the nickname "Gingerbread George" for his ornate designs. The station now houses the New Zealand Sports Hall of Fame and an art gallery, and is the departure point for the Taieri Gorge Railway — a scenic train journey up the Taieri River gorge that's one of the best rail trips in the country.
