Taiaroa Head, at the tip of Otago Peninsula, is the only place in the world where northern royal albatross breed on a mainland. These are enormous birds — wingspans up to three metres — and seeing one bank in off the sea and land on the clifftop nest site is genuinely spectacular. The Royal Albatross Centre runs guided tours that take you to viewing hides with telescopes. Book ahead in the October–March breeding season when the birds are most active. The head itself is a former Maori pa, then fortified in the 1880s, and you can also visit the unusual Armstrong Disappearing Gun mounted below ground.
