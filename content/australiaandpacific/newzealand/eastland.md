Eastland, or Tairawhiti, is the remote east coast of New Zealand's North Island. It was the first part of the country to see the sun each day and the first place Captain Cook set foot in 1769. The region is strongly Maori — the Maori name means "the coast upon which the sun shines across the water" — and the culture is more visible here than in most of New Zealand.

[Gisborne](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/eastland/gisborne) is the only city. Beyond it, the coast road runs north through small settlements and past empty beaches toward the East Cape lighthouse, the easternmost point on the mainland.
