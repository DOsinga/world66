---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

New Zealand is a long way from almost everywhere. From eastern Australia it's about 3 hours by air. From Asia (Singapore, Tokyo, Hong Kong) it's 10–12 hours. From Los Angeles it's around 12–13 hours nonstop. From London you're looking at 24–28 hours with a connection, usually through Dubai, Singapore, or a US city.

The main international gateway is [Auckland](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/auckland) (AKL), which handles the majority of long-haul flights. [Christchurch](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/christchurch) (CHC) and [Wellington](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/wellington) (WLG) also receive international flights, primarily from Australia. Queenstown (ZQN) has some direct Australian services. Air New Zealand is the national carrier and has the most comprehensive network. Qantas, Emirates, Singapore Airlines, and Cathay Pacific also fly in. Budget carrier Jetstar operates trans-Tasman routes.

**Visas and entry requirements**

New Zealand and Australian citizens have automatic entry rights. Most other nationalities visiting for tourism need either a visa or an NZeTA (New Zealand Electronic Travel Authority). The NZeTA is available to passport holders of around 60 visa-waiver countries — apply online or via the official app before travel. Since October 2024 the NZeTA costs NZ$17 (app) or NZ$23 (online), plus the International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy (IVL) of NZ$100, so budget around NZ$117–123 total. The NZeTA is valid for two years and covers multiple visits.

If your country is not on the visa-waiver list, apply for a Visitor Visa, which allows stays of up to 6 months.

**Traveller Declaration**

All arriving passengers — including New Zealand citizens — must complete the New Zealand Traveller Declaration (NZTD) before arrival. It covers customs, immigration, and biosecurity. Do it via the free app or website; it opens 24 hours before departure.

**Biosecurity**

New Zealand has strict biosecurity rules. Declare everything — food, wooden items, soil, outdoor equipment, fresh produce. Failing to declare biosecurity-risk items (fruit, seeds, honey, plant material) carries an on-the-spot fine of NZ$400. There are amnesty bins in the arrivals hall if you decide to ditch something before the checkpoint.

**By sea**

Cargo ships with passenger berths call at Auckland, Wellington, and Lyttelton. A handful of cruise lines include New Zealand in South Pacific itineraries, with stops in Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch (via Lyttelton), Dunedin, and the Marlborough Sounds.
