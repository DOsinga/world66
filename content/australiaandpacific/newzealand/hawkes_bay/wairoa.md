Wairoa is a quiet river town in northern Hawke’s Bay, set where the Wairoa River meets the sea. It is not a tourist hub, but it is a useful base for some of the best trout fishing in New Zealand and for reaching the wild Mahia Peninsula.

The town itself stretches along the river, with a Heritage Trails Walk starting at Marine Parade. The Portland Lighthouse — built of solid kauri on Portland Island in 1878 and the last in New Zealand to use kerosene lamps — now stands on the parade. Nearby, the Memorial Park Riverside Walkway leads to the birthsite of Sir James Carroll, New Zealand’s first Maori Acting Prime Minister, and past remnants of the old river wharves from Wairoa’s days as a working port.

The real draws are outside town. The rivers around Wairoa are world-class for trout fishing. Morere Hot Springs, off SH2, offer hot mineral pools in native bush. The Mahia Peninsula has safe swimming beaches at Opoutama, Mahanga, and Taylors Bay, and good surfing at Black’s Beach. Game fishing off Mahia is excellent in season.
