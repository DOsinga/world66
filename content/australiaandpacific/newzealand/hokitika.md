Hokitika is a small coastal town on the West Coast of the South Island, known above all for its greenstone (pounamu) — the jade-like stone that was deeply significant to Māori and continues to be worked and sold here in galleries and craft shops throughout the town. The greenstone buying is genuine rather than tourist-trap; this is where the real carvers work.

The Hokitika Gorge, a short drive inland, is one of the most photographed spots on the West Coast — a narrow limestone gorge with startlingly turquoise water. The town itself sits at the mouth of the Hokitika River on a wild West Coast beach, and the sunsets looking back toward the mountains are spectacular. The Hokitika Wildfoods Festival in March draws a crowd keen on eating unusual things.
