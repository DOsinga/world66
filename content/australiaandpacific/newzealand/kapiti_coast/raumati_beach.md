Raumati Beach is a small seaside suburb on the Kapiti Coast, sitting between Paraparaumu to the north and Paekakariki to the south. It has a long sandy beach facing west across the Tasman Sea, with Kapiti Island sitting just offshore — close enough to look enormous at sunset.

The village centre is modest: a few cafes, a dairy, and not much else. That's mostly the point. People come here to slow down, walk the beach, and watch the light change over the island. At low tide the beach stretches wide and firm, good for walking or a run. Swimming is possible but the surf can be choppy.

Raumati is effectively suburban — many residents commute to Wellington on the train, which stops nearby at Paraparaumu. For access to Kapiti Island itself, boats depart from Paraparaumu Beach; a permit is required and should be booked well ahead.
