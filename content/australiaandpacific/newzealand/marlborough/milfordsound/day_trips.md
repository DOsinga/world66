**Fiordland National Park**

A day trip from Milford Sound can take you through the 14 fiords in
Fiordland National Park. Real Journeys Excursions have several
different day trips that are interesting and take you to see some of
the most beautiful scenery on the face of the earth. It is advisable to
go in these areas with a guide because of the natural wildlife and the
remoteness of the area.

**Doubtful Sound**

Visit Doubtful Sound and area of wilderness and wildlife that is
much larger than Milford Sound. Here bottlenose dolphins and seals play
in the waters and you may even see the crested penguin that roams the
area. This is an area of untouched wilderness and filled with caves,
peaks, and rainforests. It is hard to
believe there is civilization only a short way away.

An excursion to Doubtful Sound starts with a cruise across Lake
Manapouri and then a bus ride over Wilmot Pass through the rainforest.
At the sound you go by catamaran manned by a naturalist that will give
you information about the area. This tour costs about $200 for adults
and $50 for children, but it is well worth it.

Included in the Doubtful Sound Excursion is a trip to the Manapouri
Underground Power Station. You can also go there on its own in another
excursion. This Power Station can’t be seen from land. The only thing
you will see is a control building, switchyard and some electrical
lines going across one area of the lake. Everything else is
underground. Here the water of the lake generates electricity. Costs
for this tour separated from the Doubtful Sound Excursion runs $65 for
adults and $20 for children.

**Te Anau Glowworm Caves**

The Te Anau Glowworm Caves used to be a legend until Lawson Burrows
vowed to find them and did in 1948 after three years of searching.
These limestone caves provide you with the most beautiful limestone
formations, whirlpools, and a large and very noisy waterfall. The caves
are relatively young and are still being carved by the rushing river
inside. You will board a small boat and be taken out to a grotto where
you will see a magnificent display of glittery glowworms that will
delight the eye and astound you. Cost for this tour is $60 for adults
and $20 for children.

**Stewart Island**

Stewart Island is another place worth seeing while in Milford Sound.
The island has sandy beaches and is home for multitudes of aquatic life
and birds. Here you can take a boat to Paterson Inlet, stopping at a
historic whaler’s base, and then go for a ride on a semi-submersible
vessel to see what is under the water. Also available is a walking tour. If you wish to go biking, you can rent a bike or scooter. Cost for
the walking tour is $45.
