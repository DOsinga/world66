Motueka sits at the head of Tasman Bay on New Zealand's South Island, a sun-soaked town that serves as the main gateway to [Abel Tasman National Park](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/abeltasmannationalpark). The surrounding region produces most of New Zealand's hops, apples, and kiwifruit, and the orchards and vineyards give the landscape a gentle, cultivated feel that contrasts with the wild coastline just to the north.

The town itself is relaxed and unpretentious — a single main street with good cafes, a Sunday market, and enough outdoor gear shops to remind you why you came. Most visitors use Motueka as a base for the Abel Tasman Coast Track, one of New Zealand's Great Walks, or for kayaking trips along the golden-sand bays of the national park. Water taxis depart from nearby Kaiteriteri and Marahau.

Beyond Abel Tasman, the area offers excellent cycling along the Great Taste Trail through vineyards and hop gardens, rock climbing at Paynes Ford, and day trips south to [Nelson](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/nelson) or the remote northwest coast of Kahurangi National Park. The Motueka River is one of the South Island's best brown trout fisheries.
