The **North Island** of [New Zealand](/australiaandpacific/newzealand) is home to around three-quarters of the country's population. It's warmer, more urbanised, and geologically more active than the South Island — the Taupo Volcanic Zone cuts across the centre of the island, producing geysers, boiling mud pools, and the thermal lakes around [Rotorua](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/rotorua) and [Taupo](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/taupo). Mount Ruapehu, an active volcano, dominates the Central Plateau.

[Auckland](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/auckland) is the largest city by far — around 1.7 million people live in the greater Auckland region. [Wellington](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/wellington), at the southern tip, is the capital. [Northland](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/northland) to the north of Auckland holds the [Bay of Islands](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/bayofislands) and Cape Reinga, the northernmost point of the country. [Taranaki](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/taranaki) on the west coast is dominated by Mount Taranaki, a near-perfect volcanic cone.
