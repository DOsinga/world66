Originally founded in 1852, the Auckland Museum moved to its current location in the Domain in 1929, when it was rebuilt as a war memorial after the losses of World War I.

The exhibits occupy three floors. The ground floor is devoted to Maori culture and the related Polynesian cultures of the Pacific islands, with artifacts and displays that trace the history and traditions of both. The second floor covers Natural History, with an emphasis on New Zealand's unique flora and fauna. The third floor houses the Auckland War Memorial, where the names of soldiers and sailors from the region who died in combat are carved into marble walls, surrounded by galleries tracing New Zealand's military history from the colonial-era wars through to current peacekeeping missions.

A koha (donation) of $5 is appreciated for adult admission. Children under 16 are free.
