The **Bay of Plenty** is a sun-drenched region on the northeastern coast of New Zealand's North Island, named by Captain Cook for the abundance of food he found when he arrived in 1769. It stretches from Waihi Beach in the west to Opotiki in the east, with some of the best beaches and warmest weather in the country.

[Tauranga](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/tauranga) and the neighbouring beach town of Mount Maunganui are the main urban centres — popular for surfing, swimming, and kiwifruit orchards. Inland, [Rotorua](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/rotorua) has geothermal attractions and strong Maori cultural heritage, while [Taupo](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/taupo), further south, sits on New Zealand's largest lake. White Island (Whakaari), an active marine volcano, is visible offshore from Whakatane and can be visited by boat or helicopter.
