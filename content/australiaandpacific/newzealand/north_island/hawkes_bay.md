Hawke's Bay lies on the sunny east coast of the [North Island](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/north_island) of [New Zealand](/australiaandpacific/newzealand) — one of the country's warmest and driest regions, and its oldest wine-producing area. The landscape is a mix of rolling farmland, orchards, and vineyards backed by the Kaweka and Ruahine ranges.

[Napier](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/napier) is the region's most distinctive town, rebuilt almost entirely in Art Deco style after a devastating earthquake in 1931. Hastings, its twin city just 20 minutes south, is the commercial centre and gateway to the wine country. Between them, the two towns offer more than 70 wineries, most open for tastings and many with excellent restaurants attached.

The region is also known for its food — orchards, olive groves, farmers' markets, and a strong local produce culture. Cape Kidnappers, at the southern end of the bay, has one of the world's largest mainland gannet colonies, accessible on foot or by tractor at low tide.
