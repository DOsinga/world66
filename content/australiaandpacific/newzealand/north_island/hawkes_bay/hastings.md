Hastings is the commercial hub of the Hawke's Bay region, sitting inland from [Napier](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/napier) amid fertile plains. While Napier gets the architectural attention, Hastings and surrounding areas offer the best access to the region's wineries — many of New Zealand's most respected red wines come from the Gimblett Gravels district just northwest of town.

The Hastings Farmers' Market on Sunday mornings is one of the best in New Zealand. Te Mata Peak, a short drive south, offers panoramic views across the plains to the coast. The beaches at Ocean Beach and Waimarama are excellent in summer, and paragliding from Te Mata Peak is popular with thrill seekers. The town has a growing arts and gallery scene, and the local food culture — orchards, organic growers, wine — is the real draw.
