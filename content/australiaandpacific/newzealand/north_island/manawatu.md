The Manawatu is a region of the lower North Island named for its great river — powerful enough to cut a gorge through the Ruahine Range that divides the eastern and western coasts. The Manawatu Gorge is one of the more dramatic natural features of the lower North Island, and the river drains catchments on both sides of the main mountain range.

The largest city is [Palmerston North](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/palmerstonnorth), a university town with a pleasant central square and good transport connections. [Levin](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/levin) is the main town in the Horowhenua district to the southwest. The region is primarily agricultural — flat, green, and productive — but the surrounding ranges offer solid tramping.
