The Waikato is New Zealand's largest region by population and occupies the broad lowland plains south of Auckland. It is named for the Waikato River, which runs 425 km from Lake Taupo to the Tasman Sea — New Zealand's longest river, flowing through the heart of the region.

The farming country here is some of the most productive in New Zealand, a landscape of green paddocks, dairy farms, and the kind of rolling hills that earned Waikato its reputation as the country's thoroughbred heartland. The largest city is [Hamilton](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/hamilton), which serves as the regional centre. The Waitomo Caves, where glowworms light the roof of a cave river, are the single biggest attraction in the region and worth the detour from any itinerary. Lake Taupo, at the region's southern edge, is the country's largest lake and a hub for trout fishing, water sports, and bungee jumping.
