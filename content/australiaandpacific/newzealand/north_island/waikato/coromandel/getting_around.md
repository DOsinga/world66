---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

A car is effectively essential for exploring the Coromandel. The peninsula is long and narrow, the main towns are spread along both coasts, and the road over the range is the only connection between east and west. Many of the best beaches require driving down side roads, and the northern tip (the Coromandel Walkway, Colville) is a considerable drive from anywhere.

The main road up the western coast (SH25) is well sealed. The eastern coastal route between Thames and Whitianga via the Kopu–Hikuai Road is narrower and slower — allow more time than the map suggests. Some back roads to remote beaches are unsealed and may not be suitable for rental cars; check your rental agreement and conditions.
