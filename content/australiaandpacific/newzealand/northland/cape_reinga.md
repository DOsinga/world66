---
title: "Cape Reinga"
type: location
---

This is the northern most accessible part of the North Island, and from
here you can see the meeting of the Pacific Ocean and the Tasman Sea
which forms a white line of water, stretching into the horizon.
The two seas are different colours, with different moods and
tides.

The Tasman Sea is on the west coast, and in New Zealand, the west coast
is known as being the more wild side. Standing looking back to
the west coast you will be able to see Te Werahi, with the surf waves
clearly noticeable. Looking to the Pacific side, you will notice
that the waves do not have too much surf.
