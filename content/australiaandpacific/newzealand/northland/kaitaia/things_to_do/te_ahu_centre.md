The Te Ahu Centre is Kaitaia's civic and cultural heart — a combined museum, visitor information centre, library, and cinema under one roof. The museum covers the Far North with genuine care: the five local iwi (Ngāti Kuri, Te Rarawa, Ngāpuhi, Ngāti Kahu, and Te Aupouri), the Dalmatian gum-diggers who came here from Croatia in the 19th century, and the kauri timber industry that shaped the region. The displays are well done and the staff are knowledgeable. Worth a stop of an hour or so, especially on a wet day.
