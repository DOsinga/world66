Fiordland lies in the southwest corner of the [South Island](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/south_island), and most of it is protected as Fiordland National Park — one of the wildest and most untouched regions in [New Zealand](/australiaandpacific/newzealand). There are places here where no one has ever set foot, only flown over.

The landscape is dramatic: deep fiords cut into sheer mountain walls, dense temperate rainforest, cascading waterfalls, and a coastline so remote that parts of it have barely been explored. Milford Sound is the most famous and most visited fiord, but Doubtful Sound is larger and quieter. The Milford Track, one of New Zealand's Great Walks, runs through the heart of the park.

[Te Anau](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/otago/fiordland/te_anau) is the gateway town, sitting on the shore of Lake Te Anau and serving as the base for trips into the park.
