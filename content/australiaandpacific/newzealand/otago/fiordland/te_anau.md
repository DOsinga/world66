Te Anau is a small town on the eastern shore of Lake Te Anau, the largest lake in the South Island. It sits roughly halfway between Queenstown and Milford Sound — about two hours' drive to each — making it the natural base for exploring [Fiordland](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/otago/fiordland).

The town is the departure point for the Milford Track, one of New Zealand's Great Walks, and for boat trips to the Te Anau Glowworm Caves. If you are driving to Milford Sound, fill up here — there is no petrol between Te Anau and Milford, and the road passes through some of the most remote forest in the country.
