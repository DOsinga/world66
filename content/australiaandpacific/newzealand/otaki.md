Ōtaki is a small town on the Kapiti Coast in the lower North Island of New Zealand, about 70 kilometres north of Wellington. It's significant in New Zealand Māori history as one of the heartlands of the Ngāti Raukawa ki te Tonga iwi, and the town has a higher proportion of Māori residents than most Kapiti Coast settlements. The Rangiātea Church, built in 1851 and consecrated by the great Māori chief and Christian convert Rauparaha, was one of New Zealand's most important Māori churches before it burned down in 1995; a replica was completed in 2003.

The town has developed something of a reputation as an outlet shopping destination — the Ōtaki Factory Shopping area draws visitors from Wellington and the Wairarapa — and the old main street has a mix of antique shops, cafes, and food outlets that make it a pleasant stop. The beach is accessible about 3 kilometres west of the town, and the Tararua Forest Park is accessible to the east.

Most travelers pass through on the way to or from Wellington, but Ōtaki is worth a stop for the church, the Māori cultural connections, and the good food options on the main street.
