Home to the Pohutu Geyser, which erupts up to twenty times a day and reaches heights of 30 metres. The complex also houses the New Zealand Maori Arts and Crafts Institute, where you can watch carving and weaving demonstrations. Evening cultural performances include a hangi feast cooked in underground earth ovens.
