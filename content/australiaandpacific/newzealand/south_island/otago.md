Otago is one of [New Zealand](/australiaandpacific/newzealand)'s original provinces, stretching from the southeastern coast deep into the Southern Alps. Its largest city is [Dunedin](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/dunedin), founded by Scottish settlers in 1848 and named after Edinburgh — Scottish place names are everywhere in the region.

Gold was discovered in [Central Otago](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/south_island/otago/central_otago) in the 1860s, triggering a rush that shaped the landscape. When the gold ran out, the miners left behind a dry, dramatic countryside of schist rock, tawny tussock, and deep gorges. For nearly a century it was quietly forgotten. Today, centred on [Queenstown](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/queenstown), tourism is the new gold — though the old mining heritage, the vineyards of the Bannockburn and Cromwell Basin, and the Otago Central Rail Trail offer a quieter, more rewarding way to experience the region.
