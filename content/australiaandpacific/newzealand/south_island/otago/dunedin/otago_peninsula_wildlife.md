Beyond the albatross colony, Otago Peninsula has more wildlife than almost anywhere else accessible by road in New Zealand. Yellow-eyed penguins — one of the world's rarest — come ashore at several beaches around dusk; Penguin Place near Harington Point has hides set into the dunes where you can watch without disturbing them. New Zealand fur seals lounge on rocks at the peninsula's tip. Little blue penguins nest along the shoreline. Several operators run wildlife cruises from Dunedin's waterfront that take you around the harbour and out to the peninsula's rocky shore.
