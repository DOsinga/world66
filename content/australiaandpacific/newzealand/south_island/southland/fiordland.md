Fiordland occupies the wild southwest corner of the [South Island](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/south_island), a vast expanse of mountain, rainforest, and deep glacial fiords that makes up one of [New Zealand's](/australiaandpacific/newzealand) most spectacular national parks. There are places here where no one has ever set foot — only flown over. The scale is extraordinary: over 1.2 million hectares of near-untouched wilderness, enough rain to keep everything perpetually green, and a coastline carved into fourteen major fiords.

[Milford Sound](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/milfordsound) is the most famous and most visited — towering cliffs, waterfalls, and dark water accessible by a stunning mountain road or by air. But Doubtful Sound, reached via a boat crossing of Lake Manapouri, is larger, quieter, and arguably more impressive. The Milford Track, one of New Zealand's Great Walks, runs through the heart of the park. [Te Anau](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/south_island/southland/fiordland/te_anau), on the shores of the lake of the same name, is the gateway town and the place to organise trips, gear, and Department of Conservation hut bookings.
