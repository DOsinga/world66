Taupo sits on the shore of New Zealand's largest lake, which fills the caldera of a supervolcano that produced one of the most powerful eruptions in recorded history — the Hatepe eruption of 232 AD, which turned skies red as far as China. The lake is calm and beautiful today, popular for trout fishing, kayaking, and swimming, with the snow-capped peaks of Tongariro visible to the south on clear days.

The town is a popular holiday base for the central North Island, and the surrounding area is packed with things to do. The Huka Falls, a few minutes north, are a spectacular turquoise torrent where the Waikato River forces through a narrow rock channel. The Craters of the Moon geothermal walk is a surreal landscape of steaming vents and bubbling mud. And the Tongariro Alpine Crossing — widely considered the best one-day walk in New Zealand — starts about an hour's drive south.

Taupo also has a strong adventure-sports scene: skydiving, bungee jumping, jet boating, and mountain biking. The town itself is relaxed and unpretentious, with a waterfront strip of cafes and a good Saturday market. The trout fishing in the lake and its tributaries is world-class — fly-fishing guides are easy to find.
