Hamilton is New Zealand's fourth-largest city and the heart of the Waikato region, straddling the banks of the country's longest river. It is not a city that makes many tourist bucket lists, but it has a relaxed, unpretentious character and makes a solid base for exploring some of the North Island's best attractions.

The Waikato River defines the city. Walking or cycling the riverside paths is the best way to get a feel for the place, and the Hamilton Gardens -- one of New Zealand's most visited attractions -- sit right on the riverbank. The gardens are themed around different traditions, from Italian Renaissance to Japanese to Maori, and they are genuinely impressive. Allow a couple of hours.

The Tainui people, who arrived on the Tainui waka, called this stretch of river Kirikiriroa, meaning "long stretch of gravel." European settlement followed the Waikato Wars of the 1860s and the confiscation of Maori land -- a history the city is still reckoning with. The Waikato Museum on the riverfront covers both the Tainui story and the region's natural history, and is worth a visit.

Hamilton is a university town -- the University of Waikato gives it a younger, livelier feel than you might expect from a farming service centre. Victoria Street and the Hood Street area have decent cafes and restaurants, and the city has a growing craft beer scene. Beyond the centre, the [Waikato](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/waikato) region offers easy access to [Raglan](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/waikato/raglan) on the coast, the Waitomo Caves, and Hobbiton near Matamata.
