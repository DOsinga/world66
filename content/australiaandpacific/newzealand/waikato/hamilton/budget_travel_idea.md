type: section
---

Following are some **Hamilton budget travel** tips for tourists planning to visit this vibrant town with a tight budget.

**Stay**

Hamilton has all kinds of hotels to suit every pocket. Those who want to cut down on the expenses can easily find budget accommodation in Hamilton. If you plan on spending more time sightseeing Hamilton rather than staying indoors, it is best to settle for a cheaper hotel. This way you pay a reasonable amount for using the room to sleep in and keep your luggage safe.

**Travelling**

Car rental is the only travel option that Hamilton doesn’t offer. Besides that, the town has other modes of transport like buses, taxis, ferries, mopeds and bikes. Tourists that are looking to cut down on their traveling budget should definitely consider using buses rather than hiring taxis. This can result in quite a bit of savings. Buses are available all over the town. Most attractions in Hamilton can be reached easily by walking.

**Holiday Activities**

Although beaches in Hamilton are small, they are definitely the highlight of the town. Spending a day here does not cost a dime and it is a great way to relax, soak up some sun and enjoy crystal clear water. Horseshoe Bay Beach is a must-visit of all the beaches closest to Hamilton. A walk through the Spittal Pond Nature Reserve is also a budget friendly activity in Hamilton. This is the largest wildlife sanctuary in Bermuda and offers a mile long walk across a wide range of flowers and trees. Fort Hamilton is another great place to visit. It is open from 9:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. from Mondays to Fridays.

**Eating**

Hamilton has plenty of restaurants and bars. There are many hotels that serve exquisite cuisines at exorbitant rates. But tourists on a budget can reduce the amount they spend on eating by trying smaller local hotels. They serve authentic Bermudian cuisine and at affordable prices. Some of the traditional dishes that tourists must try are the hashed shark, fish chowder laced with hot peppers and black rum and conch dishes.
