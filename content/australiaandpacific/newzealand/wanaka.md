Wanaka sits on the southern shore of Lake Wanaka in New Zealand's Southern Alps, an hour's drive from [Queenstown](/australiaandpacific/newzealand/queenstown) but with a fraction of the crowds and commercial bustle. It is one of the most beautiful lake settings in New Zealand — mountain peaks rising directly from clear blue water, with the town itself low-key and unpretentious.

The lake is the centrepiece: swimming, kayaking, and paddleboarding in summer, with the famous lone willow tree (known simply as "That Wanaka Tree") drawing photographers at sunrise. The town is compact, with good cafes and restaurants along the lakefront and Ardmore Street. Roy's Peak, a steep but well-maintained track that takes about five hours return, offers one of the most photographed views in the country — a panorama of the lake and surrounding ranges that rewards every step.

In winter, Treble Cone and Cardrona are the main ski fields, both within easy reach. The Puzzling World attraction on the highway is genuinely entertaining, especially with children. Mount Aspiring National Park begins just up the Matukituki Valley — the Rob Roy Glacier track is a spectacular half-day walk through beech forest to a hanging glacier and waterfall amphitheatre.

Wanaka has grown rapidly and is no longer the sleepy alternative to Queenstown it once was, but it retains a more relaxed, outdoorsy character. The Warbirds Over Wanaka airshow, held every second Easter, is one of the best in the southern hemisphere.
