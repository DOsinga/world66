Alofi is Niue's capital and only real town, with a population of under 1,000. It sits on the west coast and looks out over a wide bay. The cliffs here are lower than on much of the island, making access to the water easier, though there is no beach — just a rocky shoreline and a concrete pier.

The town is small enough to walk around in half an hour. There are a few shops, a bank, the government buildings, the tourism office, and most of the island's restaurants and cafes. The Fale Fono (parliament) has a display of traditional tapa cloth worth a quick look.

The Niue Tourism Visitor Information Centre is useful for maps, activity booking, and advice. The market next door runs Tuesday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings — the place to buy local produce and, if you're lucky, coconut crab.

Alofi is where most practical life on Niue happens. You'll pass through repeatedly even if you're staying elsewhere.
