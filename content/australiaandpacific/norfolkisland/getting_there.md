---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Norfolk Island is served only by air. There are no passenger ferries or cruise ship terminals.

Qantas operates the most flights, running several times weekly from Sydney (about 2h 30min) and Brisbane (about 2h 10min). Air Chathams offers seasonal direct services from Auckland, which is useful if you're coming from New Zealand or want to combine the trip with a wider Pacific itinerary.

Despite being an Australian territory, you check in at the international terminal in both Sydney and Brisbane — international-style biosecurity and border formalities apply. Flights are treated as domestic for Australians, meaning no passport is technically required, but carrying one is strongly recommended in case of a diversion.

Fares fluctuate considerably. Booking two to three months out typically gets reasonable prices; mid-week departures tend to be cheaper than weekends. Shoulder season (May, or September to October) offers the best combination of lower fares and decent weather. Peak season (December to February) sees prices climb and capacity tighten, so book early.

Package deals bundling flights with car hire and accommodation are often better value than booking separately, and several operators specialise in Norfolk Island travel. Given the limited options on the island, sorting logistics before you arrive makes sense.
