---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Status and entry.** Norfolk Island is an Australian external territory. Australian citizens do not need a passport but should carry one regardless, as identity checks on arrival are thorough. Visitors from countries that require a visa for Australia must have one before flying to Norfolk Island. New Zealanders can enter without a visa.

**Currency.** The Australian dollar is the currency. Major credit cards are accepted at most tourist businesses. There is one ATM on the island, operated by the Commonwealth Bank in Burnt Pine. Carry cash as a backup — some smaller places, markets and local sellers prefer it.

**Biosecurity.** Take the biosecurity rules seriously. Norfolk Island has strict controls to protect its agriculture and native species. All food items must be declared on arrival. Fruit, vegetables, plants and seeds are prohibited. Pork and poultry from New Zealand are banned. Commercially packaged and sealed goods are generally fine but must be declared. Non-declaration can result in fines.

**Health.** Australian Medicare applies on Norfolk Island. There is a small hospital in Burnt Pine (Norfolk Island Hospital) capable of handling routine emergencies. Serious illness or injury requiring specialist care means medical evacuation to the mainland, which is expensive without travel insurance. Get comprehensive travel insurance that covers evacuation — this is not optional on a remote island two hours from the nearest major hospital.

**Communications.** Mobile coverage on the island uses Telstra's network. Roaming should work for Australian and New Zealand customers but check with your provider. Internet is available at most accommodation and some cafes, but speeds are nothing like the mainland. The island's remoteness is part of its character.

**Shopping.** Burnt Pine is the island's commercial centre — a modest strip with a supermarket, pharmacy, post office, a few clothing and souvenir shops, and the Visitor Information Centre. Opening hours are shorter than you'd expect; many businesses close early or adjust hours outside peak season. Check before you go.

**Time zone.** Norfolk Island observes UTC+11 (UTC+12 during daylight saving time, which runs from the first Sunday in October to the first Sunday in April). This puts it one hour ahead of the eastern Australian states.
