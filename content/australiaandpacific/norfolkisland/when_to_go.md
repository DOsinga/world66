---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Norfolk Island is small enough and temperate enough that any month is viable, but the differences between seasons are real.

Summer (November to April) is warm and humid, with temperatures typically between 19°C and 25°C. This is the best time for swimming, snorkelling and beach days — Emily Bay is at its best. December through February is peak season, with higher prices and the most visitors, which on a place this size is noticeable. Be aware that tropical cyclones occasionally affect the island between January and March, though direct hits are rare.

Winter (May to September) is cooler and drier, with temperatures ranging from about 14°C to 19°C. The humidity drops, which makes hiking and walking significantly more comfortable. The national park trails are particularly good in this season. Prices are lower, the island is quieter, and the sea is rougher — swimming is less appealing but not impossible.

The shoulder seasons — March to May and September to October — offer the most balanced experience: reasonable weather, manageable crowds and mid-range prices. If you're visiting for the first time, September or October are good choices.

Bounty Day on 8 June is worth planning around if the island's Pitcairn history interests you. The Kingston foreshore celebration draws the community together in a way that's genuinely hard to witness elsewhere.

Whatever time you visit, pack a light layer for evenings, even in summer. The island is subtropical but sits alone in the ocean, and the temperature drops after dark.
