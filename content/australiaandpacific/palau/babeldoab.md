Babeldaob is the largest island in [Palau](/australiaandpacific/palau) but far less developed than neighbouring [Koror](/australiaandpacific/palau/koror), connected to it by a bridge. Most of the island is covered in rainforest, with scattered villages along the coast and a new capital complex at Melekeok on the east side.

The main attractions are spread around the island and a car is essential. In Melekeok you will find ancient carved stone heads, a beautifully maintained bai (a thatched-roof traditional meeting house for chiefs and elders), and an ancient dancing ground. The best beaches on the island stretch northward from here to the northern tip. In the north, the 25-metre Taki Falls make for a good jungle walk, and the stone monoliths of Badrulchau — 37 enormous basalt columns arranged in rows on a hillside overlooking the coast — are one of the Pacific's more mysterious archaeological sites.

Japanese wartime ruins, interesting caves, and remote villages round out the picture. Babeldaob rewards a day or two of exploration if you can arrange transport.
