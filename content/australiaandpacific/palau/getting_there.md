---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Palau is not easy to reach, which partly explains why it remains uncrowded. Almost all visitors arrive by air into Roman Tmetuchl International Airport (ROR) near Koror.

**From Asia**, the main connection is via Manila with United Airlines or Philippine Airlines, with flights running a few times a week. Korean Air flies from Seoul, and there are connections through Tokyo (Narita) and Taipei. Expect at least one layover from anywhere in Europe.

**From Australia**, Qantas operates flights from Brisbane — roughly 5.5 hours — though schedules have varied. Check current timetables as Pacific routes change frequently.

**From North America**, the most common route is via Honolulu and Guam on United Airlines, continuing to Palau. Flight times from the US West Coast total around 14–16 hours including connections.

Most nationalities receive a visa on arrival valid for 30 days at no charge. Bring a passport valid for at least six months beyond your arrival date and an onward ticket.

**Entry form**: Palau requires all visitors to complete an online Palau Entry Form within 72 hours before arrival. You'll receive a QR code by email that you must show at the airport. Fill it in at the official government portal before departure.

**Environmental fee**: Every visitor pays a $100 Pristine Paradise Palau fee, which is built into airline ticket prices. This funds reef conservation and national park maintenance. A separate Rock Islands day-use permit costs $50 (valid 10 days); if you want to visit Jellyfish Lake, that's an additional $100 permit valid for 5 days.

The US dollar is the official currency. There are no fees or documentation required beyond the above for citizens of most Western countries.
