---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Palau's appeal is built almost entirely on what's underwater — and a few remarkable things that float on the surface. This is one of the world's great diving destinations, and the marine life here is extraordinary by any standard.

**The Rock Islands Southern Lagoon** is the centrepiece. About 200 uninhabited limestone mushroom-shaped islands rising from a protected lagoon, surrounded by reefs that hold some of the densest concentrations of marine life on the planet. The whole area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Day trips from Koror take you through passages, past blue holes, and along walls dropping hundreds of metres.

**Jellyfish Lake** is one of those experiences that's hard to exaggerate. A landlocked marine lake on Eil Malk island holds millions of golden jellyfish that have evolved without stingers — you can snorkel among them as they pulse through the water. It's surreal and peaceful in equal measure. Note that scuba diving is banned here, and the permit (currently $100) is separate from the Rock Islands fee.

**Diving** is world-class at sites like Blue Corner, Blue Hole, German Channel, and Chandelier Cave. Sharks — grey reef, whitetip, hammerhead — are common. Manta rays frequent German Channel year-round. The WWII wrecks in the lagoon, including Japanese ships and aircraft from the 1944 Operation Desecrate, are a bonus for wreck divers.

**[Peleliu](/australiaandpacific/palau/peleliu)** is for history. The island was the site of one of the bloodiest battles of the Pacific War, and the coral ridges, jungle, and rusting equipment left behind make for a sobering visit. The small museum gives context, but the real impact comes from walking the terrain.

**[Babeldoab](/australiaandpacific/palau/babeldoab)**, Palau's largest island, is the opposite of a beach resort. Jungle-covered hills, waterfalls, traditional villages, and the ancient stone face carvings at Badrulchau make it worth a day trip or two. The road around the island was paved with Japanese assistance in the 2000s and is now navigable by car.
