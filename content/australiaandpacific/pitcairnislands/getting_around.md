---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The island covers about 4.5 square kilometres. Three dirt roads connect Adamstown to the main landing point at Bounty Bay and to the agricultural areas inland. The total road network is roughly 6 kilometres.

**On land**

Quad bikes and three-wheeled ATVs are the standard way to get around. Islanders will often give visitors a lift or arrange guided tours by quad. The roads turn to sticky mud when it rains — which happens regularly — so sturdy footwear is useful.

Walking is entirely feasible for fit visitors. Trails lead to most of the key sites: Christian's Cave, St Paul's Pool, the lookout points above Bounty Bay. The terrain is hilly and the paths can be overgrown; a local guide is recommended for anything beyond the main tracks.

**Between islands**

The Pitcairn group includes three other islands — Henderson, Ducie, and Oeno — but none are permanently inhabited. Occasional community excursions to Oeno (a low coral atoll about 120 km to the northwest) and Henderson (a raised coral island with a remarkable endemic bird population) take place by longboat. These trips are weather-dependent and infrequent.

**At Bounty Bay**

The longboats *Tin*, *Tub*, and *Moss* transfer passengers and cargo between the island and ships anchored offshore. There is no jetty capable of taking deep-draft vessels. All arrivals and departures depend on sea conditions and the skill of the islanders who operate the boats.
