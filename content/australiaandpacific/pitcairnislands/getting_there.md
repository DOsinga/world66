---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

There is no airport on Pitcairn and no direct scheduled transport from anywhere. All visitors arrive by sea.

**The route**

Fly to Tahiti (PPT), then take an Air Tahiti flight to Mangareva (GMR) in the Gambier Islands. From Mangareva, board the MV Silver Supporter — the island's dedicated government supply and passenger vessel — for a 32-hour crossing to Pitcairn. The ship holds 12 passengers in 6 twin-share cabins, each with a private ensuite. Meals are included.

You land not at a dock but via longboat through the surf at Bounty Bay. Islanders bring you ashore. Departure works the same way in reverse.

**Booking**

The MV Silver Supporter operates on a schedule published by Pitcairn Islands Tourism. Voyages run most frequently between April and September. Stays are structured: 4-day, 11-day, or 18-day visits, depending on when the ship returns.

Contact tourism@pitcairn.pn well in advance — spaces are limited to 12 passengers per voyage and trips do sell out. Schedules are subject to change; confirm before booking onward flights.

**Cost**

Accommodation is priced at roughly US$70 per person per day for homestays, more for self-contained rental properties. Add the cost of the ship passage plus flights to Mangareva. Budget for the full package including meals.

**Visas**

Visitors staying 14 days or fewer who arrive and depart on the same vessel do not need a visa. Stays longer than 14 days require an entry clearance application submitted at least three months in advance, with a non-refundable NZ$150 fee. Apply through the Pitcairn Island government: government.pn.

**Medical insurance**

Compulsory. Your policy must include medical evacuation — the nearest hospital is days away by sea. This is not a formality.
