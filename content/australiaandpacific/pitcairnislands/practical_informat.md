**Currency**

The official currency is the New Zealand Dollar (NZD), but accommodation and souvenirs are typically priced in US Dollars. The government Treasury Office can exchange money and process credit card transactions; there is a 5% surcharge on card payments. Bring adequate cash — do not rely on card facilities for everything.

**Internet**

Since March 2024, Pitcairn has been connected via Starlink. Most homes, government buildings, and public areas have wi-fi. Connection quality is vastly better than it used to be, though it remains a remote island and service can be affected by weather.

**Phones**

Your home SIM is unlikely to work. Check with your carrier before you go. The island has a local telephone network.

**Health**

There is a basic medical facility on the island but no hospital. The doctor or nurse practitioner can handle minor issues. Anything serious means medical evacuation — which is why insurance covering evacuation is compulsory for all visitors, not optional.

Bring all prescription medications in sufficient quantity for your trip plus some margin. Nothing is obtainable locally.

**Sabbath**

The community is Seventh-day Adventist. Saturday is the Sabbath. Most activity on the island stops. No alcohol is sold anywhere on Pitcairn.

**Shopping**

Islanders sell honey, carved wooden items (including models of the Bounty and traditional Polynesian designs), woven baskets, and postage stamps — Pitcairn stamps are prized by collectors worldwide. The community store stocks basic groceries.

**Electricity**

240V, 50Hz. New Zealand-style plugs (Type I). Bring a universal adapter.

**Time zone**

UTC-8 (no daylight saving).

**Emergency contact**

In an emergency, contact the island's government or the Pitcairn Island radio operator. The official government website is government.pn.
