---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Pitcairn's climate is subtropical oceanic. Temperatures stay moderate year-round: summer (December to March) reaches around 27–28°C, while winter (June to September) drops to around 22–23°C. Rain falls throughout the year but is heaviest between November and March.

**Best time to visit: April to October**

This is the dry season and the period when the MV Silver Supporter operates most frequently. Sea conditions for the Mangareva crossing and for landing at Bounty Bay are generally calmer. The weather is warm without being oppressive.

July to October is the best window for whale watching — humpbacks migrate through the waters around Pitcairn during these months. September is typically the calmest month of the year.

**Avoid: December to March**

The wet season brings heavier rain, higher humidity, and a small but real risk of tropical cyclones. Conditions at sea are less predictable, and the ship schedule is thinner. The January to March period does offer honey season — Pitcairn honey from the Miro tree has a strong local reputation — but this is rarely enough reason to choose the more difficult time of year.

**Practical note**

The ship schedule controls when you can visit more than weather does. Check the current MV Silver Supporter schedule at visitpitcairn.pn and book around it. Voyages fill up; plan at least three to six months ahead.
