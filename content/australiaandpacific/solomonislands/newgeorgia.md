New Georgia is the largest island in the Western Province of the [Solomon Islands](/australiaandpacific/solomonislands) and the hub of some of the best diving in the Pacific. The island sits at the edge of Marovo Lagoon — one of the largest saltwater lagoons on earth — where pristine coral walls drop into deep blue water teeming with sharks, barracuda, manta rays, and sea turtles. Visibility regularly exceeds 30 metres.

The island saw heavy fighting during World War II, when American forces landed in 1943 to capture the Japanese airfield at Munda. Wrecks from the campaign litter the surrounding waters and jungle. The former airfield is now Munda Airport, the main gateway to the western Solomons and the starting point for dive trips into the lagoon.

Beyond the diving, New Georgia offers dense rainforest, traditional Melanesian villages, and some of the most striking wood carving in the Pacific. The village of Munda and the surrounding communities produce intricate inlaid carvings that are among the finest in the Solomon Islands. Skull shrines — ancestral sites where the skulls of chiefs and warriors were kept — survive in several locations and can be visited with local guides.

Getting to New Georgia requires either a flight to Munda from [Honiara](/australiaandpacific/solomonislands/honiara) or a long boat journey through the islands. Once there, transport is by outboard motorboat or on foot. Accommodation is limited to a handful of lodges and village stays, and you will need to plan ahead. The reward is one of the most unspoiled marine environments anywhere in the world.
