Makira — long known as San Cristobal — is the main island of Makira-Ulawa Province, lying about 200 kilometres southeast of [Honiara](/australiaandpacific/solomonislands/honiara). It is a rugged, densely forested island crossed by a mountain spine that rises to nearly 1,000 metres. The provincial capital, Kirakira, sits on the north coast and has the island's small airstrip, a market, and a handful of guesthouses.

Most of the interior is covered in primary rainforest that is home to several bird species found nowhere else — the Makira moorhen, the Makira flycatcher, and the shy Makira woodhen. The beaches along the south coast are long and empty, and the reefs offshore are in excellent condition. This is quiet, slow travel: a Solomon Airlines flight from Honiara, a truck or canoe into the villages, and plenty of time to wait for the weather.
