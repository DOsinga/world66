---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The region rewards travellers who are willing to get specific about what they want — because the range of experiences is enormous and the distances between them are too.

**Dive or snorkel the Great Barrier Reef.** The reef stretches over 2,300 kilometres along Queensland's coast and is home to around 1,500 species of fish and 400 species of coral. The best access points are Cairns, Port Douglas, and the Whitsundays. Visibility and coral health vary — ask operators which sites are in the best condition when you visit.

**Walk in New Zealand.** The country's nine Great Walks are some of the finest hiking routes in the world. The Milford Track, Routeburn Track, and Tongariro Alpine Crossing are the most famous and book out months in advance for peak season. Less-known routes like the Abel Tasman Coast Track or the Heaphy Track offer similar quality with fewer crowds.

**Drive the Great Ocean Road.** The 240-kilometre coastal road west of Melbourne is one of the world's great road trips. The Twelve Apostles sea stacks are the marquee attraction, but the rainforest sections around Lorne and Apollo Bay are equally good.

**Watch wildlife.** Kangaroos and wallabies are visible in the wild across most of Australia — you don't need to go to a zoo. Phillip Island's penguin parade, the Daintree Rainforest's cassowaries, and whale watching along the east coast (May to November) are all worth planning around. In New Zealand, Kaikoura offers reliable marine wildlife encounters — sperm whales, dusky dolphins, and fur seals within close range.

**Spend time in Aboriginal country.** Parts of the Northern Territory and Western Australia offer genuine cultural experiences led by Aboriginal communities. Kakadu National Park and the Kimberley region are the most accessible. Uluru, in the Red Centre, is one of the most significant places in Australia — the surrounding cultural landscape matters as much as the rock itself.

**Island-hop in the Pacific.** [Fiji](/australiaandpacific/fiji) makes island-hopping easy with a network of small ferries and domestic flights. [Tonga](/australiaandpacific/tonga) is the place to swim with humpback whales from July to October. [Vanuatu](/australiaandpacific/vanuatu) has one of the most accessible active volcanoes in the world on Tanna Island. [French Polynesia](/australiaandpacific/frenchpolynesia) offers everything from luxury overwater bungalows to remote atolls where you may be the only visitor.

**Ski in the Snowy Mountains or the South Island.** Australia's ski resorts — Thredbo and Perisher in New South Wales, Falls Creek and Mount Hotham in Victoria — are modest by international standards but serviceable. New Zealand's South Island is a different proposition: Queenstown's Remarkables and Coronet Peak are world-class, and the region's combination of skiing, scenery, and après-ski infrastructure is genuinely impressive. Season runs June to September.
