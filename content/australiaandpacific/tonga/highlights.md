## Swimming with Humpback Whales

Tonga is one of the few places on earth where you can legally enter the water with humpback whales. The whales come here from July to October to breed and raise calves. Tours run from Vava'u, Ha'apai, and Tongatapu, with licensed operators who limit group sizes and follow established protocols to minimise disturbance. It is an extraordinary experience and genuinely not available in many other places. Book early — peak season slots, especially in August and September, sell months ahead.

## Ha'amonga 'a Maui Trilithon

On the northeast tip of Tongatapu, this ancient stone gateway — three massive coral limestone slabs arranged like a doorway — dates to around 1200 AD. It's sometimes compared to Stonehenge, though more modest in scale. The site is peaceful and atmospheric, with a notch in the lintel aligned with the winter solstice sunrise. Getting here requires transport or a long bike ride, but it's worth it.

## Vava'u Island Group

Roughly 50 islands clustered around a deep central harbour that's considered one of the finest natural anchorages in the Pacific. Neiafu, the main town, sits above the harbour with good views and a small cluster of restaurants and dive shops. The outer islands offer deserted beaches, sea caves, and clear water. Kayaking, sailing, and diving are all excellent here.

## 'Eua

Often overlooked, 'Eua is Tonga's oldest island and its most rugged — forested hills, sea cliffs, and a national park with good hiking trails. Just 30 minutes by flight from Tongatapu. If you want to get away from other travellers entirely, this is the place.

## Ha'apai Group

The middle island chain is the quietest and least visited. Fewer facilities, no crowds, spectacular snorkelling and diving on near-pristine reefs. Lifuka Island and Pangai town are the base. Ha'apai is where the Bounty mutiny occurred in 1789 — the bay near Pangai is the reputed location. For those who want remoteness without giving up air access entirely, Ha'apai is worth the trip.

## Blowholes at Houma

On the south coast of Tongatapu, waves crashing into the reef force water up through natural rock formations, creating spectacular jets. The effect is most dramatic at high tide or during swells.

## Kava Ceremony

Kava — a mildly sedating drink made from the ground root of the kava plant — is the social glue of Tongan life. Ceremonies mark everything from welcoming visitors to national occasions. If you're invited to participate in one, accept. The drink has a earthy, slightly numbing taste; the ritual matters more than the flavour.
