Kolovai Beach is a long stretch of sand on the western coast of Tongatapu, Tonga's main island. The beach is good for swimming and snorkelling, and quieter than the more visited spots closer to Nuku'alofa. The area around Kolovai is also known for its colony of fruit bats — thousands of flying foxes roost in the trees near the village, considered sacred and protected by tradition.
