Nanumea is the northernmost atoll of Tuvalu, a remote ring of reef enclosing a small lagoon about 460 kilometres from the capital on Funafuti. Around 500 people live in a single village on the main islet, alongside a big coral church and the remains of an American wartime airstrip and a few rusting relics left over from the Second World War. The airstrip is long disused, so the only way to reach Nanumea is the government supply ship — a two-day crossing that runs every few weeks.

There is no tourist accommodation and no shops beyond the village cooperative; visitors are expected to stay with a host family and bring their own supplies. What you get in return is one of the quietest, least-visited places in the Pacific and a community that still lives largely from fishing, pulaka, and coconuts.
