Niulakita is the smallest and southernmost island of [Tuvalu](/australiaandpacific/tuvalu), a tiny speck of raised coral barely half a kilometre across. It was uninhabited until 1949, when families from the overcrowded atoll of [Niutao](/australiaandpacific/tuvalu/niutaoisland) were resettled here by the colonial administration. Today the island is home to a few dozen people, making it the least populated of Tuvalu's nine islands.

The island is densely covered with coconut palms and pandanus trees, and the surrounding reef drops off steeply into deep ocean. There is no lagoon and no harbour — supplies arrive by boat from Funafuti when sea conditions allow, which can mean weeks between deliveries. The community is almost entirely self-sufficient, relying on fishing, coconut, and taro.

Visitors essentially never come to Niulakita. There are no guesthouses, no shops, and no scheduled transport. Even by Tuvaluan standards, this is as remote as it gets — a place where the entire population could fit in a single room and where the nearest traffic light is well over a thousand kilometres away.
