Niutao is one of the northern islands of Tuvalu, a small closed-lagoon reef island about 250 kilometres from the capital on Funafuti. Fewer than a thousand people live here, mostly growing pulaka and coconuts and fishing the surrounding reef. The island has a handful of churches, a few unpaved roads, and the traditional open-sided meeting houses (falekaupule) that are the centre of every Tuvaluan village.

There is no regular tourism infrastructure. Getting here means catching the government supply ship from Funafuti — a slow trip of a day or more, and the boat visits only every few weeks. Anyone who makes it should expect to stay a while and live simply alongside the community.
