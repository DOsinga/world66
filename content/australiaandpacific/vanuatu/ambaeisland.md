Ambae — sometimes still called Aoba — is a large volcanic island in Penama Province in the north of Vanuatu, lying between Maewo and Pentecost. Its single enormous shield volcano, Manaro Voui, holds a cluster of crater lakes at its summit and dominates every view; when the cloud clears the peak rises almost 1,500 metres straight out of the sea. James Michener used Ambae as the model for the mythical "Bali Ha'i" in *Tales of the South Pacific*.

Life on the island is mostly subsistence farming in small villages scattered around the coast. Small-plane flights from [Port Vila](/australiaandpacific/vanuatu/portvila) and Santo serve the airstrip at Longana. Manaro's eruptions and ashfalls have forced full evacuations of the island more than once in recent decades, and travellers should check the current volcanic alert level before making plans — when it's quiet, the climb to the summit lakes is one of the great walks in the country.
