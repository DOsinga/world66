Ambrym is one of the most volcanically active islands in Vanuatu, dominated by a vast caldera containing two live volcanoes, Marum and Benbow. These are among the most accessible active lava lakes in the world, making Ambrym a genuine destination for volcano enthusiasts willing to put in the effort.

Reaching the crater rim requires a serious hike of several hours, ideally with a local guide, and the activity levels vary — at times the lava lakes are clearly visible, at others the craters are too gassy to approach safely. Check conditions before you go. Beyond the volcanic spectacle, Ambrym is also known for traditional sand drawing, a complex artistic tradition with dozens of geometric patterns, and for its powerful custom magic, which has a strong reputation across Vanuatu. Access is by light aircraft from Port Vila or by cargo boat.
