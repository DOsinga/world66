**Naghol (Land Dive), Pentecost Island — April to June**

The Naghol is Vanuatu's most famous ceremony and one of the most extraordinary rituals anywhere in the Pacific. Each Saturday during the yam harvest season, men and boys on Pentecost Island climb a wooden tower built fresh from the jungle each year and dive headfirst toward the earth with vine ropes tied around their ankles. The vines are cut to precise length by the diver's family — the goal is for his hair to just graze the ground. A successful dive ensures a good yam harvest and marks a boy's transition to manhood.

Access is via charter flight or boat from Port Vila or Santo, followed by a drive to the dive site. Spectators gather at the base of the tower. Fees apply and go directly to the village. The experience is deeply serious — this is a ceremony, not a show — and that gravity is palpable.

**Toka Festival, Tanna — irregular (typically every few years)**

The Toka is the largest traditional festival on Tanna, and one of the most significant in Vanuatu. It is a multi-day event in which tribes from across the island converge on a single village for feasting, dancing, and ceremony. Hundreds of men and women dance through the night. It takes place when the paramount chief declares conditions appropriate, which happens only every few years. When it coincides with your trip, it is extraordinary. Check with local operators in Port Vila for current information on scheduling.

**Rom Dance, Ambrym Island — throughout the dry season**

Ambrym is known across Vanuatu for its powerful kastom, and the Rom dance — performed by men in elaborate painted masks representing spirits — is its most striking expression. Performances are staged for visitors during the dry season, but the dance also appears at ceremonial occasions. The masks themselves are among the finest examples of Pacific art.

**Vanuatu Agricultural Show, Port Vila — July**

The national agricultural show brings people from across the archipelago to Port Vila for competitions, cultural performances, and the general sociability of a large public gathering. It is a good opportunity to see kastom dancing and traditional produce from islands you might not visit.

**Independence Day — 30 July**

Vanuatu's national day, marking independence from France and Britain in 1980, is celebrated with processions, kastom dancing, and events in Port Vila and across the islands.
