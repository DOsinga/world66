Tanna is an island in southern Vanuatu, and one of the most rewarding destinations in the Pacific for travelers willing to get off the standard tourist circuit. The island's defining feature is Mount Yasur, an active stratovolcano whose crater is one of the most accessible in the world — you can walk to the rim and look directly into the glowing, erupting interior. The volcano erupts frequently (multiple times per hour at peak activity), producing spectacular showers of lava bombs and a continuous deep rumble that you feel as much as hear. Watching Yasur at night, with the crater lit from below, is an experience that people don't forget.

Beyond the volcano, Tanna has extraordinary landscape diversity: black sand beaches on the coast, dense jungle inland, and the Siwi area with its remarkable hot springs. The John Frum movement originated here — one of the better-known Melanesian cargo cults — and Friday ceremonies at the village of Lamakara are open to respectful visitors. The Kastom (traditional) culture is unusually strong on Tanna compared to much of Vanuatu, with many villages maintaining traditional dress, dance, and way of life.

Getting to Tanna involves flying from Port Vila (about an hour), and accommodation ranges from basic bungalows to more comfortable lodge-style places near the volcano. The roads are rough and a 4WD is recommended for visiting Yasur, though guides and transport can be arranged locally.
