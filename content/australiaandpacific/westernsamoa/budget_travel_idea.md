Western Samoa, along with American Samoa, was discovered by Jacon Roggevven. It is a group of islands that has white sand beaches, tropical coconut plantations and forested mountains. It consists of two major islands and seven smaller islands. These islands are volcanic in nature and are surrounded by rugged forested mountains. This **Western Samoa budget travel** guide lists helpful tips for travelers to maximize their fun while minimizing costs.

**Budget Sights**

Museums are plentiful in Western Samoa, but tourists can still see the history of this island by just walking and touring around the city. There is a small museum located in Apia's courthouse. The structure, though tiny, is rich with displays that mirror the culture and history of the Western Samoa region. There is another museum in Vailima. The former house of Robert Stevenson was converted into a museum to highlight his life and works. Touring around the city, visitors will be able to appreciate the history of Western Samoa just by witnessing the monuments that are scattered along the cobbled streets of the old town region.

**Budget Recreational Activities**

Western Samoa is a group of islands that is brimming with water sports adventure resorts, where visitors can enjoy several activities. Some of the famous recreation places include Piula Cave Pool for snorkeling and scuba diving. These activities don't cost much if you book for a small group of 5 or 6 people to participate. Another resort is at Lefage Beach, which has clear, turquoise water and white fine-grain sand. There's also the Papaseea Sliding Rock, where one can experience exciting water sports activities.** **Scuba diving is one of its main attractions, because of the nice tropical underwater ecology.

Tourists can enjoy island hopping due to the fact that Western Samoa is comprised of several picturesque islands. Sightseeing around these islands is enjoyable and affordable.

**Budget Shopping**

A holiday trip to any destination is never complete without shopping for souvenir items and local merchandise. Marketi Tuai is the best place to indulge in budget shopping. Tourists can purchase anything from traditional fabrics to local Samoan handicrafts. Merchants offer lovely hand-carved merchandise at an affordable price.

The scenic beauty of Western Samoa's islands can be enjoyed as much on a modest budget as a lavish one with a few smart tips and some handy travel information.
