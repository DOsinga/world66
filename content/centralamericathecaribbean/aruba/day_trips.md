---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Antigua and Barbuda are beautiful on their own; but what do you do if you want to take a little trip just for the day? Since both are islands, the most logical place to start would be the water.

There are several ways that you can explore the water around the islands. You can sail above water or explore the depths below, go fishing, sailing, explore and relax. Couples can charter romantic cruises to watch the sunset or get married.

Eco-tours are a great way to spend the day. You can take a tour on a high-powered inflatable boat. You observe birds like brown pelicans, egrets and herons. Or you can watch the marine life below the surface. You may see sting rays, sea turtles and sunfish.

Depending on how adventurous you want to be, there are tours that take to the air. You can ride in a helicopter and see the islands from a different view. What’s the most pulse racing way to enjoy the day?

You can take a trip of a lifetime through the treetops. Yes, the treetops. Through various means like suspension bridges and zip lines this is made possible. This is not for the faint of heart. And how can you top off such an adventure? How about plummeting on a 60-foot controlled parachute decent?

Below the surface, you can discover a whole new world of marine life. The islands are surrounded by coral reefs and walls that are brimming with life. You can discover these areas by either snorkeling or diving.

If you want venture a little further, you can go to St. Johns for a shopping excursion. Or for the workaholic, you can go to St. Johns to handle work or business obligations. Bring a lot of money not only for the tour, which will cost you from $80 for an infant to $195 for an adult round-trip, but for the shopping.

Another option would be the Extreme Dominica tours. Activities here can range from plane rides to hiking to canyoning through waterfalls. Reservations have to be made to find out rates.
