---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Sosua"
type: section
---

Make sure to compare your **Sosua Must Do&rsquo;s** with the things listed below, before visiting this popular beach resort located on the north coast of the Dominican Republic. Many tourists miss some of these places due to the lack of information - you shouldn&rsquo;t make the same mistake.

**Sunbathing at the ****Sosua**** ****Public**** ****Beach****
**

You can&rsquo;t really miss this one because it&rsquo;s a definite must-do. The Sosua Public Beach is roughly a one-km-long strip and is perfect for sunbathing and other water sport activities such as surfing, snorkeling and diving. For a small fee, you can rent a lounge chair from the boys working there and if you ask them, they&rsquo;ll go and bring you drinks and refreshments from the shops located all along the back line of the beach. You can also buy some souvenirs.

**Diving the Coral Reef**

If you&rsquo;re willing to try something more extreme – diving - then diving the coral reef is a good option. You don&rsquo;t need any prior experience as there are a couple of diving schools in the area. The Sosua reef is actually a great place for beginners to learn diving, since it&rsquo;s very shallow and the waters are very clear. The rich underwater life around the reef is also a sight, one worth being seen.

**Whale Watching**

Whale Watching tours are very popular in Sosua, but are unfortunately are available only in the winter, from January to March. More than 10 000 humpback whales gather here each year, since the north coast of the Dominican Republic is their natural breeding habitat. All whale-watching tours are guided and can be booked through one of the local hotels.

**Visiting ****Columbus**** ****Water**** ****Park**

Columbus Water Park is located near Puerto Plata, about 20 km west of Sosua, which should be less than 30 minutes by car. It&rsquo;s the biggest water park in the Caribbean, has about 14 different water slides as well as swimming pools, bars and restaurants. The park is a bit busy on the weekends, but is still a lot of fun.

**Playing Golf at the Playa Grande**

A little further, about 95 km east of Sosua and next to the town of Cabrera, is the Playa Grande. It&rsquo;s a well-maintained 18-hole golf course, with some of the holes having a direct view to the ocean. The grass is in excellent condition and the staff is really professional. After finishing your game, stop by one of the delicious local restaurants.
