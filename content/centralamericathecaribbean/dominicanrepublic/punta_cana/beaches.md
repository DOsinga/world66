---

Punta Cana in the [Dominican Republic](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominicanrepublic) is such a popular travel destination mainly because of its stunning beaches. It is known that Punta Cana has some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, and these beaches provide excellent opportunities for relaxation as well as a wide range of outdoor activities.

The beaches in [Punta Cana](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominicanrepublic/punta_cana) are very well-maintained. Most of them have fine white sand, and they are lined with lots of tall palm trees that offer ample shade. The calm and clear waters around the beaches are perfect for swimming as well as other [water activities](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominicanrepublic/punta_cana/things_to_do), including boating, paragliding and sailing. There are also a number of centers on these beaches that provide instructions for scuba-diving and other water sports. Those who are vacationing in Punta Cana with their children should make sure that they visit the right beaches, because some of the beaches have very strong waves and rocks. Here are some of the most popular beaches in Punta Cana:

**Punta Cana Beach**

The Punta Cana Beach is a very long beach that covers a total distance of about 30 miles. It is the most popular beach in Punta Cana, because it has very clean and white sand and a wide range of amenities. It is lined with well-known resorts that offer excellent [accommodation](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominicanrepublic/punta_cana/accommodation) as well as [entertainment centers](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominicanrepublic/punta_cana/things_to_do). The Punta Cana Beach is arguably the most beautiful beach in the Dominican Republic, and it is the center of tourist activities in Punta Cana.

The Punta Cana Beach area is divided into 6 different sections, which are the Arena Gorda Beach, Macao Beach, El Cortecito, Uvero Alto, Cabeza de Toro and Bavaro Beach. The Bavaro Beach is definitely worth a visit, because it is a great place for swimming and snorkeling. The beach is surrounded by a [coral reef](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominicanrepublic/punta_cana/sights), which forms a turquoise-colored natural swimming pool that reveals an impressive underwater world.

**Dominicus Beach**

This place is a preferred destination for beachgoers mainly because of its cleanliness. It has earned a blue flag status, and it is a very well-maintained beach. The beach has very clear waters and a large coral reef, which makes it an ideal place for scuba-diving.

**Juanillo Beach**

The Juanillo Beach is also known as the Cap Cana, and it is located at the southeastern end of the Dominican Republic. It is a long and spacious beach that has brilliant white sand. There are lots of huts, tents and umbrellas along the beach to provide shade. This beach faces the southeast direction, and it offers spectacular views of sunrise.
