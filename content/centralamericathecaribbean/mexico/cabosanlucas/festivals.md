title: "Festivals in Cabo san lucas"
type: section
---

**Cabo San Lucas Festivals** provide an insight into the social and cultural trends that define Cabo San Lucas—the most prominent financial center among southern, Mexican states. Cabo San Lucas boasts of an unending range of festivals that are celebrated throughout the year. Some of the most popular Cabo San Lucas Festivals have been listed below:

**Todos Santos Arts Festival **

Held during the initial weeks of February, this festival aims to celebrate the arts and crafts of rural Mexico. The organized events include exhibits among the local art galleries. The celebrations seek participation from neighboring towns like Todos Santos. The evening time is reserved for drinking local wines and partying past the midnight hours.

**Festival of San Jose**

This festival is celebrated for almost the entire duration of March. Usually, the celebrations begin during the first week of March and last until March 20. The festivities include worshipping Saint Joseph, hosting carnivals/processions and singing folk songs during the evening.

**Benito Juarez's Birthday Celebrations
**

Celebrated on March 21, these are birthday celebrations of a popular, former president. The celebrations are spread across a few days. The emphasis is on street entertainment with many kinds of food-stalls and live-music competitions arranged throughout Cabo San Lucas.

**Festival of Easter**

Celebrated in the early week of April, this is Mexico's version of Easter. The festivities include colorful processions that include live music and use of bizarre costumes. Further, the eggs are stuffed with local offerings like confetti. The egg-hunting part of the Easter celebrations is like a city-based event and thousands of people search for a handful of eggs. Visitors wanting to be a part of these celebrations should reach Iglesia San Lucas Church—the starting-point for the processions.
