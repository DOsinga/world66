Centered in the state of [Chihuahua](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/chihuahua) is [Ciudad Juarez](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/ciudadjuarez), a massive cosmopolitan area and traveler’s escape. With its rare coupling of modern [sophistication](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/ciudadjuarez/nightlife_and_ente) and old-world [charm](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/ciudadjuarez/history), it becomes the ultimate destination. And, with **Ciudad Juarez Museums**, the city can now be fully explored and understood.

**Juárez Museum of Art**

This quaint tribute to [Mexican](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico) art will inspire any who walks its curved corridors. Housed in contemporary lines (the structure is shaped like a cone, marking it unique among the other urban centers), it contains 3 galleries and a rotating list of exhibitions. There is always something new to learn. Open from 11 a.m. to 7 p.m. every Tuesday through Sunday, admission is free.

**La Misión de Nuestra Señora Guadalupe**

This spectacular church and gallery is a historical treasure. Crafted in 1668, it now stands as one of the oldest chapels in the entire country, and countless visitors trek each year to marvel at the baroque architecture and 18th century paintings. Since it is still a functioning church, there is no need for reservations. Come whenever you wish.

**National Museum of Archaeology **

Located in [Parque Chamizal](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/ciudadjuarez/sights), this gathering of statues, artwork and exhibitions is certain to astound--even with its tiny size. Within a mere 2 rooms, visitors will find Aztec artifacts, Cubist paintings and figurines. The time spent here may be short but it is certainly worthwhile. And, though closed on Mondays, the museum is open Tuesday through Saturday (9 a.m. to 5 p.m.) and Sunday (11 a.m. to 5 p.m.). Reservations are recommended.

**Museo del Concorde**

Of all Ciudad Juarez museums, this may be the most unexpected. This complex seeks to tell of air travel and the origins of the supersonic plane, the Concorde. With exhibits featuring engines, control panels and more, it’s the uncommon treat. Admission is free and guided tours are available between 12 a.m. and 7 p.m. every Sunday through Wednesday.

**History Museum **

With its Victorian-style curves and stirring attractions, the History Museum stands as the city’s greatest offering. With its grand rooms and intricate designs, it chronicles the city’s rise. And, with the aid of impressionist paintings, [Spanish](/europe/spain) weaponry and [American](/northamerica/unitedstates) memorabilia, it fully succeeds. Open from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. each Tuesday through Sunday, guests will always find a chance to explore. There is no admission charge.

Ciudad Juarez museums examine a culture unlike any other. Grant yourself the privilege to see it for yourself.
