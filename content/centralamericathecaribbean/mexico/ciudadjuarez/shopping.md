---
title: "Shopping in Ciudad juarez"
type: section
---

Ciudad Juarez is one of the fastest-growing cities in Mexico, and
along with this growth has come plenty of **Ciudad Juarez shopping**.
This is an important transport city, as there are four different ports
of call to travel into Mexico from the USA. It is directly across the
river from El Paso, and is one of the largest cities in Mexico.
**Markets
**

The city market is quite famous; even if you do not buy anything, you
should walk through it. You can find curios, art work, leather goods,
blankets, turquoise jewelry, silver pieces, blankets and pottery here.
It is operated by the city’s municipal government and offers a great
selection of quality items. In front of the market are a few open-air
restaurants where you can have a drink or light meal when you need a
rest from your shopping. Another market, JJ’s market, is found in the
northeast section of the city, and is perfect for those looking for
clothing or home furnishings. This is near the Pronaf circle, and while
it is not as large as the city market, it is still excellent for
shopping.
**Recommended Stores
**

Tony’s Curio along Avenida Juarez is a great little store. It is
small, but you can find a lot of interesting objects, and the street
itself is quite historic. You can find artwork, leather saddles, purses,
boots, jackets, belts and much more. The owners and staff are quite
friendly and love to chat.

If you need to pick up a book, then stop in at Neveria Acapulco
Bookstore. This bookstore is not organized, but has piles of books to
search through. You can find beautiful older books as well as newer,
more popular paperbacks. Think of searching through the bookstore like
going on a treasure hunt.
**The Bridal District
**

Wedding dresses in Mexico are much less expensive than those found in
the US, and you can find absolutely gorgeous wedding dresses. The main
bridal district is on Avenida Lerdo, just across from the American
border. You can also find bridal stores throughout the city but not
nearly as many as in the bridal district.
**Grocery Shopping**

If you should need to pick up some food, there are two fantastic
grocery markets. These are chains called Smart and Soriana. They are
found all over, so you should be able to find one quite easily. They
contain everything you could need, at jaw-dropping prices. Besides
common products you may be used to, you can also find many products
unique to Mexico that you should try. If you should need to get a
prescription filled, then a recommended location is Hipermart which is
on Paseo Triunfo de la Republica.
