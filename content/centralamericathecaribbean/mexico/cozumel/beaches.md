---
title: "Beaches in Cozumel"
type: section
---

Cozumel’s beaches are best explored amidst their lovely natural
tropical environment at a peaceful and laidback pace that is not
punctuated with too much tourist frenzy. The Cozumel Beaches Guide offers visitors information on the beach scene and best coastal hotspots to hit in this charming Mexican Island.

**Orientation**

Cozumel's beaches are not conspicuously attractive, unlike many
other islands in the Mexican strait, however their indigenous physical
characteristics and serene settings make them highly alluring by
visitors looking for some peaceful beach side frolicking. Cozumel is a virtually safe beach haven compared to a lot of other
islands and has a lot of variety to offer in terms of the physical
characteristics of its beaches. There are the soft white sand shores
located near the extreme northern and southern tip of the island’s
western belt. The rocky shores which though not every visually
attractive are great for diving and snorkeling. There are also stunning
artificially created hybrid beaches that nestle several condos, resorts
and villas in the lap of its shores. A smart way to savor Cozumel’s
sultry spirit is to grab a beer and make yourself comfortable at a
beach club, which is a sea-side thatched roof shack that lets you
lounge around relaxingly and watch the amusing beach-side revelry.

**Chankannab Park
**

The Chankannab Park is a beautiful contrived hybrid beach that is
located a few kilometers from the downtown region and is one of the
best options for a day-out in Cozumel. It has an array of restaurants,
water sports rentals, picturesque gardens and even a quaint museum that
displays memorabilia from the Islands evocative past. There is a
fantastic blue lagoon flanking the beach and is great to keep the
children occupied. The entry fee is $ 12/per person and the best time
to go there is in the early hours of morning when there is less
activity and plenty of shaded seating spots are available.

**Playa Azul**

Playa Azul
is another great hotspot that is easily accessible from downtown
Cozumel and features a host of beach clubs with shaded palapas and
comfortable lounge chairs. This is the place where visitors are most
likely to befriend locals over a nice friendly drinking session, while
enjoying a fascinating sunset vista. Playa Azul is packed on weekends
and most of the time it is difficult to grab a seating place in the
beach clubs on a Sunday morning. Dzul Ha Beach Club is superb for
snorkeling and other water sports along with Corono, both of which can
be conveniently reached through a cab from the main town.

Cozumel, though not overtly striking, is an unforgettable odyssey of
bare charm, exciting water sports and lovely beach-side club merriment.
