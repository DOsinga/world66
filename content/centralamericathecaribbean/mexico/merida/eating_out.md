Where to go for a good meal in Merida and the surrounding Yucatan.

About 20 years ago, there was an extremely limited selection of restaurants - people actually cooked back then (or had servants that did it for them) - mostly Yucatecan, Mexican, Lebanese and some stodgy international fare.

Now, in 2006, there is so much out there waiting for you to discover. From sushi to salbutes, Merida is much more than just tacos.

**Contributors**
March 22, 2006

### [Trotter's](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/merida/eating_out/trotters)

Steaks, pastas and vino. That's their slogan and they do them well. This is Merida's newest and flashiest restaurant - the place to see and be seen. Along with Nectar, this is the place to go to celebrate in style. Try any of their steaks - they are all delicious. The only down note is the quality of their desserts - they desperately need a pastry chef or someone in the kitchen to spend some time bringing the desserts up to the level of the rest of the menu.

### [Campay Sushi](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/merida/eating_out/campay_sushi)

If you can believe it, Merida has seen an explosion in the number of sushi restaurants lately. Sushi in the land of the Maya, you ask? Yes, sushi.

The best option for fresh fish, rice dishes and all the other Japanese food you have come to know and love, is Campay. What makes the difference is the consistently attentive service, freshness of the raw fish and availability of ice-cold Kirin or Sapporo beer. ### [Nectar](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/merida/eating_out/nectar)

International seems a little staid a classification for the adventurous menu items the chefs put together at Nectar. ### [Kukis By Maru](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/merida/eating_out/kukis_by_maru)

Kukis By Maru, located in the Gran Plaza mall in the northern part of the city where the Prolongacion del Paseo de Montejo becomes the highway to Progreso, is the place to go when you need your fix of a homemade bran muffin, some freshly baked chocolate chip cookies or a slab of made-from-scratch carrot cake. Along with the usual assortment of coffees, Kukis By Maru also has a unique, crunchy biscotti, with the most almonds per bite of any biscotti I have ever tasted. There are several other baked items to choose from, always fresh, always homemade.

### [Tacos PM](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/merida/eating_out/tacos_pm)

There are literally thousands of taco places in Mérida. OK, an exaggeration. But you will see so many of them strewn along the Paseo de Montejo and it's Prolongacion (the extension of the Paseo) that you will forget you are in Yucatan and not in Mexico City. The taco you see, is from the central part of the country and not at all a Yucatecan thing, but this typically Mexican (as in from Mexico) food has caught on in the last 30 years in a huge way to the point of outnumbering the traditional panucherias at least 10 to 1.
