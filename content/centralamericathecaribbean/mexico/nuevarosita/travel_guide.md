This **Nueva Rosita Travel Guide** tells you what to do in this northeast Mexican city. Located on the River Sabinas just 62 miles/100 km from the US border and the Eagle Pass border crossing, Nueva Rosita is an old coal mining town and an industrial center.

When visiting Nueva Rosita, don&rsquo;t forget to sample the cuisine of northeast Mexico and visit the town museum to learn more about local culture and history.

Nueva Rosita is located in the Coahuila area and this region is known for natural areas, prefect for outdoor activities.

**Things to See and Do in Town**

The Nueva Rosita museum has exhibitions and information about local history and culture in this coal mining area. Coal mining is what made Nueva Rosita what it is today, and you can learn a lot more in the museum.

Historical monuments in Nueva Rosita include the Pante&oacute;n Municipal, the Iglesia San Juan, the Ignacio Zaragoza school and the former Presidencia Municipal. The El templo de San Juan or the La Mansi&oacute;n plant are good examples of regional architecture.

The Los Sabinitos Park by the Sabinas River is a good place to go for a relaxed walk or a picnic. There are also two other parks in the area: Los Gritos or San Antonio, both on the shores of the Sabinas River.

**Eating out in Northeast Mexico**

When visiting Nueva Rosita, you should sample the northeast Mexican cuisine. This area has many specialties although for vegetarians, some of them can be quite disturbing.

"Cabrito*" *is a local delicacy and is a whole roasted goat. Any parts of the goat can be ordered from legs to the shoulder. Alternatively, try a local steak or "carne machacado," salted, dried and pounded beef that comes with tomatoes, chillis and eggs.

"Gorditas" are corn cakes that can be stuffed with meat filling. Vegetarians can enjoy gorditas too and ask for a beans or cheese stuffing.

**Things to See Around Nueva Rosita**

When driving on from Nueva Rosita, make sure you visit the Area de Preteccion de Flora y Fauna Cuatrocienegas. This is a beautiful nature reserve in the middle of the desert, and a protected area that was created to protect the fragile local ecosystem and the diverse natural life.

Although most of it is a protected area, there are rivers and pools, formed by underground springs, that are used for swimming and for snorkeling. The desert scenery is unique and there is a wide variety of animal and plant life here.
