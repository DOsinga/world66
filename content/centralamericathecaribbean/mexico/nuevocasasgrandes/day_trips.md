title: "Day Trips in Nuevo Casas Grandes"
type: section
---

There are a couple of **Nuevo Casas Grandes day trips **that any willing traveler can embark on, in order to further explore the area around the city. With a car, there is quite a lot to do and discover, particularly hidden parts of Mexico that the country is not known for.

**Colonia Dubl&aacute;n**

The Colonia Dubl&aacute;n is technically a part of the municipality of Nuevo Casas Grandes, but it operates in such a manner that it seems like it is entirely self-sufficient. The town is a Mormon colony, and is one of the few surviving Mormon colonies in Mexico. This is an interesting place to visit for tourists who wish to see a different side of Mexico. The Mormon settlers here speak an entirely different language, with Spanish as their second language. Many of these settlers are surprisingly not descendants of the original settlers, but Mexican converts, so conversing in Spanish is not a problem – just do not expect any communication in English!

The colony is grows their own fruit and vegetables, and have their own farms, so the produce in the place is wonderfully fresh. Chilies, peaches and wheat are some of the many things that are grown here, so be sure to walk through the fields to see what is being grown during that season. It is a good chance to speak with the people living in the isolated community, and to understand the calmness of their mindset and contentment with their lives.

**Sierra Madre Occidental******

The Sierra Madre Occidental is the main mountain range in the region surrounding Nueva Casas Grandes, and stretches about 1500 kilometers from Arizona into Mexico. The range surrounding the city of Nuevo Casas Grandes is located only about a 20 minutes drive away from the city, and leads any tourist into the natural setting that Mexico is not usually known for. With a hilly terrain, hiking can be done, but the highlight of the place is hardly the hiking and trekking around. Instead, take the time to smell the flowers and listen to the call of the birds – literally. The place is known among academics and scientists for its rich diversity of species, particularly of the plants species. The range is known for its pine-oak forests. Those willing to venture a little further will also find that indigenous tribes call this place home – there are about five different groups living within the range at different parts of it.
