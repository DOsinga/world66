title: "Getting Around in Nuevo Casas Grandes"
type: section
---

The city of Nuevo Casas Grandes in Mexico was one of the first Mexican cities to have a train station, and **Nuevo Casas Grandes Transportation **is varied from bus to airports.

**Bus****
**

Buses still remain the most common and reliable form of transport in and out of the city. Perhaps a little dusty and bumpy sometimes, buses are a cheap way to enter Nuevo Casas Grandes. The Nuevo Casas Grandes Bus station has several bus lines using the station as a hub. They are owned by local or international companies. The fare from Phoenix, a popular pick up point, to Nuevo Casas Grande can be as cheap at $16. The majority of buses are kept in good shape. For students looking to stretch their budgets, grabbing a bus from any destination to Nuevo Casas Grande is a wise choice.

**Train
**

The train station is what created the town of Nueva Casas Grande. No trip to the city would be complete without a visit. The railroad station was constructed here in the 1870&rsquo;s and the town subsequently was built around it. Today, the railroad is an excellent way to enter the city and the surrounding locale. For people traveling short distances, this train is a good way to get around, especially for those traveling to the archeological digs and the city of Mata. The new station is located by the bus station, while the older station is between Nueva Casas Grandes and Mata.

**Airport**

The Paloma Grande Nueva Casas Grandes Airport is a small airport located outside the city. The airport is a small private airport that has few flight domestic flights. For tourists looking to travel by air into the area, they will have to make a connecting from another major city like the Tijuana Airport or Mexico City. The airport mostly deals with charter flights. For business travelers, this airport is ideal for traveling to Nueva Casas Grandas for a brief stop, as parts of the Mexican roadway are still not quite developed. For people on a time crunch, this airport is a good choice instead of a bus or train that might make several stops depending on the origin location. There is security onsite for the train, as the guards live on site. The fee is $20 per night, but the owner of the plane will have peace of mind.
