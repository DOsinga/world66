title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Nuevo Casas Grandes"

---

Nuevo Casas Grandes is found in the state of Chihuahua, in northern Mexico. This town is historically important to Mexico, and is a popular tourist spot. Here are some **Nuevo Cases Grandes Must Do&rsquo;s**.

**See the Casas Grandes**

The single most important archaeological site in Mexico is definitely worth a visit for any tourist. See some of the magnificent stone structures as they rise about the ground, and marvel at their sheer size, being able to fit nearly 2500 people in the 14th century when they were being inhabited. The layout of the structure is, in itself, sheer genius, considering the modern use of kitchen and dining areas, as well as a sewage system.

**Head to Mata Ortiz**

The village-town of Mata Ortiz is most famous for its pottery, with many of the items being on display in some of the world-renowned art galleries. Many of the artists here are internationally acclaimed and well known for marrying the traditional artwork of the Mexicans with a modern interpretation of what the art means. There are also replicas of the pottery found in the ruins of the Casas Grandes, located very near the village.

**Visit the Mormon Community**

The area is home to a large Mormon community, most of which is found in the north and bordering the colony of Colonia Dubl&aacute;n. Many of these communities are self-sufficient, and grow their own produce to support themselves. Walk through the orchards of endless fruit trees – peach, apples, pears and pecans are common crops. These communities also often have livestock, so take the afternoon to drive down to one of these farms and experience the Mormon way of life.

**Taste the Cuisine**

Nuevo Casas Grandes is found in the state of Chihuahua, and is home to the cuisine known as Norteno. Many will find the cuisine here very acceptable, appealing and even familiar, since this is where the Tex-Mex in the U.S. draws most of its inspiration from. Chilies, cheese and beef are the most often used ingredients in the cooking, together with plenty of oil and fried food to create a blend of tastes that could only be described as explosive.

**Meet Some Locals**

The local people are incredibly friendly in this little town, as are many of the people in Mexico to anyone traveling through the country. Many of the locals are willing to help a lost tourist, and even those who require something in return often just ask for an ice-cold beer to cool off in the torrid Mexican heat.
