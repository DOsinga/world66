**Best of Queretaro Festivals—Santiago de Queretaro **

Visitors in Mexico are recommended to participate in Queretaro festivals since Queretaro is widely regarded as host of the most vibrant, cultural celebrations.

#### **Festival of Santiago de Queretaro **

This is the biggest festival of Queretaro and is counted among Mexico&rsquo;s list of prestigious, international festivals. The scale of the festivities and overall arrangements for ensuring a visitor&rsquo;s comfort are topnotch since the festival is sponsored by the state government. Santiago de Queretaro is essentially an arts festival.

#### **Festival Schedule **

The festival is held in April, usually during the second week of the month. The celebrations are scheduled across 7 or 8 days. The festivities begin with a colorful, inaugural parade that passes through the historic streets of Queretaro. The starting point for the parade is usually Corregidora Street. Visitors can catch a glimpse of the parade from the Angela Peralta or any of the smaller Guerrero streets if the main streets seem crowded.

#### **Festival Venues **

There are no set venues for the festivities. The entire city forms the background with its plazas and gardens playing host to various artists and exhibitions. Even the public libraries and auditoriums are decorated and used as stages for various exhibits. Visitors who don&rsquo;t want to feel mobbed by the rush should try to reach the more popular venues around 12:00 p.m. The most sought-after venues include the City Museum, Zenea Garden, Guerrero Garden and the Rosalio Theatre.

#### **Visitor Participation **

There are no charges, and visitors are allowed entry into each exhibit without any questions. There are no age-related barriers, either.
