---
title: "Sights in Queretaro"
type: section
---

There are many **Queretaro sights** to see; the city has an
extensive history and is very important to the many industries in
Mexico. The name most likely translates into "the place of the great
people,” and the Aztecs were the first to live in this area. There are
many archaeological sites that have uncovered the city’s history over
the years.
**The Plaza de Armas

**

This is the main gathering place of the city, and there are many of
the cafes and restaurants here. The buildings, including the government
palace, are all excellent examples of colonial architecture. The plaza
was originally an Indian market in which items were bought, sold and
traded until the 17th century. This did not turn into a public plaza
until 1886. In the middle of the plaza is a statue of the Marquis of
Villa del Villar de Aguila. This famous man constructed the town’s
aqueducts.
**The Start of Independence

**

This palace was completed in 1770, and is an excellent example of
neo-Hispanic architecture. The palace is thought to be the cradle of the
Independence Movement in Mexico, as there were many "literary meetings"
in the palace (these actually discussed liberation issues, not
literature). It was the disruption of an infamous meeting in which the
Cry of Dolors was unleashed, marking the start of the independence
struggle in 1810.
**A Historic Theatre

**

The Theatre of the Republic is a beautiful building to behold, as it
has many columns and balconies that are very reminiscent of the
neoclassical style. There have been many performances on this stage, and
it is also an important part of Mexico’s history. In 1867 the War
council met on the stage and ruled for the execution of Maximilian of
Habsburg. The Mexican Constitution was written here and, since this
historic date, the building is considered a National Monument.
**Hill of the Bells

**

This is a beautiful park that contains several playgrounds and green
areas. It is here that Napoleon's Envoy and Maximilian of Habsburg
fought with the Republic forces and the Republic won. On the top of the
hill is a statue dedicated to Benito Huarez y Gargia, one of the leaders
of this historic battle. The hill received its name due to the stones
there that sound just like a bell ringing when they hit one another.
**The Cathedral

**

The Cathedral is an interesting mix of neoclassical and baroque
styles, and was finished in 1805. It is built of quarry stone and light
volcanic rock. This Cathedral is near the Plaza de Armas, and was not
promoted to a Cathedral until 1931 by the Pope.
