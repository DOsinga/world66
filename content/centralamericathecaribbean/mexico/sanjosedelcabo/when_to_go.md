---
title: "When to Go in San Jose del Cabo"
type: section
---

Bumper stickers in San Jose del Cabo spout slogans like “No Bad Days,” and while that is virtually true, like any other destination, there are some times of the year that are more enjoyable than others.

**Weather in San Jose del Cabo**

Though most of the year is sunny, with an average of 360 days of sun per year, visitors should avoid hurricane season and especially hot or cool times. The best weather in San Jose del Cabo occurs from mid-October to November and then again from April to mid-May.

After mid-May, south winds begin and bring with them a blanket of hot, humid and stifling weather that stays on the Baja California Peninsula through September. At that time, hurricane season is in full swing and trips to the area should be limited. Though hurricane season officially runs from June through October, September is the peak and generally when storms do hit the area they are only Category 1 or 2 storms.

As mid-November sets in, the milder temperatures come back as a result of the cooler northern winds--and the level of visitors steadily increases. Though temperatures are moderate, ranging in the 70s and 80s during this time, the sun is very intense and visitors should be vigilant about applying sunscreen so as not to ruin their vacation with a painful sunburn after being out on a cool day.

**Seasonal Variations in San Jose del Cabo**

If you plan on fishing while in San Jose del Cabo, book your trip for August through November, as this is the prime season for a variety of game fish and the best time for the sport.

San Jose del Cabo and neighboring city Cabo San Lucas are both prime destinations for students on Spring Break and can be extremely crowded and lively during this season. If you are traveling with young children or want to avoid these crowds do not visit San Jose del Cabo during March or April. If you do visit during this time, be sure to book your accommodations well in advance to ensure availability.

Summers are generally avoided in the Baja peninsula due to their intense heat; however, if you do not mind the temperatures, there are many deals during this time on lodging and entertainment. Consider a trip during this time if you are on a tight budget.
