**Visit a Few San Miguel de Allende Museums**

For those who want cultural and historical insight to the lives of old and modern residents in this part of town, check out the different **San Miguel de Allende museums**. Considering the town&rsquo;s history as a former ghost town, the museum is a great way to catch the stories of the past and present of the town.

#### **Angela Peralta Theatre**

The Teatro Angela Peralta is a beacon of cultural abundance. It is a preserved theater with beautiful wood detail, chronicling the art and cultural scene of San Miguel through pictures and different memorabilia. There is a balcony which has a beautiful view of the city and its spacious square out front. The Nidart Gallery with beautiful art pieces from local artists is just across from the theater. It is a great way to cool off from the outdoors. The entrance fee is an affordable 10 pesos in summer 2010.

#### **Ignacio Allende Residence**

Also known as the Historic Museum of San Miguel, the residence is a well-preserved shrine to one of the heroes of Mexican independence, Captain Ignacio Allende. He is the namesake for the town. The Museum contains different artifacts of the war against Spain as well as different Pre-Columbian collections. Standing in this colonial house is also an impressive life-sized sculpture of the said hero. The entrance is quite steep at $34, so be prepared to shell out a bit for this cultural experience.
