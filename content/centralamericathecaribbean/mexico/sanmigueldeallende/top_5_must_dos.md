---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in San Miguel de Allende"
type: section
---

**San
Miguel de Allende’s must-do’s** keep visitors busy with things ranging
from colonial architecture, to natural springs and local crafts. In
2008, San Miguel de Allende was given a place on UNESCO’s World Heritage
Site list, making it a favorite destination in Mexico. Here are some
things you don’t want to miss.
**1.
El Jardín: La Parroquia
**
El
Jardín is the name given to the city’s central square. Like many
colonial cities, the mayor’s office, a parish and various restaurants
surround it. Musicians often play at the kiosk that is located in its
center. The cathedral, Parroquia San Miguel, is one of the city’s most
famous landmarks. It was originally built in the 1600s but underwent a
drastic change in the 1800’. Inspired by the Gothic architecture in
Europe, a curious mixture of influences came to yield its Neo-Gothic
style. Its pink façade has become an iconic part of this city.
**
2.
Historic Downtown****
**
San
Miguel de Allende is a city of artists and artisans. The streets that
surround the main square are filled with galleries and workshops. After
visiting La Parroquia, head to the Mercado de Artesanías. At this
marketplace you’ll find hand-blown glass and regional ceramics called
talavera. Nearby, the public library offers a home and garden tour of
the vicinity. These homes exemplify the city’s architecture. Call (415)
1520-293 for information.
**
3. Centro Cultural Ignacio Ramírez:
El Nigromante**
This
building, once a convent, is referred to by locals as Bellas Artes. The
cultural center is comprised of a museum, an auditorium and galleries
and it has a charming café. It houses work done by the famous Mexican
muralist, David Alfaro Siqueiros. Nowadays, it holds performances and
art shows. Dance, painting and other workshops are given here.

**4.
El Chorro**
As the
story goes, Juan de San Miguel, a friar, discovered a natural spring in
this area. This gave origin to the city of San Miguel de Allende. Some
of the city's oldest and most sinuous streets lead to this spring. El
Chorro is made up of pleasant paths and gardens. At its highest point
there is a rustic chapel. Artists often congregate here to paint and
work.

**5. Festivals**
San
Miguel de Allende is known for its numerous festivals that are held
year-round. To truly experience Mexican culture, try timing your trip to
attend one of them. Most of them have a religious origin, but all of
them are colorful and folkloric. One festival that is very cosmopolitan
and draws over 80,000 visitors during July and August, is the film
festival, “Expression de Corto.” The “Fiesta de los Locos,” is a lively
carnival that also takes place in June. Check with organizations like
the San Miguel Guide for a calendar of events.
