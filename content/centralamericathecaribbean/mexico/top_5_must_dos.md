---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Mexico"
type: section
---

**Mexico must do's** are plentiful and all are definitely worth a visit, making it difficult to narrow them down to a particular top 5. However, if one discounts the beaches, of which there are many, here are 5 must see destinations-and one "must do" event-in Mexico.

**[Mexico City](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/mexicocity)**

This extensive, Colonial-style city is filled with Baroque architecture, lush, green areas, bustling centers and quiet streets. There are literally hundreds of galleries and museums that you can explore, and it's worth timing your day to see the lowering of the flag at the Zocalo. There are also incredible cathedrals situated right next to ancient temples. Just be careful, as with all cities there is the risk of theft and other petty crimes.

**[Palenque](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/palenque)**

This ancient Mayan city is thought to be the most beautiful in all of Mexico, thanks in large part to its location buried within dense jungles. For over 1,000 years the city remained hidden, thus saving it from looters and resulting in its unspoiled frescoes. You can climb the pyramids or descend into underground temples. All the while you explore the grounds, you'll be accompanied by friendly monkeys.

**[Chichen Itza](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/chichenitza)**

Of course you can't miss a trip to Chichen Itza, on everybody's "Mexico Must Do" list thanks to its inception onto the list of New World Wonders. See the pyramid of Kukulcan, most popular during the equinoxes, when shadows cast illusions of serpents writhing down the stairs. You can also visit the Temple of Warriors and the observatory. The Caves of Balankanche are incredible, as is the Sacred Cenote, where human sacrifices once occurred.

**[Teotihuacan](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico/teotihuacan)**

This original capital city of Mexico is known as the "City where the Gods are born." The Pyramids of the Sun and Moon are incredible sights to behold, as is the citadel. In this city built at the crux of transition between what we now call B.C. and A.D. is the Temple of Quetzalcoatl and the Palace of Quetzalpapalote, not to mention the eerily named Way of the Dead.

**Bull Fighting**

It may be rather savage-or at the very least vicious-but a bull fight in Mexico is definitely a one-time must do event. Watch the matador taunt the bull on foot before the picadors ride in on horse
