title: "Getting Around in Darien gap"
type: section
---

The Darien Gap is a large section of jungle between Panama and Columbia, and **Darien Gap transportation** is useful if you are going to try to cross the jungle. The Darien Gap is a dangerous area, and travelers should use caution and careful planning when considering crossing this area.

**Airplane**

Flying across the Darien Gap is probably the easiest method of transportation. There are several flights that will take you across the Darien Gap. The flight between Panama and Columbia is about one hour and twenty minutes. Prices are normally reasonable, but it does pay to shop around. Flying across the Darien Gap is a safe way to travel, but keep in mind you will have to go through customs.

**Boat**

Crossing the Darien Gap by boat is another safe and reliable option. There are several companies that offer ferry services in order to cross the Darien Gap. If you want to travel by boat, you should research the ferry companies in advance. It is best to book your trip at least two weeks in advance. It seems like the companies are always switching owners and the rates are always changing. You also have the option of renting a container if you need to ship large cargo such as motorcycles, cars or furniture. If you decide to cross the Darien Gap via boat, you will normally dock the boat in Colon.

**By Foot**

Crossing the Darien Gap by foot is dangerous and is not recommended for anyone! The Darien Gap is a hot spot for drug smugglers and is not considered a safe place to be. If someone was to cross the Darien Gap by foot, they would need a machete, mosquito repellent, food, lots of water, dry weather and malaria prophylactic.

**Vehicle**

There are not any roads in the Darien Gap, so crossing by vehicle would be hard to accomplish. Attempting to cross the Darien Gap by vehicle is just as dangerous as crossing by foot. You should not try to cross by vehicle, and do not believe anyone that tells you they can cross you.

**Customs Information**

If you cross the Darien Gap and land in Columbia, you will need to hire a customs agent. A customs agent will do all the leg work and required paperwork for you. If you cross the Darien Gap and land in Panama, the customs process is fairly simple and there is no need to hire a customs agent.
