One of the most beautiful islands of the Caribbean, St. Lucia has two easily recognizable features: the twin volcanic peaks of Petit Piton and Gros Piton are visible from far out to sea. Most of the islands is covered with thick greenery - from wild forests to banana plantations.

 [Castries](/centralamericathecaribbean/thecaribbean/saintlucia/castries) is the main travel hub but does not have a lot of sights. The 100 year old marketplace on Jeremy street is well worth a visit though.

 [Marigot Bay](/centralamericathecaribbean/thecaribbean/saintlucia/marigotbay) is 12 km south of castries and has a great natural harbour, boat rentals, a beach and good baord sailing are on offer there.

 Saint Lucia National Rain Forest is a must see for nature lovers. Over 19,000 acres of towereing hardwoods filled with bird life. Soufriere and Sulphur Springs are other ineresting sights on the island.

 Just opff the caost are the Maria islands and the Fregate islands. Both are nature reserves, so contact the National Trust for permissions and a guide to visit the islands.
