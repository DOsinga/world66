---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The ancient Maya built some of the most impressive cities in the pre-Columbian Americas, and their ruins are among the top reasons to visit this region. Tikal in [Guatemala](/centralamericathecaribbean/guatemala) rises above the jungle canopy — arrive at dawn and you'll hear howler monkeys before you see the temples. Chichen Itza in [Mexico](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico) is iconic but crowded; for a more atmospheric experience, try Palenque in Chiapas or Calakmul near the Guatemalan border. Copan in [Honduras](/centralamericathecaribbean/honduras) is smaller but famous for its intricate stone carvings and stelae. You could spend weeks visiting Maya sites across the Yucatan, Guatemala, Belize, and Honduras without running out.

The diving and snorkeling here rank among the best in the world. The Belize Barrier Reef, the second largest on earth, offers wall dives, the famous Blue Hole, and pristine coral at Turneffe Atoll. The Bay Islands of [Honduras](/centralamericathecaribbean/honduras) — Roatan and Utila in particular — are where budget-conscious divers come to get certified, with some of the cheapest PADI courses anywhere. Cozumel off [Mexico](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico)'s Yucatan coast has drift diving through crystal-clear water. In the Caribbean, Bonaire is legendary for shore diving, and the marine parks of the Cayman Islands and [Dominica](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominica) are exceptional.

Central America is one of the most volcanically active regions on earth, and hiking its volcanoes is unforgettable. Climb Acatenango in [Guatemala](/centralamericathecaribbean/guatemala) for a night camp overlooking the actively erupting Fuego — one of the most spectacular sights in all of Central America. Concepcion on Ometepe Island in Nicaragua is a grueling full-day hike with massive views. Arenal in [Costa Rica](/centralamericathecaribbean/costarica) is no longer erupting but its national park has excellent trails and hot springs. In [El Salvador](/centralamericathecaribbean/elsalvador), you can hike the rim of Santa Ana volcano and peer into its turquoise crater lake.

Beach life in the Caribbean needs no introduction, but the variety is worth noting. You'll find everything from the resort-lined strips of Cancun and Montego Bay to the quiet, undeveloped stretches of [Dominica](/centralamericathecaribbean/dominica)'s black sand coastline and the Corn Islands off [Nicaragua](/centralamericathecaribbean/nicaragua). The San Blas Islands of [Panama](/centralamericathecaribbean/panama), managed by the indigenous Guna people, offer a Robinson Crusoe experience with palm-fringed islands and no resort development. On the Pacific side, the beaches of [Costa Rica](/centralamericathecaribbean/costarica)'s Nicoya Peninsula and Mexico's Oaxacan coast are less flashy but wonderful.

Surfing draws a dedicated crowd to the Pacific coast. [Costa Rica](/centralamericathecaribbean/costarica) is the best-known surf destination, with breaks for every level from beginner-friendly Tamarindo to the powerful barrels at Playa Hermosa. [El Salvador](/centralamericathecaribbean/elsalvador) has emerged as a serious surf destination, with El Tunco and Punta Roca offering consistent right point breaks. Mexico's Pacific coast, from Puerto Escondido's heavy beach break to Sayulita's mellow waves, covers the full spectrum. [Panama](/centralamericathecaribbean/panama)'s Santa Catalina is more remote but rewards the journey with uncrowded waves.

Cultural experiences are everywhere if you look beyond the beach. [Cuba](/centralamericathecaribbean/cuba)'s live music scene — son, salsa, rumba — is reason enough to visit Havana, where you'll stumble into performances in bars, parks, and living rooms. Mexican food culture runs deep and varies region by region; eating your way through Oaxaca's markets and mezcalerias is a trip in itself. Catch the Day of the Dead celebrations in early November in [Mexico](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico) for one of the world's most moving cultural festivals. In [Guatemala](/centralamericathecaribbean/guatemala), the indigenous markets at Chichicastenango and the textile traditions of highland Maya communities offer a window into living pre-Columbian culture. And across the Caribbean, Carnival celebrations from [Trinidad](/centralamericathecaribbean/trinidadandtobago) to [Jamaica](/centralamericathecaribbean/jamaica) are explosive expressions of music, costume, and joy.
