---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The dry season, roughly November through April, is the most popular time to visit Central America and the Caribbean. Skies are clear, humidity drops to tolerable levels, and roads that turn to mud in the wet months stay passable. This is high season, which means higher prices and more crowds at popular spots — book ahead for Christmas, New Year, and Easter, when the entire region fills up with both international visitors and local vacationers.

Hurricane season runs from June through November, with the greatest risk between August and October. The Caribbean islands, the Yucatan coast of [Mexico](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico), [Belize](/centralamericathecaribbean/belize), and the Caribbean coast of Central America are all vulnerable. A direct hit from a major hurricane is genuinely dangerous and can strand you for days. That said, most hurricane seasons bring only a few significant storms, and modern forecasting gives several days of warning. If you travel during this period, keep an eye on weather reports and have flexible plans. Travel insurance that covers weather disruptions is essential.

The rainy season — called "green season" by optimistic tourism boards — runs from May through November on the mainland, with some regional variation. Mornings are typically sunny, with heavy afternoon downpours that clear quickly. The landscape is lush and gorgeous. Prices drop significantly, sometimes by 30 to 50 percent, and tourist sites are wonderfully uncrowded. If you don't mind getting rained on for an hour each day, this can be the best time to travel. [Costa Rica](/centralamericathecaribbean/costarica) in particular markets its green season aggressively with good reason.

Regional variations matter. The Pacific coast of Central America has a more predictable dry/wet split than the Caribbean side, which can get rain year-round. [Mexico](/centralamericathecaribbean/mexico) is enormous and varied — the Baja Peninsula and northern deserts are dry most of the year, while the Gulf coast and Chiapas get drenched in summer. The southern Caribbean islands like [Aruba](/centralamericathecaribbean/aruba), [Bonaire](/centralamericathecaribbean/bonaire), and [Curacao](/centralamericathecaribbean/curacao) sit below the hurricane belt and are drier than their northern neighbors, making them more reliable year-round destinations.

For specific activities, timing matters too. Whale sharks appear off [Belize](/centralamericathecaribbean/belize) and Mexico's Isla Holbox from May to September. Sea turtle nesting season peaks from July to October on Caribbean beaches. Surfing on the Pacific coast is best from April to October when southern swells arrive. And if you want to experience Carnival — one of the great spectacles of the Caribbean — plan for February or March in [Trinidad and Tobago](/centralamericathecaribbean/trinidadandtobago), or hit the Junkanoo celebrations in the [Bahamas](/centralamericathecaribbean/bahamas) around Christmas and New Year.
