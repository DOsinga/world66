The Monastery of Ardenica stands on a pine-covered hill above the plain south of [Berat](/europe/albania/berat), commanding views in every direction. The monastery's main church, dedicated to St Mary, dates to the 14th century, with extensive interior frescoes added in the 18th century by the Zografi brothers, two of the most accomplished Albanian-Greek painters of the period.

The monastery has a notable place in Albanian legend: Skanderbeg is said to have married Donika Arianiti here in 1451. During the communist period it served as a museum rather than a place of worship and, as a result, much of the interior decoration survived intact.

The site can be combined with a visit to [Apolonia](/europe/albania/apolonia) nearby and makes a reasonable day trip from [Berat](/europe/albania/berat) or even [Tirana](/europe/albania/tirana).
