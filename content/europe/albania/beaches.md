---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Albania's coastline divides into two very different stretches.

The **Adriatic coast** north of Vlorë — around Durrës, Kavajë, and Shëngjin — consists mainly of long sandy beaches that get crowded in summer and heavily developed in places. Durrës has the longest and most accessible beach near a major city, popular with Albanian families and day-trippers from Tirana.

The **Ionian coast** (the Albanian Riviera) south of Vlorë is the real draw. The road winds along dramatic cliffs dropping to clear turquoise water, with small coves and pebble beaches tucked below. The best beaches here are around **Dhërmi**, **Himara**, **Borsh**, and **Ksamil**. Ksamil, near [Saranda](/europe/albania/saranda), has crystal-clear shallow water and small islands you can wade to — it is the most photographed beach in the country and gets genuinely crowded in July and August. Come in June or September for the same scenery with far fewer people.

**Llogara Pass** separates the two coasts and offers spectacular views over both. Driving the coastal road south from the pass is one of the best drives in the Balkans.

The **Blue Eye** (*Syri i Kaltër*), about 25 km east of Saranda, is not a beach but deserves mention here: a natural spring where impossibly clear water wells up from a deep aquamarine pool. It is one of the strangest and most beautiful natural features in the country.
