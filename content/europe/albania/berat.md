Berat is one of the most beautiful towns in Albania and, with [Gjirokastër](/europe/albania/gjirokaster), is a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its remarkably well-preserved Ottoman architecture. The nickname "town of a thousand windows" refers to the distinctive Ottoman-era houses stacked up the hillside, each with rows of large windows looking out over the Osum river valley.

The town divides into three parts: Mangalem (the Muslim lower quarter), Gorica (the Christian quarter across the river), and Kala (the hilltop citadel). The castle walls enclose an inhabited village — people have lived here continuously since antiquity — with Byzantine churches, old houses, and a small museum. The Onufri Museum, in the Church of the Dormition of St Mary within the castle, houses an outstanding collection of 16th-century Albanian iconography. Onufri was one of the great Byzantine painters of his era.

The walk up through the old cobbled streets to the castle is the main experience. Allow a full day.

Berat is a useful base for the surrounding region. The Monastery of [Ardenica](/europe/albania/ardenica) is around 30 km away, and the Greek-era ruins of [Apolonia](/europe/albania/apolonia) can be visited as a day trip.
