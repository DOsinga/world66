Delvina is a small town in southern Albania, set on a hillside between [Saranda](/europe/albania/saranda) and [Gjirokastër](/europe/albania/gjirokaster). It is not a destination in its own right but a convenient stop on the road between the two major southern cities.

The ruins of the ancient city of Phoenice (Phoinike), an important Illyrian and later Roman city, are nearby — one of the largest ancient sites in the region and still only partially excavated. A Greek minority community has traditionally lived in this area (the region was known as "Northern Epirus" in Greek nationalist terminology).

The old castle above the town is worth a brief look for the views.
