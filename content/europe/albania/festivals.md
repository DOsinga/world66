**Gjirokastra National Folk Festival**

Held every five years at the fortress of [Gjirokastër](/europe/albania/gjirokaster), this is Albania's most important cultural event. About 1,000 folk artists from Albania, Kosovo, and North Macedonia perform traditional music, dance, and costume. The iso-polyphonic singing tradition — a UNESCO intangible heritage — is a highlight. The festival dates back to 1968. Check the Gjirokastra municipality website for the next scheduled edition.

**Kala Festival**

An electronic and underground music festival held each June on the beach at Dhërmi on the Albanian Riviera. Intimate by design, with six outdoor stages and a line-up that draws serious international acts. One of the more distinctive music festival experiences in the Balkans.

**Tirana Jazz Festival**

Held annually in Tirana, usually in October or November, bringing together Albanian and international jazz musicians. A good reason to be in the capital in autumn.

**Dita e Verës (Summer Day)**

Celebrated on 14 March, this pagan-rooted spring festival is particularly vivid in Elbasan, where the streets fill with food stalls, music, and people gathering outdoors after winter. It predates both Christianity and Islam in Albania.
