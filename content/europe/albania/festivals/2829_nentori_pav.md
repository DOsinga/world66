November 28 is Albanian Independence Day, commemorating the declaration of independence from the Ottoman Empire in 1912. The central figure was Ismail Qemali (1844-1919), a distinguished leader of the Albanian national movement who became the country's first head of state.

Born in Vlora to a noble family, Qemali served in the Ottoman administration while quietly working toward Albanian self-determination. On November 28, 1912, he raised the Albanian flag on a balcony in Vlora alongside Isa Boletini and Luigj Gurakuqi, ending nearly 500 years of Ottoman rule. He served as prime minister until 1914.

The day is marked with ceremonies, parades, and cultural events across Albania, with the largest celebrations in Vlora where the original declaration took place. The Flag Day celebrations on the 28th often extend into the 29th.
