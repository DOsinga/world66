**Buses and furgons**

The backbone of Albanian transport is the furgon — a shared minibus that plies a fixed route but has no fixed schedule. Flag one down anywhere along its route and pay cash when you arrive. For long-distance travel, regular buses connect the major cities: Tirana to Saranda takes about 5 hours (500–800 lek), Tirana to Shkodër about 2 hours. Schedules are loose; the app/site Gjirafa is the most reliable source for departure times. Bus stations in Tirana are spread around the city by destination rather than consolidated in one terminal.

**Taxis and ride-hailing**

Metered taxis operate in Tirana and other cities. Agree on a price before getting in, or use the Bolt app, which works in Tirana. Uber does not operate in Albania.

**Driving**

If you want to explore the country at your own pace — especially the Albanian Riviera, the Accursed Mountains, or the interior — a rental car is the best option. Roads have improved enormously since the 1990s but mountain roads can be narrow and unmarked. Albania has zero tolerance for drink-driving. International driving licences are accepted.

**Getting around cities**

[Tirana](/europe/albania/tirana) is compact and walkable. Other cities like [Berat](/europe/albania/berat), [Gjirokastër](/europe/albania/gjirokaster), and [Shkodër](/europe/albania/shkoder) are also best explored on foot once you're there.
