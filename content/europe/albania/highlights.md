title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Albania is no longer the secret it once was, but it still feels like a discovery. Here are the places that justify the journey.

**Gjirokastër and Berat** — the two UNESCO towns are the most rewarding cities in Albania after Tirana. Both have hilltop fortresses, well-preserved Ottoman architecture, and enough nearby countryside to spend several days. [Gjirokastër](/europe/albania/gjirokaster) has the bigger castle and the more dramatic setting; [Berat](/europe/albania/berat) is slightly gentler and arguably more beautiful.

**Butrint** — the UNESCO ruins near [Saranda](/europe/albania/saranda) are among the best-preserved in the Balkans: a Greek theatre, Roman forum, Byzantine basilica, and Venetian tower all within walking distance of each other, in a lakeside national park that feels genuinely untouched. Allow a full morning.

**The Albanian Riviera** — the coastal road from Vlorë south to Saranda passes dramatic cliffs, clear water, and a string of coves that rival anywhere in Greece or Croatia at half the price. Dhërmi and Ksamil are the standout beaches. Go in June or September to avoid the peak-summer crowds.

**Krujë** — the castle town north of Tirana that defines Albanian national identity. Skanderbeg held off the Ottomans here for 25 years. The fortress museum is decent; the view from the walls is excellent. Easy as a day trip from [Tirana](/europe/albania/tirana).

**The Accursed Mountains (Alpet Shqiptare)** — the remote north is increasingly accessible to trekkers. The Peaks of the Balkans trail, shared with Kosovo and Montenegro, is one of the finest multi-day hikes in Europe. Theth and Valbonë are the main bases; transport from Shkodër.

**The Blue Eye** (*Syri i Kaltër*) — a natural spring of extraordinary clarity near Saranda. The water is a deep, shifting blue over an apparently bottomless pool. It sounds like a tourist cliché; it genuinely is not.
