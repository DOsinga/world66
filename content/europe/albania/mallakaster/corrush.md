Corrush is a small village in the [Mallakastër](/europe/albania/mallakaster) district of southern [Albania](/europe/albania), set among the dry, rolling hills between the Vjosa river valley and the Adriatic coast. The landscape here is quintessentially southern Albanian — olive groves, scrubby hillsides, and a few scattered stone houses connected by rough roads.

The village is known locally as the birthplace of Mehmet Shehu, who served as Prime Minister of Albania for nearly three decades under the Hoxha regime before his mysterious death in 1981, officially ruled a suicide but widely suspected to have been murder ordered by Hoxha himself. His former house still stands, though it is not maintained as any kind of memorial.

Corrush is not a tourist destination by any measure, but travelers passing through the Mallakastër area on the way between Fier and the coast may find the surrounding countryside appealing in its austerity. The nearby ruins of Apollonia, one of the most important ancient Greek colonies on the Adriatic, are the main reason anyone comes to this part of Albania and are well worth a detour.
