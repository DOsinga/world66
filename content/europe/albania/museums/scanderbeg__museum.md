Inside the restored castle of Kruje, this museum tells the story of Gjergj Kastrioti Skanderbeg, the 15th-century Albanian lord who held off the Ottoman Empire for a quarter century. The museum building, designed by Pranvera Hoxha, is a modern concrete structure that echoes the castle walls. Displays include replicas of Skanderbeg's helmet and sword, Ottoman-era weapons, and documents from the period. The castle grounds themselves offer views across the valley below.
