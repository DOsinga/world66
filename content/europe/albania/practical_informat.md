**Currency**

The Albanian lek (ALL). As of 2025, roughly 100 lek = €0.90. Cash is still widely used — carry lek for small restaurants, furgons, markets, and rural areas. ATMs are plentiful in Tirana and other cities. Many tourist-facing businesses also accept euros, though you will usually get a worse rate.

**Visas**

Citizens of EU countries, the UK, the US, Canada, and Australia can enter without a visa and stay up to 90 days. A valid passport is required (identity cards suffice for EU nationals). US citizens can stay up to one year without a residency permit.

**Language**

Albanian (Shqip) is the official language. The two main dialects are Gheg (north) and Tosk (south); standard Albanian is based on Tosk. Italian is widely understood, especially in the south and among older generations. English is spoken by most younger people in the cities.

**Safety**

Albania is generally safe for travellers. The blood feud tradition (gjakmarrja) associated with northern mountain clans has declined sharply and is not a hazard for visitors. Petty crime exists in the cities as everywhere. Traffic is the most real hazard: pedestrian crossings are not always respected.

**Mobile and internet**

SIM cards from local operators (ALBtelecom, Vodafone Albania, ONE Telecommunications) are cheap and easy to buy. 4G coverage is good in cities and along main roads. Free Wi-Fi is widely available in cafes and hotels.

**Health**

No vaccinations are required for Albania. Tap water is drinkable in most cities but locals and travellers often prefer bottled. Medical facilities are adequate in Tirana; elsewhere carry basic supplies.

**Electricity**

230V, European two-pin plugs (Type C/F).
