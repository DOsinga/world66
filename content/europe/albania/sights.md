Albania packs a remarkable amount of history into a small country. Its position as a crossroads between east and west — Greek, Roman, Byzantine, Ottoman, and Italian all left marks — makes for a dense archaeological and architectural landscape.

**UNESCO World Heritage Sites**

[Berat](/europe/albania/berat) and [Gjirokastër](/europe/albania/gjirokaster) are jointly listed for their exceptional examples of Ottoman-era architecture. Berat's layered hillside of white Ottoman houses, each with rows of large windows (earning it the nickname "town of a thousand windows"), is one of the most striking urban landscapes in the Balkans. Gjirokastër is known as the "City of Stone" — grey slate roofs, steep cobbled streets, and a massive hilltop fortress.

[Butrint](/europe/albania/butrint) is Albania's other UNESCO site: a compact peninsula containing layers of Greek, Roman, Byzantine, and Venetian ruins in a lakeside national park near Saranda.

**Castles and fortresses**

Rozafa Castle above [Shkodër](/europe/albania/shkoder) offers sweeping views over three rivers and the city below. [Krujë](/europe/albania/kruje) has the castle where Skanderbeg held off Ottoman forces for decades in the 15th century, with a good national museum attached. Lezha Castle is a less-visited but atmospheric ruin with Skanderbeg's tomb in the town below.

**Ancient sites**

[Apolonia](/europe/albania/apolonia), near Fier, was a significant Greek city-state; the standing temple portico and museum are well worth the detour. [Butrint](/europe/albania/butrint) offers more, with a Greek theatre, Roman baths, and Byzantine basilica all within a short walk.

**Tirana**

[Tirana](/europe/albania/tirana) itself has grown into a lively capital with an improving museum scene. The National History Museum on Skënderbej Square is a good introduction to Albanian history. The former Enver Hoxha residence and the pyramid built in his honour (now a tech education space) are curiosities of the communist era. The colourful facades painted under Mayor Edi Rama in the early 2000s are now embedded in the city's character.
