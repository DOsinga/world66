---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The city centre is the starting point: Skanderbeg Square, with its equestrian statue and the National History Museum on one side, and the Et'hem Bey Mosque in the corner — one of the few religious buildings to survive communist-era demolitions. The museum covers Albanian history from ancient Illyria through independence and communism; the communist-era section is particularly striking.

The Pyramid (Piramida), originally built as a museum dedicated to Enver Hoxha and now repurposed as a tech education space, is a short walk from the square. Its brutalist form is unmistakable and worth seeing as a physical relic of the communist era. Bunk'Art 1 and Bunk'Art 2, both in former nuclear bunkers, are the most original museums in the city — one focused on the communist secret police (Sigurimi), the other on military history. The Martyr's Cemetery on the city's eastern edge has good views and holds the graves of those who died in World War II resistance.

Mount Dajti is reached by cable car (gondola) from the city outskirts — a 15-minute ride to 1,600 metres with views over the city and surrounding hills. The park at the top has restaurants and hiking trails.
