Vlorë is Albania's second port and a city with significant historical weight: it was here, on 28 November 1912, that Ismail Qemali declared Albanian independence from the Ottoman Empire. The Independence Museum commemorates the event and is worth a visit.

The city sits where the Adriatic and Ionian seas meet, and the geography is reflected in the character of the coast nearby. North of Vlorë the beach is flat and sandy (the Adriatic side); south of the city the road climbs into the dramatic cliffs of the Riviera, and the Ionian character of the water — clearer and deeper blue — becomes immediately apparent.

Kanina Castle, about 4 km from the city on a hilltop, offers views over Vlorë Bay and the surrounding coast. The Muradie Mosque, built in the 16th century, is one of the finest Ottoman buildings in Albania.

Vlorë is the main jumping-off point for the Albanian Riviera. Llogara Pass, 40 km south, marks the beginning of the most dramatic coastal stretch. Ferry connections to Brindisi in Italy operate from the port.
