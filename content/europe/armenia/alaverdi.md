Alaverdi is an industrial copper-mining town at the bottom of the Debed Gorge in [Armenia](/europe/armenia)'s northern Lori province, close to the Georgian border. The name means "Allah gave" in Turkish. The town itself, with its Soviet-era apartment blocks and defunct smelter, is not much to look at — but it is the base for visiting some of Armenia's finest medieval monasteries.

[Haghpat](/europe/armenia/haghpat_3) and [Sanahin](/europe/armenia/sanahin_1), both UNESCO World Heritage Sites, are perched on the cliffs above the gorge and are among the masterpieces of Armenian architecture. [Odzun](/europe/armenia/odzun), with its 6th-century basilica, is also nearby. The gorge scenery along the road from [Vanadzor](/europe/armenia/vanadzor) is dramatic — steep, forested walls dropping to the river.

Marshrutkas (shared minivans) run from [Yerevan](/europe/armenia/yerevan) several times a day — the three-hour journey goes via the Spitak Pass and Vanadzor before descending into the gorge. The overnight [Yerevan](/europe/armenia/yerevan)–[Tbilisi](/europe/georgia/tbilisi) train also stops here.
