Amberd is a medieval fortress and church complex on the southern slopes of Mount Aragats at around 2,300 metres, in Aragatsotn province. It was the stronghold of the Pahlavuni princes in the 10th–11th centuries and one of the most important feudal castles in medieval Armenia.

The fortress walls are largely intact and the single-nave Vahramashen Church (1026 AD) stands next to them in good condition. The landscape is the real draw: volcanic rock, high alpine meadows, and on a clear day, sweeping views over the Ararat valley. A small river runs through the gorge below.

Amberd is accessible by car from Yerevan in about two hours via Aparan and the Aragats massif road. The last section of road is unpaved. The site is usually accessible from May or June once the snows melt, and is popular with day-trippers in summer. There are no facilities at the site itself.
