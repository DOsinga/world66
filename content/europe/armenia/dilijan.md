Dilijan is a small, forested town in Tavush province in northeastern Armenia, popular with Armenians as a weekend retreat and known as "Armenia's Little Switzerland." The comparison is flattering but the basic point holds: the air is cool, the hills are covered in deciduous forest, and it feels very different from the dry Ararat plain around Yerevan.

The town has an attractive restored old quarter (Old Dilijan) with artisan workshops, cafés, and craft shops. It is also the location of Dilijan International School of Armenia, a UWC school, which has brought an international community to the area.

The main reason to linger is the access it gives to two outstanding monasteries. **Haghartsin Monastery** (13th century) is a 20-minute drive through forest north of town — a complex of three churches, a gavit (vestibule), and a refectory in a quiet wooded valley. **Goshavank** (also 13th century) is a further 20km east in the village of Gosh, and has intricate stone carvings and a distinctive setting.

Dilijan is also the gateway to the Transcaucasian Trail, a long-distance hiking route through the region.

The drive from Yerevan takes about two hours via the Sevan highway. Marshrutkas run several times a day.
