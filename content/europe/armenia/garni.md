The main reason to come to Garni is the two-thousand-year-old Temple of Garni, the only standing Greco-Roman colonnaded building in Armenia. Built in the 1st century AD and dedicated to the sun god Mihr, it stands on the edge of a gorge with a drop of 300 metres to the Azat river below — a spectacular setting.

The temple was destroyed by an earthquake in 1679 and painstakingly reconstructed in the 1970s from its original basalt blocks. Next to it are the remains of a Roman bath with a well-preserved mosaic floor. The gorge itself is worth walking down to see the "Symphony of Stones," a formation of basalt columns that looks like a natural organ pipe.

Garni is usually visited as a day trip from [Yerevan](/europe/armenia/yerevan), combined with the cave monastery of [Geghard](/europe/armenia/geghard) a few kilometres further up the valley.
