title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Air:

*Armenian Airlines *operates weekly flights linking Yerevan with Paris Amsterdam London Frankfurt/M Delhi Athens Beirut and Tehran and twice-weekly flights to Fujairah UAE. Lack of demand unreliable fuel supplies and an uneconomic fare structure has resulted in greatly reduced services to other CIS republics. *Aeroflot* and *Armenian Airlines* operate flights to Yerevan from Moscow; these are often subject to delays and cancellations. For political reasons there are no international transport links between Armenia and Azerbaijan; Georgia is sometimes used as a stopover point.

International airport

*Zvartnots (EVN)* 10km (6 miles) from Yerevan. Buses and taxis ferry passengers from the airport to the city centre. Buses run every 15 minutes between 0700-2100 (journey time  30 minutes).

Rail:

Armenia's rail links to Azerbaijan and Turkey have been closed indefinitely but international lines do run to Georgia and Iran. Passengers travelling to Georgia should be aware of the possibility of theft or robbery.

Road:

Traffic drives on the left. A road link between Armenia and Iran the Kajaran highway has become the most important international road link. A road linking Yerevan to the Georgian capital Tbilisi has a bad reputation for highway robbery although efforts by the Georgian authorities to enforce law and order are reported to be paying off. There are no road crossings into Turkey. It is possible to travel by road to the enclave of Nagorno Karabakh however it is essential to obtain a visa from the permanent representative of Nagorno Karabakh in Yerevan.
