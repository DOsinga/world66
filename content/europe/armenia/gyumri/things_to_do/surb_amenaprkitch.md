The grandest church in Gyumri and the one that dominates Vardanantz Square. Built between 1854 and 1873 under the direction of Tadevos Andikyan, it was modelled on the medieval cathedral of Ani and is impressive for its sheer scale — it literally cast a shadow over the Greek and Catholic churches that stood on either side. Badly damaged in the 1988 earthquake, it has been under restoration for years; the scaffolding is part of the scenery now, but the building's ambition is clear even partially draped.
