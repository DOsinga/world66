Built in 1870, this Apostolic church is also known as "Sev Zham" (Black Church) for the dark hue of its tuff stone. It had one of the more unusual Soviet-era repurposings: converted into an astronomical observatory, with the dome serving as the telescope housing. The cross on top bent when lightning struck — and, according to local lore, straightened itself again after the observatory closed. Damaged in the 1988 earthquake and since partially restored.
