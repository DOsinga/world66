The spiritual heart of Gyumri, and one of only two churches in all of Soviet Armenia — the other being Echmiadzin — permitted to operate throughout the Soviet period. When all other Gyumri churches were closed, one vestry here served the Catholic community, the other served Russian believers, making it an unlikely ecumenical refuge. The church dates to the 17th century and takes its name from the icon of "Yotverk" (Seven Wounds of the Virgin), brought here by refugees and still kept inside.
