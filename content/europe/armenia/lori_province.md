Lori is Armenia's greenest province, a landscape of deep river gorges, dense forests, and medieval monasteries perched on canyon edges. The provincial capital Vanadzor serves as a base, but the real draws are scattered through the countryside.

The UNESCO-listed monasteries of Haghpat and Sanahin, both dating to the 10th century, sit on opposite sides of a gorge near Alaverdi. Haghpat is the better preserved of the two, with remarkable khachkars (carved stone crosses) and a scriptorium. Sanahin's academy was one of medieval Armenia's most important centers of learning.

The Debed Canyon between the two monasteries is dramatic — sheer walls dropping hundreds of meters to the river below. The town of Alaverdi at the bottom was once a copper-smelting center and retains a gritty industrial character.

Further south, Stepanavan offers access to Lori Berd, a 12th-century fortress on a promontory between two rivers, and the pleasant Dendropark with walking trails through planted forest. The province rewards those willing to explore beyond the main highway.
