Sevanavank (Սևանավանք) is a monastery on the peninsula of Lake Sevan, near the town of Sevan. The lake is a popular summer destination — [Armenia](/europe/armenia) is landlocked, and Sevan is the closest thing to a sea resort. The monastery's setting on the lake shore is beautiful.

It was built in the 9th century soon after Armenia freed itself from Arab rule. Originally it stood on an island, isolated and strict — monks from [Echmiadzin](/europe/armenia/echmiadzin) who had transgressed were sent here as penance.

Two small churches remain: Sourb Arakelots (Holy Apostles) and Sourb Astvatsatsin (Holy Mother of God). During the Soviet era, Stalin ordered the lake level to be lowered to provide water and hydroelectric power; the island became a peninsula. The level dropped around 20 metres from its original height. After Stalin's death the project was stopped and partially reversed, but the peninsula remains. The eastern part is fenced off as a presidential residence.

No entry fee — visitors are expected to buy and light candles inside the churches. Because the area is a beach resort in summer, the dress code is notably relaxed compared to most Armenian churches.

Buses and marshrutkas to Sevan town run frequently from near Yeritasardakan metro station in Yerevan; the journey takes about an hour. From Sevan town, the monastery is about a 30-minute walk. A taxi from the town to the monastery costs around 1,000 AMD; be wary of higher offers.

The lake sits at approximately 2,000 metres above sea level — sun exposure is intense in summer. Wear sunscreen and a hat.

There are plenty of hotels, restaurants, and beach facilities along the lake shore, though the town of Sevan itself has limited options.
