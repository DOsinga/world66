---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Armenia's attractions are spread across ten provinces (marzer), and almost every region has something worth seeing. The concentration of medieval monasteries is extraordinary — many are UNESCO-listed, several are still active, and some sit in landscapes that would justify the trip on their own.

The most-visited sites are all within easy reach of [Yerevan](/europe/armenia/yerevan): the Hellenistic temple at [Garni](/europe/armenia/garni), the rock-carved monastery of [Geghard](/europe/armenia/geghard), the holy see of [Echmiadzin](/europe/armenia/echmiadzin) and its surrounding early Christian churches, the ruins of [Zvartnots](/europe/armenia/zvartnots) cathedral, and [Khor Virap](/europe/armenia/khorvirap) monastery at the foot of Mount Ararat. These can each be done as a day trip, or combined in pairs.

Moving further afield: the Debed canyon in northern Lori province contains the UNESCO monasteries of [Haghpat](/europe/armenia/haghpat_3) and [Sanahin](/europe/armenia/sanahin_1), both near the industrial town of [Alaverdi](/europe/armenia/alaverdi). The monastery of [Sevanavank](/europe/armenia/sevanavank) sits on a peninsula jutting into [Lake Sevan](/europe/armenia/gegharkunik_marz) at 2,000 metres. And in the far south, the 9th-century Tatev Monastery in Syunik province is reached by the Wings of Tatev cable car — at nearly six kilometres, one of the longest reversible aerial tramways in the world.

Other highlights worth building a trip around: the petroglyphs of Ughtasar mountain in Syunik; the standing-stone astronomical site at Carahunge near Sisian (older and broader than Stonehenge); the volcanic peak of Mount Aragats (4,090m), Armenia's highest mountain, accessible with a guide from the Aragatsotn plateau; and the Noratus khachkar cemetery near Lake Sevan, the largest collection of carved stone crosses in the world.

The forest monasteries of Tavush province — [Dilijan](/europe/armenia/dilijan), Haghartsin, Goshavank — are popular for their wooded setting and tend to be less crowded than the Yerevan-circuit sites.
