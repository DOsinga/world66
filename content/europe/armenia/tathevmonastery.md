Tatev Monastery, founded in the 9th century, sits on a dramatic basalt promontory above the Vorotan River gorge in southern Syunik province. It is one of the most impressive monastic complexes in Armenia — architecturally and in its setting.

The monastery was a major centre of medieval Armenian scholarship and housed an important university between the 14th and 18th centuries. The main church of St. Paul and Peter (895 AD) and the smaller Church of St. Gregory are well-preserved. Inside, look for the oscillating column (gavazan) — a slender stone pillar that was designed to sway during earthquakes as an early warning system.

Getting there is half the appeal. The Wings of Tatev cable car (Tatever) makes the 5.7km crossing above the Vorotan gorge in under 12 minutes. At the time of its opening in 2010 it was the world's longest non-stop double-track cable car. The views over the canyon are extraordinary. The cable car operates daily; check locally for current hours and ticket prices.

A guesthouse in the monastery itself offers simple accommodation for those who want to spend a night in the complex after the day visitors leave.
