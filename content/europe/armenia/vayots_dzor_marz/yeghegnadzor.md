Yeghegnadzor is the capital of [Vayots Dzor](/europe/armenia/vayots_dzor_marz), Armenia's least populated province, sitting in a sun-baked valley along the main highway that runs south from Yerevan toward the Iranian border. The town itself is modest — Soviet-era apartment blocks, a central park, a few shops and cafes — but its location makes it the natural base for exploring one of Armenia's most rewarding regions.

The real attractions are all within day-trip range. Noravank monastery, about 25 minutes south, sits at the end of a dramatic red-rock gorge and is one of the most photogenic buildings in the country. The Areni wine region is just down the road, where you can taste Armenian reds at small family wineries. The Areni-1 cave complex nearby yielded the world's oldest known leather shoe, dating back over 5,500 years, along with evidence of the earliest known winemaking — a discovery that gave Armenian winemakers considerable bragging rights.

Yeghegnadzor has a few simple restaurants serving khorovats and other Armenian staples, and the town makes a good overnight stop if you are driving between Yerevan and the south. The Selim Caravanserai, a beautifully preserved 14th-century Silk Road waystation on the mountain pass to the north, is another worthwhile stop in the area.
