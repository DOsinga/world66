---
title: "Day Trips in Yerevan"
type: section
---

**Yerevan day trips** include Lake Sevan, Mt. Aragats, and the
Vayots Dzor. All are within an hour or two drive from the capital of
Armenia, but travelers may wish to maximize the days by leaving early
or by allowing for last-minute overnight stays if the area is found
compelling. The areas provide a look into the culture, geography, and
history of Armenia.**
Vayots Dzor **

Literally meaning the "valley of woes," the Vayots Dzor region was
damaged by several earthquakes over the course of centuries. Although
sparsely inhabited by people, the region is full of medieval castles,
churches, canyons, and caves. The area near the Arpa River is popular
for fishing, and the more mountainous area is known for hiking and
mountain biking.

The region consists of multiple
municipalities, some of which have small hotels or B&B's. Some
visitors to the Vayots Dzor may find a day trip is not sufficient to
fully appreciate the area. The region might be better appreciated with
a tour guide who could provide historical context along the drive.

**
Mt. Aragats**

The highest peak in present-day Armenia, Mt. Aragats stands at more
than 4,000 meters (13,000 feet). The mountain is an extinct volcano. It
stands snow-covered for most of the year, so climbing is advisable
during the summer months. Early morning departure times are recommended
for the best conditions and views.

Day visitors can stick to
the picturesque foothills or hike near the southern access route, which
is easier and less strenuous than the northern access route. The
northern approach may be better explored with a guide.

**
Lake Sevan**

Lake Sevan is Armenia's largest lake. It is also one of the higher
altitude lakes in the world, at 1,900 meters (6,200 feet) above sea
level. It is one of three large lakes with historical significance to
Armenia; however, it is the only lake to still reside within the
country's borders. Attractions at the lake include the Sevanavank
Monastery, on the northwestern shore. The monastery used to be on an
island, but over time the island became a peninsula. The area also has
several beaches and recreational areas all around the lake, including
several hotels and resorts. Some of the beaches have entry fees.

During the summer there is a train to Lake Sevan, but the trip takes several hours and is not ideal for a day trip.
