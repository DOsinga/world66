Baden bei Wien is a spa town about 25 km south of [Vienna](/europe/austria/vienna), set at the edge of the Vienna Woods where hot sulphur springs have drawn visitors since Roman times. The town reached its peak of fashionability in the early 19th century, when the Habsburg court spent summers here, and Beethoven composed parts of his Ninth Symphony in a house on Frauengasse.

The Kurpark is the centrepiece — a large, well-tended park with a bandstand, rose garden, and access to the thermal baths. The town centre has an attractive Biedermeier character, with a pedestrian zone lined by cafes and pastry shops. The Casino Baden, one of Europe's largest, draws weekend visitors from Vienna. If you are staying in Vienna and want a half-day escape with thermal baths and good coffee, Baden is an easy train ride away.
