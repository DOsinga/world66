The Bregenzerwald is a valley region in the Austrian state of Vorarlberg, east of Bregenz and running up into the Allgäu Alps. In winter it offers skiing and snowboarding across a linked network of mid-sized resorts — Schoppernau, Damüls, and Mellau among them — at accessible prices compared to the larger Austrian ski areas. In summer the valley transforms into excellent hiking and cycling terrain with well-marked trails through green meadows, forests, and alpine pastures. The region is known for its strong local craft and architecture traditions, including a distinctive school of contemporary timber construction.
