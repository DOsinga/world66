---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

[Vienna](/europe/austria/vienna) is one of Europe's great capital cities, and it doesn't let you forget it. The Habsburgs built on an imperial scale — Schönbrunn Palace, the Hofburg, the Ringstrasse boulevards — and the city wears its grandeur without apology. But Vienna is more than monuments. Its coffee house culture is a UNESCO-recognized tradition: sit in Café Central or Hawelka, order a Melange, read the newspaper on a wooden stick, and understand that in Vienna, lingering is not laziness but a way of life.

[Salzburg](/europe/austria/salzburg) trades on Mozart and The Sound of Music, and honestly, both are worth your time. The old town, wedged between the Salzach River and the Festungsberg with its hilltop fortress, is impossibly photogenic. Beyond the obvious, Salzburg is a serious music city — the summer festival draws the world's best performers, and even outside festival season, there's a concert or recital happening somewhere every night.

The Austrian Alps are the real thing. This is some of the best skiing in the world — St. Anton, Kitzbühel, the Arlberg, the Zillertal — with reliable snow, excellent infrastructure, and lively après-ski. In summer, the same mountains become a hiker's paradise, with thousands of kilometers of marked trails, staffed mountain huts serving hot meals, and views that make you stop mid-stride. You don't need to be an extreme mountaineer: Austria does gentle valley walks and family-friendly trails just as well as serious Alpine routes.

[Hallstatt](/europe/austria/hallstatt) and the Salzkammergut lake district deserve their fame, even if Instagram has made Hallstatt absurdly crowded in peak season. Come early in the morning or in the shoulder months and you'll see why people have lived here for thousands of years — literally, this is one of Europe's oldest continuously inhabited settlements. The surrounding lakes, especially Wolfgangsee, Attersee, and Traunsee, are stunning and far less visited.

The Wachau Valley, a short trip west of Vienna along the Danube, is Austria's premier wine country and a UNESCO World Heritage landscape. Terraced vineyards climb steep hillsides above the river, ruined castles sit on rocky outcrops, and apricot orchards bloom in spring. This is Grüner Veltliner and Riesling territory. Cycle the river path, stop at a riverside Heuriger, and taste wines where they're grown.

[Graz](/europe/austria/graz), Austria's second city, is the one most visitors miss, and that's their loss. It has a beautiful medieval old town, a thriving university that keeps things young, and arguably the best food scene in the country — Styria is Austria's culinary heartland, known for pumpkin seed oil, wine, and farm-to-table cooking. The Kunsthaus, a blob-shaped modern art museum on the riverbank, is either a masterpiece or an eyesore depending on who you ask, but it's never boring.
