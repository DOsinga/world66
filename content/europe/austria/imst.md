Imst is a small market town in the **Tyrol**, about 50 km west of Innsbruck on the road to the Arlberg pass. It sits at around 830 metres in a broad sunny valley sheltered by the 7 km-long cliff wall of the **Heiterwand**, which gives the town one of the mildest microclimates in the Alps — the surrounding slopes are dry enough that almond and fig trees grow at altitudes where you would normally expect only pines. Despite this, the winters are still proper Alpine winters and Imst has a small ski area of its own at **Hoch-Imst**, reached by gondola from the edge of town.

Imst itself has around 10,000 inhabitants and is best known in Tyrol for two things. The first is the **Schemenlaufen**, a pre-Lenten masked carnival procession held every four years (a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage tradition) in which locals parade through the streets in elaborately carved wooden masks representing mountain spirits and devils. The second is the town's setting: it is the natural base for exploring the **Ötztal** and **Pitztal** valleys just to the south, both of them full of high-altitude walks, glaciers and ski resorts, and for visiting the **Fernpass** and the lakes of the **Ausserfern** district to the north. For travellers passing through, Imst is less a destination than a pleasant, unfussy Alpine town to spend a night.
