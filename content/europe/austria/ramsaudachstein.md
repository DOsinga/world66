Ramsau am Dachstein is a mountain village in the Styrian Alps, spread across a sunny plateau at around 1,100 metres with the massive Dachstein glacier looming behind it. The setting is spectacular — green meadows in front, sheer limestone walls behind — and the village has managed to stay genuinely Alpine without tipping into theme-park territory.

In winter, Ramsau is one of Austria's premier cross-country skiing destinations, with over 200 kilometres of groomed trails winding through the valley and up toward the glacier. Downhill skiing is available on the Dachstein glacier itself, which offers year-round snow, and at the nearby Planai resort in [Schladming](/europe/austria/schladming). In summer, the area transforms into hiking and climbing country. The Dachstein Skywalk, a glass platform jutting out from the mountain at 2,700 metres, offers vertigo-inducing views across the Alps, and the ice caves inside the mountain are worth the cable car ride up.

The village is quiet and traditional, with wooden farmhouses, a couple of inns, and a small church at its center. Ramsau makes a good base for exploring the wider Schladming-Dachstein region, whether you are here for the mountains, the glacier, or simply the clean air and the views.
