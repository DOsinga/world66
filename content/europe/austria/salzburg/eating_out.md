---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Salzburg's dining is solidly Austrian with a strong Italian influence from the nearby border. The local speciality is **Salzburger Nockerln**, an enormous fluffy egg-and-sugar soufflé traditionally shaped in three peaks to represent the hills around the city — more of an event than a dessert. Beyond that, expect **Wiener schnitzel**, **Tafelspitz** (boiled beef in broth), mountain trout, and seasonal game. The **Sternbräu** and **Stiegl-Keller** beer halls are the obvious choices for hearty food and local beer; **St. Peter Stiftskeller**, attached to St Peter's Abbey, has been serving meals since at least 803 CE and claims to be the oldest restaurant in Europe. For something more refined, **Magazin** and **Esszimmer** both hold Michelin stars. For a coffee and *Sachertorte*, head to **Café Tomaselli** on Alter Markt, Salzburg's oldest coffeehouse and a favourite of the Mozart family themselves.
