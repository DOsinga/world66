---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Almost everything worth seeing in Salzburg is on foot and in the UNESCO-listed old town on the left bank of the **Salzach**. A sensible day starts at **Mozart's Birthplace** on Getreidegasse, continues through **Residenzplatz** and the **Residenz** state rooms, into the **Dom** (cathedral) and across the square to **St Peter's Abbey** with its remarkable cemetery. From there, the funicular at the back of the abbey climbs to the **Festung Hohensalzburg**, the enormous hilltop fortress that has dominated the city for nearly a thousand years — take an hour for the interior and the view. Cross the river for the **Mirabell Palace and Gardens** and the **Mozart Wohnhaus** (the family home). If you have a second day, take a bus or a taxi out to **Schloss Hellbrunn** for the famous trick fountains. And if you are in town during the **Salzburg Festival** (July–August), even a single concert in the Festspielhaus is unforgettable.
