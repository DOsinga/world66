The narrow yellow townhouse at **Getreidegasse 9** was rented by Leopold Mozart and his family from 1747 to 1773, and it was on the third floor of this building that **Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart** was born on 27 January 1756. It is now the most visited museum in Salzburg — the rooms hold Mozart's childhood violin, his concert violin, his clavichord, the pianoforte on which he composed *The Magic Flute*, and a fine collection of family portraits and letters. The approach through Getreidegasse — the narrow medieval shopping street with its wrought-iron guild signs — is one of the great sights of the city in its own right.
