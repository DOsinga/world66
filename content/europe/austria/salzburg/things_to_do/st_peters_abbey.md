**St Peter's Abbey** (Stift St Peter) was founded around 696 CE by St Rupert, the patron saint of Salzburg, and may be the oldest continuously occupied monastery in the German-speaking world. The Romanesque church was converted to baroque in the 18th century, and both the monastic cloisters and the famous library (containing over 100,000 volumes, some of them 9th-century manuscripts) are still in daily use by the Benedictine community.

The real attraction for most visitors is the adjacent **Petersfriedhof** — St Peter's Cemetery — one of the oldest in Europe, with elaborate wrought-iron grave markers, family crypts going back to the 17th century, and the graves of Mozart's sister *Nannerl*, the composer Michael Haydn, and Santino Solari (architect of Salzburg Cathedral). Above the cemetery, cut into the cliff face of the Mönchsberg, are the **catacombs** — small hermits' chapels reached by a narrow stone stairway. The *Sound of Music* buffs may recognise the cemetery as the set for the scene in which the Von Trapp family hides from the Nazis.
