**Schladming** is a small town of about 7,000 people in the **Enns Valley** of Styria, in the southern Austrian Alps, and one of the most important ski resorts in Austria. It sits at 745 metres, wedged between the limestone peaks of the **Dachstein** massif to the north and the schist mountains of the **Schladminger Tauern** to the south. The town has a pretty pedestrianised centre of pastel-painted houses and onion-domed church towers, and a history going back over 600 years (originally as a silver and copper mining town). But most visitors come for the snow.

The **Ski amadé** network — Schladming is one of its main centres — is the largest single ski area in Austria, linking four mountains directly above the town (**Planai**, **Hochwurzen**, **Hauser Kaibling**, **Reiteralm**) with a high-speed lift system and giving skiers access to over 200 km of marked runs on a single pass. The terrain is intermediate-friendly and extremely well-maintained, snow-making is almost complete, and the lifts are modern and fast. The area hosts regular **World Cup** ski races — most famously the **Night Slalom** on Planai every January, one of the most atmospheric events on the World Cup circuit, watched by 40,000 spectators cheering under floodlights. Schladming also hosted the 2013 Alpine Ski World Championships, which triggered a major infrastructure upgrade.

In summer the same cable cars run for hikers and mountain bikers. The **Dachstein Glacier** a few kilometres north of the town is accessible by cable car from the village of Ramsau and offers year-round skiing, high-altitude walks, and the spectacular **Dachstein Skywalk** glass platform suspended over a 250-metre drop. The nearby **Grossglockner High Alpine Road**, the **Hallstatt** lake (a short drive north), and the mountain lakes of the Salzkammergut are all within day-trip range. Schladming is easily reached from **Salzburg** (90 minutes) or **Graz** (2 hours) by train or road.
