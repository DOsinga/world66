Whether you're planning to spend a day, a week, or a month in Vienna, this is a guide you'll want to take along.

This book is beautifully illustrated with maps and photos, but its
relatively compact size (8.6 x 5 inches) makes it convenient for
carrying along while traveling.

It contains maps of various parts of Vienna, although not a good
comprehensive city map, so you'll want to purchase one of those when
you reach Vienna.

In keeping with the DK Publishing trademark, the book is visually
beautiful, featuring 3-D aerial views of various parts of the city.

The book is not heavy on detail, but tells most of what you would
need and want to know as a tourist: some basic dates important to
Viennese history and brief segments on various popular tourist
locations. There is a star rating system of tourist spots, helping you
easily find "must see" attractions, complete with cutaway illustrations
and floorplans to help you get the most out of your visit.

A "survivor's guide" to the city is helpful as well; it contains
practical information such as how to use the city's underground metro
system (U-Bahn) and a diagram of the system's various lines, and more.

Various hotels and restaurants are described and rated as well.

A common drawback of tourist books is the problem of keeping all
information timely. For the most up to the minute information, you can
check http://us.dk.com/ for updates on this and other travel guides.
