The MuseumsQuartier is one of the world's largest cultural complexes, a cluster of museums and creative spaces set within the converted former imperial stables. The Leopold Museum holds the definitive collection of Egon Schiele alongside major Gustav Klimt works, while MUMOK (Museum of Modern Art) covers everything from pop art to Vienna Actionism. The central courtyard is a gathering place in its own right — Viennese of all ages sprawl on the colorful Enzis (oversized sculptural benches) with drinks and laptops, blurring the line between museum campus and public living room. Just beyond the complex, Mariahilfer Straße is Vienna's main shopping boulevard, stretching west with a mix of international chains and local shops. The surrounding Seventh District (Neubau) has a creative, slightly alternative edge with concept stores and specialty coffee roasters. It's the part of Vienna that feels most like a modern European city rather than an open-air museum.
