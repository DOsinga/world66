---
title: "Getting There in Zell am See"
type: section
---

Zell am See is a popular skiing destination in the west of Austria.
Its set into the mountains of the Alps near the Kaprun glacier; the
latter is a ten-minute journey by bus. Most of the town centre is free
from traffic, and its calm, medieval traditions keep tourists coming
back to the town year after year.

**Getting to Zell am See by Plane**

While not gifted with an airport, the town of Zell am See is
adequately served by trains from Salzburg and other main cities. For
people looking to visit Zell am See, getting there is comparatively
easy: the town is about 50 miles from Salzburg, and the latter city has
an airport which is served by some of the major airplane companies.-

Catch a plane to Salzburg from an international airport, such as London or Paris- Take the shuttle from Salzburg airport to the train station- Take a train to the resort- You’re there!**
Getting There by Other Means**

The alternative to taking the train is to get into a car and drive
there. Zell am See is about an hour and a half’s drive from Salzburg,
and three hours from the city of Munich. If you plan to land in
Salzburg airport and rent a car, here is the route:

- Head south from the airport on the A1 or A10- Take the B311 for Zell am See- Cars heading from Munich should take the autobahn to Salzburg and follow the above directions.
