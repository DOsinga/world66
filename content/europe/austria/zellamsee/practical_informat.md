title: "Practical Information in Zell am See"
type: section
---

The popular skiing resort of** **Zell am See** **can be found in the
Alps, on the Austrian side; it's in the western part of Austria, a little bit
further south than Salzburg. Due to its popularity, not only with
skiers and snowboarders, but also hikers and mountain bikers (and it is
even possible to do horseback-riding in Zell am See), the town has a good
selection of tourist services.

Practical information on weather, transportation and other
attractions in the town are all vitally important to skiers planning a
trip to the Alps. Learning the best time to visit, hotel resorts available and
places to see are all vitally important.
**Visiting the Town**

Zell am See attracts tourists to the resort
purely on the basis of its own worth as a tourist destination. It has
an interesting medieval town, and attractions include the Tower of
Kastnerturn and Pfarrkirche Church, both of which can be dated to the 12th
century – what other ski resorts can make that boast? The Tower also
contains a museum of Austrian folklore, making it an interesting
cultural attraction for many Europeans. Many European tourists visit
Zell am See for its well-known nightlife, considered lively but not too
demanding.

**Weather**

The weather in Zell am See can actually be surprisingly moderate for
a skiing resort, which sometimes affects the availability of snow –
during times of warm weather, there can be little to do except queue
endlessly for the Kuprun Glacier – so it's necessary to consider this
when planning a holiday. June and July are traditionally the warmest
months in the town, and summer activities are best planned for these
months. Winter months can also be bitterly cold, so be careful to
prepare for the weather when booking skiing holidays. Below zero
temps are the norm.
**

Travelling to Zell am See**

As is typical of much of the European skiing industry, the best form
of transport to Zell am See is via train. There are connections to be
made from Salzburg and other cities in the area. Tourists may find
that Salzburg is the best option, as there are many flights there
from major airports such as London and Paris. Once in Zell am See, there
are ski-buses to the slopes, as well as rental cars for those who wish
to explore the surrounding countryside.
