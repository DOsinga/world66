---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The main gateway is **Heydar Aliyev International Airport** (GYD) in [Baku](/europe/azerbaijan/baku), which has direct connections to most European capitals, Istanbul, Dubai, Moscow, Tehran, and major Central Asian cities. Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) is the national carrier; Turkish Airlines, Flydubai, and several other carriers also serve Baku. Ganja has its own small airport with flights from Istanbul and a few CIS cities, useful if you are heading straight to western Azerbaijan.

**Visa:** Most nationalities need a visa. The easiest option is the ASAN e-visa, applied for online at evisa.gov.az. The fee is around USD 25, and most applications are approved within three working days. The e-visa allows a single entry and a 30-day stay. Citizens of Turkey, Georgia, and several other countries can enter visa-free; check the current list before you travel. Importantly, if you have evidence in your passport of visiting Armenia, you will be refused entry — Azerbaijani border officials take this seriously.

**By land from Georgia:** The main crossing is at the Red Bridge (Krasny Most) near Agstafa, on the Baku–Tbilisi highway. The road is well-travelled and the border is generally straightforward. Marshrutkas and shared taxis run between Tbilisi and Baku via this route.

**By land from Iran:** The crossing at [Astara](/europe/azerbaijan/astara) on the Caspian coast is the most used. The Baku–Tehran bus stops here. Expect some delay; bring patience.

**Border with Armenia:** Closed. There is no crossing between Azerbaijan and Armenia.
