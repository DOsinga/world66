The Mausoleum of Huseyn Javid is a white marble monument to Azerbaijan's most celebrated playwright, who was arrested during the Stalinist purges and died in a Siberian labour camp in 1941. His remains were brought back to Nakhchivan in 1982 and the mausoleum was completed in 1993. It stands near his house museum and has become one of the defining landmarks of the city, representing both cultural pride and the memory of Soviet repression. The house museum adjacent contains manuscripts, photographs, and personal effects.
