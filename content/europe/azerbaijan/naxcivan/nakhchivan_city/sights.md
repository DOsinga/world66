### [Momine Khatum Mausoleum](/europe/azerbaijan/naxcivan/nakhchivan_city/sights/momine_khatum_maus)

The main sight in the city is the 12th century Momine Khatum Mausoleum, also known as 'Atabek Gumbezi'. Momine Khatum was the wife of Ildegizid Atabek Djakhan Pakhlevan, ruler of the the Atabek Eldegiz emirate. The 10-sided monument is decorated with intricate geometrical motives and Kufic script, it uses turquoise glazed bricks. It shares the neighbourhood with a statue of its architect - Abubakr oglu Ajami - and a bust of Heidar Aliyev. ### [Mausoleum of Hussein Javid](/europe/azerbaijan/naxcivan/nakhchivan_city/sights/mausoleum_of_husse)

More recent (1993) is the white marble mausoleum of Hussein Javid . The Azeri writer died in the Gulag under Stalin. Both the mausoleum and his house museum are located east of the theatre. Although being a recent construction, Hussein Javid's mausoleum is of great iconic importance, representing the ability of the exclave to live despite the Armenian embargo and becoming a symbol of Nakhchivan itself.
