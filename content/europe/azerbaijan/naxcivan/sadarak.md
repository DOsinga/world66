Sadarak sits near the point where [Armenia](/europe/armenia), [Turkey](/asia/turkey) and [Nakhchivan](/europe/azerbaijan/naxcivan) converge, just a few kilometres from [Iran](/asia/iran) — a crossroads that has brought the town more trouble than benefit over the centuries.

The town grew in importance thanks to a railway running north to Armenia, but that same proximity nearly destroyed it during the fighting of 1992, when it was shelled and completely evacuated before being reoccupied. These days Sadarak is quiet, sitting at the end of a rail line that goes only south.

The main draw is an ancient **Urartu fortress** on the edge of town — a remnant of the kingdom that controlled this region in the 9th to 6th centuries BC. The snow-capped summit of Mount Ararat, visible across the border in Turkey, makes for a striking backdrop.
