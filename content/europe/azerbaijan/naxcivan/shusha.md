Shusha (also written Şuşa) is a historic city in the Karabakh highlands of Azerbaijan, perched at around 1,400 metres above sea level. It is culturally significant to both Azerbaijanis and Armenians — both communities consider it central to their heritage — which made it one of the most contested prizes of the Nagorno-Karabakh conflict.

The city was controlled by Armenian forces from 1992 until November 2020, when Azerbaijani forces retook it during the Second Nagorno-Karabakh War. It has since been declared a "cultural capital" of Azerbaijan and reconstruction work is underway, though access for independent travellers remains restricted.

Shusha was historically renowned as a centre of Azerbaijani music and poetry — many prominent composers and mugham (classical music) performers were born here, earning it the title "cradle of Azerbaijani culture." The **Şuşa State Historical Reserve** encompasses numerous historic buildings, mosques, and churches from the city's 18th-century founding period.
