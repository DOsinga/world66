The Garabaghlar Mausoleum stands in the village of the same name in the Nakhchivan exclave, a medieval tower tomb dating to roughly the fourteenth century. Two flanking minarets, built somewhat later, mark what was once a larger spiritual complex from the late twelfth or early thirteenth century.

The structure follows the classic Azerbaijani tower-mausoleum form: an underground burial chamber below, a cylindrical body of semi-cylindrical stone elements above, and colourful glazed tiles covering the exterior in geometric and vegetal patterns. The entrance portal is deliberately oversized relative to its small doorway — an architectural trick that makes the building appear larger than it is.

The mausoleum sits in open countryside and is easy to reach from Nakhchivan city by car. There is no entrance fee or formal visitor infrastructure, so bring water and be prepared for a quiet, unattended site.
