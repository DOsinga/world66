A medieval tower tomb standing near Gulustan village in the Julfa district of Nakhchivan. Built in a style influenced by the celebrated Momine Khatun mausoleum in Nakhchivan city, it belongs to the tower-tomb tradition found across Azerbaijan and eastern Anatolia. The twelve-sided body is constructed of red sandstone set on a platform of dressed stone with cut corners. The exterior surfaces are decorated with recessed geometric ornaments and decorative arches, while the transition from the body to the conical roof is ringed with stalactite-shaped carved bands — a hallmark of Seljuk-era funerary architecture in the region.
