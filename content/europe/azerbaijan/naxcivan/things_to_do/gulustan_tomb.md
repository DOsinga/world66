A medieval tower tomb near Gulustan village in the Julfa district of Nakhchivan. Built under the influence of the celebrated Momine Khatun mausoleum in Nakhchivan city, the tomb has a distinctive twelve-sided body constructed from red sandstone, set on a platform of dressed stone with cut corners.

The exterior is decorated with geometric relief ornaments and decorative arches typical of Azerbaijani funerary architecture. Stalactite-shaped carved bands mark the transition from the base to the tower section. It's a quiet, little-visited site that rewards anyone with an interest in Islamic architecture making the trip out to the Julfa region.
