Brest sits at the southwestern edge of Belarus, right on the border with Poland, with the Bug river marking the frontier. It is 200km from Warsaw and 350km from Minsk, which makes it a natural entry point for travelers arriving from Poland by train or road. The city is also gateway to one of Europe's last primeval forests.

Most visitors come for the Brest Fortress, one of the Soviet Union's most resonant war memorials. When Germany launched its surprise invasion on 22 June 1941, the fortress was among the first places attacked — and held out for weeks against overwhelming force. The memorial site is sober and affecting.

Beyond the fortress, Brest rewards a short stay: a pedestrian-friendly old town, a handful of decent restaurants, and easy access to Belovezhskaya Pushcha, the ancient forest reserve 60km to the north where the last wild European bison roam.
