There are many squares in the centre of Antwerp. Here are the most important ones:

**Grote Markt**

The heart of the city beats at the Grote Markt, an irregularly-shaped square surrounded by decorative guild houses dating from the 16th and 17th century. In the centre stands the Brabo fountain, depicting the Roman soldier Silvius Brabo flinging the severed hand of the giant Antigon into the Scheldt — a legend that supposedly gave the city its name (hand-werpen, "hand-throwing").

**Groenplaats**

Just south of the cathedral, Groenplaats was built over the old cemetery and is now lined with cafe terraces. The bronze statue of Rubens in the centre reminds everyone who the city's most famous son was.

**Cogels-Osylei**

Not a square but worth the detour: a residential street in Zurenborg packed with exuberant Art Nouveau and eclectic architecture from around 1900. Every house tries to outdo its neighbour.
