Bouillon is a small town in the Belgian Ardennes, tucked into a deep loop of the Semois River. The landscape does most of the work: dense forest, steep valley walls, and a river that doubles back on itself so tightly that the old town sits on a narrow peninsula, almost entirely surrounded by water.

The town's main sight is its medieval castle, one of the best-preserved in Belgium. It occupies a dramatic ridge above the Semois, and the walk up earns you a proper view of the river bends and forested hills below. The castle is associated with Godfrey of Bouillon, the local duke who sold it to the Prince-Bishop of Liège in 1095 to finance his role in the First Crusade — and who went on to become the first ruler of the Kingdom of Jerusalem. Drawbridges, towers, and a dungeon are all intact.

Beyond the castle, Bouillon is good walking and cycling country. The Semois valley has well-marked trails through the forest, and the river is popular with kayakers in summer. It's a quiet place — a long weekend base rather than a city destination — but if you're already in the Ardennes, it's worth the detour.
