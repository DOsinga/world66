The Groeningemuseum is Bruges' essential art museum, home to a world-class collection of Flemish Primitives that you simply cannot see anywhere else in this depth. Jan van Eyck's *Madonna with Canon Joris van der Paele* and works by Hans Memling, Gerard David, and Hieronymus Bosch anchor a collection that traces painting in the Southern Low Countries from the 15th century to the present.

Just across the lane, the Gruuthusemuseum occupies a medieval merchant's palace and covers daily life in Bruges through furniture, tapestries, and silverwork. A few minutes further, the former Sint-Janshospitaal -- one of Europe's oldest surviving hospital buildings -- now houses the Memling Museum, with six intimate masterpieces by Hans Memling painted for the hospital itself.

You can visit all three in a single morning. The Groeningemuseum is small enough to absorb in an hour without feeling rushed.
