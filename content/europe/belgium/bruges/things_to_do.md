---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Bruges is small enough to walk end to end in twenty minutes, and almost everything worth seeing is within that walk. The essential sights are the **Markt** and the **Belfort**, the adjoining **Burg** with its basilica and town hall, the **Groeningemuseum** and **Sint-Janshospitaal** for Flemish Primitives, and the **Begijnhof** for a quiet breather. Beyond the greatest hits, wander the canal walks north and east of the centre — the **Sint-Anna** quarter around the Jerusalem Church and the Lace Centre is particularly pretty, and has fewer tourists. **Boat trips** on the canals (30 minutes, a few euro, departures from half a dozen landing stages) are cheesy but genuinely rewarding: the views from water level are the best in the city. For something longer, the historic **Lamme Goedzak** canal boat runs the five kilometres from Bruges to the village of **Damme** — a pleasant half-day excursion through flat polder landscape.
