Brussels' most recognisable piece of modern architecture: nine stainless-steel spheres arranged as the unit cell of an iron crystal, magnified 165 billion times, built for the 1958 World's Fair and never taken down. The top sphere holds a restaurant and panoramic lookout; the connecting tubes contain escalators and the lift up. Next door are **Mini-Europe** (scale models of European landmarks, a guilty pleasure) and the excellent **ADAM design museum**.
