---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Brussels is a beer city, and drinking here is less a night out than an ongoing study. **Delirium Café** near the Galeries Saint-Hubert holds a Guinness World Record for the most beers on sale — over 2,000 — and is mostly full of tourists; the neighbouring streets have quieter options that locals prefer. **À la Mort Subite** on Rue Montagne aux Herbes Potagères is an old-school brown cafe serving its own gueuze and kriek in an unchanged 1920s interior. **Au Bon Vieux Temps** is a tiny medieval bar down an alley off Rue du Marché aux Herbes — easy to miss, wonderful inside. For a more modern craft scene, head to Saint-Gilles or Ixelles, where bars like **Moeder Lambic** on Place Fontainas serve a rotating list of Belgian and European craft beers.

Cafes are different — Brussels has a proper French-style cafe culture, with terraces that fill up the minute the weather cooperates. **Café Belga** on Place Flagey, **La Fleur en Papier Doré** (Magritte's hangout), and the cafes around Place du Châtelain are good starting points.
