Dinant sits in a dramatic setting on the Meuse River, squeezed between limestone cliffs and the water. The town is dominated by its citadel, perched high above the distinctive onion-domed Collegiate Church of Notre-Dame, and makes a fine base for exploring the Belgian Ardennes.

South of Dinant the Meuse cuts a deep trench through the Ardennes plateau. The most spectacular stretch is at Freyr, six kilometres downstream, where sheer rock faces on one bank face a Renaissance castle and classical gardens on the other. Boat trips along the river run throughout the summer.

Dinant is also the birthplace of Adolphe Sax, inventor of the saxophone — a fact the town celebrates with saxophone-shaped benches and a small museum. The surrounding countryside offers kayaking, cycling, and walking, with caves, castles, and villages within easy reach.
