Essen is a small border town in the northern Kempen region of Antwerp province, sitting close to the Dutch border about 30 km north of [Antwerp](/europe/belgium/antwerp). The surrounding area is flat and wooded, typical of the sandy Kempen heathlands.

The town is modest and largely residential, without major tourist attractions. It is a quiet stopover point for cyclists and walkers exploring the Kempen, which has an extensive network of trails through heather, pine forest, and along the Mark river valley. Day-trippers from Antwerp occasionally visit the nature areas nearby.
