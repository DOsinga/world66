---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Belgium takes its festivals seriously.

**Carnival of Binche** (February/March) — The most famous carnival in Belgium, held in the small Walloon town of Binche in the days before Ash Wednesday. The highlight is the procession of Gilles — men in elaborate costumes and wax masks who throw oranges into the crowd. UNESCO lists it as an Intangible Cultural Heritage. Worth the trip.

**Brussels Jazz Weekend** (late May) — Three days of free open-air jazz concerts across the city. The main stages are on the Grand-Place and Place Sainte-Catherine. Relaxed and very Belgian.

**Gentse Feesten / Ghent Festivities** (late July, 10 days) — One of the largest free street festivals in Europe. The entire historic centre of [Ghent](/europe/belgium/ghent) transforms into an outdoor party with hundreds of stages, street performers, and temporary bars. Free entry everywhere; accommodation books up months in advance.

**Tomorrowland** (late July, two weekends) — The world's most famous electronic music festival, held in Boom near [Antwerp](/europe/belgium/antwerp). Enormous, expensive, and sells out within hours of tickets going on sale. A world of its own for those who go.

**Brussels Beer Weekend** (early September) — Held on the Grand-Place, this is one of the best occasions to sample a huge range of Belgian beers in one place. Breweries from across the country set up stands; entry is free.

**Procession of the Holy Blood** (Bruges, Ascension Day) — A religious procession that has taken place since the Middle Ages, carrying a relic said to contain the blood of Christ through the streets of [Bruges](/europe/belgium/bruges). A striking piece of living tradition.

**Procession of Penitents** (Veurne, last Sunday of July) — Since 1644, penitents have carried heavy wooden crosses through [Veurne](/europe/belgium/veurne) in this solemn annual procession. Austere and moving.

**Christmas Markets** (December–January) — Brussels' market on and around the Grand-Place is among the better ones in Europe. Ghent, Bruges, and Liege all run their own. The season runs from late November through early January.
