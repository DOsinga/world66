---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The historic centre is compact and walkable. The two medieval **Broeltorens** on the Leie are the most recognisable image of the city — twin defensive towers from the 12th and 15th centuries, still standing where the old city wall met the river. From there it is a short walk to the **Onze-Lieve-Vrouwekerk**, a Gothic church containing one of Van Dyck's finest paintings, and on to the peaceful **Begijnhof**, a walled béguinage that has been inhabited almost continuously since the 13th century. The **Grote Markt** is the lively heart of the city, lined with guild houses and dominated by the belfry.
