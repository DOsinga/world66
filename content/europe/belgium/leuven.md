Leuven is Belgium's ultimate university town. With more than 25,000 students at the 500-year-old KU Leuven — one of Europe's oldest universities — the city has an energy that belies its modest size. The campus is woven into the fabric of the town itself: medieval colleges stand alongside research labs, and the student population keeps the bars, restaurants, and cultural scene thriving year-round.

The Oude Markt (Old Square) is the social heart of the city, ringed by over 60 cafes and bars — locals call it the longest bar in the world, and on a warm evening it certainly feels like it. The Grote Markt is the architectural centrepiece, dominated by the extraordinarily ornate 15th-century Stadhuis (town hall) and the imposing Sint-Pieterskerk (Church of St Peter), which houses Dirk Bouts' masterpiece *The Last Supper*.

Beyond the centre, the Groot Begijnhof is one of the most beautiful beguinages in Flanders — a traffic-free enclave of cobbled streets and brick houses along the river Dijle, now used as university housing. The Abdij van Park in Heverlee, just south of town, is a magnificent abbey complex with a baroque church and landscaped grounds. And on Ladeuzeplein, the university library building stands as a moving symbol of resilience, rebuilt twice after destruction in both World Wars.
