The stately university library on Ladeuzeplein is one of Leuven's most striking buildings and a powerful symbol of the city's resilience. The original medieval library was destroyed by German troops in 1914; the replacement, funded by international donations and designed by the American architect Whitney Warren, was burned again in 1940. The current building is a faithful reconstruction of Warren's design, with its distinctive tower and Renaissance Revival facade. Inside, it holds a vast collection rebuilt through donations from universities worldwide.
