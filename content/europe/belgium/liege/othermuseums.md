Liège has several other museums worth seeking out beyond the big-name attractions:

- **The Glass Museum** (Musée du Verre) — Quai de Maastricht 16. A small collection tracing glassmaking from antiquity to Art Nouveau, housed in a handsome riverside building.
- **Museum of Prehistoric Archaeology** — Place du Vingt Août 7. Part of the university, with stone-age finds from the Meuse valley.
- **La Boverie** (Museum of Modern Art) — Parc de la Boverie 3. Reopened in 2016 after a Rudy Ricciotti renovation, it hosts a decent permanent collection and travelling shows from major international museums.
- **Museum of Walloon Life** — Cours des Mineurs. Folklore, crafts, and daily life in Wallonia, set in a former Franciscan friary.
- **Tchantchès Museum** — Rue Surlet 56. Dedicated to the beloved Liégeois puppet character, with a collection of marionettes and regular puppet shows.
