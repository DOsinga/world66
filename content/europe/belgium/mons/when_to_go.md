---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Mons is pleasant year-round, but the highlight of the calendar is the Ducasse de Mons (also known as the Doudou), held on Trinity Sunday (late May or early June). The festival culminates in two events: the Procession of the Car d'Or, in which a golden carriage carries the relics of Saint Waudru through the streets, and the Lumeçon, a theatrical battle between Saint George and the dragon on the Grand-Place. The whole city turns out, and the atmosphere is electric. The festival has been recognised by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage.
