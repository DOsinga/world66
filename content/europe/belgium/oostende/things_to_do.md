title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Ostend has more going on than the beach. The pier, the parks, the fish market, and a handful of museums give you plenty of reason to stay beyond a sunny afternoon.

## Sights

### Europa Centre

At 100 metres, this is the tallest building on the European coast. The café on the 34th floor offers a panoramic view over Bruges and the Flemish interior on clear days — an ultra-fast lift gets you there in seconds. Check current opening hours before visiting.

### Mercator Yacht Harbour

The three-masted sailing ship *Mercator* is moored here among more than 300 yachts. A pleasant walk for anyone who likes boats.

### The Pier

A walk along the pier takes you out above the sea itself. There is a café at the far end where you can sit with a drink and watch the shipping traffic.

### King Albert I Promenade

The traffic-free promenade along the seafront is the town's main walkway. Tearooms with terraces, a sandy beach, and in summer a designated area called the Lido with beach sports and children's activities. The clock tower at Kemmelbergstraat is a classic meeting point.

### Fish Market

Just outside the centre, the fishing fleet holds its auctions here. Worth visiting early in the morning when the boats come in.

### Fishermen's Quay — De Trap

The quay is at its best in the morning when the shrimping boats return. Fresh shrimps and fish are sold from typical stands along the water. A good place to hear the Ostend dialect.

### The Little Beach

A small beach between the Zeeliedenplein and the harbour mouth. The square holds a 1953 monument by Willy Kreits dedicated to seamen lost at sea; fishermen and naval veterans gather here each year to pay their respects.

### Napoleon Fortress

Built from 1810 to 1814 on Napoleon's orders, the fortress was later used by the Germans as a command post in World War II. Now restored, it hosts exhibitions, a restaurant, and occasional musical performances. Near the 'Dune and Sea' buildings.

### Leopold Park

Five hectares of park in the centre of town, laid out in 1870. The famous flower clock is at the edge; a 19th-century bandstand stands in the middle.

### Maria Hendrika Park

Known locally as 't Bosje (the wood), this 45-hectare park at the edge of town is traffic-free. Footpaths, jogging circuits, fishponds, rowing boats, pedal boats, a playground, and a miniature golf course. You can also rent bikes here for free.

### Wellington Racecourse

Named after the Duke of Wellington. Trotting and gallop races take place daily in summer; the annual Night of the Horse is the highlight of the season.

### Stene

A picturesque village at the edge of Ostend with several good restaurants. Known for its windmill, annual horse-blessing, and Christmas market.

## Museums

### Museum of Local History — De Plate

Covers Ostend from Neolithic and Roman times through to the Second World War. Strong sections on the Belle Époque, maritime history, and both world wars, with photographs, posters, and artefacts. Currently in the Feest- en Kultuurpaleis on the main market square; scheduled to move to the Langestraat.
