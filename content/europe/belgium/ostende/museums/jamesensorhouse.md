The restored home and studio of James Ensor, one of Belgium's most important painters, whose grotesque carnival masks and skeletons influenced the Expressionist movement. The ground floor recreates the curiosity shop run by his aunt and uncle, filled with shells, chinoiserie, and seaside souvenirs that found their way into Ensor's paintings. Upstairs you'll find his sitting room and studio, largely as he left them, along with a documentation room covering his life and work. It's a small museum but an atmospheric one, especially if you've seen his paintings at the nearby Mu.ZEE.
