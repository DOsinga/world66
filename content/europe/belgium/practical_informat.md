## Money

Belgium uses the euro (€). ATMs are widespread in cities and at airports and train stations. Cards are accepted almost everywhere, including contactless. In rural areas and at markets, carry some cash.

## Language

Belgium has three official languages: Dutch (Flemish) is spoken in Flanders (north), French in Wallonia (south), and German in a small area near the eastern border. Brussels is officially bilingual (Dutch and French). English is widely spoken, especially in Flanders and among younger people throughout the country. Don't assume a French speaker will cheerfully switch to Dutch or vice versa — the language divide is real and occasionally sensitive.

## Visa and Entry

Citizens of the EU, US, UK, Canada, and Australia can enter Belgium without a visa for stays up to 90 days. Belgium is part of the Schengen Area. Non-EU visitors arriving from outside the Schengen zone should be aware of the EU's Entry/Exit System (EES), which began operations in late 2025 and registers biometric data at border crossings.

Your passport should be valid for at least three months beyond your planned stay.

## Electricity

230V, 50Hz. Belgium uses type E plugs (two round pins plus a grounding pin). UK and US travellers need adaptors.

## Phone and Internet

Mobile coverage is excellent throughout the country. Wi-Fi is available in hotels, cafes, and most public spaces. EU residents pay no roaming charges; non-EU visitors should check rates.

## Emergency Numbers

- General emergency: 112
- Police: 101
- Fire and ambulance: 100

## Tipping

Service is often included in restaurant bills. Rounding up or leaving a few euros is appreciated but not obligatory. Tipping in bars is not expected.

## Tourist Information

The Belgian tourist offices operate separately by region: Visit Brussels (www.visit.brussels), Visit Flanders (www.visitflanders.com), and Visit Wallonia (www.belgique-tourisme.be).
