Sint-Niklaas is the main city of the Waasland region in East Flanders, lying roughly halfway between Ghent and Antwerp. The city's central market square — one of the largest in Belgium — dominates the town centre and is a lively focus for market days and events. The area is known for its textile and crafts heritage, and the local museum holds a fine collection of objects relating to Mercator, the Flemish cartographer, who was born in the Waasland region.

Sint-Niklaas is a pleasant, unhurried Flemish city that rewards a half-day visit, particularly if you are traveling between Ghent and Antwerp.
