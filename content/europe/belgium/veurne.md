Veurne is a small, handsome town in the Westhoek, the far western corner of [Belgium](/europe/belgium), about 6km from the coast and close to the French border. The Grote Markt is one of the finest town squares in Flanders, lined with step-gabled buildings and the ornate Spanish Pavilion.

The town is best known for its annual Procession of Penitence, held on the last Sunday of July since 1644 — a solemn and striking event in which hooded penitents carry crosses through the streets.

The surrealist painter Paul Delvaux (1897–1994) lived in Veurne. The surrounding polders landscape is flat and quiet, with cycling paths running along the old canal dykes.
