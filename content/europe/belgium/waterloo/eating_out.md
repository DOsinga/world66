---
title: "Eating Out in Waterloo"
type: section
---

Waterloo, Belgium the city made famous by Napoleon's great defeat, is to heaven for any history buff. Travelers will have an enjoyable
time with all the city has to offer, including some great eateries.

**Ferme du Hameau du Roy**

A wonderful place to start for breakfast or brunch is one of the
town’s many bakeries or cafes. One in particular, Ferme du Hameau du
Roy, is casual and has a lovely patio and cozy fire to sit by and
enjoy your meal. Treat yourself to a freshly brewed espresso and a
buttery croissant. This café is located at
the battlefield so don’t forget some freshly baked bread to nibble on
later as you enjoy this historical area.

**Famous Waffle Stands**

If your morning is hurried, try one of the city’s waffle stands.
They have the famous “gaufres” (what Americans call Belgian waffles)
but they are so much tastier than the American version. Gaufres are not
as thick and fluffy as their U.S. counterparts and have a denser
texture and a tangier flavor. These waffles are delicious on their own
or the stands offer a variety of fruit and toppings to accompany them.
Waffles from these stands are wrapped and should be eaten while on the
go.

**“Frieries” **

When in Waterloo, it is essential to stop at a local “friterie” that
serves frites (thin crispy fries), hamburgers, meatballs, chicken and
more. Friteries are the Belgian equivalent to American fast food,
except better. They are known for their variety of sauces and for a
gastronomic delight called a “mitraillette”. The translation means
“submachine gun” and it consists of meat, sauce, frites, possibly salad
and your choice of sauce atop a freshly baked baguette. That will
sustain anyone through a day of sightseeing.
