The 520-metre hill topped by a 14-metre concrete cross, raised in 1934 by villagers in memory of Christ's 1,900th anniversary of death. The climb takes about two hours along a rough stony path lined with fourteen bronze stations of the cross. The view across the plain is worth the effort; the pilgrimage is what most come for.
