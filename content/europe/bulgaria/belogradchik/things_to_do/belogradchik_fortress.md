---
title: "Belogradchik Fortress"
type: poi
category: Sight
latitude: 43.6268
longitude: 22.6833
---

One of the best-preserved medieval fortresses in Bulgaria, built directly into the rock faces that surround it. The Romans established the first fortifications here in the 3rd century AD; Bulgarian rulers and later the Ottomans expanded it over the following centuries. The walls and towers integrate so seamlessly with the sandstone cliffs that it is sometimes hard to see where the rock ends and the masonry begins. Walkways through the fortress offer elevated views over the rock formations and the town below.
