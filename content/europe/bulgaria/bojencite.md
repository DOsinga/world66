Bojentsi is a small village on the northern slopes of the Stara Planina mountains, 16 km east of [Gabrovo](/europe/bulgaria/gabrovo) and 40 km from [Veliko Tarnovo](/europe/bulgaria/velikoturnovo). At 750 metres altitude, tucked into the valley of the Bojanka River, the entire village has been declared an architectural and historical reserve.

According to legend, Bojentsi was founded by a noblewoman named Bojana who fled into the mountains with her nine sons after the Ottoman conquest of Veliko Tarnovo in 1393. The village grew through trade and crafts during the Bulgarian Renaissance, and the houses from that period — small stone and timber structures with characteristic overhanging upper stories — survive remarkably intact along both banks of the river.

Today Bojentsi is known as the birthplace of a disproportionate number of Bulgarian writers, artists, architects and scientists. The village is very quiet, with a handful of guesthouses in restored Renaissance-era buildings. It makes a good base for walking in the surrounding hills, or a half-day visit combined with Gabrovo's humour museum.
