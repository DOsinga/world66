Bruce McDonald and his wife Stormy served as Peace Corps volunteers in Bulgaria and came away with a deep affection for the country. Their memoir is a ground-level account of rural Bulgarian life — warm portraits of people who take pride in their history, sharp observations about post-Soviet transition, and the kind of detail you only get from actually living somewhere rather than passing through. A useful read before spending time outside the main cities.
