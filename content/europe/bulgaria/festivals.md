**Rose Festival, Kazanlak — late May/early June**

The Valley of the Roses produces much of the world's rose oil, and during the brief bloom season (roughly three weeks from late May), the region around Kazanlak celebrates with rose-picking ceremonies, folklore performances, parades, and music. The festival is genuinely rooted in local tradition, not a tourist invention. Arrive early in the morning to see the actual picking before the heat of the day causes the flowers to close.

**Kukeri — late January to early March**

The *kukeri* tradition involves men dressed in elaborate costumes of bells and animal skins, performing ritual dances to drive away evil spirits and bring fertility for the coming year. Different regions have different versions. The village of Pernik holds the Surva International Festival of Masquerade Games in late January — the largest kukeri gathering in the country, with participants from across Bulgaria and the Balkans. Strange, loud, and genuinely old.

**Sofia Film Festival — March**

Bulgaria's main film festival, running for over 25 years, screens around 150 films from world cinema with a focus on European and Balkan productions. Good programme, serious audience, reasonable ticket prices.

**International Folk Festival, Koprivshtitsa — every five years (next: 2025)**

Held in August in the beautifully preserved National Revival village of [Koprivshtitsa](/europe/bulgaria/koprivshtitsa), this is the biggest traditional folk music and dance gathering in the country. Groups come from all regions of Bulgaria, performing music and dance traditions specific to their areas. One of the most authentic folk festivals in the Balkans — the next edition should be confirmed for 2025.

**Apollonia Arts Festival, Sozopol — early September**

Named after the ancient Greek colony, the Apollonia festival brings together Bulgarian and international theatre, music, and visual arts in [Sozopol](/europe/bulgaria/sozopol) each September. The timing — after the summer crowds have thinned — makes it a particularly pleasant visit.

**Plovdiv Night of Museums and Galleries — October**

Plovdiv's cultural institutions open for free through the night in October, with special exhibitions, performances, and events. Part of the broader European Night of Museums initiative, but Plovdiv's dense concentration of galleries and historic houses makes it one of the better versions.
