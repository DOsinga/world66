story: >
  The frescoes at Ivanovo were painted in the 13th and 14th centuries by artists of the Turnovo School — the most sophisticated school of Byzantine painting in medieval Bulgaria. When the Ottoman conquest came in 1396, the monks hid and the monastery was forgotten. It wasn't rediscovered by scholars until the 19th century, which is partly why the paintings survived in such good condition.
---

About 20 km south of Ruse, in a dramatic limestone gorge carved by the Rusenski Lom river, the Ivanovo Rock Monastery is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Churches, chapels, and cells were carved directly into the cliff face in the 13th century, and the surviving frescoes — in deep golds, reds, and blues — are exceptional. The access path descends steeply from the road and crosses the river; the setting is as striking as the paintings. A local bus runs from Ruse bus station to the village of Ivanovo, from where it's a 20-minute walk to the monastery.
