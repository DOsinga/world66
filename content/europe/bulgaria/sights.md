title: "Sights"

---

Bulgaria punches well above its weight for historical and natural sights. The country has nine UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

The Rila Monastery is the one that most travelers come for, and rightly so. Founded in the 10th century by the hermit St. Ivan Rilski, it sits in a spectacular valley in the Rila Mountains south of Sofia. The frescoed portico is extraordinary — vivid scenes covering every surface. It's an active monastery and a national symbol, but not overrun in the way that comparable sites in Western Europe would be. See [Rila Monastery](/europe/bulgaria/rilamonastery).

[Plovdiv's](/europe/bulgaria/plovdiv) old town is Bulgaria's finest collection of National Revival architecture — 19th-century merchant houses with overhanging upper floors, painted in deep blues, yellows, and greens, perched on three hills above a Roman theatre that still hosts concerts. Plovdiv is reputedly the oldest continuously inhabited city in Europe, and the layering of Thracian, Roman, Byzantine, Ottoman, and Bulgarian history is visible everywhere.

[Veliko Turnovo](/europe/bulgaria/velikoturnovo) was the capital of the Second Bulgarian Empire (12th–14th centuries). The Tsarevets fortress sits on a dramatic horseshoe bend in the Yantra River; the setting is as good as the history. The town itself is a lively student city.

The Belogradchik Rocks, in northwest Bulgaria, are a strange and beautiful landscape of red sandstone formations rising from the hills — some reaching 200 metres. The medieval fortress built into the rocks is well preserved and the area is rarely crowded.

Nesebar, on the Black Sea coast, is a UNESCO site — a small peninsula packed with Byzantine and medieval churches. Unfortunately it has been badly over-touristed and is now largely souvenir shops; it's worth a couple of hours but go early in the morning. [Sozopol](/europe/bulgaria/sozopol), a few kilometres south of Burgas, is the more charming Black Sea old town.

The Madara Rider, near Shumen, is one of the oldest and most unusual monuments in Europe — a 23-metre-high rock relief carved around 710 AD, depicting a mounted warrior triumphing over a lion, with Greek inscriptions recording the history of the First Bulgarian Empire. UNESCO World Heritage Site.

For nature, the Pirin and Rila mountain ranges offer serious hiking at relatively low cost. The Rila Lakes — seven glacial lakes at altitude between 2,100 and 2,500 metres — can be reached by gondola from Panichishte and make for an excellent day hike.
