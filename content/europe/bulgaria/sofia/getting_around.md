title: "Getting Around in Sofia"
type: section
---

Over a million people reside in Bulgaria&rsquo;s capital, the ancient-yet-increasingly-modern city of Sofia, and there are many means of **Sofia transportation**. The city, over 7,000 years old and located in western Bulgaria, provides something for every kind of traveler. Whether you&rsquo;re in the mood to admire the city&rsquo;s ancient architecture, shop till you drop, or listen to live music and dance all night long, you&rsquo;ve got lots of options when it comes to getting around.

**On Foot**

Sofia is a fairly pedestrian-friendly place with a compact city center and countless attractions within walking distance. Be a bit cautious when crossing the streets, though – drivers won&rsquo;t automatically stop for pedestrians. And, if you venture outside of the city center, be aware that some of the walkways are uneven, rough, and have treacherous potholes.

**By Taxi**

To avoid those uneven walkways, grab a cab! Taxis in Sofia are inexpensive and not difficult to find. Check the fare sticker that you&rsquo;ll find in the taxi as the prices will vary a bit, depending on whether you hail the taxi from a taxi stand, the street or order one by phone. And, make sure to choose a taxi that has been officially licensed. You&rsquo;ll be able to tell it&rsquo;s a legal vehicle because it will be yellow with a &lsquo;taxi&rsquo; sign on the roof.

**Public Transportation**

Sofia offers a fairly reliable and recently renovated public transportation system. Choose from buses, trams, trolleys or the subway. Public transportation is available for a large part of the day, and it is not terribly expensive.

And, of course, you can always rent a car in Sofia to get around. But, expect traffic jams and some pretty aggressive driving.
