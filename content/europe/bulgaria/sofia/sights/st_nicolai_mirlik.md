The Tsurkva Sveta Nikolai - "St Nicolai Mirlikiiski Russian Church", considered by many to be the prettiest church in Sofia. 'I had to agree.' We were only given a quick glimpse inside.

We were told by our guide that the church was built by Russian workmen in 1912 and dedicated to St Nicholas, 'the miracle worker'. The exterior has bright yellow tiles and the domes are covered with emerald green majolica tiles, crowned with five gold-plated "onion" domes which contrast greatly with the churches dim interior. The exterior has recently been renovated by the Moscow Patriarchate, who provided the gold for the domes.
.
.
The crypt, accessed to the left of the main entrance is more popular than the church. It contains the tomb of Serafin a former Bishop who died in 1950. Although never canonised, he is revered as a saint. Locals come here to post hand-written messages into a box to the right of the tomb believing that he can make their prayers and wishes come true.
.
.

Services Sat. 0900 and 1700, Sun. 0900.
Admission is free.
