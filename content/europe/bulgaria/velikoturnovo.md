Veliko Turnovo (also spelled Tarnovo or Trnovo) was the capital of the Second Bulgarian Empire from the 12th to 14th centuries, and it still feels like it. The town is built on steep slopes above a horseshoe bend in the Yantra River, with old houses cascading down the hillsides and the Tsarevets fortress rising on a rocky promontory at the centre of it all.

Tsarevets is the main reason to come: the ruins of the medieval royal palace and the Patriarchal Cathedral, with reconstructed walls and towers, cover the entire hilltop. The views over the river gorge and the surrounding town are excellent. In summer, a sound-and-light show is projected onto the fortress walls in the evenings — touristy, but genuinely dramatic.

The old merchants' quarter of Samovodska Charshia has been partially restored, with craft workshops selling pottery, woodcarving, and leatherwork. The Archaeological Museum holds finds from the medieval capital period. The Church of SS Peter and Paul, across the river, has some original 14th-century frescoes.

Veliko Turnovo is a university town, which keeps it lively out of season. There are plenty of cafes, bars, and restaurants along the main pedestrian street and in the old town. It's a good base for the surrounding region — the nearby villages of Arbanasi (with 17th-century churches) and the monastery circuits around the Yantra valley are worth a day or two.

About 240 km east of Sofia by road; 4–5 hours by bus or train.
