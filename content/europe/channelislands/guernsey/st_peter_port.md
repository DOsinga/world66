St Peter Port is one of the most handsome harbour towns in the British Isles. The capital of Guernsey climbs steeply from a working waterfront lined with yachts and ferry terminals, its lanes of granite townhouses rising toward Castle Cornet, a medieval fortress that has guarded the harbour since the 13th century. Stand on the quayside on a clear day and the French coast is visible on the horizon.

The town has real character — Norman street names, a French-inflected café culture, and pubs that feel genuinely local rather than tourist-facing. Victor Hugo spent fourteen years in exile here, and his house, Hauteville House, is the town's most distinctive literary landmark, decorated in an eccentric style entirely his own.

The covered market in the town centre is worth a visit for local produce: Guernsey butter, fresh crab, and the island's rich dairy products. For something livelier, the July Town Festival brings the waterfront to life with music and street food. Ferries to [Herm](/europe/channelislands/herm) and [Sark](/europe/channelislands/sark) depart from the harbour, making St Peter Port a natural base for exploring the smaller islands.
