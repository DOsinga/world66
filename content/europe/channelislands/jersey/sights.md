**Sights**

**Elizabeth Castle and The Hermitage**

Built on a rocky islet in the 1590's, the castle was named after Queen Elizabeth I by Sir Walter Raleigh whilst he was Governor of Jersey. There are exhibitions explaining the role of the castle in Jersey's history. The Hermitage, a small chapel has been a holy place since the 12th century and is [more..](/europe/channelislands/jersey/sights/elizabethcastleandthehermitage)

address: St Aubin's Bay, St Helier.

tel: 723971

**La Hougue Bie**

Described as "one of Europe's finest prehistoric monuments". It is dominated by the massive burial mound dating from 3800 BC. There is also a Neolithic passage grave and two Medieval chapels, archaeology and geology galleries and an Inter-active discovery centre.  

address: Grouville.

tel: 853823

**Le Moulin de Quetivel**

This historic watermill has been carefully restored to working order by the National Trust for Jersey.  

address: Mont Fallu, St Peter.

tel: 483193

**Mont Orgueil Castle**

A medieval stronghold which dominates the east coast and protected the Islanders from the time of King John in the early 13th century. The tableaux give a vivid reminder of the castle's rich history.  

address: Gorey.

tel: 853292

**Faldouet Dolmen**

Near Gouray Church, this is a passage grave around 4,500 years old. The large capstone is estimated to weigh 24 tonnes.  

address: Gorey.
