Baška is the best-known resort on the island of Krk, at the island's southern end, set in a sheltered valley surrounded by vineyards and olive groves with a dramatic backdrop of karst mountains and the islet of Prvić in the bay. Its beach — the **Vela Plaža** — is 1,800 metres of pebble, one of the longest and most celebrated on the Adriatic, with Blue Flag status since 1999.

The town has genuine historical significance. The **Baška Tablet** (Bašćanska ploča), found in the local church of St Lucy in 1851, is one of the oldest and most important examples of the Glagolitic script — a pre-Cyrillic alphabet used by the early Slavic church — and dates from around 1100 AD. The original is in the Academy of Arts and Sciences in Zagreb, but a replica stands in the church.

Baška is quieter than the more developed resorts on the northern coast of Krk, and the surrounding hills offer good walking with views over the sea. Getting there: ferries and buses connect Baška to Krk town and the mainland.
