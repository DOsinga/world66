The Brioni Islands (Brijuni in Croatian) are a small archipelago of two larger and eleven smaller islands off the Istrian coast, a short ferry ride from Fažana. The islands were declared a national park in 1983 and are one of the more unusual destinations in Croatia — partly for the extraordinary wildlife, partly for the history.

Josip Broz Tito used the main island as his personal retreat for decades, receiving foreign heads of state here and keeping an exotic animal collection (the giraffes and elephants were gifts from visiting dignitaries). The Safari Park is still open, with llamas, deer, and Tibetan yaks roaming freely alongside the deer. A small museum documents the Tito era, with the original Blue Train railway carriage, vintage cars, and gifts from Nehru, Nasser, and others.

The island landscape is beautifully preserved — no private cars, no new buildings, horse-drawn carriages instead of vehicles. The archaeological sites include Roman villas dating to the 1st century BC. Access is by organized ferry from Fažana with tour packages, though independent entry to the national park is also possible.
