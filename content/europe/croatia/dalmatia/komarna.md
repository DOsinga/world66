Komarna is a small village on the mainland coast of Dalmatia, in the Dubrovnik-Neretva region, directly opposite the Pelješac peninsula across the narrow Bay of Mali Ston. It sits on a hillside above a small harbour, looking across to the oyster beds that made Mali Ston and Ston famous throughout Croatia.

The village is quiet — a handful of houses, a harbour, and not much else — but its position makes it a useful base. The **Pelješac Bridge**, completed in 2022, connects the mainland here to the Pelješac peninsula, finally giving Croatia continuous road access to Dubrovnik without passing through Bosnia-Herzegovina.

From Komarna, the oyster towns of **Ston** and **Mali Ston** are minutes away across the water by boat or a short drive via the bridge. The walled town of Ston has the longest defensive walls in Europe after the Great Wall of China. [Mostar](/europe/bosniaandherzegovina/mostar) and Medjugorje in Bosnia are accessible via the nearby border crossing for those combining Croatia with a Bosnian excursion.
