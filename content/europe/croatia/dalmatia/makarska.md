Makarska is the main town of the Makarska Riviera, on the Dalmatian coast about 60 kilometres southeast of [Split](/europe/croatia/split). The town sits at the foot of the Biokovo massif — a dramatic limestone range that rises sharply from the sea — giving it one of the most striking backdrops on the Croatian coast. The old town is centred on a Baroque square near the harbour.

The Makarska Riviera extends roughly 50 kilometres, with a string of pebble and shingle beaches sheltered by the mountains. The Biokovo Nature Park above the town has hiking trails and a skywalk with views across to the islands of [Hvar](/europe/croatia/hvar) and Brac. Makarska is a lively resort town in summer, popular with both domestic and international visitors, with a good waterfront promenade, open-air bars, and seafood restaurants along the Riva.
