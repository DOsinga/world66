---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Dalmatia's main draw is its combination of historic walled towns, islands, and clear Adriatic water. Split is the regional centre, built directly around Diocletian's Palace. Dubrovnik to the south has the finest intact city walls in the region. Between them, the Makarska Riviera offers some of the best beaches on the mainland coast, backed by the limestone Biokovo mountains. The islands — Hvar, Brač, Korčula, Vis — are reached by regular ferries. Krka National Park, inland, gives access to a spectacular series of travertine waterfalls on the Krka river.
