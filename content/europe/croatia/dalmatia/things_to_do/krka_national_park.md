Krka National Park protects a stretch of the Krka river in central Dalmatia, designated in 1985. The river descends through a series of travertine waterfalls, the most visited being Skradinski Buk near the town of Skradin. A boardwalk circuit around the falls passes through wetland and scrub forest; swimming in the pools below the falls is permitted at certain points. Further upstream, the island monastery of Visovac sits in the middle of the river. The park is a feasible day trip from Split or Šibenik.
