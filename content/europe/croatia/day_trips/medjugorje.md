Medjugorje is a small town in Bosnia and Herzegovina, about two and a half hours from Dubrovnik, that became one of the world's most visited Catholic pilgrimage sites after six local teenagers reported apparitions of the Virgin Mary in 1981. The Vatican has never formally confirmed the apparitions, but more than 30 million pilgrims have visited since.

The main draw is Apparition Hill (Podbrdo), where the first visions were reported, and Cross Mountain (Križevac), topped by a concrete cross built in 1934. St. James Church in the town centre holds daily masses in multiple languages. The atmosphere is intense — expect rosary vendors, prayer groups, and a town economy entirely built around the pilgrimage trade.

It is technically in Bosnia, so check border-crossing requirements if you are coming from Croatia. Most visitors from the Dalmatian coast go as a day trip from Dubrovnik or Split, often combined with a stop in Mostar.
