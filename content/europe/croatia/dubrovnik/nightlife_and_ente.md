There are a lot of little bars and cafes scattered around the old town. Try to stay away from those places that are located on the main strips in the old town, their drinks are very expensive. Venture out in the side streets there are a few places I found on my trip to be great places where you can have a good time, enjoy good music, friendly atmosfere, and good drinks without gettin ripped off. Here are a couple of them: Fresh*, Caffe Bar Rio, Galleria, Trokadero.

Discos are usually located out of the city gates and if you are over the age of 18 I wouldn't reccommend even going. It is overly crowded filled with teenagers, you can cut the smoke with a knife and the music is so loud your ears ring for a week.

### [Katie O'Connor's Irish Pub](/europe/croatia/dubrovnik/nightlife_and_ente/katie_oconnors_i)

This fantastic irish pub is set in a stone building and the staff and owners are so friendly and helpful.A word of advice: get there early, as from 10pm the place gets so busy till the early hours.

### [Fresh*](/europe/croatia/dubrovnik/nightlife_and_ente/fresh)

We spent 3 nights in Dubrovnik but only found Fresh on our last night. It was a great place to end our trip and we are sorry that we didn't discover it earlier. It's a new cocktail bar with an awesome vibe and great music. The staff were very friendly and the atmosphere is different than anywhere else we'd been. If you are looking for somewhere to drink gigantic beer and cocktail towers, and meet other travelers, you won't be disappointed.

It's a bit hard to find but worth it. Located just off Stradun (the main street) between Netcafe and OTP bank.

### [Becketts Irish Bar](/europe/croatia/dubrovnik/nightlife_and_ente/becketts_irish_bar)

This lovely little gem is located 10 mins outside the old town of Dubrovnik, and is a great place to escape from the hustle and bustle of the town and enjoy a pint. They have a large terrace and you can sit under a 100 year old olive tree and watch the world go by. And as they are outside the walled town they stay open much later. I watched the english FA cup final here and the atmosphere was electric. A good mix of tourists and locals, and the Irish owners were there to ensure good craic was had by all. Smashing pint of Guinness too.
