Held every July in the town of Omiš — a dramatic spot where the Cetina River cuts through a canyon to meet the sea — this festival has been the centrepiece of klapa music since 1966. Klapa is a form of a cappella singing indigenous to Dalmatia, typically performed by a group of four to a dozen male voices (first tenor, second tenor, baritone, bass), with roots in medieval church music. In 2012 it was inscribed on the UNESCO list of Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

The festival runs across several weeks in July, with performances by dozens of groups from Croatia and the diaspora. The setting amplifies everything: warm nights, the river gorge, voices carrying across the old town square. Even if you've never heard klapa before, it gets under your skin.
