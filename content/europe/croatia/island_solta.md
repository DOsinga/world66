Šolta is a mid-sized Dalmatian island, 19 km long and about 5 km wide, easily reached by ferry from [Split](/europe/croatia/split). It is the thirteenth largest of Croatia's islands but far less developed than its neighbours, which is precisely its appeal. Summer temperatures regularly exceed 30°C and the sea reaches a comfortable 21–22°C.

The island's history runs deep. Prehistoric hillforts, Roman villa remains, early Christian basilicas, and Venetian-era sarcophagi are scattered across its villages — **Grohote**, **Nečujam**, **Gornje Selo**, and **Maslinica**, the last of which has an eighteenth-century castle built by the Marchi family in 1706. According to legend, the Illyrian queen Teuta had a fortress on the hill above the bay of Senj, and fishermen would throw part of their catch to the four winds to earn her favour.

Šolta has served as a film set too — parts of the television series *Velo Misto* were filmed in its streets and courtyards, and the movie *The Fall of Italy* was shot here entirely. The island's quiet pace, clear water, and olive groves make it a fine escape from the bustle of Split.
