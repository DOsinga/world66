The village of Grohote, Šolta's main settlement, holds some of the island's most significant early Christian remains. In and around the parish church you can find covers of ancient sarcophagi, and in the surrounding fields the ruins of a 5th- or 6th-century basilica survive along with its baptismal font. The chapel embedded in a village wall is easy to miss but worth finding. These are not managed tourist sites — they sit in the ordinary flow of village life, which makes them all the more striking.
