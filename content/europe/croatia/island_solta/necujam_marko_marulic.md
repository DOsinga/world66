The sheltered bay of Nečujam on Šolta's north coast was a favourite retreat of Marko Marulić, the 15th-century Split humanist and poet considered the father of Croatian literature. His memorial house here is a modest pilgrimage for anyone interested in Dalmatian cultural history. The bay itself — calm, wooded, well-protected — explains why he kept coming back.
