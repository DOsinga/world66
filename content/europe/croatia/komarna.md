Komarna is a tiny village on the [Croatian](/europe/croatia) Adriatic coast, 70 km north of [Dubrovnik](/europe/croatia/dubrovnik), overlooking the Mali Ston Bay and the Pelješac peninsula. It is the kind of place where the entire infrastructure consists of a mini market, a bar, a pizzeria, and two restaurants — and that is the appeal.

There is one small hotel; otherwise, accommodation is in private houses, many of them for rent. The tourist resort of Klek is 3 km away, and Neum — the only coastal town in [Bosnia and Herzegovina](/europe/bosniaherzegovina) — is 8 km to the south with supermarkets and fish markets. The nearest larger town is Metković, 25 km north.

Komarna's real value is as a quiet, affordable base for exploring the coast. [Dubrovnik](/europe/croatia/dubrovnik) is an hour's drive south. Ston, with its 5.5 km of medieval walls and famous oyster beds, is 30 minutes away. And Mostar, with its UNESCO-listed Old Bridge, is 65 km inland.
