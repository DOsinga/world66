Kornati National Park is an archipelago of 89 islands, islets, and reefs in the central Adriatic, about 15 nautical miles west of Šibenik. The park covers around 220 square kilometres and has 238 kilometres of coastline — the most indented group of islands in the Mediterranean.

The islands are largely bare and uninhabited, grazed by sheep in summer, with dramatic limestone cliffs dropping straight into the sea on their seaward sides. These cliffs, some reaching 80 metres above the waterline and plunging another 100 metres below, are known locally as "crowns" (krune) — possibly the origin of the name Kornati. The underwater walls make this one of the best diving destinations in the Adriatic.

The islands are accessible only by boat. Organised day trips and sailing excursions run from [Zadar](/europe/croatia/zadar), Šibenik, and the island of Murter, which is the closest inhabited island to the park. There are no permanent residents in the national park area, but several small restaurants (known as konobe) operate on the islands in summer, serving grilled fish and lamb.
