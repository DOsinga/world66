Kraljevica is a small coastal town on the Kvarner Gulf, just south of [Rijeka](/europe/croatia/rijeka) where the mainland meets the bridge to the island of Krk. Two Frankopan castles — one from the early 17th century, the other older and partially ruined — sit above the harbor and are the town's most visible landmarks. The Frankopan dynasty once controlled much of this coastline, and Kraljevica was where they built their ships.

That naval tradition carried forward: the town's shipyard, founded in 1729, is one of the oldest in Europe and still operates today. The waterfront is modest and pleasant, with a pebble beach, a handful of restaurants, and views across the bay toward Rijeka's industrial port. Kraljevica is not a resort destination in the way that nearby [Opatija](/europe/croatia/opatija) is, but it makes a reasonable base if you want to be close to Rijeka without staying in the city itself.
