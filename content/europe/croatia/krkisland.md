Krk is one of the largest islands in the Adriatic, covering 406 square kilometres off the northern Croatian coast. Unlike most Croatian islands, it is connected to the mainland by a bridge, and Rijeka airport sits on the island itself — making it one of the most accessible islands in the country.

The town of [Krk](/europe/croatia/krkisland/krk) is the main settlement, with a compact medieval centre, a Romanesque cathedral, and the Frankopan Castle on Kamplin Square. [Vrbnik](/europe/croatia/krkisland/vrbnik), perched on cliffs on the eastern coast, is known for its narrow streets and the local white wine Žlahtina. [Punat](/europe/croatia/krkisland/punat), a fishing village in a sheltered bay, is the jumping-off point for the tiny island of Košljun and its Franciscan monastery.

The coastline alternates between pebble beaches, rocky coves, and pine-shaded stretches, with clear water throughout. The island is a popular holiday destination for Croatians and a growing number of European visitors, but it retains a quieter, more local feel than the islands further south.
