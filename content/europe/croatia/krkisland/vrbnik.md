Vrbnik is a hilltop town perched on a cliff above the eastern coast of [Krk Island](/europe/croatia/krkisland), overlooking the narrow Vinodol Channel. The setting is dramatic — stone houses cluster along a ridge that drops steeply to the sea on one side.

The town is known for Žlahtina, a dry white wine grown only in the surrounding vineyards, and for having what locals claim is the narrowest street in the world (you have to shuffle sideways through it). Vrbnik is also historically significant as one of the last places where the Glagolitic script was in regular use — the oldest Croatian alphabet, which you will see carved into stones around town.
