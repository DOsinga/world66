Lopud is the middle island of the Elafiti archipelago, lying between Kolocep and Sipan about 12 kilometres northwest of [Dubrovnik](/europe/croatia/dubrovnik). It is small enough to walk across in an hour — no cars are allowed on the island — and quiet enough that you can escape the summer crowds of the city entirely.

The main attraction is Sunj Beach, a sandy bay on the south side of the island that is among the few sandy beaches in an otherwise rocky Dalmatian coast. The medieval monastery of St. Mary and a ruined hilltop fortress offer views across the Adriatic and back towards the mainland. The village of Lopud itself has a palm-lined waterfront, a cluster of Venetian-era mansions in various states of repair, and restaurants that rely on boats from the mainland for supplies.

Ferries run from Dubrovnik's Gruž harbour several times a day in season. Lopud makes an easy day trip or, better, an overnight stay to experience the island after the day-trippers leave.
