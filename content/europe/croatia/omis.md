Omis sits where the Cetina river cuts through a dramatic gorge and meets the Adriatic, about 25 kilometres south of [Split](/europe/croatia/split). For centuries it was a pirate stronghold — the Omiš pirates terrorised Venetian and Papal shipping from the 12th to the 14th century, using the narrow river canyon as a natural fortress. Two ruined fortresses still cling to the cliffs above town.

Today Omis is best known for adventure sports. The Cetina gorge offers some of the best rafting and canyoning in [Croatia](/europe/croatia), with rapids running through a steep limestone canyon past waterfalls and caves. Zip lines span the gorge and rock climbing routes scale the cliffs above. The town itself is small and pleasant, with a car-free old centre squeezed between the river and the rock face, a long pebble beach, and a handful of good seafood restaurants along the waterfront.

Omis makes an easy day trip from Split or a quieter alternative base for exploring the Dalmatian coast. In August, the town hosts the Festival of Dalmatian Klapa — traditional a cappella singing that echoes off the canyon walls.
