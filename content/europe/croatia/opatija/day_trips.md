Opatija is a good base for the northern Adriatic. [Rijeka](/europe/croatia/rijeka) is fifteen minutes away by bus and worth an afternoon for its Art Nouveau architecture and excellent fish market. The island of [Krk](/europe/croatia/krkisland) is connected to the mainland by bridge and reachable in under an hour; its old stone towns and quiet coves make a worthwhile contrast to Opatija's resort character. [Pula](/europe/croatia/pula) in Istria is about 90 km south — its Roman amphitheatre is one of the best-preserved in the world.

Closer to home, the medieval hill towns above the Riviera — Kastav, Veprinac, and Mošćenice — are each worth a half-day. Kastav sits on a ridge above Rijeka with commanding views in both directions; Veprinac is a tiny fortified village above Opatija that most visitors completely overlook.
