---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Opatija is unusual among Croatian resorts in being genuinely worth visiting year-round. The Ucka mountain shelters the bay from cold northern winds, and winters are mild enough for the camellias in Villa Angiolina's garden to bloom from February. This was, after all, designed as a winter resort.

Peak summer (July and August) brings crowds and higher prices, as it does everywhere on the Croatian coast, but Opatija is less overwhelmingly busy than Dubrovnik or Hvar. The Lungomare stays pleasant even in the heat because it faces west and catches a sea breeze.

May, June, and September are the ideal months — warm enough to swim, quiet enough to find a table at the good restaurants without booking weeks in advance, and with more favourable light for the dramatic hillside scenery. October brings the chestnut harvest in Lovran (the town celebrates with a festival) and the first autumn mushrooms appear on menus.

The town's main festival, RetrOpatija, runs in early spring and celebrates the town's Habsburg-era heyday with concerts, balls, and period costume — a slightly kitsch but enjoyable occasion.
