Osijek is the largest city in Slavonia, eastern Croatia's agricultural heartland. It sits on the Drava river and feels quite different from the coastal cities that draw most visitors to Croatia — flatter, quieter, more Central European in character, with wide boulevards and Habsburg-era architecture.

The centrepiece is Tvrđa, a remarkably well-preserved 18th-century Baroque fortress built by the Austrians to defend against the Ottomans. Its cobbled squares and yellow facades now hold cafes, galleries, and the Museum of Slavonia. The city suffered heavily during the Croatian War of Independence in the 1990s, and traces of that conflict are still visible, but Osijek has rebuilt with energy. The food culture is excellent — Slavonian kulen (spicy sausage), freshwater fish paprikaš, and local wines from the nearby Baranja region. Kopački Rit, one of the largest wetlands in Europe, is just north of the city and superb for birdwatching.
