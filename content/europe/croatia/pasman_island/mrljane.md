Mrljane is a small fishing village on the eastern shore of [Pašman Island](/europe/croatia/pasman_island) in the Zadar archipelago, facing the mainland across the narrow Pašman Channel. The village has a handful of stone houses clustered around a small harbour, a couple of sandy beaches, and clear Adriatic water that is excellent for swimming.

The residents of Mrljane have traditionally been among the most skilled navigators on the island, and many still work the waters around the archipelago. In recent years the village has become quietly popular with nautical tourists — yachts and sailboats anchor in the channel, and small boats can be hired locally. The seafood here is as fresh as it gets, pulled from the channel that morning and grilled over open flame.

A hill behind the village offers a fine panorama across the channel toward the Velebit mountains on the mainland, particularly beautiful at sunset. Mrljane is the kind of place where you come to do very little — swim, eat, read, and watch the boats — and that is its greatest appeal. Access is by car ferry from Biograd na Moru to Tkon, at the southern end of the island, followed by a short drive north.
