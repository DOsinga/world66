## Currency

Croatia adopted the euro (€) in January 2023, replacing the kuna. ATMs are widely available in cities and most tourist areas, though less common on smaller islands — carry some cash. Credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and shops, but smaller guesthouses and markets may be cash only.

## Visas

Croatia is a member of the Schengen Area. Citizens of EU countries need only a valid ID card. Visitors from the US, Canada, Australia, and many other countries can stay up to 90 days within any 180-day period without a visa. Your passport should be valid for at least three months beyond your intended departure date. Check current requirements if you hold a passport from outside the EU/EEA.

## Language

Croatian is the official language. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, particularly among younger people working in hospitality. In Istria, Italian is commonly understood. A few words of Croatian are appreciated — locals warm to any attempt.

## Time Zone

Croatia is on Central European Time (CET, UTC+1), switching to Central European Summer Time (CEST, UTC+2) from late March to late October.

## Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Standard European two-pin round plugs. Bring an adapter if you're coming from the UK or North America.

## Health

Croatia has a public health system and is generally safe. EU citizens with a European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) can access public healthcare. Travel insurance is strongly recommended for everyone else. Tap water is safe to drink throughout the country. Pharmacies (ljekarna) are well-stocked and found in all towns.

## Safety

Croatia is one of the safest countries in Europe. Petty theft can occur in crowded tourist areas — keep an eye on bags in Dubrovnik's old town and on busy ferry terminals. The sea and weather are the main hazards: currents can be strong around headlands, and the bura (north wind) can arrive suddenly. Check forecasts before any boat trip.

## Communications

Mobile coverage is good across the mainland and most populated islands. EU roaming rules apply for European SIM cards. Local SIMs are available from A1, T-Mobile, and Telemach. Wi-Fi is standard in hotels, restaurants, and cafes.
