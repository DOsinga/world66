The main square of ancient and medieval Pula, built in the 1st century BC on embanked shoreland. The Forum served as the religious, administrative, and legal centre of the Roman city. Of the original three temples on the north side, only the Temple of Augustus survives intact — a beautifully proportioned building that now houses a small collection of Roman sculpture. The Forum remains the civic heart of Pula, and in summer it hosts cultural performances.
