Rab is one of the most beautiful islands in the Kvarner Gulf, a green, forested island with sandy beaches — a rarity on the Croatian coast — and a perfectly preserved medieval old town. The town of Rab sits on a narrow peninsula, its skyline defined by four bell towers rising above terracotta roofs, and its streets lined with Renaissance palaces, churches dating from the eleventh to the sixteenth century, and quiet stone lanes.

The old town rewards slow wandering. The Upper Street runs the length of the peninsula past the remains of St. John's Basilica (fifth century) and several churches with fine bell towers. The town walls and Komrčar Park, a shaded promenade along the waterfront, provide relief from the summer heat.

The island's beaches are its other great asset. Rajska Plaža (Paradise Beach) near Lopar is a long crescent of sand in shallow, warm water — unusual for Croatia and popular with families. The Lopar peninsula has dozens of smaller sandy coves as well. The western coast is rockier and quieter, with pine-shaded swimming spots.

Croatian cuisine on Rab draws on Dalmatian and Italian traditions — expect grilled fish, risotto, gnocchi, octopus salad, and lamb. The island's own speciality is Rab cake (rapska torta), a spiral pastry filled with almonds and maraschino liqueur.
