Split is the best base on the Dalmatian coast for day trips. The easiest are the **islands**: ferries from the main port run to **Brač** (home of the Zlatni Rat beach) in about an hour, to **Hvar** (the trendiest of the Dalmatian islands, with a walled old town and good nightlife) in an hour by catamaran, to **Šolta** (quieter, less developed) in 50 minutes, and further out to **Vis**, the most remote. On the mainland, the **Krka National Park** — a chain of limestone waterfalls tumbling through a wooded gorge — is an hour north and one of the most popular outings in Croatia. The UNESCO-listed walled town of **[Trogir](/europe/croatia/trogir)**, a smaller and prettier cousin of Split, is 25 minutes west by local bus. **[Omiš](/europe/croatia/omis)**, at the mouth of the Cetina river, is known for river rafting and canyoning and is 35 minutes south. And the Roman ruins of **Salona**, Diocletian's original birthplace, are a short bus ride from the city.
