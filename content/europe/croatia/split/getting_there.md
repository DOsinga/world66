---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

**By air**, **Split International Airport** (Resnik, 20 km west of the city) is Croatia's second-busiest airport and the main gateway to Dalmatia. It has flights from most major European cities, particularly in summer, when low-cost carriers add a blizzard of seasonal routes. A regular airport bus runs into the city centre. **By road**, Split is 380 km from Zagreb via the A1 motorway (about 4 hours), and a similar distance from Ljubljana and Venice. **By bus**, a large and efficient coach network connects Split to every major town in the Balkans — Dubrovnik (4 hours), Zagreb (6 hours), Sarajevo (8 hours), Belgrade (12 hours). **By train**, overnight sleeper services from Zagreb still run in summer, though the bus is usually faster. **By ferry**, Split is the biggest passenger port on the Adriatic: catamarans and car ferries leave every day for Brač, Hvar, Vis, Šolta, Korčula, Dubrovnik and Ancona in Italy.
