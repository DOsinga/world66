title: "Shopping in Split"
type: section
---

Split, Croatia is the second largest city in Croatia and is a sister city with Los Angeles. **Split shopping** offers unique shopping opportunities for visitors. Shops in Split generally open from Monday to Friday 9:00 a.m to 1:00 p.m and 5:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m. and Saturday 9:00 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. Since the end of the Serbian-Bosnian conflict and the fall of communism, Split has slowly rebuilt its community and has become a thriving city.

**Shoes**

Split is considered one of the &ldquo;Shoe Shopping&rdquo; capitals of the world. People consider this city to have the world&rsquo;s largest collection of shoes. This has become a popular stop for both business and leisure travelers. People can purchase handmade shoes for as little as $100 per pair. Name brand shoes can be purchased for little as $20 a pair. Prices are reasonable and you might find a great lightweight item to to take home from your trip.

**Local Goods**

Croatia is famous for handmade clothes and accessories. For men, Croatian artisans produce handmade ties. The artisans take this art seriously as tie making has been done for centuries in Croatia. Cravats originated in the area and eventually evolved into the modern tie. Another item of high value from this area is the leather bag. They sell anywhere from $60-$200.

Clothes are not the only thing available from local producers. Visitors can buy groceries from the growers. Some of the more popular items are olive oil, wine, and cheese. In addition the artisans make and sell their own jewelry. A savvy shopper can find hand brooches, earrings, rings and earrings at good prices. Tourists can find handmade crafts made by the artisans. This includes oil paints and stoneware. The White House purchased its stoneware from Split.

**Shopping Sites**

Split boasts open-air markets and shopping centers. One of the more famous malls, Palace, hosts a number of luxury stores inside. Outside the Palace there is an open-air market for tourists looking for the authentic bazaar experience. Locally-made items are located here as are gourmet foods. The more expensive items are located in the Palace. A visitor looking for a place to take a break can window shop through the boutiques and art galleries. For art lovers, they can enjoy looking at the paintings. Business travelers can go to the stores and put in orders for quality goods to sell back home.

Split&rsquo;s shopping has attracted more tourists to the city, allowing it to thrive.
