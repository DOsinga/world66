---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**Summer** (June to early September) is high season: hot, sunny, busy, and with ferries to every island running at full capacity. It can be intense — Split is on the regular European cruise-ship circuit and the old town fills up by mid-morning — but the weather is perfect for swimming and the nightlife is at its peak. **May, June and September** are better: still warm enough for the beach, the sea is warm from July onwards, and prices and crowds are noticeably lower. **Late April** and **early October** are the shoulder seasons and are pleasant for sightseeing but not always for swimming. **Winter** is mild (daytime highs around 10°C) and the old town is peaceful, but most of the island ferries are cut back and a lot of restaurants and hotels close between November and March.
