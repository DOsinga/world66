Velika Gorica is a town of about 35,000 just south of Zagreb, best known internationally as the location of Zagreb Airport. It is a satellite city of the Croatian capital, functioning largely as a residential and commercial suburb. The town has its own identity and history — it developed as a market town in the medieval period and has a compact old centre with a few historic buildings.

For most travellers, Velika Gorica is where you land when flying into Zagreb rather than somewhere you go specifically. The airport is about 15 km south of the city centre, and buses and taxis run regularly to Zagreb. If you have time between flights or need an overnight before an early departure, the town is perfectly functional. The Turopolje region around it, a flat agricultural plain with traditional wooden architecture, is of interest to those exploring the area around Zagreb.
