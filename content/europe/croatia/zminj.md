Žminj is a small hilltop town in central [Istria](/europe/croatia/istria), about 15 kilometres south of Pazin. First mentioned in 1177, it sits on the old road between Pazin and Pula and has the quiet, unhurried feel of inland Istria — stone houses, a parish church, and views over rolling countryside.

The main draw nearby is the Feštinsko Kraljevstvo cave, a stalactite-filled cavern discovered accidentally in 1930 when a vineyard owner's dog fell through a hole in the ground. The cave stayed hidden through the war — it sheltered locals from the German occupation — and was only opened to visitors much later.
