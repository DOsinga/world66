**By Air**

Václav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) is the main international gateway. It handles flights from across Europe and beyond, with direct connections to North America, the Middle East, and Asia. Budget carriers including Ryanair and easyJet operate frequent routes from across Europe. The airport is in Prague 6, about 20 km from the city center; public bus and metro connections take around 40 minutes, taxis and ride-share around 30 minutes depending on traffic.

Brno-Tuřany Airport (BRQ) has limited international service, mainly seasonal charters and a handful of European routes.

**By Train**

Czechia sits on major European rail corridors. From Vienna the journey to Prague takes around four hours; from Berlin around four and a half hours. Munich to Prague takes about four hours. Trains from Budapest pass through Brno before reaching Prague. RegioJet operates comfortable and affordable international services from Vienna, Budapest, and beyond. Check schedules at [cd.cz](https://www.cd.cz) or [regijet.com](https://www.regiojet.com).

**By Bus**

FlixBus and RegioJet connect Prague with most major European cities. Buses are cheaper than trains and sometimes faster on certain routes. Prague's main bus terminal is Florenc (Prague 8), well connected to the metro.

**By Car**

Czechia is easily reached by road from Germany, Austria, Poland, and Slovakia — all share land borders. From the Schengen area you won't face routine border checks. If you're driving, you need a motorway vignette (dálniční known as eálnice) for use on Czech motorways. Buy it online at [edalnice.cz](https://www.edalnice.cz) — the official channel — before arrival. Vignettes are available for 10 days, one month, or one year.

**Visas and Entry**

Czechia is a member of the EU and the Schengen Area. EU/EEA citizens need only a valid passport or national ID card. Citizens of many other countries, including the US, Canada, Australia, and the UK, can enter visa-free for short stays. ETIAS (the EU's electronic travel authorization) is planned for visa-exempt non-EU visitors — check current requirements before traveling.
