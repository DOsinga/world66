---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**[Prague](/europe/czechrepublic/prague)**

The capital is one of Europe's most beautiful cities — a compact medieval core, a castle district, and enough bars, restaurants, and concert halls to keep you busy for a week. It survived the 20th century remarkably intact. Don't rush it.

**[Český Krumlov](/europe/czechrepublic/ceskykrumlov)**

A small town in South Bohemia built around a dramatic bend in the Vltava river, dominated by a vast castle complex. The old town below it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Worth a night or two rather than a day trip — it's best when the day-trippers have gone.

**[Karlovy Vary](/europe/czechrepublic/karlovyvary)**

The grandest of the western Bohemian spa towns, built along a wooded river valley. Ornate 19th-century colonnades, thermal springs, and the home of Becherovka — the bitter herbal liqueur. Hosts a major international film festival each July.

**[Plzeň](/europe/czechrepublic/plzen)**

The birthplace of Pilsner lager. The Pilsner Urquell Brewery runs excellent tours, and the underground cellars beneath the brewery are genuinely impressive. The historic city center is worth a few hours on its own.

**[Brno](/europe/czechrepublic/brno)**

Moravia's capital and Czech Republic's second city. Less polished than Prague, more genuinely lived-in. Strong university culture, good bars, and excellent modern architecture from the interwar period — including the Villa Tugendhat, a Mies van der Rohe masterpiece and a UNESCO site.

**Bohemian Switzerland (Česká Švýcarsko)**

A national park in northern Bohemia near the German border, known for its sandstone rock formations, deep gorges, and the Pravčická brána — the largest natural sandstone arch in Europe. Best for hiking and outdoor photography.

**Telč**

A small town in the Bohemian-Moravian Highlands whose main square is ringed by perfectly preserved Renaissance and Baroque façades. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, often overlooked. An easy day trip from Brno.

**Kutná Hora**

A medieval silver-mining town east of Prague. The Sedlec Ossuary — a small chapel decorated with the bones of some 40,000 people — is the main draw, and genuinely extraordinary. The Gothic cathedral of St. Barbara is more conventionally beautiful and arguably more impressive.

**Šumava**

The forested mountains along the German and Austrian borders in southwest Bohemia. Good for quiet hiking, cycling, and escaping crowds. The border area was a sealed military zone during communism, which left it surprisingly wild.
