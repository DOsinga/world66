Jachymova 3, Praha 1. Phone: +42 (0)02 24810099. Open: Mon.-Sat.: 9.00-16.30 The Old New Synagogue, which bears the dubious distinction of having been chosen by Hitler to be a 'museum to an extinct race' is actually separate from the Jewish Museum itself. It is also one of the oldest in Europe, dating from the 12th century.

There are actually six sites of the Jewish Museum proper which mean a bit of walking between places.. The tiny Old Jewish Cemetery has over 12000 graves dating back to the 15th century and is part of the museum, as is one of the most moving exhibitions that I have ever seen, that of art from the Terezin concentration camp. The artists are children and the pictures were painted for their families who were dying elsewhere. The artists themselves did not have long to live. This is shown in the Memorial Hall.

The other four sites are synagogues of which the Spanish Synagogue is the most ornate and the Pinkas Synagogue the most emotive, every interior wall being covered with a record of the names of all Czech Jews murdered in camps durin WW2.
