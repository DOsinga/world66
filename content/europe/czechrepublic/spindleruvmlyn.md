Špindlerův Mlýn is the largest ski resort in the Czech Republic, located in the Krkonoše (Giant Mountains) in northeastern Bohemia, close to the Polish border. The area is a national park, and the landscape is pleasant even outside the ski season — good for hiking in summer and cross-country skiing in winter.

The downhill skiing is modest by Alpine standards — runs are mostly suited to beginners and intermediate skiers — but the resort is well equipped and popular with Czech and German visitors. The ski season typically runs from December through March depending on snowfall.

In summer, the town becomes a base for hiking in the Krkonoše national park. The highest Czech peak, Sněžka (1,602 m), is reachable by cable car from nearby Pec pod Sněžkou.
