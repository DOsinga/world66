---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Denmark has a lot of coastline — over 7,000km of it — and beaches are one of the country's best free amenities. The North Sea coast of Jutland has the most dramatic beaches: wide, windswept, and backed by sand dunes. The water is cold and the waves can be substantial, which makes it popular with surfers and less suitable for leisurely swimming. The beaches around Henne Strand and Nymindegab near [Esbjerg](/europe/denmark/esbjerg) are the best known.

The east coast of Jutland and the straits between the islands are calmer and warmer. The water here faces away from the North Sea, so swimming is more comfortable. Beaches around Aarhus Bay are popular with locals.

**Amager Beach Park** (Amager Strandpark), 3km from central Copenhagen, is the city's main beach — a long artificial sand peninsula with a lagoon on one side. Easy to reach by Metro (Amager Strand station). Popular with locals in summer; the lagoon side is sheltered enough for swimming even with children.

**Hornbæk** on the north coast of Zealand is considered one of the best beaches on that island — long, sandy, and reasonably calm. It's become fashionable with Copenhagen weekenders; the train takes about 75 minutes from the city.

**Bornholm** has some of the most varied and beautiful beaches in Denmark. The south coast has fine white sand; the north coast is rockier. Dueodde on the southern tip has famously fine sand.

The Danish beach culture is relaxed. Nudism is accepted at designated areas on most beaches, and a topless approach is common generally. Many beaches have changing facilities and some have lifeguards in summer, but facilities are often minimal.
