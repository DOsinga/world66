A self-proclaimed "free state" on a former military base, Christiania has been home to a commune since 1971. The area is full of hand-built houses, workshops, music venues, and organic eateries. It remains one of Copenhagen's most distinctive and controversial neighbourhoods, with its future perpetually under political debate.

The main entrance is on Prinsessegade. Walk in and you'll find yourself on a car-free lane lined with murals, craft stalls, and improvised architecture that ranges from whimsical to surprisingly sophisticated. Nemoland and Morgenstedet are popular spots for a meal -- the latter serves decent vegetarian food at fair prices.

Photography is not allowed on the infamous Pusher Street, and the community takes this rule seriously. Beyond that stretch, the atmosphere is relaxed. The ramparts along the old fortifications offer pleasant walks with views over the water, and in summer the whole area takes on a festival feel with live music and outdoor seating.
