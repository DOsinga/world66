Frederiksberg is a municipality entirely surrounded by Copenhagen, though it is technically an independent municipality and not part of the city. From a visitor's perspective the distinction is invisible: Frederiksberg is a wealthy, gracious inner suburb that feels like a seamless part of central Copenhagen, with wide tree-lined avenues, elegant apartment buildings, and an excellent collection of parks and cultural institutions.

Frederiksberg Have (Frederiksberg Gardens) is one of the finest landscape gardens in Denmark, running up to the Frederiksberg Palace — a Baroque royal summer residence that is now a military academy but whose gardens are open to the public. The Copenhagen Zoo is at the southern edge of the park. The neighbourhood around Frederiksberg Allé has good independent restaurants and cafes. Cisternerne, a former water reservoir converted into an exhibition space for contemporary art, is one of Copenhagen's more unusual venues. Frederiksberg is a natural base for visiting Copenhagen, with good transport links and a slightly more residential atmosphere than the most central areas.
