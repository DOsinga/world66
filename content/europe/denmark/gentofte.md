Gentofte is an affluent suburb north of central [Copenhagen](/europe/denmark/copenhagen), straddling the coast and the deer park. Most visitors pass through it without realising — but a couple of things here are worth a deliberate visit.

Dyrehavsbakken (Bakken), on the edge of Jægersborg Dyrehave, claims to be the world's oldest operating amusement park, founded in 1583. The nearby Ordrupgaard Museum houses one of northern Europe's finest collections of French Impressionist and Danish Golden Age painting, in a building extended by Zaha Hadid. The Gentofte Church, in the centre of the municipality, is the second oldest in Denmark.

Bernstorff Park, a former royal summer residence, is a pleasant green space for a walk, and the Experimentarium science centre is a good option with children.
