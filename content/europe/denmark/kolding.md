Kolding sits at the head of Kolding Fjord in southeast Jutland, a compact city of around 60,000 that makes a natural stop between Copenhagen and the Jutland peninsula. The centrepiece is Koldinghus, a medieval royal castle perched above the harbour that burned down in 1808 and was controversially rebuilt in the 1990s with raw concrete and steel — the result is striking and the museum inside is one of Denmark's best regional collections.

The old town around the castle is pleasant for walking, with half-timbered houses and the pedestrianised shopping streets typical of Danish provincial cities. Geografisk Have (the Geographic Garden) is an unexpected delight — a botanical garden organized by continent with over 2,000 plant species from around the world. The fjord itself is good for kayaking and swimming in summer.

Kolding is also the home of the Trapholt museum of modern art, craft, and design, set in a park overlooking the fjord with an Arne Jacobsen summerhouse in the grounds. If you are interested in Danish design, it is worth the short drive south of town.
