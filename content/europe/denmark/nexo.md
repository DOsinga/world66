Nexø is the second-largest town on [Bornholm](/europe/denmark/bornholm), a working fishing port on the island's east coast. It lacks the half-timbered charm of Gudhjem or Svaneke, but it has an honest, unpolished character — the harbour is full of actual fishing boats rather than pleasure craft, and the fish you eat here came out of the Baltic that morning.

The town was heavily bombed by Soviet forces in May 1945, days after the rest of Denmark had been liberated, which explains why much of the architecture is postwar rather than picturesque. The Nexø Museum covers this history along with the life of Martin Andersen Nexø, the novelist who took the town's name as his own. South of town, the sandy beaches at Balka are among the best on Bornholm and draw Danish families throughout the summer.
