Randers is a mid-sized Danish town on the Gudenå river in eastern Jutland, best known for its tropical zoo. Randers Regnskov (Randers Rainforest) is a domed complex built over the river that houses three climate zones — South America, Africa, and Asia — under interconnected glass domes. It is one of Denmark's most-visited family attractions, and the reason most people stop here.

Beyond the rainforest, Randers has a pleasant if unremarkable old town with half-timbered houses and a pedestrianised shopping street. The medieval Sankt Mortens Kirke is worth a glance. The Gudenå — Denmark's longest river — flows through town and is good for kayaking downstream toward Randers Fjord, where the river meets the Kattegat. The Kulturhuset (Culture House) hosts concerts and exhibitions.

Randers is not a destination in itself, but it makes a reasonable stop on a Jutland road trip, particularly if you are travelling with children. The town is well-connected by train to Aarhus (30 minutes south) and Aalborg (an hour north).
