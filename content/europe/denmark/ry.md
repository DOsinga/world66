Located between Århus and Silkeborg, Ry (pronounced like the French 'rue') sits in the Danish Lake Highlands — the closest Denmark gets to hilly terrain. The river Gudenå runs right through town, and the surrounding area is all forests, lakes, and gentle hills.

You can sail, canoe, or walk through the woods. Wild roe deer are common. It is not a hugely touristic area, though you will see German and Dutch visitors in summer.

Ry is easy to reach by train from Århus. While here, walk up Himmelbjerget — at 147 metres it is one of Denmark's highest points, with views across the lakes that make the modest climb worthwhile.
