title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Denmark's highlights range from world-class city museums to distinctive landscapes. Here are the standouts at the national level — individual cities have their own entries.

**Kronborg Castle**, in [Helsingør](/europe/denmark/helsingor), is Shakespeare's Elsinore — the setting for Hamlet. The Renaissance fortress sits at the narrow strait between Denmark and Sweden and is genuinely dramatic. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

**Frederiksborg Slot**, in [Hillerød](/europe/denmark/hillerod), is the most magnificent Renaissance castle in Scandinavia. Built on three islands in a lake, it now houses the Museum of National History. The gardens are formal Dutch-style. Take the train from Copenhagen (40 minutes).

**Møns Klint** on the island of [Møn](/europe/denmark/mon) is Denmark's most striking natural landscape — 128-metre white chalk cliffs dropping to the Baltic. There's a good visitor centre and fossil hunting on the beach.

**The Viking Ship Museum** in [Roskilde](/europe/denmark/roskilde) displays five original Viking ships raised from the fjord in 1962. You can also watch craftspeople build replica vessels. The waterfront setting is excellent.

**Hammershus** on [Bornholm](/europe/denmark/bornholmisland) is the largest castle ruin in Scandinavia, perched on a granite cliff above the Baltic. The island itself — with its distinctive round churches, smokehouses, and coastal scenery — is worth the journey.

**Legoland** in [Billund](/europe/denmark/billund) is the original, and still good. Miniland, where famous world landmarks are built in Lego, remains the centrepiece. Best for children, but the sheer craftsmanship of the models impresses adults too.

**Skagen** at the northern tip of Jutland is where the North Sea and Kattegat visibly meet. The town has an unusually good art museum (the Skagen Painters worked here in the 19th century) and a relaxed, end-of-the-world atmosphere.

**ARoS Aarhus Art Museum** is one of the best art museums in Scandinavia. The rooftop rainbow panorama walkway by Olafur Eliasson alone is worth the visit.
