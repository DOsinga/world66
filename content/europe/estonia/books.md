**Travel guides**

*Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania* (Lonely Planet) — the standard backpacker guide, updated regularly. Covers all three Baltic states in one volume with solid practical information.

*The Bradt Guide to Estonia* by Neil Taylor — more focused and literary than Lonely Planet, good for independent travellers who want more context alongside the listings.

**History**

*The Baltic Revolution: Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania and the Path to Independence* by Anatol Lieven (1993) — still the best single-volume account of how the three states broke free from the Soviet Union. Lieven was a journalist on the ground during the independence movements.

*War in the Woods: Estonia's Struggle for Survival 1944–56* by Mart Laar — the former Estonian prime minister tells the story of the Forest Brothers, the guerrilla fighters who resisted Soviet occupation for more than a decade after World War II.

*The Baltic States: The Years of Independence 1917–1940* by Georg von Rauch — dense but thorough history of the interwar independence period.

**Culture and literature**

*The Czar's Madman* by Jaan Kross — the most celebrated Estonian novel in translation, set in 19th-century czarist Estonia. Kross was the country's foremost novelist and was repeatedly nominated for the Nobel Prize.

*Treading Air* by Jaan Kaplinski — poetry by one of Estonia's most important contemporary poets, with a strong sense of landscape and identity.
