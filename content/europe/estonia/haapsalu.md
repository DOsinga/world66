Haapsalu is a small resort town on the west coast of Estonia, known for centuries for its warm sea water, curative mud baths, and peaceful atmosphere. The narrow streets lined with early twentieth-century wooden houses make the town centre a pleasant place to wander, and the pace of life here is noticeably slower than Tallinn, two hours to the east.

The main sight is the Episcopal Castle, a vast thirteenth-century fortress with a cathedral built into its walls. The legend of the White Lady — a ghostly figure said to appear in one of the castle windows during the August full moon — has turned into the town's biggest annual celebration, the White Lady Days festival. The seafront promenade and the elegant wooden Assembly Hall (Kuursaal), built in 1898, are the other centrepieces, offering a picturesque walk along the shore.

Haapsalu comes alive in summer with a string of music festivals: the Old Music Festival, the strings festival Viiulimängud, the Tchaikovsky Festival (the composer was a regular visitor), and the August Blues Festival. The town is also a convenient jumping-off point for the islands of Hiiumaa and Vormsi.
