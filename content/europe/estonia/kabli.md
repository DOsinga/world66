Kabli is a quiet coastal village on Pärnu Bay in southwest Estonia, known for its long sandy beach and as one of the best birdwatching spots in the Baltics. The beach stretches for several kilometres — shallow, sandy, and uncrowded even in summer.

Twice a year, in May and again in October, tens of thousands of migratory birds pass through, and the observation tower on the shore gives a grandstand view. The autumn migration is particularly spectacular, with geese, cranes, and waders passing in huge numbers. Kabli is part of the Pärnu Bay Important Bird Area and attracts serious birdwatchers from across northern Europe.
