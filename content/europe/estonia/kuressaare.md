Kuressaare
is the capital of Saaremaa. The town, located at the Livonia Bay, has
territory of about 15 sq.km and a population of about 16,000.

Kuressaare
that obtained the freedom of a town in 1563 has been called with
different names throughout its existence. Its historic German-like name
used to be Arensburg that was replaced by Kuressaare in 1917. During
the Soviet period, in 1952-1988 it was called Kingissepa after the
bolshevik Viktor Kingissepp who was born here.

Kuressaare was
the first town in [Estonia](/europe/estonia) where the self-governing status was restored
in October 1990. Today, Kuressaare is a safe and healthy town, open for
international cooperation and communication and having eight twin-towns.
