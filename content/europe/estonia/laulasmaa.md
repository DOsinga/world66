Laulasmaa is a small coastal village about 40 km west of Tallinn on the northern Estonian coast. Its name comes from the singing sands on its beach — "laulev maa" means "the land that sings" in Estonian.

The village has been a popular holiday spot since the 1930s, and it remains a quiet retreat from the capital. The beach is the main attraction: a long stretch of sand backed by pine forest, uncrowded even in summer. The nearby Lohusalu marina gives access to the coast by boat.
