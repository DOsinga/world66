Muhu is the third largest island in Estonia and the stepping stone between the mainland and [Saaremaa](/europe/estonia/kuressaare). The causeway to Saaremaa means most visitors pass straight through, which is a shame — the island rewards those who stop.

The landscape is quietly beautiful: thatched limestone cottages, working windmills, juniper meadows, and the constant company of deer, moose, and wading birds. It feels unhurried in a way that even rural Estonia sometimes doesn't.

St. Catherine's Church, one of the oldest in Estonia, is worth a stop. Look for the pagan tombstones incorporated into the walls — a trace of the island's pre-Christian past that the builders apparently couldn't quite bring themselves to discard.
