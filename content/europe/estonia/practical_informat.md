**Currency**

Estonia uses the euro (€). ATMs are available in all towns and most villages. Cards are accepted almost everywhere, including small cafés and market stalls — Estonia is one of the most cashless societies in Europe. Carry a small amount of cash for rural ferries and farmers' markets.

**Language**

Estonian is the official language, a Finno-Ugric tongue unrelated to the Slavic or Germanic languages. Russian is still widely spoken in eastern Estonia and among older residents. English is well understood in Tallinn, Tartu, and tourist areas; younger Estonians generally speak it fluently.

A few useful phrases:
- Hello: *Tere*
- Thank you: *Tänan* or *Aitäh*
- Please: *Palun*
- Yes / No: *Jah / Ei*
- Do you speak English? *Kas te räägite inglise keelt?*

**Electricity**

230V, 50Hz. Type F plugs (standard European two-pin). No adapter needed for most European devices.

**Emergency**

The emergency number is **112** (police, ambulance, fire). English-speaking operators are available.

**Visas**

Estonia is a member of the EU and Schengen Area. Citizens of EU countries, the US, Canada, Australia, and many others can enter without a visa for up to 90 days. Check current requirements if you're unsure.

**Internet and connectivity**

Estonia has excellent mobile and Wi-Fi coverage, including in national parks. Free Wi-Fi is available in most cafés, hotels, and public spaces. EU citizens pay no roaming charges.

**Tipping**

Tipping is not obligatory but is appreciated. Rounding up the bill or leaving 10% in restaurants is common in Tallinn.
