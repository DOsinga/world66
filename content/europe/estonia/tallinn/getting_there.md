---
title: "Getting There in Tallinn"
type: section
---

**By Sea**

Perhaps the easiest way to arrive in Tallinn is by boat and indeed many foreign visitors to the Estonian capital arrive on cruise ships. Regular ferries arrive in and depart from Tallinn from St. Petersburg, Stockholm, Helsinki, Riga and Rostock. The passenger ship port is about one kilometer northeast of Tallinn’s Old Town. Trams (lines 1 and 2), buses (numbers 3,4 and 8) run to the Old Town from the Linnahall stop which is approximately a five minute walk from the arrival terminal of the port. A taxi between the port and the center of the Old Town will cost approximately 45 EEK.

**By Air**

The Tallinn Airport (TLL) has limited flights. Lufthansa, Estonian Air, FinnAir and AirBaltic are the major carriers.

**By Land**

The train network in Estonia is not as good as in other parts of Europe. Domestic service is limited. International train lines do include Tallinn, but most travelers will find that buses are a better choice for both long and short-haul journeys. For example, there are more than twenty buses from Tallinn to Pärnu every day and the trip takes about two hours. In contrast, there are only two trains daily from Tallinn to Pärnu each day and the trip takes about three hours.

Estonia is a lovely country to explore by car. If you do decide to rent one though, consider taking a bus to Tartu or Pärnu and renting from one of the agencies there. For some reason, renting a car either at the Tallinn airport or in the city center is more expensive than renting in other Estonian cities.
