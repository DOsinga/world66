Norðoyggjar is the collective name for the six north-eastern islands: Borðoy, Fugloy, Kalsoy, Kunoy, Svinoy and Víðoy. Klaksvík on Borðoy is the main town and the ferry hub for the group.

Svinoy and Fugloy are only reachable by ferry from Hvannasund on Víðoy, often running in fierce Atlantic conditions. The remaining four islands are connected by tunnels and causeways and can be explored by road.
