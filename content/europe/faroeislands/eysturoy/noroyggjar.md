Norðoyggjar — the Northern Isles — is the collective name for the six north-eastern islands of the Faroe archipelago: Borðoy, Víðoy, Kunoy, Kalsoy, Svinoy, and Fugloy. Klaksvík, on Borðoy, is the group's main town and the second-largest settlement in the Faroes, and it functions as the ferry hub for the whole cluster.

The four inner islands — Borðoy, Víðoy, Kunoy, and Kalsoy — are connected by a network of tunnels and causeways and can be explored by car. Svinoy and Fugloy are only reachable by helicopter or by ferry from Hvannasund, often in fierce Atlantic conditions; they are among the most remote and least-visited communities in the Faroes. Kalsoy, sometimes called "the flute" for its long, narrow shape, is best known for the Kallur lighthouse at its northern tip — one of the most dramatic viewpoints in the archipelago, reached by a 1.5-hour hike.
