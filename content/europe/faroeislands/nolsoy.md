Nólsoy is both an island and its principal settlement, easily reached by ferry from Tórshavn. The crossing takes about 20 minutes and drops you in a small village with a church, a shop, a cafe, and an arch made from the jawbones of a whale.

The island has one of the world's largest colonies of storm petrels, but seeing them requires an overnight stay — the birds return to their burrows only after dark. A walk to the southern end of the island makes for a good half-day outing, with views across to Streymoy and the open Atlantic. For a shorter visit, head for the viewpoint on the lower part of the island.
