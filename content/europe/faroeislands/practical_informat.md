---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency.** The Faroese króna is pegged to and interchangeable with the Danish krone (DKK). Danish notes are accepted everywhere; Faroese notes are only valid in the Faroes. Card payment is widely accepted, even in small villages, but it's worth carrying some cash for ferries and remote stops.

**Visas.** The Faroe Islands are an autonomous territory of Denmark but are not part of the EU or the Schengen area. Citizens of EU and EEA countries, the UK, the US, Canada, Australia, and most other Western countries may enter without a visa for up to 90 days. Importantly, a Schengen visa does not grant entry to the Faroes, and a Faroese entry stamp does not count toward your Schengen days. Check requirements for your specific passport.

**Language.** Faroese is the language of daily life. Danish is widely understood. English is spoken well by most people, especially in [Tórshavn](/europe/faroeislands/torshavn) and among the under-50s.

**Costs.** The Faroes are not cheap. Budget travel is difficult — there are no hostels in the classic sense, and the few guesthouses tend toward mid-range prices. Self-catering or camping can reduce costs. The public bus and ferry network is affordable once you're there.

**Health and safety.** No vaccinations are required. Medical facilities are good in Tórshavn; on smaller islands you will be evacuated by helicopter in a serious emergency. The islands are extremely safe — crime is rare and personal safety is not a concern.

**Weather.** Pack for everything. Even in summer, the mountains can be in cloud, rain can sweep in without warning, and temperatures rarely rise above 15°C. Wind is the constant. Waterproof layers are essential year-round.

**Electricity.** 220V, type C/F plugs (standard European).

**Internet.** 4G coverage is good across the main islands. Wi-Fi is available in most accommodation.
