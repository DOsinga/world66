Sandur is the main village on Sandoy, the quietest and most pastoral of the Faroe Islands. Known locally as "Heima á Sandi", it sits at the head of a sheltered fjord surrounded by green hillsides and farmland — a slower pace than Tórshavn, and all the better for it.

The village has a small art museum, Listasavnið, dedicated to Faroese art, and a historic house museum called Í Koytu that gives a sense of traditional island life. For most visitors, though, the draw is the landscape itself: the ferry crossing from Gamlarætt is scenic, and Sandoy's walking trails are uncrowded even by Faroese standards.
