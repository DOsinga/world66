Vágur is one of the largest towns on Suðuroy, the southernmost island in the Faroes. It sits on the east coast around Vágsfjørður, between the villages of Porkeri and Lopra. About 1,370 people live here.

The surroundings are dramatic even by Faroese standards. West of the village, Vágseiði faces the open Atlantic. To the south, Eggjarnar drops away in sheer vertical cliffs. Climb Skúvanes and you're looking straight down into the ocean. North of town at Botni, the Faroes' first hydroelectric power plant was built in 1921 — and it's still running.
