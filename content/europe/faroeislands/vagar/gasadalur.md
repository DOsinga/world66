Gasadalur is one of the most remote villages in the Faroe Islands, perched above dramatic sea cliffs on the western tip of Vágar. Until 2004, when a tunnel was blasted through the mountain, the only way in was by boat or on foot over the ridge — the postman made the trek regularly. Now a short tunnel connects it to the rest of the island, and every house has a car outside, but the village itself remains tiny and unhurried.

The main reason to come is the walk beyond the road end to the viewpoint above the Múlafossur waterfall, which drops straight off the cliff into the sea far below. From here, the stacks and the island of Tindholmur rise dramatically to the south, and on clear days you can see Mykines to the west. Puffins nest in the cliff faces and are easy to spot from the path in summer.
