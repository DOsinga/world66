The Aland Islands group, comprising over 6000 islands, is located in the Baltic Sea between Sweden and Finland. The main language is Swedish, dating back from the 13th century when the islands still belonged to the King of Sweden. At the beginning of the 19th century, Aland was claimed by Russia and it was not until 1921 that it was returned to Finland.



The islands have a high degree of autonomy within Finland — their own parliament, their own postal stamps, and Swedish as the only official language. They are also demilitarised under international treaty. The main island, with the capital Mariehamn, is easily reached by ferry from Turku or Stockholm.
