Espoo is Finland's second largest city and one of its most unusual in structure: rather than a single urban core, it consists of five distinct district centres spread across a large area, all connected by public transport. The carefully designed garden city of Tapiola is the best known, with its thoughtful blend of modernist architecture, green space, and residential neighbourhoods.

Just 15 minutes from Helsinki by metro or train, Espoo is home to the main campus of Aalto University and several technology companies. The city has a strong cultural programme — the April Jazz festival and the Espoo Ciné International Film Festival are highlights — and the **Nuuksio National Park** is on its western edge, offering forest, lakes, and good hiking within easy reach of the capital. The **EMMA museum** (Espoo Museum of Modern Art) is worth a visit for those interested in Finnish contemporary art.
