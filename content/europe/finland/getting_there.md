## By Air

The main gateway is **Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL)**, about 20 km north of the city centre. It handles flights from across Europe, North America, and Asia. Finnair is the national carrier and offers good connections to many destinations; budget airlines including Ryanair and Norwegian cover most European routes. From outside Europe, Finnair flies direct from New York, London, and several Asian hubs.

Secondary airports worth knowing: **Tampere-Pirkkala** (used by Ryanair for budget connections to the south), **Turku**, **Oulu**, and **Rovaniemi** (the main Lapland gateway, especially busy in winter). Domestic flights to Lapland are quick and worth considering if you're pressed for time.

## By Ferry

Ferries are a scenic and popular way to arrive, especially from Sweden. The main routes:

- **Stockholm to Helsinki** — operated by Tallink Silja and Viking Line, overnight crossings (~17 hours), with departures from both Turku (shorter, ~11 hours) and Helsinki.
- **Stockholm to Vaasa** — a shorter daytime crossing (~8 hours) by Wasaline.
- **Tallinn to Helsinki** — fast ferries and standard car ferries cross in 2–3 hours. Multiple daily departures. Operators include Tallink, Viking Line, Eckerö, and FinFerries.
- **Travemünde to Helsinki** — Finnlines runs an overnight route from Germany.
- **Gdynia to Helsinki** — Finnlines also serves this Poland connection.

Ferry travel is popular with Finns and Swedes who book well ahead in summer.

## By Train

Rail connections from the east exist, but the Russia border has been closed since 2022 with no sign of reopening soon. There is no direct train from central Europe into Finland; most travellers coming overland use Stockholm as a hub and take the ferry.

## By Bus

Several international bus companies connect Helsinki to Tallinn, Riga, and St Petersburg (when the border is open). Checking current cross-border bus availability before travel is advisable.
