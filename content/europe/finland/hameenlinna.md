Hämeenlinna is one of [Finland's](/europe/finland) oldest inland towns, built around a red-brick medieval castle on the shore of Lake Vanajavesi, about 100 kilometres north of [Helsinki](/europe/finland/helsinki). The castle, Häme Castle, dates from the 13th century and is one of the best-preserved medieval fortifications in the country — its thick walls and courtyard are open to visitors year-round, and the interior houses exhibitions on the castle's history as a fortress, granary, and prison.

The town's most famous son is Jean Sibelius, born here in 1865. His childhood home on Hallituskatu has been preserved as a small museum, and the connection is celebrated throughout the town. But Hämeenlinna has more to offer than Sibelius pilgrimage. The Hämeenlinna Art Museum and the nearby Iittala glass village, where you can visit the factory and museum of Finland's most iconic glassware brand, are both worth a stop.

The real highlight of the area is Aulanko Nature Reserve, a landscaped park and forest on a promontory jutting into Lake Vanajavesi. A granite observation tower at the top of the hill gives panoramic views across the lake district — endless water, islands, and forest stretching to the horizon. Hämeenlinna is an easy day trip from Helsinki by train, with the journey taking just over an hour, or a worthwhile stop on the way to [Tampere](/europe/finland/tampere) and the Finnish lake district.
