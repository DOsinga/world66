Finland is a country of extremes: extreme north, extreme quiet, extreme seasons. Its highlights aren't cities packed with monuments — they're often landscapes, experiences, and encounters with nature that are hard to find anywhere else in Europe.

## Helsinki

[Helsinki](/europe/finland/helsinki) is a compact, handsome capital that's easy to fall for. The neoclassical Senate Square, the covered market halls, the Suomenlinna island fortress (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), and the Design District are the set pieces. But what makes Helsinki work is the combination of sea, green parks, a confident design culture, and a restaurant and bar scene that has improved enormously in recent years. Give it two or three days before heading north.

## Lapland

The far north is Finland's most dramatic region. In winter it's a snow-covered wilderness offering dog sledding, reindeer safaris, ice fishing, snowmobiling, and — on clear nights — the Northern Lights arcing across the sky. In summer it's the land of the Midnight Sun: the sun simply doesn't set for weeks. [Rovaniemi](/europe/finland/rovaniemi) is the main hub, good for day trips to Santa Park and the wilderness. For proper wilderness, head further north toward [Saariselkä](/europe/finland/saariselk) and the Urho Kekkonen National Park. [Inari](/europe/finland/inari) is the centre of Sámi culture.

## The Lake District

Finland has around 188,000 lakes. The lake district in the south-centre — roughly between Tampere, Mikkeli, and Joensuu — is where the classic Finnish cottage holiday happens. Towns like [Savonlinna](/europe/finland/savonlinna) (medieval castle, opera festival), [Kuopio](/europe/finland/kuopio), and [Mikkeli](/europe/finland/mikkeli) make good bases. Getting out on the water — by canoe, kayak, or slow lake steamer — is the point.

## Tampere

[Tampere](/europe/finland/tampere) is Finland's third-largest city and arguably its most likeable. Built on the banks of the Tammerkoski Rapids between two lakes, it has an industrial heritage that gives it a grittier character than Helsinki, a strong live music scene, good museums (including the Vapriikki complex), and several excellent restaurants. Worth a full day or two.

## Turku and the Archipelago

[Turku](/europe/finland/turku) was Finland's capital until 1812 and still carries itself with quiet authority. The 13th-century castle and cathedral are the main sights. More compelling is the archipelago to the southwest — thousands of islands scattered across the Baltic, accessible by ferry and offering some of Finland's most beautiful cycling and kayaking country. The Åland Islands sit further out, autonomous, Swedish-speaking, and well worth the extra ferry journey.

## Porvoo

[Porvoo](/europe/finland/porvoo) is the most charming easy excursion from Helsinki — just 50 km east. Its medieval old town, with narrow cobbled streets and distinctive red riverside warehouses, is genuinely lovely and not overrun. Accessible by bus or summer ferry.

## The Karelian Border Region

Eastern Finland, around [Lappeenranta](/europe/finland/lappeenranta), [Joensuu](/europe/finland/joensuu), and Lieksa, offers a different Finland: more rugged, historically complex, with strong Karelian cultural traditions. The Saimaa Canal near Lappeenranta connects the lake system to the Gulf of Finland and is a notable cruise route. Savonlinna belongs here too, though it's covered above.

## Pori and the West Coast

[Pori](/europe/finland/pori) is the home of one of Europe's best jazz festivals each July. The west coast towns — [Rauma](/europe/finland/rauma) (UNESCO old wooden town), [Vaasa](/europe/finland/vaasa), [Jakobstad](/europe/finland/jakobstad) — have a different character to the south and east, often bilingual Finnish-Swedish, with strong craft and fishing traditions.
