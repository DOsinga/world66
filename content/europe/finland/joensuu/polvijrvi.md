Polvijärvi is a quiet rural municipality about 30km north of Joensuu in the North Karelia region of eastern Finland. It is lake country — forests, water, and small farms — the kind of place that draws Finnish families on summer holidays rather than international tourists. The pace is slow and intentionally so.

The main draw in the area is Huhmari Holiday Centre, a well-regarded resort with lake access, cottages, and outdoor activities. Nearby Juuka — a few kilometres further north — has Koli National Park on its doorstep, with some of the finest panoramic views in Finland: forested hills dropping down to Lake Pielinen, a landscape that inspired the Finnish national romantics of the 19th century.
