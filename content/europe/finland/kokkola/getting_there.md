---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Kokkola sits on the Gulf of Bothnia coast in central western Finland, roughly halfway between Vaasa and Oulu. Several main roads connect the city to [Turku](/europe/finland/turku), Jyväskylä, Kajaani, and [Oulu](/europe/finland/oulu). The scenic Seven Bridges Archipelago Road between Kokkola and [Jakobstad](/europe/finland/jakobstad) (Pietarsaari) is worth the detour if you are driving.

The nearest airport is Kokkola-Pietarsaari Airport (KOK), about 22 km southeast of the city centre. There are daily flights to Helsinki, and the airport also handles some international connections to Stockholm. A bus connects the airport to town.

The train station is right in the centre of Kokkola. Express trains from Helsinki take around four and a half hours, and the Pendolino service runs daily. Trains also connect to Oulu (about two hours) and Vaasa (about an hour and a half). Long-distance buses serve the same routes and are sometimes cheaper.
