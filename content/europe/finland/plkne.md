Pälkäne is a small lakeside municipality in the [Tampere](/europe/finland/tampere) region of southern Finland, with around 4,300 inhabitants. The landscape is dominated by lakes — Pälkänevesi and Mallasvesi are the largest — connected by the river Kostianvirta, which formed suddenly in 1604 when the water broke through between the lakes.

The area is known for the Battle of Kostianvirta (1713), where a Swedish army was defeated by Russian forces in the Great Northern War, leading to eight years of Russian occupation of Finland. Today the site is a quiet riverside spot. Pälkäne is primarily a summer cottage destination for Finns, with good swimming, fishing, and cross-country skiing in winter. The Sappee ski centre is nearby.
