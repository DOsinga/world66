A grieving merchant named Fritz Arthur Juselius built this neo-Gothic mausoleum in the early 1900s for his daughter Sigrid, who died at eleven. The interior is decorated with frescoes by Akseli Gallen-Kallela, Finland's most celebrated artist — though the originals deteriorated and had to be replaced by copies painted by his son. The result is unexpectedly beautiful, and the cemetery setting gives it a quiet dignity.
