Porvoo punches above its weight for a small city. The old town alone rewards several hours of wandering, and the cluster of museums covering everything from Finland's national poet to Albert Edelfelt's studio keeps the culturally inclined busy for a full day.

**The Home of J. L. Runeberg** (Aleksanterinkatu 3) is the most visited of the lot — the house where Finland's national poet lived with his wife until his death in 1877 is preserved as it was, and it's hard not to be moved by the intimacy of the rooms. The **Porvoo Museum** in the old city hall on Välikatu 11 covers the town's cultural and historical heritage. The **Porvoo Museum of Natural History** (Kaivokatu 40) has a permanent exhibition on Finnish animals in their natural surroundings. The **Doll and Toy Museum** on Jokikatu 14 displays a large private collection of toys and dolls from the 19th and 20th centuries.

Just outside the centre, **Kiiala Manor** is one of the area's old historical estates, and the **Studio Museum** at Edelfeltinpolku 3 is dedicated to the life and work of painter Albert Edelfelt, who did much of his work on this site.
