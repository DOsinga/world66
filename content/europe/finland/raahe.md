Raahe is a small coastal city on the Gulf of Bothnia, about 75 km south of Oulu. Count Pehr Brahe founded the town in 1649, and its history as a trading and shipbuilding port is still visible in the old town quarter — one of the best-preserved wooden town centres in Finland. The old streets and 19th-century timber houses have been carefully maintained and still function as a lived-in neighbourhood.

The Raahe Museum, housed in an old warehouse, contains an unusual collection of maritime artefacts including what is believed to be the oldest surviving diving suit in the world — an 18th-century leather contraption donated by a local sea captain.

The Rautaruukki steelworks dominate the modern economy. The town is not a major tourist destination, but the old wooden town and the sea breezes of the Bothnian coast make a stop worthwhile if you're travelling the west coast route.
