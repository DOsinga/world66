Finland's most significant sights are distributed across its regions and are covered in the individual city and destination pages. A few highlights at the national level:

**Suomenlinna** — A UNESCO World Heritage Site sea fortress built across four islands just off Helsinki, accessible by ferry from the Market Square. One of the largest sea fortresses in the world, with 18th-century ramparts, museums, and a working community of artists and craftspeople. Worth half a day at minimum.

**Olavinlinna Castle, Savonlinna** — A 15th-century island castle in the middle of the lake district, still spectacularly intact. This is where the Savonlinna Opera Festival is staged each July. The castle itself is open to visitors year-round.

**Turku Castle** — Finland's oldest castle, dating from the 1280s. Houses the Turku Historical Museum inside. On the river at the edge of the city centre.

**Koli National Park** — In North Karelia, the forested fell views here represent the classic Finnish landscape that inspired many national romantic painters. A pilgrimage site for Finnish identity. Good hiking in summer, skiing in winter.

**Urho Kekkonen National Park** — The largest national park in Finland south of the wilderness reserves, stretching across Lapland from Saariselkä. Proper Arctic wilderness: fell, forest, river, and silence.

**Siida Sámi Museum, Inari** — The best museum in Finland for understanding Sámi culture and Arctic nature. Set on the shores of Lake Inari in deep Lapland.

**Ainola, Järvenpää** — The home of composer Jean Sibelius and his wife Aino for 50 years, preserved as a museum about 40 km north of Helsinki. The garden and surroundings give a real sense of his connection to Finnish nature and landscape.

**Alvar Aalto buildings** — The great Finnish architect left buildings across the country. The Finlandia Hall in Helsinki, the Säynätsalo Town Hall near Jyväskylä, and the Paimio Sanatorium are among the most significant. Jyväskylä has the densest concentration.

**Rauma Old Town** — A UNESCO World Heritage Site, one of the best-preserved wooden urban environments in Scandinavia. The old town centre is still a living neighbourhood.

For city-specific sights, see the individual destination pages.
