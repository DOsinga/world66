**1.** For finnish people sauna is a way of living. Traditional finnish sauna is often followed by a dip into the icy water in the winter (called *avanto)* and in the summer jump into the nearest lake/river/see you can find. Drink finnish beer and sing songs with fellow mates and you´ll get a sense of the most loved finnish tradition.

**2.** If you are an outdoor person, there is thousand of different sceneries to choose from, wheter you want to hike in the rough "tunturi" Lappland, alongside the thousands of lakes in the Middle Finnland or enjoy a nice view from high rocks of SoutherN Finland over looking to the unic Baltic see. Also go on a little walk to the old pine woods where you´ll find berries and amazind wildlife. Finland has many marks from the ice age f.e cliffs and very beautiful swamps. It is not hard to find a quite place in the nature because accommodation is in mainly in the towns.

**3.** Take a road trip to the finnish countryside. Beautiful crops and narrow roads have a lovely vibe and on the way stop to a nice little swimming place by the lake (there are thousands of these) or spend the night camping and especially in the summer enjoy light nights when the sun never sets.

**4. **Visit the Santa Claus at *Korvatunturi*. In Lappland is also possible to ride a reindeer sleigh, go to a snow scooter safari and go cross country skiing to the frozen lakes in the winter. Lappland is at its most beautiful in the autumn when the leaves turn red and yellow and nature prepares to winter ( this is called* ruska) *this ideal time to hike in the "mountains"*. *

**5.**. Finland has very nice cities to visit: the capital Helsinki has a various activities from culture, shopping, sports and lovely cruises in the Baltic see. Don´t miss *Suomenlinna* a great fortress just outside Helsinki (very beautiful) and in the summer beaches are filled with hip young crowd to enjoy the day. Helsinki is a cool city which has everything when you know where to look. Other nice citys are f.e Turku and Tampere and their surroundigns.

**+ 6. make frieds with natives. English is very well spoken and natives can advice you how to make the most of you vacation. Finnish people seem shy but they are very lively and friendly nation**
