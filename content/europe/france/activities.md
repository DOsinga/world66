title: "Activities"

---

France rewards active travellers. The country has invested heavily in outdoor infrastructure over decades, and almost every region offers something worth doing beyond looking at old buildings.

**Hiking**

France has probably the best hiking infrastructure in the world, with over 140,000 km of marked footpaths. The Grande Randonnée (GR) network covers the entire country — more than 50 long-distance routes traversing everything from Breton coastline to Alpine glaciers to Pyrenean passes. The GR20 across Corsica is considered one of the finest long-distance routes in Europe. The Dordogne and [Ardèche](/europe/france/midi/ardeche) river valleys are perfect for shorter walks with spectacular scenery and minimal altitude.

**Skiing and Winter Sports**

The French Alps and Pyrenees both offer world-class skiing. The Alps have the highest resorts and the most extensive lift systems — Les Trois Vallées (Courchevel, Méribel, Val Thorens) is the largest interconnected ski area in the world. [Chamonix](/europe/france/alpes/chamonix), in the shadow of Mont Blanc, is the spiritual home of extreme skiing and mountaineering. The Pyrenees are smaller and less glamorous but less crowded and less expensive.

**Cycling**

The Tour de France isn't just a spectacle — it's a reflection of France's deep cycling culture. Dedicated cycling routes exist throughout the country. The Loire à Vélo is 900 km of cycling paths through the Loire Valley, passing châteaux and vineyards. The Vélodyssée runs 1,200 km from Roscoff in Brittany to Hendaye on the Spanish border along the Atlantic coast.

**Watersports**

The rivers of southern France — the Dordogne, Ardèche, Tarn, and Verdon — are ideal for canoeing and kayaking, with routes ranging from gentle family paddles to challenging white water. The Verdon Gorge is one of the most dramatic river canyons in Europe. The Atlantic coast around Biarritz has good surfing, and the Mediterranean coast offers sailing and diving.

**Rock Climbing and Bouldering**

The forest of Fontainebleau, south of Paris, is one of the world's finest bouldering destinations and where modern bouldering technique was essentially developed. The Calanques near Marseille offer spectacular sea cliff climbing. The Alps, of course, have everything from beginner crags to the most serious mountaineering in the world.
