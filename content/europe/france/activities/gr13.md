The GR 13 is a long-distance walking trail that cuts through the Morvan, the granite massif at the heart of Burgundy. The route runs roughly 200 km from [Auxerre](/europe/france/centre/auxerre) south through the Morvan Regional Natural Park to Autun. It passes through dense oak and beech forest, along rivers and reservoirs, and through quiet villages largely bypassed by tourist traffic.

The Morvan is one of the less-visited corners of Burgundy — green, hilly, and sparsely populated. The trail is well-marked with the standard red-and-white GR blazes. Walking the full route takes around ten days; shorter sections are easily accessible from towns along the way.
