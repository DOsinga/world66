Grenoble sits at the confluence of the Drac and Isere rivers, hemmed in by three mountain ranges — the Chartreuse, Vercors, and Belledonne. The setting is dramatic: snow-capped peaks visible from almost every street corner, the Bastille fortress looming above the old town. It hosted the 1968 Winter Olympics, and the outdoors still define the city's character.

The old town is compact and walkable, centred on Place Grenette and Place Saint-Andre. Medieval streets give way to 18th-century squares with terrace cafes. North of the Isere, the Saint-Laurent quarter has a bohemian edge — galleries, small restaurants, and the archaeological museum built on centuries of excavated remains.

Grenoble is also a serious university city, home to one of France's largest student populations. That means affordable restaurants, lively bars, and a cultural calendar that punches above its weight. The Musee de Grenoble holds one of France's best art collections outside Paris, and in summer the parks fill with festivals. Come for the mountains, but give the city itself a couple of days — it rewards the time.
