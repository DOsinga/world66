Grenoble sits at the confluence of the Drac and Isere rivers, hemmed in by three mountain ranges that give it one of the most dramatic settings of any French city. It is the gateway to the high Alps, a university town with a youthful energy, and a place where 2,000 years of history coexist with cutting-edge technology research.

The star attraction is La Bastille, a sprawling hilltop fortification reached by the famous cable cars known as "les bulles" (the bubbles). The views from the top sweep across the entire valley and the surrounding peaks. Below, the Old Town preserves medieval and Renaissance streets, the Gothic Palais de Justice, and the 11th-century Notre-Dame cathedral. The Musee de Grenoble houses an excellent art collection with works by Monet, Picasso, Matisse, and Warhol.

Grenoble is a natural base for skiing in winter and hiking or cycling in summer. Resorts like [Alpe d'Huez](/europe/france/alpes/alpdhuez) and Chamrousse are less than an hour away. The city itself has good parks, a lively cafe culture, and the Halle Sainte Claire indoor market for local cheeses, meats, and wines. Turin is only two hours by car, and Geneva about ninety minutes, making Grenoble an ideal hub for exploring the western Alps.
