---
title: "Getting Around in Grenoble"
type: section
---

Located in the French Alps, the city of Grenoble offers a wide
variety of places to visit ranging from ski slopes to museums to
historic ruins, and since the system of **Grenoble Transportation**
is vast, you will have no trouble getting around in this historic city.
The array of options includes trams, buses, cars, taxis and even
bicycles to transport visitors safely from place to place.

Grenoble is among the largest of French cities, complete with modern
accommodations, an economic soul and an extensive academic community.
Its historic old town area gives visitors a taste of the city’s
primitive history with its third century fortifications, called
Bastille Hill. Plan to visit the Musée Dauphinois, housed in a former
convent just adjacent to Bastille Hill. If a picnic is in order, head
over to Parc Paul Mistral where visitors can watch an exciting game of
street hockey or take in the charm of the beautifully manicured lawn
and stunning landscaping. Since many attractions throughout Grenoble
offer English translations, the language barrier will not be a problem
for those who do not speak French.

If skiing is on the agenda, visitors will find no shortage of
spectacular ski resorts, which is not surprising considering the city
is surrounded by a trio of mountain ranges. Favorite resorts include
Tignes and Val D’Isere and are within an hour’s driving distance of the
city. Climbing and hiking are also very popular activities because of
the terrain. Not surprisingly based on the popularity of winter sports
in the area, the tenth Olympic Winter games were hosted in Grenoble in
1968.
