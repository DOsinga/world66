---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Lyon is famously one of the best places to eat in France, and the food here divides neatly into two worlds. At one end, the **bouchons lyonnais** — small, boisterous, red-checked-tablecloth bistros serving traditional Lyonnais cooking: **salade lyonnaise** with frisée, lardons and poached egg, **quenelles de brochet** in sauce Nantua, **tablier de sapeur** (breaded tripe), **andouillette**, and **praline tart** for dessert. Look for the "authentique bouchon lyonnais" certification sticker. At the other end, Lyon is the historic heartland of French *haute cuisine* — Paul Bocuse opened his eponymous three-star in Collonges-au-Mont-d'Or just north of the city in the 1960s and set the template for a generation of chefs. More Michelin stars per capita than any other French city. In between, the covered food hall **Les Halles de Lyon Paul Bocuse** is the essential stop for cheese, charcuterie and the city's famous pralines roses.
