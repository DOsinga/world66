The white Byzantine-Romanesque basilica crowning Fourvière Hill, built in the late 19th century as a votive offering for the city's deliverance from plague (and, later, from Prussian invasion). The interior is a jewel-box of polychrome mosaics and gilded chapels. The real reason to come up is the panoramic view over Lyon from the esplanade — on clear days the Alps and even Mont Blanc are visible 200 km to the east. Take the steep **funicular** from Vieux Lyon up, walk down through the old Roman theatre ruins.
