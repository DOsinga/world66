The Lège-Cap Ferret peninsula (presqu'île) is a thin strip of land separating the Bassin d'Arcachon from the Atlantic Ocean, and it contains some of the finest ocean beaches in the southwest of France. On one side, the calm waters of the bassin are perfect for oysters and sailing; on the other, the open Atlantic delivers long, powerful surf.

The peninsula is a favourite retreat for well-off Bordelais and Parisians, who come for the pine forests, the quiet lanes, and the unhurried pace. The village of Cap Ferret at the tip has a lighthouse you can climb for sweeping views over the bassin and out to sea, and the oyster huts along the bassin shore are the best place to eat oysters washed down with cold white Bordeaux. Lège-Cap Ferret is best visited out of the July-August peak, when the single road becomes severely congested.
