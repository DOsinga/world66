Cannes is glamour on the French Riviera — a city that built its identity on celebrity, film festivals, and designer shopping, but still has the bones of a Provençal fishing town underneath. The annual Cannes Film Festival turns the Croisette into a global stage every May, but outside those two frantic weeks, Cannes is a surprisingly walkable place with good beaches, fine dining, and easy day trips along the coast.

The old quarter, Le Suquet, climbs the hill above the harbour with narrow lanes and views over the bay. Below it, Boulevard de la Croisette stretches along the waterfront past grand hotels and private beaches. The shopping here is high-end — Dior, Gucci, Sonia Rykiel — but rue d'Antibes a block inland offers more accessible retail. The Marché Forville morning market is the best place in town for Provençal produce and people-watching.

Offshore, the Lérins Islands make an easy half-day escape: Île Sainte-Marguerite has forests, a fort, and the Musée de la Mer; tiny Île Saint-Honorat is home to Cistercian monks and vineyards. Ferries leave from the Vieux Port.

Cannes sits just 16 miles from [Nice](/europe/france/nice), five miles from Mougins, and 11 miles from Grasse — the perfume capital. With the whole Côte d'Azur within day-trip range, the only problem is deciding where to go next.
