From the second you take your seat at a Cannes restaurant, the staff is there to greet you, inform you of the specialties, and take your order in the friendliest manner possible. This is what Cannes is all about — star treatment. Not only will you be treated like a star, whether the restaurant is grand or small, the cuisine is almost always excellent. Cannes restaurants offer everything from Provençal fare to cuisines from around the world. While the majority are expensive — remember, this is a very wealthy city — you can still find a few ranging from moderate to somewhat inexpensive.

### La Palme d'Or

La Palme d'Or is the number one choice for the see-and-be-seen crowd and those with deep pockets. Many celebrities dine here, especially during the International Cannes Film Festival. The restaurant serves the finest modern French cuisine in the region and features some of the finest views, with terraces overlooking the sea. Open Sunday and Tuesday–Friday. Lunch: 12:30–14:30. Dinner: 20:00–22:30. Hotel Martinez, 73 La Croisette. Tel: +33 04 92 98 74 14.

### La Canna Suisse

Want to try something other than French food? How about Swiss? This moderately priced restaurant features tasty fondue and traditional Swiss dishes like Raclette — melted cheese over boiled potatoes with pepper, pickled onions, and gherkins — and Tartiflette, a hearty dish of cheese, potatoes, and bacon. The restaurant offers an impressive list of French and Swiss wines. 23 rue Forville-le Suquet. Tel: +33 04 93 99 01 27.

### Le Loft

This hip Asian eatery serves everything from sushi and tempura to Japanese-style steak and Asian dishes with a French twist. The restaurant also has a disco upstairs, so if you want a quiet meal, get here early — the club really gets going between 22:00 and 23:00. 13 rue du Dr. Monod. Tel: +33 04 93 39 40 39.
