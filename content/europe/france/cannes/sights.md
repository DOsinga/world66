Cannes has plenty of sights beyond its famous beaches and boulevards — churches, islands, museums, and monuments are all worth exploring.

### Île Sainte-Marguerite

Home of the Musée de la Mer, Île Sainte-Marguerite has plenty of sites to explore and numerous recreational opportunities. The museum highlights artifacts, paintings, mosaics, and ceramics from Arab, Ligurian, and Roman civilisations. As soon as you leave the dock, you can begin a walking tour of the island. Open April–September Tuesday–Sunday 10:30–17:45, October–March Tuesday–Sunday 10:30–16:45. Fort Royal. Tel: +33 04 93 38 55 26.

### Musée de la Castre

Open since 1952, the Musée de la Castre features Mediterranean artifacts from Egypt, Greece, Syria, and Rome, primitive art from Africa, Asia, and the Americas, musical instruments from around the world, and 19th-century Cannes landscapes. Le Suquet. Tel: +33 04 93 38 55 26.

### Notre Dame de Bon Voyage

This 1879 church is probably the most notable in Cannes. The faux-Gothic style is a sight in itself, and the entrance was designed by artist Roger Capron. Square Mérimée. Tel: +33 04 93 39 16 22.

### Île Saint-Honorat

Sunbathing, strolling, and sightseeing — Île Saint-Honorat offers all of this on an island only one mile long, the second largest of the Lérins Islands. Points of interest include the Abbaye de Saint-Honorat, home to medieval ruins, early 20th-century ecclesiastical structures, and some 30 Cistercian monks. Visitors can hike through the island's pine forests. Smoking is not allowed on the island.

### Plage de la Croisette

Home to Cannes's most magnificent and most expensive beach, stretching between Vieux Port and Port Canto. Entrance fees range from €15–22 and include loungers, mattresses, showers, parasols, and snack kiosks. The beach also offers easy access to spots where you can dine in your bathing suit.
