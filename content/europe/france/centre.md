[Chartres](/europe/france/centre/chartres) is both an ideal daytrip from Paris and a good first step to visit central France. The city itself has one of the most beautiful medieval cathedrals in the world and more wonders of the world are close at hand.


 The [Loire valley](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley) has higher number of castles per square kilometer than any other region in the world. Drive along the river from [Orleans](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/orleans) to [Tours](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/tours) and you will see them everywhere. Manors, small castles, big castles. Most of them masterpieces of Renaissance art such as [Chambord](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/chambord), [Usse](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/usse), [Blois](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/blois) or [Amboise](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/amboise).
 

Just south of the Loire region, the [Limousin](/europe/france/centre/limousin) on the western slopes of the Massif Central attracts visitors in search of unspoiled countryside. The Corrèze, Creuse and Haute Vienne contain hills and gorges, and lush green meadows. Numerous ancient village churches dot the landscape as well as more imposing abbey churches and fortresses. [Limoges](/europe/france/centre/limousin/limoges) is the most important city in this region


 A bit further to the east, the [Auvergne](/europe/france/centre/auvergne) with its awe-inspiring non-active volcanoes, lakes, rivers and forests makes it a wonderful destination for the lover of nature. The capital [Clermont-Ferrant](/europe/france/centre/auvergne/clermontferrant) has many historic sights.
