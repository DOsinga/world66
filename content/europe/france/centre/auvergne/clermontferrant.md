Clermont-Ferrand sits on a plateau surrounded by the volcanic peaks of the Chaîne des Puys, a landscape unlike anything else in France. The city itself is built from dark Volvic lava stone, giving it a striking, almost brooding character — especially the Gothic cathedral, which rises above the old town in deep black basalt.

The historic centre is compact and walkable, centred on the Place de Jaude and the narrow streets around the cathedral and the Romanesque Basilique Notre-Dame du Port, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Clermont-Ferrand is a university city with a lively café culture, good restaurants serving Auvergnat specialities like truffade and Saint-Nectaire cheese, and a surprisingly vibrant nightlife for its size.

The real draw, though, is what surrounds it. The Puy de Dôme — the highest of the volcanic domes — is a short drive away and offers panoramic views across the entire chain. The Vulcania theme park, the Parc des Volcans d'Auvergne, and dozens of hiking trails are all within easy reach. If you are interested in outdoor pursuits — hiking, paragliding, mountain biking — Clermont-Ferrand is one of the best bases in central France.
