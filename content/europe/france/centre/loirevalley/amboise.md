Amboise sits on the Loire 25 km east of [Tours](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/tours), a small town with an outsized history. The chateau looms over everything — one of the first true Renaissance buildings in France, built by Louis XII and later used by Francois I, who invited Leonardo da Vinci to spend his final years here. Leonardo is buried in the castle's Saint-Hubert chapel.

The old town below the castle is compact and walkable. The Tour de l'Horloge (14th century) marks the entrance to the market district, and the Church of Saint-Denis dates back to the 4th century. A short walk south of town, the Pagode de Chanteloup is the surviving folly of an 18th-century chateau — a Chinese-style pagoda rising 44 metres above ornamental gardens.

Yes, it's touristy. Amboise gets day-trippers from Tours and overnight visitors following the Loire Valley chateau trail. Come in the evening or early morning if you want the castle to yourself.
