---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Chambord is essentially a single sight — the chateau itself — but it is vast enough to fill a full day. The main areas to explore inside the castle:

**The Keep** — The central building, with its famous double-helix spiral staircase. The terraces on top give a panoramic view over the entire estate.

**Francis I Suite** — Francis moved his living quarters to the eastern wing, though he died before they were finished. The suite includes a king's hall taking up an entire floor, two cabinets, a bed chamber, and a small oratory.

**Chapel** — Linked to the keep by a gallery, this chapel was built by Louis XIV. It occupies two full floors and is surprisingly large.

**Apartments of Louis XIV** — In the keep. Two quarters were combined by partitioning the north vestibule, following the etiquette that required the royal suite to be at the centre of the main facade.

**Museum of the Comte de Chambord** — On the first floor, containing memorabilia of the last private owner of the chateau.

**Museum of Hunting** — Francis I was as passionate about hunting as about building. The museum covers the history of hunting in the region, with tapestries, paintings, sculptures from the 16th to 18th centuries, and hunting weapons.
