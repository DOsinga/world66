The Château de Valençay rivals [Chambord](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/chambord) in scale but draws a fraction of the visitors — a real advantage if you prefer your Loire châteaux without the crowds. The building is a handsome blend of Renaissance and classical styles, set above beautifully designed English-style gardens with formal parterres, a deer park, and a children's playground.

The castle was the residence of Talleyrand, Napoleon's brilliant and slippery foreign minister, who bought it in 1803 at the emperor's insistence — Napoleon wanted his chief diplomat to have a suitably impressive address for receiving foreign dignitaries. The interior is furnished from the period and gives a vivid sense of early 19th-century aristocratic life. The town of Valençay itself is also known for its goat's cheese, which carries its own AOC designation.
