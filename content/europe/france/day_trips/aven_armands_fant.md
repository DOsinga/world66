In the south east of France (in the Unesco World Heitage’s listed Site of Lozère’s French region), an amazing cave is nettled under the ground. This cave called Aven Armand has recenty been awarded 3 stars by the Michelin tourism Guide. This fantastic site is situated on the ‘Mejan Causse’, itself situated about an hour and 30 minutes drive from Montpellier’s train station and International airoport.

With easy access alowed by a funicular, 100 meters under the ground, you will dicover one of the most natural jewel of speological history. The room, that could potentialy accomadate Paris’s Notre Dame big Cathedral, hosts an unique and breath-taking forest of more than 400 stalagmites. These impressive stalagmites exeeds a meter high, and the tallest one of them all that reaches 30 meters high, lands a world record for being the highest stalagmite ever discovered in the history of under-world.

The 45 minutes guided Tour stops to 8 strategigic places in the cave, six of which add sound and light to add to the magic. Projected images project colourful waterfalls, strange plants and giant crystals on the walls and on the laced stone stalagmites that has been built drop by drop over thousands of years.

The highest part of cave, from where you can overview the totality of the stalagmite’s forest is accessible to people with reduced mobility.
The Aven Armand’s site also provides a shop and a snack restaurant. Less than 2 miles away from there, the village Hures la Parade and then a few miles further in the town of Meyrueis, you will find all accommodations to eat, shop and sleep.
To learn more about the Aven Armand’s cave please visit the website.
