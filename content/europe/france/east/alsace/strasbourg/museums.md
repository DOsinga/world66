---
title: "Museums in Strasbourg"
type: section
---

The charming city of Strasbourg, France
attracts countless visitors each year. Within easy reach from many
European cities it offers an endless array of activities including many
world class museums which is sure to please even the most
discriminating traveler.

**MUSEE ALSACIEN**

This museum features typical family life back in the 18th and 19th centuries. Through painstaking effort it has managed to
acquire countless authentic mementos ranging from period clothing,
toys, furniture, pottery and stunning paintings. It is located at 23-25
Quai Saint Nicholas. The hours of operations are open every day except
Tuesday from 12-6. The cost is 4 euros and under 18 is free.

**MUSEE DES BEAUX-ARTS**

The Beaux-Arts houses the paintings of the masters, those painters
whose works have stood the test of time and are more appreciated today
than when they were alive. Some of these men were, Raphael, Rubens,
Rousseau and Goya. There are over 850 works on permanent display. On an
interesting side note, the stunning Barouqe building used to house the
collection is just as impressive as the paintings themselves. The
museum is open every day except Tuesday, 10-6 and costs 4 euros. The
address is Palais Rohan -2, Place du Chateau.

**THE MUSEE D'ART MODERNE ET CONTEMPORAIN DE STRASBOURG**

This is a thoroughly modern museum that houses an impressive
collection of period pieces consisting of sculptures, paintings and
graphic arts. Representing the Impressionism period it starts from 1870
and continues into modern times. Some of its most important exhibits
are teh collections of Gustave Dore, Jean Arp, Victor Brauner. Other
notable works include pieces by Monet, Rodin and Picasso. One major
ongoing exhibit is a retrospective by Hubard Severine Village. This
will be featured for the next nine months. It is located at 1 Place
Hans and opens every day except Tuesday. The hours are 10-6 and costs 5
euros. So if you are ever in Strasbourg make time to explore some of
its intersting and charming museums. You never know what you may
encounter!
