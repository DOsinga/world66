Hattonchâtel is a fortified village perched on a rocky promontory overlooking the Woëvre plain in [Lorraine](/europe/france/east/lorraine). It is named after Hatton, a 9th-century Bishop of Verdun who built a palace here. Cardinal Richelieu had the buildings destroyed in 1634, and the village fell into ruin.

The place was rescued in the 1920s by Belle Skinner, a wealthy American who fell in love with the village while visiting the World War I battlefields and funded its restoration. What you find today is a charming cluster of stone houses, a restored château (now a hotel), a 14th-century cloister, and views across the Lac de Madine to the Meuse hills.
