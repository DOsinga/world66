A small hilltop village in the Meuse department of Lorraine, Hattonchâtel sits on a rocky spur with wide views over the Saint-Mihiel salient. The name comes from a 9th-century bishop of Verdun who built a palace here. Cardinal Richelieu had it demolished in 1634, and the village remained largely forgotten until the early 20th century, when American philanthropist Belle Skinner fell in love with it and funded a substantial restoration.

What Skinner restored is now the village's main draw: a château, a collegiate church with notable Renaissance choir stalls, and a small museum. The surrounding landscape is agricultural Lorraine, quiet and unhurried, with the World War I battlefields of Saint-Mihiel and Verdun within easy reach.

Hattonchâtel makes a good half-day stop if you're travelling through Lorraine or visiting the WWI sites of the Meuse.
