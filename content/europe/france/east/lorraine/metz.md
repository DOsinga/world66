Metz is one of the most beautiful cities of the East of France. The 15th-16th century St. Louis Square with vaulted gallery and arches is an abslote highlight. In the center of town you also find many medieval mansions, some of which can be visited from the inside.

Metz is also a city of churches. The Saint Etienne Cathedral is world famous for its stained glass windows by Chagall. The 4th century Saint-Pierre-aux Nonnains is the oldest church in all of France. Finally the Saint Maximin church with stained glass windows by Cocteau deserves mention.

Those coming by train can enjoy the monumental railway stations built during German occupation.
