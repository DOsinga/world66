Nancy is one of the finest cities in eastern France, a former ducal capital whose centre is built around the magnificent Place Stanislas — a UNESCO World Heritage Site of gilded wrought-iron gates, fountains, and grand neoclassical facades. It is one of the most beautiful squares in Europe, and reason enough to visit.

Beyond Stanislas, Nancy is a city of two halves: the medieval Vieille Ville (old town) with its narrow streets and the more spacious Ville Neuve (new town) built by the dukes of Lorraine. The city is also one of the capitals of Art Nouveau, with houses, shops, and villas from the Ecole de Nancy school of design scattered through the residential quarters. The Musee de l'Ecole de Nancy, in a converted villa, shows the best of the movement.

Just outside the city, the chateau of [Fleville](/europe/france/east/lorraine/fleville) is one of the finest Renaissance castles in the east of France and makes for a pleasant half-day excursion.
