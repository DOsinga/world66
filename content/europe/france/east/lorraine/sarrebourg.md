Sarrebourg is a small town in the Moselle valley of Lorraine, close to the Alsatian border. It's an unhurried place without obvious tourist ambitions — which makes its single great attraction all the more striking. The Chapel of the Cordeliers houses a monumental stained-glass window designed by Marc Chagall in 1976: twelve metres wide and ten metres high, in deep blues and purples depicting themes of peace, it's one of the finest examples of 20th-century religious art in France.

The town also has a decent regional museum covering the Gallo-Roman heritage of the area, and the surrounding countryside — the Pays de Sarrebourg, edging into the Vosges — is pleasant cycling territory. But be honest: most people come for the Chagall, as they should.
