The French produce more different kinds of cheese than any other country. A system of "Appelation Controle" applies just like for the wines. Here are some regional highlights.

**Restaurants**

**Roquefort**

One of the best blue cheeses of France. It is produced entirely from the milk of the ewes that feed on the vast plateaux in the Aveyron , in the [Languedoc](/europe/france/midi/languedoc)Languedoc .

**Camembert de Normandie**

True Camembert comes from only five departments in Normandy. Its origin dates . It's been an AOC cheese since 1996.

**St. Nectaire**

This is a semi-soft, disc-shaped cheese from Auvergne, made from milk of Salers cows that feed on the very rich and perfumed volcanic pasture lands of the uplands of the Auvergne region of France.
