Saint-Nectaire is a semi-soft, disc-shaped cheese from Auvergne, made from the milk of Salers cows that graze the volcanic pastures of the Massif Central. The fermier (farm-made) version carries a green oval label — the telltale sign you're getting the real thing rather than a factory product.

After pressing, the wheels are aged in old stone cellars on beds of rye straw for eight weeks. That maturation in damp, earthy surroundings gives Saint-Nectaire its distinctive mushroomy, slightly nutty flavour — it genuinely tastes like the landscape it comes from.

You'll find it on almost every cheese board in the Auvergne. Pair it with a glass of local Saint-Pourçain wine or simply eat it with bread and nothing else.
