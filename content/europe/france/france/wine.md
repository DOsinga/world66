No other country produces as much wine as France. [Spain](/europe/spain) and [Italy](/europe/italy) are good runner ups, but France is still the uncontested number one wine producing country. 

Here we give you a short overview of different regions where you can find great wines. More info about the specific regions is found on the pages dedicated to those regions.

**all**

**Loire valley**

The Loire valley produces great white and good red wines. Most of the vineyards are to be found around the city of [Tours](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley/tours) . The Pouilly Fume (a white wine) is the most famous.

**Champagne**

The one and only Champage, comes from a region in the east of France called [Champagne](/europe/france/east/champagne). [Reims](/europe/france/east/champagne/reims), the capital of the region is the place to go if you are into bubb [more..](/europe/france/wine/champagne)

**Bordeaux**

Famous especially for its reds, but not without its notable whites, Bourdeaux´s subtle wines have a world wide following.

**Burgundy**

Both deep rich reds and light fruity whites come from the Burgundy region.
