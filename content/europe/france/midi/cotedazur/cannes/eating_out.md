---
title: "Eating Out in Cannes"
type: section
---

Cannes eats well, and expensively. The Croisette end of the city is dominated by hotel restaurants and places calibrated for Film Festival expense accounts — La Palme d'Or at the Martinez is the local benchmark for special-occasion dining, with a menu built around seasonal Provençal produce and fine seafood. Reservations are essential and prices match the setting.

Away from the seafront, the streets behind the Marché Forville and around Le Suquet offer better value. The covered market itself is worth a morning visit for olives, cheese, and charcuterie. A number of smaller bistros in this area serve honest regional cooking — ratatouille, soupe de poisson, grilled fish — at prices that feel more like the south of France and less like a film premiere.
