---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Marseille"
type: section
---

One of the things not to be missed while visiting Marseille are the **Marseille
Bars and Cafes**. When visiting Marseille during the afternoon you
might want to stop by a café and grab a mid-afternoon snack or a nice
cup of coffee. During the early evening hours or even late at night, the
many bars in Marseille are a great place to go to sit back and relax
while enjoying some music or other form of entertainment. If you choose to visit any Marseilles bars and cafes, you want to make sure
you are visiting the top spots in the area.

**Le Trolleybus**

If you are looking for
something to do in the evening, this is the best place to go, and it is also one
of the busiest places you can go. This bar literally has something for
everybody, so people of all ages will find this a delightful
experience. Inside The Trolley you have the choice of visiting four
different rooms. Each room has something different to offer, including a
whiskey bar, a bowling area and areas that can host concerts or even
product launches.

**Le Crystal**

This restaurant is located in the
Old Port section of Marseille, and is usually visited by a younger
crowd. Most of the younger crowd comes in to Le Crystal because it is a great meeting
spot for their group before going out to the other nightclubs in town.
This place is not known for its food, but is a great place to go and
grab a drink with friends, as there are plenty of cocktails to choose
from.

**La Trilogie des Cepages
**

One of the best features of this
nice restaurant is that it offers over 950 wines and 150 different kinds
of cheeses. Depending on when you come and visit the café you can also
take part in their wine tasting. Since the timing varies, be sure to check
out the website for exact dates and times. This restaurant is more suitable for an older crowd than a younger crowd, but nobody will be turned
away.

**O’Stop**

This café is where you will find
people recuperating from being out on the town all night, and is one of
the few cafes in town that is open all day and night. The biggest
problem with this café is that it only seats 12 people so you will have
to wait, but that will give you plenty of time to people watch, and
O’Stop attracts all sorts.

**Nomad’ Café**

One of the main attractions to
this place is the concerts featured here on occasion, which only take
place after dark. Aside from the concerts, if you are looking for a
relaxing atmosphere that has a large selection of teas and coffees, this
is the place to go. Here you can find different teas and coffees from
different places around the world, so one will be sure to match your
taste.
