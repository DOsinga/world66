Take bus 81 from the Vieux-Port or walk twenty minutes up from Noailles to the [Palais Longchamp](/europe/france/midi/cotedazur/marseille/palaislongchamp). Arrive around two o'clock. The palace is at its best in afternoon light — the fountain cascade catches the sun from the west and the whole thing glows a warm honey colour. Walk around it slowly before going into either museum; the exterior is the main event.

The Musée des Beaux-Arts in the left wing has a solid collection of Provençal and Flemish painting, plus some surprisingly good 17th-century Italian work, and the interior of the wing is almost as impressive as the outside. The natural history museum on the right is more old-fashioned — glass cases of taxidermied animals, whale skeletons — but has a certain dusty charm. Admission to both is cheap.

After the museum, take the gardens behind the palace: a long formal park that climbs the hill in terraces, with plane trees and benches, popular with families in the afternoon. A good place to read or do nothing.

From Longchamp, walk or take a bus down to [Cours Julien](/europe/france/midi/cotedazur/marseille/coursjulien) — about fifteen minutes on foot. The cours is the bohemian heart of the city: a slightly sloping square surrounded by bars, record shops, vintage clothing stores, and a bookshop or two. The walls of the streets leading into it are covered in murals by local and international street artists. Pick a terrace for a glass of wine or a pression and watch the square fill up as the afternoon shades into evening.

If you're hungry, there are good inexpensive restaurants on and around the cours — Moroccan, Vietnamese, and a handful of French bistros that change their menu by the week.
