**Nice day trips **allow you to see more of what the country of
France and the continent of Europe has to offer--without taking a lot
of time away from your trip to the beautiful shores of the
Mediterranean Sea. In fact, other destinations along the Mediterranean
coast make popular day trip destinations from Nice.

**Explore More of the French Riviera in Cannes**

The home of the world-famous International Cannes Film Festival is
only a 35-minute drive from Nice, or you can take the convenient train
that runs between Nice and Cannes in 30 minutes. Fare costs only around
€5 (US$7) each way. If you visit in May, you can get a glimpse of
world-famous actors and directors at the Cannes Film Festival, but
there is plenty to see and do the rest of the year as well.

There is a number of popular museums in Cannes, including the Musee de la Mer
(Museum of the Sea), which is located on the Saint Marguerite Island a
little over half a mile away from the Cannes coast. You take a
15-minute boat ride to the island, where you can tour the famed fortress that held Alexandre Dumas' fictional Man in the Iron Mask for 11 years. The fortress/museum shows archaeological finds of shipwrecked and lost shops and as well as examples of maritime life in the Bay of Biscay.

**Spend Time in Marseille (Marseilles)

**

Another lovely destination along the French Riveria is Marseille, which you can drive to in about 2 hours from Nice. Perhaps more convenient is the train that runs between Nice and Marseille in 2 1/2 hours and costs €30 (US$40) each way. The third most populous metropolitan city in France, Marseille has plenty to offer for tourists visiting for a day.

Another famous fictional Alexandre Dumas character, the Count of Monte Cristo, has a connection to the Chateau d'If, a large fortress that takes up virtually the entirety of the small island of If off the coast of Marseille. There are many other archaeological sites to visit, such as La Vielle Charite. Fashion enthusiasts might like touring the Musee de la Mode, which displays the history of modern fashion over the past 30 years.

**Visit Ventimiglia in Italy**

Nice is so close to the Italian border that taking day trips to the
neighboring country can take less time than visiting other areas in
France. Ventimiglia, Italy, is another city on the Mediterranean Sea coastline that's only a 40-minute drive from Nice. There's also a train that takes passengers directly between Nice and Ventimiglia in about 45 minutes. Round-trip tickets cost about €20 (US$27).

Ventimiglia is renowned for its old-word architecture, particularly as seen in churches and cathedrals such as the Cattedrale dell’Assunta di Ventimiglia and the San Michele Arcangelo. The Ventimiglia
market is another popular stop with tourists, who can purchase fresh
foods, wines, locally-made crafts and souvenirs. Stop by the Balzi Rossi Caves Museum for a tour of natural rock caves and a display of historical artifacts.
