---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in St Tropez"
type: section
---

A groovy party destination that sets the pace for undiluted music
and madness throughout the night, St. Tropez is a chic place to rub
shoulders with the crème de la crème of Cote d'Azur. **The St. Tropez nightlife** guide gives visitors comprehensive information on the party scene and best places to visit in this spunky town.

**Description**

St. Tropez’s nightlife can leave a new visitor baffled from its sheer
array of choices. The indecisive streak in tourists is natural
considering the assemblage of some of the most pulsating and exciting
nightclubs, gay bars, dance floors and live performance bars. A night
in this buzzing town can be very expensive, however given its
dexterity in entertaining its visitors, the smart ones can find some
moderately priced destinations as well. St. Tropez is as much a charmer
for trendy youngsters as it is for a slightly more old fashioned and
placid lot. There are numerous quaint Café bars that line up prettily
alongside the more reckless nightclubs.

**The Best Places to Party**

Café de Paris is a nice ‘old-world’ charm radiating place that is
flocked by tourists preferring a more unruffled nocturnal rendezvous.
This charming hotspot is housed at the port with a sprawling open
terrace and an expansive zinc bar that provides an impeccable backdrop
for calmly witnessing the frenzy of the bustling cafe and the celebrity
clientele encircling it. Visitors here exude an unperturbed spirit and
cool demeanor while watching the world buzz by. Most night clubs
require visitors to adhere to a formal dress code, so it is pragmatic
to check in advance, and there are a few clubs that restrict access to
an all male group as well.

If you want to go celebrity spotting in St. Tropez, head to La
Bodega de Papagayo, which will cost you a bomb but will allow you an
opportunity to hobnob with some of the most famous faces on St.
Tropez’s party scene. For an intimate setting and brilliant live
performances, Octave Café rules. Go there for their pureblooded jazz
performances. For more upscale options, consider Les Caves du roy,
located in the lobby of Hotel Byblos at Paul-Signac, a place
frequented by the rich and famous for its glitzy décor and exclusive
concoctions. Other high profile haunts include Le Papagayo and Le VIP
Room. Some classic gay nightlife options are Le Pigeonnier and Chez
Maggi. For a more teeny bopping crowd, go to Le Bar du Port- a
self-confessed hip joint that is flashy and throbbing with effervescent
energy.

St. Tropez‘s nightlife is a nice medley of charming old-fashioned cafe culture and a hyperactive party scene.
