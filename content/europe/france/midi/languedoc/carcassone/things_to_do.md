Carcassonne's main draw is the Cité — the medieval walled fortress on the hill above the modern town. Beyond the ramparts, the Aude river offers kayaking, and there's a golf course nearby for those who want a break from history.

**The Cité**

The medieval walled city — 52 towers, two concentric rings of ramparts, and 3 kilometres of walls — is among the best-preserved fortifications in Europe. Freely walkable; the castle (Château Comtal) requires a ticket. The alleyways inside the walls are crowded in summer but atmospheric in the early morning or evening.

category: Sight

**Basilica of Saints Nazarius and Celsus**

The jewel of the Cité — a Romanesque and Gothic basilica begun in 1096. The barrel-vaulted nave dates from the original 11th-century construction; the Gothic transepts were added in the 13th and 14th centuries. The tomb of Bishop Pierre de Rochefort (14th century) is the finest sculpture inside.

category: Sight

**Saint-Louis Bastide**

The lower town on the left bank, built on a grid plan in the 13th century by Louis IX. Less visited than the Cité but worth a wander — the main square (place Carnot), the churches, and the 17th–18th-century merchant houses reflect the prosperity of the old cloth trade.

category: Sight

**Kayaking on the Aude**

The Aude river has good kayaking. Contact La Boulal (11290 Alairac, tel: 04 68 26 93 56) for rentals and guided trips.

category: Activity

**Golf**

The Domaine d'Auriac golf course is set in pleasant countryside south of the city.

tel: 04 68 72 57 30
