title: "Things to Do in Carcassonne"
type: section
---

Carcassonne's Cite is the main attraction — a medieval fortress city you can walk through freely. Beyond the walls, the Aude river and surrounding countryside offer outdoor activities.

### [Romanesque Cathedral](/europe/france/midi/languedoc/carcassonne/sights/romanesquecathedral)

The jewel of the Cite. Construction began in 1096 and was completed before 1130, though only the barrel-vaulted nave with its semi-circular side aisles survives from the Romanesque period. Towards the end of the thirteenth century the original apse was replaced by a Gothic transept and choir.

### [Tomb of Bishop Pierre de Rochefort](/europe/france/midi/languedoc/carcassonne/sights/tombofbishoppierrederochefort)

Inside the cathedral, the tomb of Bishop Pierre de Rochefort dates from the fourteenth century and is the building's finest sculpture — a full half-century older than Bishop Radulphe's tomb nearby.

### [Kayaking on the Aude](/europe/france/midi/languedoc/carcassonne/activities/kayaking)

The Aude river has good kayaking options around Carcassonne. Try La Boulal in Alaigne (04 68 26 93 56).

### [Golf at Domaine d'Auriac](/europe/france/midi/languedoc/carcassonne/activities/golf)

The Carcassonne Golf Club is set in the grounds of the Domaine d'Auriac on the Route de Saint-Hilaire (04 68 72 57 30).
