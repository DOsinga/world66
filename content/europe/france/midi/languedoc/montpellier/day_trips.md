---
title: "Day Trips in Montpellier"
type: section
---

Montpellier France is situated in Southern France in the Languedoc
Region near the Provence Region. There are many things to see outside
of the city only a few hours away.

**Nimes**

The town of Nimes is about 32 miles from Montpellier and is on the
border of the two regions. There is a great deal of beautiful Roman
Architecture in the city including an amphitheater, temple, and Roman Bath. The city also boasts several
museums that depict history and showcase contemporary art.

**Sete**

The beautiful town of Sete is only 19 miles from Montpellier and it
is almost right on the Mediterranean. A series of canals link it with
the sea giving it the nickname of ‘Venice of Languedoc’. Here you will
find a more social atmosphere with many fine restaurants and some
tourist attractions. St. Louis Pier is a good place to see large and
small yachts or watch the fishermen unload their catch.

**Palavas les Flots**

The fishing village of Palavas les Flots is 6 miles from Montpellier
and it is not just a fishing village anymore. It has been converted
into a seaside resort with the family in mind. There is about 4 miles
of beautiful sand filled beaches. Make sure to see the Phare de la
Mediterranee that used to be a water tower and is now a modern
conference and tourist center with a restaurant that revolves so you
can see the view for miles at the tip top.

**Aigues Mortes**

Another day trip away is the town of Aigues Mortes which sits on the
coast. This town is historic because the walls that were built in
ancient times still stand. There are salt marshes on the outskirts and
are the oldest in the Mediterranean because the town resisted urban
redevelopment. You can tour the marshes to see the animals and plants
native to the area.

**St. Guilhem le Desert**

If you have ever wondered what life was like during the medieval
time visit St. Guilhem le Desert on a day trip from Montpellier. The
narrow streets have many houses that date back to those times. The
abbey is a UNESCO World Heritage site and part of the pilgrimage route
of Santiago de Compostela. During the summer the village opens
boutiques that sell local crafts. There is a beautiful gorge in the
area and it is the perfect place to go hiking along the river.
