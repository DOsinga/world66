Montpellier is a surprisingly green city, with historic gardens, riverside promenades, and wild parkland all within easy reach. Here are the best places to stretch your legs.

## The Botanical Garden

France's oldest botanical garden, created in 1593 under Henry IV for the university's medical students. It covers 4.5 hectares and includes tropical greenhouses, a 19th-century orangery, and a lovely English garden tucked behind the formal beds. A peaceful escape right in the city center.

## Promenade du Peyrou

One of Montpellier's most iconic spots, perched at the western edge of the old town. The broad terrace centers on an equestrian statue of Louis XIV, framed by the Arc de Triomphe at one end and the elegant 17th-century water tower at the other. The views stretch to the Pic Saint-Loup on clear days. Come for the Sunday flea market.

## Esplanade Charles de Gaulle

The plane-tree-lined promenade running from Place de la Comédie to the Corum conference center. Fountains, benches, and playgrounds make it a natural gathering spot. The adjacent Champ de Mars hosts visiting fairs and circuses.

## Les Rives du Lez

The banks of the River Lez have been transformed into a pleasant walking and cycling corridor linking the Antigone district to Port Marianne and beyond. Tree-lined paths, pedestrian bridges, and waterside cafés make it one of the city's best urban walks.

## Parc du Domaine de Méric

A beautiful estate that once belonged to the family of Impressionist painter Frédéric Bazille. The grounds run along the Lez with shaded paths and open lawns. Open daily, with shorter hours in winter.

## Domaine de Grammont

A 90-hectare estate near the eastern entrance to Montpellier, home to a theater, concert hall, and sports facilities. The parkland itself is good for a long rambling walk away from the city bustle.

## Lunaret Zoological Park

A free zoo set in thick Mediterranean garrigue north of the city, shaded by old pine trees. Home to around 145 animal species, it also has walking trails through the surrounding scrubland. Open daily from 9:00, closed Monday mornings from mid-September to mid-May.

## Domaine de Restinclières

A grand country estate between Prades-le-Lez and Saint-Mathieu-de-Tréviers, about 15 minutes north of the city. Discovery circuits, hiking trails, formal gardens, and play areas make it a solid half-day outing, especially with kids.
