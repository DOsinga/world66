---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

The old town is the sight. Hôtel Jacques Coeur (with its gargoyles) and Hôtel de Wicque (a Renaissance courtyard) are the two most photographed buildings, but almost every block has a doorway worth stopping for. The Collégiale Saint-Jean church, 18th century, has a notable pipe organ. The old Penitents Noirs chapel became a theater in the 19th century and is still used for performances. The town is listed as a historic monument in its entirety — any guided tour from the tourist office is worthwhile for access and context.
