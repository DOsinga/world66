title: "When to Go in Pezenas"
type: section
---

When visiting **Pezenas, when to go **is just as important an
issue as deciding where to go or how to get there. Before you make your
reservation, review what you can expect during each season so you can
choose the right time of year to go.

**Spring**

Spring is the second most popular season for tourists to Pezenas. Average temperatures are between 40 and 70 degrees F
and rainfall is at its highest, particularly in April and May. Spring
is an ideal time, particularly during the later months, to go boating
or practice water sports because the crowds haven't quite peaked but
the temperatures are still pleasant enough to be outdoors for longer
periods of time. Do pack a light coat and prepare to dress in layers so
you can easily peel on or off clothes as the temperature changes. If
you plan to visit during spring, particularly if you hope to spend time
on the sea, packing water-repellent clothes is a good idea.

**Summer**

Summer alongside the Mediterranean is such an appealing idea that you're sure to find the most crowds in Pezenas during the June through August. Temperatures average between 60 and 85 degrees F, so the air is hot but not scorching, although it can be rather humid and dry in Pezenas
during the summer due to somewhat low rainfall. Summer is the ideal
time for outdoor activities, particularly lying on the beach, swimming
and water sports, but you may have to fight for space. Pack t-shirts and shorts, but don't forget a light jacket for cooler evenings and indoor entertainment.

**Autumn**

Autumn can be an ideal time for people who still want to participate
in water sports but don't want to deal with large crowds to visit. The
flora isn't as appealing as it is in spring, but otherwise you'll have
similar temperatures to enjoy and fewer people with whom to compete for
space on the water and on the beach. Temperatures range from 45 to 75
degrees F, with the season starting out hotter and getting progressively cooler. Rainfall
is fairly high, particularly in October and November, although not as
bad as it is during the spring. Pack multiple types of breathable
clothing so you can dress in layers.

**Winter**

As Pezenas is in the south of France along the Mediterranean coastline, winter in Pezenas
if quite mild, but it can still be cool enough to make participating in
water sports unlikely. Expect temperatures between 35 and 55 degrees F with moderate amounts of rainfall and only the very occasional
light snowfall. Bringing lightweight warm clothes, like a thermal
jacket, as well as a hat and gloves can make even outdoor activities,
like touring the city, quite bearable. Plus, you won't have to fight so many crowds at the hottest tourist spots because winter is the season in which the fewest tourists arrive.
