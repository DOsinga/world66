The largest remaining Romanesque church in Europe, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 11th-12th centuries in pale brick, it has a soaring nave with five aisles, elaborate carved stone capitals, and a famous portal carved with scenes of the Last Judgement. The crypt holds relics of Saint Saturninus and various apostles. Free entry to the nave; small fee for the crypt and ambulatory.
