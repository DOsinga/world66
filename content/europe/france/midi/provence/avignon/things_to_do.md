Avignon's headline attraction is the Palais des Papes, but the city has much more to offer. The medieval walls are remarkably intact, and the famous broken bridge carries centuries of legend.

**Pont d'Avignon (Pont Saint-Benezet)**

The ruined bridge near the Palais des Papes was built in the Middle Ages, possibly to allow the local bishop to cross the river to Villeneuve-les-Avignon. Legend says a shepherd named Benezet was inspired by angels to build it, and when the town authorities refused, he picked up a vast stone and hurled it into the river as a foundation. Convinced by this demonstration of divine will, the bridge was quickly built.

The bridge was never very durable. Following numerous floods, most of it collapsed. Of the original 22 arches reaching across to the ile de la Barthelasse, only four remain. A multilingual audio tour explains the history.

The well-known song "Sur le pont d'Avignon" actually started as "Sous le pont d'Avignon" — referring to festivals held on the island beneath the bridge, not dancing on the narrow bridge itself. A 19th-century operetta changed "sous" to "sur," presumably because singing about being under a bridge sounded odd.
