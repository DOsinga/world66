Cavaillon is a market town in the Luberon, and in France its name is virtually synonymous with one thing: melons. The sweet, perfumed Cavaillon melon has been celebrated here since at least the 16th century — Alexandre Dumas loved them so much he offered the town his complete works in exchange for a lifetime supply — and the Monday morning market remains one of the largest and most lively in Provence, piled high with produce from the surrounding plains.

Beyond the melons, Cavaillon has an old town worth an afternoon. The small cathedral of Saint-Véran dates to the 12th century, and the Arc de Triomphe — actually a Roman arch from the 1st century AD — still stands at the town's edge. The synagogue and attached Jewish museum are among the most significant reminders of the Comtat Venaissin's Sephardic Jewish community, one of the oldest in France, which flourished here under papal protection for centuries.

The surrounding countryside is classic Luberon: lavender fields, limestone outcrops, hilltop villages. The market town of Apt is close by, and the Luberon's famous perched villages — Gordes, Roussillon, Bonnieux — are all within easy reach.
