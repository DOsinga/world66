Port Grimaud is a purpose-built waterfront village on the Gulf of Saint-Tropez, designed in the 1960s by architect Francois Spoerry. The canals, bridges, and colourful Provencal-style facades give it the feel of a miniature Venice. Every house has its own mooring. It is pretty, artificial, and surprisingly charming for a walk or a boat ride through the canals.
