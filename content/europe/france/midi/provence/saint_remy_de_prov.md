Saint-Rémy-de-Provence is a handsome small town at the foot of Les Alpilles, about 40 minutes south of [Avignon](/europe/france/midi/provence/avignon). It is best known for two things: the Roman ruins of Glanum, just outside town, and the asylum of Saint-Paul-de-Mausole where Van Gogh spent a year and painted some of his most famous works, including The Starry Night.

The town itself is a pleasant grid of plane-tree-lined boulevards, good restaurants, and a lively Wednesday market. The historic centre is compact and walkable; most of the main sights are clustered along the southern edge of town, within easy reach of each other. Saint-Rémy makes an excellent base for exploring Les Alpilles and the nearby hilltop villages of [Les Baux-de-Provence](/europe/france/midi/provence/les_beaux_de_prove) and [Roussillon](/europe/france/midi/provence/roussillon).
