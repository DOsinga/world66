Vaison-la-Romaine is split in two by the River Ouveze. The lower town sits on extensive Roman excavations — two large residential sites that are among the best in France. The upper town, reached by crossing a Roman bridge and climbing through a medieval gate, is a quiet tangle of stone houses clinging to a cliff topped by a ruined castle. The Tuesday market is one of the best in the Vaucluse.
