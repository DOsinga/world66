The French Pyrenees stretch from the Atlantic to the Mediterranean along the Spanish border, offering some of the most dramatic mountain scenery in Europe. The Hautes-Pyrénées département is the heart of it — high peaks, ski resorts in winter, and superb hiking and cycling in summer. The Pic du Midi de Bigorre, accessible by cable car, has an observatory at its summit and panoramic views across the range.

Ski resorts like Luz Ardiden and Val Louron are smaller and less crowded than the Alps, with reliable snow and more sunshine. In summer the same mountains become hiking and cycling territory — the great Tour de France cols (Tourmalet, Aubisque, Aspin) are here, and you can ride them yourself. The Cirque de Gavarnie, a vast natural amphitheatre with one of Europe's highest waterfalls, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most spectacular sights in the Pyrenees.

[Lourdes](/europe/france/midi/pyrenees/lourdes) is the major town, famous worldwide as a Catholic pilgrimage site since the Virgin Mary reportedly appeared to a local girl in 1858. Millions visit each year, and the town is entirely shaped by this industry — but the setting at the foot of the mountains is genuinely beautiful.
