Lac des Bouillouses (Bollosa in Catalan) sits at 2,017 metres in the eastern Pyrenees, a classified natural site since 1976. The dam-created lake is surrounded by alpine meadows dotted with smaller lakes and framed by granite peaks — a landscape that draws hikers, trail runners, and picnicking families throughout the summer months.

The lake is the most popular starting point for the ascent of [Pic Carlit](/europe/france/midi/pyrenees/pic_carlit), the highest peak in the eastern Pyrenees at 2,921 metres. The route passes a chain of lakes and takes around four hours return — demanding but not technical. Pic Péric, slightly lower, offers a quieter alternative with equally rewarding views.

In July and August, the access road from Les Angles or Mont-Louis is closed to private vehicles during the day; a shuttle bus runs from the car parks below. Come early or late in the day, or visit in June or September, when the road is open and the crowds are thinner.
