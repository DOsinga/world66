---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Lourdes has a cluster of museums and attractions beyond the pilgrimage sites, many focusing on the story of Bernadette and the apparitions. The Sanctuary itself is free to enter and open year-round. A walk along the Gave de Pau river and through the illuminated procession routes gives a good sense of the scale of the whole operation.

**The Miraculous Medal Museum**

Reconstitution of the life of St Catherine Labouré, with life-size wax figurines and a historical diorama of the Miraculous Medal.

address: 3, rue du Porche

tel: 05 62 94 42 21

category: Museum

**The Panorama of St Bernadette's Life**

Audiovisual reconstitution of the life of Bernadette and the apparitions.

address: 38, rue de la Grotte

tel: 05 62 94 43 84

category: Museum

**Planet Aquarium Pyrenees Lourdes**

A chance to discover the aquatic fauna and flora of the Pyrenees in an entertaining and educational setting.

address: 71, avenue Alexandre Marqui

tel: 05 62 42 01 00

category: Sight

**The Nativity Museum**

The animated crib of Jerusalem, a Biblical landscape made of animated Santons retracing Jesus' childhood.

address: 21, Quai Saint Jean

tel: 05 62 94 71 00

category: Museum

**The Little Lourdes Museum**

A miniature world showing the history and birth of Lourdes in 1858, set in a pleasant flower and bonsai garden.

address: 68, avenue Peyramale

tel: 05 62 94 24 36

category: Museum

**The Castle**

Lourdes' fortified castle was built on a rocky peak dominating the town. It has served as a military fortress, a state prison, and a residence of the Counts of Bigorre. Today it houses a museum of the Pyrenees.

address: Rue du Bourg

tel: 05 62 42 37 37

category: Sight
