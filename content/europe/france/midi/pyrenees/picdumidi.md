The Pic du Midi de Bigorre rises to 2,877 metres above the Pyrenean foothills, crowned by an observatory that has been in operation since 1878. What makes it exceptional is not just the altitude but the access: a cable car from La Mongie ski station takes you to the summit in fifteen minutes, delivering one of the most spectacular mountain panoramas in France. On clear days you can see from the Atlantic to the Mediterranean, with the entire chain of the Pyrenees spread out below.

The observatory itself is open to visitors and well worth the entrance fee. The astronomical instruments are impressive, but the real draw is the terraces — exposed walkways at nearly 3,000 metres where you stand in the wind with nothing between you and the mountains. The museum inside covers the history of the observatory and its role in early high-altitude photography; the coronagraph here produced some of the first detailed images of the sun's corona.

For an unforgettable experience, you can book an overnight stay at the summit. After the last cable car descends and the day visitors leave, you have the peak to yourself for sunset, stargazing (the site has some of the clearest skies in Europe), and sunrise over the mountains. The accommodation is basic but the experience is extraordinary. Book well in advance, especially for summer nights.

The cable car operates from late May through October and during the ski season. La Mongie, the base station, is part of the Grand Tourmalet ski area and is accessible from [Lourdes](/europe/france/midi/pyrenees/lourdes) via the Col du Tourmalet, one of the most famous climbs in Tour de France history.
