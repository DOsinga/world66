The larger of Arras's two main squares, lined with 155 identical Flemish baroque facades dating from the 17th and 18th centuries. The uniformity is deliberate — after the First World War the city was rebuilt to a consistent design, using historical records to reproduce what had stood before. Sit at one of the terrace cafes in the evening and the effect is remarkable: the proportions are perfect, the stone glows amber, and the scale feels just right for a provincial capital.
