A network of chalk tunnels running beneath the old city, first dug in the 10th century as quarries and expanded over the centuries into storage cellars, refuges, and ultimately military infrastructure. During the First World War the British Army extended the tunnels dramatically — the system sheltered up to 24,000 soldiers on the eve of the Battle of Arras in April 1917. Guided tours depart from the Hôtel de Ville and take around an hour. It is cool underground regardless of the season; bring a layer.
