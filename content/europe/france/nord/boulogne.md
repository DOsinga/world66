Boulogne-sur-Mer is France's busiest fishing port and, for many British visitors, the first taste of France. Most people roll off the ferry and drive straight south — which is a mistake. The old town perched on the hill is genuinely lovely: medieval ramparts you can walk in their entirety, a handsome basilica, and cobbled streets that feel nothing like a tourist trap. Give it a night, not just a lunch stop.

The lower town along the harbour has good seafood restaurants and a lively daily fish market. The Nausicaä sea centre is one of Europe's best aquariums, worth a couple of hours even if you're not travelling with children.
