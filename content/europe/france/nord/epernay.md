Epernay is the champagne capital of the world. The town itself is modest — a quiet place in the Marne valley with none of the architectural grandeur of nearby [Reims](/europe/france/nord/reims) — but beneath its streets lie over 100 kilometres of chalk cellars holding millions of bottles of champagne. The Avenue de Champagne, a single elegant street lined with the great houses, is where you come to understand why this particular stretch of northern [France](/europe/france) produces a wine that no other region has managed to replicate.

Moët & Chandon, Mercier and De Castellane all have their headquarters on or near the avenue and offer cellar tours with tastings. Be aware that most houses close from noon to around 2 p.m. and are shut on Sundays. For a smaller, more personal experience, seek out the grower-producers (récoltants-manipulants) in the surrounding villages — they make champagne from their own vineyards and are happy to receive visitors by appointment.

The town makes a good base for exploring the Champagne vineyards by car or bicycle. A miniature tourist train runs from May through October, looping through the key sites with audio commentary. The weekly markets at Place des Arcades sell local produce on Wednesdays, Thursdays, Saturdays and Sundays.
