**Lille nightlife** sparkles with students, visitors and Brits who choose this northern city to get their French fix. Lille has a strong Flemish influence, which translates into a unique cuisine and atmosphere. Clubbing at Lille starts around 11 and lasts until dawn. Try the beer, taste the wine, dine out and make the night yours.

**Meters of Beer and Winning Wine**

Les 3 Brasseurs is an inexpensive small brasserie that doubles as a micro-brewery. All the beer is made on their premises. Their menu has a novel way of enticing patrons to enjoy a beer tasting. They call it "1 metre de biere." Order this and you'll get 10 large glasses of beer to split with your friends. This bar is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. It's located at 22 Place de la Gare.

If wine is what you crave, visit La Part des Anges, which is located in the old section of Lille. It stands on a cobblestone road, which is incidentally Lille's oldest street, Rue de la Monnaie. The wine list is extensive and if you can handle strong cheese, try the town's specialty: Vieux Lille. As the night progresses, DJs liven up the atmosphere. Dial (03) 2006-4401 for more information.

**Clubbing, Live Music and Luck**

To get a good feel for Lille's nightlife, walk down Rue Masséna or Rue de Gand. Between the two you'll find a variety of bars, clubs and restaurants. Check out the lounge Le Sapo at 11 Rue de Gand, for some good cocktails. If dancing is what you're in the mood for, go to the Network Café. This club plays techno, progressive and rock. It's popular with the town's college crowd. Visit their Facebook page for information about their upcoming events.

If you like live music, stop by Le Splendid. They often feature British bands. The venue is located at 1 Place de Mont de Terre. You can also catch live jazz, funk or rock at Le Bar Parallele, where a mixed crowd (over 30) gathers. If you're part of the sophisticated bunch for whom "live music" means classical or opera, try Lille's Opera House, which is of excellent repute: (03) 2838-4050. Need something more daring? Take a gamble and go to 777 Pont de Flandres, where Casino Barriere Lille awaits.

**Tips on Dining**

Vieux Lille is lined with restaurants that specialize in regional dishes. Make sure to try "Carbonnade Flamande" or "Potjevleesch." They are worth the tongue-twister.
