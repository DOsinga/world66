story: >
  In the small hours of 6 June 1944, six Horsa gliders carrying D Company of the Oxfordshire and Buckinghamshire Light Infantry landed silently in fields just metres from the bridge. Within ten minutes of touching down, the troops had seized both the canal and river bridges — the first Allied soldiers to capture enemy-held territory on D-Day. Major John Howard's men then held on until relieved by Lord Lovat's commandos marching from Sword Beach, reportedly to the sound of a piper.
---

The original swing bridge that British airborne troops captured in the opening minutes of D-Day. Replaced in 1994 by a wider structure, the original bridge now sits in the grounds of the Pegasus Memorial museum nearby. The new bridge still carries the name Pegasus Bridge and crossing it — above the same dark canal water — remains a memorable moment. The site is marked with memorials and information boards.
