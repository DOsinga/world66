The farmhouse beside Pegasus Bridge that became the first building liberated in occupied France on 6 June 1944. The Gondrée family sheltered Allied soldiers in the hours after the bridge was taken, and the café has been run by the family ever since. Open March to November, it serves drinks and light refreshments and sells D-Day souvenirs and postcards. More than a tourist stop — the walls are covered in veterans' photographs and the atmosphere is genuinely moved by its own history.
