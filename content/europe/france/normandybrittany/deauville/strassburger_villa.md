The most emblematic example of Deauville's Norman and neo-Norman architectural heritage. This grand villa — with its timber framing, pointed turrets, and ornate woodwork — represents the height of late 19th-century resort architecture. It now belongs to the City of Deauville and is open for visits, giving you a rare look inside one of the town's showpiece buildings rather than just admiring it from the street.
