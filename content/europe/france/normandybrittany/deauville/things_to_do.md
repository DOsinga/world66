Deauville offers a mix of architectural sightseeing, outdoor activities, and seaside leisure. The Belle Époque villas scattered through the town are reason enough to wander the streets, and the Strassburger Villa is the finest example. Active visitors can golf, sail, ride horses, or take a thalassotherapy cure at one of the seafront centres.

**Strassburger Villa** is the most emblematic example of Deauville's Norman and neo-Norman architectural style. The villa now belongs to the city and is open for visits — a rare chance to see inside one of the grand private estates that shaped the town's identity.

**Mont-Canisy** rises 110 metres above sea level just outside town. Its 13 hectares include walking trails with panoramic views over the Calvados coast and a fascinating military history from World War II. Walking tours can be arranged through the tourism office.

**Calouste Gulbenkian Park** (Domaine des Enclos) covers 24 hectares at the edge of town and has been city property since 1973. It is planted with rare species and makes for a peaceful walk, open July to August.
