Dinan is one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Brittany, perched above the River Rance with a ring of 13th-century ramparts still largely intact. Half-timbered houses lean over cobbled streets in the old centre, and the view from the Jardin Anglais down to the old port is one of the finest in northern France.

The town is small enough to walk in an afternoon but rewards a longer stay. Rue du Jerzual — a steep, winding lane lined with stone houses and artisan workshops — leads from the hilltop centre down to the port, where you can rent a kayak on the Rance or take a boat downriver to [Saint-Malo](/europe/france/normandybrittany/saint_malo). The ramparts walk circles the old town and gives you rooftop views across to the medieval clock tower and the Basilica of Saint-Sauveur, which mixes Romanesque and Gothic in the distinctly Breton way.

Thursday is market day in Place du Guesclin, and it is a proper Breton market — crêpes, cider, oysters, local cheese. The Fête des Remparts, held every two years in July, turns the whole town into a medieval festival with jousting, costumes, and far too much chouchen.
