Known in Roman times as **Noviodunum**, Jublains is a quiet agricultural town in the Mayenne département with an outsized archaeological heritage. The remains of a Roman fortress, theatre, and thermal baths are all within walking distance, making it one of the best-preserved Gallo-Roman sites in northwestern France.

A small but well-presented museum, upgraded in 1995, houses finds from the site and explains the town's importance as a regional capital under Roman rule. The fortress walls are the highlight — unusually intact and atmospheric, especially on a misty morning.

Jublains is a short detour if you are driving through the [Loire Valley](/europe/france/centre/loirevalley) or heading toward Brittany, and worth the stop for anyone interested in Roman history.
