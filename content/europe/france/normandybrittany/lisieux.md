Lisieux is an important centre of Catholic pilgrimage in Normandy, devoted to Saint Therese of Lisieux. Pilgrims head for the Basilique Sainte-Therese, an ornate neo-Byzantine building on the hill above town, reminiscent of the Sacre-Coeur in [Paris](/europe/france/paris). It is one of the largest churches built in France in the 20th century.

From a purely architectural standpoint, the Gothic cathedral of Saint-Pierre in the old town centre is the more interesting building — a fine 12th-century structure that survived the heavy bombing of 1944 largely intact. Much of the rest of medieval Lisieux was destroyed and rebuilt in a functional postwar style, but the area around the cathedral retains some character.
