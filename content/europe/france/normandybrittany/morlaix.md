Morlaix is a handsome Breton town built around the steep valley of the Dossen river, dominated by a colossal 19th-century railway viaduct that spans the town centre two storeys above the rooftops. The viaduct is the first thing you see and the thing you remember — it gives Morlaix an almost surreal sense of scale.

Below the viaduct, the old town is a tangle of medieval half-timbered houses, many with the distinctive Morlaix "lantern houses" — tall, narrow buildings with internal courtyards lit from above. The Maison à Pondalez on Grand'Rue is the finest surviving example and is open to visitors. The streets climb steeply on both sides of the valley, rewarding walkers with views over the slate rooftops.

Morlaix makes a good base for exploring the northern Finistère coast. The Cairn de Barnenez, one of the oldest megalithic monuments in Europe, sits on a headland just north of town. The pink granite coast and the parish closes of the Élorn valley are both within easy reach.
