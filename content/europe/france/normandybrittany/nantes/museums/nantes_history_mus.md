(Inside the *Castle of the Dukes of Brittany*)

It is a museum of urban history, awarded a "Museum of France" quality mark. Designed specifically for a wider public, its contemporary layout tries to reflect the best of the monument and its works.

Once a ducal palace, the Museum exhibits both chronologically and thematically in 32 rooms, from the basements to the attics. The entire collection, delicately arranged in seven sequences, is displayed by **multimedia installations** as well as **historic items**.

The aim of this museum is to offer its visitors a view towards the history which not only gave Nantes its current identity but also marked its individuality. The multimedia equipment not only provides the visitors much more in-depth information, but also reinforces the pleasure of learning, the interactivity and a sense of immersion back into the old times in the given atmosphere.
