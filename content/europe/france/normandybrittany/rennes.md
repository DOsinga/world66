Rennes is the capital of Brittany and one of the most liveable cities in France — a university town with a young population, beautiful half-timbered medieval streets, and a food and bar scene that punches well above its size. The old town around Place des Lices and Rue Saint-Michel (known locally as Rue de la Soif — "Thirst Street") is packed with restaurants, crêperies, and bars that fill every evening.

The city's medieval core survived a devastating fire in 1720 that destroyed much of the centre, but enough remains to give Rennes its distinctive character: colourful colombage houses lean over narrow streets, with the grand 17th-century Parlement de Bretagne presiding over the Place du Parlement. The Saturday market at Place des Lices is one of the largest in France and worth timing a visit around.

Rennes is also a natural base for exploring Brittany. [Mont Saint-Michel](/europe/france/normandybrittany/montsaintmichel), the Breton coast, and the forests of inland Brittany are all within easy reach.
