**Currency**

France uses the Euro (EUR). Cards are widely accepted, though some smaller restaurants, markets, and rural businesses still prefer cash. ATMs are plentiful in cities and most towns. If you use an ATM, stick to machines at real banks to avoid high private-operator fees.

**Visas**

EU citizens need no visa. US, Canadian, Australian, and UK citizens can stay for up to 90 days without one. Starting in late 2026, non-EU visitors will need to register via the ETIAS online scheme (a short pre-travel authorization, not a full visa). Check requirements for your country before travelling.

**Language**

French. Don't assume everyone speaks English, especially outside the major cities and tourist areas. Making an effort with French — even a mangled bonjour and merci — goes a long way. People are generally more helpful when you try.

**Health**

Healthcare is excellent. EU citizens should carry their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC). Non-EU visitors should carry comprehensive travel insurance. Pharmacies (look for the green cross) are well-stocked and pharmacists are trained to give basic medical advice — often a useful first stop for minor ailments.

**Phones and Internet**

Mobile coverage is good throughout the country, though can be patchy in mountainous areas. Wi-Fi is available in most hotels and many cafes. French SIM cards are easy to buy; EU roaming rules mean visitors from other EU countries pay no extra for using their home plan.

**Tipping**

Service is included in restaurant bills by law (service compris). You don't have to tip, but rounding up or leaving a euro or two for good service is appreciated. Tipping taxi drivers is not expected but common.

**Electricity**

230V, 50Hz. Standard European two-pin plugs (Type E). Visitors from the UK or North America need an adaptor.

**Public Holidays**

France has 11 public holidays a year. Many businesses close on these days, particularly on May 1 (Labour Day, which is taken very seriously) and August 15. July 14 (Bastille Day) is celebrated with fireworks and parades nationwide.
