6 May Park, named after the day of the revolution in Adjara, is another charm of Batumi. Smekalov, then Governor of the city, came up with the idea in 1881. The park is situated around Nurie Lake. It used to be a swamp, before it was cleaned out and turned into a lake. The territory turned into the first park in Batumi.

In 1932, the name of the garden was changed to "culture and Recreation" and into "Children's park in 1933, and later on to "Pioneer's Park". Many different clubs and attractions were built to attract kids and visitors. A sumer Theater - beautiful building - where many shows and concerts took place - was built in the middle of the park.

Unfortunately it did not survive the time and burned down in the late 90's.

In 1937, a Zoological Corner was created in one of the corners of the Park. At present the Zoo has been furnished and new departments have been added, to entertain both young and adult visitors. Another pearl of the city, or the Batumi Aquarium, was founded in 1966 and situated next to the territory of the park. It has been remodeled offer the past years and it offers many attractions.

Batumi Dolphinarium was founded in 1974 and it became of the main attractions of this city. People from different countries came to see a dozen dolphins perform a show and do magnificent tricks.
The dolphinarium stopped working for some time, but in 2007 the government of Adjara decided to bring back the tradition and build a new dolphinarium. Six young dolphins found their new home.
