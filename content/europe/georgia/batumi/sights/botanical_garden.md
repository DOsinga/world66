The Batumi Botanical Garden is situated 9km north of Batumi. The area where it is located is called Mtsvane Kontskhi (Green Cape). The garden is tied to the name of Andrey Krasnov, who was an acknowledged scientist in botanical and physical geography. The Batumi Botanical Garden has no analogue in the world. Plants from completely diverse climatic and landscape zones co-exist side by side.

It may seem strange but the date-palm and the Japanese Sakura grow equally well in there. The Garden represents thousands of plant species of astonishing beauty. Visitors are always fascinated with the 1200 kinds of rose. The marvelous and unforgettable beauty of the Garden is represented in nine diverse segments. Those segments are; East Asia, North America, New Zealand, South America, the Himalayas, Mexico, Australia, the Mediterranean Sea and Caucasian Humid Subtropics.

The garden collection includes 2037 taxonomic units of ligneous plants. Among them, 104 are of Caucasian origin and the rest are representatives of foreign flora.
The botanical garden is also important in the sense that a plot has been created, for the selection of endemic species that are rare or on the verge of extinction.

The sea view from the Botanical Garden is really impressive. Equally impressive is the view of the Garden from the sea. There are no seasons without blooming in this botanical paradise. It is especially fascinating in late spring, summer and early autumn, when exotic plants burst into colorful blossoms in turn and wrap the whole cape with a passionate aroma.
