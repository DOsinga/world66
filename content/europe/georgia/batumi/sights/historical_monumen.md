Gonio-Apsaros Fortress had a unique strategic importance. It protected the entrances to chorokhi and western inlands to the black sea coast. Its location was what made the Gonio Fortress, one of the citadels of the Roman Empire and later Byzantine. It is a region where Cholchic bronze culture and the first Georgian state unions found their birthplace. According to Roman and Byzantine sources, Apsaros was formed as a settlement during that period. Its history is closely connected to the myth of the Argonauts. According to legend, this is the site where king ayet buried his son Apsaros, whose body was dismembered and thrown into the sea by Jason. One of the twelve apostles of christ, or St Mathias, is also buried in apsaros.

Khikhani or Khikhati Fortress, located on the territory of Khikhadziri village, in Khulo region, is medieval fortress, although the exact date of its construction is not known. In 1230, Saint George's church was built there. A fortress was built near the church later on. Therefore, we assume that the fortress was built in XIII century. From the strategic point of view, the Fortress is located on a very significant place. It is inaccessible from three sides. It is still difficult to clim the fortress to tis day, which makes it more interesting and attractive to adventurers. The fortress has two main towers. Its walls are 7-8m high. It is interesting to note that the fortress never lost its function for 700 years. Khikhani retained its economic and military strategic importance up to beginning of XX century.

Khino Episcopal Cathedral is one of the most important cultural centers in Khino. It was established in the Middle Ages, during the disintegration of the united fundal Kingdom of Georgia. Today, the name has only been preserved for a small village at the source of kintrishi river. Ruins of Episcopal Cathedral have still been preserved. The walls of the chapel are destroyed, and only remains of the foundation and the main fence are preserved. It is known today as remarkable symbol of the spiritual courage and bravery of the Adjarian people.

Skhalta Monastry is the XII century monument of Georgian architecture in Khulo. The interior of this beautiful church remains fragments of original paintings. During the services, the praying is constantly accompanied by the sound of Skhalta river. One of the oldest and most unusual arched bridges is stretched over this river.
