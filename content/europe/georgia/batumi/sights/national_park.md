Mtirala's national Park

The Mtirala national park was developed in 2001. The Mtirala national park is situated on the Kobuleti-Chakvi Ridge in the westernmost part of the Adjara-Imereti Range of the Smaller Caucasus Mountains. The park covers and area of 16000ha and includes a well-preserved ecosystem of forests and shrubs. Another important function of the park is promoting the natural ecosystem balance of the Kobuleti-CHakvi Ridge, which prevents erosion, landslides and floods, both in the mountains and the plains. The highest peak of the Mortvili Ridge going along the eastern border of the park is 1761m. Didi Mtirala, Tereti and other mountains are over 1300m above sea-level.
Today the Mtirala National Park is model protected area in Georgia, that combines mountain tourism with ecotourism. The national park has two vistitor centers located in the town of chakvi (at the premises of the Parks's administration) and Chakvistskali village. There are accommodation facilities, as well as other services such as tour-guides and rentals of hiking/camping equipment for the visitors of the park
