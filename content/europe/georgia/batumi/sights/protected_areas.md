Kintrishi

The area of Kintrishi Reserve is about 14000ha. It was established in 1956 to protect the characteristic Colchic type of forests, covering the mountains on both sides of upstream of Kintrishi River and it's tributaries. The forests are distinguished with centenarian oaks, beeches, chestnuts, pines, fir=trees and box-trees. The climbing plants make these sights even more beautiful as trees seem to be dressed in green chokhas (the national Georgian suit for men). The site has a visitors center and camping facilities, with several foot-trails leading to the ancient Khino village and high altitude lake.

Kobuleti (Ispani)

Another nature reserve in Adjara is located near the town of Kobuleti. Ispani wetland deserves its uniqueness to the occurrence of a unique mire type: the percolation bog with special characteristics in vegetation, hydrology, and peat stratigraphy. It is situated 300m away from the black sea coast, and together with the Kolkheti wetlands, it is the only warm-temperated region in the world where Sphagnum dominated, rain fed peatlands occur. The site has a visitor center and an interpretive foot-trail with an observation tower.
