You will hear the name Borjomi everywhere in Georgia — it is the brand that made the country's mineral water famous, and like Spa in Belgium, the town's name has become a generic term. The actual town sits in a narrow gorge in the Samtskhe-Javakheti region, surrounded by pine-forested hills and criss-crossed by the Mtkvari River.

The main draw is Borjomi-Kharagauli National Park, one of the largest national parks in Europe, with hiking trails ranging from a half-day walk to a full week's traverse. In town, the Central Park contains a spring where you can drink the famous mineral water straight from the source — it tastes noticeably sulphurous and warm, nothing like the bottled version. Borjomi also serves as the natural base for visiting [Bakuriani](/europe/georgia/bakuriani), the nearby ski resort reached by a charming narrow-gauge railway.
