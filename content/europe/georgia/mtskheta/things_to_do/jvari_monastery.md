Jvari — "cross" in Georgian — sits on a rocky promontory above the confluence of the Mtkvari and Aragvi rivers, directly overlooking Mtskheta. The monastery dates from the 6th century and is one of the oldest surviving churches in Georgia. Its compact stone form and dramatic hilltop position have made it an enduring symbol of the country.

The view from the terrace is the main reason to make the climb: Mtskheta spread below, the two rivers meeting, and the Caucasus ridges beyond. It's a short drive or a steep walk from town. Lermontov used this spot as the setting for his famous poem "Mtsyri."
