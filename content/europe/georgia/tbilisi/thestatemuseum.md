The main building of the Georgian National Museum sits on Rustaveli Avenue, and the highlight for most visitors is the treasury — a collection of gold and silver jewelry, coins, and regalia spanning five millennia of Georgian civilization. The pieces range from Bronze Age torques and Colchian gold ornaments to Byzantine chalices and medieval crowns, and the workmanship across all periods is extraordinary.

Beyond the treasury, the museum covers Georgia's natural history and archaeology across several floors, with exhibits on the ancient kingdoms of Colchis and Kartli-Iberia. The Soviet-era building is showing its age, but the collections inside more than compensate.
