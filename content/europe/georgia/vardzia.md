Vardzia is situated in South-West part of Georgia. It's historical name is Samtskhe-Javakheti. In 12th century King George III started constructing a castle. His plan was to create a huge defeated station, where our army would be staying always ready to fight. In this period Georgia was the only Christian country, so having such castle on the Southern border had a political meaning. Vardzia was constructed during the reign of two Kings: George III and his daughter King Tamara. But Tamara changed the meaning of this place and made it as a Monastery. And just this was that made Mohammedan conquerors interested in Vardzia. 



Today Vardzia is a complex of grand ruins. It consists of more than 600 rooms, connected with corridors. There also were secret corridors and holes in roof. Their length is 227 metres. They are situated in the East part of the comlex and go towards the bank of River Kura. Each dwelling place consists of three rooms. There exists also the room of Tamara but according to the legend, she had more than 366 rooms in order that the enemy couldn't know her real dwelling place.
