Baden-Baden is one of Europe's grand spa towns, built on a series of hills at the northern edge of the Black Forest in southwestern [Germany](/europe/germany). The Romans discovered the hot springs here two thousand years ago, and the town has been drawing visitors ever since — Russian aristocrats, European royalty, and writers from Dostoevsky to Mark Twain all came to take the waters.

The **Kurhaus** and its casino, designed in 1824, anchor the centre of town with the kind of grandeur that makes Las Vegas look restrained. The surrounding Lichtentaler Allee, a riverside promenade lined with exotic trees, is one of the most elegant walks in Germany. The town is impeccably maintained — flower gardens, classical concerts, and an unhurried pace that makes a weekend here feel like a week.

The thermal baths are the essential experience. The **Friedrichsbad** is a 19th-century Roman-Irish bathhouse where you follow a 17-stage programme of dry saunas, steam rooms, scrubs and thermal pools — clothing is not an option, and the experience is liberating. The **Caracalla Therme** next door is a modern complex with indoor and outdoor pools for those who prefer a more relaxed approach. The remains of the original **Roman Baths** at Römerplatz prove that the tradition here is genuinely ancient.
