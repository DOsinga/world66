Freiburg's Minster (Freiburger Munster) dominates the old town with its 116-metre openwork spire, once called the most beautiful tower in Christendom by the Swiss art historian Jacob Burckhardt. Construction began around 1200 in Romanesque style and continued well into the sixteenth century, resulting in a blend of Romanesque, High Gothic, and Late Gothic elements.

The interior rewards a slow visit. The medieval stained-glass windows in the nave and side aisles are originals, remarkably intact, depicting trades and guilds that funded them. The high altar by Hans Baldung Grien is one of the finest Renaissance altarpieces in Germany. Climb the tower for sweeping views across the city to the Black Forest and, on clear days, the Vosges mountains in France.

The daily market on Munsterplatz outside is one of southern Germany's best, with local produce, flowers, and the famous long red sausages (Lange Rote) that are a Freiburg institution.
