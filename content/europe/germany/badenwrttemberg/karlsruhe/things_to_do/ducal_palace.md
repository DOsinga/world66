The former residence of the Dukes of Baden was built between 1749 and 1781 in a distinctive radial plan — the palace tower stands at the hub from which 32 streets fan outward across the city. Destroyed in World War II and rebuilt without its original interiors, the palace now houses the Badisches Landesmuseum: a strong collection spanning antiquities, decorative arts, medieval sculpture, porcelain, and furniture through to the present day.
