Lindau is one of the most photogenic small towns in Germany. Its old town sits on a small island in Lake Constance (Bodensee), connected to the mainland by a bridge and a rail causeway, with the Austrian and Swiss Alps rising directly across the water. The harbour entrance, flanked by a Bavarian lion statue and a nineteenth-century lighthouse, is the town's signature view and one of the most recognizable images in southern Germany.

The island old town is compact and walkable, a tangle of medieval and baroque buildings along the Maximilianstrasse, the main pedestrian street lined with painted facades and sidewalk cafes. The Altes Rathaus, dating from the fifteenth century, stands out with its frescoed exterior. The Mangturm, a squat medieval watchtower at the harbour, and the Diebsturm (Thieves' Tower) round out a streetscape that manages to be genuinely old without feeling like a theme park.

Lake Constance itself is the real draw for longer stays. Ferries connect Lindau to Bregenz in Austria, Rorschach in Switzerland, and other lakeside towns. The cycling path around the lake is one of Europe's great long-distance bike routes, and Lindau makes a natural starting or ending point. In summer the lake is warm enough for swimming, and the public beach (Lindenhofbad) on the mainland side is popular.

Lindau belongs to Bavaria rather than Baden-Württemberg — a geographical quirk that puts it in a different state from the rest of the Bodensee shoreline. You will notice this mostly in the beer selection and the general air of Bavarian contentment.
