---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Mannheim has a relatively mild climate for Germany, sheltered by the Rhine plain and the Odenwald hills to the east. Summers are warm and can be hot — July and August regularly reach 28–32°C. Winters are grey and damp but not especially cold.

**Spring (April–May)** is a good time to visit: the Luisenpark and palace gardens come into colour, outdoor terrace season begins, and the city is not yet crowded with summer visitors.

**Summer (June–August)** brings outdoor events along the waterfront, open-air cinema, and an animated atmosphere in the Jungbusch. The heat can be oppressive in August.

**Autumn (September–October)** has the advantage of harvest season in the nearby Palatinate wine region, making a side trip to the wine route particularly worthwhile. The Luisenpark holds its Illuminationen light festival in autumn — genuinely worth seeing.

**Winter** has a decent Christmas market on Friedrichsplatz around the Wasserturm, running from late November through late December. Otherwise it is a quieter and less rewarding time.

The **Maifeld Derby** (usually June) draws an international crowd for a music festival with an eclectic lineup, held on the old military training ground. It has a reputation for a relaxed, well-organised atmosphere.
