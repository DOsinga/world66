Speyer is a small city on the Rhine in the Palatinate region of southwestern [Germany](/europe/germany), with a history far larger than its size suggests. As a free city of the Holy Roman Empire from 1294 to 1779, it hosted over 50 imperial diets and was the seat of the Imperial Chamber Court. The Protestant Reformation takes its name from the "Protestation at Speyer" of 1529.

The city is best known for the **Speyer Cathedral** (Dom St. Maria und St. Stephan), the largest surviving Romanesque church in Europe. Built between 1025 and 1061 under Emperor Conrad II, who is buried in the crypt alongside seven other emperors and kings, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the defining monument of the city. The Dreifaltigkeitskirche, a Baroque church from 1701–17, and portions of the medieval fortifications including a 14th-century gate add to the old town's appeal.

The **Historisches Museum der Pfalz** covers the region from prehistoric times, with a highlight being a gold Neolithic cone hat — one of only four ever discovered. The **Technik Museum** on the edge of town is a crowd-pleaser, with a Boeing 747, a Soviet space shuttle and a submarine among its exhibits.
