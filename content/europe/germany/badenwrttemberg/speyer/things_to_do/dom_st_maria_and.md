The cathedral was built between 1025 and 1061 at the initiative of Emperor Conrad II, who is buried in the crypt alongside seven other emperors and kings. It is an immense triple-nave basilica crowned by four towers, and was the largest Romanesque structure in existence until the building of Cluny. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is considered by many to be the finest Romanesque cathedral in the world.
