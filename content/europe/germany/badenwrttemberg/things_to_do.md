---
title: "Things to Do in Baden-Württemberg"
type: section
---

Baden-Württemberg rewards travelers who go beyond Stuttgart and Heidelberg. The region's real pleasures are outdoors: hiking and cycling through the Black Forest, taking the waters at thermal spas, sailing on Lake Constance, and wandering the medieval streets of smaller towns like Tübingen and Schiltach.

**Hiking the Black Forest**

The Schwarzwald has more than 23,000 km of marked trails, ranging from gentle valley walks to multi-day ridge routes. The Westweg, running 285 km from Pforzheim to Basel, is the classic long-distance trail. Day hikers can base themselves in [Freiburg](/europe/germany/badenwrttemberg/freiburg) and reach good trailheads within 30 minutes by bus.

**Thermal spas**

[Baden-Baden](/europe/germany/badenwrttemberg/badenbaden) has been famous for its hot springs since Roman times. The Caracalla Terme and the historic Friedrichsbad offer very different experiences — the first modern and family-friendly, the second a formal 17-stage bathing ritual that requires you to leave your clothes and your phone at the door.

**The Cannstatter Volksfest**

Held each autumn in Stuttgart's Cannstatter Wasen, this is Germany's second-largest folk festival after Munich's Oktoberfest, and considerably less overrun by tourists. Two weeks of beer tents, fairground rides, and traditional Swabian food. A smaller spring edition runs in April or May.

**Christmas markets**

Baden-Württemberg's Christmas markets are among Germany's finest. Stuttgart's Weihnachtsmarkt, one of the oldest in the country, runs for four weeks before Christmas around the Schlossplatz and Schillerplatz. Esslingen goes further with a medieval market complete with craftspeople in period costume.

**Lake Constance**

[Lake Constance](/europe/germany/badenwrttemberg/lakeconstance) (the Bodensee) sits at the corners of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. In summer it's ideal for cycling — a flat 260 km circuit rings the whole lake — and for swimming from the many public beaches. The island town of Konstanz and the monastery island of Reichenau (UNESCO-listed) are the highlights on the German shore.

**Open-Air Museum Vogtsbauernhof**

Near Gutach in the central Black Forest, this is one of Germany's best open-air museums: actual farmhouses dismantled from their original locations and rebuilt on site, staffed by demonstrators showing traditional crafts. It gives a vivid sense of what Black Forest rural life looked like before electricity.
