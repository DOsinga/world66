Ansbach is a quiet Franconian town best known for its baroque Residenz, the former palace of the Margraves of Brandenburg-Ansbach. The palace's 27 state rooms are lavishly decorated and the Orangery gardens behind it are one of the finest baroque garden ensembles in Bavaria.

The old town has a pleasant collection of half-timbered and baroque buildings around the Markgraf-Georg-Brunnen fountain. Ansbach is also associated with the mysterious Kaspar Hauser, who appeared in [Nuremberg](/europe/germany/bavaria/nuremberg) in 1828 claiming to have been raised in a dark cell, and who was later murdered in the Ansbach court garden — a monument marks the spot.

The town sits on the Franconian cycling and hiking network and makes a peaceful base for exploring the region. The Rokoko-Festspiele in July fills the palace and gardens with music and costumed performances.
