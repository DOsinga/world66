Wagner's own opera house, completed in 1876 on the Gruner Hugel (Green Hill) above the city, was designed from the ground up to present his works. The raked orchestra pit is hidden beneath the stage, creating the "mystic gulf" between audience and performance that Wagner intended. The acoustics are extraordinary. Tours run outside the festival season; during July and August the house is in full use and tickets are among the most coveted in opera.
