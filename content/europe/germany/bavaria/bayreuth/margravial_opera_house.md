Built between 1744 and 1748 for the Margravine Wilhelmine of Brandenburg-Bayreuth, this is one of the finest surviving Baroque court theatres in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The auditorium is a marvel of painted wood, gilded stucco, and tiered boxes — all intended to project the power of a minor German principality. Wagner was drawn to Bayreuth partly because of it, though he ultimately found the stage too small for his ambitions and built his own theatre instead.
