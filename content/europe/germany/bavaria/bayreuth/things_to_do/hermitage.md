Laid out in the early 18th century as a summer retreat for the Bayreuth court, the Hermitage is a sprawling park some 4 km east of the city centre. The gardens mix formal French design with the deliberately rustic "hermit" aesthetic fashionable in the period: grottoes lined with shells and stones, mosaic-encrusted walls, and water features that still operate. The Margravine Wilhelmine — the same figure who commissioned the opera house — added a new palace and fountains in the 1740s. It's an odd, charming place, rarely on the tourist radar outside of Germany.
