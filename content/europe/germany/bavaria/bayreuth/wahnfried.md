Wagner named his Bayreuth villa "Wahnfried" — loosely, "peace from madness" — and lived here from 1874 until his death in 1883. The house is now a museum, substantially reconstructed after wartime bombing, with a strong permanent collection covering Wagner's life, creative process, and the festival's history. He and Cosima are buried in the garden; Liszt, his father-in-law, is interred nearby in the city's main cemetery.
