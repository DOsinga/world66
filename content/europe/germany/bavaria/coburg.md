Coburg sits at the northern edge of Bavaria, close to the Thuringian border — a quietly impressive town that most visitors to Germany skip entirely. That's their loss. The Veste Coburg, the massive hilltop fortress looming over the town, is one of the best-preserved medieval castles in Germany and houses a significant art collection including works by Cranach and Dürer. Down in the town centre, Schloss Ehrenburg served as the ducal palace of the House of Saxe-Coburg, whose descendants ended up on the thrones of Britain, Belgium, Portugal, and Bulgaria — Coburg's royal connections are genuinely surprising for a town this size.

Coburg is also famous throughout Franconia for its bratwurst, which is notably different from the Nuremberg variety — longer, grilled over pinecone embers, and served in a hard roll rather than a soft one. The market square is a good place to try one.
