  Dachau was the first concentration camp opened by the Nazi regime, on 22 March 1933, just weeks after Hitler came to power. It served as a model for all subsequent camps and as a training ground for SS officers. Over 200,000 prisoners were held here between 1933 and 1945.
---

The KZ-Gedenkstätte Dachau is one of the most important memorial sites in Germany. The grounds include the reconstructed barracks, the bunker (the camp prison), the crematorium, and a museum in the former maintenance building with an extensive exhibition on the camp's history. Allow at least two to three hours.

The memorial is free to enter and open daily except Mondays. Take bus 726 from the Dachau S-Bahn station directly to the site. Audio guides are available for a small fee and are well worth it.
