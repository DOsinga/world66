Marktl am Inn is a quiet village on the Inn river in southeastern [Bavaria](/europe/germany/bavaria), near the Austrian border. It became briefly famous in 2005 as the birthplace of Cardinal Josef Ratzinger, who that year became Pope Benedict XVI — the house where he was born and the church where he was baptised are the main pilgrimage draws.

Beyond the papal connection, Marktl is a pleasant base for the surrounding area. Nearby Burghausen has the longest castle complex in Europe, stretching over a kilometre along a ridge above the Salzach river. Altötting, one of Germany's most important Catholic pilgrimage sites, is also just a short drive away. The countryside along the Inn is gentle and green, good for cycling and walking.
