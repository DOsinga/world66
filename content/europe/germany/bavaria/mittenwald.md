Mittenwald is one of the best-known resort towns in the Bavarian Alps, set between the Karwendel and Wetterstein ranges, just 16 km from Garmisch-Partenkirchen and 30 km from Innsbruck across the Austrian border.

The Karwendel cable car takes you up to 2,385 metres for a breathtaking panorama of the Alps. In town, the main attraction is the violin museum — Matthias Klotz established Mittenwald as a centre for violin-making in the 17th century, and the craft continues here to this day. Many of the old houses in the centre have their facades decorated with painted scenes, often biblical figures, a Bavarian tradition called Lüftlmalerei that gives the town a storybook quality.
