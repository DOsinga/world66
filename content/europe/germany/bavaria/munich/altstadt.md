Munich's Altstadt is the city's historic heart, radiating out from Marienplatz where the neo-Gothic Neues Rathaus puts on its famous Glockenspiel show daily. Viktualienmarkt, just south of the square, is an open-air food market that's been running since 1807 — its stalls sell Bavarian cheeses, sausages, flowers, and exotic spices, with a communal beer garden shaded by chestnut trees at its center. The twin onion domes of the Frauenkirche, the city's cathedral and unofficial symbol, rise above the pedestrian zone and can be spotted from across the city. The Hofbräuhaus on Platzl is Munich's most famous beer hall — loud, touristy, and still somehow worth experiencing at least once for its sheer scale and history dating to 1589. Between these landmarks, the pedestrian streets are lively with shops, pretzel vendors, and buskers. The Altstadt is compact enough to walk end to end in twenty minutes, but there's enough tucked into its lanes to fill a full day.
