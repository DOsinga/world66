title: Beer and Oktoberfest Museum
type: poi
---

Housed in one of Munich's oldest surviving townhouses — a narrow medieval building dating to around 1340 — this small museum traces the intertwined histories of Bavarian brewing and the world's most famous beer festival. The exhibits walk you through 500 years of brewing tradition, from monastic origins and the 1516 Reinheitsgebot purity law to the first Oktoberfest in 1810 and its evolution into the massive spectacle it is today. It is charmingly low-tech, with vintage photos, old brewing equipment, and plenty of quirky memorabilia. The visit ends, naturally, in a small tavern downstairs where you can sample the subject matter firsthand. Budget about an hour.
