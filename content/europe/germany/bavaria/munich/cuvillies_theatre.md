title: Cuvilliés Theatre
type: poi
---

Hidden inside the Residenz complex, this intimate rococo theatre is one of the most beautiful in the world. Francois de Cuvillies designed it in the 1750s for the Bavarian court, and every surface drips with carved and gilded ornamentation — tier upon tier of red, ivory, and gold boxes rising toward a frescoed ceiling. Mozart's Idomeneo premiered here in 1781. The original wooden carvings were dismantled and stored during World War II, saving them from the bombing that destroyed the rest of the building. The theatre was painstakingly reassembled in a new wing and reopened in 1958. Visit as part of the Residenz museum ticket or, better yet, catch one of the occasional performances.
