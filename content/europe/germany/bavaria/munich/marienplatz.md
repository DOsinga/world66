Munich's central square has been the city's beating heart since 1158, and it still pulls in everyone from commuters to buskers to bewildered tourists craning their necks at the Glockenspiel. The mechanical clock on the New Town Hall facade performs daily at 11am and noon (plus 5pm in summer), reenacting a 16th-century jousting tournament and the Schaffler dance. It is charming exactly once; after that, use the crowds as your cue to grab a table somewhere quieter. The golden Mariensaule column at the center dates to 1638, celebrating the city's survival of Swedish occupation and plague. From here, every major sight is walkable.
