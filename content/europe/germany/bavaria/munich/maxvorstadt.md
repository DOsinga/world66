Maxvorstadt is Munich's museum powerhouse, home to the three Pinakotheken — the Alte Pinakothek for Old Masters, the Neue Pinakothek for 19th-century art, and the Pinakothek der Moderne for contemporary design, art, and architecture. This concentration of world-class collections within walking distance of each other is rivaled by few neighborhoods anywhere. Königsplatz, lined with neoclassical temples built by Ludwig I to echo Athens, adds a grand architectural punctuation mark. The Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität campus keeps the neighborhood youthful, with affordable lunch spots, bookstores, and bars catering to the student crowd along Türkenstraße and Amalienstraße. The area between the museums and the university is rich with small galleries and design studios, making it a natural draw for anyone interested in art beyond the big institutions. Maxvorstadt is less about nightlife or dining scenes and more about spending a thoughtful day moving between galleries, then unwinding in a quiet café afterward.
