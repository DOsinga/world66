title: National Theatre Munich
type: poi

Home to the Bavarian State Opera, this magnificent neoclassical building on Max-Joseph-Platz is one of Europe's great opera houses. The original 1818 structure burned down twice and was rebuilt each time with fierce civic pride — the current version, restored after World War II bombing, seats over 2,000 beneath an enormous chandelier. Wagner premiered Tristan und Isolde and Die Meistersinger here. Even if opera is not your thing, the building's Corinthian portico makes Max-Joseph-Platz one of Munich's most handsome squares. Guided tours run regularly, but catching a performance is the real experience — tickets can be surprisingly affordable if you book early.
