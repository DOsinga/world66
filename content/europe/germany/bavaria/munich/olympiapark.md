Venue of the 1972 Summer Olympics, Olympiapark remains one of Munich's most striking public spaces. The park is best known for its sweeping acrylic-glass tent roof, designed by Frei Otto and Günter Behnisch, which drapes over the stadium, swimming hall, and sports hall in a single flowing canopy. The design was revolutionary at the time and still looks futuristic.

The Olympiaturm (Olympic Tower) rises 291 meters and offers panoramic views of the city and, on clear days, the Alps. At ground level the park is a pleasant place to walk, jog, or picnic around the artificial lake. The stadium no longer hosts major football (Bayern Munich moved to the Allianz Arena in 2005) but still stages concerts and events through the summer.

Admission to the park is free. Tower tickets cost around 13 euros for adults. Take the U3 to Olympiazentrum.
