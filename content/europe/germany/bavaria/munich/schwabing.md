Schwabing is Munich's intellectual and bohemian quarter, the neighborhood where Kandinsky, Klee, and Thomas Mann once lived and where the city's counterculture took root. Today it's mellowed into a leafy, café-rich district popular with university students and young professionals. Its greatest asset is the English Garden, one of the world's largest urban parks — you can watch surfers ride the standing wave at the Eisbach, sunbathe on vast meadows, or drink a Maß at the Chinese Tower beer garden. Leopoldstraße, the broad boulevard running through Schwabing's center, is lined with cafés, bookshops, and galleries. The neighborhood's creative heyday may have passed, but the atmosphere remains more relaxed and intellectual than the buttoned-up city center. Side streets reveal small art cinemas, vinyl shops, and family-run Italian restaurants that have been here for decades. It's the best neighborhood for an aimless afternoon walk followed by a long coffee.
