type: poi

Munich's oldest parish church — locals call it Alter Peter — sits just south of Marienplatz and offers the best panoramic view in the city. The church itself dates to the 11th century, though its current baroque interior is a lavish 17th-century reworking full of gilded altars and ceiling frescoes. The real draw is the tower: climb 306 narrow steps past the bell chamber (bring earplugs if you time it wrong) and you emerge onto a cramped outdoor gallery with sweeping views over the Altstadt rooftops, the Alps on clear days, and the New Town Hall's Glockenspiel from above. It costs a few euros and is worth every step.
