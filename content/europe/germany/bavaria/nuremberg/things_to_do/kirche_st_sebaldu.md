latitude: 49.455997

Nuremberg's oldest church, built in Romanesque style between 1230 and 1273, then expanded twice: side naves and a Gothic choir in the 14th century, and the distinctive twin Gothic towers in the 15th. Several sculptures by Veit Stoss adorn the interior. The tomb of St. Sebaldus, cast in bronze in 1397, contains relics of the city's patron saint.
