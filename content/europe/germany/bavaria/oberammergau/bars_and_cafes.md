---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Travelers will be sure to
find wonderful food and drink in the cafes and bars in the Alpine village of Oberammergau . Both abound in this
year-round destination. They are filled with the charm of the area, the
wonderful mountain views and there are many locals who enjoy meeting and
greeting their foreign guests. While most bars and cafes offer a wide
assortment of food and drink, not all bars serve food here.

Cafes

Many cafes are located in
the various hotels in Oberammergau ,
and they feature local Bavarian food as well as libations. One of the most
popular is in, and outside, the Hotel Alte Post. Located right in downtown Oberammergau , tourists can
sit outside and enjoy a beer or Bavarian specialty while watching hand gliders
swoosh down into the valley below. Many smaller cafés sell hot drinks rather
than alcoholic drinks and some are dedicated to selling ice cream and coffee,
but most have a wide range of alcoholic options. Another popular café is
located in the Hotel Turmwirt, which is also in the downtown area and close to
everything. This one, like most, offers local fare in addition to more
international dishes.

Sauberstubn

One of the largest and
well-known of the cafes is called Sauberstubn. With room for more than 80
customers, this institution prides itself on its sauerbraten and deer steaks.
From the restaurant terrace, diners will have a view of the "Kofel," an
Oberammergau landmark, a rather low craggy peak
in the Alps that is a favorite among hikers.
In addition to its large number of meat entrees, Sauberstubn also has a good
assortment of salads, fish and noodle dishes. This café is a destination for
many tourists.

Bars

There are a large number
of bars in Oberammergau
and some of them (imbiss) serve food as well at a relatively low cost. Other
bars serve only drinks. And there is one unique bar called Kino Café that is a
combination of a bar and a movie theater. It’s a big hangout for young people.

For those not looking for
a swinging spot, there is the more upscale bar at the trendy Maximilian Hotel ,
which also features a beer garden where its own brew is served. Charley’s
Imbiss is an impressive rustic bar, offering views of the mountains from the
wooden tables outside the establishment. While the choice of snacks is not
huge, it does offer local delicacies for tourists to indulge in.
