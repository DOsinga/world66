---
title: "Eating Out in Oberammergau"
type: section
---

Oberammergau’s gastronomical identity is sketched by its
humble, value-for-money eating joints and imbiss bars that serve food
along with drinks. There are also some swanky fine dining restaurants
in up-market neighborhoods, and five star hotels along with a series
of international fast food chains. At heart, Oberammergau remains a
non-fussy eater’s paradise. The kind of eaters who love to polish off
their grub sitting on a nice, unassuming sidewalk café.

**Zur Rose **

This is one of the most popular eateries in the town and features
some great Bavarian cuisine. Try the sinful ham with cheese preparation
and the Kaiserschmarn. The shrimp salad, unlike a lot of other places,
actually has a lot of shrimp in it. There are innumerable fish dishes
and a wholesome green salad with a dressing twist. A hearty meal for
two should set you back by about 25 Euros. Zur Rose is located at
Dedlerstr 9 on the main street.

**Charly’s Imbiss**

This is a classic bar-cum-restaurant that is famous for its
delicious and large burgers. There is a whole variety of them,
including a salad burger for the more health conscious and an array of
generously filled meat burgers for carnivores. The place also has some
nice and light desserts.

**La Montanara**

La Montanara, located at Schnitzlergasse 10, is the place to head to
for mouth-melting Italian cuisine in a fine dining ambiance. They have
a great range of pizzas and pastas; especially notable is the pasta
served with four different varieties of premium cheese. The prices,
like most Italian fine dining hotspots, are slightly on the higher
side. There is a nice outdoor seating, ideal for lunch on a pleasant
summer afternoon.

**Ammergauer Maxbrau**

Ammergauer Maxbrau, located inside the Maximilian Hotel on Ettaler
Strasse 5, serves thoroughbred German cuisine, that though a tad too
pricey, is well worth it if you take into account the entire experience.
Have delicious crunchy pork to go with their in-house brewed beer.
There are also some nice, fancy steaks and unique soups for
connoisseurs of fine German cuisine.

**Zauberstub’n**

This is a pleasant English eatery that to begin with, boasts of a
nice terrace ambiance and rooftop view. The food offers great value for
money and the service is top notch. Here’s the scene stealer that
beats all the other pluses of this chic, al fresco diner. The owner
Vlado is an amusing entertainer, who performs superb table magic tricks
and helps you thoroughly enjoy your dinner. Zauberstub’n is housed on
the busy Ettaler Str. 58.

Small town Oberammergau is no gourmet haven, though it does feature
a range of fine dining options, humble sidewalk cafes and traditional
imbiss restaurants.
