---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Walking around the village to look at the painted facades (Lüftlmalerei) is the most distinctive activity in Oberammergau — the Pilatushaus, painted in 1784 by Franz Zwink, is the most celebrated example. The Rococo parish church, built between 1736 and 1742 by Josef Schmuzer, has a dramatic painted ceiling and a carved altarpiece worth seeing regardless of your interest in Baroque decoration. The Oberammergau Museum covers local woodcarving history from the Gothic period onwards, including a large collection of nativity cribs. In the surrounding area, hiking in the Ammergau Alps is accessible directly from the village. Schloss Linderhof (10 km) is an easy half-day trip; Neuschwanstein is about 45 minutes by car.
