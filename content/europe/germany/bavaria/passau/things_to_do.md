---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Passau's compact old town is packed with Baroque architecture, all built after the great fire of 1662. The main sights cluster around the confluence of the three rivers and can be covered on foot in a day.

The **Cathedral of St. Stephan** is a magnificent Italian Baroque structure housing the world's largest church organ — organ concerts are held daily in summer. The **Neue Residenz**, the episcopal palace, has a stupendous Rococo stairwell. **Veste Oberhaus**, the hilltop citadel of the prince bishops, offers the best view of the old town and the meeting of the three rivers. The **Town Hall** has lavishly decorated halls with monumental paintings depicting scenes from Passau's history.

The **Dreiflüsse-Eck** (Three Rivers Corner), where the blue Danube, green Inn, and dark Ilz meet, is the defining sight of the city and best appreciated from the Veste Oberhaus above.
