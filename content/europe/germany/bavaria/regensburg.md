Regensburg is one of Germany's best-preserved medieval cities, a UNESCO World Heritage Site on the Danube in eastern Bavaria. For over 600 years — from 1245 until Napoleon arrived in 1803 — it was a free imperial city and a prosperous trading centre. Unlike most German cities, it came through the Second World War largely unscathed, leaving an extraordinary concentration of medieval and early-modern architecture intact.

The Steinerne Brücke (Stone Bridge), built in 1135–1146, is Germany's oldest surviving stone bridge and gives the finest view of the city's skyline — a forest of towers rising above the Danube. St Peter's Cathedral is the most important Gothic building in Bavaria, with stunning 14th-century stained glass and the famous Domspatzen boys' choir. The old town is a tangle of narrow lanes, patrician tower-houses (Regensburg has more medieval towers than any German city), and lively squares.

Regensburg is also a university city with a young population, good restaurants, and a beer garden culture that rivals Munich's — at a fraction of the tourist crowds.
