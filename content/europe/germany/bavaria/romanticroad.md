Germany's most famous scenic route runs 350 km from [Würzburg](/europe/germany/bavaria/wurzburg) on the Main River south to [Füssen](/europe/germany/bavaria/fussen) in the Allgäu, at the foot of the Alps. Along the way it threads through a string of medieval towns, baroque churches, and rolling countryside that feels like an illustration from a Brothers Grimm fairy tale.

The big names are [Rothenburg ob der Tauber](/europe/germany/bavaria/rothenburg), with its perfectly preserved city walls, and [Augsburg](/europe/germany/bavaria/augsburg), one of Germany's oldest cities. At the southern end, [Neuschwanstein](/europe/germany/bavaria/neuschwanstein) near Schwangau draws enormous crowds. But the smaller stops are just as rewarding — Dinkelsbühl's colourful half-timbered centre, the Wieskirche near Steingaden, and the quiet wine villages of the Tauber valley.

You can drive the route in a day, but that misses the point. Allow at least three or four days to stop, walk around, and eat well. A Europabus runs the full route daily between April and October if you prefer not to drive. Cycling is another popular option — the route is well signposted and mostly flat in the north, hillier as it approaches the Alps.
