Rosenheim is a Bavarian market town at the confluence of the Inn and Mangfall rivers, about an hour southeast of [Munich](/europe/germany/bavaria/munich) on the road toward Austria. It is a pleasant, unpretentious place with a handsome old town centre — colourful facades, arcaded streets, and a pedestrian zone around the Max-Josefs-Platz.

The town is popular with German visitors for its traditional Bavarian character without the tourist crush of the bigger Alpine towns. The Herbstfest, Rosenheim's autumn beer festival, is one of the largest in Bavaria after Munich's Oktoberfest and has a more local feel. The Inn-Museum, housed in a medieval toll house on the river, tells the story of salt transport along the Inn — a trade that made the town wealthy.

The Chiemsee, Bavaria's largest lake, is about 20 minutes east, and the mountains around Kufstein and the Inntal are close enough for day trips. Rosenheim works well as a base for exploring the Bavarian Alps without the higher prices of resort towns like Garmisch-Partenkirchen.
