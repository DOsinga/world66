Schleissheim is a small town just north of Munich, easily reached by S-Bahn and well worth half a day. It is best known for its two palaces: the grand Neues Schloss Schleissheim — a Baroque pile commissioned by the Wittelsbach dynasty — and the older Altes Schloss nearby. Together they make up one of Bavaria's more impressive royal ensembles, without the tourist crowds of Munich's city centre.

The third major draw is the Flugwerft Schleissheim, an aviation museum that forms an annex to the Deutsches Museum. It houses a superb collection of historic aircraft and is one of the best of its kind in Germany.
