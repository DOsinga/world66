An easy way to make your trip to Wurzburg more fun is to take a few **Wurzburg day trips**. See more of the country and the continent by setting aside a day or two for any of these trips:

**Stuttgart**

Just a little more than 90 miles southwest of Wurzburg is Stuttgart, the capital and most populous city of the Baden-Wurttemberg state. Driving takes about an hour and a half. If you prefer public transportation, you can hop on a train in Wurzburg and ride for a little over two hours to Stuttgart for about US$52 each way.

Start your Stuttgart day trip by visiting Schlossplatz (Palace Square), the largest town square in the city and the place with the main shopping district, several museums, two castles and a park intersect. From there, you can visit Schlossgarten Park or you can shop along the longest pedestrian street in Germany, the Konigstrasse shopping street. The two castles are the Altes Schloss (Old Palace) and the Neues Schloss (New Palace). One of the most popular museums along the Schlossplatz is the Wurttembergishes Landesmuseum, a State Museum with artifacts related to the area's history dating back more than two thousands years.

**Plzen in the Czech Republic**

One of the best reasons to take a day trip during your visit to any European city is that it's so easy to see other countries in a day. From Wurzberg, you might choose to visit Plzen in the western part Czech Republic. It's easy to get to in a day and it's the fourth most populous city in the country, which means it offers plenty of activities. You can drive the 190 miles east in about three hours, or you can get there in about the same amount of time with a few connecting trains through Nuernberg and Schwandorf.

Beer fans rejoice, as Plzen is home to the Czech Republic's largest distillery. A trip to Plzen without imbibing a glass of Pilsner Urquell or Gambrinus is a trip a beer enthusiast will regret. You can also tour the Plzensky Prazdroj Brewery. Alcohol aside, you can count on seeing beautiful architecture, such as the Renaissance Town Hall and the Gothic St. Bartholomew's Cathedral. Plzen is also home to the European continent's second biggest synagogue, the Moorish Revival Great Synagogue. You also can't miss an underground tour of the tunnels below the city, as they're some of the longest underground tunnels in all of Europe.
