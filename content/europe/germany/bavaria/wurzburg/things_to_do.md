The charming town of Wurzburg offer plenty of **Wurzburg things to do**. There is no shortage of interesting history, grand castles and food. Wurzburg things to do will fill the imagination with jousting, romance and the long lasting remains of WWII. What are Wurzburg things to do?

**Watch Theater at Mainfranken Theater**

Having been through turbulent times, this theater has come back from the brink of total ruin after being bombed in 1945. After the bombing, hard core teachers and students refused to let this theater die and continued to hold performances on the stage of the ruin theater. It was rebuilt on the former rail station and reopened in 1966. This struggling theater has seen some hard financial times and has kept its cool and composure. In 2005 the first Theater Summer was held with operettas and musical talents from the year before. Any time one travels to Wurzburg, this theater is definitely a place to visit.

**Visit Juliusspital**

A hospital founded by Julius Echter, in 1576, was both a place for the infirm and a place to marvel at. With an inner courtyard and gardens to wander, this hospital will delight the traveler with a pavilion that was built in 1714 and fountain from the Baroque era. In 1945 the hospital sustained some damage and has been reconstructed. The painted wood carving at the entrance, indicate that it is the foundation stone for the structure, built around the late 1500's. If the traveler looks closely, they may spot the green man that graces parts of the building's exterior. Enjoy a great glass of wine and fine lunch at the Juliusspital.

**Enjoy at Mozart Festival**

Wurzburg hosts this wonderful festival in the celebrations of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's life and music. Local musicians do live performances displaying the wonderful works of the composer. Filled with food and fine music, this festival will give the traveler a tiny glimpse into the man and his music.

**Explore the Market Square**

This town square gives the traveler easy access to the shops and restaurants that line the area. Buy fresh produce or a bratwurst at the food stand within the square. During the Christmas season the square takes on a new attitude with seasonal knick knacks and all the fine treats of the season. The traveler cannot miss this square, as most of the sights are situated around it. It is a great place to sit, eat a meal and enjoy the unique personality of Wurzburg.
