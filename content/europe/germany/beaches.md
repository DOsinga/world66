---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Germany has two distinct coastlines: the **North Sea** (Nordsee) in the northwest and the **Baltic Sea** (Ostsee) in the northeast.

The Baltic coast is the more popular beach destination. The water is calmer and slightly warmer than the North Sea, and the sandy beaches on the islands of Rügen (Germany's largest island), Usedom, and Hiddensee are genuinely beautiful. Rügen's famous chalk cliffs (Königsstuhl) are a highlight of the whole coastline. The small resorts of Binz, Sellin, and Göhren on Rügen have their distinctive white seaside architecture intact.

The North Sea coast has the East Frisian Islands — a string of barrier islands off Lower Saxony — popular with German families for their wide tidal beaches and fresh air. Sylt, the northernmost island, has a reputation for fashionable excess and matching prices.

Germany's beach resorts are busy in July and August. The *Strandkorb* — a hooded wicker beach chair, rented by the day — is the defining symbol of German coastal holidays.

Urban beaches have become popular in recent years: Berlin in particular creates temporary riverside beach bars and sandy areas along the Spree and Müggelsee in summer.
