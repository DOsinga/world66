Berlin is one of the most fascinating capital cities in Europe. With its vibrant history and unique architecture Berlin promises to captivate its visitors with everything from scenic beauty to daring stories of escape. Choosing this former East German city as a vacation spot will astound visitors. In just a small area of the city there are so many wonderful things to do for people with all ranges of taste.

**Berlin Zoo
**For children and animal lovers the Berlin Zoo promises to impress. Most recently made famous by the birth of the polar bear Knut, this zoo provides several exhibitions and education opportunities. Germany's biggest media star since Boris Becker and Claudia Schiffer, Knut is still in residence. It is possible to see him but autographs can be a challenge.

**East Side Gallery
**For lovers of raw art the East Side Gallery might be of interest. A word of warning if you visit in the winter, dress warmly. The East Side Gallery is graffiti art on what remains of the Berlin Wall. Although not your typical art gallery it does provide an interesting look at the street art Berlin offers. It is also free so those visiting on a budget can stretch their pennies further.

**Check Point Charlie
**Once one of the most feared border crossings in the world, Check Point Charlie still stands as a reminder of what life was like in former East Berlin. The Check Point is no longer operational and visitors can pass freely from one end of the city to the other.

**Museum at Check Point Charlie
Those that visit this museum will be moved beyond belief. For the adventurer, daring stories of escape fill the room. Located in the old Barracks at Check Point Charlie the museum is filled with everything from escape devices and heart wrenching stories. those with iron hearts will be moved to tears.**
