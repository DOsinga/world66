The "*German-American Volksfest*" is a great relict from the
times when the American troops were stationed in Berlin and Germany.
Every year in summer (July / August) the fair opens its gates for
Berliners and international visitors. Until 1994 it was organized
mainly by the American Allies. It was a place were Americans (mostly
the troops) and Germans could meet and get to know each other better. A
lot of German-American marriages began here...
Even though it has become a bit commercialized nowadays it's still fun to go there and enjoy the ambiance.

The *Volksfest*
has two parts: the fairground with the rides (usually "taken over" by
teenagers) and the American village. Every year another U.S. region or
city is presented here. You can get American and tex-mex food like
hamburgers, spare ribs and tacos. The food is quite expensive, but very
fresh. There is a stage with live music and in some years a rodeo
arena.

**Opening hours**:
Everyday 2 p.m. to 11 p.m.,
on Saturdays and Sundays until midnight

**Entrance fee**:
1,50 Euro
(On Wednesdays it's "Family Day": you can get 50% off on most of the rides,
on Fridays it's "Ladies' Day", reduced prices for the rides for ladies.)

**Location:
**At bit off the beaten path at Clayallee / Argentinische Allee, Berlin-Zehlendorf,
at the former Truman-Plaza (corner of Clayallee and Argentinische Allee).
Take the subway U3 (get out at "Oskar-Helene-Heim") or the busses M11, 110, 115, X10.
There are two entrances to the *Volksfest*: at the corner of Clayallee and at Marshallstrasse.
