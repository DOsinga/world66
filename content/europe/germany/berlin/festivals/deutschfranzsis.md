The "*German-French Volksfest*" opens its gates every summer from June through July at the Central Fairground ("Zentraler Festplatz").

The fair originates from the times when the Allied troops were stationed in Berlin. Until 1994 it was organized mainly by the French Allies to make the French and Germans meet and get to know each other better. On July 14th, the French national holiday, there is often grand fireworks.

The *Volksfest* consists of the fairground with the rides and the French village. Every year another French region or city is presented here. They serve what is regarded as "typical" French food. Nevertheless you should not miss trying the "*Elsässer Flammkuchen*" (Alsatian Tarte Flambée). Even though it's very expensive (about 7 Euro), its worth a try. It's simply yummy!

If you have a bit time left and you want to get to know a bit more of Berlin's history, take a stroll through the former French quarter (Quartier Napoléon). It is situated right next to the fair. Here was the location of the French Army's barracks and the housing area for the French soldiers with their families. Today it's the Julius-Leber-Kaserne, the barracks of the "Bundeswehr", the German Army.

**Opening hours:**
Monday, Tuesday and Thursday 3 p.m. to 11 p.m.,
Wednesday and Sundayto 2 p.m. to 11 p.m.,
Friday and Saturday 2 p.m. until midnight.

**Entrance fee:**
1,50 Euro
(On Wednesdays it's "family day". You can get 50% off on most of the rides.)

**Location:**
Not far from Tegel Airport in Berlin-Reinickendorf at the Zentraler Festplatz, subway U7 to "Jakob-Kaiser-Platz" and then bus X21, 121, 225, or U6 to "Kurt-Schumacher-Platz" and then bus X21, 121,128, 225.
