**History**

This Berlin museum was actually opened on June 14th 1963 and became a small island of freedom for those that wished to escape from East to West Berlin. The location, just next to the checkpoint provided the perfect opportunity to view border crossings and look for weaknesses. When the border restrictions were lifted in 1989, leading to the eventual fall of the Berlin Wall and German Reunification, the island of freedom soon became a moving tribute to those that aided so many in escape.

**Exhibits

**The museum is full of devices and contraptions that people would use to hide from border guards. Specially designed suitcases, cars, special guns that wouldn't fire for border guards, even a homemade hot air balloon complete with the true story of how and entire family used it for escape are housed in the museum. Escape stories of people are in abundance showing their bravery and the lengths they were willing to go to escape the oppressive regime that had taken over their once thriving city.

**Art

**Pictures and drawings of people who escaped or risked their own lives to help others over the border are on show. However one of the most emotional collections of drawings is arguably an album of sketches and paintings created by school children depicting their thoughts on what life was like on the other side of that menacing wall.

**Videos

**Traveling through the museum and seeing all that it has to offer takes you on quite an emotional journey. The final stop is where one needs the tissues. With all the stories of sadness, bravery and tragedy they end on a positive note. Visitors are treated to the entire reunification story from the day the wall started to crumble to the night Germany reunified for the first time since the cold war. The museum is open every day of the year from 9am-10pm.
