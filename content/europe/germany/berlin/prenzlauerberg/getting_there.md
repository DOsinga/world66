---
title: "Getting There in Prenzlauer Berg"
type: section
---

One of the most exciting and distinctive districts in Berlin is the
area known as Prenzlauer Berg. This has become a magnet for up and
coming artists from both America and Europe. Here is how to get there.**
**

**Location**

Prenzlauer Berg is situated within the Pankow District of Berlin. It
can be approached via Mitte from the southwest,
Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg from the south, Lichtenberg from the east and
Weissensee from the north. The best mode of getting into Prenzlauer
Berg from these regions is by road in a private vehicle, using either
the U-Bahn or the S-Bahn routes. Vehicle hires are available from all
these regions.**
**

**Plane and Train**

If you are arriving from another country, the quickest way to get to
this part of Berlin is by plane. Once you're on the ground in Berlin,
make arrangements to travel on the subway. A quick ride on the
Eberswalder Strass, and you will find yourself in Prenzlauer Berg.**
**

**Arriving by Vehicle**

If traveling by vehicle, the best way to arrive in Prenzlauer Berg is
by utilizing one of two specific roads. Those are the U-Bahn
Eberswalder Strasse or the S-Bahn Scholnhauser Allee. If using these
routes, you will have to get off at the Pankow station, which is a
station for the S-Bahn and the U-Bahn.

Another option available to the visitor is utilizing transportation
services that are available in the city. Getting around is easier if
you take a taxi. There's also a public bus service that can help you
take in all the sights and sounds of this historic neighborhood.
