Built 1788-1791 as a victory gate by Carl Gustav Langhans. Berlin once had 18 city
 gates - this is the only one remaining. Badly damaged in the war, it was rebuilt in 1957 and a new casting of the Quadriga satue on top was made in West Berlin. After the wall was built the Gate became a landmark and symbol of the division and reunification of Germany.
