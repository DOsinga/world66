Named after the old postal code for eastern Kreuzberg, SO36 has been a fixture of Berlin's underground scene since the late 1970s. The venue started as a punk club -- Iggy Pop, the Dead Kennedys, and Die Toten Hosen all played here -- and has evolved into one of the city's most eclectic nightlife spots.

The weekly program rotates through genres: Monday's "Electric Ballroom" draws the techno crowd, while the long-running "Gayhane" party (monthly, on a Saturday) mixes queer culture with Turkish and Arabic pop in a way that could only happen in Kreuzberg. Admission runs €8-15 depending on the night. Doors usually open around 10pm or 11pm. Check the website for the current schedule.
