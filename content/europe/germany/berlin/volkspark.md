Berlin's oldest public park, opened in 1848 and now one of the busiest green spaces in the city. It holds the **Märchenbrunnen** (Fairy Tale Fountain), with sandstone sculptures of Brothers Grimm characters around a baroque water feature; two grassy hills ("Mont Klamott") built from WWII rubble; summer open-air cinema; and beer gardens that fill up as soon as the weather warms.
