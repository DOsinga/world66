---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Berlin is worth visiting any time of year, but the mood shifts dramatically with the seasons. Summer (June–August) is warm, social, and crowded — rooftop bars open, the parks fill up, and the city stays light until 10pm. May and early June hit a sweet spot: warm enough for terraces, without the peak-season crowds. Winter is harsh but atmospheric: Christmas markets with glühwein and handmade gifts appear in every neighbourhood, and the cold strips the city back to its more austere, melancholic self — which suits Berlin's history rather well.
