Rheinsberg is a small town in northern Brandenburg, set on the shore of the Grienericksee and surrounded by forests and lakes. It is best known for Schloss Rheinsberg, a graceful palace where the young Frederick the Great spent his happiest years before ascending the Prussian throne. The writer Kurt Tucholsky later immortalised the town in his novella *Rheinsberg: A Picture Book for Lovers*, and Tucholsky fans still make the pilgrimage.

The palace grounds are open for walks along the lake, and the Schloss itself houses a music academy and a literary museum. Boat tours on the Grienericksee and the connected waterways are a good way to see the surrounding Ruppiner Seenland. At Pentecost the town hosts a jazz festival with live music around the clock.
