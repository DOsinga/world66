---
title: "Spreewald"
type: location
---

Spreewald is the green lung of the Berlin Area. The towns of Lubben and Lubbenau, only 13 km apart, rival as the charm capital of the Wald. The nicest thing to do in the Spreewald is to visit the forrest by boat. There are small punt boats that can take you over the canals. 



Hiking and walking are also a very good option. The tourist office in Lubben sells maps with wlaking routes. The nicest hike is probably from Lubben to Lubbenau (13 km). 

When you have time you should try to visit Lehde as well, the Venice of the Spreewald. It has a museum that has nice examples of original Sorbian architecture. 

The Spreewald is easily reached by train to Lubben.
