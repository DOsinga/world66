---
title: "When to Go in Bremen"
type: section
---

Bremen, known as the multifaceted city, is a bustling port city along the River Weser with a rich history going back over a thousand years. Located in the northwest of Germany near the North Sea, Bremen has a climate that stays much cooler than the southern towns. Grey, overcast skies prevail much of the year, bringing rain and hail with them. No matter what time of year you visit Bremen, an umbrella is always useful. The climate is temperate, with warm summers, cool winters, and precipitation year round. The best time of year to visit Bremen depends on what activities you plan to do, as every season has its charms, and there are public festivals, art exhibitions, and plays basically every month.

**Winter**

Winter is from November to March and is quite cold with temperatures hovering in the 30s and low 40s (Fahrenheit). Most winter nights turn frosty due to the daily precipitation and humidity. On average, all but four days during December receive precipitation. Despite the seemingly uncomfortable weather, winter is a very popular time to visit Bremen. Tourists just can't resist the quaint, yearly Christmas Market along the Schlachte Embankment and other Christmas festivities. Winter marks the beginning of Bremen's winter sports season,as well, which runs from November to April. Peak ski season is from December to March, so if you're trying to avoid high prices and crowded hotels and attractions, it's best not to plan your trip during December and January. However, if skiing is specifically what you're going to Bremen for, by all means, December through March is the time to go despite the high prices.

**Summer**

Summer, from June to August, is much more comfortable when it comes to weather. Temperatures rise to an average of 73 degrees F, although during the day because of the extended hours of sunlight, temperatures can rise to the mid 80s or higher. Although there is more sun in the summer, hailstorms can still pass through the area unexpectedly, so be prepared. A swim suit is a must if you travel to Bremen during the summer; there are plenty of public pools to swim in and various riverside areas to sunbathe in. It is probably the best season if you plan to explore the city by foot, checking out The Schnoor, the oldest district in Bremen, seeing the famous Town Musicians statue, wandering along the Schlachte Promenade next to the river, or taking a walk to The Roland on market square, which has been a sort of symbol of independence in the city since 1404.

**Tourist Season**

The main tourist season is during July and August. Hoards of tourists crowd the city upping the prices of everything from food to hotels. Hostels are full of students and school groups during those months, and it's harder to get a true sense of the city with so much outside influence. If you plan to visit during summer, June is the time to go because there are fewer visitors and more deals. May and September are also excellent months to visit Bremen. There are no crowds, and although the temperatures cool down a bit, there are still plenty of things to do--like visiting Beck's Brewery, walking around historic Bremen, or going to the Universum Science Center.
