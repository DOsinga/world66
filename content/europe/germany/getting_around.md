## By Train

Germany's rail network is excellent. Deutsche Bahn (DB) operates a dense web of long-distance ICE (high-speed), IC, and regional trains connecting virtually every town. ICE trains link major cities fast: Berlin to Munich in under 4 hours, Frankfurt to Cologne in about an hour. Book in advance at [bahn.de](https://www.bahn.de) — early booking discounts (Sparpreis tickets) can be substantial. On the day, full-price tickets are expensive.

The **Deutschlandticket** (€63/month in 2026) is a flat-rate subscription giving unlimited travel on all local and regional public transport across Germany — buses, trams, U-Bahn, S-Bahn, and regional trains (RE, RB). It does not cover ICE or IC long-distance trains. It is sold as a monthly subscription, cancellable by the 10th of each month, and is personal and non-transferable. For anyone spending more than a few days and moving between cities, it is usually worth it.

## By Car

Driving in Germany is straightforward. The Autobahn is fast and well maintained, with no general speed limit on many stretches (though 130 km/h is recommended, and some sections have permanent limits). Traffic can be heavy around Frankfurt, Cologne, and Munich. There are no motorway tolls for private vehicles. Parking in city centres is expensive and often scarce — trains are almost always faster and easier within cities.

Inner cities in many German towns are covered by low-emission zones (Umweltzonen). Foreign vehicles generally need to comply; check whether your rental car includes the required sticker.

## In Cities

Every major German city has good public transport: U-Bahn (metro), S-Bahn (suburban rail), trams, and buses. Day tickets are good value. The Deutschlandticket covers all of these. Cycling is popular in most cities — [Berlin](/europe/germany/berlin), [Hamburg](/europe/germany/hamburg), and [Munich](/europe/germany/bavaria/munich) all have extensive bike infrastructure and rental options.

## By Air

For domestic flights, the train is usually faster and more central once you factor in airport time. Flying domestically makes sense mainly from Berlin or Hamburg to Munich or Frankfurt if you can't face the 4-6 hour train journey.
