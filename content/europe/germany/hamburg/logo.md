Logo is one of Hamburg's most enduring small live music venues, tucked into the university quarter near the Dammtor S-Bahn station. The room holds only a few hundred people, which means even mid-level touring acts feel genuinely close. The programme leans towards British and American indie, alternative, and alt-country — the kind of bands that play 500-seat venues across Europe. It has been running since the 1970s and has a loyal, unpretentious crowd. Tickets are usually cheap, the beer is cold, and there is no backstage mystique — just a dark room and loud music.
