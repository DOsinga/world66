---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Hamburg's harbour and waterways lend themselves to boat trips — tours of the massive container port, cruises on the Alster lakes, and canal trips through the Speicherstadt warehouse district are all worthwhile. The harbour tour in particular gives you a sense of the scale of Europe's third-largest port.

For trips beyond the city, the towns of Lubeck and Luneburg are both about an hour away by train. Lubeck's medieval Altstadt is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and Luneburg has one of the best-preserved medieval town centres in northern Germany. The North Sea coast and the Hamburg Wadden Sea National Park are reachable for a long day trip.
