Limburg an der Lahn has one of the most photogenic old towns in Germany. The cathedral, perched on a rock above the River Lahn, is visible for miles — a seven-towered Romanesque and early Gothic structure painted in its original orange and white, looking almost impossibly dramatic. Below it, the Altstadt is a tight maze of half-timbered houses dating back to the 13th century, many of them meticulously restored and tilting at angles that defy engineering.

The old town is small enough to explore in an hour or two, but it rewards a slower pace. The Bischofsplatz at the foot of the cathedral has good views down to the river, and the Alte Lahnbrücke (old bridge) is the classic vantage point for photographing the cathedral and old town together. The Staurothek in the cathedral treasury holds a relic of the True Cross in a Byzantine case — one of the finest pieces of medieval metalwork in Germany.

Limburg sits on the main rail line between [Frankfurt](/europe/germany/hesse/frankfurt) and Cologne, making it an easy day trip from either city. The Lahn valley in both directions is good cycling and canoeing country.
