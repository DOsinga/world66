[Frankfurt](/europe/germany/hessen/frankfurt) is the 5th largest city in [Germany](/europe/germany) and is
located on the Main River. Out of all of continental Europe, Frankfurt
is the largest financial center. This city may not seem very important
compared to Berlin or Munich, but it is in fact the transportation
center in Germany, boasting of one of the busiest airports in Europe
and one of the largest train stations in Europe. Frankfurt is also home
to one of the high-speed autobahns. A city with such attractions is a
sure bet in having some great activities for those on a budget.

**Getting There**

**Lufthansa**
is a very popular German airline. When going to Germany, you definitely
want to take a look at the Lufthansa website to check prices, but if
they seem to high, try looking at **cheaptickets.com**, **expedia.com**, or
another multi-airline searcher for better prices. Remember that flying
during the week will usually get you better prices than flying out on a
Friday or Saturday, and, if at all possible, going during off-season
will often spring you better savings, as well.

**What to Do**

**The
Frankfurt Cards** come in either 1- or 2-day choices and are sold for
both individuals and groups. For just one adult, the price would be
around $12 for 1 day or $18 for 2 days, or if you are in a group with
under 5 visitors, you can get an excellent deal on the card that will
give you the price of $21 for 1 day and $34 for 2 days. The card gives
you free use of public transportation within the city and to and from
the airport. You will also get discounts to almost everything in the
city from motorized tours to participating retail outlets, including a
50% discount on the entrance fee for 20 **museums**. Go on a **river cruise**
or to an **opera** at a great price. Worth noting, though, is that the
prices for these cards are going up slightly in 2010.

**Where to Stay**

Located
near the city center is **Frankfurt Hostel**, just across the street from
the main train station and only a walk away from the theater, opera,
and downtown area. You will get free internet service and places to
store extra bags. Single, double, and dorm rooms are offered, prices
starting at $27 per bed per night. **The 5 Elements Hostel** is a popular
accommodation for tourists. Also centrally located, a bed in one of the
dorm rooms starts at $30 per night.
