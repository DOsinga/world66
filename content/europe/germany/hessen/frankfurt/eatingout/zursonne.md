Despite what many guides say, the genuine cider taverns are not in Sachsenhausen -- locals know they are in Bornheim or Seckbach. An all-time favourite is the over 100-year-old Zur Sonne, housed in a picturesque timber-frame building from 1768.

In warmer months large crowds gather in the garden under chestnut trees, but it is in winter that its charm really shines, when the kitchen serves succulent game dishes with homemade dumplings and red cabbage. Cider is what the place is all about, but beer and wine drinkers are also well taken care of. Long wooden tables are shared, and the entertainment value is high. Reachable from the Messe with the U4 in about 15 minutes.
