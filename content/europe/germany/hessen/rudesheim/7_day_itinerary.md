Here is a 7 day **Rudesheim itinerary **will help you to make the most of your trip to this city.

**Day 1- Bask in the Glory of Rudesheim Music**

Siegfrieds Mechanisches Musikkabinett, is the first German museum that is dedicated solely for musical instruments. The exhibition covers an area over 400 m². The musical instruments stored here belong to different centuries. There are around 350 self-playing instruments varying from a musical box to a piano-orchestra. You can get an understanding of how different instruments work through the exhibits. The museum is open from March to December and has special facilities for the disabled along with multi-lingual guided tours.

**Day 2- Relish the Rudesheim Wine**

Rudesheim is known for the distinct varieties of wine and has a special museum dedicated for this purpose. The Rheingauer Weinmuseum Bromserburg is evidence to Rudesheim’s 1000 year-old wine-growing history. The ancient Bromserburg Castle is a World Heritage site with ancient trees that date back to the 16th century. It also has many barrels and pumps of wine displayed in the gardens of the castle. The museum is open from mid-March to the end of October.

**Day 3- Medieval Torture Museum**

The museum area encompasses around 1,000m² and will provide you with an insight into medieval torture systems. The museum has a collection of several torture instruments. Visitors can also get information about ancient practices of witchcraft, witch-hunts and burning of witches. It is open daily from the month of April to November and on weekends from December to March.

**Day 4- Visit the ****Germania ****Monument**

Germania is situated on the ridge of Niederwald in the Rhenish-Westphalian Slate Mountains. This monument is around 38 meters in height and was built to celebrate the formation of the new German empire in the year 1871. Visitors can also visit the monument via the cable car which goes through the vineyards.

**Day 5- Enjoy the Local Cuisine at Drosselgasse

**You should specially assign 1 day to devour the exotic dishes of** **Rudesheim. Drosselgasse is the most famous street in Rudesheim which has several well-decorated restaurants. You can enjoy your food amidst live musical entertainment. This place is usually crowded with tourists.

**Day 6 – Hiking Trail to Rheinsteig**

If you want to participate in any adventurous activities then you should surely embark on a hiking trip to Rheinsteig. This trail passes through the woodlands and vineyards. You can enjoy spectacular views of the castles as you pass through the ascents and descents.

**Day 7- Auf Der Lach**

This recreational park has an outdoor pool, miniature golf course along with a tennis court. The park also houses an adventure playground along with an indoor pool. If you are planning to visit Auf Der Lach in a small group then ensure that you contact the authorities to book the adventure playground well in advance.
