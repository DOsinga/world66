---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Rudesheim"
type: section
---

If you are looking for **Rudesheim family travel** ideas, this area has plenty to offer. Rudesheim has a long history, as it is part of a UNESCO world heritage site. The town is on the bank of the Rheine River in the famous Rheine wine region.

**Exploring the Drosselgasse**

A touristy area that is worth a visit is the Drosselgasse. This is a short street full of beautiful buildings. You can find many wine bars and restaurants, and there is always music playing and something fun going on from midday into the wee hours of the morning during the summer. This is found in the center of town and is worth a few pictures, as it is very picturesque. A trip to the clock, known as the Glockenspiel, in Drosselgasse should not be missed. The Glockenspiel rings every hour, and its doors open to reveal different wooden figurines.

**Taking the Cable Car to the Monument**

Tak a fun ride on a cable car up to the Niedewald Monument. If you visit early in the morning there is virtually no crowd, and you get great views of the vineyards as you travel by cable car. The monument itself is a huge statue of Germania. There is a lovely garden at the base, and the views from this spot are well worth the trip. Bring your lunch up and have a picnic in the middle of the day.

**Historic Buildings**

A great historical building is the Adler tower, which is over 1,000 years old and built in the Gothic style. This tower is also known as the Eagle Tower, as it once housed the Eagle Inn. It was originally connected to the old town wall.

Another historical spot is the Bromserburg Castle, which is the oldest in the Rheine region. When this castle was originally built, it was in the river and surrounded by water. The walls are very thick, and managed to sustain themselves through all battles and attacks. The city bought the castle in 1941 and turned it into a wine museum.

**Automated Musical Instruments**

A great museum for those with children is the Broemserhof, which houses the museum of mechanical instruments. Everyone in the family loves this museum, as there are more than 350 exhibits of mechanical or self-playing instruments. There are specific rooms dedicated to special instruments' sounds, such as the violin sound room. The museum is found in the Broemserhof castle, which is a beautiful example of architecture.
