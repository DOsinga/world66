title: "Getting Around in Rudesheim"
type: section
---

**Rudesheim transportation **is quite well developed, making it a popular tourist destination in Germany.

Rudesheim is a small town and therefore tourists can explore it on foot. It also close to Assmannshausen and you can travel between the 2 by train or walk along the Rhine (6 to 7 km). Another option is using the cable car which will take you up to Niderwald, while a chair lift will take you back down to Assmannshausen. You can get a glimpse of the enticing Rudesheim vineyards if you follow the Riesling Route which leads along the Rhine.** **Rudesheim is well-known for Drosselgasse which is lined with wine taverns and gardens. You can reach the place by foot or via the cable car or chair lift.

**Bicycle Ride

**You can explore the city of Rudesheim with a bicycle. There are several shops that deal in renting bicycles. Rudesheim is also known for the number of bike trails that are perfect for both leisurely sightseeing to cross-country mountain biking. The national highways from Rudesheim to Bingen are closed for cars and buses on the last Sunday of June but are open to bikers and in-line skaters. The ‘Vulkanpark Radeweg’ cycling route that runs from Anderach on Rhine to Mayen is suitable for families.

**In-Line Skating Routes**

Rudesheim can also be explored via skates and has in-line skating routes in the Wiesbaden and Mainz region. You can also be a part of the longest in-line skating tour that runs 135 km.

**Train or Bus**

The rail station of Rudesheim is well-connected to the regional towns and main cities. Rudesheim's rail station is situated on the Wiesbaden-Koblenz line, while the bus service in this region is operated by the Omnibusverkehr Rhein-Nahe GmbH. Buses are a good way to travel within the city. The fares are also quite reasonable.
