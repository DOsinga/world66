title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Rudesheim"
type: section
---

Rudesheim is a popular tourist town in the Rheine region, with several **Rudesheim must do’s **that should not be missed. You can easily reach the town by train which makes a great day trip. Of course, you can completely explore the area by staying for a few days.

**Wine Country**

Rudesheim is in the famous wine region of the Rheine. You can find some world class Riesling here. Many of the wine bars and taverns in town offer local wines. Visit the Rheingau wine museum in Bromserburg castle, where exhibits provide fascinating information on the history of the wine region as well as the viticulture used. You can easily see the vineyards along the river and, for further experience, take the Riesling route: a long hike that winds along the river and brings you through many Riesling vineyards until you come to Wiesbaden. There is also a wine festival each year, where a “Wine Queen” is crowned.

**The Monument and Views of the City**

One of the best spots to see views of the city and river is at the Niederwalddenkmal monument. This monument is near the forest on a hill found in the Taunus range. One side of the hill is covered in vineyards, and the other is forest. You can see the vineyards by taking the cable car up to the monument. This monument is a huge statue of Germania that was built at the end of the 1800s. It makes for a great trip.

**A Unique Medieval Museum**

The Medieval torture museum is an interesting museum that will interest everyone in the family. Some of the many exhibits do make viewers wince, but it is very educational and interesting. A scary thought is the fact that several of the same torture methods are still used today. There are some very interesting exhibits that specifically deal with the witch hunts and the role of the church and inquisitors.

**Asbach**

Asbach Ulrat is a grape product that is very famous in Germany, and is made by distilling grapes much longer than usual, and then aging the alcohol in oak barrels. Asbach is typically used in a drink that is reminiscent of Irish coffee. You can stop in at the Asbach visitor center to try this drink and see how Asbach is made.

**Mechanical Musical Instruments**

Siegfried’s mechanical musical instrument cabinet is the largest collection of automated musical instruments in the world. This museum is housed in a beautiful castle and has more than 350 different exhibits.
