---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Wiesbaden"
type: section
---

**Wiesbaden nightlife **offers plenty of entertainment options for travelers who want to have some fun after dark. Whether your version of the nightlife entails drinking or not, you're sure to enjoy at least one of the following popular places for nighttime entertainment:

**Casino Wiesbaden and the Kurpark Beer Garden**

At Kurhausplatz 1 in the middle of Wiesbaden is the Casino Wiesbaden, where you can spend the evening gambling and enjoying the music shows put on by the casino. You'll have your choice of game machines, roulette, poker, and Black Jack, to name a few. This casino has been a part of the Wiesbaden landscape since the late 1700s, so enjoying its classic and elegant architecture is worth the casino visit alone.

When you're done gambling, or if you just want to bypass that altogether, head for the Kurpark Beer Garden out back. The calming ambiance allows you to sit back and enjoy some genuine German beers and finger foods among the trees while listening to calming jazz music.

Dress is formal at the casino. Men should wear ties, and ladies should wear dresses or business suits. It's best to take a taxi or public transportation, since there aren't a lot of parking spots. Expect to spend at least $100 if you intend to go gambling. If you're headed straight for the beer garden, you should expect to spend $10 to $20 per person.

**Nova Lounge**

The lounge at Rheinstrasse 43 qualifies as a trendy, sleek nightclub for the young and the young at the heart. Relax in a lounge with minimal, modernist decor. The food you can order is as artistic as the decor, and is presented on stark white plates. Vegans will find plenty of options, but so will seafood lovers and those looking for dessert. If you want to come in for a nightcap, there's a full bar featuring mostly hard liquor and wine. Prices are average: about $10 to $20 per entree and about $5 to $10 per drink.

**Hessisches Staatstheater Wiesbaden**

If you're looking to entertain at night with a family or you don't necessarily want to head straight to a nightclub, stop by Hessisches Staatstheater Wiesbaden at Christian-Zais-Strasse 3 first for an evening of entertainment. The theater is the stage for plays, operas, musicals, ballets, concerts, and children's programs throughout the year. If you're worried about your German comprehension, pick an opera or a ballet, which don't rely heavily on the audience's understanding of the spoken word.

Ticket prices depend on the scale of the performance, your seating choice, and the age of those in your party, but are generally reasonable for live performances. Students and military can get a 50% discount and disabled patrons can get a 70% discount. You can dress casually for this theater.
