Wiesbaden is an ancient city located in the southwest part of Hessen,
Germany. **Wiesbaden Must Do's** include visiting the architectural
sites, going to the spas and gambling at the Wiesbaden Casino.

**1. Wiesbaden Architecture**

Wiesbaden is a city that dates back to A.D. 6 with the establishment
of a Roman fort. There is evidence of the Roman Empire with the remnants
of the Aqueduct Heidenmauer. Other Roman ruins are still around the
city, allowing the visitors a glance into antiquity.

The Place Square, also known as the Schlossplatz, was built in 1849
as a ducal palace for the royal family. Visitors to this building will
learn about how the royal family lived there until World War I, and its
journey to becoming a Parliament building.

Wilheim I ordered the construction of the Kurhaus Theater in 1904.
Architecture buffs will be amazed by the Neoclassical style used in
making the building. The building is actually the longest hall in Europe
and part of it is used as a casino to fund the maintenance of the
building.

**2. Nerobergbahn Funicular**

This cable car trip, one of the most fun-filled rides in town, will
give you a complete panoramic view of the beautiful Wiesbaden city
beneath you. The 1888 line linking the city to the Neoberg hill is one
of the best ways you can collectively enjoy the beauty of this city.

**3. Griechische Kapelle (The Russian Orthodox Church)**

If you have taken a ride to the top of the hill in the funicular then
a visit to this beautiful Russian Orthodox Church situated here is
something that you cannot miss. Though you cannot capture images of the
church, you can purchase postcards of the beautiful interiors from shops
outside.

**4. Biebrich Palace**

At the main banks of the Rhine, you can get a clear view of the
Biebrich Palace, which is beautiful in its construction style. If you
want to enjoy the scenic beauty of the place as well as see some of the
historical castles around, the best way to do so would be to catch a
train from Wiesbaden that goes to Koblenz. This is where Mosel connects
into Rhine and you will view a lot of picturesque castles that you can
admire.

**5. Friedrichsbad Spa**

Wiesbaden has been famous for its spas since the ancient times. The
Romans recognized that the area has a substantial number of thermal
springs in the area. The springs are still in use today and are popular
among both tourists and locals. The Friedrichsbad is a spa in the
tradition of Roman baths. Visitors can get a facial, use a sauna or go
into the baths.=======================================================

Wiesbaden, the capital of the federal state of Hesse, is located in
south western Germany at the foot of the Taunus Mountains. The Wiesbaden Must Do’s are diverse and
interesting due to its multifaceted appearance: the refined atmosphere of a spa
resort city, the elegance of a capital city and the charm of its surroundings.

Impressive architecture, luscious parks and green areas as well as a
rich cultural offer are some of the alluring attributes of this elegant city on
the Rhine. Wiesbaden is also strongly recommended for all those people looking
for wellbeing related holidays because of its healthy hot springs. Here you
will find the top 5 Wiesbaden Must Do’s:

**Walk
around the Historic Old Town**

All first time visitors should take a walk around the
narrow passages of the old town. If you are interested in history you will like
the 18th and 19th century houses that are spread throughout the old town. You
should also visit the Heidenmauer Wall which is the oldest building in
Wiesbaden and is located on the side of the Roman gate. If you prefer to see the
lively side of the town, then you should head to Bäckerbrunnen “Baker's
Fountain” in Grabenstraße, the meeting point of the city.

**Relax
at the Kaiser-Friedrich-Therme**

Sauna and spa fans cannot leave the city without
visiting the Kaiser-Friedrich-Therme. Built on the former site of an old Roman
sauna, the Kaiser- Friedrich-Therme restores the spa luxury of the days of the
Emperor Wilhelm and fulfils all wishes modern guests might have.

You make your way through the different facilities,
according to your likes, and leave the place rested and relaxed. Water comes
from the thermal waters running in Wiesbaden, which gave the city its reputation
many centuries ago.

**Cultural
Visit to Kurhaus**

Architecture lovers will understand why the Emperor Wilhelm
called the thermal establishment "the most beautiful healing house in the
world." Built in 1907, its richly decorated interior underwent a thorough renovation
in 1987 and was carefully restored following the original look.

**Shopping
in Wilhelmstraße Street**

Smart and posh people will find their haven in Wilhelmstraße
Street. This area of the city represents the splendour of Wiesbaden. Here you
will find anything that is high quality and expensive: haute-couture, jewellery,
home accessories and many internationally renowned brands.

**Peaceful
Picnic at Mount Neroberg**

Those looking for a quiet day in touch with nature
will love the Mount Neroberg where you can take long walks and enjoy a
panoramic view of the city. The splendid meadows are ideal for a picnic or to
disconnect from the daily routine.
