Juist is one of the East Frisian Islands, a narrow sliver of sand about 17 kilometres long and barely 500 metres wide at most points. No cars are allowed on the island — the only motorised vehicles are the horse-drawn carriages and the ambulance. This makes it one of the quietest places on the German coast and a genuine escape for anyone who wants to walk or cycle through the dunes without traffic.

The island sits within the Lower Saxon Wadden Sea National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. At low tide, the mudflats between Juist and the mainland stretch for kilometres — you can walk to the island at certain times with a guide, though the ferry from Norddeich is the more reliable option. The tidal flats are an important stopover for migratory birds, and birdwatching is one of the main reasons people come outside summer.

The village of Juist has a small cluster of hotels, guesthouses and restaurants concentrated at the western end of the island. The rest is beach, dunes and sky. At the eastern tip, a nature reserve protects nesting areas from visitors during breeding season.
