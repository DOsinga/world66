Spiekeroog is the quietest and most unspoiled of the East Frisian Islands. No cars, no airport, no chain hotels — just a small village of brick cottages, a church dating to 1696, and kilometres of empty beach backed by high dunes. The island has a wild, windswept character that sets it apart from its more developed neighbours.

The village is compact and charming, with a few guesthouses, a handful of restaurants, and the oldest Protestant church in the East Frisian Islands — the Alte Inselkirche, which contains a ship model hanging from the ceiling in the old seafaring tradition. The island's old trees, unusual for the exposed North Sea islands, give parts of the village an almost garden-like feel.

Spiekeroog's beach is arguably the finest on the chain: wide, clean, and often deserted outside the school holidays. The eastern end of the island is closed to visitors to protect nesting birds, but there is still plenty of space. The Wadden Sea mudflats on the south side offer excellent guided walks at low tide.

The island is reached by ferry from Neuharlingersiel, with sailings dependent on tides. The crossing takes about 45 minutes. Since there is no car ferry, the island truly feels removed from the mainland.
