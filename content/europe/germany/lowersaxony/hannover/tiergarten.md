Not a zoo, despite the name — the Tiergarten is a 112-hectare recreational forest in the Kirchrode district where roughly 120 fallow deer roam free, much as they have since Duke Johann Friedrich set the first herd loose here in 1679. He had created the park for hunting but died on a journey to Italy a year before the first hunt. Today you walk in for free, look for deer, red deer in the Hubertus enclosure, and a long list of woodland birds and rare bats. The annual Tiergartenfest, held to thank local children for collecting acorns and chestnuts to feed the animals over winter, is a particular highlight.
