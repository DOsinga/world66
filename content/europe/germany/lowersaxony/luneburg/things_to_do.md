---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Lüneburg's appeal is largely in the walking: the medieval Altstadt is compact and almost entirely car-free in the core. The big draws are the old salt quarter around Am Sande, the leaning buildings along Auf dem Meere, and the Stintmarkt waterfront where crane warehouses once loaded barges. Beyond the architecture, the Deutsches Salzmuseum gives the whole city its narrative context — it's genuinely one of the better local history museums in Lower Saxony.
