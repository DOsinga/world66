Salzgitter is one of the more unusual cities in Germany — a post-industrial urban district in southeastern Lower Saxony created in 1942 by pooling 29 previously independent villages around the then-new Hermann Göring steelworks built to supply Nazi armaments. Two more villages were added in 1974. The 31 quarters still dot a remarkably large municipal area (over 220 sq km, one of the biggest in the country), with green fields and forested hills between them, and the biggest population centres at **Salzgitter-Lebenstedt** and the old spa town of **Salzgitter-Bad**. Few tourists come this way, but if you are passing through on the way between Hannover and the Harz mountains, there are a couple of pretty corners worth a short detour.
