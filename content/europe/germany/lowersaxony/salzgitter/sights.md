### [Burg Lichtenberg](/europe/germany/lowersaxony/salzgitter/sights/burg_lichtenberg)

If you like walks in the woods, you should not miss visiting the ruins of **Burg Lichtenberg** . The castle is said to have been built by Heinrich dem Löwen ("Henry the Lion"), one of the most powerful princes of his time, at the end of the 12th century. **Burg Lichtenberg** , stategically well situated at a slope of the Lichtenberg hills amidst the woods, served as one of Heinrich's bases to defend his power against the rulers of Hildesheim and Goslar.

### [Schloß Salder](/europe/germany/lowersaxony/salzgitter/sights/schlo_salder)

**Schloss Salder**, a pretty renaissance castle, was built in 1608.
Today it's a museum for local history from prehistoric times to the present.
It has a beautiful toy collection, that illustrates children's play for the last 200 years.
At the "cowshed" ("Kuhstall"), one of the barns that are built around the yard, local artists exhibit their works annually.

How to get there:
Salzgitter-Salder; Bus 612: get out at "Museumsstraße".

### ["Salzgitter Smeltery" - A Special Hotspot](/europe/germany/lowersaxony/salzgitter/sights/salzgitter_smelte)

Salzgitter is known for its steelmaking and steelworks industry. In Salzgitter-Watenstedt you can visit the **smeltery at the Salzgitter AG**. In a guided tour you are shown step-by-step how steal is produced. You can take a close look at the furnace and the roller plant.
Don't forget a shirt with long sleeves!

The tour costs € 10,50; you must be at least 16 years old.
