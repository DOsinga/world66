Wolfenbüttel is a small town just south of [Braunschweig](/europe/germany/lowersaxony/braunschweig) in Lower Saxony, with one of the best-preserved timber-frame old towns in northern Germany. Over 600 half-timbered houses line the streets of the centre, many of them dating from the 16th and 17th centuries when Wolfenbüttel served as the residence of the Dukes of Brunswick-Lüneburg.

The town's greatest claim to fame is the Herzog August Bibliothek (Duke August Library), which in the 17th century was the largest library in Europe. Gotthold Ephraim Lessing served as its librarian from 1770 until his death in 1781, and the Lessing House next door is now a museum. The ducal palace, a large Baroque schloss on the river Oker, houses a local history museum. Jägermeister, the herbal liqueur, was invented here in 1934 — the company's headquarters are still in town, though there is no distillery tour.
