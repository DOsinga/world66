Wolfenbüttel is a small ducal town a few kilometres south of [Brunswick](/europe/germany/lowersaxony/brunswick), with one of the most intact half-timbered old towns in northern Germany — partly because, unlike most of its neighbours, it came through the Second World War almost untouched. Narrow streets of crooked timber houses lead to the Schlossplatz, dominated by the white Baroque facade of the residence of the dukes of Brunswick-Wolfenbüttel.

The real treasure is the **Herzog August Bibliothek**, founded in the seventeenth century by Duke August the Younger and once the largest library in Europe. It still holds one of the world's most important collections of medieval and Renaissance manuscripts; Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz worked here as librarian, and Lessing wrote *Nathan der Weise* during his time in the town. The neighbouring Lessinghaus, where he lived, is now a small museum. Foodwise, Wolfenbüttel's other contribution to the world is **Jägermeister**, distilled at the factory on the edge of town.
