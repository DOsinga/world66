Burg Stargard is a quiet town of a few thousand people in the lake-strewn Mecklenburg countryside south of Neubrandenburg. Its name and reason for being is the **Burg Stargard castle**, perched on a hill above the town and reckoned to be the oldest surviving hilltop castle in northern Germany. Built in the thirteenth century by the margraves of Brandenburg, the complex still has its keep, towers and chapel, and the climb up gives a wide view over the rolling fields and lakes of the Mecklenburg Lake District. Beyond the castle there is not much to detain you, but the town makes a pleasant detour on a drive through the region.

Burg Stargard is a small town just southeast of [Neubrandenburg](/europe/germany/mecklenburgwesternpomerania/neubrandenburg) in the Mecklenburg Lake District, dominated by the brick-and-fieldstone castle that gives it its name. Built in the 13th century by the Margraves of Brandenburg, the Burg sits on a hill 90 metres above the surrounding countryside — the highest medieval hilltop castle in northern Germany — and is well preserved enough to wander through, with a small museum, a falconry centre, and views over the lakes and forests of the Tollensesee region.

Below the castle is a quiet town of about 5,000 people, half-timbered houses around a market square, the kind of place where everything closes early. The main reason to come is the castle itself; combine it with a day on the Tollensesee or a stop in Neubrandenburg.
