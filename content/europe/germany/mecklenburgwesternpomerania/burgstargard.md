Burg Stargard is a quiet town of a few thousand people in the lake-strewn Mecklenburg countryside south of Neubrandenburg. Its name and reason for being is the **Burg Stargard castle**, perched on a hill above the town and reckoned to be the oldest surviving hilltop castle in northern Germany. Built in the thirteenth century by the margraves of Brandenburg, the complex still has its keep, towers and chapel, and the climb up gives a wide view over the rolling fields and lakes of the Mecklenburg Lake District. Beyond the castle there is not much to detain you, but the town makes a pleasant detour on a drive through the region.
