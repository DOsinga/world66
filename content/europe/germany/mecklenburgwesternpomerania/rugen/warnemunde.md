Warnemünde is [Rostock](/europe/germany/mecklenburgwesternpomerania/rostock)'s seaside district, sitting where the Warnow river meets the Baltic Sea. It has been part of the city since 1323, but feels like its own place — a fishing village turned beach resort with a wide sandy beach, a historic lighthouse, and a lively harbour front.

Most of the cosy pubs and attractive shops line the Alter Strom (old channel), where fishing boats still tie up alongside the restaurants. Warnemünde is one of the most popular seaside resorts on the German Baltic coast, and a major port of call for cruise ships.
