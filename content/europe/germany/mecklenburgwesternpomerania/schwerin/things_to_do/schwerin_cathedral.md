This imposing three-naved red-brick Gothic basilica dates from the fourteenth century and is one of the finest examples of Brick Gothic architecture in northern Germany. The 117-metre spire was added in the 1890s and its lookout platform offers the best panoramic views in the city, taking in the castle, the lakes, and the surrounding countryside. The interior has a striking medieval triumphal cross and a beautiful bronze baptismal font from 1325.
