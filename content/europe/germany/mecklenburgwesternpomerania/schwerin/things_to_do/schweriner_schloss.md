Schwerin's fairy-tale castle sits on an island between the Schweriner See and the Burgsee, connected to the city by a bridge. The current building dates mostly from the mid-nineteenth century, built in a romantic historicist style that draws on French Renaissance chateaux, particularly Chambord. It serves as the seat of the state parliament, but the first floor is open as a museum with magnificent reception halls and ballrooms. The surrounding castle gardens are free to explore and perfect for a lakeside walk.
