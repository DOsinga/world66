Warnemünde is [Rostock](/europe/germany/mecklenburgwesternpomerania/rostock)'s seaside district, a fishing village turned beach resort that has been part of the city since 1323. It is one of the most popular seaside resorts on the Baltic Sea, with a wide sandy beach stretching for kilometres along the coast.

Most of the action is along the Alter Strom (old channel), a narrow waterway lined with cosy pubs, fish restaurants, and small shops. The lighthouse and the adjacent Teepott building, with its striking hyperbolic paraboloid roof, are the landmarks of the town. Warnemünde is also a major cruise port, bringing waves of day-trippers in summer.
