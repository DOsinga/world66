Warnemünde is a Baltic Sea resort that has been part of [Rostock](/europe/germany/mecklenburgwesternpomerania/rostock) since 1323, though it has retained the feel of a fishing village rather than a city suburb. Long sandy beaches, a lighthouse, and a lively waterfront draw visitors from across northern Germany throughout the summer.

The heart of the village is the **Alter Strom** (old river channel), a narrow waterway lined with fishing boats, restaurants, and souvenir shops. It is the most atmospheric part of town — particularly in the early morning when the fishing fleet is still out. The white lighthouse at the mouth of the channel is one of the most photographed spots on the German Baltic coast, and you can climb it for good views over the water.

The beach is wide and stretches for several kilometres, backed by the typical Baltic dune landscape. In summer it fills up quickly, so arrive early or head west along the shore to find a quieter stretch. The water is calm and relatively shallow — good for swimming once the season warms up.

Warnemünde also serves as a departure point for ferry crossings to Denmark and Scandinavia, and large cruise ships call here regularly in summer, which means the Alter Strom can get crowded on ship days.
