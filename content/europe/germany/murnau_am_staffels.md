Murnau am Staffelsee is a small Bavarian town at the foot of the Alps, about 45 minutes by train south of Munich. It sits between the Staffelsee lake and the Murnauer Moos, one of the largest intact raised bogs in Central Europe -- the landscape is open, green, and framed by mountains in every direction.

The town is best known for its connection to the Blaue Reiter movement. Wassily Kandinsky and Gabriele Munter lived here from 1909 to 1914, and the Munter-Haus (the "Russians' House") where they worked is now a small museum. The Schlossmuseum Murnau in the town castle has a good collection of Blaue Reiter paintings as well as local history. Murnau's main street, the Obermarkt, is lined with colourful painted facades and is one of the prettiest town centres in Upper Bavaria.

From Murnau you can easily reach [Garmisch-Partenkirchen](/europe/germany/garmisch_partenki) and the Zugspitze (Germany's highest peak), Oberammergau with its Passion Play tradition, and the castles of Ludwig II at Neuschwanstein and Linderhof. The Staffelsee itself is good for swimming and boating in summer.
