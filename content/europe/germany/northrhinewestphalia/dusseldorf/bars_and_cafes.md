---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Dusseldorf"
type: section
---

Dusseldorf, the capital city of the North Rhine-Westphalia
state and the second most influential city (economically), sees an
influx of tourists annually. Being an tourist city means providing
visitors with a variety of entertainment, restaurants, bars, and cafes.
Here (in no particular order) are some of the interesting bars and
cafes scattered around the Dosseldorf metropolitan area, with a little
something for everyone.

**Buttershaker **is located on Worringer Str. and is considered
an American restaurant/bar/cigar lounge. Most visit for the
bar atmosphere, resident and traveling DJ's, decent food, and unique
cigar lounge in a bar ambiance. Buttershaker has a decent happy hour
from 4pm to 7pm daily, offering discounts on food and drinks, and it
can get quite lively late night on weekends. The clientele is young and
trendy, and they like to dance. Buttershaker also has the capabilities
for private parties.

**Lauren's** is located at Bismarckstr. 62, and is more along the
line of a cafe/bar. Lauren's is the perfect place to meet up with
friends and be able to have a conversation over cocktails and
tapas. The menu is small and delicate, and the decor is simple and
relaxing. If you are looking for a quiet drink and light meal, Lauren's
is a great spot. The crowd is mixed, all ages, and the prices are
moderate.

**Cafe Liebevoll **is located in Oberkassel, just off of Ratinger
Str. This small cafe serves traditional German fare, with a long and
extensive menu. The bar is quite lively, day and night. Locals and
tourists both mingle within the walls of Cafe Liebevoll, where the food
is great, and the cheer is merry. Cafe Liebevoll offers happy hour in
the late afternoon on week days.

**Bossa Nova **is located on Neusser Str. and is considered a
Brazilian cocktail bar and cafe. Bossa Nova has a very eclectic menu,
serving traditional Brazilian dishes, traditional German
dishes, and a fusion of both. At Bossa Nova they prepare fresh squeezed
juices made to order, which they also use in cocktails, and the
cocktail prices rival most of the other bars in Dusseldorf. It isn't
hard to find Bossa Nova if you follow the stream of live music, which is
played there 7 nights a week. It's a great spot for something off the beaten
path.

**Guzel Voyage **is located at Konkordiastrasse 85, and at first you might be surprised to see the
streaming line of patrons waiting for a table. This small
bar/restaurant serves excellent Turkish cuisine. The menu is fairly
small, but the food is amazing. Take a chance on any of the dishes offered and odds are you won't be let down. Guzel's can get quite
lively in the evening, but be prepared to wait.
