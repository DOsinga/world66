Dusseldorf,
released by the Thomas Cook Publishing Company, is a travel guide in
the CitySpots series. The book is 144 pages long, and measures 6.2 x
4.2 x 0.5 inches. It will easily slip into a back pocket or a travel
bag, such as a backpack or attaché.

The City of Dusseldorf itself is
one of Germany’s two economic centers, and is renowned for its many
fashion and trade fairs. It's one of the major fashion centers of
Europe. The CitySpots guide will provide you with ample details on
fashion event dates and locations, along with detailed maps of the City
and suggestions for other events to take in while visiting.

The book
has a small section on German customs that you should be familiar with,
and provides a list of German phrases and vocabulary that you should
know, even if you do not sprecht de Deutche. It of course lists
reputable places to stay and restaurants where you may wish to dine. It
has an extensive list of Dusseldorf Brewhouses.

The Königsallee
("King's Avenue") is a very popular shopping destination in Dusseldorf,
and this guide book covers the area quite nicely, giving detailed maps,
shop locations and examples of the types of merchandise sold by each
shop. The images are acceptable, although not the magazine quality that
you get in some travel guides. You won’t be leaving this out on the
coffee table, but that isn’t what it is designed for anyway. It’s a
very functional, very informative travel guide for this lovely City on
the Rhine.
