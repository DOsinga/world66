Essen was the steel and coal capital of the Ruhr for well over a century, a city built on industry and reshaped by its decline. Today it is one of Germany's more interesting post-industrial cities — the Zollverein Coal Mine, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the most striking example of how the region has repurposed its industrial heritage into culture and tourism.

The Folkwang Museum holds one of Germany's finest collections of 19th- and 20th-century European art, from the Romantics through to the Impressionists and Expressionists. The Grugapark, a large public park on the western edge of the city, is a green escape from the dense urban fabric. Essen's shopping high street around the Limbecker Platz is a major draw for the wider Ruhr region.

Essen is a useful base for exploring the Ruhr, with good transport links to [Dortmund](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/dortmund) and Düsseldorf.
