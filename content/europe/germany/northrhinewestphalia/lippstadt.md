Lippstadt is a small town in eastern North Rhine-Westphalia, on the Lippe river between Paderborn and Soest. It was founded in 1185 as one of the oldest cities in the region and has a compact medieval old town centred on the 13th-century Marienkirche, whose unusual westwork tower is visible from across the flat surrounding countryside.

The old town is pleasant for a walk — half-timbered houses, a small market square, and the River Lippe flowing through the centre — but Lippstadt doesn't have the concentration of sights to justify a dedicated trip. It works well as a stopover on the way between Paderborn (with its famous cathedral and Carolingian Kaiserpfalz) and the Soest Börde region.
