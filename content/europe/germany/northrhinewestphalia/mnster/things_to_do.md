---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Münster's centre is compact and very walkable. The **Dom** (St Paulus Cathedral) dominates the old town and houses a notable treasury. The **Rathaus** (city hall) on the Prinzipalmarkt is one of the finest civic buildings in Westphalia — it was here that the Peace of Westphalia was signed in 1648, ending the Thirty Years' War. The nearby **Schloss** is worth a look from the outside for its Baroque façade.

The city also has a good cluster of museums: the **Stadtmuseum** covers local history and is free to enter, as is the **LWL-Museum für Naturkunde** (natural history, including a mammoth skeleton). The **Pablo Picasso Museum** holds a significant collection of Picasso's graphic work.

For a change of pace, rent a bicycle and explore the Münsterland beyond the city — the flat landscape and network of cycling paths make this one of the best cycling regions in Germany.
