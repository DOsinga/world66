Oberhausen is an industrial city in the Ruhr, wedged between [Essen](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/essen) and [Duisburg](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/duisburg). Like its neighbours, it grew rich on coal and steel and spent the late 20th century figuring out what to do after the mines closed. The answer, in Oberhausen's case, was shopping and industrial heritage.

The CentrO is one of Europe's largest shopping and leisure centres, built on the site of a former steelworks. It draws visitors from across the region. More interesting is the Gasometer Oberhausen, a former gas storage tank converted into an exhibition space. The building itself is extraordinary — 117 metres tall and 68 metres in diameter — and the installations it hosts use the vast interior to dramatic effect.

The Ludwig Galerie in the Schloss Oberhausen shows rotating exhibitions from the Ludwig collection, and the Kaisergarten park around the castle is a pleasant green space. Oberhausen is not a destination in itself, but the Gasometer alone justifies a stop if you are exploring the Ruhr's remarkable industrial-heritage-turned-culture corridor.
