The building has an impressive red sand-stone facade. It has large sculpted adornments and a garden for the priests, but no steeple. The church dates back to 1297, and was built by Johann Peter Jäger between 1763 and 1774. The Ignatius quarter was named after this church. The style of its construction is considered to be classicist, and is more modern than St. Augustin's Church which was built later. The development from the rococo style to the classicist can be followed by Johann Baptist Enderle.
