The main reason to visit St. Stephan is the nine stained-glass windows by Marc Chagall, installed between 1978 and 1985. The luminous blue glass depicting Old Testament scenes fills the Gothic interior with an unearthly light. Chagall created them as a symbol of Jewish-German reconciliation — he was 91 when the last window was completed.
