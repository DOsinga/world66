Lieser is a small wine village on the Mosel, tucked between steep vineyards just downstream from Bernkastel-Kues. The village takes its name from the Lieser stream, which tumbles down from the Eifel hills and meets the Mosel here. It is one of those places that perfectly captures the character of the Mosel valley — slate-roofed houses, terraced vineyards rising at improbable angles, and the river curving slowly past.

The main landmark is Schloss Lieser, a grand neo-Gothic wine castle built in 1884 by the Puricelli steel dynasty. The castle sits in a landscaped park and has been converted into a hotel, but even if you are not staying, the grounds are worth a stroll. The vineyards surrounding the village produce some excellent Rieslings — the Lieser Niederberg Helden is considered one of the top vineyard sites on the Mosel, producing wines of remarkable mineral intensity.

From Lieser you can walk or cycle the Mosel-Radweg in either direction — downstream to Bernkastel-Kues takes about twenty minutes by bike and brings you to one of the most picturesque medieval market squares in Germany. The village has a couple of small Weinstuben (wine taverns) where you can taste local wines with simple food. It is a quieter, less touristy alternative to staying in Bernkastel itself.
