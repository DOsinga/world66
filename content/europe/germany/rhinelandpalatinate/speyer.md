Speyer is a small Rhine city with an outsized architectural legacy. It was a free city of the Holy Roman Empire from 1294 until 1779, and its cathedral — the Kaiserdom, or Dom St. Maria und St. Stephan — is by most accounts the finest surviving Romanesque church in the world. Four Holy Roman Emperors are buried in its crypt.

The Kaiserdom was built in the eleventh century under the Salian emperors and remained the largest church in Christendom for many years. The reddish sandstone exterior is best seen from the east end, where the symmetry of the apses and towers is striking. The interior is vast and cool, with painted vaulting and a crypt that actually rewards close attention.

Beyond the cathedral, Speyer is a pleasant, walkable city. The Altpörtel, a preserved medieval city gate, stands at the western end of the main street. The city's Jewish heritage — it was one of the original cities of the ShUM alliance alongside Worms and Mainz — is recalled at the Judenhof, where a medieval mikveh (ritual bath) has been preserved underground. The ShUM heritage sites were designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2021.
