**Welcome to Trier**

When planning a trip to **Trier**, **Getting There** is at the top of any traveler's list. Located in the Rhinelad Palatinate State in south eastern state of Germany, Trier claims to be the country's oldest city with a foundation dating back to the 16 century BC. This city has a wonderful location that is not only blessed by being so close to the Luxembourg border, but also by being home to the Mosel wine.

**Getting There by Plane**

Given that Trier is located in close proximity to the Luxembourg border with Germany, travelers to this city can be combined with a visit to Luxembourg. The Findel Airport (IATA airport code: LUX) serves the city of Luxembourg and is just a 40 minute drive away from Trier. If you decide to visit Luxembourg before Trier, make sure to stamp your passport with the Schengen stamp so that you have no problems at the German border.

In Germany, the Frankfurt Hahn International Airport (IATA airport code: HHN) and the Saarbrucken International Airport (IATA airport code: SCN) are the closest airports to Tier. Both airports are about an hour drive away from Trier. If you're planning to arrive to the Frankfurt Hahn International Airport, make sure to check out the rates of Ryanair because it offers very competitive rates. To make sure that you get the lowest possible rate, book your flight at least a week in advance.

**Getting There by Car**
If you're planning to rent a car at Luxembourg or Germany, it is a good idea to apply for an international driver's license at your country of origin. Having an international driver's permit allows the car rental process to go much smoother in any European nation. The German highways A1 (passing through Koblenz), A48 (passing through Saarbrucken) and A64 (passing through Frankfurt) lead to Trier.
