---
title: "Wine"
type: section
---

Trier is the centre of the Mosel-Saar-Ruwer wine region, one of the most celebrated Riesling-producing areas in the world. Dozens of wine cellars operate right in the city centre, many offering tastings and cellar tours. The steep slate slopes along the Mosel, Saar, and Ruwer rivers produce intensely mineral, off-dry Rieslings that can age for decades.

Do not overlook *Viez*, the local apple wine — it is barely known outside the region but enormously popular here, especially fresh in September and October when it is still sweet and nearly alcohol-free. Later in the year it ferments to about 7%, and locals drink it mixed with water (*Viez-Schorle*) or lemonade.

German sparkling wine (*Sekt*) made from Riesling grapes is also increasingly good in this area. Late September and October, during the grape harvest, is the best time to visit — vineyards are busy, wine festivals pop up in every village along the Mosel, and some estates welcome volunteer pickers.

For tastings, try the Wein-Information at the Basilika, the Bischöfliche Weingüter on Gervasiusstrasse, or the Friedrich-Wilhelm-Gymnasium Güterverwaltung on Weberbachstrasse. The suburb of Olewig has several vintners offering tastings to walk-in visitors.
