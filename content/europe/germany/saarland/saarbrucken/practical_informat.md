Most of the **Saarbrucken Practical Information** that people need to know is that it is located along the Saar River and borders France. Because Saarbrucken borders France, the city has a strong French influence. This German city has a populous of 181,227 people, and speaks the Rhine-Franconian dialect of German. Saarbrucken is known for its production of iron and steel, sugar, beer, pottery, machinery, and construction materials.

**Historic Landmarks**

The bridge that crosses the Saar River was built in 1546 and remains as one of the oldest landmarks in Saarbrucken. There is also the Gothic church of St. Arnual, an 18th-century castle called Saarbrucker Schloss, and the St. Johanner Markt.

**Climate
**
Another bit of Saarbrucken practical information that people need to know is that the climate in Saarbrucken is mostly cool, with temperatures reaching their highest at 80 degrees in the months of June and July. While these months are the warmest during the year, they also receive the most rain. The coldest months in the year are December and January; these months have freezing temperatures.

Trains are the best way to get around in Saarbrucken. The railways include Inter City Express trains that transport passengers to nearby cities and local destinations.
