---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Dresden has an excellent public transport network. The **DVB** (Dresdner Verkehrsbetriebe) runs 12 tram lines and a dense bus network that together cover everything you are likely to want to see; a one-day pass is cheap and lets you hop on and off freely. For getting up to the villa suburbs on the north side of the Elbe, the two historic **Loschwitz cable railways** from 1895 and 1901 are worth taking for their own sake. And no visit to Dresden is complete without a trip on one of the **Saxon Steamship Company's** paddle steamers — eight of them, the oldest still-operating paddle-steamer fleet in the world — which ply the Elbe between the city, Pillnitz, and the cliffs of Saxon Switzerland. The **S-Bahn** suburban rail network connects the centre with Meißen, Pirna and the national park. Most visitors won't need a car; if you have one, leave it on the edge of the city and use the trams.
