---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Almost everything worth seeing in Dresden is within walking distance of the Elbe. On one side of the river lies the **Altstadt**, the rebuilt baroque heart — the Zwinger, the Frauenkirche, the Residenzschloss with its Grünes Gewölbe, the Semper Opera, the Brühl Terrace. On the other side is the **Neustadt**, much of it untouched by the 1945 bombing, and now the most atmospheric part of the city for wandering, with late-19th-century courtyards like the **Kunsthofpassage** (whose "Courtyard of the Elements" has musical drainpipes that play in the rain). Slightly further out, the **Pillnitz Palace and Park**, set on the Elbe upstream of the city, is worth a half-day trip; the easiest way to reach it is on the historic **Saxon Steamship Company** paddle steamers, which still run a regular service up the river.
