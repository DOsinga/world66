Bad Schwartau is a small spa town on the Bay of Lübeck, just north of the medieval port city of [Lübeck](/europe/germany/schleswigholstein/lubeck) and effectively a quieter suburb of it. It earned its "Bad" prefix — the German marker for a recognised spa — from the brine springs that still feed the local Sole-Therme bath. Most visitors stop here for the spa, the jam factory (Schwartauer Werke is one of Germany's best-known preserve makers), or simply as a calmer base from which to explore Lübeck and the Baltic coast around Travemünde a few kilometres further north.
