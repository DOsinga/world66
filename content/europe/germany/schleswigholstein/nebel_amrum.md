Nebel is the main village on Amrum, one of the North Frisian Islands off the coast of Schleswig-Holstein. The island is tiny — 10 km long and barely 3 km wide — but it has one of the best beaches in northern Europe: the Kniepsand, an enormous sandbank on the western shore that stretches for kilometres and is so wide you can walk for ten minutes from the dunes before reaching the water.

The village itself is a cluster of thatched Frisian houses, a small 13th-century church (St. Clemens), and the Öömrang Hüs, a preserved sea captain's house turned museum. The old cemetery next to the church has carved "talking headstones" from the 17th and 18th centuries that tell the life stories of the island's seafarers.

Amrum is quieter and less developed than neighbouring Sylt and Föhr, which is its main appeal. You can cycle around the entire island in a couple of hours, stopping at the Amrum lighthouse for views across the Wadden Sea to Föhr and the Halligen islands. The ferry runs from Dagebüll on the mainland via Föhr — the crossing takes about two hours and is part of the pleasure.
