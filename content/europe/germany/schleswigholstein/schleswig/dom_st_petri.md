Schleswig's cathedral is a Gothic pile of red brick that took several centuries to complete, and the result is imposing rather than graceful. But step inside and the Bordesholmer Altarpiece stops you cold. Carved by Hans Brüggemann between 1514 and 1521, it depicts nearly 400 figures across multiple tiers — one of the most detailed and technically ambitious pieces of late-Gothic woodcarving in existence. It was commissioned for a monastery in Bordesholm and moved here in 1666 when the monastery dissolved. The cloister garden is a calm spot.
