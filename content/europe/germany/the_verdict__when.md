Fifty-six years after World War II, Germany again participated in military actions against Yugoslavia. Ralph-Thomas Niemeyer fictionally puts German Chancellor Gerhard Schröder and Foreign Minister Joschka Fischer on trial for war crimes, using original quotations and a large collection of documentary evidence. By strictly adhering to German and international law, the fiction becomes uncomfortably real and pushes readers to question how governments justify war in the name of their people.
