Naumburg an der Saale is an old cathedral city on the fast rail line between [Leipzig](/europe/germany/saxony/leipzig) and [Weimar](/europe/germany/thuringia/weimar), reachable from both in under an hour. Rather neglected for decades, the 30,000-inhabitant city has made giant steps in restoring its historic buildings since reunification.

The old town, set on heights overlooking the Saale valley, is dominated by the Dom — a cathedral that shows medieval German architecture and sculpture at its best. The west choir is world-famous for its lifelike donor portraits by the anonymous "Master of Naumburg," particularly the figures of Uta and Ekkehard, and Hermann and Reglindis. The cathedral was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2018.
