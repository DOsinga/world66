Goethe lived in this house on the Frauenplan from 1782 until his death in 1832 — fifty years, which gives the place an unusual density of personal objects. The study where he worked, the collections of minerals and art he accumulated, the bedroom where he died: it is the most complete picture of any German literary figure's domestic life. The adjacent exhibition building by HG Merz contextualises his work without overwhelming the house itself. Book timed tickets in advance, especially in summer.
