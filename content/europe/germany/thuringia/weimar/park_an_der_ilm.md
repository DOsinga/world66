The park running along the Ilm river through the centre of Weimar was laid out under Goethe's supervision from the 1770s onward — he had a personal hand in shaping it for decades. It is an English-style landscape park: meadows, old trees, winding paths, and a few follies placed with care. Goethe's Garden House, where he lived before moving to the Frauenplan, stands in the middle of the park and is open to visitors. The park is free and open at all hours; it is the best way to arrive in Weimar feeling unhurried.
