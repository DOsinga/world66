Buchenwald was established in 1937 on the Ettersberg ridge, eight kilometres north of Weimar. Over 250,000 people were imprisoned here; around 56,000 died. The memorial site includes the preserved remains of the camp — roll-call square, the crematorium, the gatehouse with its clock stopped at 3:15, the time of liberation — and a substantial exhibition in the former storehouse. The bus from Weimar (line 6) runs regularly. It is not an easy visit, but it is an important one, and the way Weimar sits in its shadow is part of what makes the city worth understanding.
