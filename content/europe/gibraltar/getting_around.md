---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Gibraltar is only about 6.5 square kilometres, and the town centre is walkable in 20 minutes end to end. For most visitors, the best transport is their own feet.

**On foot** is the default for town. Main Street runs almost the full length of the commercial centre and is pedestrianised for much of its length. The older residential streets are steep in places but navigable.

**Cable car**: the cable car to the top of the Rock has been closed for refurbishment and is not expected to reopen until around 2028. In the meantime, the Upper Rock Nature Reserve is accessible by minibus tour (the standard "Rock Tour" takes about 1.5–2 hours and covers the main sites), on foot via marked paths, or by taxi.

**Rock Tour taxis**: the most popular way to see the Upper Rock. Licensed guides operate from the border and from Cathedral Square. A full Rock Tour by taxi takes about 1.5 hours and covers St Michael's Cave, the Great Siege Tunnels, the Moorish Castle, and the Apes' Den. Agree on the price and what's included before you set off.

**Buses**: a small fleet of buses covers the main routes. Line 2 goes to Catalan Bay on the eastern side; line 3 goes to the airport and the frontier; line 4 serves the southern tip at Europa Point. Fares are cheap. The central stop is Market Place.

**Taxis**: metered taxis are available from the border crossing and at Cathedral Square and Casemates Square. They're a practical option for a quick trip to the beaches or Europa Point.

**Cycling** is possible but the town is hilly and traffic on the main roads can be heavy. Most visitors don't bother.
