---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

**By air** is the easiest way to arrive. Gibraltar Airport (GIB) sits right at the northern edge of the territory — the runway actually crosses the main road into Spain, which closes briefly for each landing. British Airways flies from London Heathrow and London City; easyJet operates from London Gatwick, Manchester, and Bristol. Flight time from the UK is around 2.5–3 hours. The terminal is tiny and a short walk from the town centre, or take bus 3 (runs every 30 minutes).

**By road** from Spain is straightforward. The border crossing at La Línea de la Concepción is open 24 hours. Waits can be long at peak times — an hour or more is not unusual. Pedestrians and cyclists cross more quickly than cars. Once across, the town is compact enough to walk. If you're driving, note that parking in Gibraltar is tight and expensive; most visitors leave cars in La Línea.

**By bus** from Spain: regular buses run to La Línea from Algeciras, Málaga, and other Andalusian cities. From La Línea bus station it's a 10-minute walk across the border. There is no direct long-distance bus into Gibraltar itself.

**By ferry**, a regular service runs to Tangier, Morocco. Cruise ships dock frequently at the port.

**Entry requirements**: EU nationals can enter on a valid national identity card. British citizens need no visa. US, Canadian, Australian, and New Zealand passport holders can visit visa-free for stays up to 90 days. Holders of a Schengen visa should be aware that Gibraltar is outside the Schengen area — you will need a valid re-entry visa for Spain if you plan to continue into the EU. Nationals who need a UK visa will generally also need a Gibraltar visa; check before travelling.
