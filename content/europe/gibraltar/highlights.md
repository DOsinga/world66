---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Gibraltar is a strange and compelling place. A British overseas territory of 6.5 square kilometres sitting at the entrance to the Mediterranean, on the southern tip of Spain, within sight of Morocco — politically and culturally unlike anywhere else in Europe.

The Rock dominates everything. That 426-metre limestone ridge is riddled with caves and tunnels, home to a troop of semi-wild monkeys, and offers views across two continents on a clear day. It's not just a backdrop — it's the reason Gibraltar exists where it does, and exploring it (by Rock Tour taxi, minibus, or on foot) should be any visitor's first priority.

**Don't miss:**

**St Michael's Cave** — the most impressive of the Rock's cave systems, with extraordinary formations and a natural concert hall in the largest chamber. One of the best natural attractions in southern Europe.

**The Great Siege Tunnels** — hand-carved through solid limestone during the 1779–1783 siege, with the WWII extensions forming a labyrinth of underground military infrastructure. Genuinely impressive engineering, well presented.

**The Barbary Macaques** — Europe's only wild primates, living in troops across the Upper Rock. They're bold, entertaining, and will steal your lunch if given the chance. Keep bags zipped.

**National Day (10 September)** — if the timing works, this is the best day to visit Gibraltar. The entire population pours onto the streets in red and white to celebrate Gibraltarian identity. The atmosphere is warm and entirely genuine.

**Catalan Bay** — a small fishing village community squeezed between the cliffs and the eastern shore. The most characterful of the beaches, with good seafood nearby.

**The Strait itself** — dolphin-watching trips from the marina take you into waters where three dolphin species are resident year-round, with orca passing through seasonally. One of the most reliable dolphin-watching locations in Europe.

**Calentita** — Gibraltar's national dish, a baked chickpea flour cake of Genoese origin. Humble, filling, historically interesting, and hard to find outside the territory. Seek it out.
