---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

Gibraltar's nightlife is concentrated in a few areas and punches slightly above its weight for a territory of 34,000 people. The main hubs are Casemates Square in the centre and the Ocean Village marina development to the south.

**Casemates Square** is the liveliest outdoor space, with a cluster of bars and restaurants spilling onto the square. In summer it stays busy well into the night. This is where you'll find a mix of locals and visitors, and where most of the impromptu outdoor entertainment happens.

**The Star Bar** on Parliament Lane, just off the northern end of Main Street, claims to be Gibraltar's oldest bar. The atmosphere is relaxed and unpretentious, with a clutter of photos and memorabilia on the walls and the occasional live music night.

**Lord Nelson's** in Casemates Square stays open later than most, regularly hosts live bands, and is the official home of the Gibraltar Rugby Club. It has won recognition as a live music venue and the on-site restaurant is popular with locals.

**O'Reilly's** at Ocean Village is Gibraltar's long-standing Irish pub, with proper Irish draft beer and a traditional Victorian interior. Good if you want a quieter drink away from the Casemates crowd.

**The International Casino Club** on Europa Road accepts all visitors with no membership required. Smart casual dress. There's also a terrace restaurant with views over the Bay.

The live music scene is small but active. The Gibraltar International Jazz Festival each October has become a genuine highlight in the calendar, drawing well-regarded artists. St Michael's Cave also hosts occasional concerts — the acoustics in the cavern are remarkable and an evening there is a memorable experience.
