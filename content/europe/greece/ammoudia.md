Ammoudia is a small village on the Ionian coast of northwestern [Greece](/europe/greece), at the mouth of the Acheron River — the river that in Greek mythology led to the underworld. A boat trip up the Acheron passes through a gorge of striking natural beauty, with rare plant and bird species protected under the Natura 2000 programme.

Following the river inland, you reach the ancient Nekromanteion (Oracle of the Dead), where pilgrims once came to communicate with the souls of the departed. It is one of the more atmospheric archaeological sites in Greece, built over underground chambers that were believed to be the entrance to Hades.

The village itself is quiet and unpretentious, with a long sandy beach and shallow water that is safe for young children. The surrounding area is green and lush, a mix of river delta and coastal wetland, and the combination of mythology, nature, and beach makes Ammoudia a rewarding stop on this part of the coast.
