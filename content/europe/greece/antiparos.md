Antiparos is the quiet neighbour of [Paros](/europe/greece/paros) — a small Cycladic island reached by a short ferry from Parikia, with a pace of life that feels several decades behind the rest of the Aegean. The main town (Chora) is a cluster of whitewashed houses around a 15th-century Venetian kastro, and mopeds are the best way to explore the rest.

The island's star attraction is the Cave of Antiparos, one of the oldest known tourist sites in Europe — visitors have been scratching their names on its walls since the 15th century. The stalactite and stalagmite formations inside are spectacular, reached by a long descent of concrete steps into the hillside. The cave sits on the mountain above the village of Agios Ioannis.

Antiparos also draws windsurfers to its western coast, where reliable Meltemi winds blow through the channel between the islands. The beaches are quiet and clean, with warm, shallow water and enough tree shade to survive the afternoon heat.
