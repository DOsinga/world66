---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Corfu"
type: section
---

Corfu, also known as Kerkyra in Greek, is the northern most island
in Greece. The sea water is calm and clear and the island offers many
things to do and see. Here is a list of the Top 5 Must Do's:

**1.** **Beaches
**

The beaches of Corfu are some of the most
beautiful in the world offering stunning views, clear waters, and a
multitude of water sports - choose from jet skis, pedalos, banana boat
rides, kayaking, water skiing, windsurfing, paragliding, kite surfing,
snorkeling, and even scuba diving is available. Whether you are looking
to kick back and relax near the calm waters, or jump into an activity,
Corfu beaches are just what the doctor ordered.

**2.** **Kassiopi **

This is a charming little fishing village on the
north east corner of Corfu, with a traditional port. Here you can watch
the local fishermen bring home their morning catch, or have a seat in
one of the many harbour front bars and watch the world go by. The
resort in Kassiopi is more inclined for those seeking a bit of lively
nightlife. The beach in Kassiopi is a pebble beach, but there are
plenty of sunbeds and umbrellas for hire. Showers, bathrooms, and
changing rooms are available on the main beach. Make sure to glance up
to your left on the hillside to see the remains of the Kassiopi Castle.

**3. ****Sissi's Palace
**

Located in Achilleon, and built in 1892,
Sissi's Palace is a lovely Corfu day trip loaded with breathtaking
views and gorgeous gardens. The palace was built by Empress of Austria
Elisabeth of Bavaria, also known as Sissi. She had the palace build on
top of the mountain overlooking the sea on one side, and the mountains
on the other, with the mythical hero Achilles as its central theme.
Inside the palace you can visit the museum that houses the relics that
Sissi collected, and her portraits. Tours are available, but to beat
the crowds try to arrive in the early morning.

**4.** **Palaiokastritsa
**

Considered to be one of most beautiful
villages in Corfu, Paloikastritsa is a renowned tourist spot. Boasting 5 beautiful
bays, a monastery high on the mountaintop, and ideal conditions for
snorkeling and diving, leaves Palaiokastritsa a spot not to be missed.
Some of the best seafood on the island can be found in this picturesque
village in one of the many excellent restaurants scattered about town.
Palaiokastritsa (meaning old castle place) is the place where Odysseus
disembarked and met Nausicaa for the first time.

**5**. **Aqualand**

If you are traveling with children, then no trip
to Corfu will be complete without a stop to Aqualand - one of Europe's
largest water parks. The water is clean, the slides and attractions are
numerous, and the fun is unlimited. Opened daily from May - October.
