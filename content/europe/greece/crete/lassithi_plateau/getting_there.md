---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The Lassithi plateau sits in the Dikti Mountains above the north coast of [Crete](/europe/greece/crete). All approaches involve a winding climb, but the roads are paved and the views are worth it.

**By bus** -- KTEL buses run from [Iraklion](/europe/greece/crete/iraklion) bus station A to the plateau once a day. It is cheap and comfortable, but the single daily service means you will likely want your own transport for flexibility.

**By car from Iraklion** -- Take the coastal road east toward Agios Nikolaos. Just before Hersonissos, a signed road heads south toward the Lassithi plateau and the Dikti Mountains. The climb to the Kera pass is steep but rewarding -- from the top you get a sweeping view over the entire plateau.

**By car from Agios Nikolaos** -- Head west toward [Iraklion](/europe/greece/crete/iraklion) for about 10 km, then turn south toward Neapolis and follow signs for Tzermiado and the Lassithi plateau. The road is paved but narrow and winding.

**By car via Malia** -- Malia sits roughly halfway between Iraklion and Agios Nikolaos on the main coastal road. Follow signs south for Tzermiado and the plateau.

**By car via Kastelli** -- A quieter alternative from Iraklion. Head south through [Knossos](/europe/greece/crete/knossos), Peza, and Kastelli before reaching Tzermiado. Slower, but you will see more of the inland villages.
