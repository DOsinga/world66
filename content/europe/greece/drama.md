Drama is a city in northeastern Greece, in the region of Eastern Macedonia and Thrace, set in a broad plain between the Rhodope mountains to the north and the Kavala plain to the south. It is one of the greenest cities in Greece, with numerous springs and streams running through the city centre, feeding parks and gardens that give it a pleasant, almost alpine character unusual for Greece.

The city is the capital of the Drama regional unit and has a modest but genuine old quarter with some Ottoman-era buildings and a lively central square. Drama is known across Greece for its spring festival celebrating water and flowers, and for its short film festival which has been running since 1978. It is not a major tourist destination, but it makes a comfortable base for exploring the Nestos river gorge to the east and the monasteries of the Kavala region. The Philippi archaeological site is about 40 kilometres away.
