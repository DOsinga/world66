Eleusis, modern Elefsina, is one of the most important sacred sites of the ancient world, and also one of the most jarring to visit. For nearly two thousand years, pilgrims walked from Athens to be initiated into the Eleusinian Mysteries, secret rites honoring Demeter and Persephone that promised a blessed afterlife. The secrecy was so well kept that we still do not know exactly what happened during the initiation. Today the archaeological site sits in the middle of an industrial port town, surrounded by refineries and cement works, which makes the experience surreal rather than serene.

The ruins themselves are substantial. You can trace the Sacred Way that initiates walked, see the foundations of the Telesterion where the mysteries were performed, and visit the small museum that houses votive reliefs and a model of the sanctuary at its peak. The Telesterion was enormous, designed to hold thousands of initiates at once, and even in its ruined state the scale is impressive.

Elefsina was named European Capital of Culture in 2023, which brought new cultural infrastructure, public art installations, and a renewed effort to connect the ancient site with the modern city. The town is only about 20 kilometers from central [Athens](/europe/greece/athens), easily reached by suburban rail or bus, making it a straightforward half-day trip for anyone interested in ancient religion and the stranger corners of Greek history.
