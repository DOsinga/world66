Epirus is a mountainous region in northwestern Greece, bordering Albania to the north and the Ionian Sea to the west. It is one of the most dramatically beautiful parts of Greece, with the Pindus mountains running through its interior and the Vikos Gorge — one of the deepest gorges in the world — cutting through its heart.

The region has a character quite distinct from the islands or from Athens. The Zagori villages, a collection of around 46 traditional stone settlements in the mountains above Ioannina, are among the finest examples of traditional Greek architecture and are now a UNESCO Global Geopark. The regional capital Ioannina sits on the shores of Lake Pamvotis and has a well-preserved Ottoman old town on a small island in the lake where Ali Pasha, the powerful Ottoman ruler, was assassinated in 1822. Epirus is excellent for hiking, white-water rafting on the Voidomatis river, and winter skiing in the mountains. The coast at Igoumenitsa is a major ferry port for Italy.
