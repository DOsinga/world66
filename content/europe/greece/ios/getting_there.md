Unless you have your own sailing yacht, you'll have to use the public transportation which means the ferries:

**Ferry**

Four daily ferries link Ios to
[Piraeus](/europe/greece/piraeus) (7 hours),
[Paros](/europe/greece/paros) (2, 5 hours) and
[Naxos](/europe/greece/naxos) (1 hour and 15 minutes).

Daily ferries sail to
[Santorini](/europe/greece/santorini) (1 hour) and
[Mykonos](/europe/greece/mykonos) (4 hours).

Five weekly ferries go to Sikinos (20 minutes) and Folegandros (1, 5 hours).

Four ferries per week link Ios to Anafi (2 hours) and two weekly to Syros (2 hours and 45 minutes).

One ferry a week goes to Amorgos (2, 5 hours), Kimolos (2, 5 hours),
[Milos](/europe/greece/milos) (3, 5 hours), Sifnos (5 hours), Serifos (6 hours) and
[Crete](/europe/greece/crete) (6 hours).

**Flying Dolphins (hydrofoils)**

Three daily Flying Dolphins and more link Ios to
[Santorini](/europe/greece/santorini) (30 minutes) and
[Naxos](/europe/greece/naxos) (50 minutes).

Two to three daily hydrofoils go to
[Paros](/europe/greece/paros) (1, 5 hours),
[Mykonos](/europe/greece/mykonos) (2 hours and 45 minutes) and
[Tinos](/europe/greece/tinos) (3, 5 hours).

One to two daily Flying Dolphins go to Syros (2 hours and 15 minutes).

Six weekly Flying Dolphins leave from
[Ios](/europe/greece/ios) to Sikinos (10 minutes) and Folegandros (45 minutes), four weekly to Amorgos (2 hours) and three weekly to Anafi (1 hour).

Two weekly hydrofoils sail to Koufonissia (3, 5 hours), Shinoussa (3 hours), Iraklia (3, 5 hours) and
[Crete](/europe/greece/crete) (2 hours and 45 minutes).

One weekly Flying Dolphin goes to Donoussa (2, 5 hours).

**Catamaran**

Daily Catamarans link Ios to
[Santorini](/europe/greece/santorini) (30 minutes),
[Naxos](/europe/greece/naxos) (50 minutes),
[Paros](/europe/greece/paros) (1, 5 hours), Syros (2 hours and 45 minutes) and Rafina (4 hours).
