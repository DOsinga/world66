Heraklion (also spelled Iraklion or Iraklio) is the capital and largest city of Crete and its main point of entry for flights and ferries. It is a busy, working city — noisy and commercially dense — but it contains some of the finest Minoan archaeology in the world.

The Heraklion Archaeological Museum is one of the great museums of Europe. Its collection spans the entire Bronze Age Minoan civilisation, from simple figurines to the extraordinary frescoes from Knossos — the bull-leaping scene, the Blue Ladies, the Prince of Lilies — displayed at a quality the ruins themselves cannot match. Knossos itself, the mythological home of the Minotaur and the most complex palace complex of the Minoan world, is 5 km south and is the most visited site in Greece outside Athens.

In the city, the Venetian harbour with its 16th-century Koules fortress is the most atmospheric spot — particularly at dusk when the walls turn gold. The market street of Odos 1866 is lively and good for food: dried herbs, fresh cheese, raki, and honey from the Cretan mountains.
