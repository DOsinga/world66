Kalambaka is the town at the foot of the Meteora, one of the most extraordinary landscapes in Greece. The Meteora are massive sandstone pinnacles that rise from the Thessaly plain, topped by medieval monasteries that seem to defy gravity. Six monasteries are still active today, open to visitors in the mornings and again in the late afternoon, and together they are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The town itself is pleasant but essentially exists to serve visitors to the monasteries. It has a range of guesthouses, restaurants, and cafes, and it is easy to spend a couple of days here exploring the rocks and their religious communities. Sunrise and sunset light on the Meteora are spectacular, and the rocks attract rock climbers as well as pilgrims and tourists. Kalambaka is about 340 km northwest of Athens, with regular bus and train connections.
