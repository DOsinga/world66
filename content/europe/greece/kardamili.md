Kardamyli is a small village on the Mani coast of the southern Peloponnese, where the Taygetos mountains drop sharply to the sea. It's one of the most beautiful places in Greece — a huddle of stone tower-houses above a clear turquoise bay, with hiking trails into the mountains that begin practically from the village. The English travel writer Patrick Leigh Fermor lived here for decades, and the house he built on the sea's edge lent the place a literary reputation that still draws a certain kind of traveller.

The Mani is rugged and austere even by Greek standards — this was never easy land, and the tower-houses were built for feuding families to shoot at each other from. Kardamyli has softened into a proper village with good tavernas and some accommodation, but it hasn't been ruined. The walk up the gorge of the Viros river, rising into the Taygetos, is exceptional.
