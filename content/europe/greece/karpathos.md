Karpathos is the second-largest of the Dodecanese islands, lying midway between [Rhodes](/europe/greece/rhodes) and [Crete](/europe/greece/crete) in the southeastern Aegean. It is mountainous, rugged, and far less developed than its famous neighbours — which is exactly its appeal.

The island splits into two distinct halves. The southern end around Pigadia (the capital and port) has most of the tourist infrastructure, good beaches at Afiartis and Kyra Panagia, and the dramatic Apella beach wedged beneath cliffs on the east coast. The northern end is wild and roadless, dominated by the village of Olympos — a mountaintop settlement where women still wear traditional embroidered dress and the local dialect preserves ancient Doric Greek forms. Getting to Olympos requires a boat or a rough mountain track, and the isolation is part of the experience.

Karpathos has strong winds, especially in summer, making it one of the best windsurfing spots in Greece. Afiartis bay is the main launch point. Ferries connect the island to Rhodes and Piraeus, and there is a small airport with seasonal flights.
