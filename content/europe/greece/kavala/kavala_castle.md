The Byzantine castle on the Panagia promontory dominates the old city from above. Originally built in the 5th or 6th century to defend the port of Neapolis — as Kavala was then known — it was substantially expanded during Byzantine and Ottoman times. The walls enclose the old residential quarter and provide the best panoramic view of the city, the bay, and the island of Thassos on the horizon.
