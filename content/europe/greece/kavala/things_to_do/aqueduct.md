The Ottoman aqueduct known as Kamares was built between 1520 and 1550, commissioned by Suleiman the Magnificent to supply the Panagia district with fresh water. Its multiple arches — some rising more than 20 metres — still span the road leading into the old city. It is the most visible Ottoman monument in Kavala and one of the best-preserved aqueducts in Greece.
