Using this **Kavala Travel Guide**, you will be able to learn some important facts about this Greek city. Kavala happens to be the second largest city in the north of Greece. The city is famous for its rich history, complete with fortresses and castles. It's no wonder then that Kavala is frequented by tourists every year.

**Location**

The beautiful city of Kavala, which happens to be the largest port in the entire Aegean Sea, overlooks a bay. The entire city looks like it is built on a hill, much like an amphitheater. Tourists come here to enjoy Kavala's clean beaches, most of which remain untouched and pristine today. Some of the beaches that shouldn't be skipped when visiting Kavala include Palio, Batis and Perigiali.

**Activities**

There is much to see and do in Kavala. One can spend an entire day visiting the city's many fortresses and castles, which is a great way to revisit this Greek city's rich history. There are many beach activities and walking around the city itself will prove to be an interesting activity because of the city's many old world architectural sites.

**Eating Out**

You will have absolutely no problem when it comes to eating out in Kavala. This is because this tourist city has a good number of cafes and restaurants, all of which serve excellent Greek fast food and many are noted for fine dining too. Apiko is a good option if you wish to eat freshly prepared seafood dishes while Tembelhanio is an excellent choice for sampling local delights in a cozy setting. You will also find a plethora of taverns and pubs to choose from here.

**Accommodations**

Just how the Greek city of Kavala caters very well to its tourists' need for eating out, so does it when it comes to accommodations. Choose from a wide range of seaside resorts or if comfortable luxury is what you are after, then a fancy hotel will do it for you here. The Imaret Hotel is a good hotel to stay at when visiting Kavala, as it is set in an old-world building with excellent architecture and service from the staff.

**History**

Few know about the rich historical background of the Greek city of Kavala. In the 19th century, the new city of Kavala was built on the erstwhile city of Neopolis, which was famous for its gold mines. The city also was a part of the Roman Era, Byzantine Era and the Ottoman Era.
