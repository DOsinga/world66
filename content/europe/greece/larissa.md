Larissa is the capital of Thessaly and Greece's largest inland city, yet it barely registers on most travellers' itineraries. That is precisely its appeal. This is a working Greek city with a lively university population, excellent tavernas, and none of the tourist infrastructure — or prices — of the islands.

The city sits on the Pineios river with the bulk of Mount Olympus visible to the northeast. Two ancient theatres survive on the Frourio hill in the centre: a large Hellenistic theatre and a smaller Roman odeon, both free to visit and often empty. The hill itself is a pleasant walk with views across the Thessalian plain.

The main square, Plateia Laou, is ringed by cafes and comes alive in the evenings when half the city seems to be out for a stroll. For food, head to the side streets around the old Turkish bazaar area — the mezedes here are some of the best in central Greece, and prices are a fraction of what you would pay in Athens.

Larissa works well as a base for reaching [Meteora](/europe/greece/meteora), which is about an hour west by car or train. Mount Olympus and the Pelion peninsula are both within easy striking distance.
