Mandamados is a small village in the northeastern part of [Lesbos](/europe/greece/lesbos), about 35 km from Mytilini. It sits in hilly country inland from the north coast, surrounded by olive groves and pine forest, and has two things that draw visitors from all over the island: its pottery and its monastery.

The pottery tradition here runs deep. Mandamados is one of the few places in Greece where you can still find workshops producing hand-thrown, wood-fired ceramics in the old style — heavy storage jars, pithari (large earthenware storage vessels), and everyday domestic pieces. Several workshops operate in the village, and you can often watch the work being done.

The Monastery of the Taxiarchis (Archangel Michael) stands just outside the village and is the most visited religious site on Lesbos. The monastery's icon of the Archangel Michael is unlike any other in the Orthodox world: it is made of clay mixed with the blood of monks who were killed during a pirate raid, according to tradition. The icon is three-dimensional, dark-coloured, and deeply strange. Pilgrims come year-round to touch it and leave offerings. The monastery also keeps a large black bull on the grounds as a votive offering — a practice that continues to this day and that visitors tend to find either charming or baffling.

Mandamados itself is quiet and unhurried. There is a central square with a couple of kafeneions, and the village sees few tourists despite its proximity to better-known destinations. A visit pairs well with a drive along the northern coast toward Sigri or up to the Petrified Forest.
