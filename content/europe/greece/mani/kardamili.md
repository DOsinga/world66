Kardamili is a small coastal village in Exo Mani, about 35 kilometres south of [Kalamata](/europe/greece/kalamata), backed by the grey limestone wall of the Taygetos mountains and fronted by a pebble beach the colour of slate. It is quieter and less developed than [Stoupa](/europe/greece/mani/stoupa) to the south, which is precisely what draws the kind of traveller who finds Stoupa too crowded.

The village has been continuously inhabited since antiquity — it is one of the seven cities that Agamemnon offered Achilles in the Iliad — and a ruined Venetian tower and a clutch of old stone houses at the top of the village hint at the layers underneath. But Kardamili's modern fame rests largely on one man: Patrick Leigh Fermor, the great British travel writer, who fell in love with the Mani in the 1950s and built a house in the olive groves just south of the village. He lived here, on and off, for the rest of his long life. His book *Mani: Travels in the Southern Peloponnese* is one of the finest pieces of travel writing in English, and reading it before you arrive will change how you see everything around you.

Hikers come for the Viros Gorge, which cuts up into the Taygetos from just outside the village. The trails are well-marked and range from easy riverside walks to strenuous ridge climbs with views over the entire southern Peloponnese. In spring, the gorge is full of wildflowers and the sound of water; in summer, it offers shade when the coast is baking.
