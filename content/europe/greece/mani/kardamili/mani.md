Leigh Fermor arrived in the Mani in the 1950s when the peninsula was still largely cut off from the rest of Greece — no proper roads, stone towers, families still nursing blood feuds generations old. His account of travelling through it is one of the great feats of English travel writing: part archaeology, part history, part pure intoxication with landscape and people. He writes about Kardamili, about the gorge above it, about the old families in their towers. Reading it transforms the landscape around you into something inhabited by ghosts and stories. There is no better preparation for a visit.
