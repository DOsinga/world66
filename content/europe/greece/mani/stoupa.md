Stoupa is a small resort village on the coast of Exo [Mani](/europe/greece/mani), popular with British and German visitors but still relatively unknown to North American travellers. The main draw is a pair of excellent sandy beaches — Kalogria and Stoupa beach — sheltered by headlands and ideal for swimming.

The village has more tourist infrastructure than most of the Mani: tavernas line the waterfront, and there are enough shops and services to make it a comfortable base. It is livelier than [Kardamili](/europe/greece/mani/kardamili) to the north but still far from overdeveloped. Nikos Kazantzakis, author of Zorba the Greek, lived here briefly, and the character of Zorba's lover was inspired by a local woman.
