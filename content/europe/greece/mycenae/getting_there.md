---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Mycenae is 120 km southwest of Athens and 47 km north of [Nafplio](/europe/greece/nauplia). Most visitors use Nafplio as a base and reach Mycenae as a day trip.

**From Nafplio:** Buses run several times a day from Nafplio bus station to the village of Mykines (the modern settlement below the archaeological site), a journey of about 45 minutes. Taxis from Nafplio take around 30 minutes and are reasonably priced for a group. By car, take the road north toward Argos and then follow signs west toward Mykines — the site entrance is clearly signposted.

**From Athens:** KTEL Argolida buses depart from the Athens bus terminal at 100 Kifissou Street to Nafplio (about 2.5 hours), from which you connect to Mycenae. Driving from Athens takes about 1.5 hours via the E65 motorway through Corinth; the toll road is fast and well-maintained.

**From Corinth:** Mycenae is about 50 km southwest of [Corinth](/europe/greece/corinth), making it possible to combine both sites in a single day if you have a car.

The archaeological site sits about 2 km above the village of Mykines. If you arrive by bus, it is a walkable uphill distance along a road, or a short taxi ride from the village.
