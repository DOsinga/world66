---
title: "The Palace and Citadel Walls"
type: poi
category: Sight
latitude: 37.7310
longitude: 22.7568
---

The heart of the citadel sits on the highest ground, commanding views over the plain of Argos to the sea. The palace itself is reduced to foundations and some column bases, but the plan is readable: a great hall (megaron), a courtyard, storerooms, workshops. The Cyclopean walls that ring the citadel are the most dramatic feature — in places they are 6 metres thick and still stand 8 metres high. The stones, some weighing up to 100 tonnes, were fitted together without mortar with remarkable precision. A narrow underground cistern at the northeastern corner of the walls, reached by a steep staircase, was the citadel's emergency water supply.
