  The corbelled dome was built without mortar, each ring of stones slightly overlapping the one below until they met at the top. The original bronze decorative rosettes that studded the entrance facade are now in the British Museum in London and the National Museum in Athens.

A short walk downhill from the main citadel entrance, the Treasury of Atreus (also called the Tomb of Agamemnon) is a tholos tomb — a beehive-shaped underground chamber reached through a long stone-lined passage called a dromos. The domed chamber itself is 14 metres in diameter and nearly 14 metres high. The engineering is extraordinary: the stones of the doorway weigh over 100 tonnes each. This was the tomb of a Mycenaean ruler of the highest rank, probably dating to the 13th century BC.
