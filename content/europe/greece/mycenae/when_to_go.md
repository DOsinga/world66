---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Mycenae is open year-round, but the experience varies considerably with the season. Spring (April to June) is the best time to visit: the hillsides are green, wildflowers grow in the cracks between the ancient stones, and the temperatures are comfortable for walking an open, largely shadeless site. Autumn (September to October) is similarly pleasant.

July and August are hot and crowded. The site has almost no shade, and midday temperatures can exceed 40°C. If you visit in summer, go early — the site opens at 8am — and plan to be back in the shade before noon.

Winter visits are quiet and atmospheric, though some sections may be closed for conservation work and the days are short. The site is rarely cold, but it can be wet.
