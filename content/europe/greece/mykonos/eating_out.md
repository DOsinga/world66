---
title: "Eating Out in Mykonos"
type: section
---

From traditional Mediterranean entrees to classic Italian dishes, **Mykonos resaturants **offer
you a great selection of food. Although many of their restaurants
specialize in Mediterranean dishes more than other flavors, you will
find plenty of Italian, French, and Asian food. Expect your fish to be
fresh daily and your meat dishes flavored with authentic Mediterranean
dressings and seasonings. Many of the restaurants on the island will
provide more than one taste, offering steaks, pastas, or other Greek
dishes.
Where to Find Restaurants

You can expect to find the majority of Mykonos restaurants on the
eastern and southern areas of the island. It is these areas that fish
is caught daily and the fresh breeze flows off the ocean, giving you
the best restaurant experience possible. You will enjoy the scenery
almost as much as the food itself and can find a restaurant on nearly
every corner near the water.
When to Eat

Many of the restaurants on Mykonos are only open in the warmer
seasons of the year and close for the winter due to low volume of
customers. The best time to visit in order to get the best food
selection is April-September. You will find plenty of restaurants open
and happy to feed you any other time of the year, but you can expect
many of them to be closed during that time. Mykonos restaurants are
open early and stay open late, to about 1 or 2am.
Restaurant “Must-Try”

The candle and lantern-lit garden area of Chez Marinas has been a
favorite hot spot for years. Here you can listen to the live music or
dance the night away while enjoying the garden full of bougainvilleas
and cacti. Try the seafood or lamb entrees and enjoy the fresh
vegetables for a set, affordable price. For a reasonably priced and
delicious experience, head on out to Mamakas Mykonos (right by the taxi
square) and try the large array of appetizers and dishes. Dine inside
the century-old house or in the courtyard while surrounded by
terra-cotta planters.
