Little Venice
Where the most western part of the town meets the sea is the area known as "Little Venice". Here the buildings have been constructed right on the sea's edge with their balconies overhanging the water. During the 16th and 17th century pirating was common and it is believed this area was used for the necessary quick loading and unloading of goods. Mykonos hides a lot of surprises. One of these is the settlement of Aleukantras or the Little Venice, which is one of the most picturesque places in Mykonos. Little Venice can be found at the southwest end of the port and owes its name to the fact that many wealthy captains built their houses directly on the sea here. Little Venice it is considered one of them most idyllic places and visitors can enjoy a wonderful sunset at the bars overlooking the sea. Many international tourists prefer to pay huge amounts just to stay in those charming fisherman's hutments. It is a completely different experience. A whole new world waiting to be explored.
