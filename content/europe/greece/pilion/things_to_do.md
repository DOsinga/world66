Walking the old kalderimi mule paths between villages is the best way to experience Pelion — many are well-marked and pass through orchards and dense forest. The peninsula is also known for its beaches, its traditional stone architecture, and a historic narrow-gauge railway that still runs on weekends.

### Pelion Railway

The Pelion Railway is a historic narrow-gauge line whose construction was completed in 1904. For decades it linked the village of Milies in the heart of the peninsula to the port city of Volos. Today it operates as a heritage railway on weekends and holidays, running from Ano Lehonia on the coast up through the hills to Milies — a scenic ride through forest and over stone viaducts that's well worth the trip.
