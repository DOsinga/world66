## Currency

Greece uses the Euro (EUR). ATMs are widely available in cities and on the larger islands, though smaller or more remote islands may have only one or none. Carry some cash when heading off the beaten track. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels, restaurants, and shops, but smaller tavernas and village shops often prefer cash.

## Visas

Citizens of EU countries, the US, Canada, Australia, and most other Western countries do not need a visa for stays of up to 90 days within a 180-day period. Greece is part of the Schengen Area.

## Language

Greek is the official language. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and by younger people in cities and on the popular islands. In smaller villages and with older generations, basic Greek phrases go a long way and are warmly appreciated.

## Health

Greece has adequate medical facilities in major cities and on larger islands. Remote islands may have only a basic health centre. EU citizens should carry their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) for access to public healthcare. Travel insurance with medical coverage is strongly recommended for all visitors.

Tap water is safe to drink in most of the mainland and larger islands. On some smaller islands, tap water may be desalinated and locals prefer bottled water — ask locally.

The summer sun in Greece is intense. Sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water are essential, especially when visiting open archaeological sites midday.

## Opening Hours

Most shops open around 9:00 and close for a midday break from roughly 14:00–17:00, then reopen until 21:00. In tourist areas this pattern breaks down and many shops stay open continuously. Archaeological sites and museums typically open 08:00–15:00, with longer hours in summer. Many sites close on Mondays.

## Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Plug type is Type C/F (two-round-pin, the standard European plug). Same as most of the EU.

## Tipping

Tipping is not mandatory but is appreciated. Rounding up the bill or leaving 10% at a restaurant is the norm. For taxis, rounding up is customary.

## Communications

Mobile coverage is good across the mainland and all major islands. Greek SIM cards are cheap and easy to buy. EU roaming rules mean travellers from EU countries can use their home plan without extra cost. Free WiFi is available at most cafes, hotels, and restaurants.

## Emergency Numbers

- General emergency: 112
- Police: 100
- Ambulance: 166
- Fire: 199
- Coast guard: 108
