---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Santorini's main draws are its volcanic landscape, ancient history, and the dramatic caldera views. The cliff walk from Oia south to Fira is one of the best ways to take in the island — a 10 km trail along the caldera rim with sweeping views the whole way. The ancient site of Akrotiri is a genuine highlight: a Bronze Age city frozen in time by the Minoan eruption, with multi-storey buildings, frescoes, and drainage systems intact.

**Oia**

Oia sits at the northern tip of the island, perched on the caldera rim. The village is known for its whitewashed cubic houses, blue-domed churches, and the most-photographed sunset in Greece. Arrive at least an hour before sundown to secure a spot — the main viewpoint fills up quickly. The narrow lanes are worth exploring during the day too, before the sunset crowds arrive.

**Akrotiri Archaeological Site**

The Bronze Age settlement of Akrotiri was buried by volcanic ash around 1600 BC, preserving it in exceptional detail. Multi-storey buildings, frescoes, pottery, and drainage infrastructure are all visible under the protective roof. It's often compared to Pompeii but predates it by over a thousand years. Allow two hours and hire an audio guide.

**Caldera Boat Trip**

From the port at Athinios or the old port below Fira, boat trips run to the volcanic islands in the caldera. Nea Kameni is an active lava islet where you can walk to the summit crater and swim in warm sulphurous waters nearby. Half-day tours are easy to book in Fira.

**Cliff Walk: Oia to Fira**

The path along the caldera rim connects Oia to Fira via a series of villages — Finikia, Imerovigli, and Firostefani. The full walk takes around four to five hours at a leisurely pace. The views are extraordinary throughout, particularly above Imerovigli. Go early in the morning to avoid the heat and the crowds.

**Caldera Massage Studio**

Located in the center of Oia in a restored 1864 sea captain's house on the caldera edge, this small studio offers massages and aromatherapy treatments with views over the volcano. The rooftop terrace is the standout spot. Book ahead — capacity is deliberately limited.
