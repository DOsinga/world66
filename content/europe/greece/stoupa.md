Stoupa is a small beach village on the Outer Mani coast of the southern Peloponnese, about 45km south of Kalamata. It sits in a sheltered bay with two main beaches — Stoupa Beach and Kalogria Beach — and has been a popular destination with British and German summer visitors since the 1980s. It remains small and relatively undeveloped by Greek resort standards.

The village is compact, with the main road running along the bay, a handful of tavernas and cafes facing the sea, a supermarket, and a scattering of small hotels and apartments. The writer Nikos Kazantzakis lived here briefly in the 1910s and a bust commemorates him above the beach.

The wider Mani Peninsula is the main draw beyond the beach — a stark, beautiful region of tower-houses, Byzantine churches, and remote coastline. Kardamyli, 15km north, is a more architecturally interesting village that Patrick Leigh Fermor made his home and wrote about extensively. The Diros Caves, 35km south, are among Greece's most impressive geological sites, accessible by boat through underground river passages.
