---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

**Stoupa Beach** is the main beach — a wide sweep of fine sand and pebble along a sheltered bay. The water is calm and clear. A natural spring rises from the sandy bottom at the southern end, creating a slight chill and an excellent snorkelling site with eels, octopus, and fish visible in the underwater rocks. Kayaks and paddle boats are available for hire.

**Kalogria Beach**, a 10-minute walk south over the headland, is smaller and somewhat quieter. A fresher underwater spring keeps the temperature a notch cooler. Tamarisk trees provide shade along the back of the beach. A small supermarket and tavernas are close by.

**Agios Dimitrios Beach** (also called Pantazi), further south along the coast, is a remote horseshoe-shaped bay with very clear water and a small seasonal taverna. Far fewer visitors make it this far.
