This **Stoupa Practical Information **guide can give you useful facts and other essential information about the village of Stoupa. This little town, popularly called as the &ldquo;Gem of Greece,&rdquo; is found in Mani on the coast of southern Peloponnese. Known for its relaxed atmosphere, Stoupa can offer you a genuine Greek experience.

**Language**

Many locals speak English, though you may want to greet and thank them in Greek. This portrays good manners and leaves a positive impression on your nationality.

**Clothing**

There are no specific dress codes in Stoupa, as it is situated in a modern country. However, you need to dress in a conservative manner when visiting monasteries and churches.

**Time**

Take note that Greece is 7 hours ahead of Eastern Standard Time, 1 hour ahead of Central European time, and 2 hours ahead of Greenwich Mean Time. Bear in mind that clocks are put forward 1 hour during summer, from March to September. You can call 14814 for the exact time.

**Currency**

Stoupa uses the Euro as its currency. If you opt to use your credit card, make sure to change your 6-digit PIN to 4 digits, since PINs in Europe are 4 digits only. You can also find several ATMs in the area.

**Electricity and Telecommunication **

Electric Current 220V AC or 50 Hz is the standard electric current in Stoupa. British appliances necessitate adaptors, while those coming from North America need transformers. You can go to the Hellenic Telecommunications Organization office to avail of a cheap way to make a local or international call. Public phone card boots and pre-paid phone cards are also available for your convenience.
