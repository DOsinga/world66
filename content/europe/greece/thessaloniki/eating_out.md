---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Thessaloniki has a strong claim to being the best food city in Greece. The Sephardic, Anatolian and Pontic Greek refugees who arrived in the 20th century each brought their own cooking, and it has been folded into a local cuisine that runs from cheap street snacks to long, alcohol-fuelled *mezedopoleio* meals. Start your day with **bougatsa** — flaky phyllo filled with semolina cream, cheese or mince — from a *bougatsatzidiko* near the centre. Lunch on a *koulouri* sesame ring from a street cart. For dinner head to **Ladadika** or **Ano Poli** for small plates of grilled octopus, *mydia saganaki* (mussels in tomato and feta) and *mydopilafo*, washed down with carafes of tsipouro.
