Constructed in the 15th century this tower served as a defensive bulwark, an infamous prison, a place of execution, and now is home to a wonderful collection of sculptures, frescoes, and other interesting artefacts from 300 to 1400 AD portraying the amazing history and culture of the city. The White Tower, once known as the Bloody Tower, has become the city’s most famous landmark. ** Labros Hatzilabrou:** I was born and raised 300 yards from the White Tower, and spent there the first 27 years of my life.  It is best to visit in late afternoon, on a warm summer evening, when the whole place is full of life, boats on the shore, kids playing, and people enjoying the Mediterranean outdoors. You will never forget the sunset...
