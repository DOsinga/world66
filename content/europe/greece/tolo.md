Tolo sits at the foot of the Argolid hills where a long crescent of sand meets the calm waters of the Argolic Gulf — one of the finest beaches in the eastern Peloponnese. The bay curves gently, the sea stays shallow for a good distance out, and in summer the water reaches the kind of temperature that makes it hard to leave. Despite being popular with Greek families and European visitors, Tolo has resisted the worst excesses of resort development. The main strip of tavernas, cafés and small hotels runs directly behind the beach, and the pace is decidedly unhurried.

The village has a small fishing harbor at its northern end where caiques still go out each morning, and the catch tends to end up on your plate the same evening. Accommodation ranges from simple rooms above family-run apartments to modest seafront hotels, nearly all within easy walking distance of the beach. The scale of the place works in its favor — you can walk from one end to the other in ten minutes, and the surrounding hills mean the evenings cool down pleasantly even in August.

What sets Tolo apart from other beach resorts is how much lies within easy reach. [Nafplio](/europe/greece/nafplio), one of the most beautiful towns in Greece, is just ten kilometers away — close enough for an afternoon trip to its Venetian old town, waterfront fortress, and excellent tavernas. The ancient site of Asini occupies a rocky headland just east of the village, its Bronze Age remains visible against the sea. Epidaurus, with its breathtaking ancient theater where performances are still staged in summer, is less than an hour's drive.

Tolo makes a sensible base for anyone who wants a proper beach holiday without sacrificing access to the rich archaeology and towns of the Argolid. You can spend mornings at the sea, afternoons at Mycenae or Tiryns, and evenings eating grilled fish by the harbor.
