*Europe > [Greece](/europe/greece) > Trikala*

Trikala is a lively, mid-sized city set in the broad plain of Thessaly in central [Greece](/europe/greece), about 20 kilometres south of the dramatic rock formations of Meteora. Most travelers pass through on their way to [Kalambaka](/europe/greece/kalambaka) and the famous monasteries perched overhead, but Trikala rewards a slower look. The city has a relaxed, lived-in feel — more students and market stalls than tourist shops — and a handful of genuinely interesting ancient remains.

The city sits on the site of ancient Trikka, one of the places credited in antiquity as the birthplace of Asclepius, the god of medicine. The ruins of an Asklepieion — a healing sanctuary dedicated to him — lie near the central square, tucked between the church of Agios Nikolaos and the modern town. These are the oldest such ruins in Greece, and wandering through them takes only a few minutes, but puts you in touch with something very old. Nearby, mosaic floors and traces of a Roman stoa and baths surface among the layers.

The Byzantine fortress on the hill — built on Hellenistic foundations occupying the old acropolis — overlooks the town and the river Lithaios, which splits Trikala neatly in two. The riverbanks are pleasant to walk along in the evening. The churches of Agios Demetrios and Agii Anargyri add more texture to the Byzantine end of the city's history.

If you are heading to Meteora, Trikala makes a reasonable base — Kalambaka is barely half an hour away by bus or car. The city is also well connected to [Athens](/europe/greece/athens), [Thessaloniki](/europe/greece/thessaloniki), [Larissa](/europe/greece/larissa), and [Ioannina](/europe/greece/ioannina).
