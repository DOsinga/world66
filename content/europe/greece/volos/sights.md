---
title: "Sights in Volos"
type: section
---

Volos at the head of the Pagasitic Gulf has the reputation for being one of the most beautiful cities in Greece. Flattened by an earthquake in the fifties and rebuilt, it has an open airy feel with the occasional old characterful building hiding amongst the more modern stuff.
The pedestrianized sea front is a must – lots of cafes and the tsipouradika (ouzo restaurants) for which the city is famous. The archeological museum is possibly one of the best medium sized museums in Europe.
Leaving the city, head for Makrynitsa – the balcony of the Pelion. A bit of a tourist honey pot but its fortified mansions are well worth a visit – one is open to the public as a small museum, just down from the main square.
Heading further down the peninsula – stop off in Ano Gatzea for a really good meal at the traditional tavern, Angeliko. A good range of vegetarian and non vegetarian food and wonderful views across the Pagasitic Gulf from the balcony.
The Pelion peninsula is an ideal destination if you want to get away from the normal tourist traps – low key tourism is the watch word. So things like guided tours and boat trips are a little hard to find. One company filling that gap is Eclipse Sailing (www.eclipsesailiing.gr) who do day trips on the Pagasitic Gulf and cruises to the Sporades ([Skiathos](/europe/greece/skiathos), [Skopelos](/europe/greece/skopelos) and Alonissos) and Trikeri in a classic wooden sailing yacht. They operate out of (Volos}, Milina and Kala Nera.
Heading to the Aegean side of the peninsula, you should not miss Milopotamos, with its pretty beach and sea arches. One word of warning – the beaches on the Aegean side really fill up at weekends in high summer, so best time your visits for mid week.
