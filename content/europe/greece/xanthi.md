Xanthi sits in the northeast corner of Greece, in Thrace, about 220 km east of [Thessaloniki](/europe/greece/thessaloniki) and close to the [Bulgarian](/europe/bulgaria) border. It is a lively university town with an atmosphere quite unlike the rest of Greece — partly because of its significant Muslim minority, partly because of its well-preserved Ottoman-era old town climbing the hillside above the modern centre.

The old town is the reason to come. Narrow cobbled lanes wind between colourful mansions with overhanging upper floors, many dating from the tobacco boom of the 19th century. A stroll up through these streets on a quiet afternoon gives a strong sense of the layered history of Thrace — Greek, Ottoman, Bulgarian — all visible in the architecture.

Xanthi is at its best during the Carnival in February or March, one of the liveliest in northern Greece, and during the Old Town Festival in early September. The Saturday bazaar is a weekly institution and worth timing a visit around.
