Kalamaki sits on the southern coast of [Zakynthos](/europe/greece/zakinthos), a quieter alternative to the party strip of neighbouring Laganas. The beach here is part of the nesting ground for loggerhead sea turtles, which means sunbeds are cleared at dusk and lights are kept low at night — a rule that keeps the resort pleasantly low-key.

The long sandy beach is the main draw, backed by olive groves and low hills rather than high-rise development. The airport is close enough that you will hear planes on approach, but flights are infrequent enough that it is more a novelty than a nuisance. For families and anyone wanting a relaxed base on Zakynthos without the noise of Laganas, Kalamaki is a solid choice.
