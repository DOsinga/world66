Bükfürdő is one of Hungary's most popular thermal spa resorts, a purpose-built village around a cluster of mineral-rich springs on the flat country of western Hungary, about 35 km north of Szombathely and just 15 km from the Austrian border. The waters were discovered by accident in 1957 during oil drilling, and within a decade Bükfürdő had been built from scratch as a spa town — today its enormous **Bükfürdő Thermal & Spa** complex is one of the largest bath facilities in the country, with dozens of pools ranging from warm outdoor sunning baths to very hot mineral soaks to rapids, slides and whirlpools for children.

It is a genuine destination resort rather than a sightseeing town — people come here to soak, eat, and sleep, and most of the village is given over to hotels, pensions and restaurants arranged around the baths. Combine a visit with a day trip to the baroque palace of **Nagycenk** (the Széchenyi family seat) or the historic lakeside town of **Sopron** an hour to the north.
