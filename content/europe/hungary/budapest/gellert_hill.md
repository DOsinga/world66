Gellért Hill rises 235 meters above the Danube on the Buda side, delivering the single best panorama in Budapest. The Citadella fortress at the summit, built by the Habsburgs after the 1848 revolution, offers a 360-degree view sweeping from the Parliament to the Castle District to the bridges linking Buda and Pest. The Liberty Statue, a green bronze figure holding a palm leaf aloft, has stood here since 1947 and is visible from across the city. The climb up is steep but short — twenty minutes from the river through wooded paths and rocky outcrops. At the base, the Gellért Baths are among Budapest's most beautiful thermal complexes, with art nouveau interiors, an outdoor wave pool, and ornate mosaic columns. Come up for sunset when the city lights begin to flicker on, then reward yourself with a long soak in the baths below. It's the essential Budapest double feature.
