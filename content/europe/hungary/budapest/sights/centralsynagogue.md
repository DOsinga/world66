The Great Synagogue of Budapest is the largest temple outside of New Yorks Emanuel synagogue. The detailed façade is decorated with yellow, red and blue bricks - the heraldic colours of Budapest- and topped by two minaret-like towers. The synagogue was completed in 1859 after a design by Lajos Förster and houses not only a temple, but in a adjacent building also a museum on Hungarian Jewish history and art.
