Budapest has a long and stirring history that is reflected in its buildings. The Roman settlements, the turkish invasion, the following reign of the Habsburgers, the communist era etc. all left their mark on Budapest. Since the city got most expanded and developed during the 18th and 19th century, you will find most examples of buildings is the styles of that era. But if you know where to look, you'll be able to find remnants of most periods in Budapest history. There is the archeological site of Aquincum where the Romans built their settlement and some sites dating back to the Turkish rule, like the tomb of Gül Baba. One of the highlights of the city is the Synagogue at Dóhany u. This beautiful yellow brick temple also contains a museum of Hungarian Jewish history and art. Lovers of bizarre architecture must pay a visit to the Vajdahunyad Castle, a peculiar mix of building styles ranging from the Medieval to Rococo.

**Aquincum **

Aquincum dates back to around 35BC, when the Romans colonized the area around Budapest. Back than it was a trading settlement as well as a garrison town protecting other stratigic places along the Danube. You can now visit the museum, see the amphitheatre and admire the water organ.

address: Szentendrei út 139

openinghours: 9am-5pm Apr 15-30 and Oct 1-31, 9am-6pm May 1 Sep 30, closed Nov 1-Apr 14
