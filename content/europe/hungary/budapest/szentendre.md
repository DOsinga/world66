A pretty riverside town popular with artists and day-trippers, about 20 kilometers north of Budapest. Founded by Serbian refugees in the 14th century, it still has several small Orthodox churches and a Mediterranean feel to its cobbled streets and pastel-colored houses. The Margit Kovacs museum displays fine Hungarian ceramics, and the open-air ethnographical museum (Skanzen) just outside town recreates rural Hungarian life from the 17th and 18th centuries. Easy to reach by the HEV suburban railway from Batthyany ter.
