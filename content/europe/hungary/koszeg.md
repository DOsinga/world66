Koszeg is one of Hungary's most charming small towns, tucked right against the Austrian border at the foot of the Koszeg Mountains. It is best known for a single extraordinary event: in August 1532, the tiny garrison of Jurisics Castle held off the Ottoman army of Suleiman the Magnificent for 25 days, delaying the sultan's march on Vienna long enough that he abandoned the campaign. Every year on August 30th, the town rings its bells at 11am to commemorate the siege — a tradition that has continued for nearly five centuries.

The old town is beautifully preserved and small enough to walk in an hour. Jurisics tér, the main square, is lined with painted baroque and renaissance houses and anchored by the Church of the Sacred Heart and the Heroes' Gate. The castle itself is modest but atmospheric, with a small museum inside. Just off the square, the General's House displays a fine collection of local history. The whole scene feels unhurried and largely untouched by tourism.

Beyond the town, the Koszeg Mountains offer easy hiking through beech and oak forests. The Irott-ko, at 882 meters the highest point in Transdanubia, sits on the Austrian border and makes a pleasant half-day walk. Koszeg is an easy day trip from [Sopron](/europe/hungary/sopron) or [Szombathely](/europe/hungary/szombathely), but it deserves an overnight stay if you have the time.
