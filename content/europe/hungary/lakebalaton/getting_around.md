---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

A local train line runs along the south shore, connecting Siófok and the resort towns all the way to Keszthely. The north shore has its own line, though service is less frequent and some villages are only reachable by bus or on foot. A ferry crosses the lake between Tihany and Szántódpuszta on the south shore — a scenic shortcut that cuts out a long drive around the western end.

In summer, bicycle paths run along much of the lakeshore. Cycling is one of the best ways to explore the north shore, where the distances between villages are short and the roads through the vineyards are lightly trafficked. Bike rental is available in most of the main resort towns.
