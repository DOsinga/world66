---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Lake Balaton is overwhelmingly a summer destination. July and August are peak season — beaches are packed, prices are high, and the south shore in particular is relentlessly lively. If you prefer fewer crowds, June and early September are better: the water is still warm enough for swimming, the sun is reliable, and the resort towns haven't yet surrendered completely to mass tourism.

Outside summer the lake is largely shut down. Many restaurants, bars, and smaller hotels close from October to April, and the resort towns can feel eerily empty. The north shore is more livable year-round — wine country visits, thermal baths at Hévíz, and the Festetics Palace in Keszthely all work well in spring or autumn.

The Balaton Sound music festival, held near Zamárdi on the south shore in early July, draws large crowds and raises prices significantly in the surrounding area. Book accommodation well in advance if visiting during festival week.
