Miskolc is Hungary's third-largest city, tucked into the foothills of the Bükk Mountains in the northeast of the country. It is an industrial city that does not pretend to be a tourist magnet, but the surrounding area has genuine appeal — most notably Miskolctapolca, a suburb where you can swim through a natural cave system fed by thermal springs.

The Bükk National Park starts practically at the city's edge, with hiking trails through beech and oak forests, limestone karst formations, and lookout towers with views across the Northern Hungarian hills. The small town of Lillafüred, a short drive into the mountains, has a dramatic waterfall and a grand lakeside hotel from the 1920s.

The city centre itself has an attractive pedestrian main street, a handful of churches and museums, and enough restaurants and cafes to keep you occupied for an evening. Miskolc is also a convenient base for visiting the wine region of [Eger](/europe/hungary/eger) and the Tokaj vineyards to the east.
