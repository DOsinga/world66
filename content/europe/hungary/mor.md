Mór is a small Hungarian wine town in the Fejér county foothills, about 70 km southwest of Budapest. It has been producing white wines — particularly from the Ezerjó grape — for centuries, and the local cellars (pincék) are the main reason to visit. The pace is slow and genuinely local; you'll encounter few tourists.

Wandering the streets leads you to the pincék scattered across the hillsides. Each cellar has its own character, and most welcome visitors for a tasting. A favourite experience is sipping wine inside a barrel room — some cellars have repurposed giant casks as seating. Climb to the top of the vineyarded hill for a view over the whole town.

The local cemetery is worth a visit: the care given to individual plots, with raised flower gardens at each one, says a lot about the town's character.
