## Currency

Hungary uses the Hungarian Forint (HUF). Hungary is in the EU but has not adopted the euro. Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in cities and tourist areas, but carry cash for markets, rural restaurants, and smaller towns. ATMs are plentiful in Budapest and most towns; they generally offer better rates than exchange offices. Avoid exchange booths at the airport or in tourist areas — rates are poor. Some places near tourist attractions may accept euros but will give unfavourable exchange.

## Language

Hungarian (Magyar) is the official language. It's famously difficult — unrelated to most European languages — and even a few words of greeting (köszönöm = thank you, jó napot = good day) will go down well. In Budapest and major tourist towns, English is widely spoken by younger people and in hospitality. Outside the capital, German is often more useful than English in western Hungary near the Austrian border.

## Visas

EU/EEA citizens need only a valid ID to enter. US, UK, Canadian, Australian, and New Zealand citizens can visit visa-free for up to 90 days within any 180-day period. Hungary is part of the Schengen Area. Check current ETIAS requirements before travelling, as new authorisation requirements may apply for some nationalities from late 2026.

## Safety

Hungary is generally safe. Petty theft — pickpocketing on public transport, at busy tourist sites, and in crowded markets — is the main concern. Keep an eye on bags and wallets in Budapest, especially on the M2 metro and around the Great Market Hall. Taxis can overcharge; use metered cabs or apps like Bolt.

## Health

No vaccinations are required. Tap water is safe to drink throughout the country. Hungary has a functioning public health system, though private clinics with English-speaking staff are easier to navigate for visitors.

## Electricity

230V, 50Hz. Standard European two-pin plugs (Type F). UK and US visitors need adaptors.

## Tipping

Tipping is customary in restaurants — 10–15% is normal if service is not already included. Check the bill, as some tourist-area restaurants add a service charge automatically. It's polite to tip taxi drivers and hotel porters.

## Internet

Wi-Fi is widely available in hotels, cafes, and restaurants. Hungary has good 4G/5G coverage in cities. EU roaming rules apply for European SIM cards.
