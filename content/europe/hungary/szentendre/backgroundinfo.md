---
title: "Background"
type: section
---

Szentendre was originally settled by Serbian Orthodox refugees fleeing the Ottoman Empire in the 17th and 18th centuries. The town retains a distinctly South Slav character — Orthodox churches, Cyrillic inscriptions on tombstones, and Mediterranean-style architecture distinguish it from typical Hungarian towns along the Danube. The Serbian population largely abandoned the town in the 19th century following floods and crop disease; these disasters also prevented industrial development, which had the unintended effect of preserving the Baroque townscape intact. Artists discovered this picturesque village in the 1920s and established a colony here. Tourists followed in the 1960s and 1970s; today visitors greatly outnumber Szentendre's permanent population of about 20,000.
