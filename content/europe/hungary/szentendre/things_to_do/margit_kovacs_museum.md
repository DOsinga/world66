A museum dedicated to Margit Kovács (1902–1977), the most celebrated Hungarian ceramic artist of the 20th century. Her work — relief sculptures and figurines addressing themes of motherhood, love, death, and folk tradition — fills the collection. Her style, drawing on medieval folk art and expressionism, was widely loved by the public and is now considered a significant strand of 20th-century Hungarian art. Open daily except Monday; admission charged.
