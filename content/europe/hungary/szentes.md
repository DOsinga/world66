Szentes is a quiet market town on the Great Hungarian Plain, about 30 km north of [Szeged](/europe/hungary/szeged). It sits on the banks of the Kurca, a small tributary of the Tisza, and has the unhurried feel of a town where agriculture is still the main business. The surrounding countryside is flat as a table — fields of sunflowers, wheat, and paprika stretching to the horizon.

The town centre has a pleasant main square with some fine 19th-century buildings, including a handsome town hall. The thermal bath, fed by mineral springs, is a popular local attraction and a good reason to break a journey across the plain. Szentes is not a destination in itself, but if you are travelling between Szeged and the Hortobágy or exploring the quieter parts of the Alföld, it makes a comfortable stop.
