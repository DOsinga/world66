Tokaj is a small town in northeastern Hungary at the confluence of the Tisza and Bodrog rivers, and the heart of the Tokaj wine region — a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2002. It is worldfamous for the golden Tokaji Aszú dessert wines, produced from botrytis-affected grapes grown on the volcanic hillsides above the town. The wines have been produced here since the 17th century and were a favourite of Russian tsars and French kings.

The town is small and easily explored on foot. The **Tokaj Museum** in the former Greek merchants' house (c. 1790) on the main square has exhibits on the wine-growing traditions of the region and an atmospheric cellar system underneath. **Castle of Tokaj**, on the hill above the town, played an important defensive role when Hungary was divided between the Habsburg, Ottoman, and Transylvanian powers in the 16th century.

Wine cellars cut into the hillside can be visited and their Tokaji Aszú and Furmint tasted. Most cellar visits can be arranged independently or through local tourism offices.
