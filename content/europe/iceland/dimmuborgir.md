**Dimmuborgir** (the Dark Castles) is a dramatic field of lava formations on the eastern shore of Lake Myvatn in northeast [Iceland](/europe/iceland). The pillars, arches, and cave-like structures were created about 2,300 years ago when a lava flow passed over a lake, causing steam explosions that left behind these strange, castle-like shapes.

Several well-marked walking paths loop through the formations — the easiest takes about 20 minutes, the longer routes up to an hour. In Icelandic folklore, Dimmuborgir is said to be a gateway to the underworld and the home of the Yule Lads, Iceland's mischievous Christmas figures. The site is free to visit and just a short drive from the main road around Myvatn.
