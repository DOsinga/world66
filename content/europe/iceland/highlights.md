---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Iceland rewards visitors who go beyond Reykjavik's immediate surroundings. The country is small enough that you can cross it in a day's drive, but varied enough to fill weeks. Here are the things most worth your time.

**[Reykjavik](/europe/iceland/reykjavik)** — the capital deserves at least two days. Hallgrímskirkja, the old harbor, the National Museum, the Harpa concert hall, and the Saturday flea market at Kolaportið give you a decent sense of the city. The nightlife on Friday and Saturday evenings is genuinely good.

**The Golden Circle** — Þingvellir, Geysir, and Gullfoss are classics for a reason. The rift valley at Þingvellir is geologically astonishing, and Strokkur erupts every five to ten minutes. Worth a full day from Reykjavik.

**The South Coast** — the drive east from Reykjavik along Route 1 passes Seljalandsfoss and Skógafoss waterfalls, the black sand beach at Reynisfjara, [Vík](/europe/iceland/vik), and the glacier lagoon at Jökulsárlón. One of the most photogenic stretches of road in Europe.

**[Lake Mývatn](/europe/iceland/lakemyvatn) and the north** — volcanic craters, boiling mud pots, and the strange lava formations at [Dimmuborgir](/europe/iceland/dimmuborgir). [Húsavík](/europe/iceland/husavik) is the best place in Iceland for whale watching. [Akureyri](/europe/iceland/akureyri), the northern capital, has a good botanical garden and easy access to the highlands.

**Landmannalaugar** — accessible only in summer by 4WD or highland bus, but the rhyolite mountains and natural hot springs make the effort worthwhile. The start of the Laugavegur trail, Iceland's most famous multi-day hike.

**The Westfjords** — isolated, dramatic, and rarely crowded. The bird cliffs at Látrabjarg and the remote fjords around [Ísafjörður](/europe/iceland/isafjordur) are for travelers who want to go somewhere genuinely out of the way. The roads are slow and winding; allow plenty of time.

**[Vestmannaeyjar](/europe/iceland/westmanislands)** — the Westman Islands, off the south coast, are home to the largest puffin colony in the world and the site of the 1973 Eldfell eruption, which buried a third of the main town under lava. The history is extraordinary and the ferry crossing is scenic.

**Northern lights** — Iceland is one of the best places in the world to see them, and solar activity around 2025–2026 is near its peak. You need dark skies and clear weather: late August to April, away from city lights, on a clear night. Patience is required — there are no guarantees.
