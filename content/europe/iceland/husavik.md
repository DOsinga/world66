Husavik is a small fishing town of about 2,500 people on the shores of Skjalfandi Bay in northern [Iceland](/europe/iceland). It has earned the title of whale watching capital of Iceland — humpback whales feed in the bay from April through October, and sighting rates on boat tours regularly exceed 95 percent.

Beyond the whales, Husavik has a genuine small-town charm that the more touristed stops on the Ring Road often lack. The harbour is lined with colourful wooden houses, and the Husavik Whale Museum is one of the best small museums in the country. The town also makes a good base for visiting the volcanic landscapes around [Myvatn](/europe/iceland/myvatn) and the powerful Dettifoss waterfall, both less than an hour's drive away.
