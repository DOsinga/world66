The Kjölur Route (Kjölvegur, Road 35) is one of Iceland's great highland traversals — a 200-kilometre gravel track that cuts straight through the interior from Gullfoss in the south to Blönduós on the north coast. Unlike the more famous Sprengisandur route, Kjölur runs between two glacier systems — Langjökull to the west and Hofsjökull to the east — so the landscape is never simply barren. Hot springs steam from the ground, rivers need fording, and the plateau opens out into broad moorland where the wind has full authority.

The route has been used since the Saga Age. Medieval Icelanders drove sheep across it every autumn, and the ruins of the old mountain hut at Ásgarður are still visible. Today most people travel it by 4WD or on one of the scheduled bus services that run in summer, but Kjölur remains one of Iceland's classic multi-day horse treks — several operators offer week-long rides in the tradition of the old drovers.

The highlight along the way is Hveravellir, a geothermal oasis roughly halfway across. It has natural hot pools (one of them a perfect bathing temperature), fumaroles, and a small mountain hut where you can spend the night. Nothing else on the route comes close to it for warmth or character.

Kjölur is open from roughly late June to September, depending on snow conditions. A standard 4WD can manage it in good weather, but the river crossings can be treacherous after rain and the route should not be attempted in poor visibility. Check conditions with the Icelandic Road and Coastal Administration (Vegagerðin) before setting out.
