---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Reykjavik is the natural base for the southwest of Iceland, and a surprising amount of the country's headline scenery is within day-trip range.

The classic loop is the **Golden Circle** — Þingvellir National Park (where the Eurasian and North American tectonic plates meet, and where the world's first parliament sat), the Geysir geothermal field with the still-erupting Strokkur, and the thundering Gullfoss waterfall. Buses and self-drive both work; the full loop is about 230 km and takes a long day.

Closer in, the **Blue Lagoon** is forty minutes toward the airport — a milky-blue geothermal spa famous enough that you should book ahead. The newer **Sky Lagoon**, on the edge of Reykjavik itself, is cheaper and quieter. The **Reykjanes peninsula**, the volcanic landscape between the city and the airport, is one of the rare places where you can see the Mid-Atlantic Ridge above ground; recent eruptions near Fagradalsfjall have only added to the appeal.

For something further: **Vík** and the south coast (black sand beaches, glacier tongues, waterfalls) is do-able in a long day; the **Snæfellsnes peninsula** to the north is a more relaxed twelve-hour loop.
