Perhaps what [Iceland](/europe/iceland) is best known for are their glaciers. One thing
that is of the utmost importance, though, is that these glaciers can
present dangerous complications, and it is highly suggested you only go
with a tour group. That being said, you will be taken to the glacier in
either a bus, off-roading vehicle, or by snowmobile in winter months.
Many tours of these glaciers can get pricey, as the vehicles are
expensive and they take you to numerous, beautifully scenic areas. If
you don't have a lot of money to spend but still want the adventure of
Icelandic glaciers, try the Blue Ice AR tour that takes you less than 2
hours outisde of the capital [Reykjavik](/europe/iceland/reykjavik) to the Sólheimajökull glacial
tongue. For only $184, you will have the chance to walk along the
glacier to a large ice wall - this is where the fun begins. Included in
your fee are tools you will need to climb - yes, climb - this ice wall.
Don't worry, though. You will be provided with a safety line so you do
not run the risk of falling. This is for both beginners who have never
tried iceclimbing, and for those that are familiar to it.

The
Blue Lagoon in Iceland is an extremely popular destination. The Blue
Lagoon is a geothermal spa that is world-renound for it's healing and
relaxing properties. The average temperature of the seawater is 98-102
degrees. The healthy algae in the Blue Lagoon is known for it's
anti-aging properties, the silica is knows for strengthening and
tightening skin, as well as deep-cleansing, and the minerals are an
excellent refreshment for your entire body. The Blue Lagoon is located
about 40 minutes from Reykjavik, and you can drive there by car or take
one of the daily buses there for $22. The entrance fee for adults is
$33. You can rent towels, bathing suits, and robes for an extra fee.

Iceland
is also home to Europe's most powerful waterfall, Gullfoss (gold
waterfall). The Gullfoss-Geyser Direct RE is a tour that will not only
take you to the waterfall, but it will take you to Geyser (yes, the
name originated in Iceland), the largest in the world. You won't see
this geyser erupt, but another small one in this geyser "field",
Strokkur, erupts every 5-10 minutes. For $115 May through September,
$89 the rest of the year, the tour bus will pick you up from wherever
you are staying and will take you for 5.5 hours to both sites.

For booking or to view other tours, visit www.icelandtotal.com.
