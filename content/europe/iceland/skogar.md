Skógar is a small settlement on Iceland's south coast, best known as the site of Skógafoss, one of the biggest and most spectacular waterfalls in Iceland. The falls plunge 60 metres from the edge of the former sea cliffs, and in good light a rainbow is almost always visible in the mist at the base. You can walk right up to the foot of the falls and, via a steep staircase, to the top for views across the coastal plain to the sea.

The Skógar Folk Museum beside the falls is one of the best of its kind in Iceland, with an open-air collection of traditional turf houses and a very thorough collection of historical artefacts from rural Icelandic life. The Fimmvörðuháls hiking trail begins here, crossing the pass between Eyjafjallajökull and Mýrdalsjökull glaciers to Þórsmörk — a demanding two-day walk across an active volcanic landscape. Skógar is on the Ring Road about 150 kilometres east of Reykjavik.
