Stykkishólmur is a small fishing town on the northern shore of the Snæfellsnes peninsula, with brightly painted houses clustered around a natural harbour. It is the main settlement on the peninsula and the departure point for the Baldur ferry across Breiðafjörður to the Westfjords.

The town has a pleasantly end-of-the-road feel. The harbour area is the heart of it — fresh seafood, a handful of cafes, and views across the island-dotted bay. The Library of Water, an art installation by Roni Horn in the old library building on the hill, is worth the short walk up. Stykkishólmur makes a good base for exploring Snæfellsnes, with the glacier, lava fields, and dramatic coastline all within an hour's drive.
