title: Dún Chonchúir
type: poi

The largest and most impressive of the Aran Islands' stone forts, this massive oval structure sits on Inis Meáin's highest point. The walls are over three metres thick and five metres high, built entirely of dry stone without mortar. Its age is uncertain — estimates range from 700 BC to 500 AD — but the engineering is remarkable regardless. The single entrance faces north, and the fort would have been nearly impossible to storm from the steep southern approach. Climb to the top of the walls for views across all three islands and the Connemara mountains.
