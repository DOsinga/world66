Blarney is a small village a few miles northwest of [Cork](/europe/ireland/cork), visited almost entirely for one reason: Blarney Castle and the stone set in its battlements that, according to legend, gives the gift of eloquence to anyone who kisses it. The ritual involves leaning backwards over a sheer drop while a guide holds your legs — undignified, mildly terrifying, and enormously popular.

The castle itself is a well-preserved 15th-century tower house set in attractive grounds. The gardens surrounding it are surprisingly extensive, with a poison garden, fern garden, and rock close with druidic associations. Most visitors spend two to three hours here before returning to Cork.

Blarney is an easy half-day trip from Cork city. The village has a handful of pubs and the obligatory Blarney Woollen Mills shop, but the castle is the draw.
