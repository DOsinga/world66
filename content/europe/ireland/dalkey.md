Dalkey is a small, upscale village on the coast south of [Dublin](/europe/ireland/dublin), reachable in half an hour on the DART train from Pearse Station. It makes one of the best day trips from the city — scenic, walkable, and blissfully far from Temple Bar.

The centre of the village is compact and pleasant, with good restaurants alongside the usual pubs. A walking tour takes you past several medieval castles: Goat's Castle and Archbold's Castle both date from the twelfth century. Follow the winding streets eastward and you reach the coast, where Dalkey Island sits just offshore — its ruins of eighteenth-century fortresses and an early medieval church visible from the mainland.

Sorrento Park, nearly hidden on a steep little hill, offers views along the coastline and out to the island, and on a clear day you can see all the way to Wales. For a longer walk, the Vico Road south toward Killiney has some of the best coastal scenery in the Dublin area.
