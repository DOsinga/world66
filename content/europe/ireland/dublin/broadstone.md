The Home of the famous Cork exile and patriot Goosed. 

It is one of the oldest quarters of Dublin and has been continously inhabited since Viking Times. 

The nearest drinking establishments would be in Phibsborough - although Goosed prefers to totter down to Cumiskeys across from the bus station for a swift half but he does recommend the crocodile steak in McGowans on Phibsborough Road. 

The crocodiles are imported live from warmer climes each week and kept in a large tank at the back of the pub - all those who dare can partake in a fight with the croc. If you kill it you get to eat for free. However if you have to be rescued by the bouncers with electric cattle prods then you have to pay for the recharging of the prods. 

A major attraction is the fine old Gandon building which is now known as Kings Inns on Constitution Hill.Apparently it used to be the gaff of some geezer who was jealous of Lord Leinster and built this huge house - but he built it upwind of the then market in Smithfield and the smells devalued the price of the place. The market is now gone, apart from horse trading on a Sunday, but the pubs there have an early morning license which is nice. 

The Mummies in St Fachtna's Church are also worth a visit. 

Other things to see are the war memorial, the old railway tracks make a lovely walk in summer and of course there is the synagogue - where apparently one of Anne Franks relations is the rabbi. 

Eating wise a batter burger in guilios of capel street is a must for the walk up constitution hill at 3am. 

Further advice can be obtained from Goosed via www.peoplerepublicofcork.com
