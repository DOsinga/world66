One of the great country estates of Ireland, set against the backdrop of Sugar Loaf Mountain about 20 km south of Dublin in Co. Wicklow. The Palladian mansion dates to the 1730s and was gutted by fire in 1974; the ground floor has been restored and now houses shops, a cafe, and an exhibition on the house's history.

The real draw is the 47 acres of formal gardens, among the finest in Ireland. Italian and Japanese gardens, a walled garden, a pet cemetery, ornamental lakes, and over 200 varieties of trees spread across terraced grounds with views toward the Wicklow Mountains. The gardens were laid out in the 18th and 19th centuries and have been continuously maintained since.

Powerscourt Waterfall, Ireland's highest at 121 metres, is a separate entrance about 6 km further into the valley. The drive through the Wicklow countryside is scenic, making the whole outing a pleasant half-day escape from the city. Bus tours run from Dublin, or you can drive in about 40 minutes.
