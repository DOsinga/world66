Enniskerry is a small village in County Wicklow, perched on the Glencullen River in the foothills of the Wicklow Mountains. It sits just south of the Dublin border, about 24 km from the city centre, making it an easy escape from the capital.

The village is the gateway to Powerscourt Estate, one of Ireland's grandest country houses with spectacular gardens and a waterfall that is the highest in the country. The Wicklow Mountains stretch out behind the village, and there is good walking in every direction. Enniskerry itself has a pretty triangular village green, a handful of cafes, and the kind of quiet that makes a half-day trip from Dublin feel like a proper retreat.
