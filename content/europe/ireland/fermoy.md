Fermoy (Mainistir Fhear Maigh in Irish) is a town of about 5,000 on the River Blackwater in County Cork, roughly midway between Cork city and Cahir. The modern town was planned and built by the Scottish entrepreneur John Anderson in 1791 — he is also credited with developing Ireland's mail coach system. The site, however, is older: a Cistercian abbey founded in the 12th century stood here, and a ford on the Blackwater long before that.

Fermoy sits in a scenic stretch of the Blackwater valley, beneath the Galtee mountains. The Blackwater is renowned for salmon and trout fishing, and draws anglers — particularly from Britain — throughout the season. The town lies on the N8 Cork–Dublin road, making it a convenient stop on that journey.
