The big draw of the town, an imposing Anglo-Norman castle founded in the 12th century by William Marshal and rebuilt in the 19th century by the Butler family, who held it for over 600 years. The east wing is furnished in 19th-century style; the Long Gallery, hung with Butler family portraits, is the highlight. Guided tours run roughly every 20 minutes through the summer. The surrounding parkland is free to enter and pleasant for a walk. The Butler Gallery, in the former castle kitchen, shows contemporary art.
