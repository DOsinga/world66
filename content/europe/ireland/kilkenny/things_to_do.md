---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Kilkenny is compact enough to walk in a morning, anchored at one end by the castle and at the other by St Canice's Cathedral with its round tower. Between the two runs the Medieval Mile along the old High Street, passing Rothe House (a 16th-century merchant's complex with a museum), the Tholsel, and the Black Abbey. Climb the round tower at St Canice's — one of only two in Ireland you can still go up — for a fine view over the rooftops.

The Kilkenny Design Centre opposite the castle is one of the best places in Ireland to shop for Irish ceramics, glass, textiles, and jewellery. Kyteler's Inn on St Kieran's Street, dating from the 14th century, is the most atmospheric pub in town and the site of Ireland's first witchcraft trial.
