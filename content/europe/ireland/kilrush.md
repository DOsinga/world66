Kilrush is a small market town on the Shannon Estuary in west Clare, the kind of place you pass through on the way to Loop Head and then wish you had stopped longer. The town has a wide main street, a handful of good pubs, and a marina that serves as the departure point for Scattery Island — a monastic settlement in the estuary with a well-preserved round tower and the ruins of six churches, all deserted and atmospheric.

The real draw is the surrounding coast. Loop Head Peninsula stretches west from Kilrush to the lighthouse at Loop Head itself, where the cliffs are as dramatic as the Cliffs of Moher but without the tour buses. The drive out along the peninsula passes through tiny villages, past blow holes and sea stacks, and ends at a headland where you can watch gannets dive. The Shannon estuary is home to a resident pod of bottlenose dolphins, and boat trips from Kilrush marina offer reliable sightings.

Kilrush had its moment as a planned town in the eighteenth century, and the broad streets and market square still reflect that ambition. Today it is quiet, unpretentious, and useful as a base for exploring the western Clare coast that most visitors to the county never reach.
