Kiltimagh is a small town in County Mayo, in the rural west of Ireland. Like many towns in this part of the country, it lost much of its population to emigration over the decades — a history reflected in its quiet main street and the surrounding empty countryside.

The town has made efforts to reinvent itself in recent years. A local sculpture trail and community arts projects have added some character, and the surrounding bogland and hills offer good walking. It is not a destination in its own right, but if you are driving through Mayo between Knock and Swinford you will pass through, and it is worth a stop for a cup of tea and a look around.
