title: "Sights"

---

Ireland's most visited sights range from prehistoric monuments to medieval castles to dramatic coastline. The cities hold most of the museums and galleries, but the countryside contains much of what makes Ireland worth visiting.

**Newgrange** in County Meath is a passage tomb older than Stonehenge or the Pyramids at Giza — built around 3200 BC. The interior chamber is aligned with the winter solstice sunrise; there is a lottery each year to attend. The site is managed by the OPW (Office of Public Works) and visits are by guided tour only. [Newgrange](/europe/ireland/newgrange) is about 50km north of Dublin.

**The Rock of Cashel** in County Tipperary is a cluster of medieval ecclesiastical buildings on a limestone outcrop rising from the plain — one of the most dramatic sites in Ireland. The cathedral, round tower, and Cormac's Chapel (12th century, with remarkable Romanesque carvings) are all on the same rock. [Cashel](/europe/ireland/cashel) is easily reached from Cork or Dublin.

**The Cliffs of Moher** in County Clare rise 214 metres above the Atlantic and stretch for 8km. They are one of the most visited natural sights in Ireland and can be crowded in summer. Visiting at the northern end, away from the main visitor centre, gives a better experience. [Clare](/europe/ireland/clare) is the surrounding county.

**Glendalough** in County Wicklow is a 6th-century monastic settlement in a glacial valley, with a round tower and several churches surviving in good condition. It is about an hour from Dublin and can be visited on a day trip. The valley itself is beautiful for walking. [Glendalough](/europe/ireland/glendalough) is well signposted from the N11.

**The Skellig Islands** off the Kerry coast include Skellig Michael, a UNESCO World Heritage Site where early Christian monks built a monastery on the summit of a 218-metre sea stack. Boat trips to the island run from [Portmagee](/europe/ireland/ringofkerry) and other harbours in summer. Landing is weather-dependent and numbers are limited; book well in advance. The island is steep and unforgiving — not suitable for everyone — but the experience is extraordinary.

**The Book of Kells** at Trinity College Dublin is the most famous illuminated manuscript in the world, produced by Irish monks around 800 AD. The exhibition has been substantially upgraded and explains the manuscript's creation in detail. Joining the queue early in the day or pre-booking online is strongly recommended. [Dublin](/europe/ireland/dublin) is the obvious base.

**The Burren** in County Clare is a 250 square kilometre limestone plateau with a landscape like nowhere else in Ireland. Underground rivers, turloughs (seasonal lakes), megalithic tombs, and a bizarre combination of Arctic, Mediterranean, and alpine plants growing in the same cracks in the rock. The area is best explored on foot or by bike; the Burren National Park has marked walking routes.

**Kilmainham Gaol** in Dublin is a 19th-century prison where the leaders of the 1916 Easter Rising were executed. It is now one of the most visited museums in Ireland and one of the most effective for understanding modern Irish history. Pre-booking is essential in summer.
