Skellig Michael is a dramatic rock island rising from the Atlantic off Ireland's southwest coast. Local boatmen ferry visitors out from Portmagee or Ballinskelligs, dropping them at the base of the cliffs to climb roughly 600 stone steps — built by monks over a thousand years ago — up to a cluster of beehive huts perched near the summit.

The monastery, founded in the 6th century, was home to a small community of ascetic monks for several hundred years. The setting is extraordinary: sheer cliffs plunging into the sea, colonies of puffins nesting on the ledges in summer, and sweeping views back to the Kerry coastline. UNESCO listed it as a World Heritage Site in 1996, and it gained wider fame as a filming location for Star Wars.

Boat trips run from roughly mid-May to early October, weather permitting, and numbers are strictly limited. Book well in advance. Geoffrey Moorhouse's *Sun Dancing* is worth reading for the history of life on the Skelligs.
