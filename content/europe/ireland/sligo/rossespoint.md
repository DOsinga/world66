Rosses Point is a small seaside village on a peninsula jutting into Sligo Bay, about eight kilometres from [Sligo](/europe/ireland/sligo) town. It has fine beaches, a pier, and commanding views across the bay to Knocknarea and the Ox Mountains.

The County Sligo Golf Club, one of the best links courses in Ireland, occupies much of the headland. The village itself is quiet — a few pubs, some holiday houses, and a long strand popular with walkers. In summer it fills up with families, but out of season it is a peaceful spot with Atlantic wind and big skies.
