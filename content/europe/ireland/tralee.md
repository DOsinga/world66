Tralee is the county town of Kerry and the main gateway to the [Dingle Peninsula](/europe/ireland/dingle), one of the most spectacular stretches of coastline in Ireland. The town itself is a practical, unpretentious place — not a beauty spot, but a good base with decent pubs, restaurants, and all the services you need.

The town park, with its rose garden and canal walk, is one of the nicest urban green spaces in the west of Ireland. The Kerry County Museum does a good job of covering local history from the medieval town to the War of Independence. But the real draw is access: the Dingle road runs west from here into some of Ireland's wildest scenery, and the Ring of Kerry starts just to the south. Tralee also hosts the Rose of Tralee festival every August, a distinctly Irish affair that fills every pub and B&B in town.
