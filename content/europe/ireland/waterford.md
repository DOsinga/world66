Waterford is Ireland's oldest city, founded by the Vikings around 914 AD and still retaining much of its medieval and Georgian character. The Viking triangle at the centre of the city contains the most compact concentration of historical monuments in Ireland, anchored by **Reginald's Tower** — the oldest urban structure in the country — which now houses a Viking museum. The city walls, the best preserved after Derry, can be traced around much of the old centre.

Waterford is best known internationally for **Waterford Crystal**, whose factory produces hand-cut crystal using techniques unchanged since the 18th century. Factory tours (weekdays only) are a popular draw, watching the entire process from glassblowing to cutting.

The **Waterford Greenway** is a 46-kilometre cycling and walking trail along a former railway line from the city to Dungarvan, passing through scenic countryside and the Mahon and Tay river valleys. It is one of the most enjoyable recreational trails in Ireland.

Waterford serves as a useful base between [Cork](/europe/ireland/cork) and [Dublin](/europe/ireland/dublin), and the surrounding countryside — the Nore and Barrow valleys, the Knockmealdown Mountains — rewards a day or two of exploration.
