**Air**

Waterford has its own regional airport (WAT). Aer Arann runs daily flights to Waterford from London Luton Airport (LTN). On Mondays, Thursdays, Fridays, and Sundays, Aer Arann runs flights to Waterford from Birmingham (BHX) and the airline runs a flight to and from Manchester (MAN) every day except Saturday. If you do choose to fly into Waterford, there is no bus service to or from the airport. You will need to either rent a car or take a taxi. The taxi from the airport to the city center will cost approximately 17 euro. Cork Airport (ORK) or Dublin Airport (DUB) are better options if you are arriving from further away.

**Car**

Waterford is on the N25 from Cork City and the N24 from Limerick. The drive from Cork to Waterford should take an hour and a half if there is no traffic and the drive from Limerick to Waterford should take two hours in good conditions. To drive from Dublin, you would need to take the N7(M7) out of the city and then take the N9 to Waterford. In a car, the route from Dublin to Waterford should take two and a half hours.

**Public Transport**

There are several options available for travelers who prefer to use public transport. Bus Eireann runs frequent buses to Dublin (10.50 Euro, three hour journey) and to Cork City (15 Euro, two and a quarter hour journey). Bus Eirann also runs buses to Wexford, Killarney, Dungarven and other area cities. A private bus company called Rapid Express runs buses to Dublin for ten euro. The Rapid Express bus continues through the city to Dublin Airport.

Trains are also an available method of transportation although they are more expensive and less frequent than the buses. A train to Dublin costs 28 Euro and takes three hours and a train to Limerick costs 21.50 and also takes three hours.
