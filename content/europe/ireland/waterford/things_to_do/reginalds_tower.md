Built in the 10th century by the Vikings, Reginald's Tower is the oldest urban civic building in Ireland and possibly one of the oldest in the British Isles. The cylindrical tower has served as a mint, a prison, and a military fortification, and now houses the Waterford Treasures Viking Museum with artefacts from the city's Viking and medieval past.
