**Visiting Ireland: When To Go Questions Answered**

If you are planning a trip to Ireland, when to go questions shouldn&rsquo;t be a cause for any apprehensions. Ireland's climate is pleasant for a major part of the year. The island nation receives frequent rainfall throughout the year. This is why tourists here are advised to always carry some form of wet weather gear. Packing umbrellas and raincoats is recommended.

#### **Best Time to Visit Ireland**

It is assumed that most travelers prefer a season during which the temperatures are slightly milder and don&rsquo;t hamper activities. The period from May to September, or the official summer months of Ireland, is the best traveling time. Summers provide a blend of pleasantly cold days. The snow melt has ensured the streams are gushing with water. The summer season means a few sporadic showers and some fog but not to the extent of hampering day trips and shopping. Within the summer season, June to August is the nation&rsquo;s high season for tourism. People wanting to budget their trip should arrive by the end of May or during the first week of September.

#### **Daytime–based Traveling Season**

People planning trips to Ireland should look beyond the weather perspective. One of the most important factors is the amount of daylight which can vary considerably across different seasons. This is particularly important for people who plan to explore the outdoors. Ireland&rsquo;s location ensures the daytime length during summer is uniquely long. Sunset is as late as 11 p.m. during the longest days. Some people plan winter tours to Ireland because of the pristine, ice-capped landscape. However, during this time the sunset could be as early as 4 p.m.
