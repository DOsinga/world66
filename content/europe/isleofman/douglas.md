Douglas is the capital of the Isle of Man, a self-governing Crown dependency in the Irish Sea between England and Ireland. The town curves around a wide bay, with a Victorian promenade running the length of the seafront and the horse-drawn trams that have been clopping along it since 1876.

The island is best known for the TT motorcycle races each June, when Douglas transforms from a quiet offshore town into a petrolhead pilgrimage site. Outside race season, the appeal is quieter — a compact town center with decent pubs and restaurants, easy access to the island's beautiful glens and coastline, and a pace of life that feels a few decades behind the mainland in the best possible way.
