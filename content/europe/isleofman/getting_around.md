---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The Isle of Man has a surprisingly good public transport network for its size, and the heritage railways are themselves worth the trip.

**Bus Vannin** operates an island-wide bus service departing from Lord Street Bus Interchange in central Douglas. Buses reach most towns and villages; journey times are short (Douglas to Peel is around 40 minutes). Contactless payment is accepted.

**Isle of Man Railways** runs four distinct services. The Steam Railway operates between Douglas and Port Erin on the southern line — one of the longest narrow-gauge steam railways still running in the British Isles. The Manx Electric Railway (MER) runs along the east coast from Douglas to Ramsey, passing through Laxey. From Laxey, the Snaefell Mountain Railway climbs to the island's summit. The Douglas Bay Horse Tramway runs along the Douglas promenade in summer, connecting the ferry terminal with the northern end where you can join the MER.

The **Go Explore card** gives unlimited travel on all buses and railways and is the best value option for visitors spending more than a day on the island. It is available as a one-day, three-day, or five-day pass and also includes free entry to heritage attractions.

**Car hire** is available at the airport and in Douglas if you want more flexibility, particularly for exploring the less-served western and northern parts of the island. Roads are generally quiet outside TT season. Speed limits are enforced — and during the TT, road closures affect large parts of the network.

**Cycling** is popular, and the island has a growing network of signed routes. Roads outside Douglas tend to be narrow but low-traffic.
