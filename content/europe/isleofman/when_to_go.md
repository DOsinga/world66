---
title: "When To Go"
type: section
---

The Isle of Man has a temperate maritime climate — mild, damp, and changeable. Locals will tell you that you can get four seasons in a single day, and they are not far wrong. Summer highs typically reach 17–19°C; winter temperatures hover around 5–7°C. Snow is rare at sea level. Wind is a near-constant feature, especially on the uplands.

**May and June** bring the longest days and the most dramatic event on the Manx calendar: the TT Races. If motorcycling is your thing, this is an unmissable experience. If not, avoid the island entirely during these two weeks — accommodation is largely pre-booked months in advance, prices are high, and the roads are either closed or clogged. The Manx Grand Prix in August–September is a smaller version of the same experience.

**July and August** are the warmest months and the best for walking, cycling, and beach days. The island is busy but not overwhelmed outside of race events.

**September and October** are quieter, the light is good, and the heather on the uplands is in bloom. This is an underrated time to visit.

**Winter (November–March)** is quiet, some attractions reduce their hours or close, and ferry crossings can be disrupted by bad weather. That said, the island has a real off-season character and prices drop considerably.
