Pescasseroli is the main town inside Abruzzo, Lazio and Molise National Park — one of Italy's oldest protected areas and the last stronghold of the Apennine brown bear. It's the most accessible point in the park, with bus connections to Rome and Pescara, and it makes the natural base for anyone wanting to explore the surrounding mountains and valleys.

The town itself is small and pleasant rather than remarkable — a cluster of stone buildings at 1167 metres, with the park headquarters and a small natural history museum. The surrounding landscape is the real attraction: deep beech forests, open meadows grazed by Apennine chamois, and in summer the meadows fill with wildflowers. Serious hikers can strike out into the backcountry; more casual visitors are content with the marked trails near town and the chance of spotting deer, wolves and — with luck — a bear.

The park authority office in Piazza Sant'Antonio can provide maps and information on current trail conditions and wildlife sightings. Summer and winter both have their appeal: in July and August the meadows are at their best, while in winter the area around Pescasseroli is used for cross-country skiing.
