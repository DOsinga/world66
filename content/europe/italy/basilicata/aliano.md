Aliano is the village where Carlo Levi was exiled by the Fascist government in 1935, an experience he turned into *Christ Stopped at Eboli*, one of the great books about southern Italy. The village clings to a ridge above a dramatic landscape of calanchi — eroded clay badlands that look almost lunar in the afternoon light.

The small Museo Carlo Levi preserves the house where he lived and some of his paintings. Levi asked to be buried here rather than in Turin, and his grave sits in the village cemetery. The whole place has the feel of a literary pilgrimage, quiet and a little melancholy.

Aliano is remote even by Basilicata standards, which is part of the point. The drive in, through empty hills and deep ravines, gives you a visceral sense of the isolation Levi wrote about. If you are exploring this undervisited corner of southern Italy, it is well worth the detour.
