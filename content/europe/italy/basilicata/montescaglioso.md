Montescaglioso is a hilltop town in [Basilicata](/europe/italy/basilicata), perched above the Bradano valley about 20 km south of [Matera](/europe/italy/basilicata/matera). It is one of the more rewarding stops in a region that rewards detours.

The town's highlight is the Abbey of San Michele Arcangelo, a Benedictine monastery founded in the 11th century with fine cloisters and frescoed rooms. But the old centre itself is worth exploring — narrow streets wind between stone houses, and the views from the edges of town stretch across the valleys of the Murge plateau.

In August, the Festa di San Rocco brings colour and noise to the streets. It is a local affair — no tour buses, just a town celebrating its patron saint with processions, food, and fireworks.
