Soveria Mannelli is a small mountain town in the Sila foothills of Catanzaro province, sitting at about 750 metres elevation and surrounded by dense chestnut forests. It is best known in Italy as the home of Rubbettino Editore, one of the country's most respected independent publishing houses, which has given this otherwise quiet place an outsized cultural footprint.

The town itself is compact and pleasant to walk around, with a handful of churches and a lively central piazza where locals gather in the evenings. The surrounding hills offer good walking trails through forests of chestnut and beech, and in autumn the woods are popular with mushroom foragers. The altitude keeps summers cooler than the sweltering Calabrian coast below, making it a welcome retreat in July and August.

Soveria Mannelli works well as a base for exploring the western Sila plateau, one of Calabria's great natural areas. The Sila National Park, with its highland lakes and ancient Laricio pines, is a short drive east. The town is reached by a winding road from the SS19, and while public transport connections are limited, having a car opens up the surrounding countryside beautifully.
