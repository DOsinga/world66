Cosenza sits at the confluence of the Busento and Crati rivers, the largest city in northern Calabria and capital of its province. It was the chief city of the ancient Brutii before the Romans took it in 204 BC.

According to tradition, the Visigothic king Alaric I was buried in the bed of the Busento here around 410 AD, his tomb hidden by diverting the river. A castle built by Emperor Frederick II dominates the old town from the hilltop above.

Modern Cosenza has a lively centro storico with good restaurants and bars, and a pedestrianised Corso Mazzini lined with outdoor sculptures. The city is a natural gateway to the Sila mountains and the Pollino National Park.
