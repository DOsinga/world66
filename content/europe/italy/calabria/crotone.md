The town of Steccato lies in the Crotone province, on the coast of the Ionian Sea within the Capo Rizzuto national park. The protected marine area provides rich and diverse sea life, and the beaches are uncrowded compared to the Tyrrhenian coast.

Steccato makes an excellent base for visiting the [Sila](/europe/italy/calabria/la_sila) mountains, a national park of forests, lakes, and mountain views. About 25 kilometres away is the archaeological site of Capo Colonna, a remnant of Magna Graecia. Its famous single column is all that remains of a temple dedicated to the goddess Hera Lacinia.

Because Steccato sees few overseas tourists, visitors get a genuine experience of southern Italian life. English is not widely spoken, so a phrase book helps, but the locals are patient and welcoming.

The closest airport is Lamezia Terme. By road, the SS106 connects Steccato to both Crotone and Catanzaro.
