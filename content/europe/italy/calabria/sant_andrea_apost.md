Sant'Andrea Apostolo dello Jonio is a small town in Catanzaro province perched on a hillside above the Ionian Sea coast. Like many towns on the eastern side of Calabria, it is quiet and largely untouched by international tourism, which is precisely what makes it appealing. The views from the upper town stretch across the blue Ionian to one side and up toward the Sila mountains on the other.

Below the town, a long stretch of sandy beach runs along the coast, uncrowded even in the height of summer. The water is clean and warm, and a few seasonal lido bars set up umbrellas and serve cold drinks. The old centre of town has narrow lanes, a couple of churches, and a handful of family-run trattorias serving simple Calabrian cooking -- grilled fish, pasta with 'nduja, and local wines from the surrounding hills.

Getting here requires a car or patience with infrequent regional buses. The nearest train station is at Soverato, about 15 kilometres south. The reward for the effort is a genuine slice of everyday southern Italian life, far from the crowds of the Amalfi Coast or the Sicilian resorts.
