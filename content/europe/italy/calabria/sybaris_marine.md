Sibari (historically Sybaris) was the wealthiest and most powerful city of Magna Graecia, so famously luxurious that "sybarite" became a byword for decadence. The ancient city was destroyed in 510 BC and later buried under river silt.

Today the archaeological park preserves excavations from several periods of settlement, and the Museo Nazionale della Sibaritide houses finds from the site. Sibari Marina, on the Ionian coast, has a long sandy beach and is a popular summer destination for Italian families.
