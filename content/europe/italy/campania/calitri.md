*Europe > Italy > Campania > Calitri*

Calitri is a small medieval hill town in the interior of Campania, in the Irpinia area of Avellino province. It sits about 80 km east of Naples, perched above a valley of rivers, fields, and chestnut woods. Locals call it the "Positano d'Irpinia" — the comparison flatters the coast somewhat, but the pastel colours and layered streets of the old town (the Antico Borgo) are genuinely attractive.

The town is best known in the region for its ceramics tradition, producing distinctive red and green-glazed pottery that has been made here for centuries. Courses in ceramics, needlepoint, and local cooking are available. The surrounding countryside offers walking, cycling, horse riding, and access to thermal baths at nearby Contursi Terme.

Calitri is not on most tourist itineraries, which is part of its appeal. If you want to see an inland Campanian hill town that still functions as a working community, this is a reasonable choice.
