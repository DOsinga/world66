---
title: "Eating Out in Calitri"
type: section
---

Calitrian cooking is rustic, meaty, and spicy — a southern Italian inland cuisine with strong Irpinian character. The local pasta is hand-made: *cingul'* (short twirly pasta with a thick tomato sauce), *lahan'*, and *cannazze* with tomato sauce and pecorino. For meat, look for *m'gliatiegghij'* — rolled lamb or kid casings stuffed with cheese, offal, garlic, and sausage — and *sfritta* (chopped pork with hot peppers). Stockfish prepared with garlic, herbs, and chili (*a la ualanegna*) is a traditional dish from the farming past.

**La Locanda dell'Arco di Zampaglione** (Via dell'Arco di Zampaglione 5). Restaurant in the medieval borgo, carved into the hillside. Outdoor dining in summer with views over the rooftops. Tel: +39 334 7921880.
