*Europe > Italy > Campania > Capri*

Capri is a small limestone island in the Bay of Naples that has been a playground for the wealthy since the Roman emperors built their villas here. It remains glamorous and expensive, and in July and August it is genuinely crowded — the day-trippers from Naples pour off ferries into Marina Grande and make the piazzetta look like an airport terminal. Come in May, June, or September and it's a different place: beautiful, occasionally quiet, and still worth the trip.

The island has two main towns: Capri town (with its famous piazzetta) and Anacapri, higher up the hill and somewhat calmer. The **Blue Grotto** (Grotta Azzurra) is the most-visited attraction — a sea cave lit by light refracting through an underwater opening, creating an extraordinary azure glow. Get there early; the queue at midday can be two hours. The boat from Marina Grande runs from about 9am.

For walking, the path up to **Villa Jovis** (Emperor Tiberius's main residence, on the eastern peak) takes about 45 minutes from Capri town and offers views in every direction. **Villa San Michele** in Anacapri, built by Swedish writer Axel Munthe on Roman ruins, has beautiful gardens open to visitors. The **Faraglioni** — three sea stacks off the eastern coast — are best seen from the water or from the Arco Naturale trail.

The island's food is based on the sea and the garden: seafood pasta, rabbit, local cheese, and the excellent lemons that flavour everything from drinks to dessert. Prices are high; eat away from the piazzetta.
