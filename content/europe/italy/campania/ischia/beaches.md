---
title: "Beaches in Ischia"
type: section
---

Ischia has 21 km of coastline and beaches that range from sandy family stretches to volcanic-black coves. Most of the best ones have lidos (beach clubs) that charge an entry fee including sun lounger and umbrella; free public sections usually exist at the edges.

**Maronti** (near Barano) is the longest beach on the island — 3 km of sand with thermal springs emerging from the shore. Water taxis run there from Sant'Angelo; there's also a road from Barano. Less crowded than most.

**Citara** (Forio) is popular and accessible, with the Poseidon Thermal Gardens adjacent. Most of the beach is privately managed but free sections exist.

**Spiaggia degli Inglesi** (English Beach, near Ischia Porto) is small and rarely crowded. Calm water.

**Fumarole** (near Sant'Angelo) is where geothermal steam rises through the black sand. The volcanic origin gives the water a slight warm tint. BBQ facilities on the beach.
