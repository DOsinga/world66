Casamicciola Terme is one of Ischia's six communes, on the northern coast of the island. It was the original centre of the island's thermal tradition — the mineral springs here were used therapeutically from ancient times, and by the 19th century the town attracted distinguished visitors from across Europe. Ibsen wrote *Peer Gynt* partly during a stay here.

A devastating earthquake in 1883 killed roughly a third of the island's population and destroyed most of the town. It was rebuilt but retains its quieter, more old-fashioned character compared to Ischia Porto.

Behind the centre, pine forests and chestnut woods cover the slopes of Monte Epomeo. Paths lead up the mountain from here. The main thermal park, **Parco Termale Castiglione**, sits above the town in a wooded setting with ten pools at different temperatures, descending to a private beach.
