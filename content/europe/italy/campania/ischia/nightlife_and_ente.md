---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Ischia"
type: section
---

The** Ischia nightlife **is just as fiery and entertaining as the
daytime on this exotic volcanic island. When looking for fun after the
sun has set, try any of the following:

**O'Spasso**

In the Piazza degli Eroi (Heroes' Square) is one of the Ischia's most
eclectic nightclubs. Comfortable wicker-backed chairs congregate around
sleek wavy lines and warm lighting. The centerpiece is probably the
small pond next to the grand piano, at which local professional
musicians and comedians alike put on a cabaret or comedy show nightly.
The windows give a breathtaking sight of the surrounding pine forest at
Heroes' Square. O'Spasso has a full bar, but the atmosphere is rather
laidback and the comedians are family-friendly enough to make taking
even older teens along with you a positive experience. O'Spasso is only
open during the summer, so don't miss it if you're there during the
prime nightclub season!

**Bar Calise**

Near Ischia Harbor at Via Sogliuzzo Antonio, 69 is Bar Calise, a
classy cafe/nightclub for drinks and mouthwatering desserts. You can sit
outdoors under a stylish canopy and have an up-close view of the
tropical garden, which is worth the price of drinks and dessert alone.
Desserts include a variety of fruits, cakes and biscuits. The bar has a
full range of drinks, but the easygoing, tranquil atmosphere makes this a
good destination to take your kids, particularly older kids, to for
dessert before heading off to bed.

**Villa La Mortella
**

At Via F. Calise no 35 is the Villa La Mortella, which
are spectacular gardens in which the William Walton Foundation regularly
organizes jazz and classical concerts from April through November. The
highlight of these concerts is the Ischia Jazz Festival held annually in
September. Enjoying music in the gardens under the night sky is an
experience for single travelers, couples and families with children ages
8 and up. Tickets usually cost around 15€ per adult and 12€
per kid ages 8 to 12. Price includes a pass to walk through the
entirety of the gardens.

**De Maio**

If ice cream is more of your idea of a nightcap than an actual
nightcap, or you want to show the kids a good time after dark, head to
De Maio at Piazza Antica Reggia 9. Regularly voted one of
the best parlors for ice cream in Ischia, De Maio has been in business
since 1930, serving homemade creamy ice creams. You can also actually
get a variety of cocktails, as well as coffee and appetizers. The
average cost for a scoop is 2€.
