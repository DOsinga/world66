---
title: "Beaches near Naples"
type: section
---

Naples itself doesn't have great beaches — the nearest are in Posillipo, where lidos (private beach clubs) line the waterfront. The better option is to go further. The Circumvesuviana train takes you south to the Sorrentine Peninsula, where [Sorrento](/europe/italy/campania/sorrento) has access to small rocky beaches and several lidos. Meta di Sorrento has a proper sandy beach that's popular with Neapolitans.

The real beaches are on the islands. [Ischia](/europe/italy/campania/ischia) has 21 km of varied coastline — Maronti Beach in the south is the largest and most beautiful, reachable by sea taxi. Spiaggia dei Pescatori on Ischia Porto is accessible and pleasant. [Capri](/europe/italy/campania/capri) has Marina Piccola, a small but dramatic bay below the Faraglioni rocks; it gets crowded in July and August.

Ferries for Ischia and Capri leave from Molo Beverello near Piazza Municipio, with frequent departures in summer.
