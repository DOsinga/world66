The first of Ferrante's four Neapolitan novels, following two girls growing up in a poor Naples neighbourhood from the 1950s onwards. The series is the most vivid literary portrait of the city in existence — the violence, the class dynamics, the relationship between education and escape, the way the camorra saturates daily life. Essential reading before visiting; even more powerful after.
