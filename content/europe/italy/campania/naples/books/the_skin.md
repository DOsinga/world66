Set in Naples in 1943–44, during the Allied liberation. Malaparte served as a liaison officer and his account of the city — starving, humiliated, doing anything to survive — is hallucinatory and deeply uncomfortable. Part novel, part memoir, entirely unreliable but electrically written. The Naples it describes, with its black markets and ancient desperation, sits just beneath the surface of the modern city.
