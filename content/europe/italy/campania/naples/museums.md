There are a lot of **Naples museums** that you can visit while you are in the city of Naples in Italy. Due to the rich history, art, architecture and culture of Naples, a lot of museums were created. In fact, the city is considered as one of the cultural capitals of Italy. Thus, if you are traveling to Naples, here are some of the best museums that you can visit in the area.

**Museo Archeologico Nazionale di Napoli**

This is one of Naples oldest museums, which was built during the seventeenth century. The museum boasts of the many archeological artifacts that are found in Italy. Thus, if you want to learn all about the history of Italy, then visiting this museum should be your first option. This museum is located at Piazza Museo Nazionale 19, 80135, which is in the heart of the city.

**Museo Nazionale della Ceramica Ducca di Martina**

Home to the largest collections of decorative arts in Italy, this museum has housed a lot of ceramics made by various artisans in Italy since the beginning of the 19th century. You can also see historical designs of ceramics and ceramics that were favored by royalties. The museum is located in Via Aniello Falcone.** **

**Museo Cappella Sansevero**

Located in Francesco De Sanctis, this museum is also located in the metropolis of Naples. The museum also houses a nearby chapel, and it showcases religious artifacts which mainly focus on Christianity. In this museum, you will be able to see how Christianity has spread in Italy particularly in Naples. This museum is also the best place to learn about the life and works of Jesus Christ. Moreover, life-size statues of biblical characters also adorn the walls of the museum.

**Museo di San Martino**

This museum is located on the hill that overlooks the entire Bay of Naples. This structure was constructed during the 1300&rsquo;s and served as the house of Charles d&rsquo; Anjou who was the Duke of Calabria. Over time, this house has been transformed into a museum and a lot of artworks by Tino di Camaino, Caracciolo and Vaccaro have been displayed in the museum.

**Museo di Villa Pignatelli**

The museum was once a part of the grand estate of the Acton family and is located in Riviera di Chiaia. This museum contains the heirlooms of the Acton family, thereby giving tourists a closer look at what old houses and estates in Naples look like.
