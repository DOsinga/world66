---
title: "Things to Do in Naples"
type: section
---

Naples rewards curiosity. The historic centre, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a dense labyrinth of Greek-Roman streets overlaid with medieval churches, baroque palaces, and street food vendors. Beyond sightseeing, the city's volcanic hinterland — the Phlegraean Fields to the west, Vesuvius to the east — offers dramatic excursions.

The **National Archaeological Museum** is the single best reason to spend a day indoors anywhere in Campania. It holds the finds from Pompeii and Herculaneum that couldn't be left on-site, including the Farnese collection and the famous Secret Cabinet of erotic art. Allow at least three hours. **Castel dell'Ovo**, the oldest castle in Naples, sits on a small harbour promontory and offers sweeping views of the bay. Entry is free; it opens most mornings. The **Sansevero Chapel** is small but unmissable — the Veiled Christ sculpture alone justifies the entry fee.

For something less touristic, the **Phlegraean Fields** (Campi Flegrei) west of the city are a supervolcanic area with ancient ruins, a caldera you can walk into, and far fewer visitors than Pompeii.
