The Cilento and Vallo di Diano National Park covers a vast stretch of southern Campania, from the Tyrrhenian coast into the Apennine highlands. It is one of Italy's largest national parks and a UNESCO World Heritage Site — recognized both for its exceptional biodiversity and its cultural significance as a landscape that shaped the development of Western civilization.

The ancient Greek colonies of Paestum and Velia lie within the park's boundaries. Paestum's three Doric temples, dating from the 6th and 5th centuries BC, are among the best-preserved in the world and far less crowded than better-known sites in Sicily. The park's interior holds medieval hilltop villages, deep river gorges, and the cave systems of Pertosa-Auletta, open to guided boat tours on an underground river. The coastal section — the Cilento coast — has clear water, steep cliffs, and a series of coves that remain relatively quiet even in August.
