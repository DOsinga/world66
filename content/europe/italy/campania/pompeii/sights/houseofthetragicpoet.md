---
title: "House of the Tragic Poet"
type: poi
---

This is a good example of a house built during the Roman empire. It is not very big, with intimate rooms. Connected to the house are to tabernae or shops, indicating that the house belonged to a merchant. The entrance has a charming mosaic: a large chained dog and the motto *Cave Canem* (Beware of the dog).

As in most houses of well-to-do Romans, the walls are covered in murals, depicting mythical and erotic scenes. The house is named after a mosaic in the atrium, depicting a theatre rehersal led by the poet.
