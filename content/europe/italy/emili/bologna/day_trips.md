---
title: "Day Trips in Bologna"
type: section
---

People can be carefree when planning their **Bologna Day Trips**. Bologna is among the most significant of Italy&rsquo;s cities. It is well-connected to nearly every nearby location that is known to attract foreign tourists. Bologna is located along some of Italy&rsquo;s most important commercial routes and literary hubs. As a result, most of the historical sites within the city are mentioned across every tour-guide. It is unlikely that a visitor can miss any of these. However, the real fun lies in exploring things that are beyond such obvious landmarks. These are the most exciting form of self-explorative, day trips. Two such options have been listed below:

**1. Gastronomic Bologna Day Trips: Exploring Wines & Cheeses**

This can be a very entertaining tour as long as you are willing to spend some time exploring the lesser-acknowledged locations. Some tour-guides take visitors to the Bologna countryside. Here, visitors can visit internationally-renowned producers of Italian favorites like flavored vinegar. This invariably means visiting the Old Vinegar Works. A bit deeper into the rural districts and visitors can get a first-hand experience of how Italian cheese is prepared. This includes visiting the supposed birthplace of globally-popular cheeses like the Parmigiano. Visitors can also ask for a guided tour of the local wineries. Here, wine is still produced through century-old practices. A trip to a local winery invariably means a free, wine-tasting session!

**2. Explorative Bologna Day Trips: Sightseeing through Cycling**

Visitors are seriously recommended to ditch the cabs and high-speed rails and instead, rent a cycle to explore the scenic landscape of Bologna. This means an opportunity to view the rather unacknowledged historic ruins that are present along the back-lanes of buildings like the Palazzo Accursio and the Palazzo Protesta. However, the best part of a day-long bicycling trip is the opportunity to ride along the historic canal system that is seldom included in packaged tours. Those willing to pedal a bit more can also visit the nearby town of San Giacomo and see the Sanctuary of San Luca and Oratory of Santa Cecilia.
