The Cattedrale Metropolitana di San Pietro sits on Via Indipendenza, just a short walk from Piazza Maggiore. The facade has that characteristically Bolognese quality of looking half-finished — the lower part clad in marble, the upper part still rough brick — which somehow adds to its charm rather than detracting from it. The church dates back to the 10th century but has been rebuilt and remodelled several times, most dramatically in the Baroque period.

Inside, the single wide nave is imposing and surprisingly bright. Look for the large terracotta Annunciation group by Niccolò dell'Arca and the frescoed ceiling. The cathedral tends to be much quieter than the Basilica di San Petronio around the corner, making it a peaceful stop between the busier sights.
