---
title: 'Nightlife and Entertainment'
type: section
---

Because this Italian city has a large student population, there is a wide range of nightlife to choose from. Some of the main club areas are near the University of Bologna, but no matter what your interests are, you will find entertainment to suit your desires. Many of the cafes stay open until 2am.

**Jazz Clubs.** For great jazz, stop by the **Cantina Bentivoglio**. The cellars are a jazz club and the building is a palazzo built in the sixteenth century. It opens at 8 in the evening, but the jazz goes until late. It is always crowded, so get a reservation. Another great jazz club is the **Chet Baker's Jazz Club**, with a New York feel.

**Teatro.** If you are interested in concerts, opera, or theatre, see what is playing at the **Teatro Comunale**. This is the main venue for theatre in the city -- in the 1970s this was where Luciano Pavarotti frequently performed. Another great spot is the **Sala Mozart** at the Accademia Filarmonica di Bologna, where Mozart performed when he was 14.

The **Teatro Duse** is good for more traditional theatre, the **Teatro Dehon** for comedy, and the **Teatro Testoni** for children's performances.

**Bars and clubs.** **Bar Giuseppe** at the Piazza Maggiore makes one of the best espressos in town and has great gelato. The **Nu Lounge Bar** is the best spot for a dry martini, popular with 30-somethings and fashionistas.

For clubs, **Soda Pops** plays hip hop and techno. **Cassero**, located on top of one of the city's medieval gates, is one of the most popular gay clubs. And you can always do an osteria crawl -- there are many throughout the city, often in buildings dating back centuries.
