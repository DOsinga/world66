**Language.** Standard Italian is widely spoken. The Bolognese dialect is still heard among older residents, but everyone speaks Italian and many young people speak some English thanks to the university.

**Currency.** Italy uses the Euro.

**Climate.** Bologna has a humid subtropical climate. Winters are cold with fog common in the Po Valley, and occasional snow from December to February. Summers are hot and humid, with temperatures regularly above 30C. Heatwaves are common in July and August. Spring and autumn are the most pleasant seasons.

**Getting to the centre.** Guglielmo Marconi International Airport is connected to the city centre by the Aerobus. Bologna Centrale is the main train station, one of Italy's busiest rail hubs, with high-speed connections to Milan, Florence, Rome, and Venice.

**Tourist office.** Ufficio Informazioni e Accoglienza Turistica, Piazza Maggiore 1/E, tel. 051-246 541.
