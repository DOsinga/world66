title: 'Shopping'
type: section
---

**Opening hours.** Shops in Italy typically open between 9am and 12:30pm, close for a long lunch, then reopen from 3:30pm until 7:30pm. Some supermarkets stay open through the day. On Thursday afternoons and Sundays, most shops are closed. Some also close on Monday mornings.

**Markets and food shops.** A daily food market operates in the east of the city centre near Santa Maria dei Servi on Strada Maggiore. Great for fresh food and Italian cheeses -- gorgonzola, mozzarella, mascarpone.

**Panificio Vacca Angelo** (Via Riva Reno 104, tel. 051-262855) is the best bakery in town for *streghe* -- typical Bolognese crackers with rosemary, sesame seeds, or pepper. Buy some streghe and gorgonzola from the PAM supermarket across the street on Via Marconi and you have yourself a lunch.

**Panificio Osti Giuliano** (Via Galliera 34) is another great bakery just west of Via Indipendenza. Try the *raviola con la mostarda*, a Bolognese cookie with pear and apple jam.

**Shopping streets.** Via Indipendenza has everything from perfumes to cameras. The fanciest shops are in **Galleria Cavour**, just southeast of Piazza Maggiore, or on Via Farini nearby. During the whole month of July, shops hold their summer sales.

**Market days.** On Fridays and Saturdays a big market is held around Piazza VIII Agosto. It has the largest second-hand clothing market in Italy. Be aware of pickpockets.
