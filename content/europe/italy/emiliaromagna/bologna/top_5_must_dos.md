---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Climb the Torre degli Asinelli.** The two towers are perhaps the most iconic landmarks in the city. At one point Bologna had more than 100 towers, signs of stature and wealth for noble families. About 20 still stand, but the most famous are the Garisenda and the Asinelli. You can climb to the top of the Asinelli tower -- 498 steps up a narrow wooden staircase -- for an unforgettable view of the red rooftops stretching to the Apennines. The Garisenda leans more dramatically but is not open to visitors.

**2. Explore the Pinacoteca Nazionale.** The National Gallery contains works from the Baroque and early Baroque periods, including major pieces by the Carracci brothers and Guido Reni. Much of the work is deeply emotional religious art from the Counter-Reformation. Don't miss the Last Supper by El Greco and Ecstasy of St Cecilia by Raphael.

**3. Gaze at the Basilica of San Petronio.** The largest and most impressive church in the city, and one of the largest basilicas in the world. When it was first started, it was supposed to be larger than St Peter's in Rome, but the pope put a stop to that. The carvings on the door are spectacular, though the facade was never finished. Inside, a solar clock played an important role in establishing the leap year.

**4. See the Neptune Fountain at night.** Designed by Jean Boulogne de Douai, the fountain is completely lit up at night and looks its best then. At the top is a bronze Neptune, below him four angels representing the four winds, and below those four sirens symbolising the four continents known when the fountain was built.

**5. Wander the churches of Santo Stefano.** The Basilica of St Stephen is actually a complex of interconnected churches, crypts, and courtyards. Originally seven churches, now four remain. The site has been a place of worship since pre-Christian times, and the surviving churches date from the fifth to twelfth centuries. Walking from one into the next, trying to find all the connecting passages, is one of Bologna's great pleasures.
