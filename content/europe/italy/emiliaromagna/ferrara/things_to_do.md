title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Ferrara's Renaissance core — the whole of which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — is compact enough to cover in a day on foot or by bicycle. The Este Castle, the cathedral, and the frescoed Palazzo Schifanoia are the main draws; the Palazzo dei Diamanti hosts major temporary exhibitions.

**Palazzo Schifanoia**

Built in 1385 and extended under Borso d'Este, the palace's famous Sala dei Mesi (Room of the Months) contains some of the finest 15th-century secular frescoes in Italy — an astrological cycle by Ferrarese painters depicting the months, the zodiac, and the court life of the Este family.

address: Via Scandiana 23

category: Museum

**Palazzo dei Diamanti**

Named for the 8,500 diamond-shaped marble blocks on its facade, the Palazzo dei Diamanti houses a gallery and stages major travelling exhibitions — past shows have featured Dalí, Miró, Monet, and Chagall.

category: Museum

**Palazzo Municipale**

The Town Hall begun in 1243 was the Este family residence until the court moved to the castle in the 16th century. The piazza in front is the social heart of the city.

address: Piazzetta Municipale

category: Sight
