---
title: "Montechiarugolo"
type: location
---

The
original structure of this castle situated in the valley of the
river Enza dates back to the XIIth century. The noble family of
the Sanvitale rules it incontestably to 1313, when the guelph
armies conquers and almost destroys it. After that, between the
XVth and the XVIth century, it sees an interminable series of
conflicts: the pope, the emperor and powerful families like the
Visconti and the Este fight for its possession with histories
of conspiration and betrayals.

Nevertheless the fortress grows again every time out of its ruins
so that today we can enjoy today its extremly suggestive and terrible
beauty charged with its human and historical vicissitudes.
