**Mausoleum of Theodoric.** Built by Theodoric himself in 520 as his tomb. The structure is divided into two decagonal levels, completely made of Istrian stone. It is roofed with a single block of Istrian stone ten metres in diameter weighing 300 tons. A niche leads down to a room that was probably a chapel for funeral liturgies. A staircase leads to the upper floor where there is a circular porphyry tub in which Theodoric was supposedly buried. His remains were removed during Byzantine rule.

**National Museum.** Housed in the cloisters of the ex-Benedictine Monastery in the monumental complex of San Vitale. Important collections of minor arts including funeral steles, archaeological fragments, textiles (from Coptic to Renaissance), ivories, icons, coins and medals, and ancient weapons. Also houses fourteenth-century frescoes of Santa Chiara and floor mosaics discovered in 1993.
