---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Ravenna's eight UNESCO World Heritage monuments are the main draw. The mosaics in the Basilica of San Vitale and the Mausoleum of Galla Placidia are among the finest in the world — shimmering gold and blue compositions that have survived largely intact since the 5th and 6th centuries. The Mausoleum of Theodoric, built entirely of Istrian stone and capped by a single 300-ton block, is striking in a completely different way.

The National Museum, housed in the cloisters of the ex-Benedictine Monastery next to San Vitale, has an excellent collection of archaeological fragments, ivories, textiles, and ancient weapons. Dante's tomb, a small neoclassical temple in the centre of town, marks where the poet was buried after dying in Ravenna in 1321.
