This crowded beach resort on the Adriatic Sea in northeastern Italy is part of a coastal recreation area that stretches 120 km north-south from Porto Garibaldi to [Cattolica](/europe/italy/emiliaromagna/cattolica). The region offers fine sandy beaches, deep-sea fishing, hot springs, mud baths, water sports, good nightlife, and restaurants.

Of all the resorts on the Adriatic Riviera, Rimini's versatility makes it the most popular holiday destination. The town itself counts 131,000 inhabitants. Built in 27 BC, the **Arch of Augustus** is still the solemn entrance for those reaching Rimini from the Via Flaminia -- one of the oldest Roman triumphal arches in the world.

Between the seaside resort and the surrounding hills stretches the city's historic centre. The Palaces of the Podesta and Arengo, the old Fish Market, and the Fontana della Pigna are all to be admired in Piazza Cavour. Beyond the piazza, the ruins of **Castel Sismondo** are a severe reminder of the long Malatesta Dynasty. The **Bridge of Tiberius** marks the end of the first historic itinerary.

Don't miss a visit to the **Tempio Malatestiano**, a fifteenth-century jewel of Renaissance architecture, and the **Museo Civico** housing works from the fourteenth-century Riminese school.

Rimini is at the same time a busy centre for trade fairs and conventions, culture, and cinema -- it was the birthplace of Federico Fellini. [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence), [Bologna](/europe/italy/emiliaromagna/bologna), and [Venice](/europe/italy/veneto/venice) are within a few hours' drive. The Republic of [San Marino](/europe/sanmarino) is just 25 km west of Rimini -- plan a half-day excursion there.
