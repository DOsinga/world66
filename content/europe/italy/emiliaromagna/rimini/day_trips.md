title: "Day Trips in Rimini"
type: section
---

Rimini is a rich concoction of cultural grandeur, natural beauty and an enviable architectural legacy. Here's some information of the best option for day trips in this stunning Italian wonder.

**A slice of Roman magnificence**

The best way to enjoy Rimini is to take a day trip of its
architectural hotspots that resonate with historical elegance. Start
with the Augustus Arch, a quintessentially Roman structure erected in
honor of the erstwhile Roman Emperor. Move along to the Tiberius
Bridge, an impressive ancient site that connotes the commencement of
Aemilian Way. The bridge comprises of five stunning arches that are
supported by magnificent pillars. The structure is built using Istria
stone and connects the main city center with Borgo San region. Witness
the beauty of one of the most beautiful Roman amphitheatres in the
world at Rimini. This one’s a classic masterpiece built in the 2 nd
century and features more than fifty breathtaking arcades and an
elliptical shape. It is a great idea to include the archeological
museum in the trip for an added dash of heritage splendor. ** **

**The Churches of Rimini**

A trip to Rimini is not consummated till visitors view its
awe-inspiring churches and cathedrals. Start with Tempio Malatestiano,
a gothic style structure that witnessed several hybrid restoration
techniques in the past and has still endured its rich original
detailing. The San Giuliano Martire depicts several illustrations of
Bittino da Faenza and traces the life of the revered saint through a
plethora of evocative memorabilia. There are numerous other churches to
include in your church day sightseeing trip including The church of St.
John the Evangelist and the San Giovanni Battista that was built in the
12 th century. Visitors can also see the lofty and dramatic Bell Tower located at the Cathedral of Santa Colomba.

**Ah! Beaches**

Take a day trip to the town’s famous beachfront. Rimini has a nice
15 kilometer stretch of picturesque beaches called the La Marina and
this is the most frequented tourist spot of the town. The beaches of
the region often obliterate Rimini’s illustrious historical heritage
and ancient legacy and are packed in summers when visitors come here to
experience its warm glow. The shores are dotted with several shops and
eateries and stage cultural events in the peak tourist season.

Rimini is a fun-filled family holiday destination that has an eclectic array of options for perfectly charming day trips.
