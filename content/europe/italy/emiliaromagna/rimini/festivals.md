---
title: 'Festivals'
type: section
---

Rimini has festivals throughout the year. Whatever time you visit, something is likely to be going on.

**January-March.** New Year events last for several days and include opera performances. The SIGEP exhibition in January displays bakery goods and ice cream. In February, the Rimini Fiera hosts fashion trade shows. The Crossroads Jazz Festival opens and lasts until early summer. In March, **La Fogherraccia** marks the end of winter with bonfires across the city for the feast of Saint Joseph.

**April-June.** **Paganello**, held at Easter, is an international frisbee competition on the beach, with concerts and children's activities. In May, the **Balconi Fioriti** sees balconies across the city decorated with flowers. June brings **BluRimini** with regattas, races, and theatre shows. The **Gradisca** is the biggest summer festival -- about 100 days of parties, dancing, food, and fireworks along the beaches from Torre Pedrera to Miramare.

**July-September.** In July, the **Borgo San Giovanni** festival features crafts, artwork, music, and traditional food. **Ferragosto** in August celebrates the Assumption with concerts and partying. In September, the **Borgo San Giuliano** is Rimini's most famous traditional festival, reliving people and events from the city's past. The **Sagra Malatestiana** brings top classical musicians and conductors.

**October-December.** The **Mangiar Sano** in October is a two-day food festival. In November, the **Lucincitta** lights up the city's Christmas decorations. December brings Christmas fairs throughout Rimini.
