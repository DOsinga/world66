---
title: 'Museums'
type: section
---

**City Museum.** The main museum in Rimini, with 36 galleries showcasing the city's 2,000-year history through art and historical artefacts. Via Tonini 1, open Tuesday to Sunday. Generally 8:30-12:30 and 17:00-19:00, Sundays 10:00-12:30 and 15:00-19:00. Around 5 euros.

**Federico Fellini Museum.** The famous Italian director was born in Rimini and set some of his films here. The museum covers all parts of his life and career. Via Clementini 2, free entry. Open 16:00-19:00 Tuesday to Friday, 10:00-12:00 and 16:00-19:00 weekends.

**Gambalunga Library.** Built between 1610 and 1614, this was the first public library in Italy. It contains over 200,000 books including rare manuscripts and periodicals from as early as the 1600s. Also houses a children's library and hosts exhibitions. Via Gambalunga 27, open Monday to Friday 8:00-19:00, Saturday 8:00-13:00. Free entry.
