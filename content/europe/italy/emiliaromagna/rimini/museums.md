---
title: "Museums in Rimini"
type: section
---

Rimini is located on the Adriatic Sea in Italy and is the most
famous of the seaside resorts on the Adriatic Riveria. It has a long
history and much of this can be viewed in the many museums in the city.
The following provides details of some of the best of these.

**City Museum**

This is the main museum in Rimini and has many displays showcasing
the interesting 2000 year history of the city. There are 36 different
galleries which house many works of art and historical artifacts which
are on view to illustrate the history and cultural development of
Rimini and the surrounding area. The museum is located on the Via
Tonini 1 and is open Tuesday to Sunday. Opening hours are generally
8.30-12.30 and 17-00-1900, although on Sunday these change to
10.00-12.30 and 15.00-19.00. Ticket prices are around Euro 5.

**Federico Fellini Museum**

Fellini was a famous Italian movie director who was born in Rimini
and also set some of his movies in the city. The museum has displays
and exhibits covering all parts of his life and career from his early
days to when he was creating some of his most memorable films. The
museum is located on Via Clementini 2 with entry being free. It is open
in the evening from 16.00-19.00 Tuesday to Friday and 10.00-12.00 and
16.00-19.00 on Saturdays and Sundays.

**Gambalunga Library**

This library is located on Via Gambalunga 27 and the building which
house it was built between 1610 and 1614. It contains over 200,000
books including rare manuscripts and periodicals from as early as the
1600’s. As well as the impressive collection of books the building also
houses a special children’s library, the cineteca and also puts on many
exhibitions. The Library is open Monday to Friday 08.00-19.00 and
Saturday 8.00-13.00 with entry being free. Rimini is a beautiful
location and the city has many fine museums. The ones mentioned above
will provide a visitor with the chance to view some of the culture and
history of the city.
