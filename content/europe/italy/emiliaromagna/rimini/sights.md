---
title: 'Sights'
type: section
---

**Ponte di Tiberio (Tiberius Bridge).** A well-preserved Roman bridge about 2,000 years old, built in Istrian stone. Crossing it on foot or by bicycle takes you straight into the past.

**Roman Amphitheatre.** Built in the second century to accommodate about 10,000 spectators, with more than fifty arcades. Concerts and classical music events are sometimes held here. An easy walk from the city centre.

**Tempio Malatestiano.** The church of San Francesco, the most important monument in Rimini. A Gothic structure that has undergone several restorations while preserving its rich original detailing. Classic Renaissance architecture at its finest.

**Porta Montanara (Porta Sant'Andrea).** A gate located near Piazza Tre Martiri at the end of a lively shopping street. It was used by traders and farmers coming from the hills of the Marecchia Valley to enter the market. Destroyed and rebuilt several times during wars, including heavy damage in the Second World War.

**Arco di Augusto (Augustus Arch).** One of the oldest surviving Roman arches, constructed in 27 BC and dedicated to Augustus. You can still see ornamental carvings of Apollo, Jupiter, Neptune, and Roma. In ancient times this was the main entrance to the city.
