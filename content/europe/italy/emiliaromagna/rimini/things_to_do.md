---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The historic centre rewards a half-day walk. The **Arch of Augustus** (27 BC) at the southern entrance to the old city is one of the best-preserved Roman triumphal arches in Italy. The **Tempio Malatestiano**, a 15th-century church wrapped in a Renaissance marble shell designed by Leon Battista Alberti, is the finest building in Rimini and contains works by Piero della Francesca. The **Bridge of Tiberius** (21 AD) still carries pedestrians and cyclists across the Marecchia. The **Museo della Città** on Via Tonini covers 2,000 years of local history. The **Federico Fellini Museum**, opened in 2021, celebrates the city's most famous son with archives, costumes, and film memorabilia. Beyond the old town, the 15 km of fine sandy **beach** is the main summer attraction, and the **Italia in Miniatura** theme park on the outskirts is an enjoyable family stop.
