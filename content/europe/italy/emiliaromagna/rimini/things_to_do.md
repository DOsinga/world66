---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The historic centre rewards a half-day walk. The Arch of Augustus (27 BC) at the southern entrance to the old city is one of the best-preserved Roman triumphal arches in Italy. The Tempio Malatestiano, a 15th-century church wrapped in a Renaissance marble shell designed by Alberti, is the finest building in Rimini and contains works by Piero della Francesca. The Bridge of Tiberius (21 AD) still carries pedestrians and cyclists across the Marecchia. The City Museum on Via Tonini covers 2,000 years of local history across 36 galleries. The Federico Fellini Museum celebrates the city's most famous son with archives, costumes, and film memorabilia.
