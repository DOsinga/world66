---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Visit the Gambalunga Library.** Built in 1610, this was the first public library in Italy and houses at least 200,000 books, including rare manuscripts and original editions from the sixteenth century. A must for any book lover.

**2. Stand in Piazza Tre Martiri.** According to local legend, this is where Julius Caesar made his speech to soldiers before crossing the Rubicon and marching on Rome. The piazza is right in the middle of town.

**3. Spend a day at the beach.** Rimini has over 15 km of beaches. In August, life exists only at the beach. Paid beaches offer children's activities and sun loungers; free beaches have lifeguards and facilities.

**4. Dance the night away at Carnaby.** Three floors of non-stop music and dancing. The style and volume depend on the floor. The Lounge on the third floor serves great cocktails at reasonable prices.

**5. Eat at Osteria de Borg.** Start with the summer starters -- nice cheeses and good crostini. For a main course, the beef fillet and medallions are always a good choice. Via Forzieri 12, evenings only.
