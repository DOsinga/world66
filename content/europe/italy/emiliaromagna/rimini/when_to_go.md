---
title: "When to Go in Rimini"
type: section
---

In **Rimini, when to go** can have a huge impact on the style of vacation that you'll have. If you're trying to decide on the best time to visit Rimini, here's a look at what you can expect in each season in order to help you out.

**Winter**

Lasting from December through February is Rimini's winter, a time period that is usually characterized by mild and humid weather. The skies are often gray and rain can be common, so don't forget your umbrella along with your coat and sweaters. Temperatures rarely hit freezing, although it has been known to happen, and more often than not will hover around 10 degrees C. If you're visiting in the winter, though, you'll love the opportunities to sample local foods. The Olive Oil [Festival](/europe/italy/emiliaromagna/rimini/festivals) is held every December and you can roam the street stalls tasting various recipes while having a mug of hot mulled wine. In February the Mediterranean Seafood Exposition is in full swing, and you'll love the local and international dishes being offered.

**Spring**

From March to May springtime hits Rimini, and "when to go to the beach" is at the forefront of everyone's mind. Indeed, by late spring the water is often warm enough for a swim. In the meantime, enjoy the sunny days that see temperatures rapidly warming to a pleasant 22 degrees C, on average. You can also had to the beach to watch the various sports tournaments. In March is Paganello, the annual frisbee competition, and in May you can watch the polo competition. It's also worth noting that if you want to hit the sand and sun without the crushing crowds, May is the time to visit.

**Summer**

Peak season in Rimini is the summertime, from June through August (although it does extend into September). Days are hot and dry, with minimal rainfall and bright blue skies. Temperatures average around 27 to 30 degrees C, occasionally falling lower or rising higher. It's the perfect time to hit the beach during the day and the [night clubs](/europe/italy/emiliaromagna/rimini/nightlife_and_ente) each night. If you're looking for a bit more culture, the International Jazz Festival takes place for 1 week in June. Also in June is the Ancient World Festival, during which time noted professionals from the worlds of archaeology, philosophy, arts and [history](/europe/italy/emiliaromagna/rimini/history) descend on Rimini to give lectures and presentations.

**Autumn**

Fall kicks in during September and stays through November. The beginning of the season is often warm and sunny, and is still popular with beach goers. As the season progresses, however, rain becomes more common and the temperatures start to dip. The weather is very changeable, so come prepared for all possibilities. Temperatures range between 5 and 25 degrees C. October is usually a good time to visit, as you can get better deals on [accommodations](/europe/italy/emiliaromagna/rimini/accommodation) after the crowds disperse. It's also the time for the Sagra Musicale Malatestiana, the annual symphonic festival. And don't forget to visit the Cheese and Truffles Fair if you're in Rimini in November.
