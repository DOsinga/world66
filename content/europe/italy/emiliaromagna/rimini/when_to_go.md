---
title: 'When to Go'
type: section
---

**Winter (December-February).** Mild and humid with often grey skies -- bring an umbrella along with your coat. Temperatures rarely hit freezing, hovering around 10C. The Olive Oil Festival in December features street stalls with recipes and hot mulled wine. In February, the Mediterranean Seafood Exposition offers local and international dishes.

**Spring (March-May).** Sunny days with temperatures warming to a pleasant 22C. By late spring the water is warm enough for swimming. March brings Paganello, the annual frisbee competition on the beach, and May has a polo competition. If you want sand and sun without the crushing crowds, May is the time to come.

**Summer (June-August).** Peak season. Days are hot and dry, with temperatures around 27-30C and bright blue skies. Perfect for the beach by day and the clubs by night. The International Jazz Festival takes place for one week in June, and the Ancient World Festival brings archaeologists, philosophers, and historians to Rimini for lectures and presentations.

**Autumn (September-November).** The start of the season is often warm and still popular with beachgoers. As it progresses, rain becomes more common and temperatures dip. October is a good time for better deals on accommodation after the crowds disperse. The Sagra Musicale Malatestiana brings symphonic performances, and the Cheese and Truffles Fair runs in November.
