This **Sorrento Travel Guide** will give a history of the area, popular activities and sights, in addition to useful vacation information. Sorrento, Campania is a popular tourist spot located in southern Italy. It's situated just above the Bay of Naples on a high cliff-like ridge, and has phenomenal views of Mount Vesuvius, the Island of Capri and the city of Naples.

The Mediterranean climate in southern Italy is fairly mild with drier summers. In fall and winter months, the area experiences more rainfall but the climate remains temperate allowing locals and tourists to still go out and enjoy Sorrento. The high season of tourism is from May to September, when the temperatures are hottest, and the marine wildlife is most abundant. August is also a time during summer when locals will go on Holiday, meaning some shops and restaurants will close for at least a part of the month. Don't worry though, not all of Sorrento will shut down during this time in summer.

The city of Sorrento does not offer much of a beach area. The coastline features more rocks on the beach instead of sand, but you can travel to nearby Pompeii, Capri or even Naples if you want to visit the beach and participate in water sports or activities there. In the city you can go on walking tours along ancient paths, view the white stone walls of some of the older city buildings, and then explore the lemon, orange and terraced olive groves. Embark on a boat ride and view the beautiful grottos along the coastline, and come face to face with various mammals of the sea as well.

Sorrento has a great deal of history to learn also. In the old city center, you'll find remains of statues, majestic churches and other buildings with magnificent architecture. Many different civilizations came through the city of Sorrento over hundreds of years including the Etruscans, the Oscans, the Romans, the Normans, the Aragonese and probably the most influential, the Greeks. Ancient Greeks gave Sorrento the city layout that is still used today in the historical city center.

If you're looking to stay in Sorrento when visiting, there are several different kinds of accommodations available to you. Choose from nicer hotels, cozy hostels, private apartments and guesthouses, homey bed and breakfasts, rooms for rent, camping and village amenities, or even the opportunity to stay on a local farm. The accommodation prices will vary from small budget to lavish five-star hotel prices.
