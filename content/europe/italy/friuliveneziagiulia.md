Friuli-Venezia Giulia is often skipped by tourists. It does not receive half as many people as nearby [Veneto](/europe/italy/veneto). This means that Friuli offers you a chance to discover a truly interesting region without too many other tourists around.


 Friuli is close to Austria, Slovenia and Croatia and, you can sense Austrian and even Slavic influences. [Trieste](/europe/italy/friuliveneziagiulia/trieste), the main tourist destination of the region, was Austrian for a long time and Italy only incorporated it in 1949. Trieste was also the place where James Joyce taught his English Lessons, among others to Italian writer Italo Svevo (Ettore Schmidt).


 Other interesting places to visit include the capital [Udine](/europe/italy/friuliveneziagiulia/udine) - it's not on most tourist itineraries in Italy, but it's a captivating town with graceful Renaissance architecture and many interesting works of roccoco pioneer Tiepolo. Udine also offers good onward railroad connections to Zagreb and [Austria](/europe/austria) Austria.

The region has a number of good beach resorts as well. [Grado](/europe/italy/friuliveneziagiulia/grado) and Lignano probably offer you best value for money of all Italian beach resorts on the Adriatic coast.
