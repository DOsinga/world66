A classic Triestine *bacaro* near the Canal Grande, serving *cicheti* (small plates) and local wine by the glass. The format is straightforward: stand at the bar, point at what looks good, order a Malvasia or a Vitovska, and repeat. Typical plates include sardele in saor, nervetti, and polpette.

One of those places where the menu changes daily depending on what came in. Cash only, arrive hungry, and don't expect to linger — the bar is narrow and the pace is brisk.
