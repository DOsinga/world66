A modern osteria that takes its name seriously — *radici* means roots — focusing on Friulian and Carso ingredients treated with care. The cooking is unfussy: good bread, honest antipasti, pasta made in-house. The wine list is almost entirely natural wines from local small producers, which makes it a good option alongside La Muta if you're spending a few days in the city.

Tables are small and closely spaced; the room gets loud on busy evenings. Reservations recommended for dinner at the weekend.
