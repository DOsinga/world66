A small, warmly decorated restaurant in the centre that takes the Triestine tradition of mixing Austro-Hungarian and Italian cooking seriously. The menu changes with the season and the fish market — expect dishes like *jota* (bean and sauerkraut soup), grilled Adriatic fish, and house pasta with Carso truffles in autumn.

Booking is advisable for dinner. The lunch menu is shorter and better value; the €14 two-course set menu is one of the better deals in the city centre.
