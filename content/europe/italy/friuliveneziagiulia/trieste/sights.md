title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Trieste rewards slow exploration. The city's layered Habsburg, Roman, and Italian identity is written into its architecture — from the monumental Piazza Unità d'Italia facing the sea, to the Roman theatre tucked behind the cathedral hill, to the ornate 19th-century coffee houses that line its streets. For literary travellers, Trieste holds a special significance: James Joyce lived here for much of the decade between 1904 and 1915, teaching English and writing, and traces of his presence remain throughout the city.
