A short canal cut into the city in the 18th century to allow trading ships to unload directly in the centre. Today it is one of Trieste's most photographed spots — the water reflects the grand facades on either side, and the Serbian Orthodox church of Sant'Antonio Nuovo closes the far end in a distinctly un-Italian composition. The James Joyce statue stands on the Ponte Rosso bridge over the canal.

Wander along the canal in the early morning before the café terraces fill up; the light and the reflections are at their best then.
