The cathedral on the San Giusto hill is actually two Romanesque basilicas merged into a single church in the 14th century — you can still see the join in the nave. The interior contains some of the finest Byzantine mosaics in Italy, dating from the 11th and 12th centuries, glittering in gold and deep blue above the apses.

The cathedral shares the hilltop with the Castello di San Giusto and a Roman temple foundation, making this compact promontory the most historically layered spot in the city. Allow around 45 minutes for both the cathedral and castle combined.
