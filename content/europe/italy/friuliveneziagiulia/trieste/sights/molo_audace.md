A 246-metre stone pier jutting out into the Gulf of Trieste, originally built by the Austrians in the early 19th century and named after the Italian destroyer *Audace*, the first Italian vessel to dock here when Italy took the city in 1918. It is now a beloved promenade — locals come here at all hours to walk, sit on the edge, fish, and watch the sun set over the water.

Come for sunset. The light over the gulf is exceptional, and on clear evenings you can see the Julian Alps across the bay. Bring a light jacket — the *bora* wind can arrive without warning.
