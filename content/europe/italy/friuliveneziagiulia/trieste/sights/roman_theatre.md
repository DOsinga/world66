A remarkably well-preserved 1st-century Roman theatre built into the hillside below the San Giusto promontory. It is one of the largest Roman remains in northern Italy and can seat an estimated 6,000 spectators. The best view is from Via del Teatro Romano, where you can look down into the semicircular cavea without paying any entry fee.

The theatre is a reminder that Trieste — then called *Tergeste* — was a significant Roman port city long before the Habsburgs arrived. It is still occasionally used for open-air performances in summer.
