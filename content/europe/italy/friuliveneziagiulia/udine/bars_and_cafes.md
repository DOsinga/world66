---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Udine"
type: section
---

Udine, a beautiful city in
the Friuli region of Italy,
is a quiet place to spend a few days strolling through wide cobblestoned
streets, each packed with quaint cafes, bars, and gift shops. This small city has yet to be affected by
mass tourism, and thus the cafés and bars, all moderately priced, are still run
by local chefs and feature locally-grown food. The gelato stands will be most
crowded in the summers, but still worth a visit to cool off. Be mindful that
not all of these restaurants take credit cards and some are closed during
siesta hours, but Udine
has plenty of ATMs.

**I Poimbi**

Address: Via Manin, 12, Udine

Phone: 0432506168

Serving mostly beers and sandwiches, I Poimbi is perfect for
the hungry hiker wanting a quick snack. The daily sandwich menu varies, but
always feature a chicken, beef, and vegetarian option. Pair up your lunch with
a local beer on tap. Service can be a little slow, but on the whole reviewers
have enjoyed Udine’s
best hole-in-the-wall. Closed Mondays.

**Osteria Al Vecchio Stallo **

Address: Via Viola 7, Udine

Phone: 0432/21296

Osteria Al Vecchio Stallo, a moderately-priced traditional
Italian restaurant located in the center of Udine, features a variety of delicious
options, including raviolis baked with spinach, fruits, and cheese and
sprinkled with cinnamon. Unorthodox diners
might like to try a plate of cow’s knee, something you won’t find anywhere else
in the country. Order wine by the glass
or the bottle from the local vinery, and don’t miss out on the mouth-watering
desserts and custards. Be sure and stop by the ATM on your way here; Osteria
doesn’t accept credit cards. Closed Wed.

**Bar Caucigh**

Address: Via Gemona, 36, Udine

Phone: 0432502719

Featuring some of the city’s best jazz music, Bar Caucigh is
the ideal place to unwind after a long walk and dinner. Free admission every
Friday, this bar serves Illy coffee (Italy’s best), foamy beer, wine
from all over the region, and a fully-stocked bar. For those who still have
room in their bellies, check out the simple yet tasty dessert menu. The food is
appetizing, but most tourists come here for the jazz music and local beer.

**Agli Amici Trattoria**

Via Liguria, 250, 33100 Udine

Tel: 0432565411

Closed for dinner Sunday and Mondays, Agli Amici Trattoria
is still worth a visit any other day of the week. Agli is known for its taps
(small dishes) featuring a variety of meats, locally grown vegetables, and eggs.
You can also find vegetarian tapas and dessert tapas, and a good selection of
wine. Although this café is very romantic and the food is tasty, Agli is not
for the budget diner. A full dinner can cost as much as $80 per person.

**Gelateria dell'Orso**

Address: Viale Giacomo Leopardi, 128 Udine

Phone: 0432502477

Gelateria dell’ Orso, one of Udine’s best ranked ice cream
stands, features a variety of gelato flavors, including pineapple and hazelnut,
for a cheap price. With its quick service, cheap prices, and open late in the summers,
Gelateria dell’ Orso is the perfect dessert stop after a night stroll through
the idyllic streets of Udine.
