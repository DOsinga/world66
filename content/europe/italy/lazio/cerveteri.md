Cerveteri, ancient Caere, is one of the most important Etruscan sites in Italy and well worth the short detour from [Rome](/europe/italy/lazio/rome) or [Tarquinia](/europe/italy/lazio/tarquinia). The Necropoli della Banditaccia — a vast city of the dead — is where most of the Etruscan objects now filling the museums of Rome and beyond were excavated.

The necropolis is unlike any other ancient site in Italy. It was planned on the same principles used by the Etruscans for their cities of the living: laid out in streets, with tomb-houses that have doorways, windows, and rooms carved from the tufa rock. Walking through it with a torch is an extraordinary experience — the chambers are cool and quiet, the carvings remarkably intact. Several hours can easily pass. The site covers about three kilometres and involves a fair amount of climbing.

Bring a good torch. The site has no artificial lighting inside the tombs.
