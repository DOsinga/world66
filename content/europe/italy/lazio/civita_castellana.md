Civita Castellana is a small town in northern [Lazio](/europe/italy/lazio), set on a tufa plateau above a deep gorge carved by the Rio Maggiore. It lies in the heart of the ancient Etruscan territory — the nearby Faliscan settlements date back to the 8th century BC — and has maintained a tradition of ceramic arts for centuries.

The town's centrepiece is the Forte Sangallo, a pentagonal Renaissance fortress designed by Antonio da Sangallo the Elder for Pope Alexander VI. Inside is the Museo Archeologico dell'Agro Falisco, with an excellent collection of Faliscan pottery. The Romanesque cathedral, with its 12th-century portico by the Cosmati family, is also worth a look.

From here the Etruscan sites of [Cerveteri](/europe/italy/lazio/cerveteri) and [Ostia Antica](/europe/italy/lazio/ostiaantica) are easily reachable, as are [Viterbo](/europe/italy/lazio/viterbo) and [Rome](/europe/italy/lazio/rome).
