Latina is a planned city about 60 kilometres south of [Rome](/europe/italy/lazio/rome), built in 1932 during the draining of the Pontine Marshes. The rationalist architecture of the city centre — all clean lines and wide piazzas — gives it a distinctive look quite unlike the older towns around it.

The city itself is not a major tourist draw, but it sits close to some excellent coastline. The beaches at Sabaudia and Sperlonga are among the best in Lazio, and the Circeo National Park, with its forested promontory and coastal lakes, is a worthwhile day trip. Latina also makes a practical base for exploring the southern Lazio coast without Rome's prices or crowds.
