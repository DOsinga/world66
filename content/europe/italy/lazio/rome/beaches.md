---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Rome is 30km from the coast, and the Lazio shoreline is easily reachable by train or metro. The beaches are sandy and flat, ranging from packed public stretches to private stabilimenti (lido clubs) where you pay for a sun lounger, umbrella, and facilities.

**Ostia (Lido di Roma)** is the closest beach, 35 minutes from Termini by train (Roma-Lido line from Porta San Paolo station). It's the busiest and most urban, with a mix of free and private beaches. The free public beach at Castel Porziano, amid sand dunes, is better for serious sunbathing.

**Anzio**, an hour south by train, is a quieter port town with a small beach flanked by Roman ruins. Good seafood restaurants along the harbour. Made historically famous as an Allied landing point in 1944.

**Nettuno**, just past Anzio, is a well-preserved medieval town with cleaner and less crowded beaches than Ostia.
