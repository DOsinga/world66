Villa Borghese is Rome's largest public park, a 148-acre green oasis with fountains, lakes, temples, and museums. The Bioparco zoo sits at its center and makes a good break from ruin-hopping, especially with children. The Galleria Borghese within the park houses a world-class sculpture collection including works by Bernini and Canova -- booking is mandatory and fills up weeks ahead.
