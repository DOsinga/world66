Set in Rome from 1941 to 1947, this is one of the great novels of World War II — the story of a widowed schoolteacher and her young son surviving German occupation, partisan violence, and the chaos of liberation. Morante renders the Testaccio and Tiburtino neighbourhoods with documentary precision. Reading it makes the Quadraro and Trastevere walk differently.
