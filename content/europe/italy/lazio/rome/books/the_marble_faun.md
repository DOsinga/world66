Four artists living in Rome — Americans and an Italian — become entangled in a mysterious crime. Hawthorne wrote it after living in Rome in 1858 and the novel doubles as a sustained meditation on the city's weight of history and beauty. Dense in places, but the descriptions of the Capitoline and the Protestant Cemetery are remarkable.
