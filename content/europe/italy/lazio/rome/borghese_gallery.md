One of Rome's finest museums and one of its hardest to get into — timed entry is mandatory and slots sell out weeks ahead. The collection was assembled by Cardinal Scipione Borghese in the early 17th century and includes the greatest concentration of Bernini sculptures anywhere: Pluto and Persephone, Apollo and Daphne, and David, all carved when Bernini was in his twenties. Canova's reclining Venus (modelled on Napoleon's sister) and Caravaggio paintings round out the ground floor. The picture gallery upstairs has Raphael, Titian, and Cranach.

Book well in advance on the gallery's website. The two-hour limit is strictly enforced but is usually enough for the ground floor highlights.
