A thorough guide to Testaccio — the old slaughterhouse district south of the Aventine that remains the true heartland of traditional Roman cucina. The Roman Guy covers the historic trattorias that have been feeding this working-class neighbourhood for over a century, the Testaccio Market (Rome's best food market), and the street food stalls that draw Romans from across the city. Good for understanding why quinto quarto (offal cookery) isn't just historical curiosity but still the thing to order here. Includes practical advice on what's tourist-facing and what isn't.
