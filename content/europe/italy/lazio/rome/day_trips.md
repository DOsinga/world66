---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Several of the best day trips from Rome are within an hour by train or bus.

**Tivoli** (45min by bus from Tiburtina) has two UNESCO-listed sites: Hadrian's Villa, a vast 2nd-century imperial complex, and Villa d'Este, famous for its extravagant Renaissance gardens and hundreds of fountains.

**Frascati** (45min by train from Termini via Ciampino) is a hilltop town in the Castelli Romani, 13 miles from Rome at 1,280 feet above sea level. Known for its crisp white wine, 16th-century villas (Villa Aldobrandini, Villa Torlonia), and good trattorias.

**Ostia Antica** (30min by train from Porta San Paolo) is the remarkably preserved port city of ancient Rome — better than Pompeii for accessibility, with intact streets, theatres, baths, and apartment blocks spanning several centuries.

**Tarquinia** (1h 40min by train and bus from Termini) holds the best Etruscan site in central Italy: a necropolis of painted tombs and the Tarquinia National Museum, which has one of the finest Etruscan collections anywhere.
