Hilltop town in the Castelli Romani, 21km southeast of Rome at 450m above sea level. Famous for its dry white wine (Frascati DOC), its 16th-century villas, and views over the Roman Campagna. Villa Aldobrandini, with its gardens, grottoes, and fountains, is the main architectural landmark. Villa Torlonia's garden and Theater of Fountains are also worth seeing.

Frequent trains from Termini via Ciampino (45min, €2.10). Also reachable by Cotral bus from the Anagnina Metro A stop.
