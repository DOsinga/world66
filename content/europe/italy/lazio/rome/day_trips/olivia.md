The finest Etruscan site in central Italy. The Tarquinia National Museum, in a Gothic palace from the 1450s, holds an excellent collection of Etruscan artefacts including jewellery, decorated sarcophagi, and the famous terracotta Winged Horses — one of the masterpieces of Etruscan art. The painted tombs of the Etruscan Necropolis are the real draw: vivid frescoes of banquets, musicians, and athletes, some dating to the 7th century BC.

Eight buses daily from Via Lepanto in Rome (2hr), or by train from Roma Ostiense to Civitavecchia and then a connecting bus (around 90min total).
