The ancient port city of Rome, 30km from the city centre. Better preserved than Pompeii in some ways and far less crowded. The site covers several square kilometres of intact streets, warehouses, baths, an amphitheatre, apartment blocks (insulae), and temples spanning the 2nd century BC to the 4th century AD. The mosaic floors in the guild offices (Piazzale delle Corporazioni) are outstanding.

Take the Roma-Lido train from Porta San Paolo station (Metro B, Piramide stop) to Ostia Antica — around 35 minutes. Allow three to four hours for the site.
