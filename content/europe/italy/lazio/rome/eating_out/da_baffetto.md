One of Rome's legendary pizza joints, near Piazza Navona. A classic no-frills Roman trattoria — simple tables, cash only, perpetual queue. The pizzas are thin and good, especially the one with courgette flowers. Prices are low and the atmosphere is genuinely Roman. The owner, nicknamed Baffetto (little moustache), has walls covered in photos of Italian celebrities who've eaten here. Closed Mondays.
