---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

**On foot**

The historic centre is compact and the best way to see it is walking. Most of the major sights between the Pantheon, Piazza Navona, Campo de' Fiori, Trastevere, and the Forum are within 30 minutes of each other on foot.

**By metro**

The Metro has two main lines: **A** (orange) and **B** (blue), intersecting at Termini. Line A serves most tourist-relevant stops: Spagna (Spanish Steps), Barberini (Trevi Fountain), and Ottaviano (Vatican). The metro misses much of the ancient centre because excavation keeps hitting archaeological remains. A single ticket costs €1.50 and is valid for 100 minutes on buses too. Validate your ticket before boarding. Running approx 5:30am–11:30pm (until 12:30am Saturday).

**By bus**

ATAC buses cover the whole city, including areas the metro misses. Routes 40 and 64 run from Termini through the historic centre to the Vatican. Timetables and route maps at atac.roma.it. Night bus routes (labelled N) run after midnight.

**By taxi**

Official taxis are white with a TAXI sign. Only take licensed metered cabs — the flat rates from airports are fixed (Fiumicino €48, Ciampino €30), but in the city the meter applies. Don't accept rides from touts at the airports or Termini. Phone bookings are available through Radio Taxi (tel. 06 3570 or 06 4994).

**By car**

Don't drive in Rome if you can avoid it. The ZTL (limited traffic zones) cover most of the centro storico and are enforced by cameras. Parking is scarce and expensive.
