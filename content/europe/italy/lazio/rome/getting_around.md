---
title: 'Getting Around'
type: section
---

Rome rewards walking above all else -- the historic center is compact and full of surprises around every corner. The metro has three lines but misses most of the old city because the ground is too full of archaeology to tunnel through. Buses run by ATAC fill the gaps, operating roughly 6am to midnight with some night services. Buy tickets before boarding from tobacconists or newsstands, and validate them on board or face a hefty fine. Taxis are plentiful and metered, but driving yourself is not recommended unless you enjoy chaos, creative parking, and the constant risk of traffic violations.
