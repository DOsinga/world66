---
title: 'Getting There'
type: section
---

Most international visitors arrive at Fiumicino airport (Leonardo da Vinci), 26 km southwest of the city. The Leonardo Express train runs every 15 minutes to Termini station and takes about 30 minutes. Budget airlines use Ciampino airport, 15 km southeast, from which buses connect to the metro. Rome's main train station, Termini, sits on the edge of the historic center with high-speed rail links to Florence (1.5 hours), Naples (1 hour), and Milan (3 hours). Four motorways feed into the city's ring road for those arriving by car.
