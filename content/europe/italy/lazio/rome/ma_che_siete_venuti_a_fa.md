Named after a Roman football chant, this tiny Trastevere bar has one of the widest selections of craft draft beer in the city — Italian microbreweries alongside Belgian and German classics. Standing room only on busy evenings, spilling onto the cobblestones. A counterpoint to the wine-everywhere culture of Rome, and a reminder that Italian craft beer has become genuinely interesting over the last decade. Elizabeth Minchilli brings friends here; Katie Parla lists it as a reliable stop.
