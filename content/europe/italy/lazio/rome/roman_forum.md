The Forum Romanum was the civic heart of ancient Rome -- a sprawling complex of temples, basilicas, and arches wedged between the Capitoline Hill, the Colosseum, and the Palatine. Here you will find the Arch of Titus, the Basilica of Maxentius, and the Temple of Saturn, among many others. What began as a marketplace grew into an increasingly grand civic center as successive rulers donated buildings to cement their legacy. Covered by the combined Colosseum ticket.
