According to tradition, this is the staircase Christ climbed to face Pontius Pilate, brought to Rome from Jerusalem by Helena, mother of Emperor Constantine. The marble steps are protected by wooden coverings and the faithful ascend on their knees. At the top lies the Sancta Sanctorum, the pope's former private chapel, which houses the most sacred relics including a portrait of Christ attributed to St. Luke.
