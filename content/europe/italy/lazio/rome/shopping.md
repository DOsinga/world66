---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Rome's main shopping streets run through the historic centre and are broadly divided by budget. **Via Condotti** and the streets radiating from it (Via Borgognona, Via Frattina) are Rome's luxury corridor — Gucci, Fendi, Valentino, Bulgari. **Via del Corso** is the high-street alternative: Zara, H&M, and Italian mid-range chains. **Via Cola di Rienzo** near the Vatican has more practical everyday shops at lower prices.

The best market is **Porta Portese**, a vast Sunday flea market in Trastevere (Via Portuense, early morning until 2pm). Hundreds of vendors sell antiques, vintage clothing, books, records, and plenty of junk. Arrive early for the best finds and watch your pockets.

For food shopping, the market at **Campo de' Fiori** (Mon–Sat mornings) sells good produce, though it's touristy. The covered **Mercato Testaccio** (closed Sundays) is more local and has excellent charcuterie, cheese, and street food stalls.

Shopping hours are generally 10am–1pm and 3:30–7:30pm, Tuesday–Saturday. Mondays some shops open in the afternoon only.
