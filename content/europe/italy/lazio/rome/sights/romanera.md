The Roman Empire lasted about five centuries, so it’s no wonder this period made strong impression on the capital or “The City” of this realm. The remains from this era were never totally forgotten, not even in the medieval period. Just the way one dealt with this huge heritage was different in different times. In the Middle Ages Rome could not upkeep the vast city build by the Romans, so many buildings deteriorated and became covered with earth and dirt, but some, like the Pantheon, the Collosseum and the churches build by the Christian emperor Constantine kept being used one way or the other up till present day.

The reevaluation of the Roman historical buildings began with the visits of the Florentine architect and sculptor Filippo Brunelleschi (1377-1446) around the beginning of the 15th century. In a way he started the unveiling of the monuments that can be seen in present day Rome and which is still taking place on numerous different sites.
