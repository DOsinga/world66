Housed in the 16th-century Palazzo Spada, this gallery features Borromini's famous forced-perspective colonnade, which makes a short corridor appear to stretch into the distance. The collection includes works by Guido Reni, Annibale Carracci, and Jan Brueghel the Elder. It sees far fewer visitors than the major museums, making for a pleasantly unhurried visit.
