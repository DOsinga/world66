---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Rome has more significant architecture and art than can reasonably be absorbed in a single trip. The concentration of ancient monuments, Renaissance churches, and baroque squares is unmatched anywhere in the world. The challenge is managing the crowds and deciding what to prioritise.

The essential sites — the Colosseum, Roman Forum, Pantheon, Vatican Museums, and Sistine Chapel — deserve their reputations and should not be skipped. Book tickets for all of them online in advance; the queues without reservations can add two hours to your morning. The Borghese Gallery is one of the finest collections in Italy and requires advance booking by design.

Beyond the landmarks, Rome rewards wandering. The historic centre is compact enough to cover on foot; getting lost between Piazza Navona and Campo de' Fiori, or along the streets of Trastevere, turns up baroque churches, Renaissance courtyards, and hidden piazzas that aren't in the guidebooks. Avoid the overheated centre in July and August if you can — spring and autumn are far more comfortable.
