Built by Emperor Hadrian as a mausoleum in 139 AD and later converted into a fortress, prison, and papal refuge. The popes had a fortified passage — the Passetto di Borgo — connecting the Vatican to the castle for emergencies. Clement VII used it to escape the Sack of Rome in 1527. The rooftop terrace gives outstanding views over the Tiber, St. Peter's, and the city. The Ponte Sant'Angelo leading to it, lined with ten angels carved to Bernini's designs, is one of Rome's finest approaches.
