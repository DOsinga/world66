One of Rome's most atmospheric museums: 450 Greek and Roman sculptures from the Capitoline Museums exhibited in a former early-20th-century power plant. The contrast between the marble gods and the massive industrial turbines makes for one of the city's most memorable displays. Far from the tourist centre in the Ostiense neighbourhood, it sees fewer visitors than it deserves. Worth combining with a walk through the area, which has good street food and a young local crowd.
