Rome's showiest baroque square, built on the foundations of Domitian's stadium — the elongated shape betrays its origins. Bernini's Fountain of the Four Rivers dominates the centre, the four figures representing the Nile, Ganges, Danube, and Río de la Plata. The church of Sant'Agnese in Agone on the western side is a Borromini design, and the story that the two architects' works face each other in deliberate rivalry is probably apocryphal but entertaining.

The square is touristy and the cafes charge accordingly, but it's worth sitting on the fountain steps at dusk when the light is good. In December it fills with a Christmas market.
