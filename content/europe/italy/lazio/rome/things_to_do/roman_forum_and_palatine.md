The Roman Forum was the beating heart of the ancient city — the marketplace, legal centre, and stage for public life. What remains are columns, arches, and temple platforms spread over a dramatic open-air site. The Arch of Titus, the Temple of Saturn, and the Basilica of Maxentius are the standout pieces. The Palatine Hill above it is where Rome was legendarily founded; later emperors built their palaces here, and the ruins run across the whole summit with views over the Forum and Circus Maximus below.

The combined Colosseum ticket is valid for two days, which is practical — the Forum and Palatine together take a good two hours.
