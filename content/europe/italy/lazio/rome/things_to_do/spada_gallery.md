Housed in the 16th-century Palazzo Spada, this gallery features Borromini's famous forced-perspective colonnade, which makes a short corridor appear to stretch into the distance. The collection includes works by Guido Reni, Annibale Carracci, and Jan Brueghel the Elder. A hidden gem that sees far fewer visitors than the major museums.
