Time Out's curated list of Rome's best restaurants, with a decent spread across neighbourhoods and price ranges. Strong on the classics — the Testaccio trattorias, the thin-crust Roman pizzerias — but also includes some of the newer wave of cooking that has made Rome more interesting over the last decade. Not as deep as Katie Parla or Elizabeth Minchilli but good for a quick orientation. The descriptions are honest about atmosphere and who each restaurant is really for.
