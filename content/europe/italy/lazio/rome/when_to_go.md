---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**Spring (March–May)** is the best time to visit: mild temperatures (15–22°C), long days, and the city at its most photogenic before summer crowds arrive. Easter week is extremely busy and expensive.

**Summer (June–August)** is hot and humid, regularly above 30°C, and the city is packed with tourists. Many Romans leave in August, which means some restaurants close for a few weeks but also that the city is quieter in places. Evening temperatures are more comfortable. The outdoor cinema and concert season runs through summer.

**Autumn (September–November)** is another good window. September into October offers warm weather without summer's intensity. Rain increases in November, but crowds and prices drop.

**Winter (December–February)** is the least visited season. Temperatures typically 5–12°C with occasional cold snaps. Christmas brings extra visitors and decorations, particularly around the Vatican. Prices are lower outside Christmas week, and the major sights have shorter queues.
