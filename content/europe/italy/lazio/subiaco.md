Subiaco is a small town about 70 km east of [Rome](/europe/italy/lazio/rome), tucked into a valley of the Simbruini Mountains. It has been a favourite retreat for more than two millennia: Nero built himself a villa here after the fire of Rome, and Saint Benedict came here to live as a hermit in the sixth century, founding the monastic tradition that would shape medieval Europe.

The main attractions are the two monasteries just outside town. The Convento di Santa Scolastica is the older — a working Benedictine monastery with three beautiful cloisters, one Romanesque, one Gothic, one Renaissance. Further up the hill, the Sacro Speco (Holy Cave) is a monastery complex built dramatically into the cliff face around the grotto where Saint Benedict lived. Its medieval frescoes are extraordinary.

In town, the Rocca Abbaziale — the fortress of the Borgia family — crowns the highest point of the medieval centre, and the church of San Francesco is worth a visit. You will notice that Nero's villa is missing from the list: its columns and marble were long ago scattered across all the other buildings in town.
