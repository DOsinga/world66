Terracina is a coastal town in southern Lazio, halfway between Rome and Naples along the old Via Appia. It splits into two parts: a modern seaside town along the beach, and the atmospheric upper town (centro storico) built over ancient Roman ruins on the hillside above.

The highlight is the Temple of Jupiter Anxur on Monte Sant'Angelo, above the old town. The terrace of the ruined temple has sweeping views over the Tyrrhenian Sea and the Pontine Plain — on a clear day you can see the islands of Ponza. The Roman forum, partially excavated beneath the main piazza of the old town, is also worth a look.

The beach below is long and sandy, popular with Roman day-trippers in summer. Terracina is less touristy than the Amalfi Coast and makes a pleasant stop on the coastal route between Rome and Naples.
