The Villa d'Este is right in the centre of Tivoli, about a fifteen-minute walk from the station. The palace itself is pleasant, with frescoed rooms and good views, but it is the gardens that steal the show. Terraced down a steep hillside, they contain hundreds of fountains fed by an ingenious gravity-powered water system — jets, cascades, pools and grottos tumbling from level to level. The descent involves many flights of steps, but even from the terrace at the top the view is extraordinary.
