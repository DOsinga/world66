Bocca di Magra is an unusually well-placed base. The **Cinque Terre** is a quick train or boat ride north. **Portovenere** and the **Gulf of the Poets**, including the little island of Palmaria, are just around the headland. The Roman port city of **Luni** — once the source of the marble used on most of Rome, now a set of ruins in the fields with a small but excellent museum and an amphitheatre — is ten minutes away by car. Further inland, the marble quarries of **Carrara** in the Apuan Alps can be visited on a half-day trip, and the pretty walled town of **Sarzana** is the obvious stop for lunch.
