Celle Ligure is a small beach resort on the Ligurian Riviera di Ponente, about 30km west of Genoa, tucked into the bay between Cape Celle and Cape Olmo. The bay faces almost due south, which gives it sheltered water and a long sandy beach — rare on this stretch of coast, where most towns make do with pebbles.

The town has two distinct parts: the old village, called Celle Ligure Borgo, climbs the hillside in the characteristic Ligurian fashion — narrow lanes, pastel-coloured houses, a church or two — while the seafront promenade below is all beach clubs, gelaterias, and summer restaurants. The hills behind the town are thick with Aleppo pines, and the coastal footpath that runs east toward Albisola offers good views back over the bay.

This is not a destination for sightseeing. Celle Ligure is a place to slow down: a week at the beach, morning espresso on the lungomare, fresh anchovies at lunch. The Genoese have been coming here for decades, and the town retains a modest, family-oriented character that the more famous resorts to the west — San Remo, Alassio — have long since traded away.
