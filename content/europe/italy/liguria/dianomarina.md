Diano Marina is a small seaside town on the Riviera di Ponente in western Liguria, between Imperia and Alassio. It has a long sandy beach, a mild microclimate, and the quiet, slightly old-fashioned atmosphere of a town that has been welcoming Italian holidaymakers for over a century without ever becoming flashy.

The Civic Museum (Museo Civico) in the 19th-century Villa Ardissone displays pre-Roman and Roman artefacts found in the area, offering a window into the region's ancient trading history. The nearby Olive Tree Museum in Imperia documents six thousand years of olive cultivation — a fitting subject for a stretch of coast where olive groves blanket every hillside.

Diano Marina is a pleasant base for exploring the quieter western Ligurian coast, with its coves, hill villages, and excellent local cooking built around olive oil, fresh fish, and pesto.
