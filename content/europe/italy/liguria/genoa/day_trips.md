Here are some **Genoa day trips** to enjoy near the beautiful city.

#### **Spend th*e *Day**** at Arenzano**

The town of Arenzano is about 20 minutes away from the capital city of Genoa, and it is a good place for a day trip. It is not difficult to navigate to this city, since there are public buses running there. Arenzano is a small town with a small population, but during the summer months it becomes a resort haven as Italians travel there for the holidays. The summer is marked by some of the largest festivals in the Genoa province, including the Summer Festival and the ever-famous Chiarivari. The pubs by the marine promenade are also outstanding, with cheap drinks and excellent ambiance.

#### **The Pearl of Liguria**

History buffs should not miss out on Quiliano, as this little town maintains some of the best-kept bits of Roman architecture and archaeological sites. The highlight is a still-functioning Roman bridge in Quazzola. Quiliano is referred to as the pearl of Liguria. This town is located about 45 kilometers from Genoa and takes less than a half hour to get there on the highway. After observing the Roman architecture and buildings in the town, drive to the outskirts. There is a sharp difference between the town area and the country area. The country consists of villages nestled between the mountain ranges of the Alps and the Apennines.
