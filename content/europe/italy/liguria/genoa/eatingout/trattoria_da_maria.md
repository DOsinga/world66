Known to locals as "la sucida" — a term of rough affection. This is *cucina casalinga* at its most honest: no menu, whatever Maria is cooking that day, prices that haven't changed much in decades. Expect shared tables, no-frills surroundings, and some of the best home-style Genoese food in the city. Get there early for lunch — it fills fast.
