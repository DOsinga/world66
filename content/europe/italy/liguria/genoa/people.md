---
title: "People"
type: section
---

Genoa has produced a disproportionate share of world-historical figures. Christopher Columbus was born here around 1451; the house associated with him still stands near Porta Soprana. Admiral Andrea Doria shaped the politics of 16th-century Europe. Niccolò Paganini — arguably the greatest violinist who ever lived — was born in the Pré neighbourhood. More recently: Eugenio Montale won the Nobel Prize for Literature; Fabrizio De André became Italy's most beloved singer-songwriter; and Renzo Piano, who redesigned the Porto Antico, remains one of the world's leading architects.
