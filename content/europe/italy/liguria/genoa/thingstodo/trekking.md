A beautiful natural countryside and some interesting places and mountains are sorrounding Genoa and crossed by some of the most important Italian walking trails.
 
 The High Way of the Ligurian Mountains is a trail
 going - the most part - all along the top of the mountains and where it is still possible to walk, in many places around Genoa with sea sight sceneries with enchanting dawns and breathtaking sunsets, places sometime hided, often green, always fascinating.


 The Columbus Itinerary or The Bread Path is the 
 path used to walk to [Genoa](/europe/italy/liguria/genoa) harbour by the ancestors of Christopher Columbus from the Fontanabuona Valley near the [Tigullio](/europe/italy/liguria/italianriviera/tigullio) coast, bringing food and the slate tables to and from the sea ships along the coast and the inland. Other itineraries followed in ancient times were along the coast to the inland villages and many of them have been rediscovered by the public authorities and restored for the occasion of the year 2000 the Jubilee year.


 
 All the itineraries are available by car and in many cases well serviced by nearby bus lines. Restaurants and shops to plan itineraries with the right equipment, food and preparation. The most part of these itineraries are also used for a mountain bike itinerary.
