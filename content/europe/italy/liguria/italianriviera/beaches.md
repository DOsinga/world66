---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The Ligurian coast is mostly pebbly rather than sandy, with the notable exception of Monterosso al Mare in the Cinque Terre and the long beach at Alassio. Most beaches are managed (stabilimenti balneari) — you pay to rent a sun lounger and parasol — but free stretches exist if you're willing to hunt them out. Water quality is generally excellent; the sea is calm and clear, especially in early summer before August crowds arrive.

Portofino's Paraggi bay has a beautiful crescent of pale sand, though it fills up fast. The small coves between the Cinque Terre villages are dramatic and rocky — fine for a swim but not for sunbathing. For a proper beach day, head to Alassio on the western Riviera or Sestri Levante to the east.
