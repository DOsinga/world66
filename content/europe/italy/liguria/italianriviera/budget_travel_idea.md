---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Italian Riviera"
type: section
---

Even though you might think that vacationing
in the Italian Riviera is only for wealthy tycoons and well-to-do
actors, there are several options for** Italian Riviera budget travel**.
You can make your dream Mediterranean vacation come true. Here are some
useful suggestions that will allow you to save money in the Italian
Riviera.
**Where to Start your Italian Riviera Budget Travel**

It is a good idea to start your Italian Riviera trip in Genova,
because this port city offers many transportation options. Genova also
offers several options for entertainment which are free of charge, such
as museum exhibitions and archeological site visits. This city is a
favorite of architecture fans, because it offers many good examples from
classic and new schools of architecture. It is a good idea to bring a
camera. The Old Harbour area has many affordable options for lunch and
dinner, and it is a great spot for people-watching.
**Saving Money with Hostelling International**

One of the most expensive parts of traveling in the Italian Riviera
is the cost of hotels. Having a membership card from Hostelling
International will not only give you access to the lowest rates at
hostels, but it will also provide you with discounts at many
restaurants, retailers and special attractions. The applications for
this card within Italy is limited only to locals and exchange students
with a valid student identification, so it is a good idea to apply for
one before your arrival to Italy. You can use your card at hostels in
Genova, Savona, Moconesi and Osiglia.
**Traveling around Italy on a Budget
**

Depending on your time availability, you will have different choices
available for transportation. If you're only in Italy for a couple of
days, it is a good idea to travel mostly by plane. If you use discount
airlines such as GermanWings and RyanAir, you will be able to travel
economically and to free up time to see more of the Italian Riviera.
Given the cheap prices of these popular routes, it is best to book your
flights at least a month in advance. If you got more time available,
travel by ferry, bus and train are other options. While they take
longer, they are very affordable.
