---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Ligurian cooking is one of Italy's most distinctive regional cuisines — herb-heavy, vegetable-forward, and tied closely to the sea. The signature dish is pesto Genovese: basil ground with pine nuts, garlic, Parmigiano, Pecorino, and good olive oil. Eat it on trofie (short twisted pasta) or trenette. Focaccia is everywhere and universally good; the best version is focaccia di Recco, a thin, crispy flatbread stuffed with soft stracchino cheese.

Seafood is local and unpretentious — anchovies from Monterosso, fried bianchetti (tiny whitebait), and ciuppin (a Ligurian fish stew). Look for restaurants near the fishing harbours rather than the main piazzas for the freshest catches and better prices. The local wines — Vermentino, Pigato, and the rare Sciacchetrà dessert wine from Cinque Terre — are worth trying at least once.
