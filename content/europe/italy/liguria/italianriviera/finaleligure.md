Finale Ligure is a resort town on the Ligurian Riviera di Ponente, west of Genoa, made up of three distinct settlements: Finalborgo (the medieval walled village inland), Finalmarina (the main seaside resort), and Finalpia (to the east). It has become one of Italy's best-known destinations for rock climbing, with limestone cliffs above the town offering hundreds of routes.

The old village of Finalborgo is the most rewarding part. Its medieval walls, towers, and the former convent of Santa Caterina — now the Civic Museum — are excellent. The museum holds finds from the many prehistoric caves in the surrounding cliffs, including the caves of Arene Candide, Arma delle Manie, and Pollera, which were inhabited long before the Roman conquest. The rocky hinterland is pockmarked with cavities and cliffs that draw both climbers and walkers.

The coast here is good for swimming and watersports. The Ligurian cuisine — pesto, focaccia, fresh pasta, grilled fish — is excellent throughout the area.
