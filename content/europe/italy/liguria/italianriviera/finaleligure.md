It has become one of Italy's best-known destinations for rock climbing, with limestone cliffs above the town offering hundreds of routes.

The old village of Finalborgo is the most rewarding part. Its medieval walls, towers, and the former convent of Santa Caterina — now the Civic Museum — are excellent. The museum holds finds from the many prehistoric caves in the surrounding cliffs, including the caves of Arene Candide, Arma delle Manie, and Pollera, which were inhabited long before the Roman conquest. The rocky hinterland is pockmarked with cavities and cliffs that draw both climbers and walkers.

The coast here is good for swimming and watersports. The Ligurian cuisine — pesto, focaccia, fresh pasta, grilled fish — is excellent throughout the area.

Finale Ligure is a seaside town on the Ligurian coast between Genoa and the French border, made up of three distinct parts: **Finalmarina** on the seafront, **Finalpia** to the east, and the medieval walled village of **Finalborgo** slightly inland. The rocky hinterland is riddled with caves that were inhabited in prehistoric times — the most important, including the **Arene Candide** and **Pollera** caves, have yielded finds now displayed in the **Civic Museum of Finale** in the former monastery of Santa Caterina at Finalborgo.

The coast here is dramatic, bounded by Cape Caprazoppa to the west and Cape Noli to the east. In recent years Finale has also become one of the top mountain biking destinations in Europe, with trails running through the limestone cliffs above the sea. The food, like everywhere in Liguria, is excellent — pesto, focaccia, fresh fish, and the region's distinctive olive oil.
