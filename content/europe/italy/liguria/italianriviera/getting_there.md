---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The Riviera is well connected by rail, and the train is almost always the best option for moving between coastal towns. The main line (Ventimiglia–Genova–Livorno) runs the full length of the coast and is fast and frequent.

**By train:** Trenitalia's Ventimiglia–Genova–Livorno line stops at all the major resort towns. From Genoa, Cinque Terre villages are about an hour by regional train.

**By car:** The A10/A7/A12 autostrada runs parallel to the coast. For the scenic route, follow the SS1 (Via Aurelia), but allow extra time — it's slow through the villages. Parking in most towns is limited and expensive in summer.

**By air:** Genoa's Cristoforo Colombo airport (GOA) is the closest hub, about 6 km west of the city. Nice Côte d'Azur (NCE) is an alternative for the western Riviera; Pisa airport serves the eastern end.
