Situated in the Ligurian Appennines, Altare
 was a possession of the marchesi di Monferrato
 and its history is linked to the working of glass,
 a craft brought to this densely-wooded area by
 some French families in the eleventh century.
 The statutes of the guild of craftsmen known as
 the “University of Glass” were recognized by
 the Monferrato in 1552 and governed the
 commune until 1823. Since 1856 the tradition
 has been kept alive by the Società Artistica
 Vetraria, which has maintained a furnace for glass blowing alongside the industrial plant.
 Splendid examples of this distinctive craft are
 on show at the Villa Rosa, a fine townhouse in
 the art nouveau style that houses a museum.
 Toward the end of the fifteenth century the
 church of the Annunziata was constructed over
 an existing Romanesque building, whose
 campanile still stands, while the parish church
 of Sant’Eugenio, with a façade set between two
 campaniles, is a late seventeenth-century
 baroque construction.
