A scattered municipality situated in the valley
 of the Neva in the Ligurian Alps, it grew up on
 the ancient Salt Road. A possession of the
 marchesi di Clavesana since the twelfth
 century, it was sold to the Savoy in 1623.
 Subsequently acquired by the republic of
 Genoa, it shared its fortunes. It was sacked by
 Napoleon’s troops in 1796. The parish church
 of Santa Caterina, opposite the oratory of San
 Giovanni and flanked by a campanile built in
 the late Middle Ages, is a simple building of the
 baroque era. The parish church of San Martino
 di Erli, from the eleventh century, houses
 fifteenth-century frescoes. The extremely well-preserved
 Borgo dei Bassi, on an elliptical plan,
 is a significant example of a medieval settlement. The agriculture is typical of the
 mountains. There are extensive chestnut woods
 where mushrooms are collected.
