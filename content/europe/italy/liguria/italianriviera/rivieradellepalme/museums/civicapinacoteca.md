CIVICA PINACOTECA DI VILLA GROPPALLO - VADO LIGURE

 In 1982 the commune of Vado Ligure inaugurated a cultural center in the eighteenth-century residence of the De Mari family, which includes a library and an art gallery. The latter is divided into several different sections: one of these is devoted to the works of sculpture and painting that won the competition for artists called the «Premio Vado,» which was held by the commune for the first time in 1951, at the urging of the Provincial Guild of Painters and Sculptors. Another section houses a selection of the most important works produced since the Second World War by artists from Vado, including A. Cabiati, R. Collina, R. Barsotti and M. Nencioni. In addition to nineteenth-century canvases from the collection of Don Cesare Queirolo, the museum has a number of sculptures by Arturo Martini: the plaster casts that served as the prototypes for the bronze statues on the Monument to the Fallen in Vado, the plaster statue of the Blind Man and the terra-cotta sarcophagus of the Benefactor. Villa Groppallo is also used to stage temporary exhibitions, such as the recent one by the Genoese group «Campo Aperto,» organized by the art critic Vico Faggi with the intention of reviving the tradition of the «Premio Vado» and demonstrating the renewed vitality of the Pinacoteca.
