MUSEO DEL VETRO E DELL’ARTE VETRARIA IN ALTARE

 Up until the twelfth century, Altare was famous for the skill of its glassmakers, whose popularity and reputation were on a par with those of Murano all over Europe. «Altare means Glass,» and therefore craftsmanship, art and culture: a heritage of priceless value to be preserved, defended and displayed with pride. While the ancient traditions are kept alive in a number of workshops, the activity of promotion and cultural research is entrusted to the Istituto per lo Studio del Vetro e dell’Arte Vetraria, which has published the review Alte Vitrie since 1988, and the Museo del Vetro di Altare, housed in the Villa Rosa, a splendid building in pure art nouveau style. Its exhibits include around 300 precious articles (amphorae and carafes, altar chalices and holy-water stoups, vases, bottles, lamps, etc.), 1570 glass objects with a wide variety of functions (flytraps, oil siphons, bell jars for holy figurines and clocks, ewers, etc.), around 800 old wooden and cast-iron molds and over 400 tools for working glass by hand, in use from the end of the eighteenth century to the beginning of the nineteenth.
