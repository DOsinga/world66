---
title: "MUSEO «MANLIO TRUCCO» -ALBISOLA"
type: poi
---

The Museo Civico della Ceramica «Manlio Trucco» in Albisola Superiore houses and displays collections of locally-made antique pottery, from Savona and Albisola. They include a group of around forty majolica pharmacy vessels with blue monochrome decorations, dating from the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, on loan from the administration of the Civil Hospitals of Genoa. Another bequest consists of a collection of around eighty pots, dating from the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, including eighteenth-century majolica ware from Savona with anthropomorphic decorations, popular yellow terra-cotta pots from Albisola, wonderful glazed brown pottery decorated with abstract black stripes, known as taches noires,» exemplars of black earthenware and around fifty crèche figures or «shepherds,» some of them made by the potter Tambuscio from Savona. Also on show are ceramic works by Manlio Trucco, some of them made from molds by Arturo Martini. A special section of the museum is devoted to the Schiappapietra collection of objects from the ancient Roman period, consisting of pottery, glassware, marbles and pieces of painted plaster found in the excavations carried out over a hundred years ago by Schiappapietra himself at the Roman villa of Alba Docilia, located in the vicinity of the modern railroad station.
