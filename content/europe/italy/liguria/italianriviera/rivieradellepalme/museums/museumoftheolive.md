MUSEUM OF THE OLIVE AND OF PEASANT CULTURE - ARNASCO -  The Museum of the Olive and of Peasant Culture houses documents dating from the seventeenth century testifying to the presence of the prized Pignola variety of olive in the Arnasco region. Since time immemorial the local population, employed chiefly in agriculture, has made it the center of their rural activities. Within a short space of time, in fact, the cultivation of olives became so vital and important that it constituted the area’s principal resource. The «Arnasca,» an olive with the savor of pine-seed, requires a very special kind of soil, stony, dry and exposed to large amounts of sunlight. This important variety of olive, unique in the Mediterranean region, is both a source of wealth and a constant reminder to the people of their agricultural origins. Consequently, in 1984, the olive producers of Arnasco decided to create the «Cooperativa Olivicola» («Olive-Growing Cooperative») to foster an agricultural industry in decline, to curb the continual depopulation of the countryside and to preserve the heritage of implements that were once used for the cultivation, picking and pressing of olives. Thus the «Museo dell'Olivo e della Civiltà Contadina,» set up in 1986, displays a collection of over 400 tools, a restored olive press dating from 1796, and a partial reconstruction of a «dry wall.» In order that the fascination of the culture of the past not be forgotten, the Cooperativa Olivicola of Arnasco operates a genuine olive press every year, cold pressing the olives with millstones to produce a Pignolo extra virgin oil and keep alive a tradition with a nostalgically rural flavor.
