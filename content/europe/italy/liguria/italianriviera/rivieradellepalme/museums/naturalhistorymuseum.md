NATURAL HISTORY MUSEUM OF ALASSIO

  The Museo di Scienze Naturali «Don Bosco» in Alassio is split up into a large number of sections. The zoological one, with its butterfly collection, is the most fascinating: here the visitor can admire the golden eagle, various species of duck, stork, albatross and humming bird and a marvelous specimen of the quetzal in its rich ornithological collection. The icthyological section presents examples of the gulf’s most significant marine life, while a wide range of local medicinal herbs can be seen in the botanical section. The exhibition on the natural history of the sea is very interesting, with panels and diagrams on all the species of mollusk described in the scientific texts. Along with the other sections (on mineralogy, petrography, paleontology and meteorology), the pride of the museum is the latest addition devoted to mycology, which allows visitors to participate directly in the recognition and classification of any of the over 75,000 known varieties of fungi. The extremely didactic and scientific approach to the layout of all the sections brings out the richness of the exhibits, making them easily comprehensible to all visitors.
