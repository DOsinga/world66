---
title: "THE MUSEUMS OF ALBENGA"
type: poi
---

Visit the museums of Albenga to discover the origins of the Ligurian civilization. The Civico Museo Ingauno also gives you the opportunity to admire the Palazzo Vecchio del Comune, with its loggia decorated with a large fifteenth-century fresco of the Crucifixion in which Roman, medieval and Byzantine marble carvings and a mosaic floor from the first century AD are on display. The Sala dei Consoli, with its massive frescoed groin vault, houses epigraphs and sculptures from the ancient Roman town of Albingaunum, while the large and austere reception room on the second floor contains thin-walled earthenware pots decorated with seals, a small but fine urn made of carved alabaster and the contents of a second-century AD tomb among the many objects displayed in its showcases. The Palazzo Peloso Cepolla houses the Museo Navale Romano, where the materials recovered from the wreck of a Roman cargo ship lying on the seabed one mile off the coast of the island of Gallinara are on show. They consist of 100 intact wine amphorae out of the over 700 that were brought to the surface, wooden and metal pieces of the hull and remains of the cargo and fittings. The bishop’s palace is the seat of the Museo Diocesano, whose various rooms, some of them decorated with frescoes, contain a broad range of valuable pottery, from Liguria and elsewhere, dating from the sixteenth to seventeenth century; precious reliquaries and church ornaments; paintings on panel or canvas from the fourteenth to eighteenth century (De Ferrari, Reni, Piola etc.); and sixteenth-century tapestries from the Brussels factory representing Scenes from the Childhood of Moses.
