---
title: "Orco Feglino"
type: location
---

A municipality located in the hinterland of Finale Ligure, on the southern side of Monte Alto (956 meters or 3137 feet). The township of Feglino is situated on the floor of the Aquila valley, while Orco is perched on a ridge, in a panoramic position. A possession of the Del Carretto, who built a castle there, it was ruled by Spain from the second half of the sixteenth century until 1713, when it was acquired by the republic of Genoa. A few remains of the walls and tower of the medieval castle of Orco still stand on a crag near the town. The nearby church of San Lorenzino was built in the twelfth to fourteenth century and has a fourteenth-century campanile with two rows of two-light windows. The church of San Lorenzo at Feglino was renovated in the baroque era, while the shrine of Santa Maria Ausiliatrice dates from 1874. Surrounded by woods, the terraced farmland produces fruit, flowers and vegetables.
