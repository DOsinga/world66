---

Occupying the characteristic Piazza Vecchia (also called Piazza del Mercato), this oratory dates from the tenth century and was the town's original parish church, dedicated to Saint Nicholas. Restructured in the baroque era, its nave and two aisles are built with columns of local stone. Inside, it preserves oil measures from the Republic of Genoa and the remains of a medieval well that once stood in the square outside. Today it functions as an auditorium.
