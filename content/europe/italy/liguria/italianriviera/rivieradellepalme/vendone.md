An extensive municipality in the valley of the
 Arroscia, on the southern slopes of
 Castell’Ermo (1092 meters or 3583 feet). In the
 Middle Ages it had its own lords, subject to the
 jurisdiction of the commune of Albenga. A
 partial fief of the marchesi di Clavesana, it was
 ceded in the first decade of the fourteenth
 century to the commune of Albenga, eventually
 passing with it to the republic of Genoa. In the hamlet of Castellaro it is possible to see the
 ruins of the imposing castle of the Clavesana,
 with its well-preserved hexagonal tower. A
 series of fortified houses stand high above the
 river at Curenna. It is an attractive place for
 excursions, among the olive groves, in the
 direction of the Savonese Alps.
