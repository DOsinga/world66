---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Ligurian cooking is light, fragrant, and almost vegetarian in character — built on olive oil, fresh herbs, pine nuts, and vegetables. Pesto is the star, but look out for trofie (hand-rolled pasta served with pesto), pansoti (pasta stuffed with wild greens), and focaccia di Recco (a thin, crisp bread filled with melting stracchino cheese). Seafood runs to sardines, anchovies, and sea bass rather than lobster and prawns.
