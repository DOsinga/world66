Monterosso al Mare is the largest and most westerly of the five Cinque Terre villages, and the one most geared to visitors. Unlike its more vertical neighbours, it spreads out along a proper sandy beach — two beaches, in fact, separated by a rocky headland — which makes it a genuine resort as well as a historic fishing village. The old town, huddled behind the headland, has narrow caruggi lanes, a medieval tower, and a handful of good restaurants serving anchovies caught right off the coast.

Monterosso grows the lemons and grapes that define Cinque Terre cooking. The local anchovies (acciughe) are cured with sea salt and eaten as a starter or tossed through pasta; the white wine Sciacchetrà, made from partially dried grapes, is sweet and powerful. Both are worth seeking out before you leave.

The village draws more day-trippers than the others precisely because of its beach and relative accessibility, so early morning and late afternoon are the best times to wander the old town. Stay overnight and you get the place to yourself once the ferries have gone.
