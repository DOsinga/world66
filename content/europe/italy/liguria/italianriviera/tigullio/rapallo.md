Rapallo is the largest town on the Tigullio Gulf, with around 30,000 residents and a long history as a resort. Its symbol is the small sixteenth-century castle that stands on a promontory in the bay — once a defence against Saracen pirates, now a venue for art exhibitions with its feet in the sea. The seafront promenade curves around the harbour in the classic Ligurian fashion, all palm trees and pastel-painted hotels.

The town's most distinctive attraction is the sanctuary of the Madonna di Montallegro, perched on a ridge above the town and reached by the only funicular railway in Liguria. The ride up offers increasingly dramatic views over the gulf and out to sea; on a clear day you can see as far as Corsica. The sanctuary itself dates from the sixteenth century and its walls are covered with a remarkable collection of votive offerings — small painted panels recording disasters narrowly averted by divine intervention.

In early July, Rapallo holds its main festival in honour of the Madonna di Montallegro. The castle is set ablaze with fireworks in one of the most spectacular displays on the Riviera.
