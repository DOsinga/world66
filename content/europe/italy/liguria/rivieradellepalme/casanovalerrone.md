Casanova Lerrone is a quiet farming town in the upper valley of the Lerrone River, inland from the Ligurian coast. Its history is typical of the small fiefdoms that changed hands repeatedly between Genoa's noble families — Del Carretto, Clavesana, Laigueglia, Negroni — and it was the centre of a short-lived uprising against Genoa in 1763–64, prompted by excessive taxation.

Several structures from the feudal period survive, including the castles of Poggiolo (with seventeenth-century decorations, privately owned) and Doria, as well as the ruins of the Del Carretto and Maremo castles. The baroque parish church of Sant'Antonino houses a polyptych from 1552 attributed to the school of Brea, and the shrine of Nostra Signora della Visitazione at Degna has a group of wooden sculptures possibly by Maragliano.
