---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Sestri Levante's pleasures are largely slow ones. The Baia del Silenzio (Bay of Silence), the smaller and quieter of the town's two bays, is genuinely peaceful — a curving beach backed by pastel-coloured houses with no traffic, only the sound of water. The Baia delle Favole (Bay of Fairy Tales) on the other side of the peninsula faces south and has a longer beach with more activity. The Marconi Tower at the top of the promontory offers a panoramic view extending to the Alps on clear days — worth the walk. The 12th-century Church of San Niccolò, Roman-Gothic in style, sits near the old castle at the tip of the peninsula. The waterfront promenade is where everyone strolls at aperitivo hour.
