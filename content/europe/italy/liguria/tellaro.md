Tellaro is a tiny fishing village clinging to the rocks at the southeastern tip of the Gulf of La Spezia, about 4 kilometres south of [Lerici](/europe/italy/liguria/italianriviera/lerici). It is one of the most picturesque spots on the Ligurian coast — a cluster of pastel-coloured houses, a small church on a rocky promontory, and narrow passageways that wind between buildings and along the cliff edge above the sea.

The village is car-free (there is a car park on the road above) and small enough to walk through in twenty minutes, but it rewards lingering. The swimming is good off the rocks below the village, and there are a couple of small restaurants. Tellaro has been named one of Italy's *borghi più belli* (most beautiful villages), and on a quiet morning out of season it is easy to see why.
