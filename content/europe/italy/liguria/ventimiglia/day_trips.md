---
title: "Day Trips in Ventimiglia"
type: section
---

**Ventimiglia day trips **will ensure that you'll be able to see more of what the country - and the continent - have to offer during your stay in this charming city. When heading to Ventimiglia, clear a day or two in your travel itinerary to visit any of these places:

**Torino (Turin)**

Driving for about 2-1/2 hours or taking a train for about 3 hours can get you to Torino, which is north of Ventimiglia. Torino is a city with more modern architecture than Ventimiglia, but it still has some classic spots, such as La Mole Antonelliana,
which was, in 1889, the world's tallest brick building, and the San
Lorenzo Church. Or you can spend your day at one of the 100+ museums in Torino devoted to cinema, art, culture, ancient Egyptian artifacts, motor vehicles and more.

**Genova (Genoa)
**

Another popular Italian city along the coast of the Mediterranean Sea is Genova, located about a 1 hour and 40 minutes' drive northeast of Ventimiglia. A ride on the high speed Genova-Ventimiglia railway can get you to the other city in less time and costs a little over €10 each way (US$12). Spend your day trip to Genova at one of the city's many public parks and gardens or tour the architectural highlights of the area, such as the St. Lawrence Cathedral, the Piazza de Ferrari and the Church of San Danto. Art museums and the supposed birthplace of Christopher Columbus are other popular tourist attractions.

While in Genova, don't pass up the Porto Antico ("Old Harbor") along the coast. Restored architecture
makes the area feel like something out of hundreds of years before. One
highlight of the area is the Aquarium and Museum of the Sea, which
features both maritime animals and dioramas of Italian conquests via the sea.

**Nice, France**

Because Ventimiglia is near the Italy/France border, it'd be a waste not to include
a day trip to the nearby country. Nice makes an excellent destination,
as it's a large city with many sights that is only a 40-minute drive
from Ventimiglia. A train ride costs about €10 each way (US$13) and takes about 45 minutes.

Nice, like Ventimiglia, is located along the Mediterranean Sea coastline and offers plenty of beach and water activities with year-round warm temperatures. Walk along the Promenade d'Anglais
("Walkway of the English"), a coastline area where English immigrants
once settled that has breathtaking architecture and views. Make a point
to tour much of city's architecture, such as the Place du Palais, the Place Rossetti and the Place Garibaldi.
