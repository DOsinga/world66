Ventimiglia is a town located in Italy and is home to nearly 26,000 people. In **Ventimiglia, when to go **is not a particular issue, as this region experiences a mild Mediterranean climate and generally has pleasant weather all year round. Situated in the province of Imperia, this cultural city offers a lot to do and many breathtaking things to see. It is also situated on the border of France and Italy, making it even more fascinating. Read on to learn about the climate and weather to expect while visiting Ventimiglia.

**Mild Mediterranean Climate**

For the most part, Ventimiglia possesses a very mild Mediterranean climate. The occasional wind from the north will blow, coming from the Roia Valley, but this is quite rare. Summers in this region are warm and dry, while winters are wet and moderate. Rainfall is likely to occur in the winter months, but temperatures are mild and rarely drop below 10 degrees C at any point. The summer can be hot, but is usually not excessively warm. The driest months are July and August, and often the hottest as well. The months of May and June are very mild in temperature and may be the perfect time to visit, although Ventimiglia weather is mild enough to be enjoyed all year long.

**Precipitation in Ventimiglia**

During summer this region does not experience any moisture due to the subtropical high pressure cells making rainfall nearly impossible. In the rare occasion rain does happen, it will be in the form of a thunderstorm.

Winter is when the precipitation occurs in Ventimiglia. The cause of this rainfall is due to the polar jet stream and periodic storms that travel into the lower latitudes of the region. The result is heavy rainfall during winter, and almost no wetness during summer. If you do not want to experience rainfall while traveling to this area, it is wise to travel during summer, and to avoid the winter months.

**What to Wear in Ventimiglia**Clothing for this region should be lightweight and comfortable, preferably cotton in the summer when there is the possibility of high temperatures. Sunscreen is advised to protect against sun burn. During the cooler winter months, one should wear heavier clothes, but not too heavy, as the winters rarely get excessively cold and generally stay mild. Rain gear will be needed if traveling during winter because precipitation is likely to occur.
