Val Brembana stretches north from Bergamo into the Orobie Alps, following the Brembo river through a succession of small towns, gorges, and high pastures. In winter the valley offers affordable skiing at Foppolo and cross-country trails around Piazzatorre. In summer the same slopes become hiking country, with paths climbing to mountain lakes and rifugi above the treeline.

The valley is also known for its food — Branzi and Formai de Mut are local Alpine cheeses worth seeking out, and the polenta taragna served in village trattorias is rich and satisfying. San Pellegrino Terme, about halfway up the valley, has a faded Belle Époque grandeur and is the source of the famous mineral water.

From Bergamo the valley is an easy drive or bus ride — the road follows the river the whole way, with the mountains closing in as you climb.
