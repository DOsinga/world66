San Tomè is a Romanesque religious temple built between the XI and XII centuries. It is of circular form with a cross-shaped window that lets the sun shine on the altar on the day of summer equinox. Next to it is a restored convent from the XIII century.

It is located in Almenno San Bartolomeo on the edge of the cliff overlooking the Brembo river in a forest of chestnut trees and near some beautiful agricultural fields.

Hours from November thru April: Sunday and holidays 10.00-12.00 and 14.30-17.00;
from May thru October; Saturday 14.30-18.00; Sunday and holidays 10.00-12.00 and 14.30-18.00.
Others visits can be made with a reservation.
Free entrance.
