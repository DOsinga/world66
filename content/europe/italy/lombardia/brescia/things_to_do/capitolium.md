The best-preserved Roman public complex in northern Italy: a Capitoline temple rebuilt by the emperor Vespasian in AD 73, its Corinthian facade rising over the open space of the ancient forum. The cellae still hold the original Roman floors and the six bronze statues (including the Winged Victory of Brescia) that were found buried here in 1826 and are now the city's emblem — the statues themselves are in the Santa Giulia Museum next door.
