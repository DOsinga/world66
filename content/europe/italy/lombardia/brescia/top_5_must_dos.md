type: section

If you happen to be planning a trip to the beautiful Brescia of Lombardi, Italy, you may be wondering which of the activities available are among the **Brescian must do&rsquo;s** that have made the city so popular. Listed below are some best activities the city offers.

#### 1. Visit Castello di Brescia

Castello di Brescia, or "The Castle" as it is often known, is one of the largest castles in all of Italy and a huge tourist attraction. The castle features two separate museums within its doors: the Museo Civico del Risorgimento and the Museo Civico delle Armi Antiche Luigi. However, whereas the general visit to the castle is free, admission to the museums inside will cost money. Built in the 15th century, the Castle still features an intact moat and beautiful interior and exterior views that ensure you will enjoy your visit to this extravagant and historical landmark.

#### 2. Enjoy Yourself at Franciacorta Golf Club

This destination is for all the golf fans. The Franciacorta Golf Club is an 8-hole luxury golf course that covers well over 150 acres of gorgeous green-way. Converted from land that was once a vineyard, this popular golf course was fashioned after American golf courses and is an excellent choice for spending an afternoon in Brescia, Italy.

#### 3. Spend the Day at Aqua Splash

A spot for the entire family to cool down on a hot summer day in northern Italy, the water amusement park Aqua Splash offers spills and thrills for all of its patrons. With a fair number of rides, many for adults and children alike, this attraction is great for the entire family. There are approximately 7 adult rides and 4 child rides, one of the more popular rides being the "Black Hole," a water slide that will baffle your senses as it plunges you into the darkness of a tubular waterway, giving you the impression that you are falling endlessly into a cavernous hole.

#### 4. Take a Stroll

Even strolling through the streets of Brescia is a favorite to-do, as it is in many European cities. While taking a stroll you can check out the local buildings which have huge histories behind them. One of the most famous buildings is Via dei Musei, which was built during the Flavia time.

#### 5. Go Shopping

The city boasts a number of markets and shopping complexes. Don&rsquo;t forget to visit Piazza del Foro, which is one of the most famous markets. Tourist and local residents often visit here for the large number of shops that line the street.
