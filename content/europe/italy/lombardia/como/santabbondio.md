Standing just outside the old city walls, Sant'Abbondio is one of the finest
Romanesque churches in northern Italy. Built in the eleventh century by the
*Maestri Comacini* — the master builders who spread their craft across medieval
Europe — it has two slender bell towers flanking a deep apse covered in
fourteenth-century frescoes depicting the life of Christ. The interior is
austere and affecting.
