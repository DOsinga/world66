The Cathedral of Como is one of the last great Gothic churches built in
Lombardy, begun in 1396 and finally completed — dome and all — in 1744.
Its west facade is a richly decorated mix of Gothic tracery and Renaissance
relief work. Inside, Flemish and Italian tapestries line the nave, and the
side chapels hold paintings by Bernardino Luini and Gaudenzio Ferrari.
