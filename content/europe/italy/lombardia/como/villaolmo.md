This stately neoclassical villa at the northern edge of the lakefront promenade
was built in the 1780s for the Odescalchi family. Its grounds run down to the
water and its colonnaded facade is one of Como's most photographed views.
The villa hosts rotating exhibitions — often on science, design, or local
history — and the lakeside gardens are open to the public for free.
