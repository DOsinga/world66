Peschiera del Garda sits at the point where Lake Garda drains into the Mincio river, its old town ringed by star-shaped Venetian walls that are now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. With around 10,000 inhabitants, it is one of the smaller towns on the lake, but its position at the southern tip — just 25 km from Verona — makes it a practical and genuinely pleasant base.

The walls are the main attraction, and they reward a slow walk: the ramparts give views over the lake and the surrounding moraine hills, and the gates and bastions are well preserved. Inside the fortifications, Piazza Bettotti anchors the old centre, flanked by the Palazzo di Comando del Presidio and a small local history museum. The church of San Martino dates from the 18th century and is worth a look inside.

The nearby hamlet of Broglie, a couple of kilometres out, has a cluster of churches. In Colà you will find a handful of 16th- and 17th-century villas — Villa Cedri, Villa de Sacco, and Villa Miniscalchi among them — set in the gentle countryside between the lake and the plain.

If you have a bike, the ride along the Mincio river to Valeggio sul Mincio is one of the better half-days in the area. The village of Borghetto, just below Valeggio, is famous across the region for its tortellini — lunch there makes a fine destination in itself.
