The Borromean Islands are the highlight of any visit to Lago Maggiore. Ferries run regularly from [Stresa](/europe/italy/piemonte/stresa) and Baveno.

**Isola Bella**

In 1670 Count Vitaliano Borromeo started the construction of a monumental baroque palace, and began landscaping the gardens for which the island became famous. The stately Borromeo residence contains priceless masterpieces: tapestries, furniture, and paintings by great masters. The gardens bloom with every variety of trees and rare flowers which, in their succession of ornate terraces, provide a classic example of the celebrated "Italian garden" of that period.

**Isola Madre**

The largest of the Borromeo islands and the most atmospheric, with its gardens of exotic plants where white peacocks, parrots, and pheasants roam freely. Isola Madre is particularly famous for its flourishing azaleas, rhododendrons, and camellias. The 16th-century palace was opened to the public in 1978 and houses a notable collection of puppet theatres from the 18th and 19th centuries.

**Isola dei Pescatori**

Fishermen's Island owes its name to its inhabitants. The charm of this island lies in the simplicity of its houses and narrow streets. Good restaurants offer the opportunity to taste the best fish from the lake.
