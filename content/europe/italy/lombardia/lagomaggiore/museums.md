---
title: "Museums in Lago Maggiore"
type: section
---

Lago Maggiore is a beautiful location in Italy and the area has a
rich history which is reflected in the museums which can be visited.
The following is a guide to some of the more interesting museums that
can be visited in the towns around the region.

**The Landscape Museum**

With Lago Maggiore being a beautiful location it has inspired many
artists to paint and many of these paintings are now displayed in the
Landscape Museum in Pallanza. The museum is located on Via Ruga in the
Viani-Dugnani Mansion and has been exhibiting paintings for many years.
It contains many notable paintings from important Italian artists and
also has archeological and sculpture exhibits. The museum is open from
April to October every day except Mondays. Opening hours are
10.00-12.00 and 15.30-18.30.

**The Umbrella Museum**

A more unusual museum located in Gignese is the Umbrella Museum.
Opened originally in 1939, this museum has displays and exhibits of
umbrellas through the ages. This takes you from the parasols of the
1800’s all the way through to the modern day. It also showcases the
history of some of the umbrella makers who lived in the area and gives
an explanation of their dialect which was known as ‘Tarusc’. Entrance
fee is Euros 2.50 and the museum is open everyday except Mondays
between April and September. Opening hours are 10.00-12.00 and
15.00-18.00

**The Chimney Sweep Museum**

The area around Lago Maggiore was known for many years as the land
of the chimney sweeps and many of the population travelled around
European cities working in the trade. This museum showcases some of
this history and has many of the work tools which were used. It also
has exhibits of chimney sweep mementos and artifacts from all over
Europe and America. The museum is located in the Villa Antonia in Santa
Maria and opening hours are 10.00-12.00 and 15.00-18.00. These museums
will provide the visitor to the Lago Maggiore area a fascinating
glimpse into the history of the region
