---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Lago Maggiore has a mild climate sheltered by the Alps, making it pleasant from spring through autumn. May and June offer the best balance of warm weather and manageable crowds. July and August are the hottest and busiest months. The gardens on the Borromean Islands are at their most spectacular in late spring. Winter is quiet and damp but never bitterly cold.

**Festivals and Events**

The Swiss end of the lake around Locarno and Ascona hosts most of the major events. The Locarno Film Festival in early August draws international audiences to open-air screenings in the Piazza Grande. Jazz Ascona in late June brings over 200 concerts from international jazz artists. In May, the International Street Artists Festival in Ascona features jugglers, musicians, and performers from around the world. The Moon and Stars Festival in Locarno in July offers pop and rock concerts on the Piazza Grande. Smaller events run throughout the season, including the Chestnut and Autumn festival in Ascona in October.
