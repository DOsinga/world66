---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Mantua is a city of palaces, churches, and paintings. The enormous Palazzo Ducale — once home to the Gonzaga family — sprawls across the northeast corner of the old town with hundreds of rooms, courtyards, and gardens; Andrea Mantegna's Camera degli Sposi, with its illusionistic ceiling, is the highlight. Across town, the Palazzo Te is a Mannerist masterpiece by Giulio Romano, with fantastical frescoed rooms including the jaw-dropping Sala dei Giganti.

The churches are worth seeking out too: the Basilica di Sant'Andrea (designed by Alberti) and the Romanesque Rotonda di San Lorenzo are architectural landmarks. Several smaller museums and galleries round out a city that rewards slow exploration.
