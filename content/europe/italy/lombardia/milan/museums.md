Here are a few suggested museums in Milan.

### [Museo del Duomo

Situated in the Palazzo Reale next to the cathedral you will find here several masterpieces of art and craftworks, that still belong to the huge cathedral treasure. The exhibited objects (mass requisites, drawings, sculptures etc.) are like a window to religious life during the times, from the late Gothic age till today. For more information call the museum (Tel. 0039-02-860358 or fax 0039-02-72022419) or have a look at their WEBPAGE  *Section:Getting Around

**Public transports**

The municipal transports company in Milan is called ATM and you can find its offices in many underground stations. 

**Underground** 

In Milan there are 3 underground lines: red, green and yellow. This is probably the best way to go from one corner to the other of the city, even though, as you can imagine, the underground doesn't allow you to see the surrounding places.

To get the underground, you have to buy the ticket which is sold in the underground stations and in many tobacconist's shops and newspaper kiosks. A normal ticket costs 1500 Lire (less than 1 US$), but it is also possible to buy a one-day ticket or a weekly ticket-book.

Each ticket lasts 45 minutes but, if you exit the underground station and then you enter another underground station or if you have a still valid ticket you used on a bus or on a tramway and you enter an underground station, you must obliterate a new ticket. 

**Buses and tramways** 

Instead of the underground, you can get a bus or a tramway. The ticket is the same you use for the underground and you can reuse it on other buses, tramways or underground lines if the time of 45 minutes has not expired yet.
