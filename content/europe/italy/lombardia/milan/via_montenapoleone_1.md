Via Montenapoleone is the centrepiece of Milan's Quadrilatero della Moda — the fashion district formed by four streets in the city centre. This is where Armani, Versace, Prada, Gucci, and their neighbours have their flagship stores, in elegant 19th-century palazzi. The street is short but the density of Italian luxury is unmatched.

Even without shopping, it's worth a walk. The architecture is handsome, the window displays can be extraordinary, and the side streets — particularly Via della Spiga and Via Sant'Andrea — are just as polished. Aperitivo time brings well-dressed Milanesi out in force.
