---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Spring and autumn are the sweet spots for Lombardia. May, June, September, and October bring good weather for city sightseeing, manageable crowds, and pleasant temperatures on the lakes. Summer is fine for hiking and swimming but the lakes get packed — August especially, when half of Italy is on holiday and prices spike accordingly.

Winter is cold and grey in the Po Valley, but if you're mainly visiting museums and churches it's perfectly workable and much cheaper. The Alps can be stunning in snow, and skiing is a short drive away. January and February are the quietest months throughout the region.
