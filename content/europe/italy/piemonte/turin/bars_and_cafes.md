---
title: 'Bars and Cafes'
type: section
---

Turin's cafe culture is legendary. The city invented the bicerin (a layered drink of espresso, chocolate, and cream) and claims to have created the tramezzino sandwich. Many of the historic cafes date back centuries, with ornate interiors that make ordering a simple espresso feel like a cultural event. In the evening, the aperitivo tradition takes over and nearly every bar in the centre lays out generous spreads of snacks with your drink.

**Caffe San Carlo** -- Often called the "living room of Turin," this grand cafe on Piazza San Carlo stuns with its massive chandelier and ornate decor. The pastries are freshly baked and the service is brisk. Open until midnight daily.

**Caffe Torino** -- Look for the brass bull embedded in the pavement outside; standing on it is said to bring good luck. On Piazza San Carlo, this stylish cafe serves rich coffee under the porticoes and puts out aperitivo plates from 6 p.m. to 10 p.m.

**Caffe Mulassano** -- A tiny, exquisite Art Nouveau jewel box that claims to have invented the tramezzino. The decorations on the walls, ceiling, and floor are worth the visit alone. Open 7:30 a.m. to 9 p.m., cocktails around 5 to 7 euros.

**Birrificio** -- If you have had your fill of Piedmontese wine, this brewery on Via Parma 30 is all about craft beer. Popular with students and locals.
