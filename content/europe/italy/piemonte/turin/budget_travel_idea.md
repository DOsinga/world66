Known as the "Automobile Capital of Italy," Turin is a city found near the Po River and is home to Fiat and Alfa Romeo. It is also home to the controversial "Shroud of Turin." However, this 50.3 square mile city is more than a automobile haven or a holy cloth. On a budget travel scale, you can have fun and unique experiences on the streets of Turin. The **Turin budget travel** guide lists several ideas for tourists to enjoy their vacation in this intriguing city, minus a fancy budget.

**Budget Sightseeing and Activities**

There are many wonderful places to visit in Turin but to help you save up on entrance fees and transport costs you should get the Torino Card. This is a convenient way to gain access to see over 130 museums, gardens, palaces, landmarks and other tourist spots. With the card, you can also get free rides on the urban transport bus. The card is offered for different durations which you can integrate to fit in your travel itinerary.

Attractions worth visiting on a budget include Mole Antonelliana, the Piazza San Carlo where the "Caval ‘d Brons" is located, the Cathedral of St. John for the famous Shroud, the Piazza Castello for landmarks such as the Palazzo Madama, the Motor Museum-which houses 170 cars--and the Botanical Gardens where lovers flock for its romantic scenery.

**Budget Shopping, Eating and Entertainment**

Share delicious pizza at an affordable price at Fratelli La Cozza. Located in Aurora, the restaurant serves large helpings (ideal for groups) with different flavors such as Burrata, Spadellati and Cuprese. You should also never leave Turin without trying the city's famous gianduia ice cream at Caffe Fiorio or Caffe Miretti. For low-budget entertainment, there’s Cab 41, a comedy bar that guarantees you a night of laughter. You may also opt to watch a movie at Fiamma near the city center.

**Budget Shopping**

It is best to travel light so you will have room in your luggage for things you buy in Turin. If you want discount prices for designer leather shoes, the place to go is Il Grifone. Shopping malls like the Le Gru and the 8 Gallery are also places where you can find good items at bargain prices. You can also scour along Via Roma, Via Po and Via Garibaldi for best deals on clothes, shoes and accessories.

**Budget Accommodations**

To get the most out of any budget travel accommodations, booking early is an advantage. Choosing between hotels, hostels or apartments will depend on your budget. Hosteling International Torino offers nice and clean dormitory accommodations. Doria which is located near the Porta Nuova Train Station is a good choice for affordable singles or doubles. Other options for reasonable prices are Hotel Europa, Belvedere Bed and Breakfast and the Augustus Hotel.

Turin is a city where culture, history and art are meshed to give any tourist on a budget, the experience of a lifetime. It is guaranteed to capture your heart without leaving a hole in your pocket.
