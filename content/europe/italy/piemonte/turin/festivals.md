---
title: "Festivals in Turin"
type: section
---

**Turin
festivals** offer tourists celebrated saints and outdoor
concerts. Plan travel to this region in Italy around some of the most
well-known festivals. Visitors see the culture and tradition of Turin residents
through the festivals that this city offers.

**Turin
Festivals: Jan – March **

Torino Anima Tango, presented in January, has this
traditional dance that attracts tango dancers from all over the world. Visitors
to Turin watch the spectacular outfits and dances choreographed by teams. The
New Year’s Eve concert features music to all in Turin’s main piazza.

Automotoretro fair in February has vintage cars lined
up by the dozens. Enthusiasts will want to see this festival held in Turin or
show off their own vintage car. In March, CioccolaTO has spectators indulging in
tastings of the chocolate kind. Performances and shows offer entertainment to
attendees.

**Turin
Festivals: April – June **

Il Gioco del Teatro, held in April, attracts theatre
lovers to this international event. Many acting companies perform for
entertainment at this festival in Turin. Also in April is the Turin Marathon
where participants compete in the race to prove endurance among other
competitors from all across the globe.

In May, the Turin International Book Forum has over
100 exhibitions and draws well over 200,000 attendees. The publishing industry
in Italy’s event is the largest to see while visiting Turin. In June, tourists
go to see the Feast of San Giovanni. Traditional costumes are seen in parades
throughout the streets of Turin. Sideshows and exhibitions are held along the
route. Firework displays are included as well as dancing at the Turin
Cathedral.

**Turin
Festivals: July – September**

In July, Centro Interculturale Festival’s 2 day event
features dance and theatre. International shows offer a wide range of activity
and also include workshops. Docume within the Park offer tourists shows that
are seen at Parco del Valentino. The documentaries are focused upon ethical and
social issues across the globe.

In August, the Identita e Differenza festival features
celebrations of identity and diversity. International music and food gives travelers
to this region a sampling of food from across the world. September brings about
the Settembre Musica. This 80 concert music performances offer a range of music
from classical to modern.

**Turin
Festivals: October – December **

Salone del Gusto, held in October, feeds over 150,000
tourists and attendees. Gourmet food is enjoyed during this 5 day Turin
festival. Artissima International Fair of Contemporary Art in November attracts
galleries to display artwork and sculptures from all across the world. In
December, the Christmas Market in Turin festivities kicks off. Celebrate the
season in Turin during this festival with many traditional and modern
decorations and traditions.
