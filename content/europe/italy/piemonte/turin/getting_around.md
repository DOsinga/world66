---
title: "Getting Around in Turin"
type: section
---

Getting around Turin is both easy and affordable, making for a low
stress and enjoyable vacation.

**Air Travel**

There is 1 small airport in Turin - “Citta di Torini – Sandro
Pertini” - that offers over 500 European flights each week. It also has flights
to and from the Milan, just an hour away, which receives planes directly
from the United States. The Turin airport is conveniently linked to the local
railway station by bus service. An alternative to taking a connecting flight
from Milan to Turin is to take a train or rent a car.

**Train Lines**

The train line connects travelers to every major Italian city and can be used
to travel throughout the country. “Porta Nuova” and “Porta Susa” are the 2
main train stations operating within central Turin, and they offer 5
different train lines. Trains run every day from 5 a.m. to 9:20 p.m. and departures
leave every 30 minutes. Single-trip tickets or full day passes for public transportation within Turin can be purchased.

**Bus/Tram Travel**

Turin has numerous tram and bus lines that connect the traveler to the
airport, train station, and to any location within the city. An all-day city
ticket is only 3.5 Euros - making this option very affordable. Over 1,000 buses
and 200 trams operate within the city and run daily from 5 a.m. to midnight. Tour buses and boat tours are also available in Turin.

**Car Travel**

Travelers may use the autostrade, or the highway system of Italy, to get
around. A rental car offers unlimited access to the city and beyond.
