---
title: 'Getting There'
type: section
---

**By air:** Turin-Caselle Airport (Sandro Pertini) is 15 km north of the city centre, connected by a regular bus service. The airport handles European flights; for intercontinental connections, Milan Malpensa is roughly 90 minutes away by bus or train.

**By train:** Porta Nuova is the central station, facing the elegant Piazza Carlo Felice, with direct high-speed services to Milan (under an hour), Rome, Naples, Venice, and Florence. Porta Susa handles many through-trains and the high-speed Frecciarossa services. Both stations connect to the metro and bus network.

**By car:** Turin sits at the junction of several motorways: the A4 to Milan and Venice, the A5 to Aosta, the A6 to the Ligurian coast via Savona, the A21 to Genoa via Asti, and the A32 through the Frejus tunnel to France.

**By bus:** Long-distance coaches arrive at the terminal on Corso Vittorio Emanuele, near Porta Susa. Services connect Turin to destinations across Italy and to cities in France, Spain, and beyond.
