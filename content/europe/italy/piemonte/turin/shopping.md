---
title: 'Shopping'
type: section
---

Turin's forty-plus kilometres of arcaded porticoes make shopping here a distinctly civilised affair, sheltered from rain and summer heat alike. Via Roma is the classic high-fashion street, while Via Garibaldi, the city's longest pedestrian strip, runs from Piazza Castello to Piazza Statuto with more affordable shops. For something different, explore the narrow streets around Via Barbaroux and Via San Tommaso, where old-fashioned shops sell handmade grissini, local wines, haberdashery, and costume jewellery.
