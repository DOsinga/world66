Inside the Museo Egizio building, a monumental staircase leads up to the Galleria Sabauda, the picture gallery of the Royal House of Savoy, enriched by subsequent acquisitions and passed over the Italian State in 1860. The important collection is one of the largest in Italy and includes, besides the works of art by Piedmontese and Italian masters (Fra Angelico, Guercino and Paolo Veronese, Andrea Mantegna, Filippo Lippi, Antonio and Pietro Pollaiolo, among others), Italy's most important collection of Flemish and Dutch paintings that includes works by Van Eyck, Memling, Rembrandt and Van Dyck.
