Turin is located in northern Italy and is a city of major business and cultural prosperity. In **Turin, when to go **may
depend on your preference of seasons and temperatures. Regardless of
when you decide to visit Turin, it is surely to be a fascinating and
enjoyable experience. Turin is a well-known cosmopolitan city with a
flourishing economy and rich history and customs. Read on to learn more
about the climate of Turin, and the best time to visit this city.

**Humid Subtropical Climate**

Turin
experiences a humid subtropical climate, but the city's close proximity
to mountains may cause weather characteristics that vary. In contrast
to the regions of the coast of Italy that have Mediterranean climates, Turin has
cold and dry winters and mild or hot summers. Summers in the plains may
be more hot than in the hills of this city.

Rainfall is most
common in autumn and spring. The month of May typically experiences the
greatest amount of rain. In the hottest summer months of Turin, the
rainfall tends to be less common, but usually produces thunderstorms
when it does occur. Winter and autumn months can bring about large
banks of fog that form in the plains. The fog is very thick at times,
making visibility difficult.

**Temperatures in Turin**

The
city of Turin typically has cold winters and hot summers, though not
too extreme on either end. Summer temperatures average around 24
degrees Celsius, and July is typically the warmest month. Winter
temperatures are cold, but they rarely drop below zero at any point.
The average winter temperature is around 3 degrees C. January is
usually the coldest month. Spring experiences very mild temperatures,
and often is the ideal time to visit. Autumn is mild as well, but often
humid and foggy.

**What to Wear in Turin**

Turin is
moderately cold in the winter months, so travelers visiting during this
time should wear heavy clothing and warm winter coats and accessories.
Autumn is generally mild in temperature but has the strong possibility
of rain, so visitors should have the appropriate rain gear on hand.
Summer in Turin is mild to hot, but comfortable. Travelers should wear
light-weight and breathable clothing during these months. Spring
weather is very mild in this area, so lighter apparel may be desired. Rainfall is likely to occur during spring so it is important to have the proper rain gear.
