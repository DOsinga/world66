---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Lecce's Baroque churches and palaces are the main draw. Most of the city's highlights are clustered in the old centre, and the best approach is simply to walk — you'll stumble on something remarkable every few minutes. Here are the unmissable ones.

**Santa Croce**

The most exuberant example of Leccese Baroque, with a façade covered in fantastic monsters, caryatids, and writhing ornamentation. Inside, the altar by Zimbalo is spectacular. This is the church to see in Lecce.

address: Piazza Castro Mediano

opening_hours: 9am-1pm, 5:30pm-7:30pm

category: Sight

**Duomo**

Completed in 1670 to a design by Giuseppe Zimbalo, one of the master architects of the Lecce style. The interior is mostly 18th-century. The cathedral faces a handsome arcaded piazza.

address: Piazza del Duomo

category: Sight

**Church of Sant'Irene**

A fine example of Roman Baroque, just off Piazza Sant'Oronzo. Quieter than Santa Croce and worth a look for the contrast.

address: Just off Piazza Sant'Oronzo

category: Sight

**Palazzo Vescovile**

The Bishop's palace, built in 1632 and remodelled several times since. The open portico once held market-day shops. Forms part of the harmonious ensemble around the Duomo piazza.

address: Piazza del Duomo

category: Sight
