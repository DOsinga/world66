Toritto is a small town in the Murgia hills about 20 minutes by train from [Bari](/europe/italy/puglia/bari), known above all for its almonds. The Filippo Ferrara almond, a local variety recognised by Slow Food as a Presidium product, is considered among the finest in Italy — ground into paste for pastries, roasted, or pressed into oil.

The countryside around Toritto is dotted with trulli and old masserie (fortified farmhouses), and makes for a pleasant half-day drive or cycle through olive groves and almond orchards. The town itself is quiet and unhurried, with a compact historic centre and a few trattorias serving straightforward Pugliese cooking. It is not a destination in its own right, but a worthwhile detour for anyone exploring the inland side of Puglia beyond the coast.
