title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Alghero's medieval old town is the main draw and the best starting point. Walk the complete circuit of the city walls — it takes about 45 minutes at a stroll, with views over the bay and the rocky coastline to the south. The seven defensive towers that survive are each accessible; the Torre di Sulis on the seafront hosts occasional art exhibitions.

**Neptune's Grotto (Grotta di Nettuno)**

The most dramatic excursion from Alghero. The grotto is a sea cave 24 km north of town at the foot of the Capo Caccia headland. You can reach it by boat (trips run from Alghero port) or by descending the 654 steps of the Escala del Cabirol carved into the cliff face. Inside, the cave extends for 4 km, with a lake, stalactite and stalagmite formations, and extraordinary acoustics. Open daily; the boat trip takes about 45 minutes each way and is the more scenic option.

**Capo Caccia and the Punta Giglio nature reserve**

The headland above Neptune's Grotto is a protected natural area with walking trails through macchia and limestone cliffs. The views from the cape are spectacular. A trail also leads down to a small beach at Cala dell'Inferno below the cliffs.

**Diving and snorkelling**

The waters around Alghero and Capo Caccia are part of a marine protected area. Several dive centres in town offer guided dives among Posidonia meadows, rocky walls, and the occasional wreck. Coral harvesting in the area historically produced some of the finest red coral in the Mediterranean — look for coral jewellery in the old town shops.

**Old town**

The Cathedral of Santa Maria, begun in the 16th century in Catalan Gothic style, is worth a look for its five-sided apse and the late-Catalan tower. The Church of San Francesco, a couple of blocks away, has a Romanesque cloister that hosts summer concerts.
