Cagliari is the biggest town of the island and has been its capital since Roman times. For many travelers it is their arrival point in Sardinia - there are ferry services to [Naples](/europe/italy/campania/naples), [Sicily](/europe/italy/sicily) and other towns.


 The old city centre is protected by a set of Pisan fortifications and it can be explored on foot easily. The most important sights are the ** Bastione San Remy** on the Piazza Costituzione a great bastion whihc gives you access to the old city and from where you have a great view over the harbour, the **Cathedral** on Piazza Palazzo dating from the 13-th century as well as a a few old towers such as the ** Torre San Pancrazio** and the **Torre dell'Elefante** which form part of the defensive structures of the city.


 Outside of the old city centre the main sight is the **Dan Saturnino** church the oldest Christian church on the ilsand dating all the way from the 5-th century. It's on the Via Dante.
