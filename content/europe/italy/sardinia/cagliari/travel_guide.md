The **Cagliari travel guide** will inform you about all that you need to know when visiting the capital city of the Italian island of Sardinia. Cagliari is rich in ancient history and is abundant in art and culture. Besides, Cagliary is known for its climate and abundance in parks and other green zones. Let's take a look at a few important facts about Cagliari.

**Climate**

The Italian city of Cagliari enjoys a Mediterranean climate. Thus, the summers here are dry and hot, while winters are very mild. Tourists come here at all times of the year because of Cagliari's good climate. In fact, Cagliari is often known as the Southern California of the Mediterranean, because of similarities in both of their climate patterns.

**Green City**

Cagliari is one of the greenest cities in the whole of Italy. This is because the city enjoys a very low level of smog. Further, Cagliari also houses many green zones in the form of parks and gardens, all of which have well-maintained plants and flowers. Some of the notable parks that one should not miss include the Monte Urpinu Park, the Park of San Michele Hill, the Terramaini Park and the Ex-vetreria pirri Park.

**Beaches**

Cagliari is also known for its beaches, most of which are very pristine and clean. Cagliari's main beach, however, remains the beach of Poetto, which stretches for over 8 km. Other dreamy beaches that one shouldn't give a skip are Cala Mosca, Call Cipolla, Sant Andrea and Stella di Mare.

**Gastronomy**

Cagliari is also known for its rare food. Usually fish and other seafood are a part of most dishes in Cagliari. Although Cagliari's cuisine has its own taste, traces of Spanish cuisine can also be found in this Italian city's food. Besides its food, Cagliari is also known for its delicious wines, which always play a large role in a Cagliaritanian's meal.

**Sightseeing**

Cagliari is filled with interesting sights to enjoy. The Sanctuary of Our Lady of Bonaria was built adopting a gothic style. Word has it that it houses a wooden Madonna statue that was once thrown off a Spanish ship and landed at the foot of the Bonaria hill, where the sanctuary is now situated. Cagliari's Bastione San Remy is also worthy of any tourists' visit. Amongst the many sightseeing zones in Cagliari, Castello di San Michele, Basilica di San Saturnino and the Cattedrale di Santa Maria are all noteworthy.
