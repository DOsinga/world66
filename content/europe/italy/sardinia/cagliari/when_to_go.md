**Spend an Excellent Summer on the Shores of ****Cagliari**

If you are planning a visit to **Cagliari****, when to go** to the city may be one of the first questions that come into your mind. Except for its magnificent sights (which can be seen throughout the year), Cagliari is popular with its splendid beaches and excellent water sport opportunities. That&rsquo;s why, to take the most of your trip, you should definitely consider visiting Cagliari in the summer. Here&rsquo;s what the weather offers in the summer, as well as through the rest of the year.

**Summer**

Typical for its Mediterranean climate, Cagliari experiences hot and dry summers. The temperatures start increasing early in May and throughout the summer they have average values of 30 degrees Celsius (86 degrees Fahrenheit). This is excellent for having a long day of swimming, sunbathing or wind surfing. However, do remember to use sunscreen, as the sun beams can be quite dangerous during the afternoon hours. And if you feel it&rsquo;s too hot, you can always take a walk in the nearby mountain parks, where it is much cooler.

**The Rest of the Year**

The weather during the rest of the year stays mild even in the winter. Average winter temperatures are usually 15 degrees Celsius (59 degrees Fahrenheit), so it&rsquo;s not cold at all, and you can still walk around the city and see its sights. Those who don't like the winter will surely enjoy the stay in Cagliari, as snowing is almost impossible here, with the temperatures rarely dropping below 0 degrees Celsius (32 degrees Fahrenheit).
