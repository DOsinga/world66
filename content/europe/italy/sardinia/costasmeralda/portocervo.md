Porto Cervo is the principal resort of the Costa Smeralda, built from scratch in the 1960s as a private development. The marina is one of the most prestigious in the Mediterranean and during summer fills with superyachts. The old town is a pedestrian enclave of boutiques, restaurants, and cafes arranged on terraces above the port. The surrounding beaches — Capriccioli, Liscia Ruja, and Cala di Volpe — are among the finest on the island.
