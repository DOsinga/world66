---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The beaches dominate: Capriccioli, Liscia Ruja, and Cala di Volpe are all within easy reach and consistently rank among Sardinia's best. Capo Ferro, the rocky promontory with a lighthouse that marks the town from the sea, is worth a short drive for views over the coast. The Church of Stella Maris above the old port, designed by Michele Busiri Vici in the 1960s, holds a notable painting attributed to El Greco. The Pevero Golf Club at Cala di Volpe, designed by Robert Trent Jones Sr, is one of Italy's most celebrated courses. The marina itself — lined with some of the largest private yachts in the world during July and August — is worth a walk regardless of whether you have a boat.
