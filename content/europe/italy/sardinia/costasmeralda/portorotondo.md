Porto Rotondo is the second resort village of the [Costa Smeralda](/europe/italy/sardinia/costasmeralda), less famous than [Porto Cervo](/europe/italy/sardinia/costasmeralda/portocervo) but similar in character — a purpose-built marina resort aimed at wealthy Italian and international visitors.

The village is built around a small piazza and harbour. The wooden church of San Lorenzo, designed by Andrea Cascella, has a distinctive carved wooden interior. The beaches nearby — Spiaggia di Porto Rotondo and Cala Ruja — are among the area's best.

Like Porto Cervo, Porto Rotondo is most lively in July and August when the jet-set crowd arrives. Outside those months it can feel somewhat empty.
