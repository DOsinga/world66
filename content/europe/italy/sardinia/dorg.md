Dorgali sits on the slopes of Monte Bardia in the heart of the Barbagia region, about 15 km south of [Nuoro](/europe/italy/sardinia/nuoro). It's one of the best places in Sardinia to find traditional crafts — the town is known for its leather work, ceramics, and woven textiles, which you can buy directly from artisans' workshops.

The town has a good archaeological museum (Museo Civico) with finds from local nuraghi and the nearby Tiscali site. Tiscali itself — an ancient Nuragic village hidden inside a collapsed cave on the flanks of Monte Tiscali — is one of the most atmospheric archaeological sites on the island, reached by a demanding two-hour hike through the Lanaittu valley.

Dorgali is also the gateway to the Gola Su Gorropu, a dramatic limestone canyon sometimes called Europe's deepest. The gorge entrance can be reached on foot from the Flumineddu river valley. Walking conditions require proper footwear.

The coast below Dorgali — the Gulf of Orosei — is spectacular. Cala Goloritzé, Cala Biriola, and Cala Sisine are among the finest coves on the island, all accessible by boat from Cala Gonone (8 km downhill from Dorgali).
