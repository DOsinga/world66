---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Shopping in Olbia is low-key. The Corso Umberto and the streets around it have a mix of clothing boutiques, souvenir shops, and local food stores.

The Saturday and Tuesday markets are the most interesting option — street stalls selling crafts, textiles, and food from across Sardinia, as well as Olbia's local specialities: olive oil, pecorino, and mountain honey. Woven goods and ceramics from inland Sardinian towns also appear at these markets.

For food shopping, the small local supermarkets near the port are handy for ferry supplies. If you want a better selection of Sardinian food products (bottarga, pane carasau, local wines and cheeses), look for specialist delicatessens on the side streets off Corso Umberto.
