Arzachena is a small town 25 km north of [Olbia](/europe/italy/sardinia/olbia), known primarily as the gateway to the [Costa Smeralda](/europe/italy/sardinia/costasmeralda). The town itself is unremarkable, but the surrounding area contains a remarkable concentration of Nuragic and Neolithic monuments.

The most important sites are the Li Muri necropolis (Sardinia's oldest known cemetery, around 4,000 BC), the Giants' Graves of Li Loghi and Coddu Vecchiu (Bronze Age collective tombs, their curved stone facades still impressive), and the Nuraghe Albucciu (an unusually well-preserved nuraghic tower). Most sites are within a few kilometres of each other and are signposted from the town. Admission is charged at the main sites; a combined ticket is available.

Arzachena also has a small town centre worth a brief stop — the granite mushroom rock (Fungo di Arzachena) balanced on a hill above town is a local landmark.
