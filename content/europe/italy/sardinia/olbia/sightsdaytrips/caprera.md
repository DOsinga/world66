Caprera is a small island connected by causeway to [La Maddalena](/europe/italy/sardinia/lamaddelena), 3 km by sea from Palau. The island is covered in fragrant Mediterranean scrub — cork oak, juniper, and myrtle — and has several beautiful beaches, particularly on the eastern coast.

The main attraction is the Casa Museo di Garibaldi, the house where Giuseppe Garibaldi lived from 1855 and where he died in 1882. The house is preserved more or less as he left it — personal belongings, military memorabilia, his red shirt. His tomb is in the garden. It's an unexpectedly moving place and far less visited than its historical significance warrants.

Caprera was a military zone for most of the 20th century, which is why it remains so unspoiled.
