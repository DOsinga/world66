The La Maddalena Archipelago lies 3 km by sea from [Palau](/europe/italy/sardinia/olbia/sightsdaytrips/palau). Regular ferries cross in about 15 minutes. The archipelago — declared a national park in 1996 — contains around 60 islands and islets with some of the most beautiful beaches and clearest water in the Mediterranean.

The main island (La Maddalena) has a pleasant small town with good seafood restaurants and bars. Day-trip boats from the town visit the smaller islands, including Budelli (famous for its pink-sand beach, now a protected nature reserve where you can no longer land), Spargi, and Santa Maria. The island of Caprera, connected by causeway, was the home and last refuge of Giuseppe Garibaldi — his house-museum is open to visitors.
