Palau is a small town 40 km north of [Olbia](/europe/italy/sardinia/olbia), best known as the ferry port for the [La Maddalena Archipelago](/europe/italy/sardinia/lamaddelena). Ferries run every 15 minutes in summer, less frequently out of season; the crossing takes 15 minutes.

The town has good beaches — Porto Raphael and Punta Nera are both excellent — and the area around Porto Faro (8 km northeast) has some of the most dramatic granite rock formations on the island, including the famous Roccia dell'Orso (Bear Rock), a granite outcrop weathered into the shape of a bear. There's a small archaeological site nearby with Nuragic ruins and a viewing platform over the archipelago.

Palau is a pleasant base for exploring the far north of Sardinia, less pricey than the Costa Smeralda and with easy access to the archipelago.
