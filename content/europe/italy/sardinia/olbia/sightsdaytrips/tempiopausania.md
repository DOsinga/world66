Tempio Pausania is a small granite town 54 km from [Olbia](/europe/italy/sardinia/olbia), in the highland area of Gallura north of Monte Limbara. The drive there is worth it for the scenery — the train through Gallura is one of the scenic Trenino Verde routes if you want to go slowly.

The town centre is built almost entirely in grey granite — a distinctive, sober look that sets it apart from coastal Sardinia. The Piazza Gallura has a good collection of granite-clad civic buildings. Tempio produces a well-regarded Muscat wine (Moscato di Tempio) and also has a small production of vernaccia.

The town hosts a famous Carnival in February, one of the more elaborate on the island, with traditional masks and costumes.
