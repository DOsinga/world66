---

Olbia is primarily a transit hub — most visitors pass through on their way to the Costa Smeralda or the La Maddalena Archipelago. But the town itself is worth half a day, and the surrounding area has some excellent day-trip options.

**Old town**

The Basilica di San Simplicio, on the piazza of the same name, is the most important medieval church in northeastern Sardinia — a plain 11th-century Romanesque structure in grey granite. Inside, Roman and early Christian inscriptions are built into the walls and columns. It feels appropriately unshowy for a building this old.

The town's ancient origins (it was a Greek colony, later expanded by the Romans) are visible at the small archaeological site near the port, where Roman-era street remains have been excavated.

**Tavolara**

A huge limestone table mountain rising 566 metres out of the sea, 15 km from Olbia. Boat trips run from Olbia and Porto San Paolo. The rock looms over a marine protected area where the water is extraordinarily clear. You can snorkel or dive around the base. The island has a permanent population of about five people and hosts an outdoor film festival in summer.

**Nuragic sites near Arzachena**

The area around Arzachena, 25 km from Olbia, has a remarkable concentration of prehistoric monuments: the Li Muri necropolis, the Giants' Graves of Li Loghi and Coddu Vecchiu, and the Nuraghe Albucciu. Most sites are within a few kilometres of each other and can be visited on a half-day drive.

**Day trips**

For the [La Maddalena Archipelago](/europe/italy/sardinia/lamaddelena), ferries leave from Palau (40 km north). For the [Costa Smeralda](/europe/italy/sardinia/costasmeralda), it's a 30-minute drive south.
