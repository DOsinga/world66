Acireale is a small Baroque town on Sicily's eastern coast between Catania and Taormina, perched on a lava cliff above the Ionian Sea. The town is known for its ornate churches, its thermal baths, and one of Sicily's most colourful carnival celebrations, featuring elaborate papier-mache floats.
