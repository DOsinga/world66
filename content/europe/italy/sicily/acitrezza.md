Acitrezza is a small fishing village on the Sicilian coast between Catania and Taormina. It is best known for the Faraglioni, a cluster of dramatic basalt sea stacks just offshore that legend attributes to the Cyclops Polyphemus hurling rocks at Odysseus. Giovanni Verga set his novel *I Malavoglia* here, and the village still has an authentic, unhurried character.
