Catania is a baroque city at the foot of Mount Etna. Although it can appear a bit unfriendly and drab when you first arrive, the city has a lot of charm and many interesting monuments. Built largely from dark lava stone after the catastrophic eruption and earthquake of 1693, it has a brooding atmosphere that grows on you.

The fish market near Piazza del Duomo is one of the great spectacles of Sicilian daily life. Catania is also the starting point for excursions to Mount Etna and the gateway to the eastern coast.
