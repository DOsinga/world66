Messina is the gateway to Sicily for anyone arriving from the mainland by ferry. The city was devastated by an earthquake in 1908 and heavily bombed in World War II, so it lacks the historic centre you find elsewhere on the island. Most travellers pass through quickly, but the Norman-era cathedral and its astronomical clock are worth a stop, and the swordfish here is some of the best in Sicily.
