Milazzo occupies a narrow peninsula jutting into the Tyrrhenian Sea on Sicily's northern coast. Most travellers pass through on their way to the [Aeolian Islands](/australiaandpacific/marshallislands) — the hydrofoil terminal here is the main departure point — but the town itself deserves a stop.

The Castello di Milazzo dominates the headland above the old town. It is one of the largest fortified complexes in Sicily, layering Arab walls, Norman towers, and a Spanish-era citadel across a site that has been defended since the Bronze Age. The views from the ramparts stretch across to the Aeolians on a clear day.

Below the castle, the old quarter has a handful of churches and a quiet atmosphere that contrasts with the busier modern town around the port. The Capo di Milazzo promontory beyond the castle offers good walking along cliff paths, with a small bay (Baia del Tono) that is popular for swimming.

Milazzo is about 40 km west of [Messina](/europe/italy/sicily/messina) and well connected by train and bus.
