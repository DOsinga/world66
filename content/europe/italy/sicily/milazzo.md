Milazzo sits on a long rocky peninsula jutting north from the Sicilian coast, about 40 kilometres west of [Messina](/europe/italy/sicily/messina). Most travellers come here for one reason: to catch the hydrofoil to the [Aeolian Islands](/europe/italy/sicily/aeolianislands). What many don't realise is that the town itself is worth a day.

The old upper town is dominated by one of the largest castles in Europe — a walled citadel begun by the Arabs, expanded by the Normans, and finished by the Spanish. Inside the ring of walls lies an entire abandoned borgo with a cathedral, a prison, a parade ground, and sweeping views across the Tyrrhenian towards Stromboli. It's a remarkable place to walk around at sunset.

Below the castle, the seafront along the western side of the peninsula is lined with fish restaurants and bars. At the northern tip, the Capo Milazzo nature reserve has clifftop walks and a small swimming cove at Piscina di Venere, a natural rock pool fed by the sea.

Hydrofoils and ferries to Lipari, Vulcano, Stromboli and the other Aeolian islands leave from the port several times a day in summer, less frequently in winter. Liberty Lines and Siremar are the main operators.
