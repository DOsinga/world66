Motta Sant'Anastasia is a small town about 15 kilometres west of [Catania](/europe/italy/sicily/catania), instantly recognisable by its Norman castle perched dramatically atop a volcanic basalt plug. The dark tower rising from the sheer rock face is one of the most striking sights in the Catania hinterland, and visible from a considerable distance across the plain of the Simeto river.

The castle dates from the eleventh century and was built by Roger I during the Norman conquest of Sicily. You can climb up to the base of the rock for panoramic views stretching from Mount Etna to the coast. The old town clustered around the rock has a quiet, authentic character, with a handsome Chiesa Madre and narrow streets that see very few tourists.

Motta Sant'Anastasia is not a destination in itself, but it makes a worthwhile stop if you are driving between Catania and the interior of Sicily. The town is also close to NAS Sigonella, and many American and NATO families live in the area, giving it a slightly international flavour unusual for a Sicilian country town.
