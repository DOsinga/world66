The private chapel of Roger II and an absolute must-see in Palermo. Every surface glitters with Byzantine mosaics dating from the 1140s, and the wooden muqarnas ceiling is painted with Kufic figures and inscriptions. The chapel sits on the second floor of the Palazzo dei Normanni.

Basilican in form with a nave and two aisles, mosaic floors, and marble-faced walls. The small side aisles have pitch roofs with painted beams. It is one of the finest examples of Arab-Norman art anywhere.
