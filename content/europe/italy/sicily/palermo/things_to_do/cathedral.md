Built by the Normans in 1185 and later enlarged in Baroque style, Palermo's cathedral is one of those architectural mash-ups you only find in this city. The exterior is a gorgeous jumble of Gothic towers and Arab-Norman arches, while the interior was remodelled in neoclassical style. The royal tombs of Frederick II and Roger II are here.
