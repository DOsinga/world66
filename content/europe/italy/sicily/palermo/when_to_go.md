title: "When to Go in Palermo"
type: section
---

Palermo is located in southern Italy within Sicily, and is known for
its culture, architecture, customs, and fascinating history. In **Palermo, when to go **can
be in the summer if you enjoy sunshine and heat, or in the winter if
you prefer a mild temperature and precipitation. Being one of the
warmest cities in the Mediterranean, Palermo truly is a pleasant and
comfortable destination for travelers. Read on the learn more about the
climate to expect while visiting Palermo.

**Mediterranean Climate**

Palermo
has a Mediterranean climate, which means summers are hot and dry while
winters are mild and wet. Palermo is considered one of the warmest
cities in the Mediterranean region and experiences abundant sunshine
throughout the year. This is typically an excellent place to visit if
you enjoy a year-round mild climate that is neither too hot or too
cold. The temperature in Palermo hardly ever gets below 10 degrees
C and most of the time the summer temperatures are only slightly
hot, even during peak summer months. The months from May to June are
considered the mildest months of the year, with the weather warm and
comfortable, ideal for relaxing on the beach or walking around.
November is generally the wettest month of the year. Even so, rainfall
during this time is fairly low. July and August are the warmest months,
and tend to be quite dry.

**Temperatures in Palermo**

The
average annual air temperature in Palermo is around 18.5 degrees
Celsius. The months of January, February, March, April and December
have the coldest temperatures of the year, but are not intolerable. The
months of May, June, October and November have a mild temperature. The
months of July, August, and September are the hottest months, but still
comfortable.

**What to Wear in Palermo**

Because of
the mild Mediterranean climate of this area, visitors can bring
moderately light-weight clothing throughout the year. During the peak
summer months, one should wear light-weight cottons to keep cool. In
addition, visitors should carry sunscreen and other protection against
the sun during this time, as Palermo is one of the sunniest regions in
Italy. Winter temperatures will be lower, so visitors should have
warmer clothing and light-weight winter coats while traveling in
Palermo during this time. During the months of October and November
when there is a strong possibility of rain, travelers should be sure to
bring the proper rain gear.
