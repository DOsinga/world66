---
title: 'Beaches'
type: section
---

The beaches are the reason most people come to Lampedusa, and they live up to the reputation. The water is warm, shallow in places, and an extraordinary shade of blue-green that looks photoshopped until you see it in person.

**Spiaggia dei Conigli** (Rabbit Beach) is the island's showpiece — a curved bay of white sand backed by low dunes, with water that shifts from pale turquoise to deep cobalt. It's protected as a loggerhead turtle nesting site, so sections are closed off in summer and camping is not allowed. Get there early if you're going in July or August.

**Cala Guitgia** is the most accessible beach, close to town, and the one most families use. **Cala Madonna** is smaller and quieter, reached by a short walk down a rocky path. For real solitude, head to the northern coast where there are several unmarked coves — you'll need a boat or a sturdy pair of legs to find them.
