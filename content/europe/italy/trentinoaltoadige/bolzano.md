Bolzano (Bozen in German) sits at the confluence of the Talvera and Isarco rivers, in a broad valley surrounded by the Dolomites. The city has a distinctly Austrian character — German is co-official with Italian, the architecture is central European, and the food leans toward dumplings and apple strudel rather than pasta. It is the capital of the South Tyrol, a region that only became part of Italy after the First World War.

The old town centres on Piazza Walther, named for the medieval German poet Walther von der Vogelweide. The Gothic Duomo is nearby. The city's star attraction is Ötzi the Iceman at the South Tyrol Museum of Archaeology — a 5,300-year-old mummified man discovered in 1991 in the glacial ice of the Alps, displayed remarkably well preserved.

The surrounding mountains are the main draw for outdoor enthusiasts. The Dolomites are accessible in all directions from Bolzano — cable cars reach the high ground quickly, and there are excellent hiking routes. The mountain huts (rifugi) are generally open from late June through September.
