Chianciano Terme is a spa town in the hills of southern [Tuscany](/europe/italy/tuscany), about 90 km southeast of [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence) and 50 km from [Siena](/europe/italy/tuscany/siena). The town has been known for its thermal waters since Etruscan and Roman times — the springs are said to be particularly good for the liver, which the Italians take seriously.

The old hilltop centre is pleasant for a stroll, with views over the Val di Chiana. But the real draw is the thermal parks: Terme Sensoriali and the outdoor pools of Theia offer a mix of therapeutic and leisure bathing. The town makes a practical base for exploring the surrounding hill towns — [Montepulciano](/europe/italy/tuscany/montepulciano), Pienza, and Montalcino are all within easy reach.
