Cortona is a compact medieval hill town in eastern [Tuscany](/europe/italy/tuscany), perched on a steep slope with sweeping views over the Val di Chiana to Lake Trasimeno. It became internationally famous as the setting for Frances Mayes' memoir *Under the Tuscan Sun*, but the town's appeal long predates the book — the Etruscans built walls here that still stand.

The Piazza della Repubblica and its surrounding streets are full of small restaurants, wine bars and artisan shops. The Museo dell'Accademia Etrusca houses an impressive collection of Etruscan artefacts, and the quiet lanes above the centre lead to the Basilica di Santa Margherita and the Medici fortress, both offering panoramic views. Cortona is small enough to explore in half a day, but the atmosphere — and the wine — tend to make people stay longer.
