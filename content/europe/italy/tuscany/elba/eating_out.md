---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Elba's cooking is what you would expect of a small Tuscan island — simple, seafood-led, and unfussy. The classics are **cacciucco** (a rich fish stew shared with Livorno), **risotto with squid ink**, stockfish **"alla riese"**, *gurguglione* (a summer stew of peppers, aubergines and tomatoes), and wild-fennel risotto. Sweets have their own Elban identity: **schiaccia briaca**, a dense cake packed with pine nuts, raisins and aleatico wine, is the one to try. Local **wines** are taken seriously — look for Aleatico Passito (a sweet red served with dessert), the white Ansonica, and robust reds from Sangiovese. Portoferraio, Porto Azzurro and Capoliveri have the widest choice of restaurants; in summer, book ahead.
