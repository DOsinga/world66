---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**May and June** are the best months. The sea is warm enough to swim, the beach is quiet, prices are at their lowest, and the island is green from the spring rains. Most restaurants and shops are open but not overcrowded.

**July and early August** are busy. The beach fills up, prices roughly double for accommodation, the roads can be slow, and water shortages are an occasional problem across Elba during the August peak. If you come then, book well ahead and accept that Marina di Campo will feel like a resort rather than a village.

**September** is nearly as good as June — the sea is at its warmest, crowds thin out sharply after the Italian school holidays end in early September, and the evenings start to cool pleasantly. A good choice if you want swimming weather without the August crowds.

**October to April:** Most beach-facing businesses close. The permanent population of around 2,000 takes the town back, and the atmosphere changes completely. It is not a dead place — the harbour still functions, and a handful of restaurants stay open year-round — but visitors come for walking and quiet rather than beach holidays.
