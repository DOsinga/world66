Set in Florence during the 1490s — the years of Savonarola's ascendancy and the Medici collapse — and researched with extraordinary thoroughness. Eliot spent months in Florence studying the period before writing it. Dense and unfashionable now, but the portrait of a city torn between humanism and religious fanaticism, and of a woman trying to act morally in an impossible situation, rewards the effort.
