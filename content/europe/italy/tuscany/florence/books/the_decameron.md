---

Ten young Florentines flee the Black Death of 1348 to a villa outside the city and tell each other a hundred stories over ten days. Boccaccio's frame gives you both a vivid account of the plague that devastated Florence and a gallery of medieval Italian life — merchants, clergy, lovers, tricksters. The opening description of the plague-struck city is one of the great pieces of historical writing.
