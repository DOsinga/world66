The Alto Mugello is the mountainous northern edge of the [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence) province, where Tuscany meets Emilia-Romagna across the Apennine ridge. It is deeply rural — small stone villages, chestnut forests, and green valleys cut by streams — and sees very few tourists. The landscape is beautiful in an understated way, ideal for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

The two main centres are [Firenzuola](/europe/italy/tuscany/florenceprovince/altomugelloarea/fiorenzuola), a small town founded by the Florentine Republic in 1332, and [Palazzuolo sul Senio](/europe/italy/tuscany/florenceprovince/altomugelloarea/palazzuolo_sul_sen), a village that has won awards as one of Italy's ideal small towns. Both make quiet bases for exploring the surrounding countryside and its scattering of medieval churches, archaeological sites, and Apennine trails.
