Firenzuola is a small town in the upper Santerno valley, founded by the Florentine Republic in 1332 as a fortified outpost near the Apennine passes. The commune covers a vast area — over 270 square kilometres of mountain territory, making it one of the largest in Tuscany — but the population is tiny, scattered across the valleys and ridges between the Futa Pass and the Emilia-Romagna border.

The town itself was heavily damaged in World War II and rebuilt, so the architecture is modest. The appeal is the landscape: chestnut woods, mountain streams, and walking trails through the Apennines.
