Greve in Chianti sits in the heart of the Chianti Classico wine country, roughly equidistant from [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence) (30 km) and [Siena](/europe/italy/tuscany/siena) (35 km). The town's triangular piazza, lined with arcaded shops and wine bars, is the natural gathering point and a good place to start tasting.

The weekly market fills Greve's piazza on Saturday mornings, while Panzano in Chianti — a smaller hilltop village in the same commune — has its market on Sundays. Throughout the year the area comes alive with local festivals: the Chianti Classico Festival in the second week of September is the main event, but there are also antique markets at Easter and in October, a flower show in May, Panzano's Festa della Stagion Bona on 25 April, and a chestnut festival in Lucolena in late October.

The surrounding countryside is classic Tuscan postcard: rolling vine-covered hills, cypress-lined roads, stone farmhouses. The wine estates welcome visitors for tastings, and the walking and cycling between the villages is superb. Panzano is particularly known for its butcher, Dario Cecchini, who has turned the art of the bistecca fiorentina into a theatrical experience.
