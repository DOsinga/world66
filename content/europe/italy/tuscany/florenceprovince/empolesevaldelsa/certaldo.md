Certaldo is a small town in the Val d'Elsa, south of [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence), split between a modern lower town and a beautifully preserved hilltop village — Certaldo Alto — reached by funicular. The upper town is entirely medieval, with brick towers, narrow streets, and views over the Tuscan hills.

Certaldo Alto is best known as the home of Giovanni Boccaccio (1313–1375), author of the Decameron and one of the founders of Italian literature. His reconstructed house is now the seat of the National Boccaccio Study Centre, with a library and a collection of over 500 works by contemporary artists. His tomb is in the church of Santi Jacopo e Filippo nearby.

The Palazzo Pretorio, built in the 12th century for the Alberti Counts and later used as the seat of the Vicariate, dominates the upper town. Inside and in the adjoining de-consecrated church of Santi Tommaso e Prospero, there are frescoes and sinopites from the 15th and 16th centuries.
