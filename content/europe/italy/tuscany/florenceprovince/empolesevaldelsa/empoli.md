Empoli is a working town in the Arno valley about 30 km west of Florence. It is not a tourist destination in the usual sense, but it has a compact historic centre organised around Piazza Farinata degli Uberti that rewards a short visit.

The most important building on the piazza is the Collegiata di Sant'Andrea, a Romanesque church with a striking green-and-white marble facade. Inside, the adjacent museum houses a collection of Renaissance art that would be the pride of a much larger town, including works by Masaccio and Filippino Lippi. The Palazzo Ghibellino, also on the square, houses the Museo Paleontologico and the town's historical archive.

Empoli appears in documents from the 8th century as a castle, but the town as a civic entity dates from 1119. Today it is a practical place — a transport hub on the Florence-Pisa rail line, handy for day trips to the smaller Tuscan towns of the Valdelsa.
