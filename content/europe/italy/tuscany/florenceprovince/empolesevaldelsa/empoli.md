This town appears in documents of the 8th century as a castle, but the city was recognised in 1119 as organised around the Parish church of Sant’Andrea. Today the town is still organised around Piazza Farinata degli Uberti where the most important historical buildings of the city are to be found: the Palazzo Ghibellino, which houses the Museo Paleontologico and the Archivio Storico, and the Palazzo Pretorio, which was at one time the town hall.
