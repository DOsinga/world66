Montaione is a small medieval hill town in the Valdelsa, halfway between Florence and Volterra. It sees far fewer visitors than its neighbours, which is part of the appeal — this is workaday Tuscany, not postcard Tuscany, though the views from the old centre are postcard-worthy all the same.

The town preserves a compact medieval layout and a pleasant piazza. On the road to Gambassi Terme, about a kilometre out, stands the Villa Da Filicaia, built on the site of a Roman settlement and once gifted by the Medici to the poet Vincenzo da Filicaia. A Roman cistern nearby hints at the area's ancient history. Montaione makes a quiet, affordable base for exploring the Tuscan countryside by car.
