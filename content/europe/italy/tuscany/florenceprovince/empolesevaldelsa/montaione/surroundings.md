On the road to Gambassi, about 1 km far from Montaione, you can find the Villa Da Filicaia at S. Antonio. It stands on the site of the Roman village of Figline. It was presented to the poet Vincenzo da Filicaia by the Medici family, along with a great deal of land and a house in the town.

   The imposing Roman cistern (on the road to Gambassi) goes back to the 2nd century B.C.. It is 27 metres long and 3 wide, divided into three rectangular sections and contained 400 cubic metres of water from the springs of Poggio all'Aglione. Tradition has it that there was once a sumptuous villa belonging to either a Roman patrician or an Etruscan village, which was destroyed in order to build the castle of Figline.



   The district of Iano - California, famous for its onyx quarries and for the working of precious stones, is found 4 km from S. Vivaldo, along the via Volterrana in the valley beneath the ruins of the medieval castles of Pietrina and Camporena.



   The village and the stronghold of Castelfalfi on the spur of a steep hill in the direction of Val d'Era (11 km far from Montaione on the road to Pontedera), were founded around 700 by the Longobard Faolfi (hence the name "Castrum Faolfi", which later became Castelfalfi). The castle was destroyed in 1395 during the war between Florence and Pisa, but reconstructed and crowned by four towers, bastions and solid walls, just as it presents itself today. The Romanesque church of S. Floriano in the village is interesting.



   The pine forested public park of Poggio all'Agione, 461 mts above sea level starts 3 km from Montaione, towards Gambassi. This large green space is pratically a reservation for fauna and species of mushroom extending as far as S. Vivaldo, Volterra and S. Gimignano.
