Bagno a Ripoli is a quiet municipality on the southeastern edge of [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence), spread across gentle hills covered in olive groves and vineyards. It sits along the right bank of the Arno, just five kilometres from the city centre, making it an easy escape from the crowds.

The area has deep medieval roots. It was once the centre of one of the Florentine countryside's historic Leagues — associations of parishes that served as both ecclesiastical and civil districts. The League of Bagno a Ripoli encompassed dozens of parishes, including Santa Maria at Antella and San Donnino at Villamagna, and the old parish of Antella once sat on the important road linking Florence with Arezzo and Rome.

Today the municipality is a patchwork of small villages — Antella, Grassina, Ponte a Niccheri — set among terraced hillsides where Chianti DOCG and extra-virgin olive oil are still produced on small family farms. The landscape is classic Florentine countryside, with Renaissance villas, Romanesque churches, and cypress-lined lanes.
