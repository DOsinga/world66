The Mugello is a broad valley north of Florence, tucked between the Apennines and the Florentine hills. It's Medici country — the family originated here, and they left their mark on almost every village: fortresses, villas, convents rebuilt to their commission. Today the valley is quiet farming and small-town territory, easy to reach from Florence but rarely crowded with tourists.

The main town is **San Piero a Sieve**, a medieval village with a Medici fortress on the hill above it. The surrounding countryside holds a cluster of Medici-commissioned buildings within a short drive of each other, including Trebbio Castle and the Convento di Bosco ai Frati with its Donatello crucifix.

For walkers, the Apennine ridges above the valley offer straightforward day hikes with good views south towards Florence.
