San Piero a Sieve sits in the centre of the Mugello valley, about 30 km north of Florence. It's a small medieval village — population under 4,000 — that gained real importance under the Medici family, who used this valley as their country base before they ruled Florence. The town centre still has its old character intact, with the Palazzo Adami, the Villa di Schifanoia (a former Medici property), and the ancient Pieve di San Pietro.

The big draw is the hilltop Fortezza di San Martino, built on Cosimo de' Medici's orders in the sixteenth century, with a footpath running around the fortifications. From here you can reach Trebbio Castle and the Convento di Bosco ai Frati within a short drive — all Medici-commissioned buildings, each worth a stop.
