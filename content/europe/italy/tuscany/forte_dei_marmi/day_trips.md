---
title: "Day Trips in Forte dei Marmi"
type: section
---

**Forte Dei Marmi day trips **offer plenty of outdoor excursions for the Italian traveler. Whether it's exploring the Ligurian coastline or venturing inland towards the Apennine mountains, possibilities for adventure abound.

#### **Cinque Terre**

The obvious choice for a hiking trip is to head to one of the 5 towns which comprise the Cinque Terre. Accessible by car via the A12 and also by rail or sea, the green-terraced hills that shoot up from the crystal blue waters provide a unique visual delight. A cost of only 5 euros will get you access to a day's worth of hiking through this national park.

#### **Parco Delle Alpi Apuane**

Situated approximately 5 miles inland from Forte Dei Marmi, this park is a nature lover's paradise. Rent a bike from Ciclimaggi on Viale E. Morin and wander the hillsides for some truly stunning views.

#### **Grotta del Vento**

This archeological gem, formed some 20 million years ago, is well worth a visit. The wind cave, an underground complex of caverns, is open to tourists year round with the exception of Christmas Day. Choose from 1 of 3 itineraries, each lasting approximately 1 hour. If you have time, you can choose all 3.

#### **Viareggio**

Another shoreline town about 5 miles south, Viareggio is a tourist haven in the spring and summer months. Concentrated along the boardwalk, you can find good seafood at a number of restaurants. While most of the beaches are private, there is a small public option in the southern part of town. Every Thursday morning the clothes market opens, and you can find some real bargains.
