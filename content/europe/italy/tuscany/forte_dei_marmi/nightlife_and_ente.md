---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Forte dei Marmi"
type: section
---

The Forte Dei Marmi is a seaside paradise found in Tuscany, Italy full of **Forte Dei Marmi Nightlife**. Where it is ever exciting in the day, the night promises endless fun.

**Visit a local pub**

Italians love their alcohol, and there are numerous wine bars and pubs that can be found around the city center of Forte Dei Marmi. The Twiga is one of the most fashionable clubs found in the area, frequented by local and international celebrities who call the town their occasional home. Entry to the club is very exclusive, so be prepared to wait a long time and pay a large entry fee to get in. Those looking for something less upmarket can walk along the streets of the city center, where most of the pubs can be found. Most of these feature some sort of live music from home bands. During Fridays and Saturdays, though, these pubs turn into a mess of rowdy fans and cheering youngsters during the soccer season, when the teams closer to home play. Alcohol flows freely during these weekends, so just sit in any one of these pubs and soak up the atmosphere of very enthusiastic Italian soccer fans.

**Walk the streets**

Most of the sightseeing of the city should be done in the day, but the nights provide a different view of Forte Dei Marmi. Famous for its marble quarries, beautiful architecture and upscale boutiques, the streets of the city are far quieter in the evenings, and exude an elegance that is profoundly Italian yet close to anyone&rsquo;s heart at the same time. Take a walk along the river banks in the famous district of Versilia, and peer into some of the large mansions that can be found there. Those looking for something more exciting in Versilia can always head to the Capaninna Franceschi – the world-famous local club-bar that has hosted and launched some of the careers of the greatest cabaret stars in the world.

**Swing by the Theater**

The Theater of Forte Dei Marmi is one of the most highly frequented spots by the local people, and more often than not, strives to showcase some of Italy&rsquo;s best works of theater and opera. Famed Italian tenor Andrea Bocelli calls the town home, and occasionally plays at the Theater. In addition, plays are commonly put up – and tourists to the area can always pick up cheap tickets to local productions that are still professionally produced and acted out. Those thinking of a night out in the theater should be prepared to dress up though, for the Italians take their night outs here seriously!
