---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Montecatini Terme"
type: section
---

If you've come to Italy for a wild and crazy adventure, Montecatini Terme Nightlife and Entertainment
is probably going to leave you a bit disappointed. This small town in
Tuscany first gained fame for it's mineral hot springs, and is now a
popular tourist destination with many excellent spas. There are things
to do in Montecatini Terme
however, so don't worry too much. Also, nearby you will find the capital
of Tuscany, Florence, a much bigger city that offers plenty of nightlife.

**Stay Local
**

Montecatini Terme
is well known for its spas, so it's hardly surprising that this
small town gets a little sleepy at night. For those of us who don't
mind a more relaxed evening atmosphere, there's still plenty to do. To
find the cafes and bars, your best bet is to head towards the center of town. One advantage to Montecatini Terme
is that it is small enough to walk everywhere, a definite plus if you
have a little to much to drink. If you're trying to decide what to
drink, consider your location: Tuscany is famous for its wines, and Montecatini Terme
is no exception. Even house wines tend to be quite good, and if you
want something a bit more expensive you shouldn't have any trouble
finding a great glass to enjoy.

**Head on Out
**

If you're looking for a more active nightlife you might consider Florence, or Firenze
in Italian. The capital of Tuscany is a city of just under 400,000
people, so it comes as no shock that there is more going on after the
sunset than in Montecatini Terme. Florence is only about 50 minutes away from Montecatini Terme by car, and is also connected by train and bus, so there is nothing stopping you from spending some time here during your Montecatini Terme vacation, even if you don't spend the night. Florence offers some great cafes and bars, including one that is inside the Uffizi Museum, Florence's most famous art museum. You have to buy a ticket to get in, but the bar in the Uffizi is well worth it, as it offers a great view of the Piazza della Signoria, one of the most important squares in Florence. As in Montecatini Terme, the wine is one of the greatest assets of the Florence nightlife, and if you are a connoisseur you can find some truly one-of-a-kind vintages in the more up-scale bars and restaurants.
