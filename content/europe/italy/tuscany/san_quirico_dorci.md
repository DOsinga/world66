San Quirico d'Orcia is a small walled town in the Val d'Orcia, the stretch of southern Tuscany that appears on every other postcard of Italy — rolling hills, lone cypress trees, golden light. The town sits on the old Via Francigena pilgrimage route and has an elegant Romanesque collegiate church (the Collegiata) with three ornate carved portals that are worth a stop on their own.

The Horti Leonini, a formal Italian garden laid out in the 16th century, sits within the town walls and is a peaceful spot with clipped box hedges and views over the valley. San Quirico is a good base for exploring the Val d'Orcia: [Pienza](/europe/italy/tuscany/pienza) and [Montalcino](/europe/italy/tuscany/montalcino) are both a short drive away, and the landscape between them — the famous winding road lined with cypresses near Monticchiello — is one of the most photographed in Italy.
