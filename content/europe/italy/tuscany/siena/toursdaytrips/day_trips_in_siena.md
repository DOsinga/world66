There are a lot of possibilities for travellers in Siena. You'll have at least two days to the visit of the city. The famous Piazza del Campo, with the town building and the tall Tower of Mangia, the imposing Cathedral made of white and black marble, the medieval alleys, the fountains. It is worth to get lost in the historical center that can be crossed from one side to the other in half an hour by foot.
What can I say 'bout outskirtses: Monteriggioni is famous for his entourage of intact medieval walls, Colle val d'Elsa and Casole d'Elsa are attractive ancient villages. The Chianti, with his sweet hills, castles and vineyards is one of most famous places in the world for wine production. We can go on then with the severe abbeys of St. Galgano (which roof has collapsed) and of Sant'Antimo (still in use) in which still monks sing Gregorian songs to the dawn. And then Pienza, famous for cheeses production and Montalcino, where is produced the most delightful and appreciated wine of Tuscany: the Brunello.
Yuo can visit as well rivers, forests and natural riseve in which it is not hard to meet fallow deer, roes, bucks, wild boars and rates.
A week in Siena and it's countryside will be as soon as enough to visit all of its artistic and natural beauties, but anyway, if you want a help to organize your stay in Tuscany, visit www.2beinsiena.com
