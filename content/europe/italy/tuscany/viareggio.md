Viareggio is the largest town on the Versilia coast in northern Tuscany — an old seaside resort and boating centre with a long sand beach lined with private bathing establishments (you can buy a day pass) and a boardwalk packed with designer shops and cafés. The town's Carnival is one of the biggest in Italy, with enormous papier-mâché floats parading through the streets each February.

The Versilia makes a superb base for exploring Tuscany. [Lucca](/europe/italy/tuscany/lucca), the beautiful walled city, is a short drive inland. [Pisa](/europe/italy/tuscany/pisa) is 20 minutes south by car or train, and [Florence](/europe/italy/tuscany/florence) is about an hour away. International flights arrive at Pisa airport.

Along the coast, Forte dei Marmi is where wealthy Italians and Europeans spend their summers — a small, elegant town with beautiful beaches and excellent shopping. The Wednesday and Saturday outdoor markets sell lace, handicrafts, and goods from nearby stores.

Just inland from the coast, the marble mountains begin. Pietrasanta is where Michelangelo sourced his marble from the nearby quarries, and the town remains home to artists and sculptors from around the world who work in its marble workshops. Twenty minutes further up the road, the quarries of Carrara — where marble has been mined for over two thousand years — are a remarkable sight in themselves.
