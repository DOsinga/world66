Gubbio is one of Umbria's finest medieval towns, spread along the foothills of Monte Ingino about 40 km north of [Perugia](/europe/italy/umbria/perugia) and a short distance from [Assisi](/europe/italy/umbria/assisi). Its medieval walls, towers, and palaces are remarkably intact, and a walk through the old town feels like a genuine step back into the Middle Ages. The Piazza Grande, overlooked by the stern Gothic Palazzo dei Consoli, is one of the most impressive medieval squares in Italy.

Gubbio has been producing distinctive ceramics since the sixteenth century, and the botteghe (workshops) selling local pottery are worth browsing. The town is also famous for its **tartufo** (truffle) cuisine — white truffle in particular appears on menus throughout the year, and the local wines are excellent accompaniments.

Every May, Gubbio hosts the Corsa dei Ceri, one of Italy's most dramatic and ancient festivals, in which teams of men race through the streets carrying enormous wooden candles (ceri) up the steep slope to the Basilica of Sant'Ubaldo on the summit of Monte Ingino. A cable car runs to the same summit for those preferring a gentler ascent and panoramic views over the valley below.
