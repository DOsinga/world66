Gubbio is one of Umbria's finest medieval towns, spread along the foothills of Monte Ingino about 40 km north of [Perugia](/europe/italy/umbria/perugia) and a short distance from [Assisi](/europe/italy/umbria/assisi). Its medieval walls, towers, and palaces are remarkably intact, and a walk through the old town feels like a genuine step back into the Middle Ages. The Piazza Grande, overlooked by the stern Gothic Palazzo dei Consoli, is one of the most impressive medieval squares in Italy.

Gubbio has been producing distinctive ceramics since the sixteenth century, and the botteghe (workshops) selling local pottery are worth browsing. The town is also famous for its **tartufo** (truffle) cuisine — white truffle in particular appears on menus throughout the year, and the local wines are excellent accompaniments.

Every May, Gubbio hosts the Corsa dei Ceri, one of Italy's most dramatic and ancient festivals, in which teams of men race through the streets carrying enormous wooden candles (ceri) up the steep slope to the Basilica of Sant'Ubaldo on the summit of Monte Ingino. A cable car runs to the same summit for those preferring a gentler ascent and panoramic views over the valley below.

Gubbio is one of Umbria's finest medieval towns, spread along the foothills of Monte Ingino with a commanding view of the valley below. It lies about 40 km north of [Perugia](/europe/italy/umbria/perugia) and a short drive from [Assisi](/europe/italy/umbria/assisi).

The town has been called the most beautiful medieval city in Europe, and while that is a bold claim, it is hard to argue when you walk the steep grey stone streets within its perfectly preserved walls. Almost every building has medieval origins. The **Palazzo dei Consoli**, a massive Gothic civic palace dominating the main square, houses the Eugubine Tablets — seven bronze tablets inscribed in the Umbrian language around the 3rd century BC, among the most important documents of ancient Italy. The **Palazzo Ducale** and the **Roman theatre** below the old town are also worth visiting.

Gubbio has a strong artisan ceramic tradition dating to the 16th century, and the workshops still produce fine pieces. The local cuisine revolves around **tartufo** (truffle) — both black and the prized white truffle — and the wine is excellent. Every May, the **Corsa dei Ceri** (Race of the Candles) sees teams carry enormous wooden structures shaped like candles through the streets at a dead run — one of Italy's most spectacular and chaotic festivals.
