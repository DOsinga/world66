Spoleto is a hilltop town in southeastern Umbria that has been accumulating layers of history for nearly three thousand years. Roman remains sit beneath medieval walls, Romanesque churches stand next to baroque palazzi, and the whole thing perches dramatically above the Tessino valley with the Apennines as a backdrop.

The showpiece is the Ponte delle Torri, a breathtaking 13th-century aqueduct that spans a deep gorge on ten soaring arches — 230 metres long and 80 metres high. Walking across it, with the wooded ravine falling away below, is one of those moments that stays with you. The Duomo, with its mosaic facade and Filippo Lippi frescoes, is another highlight, and the town is full of smaller Romanesque churches worth ducking into.

Every summer since 1958, Spoleto has hosted the Festival dei Due Mondi (Festival of Two Worlds), an international arts festival of opera, theatre, dance, and music that fills the town for two weeks in late June and early July. Even outside the festival, Spoleto is a rewarding place to wander — compact enough to explore on foot, with good restaurants serving Umbrian cooking and enough quiet corners to feel like you have the place to yourself.
