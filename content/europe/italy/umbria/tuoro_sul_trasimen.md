Tuoro sul Trasimeno is a quiet hilltop village on the northern shore of Lake Trasimeno, in the province of Perugia. It sits right on the border between [Umbria](/europe/italy/umbria) and [Tuscany](/europe/italy/tuscany), and the landscape around it — olive groves, vineyards, and the wide blue lake — belongs to both.

The village is best known for the Battle of Lake Trasimene in 217 BC, one of the worst defeats in Roman history, when Hannibal ambushed and destroyed a Roman army in the hills north of town. A marked trail follows the route of the battle through the countryside, with information panels at key points. The Museo della Battaglia in the village centre tells the story with maps, weapons, and archaeological finds.

The lake itself is shallow, warm, and good for swimming in summer. The Lido di Tuoro has a small beach, and ferries cross to Isola Maggiore, a tiny inhabited island with medieval streets and a lace-making tradition. Tuoro makes a peaceful base for exploring the lake and the hill towns of both Umbria and Tuscany.
