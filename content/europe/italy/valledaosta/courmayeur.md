Courmayeur is the most fashionable of Valle d'Aosta's resorts, popular with the smart set from [Milan](/europe/italy/lombardia/milan) and [Turin](/europe/italy/piemonte/turin) who can reach it in under two hours by motorway. Its appeal is less about the skiing — the runs are predominantly intermediate, and a keen slope-basher can cover the mountain in a day — and more about the total package: the scenery is spectacular, the village has a handsome stone centre that hasn't been entirely swallowed by chalet-resort blandness, and the nightlife is lively by Alpine standards.

The Mont Blanc massif dominates the resort, and in summer it becomes a base for serious mountaineers and walkers. The Skyway Monte Bianco cable car rises to 3,466 m on the Italian-French border, from where you can look out over a panorama of peaks or continue by cable car across the Vallée Blanche to [Chamonix](/europe/france/alpes/chamonix). In summer the same lifts serve a network of high-altitude walking routes with views of the glacier system above the valley.

The Mont Blanc tunnel provides a direct route to Chamonix, making cross-border day trips straightforward for those based here.
