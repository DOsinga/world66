Bibione is a seaside resort on the northern Adriatic coast, about an hour's drive east of [Venice](/europe/italy/veneto/venice). The town exists almost entirely for beach tourism — an eight-kilometre stretch of wide, fine sand backed by a solid wall of hotels, apartments, and holiday parks. It is extremely popular with German and Austrian families, and in summer the beach is packed.

The sand is genuinely good — broad, clean, and gently shelving, which makes it ideal for small children. The eastern end of the beach, Bibione Pineda, is quieter and backed by a pine forest. A thermal spa complex (Bibione Terme) draws visitors for its hot springs. Beyond the beach, there is not much to see in Bibione itself, but the lagoon behind the resort is a protected nature area with birdwatching opportunities, and the historic towns of [Portogruaro](/europe/italy/veneto/portogruaro) and Concordia Sagittaria are a short drive inland.
