Lido di Jesolo is a beach resort town on the Adriatic, about 40 kilometres northeast of Venice. It's not trying to be anything other than what it is: 15 kilometres of sandy beach, a string of hotels, and the infrastructure to keep a large number of summer visitors comfortable and entertained. It does this job very well.

The beach itself is the reason to come. Organised into private concessions, most of it is well-maintained, with lifeguards, sun loungers, and beach bar service. The water is calm and warm in summer. Aqualandia water park and a few other family attractions fill the gaps when the beach isn't enough.

Jesolo is also a useful base for visiting Venice. The lagoon city is 40 minutes by bus, and hotel prices here can be less than half what you'd pay on the island. Off-season — spring and autumn — the crowds thin out, prices drop further, and the town has a genuinely pleasant quiet to it. In winter it's largely shut.
