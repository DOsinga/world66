Lido di Jesolo is a beach resort on the Adriatic coast of the Veneto, about 40km northeast of [Venice](/europe/italy/veneto/venice). It has 15km of fine sandy beach backed by a dense strip of hotels and tourist infrastructure — gelaterias, restaurants, beach clubs, and bars. In summer it is packed; in winter it is essentially closed.

The beach itself is the point. It is well-organised, kept clean, and set up for long lazy days: umbrella and sunbed rentals, beachside cafes, and calm, shallow water. It is a classic Italian seaside resort rather than a destination with cultural depth, but it works very well for what it is. The great advantage is [Venice](/europe/italy/veneto/venice), 40 minutes away by bus or boat — one of the world's great day trips.
