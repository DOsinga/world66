---
title: "Day Trips in Lido di Jesolo"
type: section
---

The Adriatic town of Lido di Jesolo is not only a destination in
itself because of its sandy beaches, cafes, restaurants and
generally lively ambiance. It's also the perfect spot from which
to venture out for day trips, and there are so many places to explore.

**Venice**

With its wondrous canals and architecture, Venice is
definitely one place visitors will want to see. Only 40 minutes away,
this city beckons with its excellent museums, many historic churches
and trendy cafes and restaurants, as well as upscale designer shopping.

The idea of sitting in St. Mark’s Plaza sipping a cappuccino or
having a photograph taken with the plaza’s resident pigeons sitting on
a visitor’s head and arms, is a memory worth having. Taking a
gondola ride through many of the charming canals that connect the
different parts of Venice is also a must.

Another part of Venice that is a popular spot is Murano Island, where
glass blowers create beautiful items using the skills that have been
passed down to them from generation to generation.

**Padua**

Padua is less than an hour from Lido di Jesolo,
and has distinguished architecture spanning from the medieval and
renaissance periods to modern times. Its most important attraction is
the Scrovegni Chapel, which was created by Giotto and is considered to
be his masterpiece. Visitors will be impressed by the cobbled streets
that speak of this city’s past, as well as the lively market places that
have attractive local goods for sale.

**Verona**

Not far away from Padua is the city of William Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet ,
and a destination for many tourists anxious to see the balcony where
Juliet supposedly stood and voiced her famous “A rose by any other
name…” soliloquy. It's also a city for opera lovers with its Roman
Arena, where operas and other concerts are performed. The medieval city
also has many archeological sites. All of this, plus the stone
streets lined with palazzos, will enchant the visitor.

**Lake Garda**

Close to Verona is Lake Garda, the largest lake in Italy, and also
one of its loveliest. Dotted with castles and sandy beaches, it's a
popular vacation spot and one where visitors will find plenty to do,
including taking a ferry ride across the lake or exploring the many
towns along its shores.
