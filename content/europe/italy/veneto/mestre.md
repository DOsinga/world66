Mestre is the mainland half of Venice — a working Italian city that most visitors blow through on their way to the lagoon. That's a mistake, or at least a missed opportunity. Hotels here cost a fraction of Venice prices, the restaurants are better value, and the bar scene actually caters to locals rather than tourists.

The Freedom Bridge (Ponte della Libertà) connects Mestre to Venice in under ten minutes by train, so using Mestre as a base makes obvious logistical sense. Piazza Ferretto is the town centre: a lively square with an 11th-century watchtower, lined with cafes and restaurants. Parco San Giuliano, on the edge of the lagoon, offers a clear view of Venice across the water and doubles as an outdoor concert venue in summer.

Mestre has no great art to speak of — everything worth seeing on that front is across the bridge. What it does have is an authentic northern Italian city going about its daily business, which after a few days of tourist-choked Venice can feel like a genuine relief.
