title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

**Mestre Beaches** are a good place to relax, enjoy the sun and
the waves. There are many different beaches near Mestre and most of
these beaches can be reached by train, vaporetta or water taxi. No
matter what choice of transportation people choose, they will be pleased
once they reach the beach.

**Lido Di Jesolo**

The word "lido" is Italian for "seashore." The beach has different
sections; some of these sections are public beaches and some are
private. Both sections rent out parasols and resting chairs. Tourists
are recommended to use the private beaches, because these beaches are
clean and they have lifeguards. The beaches that are free have trash on
them and there are no lifeguards on duty.

Lido Di Jesolo has different contests during the year. One of these
contests is the sand sculpture contest that is held in June and July.
There is also a Jolly Roger boat that sails around the beach. This boat
is like a museum where people get to experience the life of the pirates.

People can reach lido by taking different trains numbers: 773,777
and 5817. The ride takes anywhere from 15 to 20 minutes from Mestre to
Jesolo.
**Lido Di Venezia

**
Tourists can reach this beach by boarding the
train from Santa Lucia Railway Station and then getting on a vaporetto.
Lido Di Venezia has sandy beaches and different resorts and hotels. Some
of the hotels offer honeymoon packages that start at $150 Euros.

This beach is one of the least crowded beaches near Mestre. The beach
is 12 kilometers long and it is between the Adriatic Sea and the
Venezia lagoon. The beach sands in Lido Di Venezia seem to have a golden
color.

The beach has different huts that are used by the tourists who can
stay in the beach most of the day. People can stay under the shade.

**The Lido**

The Mestre Beaches form part of a seashore nearby called the Lido.
Tourists can take a boat that can take them to the lido. It takes around
30 minutes to get there. The beaches are owned by most of the hotels.
People can rent bicycles to explore the seashore.

People can also practice tennis or golf in the resorts and clubs.
These are alternative activities, because the beaches of The Lido tend
to get crowded. There are also many shops that are on Mestre Beaches.
Some of them sell jewelry, souvenirs and clothing.
