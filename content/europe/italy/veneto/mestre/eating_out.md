type: section
---

Mestre has a solid restaurant scene that most Venice-bound travellers overlook. The quality is good and the prices are substantially lower than the lagoon side. Fish features heavily — the proximity to the Adriatic means supply chains are short.


**La Cascina**

This Mestre restaurant is a large bar and snack bar that is in the style of the old farmsteads that used to be found in the Po valley. Live music is played on weekends, thus the crowd varies in range from students to music lovers on these days. Items on the menu include panini, chips, and salad. Drinks include beer and other drinks.

**Trattoria Dall&rsquo;Amelia**

This is actually one of the most famous restaurants in Venice and is located for easy access by both land and sea. Here the service could not get any better an the cuisine is a mixture of flavors from the entire region. They serve rare vintage wine and many specialty meals. Be sure to try the Bigoli Pasta with Shellfish or a Prawn and Marrow Flan.

**Antico Dolo**

This restaurant started out as an individual&rsquo;s catering business and is located by the fresh fish, fruit, and vegetable markets. It is a restaurant with great success, featured in many cooking magazines.

**Taverna San Lio**

This restaurant uses only the freshest of ingredients, found locally at the Venice lagoon. It has a wine list that pays attention to the local tradition of excellent flavor as well. Aesthetically pleasing, palate pleasing, all around pleasing.
