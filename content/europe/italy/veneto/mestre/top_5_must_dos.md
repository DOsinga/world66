Because Mestre, a town found in Veneto, is so rich in culture and history, there are several **Mestre must do’s** to put on your list. Veneto is connected to Venice by a great road and rail bridge called the Freedom Bridge, or Ponte della Liberta. It is one of the largest cities in Italy, and is said to have been founded by a deserter from Troy named Mesthles. But even if the beginnings of this city are not certain, its rich culture is definitely something to visit for. There can be a lot of things to do when taking a vacation in Mestre, but here are the top 5 things you must do for you to get the vacation of a lifetime in this city.

**1. Witness Mestre’s Festivals and Other Events**

Like any other country which has a rich culture, Mestre offers tourists a lot of festivals and events that are truly memorable. One of the most sought-after events in this city is called the Carnival. Not to be misconstrued as the carnival which has rides in it: the Carnival in Mestre is a different kind of entertainment. This event is celebrated by the Roman Catholics as a means of getting all the pleasure and fun they need before the Lenten Season. One of the highlights of this event is the masquerade. These extremely collectible masks are showcased for the intricate craftsmanship of their makers.

**2. Enjoy the Exciting Nightlife in Mestre**

Everybody wants to have a good time, especially after a long and stressful work week. What better way to reward yourself than with the endless fun and relaxation of Mestre’s nightlife. Here, you will find a chain of night clubs and bars which offer the great selections of wine and beer, as well as several restaurants that cater to all classes of people.

**3. Have a Great Shopping Experience in Mestre**

Mestre is known all over the world for its shopping. Here you will find a lot of items which are not commonly found anywhere else--from designer clothes to utensils to furniture. Aside from these, they also offer souvenirs and unique finds like luxurious leather materials, handmade furniture, blown glasses and rare books.

**4. Watch the Rowing Regatta and Gondola Races**

Because Mestre is surrounded by bodies of water, boating is one of the most popular things you can do in this city. With its annual rowing regattas and gondola races, it is definitely the place to check out competitive boating.

**5. Become a Fan of Mestre’s Popular Sport**

Mestre is home to one of the most coveted teams in football: Venezia. You can watch them play at their home stadium the Pierluigi Penzo.
