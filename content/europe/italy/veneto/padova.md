Padova — Padua in English — is one of northern Italy's most rewarding cities, often overlooked by travellers heading straight to [Venice](/europe/italy/veneto/venice) just 40 minutes away by train. That is a mistake. The city has one of the oldest universities in the world (founded 1222, where Galileo taught), the extraordinary Scrovegni Chapel with Giotto's frescoes — widely considered the starting point of Western painting — and the oldest botanical garden in Europe.

The city centre is a pleasure to walk: arcaded streets, three linked piazzas that form the commercial and social heart of the city, and the vast Prato della Valle, one of the largest squares in Europe, ringed by a canal and statues. The Basilica of Sant'Antonio draws pilgrims from across the Catholic world, and the daily markets in the piazzas are lively and affordable.

Padova is a university town first and foremost, which keeps it young, affordable, and full of good bars and cheap restaurants. It makes an excellent base for exploring the Veneto, or a rewarding day trip from Venice.
