Quarto d'Altino is a small town in the Veneto, about 15 minutes by train from Venice and a few kilometres from Marco Polo International Airport. For most visitors it functions purely as a transit point, but the town has an ancient history worth knowing.

Modern Quarto d'Altino sits near the site of Roman **Altinum**, one of the most important cities of northeast Italy before the barbarian invasions of the 5th century forced its population to flee to the lagoon islands — including the island that would become Venice. The **Museo Nazionale di Altino** holds finds from the Roman and pre-Roman settlement, including mosaics, funerary monuments, and everyday objects from a prosperous Roman city that otherwise left no standing ruins.

The town itself is unremarkable, but it occasionally hosts local festivals including wine and glass-blowing events. Useful as a base if Venice accommodation is full or overpriced.
