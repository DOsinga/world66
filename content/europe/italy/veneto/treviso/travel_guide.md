**Your Handy Guide for a Stress Free Travel to Treviso**

Before visiting Treviso, it is important that you have a **Treviso travel guide **to help you get around this Italian city without any problems. Treviso is a commune located in the northern Italy and is considered as one of the historical sites in Italy. The many historical places in the city make it a popular destination of tourists from all over the world. So, here are some of the things that you need to know about Treviso.

**Getting Around **

You have two options to get around Treviso. You can ride the bus to take you to major attractions or you can walk. If you ride a bus, the fare of the bus in Treviso is $1.25, which is really cheap. You can also walk around the historic center of the city as it is small and that there are also a lot of narrow streets all over Treviso which are no longer accessible by the bus. That is why walking is also recommended in order to visit the nook and cranny of Treviso.

**Historic Sites**

The great thing about traveling to Treviso is that there are a lot of historical sites that you can visit all over the city. You can visit Villa Martin or Villa Emo which are located in near proximity to each other. Further, the historical places are mostly concentrated within the center of the metropolis.

**Foods **

There are a lot of restaurants in Treviso that serve great Italian cuisine. The food in the city are heavenly but finding cheap food can be a bit of a challenge the fact that getting a full meal in Treviso can cost you around $20 to $50. However, finger foods are sold in food carts in the city streets for as little as $5 to $10. Although this is the case, the taste of the foods served by the local restaurants in Treviso is very sumptuous, so the price is negligible. One of the good eats in Treviso is tiramisu.

**Accommodations**

The city has plenty of places where you can sleep for the night. However, the accommodations in Treviso are only available in mid-range to expensive prices. So, for budget travelers, this can be a problem but it would not hurt you to spend a couple of bucks to sleep in one of the many beautiful hotels that dot the city.
