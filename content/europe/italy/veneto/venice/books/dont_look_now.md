The title story in this collection follows a grieving couple in Venice who encounter two women claiming to have a message from their dead daughter. Du Maurier's Venice is wintry, deserted, and labyrinthine — the off-season city of narrow calli, dead-end bridges, and a lagoon that absorbs everything. The story was made into Nicolas Roeg's 1973 film; both versions are worth experiencing.
