An American editor rents a room from an elderly woman in a decaying Venetian palazzo, hoping to get his hands on letters written by a long-dead poet. James's Venice is the city of faded grandeur and slow intrigue — shuttered rooms, overgrown gardens, the particular light on the lagoon. One of his most readable novellas and a perfect companion to a stay in the quieter, residential parts of the city.
