Located around a two-hour ride by boat from Venice is the City of Chioggia. Chioggia is considered the second most important city in the Venetian lagoon. Venice is, of course, the first. Chioggia features several notable canals, some worthy structures (although many are crumbling), and a more relaxed environment than Venice. An added benefit -- the boat on the way to Chioggia stops at Alberoni and Pellestrina. Both areas are popular for their beaches. To get to Chioggia, take a bus from the Lido (*www.actv.it*). From central Venice, take the #1 vaporetto to the Lido. During the summer months you can take the #1 or #2. Purchase a ticket for the #11 bus at Lido to Pellestrina. Buses run every hour or so. In Pellestrina, transfer from the bus to a ferry for a 25-minute boat ride to Chioggia. It's an hour and a half to Chioggia from the Lido. You can also take a bus from Venice's Piazzale Roma to Chioggia via the mainland. FYI: The scenery isn't that great by bus from Piazzale Roma to Chioggia and you won't save enough time to justify this using this option.
