Chioggia sits at the southern tip of the Venetian lagoon — a working fishing town with canals, crumbling palazzi, and far fewer tourists than Venice. It's sometimes called "little Venice," though the vibe is more workaday Italian town than museum piece.

The most enjoyable way to get there is by water. Take the vaporetto #1 to the Lido, then bus #11 south through Pellestrina, with a short ferry crossing along the way. The whole journey from the Lido takes about ninety minutes, and the route passes good beaches at Alberoni and Pellestrina worth a stop on the way back. You can also take a bus from Piazzale Roma via the mainland, but the scenery is dull and you barely save any time.
