This palace housed Venice's former governmental departments. It dates from the early ninth century, but the earliest recoverable traces are from around the 1340s. The main entrance, the Porta della Carta, is one of the finest Gothic works in Venice. Inside are paintings by Tintoretto and Veronese, and the famous Bridge of Sighs connecting to the prison.
