---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Verona"
type: section
---

Verona is often seen as one of the most romantic cities in the
world, and people travel from all quarters of the globe to visit the
city. Its links with Romeo and Juliet mean that it has a
regular stream of visitors, and as a consequence, Verona is rather
‘touristy’. It's not always possible to visit this city with a large
amount of capital, and so visiting Verona on a budget is necessary.
There are a range of ways to save money in Verona, either through cheap
flights into the city, budgeting accommodation or by shopping for
cheap items in Verona itself. All of these things can be considered by
anyone wishing to travel to Verona on a budget.

**Finding Budget Travel to Verona**

When flying into Verona, budget travel airlines are always a good
option, although it probably means that the traveler will land at an
airport further removed from his final destination. For instance,
Verona can be accessed by a number of airports, including the Catullo
Airport, only about 12 km from the city, but most budget airlines will
fly travelers to D’Annunzio Airport (sometimes called Verona Brescia
Airport) which is some 50 km way from Verona. It would probably be
necessary to rent a car to make the journey into Verona, or wherever you might be staying.

**Staying in Verona on a Budget**

There are some low-budget bed-and-breakfast places, either near the
city center, or a little bit further out. The
Sommavalle, in the Via Marsala, is just a short walk from the center,
and costs about 60 EUR. The Agriturismo Sommavalle is
around 4 km away from the town center, and costs 70 EUR. Some mid-range
places will also offer single rooms for about 45 EUR, but these will
also probably be out of the city center and will require a rental car
to reach Verona. The cost of this must be added to the price of the
room before any budget can be calculated.

Youth hostels are another option, so long as you don’t
mind sharing with strangers, and having to locate the youth hostels
before turning in. Several small bed and breakfasts in the area will offer a range of
prices, but it's important to remember that car rental will cost
about 30 EUR a day. It's up to travelers to weigh the pros and cons
of staying further from the city in exchange for somewhat reduced prices.
