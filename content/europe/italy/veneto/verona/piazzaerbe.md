Verona's oldest market square, built on the site of the Roman forum. Palazzo Maffei closes off the north end with its baroque facade, the Lamberti Tower rises to the east, and Casa Mazzanti's 16th-century frescoes still blush across one long wall. Market stalls under large white umbrellas sell fruit, clothing, and souvenirs through the morning. The surrounding cafés fill up in the afternoon. Worth walking through even without stopping to buy anything.
