A well-preserved 16th-century city gate designed by Michele Sanmicheli, the same architect responsible for several of Verona's Renaissance palaces. The gate's five grand arches and Doric columns make it one of the finest examples of military architecture from the period. This was historically the turning point for the Palio horse race through the city.
