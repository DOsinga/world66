**Sights**

**Duomo**

In a small square, which complements it to calculated effect, is Santa Maria Matricolare, the Cathedral of Verona. Built on the site of a previous Early Christian church and consecrated in 1187, the Cathedral has a façade which is a perfect composite of Romanesque and Gothic forms. The grandiose [more..](/europe/italy/veneto/verona/sights/churchescathedral/duomo)

address: Via Duomo

**Sant'Anastasia**

Gothic masterpiece that is somewhat hidden in between narrow streets and piazzas. Inside are, amongst other works of art, frescoes from Giotto's pupils. 

address: Corso S. Anastasia

**San Zeno Maggiore**

One of the most renowned Roman churches of Italy. Although history goes back to the 5th century, the present building dates from the 11th century. The triptych from Andrea Mantegna is one of the many impressive decorative aspects of the San Zeno Maggiore. 

address: Piazza S. Zeno

**San Bernardino**

This church is named after Bernardinus from Siena who started building this church and the adjacent monastery halfway the 15th century.

address: Vic. Lungos S. Bernardino

**San Fermo Magiore**

What makes this church particularly interesting is the comination of a Roman 'underchurch' and a Gothic 'upperchurch'. The tomb of Nicolo Rangino, who designed amongst other the things the impressive portal of the Duomo, can be seen in the first chapel on the lef.

address: Stradone S. Fermo

**Santa Maria in Organo**

Maybe a bit off the beaten track, but worth the walk because of its astonishing frescoes and Sammichele's marble structures.

address: Interrato del Acqua Morta
