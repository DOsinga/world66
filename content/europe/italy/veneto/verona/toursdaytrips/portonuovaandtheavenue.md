---
title: "Porto Nuova and the Avenue"
type: location
---

The main and most representative entrance in Verona is situated immediately out of the Venetian and Austrian walls still sorrounding the old city. The entrance of Verona shows signs of its antique nobility, above all the monumental Porta Nuova, which partly keeps its original structure. It was built by Michele Sanmicheli from 1435 to 1440 and was largely worked over by the Austrians in 1854. From here the wide, lined with trees Corso Porta Nuova leads, with its modern buildings and the suggestive landscape of towers, hills and prealps, directly to the city center.
