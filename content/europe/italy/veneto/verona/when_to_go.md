---
title: "When to Go in Verona"
type: section
---

After centuries of holding its stake as one of the most romantic and
historically appealing cities in the world, Verona is still offering
its visitors (as well as its residents) a taste of the charmed life,
with its hot summer festivities, cool historic architecture, and of
course, the mild-mannered weather.
**Fall for Springtime Travel
**

Springtime in Verona showcases mild temperatures with sunny skies
and light breezes. The air is warm and inviting, but has yet to gather
the stale heat that mid-summer tends to bring. The city is home to a
temperate climate all around, generally, and although it reels in
travelers from all over the world in the mid-year months, the end of
spring or beginning of the fall seasons are ideal. That is, if
absorbing the beautiful sights and sounds of the city at your leisure
without battling hoards of people is your idea of a good time.

It is
these times of year that the city is a little quieter, the sun is a
little less vengeful and festivities such as Schermi d'Amoren of May
and Sagre of September delight all around. Besides the crowds and
heat, late spring in Verona opens up the hills with the lush greenery
of the countryside.
**Sticky Summers, Misty Winters
**

As mild as the spring and autumn months are, the summers are hot,
humid, and sticky, specifically between June and July. Not only that,
but the majority of the city’s rainfall occurs in the month of June.
You will want to pack an umbrella and some galoshes for day trips
during this time. A plus side for weather junkies is that these
humid, hot summer nights bring incredible thunderstorms on occasion. Beware of the crowds during the summer months, though for summer is the
busiest time for the city. The boutiques and shops open up their doors
for longer hours and and offer a greater variety of products, but up their prices
as well.

Opposite to its sticky summers are Verona's chilly winters.
Temperatures can drop below freezing on certain days, although you
won’t have to worry about feeling gloomy because the skies stay bright
and sunny for the most part. These brisk, clear days are perfect for
viewing Bacanal del Gnoco or Verona in Love in February, as well as
general sightseeing. There are plenty of photo opportunities in the
winter months since there are many traditional festivals and events
taking place then. Throw on some layers and head on out to Fiera
Cavalli to catch a glimpse of a horse show or Santa Lucia to buy some
toys or treats.
