---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Kosovo punches well above its weight in festivals, particularly in Prizren in August.

**Dokufest** (Prizren, August) is the headline event. Running since 2002, it is one of the most distinctive documentary film festivals in Europe — held outdoors in Prizren's old bazaar, the fortress, and makeshift venues across the historic centre. Over ten days it screens more than 200 films, hosts industry events, and draws international filmmakers, journalists, and NGO workers alongside Kosovars from across the country and diaspora. The atmosphere in Prizren during Dokufest is hard to match anywhere: warm evenings, packed cafes, screenings in improbable settings. Book accommodation months in advance.

**Sunny Hill Festival** (Pristina, late July/early August) is Kosovo's main music festival, held on a hillside outside the capital. In recent years it has attracted genuinely major international acts — the festival is partly owned by rapper Dua Lipa's father, which explains some of the international bookings. A large, well-organised event.

**Pristina Jazz Festival** (October) is a smaller but well-regarded event bringing international jazz musicians to venues across the capital.

**Shën Gjini** (St John's Day, mid-June) is celebrated across Kosovo with outdoor gatherings, traditional music, and dancing, particularly in village communities.

**Ramadan** changes the rhythm of Kosovo's cities noticeably — breaking fast (iftar) is a communal event, with families gathering in cafes and restaurants at sunset. The month ends with Eid al-Fitr (Bajrami i Madh), a public holiday with celebrations across the country. Dates shift annually with the Islamic calendar.

**International Film Festival Prishtina** (IFFP) (Pristina, November) is a smaller film festival focused on Balkan and European cinema.
