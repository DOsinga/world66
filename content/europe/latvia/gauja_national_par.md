Gauja National Park is the largest protected area in Latvia, covering around 920 square kilometres of forested river valley along the Gauja River between Sigulda and Valmiera. It's the easiest countryside escape from Riga — trains and buses reach the park's main towns in under an hour.

The landscape is defined by the Gauja Valley itself: a steep-sided sandstone gorge draped in mixed forest, with caves, springs, and river beaches. Nineteen ancient hill forts and six medieval castles dot the valley, concentrated around the towns of **Sigulda**, **Cesis**, and Valmiera.

**Sigulda** is the most visited entry point. Highlights include the ruins of Sigulda Castle, the red-brick **Turaida Castle** (1214) across the valley, and the **Gutmanis Cave** — the largest cave in the Baltics, its sandstone walls carved with inscriptions going back centuries. A cable car runs across the valley 40 metres above the Gauja River and is worth taking just for the view. Sigulda also has a functioning bobsled and luge track used in international competition; in summer you can ride it in a wheeled sled.

**Cesis** is less touristy and more satisfying for a longer visit — see the [Cesis](/europe/latvia/cesis) page.

Activities in the park include hiking, cycling, canoeing on the Gauja (canoe rental is available in both Sigulda and Cesis), and birdwatching. Trails are reasonably well marked. The valley between Sigulda and Turaida is a pleasant two-hour walk.
