Liepaja is Latvia's third-largest city, on the west coast where the Baltic Sea meets the Liepaja Lake. It's a three-hour bus ride from Riga but worth the trip — the city has a lived-in character, good beaches, and a handful of genuine sights that reward a day or two.

The most extraordinary is the organ in **Holy Trinity Church** (Sv. Trīsvienības baznīca): built in 1779, with 7,000 pipes and 141 stops, it's reportedly the largest surviving historically unreconstructed mechanical organ in the world. Recitals are held regularly; even if you aren't usually drawn to organ music, hearing it here is something else.

**Karosta** (Naval Port) is a large district north of the city centre that was built as a tsarist naval base in the late 19th century and subsequently used by the Soviet military until the 1990s. The scale is striking: an Orthodox cathedral, officers' mansions, fortifications, a Northern Breakwater you can walk out on. The old military prison offers tours and a deliberately uncomfortable overnight stay for those who want it.

The **beach** is wide and backed by dunes, awarded a Blue Flag for water quality. In summer the city runs a programme of music festivals, including rock events that have made Liepaja something of a cultural capital for Latvian rock music.

The **Liepaja Symphony Orchestra** is the oldest professional symphony orchestra in the Baltic states, founded in 1921.
