Ventspils is a port city on Latvia's northwest coast, about 190 kilometres from Riga. It has a reputation as one of Latvia's cleanest and best-kept cities — the municipality has drawn steady income from its busy commercial harbour and, to its credit, has spent much of it on parks, cycle paths, and a well-maintained city centre.

At the heart of the old town stands the **Livonian Order Castle**, dating to 1290 and among the oldest structures in Latvia. It now houses the Ventspils Museum, with exhibits covering the city's maritime and cultural history. The waterfront along the Venta River is a pleasant place to walk, with a riverside park and the distinctive fountain known as the "Boats of the Sun."

The **city beach** is broad and Blue Flag certified, backed by dunes and equipped with summer facilities. It draws Latvian families in good numbers but sees far fewer foreign visitors than Jūrmala — which is part of the appeal.

Stena Line ferries run from Ventspils to Nynäshamn in Sweden and to Finland, making the city a practical entry or exit point if you're travelling the Baltic by sea.
