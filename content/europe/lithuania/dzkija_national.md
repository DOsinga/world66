Dzūkija is Lithuania's largest national park, covering dense pine and mixed forests in the southeast of the country, roughly centred between Druskininkai and Varėna. The landscape is flat and sandy, shaped by glacial outwash — long stretches of old-growth pine, wetlands, and clear rivers cutting through the forest. It feels genuinely remote in a way that few places in the Baltic states still manage.

The Dzūkija region has preserved old Lithuanian folk traditions more completely than almost anywhere else in the country. The villages here still have people who weave traditional textiles, sing the old songs, and treat mushroom-picking as a serious seasonal ritual rather than a weekend hobby. If you want to understand what Lithuanian village life looked like before the Soviet kolkhoz era, this is the place to look.

The rivers — primarily the Merkys and Ūla — are the park's arteries, popular with canoeists from spring through autumn. Traditional wooden villages dot the riverbanks, some with the oldest surviving vernacular architecture in Lithuania. Sandy riverside beaches appear unexpectedly in the middle of deep forest, ideal for swimming and camping.

The main visitor centres are at Marcinkonys and Merkinė, where you can pick up trail maps and arrange guided excursions. The park is most easily reached from [Druskininkai](/europe/lithuania/druskininkai) to the west, which has good transport connections and accommodation.
