Kaunas is the second largest city in Lithuania, located at the juncture of Nemunas and Neris, which is very popular for its adventurous **Kaunas day trips**. It is said that the Romans first established Kaunas during the ancient times. After the world war, Kaunas was proclaimed Lithuania&rsquo;s main industrial city. The exceptional evolution of mankind and its modern architecture proves to be dominating, thus enabling the city of Kaunas to build new and modern building structures.

Today, the prospering city of Kaunas produces from its well defined industry of machineries, chemicals, plastics and textiles. Its massive and modern building structure has partly erased its rich history of the medieval period, yet its written past has remained intact and the history of the city remains rich and unprecedented. If you intent to visit the place, here are some places to go for Kaunas day** **trips:

**Kaunas**** Old Town**

The splendid and breathtaking Kaunas Old town is dominated by the Gothic and Renaissance style of architecture and is surrounded by several 16th century merchant houses. Kaunas castle, which boasts of a double-walled castle in Lithuania, has been one of the few but strong stone made castles in the world. Here, you can also find a sample of a late Gothic architecture in Lithuania that clearly showed the power of the German expansion.

**Devil&rsquo;s Museum**

It was called the devil&rsquo;s museum because of its collector who collected over 2,000 depictions of devils all over the world. The museum also consists of wood carvings and references to music and alcohol. Culture and religion also transpire in this museum, as well as their beliefs of the unknown. This one of a kind museum can send shivers up and down your spine, while letting you appreciate the beauty of the art at the same time.

**The Hill of Crosses and Siluva**

The history of the Hill of the Crosses started when relatives of victims of rebellion started to put crosses in remembrance of their loved once, as well as a symbol of surrendering their relatives to Christ. It is said to have continued now by people who&rsquo;s belief and faith in Christ stands strong and mighty. Siluva, the place where it&rsquo;s believed to be the Virgin Mary&rsquo;s first appearance in the beginning of the 17th century, has been one of the main tourist attractions.
