**Popular Kaunas Transportation**

Public transportation forms the core of **Kaunas transportation** facilities. The public transit system has been established in the form of an extensive network. The process of buying tickets has also been simplified. Visitors can buy daily or monthly tickets from multiple locations. The electronic passes are an easier option than the regular paper tickets that have to be bought at the boarding junction. Similarly, tickets for public buses or trolleybuses can be bought from kiosks set up near newspaper-stands and popular cafes. Most of the touring locales in the city are equipped with maps displayed on large screens that explain the transit routes and recommended forms of public transport. Using these computerized interfaces, visitors can also search for commuting information for no charges.

**Boat-rides**

Kaunas has a dedicated waterway network that is used for public transportation only. Thus, there is no interference by commercial carriers. Visitors can use the boats for traveling to most of the nearby locations. The important boat-boarding sites include the Kruonis Storage Plant, Reservoir Pier and the Pazaislis Pier. The Reservoir Pier is recommended for first-time visitors since most of the boat rides begin here.

**Microbuses**

A microbus refers to the shuttle service that intersects all the major commuting routes within the city. This network is truly remarkable, with the shuttle service functioning with absolute precision. The network is owned by private firms, and the overall service is commendable.

**Trolleybus **

These are the favored form of transportation when traveling to nearby towns. The trolleybuses might be a bit slower than the regular bus service, but they ensure a smoother, uninterrupted ride. Various trolley junctions have been set-up in Kaunas. Visitors can easily spot a trolley junction since various displays show directions for the nearby or approaching junction.**:**

**Taxi**

There are no set prices for fares to a particular destination. Most of the taxi-drivers charge by the kilometer. Thus, it is advisable that visitors confirm the rate that is being sought by the taxi-driver because it cannot be argued later. Taxis are used by a small number of people since distances can be easily covered via the commuting facilities described above. However, for traveling in the late evening hours, taxis are the safer option.

**Bike Rentals**

Kaunas has always emphasized using carbon-free modes of transportation. As a result, the city has dedicated biking lanes. Visitors can easily rent a bicycle for a small security deposit. Visitors can seek information about the biking trails from the tourism office or seek the help of the rental-owners.

**Car Rentals**

Visitors can also book a car for a short duration. There are plenty of car-rental brands to choose from, including international providers like Europcar. Driving in the city is relatively easy since traffic rules are seldom violated. Furthermore, the public transit system ensures that most people are kept off the main streets. It is better to seek car rental packages for a few days rather than 24-hour rentals, which can be quite expensive.
