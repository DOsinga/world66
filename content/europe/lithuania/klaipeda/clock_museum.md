An unusual museum in a 19th-century building on Liepų Street, tracing the history of Lithuanian clockmaking from guild records of the 16th century through the early 20th century. The collection includes wall clocks, table clocks, and pocket watches, together with the tools of the clockmaker's trade. There is also a permanent display on the first Lithuanian tower clock at Vilnius Cathedral (1557).
