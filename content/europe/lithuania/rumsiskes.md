Rumsiskes is a small town on the northern shore of the Kaunas Reservoir, about 20 kilometres east of Kaunas. It would be easy to drive past — but that would be a mistake. The town is home to the Lithuanian Open-Air Museum, one of the finest ethnographic museums in the Baltic states and a reason in itself to make the trip from Kaunas or Vilnius.

The museum spreads across more than 170 hectares of rolling countryside, with farmsteads, villages, and manor complexes transplanted from across Lithuania's four ethnographic regions: Aukstaitija, Suvalkija, Zemaitija, and Lithuania Minor. Walking through it feels like stepping into the nineteenth century — the buildings are genuine, the interiors are furnished with period tools and textiles, and in summer, craftspeople demonstrate traditional skills like pottery, amber-working, and weaving. It is the kind of place that rewards an unhurried half-day.
