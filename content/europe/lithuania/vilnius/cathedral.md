At the foot of Gediminas Hill stand the Lower Castle and the Cathedral. In the Cathedral square, in the 13th century, the first Catholic Church was built to mark Lithuania's conversion to Catholicism. It was burnt down and rebuilt many times. Today the Cathedral is an 18th-century classical building where many valuable paintings are on exhibit alongside ornamental gravestones of historic families. You can visit the mysterious 12th-century cellars, remains of the pagan sanctuary where famous Lithuanian personalities from medieval times are buried. In the Cathedral square, citizens celebrate the city's main national events; the Baltic Freedom Way joining people in a line from Vilnius to Tallinn was started here.
