The most magnificent Gothic church in Lithuania. It is small — leading into the presbytery of the adjacent Church of the Bernardines — but its elaborately ornamented facade is extraordinary. The church took shape in its present form after reconstruction in 1582 and is possibly the only Gothic building in Lithuania where bricklayers succeeded in surpassing the purely constructional function of bricks. Rows of profiled bricks (thirty-three types) gracefully rise skyward or curve into various decorative motifs. The interior has been changed more than once; three Baroque altars were installed in the mid-18th century, and an adjacent neo-Gothic belfry was built in the second half of the 19th century. According to legend, Napoleon was so impressed that he wanted to carry the church back to Paris. Maironio St. 8. Open daily 10:00–15:00.
