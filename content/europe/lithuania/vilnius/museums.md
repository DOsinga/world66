Vilnius has numerous museums, many housed in the palaces of former nobility in the Old Town.

### M.K. Čiurlionis House

The house where the great Lithuanian artist and composer lived and worked in 1907–1908, opened as a museum in 1995. Chamber music concerts are held in the cosy premises. Savičiaus St. 11.

### Lithuanian Museum of Genocide Victims

In the former KGB headquarters, this museum documents Soviet-era repression and Lithuanian resistance. Aukų St. 2a / Gedimino Ave 40. Open Tues–Sun 10:00–16:00.

### Lithuanian State Jewish Museum

Documents the rich Jewish history of Vilnius and the destruction of the community during the Holocaust. Pamėnkalnio St. 12 / Pylimo St. 4. Open Mon–Thurs 9:00–17:00, Fri 9:00–16:00.

### Alexander Pushkin Literature Museum

In the house where Grigorij Pushkin, younger son of the poet, lived from 1899 to 1905. The museum displays the family's literary works and the living conditions of Russian landlords. Subačiaus St. 124. Open Wed–Sat 11:00–18:00.

### Vilnius Castle Museum

Arsenalo St. 3. Open Tues–Sun 11:00–18:00.

### Architecture Museum

Šv. Mykolo St. 9. Open Mon, Wed–Sat 11:00–18:00.
