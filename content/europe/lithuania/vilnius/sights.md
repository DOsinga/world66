Vilnius has one of the largest surviving medieval old towns in Europe. The city is dominated by church spires — more than 40 churches in a variety of architectural styles, from Gothic to Baroque and Neoclassical.

### Gediminas Castle

The castle is in the heart of the city, one of the first Gothic structures in Vilnius. The castle museum reveals the eventful history of Lithuania. Climb the octagonal tower for a breathtaking view of the Old Town.

### Church of St. Anne

The most magnificent Gothic church in Lithuania. Its facade is patterned with 33 varieties of bricks. According to legend, Napoleon was so impressed he wanted to carry it back to Paris. Maironio St. 8. Open daily 10:00–15:00.

### Church of SS. Peter and Paul

Commissioned by Mykolas Kazimieras Pacas and completed in the late 17th century. The interior is stunning — over 2,000 stucco figures by Italian sculptors cover the walls and ceiling. Antakalnio St. 1/1.

### Church of St. Nicholas

One of the oldest brick and stone churches in Lithuania, already mentioned in the time of Gediminas in the 14th century.

### Vilnius University

The oldest university in Eastern Europe, opened in 1579. The complex takes up a large section of the Old Town, with original courtyards, arched corridors, and decorations spanning over 400 years.

### The Town Hall

The main Vilnius city building, reconstructed by Laurynas Stuoka-Gucevičius between 1785 and 1799 in a Neoclassical style.

### The Hill of Three Crosses

Located near Gediminas Castle, this is the symbol of Lithuanian mourning and hope. According to legend, seven Franciscan monks were crucified here. The first crosses were erected in the 17th century in their memory.

### Cathedral

At the foot of Gediminas Hill, first built in the 13th century to mark Lithuania's conversion to Catholicism. Today it is an 18th-century classical building with valuable paintings and ornamental gravestones. The 12th-century cellars beneath can be visited.

### Gates of Dawn

The 16th-century Renaissance gates originally formed part of the Old Town fortifications. A chapel houses a holy image of the Virgin Mary, reputed for miracle-working powers and a pilgrimage site for visitors from neighbouring countries.
