St. Anne's Church represents the peak of Gothic architecture in Lithuania. Its facade is built from 33 different types of brick arranged in intricate patterns, making it one of the most graceful small churches in northern Europe. According to legend, Napoleon was so taken with it during the 1812 campaign that he wished he could carry it back to Paris on the palm of his hand. The neighboring Bernardine Church and monastery, connected to St. Anne's, are worth stepping into as well.
