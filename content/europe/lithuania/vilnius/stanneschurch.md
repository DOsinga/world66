St. Anne's Church represents the peak of Gothic architecture in Lithuania. Its facade is patterned with 33 different varieties of bricks, making it amazingly graceful. According to the legend, Napoleon Bonaparte was so impressed by St. Anne's Church that he wanted to carry it back to Paris.
