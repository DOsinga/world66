---
title: Top 5 Must Do's
type: section
---

**1. Vilnius Cathedral**

The splendid Vilnius Cathedral is a landmark in the Old City with a striking Greek temple-like appearance. Built on an old pagan temple site, the 13th-century monument houses several frescoes and paintings and has been restored many times due to war and natural calamities. The Cathedral regained its glory after the Soviet era and is now an impeccable illustration of art, history, and religion.

**2. Gediminas Castle**

This classic 13th-century monument has served as a war prison, defence bastion, and is now a popular tourist attraction. The carefully restored castle boasts a well-stocked museum exhibiting memorabilia that shaped the ancient identity of Lithuania. Climb the soaring octagonal tower for a stunning view of the Old Town.

**3. Europos Parkas**

Head to Europos Parkas, north of the Old Town centre, for an extraordinary open-air sculpture museum. The sprawling 55-hectare park attracts art lovers to view original sculptures displayed among lush woods and natural springs. Guided tours can be organised by booking in advance.

**4. Jewish Memorials**

A stark reminder of the Jewish genocide by the Nazis during World War II, established in memory of Vilnius's once-thriving Jewish community. The memorials include a synagogue and a centre depicting pre-war Jewish life.

**5. Hot Air Balloon Sightseeing**

Go sightseeing in a hot air balloon for a fantastic aerial view of Vilnius. Rides last approximately an hour, start from about €250, and can accommodate up to four people. The balloon picks you up from your chosen location.
