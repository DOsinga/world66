---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Lithuania has a continental climate — cold winters, warm summers, and changeable weather year-round. Rain is possible at any time; bring a waterproof layer whatever the season.

**Summer (June–August)** is the most popular time to visit. Days are long (it stays light until after 10pm in June), temperatures reach the low-to-mid 20s°C, and the country is at its most festive. The Curonian Spit and Baltic coast beaches are in full swing in July and August. Vilnius and Kaunas fill up with tourists; book accommodation ahead.

**Spring (April–May)** and **early autumn (September–October)** are excellent times if you want pleasant weather without the crowds. City sightseeing is comfortable, prices are lower, and in September and October the forests turn gold — Lithuania is unexpectedly beautiful in autumn.

**Winter (December–February)** is cold and dark, with temperatures regularly below freezing and frequent snow from December through February. But Vilnius has a particularly good Christmas market, and the snowlit old town has a charm of its own. Druskininkai's Snow Arena is open for skiing in winter. January and February are the quietest (and cheapest) months.

The **Kaziukas Fair** in early March and **Užgavėnės** carnival in late February or March are worth timing a visit around. The **Dainų Šventė** (Song and Dance Celebration), held every four years in Vilnius, is one of the most spectacular cultural events in the Baltics — worth planning ahead for.
