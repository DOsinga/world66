---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Luxembourg's festival calendar is lively given the country's size. Most of the main events happen between spring and autumn, but the Christmas markets draw visitors well into December.

**Emaischen (Easter Monday)**

One of the oldest folk traditions in the country. Locals buy *peckvillercher* — small clay whistling birds — from market stalls in Luxembourg City and Nospelt. Street festivities, folk dancing, and general good cheer. A distinctly Luxembourgish occasion.

**Buergbrennen (mid-Lent)**

Villages across the country build large bonfires to burn winter away — a pre-Christian custom that survived Christianisation as a mid-Lent ritual. The bonfire is paraded through the village, effigies of winter are sometimes thrown in, and the community gathers around for sausages, pea soup, and beer.

**Fête de la Musique (21 June)**

One of Luxembourg City's biggest annual events, with free concerts across the city on the summer solstice. Every genre, every size of venue, most of it free.

**Festival de Wiltz (July)**

Theatre, concerts, and musical events through the whole month of July, staged against the backdrop of Wiltz Castle in the northern Ardennes. An atmospheric setting for anything from opera to contemporary dance.

**Schueberfouer (late August – early September)**

Luxembourg City's great annual funfair, held on the Champ du Glacis at Limpertsberg. One of the oldest fairs in Europe, dating to 1340. Rides, food stalls, and a cheerful late-summer chaos that the whole country seems to attend.

**Grevenmacher Wine and Grape Festival (September)**

A long-weekend celebration in the Moselle wine town of Grevenmacher, with the harvest providing the occasion. Wine, music, and local food in an unpretentious setting.

**Christmas Markets (December)**

Luxembourg City runs three separate Christmas markets — at Place d'Armes, Place de Paris, and Place de la Constitution — with light installations, an ice rink, and craft and food stalls. Less heaving than the markets in Strasbourg or Cologne, but well-organised and genuinely festive.

**National Day (23 June)**

The Grand Duke's official birthday, celebrated with a torchlight procession on the evening of 22 June and military parade and festivities on the 23rd. A good moment to see Luxembourgers express their national pride.
