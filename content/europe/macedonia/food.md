---
title: "Food"
type: section
---

Macedonian food is hearty, fresh, and very good value. It draws on Ottoman, Greek, and Balkan traditions, with an emphasis on grilled meats, fresh vegetables, cheese, and beans.

**Tavče gravče** — baked white beans in a clay pot with onion and peppers — is the national dish. It's filling, cheap, and available almost everywhere. Don't leave without trying it.

**Ajvar** is a roasted red pepper and eggplant relish that appears on almost every table. The homemade version, made in late September when peppers are harvested, is extraordinarily good.

**Grilled meats** (skara) dominate restaurant menus: kebapche (minced meat rolls), pljeskavica (a flat burger patty), and various cuts served with shopska salad — chopped tomatoes, cucumber, and onion topped with grated white cheese (sirenje).

**Cheese and dairy** are excellent. Macedonian white cheese (a firm brined variety) and yellow cheese appear in salads, pastries (burek), and as appetisers. Burek — flaky pastry filled with cheese or meat — makes a cheap and satisfying breakfast, found at any bakery.

**Macedonian wine** has a long history and is underrated internationally. The Tikveš region produces good reds, particularly Vranec, a full-bodied local grape. Rakija (grape or plum brandy) is ubiquitous and offered as a welcome drink everywhere from homes to petrol stations.

Eating out is cheap. A full meal with drinks rarely exceeds 500–600 MKD (€8–10) in a sit-down restaurant. A burek and coffee for breakfast costs under 100 MKD.
