---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

North Macedonia is small — you can cross it in a few hours — but it packs in a surprising amount. It's one of the cheapest countries in Europe and, outside the Balkans, mostly undiscovered.

**[Ohrid](/europe/macedonia/ohrid)** is the undisputed highlight. Perched on the shores of one of Europe's oldest and deepest lakes, this small town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site for both its natural and cultural values. Medieval churches cling to a hillside above glassy water. In summer it's busy; in spring or early autumn it's quietly beautiful.

**[Skopje](/europe/macedonia/skopje)** is a city worth at least two days. The Ottoman Old Bazaar (Čaršija) — one of the largest in the Balkans outside Istanbul — is the real Skopje, full of craft workshops, tea houses, and old mosques. The bizarre neoclassical statues and monuments erected since 2010 are a spectacle in themselves, for better or worse.

**[Bitola](/europe/macedonia/bitola)** is North Macedonia's second city, with a graceful Ottoman streetscape along Širok Sokak and the extensive ruins of Heraclea Lyncestis on its outskirts.

**Mavrovo National Park** offers the best scenery in the country: beech forests, glacial lakes, the partially submerged Church of St Nicholas, and the ski centre at Mavrovo.

**The monasteries** scattered across the countryside are some of the finest medieval art in the Balkans. Sveti Jovan Bigorski (near Mavrovo) and Treskavec (above Priep) are highlights.

**[Galičnik](/europe/macedonia/galichnik)** hosts a spectacular traditional wedding festival each July — an unusual and genuinely moving event.

North Macedonia rewards travellers who slow down and wander. The country is compact enough that Ohrid, Skopje, Bitola, and Mavrovo can all be done in a week without feeling rushed.
