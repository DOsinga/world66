Radovis is a small city in eastern North Macedonia, tucked into a fertile valley at the foot of the Plachkovica mountain range. It is one of those places that most travellers never hear of, but it has an agreeable, unhurried character and makes a good base for exploring the surrounding mountains and countryside.

The town centre has a pleasant main square with several modern cafes where locals linger over espresso. The Church of the Holy Trinity, a striking modern Orthodox church, dominates the skyline and is worth a visit for its interior frescoes. The surrounding Plachkovica mountains offer good hiking and mountain biking on trails that are blissfully uncrowded, and in the valleys around the town you will find vineyards producing some of North Macedonia's lesser-known wines.

Radovis is also known locally for its agricultural traditions -- the area produces excellent tobacco, rice, and pomegranates. The town hosts a lively market, and the local restaurants serve hearty Macedonian fare: grilled meats, shopska salata, and tavche gravche (baked beans), all at prices that make Western Europe feel like another planet. Getting here is easiest by car, though regional buses connect Radovis with Stip and Strumica.
