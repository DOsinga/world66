---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

Skopje is a thriving commercial center of Macedonia that has a
stunning medley of Roman-era architecture and charming Turkish bazaars
along with generous sprinkles of Byzantine and Bulgarian culture. The Skopje Must Do's guide offers visitors information on the attractions and activities in this Macedonian Capital.

**1. The Churches of Skopje**

Skopje boasts of a classic assortment of medieval Romanesque
churches that elegantly adorn its old town streets. Church of St. Spas
is a famous attraction erected in the late 16 th century and
features an exquisite wooden carved altar along with a vivid imagery
depicting biblical characters and the city’s natural landscape. Another
landmark structure is the Church of St. Panteleimon located in the
Gorno Nerezi region. Witness beautiful illustrations of Comnenian art
and take a walk down Nerezi for its celebrated 12 the century frescoes.
Other churches worth visiting are Church of St. Demetrius and Church of
St Clement.

**2. ****The Skopje Cultural Summer Festival**

The culturally charged atmosphere and infectious spirit of the
Skopje Cultural Summer Festival is worth experiencing. The event brings
together over 2500 participants every year from all over the world to
give awe inspiring performances in the genre of musical concerts,
ballets, operas and plays. Art enthusiasts can also look forward to art
and photography exhibitions.

**3. ****Old Town and Bazaars**

This is a true representative of the primary identity of Skopje-
that of a striking confluence of Western and Eastern culture. The
streets of the ancient town are richly decorated with quaint bazaars
that are filled with buzzing shops and eateries where hawkers are
hoarsely crying out to solicit buyers for their wares. Take a walk down
the path situated between Vardar River and Kale fortresses to
experience the authentic essence of Skopje’s exoticism.

**4. Time For Some Arty Indulgence**

Visit the Museum of Contemporary Arts for discovering some of the
finest examples of the modern artistic treasures of Skopje. The
sprawling 5000 square meters art haven comprises of several halls that
hold exhibitions, lectures on contemporary art, audio visual
presentations and even a conservation workshop.

**5. Macedonia Square**

Macedonia Square is a bustling region of the city with attractions
like the National Bank and Army House and the Stone Bridge situated in
the vicinity. Take a walk or relax around the largest square of Skopje
to catch glimpses of its unguarded charm.

Skopje is a mesmerizing piazza of bustling industrial activity and an eclectic cultural identity.
