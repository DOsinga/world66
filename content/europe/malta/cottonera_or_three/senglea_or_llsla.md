Senglea — known in Maltese as L-Isla — is the smallest of the Three Cities on the Grand Harbour, directly across the water from [Valletta](/europe/malta/valletta). Planned on a tight grid pattern in 1551 by Grand Master Claude de la Sengle, it occupies a narrow peninsula that juts into the harbour, giving it water views on three sides.

The town saw some of the fiercest fighting during the Great Siege of 1565, when the Knights of St John held out against the Ottoman fleet. Much of what stands today was rebuilt after heavy bombing in World War II, but the Gardjola Gardens at the tip of the peninsula — with their iconic vedette watchtower carved with eyes and ears — remain one of the most photographed spots in Malta. The view from here across the Grand Harbour to Valletta's bastions is spectacular.

Senglea is compact and can be walked in an hour. The main street, Victory Street, runs the length of the peninsula, lined with traditional Maltese balconies and the Basilica of Our Lady of Victories. A water taxi from Valletta's waterfront drops you at the harbour steps — it is one of the easiest and most rewarding short trips from the capital.
