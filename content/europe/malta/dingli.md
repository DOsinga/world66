Dingli is a quiet hilltop village on Malta's southwest coast, known above all for the dramatic cliffs that bear its name. The Dingli Cliffs are Malta's highest point — around 250 metres above sea level — and the view from the edge, straight down to the Mediterranean with nothing between you and the horizon, is one of the finest on the island. Come at sunset and you'll understand why locals drive out here on weekends.

The village itself is small and unhurried, with a central piazza anchored by St Mary's Parish Church. A few family-run restaurants serve traditional Maltese food. The real draw, though, is the coastal path: the Triq Panoramika runs for over three kilometres along the cliff edge, with the sea wind in your face and the terraced fields falling away on one side. It's easy walking and one of Malta's best afternoon outings.

Just inland, Buskett Gardens provides a shady contrast — Malta's most extensive woodland, with groves of olive trees, citrus orchards, and tall pines. The Knights of St John established it as a hunting ground; it's now a peaceful place to picnic or escape the summer heat.
