Marsaxlokk is Malta's most photogenic fishing village — a sheltered bay on the southeast coast lined with brightly painted *luzzu*, the traditional wooden boats with Eyes of Osiris on their prows. The harbour has been in use since Phoenician times, and fishing is still the main business here.

The Sunday fish market draws half of Malta to the waterfront, where fishermen sell the morning catch alongside stalls of honey, capers, and sun-dried tomatoes. On any other day the quayside is quieter, and you can sit at one of the harbourfront restaurants eating grilled lampuki or octopus stew while watching the boats bob. The village is small enough to see in an hour, but the combination of colour, fresh seafood, and working-harbour atmosphere makes it one of Malta's essential stops.
