---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Malta punches well above its weight for historic sites. The main island and Gozo together hold seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites, most of them prehistoric temples that predate Stonehenge by a thousand years.

The single most impressive site is the Hal Saflieni Hypogeum in Paola — a vast underground burial complex carved into the rock around 3600–2500 BC. Visits are strictly limited to protect the delicate environment; book weeks or months in advance through Heritage Malta. Nearby are the Tarxien Temples, a large temple complex also from the same era.

On the south of the island, the twin temples of Hagar Qim and Mnajdra sit on a ridge above the sea near Qrendi, surrounded by nothing but limestone and sky. The setting is extraordinary. Both are now sheltered by protective tents, which take some of the visual drama away but preserve the stone from weathering.

In [Valletta](/europe/malta/valletta), St John's Co-Cathedral is the standout sight: the plain exterior gives no warning of the interior, which is covered floor to ceiling in carved marble tombstones, gilded carvings, and paintings — including two of Caravaggio's finest works. The Grand Masters' Palace on Republic Street houses a significant armoury.

[Mdina](/europe/malta/mdina), the old hilltop capital, is worth half a day. The fortified city is small — a few narrow lanes of palaces, churches, and quiet squares — but atmospheric, particularly in the early morning before the tour groups arrive. Just below Mdina, Rabat has its own points of interest, including St Paul's Grotto and the Domus Romana, a small museum over a Roman town house.

The Three Cities (Vittoriosa, Senglea, and Cospicua) across the Grand Harbour from Valletta are often overlooked by visitors who stay in the capital. Birgu/Vittoriosa in particular is worth the trip for Fort St Angelo, the Maritime Museum, and a sense of the medieval waterfront that Valletta has largely lost.

[Gozo](/europe/malta/gozo) has its own highlight: the two Ggantija Temples near Xaghra, older than the Maltese temples and in excellent condition.
