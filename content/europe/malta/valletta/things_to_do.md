---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Valletta packs an extraordinary density of history into its compact grid. The city was purpose-built by the Knights of St John after the Great Siege of 1565, and almost every street holds a palace, church, or fortification from that era. St John's Co-Cathedral alone is worth a trip to Malta. From the Upper Barrakka Gardens you get the classic view over the Grand Harbour — then take the ferry across to the Three Cities for a perspective back at Valletta's honey-stone bastions.
