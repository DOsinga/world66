Moldova is one of Europe's least-visited countries and, for those who find their way here, one of its most rewarding surprises. Wedged between [Romania](/europe/romania) and Ukraine, this small landlocked republic has no mountains, no sea, and no famous monuments — yet it has the world's largest wine cellar, cave monasteries carved into limestone cliffs, and a capital that mixes faded Soviet grandeur with a lively café scene.

The country is poor by European standards, and emigration has hollowed out many villages. But the people who remain are hospitable in a way that feels unperformed, and the countryside — rolling hills, fruit orchards, sunflower fields — is quietly beautiful. Traveling here takes a little patience; infrastructure is thin outside [Chisinau](/europe/moldova/chisinau). In return, you'll have the place largely to yourself.

Wine is the country's pride and its most convincing argument for a visit. Moldova has been making wine since ancient times, and today it produces more per capita than almost anywhere in the world. The two great underground cellars — Mileștii Mici (the world's largest) and Cricova — are destinations in their own right. A guided tour ending in a tasting is mandatory.

Transnistria, the breakaway sliver along the eastern border, is a genuinely strange experience: Soviet-era monuments, a currency no one else accepts, and border officials who treat the crossing with dead seriousness. Day trips from Chisinau to [Tiraspol](/europe/moldova/tiraspol) are straightforward and oddly fascinating. [Tighina](/europe/moldova/tighina) (Bender) has an impressive Ottoman fortress on the Dniester.

The monastery complex at [Orheiul Vechi](/europe/moldova/orheiul_vechi_2), cut into a limestone bend of the Raut river, is the single most striking sight in the country. Further north, [Soroca](/europe/moldova/soroca) on the Dniester has a well-preserved Genoese fortress and a Roma hilltop quarter worth exploring.

## Destinations in Moldova

- [Chisinau](/europe/moldova/chisinau) — the capital, lively and walkable
- [Orheiul Vechi](/europe/moldova/orheiul_vechi_2) — cave monasteries in a limestone canyon
- [Soroca](/europe/moldova/soroca) — northern fortress town on the Dniester
- [Tighina](/europe/moldova/tighina) — Ottoman fortress city on the Transnistrian border
- [Tiraspol](/europe/moldova/tiraspol) — capital of breakaway Transnistria
- [Orhei](/europe/moldova/orhei) — regional town near Orheiul Vechi
- [Baltsi](/europe/moldova/baltsi) — Moldova's second city in the north
- [Hincesti](/europe/moldova/hincest) — small town south of Chisinau
