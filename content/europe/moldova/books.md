**Moldova: A Romanian Province Under Russian Rule** by Vladimir Socor — a concise political history of how this territory became a Soviet republic and then an independent state. Dense but useful background reading.

**Wines of the Former Soviet States** — if you are visiting primarily for the wine, this academic but accessible survey covers the Moldovan wine regions in some depth.

**The Odessa File** by Frederick Forsyth — set partly in Odessa, close enough to set the regional mood, and a good thriller for the train from Bucharest.

**Eastern Europe on a Shoestring** (Lonely Planet) — the regional guide covers Moldova with more practical detail than most. The Moldova-specific chapter is limited but useful for logistics.

**Easter in Kishinev: Anatomy of a Pogrom** by Edward H. Judge (isbn: 0814741932) — a careful historical account of the 1903 Chisinau pogrom, one of the most significant events in the city's modern history. Essential reading for understanding the Jewish community that once thrived here.

**Studies in Moldovan: The History, Culture, Language and Contemporary Politics of the People of Moldova** edited by Donald Leroy Dyer (isbn: 0880333510) — an academic collection covering language politics, ethnic identity, and post-Soviet transition. Dry in places but thorough.

**Jewish Roots in Ukraine and Moldova** by Miriam Weiner (isbn: 0965650812) — a guide to Jewish genealogical research with archival inventories. Useful for anyone tracing family history in the region.
