The Tipova cave monastery is one of the largest rock-hewn monastic complexes in Eastern Europe, carved into the limestone cliffs above the Nistru (Dniester) River in central Moldova. The site was founded before the Moldavian state itself — some of the earliest cells are believed to date from the 10th to 12th centuries. At its peak, the monastery housed over 700 monks in cells and churches cut into the rock face across several levels.

The most impressive space is the Church of the Dormition of the Mother of God, a spacious basilica-style chamber with plastered walls and a vaulted ceiling. The cliffs drop a hundred metres to the Nistru below, and the views over the river floodplain are extraordinary. Local legend holds that Ștefan cel Mare (Stephen the Great) was betrothed to his wife Maria Voichița in one of the monastery's chambers.

The monastery is accessible from the village of Tipova and makes a compelling day trip from Chișinău. The road is rough but the setting — wild, remote, and steeped in history — rewards the effort.
