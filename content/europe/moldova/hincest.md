Hîncești is a small town about 35 km south of Chișinău, the administrative centre of Hîncești district. It sees little tourist traffic but sits in decent wine country — the southern Codru wine region extends through this part of Moldova.

The town was known as Kotovsk during the Soviet period, named after the Moldovan-born Bolshevik commander Grigori Kotovsky, who was born in the nearby village of Gănchești in 1881.

It makes an easy day trip from Chișinău by marshrutka if you want a feel for small-town Moldovan life, but most visitors come this way as part of a winery itinerary rather than for the town itself.
