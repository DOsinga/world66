Hincești (also written Hâncești) is a small town about 35 km south of Chisinau, the administrative centre of Hâncești district. It sees little tourist traffic but sits in decent wine country — the southern Codru wine region extends through this part of Moldova.

The town was known as Kotovsk during the Soviet period, named after the Moldovan-born Bolshevik commander Grigori Kotovsky, who was born in the nearby village of Ganchiesty in 1881.

It is an easy day trip from Chisinau by marshrutka if you want to get a sense of small-town Moldovan life, but most visitors come this way as part of a winery itinerary rather than for the town itself.
