Soroca is a town of around 25,000 on the Dniester river in northern Moldova, close to the Ukrainian border. Its main draw is a well-preserved circular fortress built in the early 16th century by Stephen the Great — one of the finest examples of Genoese-influenced military architecture in the region.

The town also has a famous Roma quarter on the hill above the fortress, known informally as Gypsy Hill. The palatial houses there — some modeled on the US Capitol, others with turrets, columns, and gold-painted domes — make for a striking and genuinely unusual sight. The community has deep roots here and the architecture reflects wealth accumulated over generations.

Soroca is about 160 km north of Chisinau, reachable by bus in around three hours.
