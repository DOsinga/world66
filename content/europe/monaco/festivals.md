---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

For a tiny state, Monaco maintains an impressive calendar of events — partly because the principality uses major events to sustain its international profile, and partly because the Grimaldi family has always been an active patron of culture and sport.

**Monaco Grand Prix** (late May) is the centrepiece of the year. The Formula 1 race on the streets of the principality is one of the sport's most famous events — technically not the most competitive race due to the difficulty of overtaking, but historically the most glamorous. The city fills up completely the week before. Book accommodation months ahead and expect prices to triple.

**International Circus Festival of Monte-Carlo** (late January) was founded by Prince Rainier III, who was a circus enthusiast. It has grown into one of the world's premier circus competitions, drawing acts from across Europe and beyond. The Golden Clown is the top prize.

**Monaco Yacht Show** (September) brings the world's largest superyachts to Port Hercule for four days. It's a trade show as much as a spectacle, but the boats are genuinely impressive and the port becomes unusually animated.

**Monte-Carlo Jazz Festival** (November–December) has been held since 2006 under the patronage of the Grimaldi family. International jazz and soul acts perform at Salle des Étoiles.

**Monte Carlo Gastronomie** (late November) is an international food fair in the Grimaldi Forum, featuring wines, champagnes, caviar, cheeses, and demonstrations by Michelin-starred chefs. Primarily aimed at trade buyers but open to the public.

**Monte-Carlo Rally** (January) is one of the oldest motorsport events in the world, dating to 1911. Stages run in the mountains above Monaco and Nice; the ceremonial start and finish take place in Monaco.
