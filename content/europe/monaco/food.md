---
title: "Food"
type: section
---

Monaco's food scene sits firmly in the Provençal and Ligurian tradition — olive oil, seafood, fresh vegetables, pasta — elevated by the principality's wealth into some of the finest restaurants on the Mediterranean. It also means you can eat extremely well or extremely expensively, depending on your choices.

The flagship is **Le Louis XV**, Alain Ducasse's three-starred Michelin restaurant in the Hôtel de Paris. It has been one of Europe's great restaurants since it opened in 1987 — the setting alone, in the Belle Époque dining room overlooking the casino, is extraordinary. A dinner here costs several hundred euros per person. The Monte Carlo resort also has **Blue Bay** (two stars) and **Pavyllon** by Yannick Alléno (one star).

For less spectacular but still very good meals, the streets of Monaco-Ville and the La Condamine district have bistros and restaurants where you can eat well for €25-40 per person. Look for barbagiuan — a Monégasque speciality of fried pastry stuffed with ricotta, spinach, or chard — and socca, the chickpea flatbread common across the region.

The market in La Condamine (Marché de la Condamine) is the best place to buy local produce, olives, cheese, and charcuterie. It opens mornings, Tuesday to Sunday.

Eating in Monaco is never cheap. If you're watching your budget, the supermarkets in the Fontvieille district stock everything you need for a picnic by the port.
