---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Monaco is small enough to walk across in about 30 minutes on flat ground, but it isn't flat — the principality climbs steeply from the port up to Monaco-Ville and Monte Carlo. What saves you is the network of free public lifts and escalators that connect the different levels. These are surprisingly useful and marked on most maps.

**On foot** is the best way to explore. Most of the key sights are within easy walking distance of each other once you're oriented. Give yourself more time than you think you need — the terrain adds effort and the views slow you down.

**By bus**, Monaco has five routes serving 143 stops, running roughly 6am to 9pm. A single ticket costs €2 on board or €1.50 if you buy in advance at a news stand or shop. A day pass is €5.50. The buses are useful for getting between Monaco-Ville and Fontvieille, or out to Larvotto beach.

**By taxi**, cabs are available but expensive, as you'd expect. There are ranks near the casino and at the main train station. Uber does not operate in Monaco.

There is no metro. The train station (Monaco-Monte Carlo) connects you to Nice and the Italian border town of Ventimiglia, and is useful for day trips along the coast.

Renting a car makes no sense within Monaco — parking is almost entirely underground, limited, and costly. If you're based here and exploring the wider Riviera, pick up a rental in Nice.
