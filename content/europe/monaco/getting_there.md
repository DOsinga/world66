---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Monaco has no airport of its own. The nearest is Nice Côte d'Azur Airport (NCE), about 30 km away — roughly 20 minutes by train or 40 minutes by road depending on traffic.

**By train** is the easiest option from Nice. Trains run frequently throughout the day along the coast, stopping at Monaco-Monte Carlo station (which is mostly underground). The journey from Nice takes around 20-25 minutes. From Paris you can take a TGV to Nice and connect from there. The station sits below Monaco-Ville, with lifts and escalators bringing you up into the city.

**By bus** from Nice airport, the No. 110 express bus runs directly to Monaco. It's slower than the train but drops you close to the casino area.

**By car** from Nice, take the A8 motorway or the more scenic Moyenne Corniche (D6007). Parking in Monaco is mostly underground and expensive. For a day trip, consider leaving the car in Menton or Beausoleil (just across the French border) and walking in.

**By helicopter** from Nice airport takes about 7 minutes and operates several times daily with Monacair. It's not cheap, but it's memorably dramatic, landing on the helipad above the port. Worth it for a special occasion.

Monaco is not a practical drive-through destination — it's a cul-de-sac at the end of a promontory. You come here on purpose.
