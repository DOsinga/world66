title: "Highlights"

**Oceanographic Museum**

The single best thing in Monaco. Prince Albert I founded it in 1910; Jacques-Yves Cousteau ran it for decades. The aquarium on the lower floors is one of the finest in Europe, with species — sea dragons, deep-water fish — that you won't see elsewhere. The building itself, on the cliff edge above the sea, is dramatic. Allow two to three hours. The terrace looks along the Riviera toward Italy.

**Monaco-Ville**

The old town on the rock above the port. Narrow cobbled streets, the 13th-century palace, the cathedral with Grace Kelly's tomb, and some of the best views in the principality. The changing of the guard at the palace happens daily at 11:55am. Less crowded than the casino district and the most atmospheric part of Monaco.

**Casino de Monte-Carlo**

Even if you don't gamble, the casino is worth seeing. Charles Garnier designed it — the same architect who did the Paris and Monte Carlo opera houses — and the Belle Époque interiors are extraordinary. Entry to the main gaming rooms costs €18 and requires a passport. Formal dress is enforced after 7pm (jacket and tie for men). The square in front of it, lined with Ferraris and Bentleys, is its own spectacle.

**La Condamine and the Port**

The working harbour district between Monaco-Ville and Monte Carlo. The port is where the grand yachts moor, and during the Grand Prix, it doubles as part of the circuit. Wander the waterfront for the best views of the principality's skyline.

**Prince's Car Collection**

Prince Rainier III assembled over 100 vehicles — Rolls-Royces, Bugattis, Formula 1 cars — which are now on permanent display in Fontvieille. Worth the visit if you have any interest in automotive history.

**Grand Prix Circuit**

Every May, the streets of Monaco become one of Formula 1's most prestigious circuits. Outside race weekend, you can walk the route: through the tunnel, up to Casino Square, down the hairpin at the Hotel de Paris. The combination of impossibly tight streets and the memory of the races gives it a particular atmosphere.
