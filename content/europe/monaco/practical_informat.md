**Visas**

Monaco follows the Schengen Agreement. If you can enter France, you can enter Monaco. Citizens of the EU, US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, the UK, and most other Western countries do not need a visa for stays up to 90 days. Carry your passport — it's worth having on you even though border checks are rare.

**Currency**

The euro. Credit cards are accepted almost everywhere, including small purchases. There are ATMs throughout the principality.

**Language**

French is the official language and what you'll hear in shops, restaurants, and government offices. English is widely spoken in tourist areas and hotels. Italian is common near the Italian border. Most signage is in French.

**Costs**

Monaco is expensive. A coffee at a café in Monte Carlo will cost €4-6; a modest restaurant lunch runs €25-40 per person. There's no way around the fact that this is one of Europe's priciest destinations. Budget travellers should base themselves in Nice or Menton and visit as a day trip.

**Opening hours**

Shops typically open Monday to Saturday, 10am to 7pm. Museums vary — check ahead for the Oceanographic Museum and the Palace, which have seasonal hours. The Casino de Monte-Carlo opens at 2pm for slot machines and from around 4pm for table games; formal dress is required in the gaming rooms after 7pm.

**Casino entry**

You must be 18 or over, show a passport (driver's licences are not accepted), and pay an entry fee of €18 for the main gaming rooms. The public areas with slot machines have a lower cover charge.

**Electricity**

230V, type E plugs (French/Belgian two-pin round). Same as the rest of continental Europe.

**Emergency number:** 112 (European standard). Monaco also has its own police: 17.
