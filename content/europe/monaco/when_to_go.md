---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Monaco's Mediterranean climate means it's pleasant year-round, but the seasons vary more than you might expect.

**Spring (April–May)** is the best time to visit. Temperatures are warm (18–22°C), crowds are manageable, and prices are lower than summer. The rose garden at Fontvieille is at its best in late April and May. The Monaco Grand Prix falls in late May — spectacular if you're there for it, but the city fills up completely and hotel prices become eye-watering for that week.

**Summer (June–August)** is peak season: hot, sunny, and crowded. Sea temperatures are ideal for swimming at Larvotto beach. July and August bring the densest tourist numbers and the highest prices. The heat can be oppressive in the middle of the day — the principality's density of concrete and glass intensifies it.

**Autumn (September–October)** is another good window. The Monaco Yacht Show takes over the port in September, which is either a draw or a reason to avoid it depending on your interests. By October, the crowds have thinned and the weather is still warm enough to enjoy the terraces and the seafront.

**Winter (November–March)** is quiet and mild. Temperatures rarely drop below 8°C and snow is essentially unheard of. The International Circus Festival runs in late January, and the Monte Carlo Rally happens in January too. Otherwise it's a good time for a peaceful visit at off-season prices — though some beach facilities close.
