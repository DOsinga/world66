Bjelasica is a mountain massif in northern Montenegro, covering parts of five municipalities. The highest point is Crna Glava (Black Head) at 2,139 metres. The mountain has 630 square kilometres of territory and is protected as a nature park.

**Kolašin** is the main town and the base for winter skiing — the Kolašin 1450 and Kolašin 1600 ski areas sit on the mountain's slopes. The facilities are newer and better-maintained than Durmitor's Žabljak ski area, and the town itself is more developed as a resort. Kolašin sits on the Belgrade–Bar railway line, making it unusually accessible by train from the coast and from Serbia.

In summer, Bjelasica is hiking territory. The Biogradska Gora National Park on the mountain's western flank contains one of the last primeval forests in Europe — a dense, old-growth beech and fir forest around Lake Biogradsko. The lake and the surrounding forest can be reached by car from the main Podgorica–Belgrade highway; a short walk leads through forest that has barely changed in centuries.

Bjelasica is roughly 80km from Podgorica, about an hour and a half by road.
