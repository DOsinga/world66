---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**[Kotor](/europe/montenegro/kotor)** is the star of the Adriatic coast — a compact medieval city enclosed by 4.5km of Venetian walls, with the Bay of Kotor fanning out below. The old town is genuinely beautiful, with well-preserved churches, cats everywhere (the city has an official cat mascot), and a market under the walls selling dried figs, mushrooms, and olive oil. It's also a popular cruise port, so aim to be there in the morning before the ships dock.

**[Budva](/europe/montenegro/budva)** is the main beach resort, with a photogenic old town on a small peninsula surrounded by water. The beaches south of town are better than the crowded strip in front of the hotel zone. In summer it is loud and packed; in May and September it is considerably nicer.

**[Sveti Stefan](/europe/montenegro/sveti_stefan)** is the image on every Montenegro postcard — a turreted village on a tiny island connected to the shore by a narrow causeway. The island itself has been a private hotel since 2007 (Aman Resorts; very expensive). But the mainland beaches on either side are public and excellent, with fine pink-tinged sand and clear water.

**[Durmitor](/europe/montenegro/dormitor)** National Park offers the best hiking and rafting in the country. The Tara Canyon — at 1300 metres deep, the deepest in Europe — is spectacular even from the road, and rafting the lower section takes you through the gorge properly. Žabljak, the nearest town, is a decent base. Snow usually covers the plateau into late spring.

**Lake Skadar** is the largest lake in the Balkans, shared between Montenegro and Albania. The Montenegrin side is a national park — you can rent a boat or kayak from Virpazar to visit floating meadows of water lilies and tiny island monasteries. Birdwatchers can tick 270 species.

**[Cetinje](/europe/montenegro/cetinje)** was the capital of Montenegro until 1918 and has a slightly melancholy beauty to it — grand embassies built for a tiny kingdom, a palace turned museum, and streets that feel genuinely untouched by tourism. Worth a half-day, especially combined with the coastal road from Budva.

**[Perast](/europe/montenegro/perast)** is an immaculate baroque village on Kotor Bay, with two small islands just offshore — Our Lady of the Rocks (man-made, with a church full of votive offerings) and a natural island with a monastery. A boat to the islands takes ten minutes. There's not a lot to do but it's one of the most pleasant places in the country to sit and have coffee.
