title: "Books"
type: section
---

Amersfoort is a small city but it has produced one of the most internationally recognised figures in modern art. These books help place the city and its most famous son in context.

**Mondrian: His Life's Work and Evolution** by Michel Seuphor — The standard biography of Mondriaan in English, tracing his path from the Dutch Reformed household in Amersfoort through the Hague School to the primary-colour grids that made him famous. Useful to read before visiting the Mondriaanhuis.

**The Embarrassment of Riches** by Simon Schama — Schama's cultural history of the Dutch Golden Age explores what it meant to be Dutch — the Calvinist frugality, the merchant ambition, the tension between wealth and guilt — in a way that makes sense of small Dutch cities like Amersfoort as much as Amsterdam.

**The Assault** by Harry Mulisch — Set in the occupied Netherlands, Mulisch's novel follows the long aftermath of a wartime reprisal. Kamp Amersfoort, the concentration camp just outside the city, is part of this story's national context.

**Het Bureau** by J.J. Voskuil — A seven-volume Dutch novel about office life in an Amsterdam research institute, beloved in the Netherlands for its deadpan comedy and relentless specificity. Voskuil's flat, controlled prose captures something essentially Dutch that you can feel in a city like Amersfoort.
