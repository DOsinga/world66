A spectacular medieval gate from 1425 that controls both land and water traffic into the city — one of the few combined land-and-water gates still standing in Europe. The huge wooden wheels that operate the water gates still work; watching them chug open is a highlight, especially for children. The gate sits at the point where the Eem enters the old city, and the view from the water side is one of the most photographed scenes in Amersfoort.
