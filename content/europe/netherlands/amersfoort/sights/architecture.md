Above the city of Amersfoort rises the "Lange Jan", the tower of the in 1797 accidentally obliterated “Lieve vrouwe kerk” when the ammunition that was stored here exploded. The tower resembles that of the Utrecht cathedral in form and in the way it was build dis-attached from the main building. There is however no direct source that explains these similarities. 
 

Amersfoort is architecturally speaking a schizophrenic city that has both romantic old buildings and very modern ones that don’t really interact or react on eachother. They just seem to happen to stand in the same city. A renown project is “Kattenbroek” a town development scheme just outside Amersfoort where most leading architects of the Netherlands had the opportunity to build one or a few houses. The result is in some cases very beautiful, in some cases more interesting, but on the whole the experiment can be called a success in the sense that it resulted in a (more or less) social development scheme that’s actually diverse and offers a good quality of living.

**City Walls**

One of the most pleasant ways to discover Amersfoort is a walk along the old city walls. Large parts of this 16th century defense system are still intact, among which some unique city gates.
