---
title: "Things to do in Amersfoort"
type: section
---

The
city of Amersfoort has the appearance and charm of a small town, yet
possessing the bustle of a large city. This second largest city in the
province of Utrecht, Netherlands, had humble origins as a medieval
township. There are many **Amersfoort things to do**. Amersfoort
offers a great deal of sightseeing and shopping in quaint little shops.
**

Sightseeing**

Sightseeing
in Amersfoort is nearly a given with the number of medieval monuments
in the city. Visit the Koppelpoort, the city’s medieval gate, located at
the entrance of the city center. See the Muurhuizen, the traditional
wall houses with outstandingly vivid colors and architectural features.
Also, walk along the city’s many canals that have become a feature of
Amersfoort and other Dutch cities. As a medieval city, Amersfoort’s
Flehite Museum is a must-see, with a clear detailing of the city’s rich
history.

**Shop**

Continuing
with the city’s proud medieval heritage, Amersfoort’s shopping also
generally includes traditional items such as cheeses and handicrafts.
The city center is lined with antique shops that offer anything from a
souvenir trinket to large, carved pottery pieces. Those looking for
something more modern can head to Saint Joris Square, a typical Dutch
mall where anything from clothes to electronics can be found.
**

Dine**

Dining
in the Netherlands can be a challenge with a new cuisine and tastes that
come along with it. Amersfoort is no exception. Taste traditional Dutch
food such as creamed soups and seared tuna at Mama Roux Eethius,
located at the city square. Similarly located in the city center is the
Mariënhof, an upmarket restaurant with a medieval ambiance taken from an
old cloister. Those looking for finger foods can always pop into one of
the many pubs in the area that serve pub food, cheeses and beer.
**

Entertainment**

Dutch
nightlife is famous for being classy, yet wild at the same time.
Experience this at any of the traditional Dutch pubs – alcohol,
traditional music and dancing make an interesting and fun combination.
The arts scene is also active in Amersfoort, with small art galleries
peppering the city. Those looking for something more spontaneous would
surely be delighted by the sheer number of buskers and street
artists who flaunt their skills on the streets of the city.
This
little Dutch city-town offers so many nuggets of treasure among the
typical touristy things that can be done here. Those who are willing to
venture deeper into the city will surely find that there are lots to
things to do in Amersfoort!
