An **Amersfoort travel guide** is a handy resource, as it can help you plan a trip to this Netherland city. While Amersfoort is a fast-growing city, there is also a protected medieval area that is a major tourist draw. The city itself is over 750 years old, and the ruins and remains date back to 1,000 B.C.

#### Main Attractions

The main historic area is in the center of the city. You can view the old city walls on the eastern side of the city. The Koppelpoort is a water and road city gate that is very well preserved. Along the canal are the Muurhuizen, or houses built on top of the medieval wall. The Amersfoortse Kei is a glacial boulder. This is an important landmark, as the city itself has received many boulders as gifts. There is a culinary museum that displays the traditions and eating habits of the surrounding area. There is also the excellent Cavalry Museum, which details the development of the military in the Netherlands.

#### Bicycling

There are several well marked cycling routes around the city. You can find cycle paths that circle through the city or routes that begin in Amersfoort and end elsewhere. The Eneco Veluwe Route begins in Amersfoort and ends at Hoevelaken, a nearby village. The Rondje Utrecht passes through the city and circles around the entire province. The long cross country route, LF9, passes through the city and ends at the German border.

#### Do-it-Yourself Tours

There are some great do-it-yourself tours. You can choose to see the main sights or get off the main tourist track and see some hidden gems. Many travelers enjoy the do-it-yourself tours, as they give them the freedom to change their plans and to enjoy Amersfoort at their own pace. You can pick up some excellent maps at the information center to enjoy several walking and driving tours of the city and the surrounding areas.

#### Where to Eat

You can find a large range of cuisine in the city. Some excellent Dutch restaurants include The Pastinaeck at Court 8, The Mountain Pavilion at Utrectsewg 180 and De Flint at Coninckstraat 60. If you have a hankering for Italian, then stop by Da Luca Italian at the Fair 44-45, Terrazza at Arnhemseweg 1996, or O Sole Mio and Zuidsingel 57. Some recommended French restaurants include Avocado at Piet Mondrian Square 3, the Aubergerie at Camp 88, and Saffraan at Small Troque 3. Some good Spanish options include Bosphorus Restaurant at Krommestraat 28 and Dela Rosa Restaurant Marisqueria at Krommestraat 60.
