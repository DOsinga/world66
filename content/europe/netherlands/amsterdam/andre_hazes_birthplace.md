Gerard Doustraat 67, third floor: the address where André Hazes was born in 1951 and grew up, one of six children in a De Pijp working-class family. In 1957, aged eight, he was discovered singing on the Albert Cuypmarkt by the entertainer Johnny Kraaykamp sr., who heard him busking for money to buy a birthday present for his mother. Hazes went on to become the most beloved Dutch folk singer of the 20th century — his style, a direct descendant of the neighbourhood he came from. The Eddy Bar further along the street was his regular spot.
