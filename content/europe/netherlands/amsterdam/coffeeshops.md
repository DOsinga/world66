Amsterdam's coffeeshops — the ones with the green-and-white sticker in the window — are cannabis cafes where you can legally buy and smoke weed or hash on the premises. They are not the same as coffee houses (koffiehuizen), which just serve coffee. The distinction matters.

Most coffeeshops have a menu listing strains and prices, typically ranging from 8 to 15 euros per gram. Staff can advise if you are not sure what you want. You can usually also get coffee, tea, juice, and snacks, but not alcohol — that is not allowed. Tobacco smoking indoors is also banned, so joints are typically rolled pure or with herbal substitutes.

A few well-known spots: The Bulldog in the Leidseplein area is the most touristy chain but easy to find. Dampkring on Handboogstraat is smaller, mellower, and was featured in the film Ocean's Twelve. Barney's on Haarlemmerstraat is popular with tourists but has consistently good product. For something quieter, try Paradox in the Jordaan or Grey Area on Oude Leliestraat, a tiny American-run shop with a loyal following.

Coffeeshop etiquette is straightforward: buy something if you are going to sit, do not take photos of the menu or other customers, and keep it low-key. Most places close by midnight or 1 AM. The city has been tightening regulations in recent years, but coffeeshops remain legal and widely available in the city center.
