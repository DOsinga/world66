The Amsterdam School housing complex at Burgemeester Tellegenstraat and Pieter Lodewijk Takstraat, designed by Michel de Klerk and Piet Kramer for the socialist housing association De Dageraad ('The Dawn') between 1919 and 1923. Three hundred and fifty dwellings built in multi-coloured brick, with facades that rise in undulating waves — the brickwork itself tilting and curving, the boundary between red and yellow bands suggesting a waterline. The corner at P.L. Takstraat is the best point: the roofline lifts and twists like a ship's prow. This was social housing built on the argument that working people deserved buildings as beautiful as anything in the city centre. Museum De Dageraad inside runs tours.
