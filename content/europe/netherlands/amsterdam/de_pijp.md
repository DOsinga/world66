A dense, lively neighbourhood immediately south of the Rijksmuseum, built in the 1870s and 1880s for workers priced out of the canal ring. The Albert Cuyp market runs through the middle of it six days a week — the largest street market in the Netherlands. De Pijp is where Amsterdam eats and drinks on an ordinary weeknight: a dense concentration of restaurants, brown cafes, and coffee bars, many on the side streets off Ferdinand Bolstraat. The neighbourhood's late-19th-century brick terraces are visually uniform but architecturally interesting for what they reveal about speculative development and the pressures of rapid urbanisation.
