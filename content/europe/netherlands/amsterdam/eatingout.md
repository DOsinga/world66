---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Amsterdam eats well and internationally. The city's colonial history means Indonesian food is everywhere — rijsttafel (rice table) is worth trying at least once. The Korte and Lange Leidsedwarsstraat near Leidseplein are the most concentrated restaurant streets if you want variety in a small area. De Pijp, around the Albert Cuyp market, has better and more authentic options at lower prices. Most Dutch people eat early — kitchens close by 22:00 in most places.
