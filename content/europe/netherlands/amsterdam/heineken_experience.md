The original Heineken brewery, a vast red-brick industrial building at Stadhouderskade 78 that Gerard Adriaan Heineken opened in 1867 on the northern edge of what would become De Pijp. Brewing continued here for 121 years until April 1988, when the site closed and production moved outside the city. The building is now a tourist attraction, but the exterior — gabled Dutch facades wrapping an industrial-scale plant — is worth stopping at: it marks the moment the neighbourhood was born, built to house the workers who filled places like this.
