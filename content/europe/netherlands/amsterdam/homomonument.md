Three pink granite triangles at the edge of Westermarkt, opened in 1987 as the world's first memorial to gay men and lesbians persecuted by the Nazi regime. The pink triangle was the badge homosexuals were forced to wear in Nazi concentration camps; the LGBTQ+ community reclaimed it as a symbol of resistance. Designer Karin Daan oriented the three triangles purposefully: one points toward Dam Square and the national war memorial, one toward the COC headquarters — home to the oldest gay rights organisation in the world, founded in Amsterdam in 1946 — and one toward the Westerkerk.
