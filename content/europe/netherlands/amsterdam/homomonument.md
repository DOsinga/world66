Three pink granite triangles set into the paving of Westermarkt, a few steps from the Westerkerk. The monument was designed by Karin Daan and unveiled in 1987 — the first of its kind in the world. Each triangle has a meaning: one for the past (commemorating gay men and women killed by the Nazis, who were forced to wear a downward-pointing pink triangle in the camps), one for the present (the struggle for equality), one for the future. On national Remembrance Day, 4 May, the square fills with people who lay flowers here alongside the other war memorials.
