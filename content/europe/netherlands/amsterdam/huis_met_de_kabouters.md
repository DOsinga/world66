Ceintuurbaan 251–255, built in 1884 by A.C. Boerma: a 27-metre-wide, ornately decorated building standing conspicuously out among the neighbourhood's plain 19th-century blocks. Two gnome (kabouter) figures, each 2.5 metres tall, stand at opposite ends of the roofline as though mid-throw. A rijksmonument since 1984. Local legend holds that the gnomes exchange the ball at midnight — though in the more demanding version of the story, only on 29 February in a leap year. The building is three houses combined into one facade, which explains both the scale and the ambition.
