A largely forgotten cemetery on the southern edge of the city, overgrown with ivy and wildflowers and rarely visited. Huis te Vraag was Amsterdam's main municipal cemetery from 1894 until it was closed for new burials in the 1950s, and nature has been slowly reclaiming it since.

The atmosphere is unlike anything else in Amsterdam — quiet, green, and slightly melancholy. Grand 19th-century tombs and monuments stand among mature trees, some tilting as roots shift the ground beneath them. Several notable Amsterdammers are buried here, including members of prominent Jewish families. The cemetery is technically open to the public but feels like a secret — the entrance is unmarked and you may have the entire grounds to yourself. Located near the Zuidas business district, a strange contrast with the glass towers visible over the treeline. A good destination for anyone who appreciates the beauty of forgotten places.
