Amsterdam's most atmospheric neighbourhood, built between 1612 and the 1650s as a working-class district west of the main canals. The grid of narrow streets, stepped-gable houses, and hidden almshouse courtyards (hofjes) is largely intact. Today the Jordaan is expensive and fashionable, but the bones of the original neighbourhood remain legible at street level. The Westerkerk anchors the south end; Brouwersgracht forms the northern boundary. The best walking is on Bloemgracht and Egelantiersgracht, away from the tourist routes on Westermarkt.
