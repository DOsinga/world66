The best neighbourhood in Amsterdam for walking without a plan. Built in the early 17th century as a workers' district, it was run-down for most of the 20th century before artists and young residents took it over in the 1970s. Now it is expensive but still has the feel of a place where people actually live — narrow streets, small canals, hofjes (almshouse courtyards), brown cafes and independent shops.
