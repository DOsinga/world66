---
title: "Mondrian's Studio, Albert Cuypstraat 272"
type: poi
latitude: 52.356877

---

Piet Mondrian registered at Albert Cuypstraat 272 on 28 June 1906 and lived here until 1908, using a top-floor room with large north-facing windows as a studio. The market that would make this street famous was already operating below. Mondrian was then working in a representational style — canals, dunes, church facades — before the shift towards abstraction that eventually produced the black-grid paintings. He left De Pijp for Ruysdaelkade 75, a few streets north, and Amsterdam altogether in 1912, bound for Paris.
