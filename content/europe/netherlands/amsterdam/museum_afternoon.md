Choose one museum. The [Rijksmuseum](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/rijksmuseum) and the [Van Gogh Museum](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/vangogh_museum) are both on Museumplein and are both world-class — you do not have time to do both properly in a single afternoon. The Rijksmuseum is broader: Rembrandt, Vermeer, Delft, applied arts, the whole sweep of Dutch culture across the centuries. The Van Gogh is narrower and more intense: 200 paintings by one artist spanning a decade of work that moves from the dark browns of Nuenen to the burning yellows of Arles to the grey urgency of the last months in Auvers. Pick based on your mood and book tickets in advance for both.

Spend two hours in whichever you choose. Look at fewer things more carefully rather than covering the whole floor. Then leave.

Walk across Museumplein to [Vondelpark](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/vondelpark). This is Amsterdam's central park — 47 hectares of paths, ponds, rose gardens, and grass used by everyone in the city. On a warm afternoon it fills completely: students with bikes, families on picnic blankets, dog-walkers, skaters, two-year-olds chasing pigeons. Find a patch of grass, lie down, and let the afternoon dissolve. The open-air theatre at the north end runs free performances in summer. The pavilion café has a large terrace.

Leave when you're hungry. The Cornelis Schuytstraat, five minutes south of the park, has good food options for dinner.
