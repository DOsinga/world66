Walk to the back of Centraal Station and take the free ferry across the IJ to Amsterdam-Noord. The crossing takes fifteen minutes and costs nothing. On the far side, the view back to the city — church spires, the station roof, cranes, water — is one of the better views Amsterdam offers.

Take the NDSM ferry (not the Buiksloterweg one). The [NDSM wharf](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/ndsm) is a former shipbuilding yard the size of several city blocks, closed in the 1980s and now occupied by artists, creative businesses, and what is arguably the most convincing industrial regeneration in northern Europe. The buildings are enormous — designed to contain ocean-going ships — and largely unrestored. That is part of the point.

[STRAAT](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/straat) museum is in a sub-hall of the main wharf: 150 large-scale murals by artists from 30 countries, painted directly on the walls and floor. The scale is genuinely immense — many pieces are several storeys high. Spend an hour to ninety minutes here. Photography is allowed and the light, filtering through the industrial skylights, is good.

After STRAAT, walk around [NDSM Plein](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/ndsm_plein), the central square. Pllek, the restaurant and bar built from shipping containers at the water's edge, is the best outdoor drinking spot in Amsterdam when the weather is right. Get a beer and look across the water at the city you came from.

The ferry back runs every fifteen minutes. The light on the return crossing in late afternoon is exceptional.
