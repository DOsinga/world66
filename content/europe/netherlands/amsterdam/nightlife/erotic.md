Besides rock 'n'roll, sex and drugs are also part of the (much overrated) myths about Amsterdam. The main red light district is located in ancient center of town. This used to be the center of the seafaring city Amsterdam and with the sailors came all things seamen like, including prostitution. Over the years, as the harbour declined, the red light district took over the old warehouses and bank buildings and allthough sometimes you still stumble onto a little shop where coffee has been sold since 1659, it is mostly sex on sale, though you can also find departments of Amsterdam University, art galleries and very good hotels.


 That said, the red light district is a very unsleazy affair as far as unsleazy prostitution areas go. Indeed as one of the more liberal American politicians observed in amazement, it is here that you can see a bus of elderly German tourists unload at 11:00 pm to take a walk around.


 The main red light district centers around the Oudezijdsachterburgwal, south-east of Central Station, behind Dam square. There are also some windows open for business along the Singel and the Ruysdaelkade near the Albert Cuyp market. 


 It is fairly safe to walk this area where the canals get a red glow from all the red lights streaming out of the windows, illuminating the prostitutes. Sex clubs, videoshops and sex stores also show their offerings, creating an atmosphere that sometimes makes visitors of a more conservative nature feel uncomfortable.
