Amsterdam is well known for its 'bruin cafes', a typical Dutch sort of pub. Literally it means brown pub, and basically that's what it is: brown wooden interior, fake persian carpets on the tables, the smell of beer, local people sitting in their regular spot. Where old men play cards and billiards and drink their genever.

 Of course, you can also find plenty of trendy bars and grand cafés, with tapas, cocktails and cappucino, visited by the young and fashionable, or any place else that you fancy. 

 A typical café stays open during the week until 1am, 2am in the weekend. Night cafes stay open for one or two hours longer. If you're desperate for a drink after two during the week, you'll have to go to a club, or a late night café.

 The list here should give you a general idea of what's available. Amsterdam is a city of opposites: so go out exploring and you'll find much interesting places. And you're welcome to add your findings and suggestions to our site!
