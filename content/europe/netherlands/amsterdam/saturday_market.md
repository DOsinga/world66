The Noordermarkt on Saturday morning is the most Amsterdam experience the city has left. Arrive at the square in front of the [Noorderkerk](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/noorderkerk) before nine if you want the farmers' market at its best — organic vegetables, raw-milk cheese, sourdough bread, and honey from hives kept somewhere in the Dutch countryside. Locals come here to actually shop; bring a bag. The flea market wraps around the church at the same time, selling second-hand clothing, books, and records to a different, equally local crowd.

By ten the stalls are busy and the surrounding cafés have filled up. The De Bolhoed on Prinsengracht has been here for decades and does a good breakfast. Or get a coffee and a stroopwafel from one of the stalls and eat standing at the square.

After the market, walk the smaller canals. [Bloemgracht](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/bloemgracht) — two streets south of the Noorderkerk — is the most beautiful canal in the Jordaan: three rows of 17th-century houses reflected in still water, almost no foot traffic even on a Saturday. Then [Egelantiersgracht](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/egelantiersgracht), narrower still, lined with houseboats and leaning facades. The Jordaan is best on foot, at walking pace, without a destination. Trust the layout.

The market breaks down by noon. Be there before it does.
