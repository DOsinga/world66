A 17th-century *hofje* — a small courtyard almshouse built by wealthy merchants for the city's poor — at Egelantiersgracht 107. Step through the gate and a covered passage leads into a courtyard with whitewashed walls, a garden, and complete quiet; the noise of the surrounding streets disappears. Usually open during the day. One of the best-preserved hofjes in the Jordaan and one of the easiest to enter.
