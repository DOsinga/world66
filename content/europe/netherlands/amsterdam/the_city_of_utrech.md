Utrecht sits just 45 minutes by train from Amsterdam and repays an easy day trip. The city's canals are unlike Amsterdam's — they run at a lower level than the street, with wharf cellars built into the banks that now house cafes and restaurants. Walk along the Oudegracht (Old Canal) and you can sit at water level, looking up at the medieval facades above you.

The Dom Tower is the tallest church tower in the Netherlands at 112 metres, and you can climb it for panoramic views across the flat Dutch landscape. The cathedral nave it once anchored was demolished by a violent storm in 1674, leaving the tower stranded in the middle of a square at an odd distance from the choir. The gap between them is still there — it became a street.

Utrecht was founded by the Romans as Trajectum ad Rhenum (ford on the Rhine) in the first century. The old centre is compact and walkable. The Centraal Museum has a strong collection of Dutch design and art, including the De Stijl furniture of Gerrit Rietveld, whose Rietveld Schröderhuis — a UNESCO-listed house just outside the centre — is one of the masterpieces of 20th-century architecture.

Trains run several times an hour from Amsterdam Centraal.
