Built between 1648 and 1665 as the most prestigious town hall in the Dutch world, the palace on Dam Square was designed by Jacob van Campen in Palladian style. Napoleon's brother Louis converted it into a royal residence in 1808, and the 18th-century furniture from that period is still inside. The Dutch royal family lives in The Hague — this is used for state occasions and is open to visitors.
