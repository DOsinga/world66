Arnhem is a mid-sized Dutch city on the Lower Rhine, known internationally as the setting of *A Bridge Too Far* -- the failed Allied airborne operation of September 1944. Today the city wears that history lightly: a rebuilt bridge named after the operation, the excellent Airborne Museum in nearby Oosterbeek, and a few memorials are all that remain.

What strikes visitors first is how green the city is. Arnhem sits at the edge of the Veluwe, the largest forested area in the Netherlands, and the parks spill right into the centre. The Sonsbeek park alone is worth an afternoon of wandering. The city is also one of the last places in the Netherlands where trolleybuses still run -- quiet, electric, and oddly charming.

The centre is compact and walkable. The [Korenmarkt](/europe/netherlands/arnhem/bars_and_cafes) and surrounding squares come alive in the evening with a good mix of pubs, restaurants, and clubs. Shopping is concentrated around the Ketelstraat and Vijzelstraat, and the Saturday market on the Marktplein is worth a visit.

Arnhem itself rates a half-day if you stick to the centre, but the real draws are nearby: the [Dutch Open Air Museum](https://openluchtmuseum.nl), [Burgers' Zoo](https://www.burgerszoo.nl), and further out the [Hoge Veluwe National Park](/europe/netherlands/dehogeveluwe) with its Kroller-Muller Museum and its collection of van Goghs.
