Painters, poets, and other artists have been drawn here since the nineteenth century, and something of that creative atmosphere survives today.

Bergen is the artist village of the Netherlands. A few kilometres from [Alkmaar](/europe/netherlands/alkmaar) and its famous cheese market, Bergen sits where the woods meet the dunes and the flat polders stretch inland. Trees outnumber houses in every street, and the landscape has drawn painters, poets, and musicians for over a century.

If you care about art, come in October for the **Kunsttiendaagse** — ten days during which a hundred local artists open their studios to the public and explain their work. If you prefer classical music, the **International Holland Music Sessions** in August bring the most promising young musicians to Bergen for masterclasses and concerts. Three weeks in a row, two or more concerts a day take place in venues across town. Try to catch one in the **Ruïnekerk**, a small church five centuries old in the very centre of Bergen.

The **Jazz & Sail** festival on the first weekend of September combines three days of jazz in the village and on the beach at Bergen aan Zee with catamaran racing offshore.
