Bergen is the artist village of North Holland — a green, quiet place where trees outnumber houses and the dunes and polders are always a short walk away. Located just a few kilometres from [Alkmaar](/europe/netherlands/alkmaar), famous for its cheese market, Bergen has attracted painters, poets, and musicians for over a century. The Bergen School of expressionist painters made their name here in the early 20th century, and the creative atmosphere hasn't faded.

The centre of Bergen is small and walkable. Everything worth seeing is within strolling distance: the café-lined main square, the 15th-century Ruïnekerk at the heart of town, the modest galleries dotted through the streets. In October the Kunsttiendaagse brings that world out into the open — some hundred local artists open their studios for ten days so visitors can watch them work and talk directly to the people making the art.

For classical music, August is the month to be here. The International Holland Music Sessions run for three weeks, bringing promising young musicians to Bergen for masterclasses and public concerts. The Ruïnekerk is the best venue — an ancient ruined church with remarkable acoustics and atmosphere.

Bergen aan Zee, a short bus ride away, is the seaside annex: a small beach resort with wide dune scenery that many Dutch families return to year after year.
