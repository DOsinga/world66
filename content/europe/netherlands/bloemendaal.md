Bloemendaal is a prosperous municipality on the North Holland coast, sandwiched between the dunes and the city of Haarlem just to the east. It is best known for its stretch of beach — Bloemendaal aan Zee — which rivals nearby [Zandvoort](/europe/netherlands/zandvoort) for sand and surf but has a livelier, more festive atmosphere, particularly in summer when its beach clubs draw large crowds from [Amsterdam](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam).

The beach clubs are the main draw. Places like Woodstock and Republiek have made Bloemendaal synonymous with outdoor dancing, DJs, and cold drinks in the sun. On a hot weekend the scene can feel more like Ibiza than the Netherlands, which is either a selling point or a reason to head further up the coast, depending on your mood.

Getting here takes a little more effort than reaching Zandvoort — there is no direct train, so you will need to take a bus from Haarlem or use one of the seasonal direct connections from Amsterdam. The dunes between Haarlem and the coast are part of the Zuid-Kennemerland National Park, and worth exploring on foot or by bike if you have time before or after the beach.
