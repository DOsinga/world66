Den Helder is the northernmost city on the Dutch mainland, sitting at the tip of the North Holland peninsula where the North Sea meets the Wadden Sea. It is best known as a navy town -- the Royal Netherlands Navy has been based here since the early nineteenth century -- and as the departure point for ferries to the island of [Texel](/europe/netherlands/texel), one of the most popular of the Wadden Islands.

The Marinemuseum, set in a former naval dockyard, is the main attraction and one of the best maritime museums in the Netherlands. You can board a submarine, walk around a minesweeper, and explore exhibits covering centuries of Dutch naval history. The Fort Kijkduin complex, a Napoleonic-era fortification at the tip of the peninsula, houses a small aquarium and offers views across the shipping lanes of the Marsdiep strait.

Den Helder is not a pretty town -- wartime bombing and postwar rebuilding left it with a functional rather than charming appearance. But the surrounding coastline is dramatic, with wide beaches on the North Sea side and the vast tidal flats of the Wadden Sea on the other. The ferry to Texel takes just twenty minutes and runs frequently, making Den Helder an almost unavoidable stop if you are heading to the islands.
