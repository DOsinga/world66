title: Things to Do
type: section
---

Den Helder's maritime past is its main draw. The Marinemuseum is the standout attraction — it traces four centuries of Dutch naval history with an impressive collection of ship models, navigational instruments, and full-sized vessels moored in the harbour outside. The historic fort and the submarine moored alongside are among the best preserved of their kind in the Netherlands.

The fortifications around the city form part of the Defence Line of Amsterdam, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and several of the old coastal batteries and bunkers can be explored on foot or by bike.
