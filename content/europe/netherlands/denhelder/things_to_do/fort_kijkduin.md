Fort Kijkduin is a nineteenth-century coastal fortress built into the dunes south of the city. The fort itself is partially open to visitors, and the area around it is good for walking along the sea dike with views over the Marsdiep. A colony of grey seals regularly hauls out on the sandbanks visible from the dike — binoculars are useful.
