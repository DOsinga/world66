Deventer is one of the oldest cities in the Netherlands, sitting on the banks of the IJssel river in the province of Overijssel. It has a beautifully preserved medieval centre, a strong literary tradition, and one of the best-known food specialities in the country: the Deventer Koek, a spiced honey cake that has been baked here since the fifteenth century. You can buy it at Bussink on the Brink, which has been selling it since 1593.

The old town is compact and rewarding to explore on foot. The Brink, the main square, is one of the largest and most handsome in the Netherlands, lined with medieval and Renaissance buildings and anchored by the Waag (weigh house), which dates from 1528. The Bergkerk, up on the hill above the river, offers fine views. Deventer's literary credentials are strong -- it was a centre of the Brethren of the Common Life, the religious movement that educated Erasmus, and today it hosts one of the largest outdoor book markets in Europe each August.

The city has a lively cultural scene for its size, with good restaurants and cafes along the riverfront and in the old centre. The Speelgoedmuseum (toy museum) and the Museum de Waag are both worth a visit. Deventer is easily reached by train from Amsterdam, Utrecht, or Zwolle, and makes a fine day trip or a pleasant overnight stop on a tour of the eastern Netherlands.
