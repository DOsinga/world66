Deventer is one of the oldest cities in the Netherlands, a former Hanseatic trading town on the river IJssel with a medieval centre that has survived remarkably intact. The Bergkwartier neighbourhood, climbing the hill above the river, is a dense tangle of cobbled streets, leaning gabled houses, and hidden courtyards — one of the most atmospheric old quarters in the country.

The city is famous for its Deventer Boekenmarkt, the largest book market in Europe, held every August when kilometres of stalls line both sides of the river. Outside festival season, Deventer is a pleasant, walkable city with good museums (the Speelgoedmuseum and Historisch Museum Deventer), a thriving food scene centred on the Brink square, and a local delicacy — Deventer koek, a spiced cake that has been baked here since the 15th century. It makes an excellent stop between [Amsterdam](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam) and the east of the country.
