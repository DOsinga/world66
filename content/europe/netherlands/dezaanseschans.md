De Zaanse Schans is a living open-air museum on the Zaan River, about 30 minutes north of [Amsterdam](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam). Named after an entrenchment built in 1574 to hold back Spanish troops during the Eighty Years War, the area preserves a cluster of wooden windmills, workshops and houses from the Zaan district's industrial heyday.

People still live and work here, which keeps it from feeling entirely like a theme park — though on summer weekends it comes close. The main attractions are the working windmills (grinding mustard, oil and pigments), a clog-making workshop where you can watch wooden shoes being carved on a lathe, and the original Albert Heijn shop, where the now-vast Dutch supermarket chain started in 1887. There is also a cheese museum and several craft shops.

The site is free to enter; individual museums and workshops charge small fees. It is an easy bike ride from Amsterdam — about 20 km — or a short train ride to Koog-Zaandijk station.
