Diever is an old village on the edge of the Drents-Friese Wold national park in Drenthe, in the north of the [Netherlands](/europe/netherlands). The village has old farms, a medieval church, and just outside it a hunebed — one of the megalithic dolmens that are Drenthe's most distinctive ancient monuments.

The surrounding woods and heathland are excellent for cycling and walking, with trails that wind through the national park for hours. In summer, the Shakespeare theatre festival in Diever — performed outdoors in the forest — draws audiences from across the country.
