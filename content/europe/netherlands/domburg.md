Domburg is the oldest seaside resort in the Netherlands, perched on the Zeeland coast where the sand dunes meet the North Sea. It has been drawing visitors since the early 19th century, and painters followed — Piet Mondrian spent summers here around 1909, inspired by the sea, the church tower, and the dunes. The light on this coast is genuinely different from the rest of the Netherlands: bright, clean, and constantly changing.

The town is small and walkable, with a compact centre of shops and restaurants set back from a wide sandy beach. In summer it fills with Dutch and German families; outside the season it is quiet enough to have the beach to yourself. The dunes behind the town are protected nature, good for walking, and the rest of the Walcheren peninsula — [Middelburg](/europe/netherlands/middelburg), Veere, the Delta Works — is all within easy reach.
