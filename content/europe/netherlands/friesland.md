Friesland (Fryslân in the local tongue) is the Netherlands' northernmost mainland province and the only one with its own language. Frisian sits closer to English than to Dutch, and Frisians wear their identity proudly: their own flag with red water-lily leaves, their own forenames, their own anthem, and a long memory of having never quite been ruled by anyone for good. It is a province of wide skies, water everywhere, and a people who would rather be on the ice or under sail than indoors.

The capital is **[Leeuwarden](/europe/netherlands/friesland/leeuwarden)**, a handsome old town that was European Capital of Culture in 2018 and home to the leaning Oldehove tower and the Fries Museum. Around it lie the famous **Elf Steden** — the eleven historic cities including [Sneek](/europe/netherlands/friesland/sneek), [Bolsward](/europe/netherlands/friesland/bolsward), [Franeker](/europe/netherlands/friesland/franeker), [Dokkum](/europe/netherlands/friesland/dokkum), [Harlingen](/europe/netherlands/friesland/harlingen), [Hindeloopen](/europe/netherlands/friesland/hindeloopen), [Stavoren](/europe/netherlands/friesland/stavoren), [Workum](/europe/netherlands/friesland/workum), [IJlst](/europe/netherlands/friesland/ijlst) and Sloten — all small, all walkable, all linked by a 200km route that becomes the legendary **Elfstedentocht** when the canals freeze hard enough. That has happened only fifteen times in the last century, but when it does the country stops to watch.

Off the north coast lie four of the five Dutch **Wadden Islands** — Vlieland, Terschelling, Ameland and Schiermonnikoog — sandbar worlds of dunes, empty beaches, bicycles and quiet villages. The mainland in between is a flat patchwork of meadows grazed by the black-and-white Frisian cow (the breed every dairy farmer in the world has heard of), interrupted by the sail-dotted lakes of the southwest, the **Friese Meren**, where the Dutch upper middle class keeps its boats. Speed skating is a religion here, with **Thialf** in [Heerenveen](/europe/netherlands/friesland/heerenveen) the temple — turn up during a championship and you will see what real fanatics look like. And once a year someone wins **fierljeppen**, the local sport in which competitors sprint at a long pole, climb it mid-vault and try to land in a sandpit on the far side of a ditch. Not every province has its own pole-vaulting-over-water tradition.

Friesland rewards travellers who slow down. Hire a sailing boat in [Sneek](/europe/netherlands/friesland/sneek), bike out to [Holwerd](/europe/netherlands/friesland/holwerd) and take the ferry to Ameland, wander the cobbles of Hindeloopen with its painted furniture tradition, or just find a terrace by a canal in any of the eleven cities and watch the boats go through.
