Built by wool comber Eise Eisinga between 1774 and 1781 in response to a popular panic about a predicted planetary catastrophe, this mechanical planetarium is set into the ceiling of his house and still runs on its original clockwork mechanism. It is the oldest working planetarium in the world, and a UNESCO Memory of the World listed object. The house itself is preserved much as Eisinga left it.
