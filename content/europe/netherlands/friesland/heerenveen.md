Heerenveen is a town of about 50,000 in central [Friesland](/europe/netherlands/friesland), best known internationally as the spiritual home of Dutch speed skating. The Thialf arena here is the most famous speed skating venue in the world — World Cup events and Dutch championships are held here regularly, and the atmosphere on race nights is electric.

Beyond ice, Heerenveen is a pleasant if unremarkable Frisian town. The football club SC Heerenveen punches above its weight in the Eredivisie and fills the Abe Lenstra Stadium on match days. The surrounding Frisian countryside of lakes, canals, and wide skies is good cycling territory, and the tiny fortified town of [Sloten](/europe/netherlands/friesland/sloten) — one of the smallest cities in the Netherlands — is a short ride away.
