Hindeloopen (Hylpen in Frisian) is one of the eleven historic Frisian towns, sitting on the western shore of the IJsselmeer. It reached its peak as a trading port in the 17th century, when its sailors carried goods across the Baltic and developed a distinctive local culture that set them apart from the rest of Friesland — their own dialect, their own style of dress, and their own vivid decorative folk art, with furniture and household objects painted in bold red, green, and blue patterns.

The town has been in steady decline since those days but retains its old character: cobblestone streets, historic gabled houses, and a quiet waterfront that invites an aimless wander. The Hidde Nijland Museum, housed in the former town hall, gives a thorough survey of 18th and 19th-century Hindeloopen life, including the famous painted furniture and costumes.

The IJsselmeer makes Hindeloopen a decent spot for watersports, and in a hard winter the frozen lake draws speed skaters — the town has a particular place in the mythology of the Elfstedentocht, the legendary skating race through all eleven Frisian towns.
