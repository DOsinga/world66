Holwerd is a small village on the Friesland coast, best known as the ferry port for [Ameland](/europe/netherlands/friesland/ameland). The crossing takes about 45 minutes and runs several times a day.

The village is also an excellent base for wadlopen — guided walks across the Wadden Sea mudflats at low tide. It is a strange and exhilarating experience: kilometres of open seabed stretching to the horizon, with nothing between you and the islands but wet sand and the occasional seal.
