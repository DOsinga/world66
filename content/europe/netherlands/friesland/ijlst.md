IJlst is one of the eleven historic Frisian cities, though it is barely more than a village — the smallest of the eleven by far. It sits on the river Geeuw, practically touching [Sneek](/europe/netherlands/friesland/sneek) but stubbornly holding on to its own city rights since 1268.

The old centre is charming in a quiet way: a single main street, a working wind-powered sawmill (De Rat), and a handful of houses along the water. IJlst was once the centre of the Dutch ice-skate manufacturing industry, and a small museum documents the craft. Today the town is mostly a base for watersports on the Frisian lakes.
