---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Giethoorn has a handful of restaurants rather than a proper dining scene — it's a small village. Most places are casual and cater to day-trippers. Smit's Paviljoen, just outside the village toward Weerribben-Wieden National Park, is the most established restaurant: traditional Dutch cooking, a good buffet, and capacity for large groups. Grand Café Fanfare is a reliable all-day option with homemade soups and Dutch stews. T'Vonder and De Punter are pleasant canal-side places for a quiet lunch. Most restaurants are closed or reduced in winter.
