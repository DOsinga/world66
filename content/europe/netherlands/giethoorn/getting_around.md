---
title: "Getting Around in Giethoorn"
type: section
---

Giethoorn
is a beautiful village in the Dutch country of Netherlands, which is
famous for its natural beauty and is often addressed as Venice of the
North. If you are planning to visit the city, then this travel guide
will help you to understand the details and travel modes of the
village.

**Travel by Car **

The road
connectivity of the village to the major cities is very possible, as the
A1 highway runs down directly to the village through the Overijssel
province. A28 road can be taken from Zwolle city to move
in and out of the village. Car travel will enable you to see the
picturesque beauty of the country roads and the canal that runs all the
way, until you reach Giethoorn.

**Railway Travel **

The
intercity trains run directly from the Schiphol Airport and Amsterdam’s
Central Station, which have a minor halt at the village.

**Hire a Cycle or a Boat **

The
village is famous for its scenic beauty, and there are several small
shops and camps that provide cycles for moving along the rivers and
canals. Those who enjoy the water and adventure
sports can hire canoes and boats to enjoy the lakes and canals in the
village. The village is famous for its boating adventures, and you can
find many big and small boats for rent in the village. However, the
canals of the village are narrow and you must not create problems by
driving above the speed limit, if your boat is motor driven.
