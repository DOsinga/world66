**Giethoorn Practical Information** is a great way to get acquainted with some basic information pertaining to Giethoorn and the Netherlands before your arrival, to make your trip run smoothly. Giethoorn (translated as horn of goats) is a small village within the country of the Netherlands, and was made famous by the Dutch film maker Bert Haanstra in his comedy film "Fanfare." Often referred to as the "Venice of the North" or the "Venice of the Netherlands", Giethoorn is a quaint village where the mode of transportation is either by bicycle or by boat through one of the many canals.

**Location**

Giethoorn is located in the Dutch province of Overijssel, in the municipality of Steenwijkerland, roughly 3 miles from Steenwijk. Within the Netherlands, Giethoorn is located in the northern region of the country, and bordered by the North Sea in the north, Belgium to the south and Germany to the east. Giethoorn, Netherlands are part of the European Union on the continent of Europe. The exact coordinates for Giethoorn are 52 degrees 44'20" north and 6 degrees 4'41" east.
**
Population**

The population of Giethoorn is estimated at a little over 2,600 residents.

**Language**

The official language of Giethoorn, as well as the entire Netherlands, is the Dutch language. The Dutch language is derived from a West German language, and is spoken by over 22 million people world wide as their native language. The Dutch language is also associated with Belgium and Suriname.

**Currency**

The official currency of Giethoorn, and the entire Netherlands, is the European Euro. The Euro is the official currency of the European Union (EU), and is used within 16 member states of the EU. The euro is subdivided into 100 cents, and the Euro coins are the 1c, 2c, 5c, 10c, 20c, 50c, 1 euro coin and 2 euro coin. The Euro banknotes are the 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200 and 500 euro notes. The Euro is the second largest reserve currency in the world, and if you are traveling within the EU or from another member state where the official currency is the Euro, there is no need to exchange currency. If you are traveling to Giethoorn from outside the EU, it is advisable to exchange a small amount of cash before your arrival into the Netherlands. Once in the Netherlands, currency can be exchanged at most major banks and hotels. ATM's are a great way to pull local currency from your local bank, but check with your local bank before leaving to make sure your bankcards are accepted in the Netherlands. It is always advisable to carry a small amount of cash on you at all times.
