---

Gouda is not a city that has generated a great literary tradition of its own, but these titles illuminate the Dutch world it inhabits.

**The Embarrassment of Riches** by Simon Schama — A cultural history of the Dutch Golden Age that explains how the Netherlands became one of the world's great trading powers, and how that wealth shaped everything from domestic architecture to cheese production. Gouda's cheese trade was a direct product of the 17th-century Dutch agricultural revolution Schama describes.

**Girl with a Pearl Earring** by Tracy Chevalier — Set in Delft, just 30 kilometres away, Chevalier's novel captures the textures of 17th-century Dutch domestic life — the smells of the kitchen, the quality of the light, the social hierarchies — that you can still sense in a well-preserved Dutch city like Gouda.

**The Diary of a Young Girl** by Anne Frank — The occupied Netherlands is the backdrop to any visit to a Dutch city. Gouda's Jewish community was largely deported during the war, and reading Anne Frank's diary gives the historic streets a different weight.

**Tulip Fever** by Deborah Moggach — A novel about the Dutch tulip mania of the 1630s, set in Amsterdam but ranging across the Dutch Republic. Lightweight but entertaining, and a useful introduction to the speculative excess that ran alongside the Golden Age prosperity.
