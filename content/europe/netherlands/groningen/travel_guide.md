**Groningen travel guide** is the best source to get all the information about this northernmost city of the Netherlands. Considered as a popular tourist terminus, Groningen attracts a throng of visitors throughout the year.

There is a bit of everything that makes Groningen a popular holiday destination in Netherlands. It is considered a major Dutch educational hub and has a considerable high student population. With a rich heritage and vibrant nightlife, this city is a perfect amalgamation of the quiet and rural surroundings along with the cosmopolitan feel. Groningen is also famous for its fascinating museums. Exotic local Dutch cuisine at competitively-priced restaurants is another attraction in Groningen.

The city is well-connected by major routes. Although good climate prevails throughout the year, Groningen travel guide suggests early spring to late autumn is the best time to visit this city.

Art infiltrates in many significant monuments of the city. As per Groningen travel guide, the traditional canal-side townhouses showcase arts and architecture of this popular tourist point. Even the local railway station has magnificent artwork on stained glass windows.

Although there are many places to explore in Groningen, some must-visit places are briefly described as follows.

**Martinitoren**

Located on the Grote Market, this architectural building has an 11th century historic church. It has one of the most beautiful towers in entire Groningen. Tourists can get a great view of the entire city and the surrounding areas from the tower of this building. Even today one can still find the ancient architectural flavor in the walls and roof of the building.

**Groningen Museum**

Situated right in the heart of the city, this museum attracts a throng of visitors due to its unique design style which is designed by three different architects.

**Prinsenhof Gardens**

If you are a nature lover, this place should be in you must-visit list during your trip to Groningen. The garden is adjacent to a 16th-century building which now is converted to a hotel. Tourists can relax here among the lush greenery and get closer to nature.

**The Stadsschouwburg**

This city theater with a blend of pink resurgence with a unique ornate interior is worth visiting. You can also enjoy the productions when they are taking place on the stage.

Apart from these, Groningen travel guide also suggests you explore the local university and visit popular local markets like Grote Markt and Vismarkt where you can find souvenirs and local artifacts.
