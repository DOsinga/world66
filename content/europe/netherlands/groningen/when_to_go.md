If you are planning a trip to **Groningen, when to go **is probably the question on your mind. Groningen is the largest city in the Netherlands and is a great place to go on sightseeing tours. However, it is important to consider the best time to go to Groningen.

**Climate**

Groningen is blessed with a moderate temperate climate. The city experiences warm summers as well as cool winters. In fact, the average annual temperature in Groningen is 13.2 degree Celsius. Winter is from December to February. The temperature during winter is normally 5 degree Celsius to 10 degree Celsius. Snow starts to melt during the mid of March until May, followed by summer during the months of June to September. Summer is characterized with strong and cool winds and moderate temperature ranging from 15 degree Celsius to 21 degree Celsius. Summer in Groningen is not characterized with rain. After summer, leafs begin to fall as autumn starts from October to November before the cycle continues again.

**All Year Round**

The different climates in Groningen changes the scenery of the city, which makes this a perfect place to visit all year round. Unlike other countries, Groningen does not experience real extreme weather like hurricanes, excessive cold or heat. Moreover, Groningen does not experience heavy snow and, in fact, a carpet of snow during the winter rarely happens in the city. Summer in Groningen, too, is moderate, which makes it a perfect time to go to the park for some picnic and sunbathing activities.
