Heerlen is a city in the hilly south of Limburg, the narrow strip of the Netherlands wedged between Belgium and Germany. It does not have the prettiness of nearby [Maastricht](/europe/netherlands/maastricht), but it has one thing most Dutch cities cannot match: a Roman past. The Thermenmuseum, built over the excavated remains of a Roman bathhouse, is one of the best-preserved Roman sites in the Low Countries, with hypocaust floors and wall paintings still visible in situ.

The city itself is post-industrial — coal mining shaped it for over a century, and the closure of the last mine in 1974 hit hard. But Heerlen has reinvented itself with a lively cultural scene, including the Parkstad Limburg Theater and a growing street art programme. The surrounding hills offer good cycling, and the Aachen border is minutes away.
