Hellevoetsluis is a former naval harbour town on the Voorne-Putten island in South Holland, about 30 km south of Rotterdam. The name means "lock of Hell" — the "Hell" referring to the tidal creek that once ran here. The town has a significant naval history: it was the main base of the Dutch fleet in the 17th and 18th centuries, and the historic harbour fortifications and drydocks from this period are still visible in the old centre.

The Vestingmuseum (Fortification Museum) in the old arsenal is worth visiting for its documentation of the Dutch naval past and the town's role in the wars against England and France. The Kanaalweg along the old canal is pleasant for walking. Hellevoetsluis today is a quiet provincial town that does not see many foreign visitors, but for those interested in Dutch maritime and military history, or travelling the Delta region south of Rotterdam, it makes a worthwhile stop.
