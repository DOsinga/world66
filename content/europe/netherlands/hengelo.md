Hengelo is an industrial city in Overijssel, in the eastern Netherlands near the German border. It grew up around textiles and metalworking in the 19th century, and the legacy of that industrial past is visible in the converted factory buildings and wide working-class neighbourhoods that give the city its no-nonsense character. It is not a tourist destination, but it has more going on than most people expect.

The centre was heavily bombed in 1944 and rebuilt in a practical postwar style, but pockets of older architecture survive around the Markt and along Enschedesestraat. The Twentse Welle museum covers the region's industrial and natural history in an old factory hall. Hengelo's main draw for visitors is its position as a base for exploring the surrounding Twente countryside — gentle hills, old farmsteads, and a network of cycling and walking paths that wind through the Saxon landscape.

The city has a decent selection of restaurants and cafes around the Markt and the station area. Train connections are good: [Enschede](/europe/netherlands/enschede) is ten minutes east, and you can reach [Deventer](/europe/netherlands/deventer) or cross into Germany without much effort.
