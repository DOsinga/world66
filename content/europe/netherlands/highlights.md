---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

The Netherlands is a small country that punches far above its weight. In a long weekend you can cover remarkable ground — historic canal cities, world-class museums, working windmills, tulip fields in bloom — and still feel like you've only scratched the surface.

**[Amsterdam](/europe/netherlands/amsterdam)** is the obvious starting point and for good reason: the ring of 17th-century canals, the Rijksmuseum's Rembrandts and Vermeers, the Van Gogh Museum, the Anne Frank House. It can feel overwhelmed by tourists in summer, but most of the crowds cluster on a handful of streets. Wander ten minutes from the main drag and you'll find quiet neighbourhoods and neighbourhood cafés that feel entirely local.

**[Rotterdam](/europe/netherlands/rotterdam)** is the city that surprises people most. Bombed flat in 1940 and rebuilt from scratch, it has become one of Europe's great laboratories for contemporary architecture — the cube houses, the Market Hall, the spectacular Van Brienenoord Bridge, the port that is still the largest in Europe. It lacks Amsterdam's historical grandeur but makes up for it with energy and ambition.

**[Delft](/europe/netherlands/delft)** and **[Leiden](/europe/netherlands/leiden)** are the quieter, more beautiful alternatives to Amsterdam for canal-city atmosphere. Delft has its famous blue-and-white pottery, Vermeer's birthplace, and a remarkable old town. Leiden has the oldest university in the Low Countries, magnificent canal architecture, and one of the best ethnology museums in Europe.

**[Maastricht](/europe/netherlands/maastricht)** in the far south feels like a different country: Catholic, francophone in character, full of good restaurants and medieval architecture. It was the birthplace of the European Union treaty and remains one of the most pleasant cities in the Netherlands for simply wandering and eating well.

**The Keukenhof gardens** near Lisse are open only from mid-March to mid-May, but if you time your visit for tulip season, the spectacle of fields stretching to the horizon in reds, oranges, and yellows is genuinely extraordinary.

**De Hoge Veluwe** national park near [Arnhem](/europe/netherlands/arnhem) is the Netherlands' finest stretch of wild landscape — heathland, pine forests, and drifting sand dunes. The Kröller-Müller Museum within the park holds the world's second-largest Van Gogh collection and a remarkable sculpture garden.

The **Wadden Islands** — Texel, Terschelling, Vlieland, Ameland — are an underdiscovered treasure. A ferry ride from the coast takes you to flat, wide, quiet islands with long empty beaches and extraordinary birdlife. Texel is the most accessible; Vlieland is car-free.
