title: "Family Travel Ideas in Maastricht"
type: section
---

The Dutch city of Maasticht is well situated within Holland,
on the border of Belgium, and near the border of Germany. Considered perhaps the oldest city in the Netherlands, Maastrict is
not only popular for shopping, but also as an historical location.

**Fortifications and Ruins**

Forts and ruins can be interesting to the whole family, and the city
fortifications of Maastricht provide such an opportunity. Within the
fortifications, travelers can see the thirteenth century town gate and
fragments of the medieval city walls, as well as Fort Willem, a
fortress from the early eighteen hundreds.

An especially entertaining tour for families is the guided tour of
the casemates, underground tunnels which were built to house weaponry
such as cannons.

Sint-Pietersberg, south of the city, is a location of similar
interest. Fort Sint-Pieter, dating from the seventeen hundreds was
extensively restored in the past year, and the ruins of a medieval
castle have also been preserved in the area.

**Culture and Museums
**

Within Maastricht, there are museums to satisfy many varied tastes,
but although the Bonnefanten is the most prominent of the museums of
Maastricht, other museums are more wide-ranging in their appeal.

The Spanish Government Museum, containing several furnished rooms
from the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries is more absorbing to all
ages, and the Derlon Hotel Museum is perhaps the most unusual, housing
Roman ruins in the basement beneath the hotel.

**Shop Hopping**

Although the city center is the main shopping destination, the newly
rebuilt shopping center, Entre Deux, has been praised for containing
several beautiful or unusual stores, including a bookstore built into a
former church.

It is also possible to combine retail hunting with sightseeing by
visiting the market square, which includes both a new shopping center
and historical venues such as the seventeenth century town hall.

**Activities and Days Out**

As a restored harbor, Bassin, littered with restaurants and cafes
can be a pleasant and relaxed atmosphere for families. Alternatively,
families may prefer to take a picnic to one of the many parks, and
Griendpark, on the river, features a course for skating and
skateboarding.

Belgium is also within walking distance, and depending on family interests, it is worth exploring some of the nearby area.
