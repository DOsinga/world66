---
title: "When to Go in Nijmegen"
type: section
---

One of the warmest places in all of the Netherlands is **Nijmegen. When to go **to this historic town depends heavily on what your plans will be once you are arrive and your own particular interests. With summer temperatures near 100 degree and winter temperatures in the 10s, Nijmegen offers a great range of activities for visitors year round.

**Climate in Nijmegen**

The weather in Nijmegen is semi-continental. Nijmegen, Eindhoven and Venlo are generally recorded as the hottest cities in the Netherlands during the summer months. Spring and fall have a milder climate and make a great time to visit the city.

As with many European vacation destinations, summer months bring large crowds of tourists from throughout the region and continent, so this time can be quite crowded. If you plan on visiting during the summer months - from June to August - be sure to book your accommodations well in advance to ensure vacancy. On the flip side, Nilmegen is a college town with roughly 20,000 students, and with holidays in the summer, many of the students leave town, allowing for added tourist traffic without too much congestion.

**Events in Nijmegen**

A great time to visit Nijmegen is during one of the city's many festivals or events. In spring is the Go Short film festival, featuring European Short Films. This is a fun time to visit the city, especially as the weather begins to warm and foliage begins to look great throughout the city.

The Christmas and New Years holidays also make a great time to visit Nijmegen, as there are many festivities to get you in the holiday spirit. Neighboring city Elst holds a Winter Festival complete with a market for unique Christmas shopping and a covered ice skating rink. If you want something different for your holiday celebration, visiting during this time of year can be a great way to start a new family tradition.

The coinciding Summer Festival and Four Day Marches of Nijmegen is also a nice time to visit - if you plan well in advance and don't mind crowds. The Summer Festival lasts for 10 days, starting the Friday before the marches and continuing until the Sunday after. During this time the population of the city swells from 160,000 to 1.8 million, so planning in advance is absolutely imperative.
