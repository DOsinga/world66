Oss is a mid-sized town of about 95,000 in Noord-Brabant, situated on the Maas river in the southern Netherlands. It has a lively market square and a small but worthwhile city museum covering local history, including Oss's past as a centre of the Dutch meat-processing industry — the town was home to the Zwanenberg factory, which launched the Spam-like canned meat OXO and later became a major pharmaceutical manufacturer. The town also has a curious claim to fame: it was the birthplace of Jan de Doot, the 17th-century blacksmith who famously removed his own bladder stone with a razor, a story immortalised in a local sculpture near the town centre.

Oss is not a destination in itself but makes a reasonable base for exploring the area. The Maashorst nature reserve, with its free-roaming wild horses and highland cattle, is a short drive away, and the fortified city of 's-Hertogenbosch — one of the finest medieval city centres in the Netherlands — is easily reached in under twenty minutes.
