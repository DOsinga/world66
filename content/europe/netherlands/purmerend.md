Purmerend is a medium-sized Dutch town about 20 kilometres north of Amsterdam, in the flat polder landscape of North Holland. It was a prosperous market town in the seventeenth century — the weekly cheese and cattle markets drew traders from across the region — and some of that compact historic centre survives around the Kaasmarkt square and the Gothic church.

Today Purmerend is largely a commuter town for Amsterdam, expanded dramatically with postwar housing estates. It is not a destination in itself, but travellers passing through the north of North Holland will find it a functional stopping point. The Waterlandroute cycling path connects the town to Amsterdam via the quiet countryside of the Waterland nature area, a rewarding ride through an almost unchanged Dutch polder landscape of narrow ditches, wooden farmhouses, and grazing cattle.
