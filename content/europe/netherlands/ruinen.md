Ruinen is a traditional brink village in Drenthe, one of those quiet Dutch settlements built around a village green where sheep once grazed. The surrounding landscape is all heathland, forest and cycling paths — this is the kind of place where you come to slow down.

There are a handful of restaurants, including a pannenkoekenboerderij (pancake farm) popular with families, and a couple of bungalow parks for longer stays. The village makes a good base for exploring the prehistoric dolmens (hunebedden) that dot the Drenthe countryside.
