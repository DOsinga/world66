Scheveningen is The Hague's seaside district and one of the most popular beach resorts in the Netherlands. What was once a small fishing village is now a long stretch of sandy beach backed by a boulevard of restaurants, bars, and hotels — but it still has more character than most Dutch beach towns.

The Pier is the centrepiece, jutting out over the North Sea with a Ferris wheel at its end and views up and down the coast. The Kurhaus, a grand 19th-century hotel, anchors the boulevard and is worth a look inside even if you are not staying there. North of the main beach, the harbour area retains something of Scheveningen's fishing past — there is a daily fish auction, a couple of good fish restaurants, and the Muzee Scheveningen, a small museum about the village's history.

The beach itself is broad and clean, backed by a row of beach clubs (strandjutters) that open from April through September. In summer it gets packed, especially on warm weekends, but walk ten minutes north or south and you will find space. The water is the North Sea — swimmable in July and August if you don't mind a chill, and popular with surfers year-round.

Scheveningen is easy to reach from The Hague's city centre: tram 1 or 9 gets you there in fifteen minutes. Most visitors combine a day at the beach with a trip to [The Hague](/europe/netherlands/thehague) itself, which has the Mauritshuis, the Binnenhof, and good Indonesian food.
