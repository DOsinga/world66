Beelden aan Zee (translated: sculptures at sea) is one of The Hagues
most beatiful museums. Located right on the beach boulevard of the
Scheveningen district, it offers spectacular sculptures in an
extraordinary building.

It is difficult to find the museum; it is located inside a dune below
the pavillion Von Wied, once upon a time a (small) royal palace. When
the museum was built the city council demanded that no sand could be
removed from the building site: because the dune it is built in is part
of the coastal defences. In a masterstroke, the architect therefore
located the new museum inside the dune, in effect creating a hidden
building. Once inside, you enter an oasis of tranquility, a place of
contemplation and admiration of wonderful sculptures.

Entrance €6,00 adults; €3,00 children
