---

Utrecht has produced or inspired a modest but worthwhile body of writing. These titles illuminate the city and the Dutch world around it.

**The Diary of a Young Girl** by Anne Frank — Not specifically about Utrecht, but the Netherlands under occupation is the inescapable context for any Dutch city. Reading it before your trip changes how you look at the streets.

**The Assault** by Harry Mulisch — A Dutch novel set in Haarlem but resonating across all of the occupied Netherlands, following a man whose family was killed during a reprisal in 1945. One of the essential Dutch novels of the 20th century.

**The Evenings** by Gerard Reve — A week in the life of a young man in post-war Amsterdam, suffocating in family routine and Dutch provinciality. Reve grew up partly in Utrecht and the flat melancholy of Dutch winter life is perfectly rendered.

**Mokusei!** by Cees Nooteboom — A novella about love, obsession, and Japanese art, by the Netherlands' best-known literary travel writer. Nooteboom was born in The Hague but spent years in and around Utrecht — his precise, cool style is very Dutch.

**De Ontdekking van de Hemel (The Discovery of Heaven)** by Harry Mulisch — Mulisch's most ambitious novel, partly set in Utrecht and Amsterdam, tracing the lives of two friends against the backdrop of 20th-century European history. Published in Dutch in 1992 and available in English translation.
