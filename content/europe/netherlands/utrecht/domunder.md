An underground archaeological site beneath the Domplein, Utrecht's central square. Guided tours descend below street level to explore 2,000 years of history layered under your feet: the foundations of the Roman castellum that once stood here, the remains of a Romanesque cathedral, and the rubble of the Gothic nave that collapsed during a tornado in 1674 and was never rebuilt.

The tour uses handheld flashlights and light projections to reveal the ruins — you literally illuminate the history as you walk through it. The experience is atmospheric and surprisingly moving, especially the section showing the tornado damage. The gap between the [Dom Tower](/europe/netherlands/utrecht/dom_tower) and the [Domkerk](/europe/netherlands/utrecht/domkerk) makes much more sense after you understand what used to stand between them. Book tickets in advance; tours run throughout the day. About an hour, underground.
