---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The old city centre is compact and almost entirely car-free — walking is the main way to get around. From the station, the Dom Tower is a fifteen-minute walk through the Hoog Catharijne mall.

Cycling is how locals move around Utrecht, and the infrastructure is exceptional even by Dutch standards. The Stationsplein has the [world's largest bicycle parking garage](/europe/netherlands/utrecht/bicycle_parking) (12,500 spaces) right beneath the station. OV-fiets bike rental is available at Utrecht Centraal and at several other stations in the city.

For the Rietveld Schröder House or the Castellum Hoge Woerd, take a local bus or hire a bike — both are 20–30 minutes from the centre. The RET city bus network is run by U-OV; a day pass or OV-chipkaart covers all buses.
