If you want to learn more about the **Volendam transportation** options available, read the information below. Although walking is the usual mode of transportation here, you may want to look for some alternatives, just in case you get tired.**
**

**Bicycle
**

Riding a bicycle is a popular way for transportation among the local residents, maybe due to the fact that the city is relatively small (with a population of about 22,000 people), and thus, the distances are short. Many of the nearby hotels offer bicycle rentals starting at $9 per day, and there are discounts available if you rent a bicycle for a longer period of time. There are some established bicycling rules in here. First, always lock your bike. Second, when on a crowded street, walk your bicycle instead of riding it. Third, always use a light when riding at night, and follow the road rules, as the traffic police won&rsquo;t hesitate to fine you just because you&rsquo;re on a bicycle.

**Taxi
**

Taxis are a good way of transportation, but be aware that they are a bit expensive. Their rate is $2 per km, and by the end of the ride, the taxi drivers usually expect a tip of 5-10%. The good thing is that this rate is steady and doesn&rsquo;t depend on whether it&rsquo;s day or night. The cars are new and well maintained, which guarantees a comfortable ride. It is recommended to call a taxi from your hotel to avoid waiting for one.
