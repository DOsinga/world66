The **Wadden Islands** (Dutch *Waddeneilanden*) are a string of low, sandy islands off the north coast of the Netherlands, stretching along the Dutch, German, and Danish seaboards of the Wadden Sea. Five are inhabited on the Dutch side: from west to east, **Texel**, **Vlieland**, **Terschelling**, **Ameland**, and **Schiermonnikoog**. The Wadden Sea that separates them from the mainland is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — the largest unbroken system of tidal mudflats on earth — and the islands are mostly nature reserve: dunes, salt marshes, cranberry bogs, pine plantations, and some of Europe's best bird migration stopover sites.

The appeal is the opposite of posh: wide empty beaches, cycling paths everywhere, old fishing villages, and the very particular quality of Dutch wind-and-sky-and-water. Most visitors rent a bike and spend a few days island-hopping, or stick to one island and do nothing. The islands fill up in July and August but are quiet the rest of the year.

- [Texel](/europe/netherlands/waddenislands/texel_island), the biggest, is reached by ferry from Den Helder (20 minutes); it has beaches, the Ecomare seal refuge, and the largest sheep population in the Netherlands.
- [Terschelling](/europe/netherlands/waddenislands/terschelling) is reached by ferry from [Harlingen](/europe/netherlands/friesland/harlingen) in Friesland. The 1594 Brandaris lighthouse and the annual Oerol performing-arts festival (June) are the highlights.
- [Ameland](/europe/netherlands/waddenislands/ameland) is smaller and quieter, with ferries from Holwerd.
- [Vlieland](/europe/netherlands/waddenislands/vlieland_island), the smallest inhabited island, has a single village (Oost-Vlieland) and no cars — a car-free retreat.
- **Schiermonnikoog** is reached by ferry from Lauwersoog and is almost entirely national park.

At low tide, guided **wadlopen** (mudflat-walking) tours cross the Wadden Sea from the mainland to the nearest islands — a strange, squelchy, unforgettable experience.
