Ameland is one of the [Wadden Islands](/europe/netherlands/waddenislands) off the north coast of [Friesland](/europe/netherlands/friesland), a long, flat strip of sand dunes, beaches, and grass polder that feels a world away from the mainland. The island has four villages — Hollum, Ballum, Nes, and Buren — connected by a single road across a landscape of wide skies and salt marshes.

The main attractions are the beaches (long, wide, and windswept), cycling the network of paths that crisscross the island, and the Wadden Sea mudflats, which you can walk across at low tide with a guide. Birdwatching is excellent, especially during spring and autumn migration. The island is quiet and family-oriented — the pace of life here is deliberately slow.

The ferry from Holwerd on the Frisian mainland takes about 45 minutes and runs several times daily. You can also drive to the port and park (or leave the car on the mainland and cycle on the island — most people do). Nes is the main village with shops and restaurants.
