Several operators offer crossings to the Wadden Islands aboard traditional Dutch sailing ships — flat-bottomed vessels called platbodem or tjalk that are well-suited to the shallow waters of the Wadden Sea. These trips take longer than the regular ferry but are a pleasant way to arrive, especially in summer.

Most sailings depart from Harlingen or other Frisian ports. Trips can range from a day crossing to multi-day voyages staying aboard overnight. Check operators on the Dutch sailing charter sites for current schedules and booking. The Wadden Sea's tides govern departure times, so check ahead.
