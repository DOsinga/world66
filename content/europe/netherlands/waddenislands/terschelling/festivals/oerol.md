Every year in **mid june**, there is the eccentric festival called **Oerol**
('everywhere', in dialect).
Site-specific theatre and theatre on location, shed theatre, street
theatre, modern dance, opera, visual art, music and the many crossovers
between all of these are the ingredients for a festival that uses the
island as a giant stage.
The festival has a long and rich tradition.
Over a period of more than twenty years, the festival has grown from a
small street festival into a highly valued, multi-disciplinary
festival. Be sure to catch it when you're around.
Due to the popularity
(50.000 people visit the festival) camping is the only last-minute
option. Most accommodations are booked a year in advance (!) It's the
most 'crowded' week of the year. And even then, you can walk or cycle
around for hours without seeing other people.
