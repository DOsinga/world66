We have two suggestions for day trips in Texel.

The first brings you to the southern section of the unique tidal area of **De Slufter**
NE from De
Koog. The Slufter is a tidel inlet
from the North Sea. You will experience the fascinating interplay
between land, sea and
wind. *Attention:
During breeding time (March 1st to September 1st) the western side
of the marsh is closed. Only the southern section has free public
access.*

If you are more interested in bird life, you'd better choose for
the *Grote Vlak* trip below.

The second is an excursion to the bird sanctuary of the **Grote Vlak** and to Texel's impressive southwestern beach plains: **De Geul** and **De Hors**.

*(There is also an ANWB signposted route [De Geul & De Hors]
starting from the ferry terminal, but large parts are closed from March
1st to September 1st).*

### [De Koog > De Slufter > De Koog

**By bus + on foot**, *12 kms, at ease: 4 to 5 hrs of walking

Check map in url below.

*At the ferry terminal 't Horntje, take a bus to De Koog.

Check when your bus leaves again for the afternoon or evening ferry.

Follow the walking/cycling signs to De Slufter NE from De Koog.

Then head for the shore in the NW and go left to return to De Koog following the tide line.

*Safety Warning: During flood tides the whole
Slufter area is under water*. *Should there be any warning signs on your path, for your own safety go inland again! **Be aware that tide can rise very fast, especially here on the North Sea side of the island!*
