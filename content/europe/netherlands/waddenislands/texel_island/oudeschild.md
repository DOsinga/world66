Oudeschild is Texel's harbour village, sitting on the sheltered eastern coast of the island where the Waddenzee laps at the quayside. It's a small, unhurried place — fishing boats unloading, nets drying, the smell of salt and diesel — and a welcome counterpoint to the busier resort atmosphere of De Koog on the other side of the island.

The village has one unmissable attraction: Museum Kaap Skil, the island's maritime and beachcombing museum. Texel sits at a historic crossroads of North Sea shipping routes, and the wrecks on its shores have yielded extraordinary finds over the centuries — the museum tells that story with genuine flair. The harbour itself is worth a slow walk, especially when the fishing fleet is in.

Oudeschild is best visited as part of a broader exploration of Texel rather than as a standalone destination, but if you're cycling around the island it makes an excellent stop for lunch and a museum visit.
