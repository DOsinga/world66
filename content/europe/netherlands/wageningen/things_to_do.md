---
title: "Things to do in Wageningen"
type: section
---

Giving off a truly small town, countryside charm, Wageningen is a
little place found in central Netherlands. With a tiny area, the **Wageningen
thing to do** list does not appear large at first glance. But, with
the university in the town, it does have more activity than the typical
Dutch town, and it can give a fresh experience to anyone new to the
Netherlands.

**Sightseeing**

The Wageningen University is the centrepiece of the town. Regarded
as one of Europe’s most beautiful and multicultural universities, the
university is open to the public. Walk around the grounds and admire the
architecture, or visit one of the two botanical gardens located on the
campus. The town is also home to the Hotel de Wereld, where the formal
document of surrender was signed by the Germans to the Allies in 1945.
The Netherlands would never be the Netherlands without the windmill –
the Windkorenmolen De Vlijt is an actual operating windmill that was
built in the 19th-century. Also worth a visit is one of the
many parks that are scattered around the town, as well as the old city
walls that surround the entire town.

**Activities**

Wageningen is situated strategically by the Rhine River, and evening
walks along the river are truly enjoyable. The Rhine is also a good
place to fish and swim, and is a popular leisure spot for the locals.
The town is also near to the High Veluwe, a nature reserve good for
walks and cycling, as a good place for spotting some wildlife.
Wageningen can be good for shopping as well, mainly done in the
Hoogstraat area – however, don’t be disappointed if the variety is not
comparable to the larger cities.

**Food**

The Open Market on Saturdays is always a good place to start with
Dutch food in Wageningen. Dutch pancakes are always popular here, and
you can simply buy a plate and eat it as you walk. Fresh Dutch cheeses
and wines are available, as are homemade preserves and vinegars. There
are also a myriad of restaurants in the inner city, and most serve
decent food with a warm Dutch atmosphere. After nightfall, pubs are
always popular here, even though there are no clubs around. Coffee clubs
are becoming trendy amongst the younger crowd, and generally open late
for those looking for a late night coffee and chat.

Wageningen can be interesting with its various activities in the
town. To those who wish to relax and enjoy a slice of small town life,
Wageningen can truly be a joy to experience.
