This **Wageningen travel guide** will give you valuable insight about this town and help you plan your holiday.

**Getting In and Around**

Once you get to Schiphol Airport, which is the nearest airport to Wageningen, you can either take the train or bus to reach this town. If you are coming from Arnhem, you can take bus number 86 and 88, which travels to and from the town of Wageningen. You may also ride the train from the Ede-Wageningen train station.

When exploring around the city your best public transportation option is renting a bicycle. There are several spots around town which offer bike rentals for a reasonable fee. This is also one of the popular ways of getting around the town without spending a lot of money on transport.

**Accommodations**

There are many hotels that offer great accommodations. You can choose from different luxury and mid-range hotels, as well as boutique and bed and breakfast establishments. Great places to stay in Wageningen are Hotel de Wereld, Golden Tulip, Nol In't Bosch and Hotel de Wageningse Berg.

**Attractions**

There are several great places to explore and things to do around Wageningen. Some of the places you can visit are Windkorenmolen De Vlijt, Casteelse Poort Museum, Wijngaard Wageningse Berg, the Rhine River and High de Veluwe. You can also try nature trekking, cycling, fishing and explore rural living along the marvelous countryside. Within the famous Agricultural University of Wageningen, you will be amazed by the old architecture and special spots like the Tree Park and the two botanical gardens.

**Eating Out**

Of course, your journey wouldn&rsquo;t be complete without feasting on the sumptuous and delectable Dutch cuisine. Most restaurants around Wageningen offer local and international cuisine. There are also some caf&eacute;s around the town center. Duniya is a restaurant that offers Indian and Surinamese cuisines and Pannekoek House offers traditional Dutch cuisine and local delicacies. You might also want to explore Wageningen&rsquo;s open market and discover traditional Dutch fare.

**Nightlife and Entertainment**

If you are looking for some good entertainment, you can venture around the town center. Here you will find bars and caf&eacute;s that offer great Belgian beer, a vibrant atmosphere and simple entertainment. Not to miss is the De Vlaamsche Reus which is one of the popular entertainment hot spots for students and tourists alike. This bar features more than 120 kinds of beer and draughts and also serves traditional Dutch cuisine.
