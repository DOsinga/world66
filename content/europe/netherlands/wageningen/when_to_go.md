This **Wageningen when to go** article informs you on the best times to visit this historical town in the center of Netherlands. Aside from housing the famous Wageningen University, Wageningen is the center of the Dutch food and the Nutrition Cluster, where modern facilities for the food and nutrition field are located.

**Climate**

Basically, Wageningen has a moderate climate with a predominant wind direction of Southwest. The average temperature of Wageningen during the cold months of October to April is between -0.1 to 6.5 degrees Celsius, which is not as freezing cold as compared to other countries. During the hottest months of May to September, the temperature is just mild from 7.5 to 22.3 degrees Celsius, which is just moderately hot as compared to tropical countries. Therefore, with regards to temperature and climate, it is quite okay to visit Wageningen any time of the year. If you are averse to cold conditions, then you better not plan your visit here during the months of October to April.

Since Wageningen is a center of study for food, expect that the municipality always hosts symposia, meetings and conferences on National and International Food Development advances. You can get in and listen to the speakers, and you might even be lucky enough to get a free taste test. Aside from this, there are restaurants in the area that initially offer these recent food advancements. Plus, you can also learn the latest updates on healthy and delicious food.
