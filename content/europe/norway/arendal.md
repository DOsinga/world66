Arendal is a small coastal town on [Norway's](/europe/norway) southern shore, the kind of place Norwegians head to in summer for wooden boats, white-painted houses, and island-hopping in the Raet archipelago. The old town, Tyholmen, sits on a cluster of islands connected by bridges, its 17th- and 18th-century timber buildings packed tightly together along narrow lanes.

The harbour is the centre of life. In summer it fills with boats and the waterfront cafes stay open late in the long Scandinavian evenings. The Merdø island, a short ferry ride from the harbour, has a preserved sea captain's house and quiet beaches. The Raet National Park, Norway's first marine national park, protects the coast and seabed east of town.

Arendal is quieter and less touristed than [Kristiansand](/europe/norway/kristiansand) to the west, and that is part of its appeal. It makes a good stop on a drive along the southern coast.
