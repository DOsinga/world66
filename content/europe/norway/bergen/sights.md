Sights in Bergen sounds a bit like Buildings in New York - there must be nearly as many and as much variety. Some are of nature like the wonderful view down from the top of the funicular - an amazing thought that you are so close here to the heart of [Norway](/europe/norway)'s second city.

Others are of buildings, like those in the outdoor museum or the stave church.

Still others are of a delightful modern city.

**Bryggen**

Facing the water from the market, you will see the impressive old buildings of Bryggen to your right across the water. These are reminiscent of the days when Bergen was an important port of the Hanseatic League. [more..](/europe/norway/bergen/sights/bryggen)

**Ole Bull statue**

Ole Bull is mentioned on the main Bergen page and his home, Lysøen, is one of the Tourist Board's suggested sights. He was a violinist with an international reputation. [more..](/europe/norway/bergen/sights/ole_bull_statue)

**Fantoft Stave Church**

This is only a short bus ride from the centre of Bergen. Make sure you get off at the FIRST stop for the church or you will have a steep walk up through the woods from the next. [more..](/europe/norway/bergen/sights/fantoft_stave_chur)
