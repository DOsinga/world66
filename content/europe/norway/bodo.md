Bodø is the second-largest city in northern Norway and the northern terminus of the Norwegian railway network. It sits just north of the Arctic Circle in Nordland county, making it one of the most accessible gateways to the far north of Norway. The city itself was almost entirely destroyed during the Second World War and is consequently modern in character, but its surroundings are spectacular.

For most travellers, Bodø is a transit point rather than a destination in its own right. It is the main gateway to the [Lofoten Islands](/europe/norway/lofotenislands), with frequent ferries, Hurtigruten coastal steamers, and flight connections. The E10 road also starts here, heading east along the coast.

That said, the area around Bodø rewards exploration. The coastline, archipelago, and nearby mountains offer excellent hiking and sea kayaking. The world's strongest maelstrom — the Saltstraumen — is just 33 km south of the city and is worth visiting at peak tidal flow. The Norwegian Aviation Museum is one of the better museums in northern Norway.
