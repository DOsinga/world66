Ausevika is about 40 minutes' drive from Floro, heading south toward Stavang. The site contains more than 300 rock carvings — human and animal figures alongside fertility and agricultural symbols — dating back over 3,000 years. Known locally as "Veidemannskunst" (hunter's rock art), large parts of the site are well preserved and signposted for visitors.
