Førde is a small town in Sunnfjord, Vestland county, serving as a regional hub for the surrounding fjord and mountain landscape of western [Norway](/europe/norway). It is not a destination in itself, but it is a practical base for exploring some of the most dramatic scenery in the country.

The big draw nearby is Jostedalsbreen, mainland Europe's largest glacier, accessible via guided walks on the glacier arms at Bøyabreen and Nigardsbreen. The drive from Førde to the glacier takes about two hours through steep-sided valleys and past waterfalls. Closer to town, Jølstravatnet lake stretches east towards the mountains and is good for fishing and kayaking in summer.

Førde also hosts the Førde International Folk Music Festival each July, one of Norway's best folk and world music events, which brings an unexpected burst of energy to this otherwise quiet town. The Sunnfjord Museum on the outskirts gives a sense of traditional life in western Norway, with old farm buildings and exhibitions on local history.
