Harstad is the largest town on the island of Hinnøya in Troms, northern [Norway](/europe/norway). It sits well above the Arctic Circle, which means midnight sun from late May to mid-July and a chance of northern lights through the winter months.

The town is a stop on the Hurtigruten coastal ferry and makes a practical base for exploring the [Vesterålen](/europe/norway/vesteralen) islands. Nearby Trondenes church, a medieval stone church from the 13th century, is one of the northernmost medieval churches in the world. The surrounding coastline is dramatic — fjords, mountains, and open sea — and whale-watching trips run from the area in season.
