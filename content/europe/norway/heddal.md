Heddal is a small village in Telemark, about 150 km southwest of Oslo, known for one thing: the Heddal Stave Church, the largest of Norway's surviving stave churches. Built in the early 13th century, the triple-nave structure rises in tiers of dark tarred timber and looks almost impossibly intricate from the outside.

The church is still in active use and open to visitors in summer. Inside, a medieval bishop's chair and traces of 17th-century rose painting remain. The setting — green farmland, a small river, and forested hills — is quintessential Telemark.

Most visitors come as a day trip from the Notodden area or as a stop on the drive between Oslo and the western fjords.
