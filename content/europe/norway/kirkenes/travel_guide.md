---
title: "Travel Guide in Kirkenes"
type: section
---

Using this **Kirkenes travel guide** will improve your vacation because you will know the best places to sleep, and you will know what you can't miss. Kirkenes is located about 240 miles away from the Arctic Circle, which is why there is snow in the mountains all year long. Kirkenes also allows tourists the chance to visit three countries at once when visiting Ovre Pasvik National Park. The border for Norway, Russia and Finland all meet inside the National Park.

**Accommodations**

In Kirkenes, you are going to have plenty of beautiful resorts to choose from, but you can also opt for something a little more private. If you are looking for a private place to stay that doesn&rsquo;t have a hotel atmosphere, you want to check out the Direktorboligen, which is a house that has been turned into a hotel of sorts. Rooms here start around $175 a night. If you want a hotel that is conveniently located, you need to check out the Rica Hotel; most of the tours use this spot as a meeting place. Rooms at the Rica Hotel average $176 a night.

**Nightlife and Entertainment**

If you are looking for something to do on a Saturday night, you will want to check out the disco at the Kirkenes Hotel. The disco is open to anyone who wants to attend; it is not just limited to guests of the hotel. The disco is located on the ground floor of the hotel and goes on until the early hours of the morning.

**Things to Do**

Kirkenes is a great place to go if you love the outdoors because most of their recreational activities focus on the great outdoors. If you are visiting during the winter months, you will definitely want to take one night to stay in The Snow Hotel, which is a hotel made entirely from snow and ice. Other things to check out during the winter months are dogsledding and snowmobile rides through the countryside. If you are visiting during warmer months, be sure to check out the Tana River for some of the best salmon fishing around.

**Getting Around**

Getting around town is no more difficult than walking from one place to the next because of how small the town is. However, if you have signed up for any of the guided tours, such as dogsledding or snowmobiling, you will be picked up and dropped off by a shuttle, usually at the Rica Hotel.

**Eating Out**

If you are looking to sample the local cuisine, the best restaurant to try is Vin & Vilt. All of the food is cooked by the owner and his wife. If they recommend a dish for you to try, follow their advice. Dishes range from $25 to $100, but the final price depends on what you are going after. If it is in season, you need to try out the hare, grouse or even reindeer dishes. Gapahuken ved Pikevannet along the Russian border is also a great place to go for local cuisine.
