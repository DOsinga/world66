**Experience Winter and Summer Wonderland in Kirkenes**

When planning a visit to **Kirkenes, **this **when to go**** **guide will familiarize you with local weather conditions to help you choose the best time for your trip. Kirkenes is a town in Norway located in the extreme northeastern part of the country, a mere 400 km away from the Arctic Circle, which is the reason why Kirkenes remains covered in snow for most of the year. Visitors find Kirkenes a unique winter and summer wonderland adventure. Here are the best times to go to Kirkenes.

**Winter in Kirkenes**

Winter might be harsh in Kirkenes the fact that the temperature can drop as low as &minus;11.5 degrees C (11.3 degrees F) in January. It is during winter when you will be able to experience the best of Kirkenes. Although winter in Kirkenes can be cold, it is not accompanied by strong winds thus making it relatively comfortable to the tourists. During winter, the town is famous for its snow hotel which is built during the onset of winter and lasts only until summer before the entire structure melts. In fact, Kirkenes is famous for this ephemeral hotel.

**Watch the Midnight Sun in Kirkenes**

If you want to experience a unique natural phenomenon, then you can stay further for the summer months to experience the infamous Norwegian midnight sun which happens from May to July. You can marvel at the sun setting low in Norway but never seemingly getting dark thus giving you more daytime hours to frolic around the city.
