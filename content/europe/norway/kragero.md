Kragerø is a small coastal town on Norway's southern shore, tucked into a maze of islands, skerries, and sheltered inlets. Edvard Munch called it "the pearl of the coastal towns," and in summer it is easy to see why — white wooden houses climb the hillsides above a harbour full of boats, and the surrounding archipelago offers some of the warmest swimming water in Norway.

The town comes alive between June and August, when Norwegians descend on the islands for sailing, kayaking, and long evenings on the rocks. The rest of the year it is quiet, almost sleepy, but the light is beautiful and the walking trails along the coast are empty. Kragerø has a small art scene — the Edvard Munch-inspired Berg-Kragerø Museum is worth a visit — and the seafood from the harbour is as fresh as it gets.
