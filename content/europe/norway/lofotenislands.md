---
title: "Lofoten islands"
type: location
---

The **Lofoten Islands** extend dramatically out into the Atlantic Ocean, off the coast of Northern Norway. Steep mountains plunge into the clear, cold waters, and charming fishing villages appear suddenly from behind majestic cliffs. 

Visitors with a taste for unspoiled nature, old world charm, and adventure will enjoy these magnificent islands near the Arctic Circle. A relaxed, "away from it all" atmosphere still prevails; the traditional mainstay--stockfish--still hangs to dry over the wooden racks the way they have since the Viking Age. 

The many sights include **Svolvær**, the "Capital" of Lofoten, a town made wealthy in its day from the lucrative stockfish trade; the village of **Reine**, nested amongst tall, imposing mountains; the narrow **Nusfjord** with its charming **Rorbu** (traditional Fisherman's dwellings) accommodations set in a spectacular, narrow granite fjord; and countless other fishing villages and homesteads.
