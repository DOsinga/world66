Molde bills itself as the "City of Roses," though the reason for the name is not immediately obvious. What is obvious is the setting: the town sits on the north shore of the broad Romsdalsfjorden, and from the park you can count over 200 mountain peaks on both sides of the fjord. It is one of the most panoramic locations on the Norwegian coast.

Molde is a stop on the Hurtigruten coastal route and has ferry connections south toward [Ålesund](/europe/norway/alesund). The town itself is small and quiet for most of the year, but it comes alive during the Moldejazz festival in July — one of Europe's oldest jazz festivals, drawing international acts and filling the town to capacity.

Outside festival season, the main reason to linger is the surrounding landscape. The Atlantic Road (Atlanterhavsveien), one of Norway's most spectacular drives, is a short trip to the west. The Hjertøya island fishing museum, reached by a short boat ride from the centre, is an unexpectedly rewarding visit — it tells the story of a fisherman's life rather than just the mechanics of fishing, with well-preserved living quarters, sheds, and boats set among pines and birches.
