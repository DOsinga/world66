Åndalsnes sits at the end of the Romsdal valley where the river meets the Romsdalsfjord, surrounded by some of Norway's most dramatic mountain scenery. It is a small town — more a base than a destination — but the surrounding landscape more than compensates.

The **Trollveggen** (Troll Wall), Europe's highest vertical rock face at over 1,000 metres, looms above the valley just outside town. The **Trollstigen** (Troll's Path), a serpentine mountain road with eleven hairpin bends and a waterfall thundering alongside, is one of Norway's most famous drives. In summer, the **Golden Route** from Åndalsnes over the mountains to [Geiranger](/europe/norway/geiranger) — via the Trollstigen Pass and the Eagles' Highway — is one of the great scenic road trips in Scandinavia.

Åndalsnes is a popular starting point for hikers and climbers in the Romsdal Alps. The **Romsdalseggen Ridge** walk, with views over the fjord and surrounding peaks, is considered one of Norway's finest day hikes.
