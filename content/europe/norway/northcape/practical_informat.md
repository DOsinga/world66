type: section
---

**Understanding North Cape practical information** will help you navigate and appreciate the beauty of this port city during your next visit. Known to the locals as Nordkapp, The North Cape area of Norway features amazing sunsets over the Artic Ocean. Unless you visit on a very cloudy day, you can view the midnight sun here during the summer months of May through July in this point, which is very near to being the northernmost in Europe.

**Getting to Town**

You can get to the small harbour town by plane by way of Honningsvag airport, or by taking a car to the point where E69 ends. You may also arrive by boat. Several cruise ships also make this beautiful port city a common stop. Once in town you'll find that bicycle riding is a common mode of transportation. If you go off the paved roads, watch for markers of red "T." These will keep you safely on traveled routes.

**Lodging**

Once you've arrived you'll find a variety of lodging options, including a wide variety of budget and low to mid priced hotels. You can navigate the city by car, foot, or bicycle. If you decide to ride your bike, make sure to sign in at the cyclists welcome book at the North Cape Center.

**Currency, Language**

The official currency is the Norweigan Krone. The American dollar is very strong here, you'll find one Krone exchanges for roughly .15 American dollars.

Although there is no official language, most locals speak fluent Norweigan. English is also widely spoken here.
