Skarsvåg bills itself as the northernmost fishing village in the world — a tiny cluster of red-and-white wooden houses tucked into a cove 12 km east of the North Cape. A few dozen people live here year-round, fishing for cod and halibut through the short summer. Drop in for a coffee at the King Crab House, walk out to the Kirkeporten arch on the nearby headland, and you have seen the place.
