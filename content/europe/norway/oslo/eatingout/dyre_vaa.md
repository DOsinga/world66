Trendy cafe, restaurant and bar serving lunch and dinner. Dyre Vaa is focusing on great food to affordable prizes. Is also open night-time in weekends. Serves brunch every Sunday.

The restaurant's
bar and table tops were made of teak, reused from the cruise ship MS
Monterey. Interior is by the Norwegian designer Lars E. Hole, from Hole Design
AS.

The installation on the wall, in the hallway
on the right side of the bar, is Welhaven’s poetry “Dyre Vaa” written by the
young artist Jan Haakon Robson. The Installation on the back wall, with the
statues, on the left side of the bar, is created by designer and photographer Annette Nordström Steen.

The Statue in the left teak box is an original
plaster sculpture by Dyre Vaa of the Norwegian artist Christian Krogh. The
right sculpture is “self sculpture” of Dyre Vaa. He created it when he got the
assignment to make the statues on the Anker Bridge.
