***Café Art'é*** is an
intimate resort, with a kitchen predominantly inspired by Italian
cucine. It’s located in
the venerable St. Olavs gt. 7 where it shares facilities with ***Gallery Frie
Kunster***. The Gothic
revival style building was constructed by
the German architect *Von Hanno*, who brought it to completion in
1873. Von
Hanno and his
family kept residence in the second floor of the
building. It is
rummored that the famous Norwegian writer *Knut Hamsun* was a tenant of the
building’s loft apartment during his residence in Christiania ( Oslo
). St Olavs
gt. Extends from St Olavs plass, in which lived Ylajali, the love interest of
Hamsun’s book “Hunger”.

Visit us
to enjoy the continental touch of this cafe who host a great deal established
artists and students. We have
an exotic backyard who can offer you peace and silence in the heart of Oslo as
well as a figurative art
gallery.
