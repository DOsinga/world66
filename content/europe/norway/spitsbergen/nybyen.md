Nybyen sits at the southern end of Longyear Valley, at the foot of the Longyear Glacier. Store Norske Spitsbergen Kulkompani built this tiny settlement after the Second World War to house its miners — eight solid timber houses, a bathhouse, a laundry, a shop, and a canteen known as Stormessa ("the big mess").

The miners have long since moved out, and today the houses serve as student accommodation for the University Centre in Svalbard (UNIS) and seasonal workers. The buildings are original, redecorated and modernised over the years but still carrying traces of their mining past. Nearby you will find Huset, a cultural venue with a cinema and restaurant, and Galleri Svalbard, which shows art inspired by the Arctic landscape. Old mining equipment still stands on the hillsides around the settlement.

Nybyen feels slightly removed from central [Longyearbyen](/europe/norway/spitsbergen/longyearbyen), giving it a quieter, more wilderness-adjacent atmosphere — the glacier is right there.
