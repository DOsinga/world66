Ryfylke is the region of deep fjords, dramatic mountains, lush valleys and idyllic islands stretching north and northeast of Stavanger — the southern gateway to Fjord Norway. Best explored by combining transport: car, bicycle, boat, or on foot.

The cultural landscape here has been shaped by thousands of years of fishing and agriculture. Modern aquaculture continues that tradition in the fjords, producing salmon, halibut and shellfish. In summer the area buzzes with local festivals; in winter the mountains hold snow while the fjord shores stay mild and green. The Lysefjord and Kjerag are the headline attractions, but the quieter islands and valleys reward slower exploration.
