Tønsberg is Norway's oldest city, founded by the Vikings over a thousand years ago. The ruins of the medieval fortress atop Slottsfjell hill and its distinctive tower offer wide views across the Oslofjord, while the royal burial mounds at nearby Borre National Park are among Scandinavia's most important Viking-era sites. History here isn't behind glass — it's built into the landscape.

The waterfront district known as Brygga is where the town comes alive, lined with restaurants, bars and small shops that fill up on summer evenings. The Whaling Museum (Hvalfangstmuseet) documents the region's seafaring past with surprising honesty. Beyond the centre, the surrounding coastline and islands are popular for swimming and sailing in the warmer months.

Tønsberg sits about ninety minutes south of Oslo by train or car, making it an easy day trip or a relaxed overnight stop on the way down the coast. It works well as a base for exploring the Vestfold region, with its cluster of Viking heritage sites and quiet seaside towns.
