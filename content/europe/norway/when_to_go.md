**Norway, A Place to Witness Two World Phenomena**

If you are planning a vacation in **Norway when to go** is not a very hard decision to make. This is because it is an ideal place to travel anytime of the year. It has both cold and sunny weather activities, which would interest tourists. You can choose to go during the season that you feel most comfortable with.

**Winter Months**

Winter include the months of November to the first weeks of March. November usually gives the first snowfall. Wintertime is ideal for visiting the quaint Sami villages. This is also the best time for dog sledding, skiing and snow mobile activities. It is during winter that you can watch the world-renowned &ldquo;Polar Night&rdquo; from any point at the Arctic Circle. This is a natural phenomenon in which the sun never shines and everything is in eerie darkness, or under the shadows of the twilight sky. Taking a dog sledge, you can cross the Arctic Circle and watch the breathtaking &ldquo;Polar Night&rdquo; from different angles.

**Summer Months**

There are also wonderful places to go and exciting things to do during summer, the months of May to July. The Arctic Circle will provide a superb view of the phenomenal &ldquo;Midnight Sun.&rdquo; The sun never sets and night is turned into day. This is a good time to go village trekking. Villages have their own attraction during winter and summer. In summer, there are daily tours to the fjords, like Trollfjord to accommodate the influx of tourists. Summer also allows more interesting outdoor activities in the mountains and wild rivers of Norway. You can go rafting, kayaking, fishing, mountain climbing or play golf all day long amidst the picturesque terrain.
