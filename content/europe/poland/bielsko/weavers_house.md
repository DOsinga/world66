A reconstructed weaver's house and workshop showing how a guild master lived and worked at the turn of the 19th and 20th centuries. The hall splits the house in two — workshop on the left, living quarters on the right, with a kitchen and bedroom. The building itself is an original example of the city's former wooden housing, and the exhibition does a good job of conveying how closely work and domestic life were intertwined under the old guild system.
