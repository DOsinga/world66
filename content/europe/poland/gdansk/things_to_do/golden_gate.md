The western entrance to the Royal Way, rebuilt in its current Renaissance form in 1614. Two tiers of columns with allegorical figures on top — on the city side, figures representing Peace, Freedom, Wealth and Fame; on the other side, Prudence, Piety, Justice and Concord. The gate stands between the Prison Tower and the Torture Chamber, both of which now house small museums. From here Dluga Street — Long Street — stretches east through the heart of the old town.
