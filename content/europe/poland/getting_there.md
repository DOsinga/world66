## By Air

Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) is Poland's main international hub, with direct flights from most European cities and transatlantic connections via carriers including LOT Polish Airlines, Lufthansa, and KLM. Krakow Airport (KRK) is the second busiest and handles heavy tourist traffic, especially from the UK and Ireland. Gdansk (GDA), Wroclaw (WRO), Katowice (KTW), and Poznan (POZ) all have growing European networks.

Budget carriers Ryanair and Wizz Air have large Polish operations, making Poland one of the more affordable countries in Europe to fly into. Check both Warsaw and Krakow airports — fares can differ significantly.

## By Train

Direct trains connect Poland to its neighbours. Berlin to Warsaw takes around 5.5 hours on the Berlin-Warsaw Express; there are several departures daily. The ÖBB Nightjet sleeper runs Vienna–Krakow–Warsaw, a good option if you want to arrive well-rested. Trains also connect to Prague, Bratislava, Budapest, Vilnius, and Kyiv (check current schedules as routes change).

## By Bus

FlixBus and Polish coach operators connect Poland to cities across Europe. Journeys from Berlin, Prague, or Vienna are comfortable and significantly cheaper than trains, though longer. Useful if flying into a nearby hub city.

## By Car

If driving from EU countries — Germany, the Czech Republic, Slovakia, or Lithuania — there are no border formalities. Border crossings with Belarus and Russia (Kaliningrad) are closed to most travellers. The border region near Belarus and Ukraine has some restricted access zones within 2km of the border.

## Entry Requirements

Poland is part of the Schengen Area. EU and EEA citizens enter with a national ID card or passport. Most other nationalities can enter without a visa for stays under 90 days; check current requirements before travel. Poland uses the zloty (PLN), not the euro.
