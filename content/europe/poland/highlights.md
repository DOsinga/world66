Poland is a large country and no single trip covers it all. These are the places and experiences worth building a visit around.

## Cities

**[Krakow](/europe/poland/krakow)** is the country's most visited city and deservedly so. The Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Wawel Castle dominates the hill above the Vistula, and Kazimierz — the former Jewish quarter — has become one of Central Europe's most atmospheric neighbourhoods, full of bars, galleries, and synagogues. Two to three days minimum.

**[Warsaw](/europe/poland/warsaw)** is the capital and a different kind of city — rebuilt from rubble after wartime destruction, with a mix of reconstructed baroque Old Town, communist-era housing blocks, and a rapidly evolving modern city. The Warsaw Rising Museum is essential. The Palace of Culture is a Soviet-era landmark you'll either love or hate. Allow at least two days.

**[Gdansk](/europe/poland/gdansk)** on the Baltic coast combines extraordinary architecture (the Royal Way, Long Market, medieval crane) with deep historical significance as the site where World War II began and where Solidarity was born. The European Solidarity Centre museum is outstanding. Combine with a day in [Sopot](/europe/poland/sopot) for the beach and resort atmosphere.

**[Wroclaw](/europe/poland/wroclaw)** is one of Poland's most enjoyable cities — a lively university town with a beautiful market square, dozens of gnome statues hidden around the city, and a good food and nightlife scene. Less visited than Krakow or Warsaw, which adds to its appeal.

## Historical Sites

**Auschwitz-Birkenau** ([Oswiecim](/europe/poland/oswiecim)) is Poland's most visited site after Krakow's Old Town — and one of the most important historical sites in Europe. The former Nazi German concentration and extermination camp is preserved as a museum and memorial. Book guided entry in advance.

**Malbork Castle** ([Malbork](/europe/poland/malbork)) is the world's largest Gothic brick castle, built by the Teutonic Knights in the 14th century. An easy day trip from Gdansk (30 minutes by train). Bigger and more impressive than most visitors expect.

**Wieliczka Salt Mine** (near Krakow) descends nine levels through centuries of carved salt chambers, including a vast underground chapel. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and genuinely unlike anything else. Half-day trip from Krakow.

## Nature

**Bialowieza Forest** is a UNESCO-listed primeval forest straddling the Polish-Belarusian border — the last lowland old-growth forest in Europe. European bison roam here. Access requires a licensed guide for the strict reserve area.

**Tatra Mountains** ([Zakopane](/europe/poland/zakopane)) offer the country's best alpine scenery, with high peaks, glacial lakes (Morskie Oko is the most famous), and good hiking trails. The area around Zakopane also has a distinct highland folk culture.

**Mazury Lake District** in northeastern Poland is popular with Polish holiday-makers — a region of interconnected lakes, rivers, and canals ideal for sailing, kayaking, and cycling. [Mikolajki](/europe/poland/mikolajki) is the main tourist base.
