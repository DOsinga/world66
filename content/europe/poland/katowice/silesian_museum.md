The Silesian Museum is one of the best museum buildings in Poland, occupying the surface structures and shafts of the historic Katowice coal mine. The 2015 complex was designed by Riegler Riewe Architekten, who sank most of the building underground so that the original winding towers and brick engine houses remain the dominant presence on site. The main galleries descend floor by floor below ground level, lit by skylights that frame views of the shaft towers above. The permanent collection covers Silesian art, the history of the region's industrial development, and a superb section on everyday life under socialism. The building alone justifies a visit.
