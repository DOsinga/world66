Koszalin is a mid-sized city in Western Pomerania, set a few kilometres inland from the Baltic coast. It is not a beach destination itself, but it serves as a practical base for the sandy coastline around Mielno and Kołobrzeg, both within easy reach.

The city has a Gothic cathedral, a handful of museums, and a pleasant if unspectacular town centre rebuilt after wartime damage. The real draw is the surrounding landscape: lakes, forests, and the coastal dunes of Słowiński National Park to the east, where shifting sand buries entire trees and the beach stretches for kilometres.
