A branch of the National Museum housed in a former 16th-century bishop's palace near Wawel. The ground floor covers Orthodox church art from the 15th to 20th centuries; upstairs focuses on medieval Polish religious art. Two Gothic masterpieces stand out: the Madonna of Kruzlowa (1420) and a Gethsemane scene carved by Veit Stoss in 1485. Free on Sundays.
