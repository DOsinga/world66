Lancut is a small town in southeastern Poland that would be entirely unremarkable if not for one thing: its castle. The Lancut Castle is one of the finest aristocratic residences in Poland, a sprawling palace surrounded by an English-style landscape park. Built in the early seventeenth century and expanded over the following centuries by the Lubomirski and Potocki families, it survived both world wars largely intact — a rarity in this part of Europe.

The interiors are extraordinary. Room after room of original furnishings, paintings, porcelain, and textiles, all maintained as they were when the Potocki family lived here. The ballroom, the Turkish apartment, and the theatre are particularly impressive. The carriage house holds one of the largest collections of horse-drawn carriages in Europe. In the park surrounding the castle, an orangery, a synagogue, and several outbuildings add to the estate's scale.

Lancut hosts a well-regarded classical music festival each May, which fills the castle rooms with chamber concerts. The town itself is quiet and can be seen in a couple of hours, but the castle and grounds deserve a full half-day. It makes an easy stop between [Krakow](/europe/poland/krakow) and [Rzeszow](/europe/poland/rzeszow), or on the way to the Bieszczady Mountains further south.
