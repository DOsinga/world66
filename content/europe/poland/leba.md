Leba is a small fishing port and beach resort on Poland's Baltic coast, best known for the Slowinski National Park that brackets it on both sides. The park's crowning feature is the Lacka Dune, one of Europe's largest moving sand dunes — a wall of white sand that creeps steadily inland, slowly swallowing the forest behind it. The sight of pine trees half-buried in sand is genuinely strange and memorable.

In summer, the town fills with Polish holidaymakers who come for the broad, clean beaches and the sea air. Outside peak season it's quiet and a little melancholy, which suits it well. There's a small harbor where fishing boats come in, a decent selection of seafood restaurants, and not much else — which is exactly the point.
