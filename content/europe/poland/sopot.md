Sopot is the smallest of the three cities forming *Trojmiasto*. It is traditionally known as a resort town and so has the usual seaside activities on offer. It is known in the area for it's pretty main street and the Molo (or pier) - very long and sticking several hundred metres into Gdansk Bay. Try fresh fish along the beach but beware - they measure chips by the gram in this neck of the woods and so you can expect 200g exactly. I have known burly chip shop ladies plucking a chip off my plate before taking my money in the search for the exact 200g plateful. Still delicious, though.
