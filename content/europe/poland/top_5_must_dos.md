---
title: "Top 5 Must-Dos"
type: section
---

Poland rewards travellers who explore widely, but if you have limited time, these five experiences stand out.

**1. Walk Krakow's Old Town at dawn.** The main market square (Rynek Główny) is one of the largest medieval market squares in Europe. Before the tour groups arrive, it's magical — the Cloth Hall, St Mary's Basilica, the old university quarter. Spend at least two days in [Krakow](/europe/poland/krakow), including an afternoon in Kazimierz.

**2. Visit Auschwitz-Birkenau.** This is not easy, but it's important. The Auschwitz I and Auschwitz II-Birkenau sites at [Oswiecim](/europe/poland/oswiecim) are an hour from Krakow and can be visited in a day. Book guided entry in advance — it often sells out. Go with adequate time and don't rush it.

**3. Explore the Wieliczka Salt Mine.** A UNESCO World Heritage Site just outside Krakow: nine levels of carved salt chambers, chapels, lakes, and sculptures going back to the 13th century. More impressive than it sounds. The standard tourist route takes about two hours underground.

**4. Walk Gdansk's Long Market.** [Gdansk](/europe/poland/gdansk) has one of the most beautiful reconstructed old town streetscapes in Northern Europe, lined with tall merchant houses along Długi Targ (Long Market) and leading to the Green Gate. Add a day trip to Sopot for the pier and beach atmosphere.

**5. Take the train into the Tatras.** [Zakopane](/europe/poland/zakopane) is the gateway to the Tatra Mountains and the traditional highland culture of the Górale. The train from Krakow takes about 2.5–3 hours (or take a bus, which is faster). In summer, hike up to Morskie Oko lake. In winter, ski. The food — grilled oscypek cheese, lamb, hearty soups — is excellent.
