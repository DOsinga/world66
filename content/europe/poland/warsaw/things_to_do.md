---

Warsaw was 90% destroyed in World War II and its reconstruction is itself one of the most remarkable stories in European history. The Old Town — lovingly rebuilt from photographs, paintings, and memory after the war — is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Warsaw Rising Museum is essential viewing for understanding what happened here. The Royal Route running south from the Old Town passes palaces, churches, and parks; the Łazienki Palace complex at the southern end is the finest green space in the city.
