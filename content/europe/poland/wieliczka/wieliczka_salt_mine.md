One of the most remarkable places in Poland, the Wieliczka Salt Mine has been operating since the 13th century and descends to a depth of 327 metres across nine levels. Guided tours follow roughly 3.5 kilometres of tunnels past lakes, chambers, and sculptures all hewn from salt — including the extraordinary Chapel of St. Kinga, a full underground cathedral used for masses and concerts to this day. The tour lasts around two hours and is conducted in groups; English-language tours run frequently. Allow time to queue in high season.

The mine is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and receives well over a million visitors a year — it is genuinely spectacular and the crowds are warranted.
