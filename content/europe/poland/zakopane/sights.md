---
title: "Sights in Zakopane"
type: section
---

**Zakopane Sights** might not make up an elaborate list, but one
thing is for sure: the quality of the sights will be incredible.
**Mount Kasprowy

**

Mount Kasprowy, also known as Kasprowy Wierch, has been a popular
European Ski destination since the beginning of the 20th century. Its
tramway, which goes almost to the top of the mountain, is the oldest in
Europe, dating before the Second World War. But don’t worry. You can be
assured it has been reinforced a few times since its construction. Just
don’t expect a quiet trip to the top. This is a very popular resort, and
you should plan on waiting a couple of hours to get your ticket, but it
is definitely worth the wait.
**Lake Morskie

**

Lake Morskie, also called the Morskie Oko, is the largest lake in
these mountains, though not quite the deepest. It is also called Fish
Lake (in Polish). The water is extremely clear, and you can watch the
native trout here. You are not allowed to feed the trout or swim in the
lake, however. There is not even a road that can be used by automobiles
near the lake. In fact, you have to walk almost two hours to get to a
motorized road. Fortunately, there are horse-drawn carriages available
for those who cannot walk for two hours straight.
**Krupowki Street

**

A visit to Zakopane without a visit to Krupowki Street would be a
great missed opportunity. In fact, missing it may be impossible,
considering that it is right in the middle of Zakopane. Krupowki Street
is just a little over a kilometer in length, but the sheer amount of
life in this small area is amazing. The name comes from the Krupa
Family, who once had a clearing at the end of the street. For most of
the 19th century this was quite a swampy area. It became a holiday area
in the early 20th century, and grew into a bustling trade area right
after that. There are really awesome clubs and bars on this street, some
of which may claim to be the best in Poland. Apart from the clubs and
bars, you also have traditional Polish restaurants, where they have a
quaint tradition of Polish folk singers entertaining the diners. There
are jewelry shops where you will find amber in good quantity. You can
easily spend a few days just on Krupowki Street and not exhaust all the
possibilities of fun.
