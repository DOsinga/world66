---
title: "Things to do in Zakopane"
type: section
---

Having a **Zakopane things to do** list while visiting the town of
Zakopane in the south of Poland is a must! Zakopane is considered the
winter capital of Poland and a great destination all year round. Located
near Tatra Mountain, Zakopane is perfect for travelers who love nature
and sports. Here are some of the things that you can do while you are
visiting this small Polish town.

**Extreme Skiing and Snowboarding**

The best time to travel in Zakopane is during winter. This small town
has hosted several Nordic World Ski Championships and other winter
sports Olympics. There are a lot of ski resorts where you can brush up
your skills in skiing or snowboarding. Don’t worry if this is your first
time to try these extreme winter sports, since all ski resorts in
Zakopane have capable instructors who will make you love the sport
before the day ends.

**Yodel On Top of Mount Tatra**

Mount Tatra is a popular site for European climbers, so if you are up
for a great outdoor adventure, your travel agent can arrange for you to
take on a climbing expedition to the top of this peak to yodel--just
like the characters in classical films. Mount Tatra is open for climbers
during winter and summer, but the best time to climb the mountain is
during winter, when you can climb using crampons and ice axes to get
your way to the top.

**Ice Skating at a Frozen Lake**

You can also go to Morskie Oko, which is located in the higher
elevation of Zakopane. Look for the Czarny Slaw, which literally means
“Black Lake” in Polish. This is a deep lake found in the mountains of
Zakopane. This lake provides a great view during summer, but it freezes
up during the winter and provides a great location for ice skaters. In
fact, this frozen lake is a popular site for ice skating competitions in
Poland.

**Catch the Local Flavors**

Before you finish your vacation in Zakopane, you must go to Krupowki
Street, located in the main thoroughfare of the town. The street is
filled with a lot of local restaurants, bistros, galleries and gift
shops. Sit in one of their bistros, and don’t forget to try their local
cheese made from sheep’s milk called “oscypek” and their sauerkraut
soup. Sampling the local cuisine is highly recommended before heading
back home.
