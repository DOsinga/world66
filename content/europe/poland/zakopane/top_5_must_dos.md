---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Zakopane"
type: section
---

If snow and winter sports are at the top of your** Zakopane must
do's** list, then you are not alone. Zakopane, Poland, is a winter
wonderland for a better part of the year. However, there is more than
just skiing available in this small, cozy mountain town. Zakopane must
do's should include:
**1. Off Road Adventures

**

Off road tours are offered by way of ATV's. Don't have any
experience on 4-wheelers? Don't sweat it; the highly trained guides will
walk you through everything you need to know. Your safety is their
number one priority. You will be taken through valleys and hills,
through forests, and to many stopping points to admire the views.
Another off road adventure is the patrol. You can spend a half day or a
full one off road with instructors.
**2. Zakopane International Festival

**

The Zakopane International Festival of Highland Folklore takes place
in August, which is a great time to visit. Artists fill the streets with
live performances of music, poetry and dancing. Art exhibits are
displayed, and great food specialties can be had. It is guaranteed to
be a great time when Zakopane and other countries come together and
celebrate folklore. Celebrate art and ties to the past in this festive
event in August. Don't forget the camera, as you will want plenty of
pictures.
**3. The Tatra Museum

**

The Tatra Museum of ethnography and natural history is first on the
list of animal and bird exhibits, but houses folk art as well. Original
Highlander costumes are on display here. Admire beautifully designed
wooden interiors and glass paintings that will awe and amaze you. There
are of course other great museums that will delight the visitor, but the
Tatra Museum should be the first place you start.
**4. The Cable Car

**

The cable car to Kasprowy Wierch may be a little expensive, but it is
well worth the price. You will be carried up the mountain and come face
to face with a marvelous view of the valley below. Catch your breath as
you feel like you are on top of the world. Depending on the season,
you can either catch the cable car back down the mountain or ski your
way to the bottom.
**5. Husky Rides

**

Husky rides are a great family favorite and a superb way to see the
snow covered wilderness. You glide along with only the sound of the wind
whispering past your ears and the sound of the dogs panting as they
take you on an unforgettable trek through the Tatra National Park
valley. You can enjoy the ride or drive a team yourself with a guide
accompanying you on the sleigh. It is an awesome way to spend a day.
