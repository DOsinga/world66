---
title: 'Beaches in the Algarve'
type: section
---

The Algarve has over 150 kilometres of coastline and some of the best beaches in Europe. The character changes dramatically from west to east.

**Western Algarve** -- The coast from [Sagres](/europe/portugal/algarve/sagres) to Lagos has wild, windswept beaches backed by high cliffs. This stretch sits within the Natural Park of Sudoeste Alentejano e Costa Vicentina and offers excellent surf. Many beaches are relatively deserted even in summer.

**Central Algarve** -- The golden sandstone coast around [Albufeira](/europe/portugal/algarve/albufeira) and [Carvoeiro](/europe/portugal/algarve/carvoeiro) has the classic Algarve scenery: sculpted cliffs, sea caves, and sheltered coves. These are the most popular and most crowded beaches.

**Eastern Algarve** -- From [Faro](/europe/portugal/algarve/faro) to [Vila Real](/europe/portugal/algarve/vilareal), long barrier islands protect the Ria Formosa lagoon. The island beaches -- reached by ferry -- have wide flat sand, warm calm water, and significantly fewer visitors. [Praia Verde](/europe/portugal/algarve/praia_verde) near the Spanish border is one of the finest.
