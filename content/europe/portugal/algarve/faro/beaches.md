---
title: "Beaches in Faro"
type: section
---

Faro is surrounded by a collection of tiny islands with fantastic beaches and the Ria Formosa Natural Park. Almost all of these** Faro beaches **can
be reached only by taking a short ferry ride. There is only 1 beach
that can be visited without taking a ferry, Praia de Faro. Sadly, it is
in a very inconvenient location, far from the bustling tourist center
of the town. However, you can take the airport bus to get there, and
from there, all you need to do is cross the small bridge connecting the
sand bank to the mainland. A trip to Praia de Faro is very much worth
the effort because the sand is fine, there are few tourists, and the water is
warm and crystal clear. Here are some of the other island beaches that
are worth a visit.

**Ilha de Tavira Beach**

This beach is full of amenities from beach umbrellas, to parking and
sun beds. Lifeguards are on duty throughout the day and if you tire of
the sun, you can hop over to one of the many fantastic restaurants
nearby.

**Ilha de Armona Beach**

One of the most beautiful beaches in the area, Ilha de Armona has a
relaxing pier to stroll along and spectacular sunsets to enjoy. The
sand is clean and the water is warm and calm. There are lifeguards, sun
umbrellas, and restaurants as well.

**Burgau Beach**

This horseshoe-shaped beach is the best place for kids. It is a safe
beach with warm water and plenty of space to play and build sand
castles. During low tide, children can explore the small tidal pools
created among the rocks and see what sea creatures they can find. There
are lifeguards on duty for extra security, and there is plenty of
parking available. For the adults, there is also a nice beach bar.

**Ilha da Culatra Beach and Ilha da Barreta Beach**

The warm, tranquil, crystal clear water of Ilha da Culatra makes it
the perfect place to go snorkeling or scuba diving. There are also many
interesting species of birds in the area making it a great place to go
birdwatching. Ilha de Barreta Beach is another excellent bird-watching
beach where you can check out flamingos, sandwich terns, and many
others. Both of these beaches have gorgeous views and are nice places
to simply relax and soak up some sun.
