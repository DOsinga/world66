### [Faro Jewish Heritage Centre](/europe/portugal/algarve/faro/sights/faro_jewish_herita)

Officially established in 1851, the Faro Jewish Cemetery is the only remaining vestige of the first post-Inquisition Jewish presence in Portugal.
It served as a burial place for a thriving 60 family community (also
known as "Little Jerusalem"). The Jews of Portugal fled the Inquisition
from the end of the XVth century and settled in Gibraltar and North
Africa. After the devastating 1755 Lisbon Earthquake their descendants
have been invited by the Marquis of Pombal to help rebuild the economy.
Although the community is long gone, the Cemetery still remains to
