---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The walled **Cidade Velha** is compact enough to explore in a few hours. The **Cathedral (Sé)** dates to the 13th century and its bell tower offers rooftop views worth the climb. The former convent housing the **Municipal Museum of Archaeology** has beautiful courtyards and a collection of Roman mosaics and artifacts from the region. Next to the Carmelite Church, the **Capela dos Ossos** (Chapel of Bones) is lined with over a thousand skulls and bones from the adjoining cemetery — one of the most unusual sights in Portugal. For something quieter, the **Jewish Cemetery** on Rua Leão Penedo is Portugal's oldest surviving post-Inquisition Jewish burial ground, a sobering and beautiful place. Beyond the city, the **Ria Formosa Natural Park** offers boat trips, birdwatching, and some of the best beaches in the Algarve.
