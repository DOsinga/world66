---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The walled Cidade Velha is compact enough to explore in a few hours. The Cathedral (Sé) dates to the 13th century and its bell tower offers rooftop views worth the climb. The former convent housing the Municipal Museum of Archaeology has beautiful courtyards and a collection of Roman mosaics and artifacts from the region. For something more unusual, the Jewish Cemetery on Rua Leão Penedo is Portugal's oldest surviving post-Inquisition Jewish burial ground — a sobering and quietly beautiful place.
