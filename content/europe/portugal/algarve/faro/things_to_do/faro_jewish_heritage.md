The only surviving remnant of the first post-Inquisition Jewish community in Portugal. The cemetery, officially established in 1851, served a community of around 60 families who were descendants of Jews expelled during the Inquisition and later invited back by the Marquis of Pombal to help rebuild after the 1755 Lisbon earthquake. Over a hundred Sephardi-style graves remain, with engraved horizontal slabs rather than headstones. Opening hours: 9:30–12:30 and 14:00–17:00.
