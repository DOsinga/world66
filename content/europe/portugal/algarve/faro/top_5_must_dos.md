type: section

Faro is easy to reach as it has an international airport. It is also
serviced by a seaport, a marina and a railway system. The bus network
within Faro provides easy transportation options to explore Faro's
attractions. There are many historical and religios sites in Faro that are worth visiting.

**Capela d'Ossos**

Capela d'Ossos is otherwise known as the Chapel of Bones. The chapel
was built in the 19th century. This is a chapel with a difference as
the walls are covered with human skulls and bones. There are
approximately 1,245 skulls in total. The entrance is through the
courtyard of the church. The church was founded in 1713 and has a
baroque style. Items of interest in the church include an altar,
statues and gold framed windows. There is also a monk's cemetery. The
church is located on Largo do Carmo street. The church is open everyday
and there is no admission fee. The chapel has a small admission fee.
