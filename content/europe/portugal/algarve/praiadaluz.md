Praia da Luz is a low-key beach village in the western Algarve. The main sight in town is a 17th century coastal fort, now converted into a restaurant.

The beach is decent but gets crowded in summer. The real attraction is the coastline to the east, where spectacular cliffs frame the prominent Rocha Negra -- a black rock formed by lava from the extinct volcanoes of Monchique, some 135 to 190 million years ago.

The village has plenty of bars and restaurants, many catering to British visitors. There is a cinema showing films in their original language with Portuguese subtitles, and a few venues along the seafront.
