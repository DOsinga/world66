Aljezur is a small town on the western Algarve coast, inside the Parque Natural do Sudoeste Alentejano e Costa Vicentina — a protected stretch of wild Atlantic coastline that has escaped the resort development further east. The town itself is split in two: the old Moorish quarter climbs a hillside topped by a ruined 10th-century castle, and the newer part sits in the valley below.

The beaches are the main draw. Praia da Arrifana, a wide crescent of sand beneath high cliffs, is one of the best surf spots in the Algarve. Praia de Monte Clérigo is a good family beach, and Praia da Bordeira to the north is vast and windswept. The Rota Vicentina hiking trail passes through the area, following the clifftops south toward [Sagres](/europe/portugal/sagres).

Aljezur has a relaxed, slightly bohemian atmosphere — a handful of good restaurants, a weekly market, and a pace of life that feels far removed from the tourist bustle of [Lagos](/europe/portugal/lagos) or Albufeira an hour to the east.
