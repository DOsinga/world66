Arcos de Valdevez is a quiet market town in the Minho, tucked where the Vez river meets a landscape of terraced vineyards and granite hills. It sits at the edge of the Peneda-Gerês National Park, which makes it a good base for anyone wanting to explore the wilder northern reaches of Portugal without the crowds of Viana do Castelo.

The town itself is unassuming but pleasant — a riverside esplanade, a couple of old churches, and the kind of unhurried pace that feels genuinely Portuguese rather than staged. The real draw is the countryside around it.

The bus ride from [Ponte de Lima](/europe/portugal/ponte_de_lima) is worth taking slowly. One route passes through [Ponte da Barca](/europe/portugal/ponte_da_barca) and the tiny hamlet of Bravães, where a 12th-century Romanesque church stands in a field almost too good to be real — carved stone tympanums, oxen and apostles, and almost nobody else there. If the weather is mild, the walk between Arcos and Ponte da Barca along the river is excellent.
