---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Given its wonderful location on the coast, it´s no surprise that seafood and fish feature highly on the menus of most Cascais restaurants. And absolutely delicious fish and seafood it is too. Freshly caught by the fishermen of this picturesque Portuguese town, the quality is unbeatable. The Portuguese are famed for their use of salted cod and, while it´s worth a try as a national speciality, eating out in Cascais should really be all about making the most of the catch of the day. There are abundant seafood restaurants in Cascais, ranging from cheap and cheerful to upscale, meaning that travelers on any budget will have a chance to try the delicious dishes. Vegetarians are less well catered for in Portuguese cuisine, but with a increasing number of international restaurants in Cascais, nobody need go hungry. At the very least, most places will be happy to rustle up a quick omelette and salad.

Best for a Blowout

Cascais is a rather refined resort, and there are plenty of formal restaurants catering to the well-to-do types that flock here during the summer months. If the budget will stretch to it, fine dining in Cascais is a wonderful experience thanks to the beautiful scenery of the town itself. One of the best of the bunch is Baluarte, at Rua Tenente Valadim, and elegant restaurant and bar with some truly excellent food. Don´t miss the *feijoada* - a bean stew that is typically made with meat but here features seafood and rice, seasoned to perfections. All the fish dishes are here are good, and the desserts are famous across Cascais. Try one of the deliciously rich pastries or tarts to see what the fuss is about.

Best on a Budget

There are plenty of informal, family-run cafes and restaurants in Cascais where hungry travelers can refuel without spending a fortune. Set meals are usually good value, even at the pricier restaurants, and a bowl of tasty *Caldo Verde *(soup made with green cabbage and sausage) will rarely set you back more than a few Euros. For something special that won´t make too much of a hole in the budget, try Restaurante B&B at Rua Poco Novo. Although its name suggests a guest house, this is in fact a great restaurant, much-loved by locals. Diners have a view of the kitchen, so can watch the chef putting together the wonderful fish and seafood dishes that the restaurant is famous for. Expect to pay around 20 Euros a head for two or three courses with coffee.

More than Seafood

Those who have had their fill of seafood will find plenty of places to enjoy other dishes too, and there´s an international flavour to the menu in Cascais French, Italian and Spanish dishes are all easily available, and there´s no shortages of places to grab a quick sandwich, burger or slice of pizza. Head to Praca Luis de Camoes to find scores of restaurants of all varieties, with stiff competition leading to some very good prices.
