---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Cascais is well-served for restaurants, with seafood and fish the obvious choice given its fishing-port history. The catch of the day — grilled simply or prepared in the Portuguese tradition with olive oil and garlic — is reliably excellent. Praça Luís de Camões and the streets around it have scores of restaurants catering to all budgets.

**Baluarte** on Rua Tenente Valadim is one of the best dining options for a proper sit-down meal. The feijoada with seafood and rice is a standout, the fish dishes are uniformly good, and the pastry selection is worth saving room for.

**Restaurante B&B** on Rua Poço Novo is a local favourite on the more affordable end. Diners face an open kitchen and the fish and seafood dishes are what the place is known for — expect to pay around €20 a head for two to three courses.
