Cascais is well-served for restaurants, with seafood and fish the obvious choice given its fishing-port history. The catch of the day — grilled simply or prepared in the Portuguese tradition with olive oil and garlic — is reliably excellent. Praça Luís de Camões and the streets around it have scores of restaurants catering to all budgets.

**Baluarte** on Rua Tenente Valadim is one of the best dining options for a proper sit-down meal. The feijoada with seafood and rice is a standout, the fish dishes are uniformly good, and the pastry selection is worth saving room for.

**Restaurante B&B** on Rua Poço Novo is a local favourite on the more affordable end. Diners face an open kitchen and the fish and seafood dishes are what the place is known for — expect to pay around €20 a head for two to three courses.
=======
Seafood dominates in Cascais — freshly caught by local fishermen, the quality is outstanding. Salted cod (*bacalhau*) is the national speciality, but the real pleasure here is the catch of the day: grilled sea bass, clams, and shellfish platters. Restaurants range from cheap family-run places to elegant fine dining, so every budget is covered.

**Baluarte** on Rua Tenente Valadim is one of the best for a blowout — try the seafood *feijoada* (bean stew with seafood and rice). For good food at a reasonable price, **Restaurante B&B** on Rua Poço Novo is a local favourite with an open kitchen and excellent fish dishes — expect around €20 per head for two or three courses. The main square, **Praça Luís de Camões**, is lined with restaurants of all kinds, where stiff competition keeps prices competitive.

A bowl of *caldo verde* (green cabbage and sausage soup) rarely costs more than a few euros and makes a fine light meal.
