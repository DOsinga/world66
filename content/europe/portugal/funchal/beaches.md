---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Funchal's beaches are not what most people picture when they hear the word. White sand is rare on Madeira; the coastline is volcanic, so the sand is black and the shore is rocky. But the water is crystal clear and pleasantly warm, and the island has built a string of bathing complexes that make the most of what nature provided.

**Lido**

The most popular bathing complex in Funchal. Two seawater pools are refilled daily, and there is direct ocean access for open-water swimming. The complex also has a water slide, diving center, table tennis, and a baby care room.

**Ponta Gorda**

One of the newer complexes and quickly becoming a favorite. Two swimming pools and a children's pool, plus direct sea access. Sandy areas for beach sports.

**Doca do Cavacas**

Natural pools formed by volcanic rock, enclosed from the open sea but connected to it. Less developed than other complexes but more beautiful. Basic facilities: restaurant, bar, changing rooms.

**Praia Formosa**

About ten minutes from the city center. Three small sandy beaches plus a larger pebble beach. Popular in summer with a lively atmosphere including sports activities and evening events.

**Barreirinha**

At the end of the old town. A children's pool and direct ocean access. Gets very crowded on weekends, so visit during the week if you can.
