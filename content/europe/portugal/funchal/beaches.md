---
title: "Beaches in Funchal"
type: section
---

**Funchal Beaches** are not what people expect when they hear the word beach. The one thing that you are not going to find a lot of is white sand, unless you happen to go to one of the beaches on the island that imports the sand. The beaches around Funchal feature black volcanic sand, but the water is still crystal clear and pleasantly warm.

**Lido**

This is one of the most popular beaches in Funchal, and it is one of the best beaches on the island. The beach promotes two seawater pools that are filled with fresh seawater each day, but if you don&rsquo;t enjoy swimming in a confined pool you also have direct ocean access. The bathing center has a lot more to offer than other bathing centers, which makes it a favorite it among the locals. The complex provides table tennis, a diving center, a water slide, a baby care room, and much more.

**Ponta Gorda**

This is one of the newest bathing complexes on the island and is quickly becoming one of the best places to go, partly because of its location, but also because of everything that it has to offer. Here you will find two swimming pools and one children&rsquo;s pool, but you will also have direct sea access for those who enjoy swimming out in the open water. There are also sandy areas that allow you to practice various sports.

**Doca do Cavacas**

This bathing complex doesn&rsquo;t offer nearly as much as some of the other bathing complexes, but its natural beauty is what draws people to it. Here you have some natural pools, which are a part of the ocean but are enclosed so you are not swimming in the open sea. These natural pools are not to be confused with swimming pools. You also have direct access to the ocean if that is what you prefer. These pools offer the basics, such as restaurants and bars and a changing room, but not much else.

**Praia Formosa**

This beach is located within a 10-minute drive of Funchal&rsquo;s city center and is one of the most popular beaches in the summer. This beach offers you the chance to choose what type of beach you want to visit because you have the choice of three small sandy beaches and a larger, but pebbled, beach. Many adventurous people enjoy walking on the rocks despite how tricky it can be. During the summer months the beach has a great nightlife, offering a variety of contests and sports activities.

**Barreirinha**

This is one beach that you really want to visit during the week rather than the weekend because of how crowded it gets. It is located at the end of the older part of town, but still offers some great facilities for its visitors. Here you have a swimming pool for children, but also direct ocean access for adults or families that prefer the open water.
