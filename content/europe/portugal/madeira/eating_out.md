The only problem you are going to have concerning restaurants in
Madeira is to decide which one to go to. Restaurants are abundant and
cater to the tastes of just about anyone. You can find gourmet fair all
the way to street vendors and spend a lot of money to just a little.

**Ristorante Villa Cipriani**

Ristorante Villa Cipriani owned by Reids Palace Hotel not only has
good food but the atmosphere and views from the windows by the cliffs
overlooking the sea are simply breathtaking. The Italian food is very
good and dinners can cost $40 and up. Go there merely to see the view
but make sure to make reservations first and ask for a cliffside table.

**Combatentes in Funchal**

A mid-range restaurant with very good seafood is Combatentes in
Funchal. They offer a fresh day’s catch and other traditional dishes of
the area. Their wine list is extensive as well. You can get a good
meal here for about $10 to $20.

**San Antonio**

Another mid-range restaurant that has the best espetadas, which is
beef roasted on a spit, is a restaurant called San Antonio. Here the
staff is very friendly and the food is very good. The restaurant is
very colorful and is usually full of people. You can get a meal here
for $10 to $20.

**Ferro Velho in Sao Vicente**

Seafood is king at Ferro Velho in Sao Vicente is on a promenade near
a whole row of restaurants. The tables are outside and their fish
offerings are very good. Dinners will cost you about $10 to $20.

**Doca do Cavacas**

Looking for a place with some gorgeous scenery and romantic
ambiance? Go to Doca do Cavacas and partake of the seafood. Watch the
spices as everything is highly spiced in this restaurant. Also note
that many waiters do not speak English very well but it is worth it just
to see the ocean from the restaurant. The restaurant is near mall
Centro Comercial Centromar in Funchal and it is hard to find because
you have to walk down many stairs to get to it from the parking lot.
This restaurant is a mid range and you can get a dinner for $20 to $30
per person.

**Inexpensive Dining**

For the budget minded individual you can go to small bars and local
restaurants and find food that is extremely inexpensive. Many of them
have soup and a few main dishes but they can be very delicious and cost
less than $10. You can find a bunch of these small places in Old Market
Square in Machico. Many serve outdoors and in and they beat fast food
any day.

Expensive or cheap, you can find anything you want to eat in Madeira.
