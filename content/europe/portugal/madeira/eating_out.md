---
title: "Eating Out in Madeira"
type: section
---

Restaurants are abundant in Madeira and cater to a wide range of budgets, from gourmet dining rooms to simple local bars with a chalked menu on the wall. The island has its own distinctive cuisine worth seeking out alongside the international options.

The local specialty is espada — black scabbardfish, usually served fried and often accompanied, oddly but well, by banana. Espetada is beef (or sometimes tuna) threaded onto a stick of bay laurel and grilled over wood: it originated in the mountains but you'll find it across the island. For cheap, filling meals, look for small bars and local restaurants in places like Old Market Square in Machico, where the food is straightforward but very good and usually costs under €10.

**Ristorante Villa Cipriani** (Reid's Palace Hotel) is one of the island's more theatrical dining experiences — the Italian food is very good, but the real draw is the location, perched on a cliff with views over the ocean. Dinner runs to €40 and up; reservations essential and ask for a cliffside table.

**Combatentes** in Funchal is a reliable mid-range choice with good seafood and a fresh daily catch. **San Antonio** does espetada well in a colourful, lively room — usually full of people, which is a good sign. **Ferro Velho** in São Vicente sits on a promenade near a row of other restaurants; the tables are outside and the fish is the thing to order.

**Doca do Cavacas**, near Centro Comercial Centromar in Funchal, is worth the effort of finding — you have to walk down a lot of stairs from the car park — for the ocean scenery alone. The food is highly spiced, which can catch you off guard, and English is not always spoken fluently by the staff. Budget €20–30 per person.
