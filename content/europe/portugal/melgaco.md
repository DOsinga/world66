Melgaço is the northernmost town in Portugal, sitting in the upper Minho valley near the Spanish border. It is reached by a scenic bus ride up the river from [Valença do Minho](/europe/portugal/valena_do_minho). The town has a medieval castle keep and a small historic centre worth an afternoon's wandering.

A Friday market brings life to the town. The same day, a midday bus runs out to the Peneda-Gerês National Park — though it does not run on public holidays, when only the early morning bus operates. The national park makes Melgaço a useful base for walkers and anyone wanting to explore the Gerês mountains without going through Braga.

The area is also known for Alvarinho wine, the crisp and aromatic white that crosses the border into the Spanish Rías Baixas as Albariño.
