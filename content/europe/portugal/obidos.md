Óbidos is a perfectly preserved walled town about an hour north of [Lisbon](/europe/portugal/lisbon), its whitewashed houses and narrow cobblestone streets enclosed by medieval battlements you can walk along end to end. In high season it gets terribly crowded — believably so, given how photogenic the place is — but visit on a quiet weekday or in winter and you will have its lanes largely to yourself.

The castle at the top of the hill has been converted into a pousada, but you can walk in for a coffee and enjoy the courtyard without being a guest. Below it, the main street runs downhill past craft shops, bookshops, and cafes selling ginjinha — the local sour cherry liqueur served in a tiny chocolate cup. The old stocks still stand in the village streets, a reminder that Óbidos was not always so picturesque.

The town hosts a popular medieval festival each July, with jousting, period food stalls, and costumed parades. At Easter, keep an eye out for the Palm Sunday procession — an oddly sombre affair for what should be a day of rejoicing, led by a barefoot figure of gloom.
