These gardens are well worth a visit if you want to rest for a while in a peaceful environment or just to get to know a bit more about the city's green spaces. Get inside and enjoy the calm atmosphere, and if you go all the way to the end (south) you'll have a fantastic view over the river and the sea. Grab your camera!

The big UFO at the entrance is a sports dome built in 1956 to replace an iron and glass pavillion, the original Crystal Palace (Palácio de Cristal). There's also a restaurant and an open air concert stage. The 19th century atmosphere is complemented with a recent addition: the Biblioteca Almeida Garrett, a modern library where you can go for some quiet book or newspaper reading, a cup of coffee or just to check your email...

At Rua de Entrequintas, behind the park, the Quinta da Macieirinha is home to a romantic museum that features the life of a typical wealthy family from the 19th century. At the Solar do Vinho do Porto you can also have a taste of Port.

If you're into art, don't miss the nearby Rua Miguel Bombarda, home to the highest concentration of art galleries in the city.
