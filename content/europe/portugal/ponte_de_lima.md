The Romans are supposed to have thought the Lima was the mythical river Lethe, so beautiful it took men's memories away. The story goes that the legionaries would not cross until their commander swam to the far bank and proved he still remembered their names.

Whether or not the legend is true, Ponte de Lima earns the comparison. This small town in the Minho region is one of the most attractive in northern [Portugal](/europe/portugal), set on a bend of the Lima river and reached by a long medieval bridge. The town claims to be the oldest in the country — it received its charter in 1125.

The old centre is compact and walkable, with a fortified tower, the church of Santo António, and a pleasant riverside promenade lined with plane trees. Ponte de Lima is also the starting point of the Portuguese Camino de Santiago and a centre of the vinho verde wine region. The twice-monthly Monday market on the riverbank is one of the oldest in Portugal and worth timing a visit around.
