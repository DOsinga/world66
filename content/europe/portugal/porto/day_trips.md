title: "Day Trips in Porto"
type: section
---

**Porto Day Trips** are easy to plan since Porto is well-connected to most of the nearby locations. Porto, or Oporto, is the largest city in northern Portugal, located in the Iberian Peninsula. Porto is also a significant port, serving as a gateway to most of the commercial centers of Spain and Portugal. Some of the most popular Porto day trip options include:

**Exploring Landscape of Douro Valley**

The popularity of Douro Valley is sure to engage the attention of every Porto visitor. This valley is counted among eastern Europe&rsquo;s most popular natural landscapes. It is now an official World Heritage Site. Visitors have options for reaching the valley. They can take the usual combination of local trains and cabs or be a bit more adventurous and take a boat-ride. Most of the connecting boat-rides begin from Cais de Gaia, located along the outskirts of Porto. The Douro Valley is actually hard to explore within a day since it has so much to offer. This includes the vast wine plantations and the Mateus Palace and the Mateus Gardens. Other notable sights here include the Amarante and the Vila Real Center. Visitors to the Douro should make it a point to visit the long underground tunnel that stretches beyond 100 miles. Further, some time can be spent soaking-in the natural beauty of Douro, complete with cedar trees and lush-green outfields.

**Exploring Braga & Other Nearby Cities**

Braga is seriously recommended to folks who like exploring places of historical significance. Braga is home to many monuments that help to understand the history of Portugal. Further, some time can be spent at the Bom Jesus Sanctuary or another World Heritage site—the city of Guimaraes. Here, visitors can explore the lavish castles that were built during the 12th century. A slightly longer schedule means that visitors can also visit the nearby cities of Viana do Castelo and Vila Real. These cities are popular for many well-preserved historical ruins. People willing to return to Porto, later in the night, can explore a bit more by taking a short, walking-tour through the cultural quarters of Ribeira.
