Praia da Rocha is a beach resort at the mouth of the Arade River, just south of Portimão in the Algarve. The main beach is wide and long, backed by eroded sandstone cliffs and sea stacks in characteristic Algarve orange-red. A 16th-century fort (Fortaleza de Santa Catarina) stands at the eastern end of the beach, where the river meets the sea.

The resort is one of the older and more established in the Algarve, with a strip of restaurants, bars, and hotels along the clifftop promenade. It gets crowded in July and August but is significantly quieter in May–June and September. To the west, Praia do Alemão and Praia do Vau are less crowded extensions of the same coastline.

Portimão, a short walk or bus ride north, is the nearest town with a market, supermarkets, and the Museu de Portimão (housed in a former sardine factory — one of the best regional museums in Portugal).
