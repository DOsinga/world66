---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Praia da Rocha"
type: section
---

**Praia da Rocha must do’s** tend to be centered around the beach,
although there is plenty to do if you are not a keen swimmer. This
town is a popular seaside resort in Portugal in the famous Algrave
region.
1. Historical Fort

St. Catarina Fort is a great historical sight, and it is found at one
end of the main beach. The fort was built in 1621 in order to protect
the city from the Moors and the Spanish pirates. Today you can visit the
fort and see the fantastic panoramic views, as the fort is at a high
point overlooking the city. This is a popular spot for bird watching and
viewing the sunset. There is a small café and bar where you can get a
drink and something to eat.
2. A Walk Along the Cliffs

The cliffs are quite spectacular, and there is a great scenic walk
along the cliff tops from Buraco da Avo that ends a little bit beyond
Praia da Rocha. The cliff edges are quite dangerous but there are
walkways that are newly cobbled, making it very easy to enjoy the walk
and the views without having to worry about falling.
3. Shopping, Eating and Nightlife

The best spot for restaurants and shopping is at Tomas Cabieira
Avenue, which follows along the coastline. It is here where you can find
many hotels, discos, restaurants, the casino, and several bars. This is
the spot for nightlife in Praia da Rocha. You can enjoy this area
during the day and at all hours of the night.
4. Time at the Beach

The beach is very long and wide, so even during the busiest days in
the summer, there is plenty of space. Behind the beach are reef cliffs
that glow in the morning and evening during sunrise and sunset. There
are concession areas where you can rent an umbrella. It is easily
possible to spend all day at the beach and never have to leave. You can
partake in many water sports including pedaloes, banana boats, water
skiing, jet skiing, sailing, and more. There are several different spots
to which you can access the beach, most having plenty of steps.
5. A Round of Golf

Many people travel to Praia da Rocha for the golf, as there are some
beautiful courses. The very first course is Penina, and is a masterpiece
designed by Sir Henry Cotton. Palmares Course is known for its
spectacular scenery. The Morgado do Reguengo is the newest of all the
courses and is spread out over 980 hectares. The last golf course to be
designed by Henry Cotton is Alto Golf, which runs through two different
valleys.
