Santarém sits on a bluff above the Tagus river in central [Portugal](/europe/portugal), about an hour's drive northeast of [Lisbon](/europe/portugal/lisbon). It was once one of the most important cities in the country — a Moorish stronghold taken by Afonso Henriques in 1147, and later a favoured residence of medieval Portuguese kings. The city still has a remarkable concentration of Gothic churches for its size.

The viewpoint at the Portas do Sol garden, on the site of the old Moorish citadel, offers sweeping views over the Tagus plain — one of the best panoramas in central Portugal. Below, the Ribatejo flatlands are cattle country, and Santarém is the unofficial capital of Portuguese bullfighting. The annual agricultural fair in October is one of the largest in the country.

The old town is pleasant to walk through, with the churches of São João de Alporão (now a museum), the Convento de São Francisco, and the Igreja do Seminário worth seeking out. Santarém is also known for its pastry shops — try the queijadas and the celestes.
