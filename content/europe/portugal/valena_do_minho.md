Valenca do Minho sits on the northern border of [Portugal](/europe/portugal), perched above the Minho River with [Spain](/europe/spain) visible on the opposite bank. The town is dominated by its massive double-walled fortress, built in the 17th century to defend against Spanish incursions. The fortifications are remarkably intact — you can walk the full circuit of the walls, with views over the river, the Eiffel-designed railway bridge, and the Spanish town of Tuy across the water.

Inside the fortress walls, the old town has been colonised by shops selling linen, towels, and household goods, which draw busloads of Spanish day-trippers looking for bargains. The commercial bustle can feel at odds with the medieval setting, but if you venture past the shops you will find quiet corners, a Romanesque church, and some excellent views from the ramparts.

Beyond the fortress, there is not a great deal to detain you in Valenca itself. The town makes a natural stop if you are travelling the Minho valley — from here you can continue upriver to Moncao or Melgaco, both of which have more character as destinations. The railway bridge connects directly to Tuy, making a cross-border day trip easy on foot.
