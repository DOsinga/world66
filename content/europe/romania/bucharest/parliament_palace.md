The second-largest administrative building in the world after the Pentagon, built at Ceaușescu's command between 1984 and 1989. A fifth of Bucharest's historic centre was demolished to make room for it. The 45-minute guided tours run every half hour and take you through marble halls of staggering scale — 3,100 rooms, twelve stories, 330,000 square metres. The view from the balcony where Ceaușescu once addressed crowds is worth the visit alone.
