One of Europe's first and finest open-air ethnographic museums, spread across 30 acres on the shore of Lake Herăstrău. Over forty traditional buildings — log cabins, churches, watermills, thatched barns — have been brought here from every region of Romania. Founded in 1936 by Professor Dimitrie Gusti, it is the most comprehensive introduction to Romania's rural architecture and folk traditions you will find anywhere.
