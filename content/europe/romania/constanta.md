Constanta is Romania's main port and the gateway to the Black Sea coast. Founded in the 6th century BC by Greek merchants as Tomis, it was later developed by the Romans and renamed after Emperor Constantine. The poet Ovid was exiled here by Augustus in 8 AD and spent his last years writing elegies about the cold and the barbarians — his statue still stands in the main square.

The city has a faded grandeur that rewards exploration. The Art Nouveau Casino on the waterfront, overlooking the sea, is Constanta's most iconic building. The old town has Roman mosaics, an archaeological museum, a mosque, and an Orthodox cathedral. The Mamaia resort strip stretches north of the city along a narrow sand bar between the sea and a lagoon — it is Romania's most popular seaside destination, busy in summer with Romanian and international tourists.
