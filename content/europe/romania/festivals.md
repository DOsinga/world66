title: "Festivals"

---

Romania has a lively festival calendar that spans Orthodox religious tradition, medieval pageantry, and some of Europe's biggest music events.

**Orthodox Easter**

Easter is the most important celebration in the Romanian Orthodox calendar, and it falls later than Western Easter in most years. On Holy Saturday at midnight, Romanians gather outside churches across the country. The priest brings out the holy light, candles are passed through the crowd, and people carry their flames home through darkened streets to bless their households. The sight of hundreds of candles moving through a village square at 1am is unlike anything in Western Europe. Easter Sunday means sarmale (stuffed cabbage rolls), lamb, and cozonac (sweet bread). The egg-decoration tradition reaches its highest form in Bucovina, where villages like Ciocănești are famous for their elaborate painted eggs.

**Sighișoara Medieval Festival**

Held in July in the medieval citadel of [Sighișoara](/europe/romania/sighisoara), this festival fills the cobbled streets with knights, merchants, fire-eaters, and musicians for a long weekend. It's touristy but genuinely atmospheric — the setting, a 12th-century Saxon walled town, does most of the work.

**Untold Festival**

Held in early August at Cluj-Napoca's stadium, Untold is one of Europe's largest electronic music and pop festivals, drawing 350,000+ attendees over four days. Headliners have included Armin van Buuren, Tiësto, Martin Garrix, and major pop acts. Accommodation in Cluj books out months in advance during Untold.

**Electric Castle**

Electric Castle takes place in late July at the grounds of Bánffy Castle in Bonțida, 30km from Cluj. Smaller and more eclectic than Untold, it blends electronic music with live bands, art installations, and theatre in a genuinely magical setting. It has a devoted following and a reputation for good atmosphere.

**George Enescu International Festival**

Held in Bucharest every two years in late August and September, this classical music festival is one of the most prestigious in Eastern Europe, named after Romania's greatest composer. Orchestras and soloists from around the world perform at the Romanian Athenaeum and other Bucharest venues.

**Christmas Markets**

Sibiu, Brașov, and Bucharest all host Christmas markets from late November through December. Sibiu's market, held in the Grand Square, is considered among the best in Eastern Europe — smaller and more authentic than its Austrian or German counterparts.
