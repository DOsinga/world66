Galați is a port city on the Danube in eastern [Romania](/europe/romania), close to the border with Moldova and Ukraine. With around 250,000 people it is one of Romania's larger cities, though it rarely features on tourist itineraries. The massive ArcelorMittal steelworks — formerly SIDEX, once the largest in southeastern Europe — still dominates the skyline and the local economy.

The city's position on the lower Danube gives it a rough charm. The waterfront promenade along the river has been modernised, and the old centre has a scattering of 19th-century buildings, churches, and a botanical garden. The "Dunărea de Jos" University brings student life to the city. Galați can serve as a base for exploring the Danube Delta, which begins not far downstream — though most travellers head to [Tulcea](/europe/romania/tulcea) for that.
