---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Romania rewards travellers willing to look past the Dracula clichés. Its real draws are a remarkable variety: painted medieval monasteries, intact Saxon villages, serious mountain wilderness, a vast river delta, and a capital city that has been rebuilding its identity since 1989.

**Transylvania**

The most visited region, and deservedly so. [Brașov](/europe/romania/brasov) is the practical base — a well-preserved medieval town ringed by the Carpathians, with good transport connections. From here you can reach [Bran Castle](/europe/romania/bran) (the "Dracula's castle" of popular imagination, though the association with Vlad Țepeș is thin), the stunning Peleș Palace in [Sinaia](/europe/romania/sinaia), and the Saxon citadel of [Sighișoara](/europe/romania/sighisoara) — arguably the finest preserved medieval town in Romania.

[Sibiu](/europe/romania/sibiu) stands apart: a confident, well-restored city with a handsome main square and a string of excellent museums, including the superb open-air folk museum at Dumbrava. Corvin Castle (Huniazilor) near [Hunedoara](/europe/romania/hunedoara) is the most dramatic castle in Romania — a Gothic-Renaissance fortress that looks like it was designed by a child imagining what a proper castle should look like.

**The Painted Monasteries of Bucovina**

In the Moldavia region, a cluster of 15th- and 16th-century monasteries bear exterior frescoes that have survived six centuries of weather and warfare. The colours — vivid blues, greens, reds — depict biblical scenes, saints, and the Last Judgement. Voroneț (the "Sistine Chapel of the East"), Sucevița, Moldovița, and Humor are the ones most worth the effort. The area around [Suceava](/europe/romania/suceava) makes a good base.

**The Danube Delta**

Where the Danube meets the Black Sea, a vast, labyrinthine wetland of channels, reed beds, and lakes hosts over 300 species of birds and 160 species of fish. It is the second-largest river delta in Europe and a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. [Tulcea](/europe/romania/tulcea) is the gateway — from here, ferries and private boats fan out into the delta. Staying overnight in one of the small fishing villages (Sulina, Sfântu Gheorghe) is far better than a day trip. Bring mosquito repellent.

**Bucharest**

[Bucharest](/europe/romania/bucharest) is chaotic, contradictory, and more interesting than its reputation suggests. Ceaușescu's megalomaniac Palace of the Parliament dominates one end of a brutally cleared boulevard, while a few streets away art nouveau villas crumble alongside 1930s modernist apartment buildings. The city's cafe and restaurant scene has transformed since the 2010s; the Floreasca, Dorobanți, and old city centre neighbourhoods are worth half a day each.

**The Black Sea Coast**

[Constanța](/europe/romania/constanta) is Romania's ancient port city, with a 2,500-year history and a decent archaeological museum. [Mamaia](/europe/romania/mamaia) is the main resort strip — busy, brash, and fun in July and August. At the southern end of the coast, Vama Veche has a counter-culture tradition and a more relaxed, bohemian character than the manicured resorts further north.

**The Carpathian Mountains**

The mountains running through the centre of Romania offer serious hiking, ski resorts, bear-watching (Romania has Europe's largest brown bear population), and a string of highland villages that feel genuinely remote. [Poiana Brașov](/europe/romania/poiana_brasov) and [Predeal](/europe/romania/predeal) are the main ski resorts, both close to Brașov.
