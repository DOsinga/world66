Otopeni is a small town in Ilfov County, 15 kilometres north of [Bucharest](/europe/romania/bucharest) on the road to Ploiesti. Most travellers know it only for Henri Coanda International Airport, Romania's busiest airport and the main gateway to the capital. There is little reason to linger in Otopeni itself — Bucharest is easily reached by express train (about 25 minutes to Gara de Nord) or the 780 bus.

The town has grown rapidly in recent years, with new residential developments and a few business hotels catering to transit passengers. If you have a long layover and want to kill time outside the terminal, there are some restaurants and a small park along the main road, but honestly you are better off heading into Bucharest. The airport express train makes it practical to visit the capital even with just a few hours to spare.

Otopeni's only real landmark is the airport itself, named after Henri Coanda, the Romanian engineer who built one of the world's first jet-propelled aircraft in 1910. A replica of his plane stands in the departures hall.
