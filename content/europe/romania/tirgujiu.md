Târgu Jiu is a small city in the Jiu Valley of southwestern Romania, and it would be entirely unremarkable if not for one thing: Constantin Brâncuși was born nearby, and in 1937 he created an extraordinary ensemble of monumental sculptures here that ranks among the most important public art in Europe.

The three works — the Table of Silence, the Gate of the Kiss, and the Endless Column — are arranged along a single axis running through the city. The Endless Column, nearly 30 metres of stacked rhomboid modules in cast iron, is the centrepiece and arguably Brâncuși's masterwork. Walking the axis from the river to the column takes about twenty minutes and is one of Romania's most moving experiences.

The city itself is a modest regional centre with a pleasant enough old town and a park along the Jiu River. Most visitors come for the sculptures alone, and that is reason enough.
