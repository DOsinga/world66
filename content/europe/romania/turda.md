Turda is a small city in Cluj County, Transylvania, about 30 km south of Cluj-Napoca. For most visitors it's a day trip from Cluj, and one attraction in particular makes it worth the journey: Salina Turda.

The Salina Turda is an extraordinary salt mine converted into an underground amusement park and wellness centre. Mining here dates back to antiquity, with large-scale Roman extraction documented. The vast underground chambers — some reaching 100 metres high — now hold a Ferris wheel, rowing boats on a subterranean lake, and a mini golf course, all lit up in eerie colours against ancient salt walls. It's genuinely unlike anything else in Romania and has become one of the country's most visited attractions.

The old town of Turda itself is relatively modest, with a main square and some Baroque churches, but most visitors come specifically for the mine. There are regular buses and minibuses from Cluj-Napoca.
