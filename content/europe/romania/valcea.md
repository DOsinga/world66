Vâlcea is a county in southern Romania where the Olt river cuts through the Carpathian foothills, creating a valley that has been a trade route and a place of retreat for centuries. The main draw is the painted monasteries of the Olt Valley — a string of Orthodox foundations that rival Bucovina's painted churches, though they are far less visited.

The star is Horezu Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage Site founded in 1690, famous for its Brâncoveanu-style architecture and frescoes of extraordinary delicacy. The pottery tradition of Horezu — bold, geometric designs in white, brown, and green — is alive and well in workshops around the town. The county capital, Râmnicu Vâlcea, is a practical base without much character of its own, but the surrounding countryside of forested hills, thermal springs (Călimănești-Căciulata), and hilltop monasteries rewards slow exploration.
