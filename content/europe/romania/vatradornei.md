Vatra Dornei is a well-known spa and ski resort in the north of [Romania](/europe/romania), set in a valley of the Eastern Carpathians. The town has been a health resort since the Habsburg era, and the mineral springs and mountain air still draw visitors. In winter the surrounding mountains offer modest skiing; in summer the area is good for hiking.

The real draw is the proximity to the painted monasteries of Bucovina, some of the finest medieval art in Europe. **Voronet**, called the "Sistine Chapel of the East," is remarkable for the famous "Blue of Voronet" in its exterior frescoes. **Humor** (built 1530), **Moldovita** (1532), and **Sucevita** (1582–1584) are all covered with frescoes depicting biblical scenes. **Putna**, built by Stephen the Great between 1466 and 1469, holds the ruler's tomb and an important museum of medieval art.
