The ancient citadel of Narin-Kala sits on the hilltop above Derbent and is the centrepiece of the city's UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its walls enclose 4.5 hectares and contain the ruins of Sassanid-era baths, cisterns, a caravanserai, and the remains of a palace. The citadel was founded in the 5th–6th century AD as a military fortress guarding the narrow Caspian corridor. Walking the ramparts gives a commanding view over the city, the sea, and the two great walls that once extended all the way to the water's edge.
