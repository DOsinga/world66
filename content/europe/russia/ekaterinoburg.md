Yekaterinburg is Russia's fourth-largest city and the capital of the Urals, straddling the geographic boundary between Europe and Asia. A major stop on the Trans-Siberian Railway, it is the place where the last tsar and his family were executed in 1918 — the Church on Blood, built on the site, is the city's most visited landmark.

The city has a lively cultural scene, with a strong tradition of theatre, music, and contemporary art. The wide central avenues are lined with constructivist architecture from the 1920s and 1930s, some of the best examples in the country. The Yeltsin Center, a modern museum dedicated to Russia's first president (a local), is surprisingly engaging regardless of your politics.

Yekaterinburg makes a natural base for exploring the Ural Mountains — hiking, skiing, and visits to old mining towns are all within reach.
