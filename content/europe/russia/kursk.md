Kursk is a regional city in southwestern Russia, about 500 km south of Moscow, sitting on the Tuskar and Kur rivers. It's a mid-sized industrial and administrative centre, not much visited by foreign tourists, but historically significant as the site of one of World War II's decisive battles.

The Battle of Kursk in July–August 1943 was the largest tank battle in history, and the city takes this history seriously. The Kursk Military History Museum and a number of war memorials commemorate the campaign, and the Prokhorovka battlefield — about 80 km north near Belgorod — has a major memorial complex at the site of the armoured clash that turned the tide of the battle.

The city itself has a pleasant park along the river and a few Soviet-era landmarks, but offers little beyond its wartime significance to most visitors. It functions mainly as a gateway for those following the Eastern Front heritage trail through this part of Russia.
