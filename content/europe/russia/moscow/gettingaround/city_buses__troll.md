**City Buses & Trolley Buses & Trams** (or over-ground transportation, as opposite to the under-ground, or Metro) is inexpensive mode of transportation in Moscow.

**Tickets and prices** are the same for bus, trolley or tram. The single fare is 13 roubles when you buy a ticket from a ticket booth or a newsstand by the stop and 15 roubles if you buy ticket from the driver.

You have to enter bus/trolley/tram through the front door where you have to buy ticket from driver if necessary and then to run your ticket (face and arrow down) through a slot in a turnstile for admittance.
To get off at any of the intermediate stops, you have to buzz the driver by using any of the buzzer buttons provided over each door or slightly to the side on a handrail.

Note that **trams **are not so reliable as they could be because car drivers often block tram ways.

**Trolley Buses** are slow (sometimes VERY slow) and not very comfortable (especially in winter because they are COLD) so use them only if you have no other mode of transportation available.
