Novgorod is famous for its medieval churches, monasteries, and museums — most of the city's main sights cluster around the Kremlin (Detinets) and the Yaroslav's Court district just across the river. Nearly everything is walkable from the centre.

**Kremlin (Detinets)**

The impressive walls of the Kremlin date from the 15th century. Inside, St Sophia's Cathedral (1045-1050) is one of the oldest churches in Russia, with a beautiful golden dome and a well-preserved iconostasis. The complex also contains a museum with summer hours noon-1pm and 2:30-5pm (closed Mon-Tue in winter).

latitude: 58.5226
longitude: 31.2744

category: Sight

**Museum of History, Architecture and Art**

Housed within the Kremlin complex, with a fine collection of icons from the Novgorod School and other precious works of art.

opening_hours: 10am-6pm, closed Tuesday and last Thursday of each month

category: Museum

**St Nicholas' Cathedral (Nikolsky Sobor)**

Built in 1113, this Byzantine-style cathedral marked a significant departure from the style of St Sophia. One of the oldest churches in the city.

opening_hours: 10am-6pm, closed Tuesday

category: Sight

**Museum of Wooden Architecture**

Situated in the grounds of the Yuryev Monastery south of the city. A collection of traditional timber buildings from the surrounding region, set around the majestic Cathedral of St George.

opening_hours: 10am-6pm, closed Wednesday; October-April 10am-4pm

category: Museum
