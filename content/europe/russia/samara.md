Samara is a large city on a bend of the Volga River in southeastern European Russia. During the Second World War it served as the Soviet Union's reserve capital, and Stalin's underground bunker — built in secret beneath the city — can now be visited. The city's riverfront promenade, one of the longest in Europe, is the heart of summer life: locals swim in the Volga, barbecue on the sandy beaches, and stroll past the Art Nouveau mansions that line the embankment.

The old centre has a mix of wooden merchant houses and grand Soviet-era buildings. The Samara Space Museum, housed beneath an actual Soyuz rocket mounted vertically on the building's roof, celebrates the city's role in the Soviet space programme — this is where the rockets that launched Gagarin into orbit were built.

Samara is a stop on the Trans-Siberian Railway and a good base for boat trips on the Volga, including to the Zhiguli Mountains just downstream.
