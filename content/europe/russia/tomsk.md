Tomsk is one of Siberia's oldest and most attractive cities, founded in 1604 as a fortress on the Tom River. Unlike most Siberian cities, which were rebuilt in Soviet concrete, Tomsk preserved a remarkable collection of wooden houses — ornately carved, painted in faded pastels, and leaning slightly with age. The wooden architecture alone makes it worth a detour.

The city is a major university centre. Tomsk State University, founded in 1878, was the first university in Siberia, and the student population gives the city a livelier, younger feel than you might expect this deep into Russia. The campus grounds are pleasant for a walk, and the university's botanical garden is one of the oldest in Siberia.

The Resurrection Hill area offers good views over the river and the old town. In winter, Tomsk is genuinely cold — minus 30°C is not unusual — but the snow-covered wooden houses are beautiful. Summer is brief and warm, and the best time to visit.
