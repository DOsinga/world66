Tyumen holds the distinction of being the first Russian city in Siberia, founded in 1586 on the ruins of a Tatar settlement along the Tura River. Today it is the administrative capital of the oil-rich Tyumen Oblast, and the wealth shows — the centre is better maintained than many Russian cities of similar size, with restored merchant houses, a lively embankment, and a handful of good restaurants.

The city is not a typical tourist destination, but it has genuine character. The Lovers' Bridge across the Tura is a local landmark, and the cluster of wooden houses in the old town — carved window frames, painted facades — gives a sense of what Siberian towns looked like before Soviet-era concrete. The hot springs at Verkhniy Bor, about thirty minutes outside the city, are popular year-round and worth a half-day trip.
