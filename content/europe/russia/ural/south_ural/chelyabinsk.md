Chelyabinsk is the administrative capital of the South Ural and one of Russia's ten largest cities, with a population of over a million. Founded as a fortress in 1736, it grew into a major industrial centre during the Soviet era — a reputation that has left it with an image of grey factory smokestacks, not entirely deserved.

The city has broad avenues, original fountains and sculpture compositions, and a richer cultural life than you might expect. Five state and four municipal theatres, a philharmonic, a circus, an art gallery and several museums keep the city entertained through the long winters. The organ in the concert hall, with its 2,504 pipes built by the German firm Hermann Eule of Bautzen, is a particular point of pride, and organ music festivals have become a local tradition.

Chelyabinsk is also known internationally for the spectacular meteor that exploded over the city in February 2013, an event captured on hundreds of dashcam videos. Fragments are displayed in the Chelyabinsk Regional Museum.
