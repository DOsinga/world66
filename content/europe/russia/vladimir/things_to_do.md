---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Vladimir's main attractions are its medieval white-stone churches, among the oldest surviving buildings in Russia. All three major monuments sit along the main east-west avenue and can be visited in a half-day walk.

**Assumption Cathedral** — The masterpiece. Built in 1158 and enlarged in 1189, this was the model for the later Assumption Cathedral in the Moscow Kremlin. Inside are frescoes by Andrei Rublev, painted in 1408.

**Cathedral of St. Demetrius** — A smaller church from 1194, famous for its extraordinary carved stone exterior — hundreds of figures of saints, animals, and plants cover the upper walls.

**Golden Gate** — Built in 1164 as the main ceremonial entrance to the city. The small military history museum inside has a diorama of the Mongol siege of 1238.
