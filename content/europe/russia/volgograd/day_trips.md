title: "Day Trips in Volgograd"
type: section
---

If you're looking for good **Volgograd day trips** to take, visit the World War II Complex. The World War II Complex is a must-see and must-visit place in Volgograd for local and international visitors. This complex has been made to commemorate the Stalingrad Battle. This World War II Complex is more popularly known as Mamayev Kurgan in Russia.

**Singing Walls**

The Singing Walls are found in Mamayev Kurgan. This is the first place you should visit before you go to the next station, which is the Hall of Glory. In this area, you will hear the agony of the people who were in pain during the war, the different voices during the battle, the music of those years, deafening sound of explosions, and news summary from the radio information center.

**Hall of the Glory**

The Hall of the Glory is located at the top of the Mamaev Hill. In this area, you will see thousands of names of the dead, and these names are written on 32 banners and eternal flame. There are honor guards and funeral music played for the visitors to feel the ambience of mourning and respect. All the names of Soviet Union nationalities are found here. They have struggled a lot during the World War II and are buried together in one grave. The visitors are asked to be silent for a minute to show their reverence to the heroes of the battle. The words "We have fulfilled our patriotic duty..." can be found at one corner of the walls in the Hall of the Glory.
