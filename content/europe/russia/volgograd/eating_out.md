---
title: "Eating Out in Volgograd"
type: section
---

Russia, with its long, cold winters, is known for vodka and rich, hearty food
to keep warm. Volgograd is no exception to this
stereotype. All over the 80 kilometer long city along the Volga River
are cafes and restaurants featuring hundreds of types of vodka and
carbohydrate-rich foods. The main food staples in the area are breads,
meats, butter, and potatoes. Eggs and cabbage are also commonly used in
the dishes of Volgograd. In general, the traditional Russian food in
this city is very cheap, meaning most meals cost under $10 U.S., and many
are even under $5, including drinks. The foreign restaurants, such
as those serving American food, Japanese food, and English food, are
those that have extremely high prices, some going over $30 U.S. for one
meal. It is best to avoid those restaurants, as they are overpriced and
provide the same food that you can get at home. You might as well enjoy
the more traditional food, that you cannot get just anywhere, for a
much cheaper price.

**Desserts**

When going out to eat in Volgograd, you cannot miss the interesting
traditional desserts of Russia. Apple pastries are common, as are
pancakes with honey, sour cream, or marmalade. A truly exotic dessert is
Tvorog, which is cottage cheese with honey or marmalade made up of a
mix of berries. Paskha with hard boiled egg yolks is another unique
dessert you can find in Volgograd.

**Drinks**

Obviously, while in Volgograd, you must try the vodka. With only
$2.50 U.S. you can buy a vodka guaranteed to be delicious. There are so
many varieties though, that it is often difficult to pick just one. The
wine is not recommended, but the beer definitely is. Russian beer is
cheap and of a good quality. There are also some interesting non-
alcoholic drinks like kvas, which is a very sweet drink that has a bread
base.

**Restaurants**

There plenty of Russian cafes on every corner that charge inexpensive prices for foods
tourists will enjoy. There are also a few cheap, foreign restaurants with
distinctive food that you cannot get just anywhere. Cafe in the Alley,
for example, is a Georgian restaurant with great prices. Bochka,
meaning barrel, offers German and Austrian food for cheap prices, as
well, and there is even live music every Friday. Druzhba is one of the
only expensive Russian/international restaurants in the city, but it
features delicacies like zebra. One meal there can cost
around $40 U.S.
