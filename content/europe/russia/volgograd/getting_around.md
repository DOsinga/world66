---
title: "Getting Around in Volgograd"
type: section
---

The **Volgograd transportation** network offers excellent transportation options. Public transport is cheap, fast and reliable and is decent enough to be used by tourists. All public transport vehicles operate from 6 a.m. to almost 1 a.m., and tickets cost roughly $0.20 for a single ride. Here are some details on the various options:**
**

**Public Transportation
**

Trams are maybe the most used form of public transport in Volgograd. The city has an extensive tram network with a total length of about 215km. A part of the network runs underground and resembles a metro network, although the vehicles are trams and not metro trains. The trams aren&rsquo;t new, but are very well maintained and in good technical condition. Trolleybuses are another form of transport in Volgograd. These are electric buses that draw their power from overhead wires. The city has in fact its own trolley bus factory called Transmash. Regular buses are also widely available.

**Minibus Taxis
**

The minibus taxis are also called Marshrutki by the locals. Their work time and ticket prices are similar to the ones of the public transport. These taxis have predefined routes and work on a shared service basis. They have no stops, so you have to wave by hand in order to stop them. As you jump on a minibus taxi, you buy a ticket from the driver and ride along with other passengers. When you decide to get off the minibus, you just have to go to the driver and tell him to drop you.
