**Things You Need to Know Before Going to Volgograd**

These few **Volgograd practical information** tips will come in handy if you are planning a business or leisure trip to this city in Russia. Volgograd is an industrial city located in Russia. The city became famous after being extensively damaged during the Second World War. Here are a few tips that you can use when planning a trip here.

**Visa and Passport**

You will need to have a valid passport to be able to travel to Volgograd in Russia. You will also need to apply for a visa to enter Russia and then proceed onto Volgograd. A visa can be obtained by contacting the Russian Consulate in your own country. You will have to state the purpose of your visit to Volgograd, whether it is for business or leisure. You will need to provide information of where you will be staying while you are in Volgograd when you apply for a visa. A normal tourist visa for 30 days will cost you $45 and will be processed on the same day you fill in the application and pay the visa fees.

**Climate**

The weather in Volgograd is both hot and cold. The summer months – May to September can see temperatures touching 39 degrees Celsius/102 F at times; however it is normally in the range of 22 degrees Celsius to 27 degrees Celsius/70 to 80 degrees F. Winter is cold and the temperature can drop down to as low as -32 degrees Celsius/-25 F. However; you can expect a normal winter temperature of around 4 degrees Celsius to -4 degrees Celsius/25 to 39 F. It would be wise to carry sufficient warm clothes if you travel in winter.
