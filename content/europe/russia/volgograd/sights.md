---
title: "Sights in Volgograd"
type: section
---

**Volgograd Sights** can be a sobering experience with focus on the events of World War II or they can simply be relaxing as you sit on a bench and watch the Volga river.

#### The Panorama Museum

The Panorama Museum holds over 4,000 exhibits in its halls. The center piece of these exhibits is the Panoramic painting of the 1946 battle in January when Russian troops defended the city against the German armies. This museum offers photos, documents, and art work from local painters and artisans. This is a museum dedicated to the history of war and the very large canvas painting captures the true horror of World War II and a country's determination to defend her borders.

#### Mamayev Kurgan

Mamayev Kurgan was an important battlefield during World War II. Now a monumental memorial, Mamayev Kurgan houses Mother Russia at the crest of the hill. There is a circular memorial building that holds the names of over 6,000 soldiers, in mosaics on the inner wall. Enjoy the changing of the guard by the hour. You can take the tram to Mamayev Kurgan and hop of the tram as soon as you see the stairs leading to the statue of Mother Russia. If you can get to the memorial in the morning to watch the sunrise, you can enjoy a moment of solitude before the daily crowds begin to pour into the sight.

#### The Volga Embankment

The Volga Embankment is definitely a beautiful part of the city. There are lovely gardens to wander through and lively bars and restaurants that offer plenty of food and spirits. Live performances are held outside in the summer time. The cruise ships dock at the embankment in Volgograd and that is always an exciting time, watching the people disembark and then going back on board at the end of the day.

#### The Volgograd Hotel

The Volgograd Hotel is not only a lovely place to stay while you are in Volgograd but it is a monument to the romantic era of palaces and castles. It is one of the few remaining buildings in Volgograd left and the architecture is classic of the 19th century. It is a sight worth viewing and maybe stepping into for a bite to eat or a cocktail at the bar.

#### Lysays Gora

Lysays Gora is a mountain bare of Flora. It is also another Stalingrad monument. This hill was a strategic vantage point next to Mameay Hill. This hill still holds memories of the mines and shell shrapnel that bombarded the mountain in 1946. In memory of the fallen on this particular hill, stands an obelisk 20 meters tall.

#### Watchtower

Watchtower is located at the central fire house in the center of the city. The tower was built when the city's name was Tsaritsyn, still standing today, it is symbolic to the people of Volgograd. The tower has been restored to its former glory using the same Tsarisin masonry work.
