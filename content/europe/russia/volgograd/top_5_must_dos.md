---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Volgograd"
type: section
---

One of **Volgograd must do's** is a tour through World War II and the devastation of an entire city. The city was completely destroyed in the war and very little of the original buildings are scattered throughout the city itself. Volgograd inhabitants don't encounter many foreigners but when they do they will greet you with a warm and hearty welcome and lots of vodka. If you really want to get to know the city, it is a good idea to have a local guide you through all the great places and some of the hidden away places.

#### 1. Visit Mamyev Hill

Mamyev Hill displays statues and memorials and at the very top of the hill is the Mother Russia statue. She stands proudly holding a sword in one hand, while looking over her shoulder and the other hand reaching for her country. On your way to the top you will pause beside other sculptures and memorials, and you will hear the sounds of the soldiers and the battles take place through loud speakers. You can view the memorial of the Eternal Flame here as well. As you pass the memorial pond, there is an entrance to an underground display. In the center of the room is the torch of the Eternal Flame held in the grip of a massive hand protruding from the ground. There are no sounds of gun fire and battles; instead it is replaced with soothing music that will help to relax you as you stroll around the room looking at the many names of the Soviet soldiers who died in battle. It is an eye opening experience.

#### 2. Check Pavlov's House

Pavlov's House is symbol of a city's determination to hold a city no matter what. Pavlov was a Sergeant at the time of the historic battle; he was forced to take command when his commanding officer was blinded earlier in the fight. There were a few civilians in the basement of the house and they remained with Pavlov and his unit through the duration of the battle, with one civilian participating in the fighting. Holes were dug and walls were smashed as they prepared to defend themselves from the Germans. The apartment building was destroyed and a new one built in its place. On the East side of the building is a memorial made from of the original building and the inscription speaks to the determination and love of a people for their city.

#### 3. Spend the Day at State Panoramic Museum

The State Panoramic Museum &ldquo;Stalingrad Battle&rdquo; is the war museum of all time. Outside are exhibits of airplanes and tanks, inside you will see the large painting that incorporates the talents of seven different artists working on it at the same time. The Panorama depicts the Defeat of the fascist armies in Stalingrad. The epic and bloody battle of January 1943 is a somber reminder of the ravages of war. The museum boasts of other war related exhibits with over 4000 artifacts, photographs, documents, and great military commanders.
