The Volgograd** Travel Guide** will help you predict and plan your trip to this foreign land in Russia. Read on to find out about this prominent tourist destination.

**Getting There**

The closest airport is located at a distance of 15 kilometers from Volgograd. Visitors generally fly into Russia and then proceed to Volgograd. There are several airlines that fly from Russia to Volgograd Airport. Aeroflot will give you competitive fares. Other airlines like Air Volga, Donavia, Rossiya and Avianova connect Volgograd to cities like Moscow, St. Petersburg, Surgut and beyond. You can also travel by train from Moscow, Ukraine and Siberia, as Volgograd is one of the prominent rail junctions.

**Accommodation**

Volgograd has accommodations that will fit any budget. If you book in advance, the hotels generally extend discount fares. For high end hotels you can choose from the Intourist, which is located five minutes from the Volga River. You can also try out the Yuzhnaya, which is run by the Best Western group and offers a comfortable stay. The Tourist is a budget hotel located in downtown Volgograd.

**Sightseeing**

Volgograd is a historically significant city in Russia. Today it is considered to be an industrial city, but the bloodiest of wars have been fought on this land. Tourists come to Volgograd to witness the sites of historical value. The Mamayev Kuragan was where the Battle of Stalingrad was fought in the 20th century. It is now a memorial ground located on a hill. You will get an eagle's eye view of the city and the Volga River from this memorial. Another must-see attraction is the Museum of Panorama. It is located on the banks of the Volga River and showcases artifacts from World War 2. You can scan through various paintings of the battlefields.

**Dining**

There are several places to dine in Volgograd. The city caters to tourists and hence has restaurants that serve international cuisines. The menu in most restaurants will be in both Russian and English. Experiment with the local cuisine in restaurants like the Labamba, Sakur and Vizantia. If you wish to stick to the known then you can choose between McDonald's, Grand Pizza or the Steak House.

**Traveling in Volgograd**

It is easy to travel in Volgograd. The easiest but comparatively expensive means would be to call for a taxi. The taxis arrive on time and are dependable. You can also use the public transport system. The trolley buses have been around for centuries now and are a cost effective way to travel within the city. Mini bus is another means of transport and is faster than the trolley bus. The information on the bus will be in Russian, so make it a point to ask the driver before you board.
