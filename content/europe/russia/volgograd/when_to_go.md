**Travel to the Volga River Bank **

When you are planning a trip to **Volgograd**, **when to go **can be a question you want answered. This city shot to fame due to its resistance of the Nazi forces during WWII. Today, Volgograd has been reconstructed, and the ruins from the war has given way to a new and highly developed city. It rests on the western banks of the famous Volga River and is at the southern part of Russia. Known for its tall statues, bridges, riverfront and beautiful women, Volgograd is any traveler's dream come true. Read on to find out what the best time to visit this Industrial city is.

**Climate**

Russia as a whole is known to experience the harshest of winters. Even though Volgograd looks spectacular during the winter season, with the white blankets of snow covering its bridges and domes, it would be advisable not to travel during December to March. This city is at its best during summer, when the locals come out into the street to enjoy the sun and the cool breeze.

**Activities**

Another aspect to consider while deciding on the time to travel is the activity you wish to embark on once in Volgograd. Unlike other cities in Russia, Volgograd does not have a lot of winter activities. Hence it would be advisable to travel during the months of June to September. You can go on the various river cruises and drive on the longest and the most famous street in the whole of Mother Russia. Also, the farmers' market comes to life only in summer.
