Serbia is a country where East meets West in the most literal sense. Shaped by Romans, Byzantines, Ottomans, and Austro-Hungarians, it has absorbed influences from all of them and turned them into something distinctly its own. The food blends Central European and Turkish traditions. The architecture ranges from Habsburg grandeur in the north to Ottoman mosques in the south. The people are famously hospitable, passionately opinionated, and always ready to pour you another glass of rakija.

[Belgrade](/europe/serbia/belgrade) is one of Europe's most underrated capitals -- a gritty, energetic city at the confluence of the Danube and Sava rivers, with a fortress that has been fought over by 115 armies and a nightlife scene that rivals Berlin. Novi Sad, Serbia's second city, hosts the legendary EXIT music festival in its clifftop Petrovaradin Fortress. In the south, Nis was the birthplace of Constantine the Great and retains a strong Ottoman character.

Beyond the cities, Serbia rewards exploration. [Zlatibor](/europe/serbia/zlatibor) and Tara are mountain regions with hiking, traditional villages, and the remarkable Sargan Eight narrow-gauge railway. The Danube cuts through the Djerdap Gorge (the Iron Gates), one of Europe's most dramatic river landscapes. Medieval monasteries, many of them UNESCO World Heritage Sites, are scattered across the southern hills -- Studenica, Sopocani, and Decani among the finest.

Serbia is affordable, safe, and easy to navigate. The food is outstanding, the wine is improving fast, and the welcome is genuine. It is one of those places that travelers discover and immediately want to return to.
