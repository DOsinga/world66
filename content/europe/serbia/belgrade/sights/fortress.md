The fortress is the most remarkable monument of history preserved in the city. This
is where the remains of Belgrade's distant past can best be seen: the
site where Belgrade was founded as a small fortified settlement in the
1st century AD, and on which it developed right down to the second half
of the 18th century. Along with artillery structures of the mid-18th
century, there are medieval ramparts, towers, gates and barbicans (15th
century), and some Byzantine and Roman remains. This historical site is
surrounded by a city park, bearing the old Turkish name of this place -
Kalemegdan.
