The Parliament is one of the most attractive palace-style buildings in Belgrade. It
was constructed in the period 1906-36 after the projects of the
architect Jovan Ilkic. Construction works were completed under the
supervision of his son, Pavle Ilkic. The palace is an example of
various architectural styles applied: Neo-Renaissance, Baroque,
Classicism. Great attention was also dedicated to the internal
decorations. The front entrance of the building is decorated by two
monumental sculpture groups of the black horses, authored by Toma
Rosandic.
