title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

Belgrade has the distinction of being one of Europe’s oldest cities dating back to 6 th
century B.C. and is today is meshwork of stunning architectural and
natural beauty. The Belgrade Must Do's guide offers visitors helpful
information on the attractions and activities in this buzzing Serbian
Capital.

**1. Cathedral of St. Sava**

This is largest Orthodox church in the world and is built in the
memory of St. Sava, pioneer of the Serbian Orthodox Church and an
eminent personality to shape Serbia’s medieval historical identity. The
Cathedral of St.Sava towers over Belgrade’s skyline and offers a
spectacular view of the city at night. The church has been built on a
location which is rumored to be the site that houses the remains of the
illustrious saint. Other religious monuments worth visiting are St.
Michael’s Cathedral and St. Mark’s Church along with the Fenek and
Zemun Monastery

**2. ****House of Flowers**

The house of flowers is replete with a checkered history that has
seen it opening and closing its doors for tourists several times during
the last few years. Yugoslavian leader Josip Broz Tito’s tomb is
ceremoniously housed within the confines of this site. There are myriad
flowers and pebbles surrounding the tomb and a small, old-fashioned
fountain preens over it. The House of Flowers also nestles an
extensively stocked museum that exhibits gifts that were given to Tito
by foreign dignitaries.

** 3**. **Ancient Revelry**

The National Museum is one of the best stocked museums in the world
and boasts of an enviable and prolific collection of more than 40000
exhibits of art that range from original Italian artworks and to a
medieval French collection and from classic pieces of Dutch
artisanship to exquisite illustration of Sebian art. There is a
Japanese art center as well that houses over 82 paintings by famed
artists like Toyokuni and Hirosige. Watch the masterpieces of Vincent
van Gogh and art will never mean the same again.

**4. Lake side fun**

Lake Ada Safari is the most attractive tourist spot in Belgrade and
exudes a tranquil charm that engulfs visitors within minutes. The lake
is nestled amidst a lush centennial forest and stocks some really
prized fish. This spot it great for a family day out fishing.

**5. Belgrade Arena**

An iconic and imposing trademark of contemporary Belgrade, the
world-class Belgrade Arena boasts of the best technologically
sophisticated displaying facility in the continent. It is actually a
multi-functional arena for sporting and cultural events and is divided into six massive floors that can accommodate a whopping
20,000 viewers. Regular musical concerts, cultural events and trade
events are held here every few days.

Belgrade has stood true to Sebia’s rich cultural identity while
carving its own technoligically advanced personality. This is one city
which has transformed without rebelling or overthrowing its native charm.
