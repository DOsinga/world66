---
title: "Sights in Kaqanik"
type: section
---

One of the most beautiful and beleaguered parts of all of Serbia is the Kosovo Province; among the various parts of Kosovo that have recently become of interest to tourists is **Kaqanik. Sights** in this part of Serbia are varied. While Kaqanik is certainly not the best place for many different people to travel, given that it has recently been embroiled in ethnic conflict and warfare, hearty and adventurous travelers who keep their wits about them and avoid dangerous situations find that this part of central Europe is an engaging cultural site.

**Farmer's Market**

Downtown Kaqanik has a population of somewhere around 40,000 permanent residents. Nearly all of these people are ethnically related and are Kosovo Albanian. Sustained conflicts between different ethnic groups have resulted in strife throughout the area, but they have not stopped a weekly farmer's market that takes place downtown. This market is a hub of activity for the area, with farmers, townspeople and others coming from miles around to purchase livestock, kitchen utensils, housewares, food supplies and more. There are souvenirs and other items to be found in this part of Kaqanik.

**Turkish Fort**

The area of Kaqanik has been inhabited consistently for several centuries, and at one point was dominated by the Turks. During this period, the residents built a central fort in the middle of the municipality as a means of protecting themselves from invading armies. Remains of the fort are still visible throughout town today, and provide a unique glimpse into a life from another time period.

**Outer Hills**

Hills surround several sides of Kaqanik proper. This countryside is unique and has a distinctly different feel from western Europe or anywhere in the Americas. For this reason, many travelers enjoy spending time out above the city in the hills, either exploring or looking down on the main portion of Kaqanik below them. Be very careful when leaving the major city area, as there is still unexploded ordnance (mines and other explosive devices) planted off of the roads. Generally, if you stay on paved and main roads, you'll have nothing to worry about.

The central portion of Kaqanik also has bars, restaurants and accommodations, although they are not many in number. Plan ahead to get the best deals on these activities and to capitalize on your time in Kaqanik and Serbia in general.
