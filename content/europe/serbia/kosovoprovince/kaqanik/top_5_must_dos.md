title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Kaqanik"
type: section
---

When traveling in central and eastern Europe, most visitors wisely avoid approaching the Kosovo province in Serbia; however, in recent years, adventure and "high-risk" tourism has boomed in this war-torn part of the world, and **Kaqanik must do's** have helped to contribute to that. Kaqanik is a small aggregation of villages in the southern portion of the Kosovo area. It has a total population of about 40,000 Kosovan Albanians. This area is politically, economically and socially decrepit, but it provides one of the starkest and most dramatic cultural experiences that you can have. If you're interested in exploring Kaqanik, read on for a list of some of the top sights.

**1. Turkish Fort**

The Turks held control over Kaqanik for several decades in the 19th century. During this time, they built a substantial fort in the center of town to help protect against raiders and other invading forces. Portions of the fort still stand today, and serve as one of the only memories of the time when Kaqanik was run by Turkish peoples.

**2. Outer Hills**

One of the more popular destinations around Kaqanik is the set of hills that exist just outside of the town. These provide excellent views of the Kaqanik center and also of the surrounding countryside. Exercise extreme caution when leaving the paved roads of the city, as the area is riddled with unexploded land mines and other dangers.

**3. Farmer's Market**

A weekly farmer's market sees people from all around the area gather in downtown Kaqanik to exchange housewares, food, supplies and other goods. It's an excellent way to interact with locals.

**4. Central Bars and Restaurants**

A number of restaurants have sprung up in the small main part of the Kaqanik downtown. Many of these are more western in style and will likely be part of increasing the tourist draw to Kaqanik in the coming years.

**5. Railway**

Kaqanik is an important stop on the railway system that connects Polje and Thesaloniki. This makes Kaqanik an easy stopping point on a journey between central Europe and Greece, and it also means that these other areas are easily accessible from Kaqanik as well.

Ethnic and other types of strife are still rampant in the Kosovo area, and it's crucial that any travelers exercise extreme caution when traveling out of doors. Do not advertise your presence as a tourist and keep your activity low in order to avoid detection and alarm.
