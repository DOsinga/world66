---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Zlatibor works in every season, which is part of why Serbs keep returning. Snow arrives around mid-November and stays until March, and the plateau is good cross-country skiing terrain — the open meadows allow almost unrestricted movement once there is enough cover. The resort has limited downhill infrastructure but the landscape suits Nordic skiing and snowshoeing well.

Spring, summer, and early autumn are warm and mostly sunny. The meadows fill with wildflowers from April onward, and the mild altitude (1,000–1,400 m) keeps summer temperatures comfortable even during the hottest part of the Serbian year. Late spring and early autumn are the quietest periods; July and August bring crowds of Serbian holidaymakers.

Wildlife is present year-round, but spring and autumn are best for birdwatching — Griffon Vultures, Golden Eagles, and European Eagle Owls are regulars.
