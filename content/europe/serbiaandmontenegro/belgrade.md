Belgrade is back on the tourist track! After the war and the Milosevic years that followed it, Yugoslavia is opening up to foreign travelers and Belgrade is right on the main road crossing the Balkan. It will take a while before the city will be on the "See Europe in 30 days" tours, but for tourists who boldly want to travel, this is a prime destination.  

 

 

The **Knez Mihailova **is the major axis of downtown Belgrade. It is the best looking street in Belgrade with many 19-th century buildings and a lot of street vendors, money changers, Bulgarian CD sellers, clothes and many snack stands.  

**Kalemegdan park **and its fortress, which overlooks most of the city is the closest thing to a real "tourist attraction". From the fortress you can see how well Belgrade is positioned at the crossroads of the rivers Danube and Sava.  

**Skadarlija** is the place to head for when dawn falls. The cobble-stone street is filled with restaurants featuring live music and couple of interesting nightspots. These rae the best places to meet U.N. workers on leave, diplomats and the rare Western businessman.The famous restaurants are "Sesir moj", "Tri sesira"...
