Budva is one of the most popular resort towns on the Montenegrin coast, with a mix of good beaches, a well-preserved old town, and a lively summer atmosphere. The old city core is around 2,500 years old and survived a serious earthquake in 1979 largely intact. Its narrow stone lanes, small churches, and fortified walls make for pleasant wandering.

The town is best known as "the theatre city" — the old citadel stage hosts an annual summer theatre festival that was once the largest open-air festival in the former Yugoslavia. Just down the coast lies Sveti Stefan, a photogenic fortified island village connected to the mainland by a narrow causeway, now an exclusive resort but striking to see from the beach nearby.

The beaches around Budva — Mogren, Jaz, and Bečići among them — are the main draw for most visitors. The waterfront promenade (*Slovenska obala*) is lined with restaurants and cafes and gets busy on summer evenings.
