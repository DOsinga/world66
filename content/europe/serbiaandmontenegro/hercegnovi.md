Herceg Novi sits at the entrance to the Bay of Kotor, close to the border with Bosnia-Herzegovina and [Croatia](/europe/croatia), about 70 km by road west of [Podgorica](/europe/serbiaandmontenegro/podgorica). Its strategic position at the mouth of the bay gave the city its historical importance, and the main sights reflect this — city gates, fortresses, and defensive walls built by a succession of rulers from the Bosnians to the Ottomans to the Venetians.

The old town climbs steeply from the waterfront, a tangle of narrow steps and squares shaded by Mediterranean vegetation. The Savina Monastery, set in gardens above the town, is worth a visit for its collection of icons and its views over the bay. The seafront promenade runs for several kilometres and is pleasant for an evening walk.

A visit to Herceg Novi combines well with [Perast](/europe/serbiaandmontenegro/perast) and [Kotor](/europe/serbiaandmontenegro/kotor), both further into the bay.
