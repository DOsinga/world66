Recently Kosovo has seen a lot of turmoil. NATO bombings made an end to Serbian rule and now some sort of a local government has been installed. The fighting has badly damaged all of Kosovo. 

The situation is especially complex because Kosovo is a holy land to the Serbs: It’s the ancient birthplace of Serbia and rich in national history and tradition. (After the Battle of Kosovo in 1389 Serbia became a vassal state under Ottoman Turkish rule. That lasted almost 500 years until Russia helped oust the Turks.) The Turkish influence lingers. 

The main towns of this troubled province are [Pristina](/europe/serbiaandmontenegro/kosovoprovince/pristina) and [Prizren](/europe/serbiaandmontenegro/kosovoprovince/prizren), a city of many mosques and the burial place of Serbian king Stephen Dushan. 

Travel in Kosovo is far from easy. But if things clear up a bit more it can be a rewarding experience.
