Gjilan (Serbian: Gnjilane) is the main city of eastern Kosovo, sitting in the Morava e Binçës valley close to the North Macedonian border. It is the third-largest city in Kosovo and the administrative centre of the Gjilan region.

The city centre has a pedestrian zone with cafés and shops, and a covered bazaar. The area around Gjilan has several Orthodox churches and monasteries, including the Monastery of the Holy Archangels in the nearby village of Binç. The surrounding countryside is agricultural and relatively quiet compared to Pristina.

Gjilan was under US military authority (KFOR) after 1999 and has a mixed Albanian and Serb population, though the balance shifted significantly after the conflict.
