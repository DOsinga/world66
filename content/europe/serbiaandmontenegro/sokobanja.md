Sokobanja is a well-established spa town in eastern Serbia, set in a wooded valley at around 400 metres elevation. The town has been drawing visitors for its thermal mineral springs since the 19th century, and today it remains one of Serbia's most popular domestic resort destinations, particularly for Serbs seeking a cure or simply a break from the city.

The surrounding landscape is pleasant — forested hills, the Moravica river threading through the valley, and the ruins of Sokograd fortress perched on a crag above town. The fortress, medieval in origin, is the main historical sight and worth the short climb for the views over the valley.

The thermal baths are the main draw. Several spa centres and sanatoriums offer treatments using the local sulphur-rich waters, ranging from clinical rehabilitation to straightforward relaxation. The town is compact and walkable, with a modest pedestrian centre, a few cafes, and the unhurried pace of a place that takes recovery seriously.

Sokobanja works well as a quiet base for a few days — it is not a destination for nightlife or sightseeing, but for those who want rest, clean air, and thermal baths in a low-key Serbian setting, it delivers reliably.
