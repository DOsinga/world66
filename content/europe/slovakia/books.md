**A History of Slovakia: The Struggle for Survival** by Stanislav Kirschbaum -- The most comprehensive English-language history of Slovakia, covering the long arc from the Great Moravian Empire to independence in 1993. It explains why Slovakia is not simply "the other half of Czechoslovakia."

**The Czech and Slovak Republics** (Lonely Planet or Rough Guide) -- The most practical English-language travel guides available. Both cover Slovakia thoroughly, with good sections on the Tatras, Bratislava, and eastern Slovakia.

**Katarina** by Kathryn Winter -- A memoir set in wartime Slovakia, based on the author's childhood. It offers an intimate portrait of Slovak life during one of its most turbulent periods.

**Rivers of Babylon** by Peter Pist'anek -- A darkly comic novel about the chaos of post-communist Bratislava, following a dodgy parking attendant who builds a criminal empire. Not available in every language, but if you can find it in English, it captures the wild energy of the 1990s transition.
