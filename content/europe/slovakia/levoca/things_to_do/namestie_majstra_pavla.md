The main square of the old town, named after the master woodcarver whose altar dominates St. James Church. It's surrounded by Gothic and Renaissance townhouses built by the merchants who made Levoča wealthy on the Via Magna trade route. The freestanding Town Hall in the centre dates from the 15th century. It's one of the most coherent medieval squares in Slovakia — largely because the town never grew wealthy enough after the 17th century to tear it down and rebuild.
