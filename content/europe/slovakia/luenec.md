Lučenec is a small town of about 35,000 people in the south of central Slovakia, roughly 250 km from Bratislava and 150 km from Košice. The town sits in the Novohrad region, with a history dating back to around 1200 AD and a strong Hungarian-speaking minority — the Hungarian border is only about 30 km away.

It is an inexpensive and friendly base for exploring the region's mix of Slovak and Hungarian culture, historic architecture, and the volcanic hills of the surrounding countryside.
