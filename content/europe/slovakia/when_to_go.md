---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Slovakia has a continental climate with warm summers and cold, snowy winters. The best time depends on what you want to do.

**Summer (June-August)** is ideal for hiking in the High Tatras and exploring the castles and towns. Temperatures reach 25-30C in the lowlands, cooler in the mountains. This is peak season for the Tatras, so book mountain huts in advance. The folk festival season is in full swing, with events across the country.

**Autumn (September-October)** brings beautiful foliage, wine harvest festivals, and pleasant hiking weather. The crowds thin out and the mountains turn golden. September is one of the best months to visit.

**Winter (December-March)** is ski season. The Tatras, Jasna in the Low Tatras, and other resorts offer affordable skiing compared to the Alps. Christmas markets in Bratislava and Kosice are atmospheric. Temperatures regularly drop below freezing, and mountain areas see heavy snow.

**Spring (April-May)** is unpredictable but rewarding. Wildflowers bloom in the meadows, and the mountain trails open up as the snow melts. Easter traditions in eastern Slovakia, with decorated eggs and folk customs, are worth seeking out.

For hiking, aim for June through September. For skiing, January and February. For the best balance of weather, crowds, and prices, try late May or September.
