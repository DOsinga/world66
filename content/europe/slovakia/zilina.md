Žilina is the main city of northwestern Slovakia, set in a valley where the Váh and Kysuca rivers meet, with the Beskydy mountains visible on the eastern horizon. With around 80,000 people, it's Slovakia's third-largest city — a regional industrial and university centre that sees relatively few foreign visitors.

The historic centre is anchored by **Mariánske námestie** (Marian Square), a well-preserved arcaded Renaissance square that dates mainly from the 16th century. The surrounding old town is compact and walkable. The nearby **Budatín Castle**, at the confluence of the rivers, is the city's most distinctive landmark and houses a regional museum.

Žilina is a practical base for the surrounding region: the Malá Fatra national park is 15 kilometres east, with good hiking and skiing. The castles of Strečno and Starhrad, perched dramatically above the Váh gorge, are worth a half-day trip.
