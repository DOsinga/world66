Celje is Slovenia's third-largest city, set on the Savinja River in the Savinja Basin about an hour's drive northeast of [Ljubljana](/europe/slovenia/ljubljana). The Romans knew it as Celeia, and the city's history stretches back further still — but its greatest period came in the 14th and 15th centuries under the Counts of Celje, the most powerful feudal dynasty in the Slovenian lands.

The Old Castle, perched on a hill above the city, is the most visible legacy of that era and one of the largest medieval fortifications in Slovenia. The views from the top sweep across the city and the surrounding hills. In the centre, the 16th-century Celje Hall and the reconstructed Counts' Palace house the regional museum, which includes a remarkable Roman-era Celje Ceiling — painted plaster panels discovered during renovations.

Celje is a working city rather than a tourist showpiece, which gives it an honest, unhurried feel. The pedestrianised old town has a handful of good cafés and restaurants, and the Savinja riverbank is pleasant for an evening stroll. The city makes a reasonable base for exploring the Savinja Valley and the nearby Šempeter Roman Necropolis.
