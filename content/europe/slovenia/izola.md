Izola is a small fishing town on Slovenia's short Adriatic coast, wedged between [Koper](/europe/slovenia/koper) and [Piran](/europe/slovenia/piran). It lacks Piran's postcard-perfect Venetian architecture, but that is part of its charm — Izola is quieter, less polished, and more genuinely lived-in. The old town sits on what was once an island (the connecting causeway was filled in centuries ago), and the narrow streets and pastel-coloured houses still have an Italian feel.

The harbour is the heart of the town — fishing boats unload their catch here, and several restaurants along the waterfront serve fresh seafood at reasonable prices. The swimming is mostly off rocks and small concrete platforms, though Simonov Zaliv to the east has a stretch of beach. The Parenzana cycle trail, following the route of a former narrow-gauge railway, passes through Izola and runs along the coast toward Piran — a flat, scenic ride through olive groves and seaside villages.
