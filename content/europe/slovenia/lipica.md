Lipica is a small village in the Karst region of southwestern Slovenia, a few kilometers from the Italian border, best known as the birthplace of the Lipizzan horse — the white horses of the Spanish Riding School in Vienna. The Lipica Stud Farm, founded in 1580 by Archduke Charles II of Austria, is one of the oldest continuously operating stud farms in the world and remains the main reason to visit.

The farm covers several hundred hectares of karst plateau. Visitors can tour the stables, watch the horses in the paddocks, and attend classical dressage performances where riders in formal attire put the horses through their paces. The performances are choreographed and elegant, a tradition stretching back four centuries. Foals are born grey or dark and whiten gradually over several years — the classic white coat does not fully appear until the horse is between six and ten years old.

Lipica also has a small hotel, a golf course, and tennis facilities, making it a quiet countryside retreat in addition to an equestrian attraction. The surrounding landscape is typical Karst: rocky, dotted with scrubby oak and pine, and underlain by the caves and sinkholes that give the region its name.

## Getting There

Lipica is about 75 km from Ljubljana and 25 km from [Koper](/europe/slovenia/koper). By car, take the motorway southwest toward Koper and exit at Divaca, then follow signs to Lipica. There is limited public transport; a rental car or taxi from Divaca is the most practical option.
