Maribor is Slovenia's second city — overlooked by most visitors who rush between Ljubljana and the Slovenian Alps, which is their loss. A university town of around 100,000 on the Drava river in the northeastern corner of the country, it has a relaxed, lived-in quality that the more self-consciously picturesque capital sometimes lacks.

The old town stretches along the left bank of the Drava, with **Lent** — the riverside promenade — as its social heart. This stretch comes alive every summer for **Festival Lent**, a sprawling music and cultural festival that draws performers and visitors from across Central Europe. The main square, Glavni trg, is anchored by a 16th-century plague column.

Maribor sits in the middle of Styrian wine country, and the surrounding hills are thick with vineyards. The **Maribor Vineyards** along the south-facing slopes above the old town include the oldest productive grapevine in the world — over 400 years old, according to the Guinness World Records — which produces a small quantity of wine each year. Wine tourism is a serious part of the regional economy, and the Mariborske Pohorje hills immediately south of the city offer skiing in winter and hiking in summer.
