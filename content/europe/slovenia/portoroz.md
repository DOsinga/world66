Portoroz is Slovenia's main seaside resort, tucked into a sheltered bay on the country's short strip of Adriatic coastline in the Istrian peninsula. The name means "port of roses" in Slovenian — a fitting image for a town that trades on mild Mediterranean air, spa hotels, and a long sandy beach that draws visitors from across Central Europe every summer.

The town is compact and easy to navigate on foot. A broad promenade runs along the beachfront, lined with cafes, restaurants, and the occasional casino. The thermal spa tradition here dates back centuries — the salt pans and mineral-rich mud of the area have been used for health treatments since the Middle Ages — and today Portoroz is one of Slovenia's leading wellness destinations, with several large spa hotels offering thalassotherapy and salt treatments that won't cost nearly as much as equivalent resorts in Italy or Croatia.

Just a couple of kilometres away, the medieval walled town of [Piran](/europe/slovenia/piran) is the real jewel of this coastline. Its Venetian architecture, narrow lanes, and sea-facing piazza make for an easy and rewarding half-day excursion. A short drive south leads to the Secovlje saltpan nature park, where salt has been harvested using traditional methods for over seven hundred years and migratory birds stop in surprising numbers. Neither should be missed.

Portoroz sits at the affordable end of the Adriatic spectrum. Prices are noticeably lower than in Trieste or the Croatian resorts to the south, the summers are warm and reliably sunny, and the food is excellent — fresh seafood, Istrian olive oil, and local Malvazija white wine are the staples. It is a place that rewards a few days of unhurried exploration rather than a single rushed stop.
