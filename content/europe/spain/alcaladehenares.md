Alcalá de Henares is a university city 35km east of Madrid, declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1998. It is best known as the birthplace of Miguel de Cervantes, author of Don Quixote, and for its 15th-century university — one of the first planned Renaissance university cities in the world.

The historic centre is compact and walkable. The Calle Mayor, with its arcaded walkways, is lined with old buildings and leads to the University's beautiful Plateresque facade. The Cervantes birthplace museum on Calle Mayor is worth a visit. The city is easily reached from Madrid by Cercanías train in about 50 minutes.
