Alora is a small village on the rail line from Málaga to Bobadilla and Córdoba, and also on two of the three bus routes to [Ronda](/europe/spain/andalucia/ronda). Its position makes it a reasonable alternative to Málaga for a first or last night if you are traveling between the coast and the Andalucian interior.

The village is built on several terraces, connected by steep roads and tracks. The climb from the station to the old centre is not short, so if you arrive with luggage it is worth arranging a taxi number in advance — the local phone box has historically been unhelpful for this. The castle at the top rewards the effort, and the streets between the terraces are pleasant to wander.

There is not much to justify a long stay, but for one night it works well as a quiet, unhurried stop.
