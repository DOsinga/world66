A low-key resort town on the Atlantic coast of Andalucía, Chipiona draws Spanish families and older visitors who come for the beaches, the seafood, and the mineral waters. It has none of the gloss of more famous Costa resorts — which is precisely its appeal outside of August, when it fills up.

The two main beaches are Playa de Regla to the south and Playa de Las Canteras to the north. Regla tends to be the quieter of the two. The town's lighthouse — one of the tallest in Spain — is a landmark visible from far out to sea.

Chipiona is also known for its muscatel wine, a sweet local white made from Moscatel grapes grown in the surrounding vineyards. You'll find it in almost every bar in town.
