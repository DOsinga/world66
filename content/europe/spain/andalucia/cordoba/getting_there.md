---
title: "Getting There in Cordoba"
type: section
---

Getting to Cordoba will probably require some ground transportation because there is no airport in Cordoba. However, there are multiple ways by which you can reach your destination, including planes, trains and automobiles.

**Planes**

Since travelers cannot fly directly into Cordoba, the most financially reasonable option for international travel is to fly into either [Madrid](/europe/spain/madrid) or [Malaga](/europe/spain/andalucia/malaga). If cost is not a factor flights into either [Seville](/europe/spain/andalucia/seville) or [Granada](/europe/spain/andalucia/granada) can cut road travel time by half.

**Trains**

Cordoba has its own train station and visitors can connect to the train from any of the cities they fly into. From Madrid, trains depart to Cordoba every 30 to 60 minutes from 06:30 to 22:00. The trip takes approximately 2 hours. The train runs between Malaga and Cordoba at similar intervals from 06:30 to 21:00 and takes approximately 1 hour. From Seville, the trip takes about 40 minutes and as many as 4 trains run hourly between 06:15 and 23:00. Only 2 trains run daily from Granada--at 09:45 and at 18:00. The trip takes about 2 1/2 hours.

**Buses**

Taking a bus to Cordoba saves money. The bus from Madrid takes 4 1/2 hours and runs regularly for a cost of approximately 14 euros. It takes the same amount of time from Malaga, although the trip takes 3 1/2hours and costs about 13 euros. From Seville to Cordoba the trip is a little over 2 hours and costs about 10 euros. From Granada there are 6 buses a day at regular intervals from 07:30 to 18:30.

**Car**

Alternatively, you can rent a car in any town and drive to Cordoba. The trip is scenic but crosses mountains, so get something reliable. It's also worth noting that unless you book your car months in advance, you're unlikely to find one during late July or August, when all of the locals start traveling.
