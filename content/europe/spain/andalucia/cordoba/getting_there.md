---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Cordoba has no airport but it sits on Spain's main north–south high-speed rail line, which makes it one of the easiest cities in Andalusia to reach.

**Train.** The fastest option from anywhere. AVE high-speed services run from Madrid Atocha in around 1h 45m, from Seville in 45 minutes, and from Malaga in around an hour. Trains arrive at Cordoba's central station, a 15-minute walk from the old town. Book ahead via Renfe for the best fares.

**Plane.** The nearest airports are Seville (around 2 hours by train), Malaga (1 hour by AVE), and Madrid (1h 45m by AVE). All three are well connected to the rest of Europe.

**Bus.** ALSA runs long-distance coaches from Madrid (around 4h 30m, much cheaper than the train) and from cities across Andalusia. The bus station sits next to the train station.

**Car.** Cordoba is on the A-4 motorway between Madrid and Seville. Useful if you're touring Andalusia, but be warned: most of the old town is a low-emission zone with very restricted access for non-residents. Park outside the walls and walk in.
