Set in a 16th-century mansion in the Judería, the Bullfighting Museum traces the history of the corrida and Cordoba's four legendary native matadors: Lagartijo, Guerrita, Machaquito and Manolete. Posters, capes, suits of lights, and — for those with the stomach — the mounted head of the bull that killed Manolete in 1947. Whatever you think of bullfighting, the museum is genuinely informative and the building itself is worth the visit.
