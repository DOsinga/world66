---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**May** is the best month. Temperatures are pleasant (before the brutal summer heat), and the city hosts its greatest festivals back to back. **Cruces de Mayo** (first week) fills public squares in the old town with flower-bedecked crosses, music, and drinks. **Festival de los Patios** (about two weeks in May) is when residents of the historic districts open their private courtyards — decorated with flowers — to visitors, competing for the best patio. It is genuinely lovely and unlike anything else in Spain. **Feria de Córdoba** (last week of May) is a city-wide party of flamenco, dancing, and eating.

**Semana Santa** (week before Easter) brings nightly processions through the streets — six processions per night from 6pm to midnight.

**July** sees the **Festival Internacional de la Guitarra**, two weeks of concerts ranging across classical, jazz, blues, and flamenco — one of Spain's best music festivals.

**Summer (July–August)** is extremely hot — temperatures regularly exceed 40°C. Avoid if you can, or limit sightseeing to early morning. **Spring and autumn** are ideal.
