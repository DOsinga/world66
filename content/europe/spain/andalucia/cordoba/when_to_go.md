---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Cordoba has the most extreme climate of the major Andalusian cities. Summers are punishing — daytime temperatures regularly past 40°C in July and August, sometimes much higher. Winters are mild but can be surprisingly damp and cool in the evenings. The sweet spots are **April–May** and **late September–October**.

May is the month to aim for. The **Festival de los Patios** runs for around two weeks in early-to-mid May: residents of the old town open their private courtyards to the public and compete for the best display of geraniums, jasmine and fountains. It's a proper Cordoban institution and the city gets booked out months in advance.

The same month brings the **Cruces de Mayo** in the first week — neighbourhood squares are decked with huge flower-covered crosses and turn into impromptu street parties — and the **Feria de Córdoba** in the last week, with flamenco, sevillanas, fino, and a casetas-and-fairground site on the edge of town. Across town there's also the **Cata de Vino Montilla-Moriles**, a wine-tasting fair under a giant tent in the centre.

Other dates worth timing for: **Semana Santa** (Holy Week, March or April), with nightly processions through the old town; the **Festival Internacional de la Guitarra** in early July, two weeks of concerts spanning flamenco, classical, jazz and blues; and **Carnaval** in February, a smaller affair than Cadiz's but still good fun.
