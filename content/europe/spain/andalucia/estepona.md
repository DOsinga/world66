Estepona sits on the western end of the Costa del Sol, and it has managed to hold onto more of its Andalusian identity than most of its neighbours. The old town, with its whitewashed walls and flower-draped balconies, is genuinely charming, and the town has invested heavily in public art and street murals that make it worth wandering on foot.

The beach runs for several kilometres and is relatively uncrowded by Costa del Sol standards. The marina area has restaurants and a fish market. Golf is the other main draw — the surrounding hills hold dozens of courses, and the sport is woven into the local economy.

Estepona makes a useful base for exploring the western Costa del Sol, with Marbella about 20 minutes east and Gibraltar reachable in under an hour.
