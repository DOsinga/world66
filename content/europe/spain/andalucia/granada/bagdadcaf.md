A laid-back tearoom on Calle Elvira, the busy street connecting Plaza Nueva with the Albaicín quarter. The décor leans Moroccan-bohemian — colourful cushions, low tables, tea lamps — and the menu runs to Arabic-style teas, fresh juices, and light snacks. It draws a mixed crowd of students, backpackers, and locals, and stays open late. A decent spot to rest your feet after climbing the Albaicín's hills.
