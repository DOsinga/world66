The summer retreat of the Nasrid rulers sits just above the Alhambra on the slopes of the Cerro del Sol. Built in the 13th century and expanded in the 14th, it's less a grand palace than a series of gardens and pavilions connected by water channels and fountains. The Patio de la Acequia — a long reflecting pool flanked by jets of water — is the centrepiece, and in a region where water was precious, the abundance of it here was an unmistakable display of wealth. The Patio de la Sultana, with its ancient cypresses, is quieter and easier to linger in. Included in the Alhambra ticket; book the whole complex well in advance.
