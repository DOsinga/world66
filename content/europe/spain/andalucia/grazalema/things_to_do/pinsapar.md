This is a wonderful walk and the return can well be done by road, giving terrific views at the end of the daylight hours. You may like to try out the bar in Benamahoma before you start back.

As you go up the main road above the village one part goes to Zahara and the other to Benamahoma. Take the **Zahara** road a short distance and then the path steeply up the mountain on your left. This leads up to the ridge from which it would be easy to gain the summit - but your route goes straight over. In fact you will not get lost between here and Benamahoma, provided that you follow the somewhat surprising arrow at the one point where you could easily go wrong otherwise.

A major feature of this walk is the Spanish fir which many centuries ago covered much of the Med area but is now restricted to two parts of Spain - this [the Pisapar] being the more important. As you will see from the excellent website below, there are restrictions on the number of people allowed in so, if you can't avoid the main tourist period, go to the Park Pffice in Grazalema and book a few days before.
