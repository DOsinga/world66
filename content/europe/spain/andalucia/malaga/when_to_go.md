---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Málaga has the warmest winter of any city on mainland Europe and over 300 sunny days a year. The shoulder seasons — **April–June** and **September–October** — are the most pleasant: temperatures of 20–28°C, sea warm enough to swim in, and the crowds thinner than midsummer.

**July and August** are very hot (35–40°C is common, occasional spikes above 43°C) but the constant Mediterranean breeze keeps the coast bearable. This is also when the **Feria de Málaga** takes over the city for nine days in mid-August — a continuous open-air party of flamenco, sherry, fairground rides, and horseback parades, held first in the old town by day and then on the fairground south of the river by night. **Winter** is mild (averages around 13–17°C, lows rarely below 8°C) with occasional rain in November and December; even in January you can sit outside at lunchtime in a T-shirt.

Other dates worth planning around: **Semana Santa** (Holy Week, March or April), one of the most spectacular in Spain — vast wooden floats carried through the streets by hundreds of bearers, most famously the Cristo de Mena escorted by serving Spanish Legionnaires; the **Málaga Film Festival** in March, the country's main showcase for Spanish-language cinema; and **Picassian October**, a month of exhibitions and events around the artist's birthday on the 25th.
