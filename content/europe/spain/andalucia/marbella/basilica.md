A small paleochristian basilica on the coast at San Pedro de Alcántara, a few kilometres west of Marbella. Built in the 4th century and one of the oldest Christian structures in Spain, it sits in a pine grove near the beach with surviving walls, tombs and a baptismal font — modest but genuinely ancient.
