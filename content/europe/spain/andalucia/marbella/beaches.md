---
title: "Beaches in Marbella"
type: section
---

A favourite destination for sunseekers and sports fans alike, life in Marbella is based around its beaches. And there are plenty of them. This resort itself is surrounded by nearly 30 kilometers of sands, and there are some 24 beaches to choose from.

**Lively Resort**
Marbella is a former traditional fishing town that has come into its own as a resort offering no end of opportunity for fun in the sun. In and around Marbella you will find family-friendly beaches with waters that are gentle enough for swimming; secluded white sand coves ideal for romantic escapes; and long stretches of golden sand packed with holidaymakers from across Europe. Lying at the heart of the Costa del Sol (Coast of the Sun) in the beautiful Andalucia region of Spain, it should come as no surprise that the climate is warm and sunny here throughout much of the year, making beach breaks a real opportunity even outside of the summer season. Those who want to get back to nature and achieve an all over tan will find a range of nudist beaches in Marbella, including the 1,500 meter long Artola beach, in the Dunas de Artola protected area.
**
Beach Actitivies **
But the beaches of Marbella aren’t just for sunbathing. Active types can try their hand at jet-skiing, sailing, deep sea fishing, kite surfing and many other water-based activities, while beach football and beach volley are good ways to top up your tan while keeping mind and body occupied. Those who like to take things a little easier can enjoy a cocktail or ice cold beer and a plate of freshly-caught fish at one of the many beach bars that line the sands at Marbella. The busiest beaches can be found towards the center of the town, although most of the beaches in and around Marbella are lively and well-equipped.

**Family-friendly**
Marbella caters well to families, and the beaches are no exception. Many are equipped with children's play areas, while life guards are always on hand to make sure everybody swims in safety. Parasols to keep kids cool, plus plenty of opportunity for sandcastle building and paddling, all mean that young kids will have a blast on the beaches of Marbella, while older children and teens can have a go at snorkelling, fishing or any other of beach sports and water sports. The beaches to the east of Marbella, such as El Pinillo, tend to be the best suited to families.
