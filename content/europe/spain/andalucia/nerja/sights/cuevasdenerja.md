The Caves of Nerja are one of Spain’s top tourist attractions – complete with floodlit stalactites, stalagmites and other magnificent rock formations. Just over 3 kms from the town on the Ctra Maro, the largest of the caves contains paintings some 20,000 years old. 
 ** BRUCE O'MARA:** My wife and I visited these caves 2 weeks ago and were OK with them, but not overly impressed. We had seen caves in the US before, so the initial impression of just being in a cave was lost. However a reason for the popularity of these caves is the presence of ancient wall painting of wild animals and this whole section was "off limits" for the time being, so we were basically going from room to room looking at hanging limestone formations which are impressive but not as exciting as the drawings would be.
