Sanlúcar de Barrameda sits at the mouth of the Guadalquivir River in Andalucía, right where the river meets the Atlantic and the marshlands of Doñana National Park begin on the opposite bank. It is one of the three towns of the sherry triangle, famous for its manzanilla — a dry, salty fino sherry that supposedly gets its character from the sea air in the bodegas.

The town has a long shell-covered river beach where the water stays warm and the crowds stay thin. Boat cruises cross to the Doñana marshes, one of Europe’s most important wetland reserves. Back in town, you can visit the historic bodegas, explore the Palacio Ducal de Medina Sidonia, or wander up to the castle overlooking the lower town. This is also where Columbus set sail on his third voyage and Magellan departed for his circumnavigation of the globe.
