Seville is the most southern from the three biggest andalusian cities.

Sevilla represents all that is Andalucian - flamenco, the horses, el rocio. Once a port this is now one of the most important cultural centres of Spain.

It is connected via an excellent high speed train link from Madrid as well as its own airport and a good road system.

The expo in 1992 has left a good infrastructure as well as preserving the beautiful buildings and streets and quiet alleys of the old town.

Seville was the choice of the first royal wedding of the current generation of Spanish royal family.
