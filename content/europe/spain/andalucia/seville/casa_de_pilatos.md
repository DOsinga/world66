title: Casa de Pilatos
type: poi
---

This 15th-century palace is Seville's finest example of Mudéjar architecture outside the Alcázar, and you will have it nearly to yourself. Built by the Marquis of Tarifa after a pilgrimage to Jerusalem — hence the name — it blends Moorish tilework, Gothic arches, and Renaissance marble in a way that feels less curated than the Alcázar. The ground-floor rooms and gardens are included in the basic ticket; the upper floor, with its frescoed ceilings, costs extra but is worth it.
