---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Seville is exceptionally well placed for day trips. **Córdoba**, 45 minutes north by AVE high-speed train, has the extraordinary **Mezquita-Catedral** — a 10th-century Umayyad mosque with a Christian cathedral dropped into the middle of it, now the city's defining building. **Cádiz**, 90 minutes south by train, is an ancient sea port on a tongue of land poking out into the Atlantic, with the oldest continuously inhabited city centre in western Europe. **Jerez de la Frontera**, an hour south, is the home of **sherry** and one of the best places in the country for tastings and cellar tours, along with flamenco and Andalucían horses. **Doñana National Park**, about 100 km south-west, protects the vast wetlands at the mouth of the Guadalquivir — Europe's most important bird sanctuary, and one of the last refuges of the Iberian lynx. Further afield, **Granada** and the Alhambra are three hours by train — a long day out, but possible.
