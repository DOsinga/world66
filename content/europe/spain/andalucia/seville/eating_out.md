---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Seville is one of the great **tapas** cities of the world — the format is said to have been invented here (the word *tapa* means "lid", and comes from the slice of bread once used to cover a glass of sherry to keep the flies out). The local style is small, cheap, plate after plate, and eaten standing at the bar. Look out for **espinacas con garbanzos** (spinach and chickpeas), **carrillada** (slow-braised pork cheeks), **pescaito frito** (tiny fried fish), **solomillo al whisky**, **salmorejo** (a chilled tomato-and-bread soup thicker than gazpacho) and, in season, **caracoles** (snails). The historic **Barrio Santa Cruz** and **Alameda de Hércules** have the greatest concentration of good bars; for a more authentic, less touristy experience, cross the river to **Triana**, where the streets around Calle Betis and the Mercado de Triana have some of the best simple seafood in the city. Finish with a glass of **fino** or **manzanilla** — the dry sherry from nearby Jerez and Sanlúcar that is the traditional pairing — or a glass of ice-cold **orange wine** (*vino de naranja*) for a local twist.
