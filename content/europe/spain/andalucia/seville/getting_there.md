---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

**By train** is the best option if you are already in Spain. The **AVE** high-speed line from Madrid runs roughly hourly and takes 2 hours 30 minutes to cover the 500 km; trains arrive at **Santa Justa** station, a 20-minute walk or short taxi ride from the old town. There are also direct AVE services to Córdoba (45 minutes), Málaga, Barcelona and Valencia. **By air**, **Seville Airport (SVQ)**, 10 km east of the city, has flights from most major European capitals and low-cost carriers from the UK, Ireland, Germany and Italy; a dedicated EA airport bus links the airport to the centre every 30 minutes. Many travellers on a budget fly into **Málaga** (2 hours away by bus or train) or **Faro** in Portugal and continue by land. **By bus**, long-distance coaches serve Seville from all over Spain; the two terminals are Plaza de Armas (services to the north and west) and El Prado de San Sebastián (east and south).
