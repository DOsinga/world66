---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Seville is a good place to buy things you can't easily find elsewhere: hand-painted **ceramics** (particularly the distinctive blue-and-yellow *cerámica sevillana* from Triana), **flamenco dresses** and accessories, hand-painted **fans**, and **azulejo** tiles. The main shopping street is **Calle Sierpes**, a long pedestrianised strip of boutiques and traditional shops that runs north from the Plaza de San Francisco; **Calle Tetuán**, parallel to it, has the mid-range chain stores (Zara, Mango, Massimo Dutti). The **Triana ceramics workshops** on Calle Antillano Campos and the Mercado de Triana are the best places to buy pottery. For antiques and vintage, the Thursday-morning **El Jueves** flea market on Calle Feria (running since the 13th century, the oldest in the city) and the Sunday **Alameda de Hércules** flea market are both worth a look.
