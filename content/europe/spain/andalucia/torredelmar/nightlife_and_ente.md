---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Torre del Mar"
type: section
---

When planning a visit to the coastal Spanish resort town of **Torre del Mar, nightlife **is very often one of the most important considerations to keep in mind. While this town of 20,000 is quaint and quiet during the other months of the year, over the summer it balloons in size up to 150,000 or so, thanks to tourists from all over Spain, Europe and the rest of the world, as well. It's a perfect place for a family to relax with young children on the beach and for older travelers to soak up some sun. However, Torre del Mar is most popular among the young travelers who visit in groups, as couples or alone. Read on for a brief overview of the nightlife and entertainment in this bustling tourist spot.

**El Copo**

The primary nightlife oriented part of Torre del Mar is called El Copo. It's located off of the Paseo Maritimo, the paved boardwalk that stretches along the beach on the Mediterranean. El Copo is home to dozens of bars, discos and clubs that are open throughout all hours of the night during the summer months. Many of them are cheap, although you should expect to pay a small cover charge to enter, particularly on weekend nights in the summer.

**Sea Front**

Another popular source of night time fun and other entertainment is the beach itself. Although it doesn't provide a bar atmosphere, the beach is home to many couples walking romantically, to games of beach volleyball and other activities as well, throughout the day and night. Best of all, the beach is the longest along the Mediterranean, stretching for over two km. This means that there is almost always a spot to sit or otherwise stake out a territory to relax or play.

**Paseo Maritimo**

Paseo Maritimo is the boardwalk area that parallels the beach throughout Torre del Mar. In addition to being the home to many different vendors, entertainers and other sources of fun, it's also where the majority of the restaurants in Torre del Mar are located. Torre del Mar is famous for its selection of fresh seafood, and the restaurants in this area capitalize on this fact to a delicious result. Restaurants are open late and are often bustling throughout the night.

Consult with the Spanish Tourism Board for additional information.
