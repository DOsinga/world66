---
title: "Shopping in Torre del Mar"
type: section
---

One of the most idyllic and peaceful beachfront tourist towns in all of Spain (and Europe, for that matter) is **Torre del Mar**. **Shopping**, outdoor adventures, relaxing on the beach, active nightlife activities and more await the traveler to this southern coastal town. Although it sports just 20,000 full time residents, Torre del Mar (Spanish for "Towers of the Sea") balloons in size with tourists from all over Spain, Europe and the rest of the world during the summer months. For those who have had their fill of beach relaxation and water sports, read on for a brief overview of the many shopping options in and around this coastal city.

**Paseo Maritimo**

Paseo Maritimo is the paved walkway that stretches along the gorgeous beach in Torre del Mar. Set back several hundred feet from the water itself, this boardwalk is home to a variety of different vendors. It's a great place to find tourist souvenirs and local handicrafts at good prices. You can also find food stalls and various other items for sale along here as well. be sure to barter with the vendors for the best deals on their merchandise.

**Velez-Malaga**

Velez-Malaga is a larger town located approximately four km inland from Torre del Mar. The close proximity makes it an ideal spot to get outside of the resort area to explore. It's easily driven to by car, and taxis and buses also operate between the two cities for a minimal charge. Because Velez-Malaga is a larger city than Torre del Mar, it features a number of amenities and attractions that the smaller tourist resort town does not, including shopping malls. Velez-Malaga is not as picturesque or as ideally located as Torre del Mar, but it does make an excellent place to explore for a day or two during a longer trip to the Spanish coast, particularly for those interested in shopping.

**La Caleta de Velez**

La Caleta de Velez is the next town up to the east of Torre del Mar. It's home to a marina and a small fishing port, and it also houses great seafood restaurants and crafts for sale. Walk the two miles along the sea walk to get to La Caleta de Velez or arrive by taxi. You can shop at the various vendors who sell their wares and complete the trip with a ride on a boat back to the main part of Torre del Mar.
