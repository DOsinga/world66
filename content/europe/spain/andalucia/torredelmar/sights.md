---
title: "Sights in Torre del Mar"
type: section
---

If you're interested in escaping to an idyllic and gorgeous beach resort town, the **Torre del Mar sights** in southern Spain may be one of the best options for you. This coastal resort town, located at the south of Spain, is famous among Spanish travelers for its beautiful summers and magnificent sights. With only 20,000 full time residents, the city expands to around 150,000 throughout the summer as tourists flock in from around Spain, Europe and the rest of the world. Read on for a brief overview of some of the sights that they find.

**Sea Front**

The single biggest draw to Torre del Mar is the sea front area. This two-km stretch of beach along the Mediterranean is the largest on the European side of the sea. The beach is also quite wide at various points, allowing for a huge number of people and a variety of activities like swimming, sunbathing, volleyball, running, walking and more.

**Paseo Maritimo**

For those who are not interested in lying out on the beach to suntan, there is the paved Paseo Maritimo. This walkway is located parallel to the beach and just off of the waterfront. It's home to a huge variety of famous seafood restaurants, vendors and other tourist delights. Torre del Mar is famous for its seafood, and the excellent food combined with the superb views out over the Mediterranean draw in tourists and locals by the thousands.

**Paseo de Larios**

Named after the founding family of Torre del Mar, Paseo de Larios is the major street that runs along the Mediterranean Sea and the beach in this coastal town. It's home to El Copo, a stretch of popular nightlife venues including bars, discos and clubs. Suitable for younger families and older travelers as well, this street has a variety of shopping options and additional restaurants, many of which are priced somewhat more reasonably than the restaurants right along the water.

**La Caleta de Velez**

The next town to the east of Torre del Mar is home to a marina and a fishing port. It makes an excellent day trip from Torre del Mar, where tourists can rent boats and experience the Mediterranean Sea first-hand.

Consult with the Spanish Tourism Board for additional information about the sights and other accommodations in Torre del Mar.
