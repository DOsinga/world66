---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Torre del Mar"
type: section
---

If you're thinking of visiting the coast of Spain, make sure that you pay attention to a list of **Torre del Mar must do's**. Torre del Mar is one of the most popular summer tourist destinations in this country, located on the southern coast of Spain in Malaga province. With a permanent population of only around 20,000, this city (called "Towers of the Sea" in Spanish) is home to many times that in tourists each summer season. Read on for a few of the must-do activities in this lovely beach getaway locale.

**Sea Front**

Many tourists arrive in coastal Torre del Mar for one reason alone--the magnificent sea front is one of the most beautiful in all of Europe. It is also the longest continuous sea front area in all of the Mediterranean, spanning over two km (1.5 miles) in length. This is an excellent place to go with a family, with younger tourists or older travelers as well. It's home to many people seeking rest, relaxation, beach sports and more. The sea front is most popular between the months of May and August, although some travelers complain of it getting dirty toward the end of the summer season.

**Paseo Maritimo**

If lying in the sand is not for you, try walking along the Paseo Maritimo, the paved boardwalk that runs along the stretch of the sea front in Torre del Mar. Here you can find vendors, food stands and more.

**El Copo**

El Copo is the bar and disco neighborhood in Torre del Mar. It's located just off of the Paseo Maritimo and is a popular destination for younger travelers. This area is appropriate for all ages during the daytime but may not be as appropriate for the very young or the very old come nightfall.

**La Caleta de Velez**

La Caleta de Velez is a small fishing port that is located approximately two kilometers east of Torre del Mar. It harbors a marina, where tourists can rent boats to take sailing and exploring on the coastal waters.

**Seafood**

No trip to Torre del Mar would be complete with a sampling of the region's local seafood specialties. This part of Spain is well known for having some of the best seafood in all of Europe. Seafood restaurants abound along the Paseo Maritimo.

Contact the Spanish Tourism Board for additional information about lodging and activities in Torre del Mar.
