title: "7 Day Itinerary in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

There are many options for spending an activity-filled week in and around Torremolinos, and here we explore some examples.

**Day 1 - Tapas Bars**

Andalucia is rich in culinary heritage. Take the day to discover the charming Tapas bars and cafes in the old town of Torremolinos. Calle Quintana Rosado is a street which is particularly good for eating out on a budget.

**Day 2 - Hit the Beach**

There are 6 main beaches here--Playamar, La Cariheula, Los Alamos, Bajondillo, El Saltillo and Montemar. They are all generally of a good quality and have good facilities. Do bear in mind that they become very crowded in the peak season. The quietest beach is Los Alamos.

**Day 3 - ****Tivoli****World and Benalmadena Cable Car**

The Costa del Sol's biggest theme park, Tivoli World, has something for everyone-- from toddlers to seniors. Situated 3 km north of Torremolinos, there are many rides to choose from. There is also a selection of bars, restaurants, concerts and Flamenco dance displays to keep oneself entertained. Tivoli World is open daily from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. and can be easily reached by taxi or bus. Also, the park hosts a street market every Sunday.

After a day of thrills at the amusement park than take a relaxing cable car ride from just next door. This ride takes you to a height of 2600 meters and affords wonderful views of the surrounding area.

**Day 4 – Equestrian Show Ritmo A Caballo**

There is a celebrated horse riding tradition in Andalucia and this show is a wonderful way in which to partake in the excitement first-hand. Narrated in Spanish with an English translation, the show is held at the El Ranchito stables. Tel: 95 238 3140.

**Day 5 – Sea Life**

This is a world class aquarium which houses an extensive array of aquatic life. Children will enjoy getting up close to the sea life during the educational sessions which are run most days.

**Day 6 – Shopping**

Torremolinos is a good are for shopping and when one looks beyond the usual tourist shops, there are some real gems to be found.

Check out the weekly market held every Thursday morning behind the ayuntamiento (town hall). Also there is a “car boot” sale which is held near the Bull Ring every Sunday and is very popular among both tourists and locals.

Be sure to visit the larger shopping centers such as The San Miguel Shopping Center for some decent quality clothing and gifts.

**Day 7 – Day Trips and Excursions**

Torremolinos is a great base from which to discover many other Spanish cities such as Nerja, Seville and Cordoba. Also one can travel further afield to Morocco and Gibraltar and experience diverse and rich cultures.
