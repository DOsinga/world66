---
title: "Beaches in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Torremolinos, one of the busiest beaches on the Costa
del Sol, is visited by tourists from all walks of life, from
toddlers with their buckets and spades to practiced scuba divers. This multifaceted
beach town offers something for everyone. Busiest in the summer, the fall and
spring months are an ideal time to visit, where rainfall is at a minimum and
temperatures are still warm.

**Torremolinos**

Outdoor sun beds, complimentary shades and skilled lifeguards are just a few
things that set off this nine kilometers of sandy beach from the others. Torremolinos
also features an array of local chiringuitos (also known as “beach bars”) with
an eclectic range of international and local cuisine. You can walk from beach
to beach within a day, but be mindful of the high tides when crossing the
channel becomes difficult.

Here you can fish, sail, waterski, windsurf, dive and paraglide. If that’s
not enough for you, take a walk to La Carihuela, the original fishing quarter
that survived the ambush of mass tourism, and eat lunch at classic, quaint
seafood diners serving some of best fresh fish on the Costa. After lunch you can
take a leisurely walk to the neighboring beach of Benalmadena,
to get a different view of the ocean.

**Diving**

Interested in learning how to dive? Simply Diving in Calle Carmen Montes
caters to beginners and experienced divers, and all your instructors are certified.
Simply Dive will provide snorkeling equipment, buoyancy aids and for those who
don’t want to dive, there's space on their boats to watch! If you dive at the Marine
Reserve at Almuñecar you’ll be sure to see some beautiful marine life, also an
excellent place to snorkel. Want to see the wrecks of WW2 vessels? The kids
will love it! Take a day trip to Gibraltar to
see these vessels, and don’t forget the camera.

**Aquapark Torremolinos**

Don’t miss visiting the Aquapark Torremolinos, the biggest water park on the
Costa del Sol, with more than a dozen attractions,
games and pools. Attractions include a wave pool, tropical lake, wild river
ride, a bubble pool, kamikaze death slide and foam slides. For the less active
there are restaurants, mini-golf and casino games.

**Sea Life Centre**

Finally, another huge family attraction is the Sea Life Centre, located in
the port area of the neighboring beach Benalmadena. There are more than 30 compelling
displays of sea creatures, from shrimps to sharks, with feeding demonstrations
and special presentations by staff. Check out the gift shop, bar and café!

No matter which beach you go to in Torremolinos, you won’t be disappointed.
Now start packing your gear!
