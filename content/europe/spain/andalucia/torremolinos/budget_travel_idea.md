---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

There are many inexpensive accommodation options in Torremolinos, and although it is not necessarily nowadays the cheap and cheerful resort that it was years ago, there are still many tips and tricks for travelers on a budget.

**Hostels**

Hostels in Torremolinos are generally of a good standard and offer decent, but basic, facilities. An example of this is the Hostal Castilla which is situated on Calle Manila, ten minutes' walk from the beach. The hostel offers a choice of rooms which are either private or communal, and is very inexpensive in comparison with the local hotels.

**Self Catering**

There are many self catering facilities and holiday flats available in and around the area. Preparing your own food can potentially save a great deal of money in comparison to the expense of eating out three times a day. Also, the money one saves can be spent on days out or saved for your next holiday!

**Go out of Season**

Peak season for travel to Torremolinos is from early June to October and as such, accommodation prices are at a premium. The weather is still very pleasant on either side of these months, so why not do your research and visit just outside these times? It's surprising how much one can potentially save. November and December temperatures are still 22 and 17 degrees centigrade, respectively.

**Late Deal**

Sun worshipers with late availability may like to take the potluck option and book very close to the time of travel, which can be a cost effective option.

**Lazing on the Beach**

The 9 kilometers of beaches in this area are consistently awarded the European Blue Flag award for cleanliness. They have very good facilities, beach bars and life guards. It's very easy to spend a full day enjoying the sun and the sea here. Bring along a packed lunch along with plenty of water to keep yourself hydrated, without breaking the bank.

**La Carihuela**

A short 10 or 15 minute walk away from Torremolinos is La Carihuela, with its very inexpensive bars, such as the International, which sells selected drinks for one euro. Explore this quaint town on foot for a real taste of Andalusia. There is also a lovely beach here. La Carihuela is well worth a visit in July when the fiesta is on - lots of free entertainment!
