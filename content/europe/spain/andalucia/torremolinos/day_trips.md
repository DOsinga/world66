Within easy driving distance of Torremolinos are some major sights of interest, ranging from the birthplace of Picasso to the breeding grounds of flamingos. These beautiful cities offer spectacular architecture, venerable histories and traditional Spanish ways.

**[Malaga](/europe/spain/andalucia/malaga)** 16.6 km

Just a short 22 minute drive away is Andalucia's main city, Malaga. Here you can find caves with paintings that are thought to be the oldest in the world. Alcazaba fortress is a definite must-see with its views over the city. The structure dates ** 18.6 km

Also just a 22 minute drive away-albeit in the opposite direction-is Mijas, a town that can be described as both a coastal (Mijas Costa) and a mountain (Mijas Pueblo) town that encapsulates all of Spain's charm. The main section of town is Mijas Pueblo, which sits 428 meters above sea level and has lost none of its traditional outlook on life. The buildings are quaint, with narrow cobbled streets running between them. Join the people sitting outside having a meal or a cup of coffee while the donkeys go by, bedecked in bells and pulling carts. Afterward, wander through the many shops that sell traditional arts, crafts and souvenirs as well as artwork by local artists. Every Wednesday you can catch the weekly flamenco show, and regardless of what time of year you visit there's bound to be an extensive festival going on.

**[Antequera](/europe/spain/andalucia/antequera)** 64.1 km

A bit further away, but still just under an hour's drive, is the beautiful town of Antequera. It's a prime visiting spot not only for people, but for the majestic pink flamingos that descend on the Pink Lagoon every year from January through August. Najera Palace, built in the 18th century and home to the municipal museum, displays Roman artifacts found in the area as well as artworks by the native Christobal Toral.
