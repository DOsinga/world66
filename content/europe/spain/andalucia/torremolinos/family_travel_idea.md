---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Torremolinos was one of the first villages on the Costa Del Sol to be developed and built up into a tourist resort. As such it offers a plethora of activities for all ages - from toddlers right up to grandparents.

**Aqualand Adventures**

Don’t miss visiting the famous Aquapark in Torremolinos, a large water play area featuring
a range of a family friendly attractions, such as swimming, games, waterslides
and tropical lakes. You can also partake in mini-golf or just take a stroll
around the premises.

**Crocodile Park**

This is the home of Big Daddy, a huge 600 kg crocodile. Go on a guided tour and visit the educational area. Young and old alike will be fascinated by the creatures in this park. The park is open every day from 10 a.m. with 3 guided tours each day.

**Yelmo Cinema**

A huge 20-screen cinema (also with an Imax and 3D screen), the Yelmo can be found in the Plaza Mayor and is very popular. N.B. Movies shown here are mostly in Spanish.

**Karting for Kids
**

In nearby Marbella, there is Funny Beach that boasts a karting facility. There are bumper boats, video game areas, electric cars and bikes, a swimming pool and many watersports to choose from.

There is also a restaurant on site and a full day can easily be spent here.

**Tivoli World**

One of the most popular places for a family-day-out in the Torremolinos area has to be Tivoli World. With its shows, rides, many themed eateries and 12-month calendar - including special Christmas and Halloween themed shows - along with its Sunday Market between 11 a.m. and 1 p.m., there is certainly something for all the family. The park is easily reached by all forms of transport.

**Forest Fun**

Torremolinos is surrounded by 3 forests filled with freshwater springs and ample picnic and barbecue spots. Here visitors can escape from the hustle and bustle of the shopping areas and the crowded beaches and relax with nature. Also, there are some very good horse riding schools in the Andalucia area--and Torremolinos in particular. All riders from beginner to advanced can book a few lessons at a local school and take a leisurely trek through the pine forests.

Forget the antiquated image of the bucket and spade beach holiday. Torremolinos has something to offer everyone if you look beyond the expected.
