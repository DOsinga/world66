title: "Festivals in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

**
In Andalusia, Spain, the Torremolinos Festivals are now a very popular tourist destination.
**
With the many high rise hotels and apartment buildings, it's hard to remember that this was once an original fishing village. The city of Torremolinos was one of the first to be groomed for the tourist dating back to the early sixties. The town is actually considered in two different sections. The first to be developed is called Torremolinos centre. To the west is the authentic original area called La Carihuela. Torremolinos is not too far from Malaga and makes for a great day hopping destination. **

Festivals

Semana Santa
**
This is probably the most popular and celebrated event in Torremolinos and all of southern Spain. This is a week long celebration beginning the week before Easter Sunday each year. In Torremolinos you will be able to view the processions on Maundy Thursday and Good Friday. Train service to Malaga is available and frequent if you care to see the daily processions there.**

Fried Fish Day
**
The first Thursday in June is another event you won't want to miss. On La Carihuela Beach is the famous Fried Fish Day or Dia del Pescaito.**

Festival of the Virgen del Carmen de La Carihuela
**
Also in La Carihuela is the Festival of the Virgen del Carmen de La Carihuela on the
sixteenth of July every year. As the feria ends, the procession carries the image of the Virgen from the small church in La Carihuela to the seafront where the procession continues on the beaches of Torremolinos.**

Festival of San Miguel Arcangel

**The Festival of San Miguel Arcangel is the last week in September. It is scheduled to end on St. Michael's Day, September twenty nineth. Saint Michael is the patron saint of Torremolinos so the town is busy with this week long celebration of events processions and parades.**

The beach, fisherman's quarters and Crocodile Park
**
The busiest season is summertime, but the weather is beautiful every day in Torremolinos. It is the most popular destination in the Costa del Sur. If you are budget minded, picking a more reasonable time to vacation there is better than not going at all. Many have fallen in love with the weather, the nine kilometers of clean beautiful beaches and have chosen to live there. The beach and the fisherman's quarters will be there all year long. Crocodile Park is also open every day. Take the family on a guided tour and see a live demonstration of the prehistoric monster crocodiles. Enjoy the rhythm, the cool winter mornings, evenings and mild afternoons. The seven mile long board walk stroll of the Mediterranean coastline is free and is always there waiting to be explored.**
