---
title: "Getting Around in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Torremolinos is a resort on the Costa Del Sol. Tourists may find
that there are so many things to do that they need to work out a method
of transportation from one place to another.

**Public Transport**

Torremolinos has both buses and trains serving the area. Buses are
found at the center of Calle Hoya, and can be caught to all the major
towns. A 5-hour journey will cost about 19 EUR.

The train station is found at Calle San Miguel. Trains run around
every half an hour to Malaga and the airport, and also stopping at
Fuengirola.

**Transporting Yourself**

For independent visitors to Torremolinos, transportation is
necessarily a rental car, or walking. Rental cars can be hired from the
airport nearby, which also saves on finding transportation to the
resort. Roads are well-kept, and secondary roads are often maintained
by the state at the residents’ expense. Be aware that
sometimes European standards of driving might be classed as “Alarming”
– they are certainly more confident and reckless than the average
American driver. Bikes and scooters can also be rented, with a valid
driver’s license.

For those that wish to try a walking option, there are several
kilometres of beach promenade, with any number of places to rest and
wait. However, as with everywhere in Europe there are lots of steps (one place has 178 steps) although it is possible to stop and rest if
necessary.
