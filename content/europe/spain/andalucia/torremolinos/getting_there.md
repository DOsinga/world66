---
title: "Getting There in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Torremolinos, on the Costa del Sol, is a popular tourist destination.
As a result, there are many ways of getting to the city, from almost
anywhere in the world.

**By Plane**

Of all the things that need to be considered when visiting
Torremolinos, getting there is not one of them. Chartered flights and
budget airlines call regularly at the nearest airport, Malaga. This is
only 7 km away, and tourists can easily transfer themselves to their
destination in Torremolinos. Other flights can also be booked to
Alicante and Seville, none of which are more than a couple of hours’
journey away.

**Getting to the Beaches**

There are no flights which land directly in Torremolinos, which can
be a good thing (no constant air noise). Most tourists land at Malaga,
which is a nearby town. Flights typically travel either from the UK, or
from the Irish republic. A direct train service operates from Malaga
airport to Torremolinos, which takes about half an hour. The center of
Malaga is about nine stops away from the resort, and the train also goes on
to Fuengirola, another resort.

For those unwilling to travel by train, it's also possible to get a
bus from Malaga, which takes tourists to Torremolinos. There are also taxis, which wait outside Malaga for tourists, but it's important to use a fixed price system, and check the fare before
traveling. Don’t enter the taxi if you are unsure about the price.
