---
title: "Museums in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Located along the Costa del Sol in the province of Malaga, Torremolinos is a small touristy city. There are relatively few museums, 3 main ones, actually, but the museums that are there are have a unique appeal and atmosphere about them. The entrance fees are reasonable, and the staffs are friendly.

**Museo Picasso Malaga**

The Museo Picasso Malaga in Torremolinos costs 6 euro to see the permanent collection and 4.50 eur to see the temporary exhibits. It's perfect for students who are given a 50 percent discount, but remember that the museum is closed on Mondays. The Picasso collections are located in the Palacio de Buenavista, a 16th century building of Andalusian architecture. Under the building, you can see the Phoenician, Roman, Moorish and Renaissance remains, giving you a look into Malaga's history.

**Museo del Tatuaje "El Templo"**

Many tourists visit Torremolinos to see the Tattoo Museum, or the Museo del Tatuaje "El Templo". There are over 100,000 tattoos displayed as well as various relics tracing the history of the art of tattooing. It is a unique and famous museum in the city.

**Museo Casa Natal de Picasso**

The other museum in the area is the Museo Casa Natal de Picasso in Plaza de la Merced. This was the birthplace of Pablo Picasso and the museum is open Monday through Sunday and costs 1 euro to see both the museum and the temporary exhibition. The exhibition room is located on the east side of the Plaza de la Merced. There are a variety of other museums to visit outside Torremolinos but still along the Costa del Sol in Malaga, such as the Bonsai Museum in Marbella.
