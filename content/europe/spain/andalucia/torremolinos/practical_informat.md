---
title: "Practical Information in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

The area of Torremolinos is traveler friendly, so far as English speaking tourists are concerned. Being the most well established resort destination in the region, there is a very highly developed and extensive tourist infrastructure.

**Currency:** the Euro, as with the rest of Spain.

**Power Supply: **220 - 240V which means that not all US appliances will work in Torremolinos. Check the input details on the information section of your appliance.

**Visas and Passports: **For stays of under 90 days, there are is no visa requirement for US citizens visiting this area and passports must be valid for at least three months beyond the period of stay.

**U.S. Consulate** **Location: **Fuengirola, Telephone (34) (952) 474 891, operating hours are 10:00am until 2:00pm.

**Language: **Spanish and English are both widely spoken in Torremolinos, although it is courtesy to learn a few Spanish phrases and greetings in advance of traveling.

**Population:** 56,000 living in an area which is approximately 20 km squared.

**Identification: **Remember to bring a national ID card, driver license, passport and credit card with you to enable you to hire a car, check into your room.

**Tourist Information Center: **Located at the Playamar beach. A wide selection of leaflets are available here.

**Internet: **Try to avoid using the Internet services in your hotel if possible as this can be very expensive. Try the local Internet cafes where the usual rate is around 2 euro per hour.

**Warnings: **It is advisable to be very careful whilst walking in certain ares of the town late at night, in particular on the steps leading to the beach. There is a lift which can be used for a nominal charge which will ensure a safer journey. This is located at near The Ship Inn.

Walking around the rock at La Carihuela is also another danger spot at night. Try to avoid this.

There are gypsy people who sell flowers in and around the city. These people are to be avoided, if possible, as there is a high instance of pickpocketing when they area around you.

Also, keep an eye out for begging children who are in reality collecting money for organized gang crime.

If in doubt, ask to see the collectors badge which by law must be signed and stamped by the police.

**Don't Worry!**

Enjoy your holiday here and do not be overly concerned about the warnings above. Following similar safety rules to those you do at home is always advisable wherever you are in the world.
