---
title: "Sights in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

There are many places of interest around Torremolinos although the city itself is not really renowned for sightseeing opportunities. Here we look at some options.

**The Old Town**

On taking a walk through the pedestrianized old town (La Carihuela) one can really see and feel that traditional Andalusian charm, with it's many and celebrated restaurants, charming bars and cafes. This area has not lost its Spanish feel unlike the main resort of Torremolinos and as such attracts travelers enticed by its culinary reputation and beautiful old architecture.

If you are visiting the village around the month of June, a sight not to be missed is Fried Fish Day. On this day, the beach is filled with chefs cooking up tasty fish and the best thing of all is that it is free! Also there is often food sampling in various local bars and restaurants on this day.

**Torre de Pimentel**

The name Torremolinos derives from the Torre de Pimentel or Torre de los Molinos, a defensive construction which dates from the 14th Century and still stands at twelve meters high. Known locally as the 'Torre Vigia' it can still be climbed, there is no entry fee.

**Beautiful Architecture**

The church of Nuestra Señora del Carmen can be found in the old town and is a beautiful place to visit even for the non believers. Another outstanding example of noble architecture is Casa de los Navaja, which was built in the 19th century.

**Cortijo del Tajo**

A wonderful area for those interested in archaeolgy to visit, Cortijo del Tajo plays host to Roman, pre Roman and Neolithic remains.

**Miles of Beaches**

This area of the Costa del Sol is famous for its miles of golden sands. No visit to Torremolinos would be complete without a few days trying out a few of its six main beaches - Playamar, La Cariheula, Los Alamos, Bajondillo, El Saltillo and Montemar. Of these beaches Los Alamos is generally the most quiet.

Each of these beaches have a good variety of facilities and sunbeds and offer a myriad of watersports including diving and sailing.

**Parque la Bateria**

To the west of Torremolinos, lies the Parque la Bateria which is situated on a hill which used to be used as a look out point for a military base in the 1940's. This has now been replaced by a lovely park filled with fountains, cycling paths, statues and a lake. A perfect place to while away an afternoon.
