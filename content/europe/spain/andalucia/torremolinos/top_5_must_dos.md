---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Perched along the banks of the Mediterranean Sea, Torremolinos
reigns as one of Spain’s most popular beach resort towns. Torremolinos
boasts a spectacular waterfront setting and a host of exciting
attractions. These 5 particular hotspots will only add to the
enjoyment of your vacation.

**El Bajondillo and La Carihuela**

Some of the most
popular attractions in Torremolinos are the beaches. El
Bajondillo and La Carihuela stand out as the highlights, with both
offering warm, sandy shores upon which to work on your tan. As an added
bonus, El Bajondillo plays host to an onslaught of shops, cheap
eateries and bars. Meanwhile La Carihuela, a charming neighborhood that
once served as a famous fishing district, houses a slew of renowned
seafood restaurants.

**Aqualand**

Adults and children alike will enjoy a day full of
fun and adventure at Torremolinos’ own Aqualand, one in a series of
popular Spanish water parks. Open May through September, guests have
the chance to cool off in the host of pools scattered throughout the
park. Daring tourists may wish to try their hand at the park’s
water-filled chutes and toboggan runs, as well as the always exciting
artificial wave pool.

**El Ranchito**

Tourists looking for something a little out of
the ordinary need to search no further than the fascinating equestrian
center, known simply as El Ranchito. Here you will find professional
riders offering a show filled with grace and beauty. The
horses at El Ranchito demonstrate an ability to perform difficult but
graceful equestrian ballet moves, complete with music. The shows are open to the public, but book early so that you can be guaranteed the
chance to experience this unusual but captivating event.

**Calle San Miguel**

Calle San Miguel remains Torremolinos’
premiere shopping thoroughfare. While some of the vendors here try and pawn shoddy or tacky merchandise, you can still find an
assortment of high quality local products and goods. Bring some lovely
crafts made by local artisans back home with you as souvenirs or
personal mementos of your travels. Outdoor cafes and restaurants line the pedestrian-only street, serving as a delightful retreat from a
day of shopping.

**Crocodile Park**

Torremolinos offers another unusual but fun
spot for children to spend their days while on holiday. The Crocodile
Park stays open year round, granting both children and adults up-close
access to more than 300 crocodiles, many of different varieties.
The park offers educational insight and access to a host of other
animals as well, including birds, goats, prairie dogs and emus.
