---
title: "When to Go in Torremolinos"
type: section
---

Torremolinos on the Costa del Sol is famous for its year round comfortable climate. You can have a fantastic vacation there any time of year without bad weather ruining it. The area is located between the Mediterranean Sea, low hills, and high mountains, so it is very sheltered from harsh weather conditions like constant rainshowers, cold fronts, and violent storms. Rain is actually quite rare in Torremolinos, as clear skies and sunshine prevail for over 320 days per year.

**Summer**

Hot and dry are the two words that best describe the summer season in Torremolinos. The temperatures usually stay above 85 degrees Fahrenheit. There is basically no rain at all, so if you go during summer, you're guaranteed a vacation of days in the blazing, Spanish sun. Even at night the temperatures can be unbearably hot, so make sure that your accomodations include air conditioning if you want to get a good night's rest. Luckily, the humidity is quite low, so the heat it slightly less intense. Most people find the weather in August to be unbearable; it is the hottest month of the year, so it's not recommended to visit Torremolinos at that time. Summer really isn't an ideal time to travel to the Costa del Sol, anyways, because it gets very crowded and touristy with a lot of young vacationers. This hides the true essence of the area, makes prices go up, and changes Torremolinos into a stressful, chaotic place.

**Autumn**

The best time to visit is in autumn. Temperatures are still warm, as is the water in the Mediterranean Sea, making it perfect for swimming, even in October! Towards the end of fall, you'll be at risk for some flooding and isolated electrical storms, but it's still a much better time to go to Torremolinos than in summer. Everyone is more friendly and less stressed, plus, hotels and tours offer discounts since it is the low season.

**Winter**

Winter can also be a nice time to travel. Sunny days are common, and you only need a light jacket to comfortably walk around and explore the area. Strong rain showers can occur from October to December, but they are very rare, and when they do occur, they quickly stop. During the day temperatures generally stay in the 60s. Again, because it is the low season, prices go down, and you can get a more intimate look at the culture of Torremolinos.

**Spring**

Tourists start coming into the area during springtime, although the water in the Mediterranean Sea is still quite cold. After March, both air and water temperatures warm up a bit, packing the beaches with visitors. Spring is not as bad as summer, but still not ideal.
