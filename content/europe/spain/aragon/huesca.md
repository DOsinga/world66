Huesca is a modest provincial capital sitting in the foothills of the Pyrenees, an easy stopover between Zaragoza and the mountain passes to the north. It's not a place most people put on their itinerary, but if you find yourself here — and it's worth it — you'll discover a well-preserved old town with real depth.

The Cathedral, begun in the 13th century and embellished for several hundred years afterward, anchors the historic centre. The Diocesan Museum next door holds altarpieces and murals rescued from churches that no longer exist — some of the panels are genuinely striking. Nearby, the 16th-century Town Hall is a good example of Aragonese Renaissance, and the former university buildings on the Plaza de la Universidad now house a museum open to visitors.

The Church of San Pedro el Viejo is arguably the most interesting stop: built on the site of an ancient temple, it contains the tombs of several Aragonese kings and has a cloister that rewards a slow walk around. The Baroque Basilica of San Lorenzo, the Church of Santo Domingo, and Santa Teresa round out the religious architecture if you're in the mood for more.

Huesca works well as a base for day trips into the Pyrenees — the town is calm and reasonably priced, the old quarter takes no more than an afternoon to explore, and the surrounding countryside is worth the detour.
