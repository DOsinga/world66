---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**Late June to mid-September** is the prime season. The high trails are snow-free, the waterfalls are still running from snowmelt (especially in June and July), and the days are long. July and August are the busiest months — the shuttle bus from Torla runs frequently and the valley fills by mid-morning on weekends. Weekday visits are noticeably quieter.

**September** is arguably the best month: the summer crowds thin out, the light turns golden, and the beech forest starts to colour. The high routes remain open until the first significant snowfall, usually in October.

**Spring (May–June)** is beautiful but unpredictable. Snow can linger on the upper paths until June, and some sections may require an ice axe. The valley floor is accessible and the wildflowers are exceptional.

**Winter** closes most of the high trails but the lower valley is walkable year-round. Private cars are permitted on the valley road in winter (the bus service operates at reduced frequency). Snowshoeing is possible with the right equipment.

The park receives heavy rainfall — the Pyrenees catch Atlantic weather systems year-round. Always carry waterproofs, whatever the season.
