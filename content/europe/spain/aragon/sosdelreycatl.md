Sos del Rey Católico is a beautifully preserved medieval town in the Aragonese Pyrenean foothills, and one of the most handsome small towns in Spain. Its name commemorates Ferdinand II of Aragon — the Catholic Monarch — who was born here in 1452. Ferdinand later married Isabella of Castile, effectively uniting Spain, and together they sponsored Columbus's voyage to the Americas.

The town itself is a maze of narrow stone lanes, Romanesque churches, and arcaded squares that feel almost entirely unchanged from the Middle Ages. It sits on a hilltop with good views over the surrounding countryside, and the old walls and towers are largely intact. It's a quiet place with few tourists and excellent local food — this is serious ham country, and the restaurants take their ingredients seriously. Worth a night if you're traveling between Zaragoza and Pamplona.
