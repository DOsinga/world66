Teruel is a small provincial capital in Aragon, perched on a high plateau between Madrid and Valencia. It's not large and its sights aren't far apart — you can cover the highlights on foot in a day. What makes it remarkable is its collection of Mudéjar architecture: four towers decorated in elaborate geometric brickwork and glazed ceramic tiles, all dating from the 13th to 16th centuries and collectively named a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The city is also famous for its medieval love story — the Lovers of Teruel (Los Amantes), Diego and Isabel, whose tragic tale of forbidden love and death rivals Romeo and Juliet. Their mausoleum in the church of San Pedro is one of the most visited sites in the city.

The Provincial Museum of Teruel is consistently underrated; its Iberian-age collection from the 6th to 4th centuries BCE is of outstanding quality, and the building itself — a fine Renaissance structure — is worth seeing. Teruel is compact enough to make a comfortable day trip from Zaragoza or Valencia, though it rewards an overnight stay.
