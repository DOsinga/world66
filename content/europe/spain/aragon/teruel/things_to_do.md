title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Teruel is small and walkable — all the main sights are within a short walk of each other. The Mudéjar towers are the reason most people come, but the museums and the Los Amantes story give the visit real depth. Allow a full day if you want to see everything properly.

**Cathedral**

A mix of styles accumulated over centuries. The oldest part is the tower, largely Mudéjar, but the magnificent Mudéjar painted ceiling inside is the unmissable highlight. The entry ticket includes the Cathedral museum.

address: Plaza de la Catedral

category: Sight

**El Salvador — Mudéjar Tower**

Possibly the finest of Teruel's Mudéjar towers, with a particularly dramatic position over a narrow downhill street. Unusually, steps between the inner and outer towers allow you to climb inside — highly recommended.

address: Calle del Salvador

tel: +34 978 601648

category: Sight

**San Pedro and Los Amantes**

The church of San Pedro contains the last of the four Mudéjar towers, and the mausoleum of the Lovers of Teruel — Diego and Isabel, the tragic medieval couple whose story has been told for centuries. A genuinely moving place, even if you don't know the legend.

address: Calle Matías Abad

category: Sight

**San Martín — Mudéjar Tower**

Probably the oldest of the four Mudéjar towers, very similar to El Salvador but without the option to ascend. Close to the Ayuntamiento and the Cathedral.

address: Calle de San Martín

category: Sight

**Acueducto de los Arcos**

A Renaissance aqueduct built to look Roman, crossing a valley on multiple arches. The lower level is pedestrianised — a distinctive structure worth seeing.

address: Camino Bajo los Arcos

category: Sight
