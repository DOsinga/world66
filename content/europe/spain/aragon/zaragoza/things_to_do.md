title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Zaragoza is an easy city to walk. The heart of it all is the Plaza del Pilar — one of Europe's largest public squares — with the enormous Basílica de Nuestra Señora del Pilar at one end, the older Catedral de la Seo at the other, and the Lonja in between. The old town west of the basilica is a dense grid of medieval lanes and tapas bars.

Beyond the obvious, Zaragoza has Roman ruins in a handful of excavated sites (the theatre, baths, and forum), the Moorish Aljafería palace on the western edge of the centre, and a string of Goya-related museums. Goya was born just outside the city and the Zaragoza museums hold some of his best work.
