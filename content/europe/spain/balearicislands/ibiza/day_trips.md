**Life is a Party**

For mirroring the high-spirited appeal of Ibiza go beach-club
hopping by hiring a private vehicle and sampling the party flavor of
each destination. The trip will give you a good essence of the region’s
penchant for having a beach-side blast without watching the time. Most
clubs are located in San Antonio and the main Ibiza town section. Pacha
and Space are two coveted spots that sport an infamously kitsch
attitude. Ibiza is worth visiting in order to embrace the hippie persona.

**Stunning Natural Beauty**

To make the most of your Ibiza tour, take a day trip to Sant Joan
Labritjain--an enjoyable drive that takes visitors through some of the
most beautiful and lesser known beaches of the island. The naturally
striking feature here is a raised coastline that offers splendid vistas
of the serene Mediterranean--superb stuff. There are several isolated
virginal beaches here that are inviting haunts for exploration and
secret escapades. For a taste of the high-profile lifestyle of Ibiza
take a trip to Santa EulÃ ria del Riu, located on the southern tip of
the island and watch the swanky villas with designer pools that are the
summer abodes of the rich and famous of Spain. The towns of San Carlos
and Port des Torrent are great ideas for a day trip to explore some
gorgeous beaches that adorn them. For vibrant flea markets that
epitomize the hippy culture of the tow, visit Punta Arabi and Las
Dalias--famous for the colorful local attire and beach wear. This is a great
place to bag super bargains. The locals are good conversationalists and
will even sell their wares in a sing-song manner in keeping with the
party spirit of the destination.

**History
**

For those who swear by their exploratory streak, the Cova Santa
Caves are a must visit for discovering a long forgotten rock enclosure
on a horseback. Go biking, hiking or trail walking though the
intimidating paths of the region and collect the ancient natural
keepsakes that are sprinkled all over the land mass. For a stronger
dose of history visit the 15 th century Puig D’Missa Fort to
the east of the town and the Living Museum that traces Ibiza’s brush
with cultural elegance that has faded into oblivion by its more
aggressive contemporary wild image.

Ibiza is a heady mix of pulsating beats, throbbing activity and an
audacious appeal on one end of the spectrum and serene, inactive bays
that lie unassumingly on the other.
