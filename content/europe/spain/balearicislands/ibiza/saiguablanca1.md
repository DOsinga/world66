S'Aigua Blanca is one of the longest stretches of sand on Ibiza's east coast, backed by low cliffs rather than hotels. The beach faces the open Mediterranean and catches good waves by Ibiza standards, though the water stays shallow enough to wade out a fair distance.

The northern end is clothing-optional and has been for decades — it's not officially designated nudist, but nobody bats an eye. A couple of simple chiringuitos serve drinks and grilled fish. The crowd is a mix of families at the southern end and a more free-spirited bunch toward the north.

Getting here requires a short drive from Santa Eulalia or Sant Carles, and parking fills up in high summer, so arrive before noon if you want a spot close to the sand.
