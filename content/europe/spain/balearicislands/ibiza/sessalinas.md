Ses Salinas is arguably the most iconic beach on Ibiza. A broad, white-sand stretch at the island's southern tip, it draws a reliably glamorous crowd — models, DJs, people who look like they haven't slept in days and don't care. Five beach bars line the sand, each with its own soundtrack and vibe.

Sa Trinxa is the local favourite: a tiny kitchen turning out beach burgers, pastas, and salads while DJs spin from afternoon into evening. Malibu Beach skews flashier, with table service and wine coolers planted in the sand. The Jockey Club, sheltered under a roof of palm leaves, has the best wine list and a terrace where the music drifts out over the water.

Walk east and the crowd thins — fewer families, more free spirits, and eventually a quiet nudist stretch at the far end of the bay. The views across to Formentera are stunning from anywhere along the beach, but especially from this quieter end.
