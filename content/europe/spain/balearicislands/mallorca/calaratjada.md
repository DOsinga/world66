Cala Ratjada is a fishing-port-turned-resort at the northeastern tip of Mallorca, the part of the island that has changed least from its pre-tourism character. The small harbour still has working fishing boats alongside the pleasure craft, and the surrounding area has some of the best beaches on the island — Cala Agulla to the north is a long arc of white sand backed by dunes and pine forest. Cap de Formentor and the rugged Llevant peninsula are within easy reach. The town itself is busier than it once was but retains a more local character than the megaResorts on the southern coast.
