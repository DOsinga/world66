Magaluf sits on the southwest coast of [Mallorca](/europe/spain/balearicislands/mallorca), about 15 kilometres from [Palma](/europe/spain/balearicislands/mallorca/palmademallorca). Its reputation as a package-holiday party strip is well earned — the main strip of bars and clubs along Carrer Punta Ballena is loud, cheap, and aimed squarely at young British and German holidaymakers. If that is not your scene, you have been warned.

That said, the beach itself is genuinely good — a long, wide arc of fine sand with calm, shallow water. The recent renovation of the seafront promenade has improved things considerably, and away from the strip, Magaluf is slowly reinventing itself with better hotels and a waterpark (Western Water Park) that keeps families entertained. The Katmandu Park theme park and the Pirates Adventure dinner show cater to a more family-friendly crowd.

Beyond the resort, the coastline to the south is worth exploring. The headland walk to the old watchtower at Cap de Cala Figuera takes about 20 minutes and gives views over the Bay of Palma. The Calvià coast stretches southwest toward Santa Ponsa and Peguera, with quieter coves if you want to escape the crowds.
