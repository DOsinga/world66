Set on a hillside overlooking a small natural harbour on [Mallorca's](/europe/spain/balearicislands/mallorca) east coast, Porto Cristo is a pretty resort with genuine old-world charm. The tree-lined esplanade along the waterfront is lined with bars and cafes, and a sandy beach sits at the head of the inlet.

Porto Cristo's biggest draw is the Coves del Drac (Caves of Drach), a spectacular system of underground caverns that leads to Lake Martel — one of the largest subterranean lakes in Europe. Classical music concerts are performed on the lake by musicians in illuminated boats, which is as theatrical as it sounds. The nearby Coves dels Hams are smaller but less crowded.
