Laguardia is a walled medieval village sitting on a low ridge above the vineyards of the Rioja Alavesa, with the long grey wall of the Sierra de Cantabria rising behind it to the north. The old town has barely changed since the fifteenth century — you enter through one of five gates, and the cars stay outside. Inside, narrow cobbled streets run between honey-coloured stone houses, past the Plaza Mayor and the church of Santa María de los Reyes, whose late-Gothic polychrome portal is one of the finest in Spain.

The real draw is the wine. Laguardia sits in the heart of Rioja Alavesa, and almost every house in the village has a bodega carved into the rock beneath it — some of them still in use for family production. Bigger, more visitable wineries surround the town on all sides, including Ysios (a dramatic wave-roofed cellar designed by Santiago Calatrava) and Marqués de Riscal's Frank Gehry-designed hotel a short drive away in Elciego. You can easily spend a couple of days here, walking the ramparts, tasting wines, and eating pintxos in the Plaza Mayor as the swifts come out over the roofs at dusk.
