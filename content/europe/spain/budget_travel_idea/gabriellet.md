**Spain budget travel** ideas begin with booking air travel to
Spain and attractions to visit. Plan the trip ahead of time. Use time and
resources wisely so you can enjoy the most from the trip. Save money using these
tips for budget travel to Spain.

**Cheap Flights to Spain**

Start Spain budget travel planning by booking the trip using three methods.
Find tickets from Europe to Spain. Lay over in [Europe](/europe)on your journey from [America](/northamerica/unitedstates)for cheaper airfare to Spain. Find connecting flights stopping in other
countries instead of a direct flight.

Book travel to Spain during the off season for cheaper airfare. Try finding
deals in winter instead of summer. Spain has moderate weather throughout the
year so visiting in winter is just as pleasant as summer. Fares are cheaper
during this time and you can experience winter festivals during this time
too.

Early flights are cheaper than flights later in the day. Book the travel a
few months in advance to find the best deals. Plan to leave in the earlier hours
of the morning for better rates. Consider a lay over and stops during the trip
to save money for Spain budget travel.

**Budget Living**

Book your living arrangements with a hostel for the most economic rooms in
Spain. Hostels in Spain feature stylish rooms with decent bunk beds and are
close to nightlife. Meet other travelers in the social setting of a hostel.
Rooms are shared with other visitors and kitchens are communal. Most beds cost
around 15 EUR while double rooms are available at the Oasis Backpacker Hostel
for 36 EUR. [Hotels in Spain](/europe/spain/accommodation) have reasonable prices but the cheapest option is
the hostel.

**Budget Eating**

Tapas are the popular way to save money on snacks in Spain. Enjoy a light
lunch and a beer for around 3 EUR. Tapas are really light dishes with one or
more items at a time. If you need more to eat, try looking for other options.
View the menu at restaurants before deciding to eat there. Fixed price menus for
lunch time start at 10 EUR and go up. Check out a few different restaurants
around business districts that cater to the budget friendly crowd.

**Budget Museums**

Attractions in Spain vary in price. Visit municipal museums since these are
free. Granada’s archaeological museum features exhibits from Spain’s history.
View the collection at no charge. If you are journeying to Barcelona, see the
mimes at the Ramblas for free entertainment.

**Budget Monuments**

Monuments in Spain are attractions that are available throughout the week.
Plan to go when they offer free days. See the Mosque on Cordoba free every
morning from 8:30 until 10. If you want to see the local cathedrals, attend the
mass in the morning. Mass is free but not every area of the cathedral may be
open for viewing.
