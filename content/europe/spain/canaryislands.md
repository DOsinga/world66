The Canary Islands are a Spanish archipelago of seven inhabited volcanic islands in the Atlantic, closer to Morocco than to mainland Spain. The latitude (28°N, about level with southern Florida) and the persistent trade winds give them the gentlest year-round climate in Europe — coastal temperatures bounce between 18°C in January and 25°C in August, with almost no rain on the south coasts of the big islands. That consistency, combined with cheap charter flights from northern Europe, has made the Canaries the continent's winter-sun destination of choice; more than twelve million visitors arrive each year.

The islands are dramatically different from one another despite their shared volcanic origin. [Tenerife](/europe/spain/canaryislands/tenerife) is the largest and most varied, with the 3,718-metre cone of **Teide** (the highest point in Spain) at its centre, old colonial towns like La Laguna in the north, and resort beaches along the south coast. [Gran Canaria](/europe/spain/canaryislands/grancanaria) has the most spectacular range of landscapes packed into a single island — pine forests, green valleys, desert dunes at Maspalomas, and the chaotic capital of Las Palmas with its own excellent old town. [Lanzarote](/europe/spain/canaryislands/lanzarote) is almost Martian — black lava, Timanfaya's still-smouldering volcanoes, and the distinctive green-shuttered architecture shaped by the artist César Manrique. [Fuerteventura](/europe/spain/canaryislands/fuerteventura) is the beach island — hundreds of kilometres of Saharan-style dunes and empty white sand, and some of the best windsurfing in Europe.

The three smaller western islands are quieter and more dramatic. [La Gomera](/europe/spain/canaryislands/lagomera) has the Garajonay cloud forest (a UNESCO site) and the **Silbo** whistling language that locals still use to communicate across its deep ravines. **La Palma** is "the beautiful island," a volcanic ridge covered in laurel forest and the darkest night sky in Europe — home to some of the world's most important optical observatories. **El Hierro** is the smallest, farthest west, and the most off-grid.

Canary food is distinct from mainland Spanish. **Papas arrugadas** (salty wrinkled new potatoes) with **mojo** sauce (red pepper or green coriander), **gofio** (toasted grain flour), and fresh grilled fish are on every menu. **Malvasía** wines from Lanzarote and Tenerife, grown in volcanic ash, are a genuine find.
