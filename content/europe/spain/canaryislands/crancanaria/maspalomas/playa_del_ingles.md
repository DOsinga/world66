Playa del Inglés is the largest resort on Gran Canaria's southern coast, stretching east from the [Maspalomas](/europe/spain/canaryislands/crancanaria/maspalomas) dunes to San Agustín. It is purpose-built for tourism — the town is essentially hotels, apartment complexes, and shopping centres — but the beach is genuinely impressive: several kilometres of wide golden sand backed by a boardwalk promenade.

The resort attracts a younger and more lively crowd than neighbouring Maspalomas, with a concentration of bars, clubs, and late-night restaurants in the Yumbo shopping centre and along the Avenida de Tirajana. It is also one of Europe's most popular LGBT destinations, particularly around the Yumbo area.

The beach connects westward to the Maspalomas dunes, a protected landscape of Saharan-looking sand dunes that you can walk through to reach the Maspalomas lighthouse. Eastward, the coast becomes rockier towards San Agustín, where the atmosphere is quieter and more upmarket.
