Valle Gran Rey is a steep valley on the western coast of La Gomera, one of the smaller Canary Islands. The valley drops dramatically from the central highlands to the sea, its terraced sides once covered in palm groves and banana plantations. Since the 1970s it has been a magnet for alternative travellers and long-stay visitors drawn by the warm winters, cheap rents, and unhurried pace.

The settlement is really a string of small villages. La Playa at the bottom has a black sand beach, a handful of restaurants, and a small harbour where ferries run to the other islands. Vueltas is the fishing port, with fresh catch sold straight off the boats. Higher up, La Calera clings to the valley wall with spectacular views. The whole area is excellent for walking — trails lead up through the terraces to the laurel forests of Garajonay National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Valle Gran Rey remains one of the most relaxed corners of the Canaries, a world away from the mass tourism of Tenerife's southern coast.
