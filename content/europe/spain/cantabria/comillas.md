Comillas is a small coastal town in Cantabria that punches far above its weight architecturally. In the late 19th century it became a summer resort for the Spanish royal family and the aristocracy, and the rich patrons who followed brought some of the most interesting modernist architecture in Spain. The result is a town where ornate Catalan Modernisme sits alongside cobbled medieval streets and a working fishing harbour.

The unmissable building is El Capricho, a Gaudí villa built in 1885 — one of only a handful of his works outside Catalonia. The ceramic sunflower tiles on its tower and the extraordinary organic detailing make it a must-see for anyone interested in architecture. Nearby, the Sobrellano Palace by Joan Martorell and the neo-Gothic Pontifical University of Comillas complete the remarkable trio of late 19th-century buildings clustered on the hill above town.

The beaches around Comillas are good — Playa de Comillas is a fine arc of sand — and the town is well positioned for day trips along the Cantabrian coast, including the prehistoric cave paintings at Altamira (about 25 kilometres east) and the dramatically situated Picos de Europa mountains inland.
