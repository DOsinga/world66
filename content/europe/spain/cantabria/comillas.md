Comillas is a small coastal town in Cantabria that has an architectural legacy completely out of proportion to its size. In the late nineteenth century, the Marquis of Comillas — who had made a fortune in Cuba — brought the leading architects of the day here to build grand monuments in what was essentially a fishing village. The result is one of the most surprising collections of buildings on the Spanish coast, set against a backdrop of green hills and the Cantabrian Sea.

The star attraction is El Capricho, an early work by Antoni Gaudi that is one of only a handful of his buildings outside Catalonia. It is a fantastical summer house covered in ceramic sunflower tiles, with a tower that looks like a minaret and interiors full of Gaudi's characteristic playfulness. Nearby, the Pontifical University — a massive neo-Gothic pile on the hilltop designed by Joan Martorell — dominates the skyline and now houses a conference center. The Sobrellano Palace and its chapel, designed by Martorell with furniture by Gaudi, round out an extraordinary architectural triangle within walking distance of each other.

Beyond the architecture, Comillas is simply a lovely place to spend a day or two. The town beach is a beautiful curve of golden sand with the ruined medieval cemetery of the Ermita de Santa Juliana perched on the headland above it — one of the most photographed spots in Cantabria. The old town is compact and walkable, with stone houses, good seafood restaurants, and a calm pace of life that feels a world away from the busier resorts to the east.

Comillas works well as a stop on a road trip along the Cantabrian coast between Santander and the Picos de Europa. The drive in either direction is spectacular, hugging cliffs and dipping into green valleys. If you only have time for one small town in Cantabria, this is a strong contender — you will not find a place of this size anywhere else in Spain with architecture of this caliber.
