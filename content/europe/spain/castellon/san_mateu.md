Sant Mateu is a small medieval town in the interior of Castellón province in Valencia, Spain. It was once the capital of the Maestrat region and served as the seat of the Knights Templar in the area, which explains the grandeur of its Gothic church and the scale of its arcaded main square relative to its modest size today.

The town is a worthwhile detour if you are travelling between Valencia and the Ebro Delta or Tarragona. The stone arcades of the main square are well preserved, and the regional museum has a good collection documenting the area's medieval history. It is a calm, unhurried place that sees relatively few tourists despite its architectural interest.
