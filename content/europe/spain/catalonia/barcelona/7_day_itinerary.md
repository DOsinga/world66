---
title: '7 Day Itinerary'
type: section
---

A week gives you time to see Barcelona properly, mixing the big sights with neighbourhood exploration and a day trip or two.

**Day 1 - La Rambla and the Gothic Quarter.** Start at Placa de Catalunya and walk down the Ramblas, stopping at La Boqueria market for breakfast. Spend the afternoon getting lost in the Barri Gotic, visiting the Cathedral and Placa del Rei. End with tapas in the Born neighbourhood.

**Day 2 - Sagrada Familia and the Eixample.** Give the Sagrada Familia the time it deserves. Book tickets in advance. Afterwards, walk down Passeig de Gracia to see Casa Batllo and La Pedrera. The Eixample's grid streets are full of good restaurants for dinner.

**Day 3 - Montjuic.** Take the cable car or walk up to Montjuic for the Miro Foundation, the Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya, the Olympic Stadium and Poble Espanyol. If you are visiting on a summer evening, the Font Magica light and water show is spectacular.

**Day 4 - Barceloneta and the waterfront.** Spend the morning at the Picasso Museum in El Born, then walk through Barceloneta to the beach. Have a long seafood lunch at one of the waterfront restaurants. Stroll the promenade to the Port Olimpic.

**Day 5 - Park Guell and Gracia.** Visit Park Guell in the morning (book timed tickets). Walk downhill into the Gracia neighbourhood for lunch, then spend the afternoon browsing its independent shops and sitting in its village squares.

**Day 6 - Day trip.** Take the train to [Montserrat](/europe/spain/catalonia/montserrat) for the mountain monastery, or to [Sitges](/europe/spain/catalonia/sitges) for a beach day, or to [Figueres](/europe/spain/catalonia/figueres) for the Dali Museum.

**Day 7 - Your Barcelona.** Go back to the neighbourhood you liked best. Revisit that tapas bar, sit in that square, walk those streets one more time. Barcelona is a city that rewards return visits, even within the same trip.
