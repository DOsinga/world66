---
title: 'Museums'
type: section
---

Barcelona punches well above its weight in museums. The Picasso Museum, housed in five medieval palaces along Carrer Montcada, holds a rich collection of the artist's early work alongside his famous cubist variations on Velazquez's Las Meninas. The Fundacio Joan Miro on Montjuic is both a fine collection and a beautiful building, with terraces overlooking the city.

The Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya, in the grand Palau Nacional, spans Romanesque frescoes to modernisme. For something more unusual, the Museu Maritim in the medieval shipyards of the Drassanes gives a sense of Barcelona's maritime past, and the MACBA in El Raval is the city's showcase for contemporary art.
