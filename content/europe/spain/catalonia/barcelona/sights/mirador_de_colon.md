At the end of La Rambla, near the sea, you will find the Mirador de Colon, a great work built in 1888 for the Universal Exhibition as a reminder of the discovery of America.

The work combines stone, iron and bronze, and consists of a column that rises from a base sculpted with the figures of prominent people related to the discovery. At the top, the sculpture of Columbus points the sea. Inside the column, which measures 2.25 meters in diameter, there is a lift that goes up to the viewing gallery. Ride up and enjoy a panoramic view of Barcelona.

At sixty meters above the ground, the view of the city is a wonderful sight. You will be able to identify the historic buildings of the city between the mountain and the sea: admire the new seafront, the new road links, and the new skyline. Take a walk all around the viewing gallery and discover the new Barcelona.

**If you begin by looking towards the North**

The area of the Old Town, with its characteristic Cathedral bell towers and the churches of Santa Maria del Mar and La Mercè the patron saint of the city. The popular Rambla of Barcelona goes up to the Placa de Catalunya and gives way to the district of the Eixample with its Quadrat d'Or (golden square), which is a real open-air museum of Modernism, where the temple of the Sagrada Familia, a symbol of Gaudi's Modernism, stands out.

**And if you continue towards the East…**

The first thing that you see will be the sea, and then the new Barcelona seafront: the Port Veil, where a large shopping and leisure-time center including the IMAX, I'Aquàrium, and the Maremagnum with all kinds of shops, restaurants and amusements can be found. In the distance, twin towers mark the entrance to the Olympic Harbor and the Olympic Village. Over 4 kilometers of beaches extend along the coast.

**Now look towards the South**

Here you will see the mountain of Montjuic. Crowned by the castle, it is home to museums, gardens, theatres and the Olympic Ring, where the Palau Sant Jordi and the Olympic Stadium are located. At the foot of the mountain, the avinguda del Parallel can be seen with its three chimneys, as con the Drassones, the fourteenth-century royal shipyards: the largest and best-conserved shipyards in the world. They now house the Museu Maritim, The World Trade Center, a modern business center encircled by the sea, stands out.

**And end by looking towards West…**

You will see the big Collserola Natural Park, the immense city's lung, with the Torre de Collserola and the Tibidabo Amusement Park as prominent features. Farther down on the left, you con see a new business area with the avinguda de la Diagonal as the main artery.
