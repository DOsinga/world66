A Moroccan-style tea room tucked into a side street in Gràcia, the kind of low-lit, cushioned space where you can spend an afternoon over a pot of mint tea and a plate of pastries without anyone rushing you. Barcelona has a handful of these — teterías that arrived with the city's long interest in Arab-Andalusian culture — and Jazmín is among the more atmospheric ones. The menu runs to a dozen varieties of tea, hot chocolate and light sweets.
