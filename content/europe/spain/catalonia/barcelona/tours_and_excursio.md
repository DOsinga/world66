---
title: 'Tours and Excursions'
type: section
---

Barcelona rewards exploration on foot, and you can easily build your own walking tour through the city's distinct neighbourhoods.

Start in the Gothic Quarter around the Cathedral, the Royal Palace and the ancient government buildings: the City Hall and the Palau de la Generalitat. Nearby, the Ribera district (El Born) holds the Picasso Museum and the magnificent Gothic church of Santa Maria del Mar. The Ciutadella Park, with its zoo, museums and the Catalan Parliament, is a short walk further east. From here, follow the seafront to La Barceloneta, the 18th-century fishermen's quarter now lined with seafood restaurants.

The Rambla is essential, day or night. Stroll past the Liceu opera house, the flower and bird stalls, and the newspaper kiosks. The atmosphere changes by the hour as the crowd shifts from morning market-goers to evening revellers.

Head up the Passeig de Gracia to admire the Modernist masterpieces: the block of discord where Gaudi, Puig i Cadafalch and Domenech i Montaner compete for attention, and Gaudi's extraordinary La Pedrera with its undulating facade. Beyond the Eixample, the Sagrada Familia rises on the city's northern edge, and Park Guell crowns the hillside above.

For views, take the old-fashioned Blue Tram and funicular up to Tibidabo, or head to Montjuic where landscaped gardens, the Miro Foundation, the Olympic Stadium and the Poble Espanyol make for a full day.
