---
title: 'When to Go'
type: section
---

Barcelona's Mediterranean climate makes any season visitable, but **spring (April–June)** and **autumn (September–October)** are the best windows: temperatures of 18–25°C, manageable crowds, the city in full daily rhythm. **August** is the worst time — peak heat and humidity, many local shops and restaurants closed for the month, and the city handed over to tourists. **Winter** is mild (7–14°C), often sunny, and increasingly popular with travellers wanting Gaudí without the queues.

Barcelona celebrates with intensity, and timing a visit around a festival is rewarding. The biggest is **La Mercè** in late September — a week-long takeover of the old town with music, fire runs (correfocs) and the towering human castles called castellers. **Sant Jordi** on April 23rd is Catalonia's own day of books and roses, when the Ramblas becomes one long open-air market. The **Sónar** festival in mid-June draws electronic-music fans from across Europe; **Sant Joan** on the night of June 23rd brings bonfires and fireworks to the beaches. The **Grec** summer festival programmes outdoor theatre, music and dance through July and August.

For traditional Catalan culture year-round, look out for castellers, correfocs and sardana dancing in the squares — these are not tourist shows but living traditions. See [Catalonia Festivals](/europe/spain/catalonia/festivals) for events across the wider region.
