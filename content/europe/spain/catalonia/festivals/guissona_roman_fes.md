In July (date varies), as the afternoon heat sizzles away to leave a
warm and soothing evening, the roman town of Guissona, situated 100km
from Barcelona, prepares for the Battle of the Gladiators. This
adrenalin provoking contest takes place in the town’s centre square
where two Roman warriors duel to their theatrical deaths, representing
the final and most striking event at the annual Roman Sun Festival of
Guissona. The winding streets are converted into an emporium of
colourful stalls, Roman dancing and slave auctions.
