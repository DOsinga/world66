El Vendrell, situated 80 km from Barcelona, is brought to life with the festival spirit at the end of July as giants dance and twirl throughout the sunny morning streets. Straight after, lively local footwork can be viewed in the main town square which includes the Stick, Devil and Gypsy Dances. A hushed and expectant silence descends over the town as the Vendrell team of Human Castles begins the hair raising ascent skyward in a spectacular demonstration of the unique Catalan Human Castle tradition. Human Tower formations stem from 17th century Valencian dance in which a small number of people balanced on each other’s shoulders. It has since evolved into the massively celebrated Catalan event that it is today.
