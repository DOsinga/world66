Mataró is a coastal city about 30 km northeast of [Barcelona](/europe/spain/catalonia/barcelona), the capital of the Maresme comarca. It was the terminus of Spain's first railway line, opened in 1848, and today the Cercanías train still makes it an easy day trip from Barcelona.

The old town has a pleasant pedestrianised centre with a baroque basilica and the remains of a Roman villa with well-preserved mosaic floors. The beaches are wide and sandy, less crowded than Barcelona's, and the seafront promenade is good for a walk. Mataró is also known for its modernist architecture — the Nau Gaudí, an early industrial building by a young Antoni Gaudí, can be visited.
