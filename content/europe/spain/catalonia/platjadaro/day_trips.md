---
title: "Day Trips in Platja d'Aro"
type: section
---

**Platja d’Aro Day Trips** are one of the main highlights of this Spanish region. With the entertainment and facilities this place offers, there is no real need to move away from it. However, for those who like to do a little sightseeing, planning a day trip to neighboring areas can be a great idea. There are a number of tourist attractions around Platja d’Aro that are within a day’s travel.

**Palafrugell

**This is another popular place about 5 miles from Platja d’Aro that tourists can visit. Palafrugell is a small, relaxed and beautiful town that is set on a broad bay which has wooded hills and rocky inlets. Palafrugell can be accessed by road and boat. Visiting the 16th century church of Sant Marti and lighthouse at Cap Sant Sebastian in Palafrugell is a must. It is a unique town that has narrow streets, traditional buildings, fine old shops and waterfront restaurants and bars.

**Llafranc
**
Llafranc is a beautiful coastal town near Palafrugell. Tourists can easily travel here while holidaying in Platja d’Aro. With beautiful surroundings and a fine sandy beach, Llafranc is an upmarket, quiet and small region located on Costa Brava. This intimate town boasts of hillside villas and whitewashed streets which give the area its Mediterranean character and charm. The Gothic old center of Gerona, botanical gardens at Cap Roig and the medieval town of Pals are some of the places to visit here. Besides this, tourists can enjoy a variety of seafood, take boat rides, soak up the sun on the beach or simply relax at the many restaurants and bars in this gorgeous town.

**Tamariu

**For a really quiet holiday, visiting the town of Tamariu is a good idea. You may want to start off for this town a little early as it is located about 7 miles from Platja d’Aro. This charming region falls in the Palafrugell municipality. It has a Mediterranean styled village, tiny coves, scenic wooded hills, a sparkling blue sea and steep cliffs. Tourists can enjoy a range of water sports like waterskiing, sailing and diving. Some of the places to visit in this town include the old Catalan hilltop town of Begur, the ancient remains at Empuries, the monastery at Montserrat and the Salvador Dali Museum. Tamariu also has a number of beautiful costal paths that tourists can use to explore this gorgeous scenic stretch.
