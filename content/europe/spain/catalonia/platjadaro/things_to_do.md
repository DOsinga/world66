---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The beaches are the main draw: Platja Gran is the central sandy beach with lifeguards and water sports, while smaller coves dot the coastline on either side. The tourist office organises free guided walks of Castell d'Aro's old town and the coastal path (Camí de Ronda) year-round. Benedormiens Castle, built in 1041 and substantially ruined, is open to visit and now hosts art exhibitions in its partially restored rooms — the moat and outer defensive works are visible from the excavations. A Doll Museum in the old town centre displays ceramic, wood, and cloth dolls from several countries. In May and June the wine and cava festival brings local Costa Brava producers to the town centre with tastings.
