**Platja d'Aro** enjoys a typical
Mediterranean **climate**, with very mild winters and warm
summers. The following information will help you know more about **Platja d'Aro and when to go.
****
Weather **

The winter temperature, especially
between December and February, will normally dip to 13C (55F).

Spring season begins in early March,
and the temperature increases to 15C (59F), then to approximately 18C
(65) in April, and as the days become longer, to 20C (68F) in May.
The water temperature begins to rise as well so locals begin swimming
again around the end of April.
In June and July, the temperature is a
balmy 25C (77F) and the water temperature is perfect for swimming.
August is the warmest month here, with temperatures rising up to and
sometimes above 30C (86F). Nighttime temperatures remain around 17C
(63F) in summer months.
September's temperature begins its slow
descent for Fall, with an average of 27C (81F) and 23C (73.5F) in
October. October and November also have increased rainfalls of
approximately 180 to 190 mm (0.70 to 0.75 inch) per month.

**

Activities **

Platja d'Aro offers activities, guided
tours and festivals throughout the year.
**

February **

There is a carnival in February. There
are more than four thousand participants and seventy carnival groups
taking part in the Great Cavalcade of Floats and Participants.
**

March and September **

In March and September, the town offers
a Street Shopping day where stores open stalls in the streets and
offer unbeatable bargains from 10 am until 10 pm.
**

May **

Taking place in May and June is the
Wine and Cava Festival, where you can sample regional and
international wines as well as patés,
local and typical Spanish sausages, ham and cheeses and traditional
smoked meats.
**

June **

On June 23rd,
you can view an amazing display of fireworks. This celebration marks
the beginning of the summer season.
**

July and August **

Throughout July and
August,
for the Platja d'Aro Te Premi!, seashells
are thrown into the sea along the main beach, Sa Conca and Cala
Rovira. People who find these shells bring them to the tourism
office, where they draw an envelope with a price in it, from caps and
t-shirts to discounts in restaurants, admission for the Fun Parks and
even hotel nights. All participants also fill a contest ticket to be
entered to win a car in December. Merchants also offer contest
tickets to their customers.
**

October **

In October, the Beer
Festival is held in the center of town and welcomes 15,000 visitors.

**

December

**In December, the public
can take part in the lighting of the Christmas decorations and view a
live nativity scene.
