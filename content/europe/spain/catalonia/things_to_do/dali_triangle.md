Salvador Dalí was born, worked, and is buried in Catalonia, and the three sites that formed his personal mythology are all within a day's drive of each other. The Teatre-Museu Dalí in Figueres is the most visited museum in Spain outside Madrid; the castle at Púbol he gave to his wife Gala; and the house at Portlligat near Cadaqués is where he lived and painted for most of his life. All three are administered by the Fundació Gala-Salvador Dalí and require advance booking, especially in summer.
