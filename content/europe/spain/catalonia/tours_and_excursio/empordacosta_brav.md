Empordà-Costa Brava, a Northern wine region, its Northern boundaries are Roussillon and France and its Southern boundary is the Baix Empordà. It is one of the Catalan regions with the oldest ancestral culture and the most illustrious origins. Empordà comes from Emporion, today's Empúries, the ancient Greco-Roman settlement. It was from here, by way of Greece and Rome, that the future agricultural and gastronomic activities of Catalonia originated, based on the so-called "Mediterranean Trilogy" of wheat, grapes and olives.
Whilst the oil, which was excellent, has disappeared recently, the bread of the Empordà, immortalized by Salvador Dalí of Figueres, is of the finest quality, and as far as the wines are concerned, their apellation d'origine has an excellent reputation (D.O. Empordà-Costa Brava).
Garnacha and Carineña have been the heart of red wine production in this region particularly for making sweet wines. Nowadays French varieties have been introduced such as Canbernet Sauvignon, Syrah, and Merlot for red wine and, Chardonnay and Chenin for white wine. As this is also very much a cava county, the three local Catalan white grapes are to be found in most vineyards.
In addition, from ancient times comes the legacy (as is proved by the archaeological excavations in Roses, Empúries, etc.) of the unsurpassable art of salting fish, proof of which are the highly appreciated anchovies of L'Escala with its denomination of quality DQ Anxoves de l'Escala as well as the domestic production in Cadaqués and Roses.
This region, whose capital is Figueres, the city of the Tramuntana (the Northern Wind) and Salvador Dalí, combines in a surprising way two distinct yet related landscapes (the Mermaid and the Shepherd, as recite the lyrics of the popular sardana, L'Empordà): in other words, the sea and the mountain. In a similar fashion the Pyrenees perish in the wild landscape of cape Creus, Thus, the cooking of this region takes advantage both of the produce of the mountains and the interior (spring lamb, game, ducks and geese), as well as the produce of the sea, For this reason, the cooking of the Empordà, through combinations called "Surf and Turf' or fish and Meat are able to marry both worlds in the most imaginative way, It is not strange therefore that this region is considered to have the finest of Catalan gastronomy.
