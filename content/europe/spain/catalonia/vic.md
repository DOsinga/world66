Vic is a handsome cathedral city about 60 kilometres north of [Barcelona](/europe/spain/catalonia/barcelona), sitting on a plain ringed by low mountains at the heart of the Osona comarca. It has been an episcopal seat since the 6th century, and the old town is dense with Romanesque and Gothic churches, medieval arcades, and mansions with painted facades.

The centrepiece is the Plaça Major, one of the finest porticoed squares in Catalonia, which fills with market stalls on Tuesdays and Saturdays — as it has for over a thousand years. The cathedral, rebuilt in neoclassical style, is covered inside with sweeping murals by Josep Maria Sert that are worth the visit alone. The Episcopal Museum nearby holds one of the best collections of Romanesque art outside Barcelona.

Vic is also the capital of Catalan cured meats. The local fuet and llonganissa are famous across Spain, and the surrounding farmland produces some of the region's best artisan food. It makes a good day trip from Barcelona or a stop on the way to the Pyrenees.
