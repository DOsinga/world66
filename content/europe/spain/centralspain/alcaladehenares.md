Alcalá de Henares is a university city 35 kilometres east of Madrid, and one of the best day trips from the capital. Founded as a Roman settlement and later a medieval Moorish town, it reached its golden age when Cardinal Cisneros established the Complutense University here in 1499 — making it one of the first planned university cities in the world. The entire historic centre is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The city is famous as the birthplace of Miguel de Cervantes, author of Don Quixote. His family home on Calle Mayor has been turned into a museum that reconstructs a 16th-century household with period furniture and explanatory exhibits. Nearby, the Renaissance university building itself is worth visiting for its ornate Plateresque facade and the Paraninfo auditorium where the Cervantes Prize — Spain's most prestigious literary award — is handed out each year by the King.

Alcalá is compact and easily walkable. The main street leads from the train station through the historic centre past colonnaded arcades, convents, and colleges. It takes two to three hours to see the highlights properly; combine it with a meal and you can easily spend a half day here. Trains from Madrid Atocha run every 15–20 minutes and take about 45 minutes.
