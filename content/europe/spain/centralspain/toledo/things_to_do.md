---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Toledo is so packed with sights that the best approach is simply to wander and get lost in the narrow medieval streets. The tourist train (really an articulated bus) offers a good overall view of the city from across the Tagus and is worth considering unless you have several nights.

The **cathedral** is of massive historical importance, combining Moorish, Gothic, and Renaissance elements. The El Grecos in the sacristy and the extraordinary carved choir stalls alone justify the ticket. Two of Spain's three surviving **synagogues** are here: Santa María la Blanca, the older one, resembles a mosque; El Tránsito has a museum covering Jewish life in Toledo up to the 15th-century expulsion.

**El Greco** is everywhere. The El Greco House and Museum is a good starting point, and then you will want to see *The Burial of the Count of Orgaz* in Santo Tomé. The tiny former mosque of **Santo Cristo de la Luz** is worth seeking out, and the Hospital de Santa Cruz houses an excellent art collection. After dark, the floodlit city is spectacular — consider a second trip on the tourist train at night.
