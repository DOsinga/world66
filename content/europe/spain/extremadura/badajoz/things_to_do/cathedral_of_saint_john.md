Built like a fortress after Alfonso IX took the city in the 13th century, the cathedral is topped by battlements and anchored by a high square tower. Inside, the basilica plan has three naves with Decadent Gothic arches. The main entrance on Plaza de San Juan is crowned by a statue of John the Baptist. Its military appearance is a reminder that Badajoz spent most of its history as a contested frontier town.
