The Museo Extremeno e Iberoamericano de Arte Contemporaneo was built over the shell of a prison used during the Franco regime and opened in 1996. The collection focuses on Spanish, Portuguese, and Latin American contemporary art from the 1980s onward, displayed across four floors and a semi-basement. One of the more interesting modern art museums in western Spain, and the building's history gives it an edge that purpose-built galleries lack.
