---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Caceres Old Town**

Still surrounded by its ancient walls, this "Ciudad Monumental" is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Walk through its narrow, winding streets for a taste of the past. It has a ghost town-like quality which only adds sensation of being back in time. You won't see any sign of modern life, even now. The area has been occupied since prehistoric times, but the town was founded by the Romans is 25 B.C. The architecture is a unique mix of Northern Gothic, Italian Renaissance, Roman, and Islamic elements.

**2. The Roman Amphitheater
**

In this UNESCO World Heritage Site in Merida, there are various areas of the ruins to explore; winding, well-labeled paths lead to them all. The ruins have been kept in beautiful shape, making it much easier to imagine what they would've looked like when they were in use. From July to August is the best time to visit, because shows, dramas, and other performances are held in the amphitheater itself. Kids tend to be endlessly entertained by the Roman Amphitheater.

**3. Royal Monastery of Santa Maria de Guadalupe**

This imposing monastery was built in 1340 and supposedly started the cult of the Virgin of Guadalupe. It is another of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites and was constructed in a Gothic-Mudejar style. Don't miss the Mudejar Cloister, the Embroidery Museum, or the Museum of Sculpture and Paintings. Especially necessary to see is the statue of the Virgin located in the Camarin.

**4. Restaurante Atrio**

Get ready for some mouthwatering food! If you're hungry, this is just the place to go for a high quality meal in the heart of Extremadura. The self-taught chef of the restaurant has a passion for using only authentic, local products in his creations. Every one of his dishes features Extremadura Olive Oil; it has such a strong flavor and such a green color that it almost looks like a juice!

**5. National Museum of Roman Art**

Pay the standard rate of 3 Euros to visit this museum, or go for free on Saturday afternoons, Sunday mornings, and holidays. Be sure to bring some form of identification as well, you need it to get in. You'll explore archeological finds, paintings, Roman sculpture, pottery, and much more in this 4 level museum. In the crypt, you can even get to see a context for the archeological pieces shown on the higher floors.
