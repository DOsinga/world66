Galicia is the area covered by the far north-west of Spain. It is bi-lingual though its language is by no means as popular as Catalan in the north-east. Gales is related to Portuguese but is perhaps most noted for its profusion of 'x's in place names. The O and A forms of the definite article are also found in place names. 

It is a beautiful area where the coast is unspoilt - building being mainly homes for Galicians returning from 'exile' after making a bit of cash. However it is not a coincidence that it is not developed for tourism like the well known Costas of other parts. Along with other parts of north Spain which lie west of France, Galicia has a climate which it takes from the Atlantic and it's distinctly dodgy. 

The largest city is [Vigo](/europe/spain/northernspain/vigo) and the most popular is probably [Santiago de Compostela](/europe/spain/northernspain/santiagodecompostela) but the provincial capital of [Pontevedra](/europe/spain/northernspain/pontevedra) shouln't be missed and A Coruña in the north is a fine city. 

Much of the inland scenery is mountainous and the rivers are delightful.
