---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Arbo's attractions are modest but genuine. The Wine Museum (Museo do Viño) introduces visitors to the wine-making traditions of the Condado de Tea sub-zone of the Rías Baixas denomination. Os Muiños da Rocha — restored 18th-century flour mills along the Deva river — now function as an ethnographic park demonstrating traditional corn-grinding. Mount San Fins, at 700 metres, rewards the climb with views across the Deva valley and hosts a traditional festival on 1 August. The Lamprey Eel Festival in February or March is the town's most famous event, drawing visitors to taste lamprey cooked in traditional styles alongside local wines.
