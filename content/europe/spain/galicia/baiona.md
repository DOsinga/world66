Baiona is a small harbour town on the southern shore of the Ría de Vigo, in the southwest corner of Galicia. It holds a notable place in history: on 1 March 1493, the Pinta arrived here carrying news of Columbus's first voyage to the Americas — making Baiona the first town in Europe to learn of the discovery.

The old town clusters around the harbour, its narrow streets lined with stone houses, seafood restaurants, and the occasional granite church. The waterfront promenade is lively in summer, and the town beach at Praia da Ribeira is good for a quick swim. Better beaches lie a short drive south along the coast toward Oia.

The main sight is the Monterreal fortress, now a parador, which occupies a rocky peninsula jutting into the bay. You can walk the fortress walls for free — the circuit takes about twenty minutes and the views over the Cíes Islands and the mouth of the ría are excellent. A replica of the Pinta is moored in the harbour and can be visited.

Baiona is a popular day trip from [Vigo](/europe/spain/galicia/vigo), about twenty kilometres to the north, but it rewards a longer stay if you want a quieter base for exploring the Rías Baixas coast.
