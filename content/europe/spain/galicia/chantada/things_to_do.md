---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Chantada's main draws are its wine country landscape and the Roman and medieval history embedded in the countryside around it. The town centre is a starting point rather than a destination in itself.

The **Roman road** from Chantada to Monforte de Lemos is one of the better-preserved stretches in Galicia. The route passes through Lincora and crosses a bridge that disappears into the Embalse de Belesar when water levels are high — an oddly dramatic sight. It makes a good half-day walk.

The **Embalse de Belesar** is the largest reservoir in Galicia. When water levels are low enough, a catamaran runs from Belesar down the Miño to O Peares — a fine way to see the terraced Ribeira Sacra vineyards from the water. Check locally for current schedules.

The village of **Belesar** is worth a stroll. Created when the dam flooded the original settlement, it still has lanes too narrow for cars and quiet corners overlooking the reservoir.

The **Ermita do Faro** sits high on the Serra do Faro with wide views over the valley. The abandoned church at **Pesqueiras**, down near the river, is a more melancholy sight — left to the vines and the weather.
