Lugo's old town contains several churches worth a detour. The parish church of San Pedro
occupies the former temple of the convent of San Francisco, notable for its Gothic apse and
façade. The convent of Santo Domingo was founded in the 13th century and substantially
altered in the 17th; its surviving church is today the parish of Santiago. The Baroque
period left its mark on the church of San Froilán, the church of the Carmen and the chapel
of San Roque. Outside the city walls, the Romanesque churches of Meilán, Bacerín and Mazo
are worth seeking out, along with the Baroque church at the Burgo.
