  On 30 November 2000, UNESCO declared the Roman Wall of Lugo a World Heritage Site — the
  only Roman wall in the world that still conserves its entire perimeter intact. In the year

Built between 260 and 310 AD, Lugo's Roman wall is unique in Europe: it is the only one that
retains its full original perimeter of more than two kilometres. The wall stands between 8 and
12 metres high and is reinforced by over 70 of the original 85 round turrets. Ten gates pierce
the circuit — four of Roman origin, the rest opened as the city grew over the centuries. A
sentry path runs the full length of the top, offering sweeping views over the old city, the
Miño river valley and the hills beyond.
