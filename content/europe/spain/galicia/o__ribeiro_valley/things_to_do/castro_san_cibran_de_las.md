title: "Castro San Cibrán de Lás"
type: poi
category: Sight
latitude: 42.3340

---

One of the most impressive Celtic hill-forts in Galicia, located in San Amaro overlooking the Miño valley. Discovered by Florentino Cuevillas in 1922, the site stretches 380 metres long and 314 metres wide, with a double ring of fortified enclosures. Inside you can trace the outlines of stone dwellings — circular ones built by the Celts and rectangular ones from the Roman period. The fort was inhabited from the 2nd century BC to the 2nd century AD and is thought to have sheltered over 8,000 people at its peak.
