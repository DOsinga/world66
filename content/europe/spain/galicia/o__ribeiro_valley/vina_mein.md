A winery set in a traditional Galician pazo (country manor) at the heart of the Ribeiro region in San Clodio-Leiro. Viña Meín revived old winemaking traditions when it was founded in 1988, growing exclusively indigenous white grape varieties — Treixadura, Godello, Loureira, Torrontés, and Albariño — across 12 hectares of vineyard. Their Casal de Meín wines are built predominantly on Treixadura (around 60 percent), producing aromatic, silky whites with a characteristic bitter finish. The pazo itself is worth visiting for its enormous covered balcony overlooking the vines and a lookout perched on a huge boulder above the house.
