Building García Flórez is an 18th-century urban palace ordered by perpetual alderman Antonio García Estévez Fariña. The style — gable wall with arcades, high balconies — points to the Compostelan school of architecture. Restored by Fernández Cochón and opened in 1942, it houses the museum's third section: minor arts, naval galleries, Compostelan engraving, San Telmo's Chapel, and the celebrated Castelao gallery.
