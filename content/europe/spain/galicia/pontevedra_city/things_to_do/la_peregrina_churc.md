La Peregrina Church was erected in 1778 and its circular ground plan is designed to imitate the form of a scallop shell — the symbol of the Camino de Santiago. Inside, a neoclassical altarpiece is presided by the image of the Pilgrim Virgin. The shell used as a holy water font is a large specimen brought from the Pacific by admiral Méndez Núñez.
