The Santo Domingo convent was founded by Friar Preachers at the end of the 13th century. What remains — five apses, the headpieces, and a wall — is considered the finest example of Gothic architecture in Galicia. An 18th-century attempt to remodel it in Neoclassical style was never completed. The interior, maintained by the Pontevedra Museum, holds an exhibition of coats of arms, medieval sepulchres, Roman steles, and Romanesque pieces brought from temples around Galicia.
