Santa María Basilica was commissioned by the Guild of Mareantes in the 16th century. Its façade — designed by Cornelius of Holland and Joao Noble — is one of the masterpieces of Galician Plateresque architecture, carved like a great stone altarpiece. San Pedro and San Pablo flank the door; above, the Transit of the Virgin; at the top, the figure of the Assumption. Historical figures including Columbus and Hernán Cortés also appear along the main front.
