The Ribeira Sacra is where the Sil and Miño rivers have carved deep gorges through the hills of inland [Galicia](/europe/spain/galicia). Steep canyon walls drop hundreds of metres to green water, terraced vineyards cling to impossible slopes, and medieval monasteries sit on the high ground above — the "sacred banks" that give the region its name.

The wine is reason enough to visit. The Ribeira Sacra DO vineyards are among the steepest in Europe, worked mostly by hand, producing fresh Mencía reds and mineral Godello whites. But the landscape is the real draw: take a catamaran through the Sil canyon, drive the narrow rim roads between viewpoints at Cabezoas and Balcones de Madrid, or walk down through chestnut forest to the Mosteiro de Santa Cristina de Ribas de Sil.

The region is best reached by car from [Ourense](/europe/spain/galicia/ourense) (45 minutes) or Santiago de Compostela (90 minutes). Monforte de Lemos is the main town and a good base.
