There are many sights
in the Ribeira Sacra.

If you were to list
all of the places of worship like churches and monasteries the list would be
over a hundred points long. So here is a very brief insight to give you a
taste. The church of Santo Estevo de Ribas de Miño, the church of Pesqueiras,
the monastery of Santo Estevo de Ribas de Sil, The monastery at Monforte de
Lemos, the convent at Panton, the church at Diamondi, the church at A Cova, the
church at Belesar.

There are many viewpoints
too like the one at A Cova, on the Rio Miño, the one at Vilouxe on the Rio Sil.
To name but two.

You could visit the
Chestnut trees, which are over a 1000 years old.

There the domains, stone
carvings, standing stones and Castros from the pre history phase of this
landscape.

Follow the old roman
roads on foot or by mountain bike.

See the many birds
like Storks, Eagles, Falcons to name the larger species. See wild orchids among the many species of
other plants that this reviewer can not name…sorry.
