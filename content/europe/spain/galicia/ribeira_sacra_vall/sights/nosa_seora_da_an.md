The school **'Nosa Señora da Antiga' **is
a building with Herrerian design and it was built by several Jesuit
architects; the front has Renaissance style made with country stone. It
is distinguished by its impressiveness and the equilibrium in the
layout of its elements. It has three solemn and geometrical vertical
bodies. The cloister has Doric-Roman style, it is square and it is 22
metres long on its side. It is one of the most important pieces of the
building, where the hardness, elegance, and soberness of this style
stand out. The church has a Latin cross ground plan and it is one of
the most sumptuous of the classical architecture in our country. It has
a central nave with barrel vault and two lateral naves communicated
among them. The museum has the pictures of the Greco's 'San Lorenzo'
and 'San Francisco' and five pictures by Andrea do Sarto, two pictures
of the Compostelan school and six showcases with personal objects,
books and incunabula that belonged to the cardinal Rodrigo de Castro.
