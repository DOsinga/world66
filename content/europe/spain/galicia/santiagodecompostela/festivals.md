" A RAPA" a Galician Fiesta

The sun is not yet up when the lads head out for the countryside.

In front goes the bestilleiro (guide), theone with the knowledge, and there, further up the slopes, the animals prance amidst the early morning mist, sniffing and sensing that man is near, and that there is danger.

Sure enough, the lads round up the entir herd shortly afterwards, and lead them down the mountain to the curro (corral) at a spectacular gallop. "With horns and cries the chase the beasts through the hills until they manage to round them up. Some are killed for food. Others are tamed and used as mounts in their warlike struggles." Strabo wrote in his Galicianchronicles two thousand years ago.

Today, the rite, said by some to have started as early as the Bronze Age, has become a fiesta. A rapa das bestias (The Roundup of the Beasts) the ceremony in wich the horses are branded and their manes cropped, is a motive for celebration in many Galician towns. Shouts, neighs, kicks and gallops all come together in a great hubbub with its fair share of violent impressions. It is a fiesta to be seen and felt by visitors to a land whose weather and food ares so benign as to be unforgettable.

Among the corrals where the animals are enclosed, the one at Sabucedo (A Estrada, Pontevedra) is perhapsthe best known. This enclosure was built in the 18th century, right beside the church, with good stone seats for visitors, and it has survuvued as first constructed for serveral centuries. In 1997 a new curro was inaugurated, and for the first time ever some horses were marked with the brand that distinguished them as "Cabalo galego de mone" (Galician Mountanin Horse, a specific breed) whose progresss will be followed b the agricultural department of the Galician regional government, the Xunta.

Also notable is the corral on one side of the Galiñeiro mountain (750 metres), a recreational area that provided a view of the whole Miñor valle and where there are several prehistoric stone engravings. In fact some of the brands for the horses are similar to those drawings made in the rocks by our ancestors.
