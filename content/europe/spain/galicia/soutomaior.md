Soutomaior is a small municipality near the ria of Vigo in southern [Galicia](/europe/spain/galicia). The reason to come here is the Castle of Soutomaior, set on a hilltop with fine views over the surrounding countryside.

The castle dates from the 12th century and reached its peak of importance in the 15th century under Pedro Madruga, when it was the centre of political power in southern Galicia. Unlike many Galician fortifications, it is not perched on inaccessible cliffs — the approach from the east and south is gentle, making it an easy visit. In the late 19th century the Marquis of Vega de Armijo converted it into a summer residence, adding the neo-gothic Ladies' Gallery. The castle passed to public ownership in 1982 and is now open to visitors, hosting exhibitions and cultural events.

The castle grounds are as much a draw as the building itself. The gardens contain a remarkable collection of centuries-old camellias (Galicia is famous for them), an avenue of hydrangeas, banana palms, and chestnut trees said to be eight hundred years old. Above the gardens, the small Chapel of San Caetano hosts a popular festival every August.
