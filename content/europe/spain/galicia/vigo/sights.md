In the tourist season there are boat trips to the Islas Cies. These islands appeal so strongly in other seasons that the trip has to be phenominally good. 

Out of season you have to be content with [or rather, all you can get is] a distant view of the islands from the bus between Bayona and Vigo or from the ferry across from Cangas on the north side of the Ria de Vigo to the city of Vigo itself. Given that there is a bus route from
[Pontevedra](/europe/spain/northernspain/pontevedra) to Cangas, it doesn't require the wisdom of Solomon to devise a circular trip.

### Berbés Quarter

Berbés Quarter, Vigo was born from the sea, round a small fishing village that increasingly spread in direction to the mountain. Today there still remain vestiges of that fishing quarter, with their narrow-fronted houses and arcades in which fish was sold. Also some taverns and the Stone market are retained, where the act of tasting fresh oysters turns into something similar to drinking a sip of the sea.
