---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

The old Berbés quarter is the natural home of Vigo's bar life. The area around the Pedra market — where the oyster women set up their stalls each morning — flows into a string of taverns and tascas, most of them tiny, all of them serious about Galician wine. The local ritual is to drink Ribeiro white from ceramic bowls (tazas) alongside a plate of mussels or polbo á feira (octopus). As the evening progresses, the wine gets more ambitious: Albariño and Condado whites from the Rías Baixas DO appear, along with Mencía reds and Godello whites. Good Galician cheeses — Tetilla, San Simón, Arzúa-Ulloa — are the standard accompaniment. The streets around Rúa da Pescadería and Rúa dos Cesteiros are the best area to explore on foot.
