The Sea Museum occupies a former slaughterhouse and the ruins of a canning factory on the waterfront at Alcabre — a fitting setting for a museum dedicated to Vigo's relationship with the Atlantic. The permanent exhibition traces the city's maritime history, with a particular focus on the Battle of Rande (1702), when a Spanish treasure fleet was sunk in the ría.
