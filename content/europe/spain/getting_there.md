---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Spain is well connected to the rest of the world, with major international airports in Madrid, Barcelona, Málaga, Valencia, Alicante, and Bilbao. The mainland is also easily reached overland from France or by ferry from the UK, Ireland, and Morocco.

## By Air

Madrid-Barajas (MAD) and Barcelona-El Prat (BCN) handle the most international traffic. Málaga (AGP) is the main gateway for the Costa del Sol and Andalusia. Budget carriers including Ryanair, easyJet, and Vueling serve dozens of Spanish airports from across Europe, making point-to-point fares competitive.

Approximate flight times: London to Madrid, 2h 20min; New York to Madrid, 8h; Sydney to Madrid, around 24h with a connection.

## By Train

High-speed AVE trains connect Madrid to Paris via Barcelona in around 6.5 hours, and there are overnight sleeper services between Paris and Madrid or Barcelona. From Lisbon, a direct high-speed line opened in 2025, cutting the journey to Madrid to around 2h 45min. Book through Renfe (renfe.com) or Rail Europe for international connections.

## By Ferry

Brittany Ferries runs services from Portsmouth and Plymouth to Santander and Bilbao, with crossings taking around 24 hours. These are popular with travellers bringing a car or motorcycle. BALEÀLIA and Trasmediterránea connect mainland Spain to the Balearic Islands; Baleàlia also sails from Almería and Motril to Melilla and northern Morocco.

## By Road

The main overland route from France passes through the Pyrenees. The A-9 highway enters Spain at Irún (Basque Country) and the AP-7 enters near La Jonquera (Catalonia). Both are well maintained with frequent service areas. The Channel Tunnel and Eurolines coaches from London's Victoria Coach Station connect to Spanish cities, though the journey takes around 24 hours.

## Visas

EU and Schengen-area citizens enter without a visa. Citizens of the US, Canada, Australia, and most other countries can stay up to 90 days within a 180-day period without a visa. From late 2025, travellers from non-EU countries must register with the EU Entry/Exit System (EES) at the border. Check current requirements before travelling.
