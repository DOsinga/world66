Gipuzkoa is the smallest of the three Basque provinces and one of the most densely populated areas of Spain. It stretches from the Pyrenees to the Bay of Biscay, and its coastline is lined with fishing villages and dramatic cliffs.

The capital is Donostia–San Sebastián, one of the finest cities in Spain and internationally known for its food scene. The old town's pintxos bars along Calle 31 de Agosto are justifiably famous. The bay of La Concha, with its wide sandy beach and elegant promenade, gives the city a resort atmosphere that draws visitors year-round.

Inland, the Basque valleys are green and quiet — good walking country, with stone farmhouses (caseríos) scattered across the hillsides.
