Las Palmas de Gran Canaria is the largest city in the Canary Islands and one of the most underrated cities in Spain. It has the feel of a place that exists for its residents rather than its visitors — a working port city with genuine neighbourhoods, good food, and a long stretch of golden beach running right through the middle of town.

The old quarter of Vegueta is where Columbus stopped on his way to the Americas, and the cathedral and surrounding streets still have a quiet colonial grandeur. Just north, Triana is the main shopping and strolling district, with pedestrianised streets lined with art nouveau facades. The two neighbourhoods flow into each other and make for a fine afternoon of wandering.

Playa de las Canteras is the city's great asset — a three-kilometre arc of sand sheltered by a natural reef called La Barra. The water is calm, the swimming is good year-round, and the promenade behind the beach is lined with restaurants and terrace bars. At the southern end, La Puntilla is the surfer's corner; at the northern end, the Auditorio Alfredo Kraus sits dramatically on the rocks.

The food in Las Palmas is excellent and affordable. Fresh fish is everywhere, and the local specialities — papas arrugadas with mojo sauce, gofio, and sancocho — are worth seeking out. The Mercado del Puerto in the port area is a good place to eat surrounded by locals.
