Albacete is the biggest city in **Castilla-La Mancha**, flat on the sun-baked plains of the region made famous by Cervantes' Don Quixote, halfway between Madrid and Alicante on the high-speed AVE train line. It has none of the picturesque Castilian charm of its smaller neighbours — no medieval centre, no dramatic hilltop fortress — but a handful of handsome 19th-century buildings, wide pedestrian streets, and the excellent **Museum of Albacete** whose collection of Iberian stone sphinxes (the 4th-century BC **Cerrillo Blanco Bulls**) and a complete set of Roman dolls from a child's grave are worth the stop.

Two things the city is genuinely famous for across Spain: **navajas**, the folding pocket knives that have been made here since the 16th century (there is a small knife museum — the **Museo de la Cuchillería** — in a striking neo-Gothic mansion on the main square), and the annual **Feria de Albacete** in September, one of the oldest and most traditional fairs in Spain, with ten days of bullfights, horse shows, flamenco and the famous Gothic-style fairground *recinto*.
