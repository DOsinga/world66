Almagro is a small Renaissance town in the heart of La Mancha, best known for its extraordinary main square — the Plaza Mayor — which doubles as one of Spain's oldest working theatres, the Corral de Comedias. Dating from the 17th century and still used for performances, it's the kind of place that stops you in your tracks. The town's historic centre is compact, well-preserved, and worth a few hours of wandering.

The town has strong links to the Knights of Calatrava, the powerful medieval military order that once controlled this part of Castile. The old monastery that now serves as a national parador is a good place to stay if you want to soak up the atmosphere overnight.
