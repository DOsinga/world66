Oropesa is a hilltop town in the province of Toledo, perched above the plains with sweeping views of the Sierra de Gredos. It was an important strategic point during the Middle Ages and played a role in the organization of the conquest of the Americas — the local nobility had strong connections to the expeditions. The old town retains much of its medieval character, and the castle that dominates the skyline is now a parador, one of Spain's state-run historic hotels.

The upper town has several monasteries, churches, and the hermitage of Santa María de Peñitas worth exploring on foot. Oropesa sits roughly halfway between Madrid and the Portuguese border on the A-5 road, making it a natural stopping point. There's a medieval market held here that draws visitors from around the region, and the restaurants around the square are a reliable place for Castilian cooking.
