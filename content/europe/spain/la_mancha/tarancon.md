Tarancón is a small town in the province of Cuenca, on the main road between [Madrid](/europe/spain/madrid) and Valencia. It has served as a crossroads for centuries, and the old centre retains a handful of modest monuments — the 16th-century Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de la Asunción, the remains of a medieval wall gate, and the Palace of the Duke of Riánsares. It is a quiet, workaday Castilian town, more a stopping point than a destination in itself.
