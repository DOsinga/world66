Valdepeñas is the wine capital of La Mancha, sitting at the southern edge of the vast Castilian plateau about two hours south of Madrid. The town gives its name to one of Spain's most widely drunk denominations — Valdepeñas wine is the reliable house red in bars across the country, though the best producers here make far more interesting bottles than that reputation suggests.

The centre is pleasant and white-walled, focused on the long Plaza de España with its arcaded buildings and the late Gothic church of Nuestra Señora de la Asunción. The Museo del Vino, housed in a renovated bodega on Calle Princesa, covers the history of local winemaking and includes a tasting. Several bodegas on the edge of town offer visits — the underground cellars, some carved centuries ago, are impressive even if you are not particularly interested in wine.

The Molinos de Gregorio Prieto windmill park, just outside town, is one of several spots in La Mancha where you can see the kind of windmills Don Quixote famously tilted at. The painter Gregorio Prieto, born in Valdepeñas in 1897, has a small foundation museum in town with portraits of his circle, which included Lorca and Dalí.

Valdepeñas is an easy stop on the road between Madrid and Andalusia. The AVE high-speed train passes through nearby Ciudad Real, and the town is right off the A-4 motorway.
