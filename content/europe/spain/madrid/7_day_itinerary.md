type: section
---

Madrid, Spain’s capital and largest city, has many things to do for the visiting tourist. Using this **Madrid itinerary**, you can get a good idea of exactly what Madrid has to offer ranging from art gallery visits to a tour of the “Ice Palace”.
**

Day 1- Watch a Bullfight**

Ever heard of the famous Spanish bullfights? Then check out the renowned San Isidro bullfight festival if your visit coincides with the months of May and June. If not, then you can always experience an adrenaline-pumped bullfight outside San Isidro where they are normally held every Sunday from 7:00 p.m. onwards, the tickets of which are available at San Ventas, Madrid’s main bull ring.
**

Day 2- Visit the Ice Palace and a Flamenco Restaurant**

The Palacio de Hielo a.k.a The Ice Palace, is a must visit when in Madrid. At the center of the palace stands a 1800 sq meter ice rink. Cool off in Madrid’s crazy afternoon heat and go ice skating in this very rink. At sundown, get ready to watch one of Madrid’s best flamenco shows at the Corral de la Moreria Restaurant. While you sip on drinks and eat amazing food, you can take in the vivid colors and moves of Spain’s flamenco dancers.
**

Day 3- Take a Cable Car Ride**

Check out Madrid from a bird’s eye viewpoint as you sit in a cable car that carries you high above the city. Don’t forget to watch out for the Manzares River and the Royal Palace. More importantly, don’t forget to carry your camera with you! You can catch a cable car from Paseo del Pintor Rosales.

**Day 4- Madrid’s Art Galleries and Parks**

Take a leisurely stroll in one of Madrid’s many beautiful parks. Out of the many ‘green zones’ in Madrid, Parque del Oeste is a must visit for its beautifully landscaped gardens, open grassy spaces and the pretty rose garden. Next, visit the well-known Golden Triangle of Art, a gallery situated along the Paseo del Prado that comprises 3 museums. Here you’ll be able to appreciate the work of great artists like Pablo Picasso.
**

Day 5- Lunch/Dinner with Hemingway**

Visit the famous Cerveceria Alemanua restaurant and relax as you sip on wine or beer. A must try is the Spanish omelette. Word has it that Hemingway ate here. So if you’re lucky enough, you may just get to sit at the same table!
**

Day 6- Sample Spanish Wine**

Sample quality Spanish wines and be guided along the way by a professional at this centrally-located class in Calle del Padre Indalecio Hernandez.
**

Day 7- Indulge in a Spanish Delicacy**

Head to Chocolateria de San Gines Restaurant for a cup of gooey hot chocolate and with yummy churros (long fried dough sticks).
