Madrid sits at the centre of Spain and is surrounded by some of the country's finest historic towns, all reachable within an hour by train. **[Toledo](/europe/spain/la_mancha/toledo)** — the former capital, a World Heritage Site of Moorish, Jewish, and Christian architecture stacked on a rocky hill above the Tajo — is the essential day trip. **[Segovia](/europe/spain/centralspain/segovia)** has a Roman aqueduct still standing after 2,000 years and a Disney-looking Alcázar castle. **[Ávila](/europe/spain/centralspain/avila)** preserves the most intact medieval walls in Europe. **El Escorial**, the austere 16th-century royal palace-monastery of Philip II, sits at the foot of the mountains. **[Aranjuez](/europe/spain/centralspain/aranjuez)** is the cool summer retreat of the Spanish kings, with its famous gardens.

For nature, the **Sierra de Guadarrama** mountains are just 55 km north of the city — pine forests, high-altitude lakes, good hiking in summer and skiing in winter. Puerto de Navacerrada and Cotos are the main ski centres; Cercedilla, Manzanares el Real (for La Pedriza granite formations), and the Camino Schmidt trail are all reachable by Cercanías trains.
