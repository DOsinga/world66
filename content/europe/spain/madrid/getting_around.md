---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Madrid's **Metro** is one of the oldest and most extensive in Europe — fast, clean, and safe, with twelve lines covering the city and airport. Trains run from 6:00 a.m. to 1:30 a.m. A single ride is €1.50–2.00; the ten-trip *Metrobús* ticket (€12.20, shareable) is the best value for visitors. The Metro connects both Barajas airport terminals directly to the centre.

**Buses** serve every corner of the city at any hour; after midnight, night buses (*buhos*) leave from Plaza de Cibeles. **Suburban trains** (Cercanías) run from Atocha, Chamartín, Nuevos Ministerios, and Recoletos stations and are the best way to reach nearby towns like [Toledo](/europe/spain/la_mancha/toledo), [El Escorial](/europe/spain/centralspain/elescorial), and the Sierra de Guadarrama mountains (Cercedilla, Navacerrada, Cotos) for skiing in winter.

Madrid's centre is walkable — distances between the main sights along the Paseo del Prado, Plaza Mayor, and Puerta del Sol are all under fifteen minutes on foot. **Taxis** are plentiful, metered, and not expensive by European standards. App-based services (Uber, Cabify) are widely available.
