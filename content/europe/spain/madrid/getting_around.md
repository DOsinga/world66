title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

When you land in Madrid, you have 2 choices: to take the bus to the centre (€2.50) or to take the metro. The bus is very quick and you arrive in 20 min. In the city centre (Plaza Colon).The fastest way to travel into town is by Metro, which is one of the oldest in Europe with about 175 km. length and it's very clean and safe. It opens from 6.00 a.m. till 1.30 a.m. It can be taken from the Airport to go to any point of interest.

You can also travel by bus, there's service at any hour. After midnight, the nigt buses *Buhos* start leaving from *Plaza de Cibeles* in all directions.

The bus and the metro's tickets are the same. The cheapest and eaysiest way to move is to buy a *Metrobus* with 10 tickets, which costs €5.20 and can be used by more than 1 person. A single ticket costs €1.10.

You can also choose the local trains ("Cercanias", please check http://www.renfe.es/empresa/cercanias/index.html), which has 4 main stations in town: Chamartin, Nuevos Ministerios, Recoletos and Atocha. These trains are very useful to travel to the nearest historical cities and villages like [El Escorial](/europe/spain/centralspain/elescorial), [Toledo](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/toledo), or to the mountains near Madrid (Sierra de Guadarrama) with directions to Cercedilla, Navacerrada, Cotos (the ski-centers), etc. The ticket from the center of Madrid to [El Escorial](/europe/spain/centralspain/elescorial) is about €3.20 one way, it is about €6.40 one way to [Toledo](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/toledo) and it is about €3.20 to the mountain. There exist also cards of 10 tickets, which are about 30% cheaper. All tickets with 10 travels are valid for more than one person.
