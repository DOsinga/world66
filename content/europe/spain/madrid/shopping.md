---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Madrid is a city where people dress to impress, and it’s impossible
not to be struck by locals’ stylish attire when visiting the city. It’s
all about sleek and sophisticated in the Spanish capital, and if you
want to get the look there are countless places to indulge in some
serious retail therapy. Clothes aside, Madrid is also a good place for
picking up bargain booze and delicious food, with speciality
delicatessens and super-cheap supermarkets dotted around the city. All
the city’s major shopping centers have places to eat and drink –
ranging from burger joints to fine dining, so you’ll have plenty of
opportunity to refuel during your shopping spree.

**Fashion Finds**

Fashionistas will be in their
element in Madrid, with more malls and boutiques than you could
possibly count. Those with serious cash to splash might want to visit
El Jardín de Serrano, at Avenida Monforte de Lemos 36, in the Salamanca
district of the city. This elegant shopping center is as super-sleek as
its clientele, and is housed within the confines of a former palace.
Not the best place for budget shoppers.

**Kooky Cool**

Those
whose style is a little more left of center will find oodles of
opportunities to jazz up their wardrobes at Mercado Fuencarral, at
Calle Fuencarral 45, in the Chueca district. This kooky market combines
lots of independent stores, and is a gathering place for the city’s
trendy young things. Live DJ sets, off-beat clothing and accessories
and tattoo parlours are all part of the experience, and this is a real
alternative to Madrid’s more formal shopping centers. There are some
cute cafes to hang out at, too.

**Bargain Booze**

Those
who like a tipple will find Madrid’s supermarkets a joy to browse. From
ultra-cheap sangria to bargains on flavored vodkas and even Absinthe,
you’ll need to find room in your suitcase for all the liquor you’ll be
tempted to bring home. Just make sure you don’t go over your alcohol
allowance!
**

Foodie Treats**

The city’s main supermarkets are great places
to pick up cheap nibbles, from spiced sausages and meats to stuffed
olives, cheeses and palm hearts. If you’re looking for more specialised
fayre, you’ll find reasonably-priced delis and sweet stores throughout
the city, and many of these products make great holiday gifts.
