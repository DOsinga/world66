---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Madrid's great sights cluster along and around the Paseo del Prado. The three world-class museums — **Prado**, **Reina Sofía**, and **Thyssen-Bornemisza** — form the "Golden Triangle of Art" and between them hold one of the densest concentrations of important European painting anywhere. Allow a full morning for the Prado alone.

Between museum visits, the **Royal Palace** is the largest functioning royal residence in Europe, and the nearby **Plaza Mayor** (1619) is the great square of Habsburg Madrid. **Retiro Park** — once royal hunting grounds — offers a Victorian greenhouse, the Crystal Palace on a lake, and Sunday afternoons full of Madrileños. For something quieter, the **Botanical Garden** next to the Prado is a beautiful escape from the city streets; the small **Temple of Debod** — an Egyptian temple gifted to Spain — sits on a hill with one of the best sunset views in Madrid.

Madrid is also a city of great smaller museums. The **Museo Sorolla**, in the painter's own house, is an under-visited gem, and the **CaixaForum** with its vertical garden is an architectural as well as cultural stop.
