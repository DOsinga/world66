Lo Pagán is a small resort town on the northern shore of the Mar Menor, the large saltwater lagoon that separates the La Manga strip from the Murcia mainland. The Mar Menor is one of the warmest and calmest bodies of water in Europe — shallow, sheltered, and with a salt concentration that makes floating effortless. Locals have long valued its mud baths for their supposed therapeutic properties, and you will see people caked in dark mud along the waterfront.

The town is low-key and predominantly Spanish in character, without the high-rise tourism of La Manga across the lagoon. The promenade along the water is pleasant for an evening stroll, and the seafood restaurants serve fresh fish from both the lagoon and the Mediterranean. The salt flats of San Pedro del Pinatar, just north of town, are a protected nature reserve and an important stopping point for flamingos and other migratory birds.

Lo Pagán is the kind of place where Spanish families come for unpretentious summer holidays — which is exactly what makes it appealing.
