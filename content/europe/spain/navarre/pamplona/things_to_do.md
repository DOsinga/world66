---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Pamplona is a city made for walking. The compact old town is full of hidden squares, medieval churches, and ramparts with views over the river Arga and the green hills beyond. Most of the interesting sights cluster within the old walls, easily covered on foot in a day.

The star-shaped fortifications are among the best-preserved Renaissance military architecture in Spain. The Ciudadela, the enormous pentagonal fortress at the heart of the defences, is now a park and cultural space. From there, the city walls loop north past the Taconera gardens and the Caballo Blanco viewpoint, offering some of the best panoramas in the city.

**Museum of Navarra** -- Housed in a former 16th-century hospital connected to a 1540 church, this is the region's flagship museum. The collection spans prehistory to the present, with standout pieces including Flemish paintings on copper, twelve paintings depicting Genesis, a set of small ivory chests, and a Goya portrait of the Marquis of San Adrian. The Iron Age and Roman collections are also strong. Cuesta de Santo Domingo s/n.

**Cathedral of Santa Maria la Real** -- The Gothic cathedral hides behind a rather stiff neoclassical facade, but step inside and the 14th-century cloister is stunning -- widely considered one of the finest Gothic cloisters in Europe.

**Palace of Navarra** -- The neoclassical seat of the regional government, built in 1847 on the Paseo de Sarasate. In front of it stands the Monument to Los Fueros, erected in 1903 and paid for by public subscription -- a symbol of Navarre's fierce attachment to its historic rights.

**Fort of San Bartolome** -- Part of the old fortification system, this well-restored bastion gives a sense of the military engineering that once made Pamplona one of the most fortified cities in Spain.

**Taconera Park** -- The oldest park in Pamplona, set against the city walls. The dry moats have been turned into small enclosures housing deer, peacocks, and dwarf goats. A pleasant detour while walking the ramparts.

**Paseo de la Media Luna** -- A tree-lined walkway along the old walls, with ponds and a monument to the Pamplona-born violinist Pablo Sarasate. Quiet and shaded, with views down to the river Arga and the orchards below.

**Mesón del Caballo Blanco** -- In the highest part of the old town, this handsome building sits on the Redin bastion, one of the best viewpoints in the city. From here you look out over the river, the outlying neighbourhoods, and the green surroundings. Nearby stands the Mentidero Cross, dating to around 1500.
