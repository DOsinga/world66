Gernika (Guernica in Spanish) is known worldwide for the 1937 bombing by the German Condor Legion during the Spanish Civil War, and for Picasso's painting that depicted the horror — a full-size reproduction hangs in the town's museum, while the original is in [Madrid](/europe/spain/madrid)'s Reina Sofía.

But Gernika is more than its tragedy. It is the symbolic capital of the Basque people: the ancient oak tree in front of the Casa de Juntas is where Basque leaders gathered for centuries, and the parliament building is still in use. The Monday market, one of the largest in the Basque Country, fills the streets with local produce.

The Urdaibai estuary nearby is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, with spectacular tidal flats — the difference between high and low tide is dramatic. Inland, the Santimamiñe caves contain prehistoric paintings dating back 14,000 years.
