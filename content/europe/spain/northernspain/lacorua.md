La Coruna is a port city on the Atlantic coast of [Galicia](/europe/spain/galicia), built on a peninsula that juts into the sea. The old town clusters around the harbour, while the newer city stretches along a magnificent waterfront promenade — the Paseo Maritimo — that runs for 13 km around the headland, making it one of the longest seaside walks in Europe. The city has a confident, cosmopolitan feel that sets it apart from quieter Galician towns.

The Torre de Hercules, a Roman lighthouse that has been in continuous use for nearly 2,000 years, stands on the northern tip of the peninsula and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The old town, known as the Ciudad Vieja, is a compact tangle of medieval streets around the Romanesque churches of Santiago and Santa Maria del Campo. On the opposite side of the harbour, the Cidade da Cultura district has modern museums and the excellent Aquarium Finisterrae.

La Coruna is a great eating city. The streets around Placa de Maria Pita are lined with tapas bars and restaurants serving fresh Galician seafood — percebes (goose barnacles), pulpo a feira (octopus), and whatever came off the boats that morning. The Playa del Orzan and Playa de Riazor, right in the city centre, are popular surf beaches when the Atlantic swell picks up. The city is also the headquarters of Inditex, the company behind Zara, which has brought a certain commercial energy to the place.
