---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

La Coruña is well connected by rail and road, though the international air connections are limited. Most travellers from outside Spain route through Madrid or Bilbao.

**By air:** A Coruña Airport (LCG) is 8 kilometres south of the city, with domestic flights to Madrid, Barcelona, and a handful of other Spanish cities. International connections are sparse — Iberia and Vueling dominate. A taxi into the city takes about 20 minutes; bus line 12 connects the airport to the city centre.

**By train:** The high-speed AVG line connects A Coruña to Madrid in under four hours (via Santiago de Compostela, about 35 minutes away). RENFE runs frequent regional services along the coast to [Ferrol](/europe/spain/northernspain/ferrol) and south to [Vigo](/europe/spain/northernspain/vigo). The main station is San Cristóbal, south of the city centre.

**By bus:** ALSA runs long-distance buses to Madrid, Bilbao, and other major cities. Regional buses cover the Galician coast more completely than the trains. The bus station is adjacent to the train station.
