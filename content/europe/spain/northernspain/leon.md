León is a historic city in northwestern Spain, the capital of its province, and one of the great art cities of the Camino de Santiago. Its medieval old quarter is compact and well-preserved, and its cathedral — the Catedral de Santa María de Regla — is considered one of the finest Gothic buildings in Spain, famous above all for its extraordinary stained glass. More than 1,800 square metres of medieval glass filter the light into the interior with an effect that is genuinely breathtaking.

The Cathedral is far from the only reason to visit. The Real Basílica de San Isidoro is an outstanding Romanesque church containing the Panteón Real — a painted royal mausoleum often called the "Sistine Chapel of Romanesque art." The Real Colegiata's vault paintings, dating from the eleventh and twelfth centuries, are among the best-preserved Romanesque frescoes in Europe. The San Marcos convent, now a parador, is a spectacular Renaissance building with a plateresque facade.

Gaudí also left his mark: the Casa Botines (1892) on the Plaza de San Marcelo is one of his few works outside Catalonia. León is an important stop on the Camino de Santiago, and the medieval pilgrim route passes through the city's old quarter.
