León is one of the great stops on the Camino de Santiago and a city with far more to offer than most pilgrims realise. Founded as the base of the Roman VII Legion (from which it takes its name), it became the capital of a medieval kingdom and preserves an outstanding legacy of Romanesque, Gothic, and Renaissance architecture.

The **Cathedral** is the undisputed highlight — known as the *Pulchra Leonina*, it has over 1,200 square metres of stained glass windows dating from the thirteenth to the twentieth century, making it one of the finest Gothic cathedrals in Spain. The **Real Basílica de San Isidoro** has a Romanesque pantheon with twelfth-century frescoes so vivid it is called the "Sistine Chapel of Romanesque art." The **Convento de San Marcos**, a vast Renaissance building on the river Bernesga, was once a pilgrims' hospital and is now a parador.

Gaudí left his mark here too — the **Casa de los Botines** (1892) stands on the Plaza de San Marcelo. The old town, centred on the **Plaza Mayor**, is full of tapas bars, and León has one of the liveliest bar-hopping scenes in northern Spain. The **Barrio Húmedo** (the "wet quarter") is where locals gather for small plates and wine.

León is connected to Madrid and other Spanish cities by train and bus.
