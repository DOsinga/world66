The Hostal San Marcos is one of the grandest Renaissance buildings in Spain, now operating as a luxury parador. It was built in the 16th century as a monastery and pilgrim hospital on the Camino de Santiago — the long Plateresque façade stretching along the river is almost theatrical in its ambition.

You don't need to be a guest to appreciate it. Walk the length of the façade, duck into the lobby, and visit the small archaeological museum inside, which holds Roman and medieval artefacts from the region. The cloister is beautifully preserved.
