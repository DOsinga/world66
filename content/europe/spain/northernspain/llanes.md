Llanes is a small coastal town in eastern Asturias, wedged between the Cantabrian Sea and the Picos de Europa mountains. The combination is extraordinary — you can swim on a beach in the morning and be hiking in serious mountain country by lunch. The old town, built around a medieval harbour, still has its walls and towers, and the port fills with fishing boats that supply the restaurants along the waterfront.

The coastline around Llanes is one of the most dramatic in Spain. The Bufones de Pría, blowholes in the cliff where the sea shoots geysers of spray through the limestone, are spectacular in rough weather. Dozens of small beaches — Torimbia, Ballota, Gulpiyuri (a tiny inland beach fed by the sea through underground channels) — line the coast in both directions. The Senda Costera, a walking path along the cliffs, connects many of them.

In town, the Cubos de la Memoria — the painted concrete blocks along the harbour wall, covered in vivid murals by Basque artist Agustín Ibarrola — have become Llanes' most photographed sight. The old quarter is good for an evening paseo, with cider bars and seafood restaurants filling up after dark. Asturian cider, poured from height in the traditional way, is the drink here — ask for a *culín*.

Llanes has its own FEVE narrow-gauge railway station, connecting it along the coast to [Santander](/europe/spain/cantabria/santander) and [Oviedo](/europe/spain/asturias/oviedo). The Picos de Europa are about 30 minutes south by car.
