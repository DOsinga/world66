Muros sits on the unspoilt Ría de Muros e Noia in Galicia, a fishing town that has barely changed in decades. Arcaded streets, granite hórreos (raised granaries), and a working harbour where fishermen still mend nets in the afternoon sun give it an authenticity that many Galician coastal towns have lost.

The bus ride from Santiago de Compostela is worth the trip alone — first through rolling Galician countryside, then from [Noia](/europe/spain/northernspain/noia) along the coast with views across the ría. Muros works well as a day trip from Santiago, but if you linger for a seafood lunch on the waterfront you may find yourself wanting to stay the night.
