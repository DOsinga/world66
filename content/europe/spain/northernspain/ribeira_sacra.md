The Ribeira Sacra is a wine region carved into the steep gorges of the Miño and Sil rivers in inland [Galicia](/europe/spain/galicia). The name — "sacred bank" — comes from the concentration of Romanesque monasteries and churches that dot the hillsides, built when this was one of the most important monastic centres in medieval Iberia.

The vineyards here are among the most dramatic in Europe: terraced into near-vertical slopes above the rivers, many can only be worked by hand. Five sub-regions are recognised — Chantada, Quiroga, Ribeiras do Miño, Amandi, and Ribeira do Sil-Ourense — producing excellent reds from the Mencía grape and aromatic whites from Godello and Albariño. A river cruise through the Sil canyon is the best way to see the terraces from below, while the winding roads above offer one viewpoint after another.
