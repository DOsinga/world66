Vitoria-Gasteiz is the capital of the Basque Country and one of the most liveable cities in Spain, though it rarely appears on tourist itineraries. That is a shame, because its medieval old town, perched on a hill in the centre, is one of the best-preserved in the Basque region — a tight cluster of streets named after the trades that once occupied them, anchored by the Cathedral of Santa María, which has been under continuous restoration and offers fascinating guided tours of the ongoing work.

The city was named European Green Capital in 2012, and it shows. A green belt of parks encircles the centre, the streets are walkable and calm, and the pintxos bars in the old town rival those of [San Sebastián](/europe/spain/northernspain/sansebastian) and [Bilbao](/europe/spain/northernspain/bilbao) without the crowds. The Plaza de la Virgen Blanca, overlooked by the church of San Miguel, is the heart of the city and the focal point of the August fiestas.

Artium, the Basque Museum of Contemporary Art, houses a strong collection of Spanish and Basque art. The city also makes a good base for exploring the Rioja Alavesa wine region, which lies just south across the mountains.
