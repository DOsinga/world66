Palencia sits on the Castilian meseta north of [Valladolid](/europe/spain/valladolid), a quiet provincial capital that most travellers drive straight past. Their loss. The cathedral — known as La Bella Desconocida, the Beautiful Unknown — is one of the finest Gothic churches in Spain, with a stunning interior that holds its own against Burgos or León but without a single tour bus outside.

The city is small, pleasant, and deeply Castilian: a long main street (Calle Mayor) lined with cafes, a river park along the Carrión, and an unhurried pace that belongs to an older Spain. Palencia was once home to Spain's first university (founded 1212, before Salamanca) and the surrounding Tierra de Campos — the vast, flat wheat country of central Castile — has some of the most evocative Romanesque churches in the country, scattered in tiny villages that feel barely changed since the 12th century.
