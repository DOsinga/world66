Altea is the prettiest town on the Costa Blanca, and one of the few places along this stretch of coast that hasn't been swallowed by resort development. The old quarter sits on a hill above the bay, its whitewashed lanes climbing to a church with glazed blue-tiled domes that have become the symbol of the town. From the church terrace you look down over terracotta rooftops, a marina, and a long pebble beach curving around the bay.

The town has always attracted artists — there is a higher concentration of galleries and studios here than anywhere else on the Costa Blanca. This gives the old quarter a slightly bohemian character that contrasts pleasantly with the surrounding package-holiday belt. Craft markets run through summer months in the square outside the church.

Below the hill, the new town stretches along the seafront with a promenade, seafood restaurants, and an easy, unhurried atmosphere. Altea is quieter than [Benidorm](/europe/spain/spanishcostas/benidorm) (just 10 km south) by design: the town has largely resisted high-rise development and keeps its scale human.
