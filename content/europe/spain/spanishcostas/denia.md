Dénia is a relaxed port town at the northern end of the Costa Blanca, sitting below a medieval castle at the foot of the Montgó massif. Unlike the resort strips to the south, it has a functioning town centre with a year-round population and a fishing harbour that still operates.

The town divides neatly into the old quarter around the castle and the Las Marinas beach zone stretching north along the coast. Dénia is also the departure point for ferries to Ibiza and Formentera, which makes it a practical staging post even if you're not staying. The local rice dishes — Dénia prawns feature prominently — are taken seriously here.
