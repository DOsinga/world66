Lo Pagán is a small resort village on the Mar Menor in the Murcia region of southeastern Spain, at the northern tip of the La Manga strip. It sits between the Mar Menor — the large shallow salt lagoon — and the open Mediterranean, giving it beaches on both sides with very different characters: the lagoon side is calm and warm, ideal for children and paddlers; the Mediterranean side has more surf and a wider strip of sand.

The town is best known for its therapeutic mud baths. At sunset, locals and visitors wade into the shallow lagoon waters near the shoreline where volcanic mud accumulates, coating themselves in the grey-brown sediment and waiting for it to dry in the evening light — a peculiar and genuinely popular ritual that has been happening here for decades. The mud is said to be good for the skin and joints, though the main appeal seems to be the strangeness of the spectacle.

Lo Pagán is quiet and local in character, very different from the resort developments further south on La Manga. If you want tranquility, the mud, and easy access to the Mar Menor without the crowds, this is a good choice.
