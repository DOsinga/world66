---
title: "Practical Information in Picassent"
type: section
---

*Picassent has great local bars and restaurants (great value for money), lovely local people (we have made many new Spanish friends) and not too many Brits and property prices are still very reasonable.*

*The beaches are 25 mins away and often during the week these are very quite as not many have discovered this region yet. At the weekends they do get crowded by the locals.*

*Picassent and Valencia are both easy to get around. Picassent being very small but nevertheless very Spanish, not beautiful, but pleasant and getting better. When we first moved there it looked like Basra on a good night!. Now it is cleaning it's act up and is good. There are scores of festivals in Picassent and the surrounding areas and most of these ar free including a great music week in early September.*

*Valencia on the other hand is a large city with a cosmopolitan atmosphere about it. Great resaurants, bars, museums, shops etc. It has a excellent transport system, tube, buses, and trams. It boasts of having staged the world famous America's Cup in 2007 and the indoor European Athletics Championships in 2008. In August 2008 it will stage the first of nine F1 races around the streets and marina/docks.*

*Go and visit Valencia and Picassent but do not tell too many other folk as this is still not known to many and we don't want to be selfish but want this to remain a secret for us to enjoy.*

*Barry Allan*
