Torrevieja is a coastal city on the Costa Blanca in southeastern Spain, built between two large salt lakes that turn a vivid pink in summer. The salt industry has been the town's livelihood for centuries, and the Salinas de Torrevieja are still commercially active — the pink lake is one of the most photographed sights on this stretch of coast.

The town itself is a popular year-round destination for Northern European retirees and holiday-makers, drawn by the mild winters and long beaches. The seafront promenade runs for several kilometres, and the old town around the harbour has good fish restaurants and a lively market. La Mata beach, north of the centre, is backed by dunes and pine forest and feels more natural than the town beaches.

Torrevieja is not the most charming town on the Costa Blanca — package tourism has left its mark — but the salt lakes, the year-round sunshine, and the easy access to [Alicante](/europe/spain/spanishcostas/alicante) make it a practical base.
