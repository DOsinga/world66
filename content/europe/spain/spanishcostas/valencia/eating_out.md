As the third largest city in Spain, Valencia restaurants are plentiful and well known for their culinary excellence. With specialties including paella, horchata, Spanish omelets, rosquilletas and calamari, Valencian dining is sure to be a gastronomic treat.

**Eating in Valencia**
Spanish mealtimes are later than in America, so be sure to check restaurant timings before making plans for your day if you have a particular restaurant in mind. Breakfast is generally served from 9 am to 11 am, lunch is served from 2 pm to 4 pm, and dinner is served from 9 pm to 11 pm. These are general meal times and some restaurants may vary, but don’t be surprised to find a restaurant closed if you show up for dinner at 6 pm.

Paella, a simmered rice dish, is by far the most popular dish of Valencia. Here it is made in the original style with chicken, pork and/or rabbit. Tourists from all over Spain flock to Valencia to try the city’s original and authentic paella. Of the best paella restaurants in the city, La Pepica, La Marcelina, Restaurante La Alegria de la Huerta and El Rall are on the top of the list.

Some other specialties of the area include arros negre, or black rice flavored with squid ink, llet merengada, an ice cream with a cinnamon-lemon flavor, and fideua, a dish similar to paella with short noodles and fish.

If you are on a tight budget, look for one of the many back street restaurants that serve a fixed price “menu del dia,” including a three course meal and a bottle of wine. These deals are generally quite nice on the wallet and you can typically find some decent food here.

Another great option when traveling on a budget in Spain is to have a meal of tapas. These small plates allow you to sample many different things for a very cheap price. Typically a couple of dollars a plate, tapas can range from a plate of olives to exquisite seafood. When eating with a few people, this is a very social way to have a meal and lets you try many things that perhaps you would not otherwise when eating a full course meal at a restaurant.

Explore the many tastes of Valencia and enjoy some wonderful meals on the water to really get a feel for the food culture that envelops the city.
