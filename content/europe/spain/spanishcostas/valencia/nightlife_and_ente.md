---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Valencia"
type: section
---

Valencia Spain is famous for its nightlife. Party from dusk until
dawn or all week and never run out of places to go. The Nightlife areas
are in several different zones throughout the city.

**Zona Carmen**

Zona Carmen is the bohemian, alternative or underground nightlife of
Valencia. This is the oldest zone and if you like “different” this is
where you need to come. You can find jazz bars, a smoking den that
presents reggae music, a punk bar and an art cave all in the same area.
Most venues start to get crowded around 11:00 pm and close at 2:00 am.
Most drinks are pretty expensive because of the overhead pub owners
have to pay to be in this district.

**Zona Centro**

Zona Centro’s nightlife consists of bars, pubs, discos, and
nightclubs and some don’t close until 8 in the morning. Main stream
clubs include La Indiana with an exotic fish theme. Piccadilly is
another popular club and most of Carmen ends up here when they close.

**Zona Canovas**

Zona Canovas is preferred by those who do not enjoy alternative or
underground but may like pop. Here the socialization is much more
important than the music or the art. Disco bars occupy the main drag to
the north of Plaza de Canovas and you can dance until 4:00 am with no
entry fee. You can find people in their 20’s as well as people in their
50’s in Canovas. Here you can also find restaurants to ease your
hunger. Most drinks cost about 3 euros as opposed to 6 in Carmen.

**Zona Blasco Ibanez**

Zona Blasco Ibanez is near the university so this is an area for the
young. It is cheap and has pubs, rock bars and clubs with live music.
You can dance until 4 am in some of the disco bars and indie/rock is
heard in this area along with soft and hard rock.

**Zona Juan Llorenz**

In Zona Juan Llorenz you will find mainstream and trendy pubs and
clubs. Here the people have taken the time to look great and want to
show off. The age of consumers is 20’s to 30’s and Latino Music fills
the streets. This zone is decidedly Spanish and it seems that everyone
knows everyone else. Beer will cost about 4₤ and most places are open
until 4 am.

**Zona Beach**

Zona Beach is on the beach and it is seasonal. Here you will find
disco bars and nightclubs with restaurants and tapas bars. The port
area is a little more trendy and cosmopolitan and draws an older group.
This area is pretty commercial because of the seasonal patronage and
beach.

While visiting Valencia it is a good idea to sleep during the day
and party all night so you can get the unique flavor of this city's
nightlife.
