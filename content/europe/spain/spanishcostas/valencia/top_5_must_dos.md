---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Valencia"
type: section
---

Valencia, Spain is the third largest city in the country, and is one of
Europe's most fashionable destinations. The city itself is relatively
small, making it very easy to get around. Here is a list of the Top 5
Must Do's while visiting Valencia.

**1. ****City of Arts and Sciences
**

Valencia is a sprawling complex
located in the town center, and was designed by the world renowned
Valencian architect, engineer, and sculptor, Santiago Calatrava.
L'Hemisferic, or better known as "The Eye", is a spectacular piece of
architecture, sitting in a pool of brilliant blue water. It was first
opened to the public in 1998, and in the pupil of the eye you will find
an IMAX cinema, a planetarium, and a Laserium. The City of Arts and
Sciences is opened daily, and offers tours in 4 languages.

**2. ****Valencia Aquarium**

Better known as L'Oceanografico,
is a stunning building designed by Felix Candela. The aquarium is the
largest aquarium in Europe with approximately 7,400 different
specimens. The seven different aquariums recreate the natural
environment of the Mediterranean sea, and you can get up close to
underwater creatures such as eels, scorpion fish, and many tropical
fish. Don't miss the Dolphinarium, which contains 23 million liters of
water, seating capacity for 2,210 people, and a spectacular bottle nose
dolphin show featuring the infamous Flipper!

**3.** **Cathedral of Valencia (La Seu/Catedral)**

This medieval
Cathedral is located in the Plaza de la Reina, within the Old Quarter.
Home to the oldest museum in the city, the Cathedral of Valencia museum
houses the Chalice of Valencia, speculated to be the Holy Grail. There are three ornate
architectural doors not to be missed: The Puerto de Palau is Romanesque, Puerto de
Hierros is Baroque, and Puerto de los Apostoles is Gothic. Admission is free. Try to visit the Puerto de los
Apostoles on Thursdays around noon for a special depiction of local
farmers in traditional dress meeting here to solve their irrigation
problems.

**4. ****Barrio del Carmen **

Locally known as el Carme, Barrio del Carmen is a
hotbed of local nighttime activity. Located in the Old Quarter, Plaza
del Carmen is lined by orange trees and fountains. This area is
bustling with many great restaurants, cafes, clubs, and trendy
boutiques. The best time to take in the sights is Friday or Saturday
evening, when the streets start to come alive.

**5.** **Beaches
**

Las Arenas and La Malvarrosa beaches join each
other and form the city beach of Valencia. The beautiful white sand
beaches that make up the city beach, are the ideal location to sit back
and relax, work on your tan, or cool off with a swim in the gorgeous
Mediterranean waters. The city beach offers fantastic facilities, world
class hotels and resorts, and many fun fairs on the promenade for the
children.
