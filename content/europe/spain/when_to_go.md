---
title: "When to Go in Spain"
type: section
---

The best time to visit Spain is usually in the spring when the weather is
warm without being boiling hot, but it is a popular tourist location throughout
most of the year. When deciding when to plan a trip to Spain it should be
remembered that the climate across the large country is very diverse, and
desirable weather conditions vary according to the time of year in each
location.

Contrary to the assertion of the popular song from the Broadway musical “My
Fair Lady,” rainfall does not mainly fall on the plains in Spain, but rather in
the mountains.

**Madrid**** and the Inland Areas**

These areas are considered to have a Continental Mediterranean climate.
Winters are cool with periodic snowfall in some areas of high altitude, and the
average winter temperature is usually about 35 degrees F.

Spring and autumn are moderate with clement weather, although rainfall is
sometimes more prevalent during these seasons.

Summer is hot with high temperatures, and usually the months of July and
August experience the least rainfall.

**Coastal Strip Area Near the Bay of Biscay**

Often called “Green Spain,” Bilbao is the largest city of the coastal strip,
and the area exhibits the tendencies of an oceanic climate.

The weather in the area is mild and generally even throughout the year.
Precipitation levels are low, and average temperatures vary from about 40 degrees F to
55 degrees F, most of the year, with slightly higher average temperatures in the summer
months of July and August.

**Barcelona**** and the Southern and Eastern Coasts**

These areas possess a Mediterranean climate. Winters are mild yet humid with
the coldest months usually being January and February, but the average
temperatures of these two months are still often as high as 50 degrees F.

Summer months are warm and dry with the hottest months being July and August.
Average daily temperatures range from about 70 degrees F to 80 degrees F.

**The Stretch of Coast between the Southern and Eastern Coasts **

The largest area of this area is Las Palmas, and the area is considered to
exhibit a subtropical climate. This climate is characterized by warm dry summers
and moderately warm winters.

Even during the coldest month of January, temperatures average around 63 degrees F.
In the hottest months of August and September average temperatures range from
75 degrees F to 85 degrees F, and there is little to no rainfall throughout the summer
months.
