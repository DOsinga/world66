title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Gothenburg is a walking-friendly city with a good tram and bus network. The historic tram system is the most convenient way to reach attractions outside the immediate centre, and ferries connect to the archipelago islands.

**Tram and Bus**

Gothenburg&rsquo;s historic tram system travels through the city with stations located conveniently throughout the city center. Gothenburg&rsquo;s bus system will also take you where you need to go, and it&rsquo;s a good choice if you&rsquo;re going a little farther outside of the city center. All the routes and maps in the bus and tram stations are in Swedish, so if you don&rsquo;t speak the language, try to find a version in your language before your trip.

**Ticket System**

All of the public transportation in Gothenburg works on a ticket/pass system. You can buy a single ride ticket, a ticket that works for several days or, if you&rsquo;re planning on spending a good bit of time in Gothenburg, a prepaid card. The pass and all tickets can be used on all public transport options.

**Taxis**

Taxis are easy to find in Gothenburg, especially along the main thoroughfares in the city center. They can be expensive, so either establish a fixed price with the driver or make sure to grab a cab with a fare meter. There are quite a few taxis in Gothenburg operated by unlicensed drivers, so beware. You&rsquo;ll know it&rsquo;s a &ldquo;real&rdquo; taxi if it&rsquo;s got a taxi sign and driver and company identification in the cab.****

**Bike**

For a different way to experience the sights of Gothenburg, rent a bike! There are numerous bike rental places around the city center and Gothenburg boasts an impressive system of bike trails.
