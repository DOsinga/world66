Gothenburg's Konsthallen occupies part of the grand building on Gotaplatsen, the city's cultural square anchored by Poseidon's fountain. It hosts rotating exhibitions of contemporary art, often featuring Nordic and international artists working across painting, sculpture, and installation.

The same building houses the Hasselblad Centre (photography exhibitions tied to the prestigious Hasselblad Award) and the Gothenburg Museum of Art, whose permanent collection includes works by Rembrandt, Monet, and the Scandinavian masters. You can easily spend a couple of hours here moving between all three. Free admission to some exhibitions; check current shows before visiting.
