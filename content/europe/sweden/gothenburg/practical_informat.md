Founded by a king and the home of the Volvo car, Gothenburg is still the second largest city in Sweden and the largest seaport in all of the Nordic Countries. **Gothenburg Practical Information **will lead to a better understanding of this seaside city.

**Getting Here**

Landvetter Airport is located nearby and is the major point of entry for Gothenburg. The area is also serviced by train with points of connection in Copenhagen and Oslo. You can also get in by way of bus, car or ship. Once in the city, there is a nice public transportation system of trams and ferries. The city is also bicycle friendly. Taxis are available.

**Climate**

Gothenburg boasts of a unique Oceanic climate influenced greatly by the warm gulf stream. It is more mild here year round than many areas at the same Northern latitude with summer temperatures ranging from the mid 50's to the low 70's with 17 hours of daylight. In the winter you can expect temperatures in the mid 20's to low 30's and just 7 hours of sunlight.

**Currency and Language**

The currency is the Sweden Krona.

The official language is Sweedish.**
**

**See and Do**

There are many free theatres here as it is a major way of life. Take in a free show, or plan to attend the Gothenburg Film Festival in February, the largest such festival in all of Scandinavia.

As the city is a seaport, make sure to stop in for a seafood dish at one of many seaside restaurants.

You'll also find a nice botanical garden, Liseberg Amusement Park, and the Archipelago Islands. These islands are accessible by ferry and are pretty.
