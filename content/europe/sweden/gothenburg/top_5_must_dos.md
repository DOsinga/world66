---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Gothenburg"
type: section
---

**Gothenburg must do'**s include visiting Liseberg, Scandinavia&rsquo;s largest amusement park, trips to the islands in the Gothenburg archipelago and shopping on the Kungsportsavenyn.

**1. Go Island Hopping in the Archipelago**

Jump on a ferry from central Gothenburg and take a daytrip to one of the island in the Gothenburg Archipelago. This beautiful archipelago has literally hundreds of picturesque islands that are perfect for daytrips and overnight excursions, for sunbathing and swimming, for picnics and for island hopping. Boats and ferries connect the islands to each other so you can visit several islands if you wish. There is also accommodation in many of the islands if you want to stay overnight.

**2. Shop on &ldquo;the Avenue&rdquo;**

The Kungsportsavenyn, or "Avenyn", is Gothenburg&rsquo;s main shopping street and lined with shops, restaurants and cafes. &ldquo;The Avenue&rdquo; runs through the town from the old part of Gothenburg to the G&ouml;taplatsen square. Many of Gothenburg&rsquo;s tourist attractions and museums are on or around the Avenyn. The Avenue is also the center of Gothenburg&rsquo;s nightlife and there are lots of bars and clubs along this long street.

You can start the day by visiting one of the museums in the area, go shopping on the Avenue, have lunch in one of the restaurants, sit in a caf&eacute; and watch the world go by, and in the evening have a drink in one of the bars or go dancing in one of the great nightclubs here.

**3. Take the Family to Liseberg Amusement Park**

Families and those who are young at heart should plan a visit to Liseberg, the largest amusement part in Scandinavia. Liseberg has an incredible amount of rides, and there are also concerts and other entertainment. You can visit Liseberg only during the summer, but special events are organized around Christmas.

**4. Visit Slottsskogen, the Castle Forest**

There are several tourist attractions and places to visit in Slottsskogen, or Castle Forest. Gothenburg&rsquo;s Natural History Museum is located here, and there is an old observatory, an animal park and a zoo for children. This is a good place to spend half a day visiting the attractions and having a relaxing picnic on the lawns.

**5. Explore the Gunnebo Castle**

Just outside Gothenburg city is the Gunnebo Castle. This castle was built in the 18th century and there is a lot to explore here. There is a park around the castle, and you can relax in the caf&eacute; or have lunch in the restaurant. Gunnebo is an easy bus trip or taxi ride from downtown Gothenburg.
