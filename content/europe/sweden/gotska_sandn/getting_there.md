---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

In summer a boat runs from Nynäshamn, south of Stockholm, arriving at Gotska Sandön after about three hours. It then continues to Fårösund on Gotland and returns to the island in the afternoon before heading back to Nynäshamn in the evening.

There is no harbour. The boat runs straight onto the sandy shore, which means it cannot land in heavy winds — it is not uncommon for visitors to have to wait an extra day before they can leave. Bring spare food. The boat is built for beaching rather than comfort, and most passengers get seasick in rough weather. It is also impossible to predict which side of the island the boat will arrive at; in the worst case, passengers may have to walk up to 9 km to or from the landing point.
