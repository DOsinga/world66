Grebbestad is a small fishing village on the northern Bohuslan coast of Sweden, picturesquely set among smooth granite cliffs and sheltered inlets. It has about 1,300 year-round residents, but in summer the population swells as Swedes flock here for the coast.

The village is famous for its oysters — the cold, clean waters of the Skagerrak produce some of Sweden's best, and you can join guided oyster safaris to harvest and taste them straight from the sea. Beyond the oysters, there is excellent sea kayaking among the skerries, good swimming from the rocks, and a handful of seafood restaurants that are reason enough to visit.
