Sweden rewards visitors who venture beyond the capital, but even Stockholm alone justifies the trip. Here are the places and experiences worth building an itinerary around.

## Stockholm

Built across 14 islands where Lake Mälaren meets the Baltic, [Stockholm](/europe/sweden/stockholm) is one of Europe's most beautiful capitals. Gamla Stan (the Old Town) is compact and walkable; the Vasa Museum houses a near-perfectly preserved 17th-century warship; the archipelago of 30,000 islands begins right at the city's edge. Allow at least three days.

## Gothenburg

Sweden's second city has a more working-class, port-city character than Stockholm, and many Swedes consider it more likeable for it. [Gothenburg](/europe/sweden/gothenburg) has excellent restaurants, a lively music scene, and the starting point for exploring the granite skerries and fishing villages of the Bohuslän coast to the north.

## Swedish Lapland

Kiruna and the surrounding area is where you come for the Northern Lights (November to March), the midnight sun (June), the Icehotel at Jukkasjärvi, dog-sledding, and reindeer. It's a long way from everywhere but the Arctic landscapes are extraordinary. [Kiruna](/europe/sweden/kiruna) itself is in the process of being physically relocated due to iron mine subsidence — an unusual thing to witness.

## Gotland

Sweden's largest island, reached by ferry from Nynäshamn or Oskarshamn, is in a different category from the mainland. [Visby](/europe/sweden/visby), the walled Hanseatic town, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The island is popular with Swedes in summer, peaceful the rest of the year. Medieval Week in August is a big deal.

## The High Coast (Höga Kusten)

North of Sundsvall, the High Coast offers some of Sweden's most dramatic scenery: steep forested cliffs dropping into the sea, deep fjord-like inlets, and small fishing villages. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site and excellent for hiking.

## Dalarna

The heartland of traditional Swedish culture — folk costumes, Dala horses, midsummer celebrations done properly. Lake Siljan and the villages around it feel like the Sweden of a picture book. [Falun](/europe/sweden/falun) has a UNESCO-listed copper mine.

## Öresund Region (Skåne)

[Malmö](/europe/sweden/malmo) is Sweden's most multicultural city and has transformed its post-industrial waterfront into an architectural showpiece. [Lund](/europe/sweden/lund) has a beautiful 12th-century cathedral and one of Sweden's top universities. The surrounding Skåne countryside is rolling farmland, quite unlike the rest of Sweden. And Copenhagen is 35 minutes away by train.

## Öland and Kalmar

[Öland](/europe/sweden/oland), connected to the mainland by bridge, is a flat island of windmills, limestone grasslands, and Viking sites. [Kalmar](/europe/sweden/kalmar) has one of Sweden's finest renaissance castles.
