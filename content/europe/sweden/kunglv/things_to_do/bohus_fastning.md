The ruins of Bohus Fortress occupy a rocky island in the Nordre Älv river, a brooding presence over the old town. Built in 1308 by the Norwegian king Haakon V, the fortress changed hands repeatedly between Norway, Denmark, and Sweden over three centuries of border wars, withstanding fourteen sieges without ever being taken by force. It was finally abandoned in the late 17th century after Sweden won permanent control of the region. You can climb the remaining tower for views over the river and surrounding landscape.
