Lidköping is a small city on the southern shore of Lake Vänern — Sweden's largest lake — at the mouth of the River Lidan. It is a pleasant, unhurried place with a tidy town centre and decent connections to the surrounding countryside.

The main attraction in the area is Läckö Castle, about 25 km north of the city on a small peninsula jutting into Lake Vänern. Built in the 13th century and later transformed into one of Sweden's finest Baroque palaces by Count Magnus Gabriel De la Gardie in the 1650s, Läckö is worth the trip for its setting alone — a gleaming white castle mirrored in the lake, surrounded by nothing but forest and water. The interior has been carefully restored.

Lidköping itself is close to the Kinnekulle plateau, a nature reserve known for its wildflowers and walking trails. The town also has a factory shop for Rörstrand porcelain, one of Sweden's oldest ceramic manufacturers.
