Linköping is an old university city in the heart of Östergötland, Sweden’s seventh-largest and the place where SAAB builds its fighter jets. The aerospace industry gives the town a surprisingly international feel for a mid-sized Swedish city.

The cathedral is one of Sweden’s finest medieval churches, and the nearby castle dates back to the same era. Most of the town’s older buildings have been relocated to Gamla Linköping, an open-air museum village that works as a pleasant afternoon stroll. The Swedish Air Force Museum is excellent and free -- worth a visit even if military aviation is not your thing.

The university campus at Vallaskogen is lively, and the surrounding Östgöta countryside is flat, green, and good for cycling.
