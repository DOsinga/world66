Run by Lund University at Sölvegatan 2, this compact museum holds collections of Greek and Roman pottery, sculpture, coins, and everyday objects assembled over centuries of academic collecting. It is one of the more surprising institutions in the city — a genuine slice of Mediterranean antiquity in the Swedish south, and free to visit.
