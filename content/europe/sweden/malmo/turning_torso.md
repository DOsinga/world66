Santiago Calatrava's 190-metre twisting skyscraper dominates the Malmo skyline and has become the city's most recognizable landmark since its completion in 2005. The residential tower is not open to the public, but the Western Harbour district around its base is worth exploring for its inventive contemporary architecture and waterfront promenades with views across the Oresund to Copenhagen.
