Nora is a small and quiet picturesque town in the province Västmanland (Örebro county). Its roots are early medieval and it became a town in 1643. Its history is based on the mining industry and the town has an oldfashioned character with wooden houses from the 18th and 19th centuries and streets of cobble stones.

The railway between Nora and Örebro, built mainly for iron transport was completed in 1856, the oldest in Sweden. Today it is a museum line with old trains.
