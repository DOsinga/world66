Nyköping is one of Sweden's oldest towns, founded in the 12th century on the estuary of the Nyköpingsån river where it meets the Baltic. It was briefly a royal seat — Duke Erik and Duke Valdemar were starved to death in its castle during the notorious "Nyköping Banquet" of 1317 — and the ruined castle tower still stands in the town centre as a reminder of that grim episode.

Today it's a quiet regional city of around 35,000, with a pleasant old town of wooden buildings and a waterfront that comes alive in summer. The castle area and a small historical museum fill a couple of hours easily.

Nyköping is probably best known to travellers as the location of **Stockholm Skavsta Airport**, about 5 kilometres outside the city, used by budget airlines as a southern Stockholm hub. The city is about 100 kilometres southwest of Stockholm by road or rail.
