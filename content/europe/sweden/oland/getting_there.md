---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Öland is connected to the mainland by the Öland Bridge, which at 6 km was the longest bridge in Europe when it opened in 1972. The bridge links the island to [Kalmar](/europe/sweden/kalmar) on the mainland. There is no toll and crossing takes about 10 minutes by car.

Kalmar has a railway station with connections to Stockholm (about 3 hours) and Malmö. Buses run across the bridge from Kalmar bus station to Borgholm and other points on the island. If you are arriving by train, the most practical approach is to take a bus or hire a car in Kalmar — the island is long and the distances between sights make a car or bicycle the sensible choice for getting around once you're there.
