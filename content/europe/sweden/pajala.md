Pajala is a small town in the far north of Sweden, close to the Finnish border on the Torne River. It sits well above the Arctic Circle, which means midnight sun in summer and deep cold and darkness in winter.

The town is best known for two things. The first is Lars Levi Laestadius, the 19th-century priest whose revivalist movement still has followers across northern Scandinavia — there is a small museum in town dedicated to his life and work. The second is Mikael Niemi's novel *Popular Music from Vittula*, which put Pajala on the literary map and captures the peculiar character of this bilingual Swedish-Finnish border region with dark humour and warmth.

Pajala is not a place many travellers pass through, but if you are exploring Swedish Lapland it offers an authentic look at life in the far north — Tornedalen culture, vast forests, and the kind of quiet that only the Arctic provides.
