## Currency

Sweden uses the Swedish Krona (SEK). Sweden is one of the most cashless societies in the world — card payment (Visa, Mastercard) is accepted almost everywhere, including small cafes, market stalls, and buses. Many places actively refuse cash. Carry a card with no foreign transaction fees. ATMs (bankomats) are found at airports, train stations, and most town centres.

## Visas

Sweden is a Schengen member. EU and EEA citizens can enter freely. US, Canadian, Australian, UK, and most other Western passport holders can stay visa-free for up to 90 days within any 180-day period. Check the Swedish Migration Agency website for current requirements if you're unsure.

## Language

Swedish is the official language. English is spoken widely and confidently — most Swedes under 60 speak very good English, and you'll rarely need Swedish to get by as a tourist. Signs in major cities often appear in English too.

## Health

No vaccinations are required for entry. Tap water is safe to drink throughout Sweden. EU citizens should carry their EHIC (European Health Insurance Card) for access to public healthcare. Others should have travel health insurance. Pharmacies (apotek) are easy to find and can handle minor ailments.

## Safety

Sweden is a safe destination. Petty theft from cars and tourist areas does occur in cities, so take standard precautions. The terrorism threat level has been at elevated (level 3) in recent years. Emergency number is 112.

## Electricity

230V/50Hz. Type F (Schuko) and Type C plugs are standard. UK and US travellers will need adaptors.

## Tipping

Tipping is not obligatory but is appreciated. Rounding up the bill or adding 10% in restaurants is the norm. Taxi drivers usually expect rounding up to the nearest 10 SEK.

## Opening Hours

Most shops open Monday to Friday 10:00–18:00, Saturday 10:00–16:00, and increasingly on Sundays too. Systembolaget (the state alcohol monopoly) closes at 18:00 on weekdays, 15:00 on Saturdays, and is shut on Sundays. Many museums close on Mondays.

## Internet

Sweden has excellent mobile coverage and fast broadband. Free Wi-Fi is available in most cafes, hotels, and public spaces. Swedish SIM cards can be bought cheaply at convenience stores and supermarkets (7-Eleven, Pressbyrån, ICA).

## Tourist Information

Visit Sweden (visitsweden.com) is the official tourism body. Local tourist offices (turistbyrå) can be found in most towns and cities and are generally excellent.
