Travel Guide

**
Halsingland** is a landscape in central part of Sweden, about 250 km north of Stockholm. Hälsingland has about 150 000 visible ancient monuments.

The name Halsingland was first mentioned by Adam of Bremen in the late 1000's. The interpretations put forward has been a matter of either a narrow part of the water (compare with Helsingborg/Helsingör), ie a narrow watercourse, or a narrow piece of land.

Halsingland is known for its old magnificent over a century old farms, so-called Halsinge farms. These farms are often two or three stories high and built to
display the farmers wealth and independence. Much ingenuity has been
required to decorate their interiors, combining noble fashion with
traditional materials and techniques. A couple of these farms have been nominated to be included on UNESCO´s list of world heritages.

Halsingland is unique in Sweden being the only landscape, where the name is some times used as a mild curse (as an euphemism for hell), a practice that can be traced as far back as to the 1690s)
