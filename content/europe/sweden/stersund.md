Travel Guide

The city of **Östersund** has about 60000 citizens and is the capital of the county Jämtland. The city spreads from the eastern shore of *Lake Storsjön* to the island of *Frösön*.

The city is among the most beautiful in Sweden with its lakeside location, mountain view and rich nature. The winters are long and cold which makes the surroundings perfect for winter activities like skiing, snowboarding and ice skating. Nearby *Åre* is Scandinavias premier ski resort. Summertime Östersund benefits from long summer nights with daylight up to 22 hours a day. The highlight of this season is the *Storsjöyran* (Great Lake Dizzy) festival in the end of July, which attracts people from all over northern Europe.

The city's main attraction is Jamtli History Museum where visitors get the opportunity to take part in Swedish 18th and 19th century life. The indoor Museum is famous for the *Överhogdal* tapestry, dated to the 10th century.

Östersund is also capital of the self-declared Republic of Jämtland and has its own president, *Ewert Ljusberg*.
