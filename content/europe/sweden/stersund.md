Östersund is the capital of Jämtland county and one of the most beautifully situated cities in Sweden, spread along the eastern shore of Lake Storsjön with the island of Frösön just offshore and mountain views in every direction. With about 60,000 inhabitants, it is the only city of any size in the Swedish interior north of the Dalälven.

Winters here are long and cold, which makes the surrounding countryside ideal for skiing, snowboarding, and ice skating. [Åre](/europe/sweden/are), Scandinavia's premier ski resort, is less than an hour west by train. In summer, Östersund benefits from near-endless daylight — up to 22 hours a day — and the Storsjöyran festival in late July draws visitors from across northern Europe with music and celebrations on the lakeshore.

The city's main attraction is Jamtli, an open-air history museum where costumed interpreters bring eighteenth and nineteenth-century Swedish life to vivid life. The indoor museum houses the Överhogdal tapestry, a remarkable textile dated to the tenth century. Östersund is also, somewhat improbably, the capital of the self-declared Republic of Jämtland — a long-running joke with its own president and occasional diplomatic incidents.
