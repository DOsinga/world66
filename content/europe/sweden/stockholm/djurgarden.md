Djurgården is Stockholm's green lung — a leafy island that manages to pack in the city's best museums while still feeling like an escape from urban life. The Vasa Museum is the headliner, housing a nearly intact 17th-century warship that sank on its maiden voyage and was salvaged 333 years later. Nearby, Skansen is the world's oldest open-air museum, with historic buildings from across Sweden and a small zoo featuring Nordic wildlife. The ABBA Museum draws fans with interactive exhibits where you can sing and dance alongside holograms of the band. Beyond the museums, the island rewards aimless wandering — oak-lined paths wind past waterfront meadows, and Rosendals Trädgård is a biodynamic garden café perfect for a fika break. Rent a bike or take the vintage tram from Norrmalmstorg, and budget at least a full day.
