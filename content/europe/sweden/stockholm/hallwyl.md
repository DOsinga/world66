The Hallwyl Museum is a remarkably preserved aristocratic home on one of Stockholm's busiest shopping streets. Countess Wilhelmina von Hallwyl spent decades filling this palatial townhouse with art, silver, porcelain, weapons, and furniture — then donated the entire collection to the Swedish state in 1920. The rooms remain furnished exactly as they were, giving an unusually intimate look at how the Swedish upper class lived around 1900.

The building itself is worth the visit: a grand courtyard, ornate painted ceilings, and a billiard room that feels like stepping into another century. Guided tours run regularly and bring the family's story to life.
