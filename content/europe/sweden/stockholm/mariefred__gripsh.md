Mariefred is a small town on Lake Mälaren, about an hour west of Stockholm, best known for Gripsholm Castle — a well-preserved Renaissance fortress with a portrait collection spanning five centuries. The castle sits right on the water and the surrounding streets are full of wooden houses, cafes, and antique shops.

The best way to get there in summer is on the S/S Mariefred, a coal-fired steamboat that has been making the three-and-a-half-hour run from Stockholm since 1903. You can return the same way or take the narrow-gauge heritage railway from Mariefred to Läggesta, where regular SJ trains connect back to Stockholm in under an hour.
