Östermalm is Stockholm's most polished neighborhood, where old money meets Nordic design and the streets are lined with elegant apartment buildings from the late 1800s. The star attraction is Östermalms Saluhall, a beautifully restored 1888 food hall where you can graze on reindeer charcuterie, Swedish cheese, and fresh seafood at marble counters. Strandvägen, the grand waterfront boulevard, is one of the most handsome streets in Scandinavia — lined with linden trees and ornate stone facades, it's ideal for a leisurely stroll toward Djurgården. Designer boutiques and upscale galleries cluster around Biblioteksgatan and Sturegatan. The neighborhood isn't just about spending money, though — Humlegården park offers a quiet green retreat in the middle of it all. Östermalm is where you come to see how well-heeled Stockholm lives and to eat some of the city's finest food.
