A small island in the southern Stockholm archipelago, about two and a half hours from the city by a combination of commuter train and ferry. Utö has been inhabited since the Middle Ages, when iron ore was mined here — you can still explore the old mine shafts.

The island offers some of the best swimming in the archipelago. Sandy beaches on the south side, smooth granite cliffs on the north, and water that is surprisingly clear. Rent a bike at the harbour and ride the island's quiet roads — it takes a couple of hours to loop the whole thing.

For food, Utö Värdshus is the grand old option, serving Swedish classics in a building that dates to the 1700s. Båtshaket by the harbour does simpler fare and is a good spot for a beer in the sun. In summer the island fills up with day-trippers, so come early or stay overnight to get the best of it.

The ferry leaves from Årsta Brygga, reachable by commuter rail from Stockholm Central.
