Sundsvall sits on the coast of northern Sweden, squeezed between two forested hills — the North and South Stadsberget — with the Gulf of Bothnia stretching east. The town burned to the ground in 1888 and was rebuilt entirely in stone, giving the centre an unusually grand look for a city of its size. Locals call it Stenstan, the Stone City.

The rebuilt centre is handsome and walkable, with wide streets lined by ornate facades that wouldn't look out of place in a much larger European capital. Storgatan is the main commercial street, and the surrounding blocks hold most of the cafes and restaurants. For views, climb either of the Stadsberget hills — both have marked trails and the panorama from the top takes in the city, the harbour, and the Alnö island bridge.

Sundsvall is a natural stopping point on the drive or train journey up the Swedish coast. The [High Coast](/europe/sweden/the_high_coast) (Höga Kusten), a UNESCO World Heritage site of dramatic uplift coastline, begins just north of the city and makes for an excellent day trip or onward journey.
