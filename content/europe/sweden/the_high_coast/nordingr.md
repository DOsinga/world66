Nordingrå is a church village at the heart of the [High Coast](/europe/sweden/the_high_coast), the UNESCO-listed stretch of Sweden's Bothnian coastline where post-glacial land uplift has created a dramatic landscape of steep granite hills, sheltered inlets, and old fishing harbours.

The surroundings are famous for their beauty — the Rotsidan red granite cliffs, the viewpoint at Skuleberget, and the quiet coves where you can swim in summer make this one of the most scenic areas in northern Sweden. The pace of life here is slow and deliberate, which is exactly the point.
