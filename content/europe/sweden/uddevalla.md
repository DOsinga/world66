Uddevalla is a town of about 50,000 on the western coast of [Sweden](/europe/sweden), at the head of the Byfjorden inlet. It was historically a shipyard and fishing town, and the harbour still defines the place even though the big yards closed decades ago.

The town is a useful base for exploring the Bohuslän coast — the rocky islands and fishing villages to the west are among the most beautiful coastline in Scandinavia. The Bohusleden hiking trail passes through the area. Uddevalla's shell banks at Kuröd, an Ice Age deposit of oyster shells, are a minor geological curiosity and a popular school-trip destination.

Uddevalla is connected by rail and bus to [Gothenburg](/europe/sweden/goteborg) (about 80km south) and the rest of the west coast.
