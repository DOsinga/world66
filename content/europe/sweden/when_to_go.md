---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Sweden stretches from the temperate south to the Arctic north, so the best time to visit depends heavily on where you're going and what you want to do.

## Summer (June–August)

Peak season. Long days and warm temperatures make this the most popular time to visit. Stockholm averages around 22–25°C in July; the south gets warm enough for swimming. Above the Arctic Circle, the midnight sun means 24-hour daylight in June and early July — a genuinely strange and memorable experience. Expect higher prices and larger crowds at popular destinations. Book accommodation well ahead.

Midsummer (the weekend nearest 21 June) is the biggest celebration in the Swedish calendar. The whole country takes the weekend off. Maypoles, herring, aquavit, and folk dancing — it's worth planning around. Be aware that much of Sweden effectively shuts down for Midsummer weekend.

## Autumn (September–October)

A beautiful and underrated time. The forests turn spectacular shades of red and gold, particularly in Dalarna and Lapland. Temperatures are still pleasant in September. Crowds thin and prices drop. Mushroom and berry picking is taken very seriously.

## Winter (November–March)

Southern Sweden winters are grey and mild; not much to recommend them unless you like Christmas markets (which are good). In northern Sweden it's a different story. Swedish Lapland in winter means Northern Lights, dog-sledding, ice hotels, and pristine snow. Abisko National Park near Kiruna is consistently one of the best places in the world to see the aurora borealis, thanks to a microclimate that keeps skies clear.

February and March bring reliable snow and often sunny days — better conditions than the dark of December and January.

## Spring (April–May)

Shoulder season with few crowds. Snow lingers in the north into April and May. Southern Sweden wakes up quickly — May can be glorious, with blooming flowers and long evenings.

## Key Dates

- **Midsummer** — late June
- **Crayfish parties** — August (a social institution)
- **Advent and Christmas markets** — late November through December, particularly good in Stockholm, Gothenburg, and Lund
- **Northern Lights season** — November through March in Lapland
- **Midnight Sun** — June in the far north (Abisko, Kiruna, Gällivare)
