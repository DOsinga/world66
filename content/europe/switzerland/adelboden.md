Adelboden is a classic Swiss alpine village that punches well above its weight. Tucked into the Engstlige valley in the Bernese Oberland at 1,350 metres, it draws serious skiers in winter and hikers in summer without ever feeling like a theme park. The village has kept its wooden chalets and church steeple intact while building a genuinely impressive lift network that connects to Lenk and the wider Saanenland ski area.

In winter, the slopes above Adelboden are best known among racing fans: the men's World Cup slalom and giant slalom are held here each January on the Chuenisbärgli, one of the most demanding technical courses on the circuit. For recreational skiers, the terrain is varied and relatively uncrowded compared to the Jungfrau heavyweights just over the ridge. The views across to the Wildstrubel and Niesen are stunning on a clear day.

Summer brings hikers, mountain bikers and paragliders. The valley is laced with well-marked trails — the walk to the Engstligen Falls, one of Switzerland's highest, is an easy half-day that rewards with spray and noise at the base of a double cascade dropping 600 metres off the plateau above. The landscape is gentler than the dramatic cliffs of the Lauterbrunnen valley, but that means fewer crowds and better chances of finding a quiet Beizli serving rösti with nobody waiting for your table.
