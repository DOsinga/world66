Offering: Mongolian barbecue, for those of you who may be unfamiliar with it, is a self-serve buffet of raw ingredients from which the diner selects his own delectable combination which is then cooked while he waits. The meat choices at Imperial China include beef, chicken, pork, lamb, fish, and shrimp. Sliced vegetables, onions, and white mushrooms, sprouts, canned pineapple, and other non-meat items round out the selection.

Tell the host you want the barbecue and he will give you little red tickets, one for each member of your group. Pick up a plate at the buffet and choose the combination of meats and veggies, etc., that look good to you, remembering that they will cook-down. At the far end of the buffet there is a selection of oils--garlic, sesame, and chili--in large glass mugs. Choose one or a combination, but you need some oil on your food for it to cook properly. One full scoop of oil would be the minimum for a full plate; use less if you have not filled your plate to overflowing. Take your plate and red ticket to the cook at the griddle. Stay close while your food is cooking even if you aren't fascinated by the process because when it is about half done the cook will offer to add rice noodles to the mixture and you need to be available to say "no" or to indicate how many you want. While you wait, check out the other additions to the meal (behind you as you hand your plate to the cook). There is a selection of sauces--Soy, Szechuan, Imperial China, sweet and sour, chili sweet and sour, etc.--silverware (the tables are set with chopsticks only), and two cauldrons of rice--one white and sticky, the other fried and aromatic. Look out for the red sauce in the small lidded bowl by the silverware; it is burn-your-palate hot.

Atmosphere: Light and airy **upstairs** room. Entrance decorated with life-size replicas of several terracotta warriors and a terracotta horse. The rest of the room is nicely understated Chinese/modern with a little rough stone and bamboo thrown in.

Money: 19.50CHF (€12, $16.50) for a run through the buffet plus rice and sauce. Drinks not included. A la carte menu available.

Directions: Half block north of Bundesplatz on the right/east side of Bärenplatz, above Restaurant Gfeller. Facing the restaurant, use the Gfeller doorway farthest to the left and look for the stairs.
