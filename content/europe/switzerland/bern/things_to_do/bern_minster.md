The Bern Minster is the tallest cathedral in Switzerland, built in Gothic style between the 15th and 19th centuries. The main portal has a remarkable carved tympanum of the Last Judgement with over 200 figures. The 100-metre tower — completed only in 1893 — can be climbed for panoramic views over the Old Town, the Aare River bends below, and on clear days the Alps. The cathedral stands in the heart of the UNESCO World Heritage Old Town.
