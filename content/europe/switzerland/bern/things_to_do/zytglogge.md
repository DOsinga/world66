The Zytglogge is a medieval clock tower from the early 13th century and the symbolic centre of Bern. At four minutes before each hour a display of mechanical figures — a jester, a procession of bears, knights, and the figure of Father Time — plays out above the clock face. The tower originally served as a watchtower and city gate; guided tours can be arranged to see the clockwork mechanism inside. Albert Einstein, who lived nearby, famously used the tower in thought experiments about time and relativity.
