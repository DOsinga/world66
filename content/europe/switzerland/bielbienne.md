Biel/Bienne is Switzerland's only officially bilingual city — German and French coexist here on every street sign, in every shop, and in the everyday conversations of its residents. The city sits at the northeastern end of Lake Biel, where the Jura mountains meet the Swiss plateau.

The old town climbs the hillside above the lake with well-preserved medieval lanes and a ring church dating to the fifteenth century. Down by the water, the modern city is the centre of Switzerland's watchmaking industry — Omega, Swatch, and Rolex all have headquarters here. The lake itself is beautiful, with boat services running to St. Peter's Island, where Rousseau spent what he called the happiest weeks of his life. The vineyards along the northern shore produce some of Switzerland's best white wines.
