title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Switzerland"
type: section
---

**Switzerland Budget Travel** allows you to go on a nice vacation to Switzerland at a rate that you can afford. The key to budgeting when you travel is to look for the cheapest methods of travel that you can, along with cheap lodging and excursions. The more money that you save, the more things that you will be able to do on your vacation.

**Budget Airlines**

The most expensive part of any vacation is going to be the airfare, and the further you go, the more expensive it is going to be. One of the best ways to save money on your vacation to Switzerland is to look into one of the numerous low cost airlines. When looking at the low cost airlines you can also look into the no-frill flights, which means no extras at all. Some of the low cost airlines that you need to check out are British Airways, Blue 1 and Easy Jet.

**Transportation**

When it comes to getting around Switzerland your cheapest option is to take advantage of the public transportation that Switzerland has to offer, which includes trains, buses, trams, mountain cable carts and boats. You want to avoid getting the Standard Fare tickets because they can be expensive. The good news is that there are numerous discounts available, which will get you cheaper tickets. One method you need to look into are discount passes, which can get you up to 50% savings on fares and passes for the Swiss public transportation system. These discount passes are designed for non-Swiss residents.

**Accommodations**

When looking for lodging in Switzerland, if you want to save money your best bet is to stay away from hotels. You can actually find chalets and apartments cheaper than hotels. You can even look into local youth hostels. Switzerland has over 50 of them, which will save you more money and are a great place to stay if you don’t mind having only the basics. The cheapest accommodations in Switzerland are going to be the numerous campgrounds located throughout the country. If you need more than just a tent to get comfortable, some campsites even rent out cottages.

**Food**

To save money on food, the best thing you can do is to cook your own meals, which is possible if you are staying in an apartment or in certain youth hostels. When buying food from the supermarket look for the cheaper supermarkets, Denner or ALDI, or the larger chains, COOP and MIGROS. When going on day trips bring along your own food and water because the supermarkets in the big towns are cheaper than anything you will find in the tourist areas you might be visiting.

**Day Trips**

The best way to save money on day trips is to purchase a rail pass, and to use the special discount pass that is offered to tourists. The rail passes will allow you to explore the mountains by train for free, as long as you avoid the special scenic trains. You can also go on boat trips for free using the same rail pass. The rail pass will also get you free entrance into 400 different museums in Switzerland.
