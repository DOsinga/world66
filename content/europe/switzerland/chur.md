Chur claims the title of oldest city in Switzerland, with evidence of settlement stretching back more than 5,000 years. Tucked into the Rhine valley at the foot of the Alps, it is the capital of Graubunden, the country's largest and most sparsely populated canton. The old town is small enough to walk in an hour, but dense with crooked lanes, painted facades, and quiet squares that reward a slower pace.

The city works as a gateway to some of Switzerland's most spectacular mountain scenery. The Glacier Express and Bernina Express both depart from Chur, threading through valleys and over passes that justify Switzerland's reputation for dramatic train journeys. In winter, the ski resorts of Arosa and Lenzerheide are less than an hour away. In summer, the hiking trails that fan out into the surrounding mountains are world-class.

Chur's old town is refreshingly car-free. The cathedral, dating from the 12th century, anchors the upper town, while the pedestrianised lower streets are lined with shops, cafes, and the occasional fountain. For a small city, it punches above its weight in restaurants and bars, fueled in part by a steady flow of outdoor enthusiasts passing through on their way to the mountains.
