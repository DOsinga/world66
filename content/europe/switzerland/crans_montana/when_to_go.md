---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**Winter (December–April)** is the primary season. The ski area opens in late November when conditions allow, but January and February are the peak months for snow and atmosphere. The plateau's south-facing aspect and high altitude mean the resort gets exceptional sunshine even in midwinter, which is pleasant but can soften the snow quickly in March. Easter can be good if the season runs late.

**Summer (June–September)** is underrated. The golf courses are the main draw, but the hiking and mountain biking are excellent, the crowds are a fraction of what they are in winter, and prices drop considerably. The European Masters golf tournament takes place in early September and draws a knowledgeable crowd.

**Spring and autumn** are quiet. Most ski infrastructure closes in late April; the golf season opens in late April or May depending on snow. October and November are dead months — the resort has not much going on and many businesses close.

The resort sits at 1,500 metres and the glacier reaches 3,000 metres, so even in a poor snow year the upper mountain usually has reasonable skiing. Crans-Montana is one of the sunniest resorts in Switzerland — that's the main thing to know about the climate.
