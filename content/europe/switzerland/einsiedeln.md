Einsiedeln is a small town in the canton of Schwyz, sitting at about 880 meters in a broad valley below the Pre-Alps. It is about an hour from [Zurich](/europe/switzerland/zurich) by train, but feels a world away — quiet, green, and dominated by one of the most impressive baroque churches in Switzerland.

The reason most people come here is the Benedictine abbey, Kloster Einsiedeln. Founded in the 10th century, it is one of Switzerland's most important pilgrimage sites, drawing visitors to the Black Madonna, a wooden statue housed in a dark marble chapel inside the church. The abbey church itself is worth the trip even if you are not a pilgrim — Caspar Moosbrugger designed it in the early 18th century, and the interior is a riot of frescoes, stucco, and gold.

Beyond the abbey, Einsiedeln is a good base for the outdoors. In summer, hiking trails fan out into the surrounding hills. In winter, the area is known for cross-country skiing, and nearby Hoch-Ybrig offers proper alpine runs if you want something steeper.
