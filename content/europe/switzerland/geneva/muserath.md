The Musée Rath on Place Neuve is Geneva's oldest public museum building, built in 1826 in a neoclassical style. It doesn't hold a permanent collection of its own; instead it hosts rotating temporary exhibitions, typically focused on fine arts and drawing from the collections of the city's other museums or from international loans. The quality of shows is generally high — past exhibitions have featured Swiss painters like Ferdinand Hodler and major thematic surveys of European art. Check the current programme before visiting, as the museum closes between exhibitions.
