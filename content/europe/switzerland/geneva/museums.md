Geneva punches well above its weight for museums. The Musee d'Art et Histoire is Switzerland's only encyclopedic museum, covering western culture from antiquity to the present. The Musee Rath on Place Neuve hosts strong temporary exhibitions. For something different, the Automobile and Cycle Museum on rue des Bains displays over 120 vintage cars and motorcycles, all built before World War II.

The Musee d'Histoire des Sciences occupies a neoclassical villa in the Parc de l'Ariana with free admission. The Barbier-Mueller on rue Calvin has an outstanding collection of tribal and ancient art. MAMCO, Geneva's contemporary art museum, sprawls across a converted factory near Plainpalais.

Most museums close on Mondays. The first Sunday of each month is free at the municipal museums.
