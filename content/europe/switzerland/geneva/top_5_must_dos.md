---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Geneva"
type: section
---

**1. ****Place Neuve -** Considered to be Geneva's cultural
epicenter, this historic square is home to many historical landmarks
such as The Rath Museum, Conservatory of Music, and the Grand Theatre.
Located in the old town of Geneva, this beautiful and scenic park is
not to be missed on your visit. The Parc des Bastions is accessible to
the south of Place Neuve, and here you will find the famous statue of
Henri Dufour, a Swiss national hero, located in the center. The
Neoclassical temples are ornate and detailed, and the entire Place
Neuve is encompassed by the high retaining walls of the old town.

**2. ****Geneva's lakefront -** is one of the city's greatest assets,
and a leisure stroll down the lakeside quays is a must do. The
Jet'd'eau spits water as high as 450 feet into the air, and on a clear
day it is possible to see Mont Blanc! As you walk along the lakefront
admire the beautifully landscaped flowerbeds and elegant old homes.
Swans, ducks, and diving birds have taken up residence on the lake, as
well as many colorful boats. On the right bank you can see the
Brunswick Monument which contains the tomb of Charles II, Duke of
Brunswick, and after crossing the Mont Blanc bridge make sure not to
miss the Jardin anglais.

**3. ****Flower Clock **- located within the Jardin Anglais is a
symbol of the world renown Geneva watch industry. This masterpiece of
technology and floral art has been resting in the English Garden since
1955. The Flower Clock is decorated with over 6,500 flowers, which vary
with the seasons. A good piece of trivia: the seconds hard of the
Geneva Flower Clock is the largest in the world!

**4. ****Treille Promenade - **dating back to the 16th century, this
romantic promenade was first used as an observation and artillery post
for the city. Take a walk under the rows of Chestnut trees or have a
rest on what is said to be the longest wooden bench in the world. The
statue of Rictet de Rochemont and Geneva's official chestnut tree call
this lovely promenade home. According to tradition, the blossoming of
the first bud from the official chestnut tree marks the arrival of
spring.

**5. ****The Schtrumpfs -** located in the Quartier des Grottes at
23-29 rue Louis-Favre is a charming little neighborhood behind the
train station. Built in 1982 and 1984, these three unusual structures
seem to resemble the popular cartoon characters, The Smurfs. These
delightful buildings will give any visitor a run for your imagination.
If you love architecture, you won't want to miss these fine examples of
"you can do anything you set your mind to".
