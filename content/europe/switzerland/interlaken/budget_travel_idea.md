One thing that more people are trying to do is to save money while on vacation. There is a variety of different reasons that people are looking to save money while on vacation, but the main reason is the more money you save the better you will be able to afford the vacation. While visiting **Interlaken Budget Travel** ideas are going to be a top priority because they will allow you to save the biggest amount of money.

**Transportation**

While traveling throughout Interlaken you will want to use the public transportation that Interlaken has to offer. The public transportation is going to include trains and buses to help get your around town. One thing to keep in mind with the public transportation in Interlaken is that it can be rather expensive, especially if you opt for a standard fare ticket. Since you will be in the area on vacation, as long as you are not a resident of Switzerland you can qualify for a discount pass for public transportation, which can save you up to 50 percent.

**Accommodations**

To save money on your sleeping arrangements while in Interlaken you will want to look into chalets and apartments. The chalets and apartments are usually cheaper than hotels, but they will also save you money because you can do your own cooking. Youth hostels are another great option for saving money on sleeping arraignments, but usually you have a set of rules that must be followed. If you enjoy camping, you need to look into the campgrounds that are located throughout Interlaken, as long as weather permits.

**Airlines**

When flying into Interlaken you will want to look at the budget airlines because they will offer you some of the cheapest flights possible. When looking at the cheap flights you also want to look at the different types of flights that are being offered. Some of the budget airlines that you want to look into when flying to Interlaken are Easy Jet and Blue 1.

**Excursions**

Saving money on excursions is easy if you opt for the special rail pass. If you elect to get a rail pass you can get into numerous museums free of charge, but you can also explore the beautiful mountains for no extra charge--and the best part is there is no hiking involved. Other excursions that you can take part in Interlaken are the Japanese Gardens of Friendship and mountain hiking.

**Dining Out**

Eating out is going to end up costing you the most money, especially if you are feeding an entire family. Depending on where you are staying, you can cook your own meals to help cut down on the cost. This can be done in certain youth hostels and all apartments. If you go on a day trip bring your own food and water, it saves money from having to eat out all the time.
