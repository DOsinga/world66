---
title: "Festivals in Interlaken"
type: section
---

**Interlaken festivals **celebrate the local area's history,
geography and music. The festivals offer something for everyone, even
fans of country music and trucks.

**William Tell Open Air Theatre Festival**

The summer's largest festival begins in mid-June and runs through
September. It pays homage to Interlaken's local hero, William Tell. The
lengthy festival includes nearly 200 performers and dozens of
performances each Thursday through Sunday.
**

Summer Music Festivals**

The three-day Greenfield music festival is held in mid-June each year
and features more than 40 international, local and up-and-coming
bands. If you can get a pass, the three-day ticket includes the
performances, round-trip train travel within Switzerland to Interlaken,
camping and parking. It is a deal which would be hard to beat.

The Jungfrau Music Festival pairs experienced musicians with young
musicians in an effort to improve and promote music in the Interlaken
communities. The festival is held in early July.
**Other Summer Festivals**

At the end of June is the International Trucker Fair and Country
Festival. Although a traveler might not choose to visit Interlaken
solely for this festival, it may be of interest to those who are curious
how the Swiss celebrate country. The festival has been held for nearly
20 years.

The Grindelwald Yodelling Festival takes place in the
nearby Bernese Oberland in early July. The festival is based at Bachläger
Alp on First Mountain.

August 1 is Swiss National Day. Celebrations include parades,
fireworks, yodeling and bonfires to honor Switzerland's birthday.
Travelers who aren't interested in the celebrations should keep in mind
this is a national holiday in Switzerland.

Early September holds the Jungfrau Marathon. The course is the
standard 26-mile distance, but it covers an altitude change of 1,800
meters. It has been called "The World's Most Beautiful Marathon."
**Winter Festivals**

The new year is welcomed by Interlaken's youth communities during the
Harder Potschete festival each January. The event consists of a parade
from the Market Square. The young people of Interlaken wear carved masks
to honor the tradition of the mountain spirits. After the parade are
concerts and other activities.

Other winter events and celebrations move to the mountains and ski
areas. Each year brings a new event, race or competition.
**Spring Festivals**

The Interlaken Classics Music Festival is held each March and April.
Similar to the summer Jungfrau Festival, the Classics Festival shows off
young local talent. It is considered by many to be the most important
Interlaken festival related to music.
