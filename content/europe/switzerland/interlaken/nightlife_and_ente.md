---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Interlaken"
type: section
---

If you are looking for **Interlaken nightlife** then you have come to the right place as this city has the most per capita rates of nightclubs of anywhere in Switzerland. This town has a lot to keep visitors coming back. Tourism is big as much of the business in town is short term and the clientele that visit the bars change weekly.

**A Bar for Everyone**

The Victoria bar is very folksy and you can be sure to pick up a drinking buddy. This is found in the Victoria-Jungfrau Hotel. If you are in the all pedestrian area of Junfraustrasse, stop in for some wine and food at the Sternen Bar. The food is excellent. If you like a more upscale version of an Irish pub then stop in at Buddy’s Pub which is found in the Hotel Splendid.

If you are looking to completely relax and let your hair down you should visit the Brasserie 17. This bar serves cocktails and ales and has highly recommended chicken wings and ribs. On Thursday nights you can normally see a concert. You can even stop by the Mexican bar El Azteca that has live music on Fridays and Saturdays and plenty of great tequila.

**Dancing Fever**

If you like to dance, a popular choice for the locals is the High-Life Disco. You can be sure to boogie to tunes from the 1970s and 1980s. Another great choice for dancing and drinking is Johnny Carlton’s Club. If you are in your 30s or 40s and looking for a nice night out mingling, then stop by the Hotel Metropole and step into Black and White. The fee to get into a disco is about $6 USD, but this only applies on Friday and Saturday as the entry fee is waived for other days of the week.

**Experience the Folklore of Switzerland**

There is also a huge emphasis on folklore and folk music. The Tellfreilichtspiele is a Teutonic morality play that is put on in Matten. The amphitheater is about a 15-minute walk from the city center. The 2.5 hour show costs about $40 and is presented at 8 p.m. on Thursday and Saturday. It is only done during the summer months from mid-June to the beginning of September.

The Casino Kursall presents the Swiss folklore show which combines dinner and a show. This is performed from June through to the end of October. Besides tickets which cost about $20 USD there is a fixed price menu that ranges from $45 to $70 USD. The show has plenty of yodeling and other quirky performances and can get a bit raucous at times.
