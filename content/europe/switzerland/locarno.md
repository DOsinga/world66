Locarno sits at the northern tip of Lake Maggiore in the Italian-speaking canton of Ticino, making it one of the warmest cities in [Switzerland](/europe/switzerland). The Piazza Grande, one of the finest public squares in the country, opens directly onto the lakeshore and every August becomes the open-air cinema of the Locarno Film Festival, one of the oldest and most respected film festivals in Europe.

The old town rises behind the piazza in a tangle of arcaded lanes and pastel-coloured houses. Above the town, the Santuario della Madonna del Sasso perches on a rocky outcrop reached by funicular, offering views across the lake to the surrounding mountains. The Cardada-Cimetta cable car continues higher still, climbing to over 1,600 metres for panoramic views stretching from the Swiss Alps to the Po Valley on clear days.

The lake itself, palm trees along the promenade, and the mild climate give Locarno a distinctly Mediterranean feel unusual for Switzerland.
