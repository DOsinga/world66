Schiers is a small village in the Prattigau valley in the Swiss canton of Graubunden, on the road between Landquart and the famous resorts of [Klosters](/europe/switzerland/klosters) and [Davos](/europe/switzerland/davos). The valley is tucked between the Ratikon and Silvretta mountain ranges, and Schiers sits at the western end where the valley is wide and sunny. It is a quiet, affordable alternative to the glamorous resorts further up the road.

The village has a handsome old centre with traditional Prattigau wooden houses and a Reformed church dating from the 15th century. The surrounding mountains offer excellent hiking in summer — the trail up to the Scesaplana, the highest peak in the Ratikon range, starts from the valley near here. In winter, Schiers is within easy reach of the ski areas at Klosters and Davos by train, without the resort prices.

The Prattigau valley is a Walser settlement area, and the local culture reflects this heritage. The Rhatische Bahn railway connects Schiers to Chur in about 40 minutes and to Klosters in 20, making it a practical base for exploring the region. The village is small enough that you can see everything in an hour, but the valley scenery and the mountain access are the real draw.
