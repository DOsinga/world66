Switzerland sits at the heart of Europe, bordered by [Germany](/europe/germany), [Austria](/europe/austria), [Liechtenstein](/europe/liechtenstein), [Italy](/europe/italy), and [France](/europe/france). Small in size but immensely varied, it compresses four distinct language cultures — German, French, Italian, and Romansh — into a country roughly the size of the Netherlands. The result is a place that feels different depending on which direction you travel.

The country is famous for its Alps, its neutrality, its banking sector, its cheese, its chocolate, and its trains — all of which are largely deserved reputations. It is also one of the most expensive countries in Europe, and there is no point pretending otherwise. Budget carefully or you will be surprised.

Switzerland is a federal republic of 26 cantons, each with considerable autonomy. [Bern](/europe/switzerland/bern) is the federal capital, though [Zurich](/europe/switzerland/zurich) is the largest city and the commercial and cultural hub. [Geneva](/europe/switzerland/geneva) is the seat of dozens of international organisations including the Red Cross and various UN agencies. [Lausanne](/europe/switzerland/lausanne), [Basel](/europe/switzerland/basel), [Lucerne](/europe/switzerland/lucerne), [Lugano](/europe/switzerland/lugano), and [Zermatt](/europe/switzerland/zermatt) are among the other destinations worth your time.

Traveling through the three main linguistic regions — the Deutschschweiz (German), Romandy (French), and Ticino (Italian) — is one of the quiet pleasures of Switzerland. The food, architecture, and atmosphere shift noticeably as you cross invisible cultural boundaries, even within a short train ride.
