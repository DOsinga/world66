Thun is a small city of around 40,000 at the northwest end of Lake Thun, just 20 minutes by train from [Bern](/europe/switzerland/bern). It is the gateway to the Bernese Oberland — Interlaken, Grindelwald, and the Jungfrau region are all easily reached from here — and has more character than its transit-hub status might suggest.

The castle, built in 1191, is the dominant feature of the old town. Today a museum, it sits on a hill above the river with views over the rooftops to the lake and the Alps beyond. The old town centre has barely changed in centuries: the Rathausplatz still has its massive 16th-century city hall, and the Hauptgasse is famous for its two-level layout, with shops at street level and a raised pedestrian walkway running along the first-floor arcades. The covered wooden bridges over the Aare and the flower-filled ramparts add to the medieval character.

Lake Thun offers boat trips, swimming, and kayaking in summer. Paddle-steamer cruises connect Thun with Spiez and Interlaken.
