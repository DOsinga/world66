---
title: "Top 5 Must-Dos"
type: section
---

If your time in Switzerland is limited, here are five experiences that repay the effort most reliably.

**1. Ride up to Jungfraujoch.** At 3,454 metres, it is the highest railway station in Europe. The cogwheel train from Kleine Scheidegg tunnels through the Eiger before emerging at the Sphinx Observatory above the Aletsch Glacier. Go on a clear morning — afternoon cloud is common. It is not cheap, but nothing else in Switzerland quite matches it. Base yourself in [Grindelwald](/europe/switzerland/grindelwald) or [Lauterbrunnen](/europe/switzerland/lauterbrunnen).

**2. Spend time in [Zermatt](/europe/switzerland/zermatt).** The Matterhorn is more striking in person than in photographs, which is saying something. The car-free village is well organised for visitors of all budgets. Even if you are not skiing, the cable car up to Klein Matterhorn (3,883m) gives a view that is hard to beat anywhere in the Alps.

**3. Walk the old town of [Bern](/europe/switzerland/bern).** Switzerland's capital is genuinely lovely — six kilometres of covered arcades, a medieval clock tower, a cathedral with an extraordinary tympanum, and the Aare curving around it all. It takes half a day to explore properly. The rose garden above the old town has one of the best views of the bear pits and river bend.

**4. Take the Glacier Express.** The eight-hour scenic railway from [Zermatt](/europe/switzerland/zermatt) to St. Moritz is one of the world's great train journeys. It crosses 291 bridges and 91 tunnels over the Oberalp Pass, passing through alpine villages, gorges, and viaducts. Book a window seat. The full route is long — some people do just a portion, from Andermatt to Chur, which covers the most dramatic scenery.

**5. Eat fondue where it belongs.** Fondue is a serious matter in Switzerland, not a tourist gimmick. In Fribourg, the traditional moitié-moitié (half Gruyère, half Vacherin Fribourgeois) is available at dozens of restaurants. In the Valais, raclette — melted and scraped onto potatoes — is the regional variant. Either way, the combination of wine, bread or potatoes, and melted cheese in cold weather is one of the simple pleasures of travelling in this country.
