Val Ferret is a quiet alpine valley in the Valais canton, tucked against the Italian border beneath the shadow of Mont Blanc's southern massif. It's the Swiss arm of a larger valley system shared with Italy and France — the same high terrain that serious hikers and mountaineers cross on the Tour du Mont Blanc. The valley runs from Orsières up to the hamlet of Ferret, with the Dranse de Ferret river threading through it.

In winter, it draws cross-country skiers to its groomed trails along the valley floor, with a gentler, quieter atmosphere than the big Alpine resorts nearby. In summer, it transforms into prime hiking territory — the Tour du Mont Blanc passes right through, and day walkers can tackle routes toward the Grand Col Ferret (2,537m), the high pass into Italy. The views across to the peaks of the Grandes Jorasses and Mont Dolent are exceptional.

It's not a place for nightlife or shopping — come for the mountains, the silence, and the kind of slow alpine rhythm that's hard to find in more developed resorts.
