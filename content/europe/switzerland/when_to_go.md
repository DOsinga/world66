---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Switzerland works in two distinct tourist seasons: summer (June–August) and winter (December–March). Both are peak periods for different reasons, and both come with peak prices and crowds. The shoulder seasons — May and September/October — offer the best balance of decent weather, open facilities, and fewer visitors.

**Summer (June–August)** is the most popular time for hiking, cycling, lake swimming, and exploring cities. Alpine trails are snow-free, mountain railways are fully operational, and daylight extends past 9pm. July and August are busy: book accommodation in advance, especially in resort towns and around the Jungfrau region. Expect afternoon thunderstorms in the mountains — standard summer weather in the Alps.

**Autumn (September–October)** is arguably the most rewarding time to visit. Temperatures are comfortable, the summer crowds have thinned, and the foliage at lower elevations turns a deep amber. High mountain trails start closing from mid-October onwards as snow returns.

**Winter (December–March)** means skiing. The resorts at [Zermatt](/europe/switzerland/zermatt), [Verbier](/europe/switzerland/verbier), [Davos](/europe/switzerland/davos), [Klosters](/europe/switzerland/klosters), and [St. Moritz](/europe/switzerland/stmoritz) are world-class and attract a well-heeled international crowd. Prices in ski areas are very high in January and February. Christmas markets in [Zurich](/europe/switzerland/zurich), [Bern](/europe/switzerland/bern), and [Basel](/europe/switzerland/basel) are charming from late November through December.

**Spring (March–May)** is the weakest season. Many mountain lifts close during the muddy period between ski season and summer hiking. At lower elevations — particularly in Ticino and around the lakes — spring arrives early and can be pleasant from April. Higher up, patience is required until late May.

In general: come in June, September, or early October if you want the Alps without the peak-season crush.
