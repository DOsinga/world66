---
title: "When to Go in Switzerland"
type: section
---

When visiting **Switzerland, when to go **is just as important a
topic as where to go. Typical tourist months are May through July, but
there are many reasons that a trip during any season might best suit
your interests.

**Spring**

Springtime in Switzerland is generally mild and pleasant. Snow is
still present atop the Alps, so skiing and snowboarding are still an
option, even if it's not the peak season for winter sports. However,
the temperature on ground level is around 40 to 55 degrees F on
average, so there is little to no snow and plenty of greenery. Rainfall
is at its lowest average of the year and tourism is fairly slow, so
you'll have fewer crowds with which to deal and agreeable weather for
the most part. Just be sure to pack a light jacket.

**Summer**

Summer is the most popular time for tourists to take a vacation to
Switzerland (as long as they don't plan on skiing), so expect
historical sites, lake activities, cities and other popular tourist
destinations to be crowded. Most, if not all, of the snow will melt
even atop the Alps as temperatures reach in the mid-60s degrees F, so don't count on being able to ski. Rainfall will be high,
particularly during August, so pack rain gear as well as a light
jacket. There aren't any really areas with consistently warm summers in
Switzerland, so don't make the mistake of packing only shorts and a
t-shirt.

**Autumn**

Autumn is another season in which tourism is a bit slow, so if you
dislike crowds, autumn might be the best time to visit. Temperatures
average about 40 to 60 degrees F and rainfall is at the highest
average of the year. Snow will start to fall on the Alps again, so
skiing is possible, particularly in the latter months of the season.
The amber colors of the foliage on ground make for an impressive
view and it's not yet cold enough to discourage you from enjoying
outdoor activities and tours through Switzerland's most popular cities.

**Winter**

Winter is the ideal time for tourists looking to ski, snowboard and
engage in other winter activities. If you don't want to spend your
vacation essentially at a ski resort, avoid winter. Average
temperatures throughout the country are around 30 degrees F,
so while it will be cold enough to preserve the heavy snowfall
throughout the country, it won't be unbearably frigid for the most
part. While typical tourist spots are virtually empty, many, such as the country's lakes and parks are closed or otherwise just not worth seeing when barren.
