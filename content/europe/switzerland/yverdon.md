Yverdon-les-Bains sits at the southern tip of Lake Neuchâtel, a small city that has been a spa town since Roman times. The thermal baths are still the main draw — the modern centre thermal is fed by sulphurous springs at 29°C — but Yverdon has more to offer than hot water.

The 13th-century Savoy castle in the town centre now houses a museum, and the old town around it is pleasant for a stroll. The real surprise is the Maison d'Ailleurs, a museum of science fiction and utopia that is unique in Europe, with a collection ranging from Jules Verne first editions to contemporary speculative art. The lake itself is calmer and less crowded than Léman, with good swimming in summer. Yverdon makes a relaxed base for exploring the Jura foothills and the northern Vaud countryside.
