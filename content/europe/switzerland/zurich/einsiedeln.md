An easy day trip from Zurich (about an hour by train), Einsiedeln is home to one of Switzerland's most spectacular Baroque abbeys — and one of the most important pilgrimage destinations in the entire Catholic world. Around 800,000 pilgrims and tourists make the journey each year to see the **Black Madonna**, a medieval statue of Mary housed in an ornate Chapel of Grace at the centre of the enormous abbey church.

The church itself is worth the trip even without the pilgrimage context: a vast, opulent interior of gilded stucco, painted vaults, and richly ornamented altars that feels almost impossible given the quiet mountain setting. Benedictine monks still live and work here, and you can hear Gregorian chant at daily Mass.

Just outside the abbey are two more unusual sights: the **Diorama Bethlehem**, a nativity scene with more than 500 carved wooden figures, and an extraordinary circular panoramic painting — over 90 metres long — depicting Jerusalem at the time of the Crucifixion. Neither sounds promising on paper; both are genuinely memorable.

The nearby Sihlsee reservoir makes for a pleasant walk if you want to extend the day.
