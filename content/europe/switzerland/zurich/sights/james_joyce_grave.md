Joyce died in Zurich on 13 January 1941 following surgery for a duodenal ulcer and is buried in the Fluntern Cemetery on the Zürichberg hill. His wife Nora, who died in 1951, is buried beside him. The grave is marked by a bronze sculpture of Joyce seated, book in hand — a meditative, dignified monument that draws a steady stream of literary pilgrims.

Joyce had fled Nazi-occupied France and spent his final months in Zurich, a city he knew well from his earlier years here during the First World War (1915–1919), when he wrote much of *Ulysses*. The cemetery is peaceful and easy to reach by tram; the grave is well signposted from the entrance. Nearby is the zoo, which adds an incongruous cheerfulness to the visit.
