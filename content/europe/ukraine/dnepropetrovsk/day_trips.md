Most of the **Dnepropetrovsk Day Trips** involve traveling to the nearby destinations to explore the cultural heritage of Ukraine. Dnepropetrovsk is among the most populous and industrialized cities of central Ukraine, known for its large steel-manufacturing units. However, the city is also important from a historic perspective, having played a major role in the evolution of Ukraine as an independent nation. Some recommended Dnepropetrovsk Day Trips are listed below:

**Day Trip to Poltava**

Poltava is located about 160 km from Dnepropetrovsk. Visitors need to seek a cab-ride or connecting local buses to get here. This day tour is essentially about exploring the history of Ukraine, which is preserved in the monuments of Dnepropetrovsk. Poltava is a truly historic town, with a history that is more than 1,100 years old--among the oldest of Ukrainian cities. The most popular sites include the Monument of Cossaks and the Kotlyarevsky Memorial House. Cossaks was built to commemorate the Russian soldiers who died during the Russian-Persian War. The Memorial House is like a museum of the famous Russian writer M. Kotlyarevsky. After exiting the museum, visitors can explore the Kotlyarevsky estate, which still has a distinct 19th-century aura.

**Day Trip to Petrykivka**

Petrykivka is a small town located about an hour&rsquo;s drive from Dnepropetrovsk. Located at a distance of 50 kilometers from Dnepropetrovsk, visitors can travel to Petrykivka via a cab ride. The main attraction here includes the historic Petrykivka settlements. This is the place wherein the immensely popular Petrykivka style of painting originated. The day trip includes visiting the numerous art studios and art galleries. Visitors can participate in small sessions that help to understand the Ukrainian form of painting.
