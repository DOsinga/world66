type: section

**Dnepropetrovsk family travel** is a little hard to find as the city was not open to the public until the 1980s. Much of the city has close ties to the military as there have been much military manufacturing and research done in the city.

**Check the Famous Sculpture and War Memorial**

Right along the Dnieper River is the famous sculpture the Youth of the Dnieper. This was created in 1982 of bronze and weighs 3.5 tons. The inspiration for the sculpture was from a holiday, Ivan Kupala. At this holiday girls wore water wreaths.

The war walk will take you to the famous War Memorial found at the end of Central Boulevard. This spot is where a large battle was fought to free the city. The Soviets defeated the defending garrison for freedom. The museum has plenty of exhibits and you will see footage of the battle

**Walk Along the Quay**

The quay is a popular place for visitors and residents. This stretches for 20 km on the right bank of the Dnieper River. There are plenty of shops and cafes lining the quay and most weddings will have a motorcade along the quay.

**Visit Monastic Island**

Monastic Island is another favorite of all. This is a crescent shaped island in the middle of the river. The island is legendary and was founded in the 9th century by Byzantine monks. This was a place of pilgrimage for Princess Olga and Prince Vladimir.

**Enjoy an Organ Concert**

For an interesting concert experience visit the Organ Hall. This hall is in the Bryanskaya Church which is quite stunning in itself. Tickets are quite inexpensive, $2 to $3 USD. If you have never heard an organ concert, then this is the place to start.

**Explore the Park**

The entrance of Taras Shevchenko Park is quite impressive and named after the famous Ukrainian poet. The park is worth a wander as it is the largest in the city. Inside are several cafes and walks and the entire park is an excellent example of landscape gardening from the 18th century. Inside the park is Potemkin Palace which is currently the Palace of Students for the National University.

**Spend Some Time Shopping**

If you want some shopping then you need to stop by Karl Marx Avenue, the central avenue of the city. You can spend hours going through the different stores that range from high fashion to souvenir shops. Another famous shopping place is Tsum, which carries most of the popular Western and European brands. If you want more traditional crafts of the area, then go to the exhibition just across from Tsum.
