The city of Dnepropetrovsk is well known for its dynamic commercial industry, as well as its majestic green hills and profound history. Here is some **Dnepropetrovsk practical information** that will help you if you are planning a trip to Dnepropetrovsk.

**Climate, Geography and Time Zone**

Dnepropetrovsk is located along the banks of Dnieper River, in the south-central region of Ukraine. Dnepropetrovsk has very warm temperatures during the summer, between 24° to 27° C. During winters, the city’s temperature can drop as low as -4° to -6° C. It is highly recommended to visit this city during the months of April and May, or during September and October for the early spring season. Dnepropetrovsk belongs to the Greenwich Mean Time zone (GMT+2 hours), which means the city’s time is 8 hours ahead of the Eastern Standard Time zone.

**Passport and Visa**

The government of Ukraine allows certain nationalities to enter without any visa requirements. The following nationalities are allowed to enter Dnepropetrovsk visa-free: the United States of America, Japan, Canada, Liechtenstein, Switzerland, Vatican, Iceland, Turkey, Monaco, Andorra, San Marino and countries in the European Union. Other nationalities are required to apply for visas before traveling. Visas that are usually issued by the Government of Ukraine last for three months, and must come with a travel voucher from a Ukrainian travel agent.

**Customs**

The customs laws in Ukraine are almost the same as most other countries. If you are to visit Dnepropetrovsk, you are not allowed to carry dangerous weapons or prohibited drugs. If you are traveling with your pet, you must acquire certification from your veterinarian. With regard to prescription medications, you will need to obtain certification from your doctor. If you are carrying valuable things, make sure you register these upon arrival. Heavier and more expensive goods may result in larger custom fees. However, there are duty-free goods that you can bring such as $10,000 in cash or checks, 20 packs of cigarettes, 2 liters of alcohol, 100g of jewelry, and 50kg of goods like foods.

**Money Matters**

The currency used in Dnepropetrovsk is called Ukrainian Hryvna (UAH), which has an exchange rate of 7.98 UAH to the dollar, or 10.9 UAH to the Euro. It is advisable to use the authorized money changers at the airport to change your money. You can also change your money at the banks.

**Local Transportation**

You can make use of the local transportation to get around the city. Trams, electric buses, buses and Marshrutkas are available all over the city. You can also make use of the Metro system to easily travel to other parts of the city.
