The **Dnepropetrovsk things to do** list is varied and long. The list should include Zhovtneva Square, the museum of history, and Monastyrsky Island which also helped to make Dnepropetrovsk the third most highly populated city in the Ukraine. The population has grown to over 1.1 million, despite the fact that Dnepropetrovsk was a closed city until the 1990s. Dnepropetrovsk is rich in history and culture, and if visiting Ukraine, is a city not to be missed.

**Explore Monastyrsky or Monastery Island**

Monastyrsky Island is deeply rooted in history. Not only was this island which has been the home of several different cultures, the site of the first human inhabitants is in the area, and it also steeped in religious history. The island is named after the Byzantine monks who built a monastery here that was later destroyed by the Mongol-Tatars. The Taras Shevshenko statue can be found here along with St. Nicholas Cathedral. A visit ot Dnepropetrovsk would not be complete without a visit to this hauntingly beautiful island.

**Visit the Museums**

A stroll through Dnepropetrovsk would not be complete without a visit to the Dnepropetrovsk Museum of History. The outside of this remarkable building features an impressive diorama featuring an image from WWII. While you are there feel free to visit the museum which is rich in facts about Dnepropetrovsk's long and sorted history. You will learn about the multitude of name changes the area has experienced, why it was a closed city until very recently, and all about the previous inhabitants to the area. If you are interested in the history of Dnepropetrovsk you may also want to visit the Rocket Museum.

The Rocket Museum was founded after Dnepropetrovsk received its freedom, only in the last decade. Before this time, the city was closed to foreign visitors because it was a city used by the government to build missiles and rockets. After the city was opened to visitors, the factory where these objects were once forged became a museum.

**Enjoy Zhotneva Square**

Zhotneva or October Square is one of the most beautiful areas in Dnepropetrovsk. This area includes the fascinating Preobrazhensky Cathedral built by command of Catherine the Great in 1787. This is one of only a handful of cathedrals from this time period that still remain, having not been destroyed by war.

Though much of this area was destroyed during the Stalin’s reign, Dnepropetrovsk is still an impressive tourist city and one of the best cities to visit while in Ukraine. Whether you come to see the impressive cathedrals, the monuments to the steel age or the famous Rocket Museum, Dnepropetrovsk has something for everyone. The best time to visit due to the cold climate, is in May.
