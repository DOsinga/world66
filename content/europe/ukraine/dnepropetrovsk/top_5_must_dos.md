---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Dnepropetrovsk"
type: section
---

Dnepropetrovsk is one of the largest cities in the Ukraine and was a large industrial force though it still has some great **Dnepropetrovsk must do’s**. The city was closed for many years because of its close ties to the military. In 1991 the city opened its doors to everyone and there are some spectacular sights to see.

**Visit Museum of Art**

The Museum of Art contains some of the best art exhibits in the entire country and the museum itself is one of the oldest in the Ukraine. It contains a wide range of Russian, European and Ukrainian art. Two-thirds of the works are graphics and drawings to balance out the many paintings. The icon exhibit has more than 250 items.

**Check the Rocket Museum**

The rocket museum is one of the main draws of the city as Dnepropetrovsk significantly contributed to the Soviet space program. You can see different rocket sections laid out in a car park.

**Explore the Local Crafts**

If you are visiting for a few days then you should not miss Petrykivka which is a nearby village that is known for its crafts people. The village itself has a well known and rich history and most of its residents are artisans. Originally this was a small Cossack settlement but the women of the town were the artisans and they were known for their paintings. The famous crafts you can see and purchase include belts, national costumes, needle works, woven carpets and decorative chests.

**Have Fun at the Circus**

The circus should not be missed and it is found in its own unique building along the banks of the Dnieper River. The building itself has transformed many architectural forms that will depend on where you are standing and your perception. During the day this is a tent but at night with the circus lights it sparkles. Many of the circus acts take advantage of the unique structure and lighting scheme.

**Visit Several Important Parks and the Palace**

Lenin Park is small but it contains an impressive entrance, the Lenin Monument. Inside there is a park with a government building. Another park that should not be missed is Shevenko Park, the largest in the city.

This park contains the Potemkin Palace which was the residence of Prince Potemkin. Eventually the palace was deserted and partially destroyed. After the revolution it was completely renovated and turned into a youth palace. Today it is part of the National University. This palace is one of the most magnificent architectural buildings of the city. Inside are a dancing hall, concert hall, cinema, lecture room and café.
