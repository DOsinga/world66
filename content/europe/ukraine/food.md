---
title: "Food"
type: section
---

Ukrainian food is hearty, seasonal, and deeply tied to the land. It shares roots with other Eastern European cuisines but has its own strong identity — one that Ukrainians have become more assertive about reclaiming since 2022.

**Borscht** is the national dish. It's a beet-based soup, but don't expect it to be thin or simple — a proper Ukrainian borscht is dense with vegetables (cabbage, potato, carrot, tomato), often with pork or beef, and served with a thick dollop of sour cream and a pampushka (soft garlic roll). Every region, and every family, has its own version. UNESCO added the culture of Ukrainian borscht cooking to its intangible heritage list in 2022.

**Varenyky** are Ukraine's answer to dumplings — half-moon pockets of dough filled with potato and fried onion, meat, sauerkraut, sour cherry, or sweet cheese. Served with sour cream and butter, they are a staple of Sunday lunches and festive tables throughout the country.

**Salo** — cured pork fatback — is the most proudly Ukrainian of foods, the subject of jokes, poems and genuine affection. Eaten thin-sliced on rye bread with a raw onion and a shot of horilka (Ukrainian vodka), it is both a snack and a cultural statement.

**Holubtsi** (stuffed cabbage rolls with meat and rice), **deruny** (potato pancakes served with sour cream), and **kulish** (a simple millet or buckwheat porridge with lard) are also everyday staples.

In western Ukraine, and particularly Lviv, the food scene has developed a strong cafe culture with its own character — coffee is taken seriously, and the city has dozens of atmospheric places to eat and drink in the old town. Lviv's culinary identity leans more Central European than Soviet.

**Horilka** is the local spirit. Ukrainian vodka is good and inexpensive. Local wines from the Zakarpattia region (a small wine-producing area near Hungary and Romania) are worth trying — nothing on par with France, but pleasant. Beer is widely consumed; Obolon, Chernigivske and Lvivske are the main national brands.
