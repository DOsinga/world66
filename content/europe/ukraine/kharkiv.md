Kharkiv is Ukraine's second city and cultural capital — a place Ukrainians call the country's "first capital" because it served as the Soviet Ukrainian capital from 1919 to 1934. The city has a distinct character from Kyiv: younger in feel, heavily industrialised during the Soviet era, and home to a large concentration of universities.

Freedom Square (Maidan Svobody) is one of the largest city squares in Europe, ringed by monumental Constructivist and Stalinist architecture. The Annunciation Cathedral with its golden domes provides an older counterpoint, and the enormous Barabashovo Market is a fascinating place to explore.

Note: Kharkiv has been severely affected by the conflict in Ukraine that began in 2022. Check current safety conditions before planning any travel.
