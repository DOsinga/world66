Kherson is a port city in southern Ukraine at the mouth of the Dnipro River, about 50 km from the Black Sea. Founded in 1778 by Prince Potemkin and built by Alexander Gannibal (the great-grandfather of Alexander Pushkin), it was intended as the cradle of Russia's Black Sea fleet. The city has a long history as a river port and shipbuilding centre.

The surrounding Kherson region contains the extraordinary **Askania-Nova** nature reserve, about 180 km from the city — one of the few remaining patches of virgin feather-grass steppe in Europe, with vast open grasslands, free-ranging exotic animals, and a botanical park. It is a genuinely unique natural area.

Note: Kherson was occupied by Russian forces in March 2022 and recaptured by Ukraine in November 2022. The city sustained significant damage during the conflict and the security situation remains difficult. Check current travel advisories before visiting.
