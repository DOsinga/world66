Kherson sits on the lower Dnipro River in southern Ukraine, founded in 1778 as a base for the Russian Black Sea fleet. The city was built under the direction of Ganibal, the grandfather of the poet Alexander Pushkin. Today it is a regional capital with river and sea ports, shipyards, and a hinterland of fertile farmland, vineyards, and watermelon plantations.

In summer the Dnipro beaches and the city parks offer welcome relief from the heat. The region is known for its sparkling wines — "Naddniprianskoe" won its first Grand Prix in Paris in 1900 — and visits to the vineyards and winemaking plants can be arranged.

The main excursion from Kherson is to Askania-Nova, a nature reserve about 180 km away. One of the oldest reserves in Ukraine, Askania-Nova protects the last large area of virgin feather-grass steppe in Europe — around 11,000 hectares of undisturbed grassland with over 600 plant species and rare animals brought from across the world. The reserve also has a magnificent arboretum of over 200 hectares.

Note: Kherson has been significantly affected by the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. Check current conditions before planning a visit.
