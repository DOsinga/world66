---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Ukrainian cooking centres on borshch (beet soup with pork or beans), varenyky (stuffed dumplings), holubtsi (stuffed cabbage rolls), and salo (cured pork fat, served in many ways). Kyiv has restaurants across every price range and cuisine. For budget eating, cafeteria-style Ukrainian restaurants — including Akademia on Kontraktova Ploscha in Podil — serve traditional food cheaply without needing any Ukrainian. The restaurant scene around Khreshchatyk and Podil covers everything from Georgian cuisine to Japanese to traditional Ukrainian taverns.
