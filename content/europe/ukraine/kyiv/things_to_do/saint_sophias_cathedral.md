Saint Sophia's Cathedral was built in the early 11th century by Yaroslav the Wise and is the oldest surviving building in Kyiv. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it contains the most complete collection of original 11th-century mosaics and frescoes anywhere in Eastern Europe. The golden mosaic of the Virgin Orans in the main apse is particularly striking. The complex includes the Metropolitan's residence, a museum, and the famous Sophia Bell Tower. The cathedral is now a museum rather than an active church.
