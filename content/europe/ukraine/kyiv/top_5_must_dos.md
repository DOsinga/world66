---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

Kyiv is greatly responsible for inducing a heavy European
civilization streak in the entire east European region and has a rich
settlement history dating back to the 5 th century. The Kyiv Must Do's** **Guide
offers visitors comprehensive information on attractions and activities
that have to be experienced to get the essence of this fascinating
cultural bouquet.

**1. ****Motherland Statue and War Memorials**

The Great Patriotic War museum is a great place to start and get
acquainted with the war history of the Soviet-era and German invasion
with a plethora of war hardware and military exhibits. The impressive
and imposing Motherland statue along with the memorial adjacent to it
is worth watching. There are other explicit photographs and memorabilia
including battle maps to offer an in-depth insight into the historical
accounts of the region’s tumultuous struggle.

**2. St. Sophia’s Cathedral**

The oldest church in the city, St. Sophia’s Cathedral, is a magnificent 11 th
century structure of brilliant aesthetic virtue. The Virgin Orans
mosaic is also housed on this site along with a statue paying homage to
Bogdon Hmelnistski, who was responsible for Kiev’s liberation in the
late 17 th century.

**3. ****Hidropark**

A day stroll around Hirdopark in summer is the best way to explore
the unconventional beauty of Kyiv. The walk commences from the Upper
Town monument, St. Michael’s Monastery. Head straight down to Poshtova
Ploscha while wandering around the compact and column-like streets of
the historically endowed Podil region. The once upon a time merchant
district has been revamped into a trendy restaurant zone and is buzzing
with locals and tourists on weekends. Finish your tour at Andreiivsky
Uzviz.

**4. Pečerska Lavra**

The 11 th century underground cave monastery and church
are worth discovering for its innately exotic appeal and ingenious
origin. The caves were apparently created by priests who aspired to
live a sequestered life amidst religious solitude. The mummified monks
are still visited and revered by faithful visitors.

**5 Museum of Folk Architecture and Rural**

This is a nice visit to get familiar with Ukraine’s rustic charm and
ethnic folk culture that has remained well-preserved and unblemished
throughout the years. The museum houses restored villages and other
replicas and exhibits related to the country’s ancient and rudimentary
lifestyle. Great for a family outing to catch Ukraine’s local flavor.

The off-beat Kyiv is a chronicle of struggle, captivation,
liberation and a relentless spirit that is vividly visible in the
historical monuments and streets of the city, which still bleed in
reminiscence of a turbulent era.
