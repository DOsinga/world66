**What You Need to Know about the City of ****Lugansk**

This **Lugansk practical information **guide will give you valuable insight and will help you feel more confident during your stay. Here are some things to consider and prepare while planning a visit to Lugansk.

**Population**

Lugansk is located in southeastern Ukraine and is an administrative center of its own province, called Lugansk Oblast. The city has a population of almost 450 000 residents, 42 percent of whom are of Russian origin, while the rest 58 percent are of native Ukrainian origin. However, both ethnic groups are very similar to each other as they both descend from the East Slavic tribes, so you can hardly tell the ethnic belongings of the various residents.

**Language**

Although the official language is Ukrainian, over 70% of the people living in Lugansk speak Russian as well. This is mainly due to the ethnic consistency of the locals and due to Ukraine being a former Soviet country. Ukrainian differs from Russian and non-native Russian speakers will surely have problems understanding it. If you&rsquo;re not familiar to Russian, you will most probably encounter language barriers, as English is very poorly spoken among the local residents.

**Currency**

The local currency is called Ukrainian Grivna. For a Dollar, you can get almost 8 Grivnas. The most common way of payment is in cash, so make sure to exchange some of your local currency into Grivnas. Keep in mind that Dollars and Euros are frequently used for bigger payments, especially in the so-called &ldquo;shadow economy.&rdquo; The most common places to exchange money are the exchange booths, as they give much better rates compared to the local banks. Exchange booths are all over Lugansk and deal mainly with Euro and Dollars.

**Visas**

A big plus for visitors coming from the United States, Canada, the European Union, Switzerland, Liechtenstein and Japan, is that they can enter the country without a visa and stay in Lugansk for up to 90 days. Travelers coming from other parts of the world need to apply for a visa at the Ukrainian Consulates. This doesn&rsquo;t require much, only a photo, a copy of your passport, an application form and a visa support document (for example an invitation letter or a hotel confirmation).

**Religion**

Although the local people aren&rsquo;t very religious, most of them say that they are Orthodox Christians. Religious travelers can visit one of the several Orthodox churches in the city, most impressive of which is the St. Volodymyr Cathedral.
