---
title: "Shopping in Lugansk"
type: section
---

**Lugansk shopping** can be a unique and satisfying experience if you know which places (and even time of day) to look. There are dozens of shops that specialize in goods made locally, as well as shops that carry goods from all around the world. There are even occasions where you can find quality items from vendors found along the streets (although there are times when these "quality goods are scams).

#### Flower Shopping

Lugansk is famous for its flower shopping, since its possible to buy flowers at night. A new flower shop called &ldquo;Flower World,&rdquo; located in Oboronnaya Street, sells flowers from Ecuador as well as the from the Lugansk region. Other flower shops have similar promotions where they will be open 24/7 and will even offer special discounts at certain times. Flowers here are abundant, exotic, and only cost between 20 and 50 grivnas (three to six dollars) a flower.

#### East End Shopping

The East End Shopping of Lugansk provides all of the essential items you may need for your stay, such as toiletries and food staples. The Leleka supermarket acts as a one-stop shop for any basic supplies you may have forgotten at home. You can find a Leleka near the Defense Street bus terminal or by Budyonny Street. There is also a place called Eastern Market, which is an open-air market that sells goods brought in from local farmers. You can find tons of fresh fruits and vegetables, offered to you every Saturday.

#### Local Distilleries

If you enjoy authentic vodka and spirits, then you should seek out one of Lugansk&rsquo;s many local distilleries. Stores like Luga-nova sell only the purest vodka available, made with nothing but natural ingredients. Even though many of stores have a limited selection, they also offer the best of whatever it is they&rsquo;re trying to sell. The &ldquo;White Queen&rdquo; and &ldquo;European&rdquo; bottles of vodka are two examples of local vodka that you may find here. At only three to ten dollars per bottle, it is certainly worth it to seek out these places.

#### Artists&rsquo; Street

The Artists&rsquo; Street is the name given to the street behind the &ldquo;Heroes Obelisk&rdquo; where local artists sell their crafts to anyone passing by. You can find paintings, sculptures, clay pottery or even brass work here. Most things found here are purely for decorative purposes, but sometimes you may find more functional items, like a handmade dinette set. Usually things here cost no more than 50 grivnas (about six dollars), and are great souvenirs to take home with you.
