title: "Things to Do in Lugansk"
type: section
---

The city of Lugansk is not only one of the largest in the Ukraine, it is also rich with green living and historical ambiance. Dating back to 1795, a state-owned iron foundry to supply the Russian Black Sea fleet was established. In the 1890's Lugansk developed further with Donet's Coal Basin. With many different attractions to enjoy, it is said travelers typically plan to spend a month to take in as much as they can. Here are some **Lugansk things to do**.

**Sightseeing**

Throughout history Lugansk has been generously adorned with timeless monuments of its Soviet legacy while other more recent monuments have been devoted to the city and its outstanding local people--one of the most impressive and popular being of Clement Voroshilov (1881 to 1969), a five-star general who had been closely associated with Joseph Stalin. The Voroshilov monument stands in Voroshilov Square opposite of the City Council. Voroshilov was known as "the first Soviet soldier" greeting the army at the parade. Taking a journey through the many attractions such as the Lugansk City Local History Museum, World war II Memorial, Holodomor (famine) Memorial, Chernobyl Disaster Memorial, and the city's ornamental churches are bound to fulfill an appetite for Ukrainian culture.

**Activities**

Lugansk is situated 900 km to the east of, Kiev, the capital of Ukraine and lies along the Lugan River at its confluence with the Olkhovaya River. Lugnask offers a lovely array of entertainment and cultural options. There is the option of taking in a live performance at the Russian Music and Drama Theater or the Ukrainian Drama Theater. For a full flavored taste of the culture, the Railway Palace of Culture or the Palace of Culture would be a delightful experience.

**Food**

The cuisine in Lugansk is almost identical to that of Russia. For many generations most of the population has been ethnically Russian, so the menus seen in the many charming cafes and restaurants will mirror the same variations of traditional foods in Russia. The city is known to be quite charming, with many pleasant dining options along with an abundant nightlife.
