**Mykolaiv** (still widely known by its Russian name **Nikolaev**) is a city of around 480,000 people in southern Ukraine, on the **Southern Bug** river about 65 km inland from the Black Sea coast. Founded in 1789 as a Russian naval shipyard by Prince Grigory Potemkin on the orders of Catherine the Great, it grew throughout the 19th and 20th centuries as one of the most important shipbuilding cities of the Russian and then Soviet empires — the Ukrainian aircraft carriers of the Soviet navy (and most of the modern Chinese ones) were built here. The city is named after **Saint Nicholas**, the patron saint of sailors.

Most of Mykolaiv's civilian charm comes from its generous tree-lined boulevards, 19th-century neoclassical architecture and the large **Kashperovsky Park** on the river. The **Mykolaiv Regional Museum** has good exhibits on the region's Cossack and naval history, and the city's famous **Astronomical Observatory**, founded in 1821, is one of the oldest in the former Russian Empire and still a working research institution. The **Zoo** is surprisingly excellent — considered one of the best in the former Soviet Union and a serious research institution.

**Since the Russian invasion of Ukraine in February 2022**, Mykolaiv has been on the front line of the war. It was heavily shelled for months in 2022 and suffered severe damage to its water supply, civilian infrastructure, and residential districts; the nearby port of Kherson was occupied and then liberated. Travel to the region is currently not recommended, and the situation can change rapidly. Check current conditions and Ukrainian government guidance before planning any visit.
