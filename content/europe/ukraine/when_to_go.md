title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Under normal circumstances, late spring and early autumn are the best times to visit Ukraine. In 2026, the overriding consideration is the security situation — seasons matter less than the current state of the war and your own risk tolerance.

## Climate

Ukraine has a continental climate: cold winters, warm to hot summers, and moderate rainfall spread across the year. Winters can be harsh, particularly in the north and east, with temperatures regularly falling well below zero. Summers are warm to hot, with July and August averaging around 25–30°C in the centre and south. The Carpathians are cooler year-round.

**Spring (April–May)** is arguably the best time. Temperatures are pleasant (15–22°C), trees are in blossom, and the countryside is lush. Lviv and Kyiv are at their most photogenic. Crowds are light.

**Summer (June–August)** is peak season. Days are long, festivals run throughout the summer, and the Carpathians are in full hike mode. Cities can be very hot. Prices and crowds are at their highest — though both are far below pre-war levels.

**Autumn (September–October)** brings softer light, lower prices, and comfortable temperatures. The Carpathians are spectacular with autumn colour.

**Winter (November–March)** is cold and often grey. The Carpathian ski resorts around Bukovel open in December, making winter a viable time to visit the west if skiing is the goal. Christmas markets in Lviv are a genuine draw. Expect deep cold and short days everywhere else.

## Current situation

The security situation affects any time of year equally. Missile and drone attacks have occurred in every season. There is no reliably "safer" time of year from a conflict perspective. If you are considering travel to western Ukraine, check current government advisories immediately before you go — the situation can change rapidly.
