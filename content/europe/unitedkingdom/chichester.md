Chichester is a compact cathedral city in West Sussex, still largely contained within the Roman walls that have defined its boundaries for nearly two thousand years. The street plan follows the Roman grid: four main streets radiate from the medieval Market Cross at the centre, and you can walk the circuit of the walls in under an hour.

The cathedral is the centrepiece — Norman at its core, with a detached bell tower that is unique in England, and works by Marc Chagall and John Piper inside. Beyond the cathedral close, the Pallant House Gallery holds one of the best collections of modern British art outside London. The city is small enough that everything is walkable, and the surrounding countryside — the South Downs to the north, Chichester Harbour to the south — is within easy reach.

Chichester is also the gateway to the Goodwood Estate, which hosts the Festival of Speed and the Revival motor racing meeting each summer, drawing crowds from across the country. The Chichester Festival Theatre, founded by Laurence Olivier in 1962, runs a strong programme year-round.
