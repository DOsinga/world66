Crovie (pronounced "Crivie") is a single row of cottages wedged between cliff and sea on the Moray Firth coast of Aberdeenshire. Once a busy fishing village, it is now almost entirely holiday homes — but the setting is extraordinary and the peace absolute.

There is no road into the village itself; you park at one end and walk. The cottages face directly onto the shore, close enough to feel the spray in a storm. In summer the bay produces spectacular sunsets; in winter the Northern Lights are sometimes visible. Dolphins are regularly seen offshore, and you may spot minke whales from a boat.

Just east of the village, **Troup Head** is an RSPB reserve with one of mainland Britain's few gannet colonies, along with puffins, guillemots, razorbills, and cormorants. The nearby village of Gardenstown, connected to Crovie by a cliff path, has a small harbour and a pub.
