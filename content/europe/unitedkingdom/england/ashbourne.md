Ashbourne is a handsome market town in Derbyshire, often called the "Gateway to the Peak District" — and for once, the cliché is accurate. Dovedale and the southern limestone dales begin just a mile or two from the town centre, making it a practical and pleasant base for walkers exploring some of the finest scenery in the English Midlands.

The town itself is worth a few hours. The long main street is lined with Georgian and earlier buildings, and St Oswald's Church — described by George Eliot as "the finest mere parish church in England" — anchors the southern end with a graceful 14th-century spire. The town's famous Royal Shrovetide Football game, played on Shrove Tuesday and Ash Wednesday, sends hundreds of players through the streets in a tradition said to date back to medieval times.

Ashbourne is also the southern starting point of the Tissington Trail, a traffic-free cycling and walking route that follows a disused railway line northward through the White Peak. Gingerbread and Ashbourne gingerbread in particular has been made here for centuries and is still sold in local shops — worth picking up before a walk.
