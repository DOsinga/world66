---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Birmingham"
type: section
---

It may claim to be the United Kingdom's second city, but critics argue that **Birmingham Nightlife **is not as diverse or as cosmopolitan as the capital or of Northern cities such as Manchester, Leeds and Sheffield. However, there is a lively drinking scene, and the city is famous for its excellent classical music and theater facilities. Revelers looking for a fun, unpretentious and alcohol-fueled night out will have little difficulty finding nightspots to suit, and the city's multi-ethnic population means that a good variety of musical styles and sounds can be found here.

**Bars and Clubs in Birmingham**

Birmingham's reputation as something of a hotspot for stag and hen parties (bachelor and bachelorette parties), is largely built around the vast strip of chain pubs, bars and clubs that line Broad Street. This street pulses with music on the weekends, and revelers spill from one bar to another. This is a good bet for drinkers on a budget, who can pick up bargain beers, wines and cocktails at inexpensive bars such as Wetherspoon's. More sophisticated options can be found at the Mail Box, where a dressed-up crowd flocks to sip cocktails and imported beers. Birmingham also has plenty to offer all-night revelers, with 'super clubs' such as Gatecrasher Birmingham, offering multi-million pound sound systems and cavernous interiors. Oceana, on Hurst Street, meanwhile, is an experience like no other, with rooms themed around Aspen Ski Lodges and a New York Disco. Glamor is the overriding theme, so dress up for a night out here.

**Live Music in Birmingham**

Birmingham is home to two of the largest universities in the country, and the student population has had a hand in creating a lively music scene. Local bands can be heard showcasing their sounds at cozy independent venues such as the Sunflower Lounge, close to New Street train station. The area of the city known as the Jewelry Quarter is also awash with live music venues, and the outdoor seating by the city's canal is popular on summer evenings. Birmingham's classical music scene is famous across the country, and no classical music lover should miss the opportunity to catch the Birmingham National Symphony Orchestra when it's in town.

**Comedy in Birmingham**

Birmingham has a good live comedy scene, with big names heading to the city's theaters and major comedy venues such as the vast Highlights, on Broad Street, which also boasts several nightclubs within its confines. There's also a thriving amateur comedy scene, with talented local acts to be seen at many of the city's bars and pubs.
