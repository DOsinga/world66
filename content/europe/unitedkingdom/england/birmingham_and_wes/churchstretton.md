Church Stretton sits in a narrow valley between the Long Mynd and the Stretton Hills in south Shropshire, surrounded by some of the best walking country in the English Midlands. The Victorians called it "Little Switzerland" and came for the clean air; today it draws hikers, hang-gliders, and anyone who wants a quiet base in genuinely wild-feeling landscape that is surprisingly close to Birmingham.

The Long Mynd itself is the main draw — a broad moorland plateau rising to nearly 500 metres, cut by steep valleys called batches. The walk up Carding Mill Valley is the most popular, but Ashes Hollow and the Pole Bank ridge are quieter and more rewarding. East of town, Caer Caradoc is a sharp volcanic hill with an Iron Age fort on top and views across half of Shropshire.

The town itself is small and unhurried. A handful of independent shops line the High Street, there are good tea rooms, and the medieval St Laurence's Church has a rare sheela na gig carved above its north door. The rail station is on the Shrewsbury–Hereford line, making Church Stretton an easy day trip or weekend from either city.
