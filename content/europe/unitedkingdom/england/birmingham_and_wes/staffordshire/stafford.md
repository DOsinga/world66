Stafford is the county town of Staffordshire, sitting on the River Sow in the middle of England. It's a quiet market town that gets overlooked in favour of bigger neighbours, but it has a genuine medieval core — the ancient High House is one of the largest timber-framed town houses in England — and Stafford Castle, a Norman motte-and-bailey rebuilt in the 19th century, stands on a hill above town with decent views across the surrounding countryside.

The town makes a reasonable base for visiting the wider county: Shugborough Estate (the ancestral home of the Lichfield family, now run by the National Trust) is nearby, and the market towns of Lichfield and Cannock Chase are within easy reach. Stafford itself is a place for an afternoon rather than a weekend — the town centre is modest — but the castle, the Ancient High House, and the Shire Hall Gallery keep it off the empty-stub list.
