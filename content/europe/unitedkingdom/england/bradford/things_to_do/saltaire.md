Saltaire is a Victorian model village built by the mill owner Sir Titus Salt in the 1850s, when he moved his textile operation out of Bradford and housed his workers in purpose-built terraces nearby. The whole village — streets, church, almshouses, institute — was designed as a social experiment in benevolent capitalism. It worked well enough that it's now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and remarkably intact.

Salt's Mill at its centre has been repurposed as a gallery and shopping complex. Two floors are given over to one of the world's largest permanent collections of David Hockney's work — he grew up in Bradford and the connection runs deep. The rest of the mill houses shops, a diner, and a bookshop. Walk the streets around it and you'll find that some of the old communal buildings now house Shipley College. There's a railway station and a stop on the canal boat service if you want to arrive by water.
