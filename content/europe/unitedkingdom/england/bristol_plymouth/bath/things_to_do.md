title: 'Things to Do in Bath'
type: section

**Walk along the Kennet and Avon Canal**

A pleasant circular walk of about 40 minutes takes you from the centre of town along the canal. Head out on North Parade for a view of Pulteney Bridge and the weir, then walk east past the cricket ground. Cross under the road at the bridge and take the steps up to the canal towpath. Turn right to see a lock, then double back and follow the canal. You will pass allotments and reach the village of Bathampton, where you can pick up an ice cream before crossing to the other side and walking back through a short tunnel to Pulteney Gardens and Great Pulteney Street.

**Cycle the canal path to Bradford-on-Avon**

The canal path from the centre of Bath westwards to Bradford-on-Avon is an absolute delight. It is level, shared with walkers, and passes through varied scenery with views of the Georgian city giving way to open countryside. Hire a bike for the day from Avon Valley Cycles behind the main railway station. It is also possible to hire in Bradford-on-Avon and cycle back.
