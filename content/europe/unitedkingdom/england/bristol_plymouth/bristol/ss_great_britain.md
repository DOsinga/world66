Brunel's iron-hulled, screw-propeller-driven ocean liner of 1843 — the first of its kind — sits in the Great Western Dockyard where she was built. The ship has been dry-docked under a glass sea that lets you walk beneath her hull. One of the best maritime attractions in Britain and a must-see if you want to understand Bristol.
