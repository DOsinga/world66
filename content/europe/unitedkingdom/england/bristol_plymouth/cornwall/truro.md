Truro is the only city in Cornwall and its administrative, commercial, and cultural centre. It sits at the head of the Truro River, a tidal estuary that eventually opens into the Carrick Roads, one of the world's largest natural harbours.

The city's most striking feature is its cathedral — the Cathedral Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary — built between 1880 and 1910 in a neo-Gothic style that incorporates the south aisle of the earlier parish church of St Mary. Three spires dominate the city centre, visible from the surrounding hills. The city centre is compact and walkable, with Georgian townhouses and Victorian streets lined with independent shops and cafes.

Truro makes a practical base for exploring central and south Cornwall, and is on the main railway line from London Paddington.
