Devon is situated in southern England and is a beautiful county, famous for its varied coastline and countryside.

Inland Devon includes the wild, unpredictable yet enchanting Dartmoor and part of Exmoor, shared with Somerset. These moorlands have a unique environment with spectacular granite tors and a wealth of Bronze Age remains. Many wooded river valleys lie between the moors and the sea, with appealing little villages nestled throughout the county.

The coastal resorts include Torquay, known as the English Riviera, in the south, and Ilfracombe and Lynton in the north where the coastline is relatively untouched by development and very scenic.

Exeter Cathedral is a famous landmark in the region and Exeter's old Customs House is also worth a visit. [Plymouth](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/plymouth), a bustling port, boasts a stunning aquarium, the Plymouth Hoe and good entertainment at the Pavilions.

The National Trust properties at Saltram and Killerton are well worth visiting, as is Buckland Abbey on the fringe of Dartmoor, which was owned at different times by Sir Francis Drake and Sir Richard Grenville.
