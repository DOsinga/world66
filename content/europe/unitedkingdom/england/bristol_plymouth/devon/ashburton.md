Ashburton is a small town on the southeastern edge of Dartmoor in Devon, one of the four historic stannary towns where tin was weighed and taxed in the Middle Ages. It sits in a valley where the open moor gives way to green farmland, and it has more character than its size suggests.

The main street is handsome — slate-hung and colour-washed buildings, a few good antique shops, and a handful of independent cafes and restaurants. The town has become something of a foodie destination for its size. The Golden Lion and other pubs serve decent food, and there is a good deli scene.

Ashburton is a practical base for exploring southeastern Dartmoor. Holne Moor, Buckland Beacon, and the Dart Valley are all close. The River Dart runs nearby, and wild swimming spots along it are popular in summer. Buckfast Abbey, a working Benedictine monastery about two miles north, is worth a visit for the building and gardens.
