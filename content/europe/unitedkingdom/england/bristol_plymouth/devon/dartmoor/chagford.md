Chagford is a small moorland town on the eastern edge of [Dartmoor](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/dartmoor), historically one of the four Stannary towns that governed the tin-mining industry — these towns had their own laws and courts, independent of the Crown.

The surrounding landscape is wild and spectacular, with some of the best Bronze Age remains on Dartmoor, including stone rows, hut circles, and the Scorhill stone circle a short walk from town. Chagford itself has a handsome market square, a medieval church, and a handful of good pubs and tearooms that make it a natural base for walking on the moor.
