Whereas Dartmoor derives its name from the river Dart, one of the most scenic of its rivers, this is by no means the only one which provides some scenic splendour. Starting at hte southwest edge of the moor the Tavy is a tributary of the Tamar which forms the county boundary with [Cornwall](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/cornwall) and itself has the Walkham as a tributary. Double Waters, where the Walkham flows into the Tavy is a particularly beautiful spot. Then there is the Plym, joined by the Meavy at Shaugh Bridge, once the site of a railway halt. Next in succession come the Yealm, the Erme, the Avon, which brings us to the Dart, itself, followed by the Bovey and the Teign. Lastly, on the north side of the moor, flowing towards Devon’s north coast are the Okement and the Taw.

Many of these rivers appear in the following canoeing website and, by selecting them, you can get some good pictures. http://www.ukriversguidebook.co.uk/swengland.htm
