**Cremyll and Cawsand**

Choose a fine day for this and do it in either direction. Take a boat from Plymouth to Cawsand. This is a very scenic trip passing Drake's Island and then lollowing the SE Cornish coast past Mount Edgecombe Park to arrive at the twin villages of Cawsand and Kingsand. The beaches are not large; for that you need to get to Whitesands further round the coast. However they are interesting, one having a lime kiln and they allow for scrambling over rocks at low tide.

From Cawsand take a bus to the village of Cremyll from where you can take the Cremyll ferry [passengers only] across the tamar to Stonehouse Quay in Plymouth. You could get a bus all the way from cawsand to Plymouth via the Torpoint Ferry but the Cremyll ferry is far more scenic.

Just about every Plymouth resident has done this, as have their personal visitors but a lot of other visitors to Plymouth miss it. ********

### [Buckland Abbey](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/plymouth/day_trips/buckland_abbey)

This is equally easy to visit from
[Tavistock](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/tavistock). It is situated in a
[Dartmoor](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/dartmoor) setting near Yelverton. 

Built as a Cistercian monastery in the 13th century it ceased to be occupied by monks during the 16th century dissolution. It then became a house and at was owned at different times by Sir Richard Grenville and Sir Francis Drake. 

The building has seen many additions and changes since Tudor times and is now a museum.
