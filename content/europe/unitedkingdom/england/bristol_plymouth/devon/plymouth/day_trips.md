Plymouth sits at the edge of Dartmoor, a short ferry ride from Cornwall, and within easy reach of the Tamar valley. Most day trips require no car — the ferries to Cawsand and Cremyll are a Plymouth institution.

**Cremyll and Cawsand**

Take a boat from Plymouth to Cawsand, a scenic crossing past Drake's Island and along the south Cornish coast through Mount Edgcumbe Country Park. The twin villages of Cawsand and Kingsand have small beaches good for rock-scrambling at low tide. From Cawsand, take a bus to Cremyll and return to Plymouth on the Cremyll ferry (passengers only) across the Tamar to Stonehouse Quay — a much more scenic return than the Torpoint car ferry. Nearly every Plymouth local has done this trip; many visitors miss it.

### [Buckland Abbey](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/plymouth/day_trips/buckland_abbey)

A 13th-century Cistercian monastery near Yelverton, in the southern fringe of Dartmoor. After the dissolution of the monasteries it became a private house and was owned successively by Sir Richard Grenville and Sir Francis Drake. Now a National Trust museum with Drake's drum and the story of the house's long transformation. Also easy to reach from [Tavistock](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/tavistock).
