---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Tavistock's main attractions are the Dartmoor walks radiating from the town and a handful of historical sites in the surrounding valleys. The landscape here is dramatic — deep river gorges, granite tors, and old mining remains half-hidden by woodland.

The rivers Tavy and Walkham meet at Double Waters, a beautiful spot reached via Grenofen Bridge on a walk from the [Plymouth](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/plymouth) road. Upstream, the villages of Marytavy and Petertavy are fine starting points for moor walks, and Ward Bridge on the Walkham is worth a detour.

[Morwellham Quay](https://www.morwellham-quay.co.uk/) was a major industrial port at the highest navigable point of the Tamar, thriving in the 19th century on copper exports. It has been restored as an open-air museum and is genuinely enjoyable for both adults and children — you can ride a tramway into an old mine, explore the quay buildings, and get a real sense of what industrial Devon looked like.
