Dunster is a picture-book village on the edge of Exmoor in Somerset, dominated by the towers of Dunster Castle rising above the rooftops. The castle, now run by the National Trust, has been occupied since at least Norman times and sits in terraced gardens with views across to the Bristol Channel.

The village's most distinctive feature is the Yarn Market, an octagonal medieval market building on the high street dating from around 1609. It is one of only two surviving yarn markets in England. The high street itself is a handsome stretch of medieval and Georgian buildings, quiet enough that you can hear the water running in the mill leat that still feeds the working Dunster Water Mill nearby.

Dunster makes a good base for walking on Exmoor. The coast at Minehead is two miles north, and the moor stretches south and west from the village edge.
