Dunster is a postcard-pretty village tucked between the edge of Exmoor and the Bristol Channel, a couple of miles inland from Minehead. Its wide main street, lined with cream-washed cottages and tea rooms, runs up to the octagonal Yarn Market of 1609, a rare survival from the days when the village was a centre of the West Country wool trade. The market still stands in the middle of the street, open to the sky on all sides.

The red sandstone bulk of Dunster Castle looms on its wooded hill above the village. The Luttrell family held it for six hundred years; today it belongs to the National Trust, and its Jacobean interiors, terraced gardens, and medieval working watermill are all open to the public. Elsewhere in the village, St George's Church has a magnificent 15th-century rood screen, and a circular packhorse bridge and a restored tithe barn sit nearby.

Dunster makes a natural base for walks onto Exmoor proper — the South West Coast Path is a short way down the hill, and the West Somerset Railway (a preserved steam line) calls at the village station on its run between Minehead and Bishops Lydeard.
