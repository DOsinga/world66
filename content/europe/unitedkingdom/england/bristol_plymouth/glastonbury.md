Glastonbury is a small Somerset town with an outsized mythological reputation. It draws pilgrims, pagans, and curious visitors in roughly equal measure, all drawn by the same cluster of legends: King Arthur's burial, the Holy Grail, and the mystical isle of Avalon.

The centrepiece is **Glastonbury Abbey**, one of England's oldest religious sites, now a peaceful ruin set in extensive grounds. According to legend, the bodies of Arthur and Guinevere were discovered here in 1191 — a claim the monks were conveniently able to make when they needed funds after a fire. Whatever its historical basis, the atmosphere is undeniably striking.

Above the town rises **Glastonbury Tor**, the iconic tower-topped hill visible for miles across the Somerset Levels. The climb takes about 20 minutes and the view from the top is worth every step. The **Chalice Well** at the tor's foot is said to be where Joseph of Arimathea hid the Holy Grail; its waters are rich in iron and run a distinctive rust-red.

The high street is heavy on crystals, tarot cards, and New Age shops — which is either part of the charm or a reason to walk quickly, depending on your temperament. The town is also famous as the home of the **Glastonbury Festival**, held a few miles outside town at Worthy Farm.
