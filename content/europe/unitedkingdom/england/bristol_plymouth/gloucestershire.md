Located in the west of England, straddling the river Severn, Gloucestershire is a popular destination - albeit a bit touristy. But if you put on your hiking boots and are willing to go a bit further than the average tourist, there are some treasures to be discovered.

 The best of "rustic rosy-cheeked England" is to be found in the pretty, gilded stone villages in the [Cotswolds](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/gloucestershire/cotswolds). The Severn Vale with the towns of [Gloucester](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/gloucestershire/gloucester), the capital, [Cheltenham](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/gloucestershire/cheltenham), Britain's best preserved Regency town and [Tewkesbury](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/gloucestershire/tewkesbury) is the most inhabited part of the county. The beautiful [Forest of Dean](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/gloucestershire/forestofdean) is in the west of and on the border with [Wales](/europe/unitedkingdom/wales) you find the enchanting [Wye Valley](/europe/unitedkingdom/wales/wye_valley).
