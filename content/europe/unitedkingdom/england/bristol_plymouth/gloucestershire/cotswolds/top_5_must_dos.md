---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Cotswolds"
type: section
---

**1. The Roman Baths**

In Avon Valley, the Romans found a locally celebrated marsh after they invaded Britain in AD 43. They decided to drain the marsh, contain the water, and build a temple to the goddess Sulis Minerva around it. This was the first thermal spa to be made in Britain. It is supported by natural springs spouting 500 gallons of water per day at 120 degrees Farenheit. Pilgrims often went to the temple to pray to the goddess for healing before they went to bath in the mineral waters. Adult addmission to this famous site is 11 Pounds, while a child's ticket costs 7.20 Pounds.

**2. The Warwick Castle**

The long-time residence of the Earls of Warwick, this castle has been around since the times of William the Conquerer. It is perhaps one of the best preserved castles in Britain. Displays of jousting, swordsmanship, birds of prey, and weapon making fill the castle. Entertaining reenactments of historical events such as the Countess of Warwick's 1898 weekend party and Richard Neville's preparation for the Battle of Barnet are done here, too. You can also visit the Princess Tower and the Great Hall. If you pay a little more, you can even see the dungeon. Adult tickets are 17.95 Pounds for the day, but you can get a 10% discount if you buy them online.

**3. The Batsford Arboretum**

This parkland area is spread out over 55 acres and contains more than 1500 species of trees. There are tons of flower displays and plant collections featuring magnolias, local wildflowers, and bulbs, just to name a few. It has the largest collections of trees and shrubs in Britiain and is located just outside the town of Batsford. Adults pay 6 Pounds, while children pay 2.

**4. Shakespeare's Home**

You can visit the birthplace and exhibition of the famous writer William Shakespeare at Henley Street, Stratford-Upon-Avon. Next door is the so-called New Place where Shakespeare spent his last years. These houses are located near the cottages of his wife, mother, daughter, and grand-daughter, which you can visit as well. The famous writer's grave is in the Holy Trinity Church in Stratford. If you're a fan of literature or history, this is a definite must-do.

**5. Birdland Park and Gardens**

If you have kids, they'll just love this 7 acre forest run through with gardens and a river. There are over 500 bird species here including penguins, cranes, flamingos, and pelicans. There are tropical, temperate, and desert aviaries, of which there are 50 in total. For a break, there are nice picnic areas, a play area, and a cafe.
