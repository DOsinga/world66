Salisbury is a compact cathedral city in Wiltshire, dominated by one of the finest medieval cathedrals in England. The spire — at 123 metres the tallest in the country — is visible for miles across the surrounding chalk downland. Inside, the cathedral houses one of the four surviving copies of the Magna Carta.

The old town around the cathedral close is worth wandering, with half-timbered buildings, a bustling market square (markets on Tuesdays and Saturdays), and good pubs. Salisbury is the natural jumping-off point for [Stonehenge](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/stonehenge), but do not overlook the stone circle at [Avebury](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/avebury) nearby — less famous but in many ways more impressive, since you can walk among the stones.
