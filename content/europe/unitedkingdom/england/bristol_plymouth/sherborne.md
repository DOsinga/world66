Sherborne is one of the most handsome small towns in Dorset — built almost entirely of honey-coloured Ham stone, with a magnificent abbey church, two castles, and a famous public school. It feels wealthy and well-kept, the kind of English country town that appears on magazine covers.

Sherborne Abbey dates from the 8th century, though the current building is mostly 15th-century Perpendicular Gothic. The fan vaulting in the nave is exceptional — among the earliest and finest in England. The Old Castle is a ruined 12th-century palace where Sir Walter Raleigh lived briefly before deciding it was too draughty and building the New Castle next door, a handsome Elizabethan lodge set in grounds landscaped by Capability Brown.

The town centre is compact and walkable, with good independent shops along Cheap Street and Half Moon Street. Sherborne is a good base for exploring Dorset — the Jurassic Coast is about 30 minutes south, and the countryside around the Blackmore Vale to the north is some of the quietest and prettiest in southern England.
