Taunton is the county town of Somerset, sitting in the broad Vale of Taunton Deane between the Quantock and Blackdown Hills. Both hill ranges are Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty, and the town makes a good base for exploring the wider south-west.

The Somerset Levels and Moors stretch to the north-east, the Brendon Hills and Exmoor lie to the north, and the coastlines of north Somerset and south Devon are within an hour's drive. Taunton itself is a market town with a long history: its castle, now home to the Museum of Somerset, dates back to Norman times and was the site of Judge Jeffreys' Bloody Assizes after Monmouth's Rebellion in 1685.
