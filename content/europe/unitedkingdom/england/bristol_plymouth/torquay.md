Torquay is the main resort of the so-called English Riviera on the south [Devon](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon) coast, known for its mild climate and palm-lined seafront. The town has nine sandy beaches, several of which hold European Blue Flag awards for water quality.

Beyond the beaches, Torquay has Kents Cavern, one of the most important prehistoric cave sites in Britain, and the country park at Cockington Court. It is also famous as the birthplace of Agatha Christie.
