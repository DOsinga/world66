**Living the Life in Torquay**

This **Torquay travel guide **will help you as you travel to this area in England. Torquay is a county in England which is famous for its scenic view of the sea as well as its vast agricultural areas. Torquay is famed for its healthful climate. In fact, it is considered as the English Riviera and often compared to Montpellier which is famous for its beaches and good weather. If you are traveling in Torquay, here is a helpful travel guide that will help you get by in Torquay.

**Getting in and Around **

The best thing about Torquay is that it is connected to the UK highway network through the road A380. Moreover, you can also reach the different scenic places in the county as the roads are well-paved and that there are a lot of buses that go in and around town. All beaches in Torquay are accessible through the network of roads that are built around the county.

**Sights**

Torquay has nine sandy beaches that are lined with tropical palm trees. Moreover, the seaside resorts in Torquay have high water standards. In fact, this is the only place in all of Europe to have gained three European Blue Flags for its great beaches. Torquay is also famous for its other attractions such as the Kents Cavern which was the home of the earliest men in Torquay and the country park of Cockington Court.

**Seafood **

If you love the flavors of the sea, then you will enjoy the food served at local restaurants in Torquay. Food comes cheap in Torquay as restaurants buy fresh catch in the market which is abundant with fresh catch from the Emerald Bay. There are also local restaurants that specialize in dishes like French, Italian and Thai cuisines.

**Accommodations **

Torquay has a lot of bed and breakfast that are cheap. For instance, the Astor Apartments is one of the cheapest accommodations in Torquay. Cheap accommodations range from $30 to $50 which is a perfect price range for budget-conscious travelers.

However, if you do not mind splurging your money on accommodations, there are also some hotels in Torquay that are expensive and the West Wing Cottage is one of them. The Hotel&rsquo;s west wing cottage offers an incredible sea view and a direct water front. The cottage includes two-bedroom suites, en-suite bathrooms with an iron claw foot baths, Large LCD TV and many more. Staying here should be a minimum of three nights for only $430.
