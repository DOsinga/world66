Weymouth is a seaside town on the Dorset coast with a sweeping Georgian esplanade, a working harbour, and easy access to the Jurassic Coast. King George III put it on the map when he came here to bathe in the 1780s, and the town has been drawing visitors ever since — though these days the crowd is more bucket-and-spade than royal.

The harbour is the liveliest part of town, with fishing boats, cafes, and restaurants packed along the quayside. The old town behind it has narrow streets and a few good pubs. But the real draw is the coastline: Chesil Beach stretches eighteen miles to the west, the Isle of Portland juts into the Channel to the south, and the whole stretch is UNESCO-listed for its geology.
