**Useful England Budget Travel Ideas**

People planning a trip to the UK can use the following **England Budget Travel **ideas to make some handy savings:

**Transportation Options**

The cheapest way to travel in England is using the bus and tube
(local train) network. The Eurolines bus service is particularly
budgeted and very systematic. When traveling in metro trains, it is
better to buy a rail pass. This pass can be easily personalized
according to your budgetary and itinerary requirements. Trains are more
economical than buses when traveling to locations near the outskirts of a
city. Some rail passes provide a packaged deal wherein budgeted entry
to various tourist destinations is included.

**Scheduling Suggestions**

England is among Europe's most heavily-toured destinations,
attracting millions of visitors throughout the year. During some months,
the rentals and overall pricing is higher than the rest of the year.
For instance, the tourist volumes are the highest during the English
summer period stretching from July to August. The period starting from
the end of April until September is equally pleasant and encompasses
more packaged deals. Visitors looking for the most discounted season and
who don't mind the winter season should visit England after
mid-October. This is particularly useful for folks who don't plan to
tread outdoors regularly and want to explore the museums, theatres and
art galleries.

**Accommodation Tips**

Finding economical accommodation is probably the biggest challenge in
England for a budgeted traveler. However, a handy budget travel idea
can solve this issue. Instead of searching for bed and breakfast
packages or small hotels, search for regional hostels. Nearly every city
in England has a list of hostels, many with accreditation to
international organizations. Even if a traveler cannot locate a hostel
that endorses something within his professional/cultural profile, hostel
bookings can be easily made and the rates are highly budgeted.
