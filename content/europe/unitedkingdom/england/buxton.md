Buxton is a handsome spa town on the edge of the Peak District, sitting at over 300 metres above sea level — one of the highest market towns in England. The Romans came here for the warm spring water, and the Georgians turned it into a northern rival to Bath, building the grand Crescent and the domed Devonshire Hospital that still dominate the skyline.

The town works as a base for walking in the Peak District, but it has enough of its own to fill a day or two. The Buxton Opera House, a beautiful Edwardian theatre, hosts an acclaimed annual festival each July. The Pavilion Gardens, 23 acres of Victorian parkland along the River Wye, are perfect for an afternoon stroll. And the natural spring water — the same mineral water you see bottled in supermarkets — still flows freely from St Ann's Well opposite the Crescent, where locals queue with bottles.

Buxton is easily reached by train from Manchester (about an hour) on a scenic line through the hills. The Peak District National Park begins immediately south and east of town, with walks to Solomon's Temple, Poole's Cavern, and the limestone dales accessible on foot from the centre.
