Chichester is a compact cathedral city in West Sussex, close to [Portsmouth](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/portsmouth) and about 17 miles west of Brighton. The Romans founded it as Noviomagus Reginorum, and the city's cross-shaped street plan still follows the original layout, with the four main streets meeting at the Market Cross in the centre.

The cathedral dominates the skyline and is one of the few in England visible from the sea. Built in the Norman style and altered over the centuries, it contains a celebrated Chagall window and two medieval tapestries. The medieval walls that ring the city centre are mostly intact and walkable.

Chichester is also the gateway to [Chichester Harbour](/europe/unitedkingdom/england), an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty popular with sailors, and to Fishbourne Roman Palace — one of the largest Roman buildings north of the Alps, just a mile west of the city centre. The Goodwood estate nearby hosts the annual Festival of Speed and revival motor racing events that draw huge crowds in summer.
