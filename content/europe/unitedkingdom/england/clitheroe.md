Clitheroe is a market town in the Ribble Valley, Lancashire, sitting at the foot of Pendle Hill with a small Norman castle keep perched on a limestone crag at its centre. It's a handsome, lively town — with an independent market, good pubs, and a strong local food scene — that serves as the main hub for one of Lancashire's most appealing rural landscapes. The Ribble Valley stretches south and east in a sweep of rolling farmland and wooded river valleys, and Clitheroe makes a good base for exploring it.

Pendle Hill to the south is the obvious walk: it's not dramatic, but the views across to the Yorkshire Dales are wide and the hill's association with the 1612 Pendle Witch Trials gives it a persistent, slightly ominous reputation. The castle museum tells both the local and witch-trial stories well. The town's farmers' market and the Ribble Valley Food Festival draw visitors from Manchester and beyond.
