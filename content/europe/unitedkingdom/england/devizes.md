Devizes is a handsome market town in the heart of Wiltshire, best known for the Caen Hill Locks — a spectacular flight of 29 locks on the Kennet and Avon Canal that lifts boats 72 metres up a steep hillside. Watching a narrowboat work its way up or down the flight is a surprisingly absorbing way to spend an afternoon.

The town itself has a fine market square, a castle mound (the castle is long gone), and the Wiltshire Museum, which holds some of the best Bronze Age finds in Britain, many excavated from nearby Avebury and the surrounding downland. Devizes sits in the middle of one of the richest prehistoric landscapes in Europe — [Avebury](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/avebury) stone circle is 10 minutes north, Stonehenge 30 minutes south, and the chalk horse at Cherhill is visible from the town's edges. For anyone interested in ancient Britain, this is an excellent base.
