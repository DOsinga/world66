Brighton is England's most flamboyant seaside city, sitting on the Sussex coast about 80 km south of London with fast train connections (about 50 minutes from Victoria). It has been a place of pleasure-seeking since the Prince Regent (later George IV) built his extraordinary fantasy palace here in the early 19th century.

The **Royal Pavilion** — George IV's Indian-Gothic-chinoiserie pleasure palace in the centre of town — is the unmistakable landmark and still one of the most unusual buildings in England, with its domes and minarets now surrounded by the city. The adjoining **Brighton Museum** is free and good. The most enjoyable walking is through **The Laines** (the old town, a maze of independent boutiques and cafés) and **The North Laines** (a looser grid of vintage shops, independent restaurants, and music venues), which run north of the clock tower.

Brighton has a large and visible LGBTQ+ community, strong arts and music scenes, and a university population that keeps the city young. The seafront pebble beach and the long pier (the **Palace Pier**) are the obvious holiday scenery, and the promenade has been upgraded considerably over the past decade.

The **Brighton Festival** in May is one of the largest arts festivals in England, with theatre, music, and fringe events filling the city for three weeks.
