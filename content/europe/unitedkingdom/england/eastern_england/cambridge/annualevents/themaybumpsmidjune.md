Inter-college rowing races on the River Cam from Fen Ditton to Chesterton, held on the Wednesday to Saturday after exams at the end of the University's term. The dates this year are 11th - 14th June 2008.

**What are "Bumps"?**
Each college has at least one boat (8 rowers and a cox) in the Bumps, and these are divided into divisions of 16 boats. The placement within these divisions is dependent last year's results, with boats in the higher divisions retaining their final place from last year. The remaining crews compete for the final 8 places in the lowest division. Men's boats are seperate to women's, and there are two distinct sets of divisions accordingly.

The lowest division races first. Each boat lines up along the river bank a boat and a half's length away from the one in front, in order of placement within the division. A cannon shot sounds the start of the race, and each crew is off. The aim is to catch up to the crew in front of you and "bump" their boat (in practice, this generally means pulling alongside, as very few coxes are willing to risk being injured by actual contact between the boats). When a "bump" occurs, the two boats involved pull to the side of the river immediately.

The crew behind a "bump" are not out of the race, however. Not only do they have to row faster than the boat chasing them to avoid being bumped themselves, but they boat in front of them is two boats further along than previously. If they manage to catch it, this is called an "overbump". Overbumps are fairly frequent, however double and triple overbumps are much rarer - though not unheard of. In cases where a boat is not fast enough to bump or slow enough that they are bumped, they must complete the length of the course, which is a gruelling 2km, and is called "rowing over".

The starting positions of each boat involved in a bump are swapped for the next day's races.

When a boat is at the top of a division, either by rowing over or bumping to get there, they have to then race as the last boat in the division above them.

This is repeated each day for four days, with the starting positions for the last three days being the finishing positions from the first day. At the end of the races, the boat at the top of the first division is crowned "Head of the River" and burns an boat. Each crew who bumped every day is awarded a "Blade" (the term given to the oars, only if they choose to purchase their blade it is emblazoned with their names and date) and can be seen with greenery adorning themselves and their boat on their triumphant return ot the boat houses after racing on the Saturday, while each crew who were bumped each day are awarded "Spoons".
