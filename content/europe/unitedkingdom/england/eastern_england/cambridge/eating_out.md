---
title: "Eating Out in Cambridge"
type: section
---

Cambridge is most well known as the home of the University of Cambridge.
Being a college town it is visited by many people all the time. This area is full of historically significant buildings to visit. Restaurants in the area
vary from local cuisine to French and Mediterranean restaurants. You
can find a lot of inexpensive places to grab a bite to eat or you might
sit down in a fine restaurant for an extravagant meal. For the most
part you’ll be able to get exactly what you were looking for.

**Fine Dining**

Even for a college town there are fine dining options to explore in
Cambridge. Midsummer House is an example. They have a two course set
lunch menu for $90 and a three course set dinner menu for $120. It is
set in a lovely cottage with a menu that changes frequently depending
on the ingredients available. They serve mostly Mediterranean inspired
foods and the chef works hard to create new and interesting selections.
Some of the creations featured on the menu have been beef Rossini and
roast squab pigeon. Reservations are required to dine here; the earlier
you make a reservation the better table you’ll receive.

**Local Cuisine**

British foods are usually unfussy foods that are made with little
fluff or thrills. It’s simple quality food made with local
ingredients. Common dishes include meat pies, fish and chips and
puddings. One restaurant in Cambridge that serves local foods is The
Cambridge Chop House. The restaurant is open for lunch and dinner and
the menu is centered around traditional British Food. You can get
things like duck breast, suet pudding or roast rabbit at very
reasonable prices. Average prices here are about $20 to $30 for
dinner. For other local restaurants try The Free Press and the
Salisbury Arms.

**Pubs**

Pubs are a big thing all over England and that’s no different in
Cambridge. Some of the best places for cheap good food are the pub
foods that are served in a number of different of establishments. Along
with a pint of beer you can get sandwiches or heavy meals. At the Green
Man you will find a great menu of traditional pub foods like bangers
and mash or English pies. They also serve other dishes like fish cakes
in a curry sauce. Prices are usually around $10 to $20. Other pubs you
might try are The Snug and The Globe.
