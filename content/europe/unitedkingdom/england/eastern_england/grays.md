Grays is a Thames-side town in the borough of Thurrock, Essex, sitting on the north bank of the river about 30 kilometres east of central London. It is a working town rather than a tourist destination, but it has a few things worth stopping for if you are passing through or exploring the Thames estuary.

Grays Beach Riverside Park offers pleasant walks along the river with views across to Tilbury Fort on the opposite bank. The Thameside Theatre and Central Complex hosts local productions and events. State Cinema, a striking 1930s Art Deco building on the High Street, is one of the finest surviving examples of its type in Essex, though it is no longer in use as a cinema.

The town is well connected by rail, with c2c trains reaching Fenchurch Street station in central London in about 35 minutes. The Lakeside Shopping Centre at nearby West Thurrock is one of the largest in the country. For a more interesting outing, take the short ferry crossing to Tilbury and visit the well-preserved Elizabethan fort where Queen Elizabeth I rallied her troops against the Spanish Armada in 1588.
