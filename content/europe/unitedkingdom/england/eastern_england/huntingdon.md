Huntingdon is a small market town on the River Great Ouse in Cambridgeshire, best known as the birthplace of Oliver Cromwell. The Cromwell Museum, housed in the former grammar school where he studied, tells the story of the man who overthrew a king and briefly turned England into a republic.

The town has a handsome medieval bridge connecting it to Godmanchester across the river, and the surrounding Ouse valley is flat, green, and good for walking and cycling. Huntingdon is a practical stop on the way to or from [Cambridge](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/eastern_england/cambridge), about twenty minutes north by train.
