Exeter is the cathedral city of Devon, a compact and walkable place where Roman walls, medieval lanes, and a lively university quarter sit side by side. The cathedral, built between 1114 and 1400, has the longest unbroken stretch of Gothic vaulting in the world — the view down the nave is one of those moments where you stop and stare.

The city was heavily bombed in 1942 and the postwar rebuilding was not always kind, but enough of the old fabric survives to reward exploration. The medieval underground passages, originally built to bring water into the city, can be visited on guided tours. The quayside area along the River Exe has been revitalised with cafes, antique shops, and a canal basin, and the Exe Estuary is excellent for birdwatching. Exeter also serves as the gateway to both Dartmoor to the west and the Jurassic Coast to the east, making it a natural base for exploring Devon.
