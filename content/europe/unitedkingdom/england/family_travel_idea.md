**Best of England Family Travel Ideas**

Visitors to the UK shouldn't worry about **England Family Travel **options. England offers a wide range of recreational and vacationing choices. One of the most preferred England family travel options is to visit the Kew Gardens in London.

**Overview of Kew Royal Botanic Gardens**

Kew Gardens is now a listed UNESCO World Heritage Site. Kew Gardens has one of the world's most exhaustive collections of living, rare and almost-extinct orchids. These gardens were started toward the end of 18th century, under the patronage of King George III. Situated around the Kew Palace in London, the gardens have grown in size and splendor with every successive generation of the British royalty. Today, the gardens are spread across 132 hectares. The Royal Botanic Gardens host the annual Tropical Extravaganza Festival in February. As a part of a Kew Garden visit, a family can see:

**Princess of Wales Conservatory**

This includes a central pond and a small island surrounded by tropical plants, including rare, octopus-shaped flowering plants. There are more than 25,000 species of orchids on display.

**Waterlily House**

It displays biodiversity from across the world with the theme changing every few months. Waterlily House is recommended for helping children understand issues like sustainability and conservation in a fun-filled manner.

**Explore Kew beyond Garden Visits**

The estate around the Kew Palace includes many historical buildings. This includes the Palm House, Orangery, Temperate House and Pagoda, apart from landmarks like Queen Charlotte's Cottage. The complex houses RBG Kew - a leading scientific organization in botanical research.

**Reaching Kew Gardens**

Kew is a national landmark in England and known to every cab driver in London. It is situated near Richmond, along the banks of the River Thames. It is located just about 10 km from the southwest shopping districts of London. Visitors can reach Kew via the local trains (tube) also.
