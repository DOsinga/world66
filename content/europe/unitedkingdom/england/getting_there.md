**Flying to England**

The most important airport in England is London Heathrow Airport in the city of London (IATA Aiport code: LHR). It offers over 180 destinations in 90 different countries. Flying to and from England is the fastest way to travel because there a lot of major airlines that compete with each other to get you to you there. During your stay in England, you will find that flying can sometimes be cheaper than riding a train between cities, when booked in advance with discount air flight companies such as Ryan Air. If you're looking for a different kind of airport, try the London Stansted (IATA Airport code: STN). This airport was designed by the well known Manchester born architect, Sir Norman Foster. Sir Foster was responsible for major works in architecture in Europe.

**Getting to your Destination**

After you arrive to Heathrow Airport, you will have various options to travel to your final destination in England. The National Express and other coach operators (term used in England for bus) run services from the airport to more than 500 destinations, including England. The local bus network operates to several destinations in West London and the Thames Valley. If you are using the train, there are several Railair coaches (buses) that connect the Heathrow Airport with the rail network at Feltham, Watford, Woking and Reading.

If you decided to arrive via the London Stansted Airport, it is a 45 minute train ride from the London Liverpool Street station. The National Express runs regular bus service from and to this airport to the Heathrow and Gatwick Airports.
