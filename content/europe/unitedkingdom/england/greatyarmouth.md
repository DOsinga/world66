Great Yarmouth is a coastal town on the Norfolk coast in East Anglia, built on a narrow sandbar between the sea and the Broads. It has been an important port and fishing town since the medieval period — the herring fishery made it one of the wealthiest towns in England in the 13th century — and later became one of England's first seaside resorts. Today it is a traditional English seaside town with all that implies: a long sandy beach, a funfair-heavy seafront, fish and chip shops, and amusement arcades.

The town has two distinct characters. The seafront Golden Mile is unabashedly commercial, with all the trappings of British bucket-and-spade tourism. The historic quarter behind it — the old town of Rows, the medieval walls, the Tolhouse museum, the merchant's houses — is genuinely interesting and largely ignored by day-trippers focused on the beach. The South Quay has several well-presented historic buildings and the Time and Tide Museum, which tells the story of the fishing industry. The Norfolk Broads, England's largest protected wetland, begins just inland and is superb for birdwatching and boat trips.
