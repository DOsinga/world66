Hitchin is a handsome market town in north Hertfordshire with a broad market square, a fine medieval church, and a wealth of independent shops and cafes that have so far resisted the chain-store takeover afflicting many English towns. The market, held on Tuesdays, Fridays, and Saturdays, has been running since the 13th century.

St Mary's Church is one of the largest parish churches in Hertfordshire, its perpendicular tower visible from across the town. The River Hiz runs through the centre, and the streets around the square are lined with buildings spanning several centuries — timber-framed Tudor, Georgian brick, and Victorian commercial.

Hitchin Lavender, on the outskirts of town, draws visitors in summer when the fields turn purple. The town is an easy train ride from London King's Cross, making it a good day trip for anyone wanting a taste of English market-town life without venturing too far from the capital.
