Huddersfield sits in a bowl of West Yorkshire hills, about 15 miles south-east of Manchester. It's a working town with a proud industrial past — it made the cloth that dressed much of Victorian Britain — and a lively university that keeps it young. Not a typical tourist stop, but it rewards the curious traveller.

The town centre is compact and walkable, anchored by a fine Victorian railway station (John Betjeman called it "the most beautiful station facade in England"). Castle Hill looms over the skyline to the south, a landmark visible for miles. The canal network threading through town connects to some of Britain's most dramatic tunnel engineering.
