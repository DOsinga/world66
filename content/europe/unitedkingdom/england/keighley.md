Keighley is a West Yorkshire mill town that punches well above its weight as a base for exploring some of England's most evocative countryside. On its own terms it is a working town — blunt, unpretentious, with the bones of the Victorian wool trade still visible in its stone mills and terraced streets. But its real value to travellers is what surrounds it.

The Keighley and Worth Valley Railway runs steam trains from Keighley station through the Worth Valley to [Haworth](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/haworth) and on to Oxenhope — a genuinely lovely journey through upland countryside, and one of the most complete preserved steam railways in Britain. Haworth itself, just four miles up the valley, was home to the Bronte sisters, and the Parsonage where they wrote is one of the finest literary shrines in the country.

East Riddlesden Hall, a seventeenth-century manor house now managed by the National Trust, sits on the edge of town beside the River Aire and is worth an afternoon. The town is also a useful starting point for walks onto Ilkley Moor and into the Dales.
