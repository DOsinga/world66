Keswick is the main town of the northern Lake District, sitting at the head of Derwentwater with Skiddaw rising behind it. It is a compact, slate-grey Victorian town of outdoor shops, pubs, and tea rooms — the launching point for walks up Catbells, Skiddaw, Blencathra, and the Borrowdale fells, and home to the Theatre by the Lake, one of the best small theatres in the north of England. Take the ferry across Derwentwater to Hawes End and walk back along the shore, or drive the Borrowdale road south through Grange and Rosthwaite for some of the finest valley scenery in the Lakes.
