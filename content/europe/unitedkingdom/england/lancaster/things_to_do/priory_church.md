The Priory Church of St Mary sits alongside the castle on the hill and is one of the finest medieval churches in the northwest of England. The carved choir stalls date from the 14th century and are particularly good. The churchyard has views over the Lune estuary. It was founded as a Benedictine priory in 1094, though little of the original Norman structure survives — the current building is mostly 14th and 15th century.
