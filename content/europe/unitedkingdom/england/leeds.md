Leeds is the largest city in Yorkshire and the principal city of the north of England. It sits at the crossroads where the main north-south and east-west motorways and railways meet, and that central position has shaped it into a hub of commerce, finance, and industry.

The industrial past is still visible in the Victorian arcades and grand civic buildings of the city centre, but Leeds has reinvented itself convincingly. The waterfront along the River Aire and the Leeds-Liverpool Canal has been transformed into a stretch of restaurants, bars, and apartments. The city's two universities bring over 60,000 students, which keeps the cultural and nightlife scene lively and affordable.

Leeds is also Britain's greenest city by ratio of parkland to population, second in Europe only to Vienna. Temple Newsam covers 1,000 acres and is one of Europe's largest urban parks, while Roundhay Park to the north has two lakes and 700 acres of woodland and gardens. Beyond the parks, the city has serious cultural weight: the Royal Armouries Museum, the Henry Moore Institute, and Leeds Art Gallery are all worth your time. Historic houses like Harewood House and Temple Newsam House sit just outside the city in their own estates.

If you are coming for sport, Leeds is a passionate rugby league and football city. Headingley hosts international cricket and rugby, and Elland Road is home to Leeds United.
