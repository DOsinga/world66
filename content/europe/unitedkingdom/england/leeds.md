Leeds is an industrial city in Yorkshire with round and about half a million inhabitants. Although it bears the ‘industrial-city-character’ and, consequently, is not the most inviting one, Leeds has managed to recover from the industrial setbacks and media companies and government department employ most of its residents these days.

 Leeds is also an university town and houses over 25,000 students. No wonder that there will always be something going on in the evenings (and beyond). ‘Northern Star Magazine’ gives a weekly overview of everything that’s going in town. For history and culture buffs Leeds might be a disappointing, there are not really museums worth mentioning. Most interesting mansions dating from Jacobean times and Victorian times are located (just) outside Leeds in large parks.
