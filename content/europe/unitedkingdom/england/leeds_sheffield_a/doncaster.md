Doncaster is a market town in South Yorkshire built where the Great North Road crosses the River Don — a crossroads since Roman times, when it was the fort of Danum. Today it is best known for horse racing. The Doncaster Racecourse has hosted the St Leger, the oldest of England's five Classic races, since 1776. Race days transform the town.

The Mansion House on the high street is one of only three civic mansion houses in England (the others are in London and York), and the minster of St George is a fine Gothic Revival church designed by George Gilbert Scott. The market, held since the Middle Ages, still operates from the Corn Exchange and the surrounding stalls.

Doncaster is not a destination in itself, but it is a reasonable stop on a journey through Yorkshire. The surrounding area has good walking along the River Don, and Conisbrough Castle — a well-preserved Norman keep about five miles southwest — is worth the detour.
