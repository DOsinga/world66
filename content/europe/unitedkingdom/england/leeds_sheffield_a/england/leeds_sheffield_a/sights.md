This is an area full of history and full of sights, natural and manmade.

[Yorkshire's ruined Abbeys and Priories

Yorkshires abbey ruins are as noted as any in the UK and a week's holiday could well be spent with this as a theme. 

**Fountains Abbey near**
Ripon](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/leeds_sheffield_a/sights/yorkshires_ruined), one of the finest, which joins the estate grounds of Studley Royal is owned by the National Trust which has erected a substantial Visitors' Centre and eating area. This is extensive and many of the monastic builidngs can be seen. There would be no difficulty in spending a long half day here. 

**Bolton Abey** is on the Duke of Devonshire's estates by the Wharfe quite near Skipton. Although most of the priory suffered the normal fate at the time of the dissolution of the monasteries the nave, which was in use for public services was reprieved. There are footpaths beside the river - beware of the fast-flowing Strid with children - and on the far side up into the hills near Simon's Seat [no dogs on the Devonshire estates] 

**Jervaulx Abbey** at the lower end of Wensleydale has remains less imposing than some but there is a wonderful sense of peace as you walk around repiecing the buildings in your mind. 

**Rievaulx Abbey **in the area of the
[North York Moors](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/leeds_sheffield_a/north_york_moors) is another very striking building and, again the area is full of walks. 

**Mount Grace Priory** is near Northallerton and looks good from outside but I have only passed it when working and I don't know how much there is to be seen. 

**Easby Abbey** on the Swale near Yorkshireis not very large but provides a beautiful and easy short walk. 

**Byland Abbey** is very close to Rievaulx but is west of the edge of the NYM National Park. It is well worth a look on the same visit and the inn across the road provides an ideal lunch time stop. 

**Kirkstall Priory** is in the City of Leeds itself and passed without much notice every day by many on their way to work. However its remains are extensive and there is a fascinating museum attached to it. 

**Whitby Abbey** remains aren't exensive but they are splendidly sited and immenely picturesque. Ther is no need for a specific visit but they enhance the beauty of this lovely seaside town. [more..](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/leeds_sheffield_a/sights/yorkshires_ruined)
