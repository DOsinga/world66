Harrogate is a handsome Victorian spa town in [North Yorkshire](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/northyorkshire), with broad tree-lined avenues, immaculate public gardens, and an air of quiet prosperity. The town grew rich in the 18th and 19th centuries on the strength of its mineral springs — visitors came from across Europe to "take the waters" — and the legacy shows in the elegant stone architecture of the town centre.

The 200-acre Stray, a vast swathe of open grassland, wraps around the southern edge of town and is one of its most distinctive features. The Royal Pump Room Museum tells the story of the spa era, and Betty's Tea Rooms on Parliament Street is a Harrogate institution. The town is also a major conference venue, which means it punches well above its weight for restaurants — you will eat better here than in most English towns this size. The [Yorkshire Dales](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/yorkshiredales) are a short drive west.
