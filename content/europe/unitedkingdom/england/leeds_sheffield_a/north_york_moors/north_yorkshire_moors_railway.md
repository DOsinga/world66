One of England's great heritage railways, the NYMR runs 24 miles of steam-hauled track between Pickering and Whitby, crossing the full width of the national park. The journey takes about an hour and a half and passes through Goathland — familiar to millions as Hogsmeade station from the Harry Potter films — and over the open moorland of the Goathland plateau before dropping down into Eskdale and on to the coast. Trains run from spring through autumn; check the timetable as services can be busy on summer weekends.
