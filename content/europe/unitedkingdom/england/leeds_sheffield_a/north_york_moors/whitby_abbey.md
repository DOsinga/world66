The ruined Benedictine abbey stands on the East Cliff above Whitby, visible for miles along the coast. Founded in 657 AD and destroyed during the Dissolution of the Monasteries, what remains is a skeletal Gothic shell whose pointed arches frame open sky. Come at dusk when the stonework turns amber and the atmosphere Bram Stoker found irresistible becomes easy to understand — he used the abbey as a setting in Dracula after staying in Whitby in 1890.
