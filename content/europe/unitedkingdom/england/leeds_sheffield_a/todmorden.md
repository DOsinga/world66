Todmorden sits at the far west of West Yorkshire, at a point where the county boundary with Lancashire once ran beneath the town's grand Italianate town hall — a building that straddles two counties. Now part of [Calderdale](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/leeds_sheffield_a/calderdale), the town sits in a steep Pennine valley where the River Calder and several tributaries meet.

Despite its Yorkshire postal address, Todmorden's industrial character was shaped more by Lancashire cotton than Yorkshire wool. The town grew up around the textile mills in the valley bottoms, and the transport links that made it viable were Yorkshire connections first: the Rochdale Canal and the original trans-Pennine railway through the Calder valley were engineering achievements of their time. The canal has since been restored and is now popular with narrowboaters. The surrounding moorland is classic dark Pennine landscape, worth walking on a clear day.
