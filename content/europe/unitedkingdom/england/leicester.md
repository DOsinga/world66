Leicester may not be the first English city that springs to mind, but it rewards the curious. Founded by the Romans as Ratae Corieltauvorum, it has been inhabited continuously for nearly two thousand years, and fragments of Roman baths and walls still survive near the city centre. In 2012 the remains of Richard III were famously discovered under a Leicester car park, and the King Richard III Visitor Centre now tells that extraordinary story.

The city is one of the most ethnically diverse in England, and the Golden Mile — a stretch of Belgrave Road lined with Indian restaurants, jewellers, and sari shops — is the beating heart of one of the largest South Asian communities in the country. Leicester's Diwali celebrations are the biggest outside India. The New Walk Museum and Art Gallery is worth a visit, and the cathedral, where Richard III now rests, has been handsomely renovated.
