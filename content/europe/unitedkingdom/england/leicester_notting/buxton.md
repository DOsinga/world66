Buxton is a handsome spa town perched at over 300 meters in the Derbyshire Peak District, making it one of the highest market towns in England. The Romans knew about the thermal springs here, and the town's Georgian and Victorian architecture reflects centuries of visitors coming to take the waters. The Crescent, a sweeping curve of Georgian townhouses modeled after the Royal Crescent in Bath, has been magnificently restored as a luxury spa hotel, finally completing a renovation that took decades.

The Buxton Opera House, designed by Frank Matcham and opened in 1903, is a jewel. Its ornate Edwardian interior hosts the annual Buxton International Festival in July, which draws serious opera and literary programming to this small town. Even if you are not catching a performance, the building is worth admiring from the outside, and tours are sometimes available.

The Pavilion Gardens, a lovely park in the town center with a Victorian conservatory, provide a pleasant place to stroll, and the Pump Room still dispenses the warm mineral water that made Buxton famous. You can fill a bottle for free from St Ann's Well, right opposite the Crescent, and taste the same slightly warm, mineral-rich water that has drawn people here for two thousand years.

But the real reason to come to Buxton is access to the Peak District. The town sits right on the edge of the national park, and some of the finest walking in England is within easy reach. Poole's Cavern, a natural limestone cave just a short walk from the town center, is an excellent introduction to the geology that makes this landscape so dramatic. Solomon's Temple, the folly at the top of the hill above the cavern, rewards the climb with panoramic views across the peaks.
