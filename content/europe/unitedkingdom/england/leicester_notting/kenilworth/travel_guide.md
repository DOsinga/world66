A **Kenilworth Travel Guide** is essentially about presenting information regarding the scenic beauty and historical riches of the town of Kenilworth. Some towns in England are often referred as reminders of the glory days of "Elizabeth&rsquo;s England." This refers to an era of riches and artistic progression in England. Among such towns, Kenilworth is often quoted as the best example. Kenilworth offers everything that a visitor in England could ask for—well-preserved places of historical interest for self-exploration, naturally-beautiful surroundings and plenty of small shops to buy souvenirs.

**Historical Overview**

Kenilworth wasn&rsquo;t always as popular. In fact, it was first referred to during the middle part of the 11th century. It gained prominence during the reign of Elizabeth I. During this part of England&rsquo;s history, Kenilworth established an enduring association with the royalty. It was favored as a recreational locale by the likes of Henry III. Some of the town&rsquo;s landmarks like the Castle Hotel, the Kings Arms and Warwick Road owe their royal aura to this period.

**Delving Deeper into what Kenilworth Offers**

The present-day Kenilworth has turned into a major tourist destination of Europe. It is well-connected with all the major European cities. Its cramped market may still exude a distinct, medieval charm but they put forth facilities that can please the most demanding of overseas visitors. Other must-visit locations include the abbeys that still bear marks of some historical battles. Kenilworth is primarily known for its Kenilworth Castle. The ruins of this castle complex have attained legendary proportions. The ruins can be explored for hours with their partially-demolished, red sandstone walls forming a unique ambience. The Kenilworth Castle is often quoted in England&rsquo;s folklore. Among other remnants that date back to England&rsquo;s era of royalty, the Leicester&rsquo;s Gatehouse is worth mentioning. The surrounding gardens are beautifully manicured. The Gatehouse has been turned into a museum of sorts and provides an interesting peek into England&rsquo;s troubled history.

**Looking Around Kenilworth and Kenilworth Events**

On the outskirts of Kenilworth, visitors can go for long walks among the lush green surroundings. There are plenty of pubs serving fresh, cheap food and many varieties of native ales. Further, this town has been able to retain its traditional celebrations. The list of annual events here includes celebrations by local farmers. Kenilworth Carnival is very popular among the natives. There are dedicated days to host French-styled, colonial markets. There are many family events, too, like the fireworks arranged as a part of the Kenilworth Round Table celebrations.
