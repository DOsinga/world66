---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Leicester has a compact historic core with a surprising concentration of quality museums and medieval buildings — most of them free. The Jewry Wall, the Richard III Visitor Centre, and the New Walk Museum are the highlights, but the city rewards a day of wandering.

**Jewry Wall Museum**

A fine and rare section of Roman walling dating back around 2,000 years, with a permanent exhibition on Roman Leicester and changing temporary displays.

address: St. Nicholas Circle

tel: 0116 247 3021

opening_hours: Mon-Sun 11am to 4:30pm

category: Museum

**Newarke Houses Museum**

Two historic houses — Wygston's Chantry House (Elizabethan) and Skeffington House with a beautiful garden — displaying collections of toys, clocks, and social history.

address: The Newarke

tel: 0116 247 3222

opening_hours: Mon-Sat 10am to 5pm

category: Museum

**Belgrave Hall**

A Georgian manor house whose collection gives an insight into the lives of the city's upper classes and their servants.

address: Church Road

tel: 0116 266 6590

opening_hours: Mon-Sat 10am to 5pm

category: Museum

**The Guildhall**

A well-preserved medieval guildhall dating from the 15th century, one of the finest in England.

address: Guildhall Lane

tel: 0116 253 2569

opening_hours: Mon-Sat 10am to 5pm

category: Sight

**Leicester Castle**

Dating from the early 11th century, probably built by William the Conqueror. Features an impressive medieval great hall, courtyards, and vaulted cellars.

address: Guildhall Lane

category: Sight

**New Walk Museum and Art Gallery**

Collections on Ancient Egypt and natural history, plus several galleries covering Expressionist and European art from the 15th century to the present.

address: 53 New Walk

tel: 0116 255 4100

opening_hours: Mon-Sat 10am to 5pm, Sun 1pm to 5pm

category: Museum
