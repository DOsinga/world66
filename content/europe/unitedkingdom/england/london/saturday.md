A single Saturday in February 2003 — the day of the anti-Iraq war march — seen through the eyes of a neurosurgeon living in a grand square near Fitzrovia. McEwan maps central London with precision: the streets, the traffic, the hospital, the rhythms of a particular kind of London life. The political anxiety of that moment gives it an edge that hasn't faded.
