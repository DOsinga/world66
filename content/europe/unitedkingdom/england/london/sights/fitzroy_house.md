Formerly inhabited by playwright George Bernard Shaw, Fitzroy House is where
writer and philosopher L. Ron Hubbard, founder of Scientology, worked in the 1950s.

The library displays the hundreds of Mr Hubbard’s published works for which he was awarded “Most Published Author” by the Guinness Book of World Records. The building is reminiscent of the 1950s, complete with Adler typewriters, Grundig tape recorders, and Western Union telefax.
**
Open:** All year. Guided tours of the house between 11am–5pm by appointment.
**
Admission:** Free admission. Morning coffee and afternoon tea is served.
