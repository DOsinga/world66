Manchester has a lot to offer for the cultural buffs. Although Manchester looks rather modern, one of the highlights is probably a visit to the Cathedral and the Town Hall. For those interested how this industrial city came into being and achieved its present status, it might be an ides to pay a visit to the Museum of Science and History. The museums does not only offer an exhibition and an area where children (and adults) of course can entertain themselves with scientific tricks, it is also possible to trace a little bit of what life must have been like in the old days in the section where they rebuilt houses and streets from earlier Manchester. And this is only one of the many museums in Manchester.

 For those loving English soaps, such as the famous Coronation Street, a visit to the Granada Studios is a must. These are the only television studies that one can visit on a city tour and offer a good view behind the scenes. Besides that, there are several good galleries, excellent libraries, an enormous university complex, nice squares (The Albert Square and Piccadilly Square are probably best known) and parks (Castlefield Urban Heritage park is a good option).

 The centre is actually quite pretty. The thing to do is not to look at the shop windows but just above; the buildings come in all styles and many different colours and stone types. Two buildings which you can (kind of) visit are the newly opened Royal Exchange Theatre in the former exchange, which has a daytime cafe, is  impressive, but even better (get a visitor pass from the porter) is the town hall, in a neo-gothic style with little arches, corners, bridges, alleys, and lots of beautiful masonry. If the Great Hall is not in use you can look at the wall paintings depicting events from Manchester history. A favourite spot of outside architecture is Castlefield, named after the remains of a Roman fortress (minimal), but is has old bridges and buildings in red brick and cast iron all from the haydays of the industrial revolution. Nowadays it has a lot of night life.

 The real origin of the industrial revolution is on the other side of town, behind Piccadilly station, however, it is not really worth a visit unless you are into the depressing or the run down.

**general**

**Manchester Cathedral **

Restored and extended in Victorian times, but with visible remains of medieval church. Side chapels, carvings, bookshop, with accounts of 1940 and 1996 bomb damage. Free entrance.  

address: Victoria Street

tel: (0161) 833 2220

**Town Hall, Manchester **

Neo-Gothic palatial building, home of Manchester City Council. Designed by Alfred Waterhouse, and completed in 1873. Sculptures, stained glass window, paintings, carved stone. Magnificent banquet hall with painted murals depicting the history of Manchester. Venue for many events throughout th [more..](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester/sights/townhallmanchester)

address: Albert Square

tel: (0161) 234 5000

**Barton Arcade **

Beautifully restored Victorian glass arcade with shops and offices. Free entrance  

address: Deansgate, City Centre
