Chester is the best-preserved walled city in Britain, and the walls themselves — built on Roman foundations and completed in the medieval period — are its greatest attraction. A full circuit of the 2-mile walls takes about an hour and offers views over the city, the River Dee, and (on clear days) the Welsh hills to the west.

The city has deep Roman roots. **Deva Victrix** was one of the largest Roman fortresses in Britain, and the remains of a Roman amphitheatre and the Roman Gardens near the Eastgate can be explored. **Chester Cathedral**, largely rebuilt in the 14th and 15th centuries, has exceptional medieval choir stalls and a peaceful cloister garden. The **Rows** — the galleried, half-timbered shopping arcades running above ground level along the main streets — are unique in England and still house shops today.

Chester is a popular day trip from Liverpool (about 45 minutes by train) and Manchester (about an hour), though it rewards an overnight stay to enjoy the streets when the day-trippers have left.
