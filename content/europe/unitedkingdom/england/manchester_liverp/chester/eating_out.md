---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Chester has a good dining range, from traditional pubs serving British staples (pie, fish and chips, Sunday roast) to more ambitious restaurants. **Simon Radley at the Chester Grosvenor** has held a Michelin star since 1990 and is the obvious choice for a splurge. For something more casual, the city's historic pubs — especially those in the converted medieval buildings along the Rows — are good for a relaxed lunch. Be prepared for crowds on summer weekends; book in advance at popular spots.
