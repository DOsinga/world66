The Lake District is England's most mountainous landscape and one of its most visited national parks. Glaciers carved the valleys and lakes during the Ice Age, leaving behind scenery that has inspired poets, painters and walkers for centuries. The M6 motorway brings an ever-growing stream of visitors from the south to enjoy its beauty.

The road up Langdale from Ambleside, passing through Elterwater and Langdale villages, is one of the classic drives of the Lake District, culminating in the impressive view of the Langdale Pikes across Blea Tarn. The main peaks -- Pike o' Stickle, Harrison Stickle, Loft Crag and Pavey Ark -- are all above 2,000 feet but in their solitude they seem much higher.

Derwentwater is claimed by many to be the most beautiful of the lakes. Three miles long and one mile wide, it is surrounded by rich scenery -- a wonderful blending of crag, green fell and wooded slopes with an imposing background of mountains. The now National Trust-owned Lord's Island and St Herbert's Island, where a disciple of St Cuthbert lived in the seventh century, add historical interest.

By car there are two main entrances to the heart of the Lake District: south from Lancaster to Windermere, and north from Penrith to Keswick. On Fridays and Sundays you can expect traffic jams. The Lake District is at its finest when you walk alongside the hills, just at the border of farmland and moor -- the views change from moment to moment and you encounter all manner of plants, animals, streams and stone walls. For an introductory climb, the Old Man of Coniston near [Coniston](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/lakedistrict/coniston_1) in the south or Catbells near [Keswick](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/lakedistrict/keswick) in the north both have fine views and can be reached easily. The serious work is done at Scafell Pike, England's highest point, and the great ridges of Helvellyn and High Street.

If you prefer wide views and a bit of peace and quiet, consider the Yorkshire Dales, the Peak District or the Cheviots instead.
