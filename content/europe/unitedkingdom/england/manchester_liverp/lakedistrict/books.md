---
title: "Books in Lake District"
type: section
---

Many guidebooks have been written for walking in the Lake District but none is better known than the series by Alfred Wainwright. Each book takes a particular part of the Lake District and describes routes up evry mountain in it. There are brilliant sketch maps and every detail where you might go wrong is described and often sketched. Seven books cover the main fells and he added a guide to the Outlying Fells in old age. There are also five Lakeland Sketchbooks as well as some fine books on other areas.

Wainwright died in 1991 and his ashes were scatered on Haystacks, a favourite mountain. As a result it has lost some of the natural and unspoilt character that endeared it to him as visitors have been attracted but it is still a wonderful place accessible with surprising ease from Honister Pass.
