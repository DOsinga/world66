---

John Ruskin, who died in 1900, was described by Tolstoy as great by the standard of all nations at all times. He was a professor of fine art at Oxford before living at Brantwood, across the lake from Coniston village.

The **John Ruskin Museum** in the village has various works of art by Ruskin as well as ancient remains from the area and specimens of the local cottage industry in lace and linen.
