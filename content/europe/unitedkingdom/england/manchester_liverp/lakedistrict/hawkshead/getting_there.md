---
title: Getting There
type: section
---

Hawkshead has no train station. The nearest railheads are Windermere (about 10 miles east) and Ulverston (about 12 miles south-west).

**By car** is the practical choice for most visitors. From the M6, take junction 36 and follow signs to Windermere and then Hawkshead via the B5286. The village itself is traffic-free; there is a large car park on the edge of the village where you leave your vehicle. It fills quickly in summer — arrive before 10am or after 4pm if you can.

**By ferry and bus** is the scenic alternative from the eastern shore of Windermere. The Windermere Ferry crosses from Bowness-on-Windermere to Ferry Nab on the western shore (runs roughly every 20 minutes, carries cars and foot passengers). From there, the 505 bus runs to Hawkshead, continuing to [Coniston](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/lakedistrict/coniston_1). Services are infrequent outside summer — check Traveline before setting out.
