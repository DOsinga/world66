Keswick lies on the shores of Derwentwater, near five major lakes, and serves as the main base for exploring the northern Lake District. There are quaint shops, restaurants and art galleries along the shoreline.

The area was settled in the Stone Age -- three axes dating from 4,000 years ago were found at the **Castlerigg Stone Circle**, a mysterious and atmospheric site that visitors are welcome to explore freely.

Mining was once a huge industry here. Later, pencil-making took over; it is said that the first pencils were made in Keswick following the discovery of graphite deposits. The **Cumberland Pencil Museum** traces the history from those early days to the present.

Stroll along the shoreline, or take a Derwentwater Lake Cruise. Fitz Park and Hope Park are both worth exploring, and the Mirehouse Historic House and Gardens makes a pleasant visit. For a taste of the town's industrial past, the **Honister Slate Mine** offers underground tours. The **Keswick Brewing Company** is worth a stop for anyone who enjoys locally brewed beer.

The cosy pubs and restaurants of Keswick are inviting after a day on the fells. Try the local Tattie Pot, a rich lamb and potato stew, or settle for classic fish and chips.
