High Stile is the highest top of the long ridge running along the south side of Buttermere. The eastern end above Scarth Gap is steep and stony. A far better ascent can be made by way of Sheepbone Rake on the north face of High Crag, or by the north-east ridge of High Stile itself. Most walkers continue westwards towards Red Pike and descend by way of Bleaberry Tarn or, more rewardingly, by way of Scale Force, the highest waterfall in the Lake District.
