Seatallan is not one of the most popular Lakeland fells -- once you have made the long journey round to Wasdale there are much finer hills to climb. An interesting route up can be found over Buckbarrow on the long south ridge, which gives fine views of Wasdale. The flat top has a trig point and an ancient cairn. Return can be made by Greendale Tarn and Gill or over Middle Fell, though the descent is surprisingly steep.
