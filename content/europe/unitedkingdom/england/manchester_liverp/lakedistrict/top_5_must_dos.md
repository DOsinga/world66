---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Lake District"
type: section
---

**• Take to the waters**. The Lake District boasts over 16 lakes, many of
them absolutely vast, and all of them extremely beautiful. Rowing and
sailing are popular activities on the larger lakes such as Derwentwater
and Windermere, and boats are available for hire lakeside. Row boats
and pedal boats are great fun for families, while the steamer boats
that traverse the waters at Conistan are pleasant way to take in the
scenery without having to put in the leg work (and arm work!)

**•
Explore the countryside**. The glistening lakes are not the only
attraction of this beautiful part of the world. There are some 3,500
kilometers of public right of way in the Lake District, perfect for
exploring on foot, by bicycle or even on horse back. From strenuous
hill walks to gentle lakeside strolls, the countryside is open to all
ages and abilities.

**• Try an adventure sport**. Those who like to
take their exercise with a large dose of adrenaline will be in their
element in the Lake District. Wind surfing, abseiling, mountain
climbing and quad bike racing are just some of the white-knuckle
thrills to be found here, and there are numerous centers offering
equipment hire and, where necessary, lessons.

**• Visit a
traditional pub**. British pubs are famous the world over, and there is
no shortage of wonderfully traditional watering holes in the Lake
District. During the summer seek out those with ‘beer gardens’ and
enjoy a pint if beer or cider in the great outdoors, and in the winter
find a cozy corner indoors, preferably by the fireside. A spot of ‘pub
grub’ – meals that range from meat pies and chunky chips (potato
fries) to international dishes – completes the experience.

**• Follow
in Withnail’s footsteps**. The British cult film *Withnail and I* is said
to be the most quoted movie of all time, and the Lake District is the spot
where Withnail finds that he has ‘come on holiday by mistake’. There
are numerous opportunities to visit locations made famous by the film.
Much of *Withnail and I* was shot in and around the picturesque town of
Penrith, so film buffs should head here for maximum photo-snapping
opportunities.
