---
title: "When to Go in Lake District"
type: section
---

The northwest of England may be famous for its rain, but that
shouldn't prevent tourists from taking a trip to this scenic part of
the country.

**Spring and Summer Months**

One of the most beautiful regions of the UK, the lake district is a
must-see in any weather, but is perhaps best visited during the spring
and summer months, when the local flora and fauna are at their most
dramatic. Rain is, unfortunately, a threat at any time of year, but the
months of July and August offer the best chance of clear days, warm
weather and even a spot of sunshine.

**Autumn Months**

Autumn (October to November) is also a beautiful time of year to
visit the Lake District. When the leaves turn golden and the nights
turn deliciously crisp, you'll want to wrap up warm for those evening
walks by the lakes. November 5th - Guy Fawkes night or 'Bonfire night'
- is a great time to be in the Lake District, as huge bonfires and
fireworks attract hordes of visitors and locals alike. Put on a cosy
hat and gloves, then head out into the night to sip a warming mulled
wine, chew on bonfire toffee and spicy parkin cake (specialities of the
region) as you take in the dramatic displays.

**Winter Months**

Many visitors head to the Lake District for winter breaks, and
during December, January and February the beautiful countryside becomes
a real winter wonderland, with frozen lakes, snow-capped mountain peaks
and frosty mornings and evenings. The Lake District makes a great place
to celebrate New Year's Eve. Hire a cosy cottage for two for a romantic
break, or a larger country cottage for groups of friends ringing in the
new year together.

The wealth of opportunity for outdoor thrills and spills makes the
Lake District a popular destination for adventure sports enthusiasts.
The summer lends itself well to hill walking, hiking, swimming in
natural pools, rafting, climbing, and countless other adrenaline-packed
adventures. During the winter, kids will love the chance to take their
toboggans to the hills of the Lake District and hurtle along the snowy
surfaces, while the colder temperatures offer a range of winter sports
options to visitors of all ages.

Whatever the time of year, a visit to the Lake District can be as
exhilarating or as relaxing as you choose - just don't forget your
umbrella!
