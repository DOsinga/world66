Lancaster is a historic city on the River Lune, dominated by its castle, which served as one of the longest-running prisons in Europe until 2011. The pedestrianised town centre has a fish market, interesting shops and some unexpected sights, including a Roman Bath-House and the Catholic Cathedral of St Peter.

The 200-year-old Lancaster Canal winds through the city and its towpath leads to some excellent waterside pubs. Lancaster University brings a young energy to the city, and the area hosts several festivals throughout the year. From Lancaster it is a short drive to the [Lake District](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/lakedistrict) or the Yorkshire Dales.
