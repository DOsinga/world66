The castle sits at the top of the hill in the oldest part of the city, above the Priory Church, and dominates the skyline. It has Norman origins — the keep dates from the 12th century — but most of what you see is medieval and later. Parts are open for guided tours that take in the courtroom where the Pendle Witches were tried in 1612, the cells, and the drop room used for executions. The castle only ceased to be an operational prison in 2011, which gives it a particular edge: the tour isn't just heritage dressing, it's a place that was genuinely in use.
