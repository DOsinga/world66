Situated on a broad curve of the Mersey, Liverpool is one of the world's great port cities. In the 19th century it was second only to London in the British Empire, and its docks were the departure point for millions of emigrants heading to the Americas. If you live in North America, your ancestors more than likely passed through the famous Mersey docks.

Liverpool is a city alive with music, sport, good humour and vitality. The Beatles put it on the cultural map in the 1960s, and the music scene has never really slowed down. Two Premier League football clubs inspire fierce local rivalry, and the city's pubs, clubs and concert venues keep going well into the small hours.

The Victorian, Georgian and Neoclassical architecture is striking -- William Brown Street, lined with nothing but museums, libraries and galleries, is unique in Britain. The [Albert Dock](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/liverpool/things_to_do/albert_dock), beautifully restored from its industrial past, now houses cafes, restaurants, [Tate Liverpool](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/liverpool/things_to_do/tate_liverpool) and [The Beatles Story](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/liverpool/things_to_do/the_beatles_story). Two cathedrals -- one Anglican, one Catholic -- face each other across Hope Street, connected by one of the best walks in the city. Over 3,000 acres of parkland soften the edges of what is otherwise a proudly urban city.
