Manchester powered the Industrial Revolution with its cotton mills and today it is one of the most vibrant cities in England. The rebuilding after the 1996 IRA bomb and the 2002 Commonwealth Games transformed the city centre, and the investment has not stopped since. The galleries and museums are outstanding -- the Museum of Science and Industry, Manchester Art Gallery, the Lowry and the Imperial War Museum North are all worth a visit.

The city centre is compact and walkable, concentrated around Piccadilly Gardens. The Northern Quarter is the bohemian heart of the city, full of vintage shops, independent bars and record stores. Manchester is also famous for the bands it produces -- from the Smiths and the Stone Roses to Oasis -- and new talent always finds a stage, whether in one of the many pubs or clubs the nightlife scene has to offer.

Chinatown, near Piccadilly Gardens, has charming eating houses serving excellent food. A few miles south of the centre, in Rusholme, fans of Indian food will find plenty of restaurants along the famous Curry Mile. The Barton Arcade on Deansgate is a beautifully restored Victorian glass arcade, and Heaton Park to the north offers 600 acres of green space.
