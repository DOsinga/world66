---
title: 'Nightlife and Entertainment'
type: section
---

Since its early 1990s heyday, Manchester's nightlife has held a reputation for being at the cutting edge of cool. The heady days of the Hacienda nightclub and groundbreaking music from the Happy Mondays and Stone Roses saw the city earn the nickname "Madchester." While the legendary Hacienda is now a luxury apartment block, Manchester continues to pride itself on being a city full of "24 Hour Party People."

**Nightclubs**

Manchester's clubbing scene encompasses everything from glamorous posing palaces playing dance floor classics to low-key venues playing underground sounds. The student corridor along Oxford Road features interesting DJ sets at clubs such as Font, while those looking to dance the night away make their way to the city's larger venues in the Printworks entertainment complex by Exchange Square.

**Bar Scene**

The bar scene is every bit as buzzing as the famous nightclubs. Those looking to mingle with Manchester's beautiful people should head for Deansgate Locks, while Castlefield nearby is a good bet for laid-back bars with pleasant outdoor areas along the canal. For live music, innovative DJs and funky independent bars, head for the Northern Quarter, where places such as Cord, Centro and Night and Day serve good food and drink alongside a diverse musical programme.

**Gay Village**

Manchester has one of the liveliest gay scenes in England, with the majority of clubs and bars centred around Canal Street. Glass-fronted venues line the canalside, attracting a young crowd.

**Family Entertainment**

The Printworks complex has a multi-screen cinema, and bowling, LaserQuest and paintballing are all good options for active types. The city's larger theatres, such as the Palace on Oxford Road, regularly show children's productions.
