---
title: "Shopping in Manchester"
type: section
---

**Manchester Shopping **is a paradise for fashionistas, with the city boasting everything from vast shopping centers to chic boutiques and sophisticated department stores. The compact city center is easily navigated on foot, and Manchester's many cafe bars are great places to recharge the batteries during a shopping spree. Manchester's reputation as a fashion-forward city is well deserved, but Manchester shopping isn't solely about clothes - it's possible to pick up everything from home furnishings and electronics to delicious food in this cosmopolitan city.

**Designer Manchester**

If money is no object, Manchester's shopping opportunities are unlimited. The city boasts not one but two branches of the ultra-fashionable department store Selfridge's, which houses designer labels in everything from clothes and accessories to kitchenware, and the Exchange Square branch sits right next door to luxury department store Harvey Nichols. Nearby, the Triangle shopping center is home to boutiques selling designer goodies from famous names and local designers alike.

**High Street Shopping in Manchester**

Fashion-conscious shoppers on a tight budget will be in their element in Manchester. The city boasts branches of all the UK's top high street fashion stores, many of which are located along Market Street and in the Arndale Shopping Center. First port of call should be Primark, on Market Street. This chain store offers bang-on-trend clothing for incredibly cheap prices, and Manchester's branch is among the largest in the country. Across the road from Primark is TK Maxx, an enormous warehouse-style store offering designer brands at slashed prices. The Arndale Center is home to a huge branch of fashion stores TopShop and TopMan, while King Street sees pricier chain stores such as Reis, Karen Millen and Ted Baker sit alongside cheaper chains such as New Look and Warehouse.

**Shopping in the Northern Quarter **

Trendy individuals looking for threads that really make an impact should head straight for the area of the city known as the Northern Quarter. This once run-down district that leads from Market Street to Piccadilly Train Station is now home to numerous independent stores, selling everything from vintage fashion to rare vinyl. Ladies looking for vintage fashion finds should head to Rags to Bitches, on Oldham Street, while Oi Polloi, just up the road, has cool and kooky new and vintage items for the guys. Oxfam Originals offers used clothes for both sexes, alongside books, records and films, and shoppers can be content in the knowledge that the money from their shopping spree will go straight to the Oxfam charity.
