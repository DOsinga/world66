title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**Northern Quarter**
Manchester’s bohemian heartland, this
once-neglected area of the city center is now the place to find vintage
clothing stores, off-beat independent bars and cafes, record stores and
a scattering of the city’s famous nightclubs. The Northern Quarter is
much loved by Manchester’s arty types, who like to hang out at
café-bars such as Cord, Centro and Socio Rehab. The Northern Quarter
covers the streets around Oldham Street, and although it remains shabby
and run-down in parts, this is THE place to come to hang out with
Manchester’s trendy young things. Note that Nothern Quartern nightlife
does not require the ‘smart clothes and shoes’ policy of some of the
city’s nightspots. Instead, the standard Manchester uniform of
expensive yet dishevelled jeans and sneakers for guys, and some kooky
yet fashionable getup for girls. To get the look, ladies should make a
bee-line for vintage store Rags to *itches, while guys should head to
Oi Polloi.

**Old Trafford**

No trip to Manchester would be complete
without a trip to Old Trafford, the home of legendary football (or
soccer, as the Americans would have it) team Manchester United.
Something of a Mecca for sports fans, the home to the world’s most
famous football team is easily reached by the Metrolink – the tram
system that runs from central Manchester to the city’s outskirts. The
stadium has it’s own Metro station, but be aware that if you want to
catch a game here, you’d be wise to buy tickets in advance or risk
paying through the nose to buy from unofficial sellers at the gate.

**Manchester Town Hall **

This imposing neo-gothic building, completed in
the late 1880s, is a prime example of an architectural style that can
be spotted across the city. The building sits imposingly on Albert
Square, a pleasant space dominated by a statue of Prince Albert –
consort of Queen Victoria. If you happen to be in Manchester during
November and December, don’t miss the wonderfully festive Chrsitmas
Market that takes place on the square, with sellers from across Europe
arriving to sell hot, steaming mugs of mulled wine, and continental
food, toys and artisan knick-knacks.

**Wheel of Manchester **

It’s
not quite as spectacular as the London Eye, but this mammoth ‘glass and
chrome ‘big wheel’ is a great way to get up in the air and see the city
from a bird’s perspective. Located in front of Selfridges department
store at Exhange Square, the wheel takes in great views of the city and
its surrounds as it rises 60 meters into the air.

**Granada
Studios Tour**

This fun tour is a great family destination, allowing
visitors to step onto the sets of television programmes made by
Manchester’s Granada Studios. The key attraction here is the chance to
visit the set of Coronation Street, the legendary soap opera that has
been much-loved by British television viewers since it first aired in
the 1960s. Visitors can take a walk down the cobbles of Coronation
Street itself, and have their photo taken in front of landmarks such as
the Rover’s Return – the soap opera’s famous pub.
