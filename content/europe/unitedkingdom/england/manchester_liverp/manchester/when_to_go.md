---
title: 'When to Go'
type: section
---

Manchester has mild summers but winters can be cold and grey. The wet, cloudy weather that persists for much of the year is the main drawback for visitors.

**Summer (June to August)** is the most pleasant time to visit, with the longest days and warmest temperatures. This is also when many outdoor festivals take place.

**December** sees a surge of visitors for the Christmas markets on Albert Square, with stalls from across Europe selling food, drink and gifts. It is a festive and atmospheric time but accommodation fills up quickly.

**January to February** is the quietest period, with cold weather keeping visitor numbers low. This can mean good deals on accommodation.

**Spring and autumn** offer mild weather and fewer crowds. The football season runs from August to May, so sports fans can plan around match schedules.
