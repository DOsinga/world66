---
title: "Eating Out in Preston"
type: section
---

A newcomer among the cities of England´s North West, Preston is slowly but surely establishing itself as a major player in the region´s dining scene. The city may lack the trendy reputation of surrounding cities such as Manchester, Leeds and Sheffield, but this young city has a vibrant feel and a variety of places to enjoy a bite to eat. There are increasing numbers of formal restaurants offering fine food and wines in sophisticated surroundings, as well as laid-back cafe bars and coffee shops, abundant pubs and bars, and traditional restaurants where hungry visitors can enjoy a hearty fried breakfast or a tasty plate of fish and chips. There are options to suit every taste and every pocket, and with an increasing number of international restaurants in the city there´s an opportunity to sample food from around the globe.

**Firm Favorites**

While it seems as if a new bar or restaurants opens every five minutes in fast-developing Preston, it may pay to head to a well-established restaurant if you´re looking for a quality dining experience. One of the best-loved places to eat in the city is the excellent Bello Italian Restaurant, which has been charming diners for over 30 years. Located in the Friargate area of the city, this restaurant uses quality locally-grown ingredients to create simple but delicious dishes. The restaurant also offers some good vegetarian options. Another chic option is Farington Lodge, a hotel in a Grade II listed building whose Garden Restaurant offers delicious contemporary dishes--making this a nice spot for a special occasion or a romantic meal.

**Something Different**

There´s always something new on the dining scene in Preston, and establishments are being increasingly inventive in their efforts to attract customers. For a dining experience with a real difference, try Bistro Live, at 7 Avenham Street that offers cabaret entertainment in addition to food and drink. A young, fashionable crowd heads here to enjoy dishes such as chicken breast stuffed with pancetta and asparagus. Bistro Live also offers a good wine list and cocktail menu.

**International Dining**

Preston´s multi-ethnic mix is reflected in the city´s dining scene, which offers scores of Indian, Chinese and Italian restaurants alongside Mexican, Spanish, Middle-Eastern and Southeast Asian choices. For late-night snacks, there are many places to pick up a kebab.
