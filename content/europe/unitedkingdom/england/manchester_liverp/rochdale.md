Rochdale's past is inextricably linked with the Industrial Revolution, but its origins go back much further. The town is famous as the birthplace of the modern cooperative movement -- the Rochdale Pioneers opened their store on Toad Lane in 1844, and the original building is now a museum. The imposing Victorian town hall, modelled on the great medieval cloth halls of Flanders, is one of the finest civic buildings in the north of England.
