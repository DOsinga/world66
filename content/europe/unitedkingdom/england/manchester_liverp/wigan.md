Wigan is a town in Greater Manchester that most people know from George Orwell's 1937 book "The Road to Wigan Pier," a searing account of working-class life in industrial England. The pier itself, such as it was, was a coal-loading jetty on the Leeds and Liverpool Canal, not the seaside boardwalk the name implies. The area around it has been redeveloped into a pleasant waterside heritage quarter, and while the original pier is long gone, the Wigan Pier Experience tells the story of the town's industrial past.

Rugby league is the heartbeat of the town. Wigan Warriors are one of the most successful clubs in the sport's history, and catching a match at the DW Stadium is the best way to experience the fierce local pride that defines the place. The town center has undergone significant regeneration, and the market hall is worth a browse.

Wigan is not a tourist destination in any conventional sense, but if you are interested in industrial heritage, working-class history, or rugby league, it has genuine character that rewards a visit.
