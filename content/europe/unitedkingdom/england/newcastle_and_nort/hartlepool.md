Hartlepool is a post-industrial port town on the Durham coast, about 30 kilometres south of Sunderland. It's not a place most visitors to northeast England seek out, but it has a genuinely interesting historic waterfront and makes a reasonable half-day stop if you're exploring the region.

The main draw is the Historic Quay, a reconstructed Georgian seafront with the HMS Trincomalee as its centrepiece — a 200-year-old Royal Navy frigate and one of the oldest warships still afloat in the world. The surrounding Maritime Experience does the usual costumed-guide thing, but the ship itself is worth the visit.

Seaton Carew, at the southern end of Hartlepool's long sandy bay, is a traditional English seaside resort — windswept, faded at the edges, but with a decent beach and a bracing North Sea atmosphere. In summer it draws families from across County Durham.

The town centre was heavily redeveloped in the 2000s and is now dominated by retail parks and chain stores. It isn't especially atmospheric, but the waterfront is well kept and the marina is busy with leisure boats in summer.
