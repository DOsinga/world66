This cluster of picturesque villages in the far north of Northumberland, only a few miles from the Scottish border, packs in a surprising number of rewarding stops. The Heatherslaw Light Railway is a narrow-gauge preserved steam line that runs along the River Till, and the adjacent Heatherslaw Corn Mill still grinds flour by water power. Ford Castle has a walled garden now used as a plant nursery.

The outstanding sight is Lady Waterford Hall in Ford village, whose walls are covered with large Pre-Raphaelite murals painted by the Marchioness of Waterford in the 1860s and 1870s. The biblical figures all have the faces of local villagers who sat as models — a vivid combination of high art and local portraiture.
