Newton Abbot is a market town in South Devon, inland from Torbay and sitting at the head of the Teign estuary. It is primarily a commercial and transport hub for the surrounding area — the railway junction was historically important, and the town still serves as a practical base for exploring Dartmoor, the Teign Valley, and the Torbay coast. The weekly market is a lively affair, and the town has decent shops and cafes. Dartmoor's open moorland begins just a short drive inland.
