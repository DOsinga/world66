Nottingham sits in the heart of the East Midlands, trading on its connection to Robin Hood even though the outlaw's historical links to the city are tenuous at best — he was likely from Yorkshire and did most of his roaming in Sherwood Forest to the north. Still, Nottingham Castle and the Tales of Robin Hood visitor centre make the most of the legend, and Sherwood Forest itself is an easy day trip.

Beyond the folklore, Nottingham is a lively university city with a strong pub and restaurant scene. The Lace Market, a neighbourhood of Victorian warehouses that once drove the city's textile industry, is now the most interesting part of the centre — all independent shops, galleries, and restaurants. The castle, rebuilt as a Ducal mansion in the 17th century, sits on a sandstone cliff riddled with medieval caves that you can visit on guided tours.

Nottingham also makes an ideal base for exploring the Derbyshire Peak District, which begins about 30 minutes to the northwest, and the market towns of the Trent Valley.
