Plymouth's position on the Devon-Cornwall border makes it a natural base for day trips in both directions.

**Cremyll and Cawsand**

Choose a fine day for this and do it in either direction. Take a boat from Plymouth to Cawsand — a scenic trip past Drake's Island and along the southeast Cornish coast past Mount Edgcumbe Park to arrive at the twin villages of Cawsand and Kingsand. The beaches are not large (for that you need Whitsand Bay further round the coast), but they are interesting, with a lime kiln and good rock scrambling at low tide.

From Cawsand take a bus to the village of Cremyll, then take the Cremyll foot ferry across the Tamar to Stonehouse in Plymouth. You could get a bus all the way from Cawsand to Plymouth via the Torpoint car ferry, but the Cremyll crossing is far more scenic. Just about every Plymouth resident has done this trip, but a surprising number of visitors miss it.
