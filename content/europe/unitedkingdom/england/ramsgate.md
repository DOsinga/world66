Ramsgate is a Kentish seaside town with more architectural substance than most English coastal resorts. The Royal Harbour — the only royal harbour in the country — is lined with Regency and Georgian townhouses climbing the cliffs on both sides, and the inner marina still has working fishing boats alongside the yachts.

The town's finest feature is its network of tunnels. The Ramsgate Tunnels, built as air-raid shelters in the Second World War, could hold 60,000 people and are open for guided tours. Above ground, Augustus Pugin — the architect who designed the interiors of the Houses of Parliament — lived and worked in Ramsgate, and his house, The Grange, and the church he built next door are open to visitors.

The main sands beach is good, and the chalk cliffs and rock pools along the coast towards Broadstairs make for pleasant walking. Ramsgate is increasingly popular with day-trippers from London, about 75 minutes by high-speed train from St Pancras.
