The view from Richmond Hill looking south over the Thames is the only view in England protected by an Act of Parliament (the Richmond, Ham and Petersham Open Spaces Act, 1902). The river curves through meadows and woodland below, unchanged in its essentials since the eighteenth century when Turner, Reynolds, and other painters came here to capture it. The Terrace Gardens below the crest of the hill are a good place to sit and take it in.
