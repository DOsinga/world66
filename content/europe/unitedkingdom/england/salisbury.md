Salisbury is a cathedral city in Wiltshire, about 145 km southwest of London. It's best known as the nearest city to Stonehenge, but the city itself has considerable merit and is often underestimated by visitors who treat it as a quick transit stop.

Salisbury Cathedral is one of England's finest, with the tallest spire in the country at 123 metres and one of the four surviving copies of the Magna Carta on display inside. The close surrounding it — a large, tranquil green framed by medieval buildings — is one of the most peaceful spaces in any English city. The old market town centre has independent shops, good restaurants, and a twice-weekly market that's been running for over 800 years.

Stonehenge is 14 km north and easily reached by bus or taxi. Don't overlook Avebury, about 40 km north, which is a larger and less visited stone circle where you can actually walk among the stones freely. Old Sarum, just outside the city, is an Iron Age hill fort with the ruins of the original medieval cathedral before it was moved to the current riverside site.
