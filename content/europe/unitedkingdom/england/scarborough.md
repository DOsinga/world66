Scarborough is a classic seaside resort on the east coast of Yorkshire. The old town is built around a massive headland that juts out into the North Sea, dividing the town into a north side and a south side.

The North Bay is relatively unspoiled and popular with surfers. A good walk is the Marine Drive around the headland and out along to Scalby Mills, where you can visit the Sea Life Centre. The South Bay is more commercialised — amusement arcades, ice cream parlours, and fish and chips keep most people happy. At the top of the headland is Scarborough Castle, a stiff climb but well worth it for the views over the town and sea.

Scarborough has been a resort since the 17th century, when its spa waters first drew visitors. The town's grand Victorian hotels along the Esplanade are a reminder of its heyday, and the Stephen Joseph Theatre — where Alan Ayckbourn premieres his plays — gives it a cultural life that most seaside towns lack.
