Sherborne is an exceptionally pretty market town in Dorset, built almost entirely of the warm golden limestone that defines the area. At its heart is Sherborne Abbey, a magnificent building that traces its origins to an Anglo-Saxon monastery of the 8th century and still serves as a working parish church. Just outside the town stand two castles: the ruins of the 12th-century Old Castle, and the New Castle built by Sir Walter Raleigh in the 1590s, both set in parkland above a lake.

The town is small enough to walk in an afternoon — the medieval almshouses, the Abbey, and the compact high street are all within easy reach of each other. It has the feel of a place that has always known it's attractive without becoming too self-conscious about it. The private Sherborne School occupies much of the old monastic precinct and adds a collegiate quality to the town centre.
