Skegness is the classic bucket-and-spade English seaside resort — brash, cheerful, and entirely unapologetic about it. The town has been drawing holidaymakers from the East Midlands since the railway arrived in 1873, and the original Butlin's holiday camp opened here in 1936. The famous "Jolly Fisherman" poster declaring "Skegness is SO bracing" was commissioned in 1908 and the town has been living up to the slogan ever since.

The beach is vast — miles of flat sand stretching north towards the Gibraltar Point nature reserve, which is well worth a visit for birdwatching and walking among the dunes and salt marshes. The seafront has the usual amusements, arcades, and fish-and-chip shops. Skegness makes no pretence of being anything other than what it is, and that honesty is part of its charm.
