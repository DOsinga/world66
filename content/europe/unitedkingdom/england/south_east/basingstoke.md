Basingstoke sits in the centre of Hampshire, about 50 minutes by train from London Waterloo. It is not a destination most travellers seek out, but it serves as a practical base for exploring the surrounding Hampshire countryside.

The town centre was largely rebuilt after the 1960s and is dominated by the Festival Place shopping centre. The historic character of old Basingstoke was mostly lost to post-war redevelopment, which makes it an underwhelming stop compared to nearby towns like Winchester or Salisbury. The Willis Museum on the Market Place does a decent job of telling the town's story from Roman times onward.

The surrounding area is more rewarding. The Vyne, a National Trust property a few miles north, is a handsome Tudor mansion with a chapel containing rare Renaissance glass. The North Downs Way and various country walks are easily accessible from town.
