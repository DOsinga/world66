Crawley is a new town in West Sussex, designated in 1947 and built largely in the postwar decades to relieve housing pressure on London. Its main claim to fame is its proximity to Gatwick Airport, which sits just north of the town and dominates the local economy. If you are flying into or out of Gatwick, Crawley offers cheaper accommodation and better restaurants than the airport hotels.

The town is organized into neighborhoods, each with its own character, though the overall feel is suburban and functional rather than charming. The High Street in the old town center retains a few older buildings that predate the new town development, and the County Mall shopping center keeps things lively. Tilgate Park, a large country park on the southern edge of town with a lake, nature center, and walled garden, is Crawley's most pleasant green space and a good place to stretch your legs after a long flight.
