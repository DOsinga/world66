title: "Dover Castle"
type: poi
category: "Sight"

---

One of the most impressive fortresses in England, towering above the town and port. The medieval keep was built by Henry II in the 1180s, but the site has been fortified since the Iron Age. During the Second World War, the tunnels beneath the castle served as the headquarters for the Dunkirk evacuation, and they are open to visitors today. Within the castle grounds you will also find a Roman lighthouse (pharos) and the Saxon church of St Mary in Castro.
