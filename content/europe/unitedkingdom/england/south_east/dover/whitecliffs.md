The chalk cliffs stretch along the coast on both sides of Dover and have been a symbol of England for centuries. The National Trust manages a clifftop walk east of the port, starting from the visitor centre and running along the cliff edge with views across the Channel. On a clear day you can see France. Shakespeare referenced these cliffs in King Lear, and they remain one of the most recognisable landscapes in the country.
