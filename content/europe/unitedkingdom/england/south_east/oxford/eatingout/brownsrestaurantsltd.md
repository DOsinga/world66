Browns is a veritable institution. Back in the early 80s it was simply the 'in' place. Queues would form every weekend in order to get in (no reservations) and students would happily stand in the cold for an hour or more. It was then one of the places with the best atmosphere and some good food, like their steak pies. Browns today remains unchanged with regard to its decor (the only difference being the bistro chairs, now upholstered!), but its menu has changed a great deal. You will now find a good selection of European dishes and English ones, generous portions and still a great atmosphere. Service is on the whole good, but they have a large staff turnover. Though not packed as in its heydays, it's still quite busy and one of the most enjoyable places to eat in central Oxford.
