---
title: 'Sights'
type: section
---

Most of Oxford's sights are connected to the university colleges, but the city also has some outstanding museums and landmarks in their own right.

**Radcliffe Camera** is the iconic domed building that appears on every Oxford postcard. Built in 1749 as a science library, it now serves as a reading room for the Bodleian Library. You cannot go inside without a reader's card, but the exterior alone is worth the walk.

**Bodleian Library** is one of the oldest libraries in Europe, with buildings spanning several centuries. The medieval Duke Humfrey's Library and the Jacobean quadrangle are open for guided tours.

**Bridge of Sighs** at Hertford College is a covered bridge spanning New College Lane, loosely inspired by the Ponte dei Sospiri in Venice.

**Christ Church Cathedral** is both the college chapel of Christ Church and the cathedral for the Diocese of Oxford, an unusual double role. The Great Hall above it inspired the Harry Potter films.

**Carfax Tower** is all that remains of the medieval St Martin's Church. Climb the 99 steps for panoramic views across the city's spires and rooftops.

**Ashmolean Museum** on Beaumont Street is the oldest public museum in Britain, with collections ranging from Egyptian mummies to Pre-Raphaelite paintings. Free admission.

**Pitt Rivers Museum** is a delightfully cluttered anthropological collection housed behind the Natural History Museum on Parks Road. Shrunken heads, totem poles and masks fill the dim Victorian cases. Free admission.

**University of Oxford Botanic Garden** on Rose Lane is the oldest botanic garden in Britain, with over 7,000 plant species spread across glasshouses and outdoor beds.
