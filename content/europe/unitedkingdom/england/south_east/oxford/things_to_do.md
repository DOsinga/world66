---
title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

**Punting on the Cherwell** is the quintessential Oxford activity. A punt is a flat-bottomed boat propelled by a long pole, and the pace is deliberately slow. You can hire punts at Magdalen Bridge or the Cherwell Boathouse and drift along the river past college gardens and overhanging willows. If you would rather not do the work yourself, chauffeured punts are available too.

**Tour the colleges.** Most of the central colleges open to visitors outside exam periods, usually for a small fee. Christ Church is the grandest and most famous. Magdalen is the most beautiful, with its deer park, riverside walks and Great Tower. New College has a stunning medieval cloister. Each has its own character and you could spend days exploring them.

**Watch cricket in the University Parks.** Oxford University Cricket Club plays on one of the prettiest grounds in England. On a summer afternoon, few things are more pleasantly English than sitting on the grass watching a match.

**Visit the Sheldonian Theatre.** Designed by Christopher Wren in the 1660s, this is where Oxford's degree ceremonies take place. You can climb to the cupola for fine views across the city's rooftops and spires.

**Explore the covered market.** The market has been trading in the city centre since 1774 and is packed with butchers, bakers, cheese shops and cafes. The Pie Minister stall does excellent savoury pies.

**Walk to Port Meadow.** This vast common just north of the city centre has been unenclosed since before the Domesday Book. Cattle and horses graze freely, and the views back towards the spires of Oxford are wonderful.
