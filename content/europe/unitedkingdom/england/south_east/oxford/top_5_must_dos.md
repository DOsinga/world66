---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Oxford"
type: section
---

**1. Admire the Dreaming Spires**

Oxford may be the world's most famous seat of learning, and the phrase "dreaming spires," coined by poet Matthew Arnold, perfectly captures the skyline. Wander through the city centre and you will pass handsome college buildings at every turn, with towers and pinnacles forming a constantly shifting backdrop. Climb Carfax Tower or the tower of the University Church of St Mary the Virgin for the best panoramic views. The university's buildings span some 800 years, and the layering of architectural styles is endlessly rewarding.

**2. Take a Boat Trip on the River**

Few things are more pleasant on a warm summer day than drifting along the River Cherwell in a punt, picnic and bottle of wine in hand. You can punt yourself from Magdalen Bridge or the Cherwell Boathouse, or pay someone else to do the hard work while you sit back and watch the college gardens and overhanging willows drift by.

**3. Enjoy a Traditional Pub Meal**

Oxford's historic centre is packed with old pubs and inns, many dating back centuries. With creaking wooden floors, low beams, slanting stairwells and cosy fireside snugs, these are wonderful places to enjoy a pint of real ale and a plate of bangers and mash. In summer, the beer gardens are ideal for whiling away an afternoon.

**4. Visit the Museums**

The Ashmolean is a world-class museum with collections spanning ancient Egypt to modern art, and it is free. The Pitt Rivers Museum behind the Natural History Museum is even more extraordinary: a dimly lit Victorian hall crammed with anthropological treasures from shrunken heads to totem poles. Both are unmissable.

**5. Explore the Covered Market**

The covered market has been at the heart of Oxford since 1774. Browse the independent butchers, cheese shops, florists and cafes, then grab a pie from Pie Minister or a cake from one of the bakeries. It is Oxford at its most authentic and unpretentious.
