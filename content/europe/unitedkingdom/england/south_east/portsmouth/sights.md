---
title: 'Sights'
type: section
---

**Portsmouth Historic Dockyard** is the centrepiece of any visit. A single ticket gives access to HMS Victory, the Mary Rose Museum and HMS Warrior 1860, plus the Royal Naval Museum and Action Stations, an interactive exhibition on the modern navy. You could easily spend a full day here. The dockyard is open daily except over Christmas.

**Spinnaker Tower** at Gunwharf Quays rises 170 metres above the harbour. The three viewing decks include a glass floor, and on clear days the views stretch across the Solent to the Isle of Wight and along the coast in both directions.

**Southsea Castle** was built by Henry VIII in 1544 as part of his chain of coastal defences. It was from here that Henry watched the Mary Rose sink. The castle is open to visitors in summer with small displays on its history.

**D-Day Museum** on the Southsea seafront tells the story of the Normandy landings. Its centrepiece is the Overlord Embroidery, a modern equivalent of the Bayeux Tapestry depicting the D-Day operation.

**Charles Dickens Birthplace** at 393 Old Commercial Road is the modest terraced house where the great novelist was born in 1812. The rooms are furnished in period style.

**Portchester Castle** is a short drive east and worth the detour. The Romans built a naval base here in the third century, and the walls that remain are the most complete Roman fortifications in northern Europe. A Norman keep and medieval palace were built inside the Roman walls, giving you 2,000 years of history in one site.
