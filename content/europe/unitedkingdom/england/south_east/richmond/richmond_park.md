At 2,500 acres, Richmond Park is the largest of London's royal parks and one of the great natural spaces in the capital. Herds of red and fallow deer have roamed here since the 1630s, when Charles I enclosed the land as a hunting ground. The park is entirely free to enter and large enough that the city disappears within a few minutes' walk. The Pen Ponds at the centre are a popular stopping point, and Isabella Plantation — a woodland garden of rhododendrons and azaleas — is spectacular in late spring.
