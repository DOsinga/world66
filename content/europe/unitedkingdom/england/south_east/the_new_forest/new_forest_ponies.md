The free-roaming ponies, cattle, donkeys, and pigs of the New Forest are not ornaments — they are working animals belonging to local commoners who have held grazing rights here for centuries. The animals move freely across roads, villages, and car parks. Feeding them is illegal and causes real harm (they congregate near roads and become aggressive toward food). The best thing to do is watch from a distance. The open heathland around Burley and Acres Down is particularly good for encounters, especially early morning.
