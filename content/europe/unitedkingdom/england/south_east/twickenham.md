Twickenham is a prosperous riverside town in southwest London, best known as the home of English rugby. Twickenham Stadium — "Twickers" — seats 82,000 people and hosts the England national team as well as the Premiership Rugby final and the occasional concert. On match days the town turns into a sea of white shirts and good-natured noise; on quiet days it's a pleasant Thames-side suburb with Georgian houses, a couple of good riverside pubs, and more history than it gets credit for.

The Thames is the defining feature of the town. The stretch between Twickenham and Richmond is one of the prettiest on the whole river: wooded banks, Ham House across the water, the odd rowing club. Eel Pie Island — an actual island in the middle of the Thames — was once a famous music venue where the Rolling Stones and the Who played in the early 1960s. It's private now, but worth knowing about.

Orleans House Gallery is one of the better free galleries in outer London, housed partly in a spectacular early 18th-century baroque pavilion. Nearby Marble Hill House is a perfect Palladian villa with grounds that run down to the Thames. Both are easy to combine in an afternoon walk. Ham House, just across the river (a short ferry ride or longer walk), is a remarkable Stuart mansion with one of the most intact 17th-century interiors in England.

Twickenham works well as a base for Richmond, which is a 15-minute walk or a few minutes by train. The [Richmond](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/richmond) deer park, the town centre, and the Kew Gardens are all close by.
