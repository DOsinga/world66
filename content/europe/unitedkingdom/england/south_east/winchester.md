Winchester was once the capital of England, and it carries that history lightly. The city is centred on its magnificent cathedral, one of the longest in Europe, where Jane Austen is buried and medieval wall paintings still glow in the side chapels. The Great Hall is all that remains of Winchester Castle, and it houses a round table that has been hanging on the wall since at least the fourteenth century, painted with the names of King Arthur's knights.

The city sits in the chalk valley of the River Itchen, surrounded by water meadows that Keats walked through when he wrote "To Autumn." The high street climbs from the river to a statue of King Alfred, and the streets either side are full of independent shops, good pubs and restaurants. Winchester College, founded in 1382, is one of the oldest schools in England and its medieval buildings are open for tours.

Winchester makes an easy day trip from [London](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/london) or [Southampton](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/south_east/southampton), about an hour by train from Waterloo.
