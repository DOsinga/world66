Stafford is the county town of Staffordshire, a modest English market town that most travellers pass through rather than linger in — though those who stop find a place with genuine historic depth. The medieval street pattern survives largely intact, and the timber-framed Ancient High House on Greengate Street is one of the largest timber-framed town houses in England, dating from 1595.

Stafford Castle, on a ridge to the west of town, is a Norman motte with a later stone keep. What stands today is partly a nineteenth-century reconstruction, but the site is atmospheric and the views across the town and surrounding countryside are good. The castle grounds are free to enter; there is a small visitor centre on-site.

The town sits midway between Birmingham and the Potteries, and is well connected by rail. It makes a reasonable overnight stop, with Cannock Chase — an area of outstanding natural beauty just to the south — worth a half-day if you have time. [Lichfield](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/lichfield), with its spectacular three-spired cathedral, is twenty miles to the southeast.
