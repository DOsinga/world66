Staffordshire is a landlocked county in the West Midlands of England, a varied county that stretches from the industrial southern edge of the Black Country up to the southern reaches of the Peak District in the north. The county contains Stoke-on-Trent, the historic centre of the British pottery industry; Lichfield, a small cathedral city with a fine Gothic cathedral; and the Cannock Chase Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, a large area of heathland and forest south of Stafford.

The Staffordshire Moorlands in the northeast offer good walking country, with access to the Churnet Valley and some of the less-visited parts of the Peak District. Alton Towers, one of Britain's largest theme parks, is in rural east Staffordshire. Stafford itself, the county town, has a pleasant old centre with the remains of a Norman castle. The county has a genuine agricultural character in its rural areas, and the brewing and pottery traditions are deeply embedded in local culture.
