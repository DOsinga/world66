Stourbridge is a town in the West Midlands, historically significant as the world centre of the glass-making industry. The area around Stourbridge produced fine crystal and glassware for centuries, and the tradition continues today — the Ruskin Glass Centre in nearby Wollaston is a working hub for glass artists, and the Red House Glass Cone in Wordsley is a rare surviving example of an 18th-century glassmaking furnace and a scheduled monument. The town itself is an ordinary Black Country urban area, but the glass heritage makes it worth a visit for those interested in industrial history.
