---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in England"
type: section
---

There are different activities that should go on any **England Must
Do's** list. England offers a variety of fun for the whole family
since there are theme parks, beaches and famous sites to visit.
**Legoland Theme Park Windsor**

This park is packed with fun for the whole family. Children and their
families can build robots with Lego bricks, watch live pirate shows and
even battle robots with their laser guns. The park has activities for
children to build replicas of landmarks from the United States and
Europe in Miniland. There are around 40 million pieces of Lego bricks
that help kids recreate these notable landmarks.

The park is located on Winkfield Road and can be reached taking bus
number 77.
**Visiting Stonehenge**

Stonehenge is a rare architectural feat that has been around since
2500 B.C. This strange stone structure has drawn the attention of many
people all over the world. Scientists, engineers and architects are
baffled because they do not know the means of its construction. Some
people think it had religious and even magical purposes.

Stonehenge consists of stones that have been placed in a circular
formation. It has been ranked among one of the modern marvels of the
world. Stonehenge can be reached via Road A303 and Road A344.
**Visiting Big Ben**

Big Ben is one of the most famous clock towers in the world. It was
designed by Charles Barry and it has been functioning since May 1859.
Visitors are only allowed to look at the outside of the tower because
they are not allowed inside the tower. Residents of England are the only
ones able to go inside the tower on arranged tours.

Big Ben can be reached by train. There are three London Underground
lines that can take tourists to this site--District, Jubilee and Circle
Lines.

**Imperial War Museum**

One of the best things to add to any England must-do list is visiting
the Imperial War Museum in London. The museum has an exhibition that
shows places and artifacts that were important for the war effort during
WWII. The bunker from which Churchill directed the war effort is shown.

The museum is found on King Charles Street, London SW1A 2AQ.
**River Thames**

This river is one of the most popular rivers of the world. Visiting
the River Thames cannot be left out from any England's must-do list.
People sail the river on ships and cruises every year.
