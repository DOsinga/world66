England is a country full of history, folklore, ancient monuments and beautiful landscape. It's also home to royalty, phenomenal sights and their world famous fish and chips. Welcome to the **England Travel Guide**, where you'll learn all about what keeps visitors coming back year after year to this extraordinary place.

**When to Go**

If you're planning a trip to England, you'll want to know as much as you can about its weather patterns, and high tourism times of year. If you don't mind the rain and somewhat cold weather, you can visit England at any time of year. Weather is hard to predict in this country. Many have tried and don't do so very accurately. Just be prepared to experience damp weather throughout majority of the year and pack accordingly for it. If you'd rather travel to England when it's a bit drier and warmer, July and August would be the best months to visit. These months are also when the majority of other tourists make their way 'across the pond,' so be prepared to feel the hustle and bustle of the crowds.

**What to See**

England has many sights to see and other events going on year round, but one stop any tourist must make is to the city of London. There are a large number of historical monuments and attractions here, as well as unique museums. The Tower of London's bloody history is fascinating to some, while the Royal Palace is more interesting to others. There are endless possibilities of things to see and do in London, and quite honestly, you probably won't get to all of them during your first trip there. Stonehenge is another location all tourists should visit. The towering magnificence of Stonehenge will make your jaw drop in awe wondering just how people maneuvered these giant rocks into the formation they still hold today.

**Beaches**

When you think of England, you don't necessarily think of visiting the coastline because naturally the waters are colder. If you'd like to take the plunge though and say you swam in English waters, put on a wetsuit or make sure you don't stay in the water very long. You can also just stroll some of the beautiful beaches and take in the magnificent views. The beaches at both Cornwall and Devon are excellent undeveloped areas, and could very well be compared to some beaches in either California or Australia. Again, the only difference is the water is much colder in England.
