Weymouth is a seaside town on the Dorset coast, with one of the finest sandy beaches in the south of England — a long, sheltered arc of golden sand facing the sheltered waters of Weymouth Bay. The town has been welcoming visitors since King George III began bathing here in 1789, and a painted statue of the monarch still stands on the seafront.

The Jurassic Coast, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, runs along the Dorset shore to east and west. From Weymouth you can walk sections of the South West Coast Path toward the extraordinary natural arch of Durdle Door and the shingle tombolo of Chesil Beach, which stretches 18 miles westward toward Bridport. The Isle of Portland, connected to the mainland by Chesil Beach, juts out into the Channel and has a working lighthouse, dramatic cliff scenery, and the Portland Bill headland with its views across to France on a clear day.

The town itself is pleasant without being remarkable. The old harbour area has fishing boats, seafood restaurants, and traditional pubs, and the Georgian terraces along the esplanade give it a more dignified air than most English seaside resorts. In summer it is busy and cheerful; in winter it is quieter and the light on the coast is extraordinary.
