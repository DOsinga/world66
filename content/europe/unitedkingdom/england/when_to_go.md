---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

England doesn't have a bad season to visit, but the experience shifts considerably depending on when you come. Summer brings long days and packed attractions; autumn is quieter with better light for countryside walks; spring offers blooming gardens and manageable crowds. Pack layers regardless — the weather can turn at any time of year.

**Spring (April–May)**

Gardens are at their best and the days are lengthening. It's less crowded than summer, prices are lower, and the countryside is genuinely lovely. Easter weekend is busy around popular sites.

**Summer (June–August)**

Long days and the best chance of sunshine, but prices peak and London especially fills up with tourists. Book accommodation and popular attractions well ahead. The Glastonbury Festival in June and Wimbledon in July are major draws.

**Autumn (September–October)**

Often the best time to visit. Crowds thin after the school holidays, prices drop, and the light on the moors and dales is beautiful. September still offers reasonable weather.

**Winter (November–March)**

Cold, wet, and dark — but Christmas markets, cosy pubs, and uncrowded museums have their appeal. London at Christmas is genuinely atmospheric. Prices are lowest in January and February.
