Wolverhampton is a city of about 260,000 in the West Midlands, historically at the heart of England's industrial Black Country. It grew as an iron and steel manufacturing centre in the 18th and 19th centuries, and the evidence of that industrial past is visible in the landscape, though manufacturing has declined sharply. The city has a significant South Asian population — particularly from the Punjab — which has shaped the food and cultural character of parts of the city.

The Wolverhampton Art Gallery has a surprisingly strong collection of contemporary art, including a good selection of Pop Art. The old market town centre has some interesting Victorian architecture. Molineux Stadium, home to Wolverhampton Wanderers FC (Wolves), dominates the northern edge of the city centre. The Black Country Living Museum in nearby Dudley, about 10 km south, is one of the best open-air industrial museums in Britain and well worth the trip.
