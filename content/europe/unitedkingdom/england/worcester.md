Worcester is a cathedral city on the River Severn in the West Midlands, with a history that includes one of the key battles of the English Civil War — the Battle of Worcester in 1651, where Cromwell decisively defeated Charles II. The cathedral, rising above the river, is genuinely beautiful and contains the tomb of King John. The city also gave the world Worcestershire sauce (Lea & Perrins have been making it here since 1837) and Edward Elgar, who was born nearby and is celebrated throughout the county.

Worcester Porcelain — the original Royal Worcester factory — has a museum attached that's worth an hour for anyone interested in English ceramics. The racecourse on the flood meadows beside the Severn hosts regular meetings and has a lovely setting. It's an unpretentious, functional English county city that rewards a half-day rather than a full visit.
