**United Kingdom, Getting There**

If you're traveling to the United Kingdom from North America,
you have a few options available to you:

**Plane**

The most popular way to get to
the UK is by plane. From the West Coast, the trip will take around 11
hours and it takes about 6 hours from the East Coast. The cost of a
plane ticket will therefore vary on your distance from the country as
well as the class you choose to sit in, but it should cost somewhere
between $450 and $800 each way.

**Plane + Train or Car**

While it may prove to be a slightly more expensive option than
flying straight to the United Kingdom, you can expand your trip to more
of a European tour if you have time to spare by first flying to
another European country and then making your way to the United Kingdom
by train or car. That's right, you can get to the island country from
Europe by taking a train or driving under the English Channel.

If you don't have much time to spare, fly straight to France and
take a day or two to explore Paris before moving on to the United
Kingdom. If you want to tour other countries first, eventually make
your way to Coquelles, in the north of France. Driving to Folkestone (in Kent) from Coquelles via the Channel Tunnel takes only about 30 to 40 minutes and for a small fare. You can also take one of the many daily trains that makes the trip in about as much time.

**Cruise**

If flying isn't for you, consider taking a cruise to United Kingdom
instead. Taking a cruise from the East Coast typically takes about a
week, but it can take a day or two less or a few weeks more, depending
on how many stops your cruise makes. This is typically the most
expensive way to get to the United Kingdom, since you'll be spending so
much time on the boat.
