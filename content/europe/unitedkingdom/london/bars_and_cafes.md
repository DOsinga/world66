---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

The London pub is one of the great institutions of city life — a wood-panelled, real-ale-serving, slightly tatty room where you can sit for hours without anyone bothering you. The best of them are centuries old, with low ceilings, uneven floors, and history soaked into the walls. Seek out the proper pubs over the chain bars; there's one on almost every street in older parts of the city.

Beyond pubs, London has a strong cocktail bar scene concentrated in Soho and Bermondsey, and the coffee culture has caught up with the best of the world's cities. Flat whites are universal; third-wave roasters like Monmouth Coffee have been operating here since before the term was invented.
