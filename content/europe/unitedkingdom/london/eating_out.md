---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

London's food scene has changed beyond recognition in the past twenty years. It's now one of the best cities in the world to eat in, with genuine depth across cuisines: South Indian in Tooting, Vietnamese in Hackney, West African in Peckham, Lebanese along the Edgware Road. The old clichés about British food are decades out of date.

Borough Market and the street food scene around it is a good starting point. For sit-down restaurants, the neighbourhoods of Soho, Clerkenwell, and Bermondsey are particularly dense with good options. Booking ahead is usually necessary for anywhere with a reputation; walk-ins work fine for the market stalls and casual spots.
