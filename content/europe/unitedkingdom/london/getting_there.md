---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

London is one of the best-connected cities in the world. Five airports serve it, two international train stations, and an extensive coach network.

**By air.** Heathrow (LHR) is the main hub, 45 minutes from central London on the Piccadilly line (the cheapest option at around £6) or 15 minutes on the Heathrow Express to Paddington (expensive but fast). Gatwick (LGW) is south of the city, linked by the Gatwick Express to Victoria (30 minutes) or Thameslink services to London Bridge and St Pancras. Stansted (STN) and Luton (LTN) serve low-cost carriers; both are over an hour from the centre — budget the time accordingly. London City (LCY) is the closest to the centre, with a short DLR ride to Canary Wharf and the City.

**By Eurostar.** Trains from Paris, Brussels, and Amsterdam arrive at St Pancras International, in the heart of London. Paris to London is 2h15; Amsterdam to London is 3h55. A genuinely civilised way to travel if you're coming from western Europe.

**By coach.** National Express and Megabus serve most major UK cities, arriving at Victoria Coach Station. Significantly cheaper than the train, but slower.

**By train.** London has twelve mainline termini. The principal ones: Paddington (for Wales and the West Country), Euston (Manchester, Birmingham, Glasgow, Edinburgh), King's Cross/St Pancras (Edinburgh, Leeds, Cambridge), Waterloo (the south-west), Victoria (Brighton, the south-east), Liverpool Street (East Anglia).
