---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

London's sights range from the genuinely ancient — Roman walls visible in the basement of a Barbican car park — to the relentlessly contemporary. The major museums are free, which means you can spend a morning at the British Museum and an afternoon at the V&A without it costing anything beyond the entry to your own curiosity. Don't try to do them all: better to spend three hours properly in one than to sprint through five.

The city's neighbourhoods are attractions in themselves. Wander through Bermondsey on a Saturday morning for the antiques market, take the bus over Tower Bridge to Borough Market, or head east to Columbia Road for the Sunday flower market. London rewards the aimless walker.
