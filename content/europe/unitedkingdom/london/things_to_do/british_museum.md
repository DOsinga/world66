One of the greatest museums in the world, and free. The collections span two million years of human history: the Rosetta Stone, the Elgin Marbles, the Lewis Chessmen, Egyptian mummies, Sutton Hoo treasures. Plan to spend at least half a day; you will not cover everything. The Great Court — Norman Foster's spectacular glass-roofed atrium around the circular Reading Room — is worth pausing in even if you're just passing through.

Great Russell Street, Bloomsbury. Open daily. Free admission.
