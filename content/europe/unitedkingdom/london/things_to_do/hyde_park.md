Hyde Park and the adjoining Kensington Gardens together form one of the great urban parks in the world — 630 acres of open space in the middle of the city. The Serpentine lake is good for rowing or swimming (there's a lido); Speakers' Corner, near Marble Arch, has been a site of open-air oratory and heckling since the nineteenth century. Kensington Gardens hold the Serpentine Gallery, one of London's best contemporary art spaces, and the Albert Memorial, which is magnificently over the top in a way that rewards close attention.

Open daily, 24 hours. Free.
