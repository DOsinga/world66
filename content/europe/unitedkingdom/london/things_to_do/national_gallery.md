Trafalgar Square's great anchor institution holds more than 2,300 paintings from the thirteenth to the nineteenth century — van Eyck, Rembrandt, Turner, Vermeer, van Gogh. The quality is consistently extraordinary, and it's free. Even a short visit to see a handful of specific paintings — the Arnolfini Portrait, Sunflowers, The Hay Wain — is well worthwhile. Arrive early on weekdays to avoid school groups.

Trafalgar Square, Westminster. Open daily. Free admission.
