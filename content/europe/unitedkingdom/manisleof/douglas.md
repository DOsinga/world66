Douglas is the capital of the Isle of Man and home to about a third of the island's population. It wraps around a wide bay on the east coast and is where most visitors arrive, either by ferry or from the airport a few miles to the south.

The town has a long Victorian promenade lined with guesthouses and hotels, and the horse-drawn trams that run along it in summer have been operating since 1876. The Manx Museum on Kingswood Grove is the best place to get an overview of the island's history, from its Norse settlers to the internment camps of World War II.

Douglas is the starting point for the Isle of Man TT course and the hub for the island's heritage railways -- the steam railway south to Port Erin and the electric railway north to Ramsey both depart from here.
