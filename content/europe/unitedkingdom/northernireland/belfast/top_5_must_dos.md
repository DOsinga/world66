---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Belfast"
type: section
---

**1. ****Belfast Castle **

Set on the slopes of Cave Hill,
Belfast Castle rests approximately 400 feet above sea level. Admission is free, and the
Castle is open daily except for major holidays. Built in 1870, this
majestic castle is rooted deep in Belfast's history, and has some of
the most amazing views of Belfast city. The restaurants here are top
notch, whether you are in the mood for a romantic dinner or a Sunday
afternoon carvery, the food is superb and you are having a meal in a
castle!

**2. ****Black Cab Tours
**

Almost every tourist agency in Belfast
offers a Black Cab Tour of Belfast and depending on your guide, any one
of them can be fun, educational, and unpredictable. The Black Cab Tours
usually cater to the political history and division over the conflicts
in Northern Ireland over the past 30 years. View the politically
motivated wall murals, sign your name on the Belfast Peace Wall, and
drive by the bombing sites of some of the worst fought battles in
Belfast. If you are unfamiliar with the reasoning and facts behind the
Northern Ireland troubles, this tour is a good tutorial. If you want an
unbiased tour, make sure to request a Protestant and Catholic tour and
your driver will give you both sides of the story.

**3. ****Crown Liquor Saloon
**

This ornate, Victorian pub is full
of character and history. If you want to have a unique Irish Pub
experience book one of their cozy snugs for a lovely Irish lunch
complete with a pint of Guinness. The Crown Saloon is a gorgeous bar
and is easily accessible from the city center. The food is great, and
the oysters are superb! The Crown Liquor Saloon was built in 1849 and
not much has changed here.

**4. ****St. George's Market
**

Every Friday since 1604 there has
been a market on the St. George's site! The present day St. George's
Market was built in 1890, and is one of Belfast's oldest attractions.
Home to some of the finest fresh produce, it has become one of the
city's most vibrant and colorful destinations. St. George's Market has
recently been voted one of the top 5 UK markets in 2006 by the National
Association of British Market Authorities. The Friday market opens at
6am and goes until 1pm. With over 240 market stalls, you will find a
diverse array of fresh fish, locally grown fruits and vegetables, and
old antiques.

**5. ****Ulster Folk and Transport Museum
**

This museum contains
the largest transport collection in Ireland, housing everything from
carriages to cars to fighter planes, and has a wonderful exhibition on
the Titanic. The museum takes you on a journey of the birth of the
Titanic in Belfast, to the launch of its fatefully voyage. See the
stages of construction and the history of this enigma.
