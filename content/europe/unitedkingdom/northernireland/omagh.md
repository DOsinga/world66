Omagh is the county town of Tyrone, set in the rolling green hills where the rivers Drumragh and Camowen meet to form the Strule. It is a quiet, pleasant market town that serves as a good base for exploring the Sperrins, the largest mountain range in Northern Ireland, whose heather-covered slopes and lonely valleys lie just to the north.

The town's main draw for visitors is the Ulster American Folk Park, one of the best open-air museums in Ireland. It tells the story of Irish emigration to America through reconstructed cottages, a full-size emigrant ship, and buildings from both sides of the Atlantic. The experience is immersive and surprisingly moving, and worth a half-day at least. Omagh itself has a compact centre with good pubs and a handful of decent places to eat, though it is the surrounding countryside that rewards most: fly-fishing on the Strule, walking in the Gortin Glen Forest Park, or driving through the Sperrins where the roads are empty and the views stretch for miles.
