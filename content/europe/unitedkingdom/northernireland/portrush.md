Portrush is a small seaside town on the north Antrim coast, perched on a rocky headland between two sweeping sandy beaches. It is the nearest town to the Giant's Causeway, just 10 km to the east along one of the most dramatic stretches of coastline in Ireland — basalt cliffs, ruined castles, and the famous rope bridge at Carrick-a-Rede.

The town itself is unpretentious and lively. The West Strand is a long, clean beach that faces the Atlantic and picks up good surf; the East Strand is more sheltered and popular with families. Royal Portrush Golf Club, which hosted The Open Championship in 2019, is one of the finest links courses in the world.

The harbour has a handful of good seafood restaurants and the town fills up on summer weekends with visitors from Belfast (90 minutes by car or train). Barry's Amusements, a cheerfully old-fashioned funfair on the seafront, has been running since 1926. The Causeway Coast Way, a long-distance walking path, passes right through town — you can walk east along the clifftops to the Giant's Causeway in about three hours.
