---
title: "Historic Sites"
type: section
---

Aberdeen's granite bones run deep. The city's oldest buildings cluster around the Castlegate — the civic heart since the 12th century — and along the medieval streets of Old Aberdeen near the university. Most historic sites are free to enter and can be covered on foot in an afternoon.

The Kirk of St Nicholas on Union Street is the city's principal church, while St Machar's Cathedral in Old Aberdeen is a fortified 15th-century cathedral with twin spires and an impressive heraldic ceiling. St Andrew's Cathedral on King Street holds the Seabury Memorial, commemorating Samuel Seabury, the first Bishop of the United States, who was consecrated here in 1784.

Provost Ross' House on Shiprow is a 400-year-old former home of the Lord Provost, now a National Trust Visitor Centre. In Old Aberdeen, King's College Chapel dates from the 16th century with a fine crown tower and original choir stalls. The 14th-century Brig o' Balgownie, spanning the gorge of the River Don, and the 16th-century Bridge of Dee with its seven arches are both worth seeking out for a walk along the water.
