Pitfour Observatory was built in 1845 by Admiral Sir George Ferguson, the fifth Laird of Pitfour, so he and his guests could watch horse racing without leaving the estate. The Laird had a four-and-a-half-mile course constructed, exactly 50 feet wide and perfectly level, earning it the nickname "Ascot of the North."

The local authority purchased and renovated the observatory in the 1990s. It is open daily from May to September (10:00--17:00) and free to enter. The octagonal tower offers panoramic views with information boards identifying local landmarks.

The observatory is signposted from both the A952 (between Mintlaw and Fraserburgh) and the A950 west of Mintlaw. If following signs from the A950, take the first sharp left about a mile past the second junction -- if you pass a graveyard, you have gone wrong. There is a large car park with picnic benches. From there it is a short walk to the tower, and several woodland trails lead to the Louden Wood Stone Circle and a chambered cairn.
