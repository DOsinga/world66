---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The city centre is best walked — almost everything you have come to see is within thirty minutes on foot from Princes Street. Just remember it is built on hills, and the Old Town is genuinely steep; comfortable shoes matter.

For longer hops, **Lothian Buses** runs an excellent network across the city; a single fare is a flat £2 (exact change only — drivers do not give change), or a day ticket is £5 and saves the bother. The **tram** runs in a single line from the airport via Murrayfield and Princes Street out to Newhaven, useful for the airport and Leith but not much else. **Taxis** are easy to flag in the centre; black cabs use the meter, private hires are usually a little cheaper. The city is reasonably bike-friendly, especially along the Water of Leith path and the disused railway lines that have been turned into cycle routes.
