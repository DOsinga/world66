Elie is a small coastal village on the East Neuk of Fife, one of a string of pretty fishing villages that line the Firth of Forth between St Andrews and Kirkcaldy. It has a long sandy beach that curves around a sheltered bay — one of the best beaches on Scotland's east coast, surprisingly uncrowded even in summer.

The village is handsome rather than spectacular: stone houses, a harbour, a couple of good pubs, and a cricket ground right on the shoreline. The Ship Inn is the main draw for visitors — a pub overlooking the beach where you can eat seafood and watch the tide come in. In summer they play cricket on the beach at low tide, which is about as pleasantly eccentric as Scotland gets.

The East Neuk villages are best explored together. [Crail](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/crail), Anstruther, and Pittenweem are all within a short drive, each with its own harbour and character. The Fife Coastal Path connects them for walkers. St Andrews is about 20 minutes north.
