---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Glencoe is beautiful in every season but genuinely difficult in several of them. Summer (June to August) gives the longest days and the most stable walking weather, though the midges can be brutal in sheltered spots. Spring and autumn offer quieter roads and dramatic light, and the hills are often clearer than in summer. Winter brings snow to the tops and occasional spectacular conditions, but many routes become serious mountaineering propositions requiring crampons and ice axes. Whatever time of year you visit, bring full waterproofs — the weather can change fast and the glen funnels wind off the surrounding ridges.
