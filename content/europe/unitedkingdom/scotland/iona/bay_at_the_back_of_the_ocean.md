---
title: "Bay at the Back of the Ocean"
type: poi
category: Sight
latitude: 56.3242
longitude: -6.4291
---

The island's finest beach sits on the Atlantic side, a twenty-minute walk west from the village across the island's low spine. The sand is bright white shell-sand and the water is a clear turquoise that looks Caribbean when the sun is out. On overcast days the full force of the Atlantic is more apparent, and the waves can be considerable. The Gaelic name — Camas Cuil an t-Saimh, "bay at the back of the ocean" — captures its exposed position perfectly.
