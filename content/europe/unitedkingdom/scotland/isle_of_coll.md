Coll is one of Scotland's Inner Hebrides — thirteen miles long, three miles wide, and home to about 150 people. It sits northwest of [Mull](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/mull), just north of neighbouring Tiree, and is rockier and wilder than its flatter sibling.

The draw here is solitude. Coll has white-sand beaches that you will often have entirely to yourself, moorland full of wading birds, and clear night skies that earned the island Dark Sky status. Walking, cycling, bird-watching, and simply staring at the Atlantic are the main activities. The island was once home to Mairi Hedderwick, author of the Katie Morag children's books — the fictional Isle of Struay is essentially Coll.

CalMac ferries run from [Oban](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/oban) via Mull, taking about three hours. There is no public transport on the island, so bring a bicycle or arrange a lift.
