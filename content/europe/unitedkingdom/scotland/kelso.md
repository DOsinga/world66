Kelso is one of the most appealing towns in the Scottish Borders, built at the confluence of the Tweed and Teviot rivers. Its large cobbled market square, flanked by Georgian and Victorian buildings, feels genuinely lived-in rather than preserved for tourists — there are independent shops here that have no interest in being photogenic.

The ruined Kelso Abbey, founded in the 12th century by David I, is the least visited of the four great Borders abbeys and entry is free. It's smaller than Melrose or Jedburgh but has a satisfying solidity to it, and the lack of crowds makes it worth the stop. Floors Castle, seat of the Duke of Roxburghe, sits just outside town and offers house tours and grounds.

For families, Harestanes visitor centre between Kelso and Jedburgh has good indoor and outdoor play areas alongside craft workshops — a useful half-day if you have children in tow.
