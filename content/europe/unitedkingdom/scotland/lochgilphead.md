Lochgilphead is the administrative centre of Argyll and Bute, sitting at the head of Loch Gilp on the Kintyre peninsula. It's a small, practical town that serves as a base and services hub for the surrounding area. The Crinan Canal, connecting Loch Fyne to the Sound of Jura, begins nearby at Ardrishaig and is well worth cycling or walking along — the route through the canal basin at Crinan is one of Scotland's quiet delights. The town itself has good local shops and a relaxed, working community feel, far from any tourist circuit.
