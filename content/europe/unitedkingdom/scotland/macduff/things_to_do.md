Plenty things to do around Macduff, several golf courses, ten pin bowling, visit the marine aquarium, fishing from the harbour aside the lighthouse, boat trips on the Puffin , nearby beaches or clifffs, bird-watching gannets. razor bills, gullimots. puffins etc: bingo, water-sports, such as surfing and canoeing in nearby [Banff](/northamerica/canada/alberta/banff). There is also the Banffshire Maritime Museum which id due to open in June 2006 in Duff Street Macduff, just a stones throw from the harbour pay it a visit it will be well worth it.

### [Puffin sea cruises](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/macduff/things_to_do/puffin_sea_cruises)

Take a 2 or 3 hour trip on the Puffin, berthed in Macduff harbour and sailing most days at 10.00am and 2.00pm. West to Findlater castle and back, east to Pennan and the gannet colony at Troup Head, if you're lucky you may see a dolphin or a minke whale. You are guaranteed to see many seabirds including gannets, puffins, and guillemots. A trip on the Puffin is worth every penny!

### [Macduff Marine Aquarium](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/macduff/things_to_do/macduff_marine_aqu)

Macduff Marine Aquarium packed with fish and creatures native to the Moray Firth. It has a large central tank where you can see the divers feeding the fish.

### [Storm Watch](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/macduff/things_to_do/storm_watch)

Becuase Macduff is situated on the North coast of Scotland now and again the town experiences rough weather from the north, these northern winds can produce some spectacular views of large waves crashing on the shore. 'Storm watch' is what some call it. Macduff has its own micro-climate which is usually warmer and sunnier than farther south but occassionally a storm occurs which produces these large waves which are enjoyed by the local surfers.

type: Other
