---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

St Andrews has three sandy beaches, all within walking distance of the town. **West Sands** is the long one — a mile and a half of flat golden sand running alongside the Old Course, backed by dunes, and the one you've seen in the opening slow-motion sequence of *Chariots of Fire*. It is exposed and often windy, which makes it a popular spot for kite surfers and kite buggyers at the far end. **East Sands**, next to the harbour on the other side of town, is smaller, more sheltered, and the easy family option. **Castle Sands**, tucked directly below the castle walls, is a small cove where, every year on the 1st of May, hundreds of St Andrews students run into the North Sea at dawn for the traditional May Dip — a tradition that is supposed to atone for walking on the cathedral gravestones at any point during the academic year.
