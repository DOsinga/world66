Barra is the southernmost island in the Outer Hebrides and ancestral homeland of the MacNeil clan — about eight miles by six miles of dramatic coastline and rolling moorland. The main settlement is Castlebay, where Kisimul Castle sits on a small island in the bay, one of the most evocative castle settings in all of Scotland.

The island's airport is famous in its own right: planes land on Traigh Mhor, a wide cockle-strand beach where the runway disappears at high tide. CalMac ferries run from Oban (about five hours) or you can fly from Glasgow. Once there, a single-track road circles the island in about an hour, passing white-sand beaches that would look at home in the Caribbean if not for the wind.
