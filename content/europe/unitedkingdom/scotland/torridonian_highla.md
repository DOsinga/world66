The mountains of Torridonian sandstone are possibly the only mainland mountains that can compete in beauty with the Cuillin of Skye. The peaks in the immediate area of Loch Torridon are large and provide walks along their numerous tops — Liathach, Beinn Alligin, and Beinn Eighe among them, with the splendid ridge of An Teallach further south.

North of Ullapool, the Torridonian landscape changes dramatically but loses nothing. The mountains are not as high and appear as isolated, sculpted peaks rising from a moorland of tiny lakes — Stac Pollaidh (overwalked because of its proximity to the road), Cul Mor, Cul Beag, Canisp, Suilven (almost an expedition in itself), and the much underrated Quinag. From most of these summits the view takes in hundreds of lochs and the other peaks in every direction.
