Turriff is a small market town in Aberdeenshire, sitting in gently rolling farmland about 35 miles north of Aberdeen. It's not a destination in itself, but it makes a decent base for exploring the castles and coastal towns of the northeast.

The town is best known among Scots for its agricultural show — one of the oldest and largest in Scotland, running since 1864. If you're passing through in late July or early August, it's worth timing a visit around it.

The surrounding area is rich in castles. [Delgatie Castle](https://delgatiecastle.com), just a couple of miles east of Turriff, is a fine example — the seat of the Hay family for six centuries, with remarkable 16th-century painted ceilings that survive in unusually good condition. To the south, [Fyvie](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/fyvie) Castle is one of the grandest in Scotland. The Aberdeenshire castle trail is genuinely rewarding here.

The coast is only 20 minutes away. [Banff](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/banff) and [Macduff](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/macduff) sit across the Deveron estuary from each other, with Duff House in Banff — a William Adam baroque palace now run by the National Galleries of Scotland — worth the detour on its own. [Gardenstown](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/gardenstown) and [Portsoy](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/portsoy) are small fishing villages with real character.
