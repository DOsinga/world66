Ullapool is a whitewashed fishing village on the shore of Loch Broom, the main settlement on Scotland's northwest coast and an excellent base for exploring some of the most dramatic scenery in the country. It has more choice for eating, drinking and sleeping than any other town for many miles around.

Ferries to Stornoway in the [Western Isles](/europe/unitedkingdom/scotland/western_isles__th) depart from the harbour, along with boat trips to the Summer Isles — a scattering of uninhabited islands in the mouth of the loch. There is not a road in the area that does not lead to some sort of scenic splendour: the coast road north to Lochinver, the loop through Achiltibuie, and the drive south to Poolewe and the Inverewe Garden are all outstanding.
