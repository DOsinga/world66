Suffolk is a quiet, rural county on England's east coast, bordered by Norfolk to the north and Essex to the south. It is flat, green, and often overlooked — which is part of its appeal. The landscape of big skies, medieval wool churches, and gentle river estuaries inspired Constable and Gainsborough, and has changed less than you might expect since they painted it.

The county town is [Ipswich](/europe/unitedkingdom/suffolk/ipswich), a working port with a revitalised waterfront. [Bury St Edmunds](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/eastern_england/bury_st_edmunds) has the ruins of a great medieval abbey and a thriving market square. Along the coast, Southwold and Aldeburgh are genteel seaside towns popular with Londoners — Aldeburgh hosts the famous music festival founded by Benjamin Britten. Woodbridge, on the River Deben, is a handsome small town near the Sutton Hoo burial site, where the great Anglo-Saxon ship burial was discovered in 1939.

Suffolk is cycling and walking country. The Suffolk Coast and Heaths Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty runs from Felixstowe to [Lowestoft](/europe/unitedkingdom/suffolk/lowestoft), taking in marshes, shingle beaches, and the strange pagoda-like structures of Orford Ness. Lowestoft holds the distinction of being the most easterly point in the United Kingdom.
