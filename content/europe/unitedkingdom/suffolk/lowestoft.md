**Lowestoft** is a coastal town in northeast Suffolk and the most easterly point in the United Kingdom. It sits at the very tip of East Anglia, where the North Sea meets the flatlands of the Broads — a position that has shaped its history as a fishing port, a naval base, and now a quiet seaside town.

The town is made up of several distinct areas, including Kirkley and Pakefield to the south and Carlton Colville to the west. Lowestoft has reasonable transport links: trains run to Norwich, Ipswich, and London via the Ipswich line, and the A12 connects it directly to London by road.

For families, Pleasurewood Hills is a long-established theme park on the southern edge of town.
