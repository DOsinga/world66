Din Lligwy is a remarkably compact cluster of ancient monuments in the northeast of Anglesey, within walking distance of each other across open farmland. Three quite different periods of history are represented within a short distance.

The oldest is a Neolithic burial chamber, dating back around 5,000 years, with a massive capstone weighing roughly 25 tonnes resting on smaller upright stones. It is one of the best-preserved megalithic tombs in Wales.

A few hundred metres away are the remains of a late Roman-period walled settlement — an enclosure containing the footings of both rectangular and circular stone buildings, thought to have been occupied by a local chieftain in the 4th century AD. The walls still stand to a reasonable height.

Nearby stands the roofless shell of a small medieval chapel, Capel Lligwy, which served a village that has long since disappeared from the landscape.

There are no facilities and no entrance charge. The sites are accessed by footpaths across fields.
