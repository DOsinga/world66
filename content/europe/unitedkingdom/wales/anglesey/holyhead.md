Holyhead sits at the far western tip of Anglesey, and most people pass through on their way to the Irish ferries. But there's reason to linger.

Behind the town rises Holyhead Mountain — modest in height, but the only real hill on the whole island. The coastal cliffs around it are spectacular. At South Stack, the RSPB runs a seabird centre where, during breeding season, you can watch guillemots, razorbills, and puffins nesting on the cliff faces. Choughs — once virtually extinct in England — have been spotted here too, part of a slow recolonisation from their Welsh strongholds.
