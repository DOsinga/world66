Bangor is one of the smallest cities in the United Kingdom, tucked between the mountains of Snowdonia and the Menai Strait in north Wales. A population of around 20,000 — about a third of them students at Bangor University — gives the place an energy that belies its size.

The city's origins stretch back to the 6th century, when a monastery was founded here by Saint Deiniol. The cathedral that eventually replaced it still stands in the city centre. Bangor's setting is its real asset: the Menai Strait separates the city from Anglesey, crossed by Thomas Telford's elegant suspension bridge, and the peaks of Snowdonia rise to the south. The Victorian pier, restored and stretching out into the strait, is a pleasant walk with views across to the island.

For most visitors, Bangor serves as a base for exploring the surrounding area — Snowdonia's walking trails, the castles of Caernarfon and Conwy, and the beaches of Anglesey are all within easy reach.
