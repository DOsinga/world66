Cardiff is served by it's own International Airport, situated in around 15 miles to the south west of the city centre - next to the small village of Rhoose in South Glamorgan. It is linked to the city centre via a bus link into the city's main bus and rail terminal, just off St Mary's Street.

There is also a very new (June 2005) rail link that has been opened, with a shuttle bus between the terminal and the railway station, which also serves the village of Rhoose, and links hourly into the city centre. This service leaves Platform 1 (with a final destination of Caerphilly). There is also a service into the small town of Bridgend, via Llantwit Major from Platform 2 (also hourly).

Cardiff Aiport has been rated as one of the best small regional airports in the UK as opposed to the relative monsters of Heathrow and Gatwick and the ever expanding Manchester - it has one terminal and one runway and transistion between check-in and airplane and likewise airplane to arrivals hall is very simple.

Ever since the introduction of low cost airlines to the airport, it has been burgeoning; there are regular connections to several regional airports around the UK, and Europe, with charter flights to more popular European sun destinations e.g. Malaga, Tenerife and also to the United States.
