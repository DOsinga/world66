**Cardiff Central - Caerdydd Canolog**

Cardiff has one main railway station which is served by intercity and interegional trains. Unsurprisingly this is called "Cardiff Central" or "Caerdydd Canolog" (to give it its Welsh title)

It has 7 Platforms -

Platforms 0,1 and 2 generally serve interegional trains which travel to such places as (although this list is not exhaustive) Manchester Piccadilly, Nottingham, Crewe, Portsmouth Harbour, Exeter, Gloucester, Penzance, Bristol Temple Meads and Newcastle upon Tyne.

Platforms 3 and 4 generally serve the intercity routes to Swansea, Bristol Parkway, Reading and London Paddington - although some of these trains leave from 0,1 and 2, and similarly some interegional trains can leave from here.

There is no Platform 5 - (!) Spooky

Platforms 6 and 7 are reserved for local service lines. There is a widely developed suburban rail network around Cardiff, on which it is quite fun to explore the city although it is very, very heavily used by commuters in the morning and the evening rush hours.

This network also extends up through the South Wales valleys - with most valleys having their own railway line on which you can explore each seperate valley - each with it's own character. You can travel furthest in the West to a lovely town called Maesteg in the Llynfi valley, through to Treherbert, Aberdare, Merthyr Tydfil and furthest east to the Rhymney Valley.

They also link to the South of the city to Barry and also to Penarth. This is also the platform for the railway link.

It takes approximately 2 1/2 hours to travel to [London](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/london) Paddington from Cardiff Central and 55 minutes to travel to [Swansea](/europe/unitedkingdom/wales/swansea). It takes about 50 minutes to travel to [Bristol](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/bristol) Temple Meads.

Longer journeys include [Birmingham](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/birmingham_and_wes/birmingham) (2 hours), [Nottingham](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/leicester_notting/nottingham) (3 1/2 hrs), Crewe (3 hrs) and [Manchester](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/manchester_liverp/manchester) (3hrs 20 mins).

Journeys on the Valley lines are slower but none takes more than 1 hour to reach it's final destination.

Cardiff Central is operated by *Arriva Trains Wales / Trenau Arriva Cymru.*

*Wessex Trains, Central Trains, First Great Western *and *Virgin Trains* all operate services from Cardiff Central

**Cardiff Queen Street**

This is a major hub, just off Queen Street with the majority of valley line services stopping here. It has just 3 platforms - 1 for up services (to the valleys), 2 for down services (to Cardiff Central and Barry) and the third for services to Cardiff Bay.

It is v busy here around commuter time - it is usually never very quiet though.

Cardiff Queen Street is operated by *Arriva Trains Wales / Trenau Arriva Cymru*
