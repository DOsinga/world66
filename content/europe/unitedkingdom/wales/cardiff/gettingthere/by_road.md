Cardiff is well served by road links to other parts of South Wales and the South West of England.

Connections to North Wales are less well served.

**From the West (Swansea and Bridgend)**

Cardiff is bordered to the north by the M4 motorway - this generally marks the border between where the valleys stop and the city begins. The M4 links West London continuously to just outside the west of Swansea.

One should exit the M4 at Junction 33 for a speedy dual carriageway (A4232) into Cardiff Bay and easy access to the south side of the city centre, also good to get to Penarth and Barry (Island). You can also leave the A4232 for the A48 at the *Culverhouse Cross / Croes Cwrlys* interchange and enter into Canton (on the A4161) and the west side of the city centre.

One can also leave the M4 at Junction 32 and join the A470 at the rather large *Tongwynlais* Interchange. This takes you Whitchurch and Gabalfa into the north of the city centre.

Also, the A48 links the Vale of Glamorgan and Bridgend to Cardiff, although there is the option of using the Motorway. This is a rather rural route, which can be occupied by farm traffic, although is a nice 'scenic' route

**From the East (Newport, Bristol and London)**

You can leave leave the M4 at Junction 29, onto the A48 (M), which magiks itself into the A48. You can leave the A48 and join the A4161 which will take you through Roath and into the east of the city centre, or you can stay on until the Gabalfa Interchange and then attempt to join the A470 - I say attempt because the Interchange is quite hair raising.

**From the North (Merthyr Tydfil, Brecon, Most of the Valleys and Birmingham (via M50))**

All routes are likely to take you on the A470, which leads you into the city centre - if you're not on the A470, then you're probably not headed for Cardiff. (Except the road from Caerphilly - A469 or the road from Llantrisant - A4119)

You are also likely to come through Newport - then you get on the M4 and follow the directions as if you are coming from the East.
