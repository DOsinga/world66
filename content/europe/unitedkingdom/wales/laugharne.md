Laugharne is a small, unhurried town on the Taf estuary in southwest Wales, and it would be a footnote in any guidebook were it not for one fact: Dylan Thomas lived here, wrote here, and drank here. He called it "the strangest town in Wales" and made it the model for Llareggub in *Under Milk Wood*. Coming to Laugharne is a literary pilgrimage, but it is also simply a pleasant place to spend a day.

The Dylan Thomas Boathouse, where the poet lived with his family from 1949 until his death in New York in 1953, is the main draw. His writing shed stands nearby, overlooking the same estuary he described so vividly. Brown's Hotel in the town centre was his preferred pub — it has been lovingly restored and is still very much in business. His grave is in the churchyard of St Martin's, marked by a plain white wooden cross.

Laugharne Castle, a substantial ruin above the estuary, provides the historical counterweight. Built in the twelfth century and remodelled by the Elizabethan adventurer Sir John Perrot, it also had a brief connection to Thomas, who wrote in a summerhouse within its grounds. The Town Hall opposite, with its improbable Italian campanile, is a curiosity worth a glance.

The town is a short drive from [Carmarthen](/europe/unitedkingdom/wales/carmarthen) and within easy reach of the [Pembrokeshire Coast](/europe/unitedkingdom/wales/pembrokeshire). A full visit — Boathouse, writing shed, castle, pub, grave — takes a comfortable half-day.
