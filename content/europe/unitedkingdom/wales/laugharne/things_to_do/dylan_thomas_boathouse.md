The modest clifftop house where Dylan Thomas lived with his family from 1949 until his death in 1953. The interior has been preserved as a small museum, with period furnishings, manuscripts, and recordings of Thomas reading his own work. His writing shed — a rickety wooden shack overlooking the estuary — stands nearby, exactly as he left it.
