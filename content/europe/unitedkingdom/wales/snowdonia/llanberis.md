Llanberis sits at the foot of Yr Wyddfa (Snowdon) and is the most popular starting point for the climb. The Llanberis Path is the longest but most gradual route to the summit, and for those who prefer not to walk, the Snowdon Mountain Railway chugs up from the village to the top.

The town itself has a quiet charm — a lakeside setting on Llyn Padarn, the National Slate Museum in the old Dinorwig quarry, and enough pubs and cafes to recover in after a day on the hills.
