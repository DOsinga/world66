Llanberis sits at the foot of Yr Wyddfa (Snowdon), Wales's highest peak, and is the most popular base for climbing it. The Llanberis Path is the longest but most gradual route to the summit — suited to anyone who is reasonably fit — and for those who prefer not to walk, the Snowdon Mountain Railway has been carrying visitors to the top since 1896.

The setting is genuinely beautiful: the village wraps around Llyn Padarn, a lake backed by the grey slate walls of Dinorwig quarry. The National Slate Museum, housed in the old quarry workshops, tells the story of the industry that once employed most of the valley and is free to enter.

After a day on the mountain, Llanberis has enough pubs and cafes to sort you out. Pete's Eats is the classic walkers' cafe — enormous portions, long opening hours, and a community notice board of local routes and conditions.
