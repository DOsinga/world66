Wrexham is the largest town in north Wales, a former coal and steel town that has reinvented itself as a busy commercial centre. The main draw for visitors is St Giles' Church, whose ornate Gothic tower is one of the Seven Wonders of Wales — a copy of it stands at Yale University in the United States, whose founder Elihu Yale is buried in the churchyard. Inside, the ceiling is decorated with flying musical angels and there are two notable early eagle lecterns.

The area around Wrexham is rich in industrial heritage. Bersham Ironworks, where John Wilkinson set up his foundry during the Industrial Revolution, and the Minera Lead Mines — worked from Roman times until the early 20th century — are both worth visiting. Erddig Hall, a National Trust property nearby, is remarkable for the unusually detailed records it preserved of its servants' lives alongside the family's.
