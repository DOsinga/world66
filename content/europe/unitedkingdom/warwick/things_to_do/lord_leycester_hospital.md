A remarkable group of half-timbered buildings clustered around the West Gate, dating mostly from the 14th and 15th centuries. Robert Dudley, Earl of Leicester and favourite of Queen Elizabeth I, converted them into a home for retired soldiers in 1571 — a function they still serve today. The courtyard, guildhall, and chapel are open to the public. It is one of the best-preserved examples of medieval domestic architecture in England and less visited than the castle, which means you can actually look at it in peace.
