**United Kingdom When to Go **

Weather in the United Kingdom can be somewhat
unpredictable and may determine when you want to go there. There are certainly days when it can start
out sunny and warm but then change to cold and wet in the blink of an eye. The weather there is no
different than some areas of the United States (like the Pacific Northwest)
with the rain falling during most any season.

**A Summer Trip**

If you’re planning a visit to the United Kingdom, keep in
mind that for all traveling purposes, it’s a destination you can go to year
round. The busiest times of year would
have to be from early spring throughout the end of summer. In terms of what
months that would fall under, it would start in the beginning of May and end as
late as mid-September. Weather is
usually warmest during these high peak times of the season, but it’s also when
most people are traveling or taking vacation time. Temperatures in summer will
fluctuate in the 60s, but there will be plenty of sunshine too. Christmas time
each year is also more hectic with larger crowds and kids on break from school.

**Less Crowds in Spring / Fall**

If the colder, wetter weather doesn’t bother you so much,
you could try visiting in either fall or spring (mid-September to October or
March to April). At these times of year, it’s far less crowded by tourists, plus
the airfare and accommodation prices tend to be lower. And you will still get fairly decent weather during
this time of year (it is actually the normal type of weather for the United
Kingdom) with temperatures ranging in the 50s and experiencing just light rain.
Another good bit of information to have is even if you do travel to the United
Kingdom during the off season, you won’t have to worry about anything being
closed down. All of the attractions,
hotels and shops stay open all year long for the tourist’s convenience.

**Plan in Advance**

When traveling to any international destination including
the United Kingdom, it’s always wise to book your airfare and accommodations as
far in advance as possible. This will
not only secure your travel dates and place to stay, but you could save money
as well due to booking early. If you’re
absolutely unable to do that, don’t worry – there are times when you can find
fabulous deals just a couple of weeks before traveling.
