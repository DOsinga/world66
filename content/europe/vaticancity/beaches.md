title: "Beaches in Vatican City"
type: section
---

**Vatican City Beaches **are actually situated outside of Vatican
City, near Rome. Vatican City is made up of 110
acres of land, and has a population of only 800 within the
walled enclave, making it a separate city-state within the capital city
of Rome. A beautiful place to visit, it not only boasts the Vatican
Gardens, Saint Peter's Bascilica, and The Sistine Chapel but also the
Apostolic Palace, home of the Pope himself. Because the actual Vatican
City is surrounded by a wall, other sights and places to visit are
located on the other side, including several beaches that visitors can
take advantage of.**

Ostia Lido Beach**

The Ostia Lido beach is 20 miles from Rome, and takes about 35
minutes to get to by train. Rather than battling traffic, the train
provides a relaxing ride to Ostia, making several stops along the way.
The best place to exit the train in order to get to the beach is the
Cristoforo Colombo stop. Once there, you can board a bus that will take
you along several stretches of beach, and let you off near the water.
Depending on what you're looking for, you can either take advantage of
the free public beaches or, for a small fee, visit the more exclusive
and aesthetically pleasing private beaches. The private beaches offer
travelers more security, allowing them to rent lockers for their
belongings, as well as gain access to changing rooms and restrooms. You
can rent beach chairs, towels and umbrellas while having access to
snacks and drinks. If you're planning a relaxing beach trip while
visiting Vatican City, Ostia is a great place to go. In addition to
public and private stretches of beach, there are several restaurants to
enjoy.**

Sperlonga**

Sperlonga is a pedestrian-only town just outside of Rome that offers
the most beautiful beaches by far. The water is clean, and the sights
are beautiful with charming winding staircases that lead to the ocean.
Though it is a bit pricier to rent chairs and umbrellas (around US $20), it is definitely worth it. The sand gets very hot, making the trip from chair
to water a quick one, but once your feet hit the water, you instantly
forget because it is so beautiful. Extremely clear waters with perfect
temperatures make spending an entire day lazing about in the ocean
easy. Though it is a little farther than Ostia, it is well worth the trip. At night, the city is alive
with locals who enjoy having drinks and dinner, giving travelers the
full beach experience. Sperlonga offers some of the freshest fish and
seafood around, and the sight of the beach from the restaurants can't
be beat.**

Best Beach Months**

The best time to visit Vatican City beaches are during typically warm
months. Any time from May through September promises warm temperatures
ranging from the 80's to the mid and upper 90's. Though the beaches may
be crowded, they are certainly worth the trip.
