Following is a brief guide to **Vatican City Day trips** which will enable you to cover the most important tourist spots.

The Vatican City encompasses an area of more than 100 acres. Therefore, you can easily traverse the entire area by foot. However, remember that a major part of the city is off limits for tourists. The Basilica of St. Peter and Vatican Museums are some of the areas which are open to tourists. You can also pay for a guided tour of the entire city. These trips are provided by the Vatican itself. You can also book the guided tour online two months prior to the actual tour date.

**The St. Peter's Basilica**

This popular tourist destination is usually included in the guided city tour. This magnificent basilica is known for its Michelangelo designed dome. Visit the St Peter’s Basilica in the morning as the line is comparatively shorter in the morning. You can also ask your hotel to book tickets for you. Take a lift to reach to the top of the dome. The elevator to the dome will cost you around €7 while it will take €5 to climb the stairs. You can also visit the basilica at the end of your day trip as it is open from morning 9 a.m. to 7 p.m.

**Vatican Museum**

A visit to the Vatican Museum is a good idea for a one day trip. This museum is especially known for its spiral staircase along with the Raphael Rooms and the Sistine Chapel. The ticket fees will cost you around €15. The Museum is open from Monday to Saturday from morning 9 am to 6pm. The museum is closed on Sundays.

**The Monte Mario**

Monte Mario is the largest hill in Rome and you must definitely make a one day trip here. You will have to leave early in the morning if you want to reach here. Do not forget to wear comfortable shoes as the long walk can prove to be tiresome. The eastern part of the Monte Mario hill is especially preserved as a nature reserve. You can visit the Santa Maria Rosario church and convent which is situated atop the hill. The Rome Observatory is also located here.
