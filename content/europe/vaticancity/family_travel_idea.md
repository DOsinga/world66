---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Vatican City"
type: section
---

**Vatican City family travel **can be very manageable and
memorable for every group member. There are accommodations,
attractions, and dinning options perfect for travelers of every age.

**Accommodations**

Italy has more than its fair share of charming hotels and that
especially holds true for the area in and around Vatican City. Family
travel should include the option of reserving a room in a hotel in
nearby Rome. It is a short 15-minute drive away and has many hotel
options that are perfect for vacationing families. The Cardinal Hotel
in Rome offers an American-style atmosphere that will surely put
travelers and their children at ease. The stress of not having certain
amenities can be frustrating and the Cardinal Hotel provides visitors
with comforts normally found at home. Starting at 80 euros a night, all
rooms have air conditioning, comfortable bedding, and Internet
connection. The elegant yet casual hotel also offers shuttle buses to
railways and the airport. When making reservations at hotels near
Vatican City be sure to inquire about the hotel's amenities, such as
cribs, that will make your family most comfortable and at ease while in
a foreign city.

**Activities**

Family members of all ages will appreciate Saint Peter's Square.
Adults will be enthralled with the architecture and surrounding
sculptures. They will also admire the history and true significance of
the location. Smaller children will enjoy the chance to actually move
and make noise. The majority of their trip the will stuck inside
churches and museums where they will have to be quiet, so they will be
thrilled with the opportunity to actually be outside. Travelers of
every age, however, will marvel out the enormous size of the columns
and the square itself. Saint Peter's Square is open daily. The
Courtyard of the Pigna is also a great family attraction in Vatican
City. The courtyard is directly off the Vatican Museum and contains a
large lawn with interesting sculptures and fountains. Unusual pine-cone
and spherical sculptures are nice places to take family vacation
photographs. Kids and adults will also love the Sistine Chapel and all
the work that went into the attraction. It can get very crowded so be
sure to hang onto the little ones.

**Dining Opportunities**

There are several small cafes around the Vatican that serve
reasonably priced meals. Finding tables for large groups might be a bit
difficult. Another dining alternative is purchasing something from the
snack stands surrounding the Vatican and finding a quiet spot on the
edges of Saint Peter's Square to enjoy a bite to eat in Vatican City.
Family travel in the historic city will seem like a picnic in the park.
