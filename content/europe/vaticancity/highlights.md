title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Vatican City is small enough that its highlights are almost its entirety. These are the things worth your time.

**St. Peter's Basilica** is one of the largest churches in the world and free to enter. The interior is overwhelming — gilded, cavernous, and lined with Renaissance and Baroque masterworks. Michelangelo designed the dome; Bernini created the towering baldachin over the altar. Climb the dome (€8 by lift, €6 on foot) for panoramic views over Rome. Mass is celebrated daily; on Sundays the Pope presides from the central loggia. Dress code is enforced — no bare shoulders or knees.

**The Sistine Chapel** is the emotional and artistic centrepiece of any Vatican visit. Michelangelo painted the famous ceiling between 1508 and 1512 — over 300 figures spread across nearly 500 square metres. The Last Judgement covers the entire altar wall. It's worth reading about the iconography beforehand; knowing what you're looking at makes it far more arresting. No photography is permitted and guards enforce silence, though neither rule is consistently respected by other visitors.

**The Vatican Museums** are the route to the Sistine Chapel and a major attraction in their own right. Highlights include the Gallery of Maps (a 120-metre corridor of Renaissance cartography), the Raphael Rooms, the Laocoön sculpture in the Courtyard of the Pine Cone, and the Egyptian and Etruscan collections. Book ahead — tickets are €20 online (plus a €5 booking fee) and the queue for walk-in entry can be two hours or more.

**St. Peter's Square** is Bernini's great oval colonnade, lined with 140 statues of saints and framing one of the most theatrical public spaces in the world. It's always open and always crowded. Worth seeing at dawn if you can manage it, when the light is good and the tour groups haven't arrived.

**The Vatican Necropolis** lies beneath St. Peter's Basilica. A guided excavation tour (90 minutes, ages 15 and up, max 12 per group) takes you underground to see the ancient cemetery and what is believed to be the tomb of St. Peter. Book by email at scavi@fsp.va well in advance — these tours sell out quickly.
