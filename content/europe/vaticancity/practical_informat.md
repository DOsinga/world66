**Admission**

St. Peter's Basilica is free to enter. Climbing the dome costs €8 by lift or €6 on foot.

Vatican Museums (including the Sistine Chapel) cost €20 online plus a €5 booking fee. Walk-in tickets are available but cost more and involve long queues. Book at tickets.museivaticani.va — the only official ticketing site. The museums are open Monday–Saturday 8:00–20:00 (last entry 18:00). On the last Sunday of each month they are free and open 9:00–14:00; queues are long.

The Vatican Necropolis excavation tour costs around €13 and must be booked by email (scavi@fsp.va) or phone (+39 06 6988 5318). Tours are capped at 12 people and sell out weeks in advance.

**Dress code**

Covered shoulders and knees are required at St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel. This is strictly enforced — guards turn people away. Jeans and trainers are fine; sleeveless tops, shorts, and short skirts are not. Carry a scarf if your clothing is borderline.

**Papal audiences**

The Pope holds general audiences on Wednesday mornings at 9:30 in St. Peter's Square (or Paul VI Audience Hall in bad weather). Tickets are free. Request them through the Prefecture of the Papal Household (the prefecture.va website) or for American visitors via the US Visitors' Office (visitorsoffice@pnac.org). Tickets can also sometimes be obtained from the Swiss Guard at the Sant'Anna Gate. Arrive early — doors open around 7:30 and good spots fill quickly.

**Photography**

Photography is prohibited in the Sistine Chapel. It is permitted elsewhere in the Vatican Museums and in St. Peter's Basilica (no flash). Tripods and selfie sticks are not allowed.

**Currency**

Vatican City uses the euro. There are no ATMs inside the walls; use those on the Roman side of Piazza del Risorgimento.

**Security**

All visitors pass through airport-style security. Large bags may need to be left at the cloakroom. The queues for security can add 20–30 minutes to your wait.
