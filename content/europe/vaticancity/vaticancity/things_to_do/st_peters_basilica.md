One of the largest churches in the world and the centrepiece of the Vatican. Construction began in 1506 on the site of the original 4th-century basilica commissioned by Constantine; Michelangelo took over as chief architect in 1547 and designed the great dome, which remains the tallest in the world. Bernini added the baldachin over the high altar and the colonnaded square outside. The interior is overwhelming — gilded, cavernous, and lined with Renaissance and Baroque masterworks. Admission is free; climbing the dome for panoramic views over Rome costs a few euros extra. Dress code is strictly enforced: covered shoulders and knees required.
