The gardens cover about half of Vatican City's total area and are not accessible without a guided tour. They contain Renaissance and Baroque fountains, sculpted topiary, a replica Lourdes grotto, and the Casina of Pius IV — a small Renaissance palace designed by Pirro Ligorio, now used for Vatican academic events. The tour runs most mornings and can be booked alongside museum tickets. A peaceful contrast to the crowded museums, but appeal depends on your interest in formal garden design.
