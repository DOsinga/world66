---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Vatican City is open year-round, but timing your visit makes a real difference. The place is busy in every season — plan accordingly and book museum tickets in advance no matter when you come.

**Spring and autumn** are the best seasons. April, May, October, and early November offer mild temperatures (15–25°C), manageable crowds, and good light. Easter week is the exception — St. Peter's Square fills with hundreds of thousands of people for papal ceremonies, accommodation in Rome becomes scarce, and access to the Vatican is disrupted.

**Summer (June–August)** is peak tourist season. July and August bring temperatures above 30°C and high humidity. The queues at the Vatican Museums are at their longest, and standing in St. Peter's Square in midday heat is unpleasant. If you visit in summer, book tickets well in advance and arrive at opening time (8:00).

**Winter (November–February)** is the quietest period. Crowds are thinner, especially on weekday mornings. The weather is cool and occasionally rainy but rarely harsh. Christmas and New Year are exceptions — pilgrims and tourists fill St. Peter's Square for the papal Christmas mass and the Urbi et Orbi blessing on Christmas Day.

**Wednesdays** are busier because the Pope holds his weekly general audience in St. Peter's Square at 9:30, drawing large crowds. Arrive on a Tuesday or Thursday for a quieter experience.

The **last Sunday of each month** the Vatican Museums offer free admission, which sounds appealing but results in very long queues. Unless you arrive well before opening, it rarely saves time.

**Religious events** follow the Catholic liturgical calendar. Easter is the biggest: Good Friday brings the Via Crucis procession at the Colosseum; Holy Saturday the Easter Vigil in St. Peter's Basilica; Easter Sunday the Mass and Urbi et Orbi blessing in the square. Papal canonisations occur several times a year and fill the square beyond capacity. Each autumn the Festival Internazionale di Musica e Arte Sacra brings orchestras including the Vienna Philharmonic to perform in St. Peter's and other Roman basilicas.
