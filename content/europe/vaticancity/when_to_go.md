---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Vatican City is open year-round, but timing your visit makes a real difference.

**Spring and autumn** are the best seasons. April, May, October, and early November offer mild temperatures (15–25°C), manageable crowds, and good light for photographs. Easter week is the exception — St. Peter's Square fills with enormous crowds for papal ceremonies, and the surrounding streets get very busy.

**Summer (June–August)** is peak tourist season. July and August bring temperatures above 30°C and high humidity. The queues at the Vatican Museums are at their longest, and standing in St. Peter's Square in midday heat is genuinely unpleasant. If you visit in summer, book tickets well in advance and arrive at opening time (8:00).

**Winter (November–February)** is the quietest period. Crowds are thinner, especially on weekday mornings, and you can sometimes walk into the museums with little wait. The weather is cool and occasionally rainy but rarely harsh. Christmas and New Year are exceptions — pilgrims and tourists fill St. Peter's Square for the papal Christmas mass and blessing.

**Wednesdays** are busier because the Pope holds his weekly general audience in the morning, drawing large crowds to the square. If you want a quieter experience, arrive on a Tuesday or Thursday.

The **last Sunday of each month** the Vatican Museums offer free admission, which sounds appealing but results in very long queues. Unless you arrive well before opening, it's often not worth it.
