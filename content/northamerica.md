North America stretches from the Arctic to the tropics, from the world's most powerful country to tiny Caribbean islands where time barely moves. It covers an enormous range of landscapes, cultures, and travel experiences — gleaming megacities and empty deserts, ancient Maya ruins and space-age architecture, salsa and country music, tacos and lobster rolls.

The [United States](/northamerica/unitedstates) is the continent's big draw. [New York](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork), [San Francisco](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfranciscobayarea/sanfrancisco), [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago), [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles), [Boston](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/boston) and [Seattle](/northamerica/unitedstates/washington/seattle) are world-class cities, each with its own character. But the real glory of the US is its natural landscape — the national parks alone could fill a lifetime of trips. [Yellowstone](/northamerica/unitedstates/montana/yellowstone), [Yosemite](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/highsierra/yosemite), the Grand Canyon, and the [Everglades](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/everglades) are just the headliners.

[Canada](/northamerica/canada) combines cosmopolitan cities with staggering wilderness. [Toronto](/northamerica/canada/ontario/toronto) and [Vancouver](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/vancouver) are vibrant and diverse, while French-speaking [Montreal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal) and [Quebec City](/northamerica/canada/quebec/quebeccity) feel almost European. Between the cities lies an enormous expanse of forests, lakes, mountains, and coastline — the Canadian Rockies, the Maritime provinces, and the vast northern territories.

[Mexico](/northamerica/mexico) is a world in itself. From the colonial cities of [Oaxaca](/northamerica/mexico/oaxaca) and [Guanajuato](/northamerica/mexico/guanajuato) to the Maya ruins of [Chichen Itza](/northamerica/mexico/chichenitza) and the beaches of the [Yucatan](/northamerica/mexico/yucatan), it offers history, culture, food, and natural beauty in abundance. Mexico City is one of the great megacities — chaotic, creative, and endlessly rewarding. The food alone is reason to visit.

Central America packs a remarkable amount into a small isthmus. [Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala) and [Honduras](/northamerica/honduras) have some of the finest Maya ruins anywhere. [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica) pioneered eco-tourism and delivers on its promises of rainforest, wildlife, and volcanoes. [Belize](/northamerica/belize) has the second-largest barrier reef in the world. [Panama](/northamerica/panama) is where the continent narrows to a sliver, with the famous canal and the wild Darien Gap separating it from South America. [Nicaragua](/northamerica/nicaragua) and [El Salvador](/northamerica/elsalvador) are increasingly popular with travelers looking for something less polished and more authentic.

The Caribbean needs no introduction — rum, reggae, sunshine, and sandy beaches. But there is far more variety than the brochures suggest. [Cuba](/northamerica/cuba) is unlike anywhere else in the Americas, frozen and thawing at the same time. [Jamaica](/northamerica/jamaica) has a cultural influence wildly disproportionate to its size. [Trinidad](/northamerica/trinidadandtobago) has Carnival and the most diverse population in the region. Smaller islands like [Dominica](/northamerica/dominica), [Grenada](/northamerica/grenada), and [Saint Lucia](/northamerica/saintlucia) offer quieter, greener alternatives to the resort strips.

[Greenland](/northamerica/greenland) is the adventurous outlier — the world's largest island, mostly covered in ice, with a narrow travel season and a landscape unlike anything else on the continent. It is remote, expensive, and utterly spectacular.
