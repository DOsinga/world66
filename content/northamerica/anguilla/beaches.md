---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Anguilla has 33 beaches for an island just 26 km long. Most are uncrowded, accessible by car or a short walk, and free of the large resort infrastructure that dominates some Caribbean destinations. The sand is consistently white and fine; the water runs from pale turquoise in the shallows to deep blue offshore.

**Shoal Bay East** is the signature beach — a long crescent with calm, clear water, beach bar options, and snorkelling just offshore. It is the most visited and deservedly so. **Meads Bay** on the northwest coast is wider and lined with some of the island's better restaurants; the sunsets here are reliable. **Rendezvous Bay** is the longest beach on the island and home to the Dune Preserve, Bankie Banx's famous beach bar.

On the north coast, **Little Bay** requires a short boat trip or a rope descent down a cliff — the effort keeps it quiet. **Shoal Bay West** (not to be confused with Shoal Bay East) is more remote and almost entirely undeveloped. **Maunday's Bay** is where the high-end resorts cluster, and the beach reflects that — well-maintained and calm, with good swimming. **Sandy Ground** is a working fishing village with a reef-sheltered beach and a handful of casual bars; more local in character than the resort beaches.
