---
title: "Books"
type: section
---

**A Trip to the Beach** by Melinda and Robert Blanchard (2001) is the obvious starting point for anyone heading to Anguilla. An American couple quit their careers and moved to the island to open a restaurant; this is the story of what happened. Charming, honest, and full of practical observation about island life. The restaurant, Blanchard's on Meads Bay, is still open.

**Night of the Rambler** by Montague Kobbe (2013) is a novel about the 1967 revolution — the rebellion in which Anguillans expelled their Kittitian administrators and demanded the right to remain a British territory. Kobbe writes with the sprawling energy of the Latin American tradition about an event that deserves to be better known. An excellent book to read on the ferry from St. Martin.

**Under an English Heaven** by Donald Westlake (1972) is an earlier, more comic account of the same revolution, written shortly after the events. Westlake was a crime novelist; he brings the same dry eye to the absurdities of small-island politics.

**Out of Crowded Vagueness: A History of the Islands of St. Kitts, Nevis and Anguilla** by Brian Dyde (2005) provides the historical context the other books assume. Dry in places, but thorough.

For Anguillian perspectives, look for works by local author **Patricia J. Adams**, whose novels are set on the island and draw on its specific culture and landscape.
