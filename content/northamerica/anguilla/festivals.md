---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

**Moonsplash** (March or April) is Anguilla's most famous festival — a reggae and roots music event hosted by musician Bankie Banx at his Dune Preserve beach bar on Rendezvous Bay. It has run continuously for over 30 years, making it one of the oldest independent music festivals in the Caribbean. The setting is informal: bands play on an open stage, people sit on the sand, and the bar stays open late. International and regional acts join Bankie Banx and local musicians over a long weekend.

**Anguilla Day** (May 30) marks the anniversary of the 1967 revolution with boat races, cultural events, and general island-wide celebration. It is one of the liveliest public holidays and a good day to see traditional wooden-boat racing.

**Anguilla Culinary Experience (ACE)** (May) is a multi-day food and wine festival that draws chefs from across the Caribbean and beyond for a series of dinners, beach barbecues, cooking demonstrations, and wine tastings. It has grown quickly and is now a serious event on the Caribbean food calendar.

**Festival Del Mar** (Easter weekend) is held in Island Harbour, the island's fishing village on the north coast. The focus is seafood — conch, lobster, crayfish — prepared simply and eaten outside with a view of the boats. More local in character than ACE.

**Anguilla Summer Festival / Carnival** (late July to early August) is the big cultural event of the year, coinciding with the emancipation commemorations (August Monday and August Thursday). The week includes street parades, pageants, boat races, live music, and beach parties. Accommodation fills up; book early if you want to be here for it.

**Welches Festival** is a smaller, community-run event celebrating traditional Anguillan food and crafts. Local cooks make johnny cakes, souse, corn soup, and yard fowl dishes. Worth attending if you are curious about how the island eats when it is not performing for visitors.
