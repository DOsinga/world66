---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The island is small but spread out — 33 beaches are scattered across both coasts, and the villages are connected by a single main road running east to west. You will want some form of transport.

**Rental cars** are the most practical option if you want to explore freely. Rates run from around US$45 to US$85 per day. You will need a temporary Anguillian driving permit (US$25), available through any rental agency. Driving is on the left, as in Britain. Roads are paved and in reasonable condition, and the flat terrain means none of the terrifying switchbacks you find elsewhere in the Caribbean. Note that addresses do not exist — places are referred to by beach, bay or neighbourhood, so download an offline map before you set out.

**Taxis** are reliable and government-regulated, with fixed fares posted at the airport and ferry terminal. They are more expensive than renting a car but convenient for short hops. A surcharge applies between 6pm and 6am. If you find a driver you like, get their number — a local guide is worth more than a GPS on Anguilla.

There is no public bus network worth relying on. If you are staying at a resort, many properties offer shuttle services to nearby beaches and restaurants.
