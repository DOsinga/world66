---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Most visitors arrive by ferry from St. Martin, which is the easiest and cheapest option. Ferries run from Marigot (French side) and Simpson Bay to Blowing Point on Anguilla's south coast roughly every 30 to 45 minutes from early morning to late evening. The crossing takes about 20 minutes. The one-way fare is around US$30 per person, and there is a separate departure tax of US$36 for adults when leaving Anguilla by ferry.

**By air**, Anguilla's Clayton J. Lloyd International Airport (AXA) receives a limited number of direct flights. There are direct services from Miami, and seasonal nonstop flights from Boston and Baltimore operate through AnguillAir (a codeshare with BermudAir) during the winter season. The more common approach is to fly into Princess Juliana International Airport (SXM) on St. Martin and then take the ferry — it adds an hour to the journey but opens up many more flight options from North America and Europe.

From Puerto Rico it is also possible to fly direct; the flight takes about an hour. Small charter operators connect Anguilla to several other Eastern Caribbean islands.

**Departure tax** at the airport is US$28 for adults and must be paid in cash. Keep dollars handy when you leave.
