---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**Shoal Bay East** is the beach most visitors come for. A long crescent of powdery white sand backed by sea grape trees, with clear, shallow water ranging from pale turquoise to deep blue — it looks like a screensaver and feels better in person. You can rent chairs and umbrellas, snorkel just offshore, and eat at a handful of casual beach bars. It is the island's most famous beach for good reason.

**The boat races** are Anguilla's national sport and its most local spectacle. Traditional wooden sailing boats, built to designs that have barely changed in a century, race at regattas held on public holidays — Anguilla Day (May 30), August Monday, and August Thursday. The atmosphere on the beach is festive and unhurried, with music, grilling, and an absence of the tourist-facing performance that marks so many Caribbean "cultural events." This is just what Anguillans do.

**Snorkelling and diving** around the offshore cays is consistently excellent. Prickly Pear Cays, a short boat ride from the north coast, offers good reefs and an uncrowded beach. Scrub Island to the east has more dramatic underwater scenery. Several operators run day trips.

**The restaurants** are a genuine highlight — not a thing you say about most small Caribbean islands. Anguilla has more than 150 restaurants for a population of around 18,000. The standard is high across the price range: from the smoky roadside barbecue stalls (try the ribs at any of the weekend spots near The Valley) to upmarket beachfront restaurants serving French-Caribbean fusion. Reservations are advisable at the better places in high season.

**Katouche Bay** offers a hiking trail through dry scrub forest past a cave, with a swim in a secluded cove at the end. It is one of the few places on the island where you can feel like you have made some small effort to earn your beach.
