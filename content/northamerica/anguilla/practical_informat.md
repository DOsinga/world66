title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Visas and entry**: Most visitors do not need a visa for stays under 90 days. Since January 2025, US citizens visiting British Overseas Territories (including Anguilla) are required to obtain an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) before arrival. It costs £10 (around US$12) and is valid for two years. Apply at the UK Home Office website before you travel.

**Currency**: The Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD) is the official currency, but US dollars are accepted virtually everywhere. Credit cards are widely used at resorts and restaurants. EC$2.70 is roughly US$1. Keep some cash for taxis, departure taxes, and smaller establishments.

**Language**: English, spoken with a distinctive Anguillan lilt.

**Health**: No vaccinations are required, but standard Caribbean precautions apply — sun protection, insect repellent, and caution with tap water in rural areas. The main hospital is Princess Alexandra Hospital in The Valley. Medical facilities are adequate for routine care; serious cases are medevaced to St. Martin or Puerto Rico.

**Safety**: Anguilla is one of the safer islands in the Caribbean. Serious crime affecting tourists is rare, though petty theft does occur. Lock your car and do not leave valuables unattended on the beach.

**Time zone**: Atlantic Standard Time (UTC-4). Anguilla does not observe daylight saving time.

**Electricity**: 110V/60Hz, same as North America. British-style three-pin plugs are also found.

**Telephone**: Country code +1-264.
