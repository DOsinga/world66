---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Anguilla has a warm, dry climate year-round, with temperatures hovering around 27°C (80°F). The trade winds keep things comfortable even in summer. The distinction between seasons is mostly about rainfall and hurricane risk, not cold.

**December to April** is high season — sunny, dry, and busy. This is when the resorts are full, prices are at their highest, and the island looks its best. Book accommodation and restaurants well in advance. **May to November** is the rainy season, and July through October falls within the Atlantic hurricane season. The upside: hotels are significantly cheaper, beaches are quieter, and the island has a more local feel. Late May or June can be a sweet spot — mostly fine weather and much lower prices.

**Events worth timing around:**

**Moonsplash** (March or April) is Anguilla's most famous festival — a reggae and roots music event hosted by Bankie Banx at his Dune Preserve beach bar on Rendezvous Bay. It has run continuously for over 30 years, making it one of the oldest independent music festivals in the Caribbean. Bands play on an open stage, people sit on the sand, and the bar stays open late.

**Anguilla Day** (May 30) marks the anniversary of the 1967 revolution with boat races, cultural events, and island-wide celebration. It is the best day to see traditional wooden-boat racing.

**Anguilla Culinary Experience (ACE)** (May) is a multi-day food and wine festival drawing chefs from across the Caribbean for dinners, beach barbecues, and cooking demonstrations.

**Anguilla Summer Festival / Carnival** (late July to early August) is the big cultural event of the year, coinciding with emancipation commemorations. The week includes street parades, pageants, boat races, and beach parties. Accommodation fills up fast — book early.

**Festival Del Mar** (Easter weekend) is held in Island Harbour, the island's fishing village, with a focus on fresh seafood. More local in character than ACE.
