---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Anguilla has a warm, dry climate year-round, with temperatures hovering around 27°C (80°F). The trade winds keep things comfortable even in summer. The distinction between seasons is mostly about rainfall and hurricane risk, not cold.

**December to April** is high season — sunny, dry, and busy. This is when the resorts are full, prices are at their highest, and the island looks its best. If you want the classic Caribbean experience in perfect weather, this is when to come. Book accommodation and restaurants well in advance.

**May to November** is the rainy season, and July through October falls within the Atlantic hurricane season. Anguilla is not hit by hurricanes as often as some other islands, but the risk is real. The upside: hotels are significantly cheaper, beaches are quieter, and the island has a more local feel. If you avoid the peak hurricane months of August and September, late May or June can be a sweet spot — the weather is still mostly fine and prices are much lower.

**Moonsplash**, the long-running reggae festival hosted by musician Bankie Banx at his Dune Preserve beach bar, takes place in March or April and draws a loyal crowd. The **Anguilla Summer Festival** (Carnival) runs in late July and early August — a week of street parades, live music, and boat races tied to the emancipation commemorations. The **Anguilla Culinary Experience (ACE)** food festival runs in May and is worth planning around if food is a priority.
