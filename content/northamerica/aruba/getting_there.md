**By air**

Queen Beatrix International Airport (AUA) is 3.5 km southeast of Oranjestad. It handles a large volume of traffic and is well set up for arrivals, with duty-free shops, restaurants, ATMs and a tourist information desk. Taxis and buses both run to the city centre and the hotel strip.

Direct flights from the US are plentiful. American Airlines connects Aruba from multiple US gateways including New York (JFK), Miami, Charlotte and Philadelphia. Delta, United, JetBlue, Southwest and Spirit also serve the island from various US cities. Flight time from New York is around four hours; from Miami, about two and a half.

From Europe, KLM flies direct from Amsterdam; TUI Netherlands also operates seasonal routes. From Latin America, Copa Airlines, Avianca and Wingo provide connections from Colombia, Panama and beyond. Venezuela is only a short hop away.

**Departure tax**

Departure tax is typically included in the ticket price.

**By sea**

Aruba is a regular stop on Caribbean cruise itineraries, and the port in Oranjestad receives ships year-round. The island has extensive duty-free shopping, which draws cruise passengers in large numbers. If you're arriving by private yacht, Aruba has a marina at Oranjestad.
