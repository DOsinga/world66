---
title: "Books"
type: section
---

The Bahamas has not generated a large literary tradition, but a few books stand out for travelers.

**Hemingway's** time in Bimini in the 1930s shows up in *Islands in the Stream* (1970), his posthumously published novel set largely on "Bimini" — a thinly veiled version of the island where he fished and drank for stretches of the mid-30s. It's not his best work, but it captures the light and the pace of the place.

For practical sailing and cruising, the **Yachtsman's Guide to the Bahamas** (Tropic Isle Publishers) has been updated regularly for decades and remains the standard reference for boaters.

Naturalists will find **Reef Fish Identification: Tropical Pacific** less useful than the Caribbean equivalent — pick up Paul Humann's **Reef Fish Identification: Florida, Caribbean, Bahamas** instead, the standard field guide for underwater identification.
