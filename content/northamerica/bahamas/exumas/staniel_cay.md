A tiny island in the central Exuma Cays with a population of around 100 and an outsized reputation. Staniel Cay is the gateway to the Exumas' headline attractions: Thunderball Grotto is a five-minute boat ride away, the swimming pigs of Big Major Cay are just to the north, and Compass Cay's nurse sharks are a short hop south. The island has its own airstrip with charter flights from Nassau, making it the main hub for visitors to the cays.

The settlement itself is charming in a bare-bones way — a yacht club, a handful of rental cottages, a small grocery store, and not much else. The Staniel Cay Yacht Club is the social centre, with a bar, a restaurant, and a fuel dock that keeps the boating community ticking. Most visitors are sailors, private pilots, or day-trippers from George Town.

If you want to explore the Exuma Cays properly — snorkelling pristine reef, anchoring at empty beaches, island-hopping by dinghy — Staniel Cay is the place to base yourself. It is quieter and more remote than George Town, with prices to match.
