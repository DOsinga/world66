Allen's Cay, at the northern end of the Exuma chain, is home to the endangered Bahamian rock iguana — a stocky, prehistoric-looking lizard that can grow over two feet long. The iguanas are remarkably tame and will approach visitors on the beach looking for grapes and other fruit. They are wild animals, though, so keep fingers clear of their mouths.

The cay is uninhabited and accessible only by boat. It is a common first stop for boats heading south through the Exuma Cays from Nassau. There is good snorkelling offshore in clear, shallow water.
