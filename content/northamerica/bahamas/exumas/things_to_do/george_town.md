The capital of the Exumas is a small, friendly town built around Elizabeth Harbour. Pink government buildings overlook the water, and St Andrew's Anglican Church — built around 1802 — sits on a hill above town. Behind the church, Lake Victoria is a saltwater lake once used for soaking sisal.

The Straw Market, shaded by a large African fig tree, sells handmade baskets and souvenirs. The Government Dock is where the mail boats come in and where you can catch a water taxi to Stocking Island. George Town is walkable and has a couple of grocery stores, a few restaurants, and not much else — which is entirely the point.
