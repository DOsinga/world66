The swimming pigs of Big Major Cay — known universally as Pig Beach — are probably the most photographed animals in the Caribbean. A colony of feral pigs lives on this uninhabited cay and has learned to paddle out to visiting boats in hopes of food. They are surprisingly strong swimmers and not at all shy.

The experience is genuinely fun even if you have seen a thousand Instagram posts about it. Go early in the morning to beat the tour groups, bring vegetables or fruit (not processed food — it makes them sick), and watch your belongings on the beach. The pigs are friendly but assertive. Big Major Cay sits between Staniel Cay and Thunderball Grotto, so most day trips combine all three.
