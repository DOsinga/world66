A sea cave just west of Staniel Cay that you enter by swimming through an underwater opening. Inside, shafts of light pierce the rock ceiling and illuminate clouds of tropical fish in water so clear it barely looks real. At low tide the entrance is easy and you can stand inside; at high tide you need to be a confident swimmer and duck fully underwater to get through. Bring a snorkel and waterproof camera.

The grotto is best visited at low tide, ideally in the morning before tour boats arrive. Most visitors combine it with a stop at Pig Beach on nearby Big Major Cay. Day trips from George Town run by boat and take around an hour each way, or you can fly to Staniel Cay and hire a boat for the five-minute ride.
