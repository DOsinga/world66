---
title: "Tropic of Cancer Beach"
type: poi
latitude: 23.4372
longitude: -75.8819
---

The best beach in the Exumas and one of the best in the Bahamas. A mile-long arc of white sand on the south coast of Little Exuma, backed by low dunes and facing a sheltered bay. The water is shallow, calm, and an almost unreal shade of turquoise. Offshore, small islands like Turtle Island break the waves and keep things gentle.

The Tropic of Cancer itself passes through this beach — the invisible line is marked on a set of stairs leading down to the sand. Also called Pelican Beach on older maps. It is rarely crowded; on a weekday you may have it to yourself. About 20 minutes south of George Town, past the bridge to Little Exuma. No facilities — bring water and shade.
