---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Exumas have a subtropical climate — warm year-round, with a dry season from December to April and a wetter, hotter stretch from May to November. Daytime temperatures in peak season sit between 24-27C (75-80F) with low humidity and reliable sunshine. Summer temperatures climb a few degrees higher and afternoon rain showers are common but brief. Hurricane season officially runs June to November, with the highest risk in September and October.

**December to April** is peak season. The weather is ideal, the water is calm, and prices are at their highest. Book well ahead for this period.

**The National Family Island Regatta** in April is the biggest event of the year — traditional Bahamian sloop racing in Elizabeth Harbour, live music, food stalls, and a party atmosphere that fills every room in George Town. If you want to come for the regatta, book months in advance. If you want to avoid crowds, skip that week.

**May and November** offer a sweet spot: good weather, fewer visitors, and lower prices. The sea is warm enough for comfortable swimming year-round.
