type: section

The **Freeport bars and cafes** boast of inexpensive but great food and beverages. They continue to be part of the Caribbean&rsquo;s local attractions. These bars and cafes serve drinks and snacks sometimes up to the wee hours of dawn. Together with the festive air, visitors sing and dance. One popular bar locally and internationally is the small but lively Bahamas Mama Bar. This is a recommended local hangout.

**Bahamas Mama Bar**

This is one of the frequently visited bars by locals. It is opened until 4 a.m. The atmosphere is of joyful air where visitors can dance and sing. You can request what music you want played. Music is usually the dance music like rock and reggae. One of the specialties of the bar is the savory Bahamas mama drink, and the constituents are grenadine, pineapple juice, orange juice, coconut juice, dark rum and light rum. You can dance the night away if you want to. You can stay as long as you want. Enjoy your time with some locals and tourists who go there with one purpose in mind--to enjoy music, dancing and drinking.

**Price of Drinks**

The bar serves affordable drinks and snacks. Sometimes, the bar offers also free drinks. The beer is inexpensive, costing around $2.95. Other non-alcoholic beverages cost from $1.50 to $2.95. You can buy snacks for as low as $2. Premium cocktail prices range from $5 to $9. You can also order some simple food dish if you are hungry and want some carbohydrates for energy.
