title: "People"

---

The Bahamas has a population of around 400,000, with roughly 70% living on New Providence (Nassau). The vast majority — about 90% — are of African descent, the legacy of enslaved Africans brought to the islands during the colonial era, plus later migration from other Caribbean islands. There is a small white minority with roots in loyalist settlers who came from the American colonies after the Revolution, and a significant Haitian immigrant community concentrated in Nassau.

Bahamians are generally warm and direct with visitors. Religion plays an important role in daily life — the Bahamas is one of the most church-going countries in the Americas, predominantly Baptist and other Protestant denominations.

The pace of life on the Family Islands is noticeably slower than Nassau, and people will tell you proudly. Island time is not a cliché here — it's a conscious choice.

**Haitian community**

The Bahamas has a large Haitian-Bahamian population, particularly on New Providence. Haitian Creole is widely spoken in certain neighborhoods, and Haitian food, music, and culture are a visible part of Nassau life. The community's status has been a long-running political issue, and immigration enforcement has historically been strict.
