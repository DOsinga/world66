---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Bahamas is built for outdoor activity. Most of what's worth doing involves water.

**Snorkeling and diving** — The reefs here are among the best in the Atlantic. You don't need certification to snorkel; almost any beach will have something to see, and organized boat trips reach the better reef sites. Divers should look at [Andros](/northamerica/bahamas/andros), which borders one of the deepest ocean walls in the world, and the Abacos, where the San Jacinto wreck and numerous coral gardens attract good marine life. Nassau and the Exumas have plenty of dive operators for beginners.

**Fishing** — The Bahamas is world-famous for bonefishing in the shallow flats, particularly around [Andros](/northamerica/bahamas/andros), [Bimini](/northamerica/bahamas/bimini), and the [Exumas](/northamerica/bahamas/exumas). Blue marlin and yellowfin tuna draw deep-sea anglers to Bimini and the Tongue of the Ocean. Guides are available on most islands.

**Sailing and boating** — The Abacos are the sailing capital of the Bahamas, with protected waters, charted anchorages, and a well-developed network of marinas. The Exumas are popular with charter boats exploring uninhabited cays. Sea kayaking is a good way to reach the quieter corners of most islands.

**Beaches** — Pink sand on Harbour Island (Eleuthera), long white sweeps on Cable Beach in Nassau, and secluded strands accessible only by boat throughout the Exumas. Many of the best beaches on smaller islands have no facilities — bring water and food.

**Hiking** — Not the primary draw, but a few trails reward the effort. The Exuma Land and Sea Park has walking trails on some of the cays. On [Andros](/northamerica/bahamas/andros), the interior is laced with blue holes — freshwater sinkholes that open into cave systems. [Eleuthera](/northamerica/bahamas/eleuthera) has a few coastal walks through scrub and ironshore.

**Conch** — Put it on your food to-do list as much as your activity list. Watching a fisherman crack and clean a live conch and prepare conch salad on the spot, right on the dock, is one of the more satisfying Bahamas experiences.
