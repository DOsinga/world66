Bridgetown is the capital of [Barbados](/northamerica/barbados), a compact Caribbean city with a UNESCO-listed historic centre. The streets around Broad Street and the Careenage waterfront are walkable and full of character, mixing colonial architecture with rum shops and market stalls.

Trafalgar Square has a Nelson monument that predates [London](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/london)'s by almost 40 years. Just east of here stands St. Michael's Cathedral, built between 1784 and 1786. The Nidhe Israel Synagogue is a good century older and is now a National Trust property with a museum attached.

The Garrison area south of the centre is worth a morning. The Barbados Museum covers the island's history from Amerindian settlement through the sugar trade, and the old military buildings make for a pleasant walk.
