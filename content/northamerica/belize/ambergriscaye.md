Ambergris Caye is the largest and most visited of Belize's cayes, running 56 km along the northeastern coast. The main town, San Pedro, has grown from a quiet fishing village into Belize's main tourist hub, with resorts, dive shops, restaurants, and bars lining its sandy streets. Golf carts are the primary transport — cars are rare on the island.

The reef is the main attraction. Hol Chan Marine Reserve and Shark Ray Alley, just south of the island, offer some of the most accessible reef diving and snorkeling in the Caribbean: nurse sharks, rays, and hundreds of reef fish in clear, calm water. More experienced divers head further out to the Blue Hole and Turneffe Islands.

San Pedro gets busy and can feel overdeveloped compared to neighboring Caye Caulker. But the beach is good, the diving is excellent, and the range of accommodation — from budget guesthouses to upscale resorts — means it suits most budgets. Water taxis and domestic flights connect to Belize City multiple times daily.
