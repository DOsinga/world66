The Belize Barrier Reef is the second-longest barrier reef system in the world after Australia's Great Barrier Reef, stretching 300 km along the coast. It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1996.

The reef is the centerpiece of Belize's diving and snorkeling scene. The most popular sites are accessible from [Ambergris Caye](/northamerica/belize/ambergriscaye) and [Caye Caulker](/northamerica/belize/cayecaulker). Hol Chan Marine Reserve (near San Pedro) and Shark Ray Alley are the busiest sites — nurse sharks and southern stingrays are virtually guaranteed. The reef supports extraordinary biodiversity, including manatees, whale sharks (seasonal), and hundreds of fish species.

Further offshore, the [Turneffe Islands](/northamerica/belize/turneffeislands) atoll and Lighthouse Reef offer more remote diving. The Great Blue Hole — a 300-metre wide sinkhole dropping to 125 metres — is world-famous; it's a full-day boat trip from the coast and the diving is suited to those with open-water certification or above.

Tobacco Caye, reached by boat from [Dangriga](/northamerica/belize/dangriga), is a tiny, quiet caye right on the reef with simple accommodation and good snorkeling direct from shore.
