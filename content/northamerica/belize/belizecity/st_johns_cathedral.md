The oldest Anglican cathedral in Central America, built in 1812 from bricks brought over as ballast in European sailing ships. The red-brick building has survived hurricanes and a changing city around it. Four Miskito kings were crowned here in the 19th century — a legacy of British colonial influence in the region.
