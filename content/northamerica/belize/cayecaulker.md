Caye Caulker is a small coral island about 35 km northeast of Belize City, and the budget traveler's alternative to the more developed Ambergris Caye. The main village is spread along one road — no cars, just bicycles and golf carts. The motto "Go Slow" is not just for tourists.

The island was split in two by Hurricane Hattie in 1961; the northern half (The Split) is now a popular swimming and sunbathing spot with beach bars. The rest of the island has a laid-back, slightly scruffy charm — cheap guesthouses, open-air restaurants, and fish tacos.

Snorkeling and diving trips to the barrier reef are the main activities, and Hol Chan Marine Reserve is accessible from here as well as from San Pedro. Most visitors book day trips directly with the many tour operators along the main street.

Water taxis from Belize City take about 45 minutes (BZ$30-40 one way). You can also continue to Ambergris Caye in another 30 minutes.
