Corozal is a small, quiet town on the northern coast near the Mexican border, overlooking Corozal Bay. It's not a major tourist destination but is pleasant enough — laid-back, relatively safe, and useful as a stopping point if you're crossing into Mexico at Chetumal.

The town has a Maya heritage: the ruins of Santa Rita are within walking distance, and the nearby site of Cerros (accessible by boat) is one of the few pre-Classic Maya sites in Belize. The town museum in the old town hall has local history exhibits.

Buses run regularly to Belize City (about two hours), and the Mexican border at Santa Elena/Subteniente López is 15 km north.
