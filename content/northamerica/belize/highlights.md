---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**The Belize Barrier Reef** is the main draw for many visitors. It runs 300 km along the coast and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Hol Chan Marine Reserve and Shark Ray Alley off [Ambergris Caye](/northamerica/belize/ambergriscaye) are among the most-visited dive and snorkel sites. The Great Blue Hole — a massive underwater sinkhole about 70 km off the coast — is one of the most famous dive spots in the world, though it's a long boat ride and better appreciated by experienced divers.

**The cayes** offer dramatically different experiences. [Ambergris Caye](/northamerica/belize/ambergriscaye) (and its main town, San Pedro) is the most developed, with resorts, restaurants, and golf carts everywhere. [Caye Caulker](/northamerica/belize/cayecaulker) is smaller, slower, and cheaper — the motto is "Go Slow," and they mean it. The Turneffe Islands and Lighthouse Reef are remote atolls popular with divers.

**Maya ruins** are scattered across the interior. Xunantunich, near [San Ignacio](/northamerica/belize/sanignacio), is easily visited and has a large pyramid with views over the river valley. Caracol, deeper in the jungle, was one of the largest Maya cities in the region. Lamanai, accessible by river boat from [Orange Walk Town](/northamerica/belize/orangewalktown), is particularly atmospheric, with temples rising out of dense forest.

**The western interior** around San Ignacio is Belize's adventure hub — caving (Actun Tunichil Muknal is one of the best cave experiences in Central America), zip-lining, horseback riding, and whitewater tubing on jungle rivers. The Mountain Pine Ridge Forest Reserve has waterfalls, granite hills, and clear swimming holes.

**Southern Belize** is the least-visited region and worth the effort. [Hopkins](/northamerica/belize/hopkins) is a Garifuna village with good beaches and great seafood. Placencia Peninsula has some of the country's best mainland beaches. And Punta Gorda is the jumping-off point for the Toledo district's Maya communities and cacao country.

**The Belize Zoo** is worth a half-day stop on the Western Highway. All the animals are native to Belize and were rescued or born in captivity — jaguars, tapirs, harpy eagles, crocodiles, and more. It's one of the best small zoos in the Americas.
