---
title: "Things to do in Hopkins"
type: section
---

Hopkins is a slow place — and that's the point. The main activities are beach, water, and immersing yourself in Garifuna culture.

**Snorkeling and diving** The South Water Caye Marine Reserve and Glover's Reef Atoll are both accessible on day trips from Hopkins. Glover's is one of the finest reef systems in the Caribbean — a full atoll with outstanding wall diving and snorkeling. Several operators in the village can arrange trips.

**Kayaking** Calm, sheltered water along the village shoreline makes for easy paddling. Some guesthouses lend or rent kayaks.

**Garifuna drumming** Drumming is part of daily life in Hopkins, not a tourist attraction. Dügü and punta rhythms are practiced and performed regularly. If you spend a few days here you'll hear it; ask locals if you want to know when something is happening.

**Fishing** Local fishermen take visitors out on the reef and into the mangrove lagoons. Ask at the beach rather than booking through a hotel — you'll get a better price and a more genuine experience.

**Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary** About 30 km inland, Cockscomb is the world's first jaguar preserve and has good hiking trails through lowland rainforest. A day trip from Hopkins works well.

**Sittee River** A short ride south, the Sittee River is worth exploring by canoe — quiet water, birds, and mangroves.
