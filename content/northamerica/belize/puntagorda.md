Punta Gorda — "PG" to locals — is the southernmost town in Belize, a quiet port in the Toledo district with a strong Maya and Garifuna presence. It's off most tourist itineraries, which is exactly why some travelers seek it out.

The surrounding Toledo district is the most traditional part of Belize: Kekchi and Mopan Maya villages in the jungle interior, cacao farms, and waterfalls. The Toledo Ecotourism Association runs a village guesthouse program that places visitors in Maya and Garifuna communities — one of the more genuine cultural tourism experiences in the country.

Punta Gorda is also the departure point for water taxis to Guatemala: boats run to Puerto Barrios (about 1.5 hours) and Livingston.

The International Chocolate Festival in May celebrates the region's cacao heritage with tastings, demonstrations, and cacao-inspired food.
