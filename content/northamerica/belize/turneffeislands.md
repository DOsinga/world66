The Turneffe Islands are a large coral atoll about 40 km east of Belize City, enclosing a vast lagoon. Together with Lighthouse Reef (which includes the Blue Hole) and Glover's Reef to the south, they form three of the Caribbean's four true atolls.

Turneffe is primarily a diving destination. The walls and channels of the atoll offer some of the best dive sites in Belize — healthy coral, abundant fish life, hammerhead sharks, and excellent visibility. The atoll was designated a marine reserve in 2012. Accommodation is limited to a handful of dive lodges, and most visitors come on live-aboard boats or day trips from Ambergris Caye or Belize City.

Lighthouse Reef, further east, is where the Blue Hole sits — a 300-metre wide, 125-metre deep sinkhole made famous by Jacques Cousteau. It's a full-day boat trip from the coast and worth it for experienced divers, though the dive itself (mostly stalactites in the descent) is more dramatic in photographs than underwater.
