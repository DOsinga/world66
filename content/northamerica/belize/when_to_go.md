---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The dry season runs from late November through May. This is the peak tourism period, especially December to April, when temperatures are in the mid-20s Celsius (mid-70s Fahrenheit) and rainfall is rare. It's the best time for reef visibility and diving.

The wet season runs June through October, with heaviest rains in September and October — also hurricane season. Rain typically comes in afternoon downpours rather than all-day drizzle, so mornings are often fine for outdoor activities. The upside: accommodation prices drop significantly, and the jungle is lush and green.

November is shoulder season — the rains are tapering off, prices haven't yet peaked, and it's generally pleasant.

**Best for diving and snorkeling:** December through April, when seas are calm and visibility is best.

**Best for budget travel:** June through October, when rates drop and crowds thin out.

**Lobster season** opens July 1st, celebrated with festivals in San Pedro, Placencia, and Caye Caulker. If you're there, eat the lobster.

**Public holidays** when businesses may close include: New Year's Day, Baron Bliss Day (March 9), Good Friday and Easter, Labour Day (May 1), Commonwealth Day (May 24), National Day/St. George's Caye Day (September 10), Independence Day (September 21), Pan American Day (October 12), Garifuna Settlement Day (November 19), and Christmas/Boxing Day.
