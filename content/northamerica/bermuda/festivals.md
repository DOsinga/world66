---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

**Cup Match** (late July or early August) is the defining event of the Bermudian calendar. A two-day cricket match between the St. George's Cricket Club and the Somerset Cricket Club, it also marks Emancipation Day and Mary Prince Day — the public holidays honouring the abolition of slavery and Bermudian abolitionist Mary Prince. The match itself is almost secondary to the atmosphere: entire extended families take over the grounds, food stalls appear across the island, and Crown and Anchor gambling tables operate — the only time gambling is legal in Bermuda. If you are visiting in summer, plan your trip around Cup Match or plan to book very early.

**Bermuda Day** (May 24) celebrates the start of summer. The holiday includes a half-marathon road race run partly in traditional Bermudian costume, and a parade through Hamilton. It is a public holiday and the traditional first day of the year for swimming in the ocean.

**Bermuda Carnival** (June) is a newer tradition, launched in 2015 around National Heroes Day. A multi-day event that culminates in a "Revel de Road" parade, it brings together Caribbean-style costume, soca music, and revellers from across the island and the diaspora.

**Bermuda International Film Festival** (March) is a small but well-regarded festival that draws international independent films and occasional notable attendees.

**Harbour Nights** (summer Wednesdays) are informal street market evenings in Hamilton's Front Street area — vendors, food, music, and a generally festive atmosphere. A pleasant way to spend a Wednesday evening in town.
