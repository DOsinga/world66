Hamilton is the capital and commercial centre of Bermuda, with a population of around 1,000 in the city proper — making it one of the smallest capitals in the world by population, though much of the island's working population passes through daily. Front Street runs along the harbour and is the main commercial strip: department stores, duty-free shops, banks, and the visitors bureau. Cruise ships dock directly on Front Street, making the city immediately accessible from the pier.

The city is compact and walkable. The bus terminal on Washington Street is the hub for the island-wide bus network. Fort Hamilton, a 19th-century Victorian fort with gardens and underground passages, sits on a hill overlooking the harbour. Sessions House (Parliament) is also in the centre. The Birdcage at the junction of Front and Queen streets — where police once directed traffic in their distinctive white-helmeted Bermudian uniform — is an oft-photographed landmark.

Hamilton is a base rather than a destination in itself: the island's best beaches (Horseshoe Bay, Elbow Beach, Shelly Bay), the Crystal Caves in Hamilton Parish, and the National Museum of Bermuda at the Royal Naval Dockyard are all reachable by bus, ferry, or scooter from the city.
