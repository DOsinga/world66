---
title: "Sights"
type: section
---

Bermuda is small enough that you can cover most of its major sights in a long weekend, but rewarding enough that a week still feels short.

**St. George's** is the eastern anchor of the island and a UNESCO World Heritage Site — the oldest continually inhabited English town in the New World. The town centre is compact and walkable, with pastel-coloured buildings, a central square, old churches, and forts looking out over the harbour. St. Peter's Church dates to 1612. Fort St. Catherine, on the headland above the town, has a good museum of Bermudian history.

**The Royal Naval Dockyard**, at the western tip, is the island's other great historic complex. Built by the British Navy in the early 19th century using enslaved and convict labour, it now houses the National Museum of Bermuda — the best on the island — along with craft studios, restaurants, and a performance space. The ferry from Hamilton to the Dockyard is faster and more scenic than the bus.

**Gibbs Hill Lighthouse** in Southampton Parish has been operating since 1846. It is the oldest cast-iron lighthouse in the world. The 185-step climb is worth it for the views across the island. The lighthouse tearoom at the base is a good place to stop.

**Crystal Caves** near Hamilton Parish are genuine show-caves — stalactites, crystal-clear underground pools, and narrow walkways over illuminated water. The adjacent Fantasy Cave is similar but smaller. Worth a stop if you have not seen limestone cave systems before.

**The Bermuda Botanical Gardens** in Paget cover 36 acres of subtropical planting, including a cactus garden, an orchid house, and a garden for the visually impaired with labelled fragrant plants. Camden House, the official residence of the Premier, is on the grounds and opens for tours on certain days. Admission to the gardens is free.

**Horseshoe Bay** in Southampton is the most-photographed beach on the island, with pink sand and clear water. It gets crowded in summer but is stunning year-round. Connected smaller coves accessible by foot along the shore are quieter and equally beautiful.
