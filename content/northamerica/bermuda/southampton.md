Southampton is the westernmost parish of Bermuda, strung along the South Shore with some of the island's finest beaches. The parish is quiet and residential compared to Hamilton or St. George's, but the coastline is the reason to come.

Horseshoe Bay is the standout: a long arc of pink sand with red rock outcrops at either end, lifeguards in season, and equipment rentals for the water. Follow the shore path east from Horseshoe Bay and you'll find a series of smaller coves tucked between the rocks — quieter and with no facilities, connected by a clifftop trail. On Cup Match holiday (a public holiday in late July or August), local families camp on the beach with tents and it becomes a genuine street party.

South Shore Road runs the length of the parish and is easily covered on a rented moped — the standard way of getting around Bermuda.
