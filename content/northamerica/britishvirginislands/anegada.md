Anegada is the odd one out in the [British Virgin Islands](/northamerica/britishvirginislands). While the rest of the chain is volcanic and hilly, Anegada is flat coral and limestone, rising barely eight metres above sea level. It is surrounded by Horseshoe Reef, one of the largest barrier reefs in the Caribbean, which has claimed hundreds of ships over the centuries and now provides some of the finest snorkelling and diving in the territory.

The island has a single settlement, The Settlement, a scattering of pastel houses and a couple of beach bars along the south shore. The main attractions are the beaches — miles of empty white sand stretching along the north coast, with Loblolly Bay and Cow Wreck Beach the standouts. The water is shallow and turquoise for hundreds of metres out.

Anegada is famous for its lobster. The beach restaurants at Loblolly Bay and along the south shore serve it grilled, fresh from the reef, and you generally need to order by mid-afternoon so they can pull it in. The island also has a small population of Caribbean flamingos at the western salt ponds, and the rock iguana, found nowhere else, is slowly recovering thanks to a breeding programme.

Getting here takes some effort — a small ferry runs from [Tortola](/northamerica/britishvirginislands/tortola) or you can fly in on a tiny prop plane. That effort is the point. Anegada rewards people who want a Caribbean island that still feels like one.
