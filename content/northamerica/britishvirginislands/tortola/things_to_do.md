---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Tortola's best beaches are on the north coast. **Cane Garden Bay** is the standout — a long arc of calm water backed by a village with beach bars and the old Callwood Rum Distillery, where rum has been made continuously since the 1800s and you can taste what they're pouring. **Long Bay** on the west end is quieter and more exposed, with a long stretch of pale sand. **Smuggler's Cove**, at the far western tip, requires a short hike down a rough track but rewards with near-complete seclusion.

For history, **Mount Healthy National Park** preserves a sugar mill windmill from the 1700s — one of the better-preserved examples in the Caribbean. **Fort Recovery** near the west end is the oldest standing structure on the island; there's a small hotel on the grounds but the fort walls are accessible. William Thornton, who grew up at Thornton Plantation, went on to design the original US Capitol building in Washington.

Diving and snorkelling are excellent. The waters around the BVI offer wrecks, reefs, and the famous Rhone — a Royal Mail steamship that went down in an 1867 hurricane and is now one of the Caribbean's best dive sites, just off nearby Salt Island.

Road Town's **Main Street** is worth an hour on foot. The Virgin Islands Folk Museum covers the island's history from Arawak settlement through the plantation era. Bamboushay Pottery is one of the better souvenir stops in the islands, making locally inspired ceramics.
