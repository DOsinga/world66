Banff National Park rewards independent exploration, but guided tours unlock terrain and wildlife encounters that are hard to reach alone. Options range from half-day wildlife-spotting drives along the Bow Valley Parkway to multi-day backcountry horseback trips through the Rockies.

In summer, rafting on the Bow and Kicking Horse rivers is popular, as are guided hikes to remote alpine lakes. Winter brings dogsledding, snowshoeing, and heli-skiing above the treeline. Brewster (now part of Pursuit) is the longest-established operator in the Rockies and a reliable choice for coach tours to the Columbia Icefield.
