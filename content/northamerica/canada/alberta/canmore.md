Canmore sits in the Bow Valley just east of [Banff National Park](/northamerica/canada/alberta/banff), surrounded by the same spectacular Rocky Mountain scenery but without the park fees or the tour-bus crowds. What was once a coal mining town reinvented itself after hosting the 1988 Winter Olympics Nordic events, and has since become a year-round outdoor base that many Albertans prefer to Banff itself.

The town is walkable and well-equipped, with good restaurants along Main Street, independent gear shops, and enough coffee roasters to fuel a week of hiking. The trails start at the edge of town — Grassi Lakes, Ha Ling Peak, and the Canmore Nordic Centre trails are all accessible without a car. In winter, cross-country skiing, fat biking, and ice climbing take over.

The Three Sisters peaks dominate the skyline to the south and east, and the Bow River runs through the centre of town. Canmore is quieter and more local-feeling than Banff, with lower hotel prices and a growing arts scene. It is an excellent base for exploring the Canadian Rockies, with Banff, Lake Louise, and Kananaskis Country all within easy driving distance.
