Cold Lake is a city in northeastern Alberta, about 300 km northeast of Edmonton, best known for two things: Canada's largest air force base (4 Wing Cold Lake) and Cold Lake itself — one of the largest natural lakes in Alberta, which gives the city its name.

The military presence gives the city its restless character; a significant portion of the population is constantly rotating in or out, supplemented by workers from the nearby Alberta oil sands. For visitors, the lake is the main draw. Cold Lake Provincial Park on the southern shore offers swimming beaches, fishing, campgrounds, trails, and a lagoon. The lake has a local legend: the Kinosoo Beast, a creature of Lake Ness proportions said to lurk in its depths.

Winter activities dominate the outdoor calendar: snowmobiling, ice fishing, and skiing at Kinosoo Ridge are popular. The annual Maple Flag military exercise brings air forces from around the world and is one of the largest such exercises in NATO. Restaurants worth noting are the Hoof and Anchor and Clark's, both on Lakeshore Drive with views over the lake.
