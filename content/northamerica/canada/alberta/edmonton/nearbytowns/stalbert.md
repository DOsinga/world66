(Follow St. Albert Trail north) St. Albert is Alberta's oldest non-fortified community. Established in 1861, it was named after the patron saint of Father Albert Lacombe, one of the earliest and most influential missionaries in the West. St. Albert Place, on the banks of the Sturgeon River, encompasses the Arden Theatre; a library, arts and crafts facility, City Hall; and the Musee Heritage Museum, highly regarded for its exhibits coverting the city's rich history, from the Metis to the missionaries and pioneers.
