About an hour east of [Edmonton](/northamerica/canada/alberta/edmonton), Elk Island is one of Canada's only fully fenced national parks — and that fence exists to protect the animals inside, not keep them out. The park hosts free-ranging herds of both plains bison and wood bison, along with moose, deer, elk, and over 250 recorded bird species. It's a surprisingly accessible slice of wilderness, easy to reach from the city.

The park played a key role in saving the plains bison from extinction. In 1907, a herd was relocated here when the species was on the brink, and Elk Island has been supplying bison to reintroduction programs across the continent ever since. You can often spot them grazing right beside the road.

Hiking trails range from short boardwalks through aspen groves to longer loops around the lakes. In summer, Astotin Lake is popular for canoeing and picnicking. In winter, cross-country skiing and snowshoeing take over, and the park is one of the best spots near Edmonton for stargazing — dark skies, no light pollution, and a good chance of catching the northern lights.
