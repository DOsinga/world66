Grande Prairie is the largest city in northwestern Alberta, a regional hub of about 65,000 people set in the Peace Country — a broad, rolling agricultural landscape that feels far removed from the Rocky Mountain scenery most visitors associate with the province. The economy runs on oil and gas, forestry, and farming, and the city has a practical, working character.

For travellers, Grande Prairie is primarily a stopover on the way to the Alaska Highway or the northern wilderness. The Philip J. Chicken Heritage Park has a collection of pioneer buildings, and the Grande Prairie Museum documents the region's homesteading history. Dinosaur enthusiasts will want to visit the Pipestone Creek bonebed nearby, one of the richest pachyrhinosaurus fossil sites in the world. In winter, the Northern Lights are frequently visible from the outskirts of town.
