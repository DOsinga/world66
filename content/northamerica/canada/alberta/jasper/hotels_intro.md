Jasper, Alberta, offers a small number of hotels, motels, hostels and camping locations. Although the area is not as busy as nearby Banff and Lake Louise, Jasper is still a tourist destination. **Jasper Hotels **fill up quickly and reservations should be made early for the peak summer season.

Almost all Jasper hotels are a short drive or bike ride, in some cases, from the downtown area. Taxis are available for those without transportation. Similar to Banff, Jasper is located within Jasper National Park. All visitors need to purchase passes to enter the park, regardless of the length of stay.

#### **Hotels and Motels**

At the top of the price range is the Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge. Tucked away but close to town, the lodge has its own golf course.

Those wanting the familiarity of staying at a chain will be challenged in Jasper. The Fairmont is one of the few chain hotels. Located at the edge of the downtown area, the Best Western Jasper Inn and Suites** **is the second. Located on nearby Pyramid Lake, the Coast Pyramid Lake Resort** **is the third and offers lakefront accommodations and activities such as canoes, kayaks and a small beach.

Jasper offers a number of lodge-style hotels with lakefront activities and cabin-style motels for traveling families. For example, the Patricia Lake Bungalows** **offer small cabins with kitchens and barbecues. A nearby sister facility, Grove at Patricia Lake, offers slightly newer accommodations. All have lake and mountain views.

#### **Hostels**

Hostelling International has a location in Jasper off Patricia Lake Road. There are free winter shuttles from town to the hostel. Activities are available for those staying at the hostel, including trips to a nearby glacier, horseback riding and skiing.

Other nearby HI hostels include Beauty Creek and Athabasca Falls (both between Banff and Jasper), Mount Edith Cavell (just south of Jasper) and Maligne Canyon (north of Jasper). Those interested in seeing the area could do so easily by spending a few nights at each hostel.

#### **Camping**

There are ten campgrounds with over 1,700 campsites in Jasper National Park. As with hotels, camp sites also fill up quickly. Reservations are available and should be pursued for summer travel. The campgrounds have different fees and opening and closing dates.

Campgrounds Whistlers, Wapiti, Wabasso and Wilcox Creek all have trash dumping available. Only Whistlers and Wapiti offer showers. Winter camping is only available at the Wapiti campground.
