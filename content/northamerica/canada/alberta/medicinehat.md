Medicine Hat sits in the dry southeast corner of Alberta, perched above the South Saskatchewan River valley. Rudyard Kipling famously described it as a city "with all hell for a basement" — a reference to the vast natural gas reserves that lie beneath it and gave the city its nickname, the Gas City.

The setting is dramatic prairie: wide skies, coulees, and the river cutting through town. The Medalta historic clay district preserves the old pottery factories that once made Medicine Hat a ceramics centre, and the riverside parks are pleasant for walking. Dinosaur Provincial Park, one of the richest dinosaur fossil sites in the world, is about an hour's drive northwest.
