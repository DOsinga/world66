Mt. Louis is one of the Canadian Rockies' most iconic rock climbs — a sheer tower of limestone rising to 2,682 metres that only a dozen or so climbers summit each year. Rated 5.7 on the Yosemite Decimal Scale, the route demands solid trad climbing skills and a head for exposure.

The approach starts from the Trans-Canada Highway about 8 km west of Banff. A 2.5-hour hike through subalpine forest brings you to the base, and from there the climb to the summit takes roughly 10 hours round trip. The views from the top — across the Bow Valley and the surrounding peaks — are extraordinary, but this is a climb you earn.
