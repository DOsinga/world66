About an hour east of Edmonton, Elk Island is one of Canada's only fully fenced national parks — and that fence exists to protect the animals inside, not keep them out. The park hosts free-ranging herds of both plains bison and wood bison, along with moose, deer, elk, and over 250 recorded bird species. It's a surprisingly accessible slice of wilderness, easy to reach from the city.
