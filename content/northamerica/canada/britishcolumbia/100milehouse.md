100 Mile House is a small town in the Cariboo region of [British Columbia](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia), named for its position on the old Cariboo Wagon Road, 100 miles north of Lillooet (Mile Zero). It is a ranching and forestry town that serves as a gateway to some of the best cross-country skiing and lake fishing in the province.

The area around 100 Mile House has over 100 lakes within a short drive, making it a popular destination for trout fishing in summer and ice fishing in winter. The town bills itself as the "Handcrafted Log Home Capital of North America," and you will see evidence of this in the impressive log buildings around the area. The 100 Mile Marsh, right on the edge of town, is a good spot for birdwatching, with nesting grebes, herons, and various waterfowl.

In winter, the Cariboo region receives reliable dry snow, and the 100 Mile Nordics cross-country ski club maintains an extensive network of groomed trails. The town is a convenient stop on the drive between [Kamloops](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/kamploops) and the northern Cariboo. Guest ranches in the surrounding hills offer horseback riding and a taste of the ranching life that still defines this part of British Columbia.
