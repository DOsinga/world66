---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

The tourism development in the wake of Kicking Horse Mountain Resort
attracted some fine dining places to this previously pre-dominantly
blue collar railway and forestry town. Although you are visiting a
friendly small town, you will be able to experience some "haute
cuisine".

In my opinion the top spot belongs to "**Eagle's Eye**", and that's
not only because it's at an elevation of 7,700 ft. Canada's highest
full-service mountain top restaurant. Of course, the scenic location
comes into play, but particularly the dinner menu offers some excellent
culinary art.

My #2 is the cosy "**Cedar House Café & Restaurant**" on a hillside overlooking the Columbia River wetlands a couple of minutes south of Golden. The "**The Kicking Horse Grill**" and "**ELEVEN22 Grill & Liquids**" are certainly some other restaurants to explore at the south-side of town, as is "**The Turning Point Restaurant**", a favourite with the locals.

In downtown Golden, north of the Kicking Horse bridge, "**Apostoles Restoranté**" is worth a visit, as is "**The Canyon's Edge Steakhouse & Grill**" near the east entrance to town, offering as a bonus great views accross the Columbia Valley.

A little bit more off the beaten path, but also not to be missed, are the restaurants at "**Glenogle Mountain Lodge and Spa**" and the "**Timber Inn**".

Good thing that Golden offers so many summer and winter outdoor adventure activities, to work off all those calories.
