Prince George is the largest city in northern [British Columbia](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia), sitting at the confluence of the Fraser and Nechako Rivers roughly in the geographic centre of the province. With a population of about 75,000, it is the service hub for a vast region of forests, mountains, and lakes, and an important stop on any drive through the BC interior.

The city is a forestry town at heart, and the air sometimes carries the scent of the pulp mills, but Prince George has worked hard to develop its cultural side. The Two Rivers Gallery downtown has a good collection of contemporary Canadian art. The Exploration Place, a science and history museum in Fort George Park on the Fraser River, covers the region's natural history and the cultures of the Lheidli T'enneh people who have lived at this confluence for thousands of years. Fort George Park itself is a pleasant spot for a walk along the river.

Prince George is a practical overnight stop on the long drives between [Kamloops](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/kamploops) and Prince Rupert or the Alaska Highway. The Yellowhead Highway heads west through the Lakes District toward the coast, while Highway 97 continues north to Dawson Creek and the start of the Alaska Highway. In winter, the nearby Purden Lake and Tabor Mountain ski areas offer affordable skiing without the crowds of the southern resorts. The city has a decent selection of restaurants along the downtown core, with a growing number of locally owned cafes and brewpubs.
