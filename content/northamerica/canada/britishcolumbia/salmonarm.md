Salmon Arm sits on the long fjord-like southern arm of **Shuswap Lake**, in the rolling interior plateau of British Columbia between the Okanagan and the Rockies. The town itself is a small commercial centre — a single main street rising from the lakeshore — but the setting is the draw: a wide sheet of clear cold water rimmed by forested ridges, with one of the longest freshwater wharves in Canada running out into the bay. The lake is the centre of the local houseboat economy, and in summer the marinas on every arm rent out floating cabins for week-long cruises through the Shuswap's quiet coves.

The other thing Salmon Arm is known for is salmon. Every four years (the next dominant run is well-publicised locally) the **Adams River sockeye run** at nearby Roderick Haig-Brown Provincial Park turns the river red with millions of returning fish — one of the great wildlife spectacles in North America. Even in off-years a smaller run comes through every autumn. Off the lake, the surrounding hills are good walking and cycling country, with the **Larch Hills** Nordic ski trails turning into mountain-bike country in summer.
