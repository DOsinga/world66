Once you are in the Kootenays you will start enjoying yourself and the scenery. I spent a month in the Kootenays and surrounding areas. Kootenay National Park has so much to offer in itself for the outdoor lovers, I would highly recommend Kootenay National Park for backpackers with its hiking trails (Fireweed Trail), fishing, camping and about all you would ever want in a park. Make sure you have a good camera for all the wildlife you will see along the trails and off. While you are in the area visit Ainsworth, BC. Ainsworth is a very small town. There's a very small unique hot springs. Ainsworth hot springs has a horseshoe shaped cave that has continuous hot water filling it with about waist high, soothing mineral filled water and a cold plunge that is great in the winter months. The water from the cave goes into a pool. I would suggest taking a trip to the Kootenays in BC if you have about a week to enjoy it.

For more information on Kootenay National Park, including an on-line narrated video tour of the park's features, visit Kootenay National Park
