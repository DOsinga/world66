---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Vancouver has a string of usable beaches around the downtown peninsula and along the West Side. The water rarely warms past 18°C even in August — locals tend to swim, kids cheerfully splash, and adults mostly read on the sand and admire the view of the North Shore mountains.

**English Bay Beach** in the West End is the most central — a wide sandy strip with logs to lean against and one of the city's best sunset views. The annual Celebration of Light fireworks festival happens just offshore. **Sunset Beach**, a short walk south along the seawall, is quieter.

**Kitsilano Beach** ("Kits Beach"), across English Bay on the south side, is the social one — beach volleyball courts, a heated saltwater pool, and easy access to the Kitsilano restaurant strip. **Jericho Beach** and **Spanish Banks**, further west, are larger and quieter, popular with families and dog walkers. **Wreck Beach**, below the cliffs at UBC, is the city's clothing-optional beach — a serious set of stairs each way and a relaxed crowd at the bottom.

In Stanley Park, **Second Beach** and **Third Beach** are sheltered and have grass and picnic areas behind the sand.
