---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Vancouver is one of the most transit-friendly cities in North America. **TransLink** runs the integrated bus, SkyTrain (driverless rapid transit) and SeaBus (passenger ferry to North Vancouver) network on a single fare. Three SkyTrain lines cover the airport, downtown, the eastern suburbs and Surrey; the **Canada Line** connects the airport, Richmond and downtown and is the obvious airport transfer. A Compass Card or contactless tap-to-pay works on every mode.

Downtown and the West End are walkable. The **seawall** — a continuous 28km path around the downtown peninsula and through Stanley Park — is the city's most popular cycling route, and **Mobi** bike-share docks are well distributed. **Aquabus** and **False Creek Ferries** are small passenger boats that run between Granville Island, Yaletown, Olympic Village and Science World; they double as a fun, cheap way to get around.

A car is more useful for trips up the **Sea-to-Sky Highway** to Whistler, across to Vancouver Island via ferry, or up the Sunshine Coast — but is a liability downtown. Parking is expensive and street parking is tightly enforced.
