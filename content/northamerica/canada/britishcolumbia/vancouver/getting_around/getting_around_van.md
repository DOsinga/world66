**Getting around Vancouver is easy with it's integrated transit system. The transit system in Vancouver is all overseen by Translink. The sytem has 3 main components, Skytrain, Buses and the Seabus.

The Skytrain is a light rail driverless rapid transit system that travels both underground and on elevated tracks and bridges. The majority of the tracks are elevated and provide riders with some great views of the city.

The Seabus is a passenger only catamaran that sails between downtown Vancouver and Lonsdale Quay in North Vancouver in under 12 minutes and operate every 15 minutes. The Seabus also offers some great view of the Vancouver Skyline, Stanley Park and the Lions Gate Bridge.

On top of this there is an extensive bus system. That provides service throughout the city and far into the outlying communities.

You can purchase fares at the Skytrain Stations or on buses. If you are purchasing a fare on the buses you must have the exact coin fare ready, however on Skytrain you can use paper currency and debit/credit cards.
Single fares are valid in any direction for 90 minutes, however, ****during weekdays until 6:30pm****, prices vary depending on how many zones you are traveling through .

A good option for travelers is to purchase a day pass which is valid for unlimited travel through all 3 zones all day long. You can use the same fare on all 3 modes of transit.
