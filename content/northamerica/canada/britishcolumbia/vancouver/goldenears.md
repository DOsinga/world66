Golden Ears Provincial Park is one of the largest parks in British Columbia, about an hour's drive east of [Vancouver](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/vancouver). The park takes its name from the twin peaks of Mount Blanshard, which catch the sunlight and glow gold in the right conditions.

The park offers everything from easy lakeside walks to serious mountaineering. Alouette Lake is the main draw in summer — the water is surprisingly warm for the region and the swimming is excellent. The Golden Ears trail climbs to the summit ridge and rewards with panoramic views of the Coast Mountains. There are good campgrounds, and the park is popular enough on summer weekends that arriving early is wise.
