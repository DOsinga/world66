Major exhibits thrill all ages with exciting tales of the sea. The Children’s Maritime Discovery Centre explores the maritime world with hands-on activities. Life on the historic ex-RCMP schooner St. Roch, first ship to transit the treacherous Northwest Passage in both directions, is examined through a video and self-guided tour. Continuing Man the Oars, Map the Coast Pays tribute to the voyages of Capt. George Vancouver as he charted the Pacific coastline over 200 years ago. Warships Named Vancouver: Saga of the Sudburys The story of B.C.'s great salvage tugs. Fireboats! Artifacts and exhibits chronicle six decades of Vancouver’s working fire boats. Pirates Cove Tells tales of pirates from around the world. Heritage Harbour hosts local heritage vessels and is a stop for False Creek Ferries. $6, s/s $3, family $14, 5 and under free.
