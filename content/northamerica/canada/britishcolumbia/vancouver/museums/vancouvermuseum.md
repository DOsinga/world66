The city comes to life at the Orientation Gallery, which highlights Vancouver’s history, heritage and scenery. A multilayered Timeline Wall locates Vancouver in time using artifacts from the Museum’s collection. To Jan. 5 Through My Eyes: Northwest Coast Artifacts through the Eyes of Contemporary First Nations People takes you inside carved boxes, underneath baskets and behind masks to discover the artistry, history and significance of First Nations artifacts. From mid-Apr. Backyard BiodiverCity Explores the relationship between nature and urban space. To Apr. 16 City Lights: Neon in Vancouver Dazzling display of commercial neon art from the 1940s to '60s. Continuing Toy Soldiers: Playing for Keeps 1,000 miniature figures evoke the pageantry of British Army traditions. All About Blue: the Colour that Changed the World Objects ranging from ancient cobalt porcelains to contemporary indigo fashions trace the history of blue from a cross-cultural perspective. Gift shop specializes in First Nations art and jewellery. Open daily 10 a.m.-5 p.m.; Thursdays until 9 p.m. $8, youth (under 19) $5.50, one-day pass for group of five, $25. Ample parking. Located in Vanier Park, south over Burrard Bridge from downtown.
