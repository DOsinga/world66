New Westminster is western Canada's oldest city and was the first capital of [British Columbia](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia). It sits along the north bank of the Fraser River, about 25 km east of [Vancouver](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/vancouver), and is easily reached by SkyTrain.

The waterfront area around the Quay is the main draw, with the River Market, views across the Fraser and a boardwalk along the shore. The city's Columbia Street historic district retains a number of early twentieth-century commercial buildings, and the residential neighbourhoods on the hill above have fine Victorian and Edwardian houses. New Westminster feels like a distinct city rather than a Vancouver suburb — it has its own character, its own downtown, and a proud sense of its history as the original capital.
