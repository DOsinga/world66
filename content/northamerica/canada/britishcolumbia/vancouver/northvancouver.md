North Vancouver sits on the north shore of Burrard Inlet, about 8 km from downtown [Vancouver](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/vancouver) and backed by the Coast Mountains. Access is via the Lions Gate Bridge, the Second Narrows Bridge, or the SeaBus passenger ferry from Waterfront Station — which is itself a pleasant crossing with views of the harbour and skyline.

Most visitors come for the mountain attractions. The Capilano Suspension Bridge draws crowds to its swaying span 70 metres above the Capilano River, while the nearby salmon hatchery offers a free look at the fish lifecycle. The Grouse Mountain Skyride gondola climbs to a summit with hiking trails, a wildlife refuge and city views that stretch to Vancouver Island on a clear day. In winter, Grouse and neighbouring mountains offer the closest skiing to a major Canadian city.

The Lower Lonsdale waterfront area has developed into a destination in its own right, with the Lonsdale Quay public market, breweries and restaurants along the shipyard district. The North Vancouver Museum and Archives on West 4th Street covers the area's logging, shipbuilding and transport history.
