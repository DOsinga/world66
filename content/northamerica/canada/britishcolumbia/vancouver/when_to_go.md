---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Vancouver's reputation for rain is largely earned — between November and March it rains most days, and the cloud can sit low over the city for weeks. The compensation is mild winters: snow is rare downtown, and the Coast Mountains keep the local ski resorts (Cypress, Grouse, Mount Seymour) running with reliable powder a 30-minute drive from the city centre.

**July, August and September** are the prime months — long, dry, sunny days with highs around 22–25°C, the seawall packed with cyclists and walkers, beaches busy and outdoor patios full. **June** is generally pleasant but can be cool and damp early on. **October** through early November can have brilliant clear days between fronts.

For winter sports, **December through March** is the ski season; for whale watching off the coast, **May through October** is the best window. The **Celebration of Light** international fireworks festival lights up English Bay over three nights at the end of July.
