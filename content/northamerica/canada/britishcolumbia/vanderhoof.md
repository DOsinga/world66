Vanderhoof sits at the geographical centre of British Columbia, a small ranching and forestry town on the Nechako River. It is not a destination in itself, but it makes a useful stop on the drive between Prince George and the lakes district to the west.

The surrounding countryside is big, open rangeland backed by mountains. The Nechako Bird Sanctuary on the river flats attracts migrating trumpeter swans and Canada geese in spring and fall. The town's Heritage Village gives a sense of what life was like for early settlers in this remote part of the province.
