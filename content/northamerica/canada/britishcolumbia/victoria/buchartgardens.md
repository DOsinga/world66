Located on Vancouver Island about twenty minutes north of Victoria, Butchart Gardens covers fifty-five acres of floral display carved out of a worked-out limestone quarry. Jennie Butchart started transforming the barren pit in 1904, and today it includes a sunken garden, rose garden, Japanese garden, and Italian garden, all connected by winding paths past fountains and ponds. The sunken garden — built in the original quarry — is the showpiece. Summer evenings bring fireworks on Saturdays and outdoor concerts. Expect crowds in July and August, but even then the sheer scale absorbs them.
