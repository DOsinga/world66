A 39-room sandstone pile built in the late 1880s for Robert Dunsmuir, the Scottish coal baron who was at one point the richest man in British Columbia. Dunsmuir died months before it was finished and never lived in it. The interior has been restored to its Victorian state — stained glass, tiled fireplaces, ornate woodwork — and the tower at the top gives a view back over the city to the Olympic Mountains. Ten minutes from downtown.
