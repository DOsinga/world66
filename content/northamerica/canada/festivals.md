---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Canada has a strong festival culture, particularly in summer when cities compensate for the long winter with packed outdoor calendars.

**Winter**

Québec Winter Carnival (Carnaval de Québec) in Québec City, late January to mid-February, is one of the world's largest winter festivals. Ice sculptures, snow bathing (really), canoe races on the St. Lawrence, and parades fill two-plus weeks. Dress extremely warmly.

Ottawa's Winterlude runs in February across three weekends. The Rideau Canal becomes the world's longest skating rink — 7.8 km of maintained ice. There's also snow sculpture and ice sculpture, plus family activities.

**Spring/Summer**

Canadian Tulip Festival in Ottawa, May — nearly a million tulips bloom across the city. A gift from the Netherlands, dating to the Second World War.

Montreal Jazz Festival, late June to early July, is one of the world's largest music festivals. Most outdoor shows are free; indoor concerts require tickets.

Calgary Stampede, July — ten days of rodeo, chuck wagon races, midway, and concerts. "The Greatest Outdoor Show on Earth" draws over a million visitors. Book accommodation months in advance.

Just for Laughs (Juste pour rire) Comedy Festival, Montréal, July. The world's largest comedy festival, with big-name stand-up, galas, and free street performances.

Pride Toronto, late June — one of the largest Pride celebrations in North America. The main parade draws over a million spectators.

Fringe Festivals: Edmonton's Fringe is the largest in North America, running in August. Toronto, Victoria, Winnipeg, and other cities also have Fringe festivals across the summer.

**Autumn**

Toronto International Film Festival (TIFF), September — one of the world's most important film festivals, and a major premiere destination. Industry credentials get you more access, but public screenings are available to anyone with tickets.

Festival du Voyageur, Winnipeg, February — celebrates Métis and French-Canadian heritage with music, food, and the Red River Jig.
