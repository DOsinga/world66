Brandon is Manitoba's second city, known as the "Wheat City" for its big-sky prairie landscape and agricultural roots. With a population of around 50,000, it has the feel of an overgrown small town — friendly, unhurried, and easy to get around. The Assiniboine River runs through the centre, and a pleasant Riverwalk follows its banks for walkers and cyclists.

The city is not a major tourist destination, but it has its charms. The Western Manitoba Centennial Auditorium hosts professional and amateur shows, and the Keystone Centre draws big-name concerts and exhibitions. The Brandon Folk Festival in July is worth timing a visit around. The Art Gallery of Southwestern Manitoba and Brandon University's School of Music round out the cultural scene. For a stroll, the few blocks of Rosser Avenue between 4th and 11th streets have some good independent shops.
