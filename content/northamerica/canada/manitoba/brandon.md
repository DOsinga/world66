Brandon is Manitoba's second city, about 200 km west of Winnipeg on the Trans-Canada Highway, and serves as the commercial hub of southwestern Manitoba. Known as the "Wheat City" for its agricultural roots, it has a pleasant small-city atmosphere with a population of around 50,000, a university, and enough infrastructure for a comfortable stop.

The Western Manitoba Centennial Auditorium hosts touring shows and concerts, and the Brandon Folk Music and Art Festival in July is a well-regarded regional event. The Art Gallery of Southwestern Manitoba has changing exhibitions. There are good walking and cycling paths along the Assiniboine River.

Getting around requires a car — Brandon is a sprawling prairie city. Most shopping is concentrated at Shoppers Mall at the south end of town, with the main commercial strip running along 18th Street.
