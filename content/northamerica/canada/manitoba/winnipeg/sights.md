### [The Forks National Historic Site](/northamerica/canada/manitoba/winnipeg/sights/theforksnationalhistoricsite)

The site includes a 9-acre (5.5 hectare) interpretive park and scenic
river walkway along the Red River, toboggan slides and skating rink. On
the adjacent property is the Manitoba Children's Museum 956-KIDS, the
Manitoba Travel Idea Centre 945-3777, the Forks Market and Johnston
Terminal. Festival Park has a new permanent stage for events and
festivals. Hotline 957-7618. Address: Off Main St at Water Ave, Forks
Events Winnipeg Manitoba

### [Legislative Building](/northamerica/canada/manitoba/winnipeg/sights/legislativebuilding)

This stately neo-classical structure, constructed of local Tyndall
stone, rises above 12 hectares of gardens, and is topped by the famous
Golden Boy statue. Guide books available for self-guided tours from 8
am - 8 pm Mon-Fri. Free guided tours by reservation only.

type: Public Buildings



[[rate it]](/northamerica/canada/manitoba/winnipeg/sights/legislativebuilding)

address: Broadway and Osborne St., Winnipeg Manitoba

tel: 1 (204) 945-5813

### [Assiniboine Park Zoo](/northamerica/canada/manitoba/winnipeg/sights/assiniboineparkzoo)

The animals of The Assiniboine Park Zoo include representatives of
insect, fish, bird, and mammal groups. Over 237 species are housed here
with more than 1300 specimens exhibited. Emphasis is placed upon
acquisition and propagation of rare and endangered species. The
Assiniboine Park Zoo is open every day of the year and provides, a
variety of programs such as 'The Lights of the Wild', Reindeer Sleigh
Rides, interpretive play structures, a Zootique Gift Shop, and a Zoo
Classroom running interpretive programs for students.

type: Parks, Gardens and Zoos
