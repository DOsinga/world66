Fundy National Park preserves the last remaining wilderness in southern New Brunswick. A special feature of the park is the Bay of Fundy - where the tidal fluctuations are the world's highest (up to 12 metres difference between low and high tide). Over 200 species of birds have been observed in the park. For more information, plus a virtual tour of park features, visit Fundy National Park.
