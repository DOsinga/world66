At the very northern tip of [Newfoundland](/northamerica/canada/newfoundland), L'Anse aux Meadows is the earliest known European settlement in the New World — a Norse outpost dating to around 1000 AD, five centuries before Columbus. The site was discovered in 1960 by Norwegian explorers Helge and Anne Stine Ingstad and became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1978.

Reconstructions of three Norse sod buildings give a tangible sense of how Leif Erikson's crew lived and worked here. The visitor centre has exhibits on the Viking lifestyle and the archaeological excavation, and costumed interpreters bring the settlement to life in summer. The surrounding landscape is stark and beautiful — windswept headlands, icebergs drifting past in spring, and hiking trails leading to quiet bays. Getting here requires a long drive up the Great Northern Peninsula, but the remoteness is part of the experience.
