Gros Morne National Park was declared a World Heritage Site in 1987.
The park is home to 20 species of land mammals (including moose, black
bear, fox, caribou and beaver), over 230 species of birds, 11 species
of fish, 2 amphibian species, 417 species of bryophytes and 711 species
of vascular plants. One study estimates that over 7700 moose live in
the park. For more information on the park, including a picture tour of
park features, visit Gros Morne Park National Park.

The Park is a higlight for small ship cruises in Atlantic
Canada. One of the world's best kept secrets for exploring the
fjords, bays and tickles of Newfoundland and Labrador.
