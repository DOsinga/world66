Inuvik sits at the end of the Dempster Highway in Canada's Northwest Territories — the northernmost point you can reach by public road in North America. It is a small town of about 3,000 people on the Mackenzie River delta, built in the 1950s as an administrative centre and now serving as the main hub for the western Arctic.

The town is above the treeline and above the Arctic Circle. In summer the midnight sun shines for 56 days straight, and in winter the sun disappears for 30 days. The igloo-shaped Our Lady of Victory Church is the town's most distinctive building. The Great Northern Arts Festival in July draws artists from across the Arctic.

Most visitors come for the Dempster Highway itself — one of the great drives in North America, 740 kilometres of gravel road across Arctic tundra from Dawson City in the Yukon. Inuvik is also a staging point for trips to Tuktoyaktuk on the Arctic Ocean, reachable by the all-season highway completed in 2017.
