Cape Breton Island is connected to mainland Nova Scotia by a causeway at the Strait of Canso. It is home to some of the most dramatic scenery in eastern Canada, particularly along the Cabot Trail, a 300-kilometre loop through the Cape Breton Highlands National Park.

The island has a distinct cultural identity rooted in its Gaelic, Acadian, and Mi'kmaq communities. Celtic music is not a tourist attraction here but a living tradition, with fiddle sessions and ceilidhs in community halls throughout the summer. The Fortress of Louisbourg on the eastern shore is one of the largest historical reconstructions in North America.
