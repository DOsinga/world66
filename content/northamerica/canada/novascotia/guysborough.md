Guysborough is a small town on the Eastern Shore of [Nova Scotia](/northamerica/canada/novascotia), sitting at the head of Chedabucto Bay. One of the oldest settlements in the province, it dates back to the 1600s and has a long history tied to the fishing trade. Today it is a quiet, scenic stop on the drive between [Halifax](/northamerica/canada/novascotia/halifax) and Cape Breton, with a population of only a few hundred.

The town's main draw is the Old Court House Museum, which covers the area's Mi'kmaq, Acadian, Black Loyalist, and Scottish heritage. The waterfront is pleasant for a stroll, and the harbour still has a working fishing fleet. Chedabucto Bay itself offers good sea kayaking and birdwatching, particularly during the spring and fall migrations.

If you are driving the eastern shore, Guysborough makes a natural overnight stop. The surrounding countryside is rolling and forested, with hiking trails in the nearby hills and a handful of quiet beaches along the coast. The Guysborough Trail, part of the Trans Canada Trail, follows a former rail line and is popular with cyclists and hikers alike. From here it is about two hours to the Canso Causeway and the start of Cape Breton Island.
