---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Halifax sits at the crossroads of Nova Scotia, making it an excellent base for exploring the province. Most of these trips are an easy one to two hour drive.

**Peggy's Cove** is the classic day trip, just 45 minutes southwest along the coast. The drive itself is scenic, and the lighthouse is as photogenic as advertised. Go early or late to avoid the tour buses.

**Cape Split and Blomidon Provincial Park** are about two hours north. The hike to the tip of Cape Split rewards you with panoramic views of the Bay of Fundy's Minas Basin. Blomidon has similarly dramatic trails along the cliff edge.

**McNabs Island** is accessible by boat right from the Halifax waterfront. Nearly a thousand acres of trails, old forts, and wildlife, with no cars and few people. A genuine escape without leaving the city.

**Lawrencetown and Martinique Beaches** are the go-to surf spots, north of Halifax on the Eastern Shore. Lawrencetown has reliable waves and southern exposure; Martinique is three times the size with lifeguards in summer.

For swimming in warmer water, head inland to **Shubenacadie Grand Lake** or **Porters Lake**, both within 30 minutes of the city. Bring a picnic.
