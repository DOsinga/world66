Iqaluit is the capital of [Nunavut](/northamerica/canada/nunavut) and the gateway to the Eastern Arctic. Formerly known as Frobisher Bay, it sits on the southeastern coast of Baffin Island and is home to about 8,000 people — a growing city by Arctic standards, but still a place where the tundra begins at the edge of town.

St Jude's Anglican Cathedral, shaped like an igloo and decorated with local Inuit crafts, is the most distinctive building. The Nunnatta Sunaquatangit Museum has a fine collection of Inuit sculptures, prints, and artefacts, and the Government of Nunavut Building houses an excellent display of northern art. The Baffin Regional Visitors Centre provides good context on the life and culture of the South Baffin region.

Katannilik Territorial Park Reserve is an easy excursion from town. For the more ambitious, Iqaluit is the jumping-off point for trips deeper into the Arctic, including Auyuittuq National Park on the Cumberland Peninsula.
