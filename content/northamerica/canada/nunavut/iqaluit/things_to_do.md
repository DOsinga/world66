---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Arctic landscape itself is the main attraction. Hiking the tundra around Iqaluit is rewarding from June to September, when the days are very long and wildflowers dot the otherwise stark terrain. In winter, snowmobiling and dog-sledding are popular.

**Quttinirpaaq National Park**, on the northern tip of Ellesmere Island, is one of the most remote parks on Earth — glaciers, ice caps, polar desert, and even a thermal oasis. Getting there requires a charter flight and serious expedition planning, but for those who make it, the experience is unforgettable.
