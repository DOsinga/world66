Resolute is one of the most northerly communities in Canada, a tiny Inuit hamlet of roughly 200 people on the south coast of Cornwallis Island. At nearly 75 degrees north, it sits in the heart of the High Arctic, where winter darkness lasts months and summer brings 24-hour daylight.

The hamlet exists largely because of science and exploration. It is home to the Polar Continental Shelf Project research station and a weather station, and serves as a staging point for expeditions to the North Pole, typically launched in spring when conditions are most favourable. Visitors come for the sheer remoteness, the chance to see Arctic wildlife like muskoxen and polar bears, and the otherworldly landscape of ice and tundra stretching to the horizon.

Getting here requires a flight from Yellowknife or Iqaluit via Canadian North. There are no roads connecting Resolute to anywhere else. Accommodation is extremely limited, so plan well ahead.
