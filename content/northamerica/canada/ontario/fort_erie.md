Fort Erie sits at the southern tip of the Niagara Peninsula, directly across the Peace Bridge from Buffalo, New York. It is a small town in the shadow of its bigger neighbours — Niagara Falls to the north, Buffalo across the river — but has its own quiet identity as a lakeside community with good beaches and a border-town character that sets it apart from the rest of Ontario.

Crystal Beach on the Lake Erie shore was once the site of a major amusement park that drew crowds from both sides of the border. The park is long gone, but the beach area remains pleasant in summer and offers views across to the Buffalo skyline at night. The Friendship Festival, held over the Canada Day and Fourth of July weekend in early July, is the social highlight of the year — a bi-national celebration centred on the Mather Arch near the Peace Bridge. The Fort Erie Slots and Racetrack on Thompson Road caters to those interested in gaming.
