Hamilton is a mid-sized city at the western end of Lake Ontario, about an hour from Toronto. It built its identity on steel — Stelco and Dofasco still dominate the eastern waterfront — but has diversified considerably since the industry's decline, with a growing arts scene and revitalized downtown.

The Niagara Escarpment runs through the city, creating the dramatic geography that gives Hamilton over 100 waterfalls within its city limits, more per capita than anywhere in the world. Dundurn Castle, the Canadian Warplane Heritage Museum, and HMCS Haida are the main historic attractions. The Royal Botanical Gardens at the Burlington border is worth a half-day. The city is 70km from Niagara Falls.
