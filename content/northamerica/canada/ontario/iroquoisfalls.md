Iroquois Falls is a small town of about 4,500 in northeastern Ontario, on the Abitibi River about 90 km east of Timmins. It was built as a company town in the early 20th century around a pulp and paper mill — the Abitibi Power and Paper Company founded it in 1913, and the mill operated for most of the century. Like many single-industry towns in northern Ontario, it has struggled since the mill's decline.

The town has an unusual distinction: it is one of the best-preserved examples of early 20th century company town planning in Ontario, with a grid of streets laid out with workers' housing, a community centre, and civic buildings. The Iroquois Falls Museum documents this history. For travellers in northern Ontario, the town is a practical stop on the route through the region, and the Abitibi River offers fishing and paddling. The Rene Brunelle Provincial Park nearby has camping and good boreal forest walking.
