London sits at the confluence of the Thames and its north branch in southwestern Ontario, a mid-sized university city that punches above its weight on arts and food. Western University brings about 30,000 students into the city, keeping the downtown core and Richmond Row lively year-round.

The city's nickname — the Forest City — isn't marketing. Tree cover is genuinely exceptional, and Victoria Park downtown is one of the better city parks in Ontario, with a bandstand used for concerts and festivals from spring through fall. The Thames Valley Parkway runs along both branches of the river, offering an extended cycling and walking path that threads through residential neighbourhoods and green corridors.

Downtown London is compact and walkable. Richmond Row is the main strip for eating and nightlife, stretching north from the core. Covent Garden Market, a covered market near the main square, is worth a visit for local produce and prepared food. Old East Village is the neighbourhood to know for independent restaurants and coffee shops.

London is a reasonable stop between Toronto and Windsor, or as a base for exploring the Lake Erie shore and the farm country of southwestern Ontario.
