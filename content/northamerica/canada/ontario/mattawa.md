Mattawa is a small town of about 2,000 people at the confluence of the Mattawa and Ottawa rivers in northern Ontario. The name comes from an Algonquin word meaning "meeting of the waters," and that sense of convergence — rivers, wilderness, and history — defines the place.

Samuel de Champlain passed through here in 1615 on his way west, following the old fur-trade route that French voyageurs paddled for two centuries. The Mattawa River itself is now a provincial park and a popular canoe route, offering wilderness paddling through a chain of lakes with portages following the original voyageur path.

The town sits on the edge of the Canadian Shield, surrounded by boreal forest and Canadian Shield lakes. It makes a reasonable base for canoe camping, fishing, and backcountry hiking in the area between Algonquin Provincial Park and the upper Ottawa River.
