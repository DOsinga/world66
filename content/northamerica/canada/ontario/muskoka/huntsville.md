Huntsville is the largest town in Muskoka, about two hours north of Toronto on Highway 11. It sits on the shores of several lakes and serves as the main gateway to Algonquin Provincial Park, which begins just to the east.

In summer, the town fills with cottagers and day-trippers drawn to the lakes, the hiking trails, and the Algonquin canoe routes. In winter, there is cross-country skiing and snowmobiling. The Huntsville Festival of the Arts runs each summer, and the main street has a pleasant stretch of cafes and shops.
