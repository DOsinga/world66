---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Niagara falls"
type: section
---

Niagara Falls is a naturally family friendly place, so the huge
amount of family friendly attractions in the area is really no surprise.

**Exploring the Falls**

One of the main activities that you must check out while in Niagara
Falls is a ride on the famous Maid of the Mist , a boat that will take
you close to the base of the falls. This attraction offers you and your
family an incredible and up-close view of each of the waterfalls--the
American Falls, the Bridal Veil Falls, and the Horseshoe Falls. Despite
the rain poncho given to you before you get on the Maid of the Mist,
you´ll definitely get soaked due to the overwhelming mist given off as
the water crashes on the rocks below. Both kids and adults will be
thrilled by this trip. Tickets cost $14.50 Canadian dollars for adults
and $8.90 for kids, which is a bit pricey, but worth every cent.

Journey Behind the Falls is another exhilarating Niagara Falls
adventure that you can share with your children. You´ll be taken down
150 feet in an elevator to a network of tunnels behind the Horseshoe
Falls. The tunnels lead out to 2 different observation areas, 1
with a side view of the falls and the other with a view of the falls from behind,
where you can watch the heavy water fall and crash upon the rocks. It
is a spectacular sight even in winter, when you can observe the various
ice formations at the base of the Horseshoe Falls.

**Gardens and Butterflies**

There are other beautiful sights to be seen in Niagara Falls besides
the falls themselves. The Botanical Garden, created in 1936, is filled
with gorgeous, colorful flowers, ornamental plants, ponds, and winding,
tranquil paths. It is a vibrant area of natural beauty, known
particularly for its extensive and lush rose garden. Admission is free.

Within the Botanical Garden is the butterfly conservatory. After
watching a brief educational video about the butterflies, you walk into
the conservatory and see them for yourself. Over 2,000 brightly colored
butterflies fly all around you as you explore their lush, humid garden
home. The flowers and plants are just as interesting and beautiful to
observe as the butterflies. The entrance fee is $11.50 Canadian dollars
for adults and $6.80 for kids. On the way out, you and your family can
stop at the small gift shop to purchase some unique butterfly themed
souvenirs.
