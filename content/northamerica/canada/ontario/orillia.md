Orillia is a town of around 33,000 on the narrows between Lake Simcoe and Lake Couchiching in Simcoe County, about 130 kilometres north of Toronto. It bills itself as "Sunshine City" — a reference to the elevated, open character of the lakeside setting — and is a popular base for water-based recreation in summer: boating, fishing, swimming, and sailing on both lakes.

The town has two literary claims to fame. **Stephen Leacock**, Canada's best-known humourist and author of *Sunshine Sketches of a Little Town* (1912), lived here; the house is now the **Stephen Leacock Museum** and National Historic Site on Old Brewery Bay. The novel is a lightly disguised portrait of Orillia itself. The town also inspired Gordon Lightfoot, who grew up in nearby Orono but has strong connections to the area.

**Casino Rama**, on the Rama First Nation reserve adjacent to Orillia, is one of Ontario's largest casinos and a significant employer in the region.

Orillia makes a convenient stop on the drive north to Muskoka cottage country, or as a base for the Huronia region and the Martyr's Shrine at nearby Midland.
