---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Ottawa's diplomatic and bilingual character has produced one of Canada's more interesting dining scenes for a city its size. The best concentrations are in the **ByWard Market** (everything from oysters to shawarma), **Elgin Street** downtown, the strip of **Preston Street** known as Little Italy or Corso Italia, the Vietnamese and Chinese restaurants on **Somerset Street West**, and increasingly the independent kitchens of **Wellington West** and **Hintonburg**. Don't ignore Gatineau across the river either — Quebec licensing and culture mean a different feel and some excellent cheap French bistros.
