---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

A car is the most practical option for getting around the county — distances between villages are five to fifteen kilometres, and there's no reliable local transit. The roads are generally well-signed and in good condition.

Cycling is a genuine alternative for the fit and unhurried. The county is largely flat, roads are quiet outside summer weekends, and the distances between wineries, beaches, and villages are manageable. Bikes can be rented in Picton and Wellington during the warmer months. The main highway (County Road 33, known as the Loyalist Parkway) gets more traffic but most secondary roads are peaceful.
