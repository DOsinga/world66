Quinte West is a small city of about 45,000 at the western end of the Bay of Quinte, where the Trent River runs into Lake Ontario. It is a 2001 amalgamation of the old town of **Trenton** and the surrounding villages of Frankford, Sidney and Murray, and most travellers know it for two things: the southern terminus of the **Trent–Severn Waterway**, and **CFB Trenton**, the largest air base in the Canadian Forces.

The Trent–Severn is the reason to come. Boats begin (or end) the 386-kilometre canal-and-river journey from Lake Ontario to Lake Huron right in downtown Trenton at Lock 1, and the locks here are pleasant places to watch summer traffic. The waterfront has been tidied up with a park and a pier, and the **National Air Force Museum of Canada** at the base is the country's largest aviation museum, with a hangar full of restored military aircraft and an outdoor air park you can wander through for free.

Otherwise Quinte West is a useful stop on the drive between Toronto and Kingston, or a base for visiting **Prince Edward County** wine country and the dunes at Sandbanks Provincial Park, both about half an hour to the south.
