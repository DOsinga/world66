Southampton is a small beach town on the eastern shore of Lake Huron, at the mouth of the Saugeen River, on Ontario's Bruce Peninsula. It has a loyal following among Ontario cottagers and summer visitors who come for the wide sandy beaches and the easy pace of a classic lakeside town.

The beaches are the main draw — broad, clean, and backed by the kind of sunsets that Lake Huron is known for across the province. The town itself has a low-key main street with shops, ice cream, and summer restaurants. The Chantry Island lighthouse, on an island just offshore, is accessible by boat tour in summer. Southampton is about two and a half hours from Toronto, making it a popular weekend destination for city dwellers.
