A true wilderness park on the northern shore of Lake Superior, preserving a chunk of the ancient Canadian Shield that feels genuinely remote. Wolves, moose, woodland caribou, and lynx roam here. Access is limited to boat or float plane for most of the park — plan ahead and come prepared.

The Coastal Hiking Trail runs 60 km along the Superior shoreline, one of Ontario's most demanding and rewarding backcountry treks. Expect suspension bridges, boulder beaches, and boreal forest with almost no one else around. For something shorter, the day-use area at Hattie Cove has a campground, a beach, and several loop trails through old-growth forest.

The park is managed by Parks Canada and typically open from mid-May through mid-October. Winter access is possible but requires serious gear and experience. The nearest town with services is Marathon, about 35 km north.
