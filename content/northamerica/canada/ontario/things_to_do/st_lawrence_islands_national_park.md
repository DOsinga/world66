Canada's smallest national park, scattered across a string of islands in the Thousand Islands stretch of the St. Lawrence River in eastern Ontario. The park protects 21 islands and over 80 islets between Kingston and Brockville, part of the Frontenac Arch -- a granite land bridge connecting the Canadian Shield to the Adirondacks.

The paddling and sea kayaking here are superb, and the water is unusually warm by Canadian standards. Rent a boat in Gananoque to explore at your own pace. Many of the islands have basic campsites with docks and fire pits -- reserve early for July and August. Mallorytown Landing on the mainland serves as the main visitor centre, with a small beach and interpretive trail.

If you don't have your own boat, the Gananoque Boat Line runs cruises through the islands, though a kayak or canoe gives you far more freedom to explore the quieter channels.
