The last remaining covered bridge in Ontario, built in 1881 over the Grand River near the village of West Montrose. At 198 feet long, the timber-frame bridge is still open to single-lane vehicular traffic -- locals call it the Kissing Bridge, after the privacy the covered span once afforded courting couples.

The bridge sits in the heart of Mennonite country, and you may well share the road with horse-drawn buggies. It's a pleasant stop on a drive between Guelph and St. Jacobs or Elmira. Park on either side and walk through; the wooden interior creaks satisfyingly underfoot. A small park beside the bridge has picnic tables and river access.
