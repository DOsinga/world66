The Toronto Transit Commission has a comprehensive transportation network, utilizing buses, streetcars, light rail and heavy rail (subway) to service the four corners of Toronto, and everywhere in between.

Much of the service operates throughout the day, and there is still excellent service into the night, with the Blue Night Network...a network of routes that operate through Toronto every 30 minutes or better between 1:00am and 5:00am (later into Sunday mornings).

The **Yonge-University-Spadina Subway**, the backbone of the system, travels on a "U" shaped alignment better than 5 minutes throughout the day, ending at approximately 1:00am from Union Station in Downtown Toronto.

The **Bloor-Danforth subway** operates on a similar schedule, and links up with the Yonge Subway at St. George, Spadina and Bloor/Yonge Stations. It also links with the Scarborough RT at Kennedy Station.

The newly built **Sheppard Subway** originates at Sheppard/Yonge Station and follows Sheppard Avenue East until it reaches Don Mills Road (Fairview Mall).

Much of the Downtown area is served by streetcars, located on **Carlton/College** (the 506), **Dundas E & W** (#505), **Queen Street** (#501) and **King Street** (#504). From Union Station, the Harbourfront and Spadina streetcars (#'s 509 and 511, respectively) operate over **Lakeshore Boulevard** to the CNE Grounds or Spadina Street. Streetcars generally operate every few minutes during the day.

There is also an **Airport Rocket** bus route (#192) that operates into Kipling Station on the farthest west Bloor-Danforth subway station from the Airport on a 20-40 minute headway that better serves passengers that don't want to sit on a Malton-58 bus for 50 minutes from the Airport into Lawrence West Station. Kipling Station is a relatively easy ride from the Airport and a rapid transit connection at either St. George or Spadina stations will whisk you into the Downtown core in short time. According to the Toronto Transit Commission's website ([www.ttc.ca](/northamerica/canada/ontario/toronto/gettingaround/the_toronto_transi)), expanded service hours are available on this route. Route 301-Bloor Danforth (a blue night route - OVERNIGHTS) operates on 30 minute intervals from Bloor/Yonge to the Toronto Airport terminals.
