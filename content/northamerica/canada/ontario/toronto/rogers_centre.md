Home of the Toronto Blue Jays, the Rogers Centre sits right next to the CN Tower and offers behind-the-scenes tours that are well worth the price of admission. You'll walk through areas you'd never see on game day — press boxes, sky boxes with their plush bathrooms, and sections of the field itself (schedule permitting).

The guides are enthusiastic and full of good trivia. The retractable roof has four pieces and takes just fifteen minutes to open or close. The largest crowds here haven't been for baseball — they've been for WrestleMania. And the family that won the competition to name the stadium still has two seats for life at every event.

Tours can be irregular in winter, and game days may restrict access to parts of the facility. Check ahead if you're planning around a specific date.
