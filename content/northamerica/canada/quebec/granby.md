Granby is a pleasant mid-sized city of around 70,000 people in the **Eastern Townships** (Cantons-de-l'Est) of southern Quebec, about 80 km east of Montreal. It grew up as a 19th-century English-speaking textile town and still carries some of that Anglo-Quebec heritage in its red-brick downtown and the surrounding dairy-farm country, though today the population is overwhelmingly francophone.

Granby is best known for its **zoo** — one of the largest in Canada, with more than 1,500 animals in natural enclosures and an adjoining water park, set on forested grounds just outside town. It is one of the most popular family destinations in Quebec. Beyond the zoo, the **Yamaska National Park** on the town's edge has a pretty reservoir lake, bike trails, and beaches that get busy on weekends in summer. The surrounding **Cantons-de-l'Est** wine country and the microbrewery town of **Dunham** make an easy day trip into rolling hills of maple forest and small villages.
