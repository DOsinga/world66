Mont-Tremblant is the best ski resort in eastern Canada and one of the top ten in North America. It sits in the Laurentian Mountains about 140km north of [Montréal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal), an easy two-hour drive. The resort has grown from a small ski village into a full year-round destination, with a pedestrian village at the base of the mountain that fills with visitors in both winter and summer.

The mountain has 102 trails, 14 lifts and a vertical drop of 645 metres. The north side is steeper and more challenging; the south side gets more sun and is better for beginners and intermediates. Snowfall is reliable — the Laurentians average about 350cm per year — and snowmaking covers the main runs. The ski season typically runs from late November to mid-April.

In summer, Mont-Tremblant transforms into a hiking, cycling and water sports destination. The mountain itself is climbable on foot or by gondola, and the surrounding lakes offer kayaking, canoeing and swimming. The village hosts music festivals through the summer, including a popular blues and jazz festival in August.

**Getting there:** By car from Montréal on Highway 15 north, then Highway 117. There is no direct train service; buses from Montréal run on weekends in ski season.
