---
title: 'Bars and Cafes'
type: section
---

Montreal takes its drinking seriously -- both coffee and alcohol. Bars stay open until 3 AM, later than almost anywhere else in North America, and the city's cafe culture owes as much to Paris as it does to the third-wave coffee movement.

## Bars

**Dieu du Ciel!** (29 Avenue Laurier Ouest) is a Plateau brewpub that has been making some of Quebec's best craft beer since 1998. The Peche Mortel imperial stout is a classic. Arrive early on weekends -- it fills up fast.

**Le Mal Necessaire** (1106B Boulevard Saint-Laurent) is a Chinatown tiki bar hidden behind an unmarked door. The cocktails are serious, the decor is over the top, and the vibe is buzzy without being obnoxious.

**Bar Palco** (5618 Avenue du Parc) is a neighborhood wine bar in Mile End with a thoughtful natural wine list and small plates. Low-key and unpretentious.

**Whisky Cafe** (5800 Boulevard Saint-Laurent) serves a deep selection of whiskies, ports, cognacs, and grappas in a long, atmospheric room with a cigar lounge next door.

**Big in Japan** (4175 Boulevard Saint-Laurent) is another hidden-door bar, this one a sleek Japanese whisky bar. Ring the bell, head upstairs, and settle into one of the best cocktail experiences in the city.

## Cafes

**Cafe Olimpico** (124 Rue Saint-Viateur Ouest) is a Mile End institution. Strong espresso, great people-watching, and no Wi-Fi -- just conversation.

**Cafe Santropol** (3990 Rue Saint-Urbain) has been a Plateau fixture for decades, serving creative sandwiches, house teas, and organic coffee in a rambling old house with a beautiful garden terrace in summer.

**Crew Collective** (360 Rue Saint-Jacques) occupies the stunning former banking hall of the Royal Bank in Old Montreal. The coffee is good, but the room -- with its soaring ceilings and marble columns -- is the real draw.

**Pikolo Espresso Bar** (3418B Avenue du Parc) is tiny, precise, and serves some of the best espresso in the city.
