---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Montréal"
type: section
---

Montréal's bar scene runs late — last call is at 3am, later than anywhere else in Canada. The main zones are boulevard Saint-Laurent (the Main) from Sherbrooke to Laurier, rue Crescent in downtown, and rue Saint-Denis in the Plateau. In summer terrasses (outdoor terraces) open everywhere and drinking outdoors until midnight is a way of life.

The Plateau and Mile End neighbourhoods have the most interesting independent cafés; expect long weekend brunches and good espresso. The Café Olimpico on rue Saint-Viateur in Mile End has been a neighbourhood institution since the 1970s and still draws a crowd.

Bars are open until 3am throughout the week.
