---
title: "Festivals in Montréal"
type: section
---

Montréal has more festivals per capita than almost any city in the world, and the summer calendar is effectively one long outdoor party. Here are the main ones worth timing a trip around.

**Montréal en Lumière (February):** A ten-day winter festival focused on light, food and culture. The culinary side is serious — chefs from across Canada and internationally take over restaurants and hold special events. The outdoor installations and light shows brighten up the long nights.

**Just for Laughs / Juste pour Rire (July):** The largest comedy festival in the world, running for most of the month. French-language comedians take the first half, English-language comedians the second. Street performances are free; ticketed shows range from club shows to big international names. Past headliners have included Jerry Seinfeld, John Cleese and Tim Allen.

**Montréal International Jazz Festival (late June/early July):** One of the great jazz festivals, filling the Quartier des Spectacles in the city centre for eleven days. Most of the outdoor concerts are free. Book accommodation far ahead.

**POP Montréal (late September/early October):** A five-day independent music festival spread across clubs and unusual venues across the city. Strong on emerging artists and international indie acts; good for people who find the Jazz Festival too mainstream.

**Montréal World Film Festival (late August/early September):** The only FIAPF-accredited competitive festival in North America outside the US. Small-budget and world cinema films that wouldn't reach normal cinemas.

**Fête Nationale (June 24):** Québec's national holiday — bigger than Canada Day in this city. Concerts, fireworks and a lot of patriotic feeling.
