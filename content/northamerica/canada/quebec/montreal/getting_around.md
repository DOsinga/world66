---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Montréal's Metro is the backbone of public transit — four lines, 68 stations, each designed by a different architect, running on rubber tires for an unusually quiet ride. The STM (Société de transport de Montréal) also operates an extensive bus network. A single fare covers both Metro and bus; day passes and weekly passes are good value for visitors. The Metro runs until about 1am on most nights, later on weekends.

The city is flat and well suited to cycling. The BIXI bike-share system has hundreds of stations across the island and is the easiest way to get around in good weather. Dedicated cycling paths run along most major streets and along the Canal Lachine.

For car rental, the major agencies have counters at the airport and downtown locations. Driving in central Montréal can be frustrating due to construction (chronic), and parking is expensive. For most tourist purposes, the Metro and cycling are faster.

Taxis are metered; Uber operates throughout the city.
