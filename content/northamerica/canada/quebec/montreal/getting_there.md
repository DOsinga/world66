---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

**By air:** Montréal-Trudeau International Airport (YUL) is the main airport, about 20km west of downtown. It has direct flights to most major cities in North America, Europe, the Caribbean and Mexico. The 747 Express bus links the airport to downtown and several Metro stations; the journey takes about 45–60 minutes depending on traffic. Taxis to downtown cost a flat rate of around $40–45.

**By train:** VIA Rail operates frequent services on the Québec City–Windsor corridor, with several trains daily to Québec City (3 hours), Ottawa (2 hours) and Toronto (5 hours). The Gare Centrale station is centrally located downtown, linked to the Metro at Bonaventure station.

**By bus:** Greyhound and various regional carriers connect Montréal to destinations across Canada and the northeastern US. The main bus terminal is at Berri-UQAM Metro station.

**By car:** Highways converge on Montréal from all directions. From Toronto, the 401 to the 20 takes roughly 5 hours. From New York City, the I-87 to the border and Route 15 is about 6 hours.
