The two islands in the St. Lawrence — Île Sainte-Hélène and Île Notre-Dame — that together form Parc Jean-Drapeau are one of Montréal's great outdoor spaces. Originally developed for Expo '67, the islands still contain the Biosphere (a geodesic dome now housing an environmental museum), the Casino de Montréal, La Ronde amusement park, and Plage de l'île Notre-Dame, the city's most popular swimming beach. A network of cycling and walking paths links it all together. Easily reached by Metro to Jean-Drapeau station. In summer the Formula E race and major concerts take place here.
