---
title: "Shopping in Montréal"
type: section
---

Montréal is one of the best cities in North America for shopping, particularly for fashion. The city has a strong independent retail culture alongside the usual chains, and prices are generally lower than comparable cities in Europe.

**Rue Sainte-Catherine** is the main commercial street downtown, running east-west through the city centre. The Eaton Centre and Place Montréal Trust anchor the western end; the eastern end becomes more local and less chain-oriented.

**The underground city** (RÉSO) connects shopping centres through 33 kilometres of underground corridors, making it possible to spend hours shopping without going outside — useful in winter. Les Promenades de la Cathédrale, Les Cours Mont-Royal and Place Ville-Marie are the main underground malls.

**Boulevard Saint-Laurent** and the streets of the Plateau are better for independent boutiques, vintage clothing, record shops and design stores. Mile End (around rue Saint-Viateur and rue Bernard) has developed into a cluster of independent shops and studios.

**Rue Saint-Denis** is good for Québécois designers and fashion. **Rue Chabanel** in the north end is a garment district with factory outlets.

For food shopping, the **Atwater Market** and **Jean-Talon Market** are outstanding covered markets with local produce, cheese, meat and prepared foods.
