---
title: "Things to Do in Montréal"
type: section
---

Montréal rewards both the planner and the wanderer. The city has world-class museums, distinctive neighbourhoods, outstanding parks and a calendar so packed with festivals that you're unlikely to visit without stumbling into one.

Start with [Old Montréal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal/things_to_do/old_montreal) for history and architecture, then head up to [Mount Royal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal/things_to_do/mount_royal) for perspective on the whole island. The [Plateau-Mont-Royal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal/things_to_do/plateau_mont_royal) neighbourhood is the best place to experience daily Montréal life. For museums, [Pointe-à-Callière](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal/things_to_do/pointe_a_calliere) is the standout for history, while the [Biodome](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal/things_to_do/biodome) and [Botanical Garden](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal/things_to_do/botanical_garden) make a full day out in the east end.

The underground city — a 33-kilometre network of corridors connecting Metro stations, malls, offices and hotels — is a practical necessity in winter but also a curiosity worth exploring.
