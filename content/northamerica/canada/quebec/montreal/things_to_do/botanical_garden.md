One of the largest botanical gardens in the world, covering 75 hectares and containing over 22,000 plant species. The Chinese Garden and Japanese Garden are highlights, both peaceful retreats far from the city noise. In autumn the garden hosts the Montréal en Lumière lantern festival, when the Chinese Garden fills with thousands of illuminated silk lanterns — one of the most magical sights in the city. The greenhouses are open year-round. Located across from the Biodome near the Olympic Stadium; Metro to Viau.
