A new guided
bike tour company, Cycle Tours Montreal, is giving tourists and locals a
first-hand look at Montreal neighbourhoods.

Cycle Tours
Montreal wants customers to get to know Montreal as intimately as they can in a
half-day tour. While the company’s half-day rides take in sightseeing
highlights and major landmarks, they also veer off the beaten path to show how real
Montrealers live—passing through city
parks, little-known side streets, hidden alleys, and outdoor markets to see
where city residents sleep, eat, drink and shop.

From bagel
taste-testing in Mile End and picnic stops at Jean-Talon or Atwater Markets, to
cruising through the charming “Ruelles Vertes” (Green Streets) of the Plateau,
riders get an up-close feel for the rhythm and flavour of Montreal life. The
company even offers an only-in-Montreal “Sunday Urban Culture Experience,”
starting at the top of the city’s peak, Mont-Royal, pausing below at the world
famous TamTams drum circle, heading through Old Montreal, stopping for a picnic
and pint on the Lachine Canal, then observing the surfers in the St. Lawrence
River rapids, en route to Parc Jean Drapeau and Ile St. Helène, just in time to
experience the weekly Piknic Electronik family-friendly outdoor rave.

Cycle Tours personalize their tours as much as possible and provides a list of the week’s special events and festivals, and suggests places
to eat, drink and shop. Some of the some of his routes are so secret, even locals consider them to be
unknown territory.

Cycle Tours
Montreal currently offers three different city tours that run seven days a
week, as well as private tours upon request. The tours are casual and relaxed,
and suitable for just about anyone who knows how to ride a bike. All tours
include a brand-new hybrid city bicycle equipped with carry basket, comfortable
helmets, and a lock. Those supplying their own bikes are also welcome.
