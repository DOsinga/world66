A small but charming natural history museum on the McGill campus, housed in an 1882 building. The collections cover palaeontology, zoology and Egyptology — the mummy collection is particularly popular. It's free to enter, making it an easy addition to a walk along Sherbrooke Street. The Victorian-era display cases and old-fashioned labelling are part of the appeal; this feels like a museum from another era, which is no bad thing.
