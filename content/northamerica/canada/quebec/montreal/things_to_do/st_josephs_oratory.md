Perched near the summit of Mount Royal, the Oratory's copper dome is visible from much of the city and is the largest church in Canada. It was built over several decades in the early 20th century under the direction of Brother André, a lay brother credited with miraculous healings — his heart is on display in a chapel. The pilgrims who climb the 283 exterior steps on their knees are a striking sight. Even for non-believers, the interior is impressive: the main basilica seats over 2,000. The gardens outside offer fine views and are peaceful in every season.
