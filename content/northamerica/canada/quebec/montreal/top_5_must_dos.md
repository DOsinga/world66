---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Walk Through Old Montreal**

The cobblestoned streets of Vieux-Montreal are the oldest part of the city, and walking them feels like stepping into a French colonial port town. Start at Place Jacques-Cartier, where street performers and cafe terrasses spill across the square, then wander south to the Old Port along the waterfront. Duck into the Notre-Dame Basilica -- the interior is a jaw-dropping explosion of blue and gold Gothic Revival woodwork, more vivid than photographs prepare you for. The narrow streets are lined with stone buildings dating to the 1700s, now housing galleries, restaurants, and boutique hotels. Come in the evening when the gas lamps come on.

**2. Hike Mount Royal**

The mountain that gave the city its name rises right out of the urban fabric, and climbing it is a Montreal rite of passage. Take the winding path from the Peel Street entrance up through the woods to the Kondiaronk Belvedere at the summit, where the entire city spreads out below you -- the St. Lawrence, the downtown towers, the distant Monteregian hills. In summer, Montrealers picnic on the slopes and gather for the weekly tam-tam drum circle near the George-Etienne Cartier monument. In winter, the trails are good for snowshoeing and cross-country skiing. Allow about 30 minutes for the walk up.

**3. Eat Your Way Through the Markets**

Montreal's public markets are among the best in North America. **Jean-Talon Market** in Little Italy is the flagship -- an open-air bazaar of Quebec produce, artisanal cheeses, charcuterie, maple products, and prepared foods. Grab a coffee, browse the stalls, and assemble a picnic. **Atwater Market** near the Lachine Canal is smaller and more focused on butchers and specialty food shops. Saturday morning at either market is one of the great Montreal experiences.

**4. Catch a Festival**

Timing your visit to overlap with one of Montreal's major festivals transforms the trip. The Jazz Festival and Just for Laughs in summer are the headliners, but there is something happening almost every month. Free outdoor shows, packed streets, extended metro hours, and a city-wide energy that is hard to match. Even if you stumble into a festival you have never heard of, the atmosphere alone is worth it.

**5. Explore the Plateau on a BIXI Bike**

Rent a BIXI bike and spend a half-day riding through the Plateau Mont-Royal and Mile End. These are the neighborhoods where Montreal's creative, multicultural spirit is most visible -- colorful row houses with their distinctive exterior staircases, independent bookshops, bagel bakeries, street art, tiny parks, and some of the best restaurants in the city. Stop at Fairmount or St-Viateur for a fresh bagel, lock up and wander Rue Saint-Denis or Avenue du Mont-Royal, then ride the bike path along the Lachine Canal. This is Montreal at its most alive.
