---
title: "Shopping in Québec City"
type: section
---

The best shopping in the Old City is on and around **rue Saint-Jean** (both inside and outside the walls), where you'll find Québécois food products, clothing boutiques, bookshops and gift shops. The **rue du Petit-Champlain** in the Lower Town is heavily touristy but good for local crafts, clothing and gifts.

**Rue du Trésor**, the narrow lane opposite the Château Frontenac, is an open-air gallery where local artists sell original prints and paintings — a reliable spot for a distinctive souvenir.

**Faubourg Saint-Jean-Baptiste**, outside the old walls, has a more neighbourhood feel. The **J.A. Moisan** épicerie at 699 rue Saint-Jean is worth a visit — said to be the oldest grocery store in North America (established 1871), it sells local preserves, cheeses, meats and prepared foods in a beautifully preserved 19th-century interior.

For mainstream shopping, the **Galeries de la Capitale** in the Les Rivières borough is the largest mall in the region, with over 280 shops. **Place Sainte-Foy** is an upscale alternative, well served by transit.
