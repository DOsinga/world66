---
title: "Things to Do in Québec City"
type: section
---

Québec City's main appeal is the Old City itself — walking the fortification walls, exploring the narrow streets of Vieux-Québec, and taking in the view from Dufferin Terrace. Most of what you'd want to see is within easy walking distance in the Upper and Lower Towns.

The [Château Frontenac](/northamerica/canada/quebec/quebeccity/things_to_do/chateau_frontenac) is the unmissable landmark; even if you're not staying there, the terrace in front of it is the best vantage point in the city. The [Plains of Abraham](/northamerica/canada/quebec/quebeccity/things_to_do/plains_of_abraham) are a short walk west of the old walls — a beautiful park that also happens to be one of the most important sites in Canadian history. The [Musée national des beaux-arts](/northamerica/canada/quebec/quebeccity/things_to_do/musee_national_des_beaux_arts) has an excellent permanent collection and is worth several hours.

In winter, the [Hôtel de Glace](/northamerica/canada/quebec/quebeccity/things_to_do/ice_hotel) at nearby Valcartier is an extraordinary experience. The ski areas of Mont-Sainte-Anne (45 minutes east) and Stoneham (30 minutes north) are among the best in eastern Canada.

The Montmorency Falls, 15 minutes east of the city, are taller than Niagara. A cable car and suspension bridge give close-up access; in winter, a cone of frozen mist builds up at the base.
