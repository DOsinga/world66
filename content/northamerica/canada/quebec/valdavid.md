Val-David is a small artists' village in the Laurentians, about an hour and a half north of [Montréal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal). The town has a distinctly bohemian feel — the main street, rue de l'Église, is lined with galleries, craft shops, and studios, and several of Québec's most respected potters and sculptors live and work here.

In winter Val-David is a popular base for cross-country skiing and snowshoeing, with trails connecting to the regional P'tit Train du Nord network. In summer, the Jardins de Rocailles is worth a visit — a secluded rock garden with more than 450 plant varieties tucked into a woodland hillside. The village also draws rock climbers to the cliffs along the Rivière du Nord.
