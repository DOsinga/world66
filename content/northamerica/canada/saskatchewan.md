*Land of Living Skies*

Perhaps the most enduring image of Saskatchewan is that of the prairie. In the summer, fields of golden wheat, barley and bright yellow canola dominate the open landscape. In the winter, a vast blue sky ascends from blankets of freshly fallen snow, producing some of the most pristine and inspiring vistas in the country.

[Regina](/northamerica/canada/saskatchewan/regina), the provincial capital, is home to the legislature building, the grounds of which form one of the largest urban parks in North America.

[Saskatoon](/northamerica/canada/saskatchewan/saskatoon) is a thriving city well known for hosting great family-oriented festivals including Winterfest, Folkfest, and the International Fringe Festival.

Saskatchewan covers approximately 651,900 square kilometres, and contrary to the flat-province stereotype, one half is covered by boreal forest and one-eighth by fresh water. The Cypress Hills in the southwest reach 1,392 metres — the highest point between the Rockies and Labrador. North Saskatchewan, South Saskatchewan, Assiniboine, and Churchill rivers all drain into Hudson Bay.

The province is culturally diverse, with people of Aboriginal, European, Russian, Scandinavian and British heritage. Saskatchewan has the highest proportion of Indigenous residents of any province, at around 16%.
