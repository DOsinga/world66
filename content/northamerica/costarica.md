Costa Rica punches well above its weight. This small Central American country — roughly the size of West Virginia — holds around 5% of the world's biodiversity, has two coastlines, a spine of volcanic mountains, and no army. It abolished its military in 1948 and put the money into education and healthcare instead. That unusual history has shaped a confident, stable society that welcomes visitors without much fuss.

The country is genuinely beautiful. Volcanoes rise above cloud forest on the Pacific side; Caribbean lowlands stretch to the east; between them, the temperate Central Valley holds most of the population. Getting from one coast to the other takes only a few hours by bus. You can surf in the morning and hike a cloud forest in the afternoon.

Tourism infrastructure is well developed, occasionally overdeveloped. The most popular beaches and parks can feel crowded, especially December through April. Venture beyond the main circuits and you'll quickly find yourself somewhere quieter.

The capital, [San José](/northamerica/costarica/sanjose), sits in the Central Valley and is the hub for buses, domestic flights, and onward travel. Most visitors pass through at least once. The Caribbean port town of [Limon](/northamerica/costarica/limon) has a completely different feel — Afro-Caribbean culture, reggae, and some of the best food in the country.
