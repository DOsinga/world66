Atenas is a small town in [Costa Rica's](/northamerica/costarica) Central Valley, about 30 minutes west of [San José](/northamerica/costarica/sanjose) on the main road toward the Pacific coast. Sitting at around 700 metres elevation in the coffee-growing hills, it has long claimed to have the best climate in the world — warm days, cool nights, and very little humidity. Whether or not that title is deserved, the weather is genuinely pleasant year-round and has attracted a sizable expat community.

The town itself is small and unhurried. The central park has a pretty church and a Friday farmers market where you can buy fresh fruit, cheese, and homemade bread. The surrounding hillsides are covered in coffee plantations, and several fincas offer tours where you can see the whole process from cherry to cup. Botanical Orchid Garden, just outside town, is worth a visit for its collection of native orchid species.

Atenas works well as a quiet base for exploring the Central Valley. From here you can easily reach the [Poás Volcano](/northamerica/costarica/poas_volcano), the craft town of Sarchí with its famous painted ox carts, or continue west to the Pacific beaches at Jacó and [Manuel Antonio](/northamerica/costarica/manuelantonio). The road from Atenas down to the coast passes through dramatic mountain scenery and is one of the more scenic drives in the country.
