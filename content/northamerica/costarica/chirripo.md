Chirripó National Park, about 150 km south of [San José](/northamerica/costarica/sanjose), is home to Costa Rica's highest peak — Cerro Chirripó at 3,820 metres. The park protects a dramatic landscape of cloud forest, páramo grasslands and glacial lakes, and is one of the country's top highlights for hikers.

The main trail to the summit is about 20 km long with 2,400 metres of elevation gain. It is well marked and manageable for any reasonably fit hiker, though the altitude makes it harder than the distance suggests. In March, the annual Chirripó marathon sends runners racing to the top and back.

[San Isidro de Pérez Zeledón](/northamerica/costarica/sanisidrodeprezzeledn) is the nearest town with full facilities. San Gerardo de Rivas, much smaller, sits right at the park entrance and is the usual base for the hike.
