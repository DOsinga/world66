---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The base for visiting Corcovado is [Puerto Jiménez](/northamerica/costarica/puertojimenez) on the Osa Peninsula. From San José, you can fly (30 minutes, several daily departures) or take a bus to Palmar Norte and then a local bus to Puerto Jiménez — about 8 hours total overland.

From Puerto Jiménez, there are three ways into the park. The most common is to take a 4WD taxi to La Palma and hike in via Los Patos ranger station to Sirena. The coastal approach goes south by 4WD to Carate and enters at La Leona. The quickest option is a light aircraft from Puerto Jiménez directly to the Sirena airstrip — expensive but it saves a full day of hiking.

You must book Sirena accommodation and register with SINAC well in advance, especially in dry season (December–April). All visits require a licensed guide — freelancers operate from Puerto Jiménez and rates are regulated.
