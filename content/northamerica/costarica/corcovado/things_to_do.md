---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The park's activities are essentially variations on one theme: walking through one of the most wildlife-rich forests on Earth and paying close attention. There are several trail systems, all requiring a licensed guide, and the experience varies enormously depending on which entry point you use and how long you stay.

The classic route enters from Los Patos in the north and hikes 20 km through the forest interior to Sirena on the coast — about 8 hours with a good guide. The coastal trail from La Leona in the south is shorter but exposed, involving long beach stretches at low tide and two river crossings. Both trails reward patience: slow down, stop often, look at the canopy and the forest floor.

At Sirena, rent one of the dugout canoes and paddle up the Río Sirena. This is where tapir sightings are most likely. In the evening, walk to the river mouth at high tide — bull sharks and crocodiles congregate there in numbers that make the water visibly restless. It's one of the stranger wildlife spectacles in Central America.
