---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Corcovado receives up to 5,000 mm of rain per year — more than almost anywhere else in Central America. There is no dry month, but the dry season runs roughly December through April, when trail conditions are much better and river crossings less hazardous. This is also when visitor numbers peak, so book Sirena accommodation at least a month ahead.

The wet season (May–November) brings daily downpours that turn the trails into mud and raise rivers to knee- or waist-level. Some routes become impassable. That said, wildlife activity often increases in the wet season and you'll have far fewer people around. Experienced hikers who don't mind wet conditions sometimes prefer it.

Avoid September and October, the heaviest months of the rainy season, when some trails close entirely.
