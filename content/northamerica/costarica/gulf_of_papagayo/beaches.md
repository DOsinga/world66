---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The gulf's most accessible beach is **Playa Hermosa**, a long crescent of dark sand a few kilometres north of the Papagayo Peninsula junction. It's calm and swimmable year-round, with a handful of smaller hotels and restaurants along the road behind it. Sunsets are reliable theatre.

**Playa del Coco** is the most developed town beach on the gulf — busy, a bit scruffy, with dive shops, seafood restaurants and a weekly market. It lacks the scenery of quieter spots but is useful as a base: boat charters, dive trips and shuttles to other beaches all operate from here.

Further north, **Playa Ocotal** is a small cove just south of Coco, sheltered and cleaner, popular with snorkellers. The rocky headlands shelter it from swell and the visibility underwater is often good even when other beaches are stirred up.

On the Papagayo Peninsula itself, **Playa Nacascolo** is technically accessible to the public (Costa Rican law guarantees beach access) but the dirt road and lack of facilities mean it stays relatively empty — a reward for those who make the effort.
