---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The closest airport is **Daniel Oduber Quirós International Airport** in Liberia (IATA: LIR), about 35 km east of the gulf. Direct flights arrive from North American cities including Miami, New York, Toronto and Dallas during the high season, making Liberia the obvious entry point for Guanacaste visitors. A taxi from the airport to Playa del Coco or Playa Hermosa takes around 35–40 minutes.

From San José, the drive to the gulf takes around four hours on the Interamerican Highway north. Shared shuttles run daily between San José and Playa del Coco, the main hub town on the gulf; most shuttle companies also serve Tamarindo and other beach destinations with connections through the gulf area. Public buses from San José to Liberia are frequent and cheap — from Liberia, local buses and taxis cover the last stretch to the beach towns.

Car rental is the most flexible option if you plan to explore multiple beaches; the roads between them are mostly paved now but a 4WD is useful if you want to reach the more remote spots on the peninsula during the rainy season.
