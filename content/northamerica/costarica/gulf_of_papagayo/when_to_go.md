---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Guanacaste's dry season runs from late November to early May — longer and more reliable than anywhere else in Costa Rica. During these months, the gulf gets almost no rain, the roads are passable and the winds that scour the peninsula in January and February make the air fresh even at midday. High season peaks December through March, when hotel prices rise and availability at popular spots tightens.

The rainy season (May through October) is not unpleasant: mornings are usually clear, rain falls in heavy afternoon showers that blow through quickly. The landscape turns green and the gulf empties of tourists. Rates drop significantly and diving conditions can be excellent — visibility is often better after the rains flush nutrients into the water around the Bat Islands. September and October see the heaviest rainfall and some roads to remote beaches become impassable.

The Papagayo winds arrive in December and blow hard through February. They can make open-water boat trips choppy and certain beaches uncomfortable on gusty days, but they also keep temperatures down to manageable levels and are excellent for windsurfers and kitesurfers at the right spots.
