La Fortuna is a small town in northern Costa Rica that has become one of the country's top adventure tourism destinations, thanks to the enormous cone of Arenal Volcano looming directly above it. The volcano was highly active from 1968 to 2010, and although it has been quiet since then, it remains an overwhelming physical presence -- on clear days the perfect cone dominates every view in town, and on cloudy days you can feel its mass even when you cannot see it.

The town itself is a strip of tour agencies, restaurants, and souvenir shops catering to the steady flow of visitors, but it is friendly and unpretentious rather than overdeveloped. The real action is in the surrounding area. The Arenal hot springs are the headline attraction -- natural thermal waters heated by volcanic activity, ranging from basic public pools to luxurious resort complexes. Tabacon and Baldi are the best known, but smaller, less expensive options exist and are often more enjoyable.

Beyond the hot springs, La Fortuna is a base for waterfall hikes (the Catarata de La Fortuna drops 70 meters into a swimming hole), whitewater rafting on the Pacuare and Sarapiqui rivers, zip-lining through the cloud forest canopy, and kayaking on Lake Arenal. The Arenal Volcano National Park has trails through lava fields from the 1968 and 1992 eruptions, where you can see the forest slowly reclaiming the rock. Hanging bridges through the treetops at Mistico Park offer a quieter alternative to the zip lines.

The area gets a lot of rain -- this is the Caribbean slope of the continental divide -- so come prepared for wet weather at any time of year. February through April is the driest period. Despite the tourist infrastructure, the birding and wildlife around La Fortuna remain excellent: toucans, howler monkeys, and poison dart frogs are all common. The drive from San Jose takes about three hours, or you can take the scenic boat-and-van route via Lake Arenal from Monteverde.
