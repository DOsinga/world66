Montezuma is a small, bohemian beach village at the southern tip of Costa Rica's Nicoya Peninsula. It attracts a mix of backpackers, surfers, yoga practitioners, and nature lovers drawn by its waterfalls, rocky coastline, and laid-back atmosphere. The village itself is tiny -- a handful of streets with restaurants, surf shops, and a weekly organic market in the park.

The main draws are natural. A series of waterfalls are a short walk from town, with swimming holes at the base of each cascade. The beaches alternate between sand and volcanic rock, and the surf is decent if not world-class. Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve, the oldest protected area in Costa Rica, is a short drive south. The area has a well-established expat community, and the food scene is better than you would expect for a village this small.
