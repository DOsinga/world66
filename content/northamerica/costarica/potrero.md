Potrero is a small fishing village in Guanacaste, on Costa Rica's northern Pacific coast, with a relaxed character that sets it apart from the more developed resorts nearby. Its beach — Playa Potrero — is a calm, dark-sand bay sheltered from the swell that makes it ideal for swimming and kayaking year-round, and the sunsets over the Pacific here are reliably spectacular.

The village itself is simple: a handful of restaurants, some basic accommodation, and a slower pace than its flashier neighbor Playa Flamingo, a short drive to the south. If you want a quiet beach base in Guanacaste without paying resort prices, Potrero is worth considering. Playa Flamingo is close enough to use for a meal out or a boat trip, but you'll sleep better in Potrero.
