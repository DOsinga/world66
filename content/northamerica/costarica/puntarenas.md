Puntarenas is a port city on [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica)'s Pacific coast, built on a narrow sandy peninsula that juts out into the Gulf of Nicoya. For decades it was the country's main Pacific port and a popular weekend beach destination for families from the Central Valley. The port has since moved to nearby Caldera, and Puntarenas has settled into a quieter rhythm — a working town with a long waterfront promenade, fish markets, and the slightly faded charm of a place that has seen busier days.

The city is the main departure point for ferries to the Nicoya Peninsula, and many travelers pass through on their way to the beaches of Montezuma, Santa Teresa, or Tambor. The Paseo de los Turistas along the waterfront is pleasant for an evening stroll, and the seafood is fresh and cheap — try a ceviche at one of the open-air stands near the ferry terminal. Cruise ships call here occasionally, and the nearby Isla San Lucas, a former penal colony, has been converted into a wildlife refuge that makes for a worthwhile day trip by boat.
