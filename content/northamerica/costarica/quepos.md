Quepos is a small fishing town on Costa Rica's central Pacific coast, best known as the gateway to Manuel Antonio National Park. The town itself has a scruffy, likeable energy — a handful of funky bars and local restaurants line the main streets downtown, and the fishing fleet still operates from the harbour.

The road from Quepos to Manuel Antonio climbs a forested ridge with increasingly upscale hotels and restaurants along the way. At the end of the road is Manuel Antonio itself — a beautiful white-sand beach backed by rainforest, with the national park entrance just to the left. The park is small but spectacular, with monkeys, sloths, and iguanas visible on even a short visit.

Quepos is also one of the top sport-fishing destinations in Costa Rica. Sailfish run from December through April, marlin bite hardest from November to April, and snapper, tuna, dorado, and roosterfish can be caught year-round. Charter boats range from around $600 per day for a smaller vessel to $1,500 for a larger one — book direct with boat owners for the best price, since hotels and agents add commissions.
