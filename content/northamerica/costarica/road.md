The Inter-American Highway (Ruta 1 / Ruta 2) runs the length of Costa Rica, entering from Nicaragua at Peñas Blancas in the north and crossing into Panama at Paso Canoas in the south. The full drive from border to border is roughly 550 km and passes through San José.

Road conditions on the highway itself are generally decent, but secondary roads -- especially in rural areas and near the coast -- can be rough, unpaved, and prone to washouts during the rainy season (May to November). A 4WD is not strictly necessary for the main route, but it opens up a lot of the country.

Border crossings at both Peñas Blancas (Nicaragua) and Paso Canoas (Panama) can be slow and bureaucratic. Arrive early in the morning if you can. You will need your vehicle's title, registration, insurance, and your passport. Expect to pay a few dollars in exit and entry fees on both sides.
