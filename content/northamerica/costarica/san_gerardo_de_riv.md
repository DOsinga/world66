San Gerardo de Rivas is a small village in the Talamanca mountains of southern [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica), and the starting point for the climb up Cerro Chirripó, the country's highest peak at 3,820 metres. There is not much to the village itself — a handful of lodges, a few restaurants, and a ranger station — but that is beside the point. You come here to hike.

The trail to the summit is about 20 kilometres each way. Most people take two days, spending a night at the basic shelter near the top. The reward is a view that on clear mornings takes in both the Pacific and the Caribbean. Permits are required and limited in number, so book ahead through the national park service.

Even if you are not climbing Chirripó, the area around San Gerardo is beautiful hiking country — cloud forest, waterfalls, and hot springs. The village sits at about 1,350 metres, so the climate is noticeably cooler than the lowlands. Getting here involves a bus or drive south from [San José](/northamerica/costarica/sanjose) to San Isidro de El General, then a local bus or taxi up the mountain road.
