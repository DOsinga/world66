San José's foremost architectural showpiece, the National Theater is a source of pride to Costa Ricans everywhere. Inaugurated on October 19, 1897 with a performance of Faust by the Paris Opera Company, the building's origins date to 1890 when coffee barons funded its construction to attract European opera companies. The interior is lavish: marble floors, gilded columns, and a ceiling mural depicting coffee and banana harvests. Daily guided tours are available; check for evening performances.
