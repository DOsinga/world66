Santa Cruz is a mid-sized market town in the Guanacaste region of northwestern Costa Rica, serving as the commercial and administrative hub for a wide surrounding area. It is not a major tourist destination in itself, but makes a useful base for exploring the nearby beaches and the Nicoya Peninsula.

The surrounding area has some of the most accessible beaches on the Pacific coast. Playa Junquillal, about 30 km west, is a quiet Black Sand beach with good surf and a blue flag designation for cleanliness. The town of Nicoya to the south is worth a short visit for its colonial church. Santa Cruz itself holds a colourful oxcart festival in January.

Local artists work in wood, fabric, and fused glass. The Cooperativa de Artesanías (Cooper Mays) produces distinctive tropical porcelain tableware and welcomes visitors.
