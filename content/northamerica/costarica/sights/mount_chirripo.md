At 3,820 meters, Cerro Chirripó is the highest point in Costa Rica and the second-highest peak in Central America. On a clear morning from the summit, you can see both the Caribbean and the Pacific — one of very few places on Earth where that's possible.

The standard route starts from the village of San Gerardo de Rivas and takes most hikers two days, overnighting at the Crestones base camp at about 3,400 meters. The trail climbs through cloud forest before breaking out into páramo, the windswept highland grassland that feels more like the Andes than the tropics. Permits are required and limited; book through SINAC well in advance, especially during the dry season (December through April) when conditions are best.

Bring warm layers — temperatures near the summit regularly drop below freezing at night, which comes as a shock in a country most people associate with beaches.
