---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Cuba's festival calendar rewards travelers who time their visit well. Music and street celebration are embedded in Cuban culture year-round, but a few events stand out.

**Havana International Jazz Festival** (late January / early February) is one of the great jazz events in the world. Cuban jazz has its own character — rooted in son and Afro-Cuban rhythms — and the festival draws major international names alongside local masters. Performances span eight or more venues across Havana, with events also in Santa Clara and [Santiago de Cuba](/northamerica/cuba/santiagodecuba). Book accommodation well in advance.

**Festival de la Salsa, Havana** (February) follows close behind the Jazz Festival. Salsa orchestras perform across the city for several days. The Casa de la Música venues in Centro Havana and Miramar are the main stages.

**Santiago de Cuba Carnival** (last week of July, centered on July 25–26) is the largest, most exuberant, and most African-influenced carnival in the Caribbean. Conga processions, elaborate costumes, comparsas (street bands), and several days of almost continuous outdoor dancing take over the city. Plan ahead — accommodation fills up and transport in and out becomes complicated.

**Parrandas de Remedios** (December 24) — the small colonial town of [Remedios](/northamerica/cuba/remedios) erupts into one of Cuba's most extraordinary local festivals. The two halves of the town compete in building elaborate illuminated floats and setting off competitive fireworks displays. It starts after midnight on Christmas Eve and can go until dawn. Surprisingly few tourists make it here compared to Santiago's Carnival; it's genuinely spectacular.

**Havana International Film Festival** (early December) is a serious event — Latin America's most important film festival. Screenings happen across Havana's cinemas; the Yara on La Rampa is the hub. Free or very cheap entry to most screenings.
