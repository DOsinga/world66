---
title: "Food and Drink"
type: section
---

Cuban food gets an undeservedly bad reputation. State restaurants — the comedores and peso cafeterias — can be grim: overcooked rice and beans, stringy pork, warm beer. But Cuba has a thriving private restaurant sector (paladares) that is often excellent, and the basics of Cuban cooking — black bean soup, ropa vieja (shredded braised beef), rice and black beans (moros y cristianos), lechón (roast pork), and tostones (twice-fried plantain) — are deeply satisfying when done well.

Look for paladares, identifiable by a small sign or word of mouth. In Havana the paladar scene has been dynamic since the 1990s; in smaller towns there are often one or two excellent options run out of someone's front room. Prices at paladares are paid in hard currency (euros or dollars). Expect to pay €10-20 per person for a full meal with drinks at a decent paladar.

Seafood is surprisingly underrepresented on menus despite Cuba being an island — much of the catch is exported. Lobster (langosta) appears regularly and is excellent. When it's available, it's usually grilled simply with butter and garlic.

**Rum** is Cuba's great gift to the world. Havana Club is the national brand and is excellent; look for the 7-year añejo for sipping, or use the 3-year blanco in a mojito (rum, lime, sugar, mint, soda water) or a daiquirí. Cuba Libre (rum and cola) is simple and worth drinking at least once under the right circumstances. The Floridita bar in Old Havana claims to have invented the daiquirí; it's touristy and expensive but still atmospheric.

**Coffee** is good and cheap — dark and sweet is the default, served in tiny cups. The coffee grown in the Sierra Maestra foothills near [Santiago de Cuba](/northamerica/cuba/santiagodecuba) is the best on the island.

At the street level, look for vendors selling croquetas (fried ham croquettes), pan con tortilla (egg sandwich), and fresh fruit — mangoes, guavas, papayas — all sold for a few pesos. These are the cheapest and sometimes the most satisfying meals in Cuba.
