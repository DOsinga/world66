Holguín province covers the eastern end of [Cuba](/northamerica/cuba) and is where Columbus first landed in 1492, reportedly declaring it the most beautiful land ever seen by human eyes. The province has some of Cuba's best beaches, dramatic mountain scenery, and a relaxed provincial capital that sees far fewer tourists than [Havana](/northamerica/cuba/havana) or [Trinidad](/northamerica/cuba/trinidad).

The city of Holguín is a pleasant, leafy place built around a series of parks and plazas. Its landmark is the Loma de la Cruz, a hilltop cross reached by climbing 458 steps — the panoramic views over the city and surrounding hills are the reward. The city hosts the annual Romerías de Mayo, a cultural festival that fills the streets with music, art, and processions.

The coast is the main draw for most visitors. Guardalavaca, about 65 kilometres north of the city, has a 1,200-metre stretch of white sand backed by coral reefs — excellent for snorkelling and diving. Nearby Playa Esmeralda is quieter and equally beautiful. The diving around Gibara, a walled colonial town further west along the coast, includes flooded caverns at Tanques Azules that attract experienced cave divers.

Inland, the province has waterfalls — Guayabo is the tallest in Cuba — rivers, and the wildlife reserve at Cayo Saetía, a forested island where introduced buffalo, antelope, and deer roam freely. Frank País International Airport, near the city, has direct flights from Canada and several European cities.
