Trinidad is Cuba's best-preserved colonial town and a UNESCO World Heritage Site — the kind of place where the 18th century feels close enough to touch. Cobblestoned streets climb the hill past pastel-painted houses with red tile roofs, wrought-iron grilles, and heavy wooden doors that open onto cool interior courtyards. At the top sits the Plaza Mayor, flanked by former sugar baron mansions now turned into museums.

The town gets touristy in the afternoon when day-trip buses arrive from [Varadero](/northamerica/cuba/varadero) and the beach resorts. Come early in the morning or, better yet, stay the night in a casa particular — the private houses where families rent rooms are one of the best ways to experience Cuban hospitality. After dark, Trinidad comes alive: the Casa de la Música on the outdoor staircase off the Plaza Mayor is one of Cuba's great live music venues, with salsa bands playing under the stars.

Just outside town, the Valle de los Ingenios (Valley of the Sugar Mills) is another UNESCO site, and the beach at Playa Ancón is the best stretch of sand on Cuba's southern coast, a twenty-minute drive away.
