**Cuba When to Go** queries can be easily solved by understanding the climatic patterns that prevail in the island nation.

**Overview of Cuba Weather **

Cuba has a typical semi-tropical climate. The overall weather conditions in Cuba are moderate, interspersed by periods of heavy rainfall. The average annual temperature is around the 29 degrees Celsius mark. Most parts of the nation receive refreshing marine breezes. All these features make Cuba suited for vacationing purposes.

**Decoding Cuba Rainfall Patterns**

Cuba should be avoided during certain months of the year. It receives heavy rainfall that is concentrated for a period of about five months. The annual rainfall levels easily exceed 52 inches. The dedicated wet season stretches from the last week of May to the early part of October. The below-mentioned Hurricane Season is a part of this wet season. During the rainy season, the mountainous locations aren't recommended from a traveling perspective. Landslides and sudden road-closures are common. It is generally recommended that a traveler should always pack a raincoat or an umbrella, since an unannounced shower can be never ruled out.

**Understanding Cuba Hurricane Season**

This period often witnesses strong hurricanes that can disrupt travel plans. This doesn't mean that hurricanes are the norm, but the accompanying high precipitation and humidity levels can make traveling demanding. Peak of the Hurricane Season extends from July to the beginning of October.

**Cuba Humidity Issues**

The Cuban islands are known for high humidity levels for a major part of the year. In fact, relative humidity readings are often around 80%, which can make outdoor trips an exhausting affair. However, winds coming from the Gulf Stream and the northeastern winds ensure that precipitation-related problems are somewhat resolved.

**Cuban Summer and Winter Seasons**

Winters extend from November to April but the temperatures rarely dip below 20 degrees Celsius. The strong northern winds sweep through the islands, making the conditions windy throughout the day. In terms of temperature, December, January and February witness the lowest figures. Apart from the short winter period, the rest of the year is commonly referred to as the summer season.

**Cuba When to Go Queries Answered—Best Time to Visit Cuba**

The winter months are regarded as the best period to visit Cuba. The period from March to May generally witnesses the highest number of foreign visitors in Cuba. During this time of the year, rainfall is limited to a few sporadic showers. The humidity levels are also low and daytime temperatures encourage outdoor traveling.
