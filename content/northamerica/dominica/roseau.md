With so much wilderness to explore on [Dominica](/northamerica/dominica), visitors sometimes rush past the capital. Roseau is compact, genuinely lived-in, and worth a half-day. The Dominica Museum in the old courthouse on the waterfront gives a good grounding in the island's history — colonial, volcanic, and Kalinago. Admission is modest.

The old town centre has cobblestone streets — the stones were brought over as ballast in trading ships — lined with small shops and wooden colonial houses. The Anglican Cathedral is worth seeing. For shopping, head to Cork Street or along the Roseau river banks where vendors sell local produce and crafts.

The Botanical Gardens contain examples of most of the trees, shrubs, and flowers found on the island and serve as the headquarters of the National Park Office — a useful stop for maps and guides before heading into the interior.
