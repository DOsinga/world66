La Romana is a city on the southeastern coast of the [Dominican Republic](/northamerica/dominicanrepublic), best known as the gateway to some of the country's most popular resort areas. The city itself is a working sugar-mill town, but the main attraction for visitors is what surrounds it.

Casa de Campo, a vast luxury resort just east of town, has its own golf courses, private airstrip, and marina. La Marina at Casa de Campo is a pleasant spot for restaurants, ice cream, and strolling along the docks. Altos de Chavón, perched above the Río Chavón, is a recreation of a 16th-century Mediterranean village built in the 1970s as a cultural centre. Its stone amphitheatre hosts major concerts, and the views over the river gorge are dramatic.

The beaches at Bayahíbe and the offshore islands of Saona and Catalina, both reachable by boat, are the main natural draws — white sand, warm water, and easy snorkelling.
