Higüey is the capital of La Altagracia province and the gateway city for the eastern Dominican Republic. Most travelers blow through here on their way to Punta Cana and the beach resorts, but the city has one genuinely significant attraction: the Basílica de Nuestra Señora de la Altagracia, a striking modernist cathedral completed in 1971 that is the most important pilgrimage site in the country. On January 21st each year, hundreds of thousands of Dominicans converge here for the feast of the Virgin of Altagracia, the patron saint of the nation.

The basilica itself is an impressive piece of architecture, with a dramatic concrete arch rising 80 meters and an interior flooded with colored light. Even if you are not religious, the building is worth a stop. The old church next door, the Santuario de San Dionisio, dates back to the sixteenth century and provides a nice contrast.

Higüey is a busy, somewhat chaotic Dominican city that runs on its own rhythms. The streets around the central market are crowded and noisy, the traffic is creative, and the food stalls sell cheap and filling comida criolla. It is not a tourist town, which is part of its appeal. If you want a glimpse of everyday Dominican urban life away from the resort bubble, Higüey delivers that honestly.
