Luperon is a small fishing town on the north coast of the Dominican Republic, about 25 kilometres west of Puerto Plata. It is best known among sailors for its deep, protected natural harbour — one of the safest hurricane holes in the Caribbean — which attracts long-term cruisers from around the world.

The town itself is quiet and unhurried, far from the all-inclusive resort strip. Playa Grande, just minutes from the centre, is a beautiful stretch of white sand with clear, warm water and few visitors. The surrounding countryside is lush and green, and the atmosphere is about as far from mass tourism as you can get on this coast.
