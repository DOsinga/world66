Several worthwhile destinations lie within an hour or two of Punta Cana.

**La Romana and Altos de Chavon**

Vast expanses of sugar cane fields characterize La Romana, a city built and sustained by the sugar industry. Casa de Campo is a 7,000-acre property with two 18-hole golf courses, beaches, equestrian stables, and polo grounds. East of the resort is Altos de Chavon, a replica 16th-century Mediterranean village overlooking the Chavon River, featuring the St. Stanislaus Church, a Taino artifacts museum, a 5,000-seat amphitheater, and the Altos de Chavon School of Design affiliated with Parsons in New York. A shuttle boat reaches Isla Catalina in minutes -- good coral reef diving and snorkelling.

**Isla Saona**

A government-protected nature reserve off the southeast tip of the Dominican Republic, Isla Saona is famous for its fields of starfish, swimming with dolphins, and snorkelling in turquoise water. Catamarans and small motor boats leave from Bayahibe.

**Los Haitises National Park**

Northeast of Santo Domingo, Los Haitises is a protected area with wet forest, subtropical rainforest, mangrove swamps, and underground rivers flowing through caves with Taino petroglyphs and pictographs. Bird watching at Cayo Los Pajaros is excellent, and humpback whales visit the Samana sanctuary from January to March.
