Santa Bárbara de Samaná — known simply as Samaná — is the capital of the Samaná Province and the main town on the peninsula of the same name. It sits on a natural harbour at the southern edge of the peninsula, facing the wide blue arc of Samaná Bay. The setting is beautiful: green hills behind, fishing boats and pelicans in front, and coconut palms running down to the water.

The town is quieter and less developed than [Las Terrenas](/northamerica/dominicanrepublic/lasterrenas), the peninsula's other main settlement. There are a few good restaurants along the waterfront promenade, some lively bars, and a relaxed local pace that makes it a pleasant place to spend a day or two. It is also the main jumping-off point for whale watching, which is the best reason for most visitors to be here.

From January to March, hundreds of humpback whales come to Samaná Bay to breed and calve. The bay is one of the most important humpback breeding grounds in the North Atlantic, and the whale watching here — run by licensed operators from the waterfront — is genuinely excellent. You will likely see multiple whales, often very close to the boat, breaching and slapping their tails.

Beyond the whales, the surrounding landscape is worth exploring. The El Limón waterfall, reached by horseback or on foot through banana groves and rainforest, drops some 50 metres into a pool below. Playa Rincón, widely regarded as one of the best beaches in the Dominican Republic, is a short boat ride from the town dock.
