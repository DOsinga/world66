San Pedro de Macorís is a port city on the southeastern coast of the Dominican Republic, about 70 kilometres east of [Santo Domingo](/northamerica/dominicanrepublic/santodomingo). It is one of the country's most distinctive cities — a place with a real history, a fierce local pride, and a reputation that reaches far beyond its size.

The city is best known internationally as the birthplace of an extraordinary number of Major League Baseball players. Dominicans call it "the factory of shortstops," and in certain decades, it seemed like every other player on a major league roster came from San Pedro. The Baseball Museum on the Malecón traces this story, and on any afternoon you can watch kids playing in the streets and vacant lots, dreaming of the same path.

Beyond baseball, San Pedro has a layered history shaped by the sugar industry. The grand Victorian and neo-Gothic buildings along the waterfront — some beautifully maintained, others crumbling elegantly — were built by sugar barons and British West Indian immigrants in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The Cocolo community, descendants of those immigrants, brought their own traditions, including the Guloyas festivals recognized by UNESCO. The city centre rewards wandering: find the ornate Iglesia San Pedro Apóstol, the old Club Unión, and the remnants of the sugar mill infrastructure scattered around the outskirts.

The Malecón stretches along the bay and is lively in the evenings. Motoconchos — motorcycle taxis — are the dominant local transport and a quintessential part of life here. The city makes a straightforward day trip from Santo Domingo or [Boca Chica](/northamerica/dominicanrepublic/bocachica), though it rewards a longer stay if you want to go deeper into its history and character.
