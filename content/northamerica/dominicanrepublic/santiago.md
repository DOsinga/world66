Santiago de los Caballeros, the Dominican Republic's second city, sits in the heart of the fertile Cibao Valley surrounded by tobacco fields and low mountains. Founded in 1495, it is the country's major industrial centre and a proud, working city that runs on its own rhythm rather than on tourism.

This is not a beach destination — Santiago lies inland, a couple of hours south of the north coast resorts. What it offers instead is a window into everyday Dominican life: bustling markets, a lively carnival in February with elaborate costumes and floats, and a college-town energy driven by several universities. The Monumento a los Héroes de la Restauración, a towering marble column on a hilltop, dominates the skyline and offers sweeping views of the valley.

Santiago is the centre of Dominican cigar production. Several factories in the free-trade zone welcome visitors, and the local tobacco is considered among the best in the Caribbean. If baseball is more your thing, catch an Aguilas Cibaenas game at Estadio Cibao — the atmosphere is electric.
