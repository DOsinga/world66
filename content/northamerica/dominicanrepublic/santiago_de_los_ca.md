Santiago de los Caballeros is the second largest city in the [Dominican Republic](/northamerica/dominicanrepublic) and the cultural capital of the Cibao valley. Set in the fertile northern interior, Santiago is the heart of Dominican tobacco country — the surrounding plantations produce some of the world's finest cigars, and you can visit factories to watch the process from leaf to finished product.

The city has a grittier, more authentic feel than the beach resorts on the coast. The Monumento a los Héroes de la Restauración towers over the centre from a hilltop, and the streets below are full of local restaurants, merengue clubs, and a lively nightlife scene that caters almost entirely to Dominicans rather than tourists. Santiago makes a good base for exploring the Cibao region, with the mountain town of Jarabacoa and the peak of Pico Duarte within reach.
