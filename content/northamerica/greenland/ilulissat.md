Ilulissat — the name means "icebergs" in Greenlandic — is the third-largest town in Greenland and the main gateway to the Disko Bay region. It was founded in 1741 by Danish trader Jakob Severin and long known by its Danish name, Jakobshavn. About 4,500 people live here alongside roughly 3,000 sled dogs, which outnumber the human population for most of the year and are an unmistakable part of the town's character.

The town sits beside the Ilulissat Icefjord, one of the most spectacular natural phenomena in the Arctic. The Sermeq Kujalleq glacier feeds the fjord at a rate of 20–30 meters per day, calving enormous icebergs that pile up at the fjord mouth before breaking out into Disko Bay. The icefjord is a UNESCO World Heritage Site; trails along its edge and a modern interpretive center (the Icefjord Centre) make it accessible to visitors of all fitness levels.

In summer, boat trips among the icebergs run daily, whale watching is excellent in the bay, and the midnight sun means you can hike or kayak at any hour. In winter, the sea ice freezes and dog sledding begins — Ilulissat is the best place in Greenland to experience this, with organized tours available from November through April. The northern lights are visible from September through April.

Air Greenland flies to Ilulissat from Nuuk and Copenhagen; Icelandair also flies here from Reykjavik. A new international airport is planned for Ilulissat, expected to open by 2026.
