## Visa

Greenland is part of the Kingdom of Denmark but is not in the Schengen Area. However, Danish entry rules effectively apply: citizens of EU/EEA countries and most Western nations, including the US and Canada, can enter without a visa for stays up to 90 days. A valid passport is required. Check current requirements before travel; the political situation around Greenlandic independence is evolving.

## Currency

The Danish krone (DKK) is the currency. Cash is useful in smaller towns and settlements; cards (Visa, Mastercard) are accepted at most hotels, larger shops, and restaurants in bigger towns. ATMs exist in the main towns. Expect to pay Scandinavian prices or higher — imported goods are expensive, and essentially everything is imported.

## Language

Greenlandic (Kalaallisut) is the official language, and Danes are the second-largest group. English is widely spoken in tourist contexts and by younger people. Outside the main towns, Danish is more useful than English.

## Health

No vaccinations are required. The main health consideration is the cold: hypothermia and frostbite are real risks if you're hiking or traveling in winter. Medical facilities exist in the main towns; serious cases are evacuated to Denmark. Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage is strongly recommended.

## Communications

Mobile coverage (and good 4G) exists in the main towns. Outside them, you are out of range. Satellite communication devices are sensible for remote travel. The country code is +299.

## Electricity

220V, 50Hz, European two-pin plugs (same as Denmark).

## Tipping

Not customary. Prices include service.

## Shopping Hours

Most shops open Monday–Thursday 10:00–17:30, Friday 10:00–18:00, Saturday 9:00–13:00. Grocery shops and kiosks often keep longer hours. In smaller settlements, opening times are informal.
