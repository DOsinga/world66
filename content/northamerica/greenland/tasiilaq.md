Tasiilaq is the largest town on the east coast of Greenland, and the main base for exploring the remote East Greenland region. It is also known by its former name, Ammassalik — meaning "the place with capelin." The town lies just south of the Arctic Circle, set in a sheltered fjord with a narrow sea entrance, surrounded by steep mountains. Arriving by helicopter, the first view is of brightly-coloured houses clinging to the mountainsides above the fjord.

East Greenland is far less visited than the west coast, and the region around Tasiilaq has a wilder, more isolated feel. It is excellent country for hiking, ski touring in spring, and experiencing a more traditional Inuit hunting culture. The town has a small museum and a handful of guesthouses; organized tours and guides are available for backcountry travel.

Access is by Air Greenland helicopter from Kulusuk airport, which in turn receives direct flights from Reykjavik (Icelandair) in summer. It is one of the more remote entry points into Greenland.
