---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency:** The Eastern Caribbean dollar (EC$) is the official currency. The exchange rate is fixed at EC$2.70 to US$1. US dollars are widely accepted, especially in tourist areas and for larger transactions, though change will typically come back in EC$. ATMs are available at banks in St. George's and Grand Anse. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels and restaurants, less commonly at smaller guesthouses and roadside stalls.

**Visas:** Citizens of the US, Canada, UK, EU, and most Commonwealth countries do not need a visa for stays up to three months. You will need a valid passport (six months' validity recommended), a return or onward ticket, and proof of accommodation. Citizens of around 13 other countries can obtain a visa on arrival. Check current requirements before travel.

**Language:** English is the official language. A French-influenced Creole patois is also spoken, particularly among older residents.

**Time zone:** Atlantic Standard Time (AST), UTC-4. Grenada does not observe daylight saving time.

**Electricity:** 220V/50Hz, with UK-style three-pin plugs standard. Bring an adapter if you are coming from North America.

**Telephone:** The country code is +1-473. Mobile coverage is good across the main island; Digicel and Flow are the main providers. SIM cards are available at the airport and in St. George's.

**Health:** No vaccinations are required for entry, but Hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended. The main hospital is General Hospital in St. George's. Medical facilities are adequate for routine care; for serious emergencies, evacuation to Barbados or Trinidad may be necessary. Tap water in St. George's is generally safe; bottled water is widely available.

**Safety:** Grenada is one of the safer Caribbean islands for travellers. Petty theft does occur, particularly on Grand Anse Beach. Take sensible precautions with valuables. The US intervened militarily in 1983 following a coup; the island has been stable since.

**Tipping:** Not obligatory, but 10–15% is customary in restaurants where a service charge is not already included.
