Basse-Terre is the administrative capital of Guadeloupe, a small city on the southern coast of the island's western wing. It sits at the foot of La Soufrière, an active volcano that looms over the town and makes for dramatic hiking. The city is quieter and less touristy than Pointe-à-Pitre, with a colonial old town, a 17th-century fort, and good access to the Guadeloupe National Park.
