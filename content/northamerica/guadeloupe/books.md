title: "Books"
type: section
---

Reading about Guadeloupe before or during your visit can add a great deal to the experience — the island's history, its Creole literature, and the complex legacy of colonialism and slavery are all subjects with a rich body of work.

Patrick Chamoiseau is Guadeloupe's (and Martinique's) most celebrated novelist. His novel *Texaco* (1992), winner of the Prix Goncourt, follows the history of Martinique through the story of a woman living in a shantytown on the outskirts of Fort-de-France — but it speaks to the whole French Caribbean experience. *Solibo Magnifique* is a good entry point into his work.

For history, *The Mighty Experiment* by Seymour Drescher covers the abolition of slavery in the British Caribbean, useful context for understanding the French Caribbean as well. Léon-François Hoffmann's work on Haitian and French Caribbean literature is also worth seeking out.

The Memorial ACTe museum in Pointe-à-Pitre has an excellent bookshop with titles in French on slavery, Creole culture, and Caribbean history — harder to find elsewhere.
