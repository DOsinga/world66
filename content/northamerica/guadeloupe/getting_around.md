---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Renting a car is by far the best way to explore Basse-Terre and Grande-Terre. The roads are in good condition, driving is on the right, and your home driver's license is valid. Car rental agencies have offices at Pôle Caraïbes airport and in the main resort areas. Public buses (called "cars") run from early morning to early evening on major routes, but service is infrequent on secondary roads and very limited on Sundays. Taxis are widely available but expensive — always agree on a fare before getting in.

To get between the main island and the outer islands, take a ferry. L'Express des Îles and Val' Ferry operate regular catamaran services from the ferry terminal in Pointe-à-Pitre to Les Saintes, Marie-Galante, and La Désirade. Journey times are roughly 1 hour to Les Saintes, 45 minutes to Marie-Galante, and 45 minutes to La Désirade. Services run several times daily in high season, less frequently off-season. Ferries also run from Saint-François (on Grande-Terre's east coast) to Marie-Galante and La Désirade, which can be more convenient depending on where you're based.

On the outer islands, renting a scooter or bicycle makes more sense than a car, particularly on Les Saintes and La Désirade where the distances are short and car traffic is minimal. Marie-Galante has a small bus network on weekdays. Taxis are available on all the islands but should be arranged in advance outside of peak hours.

There are small airports on Marie-Galante, Les Saintes, and La Désirade with flights operated by Air Antilles from Pointe-à-Pitre — faster than the ferry but not always more convenient.
