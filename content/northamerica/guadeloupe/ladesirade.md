La Désirade is a small, quiet island about fifteen kilometres east of Grande-Terre, the easternmost point of Guadeloupe. It draws far fewer visitors than the main islands, which is precisely the appeal — empty beaches, unhurried villages, and a pace of life that the rest of the Caribbean lost decades ago.

The island is home to a protected iguana reserve where the Lesser Antillean iguana, endangered elsewhere in the region, thrives in relative peace. A single road runs along the southern coast connecting the handful of settlements, and the rocky northern shore drops steeply into the Atlantic. Come here when you want to disappear for a few days.
