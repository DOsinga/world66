Les Saintes is a small archipelago of eight islands just south of [Guadeloupe](/northamerica/guadeloupe), often called the most beautiful bay in the world — a claim that feels less like hyperbole once you arrive. Only two of the islands are inhabited: Terre-de-Haut and Terre-de-Bas, with Terre-de-Haut drawing most visitors.

The atmosphere is more relaxed and intimate than mainland Guadeloupe. Colourful houses line the waterfront of Terre-de-Haut's single village, fishing boats bob in a turquoise bay, and Fort Napoléon sits on the hill above with panoramic views. There are no large resorts and few cars — most people get around on foot or by scooter.

The beaches are gorgeous, particularly Pompierre and Pain de Sucre, a small cove framed by a distinctive sugarloaf rock. The snorkelling is excellent right off the shore. The local speciality is tourment d'amour, a coconut tart you will find in every bakery.
