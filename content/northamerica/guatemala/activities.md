title: "Activities"

---

Guatemala's topography — 37 volcanoes, two coastlines, highland lakes, and jungle — makes it one of the best adventure destinations in Central America.

**Volcano hiking**

Hiking a volcano is the quintessential Guatemala experience. Pacaya, easily reached from Antigua or Guatemala City, is an active volcano where you can walk across hardened lava fields and sometimes get close to active flows. The hike is accessible to reasonably fit travelers. Acatenango, also near Antigua, is a more demanding overnight climb with spectacular views of neighboring Volcán de Fuego erupting through the night — genuinely stunning but cold and steep. Santa María above Quetzaltenango offers the best panorama in the western highlands.

**Whitewater rafting**

The rivers draining Guatemala's volcanic highlands offer some of Central America's best whitewater. The Cahabón River near Cobán and the Nahualate near Quetzaltenango are popular. Most operators run half-day and full-day trips.

**Kayaking and swimming**

Lake Atitlán is superb for kayaking. Several villages around the lake rent kayaks by the hour. The thermal springs at Fuentes Georginas near Quetzaltenango are well worth an afternoon. The pools of Semuc Champey — natural limestone bridges over the Cahabón River — are arguably the most beautiful swimming spot in the country.

**Birdwatching**

Guatemala is one of the top birding destinations in Central America, with over 700 recorded species. The cloud forests of Alta Verapaz (around Cobán) and the highland Quetzal reserve are the best places to spot the resplendent quetzal, the national bird. Tikal's jungle is excellent for toucans, parrots, and scarlet macaws.

**Spanish language study**

Guatemala, particularly Antigua and Quetzaltenango, has long been among the best places in Latin America to study Spanish. Schools are abundant, prices are low (around Q800–Q1,200 per week for 20 hours of one-on-one instruction), and homestay options put the language in constant use. Quetzaltenango is cheaper and less touristic than Antigua; many serious students prefer it.

**Cycling**

Mountain biking is popular around Antigua, Lake Atitlán, and the western highlands. Several operators rent decent bikes and run guided rides. The road from Panajachel around the southern shore of Lake Atitlán to Santiago Atitlán is a rewarding full-day route.
