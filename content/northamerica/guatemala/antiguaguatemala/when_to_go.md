---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The best time to visit Antigua is during Semana Santa (Holy Week), the most spectacular religious celebration in Latin America. Enormous processions carry massive floats through the cobblestone streets, and local families lay elaborate alfombras — carpets of coloured sawdust, flowers, and pine needles — along the routes, only for the processions to walk over them. The atmosphere is intense and deeply moving, whether you are religious or not.

Antigua's dry season runs from November to April, making it the most comfortable time to visit. The rainy season (May–October) brings afternoon downpours, but mornings are usually clear and the surrounding volcanoes and hillsides turn brilliantly green.
