Cobán is the capital of Alta Verapaz, set among cloud-covered hills at 1,320 metres, about four hours north of Guatemala City by bus. The surrounding region is one of the wettest in Guatemala — the clouds rarely lift entirely, giving it a permanently green, misty character that locals call "chipi-chipi" (fine drizzle).

The region produces some of Guatemala's finest coffee and cardamom, and a visit to a coffee farm during harvest (October to February) is one of the better excursions in the area. The Semuc Champey natural pools — limestone bridges over the Cahabón River, filled with clear turquoise water — are about 60 km from Cobán and justify the journey on their own. The Grutas de Lanquín, caves with a dramatic river flowing through them, are nearby.

Cobán itself is a relaxed provincial town with a pleasant central park and some good cheap restaurants. The National Folklore Festival in late July/early August brings indigenous performers from across the country. The Q'eqchi' Maya are the dominant indigenous group in this region, and their influence is visible in local food, textiles, and the town's ceremonial life.

Getting there: regular buses from Guatemala City's Terminal del Norte. Shuttles from Antigua and Flores also available through tour operators.
