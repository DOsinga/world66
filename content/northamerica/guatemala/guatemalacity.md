Guatemala City is the capital and by far the largest city in Central America (excluding Mexico), with a metropolitan population over 3 million. It sits in a wide valley at about 1,500 metres elevation, giving it a mild climate year-round.

Most travelers pass through rather than linger. The city's Zone 1 historic center has the National Palace, the Metropolitan Cathedral, and the Mercado Central, and is worth a morning. The modern zones (9 and 10) have good restaurants, the INGUAT tourist office, and most international businesses. The National Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology in Zone 13, near the airport, has an excellent Maya collection including stelae and jade.

Safety is a genuine concern in certain areas of the capital — Zone 1 at night, the bus terminals, and peripheral neighborhoods. The tourist zones (Zonas 9, 10, 13, 14) are considerably safer. Use Uber rather than street taxis.

[Antigua](/northamerica/guatemala/antiguaguatemala) is 45 km to the west by shuttle (about 1 hour), and most travelers base themselves there rather than in the capital.
