Kaminaljuyú was one of the most important pre-classic Maya cities, flourishing between about 1500 BCE and 1200 CE in what is now the western edge of Guatemala City. At its peak it had hundreds of temple mounds, a ball court, reservoirs and an irrigation system, and was a major trading hub linking the Maya highlands with the Pacific coast. Today most of the site has been swallowed by urban sprawl — it sits surrounded by modern houses in Zona 7 — but a small archaeological park preserves several mounds and plazas, and the best pieces recovered from the excavations (remarkable carved stelae and ceramics) are on display at the **National Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology** in Zona 13.
