---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Most travellers give Guatemala City a single day before heading on to Antigua or the highlands, and that is about right. The essential stops are the **National Palace** and **Cathedral** on Plaza de la Constitución in Zona 1, the bustling **Mercado Central** directly underneath the cathedral, and then a taxi south to the two best museums in the country: the **Popol Vuh Museum** (private collection of Maya ceramics) and the **Ixchel Museum of Indigenous Dress**, both on the campus of Universidad Francisco Marroquín in Zona 10. If you have time, the **National Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology** in Zona 13 — near the airport — has the best state collection of Maya stelae and jade. The pre-classic Maya site of **Kaminaljuyú** on the western edge of the city is archaeologically important but has been largely swallowed by urban sprawl and will only interest specialists.
