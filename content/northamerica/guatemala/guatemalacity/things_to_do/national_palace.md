The Palacio Nacional de la Cultura — usually just "Palacio Nacional" — is the grandest building in Guatemala City and the symbolic centre of the country. Built in the 1940s under President Jorge Ubico in a heavy neoclassical-baroque style, with green volcanic stone walls that give it the nickname *Palacio Verde*, it served as the seat of government until 1996 and is now a museum and cultural centre. The interior holds a sequence of tiled courtyards, a monumental staircase, and the famous murals by **Alfredo Gálvez Suárez** depicting scenes from Guatemalan history. The "Km 0" point from which all highway distances in Guatemala are measured lies in the courtyard, marked by a two-handed hand holding a white rose that is changed daily. Free guided tours run throughout the day.
