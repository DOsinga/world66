Huehuetenango ("Huehue") is a departmental capital in the far northwest highlands, a jumping-off point for the remote Cuchumatanes Mountains — the highest non-volcanic range in Central America, with peaks over 3,800 metres. The mountains are spectacular and largely off the tourist trail.

Just outside the town are the ruins of Zaculeu, the former capital of the Mam Maya people, captured by Pedro de Alvarado in 1525. The site was controversially restored in the 1940s with fresh white plaster by the United Fruit Company, giving it an odd, too-clean appearance, but the hilltop position is dramatic and crowds are minimal.

The town of Todos Santos Cuchumatán, two hours into the mountains from Huehuetenango, is one of the most traditional communities in Guatemala. The Mam Maya here still wear the distinctive red-and-white striped trousers and embroidered shirts that identify the town. The annual horse race on November 1 — All Saints' Day — is one of the most extraordinary festivals in the country (see Festivals).

Getting there: regular buses from Guatemala City (5–6 hours). Huehuetenango is also on the route to the Mexican border at La Mesilla, useful for travelers crossing to San Cristóbal de las Casas.
