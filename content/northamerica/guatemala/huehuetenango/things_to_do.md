---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The main draws in and around Huehuetenango are the pre-Columbian ruins of **Zaculeu**, a few kilometres outside the town, and the extraordinary mountain village of **Todos Santos Cuchumatán**, two hours up into the Cuchumatanes. Zaculeu was the capital of the Mam Maya kingdom until it fell to Pedro de Alvarado's forces after a two-month siege in 1525; its dramatic hilltop position is still worth a visit, though the 1940s restoration by the United Fruit Company — who covered the ruins in bright white plaster — gave it an oddly uniform look that archaeologists have never forgiven. Todos Santos is one of the most traditional Maya towns in Guatemala: the men still wear the distinctive red-and-white striped trousers and embroidered shirts, and the All Saints' Day (1 November) **horse race** — in which drunk horsemen charge up and down the town on borrowed horses — is one of the most extraordinary sights in Central America. Further into the mountains, the Cuchumatanes themselves offer long walks, high-altitude villages, and some of the best mountain scenery in Guatemala for hikers prepared to rough it.
