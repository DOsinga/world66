Across the lake from [Panajachel](/northamerica/guatemala/lakeatitlan/panajachel), San Pedro la Laguna is a beautiful pueblo that captures the essence of Guatemala in one small place. It sits at the base of the San Pedro volcano on the southwestern shore of [Lake Atitlan](/northamerica/guatemala/lakeatitlan), and caters to the backpacker scene like no other town in the country.

The draw is the mix: indigenous Tz'utujil Maya culture alongside a revolving cast of international travellers, a bounty of Spanish schools, outdoor activities from volcano climbing to kayaking, art classes in painting and weaving, and an international restaurant scene serving local flavours alongside European and Asian cuisine. Full moon parties, live music, and local celebrations round it out — all at remarkably affordable prices.

San Pedro is less polished than Panajachel and quieter than San Marcos, which suits the longer-stay travellers who tend to settle here for weeks rather than days. The town itself is walkable, steep, and dusty, with the lake and the volcano always in view.
