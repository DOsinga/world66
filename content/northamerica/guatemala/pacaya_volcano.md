Pacaya is the most climbed volcano in Guatemala and one of Central America's most accessible active volcanoes, rising to 2,552 metres (8,371 feet) about 50 km south of [Guatemala City](/northamerica/guatemala/guatemalacity). It has been almost continuously in eruption since 1965, and on a good day you can get close enough to feel the heat from active lava flows.

The standard way to visit is on a day tour from [Antigua Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala/antiguaguatemala) — operators run groups out daily for around $15–20 per person including transport and a guide. If you have your own vehicle, head south from Guatemala City toward Escuintla and turn off for San Vicente Pacaya and Pacaya Volcano National Park before reaching Palín.

The main trail starts at San Francisco de Sales, where you pay park admission (around Q50–80 for foreign visitors). The path climbs to the rim of the old Cerro Chino crater — from here the volcanic cones of Acatenango, Fuego, and Agua are visible — before the final steep push up the main cone through ash and loose rock. The descent, near-skiing down the slope, is the most entertaining part. Two real hazards: sulfur clouds (unpleasant to breathe) and small hot vents in the ground — watch where you sit.
