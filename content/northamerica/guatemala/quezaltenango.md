Quetzaltenango — universally called Xela (SHEH-la), from its original K'iche' name Xelajú — is Guatemala's second city and, for many travelers, a more authentic and rewarding base than Antigua. Sitting at 2,333 metres in the western highlands, it is the cultural and commercial capital of K'iche' Maya Guatemala.

The city has a fine neoclassical center around the Parque Central America, with the Cathedral of the Diocese on one side and the Municipal Theater decorated with busts of local scholars. The market is large, working-class, and real — not oriented toward tourists. The surrounding hills and volcanic peaks are excellent for hiking; Santa María Volcano (3,772 m) offers one of the best highland panoramas in the country.

Xela is the second most popular Spanish-language study destination in Guatemala after Antigua, with numerous schools offering competitive rates and more immersive homestay experiences in a less touristic setting. Many students prefer it precisely because daily life is not shaped around foreign visitors.

Getting there: regular buses and shuttles from Guatemala City (about 4 hours), Antigua (about 3 hours), and the Mexican border at La Mesilla.
