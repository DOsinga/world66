---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

Guatemala produces some of the finest handicrafts in Latin America, and buying direct from artisans supports highland communities that depend on weaving and craft production as a primary income.

**Textiles**

Handwoven huipiles (blouses) and cortes (wrap skirts) are the standout buy. Each highland community has distinct patterns and color combinations woven into the fabric, and the best pieces represent days or weeks of work on a backstrap loom. Chichicastenango's Thursday and Sunday market is the most famous place to shop, though bargaining there is expected and prices reflect tourist demand. For quality and fair trade, seek out weaving cooperatives — San Juan La Laguna on Lake Atitlán has several women's cooperatives selling directly at fixed, fair prices.

**Jade**

Guatemala was the primary source of jade for the ancient Maya, and the jade deposits in the Motagua Valley are still worked today. Jade jewelry and carved pieces are widely available, particularly in Antigua. Genuine Guatemalan jade ranges from deep blue-green to pale grey-green and apple-green; beware of dyed stones sold as jade.

**Coffee**

Guatemala produces world-class coffee, particularly from the highland regions of Antigua, Huehuetenango, Cobán (Alta Verapaz), and Atitlán. Whole-bean bags from specific farms make excellent and compact souvenirs. Look for bags marked with the origin region.

**Markets**

Beyond Chichicastenango, good markets include the Mercado Central in Antigua (daily, strong on textiles and vegetables), the central market in Quetzaltenango, and the markets in San Francisco El Alto (Friday) and Totonicapán (Tuesday and Saturday), which are more oriented toward local commerce than tourism.

**Woodcarving, ceramics, and masks**

The highlands produce painted wooden masks used in traditional dances, carved furniture, and a tradition of brightly glazed ceramics centered in Rabinal. Antigua's Paseo de los Museos shopping area has quality crafts at higher prices than markets, but you avoid the bargaining.
