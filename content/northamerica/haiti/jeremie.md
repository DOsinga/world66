Jérémie is the capital of the Grand'Anse department on Haiti's southwestern peninsula, known as the city of poets for the unusual number of writers and intellectuals it has produced. The area around Jérémie has some of Haiti's most beautiful and remote coastline, with excellent beaches and lush hillsides that receive more rainfall than the rest of the country.

Getting to Jérémie overland from Port-au-Prince is a long haul — the road south through Léogane and along the southern peninsula deteriorates badly past Les Cayes. Most people flew or took a boat. Sunrise Airways has domestic service to Jérémie's small airport.

The town itself is relaxed and small. The surrounding department is one of the poorest in the country. Hurricane Matthew (2016) devastated the area, destroying crops and infrastructure, with effects still felt years later.
