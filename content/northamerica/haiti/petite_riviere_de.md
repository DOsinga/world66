Petite Rivière de Nippes is a small fishing village on Haiti's southern coast, in the Nippes department. It is a genuinely quiet place — far from the capital's chaos and from the main tourist circuit — where life moves at the pace of the sea and the market day.

There is no tourist infrastructure here. What it offers is the chance to see an ordinary Haitian coastal community: the morning fish market, boats on the beach, cooking smells from open kitchens, and the extraordinary warmth that Haitians extend to visitors who come without an agenda.
