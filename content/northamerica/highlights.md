---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

North America covers an extraordinary range — from the Arctic ice cap to tropical reefs, from ancient Maya cities to the neon canyons of Manhattan. What follows are the experiences that make this continent worth crossing the world for.

**The US national parks.** The [United States](/northamerica/unitedstates) has over 60 national parks, and the best of them are among the most spectacular landscapes on earth. Yellowstone's geysers and wildlife, Yosemite's granite walls, the Grand Canyon's mile-deep gorge, the red rock arches of Utah, the glaciers of Alaska — these are not overhyped. Go in shoulder season (September, May) to avoid the worst of the summer crowds.

**The Canadian Rockies.** Banff and Jasper national parks in [Canada](/northamerica/canada) deliver mountains, turquoise lakes, and glaciers in a setting that feels almost designed to be beautiful. The Icefields Parkway between the two towns is one of the world's great drives. [Vancouver](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/vancouver) makes an excellent base for exploring British Columbia's coast and islands.

**Mexico City.** One of the great megacities. [Mexico City](/northamerica/mexico/mexicocity) has world-class museums — the Museo Nacional de Antropología is extraordinary — a street food scene that will ruin you for tacos anywhere else, colonial architecture, thriving contemporary art, and a creative energy that surprises visitors who expect only chaos. Give it at least four days.

**The Maya ruins.** The ancient Maya civilization left ruins scattered across [Mexico](/northamerica/mexico), [Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala), [Belize](/northamerica/belize), and [Honduras](/northamerica/honduras). Chichen Itza gets the crowds, but Tikal — rising above the Guatemalan jungle canopy — is the one that takes your breath away. Palenque in Chiapas is atmospheric and beautifully set in the forest. Copan in Honduras is smaller but famous for its intricately carved stelae.

**New York City.** [New York](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork) is relentless, electric, and endlessly walkable. The neighborhoods each have their own character, and there is genuinely no other city on the continent that packs so much — art, food, architecture, culture, street life — into such a dense space. It repays multiple visits and never fully exhausts itself.

**The Caribbean reef.** The Mesoamerican Barrier Reef — the world's second longest — runs from Mexico's Yucatan coast through [Belize](/northamerica/belize) to [Honduras](/northamerica/honduras)'s Bay Islands. The Great Blue Hole in Belize is a bucket-list dive. Bonaire offers shore diving you can do right off the beach. Cozumel, the Cayman Islands, and Turks and Caicos all have world-class reef systems. Even if you only snorkel, the water clarity and marine life are extraordinary.

**New Orleans.** There is nowhere else quite like it in North America. Jazz pours out of every doorway in the French Quarter, the food is serious (beignets, gumbo, po' boys, crawfish), and the city has a decadent, subtropical character that resists the generic American city template. The cemeteries, the Garden District, the Frenchmen Street music clubs — all worth your time.

**Cuba.** [Cuba](/northamerica/cuba) is unlike anywhere else in the Americas. Havana's crumbling pastel grandeur, the classic cars, the rum, the music — it's all real, not a theme park. The country is changing, slowly and unevenly, and whatever you find when you arrive will be different from whatever you've read about. Go curious and without fixed expectations.

**Costa Rica's wildlife.** [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica) has made protecting its natural ecosystems a national project, and the results are remarkable: 25 percent of the country is protected. Monteverdo's cloud forest, the Osa Peninsula's wilderness, Tortuguero's sea turtle beaches, and Arenal's volcano and hot springs are all accessible without requiring serious expedition logistics. The wildlife density — toucans, sloths, monkeys, tree frogs, quetzals — can feel almost absurdly generous.

**Guatemala's highlands.** Lake Atitlan, ringed by volcanoes and Maya villages, is one of the most beautiful bodies of water in the Americas. The market town of Chichicastenango, the colonial city of [Antigua Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala/antigua), and the overnight volcano trek to Acatenango (with its views of Fuego erupting through the night) make [Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala) one of the most rewarding destinations on the mainland.

**Greenland.** [Greenland](/northamerica/greenland) is for the adventurous: the world's largest island, mostly ice, with a narrow summer window and a landscape unlike anything else. Kayaking among icebergs in the Ilulissat Icefjord, hiking the ice cap, watching the northern lights in September — these are experiences that require effort and cost, but deliver accordingly.
