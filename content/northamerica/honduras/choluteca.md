Choluteca is a mid-sized city in southern Honduras, close to the borders with Nicaragua and El Salvador. It sits in a broad agricultural valley on the Choluteca River, and the surrounding area is one of the hottest in Central America — temperatures regularly exceed 35°C in the dry season. The city is a transport hub for travellers crossing between the three countries.

The town centre has a pleasant colonial plaza and the city is known throughout Honduras for its street food, particularly baleadas — flour tortillas stuffed with beans, cheese, and meat. The local cuisine is hearty and inexpensive. Despite its heat and its workaday character, Choluteca is a useful base for the Gulf of Fonseca, a body of water shared by Honduras, El Salvador, and Nicaragua, and for the nearby mangrove forests.
