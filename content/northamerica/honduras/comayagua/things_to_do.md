title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Comayagua's colonial heritage is concentrated around the central plaza and can be explored on foot in a few hours.

**Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception** — Completed in 1715, the cathedral is one of the finest colonial churches in Central America. Its most famous feature is the Moorish-style clock in the tower, believed to have been made for the Alhambra in Granada over a thousand years ago and brought to Honduras by the Spanish. It is one of the oldest functioning clocks in the Americas.

**Archaeological Museum of Comayagua** — Housed in the former presidential palace, with a collection focused on the Lenca civilisation and the El Cajón archaeological project.

**Museum of Colonial Religious Art** — A small museum with religious artefacts, gold and silver pieces from the 16th to 18th centuries, and colonial-era paintings.
