Gracias is a quiet colonial town in the mountains of western [Honduras](/northamerica/honduras), founded in 1536 and briefly the capital of all Central America. It sits at the foot of Celaque, the country's highest peak at 2,849 metres, and serves as the gateway to Parque Nacional Montaña de Celaque — one of the best cloud forest hikes in Honduras.

The town itself has cobblestone streets, a handful of crumbling churches, and the restored Fuerte San Cristóbal on a hill overlooking the valley. Below the fort, natural hot springs fed by volcanic activity make for a good soak after a day of hiking. The pace is slow and the tourism infrastructure is basic but friendly — a few small hotels, comedores serving baleadas and plato típico, and not much else.

Gracias is about five hours by bus from [San Pedro Sula](/northamerica/honduras/sanpedrosula) on a winding mountain road. Most travellers come for the Celaque trek, which takes two days with a night at the summit shelter, but the town is worth a day or two on its own for its colonial atmosphere and mountain setting.
