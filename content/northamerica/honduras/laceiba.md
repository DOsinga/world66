La Ceiba is Honduras's third-largest city, on the Caribbean coast at the base of the Cordillera Nombre de Dios mountains. It's primarily known as a transit hub — the main departure point for ferries to the Bay Islands of Roatán and Útila — but also has genuine character of its own.

The city has a reputation for nightlife, and the Zona Viva strip along Avenida 15 has the densest concentration of bars and clubs in the country. Carnival in May, the Feria Internacional de La Ceiba, is one of Central America's larger street festivals and draws visitors from across the region.

Pico Bonito National Park begins almost at the city limits — a cloud forest reserve with waterfalls, rivers, and good birding. Rafting on the Cangrejal River, which runs through the park's lower reaches, is a popular day trip. The river has Class III–IV sections suitable for experienced rafters and calmer stretches for beginners.
