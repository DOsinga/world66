Lake Yojoa is Honduras's largest natural lake, set in a lush valley between two national parks. The surrounding area is one of the best birdwatching destinations in Central America, with over 480 species recorded around the lake's shores and forested hillsides.

The star attraction nearby is Pulhapanzak waterfall, a spectacular 43-meter cascade that you can walk behind with a guide. Coffee plantations dot the surrounding highlands, and several offer tours where you can follow the bean from plant to cup. The lake itself is good for bass fishing and kayaking, though swimming is less popular due to the reedy shoreline.

Getting here is straightforward. Lake Yojoa sits right along the main highway between San Pedro Sula and Tegucigalpa, making it an easy stop or side trip. Most visitors base themselves at one of the small lodges on the western shore, where the birding is best and the views across the water to the mountains are hard to beat.
