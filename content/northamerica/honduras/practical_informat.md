---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency:** The Honduran lempira (HNL) is the national currency. US dollars are widely accepted in tourist areas and on the Bay Islands. ATMs are available in all major cities; use them inside banks or hotels rather than on the street, and be aware of your surroundings when withdrawing cash.

**Visas:** Most nationalities including US, EU, Canadian, UK, and Australian citizens can enter visa-free for up to 90 days. Honduras is part of the CA-4 agreement with Guatemala, El Salvador, and Nicaragua — the 90-day allowance applies across all four countries, not per country. A departure tax of around US$48 is normally included in international airfares.

**Language:** Spanish is the official language. English is spoken widely in the Bay Islands and in some tourist-oriented businesses on the mainland. Garifuna communities along the Caribbean coast speak their own language as well as Spanish.

**Safety:** Exercise caution throughout Honduras, and a high degree of caution in San Pedro Sula and Tegucigalpa. Violent crime, particularly in urban areas, is a genuine concern. Practical measures: take radio-dispatched taxis rather than flagging cabs on the street; don't display valuables; avoid local buses in the cities; travel between cities only during daylight. Most travelers on the standard tourist circuit — Copán, the north coast, the Bay Islands — visit without incident, but complacency is unwise.

**Health:** Tap water is not safe to drink in most areas; stick to bottled or filtered water. Mosquito-borne illnesses including dengue fever are present, particularly on the coast and at lower elevations. Use repellent, especially in the evenings. No vaccinations are required for entry unless arriving from a yellow fever zone, but check current recommendations before travel.

**Communications:** Mobile coverage is decent in cities and the main tourist areas but patchy in rural regions and La Mosquitia. SIM cards from Tigo or Claro are inexpensive and widely available.

**Electricity:** 110V, 60Hz, the same as the US and Canada. Type A and B plugs (the standard US flat-pin type).

**Time zone:** UTC-6, no daylight saving.
