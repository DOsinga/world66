Puerto Cortes is Honduras's main Caribbean port, handling the majority of the country's international trade through its deep-water harbor. The town has a functional, working character that reflects this role -- container cranes and cargo ships dominate the waterfront rather than palm trees and beach bars. Yet the surrounding coastline holds some pleasant surprises for travelers willing to look beyond the port infrastructure.

Playa de Omoa, a short drive west along the coast, offers a long stretch of dark sand beach and the well-preserved colonial fortress of Fortaleza de San Fernando de Omoa, built by the Spanish in the 18th century to defend against pirates and British raiders. The fort is one of the most impressive colonial military structures in Central America and makes for a worthwhile half-day visit. East of Puerto Cortes, the coastline toward Tela becomes increasingly attractive, with quieter beaches and the Lancetilla Botanical Garden, one of the largest tropical botanical collections in the Americas.

Puerto Cortes itself has a lively market area near the central park and a handful of seafood restaurants serving the fresh catch from local fishing boats. The town is best used as a transit point -- it sits on the main highway between San Pedro Sula and the north coast, making it a logical stop if you are heading to the Bay Islands or the Garifuna communities further east.
