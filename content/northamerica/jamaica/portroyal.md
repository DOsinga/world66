Port Royal sits at the tip of the Palisadoes spit that encloses Kingston Harbour. In the 17th century it was one of the largest cities in the Americas — a notorious pirate haven and trading post where Henry Morgan and other buccaneers brought their plunder. In 1692 a massive earthquake dropped much of the city into the harbour, killing thousands instantly. The sunken city and its cargo are still down there.

What remains is a small, historically rich village with Fort Charles — Jamaica's oldest standing fort, built in 1655 — and the Giddy House, a former artillery store that tilted 45 degrees in the 1907 earthquake and stayed that way. The underwater ruins are accessible for snorkellers and divers.

Getting here: a ferry runs regularly from the Ocean Boulevard terminal in Kingston. The ride takes about 20 minutes and is cheap. It's an easy half-day trip from Kingston.
