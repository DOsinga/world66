Spanish Town, 20 km west of Kingston along the main highway, was Jamaica's capital for over 300 years — from the Spanish founding in the 1530s until Kingston took over in 1872. Almost no visitors come here, which is a pity.

The Georgian square at the centre of town is one of the finest colonial ensembles in the Caribbean. The ruins of the King's House (the former governor's residence, burned in 1925), the handsome Rodney Memorial, the old House of Assembly, and the Court House frame a formal square that gives some sense of the town's former importance.

The Jamaica Archives are here, holding the most complete records of Jamaican history anywhere, including birth, death, and marriage records going back to the 17th century.

Spanish Town today is a working-class city with a higher crime rate than its colonial grandeur might suggest. Visit in the daytime, stay in the central area, and use it as a half-day excursion from Kingston rather than a base.
