---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Jamaica's climate is tropical, warm year-round, with a genuine distinction between the dry and wet seasons.

**December to April** is the dry season and peak tourist period. Temperatures are comfortable (mid-20s Celsius), rainfall is low, and the beaches are at their best. Expect higher prices and more crowded resorts. February and March tend to be the sweetest months — sunny, dry, and not yet baking hot.

**May and June** are a good shoulder option. Rates start to drop, the island is less crowded, and the weather is still largely dry. Reggae Sumfest draws big crowds to Montego Bay in mid-July.

**July to November** is the wet season and officially hurricane season (June to November, with peak risk in August and September). Rain usually comes in short afternoon bursts rather than all-day downpours. Prices drop significantly. Jamaica is less exposed to hurricanes than the northern Caribbean islands, but it does get hit — Hurricane Melissa in October 2025 caused significant damage on the western part of the island. Travel in this period carries some risk; travel insurance covering hurricane disruption is strongly recommended.

**Inland and mountain areas** (the Blue Mountains, Cockpit Country, Mandeville) are noticeably cooler than the coast and receive more rainfall year-round. Pack a layer if you're heading up.
