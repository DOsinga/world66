---
title: "Beaches in Acapulco"
type: section
---

Every beach in Acapulco has a delightfully discrete personality forming a stunning
kaleidoscope of natural beauty, thrilling water sports, dinner by the
bay, warm drinking sessions and beach side games. The **Acapulco Beaches **guide offers visitors information on the beach scene and best beaches to visit in this coastal stunner.

**Pie de las Cuesta
**

Pie de las Cuesta is an expansive stretch of yellow sand and is the
best place to go beach walking in the entire region. The long walk can
be extremely pleasant and will help you tune-in to the miracle of
nature’s aesthetics. Coyoca Lagoon is situated adjacent to the beach
and features an array of restaurants and accommodation options. This
beach belongs to the quieter clique and is superb for visitors looking
to experience the true essence of a tropical beach ambiance.

**Playas Caleta**

Playas
Caleta is a conventional pick and a local favorite for its serene
waters and breathtaking sunset. Great place to hobnob with the locals
and catch a slice of their culture and lifestyle. For more rudimentary
beaches, head to Playa Roquete and Marin that are housed in the old
section of the town. An eco-tourist’s haven that believes in living it
up the rustic way, Rouqueta and Marin are secluded spots that can be
reached only via boats and water taxis. Playa La Angosta is a manna
from heaven for visitors seeking a more passive holiday that is hard to
find at overtly tourist beaches. The beach is a small mass that is
flanked by two overpowering cliffs on either side. Visitors will be
forgiven for missing Playas Manzanillo. The beach is shacked in an
extremely isolated location near Caleta and serves lip-smacking sea
food, while featuring some great historical sight-seeing options.

**Playa Las Hamcas**

For a
great view of the tangerine sky at twilight, visit Playa Las Hamcas. It
also offers a fabulous vista of the silhouette of fisherman and their
boats in the backdrop of the orange sky. Great subject for breathtaking
natural photography. For a dash of thoroughbred Mexican culture and
cuisine, Hornitos Beach rules. Visitors can feast on delectable local
appetizers being served by vendors here. Other good beach options
include Hornos beach, Playa Condesa, Playa Tres Vedas and Larga. La
Bonfil is rumored to have the perfect waves for surfing.

Acapulco is a treasure trove of beaches that slowly but surely unveils its gems of awe-inspiring natural beauty.
