---
title: "When to Go in Acapulco"
type: section
---

While the temperatures in Acapulco hover around 80 degrees all year long, the tourist crowd is not steady throughout the entire year. To avoid the rainy season and the winter months in America, tourists typically flock to this paradise getaway from December to April.

**Travel Seasons in Acapulco**

Though the crowds can be a bit overwhelming, some of the best weather in the city can be found during the stretch from December to April. Late February to early March are the peak of the season, as college students on Spring Break flock to the city known for its renowned nightclubs.

Though Acapulco has nearly constant sunshine, during the rainy season (June to October) there is an exception when the afternoons and evenings are marked by brief showers. If you don’t mind getting wet, there are plenty of deals throughout the rainy season. For budget travelers, this is a great time to visit, except for late July and early August when many nationals go on vacation.

For the best hotel deals and lack of crowds, the months around the rainy season – May and November – offer the best value. Crowds and storms are few during these months, and there are great opportunities for savings on lodging at this time.

**Seasonal Travel Prices in Acapulco**

If you are traveling to Acapulco during the peak season, make hotel reservations well in advance. You can expect to pay significantly more (20% to 50%) for lodging, as well as food, entertainment and transportation by taxi. The higher prices will also revert during the national travel season in July and August.

**Weather in Acapulco**

Though temperatures hover in the mid-80s to lower-90s all year, note that during the summer months of June through September, the heat and humidity can be particularly stifling. If you will be visiting in this period, be sure to look for a room with air conditioning.

**Holidays in Acapulco**

Around Easter, Christmas and New Years, prices will once again rise, as there are many popular celebrations that draw tourists. Expect beaches, streets and restaurants to be packed to capacity throughout your stay.
