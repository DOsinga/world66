Bahia de Kino is where the Sonoran Desert meets the Sea of Cortez -- a quiet beach town with none of the disco-and-resort glitz of Cabo or Cancun. The sunsets alone are worth the trip.

The town splits into two halves. Kino Viejo is the old Mexican fishing village, authentic and unhurried. Kino Nuevo is the gringo side, with beachfront houses and a long sandy strip facing Isla Tiburon. Both are relaxed, friendly places where the pace of life slows to a crawl.

Accommodations range from moderate to expensive. The Coastal Bus runs frequently from [Hermosillo](/northamerica/mexico/hermosillo), about two hours inland on a two-lane blacktop. For supplies, head to Miguel Aleman (locals call it Calle Doce), the nearest shopping town where you can find most everything you need.
