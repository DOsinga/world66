**Mexico Budget Travel **is
always possible and it does not involve letting go of comfortable
traveling. Here are several ideas that will allow you to stretch your
money all around Mexico.

**Save Money with Hostelling International**

The local branch of Hostelling International is called Hostelling International Mexico and its main office is located in the country's capital, Mexico Distrito Federal (better known as Mexico D.F. or just D.F.) at República de Guatemala #4 in the Colonia Centro. Having a Hostelling
International membership card in Mexico will allow you not only to save
money in hostels, but also in shops, bars, restaurants and nightclubs
that participate in this network. Hostelling International has 14 participating hostels in Mexico in the cities of Cancun, D.F. (3 here), Cuernavaca, Guadalajara (3 here), Merida, Morelia, Puebla, Puerto Vallarta, San Cristobal de las Casa and Zacatecas.

You can make reservations at the participating hostels via the Hostelling
International website or by calling directly to the hostels. Before
making reservations, make sure whether the hostel is co-ed or not
because some are and some are not. Breakfast is included with all rates.

Finally, after checking in at your hostel, ask the reception desk for the latest edition of the Hostelling International local booklet, which includes all of the participating businesses in this network.

**Getting Around with Public Transportation**

While
you can save some cash with public transportation in
Mexico, you must be aware of what it involves. Like other Latin
American countries, you need to stay away from certain areas and to be
aware of your surroundings at all times. Street smarts are not the norm
in all European and North American countries, but they are highly
necessary in Mexico. While riding the bus or rail in Mexico, do not
carry large amounts of cash in your purse or wallet. Do not bring
attention to you from potential pickpockets by wearing flashy or
expensive jewelry. Finally, remember that locals refer to bus routes
using landmarks or main avenues rather than bus numbers, so it is a
good idea to familiarize yourself with them.

**Driving on the Free Highway System**In
Mexico there are free and paid roadway systems between cities. You can
save a significant amount of money by using the free highway systems rather than the
paid ones. However, you must be aware that by using the free highways,
you will usually spend double the time arriving to your destination
because of lower speed limits. Also, it is not recommended to use the
free highways late at night because they are not patrolled by highway
patrols as much as the paid highways are.
