---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Cabo San Lucas is comprised of roughly ten miles of white sand
beaches. Located on a southern point, where the Sea of Cortez merges
with the Pacific Ocean, Cabo San Lucas is renowned for the variety
activities offered to travelers. Not to mention, the Los Cabos
International Airport is nestled in close vicinity to numerous
beachside destinations, facilitating travel time for visitors.

Beginning north in Bahia Chileno-known for thriving tropical
waters-it is highly recommended for travelers to book a snorkel day
trip. Chileno beaches offer calm waters, great for families and
swimmers, and a breathtaking reef. It is common to find a variety of
aquatic life including: turtles, fish, sponges, starfish, and the
occasional reef shark. Following the coastline south will reveal Bahia
Santa Maria and Playa Barco Varedo-equally adorned with soft white sand
and plenty of sunshine. Continuing south, the coastline quickly plunges
inward-uncovering Bahia Cabo San Lucas.

The shores of Medano beach cover a majority of the Cabo San Lucas
Bay. A highly concentrated area, Medano is definitely not for travelers
seeking privacy and quiet surroundings. There are several restaurants
and beachside bars and numerous resorts to choose from. Families often
prefer this area-given the large amount of activities offered. Snorkel
tours leave from the nearby El Arco and beachside vendors are easily
bargained with. There is no surf in Medano, which guarantees calm water
for swimmers and children.

As the bay plunges back into the open water, the Sea of Cortez
merges with the Pacific Ocean. Here, one will find Lover’s Beach-where
the waters are not ideal for swimmers due to strong currents and waves.
The beaches remain ideal for sunbathers and walkers, but it is
recommended that casual swimming activity remain on the bay side.
However, expert guided tours are popular-giving tourists a glimpse of
rock and cave formations.

Solmar Beach is located next to Lover’s Beach and offers annual
whale watching opportunities. Whales migrate and mate in the area in
the months of January through March. Solmar is certainly more relaxed
in comparison to Medano and considered perfect for honeymooners and
peace seekers. Playa Grande Resort and Grand Spa offers room packages
throughout the year.

Although short in distance, the shores of Cabo San Lucas offer
numerous activities and ideal conditions for all travelers. Just
remember to pack sunglasses, sun block, and a bathing suit; let Cabo
San Lucas take care of the rest.
