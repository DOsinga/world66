title: "Sights in Cabo san lucas"
type: section
---

Located on the Baja Peninsula off the coast of Mexico, **Cabo San Lucas sights** are many besides the vast ocean. It is sandwiched between Jos&eacute; del Cabo and San Luca. It has beautiful beaches and marinas and endless water sports. It has become one of the most-liked destinations for anglers and golfers. But besides the water and the scenery, Cabo San Lucas has many sights for the tourists. Among the many Cabo San Lucas sights are landmarks like the Iglesia de San Lus, El Faro Viejo, Casa de la Cultura, Land&rsquo;s End and the iconic El Arco where the Sea of Cortez meets with the Pacific Ocean.

#### Land&rsquo;s End

This is a natural landmark that lies close to Medano Beach and Playa del Amante (Lover&rsquo;s Beach). It has interesting rock structures and formations that visitors can enjoy, such as the El Arco which is an arch formed by wind and sea erosion. There is a colony of sea lions that can be found near there. Land&rsquo;s End offers exceptional views and great photo opportunities. There is no admission fee and you can go there virtually anytime of the day.

#### Casa de la Cultura

Casa de la Cultura is a famous landmark that dates back some 150 years and was used as the residence for Americans during the 19th-century war between America and Mexico. It is used for entertainment, events and exhibitions. Casa de la Cultura has a beautiful garden.

#### El Faro Viejo

The lighthouse was constructed more than 100 years ago. The only part that remains of this landmark is the base that offers a glimpse of the magnificence of what once stood there. These days the area is known as the place where sea turtles come to nest during breeding season.

#### The Church of San Lucas

The Iglesia de San Lucas is a church that is found in downtown Cabo San Lucas and is opened to tourists daily except on Saturdays and Sundays when locals hold their mass. The church was built some 300 years ago and has been maintained so well that it still has many of its original features. There is no admission fee and visitors can visit daily from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.

Besides the natural historical sights that are free-standing, one can always visit the various museums that show contemporary and traditional Mexican art. The Museo de las California hosts exhibitions of documents and photos that relate to the history of the area.
