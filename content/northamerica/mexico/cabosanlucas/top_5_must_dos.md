---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Cabo san lucas"
type: section
---

There are endless **Cabo San Lucas must do&rsquo;s** which the city offers all year round. The area hosts various events, festivals and tournaments for fishing and golf. The area has full calendars of events and it seems as if there&rsquo;s always something happening in Cabo San Lucas besides the usual tourist beach activities. But if you had to single out the top five must-do&rsquo;s in Cabo San Lucas, you would have to include the following:

#### 1. Fishing

The Bacs Billfish Invitational Tournament is for the serious anglers who come to Cabo San Lucas for big game fishing. This is held throughout June around Medano Beach. Then, there&rsquo;s the Fish and Stripes Fishing Tournament, a three-day event that includes other festivities.

The Surf and Panag Roosterfish Klassic Tournament, where anglers&rsquo; try to land the biggest roosterfish, coincides with the Festival of St. James in late July which features costumed white-horse riders re-enacting the various scenes in the Roman Catholic celebrated story of the Apostle. In the same month, the Fiesta of the Virgin of Carmen is celebrated and, in between the fishing competitions, one can also see a real live bull fight at Toros Stadium (sans blood and gore, of course). Besides fishing for prizes, one can always just fish for the fun of it.

#### 2. Scuba Diving

Scuba diving happens all year round but Cabo San Lucas has found a way to make it extra special during the Los Cabos Summer Dive Fiesta that is held throughout August. Various activities that appeal to divers are held with opportunities for spectacular nighttime scuba diving and beach parties that are accompanied by endless food and carnival parades.

#### 3. Rescue Turtles

In September people head to the northern outskirts of the city to help the turtles that come to nest on the sandy beaches. September also marks Independence Day celebrations with carnivals, markets, bullfights and rodeos

#### 4. Go Whale Watching

A number of whales migrate past the bay during the winter months. This is a great chance to spot a variety of whales from humpbacks to sperm whales. There are whale watching cruises that make it possible for visitors to be up close and personal with these big sea mammals.

#### 5. Go Jazzing

Most people don&rsquo;t associate Mexico with jazz but Cabo hosts one of the biggest jazz festivals in December. The three-day event culminates with some spectacular fireworks around sunset on the beach. This happens just before the Christmas festival, almost marking the end of yet another fun-filled year. But the fun never sets on Cabo, where every day is a fiesta day.
