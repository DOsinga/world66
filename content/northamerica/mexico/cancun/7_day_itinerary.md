title: "7 Day Itinerary in Cancun"
type: section
---

Spending a week in Cancun? Then you're going to need a 7-day **Cancun Itinerary**.

**Day 1: Beach Day
**

Spend the first day of your week soaking up the sun and enjoying the
beautiful beaches that Cancun is famous for. The beaches in Cancun have
recently been remade to their original splendor by dredging ocean bed
sand and tossing it onto the beach. After being hit by a massive
hurricane the beaches in Cancun were all but wiped out, but thanks to
modern technology they have been expanded by 50 meters. So, enjoy the
beautiful white sand for a day, after all that's why you are here!

**Day 2: Chichen Itza
**

While in Mexico, visit one of the 7 wonders of the world, the
ruins of Chichen Itza. These ruins were one of the major sites in the
Mayan Empire and remains to this day one of the most extensive
collections of Mayan ruins. There are guides available to explain the
significance of the ruins to the ancient Mayan Empire. While there, you
will have the opportunity to purchase souvenirs at the market. Also,
experience the dancers of Papantla, a group of performers who leap off
a giant pole. Allow all day for the trip, and wear plenty of sunscreen
as you will be outside all day long.

**Day 3: Xcaret
**

On Day 3 you will visit the largest theme park in all of Latin
America. The park boasts of Mexican culture, beautiful lagoons, a
nightly show, and lots of animals. This excursion will keep you
occupied day until night as it is an hour away from Cancun and the
nightly show doesn't finish until 9 p.m.

**Day 4: Playa Del Carmen
**

Playa Del Carmen is a great city only an hour's drive from Cancun.
Boasting a totally different experience than Cancun, Playa Del Carmen
is an multicultural city, and one of the fastest growing cities in the
world. Its beautiful beaches will provide you with plenty of photo
opportunities. Spend the day walking down 5th Avenue and looking
through the many shops that line the streets. Enjoy lunch in one of the
many great restaurants on 5th Avenue or directly on the beach.

**Day 5: Snorkeling in Akumal and Cenotes
**

Travel to the town of Akumal where they have a public turtle
reserve. Rent some snorkel equipment and see the turtles in the natural
habitat. Visit the information center to find out all the conservation
projects that they have created. After that, go across the highway and
visit one of the many cenotes. Cenotes are caves that are filled with freshwater and are
great for swimming and snorkeling and are part of an underground
connected system of caves that spans 140 km, making it the largest in
the world.

**Day 6: Merida
**

On Day 6 you will head to Merida. Only a short drive from Cancun, this colonial city offers its visitors a glimpse into real
Mexico, and its culture, music, dancing and food.

**Day 7: Swimming with Dolphins
**

Day 7 will offer you the opportunity of a lifetime--a chance to swim with dolphins. This excursion will teach you about dolphins and gives
you the opportunity to get in the water and swim with them.
