---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

The Yucatán Peninsula packs extraordinary day-trip options around Cancun. Tulum (130 km south) has a Mayan ruin on a cliff above a turquoise bay — arrive before 9am to beat the crowds. Chichen Itza (200 km west) is one of the most impressive archaeological sites in the Americas; the pyramid of Kukulcán is overwhelming in scale. Isla Mujeres is 20 minutes by ferry from downtown and offers a calm alternative to the Hotel Zone, with snorkeling tours and a relaxed pace. Cobá, farther inland, has a pyramid you can still climb (rare among Mayan sites) surrounded by jungle. Xcaret eco-park, 60 km south, is a well-organized half-day for families.
