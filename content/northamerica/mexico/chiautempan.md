Chiautempan is a small city in the central Mexican state of Tlaxcala, effectively a suburb of the state capital just to the west. The town is best known for textiles: this is **sarape country**, and the workshops along the streets near the centre still weave the heavy striped wool blankets that are sold at market stalls all over Mexico. The Saturday and Sunday tianguis at the centre is a good place to see them, alongside the everyday produce and clothing of a Mexican market town. The seventeenth-century parish church of Santa Ana — the town's full name is Santa Ana Chiautempan — anchors the main square. There is not much else to detain a traveller, but for anyone interested in Mexican craft and weaving Chiautempan is an easy half-day stop from [Tlaxcala](/northamerica/mexico/tlaxcala) or [Puebla](/northamerica/mexico/puebla).
