---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

The Yucatan Peninsula rewards exploration beyond the main site. A handful of destinations make easy day trips from Chichen Itza.

**Balankanche Caves**

About 3km east of the ruins, Balankanche (Throne of the Jaguar Priest) is a labyrinth of underground caverns discovered in 1959. Clay vessels, incense burners, and other offerings remain exactly where they were left on the cave floor. A chamber beneath the altar holds a limestone formation shaped like the sacred ceiba tree; deeper in, another altar surrounds a pool dedicated to Tlaloc, the Toltec rain god. The site has electric lighting throughout. Entry fee approximately 22 pesos; open 9am to 7pm daily.

**Rio Lagartos**

About 50km north, Rio Lagartos is a small fishing town on a biosphere reserve estuary where large flocks of pink flamingos come to breed. Boat tours of the reserve depart from town and offer close-up views of the flamingos alongside herons, spoonbills, and other wading birds.

**Puerto Morelos**

Around 130km east on the Caribbean coast, Puerto Morelos is a low-key town near the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef. Dive operators based here run trips to the Palancar Reef; snorkelling is equally rewarding. The town has good seafood restaurants and a relaxed central square.

**Xcaret**

About 150km southeast near Playa del Carmen, Xcaret is a large eco-theme park built around a series of natural cenotes and underground rivers. It is commercial and busy but genuinely impressive in scale, with butterfly pavilions, sea turtle exhibits, a bat cave, and the option to snorkel or swim through the underground passages. Open roughly 11am to 7:30pm; check current admission prices before going.
