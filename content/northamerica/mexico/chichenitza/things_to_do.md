---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Beyond the main ruins there is a surprising amount to occupy a full day or two around Chichen Itza — from sacred cave sites and cenotes to birdwatching at dawn and Maya ceremonial gatherings.

**Archaeological visits**

The main archaeological zone is open 8am to 5pm daily. Balankanche Cave, a few kilometres east, is a remarkable Mayan sacred site where clay offerings still sit on the floor of underground caverns exactly as they were left centuries ago; it is open daily (verify current hours before visiting). Old Chichen, the southern extension of the site with earlier structures, is not open to the general public.

**Museum**

CULTUR maintains a small museum inside the entrance building of the main site. The Dr. Merle Greene Gallery at Hacienda Chichen displays original Maya rubbings and artwork by the noted researcher; it is open to guests each afternoon from 6pm to 8pm, with special visiting hours available on request from the front desk.

**Cenotes**

A visit to a nearby cenote is worth carving out time for. Yokdzonot, about ten minutes' drive toward Merida on the free road, is run as a community project by a group of local Maya women. The small entrance fee supports maintenance of the site, which includes a palapa snack bar and bathrooms. Good for swimming and scuba diving in clear blue water.

**Birdwatching**

The surrounding jungle is excellent for birding, particularly November through May, with over 100 species recorded. Local guides trained by ornithologist Barbara McKinnon can be booked for sunrise or sunset walks.

**Maya rituals and ceremonies**

The Elder Maya Healers and Priests association (Kuch Kaab Y'eetel J-Men Maaya'ob, A.C.) holds a site within the Hacienda Chichen jungle reserve where ancient rituals and ceremonies are open to outside visitors. Contact Don Valerio Canche Yan to arrange participation.

**Stargazing**

The skies above Chichen Itza are remarkably dark and clear — few places combine such astronomical significance with such good viewing conditions.
