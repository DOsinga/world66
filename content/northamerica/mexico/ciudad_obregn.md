Ciudad Obregón (often just Obregón, also known as Cajeme) is a large agricultural city in the Río Yaqui Valley of southern [Sonora](/northamerica/mexico). It is not a tourist destination, but if you are passing through, the food alone is worth a stop.

The city sits on some of the most productive farmland in [Mexico](/northamerica/mexico), and the flat, orderly streets reflect its agricultural prosperity. The **Yaqui Museum** (Museo de los Yaquis) covers the culture of the indigenous Yaqui people who have lived in this valley for centuries. The murals by maestro Arteche at the **Municipal Palace** are worth seeing, and the **Nainari Lagoon** on the western edge of town is a pleasant escape. The beaches of the Sea of Cortez are a scenic drive to the west.
