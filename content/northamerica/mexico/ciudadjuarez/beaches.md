The large Mexican city of Juarez (in Spanish it is called Ciudad Juárez) is smack dab in the middle of the Chihuahuan Desert. That being said, you will not find any **Ciudad Juarez Beaches** in this area; but you will find sand dunes since it is a desert. This is a part of Mexico that stands on the Rio Grande and is directly across the border from El Paso Texas. And while all of Mexico is beautiful, it is definitely not the tropical beach locations you see in brochures. Juarez is however rich in its own culture and history, so there are various sights to see and things to do here.

**Ciudad Juarez Attractions**

- Visit the ruins of the Templo Mayor which the Aztecs believed to be the center of the universe- A spectacular building made of volcanic stone; the Museo Anahuacalli was designed by artist Diego Rivera and showcases his pre-Columbian art collections- Said to be one of the world's best natural history museum, the National Museum of Anthropology is home to 23 exhibition halls and four square kilometers of exhibits- The historic National Palace used to be home to Spanish explorer Hernán Cortés, conqueror of the Aztecs, and houses a panoramic mural of Mexican history- The 1,600-acre Chapultepec Park offers enough rural and cowboy like activities to fill much of your days time and is truly popular among families with children
**Closest beaches to Ciudad Juarez Mexico**

You will have to travel a good distance to reach them, but if you have the time and opportunity, either of these beaches in Mexico would be worth the trip.

**Puerto Peñasco Beaches**

Each year approximately 100,000 people come to the beaches at Puerto Peñasco to celebrate "Spring Break Rocky Point." You'll find a laid back, almost untouched atmosphere here and probably quite a few people partying on the almost 70 miles of beach front. The beaches are kept immaculate and the ocean waters are crystal clear, perfect for swimming, surfing, snorkeling or sailing.

**Mazatlan Beaches**

Mazatlan is another popular destination because of the warm pristine waters with average temperatures of 65 to 75 degrees Fahrenheit. The variety of low-cost accommodations is an added perk here as well. The sunny beaches are always bustling with sunbathers, swimmers, surfers, volleyball and Frisbee players. It's known as a port town and a transport hub as it has ferries to and from Baja California. Stroll the beachfront and find vendors of all kinds too, most with their hand-made arts and crafts.
