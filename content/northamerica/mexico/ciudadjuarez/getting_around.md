---
title: "Getting Around in Ciudad juarez"
type: section
---

When planning your tip to **Ciudad Juarez**, **transportation **arrangements
require special attention. While it is important that you are aware of
the security issues of this city in Northern Mexico, you should not be
discouraged to drive around the city or to use the city bus system. Here
are some tips and practical information on how to get around Ciudad
Juarez.

**Driving
**

If you're planning to drive in Ciudad Juarez, there is good news and
bad news. The good news is that traffic in this city is much less
chaotic than in other Mexican cities such as Monterrey or Mexico D.F.
The bad news is that fender benders and small accidents are very common,
particularly in parking lots. It is very important that you have a
valid Mexican car insurance while driving in Mexico. Even if you're
bringing your car from the United States or Canada and you have valid
American or Canadian car insurance, you still need to apply for Mexican
car insurance when entering Mexico. There are several car insurance
agencies on both sides of the U.S. Mexico border.

**Parking**

If you're renting a car, make sure to carefully check all types of
dents, scratches, marks and bumps on your car before you sign the
contract. It is very easy to get these kinds of minor damages when
parking at public places. It is common to pay about 5 to 20 pesos to
have somebody watch over your car at certain places. It is an activity
that is part of the informal economy of Ciudad Juarezm, but it will
definitely save you future costs. Be careful where you park and pay
close attention to the signs, particularly if you have a foreign car.
Don't think that you will be able to get away without paying fines just
because you own a foregin car, because the police will take away your
plates so that you will be forced to pay the fines before leaving
Mexico.

**Bus
**

In general, buses in Mexico are not identified by number but rather
by destination or landmarks throughout the route. If you're planning to
use the bus during your stay in Ciudad Juarez, then it is a good idea
that you buy a city guide at your local bookstore or that you do some
online searches on landmarks and major avenues of Ciudad Juarez (to
familiarize yourself with the city). Buses don't have a specific
schedule and run in close succession to one another, so if you miss one,
there is a good chance that another one is on its way within 10 to 15
minutes. Make sure not to draw attention to yourself by not wearing
flashy, expensive jewelry, carrying large amounts of money or carrying
expensive camera or video equipment. Finally, you don't need to have
exact bus fare, because the driver will provide you change.
