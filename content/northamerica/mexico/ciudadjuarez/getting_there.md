title: "Getting There in Ciudad juarez"
type: section
---

When planning your trip to **Ciudad Juarez**, **Getting There**
is one of the main things to figure out because it will most of the
time be one of the main components of your travel budget. Travel to
Ciudad Juarez is possible through either plane or car.

**Getting
There by Plane**

There are 2 alternatives to travel by plane
Ciudad Juarez. Either you can first arrive to a city in the United
States that is nearby the U.S and Mexico border, such as El Paso, Texas,
or you can arrive directly to the Abrahm Gonzalez International Airport
(IATA airport code: CJS), which serves Ciudad Jurez.

The Abraham
Gonzalez International Airport, better known as Ciudad Juarez Airport,
houses 4 Mexican airlines: Aeromexico, Aeromexico Connect, Interjet and
Viva Aerobus. If you're traveling from within Mexico, it is a good idea
to first look for prices through Interjet and Viva Aerobus because they
offer deeper discounts to compete with the more established Aeromexico
and Aeromexico Connect.

If you first arrive to the United States
and don't require a visa there, you might still require a visa to visit
Mexico. Make sure to check with your local office of the Mexican
Consulate or Embassy to check the visa requirements of your nationality.
Nationalities not requiring a visa and nationalities that require a
tourist visa can stay in Mexico for up to 90 days. If you plan to stay
longer than 90 days, you will need to apply for an extension permit at
the local immigration office in the city that you're staying. It is very
important that you keep the entrance form (similar to the I-94 form
when you enter the U.S.) because you will need this entrance form if you
plan to extend your visit in Mexico and again when you're leaving the
country.

**Getting There by Car**

If you plan to cross the U.S -
Mexico border, you can choose between 4 international ports of entry:
the Bridge of the Americas, the Ysleta International Bridge, the Paso
del Norte Bridge and the Stanton Street Bridge. Make sure that you have
enough time to travel by car from the United States to Mexico because
immigration waiting times can vary from 10 minutes to 6 hours, depending
on various factors. Traffic during Mexican or American long weekends is
particularly busy. Not all American insurance contracts are valid in
Mexico so it is best to purchase a local insurance policy nearby the
border. Prices range between $12 to $200 depending on the length of your
stay in Mexico.Once you're in Mexico, follow the signs towards
your hotel or hostel in Ciudad Juarez.
