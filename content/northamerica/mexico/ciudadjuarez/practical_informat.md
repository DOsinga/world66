When planning your trip, **Ciudad Juarez Practical
Information** always come in handy to avoid cultural
misunderstandings, to be aware of dangers and to know how to travel to
other cities in Mexico.

**Avoid Cultural Misunderstandings**

The
need for personal space of Mexicans is smaller than that of North
Americans or Europeans, so don't be surprised if during rush hour the
buses are packed and you find yourself really up close and personal with
somebody in front of you. Don't think much of it. Instead understand
that a person that has a 1 hour bus commute will rather just jump on a
bus and deal with being really close to somebody than wait 30 minutes
for the next bus to arrive. That being said, don't hesitate to be
assertive and speak up, if you're a lady being confronted by a man that
is just being plain rude.

As in other Latin American cultures, Mexicans are very friendly
and have a great sense of humor. Laughter is a way to cope with
everyday problems and Mexicans are strong believers of this. If you just
met somebody and they're already doing jokes, don't take it as somebody
being rude or not serious, but rather as somebody trying to make a
connection with you.

**Be Aware of Dangers**

The criminal activity in the
domestic metropolitan area of Ciudad Juárez has
increased dramatically over the last decades. This is a serious matter.
While Ciudad
Juarez is a city that offers rich cultural experiences and an active
night life, it is important to be aware of the dangers of the city. As a
foreign visitor, don't attract attention to yourself by wearing flashy
jewelry, carrying large amounts of money, taking big, full bags around
streets, or displaying expensive photographic or video equipment in
street markets. The key is to blend in and avoid confrontations.

When
traveling at night, don't travel by yourself and, whenever possible,
travel with somebody that speaks Spanish very well. At night, it is best
to travel by taxi rather than by bus. Taxi service past midnight
usually will require to negotiate a rate, rather than using a taxi
meter. Don't be alarmed and be prepared to negotiate a fare by having
previously asked your hotel desk or locals for normal fares between the 2
places that you're planning to travel.

**Know how to Travel
to Other Cities in Mexico**

Travel
by plane is most of the time the best option to travel around Mexico.
Not only will it free up time that you can use in better things, but
also it will allow you to save money. Discount airlines such as Interjet and Viva Aerobus offer deep discounts to
passengers that buy tickets at least 2 weeks in advance online. Make sure to check current
deals on the websites of these smaller discount local airlines.

When
traveling by bus, it is a good idea to pay for the servicio ejecutivo
class. This class is true VIP travel by bus because it includes light
meals (sandwich, soda and chips), on board TV service and air
conditioning. Last but not least,
travel between cities by car is also possible. The most important
decision is whether to use the free highway (carretera libre) or the paid highway (peaje). Using the carretera libre will save you lots of money,
but using the paid highway will save you time as you'll be able to
travel at a higher speed limit.
