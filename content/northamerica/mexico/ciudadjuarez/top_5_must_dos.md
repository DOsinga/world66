---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Ciudad juarez"
type: section
---

The city of Ciudad Juarez is right on the Rio Grande River across
from El Paso, Texas and there are many **Ciudad Juarez Must Do's**
that you should not miss. There are several different ports of entry to
the United States and Mexico in the city and this means many people
travel to the city. It is considered a city of the future and you should
stop in on your travels and see some of the top attractions.
Visit National Museum of Anthropology

One of the best collections of natural history in the world is found
at the National Museum of Anthropology. The museum has 23 different
exhibition halls. You can view items that are of importance both
ethnographically and archeologically to Mexico that date back to the
18th century. All of the exhibit halls offer maps, exhibits,
illustrations and audiovisual presentations. You can also attend
conferences, shop in the bookstore and see movies about the exhibits.
Boat in Xochimilco

This was the site of a pre-Columbian city and is known around the
world for its floating gardens. This is a popular tourist attraction as
you can reach it by boat. You will find Spanish boats taking passengers
down the many canals. You can get souvenirs, drinks and food from the
vendors in the small canoes. The gardens are split into two different
areas, an ecological park and the traditional area. Both are worth a
visit and are perfect for bird-watching.
Explore the National Palace

The palace contains a mural done by the famous Mexican painter Diego
Rivera and was the home of the famous explorer Cortes. This palace has
been home to the ruling families since the fall of the Aztec empire.
Today the palace contains the National Archives, offices of the
President and the federal treasury.
Visit Templo Mayor

You can still view the ruins of what is thought to be the Templo
Mayor. This was originally a double pyramid that was used by the Aztecs.
It was not only an important visible landmark but also considered to be
the center of the cosmic universe and required regular human sacrifice.
You can still explore the ruins of this important landmark.
Enjoy in Zocalo

The Zocalo is an important historic section of the city. This is one
of the largest public spaces as well as one of the most important. This
was once a ceremonial center for the Aztecs but since then many changes
have taken place. Today the park contains fountains, monuments, a market
and several green areas. The Zocalo frequently has exhibits and is a
major spot for protesting, ceremonies and celebrations.
