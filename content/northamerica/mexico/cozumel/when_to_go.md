---
title: "When to Go in Cozumel"
type: section
---

Cozumel has two distinct seasons, wet and dry. These can definitely play a part in your decision of when to go to Cozumel, as availability and prices differ as much as the weather. If you're going for the culture, festivals can also be a factor in your choice of what time of year you want to visit. Here is a rundown of what you can expect to help you choose the best time of year to visit Cozumel.

**Wet Season**

May through October is known as the wet season in Cozumel. Through September this simply means that most days you can expect a brief afternoon shower that may last through the early evening. These showers tend to be a welcome relief from the heat of summer, and go far in cooling off the nights. Be sure to bring a light jacket or wrap for any cool evenings. Once September and October hit, so does hurricane season. During these months the temperatures are more changeable, as is the weather pattern. Expect the occasional tropical storm, lots of wind, and plenty of rain. Temperatures usually fluctuate between 11 and 30 degrees C throughout the season. Waters are warmer, which is good for spotting a large variety of aquatic life. This is also considered to be the low season, so you'll find that prices are generally lower and it's easier to find a room where you want.

**Dry Season**

From November through April the weather brightens and the days are warm and sunny. It's rare that rain will fall, though humidity can rise from time to time. The sea breezes keep things pleasant, however, so once again be sure to pack your wrap or jacket. The water is generally at it's warmest toward the end of spring, so this is the best time for snorkeling and diving. Temperatures usually range between 7 and 26 degrees C. This time period encompasses the entire high season. The weather is more constant, and the intense heat stays at bay. Because of this, expect to pay more for your hotel rooms, though flights will be considerably less cost. Book well in advance, though, as accommodation tends to fill up months ahead.

**Festivals**

There are some excellent festivals throughout the entire year, but here are a few of the best. The most famous is Carnival, Cozumel's version of Mardi Gras that takes place in the week before Ash Wednesday, usually in February. Carnival involves lots of parades, costumes, dancing, music, food and competitions. For local color, this is the time to visit. Anglers will appreciate the International Billfish Tournament in March, where fishermen hit the waters in search of the biggest tuna or marlin. El Cedral takes place in April or May and involves traditional dress and bullfights. And of course, Easter is a massive celebration that lasts through April and involves several smaller festivals and an abundance of flowers. In the summertime you'll find outdoor festivals that involve kite flying and other recreational activities, and in the autumn there are several festivals commemorating various saints. Toward the end of the year you can celebrate the Day of the Dead and other religious holidays.
