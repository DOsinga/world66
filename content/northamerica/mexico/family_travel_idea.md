**Mexico Family Travel** is a great
way to escape the cold winters from North America and Europe. Central
and Southern Mexico are blessed with really mild winters. Some areas
along the Gulf of Mexico on the west and the Pacific Ocean on the east
have sandy beaches and offer inexpensive beach vacation alternatives to
their more expensive counterparts in the Caribbean and the
Mediterranean. Below are some family-friendly tips for getting there, traveling on a budget, and making sure everyone has a good time.

**Getting There
**

Air travel to Mexico is possible from several countries around the
world. The best starting point is the Mexico Distrito Federal
International Airport (also known as the D.F. Airport or the Benito
Juarez Airport, with IATA airport code: MEX) because it's the
destination that the most local and international airlines offer flights to. Arriving to Mexico's capital is a very hectic experience,
so make sure to keep your young children with you at all times. If
you're traveling with a large party, it is a good idea to hire one of
the local airport employees to help you with your bags so that you can
keep track of everybody.

**Getting Around without Breaking the Bank**

Once in Mexico, use air travel to get around the cities. Not only
will this free up time to use it to enjoy your vacation, but also will
allow you to some cash. Besides the two main local airlines, AeroMexico
and Mexicana, there are several smaller and newer local airlines such as
VivaAerobus that offer deep discounts to compete withe the more
established airlines. To guarantee the lowest fare possible, it is a
good idea to book your tickets at least two weeks in advance. Online
booking is available with all discount airlines, and you can check in
and print out your boarding pass 24 hours prior the departure of your
flight.

**What Hotels to Choose**

When traveling with a large group or family, it is best to avoid
hostels and go with hotels that are at least rated 3 stars. Most 3-star
hotels will include perks such as free breakfast, free WiFi or
free shuttle to and from airport. Make sure you find out which ones are
available. Do your hotel research in advance rather than to wait until
your arrival to Mexico. Some taxi drivers might be very aggressive in
trying to get you to go to a specific hotel, but don't be afraid to be
assertive and say no to them.

If using a Mexican airline, ask if they have a list of recommended hotels or hotels that
participate in their rewards program. This is a good starting point when looking for hotels in any Mexican city.

Now that you know how to get you and your family there...

**Visiting Northern Mexico**

Known by locals as the Lady Sultan of the North, Monterrey is a very
modern face of Mexico. The Monterrey metropolitan area has over 4
million inhabitants, and includes neighborhoods with a more
avant garde architecture such as San Pedro, as well as areas with a
more traditional architecture (e.g. Colonial era). In Monterrey there
is something for everybody. Moms will surely appreciate the large shopping
centers such as Valle Oriente (in the neighborhood of the same name)
and Galerias San Agustin (near the downtown area). Dads will have
access to driving ranges overlooking the river to practice their golf
skills. Finally, kids will enjoy the water park inside the Sesame Street
park and the gigantic La Fundidora Park, which has several activities
for the whole family.

**Guadalajara City: Home to Everything Mexican
**

Charros (as Mexicans refer to the citizens of
Guadalajara) claim that everything authentically Mexican originates from
this city. People from Guadalajara go as far as to claim that mole
poblano (obviously from Puebla, given its name) was first invented in
Guadalajara. With a claim like that, you know that the food in
Guadalajara must be really good. Like in most other Latin American
countries, eating out in this city is inexpensive, so it's easy to feed the whole family on a budget.

However, you
do need to know where to eat, especially when traveling with young
children. It is a great investment to purchase some local city guides
from Guadalajara before your arrival to Mexico to learn about suggested
restaurants and eateries from fellow travelers. Typical dishes from
Guadalajara include such delicacies as "sanduches mojados" (literraly,
soaked sandwiches), which are pulled pork sandwiches in a sea of spicy
sauce, and or course, "mole poblano," which is a thick, stew-like sauce served with chicken, pork or beef with a side of rice. Do your research ahead of time to find restaurants suitable for any picky eaters you may have along.
