---
title: "Festivals in Guadalajara"
type: section
---

**Guadalajara festivals** are usually bright and exuberant, with plenty of food and fun. If you'd like your trip to Guadalajara to involve a bit of local culture, why not plan your stay over one of the following events? They're arranged by month for your convenience.

**January**

Three Kings Day - January 6 - is the day to celebrate the visit of the 3 kings to the manger where Jesus was born. In homage to the kings, people exchange gifts. You'll find the mood in Guadalajara to be joyous and festive as everyone celebrates this last day in the Christmas season.

**February**

Founding Day - Guadalajara festivals don't get much better than on February 14, when locals celebrate the anniversary of the city's founding in the 17th century. For 2 weeks you'll get to experience parties, marathons, live music and more. Just stroll on down to the central plazas or the university, where most of the events occur.

**March/April**

Feria de Tonala - This handicrafts festival lasts for several weeks, starting at the end of March and extending into April. You can roam around the Tonala area to find pottery or ceramics to bring home as souvenirs.

Easter - For the week before and the week after Easter, you can view various traditions. Palm Sunday, the Sunday before Easter, sees people parading about with palm fronds that are later left on doors to ward off evil. The week is also filled with various passion play reenactments. It's an interesting time to be in Guadalajara.

**May**

Labor Day - May 1 - is Mexico's Labor Day, a day of relaxing and celebrating for all Mexicans.

Cinco de Mayo - Join in on the festivities on May 5 to celebrate the victory over France. This Guadalajara festival is fraught with merriment and revelry.

**June**

Fiestas de Tlaquepaque - This arts and crafts fair showcases local talents. Goods can be bought and taken home as souvenirs. You can also head to the museum to see some displays in honor of the festival.

**July**

Intermoda - Extremely popular is this fashion expo, the biggest in Mexico. In mid-July the city is inundated with models who strut their stuff in fashion shows. You can also find many of the designs at discounted prices.

**August**

International Mariachi Festival - Late August brings about the mariachi festival, where you can watch live concerts and join in the dancing. Most of the events happen in the center squares.

**September**

Independence Day - Mexico's independence day is celebrated on September 16 and involves parties, fireworks and parades. Guadalajara's celebration is a major attraction.

**October**

Octoberfest - The entire month is filled with music, dancing, food, carnivals, art displays, markets, street entertainment and more. This is an excellent time to visit Guadalajara.

Romeria de la Virgen de Zapopan - In the middle of the month locals celebrate their patron saint with massive parades and huge crowds.

**November**

Day of the Dead - November 2 is great for heading to the local cemeteries to observe graveside rituals (be polite!) or to the museum to get some background information on this festival.

International Book Fair - Take part in this 2-week expo that extends into December and includes exhibitions, concerts and even awards ceremonies. You may even get to meet your favorite author.

**December**

Las Posados - From December 16 to 24 there are nightly reenactments of Joseph and Mary's search to find shelter in Bethlehem. This leads up to the celebration of Christmas on December 25.

New Year's Eve - Head to the plazas for music, fireworks and general entertainment and then carry on at the local nightclubs for a great end of the old year and start of the new.
