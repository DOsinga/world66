---
title: "Getting Around in Guadalajara"
type: section
---

**Guadalajara Transportation**, both public and private, is well
developed because this city is among the most modern, biggest and most developed cities of Mexico. Home to everything that is
"authentically Mexican," jaliscos (people from Guadalajara) claim that even the mole poblano was first made in here. Here are some tips on getting around Guadalajara.

**Getting Around by Bus**

Getting
around bus in Guadalajara is cheap but it requires you to get
comfortable with a couple of the city's main landmarks. Buses are not
identified so much by numbers but rather by what major landmarks (e.g.
museum, neighborhood, market) are included in their routes.

Be
aware that buses are not the best way to travel with large, heavy
suitcases. Don't be surprised if a bus driver does not allow you into
the bus with big bags. It is not against the law because the buses have
no place to put them and during rush hours there would be no place at
all for your big bags.

Finally, you're not required to have
exact change when boarding the bus as drivers provide change. Bus
drivers do not accept very large bills (larger than a 50 Mexican peso
bill) or foreign currency.

**Getting Around by Taxi**

Traveling
by taxi around Guadalajara is quite convenient and inexpensive. Just
like when traveling by bus, you still need to be comfortable with
landmarks because most taxi drivers will ask for them. Plan your trip
in advance and ask locals for landmarks nearby your destination so it
is easier for the driver to get oriented. Once you're near your
destination he'll look for the specific address.
