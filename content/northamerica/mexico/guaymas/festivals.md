---
title: "Festivals in Guaymas"
type: section
---

Most **Guaymas festivals** celebrate religious holidays and occasions that are special to each city.

**Guaymas International Carnival**

The Guaymas International Carnival will offer the tourist Guaymas' most colorful festival, which is carried out around the Plaza of the 3 Presidents (Plaza de los 3 Presidentes) and goes on through Serdan avenue.

The parade of the comparsas (carnival groups), dressed in colorful ethnic and theme costumes that represent that group's name, starts the festival. The tourist will see the queen followed by many allegoric cars and their company smiling for the crowd.

Most of the visitors come from the state of Sonora and from all of the Mexican republic and the United States. The carnival is carried out from 11th to 15th of February.

**Day of Saint Joseph and Saint John Baptist**

Tourists can visit Saint Joseph village or the central Saint John Baptist church to spend a time of spiritual reflection at the religious gatherings. First, many commemorative ceremonies are carried out with special candles offered at the temple's altar. Afterwards, the popular festival begins, and the tourist can enjoy the Matachines and Pascolas dances along with other types of spectacles and watch spectacular fireworks. This celebration takes place on July 24.

**Festival de la Calaca**

This is one of the Guaymas festivals commemorating the Dia de los Muertos (day of the dead), carried out 1st of November each year at the Plaza de la Pistola and Triquis alleyway. There are presentations of figures of skeletons in real size, pictorial expositions, musical concerts, and book intros, among other activities. The attendants walk in a procession, dressed in skeleton costumes and carrying their skeleton figures from 14th to 22th Serdan street to finish with theatre presentations and music.
