---
title: "Sights in Guaymas"
type: section
---

**Guaymas Sights** are not just a list of “where,” but “when.” If
you really want to get the most of Guaymas, you need to plan your trip
for a particular time of the year. If you time it around Mardi Gras or
Easter, you are likely to see a carnival quite unlike anywhere else,
including New Orleans. But apart from that, Guaymas has some other
excellent things to see.
**Serdan Street

**

Serdan Street is the place to get to see historical Guaymas. There
are some impressive buildings and statues here reflecting the colonial
and post-independence history of Guaymas. You can see a lot of
impressive buildings here with a strong French architectural influence.
Some of the buildings to see are the Banco de Mexico, the Municipal
Palace, and the Church of San Fernando. You even need to see Municipal
Jail, the "Carcel Municipal," even though it is not used anymore. The
heavy domes in some of these buildings really stand out. The Carcel
Municipal looks like a castle right out of a fantasy. It has a sordid
history of its own that rivals the Bastille and Alcatraz.
**View of the Harbor

**

A description by John Wilhelm from fifty years ago speaks of a small
village surrounded by mountains with one of the most mirror-smooth bays.
Many who read this description expect to see paradise. Rampant
commercialism has dulled that somewhat, but if you can see the harbor
from Malecon at sunset, you may just be able get a glimpse of the
paradise John was talking about. No photographs can do it justice. While
you are at Malecon, you should definitely see the Statue of the
Fisherrman of the Guaymas. There is beautiful song which is engraved on
the statue called, “Barco de Guaymas” ( Boat of Guaymas) which
essentially says that after a fisherman dies, his transportation to
Heaven is in the boat of Guaymas. And after watching the sunset, it is
hard to argue with that.
**Mardi Gras

**

Mardi Gras is held in Guaymas around the same time that it is held in
the United States. There are fiestas and parades which are quite lively
and amazing. There are free cultural programs including dances,
mariachi, folklore, and lots of music. Some are in auditoriums, and some
are in outdoor plazas. Then there are also carnivals and fireworks.
These guys really know how to do this. The fireworks over the bay can be
watched in the very comfortable setting of your own folding chair.
