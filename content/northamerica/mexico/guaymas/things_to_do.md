---
title: "Things to Do in Guaymas"
type: section
---

**Guaymas things to do** list is a little compact list, but all of them are absolute must dos. This is a town that has a few very special features, which you absolutely want to completely experience:

#### Attend Mardi Gras Carnival

Whether you have been to New Orleans or not, this is a carnival you do not want to miss. You want to time your vacation to Guaymas about a week before Ash Wednesday. The carnival itself is one part of it. Guyamas comes alive culturally during these two weeks. You can see dancing, singing, folk music in the streets and the auditoriums. Then there are floats and fireworks during the carnival itself. Along with that you have the fiestas and a rich variety of eating opportunities.

#### Enjoy Beaches and the Harbor

For the beach lover, this is paradise. There is boating which is unlike any other. The clear tranquil water of the bay, along with the beautiful mountains combine for a relaxing experience like no other. The beaches are not only a great place to walk but, in the right season, a place for great sights and photographs. The only pearl farm in North and South America is also here for some great shopping opportunities. And, of course, you need to see the sunset from Malcone over the harbor. This in itself is an exhaustive list but there is a huge adventure still left. If you are a scuba diver with an underwater camera, you are in the most perfect place in the world. There is such a huge variety of fish here that you are not going to want to leave the water. And, if you are not a scuba diver, you might want to take this sport up. You do also need to see the Statue of the Fisherman when you are at the harbor.

#### Visit Statues and the Buildings

When you are not underwater or having a ball at the carnival, what do you do? There are still opportunities which should not be missed in Guyamas. You definitely need to go to Serdan Street and see historical Guaymas. Architecturally beautiful buildings like the Church of San Fernando, the Municipal Palace and Banco de Mexico are there to see. Even thought the old Municipal is no longer the jail, it is a great attraction. It is like a castle with its own dungeon. It was a jail until only a couple of years back and had quite a notorious reputation.
