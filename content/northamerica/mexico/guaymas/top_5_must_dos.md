---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Guaymas"
type: section
---

**Guaymas Must Do's** is a list no traveler to Guaymas should be
without. These are five things which you absolutely should not miss
doing in Guaymas. Many people who go to Guaymas perhaps do not realize
what they have there because it is fairly inexpensive and so close to
the U.S. border. Well, beauty and affordability are not mutually
exclusive, as Guaymas will show.
**1. Sunset on the Harbor at Malcone

**

You absolutely do not want to miss this one. This is a sunset like no
other. The beauty is so out of this world that no photograph can do it
justice. You just have to see it.
**2. Boating in the Bay

**

Absolutely clear water is surrounded by a necklace of mountains with
an awesome climate in this beautiful bay. Many people dock their boats
here, and you can’t blame them. If you have to have a boating vacation
hot spot, this is the place to have it.
**3. Scuba diving in the Sea of Cortez

**

If you know scuba diving, then welcome to paradise. Clear water,
awesome temperatures year-round and, according to Jacques Cousteau, the
richest sea in the world. There are over eight hundred species of sea
life here, from hammerhead sharks to sea lions. If you come during the
early part of the year, you can even witness the mating of whales. A
scuba diver with an underwater camera is in a state of bliss. Of course,
if you don’t know scuba diving, a trip to Guaymas is good a reason as
any to learn it.
**4. Pearl Market and its Architecture

**

Technically, these are separate things, but assuming that you want to
get roaming around the city and shopping done on the same day, then
this is the way to do it. Here you have the only pearl farm in North and
South America, so this is where you are going to get the best deals on
pearls. A trip to Serdan Street is definitely warranted to see both the
places of the Saint: the Church of San Fernando--and the place of the
sinners--and the old municipal jail, which looks like a castle.
**5. Mardi Gras

**

If you have gone to Guaymas and have come back without seeing the
Mardi Gras, it is kind of like going to Paris and not seeing the Eiffel
tower. Of course, you have to time your visit right. The fun starts a
week before Ash Wednesday and lasts about two weeks with parades,
floats, fireworks, folk music, dancing and lot of food. It is a cultural
celebration like no other.
