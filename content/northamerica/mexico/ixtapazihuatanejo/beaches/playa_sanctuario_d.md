No restaurants. No hotels. No beach vendors. No people!

That's what Playa Sanctuario De Las Tortugas offers. It is the perfect place to go if you want a day of total seclusion and relaxation. Nobody there but the pelicans.

First, to get there takes a little work. The beach is located about a mile and a half from the village of La Barra De Potosí. During part of the year it is separated from town by a lagoon that empties into the sea. When the lagoon is empting into the ocean a kayak can be rented.

Once across the lagoon it takes a half-hour walk through an abandoned coconut plantation to get to the beach. When the outlet dries up the trip can be made on foot ro by horseback.

On the trip out it's possible to see iguanas and lizards, a multitude of birds, and maybe even a coati mundi or two! Wildflowers abound and so do a variety of cacti.

The beach isn't a good swimming beach with powerful breakers right at the shore line. It's better for walking, with miles of sand. It's a birdwatcher's paradise, too. Pelicans, kites, and many other seabirds frequent the beach.

Seldom will anyone be seen. Once in awhile fishermen might be spotted off the west end of the beach casting their nets at the base of the cliffs.

Bring water and a good book.
