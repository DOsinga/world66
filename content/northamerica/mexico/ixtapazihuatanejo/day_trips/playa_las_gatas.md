---
title: "Playa Las Gatas"
type: poi
---

A trip to Las Gatas beach for a day of snorkeling is a "must do" when in Zihuatanejo.

The trip starts at the municipal pier where water taxis run back and
forth between the pier and Las Gatas from about 9 in the morning until 5
p.m. The cost is 30 pesos, or a litle under $3USD for a roundtrip
ticket. The trip out to Las Gatas gives some beautiful views of the
town and surrounding hills. Sometimes a cruise ship will be anchored in
the bay and the water taxis pass very close, giving an interesting
perspective of the ship.

Once at the beach, the next step is to choose a restaurant to
use for the day. A dozen small beach restaurants run the length of the
beach. All of them serve wonderful seafood and cold
drinks. They also rent snorkeling equipment, lifejackets, and floating
toys for the little ones.

Snorkeling is best earlier in the day. In the afternoon the
waves start to stir up the sand and makes the water a little less
clear. Marine life along the reef is plentiful. Sea urchins,
damselfish, angelfish, pipefish, eels, and even an ocassional sea
turtle can be seen swimming in the warm water.

Back at the restaurant a bucket of ice and cold beer will be
waiting by the lounge chairs under the big umbrella. For lunch most
restaurants have a variety of fish, lobster, shrimp, octopus, and other
assorted seafood prepared in traditional Mexican style.

The beach vendors will offer silver, peanuts, shell necklaces,
shirts, and even homebrewed Mezcal. The vendors are friendly, not
pushy, and few in number.

For those with tight muscles or in the mood for a little pampering massages are available.

Strolling musicians offer a tune for 10 pesos per song per
musician. A nice addition to a memorable "toes-in-the-sand" lunch
experience.

For an all day trip for two including water taxi, snorkeling
gear, lounge chairs, lunch, and beer will cost between $50-75USD. Money
well spent!
