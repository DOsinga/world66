---
title: "When to Go in Ixtapa-Zihuatanejo"
type: section
---

**Winter**

Ixtapa, Mexico is a beautiful area on the Pacific coast side, with
sun to last for days and that cool salt water breeze flowing in off the
water. To enjoy this paradise in all its glory, you should plan on
traveling in the winter months when the temperatures range between 70 degrees
and 80 degrees F. It is during these times that you will avoid the crowds at
the beach as well as on the streets, making your shopping experiences
easier and more enjoyable. The winter also brings warmer waters to the
beaches for you to enjoy without the hassle of large crowds. You can
also view the beautiful sunsets of westerly located Ixtapa during these
months, viewing the pinks, oranges and golds that fill the evening sky.

Of
course, during these times of the year, the mosquitoes can be obnoxious
and the heat rises while humidity soaks the air, making the temperature
feel a lot higher than it actually is.
Summer

June may be the hottest month in Ixtapa and often marks the
beginning of the rainy season, which runs through October. During this
time, you may feel stifled by the heat as well as trapped by the rain. The waters tend to be turbulent in the summer months and into early
fall, offering great waves for surfers to enjoy. With all the rain
that falls throughout the summer months, the countryside turns a bright
green, transforming the areas of the Sierra Madre Occidental and the
Sierra Madre del Sur into plush fields and hills.

Although summer is
the warmest time to visit Ixtapa, it may be the most beautiful time of
the year. You will be able to catch the green countryside, the blue
oceans, and the lush color of the mountains. It is an area surrounded
by landscape and culture and you will be able to soak it all in during
the summer. Just wear light clothing and a hat to block the sun. Also, if you will be walking around and visiting shops, beaches and
restaurants, remember to bring a water bottle to hydrate yourself with.
Fall

You should be aware that the hurricane season in Ixtapa runs from
late September to early November. Use caution when planning a trip
here during that time, including making preparations for immediately
departure if danger should arise. Beginning in November and flowing
into late spring, the fishermen of Ixtapa thrive, catching sailfish,
yellowtail tuna and mahi-mahi in large quantities.
