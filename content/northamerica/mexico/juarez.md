Ciudad Juárez sits directly across the Rio Grande from El Paso, Texas, making it one of the busiest border crossings in the Americas. The city has a complicated reputation — the drug violence of the late 2000s and early 2010s made it one of the most dangerous cities in the world, and while security has improved significantly since then, it is still not a place for aimless wandering.

That said, Juárez has a real city underneath the headlines. The old cathedral, the Misión de Guadalupe, dates to 1659. Avenida Juárez near the border has markets and street food worth exploring, and the city's burrito culture is arguably the original — the burrito is said to have been invented here. If you are crossing from El Paso, keep your wits about you and stick to the main commercial areas.
