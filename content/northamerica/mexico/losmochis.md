Los Mochis is a modern agricultural city in northwest Sinaloa that most travellers pass through for one reason: it is the western terminus of the Chepe railway, the spectacular train line that crosses the Copper Canyon (Barranca del Cobre) to Chihuahua. The canyon system is larger and deeper than the Grand Canyon, and the train ride through it — clinging to cliffsides, crossing bridges over dizzying gorges — is one of Mexico's great journeys.

The city itself is functional rather than charming, built on tomato and sugar cane money. The seafood is excellent, though — you are close to the Sea of Cortez, and the topolobampo shrimp and ceviche are worth seeking out. The nearby port town of [Topolobampo](/northamerica/mexico/topolobampo) offers boat trips into the bay and ferry connections to La Paz in Baja California Sur.

If you are catching the early morning Chepe departure, stay the night near the train station and stock up on supplies — facilities along the canyon route are limited. The first-class Chepe Regional runs three times a week and stops at all the major canyon viewpoints.
