Vacationers from all over the world travel to gorgeous Manzanillo, Mexico. The coastal town is situated south of Puerto Vallarta and just North of Acapulco along the Pacific Ocean. Previously known as an significant port city for Mexico, Manzanillo has recently surged as a top tourist destination for travelers of all walks of life. The main attractions here are the pristine beaches and unpopulated shores the town has to offer.

**La Boquita - The Locals' Beach**

La Boquita is located on the northernmost tip of Santiago Bay. It is popular with the locals due to its mild surf and pretty lagoon, and can thus be quite busy on the weekends. Available activities include boogie-boarding, banana boat rides, snorkeling, scuba diving, horseback riding and shopping. Traveling beach vendors will stop by to show you their wares, from souvenirs and toys to t-shirts and jewelry. Bring your appetite too, seafood stands offer fresh and cheap eats that you won't want to miss.

**
Playa L'Recif - A Photographer's Fantasy**

This miniscule, pebble-covered cove is known for its picturesque beauty-above and below water level. Photo ops include the sandy shore and tropical plant life, the enormous coral reef just off the shore, whales and dolphins and so much more. Diving and snorkeling are other activities available at Playa L'Recif. A word to the wise: beware of unruly tides as the ocean conditions here are very moody. You can only access this beach through L'Recif Restaurant (unless you are staying at the Vida del Mar resort, which is where this beach is located) but it is well-worth it!

**Peña Blanca – An Adventurer’s Haven**

This as-of-yet unpopulated beach's expansive sandy shore is a great place to enjoy ATV riding and Jeep tours. Palm trees provide a shady sanctuary for a quiet rest. Peña Blanca is located about 18 miles north of the town of Manzanillo, but taxi cabs are readily available to take you there. Make sure to check with your hotel first, though, as the beach is closed to the public during the season that endangered turtles lay their eggs.

**Audiencia Beach – Manzanillo’s Movie Star**

Perhaps the most famous beach in the region, Audiencia Beach served as the backdrop for most of the beach scenes in the movie “10.” Today, it is the site for the extravagant Hotel Tesoro. Audiencia Beach is located in between Manzanillo's two bays. It offers a variety of water activities such as kayaking, jet ski, boogie boarding, snorkeling and diving. Umbrellas, tables and chairs are available for rental.

### [Club de Yates](/northamerica/mexico/manzanillo/beaches/club_de_yates)

By following the signs to Las Hadas on the Peninsula de Santiago, you can visit another beach that is actually in La Audiencia Cove. A road to the right of the Las Hadas entrance takes you to Club de Yates street. Bear right and follow Yates to its end where there's a white rock wall and parking. The beach is below, and you can snorkel or dive on either side. This is a very nice cove and somewhat protected from the sea and surge that most other beaches experience.
