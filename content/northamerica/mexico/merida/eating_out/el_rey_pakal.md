Mexican is hardly the right classification for a Yucatecan restaurant, which what the Rey Pakal is all about, but this website doesn't delve that deeply into the regional foods available in Mexico.

Located on the Mérida-Motul highway, just after the Sitpach turnoff, El Rey Pakal is a huge palapa very much like the one in Mani, El Principe Tutul Xiu. It's touristy but not in an unpleasant way. Trio music and huipil-clad hostesses complete the ambience.

The food is 100% traditional Yucatecan with all the classics featured; from the delectable *queso relleno* to Yucatan's version of fast food: *salbutes* and *panuchos *and everything in between. In addition to the delicious food, the fruit waters must be sampled as well. You can not get these lemonades anywhere else! *Naranja agria*, *lima, *and* mandarina* are refreshing and exotic accompaniments to your satisfying meal.

The only wierd thing was the fact that there were french fries and nachos on the menu! One can assume they are trying to appeal to all possible palates...

Open for lunch only - they close around 6 pm or so. Live music on Sundays.
