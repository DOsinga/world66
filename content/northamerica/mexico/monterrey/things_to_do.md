Monterrey has invested heavily in cultural infrastructure, and the results are impressive — particularly the MARCO museum and the Parque Fundidora. The city also has excellent access to outdoor activities in the surrounding Sierra Madre mountains.

**MARCO — Museo de Arte Contemporaneo**

Monterrey's premier contemporary art museum, in a striking building by Ricardo Legorreta near the Macroplaza. The collection is eclectic and Latin-focused, with special exhibitions often bringing in notable North American or European contemporary artists. The central courtyard water sculpture is itself worth seeing, and the gift shop and café are among the best in the city.

**Museo de Historia Mexicana**

The city's history museum, covering Mexican history from pre-Columbian civilizations through independence. Features exhibits on Mayan, Aztec, Olmec, and Toltec cultures as well as the colonial period and modern Mexico.

category: Museum

**Parque Fundidora**

A large urban park built on the site of the city's historic iron foundry. Walking trails, a canal, cycling paths, and several smaller museums are spread across the green space. Free to enter.

category: Sight

**Chipinque Mesa**

A nature reserve on the edge of the city, in the Sierra Madre foothills. Excellent hiking with panoramic views over Monterrey. A real escape from the urban sprawl, accessible by car.

category: Hiking

**Cervecería Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma**

The historic brewery that produces Dos Equis and Tecate offers tours of the facility, and the open beer garden under old oak trees is publicly accessible — a pleasant spot for a cold beer.

category: Sight
