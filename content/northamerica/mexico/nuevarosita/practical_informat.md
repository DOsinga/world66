**Travel Information on the Town of Nueva Rosita
**

Here is a useful **Nueva Rosita practical information** guide regarding the town&rsquo;s demographics and some tips about the local currency. Hopefully, this information will make you feel more confident when visiting Nueva Rosita and will help you get around the town.**
**

**Demographics**

Nueva Rosita is a small town located in the northeastern part of Mexico, in the Coahuila state. It has a population of about 40 000 people, who descend mainly from the Criollo social class. There are almost no Native Americans left. The Criollo is a term originating from the colonial times, which refers to people who were born here, but both their parents have been born in Spain or both their parents have been Criollos. In other words, the people here are of Spanish origin. Most of them are religious and follow the Catholic religion. The main language here is Spanish, but English is understood too.

**Currency
**

The currency used in Nueva Rosita and in the rest of Mexico is the Mexican Peso, which is subdivided into 100 centavos. One US Dollar is almost 12.5 Mexican Pesos. If you need to exchange some money in local currency, look for an establishment with a sign saying "Casa de Cambio", as this is how the local exchange bureaus are called. They have favorable exchange rates and compete with local banks. Make sure to have enough smaller denomination banknotes, as it may be really tough to get money exchanged when using larger denominations.
