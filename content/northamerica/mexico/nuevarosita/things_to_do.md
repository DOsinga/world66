title: "Things to Do in Nueva Rosita"
type: section
---

**Nueva Rosita things to do** include exploring the history of this northeast Mexican coalmining town and trips to the nearby desert. Don&rsquo;t forget to try the local cuisine.

**Sightseeing and Attractions in Town**

There are several interesting historical monuments in Nueva Rosita including the Pante&oacute;n Municipal, the Iglesia San Juan, the Ignacio Zaragoza school and the former Presidencia Municipal. If you are interested in local architecture, visit El templo de San Juan or the La Mansi&oacute;n plant. If you are interested in local culture and the history of the region, visit the Nueva Rosita museum to learn more.

Go for a stroll in the Los Sabinitos Park, or try the Los Gritos or San Antonio parks on the shores of the Sabinas River. You can walk around or sit and have a relaxed picnic here and take some time out from traveling.

If you have a day to spend, drive down to the Area de Preteccion de Flora y Fauna Cuatrocienegas. This nature reserve is a beautiful protected area in the desert and was created to protect the fragile local ecosystem and the diverse natural life here. Underground water networks form rivers and pools and even though you are in the desert, there is a surprising amount of life. Even though it is a protected area, there are some pools that are used for recreation, including swimming and snorkeling.

**Eating out in Nueva Rosita**

Northeastern Mexico&rsquo;s cuisine has many similarities to Mexican food in general, but there are also some local specialties. When visiting Nueva Rosita, you should definitely sample the local food in one of the restaurants and eateries in town.

Many restaurants in the area serve "cabrito," which is essentially a whole young goat that has been roasted for hours. You can order any parts of the goat:* *"pierna" is legs, "pecho" is breast and "paleta" is shoulder, and the lower back or "rinonada" is said to be one of the best parts. If you cannot imagine eating a baby goat, local steaks can be very good and excellent value for money. "Carne machacado" is another local specialty and is made with salted, dried and pounded beef, and served with tomatoes, chillis and eggs.

Vegetarians may have some problems here, but as well as other Mexican vegetarian dishes, try "gorditas:" corn cakes that can be stuffed with meat but can also have a vegetarian filling such as beans or cheese. You should also not forget to wash your meal down with local beer.
