---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Nueva Rosita"
type: section
---

**Nueva Rosita must do&rsquo;s** include sampling local cuisine and its specialties, and exploring the history of this old coalmining town in northeastern Mexico.

**1. Learn about Local History**

There are several interesting historical monuments in Nueva Rosita including the Pante&oacute;n Municipal, the Iglesia San Juan, the Ignacio Zaragoza school and the former Presidencia Municipal. If you are interested in local architecture, visit El templo de San Juan or the La Mansi&oacute;n plant. If you are interested in local culture and the history of the region, visit the Nueva Rosita museum to learn more.

**2. Shop for Local Handicrafts**

Go shopping for local handicrafts and see if you can find some good deals on arts and crafts, or on Mexico&rsquo;s specialties: hammocks. Mexican hammocks are one of the best souvenirs from the country so make sure you get one for home.

**3. Have a Picnic in the Los Sabinitos Park**

Go for a stroll in the Los Sabinitos Park, or try the Los Gritos or San Antonio parks on the shores of the Sabinas River. You can walk around or sit and have a relaxed picnic here and take some time out from traveling.

**4. Take a Trip to the Desert**

If you have a day to spend, drive down to the Area de Preteccion de Flora y Fauna Cuatrocienegas. This nature reserve is a beautiful protected area in the desert and was created to protect the fragile local ecosystem and the diverse natural life here. Underground water networks form rivers and pools and even though you are in the desert, there is a surprising amount of life. Even though it is a protected area, there are some pools that are used for recreation, including swimming and snorkeling.

**5. Sample the Local Food**

Northeastern Mexico&rsquo;s cuisine has many similarities to Mexican food in general, but there are also some local specialties. When visiting Nueva Rosita, you should definitely sample the local food.

Many restaurants in the area serve* *"cabrito," which is essentially a whole young goat that has been roasted for hours. You can order any parts of the goat: the lower back or "rinonada" is reputedly the best part of the goat. If you cannot imagine eating a baby goat, try "carne machacado" made with salted, dried and pounded beef and served with tomatoes, chillis and eggs. "Gorditas" are corn cakes that can be stuffed with meat, but vegetarians can try "gorditas" with a bean and cheese stuffing. Mexican beer is the best accompaniment to your meal.
