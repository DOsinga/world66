---
title: "Things to Do in Nuevo Casas Grandes"
type: section
---

Located in the province of Chihuahua, the town of Nuevo Casas Grandes is one of historical and archaeological significance to Mexico. There is much to see and do here, and here are some **Nuevo Casas Grandes Things to Do**.

**See the Casas Grandes**

Standing as one of Mexico&rsquo;s most important archaeological sites, the Casas Grandes, or Great Houses in Spanish, is located just five km outside of the town itself. The ruins include some of the best preserved pottery of the Spanish times, with its museum reflecting the lifestyles of the time in which the people lived. Long home to some of Mexico&rsquo;s indigenous peoples, the Casas Grandes itself is a stone-hewn structure, existing as clusters of houses that may have housed up to 2500 people in the 14th century. The structure itself is complex, consisting of a complete sewage system, communal and dining areas, as well as multiple exits and entrances that served to be a way of circumventing enemy attacks or natural disasters.

**Head to Mata Ortiz**

Another of the city&rsquo;s very famous landmarks, the town of Mata Ortiz is located near the ruins of the Casas Grandes – for a good reason as well, considering how the township is known for its pottery and art. The pottery and art forms here have been inspired by the pottery found in the ruins of the Casas Grandes, but many of these have a modern twist on them, showing off the artists&rsquo; creativity in their products. Many of these pieces have gone on exhibition in many parts of the world, and the potters of Mata Ortiz have been internationally acclaimed to have combined the traditional indigenous art forms with modern interpretations. Tourists are welcome to buy some of the pieces here as display pieces for homes, or just for collector purposes.

**Visit the Mormon Community**

In a surprising mix, the town of Nuevo Casas Grandes is also home to a large Mormon community. Often found on the outskirts of the town, these people speak Spanish as their second language and live in isolated, self-sufficient communities away from the mainstream settlements found within the city itself. As much as they are isolated, many of these communities welcome visitors in their farms and orchards. Drive up to these communities for an afternoon, and enjoy their warm hospitality as they bring tourists around their farming areas or to see their livestock.
