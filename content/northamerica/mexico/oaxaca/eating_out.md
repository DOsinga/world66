---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Oaxaca is one of the most exciting food cities in Mexico. The headline dishes are the seven moles — **negro**, **rojo**, **coloradito**, **amarillo**, **verde**, **chichilo** and **manchamantel** — dense sauces made from chilies, seeds, spices and, in the black mole, chocolate. Beyond mole, look for **tlayudas** (a crisp foot-wide tortilla layered with beans, cheese and grilled meat), **memelas** (corn cakes with toppings), **chapulines** (grasshoppers fried with chili and lime — better than they sound), and whatever street snack appears at the 20 de Noviembre market, where rows of smoky grills offer "meal by the kilo" service. Finish with a mezcal flight; the village distilleries at Santiago Matatlán, 45 minutes east of town, are the source.
