Get your pesos ready--some of Mexico's finest shopping is in Oaxaca. Oaxaca City and the surrounding villages are home to famous handcrafted pottery, woodcarvings and weavings, and the process of shopping is one of the pleasures of visiting the colonial capital. You can peruse quaint street-side shops, sprawling outdoor markets and rural villages during your such for signature goods: shiny black pottery, woolen textiles dyed with natural cochineal , and colorful, imaginative alebrijes, wood carvings. Just be sure to bring an extra suitcase (or two).
**
Best Prices for Mexican Crafts
**
While Oaxacan crafts are available throughout the country, they are at their most authentic, and reasonably priced, in the city and its surrounding villages; expect to pay 20-60% less than in touristy shops elsewhere. Generally, markets offer better deals than stores, although stores boast a greater selection, and the higher-end ones offer desirable service such as international shipping. Strolling the ambient cobbled streets, popping into shops and galleries, is a wonderful way to spend a Oaxacan afternoon; visiting one of the villages to do a little market browsing is also a great cultural experience. Our recommendation: do both.

**Shops and Galleries**

Most of the shops, galleries, and boutiques can be found in the area between Santo Domingo and the Zócalo, along Alcala, Garcia Vigil and 5 de Mayo streets. One of the best is Mujeres Artesanas de las Regiones de Oaxaca (MARO), an all-female artists' co-op and enormous shop where you can find just about everything; crafts are high-quality, reasonably priced and support a good cause. Located in a restored mansion, Galería Indigo features ceramics, graphics, paintings, and other works by local artists. Blackbox is Oaxaca City's hippest craft store, featuring creative works by younger artists. Most stores are open from 10am to 2pm and 4 to 7pm Monday through Saturday.

**Markets **

Oaxaca City is home to two bustling markets; one is just south of the Zócalo, and the newer, Abastos Market, is about 10 blocks west. Abastos Market has particularly good pottery and basketry selections on Saturdays. The advantage of markets is that you buy from the artists directly; prices tend to be lower, and you can learn more about the crafts themselves by talking to the people that make them. Do expect to spend a little time digging, which many consider part of the fun. Markets are generally open from 8am to 5pm daily.

**Surrounding Villages**

Outside of Oaxaca City, the villages of Tlacolula, Etla, Tlaxiaco, Ejutla, Mihuatlan, Zaachila, Arrazola, La Union, San Martin and Pinotepa Nacional all make worthy side trips for serious craft hounds. Papalutla and Guelavia are known for their baskets and bird cages, while the Valley of Teotitlan is world-known for its fine wool rugs.

Buses run to these towns, but if you desire a little more comfort and convenience, cars are available for hire from Oaxaca City. Most of the villages are quite rustic, without even paved roads. Passionate artists will be eager to teach you more about their craft, giving demonstrations and explaining techniques and cultural significances.
