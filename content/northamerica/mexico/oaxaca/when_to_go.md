---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Oaxaca has a mild, spring-like climate year-round — the high elevation keeps it comfortable even in summer. The **dry season** runs October to May; the **rainy season** (afternoon thunderstorms, mostly) from June to September. The two unmissable times to come are **Day of the Dead** (Día de los Muertos) in the last days of October and first of November, when the city decks itself in marigolds and candlelit altars, and the **Guelaguetza** folk-dance festival on the two Mondays after 16 July, when indigenous communities from across the state perform on the hillside amphitheatre above the city.
