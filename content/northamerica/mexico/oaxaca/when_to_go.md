The state capital of Mexico – the city of Oaxaca, has some of the
deepest caverns and the highest mountains in the world. It has pristine
beaches and unexplored jungles. And it has the mild temperate climate
that makes it possible for visitors to enjoy these treasures.

**Varied Weather Patterns**

While
the average annual temperature in the city generally hovers around
24°C, the weather varies in different areas due to differences in
altitude.

For instance, you can expect it to be humid and hot
(28.3°C) in the coastal areas bordering the Pacific Ocean; and cold in
regions higher than 2,000 meters above sea level. On the whole, Spring
in the city sees 25°C, Summer 22°C, Autumn 22°C and Winter16°C.

Some
say that the city enjoys an “Eternal Spring” because of its elevation
of 5000 feet above sea level. The driest months are usually October to
May.
**
Storms and Rainbows in Oaxaca**

In the summer
months (mostly July and August), the city gets spells of rain. The
rainy season lasts from May to October, during which there may be
hurricanes. From May to the end of summer, the city is prone to stormy
weather. Torrential rain during this time is known to wash away roads
in the lower regions, leading to traffic jams, and road closures.

However,
there’s a bright side to the storm clouds and bolts of lightning. Not
for nothing has Oaxaca been called the “City of Jade.” Many of its
buildings have been constructed from pale green stone which takes on a
deeper hue after a thundershower, imparting to the city a tinge of
green. And there’s the possibility of a triple rainbow.

**When to Head to the Beaches of Oaxaca**

The best time for the beaches is winter with the water warm,
afternoons breezy and evenings cool. In the summer months, the weather
here is hot and steamy. However, if you’re looking for bargains, summer
on the beach is ideal.

So, the best time to visit Oaxaca is in
the winter, but getting there will be more expensive at this time, and
you’re not likely to find bargains.

November) may not sound inviting, but at this time, the city has some
interesting sights to offer. Cemeteries, home altars and even shops and
