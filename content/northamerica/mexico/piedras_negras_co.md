Piedras Negras is a border city of about 200,000 people in the state of Coahuila, facing Eagle Pass, Texas across the Rio Grande. It is less touristy than many Mexican border towns and feels more authentically Mexican as a result — less curio-shop strip, more working city.

The town claims to be the birthplace of the nacho: local legend holds that in 1943, cook Ignacio "Nacho" Anaya improvised a snack of tortilla chips with melted cheese and jalapeños for a group of US military wives at a restaurant called Victory Club. A statue commemorates the nacho in the town centre.

The surrounding area is ranchland and hunting country. Piedras Negras is a practical border crossing for travellers heading into or out of northeastern Mexico, with reasonable hotels and good road connections to Saltillo and Monterrey via Federal Highway 57.
