The Mayan ruins of Tulum sit on a cliff above the Caribbean — the only significant Mayan site built directly on the coast. The main Castillo pyramid and the Temple of the Frescoes are well preserved, and the setting is dramatic. The ruins are about 60 km south of Playa del Carmen and usually combined with a stop at one of the nearby cenotes and the beach below the ruins. Tulum town has developed rapidly as a boutique hotel destination and has a significant nightlife and restaurant scene in its own right.
