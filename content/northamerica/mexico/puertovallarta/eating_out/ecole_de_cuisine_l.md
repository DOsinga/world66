If you spend one evening outside of Puerto Vallarta while visiting Banderas Bay, make it a point to have dinner chez Margarita and Gilles Le Fort.

The show begins at 8 p.m. with you and a few select others sitting at the counter around M. Le Fort's kitchen sipping kir royale and eating housemade paté. You have a sheet of recipes for the evening's meal to reference as Gilles begins to work his magic.

After preparing the three courses for the evening, you'll adjourn to the dining room to taste the creations. The Le Forts pour two more kinds of wine selected to enhance the meal. (Of course, you're welcome to choose a different bottle from the cellar if you'd like something more sophisticated, but it's not included.)

After the main course, you'll have an opportunity to select from the expansive aperitif cart including Armagnac, Cognac, Port, Madeira, Calvados many from the mid-twentieth century. They're all worth it. If you're not certain what would be best, Gilles is very happy to guide you to something that will be perfect for you.

The food is remarkable. If you're among the first to reserve your seat (reservations are a must!), you'll have the opportunity to set the menu. Don't be shy. The quiche lorraine is to die for. The cassoulet is arguably the best on this side North America. You're sure to have your own favorites by the time the night is through!
