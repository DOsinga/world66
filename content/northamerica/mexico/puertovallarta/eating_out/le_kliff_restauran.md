Le Kliff advertises itself as the "most beautiful restaurant in Mexico"
- and it just might be. Built on a sea cliff overlooking the Pacific,
the restaurant is made up of a series of palapas linked by winding
staircases. The tables line each palapa and many have a 180 degree or
wider view of the ocean and nearby coast.

Most places with views like this have disappointing food, the thought
being that you're really just there for the view - but Le Kliff has
some wonderful, inventive dishes. We enjoyed fire-roasted lobster and
wonderful ceviche on our last visit, as well as delicious daiquiris.
The prices are expensive - more than I have ever spent in Mexico, but,
cliched as it sounds, the experience of eating at Le Kliff is truly a
once in a lifetime experience.

Warning: All of the restaurant requires stairs to access, and the decks are certainly not kid-safe.
