Puerto Vallarta's main square, right next to the Church of Guadalupe and a few steps from the Malecón. A bronze statue of Ignacio Vallarta — the 19th-century governor who gave the city its name — stands at its centre, with a bandstand nearby that hosts musicians in the evenings. Shaded benches, art vendors, and a front-row seat to the nightly promenade along the waterfront.
