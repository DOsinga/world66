Queretaro, Mexico is a small agricultural town 120 miles south of Mexico City. The water in **Queretaro **that flows to **beaches **is the cleanest in the country. Queretaro is beginning to be a highly successful tourist stop, possible due to its proximity to the shore as well as vineyards in the city. The Pacific beaches are the closest to Querataro.

**Puerto Escondido**

Puerto Escondido, also known as Hammocks on the Beaches, is small fishing village on the Pacific Ocean. The town has become famous for the quality of it beaches. In addition to tourists, retirees move to the area to take advantage of the low cost of living and the natural beauty there. People who enjoy water sports will not be disappointed, as the area is famous for diving, surfing and sailing. There is enough of an expatriate population that vacationers can feel like they are at home while on vacation.

**Playa Los Muertos**

Playa Los Muertos, also known as Dead Men&rsquo;s Beach, is located in the coastal town of Puerto Vallarta, close drive to Queretaro. The beach gets its name from the legendary pirates and the local battles that used to occur off the coast. Today the beach is more famous for windsailing than pirate battles, but the reputation still remains. Locals like to entertain visitors with stories of those battles. The government has worked hard to make this area a vibrant place for tourists. During the 80&rsquo;s, the local government worked to clean up the pollution in the area. Their efforts have paid off, and the beaches have since filled with tourists.

**Playa Camarones**

Playa Camarones, or "Shrimp beach" is famous for its local shrimping community, and is a short distance from Queretaro. The waters are warm and gentle; ideal for families with small children or couples looking for a romantic getaway. The currents can difficult and are made for strong swimmers. Lifeguards are stationed nearby to ensure the safety of swimmers. Whale watching is a popular activity in this area, as are banana boat rides, parasailing, and jet skiing. There is a variety of restaurants with locally caught seafood that tourists can enjoy.
