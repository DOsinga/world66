---
title: "Day Trips in Queretaro"
type: section
---

There are many** Queretaro day trips** to partake in around the state of Queretaro. These day trips include climbing La Pena de Bernal, seeing the famous hot springs of Tequisquiapan, and visiting the chapels of Toliman.

**La Pena de Bernal**

La Pena de Bernal, meaning Bernal Peak, is a monolith located in the state of Queretaro. It is the fourth largest monolith, standing at 1,148 ft tall. There is a small town outside of the La Pena de Bernal, where visitors can buy local rugs, jewelry and tapestries, among other things. La Pena de Bernal can be hiked, but only about half way up. There is a small chapel that can be visited at the highest climbable point.

**Tequisquiapan**

Tequisquiapan is town located in the state of Queretaro. Within the town are old buildings, Plaza Civica and a church. In the Plaza Civica, there is site seeing and shopping that can be done around the town. There are also natural thermal springs located within Tequisquiapan. The hot springs are what Tequisquiapan is most famous for, but there is also more to do.

There are hiking and mountain biking trails in this town. All throughout the week, there is a market open from 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM everyday. It is at this market that visitors can buy handmade furniture and local crafts. Visiting Tequisquiapan and spending a day outside is a good idea for a Queretaro day trip.
**
Toliman**

Queretaro day trips are not complete without visiting Toliman, a small town located about an hour outside of the capital of Queretaro. There are many sites to see in Toliman. These sites include the San Pablo Temple, a church built between 1777 and 1785, and Chalma Capels, built between 1919 and 1935. El Calvario Hill is also a place to visit--the site of ancient ceremony where villagers ascend the hill during the Holy Week in honor of Jesus.
