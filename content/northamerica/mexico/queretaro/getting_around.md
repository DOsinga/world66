---
title: "Getting Around in Queretaro"
type: section
---

There are many ways to move from one place to another in this Mexican city, although **Queretaro transportation **seems confusing for tourists at first. There are many means of transportation in Queretaro, including:

**Taxi
**

There are many taxis that can be found all around Queretaro, and they can be hailed on the streets. At times, taxi prices are negotiated with drivers before taking passengers to their destination. However, tourists should tell the driver to turn on the meter. Tourists that speak Spanish have a bargaining advantage. There are taxis that are available through hotels in Queretaro, however, these cabs are more expensive then the cabs that can be hailed off the streets.

**Metro
**
Queretaro is a city that prides itself on its Metro system. It is said to be the best metro system in the world, being fast, clean and easy to use. Queretaro metro is open from 5am-6pm on weekdays, and from 5am-7pm on weekends and holidays. A one way metro ticket can be purchased at any metro station, costing riders $2.00 USD per one way trip, regardless how far the trip is.

**Rental Car**

Travelers always have an option to rent a car in Queretaro. Getting around by renting a car costs about $25.00 USD a day. There is no price difference if a car is rented at the airport or in the city. However, money can be saved by purchasing a trip package that includes a rental car. There is also the cost of gas that needs to be factored in when renting a car. It can prove to be cheaper to use public transportation instead of renting a car.
