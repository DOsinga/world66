---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Queretaro"
type: section
---

**Queretaro Nightlife** is varied, and you will be able to find something here for everyone. There are plenty of clubs and bars, but there are also many cultural activities and festivals to enjoy throughout the year.

#### Popular Clubs and Bars

If you want to start out your weekend early, then head over to Balcao, where they offer great music and drinks on Thursday nights. Another great nightclub is Foreplay, which consists of three different rooms, each room playing its own style of music. Mint is another favorite, though you must be 18 or older to enter the club.

For those that love English pubs, step into La Taverna Beatlemania, as this is a replica of the first bar in England where the Beatles played. If you want to enjoy some jazz music, then try Harry&rsquo;s Hew Orleans Caf&eacute; and Oyster Bar. Another great bar and lounge is Night Club at Technologio.

#### Take in a Show

The Coral de Commedias is a colonial theater where classical plays are performed. The building itself is lovely, with an open courtyard and corridors framed with arches and columns. This is a beautiful natural setting for classic plays. The theatre was started by an acting family that eventually started their own acting school.

The most important theater is the Teatro de la Republic. This is where Emperor Maximilian was tried, where the national anthem was first performed, and where the Mexican Constitution was written. Today this is the performance venue of the Philharmonic Orchestra. You can visit the theater between the hours of 9 a.m. to 6 p.m., and admission is free.

#### Learn Tango

Get out for some dancing while in Queretaro. While you can find many dance clubs, there are also places such as the community center where you can learn how to Tango. Tango is a social dance through which you can meet people, enjoy a free lesson, and then get the chance to practice at the many clubs and bars once you have the basics. There are also several festivals throughout the year where you can see Flamenco at its best.

#### Other Popular Entertainment

Each Sunday you can enjoy a free concert at the Plaza de Armas. After the concert, you can also dance in Jardin Zenea. Besides music, dancing and theater, you can also see rodeos and bull fights at the Plaza de Toros, or take in a popular wrestling match called Luche Libre.

If you are in town in the middle of September, then get ready for the biggest celebration of the year--Independence Day. There are parades, dancing, costumes, fireworks, and more to take in during this holiday. It is like Mardi Gras. Christmas is also another gorgeous time of year, as all of the stores and homes put out beautiful decorations and you will find the locals walking around in the evenings chatting and enjoying the holiday spirit.
