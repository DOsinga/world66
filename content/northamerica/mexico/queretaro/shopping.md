---
title: "Shopping in Queretaro"
type: section
---

**Queretaro Shopping** is quite diverse, and you can find some excellent goods there to take home. You can find opals, arts and crafts, clothing, and more.

#### Beautiful Opals

This is one of two places in the world where opals are mined. You can find a wide range of opals, and the pricing all depends on the shape, size, transparency, and color. There are several stores that offer locally mined opals and other jewels. In Corregidora Norte, stop in at Lapidaria de Queretaro or El Artesano. At El Artesano, the owner carves miniatures out of opals. The owner can provide you with all the information you need to know about opals, but the store hours are odd (1 to 9 p.m.) so make sure you plan accordingly.

#### Shopping Centers

You can find a Mexican supermarket and other small department stores at the Plaza del Parque. It contains the Commercial Mexicana (a supermarket), Suburbia (an affordable fashion store), and Sanborn&rsquo;s (a department store). The Plaza Boulevares contains a betting club named Yak, a sports club named Sport City, several stores, and an arcade. Those looking for European and American chains should stop in at Galerias Queretaro, where you can find Sears, Sanborn&rsquo;s, Zara, Marti Sports, and more.

#### Traditional Crafts

If you are looking for more traditional souvenirs or handcrafts, stop into the historical center. Another excellent store is found at 5 de Mayo, Quinto Real. Everything in this store is made in Mexico, and they specialize in traditional furniture and handcrafts. Some items you should not miss include the basket work, textiles and rag dolls.

Very traditional pieces include lapidary works that are ornaments made from quarry stones. You can also find large fountains, sculptures and smaller ornaments. The traditional costumes are also quite famous and use a host of different fabrics and cloths produced in Mexico. Many of the cloths are edged in a regional lace trimming.

#### The Main Market

On Sundays, the market building and the parking lot of the Mercado de la Cruz are completely covered with stalls and stands. Enjoy the fresh local produce offered here at very low prices. You can find almost anything, but the vegetables, bread, cheeses, and meats are perhaps the most amazing goods offered. It is best to visit the market early, as people close up shop around 5 p.m. If you are not familiar with Mexican products, then try the many types of avocados and mangoes available. Also try the prickly pear fruits and the tomatillos. It is a great spot to have lunch or pick up the makings for tonight&rsquo;s dinner. You can also bargain for a better price, though you should expect to pay $1 per pound. Along with your produce, do not forget to buy some fresh tortillas.
