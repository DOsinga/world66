The city of Queretaro is located in central Mexico, and in **Queretaro**, **things to do** include touring the religious shrines of the area or taking a hike in the nearby mountains. The city was originally found by Spanish missionaries in 16th century, and the town has retained much of the flavor of that period.

**Leyendas Tour**

The Leyendas Tour offers a different take on the historical Queretaro. The guides on this tour dress up as historical characters and offer a more colorful view of history. For instance, a tour group will meet the Carambada, the mysterious woman who supposedly killed Benito Juarez with poisoned tea. The Marquis is another colorful figure of Queretaro. He supposedly sold his soul to the devil for an additional 50 years of life and fortune. The staff recreates his life done to the final details where his body is found suspended in the rafters of the ceiling. As travelers meet the characters, they also get a chance to dour the city and see all the cultural highlights.

**Centro Historico**

The Centro Historico is the historical center of the city. There are a number of great things for visitors to see. There are a number of things for people to do and see. The Theater of the Republic is the most site in Mexico, due to the historical nature of the building. In 1917, the Mexican Constitution and national anthem were written here. The Emperor Maximilian also had his trial there. Also located in the Centro Historico is the Palacio del Gobierno del Estado. This building has housed the governor of this state since the time of the missionaries. The El Carmen church adds a touch of Hollywood to this district. El Carmen&rsquo;s claim to fame is that &ldquo;One Upon a Time in Mexico&rdquo; was filmed here.

**El Pueblito**

The El Pueblito Pyramid is a short drive from Quenatro. A Mayan temple built in the 1st century AD, it a wonderful example of pre-Columbus architecture. Coming to the temple, there are many tours offered by guides for visitors. The talented guides show the people the tombstones, calendars and other relics found in the temple, and bring history to life.
