---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Queretaro"
type: section
---

**Queretaro must do's** are numerous when you visit this part of Mexico.

**Franciscan Missions in the Sierra Gorda**

Now a World Heritage Site, the missions were founded in the 18th century and are part of the rich Christian Evangelical history during that time. The five churches are beautifully built, each having a different style and architecture. The interiors of the missions are something to look forward to as you will be able to take those memorable pictures. Make sure you arrange for a self-drive car or hire a driver to take you round all the missions.

**Cerro de la Campanas**

Another Queretaro must-do is visiting the place known as the "Hill of Bells." Located to the west, where three famous people were killed for fighting for independence, this place has a lot of historical significance and tales. On the hill, you will be able to enjoy the beauty of the park which has many interesting areas like a theater and a lake. Visit the city's museum within the park and see various exhibits. There is a unique monument in honor of Benito Juarez. The park is open every day.

**The Aqueduct**

You should add this to your Queretaro must-do list. Built in the 1700s to bring water to the residents, these 74 tall arches are 23 meters high and more than 1000 meters long. The grounds are also very well kept. Make sure you go there equipped with a camera as you will not see such structures anywhere else in the world.

**Queretaro's Historic Center**

Make a point of going to this place in the evening as you will be able to take a walk along the clean walkways. You can enjoy a meal or drink in the many eating places along the way. Local snacks and food can also be found in the many food stalls. There are also theater groups who take tourists for a tour while recounting tales and legends from the past. It is truly a memorable experience.

**Plaza de Independencia**

Queretaro must-do list has to include going to this plaza as it is very ancient. There are very many outdoor cafes and establishments where you can enjoy the local cuisine. Very old mansions make this place a perfect picture taking venue. Cobblestones line the streets. A very well-sculptured fountain lies right in the heart of the plaza. Other places you can visit are the Libertad Gallery or an 1800s mansion known as Ecala House. Another very famous building to visit is the Palacio de la Corregidora.
