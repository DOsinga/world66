Reynosa is a major border city of about 650,000 people in the state of Tamaulipas, sitting directly across the Rio Grande from McAllen, Texas. Like many Mexican border cities, it has a split personality — a busy industrial economy driven by maquiladoras and cross-border trade, and a reputation for cartel-related violence that has made international headlines. Travelers should check current security advisories carefully before visiting, as conditions can change quickly.

That said, Reynosa is a real, working city with a bustling centro, a large commercial zone along the highway to Monterrey, and a border-crossing culture that is fascinating in its own right. The Zona Centro has a lively market where you can find everything from boots to electronics, and the Plaza Principal has a few decent restaurants. Reynosa is primarily a transit point — either for crossing to or from Texas, or for the drive south to Monterrey, which is about three hours away through increasingly dramatic mountain scenery. If you are crossing the border here, keep your wits about you and stick to well-traveled routes.
