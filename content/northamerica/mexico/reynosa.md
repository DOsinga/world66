Reynosa is a border city in Tamaulipas state, across the Rio Grande from McAllen, Texas. It is a large, industrial city — about 700,000 people — and for most of its modern history it has been a crossing point for commerce and people rather than a destination.

The Zona Rosa, the old tourist and entertainment district near the international bridge, once drew Texans across the border for cheap drinks, dentistry, and pharmacy visits. Security concerns have sharply reduced casual border crossing in recent years, and the city faces significant challenges related to cartel violence. Travellers should check current security advisories carefully before visiting.

For those passing through, the Plaza Principal and the Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe church provide a brief stop in the old centre. The mercado has the usual border-town selection of leather goods and crafts. Most visitors to Reynosa today are on business, visiting family, or in transit.
