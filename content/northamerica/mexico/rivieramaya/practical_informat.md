The Riviera Maya is the name given to the beautiful stretch of
coastline south of Cancun to Belize. The ideal tropical conditions here
coupled with the geography create some of the most amazing ecosystems
on the planet. The region features many uncrowded, peaceful beaches, a
slew of underground rivers, and over 100 cenotes. The Riviera Maya is
an eco adventure location that will suit every lifestyle.

**Location
**

Originally called the Cancun-Tulum corridor, the
Riviera Maya is located on the Yucatan Pennisula of Mexico. Bordered by
the Caribbean Sea, the Mayan Riviera tourism district is historically
detailed from Cancun to the village of Tulum along Highway 400. With
the influx of steady growth in the region, the town of Puerto Morelos
to the north of Cancun and the town of Felipe Carrillo Puerto (25 miles
south of Tulum) are now considered part of the Riviera Maya as well.

**Telephone**

For all long distance calls in Mexico, you will
need to dial 01, followed by the area code, and then the telephone
number. To access a number outside of Mexico you will need to dial 00
and the country code of the country in which you are calling, then the
area code (omitting the zero), followed by the number. The area code
for the region of Riviera Maya is 98. Mexico's country code is 52. For
a police emergency the number to dial is 18.39.54, for a fire emergency
dial 14.20.50, and for a medical emergency dial 15.20.55.

**Electricity**

All electrical outlets are 120V/60 cycles, the same as in the United States.

**Currency**

Mexico's currency is the Mexican Peso ($), to
distinguish from the American Dollar ($) the Mexican currency symbol is
seen as MX$ or sometimes $100 MN. There is one hundred cents to every
peso. The US dollar is also widely accepted. Most ATMs (cashpoints) are
found on the main streets of each of the beach cities. If you need to
exchange currency or cash in traveler's checks stop by the Casas de
Cambio during business hours. Major credit cards, and traveler's checks
are widely accepted at most hotels, and established restaurants and
retail shops. While traveling in Mexico it is advisable to carry some
cash when shopping, as street vendors, and most markets only accept
cash. Keep your wallet/money hidden safely on your person to avoid
pickpockets.

**Language
**

Spanish is the native language, but English is also widely spoken.

**Time Zone**

CST Central Standard Time or GMT-6.
