Located
right on the Yucatan
Peninsula in the state of
Quintana Roo, The Riviera Maya encompasses 81 miles of coast line filled with
an abundance of natural escapes. From pristine beaches to lush jungles, there
are plenty of sights to see in this world-renowned ancient location.

**Xcaret**

Xcaret was
once a Mayan port for departures to the nearby island of Cozumel.
It is now an eco-preserve park with a number of attractions. Here you can lay
down on the beach, float through subterranean rivers, trek through jungle
trails and even swim with dolphins. There are a variety activities that cater
to all ages. Cultural performances of ancient Mayan rituals take place
throughout the day. Most visitors stay for the whole day into the night to
watch the “Mexico Spectacular Show,” a night show featuring the history,
culture and essence of Mexican culture. It is a fascinating and colorful show
that you will not want to miss.

**Playa del
Carmen**

Playa del
Carmen was a popular fishing village during ancient Mayan times. It currently
is a bustling part of town with a variety of resorts, luxury hotels, shopping
establishments and restaurants. Fifth
Avenue is a popular street in Playa del Carmen. It
runs parallel to the ocean and is filled with many shops, restaurants and
street performers. It is reminiscent of your local carnival as you find fire
breathers, lions and giant boa constrictors being paraded around this street. The
beaches along Playa are nice and clean and have white pristine sand. Whether
you want to relax on the beach or take a quick shopping trip, Playa del Carmen
has it all. With all the pedestrian-friendly walkways and streets, you should
be able to find your way around without a hitch.

**Xel-Ha**

Known as Mexico’s
natural aquarium and biological paradise, Xel-Ha is truly a site to see. Here
the oceans combine with freshwater currents to create natural springs and
underground rivers. There are also stunning natural formations such as
underwater caves known as cenotes. You can experience the jungle by taking a
journey on Xel-Ha’s very own jungle train. This tram takes visitors from the
river to the bus depot and back in a matter of minutes. While on it, enjoy the
view while you go from jungle to open water. You can explore caves and snorkel
to find archaeological wonders in the underground rivers. Lush flora and fauna
can be found almost anywhere in this beautiful paradise. Xel-Ha is just a
wondrous place to experience when visiting the Riviera Maya.
