---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

You come to the Riviera Maya for water and ruins. On the coast, **Playa del Carmen**'s *Quinta Avenida* is the main walking street, with shops, restaurants and street performers, and the ferry from its pier runs out to **Cozumel** in about 45 minutes — Cozumel has some of the best shore diving and snorkeling in the Caribbean. Further south, the **Tulum ruins** sit on a bluff directly above the sea and can be visited in a morning. The inland city of **Chichén Itzá**, the most famous Maya site in the Yucatán, is a long day trip by car (about 2.5 hours). The quieter **Cobá** ruins, in the jungle 45 minutes from Tulum, still let you climb the main pyramid.

Away from the ruins, the region's main attraction is its **cenotes** — flooded limestone sinkholes connected by underground rivers, ranging from open swimming holes lined with tropical forest (Gran Cenote, Cenote Azul) to cathedral-like caves that you can snorkel or dive through. The eco-parks **Xcaret**, **Xel-Há** and **Xplor** package a sanitised version of the same experience with zip lines, dolphins and buffet lunches; purists will prefer to hire a car and find the quieter cenotes along the jungle roads inland. The **Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve**, south of Tulum, is the best place in the region to see manatees, dolphins, and wading birds in something close to their natural state.
