---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Riviera Maya is warm all year, but the season splits neatly into two. The **dry season** runs from **November to April** and brings clear skies, temperatures in the high twenties, and relatively low humidity — this is the peak tourist season, and also the most crowded and expensive. December to February in particular are booked out by North Americans escaping winter; book flights and hotels months ahead if you want to come then. The **wet season** runs from **May to October**, when temperatures push into the mid-thirties, humidity becomes sticky, and afternoon thunderstorms are common. June to November is also **hurricane season**; direct hits are rare but not unknown (Hurricane Wilma devastated the coast in 2005), and September and October are statistically the riskiest months. **Late April, May, and November** are probably the sweet spots — warm, dry, fewer crowds, and better prices.
