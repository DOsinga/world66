---
title: "Beaches in San Jose del Cabo"
type: section
---

Beaches abound in San Jose del Cabo for more than 5 miles along the
Baja Coast of the Sea of Cortez also known as the Gulf of California.
You are sure to find one or more beaches that you will love to while
away the time in the sun.

**The Little Beach**

The Little Beach or La Playita is East of town and a great place to
hop on a boat and go sport fishing. This is not a beach in which to go
swimming but you can watch as the fishermen pull in their catch.

**El Estero Beach**

El Estero has a freshwater lagoon is fed from underground aquifers
and has over 350 species of wildlife and plants. This is a protected
estuary that allows plants and animals to live as they would in the
wild. Here you rent horses, bikes, kayaks or ATV’s from the Presidente
Inter-continental Los Cabos Resort.

**Playa Hotelera**

Playa Hotelera or Hotel Beach is south of downtown and runs past
several hotels such as the Presidente Intercontinental, the Aguamarina,
and Best Western Posada Real. This is not good area to swim because of
the strong shore breaks and deep drop offs. Most hotels have pools in
which to swim. Sitting on the creamy sand of the beach to get a tan is
popular here and many places rent palapas for shade. There is a
children’s park across the street for the little ones in your party and
a golf course is there for those that would be interested.

**Playa Costa Azul**

Playa Costa Azul or Blue Coast Beach is right before Palmilla Bay
and the world famous surf break called Costa Azul. Here there is a surf
camp and you can rent cabanas or eat at the beach restaurant or just
wait for the big wave that will take you on a trip of pure exhilaration.**
**

**Playa Chileno**

Playa Chileno is next to Hotel Cabo San Lucas. The offshore reefs
come up to the shore making it very easy to snorkel. There is a shack
on the beach that rents snorkeling and other water sport equipment and
sells cold drinks.

All beaches in town are public, so go ahead and enjoy all of them and get the feel of each beach.
