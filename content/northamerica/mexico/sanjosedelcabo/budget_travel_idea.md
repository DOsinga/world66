title: "Budget Travel Ideas in San Jose del Cabo"
type: section
---

Visitors to Mexico are always looking for options for **San Jose del
Cabo
Budget Travel**. Here are some tips to consider to stretch your
dollar when visiting to San Jose del Cabo.

**Saving Money with Hostelling International**

Mexico
is 1 of the 90 countries that participate in the Hostelling
International network of hostels and student travel. Having a
membership card from this network will allow you to receive special
discounts--not only at hostels around Mexico but also at several shops,
bars, restaurants, museums and special attractions. You can make hostel
reservations from the Hostelling International
website or by calling directly to the hostels, which is sometimes
recommended. It is important to point out that you should apply for your
membership card before leaving your country of origin because
application within Mexico is limited only to Mexicans and foreign
exchange students with valid student IDs. Also, when applying for your
membership card, make sure to inquire about the special air fares
through the Hostelling International
participating tourist agencies in your city.

**Flying on a Budget**

If
you don't have that many days available to visit Mexico and you would
like to include in your visit as many cities as possible, it is a good
idea to book flights with local Mexican airlines such as Aerobus,
instead of larger airlines such as Mexicana or Aeromexico.
Smaller airlines offer very attractive prices on their
websites. Flight reservations can be made online. To guarantee the
lowest fare possible, it is best to book your flight at least a month in
advance. Cheaper flights go really fast, particularly for travel during national
holidays or over long weekends.

**Getting Around on a Budget**

Within
San Jose del Cabo,
it is best to travel by taxi, because it is affordable and much safer
than traveling by bus, especially at night. When getting into a cab, make
sure that the taxi driver starts the meter only after you have told him
what your destination is. Don't hesitate and be assertive. Be aware
that if you take a taxi late at night, you will have to negotiate a rate
with the taxi driver. This is not unusual and you should be prepared
for this by asking locals or the hotel desk for suggestions for fares.Finally,
if you do decide to travel by bus around San Jose del
Cabo,
avoid wearing flashy jewelry or carrying large amounts of money. You
don't need to have exact fare because the driver will provide change.
