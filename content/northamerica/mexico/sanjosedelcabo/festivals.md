---
title: "Festivals in San Jose del Cabo"
type: section
---

Mexico is host to many lively celebrations that provide insight into Mexico’s
history and traditions. Festivals provide the perfect events for party goers.
San Jose del Cabo is no exception and with its close proximity to the United States of America
it is a popular tourist destination. Not only can tourists enjoy San Jose del
Cabo’s beaches, estuaries, churches and restaurants, there is also a plenitude
of festivals to offer entertainment.

**Festival of San Jose
del Cabo Festivals**

This festival is
held every year at Plaza Mijares. Plaza Mijares is a significant reflection of Mexico's history
and around the area is a church and the town hall. The festival is in March and
is held to honor Mexico’s
patron saint. Every city in Mexico has a patron saint that
offers protection and guidance for the citizens. Immerse yourself in the local culture by joining in with the
vibrant celebrations and sampling some of the traditional food that is
available in abundance during the festival period. Carnival activities are held
as well as cock fights.

**Carnival**

Carnival is celebrated in San Jose. This serves as a final party before
Catholics have to give up pleasurable activities for Lent. This exuberant
festival attracts many people and features dancing, parades and colorful
costumes. It is held in February and is celebrated for 5 days.

**Candlemas**

Candalmas is
celebrated in Mexico
as the fiesta de la Candelaria at the end of winter. The festival includes
parades, traditional music and hanging lanterns on the streets. The festival
also marks the end of the holiday season. Festival celebrations are joined with
Catholic traditions and the purification of the Virgin Mary is an important
part of Candalmas. During the festival a cake is cut with a figure of Jesus
inside it. Whoever gets the Jesus figure hosts the Candlemas party for their
family. People go to church and participate in parades where the statue of
Virgin Mary is carried.

**The Day of the Dead**

The Day of the Dead
is otherwise known as Dia de los Muertos or All Soul’s Day. This day is held to
honor and remember family members and relatives who have passed away. During
the day people gather together to pray for lost loved ones. The Day of the Dead
is held on November 1 and 2. This falls on the same
day as the Catholic All Saints Day. Altars are created to honor the dead and gifts
are taken to the graves of the departed. This holiday can be traced back for
thousands of years.
