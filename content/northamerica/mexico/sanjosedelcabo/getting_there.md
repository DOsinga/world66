**Welcome to San Jose del Cabo**

Located on the southern tip of the peninsula of Baja California, the beach town of San Jose del Cabo (better known in Mexico as Los Cabos, a name also used for the neighboring town of Cabo
San Lucas) was a well kept secret of locals to get away from the urban
stress for many years. Such a great location was hard to keep as a
secret and nowadays is a popular destination for American tourists
(mostly from the states of California and Texas) as well as for
European tourists.

**Getting There by Plane**

The Los Cabos International Airport (IATA airport code: SJD) serves both of Los Cabos
communities through several local and international airline companies. There are
several airline companies that offer direct flights to and from cities
in the United States such as Continental Airlines
(e.g. Houston, Texas) and Alaska Airlines (e.g. San Francisco,
California). Also, there are some Canadian airlines offering direct
flights to and from Canadian cities such as Calgary, Alberta (e.g. Air
Canada) and Vancouver, British Columbia (Enerjet).

If you're flying from within Mexico, it is best to use smaller local airlines such as Viva Aerobus and Volaris over larger local airlines like Mexicana and Aeromexico because the first 2 offer deeper discounts by using alternative routes.

**Getting There by Bus**There are literally dozens of local bus companies that offer bus routes to and from San Jose del Cabo. No matter which one you choose, always look for the servicio ejecutivo because these buses provide air conditioning, larger and more comfortable seats; movie service and often several snacks.
