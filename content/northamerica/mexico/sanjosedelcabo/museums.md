---
title: "Museums in San Jose del Cabo"
type: section
---

San Jose del Cabo, located at the tip of the country
just next to the sea, is a small urban area and is famous
for several museums in the city and in its vicinity. Here is a guide to know
about the famous museums in an around the city.

**El Chopo Museum**

Located at Santa Maria la Ribera, the museum is known for its display
of visual art exhibits and experimental projects that display the artwork
of many new and old artists. It provides a theater and facilities for dancing, singing,
movies, galleries. The museum was founded in 1902 and named
as El Chopo in 1975. The opening hours of the museum are Tuesday to Sunday 10 a.m. to
2 p.m. and 4 p.m. to 7 p.m.

**National Museum of Anthropology**

It is one of the most famous museums of the country and is located just
outside the city at Reforma and Gandhi Chapultepec Polanco. It displays items that show the
history of Mexico and explain how it emerged as a nation. It also exhibits the
historical data and facts about the various ethnic groups who have lived on the
lands of Mexican nation over a period of the past 100 years. Entry fee is 45
pesos and admission is free on Sundays. Throughout the week, opening hours remain the
same--Tuesday through Sunday, 9 a.m. to 7 p.m.
