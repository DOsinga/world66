**San Jose del Cabo Practical Information**

When planning your trip to San Jose del Cabo,
there are a couple of important tips to know. Not only will these tips
allow you to save some money, but they will also allow you to have a more enjoyable stay at San Jose del Cabo.

**Los Cabos Municipality**

San Jose del Cabo is part of the Los Cabos Municipality together with the city of Cabo San Lucas. Locals refer to this 3,500 square kilometer (about 1,350 square mile) area simply as Los Cabos. The distance between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo is quite small so it is a good idea to look to consider both destinations when booking a bus or plan ticket.

**Save Money with Smaller Local Airlines**

If you're departing from a local city or from some cities in the
states of California and Texas, you should look into booking your
flight through smaller Mexican airlines (e.g. Volaris, Click and Viva Aerobus). Smaller local airlines provide provide cheaper options to more established airlines (Mexicana and Aeromexico)
but it is important to know that you will be arriving to alternative
airports. However, these smaller airlines provide complimentary shuttle
services to important stops in San Jose del Cabo such as the main bus bus station and city center.

**Get Access to Many Discounts**

Before your arrival to San Jose del Cabo, plan to apply for a membership of Hosteling International at your local office of this organization. Having this membership card will not only allow you to save money in San Jose del Cabo, but in all of Mexico. Unfortunately, there are no participating hostels of the Hosteling International network in the city of San Jose del Cabo. However, there are several retailers, bars, restaurants, night clubs, historic sites and museums that participate in the Hosteling International network and that will offer you special offers once you flash your card.

**Learn More about San Jose del Cabo by Avoiding the Crowds**

It is quite tempting to head only to the most popular and well known places in San Jose del Cabo but that will prevent you to getting to know the full city. San Jose del Cabo's true local lifestyle is best represented by fishing at La Playita. This was one of the the best kept secrets from locals and it was kept that way for a long time. However, the rapid growth of eco tourism and interest in our environment, has allowed the La Playita to develop a better infrastructure for tourism. Spending a day fishing at the calm La Playita will give plenty of time to catch up on you reading material, get a great tan and, hopefully, enjoy your catch.
Since we're talking about eco tourism, it is time to mention San Jose's Bird Sanctuary and the Cabo Pulmo
National Park. Both of these national nature reserves offer free
admission and will allow you to experience the natural side of San Jose
del Cabo.
