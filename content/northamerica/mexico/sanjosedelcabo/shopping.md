---
title: "Shopping in San Jose del Cabo"
type: section
---

Sanjose del Cabo, a calm and peaceful city in Mexico, is the ultimate paradise of peace for the weary person burdened with life’s busy pace. You tend to forget and leave behind worries and anxieties the moment you enter the place. Tranquility abounds! It sort of brings in the old Mexican charm with all the historic buildings and all imposing Paroquia San Jose church.

**Main Central Plaza Market
**

It’s a great place to go shopping with its easy laid back style of small shops that surrounds the city’s main central plaza across the church. The traditional appeal of Mexican handicrafts and paintings gets highlighted in these shops. Shoppers can find a wonderful selection of high quality folk art, jewelry and house wares.
Its also laced with a number of small restaurants. The atmosphere around these shops is casual and relaxing, allowing you to enjoy the ambience of the city. There are also few modern malls for the choosy customer.

**NECRI
**

It’s a must stop for those interested in handicrafts and is situated in Blvd. Mijares #16, downtown, right in front of the fountains and it has the largest selection of Talavera plates in the city. The shop also has very large decorative ceramics and is kind of impossible to miss with its purple- green paint (very Mexican indeed). NCERI has been in the area for more than 20 years.

**SAX
**

It’s located on Mijares next to Casa Natalia. Visit this small shop for original designs, where 2 local designers create ‘one-of-a-kind’ exclusive pieces using silver and semiprecious stones. The talented duo even creates specially ‘made to order’ garments within 24 hours. Sax is open Monday through Saturday from 10 a.m. to 9 p.m. and is closed on Sundays.

**Los Amigos Smoke Shop **

For the ones who can’t live without a puff, the Cuban/Veracruz cigars and cigarettes at Los Amigos, situated at Calle Hidalgo 11, are a rage. Apart from avant-garde cigars, the store also stocks a wide range of accessories like humidors and slick cutters.

**Galeria Veryka
**

Fine art is available at Veryka located Blvd. Mijares 418, near Damiana restaurant, which is associated with 2 of Mexico’s finest art centers, San Miguel de Allende and Oaxaca. Prices here are high, but the masks, tapestries, pottery, and embroidered blouses are coveted by collectors from all over the world.
