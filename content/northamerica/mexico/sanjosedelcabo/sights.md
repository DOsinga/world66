Christina Tilicki

In** San Jose del Cabo, sights **to see are countless. There are dozens of things to do in this neighboring city of Cabo San Lucas and the variety of activities is amazing. Whether you're seeking a scenic getaway, a day visiting museums, shopping at the hottest boutiques or just relaxing on the beach, there is something to please every traveler in San Jose del Cabo.

**Estero Beach

**Situated at the east end of another popular beach, Hotel Beach, is the popular estuary known as Estero Beach. A stunning display of a lush, tropical paradise, Estero Beach is a freshwater lagoon that is fed by underground springs. This lagoon is home to over three hundred and fifty species of wildlife and as it is a protected area, the untouched beauty is something you will definitely want to check out. If you want to do more than admire the scenery from the beach, you can rent ATVs, horses or mountain bikes and take the trails that border the estuary or rent a kayak to explore the lagoon from the water.

**Shopping

**A vacation isn't complete without doing a little shopping to stock up on souvenirs for yourself, family and friends. For local art and handicrafts, make sure to stop by the Central Plaza Market. This market offers authentic Mexican gifts such as jewelry, pottery, clothing, art and much more. A stop by this market will be a one-stop shop for you allowing more time to explore the sights.

**Cabo Museums

**If history is your thing, you've come to the right place. San Jose del Cabo is home to multiple museums including the El Chopo Museum and the National Museum of Anthropology. El Chopo offers visitors an art gallery full of native, historic art and modern pieces as well as a theater built for singing, dramatic and dancing exhibitions. Being one of the most popular museums in Mexico, The National Museum of Anthropology is definitely a place you will want to visit.

**The Baja Peninsula

**A trip to this city is a waste unless you tour the Baja Peninsula. Depending on your time frame and budget, there are a number of tours available that will accommodate any needs you may have. The most popular tours of this region are by boat (charter boats, kayaks or speed boats) or by SUVs/buses that take you on an exciting desert tour. Whichever your preference, a tour of the peninsula is worth the time.
