San Juan del Río is a colonial city of about 240,000 in the state of Querétaro, sitting in a fertile valley about an hour southeast of the state capital. It is one of Mexico's centers for opal mining and gem cutting — the region produces beautiful fire opals in shades of orange and red, and you can visit workshops and mines in the surrounding hills to watch the stones being extracted and polished. The prices are significantly better here than in tourist-oriented shops elsewhere in Mexico.

The city center has a handsome collection of colonial architecture around the main plaza, including the Templo del Sacro Corazón and several well-preserved eighteenth-century buildings. San Juan del Río has a growing wine and cheese industry thanks to its temperate highland climate, and several vineyards in the area welcome visitors for tastings. The town makes a pleasant half-day stop on the road between Mexico City and Querétaro, or a base for exploring the Sierra Gorda Biosphere Reserve to the northeast. The Sunday market is lively, with local produce, opals, and leather goods.
