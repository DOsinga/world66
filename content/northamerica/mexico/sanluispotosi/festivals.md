title: "Festivals in San Luis Potosi"
type: section
---

**San Luis Potosi festivals** celebrate the culture and history of the city. San Luis Potosi is located in the south central part of Mexico and has the unique distinction of being the former capital of the country.

**Easter Week Festival **

The Europeans in Mexico were missionaries who founded the modern cities. The original mission of San Luis Potosi was founded in 1583. Religious traditions continue to be important to this area, especially during Easter, the most important holiday in Mexico. During Easter week, also known as Semana Santa, there is a festival to celebrate the resurrection of Christ. Sponsored by local churches, the week is spent celebrating the events of the resurrection with plays, music and food. One of the more popular activities is a sand building competition on the local beaches. The entries have a religious theme to them. Past winners have included renditions of the Last Supper. All are welcome, even non-Christians.

**Feria ****Nacional**** ****Potosina**

Taking place during the last 3 weeks of the August, this festival celebrates the national industries in the country. Art, music, cultural expositions, livestock and other industries are represented here. There are a number of workshops available for people who want brush up on their skills. For business travelers, this is a good way to brush up on skills for a small amount, as admission costs 10 pesos. For leisure travelers, this a good way to immerse yourself in the culture of San Luis Potosi, as the festival holds a number of events celebrating the history of the city.
