---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

San Luis Potosí's colonial centre is the main draw. The Plaza de Armas is anchored by the 18th-century cathedral and the Governor's Palace; the nearby Templo de Nuestra Señora del Carmen, with its ornate Churrigueresque facade, is one of the most flamboyant baroque churches in Mexico. The Teatro de la Paz hosts theatre and music performances. The Museo Nacional de la Máscara on Villerías Street occupies a handsome 19th-century building and holds one of the finest collections of ceremonial masks in the country — an underrated museum. The Mercado Hidalgo is the place to find Santa María rebozos (the shawls so finely woven they can pass through a wedding ring), pottery, and queso de tuna, a candy made from prickly pear cactus fruit.
