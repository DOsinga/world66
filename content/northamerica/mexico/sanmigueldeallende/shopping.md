---
title: "Shopping in San Miguel de Allende"
type: section
---

**San Miguel de Allende Shopping** will give you a peek into the art filled life of Mexico. This is a Mexican city known for its art and its artists. Read on to find out about the key shopping areas in San Miguel de Allende.

**Fabric Aurora**

A 10-minute walk from the Main Square will take you to the Fabric Aurora store. Mexican art has a large fan following all around the world. This store brings art under one roof. The store was once a textile factory, but today offers the visitors a true Mexican experience. There are several stores that sell antique and sandstone items. You can browse through the various art galleries and buy paintings and sculptures created by Mexican artists. You can also buy linens and other home d&eacute;cor items. The working hours of the stores in this plaza are from 10:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.

**The Artisans Market**

This market is spread over three blocks and can be entered from Loreto, Hidalgo or Reloj. The market gives artists from all over Mexico the opportunity to showcase their creations. This is a unique tourist attraction and shopping area. The visitors can browse through the various stalls selling antiques, handmade jewelry, postcards, and more. Do buy the silver jewelry sold here, as you will find the best designs at reasonable rates. The working hours are between 10:00 A.M. to 6:00 P.M, and the stalls take a one-hour break for lunch at 2:00 p.m.

**Talavera Pottery
**
To witness the making of a product that requires a highly skilled pair of hands, enter the Talavera Pottery market. You can witness the pots and other items being made from scratch at this market. You can buy from the various stalls that are set up here. Set out on a 10-minute walk from the bus station and discover a world of ceramic pottery. From crockery to home d&eacute;cor, you will find all items of interest here.

**La Placita**

La Placita is an open-air market located adjacent to the Plaza Real. Business here starts early. It is advisable to set out early to avoid the crowd, and the weather will also be the best during this time. You can find stalls of every kind here, selling food, furniture, drapes, linens, and other items. The food stalls here offer true Mexican treats to the visitors. You can also buy jeans and fashion accessories like bags, hats, and jewelry.

**Gigiante**
If you wish to experience a Mexican Mall, then Gigiante is the place for you. This mall has a mini supermarket where you can buy everyday items. The hobby store has several interesting items. The mall also has a movie theater and a food court. The mall is well maintained and air conditioned. There are a couple of stores selling stained glass plates and similar items.
