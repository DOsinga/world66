Sayulita is a small surf town on Mexico's Pacific coast, about 40 kilometers north of Puerto Vallarta. It has the kind of laid-back character that draws people in for a day and keeps them for a week: colorful buildings, a long sandy beach, reliable surf, good tacos, and a town square where the main activity is sitting and doing nothing in particular.

It isn't undiscovered — the cobblestone streets fill up on weekends and the high season brings crowds — but outside peak periods Sayulita remains genuinely unhurried. It works well as a base for exploring the Riviera Nayarit coast, or simply as a few days' escape from the noise of Puerto Vallarta.

The surf break here is consistent and beginner-friendly, which makes Sayulita a good place to take a first lesson. More experienced surfers will want to head to Playa de los Muertos or La Lancha nearby for less-crowded waves.
