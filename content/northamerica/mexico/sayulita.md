Sayulita is a small surf and fishing village on the Riviera Nayarit coast of Mexico, about 40 km north of [Puerto Vallarta](/northamerica/mexico/puertovallarta). It has a reputation as one of Mexico's most enjoyable beach escapes — relaxed, colourful, and genuinely welcoming — and has been popular with surfers since the 1970s.

The main beach has consistent, beginner-friendly waves, and surfboards and lessons are available for rent from several outfits along the beach. The cobblestone streets of the village hold a good mix of small restaurants, boutiques, and cafes. The town fills up considerably in high season (December to April) but never feels as overwhelming as the larger Riviera Nayarit resorts.

Getting there from Puerto Vallarta: take MEX 200 north for about 40 km, then turn off at the signed Sayulita junction and follow the road 3 km down to the plaza.

Sayulita is a small surf town on Mexico's Pacific coast, about an hour north of [Puerto Vallarta](/northamerica/mexico/puertovallarta). What was once a quiet fishing village has become a popular destination for surfers, backpackers, and weekending Mexicans, but it has kept its laid-back small-town feel. The main beach break is ideal for beginners and longboarders, with consistent waves year-round.

The town centre is a compact grid of cobblestone streets with colourful buildings, taco stands, surf shops, and a good scattering of restaurants. The vibe is casual and friendly — flip-flops and sandy feet are the dress code. Beyond the main beach, quieter stretches of sand can be found in either direction along the coast.
