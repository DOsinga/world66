Taxco clings to a steep hillside in the Sierra Madre del Sur, about three hours south of Mexico City. Its narrow cobblestone streets wind past whitewashed buildings with terracotta roofs, all of it cascading down toward the ornate church of Santa Prisca in the central plaza. The town has been producing silver since the colonial era and still calls itself the Silver Capital of Mexico, a claim backed by the hundreds of silver shops lining every street.

The setting is dramatic. Taxco sits at around 1,750 meters, with forested mountains rising on all sides. The climate is mild year-round, warm during the day and cool at night. The town is compact and walkable, though "walkable" here means steep. Comfortable shoes are not optional.

Beyond the silver, Taxco rewards wandering. The Grutas de Cacahuamilpa, a vast cave system with cathedral-sized chambers, lies just outside town. Waterfalls and natural springs dot the surrounding hills, accessible by hiking trails. The town itself is one of Mexico's designated Pueblos Magicos, and it earns the title. On a quiet morning, before the day-trippers arrive from Mexico City, the views from the upper streets across the tiled rooftops to the mountains beyond are worth the climb.
