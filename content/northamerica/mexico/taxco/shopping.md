title: "Shopping in Taxco"
type: section
---

Known as the Silver Capital of Mexico, Taxco is the ideal shopping center for those
looking to send home inexpensive, authentic jewelry, turquoise, silver, and old
stones. You can also purchase sterling silver coffee tables and mirrors worth
thousands of dollars at a moderate, but fair price. Set high in the Guerrero mountains between Mexico City and Acapulco,
you will surely find the perfect gift for everyone back at home.

**Research Types of Silver**

Before you go shopping in one of Taxco’s hundreds of silver shops, you should
do a little research on the different types of silver so you’re not
scammed. Sterling silver is most often
stamped with the number 922 or 925, meaning that 92.2% (or 92.5%) of the
produce is real silver. Anything stamped with 100 is false, as no jewelry can be
crafted out of 100% silver – it’s too fine and soft to hold up. Stay away from
jewelry stamped with the phrase “Alpaca”, as this means “nickel silver” or
“German silver”, which actually contains zero silver. Your best bet is to
purchase only silver bearing a stamp with the number 922 or 925.

**Remember to Barter**

The shops usually feature price tags on the jewelry but you
can still barter, especially if you have cash. Some of the most well-known,
reputable, and genuine silver shops include Alicia Plata’s Family Silver Shop, Comercializadora,
Doris Day Designs, and Plata y Más.

**Alicia Plata's Family Silver Shop**

Alicia Plata’s Family Silver Shop, in business since 1950,
is a professional, family-owned shop featuring modern, traditional, and
contemporary silver designs. The family prides itself on keeping in touch with
all its buyers, including those overseas. You’ll be added to the annual catalog
and email list. Alicia’s is unique in
that the jewelry designs are updated monthly, so no two people will purchase
the exact same design!

**Comercializadora**

Comercializadora, in business for over 15 years, employs
more than 120 silversmiths, and displays over a hundred different types of
silver jewelry, ranging from necklaces and bracelets to paperweights and silver
studded picture frames. In store minimum purchase is $500 USD, but the website
only requires a minimum purchase of $200.

**Doris Day Designs**

Doris Day Designs, also located in the center of Taxco shopping, employs jewelry designers from the US and Europe.
Featuring over 1,000 pieces of silver jewelry, Doris Day Designs will make your
shopping experience hassle-free and a pleasure, so you’ll want to visit yearly.

**Plata y Mas**

Finally, Plata y Mas features more than just silver, with
stone beads, spacers, Murano glass, and Sworovski and Chinese crystal. The kids
will also enjoy making their own beaded necklaces using little silver, glass,
and paper beads. All silver guaranteed
925.

**Pottery Shops**

Tired of silver shopping? Check out one of Taxco’s
many pottery shops, located in the center market place. Prices are reasonable –
often cheaper than the silver. San Jose’s
Pottery and Tile, one of Taxco’s
best, is located next to the huge cathedral in the center of town.
