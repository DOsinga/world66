---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The dry season from December to April is peak time, with clear skies, calm seas, and the best visibility for diving and snorkelling. December and January bring the most tourists and the highest prices — book accommodation well in advance if you're visiting over the Christmas and New Year period. February and March offer the same good weather with slightly smaller crowds.

May through July is an excellent shoulder period: prices drop, the crowds thin, and the weather remains mostly fine. Rain becomes more frequent from June onward as hurricane season approaches. August through October is the heart of the rainy season and hurricane season; short but intense daily downpours are typical, and a direct storm is always possible, though Tulum has been largely spared in recent years.

November marks the transition back to the dry season — storms become rare, prices are still off-peak, and the jungle is at its most lush and green.
