Uruapan sits in the lush highlands of Michoacán and proudly calls itself the avocado capital of the world — a title that is hard to argue with, given that the surrounding region produces a staggering share of Mexico's avocado crop. The city itself, home to about 300,000 people, is a pleasant surprise: the Parque Nacional Barranca del Cupatitzio, right at the edge of town, is a beautiful riverside park where the Cupatitzio River springs from the earth through mossy rocks and tropical vegetation. It is one of the loveliest urban parks in all of Mexico.

The real draw for many visitors, though, is the journey to Paricutín, the volcano that famously erupted in a farmer's cornfield in 1943 and buried the village of San Juan Parangaricutiro. You can still see the church tower poking through the hardened lava field — a haunting and unforgettable sight. Getting there requires a trip to the village of Angahuan and then a hike or horseback ride across the lava flows. Back in town, the Mercado de Antojitos is excellent for local food, and the lacquerware crafts sold in the market have been a Uruapan tradition for centuries.
