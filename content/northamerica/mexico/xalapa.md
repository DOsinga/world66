Xalapa (also spelled Jalapa) is the capital of the state of Veracruz, sitting in the cloud forest zone of the eastern Sierra Madre at around 1,400 metres elevation. The city has a reputation as one of Mexico's most liveable and culturally lively provincial capitals — it's a university city with a strong intellectual and artistic scene, and the coffee grown in the surrounding hills is among Mexico's finest. The climate is famously mild and often misty, earning the city the nickname "City of Flowers."

The unmissable attraction is the Museo de Antropología de Xalapa, one of the great archaeological museums of Mexico and the world. It holds the largest collection of Olmec sculptures anywhere, including several of the colossal basalt heads — some weighing up to 25 tonnes — that are among the most iconic artefacts of pre-Columbian America. The Gulf Coast cultures on display here are far less visited than the Aztec and Maya collections elsewhere in Mexico, which makes the museum feel like a discovery.

The city centre is attractive, with good cafes and restaurants along the Callejón del Diamante and a lively cultural programme at the Teatro del Estado. Xalapa is about 90 minutes by bus from Veracruz city, and makes an excellent base for visiting the archaeological site of El Tajín (also in Veracruz state) and the colonial town of Coatepec.
