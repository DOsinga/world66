title: "Sights in Chichen Itza"
type: section
---

The Yucatan peninsula is home to hundreds of cenotes - underground bodies of fresh water that are accessible to tourists. The region has no rivers or lakes, but by climbing through openings in the earth down into the caverns below, you will find pools of beautiful clear water. The cenote itself is a water-filled limestone sinkhole below the ground.

Cenote Ik Kil is very close to Chichen Itza, and is an excellent stop that can be made in the same day as a visit to the pyramids. Ik Kil features the spectacular http://www.wohlmut.com/Maya/blue_cenote.htm Blue Cenote, 82 feet below ground. Divers leap from the cliffs through the lush vegetation and waterfalls into the pool below.
