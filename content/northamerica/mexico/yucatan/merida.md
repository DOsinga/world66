Merida is the capital of [Yucatan](/northamerica/mexico/yucatan) state and the cultural hub of the entire peninsula. Founded in 1542 by the Spanish conquistador Francisco de Montejo on the ruins of the Maya city of T’ho, it is a handsome colonial city of low-rise limestone buildings, shaded plazas, and streets that come alive in the evening.

The city revolves around the **Plaza Grande**, where the cathedral — the oldest on mainland America, built in 1598 from stones of the Maya temples it replaced — faces the 16th-century **Casa de Montejo** and the **Palacio de Gobierno** with its vivid murals of Yucatecan history by Fernando Castro Pacheco. Streets radiate outward in a numbered grid: odd numbers run east-west, even numbers north-south.

Merida is a city that rewards walking. The **Paseo de Montejo**, a broad boulevard lined with grand mansions from the henequen boom era, leads north from the centre. The **Central Market** hums with Maya and Spanish spoken in equal measure, and on Sundays the parks fill with music, artisans, and food vendors. The city is also the natural base for visiting [Uxmal](/northamerica/mexico/yucatan/uxmal) and [Chichen Itza](/northamerica/mexico/yucatan/chichenitza), both within day-trip range.
