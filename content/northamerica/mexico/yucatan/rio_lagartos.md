Rio Lagartos is a small fishing village on the north coast of the Yucatan Peninsula, best known for the flamingo colony in the surrounding biosphere reserve. Thousands of Caribbean flamingos breed in the shallow lagoons and salt flats here — boat trips to see them are the main reason visitors come. The reserve also supports over 300 bird species and extensive mangrove habitat.

The village itself is quiet and unpretentious — a handful of streets, simple seafood restaurants, and a waterfront where fishing boats come and go. It makes a good base for boat tours into the lagoon system, including crocodile-spotting trips at night and visits to the pink salt pans at Las Coloradas nearby. The fishing is excellent — tarpon, snook, and jacks in the protected lagoon waters.

Rio Lagartos is about two hours from Merida and 30 minutes from Tizimin. Chichen Itza and other Yucatan ruins are within a couple of hours' drive.
