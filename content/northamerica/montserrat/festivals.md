---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

**Montserrat Festival** (late December). The biggest event on the island's calendar, held between Christmas and New Year. This is a full Caribbean carnival: calypso competitions, costumed parades, live music, and dancing in the streets of Brades and Little Bay. The Montserratian diaspora returns in large numbers, the population temporarily doubles, and the atmosphere is genuinely festive. Accommodation is scarce and expensive during this period — book months ahead.

**St Patrick's Day** (March 17, observed as a week-long festival). Montserrat is the only place outside Ireland where St Patrick's Day is a public holiday, but the local significance is different. The celebration commemorates a failed uprising by enslaved Africans on 17 March 1768, who planned to revolt against the plantation owners during the St Patrick's Day festivities. The week includes cultural events, historical tours, and music. It is one of the more historically interesting festivals in the Caribbean.

**Calabash Festival** (May). A newer cultural festival celebrating arts, music, and Montserratian heritage. Programming varies year to year.

**Jumbie Fest** (October). A small Halloween-themed event drawing on Caribbean folklore and the island's tradition of jumbie (spirit) stories. More local in character than the December festival.
