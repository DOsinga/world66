---
title: "Food"
type: section
---

Montserrat's food is Caribbean home cooking, honest and unfussy. The national dish is goat water — a thick, slow-cooked stew of goat meat with cloves, mace, breadfruit, and root vegetables. You will find it at local rum shops and informal restaurants, often on weekends.

Seafood is good: freshly caught mahi-mahi, lobster, and snapper appear on most menus. Chicken and fish roti — flatbread wraps with spiced filling — are popular for a quick lunch. Jerk seasoning features widely. The local hot sauce, made from Scotch bonnet peppers, is not mild.

Most restaurants are small, informal, and only open during the day. A handful of more upscale places operate in the evenings, mostly connected to villas and small hotels. If you are staying in self-catering accommodation, there are supermarkets in Brades.

The drink to try is Cavalier rum, produced on the island. Local rum punch is served everywhere.
