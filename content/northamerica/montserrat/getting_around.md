---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The island is small — the accessible northern zone is only a few kilometres across — but the roads are steep and winding, so distances take longer than they look on a map.

**Minibuses** run the main route from Salem in the south of the safe zone up through Brades (the administrative capital), St John's, and north to Lookout. These are privately run 15-seater vans, individually painted, with no timetables or fixed stops. Flag one down when you see it passing. The fare is around EC$1.50 (about US$0.55) between any two points on the route. Drivers will make short detours off the main road for a negotiated fee, if the other passengers agree.

**Taxis** are available at the airport and ferry terminal. Your accommodation can arrange them in advance. Agree on the fare before getting in — there are no meters.

**Car rental** is the most flexible option for exploring the island on your own schedule. No major international chains operate here; a handful of local agencies rent cars. You will need a local visitor's driving permit (US$18 / EC$50), obtainable online at drivers.gov.ms, at the Brades Police Station, or at the airport. A valid licence from your home country is sufficient to get one. Drive on the left, and take the mountain roads carefully.

Guided tours are the only way to enter the exclusion zone and see Plymouth. Several local operators run volcano and island tours; ask at your accommodation or the tourism office in Little Bay.
