---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Montserrat is not easy to reach, which is part of why it remains so quiet. The main hub is Antigua.

**By air.** John A. Osborne Airport (formerly Gerald's Airport) in the north of the island handles small aircraft only. Fly Montserrat and SVG Air/BM Airways both run daily services from Antigua, a 20-minute hop in a small twin-propeller plane. Seats fill up quickly in December; book ahead. Occasional charter flights operate from St Maarten. The old Blackburne Airport in the south was destroyed by the volcano and is not in use.

**By ferry.** A seasonal ferry service operates between Antigua and Little Bay on Montserrat, taking about 90 minutes. The service has historically run during the December festival season and has faced repeated delays in recent years — check current schedules at ferry.mniaccess.com or call the Access Division at +1 664 491-3378 before making plans that depend on it.

There is no direct service from anywhere other than Antigua and occasionally St Maarten. Most visitors fly into Antigua's V.C. Bird International Airport and connect from there.
