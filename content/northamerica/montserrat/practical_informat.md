---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency.** The Eastern Caribbean dollar (XCD / EC$) is the official currency. The exchange rate is fixed at EC$2.70 to US$1. US dollars are widely accepted, but you will often get better value paying in EC dollars. ATMs are available in Brades. Credit cards are accepted at hotels and some restaurants; carry cash for taxis, minibuses, and smaller places.

**Entry.** Montserrat is a British Overseas Territory. Nationals of the UK, USA, Canada, and most EU countries do not need a visa for visits up to six months. A valid passport is required. You must have proof of onward travel and sufficient funds. An embarkation tax of EC$45 (EC$25 for residents) is charged on departure.

**Language.** English is the official language. Locals speak with a distinctive lilt that carries faint traces of Irish intonation — a legacy of the island's 17th-century settlers.

**Health.** No vaccinations are required unless you are arriving from a yellow fever transmission zone. The Glendon Hospital in St John's provides general medical care. Travel insurance that covers medical evacuation is strongly recommended given the limited facilities.

**Telephone and internet.** The international dialling code is +1 664. Mobile coverage is reasonable in the inhabited north of the island. Wi-Fi is available at most accommodation and some restaurants.

**Safety.** Montserrat has one of the lowest crime rates in the Caribbean. The main hazard is the volcano — stay within the permitted zones and follow the guidance of the Montserrat Volcano Observatory. Current zone status is published at mvo.ms.

**Tourism office.** The Montserrat Tourism Authority has an office in Little Bay. The website visitmontserrat.com is a useful starting point.
