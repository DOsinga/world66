---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The dry season runs from December through May. Temperatures stay between 25°C and 28°C year-round, moderated by trade winds, but the dry months bring lower humidity and less rain. This is the most comfortable time to hike, explore the exclusion zone, and spend time outdoors.

December is the busiest month by far. The annual Festival — a week-long carnival-style celebration of music, dance, and costume parades — draws the Montserratian diaspora home and fills the island's limited accommodation. Book flights and rooms well in advance if you plan to come in December.

St Patrick's Day (March 17) is a public holiday and a week-long event. It commemorates not the Irish saint but the 1768 uprising of enslaved Africans who rose against the plantation owners — an unusual and historically important occasion that blends African and Irish cultural threads.

The wet season runs from June through November and coincides with hurricane season. Rain tends to fall in sharp bursts rather than sustained downpours, and the island stays green and warm. Prices drop noticeably. The risk of hurricanes is real — Montserrat is more exposed than some neighbouring islands — but most years pass without serious disruption.

February through April offers the best combination of good weather, reasonable prices, and fewer crowds.
