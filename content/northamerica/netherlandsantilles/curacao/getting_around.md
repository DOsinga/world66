---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Most visitors rent a car — you will need one to reach the best beaches and dive sites, which are spread along the south coast. Drive on the right. Roads are generally good but signage can be patchy, so get a detailed map. Watch for goats on rural roads.

Within Willemstad, the yellow public buses (konvoois) are reliable and cheap, running from the waterfront in Punda across to Otrabanda. Minibuses follow less predictable routes but cover most of the island — look for "BUS" on the licence plate. Taxis are plentiful in the capital with set city fares, and can be hired for island tours at negotiated rates.

Hato International Airport is about 12 kilometres northwest of Willemstad, with rental car agencies, taxis, and buses available on arrival.
