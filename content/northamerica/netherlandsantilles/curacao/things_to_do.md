---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Diving and snorkelling are Curacao's main draw. The sheltered south coast has dozens of shore-dive sites with easy access to healthy coral reefs — no boat needed, just walk in from the beach. The Curacao Sea Aquarium complex on the southeast coast is a popular starting point, with dive operators, a dolphin encounter, and an underwater observatory.

Beyond the water, Willemstad's historic centre rewards a morning of wandering — the floating market where Venezuelan traders sell fruit from their boats, the Mikve Israel-Emanuel Synagogue (the oldest in continuous use in the Americas), and the narrow streets of Pietermaai with their restored colonial houses and open-air bars. The Hato Caves in the north of the island have impressive stalactites and a resident colony of long-nosed bats.
