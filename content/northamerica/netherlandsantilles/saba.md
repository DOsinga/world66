Saba is the Caribbean at its most improbable — a single volcanic peak rising straight out of the sea, five square miles of near-vertical terrain, no beaches to speak of, and a permanent population of about 2,000. If you came looking for a resort island, you have landed on the wrong rock. If you came to hike through cloud forest, dive pristine reefs, and stay in a place where everybody knows your name by the second day, Saba is perfect.

The island is a special municipality of the Netherlands, lying 28 miles south of Sint Maarten. Four tiny villages cling to the slopes: The Bottom (the capital, despite being in a crater), Windwardside (the main tourist settlement), St. John's, and Hell's Gate. A single road — called, without exaggeration, "The Road" — connects them all, threading through hairpin turns cut into the mountainside.

The big draw on land is Mount Scenery, at 887 metres the highest point in the entire Kingdom of the Netherlands. A well-maintained trail climbs from Windwardside through elfin forest draped in moss and bromeliads to the summit — allow two hours up and plan to arrive before clouds close in around midday. Underwater, the Saba Marine Park protects some of the best diving in the Caribbean: pinnacles, walls, and lava formations teeming with sharks, turtles, and huge barrel sponges.
