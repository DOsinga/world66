St Eustatius — known locally as Statia — is a small Dutch Caribbean island of about 21 square kilometres, lying between St Kitts and Saba in the northern Leeward Islands. It is one of the least-visited islands in the Caribbean, which is much of its appeal.

The island was once known as the "Golden Rock" for its importance as a free-trade port in the 18th century. At its peak, thousands of ships a year called here. The Americans consider the island historically significant because it was the first foreign territory to salute the American flag — in 1776, when the Andrew Doria arrived flying the new Stars and Stripes. Little remains of this mercantile glory: the town of Oranjestad consists of an upper and lower section connected by a long staircase, with ruins of old Dutch warehouses along the waterfront.

The dormant volcano, The Quill, rises to 600 metres in the south of the island and contains a lush rainforest crater. Hiking to the rim and down into it takes a morning. The diving around the island — particularly around the old ruins and anchors on the seabed — is excellent and sees very few divers.
