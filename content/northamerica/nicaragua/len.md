León is Nicaragua's intellectual and revolutionary heart, a university city of grand colonial churches, political murals, and a fierce local pride that distinguishes it from rival [Granada](/northamerica/nicaragua/granada) to the south. It was the country's capital until the 1850s, and the centro histórico still feels like it — wide streets, heavy-walled colonial houses, and more churches per block than anywhere else in Central America.

The Catedral de la Asunción, the largest cathedral in Central America, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. You can climb onto its whitewashed roof for views across the city to the volcanoes beyond. The Museo de la Revolución, staffed by former Sandinista fighters, tells the story of the 1979 revolution with an intensity you will not find in a textbook.

Outside town, Cerro Negro — a jet-black active volcano — has become famous for volcano boarding, where you sit on a plywood board and slide down the steep ash slope. It is exactly as ridiculous and fun as it sounds. Telica volcano, further north, offers more serious hiking with views into a glowing crater at night. The Pacific beaches at Poneloya and Las Peñitas are 20 minutes west, with surf breaks, simple comedores, and the Juan Venado Island nature reserve just offshore.
