Masachapa is a small fishing village on [Nicaragua's](/northamerica/nicaragua) Pacific coast, about an hour's drive from [Managua](/northamerica/nicaragua/managua). It is one of the closest beaches to the capital and gets busy on weekends with Managuan families, but during the week it is quiet and unhurried. The beach is a long, dark-sand stretch with decent surf and strong currents — swim with caution.

The village itself is unpretentious and working-class. The main reason to come is the seafood: small comedores along the beachfront serve fresh fish, shrimp, and lobster at prices that would be impossible anywhere else in the region. Fried whole fish with tostones and a cold Toña beer, eaten at a plastic table with your feet in the sand, is the quintessential Masachapa experience.

Neighbouring Pochomil, just a short walk south along the beach, has slightly more developed facilities and a few basic places to stay. Together the two villages make a straightforward day trip from Managua if you want sun, surf, and cheap seafood without the long drive to San Juan del Sur.
