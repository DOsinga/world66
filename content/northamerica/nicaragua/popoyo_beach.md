Popoyo is Nicaragua's best-known surf break, a powerful reef break on the southwestern Pacific coast that draws surfers from across Central America. The wave is consistent, with offshore winds most mornings during the dry season (November to April), and it works at all tides though low tide produces the heaviest barrels.

The area around Popoyo is still fairly undeveloped — a scattering of surf lodges, small restaurants, and rental houses spread along a dusty road behind the coast. That is part of the appeal: no high-rises, no crowds outside the peak surf season, and a pace of life that revolves around the tides. Beyond the main break there are several other spots within walking or short driving distance, including Playa Sardinas and the beach break at Guasacate, which is better for beginners.

Popoyo is about two hours south of Managua on paved roads, or roughly an hour and a half from the colonial city of [Granada](/northamerica/nicaragua/granada). Most visitors base themselves here for the surfing, but the nearby town of San Juan del Sur — about an hour south along the coast — offers more dining and nightlife options.
