The site of the ill-fated Scottish colony of New Caledonia, established in 1698 at Caledonia Bay on the Caribbean coast. The colonists — thousands of them — died of disease, starvation, and conflict with Spanish forces; the colony was abandoned within two years and the disaster nearly bankrupted Scotland. Ruins of Fort St. Andrew survive, and a shipwreck in the bay can be seen by snorkellers. The bay is accessible only by boat or plane.
