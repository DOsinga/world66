The only interruption in the Pan-American Highway, which otherwise runs from Alaska to the tip of South America. The road ends at Yaviza in Panama; on the Colombian side it resumes at Lomas Aisladas. In between is roughly 100 km of jungle, swamp, and mountain that has resisted every attempt to bridge it — partly due to cost, partly to prevent the spread of foot-and-mouth disease from South America. Yaviza is the literal end of the road and worth seeing for that alone.
