Los Santos is a province on the Azuero Peninsula in southern Panama, the heartland of Panamanian rural culture. This is where the country's traditions run deepest — the pollera dress, the sombrero pintao, and the folk dances that define the national identity all originate here. If you visit during Carnival, the celebrations in Las Tablas and other Azuero towns are among the wildest and most genuine in Central America.

Outside festival time, Los Santos is a quiet, agricultural region of dry grasslands, cattle ranches, and small colonial towns. The coastline along the Pacific has empty beaches and decent surf, particularly around Pedasí and Playa Venao. Isla Iguana, a short boat ride from Pedasí, has pristine coral reefs and a large frigatebird colony. The region sees far fewer tourists than Panama City or Bocas del Toro, which is part of its appeal.
