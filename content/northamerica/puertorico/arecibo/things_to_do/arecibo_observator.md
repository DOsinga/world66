The Arecibo Observatory was the world's largest single-dish radio telescope for over fifty years, its 305-metre reflector nestled into a natural sinkhole in the karst hills south of Arecibo. It featured in the film GoldenEye and was used in the search for extraterrestrial intelligence. The dish collapsed in December 2020 after cable failures, and the telescope is no longer operational. The visitor centre and science museum remain open, with exhibits on radio astronomy, atmospheric science, and the history of the facility.
