Cabo Rojo sits at the southwestern tip of Puerto Rico, where the island tapers into a jagged coastline of salt flats, mangroves, and wind-scoured cliffs. It is one of the less-touristed corners of the island — quieter than the east coast resorts, and all the better for it.

The old lighthouse at the very tip of the peninsula, Faro Los Morrillos, is the main draw: a sturdy 19th-century structure perched above dramatic sea cliffs with sweeping views over the Caribbean. The surrounding Cabo Rojo National Wildlife Refuge protects a stretch of coastal habitat that draws serious birders. The refuge has a visitors centre with interpretive displays and walking trails through the salt flats.

Cabo Rojo is about 30 km south of [Mayaguez](/northamerica/puertorico/mayaguez).
