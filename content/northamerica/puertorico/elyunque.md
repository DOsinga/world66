El Yunque National Forest is Puerto Rico's most popular natural attraction, and the only tropical rainforest in the United States National Forest system. The forest covers the slopes of the Luquillo Mountains in the island's northeastern corner, rising to about 1,065 metres. The Taíno people who lived here before European contact considered the mountain sacred.

The **El Portal Visitors Center** near the park entrance has good exhibits on the rainforest ecosystem, including a large light-up map showing how the forest supplies water to surrounding communities. Several hiking trails lead into the forest, reaching waterfalls that are especially rewarding during or just after the rainy season. The trails are generally well-maintained but can be slippery — good shoes are worth wearing.

**Luquillo Beach**, just west of the park, is one of the best-known beaches in Puerto Rico: a long crescent fringed with coconut palms and protected by the surrounding mountains.
