---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Renting a car is by far the best way to see Puerto Rico beyond San Juan. All major international agencies operate at Luis Muñoz Marín Airport, and your home driving license is valid. Drive on the right. One quirk: speed limit signs are in miles per hour while distances are in kilometers. Traffic in and around San Juan can be bad, especially on weekday mornings and evenings.

Within San Juan, taxis and Uber are plentiful and reliable. The Tren Urbano metro line runs through parts of the metropolitan area but has limited coverage.

Shared minivans called *públicos* connect all the main towns on the island. They're cheap and a genuine slice of local life, but they run on no fixed schedule and stop frequently. For shorter hops between nearby towns they work well; for longer journeys across the island you may need several changes. Look for vehicles with a 'P' or 'PD' on the license plate, or find the *público* terminal in any town plaza.

**Getting to Vieques and Culebra**

The government-operated ferry to the offshore islands of Vieques and Culebra departs from Ceiba, about 1.5 hours east of San Juan by car (the old departure point at Fajardo is no longer in use). The crossing takes 30–45 minutes. Ferries run several times daily and fares are very cheap — around $2 each way for passengers. Book tickets in advance at puertoricoferry.com; only 20% of tickets are sold online, so if a date shows as full it's worth going to the terminal anyway. You can take a rental car on the ferry to Culebra, but book well ahead.

Small planes operated by Cape Air and other carriers also fly between San Juan (SJU or SIG) and both islands in around 20 minutes, which avoids the ferry queues and is worth the extra cost if your time is tight.
