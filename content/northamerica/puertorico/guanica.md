Guanica is a small town on the southern coast of [Puerto Rico](/northamerica/puertorico), best known for the Guanica Dry Forest Reserve — a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve with 48 rare tree species and excellent birdwatching. The subtropical dry forest is one of the best-preserved examples in the world and makes for good hiking.

The town is also the site of the first US troop landing during the Spanish-American War in 1898. The coastline around Guanica has some of Puerto Rico's finest beaches. **Playa Caña Gorda** is a calm, family-friendly stretch with clear water and good facilities. **Playa Ballena** nearby has bigger waves and a wilder feel — keep an eye on children in the water. Boat rentals are available for exploring the coast.
