Ponce is Puerto Rico's second city and the island's finest example of a preserved colonial town. The wealth of the sugar era left its mark in the ornate buildings, balconied mansions, and cultural institutions that still define the historic centre. Much of the old city has been restored, painted in pastels of pink and mint green, and it remains far less visited than San Juan.

The heart of town is **Plaza las Delicias**, one of the most handsome squares in the Caribbean, with its Fountain of Lions and the distinctive red-and-black **Parque de Bombas** fire station from 1883 — now a small museum. The **Ponce Art Museum** holds one of the finest art collections in Latin America, including works by Velázquez, Rubens, and Rodin. Serralles Castle, the Spanish Revival mansion of the rum-making family, offers tours and commanding views over the city.

For swimming, La Guancha boardwalk serves as the city's waterfront development, with beach access, outdoor cafes, and a pleasant evening walk along the Caribbean.
