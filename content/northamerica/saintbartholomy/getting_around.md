---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

There is no public transport on St. Barts. No buses, no shared minivans, no Uber. The island is small enough that this isn't a problem if you rent a car.

**Car rental** is the standard approach and the most practical. Several rental companies operate at the airport and in Gustavia, with rates starting around €33–40 per day for a small car. Reserve in advance during high season — availability genuinely runs out. The roads are narrow and hilly, with tight switchbacks and limited signage. Driving is on the right. Many of the best beaches require driving up and over ridges on single-lane tracks. A small car handles these better than a large one.

**Scooters and mini-mokes** (open-sided buggies) are popular alternatives. They're fun, cheaper than cars, and well-suited to the terrain and climate, but leave you exposed on the narrow roads. Helmets are required for scooters.

**Taxis** exist but are limited in number and expensive even by island standards. Two taxi stations: one at the airport and one at the Gustavia dock. Pre-booking a day in advance is advisable if you need one at a specific time, especially for airport transfers at odd hours.

The island is 25 square kilometres in total. Driving from Gustavia to the far end of the island takes about 15 minutes. Once you're oriented, the scale is manageable.
