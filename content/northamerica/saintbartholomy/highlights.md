---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**The beaches.** Twenty-two of them, each with a different character. Anse des Flamands is the longest and least developed, a broad sweep of sand backed by low hills with no beach bar in sight. Gouverneur is reached by a steep road and rewards with seclusion and clear water. Saline requires a short walk over a salt pond and is clothing-optional by convention. St. Jean, near the airport, has a reef and a cluster of beach bars — more social, still beautiful. Pick your mood.

**Gustavia harbour.** The capital is small enough to walk in twenty minutes, but it earns time. The Swedish-era stone warehouses along the waterfront have been converted into boutiques and restaurants; the colour scheme is pastel and the yachts are genuinely impressive. Fort Karl and Fort Oscar sit above the harbour. The Wall House museum covers the island's Swedish period in modest but worthwhile detail.

**The food.** More than 80 restaurants for a population under 11,000 — the ratio is absurd, and the quality reflects it. Young chefs trained in France come here to cook with Caribbean ingredients: local lobster, conch in fricassée, fresh tuna, flying fish. The style tends French with Creole notes. Even beach shacks do it well.

**New Year's.** The island fills beyond capacity every December 31st. Superyachts pack the harbour, fireworks go up at midnight over Fort Oscar, and every foghorn on every boat sounds simultaneously. It is one of the Caribbean's great annual spectacles, as long as you booked ahead and have a place to be.

**Diving and water sports.** The reefs around St. Barts support good snorkelling and scuba, with sites including Pain de Sucre off Gustavia and the wreck of the Frédéric. Grand Cul de Sac lagoon on the northeast coast is the best spot for windsurfing and kiteboarding — the shallow water and consistent trades make it ideal for beginners and experienced riders alike.

**The sailing regattas.** April brings the Bucket Regatta and Les Voiles de Saint-Barth (now Les Voiles by Richard Mille), two of the Caribbean's prestige sailing events. The trade winds are at their reliable best, the racing is serious, and the post-race scene in Gustavia is festive.
