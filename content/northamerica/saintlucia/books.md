title: "Books"

---

Derek Walcott, Saint Lucia's Nobel Prize-winning poet, is essential reading before or after visiting the island. His epic poem *Omeros* (1990) reimagines Homer through the lives of Saint Lucian fishermen and is one of the great works of Caribbean literature. *The Bounty* (1997) is a later collection, elegiac and rooted in the landscape of his homeland. Walcott grew up in Castries and the island's light, coastlines, and people run through all his work.

For a deeper dive into his writing, Robert D. Hamner's *Epic of the Dispossessed: Derek Walcott's Omeros* is a solid critical introduction. Walcott died in Saint Lucia in 2017.

Kendel Hippolyte, another important Saint Lucian poet, is less well known outside the Caribbean but worth seeking out. His collection *Birthright* deals with questions of identity, land, and belonging in the post-colonial Caribbean.

If you want to understand the Kwéyòl language, Jones Mondesir and Lawrence Carrington's *Dictionary of Saint Lucian Creole* is the scholarly reference, though it's a linguistics text rather than a traveller's phrase book.

For general history and context, Patrick Leigh Fermor's *The Traveller's Tree* (1950) covers a journey through the Caribbean islands including Saint Lucia. It's dated in some of its perspectives but beautifully written and gives a vivid sense of the region before mass tourism.
