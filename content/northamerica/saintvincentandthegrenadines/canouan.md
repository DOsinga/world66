Canouan is a small island in the southern Grenadines, about five kilometres long and blessed with some of the finest beaches in the Caribbean. A barrier reef wraps around the Atlantic side, creating calm turquoise lagoons, while the hills in the centre rise steeply enough to offer views across to [Mustique](/northamerica/saintvincentandthegrenadines/mustique) and the Tobago Cays.

The island has a split personality. The northern half is occupied by a luxury resort and golf course; the southern half is where the locals live, in a small village around the harbour. Both sides share the same beautiful water. Diving and snorkelling are excellent — the reef is healthy and close to shore.

Getting here usually means a short flight from Barbados or [St. Vincent](/northamerica/saintvincentandthegrenadines), or a stop on a sailing route through the Grenadines. The island is quiet even by Grenadine standards, which is saying something.
