---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Mustique's beaches are the island's main attraction, and they deliver. There are about a dozen of them, most reached by golf buggy on dirt tracks through the vegetation.

**Macaroni Beach** is the one on every postcard — a wide sweep of pale sand on the Atlantic-facing east coast, framed by sea-grape trees. The surf here is stronger than on the leeward side, making it good for bodyboarding. A small wooden pavilion provides shade.

**Gelliceaux Bay** is the better swimming beach. Sheltered from the Atlantic swells, the water is calm and shallow, perfect for snorkelling over the reef close to shore. Usually quieter than Macaroni.

**Endeavour Bay** fronts the main village and the Cotton House jetty. It is convenient rather than spectacular, but the water is clear and it works well for an early-morning swim before the day gets going.

**Pasture Bay** on the northeastern tip is the most remote — a long walk or buggy ride — but rewards the effort with solitude and strong surf. Turtles nest here during nesting season (May to October); if you walk at night, go carefully.

Lagoon Bay and L'Ansecoy are two more quieter options worth finding on a map before you set out.
