Petit St. Vincent — known universally as PSV — is a small private island at the southern tip of the Grenadines, just north of Petit Martinique. It is operated exclusively as a single resort, meaning the entire 113-acre island is one property. Access is only by boat from Union Island or by chartered seaplane, and the island is effectively closed to non-guests.

The resort has about 22 cottages spread across the island, each positioned for privacy. The communication system involves a flag: raise a yellow flag if you want to be left alone, a red flag if you need something. There are no phones, no television, and no clocks in the rooms — a deliberate design choice. The beach at Petite Anse is one of the finest in the Grenadines.

PSV sits at the high end of Caribbean resort pricing. For those not staying here, the island is simply not accessible; the broader area around Union Island and the Tobago Cays is more easily explored.
