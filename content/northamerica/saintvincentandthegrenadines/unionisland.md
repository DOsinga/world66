Union Island is a small, mountainous island at the southern end of the Grenadines chain, part of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. It serves as the jumping-off point for the Tobago Cays, one of the Caribbean's most spectacular sailing and snorkelling destinations, and for the private island of Palm Island nearby.

The island has a rugged, undeveloped feel compared to its neighbours. Clifton, the main town, has a small harbour, a few guesthouses, and a handful of bars where the sailing crowd gathers. The beaches are quiet — Chatham Bay on the west coast is a long crescent of sand backed by hills — and a hike up Mount Taboi gives panoramic views across the southern Grenadines. If you are island-hopping through the Grenadines by boat or ferry, Union Island is a natural stop.
