**Anguilla** is still a bit off the beaten Caribbean track, and that is a large part of its appeal. The northernmost of the Leeward Islands, it is a flat, 16-mile-long sliver of coral and limestone with a population of around 15,000. There are no mountains, no rainforests, no volcanoes — just some of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean.

The sand is powdery white, the water is an almost unreal shade of turquoise, and on many beaches you will have plenty of space to yourself. Shoal Bay East is the most famous, but Meads Bay, Rendezvous Bay, and Sandy Ground are equally fine. The island has developed a reputation for upscale dining and boutique resorts, but it has not lost its laid-back character.

The Valley is the capital, sitting in the centre of the island. Most visitors arrive by ferry from Saint Martin at Blowing Point. Since nothing on the island is far from anything else, getting around is simple — rent a car or take a taxi.
