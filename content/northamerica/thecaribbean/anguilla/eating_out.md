---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Anguilla punches well above its weight in the kitchen. The island's food culture draws on West African, Caribbean, British, and French influences — the last partly attributable to the proximity of French St. Martin, whose chefs and produce cross regularly on the ferry. Seafood is central: lobster, conch, crayfish, snapper, and grouper appear on menus across the price spectrum.

The local staples are simpler: johnny cakes (deep-fried dough), saltfish in stews and with vegetables, rice and peas, and souse (pickled pork). On weekends especially, roadside barbecue stalls outside The Valley and in Stoney Ground set up grills and sell ribs, chicken, and fish for a few dollars. The roadside scene is excellent and easy to miss if you stick to resort dining.

At the higher end, Anguilla has a cluster of genuinely accomplished restaurants. Tasty's in South Hill has been a local institution for years, serving Creole and Caribbean food in a relaxed setting. Blanchard's on Meads Bay is the best-known fine-dining option, run by Americans Bob and Melinda Blanchard. The Dune Preserve, Bankie Banx's beach bar on Rendezvous Bay, is more atmosphere than cuisine but is not to be missed. Reservations are advisable at the better places in high season.
