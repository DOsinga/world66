---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

**Eagle Beach**

Consistently ranked as one of the best beaches in the Caribbean. Wide, white and less crowded than Palm Beach despite being just a short walk away. The water is calm and clear.

**Arikok National Park**

The best way to see Aruba beyond the resort strip. Caves with Arawak paintings, endemic wildlife, volcanic rock formations and the Conchi natural pool on the windward coast. Hire a 4x4 or go with a guide — trails can be hard to follow without local knowledge.

**Carnival season**

If your dates overlap with Carnival (January to early March), don't just watch from the sidelines. The Lighting Parade and Grand Parade in Oranjestad are both spectacular. The tumba music competitions in the weeks beforehand are worth attending too.

**Windsurfing and kitesurfing**

Aruba is one of the world's top windsurfing destinations. The consistent northeast trade winds and the shallow, flat-water lagoons around Hadicurari (Fishermen's Huts) make it ideal for both beginners and serious competitors. The Aruba Hi-Winds competition draws international riders each year.

**Antilla wreck dive**

At over 120 meters long, the German freighter Antilla is one of the largest wreck dives in the Caribbean. It sits in shallow water off the northwest coast near Palm Beach, making it accessible to recreational divers. Coral, fish and the sheer scale of the wreck make it a genuine highlight.

**Fort Zoutman and the Bon Bini Festival**

Oranjestad's fort is worth visiting on its own, but the weekly Bon Bini Festival held there on Tuesday evenings adds live music, dance and the chance to try local food. A good introduction to the island's culture rather than just its beaches.

**San Nicolas**

The island's second town and its artistic soul. San Nicolas has been transformed by a street art project that covers buildings across the town with large-scale murals. The Aruba Art Fair, held annually, brings artists from across the Caribbean and beyond. The atmosphere is noticeably different from the tourist resort strip — rougher, more local, more interesting.
