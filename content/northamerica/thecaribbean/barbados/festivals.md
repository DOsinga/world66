---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

Barbados punches above its weight when it comes to festivals. The calendar is anchored by two major events, plus a handful of smaller celebrations worth knowing about.

**Crop Over** (July–August) is the big one — a month-long carnival that traces its roots to the end of the sugar cane harvest in the 18th century. The festival was revived in 1974 and has grown into one of the Caribbean's premier cultural events. It includes calypso and soca competitions, food fairs, road fêtes, and the Pic-o-de-Crop Finals (a calypso competition held at Kensington Oval). It all culminates in Grand Kadooment Day, held on the first Monday of August, when masqueraders in elaborate costumes parade through Bridgetown to Kensington Oval. If you want the full experience, book costumes and accommodation months in advance.

**Oistins Fish Festival** (Easter weekend) celebrates the town's fishing heritage with fish-boning competitions, boat racing, and live music. It's a genuinely local event — a lot of Bajans come out for it too. There's plenty of freshly fried flying fish, fish cakes, and rum punch. Far more low-key than Crop Over but equally enjoyable in its own way.

**Holders Season** (March) is a more intimate affair: an arts festival held on the grounds of Holders House plantation, featuring opera, jazz, and theatre performances in an open-air setting.

**Gospelfest** (May) draws gospel artists from across the Caribbean, the US, and UK for several nights of performances at venues around the island.
