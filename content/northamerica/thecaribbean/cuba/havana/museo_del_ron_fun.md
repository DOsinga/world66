The Havana Club Foundation's rum museum sits in a restored colonial building near the waterfront in Old Havana, a short walk from the Plaza de San Francisco. The exhibition walks you through the full rum-making process — sugar cane cultivation, fermentation, distillation, and the ageing cellars — using scale models, original equipment, and explanatory panels in Spanish and English. The tour ends with a tasting of Havana Club's different expressions, from the light three-year to the aged varieties. There's a bar and shop on site where you can pick up bottles at Cuban prices.
