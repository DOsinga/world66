---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Dominica has two airports but neither can handle large jets. The main one is **Douglas-Charles Airport** (DOM) on the northeast coast — about an hour's drive from Roseau. **Canefield Airport**, just north of Roseau, handles small inter-island planes and charters.

Most visitors fly into a Caribbean hub and connect from there. Common connection points include Antigua, Barbados, St. Lucia, Puerto Rico, and Guadeloupe or Martinique. American Airlines has offered direct Miami-Dominica service; United has run a Newark-Dominica Saturday flight. Check current schedules as routes in this region change seasonally. LIAT, the main regional carrier, has had reliability problems over the years.

A new international airport is under construction and was expected to open around 2027, which will eventually allow direct flights from Europe and North America.

By sea, **L'Express des Îles** runs fast ferry services between Dominica and Guadeloupe, Martinique, and St. Lucia several times a week. Ferries arrive at the terminal in central Roseau, which is convenient. The crossing from Martinique takes roughly 75 minutes. Check the ferry schedule in advance as it shifts by season and is sometimes suspended for maintenance.
