The central park of Fort-de-France, La Savane is a palm-shaded square fronting the waterfront. It's the social hub of the city — vendors sell cold drinks and coconuts along the edges, and benches fill up in the late afternoon. A headless statue of Empress Joséphine, born in Martinique, stands in the park; locals decapitated it in 1991 as a protest against her support of slavery.
