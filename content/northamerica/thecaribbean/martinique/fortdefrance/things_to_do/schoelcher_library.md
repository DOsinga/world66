One of the most remarkable buildings in the Caribbean — an ornate prefabricated iron structure originally built for the 1889 Paris World's Fair, then shipped in pieces and reassembled here in Fort-de-France. Named after Victor Schoelcher, abolitionist and champion of Martinique's enslaved population, it now functions as the city's main public library. The exterior alone, a riot of polychrome ironwork, is worth the walk.
