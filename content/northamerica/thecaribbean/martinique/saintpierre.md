Saint-Pierre was once the cultural capital of the Caribbean — the "Paris of the Antilles," a prosperous colonial city of 30,000 people at the foot of Mount Pelée. On 8 May 1902, the volcano erupted with a superheated pyroclastic surge that killed virtually the entire population in minutes. The ruins have never been rebuilt. Walking through the overgrown streets and crumbled facades, you're essentially walking through a frozen moment in 1902.

Today Saint-Pierre is a small town rebuilt among and beside the ruins. The Musée Vulcanologique Frank Perret displays artifacts recovered from the disaster — clocks stopped at 8am, fused glass, a church bell melted out of shape. Offshore, the harbour holds the wrecks of ships sunk by the blast; it's one of the best wreck diving sites in the Caribbean, with ships at accessible depths.
