Gurabo is a small municipality in the eastern interior of [Puerto Rico](/northamerica/thecaribbean/puertorico), tucked into a valley of the Sierra de Cayey about 30 minutes south of San Juan. Known locally as the "Ciudad del Pasillo" for its association with the traditional Puerto Rican musical form, Gurabo has a distinctly rural, unhurried feel compared to the coastal cities.

The town sits in lush green countryside surrounded by hills and farmland. The Río Gurabo runs through the valley, and the surrounding area is dotted with small farms growing coffee, plantains, and tropical fruit. The town plaza is pleasant, with a Spanish colonial church and a handful of local restaurants serving comida criolla — roast pork, mofongo, and rice and beans done the traditional way.

Gurabo is not a tourist destination in the usual sense, but it offers a window into everyday Puerto Rican life outside the resort strip. The Universidad del Turabo campus gives the town a youthful energy, and there are a few hiking trails in the surrounding hills with views across the central mountain range. If you are based in San Juan and want a taste of the interior, Gurabo makes a worthwhile half-day trip.
