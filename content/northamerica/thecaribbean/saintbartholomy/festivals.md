---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

**New Year's Eve.** The island's most famous annual event is not a local festival so much as an international spectacle. Gustavia harbour fills with superyachts, every restaurant and villa is booked months in advance, and at midnight fireworks go up over Fort Oscar while every vessel in the harbour sounds its horn. A New Year's Eve regatta — a casual race around the island — runs on the morning of December 31st. The whole affair is celebratory and extremely expensive. If you want to be there, plan six months out at minimum.

**Carnival.** Carnival runs in the days leading up to Fat Tuesday (Mardi Gras), usually in February or March. The main parade moves through Gustavia with costumed groups, floats, steel bands, and dancing. The music is soca and zouk rather than the brass-heavy style of the northern Caribbean. The highlight is the burning of Vaval — the symbolic King of Carnival — at Shell Beach on the final night. It is one of the more genuine cultural events on the island's calendar.

**Les Voiles de Saint-Barth (now Les Voiles by Richard Mille).** The island's premier sailing regatta runs over five days in mid-April. It attracts a serious fleet of racing yachts from across the Caribbean and beyond, competing in multiple classes. The trade winds in April are reliable and strong, making for fast racing. The shoreside scene in Gustavia during the regatta is festive and open to non-sailors.

**The Bucket Regatta.** Also in April, the Bucket draws the largest and most impressive superyachts in the world for a racing event that is as much social event as competition. The assembled fleet in Gustavia before the start is a spectacle in itself.

**St. Barth Gourmet Festival.** Running since 2013, this autumn food festival brings chefs from France and the Caribbean to the island for a week of dinners, tastings, and cooking events. Held at various restaurants around the island.

**St. Barthélemy Day (August 24).** The island's patron saint day, a local public holiday with small celebrations, music, and a more local character than the headline events.
