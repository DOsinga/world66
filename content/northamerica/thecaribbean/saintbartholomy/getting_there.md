---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Getting to St. Barts takes effort. There are no direct flights from North America or Europe: the runway at Gustaf III Airport (SBH) in St. Jean is famously short — about 650 metres — and only handles turboprop aircraft seating 19 passengers or fewer. The approach over a hill and the rapid descent make it one of the more memorable landings in the Caribbean. Flights operate in daylight hours only.

**Via Sint Maarten (SXM):** The main gateway. Princess Juliana International Airport on the Dutch side of Sint Maarten has direct connections from the US, Canada, and Europe, with over 20 airlines serving it. From there, Winair (Windward Islands Airways) operates the 15-minute hop to St. Barts multiple times daily. Book early during high season — seats are limited and flights fill up.

**Via San Juan (SJU):** Tradewind Aviation runs scheduled service between Luis Muñoz Marín International Airport in Puerto Rico and SBH. San Juan has a dedicated terminal area for these connections that makes the transit reasonably smooth. Flight time is around an hour.

**By ferry:** The Voyager catamaran service (operated by Compagnie Maritime West Indies) connects St. Barts to both the French and Dutch sides of Saint Martin, making the crossing in roughly 45 to 75 minutes depending on conditions. Typically two departures daily in each direction, with additional sailings during peak season. The crossing can be rough if the trades are up; seasickness tablets are not a bad idea. Book ahead during December through April.

**Arriving:** Customs and immigration at SBH is a small operation. All visitors need a valid passport (minimum six months remaining validity). Americans, Canadians, and EU citizens do not need a visa for stays up to 90 days. Other nationalities may need a French visa — check with the French embassy before travelling, as this is a French overseas collectivity rather than metropolitan France, and the specific visa category matters. A return or onward ticket is required of all visitors. No vaccination requirements unless you're arriving from a yellow fever endemic country.
