Saint-Martin is the French half of an island shared with Dutch [Sint Maarten](/northamerica/netherlandsantilles/stmaarten) — the smallest landmass in the world divided between two nations, and one without any border controls between them. You can walk from a French patisserie to a Dutch casino in fifteen minutes.

The French side is the quieter, more refined half. Marigot, the capital, is a small waterfront town with a produce market, a handful of good restaurants, and a ruined fort above the harbour. The beaches on this side — Baie Orientale, Grand Case, Anse Marcel — tend to be less developed than their Dutch counterparts, and clothing-optional stretches are common.

Grand Case, a single street of restaurants along a bay, has the best food on the island and arguably some of the finest dining in the Caribbean. Creole, French, and fusion kitchens compete for your attention, and the "lolos" — simple barbecue shacks on the beach side — serve grilled ribs and lobster at a fraction of the restaurant prices.

The island is small enough to drive around in a day, so staying on either side gives you access to everything. But if you are after good food and a calmer pace, the French side is the better base.
