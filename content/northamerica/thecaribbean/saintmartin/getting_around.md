---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

The island is small enough to drive across in 30 minutes, and a rental car makes it significantly easier to explore. Car hire starts at around €40–60 per day in high season; book ahead if you are visiting between December and April. Traffic in and around Marigot can be slow due to the one-way system and general congestion.

**Taxis** are widely available but there are no meters — fares are regulated by zone and set by the government. Always confirm the fare before getting in. Drivers generally accept both euros and US dollars. There is no Uber or Lyft service on the island.

**Minibuses** run between the main towns on both sides and are the cheapest way to get around, though schedules are informal and they stop running in the early evening. The French side has a bus station in Marigot with services to Grand Case, Orient Bay, and French Quarter. Flag one down on the road rather than waiting for a schedule.

**Crossing between sides** requires no formality — there is no passport control or customs at the border. Drive or take a taxi straight through.

**Ferries to nearby islands** depart from Marigot: Anguilla to the north (20 minutes), Saint-Barthélemy to the south (45 minutes). The terminal is in the centre of town on the waterfront and is open daily 8:30 to 18:00 except in bad weather.
