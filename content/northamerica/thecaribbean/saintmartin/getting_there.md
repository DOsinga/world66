## By air

Almost all visitors arrive at **Princess Juliana International Airport (SXM)** on the Dutch side, which handles direct flights from the US, Canada, and Europe. American, Delta, United, Air France, Air Canada, and KLM all serve the island. From the US East Coast, flying time is roughly three to four hours.

The airport terminal was extensively rebuilt after Hurricane Irma and a new departures hall opened in late 2023. It is functional and reasonably modern.

There is a small airport at **Grand Case (SFG)** on the French side, served mainly by inter-island carriers such as Air Caraïbes and St. Barth Commuter. If you are arriving from another Caribbean island, Grand Case is the more convenient option. From Europe or North America, you will almost certainly land at SXM.

From SXM airport, the French side capital Marigot is around 45 minutes to an hour by road; Grand Case is a similar distance. Taxis have fixed government-set rates by zone — confirm the price before you get in.

## By sea

Ferries arrive at Marigot from **Anguilla** (around 20 minutes, multiple departures daily), from **Saint-Barthélemy** (around 45 minutes), and from other neighbouring islands. The ferry terminal is on the Marigot waterfront. Tickets are sold on-site; no advance online booking is available.

Cruise ships dock in **Philipsburg** on the Dutch side. From there, taxis to Marigot or Grand Case run around $25–35 depending on your destination.
