---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

The island packs a surprising amount of variety into a small space. The French side's standout experiences are mostly about eating and beaches; the Dutch side adds commercial energy and a few genuine spectacles.

**Grand Case** is a single road of restaurants running along a small beach on the French side's north coast. It has more good restaurants per metre than almost anywhere in the Caribbean. Come for dinner — the Creole lolos (outdoor barbecue shacks) at the northern end of the strip offer grilled fish, ribs, and rice for a fraction of what the sit-down restaurants charge. Both are worth your time.

**Orient Bay** is the island's liveliest beach: long, windy, backed by restaurants and beach bars, and partly clothing-optional. It can get crowded in high season but is big enough to absorb it.

**Baie Longue** (Long Bay) is the opposite — a long, mostly empty sweep of white sand on the western shore, calmer water, and very little commercial development. The best beach on the island for those who want space and quiet.

**Maho Beach** on the Dutch side sits directly under the approach path to Princess Juliana International Airport. Planes pass close enough overhead to feel dangerous. It is absurd, thrilling, and completely unique. Arrive before a scheduled wide-body landing for the full effect, and ignore the warning signs at your own risk — the jet blast from departing aircraft has seriously injured people.

**Marigot** is the French capital: a pleasant waterfront town with a morning market, a ruined French fort (Fort Saint-Louis) overlooking the harbour, and a good selection of French and Creole restaurants. It is not a major sight in itself, but worth half a day.

**Butterfly Farm** near Orient Bay is one of the island's longer-running attractions — a walk-through enclosure with hundreds of live tropical butterfly species. Modest but genuinely charming, and suitable for children.
