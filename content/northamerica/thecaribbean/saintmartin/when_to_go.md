---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The island has two distinct seasons. **High season** runs from mid-December through April: dry, sunny, temperatures in the mid-20s Celsius (low-to-mid 80s Fahrenheit), and relatively low humidity. This is when prices are highest, beaches are at their most popular, and restaurants are fully staffed. Book ahead for the best places to eat in Grand Case.

**Low season** (May through November) brings heat, higher humidity, more rain, and the genuine risk of hurricanes — peak hurricane season is August through October. Prices drop significantly, beaches empty out, and some smaller businesses close. If you are comfortable with the weather risk, May to early July and late October to late November offer reasonable value and much quieter conditions.

The island was effectively destroyed by Hurricane Irma in September 2017. The recovery is largely complete, but it is a reminder that late summer and autumn travel in the Caribbean carries real risk. Check conditions and travel advisories before booking low-season travel.

Average sea temperature stays around 27°C (80°F) year-round, and the water is warm enough to swim in any month.
