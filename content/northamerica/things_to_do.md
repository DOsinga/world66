---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The national parks are North America's greatest treasure. The [United States](/northamerica/unitedstates) alone has over 60 of them, from the geysers of Yellowstone to the red rock canyons of Utah to the glaciers of Alaska. [Canada](/northamerica/canada) matches with Banff, Jasper, and the stunning coastline of Cape Breton. These are vast, dramatic landscapes that feel genuinely humbling. Buy the America the Beautiful pass ($80, valid one year) if you're doing more than two US parks. Parks Canada has a similar discovery pass. Further south, [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica) packs an astonishing density of national parks into a tiny country — cloud forests, volcanoes, and coastline teeming with wildlife.

The ancient Maya built one of the great civilizations of the Americas, and their ruins are scattered across [Mexico](/northamerica/mexico), [Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala), [Belize](/northamerica/belize), and [Honduras](/northamerica/honduras). Chichen Itza gets the crowds, but Tikal — rising above the Guatemalan jungle canopy — is the one that takes your breath away. Palenque in Chiapas is atmospheric and beautifully set. Copan in Honduras is smaller but famous for its intricate carved stelae. You could spend weeks on the Maya trail alone, and every site feels different.

Road trips are practically a rite of passage up north. The Pacific Coast Highway from [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles) to [San Francisco](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfranciscobayarea/sanfrancisco) hugs cliffs above the Pacific through Big Sur — one of the world's great drives. The Trans-Canada Highway is an epic journey through prairies, mountains, and boreal forest. Route 66 from Chicago to LA still has enough diners, motels, and roadside kitsch to justify the nostalgia. For sheer wilderness, the Alaska Highway or the Icefields Parkway in the Canadian Rockies are hard to beat.

The diving and snorkeling in the Caribbean and Central America rank among the best on the planet. The Mesoamerican Barrier Reef — the world's second longest — runs from Mexico's Yucatan coast through [Belize](/northamerica/belize) to [Honduras](/northamerica/honduras)'s Bay Islands. The Great Blue Hole in Belize is a bucket-list dive. Bonaire is famous for shore diving you can do right off the beach. The Cayman Islands, Turks and Caicos, and Cozumel all offer world-class reef systems. Even if you don't dive, snorkeling in these warm, clear waters is extraordinary.

The cities deliver completely different experiences. [New York](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork) is relentless, electric, and endlessly walkable. [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago) has the best architecture in North America and a food scene that rivals anywhere. [Montreal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal) feels like a piece of Europe dropped into the continent. [Mexico City](/northamerica/mexico/mexicocity) is enormous, chaotic, and magnificent — world-class museums, street food that will ruin you for tacos anywhere else, and a cultural intensity that matches any capital on Earth. [Havana](/northamerica/cuba/havana)'s crumbling grandeur is unlike anywhere, and [Antigua Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala/antigua) is one of the most beautiful small cities in the Americas.

Volcanoes define the Central American landscape, and you can hike many of them. Acatenango in [Guatemala](/northamerica/guatemala) offers an overnight trek with views of neighboring Fuego erupting through the night — one of the most dramatic camping experiences anywhere. Concepcion on Ometepe Island in [Nicaragua](/northamerica/nicaragua) is a tough full-day climb. [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica)'s Arenal is dormant now but the surrounding area is packed with hot springs and trails. In Mexico, Nevado de Toluca has a crater lake you can drive to. Even if you don't summit anything, the volcanic terrain shapes everything from the hot springs to the black-sand beaches.

Music runs deep across the whole continent. Nashville is the home of country, and the honky-tonks on Broadway deliver the real thing any night of the week. New Orleans is jazz, brass bands, and a sound that pours out of every doorway in the French Quarter. In the Caribbean, you'll find reggae in [Jamaica](/northamerica/jamaica), salsa in [Cuba](/northamerica/cuba) and [Puerto Rico](/northamerica/puertorico), calypso and soca in Trinidad, and merengue and bachata in the [Dominican Republic](/northamerica/dominicanrepublic). Mariachi and norteño music fill the plazas of Mexican towns. If music matters to you, plan around it — there's always something happening somewhere.

Outdoor adventures could fill a lifetime. Skiing and snowboarding are world-class in Colorado, Utah, British Columbia, and Alberta. Surfing runs from the cold water breaks of northern California to the warm Pacific swells of [Costa Rica](/northamerica/costarica) and [Nicaragua](/northamerica/nicaragua) — Tamarindo, Santa Teresa, and San Juan del Sur are backpacker-surfer magnets. Hiking ranges from casual day walks to serious backcountry expeditions on the Appalachian Trail, Pacific Crest Trail, or Guatemala's highland trails between indigenous villages. Whitewater rafting on the Pacuare River in Costa Rica is world-class. And then there's [Greenland](/northamerica/greenland), where you can walk on the ice cap, kayak among icebergs, and visit communities where traditional Inuit culture is still alive. The scale of this continent means there's terrain for everything.
