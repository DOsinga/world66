The national parks are North America's greatest treasure. The [United States](/northamerica/unitedstates) alone has over 60 of them, from the geysers of Yellowstone to the red rock canyons of Utah to the glaciers of Alaska. [Canada](/northamerica/canada) matches with Banff, Jasper, and the stunning coastline of Cape Breton. These parks are where the continent shows off — vast, dramatic landscapes that feel genuinely humbling. Buy the America the Beautiful pass ($80, valid one year) if you're doing more than two US parks. Parks Canada has a similar discovery pass.

Road trips are practically a rite of passage here. The Pacific Coast Highway from [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles) to [San Francisco](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfranciscobayarea/sanfrancisco) is one of the world's great drives, hugging cliffs above the Pacific through Big Sur. The Trans-Canada Highway is an epic cross-country journey through prairies, mountains, and boreal forest. Route 66 from Chicago to LA still has enough diners, motels, and roadside kitsch to justify the nostalgia, though much of the original road has been swallowed by the Interstate. For sheer wilderness, the Alaska Highway or Icefields Parkway in the Canadian Rockies are hard to beat.

The cities deliver completely different experiences. [New York](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork) is relentless, electric, and endlessly walkable — you could spend months and not exhaust it. [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago) has the best architecture in North America and a food scene that rivals anywhere. [San Francisco](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfranciscobayarea/sanfrancisco) combines natural beauty with urban grit. [Montreal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal) feels like a piece of Europe dropped into the continent, with its own distinct culture, food, and nightlife. [Vancouver](/northamerica/canada/britishcolumbia/vancouver) puts ocean, mountains, and rainforest within minutes of downtown. [Toronto](/northamerica/canada/ontario/toronto) is one of the most genuinely multicultural cities on Earth, and its neighborhoods reflect that.

For wilderness on a bigger scale, head north. Alaska is staggering — grizzly bears fishing for salmon, calving glaciers, mountains that dwarf everything in the lower 48. The Canadian Rockies around Banff and Jasper offer turquoise lakes and alpine scenery that look retouched but aren't. And then there's [Greenland](/northamerica/greenland), where you can walk on the ice cap, kayak among icebergs, and visit communities where traditional Inuit culture is still alive. These are places that make you feel genuinely small.

Music runs deep across this continent. Nashville is the home of country music, and the honky-tonks on Broadway still deliver the real thing any night of the week. New Orleans is jazz, brass bands, and a sound that pours out of every doorway in the French Quarter. Austin built its identity around live music, with hundreds of venues for every genre. [Montreal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal) and [Toronto](/northamerica/canada/ontario/toronto) have thriving indie music and festival scenes. If music matters to you, plan around it — there's always something happening somewhere.

Outdoor sports and activities could fill an entire trip. Skiing and snowboarding are world-class in Colorado, Utah, British Columbia, and Alberta. Surfing runs from the cold water breaks of northern California to the warm swells of Hawaii and Florida. Hiking ranges from casual day walks to serious backcountry expeditions on trails like the Appalachian Trail, Pacific Crest Trail, or the West Coast Trail on Vancouver Island. Kayaking, whitewater rafting, mountain biking, rock climbing — the scale of the landscape means there's terrain for everything, and outfitters to set you up in most gateway towns.
