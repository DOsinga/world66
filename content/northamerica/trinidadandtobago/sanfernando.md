San Fernando is Trinidad's second city, perched on a hill in the island's industrial south. The city grew on the back of the oil and petrochemical industries that dominate this part of Trinidad, and the working rigs visible from the highway on the approach give you a clear sense of what drives the local economy. Yet San Fernando has a vitality and community pride that the oil-town label does not fully capture.

The Carnival celebrations in San Fernando rival those in Port of Spain for energy if not scale, and many Trinidadians argue that the "Sando" Carnival is more authentic and less commercialized. The city's hilltop park, San Fernando Hill, offers sweeping views across the plains to the Gulf of Paria, and the surrounding streets have a walkable, lived-in character with good roti shops and doubles vendors. The Naparima Heritage Park and the local market are worth exploring for a sense of everyday Trinidadian life.

San Fernando also serves as a base for visiting the Pitch Lake at La Brea, one of the world's largest natural asphalt deposits and a genuinely strange geological attraction. The Cedros peninsula to the southwest offers quiet beaches and fishing villages where the pace of life slows dramatically.
