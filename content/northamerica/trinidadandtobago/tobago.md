Tobago is the quieter, smaller sister island of Trinidad, about 30 km to the northeast. Where Trinidad is bustling and multicultural, Tobago is laidback and beach-focused — a proper Caribbean island with palm-fringed bays, coral reefs, and rainforest-covered hills.

The southwestern end around Crown Point has the airport, the best beaches (including Pigeon Point and Store Bay), and most of the tourist infrastructure. The northeastern end is wilder and less developed, with the oldest protected rainforest in the Western Hemisphere in the Main Ridge Forest Reserve, established in 1776.

Castara Bay on the north coast is a beautiful, relatively undeveloped fishing village with good snorkelling. Little Tobago, a small island off the east coast, is a bird sanctuary and one of the best places in the Caribbean to see seabirds up close. The diving and snorkelling around Tobago are excellent — the island sits where the Caribbean meets the Atlantic, creating nutrient-rich waters with abundant marine life.
