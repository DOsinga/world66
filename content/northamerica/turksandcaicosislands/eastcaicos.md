East Caicos is the largest uninhabited island in the Turks and Caicos, covering about 18 square miles of mangrove swamp, limestone caves, and some of the most pristine beaches in the archipelago. The island's highest point — Flamingo Hill, at a modest 48 metres — is also the highest point in the entire island chain.

The main attraction for the adventurous is Jacksonville, a deserted settlement that was once home to a sisal plantation and a thriving community. Exploring its overgrown ruins feels like genuine discovery. The caves on the island contain Lucayan Taíno artefacts, and the surrounding waters are excellent for bonefishing.

Getting to East Caicos requires arranging a private boat from South Caicos — there is no scheduled service. Bring everything you need, as there are no facilities on the island.
