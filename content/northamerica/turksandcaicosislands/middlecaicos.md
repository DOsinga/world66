Middle Caicos is the largest island in the Turks and Caicos but one of the least developed. Connected to [North Caicos](/northamerica/turksandcaicosislands/northcaicos) by a causeway, it has fewer than 300 residents and no resorts — just a handful of small guesthouses and rental cottages.

The main draw is Mudjin Harbour, a dramatic limestone cove on the north coast with crashing waves and a natural rock arch. The Conch Bar Caves, the largest above-ground cave system in the Caribbean, are worth exploring with a local guide. Bambarra Beach, on the south side, is a long, empty stretch of calm turquoise water.

Middle Caicos feels like what the Caribbean must have been like before the developers arrived.
