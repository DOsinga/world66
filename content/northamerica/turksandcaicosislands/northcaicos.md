North Caicos is the greenest island in the Turks and Caicos, with more rainfall than its neighbours and a lush, almost jungled interior. It is connected to [Middle Caicos](/northamerica/turksandcaicosislands/middlecaicos) by a causeway and reached by a short ferry from Providenciales.

The island has a handful of small resorts and guesthouses but nothing approaching the development of Provo. The beaches — particularly Whitby Beach and Three Mary Cays — are excellent and often empty. Flamingos wade in the ponds near Flamingo Pond Overlook, and the old Loyalist plantation ruins at Wade's Green are the best-preserved in the territory.

North Caicos is a good day trip from Providenciales or a quiet base for exploring the less-touristed side of the TCI.
