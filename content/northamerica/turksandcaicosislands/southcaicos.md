South Caicos is the fishing capital of the Turks and Caicos — a small, windswept island where the economy still revolves around conch and lobster rather than tourism. The main settlement, Cockburn Harbour, has a quiet, working-harbour feel that is rare in the modern Caribbean.

The island has superb wall diving just offshore, some of the best bonefishing flats in the territory, and a few beautiful, usually deserted beaches. A marine research station run by the School for Field Studies brings a handful of students each semester. Development is slowly arriving — a resort has been under construction for years — but for now South Caicos remains refreshingly uncommercialized.
