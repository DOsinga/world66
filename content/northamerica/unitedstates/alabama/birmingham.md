Birmingham is Alabama's largest city, nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains. It sprawls along a valley divided by Red Mountain, where iron ore was once mined to feed the steel mills that built the city. The steel industry is long gone, replaced by banking, medical research, and the University of Alabama at Birmingham, which dominates the downtown.

The most important reason to visit is the Birmingham Civil Rights Institute, a powerful museum chronicling the city's role in the civil rights movement. Birmingham was nicknamed "Bombingham" in the 1960s for the violence directed at civil rights activists, and the 16th Street Baptist Church -- where four young girls were killed in a 1963 bombing -- stands across the street from the institute.

For a taste of the city's personality, head to Five Points South. You will find interesting restaurants, live music, and the closest thing Birmingham has to a walkable neighborhood scene. The city's food scene is a highlight -- Niki's West in the north side serves some of the best Southern home cooking around, and Dreamland BBQ is a pilgrimage site for barbecue lovers.

Safety is a mixed bag. Anything south of Red Mountain is generally fine, as is anything north of Fultondale. The neighborhoods in between can be rough, especially at night. Stick to the areas you came to see and you will be fine.
