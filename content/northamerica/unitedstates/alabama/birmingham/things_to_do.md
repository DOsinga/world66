---
title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

The **Birmingham Civil Rights Institute** is the essential activity in the city. The focus is on how the civil rights movement shaped Birmingham through exhibits, events, and programs. Permanent exhibitions include a replica of a segregated streetcar, a freedom bus, and a church. The 16th Street Baptist Church, where four girls were killed in a 1963 bombing, is directly across the street.

**Vulcan Park and Museum** sits atop Red Mountain and features the world's largest cast-iron statue -- a 56-foot figure of Vulcan, the Roman god of the forge, a nod to Birmingham's steel heritage. The observation deck offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding valleys.

**Railroad Park**, a 19-acre green space downtown, is a pleasant spot for a walk or a break between sightseeing stops. The park has walking trails, a lake, and views of the city skyline.
