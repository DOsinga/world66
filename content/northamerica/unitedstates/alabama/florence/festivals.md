---
title: 'Festivals'
type: section
---

**W.C. Handy Music Festival** -- The annual Handy festival, sponsored by the Music Preservation Society, is held in late July across the Shoals area. It features jazz and blues artists from across the country. Past performers have included Dizzy Gillespie, Ramsey Lewis, Bobby Blue Bland, Diane Schuur, and Charlie Byrd. The festival celebrates the legacy of W.C. Handy, born in Florence in 1873, who composed "St. Louis Blues," "Memphis Blues," and "Beale Street Blues."

**First Fridays in Florence** -- Downtown Florence hosts an art fair on the first Friday of every month. Artists sell and demonstrate their work on Court Street, live music plays throughout the evening, and the downtown shops and galleries stay open late. The event draws visitors from Huntsville, Nashville, Birmingham, Memphis, and Tupelo. No booth rental fee for participating artists and musicians.

**Arts Alive** -- A juried gallery exhibition at the Kennedy-Douglass Center for the Arts, with artist booths in the adjoining Wilson Park. Painters, sculptors, furniture makers, jewelers, stained glass artists, and textile artists come from around the country. Held in May.

**George Lindsey UNA Film Festival** -- Named after the actor George "Goober" Lindsey, this festival at the University of North Alabama receives national and international film entries. A $3,000 prize goes to the Best of Show winner, and the Sweet Home Alabama award goes to the best film shot at least partially in Alabama. Held in March.
