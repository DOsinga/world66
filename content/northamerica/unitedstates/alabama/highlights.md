---
title: 'Highlights'
type: section
---

**Civil Rights Trail** -- Alabama is the birthplace of the American civil rights movement. Walk across the Edmund Pettus Bridge in Selma, visit the Dexter Avenue King Memorial Baptist Church in Montgomery where Martin Luther King Jr. preached, and spend time at the Birmingham Civil Rights Institute. The National Voting Rights Museum in Selma and the Rosa Parks Museum in Montgomery complete a powerful journey through history.

**Gulf Beaches** -- The beaches along Alabama's short Gulf Coast are stunning -- sugar-white sand, warm emerald water, and far fewer crowds than Florida. Gulf Shores and Orange Beach are the main resort towns, but [Dauphin Island](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/dauphin_island) offers a quieter, more natural alternative with world-class birding.

**U.S. Space and Rocket Center** -- [Huntsville](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/huntsville) earned the nickname Rocket City for good reason. The Space and Rocket Center features Apollo, Mercury, and Space Shuttle spacecraft, along with dozens of interactive exhibits. It is the world's largest space museum and a must for anyone with even a passing interest in space exploration.

**Muscle Shoals Music** -- The Shoals area around [Florence](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/florence) produced an outsized share of American music. W.C. Handy, the father of the blues, was born here. The Muscle Shoals recording studios drew artists from Aretha Franklin to the Rolling Stones. Visit the W.C. Handy Home and Museum, and the Alabama Music Hall of Fame in Tuscumbia.

**Little River Canyon** -- Near [Fort Payne](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/fort_payne) in northeast Alabama, Little River flows for most of its length atop Lookout Mountain before dropping into a dramatic canyon. The national preserve offers waterfalls, hiking trails, and sandstone cliffs -- spectacular Appalachian scenery any time of year.

**Horseshoe Bend National Military Park** -- Near [Daviston](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/daviston), this park preserves the site where Andrew Jackson defeated the Red Stick Creek warriors in 1814, the final battle of the Creek War. The 2,040-acre park has a visitor center, hiking trails, and a driving trail through the battlefield.

**DeSoto Caverns** -- A massive cave system near Childersburg with a long human history. The Creek Nation used these caves, and artifacts from Indian habitation and burial grounds are on display. There is also, improbably, a laser light show.

**Barbecue** -- Alabama takes its barbecue seriously and has its own style. Dreamland in [Tuscaloosa](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/tuscaloosa) gets the national attention, but locals will send you to Woodrow's instead. [Birmingham](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/birmingham) has its own contenders, and the state's signature white barbecue sauce -- a tangy, mayonnaise-based creation from Big Bob Gibson's in Decatur -- is unlike anything you will find elsewhere in the South.

**Noccalula Falls** -- Near Gadsden, a 90-foot waterfall drops into a gorge surrounded by a botanical garden, scenic trails, and a campground. Named after a Cherokee legend of a girl who leapt from the falls rather than marry against her will.

**Robert Trent Jones Golf Trail** -- Eighteen championship courses spread across seven locations statewide, with 324 holes of golf in total. A road trip connecting the courses is a golfer's dream.

**Covered Bridges** -- Twelve historic covered bridges still stand in Alabama, some still in use. The Swann Covered Bridge north of Oneonta is the longest in the state at over 300 feet and more than a century old. The Clarkson Covered Bridge in Cullman County sits in a park with hiking trails and a water mill.
