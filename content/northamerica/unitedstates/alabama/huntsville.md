Huntsville is the largest city in north Alabama and a surprisingly interesting place to spend a few days. The city earned the nickname Rocket City during the space race -- Wernher von Braun and his team developed the Saturn V rocket here -- and the U.S. Space and Rocket Center remains its star attraction, featuring Apollo, Mercury, and Space Shuttle spacecraft alongside dozens of interactive exhibits.

Beyond the rockets, Huntsville has a strong cultural side. The Huntsville Museum of Art, the Weeden House Museum, and the Burritt on the Mountain museum offer a good mix of art and history. EarlyWorks, one of America's oldest remaining railroad depots, is a hands-on history museum that works well for families.

The city is a convenient hub for exploring north Alabama. West of town, the Shoals area has the Alabama Music Hall of Fame and Ivy Green, Helen Keller's birthplace in [Tuscumbia](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/tuscumbia). The father of the blues, W.C. Handy, was born in [Florence](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/florence). East of the city, the mountain village of Mentone and [Fort Payne](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/fort_payne) -- gateway to Little River Canyon -- are worth the drive. South toward Cullman you will find the quirky Ave Maria Grotto, the outlet shopping in [Boaz](/northamerica/unitedstates/alabama/boaz), and the covered bridges near Oneonta.
