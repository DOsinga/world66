---
title: 'Practical Information'
type: section
---

**Money** -- Alabama is one of the cheapest states in the U.S. for travelers. Gas, food, and lodging all cost less than the national average. Tipping follows standard American customs: 15-20% at restaurants, a dollar or two per drink at bars.

**Language** -- English, with a distinctive Southern accent that varies from the hills of the north to the Gulf Coast. People are generally friendly and talkative with strangers.

**Safety** -- Alabama's rural areas and tourist destinations are safe. Birmingham and Mobile have higher crime rates in certain neighborhoods, but tourist areas are generally fine. Use common sense in cities at night. The Gulf Coast is subject to hurricanes from June through November.

**Climate** -- Summers are hot and humid, with temperatures regularly above 90°F (32°C) and high humidity. Winters are mild, rarely dropping below freezing except in the northern mountains. Spring and fall are the most pleasant seasons for travel. Severe thunderstorms and tornadoes are a real risk from March through May.

**Alcohol** -- Alabama has a patchwork of dry, wet, and semi-dry counties. Some counties do not sell alcohol at all. Check before you go if a cold beer matters to your plans.
