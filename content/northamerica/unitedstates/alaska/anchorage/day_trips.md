---
title: "Day Trips in Anchorage"
type: section
---

Blessed with a wild and beautiful location, Anchorage is in close proximity to gorgeous glaciers, fjords, national parks, and towns of historical interest. Why not take a day trip to some of the stunning places surrounding the city? Flying, driving, or taking a train are all reasonable ways to get you where you want to go for the day. A lot of pre-organized tours and day trips can be taken from Anchorage as well. Sometimes these are the best options because all of your activities are planned and transportation is provided, making everything easier for you.

**Independent Trips**

A popular day trip is to visit Denali National Park and Preserve. You board a small plane in Anchorage and fly over some incredibly scenic landscapes, plus you'll have an amazing view of Mount Mckinley. Once you land, varius narrated tours are available to help you see the highlights of the park, the diverse wildlife is impressive. Board the Alaska Railroad for another fun day trip. You can choose which places to stop at; from the Spencer Glacier, to Seward, to Grandview Valley, each place has its selection of outdoor activities including hiking, rafting, glacier viewing, and jet boating.

**Organized Trips**

For more organized adventures, Alaska Tours provides some great, stress free trips. Take a glacier cruise of the Prince William Sound. From Anchorage, a transfer to Whittler is provided where you'll take a fast catamaran through Harrimann and College Fjords, viewing the glaciers the whole way. Whales, birds, and sea otters are commonly spotted and a hot lunch is provided. This tour is $200 per person. A more expensive, but perhaps more thrilling, organized day trip takes you to Nome. Nome is a town on the edge of the Bering Sea. It is known for being the finish line for the Iditarod and a former gold mining town. On this trip, you get to see a demonstration of Iditarod dogs. You also visit Little Creek Mine, where you meet with a the family of a gold miner. They explain the history of gold mining in the area, and talk about the famous gold strike of the 1890s which changed Nome's reputation in Alaska. Afterwards, you can try panning for gold yourself. Everything you find you get to keep. This trip is really good for families with young kids, they love the gold panning. The cost of the trip is around $570 per person.

**Mt. ****McKinley Base Camp**

Out of Anchorage take the Parks Hwy north to mile 98 to Talkeetna, Alaska to experience Mt. McKinley Base Camp. This is the starting point for mountaineers on their way to ascend the tallest summit in North America. On your drive there let the signs to Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge lead you to town and the best views of majestic Mt. McKinley. Activities here include: flight-seeing, jet-boat rides and hiking.

**Lion’s Head**

Whitewater rafting always provides memories that last a lifetime. Fly down the Lion’s Head on class III and IV rapids that go right past a giant glacier. Only ninety miles from Anchorage is some whitewater rafting fun. Trips down the river are about fourteen miles and the last few miles provide a real thrill for the finale of the trip.

**Salmon Fishing**

If there are fishermen in your group, they are in for a treat. You can go fishing for famous Alaskan salmon on a day trip from Anchorage. Only a ninety minute drive from Anchorage and you are fishing for world famous Alaskan Sockeye, Coho and Pink Salmon. World class trout fishing is also available.

**Wasilla**

Now famous for being the hometown of a vice-presidential candidate on an unsuccessful ticket, Wasilla is only 44 miles away from Anchorage. Visit Independence Mine State Historical Park and its two former gold mines, there is also a historical park. Next learn about dog sledding at the Iditarod Headquarters. It’s free! Visit with a dog team and go for a ride on one of Wasilla’s dog sledding tours. There is also canoeing available at the Nancy Lakes Recreation Area.**
**

**Seward**
You don’t need a car to go on this day trip away from Anchorage to the town of Seward. You can take the Alaska Railroad the 127 miles to Seward. Seward is the point where you can pick up a boat and sail all day in the glacier formed waters of the Kenai Fjords National Park. This park has breathtaking views of glaciers and wildlife. The only part of the park access able by car is Exit Glacier. You can walk the trails here and get quite close to a real glacier.
