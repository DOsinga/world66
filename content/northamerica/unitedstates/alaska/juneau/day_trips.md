---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

The Alaskan city of Juneau is surrounded by magnificent wonders of nature offering up once in a lifetime activities and sport adventures like no others in the world. From hiking near glaciers to kayaking through icey waters, the day trips available from Juneau are extensive, informative, and always exciting.

**Mendenhall Glacier**

Mendenhall Glacier is probably the most well-known and the most visited of all the nearby destinations. This large glacier is rapidly decreasing in size, however; it loses about 100 feet every year. You can easily take a trip to the glacier for the day as it's only 13 miles North of Juneau. Renting a car and driving is the best way to go. The Mendenhall Visitor Center is very nice, and very good for kids, with its panoramic vistas, large theater, interactive exhibits providing information about the glacier and the area, and diverse bookstore. In winter it is free to visit, but in summer you have to pay a $3 fee.

**Admiralty Island**

The island is only about 10 miles South of Juneau, and despite the fact that you have to take a plane to get there, it still makes for an incredible day trip. On Admiralty Island lush rainforest foliage is the norm and brown bears rule the area. In fact, it is on this island that the concentration of brown bears is the highest in the world. This 90 mile stretch of land has many activities available; you can hike through the rainforest, sea kayak, watch for brown bears, or visit Pack Creek, where you can see the bears eating salmon. The Admiralty Island National Monument has a route for canoeing, the most dense concentration of bald eagles on earth, public cabins, kayaking, humpback whale viewing, and sportfishing. Fees can range from $10 to $50 depending on which activities or tours you choose.

**Glacier Gardens**

Set on 50 acres of land, Glacier Gardens is a natural playground of ponds, waterfalls, atriums, gardens, and lookouts. This rainforest adventure is perfect for families. You can wander through the lush, beautiful, landscapes, check out the 580 foot tall overlook of the Mendenhall Glacier, stop by the cafe and gift shop, or visit the conservatory. Glacier Gardens costs $22 per person to enter, but the fee includes a guided tour as well. It is also conveniently located only 6.5 miles Northwest of Juneau, so it's very quick and easy to drive to.
