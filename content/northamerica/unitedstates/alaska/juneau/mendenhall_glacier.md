The Mendenhall Glacier is Juneau's most visited attraction -- a 13-mile-long river of ice flowing down from the 1,500-square-mile Juneau Icefield to a glacial lake just 13 miles from downtown. The Mendenhall Glacier Visitor Center (part of Tongass National Forest) has panoramic views, interactive exhibits, a theater, and a bookstore.

Several trails fan out from the visitor center. The Trail of Time nature walk is flat and easy, suitable for anyone. The Nugget Falls Trail is steeper but brings you to the foot of a thundering waterfall right beside the glacier face. For something more ambitious, the West Glacier Trail (about 7 miles round trip) follows the lake shore and gives you a closer look at the ice.

The glacier has been retreating visibly -- photos in the visitor center from just a few decades ago show how much ice has been lost. It is a striking and slightly sobering thing to see in person.
