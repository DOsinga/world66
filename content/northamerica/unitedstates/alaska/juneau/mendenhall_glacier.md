The Mendenhall Glacier is Juneau's most visited attraction — a 13-mile-long river of ice flowing down from the 1,500-square-mile Juneau Icefield to a glacial lake just 13 miles from downtown. The Mendenhall Glacier Visitor Center (part of Tongass National Forest) has panoramic views, interactive exhibits, a theater, and a bookstore. Several trails fan out from the visitor center, including the Trail of Time nature walk and the steep Nugget Falls Trail that brings you to the foot of a waterfall beside the glacier. In summer, entry costs $3; in winter it is free.
