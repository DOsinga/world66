A popular Juneau attraction is the Mendenhall Glacier. It is part of the Tongass National Forest located about 13 miles from downtown Juneau. The glacier is approximately 12 miles long and can be viewed from the visitor center. There are several programs that families can take part in to enjoy this beautiful place. For example, there are programs about the American rain forests, self-guided birdwatching, self-guided walk over the Trail of Time, projects on Prince of Wales Island, and migration science and mystery. For visitor hours, directions, and other information, check out their website at http://www.fs.fed.us/r10/tongass/districts/mendenhall/.
