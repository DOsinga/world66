The Alaska State Museum is the official repository of Alaska's history and culture, holding one of the most significant collections of Alaska Native art and artifacts in the world. Highlights include Aleut basketry, Tlingit and Athabascan objects, Russian-American colonial material, and natural history displays. The centrepiece of the ethnology gallery is a life-sized eagle tree populated with bald eagle specimens. The museum is located at 395 Whittier Street in downtown Juneau. Summer hours are 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; winter hours are 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., closed Monday and Sunday.
