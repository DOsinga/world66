Kenai is a small city on the western side of the Kenai Peninsula, sitting at the mouth of the Kenai River where it runs out into the cold grey waters of Cook Inlet. Two big volcanoes — Redoubt and Iliamna — rise straight out of the Inlet on the far side, and on clear days they dominate every view across the water. Most travellers pass through on the way to the peninsula's bigger draws (Kenai Fjords, Homer, the Russian and Kenai rivers), but the town is worth a short stop.

The old townsite above the beach has one of the oldest standing buildings in Alaska: the Holy Assumption of the Virgin Mary Russian Orthodox Church, a white-walled, onion-domed survivor of the Russian-American Company era. The bluffs nearby give long views over the river mouth and, in July, the bizarre spectacle of the Kenai River personal-use dipnet fishery, when thousands of Alaskans wade into the shallows with long-handled nets to fill freezers with salmon. The Kenai River itself is one of the best king and sockeye salmon rivers in the state and is the reason most anglers come here.
