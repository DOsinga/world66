Petersburg is the "Little Norway" of Alaska, a small fishing town on Mitkof Island in the Inside Passage. It was founded by a Norwegian immigrant and still celebrates its heritage with Norwegian Independence Day parades on May 17th and dances at the Sons of Norway Hall.

The town is surrounded by the Tongass National Forest, with glaciers, whales, and bears within reach by charter boat. LeConte Glacier, the southernmost tidewater glacier in North America, calves icebergs into Frederick Sound just to the north. In summer, humpback whales feed in the waters nearby. The commercial fishing fleet — salmon, halibut, shrimp — still dominates the harbour and the local economy.

Petersburg sees about 40,000 visitors a year, far fewer than Juneau or Ketchikan. It is a working town rather than a cruise port, and that is part of its appeal. The population hovers around 3,000, swelling in summer when the canneries hire seasonal workers.
