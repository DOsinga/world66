Tok sits at the junction of the Alaska Highway and the Tok Cut-Off, making it the first Alaskan town most overland travellers reach when driving up from the Lower 48. It is not a destination in itself — it is a gas station, a meal, and a bed at the crossroads of two long, empty highways.

From here you can head northwest to [Fairbanks](/northamerica/unitedstates/alaska/fairbanks), southwest toward [Anchorage](/northamerica/unitedstates/alaska/anchorage) via the Tok Cut-Off and Glenn Highway, or continue southeast toward the Canadian border. The Glenn Highway stretch from Tok to Anchorage passes through some of Alaska's most dramatic scenery, skirting the Wrangell–St. Elias range.

Tok has a visitor center, a handful of motels and diners, and very long winter nights. In summer the days barely end. Stop, refuel, and keep going — that is what Tok is for.
