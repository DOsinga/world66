Amelia Island is a 21-km barrier island at the northeasternmost tip of **Florida**, just south of the Georgia state line and about 50 km northeast of Jacksonville. It is the last island in the chain of Atlantic barrier islands that stretches all the way from North Carolina south, and it keeps a distinctly Old South feel — long sandy Atlantic beaches, salt marshes on the inland side, huge live oaks dripping with Spanish moss, and a beautifully preserved Victorian main street in its principal town of **Fernandina Beach**.

The island has flown eight different flags through its history (French, Spanish, British, the Patriots of Amelia Island, the Green Cross of Florida, Mexican rebels, the Confederacy, and finally the United States) — more than anywhere else in the country, and the reason for the "Isle of Eight Flags" nickname. Today it is a quiet upmarket beach resort with good seafood, a handful of grand old wooden hotels (the **Florida House Inn**, dating from 1857, claims to be the oldest tourist hotel in the state still operating), and one of the best preserved Civil War-era forts in the country at **Fort Clinch State Park** on the island's northern tip.
