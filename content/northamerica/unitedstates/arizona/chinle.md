Chinle is one of the largest communities on the Navajo Nation, in the northeast corner of Arizona near the New Mexico border. Almost everyone comes here for one reason: Canyon de Chelly National Monument, a stunning canyon system of red sandstone walls harbouring ancient Ancestral Puebloan cliff dwellings, rock art, and ruins.

The town itself is a service centre — a few motels, restaurants, trading posts, and a supermarket — but the canyon is extraordinary. Two deeply incised gorges, Canyon de Chelly and Canyon del Muerto, cut into the Defiance Plateau with sheer walls rising up to 300 metres. Navajo families still farm the canyon floor, and their fields and hogans are visible from the rim drive overlooks.

Most of the canyon interior requires a Navajo guide to enter, but the rim drives are self-guided and free. The South Rim Drive leads to Spider Rock Overlook, where a slender 240-metre sandstone spire rises from the canyon floor. The Thunderbird Lodge within the monument operates group tours by open-bed truck, and independent guides offer jeep and horseback trips.

The free Cottonwood Campground near the canyon mouth is a pleasant place to stay, shaded by old cottonwood trees. Come in autumn for the Navajo Nation Fair and the cooler temperatures.
