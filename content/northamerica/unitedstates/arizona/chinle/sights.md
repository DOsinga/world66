The big draw here is Canyon de Chelly. Plan on spending some time to get into the canyon and see it all. Otherwise, the area's scenery is nice, but no hiking or wandering is allowed on the Navajo Nation. There are nearby lakes up the road from Canyon de Chelly, and Navajo Route 12 is exceptionally scenic. Come for the fair in the fall as well, and check out the weekly swap meet in town.

### [Canyon de Chelly National Monument](/northamerica/unitedstates/arizona/chinle/sights/canyon_de_chelly_n)

This park features two deeply incised canyons that are full of ancient cliff dwellings, ruined pueblos, rock art, and other remnants of the glory of ancient Pueblo culture. The canyons are still occupied by Navajos; their fields are easily visible from the rim drive overlooks. Tours into the canyons are available from the Thunderbird Lodge within the park, or from other jeep or horseback concessioners. Get more information at the visitor center about this. Camping is also available within the park at the pleasant Cottonwood Campground.
