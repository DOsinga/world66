Kayenta is a small Navajo Nation town on US-163, deep in the red-rock country of northeastern Arizona. It's the main base for Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park, about 25 miles to the north — those sandstone mittens you've seen in a hundred Westerns. Without Kayenta, most visitors would have nowhere sensible to sleep before or after the park.

The town itself is functional rather than scenic: a handful of motels, a Basha's supermarket, a Sonic, a pizza place. But there are two things worth knowing about.

The first is the Code Talker Exhibit at the local Burger King. It sounds unlikely, but it's genuinely moving — photographs, artifacts, and the story of the Navajo men whose unbreakable language helped win the war in the Pacific. There are also reconstructed hogans in the parking lot with expanded exhibits. Admission is free.

The second is the Hampton Inn restaurant, which serves better-than-expected Navajo cooking and has a solid gift shop stocking locally made crafts. Both the Hampton Inn, the Holiday Inn, and the Burger King are owned by a Code Talker family.

If you're heading into Monument Valley, take a Navajo-guided tour rather than driving your own car. There are no paved roads in the valley — just rocks and sand that will punish a standard rental badly.
