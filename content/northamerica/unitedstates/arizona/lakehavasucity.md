Lake Havasu City sits on the eastern bank of Lake Havasu, a reservoir on the Colorado River at the Arizona-California border. The city is known primarily for two things: spring break and the London Bridge. The bridge is real — the original 1831 London Bridge was purchased by the city's founder Robert McCulloch in 1968, dismantled stone by stone, and reassembled here. It now spans a channel that was dug beneath it, connecting the mainland to a small island and creating the English Village, a tourist development with shops and restaurants in a vaguely British style.

The lake itself is the main draw for most visitors. Lake Havasu offers warm-weather water sports nearly year-round — boating, jet-skiing, swimming, and fishing for striped bass and catfish. The stretch of Arizona coastline along the lake is dotted with beaches and campgrounds. Parker Strip, to the south, is particularly popular in summer.

Spring break brings crowds from universities across the Southwest; if you are not coming for that scene, late autumn through early spring is a quieter and still-warm time to visit. Temperatures in summer regularly exceed 40°C (104°F), which even the most devoted boater has to factor into their plans.
