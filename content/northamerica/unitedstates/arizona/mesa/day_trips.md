---
title: "Day Trips in Mesa"
type: section
---

Mesa makes a great home base for some fantastic day trips. You don't even have to travel far to experience an entertaining trip within a trip; a lot of great destinations are situated only a half hour to an hour's drive away from Mesa. Here are just a few of many nice day trips.

**Tempe**

Obviously there are a variety of activities to do in Tempe, but one of the best is spending the day at the Tempe Beach Park. Covering 25 acres, this park is decades old and considered the "historical central park" of Tempe. It is located on Rio Salado, which is only a 15 minute drive away from Mesa. You can get there by car or bus. You can fish, bike, jog, boat, or have a picnic at the beach park, and there are 10k runs and festivals practically every weekend. There are also boat rentals and tours offered by the Rio Lago Cruise Company.

For the kids, the Cox Splash Playground is heaven on Earth. It is a free waterpark with all sorts of games and fun available to children of all ages. If you're on a family vacation in Mesa, this is a must-do day trip.

**Phoenix**

Phoenix is another eclectic city near Mesa, with a huge amount of things to do. Just one of them, is to explore the Desert Botanical Garden, which contains one of the best collections of desert plants in the world. The "Living Collection" holds 21,000 plants, 75% of which were collected and documented from the wild. You can see the Agave Collection, the Cactus Collection, the Conservation Collection, and the Opuntia Collection. Everything is well-maintained, impeccable really. It costs $15 for adults and $7.50 for students.

Another good way to spend a day is to climb the Summit Trail up to Piestewa Peak. You'll hike up 1,190 ft to get to this landmark of the Phoenix Mountains Preserve. You'll get a great workout and be rewarded with beautiful views of Phoenix. Be aware that many people hike this trail and no dogs or bikes are allowed.

Families with young kids should take a day trip to Rawhide Western Town and Steakhouse. Activities include horseback riding, stunt shows, gunfights, desert train rides, a petting ranch, gold panning, bullriding, and tons of other Old West-themed attractions. There is a small fee for each activity you choose to do. There's also a steakhouse which provides dinner and a show to end the day.
