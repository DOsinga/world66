Miami is a small copper mining town in Gila County, Arizona, sitting in the rugged hill country between Phoenix and the White Mountains. It is a classic Western boomtown — the copper mines that built it are largely dormant now, but the town retains a certain faded charm.

The old downtown has been partly renovated, with a handful of antique shops and galleries setting up in the historic storefronts along Sullivan Street. Miami and neighbouring Globe are often visited together, and the drive through the Final Pass mining district on US-60 is dramatic, with open-pit mines carved into the red and ochre hillsides. It makes a worthwhile stop on the scenic route between Phoenix and the eastern Arizona highlands.
