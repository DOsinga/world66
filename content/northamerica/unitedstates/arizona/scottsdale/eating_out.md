---
title: "Eating Out in Scottsdale"
type: section
---

As a former resident of Arizona,
I can attest to the following eateries in Scottsdale,
Arizona, consistently ranked on
Zagat’s best eateries. Take first-hand advice and check them out!

**American**

**White
Chocolate Grill**

Offering a varied selection of wood-fired grilled meats,
fish, salads, and sandwiches, White Chocolate Grill offers a sleek, modern
dining experience with its wood-paneled walls and lively bar. Appetizers range from
$8-$9 and the AZ Egg Rolls are not to miss! For brunch try the Crab Cake
Benedict ($13), Grilled Filet Benedict ($13) or the Roasted Mushroom Omelet
($10). The salads are colorful ($10) and sandwiches packed high ($8). For
dessert? Go for the famed White Chocolate Brownie with vanilla ice-cream. It’s
large enough to share!

**Asian**

**Malee’s Thai
Bistro**

This inviting bistro, a full-service upscale dining
experience, offers freshly prepared Thai food for lunch and dinner. Appetizers
range from $8-$14, salads begin at $11, and entrees range from $13-$21, with
many vegetarian choices available. Many come just for the Tom Ka Gai soup, famously
known by Scottsdale
residents. The food is neatly prepared and usually enough for two people,
including appetizers. Full bar available.

**Mexican**

**Manuel’s Mexican Bistro**

This family-owned 2009 Reader’s Choice bistro is worth a visit
solely for its tasty chicken mole and sizzling enchiladas. The chips are always
served hot and refilled during your meal. For just $9.99 you can get chicken or
beef fajitas and cheese enchiladas. And, of course, you don’t want to miss out
on Manuel’s famous margaritas, such as the blue sapphire ($7). Delicious appetizers, such as the nachos and
taquitos, range from $6-$8. Meals are reasonably priced ($9-$13) and the
specials, such as carne asada, are served with many sides

Discounts for seniors, and new specials offered weekly.

**Seafood**

**Mastros Ocean
Club**

Matros is the perfect location to take your sweetheart on
Valentine’s day or weekend night. While the restaurant may be very pricey, delicious food and excellent
views make the meal well worth it. You’re sure to hear some live music by local jazz musicians. Soups and appetizers, such as the lobster
bisque and Dungeness crab cocktail range from $11-$21. The main courses can run you upward $40, but
you won’t regret the dent in your wallet. The Sautéed Sea Scallops ($32) in eclectic
sauce, tasty Swordfish ($33), and Lobster Mashed Potatoes ($33) are not to be
missed. For dessert try the Chocolate Temptation Cake ($8) served with ice-ream.

**Steakhouse**

**Don &
Charlie’s**

A huge step up from the chain Outback Steakhouse but equally
affordable, Don and Charlie’s offers flavorful juicy steaks, burgers, chicken, prime-rib
and thick pork chops ($11-$40). Start your meal with the French fried onion
strings ($5) or shrimp salad ($14). Vegetarian? Try the French onion soup ($4)
and buttery garlic bread ($3). A selection of beers ($4), cocktails ($8) and martinis
($11) available. Consistently named in the top 20 of Zagat’s annual survey of
Southwestern restaurants, this is an experience not to miss!
