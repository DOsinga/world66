The remote northwestern corner of Arizona, Grand Canyon-Parashant is one of the least-visited national monuments in the country — no paved roads, no services, no crowds. It protects a vast stretch of canyon country adjacent to the Grand Canyon's North Rim. For serious off-roaders and backcountry hikers only.
