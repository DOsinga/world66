Tombstone is the most famous Wild West town in America, and it knows it. Founded in 1879 after Ed Schieffelin struck silver in the San Pedro Valley, the town exploded from nothing to 10,000 people in just a few years — making it the largest city between San Francisco and St. Louis. The mines flooded in 1882 and the boom went bust, but unlike most mining towns, Tombstone refused to disappear. Its motto, "The Town Too Tough to Die," is earned.

The main draw is Allen Street, a boardwalk-lined stretch of saloons, museums, and shops where costumed gunslingers stage daily shootouts. The [O.K. Corral](https://www.ok-corral.com/) — site of the famous 1881 gunfight between the Earps, Doc Holliday, and the Clanton-McLaury gang — is the centerpiece, but the whole historic district is worth walking. Boothill Graveyard sits on the edge of town, its headstones telling stories both tragic and darkly funny.

Tombstone can feel touristy, and it is — there is no getting around that. But it is also genuinely atmospheric, especially in the early morning or late afternoon when the crowds thin and the desert light hits the old adobe walls. The surrounding landscape is stark Sonoran Desert, with the Dragoon Mountains rising to the east. If you are driving between Tucson and Bisbee, Tombstone is an easy and worthwhile stop. Give it a half day at minimum.
