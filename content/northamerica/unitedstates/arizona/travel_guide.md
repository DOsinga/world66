This **Grand Canyon Travel Guide **gives you tips for traveling to the Grand Canyon National Park, one of the most unforgettable destinations in the world.

Grand Canyon National Park, located in the northern part of Arizona, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts large crowds of visitors every year. The Colorado River has carved the canyon during millions of years and the canyon is over 1600 meters deep. No photographs can do justice to the beauty of Grand Canyon and the only way to experience it is to visit the place.

**How to Visit the Canyon**

The Canyon can be visited from the North Rim or the South Rim. The South Rim is easier to get to and receives more tourists, and the North Rim is quieter and more remote.

The South Rim welcomes visitors throughout the year, and while the summer season is the busiest, the winter months give the opportunity to see the canyon covered in snow. You can only drive to the North Rim between the middle of May and the middle of October, depending on snowfall.

**Things to See and Do**

Tourist attractions and amenities around the South Rim include the Grand Canyon Village, the Desert View watchtower that offers a very good view over this part of the canyon, and Hermits Rest, a rest area and a gift shop. The North Rim is less crowded and more peaceful. Its viewpoints at the Bright Angel Point and at the Cape Royal offer a chance to see some fantastic views with less people around.

There are countless hiking trails around the Grand Canyon for all levels of hikers. You can simply take a relaxed stroll along the edge of the canyon around the viewpoints, or you hike for days or even weeks. The Bright Angel Trail is one of the most famous trails, and you can plan a long hike that lasts for days or explore just a part of this trail for a few hours. For the most adventurous, a whitewater rafting expedition along the Colorado River is an experience of a lifetime.

**How to Get There by Car**

You can drive to the South Rim from the south on Route 64/highway 180 or from the east along AZ64. Driving to the North Rim, follow ALT US89 to AZ67. There is a $25.00 entrance fee for drivers to the National Park, but this is valid for seven days. Walkers or cyclists pay $12.00.
