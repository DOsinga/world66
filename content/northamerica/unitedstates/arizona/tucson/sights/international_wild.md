The International Wildlife Museum promotes the study of wildlife and wildlife management conservation. The museum opened its doors in 1988 and houses over 400 species of mammals and insect as well as birds from all over the world. There are dioramas in the museum that illustrate wild animals in their very own natural habitats. These realistic creations are perfect for understanding how these animals live and breathe in the world. In addition, there are videos, computers and other exhibits that allow you to get involved with the work that is being done at the Wildlife museum. The International Wildlife Museum is a great Tucson attraction.
