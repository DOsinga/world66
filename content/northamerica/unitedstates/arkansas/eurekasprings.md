Eureka Springs is a quirky Victorian resort town tucked into the Ozark Mountains of northwest Arkansas. The entire downtown is on the National Register of Historic Places — winding streets follow the contours of the hills, lined with ornate Victorian buildings that house galleries, restaurants, and shops. There are no traffic lights in town.

The town was founded in 1879 around natural springs believed to have healing properties, and it quickly became a fashionable health resort. That era's architecture survives remarkably intact. Today Eureka Springs draws visitors for its arts scene, live music, outdoor activities, and general eccentricity — the town has a thriving community of artists, musicians, and independent spirits.

The surrounding Ozark landscape offers canoeing and kayaking on the Buffalo River and other waterways, cave tours, hiking and mountain biking trails, and Beaver Lake for swimming and fishing. In winter, bald eagles gather along the lake.
