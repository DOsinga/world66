What all is there to do? Visitors better plan on staying several days because Eureka Springs has such a wide variety of fun things to see and do!

**"Man-made" Attractions**

Every night at dusk from April through October, with the exception of Sundays and Wednesdays, The Great Passion Play is presented. America's #1 outdoor religious drama, it features a cast of 250 local people retelling the story of Jesus Christ's last days on earth, complete with horse-drawn chariots, camels and a flock of sheep. Some travelers have made this presentation a part of their yearly pilgrimage to Eureka Springs.

When it is time to see and appreciate all the man-made attractions that Eureka Springs has to offer, the choices are simply outstanding! Jambories, Hoedowns, and pure magic -- Lions,Tigers, and Bears oh, my! Dinosaurs, aviaries of exotic birds, and ghosts can also be found in Eureka Springs.

Eureka Spings also has trains! Authentic engines pull passenger cars for riding and dining. There is also a complete model railroad for up-close viewing. Great ever-running trollies, narrated tram tours and horse-drawn carriage rides will show visitors the finest Victorian architecture, sumptuous homes and an award- winning glass chapel in the woods.

How about some golf? There are 18 holes for the serious player and the miniature version for the vacation enthusiast. And before going out for a night on the town, visitors should treat themselves to a healing massage in one of many therapeutic spas. Relax, and enjoy all that is Eureka Springs. It's distinctively different!

**"Natural" Attractions**

For the out-of-doors, "Natural State" kind of visitor, there are rivers to paddle in kayaks and canoes, caves to explore (wear good-gripping shoes!). Hiking, biking and horse-riding trails. Lakes for swimming in, skiing, fishing and sails. Gardens and springs in abundant flower, for the really brave, climb up a bluff or an observation tower! Eagles in winter, maples in fall, spring or summer -- Eureka Springs has it all!

**Nightlife**

[Nightlife in Eureka Springs](/northamerica/unitedstates/arkansas/eurekasprings/nightlife_and_ente) sparkles and shines so bright one can make memories that won't fade away. Fabulous clubs and eclectic restaurants are full of great music and delectable food. Eureka Springs has a wonderful variety of shows (matinee and evening) with hysterically funny comics and outstanding local musicians.

The newly renovated City Auditorium brings world-class entertainers, from John Prine, Willie Nelson, Merle Haggard & Kathy Mattea to Buddy Guy, Ray Charles, Ani DiFranco and Bela Fleck, into an intimate 1928 music hall. Visitors can call 888-855-7823 for more information on upcoming events.
