Fort Smith sits at the confluence of the Arkansas and Poteau rivers, right on the Oklahoma border. Arkansas’s second-largest city has a frontier past that’s hard to shake — this was the last outpost of law before Indian Territory, where "Hanging Judge" Isaac Parker held court and deputy marshals rode out after outlaws. That roughness has mellowed into a mid-sized city with a walkable downtown, a growing restaurant scene, and enough history to fill a solid weekend.

The Fort Smith National Historic Site is the main draw, preserving the remains of two frontier-era forts and Judge Parker’s courtroom and jail. The Trail of Tears passed through here, and the site does a thoughtful job covering that painful history alongside the marshals-and-outlaws story. Beyond the historic site, the Riverfront area along the Arkansas River is good for walking and cycling, and Garrison Avenue downtown has a decent mix of local restaurants and shops. The city also sits at the western terminus of the Razorback Greenway’s sister trail, the River Valley Trail, which follows the Arkansas River eastward.
