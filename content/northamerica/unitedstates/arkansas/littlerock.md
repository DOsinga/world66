Little Rock is the capital of [Arkansas](/northamerica/unitedstates/arkansas) and a city whose history runs deeper than its modest size suggests. Much of what is worth seeing is concentrated in the Quapaw Quarter, a nine-square-mile area encompassing the central business district and the oldest residential neighbourhoods. The name comes from the Quapaw Indians who lived in central Arkansas before white settlers arrived in the early nineteenth century.

The **MacArthur Park Historic District** holds buildings dating from before the Civil War. The **Arkansas Arts Center** houses one of the most significant collections of works on paper in the country, alongside paintings by Monet and Degas. Nearby, the **Decorative Arts Museum** occupies the antebellum Pike-Fletcher-Terry House, and the **Villa Marre**, an elegant Victorian home from 1881, is open to visitors on weekday mornings and Sunday afternoons.

The **Governor's Mansion Historic District** is a fine collection of Queen Anne, Colonial Revival, and Craftsman houses dating from roughly 1880 to 1920. They are private homes, best enjoyed from the sidewalk, though the Quapaw Quarter Association opens several each year during the Spring Tour in May and Christmas Open Houses in December.

The **Clinton Presidential Library** sits on the south bank of the Arkansas River. The **Old State House Museum** and the **Historic Arkansas Museum** round out the civic attractions. For evening entertainment, the **River Market District** has restaurants and bars that keep going late, and **Main Street** has live music venues worth seeking out.
