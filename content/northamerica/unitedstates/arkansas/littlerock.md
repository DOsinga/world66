Little Rock is the capital of [Arkansas](/northamerica/unitedstates/arkansas) and the state's largest city, sitting on the south bank of the Arkansas River at roughly the geographic center of the state. It is a compact, walkable city that punches above its weight culturally — home to a presidential library, a serious art museum, and a historic district that survived urban renewal largely intact.

The heart of what visitors come to see is the Quapaw Quarter, a nine-square-mile swath that takes in Little Rock's central business district and its oldest residential neighborhoods. Named after the Quapaw people who lived in central Arkansas before European settlement, it is most closely associated with two historic districts: MacArthur Park and the Governor's Mansion area. Together they hold the densest concentration of pre-Civil War and Victorian-era architecture in the state. The streets are lined with Queen Anne cottages, Colonial Revival townhouses, and Craftsman bungalows that serve as private homes and businesses rather than museum pieces — which gives the neighborhood a lived-in quality you don't find in places that have been too thoroughly preserved.

The Clinton Presidential Library anchors the River Market District on the riverfront. Whether you admire the man or not, it's a well-designed building with an honest account of the Clinton years, and the adjacent park makes a good place to watch the river. The Old State House Museum, the Historic Arkansas Museum, and the Arkansas Arts Center — which holds one of the country's better collections of works on paper alongside paintings by Monet and Degas — are all within easy reach of each other downtown. The River Market District also has the best concentration of restaurants and bars in the city, with live music spilling onto the street most weekend nights.
