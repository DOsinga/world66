**Great Photography**

Just the full color photographs
of the United States on the Road Insight Guide make it worth every
penny. Not only do they provide great context for the essays from local
writers, but also give you some great ideas on what kind of pictures to
take when visiting those destinations. The joint work with the
Discovery Channel in developing this book pays off, with contributions from some great Discovery Channel photographers.

**Off the Beaten Path**

This
guide provides a refreshing approach to road trips in the United States
by focusing on off-the-beaten-path trails and travel suggestions from
local writers that actually live on those locations. The stories about
Route 66 are entertaining, and the listings of hotels, restaurants and
attractions on it is helpful for trip planning.

**From Arizona to Los Angeles**

One
of my favorites parts of the book was the description of the road trip
from Arizona to Los Angeles, because it combines historic sites, little
known facts about the transportation revolution in the United States
and how relevant California was in these revolutions, and a list of
great roadside eateries. Also, the guide does provide some less
touristy insights about Los Angeles and how to enjoy a stay in the City
of Angels on a budget.

**
A Bit Bulky**One downside to this guide is that
it is a bit bulky. Weighing close to 3 pounds and having more than 450
pages, this guide is not easy to carry around. However, it is a good
idea to either keep it in the glove compartment or have it at home to
plan a road trip in advance.
