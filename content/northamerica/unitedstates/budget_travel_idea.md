---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

The budget travel market in the U.S. is rapidly opening up, and
today there are several little-known and enjoyable holiday ideas that
don’t necessarily cost the moon. Here are some tips to make the
most of your United States holiday at low prices.

**Cheap Airfare**

One of the cheapest and most common ways to cover the huge distances
within the USA is to travel by air. Almost all airlines operating in
the states offer slashed prices, heavy discounts and package deal
bargains on tickets booked in advance. There are round-trip fares that
sometimes include packages with hotel stays and meal vouchers.

It's
best for budget travelers to book their flights in advance. Make sure
that there are Saturday night inclusions in a round trip
itinerary, as most airlines offer a free weekend stay with round
tickets or even one-way flights, though the former works out to be
cheaper. Research the various travel websites on the net to bag the
best deals and cheapest fares available, and try a combination of dates
to check out the lowest prices.

**Economy Accommodation**

Sometimes it's hard to find an economy lodging option in
the states, but there are some really good campsites at state and
national parks in the rural belts. The campsites come with basic grill
cooking facilities, space for tents, toilets and showers and
at times amenities like warm bathing water. Visitors can rent campsites
from $5-$20 and more depending on the features and amenities. Exclusive
campsites that cost slightly more may even have a swimming pool and
laundry facility. Camping in the midst of America’s wilderness and
natural beauty is one of the cheapest and best holiday ideas in
the states.

Another economy lodging option is the dormitories of large
universities in summer, when the sessions break for the current academic
year. Visitors can inquire with the housing office of the university to
check availability. If you want a relatively comfortable (albeit a
little higher priced) option than the others, try the YMCA and YWCA
hostels-most come with clean rooms, showers and at times meals.

**Budget Eating**

For budget eating, consider picking up packed take-away meals from
drive-in eateries and other small street side vendors selling hamburgers, doughnuts, sandwiches and a range of hot beverages.
In addition to these, there are several vending machines that dole out
cheap munchies and chocolate bars to subside the hunger. Popular fast
food chains and coffee bars are also great places to eat huge burgers,
rolls and sandwiches for less.

Contrary to its pricey image, the United States has an array of
great budget holiday ideas that can help tourists cut costs, without
cutting the fun.
