Arcata is a small, progressive college town on Humboldt Bay in Northern California, home to Cal Poly Humboldt (formerly Humboldt State University). The students give the town a youthful energy, and the politics lean firmly left — this is a place of food co-ops, bicycle activism, and earnest environmental commitment.

Life centres on the Arcata Plaza, a historic square lined with locally owned shops and restaurants. The Saturday farmers market on the plaza is lively, with fresh produce, live music, and a strong community atmosphere. For nature, the Arcata Marsh and Wildlife Sanctuary is a pioneering constructed wetland that doubles as a wastewater treatment system and birdwatching site. The Arcata Community Forest offers trails through towering coast redwoods and dense ferns, minutes from town. Uncrowded beaches are a short drive away.

Arcata is about 280 miles north of San Francisco, or reachable by a short flight into the Arcata-Eureka airport. It makes a good base for exploring the redwood coast.
