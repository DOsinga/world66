Big Sur is a 90-mile stretch of [California's](/northamerica/unitedstates/california) central coast where the Santa Lucia Mountains rise abruptly from the Pacific. Highway 1 clings to the cliffs between San Simeon and Carmel, crossing famous bridges — Bixby Creek Bridge is the most photographed — and passing through groves of redwoods that somehow thrive in the fog.

There is no town of Big Sur in any conventional sense. Instead, a handful of lodges, campgrounds, and restaurants are scattered along the highway. The appeal is the landscape itself: sea stacks and coves far below, condors circling overhead, and the feeling of being at the edge of the continent. Pfeiffer Beach, Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park (with its waterfall dropping onto the sand), and the hiking trails through the Ventana Wilderness are the main draws.
