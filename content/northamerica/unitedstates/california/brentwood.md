Brentwood sits in the eastern shadow of Mount Diablo, an hour's drive from San Francisco where the delta waterways meet the rolling hills of the Central Valley. It is farm country first — known nationally for its sweet corn and for the Harvest Time farm trail, where more than thirty farms and ranches let you pick your own apricots, peaches, cherries, pears, and nectarines straight from the tree.

The town has grown fast, with new housing spreading across what were recently orchards, but the revitalised Old Downtown still has the feel of a small agricultural community: boutique shops, a handful of good restaurants, and a pace that rewards slowing down. Every July, just after Independence Day, Brentwood throws Cornfest — a family-friendly celebration of the corn harvest with food vendors from across the state.

If you are visiting the San Francisco Bay Area and want a taste of Central Valley agriculture without the long drive south, Brentwood is an easy day trip.
