The Santa Barbara Arts and Craft Show is a collection of booths selling local artists' goods that spans along the sidewalk parallel to the beach downtown. One can find anything from pottery to handmade yo-yos to landscape paintings to kaliedescopes. Everything is one of a kind and made somewhere around Santa Barbara County. The artists are eager to engage in conversation and tell you about their craft, so even if you are not planning on buying anything, you can still get a feel for the Santa Barbara artists' experiences, ranging from "that time I burnt my hand glass-blowing" to rock hunting excursions in the Santa Barbara back-country.

The SB Arts and Craft Show happens every Sunday from ten in the morning until dusk, except for on certain holdays. It is near many other Santa Barbara spots, including the zoo and the pier, which houses the sea center and other attractions. The calender showing all days that the SB Arts and Craft Show does not occur can be viewed in full on the official website.
