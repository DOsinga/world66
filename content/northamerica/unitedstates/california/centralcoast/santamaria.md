Santa Maria sits in a broad agricultural valley on California's Central Coast, halfway between Los Angeles and San Francisco along Highway 101. The town is best known for its barbecue tradition — tri-tip and pinquito beans cooked over red oak coals, a style that dates back to the nineteenth-century rancho era and remains the centerpiece of local cooking.

The Santa Maria Valley is also serious wine country. The Foxen Canyon Wine Trail winds through rolling hills planted with Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, and the region's cool maritime climate produces some of California's most distinctive wines. In spring the valley floor is covered with strawberry fields and flower farms, and the annual Strawberry Festival draws crowds from up and down the coast.

Santa Maria is not a destination most travellers plan around, but if you are driving the 101 it is well worth a stop — for the barbecue alone.
