One of the best-preserved Rancho-era adobes in California, built in the 1840s by Don raymundo Olivas and operated today as a history museum. The house is furnished in period style; costumed docents do living-history demonstrations on weekends. In summer, the courtyard hosts a concert series running from blues to jazz to country. At 4200 Olivas Park Drive, adjacent to the Olivas Park Golf Course.
