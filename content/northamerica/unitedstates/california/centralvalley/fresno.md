Fresno sits in the heart of California's San Joaquin Valley, surrounded by some of the most productive farmland on earth. It's not a glamorous city, but it has real character — a working agricultural hub that also happens to be the gateway to the Sierra Nevada and [Yosemite National Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/yosemite), just 60 miles to the north on Highway 41.

Stop by a roadside fruit stand and try the figs, sweet grapes, and strawberries. Summers are brutally hot — temperatures regularly climb past 100°F — but evenings cool down, and winters are mild and rainy, rarely dropping below freezing.

The city has a decent collection of museums and gardens, including the quirky Forestiere Underground Gardens, a subterranean complex hand-dug over 40 years as an escape from the valley heat. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, History, and Science anchors the cultural scene, while turn-of-the-century homes like the 1889 Meux Home and the 1906 Kearney Mansion offer a window into the valley's agricultural past.

In late winter and early spring, the Fresno Blossom Trail is worth a detour — a driving or cycling route through orchards ablaze with pink, white, and crimson blossoms. Skiing is available in the Sierras to the east at Sierra Summit and Badger Pass. The road to Shaver Lake climbs from near sea level to 4,000 feet in under an hour, with views to match.
