### [Crossroads of Calfornia Street Fair](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/centralvalley/manteca/festivals/crossroads_of_calf)

Nearly 500 vendors along with entertainers have helped attract upwards of 50,000 people annually to downtown manteca during the last 12 years. It takes place in downtown Manteca with its end-of-the-19th-century street lights and motif as well as a Library Park with stately sycamore trees, town square gazebo and interactive water features with a unique ground design that depicts the area's Indian, agricultural, and farming heritage.



tel: 209-823-7229

url: [www.visitmanteca.org](http://www.visitmanteca.org)

date: First weekend in April

address: Downtown

### [Manteca Pumpkin Fair](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/centralvalley/manteca/festivals/manteca_pumpkin_fa)

Manteca is the largest pumpkin growing region in the Western United States if not the nation. Fields around Manteca supply over 80 percent of California's Halloween pumpkins plus tons that go to other nearby states. The Sunrise Kiwanis conducts a street fair featuring over 300 vendors with three stages of entertainment.



tel: 1-800-872-6546

url: [www.sunrisekiwanis.org](http://www.sunrisekiwanis.org)

date: First weekend in October

### [Almond Blossom Festival](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/centralvalley/manteca/festivals/almond_blossom_fes)

Ripon is a quaint community just six miles to the south of Manteca where the 52nd annual event takes place. Manteca--along with Ripon and Escalon--is a major almond growing region. The last several weeks of February fill the air with the sweet scent of almond blossoms followed by a week where the ground in over 20,000 acres appear as if it has been snowing as the blossoms float to earth. The Almond Blossom Festival features small-town joys such as church-baked cream puffs, diaper derby, prade, dances, dinners, a carnival and more.



tel: 209-599-7519

url: [www.riponchamber.org](http://www.riponchamber.org)

### [Tule Fog Fete](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/centralvalley/manteca/festivals/tule_fog_fete)

The event as Caswell State Memorial Park six miles south of Manteca at the end of Austin road along the Stanislaus River celebrates the end of a unique San Joaquin Valley phenomenon--the tule fog season. The valley's geographic location along with soil types creates conditions after rain followed by day, cold temperatures that literally generate fog from the ground. The Great Valley Museum celebrates the end of the season with a festival at the state park featuring music, tours and displays.
