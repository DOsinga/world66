Porterville is an agricultural town in California's southern Central Valley, sitting on the Tule River where the citrus and dairy country of Tulare County runs up against the foothills of the Sierra Nevada. It is mostly a working town serving the orchards and packing houses around it, but for travellers it is the closest valley base to the southern reaches of the **[Sequoia National Forest](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/highsierra/sequoia)** and the **Giant Sequoia National Monument**: from town it is about an hour's drive up Highway 190 into the **Trail of 100 Giants** sequoia grove. The downtown has a small Zalud House Museum (a preserved 1890s home) and a few decent taquerias on Main Street, but the real reason to stop here is to fuel up before heading into the mountains.
