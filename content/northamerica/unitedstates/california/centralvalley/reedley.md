Reedley is a small farming town in the heart of the San Joaquin Valley, about 25 miles southeast of Fresno. Known locally as the "World's Fruit Basket," it sits at the edge of some of the most productive agricultural land in California, surrounded by orchards and vineyards that produce peaches, nectarines, plums, and grapes.

Despite its modest size, Reedley has a compact downtown with a handful of museums and cultural spots worth a stop if you are passing through the Central Valley. The historic Opera House and the local museum offer a glimpse into the town's agricultural heritage, and the Mennonite community — descended from immigrants who settled here in the 19th century — gives the town a quietly distinctive character.
