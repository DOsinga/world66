---
title: "Things to do in Reedley"
type: section
---

Reedley's small downtown offers a few worthwhile stops, all within easy walking distance of each other on and around G Street.

The **Mennonite Quilting Center** (1012 G Street) is the town's best-known attraction, where skilled artisans create and display intricate quilts. The **Reedley Museum** (1752 10th Street) covers the town's agricultural and immigrant history. The restored **Reedley Opera House** (1720 10th Street) hosts community events and performances. On the outskirts of town, **Watson's Antique Farm Equipment Museum** (5716 S. Frankwood) is a quirky collection of old machinery that tells the story of Central Valley farming.

On the third Thursday of every month, the **Kings River Arts Council** organizes ArtStop, a hop along G Street where local businesses display original artwork and host artist receptions from 5 to 8pm. Parking is free and there are several restaurants and a pub along the street.
