Stockton is a large city in California's Central Valley, sitting at the eastern edge of the Sacramento–San Joaquin River Delta. It is not a tourist destination in the conventional sense, but the surrounding delta — a maze of waterways, levees, and small towns — is one of the most distinctive landscapes in the state.

The delta offers superb boating and fishing. Rent a houseboat and explore the waterways past the old towns of Locke (a surviving Chinese-American settlement from the 1920s), Isleton, and Walnut Grove. Striped bass fishing is good year-round, and salmon and steelhead run in the autumn. The Grizzly Island Wildlife Area near Suisun City is excellent for birdwatching.

Stockton's Miracle Mile district on Pacific Avenue has a cluster of restaurants, pubs, and small shops. The city has strong Mexican and Chinese food scenes — look for Il Grullenese on Charter Way for Mexican, or explore the restaurants near downtown for Chinese.
