Visalia is the largest city in the Central Valley between Fresno and Bakersfield, and one of the oldest in the San Joaquin Valley. It's the kind of place travelers pass through on their way to Sequoia and Kings Canyon national parks — but it's worth slowing down for. Downtown has a walkable main street, a handful of good restaurants, and a historic Fox Theatre that's been restored to its 1930s glory.

The city sits in Tulare County, deep in California's agricultural heartland. Orange groves, vineyards, and dairy farms surround it on all sides. That agricultural identity shapes the food culture — farm-to-table here isn't a trend, it's just how things work. The Saturday farmers market downtown draws locals who've been shopping there for decades.

For the parks-bound traveler, Visalia offers a practical base: a night or two here before heading into the mountains lets you stock up, eat well, and visit Mooney Grove Park — where a replica of James Earle Fraser's famous "End of the Trail" bronze stands in a shady oak grove on the edge of the city. The Kaweah Oaks Preserve, a remnant of the valley oak woodland that once covered the valley floor, is worth an hour of your time if you can manage it.
