A 322-acre remnant of the valley oak woodland that once blanketed the San Joaquin Valley floor. Seven miles east of downtown Visalia via Highway 198, the preserve protects ancient valley oaks, cottonwoods, and willows along the Kaweah River. There are three miles of easy walking trails — it's quiet, shaded, and genuinely beautiful. A good stop if you're heading toward the national parks and want to stretch your legs somewhere that feels wild rather than agricultural.
