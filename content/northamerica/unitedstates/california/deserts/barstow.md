Barstow sits at the crossroads of Interstates 15 and 40 in the heart of the Mojave Desert — most people blow through on their way to Las Vegas, but the town rewards a stop. It grew up around the railroad and the mines, and the beautifully restored Casa del Desierto (a 1911 Harvey House) at the train station is worth seeing for its architecture alone.

The real draw is the surrounding desert. **Rainbow Basin**, about 8 miles north, has striking multi-coloured rock formations and fossil beds. **Calico Ghost Town**, a restored 1880s silver-mining camp to the east, is touristy but fun. And Barstow is one of the closest gateway towns to the **Mojave National Preserve**, a vast expanse of Joshua trees, volcanic cinder cones, and sand dunes. The **Mojave River Valley Museum** in town does a good job of covering the area's natural and human history.
