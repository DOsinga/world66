Folsom is a small city on the American River, about 32 kilometres east of Sacramento, that carries two very different identities. One is a prosperous Sacramento suburb with a pleasant historic downtown; the other is the Folsom State Prison, made immortal by Johnny Cash, who recorded his celebrated live album there in January 1968 in front of an audience of inmates. Both identities are real, and both are worth exploring.

The old part of town clusters around Sutter Street, a Gold Rush–era main drag lined with stone and brick buildings that survived the boom and bust of the 1850s. Folsom was an important stop on the Pony Express and one of the first places in California to receive hydroelectric power, generated by the American River just outside town. The river still defines the place: Folsom Lake Reservoir, held back by Folsom Dam, stretches north of the city and draws swimmers, kayakers, and cyclists to its shores.

Folsom is an easy day trip from Sacramento, or a useful overnight stop if you are following the Gold Country corridor south toward [Auburn](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/goldcountry/auburn) or [Placerville](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/goldcountry/placerville).
