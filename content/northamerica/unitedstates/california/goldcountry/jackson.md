Jackson is the county seat of Amador County in California's Gold Country, a small town in the Sierra Nevada foothills east of Sacramento. The main street has antique shops and a 19th-century feel. The Amador County Museum, housed in a period home, covers the region's mining history.

East of town, Indian Grinding Rock State Historic Park preserves a large outcrop of bedrock mortars used by Mi-Wuk people — one of the finest examples in North America. Nearby Sutter Creek is worth a stop for its historic buildings and antique shops. The Shenandoah Valley wine region, a short drive away, has grown considerably and produces good Zinfandel.
