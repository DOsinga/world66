Mariposa is a small Gold Rush town in the Sierra Nevada foothills, best known as the western gateway to Yosemite National Park — the valley is about an hour's drive east on Highway 140. The town makes a practical base for Yosemite visits, particularly in summer when accommodation inside the park fills up fast.

The Mariposa County Courthouse, built in 1854, is the oldest continuously operating courthouse in California. The California State Mining and Mineral Museum, at the county fairgrounds, houses an impressive collection of gems, minerals, and gold specimens including a 13-pound crystalline gold piece. The Mariposa Museum and History Center covers the local mining era with a miner's cabin, gold-panning demonstrations, and a reconstructed Native American village.
