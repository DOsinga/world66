Murphys is one of the Gold Country's most appealing small towns — a tree-lined main street of stone buildings, boutique wineries, and a hotel whose guest log once held the signatures of Ulysses S. Grant, Mark Twain, and the outlaw Black Bart. It has just enough to fill a day or two without ever feeling overrun.

The town sits at 2,171 feet in the Sierra Nevada foothills, which gives it a gentler climate than the valley floor and surrounds it with ponderosa pines. Wineries and tasting rooms cluster along Main Street and the back roads. Head east on Highway 4 toward Arnold and you'll reach Calaveras Big Trees State Park, where the giant sequoias are among the largest living things on earth.
