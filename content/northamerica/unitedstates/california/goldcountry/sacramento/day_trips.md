**Old Town Sacramento**

Located
along the Sacramento River is this 28-acre
State Historic Park known as Old Town Sacramento. Visiting this notable gem of
a town will take you back several decades as you enter the era of what is known as the “gold rush days.” There is plenty to see and do in this
little charming historic district. The California State
Railroad Museum
is located here, right on the river’s edge. Here you can learn about the
history of California’s
railways through exhibits, short films and more. It’s a hot-spot for families
and children enjoy visiting this museum because there is a miniature railway
system for them to play on. There are plenty of unique shops and restaurants
here and the original façade of the original historic buildings makes for
awesome photos. You can even enjoy dinner aboard the Hornblower-a dinner cruise
that sets sail from Old Town Sacramento and floats throughout the Sacramento River as you enjoy a meal. Old Town Sacramento
is a must-see when visiting the State’s capital and is an enjoyable way of
“living California’s
past.”

**Sutter’s Fort State
Historical Park**

John
Sutter, a Swiss immigrant, came to Sacramento in
1839 and established a little empire in Sacramento
which he named New Helvetia (New Switzerland). He gained a great reputation for
housing weary travelers and soon, New Helvetia became a destination for California’s early
immigrants. Sutter’s properties became overrun by gold-seekers during the gold
rush and the Fort is all that remains of New Helvetia. It is currently open to
the public for a small fee. There are plenty of exhibits showcasing some of the
Fort’s original remains and artifacts. There is even a live demonstration
involving blowing up a cannon during certain seasons. It’s a great place for children
and adults alike and is very educational. Self-guided audio tours are available
here.

**California State**** Capitol**

A visit to California’s state
capital is not complete without visiting the California State Capitol and Museum.
Admission is free and tours are
available hourly. The guided tours are very informative and will take you
throughout the public areas of the Capitol building. You might even get a
chance to enter either the Senate or Assembly’s quarters if they are not in
session. The architecture and art work displayed throughout this building is
magnificent. If you’re lucky, you may even have a run-in with the governor.

**Fairytale Town**

If you’ve
got children in tow, be sure to visit Fairytale Town.
Adjacent to William
Land Park
is this 2.5 acre town of whimsical attractions and most importantly,
story-tellers. You can visit different states and watch your little one’s
imagination float as he or she listens to stories such as Alice in Wonderland. Inspired by Mother Goose
Rhymes and fairy tale stories, this park comes to live with its lively
displays. From Robin Hood’s Sherwood Forest to Peter Pan’s pirate ship, adults
will find themselves having a great time as well because in Fairytale Town,
everything comes to life!
