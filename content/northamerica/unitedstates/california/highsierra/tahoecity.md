Tahoe City sits on the northwest shore of [Lake Tahoe](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/highsierra/laketahoe), where the Truckee River begins its run toward Nevada. It is the quieter, more laid-back alternative to South Lake Tahoe — a small mountain town with a handful of good restaurants, a pebble beach, and easy access to some of the Sierra's best skiing and hiking.

The Gatekeeper's Museum, an authentic 19th-century log cabin once used by the lake dam's keepers, is worth a quick visit. Fanny Bridge, where the Truckee River flows out of the lake, draws crowds who lean over the railing to watch enormous trout in the clear water below. In summer the river is popular for rafting, and the Tahoe Rim Trail passes through town for hikers heading into the backcountry.
