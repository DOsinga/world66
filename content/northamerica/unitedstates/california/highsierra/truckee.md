Truckee is a mountain town in the Sierra Nevada of northern California, sitting at about 1,800 metres near the Nevada border. It began as a railroad and logging settlement in the 1860s — the Central Pacific Railroad pushed through Donner Pass just south of town, and the rail yard and Victorian-era commercial buildings along Commercial Row still define the historic centre. Today it is better known as a gateway to Lake Tahoe, the Tahoe ski resorts, and the High Sierra wilderness.

The downtown is genuine rather than manufactured, with the old wooden storefronts now housing good restaurants, independent shops, and a year-round travellers' energy. Winter brings skiers heading for Northstar, Squaw Valley, and Sugar Bowl — the Truckee area has some of the best and most varied skiing in California. Summer brings hikers, mountain bikers, and swimmers heading for the Truckee River and the lake.

Just south of town, the Donner Memorial State Park commemorates the Donner Party, the 1846–47 emigrant group who were trapped in the Sierra Nevada winter and resorted to cannibalism to survive. The park museum tells the story without flinching. The pass itself, now carrying I-80, is a dramatic piece of landscape regardless of its grim history.
