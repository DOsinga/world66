Museums, shrines, and points of historical interest in Redlands.

**Kimberly Crest House and Gardens** -- Modeled after a French chateau and built in 1897, Kimberly Crest has been lived in by only three families. The Magic Castle in Hollywood is an exact mirror image. Tours run Thursday through Sunday, 1:00-4:00 PM. 1325 Prospect Drive. Tel: 909-792-2111. [kimberlycrest.org](http://www.kimberlycrest.org/)

**Lincoln Memorial Shrine** -- Built in 1932 by philanthropists Robert and Alma Watchorn as a tribute to Lincoln and a memorial to their son who died in World War I. Designed by Pasadena architect Elmer Grey, it houses a Carrara marble bust of Lincoln by sculptor George Grey Barnard. Behind the A.K. Smiley Public Library, 125 West Vine Street. Open Tuesday through Sunday, 1:00-5:00 PM. Tel: 909-798-7632. [lincolnshrine.org](http://www.lincolnshrine.org/)

**San Bernardino County Museum** -- A regional museum with exhibits in cultural and natural history, an Exploration Station live-animal discovery center, and extensive research collections. 2024 Orange Tree Lane. Open Tuesday through Sunday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Tel: 909-307-2669.

**San Bernardino Asistencia** -- Established in 1830 as an outpost of Mission San Gabriel, this is one of the "inland chain" of missions. The site includes a museum, small chapel, and gatehouse. 26930 Barton Road. Open Tuesday through Saturday, 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM. Tel: 909-793-5402.

**Morey Mansion** -- Dubbed "America's Favorite Victorian House," this Queen Anne-style home was built by retired shipbuilder David Morey in the 1880s. It has appeared in countless commercials and films. Actress Carole Lombard is reported to have spent time here. 190 Terracina Boulevard.

**American and European Contemporary Glass Museum** -- Housed in a 1905 Victorian home, this small specialty museum displays contemporary glass art. Open Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4:00 PM. Admission by donation. 1157 Orange Street. Tel: 909-793-3333.
