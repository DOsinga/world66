The Lincoln Memorial Shrine was built and presented to the City of Redlands in 1932 by philanthropists Robert and Alma Watchorn, as a tribute to Lincoln and a memorial to their son Emory Ewart, who died from injuries suffered in World War I.

Designed by noted Pasadena architect Elmer Grey, the Shrine houses the famous Carrara marble bust of Lincoln by sculptor George Grey Barnard, as well as allegorical murals by illustrator Dean Cornwell. On February 12, 1998, a newly expanded facility was rededicated, fulfilling Robert Watchorn's original vision.

The collection includes thousands of volumes on Lincoln and the Civil War, letters and documents from Lincoln and his family, rare pamphlets, Civil War newspapers, photographs, coins, stamps, and diaries.

Located behind the A.K. Smiley Public Library. Open Tuesday through Sunday, 1:00 to 5:00 PM. Groups of 15 or more should call ahead to schedule tours.
