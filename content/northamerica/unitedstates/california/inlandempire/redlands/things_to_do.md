---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Redlands' charm is its late-Victorian architecture — the "Marmalade Mansions" built by citrus-boom families in the 1890s. Start with **Kimberly Crest**, the most famous of them, a French chateau with formal Italian gardens. The **Lincoln Memorial Shrine**, attached to the public library, is the only memorial to Abraham Lincoln west of the Mississippi. Out on Barton Road, the **San Bernardino Asistencia** — a 19th-century outpost of Mission San Gabriel — is a quiet pocket of early Californian history.
