The magnificent Edwards Mansion was built on Cajon Street in Redlands in 1890. James S. Edwards, a pioneer citizen and citrus grower, purchased ready-made plans for the 14-room house. It took over a year to build the original salmon-pink structure, which sat on a rise overlooking 20 acres of young orange trees.

The Edwards family lived in the house until 1958. In 1973, the mansion was purchased for one dollar, cut in two, and moved five miles through Redlands to its current site on the old Barton Ranch, one of the most picturesque of the old California estates.

Adjacent to the mansion stands the Orange Tree Wedding Chapel, incorporating stained glass windows and pews from the first Evangelical Lutheran Church built in Redlands on Olive Avenue in 1904. The chapel holds 110 seated guests. The greenhouse, heated orangery patio, tea garden, and pavilions are used for wedding receptions and banquets.
