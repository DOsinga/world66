title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

**Mission Inn** -- Even if you do not stay the night, the Mission Inn is worth exploring. This sprawling Mission Revival masterpiece features alcoves, wooden beams, stained glass, and antiques at every turn. Kelly's Spa offers individual treatments and half- and full-day packages. 3649 Mission Inn Avenue. Tel: 800-440-5910. [missioninn.com](http://www.missioninn.com)

**California Citrus State Historic Park** -- Preserves the groves and packing houses that made Riverside the birthplace of the California citrus industry. A pleasant walk through living history.

**UCR Botanic Gardens** -- The University of California, Riverside's 37-acre botanical gardens contain 3,000 species from yuccas to redwoods. A peaceful escape from the city. Tel: 909-787-4650.

**Castle Amusement Park** -- A local family amusement park with rides, miniature golf, and an arcade. 3500 Polk Street. Tel: 909-785-4141.

**Parent Navel Orange Tree** -- A historic marker at Magnolia Avenue and Arlington Street commemorating the original navel orange trees that launched the California citrus industry.

**Koroneburg European Old World Renaissance Festival** -- Southern California's only permanent Renaissance village, set along the Santa Ana River. Hundreds of costumed performers, fine arts and crafts, and period food and drink. The festival runs in spring at 14600 River Road, Corona. Tel: 800-320-4REN. [koroneburg.com](http://www.koroneburg.com)
