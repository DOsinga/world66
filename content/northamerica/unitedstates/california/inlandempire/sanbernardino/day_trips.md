---
title: "Day Trips in San Bernardino"
type: section
---

Oak Glen, rising a mile high above the arid terrain of Southern California's San Bernardino valley, is an unlikely oasis in a region known mainly for date palms, Indian bingo, and fiberglass dinosaurs. The Glen combines the elements that make an ideal day trip: easy accessibility, gorgeous mountain scenery, quaint shops, delightful country dining, and intriguing historic sites. All this without even mentioning the area's main attraction - the apples.

Oak Glen's acres upon acres of apple orchards, with their delectable combinations of unique and heirloom varieties, bring visitors back year after year, generation after generation. Tourists often remark that they bring their children because their parents brought them, because their parents brought them . They flock here in autumn for the "u-pick" orchards and the New-England-esque splendor (fall colors being a rare sight in sunny SoCal), and come in the off season for the shops, the food, and picnicking in the crisp, clean mountain air.
