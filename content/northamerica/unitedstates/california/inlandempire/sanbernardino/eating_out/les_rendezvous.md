A hidden jewel this quaint, charming little out of the way restaurant is located in a residential area of north San Bernardino. Set at
the foot of the mountains, Le Rendez-Vous has long been a local
favorite as well as a popular dining choice for travelers on the
journey home from Big Bear or Lake Arrowhead. Large wood beams against
the white ceilings add a rustic touch. The menu, which changes
regularly, might include such classic French dishes as escargots
bourguignon, coq au vin, roast duckling à l’orange and rabbit. Crêpes
Suzette and cherries jubilee are frequently offered on the dessert menu
along with tempting fresh pastries. The fairly priced wine list is well above average and
includes some nice half-bottles.
The owner, Jean-pierre is the head chef is French and regularly has
specials that are not on the menu. There is an eclectic selection of
good wines. The selections are not inexpensive but the quality exceeds
expectations for the cost. Some samples of entre s are
salmon with tarragon cream sauce, lamb with peppercorn sauce, duck with
cherry sauce and filet mignon with bearnaise. There is the mandatory french
onion soup gratinee which can be matched in taste but not exceeded. The
vischioisse is much the same. The house salad dressing is unrivaled.
The desserts are good enough for a trip on their own.
