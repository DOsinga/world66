The beautiful and historic California Theatre of the Performing Arts was built in 1928 in opulent Spanish/Moorish style and is beautifully preserved both inside and out. The theater serves as home to many San Bernardino organizations including the San Bernardino Symphony Orchestra, under the direction of Maestro Carlo Ponti Jr, the Inland Dance Theater, San Bernardino Valley Concert Association, Sinfonia Mexicana and Theater Arts International. Theater Arts International is responsible for many of the lavish stage productions such as Phantom of the Opera, Cats and Cirque du Soleil while more classic theater such as Mark Twain and Of Mice and Men are presented by other theatre organizations. Take note of the enormous outdoor mural on the east side of the building. The mural was commissioned from renowned artist Kent Twitchell and recalls Will Rogers' last stage performance, which took place at this very theater in 1935, just before his death in a plane crash in Alaska.
