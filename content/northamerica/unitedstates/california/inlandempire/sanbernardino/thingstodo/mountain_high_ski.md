In 1929, construction began on what was then the largest ski jump in the world, with hopes of hosting the 1932 Winter Olympic Games. The structure took two years to build, and in 1931 Norwegian-born Alf Engen set a world record by jumping 243 feet -- on skis that were little more than wooden planks with leather straps. Big Pines was awarded the winter games, but poor snow conditions in 1932 caused the competition to move to Lake Placid, and the area never fulfilled its Olympic destiny.

Mountain High is currently one of the top five resorts in California and among the top 25 in the country in terms of visits.
