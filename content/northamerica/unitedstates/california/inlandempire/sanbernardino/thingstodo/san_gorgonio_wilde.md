The climax region of the San Bernardino Mountains, located on the San Bernardino National Forest about 25 miles east of San Bernardino. The wilderness receives approximately 200,000 visitors per year. Its 58,969 acres harbor two small lakes, meadows, streams, 100 miles of trail, densely forested northern slopes, and rugged terrain. Elevations range from 4,400 feet to 11,499 feet atop Mt. San Gorgonio, the highest peak between the Sierra Nevada and the Mexican border. Eleven of twelve peaks exceed 10,000 feet.

From the summit, views stretch across metropolitan Los Angeles and the Mojave Desert. All visitors must secure a permit before entering the wilderness, for both day hikes and overnight travel.
