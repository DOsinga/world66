Livermore sits at the eastern edge of the San Francisco Bay Area, in a sun-baked valley that feels a world away from the fog and tech campuses to the west. It is best known for two things: wine and science. The Livermore Valley is one of California's oldest wine regions, with dozens of wineries open for tastings along Tesla Road and Mines Road — less crowded and less expensive than Napa, and producing increasingly good results.

The science connection comes from Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, a major federal research facility that has been here since 1952. You cannot tour the lab itself, but the Discovery Center offers a free window into the work done there.

Downtown Livermore has been revitalised in recent years, with a walkable stretch of First Street lined with restaurants, tasting rooms, and the Bankhead Theater. It is a pleasant base for exploring the valley's wineries or for a day trip from San Francisco, about an hour's drive west.
