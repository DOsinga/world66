---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Los Angeles"
type: section
---

Los Angles possesses more than its fair share of trendy bars and
cafes, providing plenty of fun and excitement for tourists and locals
alike.

**Formosa Café** – This Los Angeles institution has a reputation
dating back to the 1930s and remains firmly tied to such glamorous old
Hollywood icons as Clark Gable, Frank Sinatra and Ava Gardner. While
the restaurant portion of the café dishes out some tasty Chinese food
it’s mostly known for its bar which boasts a decidedly throwback
appearance.

**The Edison** – Hidden away in a downtown Los Angeles alleyway,
The Edison remains one of the city’s most sophisticated bars. The
elegant and dimly lit décor, coupled with a strict dress code, only
adds to the bar’s charm. A spirited burlesque and cabaret floor show
entertains patrons on Wednesday nights.

**Downbeat Café **– Silver Lake and Echo Park residents flock to
the richly inviting Downbeat Café in droves. Serving up a wide array of
coffees, teas, as well as delicious salads, sandwiches and pastries,
the café projects a relaxed atmosphere amidst the occasional live music
performance.

**Tiki Ti Cocktail Lounge **– The ultimate dive bar, Tiki Ti
Cocktail Lounge remains one of the most beloved hotspots in all of Los
Angeles. Sample from a host of tropical drinks, including their famous
Hot Buttered Rum, all while lounging in a relaxed setting.

**The Coffee Table** – The Coffee Table provides a cozy and
welcoming atmosphere for all its patrons. This Silver Lake hip spot
boasts a charming interior and a comfy patio setting. Equipped with a
host of various coffees, tasty light snacks and old-fashioned pastries,
there’s something on tap for everyone to enjoy.

**The Standard Rooftop Bar **– Perched atop downtown Los Angeles’
ultra chic Standard hotel, this rooftop bar provides stunning views of
the surrounding skyline amidst a smart setting. Patrons can lounge
about the retro furnishings or hang out near the equally stylish
rooftop pool.

**Homegirl Café** – Not only does the Homegirl Café serve up
deliciously fresh cuisine, made with organic produce stemming from a
local garden, but it’s run by an organization which aims to help
reformed gang members turn their lives around. The wait and kitchen
staffs are generally composed of those in search of a second chance.

**The Dresden** – Situated in the chic Los Feliz region of Los
Angeles, The Dresden boasts an old-fashioned martini bar décor complete
with the kitschy lounge act duo known simply as Marty and Elayne.
