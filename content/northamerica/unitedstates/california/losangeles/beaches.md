---
title: "Beaches in Los Angeles"
type: section
---

The Los Angeles area plays host to some of the most famous beach communities in the world.

**Malibu **– A cluster of beaches comprises this region, including
Malibu State Beach, Topanga State Beach, Paradise Cove, Surfrider Beach
and Zuma Beach. The beaches in this region are known for their
excellent surfing and kite-surfing conditions. Stretched along 20 miles
of pristine shores, and home to world famous movie stars, Malibu also
boasts a slew of stunning beachfront and cliffside homes.

**Santa Monica** – Sandwiched between Pacific Palisades and
Venice, Santa Monica State Beach serves as one of the most popular
beaches in Los Angeles County. Although it runs only 2 miles long, it
remains jam packed with attractions and activities, including beach
volleyball facilities, basketball courts, chess tables, bike/running
trails and the historic Santa Monica Pier, featuring various carnival
rides and restaurants.

**Venice **– Venice Beach provides an unusual atmosphere unlike
any other beach community in Los Angeles. For starters, Venice Beach
features a promenade lined with boutique shops, tattoo parlors and
fortune tellers. Muscle Beach, an outdoor gym displaying extraordinary
weightlifting feats from burly participants, remains the most famous
and crowd-pleasing aspect of Venice.

**Manhattan Beach** – Wedged between the cities of El Segundo and
Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach remains one of the wealthiest beachfront
communities in Los Angeles County. The Strand, a beachfront boardwalk
reserved for walking, bicycling or skating, serves as one of the most
popular features in Manhattan Beach. The nearby pier, featuring upscale
shops and restaurants, brings in a steady stream of locals and tourists,
as well.

**Hermosa Beach** – Nestled amidst the charming South Bay region,
Hermosa Beach promotes such beloved beachfront activity as
rollerblading, bike riding, fishing off the local pier and surfing.
Each summer, the beach also plays host to the famed AVP Beach
Volleyball Championship. There’s plenty of relaxing to be had as well,
with wide open sandy shores inviting sunbathers to spend the day just
working on their tan.

**Redondo Beach** – Outside of Santa Monica, Redondo Beach plays
host to the most famous pier in Los Angeles County. Rebuilt after a
devastating fire demolished it in 1988, the pier complex features
scores of popular local restaurants and shops. Each year the beach
plays host to an impressive 4th of July fireworks display.

**Long Beach** – Long Beach reigns as one of the largest cities
in the United States, while hosting some of the finest beaches in
Southern California. Spend the day relaxing on the sunny shores
before walking, skating or riding to the host of nearby beachfront
attractions such as the Aquarium of the Pacific, The Pike at Rainbow
Harbor entertainment complex and the Long Beach Museum of Art.
Meanwhile, Dog Beach allows pet owners the opportunity to let their dogs
run lose along the sandy shores or frolic in the Pacific Ocean.
