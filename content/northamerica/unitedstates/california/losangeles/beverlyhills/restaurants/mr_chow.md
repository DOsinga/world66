Longtime, swanky refuge for celebrities, wanna-be
celebrities, the in-crowd and those who pine for delectable Chinese cuisine,
Mr. Chow has been a jewel in the Beverly Hills culinary crown for years – and
still going strong. Austere in design, with the trademark black and white
checkerboard floor and tiny tables bunched close together, the dining room is
always bustling, still channeling the ‘café society’ vibe restaurant founder
Michael Chow created for his eatery when it opened in 1974. He has since opened
Mr. Chow restaurants in London, New York and coming soon, Las Vegas.

Try the Drunken Fish.
