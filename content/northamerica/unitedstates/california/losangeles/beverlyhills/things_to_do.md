**Rodeo Drive**

How could Rodeo Drive not top
the list of things to do in Beverly
Hills? This street pretty much defines this high-class land of the rich and famous. This shopping district is home to numerous popular fashion designers such as
Louis Vuitton and Christian Dior. Hollywood icons
have been said to frequent the streets of Rodeo for some much-needed shopping. This
shopping area is upscale as is showcased by its buildings, some of the
replicating European museums and landmarks. While it is known for shopping, many
people come here simply to just people watch or enjoy coffee and good
conversations with a friend.**

Greystone Mansion**** and Park**

Now owned
by the City of Beverly Hills, the Greystone Mansion invites visitors to stroll
through its lush grounds and explore its gardens free of charge. It truly is a
wonderful sight to see and admire. Its colorful gardens provide the perfect
backdrop for a picture and the mansion itself captures the essence of the ritzy
area known as Beverly Hills.**

Paley Center ** ** for Media**

Once known
as The Museum of Television and Radio and The Museum of Broadcasting, The Paley
center for media is one of Beverly
Hills ’ top attractions. It was renamed in 2007 to accommodate
other forms of emerging media such as the Internet, mobile video and
podcasting. Though they house several collectible artifacts pertaining to
media, the Paley Center is composed mostly of screening
rooms including two full-sized theatres. Radio programs and old television
shows are available for visitors to enjoy in private consoles. Since all forms
of media are prohibited from leaving the building, you can expect to find
yourself here for hours getting lost in old shows.

**

Beverly Hills**** Trolley Tour**

What can be
a better way to see Beverly Hills
than to hop on a trolley and take a 40 minute tour around the area? This
open-air trolley takes visitors through all Beverly Hills landmarks including Rodeo Drive, stately
neighborhoods and even some celebrity homes. The cost is $5 for adults and $1
for children.**

Virginia Robinson
Gardens**** & Estate**

The home
that sits on this property is known to be one of the first homes ever built in Hollywood. Its gardens
are now open to the public. The garden boasts of its exotic tropical and
subtropical plants and flowers which is a delightful sight to eye. All the
colors of these plants bring out so much life, making them ideal subjects for
serious photographers. The 5 gardens cover a total 6 acres and visitors can
tour them by following a brick path passing along several ponds and fountains.
Guided tour is included with admission fee; $10 for adults and $5 for children
12 years of age and over.
