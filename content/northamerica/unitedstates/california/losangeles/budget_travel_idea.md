title: "Budget Travel Ideas"
type: section

Now more than ever, most people are wanting to get away want to travel on a budget. Here are the top 5 free “Must Do’s” while visiting Los
Angeles, California.

**1. Venice Beach: People Watching **

The Venice Beach area is known for its eclectic booths, shops and boutiques.
Home of the socially progressive, many creative artists hang out at Venice Beach
and peddle their handmade goods. On the weekends, you can usually find a drum
circle, skaters in the roller rink, break dancers, slam poets and a
conglomeration of starving artists and activists. It is a great spot to people
watch, take photographs, and watch the sunset. Venice Beach is also a hot surf
spot.

**2. Santa Monica: The Promenade **

While strolling around in sunny Santa Monica, the Third Street Promenade is a
must see, even if you don’t spend a dime. Stroll past countless restaurants,
abundant shopping opportunities, live music and street talent. Here, shops like Bebe, Diesel
and Club Monaco can be found. You can usually enjoy musicians playing live
music in the street while the sun is shining. Remember, you are just steps from
the beach too!

**3. Malibu: Malibu Creek State Park **

Located just 25 miles from Los Angeles in beautiful, scenic Malibu, lies
Malibu Creek State Park. At the park, observe the Santa Monica Mountains, bird
watch, hike for hours, or even jump off a cliff in to the chilly lagoon. Be
sure to check out the waterfall deep in the back of the park. This state
park accommodates campers and the park is open from dawn ‘til dusk.

**4. Los Angeles: Attend a Taping
**

Nothing can make you feel like you are really in Hollywood more than seeing
the action happen with your own 2 eyes! Tourists and locals are welcome to
attend free, live tapings of shows. Whether you want to see Chelsea Handler,
Conan O’Brien or Jay Leno, you can do so by requesting tickets online at www.
tvtix.com. You can also show up at the studio, stand in line and wait for tickets.
If you are able to become part of a studio audience, remember to follow instructions of the producers.

**5. Mount Lee: The Hollywood Sign **

No visit to Los Angeles would be complete without visiting the infamous
Hollywood sign. Standing 50 feet tall in all white capital letters, the
sign is a definitely a must do! It has undergone many changes and lots of
vandalism since it was erected in 1923, but is now protected with a security system and
maintained by a non-profit organization. The sign has been featured in many movies
over the years and is an important part of Hollywood’s history. The sign is
located just north of Mulholland Drive on Mount Lee in Griffith Park.
