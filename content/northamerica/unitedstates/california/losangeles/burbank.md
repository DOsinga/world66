Burbank is the working heart of Hollywood — not the tourist-facing side, but the place where much of American film and television is actually made. NBC, Disney, Warner Bros. and dozens of smaller production companies are all based here, spread along Olive Avenue and the surrounding blocks. If you care about how the entertainment industry operates day to day, Burbank is more interesting than the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

The **Warner Bros. Studio Tour** offers a genuine behind-the-scenes look at a working studio — you walk through actual soundstages and backlots rather than theme park recreations. You will need to book ahead. The **Disney headquarters** on Alameda Avenue is closed to the public but worth a look from the street for the Seven Dwarfs holding up the roofline. NBC's studios tape shows including The Tonight Show; free tickets for tapings are available, and a queue forms outside on taping days by early afternoon.

Nearby Glendale has DreamWorks Animation and Nickelodeon's studios (no tours), excellent shopping along Brand Boulevard, and the legendary **Zankou Chicken** — a local institution. **Griffith Park** is just south, with the Griffith Observatory offering panoramic views of [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles) and the Hollywood sign.
