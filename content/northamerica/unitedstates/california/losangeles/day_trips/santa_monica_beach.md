Santa Monica Beach and Venice Beach are two of the most scenic public beaches in the world. The millions of visitors to Santa Monica and Venice Beach each year will find a wide variety of notable landmarks and plenty of famous boardwalks to explore. One excellent Santa Monica sight is the Santa Monica Pier. The 9-½ -acre Santa Monica Pier was built in 1906 and it is the city’s number one landmark. This popular pier has starred in *The Sting, Ruthless People, The Net, Cellular,* and *Fat Albert*, as well as the world famous TV show *Baywatch*. A hop, skip, and a jump away is Venice Beach and adjacent Muscle Beach. Venice Beach and Muscle Beach are popular for peculiar sights, sunning, strolling, and swimming. People come here to browse the famous Venice Beach Boardwalk where dozens of street vendor, and wacky performers show up each day to entertain the enormous crowds of tourists and locals itching for some excitement. A bike path connects the beaches, so feel free to rent a bike or bring a pair of rollerblades join in! Santa Monica is around a 25-minute drive (with no traffic) from the center of Hollywood.
