---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Los Angeles"
type: section
---

Los Angeles, California is filled with family-friendly activities beyond the
ever popular (and expensive) Disneyland. These are some other family-friendly places
to consider for the day or weekend:

**The John Paul Getty**** Center**

Known as LA’s best stroller-friendly museum, kids will love the Gettty’s
tram ride, the beautiful gardens, and the artwork. Year-round you can catch one of the Getty’s Family Art Galleries, and in
the summer you can sit in the garden with a cool drink while listening to live music. Don’t miss the Family Room where kids can draw, write,
read, dress up in period clothing, and more. The museum has free admission and allows its visitors to bring packed lunches.

**The Huntington**** Museum & Garden in San Marino **

The Huntington Museum & Garden, in San Marino, is known for its
beautiful grounds, elaborate gardens, spacious gardens, and gorgeous landscaping. The Japanese Garden features and arched
bridge, a splendid bonsai exhibit, and cactus display. The Children's
garden, which features touchable plants, fanciful water exhibits, a
playhouse covered in vines and other charming attractions will surely delight children and adults a like. The museum features one of the
world’s greatest libraries with extraordinary, hard-to-find books on
display, including Benjamin Franklin’s autobiography *In his Own Hand*,
and the Gutenberg Bible. While not very interesting to toddlers, middle
and high school kids will love this wing of the Huntington Museum.

**Santa Monica**** Pier**

Children of all ages delight in going to the Santa Monica Pier,
which features an amusement park at the end of the boardwalk. The
carousel at the beginning of the pier was features in several famous
movies including “The Sting” and “They Shoot Horses, Don’t They.” For a change of pace,
go to the quaint and peaceful Tudor House near 3rd Street for British
afternoon tea, and juice and cookies for the children.

**Walk LA’s Coast**

Further down the coast is Marina del Rey for active families who
want to get out and about. You can walk on the calm harbor waters for
miles or rent a kayak or sailboat from one of the companies in the
Fisherman’s Village. When you tire of kayaking, rent a bike and enjoy
more than 20 miles of the South Bay Bike Path, stretching from north
Santa Monica to south Torrance. The paved, fairly flat path runs along
the beaches, an enjoyable, light ride for the entire family. Plenty of
food stands and rest stops are featured along the path.
