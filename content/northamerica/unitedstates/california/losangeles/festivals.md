---
title: "Festivals in Los Angeles"
type: section
---

The sprawling Los Angeles metropolis plays host to more than its
fair share of annual festivals, many of which cater to the needs and
interests of the city’s diverse population.

** Los Angeles Times Festival of Books** – Each spring the Los
Angeles Times hosts a book festival on the campus of UCLA. Hundreds of
publishers and bookstores set up booths so visitors can browse through
or purchase a collection of both rare and popular books. The festival
also boasts in-depth Q&A sessions with some of the world’s most
renowned literary giants.

**AFI Fest** – Widely considered as one of the most prestigious film
festivals in the world, each fall the AFI Fest showcases a mixture of
short films, international projects and highly anticipated works. The
event normally runs for a week and includes its share of VIP red
carpet premieres.

**Lotus Festival** – One of the oldest festivals in Los Angeles,
this event celebrates the blossoming of the lotus flower in sprawling
Echo Park. The Lotus Festival also serves to celebrate the region’s
thriving Asian and Pacific Islander cultures. On site, participants
will find live music, dancing, arts and craft booths, as well as a
fireworks display.

**Griffith Park Holiday Light Festival** – The Department of
Water and Power dresses a mile long stretch of Griffith Park with
millions of lights and a holiday themed display from late November to
late December. Guests can either walk or drive through the display.

**Outfest - LA Gay and Lesbian Film Festival** – With screenings
scattered throughout the city, Outfest highlights films made by or
revolving around the gay and lesbian community. Featuring full-length
films, short subjects, documentaries and videos, Outfest remains a
popular annual summertime event in Los Angeles.

**Nisei Week Japanese Festival** – Situated in Little Tokyo, this
festival celebrates the heritage of Japanese and Japanese-Americans.
Complete with a street fair, parades and Taiko performances, Nisei Week
offers a glimpse into the world of this rich culture.

**Los Angeles International Tamale Festival** – Sample a variety
of tamales, Mexican delicacies made of masa and stuffed with meat,
chili or cheese, from different restaurants and vendors across the
city. Held each fall, this popular festival also includes tamale-making
seminars as well as tamale cooking and eating contests.

**Sunset Junction Street Festival **– Held each year in the
trendy Silver Lake neighborhood, this popular street fair has featured
some big name musical acts in past years. Guests will find carnival
rides and food booths, as well as arts and crafts vendors on display.
