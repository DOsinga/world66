title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Hollywood is located in the west - northwest of Los Angeles, within close proximity to many Los Angeles highways making day trips easily accessible. Los Angeles itself is
great location in regard to proximity to many places, sights, and
attractions. If you have the time, day trips are strongly encouraged.

**Six Flags Magic Mountain
**

Located in Valencia, California
Six Flags Magic Mountain is just off of Interstate 5 (direct access from Hollywood), north of Los
Angeles. Whether you are looking for all around family fun or an
adrenaline rush, Magic Mountain has many action
packed and fun filled amusements for all ages. Make sure to check
operating days and hours before your visit, as Magic Mountain tends to
run on a limited schedule outside of peak season. Magic Mountain offers
some of the most exciting roller coasters in the nation. In addition to thrill rides, Magic Mountain
has many family rides, kids rids, water rides, and various other
entertainment. Tickets are available online or at the ticket booth
located at the entrance of the park.

**San Diego, California **

San Diego is also accessible from Hollywood by
going south about 2 hours (depending on traffic). San Diego is one of
the largest cities in California and offers a wide range of activities
and beautiful coastline. The San Diego Zoo is world famous for its
expansive animal enclosures, wide range of endangered and rare animals,
and wonderfully landscaped grounds. The San Diego Zoo is partnered with
the Wild Animal Park in Escondido, California (40 minutes northeast of San
Diego), and offers a safari-like view of exotic animals in their
natural habitats. Home to many gorgeous beaches, notorious
surf spots, and Torrey Pines State Park, the San Diego coastline is a
treasure chest for beach and ocean lovers. Not to be overlooked is the
fun filled nightlife of The Gaslamp District, located in the heart of
downtown, here you will find some of southern California's best
restaurants, bars, and nightclubs.

**Catalina Island
**

Catalina is located approximately 22 miles southwest
of Los Angeles. Ferries leave daily from Long Beach, Dana Point, Marina
del Rey, San Pedro, and Newport Beach, and take about 60 minutes to
reach the town of Avalon on Catalina Island. Catalina is a laid back
island where you are forced to get around by walking or golf cart, and
the coastline is a sight to behold. There are numerous things to do
such as water sports, kayaking, boat rentals, walking in the beautiful small town setting, or take in some of
the fine restaurants. Baseball fans will enjoy the Chicago Cubs memoribilia within the Avalon Casino, as the island was once home to the Cubs Spring Training. Make sure to check availability and hours for
ferry departures and arrivals.
