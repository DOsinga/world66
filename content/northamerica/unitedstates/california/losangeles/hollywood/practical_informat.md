**Practical Tips for Hollywood**

**Hollywood Practical Information** will not only make your stay in this city more enjoyable, but it will also help you save some time and money. Here are some thing to consider when visiting Hollywood and Los Angeles.

**Taking the Cab Around Town**

One of the most efficient ways to get around Los Angeles and Hollywood is to take a cab. Even though it is possible to flag down a cab around certain areas with heavy tourist activity, most of the time you will need to call a cab in advance, especially in suburban areas and residential areas. It is a good idea to look up the route from your departure point to your destination on the Internet or smartphone because not all taxi drivers are that knowledgeable around the city. Just having an address is not enough, you need some landmarks or nearby main avenues. Therefore, it is also another good idea to read a city guide before arriving to the city so that you are familiar with important landmarks and main avenues.

**Chicano Culture**

Chicano is a term used for Mexican Americans, that is, Americans who have strong ties to the Mexican culture. The state of California is one of the states in the United States that has one of the highest Chicano populations. Even though you might encounter somebody with a first or last name that is clearly Spanish, it does not mean that they will be fully fluent in Spanish. Before making assumptions about the ethnic background of a local, just politely ask. This way you will avoid any unnecessary arguments. Also, even though there is a high percentage of Mexican Americans, it is not a good idea to assume that every Latino that you encounter will have roots tied to Mexico. Again, the same rule applies: when in doubt, ask.

**Being Cautious**

It is important that you are aware of the high crime rate of the city of Los Angeles and its surrounding areas. When using the public transportation system, avoid attracting unnecessary attention to yourself, especially at night. At night avoid walking by yourself in dark places and try to keep around crowded areas. Also, make sure to have change and bills of smaller denomination, so that you don't have to carry and show around large amounts of cash. While traveler's checks were necessary in the past, nowadays most retailers allow you to use your debit card as long as both the retailer and your bank participate in the same network. If you plan to pay by debit card, make sure to check with the retailer or salesperson. While this is a more convenient way to make purchases, be aware of the applicable fees that might take effect.
