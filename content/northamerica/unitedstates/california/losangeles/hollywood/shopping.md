---
title: "Shopping in Hollywood"
type: section
---

The **Hollywood shopping **experience is so expansive, you may have no clue where to start. However, if you're hoping to find some classy, memorable shops during your stay in Hollywood, you can't go wrong with a visit to any of these locations:

**Beverly Center**

Hollywood's most crowded--because it's the most popular--mall to shop at is Beverly Center, an eight-floor megamall with over 160 stores and a 13-screen movie theater. You'll find national chains as well as high-end boutiques, such as Louis Vuitton, Gucci, Calvin Klein, Dior and more. Macy's and Bloomingdale's are the anchor stores. Since this is Hollywood, expect to pay prices that are a little more in tune with its upscale offerings, but you can find some affordable deals at some of the national chain stores.

**Decades**

Located at 8214 1/2 Melrose Avenue is Decades, a high-end boutique renowned for its 1960s- and 1970s-style modern-meets-vintage clothes and accessories. Designed by world-famous vintage-style designer Cameron Silver, the clothes at Decades are truly one-of-a-kind. Of course, be prepared to spend the kind of money necessary for one-of-a-kind ensembles, but it doesn't hurt to look, even if you're on a more modest budget.

**Frederick's of Hollywood**

Although it's a national chain, the original, flagship boutique is located in Hollywood at 6751 Hollywood Boulevard. If you enjoy something sexy or beautiful, an actual design from the flagship location makes a unique souvenir. Prices aren't too much more than you'd find at a Frederick's elsewhere.

**It's a Wrap**

What better way to remember your trip to Hollywood than to bring home a little bit of actual Hollywood history? It's a Wrap is the premier location to search for actual Hollywood film props and costumes for sale to the public. Situated at 1164 S. Robertson Boulevard near West Hollywood, It's a Wrap allows you to browse through movie props and memorabilia. Some of the rarer items may be a little pricey, but there are affordable souvenirs for bargain shoppers as well.

**Melrose Avenue**

This eclectic avenue of shops and boutiques is a unique experience and a great destination for spending some time walking along a pedestrian-lined street, marveling at the sights. Many of the storefronts are decorated extensively in bright and/or neon colors, making even the outside of the shops something to see. Besides clothing, you'll also find jewelry, books, antiques, cafes, bakeries and restaurants.

**The Farmers' Market and the Grove**

If upscale isn't totally your thing and you want to see more of what the local farmers and artisans have to offer, stop by the Farmers' Market, which is open daily. Fresh produce, pastries, nuts, cheese and more are just a few highlights. Plus, nearby is the Grove, which you can get to by electric trolley. The shops and restaurants at the Grove are quaint and the whole area is pretty to look at.
