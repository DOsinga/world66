Hollywood is one of the most well known
cities in the world. Home to starlets and movie studios this is a city that can
make dreams come true. It is very likely
you will see somebody famous on your walk through Hollywood. In the Los
Angeles area, always keep your eyes open for star
sightings.

**Hollywood Walk of Fame**

This world famous landmark on Hollywood Boulevard is known as
the Hollywood Walk of Fame. More than
2,500 pieces of the sidewalk are embedded with a five pointed star. Each star has the name of someone who “made
it” in Hollywood. It is an honor to have
your name placed here. Occasionally
there are ceremonies when a new star is added. The Hollywood Walk of Fame is free.**Grauman’s Chinese Theater**

The Grauman’s Chinese Theater opened in the spring of 1927. This majestic piece of Chinese architecture is
constructed using pagodas, stone heaven dogs, bells and other building
materials imported from China. In 1968
this famous theater was declared a historic cultural landmark. At the front of the theater are the hand and
foot prints of stars like Marilyn Monroe. You can still enjoy a movie here.

**Hollywood
Forever Cemetery**

This resting place is home to the stars of Hollywood. If you take a walking tour through the well
manicured landscape and see the graves of Johnny Ramone, Cecil B. DeMille,
Rudolph Valentino, Jayne Mansfield and many others. The tour guides are well informed and
entertaining. Bring a bottle of water
and comfortable walking shoes.

**The Hollywood Sign**

It really is a thrill to see
the Hollywood sign that rests atop Mt.
Lee in Griffith
Park. And then of course have your
picture taken in front of it. There are
many great places to get a good view of it. You could take public transportation to Hollywood at Highland
Shopping Center. It is also visible from
other tourist sites like the Hollywood
Forever Cemetery
and the Griffith Park Observatory. If
you take any of the houses of the stars tour a picture taking opportunity is
usually available.

**The Kodak Theatre**

This well-known
theater hosts the Academy Awards every year. This advanced theater has all state of the art
equipment and cost $94 million dollars to complete. Despite utilizing all the latest technology,
the Kodak Theatre was designed to replicate the feeling of a European opera
house. You can go inside by taking a
tour or seeing a show.

### [Griffith Observatory](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles/hollywood/sights/griffith_observato)

Griffith Observatory is a Los Angeles attraction that one should not miss. It is located on Mount Hollywood in Griffith Park. Griffith Observatory is an architectural masterpiece and can be seen from certain parts of the city of Los Angeles. The Griffith Observatory first opened its doors on May 14, 1935. Originally, it was part of the Griffith Trust, but was surrendered on that same day to the City of Los Angeles. Since then, the Observatory has attracted a number of scientists as well as the general public.

### [Hollywood Bowl](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles/hollywood/sights/hollywood_bowl)

The Hollywood Bowl is a well-known amphitheatre in Hollywood located
on North Highland Avenue. The series of concentric arches known as a
“band shell” have allowed for this venue to become one of Hollywood’s
most notable landmarks . It is visited by millions of people-both locals and tourists ,
every year. Aside from being an architectural masterpiece for all to
admire, the shell allows for amazing acoustical sounds to be produced
during concerts and other events. Tickets for concerts held at the

### [Hollywood Sign](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles/hollywood/sights/north_america__u)

The Hollywood sign is one of the most famous California icons. It was erected in 1923 by real estate investor Harry Chandler who was also the publisher of the Los Angeles times. The original sign was not designed to be a tourist attraction. It was an advertisement for a housing development in the suburbs called “Hollywoodland”. This expensive sign cost over 20 thousand dollars to complete and all the materials were carried up the hill on foot. Initially planned to be on display for a year and a half, this grand sign still stands in Hollywood today.
