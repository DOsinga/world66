The Hollywood sign is one of the most famous California icons. It was erected in 1923 by real
estate investor Harry Chandler who was also the publisher of the Los
Angeles times. The original sign was not designed to be a tourist
attraction. It was an advertisement for a housing development in the
suburbs called “Hollywoodland”. This expensive sign cost over 20
thousand dollars to complete and all the materials were carried up the
hill on foot. Initially planned to be on display for a year and a
half, this grand sign still stands in Hollywood today.

One of the great things about this tourist sight is that you can
see it from many locations throughout Los Angeles. Don’t try
hiking to the sign because it’s illegal. There are also hazards along
the way like rattlesnakes and rough terrain. Here are three great
places to see the Hollywood Sign sight.

**Hollywood and Highland**

In the center of Hollywood is the bustling shopping center Hollywood
and Highland Center. This area was designed with the view of the sign
as its focal point. This is an ideal place to see this wonderful sight
and get some shopping done or have lunch at the same time. There are
many other famous sights to see in this area. Take a walk in the
neighborhood to visit Grauman’s Chinese Theater and the Guinness Book
of World Records Museum. A large parking facility is under the center
and there is also metered street parking. Public transportation is
available by using the Metro Rail Red Line.

**Griffith Park Observatory**

Viewing the Hollywood sign at Griffith Park Observatory is a
fabulous afternoon. Step out of the hurried energy of Los Angeles and
into nature. There are walking trails and free tours of the newly
renovated Observatory. The entrances to the park are along Los Feliz
Blvd and parking is free. Bring sunglasses, walking shoes, a hat and
a bottle of water.

**Beachwood Canyon**

Los Angeles is most easily navigable by car. There are many famous
drives in this area where you can find scenic views and homes of the
stars. Beachwood Canyon is one of them and also features one of the
best views of the sign. While you are here you can see houses once
owned by Charlie Chaplin and Douglas Fairbanks. At the end of the
drive is the original Hollywoodland Gates where the part of the
Invasion of the Body Snatchers was filmed. You can access this drive
from Franklin Blvd. If you want to get out of the car and walk around
there is free and metered parking available.
