---
title: "Things to Do in Hollywood"
type: section
---

**Hollywood things to do** are plenty! There&rsquo;s always something going on in Tinseltown, also known as Hollywood, whether it be going to a free taping of a live show, seeing a new, relatively unknown museum or checking out Hollywood&rsquo;s luxurious nightlife scene. Some people may simply be content with visiting the more well-known tourist stops, but these attractions are often overcrowded and can be boring for those who want to find out what Hollywood really has to offer. Instead of seeing the same places that everyone&rsquo;s already heard of, make your stay memorable by trying out some of these activities.

#### Free Show Taping

Attending a free show taping is a blast and actually not hard to do if you request tickets well in advance. Probably the most famous show to offer free tickets is &ldquo;The Price is Right&rdquo;, a game show where contestants are randomly selected from the studio audience to play price-guessing games for cash and prizes. Another great show to get free tickets from is called &ldquo;World&rsquo;s Funniest Moments&rdquo;, where funny videos sent in from viewers are showcased by Erik Estrada. If you don&rsquo;t like either of those shows, however, there are literally dozens of shows in Hollywood sponsoring this amazing offer.

#### Hollywood Nightlife

Hollywood nightlife is not only reserved for the Lindsey Lohans and Paris Hiltons of partying. You too can enjoy some of Hollywood&rsquo;s A-list bars and nightclubs, although some of these places can end up being astronomically expensive if you aren&rsquo;t careful. Some excellent clubs include the Cabana Club (a favorite of some top-name celebrities and their paparazzi) and Element (a more casual A-list establishment). There are also comedy clubs like the Acme Comedy Club (specializing in improv and sketch comedy) and The Comedy and Magic club, where Jay Leno performs every Sunday.

#### Madame Tussauds

Madame Tussauds Hollywood wax museum has only recently gained in popularity, but is still mostly unknown to the general public. This museum, which will likely become more popular than the Hollywood Wax Museum, features life-like wax sculptures of famous celebrities like George Clooney and Angelina Jolie. The Museum in Hollywood is actually the Museum&rsquo;s 9th location, starting out as a showcase of famous people in France in the 17th century. The price ($25 to enter) is a bit high, but worth it if you are interested in film, TV or gossip magazines.
