---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The Walk of Fame along Hollywood Boulevard is free and worth a look — over 2,500 stars embedded in the sidewalk stretch from Gower to La Brea. Grauman's Chinese Theatre (now TCL Chinese Theatre) has been collecting celebrity hand and footprints in its forecourt since 1927. Griffith Observatory, up in the hills, has free telescopes and some of the best views of Los Angeles and the Hollywood Sign; open evenings most days. The Hollywood Bowl runs concerts from June through September — bring your own food and drinks. Hollywood Forever Cemetery is atmospheric and screens classic films on the lawn in summer. For studio history, Paramount Studios on Melrose offers guided tours.
