---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Hollywood"
type: section
---

The **Hollywood must do's **capture the true essence of what Hollywood is all about: film and entertainment. Originally existing as a small agricultural community in the 1890s, today Hollywood is the premier destination for events in film and entertainment. The top five must-do&rsquo;s in Hollywood are found in some of the most astounding places imaginable, especially considering the amazing architecture involved. In addition, all of the places mentioned here are family-friendly and reasonably priced.

#### Universal Studios

Universal Studios (Los Angeles) is the original movie studio theme park that also acts as a real studio for film production, still existing as one of the oldest movie studios in America. In addition to getting the full tour of the studio, you and your family can enjoy all of the wonderful attractions, restaurants and shops the park has to offer. For $85, you can purchase an annual pass that is usable all year round, including black-out dates.

#### Hollywood Walk of Fame

The Hollywood Walk of Fame is an internationally known sidewalk where every famous actor has his or her &ldquo;star&rdquo; imprinted on the ground. These stars (made of pink terrazzo and brass) honor individual achievements in various medias. Part of the fun is just getting to search for a star of your favorite celebrity, but you might even get to see a commemoration of a new star being placed in. Besides being free, the sidewalk is a great place to spend a few hours of free time if you enjoy finding things.

#### Grauman&rsquo;s Chinese Theater

Grauman&rsquo;s Chinese Theater is perhaps the most famous theater in the world. Big-budget Hollywood films try year after year to premier here, and there are over 200 hand prints of famous actors and actresses. You can purchase a tour (happening seven days a week), but it may be more cost effective to simply buy a movie ticket into the place. Tickets range from $10-$15, depending on the day and time.

#### Kodak Theater

The Kodak Theater is home to the American Idol season finale, the Oscars, and other big events like philharmonic concerts, so visiting here will very much depend on when something is going on. Tickets to this theater are only available on a reserved basis. You can also purchase a ticket for a guided tour.

#### Hollywood Sign

If nothing else, make sure to see Hollywood&rsquo;s iconic sign, found atop Mt. Lee in Griffith park. The sign was originally used to attract housing investment, but now it is a symbol for everything that makes Hollywood great.
