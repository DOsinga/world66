Long Beach sits where Los Angeles meets the Pacific, but it has never been content to play second fiddle to its famous neighbour. California's fifth-largest city has its own personality — part gritty port town, part beachside playground, part cultural hub with a surprisingly good arts scene.

The waterfront downtown area anchors everything. The [Queen Mary](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles/longbeach/things_to_do/queen_mary) looms at her permanent berth, an art deco ocean liner frozen in time. Across the harbour, the [Aquarium of the Pacific](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles/longbeach/things_to_do/aquarium_of_the_pacific) is one of the best aquariums on the West Coast. Shoreline Village and the Pike give the waterfront a boardwalk feel without too much tourist kitsch.

Head inland along Pine Avenue and you hit the real downtown — restaurants, bars, and a growing arts district. East along the coast, Belmont Shore has a neighbourhood feel with independent shops and restaurants along 2nd Street, plus the unexpected charm of gondola rides through the Naples canals. Further out, the Cal State Long Beach campus hides some lovely botanical gardens.

Long Beach is also a working port, one of the busiest in the country, and that industrial edge gives the city a grounded quality you don't find in LA's glossier neighbourhoods. The cultural diversity is genuine — the food scene ranges from Cambodian to Mexican to craft cocktails — and the Long Beach jazz festival draws serious crowds every summer.
