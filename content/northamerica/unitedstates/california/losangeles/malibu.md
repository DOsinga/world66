Malibu stretches for 27 miles along the Pacific Coast Highway north of Santa Monica, and the drive alone is worth the trip. The beaches are the main event -- Zuma for wide sandy space, Surfrider for watching (or attempting) the break that put Malibu on the surfing map, and El Matador for dramatic rock formations and photo ops.

Beyond the sand, Malibu Canyon and Solstice Canyon offer good hiking without the crowds of Griffith Park. The Getty Villa, tucked into the hills above PCH, houses an excellent collection of Greek and Roman antiquities in a recreated Roman country house -- free admission but you need a timed reservation.

For food, the Malibu Country Mart has a cluster of restaurants ranging from casual to upscale. Nobu is the celebrity magnet, but Reel Inn does better fish for a fraction of the price. The vibe is laid-back even by LA standards -- flip-flops and a hoodie will get you into most places.
