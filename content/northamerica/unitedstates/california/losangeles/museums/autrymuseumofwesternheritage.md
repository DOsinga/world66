The Autry Museum pays homage to the American West - from its Gold Rush days and the romantic notions created by Hollywood.

Recently merged with the Southwest Museum, whose American Indian collections are second only to the Smithsonian's Museum of the American Indian.

The Autry has expanded with marvelous galleries devoted to the American Indians of different regions, including NorthEast, Great Lakes, Plains, and Northwest. Ther are also galleries dedicated to the Fur Trade; the Wagon Trains, the Frontier Towns, and the Gold Rush.

There is also a gallery of Art of the West, which is not to be missed, containing pieces by the great Western Artists including Remington, members of the Hudson River School, and a few wonderful early panoramic landscapes.

And in the Hollywood Westerns galleries are memorabilia, costumes and artifacts from all of the greatest cowboy stars from the silent film era, through the shoot-em-up western matinees of the 30s and 40s, right up to the present. Particularly fascinating are the galleries dedicated to the Wild West Shows of the 19th Century.
