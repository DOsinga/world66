Extraordinary! It takes more than one day to see this, so either choose only the sites that you want to see, or plan to come back to see the rest.

BEST Museum restaurants in the world. The truly excellent cafeteria is on the lower level, with marvelous views, and delicious fare, and menus which are seasonal, and change daily. The Restaurant at Getty Center (on the plaza level) requires reservations, but is truly the best restaurant in any museum in the world. Also seasonal fare, and menus change daily.

The collections are based on J. Paul Getty's original personal collections, and one of his passions was 18th Century French Furniture. The Getty's collection is one of the finest outside of Paris, and the furniture galleries (in addition to the several period rooms), were designed not by Meier (the genius who designed the museum), but by French Architect Thierry DesPont because the trustees thought they really needed an expert to design the settings for this truly fabulous collection which includes pieces originally owned by Louis XIV, Marie Antoinette, the Elector of Hanover, the Czar fo Russia, and many, many, others.

As the best endowed museum in the world, it must be noted that the Getty does acquire new pieces from time to time.

Also noteworthy are the splendid Illuminated Manuscripts Collection, the Largest Early Photography collection in the World, a good collection of Dutch Masters, a nice collection of Baroque Masters (another of J. Paul's favorites); English 18th Century; French 18th Century; 19th Century and Impressionistes.

The Classical collections of Greek and Roman primarily are now permanently displayed in the newly re-opened Getty Villa in Malibu.
