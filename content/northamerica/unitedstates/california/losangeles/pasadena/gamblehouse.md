The masterpiece of Greene and Greene, the architects who defined the American Craftsman style. Built in 1908 for the Procter & Gamble family, every detail — the woodwork, the stained glass, the furniture — was designed as a unified whole. The hour-long guided tour is the only way inside and is worth it even if you have no particular interest in architecture. The surrounding neighborhood is full of other Craftsman-era homes worth a slow walk.
