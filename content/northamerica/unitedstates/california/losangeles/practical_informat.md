title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Size:** 500 square miles

**Population:** 3.8 million

**Newspaper of Record:** LA Times

**Time Zone:** US Pacific

**Currency:** US Dollar

**Transportation:** Los Angeles does have a
wide-ranging bus system, but due to the size of the city's sprawl,
public transit is often time-consuming. The city's subway system,
while useful for a couple of attractions, is too limited to be of
universal good. Travelers to Los Angeles should plan on renting a car
upon arrival in order to fully experience the city.

**Airports:** The main airport serving Los
Angeles is Los Angeles International Airport (LAX), located in the
southwestern portion of the city. While it is considered to be
removed from most of the city's tourist spots, it is accessible via
subway; the Green Line terminates at the airport, and using the
subway a traveler can get to downtown Los Angeles, Hollywood and even
the San Fernando Valley. Buses also run from the airport to many
westside locations.

Other southland airports within driving
distance include John Wayne Airport in Santa Ana, Bob Hope Airport in
Burbank and LA/Ontario International Airport.

**Cuisine:** Los Angeles has a wide range
of food options, as befitting of America's second largest city.
However, it's hard to say that the city has a specialty. Some of the
country's top sushi chefs reside in Los Angeles, and Mexican food is
abundant. The surprising contribution that Los Angeles has made to
cuisine, however, might come in “truck food” - mobile gourmet
restaurants that set up shop on a street corner for an evening
serving top-notch eats.

**Crime:** Over the years, Los Angeles has
put a great deal of time and money into fighting intense gang
problems. In most cases, that element of criminals have been
banished from tourist areas, preferring to lurk in some of the city's
more far-flung areas. Research is still important, though, especially
concerning where to stay.

**Nearby Attractions:** Disneyland is a
short trip down the 5 freeway, and San Diego is two hours to the
south. Santa Barbara, with some incredible coastal views, is two
hours north, and Las Vegas is a four-and-a-half hour drive east.
