Rodeo Drive in Beverly Hills is the world's most famous shopping street, three blocks of designer flagships running from Wilshire Boulevard to Santa Monica Boulevard. Versace, Dior, Gucci, Cartier, Prada, Tiffany -- they're all here, along with Via Rodeo, a faux-European cobblestone alley designed purely for high-end retail.

You don't need a black card to visit. Window shopping and people-watching are free, and the architecture alone is worth a stroll. One block east on Beverly Drive, things get more approachable with Gap, Banana Republic, and Crate & Barrel. Another block east on Canon Drive you'll find smaller boutiques and restaurants, including Wolfgang Puck's Spago.
