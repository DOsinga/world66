Santa Monica Beach and Venice Beach are two of the most recognizable stretches of sand in Los Angeles. The Santa Monica Pier, built in 1906, is the main landmark — a 9.5-acre amusement pier with a Ferris wheel, an aquarium, and restaurants. It has appeared in films from *The Sting* to *Iron Man* and the TV show *Baywatch*.

A short walk south along the bike path brings you to Venice Beach and adjacent Muscle Beach. The Venice Beach Boardwalk is the main draw: street vendors, buskers, muralists, and bodybuilders create a scene that is equal parts carnival and open-air gym. Rent a bike or a pair of rollerblades to cover both beaches in one outing.

Santa Monica is about 25 minutes from Hollywood with no traffic, though rush hour can triple that.
