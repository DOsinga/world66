title: "Chinatown"
type: poi
---

Chinatown is slightly east and north of downtown. To be honest, L.A. Chinatown is much better seen in the movies – in reality, it’s 4 or 5 drab looking streets with a few touristy looking Chinese gates and arches. There are very few actual Chinese people in Chinatown. They have nearly all moved east to Fullerton Heights/Rowland Heights. So, with the exception of the Dim Sum in the Empress Palace, don’t not eat here unless you think TV-dinner quality fried rice is exotic. There are some amusing trinket stores and other gewgaw stores but it’s pretty much nothing. Chinatown is really more Vietnamese than anything – so if you love Vietnamese noodles, you can’t go wrong with many choices.
