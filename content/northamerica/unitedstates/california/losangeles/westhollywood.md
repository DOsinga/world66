West Hollywood — "WeHo" to everyone who lives there — is a small, dense, independent city of about 35,000 people entirely surrounded by the City of Los Angeles. Only 1.9 square miles in area, it was incorporated as a separate city in 1984 and has since become one of the most distinctive neighbourhoods in greater LA: a compact, walkable grid (a rarity in Los Angeles), a large and politically active LGBT community (West Hollywood was one of the first cities in the United States to elect an openly gay majority on its city council), and an extraordinary concentration of nightlife, restaurants, design shops and music venues per square kilometre.

The city's three main strips each have their own character. The **Sunset Strip**, the western end of Sunset Boulevard between Crescent Heights and Doheny, was the cradle of American rock music in the 1960s and 1970s — the Whisky a Go Go, the Roxy, the Troubadour and the Viper Room are all still on or near the strip, and the billboards above them are still the most expensive outdoor advertising in the world. **Santa Monica Boulevard**, particularly the stretch between La Cienega and Doheny, is the heart of LA's gay nightlife and the site of the enormous annual **LA Pride** parade and the **Halloween Carnaval**, which draws half a million people to close the street every 31 October. **Melrose Avenue** and the **West Hollywood Design District** around Beverly Boulevard hold the boutiques, galleries and mid-century furniture showrooms that have made WeHo synonymous with a certain sort of Los Angeles style.

For visitors the area is extremely well placed — it sits between Beverly Hills to the west, Hollywood to the east, and the Hollywood Hills and the Chateau Marmont on the slopes directly above, with easy access to everything. Walking is actually possible here, which it isn't in most of LA, and most of the city's best restaurants and bars are a short walk rather than a long drive apart.
