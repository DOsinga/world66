---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in West Hollywood"
type: section
---

When looking for a place to enjoy the **West Hollywood nightlife**, you have to be prepared to face the pressures of exclusivity--unless you'd rather have a low-key evening. No matter what type of traveler you are, you may enjoy spending the night at one of the following:

**SkyBar**

If you're looking for an place to spot Hollywood A-listers (and B-listers), this is it. Located at the Mondrian Hotel at 8440 Sunset Boulevard, the SkyBar is notorious for having bouncers who are picky about who gets in. If you hope to make the cut, dress to a tee, be prepared to spend a lot of money and expect to wait in a long line. Young and attractive people (particularly women) have a better chance of getting in than most, but if you dress up and don't let on that you're a tourist, you may have a chance.

The view inside is breathtaking. As the name implies, you'll get an aerial view of Los Angeles, which is spectacular in the dark. The open-air lounge is comfortable and aesthetically pleasing. The SkyBar has a wide array of wine and liquor, which is somewhat on the pricey end (anywhere from $15 to $30 a glass). Parking is $20, unless you want to park down the street for $10. This bar is open daily from eight in the evening until two in the morning.

**O Bar Restaurant & Lounge**

A slightly more low-key location is the O Bar Restaurant & Lounge at 8279 Santa Monica Boulevard. The lounge is less exclusive than some nightclubs, but dressing up is a good idea. You'll love the one-of-a-kind eclectic decor, which features Buddhist statues and working fountains, colorful light features and wavy lines. You can come just for drinks or order a meal. The full bar averages around $10 per drink. Meals cost somewhere between $15 and $30 on average. The O Bar is open daily until two in the morning. If you get there in time for happy hour (six to eight in the evening), you can get shots for as little as $2.50 to $5.

**Pantages Theater**

If you can't get to New York, let Hollywood bring Broadway to you. Located at 6233 Hollywood Boulevard, the Pantages Theater regularly shows traveling Broadway productions. Spending an evening at the Pantages is ideal nighttime entertainment for travelers who prefer to stay away from the nightclub scene, for travelers who enjoy a dinner and a show and for travelers with older children along. Tickets cost between $60 and $290, depending on your seating arrangement.
