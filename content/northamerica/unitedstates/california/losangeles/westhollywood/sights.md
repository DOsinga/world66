---
title: "Sights in West Hollywood"
type: section
---

West Hollywood remains one of the trendiest cities in Southern
California. Teaming with upscale homes and a thriving gay community,
this off-shoot of Los Angeles offers a variety of attractions ideal for
all tastes. Whether your interests lie in shopping, dining, recreation
or the arts, West Hollywood is the place to be.

**The Historic Sunset Strip**

For starters, West Hollywood is home to the famous Sunset Strip.
This world renowned stretch of Sunset Boulevard plays host to some of
the most beloved venues in the music industry. Audiophiles can catch a
show at such iconic clubs as Whisky a Go Go, The Roxy Theatre and The
Troubadour. Many big name bands, like The Doors, Van Halen, Blondie,
Guns N Roses and Nirvana, played early gigs in these settings. Also
located on the Sunset Strip, The House of Blues features some of the
biggest acts in music today.

Vacationers who feel like splurging can book a room at the Strip's
legendary Chateau Marmont. This charming hotel has served as a second
home to Hollywood royalty since 1931. Anyone can enjoy a drink at the
nearby Bar Marmont, although hotel guests receive priority seating. You
can unwind with a cocktail at other West Hollywood hotspots including
Formosa Cafe, Rainbow Bar and Grill, and the Saddle Ranch Chop House.

**Shopping, Dining and the Arts in WeHo**

Those in search
of a more low-key scene can embark on a shopping excursion at such
designer stores as Marc Jacobs, Paul Smith, and Fred Segal. Meanwhile,
literary gurus may spend their days perusing the shelves at Book Soup
or the Bodhi Tree Book Store.

Treat yourself to a leisurely meal at one of the many restaurants in
West Hollywood. Located on Melrose Avenue, The Urth Caffe remains a
favorite amongst locals with its mouthwatering organic sandwiches and
coffees. Travelers with heartier appetites will appreciate Barney's
Beanery, a true WeHo dining institution, which boasts a massive and
eclectic food and beer menu.

You may also choose to spend the day touring The Pacific Design
Center. An architectural marvel in itself, this facility hosts designer
furniture showrooms, two upscale restaurants and a Museum of
Contemporary Art gallery.

**Halloween in West Hollywood**

The Santa Monica Boulevard portion of West Hollywood sits lined with
even more shops and restaurants. This area also hosts the largest
Halloween street festival in the United States, the West Hollywood
Halloween Carnaval. Casually walk the streets as you take in some of
the most elaborate costumes you have ever witnessed.
