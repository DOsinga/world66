---
title: "When to Go in Los Angeles"
type: section
---

Visiting Los Angeles is good any time of the year, but the spring is
probably the best. The weather isn't as hot as it is in the summer and
the beaches aren't near as crowded.

**Weather**

During the spring finding a hotel isn't as hard as trying to find
one in the summer. The city is in a desert area so rain isn't usually a
problem even in the spring. At the beginning of the summer the smog has
a tendency to descend upon the city and in the fall the hot Santa Ana
winds bring the danger of fire. Going in the spring bypasses both these
situations.

**Events**

There are several events that occur during the spring in Los
Angeles. March is the month for the Irish Fair and Music Festival at
the LA county fairgrounds. In April the Toyota Grand Prix is presented
in Long Beach. Enjoy the Indy-class racing along with all the
entertainment it brings during the weekend. If you enjoy the
Renaissance period the Renaissance Pleasure Faire is held in nearby San
Bernardino on weekends during late April to Memorial Day. Here you
would find performers dressed in period costumes, an Elizabethan
marketplace and lots of food. The start of baseball season is in April
so be sure to take in a game.

**Festivals**

In May Cinco de Mayo is celebrated with flair. There are many events
from festivals, craft fairs, and performances not to mention the
Mexican food. Further in to spring is the National Orange Show in San
Bernardino where there are crafts, rides, food, celebrity entertainment
and livestock shows in a county fair type atmosphere. The Venice Art
Walk is a weekend of guided tours in galleries and studios in the
area. Near the end of May is the Long Beach Lesbian and Gay pride
Parade and Festival in Long Beach. There is a parade with over 100
decorated floats, food, music, dancing and more. If you enjoy blues you
will love the Doheny Blues Festival at Doheny State Beach. There are
three stages where entertainers perform blues, rock and soul. Arts and
crafts are featured along with music memorabilia. This occurs in late
May.

Other festivities during the spring is Cuban music and culture
festival, American Indian Pow Wows, Banjo and fiddle fest, the NoHo
Theatre and Arts Festival, and Strawberry festival. There is much more
to do in LA during the spring and you can do it without all the crowds
and without the hot weather.
