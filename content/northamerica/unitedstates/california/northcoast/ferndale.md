Ferndale is a beautifully preserved Victorian village in the Eel River valley of Humboldt County. The main street is lined with ornate "butterfat palaces" — the grand homes built by prosperous dairy farmers in the late 1800s — and the whole town has the feel of a living museum, without the stuffiness. It is a pleasant stop on a drive up Highway 101, worth an hour or two to walk the main street and admire the architecture.

The town's biggest event is the Kinetic Grand Championship on Memorial Day weekend, a three-day race of elaborately decorated human-powered vehicles that runs from [Arcata](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/northcoast/arcata) to Ferndale over land, water, and sand. It is gloriously absurd and draws big crowds.

Ferndale is also the starting point for the scenic drive along the Lost Coast, one of the most remote stretches of California coastline. The Mattole Road climbs steeply out of town before dropping down to the wild, unpopulated beaches at Cape Mendocino.
