The Ford House serves as the visitor center for Mendocino Headlands State Park and doubles as a small museum covering the town's history. Built in 1854 for Jerome Ford, one of the earliest settlers, the house is a good example of the Cape Cod–style construction that defines the village. Inside, exhibits trace Mendocino's origins as a lumber port and its transformation into an artists' colony. A scale model of the town as it appeared in the late 19th century is particularly useful for understanding the layout. The adjacent bluffs offer immediate access to the headlands trails.
