The adobe is what remains of General Mariano Vallejo's enormous Rancho Petaluma, which once sprawled across some 66,000 acres. Built in the 1830s and 1840s using Native American labor, it was one of the largest private ranchos in California. Today the two-story adobe stands in good condition with period furnishings and interpretive exhibits. Admission is a few dollars; the site is quiet and rarely crowded.
