Santa Rosa is the biggest city in Sonoma County and the de facto capital of California's North Coast wine country. It's not a destination most tourists plan around — they come for the vineyards — but the city itself has more going on than people expect. A revitalized downtown with good restaurants, the legacy of Charles M. Schulz (who lived and worked here for decades), and easy access to the Russian River Valley make it worth more than a drive-through.

The heart of town is Railroad Square, a cluster of restored stone buildings from the early 1900s with shops and restaurants. Fourth Street runs through the main downtown strip, where you'll find most of the dining and nightlife. It's walkable and pleasant, if not exactly charming — Santa Rosa was rebuilt after major earthquake and fire damage, so the architecture is a mix of old survivors and modern fill.

North of the city, the Russian River Valley opens up with more than fifty wineries, hiking in Armstrong Redwoods State Reserve, and the small resort town of Guerneville. You can canoe past vineyards on the river itself. To the east, the Sonoma Valley wineries around Kenwood and Glen Ellen are a short drive away.
