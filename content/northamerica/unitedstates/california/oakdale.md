Oakdale is a small agricultural town in Stanislaus County, about 25 km northeast of Modesto in California's Central Valley. It calls itself the "Cowboy Capital of the World" and hosts a major rodeo every spring. The town sits at the edge of the Sierra Nevada foothills and serves as a gateway to the Stanislaus River canyon and the recreational areas around Knights Ferry, where river rafting and kayaking are popular in summer.
