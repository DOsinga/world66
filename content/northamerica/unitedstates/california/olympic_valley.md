Olympic Valley, formerly known as Squaw Valley, sits in the Sierra Nevada just northwest of Lake Tahoe. The valley is best known as the site of the 1960 Winter Olympics and home to Palisades Tahoe, one of California's largest ski resorts with over 6,000 acres of skiable terrain across two mountains.

In winter, the resort draws skiers and snowboarders with reliable snowfall and varied terrain from beginner groomers to serious backcountry chutes. Summer brings hiking, mountain biking, and a scenic aerial tram ride to High Camp at 8,200 feet with panoramic views of Lake Tahoe. The valley is a short drive from Tahoe City along Highway 89, making it easy to combine a ski trip with the wider Lake Tahoe area.
