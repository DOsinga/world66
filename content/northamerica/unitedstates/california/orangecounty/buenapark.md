Buena Park is a city in northwestern Orange County, best known as the home of Knott's Berry Farm — the nation's oldest themed amusement park. What began in the 1920s as a roadside berry stand and ghost town attraction has grown into a full resort destination, drawing visitors from across Southern California and beyond.

The city sits about 25 miles southeast of downtown Los Angeles, making it an easy day trip or a convenient base for exploring both LA and Orange County. Beyond the theme park, Buena Park is a largely residential community with straightforward freeway access and a practical range of hotels clustered along Beach Boulevard near the park entrance.
