Dana Point sits on the southern [Orange County](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/orangecounty) coast between [Laguna Beach](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/orangecounty/lagunabeach) and [San Clemente](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/orangecounty/sanclemente), built around one of the few harbors in the region. Named after Richard Henry Dana Jr., who described the headlands in his 1840 memoir *Two Years Before the Mast*, the town today revolves around its harbor, its surf breaks, and its whale-watching boats.

The Dana Point Harbor is the center of activity. Dolphin and whale-watching trips depart year-round, and sport fishing, jet skiing, and boat rentals are all available at the marina. Mariner's Village on Dana Wharf re-creates the feel of the port's early trading days through its shops and cafes. You can also catch a ferry to Catalina Island, about an hour offshore.

The Ocean Institute, at the harbor's edge, specializes in oceanography and marine science. It operates three research vessels and runs tide-pool tours and educational programs. Nearby, the Nature Interpretive Center offers a small but worthwhile introduction to the area's natural history. Heritage Park, up on the bluffs, has direct ocean views and makes a fine picnic spot.

Doheny State Beach, just south of the harbor, is the main stretch of sand. It is one of the best-known camping beaches in Orange County and popular with swimmers, surfers, and cyclists. Further south, Capistrano Beach is a quieter residential stretch, while Poche Beach on the border with San Clemente draws surfers looking for less crowded breaks.
