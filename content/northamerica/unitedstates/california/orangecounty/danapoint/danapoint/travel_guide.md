This **Dana Point travel guide** will introduce you to the various holiday options the city offers. If you&rsquo;re wondering where to stay or what to do, the following lines may be really helpful.**
**

**Location**

Dana Point is located in southern California, right between Los Angeles and San Diego. It is easily reachable by car, but there are other ways of getting there too – Amtrak&rsquo;s train stops in the nearby town of San Juan and there is an airport near Santa Ana.

**Accommodations**

There is a wide variety of hotels where you can stay at, ranging from 2-stars to 5-stars, with prices in the interval from $90 to $500 per night. The most luxurious is the Ritz Carlton, but there are some very nice cheap waterfront hotels too, like the Dana Point Harbor Inn for example. Camping is possible at the Doheny State Beach, known as the best camping site in Orange County.

**Things to Do**

Dana Point is a popular surfing destination and very suitable for beach holidays. The waters are safe for swimming, with many lifeguards around. Other activities you can practice on the beaches are sunbathing, biking and playing beach volleyball. The Dana Point City Harbor, one of a kind in the region, offers many interesting things to do - dolphin and whale watching trips, sport fishing, jet skiing and boat rentals. You can also take a nice day trip to Catalina Island, which is just an hour away.

**Beaches**

The main beach is Doheny State Beach, situated right next to the harbor. It offers all of the above-mentioned activities. Other beaches are the Capistrano Beach and Poche Beach. The Capistrano Beach is mainly a residential area, occupied by luxurious million-dollar private homes. Poche Beach borders between Dana Point and San Clemente and is preferred mainly by surfers.

**Places to Visit**

Except the City Harbor and the beaches, there are very few interesting places to visit around, but they still worth being seen. One of them is the Ocean Institute, specializing in oceanography and marine science. The institute operates 3 research vessels in the Dana Point Harbor, having mostly educational purposes. The Nature Interpretative Center is another interesting place – a small building, where you can get introduced to the rich natural history of the area. Finally, you can go for a picnic at the Heritage Park, which has a direct view to the ocean.
