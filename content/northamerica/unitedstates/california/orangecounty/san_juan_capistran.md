San Juan Capistrano is the oldest town in Orange County, and it shows. While the rest of Southern California races toward the next development, this small city has kept its historic core intact — Spanish colonial architecture, a crumbling adobe mission, and a walkable downtown that feels genuinely old rather than theme-park old.

The centerpiece is the Mission San Juan Capistrano, founded in 1776 and one of the most evocative in California. The Great Stone Church collapsed in an earthquake in 1812 and was never rebuilt; its ruined walls still stand open to the sky, draped in bougainvillea. The Serra Chapel — the oldest building still in use in California — is small and dim and worth a few quiet minutes.

The town is famously associated with the cliff swallows that migrate here every year from Argentina, arriving around St. Joseph's Day (19 March) and departing on 23 October. It's become a tourist ritual, but the birds really do come back, and the sight of thousands of swallows wheeling over the mission is genuinely spectacular.

Beyond the mission, the Los Rios Historic District preserves a handful of adobes from the Spanish and Mexican eras — some of the oldest surviving civilian buildings in California. It's a short walk from the Amtrak station, which makes San Juan Capistrano one of the easiest day trips from [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles) or [San Diego](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego) by train.
