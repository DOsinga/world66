Oxnard is a working agricultural city on the Ventura County coast, about an hour northwest of Los Angeles. It is not glamorous — the strawberry fields and the port set the tone — but it has genuine beaches, good Mexican food, and serves as the main departure point for boats to Channel Islands National Park.

The harbour area at Channel Islands Harbor has restaurants, kayak rentals, and the ferry terminal for trips to Anacapa and Santa Cruz islands. Oxnard's beaches — Hollywood Beach, Oxnard Beach Park, Silver Strand — are wide, uncrowded, and free of the scene that dominates the LA coast. The Heritage Square district preserves a cluster of Victorian houses moved here from around the county.

For most travelers, Oxnard is a base rather than a destination: close to the Channel Islands, cheaper than Santa Barbara up the coast, and with better taquerias than either.
