Wedged on the northern tip of the Monterey Peninsula between Pebble Beach, Carmel, and Monterey, Pacific Grove has managed to retain an unhurried, small-town feel that its neighbours long since abandoned. Locals call it P.G. Parking is free, black-tailed deer wander through pine-shaded yards, and monarch butterflies gather by the thousands in local parks each autumn.

The coastline is Pacific Grove's great asset. Almost all of it is unobstructed by development, and winding paths make the surf-swept coves accessible to walkers and cyclists. Sea lions and harbour seals sun themselves on offshore boulders, sea otters float in the kelp beds, and pelicans dive just offshore. The Victorian bed-and-breakfast inns along Ocean View Boulevard add some architectural charm.

Pacific Grove sits at the northern end of the 17-Mile Drive. It is a ten-minute drive from the Monterey Bay Aquarium.
