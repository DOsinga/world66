Palm Springs sits in the Coachella Valley, ringed by mountains on all sides and baked by desert sun for 354 days a year. It was a glamorous midcentury retreat — Frank Sinatra, Dean Martin, and the Rat Pack crowd all had homes here — and that era still defines the town's character. The midcentury modern architecture is the best-preserved collection in the country, and "Modernism Week" each February draws architecture fans from around the world.

Today Palm Springs is a mix of retirees, weekenders from Los Angeles (it is about two hours east), and a large LGBTQ+ community that has helped keep the town culturally interesting. The downtown strip along Palm Canyon Drive has good restaurants, galleries, and a Thursday evening street market (VillageFest).

The Palm Springs Aerial Tramway is the highlight for visitors: a rotating cable car that climbs from the desert floor to the top of Mount San Jacinto at 2,596 metres, where the temperature can be 20°C cooler and the views stretch across the entire valley. In winter there is often snow at the top while it is warm enough for shorts below. The Indian Canyons, just south of town, offer easy hiking through fan palm oases.
