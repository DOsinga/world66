Palo Alto is a pleasant, leafy city on the San Francisco Peninsula, best known as the home of Stanford University and the heart of Silicon Valley. The university campus — 8,000 acres of sandstone arcades, palm-lined avenues, and open space — is the main attraction and is freely accessible on foot or by bicycle.

The Cantor Arts Center houses a strong collection of Rodin sculptures (one of the largest outside Paris) and a wide-ranging permanent collection. Hoover Tower, the campus landmark, offers panoramic views from its observation deck. The Stanford Dish, a radio telescope on the hills behind campus, is the starting point for one of the most popular walks in the area — a 3.5-mile loop with views over the Bay.

Downtown University Avenue has good restaurants, bookshops, and cafes, and retains a walkable, small-town character despite the tech wealth surrounding it. Palo Alto is well positioned for day trips to San Francisco (40 minutes north by Caltrain), the Pacific coast at Half Moon Bay, and the redwoods of the Santa Cruz Mountains.
