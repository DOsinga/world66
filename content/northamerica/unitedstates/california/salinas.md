Salinas is best known as the hometown of John Steinbeck, and the agricultural valley that surrounds it is the landscape he wrote about in East of Eden, Of Mice and Men, and The Grapes of Wrath. The National Steinbeck Center downtown is the main draw for literary travelers, housing manuscripts, personal effects, and the camper truck from Travels with Charley.

The city sits at the northern end of the Salinas Valley, sometimes called the "Salad Bowl of the World" for its staggering agricultural output. The surrounding fields produce most of the country’s lettuce, broccoli, and strawberries. Salinas itself is a working agricultural city -- unpretentious and largely Latino -- and it does not have the polished feel of its coastal neighbors.

What it does have is location. [Monterey](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/monterey) and the coast are just 20 minutes west over the hills, Big Sur is an hour south, and the Pinnacles National Park is an hour to the southeast. If you are exploring this stretch of California and want affordable lodging away from the tourist markup, Salinas is a practical base.
