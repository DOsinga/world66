San Jose is California's oldest city, its original state capital, and the self-proclaimed capital of Silicon Valley. With over a million residents, it is the third largest city in the state — and one of the most ethnically diverse, with residents speaking more than 50 languages.

For visitors, San Jose is more interesting than its sprawling suburban layout suggests. The Tech Interactive (formerly the Tech Museum of Innovation) is packed with hands-on exhibits, and the Winchester Mystery House — a rambling 160-room Victorian mansion built by the eccentric heir to the Winchester rifle fortune — is genuinely bizarre and worth the visit. The Children's Discovery Museum is one of the best of its kind on the West Coast.

San Jose's Japantown is one of only three remaining Japantowns in America, a neighbourhood full of tradition with excellent restaurants and shops. The city has a growing art scene, with galleries like MACLA, ICA, and Works showcasing local and international artists. The public art programme is one of the largest in the country — you will encounter sculptures and installations throughout the downtown area.

Downtown San Jose has been steadily improving, with the area around San Pedro Square Market offering good restaurants and nightlife. The city's central location also makes it a practical base for exploring the rest of the Bay Area.
