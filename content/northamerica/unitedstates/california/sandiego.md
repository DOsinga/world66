San Diego sits at the southwestern corner of the continental United States, against the Mexican border, and benefits from one of the most agreeable climates in the country — high 60s to mid 70s most of the year, long stretches without rain, and an Pacific that's swimmable from June through October. The city is more relaxed than [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles), more compact than San Francisco, and built almost entirely around the outdoors.

The downtown is anchored by the **[Gaslamp Quarter](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/downtown)**, sixteen blocks of restored late-Victorian buildings full of restaurants, bars and music. Just across the bay sits **[Coronado](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/coronado)** with its long white-sand beach and the famous Hotel del Coronado. North of downtown, **Balboa Park** — 1,200 acres of Spanish-colonial pavilions left over from the 1915 Panama-California Exposition — houses more than a dozen museums and the world-famous San Diego Zoo. A full day there is easy.

The beach districts run up the coast. **[La Jolla](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/lajolla)** is the upscale one, with cliffs, sea caves and a colony of harbour seals. **Mission Beach** and **Pacific Beach** are the surf-and-boardwalk neighbourhoods; **Ocean Beach** is the scrappier, hippie-leaning alternative. Further north along the coast, the suburbs of **[Del Mar](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/delmar)**, **[Solana Beach](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/solanabeach)** and **[Encinitas](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/oceanside)** all have their own surf breaks and easy charm.

Don't miss the food. The Mexican border is twenty minutes south, and the fish-taco-and-carne-asada culture here is among the best in the United States. Old Town has the historic Mexican restaurants; the cheaper, better stuff is in the strip malls of Barrio Logan and City Heights. The city also has a serious craft beer scene — well over a hundred breweries in the county — that nearly rivals Portland's.

San Diego is also a major Navy town: aircraft carriers come and go from the harbour, and Coronado is dominated by the Naval Base. The retired aircraft carrier USS Midway, moored downtown, is one of the best museum visits in the city.
