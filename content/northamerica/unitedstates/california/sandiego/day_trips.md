---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

San Diego is well-positioned for day trips into the desert, the mountains and across the Mexican border. **Tijuana**, twenty minutes south, has reinvented itself in the past decade with a serious craft beer scene, an excellent Caesar salad (invented at Caesar's Restaurant on Avenida Revolución in 1924), and a wave of confident young chefs. Cross on foot at San Ysidro and walk in; bring your passport for the return.

**Anza-Borrego Desert State Park**, two hours east, is the largest state park in California — slot canyons, palm oases, badlands and (in good years) a spectacular spring wildflower bloom. **[Julian](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego/julian)**, en route, is a small mountain town famous for apple pie. **Joshua Tree National Park** is a longer haul — about three hours — but doable as a long day; the surreal rock formations and desert plant life justify it.

**Disneyland** in Anaheim is 90 minutes north on a good traffic day. **Temecula Valley** wine country, an hour northeast, has more than 40 wineries spread across rolling hills.
