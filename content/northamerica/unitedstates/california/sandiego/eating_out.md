---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

San Diego's food scene is built on Mexican and seafood, with a serious craft beer culture surrounding both. **Mexican food** is the city's strength — given the border is twenty minutes south, the carne asada, fish tacos and Cali-style burritos here are among the best in the United States. Seek out the strip-mall taquerias of Barrio Logan, City Heights and National City; the touristy Old Town restaurants are fine but rarely the best in town.

**Little Italy** downtown is the upscale dining district — modern Italian, seafood, ramen — with a good Saturday farmers' market. **North Park** and **South Park** have the city's most interesting independent restaurants, particularly along 30th Street. **La Jolla** trends fine-dining and pricey; **Pacific Beach** is the casual beachfront spot for fish tacos and post-surf burritos.

For seafood, the **Tuna Harbor Dockside Market** on Saturdays is where the local fleet sells direct off the boats. The fish tacos at any halfway-decent taqueria are the affordable, ubiquitous option.
