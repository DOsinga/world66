Imperial Beach is the most south-westerly city in the continental United States, sitting right on the Mexican border where the Tijuana River meets the Pacific. It has a scrappier, more local feel than the polished beach towns further north along the San Diego coast — no boutique hotels, no upscale boardwalk, just a long stretch of sand, a fishing pier, and a neighbourhood that still feels like a real place rather than a resort.

The Tijuana River National Estuarine Research Reserve, just south of town, is one of the last undeveloped coastal wetlands in Southern California and excellent for bird-watching. The beach itself is wide and uncrowded, popular with surfers and families. Every July, Imperial Beach hosts the US Open Sandcastle Competition, which draws elaborate sculptors from around the country.

The border fence runs directly into the ocean here, a surreal sight from the pier. Coronado and downtown San Diego are a short drive north, making Imperial Beach an affordable base for exploring the wider area.
