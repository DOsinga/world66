Julian is a small gold-rush town in the Cuyamaca Mountains east of [San Diego](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sandiego), sitting at about 1,250 metres. Gold was discovered here in 1870, and the town's Main Street still has the look of a 19th-century mining settlement — wooden storefronts, a one-room schoolhouse, and the Eagle and High Peak mines, which offer underground tours.

Today Julian is best known for its apple orchards and the pies that come from them. The town is surrounded by apple farms, and several bakeries on Main Street compete for the title of best apple pie in the county. It makes an easy day trip from San Diego — about an hour's drive — and is especially popular in autumn when the harvest is in full swing.
