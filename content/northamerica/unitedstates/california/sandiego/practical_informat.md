**MONEY

**Most credit cards are
widely accepted at all outlets, but having a little cash on hand is
always a good idea. The best way to get cash all over the US and San Diego
is at an ATM (automated teller machine).

**TELEPHONE**

The area code for San Diego is 619, in northern coastal
areas is 858, and 760 is used is outlying areas. With the boom of
telecommunications, pay phones are not as abundant as they used to be,
but still widely available; you just have look a little harder.

**BUSINESS HOURS**

Banks and Post offices are open mainly M-F from 9am
to 5pm, and some open on Sat. from 10am to 12pm. Most retail
businesses are open from 10am to 6pm 7 days a week. The bigger
department stores and shopping malls operate from 10am to 9pm M-Sat.,
and 10am to 6pm on Sunday.

**ELECTRICITY**

The United States uses 110-120 volts AC, compared to
220-240 volts in Europe. Most international countries will need a
converter to change your voltage with electronic devices.

**HOSPITALS **

UCSD Medical Center 619/543-6400 in Hillcrest, UCSD
Thornton Hospital 858/657-7600 in La Jolla, Coronado Hospital
619/435-6251. In the case of an emergency, dial 911.

**PUBLIC RESTROOMS**

You should not have a problem finding a public
restroom, they are in every shopping mall or park. Ask before using a private bathroom at a restaurant or coffee shop; most restrooms are reserved for customers only.

**SMOKING **

Smoking is prohibited in all indoor public places in the
State of California. San Diego in particular has banned smoking from
all city beaches and city parks.

**DRINKING **

The legal drinking age in the US is 21, and proof of
identity is required. Beer, wine and liquor are sold from 6am to 2am
daily.
