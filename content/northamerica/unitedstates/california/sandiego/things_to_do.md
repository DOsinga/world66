---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

San Diego's headline attractions cluster in three places. **Balboa Park**, just north of downtown, is a 1,200-acre cultural complex of Spanish-colonial pavilions built for the 1915 Panama-California Exposition. It contains seventeen museums (the San Diego Museum of Art, the Natural History Museum, the Museum of Photographic Arts, the Air & Space Museum among them) plus the **San Diego Zoo** — one of the largest and most respected zoos in the world, with around 4,000 animals across 100 acres. A single day in Balboa Park covers a fraction of it; pace yourself.

**Downtown** is anchored by the Gaslamp Quarter and the bayfront. The **USS Midway Museum**, a retired aircraft carrier moored at Navy Pier, is the standout — you can walk the flight deck, climb into the cockpits of restored fighters, and tour the hangar bays. **Seaport Village**, **Little Italy** and the harbour walk are easy add-ons.

**Old Town**, just north of downtown, preserves the Mexican-era settlement that San Diego grew out of. Adobe houses, mariachis and reasonable Mexican restaurants make it a lively hour's wander even if much of it is touristy. **Cabrillo National Monument** at the tip of Point Loma marks the spot where Europeans first landed in California, and the views back across the bay and the city are the best in San Diego.

For families, **SeaWorld** on Mission Bay is the obvious choice — controversial in recent years but still a popular day out. The **Wild Animal Park** in Escondido (now Safari Park), 30 miles north, is the zoo's open-range sister: 1,800 acres where animals roam freely and you tour by tram or off-road truck.
