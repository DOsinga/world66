[Alcatraz](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/alcatraz_island) is genuinely worth doing. The ferry ride across the bay, the walk up to the cellhouse, the audio tour narrated by former guards and prisoners — it is a serious piece of public history presented with intelligence and atmosphere. The island also has remarkable views back at the city skyline, which you cannot get anywhere else.

Book the first ferry of the morning (usually 9am from Pier 33) to have the island to yourself before the midday crowds. The audio tour takes 45 minutes to an hour; add another 30 for the grounds and the views. The last ferry back gives you the afternoon on the waterfront.

[Fisherman's Wharf](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/fishermans_wharf) is the tourist district San Francisco is slightly embarrassed by, but a few things are worth finding in the noise. The [Musée Mécanique](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/musee_mecanique) at Pier 45 is a collection of over 300 antique coin-operated machines — fortune tellers, peep shows, mechanical dioramas, and early arcade games — that is one of the genuinely strange and wonderful things in the city. Free to enter; machines take quarters.

The [USS Pampanito](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/uss_pampanito) at Pier 45 is a restored World War II submarine you can walk through — claustrophobic, detailed, and sobering. Also at Pier 39: sea lions have colonised the docking floats since 1989 and show no signs of leaving. They are loud, smelly, and excellent.

Eat a cup of chowder in a sourdough bowl if the occasion demands it. Then walk west along the waterfront toward Ghirardelli Square and the quieter parts of the bay shore.
