The [Ferry Building](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/ferry_building) Farmers Market runs Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday, but Saturday is the one to come for — the full market, with some 100 vendors spread along the Embarcadero and through the building's ground floor, draws the best of Northern California's farms and producers. The ferry building itself, a Beaux-Arts terminal from 1898, houses permanent food vendors including Acme Bread, Cowgirl Creamery, and Hog Island Oyster, all worth queuing for.

Get there by nine. The bread from Acme goes fast, the oysters from Hog Island are best when the morning is still cool, and the market is most pleasant before the tourist numbers build up by midday. The local produce is genuinely exceptional — this is one of the best agricultural regions in the world, and the Saturday market reflects that: stone fruit in summer, citrus in winter, mushrooms and root vegetables in between.

Eat on the long dock that runs behind the building, with the bay in front of you and the Bay Bridge to the left. Ferries come and go; container ships pass; the bay light is at its best in the morning. This is one of the finest places to eat breakfast in San Francisco.

The [Exploratorium](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/exploratorium) is a short walk along the Embarcadero — the hands-on science museum occupies the renovated Pier 15 and is worth an hour or two, especially if the market crowds have become too much.
