Go early. The Golden Gate Bridge in the morning — before the tourist shuttles arrive, while the bay is still quiet and the light is low and orange — is one of the great urban walks in the world. Park on the Marin side or take a rideshare to the toll plaza and start walking by seven.

The bridge is 1.7 miles from end to end. Walking takes about 30 to 40 minutes without stopping, considerably longer if you stop to look, which you will. The views down to the bay, back toward the city, and out to the Pacific are all different and all worth time. On clear mornings, the towers catch the sun while the city is still in shadow below.

At the south end, walk down to [Fort Point](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/fort_point) — a Civil War-era brick fortress tucked directly beneath the bridge's south anchorage. The view upward from here, the bridge arching overhead, is one of the best photographs in San Francisco.

Loop back east along the waterfront through [Crissy Field](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/crissy_field), the restored wetlands and beach along the Presidio's bay shore. Egrets fish in the shallows, kite surfers cross the bay, and the walk back into the city past the sailboat anchorage and the Palace of Fine Arts takes another 30 minutes. Coffee at the warming hut at the east end of Crissy Field before you head back.
