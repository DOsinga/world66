[Golden Gate Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/golden_gate_park) is 1,017 acres of engineered wilderness — an improbable ribbon of green stretching from the Haight to the ocean, built on former sand dunes in the 1870s. The two world-class museums at its eastern end are reason enough to spend an afternoon here, but the park itself rewards wandering.

The [de Young Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/de_young_museum) is the main art museum, with collections spanning American art, African and Oceanic works, and textiles, housed in a striking copper-clad building by Herzog & de Meuron that has weathered to green-brown in the park air. The observation tower is free and gives a 360-degree view over the park and city.

Across the concourse, the [California Academy of Sciences](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/california_academy_of_sciences) combines a natural history museum, planetarium, and aquarium under a living roof of native plants. The four-story rainforest dome and the underground aquarium are both exceptional. Book tickets in advance.

Between the two museums, the [Japanese Tea Garden](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/japanese_tea_garden) is the oldest public Japanese garden in the US, with pagodas, koi ponds, a drum bridge, and tea service in a pavilion. Come mid-week and it is genuinely peaceful.

The [San Francisco Botanical Garden](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/sf_botanical_garden) is free for city residents and worth an hour of wandering — 55 acres of garden with collections from California, Chile, South Africa, and New Zealand. In winter, the magnolias are spectacular.
