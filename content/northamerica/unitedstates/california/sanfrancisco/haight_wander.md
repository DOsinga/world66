[Haight-Ashbury](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/haight_ashbury) traded its 1967 counterculture for vintage shops and tourist foot traffic long ago, but it remains one of the more interesting neighbourhoods to walk through in San Francisco — the Victorians are well-preserved, the shops are genuinely eclectic, and the density of music history per block is unmatched anywhere in the city.

[Amoeba Music](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/amoeba_music) on Haight Street is the main event: one of the last great independent record stores in America, occupying a converted bowling alley and stocking used vinyl, CDs, and DVDs across almost every genre. The used jazz and soul sections are particularly deep. Go without a plan and see what you find.

Walk east along Haight Street and then climb to [Buena Vista Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/buena_vista_park) — a densely wooded hill with trails through eucalyptus and redwood, and views over the city from the top. It is rarely crowded and the contrast between the urban street below and the forest above is striking.

A short walk north brings you to Alamo Square and the [Painted Ladies](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/painted_ladies) — the row of painted Victorian houses with the downtown skyline rising behind them, probably the most photographed view in San Francisco. Come on a weekday morning to have the park to yourself.
