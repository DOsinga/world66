The Mission catches the afternoon sun while the rest of San Francisco is in fog. Start at [Balmy Alley](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/balmy_alley), off 24th Street — a narrow residential alley whose garage doors and fences have been painted with murals since the 1970s, when local artists began using them to comment on Central American politics. The work changes as new pieces go up over old ones. Walk slowly. [Clarion Alley](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/clarion_alley), three blocks north between 17th and 18th, is the other essential mural corridor — more political, more frequently updated, with a different aesthetic sensibility from Balmy.

The [Mission District](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/mission_district) is San Francisco's most contested neighbourhood, changing fast but still densely Latino along the main corridors. 24th Street between Mission and Potrero has the best concentration of taquerias, panaderías, and fruit stands. [Taqueria Cancun](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/taqueria_cancun) on Mission Street is the classic stop: a super burrito for under six dollars, eaten at the counter or at one of the sidewalk tables.

End the afternoon at [Mission Dolores Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/mission_dolores_park), which tilts south toward the sun and fills on warm afternoons with the mixed, cheerful density that still makes San Francisco exceptional. Bring something from the taqueria and find a patch of grass with a view of the downtown skyline. The park gets golden for hours on sunny afternoons.
