The Mission is San Francisco's most vibrant and complex neighbourhood -- a place where century-old murals cover entire alleyways, legendary taquerias serve burritos the size of your forearm, and Dolores Park fills with thousands of people on every sunny afternoon. It is also the warmest part of the city, sheltered from the fog by Twin Peaks, which makes it the default destination when the rest of San Francisco is socked in.

Named after Mission Dolores, the oldest building in the city, the neighbourhood has deep Latino roots visible in the murals of [Balmy Alley](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfranciscobayarea/sanfrancisco/balmy_alley), the shops along 24th Street, and the bakeries and taquerias on Mission Street. The 24th Street BART station is the heart of it. Valencia Street, one block west, has become the city's trendiest corridor of restaurants, bars, bookshops, and boutiques.
