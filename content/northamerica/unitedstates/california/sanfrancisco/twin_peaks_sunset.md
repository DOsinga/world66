[Twin Peaks](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/twin_peaks) is San Francisco's most visited viewpoint, and on weekends it shows — the parking lot fills with tour buses and the summit is crowded. But in the hour before sunset on a clear evening, especially on weekdays, the view from the two peaks — a 360-degree panorama taking in the entire city, the Bay, the bridges, Marin, the peninsula — is worth whatever company you keep up there.

Drive or take a rideshare to the upper parking lot. The walk to the north summit takes about five minutes. The view north takes in the downtown skyline, Alcatraz, the Golden Gate, and the hills of Marin beyond. The view south shows the outer neighbourhoods spreading across the peninsula to the horizon.

The best moment is the 20 minutes around sunset, when the light hits the downtown towers and the bay turns copper and then silver. Bring a layer — it is cold and windy up here even in summer.

For a less-visited alternative with a comparable view, [Corona Heights Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfrancisco/corona_heights_park) is a rocky outcrop above the Castro that offers one of the best city panoramas in San Francisco from a summit you can usually have nearly to yourself. The climb from the bottom of the park takes about 15 minutes on a dirt trail through scrub.
