---
title: "Getting Around in San Francisco Bay Area"
type: section
---

San Francisco is a substantial city with much to see. When planning a visit you'll need to consider how you'll get around this city with its hills and bridges many fascinating districts. Put your worries to rest because** San Francisco transportation** is convenient and a great ways to travel around this charming city by the bay.

**Public Buses & BART Subway
**

The city's metro system is an easy and affordable way to get around the city. Pick up a map at a bus or BART station and the city is at your fingertips. If you are still unsure of what bus to use just ask the bus driver, they are very friendly and knowledgeable. If you are going to be in San Francisco for more than a day or two, you can save money by buying a pass. The bus systems runs through out the whole city and usually operates until the late night hours.

**Taxis**

Flagging down a taxi in San Francisco is easy in most neighborhoods. Keep in mind that taxis can get very expensive if that is your main method of transportation. Prior to getting in the taxi ask how far your destination is. Your destination might be closer than you think and worth the walk.

**Walking**

San Francisco is a pedestrian friendly city and you will find most of the locals walking. Walking around the city is probably one of the best ways to really see San Francisco. Be sure to bring your walking shoes and be prepared for all the hills!

**Cable Cars**

Cable cars are a fun way to get a taste of old San Francisco city life. The waits can be long, but the cable cars do hit most popular tourist destinations. While taking a cable car is something you'll want to do, don't rely on it as your main transportation. They don't run throughout the city.

**Historic Trolley**

The trolley runs from the Castro district, Market Street and Fisherman's Wharf. If you are traveling between these popular destinations the trolley will get you there while allowing you to experience historic San Francisco.

**Rental Cars**

While rental cars are an option in San Francisco, they have their downside. Parking is hard to come by in San Francisco and when you do find parking it is very expensive. Traffic is also a problem in San Francisco and most people do not want to spend their vacation in traffic. If you do rent a car be sure to take caution when driving in San Francisco. There are numerous one-way streets and very steep hills.
