---
title: "Getting There in San Francisco Bay Area"
type: section
---

If traveling to **San Francisco**,** getting there** is easy either by air, rail, car or by boat. The San Francisco Bay Area encompasses a broad region surrounding the bay, which includes the cities of San Francisco, Oakland and San Jose amongst many others.

**Flights**

Flights arrive at either of three international airports - San Francisco, Oakland or San Jose - all providing convenient access to the San Francisco Bay area. The San Francisco International Airport (SFO) is located around 20 kilometers south of the city. The Oakland International Airport in the East Bay area, and San Jose International Airport in the Silicon Valley to the south, tend to service the majority of discount airline flights.

**Public Transportation**

Public transport options from the airports include the Bay Area Rapid Transit (BART), the Caltrain commuter rail or the SamTrans bus service. Other transport options include renting a car or catching a taxi. There are also plenty of shuttle services available.

**Freeways**

Alternatively, the San Francisco Bay Area is well serviced by an extensive network of freeways. Access US 101 or Interstate 280 from the south, or Interstate 80 from the east.

By long distance train, catch the Amtrak from San Francisco to Oakland. From there, take the local BART from the West Oakland station to the Embarcadero Station in San Francisco via the Trans-Bay Tunnel. It is renowned for being the longest immersed tunnel in the world.

**Ferries**

Travelling by boat is a scenic way to reach the Bay Area and to enjoy the famous skyline of the Golden Gate Bridge. Regular inter city ferry services run between the major cities in the Bay area. If cruising to San Francisco, plan to arrive at the famous Fisherman&rsquo;s Wharf.
