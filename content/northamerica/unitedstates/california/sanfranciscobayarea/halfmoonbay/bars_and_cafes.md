title: "Bars and Cafes in Half Moon Bay"
type: section
---

This town is located near San Francisco, but **Half Moon Bay bars and cafes** can hold their own. There are some very charming cafes to choose from. Below are a couple of places worth visiting during your stay in Half Moon Bay.

**Feed Your Sweet Tooth**

Moonside Bakery and Caf&eacute; is a delightful little European style bakery on Main Street. Open daily from 6:30am to 5pm, you can easily fulfill your breakfast or lunchtime needs here. In addition to the baked good and pastries, menu items include eggs, waffles and pancakes for breakfast and sandwiches, salads, and wood-fired pizzas for lunch. This moderately priced caf&eacute; has something to please every palate; they even have a children&rsquo;s menu. Bring the entire family for a meal and then a tasty treat.

**The Famous 3-Zero Caf&eacute;**

This famous restaurant has an award winning record of serving the best breakfasts on the coast for ten years running. It is open year round 8am -2pm weekdays and 7am-3pm on weekends. The food choice offers a wide variety of pancake house style food, served with a lot of creativity. Prices are moderate and within most budgets. Here, the &ldquo;best breakfasts on the coast&rdquo; are sure to please the entire family. The only two days this caf&eacute; is closed are Christmas and Thanksgiving, so you are sure to get a taste of what makes it so famous. Even the locals will tell you that you will not be disappointed.
