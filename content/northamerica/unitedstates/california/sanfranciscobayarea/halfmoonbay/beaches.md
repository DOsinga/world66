---
title: "Beaches in Half Moon Bay"
type: section
---

**Half Moon Bay beaches** offer miles of sandy beach, opportunities for surfing and for fishing, and hiking paths along the coastline. Or you can just lie back and be lazy on these beautiful beaches.

Most beaches have restaurants that serve delicious fresh seafood and there are nice bed-and-breakfasts to stay in as well as campgrounds for pitching your tent and enjoying the nature. Remember that the water in the ocean can be cold, and there can be some strong currents.

**Popular Beaches in Half Moon Bay**

From the city of Half Moon Bay, you can access the Half Moon Bay beaches from Francis Beach on Kelly Avenue, from Venice Beach on Venice Boulevard, and on Dunes Beach on Young Avenue. All are good beaches for sunbathing, swimming and picnics. Francis Beach also has a campground and hot showers and Venice Beach has outside showers.

If you want more from your beach holiday than sunbathing and swimming, the Coastside Trail is a three mile long path that follows the coastline along the beaches and is perfect for walking, bicycle riding or jogging. You can also go horse riding along a trail between Francis Beach and Roosevelt Beach. Nature lovers can enjoy the variety of bird life and you can even spot seals here. The Half Moon Bay State Beach Visitor Center has information about the beach and the coastline and its history and marine life.

**Beaches Around Half Moon Bay**

Majestic cliffs surround the cove on the Gray Whale Cove State Beach. This beach perfect for sunbathing and picnics. If you are lucky, you can spot a gray whale off the coast.

The Montara Beach is very clean and popular with visitors. There is a pretty old lighthouse here and the beach is wide and nice to walk along. Once you have had enough of sunbathing, there are trekking paths around the area and you can also spot some eagles and other bird life. You can also go fishing here.

The Bean Hollow State Beach and Pebble beach, south from Half Moon Bay, are great for sunbathing but also a good spot to do some fishing. There are tide pools here too, with sea anemones and other ocean creatures. For the more active visitors there is a walking trail along the beach.

For a quiet beach getaway, try the Redondo Beach. The beach is located just south of Half Moon Bay, near the Ritz-Carlton, and gets less visitors than many of the other, busier beaches in the area.
