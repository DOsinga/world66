**Planning Half Moon Bay Day Trips**

Most **Half Moon Bay day trips **involve exploring a vast range of outdoor activities. Half Moon Bay is among the most preferred of vacationing locations in California. It presents a plethora of outdoor options, including boating, kayaking, nature trails, fishing, surfing and whale watching. However, a slightly different and equally exhilarating day trip option is visiting the Ano Nuevo State Reserve.

#### **Getting Started: Reaching Ano Nuevo State Reserve **

This natural reserve is located along State Highway 1. It is at a distance of about 35 miles from the southern boundary of Half Moon Bay. It is easily accessible from neighboring cities like Santa Cruz and San Francisco. People have a range of options for traveling to Ano Nuevo. This includes boarding a local bus, hiring a taxi or driving a rental car. The entire stretch is well-marked with signs pointing towards the approaching wildlife reserve.

#### **What to Expect Once You Arrive**

Ano Nuevo Reserve can be visited at any time of the year. However, wildlife enthusiasts should check with the local authorities regarding which seal "season" is underway. This is because the major attraction of the reserve, the elephant seals, vary in their visibility according to season. During the months of December to February, the seal pups are often easily visible. Other seasons include the breeding season and the molting season. A tour is recommended for families to educate children about wildlife and humans existing in harmony with each other. Besides the giant seals, more than 300 different animals can be spotted here. The range of invertebrates is vast, including abalone, hermit crabs, limpets, clams and many types of anemones.
