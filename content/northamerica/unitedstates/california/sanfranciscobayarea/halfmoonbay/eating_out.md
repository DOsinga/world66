Eating at **Half Moon Bay restaurants **provides the visitor a variety of choices. Half Moon Bay is a city on the coast of California, approximately 25 miles south of San Francisco.

#### **Eating at Half Moon Bay**

Having a meal in Half Moon Bay can mean anything from a beach shack to a five-star restaurant. Being so close to San Francisco, one of the more popular dishes in this area is clam chowder in a sourdough bread bowl such as what is served as Sam's Chowder House. Visitors should try the local sourdough as this is a trademark of San Francisco and northern California. Being so close to San Francisco, there are a variety of ethnic restaurants in this area, including a number of Chinese, Peruvian and Italian eateries. Latin American food is popular in this part of the world. Most of the seafood comes from the bay and was most likely caught the night before or the same day as your meal. Half Moon Bay provides a beautiful setting for diners. Surrounded by the California coastline, eating here is a delightful, intimate experience, even when eating at a modest beachfront establishment like the Miramar Beach Restaurant. Tourists and locals alike enjoy dining outside, preferably on a terrace with a view. They enjoy their evenings with the perfect wine list, menus and helpful wait staffs. Restaurants in Half Moon Bay are a good way to meet both locals and out-of-towners.
