---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Half Moon Bay"
type: section
---

The **Half Moon Bay nightlife **is more varied than you'd think because of the seaside city's relatively small size. If you're looking for
entertainment after dark in Half Moon Bay, consider any of the following:

**An Evening Performance at the Coastal Repertory Theatre**

If drinking isn't how you enjoy the nightlife, you have kids along, or you like some entertainment before you settle in for dinner and a few drinks,
book tickets for an evening performance at the Coastal Repertory Theatre, 1167 Main Street. This non-profit, volunteer-run community theater is home to
at least six different plays each season, usually following a singular theme, such as examples of human perseverance and compassion. Evening
performances are at 8:00 on Fridays and Saturdays and cost $25 per adult and $20 per student or senior age 60 or over. (The price may raise to $30 per
adult, $23 per student or senior if it's opening night.)

**Enjoy Live Jazz, Food and Wine at the Cetrella**

If elegant nightclubs with live bands are your idea of a night out on the town, schedule an evening alone or with a loved one at Cetrella, 845 Main
Street. Although you can head to Cetrella for brunch and lunch, dinner is where the restaurant really shines, particularly if you come on the weekend.
Jazz and blues vocalists, pianists, and other musicians put on performances every weekend evening.

Cetrella is well known for its cheese menu, where for $8, you can have a decadent appetizer of cheese such as goat cheese, blue cheese and sheep
cheese, both domestic and imported. The dinner menu includes lamb, pork, pasta, seafood, and steak, at prices ranging from $20 to $40. You'll sometimes
find a Special Prix Fixe menu, a three-course offering for a flat rate of $25 per person. The wine menu features over 400 international selections, and
twice a month, you can sign up for a 5-course Wine Lovers Dinner, in which the chef will match the chosen wine of the day with the best tasting dishes.

**Enjoy the Pub Atmosphere at Cameron's**

Cameron's Restaurant and Inn, 1410 S. Cabrillo Hwy, is the best bet for the traveler who enjoys his or her beer in a lively English pub-like
atmosphere. Sixty-five bottled types of beer as well as twenty varieties of beer on draft are the highlight of the pub's offerings, although the
hamburgers on Cameron's menu are also fairly well received with the locals. If you don't want burgers, you can also order pizza, seafood, salad, or
steak. Expect to spend between $8 and $12 on a meal and less than $5 per mug of beer.
