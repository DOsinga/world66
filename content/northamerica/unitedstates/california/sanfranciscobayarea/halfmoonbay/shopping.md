---
title: "Shopping in Half Moon Bay"
type: section
---

You might think that **Half Moon Bay shopping **would leave a bit to be desired when you're talking about a city of no more than 12,000 residents. However, you'd be surprised how quaint and exciting shopping in this seaside city can be, especially if you include any of the following locations on your must-see list:

**Half to Have It**

Antique-lovers and collectible enthusiasts will love Half to Have It at 601 Main Street, as will travelers hoping to send home a few furnishing with which to decorate their homes or gardens. Shopping in the beautiful historic barn and building in which Half to Have It is housed is half the fun! There's over 10,000 square feet of antiques and collectibles, as well as larger indoor and outdoor furnishings. Expect to spend within a a moderate budget, as some of the rarer and larger items can get pricey. However, if you've got a keen eye, you're sure to find items at affordable prices, too.

**Colquhoun Glass Works**

Not for the tight-budgeted is Colquhoun Glass Works at 12341 San Mateo Rd, but even if you can't afford the prices, you'll marvel at what the gallery has on display. The hand-crafted glass at Colquhoun Glass Works is one-of-a-kind (and comes with a $250+ price tag to prove it), but they make stunning additions to any decor. The shop is most famous for its veggie-, fruit-, pumpkin- and orchid-shaped glass as well as its bowls and containers. If you want to save a little money and bring home your own memorable souvenir, you can sign up for a four and one-half-hour, two-person class for $160 per person.

**Half Moon Bay Wine and Cheese**

At 604 Main Street is Half Moon Bay Wine and Cheese, where you can find over 800 varieties of wine from around the world, including about 120 made right in California. Also available are over 200 kinds of cheese, including cow, goat and sheep, as well as olives and fruit to complement the wine and cheese. You can purchase small portions of the wine or cheese to sample at the full-service wine bar and you can also send home gift baskets to friends and loved ones. Prices are fairly modest but not incredibly cheap. The shop is closed on Mondays.

**Strawflower Village Shopping Center
**

If you're looking to hit a number of stores at once, head for Strawflower Village Shopping Center at the intersection of Highways 92 and 1. You'll find a book store, restaurants, a drug store, a coffee shop, and electronics store and more, including stores both locally-owned and those part of national chains.
