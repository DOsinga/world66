There are a variety of **sights** in **Half Moon Bay** for travelers who are staying a while or just passing through.

#### Downtown: Shopping and Strolling

The wide streets of historic downtown are one of the first stops on any traveler&rsquo;s itinerary to Half Moon Bay. It is here that tourists and locals alike revel in the creations of some of California&rsquo;s most talented artists and chefs while strolling quaint avenues by the seaside. Lined with a multitude of antique shops, specialty stores, cafes, restaurants, and art galleries, the small but lively downtown is a welcoming introduction to all that Half Moon Bay has to offer.

#### Beaches: Ocean Fun, Tidepools, and Wave Watching

The great Pacific is what draws most visitors to this coastal haven; the west side of town is entirely lined in public beaches that stretch up and down the neighboring coastline for many miles. Half Moon Bay State Beach offers a variety of locations for fun in the sand along with picnic areas, walking trails, and campgrounds. Some beaches contain tidepools which are an interesting way to get up close and personal with the marine life. Maverick&rsquo;s, a famous elite surfer beach, is a great place to experience the mighty crash of Mother Ocean as incredibly large waves pound the cliffs during winter storms.

#### Restaurants and Resorts: Famous Places and Cheap Eats

In addition to water fun, some unique and exceptional establishments call Half Moon Bay home. Just a few miles north of town, the Moss Beach Distillery was built in 1927 and is now most commonly known for its supernatural visitor, &ldquo;The Blue Lady.&rdquo;. Featured on NBC&rsquo;s &ldquo;Unsolved Mysteries,&rdquo; the Distillery has been the site of dozens of unexplainable occurrences which keep visitors from around the world stopping by with hopes of catching a ghostly glimpse. The nearby Ritz Carlton offers 5-star accommodation, a 16,000-square-foot spa, along with a salon, fitness center, tennis courts, pool, and California coastal cuisine at the in-house Navio Restaurant. Adjacent to the Ritz is the Half Moon Bay Golf Links, a world-class championship golf course with two full par-72 courses overlooking expansive coastal vistas.

For some quirky refreshments on a tamer budget, the Half Moon Bay Brewing Company--the only brewpub on the San Mateo Coast--is a great place to enjoy a cold one after a long day in the sun. Cameron&rsquo;s Restaurant & Inn is another sweet spot in town where families can enjoy the odd luxury of a double-decker video game bus. Also home to the world&rsquo;s only double-decker smoking bus, Cam&rsquo;s has a pub, restaurant, bed and breakfast accommodation, games room, and banquet facilities.
