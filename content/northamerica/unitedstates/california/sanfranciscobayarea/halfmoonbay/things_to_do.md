There are many **Half Moon Bay things to do**, especially if you're looking for an outdoor coastal adventure destination.
Beaches and Reserves

In and around Half Moon Bay (HMB) there is a huge variety of state
beaches that provide wonderful areas for walking, hiking, picnicking,
camping, sunbathing, and swimming. Half Moon Bay State Beach is a
4-mile-long stretch of protected coast adjacent to HMB itself and
includes Francis Beach, Venice Beach, Dunes Beach and Roosevelt Beach.
Francis Beach is the most popular and offers picnic tables, restrooms,
and campgrounds/RV sites for longer stays. Just a few miles north of
town is Maverick’s beach where the world’s top surfers gather every
winter to ride enormous waves. Even if the competition isn’t on,
Maverick’s is still a great place to go wave-watching; bring binoculars
and a warm jacket for best viewing in the winter. Many beaches charge
for parking, but the cost is typically less than $10 for the day.
Walking and Hiking

The Waterfront Coastside Trail is a great place for an easy, scenic
walk or jog that borders three miles along the entire State Beach. Easy
and short hikes can be found at nearby Cowell Ranch Beach and Burleigh
Murray Ranch State Park, while Purisima Creek Preserve can be found just
a couple miles further inland and is great for bikers and hikers and
boasts a 1,700-foot elevation change for some more challenging trails.
Montara Mountain to the north offers rugged climbs through McNee Ranch
State Park and rewards the heartiest of hikers with spectacular views
atop its 1,900-foot-tall peak.
Horseback Riding

No reservations are required, so stop by Sea Horse Ranch anytime
after 8 a.m. for a guided 1-, 1 ½-, or 2-hour ride on the extensive
local trails and beaches. The early bird special is the best deal—a
two-hour guided trail and beach ride for $45 per person! Or, take the
kids to a pony ride at Friendly Acres Ranch, only one block away.
Golf, Fishing and Kayaking

Half Moon Bay Golf Links is a world famous 36-hole course overlooking
the ocean beside the Ritz Carlton Hotel. Be prepared to pay top dollar
while you enjoy the superb coastal views on one of two full courses.
Fishing trips are available out on the great Pacific via operators like
Huck Finn, Huli Cat, and Riptide, where you can charter a boat or join a
sport fishing expedition. Kayak rentals and group tours are available
from California Canoe & Kayak and Half Moon Bay Kayak Company and
are a serene and independent way to enjoy the aquatic wonders of the
Bay.
