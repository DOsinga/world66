---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Half Moon Bay"
type: section
---

The top 5** Half Moon Bay must do's** include enjoying the sea, the beach, the food, and the many festivals that occur throughout the year.

#### 1. The Beach

Half Moon Bay is bordered to the west by Half Moon Bay State Beach which includes Francis, Venice, Dunes, and Roosevelt Beaches. Among these four beaches, and the multitude of other beaches stretching up and down the adjacent coastline, visitors of any age can sunbathe, swim, camp, picnic, and surf. The Waterfront Coastline Trail is a leisurely three mile stroll along these Half Moon Bay State beaches and is great for jogging, walking, bike riding, and beautiful views.

#### 2. Tidepools

The tidepools are a truly unique way to come face to face with the marine life in Half Moon Bay. At Montara State Beach a few miles north of town and Pescadero Beach to the south, tidepools draw visitors of all ages to spend a day hiking on the rocky seashore ogling the sea urchins and anemones at low tide.

#### 3. Horseback Riding

While not suited to young children, a horseback ride along the beach is surely a unique experience that cannot be missed for all equestrian-inclined Half Moon Bay visitors. Visit Sea Horse Ranch for trail and beach rides in 1-, 1 &frac12;- and 2-hour durations, starting at $55 per person. Or show up any day at 8 a.m. sharp for the best deal around—a 2-hour early bird ride for only $45!

#### 4. The Moss Beach Distillery

The Moss Beach Distillery—originally called Frank&rsquo;s Place when it was built in 1927—was a successful harbor for the &ldquo;rum runners&rdquo; of the Prohibition days. With the thirsty mouths of the area&rsquo;s top politicians and actors to feed, Frank&rsquo;s was a thriving center of booze bootlegging until the repeal of Prohibition in 1933. Since then it has become famous for another reason: the unexplained occurrences and repeated apparitions of &ldquo;The Blue Lady&rdquo; still dazzle visitors today.

#### 5. Festivals

The annual Art and Pumpkin Festival in late October is a draw for thousands of visitors from near and far. Fun for the whole family, this free event hosts a &ldquo;Picasso of pumpkin carvers&rdquo; contest, along with a pumpkin weigh-off competition, a parade, three stages of entertainment, craft booths, and food—including, of course, the best pumpkin pie around. Other Half Moon Bay festivals include the Rock the Block Coastside Block Party in late May, the mid-summer Classics by the Bay Car Show, and the monthly Coastal Flower Market.
