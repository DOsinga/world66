This informative **Half Moon Bay travel guide** discusses many general activities, restaurants and types of accommodation within this small coastal town. A popular destination for residents of the greater Bay Area and any coastal travelers, Half Moon Bay has a laid back, friendly atmosphere that invites people of all ages and budgets. The entire town, officially encompassing a mere 6.5 square miles, is sought out by groups ranging from schoolchildren to families to couples of all ages.

#### Activities

The main Half Moon Bay activities include visits to the many nearby beaches to sunbathe, swim, or camp; shopping in the unique shops, galleries and bookstores of historic downtown; and enjoying the culinary wonders of California coastal cuisine at the many restaurants and cafes. While not generally known as an adventure destination, easy to rigorous hikes, ocean-side horseback rides, and kayaking or fishing excursions can satisfy travelers or families who want a more challenging outdoor experience. For families traveling with children, Lemos Farm has a pumpkin patch, train rides and laser tag year around, while Friendly Acres Ranch offers pony rides to children age 5 and under.

#### Restaurants

Because of the popularity of the town amongst tourists, there are a variety of Half Moon Bay restaurants to fit any budget. Modestly priced cuisine can be found around downtown, but the best way to experience the area is to splash out for some pricier fresh seafood at one of the many ocean-side restaurants. The most popular places include the Moss Beach Distillery—famous for its long history and continual ghost sightings—along with the Navio Restaurant inside the 5-star Ritz Carlton, Mullins Bar and Grill overlooking the world renowned Half Moon Bay Golf Links, the Crab Landing Seafood & Steak House and the Half Moon Bay Brewing Company.

#### Accommodations

The range of accommodations in Half Moon Bay is also wide and caters to meet all budgets. Camping at Francis Beach is the most economical option with sites from $35 per night, while hotel chains such as Holiday Inn Express, Comfort Inn and Harbor View Inn have rooms for two starting at about $100-$150 per night. The Best Western provides fine views of the Golf Course, starting at just under $200 per night, while the Oceano and Ritz Carlton are the top dogs in the area boasting elegant and enormous facilities, gorgeous ocean views and top-notch service, with rooms starting around $300 and $450 per night, respectively. A handful of bed and breakfasts operate in the area as well, where prices can range greatly.
