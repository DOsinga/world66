**Half Moon Bay: When to Go Tips**

There is not much to think about when pondering **Half Moon Bay: when to go **questions. The destination has nearly the perfect climate for a large part of the year.

#### **Average Climate**

There is no off-season in terms of visitors arriving in Half Moon Bay. It is among the most preferred beach destinations in the San Francisco Bay area, and it is devoid of any temperature extremes. Half Moon Bay has temperate climate throughout the year. This essentially means a comforting ocean breeze is present for most of the year. The days are slightly warm, and the evenings are much cooler. Some months may have a thick fog during the night. The average annual temperature is within the mid 60-degree Fahrenheit range. Even the hottest days seldom cross the 75-degree Fahrenheit mark. The rainfall is moderate, averaging about 24 inches per year. Rainfall is limited and usually affects just about 70 days a year in the form of sporadic showers which rarely interfere with sightseeing plans.

#### **When to Go Advice**

Temperatures increase from the last part of May through July and usually peak during the early weeks of September. Daylight hours peak in June. The latter part of December and most of January comprises the coolest period with low humidity. The skies are also the clearest during this time of the year. Visitors looking for some tranquility and those who don&rsquo;t mind slightly chilly evenings, should visit Half Moon Bay during the December-to-January stretch.
