Monterey sits on a crescent bay about two hours south of San Francisco, where the cold, nutrient-rich waters of the Pacific have built one of the richest marine ecosystems on the California coast. It is a small city with a genuinely dramatic setting — sea otters float in the kelp beds just offshore, harbor seals bark from the rocks, and on clear mornings the Santa Lucia mountains frame the whole bay.

The Monterey Bay Aquarium is the city's undisputed centerpiece and worth every superlative it gets. Plan at least three hours; the open-sea tank and the living kelp forest exhibit alone justify the trip. The aquarium café serves fresh, reasonably priced food and the views over the bay make it a fine lunch spot.

Cannery Row — the old sardine-packing district made famous by Steinbeck — is now tourist-oriented shops and restaurants, some better than others. It is worth a walk for the architecture and the water views, but do not expect the working-class grit of Steinbeck's era. The best way to experience the bay itself is by kayak; guided tours take you through the kelp forests where sea otters wrap themselves in kelp to sleep.

When you leave, head south on Highway 1 toward [Big Sur](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/bigsurandcarmelvalley). The 20 miles of coast road south of Monterey are some of the most photographed in California for good reason. Fill the tank before you go — fuel in Big Sur runs significantly higher than in Monterey.
