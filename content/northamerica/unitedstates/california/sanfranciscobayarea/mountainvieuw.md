Mountain View is a mid-sized Silicon Valley city directly south of San Francisco, home to Google's main campus (Googleplex) and a long history of technology companies including NASA Ames Research Center at Moffett Field. Despite its corporate fame, the centre is unexpectedly human-scale: Castro Street downtown is a lively strip of cafes, restaurants (heavy on Asian options), and bookshops.

The **Computer History Museum** near Shoreline Amphitheatre is one of the best technology museums in the world, with exhibits tracing computing from mechanical tabulation machines through to modern supercomputers. The nearby **Shoreline Amphitheatre** hosts major outdoor concerts in summer, and the adjacent **Shoreline at Mountain View** park on the San Francisco Bay has cycling trails, a lake, and kayaking. The bay salt marshes support significant birdlife.

Mountain View blurs into Palo Alto to the south and Sunnyvale to the east, so a visit typically covers several of these neighbouring cities. The CalTrain station provides direct trains to San Francisco (about 45 minutes).
