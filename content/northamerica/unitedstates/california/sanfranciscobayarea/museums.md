****

The top pick of **San Francisco museums** is the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco. It consists of the de Young Memorial Museum and the Legion of Honor:

**de Young Memorial Museum**

The de Young Memorial Museum is located at 50 Hagiwara Tea Garden Drive within the Golden Gate Park. It holds an extensive collection of American painting and decorative art, ancient Americas, African and Oceanic art, and textile arts. Of all the collections, the ancient Americas collection is not to be missed. It provides an interesting and educational perspective on cultures and civilizations before the Spanish conquest.

**Legion of Honor**

The Legion of Honor is located within the impressive Beaux Art building located within Lincoln Park. It showcases over 4,000 years of history through European paintings and decorative art, ancient art, and a works on paper collection. A visit to the Legion would be incomplete without a viewing of the amazing Rodin sculpture collection. The Thinker - a famous Bronze sculpture by Rodin - is located in the Court of Honor.

Opening hours for both the two museums of the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco are Tuesday to Sunday, from 9:30am to 5:15pm. The cost of general admission is $11 for each museum. Good discounts are available for college students, youth and seniors. Both museums are easily accessible by either public transport or car. For public transport to the de Young Memorial Museum, take the N-Judah Muni Metro streetcar from the city. The Mini bus lines 18-46th Avenue, 38-Geary and 1-California will take you to the Legion of Honor.
