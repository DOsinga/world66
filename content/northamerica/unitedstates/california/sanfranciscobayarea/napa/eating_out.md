---
title: "Eating Out in Napa"
type: section
---

Napa
doesn't just take its wine seriously. Voluptuous viniculture and
gourmet gluttony do a decadent dance in Napa Valley, and the result is
spangled with Michelin stars and nods from critics around the world.
The same temperate climate that creates ideal grape-growing conditions
also makes the soil prime for produce, and you can feast on these
fruits at Napa County's many fine restaurants. Napa has been central to
the development of California Cuisine, which focuses on seasonal local
produce. From resort dining rooms to comfortable country gardens, Napa
Valley restaurants showcase the region's epicurean expertise. Expect
alfresco dining aplenty, a vacation-casual vibe, and, of course, some
exceptionally paired wines.

**Yountville**

The
town of Yountville fulfills every image of Napa you have, with its
picturesque rolling vineyards and quaint country feel. It's also home
to some of California's most highly regarded restaurants. The crème de
la crème of these is French Laundry. The only Michelin-three-starred
restaurant in the Bay Area, French Laundry serves epic multi-course
prix-fixe meals in an idyllic setting. For a reservation, call three
months to the day in advance. If you didn't have the foresight, don't
despair; French Laundry's chef Thomas Keller has a second Yountville
restaurant, family-style Ad Hoc. Bistro Jeanty, Bouchon, Bottega Napa
Valley, and Redd are among Yountville's other exquisite options.

**St. Helena**

Up
Highway 29, St. Helena is a similarly unsuspecting hotbed of epicurean
heaven. The vintage-feeling town is home to the Culinary Institute of
America at Greystone, as well as some heavy-hitters in the food world.
East meets West at Terra, California cuisine shines at Meadowood
Restaurant, and romantic Maritini House serves decadent desserts.

**Rutherford**

You
might miss Rutherford along the highway between Yountville and St.
Helena, and that would be a real shame. The teeny-tiny town boasts many
excellent wineries, including Francis Ford Coppola's Niebaum-Coppola
Estate, and one first-rate resort, Auberge du Soleil. Popular with
celebrities, the resort houses a French restaurant by the same name,
which boasts some of the most romantic views in Napa County.

**Napa**

Finally,
the city of Napa boasts many great restaurants as well. The most urban
and least viniculture-centric wine country city, Napa has developed its
own culinary identity in recent years, with restaurants that are more
internationally influenced and varied than others in the county. Bistro
Don Giovanni serves authentic Italian on a terrace, Barbers Q some
down-home delights, while half-restaurant-half-yoga-studio Ubuntu
features a creative vegetarian menu.
