---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Drinking and dining in the Napa Valley is a treat at any
time but there are still factors to consider when planning a trip.

**Heat**

In the summer months, the weather in the Napa Valley is very
warm. It is not uncommon for it to
be over 100 degrees Fahrenheit (37.7 degrees Celsius) on summer days. July is most commonly the hottest
month of the year followed by August, September. June and October (in that order of average high
temperatures). At all times of the
year, the heat dissipates in the evenings. You will want to have a light sweater or wrap with you even
if it was sweltering during the day.

**Rain**

December and January are the rainiest months of the
year. Early February can also be
rainy. It rains very rarely
between mid-May and early November.
During the time of year when there is no rain, the brush on the
hillsides is brown. If you are
concerned with encountering green grass on the hillsides, consider traveling in
late February or March.

**Seasonal and Regular Closures**

Many restaurants and wineries are closed the last week of
December and the first two weeks of Januaries. If a winery or restaurant closes one day each week, that day
is most likely going to be Tuesday.

**Best Days to Visit Wineries**

The weekends are the most popular time to visit Napa
wineries and expectedly this is when the tasting rooms are busiest. Visiting with the crowd does have a
possible benefit; some wineries will open additional bottles for tasting or do
barrel tastings on the weekends.
Thursdays and Fridays are the best days for tasting as the wineries are
ramping up for the busy weekend but the crowds have not yet arrived.

**Harvest**

The grapes in Napa are harvested in parts of September and
October. If you want to do a tour
with a winemaker, do not come during the harvest. Be aware as well that many hotels in the region raise prices
at this time of year.

**Transportation Considerations**

The roads into Napa Valley from San Francisco are small and
crowd easily. If you can avoid it,
do not drive from San Francisco to Napa on Fridays between about 2:00 p.m. and
7:00 p.m. If you must drive during
these times, plan at least an extra 40 to 80 minutes to reach your
destination. Similarly, returning
to San Francisco on Sunday afternoon can be very unpleasant traffic-wise. Holiday or event weekends also clog the
roads and can make driving difficult.
