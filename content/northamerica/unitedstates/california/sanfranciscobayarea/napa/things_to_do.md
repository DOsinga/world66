---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

It's not all about the wine in Napa. Wine is a way of
life in the famed county, and the fruits of the temperate climate are
yours to sip and savor. Indulge your epicurean side at wineries large
and small, tour picturesque vineyards, feast on the fine fare of
first-rate restaurants, get pampered in premium spas, golf the green
courses, and hike the winding trails.

**Wineries**

You're first priority in Napa is most likely visiting wineries, which there are
as many ways of going about as there are varietals. You
can hop on a wine train (www.winetrain.com) or book a luxury limousine
tour (www.beauwinetours.com), but the more adventurous will want to get
out on their own. Soak in the scenery and burn off those tasting
calories with a bike tour (www.napavalleybiketours.com), or rent your
own set of wheels and get off the beaten path. A heart-pounding ride up
Atlas Peak will reward with expansive views, and savory Zinfandels. Or
you can truly escape the crowds with a romantic balloon ride
(www.napavalleyballoons.com).

**Sites**

Outdoor lovers will relish in Napa's Northern California offerings.
Westwood Hills Park boasts valley views from its many trails, while the
Napa River aches to be kayaked along. You can horseback ride through
state parks (www.triplecreekhorseoutfit.com), or get sprayed by the
mist of Old Faithful (www.oldfaithfulgeyser.com). Golfers can practice
their swing at Napa Golf Course at Kennedy Park (www.playnapa.com),
while those in need of a soak will head to the hot springs of
Calistoga. Historic downtowns, like in the city of Napa, teem with
antique shops and boutiques, while quaint small towns like Calistoga
and St. Helena beg to be explored.

**Food**

Foodies won't want to miss feasting on the fruits of Napa's
Mediterranean climate. Start at the St. Helena's Farmers Market, and
grab sumptuous supplies for a winery picnic. Work your way up the
culinary ladder, advancing from Barbers Q to Meadwood Restaurant to
Auberge du Soleil, and at last French Laundry, one of the world's
premiere dining experiences.

Exploring Napa comes with a price tag, and it can range from
moderate to exorbitant. Bike rentals start at $40 for a day, while
luxury limousine tours start at $400 for a couple hours. Romping around
state parks remains a blissfully low-cost option, but remember that
Napa is about epicurean indulgence--that is to say, let yourself live a
little.
