One of the world's great university campuses, Stanford is worth a visit in its own right. The main Quad, designed by Frederick Law Olmsted in the late 19th century, is a sweep of sandstone arcades and terracotta roofs around a central courtyard dominated by Memorial Church, its facade covered in mosaic. It's free to walk around, and the scale and beauty of the place are genuinely impressive. Pick up a campus map at the visitor center and allow at least two hours.

The Cantor Arts Center, on the eastern edge of campus, houses a substantial permanent collection including works by Warhol, Diebenkorn, and Rodin. The outdoor Rodin Sculpture Garden — free and always open — holds 20 bronze casts including The Gates of Hell and The Thinker.

story: >
  Stanford was founded in 1885 by railroad baron Leland Stanford and his wife Jane as a memorial to their son, who died of typhoid at 15. The campus was built on their horse-breeding farm, which is why the athletic teams are still called the Cardinal and the original working name was "The Farm." The founders' mausoleum, a small domed building near the main entrance, is still there.
