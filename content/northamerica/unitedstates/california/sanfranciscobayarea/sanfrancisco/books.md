---
title: 'Books'
type: section
---

San Francisco has inspired some of the finest American literature. Armistead Maupin's Tales of the City captures the bohemian spirit of the 1970s city. Jack Kerouac's On the Road and Allen Ginsberg's Howl emerged from the Beat scene centered on North Beach's City Lights Books. For noir, Dashiell Hammett's The Maltese Falcon is set on these streets.

For a more recent take, try Dave Eggers' A Heartbreaking Work of Staggering Genius or Rebecca Solnit's Infinite City, a creative atlas of San Francisco's many layers.
