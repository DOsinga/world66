title: "Budget Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

San Francisco is one of the world's most popular tourist destination cities. As such, it can be quite costly to spend a week (or even a weekend) there. With a few insider tips, you can enjoy the best the city has to offer without paying an arm and leg. The guide below can help you plan your budget-friendly San Francisco getaway.

**Where to Stay**

Perhaps one of the most expensive parts of taking a trip is the lodging. With hotel rooms averaging anywhere from $125 to over $500, this can take a big bite out of your trip budget. Fortunately, San Francisco has some lodging options that can help you save some cash. If you are a low-maintenance traveler, consider staying in one of the city's many youth hostels. You may have to share a room and help keep the hostel clean, but your stay could cost as little as $25 per night. Check out the following options:

Green Tortoise Hostel, 494 Broadway, San Francisco, (415) 834-1000

Adelaide Hostel, 5 Isadora Duncan Ln, San Francisco, (415) 359-1915‎

American Youth Hostels, 425 Divisadero St # 307, San Francisco, (415) 701-1320‎

Another way to save some cash on lodging is to consider subletting an apartment from a local for a week or weekend.

**Where to Eat**

San Francisco is home to many eateries and options for inexpensive fare are plentiful. Make sure to get a famous clam chowder sourdough bowl at Fisherman's Wharf. You can have lunch for under $5 at one of the many Mission district taquerias, famous for their Mexican authenticity. For dinner, head to Chinatown for some dim sum or head to We Be Sushi for a frugal feast.

Boudin Sourdough Bakery & Cafe, 60 Jefferson St., San Francisco, (415) 928-1849

Taqueria Cancun, 2288 Mission St., San Francisco, (415) 252-9560‎

Hang Ah Tea Room, 1 Pagoda Pl, San Francisco, (415) 982-5686‎

We Be Sushi, 538 Valencia St, San Francisco, (415) 565-0749‎

Delores Park & Mission

Twin Peaks
Golden Gate Bridge (you can cross by bike or foot)
Fisherman's Wharf
Pier 39
