Harris' is an "old-boys club" style steakhouse. Lots of dark paneling, generally low-volume conversation, and a reasonably extensive wine and drinks list. The attitude seems markedly less stuffy than Morton's, and the meat was better as well. Steaks-wise, the King Kobe cut steak is allegedly excellent, but don't be fooled into thinking you're buying the Real Japanese Deal. Their Kobe is raised in Oregon. The prime rib is tender and juicy, and the filet mignon is second to absolutely none. Do yourself a favor and pop for the $3 for a dish of brandy sauce to go with your beef. You won't regret it.
