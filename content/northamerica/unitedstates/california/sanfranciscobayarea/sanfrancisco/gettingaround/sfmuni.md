title: "SF MUNI"

---

The San Francisco Municipal Railway, SFMUNI, provides an excellent and cheap ($1.25 US; exact fare required because change not given) way to get around the City of San Francisco. Buses and light-rail trolley lines (and cable cars, which cost more) are generally safe and clean and it's possible to get almost anywhere in SF without walking more than a couple of blocks. If you do take MUNI, don't forget to get a transfer either from the turnstyle as you enter the subway/light rail station or from the bus driver. These are supposed to be good for 2 more rides within 1 1/2 hours, but, in fact, you can often use them for more rides and/or a longer period of time (the time limit is indicated and often it's longer than the 1 1/2 hours it's supposed to be). Also, there are discounted tokens available in several locations such as the MUNI kiosk at the cable car turnaround at Powell and Market or at City Hall. These will save you approximately 20% off the individual fare but you have to buy them in packs of ten. There are also passes available at the same locations good for unlimited MUNI travel for 1, 3 and 7-day periods. If you plan on depending on MUNI for getting around the city, and especially if you plan on using the cable cars, these will almost certainly save you money.
