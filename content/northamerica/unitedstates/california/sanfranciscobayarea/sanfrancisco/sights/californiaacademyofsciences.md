Unfortunately, this museum is undergoing a major refurbishment. Actually that is an understatement - they're building a whole new complex to house the displays! The new area is only 100 yards away, so shouldn't be too difficult to find. If you ask for the Aquarium in the Park, most taxi drivers will know where you mean! It is situated slap bang in the middle of the Golden Gate Park - just across from the Japanese Gardens.

The full size T-Rex skeleton in the foyer was amazing as were most of the other exhibits. Particularly enjoyable was the Steinhart Aquarium which will apparently be included in the new venue when it is completed. 

The atmosphere was equally good. While tourists wandered aimlessly, schoolchildren ran around excitedly and more adult 'schoolkids' made use of the reference library. 

Finally, the in house cafe was pretty good. The decor and seating was sparse, but the food was above average.

If the new venue is half as good as the old one, then it should be a "must visit" for anyone going to San Francisco.
