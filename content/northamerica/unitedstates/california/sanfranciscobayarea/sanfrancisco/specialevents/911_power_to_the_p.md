Since 1999 Michael Franti has been organizing the free Power to the
Peaceful Festival in Golden Gate Park on or around September 11.
What started as an internation day of art and culture to raise
awareness for Mumia Abu-Jamal's case quickly grew to represent
the rights of all death row inmates and social justice in
general. More
recently, Franti has stated that "in 2001 the day of
September 11th took on a wider significance as a result of the tragic
loss of life by the attacks of the day. As the event grew, it
took on the role as a day of healing for all those affected by acts of
terrorism or the war on terrorism.

The 2005 event has added a can drive for the victims of hurricane
Katrina and has coordinated with the Amnesty Internation Film
Festival. This free event costs nothing to attend; all the
organizers ask is that attendees pick up and properly dispose of any
trash they see on the streets of the city. In addition to a very
visible earth-concious recycling program, the event produces all power
with bio-fuel generators, provides a bicycle corral, and encourages
ride sharing for those driving to the city.

The musical performers, speakers, DJs, exhibition skaters, art
installations, and films are varied and will likely appeal to many
different tastes and political viewpoints. Mostly, the event is
about healing, understanding, and welcoming everyone.
