---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in San Francisco"
type: section
---

**San Francisco** California is undeniably rich in history and possibly one of the most fascinating cities in the United States. There are **5 Must-Do activities** when visiting this beautiful location:

**1. Golden Gate Bridge and Park**

Drive, walk or bike across this picturesque rust-red suspension bridge called The Golden Gate. It is an amazing scenic venture you won’t want to pass up. Also, visit the park of the same name and view its majestic landscape. It’s said to be larger than Central Park and is filled with mammoth California redwoods.

**2. Hop a Ferry Boat to the Rock

**

The infamous Alcatraz Island Prison, once home to some of the worst criminal offenders in our country, is said to be haunted to this very day by unrest spirits of former inmates. Take one of the guided tours, but book in advance for even though there are boats departing every 30 minutes from 9 a.m. to dusk, they book very fast.

**3. Fisherman’s Wharf / Pier 39**

This is probably one of the most famous locations in the city. Filled with not only tourists, there are local sea lions who live here as well. You’ll find an endless variety of things to do here from shopping and visiting maritime and wax museums to eating at many seafood restaurants. The pier/wharf is still fully functioning, so you’ll also see fisherman at work bringing in loads of fresh fish.

**4. Ride the Cable Cars**

Catch a ride on one of the city’s famed cable cars. There are several locations where you can be picked up and/or dropped off. Whether it is at Powell/Market Streets downtown, Fisherman’s Wharf along the water’s edge, or California Street, hop on board for the riding experience of a lifetime and see the city how it was meant to be seen – up close and personal.

**5. The Crookedest Street

**

Go to a unique spot in the city called Russian Hill, right in the middle of Hyde and Leavenworth Streets. Here you’ll find Lombard Street – called the crookedest street in the world. It was created with such jaunting precision due to the hill it rests on being a 40-degree grade. Developers thought the crookedness would make it easier for people to make their way down the steep graded hill. It’s definitely a sight to see and an experience to drive down this quirky street.
