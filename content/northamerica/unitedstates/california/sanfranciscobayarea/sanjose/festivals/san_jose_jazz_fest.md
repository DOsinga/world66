Every August, in the middle of the dog days of summer, the San Jose
Jazz Festival takes over downtown San Jose. If there's a stage open, a
rhythm outfit will be working it hard. The annual festivities, which
are free except for an opening fundraiser, draw tens of thousands of
fans to the area. The festival's focus is on Cesar Chavez Park and the
main stage, where big-name acts (like Pete Escovedo) dominate on
Saturday and Sunday. But the event, sponsored by the San Jose Jazz
Society, doesn't stop there. Bands of all stripes and flavors will play
on a total of seven stages during the extended weekend.
