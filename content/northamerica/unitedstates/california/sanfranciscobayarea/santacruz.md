Santa Cruz is a beach town with a countercultural streak, perched at the northern tip of Monterey Bay where the redwood-covered Santa Cruz Mountains meet the Pacific. It is 75 miles south of [San Francisco](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/sanfranciscobayarea/sanfrancisco) and has the vibe of a university town that never quite grew up — surfers, students, artists, and tech workers share the streets.

The Beach Boardwalk is the main landmark, a classic oceanfront amusement park with the 1924 Big Dipper wooden roller coaster still running. Steamer Lane and Pleasure Point are two of the best-known surf breaks in California, and you can watch the action from the cliffs above. The UC Santa Cruz campus, set in the forested hills above town, is one of the most beautiful in the country.

Pacific Avenue, the main drag downtown, is lined with independent shops, restaurants, and cafes. The surrounding redwood forests — Henry Cowell and Big Basin state parks — offer hiking among ancient trees just minutes from town. South along the coast, the village of Capitola and the beaches of Aptos are worth exploring.
