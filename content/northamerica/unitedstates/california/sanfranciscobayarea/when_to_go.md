title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**Summer**

Under no circumstances should travelers fail to bring warm clothes (and layers) for a summer visit to the city. Although the average summer temperature is around 65 degrees Fahrenheit (18 degrees Celsius) wind, fog and dampness make it seem much colder. Despite the questionable weather, summer still brings throngs of tourists to San Francisco. If there is a season where you are actually going to get the picture of you or a loved one hanging off the back end of a cable car, summer probably isn’t going to be it because the cable cars are just too crowded (the drivers actively discourage/forbid this anyway). Lodging prices are higher during summer months and limited space attractions such as tours of Alcatraz require reservations in advance of your trip.

**Special Events**

Two major events in the San Francisco calendar attract large crowds and may make finding lodging difficult (or expensive): the first is the Bay to Breakers footrace held the third Sunday in May and the second is the events surrounding Gay Pride Week at the end of June. Both events result in an extremely festive atmosphere in the city. See the official event websites at www.baytobreakers.com or www.sfpride.com for dates or other information.

**Spring and Autumn**

Spring and autumn are lovely times to visit San Francisco. In the spring, the average temperature is in the high 50s Fahrenheit (13-15 degrees Celsius) and in the autumn, the average temperature is in the low 60s (15-18 degrees Celsius). There are tourists in San Francisco year-round, but there are far fewer in the spring and fall than there are during the summer.

**Winter**

In the winter months the average temperature is in the high 40s Fahrenheit (c. 9 degrees Celsius). The rainiest months in the city are January and February. San Francisco’s extensive shopping scene makes it a popular choice for trips around the holidays. The city’s opera and ballet seasons are in full-swing during the winter months as well and lodging prices are often lower than they are at other times of the year. Chinese New Year celebrations in February draw many visitor to San Francisco’s famous Chinatown.
