Santa Barbara has a serious restaurant scene for a city its size, benefiting from local produce, nearby wine country, and a long-established tradition of California cuisine.

**Fancy Dishes**

The talk of the town and featured on magazines for a very long time, Bouchon on Victoria Street is a must-try. Having been named as one of the best restaurants in California by Frommer&rsquo;s in 2005, Bouchon has seasonal menu changes, known to intrigue and never fail its customers with impeccable food choices and constantly commended service. Other recommendations include Nu&rsquo;s in down town Santa Barbara, which serves classic hearty American dishes.

The Wine Cask with its 20-year old wine shop, large stone fireplace, outdoor dining patio, and mouth-watering Italian fare, guarantees a breath-taking evening in more ways than one. Ca Dario is another must-try for their sumptuous Italian entrees.

**Humble Delicacies**

A great lunch venue is the Arts & Letters caf&eacute;, which serves artfully prepared and delectable soups, salads and sandwiches all for your enjoyment in their Spanish-designed courtyard. If your palate is sizzling for Cajun-inspired dishes, the Palace Grill is another Frommer&rsquo;s highly recommended restaurant. On the other hand, the Opal restaurant never ceases to surprise and please with their creative and internationally inspired dishes. Moncecito Caf&eacute; is another must visit with its Californian dishes that perfectly harmonize your meal.

To satisfy immediate cravings, do indulge in any of the Mexican dishes at the highly affordable La Super-Rica Taqueria or have your staple sandwich, very-well done, at Stacky&rsquo;s Seaside restaurant.
