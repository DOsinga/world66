---
title: "Things to Do in Santa Barbara"
type: section
---

In **Santa Barbara**, **things to do **outdoors are plentiful because of the beautiful landscape and the attractive weather. No outdoor enthusiast or traveler looking to get some fresh air will get bored on a trip to Santa Barbara that includes any of the following:

**Santa Barbara Botanic Gardens**

Not all outdoor activities have to involve breaking up a big sweat. If going for a leisurely stroll is your style, head to the Santa Barbara Botanic Gardens. Solo travelers, couples and families alike enjoy spending time at the Botanic Gardens, where the gorgeous array of flora isn't confined to a human-made garden but is spread throughout 65 acres of natural forest land. Walking along the trail is more like a nature walk than a tour of a sightseeing location, but you're guaranteed to see something breathtaking at every point on the 5 1/2-mile trail. You can walk at your own pace or take a tour. Tickets are $8 for adults; $6 for teens (13 to 17), seniors and active military; and $4 for kids (2 to 12).

**East Beach, Carpinteria State Park**

Santa Barbara has quite a few beaches, so choosing just one can be a difficult decision. Nevertheless, if you head to East Beach in Carpinteria State Park, you'll find a beach with a crowd, but one that's not overly crowded. Beach play, swimming, biking and even fishing are frequently pursued activities on East Beach. It's also close to a number of great shopping locations downtown if you need a break from the sand and the sun; plus, it's easily accessible from other parts of the city via train.

**Front Country Hiking**

Santa Barbara has a lot of prime hiking locations as well, but hikers will regret it if they skip out on Front Country, the most popular hiking location in the area. Located on the south slop of the Santa Ynez Mountains, Front Country has over a dozen prime hiking trails. Perhaps most-traveled is the Tunnel Trail, which involves steep mountain climbing for 3 1/2 miles (the entire trail is between nine and 11 miles) until you reach La Cumbre Peak (Santa Barbara's highest peak) at almost 4000 feet off of the ground. This is a hike for people who are in fairly good shape and are used to walking inclines.

**Fly Above All Paragliding School**

Santa Barbara is one of the best locations for paragliding in the country because of its vast mountain range, which makes an incredible view from overhead. For $200 to $1500, you can sign up for a paragliding course at Fly Above All Paragliding School as short as four to six hours or as in-depth as 12 days. You can rent or purchase equipment, learn the art firsthand from an instructor and become certified to make the jump yourself once you've learned what to do.
