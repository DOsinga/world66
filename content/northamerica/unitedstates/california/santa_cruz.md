Santa Cruz is a laid-back beach city on the northern curve of Monterey Bay, about 75 miles south of San Francisco. The boardwalk — one of the oldest amusement parks on the West Coast — runs along the main beach, with a vintage wooden roller coaster and a 1911 carousel that are both National Historic Landmarks.

The town has a strong counterculture streak, thanks in part to the University of California campus in the redwood-covered hills above. Pacific Avenue downtown is the main drag, with independent bookshops, cafes, surf shops and restaurants. The surf breaks at Steamer Lane, right below the lighthouse, draw serious surfers year-round and are good to watch even if you do not ride.

The surrounding area is spectacular. Henry Cowell Redwoods State Park is a short drive into the mountains and has old-growth groves you can walk through in an afternoon. Ano Nuevo State Park to the north is home to a massive elephant seal colony. The Mystery Spot, a kitschy roadside attraction in the woods, has been confusing tourists since 1939. Roaring Camp Railroads runs a narrow-gauge steam train through the redwoods.
