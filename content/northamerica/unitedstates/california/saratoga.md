Saratoga is a quiet, leafy town at the base of the Santa Cruz Mountains in Silicon Valley. Despite sitting in the heart of the Bay Area’s tech corridor, it feels a world apart — tree-lined streets, small-town shops along Big Basin Way, and hiking trails into the redwoods just minutes from downtown.

The town grew from a Gold Rush-era sawmill settlement, then reinvented itself as an orchard community before becoming one of the wealthiest residential enclaves in California. What remains from that layered history is a place with genuine character: [Villa Montalvo](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/saratoga), the 1912 Mediterranean villa turned arts centre, hosts concerts in its hillside amphitheatre all summer. The Mountain Winery, founded by Paul Masson in 1890, still pours wine and stages outdoor shows with views across the valley.

Big Basin Way is the main strip — a walkable stretch of restaurants, wine-tasting rooms, and independent shops. Saratoga is also the starting point for trails into Sanborn County Park and Castle Rock State Park, where the coastal redwoods begin in earnest. For a short walk, Hakone Estate and Gardens is a peaceful hillside Japanese garden with views over the town.
