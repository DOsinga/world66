Alturas is the county seat of Modoc County, tucked into the far northeastern corner of California where most people have never been. With a population of around 2,600, it is one of the most remote towns in the state, surrounded by high desert, sagebrush flats, and volcanic tablelands. The nearest city of any size is Redding, three hours to the west. If you want to see a part of California that looks nothing like the postcards, this is it.

The town itself is small and quiet, with a handful of cafes and shops along Main Street. The Modoc County Historical Museum documents the area's ranching heritage and the Modoc War of 1872-73, one of the last major conflicts between the US Army and Native Americans in California. A preserved turn-of-the-century jailhouse sits nearby. For food, the Brass Rail is a reliable steakhouse and local gathering spot.

The real draw is the landscape. The Modoc National Wildlife Refuge, just south of town, spreads across sagebrush-covered hills and seasonal wetlands where you can spot waterfowl, pronghorn antelope, and mule deer. The South Warner Wilderness Area to the east offers serious backcountry hiking with almost no crowds — you can walk for hours without seeing another person. In winter, the high plateaus are blanketed in snow and the silence is extraordinary.
