Redding is the gateway to California's far north, sitting in the Sacramento Valley where the Sacramento River flows out of the mountains. It is not a destination in itself for most travellers, but it is the natural base for exploring some of the state's most dramatic landscape: Mount Shasta to the north, Lassen Volcanic National Park to the east, Shasta Lake and its massive dam nearby, and the wild Trinity Alps to the west.

The Sundial Bridge, a glass-decked cantilever crossing designed by Santiago Calatrava, is the city's landmark and the centrepiece of Turtle Bay Exploration Park, which combines botanical gardens, a museum and riverside trails. Caldwell City Park and the Sacramento River Trail offer easy walks. The Redding Museum of Art and History has exhibits on the local Wintu people.

Summers are searingly hot — Redding regularly tops 40°C — so spring and autumn are the best times to visit. The restaurant scene is better than you might expect for a city this size, with good options along the main strips.
