Built between 1938 and 1945, Shasta Dam is one of the largest concrete dams in the United States and the second tallest in the country when it was completed. The visitor centre offers free guided tours of the dam's interior — a surprisingly impressive look at Depression-era federal engineering on a massive scale. The spillway, which drops 480 feet when it runs, is taller than Niagara Falls. Shasta Lake behind the dam is a popular houseboat destination; the Sacramento, McCloud, and Pit rivers all flow into it.
