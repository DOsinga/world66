Santiago Calatrava's pedestrian bridge over the Sacramento River is the centrepiece of Redding. The white steel tower and glass deck are striking in any light, but the bridge was designed to function as a working sundial — on the summer solstice at solar noon, the shadow of the 217-foot pylon falls precisely on the sundial's granite markers. The bridge is free to walk and connects the north and south sections of the Sacramento River Trail. On the south bank, it leads into Turtle Bay Exploration Park.
