Truckee sits at 5,800 feet in the Sierra Nevada, twenty minutes north of Lake Tahoe and squarely in the path of every winter storm that rolls in off the Pacific. It started as a rough-and-tumble railroad town — the transcontinental railroad came through in 1868 — and that history is still legible in the wooden storefronts along Commercial Row downtown.

Today Truckee is a proper mountain town with good restaurants, independent shops, and easy access to several ski resorts, including Northstar and Palisades Tahoe. In summer it flips into hiking, mountain biking, and river country. The Truckee River runs right through town, and the surrounding national forest is vast.

The downtown strip is small enough to walk in twenty minutes but has enough character to hold your attention. Stop at one of the brewpubs, wander through the old Jail museum, or just watch freight trains pass through — Truckee is still an active rail junction, and the sight of a long Union Pacific train threading between the mountains hasn't gotten old.

Donner Lake, just west of town, is where the Donner Party got trapped in the winter of 1846–47. The Emigrant Trail Museum at Donner Memorial State Park tells the story without sensationalizing it.
