title: "Day Trips in Aspen"
type: section
---

If you're looking to get out of Aspen for a short while, there are plenty of day trips nearby that can be reached either in a short time or with a somewhat longer, scenic drive. Here are a few of the top destinations for day trips from Aspen.

**[Carbondale](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/carbondale), Colorado**

Located just 30 minutes from Aspen, this scenic town still retains a bit of the Wild West with its summertime fun. Throughout the summer months you can see the farmer's market complete with live music and arts and crafts on Wednesdays, the rodeo on Thursdays, and take part in the late night dances on Fridays. From spring to fall you can enjoy hiking, biking and fly fishing, and all year round you can stroll down Main Street to visit the art galleries, cafes and boutiques.

**[Glenwood Springs](/northamerica/unitedstates/colorado/glenwood_springs), Colorado**

For those who are truly into the Wild West, Glenwood Springs is the place to go. Just 45 minutes from Aspen you can find the final resting place of Doc Holliday as well as explore the Frontier Museum. Explore the Glenwood Caverns or hike up to Hanging Lake to see the waterfalls. In warmer weather you can enjoy white water rafting, golfing and more. The real draw, though, is the gigantic pool and waterslide filled with water from the natural hot springs. There are also plenty of spas around to help with your relaxation. And for those who want to do some skiing, you can visit Sunlight Mountain Resort.

**[Leadville](/northamerica/unitedstates/colorado/leadville), Colorado**

Leadville is about an hour drive from Aspen and is known as "Sky City, USA." Visit the old Tabor Opera House, Silver Dollar Saloon and National Mining Hall of Fame and Museum. Twin Lakes is great for hiking, biking and fly fishing, and don't forget to stroll down Harrison Avenue to hunt up some antiques or pick up a few western souvenirs. Leadville also boasts Ski Cooper, great for novices, as well as Mayflower Gulch for more experienced skiers.

**Redstone, Colorado**

What was once an old mining village is now a quaint tourist attraction in the Crystal River Valley, just an hour and a half from Aspen. In town you can visit the antique shops and the art galleries, as well as the many boutiques and old-fashioned general store. Take a turn through the old schoolhouse and the miner's cabins that still stand, and definitely don't leave without touring Redstone Castle.

**Paonia, Colorado**

Paonia is nearly a 3 hour drive from Aspen through scenic country into Colorado's wine country. In Paonia you can stop by the Leroux Creek Vineyards, Stone Cottage Cellars and Alfred Eames Cellars. Taste the wine, buy a few bottles for home and have a bite to eat. For those who prefer beer, there's also Revolution Brewing where you can sample various types of ales. Grand Street has a pretty little park to wander, and in the fall you can enjoy apple picking.
