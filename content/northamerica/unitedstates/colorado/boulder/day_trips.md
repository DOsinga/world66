**Walks, Hiking and Climbing**

Just outside of Boulder, well visible from within the city limits, you can take a beautiful walk or hike through the Open Space and Mountain Parks. This area which has been a a nature preserve for over a hundred years houses mountain lions and bears and varies in height from 5500 to 8144 feet.

The Flat Irons Area can be accessed from 30 or so trail heads. There are several difficulty levels for the trails and quite a few of them are accessible for wheel chairs. There are also a few rocks which are open for climbing. Do look at the OSMP signs though that you will find at the trail heads, they indicate which areas are not accesible to the public due to nesting season.

The Open Space and Mountain Parks have their own website http://www.ci.boulder.co.us/openspace/ where you can get more info on the trails and their conditions, the history of the park and download some trail maps.

For about 8 dollars you can buy the Official Open Space & Mountains Parks Trailsmap at the Boulder Map Gallery on 13th and Arapahoe.
