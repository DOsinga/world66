As a premiere college town and ski resort, Boulder, Colorado offers
no shortage of shopping adventures. With over 90 business districts
adjacent to the University of Colorado, you can find everything from a
traditional mall with upscale shops to small, locally owned businesses.
Near Colorado State University, Boulder you will find plenty of college
apparel. Just a stone's throw away, in the heart of downtown, you will
find both upscale and inexpensive but tasteful gift shops. Most of the
sporting goods stores are clustered near the ski resorts, although some
are downtown. Peruse this list for more details.

**Twenty Ninth Street**

This is Boulder's newest open-air shopping district and restaurant
community. With more than 70 shops, eateries, and department stores,
you will find the perfect souvenir for everyone in your family. Shops
include Anthropologie, Borders, and Gap, among locally owned
businesses. Restaruants include reliable digs, such as California Pizza
Kitchen and Chiptole Mexican Grill. Want to work out? Plenty of
atheltic facilities along this popular drag.

**Pearl Street Mall**

Boulder's most popular pedestrain mall by locals and tourists alike,
Pearl Street showcases a wide selection of bookstores, botiques,
sidewalk cafes, and world-famous restaurants. Don't miss out on season
art shows, musical perofrmances, and craft fairs. Here you can
challenge the "Zip Code Man" to guess you hometown just by telling him
your zip code!

Specific shops listed below and classified by type:

**Gifts & Novelties**

Belvedere Belgian Chocolate Shop:
You can’t visit Boulder without stopping in this delightful little
shop, with all natural Belgian-style chocolates, very popular with the
locals. No wax, artificial colors, flavorings or preservatives used in
these Colorado-made chocolates. Reasonably priced.

Little Buddha Imports:
Boulder wouldn’t be Boulder without a store featuring the Buddha.
Clothes, incense, religious statue, singing bow, books, and music –
mostly from India, Nepal, and Tibet. A must see on your trip to Boulder.

Fiji:
Located inside the Boulder Shambhala Center, Fiji is your one stop for
all things meditation, yoga, pilates, or just general relaxation. You
can also get a tour of the Shambhala center and sit in on a meditation
class.

**Boulder-Specific Clothing Stores**

Boulder-Nepal:
This quaint, hippie store provides a large selection of souvenirs from
around the world (including Nepal), handicrafts, paintings, gift ideas,
religious items, herbal incense, spiritual books, and more. Clothes for
men, women, and children can also be found at Boulder Nepal.

Crystal Dragon:
This store represents the Boulder of 30 years ago. Hippie galore!
Incense, tapestries, candles, cards, posters, t-shirts, books,
clothing, flags, buttons, and more!

Kinsley Co:
Looking for a fine evening gown? Go no farther than Kinsley Co.
Tailored outfits, dresses, and suits crafted by top designers worldwide.

Tough Luck Cowboy:
Best store name in all of Boulder! Definitely make a visit to this
western-themed store, featuring a colorful clothes, cowboy gear,
antiques, western gifts, art, and jewelry.

Where the Buffalo Roam: Your place for that CU Boulder shirt and other college apparel. Moderately priced. Located downtown.

**Sporting Goods**

** ** Alpine Base & Edge:
This is your one stop ski shop! Offers ski/snowbards for rental or
purchase, ski apparel, ski tuning, and boots. Don’t forget the
sunglasses or goggles, sun block, gloves, and hat!

Patagonia, Inc:
Always reliable and ubiquitous, Patagonia sells a range of outdoor
clothing (all organic) for men, women, and children. Also sells
sporting goods, travel gear, books, and maps.

**Food/Coffee Houses**

KuCha House of Tea:
Need to unwind after a long day of shopping. Come to KuCha House of Tea
and sample some of the finest teas from all over the world. Located
downtown.

Papa Ben’s Brittle:
Craving gourmet peanut better? Come to Papa Ben’s locally owned brittle
shop, created 60 years ago in Indiana.. Only organic goods uses, such
as Valencia peanuts and sugar Papa Ben’s candy combines both sweet and
salty flavors. Located downtown.

Oliv You & Me:
This European-themed store sells olive oils from all over the world,
and uses them in their cooking. Serves espresso drinks, tea, pastries,
sandwiches, and ciabattas. Located downtown.
