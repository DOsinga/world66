Buena Vista Heritage Museum is located in the former Chaffee County Courthouse.  The most impressive building in town, construction started in 1882 and was completed in 1883.   The building remained the county seat until after elections in 1928, when the more populous town of Salida won the seat.   After construction of the courthouse in Salida in 1932, the building was turned over to the Buena Vista School District. All schoolchildren, first through twelfth grades went to school in the building until 1958.   The courthouse was abandoned as a school in 1972 and was used for storage until saved from destruction by the Buena Vista Heritage Museum group.  The museum occupies the ground floor. Upstairs is a research room. The former courtroom upstairs is now the Chaffee County Council on the Arts Gallery.
