If I were coming to Buena Vista here is what I'd do: 

1. Go to Kay's ice cream shop and enjoy the great weather in the park located directly behind the shop. 

2. Go to the town lake located at the corner of Hwy24 and Main St (the only stoplight in the town). In the summer there are ducks there so you can bring bread crumbs to feed them. Fishing is also a popular activity here. 

3. Go to Loback's bakery located on East Main Street. This bakery has been around forver. Go inside to get the feel of an old town bakery and the glazed doughnuts and twisties are amazing if made fresh that day. 

4. There's a road called Midland road which you can drive on that gives you an amazing aerial view of the Buena Vista valley with the colleigate mountains in the background. 

5. The Barbara Whipple Trail located at the VERY end of East Main street is a great hike with some amazing scenery as described above. 

6. As a youth, I enjoyed innertubbing in the Nature Trail creek located in back of the local highschool and middle school. Even if you are not planning on getting wet, it's a good walking-trail located directly in town. 

7. The Old Courthouse located on East Main St. next to the elementary school has some hidden gems; the lower portion serves as a local museum, the upper portion usualy is hosting a visual arts show. Don't forget to see the toy train display upstairs if you can - this will give you a great view of the surrounding valleys in mini-scale! 

8. If you follow Main street going West you will soon (3 miles) find yourself in the mountains. This is a wonderful drive during the summer and fall months. 

9. With few drive-ins remain in the US today, the drive-in theater located about 2 miles West on main street is a great option for those Summer nights. . 

10. The Mt. Princeton hot springs are a great escape into the mountains. Although over priced and lacking friendly customer service, the natural hot springs are best at anytime of the season, day or night.
