Durango is a mountain town in the San Juan Mountains of southwestern Colorado, sitting at 6,512 feet where the Animas River cuts through a broad valley. Founded in 1880 as a railroad hub for the mining camps further up in the mountains, it still has that frontier energy — a place where people come to do things outdoors and talk about it over good beer afterward.

The big draw is the sheer variety of adventure within easy reach. The [Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad](https://www.durangotrain.com/) is a genuine piece of history, steaming through the Animas River gorge on tracks laid in 1882. Mountain biking here is world-class, with trails ranging from flowy singletrack to serious alpine descents. Winter brings skiing at Purgatory Resort, 25 miles north on US-550. In summer the Animas River runs right through town, and you can watch kayakers play in the rapids from the riverside trail or join a rafting trip yourself. [Mesa Verde National Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/colorado/mesaverdenationalpark), with its extraordinary cliff dwellings, is just 36 miles to the west.

Durango is also a college town — Fort Lewis College sits on a mesa above downtown — which keeps the restaurants, brewpubs, and bars along Main Avenue lively year-round. The historic downtown is walkable, well-preserved, and has enough good places to eat and drink that you will not run out of options even on a longer stay.
