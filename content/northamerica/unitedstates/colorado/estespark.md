Estes Park sits in a wide mountain meadow at 7,500 feet, ringed by the snowcapped peaks of the Front Range. It is the eastern gateway to **Rocky Mountain National Park**, and that is the only reason most people come — the park's main entrance and the start of Trail Ridge Road, the highest paved through-road in the United States, are both ten minutes from town. In summer the elk that wander the park's tundra often wander the town as well, browsing in front yards and stopping traffic on Elkhorn Avenue.

Above town stands the **Stanley Hotel**, a wedding-cake of a 1909 resort that Stephen King checked into one September night in 1974, was the only guest, and woke from a nightmare with the idea for *The Shining*. The hotel plays the connection up — there are ghost tours, a hedge maze added later, and the Overlook from the Kubrick film projected on the lawn on summer nights.

Downtown Estes is touristy in the way of all national-park gateway towns: taffy shops, fudge, river-walk cafes, and a steady weekend crush from the Denver Front Range. Stay if you want a base for the park or for one of the great drives — Trail Ridge over the Continental Divide, or the Peak to Peak byway south to Black Hawk and the old gold-rush towns. The shoulder seasons (May, late September) are the best time; July and August are jammed and the park requires a timed-entry permit.
