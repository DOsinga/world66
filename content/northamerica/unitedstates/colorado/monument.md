Monument sits on the Palmer Divide, the high ridge separating the South Platte and Arkansas River watersheds, roughly halfway between [Denver](/northamerica/unitedstates/colorado/denver) and [Colorado Springs](/northamerica/unitedstates/colorado/coloradosprings) along I-25. At nearly 7,000 feet, it is one of the higher towns along the Front Range corridor, and the surrounding Ponderosa pine forests give it a distinctly mountain-town feel despite being just minutes from the interstate.

The small downtown along Second and Front Streets has a handful of local restaurants and shops. Monument Lake sits at the north end of town and makes for a pleasant short walk. For a bigger outing, the trails at nearby Mount Herman and Limbaugh Canyon offer views across the plains to the east and the Rampart Range to the west.

Most visitors pass through Monument on the way to somewhere else, but it works well as a quiet base if you want easy access to both Denver and Colorado Springs without staying in either city.
