Snowmass Village is a mountain resort community in Colorado's Elk Mountains, sitting about 12 km from Aspen and sharing the same dramatic scenery. In winter it operates as a major ski resort with over 90 runs and one of the largest ski areas in Colorado — less glamorous than Aspen, but with more varied terrain and generally shorter lift lines. In summer the ski trails become hiking and mountain biking paths, and the village hosts an outdoor music festival season.

The Base Village at the foot of the mountain has been substantially rebuilt in recent years and now has a concentration of restaurants, shops, and lodging options clustered around a lively plaza. The village is explicitly oriented toward outdoor activity — ski-in/ski-out is the norm in winter, and the summer trail network connects to the Maroon Bells wilderness area above.

Snowmass makes a practical and slightly less expensive alternative to staying in Aspen itself, with frequent shuttle connections between the two.
