Westminster is a suburban city of about 115,000 people in the Denver metropolitan area, sitting between Denver and Boulder in Adams and Jefferson counties. It has grown rapidly since the 1990s and is now one of Colorado's larger cities, though it functions primarily as a suburb rather than a destination in its own right. The city's main attraction is its location: Denver is 15 minutes south, Boulder 20 minutes north, and Rocky Mountain National Park is about an hour away.

The Butterfly Pavilion, a tropical insect and invertebrate zoo, is the most notable specific attraction in Westminster and is well regarded, particularly for families. The Promenade at Westminster is a large outdoor shopping and entertainment complex. For most travellers, Westminster is a practical place to stay when visiting the Denver-Boulder corridor rather than a reason to visit in itself.
