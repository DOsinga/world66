Winter Park is a ski town tucked into the Rocky Mountains about 70 miles northwest of Denver, sitting at an elevation of nearly 9,000 feet. It is one of Colorado's most accessible mountain resorts — the drive from Denver takes under two hours, and Amtrak's Winter Park Express runs a seasonal ski train straight from Union Station to the base of the slopes.

Winter Park Resort is the main draw, with over 3,000 acres of skiable terrain spread across seven territories. Mary Jane, the resort's backside mountain, is famous for its mogul runs and expert-level bump fields — locals consider it some of the best mogul skiing in the state. For beginners and families, the front side offers wide, groomed cruisers with views across the Fraser Valley. In summer, the resort converts to a mountain bike park with lift-served downhill trails that attract riders from across the region.

The town itself is a compact strip along US Highway 40, with a cluster of restaurants, gear shops, and bars. It does not have the upscale polish of Vail or the party-town energy of Breckenridge — Winter Park feels more like a working ski town, and that is part of its appeal. Nearby Granby and the Fraser Valley offer additional lodging options and a quieter pace if the base village feels too busy.

Winter Park sits at the western end of Berthoud Pass, one of Colorado's classic mountain drives. In winter, the pass can be treacherous but beautiful; in summer, it is a scenic route worth taking slowly. The surrounding Arapaho National Forest offers hiking, fishing, and camping once the snow melts.
