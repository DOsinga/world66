New Haven is a city defined by Yale University, but it has a character that goes well beyond the campus gates. The university gives the city world-class museums and a lively arts scene, while the surrounding neighbourhoods contribute some of the best pizza in the United States and a gritty urban energy that keeps things real.

Start at the Green, the original town square laid out in 1638 and still the heart of the city. Three churches line its north side, and from here it is a short walk into the Yale campus. The Yale University Art Gallery and the Yale Center for British Art face each other across Chapel Street and are both free. The Beinecke Rare Book and Manuscript Library, with its translucent marble walls, is worth a visit even if you are not a book lover.

Beyond the campus, New Haven has good green space. Lighthouse Point Park on the harbour is a recreation area and nature preserve with a restored carousel. West Rock Ridge State Park, north of the city, offers hiking trails and views across Long Island Sound from the summit of Judges Cave.

New Haven also punches above its weight in theatre. The Yale Repertory Theater and the Long Wharf Theater both stage ambitious productions, and the Goodspeed Opera House in nearby East Haddam specialises in musicals.
