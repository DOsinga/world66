---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

New Haven is famous for its pizza — locals call it "apizza" (pronounced ah-BEETS) and the rivalry between the old-school parlours is fierce. Frank Pepe Pizzeria Napoletana on Wooster Street has been serving coal-fired pies since 1925; the white clam pizza is the one to order. Just down the street, Sally's Apizza draws equally devoted crowds. Modern Apizza on State Street rounds out the holy trinity with a thinner, crispier crust.

Beyond pizza, the city has a solid restaurant scene fuelled by the university crowd. Union League Cafe on Chapel Street serves refined French bistro cooking in a handsome dining room. Ibiza on Orange Street is well regarded for Spanish cuisine. Barcelona, also on Orange Street, does excellent tapas. For Southeast Asian flavours, Bentara on Orange Street serves Malaysian dishes in a warm setting.
