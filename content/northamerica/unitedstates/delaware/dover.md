Dover is the capital of Delaware and sits squarely in the middle of the state, an easy drive from both Philadelphia and the Atlantic beaches. It is a small city — quietly proud of its history — and one that most travelers drive past on the way to somewhere else. That's a mistake worth correcting.

The city was laid out in 1717 on land William Penn had granted decades earlier, and the historic core around The Green still looks much as it did in the 18th century. The State House on South State Street is among the oldest legislative buildings in continuous use in the country. Dover Air Force Base, on the city's eastern edge, houses the Air Mobility Command Museum, one of the better aviation collections in the Mid-Atlantic.

Delaware State University, a historically Black university, adds student energy to an otherwise unhurried town. Dover is manageable on foot for a day, and the surrounding Kent County countryside — flat, agricultural, crisscrossed by small creeks — is good cycling territory.
