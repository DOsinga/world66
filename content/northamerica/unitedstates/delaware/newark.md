Newark sits in northern Delaware just a few miles from the Maryland border, a college town built around the University of Delaware. With about 35,000 residents and 24,000 students, the university defines the town's rhythm — Main Street empties out in summer and buzzes during the school year. It is a pleasant, walkable place that most travelers pass through rather than visit, but it has enough going on to warrant a stop.

Main Street is the heart of things, a long stretch of restaurants, bars, coffee shops, and independent stores running east from the university campus. Caffe Gelato is a local institution for Italian food, and Deer Park Tavern — where Edgar Allan Poe reportedly drank — has been serving beer since 1851. The university campus itself is attractive, with a long green mall lined by Georgian brick buildings.

Newark is well connected by road and rail. The Newark station on Amtrak's Northeast Corridor puts Philadelphia about 45 minutes away and Wilmington just 15 minutes. White Clay Creek State Park, immediately north of town, has good trails for hiking and mountain biking through wooded hills along the creek. The Iron Hill Museum of Natural History, housed in a former schoolhouse, is a small but worthwhile stop if you are interested in local geology and archaeology.
