Rehoboth Beach is a small, walkable beach town on Delaware's Atlantic coast that bills itself as "The Nation's Summer Capital" — a reference to the waves of Washington DC families, politicians, and lobbyists who have been coming here since the late 19th century. The mile-long boardwalk is the heart of it: Thrasher's fries, Grotto Pizza, Dolle's saltwater taffy, and a bandstand with free concerts on summer evenings.

Beyond the boardwalk, the town has a good restaurant scene that punches above its weight for a place this size. Rehoboth Avenue, the main drag, runs straight from the beach to a cluster of shops and restaurants. The town is notably welcoming and has a large LGBTQ community, particularly visible during the summer months.

Cape Henlopen State Park, at the north end of town, has wide beaches, a fishing pier, dunes, and an observation tower from a World War II coastal defence installation. Delaware has no sales tax, which makes the outlet malls on Route 1 a draw for shoppers from the neighbouring states. Lewes, a charming colonial town with a ferry to Cape May, New Jersey, is just fifteen minutes north.
