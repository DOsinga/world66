Wilmington is Delaware's largest city, sitting along the I-95 corridor roughly equidistant from Philadelphia, Baltimore, and Washington, DC. Founded in 1731 as Willingtown, it grew around the confluence of the Delaware, Christina, and Brandywine rivers — first as a colonial shipping port, then as a milling centre. The rapid flow of the Brandywine attracted E.I. du Pont, who established his gunpowder mills here in 1802, launching the DuPont company that shaped the city's fortunes for two centuries.

Today Wilmington is known primarily as a corporate hub — Delaware's business-friendly incorporation laws mean that over half of Fortune 500 companies are incorporated here. For visitors, the Brandywine Valley is the main draw: the Hagley Museum preserves the original DuPont powder works along the river, and the Winterthur estate houses one of the country's finest collections of American decorative arts. The Riverfront development along the Christina River has brought restaurants and entertainment to what was once industrial waterfront.
