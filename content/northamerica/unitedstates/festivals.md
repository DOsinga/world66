---
title: "Festivals"
type: section
---

The US has a rich and varied festival calendar, ranging from ancient cultural celebrations to modern music extravaganzas.

**Mardi Gras** (New Orleans, late February/early March) is the country's most famous party. The official celebration falls on Fat Tuesday — the day before Ash Wednesday — but the festivities in New Orleans run for weeks. Parades, costumes, brass bands, and enormous quantities of food define the event. Go before the crowds peak if you want to experience the neighbourhood parades rather than the tourist-heavy stretch of Bourbon Street.

**Coachella** (Indio, California, two weekends in April) is the US's most high-profile music festival, drawing big names and huge crowds to the Coachella Valley. Line-up quality is consistently high. Tickets sell out fast and are expensive; accommodation in the area books out months in advance.

**Burning Man** (Black Rock Desert, Nevada, late August/early September) is less a music festival than a temporary city built in the desert around radical self-expression, art installations, and communal living. Around 80,000 people attend for a week. It is deliberately self-sufficient: bring your own food, water, and shelter, and expect dust storms.

**Jazz Fest** (New Orleans, late April/early May) is the New Orleans Jazz and Heritage Festival — two weekends of music across multiple stages, with food and crafts stalls. It draws major acts across many genres alongside local New Orleans legends.

**Lollapalooza** (Chicago, late July/early August) occupies Grant Park for four days with an enormous multi-stage line-up of rock, hip-hop, and electronic music. One of the oldest US music festivals, now replicated internationally.

**Thanksgiving** (nationwide, fourth Thursday of November) is the essential American holiday — a national day of family gatherings and eating. The Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade in New York is the classic televised spectacle. Many businesses close for the long weekend.

**Fourth of July** (nationwide, July 4) is Independence Day. Every city and town holds fireworks and parades. Washington DC has one of the most impressive displays, as does New York (the East River). A good excuse to be anywhere outdoors in summer.
