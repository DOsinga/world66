Florida is a place people come to reinvent themselves — or simply to escape winter. It has built its entire identity around tourism and does not pretend otherwise. More than 60 million visitors a year flood the state, yet it still manages to surprise: pull off the interstate into the scrub pines of the Panhandle, or paddle a flat-bottomed boat through the Everglades at dawn, and you'll find something altogether different from the theme-park version of Florida most people imagine.

With over 21 million residents and 1,500 kilometres of coastline, the state is as large and varied as a medium-sized country. The western Panhandle — Pensacola, Destin, Panama City Beach — has some of the finest white-sand beaches in the United States, the state's only caverns, and excellent canoeing along spring-fed rivers. North-central Florida is home to Tallahassee, the state capital, and to the famous Suwannee River; small towns here feel unmistakably Southern in a way the rest of the state does not.

Northeast Florida anchors [Jacksonville](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/jacksonville) — the state's largest city — and [St. Augustine](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/st_augustine), the oldest continuously inhabited European settlement in America. Further south, [Orlando](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/orlando) and its constellation of theme parks dominate Central Florida: Disney World, Universal, SeaWorld, and enough other attractions to keep a family busy for weeks. But Orlando's gravitational pull means the lovely citrus towns to the south and the lake country to the north go largely ignored by tourists — worth the detour.

On the Gulf Coast, [Tampa](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/tampabayarea) and [St. Petersburg](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/tampabayarea/stpetersburg) are genuinely good cities with year-round beach weather and calm, warm water. The Southwest coast — [Naples](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/naples), [Sarasota](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/sarasota) — trends wealthier and quieter, with strong arts scenes and pristine gulf beaches.

South Florida is [Miami](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/miami) and everything it implies: Art Deco, Cuban food, neon lights, and the relentless energy of a city that takes itself seriously as a global crossroads. Beyond Miami lies the [Everglades](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/everglades) — vast, flat, and otherworldly — and then the long arc of the [Florida Keys](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/keywest), culminating in Key West, where the mood shifts to something slower and the sunsets draw actual crowds. Drive US 1 all the way down at least once; it's one of the great American road trips.
