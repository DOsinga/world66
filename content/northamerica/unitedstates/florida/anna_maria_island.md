Anna Maria Island is a seven-mile barrier island off the coast of Manatee County, just south of the mouth of Tampa Bay. It has managed to resist the high-rise development that dominates much of the Florida Gulf coast, and the result is a low-key, old-Florida atmosphere of beach cottages, small restaurants, and quiet streets.

The island has three small communities: Anna Maria at the north end (the quietest), Holmes Beach in the middle (the most commercial), and Bradenton Beach at the south end, where the historic fishing pier marks the spot where the original 1921 bridge first connected the island to the mainland.

The beaches are wide, white-sand, and uncrowded by Florida standards. The water is calm and warm. Kayaking through the mangrove channels on the bay side is excellent for spotting manatees, dolphins, and wading birds. The island is small enough to explore entirely by bicycle.
