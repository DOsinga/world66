Boynton Beach is a mid-sized city on Florida's southeast coast, wedged between West Palm Beach to the north and Delray Beach to the south. It is less flashy than its neighbours but has a pleasant stretch of public beach and a growing downtown with restaurants and breweries around the marina.

The main draw for visitors is the water. The Gulf Stream runs close to shore here, making Boynton Beach popular for drift diving on the nearby reefs and wrecks. The Boynton Beach Inlet is a productive fishing spot, and Oceanfront Park has a clean, uncrowded beach with a playground and picnic areas. Arthur R. Marshall Loxahatchee National Wildlife Refuge, just inland, protects a large section of the northern Everglades and has good birdwatching on its boardwalk trails.
