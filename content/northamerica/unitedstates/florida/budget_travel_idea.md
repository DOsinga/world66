---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Florida"
type: section
---

**Florida budget travel **may mean that you have to skip lots of
the most popular Florida travel destinations, but it also allows you to
experience remarkable activities that too often go ignored in the land
of never-ending theme parks. When traveling to Florida on a budget,
consider any of the following excursions:

**Spend Time on a Public Beach**

One of Florida's most attractive spots for a relaxing, fun vacation is completely
free and is one of the many public beaches located on the coasts of the
state. Spend a day swimming, sunbathing and playing sports or
children's games on the sand. Try Bahia Honda State Park in the Florida Keys for a breathtaking tropical beach or the beaches in South Walton for some of the world's softest, most luxurious sand.

**Historic Pensacola Village**

In Pensacola, you can visit a historical village for only $6 per
adult, $5 per senior or military member and $3 per child ages 4 to 16
(kids under 4 are free).Twenty - seven buildings (11 of which are open
to the public) make up the historic Pensacola Village, where
architecture has been preserved since the early 20th century and sponsored by the University of West Florida. Children have fun in the hands-on Discovery Exhibit but also learn much about the history of Florida while touring the buildings and museums.

**National Naval Aviation Museum**

This Pensacola national attraction, one of the world's largest aviation museums, is free for all visitors and makes the perfect addition to any budget traveler's travel itinerary. Learn the history of the U.S. Navy, the U.S Coast Guard and the U.S. Marine Corps and see over 150 decommissioned spacecraft and aircraft. Children with an interest in machines are particularly thrilled to spend a day at the museum. There's also an IMAX theater, cafe and a museum store.
