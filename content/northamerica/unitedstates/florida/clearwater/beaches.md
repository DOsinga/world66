**Clearwater Beach**

This is a beach made for play. As if the white sandy beaches and blue water weren't enough to draw people in, Clearwater Beach is loaded with activities. Volleyball and swimming are popular pastimes here. Additionally, travelers can soar 400 feet in the air for $60 with Parasail City and get some incredible photographs of the gorgeous waterfront below. Beach goers can rent jet skis or broad fishing charters from Clearwater Marina. Although, if fishing with your toes in the sand sounds better, that can be done too. Beach showers, restrooms, cabanas, and umbrellas rentals are useful amenities available. Clearwater Beach is made for the beach goer who wants to be surrounded by a crowd and have a large range of activities at their finger tips.

**Pier 60**

Pier 60, open 24 hours, is located next to Clearwater Beach and it's a must-see. On the pier you can rent fishing rods and daily fishing licenses so you can reel in that big fish over the side of the pier. There is a playground to keep the kids entertained and various inexpensive snack shops to keep appetites at ease. If you visit Pier 60 at least 2 hours before sunset you will be blessed with the vibrant atmosphere of Sunsets at Pier 60. Local artist and craftsmen display their work, which you can purchase. Also, entertainers such as jugglers and musicians, perform. Visiting Pier 60 is almost like visiting a pier from the early 1900s. This is a unique and vibrant part of Clearwater Beach.

**Sand Key Beach**

If you want a more laid-back beach experience in Clearwater visit Sand Key Beach. It is comprised of a 65-acre park that is justly rated one of the top beaches in the US. The beach is equipped with showers, restrooms, and picnic tables. Sand Key Beach is ideal for swimming and lounging. There is no launch here for jet skis or boats. It is truly a beach for travelers who want a natural, beautiful beach experience away from the large crowds at Clearwater Beach.

It is important to know that there is no alcohol allowed on the beaches and you must swim within the "Safe Bathing Limits" away from water crafts. Lifeguards watch the low rolling waves until 4:30 pm. Throw some extra change into your beach bag for the parking meters that are located at both beaches and remember the SPF! Florida beaches are enjoyable year-round, but the best time to visit Clearwater beaches is during the months of April to September during late morning to avoid common afternoon showers.
