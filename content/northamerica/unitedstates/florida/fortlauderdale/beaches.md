---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Fort Lauderdale has about 23 miles of public beach, all sand, most of it within half an hour of downtown. The main **Fort Lauderdale Beach**, running north from the 17th Street Causeway to Sunrise Boulevard, is the postcard strip — wide, pale, and lined with a low-rise promenade of cafes and shops since the 1990s rebuild. Just south, **Harbor Beach** is quieter; north of Sunrise, the sand continues past **Hugh Taylor Birch State Park** and becomes progressively less crowded. For something wilder, **John U. Lloyd Beach State Park** in Dania has a long tree-lined stretch with a surfable break near Port Everglades. All the beaches have lifeguards and most have metered parking.
