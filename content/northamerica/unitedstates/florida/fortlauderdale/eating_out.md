---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Fort Lauderdale's dining scene runs from casual seafood shacks to upscale waterfront restaurants, with a strong Caribbean and Latin American streak reflecting south Florida's population. **Las Olas Boulevard** has the biggest concentration — everything from Cuban cafés to Italian trattorias and raw bars — and the beachfront strip has the usual line-up of seafood-and-margaritas places with ocean views. For the local specialty, look for **stone crab claws** (in season October to May), fresh-caught **mahi-mahi** and **grouper**, and the Cuban staple **ropa vieja**. The area around 17th Street and the Las Olas Marina has some of the best harbourside restaurants, where half the clientele arrives by boat.
