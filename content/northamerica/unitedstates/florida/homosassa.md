Homosassa is a small community on Florida's Nature Coast, where the Homosassa River runs clear and cool from natural springs into the Gulf of Mexico. The area is best known for Florida manatees, which gather in the warm spring-fed waters year-round but are most visible in winter when sea temperatures drop.

Ellie Schiller Homosassa Springs Wildlife State Park is the main draw — a state-managed facility where injured and non-releasable manatees are cared for alongside Florida panthers, black bears, and native birds. The park offers snorkelling in the springs and an underwater observatory where you can watch manatees at close range.

The river itself is excellent for kayaking and boating, with clear water revealing fish, turtles, and the occasional manatee below. Nearby Crystal River has the largest concentration of manatees in the state and offers guided swim-with-manatees tours during the winter months.
