**St. Augustine**

St. Augustine is a city with as much beauty as there is history. It's located a little less than an hour away from Jacksonville and you can get here by car. While at the nation's oldest city you should most certainly visit the historic district. There are shops that sell unique gifts, some of which are handmade, and places to grab a quick bite to eat. Visit the Castillo de San Marcos National Monument. A guided tour of this fort will leave you with an even better appreciation of the city. Make sure that you stop by the St. Augustine Lighthouse and use your leg muscles to climb the 219 steps to the top. Be warned that it's supposedly haunted. A trip to St. Augustine will last you all day and almost every traveler will love this trip. The walking may be difficult for seniors.

**Amelia Island**

A day-trip to Amelia Island is perfect for the traveler with adventure on their mind. It's located 45 minutes from Jacksonville and is a North Florida treasure. Victorian-era homes and oak trees are mixed with untouched beaches and harbors. Visitors arrive by ferry, car or plane. While here you can explore the various shops and restaurants that are on Amelia Island's main town, Fernandina Beach. Nature-lovers will adore the prescient wilderness of the island. Kayaking, hiking, fishing, boating, and swimming are just some of the activities that this destination has to offer. It's also a great place to view wildlife like dolphins, manatees, and turtles. If you need a break from the heavy traffic of Jacksonville, this is the day-trip for you.

**Kennedy Space Center**

The Kennedy Space Center is roughly two hours away from Jacksonville and well worth the drive. The Visitor Complex is open every day from 9:00 am to 5:30 pm and is admission cost $40. This admission will include access to a special IMAX movie, all exhibits, all shows, and entrance into the Astronaut Hall of Fame. For an extra $20 you can purchase a bus tour of the launch areas with a knowledgeable guide. I recommend doing the tour because you truly get to witness incredible views of the launch pad. The weekends do get a bit crowded, but there is plenty of space for everyone to maneuver around comfortably. Families with children will love this day-trip.
