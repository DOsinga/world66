---
title: "Things to do in Key West"
type: section
---

Check out the pamphlets in the lobby of your hotel! There are tons of day trips to go on, and even night trips. I went there for my mother's wedding, and being a minor (19), I was able to explore more than the drunkenness of Duval Street. Here are a few points from my trip.

+ The Pirate museum is a must see. Pat Croce really outdid himself with this adventure. It's a little pricey, but it is absolutely worth it.

+ The Original Ghost Tours. You meet at the La Concha Hotel and then make your way to several different sites where terrible deaths have happened. You get to hear some of the colorful, yet dark history of Key West, and you might even see some ghosts! Make sure you bring your camera. This is a 90 minute tour, but you do less than half a mile of walking.

+ I went on a full day trip, but I can't remember what the tour was called. Gah! We did canoeing through the mangroves, learning about the insects and birds, etc. and then went out to dive in a sponge field. A lot of them offer free wine during the boat ride, but then you can't dive, if you drink. Keep that in mind.

+ If you have a car, go to The East Martello Museum on South Roosevelt Boulevard. It's a ways away from Duval, on the part of the island where all the beaches are.

And remember, no matter what you plan to do, Key West is only two miles by four miles, so it's all definately "doable."
