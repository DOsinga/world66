Kissimmee sits just south of [Orlando](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/orlando) and serves as the budget-friendly gateway to Walt Disney World, whose main entrance is minutes away. Most visitors come for the theme parks, and the strip along US-192 (Irlo Bronson Memorial Highway) is lined with motels, restaurants, and tourist shops that cater to families looking to save on accommodation.

Beyond the theme park orbit, the old downtown along Broadway has some character — a handful of local restaurants and shops around the lakefront on Lake Tohopekaliga. The lake itself is one of the best bass fishing spots in Florida, and airboat tours through the surrounding wetlands offer a glimpse of alligators, herons, and the flat, marshy landscape that existed here long before the parks arrived.
