Lakeland sits halfway between Tampa and Orlando, a central Florida city that most travelers blow right past on I-4. That is a mistake. The city is home to the largest single-site collection of Frank Lloyd Wright architecture in the world, spread across the campus of Florida Southern College. The twelve structures Wright designed here, collectively called "Child of the Sun," are stunning examples of his organic architecture, and you can walk the campus freely to admire them.

Beyond Wright, Lakeland has a genuinely appealing downtown centered on a chain of small lakes. Lake Mirror, with its promenade and neoclassical balustrade, is the prettiest of the bunch and makes for a lovely evening stroll. The Polk Museum of Art is a small but worthwhile stop, and the surrounding streets have enough independent coffee shops and restaurants to fill a pleasant afternoon.

If you visit between February and March, you will find Detroit Tigers fans flooding the town for spring training at Joker Marchant Stadium. The rest of the year, Lakeland offers a quieter alternative to its flashier neighbors, with significantly lower prices and an unhurried pace that feels increasingly rare in central Florida.
