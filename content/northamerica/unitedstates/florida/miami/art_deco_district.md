title: Art Deco Historic District
type: poi
---

The largest collection of Art Deco architecture in the world stretches along Ocean Drive, Collins Avenue, and Washington Avenue between 5th and 23rd Streets. Over 800 buildings survive from the 1930s and 40s, their pastel facades, porthole windows, racing stripes, and neon signs forming a coherent streetscape unlike anything else in the United States.

The Miami Design Preservation League runs daily guided walking tours from the Art Deco Welcome Center at 1001 Ocean Drive — the 90-minute morning tour is the best introduction. Even without a guide, the district rewards slow walking: look up at the rooflines, notice the eyebrow overhangs designed to shade windows from the tropical sun, and step into hotel lobbies to see original terrazzo floors and bas-relief details.

story: >
  The district was nearly demolished in the 1970s to make way for high-rise development. Barbara Baer Capitman and the Miami Design Preservation League fought a decade-long campaign to save it, achieving National Register listing in 1979 — the first 20th-century district so recognized.
