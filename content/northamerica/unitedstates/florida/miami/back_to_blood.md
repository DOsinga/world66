Wolfe's sprawling 2012 novel attempts to do for Miami what Bonfire of the Vanities did for New York — a panoramic social satire tracking a Cuban-American cop, a Russian oligarch, a Haitian professor, and a status-obsessed WASP journalist through a city where every interaction is refracted through race, money, and immigration. It is uneven but captures the manic energy of modern Miami better than most fiction.
