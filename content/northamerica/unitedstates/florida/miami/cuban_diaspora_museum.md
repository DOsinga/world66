Located in a modernist building just south of the Miami River, this museum tells the story of Cuban immigration to the United States through art, photographs, documents, and personal artifacts. The rotating exhibitions range from contemporary Cuban-American art to historical surveys of the exile experience. It is a more polished and comprehensive counterpart to the Bay of Pigs Museum, and worth visiting for context on how the Cuban community shaped modern Miami.
