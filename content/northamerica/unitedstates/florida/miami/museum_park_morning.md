Museum Park sits on the edge of Biscayne Bay just north of downtown, where two of Miami's most architecturally ambitious buildings face each other across a bayfront plaza. In the morning, before the heat becomes serious, the terrace overlooking the water is one of the best places to sit in the city.

Start at the [Pérez Art Museum Miami (PAMM)](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/miami/perez_art_museum), designed by Herzog & de Meuron, its cantilevered concrete structure draped with hanging gardens of tropical plants. The ground-floor terrace is free — you do not need to buy a ticket to sit here and look at the bay and the skyline behind you. The collection inside focuses on art from the Americas, the Caribbean, and the African diaspora, with rotating exhibitions that are usually strong. Allow 90 minutes if you go in.

Next door, the [Phillip and Patricia Frost Museum of Science](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/miami/frost_science_museum) is worth it almost entirely for the aquarium: a 500,000-gallon Gulf Stream tank that you approach from above, looking down into open water, before descending to view it from below through a massive acrylic lens as sharks and rays glide overhead. The effect is genuinely otherworldly. The planetarium and science centre add more if you have children or more time; the rooftop terrace offers panoramic views of the bay and the city.

Both museums together take most of a morning. The waterfront walk between them, with views back toward the downtown skyline and across to Miami Beach, is free and one of the better urban walks in South Florida.
