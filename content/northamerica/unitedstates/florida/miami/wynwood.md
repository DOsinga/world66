Wynwood's transformation from neglected warehouse district to Miami's most vibrant arts neighbourhood is one of the great urban turnarounds in American cities. It started with the Wynwood Walls — a curated outdoor museum of street art murals on the walls of former warehouses — and has spread outward to cover dozens of blocks with murals, galleries, breweries, restaurants, and shops.

The Walls themselves, at NW 2nd Avenue and 25th Street, are free to visit and feature work by internationally known street artists. Beyond them, nearly every building surface in the neighbourhood carries some form of art. The Rubell Museum on NW 29th Street houses one of the world's largest private contemporary art collections in a converted DEA confiscated-goods warehouse. Wynwood is also home to some of Miami's best casual dining — the neighbourhood has a density of good restaurants that rivals any part of the city.

Saturday afternoon is the best time to visit, when the galleries are open and the streets are busy but not overwhelming.
