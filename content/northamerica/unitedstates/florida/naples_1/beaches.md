---
title: "Beaches in Naples"
type: section
---

Located on the Gulf of Mexico, Naples, Florida provides beachgoers with pristine white sand and warm water 365 days per year. Naples is a popular vacation destination for tourists-offering a plethora of resorts and activities. An occasional tropical storm or hurricane does pass through the Gulf of Mexico, but major storm threats are generally limited to the months of August through late October.

Characterized by thriving coastal wildlife, many beaches are actually designated parks-accessible between 8am and sundown. Vanderbilt Beach is located in northern Naples and offers perfect conditions for sunbathers and walkers. There are hotels, restaurants, and shops in the immediate area. A parking fee of $6 is required in exchange for all day beach parking.

Delnor-Wiggins Pass is a state park and recreation area-located just south of Vanderbilt Beach. The park is separated from the busy streets of Naples by mangrove swamps and creeks. The Cocohatchee River naturally releases into the park’s waterways. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, picnics, and boat activities. The boardwalks allow a glimpse of wild sea oats, cabbage palms, and the animals that depend on the saturated environment-birds, snook fish, sea trout, mollusks and star fish. Loggerhead turtles and manatees, elusive creatures, may be spotted in the shallow waters of the park’s beaches also.

Heading further south will bring you to Lowdermilk Beach Park-providing similar facilities and activity opportunities as the other state parks. Lowdermilk is known for wonderful sunsets and peaceful escapes from the surrounding shopping area. The park offers a playground and volleyball court.

Although it is not a protected park, the Naples Pier is definitely a historical landmark in the town. Having been rebuilt on three separate occasions, due to hurricane damage, the pier has served as an easily accessible and clean beach for the public for years. Restrooms and concession stands are located on the pier.

No matter your beach of choice, be sure to choose a hotel that meets the needs of your situation. A few popular hotels include: La Playa Beach and Gulf Resort, Vanderbilt Inn, Doubletree Guest Suites, Best Western Naples Plaza, and the Hilton. The rates of rooms range in price, but packaged deals are offered throughout the year. Naples is considered a honeymooners' paradise and a fun place for the family-making this quaint town a repeated destination for most visitors.
