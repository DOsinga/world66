---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in St. Augustine"
type: section
---

There are so many vibrant **St. Augustine nightlife **places, you may have trouble deciding where to spend your time in St. Augustine after the sun sets. If you're looking for the memorable nightlife in St. Augustine, at least one of these places is sure to entertain:

**Limelight Theatre**

If you're traveling with family or just appreciate nightlife other than bars and clubs, head for the Limelight Theatre at 11 Old Mission Avenue. A wide variety of stage performances, including drama, mystery, comedy, and children's performances make for a great night out for two or the whole family. Expect performances of classic plays as well as hot up-and-coming new shows. Tickets cost between $10 and $25, depending on your seating choice and age. For the evening shows, with every adult ticket, you will also get one free admission for a child age 10 to 17. There's usually a play at Limelight Theatre for most of the year, with small breaks for a few weeks between shows.

**Mill Top Tavern**

At 19 Saint George Street, you'll find what looks like a water mill from the 19th century. Inside is Mill Top Tavern, a great place to relax and spend the evening enjoying a few beers, glasses of wine, or shots of hard liquor. Food includes sandwiches, soups, nachos and other traditional pub meals and appetizers. Daily live jazz and R&B music will help you relax. If you enjoy the fresh air, you can enjoy your refreshments on a spacious open-air patio with a gorgeous view of the harbor. Expect to spend $4 to $8 on each meal.

**Cellar Upstairs Wine & Jazz Bar
**

Located at the San Sebastian Winery at 157 King Street, the Cellar Upstairs Wine & Jazz Bar offers a more trendy, classic nightclub feel than the typical tavern. Live blues, jazz and R&B music performances are featured whenever the bar is open (Thursday through Sunday). There's no cover charge, and you'll be able to grab a sandwich or split an appetizer for $8 to $14. The highlight of the menu is, of course, the wine made right there at San Sebastian, but there's a variety of beers available, too. Expect to spend $3 to $4 per beer and $5 to $7 per glass of wine.

**Ann O'Malley's Deli & Pub**

If a classic, Irish pub is where you like to relax in the evenings, head to Ann O'Malley's at 23 Orange Street downtown. St. Augustine's oldest authentic Irish pub, the pub makes for a pleasant place to enjoy a few beers, both domestic and imported. Most of the time, sports are on TV, unless there's a trivia game taking place. Expect to spend $3 to $5 for each beer, unless you catch the $1 draft specials. On weekends, you may be able to see a live music act.
