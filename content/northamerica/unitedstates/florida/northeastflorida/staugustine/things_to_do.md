---
title: "Things to Do in St. Augustine"
type: section
---

Make sure to add these things to your **St. Augustine things to do** list when you visit this quaint city in Florida. There are numerous things that you can do in this interesting city. St. Augustine is the oldest European-established city in North America and the evidences is found on the different architectural structures of the buildings. Here are a few things that you can do while vacationing in the city.

**Visit Castillo de San Marcos**

To get a good idea on how St. Augustine was established, you need to learn the history of the city and what better way for you to do it than to visit the scenic fort of Castillo de San Marcos. This fort is considered to be the largest and the oldest fort in the United States. It was built by Spanish settlers to protect themselves from the uprising natives. Be sure to visit the Castillo de San Marcos on Saturdays since they usually have live cannon-firing on the grounds of the fort.

**Shop for Artwork **

You can shop for delicate pieces of artwork made by local and national artists every first Friday of the month in downtown St. Augustine. However, if you are not into shopping for artwork, then you will enjoy shopping for clothes and other antiques in the open air stalls on San Marco Avenue every Saturday. The many shopping places in St. Augustine make it a perfect haven for shoppers and art connoisseurs alike.

**Entertainment the Spanish Way**

If you are looking for a great place to be entertained, then head off to Taberna del Gallo for a Spanish-inspired tavern experience. This tavern comes complete with old Spanish interiors, Spanish beer and crew dressed up in old Spanish garb. This is a great way to meet and mingle with the locals. You are sure to enjoy your experience here at the tavern.

**Stroll the Beach or Walk in the Park**

If you want to spend your day in a calm and relaxing way, then go to the Washington Oaks State Park. This is a huge state park comprised of a botanical garden connected to a wide coastline facing the Pacific Ocean. You can stroll along the beach or head off to the east side of the state park and climb the rocky formations on the shore. Bring along your fishing rod because you can spend your entire afternoon fishing on the beach.
