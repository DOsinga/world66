---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in St. Augustine"
type: section
---

Although you probably won't be able to do it all, including the top five **St. Augustine must do's **on your travel itinerary will make sure you have a memorable trip to Florida's First Coast. If you only have time for five major activities during your stay in St. Augustine, try these:

**Feed the Gators at the Alligator Farm and Zoological Park**

A trip to Florida without seeing an alligator up close is a lacking trip indeed. Kids and adults alike will marvel at getting so close to these prehistoric creatures. Because the Alligator Farm is also associated with the Zoological park, you'll also have the opportunity to see other animals, such as monkeys, birds and other reptiles. You can actually help feed the alligators from afar at supervised locations at around 3:00 each day. Adults cost $22 and children ages three to eleven are $11, with discounts for seniors, military, the disabled, AAA members and large groups.

**Visit Anastasia Island**

Take the 1545-foot Bridge of Lions off the coast of St. Augustine to Anastasia Island, which is famous for its extensive span of beaches as well as its more than 150 restaurants at which to relax and dine. You can spend a day at the beach and then grab something to eat before heading to the mainland or you can spend time at the 1700-acre Anastasia State Park, where you can go camping or take walks on nature trails.

**Tour the Castillo de San Marcos**

One of St. Augustine's national monuments, the Castillo de San Marcos, is continental America's largest (and oldest) fort made entirely of stone. The fort took 23 years to build and was completed in 1695 on the orders of the Spanish queen. You can take a daily tour offered by a ranger and if you're there on Saturday, you may be lucky enough to catch a firing of the fort's cannons.

**Stop by the Fountain of Youth**

While no one in modern times will be guaranteeing that you'll suddenly drop a few years' worth of wrinkles or gray hair by stopping by, the legendary Fountain of Youth is located in St. Augustine at Fountain of Youth National Archaeological park. It was famed Spanish explorer Ponce de Leon who claimed that Florida was the location of a fountain that made aging work in reverse and St. Augustine is the reported location where de Leon first landed in Florida. (There is an actual fountain there from which you can drink, although de Leon never found his.)

**Buy Local Treasures at the Old City Farmers' Market**

Every Saturday at the St. Augustine Amphitheater is the Old City Farmers' Market, where local farmers and artisans bring fresh food and homemade crafts to sell. You can also try some pastries or bring home a plant to remind you of your Florida experience.
