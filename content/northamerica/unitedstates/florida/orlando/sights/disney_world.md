If you have never visited the Disney World theme park in Orlando Florida, it is easy to become overwhelmed by the sheer volume of sights, attractions and must-do activities. The following guide will put you on the right track.

**Multiple Parks**

The number one thing that many people are surprised to find out is that Disney World is not just one park. Multiple Disney theme parks comprise the entity commonly known as “Disney World.” A brief rundown of the Disney parks follows.

**Magic Kingdom **

Disney’s flagship park and home to all the classics. This is a great start and the best park for younger children.

**Epcot Center **

Twice the size of Magic Kingdom, this park focuses on attractions and entertainment related to technology, innovation and wonder.

**Animal Kingdom **

Home to over 1,700 animals from 250 species and sprawling across 500 acres of lush landscape, Disney's Animal Kingdom is focused on nature and conservation including animal care, education and research.

**Typhoon Lagoon **

This Disney water park features wet attractions for the whole family. The fun includes a surf pool, children’s area with raft rides and a snorkeling reef with real sharks.

**Blizzard Beach **

Disney’s other water park features one of the tallest and fastest free-falling waterslides in existence, as well as slides and rides for the everyone—like a child-sized, snow-castle fountain play area with smaller versions of the more thrilling grownup attractions.

**Disney’s Hollywood Studios **

This Disney theme park is dedicated to everything showbiz. Hollywood thrills and star-studded entertainment is sure to delight kids of all ages.

Deciding how much to squeeze in is a matter of personal choice and length of stay. If your visit to Orlando is less than 3 days, for example, you will want to prioritize which of the Disney parks you want to visit. On the other hand, if you plan on being in Florida for two weeks, you can easily visit all the parks and then some.
**
Tips**

Plan your itinerary ahead of time. Check the park schedules and make sure the parks you want to visit are open during your stay. Look into park-hopper options if you plan to visit more than one park. Review the park maps online and make a list of rides and attractions that are top priority for you.Bring plenty of sunscreen, wear a hat and bottles of water. Have fun and take lots of pictures!
