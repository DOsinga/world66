Gatorland started out as one of those little roadside
attractions in 1949 on the side of a road in Florida. Today it is a 110
acre theme park known as the Alligator Capital of the World.

**Location**

Gatorland is located between Orlando and Kissimmee on Orange Blossom
Trail and boasts being one of central Florida's oldest attractions. It
was founded by Owen Godwin and is still a family owned enterprise
today. Access Gatorland from Highway 441, only 20 minutes away from
Disneyworld. It is open every day from 9 am to 6 pm. Show times vary
during season but are repeated throughout the day so you won't miss
anything.

**Attractions**

Visitors enter the park through an interesting gate that looks like
the jaws of an Alligator. Once inside the park they will find many
different attractions.

In the Gator Jumparoo Show alligators and crocodiles jump out of the
water to grab food from out of a trainer's hand. Some can jump as high
as 5 feet. There is also a gator wrestling show that takes place in the
800 seat stadium. Six to Eight foot alligators are taken by human
wrestlers who jump on their backs then explain what they are doing to
the audience.

Alligators and Crocodiles aren't the only animals featured at this
park. In the Up Close Encounters Show many different creatures are
exhibited and explained including dangerous snakes. In the park there
is a petting zoo called Alley's Barnyard and watch while you are
walking on the paths of the park because you may encounter some of
these fuzzy and pleasant animals on your way.

Gatorland is also the home of the Very Merry Aviary where Lorikeets,
bright colored parrots, live. They eat only pollen, nectar and fruit.
Here you can carry a small cup of nectar and actually feed the birds
during their feeding time.

There is a train station that houses the Gatorland Express that
carries passengers all over the park through the Breeding Marsh and
Bird Rookery to the Wrestling Stadium. At the station there is an
opportunity for children and adults alike to get their faces painted
with a simple motif or some rather ornate paintings.

If it gets too hot take a dip in the Gator Gully Splash Park where
you can play target practice with large water guns or stand under a
waterfall coming from a giant egret's mouth.

**Admission**

You can spend the whole day at the park as there are also
restaurant, food stands, and other facilities. Prices for those age 13
and up is $22.99, Ages 3 to 12 $14.99 and under 3 is free. You can
usually find discount coupons almost everywhere in Florida.
