The Kennedy Space Center was built in 1962 and renamed in 1963 to
honor the president who helped put the first man on the moon. This is where all the
United States Spacecrafts are launched into space.

**Things to Do**

Kennedy Space Center is located in Orlando and is a great place to
visit while in the area. Tour NASA's launch sites, meet an astronaut,
try out the spaceflight simulators, and even be witness to the launch
of a spacecraft. The center is built on a wildlife park. You will
also have an opportunity to see the area by tour bus. Touring the
center is an all day experience.

**Hours and Tickets**

Hours are 9 am to 5:30 pm and tours begin at 10am and depart every
15 minutes. Each tour is about 2 hours. The Center is open every day
except December 25 (Christmas) and during certain launch dates. General Admission
to the Visitor Complex is $38 for adults, $28 for children age 3 to 11
plus tax. This entitles you to tour the space center, see IMAX space
films and see all the other exhibits and shows including the US
astronaut Hall of Fame with the spaceflight simulators. These tickets
are good for 2 days admission.

**Tours**

There are other tours that cost extra. They include the NASA Up
close tour where you tour launch areas with a guide, see shuttle launch
pads and landing facility which costs $21 adults and $15 per child plus
tax. The Cape Canaveral: Then and Now tour which takes you to the first
launch sites from the 60's and a visit to the Air Force Space and
Missile Museum. You will also see the active unmanned rocket program.
This tour requires a Photo ID for guests aged 12 and older. Costs for
this tour are $21 and $15 plus tax. Lunch with an Astronaut tour
includes a delicious lunch with a live presentation from a veteran NASA
astronaut. Prices are $22.99 adults and $15.99 for children 3 to 11
plus tax.

**Dining Options **

There are many different dining experiences available in the complex
including the Orbit Cafe, Moon Rock Cafe, G-Force Grill, Space Dots ice
cream of the future, and Milky Way soft drinks and ice cream. Expect to
pay anywhere from $8 to $20 for a meal. There are some cheaper food
kiosks in the complex as well where you can get hot dogs and such for a
much cheaper price.

The Kennedy Space Center is a must do for anyone interested in Space Travel or the United States Space program.
