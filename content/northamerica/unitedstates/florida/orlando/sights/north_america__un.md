One of Orlando, Florida’s top theme park attractions is SeaWorld. There are a few other SeaWorld locations but the adventure park in Orlando is the best and most popular. In fact it is one of the top ten most visited amusement parks in the Unites States with around 6 million visitors every year. With the variety of shows, rides, restaurants and exhibits here everyone in your group can have a great time here.

**Shows******

There are many exciting shows to see at SeaWorld. There are so many land and sea shows here you could spend the entire day being entertained while sitting in a comfortable seat. The real Shamu lives here in Orlando and can be seen in SeaWorld shows. The current shows offered at SeaWorld are: Pets Ahoy, Reflections, Blue Horizons Sea Lions Tonight, Believe, Pirate Island, Shamu Rocks, Elmo and Abby’s Treasure Hunt and A’Lure the Call of the Ocean.**
**

**Rides**

SeaWorld has more than aquatic life exhibits and entertaining shows. There are many thrilling rides here and some that will have you screaming with delight. The water coaster is called Journey to Atlantis. The scenery is like a storybook and the thrilling waterfalls might leave you wet. Kraken will have you careening along the twisted track of this intense and modern open air designed roller coaster (height requirement of 54 in.). Wild Arctic is a virtual reality ride, great for those with limited mobility (height requirements of 54 in.). Shamu’s Happy Harbor is a gentle amusement designed for children. This area of SeaWorld is really special and has a user friendly layout. SkyTower is not a parachute drop but a mellow 6 minute trip that shows you a bird’s eye-view of the surrounding area. The newest thrill ride is the Manta.

**Directions and Information**

SeaWorld has shuttle busses that will conveniently provide round trip service from your hotel for a reasonable price. If you drive parking will cost $12. There are more than 6,500 spaces so it should not be difficult to find a spot. SeaWorld is located at the intersection of I-4 and FL 528, the Bee Line Expy.

On your way into the SeaWorld the security procedure is thorough. Your bag is likely to be searched so don’t bring your pen knife or anything made of glass. Soft coolers are not allowed.

The park is open from 9 a.m. to 9 or 10 p.m. every day of the year. Their website provides meticulously detailed information on accessibility, height requirements and other regulations regarding, among other things, strollers.
