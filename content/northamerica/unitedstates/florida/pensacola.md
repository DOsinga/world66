Pensacola sits on the western tip of the Florida Panhandle, closer in feel to the Gulf Coast South than to the theme-park Florida further down the peninsula. The city has a long history — it claims to be the oldest European settlement in the United States, founded by the Spanish in 1559 — and the compact historic downtown, with its tree-lined streets and mix of colonial, Victorian, and Creole architecture, is genuinely charming to walk or cycle through.

The main draw for most visitors is [Pensacola Beach](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/pensacola/pensacola_beach), a barrier island with white quartz sand and clear emerald water that ranks among the finest beaches in the country. The Naval Air Station, home of the Blue Angels flight demonstration team, is the other big attraction — the National Naval Aviation Museum on the base is free and worth a visit even if you are not a military enthusiast.

Pensacola has a lively arts scene, with the Great Gulfcoast Arts Festival in the first week of November, regular concerts at the civic centre, and fireworks over the bay on the Fourth of July. The downtown is always decorated for Christmas.
