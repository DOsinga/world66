Sarasota sits on Florida's Gulf Coast about an hour south of Tampa, and it punches well above its weight for a city of around 60,000. It has something rare for a Florida beach town: an actual cultural life. The Ringling Museum — John Ringling of circus fame built his winter estate here — is one of the best art museums in the American South. The Asolo Repertory Theatre has been staging professional productions since the 1960s. There's a real downtown with independent restaurants and bars, not just chain hotels and souvenir shops.

The beaches are the draw for most visitors, and they deserve the reputation. Siesta Key, just minutes from downtown, consistently ranks among the finest beaches in the country — the sand is almost pure white quartz, stays cool underfoot even in July, and the Gulf water is calm and warm. Lido Key is less crowded and slightly more local.

Downtown Sarasota is compact and walkable along Main Street and Palm Avenue. St. Armands Circle on Lido Key is a touristy but pleasant shopping and dining loop. The waterfront area around Marina Jack has good views of Sarasota Bay.

Summer is hot, humid, and prone to afternoon thunderstorms — but also when prices drop sharply. Winter is the high season: snowbirds arrive, the cultural calendar fills up, and the weather is reliably beautiful. Spring is the sweet spot.
