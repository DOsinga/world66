Just 35 miles east of [Orlando](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/orlando), Florida's Space Coast offers 72 miles of Atlantic beaches backed by one of the country's most dramatic natural and industrial landscapes. [Canaveral National Seashore](/northamerica/unitedstates/florida/spacecoastarea/canaveralnationalseashore) protects miles of undeveloped barrier island — scrub, lagoon, and open ocean — while Kennedy Space Center sits just to the south, where rockets still launch regularly from the same pads that sent Apollo astronauts to the moon.

Cocoa Beach is the main hub: a laid-back beach town with the famous Ron Jon Surf Shop, a pier, and a string of seafood restaurants. Port Canaveral handles cruise ships and has a growing cluster of bars and restaurants near the docks. Inland, the Indian River Lagoon is one of the most biodiverse estuaries in North America — home to manatees, dolphins, and hundreds of bird species.

The area rewards travelers who don't just treat it as a day trip from Orlando. A couple of nights gives you time to catch a launch, explore the seashore, and get out on the water.
