---
title: "Bars and Cafes in St. Augustine"
type: section
---

The **St. Augustine Bars and Cafes** that you have to choose from while on vacation include jazz clubs and Irish pubs. St. Augustine&rsquo;s nightlife isn&rsquo;t as wild as other Florida cities but you still have a wide variety to choose from in terms of bars and cafes.

**Anne O&rsquo;Malley&rsquo;s Irish Pub
**Anne O&rsquo;Malley&rsquo;s claim to fame is that is the oldest authentic Irish Pub in St. Augustine. Whether you are looking for a cold pint of beer to enjoy on a hot summer day or a sandwich for lunch Ann O&rsquo;Malley&rsquo;s is the place to be. Every night of the week offers something special, in addition to the menu. Tuesday&rsquo;s is open mic night, while Wednesdays you can find local talent on stage.

**Caf&eacute; Eleven
**If you are looking for some good old Southern comfort meals Caf&eacute; Eleven is the place to go. Not only do they have some of the best food in town they also stock some of the hardest to find bottled beers, but they also keep a variety of import beers on draft. Caf&eacute; Eleven is not only about the food and drink it is also a great place to go if you are looking for great music. Monday nights are now open mic nights, so you can find a variety of local talent. They also have live bands come in to perform throughout each month.

**Sunset Grille
**The Sunset Grille offers both indoor and outdoor dining, along with a full bar. Located right across from the beach this is the place to go for the best chowder in town. If chowder isn&rsquo;t to your liking you can try out one of the various seafood entr&eacute;es that is always served fresh.

**Harry&rsquo;s Seafood Bar & Grille
**Harry&rsquo;s brings a bit of New Orleans into St. Augustine. From the d&eacute;cor to the food that is served it is easy to forget that you are in Florida rather than New Orleans&rsquo;s enjoying Mardi Gras. While at Harry&rsquo;s start your meal off with some Voo Doo Shrimp and then move onto some Bourbon Street Salmon.

**Mill Top Tavern
**The Mill Top Tavern is located inside of an old mill, a 19th century one to be exact. The tavern is located upstairs, which allows you to enjoy the open-air seating provided on the deck. Here you can enjoy some Ultimate Nachos, a cold beer and a shot or two while listening to live music.
