---
title: "Day Trips in St. Augustine"
type: section
---

**St. Augustine day trips** are a great way to supplement a wonderful vacation in this Florida city. Although there is much to see and explore in St. Augustine, the area surrounding the city in north Florida is interesting to discover as well. The roads leading to the various destinations are relatively easy to maneuver and drive around. Public transport is also a good option, and leads to most of the towns and cities surrounding St. Augustine.

**Jacksonville******

The city of Jacksonville is a mere 30 minute drive away from St. Augustine, and is a well known city in the world for its cityscape and the warm weather. Drive around the city of Jacksonville, along the St. John&rsquo;s river, where most of the festivals and events are held. Walk along the various shops that are nestled among the urban jungle of skyscrapers, and find some of the wonderful little eats and buys that the city has to offer. Those with a little more time can take a short drive and explore the neighborhood of Riverside, a historic neighborhood that exudes a bohemian flavor with its shops and caf&eacute;s, alongside old houses that can be found in the area.

**Palm**** Coast**

Palm Coast is accessible from St. Augustine via boat – the Intracoastal Waterway serves the route between Palm Coast and the city. Avoid driving to this spot, since it can be very crowded. The highways leading here are extremely inconvenient to navigate, requiring nearly an hour to reach this place, which is a mere 20 miles from St. Augustine. The Palm Coast is mainly a beach and a nature walk. The former is a great place to spend the afternoon, particularly for visitors bringing along families or children. The latter is a good way to escape the heat of the afternoons, with the shade of the trees blocking out most of the sun and allowing for a slight reprieve from the sun (while still being an interesting experience). Be sure to try kayaking here as well, and there are a few kayak companies that rent out kayaks for this purpose.

**Marineland**

Touted as the world&rsquo;s first oceanarium, Marineland is one of Florida&rsquo;s oldest marine parks, and is located just south of St. Augustine. The admission price of the park is one dollar, something that has been maintained since the opening of the park in 1938. The main feature of Marineland is the dolphins, visible in the large ocean tanks in the park. The shows are also a good way to watch the animals in action, while entertaining the crowd, particularly the younger ones. As of 2006, the park has been newly renovated after being destroyed by Hurricanes Floyd and Irene, and it boasts some of the newest infrastructure in the business.
