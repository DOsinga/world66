---
title: "Eating Out in St. Augustine"
type: section
---

Eating out in St. Augustine can be an exciting experience with the variety of the local cuisine and **St. Augustine** **restaurants**. The experience of St. Augustine in terms of travel, sightseeing and meeting new people can be amazing, and so can be the food. Here are some St. Augustine Eating Out options.

**O'Steens**

Found on Anastasia Boulevard, this restaurant is a typical local diner and the regular winner of Best Seafood Restaurant in St. Augustine. The restaurant is a family setting, with booths and bar seats for families to dine out in. It is the perfect place for tourists with children, and with the wonderful food, it is the perfect place for any tourist to simply drop by and have a casual meal. The shrimp here is the highlight, although the Cajun fish is delicious as well. Be sure to try out the fried shrimp dinner – deep fried, battered shrimp served on a salad platter. Prices here are pretty reasonable, ranging from about $7 to $10 a head.

**Conch House**

Conch House is not strictly located in St. Augustine, but about one mile outside the town area – amounting to about a 5-minute drive out of the city. The Conch House is a themed restaurant serving up some of the best food that is representative of the culture of St. Augustine: spicy, varied and textured. The Conch House serves Caribbean cuisine, complete with all the sauces and salsas. The feature here is the conch chowder, from which the restaurant gains its name. Those who arrive late and find dinner too heavy a meal can stop by the place for some finger food and some of the most amazing tropical drinks – alcoholic and non-alcoholic mixers, a great drink to have on a warm summer&rsquo;s night on the deck that reaches out over the water.

**Fiddler&rsquo;s Green**

With one of the best ambiences in St. Augustine, the Fiddler&rsquo;s Green restaurant is located on Vilano Beach, and has been St. Augustine&rsquo;s Best Oceanfront Restaurant for the past 20 years. The restaurant is tastefully designed, and serves a fusion of American and Italian cuisine – rich pasta sauces go along with some very fresh local seafood and starters of corn fritters. The food here is delicate and delicious, creating a good mix of flavors that complement the ocean view just outside every room. The place is designed in a manner that allows for all diners to take a look out of the window and see the ocean. Prices can be a little steep, from the mid-20s per head for a decent meal, but the food and atmosphere make everything worth its while.
