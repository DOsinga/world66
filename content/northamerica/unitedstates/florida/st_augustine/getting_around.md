**Finding the Best Transport Options in St. Augustine**

While on vacation in **St. Augustine, transportation** methods make it easy for all people to get around town and enjoy their vacation. It is a small area, and finding your way around the city is relatively easy.

#### **Walking**

Walking is the primary method of transportation in St. Augustine mainly because of how close all of the attractions are to each other. Not only can you walk to majority of the tourist attractions, you can also easily walk to any local restaurants or shopping areas. The only places that you cannot reach by foot in St. Augustine are the attractions located on the outskirts of the city, such as the Alligator Farm or the beaches.

#### **Car**

If you have driven yourself to St. Augustine or you are planning to rent a car while you are in town, you will need to be prepared for driving in the city. The one thing to watch out for is all of the one-way streets in St. Augustine, but they are easy enough to follow because they adhere a grid pattern. Parking might be a problem, because it is so limited. Be prepared to walk in addition to driving.

#### **Carriage Ride**

The streets of St. Augustine were originally made for horse-drawn carts. To help keep this tradition alive, you can take a carriage ride around town, which is not only relaxing but can also be very romantic. In 2010, carriage rides are $18.00 for adults and $8.00 for kids.

#### **Public Transportation**

The main method of public transportation is the Sunshine Bus Company, which offers transportation to all of the beaches around town and to the majority of the tourist attractions. However, the buses only operate during certain hours and do not run on Sundays, so you might need to make other arrangements. In 2010, one-way trips for adults are $1.00, while an all-day pass is $3.00. Kids and seniors will pay $0.50 for one-way trips and $1.50 for all-day passes.

#### **Trolley**

Adults and kids can ride the trolley for three straight days. at a cost of $21 for adults and $8 for kids. The tickets also include admission to the St. Augustine History Museum. Visitors also get to use the Beach Bus Shuttle to travel to the beaches or to the Alligator Farm free of charge.
