St. Augusine is a historic and beautiful seaport located in St. John's county, Florida. Understanding some **St. Augustine Practical Information** can help you make the most of your next visit to the oldest continuously occupied settlement in America.

**History**

With just over 10 square miles to its name, the historical story of this city seems larger than the city itself. The area was first explored in 1513 and originally claimed by the Spaniards. More than 200 years later, the French gained control of the port city after the French and Indian war. A mere 20 years later the Spanish would regain control of the city by virtue of the Treaty of Paris. It was not until 1845 that Florida and thus St. Augustine itself, became an American State and controlled property. Because of the changing ownership, you can see many architectural influences and historical landmarks around the city. The region also played a major role in the American Civil Rights movement.

**Getting Around**

I-95 is the major interstate servicing St. Augustine. Note that it does not take you through the historical downtown. To do this, exit onto SR 16. Other major roadways include SR A1A and SR 207.

The Sunshine bus company also has service through the city.

The nearest major airport is Jacksonville, but there is a smaller airport with 5 runways within the city. It is used only for private flights and seaplanes. **
**

**Climate**

Located in northern Florida, St. Augustine enjoys a typical subtropical Florida climate. Summers are hot and dry and winter months are largely just cool. Hurricanes here are rare and a direct hit has not been observed since the mid 1960's.

**Dining, Lodging**

As with most American cities, there is an abundance of options for dining and lodging. You'll find lodging in all price brackets easily. Make sure to take in a traditional Spanish meal while in town as the influence is still strongly felt.

**See and Do**

Some of the major tourist attractions in St. Augustine include the lighthouse, Florida's oldest house (located on Charlotte St.) and the Alligator Farm. Other points of interst include the Fountain of Youth located in an Archaeological park. The city has a vivid event calendar including holiday parades and other festivals including the Rhythm and Ribs festival, the Gamble Rodgers Folk Music Festival, Flagler College New Years' Eve Ball and the Kingbuster Kingfish fishing tournament. Make sure to check before your visit to see if there are any festivals planned during your visit.
