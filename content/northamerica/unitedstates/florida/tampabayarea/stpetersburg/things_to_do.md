---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

**Salvador Dalí Museum** (1 Dalí Blvd) — the definitive Dalí collection outside Spain: 96 oils, over 100 watercolours and drawings, and thousands of other works in a purpose-built building with a striking glass atrium. Worth the admission.

**Museum of Fine Arts** (255 Beach Drive NE) — a strong permanent collection covering American, European, and Pre-Columbian art, plus photography. Well-curated and not overcrowded. Open Tues–Sat 10am–5pm, Sun 1–5pm.

**Florida Holocaust Museum** (55 5th Street South) — the fourth largest Holocaust museum in the US. A serious and important institution; plan at least two hours. Tel: 727-820-0100.

**St. Petersburg Museum of History** (335 2nd Ave NE) — 10,000 years of local history including the Smithsonian's first-ever replica of a Benoist airboat (the first commercial airline flight departed from St. Pete in 1914).

**Fort DeSoto Park** — barrier island county park 10 miles south, consistently rated among the best beaches in the US. Rent a canoe or kayak to explore the mangrove-lined waterways. Excellent bird-watching on the north beach during spring and autumn migration.
