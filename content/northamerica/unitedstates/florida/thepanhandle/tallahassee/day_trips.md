**Apalachicola National Forest**

Tallahasse is actually right next to the largest forest in Florida, so why not take a day trip out of the city and discover what nature has to offer. There is no entrance fee into this large park; the only thing you have to pay for are fees for certain activities such as camping, which costs $10 per night plus $5 for electrical hookups.

Water recreation and historical sites are the main sources of entertainment here; you can swim, boat, fish, have a picnic or BBQ, and camp. You can also see the historical Fort Gadsden or the Langston House. Even more, there are some stunning geological areas such as Leon Sinks, which features incredible sinkholes and caverns.

**Panama City Beach**

Take a Greyhound bus from Tallahassee for $20 or simply drive (FL-57, veering left on I-10 West until exit 130) to Panama City beach for the day. The attractions are endless. Take a glass bottom boat tour, sail through the Gulf of Mexico, scuba dive, skimboard, swim, parasail, or surf. This part of the Gulf has some amazing reefs, both natural and artificial, that can be explored while snorkeling. Panama City is also the "Wreck Capital of the South", and you can visit many ship wrecks while scuba diving. If you want an adrenaline rush you can check out the slides and rides at Shipwreck Island Waterpark for even more watery fun.

For time out of the water, you can see Ripley's Believe It or Not or find your way through the Grand Maze at Coconut Creek. For wine lovers, the Seabreeze Winery is an excellent choice to unwind and end your day.

**Okefenokee National Wildlife Refuge**

Filled with forests of Cypress, wide lakes, deep marshes, and tiny islands, Okefenokee will give you a taste of the wild. A variety of tours, both in boats and walking, are available throughout the refuge. Board an almost dangerously low boat and travel though the silent swamps with alligators at every turn. Walk along the viewing platforms and head up to the observation towers for a better look at the diverse wildlife, plants, and trees in the area. You can also rent a canoe for the day, and travel through the marshy areas.

The Visitor's Center is very nice, with interactive displays and informational materials. You can even pay a visit to "Old Roy", a stuffed alligator that once lived in Okefenokee Swamp.

Driving is the best way to get there (FL-10 East until exit 303, veer left onto FL-47 and cross into Georgia). The trip takes 2 hours.
