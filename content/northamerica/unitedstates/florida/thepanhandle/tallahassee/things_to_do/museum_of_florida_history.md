A free state museum in the R.A. Gray Building, two blocks from the capitol. The permanent collection covers Florida from the Paleo-Indian period through the 20th century, with a particular emphasis on the Spanish colonial era and the Civil War. The centerpiece is a full mastodon skeleton excavated from a nearby lake. Smaller and less crowded than most state history museums, and genuinely informative about a state whose pre-resort history is often overlooked.
