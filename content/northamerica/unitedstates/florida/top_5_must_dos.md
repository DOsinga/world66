---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Florida"
type: section
---

Florida is a terrific place to visit most any time of year, and there are a few **Florida Must Do's** tourists should really try to plan into their vacation.

**Scenic Coastal Drive**

Like road trips full of beautiful scenery, fun places to stop and all the way seeing beautiful beaches and the ocean? Then make the drive from US 1 to Key West when visiting Florida. Drive over several bridges of clear blue water, see countless tiny islands and fishing villages along the way. Bring your camera, because along this 126 mile trip, you won't want to miss any of what it has to offer.

**Universal Orlando Resort**

Like its sister attraction in Southern California, at Universal Orlando you can "ride the movies", as they say. The theme park offers a few different areas of attraction: Hollywood, World Expo, Production Central and KidZone. It's also connected to another cool theme park called Islands of Adventure, comprised of six differently themed islands including Marvel Super Hero Island, The Lost Continent, Jurassic Park, Toon Lagoon and Seuss Landing.

**SeaWorld Orlando, Discovery Cove and Aquatica Waterpark**

This is a hot spot for families and individuals to visit when vacationing in Florida. SeaWorld Orlando actually connects to both Discovery Cove and Aquatica Waterpark for an entire day of marine themed fun. SeaWorld is famous for its marine mammal displays and shows; in Discovery Park you can actually swim and interact with marine mammals in a controlled setting, most likely dolphins; and Aquatica Waterpark has a myriad of water slides, rafting and other water based rides that will keep you cool while having fun.

**Cocoa Beach**

Cocoa Beach is about an hour's drive from Orlando and close to many exciting attractions. But, the beach is an attraction unto itself. Famous for having some of the best surf in all of Florida, and being one of the best priced vacation spots for families or individuals, how could you go wrong by visiting there. Swim, parasail, go deep sea fishing, walk the white sandy beach and have fun in the sun!

**Gatorland**

How could anyone resist visiting the alligator capital of the world? Whether you're a fan of this kind of reptile or not, this is a must visit attraction. Wander through a whopping 110 acres of this theme park and habitat for these intriguing creatures, and learn about their history. See man interact with them in wrestling matches, but most of all enjoy!
