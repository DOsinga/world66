Dedicated to the Albany Movement of 1961–62, one of the earliest mass campaigns of the Civil Rights era. Local activists, Black church leaders, and SNCC organizers mounted months of protests, marches, and jail-ins to challenge segregation across the city — a campaign that drew Martin Luther King Jr. but ended without clear legal victories. The institute's exhibits are honest about that complexity: Albany was a hard lesson in what it takes to force structural change, and those lessons shaped the strategy of later campaigns in Birmingham and beyond. Worth an hour if you have any interest in the movement's history.
