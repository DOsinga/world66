Americus is a small city in southwest Georgia, about 130 miles south of Atlanta. It's best known as the nearest town to [Andersonville](/northamerica/unitedstates/georgia/andersonville) National Historic Site, about 10 miles north.

The historic district has a handsome collection of restored antebellum and Victorian buildings, and the restored Windsor Hotel (1892) is a notable landmark. Habitat for Humanity was founded in Americus in 1976, and the organization's global village and Discovery Center is open to visitors. Jimmy Carter, the 39th president, grew up nearby in Plains — about 10 miles west — and the Jimmy Carter National Historical Park there covers his boyhood farm, the old railroad depot that served as his 1976 campaign headquarters, and the current family home.
