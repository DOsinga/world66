---
title: "Things to Do in Atlanta"
type: section
---

Atlanta has an impressive range of things to do for a Southern city. The cluster of attractions around Centennial Olympic Park — the Georgia Aquarium, World of Coca-Cola, and the National Center for Civil and Human Rights — can fill an entire day. The High Museum of Art in Midtown is the best art museum in the Southeast. Zoo Atlanta, in the historic Grant Park neighborhood south of downtown, is home to giant pandas.

For history, the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site in the Sweet Auburn neighborhood preserves his birth home and the church where he preached. The Jimmy Carter Presidential Library and Museum is nearby. Stone Mountain, about 16 miles east of the city, is a dramatic geological spectacle and a popular day trip.

The Atlanta Botanical Garden in Midtown is worth visiting, especially in spring. Little Five Points and the Virginia-Highland neighborhoods offer a different side of the city — independent shops, vintage stores, and a lived-in neighborhood feel quite different from the corporate downtown.
