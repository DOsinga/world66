A huge granite dome rising 825 feet above the surrounding plain, about 16 miles east of Atlanta. The mountain itself is remarkable — the largest exposed granite outcrop in the world — though the confederate memorial carving on its north face is controversial. Hiking trails to the summit take about an hour. The park also runs a cable car, a laser show in summer evenings, and various family attractions. Worth the trip for the geology alone.
