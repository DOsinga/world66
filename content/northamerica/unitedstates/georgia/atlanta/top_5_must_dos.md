---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Atlanta"
type: section
---

**Georgia Aquarium**

The Georgia Aquarium is the largest aquarium in the world with 8 million gallons of marine life. Don't miss the preserved giant squid. It's a truly awe inspiring site. The Georgia Aquarium is great for all travelers. Admission cost $35 for adults. The Aquarium is normally open 10:00 am to 5:00 pm, but there are extended summer hours. Plan to be here 2 to 4 hours. Lines to enter the aquarium can become annoyingly long and it speeds things up if you've already bought tickets ahead of time. It does get extremely crowded. The best time to visit is during weekdays. If you're traveling with small children stick to umbrella strollers to save yourself some stress when navigating through crowds. Avoid Friday and weekends at all costs!

**Atlanta Botanical Garden**

The Atlanta Botanical Garden transports you to a different world as you explore 15 acres of natural beauty. This attraction is ideal for couples, travelers with a love of gardening or photography, and families. There's even a children's garden complete with themed areas and a three-story treehouse. The admission for adults is $15. Opening time is 9:00 am and closing times vary. If it's summer, go in the morning or evening to avoid the heat.

**Imagine It!**

This is the Children's Museum of Atlanta. Although, it's best suited for kids, parents will have a blast just helping and watching their children. There's no such thing as "don't touch" in this attraction. Children are encouraged to interact with all exhibits. The Fundamentally Food exhibit lets kids experience the journey of food from a farm to the diner table. There's even an exhibit for toddlers called Crawl Space. Admission is $12.50 for those 2 and older. Operating hours are 10:00 am to 4:00 pm. Go early and plan to spend about 3 hours.

**Six Flags Over Georgia**

Think roller coasters! Six Flags is the theme park mecca for exciting roller coasters. Although, if you have children or aren't a fan of the thrill rides there are plenty of family rides and children areas. This is a destination for families, couples, and thrill-seeking travelers. Tickets cost about $40 and a full day should be spent here. Operating times and days vary. Weekdays during the summer have the lightest crowds.

**High Museum of Art**

The High Museum of Art is a hidden gem in the world of art museums. For $18 you can explore some of the most unique works of art. The contemporary collection will boggle your mind. You can easily spend several hours here. Save some money and visit after 4:00 pm on Thursdays as admission is half-priced then. This attraction is geared towards adult travelers who love art.
