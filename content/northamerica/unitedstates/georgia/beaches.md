Introduction

As the southernmost barrier island in the Georgia Sea Island, Cumberland
beach offers no shortage of activities, ranging from sunbathing to animal
tracking. Avoid the crowd’s of Martha’s Vineyard and Provincetown and spend your vacation here! Approximately
18 miles long and 3 miles wide, this white sandy beach stretches the entire
length of [Georgia’s](/northamerica/unitedstates/georgia)
eastern shore, coupled with sand dunes, dense costal vegetation, live oak
forests, wild animals, romantic hotels, quaint eateries, and historical
estates.

Rated as one of the top ten beaches of the US, this island
has many natural treasures, such as fish, ducks, fiddler crabs, oysters, wading
birds, herons, egrets, ibis, wood storks and oyster catchers.
You will also find an abundance of natural wildlife on your walk through
this island, including deer, wild turkeys, feral hogs, rabbits, raccoons,
alligators and more.

Self-Directed
Activities

Hiking, birding, shelling, camping, shark tooth hunting,
wildlife watching, relaxing, strolling through salt marches, sunbathing,
swimming, and photography are just a few activities you can do alone or with
family.

Birding: The best places to bird are along Grand Avenue (near
Dungeness gardens), on the River Trail (2 ½ mile hike each way), and between
the Sea Camp dock and beach area.

Sea Camp Camping: With 16 individual sites and two group
sites, this is the place to make your home during your visit to Cumberland Beach. Camping is limited to seven
consecutive nights, and campers must carry gear and supplies on/off island.
Facilities include treated drinking water, cold showers, and restrooms, and
each site has a picnic table, fire grill, and food box. Just a short walk to
the beach. Make reservations in advance.

Backcountry Camping: The camping experience for the
wild adventurer! There are numerous places to backcountry camp on Cumberland Island
Beach, including Stafford
(3.5 miles from mainland), Hickory Hill (5.5 miles), Yankee Paradise (7.5
miles), and Brickhill Bluff (10.6 miles). No transportation or vehicles
(including motorbikes) allowed in the wilderness. All include sources to obtain
water, trees, shade, and hiking trails
(no proper latrines). Fires are prohibited but campers can have self-contained
gas stove.

Kayaking/Boating: For just $4 a day, you can dock
your boat at Dungeness Dock. You can also kayak (rentals available).

Organized Activities

Dungeness Footsteps Tour: Beginning at 10am and
12:45pm daily at the Dungeness Dock, this tour travels through the historic
district south of the island. Your skilled National Park Ranger will weave
stories about the natural and cultural resources of the island.

Plum Orchard Mansion:
Every second and fourth Saturday of each month, you can hitch a tour to Plum Orchard Mansion, Georgia’s largest historic house.
Reservations recommended and can be placed via park’s reservation number: 912-882-4335.
