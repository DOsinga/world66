Cartersville is a small city about 45 miles northwest of [Atlanta](/northamerica/unitedstates/georgia/atlanta) in Bartow County, a convenient stop between Atlanta and Chattanooga on I-75.

The main draw for visitors is Etowah Indian Mounds, one of the best-preserved pre-Columbian Native American sites in the eastern United States. The complex includes large platform mounds dating from 1000 to 1550 AD, a defensive ditch, and a museum displaying carved stone figures and shell gorgets found on site. Downtown, the Booth Western Art Museum has an impressive collection of Western American painting and sculpture — unexpectedly strong for a small Georgia city — along with a Civil War gallery and a presidential gallery with letters from every US president.
