Crawfordville is the county seat of Taliaferro County, one of the smallest and most rural counties in Georgia — quiet, deeply rural, and largely unchanged from a century ago. It is not a place most travelers stumble upon, but those with an interest in Civil War history have a reason to make the detour.

The main draw is Alexander H. Stephens State Historic Park, which preserves Liberty Hall, the antebellum home of Alexander H. Stephens — Vice President of the Confederate States of America from 1861 to 1865. Stephens was a complicated figure: he delivered the infamous "Cornerstone Speech" defending slavery as the foundation of the Confederacy, yet spent much of his life opposing secession before ultimately supporting it. His home is open for guided tours, and the park includes a museum covering both his life and the broader Confederate period.
