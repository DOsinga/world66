Cumberland Island is the southernmost and largest of Georgia's barrier islands, accessible only by ferry from St. Marys. Most of the island is National Seashore managed by the National Park Service, and it has been kept intentionally undeveloped. There are no roads open to the public, no cars, and no shops.

The island stretches about 18 miles long and up to 3 miles wide, with a white sandy beach along its entire eastern shore. The interior has maritime forest, freshwater ponds, and salt marshes. Wildlife is abundant — wild horses (feral descendants of horses brought by settlers), white-tailed deer, wild turkeys, armadillos, and alligators are commonly seen.

The ruins of the Carnegie-era Dungeness mansion at the south end are the main historic feature, along with Plum Orchard, a grand 1898 Carnegie mansion occasionally open for tours. The tiny First African Baptist Church, where John F. Kennedy Jr. married Carolyn Bessette in 1996, is another draw.

Camping is available (reservations required well in advance), and a single small inn, Greyfield, provides upscale accommodation. Day visitors must take the ferry and are limited in number — the island's appeal is its near-wilderness character.
