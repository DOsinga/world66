---
title: "7 Day Itinerary in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

If you are planning a 7 day vacation and not quite sure what to do, here is your **Hiaswassee Georgia itinerary** for a memorable trip. Enjoy!

**Day 1**

Lake Sinclair is a very popular place for swimming during the summer months. The swimming beach is open from April 18 through October 31. If you are visiting the beach in a private vehicle the fee is $3 per day or $.50 per person if entering the area on foot, bicycle or bus. There is no reservation required to use the covered tables, so bring a picnic lunch and enjoy a day at the beach.

**Day 2**

Lake fishing is very popular during the year in Hiawassee. Lake Chatuge has an abundance of fish, especially a variety of bass fishing.

**Day 3**

Get in touch with the nature lover in you and go hiking. There are several day hiking trips in the area for example, Twin Bridges Foot Trail, a 2-mile hike at Lake Sinclair and Waters Creek which offers river and stream fishing.

**Day 4**

Spend the day horse back riding. If you have your own horse, or borrow one from one of the local stables, Jake and Bull Mountain Trail is an excellent place to spend the day. The trails are open year round, but closed during certain times of the day during archery, turkey and deer seasons.

**Day 5**

Play golf at what Golf Digest has called one of the most distinguished courses in the United States. The impressive Brasstown Golf Club is opened year round and has golf packages to accommodate any player. If your traveling companions do not share your enthusiasm for the game, no problem. Brasstown Resort offers a variety of other outdoor activities including a pool and stables or indoor activities such as a spa.

**Day 6**

Take a whitewater raft down the Nantahala or Ocoee River. If the challenge of the rapids rushing by does not appeal to you, then take a more relaxing journey on the calmer Toccoa River. Both options are offered by the Rolling Thunder River Company and are very reasonably priced.

**Day 7**

Ride the train through the Blue Ridge Mountains on the Blue Ridge Scenic Railway. The journey begins in the Chattahoochee National Forest and continues for 26 miles along the Toccoa River. The rail season is from March through December and offers discounts to military (active and retired).
