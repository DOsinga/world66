---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

**Hiawassee, GA budget travel **is easier than you'd think
because so much of what this small Southern town has to offer is
virtually free to enjoy. In other words, plan on spending a lot of time
appreciating the sights and experiences of the surrounding Georgia mountains, but if you know where to look, you can find some savings on
even your lodgings, too.

**The Great Outdoors**

The Lake Chatuge area offers plenty of opportunities for free
outdoors activity, such as hiking along part of the Appalachian Trail
and around the Lake Chatuge peninsula and fishing for bass and catfish
on the lake. You can also drive to the nearby Brasstown Bald, the
highest mountain peak in the deep south, and look out over all of
Hiawassee as well as part of 4 surrounding states. There's a Visitor
Information Center that helps explain the history of the area.
Admission is free and it only costs $4 per car to park there. Brasstown
Bald is open weekends April through May and daily June through October.

**Visit the Fred Hamilton Rhododendron Gardens**

At the Georgia Mountain Fairgrounds along the shore of Lake Chatuge
is the Fred Hamilton Rhododendron Gardens. Visitors can stroll through 3 gardens with over 3,000 blooms in what is one of the largest
hybrid rhododendron collections in Georgia. The garden was once part of
the personal garden of former Sears, Roebuck and Company CEO Fred
Hamilton. One highlight is Hamilton's own yellow azalea hybrid he named
for his wife, Hazel. Admission is free, although most visitors, even
those on a budget, find the experience worth the recommended $3
donation. The garden is open year-round, but the flowers may be at
their best in April and May, when they first bloom.

**Watch for Lodging Specials**

Although you can always stay at a motel, you'll be missing out on
the true extent of what Hiawassee has to offer if you skip the lodges,
cabins and campgrounds. Because of the gorgeous scenic views, there are
countless lodges, cabins and campgrounds for travelers to rent during
their stay, from the over 30 campgrounds at the Lake Chatuge Recreation
Center to the 1 to 4-bedroom villas at the Lake's Ridges Resort.
There are also cabins on private properties, such as on nearby
wineries, llama farms and horseback riding stables, where you can
immerse yourself in a unique interactive activity of your choice. You may find specials on rustic rentals
during your stay, too.
