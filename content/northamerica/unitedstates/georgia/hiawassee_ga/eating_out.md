---
title: "Eating Out in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

Eating at **Hiawassee, GA restaurants **is a surprisingly varied
experience for such a small, outdoors-oriented town. If camping and
cooking what you fish out of Hiawassee's vast Lake Chatuge isn't for
you--or you just want a break from the outdoors--dine at any of these
fine restaurants:

**Michaelee's Chocolate Caffe**

Located in downtown Hiawassee on Main Street, Michaelee's Chocolate
Caffe sounds like a quick place to grab coffee and cocoa, but it's
actually a fancy, sit-down restaurant that serves gourmet salads,
paninis, pasta dishes, sandwiches, veal, seafood, chicken and more.
This restaurant is open Monday through Saturday from 11:00 to 3:00 for
lunch and from 5:00 to 8:00 Monday through Thursday and 5:00 to 9:00
Friday to Saturday for dinner. The view of the surrounding mountain
area is breathtaking, so stopping by for a casual lunch with family
isn't a bad idea. However, the restaurant really shines during the
dinner hours, where it becomes a romantic haven for couples. Travelers
should call ahead for reservations, especially on the weekend.

**The Ridges Resort: The Oaks
**

Dining at The Oaks is worth it for the view alone. Located within
the Ridges Resort, The Oaks has a spectacular view alongside Lake
Chatuge and is a logical stop for travelers camping at the lake who
want to take some time off from cooking their own food. You can eat
inside the casual dining room or outside on the terrace and enjoy
salads, steaks, lamb, poultry, seafood and other fresh food. Cocktails
and champagne are available during happy hour, which is 4:00 to 5:00
Tuesday through Sunday. You can dine for dinner (reservations
recommended) Tuesday through Thursday 5:00 to 9:00 and Friday to
Saturday 5:00 to 10:00. Stop by on Sunday morning for a brunch buffet
from 11:00 to 2:30.

The Ridges Resort also has 3 other restaurants and lounges, which offer more casual dining.

**Mamasu's Brick Oven Pizza**

Travelers with families, sports fans and vacationers who enjoy
quality fast food at affordable prices alike will enjoy a jaunt to
Mamasu's Brick Oven Pizza, which is located less than a mile west of
the Georgia Mountain Fairgrounds. Three 60-inch plasma satellite TVs
display the latest sports games and arcade games keep kids distracted.

The menu's highlight is the 650-degree brick oven pizza, made fresh
by the only brick oven pizza restaurant in Hiawassee. Also available
are pasta dishes, garlic bread, chicken wings, Italian salad, brick
oven sandwiches and calzones. Drinks include beer and house wine.
Mamasu's is open seven days a week: 4:00 to 9:00 Monday through
Thursday, 11:00 to 10:00 Friday and Saturday and 2:00 to 8:00 on
Sundays. Seniors 60 and over should ask about the senior discount when
ordering.
