---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

**Hiawassee, GA family travel **is ideal for the outdoors-loving
family who enjoys reconnecting with nature, relaxing in a Southern small
town atmosphere and enjoying festivals by scenic lakes and mountains.
If you're bringing the kids to Hiawassee, make time during your trip to
partake in any of the following:

**Georgia Mountain Fair/Georgia Mountain Fall Festival**

The two largest festivals in Hiawassee, the Georgia Mountain Fair
(held in July) and the Georgia Mountain Fall Festival (October) offer
more than a week's worth of fun and entertainment at the Georgia
Mountain Fairgrounds. Kids under 10 enter the festivals for free and
admission is only $10 for kids and adults 10 and up. The two festivals
offer similar events, such as folk, country and bluegrass concerts,
magic shows, worship services, variety shows and sock hops. However,
the views of the mountains are much different in the summer and in the
fall, making a stop at either- or both -of the festivals worth a
family's time.

For just $15, kids can get an unlimited-use wristband for carnival
rides and games, such as a Ferris wheel that offers unbeatable views of
the mountainside, large slides and the ever-popular fishing games. Kids
also love the petting zoo, featuring farm animals that visitors can
feed and pet by hand and where they can watch chicks being born at the
hatchery. Your kids can also get an educational experience out of
watching the professional reenactors, who dress and speak like early
pioneers and offer interactive shows set in and around a school house,
a blacksmith's shop, a frontiersman's cannon and more.

**Hiking, Camping and Fishing
**

Hiking is a popular pastime in Hiawassee because of the stunning
mountains and scenic views. Part of the Appalachian Trail runs through
Hiawassee (the AT Hogpen Gap to Unicoi Gap), which visitors can
traverse with or without a guide. If you're interested in camping
overnight, consider staying at the Lake Chatuge Recreation Center,
which offers over 30 campsites with tent pads, picnic tables and
grills. Fishing at the lake on a boat may get you and your kids some
catfish, bream or bass, or you can take the kids hiking through a pine
forest in a confined one-mile lap around the lake's peninsula.

**Visit Llamas at Crystal River Ranch**

The Crystal River Ranch has been home for over 20 years to around 25
llamas at a time. Families that make appointments can come have a
picnic lunch at the ranch and pet, walk and feed the llamas while
learning about llama breeding from the certified professionals who run
the business. You also get to see the owners use the spinning wheel to
spin llama wool into yarn. You can take home a little of the experience
by buying hats, ponchos, scarves and more made from llama wool.
Starting in 2010, a family of four to six can even stay on the ranch at
a guest cabin with paid reservation.
