---
title: "Festivals in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

When it comes to **Hiawassee festivals**, this small town has a central location where it all goes down--the Georgia Mountain
Fairgrounds. The fairgrounds in the wilderness along mountains and
Lake Chatuge draw plenty of visitors from the more metropolitan areas near
Atlanta 2-hours away and provide a perfect stop for travelers from across the
country and the globe. Besides exclusive concerts, the Georgia Mountain
Fairgrounds is home to these festivals:

**Georgia Mountain Fair**

Held for 11 days in July, the Georgia Mountain Fair has been an annual standout tradition among the Hiawassee festivals since 1950. Country and bluegrass music acts such as Roy
Clark, Brenda Lee and the Bellamy Brothers are some of the highlights
of the festival. There are also magic acts, interfaith worship
services, variety shows, square dances and sock hops. Don't forget the
daily crafts and arts exhibits, featuring the handiwork of local
artisans, which you can purchase to take home with you.

Travelers with children will enjoy the array of carnival rides and games, such as slides, a Ferris
wheel and fishing games. One $15 armband per person offers unlimited
rides and games for the entire duration of the festival. Kids also love
the petting zoo and parades. Children and adults alike can enjoy the
educational experience of historical reenactment , including cannon
shooting, a school house and an actual blacksmith, featuring actors who
dress up and speak as if they were living in the mountain area's early
pioneer days.

**Georgia Mountain Fall Festival**

Another of the major Hiawassee festivals is the Georgia Mountain
Fall Festival that takes place for 9 days in October. An official "spin
off" of the summer festival, the fall festival is nonetheless worth a
trip by itself because the colors of the mountain foliage in autumn is
a sight that's truly breathtaking to behold. Besides more country and
bluegrass music acts such as that of Crystal Gale, The Georgia Mountain
Fair Band and Percy Sledge, 1 highlight of the fall festival is
Georgia's Official State Fiddlers' Convention, which draws fiddlers from around the state to compete in an exhaustive 2-day competition. This is true southern-style music at its finest!

In addition to more educational reenactments for both children and adults, another highlight is the Flower Show presented by Hiawassee's Garden Club that continues throughout the festival.

**Georgia Mountain Roots and Music Festival**

For 2 days in June, the Georgia Mountain Fairgrounds is home to the
Georgia Mountain Roots and Music Festival. Folk, Americana, and
bluegrass music are the themes for this 2-day event that features acts
such as Buck & Nelson, Irons in the Fire, Jim Lauderdale and The Red Stick Ramblers. During the festival, amateurs can try their hand at singing and playing some tunes at the Pickin' Pavillion and observers can get some food fresh off of the grill.

Tickets cost $18 for Friday, $27 for Saturday or $40 for both days.
Purchase a 2-day pass in advance and save $4 of the weekend pass price.
During the festival and into a 3rd day are arts & crafts booths
offering the work of local artisans for sale. Admission to the arts and crafts section is free.
