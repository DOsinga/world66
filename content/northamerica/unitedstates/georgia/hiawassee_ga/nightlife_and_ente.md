---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

The **Hiawassee, GA nightlife **may not be the same as the
nightlife in a big city, but that's precisely why visitors to the area
have to make their own unique memories. Hit the town or spend your
evenings gazing at the starry sky in Hiawassee.

**Take the Kids to Fun World**

The neighboring town of Young Harris, the other half of Towns
County, has the perfect family-friendly destination that's open pretty
late on weekends. Before you head out for your night out as a couple, take the kids one evening to Fun World at Fieldstone, where you and the kids can engage in games of laser tag, indoor miniature
golf, go cart racing and arcade games. There's also a climbing wall, a
foam-play area for toddlers and a restaurant. Fun World is open
Thursdays 4:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m., Fridays 4:00 p.m. to 10:00 p.m.,
Saturdays 11:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. and Sundays 1:00 p.m. to 7:00 p.m.

**Drinks and Food**

Hiawassee is too small a town to be littered with fancy nightclubs,
but The Yacht Club Boathouse Bar & Grill at the Ridges Resort is a popular spot for alcohol and casual tavern food that's open long
past the competition in town. Sit on a patio to take in the gorgeous
views of the mountainside and Lake Chatuge lit up under the moonlight.
You can even drive a boat right up to the bar! Food and drinks are
generally under $10--and the view alone is worth much more than that. The bar
opens at 11:00 a.m. every day and stays open until 10:00 p.m. Sundays
and Mondays, 11:00 p.m. Tuesdays through Thursdays and 12:00 a.m.
Fridays and Saturdays.

**See a Play**

Adults, whose idea of a night out doesn't include or revolve
around drinking, will love being able to take in a show featuring the
local acting community. Located in nearby Hayesville, NC, the Licklog
Players put on performances virtually every month than span the gamut
from musicals to comedies to dramas, both contemporary and classic.
While some matinee performances feature productions aimed at children,
the evening performances make for an entertaining, memorable night out
for adults. Tickets cost only around $14 for adults per show and
reservations are recommended.

**Spending the Night in Nature**

One unique feature of a small Southern mountain town like Hiawassee
is that the best "nightlife" isn't at a bar or dance club, but
outdoors, where the stars are more visible than in the city and the
moon shines off of Hiawassee's vast Lake Chatuge. Adults of all ages, and families alike, can enjoy a night camping or at a lodge along Lake Chatuge, where, once the sun sets, they can relax on a porch or on a boat and
gaze at the stars. If you do spend the night outdoors, avoid hiking
without a guide too far from your lodge or campsite, as the mountain
trails are vast and difficult to see at night.
