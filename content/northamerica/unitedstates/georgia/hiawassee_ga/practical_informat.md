Take note of the following **Hiawassee, GA practical information **if you're planning a trip there:

**Stats**

At the time of 2000 census, the basic Hiawassee demographic statistics were:-
Population: 808 (currently approximately 866)- Male/Female Ratio: 41.3 percent/58.7 percent
- Median age: 62.3- Citizens Identifying as Following Races: White: 97.4 percent, Asian: 1.7 percent, Hispanic: 1.6 percent, Black: 0.2 percent, Native: 0.1 percent

These statistics demonstrate Hiawassee's status of a small town. However, they also point out the general lack of diversity in the area. Interestingly to note, the vast majority of Hiawassee's citizens are adults and a large number of adults are beyond retirement age. (The median age throughout the U.S. is 35.3).

**Basic Information**

English is the primary language spoken in Hiawassee and the currency
is the U.S. dollar. Visitors from other countries will need
passports to get into their flight's landing destination, but once they
drive into Hiawassee , they will no longer need to present any
paperwork. As in the rest of the U.S., the electrical outlets are fitted
for the flat blade plug, and electricity runs at 120 volts at 60 Hertz.

**Safety**

Because Hiawassee is a small town, crime is very low. There has been
no murder or armed robbery in the town in the past 10 years. Violent
crimes between residents have been reported at between 0 and 6 cases
annually in the past 10 years and tourists rarely, if ever, report any
problems.

Nevertheless, as with any destination, take precautions and act with
common sense to protect your valuables, as even a small, peaceful town
like Hiawassee averages about 25 non-violent thefts per year. Keep your
vehicles locked, never leave valuables on display in a car or hotel
room or show off valuables to strangers, and keep your purses and bags in sight.

Because Hiawassee is largely a wilderness town, you may face more
danger from the wild than the residents. Always stick to trails when
exploring the mountains and hiking paths, follow the lead of a
wilderness guide if you're inexperienced and take a first aid kit along if you plan on spending a lot of time outdoors.

**Weather and Clothing**

Hiawassee enjoys moderately mild winters, but the mountains make the
area colder than other southern cities. The average temperature during
the winter months is about 25 to 47 degrees Fahrenheit. Spring and autumn temperatures average about 42 to 67 degrees F and the summer months are usually between 57 and 84 degrees F. Rainfall is highest in spring, particularly March.

If you're headed to Hiawassee during the winter, bring extra clothing, but dress in layers rather than in bulky attire, as you'll need the extra maneuvering
room if you plan on participating in outdoor activities. In the spring,
make sure to bring rain gear and bring plenty of sunscreen in summer.
Even when it's warm in summer, you may want to bring a light jacket,
particularly if you're camping outdoors, as it can get colder at night.
