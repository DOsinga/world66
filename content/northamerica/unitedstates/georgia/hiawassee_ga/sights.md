**Hiawassee GA Sights **include outdoor activities, historic attractions, and craft markets. The sights stay true to the land that they originated in and that is the best part of all. The things that travelers will see in Hiawassee have direct ties to the town and the culture of the area. It is simply a charming Georgia town.

**Pioneer Village**

Pioneer Village is located at the Georgia Mountain Fairgrounds. The Historic Village presents life as it once was and allows visitors to see a side of mountain life. Authentic buildings and other artifacts were actually moved from a nearby area to be part of the attraction. The plentiful and unique history of the area is preserved and restored to its original glory. At certain times throughout the year visitors can explore the 1-room schoolhouse, general store, blacksmith shop, and an old mountain house that make-up Pioneer Village. Most furnishings are antique and the general store is even complete with authentic items. When the Fairgrounds hold special events there are even several activities that Pioneer Village itself holds. Those activities include soap-making and moon-shining. This part of Hiawassee GA Sights is perfect for families and travelers interested in history.

**Hamilton Rhododendron Garden**

Hiawassee GA Sights include the Hamilton Rhododendron Garden which is located on the Georgia Mountain Fairgrounds. It is actually on a slopping hillside connecting with Lake Chatuge and the garden is spread out among the land like a beautiful blanket. Over 3,000 plants and flowers are in the garden. The creator of the gardens, Fred Hamilton, actually created a flower called the yellow azalea and the only flower in existence is at the Hamilton Rhododendron Garden. Aside from this unique flower, there are also magnolias, tulips, dogwoods, and lady slippers. The garden is simply a magically area which appears more like something out of the pages of a fiction book than an actual garden. There is something enchanting about walking through a hillside garden of colorful flowers and pine trees that is difficult to recreate anywhere else. The garden is open daily.

**ArtWorks Artisan Center**

ArtWorks Artisan Center is a 22,000 square foot retail store for the arts. It is additionally a school where craft classes are taught. Work from more than 56 artists and craftsmen is sold in the center. The handmade items are both for decorative and functional uses. These items include candles, glass work, paintings, quilts, dolls, leather works, pottery and much more. All work is by regional artists. The area appears overwhelming at first, but visitors really have to look at 1 piece at a time to truly discover the treasures that the center holds. ArtWorks Artisan Center is open Monday through Saturday from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
