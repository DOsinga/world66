title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Hiawassee GA"
type: section
---

Hiawassee Georgia is a nature and outdoor lover's dream. Here are the top 5 **Hiawassee Georgia must do's** when visiting the area.

**White Water Rafting on the Ocoee River**

Hiawassee white water rafting on the rapids of the Ocoee River is a fun way to spend the day. There are several companies in the area that feature adventure packages on the water, as well as tours that include rock climbing and kayaking.

**Waterfall Hikes**

Hiawassee Georgia does not have a shortage of waterfalls. If you have the time, you should see them all. Two of the most viewed are Blue Hole Falls and High Shoals Creek Falls, both in Towns County. There is swimming at Blue Hole Falls, while High Shoals Creeks reveals a beautiful cascade that drops into a fall. The falls at Joel Creek drop to 150 feet. Denton Branch Falls, in the Nantahala Wilderness, features a very impressive fall. The short hike is relatively easy. The observation deck of the Gurley Creek Falls is a short hike from the Enota Mountain Retreat Welcome Center, but the walk is well worth it. From the observation deck you can see most of the 220 feet drop. Additional falls are viewed from other trails.

**Go Fishing**

Lake Chatuge offers excellent freshwater fishing depths and is home to a variety of fish such as the smallmouth bass, largemouth bass, white bass and the hybrid striped bass. Although there is a winter drawdown, fishing is still available in the winter months as well.

**Hit the Links**

Recognized by Golf Digest as one of the best places for golf, Brasstown Valley Golf Club presents an impressive 18-hole championship golf course that is rated 73.5. After a round of golf, try relaxing in the spa or get a massage at the Equani Spa Resort. The Stables offers 5 miles of horseback riding trails. Sign up for a 1 or 2-hour guided ride.

**Sample the Vineyards**

Haiawassee Georgia is home to several vineyards and wineries. Take a tour of Crane Creek Vineyards, or sample different wines at the Habersham Winery Tasting Room or visit the Habersham Winery at Nacoochee. Crane Creek Vineyards is nestled in the Blue Ridge Mountains and hosts activities year round. Whether you want to visit on Soup Kitchen Saturdays or on Wine for Women days, or attend one of the many annual events, you are sure to enjoy the visit. Established in 1983, the Habersham Winery is one of the oldest wineries in Georgia. Habersham Winery offers tours of the wine making facility, has a wine tasting room and a gift shop.
