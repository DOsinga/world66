---
title: "When to Go in Hiawassee"
type: section
---

Spring and fall are the best times to visit Hiawassee. Spring (April–May) brings rhododendron and azalea blooms, mild temperatures, and thinner crowds before summer arrives. Autumn (October–November) is spectacular for fall foliage in the Blue Ridge Mountains — October is peak season and can be crowded on weekends.

Summer is the busiest period, particularly July and August, when families come for the lake and outdoor activities. Warm and humid, with temperatures in the 80s°F. Campgrounds fill up fast; book months ahead for prime summer weekends.

Winter is quiet. The mountains can see snow, and temperatures drop well below freezing at night. Some campgrounds close, but the town itself remains open. A good option for those who want solitude.
