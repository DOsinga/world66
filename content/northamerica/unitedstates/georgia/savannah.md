Savannah is one of the most beautiful cities in the American South. Its historic district — a grid of 22 shaded squares framed by live oaks and Spanish moss, surrounded by Federal and Regency townhouses — has remained remarkably intact since the 19th century. The city was spared during Sherman's March to the Sea in 1864, which accounts for why it still looks the way it does.

Walking the squares is the essential experience. Chippewa Square, Johnson Square, and Forsyth Park are among the finest, though all 22 are worth exploring. The River Street waterfront runs along the Savannah River on a bluff below the historic district, converted from 19th-century cotton warehouses into shops, restaurants, and bars.

Beyond the architecture, Savannah is a good base for day trips. [Fort Pulaski](/northamerica/unitedstates/georgia/fortpulaski) is a fascinating Civil War fortification 15 miles east. Tybee Island, 18 miles out, is the local beach. Further south along the coast are the Golden Isles — [St. Simons Island](/northamerica/unitedstates/georgia/stsimonsisland) and Jekyll Island — worth at least a night each.
