St. Simons Island is one of Georgia's Golden Isles, accessible from Brunswick by causeway. It's a quieter and more residential alternative to beach resorts further down the coast, with a pleasant village center, good seafood restaurants, and a handful of beaches that don't get overwhelmingly crowded.

Fort Frederica National Monument preserves the site of the town of Frederica, established by James Oglethorpe in 1736 as the southernmost British colonial outpost in North America. It served as a military base protecting Georgia and South Carolina from the Spanish in Florida until the decisive Battle of Bloody Marsh in 1742. The park has evocative ruins among massive live oaks draped in Spanish moss, with good interpretive exhibits at the visitor center.

The St. Simons Lighthouse (1872) at the southern tip of the island is open for tours and offers views of the coast. The village nearby has cafes, restaurants, and bike rentals.
