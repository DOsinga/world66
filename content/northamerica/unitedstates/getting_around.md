## By Car

The US is built around the car, and outside of a handful of major cities, a rental car is the most practical way to get around. The interstate highway system covers the country thoroughly. Speed limits are 65–75 mph on interstates and 25–35 mph in towns. Roads are generally well maintained and well signed. Fuel is cheap by international standards. Foreign driving licences are valid for tourism.

Rental cars are available at all major airports and most cities. Book in advance for the best rates and to guarantee availability. Compact cars typically run $40–$80 per day, not including taxes and insurance, which can add substantially to the cost.

## By Air

For distances over 500 miles, flying is usually faster and sometimes cheaper than any other option. Southwest, Delta, American, and United dominate domestic routes. Budget carriers like Spirit, Frontier, and Allegiant offer cheap fares to regional airports. Buy early. Checked baggage fees are standard on most airlines; Southwest is an exception.

Domestic air travel requires a **REAL ID**-compliant driving licence or passport. Standard state licences are still accepted at many checkpoints but a compliant ID has been required since May 2025.

## By Train

**Amtrak** is the national rail network. Service quality and frequency varies enormously. The Northeast Corridor between Washington DC, Philadelphia, New York, and Boston is excellent — frequent trains, reliable schedules, and the Acela high-speed service. Elsewhere, long-distance trains (California Zephyr, Empire Builder, Southwest Chief) run once daily, sometimes less, and delays are common as trains share tracks with freight. That said, long-distance Amtrak travel is scenic and comfortable, with dining cars and sleeper cabins available.

The USA Rail Pass lets you take a set number of trips over a fixed period and is worth considering for extended travel.

## By Bus

**Greyhound** is the main long-distance bus operator, covering most of the country. Tickets are cheap, especially booked in advance. FlixBus, Megabus, and regional operators compete on popular corridors. The Chinatown bus network (now largely operated under the FlixBus umbrella) connects many East Coast cities at very low fares. For city-to-city travel of 4–8 hours, buses are often the most affordable option.

## City Transport

[New York](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork), Chicago, Washington DC, Boston, San Francisco, and a handful of other cities have reliable subway systems. Most other American cities are difficult to navigate without a car. Taxis and rideshares (Uber, Lyft) are widely available everywhere.
