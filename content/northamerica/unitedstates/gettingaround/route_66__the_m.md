Get your kicks on Route 66. Immortalized in the book "Grapes of Wrath" by John Steinbeck, Route 66 became known as the "mother road." It is probably one of the single most important roads in the modern history of travel in the United States. Born from the mind of Tulsan Cyrus Avery in order to promote tourism to his community, Route 66 developed as a part of the national highway system. But unlike other roads of its day, it did not run straight east and west across the country. Route 66 has also been called "America's Main Street" and was officially called "The Will Rogers Highway."

Route 66 started in [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago), and makes its way through [Illinois](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois), [Missouri](/northamerica/unitedstates/missouri), [Kansas](/northamerica/unitedstates/kansas), [Oklahoma](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma), [Texas](/northamerica/unitedstates/texas), [New Mexico](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico), [Arizona](/northamerica/unitedstates/arizona), [California](/northamerica/unitedstates/california) and ends up in [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles). The cities that Route 66 ran through are varied in size and geography. Principle cities on Historic Route 66 include [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago), IL (pop 2.9 million); Cicero, IL (pop. 83,000); Berwyn, IL (pop. 54,000); Bolingbrook, IL (pop. 69,000); Joliet, IL (pop. 106,000); Bloomington, IL (pop. 75,000); [Springfield](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/springfield), IL (pop. 135,000); [St. Louis](/northamerica/unitedstates/missouri/stlouis), MO (pop. 353,000); [Springfield](/northamerica/unitedstates/missouri/springfield), MO (pop. 152,000); [Joplin](/northamerica/unitedstates/missouri/joplin), MO (pop. 50,000); [Tulsa](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/tulsa), OK (pop. 388,000); [Edmond](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma), OK (pop. 68,000); [Oklahoma City](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/oklahomacity), OK (506,000); [Amarillo](/northamerica/unitedstates/texas/amarillo), TX (pop. 183,000); [Albuquerque](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/albuquerque), NM (pop. 495,000); [Flagstaff](/northamerica/unitedstates/arizona/flagstaff), AZ (pop. 57,000); Victorville, CA (pop. 64,000); San Bernadino, CA (202,000); Rialto, CA (pop. 92,000); Fontana, CA (pop. 129,000); Rancho Cucamonga, CA (pop. 161,000); Upland, CA (pop. 68,000); Pomona, CA (pop. 149,000); Glendora, CA (58,000); Arcadia, CA (pop. 56,000); Pasadena, CA (pop. 146,000); [Los Angeles](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/losangeles), CA (pop. 4 million); Santa Monica, CA (pop. 84,000).
