---
title: "Beaches in Hawaii"
type: section
---

There are beaches all over Hawaii but the ones mentioned below are representative of the 8 main Hawaiian Islands. All beaches in Hawaii are public regardless of what a hotel or home owner positioned on the beach says.

**Kailua Beach**

One of the most representative beaches of Hawaii is Kailua Beach on the island of Oahu. This beach is 3 miles long and has soft, light colored sand. It is near the Kooloau mountain range and positioned in a residential area. It doesn’t get very crowded on weekdays but watch out on weekends. Here you can swim, windsurf, boogie board, and kayak but only on special occasions do the waves get big enough to surf.

**Hapuna Beach**

Hapuna Beach on the Main Island is wide and long. The sand is soft and water is clear enough to go snorkeling if the ocean is calm. In the afternoon the waves get playful enough to use a boogie board. he sands are ivory and swimming conditions are good because of a shallow sandbar that continues into the ocean for about 25 yards.

**Kaanapali Beach**

Maui has a beach that is a favorite of most tourists. Kaanapali Beach is surrounded by a resort and is 1.5 miles long.You can take surf lessons here or play volleyball. There is also a great opportunity for snorkeling near Black Rock.

**Lanikai Beach**

Lanikai Beach in Oahu has been a favorite beach for years. It has white sand and swimming is a year round activity because it is protected from an offshore reef. The waters are generally calm so it is perfect for those that don’t want waves. It is in a residential neighborhood and has a tranquil atmosphere. Sand erosion has been a problem and the beach is significantly smaller than it was in years past.

**Hanalei Bay**

If you are in Hawaii to surf, head to Hanalei Bay on Kauai. The beach is 3 miles long formed in a crescent of white sand. Behind the beach are waterfalls and mountain peaks. Large waves will come from the right side of the bay while near the pier the waves start to diminish so they are perfect for beginning surfers. This beach is not often crowded. In the summer the bay is docile but in the winter one has to watch for storms and often frenzied water conditions.

Try any of the multitudes of beaches on all islands of Hawaii and you’ll be sure to find one to suit your needs.
