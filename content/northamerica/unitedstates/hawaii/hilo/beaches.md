---
title: "Beaches in Hilo"
type: section
---

Hilo is a beach-lovers paradise. It has some beaches perfect for
families and children, others for surfers and body-boarders, and even
others for swimmers, snorkelers, and fishers. There are black sand
beaches, white sand beaches, and beaches with rocky shores as well.
Long or short, crowded or secluded, shady or sun-soaked, you’ll be sure
to find a beach in Hilo that suits you.

**Onekahakaha Beach Park**

This friendly beach is amazing for families. Its shallow, clear
water make it great for swimming and snorkeling, and the breakwaters
shelter the area from rough currents. There is plenty of space and
grassy areas for having a picnic, and shaved ice trucks pass often,
providing easy access to a deliciously cold treat.

**Hilo Bay Beachfront Park**

In the past this park provided one of the most beautiful and popular
beaches in Hilo. Its extensive stretch of black sand and warm, clear
waters were particularly inviting. Sadly, the place was contaminated by
industrial sewage, and even after attempts to clean it up, the water is
still murky. It is still a lovely picnicking place and popular for
canoeing and surfing, but it is not a common touristic beach anymore.

**Honoli'i Beach Park**

The rocky shore of this beach makes for a less than ideal sunbathing
experience, but don’t worry because its great body boarding and surfing
are its main charms. Waves can be large, and currents can be strong, so
don’t go out if you’re a beginner. There is a lifeguard on duty just in
case. Snorkeling and swimming aren’t recommended at this turbulent
beach.

**Leleiwi Beach Park**

This is another beach seemingly designed for families. It has a
partly rocky shoreline with many tide-pools perfect for children to
take a dip in. The rest of the beach is beautiful black sand. The
picnic areas are well maintained, and there are pavilions providing
shade. The water is shallow and clear, and there are many small coves
making this a must-visit location if you’re planning on doing some
snorkeling. The water is generally calm, but be careful as there can be
some surprisingly hazardous currents, especially if a storm is coming.
Additionally, the Leleiwi Beach Park has plenty of parking due to the
huge amount of locals and visitors that hang out there on the weekends.

At all of these beaches don’t forget to bring your sunscreen, towel, sunglasses, and water!
