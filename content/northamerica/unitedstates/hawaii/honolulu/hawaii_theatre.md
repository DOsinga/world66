Nicknamed the "Pride of the Pacific" when it opened in 1922, this art deco movie palace in Chinatown fell into disrepair before a loving restoration in the 1990s brought it back to its original glory. The interior is lush — gilded Corinthian columns, a mosaic tile floor, a Lionel Walden mural, and a gloriously ornate proscenium arch. Today it operates as a performing arts venue hosting concerts, films, and community events. Even if nothing is showing, guided tours run regularly and are worth the time. The theatre anchors the revitalized Chinatown arts district, so combine your visit with a wander through the neighbourhood's galleries and restaurants.
