Waikiki is the famous beach strip that put Honolulu on the tourist map, a two-mile crescent of sand backed by high-rise hotels with Diamond Head crater looming at the eastern end. The beach itself is surprisingly good given the density of development behind it, with reliable waves for beginner surfers and warm water year-round. The bronze Duke Kahanamoku statue near the police station is the obligatory photo stop. Kalakaua Avenue runs parallel to the shore, packed with shops, restaurants, and the kind of commercial energy that either thrills or exhausts depending on your tolerance. Sunset drinks at a beachfront bar while outrigger canoes glide past is the quintessential Waikiki moment. It's thoroughly commercialised but undeniably fun, and the beach access is free for everyone.
