While on Kauai plan to get wet! Sailing, surfing, windsurfing,
snorkeling, kayaking, body boarding, fishing, diving, water skiing, you
name it, if it involves water, you'll find it here in Kauai.

**
Beaches

**
The island Kauai immediately makes one think of beaches. The beaches of Kauai are not only amazing but offer tons of varied water sport opportunities. Visit Poipu
Beach on the South Shore for calm waters where the kids can try surfing
for the first time on gentle waves while the rest of the family can
snorkel around the shallows or scuba dive into caverns where lava tubes
attract parades of fish flaunting neon colors. On the North Shore, you
should visit fish filled Kee Beach. On the east side, Lydgate Beach Park is highly suitable for families with children of all ages and it offers clear waters for novice snorkelers.
**

Diving**

Diving fans should reserve a whole day for diving. Kauai offers 35 official dive sites and Makua Beach (also known as Tunnels Beach) in Haena stands out from the crowd because of its amazing reef formations. Another popular dive site is Koloa Landing on the South Shore. Along the Napali
Coast you can find several companies that offer diving excursions (some
also offer excursions that count towards diving certification). If
you're a certified diver, you should look into diving off to the forbidden island of Niihau, 17 miles of the coast of Kauai.
**

Fishing**

For the best Kauai sportfishing, look no further than Lahela Sportfishing located at ** **Slip 109 Nawailiwili Small Boat Harbor in Lihue. This native Hawaiian owned and operated business offers both big game marlin and light tackle sportfishing charters. Their 34 feet sportfisher Lahela is U.S. Coast Guard certified for up to 14 passengers and its 33 feet Bertram sportfisher is certified for 6 passengers. Also inquire about Lahela Sportfishing's whale watching tours.**

Alternatives to Water Activities**

For
those travelers travelling to Kauai for luxury, there is one absolute must-do: try a helicopter ride above Kauai with the Blue
Hawaiian Helicopters. This is a 55 minute ride over Kauai. All helicopter pilots are State of Hawaii
Certified Tour Guides. The Blue Hawaiian Helicopter company is the
only activity company in the world of any kind, honored with the Star
Diamond Award for Oustanding
Quality and Service from the American Academy of Hospitality Sciences.
From the moment of boarding, every flight is captured on a high quality
DVD that is available for purchase.

Finally, there is also the option to hike at Kokee State Park. Here you can find 40 miles of footpaths that have been classified from easy to strenuous.

For those looking to get out and explore, whether by land, sea, or air, Kauai has something for everybody!
