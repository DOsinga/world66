---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

Of all the Hawaiian islands many people feel that Kauai is the best. This tropical paradise is 5 million years old. It's thick with vegetation and long on beaches. Like all the islands, Kauai has breath taking panoramic views and incredible fauna. What makes this island special is it is more off the beaten path and you can live amongst the locals who are lucky enough to live here.

**Waimea Canyon, Koke's State Park**

This is one of the most scenic canyons on the planet. Magnificent red hues and black volcanic rock add to the already spectacular scenery. There are hiking trails that run along the canyons rim and two primary look out points. The best views are to be had away from the road as the canyon becomes deeper. This is a free activity with free parking.

**Wailua**

The only navigable rivers in Hawaii are on Kauai. One of the most popular is the Wailua River. If you enjoy kayaking, canoeing or tour boats this is perfect destination for you. The famous wedding destination of Fern Grotto is here. Wailua Falls is a large and raging waterfall you can see from the road, accessible to everyone.

**Mountain Tubing**

For the mild to wildly adventurous traveler, mountain tubing is a great time. It’s like taking a hike without doing any walking. Relax in a tube and let the tranquil current wind you through beautiful remote locations. Water friendly shoes and clothes are recommended. Sunscreen and a hat are an absolute must.

**Snorkeling**

Snorkeling is a fascinating activity and often a memorable highlight for visitors to Kauai. The great thing about snorkeling in Kauai is the availability of scenic underwater areas for all levels and ages to explore. Beginners and young children can enjoy the safe and shallow waters of Anini Beach. To see aquatic creatures in a protected cove go to Salt Pond Beach. Always check tidal and current conditions.

**Na Pali**

Just when you think you can’t appreciate panoramic views anymore, wow yourself at Na Pali. The land access to this area is a difficult 11 mile trail. Kalalau Beach at the end of the hike is delightfully secluded and a great place to play. You will need a camping permit as most hikers break this hike up into more than one day. You don’t have to hike to enjoy this gorgeous area. Boat tours, kayaking trips and helicopters are fun ways to access of these awesome views and are quicker than hiking.
