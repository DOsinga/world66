For nearly 70 years, Lanai -- dubbed the "Pineapple Island" -- was operated as a pineapple plantation by Dole Company. In its heyday it was the world's largest source of pineapple, grown on more than 16,000 acres, until the industry began to falter. By 1993 pineapple was phased out in favor of resort development, ranching, and other agriculture.

Today Lanai is the smallest inhabited Hawaiian island and one of the quietest. Most of the island is owned by tech billionaire Larry Ellison, who has invested heavily in luxury resorts and sustainable agriculture. The island has no traffic lights, one small town (Lanai City), and some of Hawaii's most secluded beaches. It is accessible by a short ferry from [Maui](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/maui) or by a quick inter-island flight.
