Here are some great day trip ideas during your stay in Maui:

**Road to Hana**The
Road to Hana should be at the top of your day trips in Maui because of
its unparalleled natural beauty and untouched landscapes. The Road to
Hana has stand the test of time as the bastion against the commercial
development of the island of Maui. For the Road to Hana we highly
recommend that you reserve at least 6 hours of a day because the road
can only fit one car at a time in lots of places, considerably slowing
down the flow of traffic. Also, expect heavy delays because people on
the Road to Hana are on vacation and want to take as many pictures as
possible.

The Road to Hana can be
dangerous with over 600 sharp curves, 54 bridges and narrow one lane
roads. On top of all of this, heavy rains can complicate the road
conditions heavily. This is another reason that you should consider
investing in a map or book to guide your drive to Hana. Make sure to
pack a bathing suit,sunblock lotion, plenty of water and hiking boots. You will need the hiking boots when hiking towards lagoons and waterfalls. **

Reach for the Stars

**This
day will start really early, as in 4:30 a.m. early. The objective is to
reach the top of Haleakala Mountain before sunrise. This trip demands a
lot of preparation. Checking that your rental car works properly and
has enough gas for about 4 hours. The trip to the entrance of the
Haleakala Mountain National Park from Kahului
is about 40 minutes and then you have about another 40 minutes to the
cabin near the summit. These times may vary depending on traffic. Don't
think that just for being 5 a.m. you will not encounter traffic because
tour companies try to arrive as early as possible with large groups of
over 30 visitors. If you're planning to take photographs, it would be a
good idea to plan to leave about 30 minutes early so you have enough
time to set up your equipment and secure a good spot. Other
important items to pack are wind breaking jackets (wind chill can range
from 30 to 40 degrees F), plenty of water and snacks, sunglasses (for
protection of glare from the early sun rays) and medication for
headaches and dizziness (a sudden change in altitude, 10,000 feet, may cause headaches, breathlessness and dizziness).

After
you have captured great shots from the sunrise at Haleakala Mountain,
you have 2 options. The first one is to start the descend towards Kahului and stop along the highway to photograph the local flora and fauna. You're bound to run into packs of nene along the road, the state bird, and volcanic remains from past eruptions of the Haleakala. Once you arrive to Kahului, you will be ready for a big breakfast and get on with your day.The second option is to take on
the over 27 miles of hiking trails available at the top of the
Haleakala mountains. Be prepared for sudden, cold winds and pack plenty
of fluids and snacks. If you're interested in camping, call (808)
572-4000 for more details.
