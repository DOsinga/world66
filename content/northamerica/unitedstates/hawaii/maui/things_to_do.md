Maui
is known as a great place for adrenaline junkies.
However, this island also offers the humanitarian an opportunity to help
islanders restore and improve Maui's natural beauty and cultural
riches.
Whether you crave adventure or would rather help out Mother Nature, Maui will have something for you.

**Zip, Ride, and Climb at Piiholo Ranch**

Located
in Makawao in Maui, this working cattle ranch is owned by the Baldwin
family and offers horseback riding, zip line tours and climbing
challenges. Horseback rides are available 3 times per day and there is
the possibility to book a private trail ride. You'll be amazed at the
variety of horse rides that Piiholo Ranch offers, ranging from 1 to 3
hours and including fine horse breeds. The zip line tours allow you to
zoom down over endemic tress to Maui such as eucalyptus, koa and ohia.
You will experience bird eye views from Maui's coasts and canyons.
Finally, you can challenge your friends in seven unique climbing trails
at the Piiholo Ranch's Tango Tower.
**

Hike Haleakala Mountain**

Over
27 miles of hiking trails are waiting for your at Haleakala Mountain, a
volcano park that is an hour and half away from Kahului in Maui. With a
summit at 10,023 feet above sea level, this a hiking trip that demands
preparation. It's really important that you plan ahead for sudden, cold
winds ( ranging from 35 to 40 degrees F) and high altitude
breathlessness, especially if you're planning to see the sunrise. The
sunrise is spectacular, so don't forget your camera! Call (808)
572-4400 to request a permit and pack warm clothes, food and water for
the long hike.**

**
Snorkel and Dive at Molokini Islet**

Off
the Maui coast, the islet of Molokini offers snorkeling and scuba
diving in an amazing coral reef with crystalline waters setting.
Sailing from Maalea Harbor, the Trilogy company offers you exciting
tours on one of its custom built catamarans. With Trilogy, you will
visit the Kaanapali beach and the Honolua Bay. Honolua Bay is a great
spot for all kinds of snorkelers (beginners and advanced) to view green
sea turtles (*honu* in Hawaiian) and schools of tropical fish.
Also, those Maui visitors who would like to gain certified diving
experience, can do so with Trilogy.
**

Volunteer to Restore Maui**

The
Koieie Fishpond in southern Maui is one of the last remaining ancient
fishpond that Hawaiians used to trap fish as they came in from tides.
Damaged by decades of weather and human abuse, the Koieie Fishpond
needs your help for its restoration. Volunteers will receive a light
meal, refreshments and a commemorative t-shirt issued by the Hawaii
Tourism Authority and the Office of Hawaiian Affairs. Book your spot in
advance because this volunteer opportunity is only available twice a
month.
