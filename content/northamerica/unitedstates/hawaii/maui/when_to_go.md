title: "When to Go in Maui"
type: section
---

The most important thing to consider when planning your trip to Maui is whether you'll be arriving during high season or off season.

**High Season**

During this time prices are up and
resorts are often booked to capacity. High season is usually from mid
December through March or mid April. Maui receives a lot of visitors
during the last 2 weeks of December. Visitors to Maui during the
Christmas and New Year holidays need to make their reservations months
in advance to guarantee accommodations. Expect premium prices on car
rentals, accommodations and airfare.**

Off Season**

The off season is usually during spring
(mid April to mid June) and during the fall (September to mid
December). Coincidentally, these are the best times times in Maui
because of consistent great weather. Visitors to Maui should try to
make travel arrangements during the off season because they will be
able not only to save money but to avoid crowds. During the off season,
hotels and airlines offer discounts to encourage visitors to visit Maui.**

Golden Week**

During the last week of April, large
groups of Japanese tourists visit the Hawaiian Islands because three
Japanese holidays take place one after the other. This prime time for
Japanese tourists is known in Japan as the Golden Week. If
you're planning to visit Maui between the last week in April and early
May, make sure to book your travel arrangements months in advance. **

June**

The Annual Kings Trail Triathlon is the
premier event for ironmen and ironwomen in Maui, who are eager to show
off their skills against the very best from all around the world.
Hosted by the non profit Maui Multi Sports Club, this is the only road
triathlon on the island of Maui. Swim 1.5 kilometers in the clear blue
waters off Makena. Bike 40 kilometers through the resorts of Makena and
Wailea. Run 10 kilometers following the ancient Hawaiian Kings. Trail
down into the area of the last volcanic activity on the island of Maui.**

Halloween**

Every year
during All Hallows Eve, a big Halloween themed block party takes the
streets of Lahaina, Maui. Most of the action happens on Front
Street, where vehicular traffic is closed for most of the day on
October 31st. The Children's Costume Parade is a popular event and it's
a "first come first seat basis", so plan to arrive early to grab a
seat. You will also find Halloween themed activities, live music
performances, food and plenty of costumed characters.
