Oahu, Hawaii offers a plethora of unique opportunities. Whether you enjoy active adventurous get-aways, or prefer to relax on the beach with mai tai in hand, here's an itinerary to garantee you make the most of your stay.

**Day 1 - Swim with the Dolphins
**
Of the
many must-do activities in the Hawaiian Islands, experiencing dolphins
in the wild is at the top of the list. The Best of The West day trip
offered by Wild Side Eco-Adventures is a good choice. A maximum of 6
passengers leave Waianae harbor on a custom built ship to reach off
shore spots where larger dolphins and even whales can be seen
year-round. Later on, you can snorkel over the coral reef with green sea turtles and schools of fish. Back on land, head to Chinatown
in Downtown Honolulu for a nice dinner at a local favorite, Indigo's.
**

Day 2 - Mountain Biking**

Explore
Oahu's countryside on the 100% Dirt Mountain Biking Kaawa Valley trip
with Bike Hawaii. The Kaawa Valley, the Koolau Mountain cliffs and the
amazing ocean views are the backdrop for this exhilirating moutain
biking experience. You'll cycle through the countryside to a
reconstructed traditional Hawaiian village, World War II bunkers, and
TV and movie locations for *Lost*, *50 First Dates*, *Jurassic Park*
and many more. This a full day actvity. With a variety of trails
available, each outing will be tailored to the skill level of those
partcipating. Back at the hotel, relax for the night and enjoy your
hotel amenities.

**Day 3 - Paddleboarding**

Interested
in surfing, but can't commit to the effort? Stand-up
paddleboarding is surfing made easy because the platform is wider,
longer, and more stable than a standard surfboard, and a long paddle makes
maneuvering simple. Riders can catch waves but also zip through smooth
water. On Oahu, the Turtle Bay Resort, Hans Hedeman, and BlueLine
Paddle Surf can arrange for lessons for singles and doubles. After your
paddleboarding session, stroll down Waikiki beach and enjoy its many
shops, restaurants, and bars. Make sure to stop by Duke Kahanamoku's
statue; it brings good luck.
**

Day 4 - Visit the North Shore**

Visit
the North Shore on Oahu, the world capital of surfing, just a 30 minute
drive from Waikiki. If you would like to spend the night here, the
oceanfront Ke Iki Beach Bungalows are family friendly accomodations
and conveniently located nearby the central street of Haleiwa, the main
town in the North Shore. Down the road from the mythical Pipeline,
Ted's Bakery is a casual eatery for surfers who enjoy its garlic
shrimp, mahi mahi sandwiches and rich Haupia chocolate pies.
**

Day 5 - Honolulu Academy of Arts and Contemporary Museum**

Visit
the Honolulu Academy of Arts and the Honolulu Contemporary Museum.
Since the doors opened April 8, 1927, the Honolulu Academy of Arts (900
South Beretania Street) has steadily grown to become Hawaiʻi’s
largest private presenter of visual arts programs, boasting a permanent
collection of over 40,000 works of art from cultures around the world.
The Honolulu Contemporary Museum (2411 Makiki Heights Dr) is the only
museum in the state of Hawaii devoted exclusively to contemporary art.
It provides an accessible forum for provocative, dynamic forms of
visual art, offering interaction with art and artists in a unique
island environment. Plan to have dinner at the Honolulu Contemporary
Museum. It's amazing.
**

Day 6 **-** Hang Gliding**
**
**Soar
over Oahu with The Original Glider Rides and experience bird's eye
views of Wai'anae Mountains, the North Shore, the Satellite Tracking
Station at Kaena Point, and the windsurfing sails at Mokule'ia. After
you've landed, book the Hike, Kayak, and Snorkel Adventure with Bike
Hawaii. This multi-sport outing starts with a short hike to a lookout
on Kaneohe Bay. After the hike, your guide will tell you about the
island's natural and geological history. Finally, your group will
proceed back to Kane'ohe Bay for a kayak trip to Mokolii Island. Once
there, you'll enjoy snorkeling, lunch and amazing views from Oahu from
the water. Feel free to take a well deserved night of rest back at your
hotel.**

Day 7 **- **Hiking and Snorkeling**
**
**
Take a 0.8 mile trail to the summit of
Diamond Head State Monument, Oahu's most recognizable landmark. You'll
reach the top in about 30 to 45 minutes depending on your pace and
you'll be rewarded with a stunning view spanning Koko Head to Waianae
from 760 feet above sea level. Bring walking shoes, a hat, sunscreen
and water. Try to go early to avoid crowds and blazing sun. In the
afternoon head to Hanauma Bay for the best and safest snorkeling in
Oahu's south shore. You can bring your own snorkeling equipment or rent
it there. They bay is open from 6 a.m. to 6 p.m. and is closed on
Tuesdays. On your final night in Oahu, book a luau dinner show at
Paradise Cove, offering most popular Hawaiian Luau Buffet packages.
