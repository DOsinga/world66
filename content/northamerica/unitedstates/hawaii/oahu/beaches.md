When visiting Oahu beaches, there is so
much more than just Waikiki. Waikiki Beach is the most developed beach
on Oahu Island, but if you have a couple of extra days, plan to visit
other breathtaking beaches on Oahu. Here's a guide to other Oahu
beaches.
**

Haleiwa

**
The world famous Banzai Pipeline Surfbreak is located nearby the Haleiwa surf town. To get there take the H1 West highway, then take the exit 8A to merge into H2 North highway towards Wahiawa/Mililani. Take exit 8 towards Wahiawa and then take the HI-99 Kamehameha highway for about 12 miles until you reach Haleiwa. At Haleiwa you will find several official and "unofficial" beach parks. Kalaka Bay Beach Park at 66-449 Haleiwa Road is an outdoor park that offers good facilities for the whole family. If Kalaka
Bay Beach Park doesn't appeal to you, then you can drive North
Kamehameha Highway towards Turtle Bay and experience over 10 beach
parks.**

Kailua Beach Park

**
One of the best kept secrets in town is Kailua Beach Park at 526 Kawailoa
Road because it combines sandy beaches with calm waters and great
conditions for kayaking enthusiasts. There are several kayak rental
shops in the Kailua area, which also offer bike rentals. During your stay at Kailua
Beach Park, you will understand why Oahu ranks high as one of the
fittest places in the world. Here you will find young and old are
biking, kayaking, surfing, running and diving. If all of this makes you
already tired, don'tworry Kailua Beach Park is full of quiet places for some well deserved and needed R&R.**

Hawaii Kai**

Hawaii
Kai is on the Eastern side of Oahu and is dotted with several beach
parks where is not unusual to find Japanese newlyweds getting their
wedding photographs take. The Sandy Beach Park at 8800 Kalanianaole Highway is known by the locals as Sandy's and is a great beach to have a picnic, and watch adventurous surfers and bodyboarders. This beach has rougher waters so we do not recommend to try bodyboarding there unless you are very advanced and have bodyboarding
fins. Sandy's offers a great opportunity to take amazing photographs of
pros showing off their skills. Another local favorite beach spot on the
Hawaii Kai area is the Makapuu Beach Park at 41-095 Kalanianaole Hwy. The surf break is not as rough as Sandy's but still be careful. From either beach, you will also be close to Queen's Beach Restaurant at 8902 Kalanianaole Hwy.**

White Plains

**White
Plains is a great and safe beach for those just trying out surfing for
their very first time. This beach owes it name to the obvious fact that
it is a bright and white beach. Some people refer to it as the "Waikiki
of the West Side" because there are small surf breaks everywhere, which
can be a bit disappointing for
advanced surfers but truly enjoyable for beginning surfers. The ocean
area is divided into a swimming and a surfing section, which makes it
an even more safer beach. To get to White Plains from Waikiki, you need
to get into the H1 West Highway towards Waianae for about 35 minutes, then take exit 2 towards Kapolei/Kalaeloa. At the Makakilo
Drive turn into Fort Barrette Road until you arrive into a 4 way stop
sign onto F.D. Roosevelt Road, then take a left. Take the fourth right
onto Coral Sea, then a left onto Tripoli and the first right takes you
to White Plains Beach. The drive sounds much more complicated than it
is actually.
