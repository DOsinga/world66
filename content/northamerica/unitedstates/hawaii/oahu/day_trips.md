Looking for a day trip idea during your stay in Oahu? Here are some ideas to get you started.**

Diving and Turtle Watching

**
If
you're looking for the best diving spots in the world, look no further
than Oahu! There are plenty of diving spots such as Electric Beach,
Shark's Cove and Hanuama Bay. Hanuama Bay is a marine sanctuary located in the direction of Hawaii Kai. You can get there easily by bus. Here you will find unintimidated reef fish, manta rays, sea turtles (honu in Hawaiian) and plenty of schools of colorful fish. Shark's Cove is an excellent snorkeling spot comparable to Hanauma Bay but free! It's a shallow reef cove almost exactly halfway in between Laie and Haliewa.
Near Shark's Cove, on the right side of Kamehameha Highway if you're heading from Shark's Cove to Haliewa
in the North Shore, there is a turtle habitat where Sea Turtles come us
to feel the warmth of the sun and the warmth of the tourist's flash
photography. You can also snorkel among the many sea turtles in this
spot as long as the waves are small. Please don't touch the sea
turtles, it's a federal offense and locals enforce this law aggressively.
**

Chase the (Movie) Stars**

Hawaii
has been the perfect setting for plenty of blockbusters so why not
visit actual movie sets! At the top of the list is the Hukilau Cafe, this is the restaurant where Adam Sandler
and Drew Barrymore supposedly met in the movie "50 First Dates".
Located in the North Shore, just about a block away from the Mormon
Temple, it is worth a visit not only for a great photo opportunity but
also for some amazing Spam and Eggs, as well as Banana Pancakes.

Another great stop in your date with movie stars is the Kualoa Ranch, where you can take ATV tours through lush, green valleys surrounded by the dramatic Kualoa mountains. The Kualoa
mountains appear in "Jurassic Park", "Pearl Harbor" and TV series
"Lost". Also horseback tours and Hummer tours are available. The Kualoa Ranch offers a great mix of Hawaiian Hollywood and of the great Hawaiian outdoors. **

Oahu's Chinatown

**Downtown
Honolulu is also known as Chinatown. Here you will get immersed in an
unique place that combines the old with the new, the East with the
West, the trendy with the kitschy. You will find a trendy clothing
store right next to a roast duck shop. This place is amazing! Oahu's
Chinatown is very friendly to pedestrians and not so friendly for
parking, so we recommend that you get there by bus. You will avoid time
wasted on finding parking and save money on expensive parking at the
downtown area. Carry sunblock lotion, a hat, and plenty of water
because it gets pretty hot in the afternoon. If you're lucky to visit
Oahu during the first Friday of a month, head to Oahu's Chinatown at 5
p.m. to experience "First Friday", a big block party where all the art
stores feature their work and provide complimentary wine (after a small
donation of $2 to $5). Don't miss it!
