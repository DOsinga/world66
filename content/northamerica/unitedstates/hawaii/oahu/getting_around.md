**E Komo Mai! Welcome to Oahu!** Getting
around the island of Oahu is really simple with The Bus. The Bus is the
name of the public bus transportation system in the island of Oahu and
its buses are very easy to identify because of the bright rainbows that
they have on the side. Unlike the other Hawaiian Islands, the island of
Oahu is the only one that has a formal bus system, completely set with
schedule and routes. So during your stay in Oahu, do take advantage of
it because it is not only economical but also very practical.

**Fare**The
Bus charges $2.00 per person and this fare includes one transfer ticket
that entitles you to one transfer (to any bus line) within 2 hours of
the purchase. Have your exact change ready because the bus drivers do
not provide change.

**Schedule**When
using The Bus, be aware that the service usually stops around midnight
and then resumes around 4 a.m. Please plan ahead your trip when using
The Bus. A very good resource is the Google
Transit web site because it includes all the bus routes and times of
the island of Oahu. If you miss a bus after midnight, we suggest that
you call a taxi cab. A very reliable company is The Cab at (808)
422-2222.

**
Major Stations**

The major stations of The Bus are the ones at Ala Moana Center, Waikiki Beach (Kuhio and Kalakaua Avenues) and Honolulu's Downtown (also known as Chinatown).

**Asking Directions to Locals**

When asking directions to locals, remember these 2 words: makai and makua. A common way to give directions in Hawaii involves the use of makai and mauka. Makai means heading towards the ocean or shore, while mauka means heading towards the mountain or inland.
