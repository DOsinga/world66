title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

Oahu, the third largest island in Hawaii is the home of
Honolulu. Filled with tourists, beaches and resorts there are lots of
places to go and things to do on Oahu. There’s a mix of crowded and
secluded beaches, plus many attractions such as Diamond Head and Valley of the
Temples here. Sure, there are a lot of fun things to do here all day
long, but if you’re looking for real fun you wait for the sun to go
down.

**Nightclubs and Bars**

There are countless nightclubs in this area. Most are aimed towards
the younger 20’s to 30’s ages and on certain nights require a cover
charge. They act as venues for different bands and DJs and on any
given night could have an event. To find out who is playing when you
can check the venue website or local papers on your visit.

**Play Bar**

Play Bar is one of the nightclubs on the island that is mostly
geared towards dancing and drinking. It’s a very casual club that
caters to a good mix of tourists and locals. There’s no dress code
here and they aren’t exclusive, so you shouldn’t have a problem getting
in. The minimum age is 19 to get in but they check for ID for drinks. Play Bar often has drink specials so you can drink
and party all night long if you wanted.

**Pipeline Cafe**

Pipeline Café is a warehouse that was turned into a nightclub. The
first floor is for dancing with a mix of DJs playing any given night.
The second floor is a sports bar with darts, pool, karaoke and a giant
television showing the various sporting events for that night. This is
another casual club that’s open to all patrons.

Other Nightclubs worth a visit are Rumors, Zanzabar and Ocean club.

**Other Nightlife**

Waikiki is the where all the action happens at all hours of the day
and night. If you’re really at a lost as to where to go you can just
walk up and down there and find a bar or club to your liking. Other
than bars you can also catch shows at various venues around there.
Waikiki Shell has a show there almost every night and if you’re lucky
you might be able to catch a free hula at one of the hotels or
luaus. Of course, the most popular nightlife activity is to sit on the
beach and watch the sun set.
