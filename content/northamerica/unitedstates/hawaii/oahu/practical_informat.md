**Locals versus Hawaiian**

In general, when referring to local people in Hawaii, refer to them as
locals not Hawaiians. Not all locals are native Hawaiians because Hawaii has an eclectic
mix of Eastern (Japanese, Chinese, Korean) and Western (Portuguese, Puerto
Rican) cultures. When something is specific to the native Hawaiian culture,
locals will surely point it out to you. Also, don't be afraid of the haole term
(used to refer to people of white skin complexion). Even though this term used
to be quite derogatory in the past, it has become a common term in day to day
Hawaiian culture.**
