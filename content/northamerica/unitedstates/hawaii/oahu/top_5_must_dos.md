E Komo Mai!
Welcome to the the Hawaiian Islands, where the expression "lucky we
live in Hawaii" is heard often. When it's sunny outside (which is most
of the year), there's no excuse to be cooped up indoors. An abundance
of beaches, hiking trails, bike and walking paths, and camping sites,
make the island of Oahu one of the best places in the world to be
physically active. Here's a list of the top 5 must do's during your stay in Oahu:**

Kayak to Mokulua Islands**

The beaches of Lanikai and Kalua
are local favorites for all kinds of water sports, ranging from ocean
swimming to snorkeling to surfing. The most recommended water sport in
this zone is kayaking because the Mokulua Islands (Moku Nui and Moku Iki) are just a one mile kayak ride away from the shore of Lanikai beach and a two mile kayak ride from the shore of Kailua beach. Remember to pack sunblock lotion, plenty of water and a life jacket. Snorkel gear is optional but we highly recommend it. The Mokulua Islands are both state bird sanctuaries for more than 12 different types of seabirds. Bird watchers and nature enthusiasts will be amazed.
**

Iolani Palace**

Located in the heart of Honolulu's downtown district, Iolani Palace is the perfect start point to start your journey around this area packed with
historical buildings and within walking distance of Oahu's Chinatown,
the King Kamehameha I statue and the Aloha Tower Marketplace. Guests
can visit this beautiful palace where King Kalakaua and his succesor,
Queen Liliuokalani held lavish parties and conducted diplomatic affairs
of the Kingdom of Hawaii. This palace holds plenty of historical tours
and items that belonged to the Kings and Queens of the Hawaiian
Islands. The Iolani Palace is open Tuesday through Saturday every week.
**

Ala Moana Center**

Wait. Didn't we say that Oahu is a place for outdoor activities? You got that right! Ala Moana Center is the largest outdoor shopping mall in the world with about 2.1 million square feet of shopping extravaganza. Opened on August 13th, 1959, the Ala Moana Center can sometimes have even more visitors than Disneyland throughout the year! Located on 1450 Ala Moana Boulevard (between Atkinson Drive and Piikoi
Street), it's very easy to access from more than 12 locations of the
the Pink Line Trolley on Waikiki Beach. If you're driving, there is
ample free parking on four levels.
**

Historic Haleiwa Surf Town**

The easiest way to arrive to Haleiwa is to drive the H1 highway heading west. When arriving to the exit 8A, merge into the H2 highway towards the North in direction of Wahiawa/Mililani. Then take exit 8 to Wahiawa and follow the HI-99 highway for 12 miles following the signs to Haleiwa. This historic surf town will welcome with a old wooden sign where a surfer is riding a wave. Home of the Surf Museum, the Banzai Pipeline Beaches and Matsumoto Shave Ice, Haleiwa offers entertainment for everybody. Don't worry ladies, the North Shore Marketplace offers plenty of options to shop until you drop!

**Hawaii Kai**

Located on the eastern side of Oahu, the region of Hawaii Kai offers an eclectic mix of outdoor shopping centers,
beach parks and water parks. There are several options to get to Hawaii
Kai: you can take The Bus ($2 each way with one free transfer within 2
hours), drive your rental car or rent one of the popular mini coupes
that can be found around Waikiki Beach. A good option to visit is the
Sea Life Park where you can swim with dolphins. Another one is Sandy Beach at 8800 Kalanianaole Highway, also known as Sandy's, is a great spot to view adventurous body boarders and surfers dare-deviling very rough surf breaks.
