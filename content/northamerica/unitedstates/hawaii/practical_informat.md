---
title: 'Practical Information'
type: section
---

**Location:** Hawaii is a chain of eight main islands in the middle of the Pacific Ocean -- the most isolated population center on Earth. It sits 2,390 miles from California, 3,850 miles from Japan, and 4,900 miles from China. The islands were formed by undersea volcanic eruptions over millions of years.

**Time Zone:** Hawaii Standard Time (HST), which is GMT-10. Hawaii does not observe Daylight Saving Time, so the offset from the US mainland changes with the seasons. When the West Coast is on PDT, Hawaii is three hours behind; when they switch back to PST, it is two hours behind.

**Capital:** [Honolulu](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/honolulu), on the island of [Oahu](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/oahu).

**Language:** Hawaii has two official state languages: English and Hawaiian. You will hear Hawaiian place names everywhere and see them on road signs. Pidgin (Hawaii Creole English) is widely spoken among locals. Samoan, Tongan, Japanese, and Tagalog are also common.

**Currency:** US Dollar ($). Credit cards are accepted everywhere on the main islands. ATMs are widely available.

**Telephone:** The US country code is 1 and the area code for all of Hawaii is 808. Dial 911 for emergencies.

**Islands:** The state consists of eight main islands. Six are accessible to visitors: [Oahu](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/oahu), [Maui](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/maui), the Big Island (Hawaii), [Kauai](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/kauai), [Molokai](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/molokai), and [Lanai](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii/lanai). Niihau is privately owned and Kahoolawe is uninhabited. Inter-island flights connect them all, and ferries run between some islands.

**Customs and Etiquette:** Remove your shoes before entering someone's home -- this is universal in Hawaii. Bring a small gift when visiting. Be aware that the word "Hawaiian" properly refers only to people of native Hawaiian ancestry; locals of other backgrounds are called "locals" or "residents," not "Hawaiians."

**Tipping:** Standard US tipping applies. 15-20% at restaurants, a dollar or two per drink at bars.
