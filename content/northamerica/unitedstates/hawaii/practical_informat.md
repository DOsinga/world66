**Location:** Hawaii is a cluster of 8 main islands in the middle of
the Pacific Ocean, and it is considered the most isolated population on
Earth (2,390 miles from California, 3,850 miles from Japan, 4,900 miles
from China, and 5,280 miles from the Philippines). Under water
volcanoes erupted thousands of years ago and formed the Hawaiian
islands.

**Time Zone:** Hawaiian Time Zone or GMT -10. Hawaii does not observe Daylight Saving Time.

**Capital: **Honolulu, located on the Island of Oahu.

**Language: **Hawaii has two official state languages, English and Hawaiian. Pidgin, Samoan, and Tongan are also spoken.

**Currency:** US Dollar ($)

**Population:** Approximately 1,288,198 according to the 2008 US Census.

**Telephone:** The country code for the US is 1, and the area code for
Hawaii is 808. Dial 911 for any police, fire, or hospital emergency.

**Highest Peak:** Mauna Kea 13796 feet

**Islands:** The State of Hawaii consists of 8 main islands: Hawaii
(also known as the Big Island), Maui, Lanai, Molokai, Oahu, Kauai,
Niihau, Kaho'olawe.

**Island Hoping:** It is possible to travel to up to six of the
islands by inter-island flights, and a couple of the islands can be
reached by boat. It is advisable to try to spend most of your time on
the island, as opposed to traveling to and from.

**Whale Watching:** Humpback whales migrate to Hawaii each year from
Alaska from December to May. The best place to see these whales is
their favorite spot, Kaanapali Shore in Maui.

**Volcanoes:** Kilauea volcano is the world's most active.

**Customs and Etiquette: **It is considered good manners to always
bring a small gift when visiting someones home, and most residents
take their shoes off before entering a home. It is rude to refer to
locals as Hawaiians, only native Hawaiians should be called Hawaiians.

**Weather: **Trade winds from the Pacific help to keep Hawaii's
temperature mild. The average temperature in Hawaii is 77 degrees
Fahrenheit, average temperature in January is 72 degrees Fahrenheit,
and average temperature in July is 82 degrees Fahrenheit. April to
November is considered peak season, and December to March is considered
low season - prices may vary significantly with hotels and resorts
depending on season.

**Produce:** Hawaii is the only state in the US that grows coffee, more
than 1/3 of the world's supply of pineapples come from Hawaii, and its
mild temperature also makes it possible to produce cane sugar, and
macadamia nuts.
