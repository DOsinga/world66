The United States is vast enough that a single trip can only scratch the surface. These are the experiences and destinations that stand out.

**New York City** remains one of the world's great cities — dense, diverse, and endlessly varied. The subway connects you to neighbourhoods that feel like different countries. Go beyond Manhattan: Brooklyn, Queens, and the Bronx each have their own distinct character. See [New York](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork).

**The national parks** are the country's great gift to itself. Yellowstone has geothermal wonders unlike anything else on earth. The Grand Canyon is genuinely humbling even if you've seen a thousand photographs. Zion's slot canyons and Yosemite's granite walls draw climbers and hikers from around the world. The national park system is vast — there are 63 parks covering deserts, glaciers, swamps, rainforests, and volcanic fields. See [Utah](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah), [Arizona](/northamerica/unitedstates/arizona), [California](/northamerica/unitedstates/california), [Wyoming](/northamerica/unitedstates/wyoming), and [Montana](/northamerica/unitedstates/montana).

**New Orleans** has a culture unlike anywhere else in North America — French and Spanish colonial history, African and Caribbean traditions, and a jazz heritage that underpins everything. The food alone justifies the trip: gumbo, po'boys, beignets, and some of the best cheap restaurants in the country. See [Louisiana](/northamerica/unitedstates/louisiana).

**The Pacific Coast Highway** from San Diego to Seattle is one of the world's great road trips. Big Sur's cliffs, San Francisco's fog, the Oregon coast's wild beaches, and the rain forests of the Olympic Peninsula all feature along the way.

**New England in autumn** — the fall foliage of Vermont, New Hampshire, and Maine is a genuine spectacle. Peak colour usually falls in late September in the north and mid-October further south. See [Vermont](/northamerica/unitedstates/vermont), [Maine](/northamerica/unitedstates/maine), and [Massachusetts](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts).

**The Deep South** offers Nashville's live music scene, Memphis's Beale Street and Graceland, the Mississippi Delta blues trail, and the food culture of the Carolinas. It is a region with a complex and difficult history that rewards honest engagement. See [Tennessee](/northamerica/unitedstates/tennessee), [Mississippi](/northamerica/unitedstates/mississippi), and [Louisiana](/northamerica/unitedstates/louisiana).

**Hawaii** is technically a state but feels like a separate destination — volcanic islands in the Pacific with a distinct Polynesian cultural heritage, extraordinary snorkelling and diving, and some of the most dramatic landscapes in the world. See [Hawaii](/northamerica/unitedstates/hawaii).

**Alaska** is the country's last great wilderness. Denali National Park, the Inside Passage, and the Arctic are accessible by cruise, rail, or small plane. Go in summer for long daylight hours; go in winter for the aurora borealis. See [Alaska](/northamerica/unitedstates/alaska).
