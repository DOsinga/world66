Rexburg is a small city in eastern Idaho, sitting in the Upper Snake River Plain near the Teton Range. It is dominated by Brigham Young University-Idaho, a private university operated by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, which gives the town a distinctly Mormon character and a young, clean-cut student population.

The city's most dramatic historical episode was the Teton Dam Flood of June 1976, when the newly built Teton Dam on the Teton River ruptured, sending a wall of water through northern Rexburg and submerging most of the city. The recovery was rapid, driven by community cooperation. The Teton Flood Museum, housed in the basement of the old Rexburg Tabernacle, documents the disaster and the history of the region.

Rexburg sits within easy reach of some exceptional scenery. Yellowstone National Park and Grand Teton National Park are both within a couple of hours' drive. The Teton Scenic Byway passes through here on its way south toward Driggs and the Wyoming border.
