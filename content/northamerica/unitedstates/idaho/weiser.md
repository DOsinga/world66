Weiser is a small agricultural town in southwest Idaho, sitting at the confluence of the Weiser River and the Snake River right on the Oregon border. It's an honest, unpretentious place — the kind of town where the main street still functions and people actually know each other. Most travelers who seek it out come for one reason: the fiddle music.

Every June, Weiser hosts the National Old Time Fiddlers' Contest, one of the most celebrated traditional music events in the United States. The competition has been running for over half a century, drawing fiddlers from across the country to compete in categories ranging from junior novice to senior champion. The week fills the town with music from morning to midnight — in the park, on porches, and in every available corner. It's genuinely worth going out of your way for.

Outside of festival week, Weiser is a quiet stop. The Snake River valley is beautiful in a wide-open, semi-arid way, and the surrounding farmland produces onions and other crops that have sustained the community for generations. If you're passing through on a road trip between Boise and Ontario, Oregon, it makes a pleasant half-day break.
