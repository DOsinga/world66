Alton is a small city of about 27,000 on the bluffs above the Mississippi River, just north of St. Louis across the Illinois border. It has a handsome 19th-century downtown, a good antique shopping scene along the main commercial streets, and historical significance as one of the sites of the Lincoln-Douglas debates of 1858. The debate marker is in downtown Alton.

The riverfront and the adjacent areas have a pleasant character for walking. Restaurant Week in mid-January is a local tradition for trying the city's independently owned restaurants at discounted prices. The **National Great Rivers Museum** at the Melvin Price Locks and Dam is worth a visit for anyone interested in the Mississippi River system.

Alton is an easy day trip from St. Louis (about 30 minutes) and is sometimes used as a base for visiting the nearby Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site in Illinois — the largest pre-Columbian settlement north of Mexico.
