This museum includes three levels of exhibits, featuring a popular multi-level Egyptology section (highly recommended) and MANY other permanent and rotating exhibits. The Field Museum is famous for housing the largest and most complete skeleton of a T. Rex ever found, dubbed Sue. 

The Museum was founded in 1893 and, in 1921, moved to it's current location on the Museum Campus, right next to the Adler Planetarium and the Shedd Aquarium in the southern part of downtown Chicago. Soldier Field is also in this area, so please note if there are any games being played during your visit. 

Open daily from 9am to 5pm. (Last admission at 4pm, but it is recommended that you give yourself at least 2-3 hours to see everything!) Open every day except Christmas. Parking available.
