Here are a few suggested hotspots.

### [Art Institute of Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago/sights/art_institute_of_c)

**The Art Institute of Chicago** hosts a collection covering 5,000 years of art. Events and exhibitions are hosted year-round (they are only closed on Thanksgiving and Christmas Day). The museum offers lectures, workshops, performances, and courses every day.

The AI is located centrally in Chicago, adjacent to Millennium Park. There is a suggested admission donation of $12 for adults, but it's acceptable to pay a little less.

### [Millennium Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago/sights/millennium_park_1)

**Millennium Park** is a good place to be in Chicago. It
has walking paths, flower gardens, "the bean" sculpture, is next to The
Art Institute, and sits between the city skyline and Lake Michigan.
It's a common place to find events year round - ice skating in winter,
concerts in summer, for example. It's very near to a lot of shopping
and restaurants. There is also a bar/cafe at the northwest entrance to
the park.

The Symphony Hall was designed by Frank Gehry,
completed in 1997, and has become a city landmark like the Buckingham
Fountain or the Picasso [more..](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago/sights/millennium_park_1)

### [Lincoln Park Zoo](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago/sights/lincoln_park_zoo)

**Lincoln Park Zoo** has as many events as it does exhibits. It's very family-oriented, with a huge petting zoo/living farm program. They do many presentations during the day, often in smaller, intimate groups - and often with animals.
**
**ADMISSION IS FREE.
Events are offered year round with a huge range of interests and age targets. There are also seven restaurants and three places to shop.
