Collinsville itself is unremarkable, but it is the site of Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site, the most important center of the Mississippian mound-building culture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. At its peak in the 11th and 12th centuries, over 20,000 people lived here. About 80 mounds survive, including Monks Mound -- the largest prehistoric earthen structure in North America at 100 feet high. The visitor center has a good introductory film and museum covering Mississippian life. Well worth a stop if you are in the St. Louis area.
