Galesburg is a small college town in western Illinois, about midway between Peoria and the Quad Cities on I-74. It is the home of Knox College, a well-regarded liberal arts school founded in 1837, and the birthplace of poet Carl Sandburg. The town has a quiet, slightly faded Main Street charm — old brick buildings, a busy railroad crossing, and the kind of place where people wave from their porches.

Knox College's Old Main is a National Historic Landmark and the only surviving site of the Lincoln-Douglas debates. Sandburg's modest birthplace on East Third Street is now a state historic site and museum, with a small park behind it where his ashes are buried under a granite boulder called Remembrance Rock. The Galesburg Railroad Museum, housed in a restored Pullman car, reflects the town's long history as a railroad junction — Amtrak's California Zephyr and Southwest Chief both stop here.

The Galesburg Historical Society Chocolate Festival in mid-February is a local highlight, with an all-you-can-eat buffet of tortes, cakes, pies, and candies. The Galesburg Antiques Mall is worth a browse for vintage furniture and collectibles. Seminary Street, near the college, has a cluster of restaurants and coffee shops — Innkeeper's is a reliable choice for a sit-down meal. The surrounding countryside is flat prairie farmland, and the sunsets over the cornfields west of town are genuinely beautiful.
