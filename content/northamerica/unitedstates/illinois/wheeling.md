Wheeling is a northwest suburb of [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago), about 30 miles from the Loop. On its own the town is quiet and suburban, but it has one genuine claim to fame: "Restaurant Row" along Milwaukee Avenue, where a remarkable concentration of restaurants -- many of them Korean, Indian, and Filipino -- draws diners from across the Chicago metropolitan area. If you are tired of downtown prices and want to eat very well for less, Wheeling is worth the drive.

The stretch of Milwaukee Avenue between Dundee Road and Lake-Cook Road is where the action is. Korean barbecue spots, South Indian dosa houses, and Filipino grills sit side by side with Mexican taquerias and Polish delis. The diversity reflects the town's immigrant communities, and the quality is high because these places are cooking for their own people, not for tourists. Lunch on a weekday is the best time to go -- you will have the places to yourself and the prices are even lower.

Beyond the restaurants, Wheeling has some pleasant parks and forest preserves, including the Des Plaines River Trail which passes through the western edge of town. But honestly, the food is the reason to come here. Plan your visit around an empty stomach.
