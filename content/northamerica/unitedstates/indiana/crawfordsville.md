Crawfordsville is a small city of around 15,000 in west-central Indiana, about halfway between Indianapolis and the Illinois state line off Interstate 74. It's the seat of Montgomery County and home to Wabash College, a well-regarded all-male liberal arts college that gives the town a certain intellectual edge unusual for a city this size.

The place owes most of its historical fame to Lew Wallace — Civil War general, first governor of New Mexico Territory, and the man who wrote *Ben-Hur: A Tale of the Christ* while living here. His study still stands as a museum. The Ropkey Armor Museum, a few miles outside town, houses one of the largest private collections of tanks and armored vehicles in the world — an unlikely but genuinely impressive sight.

Sugar Creek runs through the edge of town and provides the starting point for canoe trips downstream through Shades and Turkey Run state parks, both worth a visit. Every June, the city hosts the Strawberry Festival, a lively community event that draws a decent crowd.
