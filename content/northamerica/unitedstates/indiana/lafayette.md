Lafayette and its twin city West Lafayette sit on the Wabash River in central Indiana. West Lafayette is home to Purdue University, which dominates the local economy and gives the area a college-town energy — good restaurants, decent nightlife during the school year, and a young population.

The most interesting thing for visitors is the historical connection: the city is named after the Marquis de Lafayette, and the Tippecanoe Battlefield just north of town is where William Henry Harrison defeated Tecumseh's confederacy in 1811. The battlefield park and museum are worth a stop if you are passing through on I-65 between Indianapolis and Chicago.
