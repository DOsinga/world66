Porter is a small town on the southern shore of Lake Michigan, about 50 miles southeast of Chicago. Its main claim to interest is its location inside Indiana Dunes National Park, which stretches for 25 miles along the lakeshore.

The dunes are the draw. Miles of sandy beach, forested dunes up to 60 metres high, bogs, wetlands, and oak savanna create a surprising diversity of landscape within sight of the Chicago skyline on a clear day. The park ranks seventh among US national parks for plant diversity — over 1,400 species have been recorded, including some that are threatened or endangered in Indiana.

The Dunes Succession Trail near Porter gives a good introduction to the ecology that made this area famous: Dr. Henry Cowles conducted his pioneering studies on ecological succession here in the early 1900s, essentially inventing the field. The beach itself is wide and swimmable in summer, though Lake Michigan water stays cold.

Porter is easily reached by train from Chicago on the South Shore Line, making the dunes a practical day trip from the city.
