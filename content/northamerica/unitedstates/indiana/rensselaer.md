Rensselaer is a small city of around 4,000 people just off I-65 amid the plains of northern Indiana. Amtrak still makes stops here, though only two daily. The town's name comes from a man named Rensselaer who was involved with the railroad when it first came through. The buns for the White Castle hamburger chain are baked here, which helps keep the local economy stable.

Rensselaer is home to St. Joseph's College of Indiana, a small private Catholic college. For food, there is an Arni's pizza parlor, a Chinese restaurant, and the Busy Bee, a seasonal ice cream parlor.
