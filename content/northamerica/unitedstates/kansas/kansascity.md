Kansas City — "KC" — is an American metropolis with a state line running down the middle of it. Kansas City, Missouri is the larger and better-known half; Kansas City, Kansas (affectionately "KCK") sits just across the border. Dozens of suburbs in both states fill out the metropolitan area.

The city is famous for its barbecue — slow-smoked, heavily sauced, and the subject of fierce local rivalries — and for its jazz heritage, which dates back to the 1920s and 30s when the 18th and Vine district was one of the centres of American jazz. The Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art is world-class and free. Kauffman Stadium, home of the Kansas City Royals, and Arrowhead Stadium next door give the city one of the best sports complexes in the country.
