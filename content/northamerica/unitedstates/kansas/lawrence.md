Lawrence is a university town in eastern [Kansas](/northamerica/unitedstates/kansas), home to the University of Kansas and one of the most charming small cities in the state. The National Trust for Historic Preservation once named it among America's "Dozen Most Distinctive Destinations," and the praise is deserved.

Massachusetts Street — "Mass Street" to locals — is the heart of it: a well-preserved downtown strip lined with antiques shops, independent bookstores, cafes, and live music venues. The university campus, set on a hill above town, adds a youthful energy, and the local music scene has long punched above its weight. Lawrence was also the site of Quantrill's Raid in 1863, one of the most notorious events of the Civil War, and the town's history runs deeper than its easygoing surface suggests.
