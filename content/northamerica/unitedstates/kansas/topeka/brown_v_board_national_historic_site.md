The former Monroe Elementary School, where Linda Brown was denied enrollment because she was Black, is now a national historic site commemorating the Supreme Court decision that ended legal school segregation. The exhibits are well-done and the building itself — with its small classrooms, narrow hallways, and period furnishings — makes the human scale of the story vivid. This is the most significant historical site in Topeka.
