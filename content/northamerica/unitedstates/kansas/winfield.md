Winfield is a small town of around 12,000 people in south-central Kansas, once listed among the hundred best small towns in America. It is probably most famous for the Walnut Valley Festival, held each September, which draws thousands of musicians and fans for the National Bluegrass Banjo Championships, the National Guitar Flat Picking Championships, and competitions in fiddle, hammered dulcimer, and mandolin. During festival weekend the whole town comes alive with yard sales and impromptu picking sessions.

The rest of the year Winfield is a quiet place with a historic downtown, a college campus, and more churches per capita than most towns its size — a legacy of the Chautauqua movement that flourished here in the late 1800s.
