Lexington has something for those with a love for equine, a taste for bourbon, or an interest in history. Horse enthusiasts shouldn’t miss a trip to Kentucky Horse Park where they can view many different breeds, a working horse farm, and even take a horseback or carriage ride. Bourbon drinkers shouldn’t miss the Bourbon Festival, held the third week of every September. History buffs cannot miss the Lexington History Museum. There you can view the “Lincoln and His Wife’s Hometown” exhibit and learn about how Lexington influenced this would-be president. Complete your stay in Lexington at the Embassy Suite, situated on the University of Kentucky Coldstream Research Campus. It is close to many attractions and offers spacious rooms, a cooked to order breakfast, and dinning at the hotel’s Paddock Grille. You can also choose a more luxury chain like the Hyatt or the Griffin Gate Marriott. If its budget you are concerned about, they the Springhill Suites on the Red Mile.
