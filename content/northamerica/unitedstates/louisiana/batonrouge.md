Baton Rouge is Louisiana's state capital and second-largest city, sitting on the east bank of the Mississippi River about 80 miles northwest of New Orleans. It's a working city — the River Road chemical plants and oil refineries that line the Mississippi here are the industrial backbone of Louisiana's economy — but it has a distinct character, especially around the LSU campus and the old downtown.

The city has two capitol buildings. The **New State Capitol**, completed in 1932 under Governor Huey Long, is a 34-storey Art Deco tower — the tallest state capitol building in the US — with an observation deck and a hall of outsized governor portraits inside. Huey Long was assassinated in the building in 1935; the bullet holes are still visible in the marble. The **Old State Capitol**, a neo-Gothic castle on the bluff above the river, was once called a "mortified carbuncle" by Mark Twain; now restored, it houses a museum of Louisiana political history.

Louisiana State University (LSU) is one of the South's major universities, with a sprawling campus and a football culture that dominates autumn weekends in the city. The surrounding streets have the best concentration of bars and restaurants in Baton Rouge.
