New Iberia sits in the heart of Cajun Country, along Bayou Teche in southern Louisiana. It is a small, unhurried city where the French-speaking Acadian heritage still runs deep -- in the food, the music, and the easygoing pace of life along the bayou.

The main draw is **Avery Island**, just south of town, home to the Tabasco factory and the lush Jungle Gardens. **Shadows-on-the-Teche**, a restored antebellum plantation house on Main Street, offers a window into the area's sugar-planting past. The streets of downtown New Iberia are walkable and pleasant, lined with old oaks and a handful of good restaurants specializing in the Cajun and Creole cooking that this part of Louisiana does better than anywhere else.

New Iberia also serves as a good base for exploring the wider Acadiana region, including [Lafayette](/northamerica/unitedstates/louisiana/lafayette) to the north and the swamps and marshes of the Atchafalaya Basin to the east.
