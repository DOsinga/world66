A small town about 110 miles northwest of New Orleans (closer to Baton Rouge), worth the drive for its extraordinary concentration of antebellum plantation homes. Rosedown Plantation has some of the finest surviving formal gardens in the South. The Myrtles, draped in cast-iron lacework, claims to be one of America's most haunted houses. Oakley House is where Audubon painted many of his bird studies — the surrounding woods are still full of the species he documented.

The town itself is walkable and pleasant, with a handful of antique shops along Royal Street and Ferdinand Street. Allow a full day if you want to tour more than one house.
