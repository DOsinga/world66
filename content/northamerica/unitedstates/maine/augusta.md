Augusta has been Maine's capital since 1827, seven years after statehood. The Capitol Building anchors a small city that serves as the center of government and commerce for central Maine.

Most points of interest are downtown. Fort Western, built in 1754 to protect settlers from French and Indian raids, sits on the Kennebec River and is the oldest surviving wooden fort in New England. Other worthwhile stops include Blaine House (the governor's mansion), Capitol Park, the Children's Discovery Museum, and the Maine State Museum, which focuses on native inhabitants and 19th-century industry. The Pine Tree State Arboretum covers 224 acres and is particularly good in fall. [Belgrade Lakes](/northamerica/unitedstates/maine/belgrade_lakes) is nearby and makes a fine day trip.
