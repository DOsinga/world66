Bridgton sits in the heart of western Maine's lakes and mountains region, roughly equidistant from [Portland](/northamerica/unitedstates/maine/portland), Freeport, and North Conway, New Hampshire. It is a genuine four-season town: swimming and canoeing on Long Lake and Highland Lake in summer, spectacular foliage drives in fall, and skiing at Shawnee Peak and snowmobiling on a vast trail network in winter. The surrounding lakes and clean mountain air have drawn families here for generations, and the town still has the feel of old-fashioned New England summer vacations.

Downtown Bridgton is small but has a good cluster of shops and restaurants along Main Street. The Bridgton Twin Drive-In is one of the few remaining drive-in theaters in New England and is worth a visit on a warm summer night. The town also has a surprisingly good community theater and a farmers market that runs through the warmer months.

If you are based here, the lakes are the main attraction. Long Lake stretches north from town and is good for kayaking, fishing, and swimming. Pleasant Mountain (home to Shawnee Peak ski area) rises to the west and offers hiking trails with views across the lakes region all the way to Mount Washington on a clear day. Bridgton is also well positioned as a base for day trips to the coast or the White Mountains -- both are about an hour's drive.
