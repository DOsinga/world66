Eastport is the easternmost city in the United States, perched on Moose Island at the mouth of the Bay of Fundy where the tides are among the highest on the American coast -- over 20 feet between high and low water. The town has a quiet, end-of-the-road character that appeals to artists, writers, and travelers looking to get genuinely off the beaten path. You are closer to New Brunswick, Canada than to any city in Maine.

The downtown waterfront is compact and walkable, with a mix of art galleries, a good bookshop, and a handful of restaurants serving fresh seafood. The Tides Institute and Museum of Art is worth a visit for its focus on art and culture of the Passamaquoddy Bay region. In summer, whale-watching tours head out into the Bay of Fundy where you have good chances of spotting finback and minke whales, porpoises, and bald eagles. The Old Sow whirlpool, the largest tidal whirlpool in the Western Hemisphere, churns in the waters between Eastport and Deer Island.

Getting here requires commitment -- Eastport is about three hours from [Bangor](/northamerica/unitedstates/maine/bangor) and the last stretch of Route 190 crosses a causeway to Moose Island. But that remoteness is the point. The town hosts a lively Fourth of July celebration and a pirate festival in September. In winter it is very quiet indeed, but the light on the water and the drama of the tides make it worth a visit in any season.
