Fryeburg is a small town on the Maine-New Hampshire border, sitting in a broad valley of the Saco River with the western Maine foothills rising on all sides. It is best known for the Fryeburg Fair, the largest agricultural fair in New England, held during the first week of October. The fair draws huge crowds for livestock competitions, harness racing, agricultural exhibits, and the kind of fried food you can only get at a proper country fair. If you are anywhere in the region in early October, it is worth planning around.

Outside of fair season, the Saco River is the main draw. The stretch through Fryeburg is calm and sandy-bottomed, ideal for canoeing, kayaking, and tubing. Several outfitters in town rent canoes and run shuttle services for multi-day trips downriver. The river corridor is beautiful -- hemlock and pine forests, sandy beaches for lunch stops, and very few signs of development.

In winter, Fryeburg serves as an alternative base for skiing at Shawnee Peak or the larger resorts across the border in New Hampshire's White Mountains. The town itself is quiet and rural, with a handsome old academy (Fryeburg Academy, founded in 1792) and a small downtown. Daniel Webster once taught school here, which is about as exciting as the local history gets -- but that is rather the point of Fryeburg.
