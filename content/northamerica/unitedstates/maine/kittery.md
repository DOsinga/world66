Kittery is the gateway to Maine -- the first town you hit after crossing the Piscataqua Bridge from New Hampshire. Most visitors know it for one thing: outlet shopping. Over 120 retail stores line Route 1, and Bostonians flock here on weekends (Massachusetts plates outnumber Maine plates as a rule).

But Kittery is also Maine's oldest town, settled in 1623. In 1905 the Treaty of Portsmouth was signed at the Kittery Navy Yard, ending the Russo-Japanese War. Once you leave the shopping corridor, you find a genuine New England town with real history and coastal beauty. Locals have mixed feelings about the outlet strip -- it generates revenue and jobs, but the traffic backs up for miles and the character of the old town gets lost in the shuffle.
