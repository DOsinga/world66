Planning a **Portland, Maine day trip **can enrich your visit to
the largest city in the state, by adding new sights and memories to your
experience. See more of the state and the surrounding area by planning
any of these day trips:

**The Small, Old-World Town Experience: Bath, Maine**

A little more than 30 miles northeast of Portland is Bath, which you
can reach after about 45 minutes of driving, or by hopping on a bus that
will get you there in 50 minutes for as little as $13 each way. Bath is
a town of fewer than 10,000 residents along the Kennebec River. The town has plenty of old-world sites, particularly those of importance to the industries of shipbuilding, brassworks, ironworks and lumber. Three styles of architecture dominate the area:
Greek Revival, Federal and Italianate. The Library Park and harbor make
for excellent strolling locations when the weather is pleasant.

**The Large, Old-World City: Boston**

Despite being two states away, Boston is actually only about 110
miles southwest of Portland. You can drive there in about 1 hour and 45
minutes, but you can also hop on a bus that takes around 2 hours for
$26 each way ($18 if you purchase tickets online) or a train that takes 2 1/2 hours for $24 each way.

Boston is among the oldest established cities in the country, so
although it's home to more than 600,000 residents, it offers plenty of
old-world experiences. You can also enjoy the modern conveniences
of metropolitan areas, so this makes a great day trip destination if
you don't mind crowds but still appreciate historical sites. Boston has
zoos, museums, historical sites and JFK's presidential library to offer.
There are also plenty of parks and beaches for outdoor activities.
Don't forget to catch a show at an opera or theater.

**The Great Outdoors: White Mountain National Forest**

The White Mountain National Forest takes up almost 785,000 acres of
land across mainly New Hampshire and part of Maine. There are numerous entry points, but you can settle in Bartlett, New Hampshire in the middle of the
forest by driving only 76 miles (about 90 minutes) northwest of
Portland. Because the expanse is so purposely rural, it can be
difficult to get there via bus or train, although you can take them to
nearby cities and rent cars from there.

The White Mountain National Forest is the perfect day trip for anyone who loves outdoor activities, particularly hiking, skiing and camping. One hundred miles of the Appalachian Trail traverse the forest that some 6 million people visit each year.
