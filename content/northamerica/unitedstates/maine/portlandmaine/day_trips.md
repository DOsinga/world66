---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

Portland's location on the southern Maine coast puts several worthwhile destinations within easy reach.

**Bath** is about 45 minutes northeast -- a town of fewer than 10,000 along the Kennebec River, known for shipbuilding heritage and fine examples of Greek Revival, Federal, and Italianate architecture. Library Park and the harbor make for good walking.

**Boston** is only about 110 miles southwest, reachable in under two hours by car or bus. Despite being two states away, it is a practical day trip for anyone wanting big-city museums, historical sites, and restaurants.

**White Mountain National Forest** covers nearly 785,000 acres across New Hampshire and into Maine. Bartlett, New Hampshire, in the middle of the forest, is about 90 minutes northwest of Portland -- a good base for hiking or skiing depending on the season.
