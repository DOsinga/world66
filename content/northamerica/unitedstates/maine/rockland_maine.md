Rockland bills itself as the lobster capital of the world, and during the annual Maine Lobster Festival in August, the claim is hard to argue. The downtown main street is lined with independent shops and restaurants, and the harbor is home to a fleet of historic windjammer sailing ships that offer multi-day cruises along the coast.

The Breakwater Lighthouse, reached by walking a mile-long granite breakwater into Rockland Harbor, is one of the most photographed spots on the Maine coast. The Maine Lighthouse Museum downtown covers the state's extensive lighthouse heritage. Summer and fall bring festivals nearly every weekend.
