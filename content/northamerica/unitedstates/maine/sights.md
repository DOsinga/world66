Visit Maine and receive the
first rays of sunshine to hit the U.S. The land in the state of Maine is 90% forest and
has more than five thousand miles of spectacular coastline. There are enough
state parks in Maine
that you could visit a different on every day for a month. Maine
is famous for their seafood and their lobster is known as the best in the world. Portland, on the south coast of Maine,
is the capital city and a popular destination for tourists on a tour of Maine.**

Cape
Elizabeth**

Less than 4 miles beyond Portland in the town of Cape
Elizabeth you can find Fort Williams Park which is known for Cape Elizabeth’s
Portland Headlight, Fort Williams Park and ruins of an old fort which was
active in WWI and WWII. Almost a million people visit here each year. The Cape Elizabeth’s
Portland Headlight is the oldest lighthouse in Maine and one of the top photographed
lighthouses in the world; you will recognize it when you see it. More than ninety acres, Fort Williams Park
has hiking trails the ruins.**

Two Lights State Park – Cape Elizabeth**

The best feature of Two Lights State Park is its more than forty acres
of scenic rocky coastline. As its name suggests there are two lighthouses here,
the first twin lighthouses in Maine. Cook a delicious BBQ in the large picnic
area and enjoy the spectacular view of the Atlantic Ocean. Cool and refreshing
breezes blow in from these waters, so bring a long sleeve of some kind. This
park is open year round and there is a fee.**

Downtown Arts District**

This area of downtown Portland
is home to the cities cultural attractions. The Portland Museum of Art, Portland
Performing Arts Center, and the Symphony Orchestra are here. At the Portland
Museum of Art there are over 17,000 fine and decorative art works. The price of
tickets is reasonable and kids up to 17 receive a considerable discount, it’s
open on Sunday’s too and wheelchairs are free.

The Portland Performing Arts Center puts on over nine
hundred performances a year. So there is sure to be a show when you are in town.
It is home to the Portland Stage Company, who produces seven plays during the
production season which is May through September. Get to the show early and don't confuse Forest Street
with Forest Avenue.
