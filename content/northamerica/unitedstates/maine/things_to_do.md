---
title: "Things to do in Maine"
type: section
---

The state of Maine
is almost completely covered in forests and it boasts more than 5,000 miles of
scenic coastlines. There are plenty of things to do other than eating their
famous and succulent, Maine
lobster. Maine
offers an exciting variety of outdoors activities both in and out of the water.

**Mount Katahdin in Baxter
State Park**

The highest point in the state of Main is Mount Katahdin
located in Baxter State Park. Mount
Katahdin rises more than five thousand feet above sea level. You don’t need to
climb to the top to get amazing panoramic views. From the campsite Chimney Pond,
the Saddle Trail is not the shortest but is the easiest and most traveled. It
is a little over two miles. Those who are more adventurous can scale the Dudley
Trail to traverse the Knife Edge. The number of climbers who are permitted to
ascend the mountain is monitored closely. At different times of the year there
are different gear requirements. Get in line early in the morning before 6am.

**Canoe Trips down the Allagash Waterway **

More than ninety miles of waterways make up the Allagash
Waterway. This is a restricted zone owned by the State of Maine. Snowmobiling, fishing and canoeing
are popular activities here with both tourists and locals. If you want to go
canoeing for a few hours one afternoon or for a whole week, the Allagash
Waterway can accommodate you. Other popular rivers that are exhilarant to canoe
are the St. Croix, the Penobscot River, Moose River, St.
John’s River, Kennebec River
and the Sandy River.

**Sailing the Atlantic**

If you know how to sail, you can rent a boat and experience
the coast of Maine
from the outside looking in. There are several locations between the capital
city of Portland and the small town of Wiscasset
where you can rent sailboats. To rent a sailboat you must be over 21, have
sailed previously and know how to chart read. A credit card is required for a
deposit. Most sailboat rental places have strict no alcohol policies.

**Horseback Riding**

Trail riding on horseback is available in most areas in the
state of Maine.
You can take a short trip to the ice cream store on horseback or go for a
scenic guided trail ride through the woods. Even if you have never ridden a
horse before you will be O.K. because brief but adequate instructions are
provided. Don’t worry, the horse rides the trail all the time and knows exactly
what to do.
