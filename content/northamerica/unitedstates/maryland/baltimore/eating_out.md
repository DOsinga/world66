---
title: "Eating Out in Baltimore"
type: section
---

Baltimore has no shortage of yummy eateries! You can dine
downtown, in the suburbs, or outside at bayside restaurants. Many
restaurants are closed Monday and Tuesday – call ahead to make
reservations.

**Downtown & Little Italy Area (walking distance of Convention Center)**

**Pazo**,
(410) 534-7296, 1425 Aliceanna St. $$. Reservations strongly suggested.
Modern Italian decor with DJ spun club tunes, this is the place to take
your Friday night date. Plates are small, but two to three plates per
person is more than enough. Definitely try the sparkling side of fresh
anchovies, grilled octopus salad, and roasted almonds. Also check out
the sausages and variety of Italian bread. For those on a budget, wines
are affordable at $16/bottle.

**Blue Sea Grill**,
614 Water Street, 410-837-7300. $$$. Decorated to resemble the bottom
of an ocean, with soft curves and aqua coloring, Blue Sea is the best
spot to entertain your business colleagues or impress your parents.
Enjoy the rich crab and artichoke dip or white sturgeon caviar at the
bar. Follow this with the catch of the day or creamy, moist
herb-roasted salmon (cooked to perfection here!). Children will love
the homemade macaroni and cheese mixed with hunks of lobster.
Excellent-rated service and wines. Still hungry for dessert? Try the
marvelous upside down pineapple cake glazed with caramelized cream.

**Vaccaro's**,
222 Albemarle Street, (410) 685-4905. $. An authentic Italian deli,
Vaccaro’s is the world’s number one in Italian pastries, according to
Google. Their giant cannoli, enormous tiramisu, and gelatos are not to
miss. The perfect place for an afternoon snack. Go before dinner as
lines are known to stretch for a block or so.

**Fells Point Area**

This is quintessential Baltimore, an old city harbor where yuppie bars meet sailor joints and business meets causal.

**Kali's Court**,
1606 Thames Street, 410-276-4700. $$$. With its gorgeous mahogany and
brick walls, Kali’s rated as one of the nicest seafood dining areas in
all of Baltimore. The fish is always fresh and prepared with unique
sauces. The raw oyster plate, with fresh and juicy bivalves is a must
try as is the albino anchovy salad. Try the baklava for dessert!

**Pierpoint**,
(410) 675-2080, (410) 563-2855, 1822 Aliceanna Street. $$. Pierpoint
features Maryland’s – and arguably the world’s- best crab cakes. They
are smoked and come with mustardy brussel sprout slaw (and this is not
only edible but delicious!). The rack of lamb is also worthy of trying
as is the Maryland seafood bouillabaisse.

**North Charles Street**** & Johns Hopkins Neighborhood**

Reachable by car, North Charles Street feature some of Baltimore’s
best restaurants. Definitely worth the 5-15 minute drive out of
downtown.

**Helmand**,
806 N. Charles Street, 410-752-0311. $$. Owned by the Karzii brothers
(one, Hamid Karzai who is currently the president of Afghanistan),
Hemland is one of the most popular upscale restaurants in the city.
For an
appetizer, try the Aushak or Bowlani. Perhaps you’d prefer the tasty
beef-filled pastry accompanied by yellow split peas, or the
twice-cooked baby pumpkin with yogurt sauce (kaddo borawni). Best
entrees include the rich lamb with tomato sauce and creamy spinach, the
Mourgh Challow, and Seekh Kabob. Try the Turkish coffee as an
after-dinner drink (liquor optional).

**Tapas Teatro Cafe**,
(410) 332-0110, 1711 N Charles Street. $-$$. Start off with a pitcher
of sangaria, the chorizo-studded risotto cakes, and oven-roasted
eggplant. For entrée, customers have raved over the Serrano ham and
grilled lamb chops with rhubarb sauce. Sautéed spinach decorated with
pine nuts and raisins and the duck confit on flatbread with bean puree
is also worth a try. Meals are served on small plats, tapa style so you
can get a lot for your buck.
