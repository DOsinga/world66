Built in 1880, this small stone building served as the segregated schoolhouse for Black children in Howard County during the Jim Crow era. It operated as a school until the mid-20th century and now functions as a community museum documenting African American history in Howard County. It's a modest building but an honest one — a counterweight to the more celebrated stories of the Ellicott family and the railroad.

Open on select weekends; check ahead before visiting.
