St. Michaels is a small, well-preserved harbour town on [Maryland](/northamerica/unitedstates/maryland)'s Eastern Shore, sitting between the Miles River and Broad Creek on the Chesapeake Bay. It dates from the colonial era and still has the feel of a working waterfront town, with crabbers and oystermen going out in the morning and tourists filling the restaurants by evening.

The Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum, spread across 18 acres on the harbour, is the main attraction — a collection of historic boats, a working boatyard, and exhibits on the bay's ecology and waterman culture. The town itself is a pleasant walk: handsome colonial, Federal, and Victorian houses line the streets, and the shops run to antiques, art galleries, and specialty food stores. The seafood, fresh from the bay, is the main reason to eat here — crab cakes, oysters, and rockfish are the staples.

St. Michaels is about 75 miles from [Washington, D.C.](/northamerica/unitedstates/washingtondc) and [Baltimore](/northamerica/unitedstates/maryland/baltimore), making it a popular weekend escape. Nearby Tilghman Island and Oxford are worth a half-day side trip.
