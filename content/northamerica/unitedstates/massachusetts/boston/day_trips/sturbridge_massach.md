This tiny town of just over 2,000 residents is home to the largest history museum in the Northeast. Old Sturbridge Village is a living history museum featuring 200 acres and more than 40 original buildings, with collections, exhibits, and programs presenting the story of everyday life in a small New England town from 1790 to 1840. It attracts roughly a million visitors each year. About 90 minutes west of Boston.
