The Massachusetts Turnpike (I-90) is the main artery into Boston from all points west. It is a toll road and accepts EZ-Pass (known locally as Fast Lane). For those traveling up or down the East Coast, I-95 intersects with the Mass Pike about 15 minutes west of the city.

Boston is notorious for highway congestion. During rush hours and Friday afternoons it can take a long time to get into or out of the city. Most visitors switch to mass transit after arrival. Route 93 provides north-south access, and after the Big Dig project, now runs through a tunnel under downtown.
