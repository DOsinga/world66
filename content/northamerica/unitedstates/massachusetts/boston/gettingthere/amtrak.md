Amtrak serves four locations in the immediate Boston area; North Station, South Station, Back Bay, and Route 128. South Station also hosts the newer high-speed Acela-class trains that Amtrak operates along the Northeast Corridor between Boston and Washington DC.

Trains from Boston to New Hampshire and Maine (which Amtrak calls "Downeasters") operate out of North Station only. Connections between North Station and South Station ***are the customer's responsibility*** as there is no direct connection between the two stations via mass transit. This has historically been a sore spot for Boston commuters and improvements are planned ... someday.
