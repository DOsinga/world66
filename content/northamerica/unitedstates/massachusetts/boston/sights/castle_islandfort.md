Castle Island is one of many of the Boston Harbor Islands that isn't exactly an island anymore. It sticks out into the harbor, but is conveniently connected to a residential part of South Boston. Castle Island is home to Fort Independence, built in the early eighteenth century (on the site of several earlier fortresses) and used in the War of 1812. The fort is open on some weekends, weather permitting, in the summer for tours. Aside from the fort, there are wide paths with numerous benches and grassy knolls for walking, jogging, or relaxing by the shore. When the wind is from the northeast, north, or east, it's also a great place to watch incoming aircraft land at Logan International Airport, just across the harbor. Castle Island is now considerably easier to access thanks to the opening of the T's Silver Line extension. Take the Silver Line from South Station to City Point or the Marine Industrial Park, and you'll be in the right neighborhood. It's easily reached by car, but parking may be scarce on a summer weekend.
