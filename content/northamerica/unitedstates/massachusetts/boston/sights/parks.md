In Boston, you can find the oldest park of the United States: Boston Common. Although it has become a 'normal' park now, in the former days it resembled London's Hyde park -that is, the way you could and still can find people exclamating on foggy Sunday mornings. It has a tradition as a place where people could exercise their right to freedom of speech.


 Boston's Public Gardens are between Arlington and Charles Streets. It's a nice place for boat rides during summer and ice skating in winter.


 If you have the chance, make the effort to go to the Cordova Museum and Sculpture Park in Lincoln, west of Boston. On the Victorian estate (Sandy Pond Road 51, Lincoln), you will find a fine exhibition of 20th-century painting and sculpture.
