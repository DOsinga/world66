**Cambridge** sits just across the Charles River from [Boston](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/boston), but it is its own city with its own character — more bookish, more politically progressive, and in places more interesting than its larger neighbour.

Two of the world's most famous universities dominate the landscape. Harvard Yard is the obvious draw, though Harvard Square itself has grown touristy. More rewarding is a walk through the quieter residential streets of mid-Cambridge or along the riverbank toward MIT, where the campus architecture ranges from stately to deliberately strange (see Frank Gehry's Stata Center).

Central Square and Inman Square have better food and more local character than Harvard Square. The city is compact and walkable, and the Red Line connects it directly to downtown Boston. Locals call themselves Cantabrigians — or, informally, Cantabs.
