Scene of one of the historic battles of the American Revolution, Concord is a wonderful day trip from [Boston](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/boston). With many local shops and historic landmarks, it makes a great excursion -- roughly 45 minutes outside of Boston by car. The Old North Bridge, where the famous "shot heard round the world" was fired, is the main draw, but the literary history is just as rich: Emerson, Thoreau, Hawthorne, and Alcott all lived here.
