### [The New Olympia Restaurant](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/lowell/eating_out/the_new_olympia_re)

The New Olympia Restaurant (the "New" is typically not used when talking about "The Olympia" with locals) is Lowell's oldest restaurant to be owned and operated by the same family. It was opened in 1952 by the Tingas and Cocalis families, and is now run by the originators children.

The menu consists of traditional Greek fare (baked lamb, mousaka, pastitsio), as well as American (steak, clam chowder, baked stuffed haddock) and some Italian (fried calamari, shrimp scampi).

In addition to the dining room, the Olympia has the Zorba room available for private functions.

### [Athenian Corner](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/lowell/eating_out/athenian_corner)

Open and run by the Panagiotopoulos family since 1974, Athenian Corner is one of the best examples of Greek food and culture in Lowell.

In addition to an extensive menu of Greek and American cuisine, Athenian Corner offers live entertainment every Thursday through Saturday. The Fred Elias ensemble plays Greek and Middle Eastern music and belly dancers "captivate your heart" (as it says on their website) while you dine.
