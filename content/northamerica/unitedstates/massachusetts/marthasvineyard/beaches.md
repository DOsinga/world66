---
title: "Beaches in Martha's Vineyard"
type: section
---

Martha’s Vineyard has long been a favorite New England vacation
destination primarily because of the over 125 miles of coastline and
beaches. There are several beaches from which to choose, all who have
their own personality and flavor.

Several beaches are on properties that are considered wildlife
refuges. In order to gain admission you need to know someone who has a
membership to the Trustees of Reservations or purchase one yourself at
$65. You can also pay the daily admission for about $3 per adult if you
don’t plan to be staying for a long time.

The following beaches are just some of the more popular that Martha’s Vineyard has to offer.

**South Beach or Katama Beach**

South Beach or Katama Beach is on the Atlantic with lifeguards on
duty July and August. The surf is frequently rough and can surprise you
and take you out to sea if you aren’t careful. This beach is
frequently congested especially with the 30 and under bunch. You can
surf and in August there is a Sand Sculpting competition.

**Chappaquiddick**

Take the ferry ($12) to Chappaquiddick (which only leaves if full of passengers) to see the beaches and nature walks that are there. Mytoi Garden
is here, a Japanese garden inside a pine forest. Edgartown boasts
several beaches. East Beach is a calm area where you can see the dunes
and play with horseshoe crabs and the multitudes of sea birds. Fuller
Street Beach is near Lighthouse Beach and is open to the public. Lighthouse Beach in Edgartown is a little quieter and calmer. There is
a lighthouse there and it is a good place to watch sail boats as they
glide in and out of the harbor. This beach is only a walk away from
several hotels and restaurants.

**Inkwell Beach**

Oak Bluffs Beach, also known as Inkwell Beach, is
shallow and calm and a good place to take a family with little kids. It
begins near the Steamship Authority Dock and goes toward Sengekontacket
Pond near Joseph Sylvia State Beach both of which are open to all.
Eastville Beach is also nearby and open to all.

**Lambert’s Cove Beach**

In West Tisbury there is Lambert’s Cove Beach having the nicest sand
around. Unfortunately if you don’t know a resident you can’t get in.
Long Point is a great family beach but be sure to get there early
because once the parking lot is full no one else can come in. The water
is pretty shallow so it is great for kids.

Everywhere you look there is a beach that is just right for you in Martha’s Vineyard.
