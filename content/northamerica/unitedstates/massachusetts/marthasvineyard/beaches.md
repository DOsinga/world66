---
title: 'Beaches'
type: section
---

Martha's Vineyard has over 125 miles of coastline and no shortage of beaches, each with its own character. Some are on wildlife refuge properties and require a Trustees of Reservations membership ($65) or a daily admission fee (about $3 per adult).

**South Beach (Katama Beach)** is on the Atlantic with lifeguards in July and August. The surf is frequently rough -- be careful. Popular with the younger crowd, and there is a sand sculpting competition in August.

**Chappaquiddick** requires a short ferry ride ($12). East Beach is calm with dunes and sea birds. Mytoi Garden, a Japanese garden inside a pine forest, is a surprising find here.

**Inkwell Beach** in Oak Bluffs is shallow and calm, good for families with small children. Joseph Sylvia State Beach nearby is open to all.

**Lambert's Cove Beach** in West Tisbury has the nicest sand on the island, but access is restricted to residents and their guests. Long Point is a good family alternative -- get there early because once the parking lot fills, no one else gets in.
