The oldest settlement on Martha's Vineyard, Edgartown was once the island's whaling capital. Today it is a picturesque town of white Greek Revival captain's homes, a cute main street crowded with shops for souvenirs, antiques, preppy clothes, and ice cream.

Edgartown is a bit pricier and more formal than other Vineyard towns, but it is worth a visit at the very least, and makes an excellent base for exploring the island.
