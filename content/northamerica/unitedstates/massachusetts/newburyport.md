Newburyport is a small port city at the mouth of the Merrimack River on the north coast of Massachusetts, an hour's drive from [Boston](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/boston) and very close in feel to coastal Maine just across the New Hampshire line. In the early nineteenth century it was one of the wealthiest towns in America, fortunes made first in shipbuilding and then in clipper-ship trade with the Caribbean and the Orient. That money built the **High Street**, a long avenue of Federal-style mansions whose ranks of pillared porches make it one of the best-preserved early-American streetscapes in New England.

The compact downtown — pedestrianised brick streets running down toward the waterfront — has been carefully rescued from a 1970s slump and now does a roaring trade in independent bookshops, restaurants and bars. The riverfront boardwalk leads out toward the harbour. Just to the east, **Plum Island** stretches for eleven miles down the coast as a barrier beach: the southern two-thirds are the **Parker River National Wildlife Refuge**, one of the most important migratory bird sites on the Atlantic flyway and a fine place for an autumn walk among the dunes and salt marshes.

Newburyport sits where the Merrimack River meets the Atlantic, on the northern edge of Massachusetts a few miles short of the New Hampshire line. It was once one of the wealthiest seaports in America — a centre of shipbuilding, the China trade, and silver-smithing — and the **High Street** running parallel to the river is still lined with the great Federal-era mansions that the captains and merchants built in the 1790s and early 1800s. Few American towns preserve so complete a slice of that period.

The downtown was spared the wrecking ball largely by accident: when the harbour silted up and the trade moved on, there was no money to tear anything down. By the 1970s the brick blocks of State and Water streets had been restored as one of the country's first historic-district renewal projects, and they now hold bookstores, restaurants, and a permanent farmers' market. The Custom House and the Cushing House Museum tell the maritime story.

Beyond town, **Plum Island** stretches eleven miles of dunes and salt marsh down the coast — most of it the **Parker River National Wildlife Refuge**, one of the best birding sites on the East Coast, especially during the spring and fall migrations. A day in Newburyport works well as part of a North Shore loop with [Salem](/northamerica/unitedstates/massachusetts/salem) and Gloucester.
