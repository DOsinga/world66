Old Deerfield goes way back. In a town first settled in the 1660s, there is plenty of history to absorb. A walk down Main Street takes you past houses built in the 1700s, their dark facades set along a wide, tree-lined road, and past Deerfield Academy, with its flurry of prep-school students. A walk in autumn surrounded by yellow leaves is especially pleasant.

If you are short on time, at least visit Memorial Hall Museum, which sums up the town's history through photographs, paintings, and historic costumes. The most celebrated object in its collection is the doorway of the John Sheldon House, saved when the house was demolished in the mid-19th century. The door had survived the February 1704 Deerfield Raid — hatchet marks and poundings are still visible, still capable of sending shivers down your spine. The museum is housed in a brick building constructed in 1798 for Deerfield Academy.
