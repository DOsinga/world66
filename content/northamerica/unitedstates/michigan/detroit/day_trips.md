---
title: "Day Trips in Detroit"
type: section
---

Detroit is very close to tons of entertaining places in both Michigan and Ohio; if you want to take a break from the city, just hop on the bus or get into your car and treat yourself to a fun day trip.

**Cedar Point/Sandusky Area**

Located in Sandusky, 115 miles from Detroit, lies an incredible amusement park, Cedar Point, famous for its collection of world-record breaking roller coasters and rides. Young children will enjoy the Planet Snoopy section of the park. This place is perfect for families, thrill seekers, and the young at heart. It costs $44.99 USD for a day pass, although it’s very easy to find $10 off coupons in newspapers as well as on Pepsi cans. Soak City, an outdoor water park, is also a fun part of Cedar Point especially when it’s really hot out. Admission is $29.99 for the day.

You can get to the park by taking the Ohio turnpike to exit 118 and then following the signs to the Cedar Point Causeway. You can also go in a Greyhound bus. It costs about $30 to $40 and takes 2 hours 35 minutes to get from Detroit to Sandusky. There are two hotels bordering the park, the Sand Castle and Breakers, and there are several restaurants in and out of the park including TGI Fridays and several western-themed places.

**Frankenmuth**

This town is basically a little Bavaria in Michigan. The buildings and houses are all constructed in a distinctly German style. The best way to get there is by car; from Detroit you take I-75 northbound to exit 136. Make a right onto Birch Run, then a left onto Gera Road which eventually turns into Main Street in Frankenmuth.

Luxury hotels are common here. The Bavarian Inn Lodge has 5 indoor pools, 3 whirlpools, mini golf courses, and tennis courts. Zehnder’s Splash Village Hotel and Water Playground has 4 levels of watery fun and games, including water slides, wave pools, hot tubs, and more.

During the day you can visit Bronner’s Christmas Wonderland, where its Christmas all year long, and you can buy all of your decorations. You can also take a Bavarian Belle Riverboat tour, or stroll through the Frankenmuth Historical Museum and gift shop.

To eat, try the tasty, crispy chicken and noodle soup of the Bavarian Inn Restaurant or Zehnder’s. If it’s beer you’re craving, there’s no better place to visit than the Black Forest Brew Haus and Grill or the Frankenmuth Brewing Company. Both have many delicious beers to try.
