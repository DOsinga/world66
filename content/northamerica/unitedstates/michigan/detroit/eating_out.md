---
title: 'Eating Out'
type: section
---

Detroit's food scene reflects the city's incredible diversity. Mexicantown on Bagley Avenue serves some of the best Mexican food in the Midwest. Dearborn, just west of the city, has an outstanding concentration of Middle Eastern restaurants along Warren Avenue. Madison Heights offers excellent Vietnamese and other Asian cuisines on John R Street. Downtown Royal Oak and Ferndale have become dining destinations in their own right.

For the quintessential Detroit experience, head to American Coney Island or its next-door rival Lafayette Coney Island on Lafayette Boulevard. Both have been serving chili dogs since 1917, and Detroiters have strong opinions about which is better.
