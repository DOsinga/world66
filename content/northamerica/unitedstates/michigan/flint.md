Flint is a working-class city in eastern Michigan that was once the birthplace of General Motors and the heart of American automobile manufacturing. The boom years built grand Art Deco buildings downtown and a cultural infrastructure — museums, theatres, a university — that still anchors the city today.

The Sloan Museum chronicles the region's automotive and industrial history, while the Flint Institute of Arts houses a surprisingly strong collection for a city this size. The University of Michigan–Flint campus adds some energy to the downtown core. Flint's story is one of industrial rise and difficult decline, and it wears that history openly — a visit here offers an honest look at a side of America that most tourist itineraries skip entirely.
