---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

Flint is served by Bishop International Airport, a small but growing regional airport just west of the city with connections to major hubs including Detroit and Cincinnati. Flying out of Bishop typically costs slightly more than Detroit Metro, but the ease of a smaller airport makes up for it.

By car, Flint sits at the junction of I-69 and I-75, making it easily accessible from Detroit (about an hour south), Lansing (an hour west), and Saginaw (thirty minutes north). Greyhound and Indian Trails buses connect Flint to Detroit and other Michigan cities.
