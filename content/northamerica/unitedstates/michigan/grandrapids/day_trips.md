---
title: "Day Trips in Grand Rapids"
type: section
---

As Michigan has many natural attractions and beautiful places, Grand Rapids is in an ideal location to travel to some gorgeous and entertaining places for the day. In every direction, there's somewhere great to go. To the North there's Traverse City, Sleeping Bear Dunes, and Makinaw. To the East there's Frankenmuth and Detroit, while to the West there's Muskegon and Holland. South of Grand Rapids you'll find Kalamazo and Benton Harbor. Here are just a couple of the day trips you could take to get out and enjoy Michigan:

**Sleeping Bear Dunes**

The easiest way to get to the dunes is to drive from Grand Rapids. It's about 172 miles away and takes about 3 hours to drive there. The area is situated on the northwest corner of the lower peninsula of Michigan; and there's more than enough to do all day long, especially if you have kids. The first thing to do is check out the Philip A. Hart Visitor Center, it's open from 8 to 6 Memorial Day to Labor Day and 8:15 to 4 the rest of the year. It has tons of brochures, maps, and informative rangers to answer any question you may have. It also has a huge relief map of the dunes as well as a movie about them and fantastic displays. By visiting here you can learn about the human and natural history of Sleeping Bear Dunes, so you're more informed and can appreciate them more when you see them.

Some common activities to consider doing are the Pierce Stocking Scenic Drive, the Dune Clime, a canoe trip along the Plate and Crystal Rivers, biking trails, deer watching drives, campfire programs, and hiking. You can also learn a lot by visiting the Glen Haven Maritime Museum. If you take a day trip to Sleeping Bear Dunes, you're sure to have a busy day.

**Muskegon**

At only an hour's drive away, Muskegon makes for a close and convenient day getaway. You can take a bus such as Greyhound, as well, but it takes a little longer. Muskegon is perfect for anyone who loves to boat. Boating and fishing are hugely popular in Muskegon Lake. There are 4 launch ramps, and you can even do ice fishing if you visit during the winter. Muskegon is also a premier golf spot with your choice of 3 par, miniature, and championshsip golf in and around the city.
