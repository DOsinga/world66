Jackson sits at the geographic centre of Michigan's Lower Peninsula, a mid-sized city with a handful of claims to fame. The Republican Party held its first major convention here in 1854, under the oaks on what is now a small park. The city later served as a Civil War training facility and became a railroad hub — the switching and repair headquarters for the Michigan Central Railroad.

The main attraction is **Cascade Falls Park**, home to the largest man-made waterfall in the world — a concrete cascade illuminated with coloured lights on summer evenings. The park also has hiking trails, paddle boats in summer, and an ice rink in winter.

The **Ella Sharp Museum** preserves Jackson's farming heritage in a complex that includes a log cabin, one-room schoolhouse, country store, and the Merriman Sharp Farmhouse. The **All Star Dairy** (known locally as The Parlour) is an ice cream institution — brave visitors attempt their 21-scoop Dare-To-Be-Great sundae.

Racing fans know Jackson as the home of the **Michigan International Speedway**, one of the largest and fastest oval tracks in the United States, hosting NASCAR races each summer. On the third weekend in July, the **Hot Air Jubilee** fills the sky above Jackson County Airport with dozens of balloons.
