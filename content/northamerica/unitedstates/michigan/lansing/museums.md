Lansing has an array of museums, from art, history, and science to transportation. And of course the only museum dedicated to surveying.

A new addition to Lansing's museum scene will be finished in 2010. The Eli and Edythe Broad Art Museum will showcase modern and contemporary art.

### [Kresge Art Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/the_art_museum)

Experience over 5,000 years of intellectual and artistic development
with Greek, Roman, Egyptian, African, Asian, Islamic, pre-Columbian,
European and American works of art and artifacts. Admission to Kresge
Art Museum, on the campus of MSU, is free.



tel: (517) 353-9834

url: [artmuseum.msu.edu](http://artmuseum.msu.edu/)

address: Michigan State University 127 Kresge Art Center

### [Impression 5 Science Center](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/impression_5_scien)

This sophisticated hands-on science center is designed so kids of all
ages can experience the wonders of the physical and natural world
firsthand. Make slime in the Chemistry Lab, capture your shadow or
tinker with cool toys.



tel: (517) 485-8116

url: [www.impression5.org](http://www.impression5.org )

address: 200 Museum Dr.

### [Michigan Historical Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/michigan_historica)

Surrounding visitors with Michigan history from prehistoric times
through the late-twentieth century, 26 permanent galleries on four
levels include a walk-through copper mine, one-room schoolhouse, 1957
Auto Show, S&H Greenstamps Store, and more.



tel: (517) 373-3559

url: [www.michiganhistory.org](http://www.michiganhistory.org)

address: 702 W. Kalamazoo St.

### [MSU Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/msu_museum)

The MSU Museum has three floors of exhibits devoted to natural and
cultural history with an emphasis on the Great Lakes region. Halls of
continuing exhibits are devoted to world cultures, evolution, animal
diversity and heritage.



tel: (517) 355-2370

url: [museum.msu.edu](http://museum.msu.edu)

address: Michigan State University 103 Museum Drive East

### [Museum of Surveying](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/museum_of_surveyin)

The only museum of its kind in the United States, this museum houses a
collection of historical surveying artifacts with particular emphasis
on the era that includes Michigan's growth to statehood and early
settlement.



tel: (517) 484-6605

url: [www.surveyhistory.org](http://www.surveyhistory.org )

address: 220 Museum Dr.

### [R. E. Olds Transportation Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/r_e_olds_transpo)

A tribute to the auto industry, courtesy of one of Lansing's most
prominent citizens-Ransom Eli Olds. The very first Oldsmobile, built in
1897, is on display along with antique REOs, Stars, automotive
advertising and memorabilia.



tel: (517) 372-0422

url: [www.reoldsmuseum.org](http://www.reoldsmuseum.org )

address: 240 Museum Dr.

### [All Around the African World Museum and Resource Center](/northamerica/unitedstates/michigan/lansing/museums/all_around_the_afr)

The museum presents a pictorial and artifact presentation and display of the African presence on the inhabited continents.



tel: (517) 484-7480

address: 1136 Shepard St.
