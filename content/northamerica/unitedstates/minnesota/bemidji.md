Bemidji sits on the southwest shore of Lake Bemidji in northern Minnesota, a small college town surrounded by pine forests and more than 400 lakes. It is best known for the giant statues of Paul Bunyan and Babe the Blue Ox on the lakeshore — one of the great American roadside attractions, standing here since 1937.

The town is a gateway to Minnesota's north woods. In summer the lakes offer fishing, canoeing, and swimming, and the Paul Bunyan State Trail runs 120 miles south through forests and small towns. In winter, Bemidji embraces the cold with cross-country skiing, snowmobiling, and ice fishing — the kind of deep-freeze outdoor culture that defines this part of the state.

Bemidji also sits near the headwaters of the Mississippi River at Lake Itasca State Park, about 30 miles to the southwest. You can walk across the Mississippi here where it is barely ankle-deep — a strange and satisfying thing to do, knowing where this trickle of water ends up.
