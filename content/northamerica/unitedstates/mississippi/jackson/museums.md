For the sports fans, a great Jackson activity is a trip to the Mississippi Sports Hall of Fame in Jackson. This is the place that honors great athletes in all sports that have a Mississippi connection. Opened in 1996, there are currently over 150 inductees into the Hall of Fame. Famous past athletes inducted here are Walter Payton (football), Willie Brown (football), Guy Bush (baseball), Bonner Arnold (basketball), Troy Ricks (basketball), Joe Fortunato (football), Archie Manning (football), Crawford Mims (football), Willye White (track and field), Lester Sack (tennis), and Mary Mills (golf).

If you are an art lover, one of your Jackson activities when visiting must be a trip to the Mississippi Museum of Art. Newly remodeled, there are several exhibitions, events, and collections for guests to enjoy. Examples of exhibitions subjects have been Eudora Welty, Raoul Dufy, The Mississippi Story, Pre-Columbian Ceramics, and Richmond Barthe. Some of the painters and photographers' works that are displayed in their permanent collection are Mary Cassatt, Cindy Sherman, and Andy Warhol. There is a cafe with wireless internet so that guests can relax. For directions, hours, and ticket prices, visit their website at msmuseumart.org.
