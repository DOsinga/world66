Lake of the Ozarks is a 70-mile-long reservoir in central Missouri, about 45 miles south of Jefferson City. Created in 1931 by the damming of the Osage River, it has over 1,150 miles of shoreline -- more than the coast of California -- winding through the wooded hills of the Ozarks.

The lake draws boaters, anglers, and families looking for a long weekend on the water. Marinas, waterfront restaurants, and bars line the coves, many accessible only by boat. The main strip at the north end around Osage Beach and Lake Ozark is where most of the commercial activity clusters, while the southern arms stay quieter and more rural.

Spring and fall are pleasant, but summer is peak season -- expect crowds, warm water, and a party atmosphere, especially around the mile-marker-zero area known as Party Cove. Winters are mild enough that a growing number of retirees have made the lake their year-round home.
