Sedalia is a small city in west-central Missouri, roughly halfway between Kansas City and St. Louis on US-50. It is best known as the home of the Missouri State Fair, held every August, which draws hundreds of thousands of visitors for livestock shows, carnival rides, and concerts.

The town also has a place in American music history: Scott Joplin lived here in the 1890s and composed several of his early ragtime pieces in Sedalia, including the "Maple Leaf Rag," named after a local dance hall. The Scott Joplin Ragtime Festival each June celebrates this legacy with performances and lectures. Downtown Sedalia retains a handful of well-preserved 19th-century commercial buildings along Ohio Street.
