There are many sites to see around the Billings area,these are some of the better ones.

**all**

**Cheif Plenty Coups **

The park is located within the Crow indian reservation in south-central Montana. It preserves the log home and store of Plenty Coups, the last chief of the crow indians, and offers interetive displays and picic sites. The museum is very interesting and educational

address: 35 miles south of Billings and 1 mile west of Pryor

openinghours: May through September

**Pictograph Caves **

The Pictograph, Middle and Ghost cave complex was inhabited by generations of prehistoric hunters. As far back as 10.000 years.  A paved trail brings you past all three caves. There is also a nice picnic area among the ancient box elder trees

address: 7 miles south of Billings

openinghours: year round

**Western Heritage Center**

This regional museum offers historical exhibits. educational programs, and cultural events. Exhibits are changed every six weeks and are based on themes such as quilts, saddles, steam power contemorary gunsmiths, and western art  A great place to take the kids.

address: 2822 Montana ave. Billings

openinghours: year round

**ZooMontana**

This zoo features animals from the temperate regions of the northern latitudes. Exhibits include Siberian tigers, North American river otters, water fowl, sika deer,black footed ferrets, the sensory garden and a homestead area with a childens zoo [more..](/northamerica/unitedstates/montana/billings/sights/zoomontana)

address: 2100 Shilo Rd. Billings

openinghours: Year round
