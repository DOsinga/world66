Bozeman is a university city in the Gallatin Valley of southwestern Montana, home to Montana State University and sitting at the foot of the Bridger Range. It is one of the fastest-growing cities in the American West, with a combination of outdoor access, a lively food and arts scene, and proximity to Yellowstone National Park (about two hours to the south) driving its popularity.

The Museum of the Rockies is the main cultural attraction — it has one of the world's finest dinosaur fossil collections, including T. rex specimens excavated from local Montana sites. The Pioneer Museum covers local history. For the outdoors, the Bridger Bowl ski area is a local institution in winter, and the Gallatin Canyon to the south offers excellent whitewater, hiking, and fly-fishing on the Gallatin River. The Beartooth Highway, one of the most spectacular mountain roads in the US, is accessible in summer.

Bozeman is a good base for exploring the Greater Yellowstone area, and its Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport has connections to most major US hubs.
