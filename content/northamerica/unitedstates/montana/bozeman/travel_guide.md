Bozeman is the fifth-largest city in the state of Montana, and is easily one of the most amazing and diverse. Boasting of a population of about 40,000 people, the city is small, but it is the fastest growing county in the state. The city has produced a myriad of well-known people, and is particularly famous in the area of sports, especially sports on ice. The following is a** B****ozeman travel guide** for those who have the desire to visit this wonderful place.

**Activities in Bozeman**

Bozeman is a heaven on earth for nature lovers and conservationists, and its landscape is filled with endless wonders. The great climate and beautiful valleys have created the natural landscape that have been portrayed in many movies as part of the Great American Countryside. Hiking is one of the best loved activities here, especially when the weather is good. Since the city is located in the Rocky Mountains, endearingly known by the locals as the &ldquo;Rockies,&rdquo; one of the first stops should be the Museum of the Rockies. Museum lovers should also check out the Pioneer Museum, a place to find out about local history.

Adventure junkies will have a thrill here with the number of activities to do. Gallatin has a canyon and a river that is the best place for trekking and exploring. The best places, however, are associated with the city&rsquo;s tradition of producing ice sports athletes – the ski slopes that can be found at the Andesite Mountain and the Bridger Bowl, easily some of the best ski slopes in the country. Children will not be left out either, with the Medical Lake Outfitters providing a wonderful horseback riding service that will keep the kids occupied through the afternoon as they learn the basics of horseback riding and have a chance to interact with the animals.

**Getting around Bozeman**

Getting around Bozeman can be easy, with the installation,l in 2006, of the public bus system called the Streamline. The service is free, and since it is new, it is rather underdeveloped compared to many places in the world. Still, it provides a good way of commuting to main destinations such as the city, malls and university. Those looking to go a little out of the way must hire a driver or a taxi cab that is a little more pricey but reliable and convenient.

Bozeman&rsquo;s weather is notoriously temperamental, so be sure to check the forecast before leaving the hotel. The summer months between June to September are the best time to visit if you&rsquo;re looking to do some trekking, while winter sport junkies should head there at the beginning and end of the year when the weather is cold. Winters are very cold, and can dip to negative, so be sure to bring along warmer clothes and windbreakers to keep warm.
