Glacier National Park, straddling the Continental Divide in northern Montana, is one of the finest wilderness areas in North America. The park protects more than a million acres of jagged peaks, clear lakes, dense forests, and — still, for now — glaciers.

The Going-to-the-Sun Road is the centrepiece. This 50-mile engineering marvel climbs from the valley floor to Logan Pass at 6,646 feet, with sheer rock walls on one side and vertiginous drops on the other. Drive it, cycle it, or take the park's historic red buses. It is only open in full from mid-June to mid-September.

Hikers have 700 miles of trails to choose from. The Highline Trail along the Garden Wall is the classic route — exposed but manageable, with stunning views across the park. The Grinnell Glacier Trail offers close access to one of the park's remaining glaciers and is considered one of the best day hikes in Montana.

Wildlife is abundant: grizzly bears, black bears, mountain goats, bighorn sheep, and wolves all live here. Early morning on the Going-to-the-Sun Road is often the best time to spot them. Carry bear spray and make noise on the trail.
