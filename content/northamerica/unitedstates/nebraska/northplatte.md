North Platte is a city in western Nebraska at the confluence of the North and South Platte rivers, serving as a regional centre for the surrounding ranch country. It sits on the old Oregon, California, and Mormon pioneer trails, and the landscape here — wide, flat, and open — gives a strong sense of what those travellers crossed.

The city is associated with Buffalo Bill Cody, who lived here and ran his famous Wild West show from nearby Scout's Rest Ranch, now a state historical park with preserved buildings and exhibits about the frontier showman's life.

North Platte is also home to the **Bailey Yard**, the largest railroad classification yard in the world. The Golden Spike Tower visitor centre sits above the yard and offers views over miles of tracks and hundreds of freight cars being sorted at any given time — an oddly compelling sight for anyone interested in how goods move across a continent.
