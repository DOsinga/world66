Omaha sits on the western bank of the Missouri River and has been a transit and trade hub since the mid-19th century, when it served as the eastern terminus of the first transcontinental railroad. Today it's Nebraska's largest city, home to several Fortune 500 companies (including Berkshire Hathaway) and a quietly confident place that rewards visitors who spend more than a day.

The Old Market, a converted warehouse district in the northeast of downtown, is the liveliest part of the city on foot. The Henry Doorly Zoo is genuinely world-class and worth the trip on its own. Boys Town — the residential campus made famous by the 1938 film — is still an active community a few miles west of the centre, and is open to visitors.

Omaha has a strong Czech, Polish, and Scandinavian immigrant heritage, visible in the food and architecture of the South 13th Street corridor. The city's summers are hot and humid; winters are cold with reliable snow.
