Gerlach is a tiny town at the edge of the Black Rock Desert in northern Nevada, home to fewer than 200 people for most of the year. For one week each August, it becomes the gateway to Burning Man, when tens of thousands of people descend on the playa to build a temporary city in the desert — but for the other 51 weeks, Gerlach is as remote and quiet as Nevada gets.

The town was established by the Western Pacific Railroad in 1905, but the area's history goes back much further. The Black Rock Desert was a major corridor for pioneers heading to Oregon and California, and you can still find names carved into the rock walls of High Rock Canyon to the north. The triangular Black Rock Mountain served as a landmark for wagon trains navigating from spring to spring across the desert.

Today Gerlach's main attractions are the hot springs, the vast emptiness of the playa (where land-speed records have been set), and Bruno's Country Club — the town's social hub, famous for its homemade ravioli. The surrounding desert landscape is stark and beautiful, especially at dawn and dusk.
