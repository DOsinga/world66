Henderson is Nevada's second-largest city, sitting just southeast of Las Vegas with a very different personality. While the Strip glitters a few miles away, Henderson is quieter and more suburban — a city of parks, trails, and neighborhoods where people actually live.

The city traces its origins to World War II, when the federal government built a magnesium processing plant here to supply the war effort. After the war, the plant closed and Henderson incorporated as a city in 1953. That industrial past is long gone; today it's one of the fastest-growing cities in the country.

Henderson's own attractions are modest but genuine: a superb bird viewing preserve set in reclaimed wetlands and a solid local history museum. But the real appeal is its position as a launch pad. [Valley of Fire State Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/nevada/redrockcanyon), Hoover Dam, and [Lake Mead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Mead) are all within an hour's drive, making Henderson a quieter and often cheaper base than Las Vegas for exploring the Mojave.
