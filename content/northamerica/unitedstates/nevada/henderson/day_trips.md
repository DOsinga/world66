**Valley of Fire State Park**

Spend the whole day hiking, exploring, and picnicking in the spectacular Valley of Fire. It takes about an hour to drive there from Henderson; driving is probably the easiest and most convenient way to get there. The park is the biggest and oldest in Nevada. Named for the vibrant red sandstone that forms natural sculptures all through the Mojave desert, the valley gives you an in depth look at the past. Large areas of petrified wood and Indian petroglyphs thousands of years old mark the landscape. The entrance fee is $6, but if you decide to stay and camp and one of the many campsites, you pay $14.

For a fun-filled day, start at the Visitor Center where you can learn about the places you'll be seeing. There are various displays and informational videos and pamphlets. There is a nice picnic area when you arrive where you can have lunch. Make sure to stop at the grocery store beforehand and get everything you'll need to eat and drink. There is a modern restroom and shower there as well. Afterwards, you can take the intriguing 2 mile loop that passes by various rock structures and formations such as Arch Rock and Piano Rock.

**Hoover Dam**

Almost everyone is familiar with the Hoover Dam; being as it's so close to Hendersen, it makes for an interesting day. You can go alone on your own time, but there is a really thorough tour provided by the Hoover Dam Tour Company that is even more informative and entertaining. It's called the Premium Express Bus Tour and departs from nearby Las Vegas.

The tour includes a narration of Las Vegas and Henderson, as well as Boulder City. At Lake Mead and the Colorado River, you can stop and take pictures. You are also shown a video of how the Dam was built before you arrive. At the Dam, you take the 2 hour Hoover Dam Discovery Tour, visit the Visitor Center, and stroll through the museum. Don't miss the bronze "Winged Figures of the Republic" across from the Visitor Center. There is a presentation showing the intention behind this work of art and the map of stars below it.

The tour concludes with a stop for some rich chocolate samples at the Ethel M. Chocolate Factory and a short walk through the Cacti and Botanical Gardens. This tour costs $60 per person.
