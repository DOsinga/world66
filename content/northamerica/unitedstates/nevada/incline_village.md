Incline Village sits on the northeast shore of [Lake Tahoe](/northamerica/unitedstates/california/laketahoe), on the Nevada side. It is one of the more upscale communities on the lake — no chain restaurants, no strip malls, just pine forest, mountain views, and easy access to the water.

In winter, Diamond Peak ski resort is right above town, offering a smaller and less crowded alternative to the bigger resorts on the California side. In summer, the beaches here — particularly Sand Harbor, just south of town in Lake Tahoe Nevada State Park — are among the best on the lake, with clear turquoise water and granite boulders. The Shakespeare Festival at Sand Harbor amphitheatre runs through July and August.
