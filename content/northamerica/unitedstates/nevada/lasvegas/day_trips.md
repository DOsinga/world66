---
title: 'Day Trips'
type: section
---

Nevada's dramatic landscape is just a short drive from the Strip. Hoover Dam is only 30 miles southeast -- more than 10 million visitors come each year to see this engineering marvel. Lake Mead, the 16th largest man-made lake in the country, offers jet skiing, kayaking, and camping in the Mojave Desert. Valley of Fire State Park, 60 miles northeast, is named for its vivid red sandstone formations and contains petroglyphs thousands of years old. For the truly curious, Area 51 and the tiny town of Rachel on the Extraterrestrial Highway make for a weird and wonderful day out, about 90 miles north.
