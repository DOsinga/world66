---
title: "Family Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

Although hard to deny its risque reputation, more families are
choosing Las Vegas as their preferred vacation destination. Behind the glittering casinos and seductive venues lies a perfectly
respectable city with tons of kid-friendly activities at every turn.

**Attractions**

Where else in the world can a child ride a roller-coaster through a
miniature version of New York City, check out the Eiffel Tower, see
white tigers, or watch a volcano erupt while walking down the street?
All of the big hotels on the Las Vegas strip have plenty of
family-friendly attractions. The Bellagio has a huge fountain show
that begins every half hour; Mandalay Bay has a shark reef;
the Venetian has a river flowing through it with gondolas and Italian
opera singers; Circus Circus has the Adventuredome theme park; the
Mirage has its own volcano; Treasure Island has a pirate show; the
Stratosphere has an amusement park 100 stories in the air, the list
goes on and on. The major hotels also have fantastic pools including
lazy rivers, wave pools, baby pools, waterfalls, and waterslides. In
addition, New Horizon Kids Quest, a supervised daycare program offered
at several hotels, is available if you want to take advantage of the
infinite gambling opportunities.
**Food**

When it comes to food, the bigger your family, the better. Food is
quite cheap and delicious, and the buffets offer a huge variety of
foods which will more than satisfy picky eaters. Especially recommended
for kids is the Rainforest Cafe, where kids can go on a "dining safari".

**Shows**

If you are looking for a completely magical experience to share with
your kids, take them to one of the many Cirque du Soleil shows on the
strip. You'll have the time of your life watching impossibilities occur
right before your eyes. "O" at the Bellagio and "Mystere" at Treasure
Island are two of the best. Although tickets are expensive ($50-$120
per person), the experience is worth the price.

**Daytrips**

Don't forget, Las Vegas is more than just the strip. Red Rock Canyon
is a great place to take the kids to hike and see the beautiful wonders
of the desert. A tour of the Hoover Dam is also a good choice for the
family. The Las Vegas Springs Preserve will teach you all about the
importance of water in the desert with its colorful, interactive
displays. Children and adults of all ages will appreciate the Lied
Discovery Children's Museum which features entertaining exibits about
everything from nutrition and electricity, to the desert and water.

Las
Vegas is a one-of-a-kind place that has the potential to be not only a
hotspot for adults, but a spectacular adventure for families as well.
