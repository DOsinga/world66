#### **Viva Las Vegas**

The fun city of Las Vegas has a little bit for everybody: buffet eating for the kids, arcades for teenagers, shows for mom and gambling for dad. There are 3 main types of Las Vegas transportation: rental car, taxi and bus.

#### **Rental Car**

Renting a car at McCarran International Airport is fairly popular, because there are so many car rental agencies. Please keep in mind that renting a car in Las Vegas is the least popular method of transportation, especially if you plan to stay within your resort area or just visit the Las Vegas Boulevard (the "Strip"). Avoid driving a car on the Vegas Strip during the weekends. Only rent a car if you plan to drive outside of Las Vegas.

#### **Taxi**

The most time and cost efficient way to get around Las Vegas is by taxi. You will avoid sitting in traffic, and you will not sit at a bus stop waiting. Don't try to flag down a cab outside of a cabstand because it is illegal for taxi drivers to pick up drivers in the middle of traffic. Be aware there is an additional fee for rides to and from the airport but not within the city.

#### **Bus**

There are 2 main types of buses in Las Vegas: the Las Vegas Monorail and the Citizens Area Transit (CAT). The bus drivers do not provide change, so you will need to pay with the exact fare. If you're traveling with a large party, it would be cheaper to split the cab fare rather than to travel by bus. Most bus services end around 1:30 a.m. If you plan to use the bus often, it is a good idea to purchase a day or week pass to save some money.
