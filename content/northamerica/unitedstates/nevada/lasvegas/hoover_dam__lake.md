Hoover Dam sits 30 miles southeast of Las Vegas on the Nevada-Arizona border and is one of the great engineering achievements of the 20th century. Built during the Depression between 1931 and 1936, the 221-metre concrete arch-gravity dam holds back Lake Mead, the largest reservoir in the US by volume.

You can walk across the dam for free and take in the scale from the pedestrian walkways. The ticketed Powerplant Tour ($15) takes you inside to see the massive generators still producing electricity for Nevada, Arizona, and California. The full Tunnel Tour ($30) goes deeper into the original construction tunnels and is worth it for anyone interested in the engineering.

The Mike O'Callaghan–Pat Tillman Memorial Bridge nearby offers the best view of the dam from above — walk out to the centre for the full panorama. Lake Mead itself is worth exploring if you have more time: swimming, kayaking, and boat rentals are available at the Boulder Beach area.

Drive yourself from Las Vegas in about 35 minutes. If you're without a car, several tour operators run half-day trips from the Strip.
