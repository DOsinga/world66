**New Year’s Eve**

Las Vegas is known as a party town, a desert paradise where you can let your
hair down and have fun. Hands down, the most exciting time to travel to
Las Vegas is New Year’s Eve!

This isn’t the cheapest time to go. If you are looking for cheap
flight and hotel rates then that would be when the temperatures rises
far into the 100’s during July and August. Despite the cost, New Year’s
Eve can be the best time you’ve experienced, when it’s spent in Las
Vegas.

Some of the seasonal shows do shut down during this time but there
are parties that replace them. Getting a room shouldn’t be a problem
because the current economy has driven tourism down in Las Vegas. It is
probably wise to make reservations as soon as possible. Past years you
have to reserve rooms at least one year to nine months ahead of time
during a holiday.

Some of the Resorts in Las Vegas advertise New Year’s Eve packages
and it would be worth your while to check into those. Encore advertises
a $75 resort credit with rates from about $170 for a room. That isn’t
bad!

**The Strip**

The Las Vegas Strip on New Year’s Eve gets a little crazy after 8:00
pm. There will be a couple thousand other people on the strip; they are
wall to wall on the strip all the way through the night and no vehicles
will be allowed on it at all since the party is “in the street”. This
party is free but there won’t be any food or drinks being served. You
can bring your own and be aware that many people will have imbibed a little
too much.

**Clubs**

You can also usher the New Year in at a club, all of which will
probably be crowded. The Hawaiian Tropic at Planet Hollywood costs you
$125 for 5 hour open bar. LAX Nightclub offers to have New Year’s Eve
with the Kardashians for $200 per person. TAO has Carmen Electra with
Open Bar and Hors Doevres for $150. And this is just a few of the
opportunities you can find on the Strip in Las Vegas on New Year’s Eve.

**Midnight**

Fireworks commence at midnight and you will see a big show above the Las Vegas Lights.

**New Year's Day**

New Year’s Day you can participate in Sport Books if you want and
many restaurants will have the games on TV and a party going on. Of
course the casinos will be open and you can either start off with a
good year or a not-so-good year, financially.
