The Blue Lamp was a [Reno](/northamerica/unitedstates/nevada/reno) nightlife staple for years, a live music venue and bar that positioned itself as the alternative to the casino entertainment scene. Located on South Virginia Street, it drew a loyal crowd of locals who preferred their music in a gritty, intimate room rather than a showroom with cocktail waitresses.

The venue hosted local and touring bands across a range of genres -- rock, blues, punk, country -- and was one of the better spots in town for catching live music outside the casino circuit. The room was small enough that you were always close to the stage, and the sound was surprisingly good for a bar venue. On a good night, with a strong local band and a packed house, the Blue Lamp had the kind of energy that Reno's casino lounges could never replicate.

Cover charges could be steep, particularly on weeknights when the lineup did not always justify the price. But when the booking was right, this was the real heart of Reno's independent music scene -- a place where the city's creative side came out to play.
