The Blue Star Cafe was a casual dining spot on South Virginia Street in [Reno](/northamerica/unitedstates/nevada/reno), serving a straightforward American menu at 6350 S Virginia St. Well south of the casino strip, it was a neighborhood restaurant that catered to locals rather than tourists -- the kind of place where the waitstaff knew the regulars by name and the coffee never stopped flowing.

The menu leaned toward classic diner fare: breakfast all day, burgers, sandwiches, and daily specials that changed with the cook's mood. Nothing fancy, but portions were generous and prices were fair. It was the sort of restaurant that every city used to have on every other block, and that Reno's south side was better for having around.

If you were staying in south Reno and wanted a no-fuss meal without driving to the downtown corridor, the Blue Star filled that role reliably. It never tried to be anything more than a solid neighborhood spot, and that was exactly enough.
