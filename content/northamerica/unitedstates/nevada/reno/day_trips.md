---
title: "Day Trips in Reno"
type: section
---

If you've had enough of the bright lights and ringing slot machines of Reno, take a day trip to one of these exciting destinations near the city. From a nature lover's haven to a history enthusiast's dream, there's plenty to see and do outside of Reno, Nevada.

**Pyramid Lake**

One of the most beautiful and peaceful places you can go is Pyramid Lake. Named for a 400 foot tall pyramid-shaped rock, or Tufa, this area is a 35 mile drive northeast of Reno. You can also get there on several bus lines, but driving is the most convenient way. On the way, you'll pass through rolling hills and open areas filled with sage and rabbit brush. Pyramid lake is actually the biggest, high-desert lake in the world.

The lake is situated within the boundaries of the Paiute Indian Reservation, and the Paiute have been in the area for hundreds of years. Make sure to stop by the Visitor's Centor Museum, the Nixon Store, or the Pyramid Lake Store to get your inexpensive visitor's permit as well as any gear, maps, or food you might need.

October through June is fishing season; check out the cui-cui fish and the cutthroat trout that the lake is known for. The Western side of the lake has tons of beaches perfect for camping, picnicking, and swimming. There's even a so-called "family-beach" where drinking alcohol isn't permitted. The North end has hot springs, but they were closed to tourists awhile ago and are now used exclusively by the Paiute.

**Virginia City**

A true town of the "Old West", Virginia City is only 23 miles away from Reno. The old Main Street is its trademark, cluttered with true Western saloons, candy shops, museums, and eateries. The city was once very important due to its silver mining operations. It grew like crazy after the Comstock Lode Silver discoveries in the mid-1800s. However, much time has gone by since its heyday, and what remains is a quaint, sleepy step back in time.

Visit some of the historical museums such as the Mark Twain Museum at the Territorial Enterprise and The Way It Was Museum. There are also underground tours to check out of the old silver mines, they're very informative and well-presented. A train will take you along the old mine railway, the same route that was used in the olden days. If you want to see a truly unique event, visit Virginia City during autumn, when its annual camel races are held.
