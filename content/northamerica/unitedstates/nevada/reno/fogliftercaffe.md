The Fog Lifter Caffe at 246 W 1st St was a coffee shop in downtown [Reno](/northamerica/unitedstates/nevada/reno), offering espresso drinks and light fare just a block from the Truckee River. In a city where most morning caffeine comes from casino floor coffee stations, the Fog Lifter stood out as a proper independent cafe with a more personal atmosphere.

The espresso was well-made, and the menu included pastries and light breakfast items -- enough to fuel a morning without committing to a full sit-down meal. The location in the heart of downtown made it a natural first stop before heading to the Riverwalk, the galleries on the river, or the National Automobile Museum a few blocks east.

For visitors staying downtown and looking for coffee that did not come from a hotel lobby or a chain, the Fog Lifter was one of the better options in the neighborhood. It had the kind of low-key, unhurried feel that Reno's downtown does well when it steps outside the casino orbit.
