title: "Day Trips in Concord"
type: section
---

If you’re looking for a nice **Concord day trip** to a location
in the surrounding area, you should seriously think about visiting the
lovely Rollins State Park and having a pleasant day of recreation.
Rollins State Park is located about 35km northwest of Concord and is
easily reachable through the Interstate 89 highway.

**Rollins State Park Fees
**

It is important to know that admission to the park isn’t free and
there is also a certain work time schedule, when visitors are accepted.
For adults the fee is $4, while a fee of $2 should be paid for children
aged between 6 and 11. However, local residents aged above 65 and all
children under 5 enter the park free of charge. Staff is usually only
available in the weekends, so during the weekdays, you’ll have to use
the payment machine at the entrance. The gates are open from 9:30 a.m.
to 4:30 p.m. during the weekdays, and from 9:30 a.m. to 7 p.m. during
the weekends.**
**

**Activities
**

The Rollins State Park offers a variety of recreational activities.
It has a nice picnic area equipped with tables, which is frequently
called “The Garden”. The Garden offers an excellent mountain-view
panorama to the surrounding region. A number of hiking trails are
available as well to more adventurous tourists. There is a
2.5-kilometer trail leading to the summit of the nearby Kearsarge
Mountain, so you have an excellent opportunity to combine hiking and
having a picnic.
