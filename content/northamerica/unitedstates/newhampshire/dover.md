Dover is the oldest permanent European settlement in New Hampshire, founded in 1623 and now the state's third-largest city. It sits along the Cocheco River near Great Bay, and while it doesn't draw tourists the way the White Mountains or the seacoast do, it has a compact downtown with decent restaurants and a genuine working-city feel.

The Woodman Institute Museum is the main cultural attraction — three historic buildings holding collections of natural history, Civil War memorabilia and 18th-century furnishings. Garrison Hill Park offers views over the surrounding countryside and a climb to the top of the stone observation tower.

Dover's milltown past is still visible in its brick architecture along the river. It's a solid base for exploring the seacoast area, with Portsmouth just 15 minutes south and the Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge easily accessible.
