Portsmouth is a small, historic seaport city on the Piscataqua River in southeastern [New Hampshire](/northamerica/unitedstates/newhampshire). Founded in 1623, it is one of the oldest cities in the United States, and its compact downtown is packed with well-preserved colonial and Federal-era architecture. The streets are walkable and full of character — independent bookshops, craft breweries, and some of the best restaurants in northern New England sit shoulder to shoulder in old brick buildings.

The standout attraction is the Strawbery Banke Museum, a ten-acre outdoor history museum with restored buildings spanning four centuries of Portsmouth life. Down on the waterfront, Prescott Park is a beautifully maintained public garden that hosts a popular summer arts festival with theater, music, and dance performances against the backdrop of the river. The Isles of Shoals, a small archipelago about ten miles offshore, are accessible by excursion boat and worth a half-day trip for the seabird colonies and the views back toward the coast.

Portsmouth works well as a base for exploring the New Hampshire seacoast. The town itself rewards a day or two of wandering — the historic houses, the harbor, and the food scene are reason enough to linger.
