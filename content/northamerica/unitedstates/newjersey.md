New Jersey is the small, densely populated state across the Hudson from [New York City](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork) — perpetually overshadowed by the Manhattan skyline visible from much of its northern half, and reflexively butt of New York jokes about turnpikes, refineries and accent. The clichés cover a state that is, in fact, surprisingly varied: the densest population in the country sharing a small landmass with farmland (the "Garden State" nickname is earned), 130 miles of Atlantic beaches (the "Jersey Shore"), the Pine Barrens (a million-acre coastal pine forest), and a slice of the Appalachian Trail in the northwest.

The northeast is suburban Manhattan — [Hoboken](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/hoboken), [Newark](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/newark), Jersey City — with PATH trains running into the city in minutes. Newark Liberty Airport is one of the main international gateways to the New York region. **Princeton**, in the centre of the state, is the eponymous Ivy League college town with an attractive walkable downtown.

The shore is what New Jerseyans actually celebrate. **[Cape May](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/cape_may)** at the southern tip is a Victorian seaside town of preserved gingerbread houses; **[Wildwood](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/wildwood)** is the unironic boardwalk-and-fudge classic; **[Long Beach Island](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/longbeachisland)** is the family beach perennial; **Asbury Park** has reinvented itself as a hip music-and-bar town with Bruce Springsteen credentials. **[Atlantic City](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/atlanticcity)** offers casinos and a famous boardwalk, though it has steadily lost ground to the casinos in Pennsylvania and Connecticut.

Inland are quieter pleasures. The **Delaware Water Gap** in the northwest is a dramatic river canyon with hiking and rafting. The **Pine Barrens** in the south is a vast sandy pine forest with a folkloric reputation (the Jersey Devil) and surprisingly few people. **Thomas Edison's laboratory** in [West Orange](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/westorange) is preserved as a National Historical Park; the workshop where the lightbulb, phonograph and motion picture were perfected is largely intact.
