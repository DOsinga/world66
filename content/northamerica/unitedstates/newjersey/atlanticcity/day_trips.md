---
title: "Day Trips in Atlantic City"
type: section
---

If you ever tire of the casinos and nightlife of Atlantic City, there are plenty of cities nearby that you can explore. Here are a few places that are within easy driving distance that make for great day trips out of Atlantic City.

**[Philadelphia](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/philadelphia), Pennsylvania**

The drive to Philadelphia will take between 60 and 90 minutes depending on traffic. When you get there you'll find scores of things to do for people of all ages. The Philadelphia Zoo is a definite high point. It's the first zoo in the USA and holds over 1600 animals. You can even take a quick hot air balloon trip to see it from above. Be sure to stop at any of the numerous museums, including the Philadelphia Art Museum, were you can run up the famous steps Rocky-style. For young children, stop in at the Please Touch Museum and the Franklin Institute Science Museum. Those fascinated by medical oddities will appreciate the Mutter Museum. For a touch of history, you can visit Independence Hall, Betsy Ross House, Edgar Allen Poe Historical Site, the Liberty Bell, and Christ Church and cemetery.

**[Cape May](/northamerica/unitedstates/newjersey/cape_may), New Jersey**

Cape May Island is a 45 minute drive from Atlantic City. Leave your car on the mainland and rent a bike for the trip around the island. While you're there you can visit the bird observatory, lighthouse, zoo and salt marsh. The kids can go diamond hunting on the beach while older kids-at-heart will enjoy parasailing, surfing and kayaking. Take the whole family dolphin and whale watching, and don't forget to catch the flag ceremony held each night at sunset.

Also in Cape May county is the famous family resort, Ocean City. This is an alcohol-free area with a fabulous boardwalk, beautiful (and safe) beaches and a lovely little town. It's only about 30 minutes from Atlantic City.

**[New York City](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork/jones_beach_state), New York**

If you decide that it's either really worth the trip, or you have more than one day to spare, then you can make the two and a half hour trip to New York City. While there you can wander Times Square or catch a play on Broadway. You can save yourself the drive and hop onto the new express train that goes between Atlantic City and New York City and takes the same amount of time as the drive. Nine trains run back and forth on the weekends.

**Washington, D.C.**

Again, this trip is for those who either really want to see the nation's capital or who have a few days to spare. The drive takes about three and a half hours. In between the round trip you can visit the Smithsonian Institute Museums, the White House, the Capital Building and the many national monuments scattered about.
