---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The central Atlantic City experience is to walk the **Boardwalk**, ride a **rolling chair** (a uniquely Atlantic City institution — you sit in a padded two-person cart pushed by an attendant), play the slots at one of the casinos, eat saltwater taffy, and watch the Atlantic roll in. Beyond that, the **Absecon Lighthouse** at the north end of the boardwalk is open for climbs and has good views down the island; the **Steel Pier**, in front of Resorts Casino, has a 67-metre Ferris wheel, carnival rides and carnival food; and the **Ocean Life Center** aquarium in Gardner's Basin, just off the boardwalk, is a modest but pleasant stop with children. Nearby, in Margate, **Lucy the Elephant** is one of the strangest sights in New Jersey — a 20-metre wooden elephant built in 1881 as a real-estate stunt, now a National Historic Landmark that you can climb inside. For shopping, **The Walk** outlet stores and **The Pier Shops at Caesars** between them cover most brands.
