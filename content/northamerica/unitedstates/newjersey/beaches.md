---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

The **Jersey Shore** is 130 miles of Atlantic beach, mostly sand and warm enough to swim from late June to early September. Most beaches charge for daily badges in summer ($5–15) — buy on the beach. The character changes dramatically along the coast.

The northern shore — **Sandy Hook**, **Asbury Park**, **Belmar** — is the closest to New York City, busy on summer weekends, with a strong commuter beach culture. Asbury Park has reinvented itself with bars, music venues and the famous Stone Pony where Bruce Springsteen got his start.

The middle shore — **Long Beach Island**, **Seaside Heights**, **Ocean City** (which is dry, no alcohol) — is family-beach territory, often with full boardwalks, amusement piers and saltwater taffy.

The southern shore is the most distinctive. **Wildwood** has a vast, free beach (it has actually accreted in recent decades), an old-school 1950s-style boardwalk full of fudge and rides, and the celebrated Doo-Wop motel architecture. **Cape May** at the very tip is the historic Victorian alternative — preserved gingerbread houses, quieter beaches, the country's second-oldest seaside resort. The dolphin- and whale-watching trips out of Cape May are a worthwhile add-on.
