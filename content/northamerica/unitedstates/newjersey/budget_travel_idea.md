Need wheels on your next trip? Renting a car can give you freedom and flexibility when you're traveling, and in some parts of the world it's the only feasible way to get around. But think carefully about what kind of vehicle you'll need. If you're traveling with children or with a lot of gear, you may want a large sedan or SUV. If you're simply looking to save money on rental rates and gas, you'll want to reserve the smallest available model. No matter what your destination it is important that you understand how to go about choosing the right vehicle for your needs. Just because you own a big SUV or sports car in your own country does not necessarily mean this should be the same type of car that is taken on hire when traveling overseas.

But size isn't the only factor. Looking for something environmentally friendly? Check out for eco-friendly car rentals. Can't drive a stick shift? Be sure to reserve a car with automatic transmission. In many countries, a manual transmission is the norm -- so read the fine print before booking. An important consideration, and one that is often overlooked, is the transmission. If you live in North America and are going to visit a different part of the world then you should be aware that the prevalence of automatic gearboxes will not be so widespread. If you do not want to end up with a manual or stick shift transmission then it would be more important to make the booking as early as possible. Also, an automatic may be more expensive to hire as they are not as common. Also, be sure that the company from which you're renting offers any extras you might need or want, such as a ski rack, car seat or GPS system that might be very helpful to you.
How much space will you need in the vehicle? Usually a vacation or trip overseas involves carrying a lot of luggage and more so if you are traveling with family or friends. There would be nothing more frustrating than organizing a hire car only to discover upon arrival that all your suitcases and bags do not fit in the trunk.
