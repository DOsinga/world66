Cape May sits at the very southern tip of New Jersey, where the Delaware Bay meets the Atlantic. It is one of the oldest seaside resorts in the United States — presidents from Lincoln to Harrison holidayed here — and the entire town is a National Historic Landmark district, packed with elaborate Victorian houses painted in bright colours.

The beach is wide and sandy, and the town has a genteel, unhurried quality that feels more New England than New Jersey. Walking the streets past the gingerbread-trimmed cottages and grand old hotels is the main activity. The Washington Street Mall, a pedestrian shopping street, runs through the centre.

Cape May is also one of the best birdwatching spots on the East Coast, sitting on a major migratory flyway. In autumn, hawks, warblers, and shorebirds pass through in enormous numbers. The Cape May Bird Observatory draws serious birders from across the country.

The off-season (October through April) is worth considering: the Victorian charm is still there, the crowds are not, and prices drop considerably.
