Freehold is a small borough in central New Jersey, best known as Bruce Springsteen's hometown — he grew up here, attended Freehold High School, and immortalised the town in "My Hometown." The connection draws a steady trickle of Springsteen fans who come to see the places he sang about.

Beyond the rock and roll pilgrimage, Freehold has a genuinely pleasant downtown centred on Main Street, with a good mix of restaurants and shops. The town dates to the colonial era, and the Battle of Monmouth was fought just outside town in 1778 — one of the largest engagements of the American Revolution. The battlefield site is now a state park with walking trails and interpretive markers.

Freehold Raceway, one of the oldest harness racing tracks in the country, operates on the edge of town and has been running races since 1853.
