---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

A car is essential for most of the state — distances are short but transit between regions is patchy outside of the dense northeast corner. The **New Jersey Turnpike** and **Garden State Parkway** are the main north-south arteries, both tolled.

For commuting into New York City, **NJ Transit** runs trains and buses from across the state into Penn Station; the **PATH** subway connects Hoboken, Jersey City and Newark to downtown and midtown Manhattan around the clock. Within the dense northeast — Hoboken, Jersey City, Newark — public transport is good enough that a car is more burden than help.

The **River Line** light rail runs along the Delaware between Camden and Trenton. Down at the shore, the **Cape May–Lewes Ferry** is a useful 80-minute crossing to Delaware in summer.
