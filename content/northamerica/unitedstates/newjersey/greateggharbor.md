The Great Egg Harbor River is one of the best paddling destinations in New Jersey, running through the heart of the Pinelands Reserve on its way to the Atlantic Ocean. The river passes through undisturbed freshwater and tidal wetlands that support a surprising array of wildlife given the population density of southern New Jersey.

The bald eagle, peregrine falcon, and Pine Barrens tree frog are among the species that breed or shelter along the river corridor, and waterfowl use the wetlands year-round. The pine forest along the banks is characteristic Pinelands scenery — open, sandy, and unlike anything else in the Northeast.

The best stretch for kayaking or canoeing is between Penny Pot County Park and Atlantic County Park at Lake Lenape. Penny Pot sits off Route 561 along the Black Horse Pike (Route 322).
