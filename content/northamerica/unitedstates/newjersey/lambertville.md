Lambertville sits on the New Jersey side of the Delaware River, directly opposite New Hope, Pennsylvania, connected by a pedestrian bridge. The two towns together form one of the most visited antiques and arts destinations in the Mid-Atlantic — Lambertville is the quieter of the pair, with Victorian and Federal townhouses lining streets that have changed little since the 19th century.

The town calls itself the "Antique Capital of New Jersey," and it makes a reasonable case: the number of dealers, galleries, and antique shops concentrated in a few blocks is impressive, ranging from 18th-century American furniture to mid-century modern. The art scene is genuine too — local galleries show work by established regional artists rather than the tourist-market prints that fill similar towns.

The Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park runs through town, offering a flat towpath good for walking, jogging, and cycling. The Delaware River is nearby for canoe rentals and tubing in summer. New Hope's restaurants and nightlife are a few minutes' walk across the bridge when Lambertville's slower pace isn't enough.
