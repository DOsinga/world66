Madison is a town in northern New Jersey and in the Morris County. As
of 2000, its population is 16,530. It is also known as "the Rose City."

The earliest European settlers in Madison arrived around the year 1715.
After the Civil War, Madison's growth increased massively. The
railroads located there provided great transportation for farm produce.
Soon, a very large rose company was formed in Madison, thus making its
current nickname "the Rose City.
