title: "Day Trips in Newark"
type: section
---

**Manhattan**

A day-trip to Manhattan is quite the adventure. It is over an hour away with traffic averaged into the time. You can travel into the city by car, but the traffic can get very heavy and aggravating. Another means of transportation into Manhattan is via train. This is the best of the two options because you will not have to find a parking spot for the car or worry about navigating through the busy New York streets. If you have not yet been to Manhattan, then I suggest just exploring the city once you arrive. Grab a map at the train station so you don't get too lost. There are so many unique places to discover that you'll probably miss your train back to Newark. There are hundreds of dinning options and shops here. Also, many famous sites that are a blast to actually view. Walking can be very difficult for seniors, especially if you only have one day to see as much as you can. Families may find it stressful to look after small children as well.

**Spring Lake**

Spring Lake, New Jersey is a little over an hour south of Newark. It offers the refreshing lifestyle of a small seaside town. Time seems to slow once you arrive and hopefully the unhurried attitude of Spring Lake residents will being to wear off on you. The town is really quite picturesque with its turn-of-the-century architecture and lush gardens. There is a gorgeous gazebo near the center of town that is the ideal place to relax. Well, that is unless a concert is not taking place there. Fine boutiques and gourmet restaurants line the streets and are a delight to explore. There are, of course, water activities available. Swimming, boating, fishing, and golf are popular outdoor activities. This is a lovely day-trip for every kind of traveler. Everyone will enjoy a day in Spring Lake.

**Atlantic City**

Atlantic City is roughly two hours south of Newark. You can get here by car, bus, or train. The main draw to Atlantic City is essentially the casinos. However, the fun in Atlantic City does not end there. Attractions include the beach, aquarium, the Atlantic City Boardwalk, and various museums. Shopping is wonderful here and there are many luxury shops. Restaurants are plentiful and fitting of whatever your craving may be. If you only have one day to discover this eclectic town, then I suggest picking one or two activities to do. Cramming too much into your schedule may put too much stress on you to see everything. Enjoy one great thing at a time.
