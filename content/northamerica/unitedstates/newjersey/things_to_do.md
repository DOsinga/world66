---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

For most New Jersey visitors, the highlights split into the **Jersey Shore** in the summer (boardwalks, swimming, fudge, the seaside towns of Cape May, Wildwood, Long Beach Island and Asbury Park) and a handful of historical sites the rest of the year.

**Thomas Edison National Historical Park** in West Orange preserves the inventor's workshop largely intact — well worth the visit for anyone interested in the period when New Jersey was the engine room of American innovation. **Princeton University** has one of the most beautiful campuses in the country, particularly Nassau Hall and the Princeton University Art Museum.

For nature, the **Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area** in the northwest offers river rafting, swimming, and the Appalachian Trail crossing the Delaware on a footbridge. The **Pinelands National Reserve** in the south — a million acres of sandy pine forest, blueberry fields and slow tea-coloured rivers — is best explored by canoe on the Wading or Mullica Rivers. **Liberty State Park** in Jersey City has the closest free view of the Statue of Liberty and the Manhattan skyline, and the ferry to Ellis Island leaves from there.
