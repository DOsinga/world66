Aztec Ruins National Monument preserves structures and artifacts of the Ancestral Pueblo people from the 1100s through 1200s. People associated with Chaco Canyon to the south built and used the structures, then people related to the Mesa Verde region to the north occupied the site in the 1200s. The monument was established in 1923 and designated a World Heritage Site in 1987. The reconstructed Great Kiva here is one of the most impressive in the Southwest.
