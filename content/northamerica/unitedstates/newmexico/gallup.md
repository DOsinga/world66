Gallup bills itself as the Indian Jewelry Capital of the World, and it is not exaggerating. With over 70 retail and wholesale outlets dealing in Native American jewelry, baskets, pottery, rugs, and paintings, the town is a mecca for anyone interested in the arts of the Southwest's tribal nations. The annual Inter-Tribal Ceremonial in August brings together Indian Nations from across the country for several days of dancing, music, and food.

The town sits on Route 66 in western New Mexico, close to [Zuni](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/zuni) Pueblo and the Navajo Nation. It is not the prettiest place, but it is authentic and full of character. The old trading posts downtown are the real deal.
