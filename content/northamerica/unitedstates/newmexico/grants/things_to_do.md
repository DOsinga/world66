Hiking to the summit of one of the four sacred mountains. Touring the oldest city in the United States. Recalling the wild delirium of the Uranium mining boom. Cruising historic sections of Route 66. Mountain biking through stands of Ponderosa and Aspen. Birdwatching at La Ventana Arch. Exploring the stygian depths of a lava tube cave. It's all possible around Grants, New Mexico

### [Mount Sedgewick](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/grants/things_to_do/mount_sedgewick)

The Mount Sedgewick ride is a blast; it's easy to do but the scenery is great. For average mountain bikers, this is perfect. The ride covers about 9 miles on Forest roads, and starts at the Ojo Redondo Campground, west of Grants in the Zuni Mountains. Get the brochure for the ride at the Northwest New Mexico Visitor Center. Best time for this ride is late spring through late fall; it can be cold and snowy here in the winter, especially at the high elevation of Ojo Redondo (8900 feet ASL).

tel: 505-876-2783

### [Riding Quartz Hill](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/grants/things_to_do/riding_quartz_hill)

Yet another cool ride in the Zuni Mountains. For mountain bikers, this area is paradise! Nobody seems to know about all the little spots hidden in the Zuni Mountains that are a blast to ride. This is another ride covered by a brochure from the Northwest New Mexico Visitor Center; go there and get a copy. It's about 8 miles roundtrip, and other than a couple of tough spots that are easy to walk around, it's a very easy ride. Ponderosas, lava flows, an interesting quartz mountain, what's not to like? Plus, there's a neat little slickrock spot where you can work on slickrock riding.

type: Cycling



tel: 505-876-2783

### [Golf in Grants](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/grants/things_to_do/golf_in_grants)

Two golf courses for those who must. Coyote del Malpais (505-285-5544) is on the east side of town, next to Horace Mesa. Nice wide open links, clubhouse, restaurant, carts, pro shop, etc. Full services. The Zuni Mountain Country Club (505-287-9239) is a little 9 holer over in Milan, just west of Exit 79 off I-40. Very inexpensive.

type: Golf



### [Grants Mining Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/grants/things_to_do/grants_mining_muse)

The uranium boom left a mark on this little town. Check out the legacy of uranium mining at the museum downtown on Route 66. They have a headframe, exhibits, and a fully recreated mining operation; ride the "cage" down below to see how it was all done.

type: Other



tel: 505-287-4802

url: [www.grants.org](http://www.grants.org)

address: 100 Iron Avenue, Grants, NM 87020

### [Route 66](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/grants/things_to_do/route_66)

In the Grants area, there is a very long intact section of Route 66, the legendary "mother road". From Exit 117 near Mesita all the way to the Continental Divide west of Grants, one can motor along and enjoy the relics of the old highway. Vintage trading posts, abandoned motels, weathered neon signs, and other remnants of the glory days still await the traveler who takes this journey.

type: Other



address: From Exit 117 to Continental Divide
