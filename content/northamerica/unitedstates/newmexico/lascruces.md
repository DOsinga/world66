Las Cruces is the second-largest city in New Mexico, sitting in the Mesilla Valley with the jagged Organ Mountains rising dramatically to the east. The city is a crossroads — geographically between El Paso and Albuquerque, culturally between the American Southwest and Mexico, and economically between its agricultural roots (pecans, chile, cotton) and the university town it has become as home to New Mexico State University.

The best thing about Las Cruces is the setting. The Organ Mountains are visible from almost anywhere in town, and hiking trails lead straight from the city's eastern edge into the desert foothills. Within ninety minutes you can be at 9,000 feet in the Sacramento Mountains or skiing at Ski Apache. The Mesilla Valley itself is beautiful in autumn when the pecan orchards turn golden and the chile harvest fills the air with the smell of roasting green chile.

Old Mesilla, a short drive south of downtown, is the historic heart of the area — a Spanish colonial plaza lined with shops, galleries, and restaurants. This is where Billy the Kid was tried and sentenced to hang. The local food scene leans heavily Mexican, and the best meals are at family-run places serving enchiladas, green chile stew, and fresh tortillas.
