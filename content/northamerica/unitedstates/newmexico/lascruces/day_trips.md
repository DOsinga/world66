---
title: "Day Trips in Las Cruces"
type: section
---

New Mexico is filled with incredible natural landscapes, from windswept, white sand dunes to large, naturally sculpted rock formations. Being a small town located in the middle of national parks, reserves, and forests, Las Cruces is ideally positioned as a great home base for day trips into the wonders of nature as well as into man-made wonders. Within an hour to an hour and a half drive, in any direction, you can find yourself in a nice place to spend the day; therefore, it's best to rent a car, which makes everything more convenient. You can go to any specific place for however long you want, without having to hurry or cut things short.**
**

**Caballo Lake and El Paso**

Caballo Lake is north of Las Cruces; it takes only an hour to get there and costs $5 per vehicle. Perfect for spring and summer, Caballo Lake offers tons of water activities, swimming, fishing, windsurfing, water skiing, and jet skiing, to name a few. In spring the blooming cactus gardens are a sight to behold, while the bald and golden eagles for which the lake is known are visible most of year. El Paso, an hour to the south, also makes for an entertaining trip. Check out the El Paso Zoo, Western Playland Theme Park, or the famous Camino Real Hotel. If you want to see an ancient wonder, go to Hueco Tanks State Historic Site and gaze upon the prehistoric pictographs in its caves.

**City Rocks State Park**

City Rocks State Park is close to Las Cruces, as well. It is an hour and a half drive west from the town. In this park, more than 20 million years ago much volcanic ash was deposited, over time it ended up forming spectacular rock formations which you can admire today. Also west of Las Cruces (a two and half hour drive) is the Gila National Forest where you can have a picnic, hike, or just enjoy the peaceful environment.

**Lincoln National Forest
**

Don't miss the Lincoln National Forest. This huge area covers 3 mountain ranges, the Sacramento, the Guadalupe, and the Capitan. It is also the birthplace of Smokey Bear. Spring is the windy season making fires a major hazard; fire season is from March to mid July, so it's best not to visit during that time. The National Solar Observatory is located within the area and offers guided tours on Saturdays.

**Juarez
**

Finally, to get a taste of another culture, you can head down to Juarez, Mexico. At only a little over an hour's drive from Las Cruces, it's worth the trip. Just drive to El Paso and take the El-Paso Juarez Trolley, or "border jumper" as it's called.
