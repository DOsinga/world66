During the evening you can go to the Santa Fe Hotel or other hotels for a drink in the lounge while listening to an Indian play flute music, take in a History seminar in one of the conference rooms or see the Vegan display of information in another room, all depending on what's on for that evening.

At night there is Flamenco dancing in another hotel, the Opera House has all kinds of stage events, art museums have their opening nights with new art shows and their artists and refreshments, concerts or shows are held even at the casinos but you just need to consult the calendar of events or search around in the newspaper.

Sometimes you can listen to an author read part of his new book in a cafe and then proceed next door to the library to get an autographed copy.

For a complete listing of what's going on in the city, pick up a copy of the Santa Fe Reporter, the local alternative weekly newspaper. They maintain a comprehensive list of music, plays, poetry readings, art openings, and all the other crazy stuff that happens in Santa Fe after the sun sets. It's available in various locations around town.

### [Lensic Performing Arts Center](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/santafe/nightlife_and_ente/lensic_performing)

The Lensic Theater is a renovated historical theater with state-of-the-art lighting and sound systems. In this splendid theater of the west, built in 1931, Rita Hayworth, Roy Rogers and Judy Garland have performed.

And ever since there have been film festivals starting with the film *Santa Fe Trail *with Errol Flynn, Rudy Vallee and Olivia de Havilland. Now ballets, music, concerts and theater performances. Check the Calendar of Events.
