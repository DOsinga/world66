title: "Shopping in Santa Fe"
type: section
---

This is the oldest state capital in the
United States with over 400 years. Santa Fe is a city of rich history
and amazing sights, as well as great shopping places! Here's how to
spend a full day of shopping in the city of Santa Fe, New Mexico!**

Amazing Crafts**

Start off your day at the Place of the Governors at the 105 West Palace Avenue on the plaza's northern side, where native American craftsmen from over 23 tribes gather daily to sell their handmade
goods and crafts. Here you will be able to find great souvenirs and
gifts such as jewelry, textiles and other high quality crafts.

The second stop on your search of crafts at Santa Fe is the Dressman's
Gifts shop at 58 Lincoln Avenue. This little shop has a great selection
of carved wooden angles and other great New Mexican folk art from very
gifted artisans from Santa Fe and all around the great state of New
Mexico. This a true hidden gem among a lot of kitschy curio shops.**

Grab a Bite to Eat

**If you would like to grab a late brunch, the best place to do so is the Cafe Pasqual at 121 Don Gaspar Avenue, just a block away from Santa Fe's main plaza.
A brunch or breakfast for two goes for about $30. This restaurant has a
long tradition of over 30 years of serving unique dishes such as smoked
trout hash and huevos motulenos.

If
Mexican food is not of your liking, how about some fresh baked
croissants and baked goods from the Station Coffee? Located at 530
South Guadalupe, this is open air coffeeshop that is in the Railyard District's brick warehouse.

**Shop for Fresh Produce and Meats

**The Santa Fe Farmer's Market at 1607 Paseo de Peralta
is amazing! Here you can look for organic meats and produce, hothouse
tomatoes and fiery chilies. Plan your trip in advance because the
market is crowded at all times. Wear a hat, sunblock and have plenty of
water. This market is specially busy on Saturday mornings at the hip Railyard District.

**End on an Artsy Note**

To finish this great shopping day with some avantgarde meets old cool crafts, head to Bittersweet Designs at 901 Canyon Road, where you will find beautiful handcrafted jewelry. Bittersweet Designs is also known for its great journals and writing supplies that are made from vintage newspaper--a great gift for the writer inside of you!
