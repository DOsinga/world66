Santa Fe draws so many visitors because of its combination of heritage and location. But what to do once you arrive?

To get oriented, consider one of the tours of the city. It's sort of difficult to figure it all out on your own, although that's fun as well. Many such tours can be found in the downtown area, or through your hotel.

The museums are all worth checking out. There are many, so you may have to pick and choose. It's hard to go wrong, however; just get out there and visit one.

The shopping opportunities are alarming and amazing. A lot of the stuff being sold is available elsewhere, but there are lots of weird and wacky southwest thingies that you won't find anywhere else. Also, due to the prevalence of the wealthy in the area, there are lots of very expensive baubles and trinkets to consider.

Nightlife is not what you might find in a major city, but there is always something going on; don't let people tell you otherwise. Free concerts or events at many of the major hotels are common. There's the Santa Fe Opera. There are local music events at some of the rougher bars and hangouts. Check out Cowgirls in the Guadalupe district; they usually have something worth seeing.

Beyond Santa Fe, the Santa Fe National Forest offers hiking and biking in the summer, and skiing in the winter. Nearby pueblos offer flea markets, feast days, and other events.

### [What's Happening Now](/northamerica/unitedstates/newmexico/santafe/things_to_do/whats_happening_n)

For art, culture, skiing, activities, interesting local religious processions, Indian dances, markets of local art, jewelry and crafts - find out what to do on the current calendar.

Outdoor plays, cultural events, a cultural ticket box office and an interactive city map will all help you to find what's happening now and the event that suits you.
