The Pueblo of Zuni is the biggest of the New Mexico pueblos. Located southwest of Grants and due south of Gallup, Zuni sits in a beautiful landscape of juniper and pinon studded mesas, including the sacred Dowa Yalane mesa, a place tied up with many stories of the Zuni people. Historically, Zuni is important because it was the first of the pueblos to make contact with the European colonizers. Despite the ravages of over 400 years of tussling with the Euros, the pueblo and its inhabitants remain faithful to an ancient way of life that has endured in the southwest for millenia.
