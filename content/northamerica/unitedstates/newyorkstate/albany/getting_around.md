---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Getting around the Capital District is relatively easy, using the Capital District Transportation Authority bus system.

The Capital District covers the Cities of Albany, Schenectady and Troy with a decent schedule.

Much of the service in Downtown Albany originates at the Greyhound station at Hudson/Broadway, or Ten Eyck Plaza.

Schenectady passengers connect to buses at State and Washington in Downtown Schenectady.

Additionally, service is offered in Saratoga Springs and the surrounding rural areas of Albany, on an INFREQUENT basis. Check the CDTA website for information.

---Please note that the CDTA implemented a simple fare system, in which boarding fare is $1.00/ride. No transfers are issued, but you can deposit $3.00 (IIRC) and purchase a day pass (that will be dispensed by the farebox!). No zone charges are charged either, so it makes the long distance routes (i.e. Albany to Schenectady) an even better bargain!
