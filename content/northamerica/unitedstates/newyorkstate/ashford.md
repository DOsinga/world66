Ashford is a tiny rural community in northern Cattaraugus County, nestled in the rolling Allegheny foothills of western [New York State](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate). The town sits along US-219, the main corridor running south toward Ellicottville and the Pennsylvania border.

Most visitors pass through Ashford on their way to nearby Ellicottville, which has skiing at Holiday Valley and a lively village center. But Ashford itself has a quiet appeal for those who enjoy back-road scenery. The surrounding countryside is a mix of dairy farms, hardwood forest, and creek valleys that are particularly beautiful in autumn when the maples turn. Ashford Hollow, the main settlement, has a post office, a few houses, and the Griffis Sculpture Park, an outdoor art installation spread across 400 acres of fields and woods with over 250 large-scale sculptures -- one of the more unexpected things you will find in rural western New York.

In winter, the area gets heavy lake-effect snow off Lake Erie, which keeps the nearby ski resorts in business and the local roads interesting.
