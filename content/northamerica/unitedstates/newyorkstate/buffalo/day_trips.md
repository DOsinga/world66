---
title: "Day Trips in Buffalo"
type: section
---

Buffalo, New York has the distinction of having so much to do around the
city. There probably won’t be enough time for you to get to as many
places as you would like. Niagara Falls and the Finger Lakes are a
short distance away.

**Niagara Falls**

It will only take you twenty minutes to get to Niagara Falls from
Buffalo. Take time to see the natural wonder of the
world and you can visit both the American side and the more popular
Canadian Side. Be sure to have proper paperwork if going to Canada. You
need a current passport or Birth Certificate to prove you were born in
the US. It should be noted that not everyone that crosses the border
into Canada is checked but in case you are, be sure to have these
documents.

Take a ride on the
Maid of the Mist or take a look at all the gardens especially on the
Canadian Side. There are tons of attractions to see in Niagara Falls
that you shouldn’t miss and it isn’t so far away from Buffalo that you
can’t stick around until night fall to see the magnificent views of the
falls lit with different colored lights.

**Chautauqua**

Another place you can go and visit in one day is to Chautauqua. This
little village of beautiful Victorian Houses is home to the Chautauqua
Institution that presents concerts, lectures and theater. This is
primarily a summer place and usually shuts down during the winter. You
don’t have to attend a concert to enjoy Chautauqua because the town is
very charming and has many different specialty stores you can visit.
Check the Chautauqua website for performances during the time you will
be in Buffalo.

**Ellicottville**

Ellicottville, New York is a few miles away from Buffalo and you can enjoy
activities in this town all year round. There are places to golf in the
summer and it transforms itself into a nice ski area in the winter. Ellicottville is right on the edge of the Allegany State Park so be
sure to pack your hiking shoes or bring your bike and hit one of the
many bike trails through the park.

**Finger Lakes**

The Finger Lakes are a little further away but this area is do-able
in a day. There are about 90 wineries that grow and process grapes all
around the Finger Lakes. There is something about the soil there that
makes the grapes grow suitable to making wine. Corning, New York is the home
of the Corning Museum of Glass that is well worth the trip in itself.
There are also several glass blowers in town that make wonderful glass
art you can purchase.

Make sure to visit these other places when you go to Buffalo, New York. You’ll be missing out if you don’t.
