---
title: "Driving on Expressways"
type: poi
---

The Buffalo Niagara region has an expressway network that connects drivers to various points relatively quickly and easily. Funnily enough, the term "Freeway" is not used in Buffalo and many other area surrounding it. If you use the term freeway, you might get a funny look from a local.

The **Mainline Thruway** (NYS 90) comes in from the southwest from cities such as Cleveland, and Erie. It also comes in from the east from Albany, Syracuse and Rochester.

The **Niagara Thruway** (NYS 190) is a north-south spur of the Mainline Thruway, linking it with Downtown Buffalo, the Tonawandas, Grand Island and Niagara Falls. Additionally, the Peace Bridge (just north of Downtown Buffalo) connects drivers from the Queen Elizabeth Way in Canada.

The **Youngmann Highway** (NYS 290) may be known to older residents as the "Powerline Expressway" because it follows the New York State Power Authorities power lines. This links the Mainline Thruway to the Niagara Falls section of the 190 so that drivers don't have to go through Downtown Buffalo, a much longer drive.

The **Lockport Expressway** (NYS 990) spurs off the 290 to serve the North Campus of the University at Buffalo and Northern Amherst.

The **Kensington Expressway** (NYS 33) operates between Downtown Buffalo and the Buffalo Niagara Airport. This expressway can be very tricky during commuting periods. Please note that like the Youngmann Highway, there are no markers to state that you are on the Kensington Expressway. It is referred to (on signs) as NYS 33 East and West.

The **Skyway/Hamburg Turnpike** (NYS 5) operates from Downtown Buffalo south and serves a vast section of the Lake Erie shoreline, into Hamburg, Angola and Dunkirk. The Hamburg Turnpike switches frequently between highway and street, so be careful of upcoming traffic signals.

The **East Aurora Expressway** (NYS 400) starts just south of the 190 connection to the Mainline Thruway and goes at an easterly, then south angle to the communities of Elma, East Aurora and Holland.

The **Southern Expressway** (US 219) starts a little further south than the East Aurora Expressway and serves Orchard Park, Boston, and ends at Springville, where the 219 continues south as a local road until the New York State/Pennsylvania state line.
