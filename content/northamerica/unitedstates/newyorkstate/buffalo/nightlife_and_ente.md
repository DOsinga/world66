In the Buffalo area, three distinct areas stand out for Nightlife.

The largest Nightlife (and liveliest) is the **Chippewa Entertainment Strip**,
bounded by Tupper Street to the North, Huron Street to the South, Main
Street to the East, and Elmwood Avenue to the West. This section
of Downtown Buffalo erupts on Friday and Saturday nights from about
10:00pm (earlier in the summer) and doesn't stop until closing time at
4:00am the following morning.

Marc Croce, the owner of many of the bars in this district covers
most of the district with HUGELY popular places such as Darcy McGees
(an authentic Irish pub), Warehaus (European Style Dance Club), the
Brownstone Bistro (seafood), Buffalo Chop House (steaks) and
Laughlins (American fare). Other popular hangouts include Area
42, McMonkeez, and Crocodile Bar, Prima Pizza Pasta (food), Jim's
Steakout (food only) and Papaya (Thai food). Additionally, Guido
Finebergs, Flappy's (chicken wings), and other newer bars make it a
destination not to be missed if spending a weekend in Buffalo.

The **Elmwood Strip** (the longest strip), stretches on Elmwood
Avenue from Allen Street north to Buffalo State College offers much of
what the Chippawa Strip offers, except in a more calming
business/residential setting. This area hosts Pano's Restaurant,
Falafel Bar, Mr Goodbar, and so much more.

The **University Heights Strip** on Main Street from LaSalle
Street to Niagara Falls Boulevard caters primarily to the University at
Buffalo's many students. This area has the Steer, Molly's Pub,
Lake Effect Diner, Just Pizza, and another of many Jims Steakout's.

### [HSBC Arena

The HSBC, located just on the outskirts of the southernmost area of Downtown Buffalo is host to most of the popular concerts that arrive in Buffalo, all year long.
