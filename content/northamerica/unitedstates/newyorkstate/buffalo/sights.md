One of the most visible sights of the City of Buffalo is its rich history of notable architecture.

The Massachusetts Institute of Technology has a book available (~$15-25) called the "Guide to Buffalo Architecture". It's an excellent reference guide to the Buffalo and Niagara Region. The book can be found at most reputable book stores, and I believe Waldenbooks in the Main Place Mall (if still open there) carries it.

### [Metro Rail](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/buffalo/sights/metro_rail)

The Metro Rail is considered an unlikely place to experience anything of architectural signifigance, but it contains a sizeable amount of architectural and artistic styles unique to each individual station.

--- Some information has been gleaned from the NFTA Publication *"Art on the Metro Rail".* Every attempt is being made to have this available by speaking to the NFTA, but if you do call, many may not currently know what you're talking about.
