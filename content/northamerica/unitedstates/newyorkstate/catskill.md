Catskill is a small village on the west bank of the Hudson River that serves as the gateway to the Catskill Mountains. It was once the landing point for travellers heading into the mountains by stagecoach, and that role has not changed much — most people still pass through on their way to hiking, skiing, or a quiet weekend in the hills.

The village itself has a quietly appealing main street with a handful of galleries, antique shops, and restaurants that have arrived with the steady trickle of weekenders from New York City. Thomas Cole, the founder of the Hudson River School of painting, lived and worked here — his house and studio are now a National Historic Site and well worth a visit.

Catskill sits at the mouth of Catskill Creek, where it meets the Hudson. The views across the river to the rolling hills are the same ones that inspired a generation of American landscape painters. It is a good base if you want to explore the northern Catskills without staying in a more remote mountain town.
