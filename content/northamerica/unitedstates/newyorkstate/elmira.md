Elmira sits in the Southern Tier of New York State, along the Chemung River in the Finger Lakes region. It is best known as the place where Mark Twain spent his summers for over twenty years, writing many of his most famous works -- including *Adventures of Huckleberry Finn*, *The Adventures of Tom Sawyer*, and *A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court* -- in an octagonal study on a hilltop overlooking the valley.

Beyond its literary connections, Elmira has one of the largest concentrations of Victorian domestic architecture in New York State. The Near Westside Historic District covers 22 blocks of Federal, Italianate, Gothic Revival, Second Empire, and Queen Anne homes, many beautifully restored. It is a genuinely impressive neighbourhood to walk through.

Elmira is not a tourist town, and that is part of its appeal. The Mark Twain sites are well maintained but uncrowded, the local restaurants are unpretentious, and the surrounding Finger Lakes countryside is lovely.
