type: section

Christina Tilicki

Planning a vacation with the kids can be a challenge, but in **New York State, family travel** is easy and affordable. Depending on whether or not you want to stay in the city or travel to scenic upstate New York, there are family-friendly activities scattered throughout the state, ensuring that both parents as well as children enjoy their vacation.

**National Baseball Hall of Fame

**What better way for a father and son to bond than over sports? The National Baseball Hall of Fame is located in Coopertown and is open daily from nine to five with rates varying depending on age and the size of your group. Even those who are not baseball enthusiasts will be awed by the history and glory of this All-American game and the commemoration of everything from baseball players, to women in baseball to famed baseball anthems. There is something here to please even the most un-athletic of tourists.
**
Santa's Workshop

**In New York you will find something that you would expect at the North Pole - Santa's Workshop. An alpine village nestled against the side of the Whiteface Mountains, Santa's Workshop keeps the magic of Christmas alive year round. Home to a candy shop, multiple rides, a reindeer barn, a Christmas themed shop, Mother Hubbard's Country Kitchen and of course, Santa Clause, this attraction will delight children and adults alike.
**
Apple Picking

**New York state is known for it's apple picking. During the harvest season (June through November) there are countless orchards scattered throughout this region that are open for tourists and locals to enjoy free reign picking bushels upon bushels of apples. The most popular of these you-pick orchards are Country Dreams Farm (featuring all organic produce), Tucker Farms, Lake View Orchards, Brick House Acres, Sand Flats Orchards, Sage Creek Orchards and Bowman Orchards.
**
New York State Zoo

**Unlike your typical zoo with wild animals from all over the world, the New York State Zoo in Watertown is home to indigenous wildlife of upstate New York. This attraction lets you get up close and personal with a variety of animals such as bobcats, cougars, black bears, wolverines and many more. Throughout the year, the zoo offers special activities and events such as "Boo at the Zoo" during Halloween and "Zoo to Do" as well as many other hands-on, educational activities.
