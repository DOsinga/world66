**New York Transportation** is great. New York is much more than just New York City. The state is blessed with
a great geographic location and is full of surprises such as the Mount
Marcy, whose peak stands 5,344 feet (about 1,630 meters) high. When
you visit the state of New York, you're surround by 2 Great Lakes, 2
Canadian provinces and 5 American states (Vermont,Massachusetts, Connecticut, New Jersey and Pennsylvania). With great location and great people, the New York State has a lot to offer.

**Getting Around by Car**

Driving around New York is fairly easy, except in New York City. The general advice is to drive except during commuter rush hours in the morning and late afternoon hours as thousands of New York
residents are getting to and from work. In most of the state, the legal
speed limit within urban areas is 30 miles per hour. Make sure that you
wear a seat belt while driving and that all small children ride in
child safety seats, otherwise you risk receiving a ticket.
International drivers should look into getting an international
driver's permit before leaving their country of origin because not all
foreign driver's license are accepted in the United States. Also, having an international driver's permit will make the car rental much easier.

**Getting Around by Bus and Rail**

Getting
around by bus and rail is quite easy, especially around New York City
because it's one of the most affordable ways to tour the city. One of
the best bus rides in New York City is the Bus M5 along Fifth Avenue in
Midtown. If you plan to move a lot by either bus or rail, it's
a good idea to invest on a 1-day or 1-week pass. Money saving
passes are sold at hotels, stores and in machines at most rail stations.
