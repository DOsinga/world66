---
title: "Beaches in Hamptons on Long Island"
type: section
---

The Hamptons are famously known for the miles of beaches stretching across Long Island, NY. The beaches
provide visitors with an interesting social scene along with some sun
and fun.

**Main Beach**

Main Beach is on Ocean Avenue and no one should go near the Hamptons
in the summer without checking this beach out. This is a good beach for
swimming and there are facilities, lifeguards, and concession stands.
This beach gets very crowded during the weekend, so if you can claim a
spot on the beach be sure not to leave it until you are ready to go.
You must have a beach parking permit to park at the beach from May 15
to September 15. Daily parking permits can be purchased for week days
for $20. You must have a season parking permit to be able to park
during weekends and holidays. These permits cost around $300. Get
permits at Village Hall.

**Cooper’s Beach**

Southampton has Cooper’s Beach, where everyone congregates for a
good time. This is the only beach in Southampton. A pass is needed to
park here as well. Beach chairs can be rented for $10 and umbrellas for
$5. This beach tends to get crowded on weekends in the summer and is a
good place to swim. Surfing and body boarding is available outside the
swim area. There is a beach cafe at the pavilion with outdoor dining.
The beach is supervised by a lifeguard from 9am to 5pm from Memorial
Day to Labor Day. Parking fees are $30 per weekday and $35 on weekends.

**Sagg Beach**

Sagg Beach, in Sagaponak, is a great place to party. Here you will
find showers, restrooms, an overlook platform, food stands, picnic
tables and a volleyball court. This beach has about 1500 feet of beach
front. Lifeguards are on duty 10 am to 5pm June 29 to Sept 7. Permits
for non residents run about $250 for the season and day passes are $20.
This beach is frequented mostly by young people wishing to socialize.
Alcohol is allowed on this beach.

**Cupsogue Beach**

Cupsogue Beach is in Westhampton. Beach activities include swimming,
fishing, scuba diving, food huts, restrooms, showers and sponsors
special events during the summer season. There is a protected swimming
area complete with lifeguards on duty. There is a surfing and body
surfing area beyond the swimming area as well. Camping is permitted on
the beach but recreational vehicles need a permit to camp. This is a
family beach where children are welcome. Parking costs about $12.

These are just a few of the beaches that are available on the
Hamptons. Find one near the area you are staying and enjoy all the
benefits of the sun and surf.
