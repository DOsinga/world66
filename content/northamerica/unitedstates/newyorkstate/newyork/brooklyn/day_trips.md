New
York may be for lovers but Brooklyn is for hipsters. The neighborhoods
of Cobble Hill, Boerum Hill, Carroll Gardens and Park Slope just scream "pure cool" with plenty of hip bistros, boutiques and local bars.
**

Great Brooklyn Eats**

If a
restaurant has been around since the 1948s, you know it must be good! Located at 187 Atlantic Avenue in the Boerum Hill neighborhood,
Sahadi's is a restaurant that meets marketplace. Here you can find a wide
variety of ready-to-go meals and Middle Eastern and Mediterranean
cuisine are some favorites. Tourists can sample taramosalata (a dip made
from salted and cured roe of cod or carp, blended with bread crumbs or
mashed potatoes, lemon juice, vinegar and olive oil) and spanakopita (a Greek pastry often filled with spinach, fetta or ricotta cheese, onion or green onion).

A
great place for brunch and cocktail hour is the Robin des Bois at 195 Smith Street in the
Cobble Hill neighborhood, where a brunch for two costs about $55.
This restaurant specializes in French cuisine and has a charming open patio
setting. Give their excellent cocktails a try, too!

For
dinner, try the Al Di La on 248 Fifth Avenue in the Carrol Gardens
neighborhood. A dinner for two can cost about $60. The Al Di La
specializes in Northern Italian cuisine and offers selections such as braised
rabbit. Call in advance at (718) 783-4565 to reserve a table because waiting time for walk-ins can be an hour or more.
**

Design by Brooklyn**

Dashing
fashionistas looking for a boutique that carefully curates its
selection of women's clothing should look no farther than Bird, a boutique
that is the one-stop shopping place for Brooklyn hipsters. You can find Bird
at 220 Smith Street in the Cobble Hill neighborhood.

For those looking to put some avant garde
designs in their home, Matter, located at 227 Fifth Avenue in the Boerum
Hill neighborhood, is a great choice. Matter has a good inventory of products but also is a place where shoppers can gather fresh
design ideas. Here you will find items such as the
famous Piet Houtenbos' grenade shaped oil lamp, but unfortunately, photos are not allowed
inside the shop.

**Brooklyn after Dark**

Burlesque
is back in fashion in Brooklyn and there are two great spots that keep it
clean and classy. The Box, at 189 Chrystie Street, is a dinner theater
that channels the Lower East Side's vaudeville tradition. The
other is the Spiegeltent, located at Pier 17, South Street Seaport (under the Brookly Bridge)
which is a 350-seat theater that claims to have the best burlesque shows
during the summer tourist season.

Once
an Italian social club, the Brooklyn Social offers a great alternative
for a night out at Brooklyn. Weekend nights are extremely busy at the
Brooklyn Social but week day nights are more low key. The Brooklyn
Social is at 335 Smith Street.
