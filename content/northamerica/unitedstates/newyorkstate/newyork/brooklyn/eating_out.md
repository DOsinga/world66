Brooklyn is rapidly stealing some of the culinary spotlight from Manhattan. Brooklyn is an ethnically diverse borough and this is reflected in the many different cuisines available. If you have the time, and it only takes 10 minutes from Union Square, pop over to Williamsburg and sample a little old-school Italian; extremely cheap yet tasty Thai at Sea; or yummy French fare at Fada. If you are up for a longer train ride (30-50 minutes) visit Carroll Gardens, Park Slope or Red Hook. There are so many great places to visit and enjoy.

### [Bamonte's Restaurant](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork/brooklyn/eating_out/bamontes_restaura)

(Closed on Tuesdays)
Bamonte's has been around since 1900, and the decor is totally old-school and right out of the Sopranos, but authentic. The pasta is homemade and the food is fabulous. The waiters wear white coats, but the restaurant is very casual.

### [Chimu](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork/brooklyn/eating_out/chimu)

Peruvian Cuisine
Chimu is located on the corner of Union Ave. and Meeker in Williamsburg. It is very meat-centric but there is vegetarian/seafood fare available too. The service can be a little slow, but the food is fantastic and the portions are ginormous. It gets busy on the weekends so be prepared to wait durning the dinner hour as the restaurant is small. The Picante de Camarones is truly to die for.

### [Lodge & General Store](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork/brooklyn/eating_out/lodge__general_st)

Lodge is a great little restaurant in the heart of Williamsburg. As the name implies the decor resembles a hunting lodge (sans dead animals on the walls) lending to its cozy feeling. There is outside seating and Williamsburg is a great neighborhood to people-watch, very colorful. For example: you might see the guy previously hobbling around the BQE exit asking for change stride by purposefully with a group of friends (sans hobble). The food is reasonable and very good, as well as creative.
