Take a trip around the world without leaving Brooklyn, New
York. Brooklyn is the biggest part of New York and the
notorious home of many immigrants. In the recent past Eastern Europeans
dominated this borough. Now you can hear variety languages here including Arabic,
Chinese, Creole, Korean, Russian and Spanish. Each ethnic neighborhood offers
different sounds, sights and smells. Markets, stores and restaurants feature
imported goods to help these immigrants feel more at home. There are plenty of
entertaining and famous places to visit in Brooklyn.

**Coney Island**

One of the most famous places in Brooklyn is Coney Island. To
get there you can enjoy a ride on the notorious New York subway (Lines: D, F, N or Q to Stillwell Ave.). Coney Island is famous for Nathan’s Famous original hot
dogs, the rides at the pier and baseball. You can still eat Nathan’s Famous
original hot dogs, it is almost impossible to miss this well known tourist
destination. Get ready for some old fashioned fun. The Cyclone, built in 1927
and one of the oldest wooden roller coasters lives here. It is an exiting ride
but definitely not recommended for those with a pre-existing back condition. Baseball
still lives here. The Brooklyn Cyclones, a minor league team, play at Keyspan Park. This new and intimate baseball
facility also presents concerts in the summertime.

**The Brooklyn
Bridge**

You have seen it in the movies now see the world famous Brooklyn Bridge in person. Many tourists enjoy
the adventure of walking across it. When it was built in the late 1800’s, it
was the largest suspension bridge in the world. It doesn’t take much time at
all to walk to the other side and back, only an hour if you include time to
read the informative plaques. This activity is free and you have a scenic view
of many New York landmarks like the Statue of
Liberty, Ellis Island and Lower Manhattan. The
Brooklyn entrance is next to the Federal
Court Building,
or walk up a ramp at Johnson and Adams Streets.

**Brighton
Beach**

Next to Coney Island is one of the largest Russian
populations outside of Russian makes their home in the Brighton
Beach section of Brooklyn.
For the genuine flavors of Russian cuisine, stop into one of the many authentic
restaurants in this intriguing Brooklyn
neighborhood. Get ready to eat a cornucopia of pickled vegetables, borsht and a
carafe of vodka. This area was originally developed as a beach resort in the
late 1800’s. The famous Brighton Beach Memoirs by Neil Simon was set here. There
is also a racetrack.

One of the oldest U.S. suspension bridges, the Brooklyn Bridge is a famous New York icon. After 13 years of construction, it was completed in 1883. At that time it was the longest suspension bridge in the world and cost over 15 million dollars. This vast bridge stretches 5,989 feet across the East River which connects the New York City boroughs of Brooklyn and Manhattan. It is also a National Historic Landmark.

In the heart of Brooklyn lies 585 acres of urban oasis called Prospect Park. This park was designed by the same architects that created Central Park. It’s a great place to spend time with friends and family.

Audubon Center
There are many things to do at Prospect Park in Brooklyn. The Audubon Center is a wonderful place to visit. Dedicated to the preservation of wildlife there are nature trails here that roam the acres of this restored natural habitat. The visitor information center for the park is in this area as well. ### [North America / United States / New York State / New York / Brooklyn / Brookklyn Botanic Garden - Sights Specific](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/newyork/brooklyn/sights/north_america__un_2)

Brookklyn Botanic Garden (BBG) is beautiful 52-acre park in Brooklyn. This urban horticultural and botanical oasis has a world-class collection of plants. Each garden is carefully planned and designed to delight all your senses.

Judith D. Zuk Magnolia Plaza

Greet spring and visit the grand and elegant display of budding and blossoming magnolias from March into June. There are 17 aromatic varieties grown here. The white blossoms of the Magnolia stellata come out in March, followed by ivory, yellow, pink and purple varieties in April.
