The Brooklyn Heights Promenade runs along the East River in
Brooklyn, New York from Remsen to Orange Street. Be sure to bring your
camera because this is the place to go for outstanding views of the
Manhattan skyline.

The Promenade is a pedestrian walk-way with two rows of park benches
and flower beds spilling over with color and grace. Playgrounds abound
in the area and it is the favorite romp with joggers and roller
bladers. This is part of Brooklyn's Historic Preservation District and
the Promenade is lined with beautiful townhouses and lovely mansions.

**History**

Robert Moses, head of the New York Planning Commission, wanted to
build the Brooklyn-Queens Expressway right through Brooklyn Heights in
the 1940's. The neighborhood revolted the idea. They didn't want their
community to be chopped up by the expressway. The expressway was built but the 1,826 food long
Promenade was built over the highway above the waterfront. The expressway opened in
1950.

You have probably seen the Promenade in movies or TV shows as it is
a popular place to film. On any given day the benches are full of
people relaxing or just looking across the water to the city. There are
lots of trees here and they create a shady atmosphere. Make sure to get
to the Brooklyn Heights Promenade by subway as it is in walking
distance. It is too hard to find a parking area near it in the
neighborhoods. The Promenade is handicap accessible from the Montague
Street entrance.

**Mantague Street**

Also at Montague Street is a flagpole that honors a long time
Brooklyn Heights resident and civic leader, Genevieve Beavers Earl.
Right behind the flagpole is a marker that states the Four Chimneys
house, headquarters of George Washington during the Battle of Long
Island, stood. Near Pierrepont Street in Garden #5 there is a
Thunderbird that was built by park employees. It is made from old
paving stones and is a tribute to the Canarsie Indians who lived in the
area before European Settlers started to move in and move them out.
Volunteer groups and Department of Transportation maintains the gardens
on the Promenade and owners of the residences there give them a little
competition in gardens on their property between the houses and
walkway. It is always a beautiful place to visit in the summer or any
time of the year.

Snap pictures of the Manhattan skyline, the Statue of Liberty and the
many other landmarks that can be seen from the Brooklyn Heights
Promenade.
