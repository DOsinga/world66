Coney Island is a peninsula in Brooklyn, NY. It was once a major
resort, but has changed drastically since World War
II. The area today contains a variety of amusements including rides,
arcade games and food. Some of the same landmark attractions from the
old days are still there and working such as the Wonder Wheel and the
Cyclone Roller Coaster. The best time to visit is between Memorial Day
and Labor Day.

**Amusements**

The Cyclone Roller coaster is a must for any Coney Island visit.
It’s a historic landmark and has been made a world famous coaster. Your
first ride is $8 and each additional ride is $5. Another must see is
the Wonder Wheel located in Deno’s Wonder Wheel Amusement park. This
Ferris wheel has be a landmark in Coney Island since 1920. The
Amusement park itself also has a bunch of carnival type rides. You can
get five ride passes here for the different rides for about $25.
McCullough’s Kiddie Park is another great place for amusements. Here
you can find rides and games for younger children. More rides can be
found at 12th street Amusements.

**Food**

If you’re going to Coney Island you can’t leave without visiting
Nathan’s Famous. This is where the original Coney Island Hot dog comes
from. You can also get things like burgers and fries but why would you
if the legend is in the dogs! Ruby’s Bar and Grill is another place
you might want to visit. This is the longest running bar and grill on
the boardwalk. It was voted the best dive bar in Brooklyn so you know
it’s bound to have some great eats and drinks. Other restaurants in the
area include the Cheesesteak Factory, Cha-Cha’s, Footprints Café and
Pete’s Clam Stop to name a few.

**Other Attractions**

The New York Aquarium is located here. This is a great place to
visit with curious children who like to explore. Tickets are $13 for
adults, $9 for children and $10 for seniors. The Coney island Circus
Sideshow is a standing tradition in Coney Island. It’s ten-in-one show
that shows different acts and oddities. Tickets are $7.50 for adults
and $5 for children. Captain Bob’s Coney Island is a guided tour of
Coney Island on weekends at 12 and 2 in the afternoon. Admission is $12
per person. Don’t forget to take some time to enjoy the popular
boardwalk and beach as well!
