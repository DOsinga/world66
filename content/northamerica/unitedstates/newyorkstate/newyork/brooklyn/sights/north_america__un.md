One of the oldest suspension bridges in the United States, the Brooklyn Bridge is a famous New York icon. After 13 years of construction, it was completed in 1883. At that time it was the longest suspension bridge in the world and cost over 15 million dollars. This vast bridge stretches 5,989 feet across the East River which connects the New York City boroughs of Brooklyn and Manhattan. It is also a National Historic Landmark. **

Construction of the Brooklyn Bridge

**The construction of a steel-wire suspension bridge was a great undertaking and not without its costs. The Chief Engineer, Washington Roebling, was injured by decompression sickness during the underwater part of the construction. His father John Reobling had to have toes amputated because part of the bridge collapsed, crushing his foot. Both of these men were incapacitated by these unfortunate accidents. Records show that 27 people died during the construction of the bridge. Some construction workers simply fell off the bridge into the water and drowned.**

Opening Day**

When the bridge opened there was great fanfare and 1,800 vehicles went across. This excitement soon turned to fear when rumors were spread that the Brooklyn Bridge was poorly constructed and that it would soon collapse. This rumor caused a stampede across the bridge which killed 12 people. In order to quell doubts the great P.T. Barnum marched a parade of over 20 elephants across the bridge.**

Walk Across the Brooklyn Bridge**

Walking across the Brooklyn Bridge is a free tourist activity and takes less than an hour so it won’t take up your whole day. The bridge provides panoramic views of both Manhattan and Brooklyn making it an excellent spot for picture or a romantic sunrise. You will recognize the Empire State Building and the Statue of Liberty. Once you are on the Brooklyn side you can stop for a slice of delicious New York pizza. The walk begins on the Manhattan side near City Hall; take the 6 train to Brooklyn Bridge-City Hall or the J line to Chambers. There is a concrete ramp for walking and don’t stray off into the bike lane.**

Brooklyn Bridge Park**

There is also the Brooklyn Bridge Park which is a fabulous respite from city life. Currently the park has 40 acres of green grass and tall trees that are perfect for a picnic. There is plenty of opportunity for exercise here as well. There are 3 tennis courts, 6 basketball courts, 3 playgrounds and safe water areas for canoeing and kayaking.
