In the heart of Brooklyn lies 585 acres of urban oasis called
Prospect Park. This park was designed by the same architects that
created Central Park. It’s a great place to spend time with friends
and family.

**Audubon Center**

There are many things to do at Prospect Park in Brooklyn. The
Audubon Center is a wonderful place to visit. Dedicated to the
preservation of wildlife there are nature trails here that roam the
acres of this restored natural habitat. The visitor information center
for the park is in this area as well. Here you can find cool
activities for kids including the Con Edison Discover Nature Theater
and some live animals. There is even a human sized birds-nest you can
climb into.

**Green Market**

If you are looking for a healthy snack, meander over to the Green
Market at Grand Army Plaza and Bartel-Pritchard Circle. Here you will
find organic local produce, dairy and flowers. Don’t worry about
bringing extra cash, many vendors here take credit cards. The Green
Market at Grand Army Plaza is open on Saturday’s from 8am-4pm and at
Bartel-Pritchard Circle on Wednesdays May through Oct. 8am-3pm.

**Prospect Park Zoo**

This small zoo houses almost 400 different animals including a red
panda and a hamadryas baboon. This is a wonderful place for kids
because there are many interactive activities and opportunities to get
up close to some unusual animals. Inside the zoo visit the World of
Animals, a petting zoo where you can spend time with cute prairie dogs
and wallabies. Prospect Park Zoo is located by the Willink entrance at
Ocean Avenue and Empire Blvd.

**Wollman Rink**

No matter what the season there are entertaining things to do at
Wollman Rink. In the winter there is a 26,000 sq. ft. ice skating
rink. The rink is open to the public and there are also amateur games
held here as well. When the weather is warm you can rent pedal boats
and enjoy floating out on the lake. Wollman Rink is open Thursday
through Sunday and if you are hungry, there is a snack bar.

**Public Transportation**

You don’t need a car to get to Prospect Park because it is
conveniently accessible to public transportation. You can take the
number 2 or 3 train to Grand Army Plaza Station, the Q train to Parkside
Ave. Station. and the S or B train to Prospect Stn. There is also the
option of taking the bus. These buses have stops near the park: B41,
B68, B69, B71 and B75.
