Brookklyn Botanic Garden (BBG) is beautiful 52-acre park in Brooklyn. This urban horticultural and botanical oasis has a world-class collection of plants. Each garden is carefully planned and designed to delight all your senses.

**Judith D. Zuk Magnolia Plaza**

Visit the grand and elegant display of budding and blossoming magnolias from March into June. There are 17 aromatic varieties grown here. The white blossoms of the Magnolia stellata come out in March, followed by ivory, yellow, pink and purple varieties in April. The magnolia season ends when the sweet-bay magnolia unfolds fragrant creamy white blossoms in June.

**Lilly Pool Terrace**

You can find almost 100 varieties of tropical and hardy water-lilies here along with sacred lotus. July, August and September are the months to find these striking flowers in bloom. Heron’s and egret’s live here amongst the koi and mallards. Jenkins Fountain is the centerpiece of this engaging display, which is located at the front of the Palm House.

**Japanese Hill-and-Pond Garden**

This aesthetic and polished garden dominated by evergreens is a great sight any time of year. One of the most visited sights in Brooklyn Botanic Garden, it is also one of the most popular. Each plant is carefully cultivated and shaped by cloud-pruning. Wooden bridges escort you through the intriguing variety of trees and shrubs. Expect to see Japanese irises peonies, maples and azaleas. Although flowers are not the main attraction here, there are Japanese cherry blossoms in the spring.

**History**

Brooklyn Botanic Garden was originally an ash dump back in the lat 1800´s. The garden was founded in 1910 with the Children’s Garden created in 1914 and the Japanese Hill and Pond Garden in 1915. Through the years there have been many positive changes added to make it the attraction it is today.

**Hours and Directions**

Brookklyn Botanic Garden is closed on Monday’s but open on all other days including holidays. You can visit from Tuesday to Friday from 8 a.m. to 6 a.m. and on weekends and holidays from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. This destination is very reasonably priced and the entrance for children 12 and under is free. You can get here by taking the famous New York Subway. Trains that come close to the Brookklyn Botanic Garden are the 2, 3, B (not on weekends), Q, 4 and S. Parking lots are available at 900 Washington Ave. for $3 per hour. Mostly but not completely accessible to the mobility challenged and there is a Fragrance Garden with Braille for the blind.
