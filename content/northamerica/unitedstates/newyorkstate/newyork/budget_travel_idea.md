---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

Since the streets and subways of New York
City were "cleaned", enjoying the Big Apple on a budget can seem nearly
impossible. If you're watching your wallet, you'll want to do some
research before booking your trip. Here is a list of budget travel
ideas for where to eat, where to drink and where to stay in New York
City. **

Where to Eat**

Damon: Frugal Friday is located on 47 E. 19th Street and is a great spot for dinner. You can find snacks for $5 such as Smoked Beef Tartar, Smoked Paprika Flatbread and Crispy Chicken Wings. Salads go for $5, small plates for $8, 6 inch pizzas for $7. Try this $4 cocktail, the 19th Street Headache, made with Cognac, Cachaca, Ginger, Lemon and Kumquats.

Shopsin's is in the Essex Street Market at 120 Essex Street. It's well known for a fantastic breakfast that won't hurt your bank account. Shopsin's is open on Tuesdays through Fridays from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. and on Saturdays from 9:30 a.m. to 2 p.m. Kenny Shopsin's cooking is so good, he published a book about his tasty treats. **

Where to Drink**

Smith & Mills on 71 N. Moore
Street offers cocktails for 2 for less than $20 total. Humble, yet chic
and comfortable, the service at Smith & Mills is excellent,
friendly and speedy. Locals claim that Smith & Mills may have the
coolest bathroom in NYC.

Zibetto Espresso Bar is on 1385 Sixth
Avenue. This espresso bar offers fancy coffees for 2 for less than $6
total. On weekdays they open at 7 a.m. and close at 7 p.m. Saturdays
from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. and Sundays from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Their *caffe macchiato* is a local favorite.

PDT is at 113 Street. A true local's
secret, PDT stands for "Please Don't Tell". The best nights to go are
Tuesday through Thursday. PDT is a snug and small style bar, not too
loud, so it's easy to have a conversation. It truly lives up to its
name because of its secret entrance through a yummy hot dog joint
calledCriff Dogs (look for a door to your left with an intercom type phone). **

Where to Stay**

Visitors
to New York city will be glad to know that there is a wide range of
hostels to stay at. At the top of the list is the Chelsea Hostel at 251
West 20th Street where you can find a dormitory style room for $32
without bathroom and for $36 with a bathroom. Private rooms with either
double beds or bunk beds are available for $80 per night, whether or
not 2 people are staying in the room. From the Chelsea Hostel, the
Empire State Building is a 15 minute walk away, while the former World
Trade Center Site (Ground Zero) is a 30 minute walk away.

Another good hostel option is the Central Park Hostel at 19
West 103rd Street, near Central Park West. This is a coed hostel that
offers dormitory rooms ($28 - $45, depending on the season), private
rooms ($89 - $135, depending on the season) and studio apartments ($109
- $179,depending on the season). The hostel is conveniently a
train ride away from Times Square, Rockefeller Center, Port Authority
(bus station) and Penn Station (train station).
