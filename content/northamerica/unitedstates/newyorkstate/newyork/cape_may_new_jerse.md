Cape May is about a 2–3 hour drive (or train ride via NJ Transit) from New York. The town is one of the best-preserved Victorian seaside resorts in the US — block after block of gingerbread cottages and wraparound porches. The beach is free to access outside of summer weekends.

Beyond the beach there's whale and dolphin watching from May to November, birding (Cape May is one of the top migration corridors on the East Coast), and vineyard tours in the surrounding county. Bicycling is the best way to get around: Cape Island Bike Rentals (Howard St at Beach Ave, 609-898-7368), Shields Bike Rentals (11 Gurney St, 609-884-1818), and Village Bicycle Shop (605 Lafayette St, 609-884-8500) all rent by the hour or day.
