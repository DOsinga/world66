New York City nightlife is famous around the
world, but if you’re not looking for a cosmopolitan, head downtown to McSorley's
Old Ale House, one of the oldest bars in the city that dates back to the 1854. Serving only dark or light ale in two mugs
per person along with basic bar food like burgers, sandwiches, and a cheese
platter that comes with raw onions, a sleeve of Saltines and spicy mustard, McSorley’s
is known for its atmosphere. The floor
is covered in saw dust, the walls plastered with memorabilia from 1910 onward,
and spontaneous singing is known to break out randomly throughout the night. Women were not allowed in the bar until 1970,
though now both male and female locals and tourists alike crowd the place every
day of the week. The wait staff doesn’t
mess around, but the guys keep the beer coming and will kick you out if you’re
not behaving or just sitting around – as the McSorley’s motto states, “Be good
or be gone.”
