---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

The only real reason most New Yorkers come to Queens is to eat. Every wave of immigration of the last seventy years has left its mark, and the borough now has arguably the best regional cooking of any part of the city. **Flushing** is the most concentrated food destination: the basement food courts of the New World Mall and Golden Mall are full of Sichuan noodle stalls, Uyghur lamb skewers, Xi'an hand-pulled noodles, Taiwanese shaved ice, and Korean barbecue. **Jackson Heights** is the place for South Asian food — Himalayan Yak for Tibetan momos, Jackson Diner for North Indian, Adda for Delhi street food, and the Colombian bakeries along 37th Avenue. **Astoria** still has its Greek grill houses and fish tavernas, along with some of the best Egyptian food outside Cairo on Steinway Street. **Long Island City** has newer, trendier places; **Woodside** has the legendary SriPraPhai (long the best Thai restaurant in New York); and every subway stop in between has something worth getting off the train for.
