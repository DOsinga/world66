The jurisdiction of Queens in New
York City is the second largest borough in the
city. Sectioned off into several
different neighborhoods, the borough is a mishmash of different cultures and
culinary influences. Most nationalities
stick to their own neighborhood; Asian influences mainly in Flushing
and Astoria is
filled with Grecian restaurants. Depending on your tastes you can visit restaurants from American to
Italian; Chinese to Peruvian. Whatever
it may be that you’re craving, Queens can provide.

**Street Food**

Street food is a culture of its own
in New York
and it’s no different in Queens. There are different carts in every
neighborhood that are as culturally diverse as surrounding restaurants. Of course there is the average hot dog
stand scattered around but along with them are Chinese duck sandwiches or
Peruvian chicken stands that offer full platter dinners on the go. The best part about street food is that it’s
always very cheap and you can usually depend on it being absolutely delicious.

**Chinese Food**

If you’re craving Chinese cuisine,
head over to Flushing and try Joe’s Shanghai
Restaurant. The menu offers many Chinese
restaurant staples such as Sesame Chicken and Szechuan Beef but what they’re
really famous for is the soup dumplings. Made to order, these dumplings are handmade and arrive
to your table in their own bamboo steamers.

**Pizza**

How does
Pizza sound? Dee’s
Brick Oven Pizza serves authentic fresh pizza’s cooked in a wood burning brick
oven. You can create your own topping
designs or put your faith in one of the specialty pizza’s Dee’s
puts out every night. This isn’t your
regular pizza joint though. Dee’s menu has an extensive line up of Mediterranean pastas and
appetizers along with burgers, salads and steaks. Dee’s has a
trendy atmosphere while remaining family friendly.
