Welcome to Queens , NYC!
This vibrant borough has many sights not to miss. Take your pick!

**The Mets at
Citi Field, Queens **

You can’t visit Queens
without attending a Mets game! With plenty of parking and tailgating, Citi Field
is the place to be on that summer afternoon. Usual ballpark food is available, such
as peanuts, beer, lemon ices, and cotton candy. Go Mets!

**Unisphere and
Rocket Meadow in Flushing Meadows Park **

From the 1964-1965 world fair, the unisphere, a see-through
giant steel globe of the world, is one of Queen’s greatest attractions. Just a
few steps away is a statue of a man throwing a rocket into space. The Unisphere is a great spot
to have a picnic or just watch the locals cruise by.

**Queens Museum of Art **

Do you live in Queens? You
can find your home located on this detailed scale model of the entire city,
otherwise known as the “Panorama”. The museum also displays work from local and
international artists, the Neustadt Collection of Tiffany Art., and objects
from the 1964-1965 World Fair.

**Bohemian Hall
& Beer Garden **

Located in Astoria , Queens,
the Bohemian Hall & Beer Garden
is situated in a field that’s always packed with musicians, card-players, and
locals. You won’t have to wait long for ice-cold pitchers of beer, Czech food
and barbeque, and local snacks. The perfect place to unwind after strolling
through the city, or the Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge.

**Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge**

One of the largest bird sanctuaries in all of northern America ,
it is possible to see over a hundred different birds, including the famous
Lesser Yellowlegs. The best time to see
birds is early April and then late July to late August. You can also hike on the trail that loops
around the refuge, crossing over marshes and offering a glimpse of the rare
prickly pear cactus. Bring your camera!

** PS 1. Contemporary Art
Center **

An internationally
renowned museum showcasing contemporary art, PS 1 – the looser, crazier version
of MoMA, makes its home in a former public high school. This art center often features European
portraits, sculptures, and other “weird” and “hip” art.

** NYC’s Other
Chinatown: Flushing, Queens. **

Although not a
“formal site,” Flushing is worth a visit
solely for its great Chinese eateries and fried noodles. You can stroll the
streets, sip boba tea and dark espresso, and visit locally-owned, quirky gift
shops. This is the best place to see the
Lunar/Chinese New Year celebration in the winter.
