**Isamu Noguchi
Museum**** & Gardens**

Isamu
Noguchi is a well-known Japanese artist who produced works of art using stone,
metal, wood and clay. He also built models for public projects and gardens as
well as sets for artistic productions such as dances and plays. This museum
exhibits his work in both museum and garden style formats. The gardens have a
distinct footpath allowing guests to experience art in a more tranquil setting.**

Queens
Museum of Art**

This museum
is known for the Panorama of New York City which is a scaled model of the
entire city. Originally built for the World’s Fair in 1964, the model
encompasses 9,335 square feet of space and includes all buildings built before
1992 in all five boroughs of New York
City. Another draw to this museum is the Neustadt
Collection of Tiffany Art which showcases Louis Tiffany’s decorative works
including lamps and painted windows .

as well as exhibits containing artifacts from several productions make this

Flushing Meadows
Corona Park**

Aside from
being a former public wasteland, this park has grown to become one of New York City’s flagship
parks. The Unisphere, a stainless steel globe presented to the 1964 World’s
Fair by the United States,
is considered to be Queen’s most recognizable symbol and is deemed a popular
meeting place for locals. Another World’s Fair piece located in this park is
The Rocket Thrower which is at the center of Fountain Alley. The park encompasses
1,255 acres and is both a recreational and historical venue that is open to the
public.**

Downtown
Flushing-The Other Chinatown**

The
downtown area of Flushing is known as New York’s
“other Chinatown.” In fact, it is the 2nd
largest Chinatown in New York City.
Downtown Flushing is the largest urban center in Queens.
Here you will find a plethora of Asian restaurants ranging from authentic to
Asian-fusion. It is not entirely dominated by Asian shops as there is also a
McDonalds, a Starbucks and other recognizable chain shops and restaurants in
this district.**

Jackson Heights**

This
neighborhood is known to be the “South Asian” neighborhood of Queens.
Here, the Indians, Pakistanis and Bangladeshis have found their home away from
home. Here you will find the highest quality Indian food in New York City along with a variety of Indian
jewelry, clothing and even Bollywood films. It is a colorful and lively place
to visit and will enlighten one to appreciate the South Asian cultures.
