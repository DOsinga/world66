Gorgeous landscapes, 19th-century row houses in Gothic revival, Victorian Gothic, and Italianate style, and a statue of Edwin Booth playing Hamlet are just a few of the surprises waiting for you at New York's Gramercy Park. Other highlights include the **Players Club** (16 Gramercy Park South), The National Arts Club (15 Gramercy Park South), and a Holocaust memorial east of the **Brotherhood Synagogue**. The Brotherhood Synagogue is located at 28 Gramercy Park South. Gramercy Park, located between Lexington Avenue between East 20th and East 21st Streets, can be reached via Subway 6, R or W to 23rd Street.
