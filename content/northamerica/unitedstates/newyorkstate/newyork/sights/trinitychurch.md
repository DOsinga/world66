The Trinity Church is one of the more known churches of New York. She is in prominent neighborhood to Wall Street. It is the service place for a municipality of the Episcopal church. The topical building was established in 1846 in the style of the new Gothic, at that time the highest building of New York was a city, nevertheless, has been surpassed, in the meantime, by the surrounding skyscrapers. Before the topical building there were already two predecessor's constructions from whose time also the neighbouring cemetery comes. The first church building was established in 1698. This building was destroyed in 1776 by a fire. The construction of the second building began in 1788. This building broke down in winter, 1838/1839 under the load of the snow. Still today on the cemetery of the Trinity Church there lie the graves of some of the known personalities of the US-foundation history, under others of Alexander Hamilton (1755-1804), William Bradford (1590-1657), Robert Fulton (1765-1815) and Albert Gallatin (1761-1849).
