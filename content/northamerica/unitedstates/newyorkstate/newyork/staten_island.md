The forgotten borough deserves better than its reputation as a place you pass through on the way to nowhere. The Staten Island Ferry alone is worth the trip -- a free, 25-minute ride with unbeatable views of the Statue of Liberty and Lower Manhattan skyline.

Once on the island, Historic Richmondtown is a restored colonial village worth a couple of hours. The Snug Harbor Cultural Center, a former sailors' retirement home turned arts campus, houses the Staten Island Botanical Garden and the Newhouse Center for Contemporary Art. The Jacques Marchais Museum of Tibetan Art, tucked into a hillside in Richmondtown, is one of the more unexpected cultural finds in all of New York City.

For food, Adobe Blues at 63 Lafayette Avenue does solid Southwestern cooking with an enormous beer and tequila selection.
