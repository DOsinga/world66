Plattsburgh sits on the western shore of Lake Champlain in upstate New York, with the Adirondack Mountains rising to the west and Vermont visible across the water. It is a quiet city with a long military history — the Battle of Plattsburgh in 1814 was a decisive American victory in the War of 1812, and the former Plattsburgh Air Force Base, closed in 1995, has been repurposed into a mixed-use development.

The lake is the main draw. Fishing for perch and bass is excellent, and the beaches along Cumberland Bay fill up in summer. In winter, the Adirondack foothills offer skiing and snowshoeing within easy reach. The old downtown has a handful of restaurants and shops around the City Hall district, and the Kent-Delord House Museum preserves an 1812-era officer's quarters. Plattsburgh also serves as a practical stopover for travellers driving between [Montréal](/northamerica/canada/quebec/montreal) (about 100 km north) and the Adirondacks.
