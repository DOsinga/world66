Rochester is the third-largest city in New York State, sitting on the south shore of Lake Ontario about 90 minutes west of Syracuse. It was once the world headquarters of Eastman Kodak, Bausch + Lomb, and Xerox — a trio that made it one of the richest cities per capita in America through much of the 20th century. The decline of film photography hit the city hard, but Rochester has reinvented itself around its universities (the University of Rochester, RIT), its medical sector, and a creative economy that benefits from cheap real estate and a strong cultural infrastructure left behind by the Kodak era.

The **George Eastman Museum**, housed in Kodak founder George Eastman's 1905 Colonial Revival mansion, is the world's oldest photography museum and one of the best — an essential stop for anyone interested in the history of the medium, with a collection spanning daguerreotypes to digital. The **Memorial Art Gallery** on the University of Rochester campus is a strong small encyclopedic museum, and the **Strong National Museum of Play** is an enormous, interactive celebration of toys, games, and play that children of all ages find irresistible — it houses the National Toy Hall of Fame and the International Center for the History of Electronic Games.

Rochester's other defining feature is the **Genesee River**, which cuts through the city centre and drops over three waterfalls — the **High Falls** are 96 feet and visible from a pedestrian bridge in the middle of downtown, which is an unusual sight for an American city. The river gorge below the falls, accessible from trails in the park, is surprisingly dramatic. In summer, the **Lilac Festival** in Highland Park (the largest free lilac festival in North America) draws hundreds of thousands; in winter, Rochester gets buried in lake-effect snow, which keeps the cross-country skiing culture alive.

The **Public Market** on Union Street, operating since 1905, is one of the best farmers' markets in the north-east — it runs on Saturdays and Tuesdays year-round. Eat a **Garbage Plate** at Nick Tahou Hots (a Rochester institution: a plate of macaroni salad, home fries, hot dogs, hamburgers, and meat sauce that is exactly as excessive as it sounds), drink a **Genny** (the Genesee Cream Ale, brewed here since 1878), and appreciate a mid-sized American city that is doing more with less than it once had.
