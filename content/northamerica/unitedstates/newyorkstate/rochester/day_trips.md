title: "Day Trips in Rochester"
type: section
---

There are many interesting places around Rochester that make for fantastic day trips, whether you're with family or friends. Here are a few of the best to visit.

**Seneca Falls, New York** (1 hour)

Seneca Falls was the home of the women's rights movement and you can still visit many places that commemorate women. Start with the National Women's Hall of Fame before making your way to the Women's Rights National Historic Park and the Elizabeth Cady Stanton House. Other interests include the Montezuma Winery and the Montezuma National Wildlife Refuge, where you may even see a bald eagle in majestic flight.

**[Buffalo](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/buffalo), New York **(1 hour 20 minutes)

Home of the original buffalo wing, when in Buffalo be sure to stop in at Anchor Bar where the recipe was created in 1964. Some other sites of interest are the Botanical Gardens, Bicycle Museum and Our Lady of Victory Basilica. For shopping you can stroll down Elmwood Avenue, and if you're interested in the nightlife then be sure to swing by Chippewa Street.

**[Syracuse](/northamerica/unitedstates/newyorkstate/syracuse), New York **(1 hour 30 minutes)

Syracuse is a beautiful city with lots of attractions and plenty of museums. Among the museums are the Erie Canal Museum, Everson Museum of Art, Salt Museum and the Museum of Science and Technology. When you're all museum-ed out you can visit the Rosamund Gifford Zoo, Bristol Omnitheater or Landmark Theater.

**East Aurora, New York **(1 hour 30 minutes)

East Aurora is a charming, historic town for the young at heart and those who appreciate "the old days." Vidler's is an old-fashioned five and dime shop, and the rest of Main Street is lined with similarly quaint shops. Toy Town Museum details the birth of Fisher Price toys. You can also head to the Explore and More Museum or the Millard Filmore Museum. Be sure to visit Roycroft Campus to see the various arts and crafts on display.

**[Niagara falls](/northamerica/canada/ontario/niagrafalls), New York and Canada **(1 hour 30 minutes)

Visit the American side of Niagara, Rainbow Falls, or head across to the more famous Canadian Horseshoe Falls. You can visit the casinos or stroll down the street to visit the charming shops in town. Keep in mind that all of that rushing water is noisy, and in the winter it is beautiful but extremely cold. If you can, take a ride on the Maid of the Mist and stay for nightfall when the falls light up.

**[Ithaca](/europe/greece/ithaca), New York **(2 hours)

In Ithaca you can go kayaking, swimming or hiking in Buttermilk Falls State Park and watch the birds in Sapsucker Woods. Take a cruise on Cayuga Lake or visit the Museum of Earth and the Sciencenter. The Greater Ithaca Art Trail is a must see, and depending on the season you can head out to pick your own blueberries (later summer) or apples (autumn).

**Herkimer County, New York **(2 hours 30 minutes)

Digging for diamonds is the main attraction in Herkimer County, where you can head out and mine for quartz crystals with the kids. You can also take a trip on the Adirondack Scenic Railroad or an Old Forge Lake Cruise. The county is home to some beautiful nature that is great for fishing, hiking, skiing, biking, golf and even horseback riding.
