Corning is a small city in the southern Finger Lakes, best known for the Corning Museum of Glass, one of the finest specialty museums in the country. The museum alone draws half a million visitors a year, with live glassblowing demonstrations and a collection spanning 35 centuries.

Beyond the museum, Corning's restored Market Street district is a pleasant stretch of shops, restaurants, and galleries. The Rockwell Museum, also downtown, houses an excellent collection of Western and Native American art. It is an easy stop on the way into or out of the Finger Lakes from the south, sitting right off Interstate 86.
