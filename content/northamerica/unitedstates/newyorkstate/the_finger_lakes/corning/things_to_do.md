Corning punches above its weight for a small city, with two standout museums and a walkable downtown.

### Corning Museum of Glass

The Corning Museum of Glass holds the world's largest collection of glass art and artifacts, with more than 45,000 pieces spanning 35 centuries. The Hot Glass Show lets you watch gaffers shape molten glass in front of a live audience, and the Walk-in Workshop gives visitors the chance to make their own glass creations. Kids will enjoy the interactive exhibits on the science of glass. Allow at least half a day.

**Address:** One Museum Way, Corning, NY 14830. **Hours:** 9am-5pm daily (until 8pm in summer). **Phone:** 1-800-732-6845. **Web:** [cmog.org](http://www.cmog.org)

### Rockwell Museum of Western Art

A fine collection of original paintings and bronze sculptures dedicated to the American West, with major works by Frederic Remington and Charles Russell. The museum also holds a significant collection of Native American art. It is rarely crowded, making for a relaxed visit.

**Address:** 111 Cedar Street. **Hours:** daily 8am-5pm. **Phone:** 607-937-5386. **Web:** [rockwellmuseum.org](http://www.rockwellmuseum.org)
