There is lots of fine dining for vacationers and residents to experience in Asheville, NC. At "Vicenzo's Italian Ristorante and Bistro" there are award-winning choices of the finest wines and luscious desserts. There's an abundance of coffee choices and delicious foods in a smoke free restaurant with live Jazz performances every night. At "La Caterina Trattoria, there is Italian dishes prepared traditional ways. At "The Greenery" you can order very creative and different foods like trout, lamb, or even crab cakes served in a tranquil environment. "The Grove Park Inn and Resort and Spa" has wonderful cooking. They have been presented with many honors including the "AAA's Four Diamond Award". The "Cafe Soliel" has an outdoor patio along with graceful music and you can watch the cooks as they make fresh crepes right in front of you. "The Boathouse Restaurant" offers "simply fresh seafood" in a surrounding with a one-of-a-kind structural design. "Province Restaurant" was voted as the "best of 2003" and is open from lunch through dinner seven days a week. And there is also "Cafe on the Square" where their menu changes every season and presents a broad variety of fine foods. These restaurants are only a few of the fine dining experiences that the Asheville area has to offer. There are many more restaurants with succulent foods and inviting atmospheres to go to as well. (Asheville)

But Asheville has more restaurants to offer than just fine dining. There are many more wonderful places for people to enjoy great food and fun. For instance, "Limones" restaurant has Mexican-Californian food. Their menu includes "grilled au natural rib-eye stake" served with their special "hazelnut-ancho Chile sauce", homemade sausage, and sweetbread. There are other things to order there as well. And at "Zen Sushi" you can get real Japanese food any day of the week. At the "Urban Burrito" they have connoisseur burritos, salads, chips, and tacos for great prices. Their food is great for adults and children of all ages. And at the "Tupelo Honey Caf?" you can sit outside on the sidewalk and watch the exciting street life as you dine on Southern foods that the New York Times critics adore. There are all sorts of exciting restaurants to go to in the Asheville, NC area. You can get almost any kind of food you could imagine, while enjoying it in a pleasant atmosphere. (Asheville)

### [Tupelo Honey Cafe](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/asheville/eating_out/tupelo_honey_cafe)

Serves upscale interpretations of southern favorites such as fried green tomatoes over grits, fried peanut butter and honey sandwiches, and breaded chicken. The waitstaff is a cross-section of local flair, and the art and poetry on the walls are from local artists. Sit at the counter to joke with the kitchen staff, or sit outside to people-watch.

Popular for brunch and dinner, so plan on a wait, up to an hour on the weekends.
