---
title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

The **Biltmore Estate** is the must-see attraction. George Vanderbilt's 250-room French Renaissance chateau took six years to build and opened in 1895. The house tour takes a couple of hours, and the gardens and winery deserve at least another half day. The estate also has miles of walking and biking trails.

The **Blue Ridge Parkway** runs right past Asheville and offers some of the finest scenic driving in eastern America. In autumn, the leaf colors are spectacular. Stop at any of the overlooks for views that stretch across multiple mountain ridges.

Downtown Asheville rewards a long walk. Browse the galleries on Biltmore Avenue, catch street musicians on Pack Square, and duck into the independent bookstore **Malaprop's** -- everyone in town knows where it is. The **River Arts District** along the French Broad River is home to dozens of working artist studios.

Asheville is the craft beer capital of the South. Over 30 breweries operate within city limits, and many offer tours and tastings. The **South Slope** neighborhood near downtown has the highest concentration.

The surrounding **Pisgah National Forest** offers hiking for every level, from easy waterfall walks to strenuous summit trails. The forest is also popular for mountain biking, rock climbing, and fly fishing.

In summer, the **Bele Chere** street festival fills downtown with music and food. Fall brings **Brewfest**, celebrating the city's craft beer culture.
