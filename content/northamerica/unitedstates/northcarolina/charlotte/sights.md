---
title: 'Sights in Charlotte'
type: section
---

**Historic Latta Plantation** is a restored cotton plantation on the shores of Mountain Island Lake. The site includes the 1800 plantation house, slave cabins, and farm buildings, offering an honest look at life in the antebellum South. Costumed interpreters bring the history to life.

**Historic Rosedale** is another well-preserved plantation house, built in 1815, set among nine acres of gardens. Tours cover both the architecture and the stories of the people -- enslaved and free -- who lived there.

For a more casual experience, take a stroll through one of Charlotte's historic neighborhoods. **Dilworth** and **Myers Park** have tree-lined streets, early twentieth-century homes, and a walkable pace that contrasts pleasantly with the bustle of Uptown.

**Discovery Place** is one of the best hands-on science museums in the Southeast, with interactive exhibits, a planetarium, and an IMAX theatre. It is excellent for families but interesting enough for adults too.
