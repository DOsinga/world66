Durham is the gritty, creative corner of the Research Triangle, forming one of three points alongside [Chapel Hill](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/chapelhill) and [Raleigh](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/raleigh). Once known primarily as a tobacco town -- the old Lucky Strike factory still dominates the skyline -- Durham has reinvented itself around Duke University, a booming food scene, and one of the most interesting downtown revivals in the South.

The **American Tobacco Campus**, a redeveloped warehouse district along the railroad tracks, is the heart of the new Durham -- restaurants, offices, and a minor league baseball stadium where the Durham Bulls play. The food scene is exceptional for a city this size, with more than 300 restaurants ranging from North Carolina barbecue to Ethiopian and Caribbean.

Duke University's campus is worth a visit even if you have no connection to the school. The **Duke Chapel**, a Gothic limestone tower, is one of the most striking buildings in the state, and the **Sarah P. Duke Gardens** offer 55 acres of landscaped grounds that are free and open to the public.
