**Hanging Rock State Park**

Hanging Rock State Park is an hour and a half north of Greensboro. There's no other better way to get here than to drive. The natural sites are amazing. Once you arrive at Hanging Rock you will be met with both beauty and adventure. Visitors can hike the rocky mountain trails. Just be sure to wear the proper footwear and watch your step. Boating and fishing are also popular here. Private boats are not permitted, but there are rowboats and canoes available to rent. Fishing from the shoreline is permitted and best if your with a large group. Swimming is another activity at Hanging Rock State Park. However, there is more here than outdoor fun. Stop by the Exhibit Hall in the Visitors Complex and learn about the cultural and environmental history of the area. You will have a fantastic time here and return to Greensboro sleepy from your eventful day.

**Mount Airy**

Mount Airy is a little over an hour from Greensboro and is mostly accessible via car. The drive there is very stunning and should not be missed. Mount Airy is the town where Andy Griffith grew up and the place that Mayberry is based upon. Whether or not you are fans of the classic television series, visitors will enjoy the small-town ease of Mount Airy. A walk down Main street is simply amusing and delightful. Quaint southern shops and small cafes are scattered about Main street and offer visitors a sample of true southern hospitality. Mount Airy is set against the backdrop of breathtaking mountains. This day-trip is prefect for the traveler who wants to have a relaxing time on their vacation. A nice walk or even a lovely drive through Mount Airy is enough to relieve any ounce of stress you may have.

**Old Stone Winery**

Old Stone Winery is located in Salisbury, North Carolina and is close to an hour away from Greensboro. It is actual a working winery and sells its own brand of wine. A vineyard is onsite and grows the very grapes that become wine. Tasting are available for $3.00 a glass. It does seem pricey for just a tasting, but you receive a souvenir glass to take home. Tours are also available upon request and with the price of a tasting. On some occasions the tank room may be closed to the public, yet there are large windows that allow you to look in on the action. The winery is closed on Mondays and open Tuesday through Saturday 11:00 am until 6:00 pm. Sunday hours are 1:00 pm untill 5:00 pm. The Winery is simply beautiful and a tour through it is very interesting. Adults will enjoy this day-trip the most.
