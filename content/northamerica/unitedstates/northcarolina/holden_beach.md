Holden Beach is a quiet family island on the southern coast of North Carolina, 45 minutes north of Myrtle Beach and 45 minutes south of [Wilmington](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/wilmington). Getting here the first time takes some navigating -- there are no major roads leading directly to the island. Take I-95 or I-40 to Highway 17, then follow the signs from the town of Shallotte. Stock up on supplies there before crossing the bridge to the island.

What makes Holden Beach unusual is its east-west orientation. The sun rises over the ocean in the morning and sets back into it in the evening -- something very few beaches on the East Coast can offer. This is especially dramatic during fall and winter months, when the beach is nearly empty and belongs to you and the sandpipers.

Summer brings the island to life. The restaurants fill up with vacationers, the ocean warms into the low 80s, and a southerly breeze blows off the water. There are golf courses within minutes of the island, charter fishing boats that know the local waters, and rental companies that can supply everything from bicycles to beach chairs so you travel light.
