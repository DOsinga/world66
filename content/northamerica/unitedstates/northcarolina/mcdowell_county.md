McDowell County sits like the hub of a wheel between Lake Lure, [Asheville](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/asheville), and Grandfather Mountain -- a convenient base for a North Carolina mountain vacation. Part of the Blue Ridge National Heritage Area, the county is three-quarters forested, with 67,000 acres in the Pisgah National Forest.

[Old Fort](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/old_fort) has Andrew's Geyser, a train station with a museum, and the Mountain Gateway Museum for Appalachian history. The **Appalachian Artisan Society Gallery** and **Turtle Creek Pottery** are worth a stop, and on Friday nights you can catch free country music at Old Fort Mountain Music.

[Marion](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/marion), the county seat, offers Main Street shopping, arts, and good restaurants. See the Marion page for details.

**Lake James State Park** has a 6,510-acre lake with boat ramps, canoe rentals, and excellent fishing. Largemouth bass run from April through October, walleye is a prized catch, and crappie are best from May through July. Bluegill, catfish, and perch are good year-round.

The county has many hiking trails and waterfalls. **Linville Falls**, with three levels of pools plunging into the Linville Gorge, is the most popular -- it is an easy walk to the falls, though the Gorge Trail below is more demanding. **Linville Caverns** nearby offers guided tours through dolomite and limestone caves with stalactites, stalagmites, and blind trout living in underground streams.

Festivals run through the year: the NC Gold Festival and Pioneer Day in April, the Blue Ridge Native American Pow-Wow in May, July Fourth parades in both towns, Folkmoot USA international folk festival in July, and Octoberfest in Old Fort and the Mountain Glory Festival in Marion in the fall.
