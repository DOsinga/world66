McDowell County sits at the hub of the North Carolina mountains, positioned between Lake Lure, [Asheville](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/asheville), and Grandfather Mountain. Part of the Blue Ridge National Heritage Area, the county is three-quarters forested, with 67,000 acres in the Pisgah National Forest — making it a natural base for anyone exploring the western end of the state.

The two main towns each have a distinct character. [Old Fort](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/old_fort) is a small mountain gateway with an active arts scene, free Friday-night bluegrass jams, and the Mountain Gateway Museum for Appalachian history. [Marion](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/marion), the county seat, is the more substantial of the two, with good restaurants, independent shops, and a lively arts council on Main Street.

**Lake James State Park** anchors the county's outdoor life: a 6,510-acre lake with boat ramps, canoe rentals, and fishing for largemouth bass, walleye, crappie, and perch. **Linville Falls**, just to the north, drops through three levels of pools into the Linville Gorge — an easy walk to the overlook, a more demanding scramble down the Gorge Trail. **Linville Caverns** nearby runs guided tours through dolomite and limestone caves where blind trout swim in underground streams.

Festivals mark the calendar throughout the year: the NC Gold Festival and Pioneer Day in April, the Blue Ridge Native American Pow-Wow in May, Folkmoot USA international folk festival in July, and the Mountain Glory Festival in Marion each fall.
