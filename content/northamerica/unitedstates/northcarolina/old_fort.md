Old Fort is a small mountain town in [McDowell County](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/mcdowell_county), sitting at the eastern gateway to the Blue Ridge. It is a modest place — a few hundred people, a main street, a train depot — but it has an easy, unpretentious character and more to offer than its size suggests.

**Andrew's Geyser**, a few minutes from the center of town, is a curiously delightful sight: a fountain created in the 1880s to honor the railroad engineer who conquered the Swannanoa Gap. The old **train station** houses a small museum, and the **Mountain Gateway Museum** down the street digs into Appalachian history with genuine care. For local craft, **Talis Gallery** in the Appalachian Artisan Society building sells eclectic art, beads, and photography, and **Turtle Creek Pottery** is a short drive up Bat Cave Road.

**Fort Davidson**, a reconstruction of a pioneer-era stockade, is a work in progress and hosts an annual Civil War event in early summer. On Friday evenings, **Old Fort Mountain Music** fills a local venue with free country and old-time music — you can jam, dance, or just sit back and listen.
