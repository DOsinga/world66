The Outer Banks is a chain of narrow barrier islands stretching along the North Carolina coast, connected to the mainland by bridges and ferries. These windswept islands are where the Wright Brothers first flew at Kitty Hawk, where Blackbeard prowled the inlets, and where the mysterious Lost Colony of Roanoke vanished without a trace.

The northern beaches -- Corolla, Duck, Kill Devil Hills, Nags Head -- are the most developed, with vacation rental houses, restaurants, and surf shops. Further south, [Hatteras Island](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/hatteras_island) is quieter and more wild, with some of the best surfing and fishing on the East Coast. [Ocracoke Island](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/ocracokeisland), reachable only by ferry, is the most remote and peaceful of all.

Wild mustangs roam the dunes of Corolla, the descendants of Spanish colonial horses. Lighthouses dot the islands -- the Cape Hatteras Lighthouse, at 198 feet, is the tallest brick lighthouse in the country. And the beaches themselves are wide, uncrowded compared to most East Coast resorts, and backed by dunes rather than high-rises.

The Outer Banks is a year-round destination, though summer is peak season. Fall and spring bring milder weather, fewer crowds, and excellent birdwatching. Winter is quiet but beautiful, with dramatic storms and empty beaches.
