Cape Hatteras Lighthouse, at 198 feet (60 metres), is the tallest brick lighthouse in the United States. Its distinctive black-and-white spiral stripe pattern makes it one of the most recognisable lighthouses on the Atlantic coast. Built in 1870 to warn ships of the Diamond Shoals — a shallow underwater bank where the Labrador Current meets the Gulf Stream — it has guided maritime traffic for over 150 years. The lighthouse is open to climb seasonally; the view from the top takes in both the Atlantic and Pamlico Sound.
