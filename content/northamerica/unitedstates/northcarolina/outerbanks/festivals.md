---
title: 'Festivals in the Outer Banks'
type: section
---

The Outer Banks has a lively festival calendar that stretches well beyond summer beach season.

**Wings Over Water** in early May is a weekend celebrating the natural preserves and wildlife refuges along the coast. Bird watchers come from across the country to explore the marshes and learn about the fragile coastal environment. The **Purple Martin Roost Festival** in Manteo in August celebrates the charming birds that nest in the area.

**Wild Horse Days** in Corolla in June benefits the wild mustang herds that roam the northern beaches. The festival includes guided Jeep tours to see the horses, educational programs, and family activities. Proceeds go toward preserving the remaining herds.

**Dare Days** in June is a multi-day celebration of Dare County heritage, highlighting the colonial history of one of the earliest-founded counties in North Carolina. Later in August, **The Lost Colony** -- the longest-running outdoor drama in the country -- reenacts the story of Virginia Dare and the mysterious Roanoke settlement.

As summer winds down, the **Knotts Island Peach Festival** offers two days of music, orchard picking, baked goods, and peach wine. In October, the **Currituck Food and Wine Festival** brings local vineyards together to share the harvest, with sweet muscadine wines, fruity dessert wines, and local cuisine.
