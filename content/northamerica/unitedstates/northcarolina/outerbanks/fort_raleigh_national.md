Fort Raleigh National Historic Site on Roanoke Island commemorates the site of the first English attempts to establish a permanent settlement in North America in the 1580s. The colonists — including Virginia Dare, the first English child born in the Americas — vanished without explanation, leaving behind only the carved word "CROATOAN." The visitor centre tells the story of the Lost Colony, and the adjacent Elizabethan Gardens are a formal garden memorial to the colonists. The outdoor drama The Lost Colony has been performed here every summer since 1937.
