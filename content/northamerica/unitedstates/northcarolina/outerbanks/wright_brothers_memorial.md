The Wright Brothers National Memorial marks the site where Orville and Wilbur Wright made the first successful powered flights on December 17, 1903. The monument at Kill Devil Hills stands on a granite shaft atop Big Kill Devil Hill, the dune from which the brothers launched their glider tests.

The visitor center contains replicas of the 1903 Flyer and reconstructed camp buildings, and exhibits trace the brothers' incremental development of controlled, powered flight. Stones in the ground mark the takeoff and landing points of each of the four flights made that day -- the longest covered just 852 feet.

The memorial is run by the National Park Service. Admission is $15 per person, and it's open year-round. December 17 brings an annual celebration of the anniversary, complete with a flyover. Give yourself at least an hour here; the hilltop monument alone is worth the walk for the views across the dunes to the ocean.
