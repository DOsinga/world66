The Wright Brothers National Memorial marks the site where Orville and Wilbur Wright made the first successful powered flights on December 17, 1903. The monument at Kill Devil Hills stands on a granite shaft atop Big Kill Devil Hill, the dune from which the brothers launched their glider tests. The visitor centre contains replicas of the 1903 Flyer and reconstructed camp buildings, and exhibits trace the brothers' incremental development of controlled, powered flight. Stones in the ground mark the takeoff and landing points of each of the four flights made that day.
