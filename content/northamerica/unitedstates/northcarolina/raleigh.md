Raleigh is the state capital and one of the most livable cities in the American South. The unofficial motto is "Trees, Tees, and PhDs" -- a nod to its 150-plus parks, excellent golf courses, and the concentration of universities in the Research Triangle.

You can spend a morning exploring three free state museums within walking distance of each other: the **North Carolina Museum of Art**, the **Museum of History**, and the **Museum of Natural Sciences**. All are genuinely good, not just good-for-free.

After the museums, stroll through **Historic Oakwood**, a Victorian-era neighborhood listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The tree-lined streets, ornate houses, and old graveyard full of prominent Raleigh figures make for a pleasant walk. Several other historic homes throughout the city are open for tours.

The **State Farmers Market** is one of the largest in the Southeast and a great place to buy seasonal produce, baked goods, and local honey. The restaurants inside serve Southern comfort food that draws locals year-round.

With more than 150 parks, gardens, lakes, and greenways, Raleigh offers plenty of outdoor recreation. The **Neuse River Trail** runs for nearly 30 miles and is popular with runners, cyclists, and walkers.
