---
title: 'Day Trips from Raleigh'
type: section
---

**North Carolina Zoo** -- An hour and a half from Raleigh, this is one of the largest natural habitat zoos in the country, with more than 1,100 animals across nearly 500 acres representing North American and African ecosystems. The zoo has restaurants and snack stands, a carousel, and a children's play area. A full day trip for families.

**Edenton** -- Over two hours east of Raleigh, Edenton was the first permanent settlement in North Carolina. The colonial architecture from the late 1700s is remarkably well preserved, and the waterfront is beautiful. The town was a major European trading post in its early years, and the surrounding countryside is dotted with plantations. Worth the drive for history lovers.

**Bald Head Island** -- Three hours south of Raleigh, this car-free island is reached by ferry from Southport. You get around by foot, bike, or golf cart. The beaches are quiet, the kayaking is excellent, and the seafood restaurants are reliably good. The pace of life here is deliberately slow -- a perfect antidote to the city.
