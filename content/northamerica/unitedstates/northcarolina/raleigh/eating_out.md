If you are looking for good seafood, then one of your Raleigh restaurants should be 42nd Street Oyster Bar. The fried oysters are the best. With this, try the spinach salad. The hot bacon dressing and shrimp are excellent. You wouldn't think that a seafood bar could serve good greens and black eyed peas. The greens and peas are seasoned just right, and the ham adds nice flavor. Other entrees to try are the clams, catfish, seafood penne, crabcakes, calamari, and the Norfolk style scallops. Make sure you leave room for desert-the homemade key lime pie is to die for.

For an great dining experience, check out 18 Seaboard in the old Seaboard Train Station, this Raleigh restaurant is something to look at. The design and built are just beautiful and contemporary. Guests here can dine for lunch or dinner. Stop by and enjoy entrees like warm garlic chips with warm blue cheese dressing, shrimp cocktail with bloody mary sauce, and pork shank with blue cheese grits. For dinner, relax with a glass of wine and a plate of lamb ravioli or fried shrimp with apple horseradish slaw. Reservations are accepted and can be done online through their website, 18seaboard.com.

There are plenty of Raleigh restaurants that provide comfort while serving great food. Bloombury Bistro is one of them. Located at 509 West Whitaker Mill Road, this is the restaurant for fine dining. Guests can start their meal off with tomato gazpacho, octopus & crab ceviche, and baby spinach & mushroom salad. For the entree, try the pecan crusted pork tenderloin, szechwan style beef ribs, organic turkey breast medallions over cheese tortellini, or BLT sliders. Leave room for desserts like peach & blueberry crisp or dark chocolate caramel tart. For the phone number to make reservations, visit their website, bloomburybistro.com.

One Raleigh restaurant known for serving great American fare is Bogart's American Grill at 510 Glenwood Avenue. This is definitely the place to get your drink on. Besides their extensive wine list, they have a martini menu with some originals like African Queen, Murder My Sweet, The Big Sleep, Gummie Pear, Maltese Falcon, and We're No Angels. Guests can enjoy live music while dining on delights like coconut shrimp with mango lime sauce, rack of lamb with pomegranate glaze, bacon wrapped trout with spinach-pecan stuffing, and shrimp and crab stuffed flounder. To make online reservations, visit their website at bogartsamericangrill.com.

There might be some Raleigh restaurants that might be called "funky." Busy Bee Cafe is one of them. This place has live music, great food, and is the spot to get your drink on. During the day, customers can enjoy small plates of grilled chicken sandwich with pear, turkey sandwich with avocado & bacon, fish & chips, and veggie sandwich with squash, roasted peppers, & tomatoes. Larger plates offer organic chicken, flank steak & fries, pork with sweet potato, apple, & cauliflower, and shrimp & grits. There is always something going on here. Visit their website busybeeraleigh.com to find out.

For coffee, wine, cocktails, beer, and good food, be sure to stop in at Cafe Helios at 413 Glenwood Avenue. Stop by in the morning and enjoy a cup of coffee or a specialty drink like a chai tea latte, mocha, or a cafe au lait. Breakfast food include organic oatmeal & fruit or granola and soy milk. If you go for lunch, you will find turkey avocado sandwich on sourdough bread, hummus wrap with cucumber, chicken cheese quesadilla, and tomato & mozzarella mini-pizza. For full menu, their online store, and event information, go to their website at cafehelios.com.

### [Cherry Bomb Grill](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/raleigh/eating_out/cherry_bomb_grill)

The Cherry Bomb Grill, in downtown Raleigh, is a great place to stop in for a pint. As of this writing (January 2011), it's just over a year old, but was busy all three times I visited. They offer 66 beers on tap, including 21 local (North Carolina) brews, and better-than-average bar food. The atmosphere is casual but not shabby, and the music is not too loud. The Huevos Diablo (deviled eggs made with salsa and cumin), the Hangover Burger (with a fried egg and bloody mary mayonnaise) and the Philly cheesesteak sandwich are all perfect accompaniments to a nice beer.
