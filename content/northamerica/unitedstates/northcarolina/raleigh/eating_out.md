---
title: 'Eating Out in Raleigh'
type: section
---

Raleigh's restaurant scene has grown considerably and now offers far more than just Southern staples, though those are still done very well here.

**42nd Street Oyster Bar** is a Raleigh institution for seafood. The fried oysters are the star, but do not overlook the greens and black-eyed peas -- surprisingly good for a seafood bar. The homemade key lime pie is excellent.

**18 Seaboard** occupies the old Seaboard Train Station, a beautiful contemporary space. The menu leans toward upscale Southern -- lamb ravioli, fried shrimp with apple horseradish slaw, and a strong wine list.

**Bloomsbury Bistro** on West Whitaker Mill Road is Raleigh's go-to for fine dining. The menu changes frequently but the pecan-crusted pork tenderloin and dark chocolate caramel tart are worth hoping for.

**Busy Bee Cafe** is the funkier option -- live music, good drinks, and a menu that ranges from fish and chips to shrimp and grits. Check their schedule for events.

For a casual breakfast or coffee, **Cafe Helios** on Glenwood Avenue does specialty drinks, organic oatmeal, and sandwiches in a relaxed atmosphere.

**Cherry Bomb Grill** downtown is a good stop for a pint and better-than-average bar food. They pour 66 beers on tap, including 21 North Carolina brews. The Hangover Burger -- with a fried egg and bloody mary mayonnaise -- pairs perfectly with a local craft beer.
