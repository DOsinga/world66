Rockingham is the county seat of Richmond County in the Sandhills region of [North Carolina](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina), named after the Marquis of Rockingham. For nearly 40 years it was known to NASCAR fans as the home of **Rockingham Speedway** (formerly the North Carolina Speedway), a notoriously tough one-mile oval that hosted Cup Series races from 1965 until 2004. The speedway still hosts smaller events and remains the town's most recognizable landmark.

The town itself is a quiet place of about 9,000 people, with a small downtown centered on Hancock Street. The surrounding Sandhills landscape -- sandy soil, longleaf pine, and mild winters -- gives the area a character distinct from the rest of the Carolina Piedmont. The Hitchcock Creek Greenway offers a pleasant walking trail along the creek through town.

Rockingham sits roughly halfway between Charlotte and Fayetteville on US-74, making it an easy stop on a cross-state drive. Golfers will find that the famous Pinehurst courses are only about 30 miles northeast, and the Pee Dee River provides opportunities for fishing and kayaking nearby.
