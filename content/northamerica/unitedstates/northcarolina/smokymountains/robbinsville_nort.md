Robbinsville is the county seat of Graham County, tucked into one of the most remote and scenic corners of the Smoky Mountains. This is genuine mountain country -- untouched forests, quiet lakes, and deep valleys at prices that are surprisingly affordable compared to the more developed mountain towns.

**Lake Santeetlah** and **Fontana Lake** offer boating, fishing, and kayaking in settings that feel far from civilization. The **Cherohala Skyway**, a scenic highway running from Robbinsville into Tennessee, rivals the Blue Ridge Parkway for mountain views but sees a fraction of the traffic. Hiking, Appalachian crafts, and local history round out the appeal of this quiet corner of North Carolina.
