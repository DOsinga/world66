Wilmington is one of the most beautiful cities on the North Carolina coast. Founded over 250 years ago on the shores of the Cape Fear River, it has a rich past that is visible in its architecture -- the Historic District is one of the largest listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Many historic buildings and sites are open to visitors, so you can easily spend a few hours, if not days, exploring. After the history, browse the shops in the downtown area or take a leisurely Southern stroll down streets lined with antebellum and Victorian architecture. When evening approaches, sit in **Riverfront Park** and watch the light change over the river.

At night, Wilmington comes alive. Fine dining ranges from fresh seafood to international cuisine, and jazz, blues, and live entertainment fill out the evening. The city is also a film industry hub -- sometimes called "Wilmywood" -- and the **EUE/Screen Gems Studios** lot offers tours.

The beaches are a short drive east. **Wrightsville Beach** is the closest and most popular, a classic Atlantic beach town with surf shops, restaurants, and a fishing pier. **Carolina Beach** and **Kure Beach** to the south are more relaxed, and the **Fort Fisher State Historic Site** at the southern tip tells the story of the Civil War's largest amphibious assault.
