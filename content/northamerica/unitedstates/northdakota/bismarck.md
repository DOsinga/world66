Bismarck is the capital of North Dakota and sits on the east bank of the Missouri River, a position that shaped both its founding and its character. It's not on most travellers' radar, but it punches above its weight as a state capital: the modernist skyscraper of the state capitol, nicknamed the "Skyscraper of the Prairie," is the tallest building in the state, and the former Governor's Mansion nearby is a handsome Victorian-era residence open for tours.

The North Dakota Heritage Center, attached to the State Capitol complex, is the main reason to stop — a substantial museum covering Native American history, the Ice Age, pioneer settlement and the state's natural history, all done well. Downtown also has the original Railroad Museum documenting the Northern Pacific Railway's role in opening the region.

The Missouri River is the town's best outdoor asset. The Lewis and Clark Riverboat offers sightseeing cruises in summer, and a short drive south leads to Fort Abraham Lincoln State Park, where General Custer was stationed before his fatal expedition to the Little Bighorn. The Double Ditch Indian Village Historic Site, north of town, preserves the earthworks of a large Mandan settlement.
