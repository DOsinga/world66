Fargo is far more interesting than its reputation suggests. The largest city in [North Dakota](/northamerica/unitedstates/northdakota) sits on the Red River across from Moorhead, [Minnesota](/northamerica/unitedstates/minnesota), and has grown into a genuinely lively mid-sized city — one that punches well above its weight in dining, nightlife, and arts for a place this far from anywhere.

The downtown core is worth exploring on foot. Broadway is lined with handsome early 20th-century brick buildings, and the Fargo Theatre — a beautifully preserved Art Moderne landmark from 1926 — still screens indie and classic films. The historic architecture here ranges from ornate Victorian to streamlined Deco, a reminder that Fargo was a real boomtown when the Northern Pacific Railway came through.

For history, Bonanzaville USA in neighboring West Fargo is a sprawling outdoor museum with dozens of original 19th-century buildings — a one-room schoolhouse, a general store, an old hotel — transplanted from across the plains to form a re-created frontier village. It's better than it sounds.

Three universities (North Dakota State, Minnesota State Moorhead, and Concordia) give the city a younger energy and keep the bar scene busy. The 19,000-seat FargoDome brings in major concerts and events. Winters here are brutal — this is one of the coldest cities in the continental United States — but the locals take it in stride, and there is no shortage of things to do indoors.
