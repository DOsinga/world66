Akron is a mid-sized city in northeast Ohio, about 60 km south of Cleveland, with a history defined by rubber and tires — at its peak in the early 20th century it was the rubber capital of the world, home to Goodyear, Firestone, B.F. Goodrich, and General Tire. The manufacturing base has declined, but the city has reinvented itself around polymer science and research, and the University of Akron gives it an active cultural and restaurant scene.

The **Akron Art Museum** downtown is free and has a good collection of contemporary American work. The **National Inventors Hall of Fame** celebrates the country's significant inventors. Canton, about 25 km south, is home to the **Pro Football Hall of Fame** — worth the detour for anyone interested in American football history.

The Cuyahoga Valley National Park, a narrow strip of protected valley between Akron and Cleveland, offers hiking, cycling, and a scenic railroad.
