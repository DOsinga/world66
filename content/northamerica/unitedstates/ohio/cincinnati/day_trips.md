---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

For a quick escape from Cincinnati, there are tons of places to go less than two hours away. Some are really entertaining and have many great attractions, but be careful not to choose a sleepy, little ghost town where you'll be less than impressed. Most places are so close that driving is best and most convenient mode of transport. However, buses are available throughout the area as well to take you wherever you want to go for the day.

**Kentucky**

This state is close to Cincinnati; in fact, you can be in a ferry crossing over to Kentucky in only about 15 minutes. Why take a ferry? Well, it saves city driving time and also, it's just fun! The Anderson Ferry has been in use since 1817 and costs only $4 per car. It departs from a location 8 miles West of downtown Cincinnati and arrives about 3 miles from the airport in Kentucky.

When you arrive, travel to the nearby town of Crittenden. Here you can explore the Curtis Gates Lloyd Wildlife Management. This little-known place was created carrying out the will of Curtis Gates Lloyd who wanted his land to be a refuge for wildlife and plant life. Red Oaks, Black Walnuts, and Poplars fill the area, making it one of the biggest virgin timber forests in the state. There is also a granite statue that Lloyd dedicated to himself with a sarcastic message carved into it.

If you go slightly further South on US-25, you can visit Big Bone Lick State Park and see the bones of all the huge prehistoric animals that died there, including the giant sloth and the giant bison. Afterward, stop back in Crittenden for a bite to eat at one of the cozy, homestyle eateries in the area like Mr. Herb's Restaurant or Thornberry's Bakery.

**Bloomington, Indiana**

If you drive about 2.5 hours West of Cincinnati, you'll come to Bloomington. Known for its distinctive limestone architecture, this university town is a truly eclectic destination. Take one of the informative historic walking tours through the city to learn more about it. You'll pass through the mile long Cottage Grove Historic District, the Rose Hill Cemetary, and the Vinegar Hill Limestone Historic District, famous for its local crafts and folk art. In the evening, there's plenty to do, especially during summer. Window shop at some of the unique stores in the center of town, or stop for a drink at a local bar like The Irish Lion or the Upland Brew Pub.
