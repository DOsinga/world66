There are some good Cincinnati restaurants, but for great Indian food, try Ambar Indian Restaurant at 350 Ludlow Avenue. Ambar has created some dishes that are uniquely their own. Be sure not to miss the chicken tandoori or the chicken tikka. Other great dishes here are the lamb vindaloo, shrimp masala, and shrimp bhuna. For dinner, feast on their shrimp and lamb curry or the Ambar India Special which is a portion of a few dishes served on a platter. There is a vegetarian menu section as well with such as aloo choley (chickpeas and potatoes) or malai kofta (vegetables).

For those of us who love Brazilian steakhouses, a Cincinnati restaurant to check out is Boi Na Braza Churrascaria on 441 Vine Street. Customers can enjoy salads and appetizers such as artichokes, rice & beans, salami, and over 20 different types of salads. When the gauchos come around, try the delicious grilled meats like rump roast, top sirloin, lamb chops, chicken breast with bacon, mini chicken legs, beef tenderloin, and pork loin. Relax with a cocktail from their extensive drink menu. If you still have room for dessert, try the chocolate mousse, crème brulee, key lime pie, or papaya creme.

Remember 70's Funkadelic? If so, then you don't want to miss a chance to dine at Bootsy's. This Cincinnati restaurant is named after Funkmaster Bootsy Collins. Customers can enjoy a great meal while listening to some cool music. The menu consists of entrees with a Latin flavor like caesar espanola salad, potatoes espana, chorizos, duck tacos, frijoles negros, margarita flatbread, tuna atun, filet mignon of pork cerdo, pulled cuban short ribs, mussels & beer, and braised pork belly. The restaurant is open for lunch service as well and feature seafood paella, manchego ravioli, kobe skirt steak, and breaded flounder filet.

For an excellent steakhouse, make the choice and dine at The Celestial. This is the only Cincinnati restaurant to be awarded "four stars" and "four diamonds." Guests can enjoy appetizers like foie gras, crab cake sliders, baked oysters, shrimp gnocchi, and bay scallops. Main course choices include beef short ribs, veal strip loin, grilled swordfish steak, cajun tilapia, chilean sea bass, and lobster saltimbocca. The steaks come in several cuts and varieties. Examples are filet mignon, sirloin with shrimp scampi, NY strip, Kansas City strip, rib-eye, and porterhouse. Reservations are recommended and can be made online at thecelestial.com.

In the mood for Italian? Then check out Campanello's Restaurant at 414 Central Avenue. This Cincinnati restaurant offers lunch and dinner service, as well as Sunday brunch. Stop by to enjoy entrees like anitpasto salad, spaghetti with italian sausage, homemade lasagna, cheese ravioli, and manicotti. American foods is available also. Examples includes hoagies that come in many choices like meatball, italian, steak, roast beef, and tuna. Hot plates of swiss steak or top round beef come with mashed potatoes, vegetable, and italian bread. Reservations can be online, but they must be made four days in advance. Their website is campanellos.com.

Baseball fan? Then you can't leave town without stopping at Game Day Sports Cafe on 537 Pete Rose Way. This eatery is everything baseball and is close to the ballpark. Customers can watch the game on one of their 16 large high-definition flat screen televisions. Enjoy the game while munching on buffalo wings, half-pound burgers, fried ravioli, baby back ribs, club sandwich, chicken tenders, grilled chicken breast sandwich, chili, soft pretzels, mozzarella cheese sticks, beer battered onion rings, burger sliders, nachos, and turkey pretzel sandwiches. For more information, including live music schedule, check out their website at gamedaysportscafe.com.
