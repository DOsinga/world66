Cincinnati is the home to The Cincinnati Opera and The Cincinnati
Symphony. Both are world class standard and well worth visiting. The
Cincinnati Symphony plays at Music Hall, 1243 Elm Street. The 111 year
old orchestra is now headed by dynamic young conductor Pavo Järvi, who
succeeded Jesús López-Cobos (now Music Director Emeritus).

The Opera is also based at Music Hall, 1243 Elm Street. It features a
summer festival of about 4 operas that take place from mid to end of
July. For more information about actual events at these locations try
www.opus1classical.com.

### [Game Day Sports Cafe](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cincinnati/nightlife_and_ente/game_day_sports_ca)

Game Day is a brand new sports bar located within walking distance of both the Cincinnati Reds and Cincinnati Bengal's stadiums. It contains several HD televisions, cornhole, food, drinks, free popcorn and music. There are live bands every Wednesday, Friday and Saturday - NO COVER. Parking is conviently right across the street.

### [Main Street Entertainment District](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cincinnati/nightlife_and_ente/main_street_entert)

The Main Street Entertainment District in the heart of the Over The Rhine neighborhood of Cincinnati (the largest historic district in America) features many late night art studios, bars, clubs, live music venues, coffeehouses and cafes.
