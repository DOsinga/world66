Cleveland has an excellent mix of bars and cafes. Some of the city's trendiest are easily found downtown in the Warehouse District, Lower Euclid Avenue District. Ohio City, Tremont, and Little Italy bars and cafes are among the city's most pleasent, laid back and enjoyable. Coventry Village has a number of bars and cafes that serve the city's college student population. There are nice sports bars to be easily found in the Gateway District near Jacobs Field and Q Arena. Some of Cleveland's best bars and cafes both trendy and dive, however are often obscure and rather isolated from others througout the city.

### [Treehouse](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/bars_and_cafes/treehouse)

This pub in Tremont gets its name from the tree behind the bar in the center of the pub. It has achieved landmark status in the neighborhood with a large mural painted on an entire side of the building depicting a a tucan bird with a pint of Guiness on its beak proclaiming "Lovely day for a Guiness". It is an important stop on any Tremont pub crawl, and a worthy pub destination no matter where you are in the Cleveland area. They also offer a decent bar fare menu, and an excellent patio. Occasionaly they offer live bands playing Irish music.

### [Presti's Bakery and Cafe](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/bars_and_cafes/prestis_bakery_an)

This bakery in Little Italy has been around for a long time, and one of the most well-known bakery and cafes not only in Little Italy, but the Cleveland area. You can drop in and sample terrific specialty coffees, pasteries, homemade gelato, artisan bread, pizza, gourmet sandwhiches, and delicious desserts. The cafe has tall windows and a fresh open interior. The place can get quite busy, but there is ample seating inside and outside on the sidewalk. Cleveland's McNulty's Bier Markt is no exception. The atmosphere is truly reminiscent of a typical modern day European bar. ### [Mercury Lounge](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/bars_and_cafes/mercury_lounge)

If you are a hipster, then you will fit right in at Cleveland's Mercury Lounge. Its dark wood floors, sleek contemporary sofas, soft lighting, and white walls provide a cosmopolitan atmosphere where good-looking younger people dress up and drink martinis. This is more of the place to go and a chill with a couple drinks and close friends, rather than the type of place to go wild, dance and meet new people. It is a very fitting establishment for Cleveland's Warehouse District and thrives on the trendy address factor of W.6th.



tel: 216.566.8840

address: 1392 W.6th

### [Johnny Mango's Cafe](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/bars_and_cafes/johnny_mangos_caf)

Johnny Mangos is also one of the few "juice bars" in Cleveland, but also offers alcoholic beverages as well.
