---
title: "Day Trips"
type: section
---

There are a number of great small New England style towns and recreational opportunities outside the city that make great day trips. Chagrin Falls to the south east of Cleveland is very nearby and perhaps the most worth of a day trip. It has to be one of the most eclectic and hippest small towns in the country. It has quaint shops, such as its famous popcorn shop, funky places like Dave's Cosmic Subs, trendy places like Starbucks and Ben & Jerry's but then out of no where there are really upscale shops like Paris-based Hermes?! (one of three in the Midwest). There are also great restaurants, and one in particular, Blake's Seafood has excellent window views of the picturesque waterfall. On the way to Chagrin Falls on Chagrin Boulevard is a small, but very nice upscale shopping center with some unique stores and restaurants (called Eton Collection).

To the south of Cleveland and between Cleveland and Akron there lies the Cuyahoga Valley National Park, which is certainly worthy of day trip. Near the park is a little historic hamlet village called Peninsula with little shops and restaurants, and also a hostel. This is one day trip that could also be done in ways other than car. There is bike path from downtown Cleveland called the Towpath trail that follows along the old Erie Canal route, and a scenic train route that makes various stops in the Cuyahoga Valley.

Cedar Point in Sandusky is one of the most favorite summer destinations for Clevelanders. This peninsula over the Lake Erie boasts more roller-coasters and rides than anywhere else in the world. It also has an excellent reputation for being one of America's cleanest, safest, and nicest parks. The Lake Erie Islands nearby make great day trips, and they are full of camp-grounds, wineries, bed & breakfasts, cottage rentals, marinas, and beaches. When driving out to the Sandusky Bay area you might also want to consider a stop at the historic white-wash harbor town of Vermilion, located between Cleveland and Sandusky, and ejoy a real soda fountain.
