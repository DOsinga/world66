You certainly will not need a car to get around downtown. It is very compact and safe to walk around. Downtown Cleveland also has numerous signs and maps that let you know where you are and indicate what district you are in.

Cleveland and Cuyahoga County are served by the Greater Cleveland Regional Transit Authority. This public transit system combines bus and light rail service (known as the Rapid). The city's transit hub is located at Tower City Center downtown Cleveland. In the lower levels of Tower City you will find a helpful RTA customer service desk and brocheres available for each transit route. From Tower City using RTA is the recommended way to reach destinations like the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Great Lakes Science Center on E.9th Street Pier, the West Side Market in Ohio City, Shaker Square, and University Circle. The University Circle Rapid station links with a couple notworthy circulator buses. The Circlelink links all the museums at cultural insitutions within University Circle. The RTA 821 circulator bus connects Unvirsity Circle with Little Italy, Coventry, Cedar-Fairmont, and Shaker Square. Tower City enjoys a convenient and direct link to Hopkins International airport on the Red line Rapid Route. Fares are only $3 for an all day pass good on all RTA rapid and bus routes.

In front of Tower City Center on Public Square and in front of the Marriot Hotel in the Civic and Convention Center District there are taxi cab stands.

Should you need to get around further than Cleveland at any point it is recommended you rent a car. At the airport there are a number of rental car providers. Traffic is light, parking rates downtown Cleveland are very reasonable and the streets are very easily navigable compared to other big cities.

### [Greater Cleveland RTA](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/getting_around/greater_cleveland)

A decent public transit system that serves Cleveland and Cuyahoga County through bus routes and rapid transit with a hub at Tower City, and various community circulator buses. This should help visitors without cars get around very well to major attractions and neighborhoods when visiting without taking a cab.

### [Lolly the Trolley](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/getting_around/lolley_the_trolley)

This is one of Cleveland's well known and popular sightseeing tours. It is a narrated tour that shows you all of the sights and attractions throughout the city while learning about them at the same time. These tours operate year round, and are a good way to start your visit in the city to aquaint yourself with the surroundings and history- not to mention having a fun time on this historic-looking trolley vehicle Reservations are required, so call ahead of time. The tours depart from the Nautica Entertainment Complex on the west bank of the Flats.



tel: 1-800-848-0173

url: [www.lollytrolley.com](http://www.lollytrolley.com/)

address: 1790 Columbus Road

### [Downtown Trolley Bus Lines](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/getting_around/downtown_trolley_b)

In April 2006 Cleveland RTA created two complimentary circulator bus routes that act like and even resemble trolleys exclusive for downtown Cleveland. This is a great way to get around downtown from one sight to another and to explore a little bit of downtown worry-free. This is not a sight-seeing tour trolley bus. The drivers do not narrate their routes like sight-seeing tours, and these trolley bus lines do not go to other parts of the city where many worthy sights are to be found.
