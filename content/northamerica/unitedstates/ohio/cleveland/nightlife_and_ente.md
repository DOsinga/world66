type: section
---

Cleveland has a vibrant nightlife scene spread across several distinct districts.

The **Warehouse District** just west of Public Square is the place to be seen. Restored neoclassical Victorian-era warehouse facades house contemporary, chic restaurants and clubs along W. 6th, St. Clair, and W. 9th. Sidewalks fill with tables, most places offer valet parking, and young professionals from the district's loft apartments mix with visitors from across the region. Be prepared to dress appropriately and spend, though a few reasonably priced spots exist.

The scene is also strong around **E. 4th and Euclid**, a narrow brick-paved alley with lights strung across the buildings. The House of Blues concert venue and Pickwick and Frolic cabaret and comedy complex anchor the area, with restaurants and bars filling in around them.

**Ohio City** along W. 25th Street offers a more laid-back neighborhood alternative. The **Great Lakes Brewing Company** is a must, known for award-winning beer brewed on-site with strict adherence to organic ingredients and Bavarian Purity Laws. A wine bar, Belgian beer bar, hookah bar, and classic rock bar add to the mix.

**Coventry Village** in Cleveland Heights draws college students and the liberally inclined to sports bars, vegetarian restaurants, bookshops, funky boutiques, coffee shops, and the intimate **Grog Shop** music venue.

**Tremont** is the artists' neighborhood, an island-like enclave cut off by freeways and the Cuyahoga River. Oddly arranged streets, diverse housing, architecturally notable churches, art galleries, dive bars, and ultra-chic lounges create a quality of randomness that could hardly be duplicated. Both Tremont and **Little Italy** host popular art walks in summer.

The **Playhouse Square** performing arts district on Euclid Avenue around E. 14th is the second-largest theater district in the country after Broadway, with five lavish early twentieth-century theaters hosting plays, ballet, opera, and classic films.

The **Gateway District** has sports venues: the Q Arena for basketball and Jacobs Field for baseball.

For dinner and a movie, **Shaker Square** is a charming option accessible by Rapid from Tower City. This beautiful 1920s Georgian-style outdoor shopping center has restaurants ranging from affordable to upscale, and a restored Art Deco cinema.

### House of Blues

Cleveland's House of Blues features up-and-coming and established artists in an intimate venue. It anchors the E. 4th and Euclid entertainment district in a converted furniture store building. Restaurant and bars on-site.

308 Euclid Avenue. Tel: (216) 523-BLUE. [hob.com](http://www.hob.com/venues/clubvenues/cleveland/)

### Pickwick and Frolic's

An excellent entertainment complex unique to Cleveland, with a comedy venue, restaurant, and cabaret. Located across from the House of Blues on E. 4th Street. Regularly ranked among the top ten comedy venues in the country.

2035 E. 4th St. Tel: (216) 241-7425. [pickwickandfrolic.com](http://www.pickwickandfrolic.com/)

### Nautica Entertainment Complex

One of the largest entertainment complexes in the country, packed into an old factory building nicknamed "the Powerhouse" on the west bank of the Flats. Major concerts, comedy, dancing, pool, restaurants, and trolley tours. Excellent views of the Cuyahoga River and the Cleveland skyline.

1231 Main Avenue. Tel: (216) 861-4080

### Playhouse Square

A Cleveland institution featuring theater, ballet, opera, and classic films in five lavish early twentieth-century theaters: the Palace, State, Ohio, Allen, and Hanna. A ten-minute walk from Public Square.

Tel: (216) 771-8403. [playhousesquare.com](http://www.playhousesquare.com/)

### The Agora

One of Cleveland's and the country's finest rock concert venues, an institution for over 40 years that has hosted many legends. For rock enthusiasts visiting the Hall of Fame, this is your best bet for live music.

5000 Euclid Ave. Tel: (216) 881-6911

### Great Lakes Brewing Company

Award-winning beer brewed on-site with organic ingredients. The food is also excellent, with ingredients grown in their own greenhouse. Tour the brewery, visit the gift shop, and pick up a Cleveland souvenir. No trip to Ohio City is complete without a stop here.

2516 Market Avenue. Tel: (216) 771-4404. [greatlakesbrewing.com](http://www.greatlakesbrewing.com/)
