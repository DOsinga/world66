Downtown Cleveland has little to offer serious shoppers. Tower City Center and to a lesser extent the Galleria serve as major city-center shopping malls. There are a number of boutiques and art galleries spread thin throughout downtown in places like the Warehouse District, the Old Arcade and Colonial Marketplace.

Outside of downtown there are two excellent neighborhoods with funky alternative shops; Ohio City and Coventry Village. Ohio City is located just west of downtown around the intersection of Lorain Avenue and W.25th. The West Side Market in Ohio City is the last municpally-owned market in the country, and is located in a beautiful building with an old world atmosphere. Coventry Village is located just east of University Circle around the intersection of Mayfield and Coventry. The two best neighborhoods for art galleries in Cleveland are the charming neighborhoods of Tremont located southwest of downtown, and Little Italy located just southeast of downtown. Tremont and Little Italy also offer some of the best dining in Cleveland.

Good antique and thrift store shopping in Cleveland is spread out along Lorain Avenue that goes through the city's west side. A good antique shopping district is also located on Larchmere Boulevard just one block of north of Shaker Square on the city's east side.

The premier shopping in the region and perhaps the entire Midwest outside of Chicago is located in the suburbs, and the collection of retailers at its major shopping centers is quite impressive with Shaker Square, Crocker Park, Beachwood Place, Legacy Village, and Eton Collection. The nice thing about these shopping centers is that they all contain some of the country's best known and exclusive retailers and restaurants with shops and establishments unique to Cleveland

It is located off I-271 in Woodmere and very near Beachwood Place and Legacy Village, and on the way to Chagrin Falls.

### [Open Air in Market Square](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/shopping/open_air_in_market)

This is a well known urban outdoor market that features a variety of vendors and live performances in Ohio City's Market Square Park across from the West Side Market. It is only open during Saturday afternoons during the summer, but Clevelanders look foward to it all year round and it draws considerable crowds. It is a nice touch and element to the Saturday summer afternoon experience of the West Side Market.

### [The Avenue at Tower City Center](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/shopping/tower_city_center)

The Avenue at Tower City has a combination of major retailers, odd shops, and an excellent foodcourt. The Avenue is the retail component of the Tower City Center. Tower City is in the heart of downtown on Public Square and connected to the city's signature skyscraper Terminal Tower, central transit hub, hotels, movie theater, restaurants, and walkways to sport complexes. Parking in Tower City garages are very inexpensive with validation from customer service with the purchase of goods.

### [Shaker Square](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/shopping/shaker_square)

Shaker Square is the predecessor to the modern day suburban shopping center. It was completed in 1929 and serves as the gateway to one of Cleveland's and the country's most distinguished and historic suburbs; Shaker Heights. Its architecture is beautiful Georgian, and the square's setting is very picturesque with the Shaker Rapid running through the center of the square. The Rapid stop at Shaker Square makes it easily accessible from Tower City for visitors. ### [Flower Child](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/shopping/flower_child)

This is one of Cleveland's most well known vintage shops. It is the perfect place to find items of gently used retro clothing, accessories, furniture and collectables to complement more modern pieces. The merchandise is not priced at thrift store prices, but are still reasonable and competive with prices of brand new merchandise. This boutique is fun to browse and a great place to find truly unique items. It is conveniently located just west of downtown in a small attractive commercial strip on Clifton Boulevard near the intersection of W.117th in Cleveland's Gold Coast neighborhood.

type: Antiques



tel: (216) 939-9933

address: 11508 Clifton Blvd.

### [Hansa Import House](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/shopping/hansa_import_house)

This shops specializes in German imports. It mostly features food items, but has a some unique gift items as well. The shop is located a block from the West Side Market on Lorain Avenue in small obscure chalet-type building. It would be a shame to miss this shop if you are in the neighborhood.
