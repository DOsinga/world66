Cleveland has a rich and diverse history with many beautiful historic buildings, monuments, and churches. Several sightseeing tour operators are available.

### West Side Market

One of Cleveland's must-see attractions. This grand Beaux-Arts building finished in 1912 is the last municipally owned market in the country. You can buy excellent meats, cheeses, produce, baked goods, pasta, and other food items from individual vendors whose families have operated stands for generations. Its clock tower is unmistakable in the skyline on the west side of the Cuyahoga.

1979 West 25th St. Open Mon/Wed 7 AM--4 PM, Fri/Sat 7 AM--6 PM. Tel: (216) 664-3387. [westsidemarket.com](http://www.westsidemarket.com/)

### The Arcade

Few buildings in the United States rival the majesty of Cleveland's Arcade. Built in 1890 and inspired by the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele in Milan, this indoor pedestrian passageway runs between E. 4th and Euclid and Superior Avenue. It was the country's first indoor shopping mall and is considered irreplaceable. Currently houses a food court popular with downtown workers during lunch.

401 Euclid Avenue. Tel: (216) 696-1408

### Rockefeller Park

Cleveland's central park, donated by John D. Rockefeller. It stretches between Lake Erie and University Circle along Martin Luther King Boulevard, winding under romantic stone bridges. The park contains trails, a lagoon, a greenhouse, and the Cultural Gardens, where every ethnic group in Cleveland's history has a dedicated garden.

### Public Square

The heart of downtown Cleveland, a park intersected by Ontario and Superior Avenues. Fountains, benches, and trees make it popular with downtown workers at lunchtime. Anchored by the city's three tallest buildings: Terminal Tower, Key Tower, and the BP Building.

### Terminal Tower

The city's signature skyscraper, completed in 1929 and once one of the tallest buildings in the world. Unusual for its era in being built in neoclassical rather than Art Deco style, making it highly identifiable. It anchors Public Square and the Tower City Center complex.

Tel: (216) 771-0033

### War Memorial Fountain

A memorial to the 4,000 Clevelanders who perished in World War II and the Korean War, with names engraved on bronze plates. The figure represents humanity reaching for peace, designed by Marshall Fredericks and installed on the Civic Center mall in 1964.

### Civic Center

A fine example of an American civic center, with local, county, and federal government buildings. The mall at its center has large grassy areas, fountains, benches, and views of Lake Erie, surrounded by neoclassical civic buildings and the Convention Center. Located between Lakeside and Rockwell Avenue along E. 6th St.

### St. John the Evangelist Cathedral

Cleveland's beautiful Gothic Catholic cathedral, a landmark on E. 9th Street in the financial district. The current structure was completed in the late 1940s with warm sandstone facade and a rich interior of deep blue, gold, red, and dark wood. Stained glass windows and a celestial scene on the ceiling. Open throughout the day.

1007 Superior Ave. Tel: (216) 771-6666

### Lakeview Cemetery

More famous people are buried here than anywhere else in Cleveland: industrialist John D. Rockefeller, President James A. Garfield, and law enforcement agent Eliot Ness, among many politicians, industrialists, and sports figures. A beautiful garden cemetery surrounded by University Circle, Little Italy, and Coventry Village.

12316 Euclid Avenue. Tel: (216) 421-2665. [lakeviewcemetery.com](http://www.lakeviewcemetery.com/)
