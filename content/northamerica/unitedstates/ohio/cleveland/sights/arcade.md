This building is the city's most beloved after Terminal Tower. Few buildings in the U.S. rival the majesty of Cleveland's Arcade. It was built in 1890 and its design drew from inspiration of the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele arcade in Milan, and is deemed absolutely irreplaceable today. This building is located just east of Public Square and serves as an indoor pedestrian passageway between the intersection of E.4th and Euclid and Superior Avenue. It was the country's first indoor shopping mall, but currently houses a popular foodcourt for downtown workers during lunch, and much of the building has been converted into a Hyatt Hotel. The building is open to the public and free of charge. Recently a new upscale restaurant called Vivo has occupied space on the Euclid Avenue side of the Arcade and a cool bar called V Lounge has an entrance down an obscure staircase inside the Arcade.
