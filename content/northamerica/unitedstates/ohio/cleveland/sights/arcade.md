The city's most beloved building after Terminal Tower. Few buildings in the United States rival its majesty. Built in 1890 and inspired by the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele in Milan, this indoor pedestrian passageway runs between E. 4th and Euclid and Superior Avenue. It was the country's first indoor shopping mall. Today it houses a food court popular with downtown workers during lunch, and much of the building has been converted into a Hyatt Hotel. Open to the public and free of charge.
