This is Cleveland's beautiful gothic Catholic Cathedral, and a landmark building on E.9th Street in the city's financial district just a few blocks east of Public Square. It is open throughout the day, and many downtown workers stop in for daily prayer, Mass, or Confession. A cathedral stood on the site for many years, but the current structure was completed in the late 1940s. The facade is made of a warm-colored sandstone, and the interior is rich with colors of deep blue, shining gold, red, and dark wood. The stain glass windows and celestial scene on the interior cieling are partiuclarly beautiful. There are peaceful chapels and the relics of the Roman Christian martyr; St. Christine on display.
