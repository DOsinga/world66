This is the city's signature skyscraper. It anchors Public Square and the city's skyline. It was completed in 1929, and was one of the tallest buildings in the world when it was completed. This skyscraper is interesting in that it was built in a neoclassical style, because most skyscrapers at this time were built in Art Deco. The result is a highly unique and identifiable building for Cleveland. The tower is one of Cleveland's most prestigeous address for downtown firms. One of the major tenants of Terminal Tower is the real estate development giant Forest City Enterprises headquarters. Forest City also owns Tower City Center of which Terminal Tower is a part of.

Tower City is a large indoor complex of houses an average shopping center, various office towers, the city's two most prestigeous hotels, restaurants, a food court, indoor walkways to sports venues, a concert venue, post office, fitness center, movie theater, and the city's transit hub in a beautiful indoor complex of atriums, fountains, and marble. It really is like a city in a tower. This gateway to the rest of the city makes it an excellent place to start your visit.
