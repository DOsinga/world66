This is one of the must-see attractions in Cleveland. While the museums, restaurants, and entertainment are all wonderful, nothing says 'Cleveland' so much as the West Side Market. This grand building finished in 1912 is done in the attractive Beaux-Arts architecture, and its clock tower is unmistankenable in the skyline on the west side of the Cuyahoga. It is the last municipally-owned market in the country, and you can buy excellent meats, cheeses, produce, baked goods, pasta, and other food items from individual vendoors whose families have operated a stand in the West Side Market for generations. This is perhaps the best spot for people watching in the city, because all different types of people speaking different languages come from all over to shop and experience the West Side Market. It really is like stepping back into America circa 1912. Very little has changed. There are a number of stands in the West Side Market for great food to take out into the Market Square Park, or up to the balcony to sit and watch the busy market crowd below. It is very convenient for visitors who can park free in the lot behind the market off Lorain Avenue, or take the Rapid to the Ohio City Station and walk across the street. There is no excuse for missing the West Side Market. Beware however of the hours, because it is not open everyday.
