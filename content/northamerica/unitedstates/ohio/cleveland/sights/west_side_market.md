One of the must-see attractions in Cleveland. Nothing says "Cleveland" quite like the West Side Market. This grand Beaux-Arts building finished in 1912 is the last municipally owned market in the country. You can buy excellent meats, cheeses, produce, baked goods, pasta, and other food items from individual vendors whose families have operated stands here for generations.

The market is one of the best spots for people-watching in the city. All different types of people from all over come to shop and experience it. Very little has changed since 1912. Grab food from one of the stands and eat in Market Square Park or up on the balcony overlooking the busy market floor. Free parking behind the market off Lorain Avenue, or take the Rapid to Ohio City station and walk across the street. Beware the hours, as it is not open every day.
