Northeast Ohio is not immediately associated with natural beauty or recreational opportunities, but Greater Cleveland has more parks and opportunities for recreation than most metropolitan areas. Excellent park systems, a national park within the metropolitan area, and miles of Lake Erie shoreline make Cleveland an excellent destination for the naturalist enthusiasts. Naturalist enthusiasts will discover all kinds of opportunities for year-round enjoyment.

### [41 North Kayak Tours](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/things_to_do/41_north_kayak_tou)

41 North offers several great kayak tours along Lake Erie, including a great tour of downtown's riverfront and lakefront. Tours meet and depart from several locations along the Lake Erie coastline depending on what tour you sign up for. Prices are reasonable, and the staff is friendly and helpful. It is a good idea to reserve space on tour in advance.

### [Cleveland Metroparks](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/things_to_do/cleveland_metropar)

This park system is one of the most unique urban parks in the country. It is a countywide system of parks that include opportunities for hiking, cycling, golf, toboganning, nature centers, fishing, geocaching, swimming, horseback riding, or simply a nice drive. ### [Rock and Roll Hall of Fame](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland/things_to_do/rock_and_roll_hall)

The museum opened on September 2 , 1995 in a building designed by I. M. Pei . During early discussions on where to build the museum, the Foundation's board considered the Cuyahoga River .
