Nobody gonna mistake Columbus for a cultural mecca, but there's definitely more going on than McDonalds and Wendys, as many people think. There are good museums, good eats, cool neighborhoods, good sports, and a truly rockin' music scene - you just have a dig a little deeper. Hey, it's not New York, it's not Los Angeles, and it's not [Chicago](/northamerica/unitedstates/illinois/chicago) - but if you really want to see what the swarming masses of middle America look like (instead of spending all of your cash and energy on the coasts) then cities like Columbus, [Indianapolis](/northamerica/unitedstates/indiana/indianapolis), [Pittsburgh](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/pittsburgh), [Cleveland](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland) are worth a visit. There's a lot to learn about the United States in these places - both good and bad - so open your mind and give it a chance.
