Columbus doesn't announce itself. It's the state capital, a large university city, and the most economically resilient place in Ohio — but it wears all of this quietly. Give it time and it rewards you.

The Short North, running north from downtown along High Street, is the most walkable part of the city: galleries, independent restaurants, coffee shops, and a bar scene that runs late on weekends. The Ohio State campus is enormous and active, with its own museums and the Oval as a natural gathering place.

Downtown has the Ohio Statehouse — worth stepping inside for the Greek Revival architecture alone — and the Columbus Museum of Art, which is better than its modest reputation suggests. The Scioto Mile along the riverfront has been cleaned up considerably and makes for a good walk or bike ride.

Columbus has a genuine music scene, stronger than most cities its size, and enough ethnic food diversity (Somali, Ethiopian, Vietnamese, Nepali) to keep explorers busy. It's not New York or Chicago, but it's far more interesting than it looks from the highway.
