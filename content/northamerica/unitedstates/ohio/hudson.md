Hudson is a small, well-preserved town on the Western Reserve, halfway between Akron and Cleveland. The village green — a classic New England-style commons — anchors a compact downtown of 19th-century brick buildings, independent shops, and restaurants. It was founded in 1799 by David Hudson, a Connecticut settler, and the New England character has stuck.

The town's chief claim to fame is as the home of Western Reserve Academy, a boarding school founded in 1826, and as the birthplace of the free-soil movement — John Brown lived here before his fateful raid on Harpers Ferry. The Hudson Library and Historical Society has a good collection on the town's abolitionist history. It is a pleasant stop if you are driving between Cleveland and Akron, with a walkable centre and a handful of good places to eat.
