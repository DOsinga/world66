Kent is a small university town on the Cuyahoga River in northeast Ohio, about halfway between Akron and Youngstown. It is best known for Kent State University and for a single day — 4 May 1970 — when Ohio National Guardsmen opened fire on students protesting the Vietnam War and the bombing of Cambodia, killing four and wounding nine others. A small museum and a marked walking trail on the campus commemorate the shootings; the site is sobering and worth an hour of anyone's time.

Beyond that, Kent is a pleasant college town with a redeveloped riverfront downtown, a busy strip of bars and restaurants along South Water Street, and a good farmers' market in summer. The Cuyahoga Valley National Park begins only a short drive to the west.
