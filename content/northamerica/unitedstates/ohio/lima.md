Lima is a midsize city in northwestern Ohio, sitting along Interstate 75 in Allen County, of which it is the county seat. It sits on the banks of the Ottawa River and was founded in 1831 — named, oddly enough, after Lima, Peru, because the quinine bark used to treat malaria in the pioneer days came from there.

The city's industrial roots run deep. Lima was a locomotive-building powerhouse in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, and the Shay Locomotive — a geared steam engine designed for hauling timber out of steep terrain — was first manufactured here in 1880. A Nickel Plate 779 locomotive is on display at the Allen County Museum, which is genuinely one of the better county historical museums in Ohio and worth a couple of hours of your time.

Other worthwhile stops include the Lima Fire Fighters Memorial Museum and Lincoln Park, which has a railway exhibit alongside trails along the Ottawa River. The MacDonell House, a Victorian mansion, is a fine example of the prosperity that the industrial era brought to Lima.

Lima has a working arts scene — the Lima Symphony Orchestra has been performing since 1953, and the city hosts Broadway touring productions. The surrounding countryside offers good reservoir fishing and a surprising number of golf courses for a city this size.
