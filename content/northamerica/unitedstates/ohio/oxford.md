Oxford is the quintessential American college town, tucked into the rolling farmland of southwest Ohio between [Dayton](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/dayton) and [Cincinnati](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cincinnati). Miami University — a public university often called a "public Ivy" — dominates the town and gives it a character far larger than its population would suggest.

The centre of town sits uphill from campus, so locals call it "uptown" rather than downtown. Brick streets, mature trees and a row of independent shops and restaurants in historic commercial buildings make it one of the more pleasant small-town main streets in the Midwest. The campus itself is worth a walk: nearly all red brick, designed in a consistent Georgian style, and praised by Robert Frost as one of the most beautiful in North America.

If you are passing through the Dayton or Cincinnati area, Oxford makes for an easy day trip — good food, lively bars, and whatever event the university happens to be hosting that weekend.
