Sandusky is a city on the southern shore of Lake Erie in northern Ohio, situated roughly halfway between Toledo to the west and [Cleveland](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland) to the east. It is best known as the home of Cedar Point, one of the most celebrated amusement parks in the world and a pilgrimage site for roller coaster enthusiasts. Cedar Point sits on a narrow peninsula jutting into Lake Erie, and its skyline of towering steel coasters is visible from miles around — at various times in its history it has held more world records for roller coasters than any other park on earth.

Beyond Cedar Point, Sandusky has a pleasantly low-key lakeside character. The downtown harbour area has been redeveloped, with restaurants and bars overlooking the bay. Ferries depart from Sandusky for the Lake Erie Islands — South Bass Island (home of Put-in-Bay, a famously rowdy summer resort) and Kelleys Island (quieter, with glacial grooves carved in the limestone). The lake itself offers fishing for walleye, perch, and smallmouth bass.

Sandusky is a logical base for a Cedar Point visit, with accommodation ranging from the park's own hotels to cheaper options in town. The park runs from May through October.
