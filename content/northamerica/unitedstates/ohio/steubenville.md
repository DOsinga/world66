City Of Murals... 

Known internationally for the outdoor gallery of gigantic historical murals lining the streets of downtown, Steubenville, Ohio ranks among the top tourist attractions in the United States. "Preserving a Piece of America" is the theme carried out by the project's guided tours. Right now there are more than 25 murals, and tourists can watch live artists at work from May to October each year, painting the city's history on its walls, larger than life. 

Steubenville, home to Dean Martin, was Voted by Readers' Digest as one of the top 20 best places to live in the US, Steubenville is home to many interesting sites. 

Steubenville was founded in 1797, and is home to Dean Martin. The stretch of Route 7 that snakes through downtown was renamed Dean Martin Boulevard, and each summer Steubenville hosts the Dean Martin Days Festival. Located in eastern Ohio, on the banks of the mighty Ohio River, Steubenville is the seat of Jefferson County . With a small population of just 20,000 people, The City of Murals is a quaint town, jam-packed with history.

**OLD FORT STEUBEN** 

In 1787, Steubenville (Then Fort Steuben) became the gateway to the Northwest Territory, protecting government surveyors while they laid out the first 7 Ranges. The restored fort now hosts an archeological dig and the annual Fort Steuben Festival. Guided tours are available.

**FIRST FEDERAL LAND OFFICE** 

Built in 1801, the first federal land office is a replica of a colonial home. Also located on the grounds is the massive 2 ½ ton bell from the Jefferson County Courthouse. Located next to Old Fort Steuben at 3rd & Adams Streets. Call for an appointment.

**HISTORIC MOUNT PLEASANT** 

25 miles southwest of Steubenville, along the Ohio Scenic Byway, is Historic Mount Pleasant. Tour six historic buildings on the National Register of Historic Places. Don't miss the Friends Yearly Meeting House, a Quaker meeting place built in 1814 that seats 2,000 people.
