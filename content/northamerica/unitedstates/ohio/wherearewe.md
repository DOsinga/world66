Ohio
 The Heart of It All


 So, you want to travel to Ohio, but aren't sure exactly where we're located, eh? Well, we're close to Canada, and our neighbor to the west in Indiana...does that help? I didn't think so...


 Well, to help out our friends from out-of-state, I've provided a few major cities you can reference when devising a travel intinerary.


 First off, [Columbus](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/columbus) is our state capital. It is serviced by a major, international airport...it also is home to our beloved Ohio State Buckeyes.


 The next major city you should consider traveling to is Cleveland. Nestled on the coast of Lake Erie, [Cleveland](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/cleveland) is home to the NFL's Browns, the NBA's Cavaliers, and easily the best baseball team in Ohio, the Indians.

There is also the Cleveland Lumberjacks, and the Rockers of the WNBA. We can't forget to mention that the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame is in Cleveland, and [Akron](/northamerica/unitedstates/ohio/akron) is just 30 minutes away.


 Third we have Cincinnati, The Queen City, lying abreast to the Ohio River. A beautious, illustrious city at night, Cincinnati boasts the most River Boats of all Ohio cities...and, oh yeah, The Bengals of the NFL - and did I mention, they now have Ken Griffey, Jr.?!?


 Overall, there are so many beautiful things to see and do in Ohio, you just have to come here and see for yourself.
