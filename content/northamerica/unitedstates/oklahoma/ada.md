Ada is a city of around 17,000 people in Pontotoc County, south-central Oklahoma, situated in the rolling Cross Timbers region between Oklahoma City and the Texas border. It serves as a commercial hub for the surrounding agricultural area and is the home of East Central University.

The city has an outsized connection to Native American governance — Ada is the headquarters of the Chickasaw Nation, one of the largest Native American nations in the United States, whose tribal complex and various government services are based here. The Chickasaw Cultural Center, located a short drive away in Sulphur, is worth the detour if you want to understand the nation's history and traditions.

Ada also claims evangelist Oral Roberts as a native son. Beyond these connections, it's a modest regional city with little to pull in travelers specifically, though it serves as a reasonable overnight stop between Dallas and Oklahoma City.
