Bartlesville in northeast Oklahoma is known as the "City of Legends." It is home to dramatic architecture, world-renowned events, and the legacy of Phillips Petroleum, one of the nation's major oil companies.

The city boomed when Frank Phillips discovered oil. His **Woolaroc Ranch** houses an impressive collection of western art, artifacts, and special exhibits. The ranch is also a wildlife preserve where over 700 animals -- including buffalo, elk, deer, and longhorn cattle -- roam freely.

Frank Lloyd Wright's **Price Tower**, the architect's only realized skyscraper, is another major draw.

If you visit in June, the **National Biplane Expo** and **OK Mozart International Festival** offer two unique experiences. A day trip to nearby Dewey takes you to the Tom Mix Museum and Prairie Song, a recreated 1800s pioneer village.
