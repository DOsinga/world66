Bethany is a small city of about 20,000 people on the western edge of [Oklahoma City](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/oklahomacity), straddling a stretch of Historic [Route 66](/northamerica/unitedstates/gettingaround/route_66__the_m). Founded in 1909 as a community centered on the Church of the Nazarene, the town still has a conservative, close-knit character and is home to Southern Nazarene University.

For Route 66 travelers, Bethany is part of the long ribbon of towns along the Mother Road through central Oklahoma. The 39th Expressway corridor through town follows the historic alignment and has a few old-school businesses that recall the road's heyday. The town's most famous resident is astronaut Shannon Lucid, who held the record for the longest spaceflight by an American and the longest by any woman when she returned from Mir in 1996.

Bethany is primarily a residential suburb and not a destination in its own right, but it makes a convenient and affordable base for visiting Oklahoma City, whose attractions -- the Oklahoma City National Memorial, the Paseo Arts District, Bricktown -- are all within a short drive.
