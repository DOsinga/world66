Broken Bow sits in the Ouachita Mountains of far southeastern Oklahoma, surrounded by 450,000 acres of Ouachita National Forest and Beavers Bend State Park. It draws visitors from Dallas, Oklahoma City, Little Rock, and [Tulsa](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/tulsa) — all within about three hours — who come for the forests, the trout streams, and the quiet.

The centerpiece is **Beavers Bend State Park**, where the Mountain Fork River provides one of Oklahoma's few quality trout fisheries. Fly fishing is serious business here; the cold tailwaters below Broken Bow Lake are stocked and genuinely productive. Beyond fishing, the park offers canoeing, kayaking, hiking trails through pine and hardwood forest, mountain biking, and ATV trails on adjacent lands.

Fall brings spectacular color as the mixed forest turns, and winter weekends are surprisingly popular — bald eagles roost along the river from November through February, and the cabins scattered through the woods stay booked. Those cabins range from simple one-room retreats to large lodges sleeping 16, most privately owned and rented through local agencies. This is not a hotel town; the cabin rental economy is how most visitors stay.
