Enid sits in the wheat plains of northwestern Oklahoma, the seat of Garfield County and the state's ninth-largest city. It grew up around grain — the massive elevators along the railroad tracks make that clear at a glance — and it remains one of the largest grain storage centers in the United States. The downtown square has a quiet, unhurried feel that is typical of small-city Oklahoma.

There is more here than grain, though. The Cherokee Strip Museum covers the 1893 land run that brought settlers pouring into this part of the territory, and Leonardo's Discovery Warehouse is a hands-on children's museum worth a stop if you are traveling with kids. Woodring Airport, just north of town, hosts a regional air show. And yes, astronaut Owen K. Garriott — who flew on both Skylab and the Space Shuttle — grew up here.
