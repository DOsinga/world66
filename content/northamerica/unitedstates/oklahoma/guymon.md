Guymon is the largest town in the Oklahoma Panhandle, with about 12,000 people, and serves as the commercial center for a vast stretch of flat, wind-scoured High Plains. Texas County, despite its name, is firmly in [Oklahoma](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma), and Guymon sits roughly in its middle, surrounded by cattle feedlots and irrigated farmland.

The town's marquee event is the **Pioneer Days Rodeo**, held each May and ranked among the top professional rodeos in the country. The three-day celebration includes a parade, a carnival, and serious competition that draws cowboys from across the region. Outside of rodeo season, Guymon is a working town with a surprisingly diverse population -- meatpacking jobs have drawn immigrants from Central America and Southeast Asia, giving the town a variety of restaurants you would not expect in the Panhandle.

The No Man's Land Museum on the campus of Oklahoma Panhandle State University in nearby Goodwell covers the region's history, from the dinosaur era through the Dust Bowl. The Panhandle landscape itself is an acquired taste -- relentlessly flat, enormous skies, spectacular sunsets -- but if you appreciate wide-open spaces, this is about as open as it gets in the lower 48.
