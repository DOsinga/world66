Okmulgee is the capital of the Muscogee (Creek) Nation, a distinction that gives this small city of about 13,000 people an outsized cultural significance. The **Creek Council House Museum** downtown occupies the 1878 Creek national capitol building and traces the tribe's history from the Southeast through the Trail of Tears to Indian Territory. The Muscogee Nation's modern government complex is also located here.

The town has a pleasant, slightly faded downtown with handsome early 20th-century commercial buildings. Okmulgee Lake, just west of town, is a popular spot for fishing, picnicking, and an easy afternoon outdoors. The **Okmulgee Pecan Festival** each June celebrates the region's pecan orchards with food, crafts, and carnival rides.

Okmulgee sits about 40 miles south of [Tulsa](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/tulsa) along US-75, making it an easy day trip. If you are interested in Native American history and governance, the Creek Council House alone is worth the drive.
