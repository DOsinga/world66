Ponca City is a city of around 24,000 in Kay County in north-central Oklahoma, about 100 miles north of Oklahoma City. It was built on oil — E.W. Marland, who founded what became Conoco Oil, made his fortune here in the 1910s and 20s and left behind a remarkable legacy in the form of the Marland Mansion, a 55-room Italian Renaissance estate modelled on the Villa Farnese, now a museum open to the public.

The city also has the Pioneer Woman Statue on Grand Avenue, a 17-foot bronze by sculptor Bryant Baker that was dedicated in 1930 as a tribute to the women who helped settle the Oklahoma Territory. Three smaller versions were exhibited across the US before Ponca City was chosen for the full-size monument.

It's a modest stop rather than a destination in itself, but the Marland Mansion alone is genuinely worth the detour for anyone interested in the wild extravagance of early 20th-century oil wealth.
