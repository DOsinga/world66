Poteau (a French word meaning "post") is the county seat of LeFlore County in southeastern Oklahoma. Nestled in the fertile Poteau River Valley, the city is almost completely surrounded by mountains -- Sugar Loaf Mountain to the east, the Winding Stair Mountain Range to the south (where the scenic Talimena Byway begins), and **Cavanal Hill** to the west, which claims to be the world's highest hill.

The area has a deep history. Between 500 and 1300 AD, Mound Builders constructed temples and burial places in what is now the Spiro area. Runestones found in Poteau and nearby Heavener have sparked speculation about pre-Columbian European visitors -- a state park has been built around the Heavener Runestone.

The **Kerr Museum**, donated to the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education, has artifacts spanning the entire known history of the Poteau River Valley, from Native American artifacts and Spiro Mound relics to a collection of over 300 types of barbed wire. The museum is open to the public year-round.

**Carl Albert State College**, founded in 1933 as the first two-year community college in Oklahoma, was also the first public school in the state to fully integrate students of all races.
