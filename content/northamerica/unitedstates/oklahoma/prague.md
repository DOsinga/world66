Prague (pronounced PRAY-g, not like the Czech capital) is a small town in Lincoln County with one outsized claim to fame: it is the birthplace of Jim Thorpe (1887–1953). Widely considered one of the greatest athletes of the 20th century, Thorpe won gold medals in the pentathlon and decathlon at the 1912 Stockholm Olympics — later stripped and only restored posthumously — and went on to play professional football and baseball. The Jim Thorpe Home is preserved as a state historic site on the edge of town.

There is not much else to detain you in Prague, but if you are passing through Oklahoma's backcountry, the Thorpe connection is worth a stop.
