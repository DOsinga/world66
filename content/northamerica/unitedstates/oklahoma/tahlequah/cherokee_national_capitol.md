The 1867 Cherokee National Capitol building stands at the center of downtown Tahlequah. Built just after the Civil War, during which the Cherokee Nation was bitterly divided, it served as the seat of Cherokee government and is now a National Historic Landmark. The surrounding Cherokee National Square includes several other historic buildings from the same era.
