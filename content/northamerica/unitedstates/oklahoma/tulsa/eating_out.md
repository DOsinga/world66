---
title: 'Eating Out'
type: section
---

Tulsa sits at the crossroads of three regional cuisines -- Midwestern, Southern, and Southwestern -- and all three are represented well in the city's restaurants.

The **Midwestern influence** shows up in the diners, drive-ins, and pizza joints. Sonic Drive-Ins and Braum's Ice Cream are Oklahoma institutions. **Goldie's Patio Grill** is perhaps the burger most closely associated with Tulsa, with its unique seasonings and pickle bar. **Hideaway Pizza**, beloved by Oklahoma State graduates from [Stillwater](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/stillwater), now has Tulsa locations.

The **Southern influence** is everywhere. Biscuits and sausage gravy for breakfast, chicken-fried steak and fried okra for dinner. Barbecue is where all three traditions converge -- Oklahoma-style ribs, bologna, and hot links alongside Texas-style brisket and Kansas City-style sweet sauces.

The **Southwestern influence** brings chili in every form (on spaghetti with beans and cheese, in a cup with crackers, on a dog or burger), plus tacos, enchiladas, and salsa from fast-food to upscale.

**India Palace** on S. Lewis is an excellent introduction to Indian cuisine if you have not tried it, with a generous lunch buffet. **Cici's Pizza** locations around town offer cheap all-you-can-eat pizza buffets.
