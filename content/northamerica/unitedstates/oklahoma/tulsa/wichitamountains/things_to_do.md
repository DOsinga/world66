---
title: "Things to do in Wichita Mountains"
type: section
---

In the **Wichita Mountains**, the **things to do **are so
numerous that it can be hard for the outdoor enthusiast to narrow it
down to just one or two. However, if you enjoy outdoor activities and memorable adventures, you're sure to appreciate at least one of the following:

**Hiking**

A trip to the Wichita Mountains would be incomplete without spending
at least a day hiking on the popular trails throughout the mountains.
The nine official hiking trails make it simple for the first-timer and
the seasoned hiker alike to join in on the experience, as you can pick
one of the shorter trails (approximately 1/3 of a mile) or the longer
trails (the longest runs almost 6 miles). You can hike alone if
you have the experience and the equipment, or you can join a hiking tour
guide, who will give you historical information about this gorgeous
location as you explore.

If you are a little more adventurous, you can go hiking off-trail,
but be aware that this is only permitted in the Charon's Garden Wilderness
area. (But as that area is almost 6000 acres wide, you're sure to find
plenty to explore.)

**Camping**

Along with hiking, you may want to spend a few days camping between hiking trails. You have
the freedom to camp on your own in many areas of the Wichita Mountains,
although the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge asks that you register your intent in order to prevent overcrowding and safety
issues. Fees are usually no more than $2 per person. You must register
at least 10 days in advance most of the year, although walk-in
reservations at the Indiahoma headquarters are okay in the spring and fall and during some school breaks.

Instead of exploring the mountains on your own, you can also camp at one
of two campgrounds for $6 to $16 per group night or at one of three
campsites for $24 to $36 per site per night. Campgrounds and campsites
have electricity available and are suitable for tents and RVs. There are
also nearby toilets and running water sources. It's best to make
reservations 3 months ahead of time, but you can try to get a walk-in
reservation with these as well.

**Rock Climbing**

Besides hiking and camping, the Wichita Mountains are famous for their rock climbing opportunities. The rock climbing
sites are connected to the established hiking trails, and are free to use. The Refuge only asks that you don't chisel, bolt
or deface the rocks, and always try to make a minimal impact. (You
may be able to bolt if you get prior approval, but don't count on it.)
If you are a seasoned rock climber, bring your own equipment, but avoid unnaturally-colored webbing if you use fixed slings. If you haven't rock climbed before, only do so under the supervision of a guide.
