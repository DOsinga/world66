---
title: 'Things to do in Wichita Mountains'
type: section
---

The Wichita Mountains offer enough outdoor activities to fill several days. Hiking, camping, and rock climbing are the big three.

## Hiking

Nine official trails range from short walks (about a third of a mile) to the longest at nearly 6 miles. You can hike solo or join a guided tour for historical context. Off-trail hiking is permitted only in the Charon's Garden Wilderness Area, which at almost 6,000 acres offers plenty of room to explore.

## Camping

You can camp independently in many areas of the Wichita Mountains, though the Wildlife Refuge asks that you register in advance (at least 10 days ahead most of the year; walk-in reservations are possible in spring, fall, and some school breaks). Fees are usually no more than $2 per person.

Two campgrounds ($6 to $16 per group per night) and three campsites ($24 to $36 per site per night) have electricity and are suitable for tents and RVs, with nearby toilets and running water. Book 3 months ahead for peak times.

## Rock Climbing

The Wichita Mountains are famous for rock climbing. Sites are connected to established hiking trails and are free to use. The Refuge asks that you do not chisel, bolt, or deface the rocks. Experienced climbers should bring their own equipment. Beginners should only climb with a guide.
