---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Wichita Mountains"
type: section
---

The scenic Wichita Mountains offer a variety of activities for
visitors of all ages. With much to see and experience, here is a list of
the **Wichita Mountains Must Do's**--the sights and activities not
to be missed.

**View the Wildlife at Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge**

The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, established in 1901, is the
second most visited wildlife refuge in the country, with more than 1.5
million visitors annually. Spanning more than 59,000 acres of protected
land, the refuge is home to free-roaming bison and cattle, as well
as white-tail deer, prairie dogs, Rocky Mountain elk, various birds,
reptiles, amphibians and fish. The refuge includes camping and picnic
areas, 15 miles of trails for hiking, fishing, rock climbing, mountain
biking and the Quanah Parker Nature Center. The Center features art,
wildlife exhibits, interactive displays and a bookstore.

**Climb in Charon's Garden**

The Charon's Garden Wilderness Area, popular among rock climbers, is
located within the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge. The area spans
more than 5,700 acres reserved for day-hiking and back-country camping,
with several good climbing summits including Elk and Granite Mountains,
Mount Lincoln and Sunset Peak. While most of the climbing is across the
granite slabs and boulders, these summits offer a variety of climbing
activities, from Class 1 to 5.11c.

**Stroll through Medicine Park**

The popular resort town of Medicine Park was established in 1908 as
Oklahoma's first planned tourism resort. It is often referred to as a
cobblestone community because many of its structures are made from
natural red granite cobblestones. Nominated in 2010 as one of America's
Coolest Small Towns by "Budget Travel" magazine, Medicine Park offers a
number of lodging, dining and shopping options, as well as a local
winery.

**Fish at Lake Lawtonka**

Just outside of Medicine Park lies the man-made Lake Lawtonka, with
21 miles of shoreline. Lake Lawtonka is a popular spot for fishing and
is full of catfish, crappie, saugeye, small-mouth and large-mouth bass
and walleye. In addition to fishing areas, boat ramps and docks, Lake
Lawtonka also has several recreation areas, campsites, picnic sites with
grills, RV pads, restrooms, a playground and a concession area.

**The Museum of the Great Plains**

The Museum of the Great Plains is located in Lawton, just outside the
Wichita Mountains. A visit to this Museum is a fun way to learn about
the history of the North American Great Plains. Exhibits include a
one-room schoolhouse, a train depot and steam locomotive, gardens, and a
replicated Red River Trading Post, used by various Native American
tribes.
