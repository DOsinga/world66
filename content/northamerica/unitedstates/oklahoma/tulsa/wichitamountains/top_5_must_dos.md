---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Wichita Mountains"
type: section
---

1. **View the wildlife at Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge.** The second most visited wildlife refuge in the country, with more than 1.5 million visitors annually. Spanning over 59,000 acres, it is home to free-roaming bison and longhorn cattle, white-tailed deer, prairie dogs, and Rocky Mountain elk. The 15 miles of trails, the Quanah Parker Nature Center, and the views alone justify the trip.

2. **Climb in Charon's Garden.** The Charon's Garden Wilderness Area spans more than 5,700 acres reserved for day-hiking and backcountry camping. Rock climbers come for the granite slabs and boulders around Elk and Granite Mountains, Mount Lincoln, and Sunset Peak, with routes ranging from Class 1 to 5.11c.

3. **Stroll through Medicine Park.** Oklahoma's first planned tourism resort, established in 1908, is a cobblestone village built from natural red granite. Nominated as one of America's Coolest Small Towns by *Budget Travel* magazine, it has charming restaurants, shops, and a local winery.

4. **Fish at Lake Lawtonka.** Just outside Medicine Park, this man-made lake has 21 miles of shoreline with mountain views. Catfish, crappie, saugeye, smallmouth and largemouth bass, and walleye are all plentiful. Boat ramps, campsites, and picnic areas make it easy to spend a full day.

5. **Visit the Museum of the Great Plains.** Located in [Lawton](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/lawton), just outside the mountains, this museum brings the history of the North American Great Plains to life with a one-room schoolhouse, a train depot and steam locomotive, gardens, and a replicated Red River Trading Post.
