---
title: 'When to Go in Wichita Mountains'
type: section
---

**Fall (September to November)** is the best time to visit. Temperatures are steady and pleasant, rain tapers off, and the mountains and Lake Lawtonka look their best. This is prime time for hiking, climbing, and camping.

**Spring** can be unpredictable. The air turns humid and afternoon thunderstorms are common, which can make trail hiking difficult. Come prepared for indoor alternatives like the Fort Sill Museum in [Lawton](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/lawton).

**Summer** is hot -- temperatures regularly reach 95 degrees Fahrenheit, making long hikes and open-landscape exploring uncomfortable in the afternoons. Early mornings are the best window if you visit in summer.

**Winter** brings cold that can drop below freezing. Cross-country skiing and winter hiking are possible but conditions can be harsh. Plan accordingly and check weather forecasts before heading out.
