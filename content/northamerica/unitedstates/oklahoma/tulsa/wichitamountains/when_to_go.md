title: "When to Go in Wichita Mountains"
type: section
---

A **Wichita Mountains when to go** strategy is essential for all travelers. Those wishing to seek Oklahoma and its southwestern corner will be limited by the weather.

**Spring**

It&rsquo;s a common belief that spring yields the greatest weather. In the Wichita Mountains, however, this is not always true. The air becomes humid and thunder storms can occur daily. This can make hiking the famed trails difficult. Visitors should be prepared, if they choose to travel in spring, for indoor activities such as the nearby Fort Sill Museum.

**Summer**

The long days of summer are often noted as a vacationer&rsquo;s dream, offering more time for discovery. Any Wichita Mountains when to go plan should discount this season, though. The temperatures rise quickly (reaching an average of 95 degrees), and guests will lose much of their afternoons, forced away from the open landscapes of sites like the Wilderness Refuge. The heat is simply too much.

**Fall **

There can be no doubt that fall represents the ideal circumstances of climate and dependability. Through the months of September to November, travelers will find steady temperatures and a decline of rain. The mountains are best viewed during these times, especially areas like Lake Lawtonka and its picturesque shorelines. All are encouraged to visit then.

**Winter**

The cold can thrill. That is a truth long-since established. The Wichita Mountains, however, are not ranked among the favorites of snow-seekers. While cross-country skiing and hiking is available, temperatures can drop below freezing and may make such events difficult. Caution is required.
