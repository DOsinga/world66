Waynoka is a tiny town of about 1,000 people in the red plains of northwestern Oklahoma, and it punches well above its weight for outdoor adventure. **Little Sahara State Park**, just south of town, is the main attraction: 1,600 acres of sand dunes rising up to 75 feet, open for ATVs, dune buggies, dirt bikes, and sandboarding. The dunes are a surreal sight in the middle of the Oklahoma prairie, and the park draws off-road enthusiasts from across the region.

Each spring, Waynoka hosts the **Annual Rattlesnake Hunt and Carnival**, a weekend of guided hunts for western diamondback rattlesnakes, plus carnival rides, food vendors, and snake-handling demonstrations. It is one of those uniquely Oklahoma events that you either find thrilling or terrifying.

The town itself is quiet, with a small historic downtown that reflects its past as a Santa Fe Railroad stop. The surrounding countryside is classic western Oklahoma: flat wheat fields, red dirt roads, and big sky. Waynoka is not a place you stumble upon, but if you are looking for dune riding, rattlesnake hunting, or just a genuinely off-the-beaten-path Oklahoma experience, it delivers.
