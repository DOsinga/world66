Wewoka is the capital of the Seminole Nation of Oklahoma, a small city in the rolling prairie of central Oklahoma. The name means "barking water" in the Seminole language, a reference to the sound of a nearby creek cascading over rocks.

The town's most notable attraction is the Seminole Nation Museum, which traces the history of the Seminole people from Florida through the Trail of Tears to Indian Territory. The museum covers the Seminole Freedmen — descendants of Black people who lived among the Seminole — and their complicated shared history. Each September, Wewoka hosts the Sorghum Day Festival, celebrating the sorghum syrup production that was once a staple of the local economy.
