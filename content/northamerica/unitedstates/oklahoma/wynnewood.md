Wynnewood is a small town of about 2,300 people in south-central Oklahoma, straddling US-77 between Oklahoma City and the Arbuckle Mountains. The town gained unexpected fame as the home of the **Greater Wynnewood Exotic Animal Park**, the zoo featured in the Netflix documentary *Tiger King*. The park has changed hands and names several times since the show aired.

More enduringly, Wynnewood sits near the northern edge of the Arbuckle Mountains, one of the oldest mountain ranges in the United States. Turner Falls, about 20 miles south near [Davis](/northamerica/unitedstates/oklahoma/davis), is Oklahoma's tallest waterfall and a popular swimming hole. The **Wynnewood Refinery**, one of the oldest continuously operating refineries in the world, is an unexpected piece of industrial history.

The town itself is quiet, with a few restaurants and shops along Main Street. Wynnewood works best as a stopping point on the drive south toward the Arbuckles, the Chickasaw National Recreation Area, or the casinos and lakes around Sulphur.
