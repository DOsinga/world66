Ashland is a small, culturally ambitious city in southern Oregon, about 25km north of the California border on Interstate 5. Its reputation rests on the Oregon Shakespeare Festival — one of the largest and oldest theatre festivals in the United States — which runs from late February to October and presents Shakespeare alongside contemporary plays in three venues: the outdoor Elizabethan Theatre, the Angus Bowmer Theatre, and the intimate New Theatre. Nine other theatre groups add to the cultural density. Opera, bluegrass, folk, and chamber music fill the calendar.

The town itself is pretty and walkable, with well-preserved pioneer and Victorian architecture, a good restaurant scene, and Lithia Park running along Ashland Creek from the centre of town into the hills. The mineral springs that give the park its name taste truly terrible, which locals consider a point of pride.

The surrounding area offers serious outdoor recreation: Mt Ashland (23 ski runs, 15 miles from town), river rafting and jet-boat trips on the Rogue and Klamath rivers, fly-fishing for steelhead and salmon, and mountain biking on routes including the 28-mile Lithia Loop. Crater Lake National Park is about 90 minutes to the north.
