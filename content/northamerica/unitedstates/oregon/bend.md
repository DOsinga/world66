Bend is central Oregon's outdoor capital, a small city on the Deschutes River with the Cascade Range rising immediately to the west. In winter people ski and snowboard at Mount Bachelor; in summer they mountain bike, hike, paddle, and fish. The town itself has grown from a logging settlement into a craft-beer-and-adventure hub with a lively downtown, good restaurants, and an easy, outdoorsy energy.

The Old Mill District along the river has shops and restaurants in converted industrial buildings. Drake Park and Mirror Pond are the heart of downtown. The High Desert Museum south of town is one of Oregon's best small museums — part natural history, part living exhibit, with raptors, porcupines, and a walk-through desert canyon.

Day trips are excellent: Crater Lake National Park is two hours south, Smith Rock State Park (world-class rock climbing) is half an hour north, and the Newberry Volcanic Monument — with obsidian flows, a caldera lake, and lava tubes — is just outside town. The Cascade Lakes Scenic Byway loops past a string of alpine lakes ideal for kayaking and swimming in summer.
