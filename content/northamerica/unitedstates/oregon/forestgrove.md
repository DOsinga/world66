Forest Grove is a small town on the western edge of the Portland metro area in [Oregon](/northamerica/unitedstates/oregon), where the Tualatin Valley meets the foothills of the Coast Range. It is home to Pacific University, founded in 1849, which gives the town a bit more life than its size would suggest.

The main draw for visitors is McMenamins Grand Lodge, a converted Masonic lodge turned into a hotel, restaurant, movie theatre, and bar complex with a spa and a Japanese soaking pool. It is one of the more eccentric overnight stays in the region.

Forest Grove sits in the heart of Oregon wine country. The Tualatin Valley AVA surrounds the town, with numerous pinot noir producers within a short drive. The coast is about an hour west, and Portland is easily reached by bus or a 30-minute drive east. Mount Hood skiing and Columbia Gorge windsurfing are both about 90 minutes away.
