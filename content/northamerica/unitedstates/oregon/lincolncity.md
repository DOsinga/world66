Lincoln City stretches along seven miles of Oregon coastline, a string of small communities merged into one long beach town. It is not the most picturesque stop on the Oregon coast — the strip-mall character of Highway 101 through town sees to that — but the beach itself is wide and dramatic, with reliable wind for kite flying and a city-sponsored programme that hides hand-blown glass floats in the sand for visitors to find and keep.

The D River, which connects Devils Lake to the ocean, runs through the centre of town and holds a claim as one of the shortest rivers in the world. Devils Lake is popular for kayaking and paddleboarding. The Chinook Winds Casino draws visitors from Portland, and the outlet mall at the north end of town is a regular weekend destination for Willamette Valley shoppers.

Lincoln City works best as a base for exploring the central Oregon coast. Depoe Bay and its whale-watching boats are just to the south, and the quieter beaches around Neskowin are a short drive north.
