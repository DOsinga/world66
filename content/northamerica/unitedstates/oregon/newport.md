Newport is the working heart of the central Oregon coast — a town that manages to be both a fishing port and a popular beach destination without feeling like it is trying too hard at either. The Bayfront district along Yaquina Bay is the best part: a row of seafood restaurants, chowder houses, and galleries built on the docks where fishing boats and sea lions compete for space.

The Oregon Coast Aquarium, just across the bay, is one of the best on the West Coast and the main reason many families make the trip. The aquarium's outdoor exhibits let you walk through habitats that mirror the coastline right outside — tide pools, kelp forests, and a seabird aviary. Nearby, the Hatfield Marine Science Center offers a more scientific take on the same waters.

North of town, Yaquina Head Outstanding Natural Area is a rocky promontory topped by Oregon's tallest lighthouse. The tide pools at Cobble Beach below are among the richest on the coast, and in winter and spring you can watch gray whales migrating close to shore. South of town, Nye Beach is a quieter stretch of sand backed by a small neighbourhood of cafes and vacation rentals. Newport sits roughly halfway along the Oregon coast, making it a natural midpoint on a coastal road trip.
