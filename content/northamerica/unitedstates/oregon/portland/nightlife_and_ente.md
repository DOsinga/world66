---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Portland"
type: section
---

Portland
is teeming with clubs, bars and entertainment that will keep just about anyone
busy during their stay. A hub for microbreweries in the Northwest, independent
bar culture is strong. With a city large enough to bring national touring acts
and a culture that supports numerous performing arts, Portland is a great place to visit any time of
year.

**Bars and Clubs**

There
is a place for everyone here with numerous and diverse styles of clubs and bars.
The Crystal Ballroom is a great venue that plays host to some of the hottest bands
coming through town, as well as a dance club. Larger acts perform at the Rose
Garden, and for an intimate jazz experience you can head to Jimmy Maks for
dinner and dancing.

If you
enjoy beer, you must check out at least one pub here in the heart of the
craft-brewing explosion. Many of the area's brewpubs are family friendly, and a
great place to go for a true Portland experience. Head to one of Laurelwood
Brewing Company’s several locations for a meal and brew, checkout one of
McMenamins numerous Northwest locations for a movie and brew or enjoy a couple
of ales at Wiidmer Brothers. All family friendly spots, visiting a brewpub in
Portland is truly a different experience from most places.

**Performing Arts **

As the
Northwest’s number two cultural center (Seattle is number one), Portland has
highly rated ballet, opera and symphony orchestra, as well as countless theater
companies. The Arlene Schnitzer Concert Hall is home to the Oregon Symphony and
numerous other performances, while Keller Auditorium houses the Oregon Ballet
Theatre and the Portland Opera.

Portland
Center Stage is the city’s largest professional theater company and performs
around nine classic and contemporary plays during their season. The Artists
Repertory Theatre is a good locale for more daring plays and the Broadway in
Portland series at the Keller Auditorium is host to musicals throughout the
year.

Summer
is an especially great time to enjoy Portland’s nightlife. The Washington Park
Zoo features a great series of live music performances throughout the summer
that includes many mainstage acts, as well as local bands and performers. While the
larger acts require an admission fee, many of the local bands and performing
arts troupes are free.
