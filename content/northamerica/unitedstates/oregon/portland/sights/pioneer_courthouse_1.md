The Pioneer Courthouse was built in the 1860s and is widely known as the first courthouse on the west coast. The Pioneer Courthouse was the first building in Portland to be fully restored. The building is now used by the US Court of Appeals as well as the U.S. Post Office. There is an excellent view from the Pioneer Courthouse of downtown Portland. The Pioneer Courthouse is free for anybody who would like to enter its large doors and admire the beautiful architecture of this unique historical wonder. Portland attractions have never been more exciting than this amazing Pioneer Courthouse.
