The Berry Botanic Garden could best be described as a ‘living museum’, in that there are so many endangered and rare plants on the grounds. In addition, there is a berry seed- bank, which is used to raise money for the preserving of these endangered plants. The garden is approximately 6 and a half acres and is best visited during the summer or spring. Weekend visits to this garden must be made by appointment and the Berry Botanic Garden only accepts cash. The grounds house everything from ferny glens to their own large rock garden. Don’t miss out on Portland attractions such as this!
