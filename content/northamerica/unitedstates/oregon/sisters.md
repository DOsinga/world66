Sisters is a small town at the foot of the Cascades, framed by the snow-capped peaks of the Three Sisters volcanoes. Its entire downtown is built in an Old West theme — wooden boardwalks, false-front buildings — and it works better than you'd expect, giving the place genuine character rather than feeling like a gimmick.

The town is best known for the Sisters Outdoor Quilt Show, held every July, which draws thousands of visitors to see quilts draped across buildings and fences throughout downtown. The rest of the year, Sisters serves as a base for outdoor pursuits: Hoodoo Ski Area is a short drive west on Highway 20, and trailheads for the Three Sisters Wilderness are scattered along the roads leading into the mountains. The surrounding ponderosa pine forests are beautiful year-round.
