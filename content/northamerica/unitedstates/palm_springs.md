Palm Springs is a desert resort city in southern California at the foot of the San Jacinto Mountains. It is famous for its mid-century modern architecture — the 1940s and 50s left the city with an extraordinary concentration of flat-roofed houses and civic buildings that now attract design enthusiasts from around the world, and Modernism Week each February draws thousands of architecture fans.

The desert setting means brilliant sunshine almost every day of the year, with hiking trails winding through the canyons of the San Jacinto Mountains and across the Coachella Valley. The Palm Springs Aerial Tramway rises 2,600 metres from desert floor to mountain wilderness in 10 minutes, offering a striking change of scenery year-round. In summer the heat is intense — temperatures regularly exceed 40°C — but the shoulder seasons of spring and autumn are ideal for outdoor activity.

The city has a lively restaurant scene and a relaxed, unpretentious character. It's also a popular destination for the LGBTQ+ community, with a welcoming atmosphere and a busy calendar of events.
