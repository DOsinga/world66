Buckingham is the largest township in Bucks County, covering 33 square miles of farmland and wooded hills east of Doylestown. Settlement began around 1700, when Quaker families from England and Wales took up land grants from William Penn.

Today Buckingham is largely rural, with horse farms, antique dealers, and roadside farm stands along Route 202 and the back roads. The village of Lahaska, with its Peddler's Village shopping complex, sits within the township and draws day-trippers from Philadelphia on weekends.
