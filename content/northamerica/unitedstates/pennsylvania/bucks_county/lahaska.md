Lahaska is a small village in [Buckingham Township](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/bucks_county/buckingham), [Bucks County](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/bucks_county). It was once known as Hentown, named after a six-acre property that housed a chicken hatchery, a barn, and a cluster of coops.

The village is best known today for Peddler's Village, a 42-shop complex of colonial-style buildings set among landscaped gardens. It runs seasonal festivals — a strawberry festival in spring, a scarecrow competition in autumn — and stays busy on weekends with visitors from Philadelphia and Trenton.
