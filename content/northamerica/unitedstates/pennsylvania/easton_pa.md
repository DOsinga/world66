Easton sits at the confluence of the Delaware and Lehigh Rivers on Pennsylvania's eastern border with New Jersey. The town is best known as the home of the Crayola Experience (formerly the Crayola Factory), where visitors can watch crayons being made and try their hand at various art activities. Two Rivers Landing, the building that houses the Crayola Experience, also contains the National Canal Museum.

The revitalized downtown has a growing restaurant and arts scene, centered around Centre Square, where the Declaration of Independence was first publicly read to the citizens of Easton in 1776.
