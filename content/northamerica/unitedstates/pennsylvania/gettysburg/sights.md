### [Soldiers' National Cemetery](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/gettysburg/sights/soldiers_national)

The Soldiers' National Cemetery at Gettysburg was dedicated on November 19, 1863, when President Abraham Lincoln delivered his immortal Gettysburg Address. The cemetery contains more than 7,000 interments including over 3,500 from the Civil War.

The cemetery is located in the [Gettysburg National Military Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/gettysburg/sights/gettysburg_nationa).

### [Gettysburg National Military Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/gettysburg/sights/gettysburg_nationa)

Located 50 miles northwest of [Baltimore](/northamerica/unitedstates/maryland/baltimore), the small town of Gettysburg, Pennsylvania was the site of the largest battle ever waged during the American Civil War. Fought in the first three days of July 1863, the Battle of Gettysburg resulted in a hallmark victory for the Union "Army of the Potomac" and successfully ended the second invasion of the North by General Robert E. Lee's "Army of Northern Virginia".

It was also the bloodiest single battle of the war, resulting in over 51,000 soldiers killed, wounded, [more..](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/gettysburg/sights/gettysburg_nationa)

### [The Majestic Theater](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/gettysburg/sights/the_majestic_theat)

The Majestic theater, which originally opened in the 1920s, has been beautifully restored to its original grandeur and once again welcomes audiences with an extraordinary showcase of the performing arts and cinema.

This summer the Majestic features the first "Broadway" musical to debut in Gettysburg -- For the Glory. The show is, appropriately, a retelling of the civil war through the real words of men, women and children of that turbulent era.
