Johnstown is a small city about 60 miles east of [Pittsburgh](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/pittsburgh), set deep in the Conemaugh Valley. It is best known for its devastating floods -- three of them, in 1889, 1936, and 1977.

The Great Flood of 1889 killed 2,209 people when the South Fork Dam failed, sending a wall of water through the valley. The dam had been converted into a private fishing and hunting lake for wealthy industrialists including Andrew Carnegie and Andrew Mellon. The Johnstown Flood National Memorial, located at the dam site in St. Michael, tells the story and attracts visitors year-round.

The Johnstown Inclined Plane, built in 1891 after the first flood so residents could reach higher ground quickly, is listed in the Guinness Book of World Records as the steepest vehicular inclined plane in the world. The ride to the top rewards you with panoramic views of the valley, a restaurant, and a gift shop.

Nearby state parks including Prince Gallitzin, Keystone, and Kooser offer boating, fishing, and swimming. The surrounding rivers are popular for rafting, canoeing, and kayaking.
