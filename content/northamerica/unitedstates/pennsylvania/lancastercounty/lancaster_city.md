Lancaster is the urban heart of [Lancaster County](/northamerica/unitedstates/pennsylvania/lancastercounty) and has more historical depth than its small size suggests. The city served briefly as the capital of the United States in 1777, and was the state capital of Pennsylvania until 1812.

The Central Market, operating since the 1730s, is one of the oldest continuously running farmers' markets in America. Vendors sell everything from Amish baked goods and handmade pretzels to locally raised meats and fresh produce. The surrounding blocks of Queen Street and Prince Street have filled with galleries, restaurants, and coffee shops, making Lancaster one of the more interesting small cities in the state.
