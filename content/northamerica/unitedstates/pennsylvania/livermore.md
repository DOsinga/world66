Livermore is a ghost town in the Allegheny National Forest, once a thriving lumber town in the late 19th century. Today only foundations and cemetery headstones remain, slowly being reclaimed by the forest. The site is accessible by hiking trails and appeals to those interested in Pennsylvania's industrial past and the eerie atmosphere of abandoned settlements.
