---

**Geography and climate.** Philadelphia spans 143 square miles on the eastern edge of Pennsylvania, at the junction of the Schuylkill and Delaware Rivers. The climate is humid subtropical: winters are cold (average January temperature around 32F/0C) with occasional snow, summers are hot and humid. Spring and autumn are the most comfortable seasons for sightseeing. Rainfall is spread fairly evenly through the year, averaging about 42 inches annually.

**Getting around.** SEPTA operates buses, trolleys, and three subway lines across the city and suburbs. Most of Center City is walkable. Driving in the city center is not recommended -- parking is difficult and regulations are strictly enforced.

**Money.** Currency is the US dollar. ATMs are everywhere. Pennsylvania does not charge sales tax on clothing, which makes Philadelphia a good city for shopping.

**Sports.** Philadelphia is a passionate sports town. The Phillies (baseball), Eagles (football), 76ers (basketball), and Flyers (ice hockey) all play in the South Philadelphia Sports Complex, easily reached by the Broad Street subway line. Fans are famously loud, knowledgeable, and not easily pleased.

**Culture.** Philadelphians claim their city has more public art than any other in America, including thousands of murals visible around practically every corner. The steps of the Philadelphia Museum of Art were made famous by the film Rocky, and visitors still run up them.
