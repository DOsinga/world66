title: 'Things to Do'
type: section
---

After you have eaten a cheesesteak and visited every Independence-era historic site, Philadelphia still has plenty to offer, especially for anyone who likes the outdoors.

**Whitewater rafting on Tohickon Creek.** Twice a year, in November and March, Lake Nockamixon releases its waters from the dam, creating Class 4 rapids on Tohickon Creek. Drive out to Ralph Stover State Park for a four-hour raft trip through some of the most beautiful scenery in southeastern Pennsylvania.

**Kayaking on the Lehigh River.** The Kayak School in Jim Thorpe, about 90 minutes from Center City, offers beginner-friendly day courses on the Lehigh River. They also have weekend packages with shared dorm rooms for budget travelers. Jim Thorpe itself is a charming mountain town worth exploring.

**John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge at Tinicum.** If you are staying near the airport, this 1,000-acre freshwater tidal marsh is practically next door. Ten miles of trails wind through the refuge, offering surprisingly good birding for a spot so close to an international airport. SEPTA buses can get you close.

**Fairmount Park.** With more than 2,000 acres along the Schuylkill River, Fairmount Park is one of the largest urban parks in the country. Bring a bicycle, running shoes, or roller blades and join the locals on the river trail. The park is easily reached from the Art Museum area.
