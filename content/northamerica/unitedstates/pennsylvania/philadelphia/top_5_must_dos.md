---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's"
type: section
---

**1. Visit the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.** The Liberty Bell, cast in 1752 and cracked twice, is free to visit at the Liberty Bell Center on Market Street. Independence Hall next door is where the Declaration of Independence was signed in 1776. Security screening is required -- enter at 5th and Chestnut. Hours vary by season.

**2. Run up the Art Museum steps.** Everyone does it, and you should too. The Philadelphia Museum of Art has a world-class collection inside, and the first Sunday of each month is pay-what-you-wish. The landscaped grounds overlook the Schuylkill River and Fairmount Park.

**3. Eat a Philly cheesesteak.** The hype is justified. Jim's Steaks at 4th and South serves an excellent "pizza steak wit wiz and fried onions." Pat's and Geno's at 9th and Washington in South Philly are the famous rivals. Try more than one and pick your favorite.

**4. Walk South Street.** Philadelphia's alternative shopping, eating, and nightlife district runs from the Delaware River west. The stretch around 4th and South is packed with independent shops, vintage stores, and restaurants that are not part of any national chain. Catch a show at the TLA, then grab pizza at Lorenzo's across the street.

**5. Catch a game at the South Philadelphia Sports Complex.** Philadelphia sports fans are famous for their passionate, unforgiving loyalty. Watching the Phillies, Eagles, Flyers, or Sixers alongside these die-hard fans is an experience you will not forget. The complex is easily reached by the Broad Street Line subway.
