Philadelphia is a city rich with history and the birthplace of the Constitution. It is also famous for its restaurants and sports teams. While there is plenty to do in Philadelphia the following Must Do’s will give you the true local experience.

**Visit the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall **

The Liberty Bell is a piece of America's history that is a definite site to see. It was very easy to see and its free. In 1751, William Penn asked that the new bell being cast for the Pennsylvania Statehouse be engraved with the words, “Proclaim LIBERTY throughout all the Land unto all the inhabitants thereof.” The bell rang to call citizens to the first reading of the Declaration of Independence, and was later dubbed The Liberty Bell by abolitionists, who adopted the bell as a symbol of their fight for freedom for all Americans. Talks on the history of the bell are presented continually during open hours, which vary season to season.

**Philadelphia Art Museum**

Everyone wants to run up the Art Museum steps while singing the theme song from Rocky I, so just go and do it. While you are there you can pretend you really came for the art and see the wonderful collections and current exhibitions inside this grand and historic building. The first Sunday of each month you can “pay what you wish”. The landscaped grounds surrounding the art museum are the edge of Fairmount Park and on the banks of the Schuylkill River.

**South Street**

This is the famous alternative shopping, eating and nightlife district in Philadelphia. South St. features many stores that are not a part of a national chain. You can go there anytime of day and find things to do. The fun begins at 6th and South. At the head of South St. is the Delaware River and Headhouse Sq. Catch a concert at the TLA on 4th and South then head across the street to Lorenzo’s for some delicious pizza. If you love discount fabric or need a obscure button, walk a few blocks to legendary fabric row.

**South Philadelphia Sports Complex**

Philadelphia sports fans are famous for their sarcastic brand of enthusiasm at sporting events. These knowledgeable fans are loud and not easily pleased, but love their teams. Experiencing a Phillies, Eagles, Flyers, Sixers or Wings game with these die-hards is an experience not to be missed. You will probably have an opportunity to engage in their famous booing. South Philadelphia Sports Complex Is easily accessible by the Broad St. Line on the subway.

**Eat a Philly Cheesesteak**

All the hype is true; a Philly Cheesesteak is one of the most delicious sandwiches in the world. There are many places to get a cheesesteak in Philadelphia, my favorite is the “pizza steak wit wiz and fried onions” from Jim’s Steaks at 4th and South. Or try the “cheesesteak hoagie with mayo and provolone” from Fairmount Pizza; plus they deliver! Most famous are Pat’s and Geno’s in South Philly at 9th and Washington.

**Steven Star Signature Restaurants**

For a delicious upscale, but not out of reach, dining experience try one of the 12 world famous Steven Star signature restaurants in Philadelphia.
