## Currency and Costs

The currency is the US dollar (USD). Credit and debit cards are accepted almost everywhere — more so than in most countries. Cash is still useful for tips, farmers markets, food trucks, and some smaller establishments. ATMs are widespread, though international withdrawal fees apply on most foreign cards.

The US is not a budget destination, particularly in major cities. Expect to pay $15–25 for a sit-down lunch, $25–50 for a mid-range dinner per person (before tip). Budget accommodation in cities rarely comes in below $80/night; expect $150–250 for a decent mid-range hotel in New York or San Francisco.

## Tipping

Tipping is not optional — it is how service workers are paid. At restaurants, 18–20% of the pre-tax bill is standard; 15% is acceptable but considered low. Tips are expected for bartenders ($1–2 per drink), taxi and rideshare drivers (15–20%), hotel housekeeping ($2–5 per night), and porters ($1–2 per bag). Not tipping, or tipping poorly, is noticed and considered rude.

## Electricity

The US uses 120V/60Hz electricity. Plugs are Type A (two flat parallel pins) and Type B (two flat pins plus a round ground pin). Most electronics sold internationally accept 100–240V and only need a plug adapter, not a voltage converter.

## Health

There is no universal public healthcare. Medical treatment in the US is expensive. Comprehensive travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage is strongly recommended. Carry proof of insurance and, if you have any ongoing prescriptions, enough medication for your trip plus extra.

## Safety

The US is, in most respects, safe for visitors. Common sense applies as anywhere: be aware of your surroundings, keep an eye on valuables in crowds and on public transport, and be cautious at night in unfamiliar urban areas. Gun violence is a reality — the US has significantly higher firearm-related death rates than comparable wealthy nations.

## Phones and Internet

Mobile coverage is good in cities and most travelled routes, thin in remote areas. The main carriers are AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile. International SIM cards or travel eSIMs work well and are often cheaper than roaming on your home plan. Free WiFi is available in most cafes, hotels, and libraries.

## Smoking and Alcohol

The legal drinking age is 21 and is strictly enforced. Carry ID at all times if you look young; you will be asked. Alcohol laws vary by state — Utah has some of the most restrictive rules; New Orleans allows open containers on the street. Smoking is banned in most indoor public spaces and many outdoor areas including beaches and parks in California and New York.

## Time Zones

The contiguous US has four time zones: Eastern (UTC-5/-4), Central (UTC-6/-5), Mountain (UTC-7/-6), and Pacific (UTC-8/-7). Alaska is UTC-9/-8 and Hawaii is UTC-10 (no daylight saving). Most of the country observes daylight saving time between March and November.
