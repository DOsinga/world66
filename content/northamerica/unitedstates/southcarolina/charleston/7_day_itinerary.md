---
title: "7 Day Itinerary in Charleston"
type: section
---

If you are visiting Charleston, having a **Charleston Itinerary **will
help you plan and enjoy your holiday to the fullest. With lots of
island beach resorts, parks, and gardens to see, enjoyment never stops
in the city dubbed as one of the most romantic cities in America.

**Day 1- Charleston Waterfront Park**

Situated at the heart of downtown, Charleston Waterfront Park has a
walkway of about 1,000 feet alongside Charleston Harbor. There are
alleys that are lined with palm trees and other plants which make it a
must-see if you want a very relaxing vacation. Two fountains featuring
crafty works of art are also located inside the park.

**Day 2- Folly Island**

Folly Island is the place for you if you want to see turtles and
dolphins swimming on crystal waters. Here you can take a walk and watch
the lovely setting of the sun. You would also hear the peaceful
splashing of seawater in this resort.

**Day 3 - Drayton Hall **

Having approximately 630 acres of land area, the Drayton Hall is a
historic site where you can see the beautiful art of Georgian-Palladian
architecture as seen on the building. The hall was finished way back in
1742, but the building’s state is not too far from its original look.
The admission for adults costs $12 per head, while for children ages
6-11, it is $6.

**Day 4- Magnolia Plantation and Gardens**

Magnolia Plantation and Gardens is a picturesque combination of
beautiful plant life and wildlife. Here you can see numerous oak and
cypress trees line the walkway alongside the river where you can ride
the Nature Boat. The Horticultural Maze, Barbados Tropical Garden and
Biblical Garden are only a few must-see venues in this tourist
attraction.

**Day 5- South Carolina Aquarium**

The South Carolina Aquarium is a museum where various sea creatures
can be observed swimming in the 300,000-gallon ocean exhibit. The
marvelous stained glass wall welcomes the travelers as they enter the
Aquarium.

**Day 6 - Charleston Museum **

If you are look for some cultural and historic learning about
Charleston, the Museum is a must-visit spot. It was established in 1773
and is considered as one of the oldest museums in America. Here you get
a chance to see George Washington`s christening cup and other silver
anthology.

**Day 7 -The Hunley**

The Hunley was an American Civil War submarine which entered
Charleston by train in 1863. Tours to its wreck will lead the tourists
in the Old Naval Base of the city. The Hunley displays many artifacts
which can be interesting for both the young and old.
