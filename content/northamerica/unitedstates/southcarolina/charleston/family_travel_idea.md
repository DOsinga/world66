title: "Family Travel Ideas in Charleston"
type: section
---

**Charleston family travel **has to entertain everyone from the
energetic child to the sullen teen to the chaperoning grandparents. If
you include any of these activities in your vacation plans, you're sure
to satisfy your individual family dynamic:

**Frankie's Fun Park and Restaurant**

About a 25-minute drive from downtown Charleston in North Charleston
is Frankie's Fun Park and Restaurant, a miniature theme park that can
feed your family and burn through your kids' abundant energy all in one
stop. Activities include go-karts, bumper boats, a large slide and
other amusement rides. Even bored teens can have fun at the arcade, the
rock climbing wall or playing miniature golf. For adults and kids alike,
there's a golf driving range and a batting cage. There is no admission
fee, as you pay as you play, so a visit to the fun park can fit into
any budget for any size family.

**Children's Museum of the Lowcountry**

This hands-on, creative museum is a delight for children and parents
alike. Kids can explore a kid-friendly miniature medieval castle
replica, get hands-on in an organic garden, pretend they're pirates,
see balls race down elaborate tube playsets, spend time in the art room,
pretend they're going grocery shopping, or play around with water.
There's also a room just for toddlers with interactive activities that
engage children of that age range. Daily classes and rotating
activities make each visit unique. Admission to the museum is a flat
fee of $7 per person (children 1 year and younger are free with paid
adult).

**Spend a Day at Folly Beach**

As Charleston is on the Atlantic coast, you'll have plenty of
beaches from which to choose to spend a day entertaining the entire
family. Folly Beach is one popular beach that family members of all
ages enjoy. There's ample beach space for sunbathing and playing in the
sand and the area is popular for water sports such as surfboarding if you
have older kids or adults in the family who know how to handle
themselves on the water. Lifeguards are on duty during the day on
weekends in the spring and during the day every day during the summer.
Admission fee is only $6 per carload for parking.

**Charleston Waterfront Park**

Families can enjoy picnics at picnic tables or under shady trees at
Charleston Waterfront Park, where hot and cranky kids and adults alike
can get splashed by cleverly constructed water fountains. There are also
swings and other playground equipment. Eight acres of park alongside a
harbor make this an ideal place to visit with family members of all
ages--and it's free.
