**Visiting Charleston in the Spring? Check Out the Spoleto Festival**

One of the best **Charleston festivals** around is the Spoleto Festival, which dates back to 1977. Perhaps the best thing about the Spoleto Festival is the wide variety of performances that you will find. Every year the Spoleto Festival features over 200 premieres—both world and American premieres in the visual arts, which includes ballet and opera.

**History and Location**

A group of people, including Gian Carlo Menotti, who was an Italian composer, founded the Spoleto Festival in 1977. The history behind this festival is that the group who founded it were looking to create a festival for Americans that was similar to the Festival of Two Worlds that is held in Spoleto, Italy every year. Hence, the festival was named the Spoleto Festival in honor of the city that held its counterpart.

The founders of the Spoleto Festival chose Charleston as the location for the festival because it had everything that they were looking for. The founders were looking for a city that had a lot of performance space, including churches and theaters, but they were also looking for city that had the same type of charm that Spoleto had.

**Notable Performances**

Most of the performers that you will see in the Spoleto Festival are going to be performers from the Charleston area, but there are also others. The Spoleto Festival has seen many notable performances over the years, including The American Clock Tower by Arthur Miller and Praise House by Urban Bush Women.
