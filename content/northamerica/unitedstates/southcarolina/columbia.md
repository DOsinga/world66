Columbia is the capital of South Carolina, sitting in the centre of the state at the confluence of the Broad and Saluda rivers. It is a university city (the University of South Carolina campus anchors the downtown) with a growing food and arts scene that has developed considerably in recent years.

The city has a difficult history. Much of Columbia was burned by Sherman's troops in 1865 during the Civil War, and the scars of that era still mark the place — you can see cannon-ball marks on the State House. The South Carolina State Museum, housed in a former textile mill, covers history from prehistory through the Civil War and beyond. The Palmetto Armory, which produced arms for Confederate troops, is a notable historic site.

The Congaree National Park, about 20 minutes southeast of the city, protects the largest old-growth bottomland hardwood forest in the southeastern United States. The boardwalk trails through the floodplain are exceptional — tall cypress and tupelo trees, dense canopy, and a surprisingly wild feel for a place so close to a state capital.
