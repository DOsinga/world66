Greenville sits on the Piedmont Plateau about 100 miles northwest of [Columbia](/northamerica/unitedstates/southcarolina/columbia), with the Blue Ridge Mountains just minutes from the heart of town. For generations it was a retreat for Lowcountry planters escaping the coastal heat and mosquitoes, and the mild climate remains one of the best reasons to come here.

The revitalised downtown is walkable and well-kept. Falls Park on the Reedy River is the centrepiece — a cascade right in the middle of the city, spanned by a curved pedestrian bridge. The Greenville County Museum of Art houses a strong collection of Southern art alongside works by Jasper Johns and Georgia O'Keeffe. Main Street is lined with restaurants and shops, and the surrounding neighbourhoods are pleasant for a stroll.

The foothills to the north and west offer good hiking and waterfalls within easy driving distance, making Greenville a practical base for exploring upstate South Carolina.
