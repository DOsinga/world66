Because of the foresight of its pioneering planner Charles Fraser,
Hilton Head Island has been developed with preserving nature and her
wildlife in mind. It's not unusual to see dolphin fins surfacing near
the beach or to see enormous alligators by lagoons soaking up the sun.
Hundreds of species of birds are near as your doorway. Experience the
ebb and flow of tides, the sound of lapping waves and sea, absorb the
sun's hot rays as you stand at the water's edge. Marvel at the
varieties of trees and shrubbery, the calls and flights of birds, the
air perfumed by the sea and the foliage - experience all that Hilton
Head Island has to offer. Just a drive through the island is a blitz of
palmetto trees, pines, live oaks draped with moss, flowers and
shrubbery, tended daily for the aesthetic eye.

A twelve mile long island with deciduous forest reserves, 19 miles
of hard-packed sandy beaches, and an average year-round temperature
near 70 degrees, Hilton Head Island, South Carolina is a magnet for the
adventurous on the go. Renowned for its premier golf and tennis, the
island also has miles of trails for jogging and biking, trails for
horseback riding, and a skate park for skateboarding. If you need to
rent any equipment-golf clubs, tennis rackets, bikes-there are shops in
Sea Pines, Palmetto Dunes, Shipyard, and elsewhere throughout the whole
island to get you on the tee, on the court, or on your way down the
trails. And these are only some of the sports on land.

Should you need any equipment and vehicle to get you going,
everything is available at your convenience for rent or purchase. There
are boat, kayak, and walking tours to see it all.
