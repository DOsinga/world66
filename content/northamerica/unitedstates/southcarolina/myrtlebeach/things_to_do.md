---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Myrtle Beach is heavy on entertainment and light on conventional sightseeing. There are no museums of particular note, no historic monuments to speak of — the city is young, purpose-built for tourism, and makes no pretense otherwise. What it does offer is an unusually dense concentration of live shows, water parks, and family diversions.

Broadway at the Beach is the center of it all: a 350-acre complex on a man-made lake off Kings Highway, ringed with restaurants, shops, and attraction buildings. It looks like Las Vegas built a suburb. The evening crowds are enormous in summer, but the waterfront is pleasant enough at dusk.

The Myrtle Beach Boardwalk stretches about a mile along the oceanfront, linking the Swamp Fox roller coaster and the SkyWheel — a 187-foot Ferris wheel that gives decent views of the coastline on clear days.
