Aberdeen is the third-largest city in South Dakota, a hub of about 28,000 people on the wide-open plains of the northeast part of the state. Known locally as the "Hub City" for the railroad lines that once converged here, Aberdeen is the commercial center for a vast agricultural region. The town has a genuine small-city warmth and more going on than you might expect from its remote location.

The **Dacotah Prairie Museum** downtown covers the natural and cultural history of the Northern Plains, from the Lakota and Dakota peoples through the homesteading era. The **Storybook Land** park, based on children's literature characters, is a well-loved family attraction set in Wylie Park, which also has a campground and a small zoo. Northern State University gives the town a youthful energy and supports a modest arts scene.

Aberdeen is the hometown of L. Frank Baum, who lived here in the late 1880s before writing *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz*. The connection is celebrated around town. If you are driving across South Dakota on US-12 or US-281, Aberdeen is a natural stopping point with good restaurants and a genuinely friendly atmosphere. The surrounding prairie, particularly in summer when the grasslands are in full bloom, has a stark beauty that rewards a slow drive with the windows down.
