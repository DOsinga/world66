The Black Hills are an isolated mountain range rising from the Great Plains of western South Dakota — dark with ponderosa pine from a distance, hence the name. Sacred to the Lakota Sioux, contested in treaty wars, and eventually carved with presidential faces, this is one of the most geologically and historically layered landscapes in America.

Mount Rushmore is the headline attraction, but the Crazy Horse Memorial nearby — still under construction after more than 70 years — may ultimately be more impressive. Custer State Park, in the southern hills, has free-roaming bison herds, granite spires along the Needles Highway, and some of the best scenic driving in the Midwest. The town of Deadwood, where Wild Bill Hickok was shot, trades heavily on its frontier past but has genuine character beneath the casinos.

Wind Cave and Jewel Cave, both national park sites, are among the longest cave systems in the world. The surrounding hills offer hiking, mountain biking, and fly fishing. The Black Hills are best explored by car over several days, basing yourself in Rapid City, Custer, or Deadwood depending on your interests.
