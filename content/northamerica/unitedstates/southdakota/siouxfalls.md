Sioux Falls is South Dakota's largest city by a wide margin, a growing plains metropolis built around the waterfalls of the Big Sioux River. Falls Park, where the river drops over exposed quartzite ledges in the middle of town, is the obvious first stop — the pink Sioux quartzite gives the cascades an unexpected warmth, and the ruins of a 19th-century mill add to the scene.

Downtown has been revitalised around Phillips Avenue and the Washington Pavilion, a former school building converted into an arts and science centre. The SculptureWalk along the main streets rotates annually, and the restaurant scene is better than you would expect for a mid-sized plains city — good steakhouses, Vietnamese restaurants reflecting recent immigration, and a handful of craft breweries.

Sioux Falls is not a destination most travelers seek out, but it is a practical overnight stop on a cross-country drive and a reasonable base for trips to the [Black Hills](/northamerica/unitedstates/southdakota/blackhills) or the Badlands, both about four hours west.
