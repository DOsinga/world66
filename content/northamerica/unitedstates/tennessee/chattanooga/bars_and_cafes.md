title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Rembrandt's coffee and dessert house is a personal favorite of mine, located in the Art District of Chattanooga. It is a small, old stone building with a terrace off to the side surrounded by rod iron fencing laced with vines. Trees create a canopy above the terrace where guests are able to enjoy their food and drink on wooden benches or iron tables and chairs. White lights are strung throughout some of the trees giving Rembrandt's a slight romantic atmosphere. The mix of various coffee bean aromas is the first thing one notices when he or she walks in the front door. The chalkboard menu behind the counter is filled with colorful descriptions of what Rembrandt's has to offer to its guests. Being a popular place for residents of Chattanooga, one always hears the drone of chatter muffled by the sounds of coffee grinders, blenders, and the cash register. The glass case holds choice after choice of chocolate, breads, and other sweet desserts that satisfy any craving. After visiting Rembrandt's one could cross the street and visit the Art Museum. The new deck of the museum offers a view overlooking the city, an extraordinary view of lights in the evening.
