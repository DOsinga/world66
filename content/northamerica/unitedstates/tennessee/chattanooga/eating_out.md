One will never go hungry or want to stay in for dinner in Chattanooga. There is a taste for everyone amongst the numerous restaurants to choose from. There are the classics such as Sticky Fingers, Buffalo Wild Wings, Chili's, Panera, and Mellow Mushroom, but there are also Thai, Chinese, Italian and many other types to choose from. Residents of the area sometimes enjoy eating in the dining rooms of the Chattanooga Golf and Country Club at which they have membership. If you are feeling fancy, Northshore Grill, St. John's, Sushi Nabe, Tony's, or the Back Inn Café may be just the place for you. Dessert should never be a problem with Clumpy's and Ben and Jerry's ice cream shops, the Cake Factory, and Rembrandt's.

Rembrandt's coffee and dessert house is a personal favorite of mine, located in the Art District of Chattanooga. It is a small, old stone building with a terrace off to the side surrounded by rod iron fencing laced with vines. Trees create a canopy above the terrace where guests are able to enjoy their food and drink on wooden benches or iron tables and chairs. White lights are strung throughout some of the trees giving Rembrandt's a slight romantic atmosphere. The mix of various coffee bean aromas is the first thing one notices when he or she walks in the front door. The chalkboard menu behind the counter is filled with colorful descriptions of what Rembrandt's has to offer to its guests. Being a popular place for residents of Chattanooga, one always hears the drone of chatter muffled by the sounds of coffee grinders, blenders, and the cash register. The glass case holds choice after choice of chocolate, breads, and other sweet desserts that satisfy any craving. After visiting Rembrandt's one could cross the street and visit the Art Museum. The new deck of the museum offers a view overlooking the city, an extraordinary view of lights in the evening.

### [Tony's Pasta Shop & Trattoria](/northamerica/unitedstates/tennessee/chattanooga/eating_out/tonys_pasta_shop)

Before you go to Rembrandt's for dessert you might want to check out Tony's. This is one of my favorite places to eat when I am in the mood for pasta. Don't fill up on the fresh baked bread. Classic Italian menu specializing in fresh house-made pastas, sauces, and rustic European-style breads. Wine and beer lists are available. Loft and terrace seating. Located in the carriage house of Bluff View Inn's Thompson House.
