Johnson City is one of the "Tri-Cities" of northeastern Tennessee (together with Kingsport and Bristol), tucked into the southern Appalachian mountains close to the Virginia and North Carolina borders. It is home to **East Tennessee State University** — which anchors the downtown — and to a growing outdoor-sports scene built around the rivers, lakes and trails of the surrounding Blue Ridge.

The city itself is modest and easy to walk: a compact downtown of red-brick commercial buildings around **Founders Park**, a couple of decent breweries, and the **Hands On! Discovery Center** for children. The real draw is the countryside. Within thirty minutes you can be on the **Appalachian Trail**, cycling the **Tweetsie Trail** rail-trail to Elizabethton, or fishing on the **South Holston River** — one of the best tailwater trout streams in the Southeast. Bluegrass runs deep here too: the town is a short drive from the old railroad crossroads of Bristol, birthplace of country music.
