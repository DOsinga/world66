---
title: "Museums in Memphis"
type: section
---

A Memphis activity must be a trip to The Civil Rights Museum. It is located at the Lorraine Hotel, the same place where Martin Luther King, Jr. was assassinated. The purpose of the museum is to preserve the legacy of Dr. King and the civil rights movement. Visitors learn the history of the civil rights through exhibits, collections, and educational programs. Some of the exhibits are "Protest," chronicling the murder of Emmett Till, "Selma," regarding the fight for voting rights, and "Montgomery Bus Boycott," for the actions of Rosa Parks. For more information, visit their website, civilrightsmuseum.org.
