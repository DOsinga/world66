The most famous street in Memphis and one of the most significant in American music history. W.C. Handy lived and worked here in the early twentieth century, codifying the blues into a written form that spread the genre worldwide. Today the strip is a tourist-facing entertainment district — live music spills out of every bar most nights of the week, and you can walk from venue to venue with an open container — but the musical heritage is genuine. The W.C. Handy Park at the western end has a bronze statue of the man himself.
