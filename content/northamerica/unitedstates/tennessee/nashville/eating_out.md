---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Nashville's food scene has grown considerably and now extends well beyond the traditional "meat and three" — the Southern cafeteria style where you pick an entree and three side dishes from a steam table. The meat and three joints are still there and still worth knowing: Swett's on Clifton, Arnold's Country Kitchen downtown, and others serving precisely the kind of slow-cooked vegetables and cornbread that built the city. Hot chicken — the Nashville specialty of cayenne-dusted fried chicken served on white bread with pickles — is another essential experience; Prince's Hot Chicken Shack in East Nashville is the original.
