Understanding **Music Row transportation** will help you navigate your next trip to this Tennessee hot spot with ease. Who wants to concentrate on getting around when there is so much to do in this music capital.

#### **Ground Transportation**

The center of Music Row is around 16th and 17th Avenues South. Because of the small proximity of many of the areas you'll want to see, you can get around largely on foot. However, there are a few other options for transportation to use if you like.

- The bus system of Nashville will take you to Music Row for reasonable rates. Plan on $1.60 each way, or a full day pass for just under $5. You should note though that service either ends or is highly restricted after 5 p.m.
- The Music City Star is the local train service. The service travels through most of Nashville just like by bus will, but tickets here are sold for $5 each way. Service tends to be a bit quicker as you're not navigating with other motor vehicles. Should your location upon leaving the Row not be a train stop, the Star also operates a shuttle service at no additional cost.
- Taxi cabs are also available should you want a private ride around Music Row. Major companies of operation include Allied Cab, Music City Taxi, Checker Cab and Metro Cab. Of course, your fare will be determined by where you're going and traffic at that time of day.
- Finally, you can rent your own car. Most major car rental companies have locations nearby.
