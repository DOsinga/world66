---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Music Row"
type: section
---

One of the hottest areas in all of Nashville, Tennessee is **Music Row**. **Must do's** in this part of Nashville are all centered around music; Nashville is the heart of country music, and many of the finest performers and acts in the world got their start on this street southwest of downtown Nashville. Read on for a few of the top 5 must do's in this historic part of one of the nation's hippest cities.

**1. Studio B**

Studio B is the famous recording studio owned by record label RCA. Located in the center of Music Row, this studio provides tours and information about some of the most famous artists that got their start recording here. This sight is appropriate for families and travelers of all ages.

**2. Country Music Hall of Fame**

If you plan to visit one place on Music Row in Nashville, Tennessee, make it the Country Music Hall of Fame. Located just off of Music Row, this museum is a center for all of the major country music stars that have performed in Nashville at some of the neighboring bars and areas around Music Row. This sight is appropriate for all audiences, and will be especially interesting for country music fans.

**3. Demonbreun Street**

This particular street along Music Row was formerly the home to several different tourist-oriented museums. In recent years, however, it's changed to feature a wide variety of upscale bars and restaurants. After you've explored the country music history of Nashville, walk down this street to find a place to sit and relax over a local specialty.

**4. Owen Bradley Park**

Country music superstar Owen Bradley is commemorated in this small outdoor space, located just off of the Music Row intersection known as the "Music Row Roundabout." This area provides an excellent rest stop on a nice day and is appropriate for families with young children or anyone else interested in sitting and enjoying the outdoor scenery.

**5. Belmont University**

On the opposite end of Music Row from Owen Bradley Park is the lovely campus of Belmont University. This university has a unique place in Nashville's music history for its performing arts community. The university often features performances from local up-and-coming country music acts in the area.

Nashville, Tennessee is a must-see stop for any country music fan, but there is a variety of other draws to this unique city as well. Be sure to explore outside of Music Row for more interesting sights.
