---
title: "Travel Guide in Music Row"
type: section
---

One of the most important and exciting parts of Nashville, Tennessee is the famed **Music Row;**** travel guide** resources oftentimes highlight this as the primary must-see spot in this historic city. Truly, any trip to Nashville will not be complete without a trip to Music Row. Even if you're not personally a fan of country music, it's important to see the home of this popular art form in order to truly understand how Nashville formed and what makes it such a unique and cultural place.

**Music Sights**

One of the primary goals of any traveler to Music Row should be to see a live country music performance. Tootsie's is one of the most famous country music bars, but there are literally dozens of sites to see in this same vein along Music Row. Stroll along the strip on any night and you'll hear country music of every flavor pouring from the doors of any number of different bars. Find authentic, old fashioned music or modern cover bands playing long-time favorites. Many of these bars charge small covers, but the overall price is typically fairly low.

**Museums and Performance Halls**

Nashville Music Row is home to the single most important country music museum, the Country Music Hall of Fame. This famous home of country music memorabilia features daily tours and more for a low admission fee. It makes the perfect outing for a family, for young or old adventurers as well as any type of tourist who visits Nashville.

The Grand Ole Opry is perhaps Nashville Music Row's most famous institution. This performance hall has been in existence for several decades, and plays host regularly to some of country music's top acts. With a full performance line-up year round, it's almost guaranteed that you can find a performance that suits your fancy if you're into country music. Be sure to inquire about tickets before you arrive, as some performances sell out well in advance.

**Outdoor Activities**

The Opryland Hotel has a famous garden area that is popular among families, and especially for those with young children. For a separate outdoor experience, check the Owen Bradley Park at the opposite end of Music Row from the Opryland Hotel Gardens. Both of these make excellent spots to relax and enjoy a nice day in Nashville.

Be sure to visit the other parts of Nashville as well in order to get a complete sense for this wonderful city.**
