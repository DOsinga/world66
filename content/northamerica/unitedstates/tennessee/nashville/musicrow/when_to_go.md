---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

If you plan on visiting **Music Row**,** when to go** is an important factor to consider. When it comes to determining the perfect time to go visit Music Row, there are a few things that you need to consider. The most important factor to consider is going to be the weather because you want to be able to enjoy your trip rather than having to worry about storms. Crowds and prices are other factors that you should think about when planning a trip down south.

**Summer**

The summer months are usually the busiest months for tourism in Nashville. This is mainly due to all of the children being out of school for the summer, so parents schedule their vacation around the school year. If you don&rsquo;t like crowds, you will want to avoid visiting Music Row during the summer, but especially the months of July and August, and even the beginning of September. Not only do the crowds gather so prices are sky high, but temperatures often reach 100 degree Fahrenheit (37 degrees Celsius) and humidity is nearly 100%.

**Spring and Fall**

These months are going to be the best time to visit Music Row because of how cool the weather is. However, you need to be prepared for sudden and heavy rainstorms. Thunderstorms are also normal during this time of year. The crowds are not going to be as bad and the prices for accommodations have dropped dramatically because the summer rush is over.
