---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Nashville is a city built on music, and nearly everything interesting in it connects back to that fact. The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum downtown is genuinely excellent — a serious institution with an enormous archive. The Ryman Auditorium, the original home of the Grand Ole Opry, gives a strong sense of what made Nashville different. RCA Studio B on Music Row, where Elvis Presley recorded, offers tours that take you inside one of the most important studios in American music history.

Outside the music, the Tennessee State Museum covers the state's history effectively, and the Parthenon in Centennial Park — a full-scale replica of the Athenian original, built in 1897 — is one of the more unexpected buildings in America. The Frist Art Museum is Nashville's main fine art institution. Along the riverfront, the restored Fort Nashborough gives a sense of the city's early history.
