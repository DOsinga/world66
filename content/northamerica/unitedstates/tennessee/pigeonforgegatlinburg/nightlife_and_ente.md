---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Pigeon Forge & Gatlinburg"
type: section
---

**Pigeon Forge & Gatlinburg nightlife **offers plenty of
activities to entertain every traveler, from the club scene regular to
the traveler who likes to kick back a few beers, to the family with
young children. When the sun sets, plan on spending the night at any of
the following:

**Sweet Fanny Adams Theatre**

The Sweet Fanny Adams Theatre at 461 Parkway in Gatlinburg has been
entertaining tourists and locals for over 30 years, and it makes great
nighttime entertainment for the solo traveler as well as the traveler
with a family along. Two new shows each year are presented concurrently
(rotating every other night the theater is open, April through January)
so you can come back and see something new on the same trip.

The shows are comedy shows set in the late 19th century; even the
theater itself is meant to hearken back to the 1890s. Adults and kids
alike will love the entertainment, put on by the only professional
theatrical troupe in the area. Adults cost about $25, and kids 12 and
under cost only $9. Ask for a discount if you are 60 or better, if you
are part of a large group, or if you're a AAA member.

**UVA Wine Bar Cafe**

Head to 463 Parkway in Gatlinburg, across from the Sweet Fanny Adams
Theatre, for one of the only high-class nightclubs in the Smoky Mountain
area. Just don't worry about dressing up fancy. You can come as you
are, be comfortable, and enjoy yourself in a classy atmosphere with a
menu serving wine, beer and Italian cuisine. You might also be there on a
karaoke night, which proves popular with regulars and tourists alike.
Meals range from $7 to $13, and wine is $6 to $9 a glass or $15 to $39
per bottle.

**Dolly Parton's Dixie Stampede**

Families love the dinner and live-show combination offered by Dolly
Parton's Dixie Stampede at 3839 Parkway in Pigeon Forge. This is not
traditional theater. You'll be seated in an arena surrounding the stage
area while you eat your choice of roasted chicken and bbq pork or a
steamed vegetarian-friendly vegetable meal. The show features musicians
and rodeo acts with steers and live horses. Tickets cost $42 to $53 ($30
for kids 12 and under), depending on your choice of seating. Kids three
and under are free if you don't want them to have their own meals or
seats.

**Fox and Parrot British Pub**

You'll have to head to Gatlinburg to find bars (Pigeon Forge has
none), but if relaxing in a traditional pub atmosphere is for you, stop
by Fox and Parrot British Pub at 1065 Glades Road, the only
British-style pub in the area. Decor is reminiscent of the classic
British pub, and so is the food. Enjoy fish and chips and other English
specialties; you can even bring the family into the smoke-free dining
area! If you're there for the beer, you'll have 31 varieties from which
to choose. Meals cost $4 to $9, and beer is competitively priced. Head
to the pub's website for a 15% off coupon for your next meal.
