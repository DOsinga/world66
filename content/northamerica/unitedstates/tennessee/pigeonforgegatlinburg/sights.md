---
title: "Sights in Pigeon Forge & Gatlinburg"
type: section
---

Nestled among the breathtaking Smoky Mountains, **Pigeon Forge & Gatlinburg sights** include vast forests and interesting wildlife, as well as historical sites and entertainment venues.

**Great Smoky Mountains National Park**

Located just south of Gatlinburg is Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Dedicated in 1940, Great Smoky Mountains National Park is one of the most visited national parks in the U.S., with 8 to 10 million visitors annually. The park spans 500,000 acres, 95 percent of which is forest. It is an opportunity to see more than a thousand species of plants and hundreds of species of animals including birds, fish, reptiles, mammals, and a world-leading 27 types of salamanders.

While hiking and biking are the most popular ways to see the park, helicopter tours are available for an aerial view of the Smokies. Whitewater rafting on the Upper Pigeon River, which runs directly through park, also provides a unique view of the area for adventure-loving visitors.

**Tennessee Heritage Trail**

The Tennessee Heritage Trail was developed by the state of Tennessee to highlight various aspects of the city's cultural history. A number of places around Gatlinburg have been designated as important stops along the trail. The trail has been divided into three separate pathways for exploring the arts and crafts, significant people and events, and the evolution of bluegrass and folk music that shaped the city.

**Ogle's Cabin**

Ogle's Cabin has the distinction of being the first historic site in Gatlinburg. The land was selected by William Ogle in 1802 as the site for the first "home" in Gatlinburg. Ogle died shortly thereafter, but several years later his widow moved their family to his "Paradise." The cabin was completed, and the Ogle family lived there until 1910. Since then, the building has been used as a hospital and a museum. Its current location is on the Arrowmont Campus on the Parkway.

**Dollywood**

Pigeon Forge is home to Dollywood, a popular amusement park founded by country singer Dolly Parton. Open late March through early January, Dollywood is known for its thrill rides and live entertainment, including country, 50s and 60s, and gospel music. Onsite craftsmen also provide demonstrations and items for sale, including leather, hand-blown glass, candles, sweets and handmade wagons. The Dollywood complex also includes the Dixie Stampede dinner show "Splash Country," a "water adventure park," and the brand new Adventure Mountain, the largest challenge course in the country, with more than 100 rope features.
