---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Pigeon Forge & Gatlinburg"
type: section
---

Pigeon Forge and Gatlinburg each provide a variety of amusements for
visitors of all ages, including a vibrant live music scene,
rollercoasters, historic sites and mouthwatering Southern cuisine. No
trip to the area would be complete without the following five
activities--**the Pigeon Forge & Gatlinburg must-do's**.

**Great Smoky Mountains National Park**

It would be easy to spend an entire vacation just exploring the
breathtaking Smokies: hiking and biking through the park's 500,000
acres; viewing wildlife like elk, salamanders and bears; river rafting
and fishing. The Great Smoky Mountains National Park is one of the most
visited national parks in the U.S., with more than 9 million visitors
flocking here to enjoy the park's natural beauty.

**Tennessee Heritage Trail**

The Tennessee Heritage Trail was developed by the state of Tennessee
as a self-guided tour to highlight various aspects of the city's
cultural history. A number of sites around Gatlinburg have been
designated as important stops along the way. The trail has been divided
into three separate pathways for exploring the arts and crafts,
significant people and events, and the evolution of bluegrass and folk
music that shaped the city.

**Dollywood**

Dollywood is a popular amusement park founded by country singer Dolly
Parton. Dollywood is best known for its thrill rides and live
entertainment. There are also local craftsmen on-site providing
demonstrations and items for sale. The Dollywood complex includes the
Dixie Stampede dinner show, Splash Country, a "water adventure park,"
and the brand new Adventure Mountain challenge course.

**The Mountain Mall**

The Mountain Mall is a mountain-themed shopping mall offering a
variety of products for sale including hand-made jewelry, local artwork,
homemade candy and custom-made tobacco products. Every detail in the
mall keeps with the mountain theme, from the cedar walls and whiskey
barrel garbage cans (used to make Jack Daniel's) to the piped-in
Appalachian mountain music, to the authentic decor including millstones
and a full-size bear trap.

**The Old Mill**

The Old Mill is listed on the National Register of Historic Places
and is one of the most popular sites in the Smokies. The actual Old Mill
is a fully functional grain grinder powered by the Little Pigeon River
via a giant water wheel. Among other uses, the Old Mill's ground
products go on to become corn bread, hush puppies, biscuits, grits and
artisan breads at the on-site Old Mill Restaurant and the Old Mill
Pottery House Cafe & Grille. Also on-site you will find stone ground
flour, handmade candy, handcrafted pottery and baked goods for sale.
