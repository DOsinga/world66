Sewanee is a small college town perched on the Cumberland Plateau about an hour and a half west of Chattanooga. The place is essentially the University of the South — founded in 1857 by Episcopalian bishops from across the pre-Civil War South, it still owns the 13,000 acres of forest and sandstone bluff that make up the "Domain". Gothic stone buildings sit among old oaks, and the campus has the slightly otherworldly feel of an English college transplanted to Tennessee.

Most visitors come for the hiking. The Perimeter Trail loops 20 miles around the edge of the Domain, linking a string of dramatic overlooks, bluffs, and waterfalls. Shorter spurs run out to specific viewpoints — Green's View, Proctor's Hall, Memorial Cross — and make easy half-day walks. The best seasons are spring, when the wildflowers come out, and mid-October, when the plateau turns red and gold.
