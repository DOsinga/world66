Sewanee is a tiny community on the Cumberland Plateau in southern Tennessee, defined almost entirely by the University of the South, whose Gothic stone buildings sit on a 13,000-acre campus called "The Domain." The setting is striking — the university occupies a forested plateau rimmed by bluffs with long views over the surrounding valleys.

The main draw for visitors is the hiking. The Perimeter Trail loops 32 km around the edge of the plateau, passing waterfalls, rock formations, and overlooks. Shorter sections are easily accessible for day hikes. Bridal Veil Falls and the Memorial Cross viewpoint are popular stops.

The university's All Saints' Chapel is worth a visit for its stained glass and stonework, and the campus has a pleasant small-town feel with a coffee shop, a bookstore, and not much else — which is part of the appeal.
