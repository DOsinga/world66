Dallas takes its food seriously, from upscale dining in Uptown to taco joints on every corner. Tex-Mex is a religion here, barbecue is an art form, and the city's international food scene keeps growing. Oak Lawn, Bishop Arts, and Lower Greenville are particularly good neighborhoods for eating your way through.

### [Eatzi's Bakery, Market & Restaurant](/northamerica/unitedstates/texas/dallas/eatingout/eatzis_bakery_ma)

A team of onsite chefs prepares food before your eyes at stations resembling a miniature European market. Excellent quality at reasonable prices. Located at 3403 Oak Lawn Ave.

### [Kalachandji's](/northamerica/unitedstates/texas/dallas/eatingout/kalachandjis)

Five-star vegetarian food at the corner of Gurley and Graham, often featuring Indian cuisines like rice, dal, and curry alongside international entrees. Their tamarind cooler and cinnamon raisin bread are must-haves. Located at 5430 Gurley.
