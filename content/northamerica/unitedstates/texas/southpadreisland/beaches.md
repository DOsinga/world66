South Padre Island, a beach community nestled in Southeast Texas,
is truly one of the best vacation destinations in the U.S. Don't get caught wearing a tie; when they say 'vacation destination', South Padre
Island is serious about their vacation business. The Island ordinance forbids
anyone from wearing tie, so be prepared to enjoy your vacation. No matter what
time of year, your age or activity level, there's always something for everyone
year around. Let the warm
tropical breeze caress your sun kissed tan while you enjoy a afternoon of
shelling. When the sun slowly sets you can head over to one of the many
nightclubs located on South Padre Island. For a more physical, active vacation, choose between sailing, parasailing, surfing or kite boarding. Relax and enjoy the sun, sand and surf.

**Isla Blanca
Beach County Park**

This is truly an amazing place,
where the Gulf of Mexico mixes with the Lower Laguna Madre. These two bodies of water create a diversified ecosystem. This
is definitely a place for kids of all ages who have a desire to learn about marine life, also known as ecotourism. The ecosystem attracts numerous species of
birds as well. You'll catch a glimpse of sea turtles in their natural habitat, crabs
and other marine life. Spend the day
surfing, kite boarding, fishing, swimming or heading up to the jetty to watch
the surfers, there is so much to do at Isla Blanca. Close to the beach is the
Dolphin Dove Activities Center, which includes two buildings: The Bingo Hall
and The Recreation Hall. There are restaurants within walking distance, or you
can order breakfast, lunch and diner menu at the Dolphin Dove Activities
Center. Call ahead to book your beach rentals, R.V. or tent reservations; space
is limited depending on the time of year.
For a daytrip, the cost per vehicle is a mere $4.00. The best time to visit
is between 8:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m.

Activities: Depending on your age and interest you'll enjoy Bingo at
the cost of $5.00, sandcastle lessons starting at $20.00 per hour, beachcombing
(free), surf board rental $25.00, wind sailing, kite boarding, fishing, or dolphin
watching.

**Coca Cola Beach**

This is a spring break hot spot
during the month of March. For the whole month of March, the festivities are so jam packed,
it's standing room only! Don't care for the party scene, bikinis, music and
fanfare? Don't worry, it's only one month out the year. Coca Cola Beach is a
great place to build sand castles, tan, horseback ride, engage in water sports
and dolphin watching (keep your eyes
open, you might catch the dolphins enjoying some of the water activities as
well). If you're not into the sun, sand and surf, head over to the go
carts, bungee jumping and electric cars; all within walking distance. This is a
public beach and will cost you nothing to gain access.

Activities: Surfing lessons
can run you anywhere from $60.00 per hour on up, if you have a group you might
be able to get a discount. Surf board rentals will run you $30.00 dollars per
day from South Padre Surf Company. Parasailing will cost $65.00 per person and
if you just want to observe on the boat, that will be $35.00.

**Padre Island National Seashore**

Naturalist
beaches are posted with signs giving a visitor plenty of early notification prior to entering the vicinity of one of these nudist beaches, located
along South Padre Island's shores. The best time to
visit this beach is between 7:00 a.m. - 9:00 p.m.; don't forget your sunscreen!
