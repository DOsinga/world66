Travel Guide

Dutch John is a small unincorporated town located in eastern
Daggett County, Utah, United States, about 4 miles northeast of the Flaming
Gorge Dam on U.S. Route 191. The town was platted and constructed beginning in
1957 by the United States Bureau of Reclamation to house workers working on the
construction of Flaming Gorge Dam. After the Dam's completion in 1964, the town
became home to a smaller number of dam maintenance and operations personnel, as
well as employees of the United States Forest Service.

The Dutch John town site and its buildings continued to be
owned by the Bureau of Reclamation until 1998, when the town was privatized.
Buildings were sold to individual landowners, and undeveloped land in the town
was transferred to Daggett County. The county later completed a master plan for
the Dutch John town site, and has begun offering parcels of land for sale to
developers.

During the peak years of construction activity at Flaming
Gorge Dam, as many as 3,500 people lived in Dutch John.
