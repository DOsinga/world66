Logan is a university town tucked into the Cache Valley in northern Utah, surrounded by mountains on three sides. Utah State University gives it a youthful energy, and the setting — green farmland ringed by the Bear River and Wellsville ranges — is striking.

The town itself is small and quiet, but the canyon roads heading east into the Wasatch Range are excellent. Logan Canyon is a scenic drive year-round and gives access to hiking, fishing, and skiing at Beaver Mountain. The Cache Valley is also known for its dairy farms, and the local cheese-making tradition is a genuine point of pride.
