Manti is a small town in the Sanpete Valley of central Utah, founded in 1849 by Mormon pioneers. It sits beneath the striking white limestone **Manti Utah Temple**, perched on a hill above town and visible for miles — one of the oldest and most architecturally impressive temples in the state.

The town's main claim to fame is the annual **Mormon Miracle Pageant** (now called the Manti Pageant), a large outdoor theatrical production held on the temple grounds each June that draws tens of thousands of visitors. Outside pageant season, Manti is a quiet agricultural town with a well-preserved Main Street and access to the surrounding Manti-La Sal National Forest for hiking, fishing and autumn foliage.
