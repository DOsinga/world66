Moab is really the heart of the Red Rock Southwest. Really. Look around you; it's everywhere. Not surprising that so many movies that intend on showing the Southwest are filmed around here. Two national parks, a nearby national forest, and huge chunks of BLM (Bureau of Land Management for you non-westerners, also derisively called "Bureau of Livestock and Mining" by the more ecologically minded) land for wandering make this one of the great spots in the southwest for outdoor recreation. But be warned: it's hot as hades in the summer, and that's when it's very crowded. Try the spring or fall to avoid the hordes. Still, hot or cold, it's a fascinating place. Mountain biking, river rafting, hiking, jeeping: it can all be done here.

### [Canyonlands National Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/moab/sights/canyonland_nationa)

Big, sprawling and remote, Canyonlands is what a national park should be. The east side of the park has two districts. Island in the Sky is north of Moab, and it's where you should go if you're just touring in a car and sightseeing. South of Moab, Needles is loaded with jeep roads and trails for the more hardcore locking differential type of traveler. Finally, on the other side of the river (get there via Hanksville) is the remote Maze District, for the most insane or enlightened sort of visitor. ### [Arches National Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/moab/sights/arches_national_pa)

One of the most popular national parks in the west, Arches is actually a rather small park unit, but it's full of hundreds of natural sandstone arches of varying sizes. Delicate Arch is perhaps the most famous, but dozens more await exploration along trails and pullouts in the park. A campground is also located in the northern section of the park. The visitor center is located just past the entrance gate of the park, and rangers on duty will provide brochures and maps for exploration.

### [Manti-La Sal National Forest](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/moab/sights/mantila_sal_natio)

The La Sal Mountains dominate the eastern horizon beyond Moab, and for those seeking a break from the desert heat of the summer, it's the place to go. A few small lakes and a couple of developed campgrounds beckon, with thousands of acres of wild country for wandering and exploring. The La Sal loop road is a paved drive through the country, and many miles of dirt and gravel forest roads allow for travel deep into the countryside.

type: Parks, Gardens and Zoos



address: West of Moab, use La Sal Loop Road
