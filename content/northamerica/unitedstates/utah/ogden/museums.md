Lots of history happened around Ogden. In town, enjoy the Union Station museum. Take a drive down south to the Hill Aerospace Museum to view a great collection of U. S. military aircraft. Go northwest to Promontory and Golden Spike National Historic Site to see where the rails were joined in 1869. Enjoy the historic downtown. Visit Fort Buenaventura State Park and see what it was like for the trappers who first explored the region.

### [Hill Air Force Base Museum](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/ogden/museums/hill_air_force_bas)

Located south of Ogden near Roy, this museum has one of the best collections of American military aircraft. Two huge buildings showcase everything from canvas covered biplanes to the SR-71, the fastest airplane ever built. The collection of cold war "century series" jets is particularly impressive. A hulking C-124 Globemaster greets visitors at the entrance, and a lineup of heavy aircraft includes a rare B-47 Stratojet and a B-52 Stratofortress.



### [Union Station](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/ogden/museums/union_station)

Once the crossroads of the west, this glorious station still captures the glamour and excitement of rail travel. Inside, museum exhibits interpret railroad and automotive history, while special events like model railroad shows often take place in the main lobby. One of Ogden's best restaurants is also located here, and a model railroad hobby shop allows one to take home a railroad souvenir. Finally, outside are some huge locomotives from the glory days of the Union Pacific railroad, and the historic 24th street district is just across the street.
