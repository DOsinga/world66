Park City is a mountain resort town in Utah, sitting at 2,100 metres in the Wasatch Mountains about 50 km east of Salt Lake City. It is best known as a ski destination — **Park City Mountain Resort** and **Deer Valley** are two of the largest and best-equipped ski areas in the United States — but it also has a strong summer identity with world-class mountain biking, hiking, and the annual **Sundance Film Festival** in January, which brings Hollywood to the Utah mountains every winter.

The historic Main Street is lined with Victorian-era buildings from the silver mining boom of the late 1800s. The **Park City Museum** in the old city hall covers the mining history, and the Utah Olympic Park from the 2002 Winter Olympics offers tours and year-round facilities for ski jumping and bobsledding.

Getting there: the town is about 45 minutes from Salt Lake City International Airport. Regular shuttle and bus services make it accessible without a car, though having one gives more flexibility on the mountain.
