---
title: "Accommodation in Park City"
type: location
---

Most hotels in Park City, Utah are in the area closest to University
of Utah, University of Utah Hospital and the Douglas Army Reserve
Center. There are also a few around Sugar House Park if you don’t want
to stay in those areas. The skiing here is wonderful and the town has a
cozy, old time historic feel. There are not many budget options but
there are luxury accommodations that cost more than $1500 a night.

**The
Chateau** on 1299 Norfolk Ave offers relatively cheap rooms, but no luxury options. Expect your clean room to have a bed,
couch, TV and a fridge. It is only a 10 minute stroll over to Main St.
For serious skiing convenience this hotel is a great value and a
wonderful break from the usual chain places.

**The Washington School Inn** on 543 Park Ave., is a popular Bed &
Breakfast with European charm. This is an excellent place for
vegetarians or those with food allergies and special diets. The chef
here is happy to cater the breakfast to your needs. You can walk to
shopping, restaurants and attractions but you will have to walk up and
down a steep hill. You will feel like you are not in a hotel but
visiting family.

**The Gables Hotel**, on 1335 Lowell Ave., has an excellent location for
skiing; you are right next to the lifts. It is almost ski-in / ski-out.
The units here are condos and are fully equipped and the kitchens are
cleaned everyday. There are two restaurants close by but you will
probably take the bus to town to get more selection. Wood burning
fireplaces and balconies make your stay here special.

For a fine smaller hotel try the **Goldener Hirsch Inn** on 7570 Royal
St. East. The staff here is fantastic and the rooms are comfortable.
The beds have a down mattress pad and high thread count sheets. The
whirlpool bathtub is very relaxing. Breakfasts are terrific and the
omelets are delicious. This prime location is all suites and it’s
convenient to everything.

The most elegant and expensive hotel in Park City, Utah is the**
Trails End Loge**. This ski-in and ski-out loge has luxurious hot tubs on
the back porch. There is a large salt water pool for those who are
sensitive to chlorine. There is a lot of free food to eat here. Their
full breakfast is followed in the evening by snacks that include soups,
sushi, wine and beer. Before bed huge chocolate chip cookies are served
with ice cream.
