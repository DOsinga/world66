St. George sits in Utah's southwest corner, surrounded by red rock cliffs and desert landscape that feels more like southern Nevada than the rest of the state. With over 300 days of sunshine a year and mild winters, it has become a base for outdoor pursuits and a popular stop on the drive between [Salt Lake City](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/saltlakecity) and [Las Vegas](/northamerica/unitedstates/nevada/lasvegas).

The real draw is what lies just outside town. [Zion National Park](/northamerica/unitedstates/utah/zion_national_park) is less than an hour northeast, and Snow Canyon State Park — with its red and white sandstone formations, lava flows, and quiet trails — is even closer. The area offers excellent hiking, mountain biking along the red cliffs, and ATV riding in the hills south of town. Gunlock, Quail Lake, and Sand Hollow reservoirs provide boating and swimming when the heat gets serious.

The town itself has a distinct character rooted in its Mormon settlement history. Brigham Young sent 309 families here in 1861 to grow cotton in the warm climate, earning the area the nickname "Dixie." The St. George Temple, completed in 1877, was the first Mormon temple finished in the West and remains a striking landmark. The restored Brigham Young winter home and the Tabernacle are part of a walkable historic core that gives the town more depth than the surrounding golf courses and strip malls might suggest.
