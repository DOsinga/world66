Sterling is a tiny farming village in Sanpete County, central Utah — a handful of streets, pasture fences and the occasional Mormon chapel, set in a wide valley below the Wasatch Plateau. The reason to stop is **Palisade State Park**, just east of the village: a lake carved out of an old 19th-century irrigation reservoir, ringed by cottonwoods and popular with local families for swimming, trout fishing, paddleboarding and one of Utah's prettiest small golf courses. The park has a campground and a few rental cabins, and the quiet of the valley comes as a welcome contrast to the busier parks further south around Moab.
