Torrey is a tiny town at the western entrance to Capitol Reef National Park, one of Utah's least-visited but most rewarding national parks. The setting is spectacular — red rock cliffs rise directly behind the town, and the Waterpocket Fold stretches for a hundred miles to the south.

The town itself is just a handful of lodges, a couple of restaurants, and a gas station, but that is part of the appeal. Torrey is the quiet alternative to the more developed gateway towns around Zion and Arches. From here you can drive the Capitol Reef scenic road, hike into the slot canyons, or head south on the Burr Trail for some of the most dramatic desert scenery in the American West.
