Brattleboro is the largest town in southern Vermont, sitting where the West River meets the Connecticut. It has a reputation for progressive politics, independent-minded residents, and a lively arts scene that punches well above its size — the town has more artists and musicians per capita than almost anywhere in New England.

Main Street is lined with independent bookshops, galleries, ethnic restaurants, and food co-ops. The Brattleboro Museum and Art Center rotates quality contemporary exhibitions. The farmers' market draws crowds on Saturdays throughout the growing season. Just across the state line in New Hampshire, locals do their grocery shopping at the big-box stores — a very Vermont contrast to the organic ethos back home.
