Killington is Vermont's biggest ski resort and one of the largest in the eastern United States, with seven mountain areas, nearly 100 kilometres of trails, and a season that often stretches from October into May. Locals call it "The Beast of the East," and on a busy winter weekend the access road is a slow crawl of ski traffic.

The skiing suits all levels, though intermediates get the most terrain. Killington Peak itself tops out at 1,293 metres with long cruising runs, while the steeper terrain is concentrated on Bear Mountain and Overton. The resort also has one of the better terrain parks on the East Coast.

In summer and autumn Killington reinvents itself as an outdoor base. The mountain biking trail network is expanding, the Long Trail and Appalachian Trail both pass through (they share a route here), and the fall foliage in late September and early October draws nearly as many visitors as the snow does. The access road has a decent collection of restaurants and bars that keep things lively in season.
