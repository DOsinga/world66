Montpelier is the smallest state capital in the United States — population under 10,000 — and one of the most agreeable. Compact, walkable, and attractive, it sits in a narrow valley along the Winooski River in central Vermont, surrounded by forested hills. The golden dome of the **Vermont State House** on State Street is visible from across town and worth a close look; the building has been in continuous use since 1859 and is open for free tours.

The city is dense with historic architecture, and a surprising number of good restaurants and independent businesses for its size. Montpelier is home to the **New England Culinary Institute**, which explains why the food scene punches above its weight — the NECI training restaurants on Main Street offer good-value meals cooked by students.

In winter, Montpelier is a useful base for skiing at nearby Stowe (35 miles north) and Mad River Valley (20 miles southwest). In autumn, the surrounding hills are among the best in New England for foliage.
