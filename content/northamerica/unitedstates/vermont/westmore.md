Westmore is a tiny town on the shores of Lake Willoughby, Vermont's fourth largest lake and arguably its most dramatic. The lake sits in a glacial cleft between Mount Pisgah and Mount Hor, with cliffs dropping straight into deep, clear water. The Lake Willoughby National Recreation Area and several state parks surround the town, making it a popular destination for swimming, fishing, and hiking in the heart of the Northeast Kingdom.
