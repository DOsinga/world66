Lynchburg is a mid-sized city of about 80,000 people set on seven hills above the James River in central Virginia. Founded in 1757, it was one of the wealthiest cities in the antebellum South thanks to the tobacco trade, and its hilltop neighborhoods are filled with beautifully preserved 19th-century architecture. The city survived the Civil War largely intact, which means the historic fabric is remarkably complete.

The **Bluff Walk** along the hilltop above the James River offers sweeping views of the Blue Ridge Mountains and is a good starting point for exploring the city. **Court House Hill** and **Diamond Hill** are the most architecturally interesting neighborhoods, with Federal, Greek Revival, and Victorian houses lining steep streets. The **Legacy Museum of African American History** and the **Old Court House Museum** both offer thoughtful looks at the city's complex past, including its role in the tobacco economy and the Civil War.

Lynchburg sits at the foot of the Blue Ridge, making it a natural base for outdoor excursions. The **Blue Ridge Parkway** is a short drive east, and the **James River** offers kayaking, tubing, and fishing through the heart of the city. The **Appalachian Trail** crosses nearby, and **Peaks of Otter** on the Parkway is one of the most popular day hikes in Virginia. Downtown has been quietly revitalizing, with good restaurants and breweries along Main Street and the riverfront. The city is also home to several colleges, including Randolph College and the University of Lynchburg, which give it more cultural life than you might expect.
