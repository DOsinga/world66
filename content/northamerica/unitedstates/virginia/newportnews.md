Newport News stretches along the lower James River at the eastern end of the Virginia Peninsula, a long industrial city of around 180,000 wedged between the colonial heritage country to the west and the navy yards of [Norfolk](/northamerica/unitedstates/virginia/norfolk) across Hampton Roads. Its identity is shipbuilding: **Newport News Shipbuilding**, the largest shipyard in the United States, has been launching warships from here since the 1880s and still builds the navy's nuclear aircraft carriers.

For travellers the highlight is **The Mariners' Museum**, one of the largest maritime museums in the world, with the recovered turret and engines of the Civil War ironclad **USS Monitor** as its centrepiece. **The Virginia Living Museum** combines a small zoo, native-species aquarium and planetarium on a single forested site, and the surrounding **Newport News Park**, sprawling over 8,000 wooded acres, is one of the largest municipal parks in the country and a good place to walk off a museum afternoon. The city itself is more functional than charming — Williamsburg and Yorktown, just up the road, are the prettier bases — but the maritime collections alone justify a half-day stop.
