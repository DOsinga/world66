---
title: "Bars and Cafes"
type: section
---

Richmond's drinking scene has two halves. On one side, the restored warehouse districts of **Shockoe Slip** and **Shockoe Bottom** are full of bars, pubs and live-music venues, including microbrewery-restaurants like **Richbrau** and the historic **Tobacco Company**. On the other, the post-industrial neighbourhood of **Scott's Addition** has been taken over in the last decade by a dense cluster of craft breweries (Ardent, The Veil, Hardywood), cideries, meaderies and small-batch distilleries — walking or cycling between half a dozen of them in an afternoon is one of the most enjoyable things to do in the city. For something more classic, the **Byrd Theatre** in Carytown shows old movies accompanied by a Wurlitzer organ and is a fixture of a Richmond Friday night.
