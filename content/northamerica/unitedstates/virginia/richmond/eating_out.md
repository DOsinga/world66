---
title: "Eating Out"
type: section
---

Richmond has quietly become one of the best eating cities in the American South. The scene is concentrated in a few walkable neighbourhoods. **Carytown** — a mile-long strip of independent restaurants and shops at the western edge of the Fan — has the greatest density, from old-guard favourites like **Nacho Mama's** (Mexican) and **Amici Ristorante** (Italian) to newer places along West Cary Street. **Shockoe Slip** and **Shockoe Bottom**, down by the river, are where the upscale places like **The Tobacco Company** (set in a restored tobacco warehouse) and the long-running **Bottoms Up Pizza** hold court. **Church Hill**, on the other side of the river, has some of the most ambitious new restaurants in the city. Specific dishes to look for: Virginia ham, shrimp and grits, and barbecue — in particular the eastern-North Carolina-style pulled pork that Richmond adopts as its own. Vegetarians are well served; **Ipanema Cafe**, near the VCU campus, is a long-standing local favourite.
