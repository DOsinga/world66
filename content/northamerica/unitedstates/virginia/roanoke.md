Roanoke is the largest city in western Virginia — a former railroad town of about 100,000 people tucked into a wide valley of the **Blue Ridge Mountains**, roughly midway between Washington DC and Knoxville on the Interstate 81 corridor. It grew up in the 1880s as the headquarters of the Norfolk & Western Railway, and the city's restored downtown still revolves around the 1882 **Hotel Roanoke** and the **Virginia Museum of Transportation**, which displays steam locomotives in an old freight station by the tracks.

The real reason to stop in Roanoke is the scenery and the access to the mountains. The **Blue Ridge Parkway** runs right past the city and some of its most spectacular stretches are within a short drive — **McAfee Knob** and **Dragon's Tooth** on the Appalachian Trail, both about 20 minutes west, rank among the most-photographed viewpoints on the entire 3,500-km trail. Above the city itself, the **Roanoke Star** — a massive 88-foot neon star on Mill Mountain that has been illuminated every night since 1949 — gives the city both its nickname ("Star City of the South") and one of the best panoramic views of the valley.
