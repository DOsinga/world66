Williamsburg sits at the heart of Virginia's Historic Triangle, a small city whose main street doubles as a time machine. Colonial Williamsburg — 301 acres of restored and reconstructed 18th-century buildings — is one of America's most ambitious living-history projects, and the reason most visitors come. Costumed interpreters work the trades, the taverns serve colonial-era recipes, and the Capitol and Governor's Palace have been rebuilt to their 1770s appearance.

But Williamsburg is more than its colonial district. The College of William & Mary, founded in 1693, makes it one of America's oldest college towns, and the campus brings a year-round buzz of restaurants and bars along Richmond Road and Prince George Street. Just east along the Colonial Parkway lie [Jamestown](/northamerica/unitedstates/virginia/jamestown) and [Yorktown](/northamerica/unitedstates/virginia/yorktown), completing a triangle that covers the founding, revolution, and independence of the United States in a single afternoon's drive.

Busch Gardens, a few miles east of the historic area, adds a theme-park dimension that families appreciate. The surrounding countryside — plantation houses, the James River, quiet back roads through pine forest — rewards anyone willing to explore beyond the cobblestones.
