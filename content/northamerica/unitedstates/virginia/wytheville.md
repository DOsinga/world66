Wytheville is a small town in the Blue Ridge Highlands of southwest Virginia, sitting at the junction of Interstates 77 and 81 — which makes it one of the most passed-through towns in the state. Most travelers stop for gas; the smarter ones stop for longer.

Named after George Wythe, Virginia's first law professor and a signer of the Declaration of Independence, the town punches above its weight historically. It became notorious during the Civil War as a Confederate supply depot and suffered a Union cavalry raid in 1863. More recently, it made the national news twice in odd ways: a wave of UFO sightings in the late 1980s that ended up on *Unsolved Mysteries*, and a post office hostage crisis in December 2009.

The main annual event is the Chautauqua Festival, held the third weekend of June since 1985. It draws live music, hot air balloons, magic acts, and craft vendors to Elizabeth Brown Memorial Park — a genuine community event, not a tourist production. The town's name, incidentally, is pronounced "WITH-vill."
