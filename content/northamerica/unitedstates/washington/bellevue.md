Bellevue sits across Lake Washington from [Seattle](/northamerica/unitedstates/washington/seattle) and has grown from a quiet suburb into a city in its own right — the fifth-largest in the state, home to major tech companies, and with a downtown skyline that keeps getting taller. It is cleaner, quieter, and more manicured than Seattle, though also blander.

The **Bellevue Botanical Garden** is a genuine highlight: 53 acres of cultivated gardens and natural wetlands, free to visit and especially beautiful in spring and autumn. The downtown core has excellent shopping and a surprisingly good restaurant scene, much of it driven by the city's large East Asian community. Bellevue is connected to Seattle by the I-90 and SR 520 bridges, and the new light rail extension makes it easy to visit without a car.
