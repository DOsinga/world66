Burlington is a small city in Skagit County, northwestern Washington state, about 80 km north of Seattle in the broad agricultural plain between the Cascade Mountains and Puget Sound. The Skagit Valley here is one of the most productive bulb-growing regions in North America, and in spring the tulip and daffodil fields in bloom around Burlington and nearby Mount Vernon attract visitors from across the region.

The Skagit Valley Tulip Festival runs each April and draws large crowds to the flower fields. Outside festival season, Burlington is primarily a commercial hub with a large outlet mall and easy access to Interstate 5. The North Cascades, beginning about an hour's drive east, are the main draw for those using Burlington as a base — Highway 20 over the North Cascades Pass offers some of the most dramatic mountain scenery in the state when it's open from late spring to early winter.
