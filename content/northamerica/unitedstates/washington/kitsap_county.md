Kitsap County occupies a peninsula in Puget Sound, directly across the water from [Seattle](/northamerica/unitedstates/washington/seattle) and reachable by the Washington State Ferry system. The hour-long ferry ride from downtown Seattle to Bremerton is one of the best cheap boat trips in the Pacific Northwest, with views of the Olympic Mountains and the Seattle skyline.

The county's identity is closely tied to the U.S. Navy. **Naval Base Kitsap** is one of the largest naval installations on the West Coast, split between Bremerton (home to the Puget Sound Naval Shipyard, where you can see aircraft carriers in drydock) and Bangor (the Trident submarine base in Silverdale). The **Bremerton Naval Museum** downtown tells the story of the shipyard and the Pacific fleet. The retired destroyer USS Turner Joy, a Vietnam-era ship, is open for self-guided tours at the waterfront.

Beyond the military heritage, Kitsap has a quieter appeal. **Poulsbo**, on the north end of the peninsula, is a charming waterfront town with Scandinavian heritage, good bakeries, and kayak rentals on Liberty Bay. **Port Orchard**, the county seat, has an antiques district and a foot ferry to Bremerton. The peninsula is also the jumping-off point for the Olympic Peninsula and the vast wilderness of Olympic National Park to the west. Kitsap rewards a slower pace: take the ferry, walk the waterfront, eat some fish and chips, and watch the boats go by.
