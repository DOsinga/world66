**Puyallup** is a city in the shadow of Mount Rainier in Washington state, about 30 minutes south of Seattle and Tacoma. The name comes from the Puyallup people and is pronounced "pew-AL-up."

The city is best known for the Washington State Fair (still called "the Puyallup Fair" by locals), held every September at the Western Washington Fairgrounds — it is one of the largest fairs in the country. The surrounding valley is historically agricultural, known for daffodil farms and berry fields, and the Daffodil Festival parade in April is a long-standing tradition. Mount Rainier dominates the view to the southeast on clear days.
