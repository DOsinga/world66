South of the Reflecting pool, with symmetry to the Vietnam Veterans Memorial, is the Korean War Veterans Memorial. The memorial is an involving path that reminds visitors of the multi-national force which fought to keep Korea free. The war, being as deadly in terms of American lives as Vietnam, lasted a difficult three years, and marked the first major combat action of troops under the United Nations.

The memorial is in a generally triangular shape, with statues of soldiers of all branches of the military marching in the center. Quotations and a pool of remembrance, with an American flag are at the tip of the monument. The best time to see it is in the evening, when the low light makes the statues look more alive, and tired from battle. At night the statues are well light from below.

Foggy Bottom Metro stop is the closest to the memorial. The easiest way to find it is to find the Lincoln, and look for the signs.
