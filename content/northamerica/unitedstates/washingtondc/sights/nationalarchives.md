The National Archives - Home to the Declaration of Independence, the U.S. Constitution, and the Bill of Rights. Yes, the originals. Unfortunately, under special lights to prevent deterioration, they are hardly legible. But still, seeing these great documents in the flesh can be a profound experience. Their words are the basis for "the American Dream." But the Archives are more than just a place for Americans to say "yea for our system of government." The National Archives and Record Administration exists ". . . to ensure ready access to essential evidence . . .that documents the rights of American citizens, the actions of federal officials, and the national experience . . ." Anyone who desires to can tap into the NARA's resources for research; this editor's father uses them to study our family's genealogy. Entrance to the Exhibition Hall is made through metal detectors and no flash photography is permitted. Free. Exhibition Hall: 10am-9pm[April 1-Labor Day], 10am-5:30pm[Labor Day-March 31], enter on Constitution Ave. entrance (at 7th St.), N.W. Research Rooms: enter on see website for hours. Metro: Archives
