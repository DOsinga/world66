The
Nation's capital is one of the top destinations in North America all
year round because of its rich history, great location and amicable
Washingtonians.
**

Winter, What Winter?**

D.C.
is a city with a humid subtropical weather, typical of mid Atlantic
U.S. cities nearby bodies of water with four very well defined seasons.
This humid subtropical climate allows D.C. to still be a great
destination during the winter months because average winter low
temperatures are around 30 degrees F (-1 degrees C) which are very mild
for the Northeastern region of the United States. Be aware that a
blizzard can hit this city every 4 to 6 years.

If
you're looking for a specific time to visit during the winter months
there is no better time than the last week of March and the first week
of April during the National Cherry Blossom Festival! Every year this
festival celebrates the 1912 gift of 3,000 cherry trees from Mayor
Yukio Ozaki of the city of Tokyo to the city of Washington, D.C. This
beautiful gift reminds Washingtonians and visitors to Washington about
the lasting friendship between the United States and Japan. When
heading towards the cherry blossoms, it's highly recommended that you use
public transportation. The Smithsonian Metrorail Station (both the Blue
and Orange Lines) is most popular among the National Cherry Blossom
Festival visitors. Even though the following station, L'Enfant Plaza
Station (Blue, Yellow, and Green Lines) at the 7th Street &
Maryland Avenue exit, is about an extra 10 minute walk to the blossoms,
we highly recommend it because it offers a far less crowded view of the
cherry blossoms.
**

Bring on the Summer!**

Summers
in D.C. are not only hot in temperature, but also in major concert
tours from mainstream musicians that hit the city. Daily high
temperatures in the summer months average around 80 degrees F (about 30
degrees C), which is hot but still manageable.

If
you're looking for a specific time to visit Washington, D.C. during the
summer months, look no further that the 4th of July! This is the
biggest celebration in the United States, this is the Independence Day
nonetheless, so what better place to celebrate it than in the capital
of the United States! The Washington Mall area is the place to be
during this celebration. Make sure to get there early because people
from around the world come here to party, enjoy some outdoor grilling
and the major firework show at night. Don't let anybody tell you about
the best firework show in the United States, come live it right here,
only in the city of Washington, D.C.
