Bluefield sits in the Appalachian mountains right on the West Virginia–Virginia border, with a twin city of the same name on the Virginia side. Once a prosperous coal and railroad town, it has the handsome brick downtown of a place that was once considerably richer than it is today.

The town calls itself "Nature's Air-Conditioned City" — at 2,600 feet elevation, summers are cool by southern standards, and when the temperature does reach 90 degrees Fahrenheit, the Chamber of Commerce hands out free lemonade on Bland Street. It is the kind of small, specific tradition that makes Appalachian towns worth visiting.

Bluefield is a logical stop on a drive through the southern West Virginia coalfields, and the East River Mountain views from town are striking. The Appalachian Trail crosses nearby, and Pipestem Resort State Park is about an hour north.
