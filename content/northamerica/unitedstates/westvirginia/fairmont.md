Fairmont sits at the confluence of the Monongahela, Tygart Valley, and West Fork rivers in the rolling hills of north-central West Virginia. It is the county seat of Marion County and a small, pleasant town of about 20,000 people.

The area is known for its Italian-American heritage — the pepperoni roll, a West Virginia staple, was invented here by Italian immigrant Giuseppe Argiro in 1927. Country Club Bakery still makes them to the original recipe. Prickett's Fort State Park, just outside town, is a reconstructed 18th-century frontier fort and a good stop for history buffs. The rail-trail along the Mon River is popular with cyclists and walkers.
