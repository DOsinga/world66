Wheeling sits in the narrow Northern Panhandle of West Virginia, squeezed between Ohio and Pennsylvania along the Ohio River. In the early 1800s it was the western terminus of the National Road and the site of the first bridge across the Ohio — which made it one of the most important gateways to the American West before the railroads arrived.

That history is written into the city's fabric. The compact downtown and nearby Wheeling Island preserve some remarkably intact Victorian streetscapes — grand brick storefronts and ornate row houses built when Wheeling was a booming industrial city making nails, glass, and cigars. The remnants of that industrial era are everywhere: hulking factories on the riverbanks, and the mansions of the men who owned them up in the hills. Wheeling is an honest, unsentimental place, and that's part of its appeal.

Just outside the city, Oglebay Park is the standout attraction — a former private estate turned public park with formal gardens, a mansion museum, and good hiking. Wheeling also hosts Jamboree USA, a long-running country and Americana music series; if an act you like is playing, the setting makes it memorable.
