title: "Downtown and Wheeling Island"

Wheeling's downtown is compact and flat, built along the riverbank in the 19th century when the city was prosperous enough to put up serious architecture. Centre Market — an 1853 market house — is the heart of it, still functioning as a public market. On Wheeling Island, the mid-river sandbar connected by bridge, you'll find a dense grid of Victorian houses in various states of preservation. The contrast between the ornate brick facades and the post-industrial quiet of the streets around them is striking. The island also has a horse racing and gaming facility at Wheeling Island Hotel-Casino-Racetrack if that's your thing.
