Wisconsin is the dairy state and the Great Lakes state — a place of cheese, beer, lakes, forests, and a certain stubborn Upper Midwestern cheerfulness. It sits between Lake Michigan and the Mississippi River, with Lake Superior bordering the rugged north. Glaciers shaped most of the landscape, but missed a strip of the southwest: the **Driftless Area**, with its limestone bluffs, deep coulees, and trout streams, is one of the more beautiful and distinctive pockets of the Midwest.

[Milwaukee](/northamerica/unitedstates/wisconsin/milwaukee) on Lake Michigan is the largest city — a blue-collar town with a very good art museum (the Santiago Calatrava addition is one of the signature pieces of American architecture), German-heritage beer halls, and a walkable waterfront. [Madison](/northamerica/unitedstates/wisconsin/madison), the state capital, is a university town squeezed onto an isthmus between two lakes, with the state capitol building at its centre.

The north is lake country — thousands of lakes, pine forests, cabins, and a whole culture of fishing, hunting, and summer weekends. [Superior](/northamerica/unitedstates/wisconsin/superior) sits on the big lake across from Duluth, Minnesota. The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is a good reason to drive up.

The central and western parts of the state hold the oddities and set-pieces. The Wisconsin Dells is a peculiar mix of natural sandstone gorge and American-style water-park tourism. **Spring Green** is where Frank Lloyd Wright built Taliesin, his home and studio, which remains open for tours. **Cave of the Mounds** near Blue Mounds and **Circus World Museum** at Baraboo are other roadside classics.
