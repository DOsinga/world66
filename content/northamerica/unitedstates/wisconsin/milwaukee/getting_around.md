---
title: "Getting Around in Milwaukee"
type: section
---

Become familiar with **Milwaukee transportation **before you visit the metropolitan city in southeastern Wisconsin nestled along Lake Michigan's shores. Knowing what to expect can help you plan your trip.

**Planes**

Milwaukee's General Mitchell International Airport is the largest hub for Wisconsin air transportation. You can get virtually anywhere from Mitchell (although you may need a connecting flight for some international destinations) and you can take short, fairly inexpensive flights from Mitchell to nearby popular destinations such as Madison, Green Bay, Minneapolis and Chicago. The airport is roomy with plenty of parking, although the shopping experience leaves a bit to be desired.

**Buses**

Buses are the easiest way to get around Milwaukee using public transportation--and the most affordable. Besides trips to nearby cities such as Madison and Chicago offered by coach buses, the Milwaukee County Transit System services about 85 percent of the area of Milwaukee. Fares are $2.25 for adults and $1.10 for kids ages six through 11 as well as seniors and the disabled, although if you're going to spend a few days in Milwaukee, you may want to invest in 10-ride or weekly passes, which cost $17.50 for adults and $11 for kids and seniors. (Kids five and under are free.)

**Taxis**

Unlike other metropolitan cities, Milwaukee doesn't have a huge number of roaming taxis that you can count on to get from place to place, except at the airport. However, if you have a cell phone on hand, you can call a taxi to pick you up from anywhere in the city. A taxi between the airport and downtown Milwaukee costs around $30.

**Trains**

Highly convenient and much less expensive than a flight is Milwaukee's Amtrak service, which runs trains through downtown Milwaukee as well as near the airport and can take you to Sturtevant (Wisconsin), Glenview (Illinois) and Chicago. Expect the Milwaukee-Chicago ticket to cost about $22 and take around an hour and 30 minutes.
