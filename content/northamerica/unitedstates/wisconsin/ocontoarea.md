Oconto County stretches over 1,000 square miles of northeastern Wisconsin, from the Green Bay shoreline in the east to the Nicolet National Forest in the west. Fifty-three lakes with public access and more than 300 miles of trout streams make it serious fishing country.

The county seat of Oconto holds one of the region's more unusual historical sites: Copper Culture Mound Park, burial grounds of a civilization that worked copper some 5,000 years ago. Artifacts from these people are on display at the Beyer Historic Home Museum. The town's Historic Main Street District offers a self-guided walking tour through the later chapter of the area's past — the logging boom that shaped so much of northern Wisconsin.
