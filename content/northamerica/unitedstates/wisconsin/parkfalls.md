Located in Price County, with 300,000 acres of unspoiled public land, Park Falls is the headquarters of the 850,000-acre Chequamegon National Forest.

Just to the south, Fifield has two historic sites as well as one recently built point of interest - the Smith Rapids Covered Bridge on the South Fork of the Flambeau River. A fascinating collection of folk art can be found at the Wisconsin Concrete Park in Phillips at the center of the county. At 1,951.5 feet above sea level, Timm's Hill in southeast Price County, near Ogema, is the highest point in the state.
