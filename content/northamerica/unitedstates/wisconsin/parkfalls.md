Park Falls is a small city in Price County, northern Wisconsin, and the gateway to the Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest — a vast tract of 850,000 acres of public land that surrounds the area. The region is popular with anglers, hunters, canoeists, and hikers who come for the lakes and rivers of the Wisconsin Northwoods.

Just south of town, the historic Smith Rapids Covered Bridge spans the South Fork of the Flambeau River near Fifield. The Wisconsin Concrete Park in nearby Phillips contains a remarkable collection of folk art sculptures created by Fred Smith, a local farmer and logger. At 1,951 feet, Timm's Hill near Ogema is the highest point in Wisconsin, an easy hike with views over the surrounding forest.
