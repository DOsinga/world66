Stockholm is a tiny village on the Wisconsin side of the Mississippi River, perched on the shore of Lake Pepin — a 30-mile-long natural widening of the river that is one of the most beautiful stretches of the Upper Mississippi. Highway 35, the Great River Road and Wisconsin's only National Scenic Byway, passes right through.

Founded in 1851 by Swedish immigrant farmers, Stockholm today has a population of barely a hundred, but its charm draws visitors from across the region. The village's rural period architecture has been well preserved, and a small community of artists and craftspeople has set up galleries and studios alongside independent shops and cafes. The village park sits right on the lakeshore, with a 700-foot pier reaching out into the river.

Less than a mile from the village museum, Maiden Rock Bluff rises 300 feet above the water. The bluff and surrounding 200 acres were permanently protected in 2004, offering public access to some of the finest views on the Great River. The surrounding countryside features the Big Woods and sprawling farmland made famous by Laura Ingalls Wilder.
