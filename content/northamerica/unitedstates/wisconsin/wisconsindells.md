Wisconsin Dells calls itself the "Waterpark Capital of the World," and the claim is not far-fetched. This small city on the Wisconsin River has more indoor and outdoor waterparks per capita than anywhere else in the United States, drawing millions of visitors each summer from Chicago, Milwaukee, and the wider Midwest.

Beneath the waterslides and fudge shops, though, the actual dells are worth seeing. The original attraction is the river gorge itself — dramatic sandstone cliffs carved by glacial meltwater, best seen from the old-fashioned duck boats (amphibious vehicles from World War II) that have run tours here since the 1940s. The Upper Dells stretch is the most scenic, with narrow canyons and overhanging rock formations.

Outside the waterpark strip, Mirror Lake and Devil's Lake state parks offer good hiking and swimming without the crowds. Devil's Lake, about twenty minutes south, has quartzite bluffs rising 500 feet above the lake and some of the best day hikes in southern Wisconsin.
