---
title: "Eating Out in Cheyenne"
type: section
---

Cheyenne's dining scene is unpretentious and mostly local. A few places worth knowing about:

**The Egg & I** (2300 Carey Avenue) is a solid breakfast spot — eggs prepared just about every way, plus pancakes, french toast, and waffles. They also do lunch: sandwiches, salads, and soups.

**Twin Dragon** (1809 Carey Avenue) is the go-to for Chinese food, with both a menu and a buffet. Good-value lunch option.

**Guadalajara Family Mexican Restaurant** (1745 Dell Range Boulevard) does reliable Mexican — fajitas, enchiladas, burritos — with generous portions and strong margaritas.

**The Albany** (Capitol Avenue) is a classic American bar and grill downtown. Burgers, sandwiches, prime rib, and a full bar. Good spot for a casual lunch near the Capitol.

**Bread Basket Bakery** (1819 Maxwell Avenue) opens at 6am and does fresh cinnamon rolls, scones, muffins, and loaves. Lunch runs to chicken salad sandwiches and homemade soups.

**Texas Roadhouse** is a reliable chain steakhouse if you're traveling with kids — big portions, lots of options, and a children's menu.
