---
title: "Things to Do in Cheyenne"
type: section
---

Cheyenne punches above its weight for a small state capital. The main draws are the rodeo culture, a surprisingly good botanic garden, and the historic State Capitol building — but there's enough variety to fill a day or two.

**Cheyenne Botanic Garden** is a genuinely pleasant stop. Several hundred plant varieties are spread across multiple houses and gardens, including the Bedont Rose Garden with its water fountain and teakwood bench swing. More at botanic.org.

**Cheyenne Frontier Days Old West Museum** is the place for rodeo history and western Americana. The collection runs to over 55,000 artifacts, more than 100 horse-drawn carriages and wagons, western and folk art, and a Hall of Fame honoring contributors to Frontier Days. There's a kids' room with interactive displays. Hours, directions, and prices at oldmuseum.org.

**Wyoming State Capitol** — the cornerstone was laid on May 18, 1887, and the building remains the architectural centrepiece of downtown. It's a three-and-a-half-story structure around 300 feet long, topped by its distinctive dome. Worth a look inside if you're downtown; the interior details reflect the territory's ambitions at statehood. More at wyoshpo.state.wy.us.

**Frontier Days** — if you're anywhere near Cheyenne in late July, this ten-day rodeo and western festival is the real thing, one of the oldest and largest in the country. Expect bull riding, barrel racing, concerts, and pancake breakfasts.
