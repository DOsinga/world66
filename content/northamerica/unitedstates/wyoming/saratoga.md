Saratoga lies in Wyoming's upper North Platte River valley, just west of the Snowy Range Mountains, surrounded by high sage desert. The town exists because of the natural hot springs that surface along the river, and with a population under 2,000 it remains a quiet, friendly place.

The Hot Pool is the main attraction — a rustic concrete pool fed by natural springs that bubble up through coarse sand at temperatures regularly reaching 43°C. It is free, open around the clock, and best enjoyed after sunset in winter, when snow falls on your shoulders while you soak. The natural lithium in the water promotes easy sleep.

In summer, fishing on the North Platte is excellent — you will likely spot bald eagles along with your trout. The 9-mile trail along the cliffs above Lake Marie in the Snowy Range offers breathtaking views, though weather at altitude can change quickly. Winter brings ice fishing, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing.

Between Memorial Day and Labor Day, the scenic drive from Laramie across the Snowy Range Road (Highway 130) is one of the most spectacular mountain drives in the state. The road closes in winter.
