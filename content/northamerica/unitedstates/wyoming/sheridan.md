Sheridan sits in the Powder River basin at the foot of the Bighorn Mountains in northern Wyoming, a ranching town that has kept more of its character than most places its size. The historic downtown along Main Street has well-preserved brick buildings, good restaurants, and the Mint Bar — a classic Western saloon with saddles for bar stools.

The real draw is the landscape. The Bighorn National Forest rises steeply to the west, with fishing streams, alpine meadows, and the spectacular switchbacks of US Route 14 climbing over the range. Cloud Peak Wilderness offers serious backcountry hiking, and in winter the area gets deep snow for cross-country skiing and snowmobiling.

Sheridan is also the gateway to the region's ranching heritage — several working guest ranches in the foothills offer riding, cattle work, and a taste of Wyoming life that is far more authentic than anything in Jackson Hole. The town hosts one of the largest rodeos in the state each July.
