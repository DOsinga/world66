Star Valley is a long, fertile valley in western Wyoming, flanked by the Salt River Range to the east and the Wyoming Range to the west. A string of small towns runs down its length, with Afton as the main centre. The valley sits at around 1,900 metres and is known for clean air, big skies, and a pace of life that hasn't changed much in decades.

The valley's most unusual attraction is its "Periodic Spring" near Afton — a natural spring that pulses on a roughly 18-minute cycle, gushing water for several minutes then stopping completely before starting again. One of only three known cold-water periodic springs in the world. Afton's main arch, built from thousands of elk antlers, has become an unlikely landmark. The valley also claims Rulon Gardner, the wrestler who stunned the world by defeating Alexander Karelin at the 2000 Sydney Olympics.
