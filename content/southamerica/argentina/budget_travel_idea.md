**Argentina on a Shoe String**

**Argentina budget travel** is easier than you think. Here are some quick tips to help you prepare your next visit to this beautiful South American nation.

#### **Travel During the Low Tourist Season**

If you have a limited budget, it is a good idea to head to Argentina during the slow tourist season. During December through February, Argentina experiences hot and humid weather, which slows down tourist activity. Several travel agencies and airlines offer attractive discounts during these months. If you're traveling from any country in the Americas, Copa Airlines is a good starting point to check prices because they offer the most direct flights to and from Argentina within the American continent.

#### **Save Money by Staying in a Hostel**

Before leaving for Argentina, apply for a Hostelling International membership card because it will give you access to the lowest price at over 80 hostels around Argentina. It is important to apply for your membership card before you arrive to Argentina, because the application within Argentina is restricted to nationals and exchange students with current student IDs. This card will also give you discounts at participating bars, restaurants and retailers and other special attractions.

#### **Getting Around Buenos Aires on a Budget**

When visiting Buenos Aires, the most cost efficient way to get around is through the subway, also known as the "subte" by locals. If you're planning to travel by subway often, it is a good idea to buy the 10-trip passes for about 7 Argentinean pesos. When traveling at night, it is better to travel by taxi. It is recommended that you call for a cab to pick you up rather than to flag down one from the street, especially late at night when returning from a bar.
