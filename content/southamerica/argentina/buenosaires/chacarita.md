The working-class counterpoint to the grand Recoleta Cemetery. **Cementerio de la Chacarita** is where Carlos Gardel, the definitive voice of tango, and former president Juan Perón are buried (Perón's tomb was broken into in 1987 and his hands cut off — a classic bit of Argentine political macabre). Gardel's tomb is the pilgrimage site, with a perpetual lit cigarette placed between the statue's fingers. Less grand than Recoleta, more authentic.
