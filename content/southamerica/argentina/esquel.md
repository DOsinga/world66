Esquel is a small city in the Andean foothills of Chubut Province, in the Argentine Patagonian lake district. It sits at the edge of the Andes, surrounded by lakes, rivers, and the vast Lago Futalaufquen in the Los Alerces National Park to the west. The national park protects an ancient alerce forest — alerce trees (Fitzroya cupressoides) can live for over 3,000 years, and some of the specimens in the park are among the oldest living things on earth.

Esquel is also the southern terminus of La Trochita, the famous narrow-gauge steam railway better known as the Old Patagonian Express (after the Paul Theroux book). The train still runs on certain days and is worth riding for the experience of crossing the steppe behind a steam engine that looks as if it belongs in another century. The town is quiet and unpretentious, and it makes a good base for trekking, fly fishing, and skiing at La Hoya in winter.
