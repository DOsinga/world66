**Half a Million Penguins on one Beach**

A great stop for **Argentina family travel **is Punta Tombo. This natural reserve is home to about a half a million Magellan penguins from September to March. These penguins are about 2 1/2 feet tall and are friendly. The cute little animals are fun for adults and children alike. The eggs start hatching in November, and it's a great spectacle.

#### **The Natural Reserve of Punta Tombo**

The entrance fee is about 35 pesos per adult. With your ticket you get some information about the reserve and animals. There is a parking lot, restrooms and a small convenience store with drinks and food, but it's best to bring your own if you have children. The public is allowed to roam the protected area and some within inches of the penguins, but don't touch them. The area is also home to sea lions, whales, orcas, sheep, fox and some ostrich. The penguins, however, are the most fun.

#### **Getting to Punta Tombo**

If you are in Puerto Madryn, Rawson or Trelew, you can join a tour to bring you to Punta Tombo. Some tours are by bus, and others are by boat which combine whale watching on the same tour. You can also rent a car which will save you some money if there are at least four passengers, but it will take you from 90 to 120 minutes to get there. The last 22 kilometers (14 miles) are unpaved. If you have patient children, it's fine, but you might consider taking the boat tour.
