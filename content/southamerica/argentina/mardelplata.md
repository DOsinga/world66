Mar del Plata is Argentina's biggest beach resort, a city of around 600,000 on the Atlantic coast some 400 kilometres south of Buenos Aires. For over a century it has been where porteños escape the summer heat — the 29 miles of coastline range from packed urban beaches with full resort infrastructure to quieter stretches backed by dunes.

La Perla is the most popular beach, with a long boardwalk lined with bars, restaurants, and shops. Playa Grande draws a younger crowd and is the centre of the nightlife scene. Punta Mogotes has 24 resorts with beach sports and parasol rental. For surfing, head to Playa Mariano (also known as Honu Beach) at the southern end, near the lighthouse. The water is cool even in summer — expect 14 to 17°C — but the waves and the atmosphere make up for it.

Beyond the beach, Mar del Plata has a lively cultural scene, a working fishing port where you can eat fresh seafood straight off the boats, and some fine early 20th-century architecture along the seafront.
