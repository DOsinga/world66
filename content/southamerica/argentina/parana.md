Paraná is the capital of Entre Ríos province, sitting on a bluff above the river that shares its name. Across the water, connected by the Subfluvial Tunnel, lies the much larger city of Santa Fe. Paraná is the quieter, greener counterpart — a pleasant provincial capital with a long riverside promenade (the Costanera), parks, and a relaxed pace of life.

The city centre has some fine 19th-century architecture, including the cathedral on the main plaza and the old provincial government building. Parque Urquiza stretches along the riverfront with good views and shade. Paraná was briefly the capital of Argentina in the 1850s, a fact the city is proud of but that most visitors do not know.

It is not a major tourist destination, but if you are travelling through the Argentine Litoral — the riverside region between Buenos Aires and the north — Paraná is a comfortable stop with good river fish restaurants and a genuine local character.
