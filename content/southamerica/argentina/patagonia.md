Made famous by Bruce Chatwin's restless travelogue, Patagonia covers the vast southern cone of Argentina from roughly the Rio Colorado down to Tierra del Fuego. It is a land of rugged beauty and wilderness -- endless steppe, jagged peaks, ancient glaciers, and a coastline teeming with marine life. If you are looking for wide open spaces and serious outdoor adventure, this is it.

Patagonia does not have any big cities. The lake district hub of [Bariloche](/southamerica/argentina/bariloche) and the oil town of [Comodoro Rivadavia](/southamerica/argentina/patagonia/comodoro_rivadavia) are the closest things, but city life is not what you come here for. The headline attractions are the Perito Moreno glacier and the wider Glaciares National Park near [El Calafate](/southamerica/argentina/patagonia/calafate), the wildlife-rich [Valdes Peninsula](/southamerica/argentina/patagonia/valdespeninsula), and [Ushuaia](/southamerica/argentina/patagonia/ushuaia) at the southern tip of Tierra del Fuego -- the southernmost city in the world and a gateway to Antarctica.

Getting around takes time. Distances are enormous, roads are often unpaved, and the wind never stops. But the emptiness is the point. Patagonia rewards patience with landscapes that feel genuinely untouched.
