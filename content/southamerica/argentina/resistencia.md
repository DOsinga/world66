Resistencia is the capital of Chaco province in northern Argentina, on the banks of the Río Negro opposite Corrientes. It calls itself the "City of Sculptures" — there are hundreds of sculptures displayed on street corners and plazas throughout the city, and a major biennial sculpture event draws artists from across the continent. The Fogón de los Arrieros cultural centre is the heart of the city's artistic life and one of the most interesting cultural spaces in the north.

The city is also a useful jumping-off point for exploring the Chaco region, one of the least-visited environments in South America — a vast subtropical dry forest and wetland system with excellent birdwatching and wildlife.
