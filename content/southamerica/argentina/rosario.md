Rosario is Argentina's third largest city and, to many Argentines, its most livable — a port city on the west bank of the Paraná River, about 300 km north of [Buenos Aires](/southamerica/argentina/buenosaires), with a thriving cultural scene and a genuine sense of civic pride. It is also the birthplace of two of Argentina's most recognisable exports: Ernesto Che Guevara was born here in 1928 (the building at Entre Ríos 480 can be visited), and Lionel Messi grew up in the city.

The centre of town is the area around **Plaza 25 de Mayo** and the pedestrianised streets of San Martín and Córdoba — good for shopping and café-sitting. The **Monumento Nacional a la Bandera**, a massive riverside monument to the Argentine flag (which was first raised here by its creator Manuel Belgrano in 1812), dominates the waterfront. The art scene is well developed: the **Museo de Bellas Artes Juan B. Castagnino** has a good collection of Argentine and European painting. The costanera — the riverside promenade and series of beaches along the Paraná — is the heart of leisure life in summer.

Rosario has a strong restaurant and nightlife culture for a city its size, driven by its large student population.
