Salta is one of Argentina's most rewarding cities — a beautifully preserved colonial centre in the Lerma Valley, ringed by mountains in the country's north-west. Known as "Salta la Linda" (Salta the Beautiful), it earns the nickname: the plaza, cathedral, and surrounding streets feel genuinely alive rather than museum-piece.

The city centre is compact enough to cover on foot. Plaza 9 de Julio is the heart of things, flanked by the salmon-pink Cathedral and the Cabildo. Spend a morning wandering the streets around it and you'll quickly get a feel for a city that takes pride in its architecture without being precious about it. Locals fill the plaza cafes in the evenings; this is a city that still has a functioning street life.

Beyond the colonial centre, Salta is a superb base for exploring one of Argentina's most dramatic landscapes. The Quebrada de Humahuaca and the salt flats of Jujuy are within reach, and the route through the Calchaquí Valleys to Cachi is among the most beautiful drives in the country. The altitude is noticeable — the city sits at around 1,150m, and day trips often climb much higher.
