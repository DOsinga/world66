San Miguel de Tucumán is Argentina's fifth-largest city and the capital of its smallest province. It is known as the "Garden of the Republic" for its lush subtropical vegetation, and as the place where Argentine independence was declared on 9 July 1816 — the Casa Histórica de la Independencia, now a museum, is the city's most important landmark.

Tucumán is a gateway to the Quebrada de Humahuaca and the Yungas cloudforests, and the drive through the Tafí del Valle to the rugged Calchaquí Valleys is one of northwest Argentina's great road trips. The city itself is busy and commercial, with a lively centre around Plaza Independencia, good empanadas, and a university that keeps the nightlife going.
