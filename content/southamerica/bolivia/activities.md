---
title: "Activities"
type: section
---

Bolivia rewards people who like to do things rather than just see things. The combination of extreme altitude, vast landscapes, and intact wilderness makes it one of the better adventure destinations in South America.

**Cycling the Death Road** is the most iconic day trip from La Paz. Yungas Road drops 3,600 metres over 64 kilometres from the Andean pass at La Cumbre down to the subtropical town of Coroico. The old single-lane track hugs cliff edges above dramatic valley drops; a paved alternative route now handles vehicle traffic, leaving the original road largely to mountain bikers. Dozens of operators in La Paz run the trip — choose one that provides good equipment (full-face helmet, quality bike) rather than just the cheapest option.

**Trekking** in the Cordillera Real north of La Paz is outstanding. The classic Choro Trail descends from the altiplano through cloud forest to the Yungas over three days. The Takesi Trail and Yunga Cruz Trail are shorter alternatives. All three pre-Columbian paths are well-marked, pass through dramatic scenery, and can be done independently with a good map.

**Mountaineering** in the Cordillera Real is serious high-altitude climbing. Huayna Potosí (6,088m) is the most popular summit and can be climbed in two days by fit travellers with no technical experience (though you need crampons and ice axe). Illimani (6,438m) and Chearoco (6,127m) are harder. The season runs April to August. Multiple La Paz operators offer guide services.

**Trekking in Madidi National Park** gives access to one of the most biodiverse patches of forest on Earth. The park spans from the Andes to the Amazon basin and shelters an extraordinary concentration of bird species, mammals, and reptiles. Rurrenabaque is the gateway town; boat trips up the Beni and Tuichi rivers are the main way in. Trips typically run three to six days.

**Sport fishing** in the Beni department's river systems — the Mamoré, Iténez, and their tributaries — is excellent. Golden dorado, peacock bass, and the giant paiche (arapaima) are the main draws. Access is by small plane or multi-day boat.

**Kayaking and rafting** are possible on the rivers draining the Yungas and the eastern Andes. The wet season (November–March) brings the best flows; the dry season is safer and more accessible. Coroico and Rurrenabaque are the main bases.
