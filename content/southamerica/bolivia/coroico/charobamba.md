A pretty village in magnificent forest, with a remarkable history. In the late 1930s, Bolivia was one of the few countries that accepted Jewish refugees from Nazi Germany, on condition they work as farmers. A small community was founded near Coroico, and while most families left after the war, one stayed. Their descendants now run the El Jiri Ecolodge, a beautiful forest hotel and excellent base for hiking. The village's big Administration House and the mule path across the valley still recall those days.

Ironically, Nazi war criminal Klaus Barbie lived for years less than 50 kilometres from Charobamba after fleeing Germany.
