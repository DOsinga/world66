title: "Kori Wayku Inca Trail"

The Kori Wayku river, together with the San Juan, creates the Yolosa river. Its valley is the most attractive in the area — completely forested and wild. The Incas built one of their highways through it, and while parts have been washed away by the river, the surviving stretches are spectacular.

The standard trip is a three-hour walk from Yolosa to a magnificent canyon where the trail is carved into the cliff wall. Leave early so you have time to linger. Along the way you can visit a small artisanal gold mine and look for wildlife. On the return, either retrace your steps or — when water levels are low — follow the river or cross the valley to climb up to Coroico Viejo, from where the descent to Yolosa is stunning.

Beyond the canyon the Inca trail is in perfect condition, but going deeper requires at least a two-day trip.
