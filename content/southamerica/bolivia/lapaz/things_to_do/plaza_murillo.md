The political heart of Bolivia, flanked by the Presidential Palace, the National Congress, and the metropolitan cathedral. The square is named after Pedro Domingo Murillo, a leader of the 1809 independence uprising who was hanged here — a detail the locals haven't forgotten. On most days it's peaceful, populated by vendors and pigeons, though protests can block the surrounding streets without warning.
