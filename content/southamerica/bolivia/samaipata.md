Samaipata is a small mountain town in the foothills of the Andes, about 120 km west of Santa Cruz de la Sierra, at an altitude of around 1,650 metres. Its agreeable climate — cooler and greener than the lowlands — makes it a favourite escape for backpackers and travellers seeking hiking through cloud forest. The pre-Columbian ceremonial site of El Fuerte, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, sits just above town and is the region's main cultural draw. Samaipata is also the base for visiting Amboró National Park, one of Bolivia's most biodiverse protected areas.
