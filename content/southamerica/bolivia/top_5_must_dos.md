---
title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Bolivia"
type: section
---

The list of **Bolivia must do's** is quite long. This country is surrounded by land and is relatively small in area, but it's full of unique sites and interesting activities. Read on for a few of the must do tourist attractions in this South American country.

**1. Lake Titicaca**

This lake, which has the highest elevation of all the traversable lakes in the world, is home to a number of interesting tourist activities, ranging from standard lake events and sights to entirely unique cultural elements. The highlight of the lake for many is witnessing the Uros people, an indigenous tribe that lives on small man-made islands on the lake itself.

**2. La Paz**

La Paz is the highest elevation capital of any country in the world. The city is seated at an average elevation of 12,000 feet or more. This city is full of cultural wonders, from authentic restaurants to the Witch's Market, a bizarre festival of outdoor shops and food stalls.

**3. Madidi National Park**

The Madidi National Park is home to the highest point of the Amazon River basin as well as a dramatically diverse array of plant and animal life. Whether you pass through on your way out of the country to one of the surrounding South American lands, or if you take the time to explore and go on a safari, this area is beautiful beyond compare.

**4. The Andes**

The Andes, which make up one of the highest mountain ranges in the world, cut across the country of Bolivia. They are perfect for skiing and sightseeing as well.

**5. Salar de Uyuni**

Perhaps the most distinctive site in all of Bolivia is the Slar de Uyuni, the world's largest salt flat. It measures over 4,000 square miles, and is home to a unique brand of tourism that includes resorts and hotels made out of salt bricks.

Bolivia is a vibrant and exciting country, but it's also home to a reputation for political instability and violence. This country has struggled to accomodate tourists due to its continuing development. As with any other developing country, Bolivia is perhaps better suited to seasoned travelers, and should be avoided by families with children. It's important that you thoroughly research safe practices for when you travel through this country, and take all necessary precautions to ensure your own safety at all times. Most tourists have found that the people of Bolivia are very welcoming to outsiders and provide a comfortable, if not decadent, stay.
