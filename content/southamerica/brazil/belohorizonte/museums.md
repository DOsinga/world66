---
title: "Museums in Belo horizonte"
type: section
---

Belo Horizonte, literally meaning "Beautiful Horizon" is a very
spacious, beautiful city in the heart of Brazil. The capitol of the
up-scale state of Minas Gerais, Belo Horizonte has one of the highest
standards of living in the country. Despite the fact the city is very
new, having been planned and built towards the end of the 19th century,
it has a number of great museums documenting the history and culture of
the city, the state of Minas Gerais and Brazil itself.

**Museo Historico Abilio Barreto**

The Museo Historico Abilio Barreto is the museum of choice if you
want to learn about the history of Belo Horizonte. Although fairly
modern, it is quite interesting. The city was planned and built on the
same grid system as Washington, D.C., and a lot of hard work and
expertise went into its planning. The Museum documents this with
photographs, blueprints and more to give you an accurate idea of how
Belo Horizonte came to be.

**Museu De Artes E Oficios**

Located in what used to be the central train station of Belo
Horizonte, the Museum of Arts and Crafts is a great place to visit. It
shows the rich culture of Brazilian art, highlighting particularly the
pre-industrial and traditional work of Brazilian artists. Another
bonus: admission is free on Saturdays.

**Museu De Ciencias Naturais**

For nature lovers, this museum focuses on the natural habitat and
wildlife of the Minas Gerais region, from the forests to the oceans.
Located near the PUC campus, the Museu De Ciencias Naturais is also a
great place to bring kids.
