---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment in Brasilia"
type: section
---

Brasilia's nightlife is divided in spots. You should know where you wanna party and then pick a cab, or else you're gonna spend a lot of money and maybe ending up in the wrong place.

- Brasilia has lots of bars on the streets. Arabian, low-budget, expensive, Moulin Rouge-inspired... People from Brasilia usually go to a bar like 21pm and then go to a party. Don't forget that in Brasilia, bars must close at 1am Sun-Wed and 2am thrusday-sunday.

- people usually go to the parties between 12am-1am, so if you wanna avoid lines, get there earlier, like 11pm.

- you'll find about parties on the Internet or with a friend - if you want a alternative or gay party, you're gonna look up for the producers and their websites. For straight parties, just go to the radio or search in a party agenda site (like deBoa.com or CerradoMix.com.br)

- it's very common here to attend happy hours in the university - usually they're free, with cheap beer and pop or indie rock music. People who attend those events usually are students or friends of students and arrive early - they starts at 4pm and (with exceptions) ends at midnight, 2am. But to find those parties, you'll need to go to the university and talk with someone there.

- there are nightclubs like Hype and Moena that attracts a
more exclusive public. They're more expensive than another clubs but they're very selected too.

- for a +28 public, try the pubs here. Gates (at 403 south), UK Brasil (410 south) and O'Rilley (408 south). Nice music and ambients. At the O'Rilley, you can buy a bottle of Black Label (or Smirnoff vodka) and, if you don't drink it in one night, they keep the bottle for you in their bar. Wednesday At Gates Pub is the male gay night, with 80s music.
