---
title: "Practical Information in Brasilia"
type: section
---

- During the dry season (April/May until October/November), always carry a bottle of water with you. Umidity in Brasilia can reach 7%, and without plenty of water, you would feel a strong headache.

- In the other months, always carry a umbrella. Around 5pm/6pm is very common to start raining a lot. Don't wear your best shoes if you're going to take a walk - many places don't have a sidewalk.

- Brasilia is a city for cars, not pedestrians. If you cannot afford a hotel in the middle of the city (like at Setor Hoteleiro Norte/Sul), be ready to spend a lot with cabs. They're expensive in Brasilia and the bus service isn't good.

- It's kinda safe to walk in Brasilia during the day, but at night, except if you're in a huge group, avoid walking. Usually you don't see much people in the streets, and at night you'll only find life in some spots.

- the area near Rodoviaria, Hospital de Base, Setor Comercial Sul and CONIC is very dangerous at night.
