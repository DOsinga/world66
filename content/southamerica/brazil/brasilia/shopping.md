---
title: "Shopping in Brasilia"
type: section
---

Brasilia has three types of stores/galleries:
- open markets, except they're usually indoors. (like Feira do Guara or BSB Mix). In the first one, you will find a great variety of clothes and accessories, but usually only the stores near the entrances are trustworthy. You'll find lot of pieces from famous brands, like Puma or Nike. Don't buy them, they're fake.

- Streets: not any street: in Brasilia is very common to pick one or two blocks and put all the stores who sells similar products on them. 305/306 south is the fashion street, 408/409 north is the coffee shop/bar street, 104/105 north is the bridal street... If you want a specific product, ask someone where you can find a street with it (or if it has a street).

- Malls: like any city in the world, Brasilia has plenty of malls. At Iguatemi, you'll find more expensive stores (like the only Burberry store in Latin America), Louis Vuitton, Missoni, Zegna and others. Conjunto Nacional is another mall, but with cheaper stores (but if you want to buy any product, you can trust them. All stores are serious and sells original products). Park Shopping is another big mall - there you can find almost anything. From M.A.C. to department stores.
