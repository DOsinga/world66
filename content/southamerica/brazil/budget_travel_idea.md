The long
distances between the main cities of Brazil make it a bit impractical
to travel by land, especially if you're on a tight schedule or don't
have that many days available to visit. Travelers living in
South American countries or the state of Florida should look into
available flights from either Copa Airlines (a division of Continental
Airlines) and LAN Airlines for discounted tickets and last minute
specials. Both companies offer e-mail alert services that you can sign
up for. For traveling within Brazil, it's best to check available
flights from local Brazilian airline companies such as Varig, Gol, Azul and TAM.

While in big cities such as Sao Paulo, Brasilia and Rio de Janeiro, find out through locals about the bus routes. Traveling by bus can save you
a lot of money. Just be careful and avoid wearing flashy jewelry,
carrying large amounts of money or expensive equipment (e.g. bulky DSLR cameras or complex video equipment).

**Hostelling International Hostels

**Having a Hostelling International ID card in Brazil is one of the best investments that you can make, because it allows you to stay at more than 100 different hostels in more than 40 cities. The Hostelling
International hostels in Brazil are in the $12 to $25 price range. Most
of the hostels are open 24 hours and a couple few only from 9 a.m. to 9
p.m. Given the extra influx of travelers to Brazil during the months of
January, February and July, some hostels open only during those months
to meet the extra demand. If planning to stay in a hostel, remember that you can only stay up to a maximum of 15 days at a time. Most HI hostels in Brazil have a self-serve breakfast included in the fee.

**Volunteering**

A great alternative for a
holiday is to book a volunteer tour with a company such as Hand Up
Holidays. Not only do the all-inclusive packages from volunteer tours in
Brazil allow you to make a positive contribution to the people in need, but it allows ou to visit cities from an authentic local point of view. The city of Rio de Janeiro is home to several slums ( favelas , in Brazilian Portuguese) and about 20% of Rio's 6 million residents live there. With a 10-day trip to Rio de Janeiro
through Hands Up Holiday, packages for doubles start at $3,000,
excluding airfare. Out of the 10 days, there are only 4 days of
volunteering, while the rest are spent onsightseeing expeditions and hiking in wetlands, among other great activities. **
