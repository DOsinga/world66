Campina Grande is the second-largest city in Paraíba state, sitting on a plateau in the dry interior of northeast Brazil. It is best known for its Festa Junina — the Maior São João do Mundo — held throughout June, which draws over a million visitors with forró dancing, bonfires, fireworks, and quadrilha processions. It is one of the largest folk festivals in Brazil and rivals Salvador's Carnival in sheer energy.

Outside festival season, the city is a university town and commercial hub with a lively student population. The climate is milder than the sweltering coast thanks to the altitude. The Museu do Algodão tells the story of the cotton trade that built the city, and the Açude Velho reservoir in the centre is a pleasant spot for an evening walk. Campina Grande also serves as a gateway to the dramatic rock formations and canyons of the Borborema plateau.
