Cumbuco is a small fishing village 25 kilometres north of [Fortaleza](/southamerica/brazil/fortaleza), and one of the best kitesurfing destinations in [Brazil](/southamerica/brazil). The setting is dramatic: a long sandy beach with blue sea on one side and towering sand dunes on the other, with palm trees, a small town square, and a football pitch in between.

The wind blows reliably year-round, making Cumbuco a magnet for kitesurfers. The best months are August to December, when 10-14m kites are sufficient, but wind is good outside those months too. The main kitezone is in front of the Eco Paradise Hotel, but walk 150 metres in either direction and you will have the beach to yourself. Six kilometres downwind, the Lagoa de Cauipe is a flat freshwater lagoon reached by a 45-minute downwinder. For wave riding, Paracuru is an hour north — flat water followed by a perfect reef break, best for intermediate and advanced kiters.

Beyond kitesurfing, there are buggy tours over the dunes, quad bikes, horse riding, and surfing. The village itself is tiny — about 1,500 residents — and nightlife is limited to cold beers on the town square. For anything more, head to Fortaleza, where the clubs and live music venues are going every night of the week. The water temperature is 20-25°C year-round.
