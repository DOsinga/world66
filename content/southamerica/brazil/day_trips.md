title: "Day Trips in Brazil"
type: section
---

When visiting Brazil, a lot of people don't know exactly where to start their day trips in this beautiful country. There are many cities to visit and great places to check out. One important fact to consider is that distances in Brazil are huge and that driving around the country is only an option for those travelers with a lot of time available for their Brazil trip. Traveling around Brazil by plane is not only affordable by using local airlines but is also a great way to free up more days to enjoy. Here are 2 great day trip ideas in Brazil to get you started: Rio de Janeiro and Belo Horizonte.
****

**Rio de Janeiro**

Flying to Rio de Janeiro is very easy from any place around the world because plenty of international and Brazilian airlines offer direct flights to either Rio de Janeiro or through a connect flight via Sao Paulo. It is always a great idea to apply for the Hostelling International card because it will allow you to enjoy plenty of savings at hostels, restaurants, shopping places and other establishments. Once one arrives in Rio de Janeiro, one will be tempted to check out the world famous beaches of Copacabana and Ipanema . Every Sunday, sections of the main avenue that are right next to the boardwalk at these beaches close down for car traffic and all of Rio de Janeiro comes out to ride bicycles, jog, roller blade , skate and work out. On top of that, there are art exhibitions at the beach and amazing beach sculptures at the beach. Besides the beach scene, there's always the night scene to check out. The neighborhood of Lapla is always a great choice because of the live music performances and great bars. Unlike other places in the world, the bars and clubs in Rio de Janeiro are open until the very early hours of the morning, so go ahead and enjoy.

**Sightseeing around Belo Horizonte**

Belo Horizonte is a great place for fans of the Brazilian fighting style of Capoeira because it is one of the places where it originated. On the weekends there are plenty of capoeira demonstrations on the streets. These demonstrations are called circles (rodas in Brazilian Portuguese) and they are very lively, full of music and daredevil capoeira players. Other great ideas for sport enthusiasts in Belo Horizonte are the Parque do Caraca and the Belo Horizonte Zoo. The Parque do Caraca is great for hiking. There are several hiking paths arranged by difficulty level. Make sure to dress in layers (it gets cold at the top and it is always sunny), carry plenty of water, wear sunblock and pack your swimsuit to chill at the beach at the end of your hike.The Belo Horizonte Zoo is great for walking and jogging during the early hours of the morning. Finally, in Belo Horizonte there is also the Liberty Square (Praca Da Liberdade in Portuguese). This square has several of the most representative images of the city, so make sure to bring your camera.
