Brazil is the largest country in South America and is notorious for its diverse culture, landscape, and tourist attractions including numerous **Brazil Festivals**. From the bustling cities of São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro to the famous beaches of Ipanema and Copacabana, and Manaus in the north where the sprawling Amazon River snakes mysteriously through jungle foliage, Brazil is an attractive destination for a variety of travelers.

Carnival

The country is known for its party mentality, and this is largely attributed to its annual celebration Carnival. It is an event of epic proportions held for nearly a week prior to Lent, 40 days before Easter. The festival is a time of reckless pleasure-seeking, including round-the-clock parties, copious amounts of alcohol consumption, and free condoms given away by the handfuls. Any city in Brazil visited during Carnival will be bustling and raucous; but for the biggest parades, abundant entertainment, and the most glitz and glamour head to Rio de Janeiro. Here you’ll find every corner of the city celebrating in its own way. The dancers, musicians, and performers vary from neighborhood to neighborhood, all distinct yet connected in jubilation.

Lavagem do Bonfim

Beyond Carnival , Brazil is host to a multitude of other festivals that all happen throughout the year. On the second Thursday of January Lavagem do Bonfim , a celebration that began in the 1700s, takes place in Salvador. The festival consists of a 10-km walk from the Church of Conceição da Praia to the Church of Bonfim. Musicians, politicians, performers, and all other groups of people meet at the first church and walk together celebrating along the way with food and drink. When they arrive at the Church of Bonfim perfumed water and flowers are poured out onto its steps. Once the church is “washed” the party commences and all of the people disperse to eat, drink, dance, and be merry.

Boi Bumba

Another famous festival in Brazil occurs every June in the lesser oft-visited state of the Amazonas. The celebration is called Boi Bumba and takes place in Parintins on the Amazon River. It is the largest festival held in the region and lasts for 3 days. The first 2 nights are marked by competitions between singers, dancers, and musicians. On the third night of the festival, a winner is announced.

Brazil touts celebrations of unruly revelry, religious rituals, and cultural traditions. No matter what your style, you’re guaranteed have a good time in the land where festivities abound!
