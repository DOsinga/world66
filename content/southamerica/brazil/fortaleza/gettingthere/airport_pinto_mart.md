Fortaleza has a modern airport, the Pinto Martins; the airport was refurbished recently, to cope with the increase in the number of tourists.

As most foreign tourists come from Europe, there are several charter flights leaving from European cities directly to Fortaleza; given that Fortaleza is one of the Brazilian cities nearest Europe, these direct flights can take as little as seven or eight hours. However, most commercial flights to Fortaleza have a stop over in the Southern cities of Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo; these commercial flights take several hours more than the charter ones.

There are not direct buses between the airport and the urban beaches. It is possible to get from the airport to the beaches by urban buses, but one must change bus in the busy downtown area; if your luggage is light and you want to get in contact with the non-touristic area (during the day, the area is not dangerous), taking the buses is a good option.

To get to the hotel faster, there are plenty of taxis right outside the main gate of the airport; the fare is paid in advance, which avoids cheating by the drivers; in 2006, a ride from the airport to Iracema or Meireles was about R$ 30 (US$ 14), and to Praia do Futuro about R$ 40 (US$ 18).

There is a tourist office inside the airport. They are helpful to give directions, find a hotel and, above all, provide free maps.
