**Using the Public Transportation System in Fortaleza**Fortaleza
is not only one of the most populated cities in Brazil, but also one of
the cities with the highest population density in** **the country . Traffic can be really heavy in Fortaleza, especially during the
morning, lunch hour and evening times. Even though the public
transportation system in Fortaleza (mainly the bus and rail) allows you
to escape the heavy car traffic, there are a couple of things to know
when riding the bus and rail.

When
riding the bus, you should avoid wearing or carrying anything that
draws attention to you. Do not wear flashy jewelry or expensive
watches. During rush hours, the buses can get really crowded and the
Brazilians are okay with little personal space. If you require a lot of personal space, avoid the bus during rush hour. When
riding the rail, be aware that while it's great for traveling long
distances, it's not so good for getting around the city. Also, the
rail does not cover most of the eastern region of Fortaleza.**

Planning Your Trip to Fortaleza**

The
two busiest times of the year for Fortaleza are during the Brazilian
summer time, and the North American and European summer time. Try to book your flight and hotel accommodation
at least a month in advance during those periods. The four days of
Carnival are extremely busy for hotels in Fortaleza and rooms go fast,
so you will need to make your reservation at least 2 months in advance
for those 4 days. Another important
thing to consider when planning your trip to Fortaleza is the weather. During
the months of January through June, Fortaleza is very humid and rainy.
This rainy season is uncomfortable for small children and persons with
high blood pressure or respiratory diseases.

**Traveling on a Budget**

Young travelers to Fortaleza should look into getting a membership card from Hosteling
International (HI). Even though there is not a HI hostel in Fortaleza,
holders of the HI membership card can still enjoy plenty of discounts
at restaurants, bars and shops. Holders of the HI membership card
should look into visiting the nearest HI hostel in the city of Caucaia, about a 4 hour drive away from Fortaleza. Taking a day trip to the city of Caucaia is a good idea because it's full of museums, and the hostel is located right on the historic center of the city.
