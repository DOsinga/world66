Goiânia is the capital of Goiás state in central Brazil, a planned city built in the 1930s with broad avenues and Art Deco architecture. It is a prosperous, modern place — the centre of Brazil's agribusiness heartland — and not an obvious tourist destination, but it has genuine appeal for visitors willing to look beyond the beach-focused Brazil itinerary.

The city is known across Brazil for its food scene, particularly sertanejo cuisine — rice, beans, pequi (a pungent local fruit that divides opinion), and empadão goiano, a rich savoury pie. The Mercado Central is a good place to try the local dishes. Goiânia also has a serious music scene: it is the birthplace of sertanejo (Brazilian country music), and live music bars are everywhere.

The Bosque dos Buritis, a large park in the centre, has good walking paths and the Museu de Arte de Goiânia. The Parque Flamboyant and Parque Vaca Brava are popular gathering spots in the evenings. The historic colonial town of Goiás Velho (Cidade de Goiás), a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is about 130 kilometres west and makes an excellent day trip or overnight.
