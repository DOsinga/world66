### [Iguaçu Falls](/southamerica/brazil/iguacufalls/sights/iguau_falls)

Magnificent rainbows and a roar that can be heard for miles around, the
falls are without a doubt the reason 99% of all visitors come to Foz.
The falls are split into a Brazilian part and and Argentine part.

**The Brazilian side**

Although smaller, the Brazilians have the more spectacular side of the
falls. The entire falls can be seen from this side in a single view. A
number of walkways jettison out from the shores and allow visitors to
practically touch the Garganta do Diablo (Devil's throat).

### [Itaipu dam](/southamerica/brazil/iguacufalls/sights/itaipu_dam)

A joint Brazilian-Paraguayan venture, the Itaipu dam is at present the largest dam in the world. The electricity generated by the dam is enough to power the entire country of Paraguay and the whole of southern Brazil.

The dam doesn't affect the flow of water through the falls, as it sits in another river, but did cause the destruction of the world's largest waterfall. Local weather patterns have also changed due to the massive lake behind the dam.

Tourists can take a guided tour of the dam for free.
