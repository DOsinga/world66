Ilha do Mel — the Island of Honey — sits off the coast of Paraná state in southern Brazil, where the Paranaguá Bay meets the Atlantic. It is a protected area with no cars allowed, which gives the island a calm, unhurried quality that the more famous beach destinations of Brazil rarely manage. Getting around means walking or cycling the sandy tracks that connect the small settlements.

The island divides roughly into two sections joined by a narrow strip: the northern half, mostly undeveloped and rich in Atlantic Forest, and the southern half where the main beaches and guesthouses are concentrated. Nova Brasília and Encantadas are the two main villages, each with a cluster of simple pousadas, bars, and restaurants serving fresh fish.

Surfing, kayaking, and hiking are the main activities. The Fortaleza de Nossa Senhora dos Prazeres, an 18th-century Portuguese fort on the northeast tip, is the island's main historical site. The easiest access is by ferry from Paranaguá, or from the mainland town of Pontal do Sul — and most visitors come via [Curitiba](/southamerica/brazil/curitiba), around an hour and a half inland.
