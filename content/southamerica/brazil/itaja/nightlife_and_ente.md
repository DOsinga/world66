---
title: "Nightlife and Entertainment"
type: section
---

Itajai is not a major tourist destination, and it certainly doesn’t boast nightlife to rival that of more celebrated locations such as Rio de Janeiro, Salvador and Sao Paulo. The nightlife you will find here is local, and low-key, but if you want to see a non-touristic approach to partying, you will certainly find it here.

**Live music**
Brazilians love their music, and when the beers are cracked open it’s normally only a matter of time before somebody starts tapping out a rhythm on any available surface. More sophisticated live music, from samba to choro and pagode, can be found at bars, restaurants and the scattering of nightclubs to be found in the city, with weekend evenings your best chance of catching a live performance. Note that an ‘artistic cover’ charge of a few reias is likely to be added to your bill. If you arrive at a music-free venue and a performance starts up after your arrival, leave quickly if the music is not to your taste, as you will still be expected to pay. For live music free of charge, one of the best places to head for is Parque de Marejada, a pleasant park with regular live music performances in the evening.

**Happy hour**
Oddly, Braziilians have adopted the English phrase Happy Hour to refer to post-work drinks, and it’s a very popular concept in Itajai. Locals flock to the bars and café bars of the city center for post-work beers, and in reality tend to linger far longer than an hour. Don’t expect any special drinks offers such as you would find in a ‘happy hour’ in the UK; the phrase seems to refer simply to the concept of relaxing and unwinding with a drink.

**Nightclubs**
Many of the nightclubs located on the outskirts of Itajai are little more than strip joints, which of course is fine if that is what you are looking for. If you want to hit the dance floor yourself, rather than paying to watch someone else strut their stuff in the nude, you’ll find a few options in the city. Try Warung Beach Club, at Rua José Medeiros Vieira 1, for lively late night music and dancing from Thursday through to Saturday.
