Lauro Müller is a small city in the coal-mining heartland of southern Santa Catarina. It sits at the foot of the Serra Geral escarpment, which makes it the gateway to one of Brazil's most dramatic mountain roads.

The Serra do Rio do Rastro is the main reason to come here. The road climbs the escarpment in a series of tight switchbacks cut directly into the rock face, threading through dense Atlantic Forest with waterfalls dropping alongside it. It is genuinely spectacular — one of the great mountain drives in South America.

Near the base of the serra, the Grotto of the Amaral is accessible via the old road toward Orleans and worth the detour.

Back in town, the Castelo de Laje is a curious landmark: a building modelled on a Swiss castle, constructed between 1917 and 1919 by Henrique Laje, an industrialist who controlled salt mines, transport operations, and coal deposits across southern Santa Catarina. The main residence has a circular tower with a viewing platform, a large terrace, a bandstand, and terraced gardens. It is listed as a historic monument and the grounds now serve as a leisure and events venue.

Lauro Müller also claims the first coal mine opened in Brazil, started by the British in 1842. The Geological Museum of the Serra do Rio do Rastro has rock samples and information about the regional geology and mining history.

The city marks several festivals through the year: the Festa da Padroeira in August, the Festa do Colono in October, the Festa de Santa Bárbara (patron saint of miners, celebrated for over 65 years in the Vila Adobe Branco district) in December, and the Festa de Nosso Senhor do Bonfim at New Year.

Restaurants in town tend toward Italian food, reflecting the area's immigrant heritage. There are options for rural tourism and various accommodation choices in and around the city.
