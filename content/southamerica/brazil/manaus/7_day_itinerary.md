---
title: "7 Day Itinerary in Manaus"
type: section
---

Having a game-plan when you're vacationing is always a good idea and if you're traveling to Brazil, a **Manus 7 day itinerary** is a great thing to have. An average vacation lasts about a week so here is a list of things to do in Manaus to occupy seven days of adventure, excitement and relaxation.

**Day One - City Tour

**The best way to experience a new destination is to embark on a tour. Manaus offers a variety of tours that allow you to see the very best this city has to offer. Easy and affordable, a city tour is a great way to kick-start your vacation to become better acquainted with the city to determine how you want to spend your trip.
**
Day Two - Shopping

**Home to the Municipal Market, Manaus has shops and other locally owned markets scattered throughout the city. The Municipal Market is a large market providing everything locals and tourists could want from fresh fruits and vegetables to handicrafts and souvenirs.
**
Day Three - The Ecopark

**The Amazon Ecopark is one of the highlights of Manaus. Located a short drive outside Manaus, the Ecopark allows visitors to experience the beauty of the Amazon rain forest without having to embark on a trek into the deep, depths of the forest. A safer alternative, the Ecopark includes activities such as canoeing, jungle trekking, fishing tours and much more.
**
Day Four - Boat Tour

**If you haven't had enough of the rain forest, a boat tour up the Rio Negro and Rio Solimoes ensures that you get your full fill. There are a number of tour companies in Manaus that take tourists through smaller rivers that wind through the Amazon giving you a hands-on, once in a lifetime experience.
**
Day Five - Museums

**Manaus has a rich history and culture and what better place to learn of the history of a country that in a museum? Manaus is home to countless museums including the Museum of Northern Man and the Indian Museum. Visiting all of these locations can easily take up a full day.
**
Day Six - Ponta Negra Beach

**Ponta Negra Beach is one of the most popular and active beaches in Manaus and is favored amongst tourists and locals alike. Activities on this beach often surround the centrally located amphitheater providing entertainment on a regular basis. Spending a day basking in the sun and swimming in the warm, clear water is a perfect break from a fast-paced vacation.
**
Day Seven - A Night Out

**A perfect way to end a trip is by a night out on the town. A trip to the Teatro Amazonas is a great way to experience more of the culture this city has to offer. A great way to top off an evening theatrical performance is by stopping by one of the many nightclubs in Manaus for an evening cocktail.
