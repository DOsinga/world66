---
title: "Bars and Cafes in Manaus"
type: section
---

Manaus is a cosmopolitan city located on the Rio Negro, meaning 'Black River.' It is one of the major cities of Brazil and is well known as a popular eco-tourist destination. Located close to the magnificent Amazon rain forest, it is host for a large number of domestic and foreign tourists who can enjoy swimming, canoeing in lakes, or have adventure walks in the jungle. It has the famous Ponta Negra Beach which is the best place for nightlife and has several luxury hotels nearby which are well-known for their bars, hospitality and local cuisine. This is where people have maximum fun and a popular guide to Manaus bars and cafes is listed below.

**Bar do Armando**

Bar do Armando is situated close to the Teatro Amazonas and due to its lush location, is popular with tourists, students and expats. The ambiance is casual yet elegant and service is prompt. This is a nice place to share a cheap pitcher of beer with pals. It is closed on Sundays.

**Piccolino
Piccolino** serves well known staple, traditional food and beverage items like pizza, pasta and beer. It hosts live performers (from Monday to Friday) and is well known for playing popular Brazilian music. Piccolino is closed on Sundays.

**Laranjinha**

Located on the famous Ponta Negra Beach, Laranjinha boasts of live dance and music shows from Monday to Friday and is one of the most visited tourist spots in town. Their collection of cocktails is highly creative and keeps rotating to accommodate different tastes.

**
Café Brasil**

Café Brasil is located in the lobby of a 5-star luxury hotel named ‘The Tropical Manaus.’ It serves snacks along with seasonal fruit juices, ice-creams, fast food and beverages. It is open 24 hours and offers efficient and personalized service.

**A Tu Gosto**

A Tu Gosto serves delicious salads, pastas, meat and fish dishes. The food is eclectic and there are enough dishes to suit most homesick taste buds. It is well known for preparing salads and pasta dishes using natural and fresh ingredients.

**Pizaa Marzano Paterna**

Pizaa Marzano Paterna is easily one of the best cafes in the business since 2001 when it opened amidst much fanfare. It has a reputation for serving superb Italian food, particularly the thin crust Italian pizzas with fresh salads. The pizza is as natural as you can get with mozzarella cheese, olive oil and the tomatoes for the pizza sauce being specially imported from Italy. All the dishes are moderately priced and are worth every penny spent here.

**Chez Charufe Grill**

Chez Charufe Grill is close to the Tropical Hotel and serves great local fish entrees and barbecue. It offers fruit juices, beer and soft drinks. The menu list is printed in several well-known languages like English, French and German. It has a nice, cozy ambiance, kitsch decor, good service and occasional live music. All major credit cards are accepted and this is one of the moderately priced, value-for-money bars in town.
Manaus has several ‘old world’ style bars and contemporary cafes that exude a warm, comfortable charm and make for ideal places to relax with friends and fellow travelers.
