---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Manaus"
type: section
---

Being in the Amazon, known for its lush forests and biodiversity, Manaus offers plenty of activities that are fun for the whole family. The city is full of zoos, parks, nature reserves, and historical monuments and buildings, all of which mix education with fun and travel. Kids are fascinated by the wild beauty and interesting creatures that roam the area, as are adults. Manaus is a great family destination sure to be enjoyed by every member of the family.

**Zoos and Parks**

The Bosque da Ciencia, or forest of science, is a popular tourist destination that is small, but full of entertaining animals and plants. It has many manatees and their calves as well as groups of monkeys and fish and birds living in the lake section of the forest. The Lake Janauari Ecological Park is another area displaying the natural wonders of Brazil. Covering over 9,000 acres, this area is home to many Amazon Victoria-Njmpheaceae water lilies, famous for their huge size and the rapid color changes of their flowers. For a small science lesson, check out the Meeting of the Rivers Monument with your kids. This is where the Negro River and the Solimoes River come together. The Negro River has dark, clear water while the Solimoes River has muddy, brown water, but when they come together, they do not mix but flow side by side. This is because of the temperature difference and the speed difference between the 2 waters. To get a taste of the local culture, you can go to Ponta Negra Beach, where there are few tourists and many cultural festivals. For a real Amazon adventure you can visit the Paricatuba waterfall or the Lion waterfall, your kids will love bathing under the fresh water of these waterfalls.

**Monuments and Markets**

Apart from all of the wonderful areas emphasizing the natural world, there are other man-made wonders to behold. The Teatro Amazonas is very famous and very luxurious. It was constructed in an Italian Renaissance style and has red velvet seats, almost 200 chandeliers, and doors of Italian marble. The Adolpho Lisboa Municipal Market is more of a cultural treat. It is a national monument as well as a popular shopping area that gives you a more intimate look at the daily lives of locals in Manaus. The Palacio Rio Negro, a governmental building formerly owned by a rich German, is also worth a visit.
