title: "Festivals in Manaus"
type: section
---

Brazil is a country renound for its vibrant festivals, and despite being off the beaten track Manaus is no exception. Festivals provide a chance to mingle with locals and learn more about the local culture.

**Boi Bumba**

Boi Bumba is one of the largest festivals held in Manaus and was started after the popularity of the nearby Parintins festival. If you are in Manaus during October, joining in with the celebrations is a must. Boi is a style of dance and music from which the festival derives it’s name. Boi dancing and music celebrates the history of the locals and the festival honors the region’s rich cultural heritage.

**Opera Festival of the Amazon**

The Opera Festival of the Amazon is held annually at the Amazon Opera House, otherwise known as Teatro Amazonas. The building itself is a significant attraction as it is an architectural and historical site. It was built by rubber barons amidst the Amazon jungle and took 15 years to be completed. It was established in 1896 and restored in 1990. The building materials were imported from Europe and the Opera House boasts a magnificent lobby decorated in marble and hardwood. The festival features opera, concerts, dancing and academic programs. It showcases local talent as well as incorporating guest performers. The Opera House provides seats for 700 people. Other cultural sites around Manaus host some of the festivals events. The festival is held between April and May.

**Carnival Manaus**

Experience the famous Brazilian festival among the magnificent surroundings of the Amazon rain forest. If you are looking for a carnival experience with a difference Manaus is the place to be as the festival takes on tropical rhythms. The history of the festival means that the festival precedes Lent as Catholic beliefs require people to give up pleasurable activities during Lent. The festival is a last celebration to indulge in pleasures before giving them up for a time. During Carnival time in Manaus the streets come alive with locals dressed in their finest clothes and costumes. Samba dancing and traditional music are on display during the festival period, which is held during February. The extravagant celebrations feature vibrant colors, parties and delicious traditional food. Celebrations take place at Sambódromo which is a huge stadium. Competitions also take place here. During Carnival accommodations and transport get booked up quickly so it is important to make bookings in advance.
