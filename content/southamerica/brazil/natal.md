Natal is the beach capital of Brazil's Northeast, a city where the coast is the main event. The beaches run for over 9 km from the Forte dos Reis Magos at the river mouth south to the lighthouse, and the sand is some of the best in the country — white, wind-sculpted dunes, warm Atlantic surf, and a string of bars and kiosks that keep things lively well into the evening.

The Forte dos Reis Magos, a star-shaped Portuguese fortification dating to 1598, is the city's landmark sight, sitting at the point where the Potengi River meets the sea. The Museu Câmara Cascudo, named after the city's famous folklorist, covers the archaeology and culture of the region. But most visitors spend more time on the beach than in any museum.

Ponta Negra, about 12 km south of the centre, is the most popular beach neighbourhood, with the best concentration of restaurants, bars, and nightlife. The iconic Morro do Careca — a bare red sand dune — rises at its southern end.
