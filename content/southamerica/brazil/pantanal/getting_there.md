---
title: Getting There
type: section
---

The main gateways are Campo Grande (in Mato Grosso do Sul) and Cuiabá (in Mato Grosso), both served by domestic flights from São Paulo and other Brazilian cities. From Campo Grande, towns like Miranda, Aquidauana and Corumbá are 1–3 hours by bus or car. From Cuiabá, the Transpantaneira road (MT-060) runs south through the northern Pantanal to Porto Jofre — a classic overland route with wildlife visible from the road itself.

By road from Cuiabá you can also reach Cáceres via MT-070, or Barão de Melgaço via BR-364 and MT-361. For a more adventurous approach, river boats run between Cuiabá and Barão de Melgaço along the Cuiabá River, and from Cáceres into the Pantanal via the Paraguay River.
