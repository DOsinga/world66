---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

**April to June** is the sweet spot for wildlife watching. As the floodwaters recede, animals concentrate around shrinking pools and channels, making them exceptionally easy to observe and photograph. Temperatures are pleasant and the landscape is at its most dramatic.

**August to October** is peak fishing season. The migratory "lufada" — when fish return upriver — draws anglers from across Brazil. Wildlife remains abundant and accessible.

**December to March** is the wet season: heavy rains cause widespread flooding that can cut roads and make many lodges inaccessible. Some river-based lodges remain open and offer a different perspective on the flooded plain, but access is limited.

**July** is the driest month and increasingly popular, though it can get cold at night — bring a jacket.
