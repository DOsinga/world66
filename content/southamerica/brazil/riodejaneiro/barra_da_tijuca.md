Barra da Tijuca has the longest beach in Rio -- 18 kilometres of wide, open sand with bigger waves than the Zona Sul beaches. The neighbourhood is modern and sprawling, with high-rise condominiums and enormous shopping malls. It feels like a different city from the historic centre, but the beach is excellent and far less crowded than Copacabana or Ipanema. Popular with surfers and families looking for more space.
