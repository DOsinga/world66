title: 'Beaches'
type: section
---

Rio's beaches are not just places to swim -- they are the city's living rooms, gyms, social clubs and stages. Each beach and each *posto* (lifeguard station) along it draws a different crowd: families, surfers, footballers, bodybuilders, or the gay community all have their regular stretches of sand.

[Copacabana](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/beaches/copacabana) is the most famous, a four-kilometre arc of sand lined with the distinctive Portuguese wave-pattern pavement. [Ipanema](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/beaches/ipanema) next door is slightly more upscale and arguably more beautiful, with the twin peaks of Dois Irmaos framing its western end. [Leblon](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/beaches/leblon) continues where Ipanema ends -- quieter, more residential, favoured by families.

Further west, [Barra da Tijuca](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/beaches/barra_da_tijuca) has kilometres of wide open sand and bigger waves. For a wilder experience, head to [Prainha](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/beaches/prainha), a small surfer's beach hemmed in by green hills, or [Joatinga](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/beaches/joatinga_beach), a hidden cove accessible only through a gated residential area.
