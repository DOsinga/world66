---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas"
type: section
---

Rio de Janeiro is a destination that sports an innately split
personality and extends its contrasting character to the visitor’s
budget as well. The beach city offers extravagant merry-making choices
and budget travel options with equal gusto. Here are some smart ideas
to make the most of Rio de Janeiro on a shoe string budget.

**Sightseeing**

Rio has a treasure of relaxed and idyllic attractions that are all
easily accessible and can be squeezed into the budget traveler’s
itinerary. The public beaches are a fantastic getaway and a lazy stroll
or sunbathing session costs absolutely nothing. Visitors can even go
swimming or firefight the heat of the tropical paradise with a tall
glass of fresh juice on a sultry afternoon. A must do for budget
visitors is to take a cable car ride atop the Sugarloaf and witness the
picturesque landscape of Rio including the beaches, forests, dramatic
mountain peaks and the spectacular statue that is listed as one of the
Seven Wonders of the World-Christ the Redeemer. The ride is priced at $
15 for adults and $ 7 for children under 12.

**Eating **

Rio de Janeiro has no dearth of budget eating options and visitors
can enjoy a hearty feast for modest rates in most parts of the city.
There are cheap street-side food carts selling barbecued shrimps and
filling sandwiches. Other nice budget eating joints are the informal
and relaxed snack bars sprinkled over several neighborhoods. They serve
nice, inexpensive sandwiches and generous fresh fruit juice meal combinations. The kilo restaurants are one of the best places to enjoy
a royal buffet that is reasonably priced by the weight of the food. The
portions in most eateries in the city are fairly large and visitors can
expect to get more than their money’s worth in a majority of snack
bars, restaurants and cafes.

**Accommodation**

Rio is brimming with budget, economy and mid-ranged hotels and
hostels that offer clean accommodation and functional amenities at
strikingly low rates, however during the peak carnival season even some
of the cheapest hotels charge exorbitant rates. Hence the best time to
enjoy Rio on a budget is during the off-season when visitors have more
choices at low-priced fares. Some good economy properties worth
considering are Walk on the Beach Hostel, CabanaCopa Hostel, Casa
Carioka Rio Hostel, El Misti Hostel, Che Lagarto Copacabana and a range
of others dotted all over Ipanema, Leblon, Flamengo, Botanico,
Copacabana, Gavea, Jardim and the Western Suburbs.

**Transportation**

The subway metro is a super cost-efficient and convenient mode of
transport along with inexpensive taxis and the air-conditioned public
buses plying along the beach streets that are called ‘frescaos’.

Rio de Janeiro is a paradise for budget traveler’s and offers an
assortment of low-priced eateries, hostels and the public transport system
that lets visitors explore the attractions of Rio without burning a
deep hole in their pockets.
