Once a luxurious cabaret, Casa Rosa was built in the 1920's and served Rio's high society figures. In the the 90's, after decades of slow decline, it was transformed into a culture center, offering workshops and courses during the day and having live concerts and themed parties at night.Review:

Being a bit tired of samba, I asked my friends to show me some other typical brazillian music, they took me to Casa Rosa on a night where they played forro. Forro, I soon learned, is a characteristic northern Brazil rhythm, very folkloric and sensual. You dance it really close to your partner. I found it hard to follow, so I watched more than I danced, but I really enjoyed it! Casa Rosa has a big open air varanda and the place itself is very beautiful and romantic!
