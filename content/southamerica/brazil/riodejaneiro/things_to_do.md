Rio is a city where the outdoors is the main attraction. Beyond the famous beaches, you can hike through the world's largest urban rainforest in the [Tijuca Forest](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/floresta_da_tijuca), ride a cable car up [Sugar Loaf](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/corcovado), take in the panorama from [Christ the Redeemer](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/corcovado), or stroll through the 19th-century [Botanical Garden](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/botanicalgarden). The more adventurous can hang glide off Pedra Bonita and land on the beach at Sao Conrado, or take a sailing trip around Guanabara Bay.

The city also has a strong cultural side. The [Banco do Brasil Cultural Center](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/banco_do_brasil_cu) in Centro hosts excellent exhibitions, and the [Modern Art Museum](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/modernartmuseum) sits on the waterfront at Aterro do Flamengo. For a quieter afternoon, [Parque Lage](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro/things_to_do/parque_lage) offers lush gardens at the foot of Corcovado with a cafe in a beautiful colonial mansion.
