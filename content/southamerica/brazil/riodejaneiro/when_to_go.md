---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Though Rio de Janerio is a destination of fun and sun year round, there are certain months when this buzzing carnival
city sparkles brighter than usual. Here is a guide to the best times to visit the
Brazilian hotspot.

**Wow! Carnival**

Rio’s relentless party spirit is best unleashed in the month of
February during its annual carnival celebrations. Plan your trip around
the livewire festivities when the entire city becomes one huge party
with its bright costumes and celebratory atmosphere. The carnival
usually lasts for four days and visitors can arrive in a city a few
days before the carnival or stay back for a few days after it to enjoy
other attractions, most of which remain closed during the carnival. The
hotels and flights are overbooked during the main party season and it
is best to book at least a year in advance to get a good deal of your
choice.

**Climate**

Climate-wise the best time to see Rio de Janeiro is in the winters
from May-October. This is also the time when the city is relatively
relaxed and free of the tourist bustle that is at its peak during the
carnival season. You can bag some super off-season rates and discounted
package deals from hotels and airlines. The climate is pleasant in
winters with a crisp daytime sea-side air and breezy nights. Taking a
tour of the city in winters is a good option for tourists who want to
experience the unruffled side of Rio and are not enamored by the
razzmatazz of the carnival. Avoid the rainy months of November and
December if you want to do some serious sightseeing and beach hopping.

**New Year’s Eve**

Though this is no competition for the fascinating annual carnival of
Rio, the ambiance of gaiety and a highly carousing spirit is
conspicuous on the beaches of the city on New Year’s Eve. The city is
transformed into a mass of bright-colored tropical beaches, worship
offering candles and flowers and glistening fireworks. The nucleus of
the New Year’s Eve celebration is the Copacabana beach that draws
hordes of locals and tourists for its contagious festival character.

The best time to visit Rio is undoubtedly during the carnival and
the New Year’s Eve along with some pleasant off-season months. Rio will
sweep you off your feet irrespective of the time of the year you come.
