---
title: 'When to Go'
type: section
---

Rio is warm year-round, but the seasons make a difference. **Summer** (December to March) is hot and humid, with temperatures regularly above 35C and afternoon thunderstorms. This is also peak tourist season and includes both New Year's Eve and Carnival -- expect crowds and high prices, but also the city at its most energetic.

**Winter** (May to October) is the best time for comfortable sightseeing. Temperatures hover around 20-25C, rain is less frequent, and hotels are significantly cheaper. The beaches are quieter but still very much in use -- Cariocas do not stop going to the beach just because it is winter.

**November and December** can be very rainy, which makes outdoor activities less enjoyable. If you want to see Carnival, plan at least a year ahead for accommodation and flights. If you would rather avoid the chaos, the months immediately after Carnival (March and April) offer pleasant weather and a more relaxed city.
