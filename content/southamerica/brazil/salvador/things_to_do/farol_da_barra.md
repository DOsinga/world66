A 17th-century Portuguese fort at the tip of the Barra peninsula, where the entrance to the Bay of All Saints meets the Atlantic. The black-and-white striped lighthouse inside it, built in 1839, is the oldest in South America. There is a small nautical museum inside the fort walls, but the real reason to come is the view and the sunset — the whole city turns out here when the sky is good, and the broad promenade in front is lined with drink vendors and musicians. Walk down the seawall afterwards to the bars and restaurants of Porto da Barra beach.
