Santos is Brazil's largest port and one of the busiest in Latin America, but it is also a beach city with a long seafront and a proud footballing heritage — this is where Pelé played for 18 years. The Santos FC museum at the Vila Belmiro stadium is a pilgrimage for football fans.

The old coffee exchange building, the Bolsa do Café, is the most impressive building in town — an ornate early 20th-century hall with stained glass and painted ceilings that reflects the days when Santos was the coffee capital of the world. The historic centre has been partly restored, and the Valongo neighbourhood and old tram line are worth exploring. The seafront garden running along the beach is in the Guinness Book as the largest beachfront garden in the world.

Santos is less than an hour from São Paulo by road, making it the city's default beach escape. The beaches are long and urban, backed by high-rise apartments. The food is good — seafood restaurants and traditional Portuguese-Brazilian cooking.
