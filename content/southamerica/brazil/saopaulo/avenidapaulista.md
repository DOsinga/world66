The 2.8 km avenue running east-west across the ridge at the heart of São Paulo, the closest thing the city has to a Champs-Élysées. Originally laid out in 1891 as a boulevard of coffee-baron mansions, it is now the financial artery, lined with skyscrapers and the city's best museums. **MASP** (Museu de Arte de São Paulo) — a building elevated on massive red pillars, with 74 metres of clear span underneath — holds the best Western art collection in Latin America. On Sundays, Paulista is closed to cars and filled with cyclists, joggers, and live music.
