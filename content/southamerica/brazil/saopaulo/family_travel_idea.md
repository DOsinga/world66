---
title: "Family Travel Ideas in Sao Paulo"
type: section
---

Featuring world class service, accommodations and attractions, the largest city in Brazil is a terrific family destination. With a wide
variety of cultural, historical and modern attractions, Sao Paulo is a great place to spend some time together as a family and make
a memorable vacation with your loved ones.

Though the city is
a bustling metropolis that's very different from the scenic attractions that
draw visitors to Rio and Salvador, there are many things to keep you
and your family entertained during your stay. Whether you will be
making a one or two day trip to the area or planning your entire trip
around a stay in Sao Paulo, you will find that there is something for everyone here at this hub for shopping, eating and entertainment. **
**

**Avenida Paulista**

A great place to start is a stroll down the Avenida Paulista where you can stop in on such attractions as the Museu de Arte de Sao Paulo or the Instituto Itau Cultural. There are numerous restaurants and shops interspersed throughout the avenue that make for a good time whether young or old. Catch a movie at Espaco Unibanco
or enjoy a nice family dinner along the avenue. From here it is easy to
reach most spots in town, either by bus or underground transportation.**
**

**Parque Ibirapuera**

This
urban park truly has something for everyone. Rent bikes with your
family and explore the park on wheels, enjoy a game of basketball or
soccer at the courts/fields here, or for the younger set, enjoy the
large playground on site. The planetarium, Japanese Garden and selection
of museums here make this a great family friendly destination that is
perfect for a couple hours or a whole day, depending on the age and
interests of your children. **
**

**Kid-Themed Parks**

There are a
few kid-themed parks in close proximity to the city that depending on
the age of your children are a great addition to your trip to Sao Paulo. Hopi Hari is located in neighboring city Vinhedo
(about a 1 hour trip) and features several roller coasters and a circus
show. This park is suitable for children 5 years and older. Bus service
is provided by the park from the city, or the park can be reached via
rental car. Closer to the city is the Playcenter, the oldest park in Sao
Paulo. Rated for children 3 years and older, this park has numerous
attractions including roller coasters, free falls and other rides. Take
a taxi here, as the streets are not well marked.

Just because
Sao Paulo is a metropolis doesn't mean that there are not ample
opportunities for family travel. Book your trip and begin exploring
your options early so that you and your family have the most memorable
trip possible.
