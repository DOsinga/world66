Teresópolis sits about a hundred kilometres up the mountains from [Rio](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro), at nearly 900 metres above sea level — high enough that the air is cool, the mornings are misty and the city makes a popular weekend escape from the Rio summer. The town itself is unremarkable, a sprawl of mid-century houses and weekend chalets, but the setting is dramatic: jagged granite peaks rise on every side, the most famous being the **Dedo de Deus**, the "Finger of God", which points improbably skyward above the valley.

The real reason to come is the **Serra dos Órgãos National Park**, one of Brazil's oldest, which Teresópolis shares with neighbouring [Petropolis](/southamerica/brazil/petropolis). The park protects a slab of Atlantic Forest with toucans, monkeys and orchids, and its trails range from short walks to the multi-day **Travessia Petrópolis–Teresópolis**, a tough but spectacular trek across the range. Teresópolis is also home to **Granja Comary**, the training centre of the Brazilian national football team, an unlikely pilgrimage site for fans.
