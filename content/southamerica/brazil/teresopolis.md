Teresópolis is a mountain town about 90 kilometers northeast of Rio de Janeiro, sitting at around 870 meters in the Serra do Mar range. It has long served as a cool-weather escape for Cariocas fleeing the coastal humidity, and the temperature difference from Rio can be ten degrees or more. The town takes its name from Empress Teresa Cristina and retains some of the alpine pretensions that Brazilian mountain resorts tend to cultivate, with fondue restaurants and Swiss-style architecture mixed in with the usual Brazilian bustle.

The main draw is the Parque Nacional da Serra dos Órgãos, whose entrance is right at the edge of town. The park gets its name from the dramatic rock spires that line the ridgeline and supposedly resemble organ pipes. The Dedo de Deus, a striking pointed peak visible from the city, is the most famous landmark. Hiking trails range from easy walks through Atlantic Forest to serious mountaineering routes up the major peaks. The traverse from Teresópolis to Petrópolis is one of the classic multi-day hikes in Brazil, covering about 30 kilometers of ridge trail with stunning views.

The town itself is pleasant without being remarkable. The Praça Olímpica area has restaurants and shops, and the surrounding countryside is dotted with small farms, pousadas, and craft breweries that have proliferated in recent years. Teresópolis served as the base camp for the Brazilian national football team during the 2014 World Cup, at the Granja Comary training center. The weekends can get crowded with day-trippers from Rio, so visit midweek if you can.
