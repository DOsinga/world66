Teresópolis sits 900 metres up in the Serra dos Órgãos, the jagged granite range that rises behind [Rio de Janeiro](/southamerica/brazil/rio_de_janeiro). It was founded as a summer retreat for Empress Teresa Cristina, who came up here in the 19th century to escape the heat of the coast, and it still functions as Rio's mountain getaway: cool nights, pine trees, hydrangeas in the gardens, fondue restaurants on the main road. The contrast with the city two hours away is total.

The reason most travellers come is the **Parque Nacional da Serra dos Órgãos**, one of Brazil's oldest national parks. Its rock spires — the slender **Dedo de Deus** ("God's Finger") most famous among them — are visible from town and are some of the best technical climbing in the country. Walkers can do the classic three-day **Travessia Petrópolis–Teresópolis**, a tough crossing of the range that takes you through cloud forest and across exposed ridges to [Petrópolis](/southamerica/brazil/petropolis) on the other side.

Football fans know Teresópolis for **Granja Comary**, the Brazilian national team's training centre, on the edge of town. There is little to do in the centre itself — a market, a few churches, the cool air — but as a base for two or three days of hiking and climbing in the Órgãos, it works well.
