title: "Top 5 Must Do's in Brazil"
type: section
---

Here are the top 5 Must Do's for your trip to Brazil.

**1. The Lappa Neighborhood**
In Rio de Janeiro there is no neighborhood that best exemplifies the carioca (native of Rio de Janeiro in Brazilian Portuguese) than that of Lapa.
This neighborhood has such strong ties with music that it has not only
been featured by Brazilian musicians such as Marcelo D2,
but also in the videos of international singers such as Snoop Dogg.
Built in the 1950s, this neighborhood contains bars, small eateries
and plenty of night clubs. During the day, it's a common get together
place for artists, while at night it becomes a giant block party. The
landmark of this neighborhood is the Arcs of Lapa (also known as the Carioca Aqueduct).
**

2. Ipanema Beach and Copacabana Beach**

These
two beaches need no introduction. Million of visitors from all around
the world visit these Brazilian beaches in search of the "Girl from Ipanema", the girl that inspired the famous song that went around the world. Note: She does live there, and she owns a successful retail chain of clothing. The beach of Ipanema
is adjacent to the beach of Copacabana, but it's easy to differentiate
one from the other by taking a look at the curbs: the Portuguese wave
patterns on the curbs are different. Both beaches have excellent
restaurants, cafes, souvenir shops and tourist information centers.

**3. The Green City of Goiania**
You may hear about the destruction of the Brazilian rain
forest, but very rarely do you hear about Brazil's greenest city
(and second in the world), Goiania. Originally designed to be Brazil's
new capital city, the city of Goiania stands as a world class example
at how a city can have the least amount of harmful impact on the
environment. The outstanding planning of Goiania makes it one of the
most tourist friendly cities in Brazil, because its carefully designed
downtown area is easy to navigate by foot and is equipped with several
great hotels such as the Address Hotel, the Augustus Hotel and the Castelo Plaza Hotel.
**

4. The Metropolis of Sao Paulo**

A visit to Brazil will not be complete without a visit to Sao Paulo, because it's not only the largest Brazilian city, but also the seventh largest city in the world. It's home to the most important stock market in South America. Sao Paulo has several well known landmarks and the one that stands out the most is the Paulista Avenue, housing amazing shopping centers (Center Leste Aricanduva, Iguatemi and Ibirapuera), museums (Museo de Ipiringa, Museum of the Portuguese Language and Pinacoteca do Estado de São Paulo) and restaurants. Being an international city, Sao Paulo has several international neighborhoods such as Mooca (Italian district) and Liberdade (Asian district), but also a financial district (Brooklin) and several historic districts (Mercado Municipal).
**
5.The Carnival in Curitiba**

The Carnival is a well known event
in Brazil, but in Curitiba it is a complete different experience than
what you see in TV. Locals prefer the Curitiba Carnival over the big
ones that occur in Rio de Janeiro, Salvador and Recife. Ditch the
masses and experience and authentic Brazilian Carnival experience in
the city of Curitiba or in its nearby town. The best celebrations are
spread around the small beach towns that are within 30 minutes to
an hour drive from Curitiba. If you prefer to stay in town, then the 3
clubs known to have the best Curitiba Carnival celebrations are Santa
Monica, Parana and Curitibanos. Another plus of these smaller celebrations is that they are less scandalous, which is always a concern for families traveling with small children.
