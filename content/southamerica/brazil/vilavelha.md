Vila Velha is a coastal city in Espírito Santo state, sitting directly across the Penha Channel from Vitória, the state capital. The two cities blend into each other at the waterfront, but Vila Velha has a distinct character: more residential, a bit more relaxed, and home to some of the best beaches in the metropolitan area.

Praia da Costa is the standout — a long, clean stretch of urban beach that draws locals and visitors in roughly equal measure. Itapoã and Itaparica to the south are quieter and popular with families. The beaches here are a genuine reason to stay rather than just crossing to Vitória.

The most striking landmark is the Convento da Penha, a 16th-century Franciscan convent perched on a rocky hill that rises abruptly from the surrounding flatlands. The views over the bay and Vitória's skyline are worth the climb alone. It's one of the oldest pilgrimage sites in Brazil and still draws large crowds during the Festa do Espírito Santo.

Getting to Vitória is easy — buses and the third bridge crossing (Terceira Ponte) connect them constantly. Vila Velha works well as a base if you want beach access alongside Vitória's restaurants and nightlife.
