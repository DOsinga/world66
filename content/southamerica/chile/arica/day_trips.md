The Andes rise steeply east of Arica, and within a few hours you can be in an entirely different world. The altiplano around 4,000 meters offers cold clean air, volcanic peaks, flamingo-dotted lakes, and villages that look much as they did centuries ago.

**Parque Nacional Lauca and Parinacota**

The road east from Arica climbs from sea level to over 4,000 meters in around three hours — acclimatize before you go, or plan to stay a night in the highlands rather than rushing back the same day. Most tour operators offer day trips to Parinacota, a quiet Aymara village on the Bolivian border, with whitewashed houses and a colonial church surrounded by grazing llamas, vicuñas, and alpacas. The centerpiece of the park is Lake Chungará, set against the snow-capped cone of Volcán Parinacota — one of the most striking landscapes in northern Chile. A detour to Salar Surire brings you to natural thermal pools at altitude, a strange and memorable experience in the middle of the high desert.
