---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The Atacama is visitable year-round, but the most comfortable months are October to June (Chile's spring and summer). July and August are the dead of winter in the southern hemisphere, and while daytime temperatures in the lower desert remain mild, the nights turn bitterly cold and the high-altitude sites can be brutal.

In January and February, the altiplano experiences the Bolivian winter (locally called the *invierno boliviano*) — afternoon thunderstorms that can close roads and make some sites inaccessible. The upside is that the lagoons fill up and the flamingo populations peak. Outside those months, expect clear skies almost every day, blazing sun, and freezing nights regardless of the season.
