Viña del Mar is Chile's most popular beach resort, a city of around 350,000 people on the Pacific coast about ninety minutes northwest of [Santiago](/southamerica/chile/santiago). Chileans call it the "Garden City" for its parks, palm-lined avenues, and flower clock (Reloj de Flores), and it has a polished, Mediterranean feel that contrasts with the grittier character of neighbouring [Valparaíso](/southamerica/chile/valparaiso).

The beaches draw the crowds — Reñaca to the north is the liveliest, popular with a young crowd, while Playa de los Artistas and the calmer stretches south of the centre suit families. The water is cold year-round (this is the Humboldt Current) but the summer weather from December to February is warm and dry. The annual Festival de la Canción de Viña del Mar, held in February, is the largest music festival in Latin America.

Beyond the beach, the Quinta Vergara park and its palace are worth a visit, as is the Fonck Museum with its genuine Easter Island moai standing outside. The casino, one of the oldest in South America, still draws visitors. Viña is an easy day trip from Santiago or a good base for exploring the colourful hillside neighbourhoods of Valparaíso next door.
