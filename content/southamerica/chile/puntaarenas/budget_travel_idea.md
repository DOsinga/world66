---
title: "Budget Travel Ideas in Punta Arenas"
type: section
---

**Punta Arenas budget travel** is possible
if you do your homework in advance. Here are 2 tips to consider when
planning your next trip to Punta Arenas on a tight budget.

**Accommodations on a Budget**

Punta Arenas provides a lot of hostel options, such as the Hostal
Patagonico, the Hostal Maria Isabel and the Hospedaje Costanera. To
guarantee the lowest price possible, make sure to book your hostel with
at least a month in advance and by using your Hostelling International
membership card. Application to the Hostelling International network
within Chile is limited to nationals and exchange students with a valid
and current ID, so it is best that you apply for your membership at your
country of origin.

**Shopping on a Budget**

If you are traveling on a budget, but you would like to still be able
to bring back home a couple souvenirs, you have 2 great options: the
open market and the 'Zona Franca'. The open market is located at the
Munoz Gamero square in the heart of Punta Arenas' downtown. There, you
will find a lot of inexpensive souvenirs made by local craftsmen. The
'Zona Franca' is a large outlet mall in the outskirts of the city, so it
is a good idea to split a cab with a couple of fellow shoppers to get
to the Kilometer 3.5 Norte. At this outlet mall, you will find a lot of
items and you will not have to pay the sales tax because it is a duty
free zone (literal translation from 'Zona Franca').
