title: "Family Travel Ideas in Punta Arenas"
type: section
---

Family vacationers can feel relaxed about **Punta Arenas family travel **options.
Just like its vibrant cultural mix, Punta Arenas offers a fascinating
range of recreational activities and short-trips suited for the entire
family. One of the most recommended Punta Arenas family travel idea is
to visit the spectacular, Penguins National Nature Reserve located on
Magdalena Island.

**Reaching Penguins National Nature Reserve**

Trips headed to the national reserve start from Laredo Bay—located
about 25 km to the northern end of Punta Arenas. For reaching Laredo
Bay, visitors can use the shuttle/bus service. Most of these trips start
early in the morning. Families are advised to wear waterproof clothes.
Boats referred to as ‘Zodiacs’ accommodate nearly 20 passengers per trip
and sail towards the strait. Visitors can view Isabel Island and Marta
Island en route to Punta Arenas.

**Exploring Penguins National Nature Reserve**

The adventure starts as soon as visitors reach Santa Marta, where
some penguin colonies periodically migrate. Usually, the boat stops at
Santa Marta to allow visitors to click pictures of the Magellanic
penguins, sunbathing sea lions and cormorants. However, the magnificence
of Magdalena Island is unparalleled. This area is totally uninhabited
and the majestic; Magellanic penguins have overrun everything in sight,
including the steep cliffs. At most times of the year, nearly 100,000
penguins are present on the island!

Visitors can walk around the island to see the nesting, sea birds.
During the breeding season, penguin couples can be seen busily executing
their mating, dancing steps. Visitors can also take a small hike
towards the lighthouse. The lighthouse contains a small library
explaining the opening of the natural reserve and the discovery of the
penguin colonies.
