---
title: "Getting There in Punta Arenas"
type: section
---

Visitors planning a vacation in Mexico shouldn’t have any **Punta Arenas getting there**
apprehensions. Punta Arenas has evolved into a globally-renowned travel
destination, offering plenty of transportation facilities.

**Getting There by Air **

The international airport is located about 20 km to the north of
Punta Arenas—President Carlos Ibanez del Campo International Airport.
The airport regularly receives overseas flights from across the world.
From the airport, connecting taxis to Punta Arenas are easily available.
The traveling time via a taxi is just about 25 minutes. The airport
also receives some charter flights from North America. Some of the
airlines have their own transfer buses that take passengers to the town
center for a token charge.

Air travel options include boarding flights from:
Santiago - Flights to Punta Arenas take about 4 hours. Some of the
flights include a stopover at Puerto Montt, making the trip longer.Puerto Montt - These flights are slightly shorter and the route
is more scenic with river channels and fjords forming the aerial view.Buenos Aires - Direct flights from Buenos Aires are very
limited. The common option is to travel from Buenos Aires to Rio
Gallegos via air—this takes about three hours. From Gallegos, passengers
need to undertake a 5-hour bus-trip to Magellanic Pampa. From here,
connecting buses to Punta Arenas are easily available.

**Getting There by Road**

International road trips can be undertaken only when driving from
Argentina. To access Punta Arenas by road, passing through a long
stretch of Argentine region is necessary. Direct routes leading to the
southern part of Chile aren’t recommended to foreign travelers.

**Getting There by Sea**

Ferry services regularly operate between Punta Arenas and the Tierra
del Fuego in Argentina. Another ferry link exists between Chilean Tierra
del Fuego and Punta Arenas.
