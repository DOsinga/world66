**Glimpses of History at Braun Menedez Museum in Punta Arenas**** **

**Punta Arenas museums** are well-known in the Patagonia region.
The city provides an excellent base for several historical expeditions.
There are many museums in the area. Some of the prominent ones are
Maggiorino Borgatello, Museo del Recuerdo, the Museum of Memory, the
Punta Arenas Naval and the Maritime Museum. Braun Menendez museum is one
of the most popular museums.
**Braun Menendez Regional Museum**** **

Braun Menendez Regional Museum was founded in 1983. It has a wide
collection of objects that describe the chronological growth of
Patagonia. It is situated close to the Main Square on the historical
Magallanes Street. The museum is located in a grand palace which
belonged to the Braun-Menendez family. The palace showcases luxurious
furniture and intricate draperies from the early 20th century. The
palace was officially deemed as a national monument in 1974, and all of
its property now belongs to the government. The museum is widely
regarded as a place of art and culture.
**Practical Information**** **

The museum is open Monday to Sunday from 5 p.m. to 10:30 p.m. On
Sundays and festival days, the museum is open from 10:30 a.m. to 2 p.m.
Admission is 800 Chilean pesos for the adults, and children 8 to 18
years old are admitted at half the cost. Children under the age of 8 are
admitted free of charge. Sundays offer free entry to the museum for
all.
