Puerto Bories is a tiny settlement about three kilometres northwest of Puerto Natales in Chilean Patagonia, notable for its remarkably well-preserved frigorifico — a vast early-twentieth-century meat-processing plant that is now a national monument and museum.

The plant was built around 1913 by the Sociedad Explotadora de Tierra del Fuego, a company that controlled enormous sheep ranches across southern Chile and Argentina. The frigorifico processed mutton, wool, hides, and tallow for export to Britain, with its own coal-fired power station and refrigeration works. The operation was largely British-run, and the surrounding residential quarter — Villa Frigorifico Bories — still has a faintly colonial feel, with white chimneys and picket fences.

Today guided tours take visitors through the industrial complex, the largest and best preserved of its type in Patagonia. The Hotel Cisne de Cuello Negro — named for the black-necked swans that frequent the local waters — is still operating nearby. Puerto Bories is an easy side trip from Puerto Natales, the main gateway to Torres del Paine.
