Over 500 km west of [Valparaiso](/southamerica/chile/valparaiso) Robinson Crusoe island is pretty much
in the middle of nowhere. Although it's a small island, with 500
inhabitants, it has rainforest, volcanic cliffs and dry plains.

The main sight is undoubtedly the Plazoleta del
Yunque.
This is the historic place where the Dresdens sailor who inspired
Daniel Defoe to write Robinson Crusoe lived for 12 years after his ship
was
sunk during the First World War.

San Juan Bautista has a few places to stay - it is a boat ride of about one hour from the airstrip where the planes to [Santiago](/southamerica/chile/santiago)leave.
