Chile runs on its own clock, and understanding local business hours will save you frustration. The midday break is real — many offices and smaller shops close for lunch, and you should plan accordingly.

**Government and private offices** are generally open weekdays from 8:30 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., but most take a two-hour lunch break from noon to 2:00 p.m. Do not expect to get anything done during this window. **Banks** keep shorter hours, typically 9:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. on weekdays, and are closed on weekends. ATMs are widely available in cities, but in smaller towns you may need to plan withdrawals around bank hours.

**Shops** in major cities are open Monday to Friday from 10:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m., and Saturdays from 9:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. Shopping malls keep longer hours, often staying open until 9:00 or 10:00 p.m. and through the weekend. Supermarkets in larger cities are often open seven days a week. On Sundays, expect most smaller shops and businesses outside of malls to be closed.

**Restaurants** typically serve lunch from 1:00 to 3:00 p.m. and dinner from 8:00 p.m. onward — Chileans eat late by North American standards. On public holidays, especially around Fiestas Patrias in September, almost everything shuts down.
