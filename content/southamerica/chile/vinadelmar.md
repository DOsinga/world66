Viña del Mar is Chile's main beach resort, directly adjacent to [Valparaíso](/southamerica/chile/valparaiso) and about 120 km west of Santiago. It is a planned garden city laid out in the 1870s, with a formal grid of wide streets, parks, the casino, and a long stretch of beaches along the Pacific.

Chileans love it; foreign visitors are often puzzled. The water is genuinely cold year-round — the Humboldt Current keeps temperatures in the low teens even in summer — so the beach scene is more about promenading and being seen than swimming. The famous **Festival Internacional de la Canción** (held in February) is Chile's biggest pop music event, staged in a large outdoor amphitheatre on the seafront. The **Botanical Garden** in the hills above the city is pleasant for a walk. The **Castillo Wulff**, a turreted mock-castle on the rocks at the edge of the bay, is the most photogenic spot.

The beaches stretch north through the continuous resort strip of Reñaca and beyond. **Playa de Viña** in front of the casino is the most central; **Playa Renaca** to the north is livelier and more popular with younger crowds.

Getting there: frequent bus services from both Valparaíso (20 minutes) and Santiago (1.5–2 hours). Train from Valparaíso is the most scenic option.
