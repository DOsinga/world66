---
title: "Beaches"
type: section
---

Vina del Mar, Spanish for Vineyard of the Sea, is also known as La
Ciudad Jardin or Garden City. It is in Chile and is most known for its
beautiful beaches. The beaches here are beautiful but watch out because
the water is cold; the Humboldt Current comes up from the ocean and
gives the water a chill.

**Playa Negra**

Playa Negra is a bit far to walk to from Vina del Mar but there is
very good public transportation that will take you there if you don’t
have a car. The waves are moderate and the sand is fine and a dark
color from the volcanic minerals. There aren’t many amenities at this
beach but it is still a nice place to visit.

**Playa Amarilla**

Playa Amarilla is an urban beach that has a nice promenade and
restaurants galore. It is pretty crowded with visitors as well as
locals.

**Playa Cochoa**

Playa Cochoa is a protected by a small peninsula making the waters
safe for swimming. There are a lot of restaurants and shellfish bars
right across the street.

**Playa Renaca**

Playa Renaca has been around since the 60’s and is popular all year
round. There are local craft stalls where you can buy art from locals
and the restaurants, cafes, shops, and discotheques seem endless. The
beach is separated into sectors including a family zone and El
Cementerio, the place where everyone goes to be seen.

**Playa Salinas**

You will stay safe and sound at Playa Salinas as this beach is safe for those with very young children.

**Playa Los Lilenes**

Playa Los Lilenes is a small beach that is family friendly. It is
protected from winds and large waves from the surrounding rocks, but
beware of the undercurrents that can catch swimmers off guard.

**Playa Caleta Abarca**

Playa Caleta Abarca has a cove that gives this beach the reputation
of being a safe beach. There are children’s playgrounds and pretty
gardens along with a discotheque.

**Playa Los Marineros**

Playa Los Marineros has a playground and is the perfect place to do
some sun bathing or fishing but don’t plan on swimming here as the
currents are very strong and dangerous.

**Playa Acapulco**

Playa Acapulco is a long strip beach with gentle waves. There are
beautiful gardens behind the beach that warrant a walk through.

**Playa Mirasol**

Playa Mirasol has a walkway with gardens mixed in and watch out for
roller skaters as the roads are pretty even and smooth. Many street
vendors set up tents in order to sell their hand crafts here.

So you can see you can visit a different beach every day in Vina del
Mar and still not see them all. This makes it a great vacation
destination for beach lovers.
