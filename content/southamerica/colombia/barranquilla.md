Barranquilla is Colombia's fourth-largest city, a sprawling Caribbean port at the mouth of the Magdalena River. It lacks the colonial beauty of Cartagena or the mountain cool of Bogotá, but it has a raw, unpretentious energy and the country's greatest carnival — the Carnaval de Barranquilla, a UNESCO Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity, second only to Rio in scale and every bit as exuberant.

The city's food scene reflects its position at the crossroads of Caribbean, Arab, and Colombian cultures. Street vendors sell arepas de huevo, empanadas, and tropical fruit everywhere, while sit-down restaurants range from fresh seafood to Arab cuisine brought by generations of Lebanese and Palestinian immigrants. Calle 70 is the main nightlife strip, packed with bars and clubs that spill onto the pavement on weekend nights.
