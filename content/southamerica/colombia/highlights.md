---
title: "Highlights"
type: section
---

Colombia is one of South America's most varied countries — Caribbean beaches and colonial cities in the north, cool Andean highlands in the centre, coffee farms and cloud forest in the west, Amazon jungle in the south, and a wild Pacific coast that most travellers never reach. It rewards time and movement.

**[Cartagena](/southamerica/colombia/cartagena)** is the obvious starting point for many visitors. The walled old city is genuinely beautiful, and the Islas del Rosario offshore offer turquoise Caribbean waters within an hour by boat. The city gets crowded and expensive, but it earns its reputation.

**[Bogotá](/southamerica/colombia/bogota)** surprises people. The capital sits at 2,600 metres, which means cool weather and sometimes grey skies, but the cultural life is excellent. La Candelaria is the historic centre, dense with museums, including the world-class Museo del Oro (Gold Museum) and the Botero Museum. The Zona Rosa and Usaquén neighbourhoods have the restaurants and nightlife.

**[Medellín](/southamerica/colombia/medellin)** was once synonymous with Pablo Escobar; it is now a confident, creative city with good transport, a thriving food scene, and a transformation story it's happy to tell. The gondola system connects hilltop comunas to the metro. The Feria de las Flores in August is one of Colombia's best festivals.

**The Coffee Region (Eje Cafetero)** — centred around [Manizales](/southamerica/colombia/manizales) and [Pereira](/southamerica/colombia/pereira) — offers a different pace: fincas, mountain views, and the strange beauty of Cocora Valley's wax palms. This is also the heart of Zona Cafetera, a UNESCO Cultural Landscape.

**[Santa Marta](/southamerica/colombia/santamarta) and the Caribbean coast** give access to Parque Natural Tayrona, where jungle meets sea, and to the four-to-six day trek to Ciudad Perdida, one of the best hikes in South America. [Aracataca](/southamerica/colombia/aracataca), García Márquez's birthplace, is a short trip from Santa Marta.

**[San Agustín](/southamerica/colombia/sanagustin)** and the surrounding archaeological zone contain hundreds of pre-Columbian stone statues and burial mounds, set in dramatic highland landscape. It's a long way from anywhere else, which keeps the crowds away.

**[Popayán](/southamerica/colombia/popayan)**, the "white city," is a colonial town in the south with excellent food — it holds UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy status. Nearby, the active volcano Puracé and the Coconuco hot springs are worth the detour.

**The Amazon at Leticia** is remote and requires a flight, but offers genuine jungle experience: river boats, indigenous communities, and the chance to stand at the point where Colombia, Peru, and Brazil meet.
