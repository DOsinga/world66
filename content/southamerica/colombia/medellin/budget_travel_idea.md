Here are some** Medellin budget travel **ideas**:

L****odging
**

There are several hostels and lower
price hotels in Medellin. Hostel prices start from $18.000 COP ($9
USD) for a bed in a dorm, from $50.000 COP ($25 USD) for a private
room with a shared bathroom, and from $70.000 COP ($35 USD) for a
private room with a bathroom. The hostels here require a reservation,
so make sure to call well in advance of your trip. There are 9 motels
in Medellin. They are lower priced than hotels, from $140.000 COP ($70
USD) per night. Their prices vary with the seasons, so make sure to
reserve early.**

Hiking **

There are several parks in and around
Medellin. A local favorite is El Volador, where you can get a 360-degree view of the city. This national preserve is also home to a
training facility for the army, so you can feel safe relaxing away
from it all.
**
Parque Bolivar **

Another favorite is Parque Bolivar. You can sit and
relax in the shade while watching the world go by. There is a free
concert on Sunday mornings, and on the first Saturday of the month
the park transforms into a craft and antique market. Make sure you
leave before dusk as the area is not too safe after dark.**

Swimming

**Parque de las Aguas is the perfect
place to be a kid at heart. This water park offers several water
slides, a slow river and wave pool. Admission costs $8.000 COP ($4
USD). There are 2 other water parks in town, the Juan Pablo II and
the Los Tamarindos. They offer pretty much the same amenities but are
more crowded and the waiting lines are longer.**

Sports

**If you like sports, Parque Trebol is
the place for you because it offers paintball, a rock climbing wall, an
obstacle route, the X-Fera course (you are inside a huge sphere and
must navigate the course), and the Canopy (slide on a cable at 17 meters
above the ground). Each activity is priced separately but compared to
prices in the United States, they are still very affordable.**

Groceries **

Purchasing food from the grocery store
is still the most economical option. The Euro chain offers the lowest
prices for day to day groceries.
