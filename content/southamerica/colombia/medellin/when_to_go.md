---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Medellin's climate barely changes. It sits at 1,500 metres in a valley near the equator, which gives it an almost constant 20–24°C year round — the "Eternal Spring" nickname is earned, not invented. The drier months are December through February and July–August; the rest of the year brings short afternoon showers.

The standout event is the **Feria de las Flores** in early August: a ten-day flower festival that culminates in the Desfile de Silleteros, a procession of farmers from Santa Elena carrying enormous flower arrangements strapped to their backs. If you come for one thing, come for this.

Other worthwhile dates: **Colombiamoda** fashion week in July, the **International Poetry Festival** in July (one of the largest in the world), the **Jazz festival** in September, and **Alumbrados** — the Christmas lights along the Medellin River, running the whole of December.
