title: "Things to Do in Pereira"
type: section
---

Whether you are visiting Pereira over the weekend or for a week, there are many **Pereira**** things to do** that will keep you enthralled by the culture and beauty in this city. From vast coffee fields to magnificent monuments, the city has a perfect blend of history and nature, and offers tourists a number of wonderful ways to spend their holidays here.

**Coffee Farms**

The city&rsquo;s coffee farms are sone of the most popular tourist attractions, and no tourist who comes to Pereira should leave without visiting them. These large beautiful fields have played a significant role in sustaining the region for several centuries. Visiting these farms will allow tourists to get an idea of the way local people live and will also make them feel like a part of the family. Visitors can also enjoy the view of the surrounding landscapes, which are very beautiful.

**Natural**** Regional Park**** Ucumari**

One of the best places for nature lovers to visit is the Natural Regional Park Ucumari. It is located about 30 km to the south-east of the city. Covering 42 square kilometers, the objective of this park is to preserve the Andean forest, which is within the agricultural area. Visitors to this park can enjoy trekking the beautiful nature trails, admiring sights of waterfalls and searching for more than 180 species of birds.

**Plaza de Bolivar**

The famous naked Bolivar statue located in the city center&rsquo;s main square is not to be missed on any visit to Pereira. The monument initially caused turmoil among the people in Pereira, however, today it is one of the most famous monuments, and the locals are proud of it. Plaza de Bolivar is the main area for the city&rsquo;s most important activities. There are also many restaurants and cafes around this place where tourists can get a taste of local food and drink.

**Viaducto Cesar Gaviri Trujillo**

In addition to El Bolivar Desnudo in the City&rsquo;s main square, this is another proud symbol in Pereira. The Viaducto Cesar Gaviri Trujillo is one of South America&rsquo;s longest cable-stayed bridges. Construction of the bridge was completed in 1998 and is named after Cesar Gaviri, Colombia&rsquo;s 40th president, born in Pereira. The bridge connects Pereira with Dosquebradas, its neighboring city.

**Matecana**** City**** Zoo**

Located near the Matecana Airport, visiting this zoo is another wonderful thing to do, especially if you are holidaying with your family in Pereira. The zoo includes about 800 animals, including mammals, birds and snakes. There are about 150 different species from Africa, Asia and America, and even some endemic animals. There is also a garden that boasts of various kinds of flora that are native and endemic to the area. As the largest zoo in Colombia, the Matecana City Zoo has a lot to offer and is a must-see attraction.
