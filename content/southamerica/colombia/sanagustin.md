San Agustín is a small town in the southern Colombian highlands, set in a green valley at the head of the Río Magdalena gorge. The town itself is quiet and unremarkable, but the surrounding landscape contains one of the most important archaeological sites in South America — a collection of several hundred carved stone statues and burial tombs left by a pre-Columbian civilisation that flourished here roughly from the 1st to the 8th century AD.

The statues are scattered across multiple sites on both sides of the Magdalena gorge, in settings that are often as striking as the carvings themselves — forested hillsides, river crossings, and mountaintop clearings. The **Parque Arqueológico de San Agustín**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the main concentration, with dozens of statues arranged around burial mounds. The **Alto de los Ídolos**, a few kilometres north via the village of Isnos, contains the largest statue, about 7 metres tall. The **Estrecho del Magdalena**, where the river narrows to just 2 metres between rock walls, is a striking natural feature nearby.

The town has a few basic restaurants and is a comfortable base for exploring the sites over two or three days. Horseback excursions to the more remote statue groups are popular and cover rough terrain that would be difficult on foot.
