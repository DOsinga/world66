Providencia is the smaller, quieter, and more beautiful of the two main islands in Colombia's [San Andrés and Providencia](/southamerica/colombia/sanandresandprovidencia) archipelago, lying about 75 kilometres north of San Andrés in the western Caribbean. Where San Andrés has duty-free shopping and package tourism, Providencia has volcanic peaks, empty beaches, and a pace of life that runs on island time.

The island is ringed by one of the most extensive barrier reefs in the Americas, and the snorkelling and diving are exceptional — Crab Cay and the surrounding waters are particularly good. On land, the hike up to El Pico (360 metres) gives panoramic views over the sea of seven colours, so called for the shifting shades of blue and green over the reef.

The local population is largely Raizal, descendants of English-speaking Caribbean settlers, and the culture feels more Jamaican than Colombian. Be prepared for high food prices — nearly everything is imported. Getting here requires a short flight from San Andrés, or a catamaran that takes about three hours.
