Santa Fe de Antioquia is the oldest town in the region. It was founded in 1541 and prospered in the days of Spanish Colonial rule. The town is located some 80 km nort of [Medellin](/southamerica/colombia/medellin) , on the road to Turbo . 



The hsitoric centre is very pretty and a stroll through the old streets gives you the chance to admire the many decorated doorways of the houses, the patios full of flowers and the carved wooden guards. 

Main sights include the Iglesia de Santa Barbara, the Museo del Corral and the Museo de Arte Religioso. 

Six km eats of twon the Peunet de Occidente is an unusual bridge over the Rio Cauca. It was one of the first suspension bridges in the world.
