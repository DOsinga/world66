Colombia's oldest cathedral, begun in 1569, stands on the east side of Plaza de Bolívar. The exterior is a severe stone facade with twin towers; inside, the atmosphere shifts dramatically — carved woodwork, a gilded main altar, and side chapels that each feel like their own small world. The building blends Gothic, Mudejar, and Renaissance elements in a way that reflects the mixed cultural currents of early colonial New Granada.

It remains an active parish, so visit at off-peak hours if you want to look around without interrupting a service.
