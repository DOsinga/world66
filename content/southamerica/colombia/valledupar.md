Valledupar is the birthplace and capital of vallenato, the accordion-driven folk music that is to Colombia's Caribbean coast what tango is to Buenos Aires. Every April the city hosts the Festival de la Leyenda Vallenata, a multi-day competition that draws musicians and crowds from across the country. Even outside the festival, vallenato is everywhere — in the plazas, in the bars, on the bus.

The city sits in a valley between the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta and the Serranía del Perijá, giving it a hot but breezy climate and access to river swimming spots in the foothills. The Río Guatapurí runs through town, and locals gather at the Balneario Hurtado to cool off. Valledupar sees far fewer tourists than Cartagena or Santa Marta, which is part of its appeal — this is Colombian life without the tourist infrastructure.
