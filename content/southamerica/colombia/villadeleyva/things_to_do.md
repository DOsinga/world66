---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Villa de Leyva's main attractions lie in two directions: the colonial town itself, with its house museums and vast cobblestoned plaza, and the pre-Columbian and palaeontological sites scattered across the surrounding countryside. El Fósil, a 120-million-year-old Kronosaurus skeleton still lying in the ground where it was found, is the most dramatic of these — about 6 km west of town. The Convent of El Santo Ecce Homo, built in 1620, is on the same road and contains fossils embedded in its floor stones. El Infiernito, a Muisca astronomical site with stone monoliths used to track the planting seasons, is about 8 km away and best reached by taxi or on foot from El Fósil.
