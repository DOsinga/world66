Multi-day riverboat cruises on the Napo River are one of the better ways to get into Ecuador's primary Amazon rainforest. The Napo winds east from Coca through jungle that thins out considerably if you travel by road, but the river still threads through stretches of intact forest with genuine wildlife. A typical itinerary runs four to eight days, combining guided jungle walks, canoe trips into flooded forest at dusk, visits to Kichwa communities, and birdwatching from the boat's upper deck. Several operators run purpose-built vessels out of Coca; book through agencies in Quito and compare itineraries carefully, as quality varies.
