One of the most remote lodges in the Ecuadorian Amazon, Kapawi sits on the Pastaza River in territory belonging to the Achuar people. Getting there requires a small charter flight from Shell, followed by a canoe ride — there are no roads. That isolation is exactly the point.

The lodge was built using traditional Achuar construction techniques and is entirely solar-powered. The Achuar community manages operations and guides, offering an unusually direct window into indigenous life and forest knowledge. Activities include guided rainforest walks, canoe excursions through flooded forest, and visits to Achuar communities. Birdwatching is exceptional, with parrots, toucans, and hoatzins regularly spotted from the lodge itself.

The surrounding forest is among the most biodiverse in the Amazon basin, with minimal outside contact keeping it largely intact.
