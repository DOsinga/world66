Ambato is the capital of Tungurahua province, sitting at about 2,500 metres in Ecuador's central highlands. It is a busy, commercial city — not immediately obvious as a tourist stop, but useful as a base for exploring the surrounding sierra and as a window into everyday Ecuadorian life away from the gringo trail.

The city is best known for its Fiesta de las Flores y las Frutas, held every February during Carnival. The festival fills the streets with flower-covered floats, parades, and music, and draws visitors from across the country. Outside festival season, Ambato is a market town at heart — the Monday market is one of the largest in the highlands, with produce flowing in from the fertile valleys below.

From here you can reach [Baños](/southamerica/ecuador/banos) in under an hour, making Ambato a practical alternative base if Baños is too crowded or too tourist-oriented for your taste. The Tungurahua volcano looms to the southeast, and the road south toward [Riobamba](/southamerica/ecuador/riobamba) passes through dramatic highland scenery.
