Atacames is a lively beach town on Ecuador's northern Pacific coast, spread along a wide stretch of fine grey sand. This is where Ecuadorians from the highlands come to let loose — a beachside playground of thatched-roof bars, ceviche stalls, and thumping music rather than a resort in any polished sense. Foreign visitors are the exception rather than the norm.

The atmosphere shifts through the day: quiet mornings give way to a buzzing afternoon scene as vendors work the sand, and the evenings bring open-air dancing and cocktails served in coconut shells along the beach strip. On weekends and holidays it gets packed and loud; midweek is mellower. The water is warm year-round and the sunsets are reliable. Atacames is not refined, but it is fun, cheap, and authentically Ecuadorian.
