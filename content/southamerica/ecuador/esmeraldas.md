Esmeraldas province stretches along Ecuador's northern Pacific coast — poor in money, rich in wildlife and nature. The popular beach towns of Atacames, Tonsupa, Sua, and Same serve as Quito's seaside playground and fill up on weekends and holidays. The nightlife in Atacames is loud and lively, though the crowds can be overwhelming.

The real draw lies beyond the party beaches. South of Atacames, hidden stretches of coast like Playa Escondida, Mompiche, and Portete see far fewer visitors — quieter, harder to reach, and ideal for anyone who wants equatorial sun and sand without the crowds.

Inland, the northern part of the province holds some of the Chocó rainforest, one of the most biodiverse regions on earth, with more rainfall than the Amazon itself. Birdwatchers come for species like the Umbrella Bird and the Cock of the Rock. Sea kayaking and river canoe expeditions can take you into remote forest areas that feel genuinely untouched. The Chocó is under constant pressure from logging, and several community-based sustainable tourism projects are working to offer local families an alternative to cutting down trees.
