---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

San Cristóbal shares the Galápagos climate: two seasons, both of them acceptable. The warm, wet season runs from January to May — air temperatures in the mid-to-upper 20s, calm seas, excellent snorkelling, and the best underwater visibility of the year. The cool, dry season runs from June to December — overcast skies, cooler water (dropping to around 21°C in August), and a fresher, windier feel.

The peak visitor season is July–August (northern summer holidays) and December–January (Christmas and New Year). If you can travel in May–June or October–November, you will find fewer tourists and more availability.

For diving, December–January and May–June are the sweet spots — warm enough for comfort, clear enough for visibility, and whale sharks are reliably seen in May and June. For bird nesting and giant tortoise activity, the warm season (January–April) is most eventful.
