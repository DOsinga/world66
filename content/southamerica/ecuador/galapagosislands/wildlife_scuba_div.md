The Galápagos is one of the world's great dive destinations, not for coral but for the sheer density of large marine life. Hammerhead sharks school by the hundreds at Wolf and Darwin islands in the far north — these are liveaboard-only sites and among the top-rated dives on the planet. Closer to the main islands, you can share the water with sea lions, marine iguanas, sea turtles, and manta rays.

Most dive operators run day trips from Puerto Ayora (Santa Cruz) or Puerto Baquerizo Moreno (San Cristóbal). Popular sites include Gordon Rocks for hammerheads, Kicker Rock for Galápagos sharks and turtles, and Cousins Rock for seahorses and fur seals. Water temperatures range from 16°C to 28°C depending on season and depth — a 7mm wetsuit is standard. Currents can be strong, so most sites are rated intermediate to advanced.
