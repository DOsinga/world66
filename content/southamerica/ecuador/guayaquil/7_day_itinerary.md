**Day 1: Malecon 2000 and Las Penas Neighborhood**

Early
morning: Enjoy a heavy breakfast because you'll be doing a lot of walking
today. Pack sunblock lotion, water, and a hat, and wear comfortable
walking shoes.

Morning
to noon: Head to the Malecon 2000, the boardwalk overlooking the Guayas
River. Several historical monuments can be seen along its 2.5 kilometer
length, as well as museums, gardens, fountains, shopping malls,
restaurants, bars, food courts, and the first IMAX theater in South
America.

Noon:
Book a daytime tour up and down the Guayas River at any of the several
embarkations at the boarding docks.

After
lunch: Head to Las Penas neighborhood, located on the northeast corner
of Malecon 2000. This is the artistic centre of the city, where many of
the 400 year old houses have been converted into art galleries with several notable artists have studios in the area.

Night: Relax at your hotel.

**
Day 2: Downtown**

Early morning: Pack sunblock lotion, water, and a hat, and wear comfortable walking shoes.

Morning:
Head to Palacio Municipal (Mayor's House) and enjoy Guayaquil's
architectural development ranging from the neoclassical to the modern.
Visit nearby official buildings.

Afternoon: Visit the local parks filled with statues and monuments. Start with Parque Seminario (better known as* Parque de las Iguanas*)
where you will find yourself surrounded by tons of iguanas. Make sure
to head to the Parque Centenario, where a large Statue of Liberty (hard
to miss!) dominates the central area of this park.

**
Day 3: Relax**

You
had 2 days full of walking around. Treat yourself to some R & R at
your hotel. Ask the front desk for your hotel amenities. In the late
afternoon, visit Mall del Sol for a great shopping and dining
experience. Close to the airport, on Juan Tanca Marengo Avenue and
Joaquin Orrantia.

**Day 4: ****Parque Historico**

Early morning: Pack sunblock lotion, water, and a hat, and wear comfortable walking shoes.

Morning: Ask the front desk at your hotel on the best way to go to
Parque Historico (Historic Park) located on the northern area of
Guayaquil. You’ll find restored century-old buildings in the historical
section of this newly built park, which also has a zoo and nature
reserve.

Afternoon: Rest, because you're going out tonight.

Night: Hit the Zona Rosa nearby Malecon 2000 where you will find a
great mix of bars, pubs, and restaurants. You can also experience
Malecon 2000's pubs and bars that open only at night.**

Day 5: Protected Forest Cerro Blanco and Travel Arrangements**

Morning:
A visit to Guayaquil wouldn't be complete without a trip to the Ruta
del Sol (nickname of Ecuador's chain of beaches along its coast).
Salinas is the most developed beach city in Ecuador and a popular
destination that you can use as your headquarters to visit other nearby
beaches. Ask your front desk for more details because some hotels have
branches in Salinas.

Afternoon:
Pack sunblock lotion, water, a hat, and wear comfortable hiking
shoes. Head to the Protected Forest Cerro Blanco on Kilometer 16 at the
Via a La Costa highway. This 6,078 hectares natural reservation is the
home of near extinct species of primates and parrots. Here you have
various choices of eco-tourism activities ranging from hikes (trails
range from 1 to 4 hours) to birdwatching. Excellent for nature and
photography enthusiasts.

Night: Rest at your hotel.

**Days 6 and 7: Salinas**

Head
to Salinas beach, about 2 hours away from Guayaquil. With an average
year-round temperature of 24 degrees C (80 degrees F) and its location
on the Pacific Ocean,
the beach of Salinas are popular resort destination in Ecuador. Beaches
near Salinas include San Lorenzo, Las Palmeras, Chipipe, Mar Bravo, and
Punta Carnero. Popular tourist activities in Salinas include game
fishing, wake-boarding, water skiing, yachting, sky diving, and laying
on the beach. The city offers a number of restaurants and cafes on its
boardwalk, as well as artisan crafts markets.
