Otavalo is a small Andean town in Ecuador's Imbabura province, two hours north of Quito, and home to one of South America's most famous markets. Every Saturday the Plaza de los Ponchos fills with indigenous Otavaleño vendors selling woven textiles, blankets, hammocks, and jewellery — a tradition that predates the Inca conquest. The quality of the weaving is genuinely good, and prices are reasonable if you are willing to bargain.

The Otavaleño people are one of Ecuador's most prosperous indigenous groups, easily recognised by their dark felt hats, white trousers, and long braided hair. Many are successful traders who have taken their textiles to markets across South America and Europe. The town has a quiet pride about it.

Beyond the market, Otavalo is a base for exploring the lakes and volcanoes of the Imbabura region. Laguna Cuicocha, a crater lake with two islands, is a highlight — the rim walk takes about five hours and offers views of the snow-capped Cotacachi volcano. Lago San Pablo, just south of town, is closer and good for an afternoon walk. The waterfalls at Peguche, a twenty-minute walk from the centre, are a sacred site for the local community.
