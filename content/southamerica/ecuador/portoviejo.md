Portoviejo is the capital of Manabí province on Ecuador's central coast, a hot and busy commercial city that serves as the gateway to the province's beaches. The city itself is not a tourist destination — it was heavily damaged in the 2016 earthquake and has been substantially rebuilt — but it is where you will pass through if you are heading to the coast from the highlands.

Manabí is the heartland of Ecuadorian coastal culture: the food is excellent (try the corviche and the viche fish soup), the Panama hat actually originated here (not in Panama), and the nearby beaches at Crucita and San Jacinto are quieter and cheaper than the better-known spots further south. Portoviejo makes a practical overnight stop on the way to the coast from [Quito](/southamerica/ecuador/quito).
