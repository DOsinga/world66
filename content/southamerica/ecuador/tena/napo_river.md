The Napo is one of the Amazon's major tributaries and the main artery connecting Tena to the deeper jungle. From Tena you can take a canoe east to the village of Misahuallí, a pleasant half-day on a wide, powerful river. Misahuallí itself is a small town with a monkey-filled central square and connections to jungle lodges further downriver. The Napo is also floatable by raft — a calmer, more scenic option than the Jatunyacu for those who want river time without adrenaline.
