---
title: "Getting There"
type: section
---

The Falkland Islands are genuinely remote. Getting here takes effort and planning — and more flexibility than most destinations require.

**By air from South America**

LATAM Airlines operates a weekly service every Saturday between Santiago, Chile (SCL) and Mount Pleasant Airport (MPN), stopping each way in Punta Arenas (PUQ), where Chilean customs are completed. The return flight departs the same day; if you want a longer stay you must book in multiples of a week. A return fare costs around US$500, but flights fill up well in advance — book several months ahead in the main season.

Weather delays between Punta Arenas and the Falklands are common. Build extra time around your travel dates.

**By air from the UK**

The UK Ministry of Defence operates twice-weekly flights from RAF Brize Norton in Oxfordshire, stopping at Ascension Island to refuel. Flights leave the UK on Sundays and Wednesdays; return flights depart the Falklands on Tuesdays and Fridays. Civilian passengers can book seats through the Falkland Islands Government Office in London. The return fare is around £2,200.

**By cruise ship**

Many Antarctic and South Atlantic cruises include a Falklands stop. This is a popular way to visit for a day or two without arranging independent travel, though you'll be limited in how much of the outer islands you can see.

**Arrival**

All flights arrive at Mount Pleasant Airport, about 60 kilometres from Stanley. Bus transfers run directly to Stanley accommodation; book your transfer when you book your accommodation.
