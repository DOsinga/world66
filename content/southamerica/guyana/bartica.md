Bartica sits at the confluence of three great rivers — the Essequibo, the Mazaruni, and the Cuyuni — about 100km southwest of Georgetown. It is the main town of the interior and the traditional jumping-off point for the gold and diamond mining regions further west. As a result it has a rough, frontier quality: busy, noisy, and prosperous in a raw sort of way.

The town itself does not have much in the way of formal sights, but the river scenery is beautiful and the surrounding area has genuine appeal for adventurous travellers. Boat trips along the Essequibo and its islands, visits to the nearby [Baganara Island](/southamerica/guyana/baganara), and access to the interior make it worth a night or two.

Getting to Bartica requires a boat. The usual route is to take a speedboat from Georgetown's Stabroek Market stelling to Parika, then connect to a river vessel heading up the Essequibo. The journey takes several hours. Alternatively, some tours operate direct speedboats from Georgetown.
