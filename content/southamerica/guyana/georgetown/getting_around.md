**Getting Around Guyana**

Located on the northeastern region of South America, the beautiful country of Guyana is blessed with shared borders with Suriname, Venezuela and Brazil. Given its location, Guyana transportation has developed to match the needs of its citizens and its booming tourism industry. Here are some tips to get around Guyana by minibus and taxi.

**Getting Around by Minibus**

The price of a one-way minibus ride around Georgetown ranges from 60 to 1,000 Guyana Dollars (about $0.03 to $5.00 USD) depending on the length of your journey and how many transfers you need to make. It is not a good idea to travel by minibus late at night, especially if you're traveling by yourself.

**Getting Around by Taxi**

This is the most cost and time-efficient way to get around Georgetown. Make sure your driver only starts the meter after knowing your destination and don't feel afraid to be assertive. Often you will encounter taxis which are not equipped with a taxi meter so you will have to negotiate. It is a good idea to buy a city guide before your arrival and become familiar with major landmarks and main avenues. It is easier to give directions using a landmark rather than a specific street address. While you will have to negotiate most fares, the taxi ride from the airport to downtown Georgetown is fixed at about 4,000 Guyana Dollars. When traveling at night, it is better to use a taxi than a minibus and don't be surprised if taxi drivers charge you premium fees.
