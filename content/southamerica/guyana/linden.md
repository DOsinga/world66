Linden is Guyana's second-largest town, about 100km south of Georgetown on the Demerara River. It grew up around bauxite mining in the early 20th century and the industry still defines the place: the skyline is dominated by the old processing infrastructure, and the reddish laterite soil of the surrounding area gives it a distinctive colour.

The bauxite sector has declined and Linden has had its economic struggles, but the town remains lively and is a natural stop on the road south towards Brazil — the Linden-Lethem road, Guyana's main interior highway, begins here. If you are making the overland trip to the Rupununi, you will pass through Linden. A colonial-era guesthouse on the riverbank offers a reasonable overnight stop.

There is not much to hold a visitor for more than a day, but the river setting is pleasant and the town gives a sense of Guyana outside the capital.
