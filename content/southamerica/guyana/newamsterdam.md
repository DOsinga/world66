New Amsterdam is Guyana's second-largest town, sitting on the east bank of the Berbice River near where it meets the Atlantic coast. It was founded by the Dutch in the 18th century and its colonial origins still show in the layout of the streets and in the older buildings along the waterfront.

The town is quieter and more relaxed than Georgetown. A handful of well-preserved wooden colonial houses give it some architectural interest, and the riverside setting is pleasant. New Amsterdam serves mainly as a stopover point on the route east towards the Suriname border at Corriverton/Springlands.

Getting there from Georgetown involves crossing the Berbice River by ferry — the crossing takes about 20 minutes — after a road journey east along the coast. The total trip from Georgetown takes 2–3 hours by minibus.
