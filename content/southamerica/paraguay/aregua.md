Areguá is a small lakeside town on the shore of Lake Ypacaraí, 30km east of Asunción and a popular weekend destination for the capital's residents. It's known for its strawberry cultivation — you'll see roadside stalls selling fruit and jam from July to November — and for a growing community of ceramic artists whose workshops line the hillside streets.

The town has a relaxed colonial character: cobbled streets, colourful low houses, a 19th-century church on a hill with views over the lake. It's the kind of place to spend a few hours rather than days. The lake itself is good for swimming and there are simple restaurants and kiosks along the waterfront.

Areguá is usually visited as part of the Circuito de Oro alongside [Yaguaron](/southamerica/paraguay/yaguaron) and the nearby town of San Bernardino.
