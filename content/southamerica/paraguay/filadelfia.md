Filadelfia is the main town of the Fernheim Mennonite colony in the [Gran Chaco](/southamerica/paraguay/granchaco), 480km northwest of Asunción along the Trans-Chaco Highway. It's a tidy, well-organised settlement of around 15,000 people — a striking contrast to the surrounding wilderness. The descendants of Low German-speaking Mennonites who fled Russia and Canada in the 1920s run a successful agricultural co-operative here, primarily dairy farming and cattle.

The town has a small museum (the Museo Mennonita) that documents the colonisation of the Chaco and is genuinely interesting. A few basic hotels and restaurants cater to travellers; the local dairy products — cheese, yogurt, butter — are excellent and worth stocking up on for the road.

Filadelfia is the main base for wildlife-watching excursions into the Chaco. Local guides offer 4WD trips to water holes and wilderness areas where jaguars, giant anteaters, tapirs, and extraordinary birdlife can be seen, particularly in the dry season (May–September). Without a guide and 4WD, venturing deeper into the Chaco is not advisable.
