---
title: "Health"
type: section
---

Paraguay requires no specific vaccinations for entry, but a **yellow fever vaccination** is recommended for all travellers, particularly those visiting the eastern departments or border areas with Brazil and Argentina. Carry your yellow fever certificate; it may be requested on entry.

**Malaria** is a risk in the eastern departments of Alto Paraná, Canindeyú, and Caaguazú. If you're spending time in rural areas near the Brazilian border or in forested regions, take prophylaxis — consult a travel clinic before departure. The Chaco and Asunción present minimal malaria risk.

**Dengue fever, Zika, and Chikungunya** are present and carried by the Aedes aegypti mosquito. These are active during the day, unlike malaria mosquitoes. Use repellent (DEET-based is most effective), wear light long-sleeved clothing, and sleep in screened or air-conditioned rooms. Risk is highest in the rainy season (October–March).

**Water:** Tap water in Asunción is officially treated but many travellers and locals prefer bottled water. In rural areas, always drink bottled or filtered water.

**Medical facilities** in Asunción are adequate for routine issues. The Hospital de Clínicas and several private clinics handle most situations. Outside the capital, medical care is basic; serious illness or injury may require evacuation to Asunción. Take out comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical evacuation before you travel.

**Pharmacies** are widely available in cities and stocked with most common medications. Many drugs available only by prescription in Western countries can be bought over the counter here — useful in emergencies, but be careful with dosages and quality.
