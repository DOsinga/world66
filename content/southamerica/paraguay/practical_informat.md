---
title: "Practical Information"
type: section
---

**Currency:** The Paraguayan guaraní (PYG, symbol ₲) is the local currency. Notes come in denominations of 2,000, 5,000, 10,000, 20,000, 50,000, and 100,000 guaraníes. Paraguay is largely cash-based once you leave the main cities, so carry enough for rural travel. ATMs are plentiful in Asunción and accept Visa, Maestro, and Cirrus cards; they're much rarer in small towns and non-existent in the Chaco.

**Money exchange:** Banks and official casas de cambio offer the best rates. Avoid airport exchanges — rates are poor. Counterfeit notes circulate; use reputable sources and check your change. US dollars are widely accepted in larger establishments and are easy to exchange. Keep small bills for buses, markets, and rural areas.

**Tipping:** Not expected but appreciated. A few thousand guaraníes for good restaurant service is reasonable; taxi drivers don't typically expect tips.

**Language:** Spanish is spoken everywhere, but Guaraní is widely used in conversation, especially outside Asunción. Street signs and informal communication often mix both. English is spoken in tourist-oriented businesses but is otherwise uncommon. Learning a few words of Guaraní (mborerekó — you're welcome; mba'éichapa — how are you?) will earn you goodwill.

**Safety:** Paraguay is one of the safer countries in South America for travellers. Asunción has improved considerably in recent years, though the centre can be rough after dark. [Ciudad del Este](/southamerica/paraguay/ciudaddeleste) has a reputation for street crime and it's inadvisable to stay overnight near the market area. Use common sense: don't flash expensive equipment, take licensed taxis at night, and keep copies of your documents.

**Communications:** Mobile coverage is good in cities and along major highways; expect nothing in the Chaco interior. SIM cards from Tigo or Personal are cheap and widely available. Wi-Fi is available in most hotels and cafes in Asunción.

**Electricity:** 220V, 50Hz. Plug types are a mix — Type C (two round pins) is most common, with some Type A and B sockets. Bring a universal adaptor.

**Opening hours:** Government offices and most businesses work Monday–Friday, 7am–noon and 3pm–6pm. The midday closure is strictly observed. Banks usually open 8am–2pm. Many shops stay open later, especially in Asunción.
