---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

Paraguay sits in the subtropics and has a hot, humid climate year-round. Summers (December–February) are brutal: temperatures regularly exceed 35°C, humidity is high, and heavy rain is common. It's not an ideal time to be walking around Asunción or trekking in the Chaco.

The best time to visit is **April to September**, Paraguay's autumn and winter. Days are warm and dry (20–28°C), nights are cooler, and mosquitoes are less of a problem. For the Chaco in particular, this is the only practical window — roads become impassable mud during the wet season, and the dry months bring spectacular wildlife-watching as animals gather near water sources.

**May to August** is the sweet spot for most travellers: comfortable weather, the main festival calendar (including the Nandutí Festival in Villarrica in late July), and manageable conditions across the country.

**Spring (September–November)** is pleasant, with warming temperatures and the Festival del Lago Ypacaraí, a popular outdoor event held lakeside near San Bernardino. Wildflowers bloom and the countryside looks its best.

If you're visiting for **Carnival**, head to [Encarnacion](/southamerica/paraguay/encarnacion) in February. The city runs one of the best carnival celebrations in South America — smaller than Rio or Buenos Aires, but genuinely lively and far less commercialised. The heat is part of the deal.

The **Patron Saint's Day of San Blas** (3 February) is Paraguay's national holiday, celebrated with particular energy in [Ciudad del Este](/southamerica/paraguay/ciudaddeleste).
