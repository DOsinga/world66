Yaguarón is a small town 48km southeast of Asunción, an easy stop on the Circuito de Oro. Its main attraction is the **Iglesia San Buenaventura**, an extraordinary 18th-century Franciscan mission church — one of the finest surviving colonial religious buildings in Paraguay. The carved wooden interior is remarkably preserved, with a painted wooden ceiling and a richly decorated altarpiece. The church was built by indigenous Guaraní craftsmen under Franciscan direction and completed around 1775. Entry is free; go during morning hours when the light is best.

The town itself is small and unhurried, with a daily market and a crafts tradition in wooden carving. It's easily combined with [Aregua](/southamerica/paraguay/aregua) and a stop at Lake Ypacaraí on a day trip from the capital.
