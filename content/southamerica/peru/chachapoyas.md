Chachapoyas is a small Andean city of about 30,000 people in the remote Amazonas region of northern Peru, at 2,335 metres above sea level. It is one of those places that serious Peru travellers have been whispering about for years: hard to reach, surrounded by extraordinary pre-Inca ruins, and almost entirely free of the tourist infrastructure that crowds the southern circuit of Cusco, Machu Picchu, and the Sacred Valley. The Chachapoyas civilisation — the "Warriors of the Clouds," as the Incas called them — built some of the most impressive and least-known archaeological sites in the Americas, and the surrounding cloudforest landscape is stunning.

The main attraction is **Kuélap**, a massive walled fortress on a mountain ridge at 3,000 metres, discovered in 1843 and sometimes called the "Machu Picchu of the north." It is, by some measures, a more impressive feat of construction: the walls rise 20 metres high, the complex covers six hectares, and the site contains the remains of more than 400 circular stone houses. A cable car opened in 2017 cut the access time from a multi-hour climb to a 20-minute ride, making Kuélap much more accessible. The **sarcophagi of Karajía** — a row of clay coffins shaped like human figures, perched on a limestone cliff face overlooking a valley — are another highlight, eerie and photogenic. The **Gocta Waterfall**, one of the tallest in the world at 771 metres (in two drops), is a half-day hike from the village of Cocachimba.

Chachapoyas itself is a quiet, pleasant colonial town with a small plaza, a handful of restaurants, and a growing number of guesthouses catering to the trickle of adventurous travellers. The surrounding countryside — cloudforest, deep valleys, waterfalls, and Chachapoyas ruins on almost every ridge — is the real draw. Come in the dry season (May–September), and allow at least three days to see Kuélap, Karajía, and Gocta. Chachapoyas is reached by a long but scenic bus ride from Chiclayo (8–10 hours) or by daily flights from Lima to Jaén, followed by a 4-hour drive.
