Travel Guide

Kuelap is a mountaintop fortress and city constructed by the ancient Chachapoyas culture. On an impressive location and with a mysterious atmosphere, it is often called the [Machu Pichu](/southamerica/peru/machupicchu) of the North. As no one ever calls Machu Pichu the Kuelap of the South, you can imagine that this comparison is somewhat exaggerated. However, having been a living city with approximately 3500 inhabitants for what might have been 1000 years it really is an impressive site. Over a 1000 meters higher than the Utcubamba Valley and [Tingo](/southamerica/peru/chachapoyas/tingo), it implies a 3 to 6 hour walk or a neck breaking and spectacular two hour bus ride.
