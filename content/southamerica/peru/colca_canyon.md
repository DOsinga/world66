The Colca Canyon is one of the deepest canyons in the world — deeper than the Grand Canyon — cutting through the Andes of southern Peru southwest of [Arequipa](/southamerica/peru/arequipa). It is one of Peru's most rewarding destinations: dramatic scenery, traditional highland villages where people still wear intricately embroidered clothing, and one of the best places in the world to observe the Andean condor in flight.

The condor viewpoint at **Cruz del Cóndor** is the main draw. Condors gather here in the mornings, riding the thermal currents rising from the canyon floor — arriving before 8am gives the best chances. The canyon itself offers excellent multi-day trekking, with trails descending to the river and up the far side.

The main bases for visitors are [Chivay](/southamerica/peru/colca_canyon/chivay), at the upper end of the canyon with hot springs nearby, and [Cabanaconde](/southamerica/peru/colca_canyon/cabanaconde), higher and more remote, closer to the condor viewpoint and the start of the main trekking routes. Both are reachable by bus from [Arequipa](/southamerica/peru/arequipa) (about 4-5 hours).
