A small, dusty town at 3,287 metres that serves as the trailhead for hikes down into the deepest part of the canyon. Stock up on water before heading down — there is nothing on the trail.

Coming from Cruz del Condor you can either take a bus or walk to Cabanaconde. If you walk, follow the road until water crosses it — there is a small house on the right and behind a low wall you will see a water channel. Follow the channel along the hillside (through a small tunnel) to a large water basin, then turn right downhill and left into town.

The hike down to Sangalle (also known as the Oasis), a green paradise at the bottom of the canyon, takes about two and a half hours on a steep descent. Three lodges — Eden, Oasis, and Paradiso — offer basic bamboo huts, swimming pools, and set menus. Paradiso has the nicest pool. The climb back up is hard work: three to four hours depending on fitness, though locals reportedly do it in under an hour. Leave early in the morning or late in the afternoon to avoid the midday sun.

Buses leave from the plaza at regular intervals throughout the day, roughly every hour or two in the morning.
