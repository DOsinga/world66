While the rest of the Americas celebrates Halloween on October 31, Peru marks El Día de la Canción Criolla — Criolla Music Day. Designated in 1944 to celebrate Peruvian music and folklore, the holiday is observed most enthusiastically in Lima and other coastal cities.

The celebration centres on musica criolla, the blending of Spanish, African, and indigenous musical traditions that developed along Peru's coast. Marinera — an elegant courtship dance sometimes considered the national dance — is performed in plazas and penas across the country. Peruvian waltz and polka also feature prominently.

Bars and penas fill up on the night of October 31, with live bands playing until the early hours. If you are in Lima, head to Barranco where the celebration spills into the streets. It is a genuinely local holiday — no tourist performances, just Peruvians enjoying their own musical heritage.
