Huancayo is the main city of the Mantaro Valley in Peru's central highlands, sitting at about 3,270 metres. It is a busy commercial centre and market town — people from villages across the valley bring their handicrafts, produce, and livestock here to trade.

The Sunday market along Jirón Huancavelica is the highlight. It stretches for blocks, with stalls selling woven textiles, carved gourds, silverwork, fruit, and vegetables. It is chaotic, colourful, and one of the best markets in Peru. The Wednesday market in nearby San Jerónimo is smaller but excellent for silver filigree jewellery.

The valley around Huancayo is worth exploring. Torre Torre, just outside town, is a cluster of eroded sandstone towers on a hillside with good views. The villages of Cochas Chico and Cochas Grande are known for carved gourd work (mates burilados). The train from Lima to Huancayo, when running, is one of the world's highest railway journeys.
