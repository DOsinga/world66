Huanuco sits in a fertile valley on the eastern slopes of the Andes at around 1,800 meters, giving it a mild, pleasant climate that locals proudly call "the best in the world." The city is the capital of the Huanuco region and serves as a market town for the surrounding agricultural areas, which produce coffee, cacao, and tropical fruit. It is not on most tourist itineraries, which means you get an authentic Peruvian highland city without the crowds.

The main draw for visitors is Kotosh, an archaeological site about five kilometers outside the city that contains the Templo de las Manos Cruzadas -- a temple with crossed-hands reliefs dating back some 4,000 years, making it one of the oldest known structures in the Americas. The site is modest in scale but historically significant. In town, the Plaza de Armas is attractive and lively, and the surrounding streets have a handful of colonial churches worth a look. Huanuco is also a useful staging point for trips into the cloud forest and the Carpish mountains to the east.
