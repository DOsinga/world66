Ica sits in the middle of [Peru's](/southamerica/peru) wine country, and this is where Pisco — the grape brandy behind the Pisco Sour — is produced. Several bodegas can be visited, and a tour of the wineries makes a pleasant half-day excursion.

The Museo Regional has mummies, ceramics, and elongated skulls from the Paracas, Nazca, and Inca cultures, though as usual the best pieces have been shipped off to [Lima](/southamerica/peru/lima).

The real draw is just outside town: the oasis of [Huacachina](/southamerica/peru/huacachina), a lagoon ringed by enormous sand dunes where you can go sandboarding and take dune buggy rides. [Pisco](/southamerica/peru/pisco) is about an hour away by bus, and Lima is a four-hour ride north.
