Lambayeque is a small town 11km north of [Chiclayo](/southamerica/peru/chiclayo), and the reason to come here is its outstanding museums. Most visitors treat it as a half-day trip from Chiclayo — regular minivans make the short journey.

The Museo Tumbas Reales de Sipán (Royal Tombs of Sipán Museum) is the star attraction, housing the spectacular gold, silver, and turquoise treasures excavated from the nearby tomb of the Lord of Sipán, a Moche ruler buried around 250 AD. The collection is one of the finest pre-Columbian archaeological finds in South America. The older Brüning Museum, also in town, has a broader collection of artefacts from the region's various pre-Columbian cultures.
