The Museo Tumbas Reales de Sipán is one of Peru's finest museums, purpose-built to display the treasures excavated from the nearby tomb of the Lord of Sipán — a Moche ruler buried around 250 AD. The gold death mask, turquoise-inlaid ear ornaments, and elaborate ceremonial regalia are extraordinary. The museum building itself is designed to echo the shape of a Moche pyramid.
