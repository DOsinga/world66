---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

For upscale shopping, head to **Miraflores**: **Larcomar**, built into the cliffs above the Pacific at the end of Avenida Larco, is the glossiest mall in the city, with international brands, cinemas, and ocean-view restaurants. The surrounding streets are full of small boutiques. **Av. Petit Thouars** in Miraflores is the main handicraft strip, with half a dozen *mercados artesanales* selling alpaca knitwear, woven textiles, silver jewellery and ceramics at reasonable prices. Every evening an open-air handicrafts market fills the edge of **Parque Kennedy** in central Miraflores.

For cheaper shopping and more local atmosphere, the **Jirón de la Unión** pedestrian street in the historic centre is lined with clothing shops and street vendors; the **Mercado de Surquillo** is the best food market in the city; and **Polvos Azules**, near Avenida Grau, is a vast indoor market where limeños buy everything from electronics to pirated films — fun to browse, but pickpocketing is a real problem, so go without a camera or visible valuables.
