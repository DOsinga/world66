---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

Lima's attractions divide neatly into three categories: the colonial centre, the museums, and the neighbourhoods. The **Centro Histórico**, UNESCO-listed, is best seen on foot in a morning — the Plaza Mayor, the Cathedral, the catacombs of San Francisco, the wooden Moorish balconies along Jirón de la Unión. For museums, the **Museo Larco** in Pueblo Libre holds the best pre-Columbian gold and ceramics collection in the country; the **Museo de la Nación** covers 14,000 years of Peruvian history in one building; and the **Museo de Arte de Lima (MALI)** has a fine 3,000-piece collection of Peruvian art from pre-Columbian times to the present. South of the city, the pre-Inca pilgrimage centre of **Pachacámac** has temples, pyramids, and an excellent site museum — an easy half-day trip.

For the living city, the smart move is to skip "sights" and walk: around **Miraflores** and the clifftop **Malecón**, where paragliders launch from the park edges; through **Barranco**, the bohemian old quarter with its 19th-century mansions and small galleries; and, in the centre, through the Chinese quarter on Calle Capón. The oldest surviving *huaca* in the city, **Huaca Pucllana**, is a pre-Inca adobe pyramid hemmed in on all sides by Miraflores apartment blocks — surreal and worth a visit at night, when it is lit.
