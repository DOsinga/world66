---
title: "Things to Do"
type: section
---

The obvious thing to do at Machu Picchu is to walk the site itself. A typical visit follows a one-way circuit through the upper terraces and the main ceremonial buildings — the Temple of the Three Windows, the Principal Temple, the Intihuatana, the Sacred Rock — and down into the lower residential quarter. Beyond that, there are two worthwhile extras, both of which require separately-booked tickets. **Huayna Picchu**, the sharp peak behind the site, is a steep one-hour climb to a viewpoint and a small set of ruins above the main city. **Machu Picchu Mountain** — on the opposite side — is a higher, less popular, and arguably more impressive alternative with a panoramic view back over the ruins.

Getting to Machu Picchu on foot is part of the experience. The classic **Inca Trail** is a four-day, 43 km trek from kilometre 82 on the railway past a string of Inca ruins, culminating with a dawn arrival at the Sun Gate; permits are strictly limited and must be booked four to six months ahead. Alternatives include the longer and higher **Salkantay trek**, the less-crowded **Lares** and **Choquequirao** routes, and the gentler **Inca Jungle Trek** that combines hiking with cycling and rafting. All of them end at Machu Picchu.
