The Salkantay is the main alternative to the classic Inca Trail, and for many trekkers now the more appealing option — it does not require the limited permits the Inca Trail does, it passes through wilder, higher country, and it avoids most of the crowds. The standard route takes **5 days** from the village of Mollepata (three hours by road from Cusco) over the **Salkantay Pass** at 4,630 metres under the snowy flank of Apu Salkantay, then down through cloud forest and jungle to Santa Teresa (with its hot springs) and on to Aguas Calientes. The final day is spent at Machu Picchu. It can be done independently or — much more commonly — as part of an organised group; agencies in Cusco run trips daily in high season.
