The Nasca Lines in the Ingenio Valley are the only reason why most people visit the town of Nasca. The famous lines are cut into stone and were outlined by persons unknown. The technique was to remove large dark-colored stones and expose the lighter patches just below the surface. The lines depict not only parallels and geometrical pictures, but also depict people, fish birds (from hummers to condors) and other more abstract figures ranging in size from about 300 ft/90 m to several miles/kilometers in length. Because the designs can only be seen clearly from the air, the artists never had the chance to see their completed works.

Getting to see the lines means flying in a small plane seating 4 to 10 persons. Unfortunately the small planes can be rather hard on the stomach, so if you have an inclination to air sickness, eat afterwards or carry your lunch in a bag home.
