The main archaeological museum of Nazca, a short walk from the town centre. Run by the Italian Centre for Pre-Columbian Archaeological Studies, the museum displays over 300 objects recovered from Cahuachi — the ceremonial capital of the Nazca culture — including mummies, polychrome ceramics, woven textiles, and agricultural tools. An aqueduct section in the courtyard demonstrates the Nazca hydraulic system that sustained agriculture in this desert. Open daily.
