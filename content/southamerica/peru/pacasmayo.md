Pacasmayo is a quiet coastal town on Peru's northern Pacific seaboard, roughly halfway between Trujillo and Chiclayo on the Pan-American Highway. It sits in a bay where the wind blows constantly — which makes it one of Peru's best spots for windsurfing and kitesurfing, and the left-hand point break near the lighthouse ("El Faro") draws surfers as well.

The town has an appealing, faded charm. An old pier — built around 1860 when Pacasmayo was an important seaport — stretches into the bay, lit up at night. The seafront is lined with republican-era houses, and sunsets over the Pacific are reliably spectacular. The pace of life is slow, the seafood is fresh, and there is very little tourism infrastructure.

Nearby, the ruins of Pakatnamú — an ancient Moche and Chimú city built in adobe — are worth a visit, as is the Cerro de Dos Cabezas huaca near Jequetepeque, a temple dedicated to the moon. The Jequetepeque valley, with its rice paddies and the Gallito Ciego dam, makes a pleasant excursion on the road up to Cajamarca.
