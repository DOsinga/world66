Paracas is a small coastal town south of [Pisco](/southamerica/peru/pisco), used mainly as a jumping-off point for two of Peru's best natural attractions: the Islas Ballestas and the Reserva Nacional de Paracas.

Most visitors come on a day trip. Boats leave in the morning for the Ballestas Islands, passing the Candelabra — a giant figure etched into a coastal hillside, reminiscent of the Nazca Lines. The islands themselves are home to huge colonies of sea lions, Humboldt penguins, and seabirds. The sea lions seem as curious about the tourists as the tourists are about them.

After the boat trip, tours continue to the Paracas Reserve — a vast desert peninsula stretching along the Pacific, with dramatic cliffs, red-sand beaches, and some of the driest landscape on earth. The combination of desert and ocean wildlife makes for one of the more unusual day trips in Peru.
