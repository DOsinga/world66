The Paracas National Reserve covers a vast desert peninsula along the Pacific coast. The landscape is striking — rust-red cliffs, wind-sculpted sand formations, and empty beaches where the desert meets the sea. The reserve protects both coastal desert and marine habitats, with colonies of flamingos, pelicans, and sea lions along the shore.

Most visitors see the reserve as part of a guided tour from Paracas, which typically includes stops at the main viewpoints and beaches after the morning boat trip to the Ballestas Islands.
