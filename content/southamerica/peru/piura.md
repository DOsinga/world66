Founded in 1532, Piura is older than [Lima](/southamerica/peru/lima), the capital of Peru. Located on the banks of the Piura river, the city is charming, with colonial buildings and new structures blending together around several pleasant parks.

The heart of the centre is the **Plaza de Armas**. The cathedral with its gold-covered altar dominates the square. A few blocks away stands the church of **San Francisco**, historic ground — this is where Peru declared its independence from [Spain](/europe/spain). The independence festival is celebrated enthusiastically here every year. Other notable churches include Las Mercedes and San Sebastian.

There are two museums: the small **Museo Complejo Cultural** and the **Casa Museo Grau**, dedicated to Admiral Grau who fought the Chileans in a major sea battle during the War of the Pacific.

Day trips from Piura include Catacaos, famous for its chicha, and Narihuela, an archaeological site.
