A new restaurant opened in Tarapoto in January 2008 called **La Olla Vieja** (The Old Pot). Co-owner Milagros, a Peru native, has been cooking this food for years, having been one of the original cooks at Cafe de Mundo down the street. The pizza served at **La Olla Vieja** is a cracker-thin crust cooked in a brick oven out in the back yard by the other co-owner Werner, a German immigrant who is honing his horno skills.

Each meal is accompanied by a serving of pan al ajo (garlic bread) that is actually pizza crust with olive oil and garlic. The pasta dishes there are fresh tasting with a regional flare.

One recent addition (February 2008) has been a pasta del dia and pizza del dia, where different pasta dishes and pizzas that are not on the regular menu are featured each day.

La Olla Vieja is a favorite stop for many travelers who have learned that they can order in their native tongue, because staff-members there speak fluent English, French, German, and Spanish.

Pizzas offered at **La Olla Vieja** include Margarita (cheese, basil, tomatoes); La Classica (ham, cheese); La Exotica (ham. pineapple, peach -- it is the jungle after all); La Tres Quesos (mozzarella, Andean cheese, regional cheese); La Tarapotina (cecina [local dried pork] chorizo [local sausage], black olives, sweet peppers, regional cheese) -- a very good choice to get the local flavor of Tarapoto; La del Huerto (*Of the Orchard* -- mozzarella, basil, whatever vegetables are in season roasted over an open fire); La del Mar (*Of the Sea* -- Tuna filet, green olives, black olives, roasted peppers, sliced onion); or the more flavorful La Diabla (*She-Devil Pizza* -- Mozzarella, meat sauce, cabanosi [Italian meat, like a Slim-Jim meat snack in the USA], roasted peppers, sliced onion, and tabasco).

Pastas on the menu at **La Olla Vieja** include Spaghetti Bolognesa (with their own meat sauce); Spaghetti a la Crema (with the house white sauce, ham slices, and parmesan cheese); Spaghetti al ajo (with olive oil and garlic); Lasagna with meat sauce; Lasagna with ham and mushrooms; Lasagna de la Huerta (vegetarian lasagna). In addition to the menu items, La Olla Vieja generally has one or two choices on their Pasta del Dia board that don't hold a permanent position in the menu.

Prices for pizzas range from 8 to 13 soles ($2.65 to $4.35 in US dollars) for a personal size pizza (four slices) and 15 to 24 soles ($5.00 to $8.00) for a family size (8 pieces). Pasta prices range from 8 to 14 soles ($2.65 to $4.70) per serving.
