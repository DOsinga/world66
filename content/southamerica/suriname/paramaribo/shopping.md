---
title: "Shopping"
type: section
---

The Central Market is the most rewarding place to shop in Paramaribo — a chaotic, fragrant covered market near the waterfront where vendors sell tropical produce, spices, dried fish, and traditional goods from all of Suriname's communities. It is also a good place to find local transport to other parts of the city.

For souvenirs and crafts, ReadyTex on Maagdenstraat is the most reliable option — hand-woven palm-fibre baskets, wooden carvings, local paintings, and handmade hats. Jeruzalem Bazaar, about ten minutes on foot from the centre, is a looser affair: hammocks (both the Brazilian and Surinamese styles), mosquito netting, textiles, and printed fabrics at low prices. Haggling is the norm. It is usually closed on Sundays and open late on other days. Maagdenstraat itself is worth a wander for Chinese jewellery shops with a local following.
