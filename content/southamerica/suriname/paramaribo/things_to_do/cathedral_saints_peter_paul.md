One of the largest wooden churches in the Western Hemisphere, this Roman Catholic cathedral was built in 1885 from tropical hardwood — no nails, just mortise and tenon joinery throughout. The interior is cool and cavernous, with high vaulted ceilings and coloured light filtering through stained glass. It anchors the Gravenstraat neighbourhood and is usually open to visitors during daylight hours.
