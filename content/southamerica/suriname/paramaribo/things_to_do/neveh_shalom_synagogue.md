  Next door to the Neveh Shalom Synagogue stands the Keizerstraat Mosque, and the two congregations have been peaceful neighbours for well over a century. The juxtaposition — a working synagogue and a working mosque sharing a street in a small South American capital — is perhaps the most vivid symbol of Suriname's improbable pluralism.
---

One of the oldest synagogues in the Americas, Neveh Shalom dates to the Sephardic Jewish community that settled in Suriname in the 17th century, many of them refugees from the Inquisition. The current building on Keizerstraat was constructed in 1843. It is notable not only for its history but for its remarkable location — directly beside a mosque, the two buildings standing as a symbol of Suriname's tradition of religious coexistence. Tours can sometimes be arranged through the community; enquire locally.
