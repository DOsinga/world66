Raleighvallen is a nature reserve of 78,170 hectares situated along the Coppename River in central Suriname, and forms part of the Central Suriname Nature Reserve UNESCO World Heritage site. It is known as one of the finest birding destinations in the region, with around 400 recorded species including toucans, scarlet macaws, the Guiana cock-of-the-rock, and numerous parrot species. Primates — spider monkeys, howler monkeys, and squirrel monkeys — are frequently spotted.

The reserve is also an important nesting site for the harpy eagle. Isla Brownsberg, within the reserve, offers trails through undisturbed primary forest.

Getting there from [Paramaribo](/southamerica/suriname/paramaribo) requires either a short domestic flight to the airstrip at Raleighvallen (under an hour), or a drive of approximately 120 miles followed by a 3–4 hour boat journey on the Coppename River. Most visits are arranged through STINASU or a tour operator. Facilities are basic but adequate for overnight stays.
