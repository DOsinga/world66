Wia Wia Reserve is a stretch of coastal mangrove swamps and beaches about 100 km east of [Paramaribo](/southamerica/suriname/paramaribo). It has two main draws: exceptional birdwatching and one of the most important leatherback and green turtle nesting beaches on the Atlantic coast.

The mangroves and mudflats attract enormous concentrations of migratory shorebirds, particularly from September to April when birds from North America winter here. Scarlet ibis, roseate spoonbills, and various herons and egrets are resident year-round.

Turtle nesting runs from March through June, with leatherbacks coming ashore at night in the hundreds during peak season. STINASU manages the reserve and organises guided overnight stays during nesting season — this is one of the best ways to see the turtles without disturbing them. Numbers have faced pressure from habitat change and egg poaching over the decades, but conservation efforts continue.

Reaching the reserve typically involves a drive east from Paramaribo followed by a boat into the coastal wetlands. Day trips are possible but an overnight stay is far more rewarding for both birds and turtles.
