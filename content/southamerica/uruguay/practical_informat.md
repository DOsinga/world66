## Visas

Citizens of the EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and most of Latin America do not need a visa to visit Uruguay. Tourist stays of up to 90 days are permitted on arrival. A valid passport is required; many nationalities can also enter with a national identity card. Check with your embassy for current requirements.

## Currency

The local currency is the Uruguayan peso (UYU). US dollars are widely accepted in tourist areas and at many businesses, but you'll generally get better value paying in pesos. Credit and debit cards are accepted almost everywhere in cities and towns — and foreign visitors may receive a VAT refund of several percentage points on card purchases at restaurants, which is worth taking advantage of. ATMs are easy to find in Montevideo and the main resorts; less reliable in small towns, so carry some cash when heading off the beaten track.

## Language

Spanish is the official language. On the Brazilian border, a local creole called *Portuñol* (a blend of Portuguese and Spanish) is spoken. English is understood in tourist areas and at upscale hotels, but learning a few words of Spanish goes a long way.

## Safety

Uruguay is one of the safer countries in South America, but normal urban caution applies in Montevideo, especially in the Old Town (Ciudad Vieja) and on the Rambla at night. Petty theft and bag snatching occur in crowded areas. Violent crime is lower than in neighbouring Brazil and Argentina, but the US State Department advises travellers to remain alert. Outside the capital, Uruguay is very relaxed.

## Health

Tap water is safe to drink in Montevideo and all major towns. Dengue fever is present — use insect repellent, especially in summer. No vaccinations are required for entry, but hepatitis A and B are recommended. Travel insurance with medical coverage is strongly advised.

## Time

Uruguay is UTC–3 year round. There is no daylight saving time.

## Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Plugs are the Uruguayan standard (Type C and Type I). European two-pin plugs often fit; US visitors need an adapter.

- Fire: 104
