Salto is Uruguay's second largest city, sitting on the Uruguay River near the border with [Argentina](/southamerica/argentina) and about 500 km northwest of Montevideo. It is the centre of a major citrus-growing region, and the orange groves that surround the city give the countryside here a distinctly pleasant character.

The city's main draw is the **Salto Grande Dam**, a large hydroelectric project on the Uruguay River about 20 km north of town, jointly operated by Uruguay and Argentina. Tours can be arranged through the tourist office. The **Parque Solari**, northeast of the centre, is a pleasant green space worth a visit.

The area is also known for its hot springs. **Fuente Salto** is 6 km north of town; **Termas del Dayman** is 10 km south — both are popular weekend retreats. Most travellers pass through Salto heading to or from Argentina, crossing the river by ferry to Concordia.
