Caracas has several notable colonial churches worth visiting. The **Caracas Cathedral** on the Plaza Bolivar dates to the 17th century and houses a striking gold altar along with paintings attributed to Rubens. The Bolivar family chapel is here too. A few blocks away, the **Iglesia de San Francisco** retains its ornate colonial altars and is one of the oldest churches in the city, dating to 1575. Both are free to enter and can be visited together in a short walk around the historic centre.
