A mountain town about two hours west of Caracas, founded in 1843 by immigrants from the Black Forest region of Germany. The architecture still reflects those origins — half-timbered buildings, steep roofs, flower boxes — and the cooler altitude makes it feel like a different country entirely. The town is known for its strawberries, sausages, and German-style bread, all produced locally. Weekends draw large crowds from Caracas, so a weekday visit is more pleasant. The road up is winding but scenic, climbing through cloud forest.
