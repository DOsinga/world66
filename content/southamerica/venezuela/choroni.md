Choroni is a small colonial town on Venezuela's Caribbean coast, tucked into a valley at the northern edge of Henri Pittier National Park in Aragua state. Getting here from [Caracas](/southamerica/venezuela/caracas) means driving through the national park itself — one of the most biodiverse areas in the country, where the cloud forest drops steeply down to the sea.

The town is a favourite weekend escape for caraqueños. Colourful colonial houses line quiet streets, and the pace of life is slow. The real action is in neighbouring Puerto Colombia, where Playa Grande offers a long stretch of sand backed by coconut palms. On weekend nights, the main square in Puerto Colombia comes alive with drumming — Afro-Venezuelan tambor sessions that draw crowds and keep going until late.

Choroni is at its best midweek, when the weekend crowds thin out and you have the beaches and the town largely to yourself. The surrounding national park offers good birdwatching and hiking, and the coast in both directions hides smaller, quieter beaches accessible by boat.
