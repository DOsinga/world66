Mérida is Venezuela's great mountain city, sitting in a narrow valley in the Andes at about 1,600 metres above sea level. The climate is mild and spring-like year-round — a welcome contrast to the coastal heat — and the city has a large, lively university population. For many travelers it is the most pleasant and liveable city in the country.

The Mukumbarí cable car (Teleférico de Mérida) is the city's most famous attraction: it ascends in stages to Pico Espejo at 4,765 metres, making it one of the world's highest cable cars. The upper stations offer extraordinary views of the Andes on clear days. The cable car suffered a long closure due to technical problems but has been operational in recent years — confirm current status before visiting.

Mérida is also the base for adventure activities in the Venezuelan Andes: trekking in Sierra Nevada National Park, mountain biking, paragliding, white-water rafting on nearby rivers, and tours to Los Llanos for wildlife watching. Local tour operators are well established and English-speaking guides are available.

The city centre has a pleasant colonial plaza, a cathedral, and a good selection of restaurants and cafes. The famous Los Aleros cultural park, recreating a traditional Andean village, is a short drive away. Mérida is known for its ice cream shops (heladería), which offer hundreds of flavors including unusual local ones like trout and black beans.
