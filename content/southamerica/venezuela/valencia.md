Valencia is Venezuela's second-largest city and a major industrial hub in Carabobo state, about 180km west of Caracas. It is primarily a manufacturing and commercial center and sees few international tourists. The historic city center has a cathedral and a pleasant main plaza, and the Parque Nacional San Esteban on the northern edge of the city offers some accessible hiking. Most travelers pass through Valencia on the way between Caracas and the west.

Lake Valencia (Lago de Valencia), near the city, was once a significant lake but has shrunk dramatically due to water diversion and has serious pollution problems.
