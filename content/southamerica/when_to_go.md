---
title: "When to Go"
type: section
---

The first thing to remember is that seasons are flipped south of the equator. December through February is summer, June through August is winter. This matters most in the southern cone — [Argentina](/southamerica/argentina), [Chile](/southamerica/chile), [Uruguay](/southamerica/uruguay) — and at altitude in the Andes. Near the equator, in [Colombia](/southamerica/colombia), [Ecuador](/southamerica/ecuador), and northern [Brazil](/southamerica/brazil), the distinction is more about wet and dry than hot and cold.

Patagonia has a narrow window. December through February is the only time you'll get reliably tolerable weather for trekking in Torres del Paine or Los Glaciares. Even then, expect wind, rain, and sudden changes. January is peak season and trails get crowded — early December or late February gives you slightly fewer people. Outside those months, many lodges close, trails become impassable, and days are brutally short.

The Amazon basin is best visited during the dry season, roughly June through October, when water levels drop and wildlife concentrates around remaining water sources. You'll see more animals and have an easier time on trails. The wet season (November through May) means flooded forests, which have their own appeal — you can canoe through the jungle canopy — but mosquitoes are fierce and some areas become inaccessible.

The Andes dry season runs from May through September across [Peru](/southamerica/peru), [Bolivia](/southamerica/bolivia), and [Ecuador](/southamerica/ecuador). This is the best time for trekking the Inca Trail, visiting the Uyuni salt flats (especially June-July when the flat is dry and mirror-like after early rains), and exploring highland cities like Cusco and [La Paz](/southamerica/bolivia/lapaz). The wet season brings afternoon downpours, muddy trails, and occasional landslides that close roads. But it's also green and gorgeous, and there are far fewer tourists.

Southern [Brazil](/southamerica/brazil) and [Argentina](/southamerica/argentina) work year-round, but summer (December through March) is best for beaches, Iguazu Falls at full power, and outdoor life in [Buenos Aires](/southamerica/argentina/buenosaires). Argentine wine country around Mendoza is stunning in autumn (March-April) when the vineyards turn gold. Winter brings skiing in the Andes — Bariloche and Valle Nevado have solid seasons from June through September.

The Caribbean coast of [Colombia](/southamerica/colombia) and [Venezuela](/southamerica/venezuela) is driest and most pleasant from December through March. This overlaps with the continent's big festival season: Carnival in [Rio de Janeiro](/southamerica/brazil/riodejaneiro) and Salvador falls in February or March. Barranquilla's Carnival is the same weekend. Inti Raymi, the Inca festival of the sun, takes place in Cusco on June 24 and is worth building a trip around. If you want to avoid crowds entirely, the shoulder months — April and November — often deliver good weather with far fewer travelers.
