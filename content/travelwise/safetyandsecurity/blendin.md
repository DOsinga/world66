The worst mistake most tourists make is to wear inappropriate clothing, talk loudly in a foreign language, and generally advertise their "foreign-ness." 

Thieves know that tourists are here today and gone tomorrow. In most European countries the police can not prosecute successfully without the testimony in the trial of the victim and/or eyewitnesses. Thieves know that the tourist is not likely to stay in town for two or three weeks until the case reaches a courtroom, so the tourist is the victim of choice. 

Especially in Europe, most Americans can blend in with little effort. A few simple rules to avoid looking like a tourist: wear black or brown shoes, not running shoes; wear long pants or dresses, not shorts (except on the beach); wear a shirt with a collar, not a tee-shirt; and especially avoid shirts with large foreign logos; wear any hat except for a baseball cap. Do not, under any circumstances, wear a fanny pack on the front of your stomach -- it makes you look twice as fat and is - to European eyes - a hideous style statement. 

Want to really blend in? Carry or wear a cell phone, even if it does not actually work. It makes you look like a local. Also, buy a local newspaper (almost anything but USA Today or the International Herald Tribune) and carry it obviously under your arm. 

Most of all, as you step on the street, look around and decide -- am I dressed to blend in with most other people on the street? 

This can apply even if you are in a region where you can never truly "blend in." There are many many expatriate western citizans living in big cities all over the world who mannage to avoid much of the harrassment faced by tourists in these same cities. Take your cues from them as well. Act as though you have places to go and people to see-- not as though you're a bottomless money bag.
