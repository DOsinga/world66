Set out a box (or your suitcase) two weeks before departure. When you think of things you will need while on your trip, put them in the box for packing. If there are things that you use up until the trip, keep a pad of paper in the box or by your bedside to jot down any last minutes items. 

I keep a box in my closet that I put old clothing in that I want to take on a trip with the intention of throwing away. Usually for a week trip I try to take at least two sets of undergarments/socks that I am willing to toss out while abroad. This is an excellent way to free up room in a suitcase to bring souvenirs back. 

I also keep a couple super strong Mylar envelopes in my suitcase. I put my address in both the to and from spot. If I ever have any items that I am not allowed to carry on a flight, I can place it in my envelope and it will come to my home, with postage needing to be paid, but I get my item back. 

Photocopy your Passport, Credit Cards, Alien Registration Card, Drivers License, etc. Keep a copy of these in a safe place and if traveling with some else. Have them put a copy with their docs too. 

Always keep a change of clothing, toiletries, medications, snacks, water, headphones, earplugs, pen and any other necessities in your in-flight carry on bag. 

Use a neck pouch for important documents, money, etc. Use it and keep it on at all times. When sleeping at hotels, keep this in a locked suitcase or in your bed. Make sure you have it on every time you leave the room. I also suggest a zippered money belt for a back up of some cash. Keep it on and accounted for at all times. 

Trade some of your clothes with a traveling partner. That way if your bags get lost, you’ll still have some items to wear. 

A few multipurpose things that are handy.

Swiss Army Knife (in checked bag only)

Clothes Pins-To keep Hotel curtains completely shut.

Mini Flashlight

Electrical tape
