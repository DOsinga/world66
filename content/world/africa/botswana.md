Botswana is a very dry country - though surprisingly enough one of the major attractions are the wetlands of the Okavango delta. It has a population of only one and a half million people. Most people live in the south-east. Major cities are the capital [Gaborone](/africa/botswana/gaborone) and [Francistown](/africa/botswana/francistown). However, Botswana offers great wildlife and nature.


 Most travellers come to this country for two reasons: the [Kalahari desert](/africa/botswana/kalaharidesert), which covers about 85% of the country and the [Okavango delta](/africa/botswana/okavangodelta), a huge wetland system of up to 15,000 square kilometers. The Delta is one of the most prolific wildlife areas in Africa; Its diverse and pristine habitats, together with unmatched scenic beauty, make it one of Africa's most important wilderness destinations. A highlight of a visit to this area is the incredible profusion of birdlife - Pel's Fishing Owl, Wattled Cranes, Slaty Egrets, Rufous-Bellied Heron, Swamp Boubous, and a host of eagles, vultures and other raptors will keep the bird lover in heaven.


 The Savuti Channel area, which borders the Delta to the west and [Chobe national park](/africa/botswana/chobenationalpark) to the east, is one of Africa's most famous big game areas. Only 38 kilometers northwest of Savuti and off the main tourist track lies Botswana's best kept secret: Linyanti and the western reaches of the Savuti Channel. The Linyanti and upper Savuti areas rank among the most beautiful in Botswana.


 Chobe National Park has a wonderful concentration of animals but has become very crowded due to the number of camps and lodges in the area.


 
 South of [Maun](/africa/botswana/maun) and the [Okavango delta](/africa/botswana/okavangodelta), lies the huge open expanse of the [Kalahari desert](/africa/botswana/kalaharidesert). In March and April the Kalahari blossoms with life and huge open spaces of the Makgadikgadi pans fill with water, attracting huge migrating herds. 
 
 There is no doubt that every visitor to the natural beauties will not regret his trip.
