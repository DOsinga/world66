Asian food has a good reputation and deserves it. A journey through the countries of Asia is always a culinary experience. From Falafel and Humus in the Middle East, via Nasi Goreng & Peking Duck to Sushi in Japan.

 [Thailand](/asia/southeastasia/thailand) has some great culinary specialities. The combination of spicey and fresh makes the Thai cuisine a treat for travelers, check the [Thailand](/asia/southeastasia/thailand) section for more info.

 Chinese cooking can be found everywhere in the world - but the best place to get it is of course [China](/asia/northeastasia/china) itself. A trip to China will help you distinguish between the kitchen of [Beijing](/asia/northeastasia/china/beijing) (hot), [Guangzhou](/asia/northeastasia/china/guangzhou) a.k.a Cantonese, (very hot) and Sichuan (extremely hot).

 [Japan](/asia/northeastasia/japan) offers sushi (raw fish), tepanyaki (cook prepares food on your table) and many more exquisite things you will not find anywhere else. Have a cup of Sake with it, and you are sure to enjoy yourself. 

 The Indonesian cuisine is great and mostly quite cheap. You can order a rice table with 20 different plates for less than 10 dollar almost anywhere in [Indonesia](/asia/southeastasia/indonesia). Vegetarians will find the Indonesian kitchen quite a treat as well. 

 The Middle East has a great variety of tastes. Humus, Falafel and Kebab are the best known examples of Middle East cooking.
