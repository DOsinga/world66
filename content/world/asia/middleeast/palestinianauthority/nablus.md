Nablus is one of the most interesting cities under Palestinian control in the north. Situated 63km north of [Jerusalem](/asia/middleeast/israel/jerusalem) between the mountains of Gerzim and Ebal, Nablus was founded in 72 AD by the Roman Emperor Titus in honor of his father Flavius Vespasian. It was called Flavia Neapolis, the 'New City', until 636 AD when the Arabs took the town, changing its name to Nablus. That said, it is one and the same with the Biblical Shchem where Jacob's sons avenged Dinah's kidnapping and rape, and where Joseph is buried.

Today, Nablus is considered the major commercial and industrial center in the northern West Bank. Olive oil soap, produced from olive oil and caustic soda, is a Nablus specialty, which has been produced for more than 250 years. During the peaceful Oslo days before Arafat's latest war, Nablus was known as the largest chop shop in the Middle East. Any car stolen in Israel was sure to be butchered here within hours. Nablus is also renowned for its its Kenafa, a tasty oriental pastry. 

The old city centre is explored best in in on foot and with a bulletproof vest.
