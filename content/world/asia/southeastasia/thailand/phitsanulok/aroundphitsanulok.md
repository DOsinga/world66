[Phu Hin Rong Kla National Park

From 1967 to 1982, Phu Hin Rong Kla served as the headquarters of the Communist Party of Thailand (CPT) and was the site of numerous battles between the Thai government and communist insurgents. After the surrender in 1982 of the People's Liberation Army of Thailand (PLAT), the region was declared a national park and opened to the public. The park offers a cool climate, hiking trails, waterfalls, scenic views, and remnants of the communist occupation, such as air-raid shelters and military headquarters. Reservations can be made from the Forestry Department office in Bangkok. Large bungalows begin at 600B per night and camping costs 50B. The park is 125 km from Phitsanulok and can be reached by public bus going to the nearby village of Nakhon Thai. more..](/world/asia/southeastasia/thailand/phitsanulok/aroundphitsanulok/phuhinrongklanationalpark)
