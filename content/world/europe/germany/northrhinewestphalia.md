The state of Northrhine-Wesphalia (in German "Nordrheinwestfalen" or
short NRW) is higly industriated and, as the name shows, the Rhine is
the main river that flows through this region. Ruhr, Wupper, Lippe and
Erms are the other rivers that are situated in Europe's largest
industrial concentration. Almost 18 million people live in this region,
more than 50 per cent of them in one of the big cities like Dusseldorf,
Dortmund, [Cologne](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/cologne) or Duisburg. Altogether NRW has 30 cities with more than 100.000 inhabitants - 20 per cent of all Germans live in this state.

Life pulsates in these cities: concerts, musicals and shows in unusual places. Museums, like the big art museums in [Bonn](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/bonn),
and theaters of all kinds and in great numbers. Shopping that's a real
experience. Here, something is always going on! Europe's most dense and
diverse cultural region lies here between the Rhine and Weser rivers,
with impressive monuments such as the cathedrals of [Aachen](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/aachen) and [Cologne](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/cologne) and hundreds of palaces and castles like [Hombroich](/europe/germany/northrhinewestphalia/hombroich) or Bruhl.

Only a stone's throw away of the pulsing cities you will find
large recreation areas with forests, hills, fluvial plains and low
mountain ranges. Discover the "Bergisches Land" with its famous old
churches, the "Teutoburger Wald" or the "Muensterland." Enjoy a
romantic weekend, winter walks and fine cuisine or a skiing holiday in
the "Sauerland."
