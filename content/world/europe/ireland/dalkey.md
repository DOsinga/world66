---
title: "Dalkey"
type: location
---

Dalkey is a small, somewhat upscale suburb, south and east of Dublin, with a particularly scenic location. It can be reached via a half-hour DART ride from [Dublin](/europe/ireland/dublin)'s Pearse Station, and makes a nice side trip from the city. The center of town is agreeably quaint, with an impressive variety of restaurant offerings as well as the typical pub fare. Helpful maps are near the train station that suggest a pleasant walking tour of the area. This includes several castles: Goat's Castle and Archbold's Castle, dating from the 12th century. Following the winding streets east away from the town center will bring you to a point with a striking vista of a seemingly abandoned island. Dalkey Island has the ruins of 18th century fortresses, and a church that is earlier still. There is a small lookout point near a rock-walled cove for boats, which makes a pleasant spot to sit and enjoy the scenery. Not far away is Sorrento Park, a nearly-hidden park set on a steep but small hill offering views not just of the island but also up the picturesque coastline, and on a clear day, all the way to Wales. All in all, it's hard to conceive of a better day's respite from the pubs.
