Dublin has a rich history and a lot of monuments to show for itself. The prosperity of the British Empire in the 18th century can still been seen in the splendor of some mansions build in that era. Also an important monument in Irish history is the GPO (general post office)on O'Connell Street where Patrick Pearse, James Connolly and Michael Collins acclaimed the Independence of Ireland in 1916. One of the best of Dublin’s churches are St. Patrick’s Cathedral (whose most famous Dean was writer, Jonathan Swift) and Christ Church Cathedral (originally constructed in 1038 but has been rebuilt many times since).
