Here's my advice for the ladies: what you need to remember as a foreign -- especially American -- woman traveling in Florence is that Italian men think you are easy. Sure, if you've seen *Under the Tuscan Sun* you've come into contact with that stereotype of Italian men being sensitive, romantic, easy to fall in love with. They don't mention the fact that a lot of young Italian men believe that if he says "I love you" to a girl she'll have sex with him. As a matter of fact, sexual harassment standards are definitely not as high as they are in the US, as least in terms of basic flirting, so it isn't surprising if they react visibly to an attribute of yours (ie if you are nicely put together, or even if your chest size is more than a C-cup). And if you stay there long enough, you really will want to kill the next Sicilian dipstick who yells "Ciao, bella!", or the next drunken European tourist who starts off with, "So you're Americana, eh?" In short, you will have your toosh grabbed, but it's still better than in some American clubs, where they start grinding on you without even seeing your face.

Here are a couple things you can do for yourself:

1. If you don't have a boyfriend, have a male friend handy who is willing to pose as yours. Italian men will not move in on other men's territory. Strength in female numbers is effective, though, because groups of Italian men like to hit on groups of girls of the same number: it's a share-the-wealth principle that American boys still haven't gotten into their heads yet. But just don't go out alone.

2. Be clear about getting rid of unwanted attention. "Lascia me stare" (leave me alone) is a stronger one, for when you really have an annoying guy there.

3. Common sense: don't give out your number. It's not that he's going to stalk you; it's that he's going to call you five times a day to find out how you are. Then again, if that's your kind of guy, go ahead. Don't say I didn't warn you.

4. Good news: Italian men (usually) do not get a woman slobbering drunk in order to sleep with her the way college frat boys do. It isn't a part of the culture. So drink to loosen up, not to lose control. When you walk the streets of Florence around 12 or 1, those aren't drunken Italians; they are Germans, Brits, Aussies, but especially Americans. (The Italian language does not contain a word for "hangover".)
