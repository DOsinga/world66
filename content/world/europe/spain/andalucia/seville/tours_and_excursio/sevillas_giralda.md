Sevilla was at one time Spain's largest city, the gateway through which the riches of the New World poured. Enjoy a private walking tour to visit the Alcazar, Giralda tower of Sevilla's cathedral… 

It could be that Sevilla learned to enjoy the finer things in life in the days of the Moors, when it was the capital of King Al-Mutamid, known for his love of poetry and music. The Moors left behind marvelous examples of their architecture, such as the Giralda, at one time a minaret and today part of Sevilla's cathedral.
