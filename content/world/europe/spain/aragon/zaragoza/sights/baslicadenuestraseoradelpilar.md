title: "Basílica de Nuestra Señora del Pilar"
type: poi
opening_hours: "6 am - 8 pm, earlier in winter"
---

This church is one of the most popular destinations for catholics in Spain. The building is consecrated to the "Holy Virgin of Pilar", patroness not only of Zaragoza but of all Spanish-speaking countries. Situated next to the river Ebro it is the place of the annual celebrations to the "Day of Pilar", at October 12th. The church was built in 1681. It is flanked by four towers. Some parts, as the neoclassical main-facade, were added in later periods. In its interior the wall-paintings in the cupola, works of Goya and Bayeau, are of particular interest. The cathedral has also a number of small museums where you can see a collection of religious arts and other things.
