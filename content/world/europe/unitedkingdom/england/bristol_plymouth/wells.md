Wells actually is a city, England's smallest. Like the village city of St David's in Wales, its claim to fame is its cathedral and what a cathedral at that. It rivals the totally different cathedrals of
[Exeter](/europe/unitedkingdom/england/bristol_plymouth/devon/exeter), Worcester and
[Durham](/northamerica/unitedstates/northcarolina/durham) to provide the best external view of a cathedral in England. It provides a wonderful illustration of Early English [Early Gothic] architecture at its very best. 

Near the cathedral, outside the Bishop's Palace, the swans have been trained to pull a rope and ring a bell at appropriate times for food.
