Winnipeg Folk Festival 

Every July for the past 30 years, thousands of people from around the world have gathered at the Winnipeg Folk Festival in Birds Hill Park. And what a gathering it is! From the time the lights go up for the opening concert Thursday evening until the last refrain of the grand finale Sunday night, we promise you the opportunity to hear and discover some of the finest musicians on the planet. And there's lots more...

FOLKLORAMA

Experience the energy of Folklorama, the largest and longest running

multicultural event of its kind in the world! From traditional home-cooked meals

to electrifying nightly performances by local, national and international

entertainers, Folklorama will create a feast for the senses as you experience

40-plus cultural pavilions. Take home a keepsake of your visit by exploring the

cultural display areas found in each pavilion.

WHERE IS THE FESTIVAL SITE

There is no central site for The Festival but rather, Folklorama is spread

throughout the entire city of Winnipeg in permanent venues that each represent a

culture from around the world. In 2003, 47 cultures were showcased throughout

the two weeks.

WHO ATTENDS THE FESTIVAL

There is something for absolutely everyone at Folklorama! In 2003, Folklorama

welcomed 438,000 pavilion visits and another 50,000 people to the Government of

Canada Folklorama Kick-Off at Assiniboine Park.

The Festival can generally attract over 400,000 pavilion visits each summer! A

festival with heart and soul, Folklorama is whatever you want and make it to be.

WHERE DO I START MY PLANNING

Full of valuable information, the Free Folklorama Guide will help you plan your

tour of Folklorama during the summer of 2004.

The Guide will be available on our site by the end of June 2004 so you can get a

jump-start on your planning. The Guide includes a brief description of each

pavilion, hours of operation, showtimes and much more!

Every pavilion has a 35-45 minute show at the standard times of 6:45, 8:15 and

9:45pm. Some pavilions will offer additional shows outside of the standard times

so be sure to check The Guide.
