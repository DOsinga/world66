The Ontario Ministry of Transportation has established an extensive webcam network to monitor traffic on Highway 401, along Queen Elizabeth Way, in the Niagra region, and in downtown Toronto. Each set of cameras is presented with a map illustrating camera locations. Before travelling between points in the Toronto area, you can jump from camera to camera, and get a sense of what traffic will be like on your route. 

To illustrate the system, visit the information page for the Highway 401 traffic cameras at Highway 401. 

Toronto also has a great subway/streetcar/bus system. There is the Yonge-University-Spadina line which runs in a U shape from Finch station in the North East area of the city, south down to Union Station near Lake Ontario and then North West to Downsview station. There is also the Bloor-Danforth line that runs East-West and the Sheppard line in the North East corner of the city. 

Streetcars are plentiful downtown, running on every major road and buses run throughout the rest of the city. Current cost as of March 2004 is $2.25/adult or $7.75 for a day pass which allows unlimited travel from 9am to 5am the next day for up to 2 adults and 2 children. There is also the option to buy a Metropass for $98.50/month. 

For further information see http://www.ttc.ca
