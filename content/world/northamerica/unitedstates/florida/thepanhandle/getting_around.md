---
title: "Getting Around"
type: section
---

Odds are you won't be flying into Pensacola, or even nearby Mobile, Alabama. Check the fares, though, as connecting flights through Atlanta, Memphis, or Tampa may be feasible, and there are always flights available to Panama City during the spring. There is no really major airport less than three hours away (Jacksonville to the east, New Orleans to the west), so you'll probably be driving into the area no matter what. Interstate 10 is the main highway through the area. If you have the time, use U.S. 90 instead, which parallels the interstate through a number of farming towns, or U.S. 98, also called the Miracle Strip Parkway, which parallels the ocean from Pensacola to Apalachicola. If you are visiting the Panhandle and only the Panhandle, you need never get on I-10. 

The Panhandle is, as the name implies, essentially a long, narrow strip of land. The three above-mentioned roads are the main transportation corridors, with north-south travel handled by smaller state and county roads. There is a lot of open land out here; U.S. 98 provides the best scenery. The area's three main towns all lie along this road, and those points of interest in the interior can easily be reached by roads leading from this highway.
