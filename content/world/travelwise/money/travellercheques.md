The advantage of travelers’ checks (TC) to cash is that they are insured. The drawback is all the rest: since no taxi driver takes TC, you end up going to a bank, waiting in a long line of locals with sharp elbows, paying a fee for a reason you don’t quite understand. When you are traveling for a longer period, or are the type of person that always gets mugged when getting of the plane, you should surely take TC’s with you. There does not seem to be a big difference between different brands of TC’s, but we would suggest you take them in US Dollars and not some minor, local currency. The main exception to this is formed by former French colonies, where we would advise you to take cheques in Euros. These days Credit/Debit cards are taking over the traditional role of the Traveller Cheques.

Thomas Cook*

Thomas Cook has come a long way since inventing the forerunner of the travellers cheques in 1874. They are now the world's leading provider of foreign exchange services. We operate foreign exchange bureaux in over 1,000 locations in 22 countries, as well as servicing more than 20 million customers and over 8,000 financial institutions around the world.
