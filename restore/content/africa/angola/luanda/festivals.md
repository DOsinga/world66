# Festivals in Luanda

*Africa > Angola > Luanda > Festivals*

Just like the rest of Africa, the ancient Angolan civilization also has a large number of traditional **Luanda festivals**, which are coming down through the centuries completely unbroken. Not only are they a fascinating glimpse into the great civilizations which flourished here, but they are also joyous, and extremely memorable experiences. So for all those intrepid travelers, traveling to Luanda, and want to enjoy the magic of Luanda festivals, here is the list of the national festivals and celebrations which you would not want to miss.

**Luanda Jazz Festival and Other Festivals**

The Luanda Jazz Festival is to be held in July. Feira Internacional de Angola will be held in July in Luanda. The Independence Day celebrations is one of the most colorful and popular celebrations, enjoyed by one and all in Luanda in November. You might also want to enjoy Luanda day in January. Also in January, the day when the Martyrs of Colonial Repression are revered occurs.

**Carnival and Celebration Days**

And after that, you might want to celebrate February as the day when the armed struggle began against colonial repression and it was on April 4th when cease-fire was declared and Civil War was ended. You are definitely not going to miss the Carnival, which is going to end on the day before Ash Wednesday every year. Be prepared to celebrate Christmas starting from Christmas Eve until early January of the coming year.

**Dance and Music**

Some of the important art and cultural festivals are definitely going to consist of lots of song and dance and music, so you should not be surprised that the first international jazz festivals was held in Luanda last year. In February, another international music Festival was held here successfully.

**Carnivals and Fairs**

So, thanks to the Portuguese influence, you can enjoy the carnivals, and the fairs. In July, the world's greatest religious freedom Festival was held in Luanda, with cross-cultural religious members of every religious community from all over the world coming in celebrating the chance of religious freedom on earth. This festival is going to be continued, annually, with the venue to be announced.

**Important Tips**

Here is a point for your benefit, before you join in the festive celebrations. Do not take photos of native Angolans, in front of the police. This is just for your own safety. Also keep a cell phone always with you, so that you can communicate with your group or your family when necessary.
