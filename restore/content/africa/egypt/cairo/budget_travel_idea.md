# Budget Travel Ideas in Cairo

*Africa > Egypt > Cairo > Budget Travel Idea*

One of Africa's most exciting cities, Cairo, is also one of Africa's
most expensive destinations. Entrance fees to must-see sites add up, so
your biggest savings will be on food, transportation and lodging. But
this doesn't mean you'll be sleeping in squalor, dining in dirtiness,
or walking your way across the mammoth metropolis. You'll just be
digging a little deeper, going a little further past the well-touristed
surface.

**Transportation**

Forget across-town taxi rides; transit will be your friend during
your budget Cairo travels. Instead of taking a taxi the whole way to
the Pryamids, for instance, you can ride from Midan Tahrir to Giza
Suburban station, then take a shorter taxi ride, more than halving your
transit expense. You can ride to the Mar Girgis station and spend a
blissfully low-cost day exploring the cobbled streets of Coptic Cairo,
where you can also shop for budget-friendly souvenirs. Ride to Ataba
for a day of market meandering at the buzzing bazaar.**
**

**Eating Out**

When it comes to eating, Cairo spoils with sumptuous street stalls,
especially in Downtown. Felfela and Abu Tarek are two of your best bets
for traditional Egyptian fare, far from gussied up for foreigners. Also
in Downtown, Estoril makes for an intimate evening that similarly won't
break the bank. In Ataba, head to Egyptian Pancake House for
not-what-you-imagine "pancakes," and be sure to sip some Turkish coffee
at al-Fishawi. Outside of town, your budget dining options are more
limited. Inexpensive sandwiches can be found near the Sphinx;
otherwise, plan to fill your camel hump with big meals in town before
heading out to the see the sights.

**Nightlife**

You'll want to experience a little nightlife in Cairo, and you don't
need to blow your budget here either. Near the Opera station on
Zamalek, the al-Sawy Cultural Centre hosts inexpensive concerts that
range from modern rock to classic oud; there's almost always a free art
exhibition to peruse as well. Rub shoulders with artists and writers at
hip Horeya in Downtown, and hang with the expats at friendly Deals in
Zamalek. Lively crowds and international DJs makes Downtown's Latex one
of Cairo's best clubs.

**Accommodations**

Budget-friendly hostels abound along Talaat Harb street in Downtown
Cairo, making for central stays. If you're looking for something a
little more private, Downtown's Cosmopolitan Hotel oozes Art Deco
style. The Mayfair Hotel in Zamalek is a budget-friendly steal in a
green upscale neighborhood. The Windosr Hotel may be a little past its
prime, but the opulence ambiance lingers.
