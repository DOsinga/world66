# Family Travel Ideas in Cairo

*Africa > Egypt > Cairo > Family Travel Idea*

In a city of 16 million inhabitants, including those in the
surrounding areas, travel, especially with children, can be quite
hectic. Despite the population density and the sometimes
chaotic tourism industry, Cairo actually has plenty of fascinating
family friendly attractions that are not only entertaining, but very
educational. For most activities and attractions, kids get a special
reduced price, making doing things as a family easy on the budget.

**Ancient Egypt**

Cairo is basically next door to most of the important sites and
monuments of Ancient Egypt. Some people might assume that kids would
get bored with the many historical areas and museums of Cairo, but that's not the case. On the contrary, the archaeological sites and museums
are so dynamic and interactive that kids are usually enthralled for
hours, even if they aren't huge fans of history. There are so many
famous places to choose from, including the Valley of the Kings, the
Temples of Luxor, the Pyramids of Giza and more. Your entire family will be
impressed by the sheer size and energy of these places. The
Egyptian Museum is another place that you can't miss. With its
shocking collection of ornate, pure gold jewelry and rows of royal
mummies with their faces revealed, you and your family are sure to have
a fantastic time. Especially intriguing is the collection of
Tutankhamun.

**Nile Cruises**

For a reasonable price, your entire family can get a different
perspective of Cairo from the water. It's an especially beautiful
thing to do in the evening, when you can see all the lights from the
city and the pyramids along the riverbanks. You can also cruise to a
tiny island in the Nile called Qanatar with its amusement park
activities, picnic areas and ferris wheels. It takes about an hour and
a half.

**Khan al Khalili**

Khan al Khalili is a huge and very popular market among both
tourists and locals. For a real Egyptian experience, you should go
there at least once. There are tons of bargains to be found, but you
have to haggle a bit to get what you want. That's just part of their
culture. Kids will be entertained by this marketplace. It's colorful
and eclectic, filled with interesting sights, sounds and smells, just
be careful not to get lost as the huge network of alleyways can get
confusing. Don't miss the shops filled with cheap gold and silver items.
