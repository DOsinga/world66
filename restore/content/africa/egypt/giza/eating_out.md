# Eating Out in Giza

*Africa > Egypt > Giza > Eating Out*

Giza is a bouquet of fine dining restaurants, popular western fast
food chains, humble street side eateries and up market five star hotel
coffee shops. The ** Giza Restaurants **guide offers visitors information on the best eating options in this hustling tourist hotspot.

**Orientation**

Eating out options at Giza are thoroughly customized to appease the
tourist’s palate. There are a few local eateries, however most
restaurants serve fusion food and fast food that is universally
acceptable. Several joints of global fast food giants are frequented by
brand conscious tourists apart from the five start hotel restaurants
that are visited by their patrons. Giza boasts of some Egyptian
specialty cuisine restaurants that serve delicious meat preparations
and a few Mediterranean food haunts that serve delectable Italian fare.
There are also restaurants whipping up full-bodied Indian roasted meat
and curries and cafes selling yummy mocha coffee and sandwiches. To
cut a long story short -Giza has something for everyone, in tandem with
its spirit of playing hosts to tourists of various ethnicities and
nationalities.

**Chilli's
**

Chilli’s is a nice, inexpensive option for a casual meal. You can
get some lip-smacking salads and sandwiches here along with a host of
appetizers and main course choices.

**Spektra**

For succulent pepper steak, head
straight to Spektra, a place that is celebrated for its tasteful
cuisine and elegant ambiance. The restaurant is moderately priced and
is very popular among western tourists.

**The Moghul Room**

The Moghul Room is the best bet for flavorsome
Indian fare and exudes a chic, informal ambiance. A meal for two costs
approximately $ 50 -$ 100. Massimo serves super saucy pastas and creamy
lasagnas. The salads here are fresh and hand-picked. This is truly one
of the best Italian restaurants in the region.

**Western Chains**

There are also fast food
joints like Pizza Hut and KFC that are located opposite the main
entrance gate of the pyramids. Hardee’s whips up some super juicy
burgers along with Casino that serves genuine American fast food.

**Best Cafés**

Bellisarius Café and restaurant is great for a grilled brunch and has a
nice outdoor seating arrangement. Café Mo is a good place to hang out
in between grueling sightseeing sessions, they serve delicately spiced
light snacks and an array of rich flavorsome coffees. Tikka is great
for moderately spiced grilled Indian meat delicacies.

**Local Cuisine**

Andrea is good
for thoroughbred local cuisine and their signature chicken dishes along
with an intimate décor and friendly service. The Nile Pharos is a fine
entertainment cruise that features international and Egyptian fare
buffet along with belly dancing and other local performances.

Giza is a melting pot of various gastronomical delights that are
developed to woo the taste buds of an eclectic bunch of tourists.
