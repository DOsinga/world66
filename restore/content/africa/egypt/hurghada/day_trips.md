# Day Trips in Hurghada

*Africa > Egypt > Hurghada > Day Trips*

Hurghada is strategically sandwiched between the Sahara desert and the Red
Sea, which gives visitors ample opportunities to explore its vast ecology,
surrounding islands and breathtaking desert landscape. Here is a comprehensive
guide to taking day trips from Hurghada.

**Giftun Island **

This is the most attractive and popular day trip from Hurghada. Visitors can
take a boat trip from the town to the island and enjoy a fun-filled lazy day at
one of the beautiful islands in a cluster of islands. The secluded destination is great for
swimming, spending a day aimlessly rolling in sand or spotting rich coral reefs
and other marine ecology of the island. The most striking feature of the island
is the gorgeous coral garden that showcases tons of magnificent
colorful corals. Visitors can even book on a glass boat tour of the island,
where corals can be spotted from glass-bottomed boats.

**Desert Safaris**

Desert Safaris on bikes, quads, camels and jeeps is another very popular day
trip option from Hurghada. Visitors can enjoy miles and miles of Quad-biking
through the sandy terrains of the Sahara Desert. Tourists can also cover the
Biblical plains and see the remotest parts of the desert along with some old
monuments and ruins on a camel-back trip. Jeep rides through the desert for
big groups are also very common and provide a great way to experience the exotic Middle
East by rubbing shoulders with local tribes to learn about their interesting
customs.

**Sharm-El-Shaikh**

This super eclectic diving resort and historical town is located at
approximately 145 kilometers from Hurghada. However fast boats by International
Fast Ferries makes the town accessible in less than an hour. Tourists can visit
the churches and monuments in the old town section or head to Tiran and Ras
Mohammed, which are reportedly the best diving centers in the world. The diving
spots can be accessed from Sharm el Sheikh by boat. Tourists can encounter an
incredible collection of fish ranging from Murrays and Sharks to Stingrays and
Barracudas. You can also book on diving safaris to avoid crowds and dive through
rich reef areas that are inaccessible to day-tripping boats.

**Luxor **

Luxor is a 4-hour drive from Hurghada and has several attractions
scattered about its historical landscape. Day-trippers can visit the Valley
of Kings, Luxor and Karnak temples, Medinet Habu, Ramesseum and Hatshepsut’s
temple. The tombs of nobles are also worth visiting along with a trip to the
workers' village.

Hurghada is blessed with a beautiful topography and its surrounding deserts,
islands, diving centers and historical towns are great options for day
trips.
