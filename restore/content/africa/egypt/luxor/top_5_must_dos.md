# Top 5 Must Do's

*Africa > Egypt > Luxor > Top 5 Must Dos*

If you are planning a trip to Egypt, no visit is complete without a
stop in Luxor. Luxor is located in southern Egypt and the site of
the ancient Egyptian city of Thebes. Make sure to bring comfortable
shoes, light clothing if visiting in the summer months. Here are my Top 5 Must Do's in Luxor (in no particular
order).

**HOT AIR BALLOON RIDE OVER LUXOR**

Finances and weather permitting, I must say this is a TO DO you will
not surely forget. See the historic sites of Luxor and the Valley of
the Kings from the air, not for the faint of heart or someone with fear
of heights. An added highlight, if available, is traveling across the
Nile in the early hours of the morning and watching the sunrise over
the Valley of the Kings.

**TOUR LUXOR EAST BANK**

Feel free to do this on your own, but I recommend taking a guided
tour to get a true grasp of the history. Visit the Temples of Karnak,
and then walk along the Avenue of the Sphinx to enter the complex. Some
of the oldest ruins here are over 3000 years old! Walk through the
Great Court and view the towering 134 columns, quite impressive, and in
the rear you will find the sacred lake. The Luxor Temple is joined to
the Temples of Karnak by roughly a mile and half walk along the Avenue
of Sphinx.

**TOUR LUXOR WEST BANK**

One of the most important archaeological sites in the world, the
Valley of the Kings is not to be missed. Also referred to as ancient
Thebes, this vast area is littered with a honeycomb of tombs. Again,
feel free to do this on your own, but I would advise taking a guided
tour so you actually get some knowledge of what you are looking at.
Also on the West Bank you will find the Tombs of Nobles, and the Valley
of Queens.

**LUXOR MUSEUM**

First of all, the museum is air conditioned! And second of all, its
home to many rare and historical artifacts. The pieces here are
displayed wonderfully, allowing maximum viewing pleasure, and
everything is labeled in English which is very helpful. If you like
mummies, this is the place to go! The entrance fee is a little on the
expensive side, and the facade is not so inviting, but rest assured, you
will be impressed.

**VALLEY OF THE ARTISANS (DEIR EL MEDINA)**

Deir el Medina is a small valley on the western slope of the Theban
Mountain, where the artisans and craftsmen of Luxor lived. These were
the people who built all the amazing tombs you see scattered over
Luxor. Deir el Medina is also known as the Monastery of the town. The
images and colors of the tombs here are amazing and the bright colored
paintings are breathtaking.
