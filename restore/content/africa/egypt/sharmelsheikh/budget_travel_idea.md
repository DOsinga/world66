# Budget Travel Ideas

*Africa > Egypt > Sharmelsheikh > Budget Travel Idea*

If you're interested in
traveling to some of Egypt's most beautiful beaches without spending a lot of money, read on for some Sharm El
Sheikh budget travel tips. A beautiful
coastal town on the Red Sea, Sharm El Sheikh is one of Egypt’s most
popular beach resort locations. From the beautiful resorts of Na’ama
Bay to the shops and markets of Sharm Old Town, the city attracts
travelers from all over the world. Lodging is more expensive here
than in some cities, so you’ll have to save money elsewhere. But by
shopping smart and looking around, you should be able to leave Sharm
El Sheikh with a whole lot more than the shirt on your back.

**
Seasonal Travel**

Sharm El Sheikh has
excellent weather, and even the winter temperatures can be in the
mid-70s to 80 degrees F. This can be a little cold for snorkeling or
sunbathing, but is still a welcome change for those coming from less
temperate climates. If you’re looking for deals and are willing to
take a chance of some rain and cooler temperatures, travel between
November and January. For the less adventurous who still want to snag
a deal, travel from March to May or from September to October.

**
Eating Out**

Sharm El Sheikh has a wide
variety of dining options. Abou El Sid, located on top of the Hard
Rock Café, is a budget priced option favored by locals. To really
cut costs, there is a Metro Supermarket in Sharm Old Town. You can
also find inexpensive western dining options, such as TGI Fridays and
McDonalds, which may not necessarily what you traveled to Egypt for, but if
food is not your budget priority, sacrificing a few meals can free up
lots of room in your budget.

**
Scuba Diving and
Snorkeling**

The underwater sights of
Sharm El Sheikh are a major attraction. To save some money on diving
you may want to look into getting certified before you travel. This
can help your budget as well as allowing you to spend more of your
vacation doing the things you want to do. If you’re staying at a
resort ask about a resort course. It’s a 1-shot simplified
process that allows an experienced instructor to take you out on a
dive after a brief course in a pool. It will save you time and money,
and is perfect if you don’t intend to dive elsewhere. If you’re
trying to tighten your budget even more, snorkeling will allow you to
see the underwater world without the expense and hassle of diving,
and many dive trips allow snorkelers to come along.
