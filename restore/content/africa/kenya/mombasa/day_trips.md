# Day Trips in Mombasa

*Africa > Kenya > Mombasa > Day Trips*

Mombasa is a land that epitomizes the untamed wilderness of Africa and its charming, unpretentious vestigial culture. The Mombasa Day Trip Guide offers visitors super day trip ideas to make the most of their Kenyan sojourn.

**Dhow Cruise**

Take an enchanting Dhow (traditional Arab sailing vessel) Cruise
down the Tudor Creek and stop at the floating market that displays a
tapestry of majestic flowers, fresh local fruits and vegetables and
regional spices. The local merchants are extremely friendly and will
offer you great stuff at bargain prices. Its an ideal destination to catch
a snapshot of the region’s everyday lifestyle. The cruise also offers
its patrons lip-smacking seafood.

**Tsavo National Park**

Take a Tsavo National Park day trip that picks visitors from their
hotel and takes them 100 kilometers into the interiors of the Bachuma
Gate. The Tsavo National park is touted to be Kenya’s largest and most
ecologically prosperous reserves that is bifurcated into Tsavo West and
Tsavo East zones. The park is a rich reserve of diverse plant, animal
and bird life and offers visitors glimpses of the most ferocious
predators and rare birds that inhabit the sprawling land. The lush
green vegetation and indigenous species provide a serene backdrop
for game viewing and bird watching. Visitors can also view the
spectacular Mzima Springs on the way to the park. The springs are a
stunning conduit of white water cascading from Mt. Kilimanjaro and
attract a large number of animals who throng here for their afternoon
siesta. Other National Parks for day trips include the Masai Mara,
known for its thrilling games and the Kisite-Mpunguti Marine National
Park.

**Ngomomgo Cruise**

Ngomomgo is many Kenyan regions in one. The culturally rich center
has a whopping collection of over ten varied Tribal homesteads and is
an interesting place to witness their fascinating rudimentary culture
and traces of an innate lifestyle. The quaint settlements are complete
with thatched huts, home-grown crops and a bevy of animals. Visitors
should watch one of the peculiar ceremonies and exotic African tribal
dances.

**Chale Island**

Take a pristine boat trip to Chale Paradise, a romantic getaway near
Mombasa that is famous for its serene intimate setting amongst
tranquil beaches. The island boasts of some of the most incredible rock
formations in the region.

Mombasa’s surroundings are rich reservoirs of natural wilderness and
cultural exotics and offer umpteen opportunities for daytrips.
