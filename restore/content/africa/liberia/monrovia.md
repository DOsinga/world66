# Monrovia

*Africa > Liberia > Monrovia*

Frankly the main port and capital city of Liberia (pop. 1 million including refugees) is an utter mess. Heavy fighting in and around the city destroyed buildings roads and bridges. However the city is coming back to life and there are such basic services as grocery stores and hospitals now.

We recommend getting out of town to Sapo Park as quickly as possible but if you have some time on your hands you might check out the National Museum on Broad Street downtown. Another possible pastime is bat watching in the courtyard of the FCIB Cafeteria on the corner of Mechlin and Ashmun Streets. As the sun goes down giant fruit bats depart for the forest from a cottonwood tree.

A half-day trip is possible to the white-sand beaches and cultural center of Kendeja which is located about 10 mi/16 km down the coast from Monrovia. Kendeja is full of artisans who are descendants of those brought forcibly to the village many years ago in order to create a crafts center. Monrovia is 150 mi/240 km northwest of Greenville.
