# 7 Day Itinerary in Marrakesh

*Africa > Morocco > Marrakesh > 7 Day Itinerary*

Marrakesh is an exotic and rich cultural destination with its spice markets,
old madrasas, medieval mosques, ancient tombs and magnificent palaces. There’s
so much you can do in this fancy Moroccan town, from learning how to cook
delicious Moroccan cuisine to going quad-hiking through the deserts of the
region. Here is a 7-day itinerary for exploring the charm of Marrakesh.

**Day 1-Djemaa El Fna**

Visit Djemaa-Fna, the square in the heart of Marrakesh that houses several
historical sites, interesting museums, old buildings and a large assortment of
stalls selling fine Moroccan food. Start with the Koutoubia Mosque, a
masterpiece minaret, known as the Eiffel Tower of Marrakesh. Proceed to the Dar
Si Sid Museum, which contains a collection of artifacts from the Moroccan legacy ranging from
carpets, pots, and ceramics to clothing and musical instruments. At night, enjoy
the performances of musicians, story-tellers, magicians and dancers at the
square and savor a Moroccan meal in the backdrop of drum beats.

**Day 2- Saadian Tombs**

Visit Saadian Tombs at Rue de la Kasbah, a 20th century discovered
site, that chronicles the glory of the Saadian rulers. The tombs are
well-preserved and decorated with Moroccan mosaics. Proceed to the Majorelle
Gardens, a beautiful park boasting of a fine collection of botanical species
from around the world. There is also a Musuem of Islamic Art in the park
premises.

**Day 3- Palaces**

Visit the El Bahia Palace, a popular guided tour attraction that depicts the
life of 19th century Moroccan royalty. There is a nice courtyard and
a spacious garden with a striking tapestry of flowers. Move to the El Badi
Palace, the ruins of a once glorious site that has some mysterious underground
passageways to discover.

**Day 4- Marco-Expe Rondo Qua**

Go on the famous quad ride through the Berber villages and desert exploratory
trails of the Moroccan desert.

**Day 5- La Maison Arabe Cooking Workshops**

Learn the fine art of cooking fantastic Moroccan cuisine with this class held
outside the external strip of the Medina on the hotel-owned property. The expert
chefs will demonstrate super techniques for visitors to be able to whip up a
Moroccan meal in a matter of a few hours. Enjoy cooking in a relaxed and
tranquil setting. Discover the Medina of Marrakesh, which is a World Heritage
Site. There are quaint Kasbahs, mosques and old town squares.

**Day 6- Les Bains de Marrakech**

Spend a relaxing day at the Les Bains de Marrakesh, located at 2 Derb Sedra.
The romantic getaway is ideal for couples who want a soothing
massage and the famous hammam baths in the ambiance of bright floating candles
and rose petals.

**Day 7- Shop at a Souk**

Visit the Arabian Nights flea markets of Marrakesh to bag great deals on
souvenirs and traditional items ranging from spices, dry fruits, ceramic
tea-pots and shoes to kaftans, carpets, lanterns, jewelry and carved wooden
handicrafts. The Medina Souk is the most popular.

Marrakesh is a fascinating ‘Europe meets Africa’ city, which offers snapshots
of several exotic cultures that blend to create a wholesome and mystical
appeal.****
