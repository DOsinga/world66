# Meeting place of nomads

*Africa > Morocco > Tafraoute > Shopping > Meeting Place Of N*

"Speciality shop" tag is a misnomer. It's small tourist/gift shop
operating on a totally different concept to the highly organised and
very large "wharehouse-style" Maison Tuareg and Maison Berbere carpet
shops.

Houssain Laroussi runs a small shop just metres from the hotel Salama
(from the hotel reception door it's left, about 20-30 metres, on a
corner of 3 streets) just ask anyone working or hanging about nearby.

He'll give excellent advice to the more independant traveller or
climber/trekker, and can help obtaining almost anything in the town. He
knows several guides (not the hussler/hassle-type, but the real thing,
- walking/trekking guides. He's also a good guide himself, so
occaisonally the might be closed for a day or half day. Just ask the
nearby shopkeepers if anyone knows where he is or when he'll be back.

He sometimes has a trusted friendworking with him (but check with him
first!), but Houssain is so popular that some out-of-town chancers have
been impersonating him! Ask to see his moroccan Identity Card so you
know you've got the right person.
The
shop has been a popular information spot since well before 1997 (my
first trip there). The soft-sell, no-pressure approach pays off
here and a welcome
change from the high-pressure carpet shops.

He knows the best independant belgha (babbouche/slipper) makers in town,
and is a real help when you need a thing-for-doing-this-or-that-that
you saw in a hardware shop a week ago but you don't know what it's
called or where to get one.

Of course, a mine of information has running costs too! It would be
polite to show your appreciation for local information/help with a
small purchase from the shop.

Remember, in Morocco, every encounter or conversation works on a
mutual satisfaction basis, a sort of barter-friendship/exchange of
favours. If persistent large carpet shop touts succeed in selling you
50000DH carpets you've missed the point. Both parties to any
conversation profit, by knowledge, financially or
spiritual upliftment, or even just a joke. "westerners" with a busy,
money-led lifestyles find this a difficult concept to actually
practice, although it's an easy enough principle. Eg: you meet a
Moroccan friend in the street, he's with 20 other friends or family, so
you must shake everyone's hand in turn also and greet them all, or
they have"missed out" on your pleasurable encounter. Try this in
London, Paris or New York and people think you are strange!

All local stuff, local knowledge, by local people. This is micro-tourism!
