# Day Trips in Mozambique island

*Africa > Mozambique > Mozambiqueisland > Day Trips*

**Mozambique Day Trips **are ideally suited for travelers who want
to explore the vast wildlife and natural landmarks of Mozambique. Like
many other African nations, Mozambique has a vast expanse of forest area
maintained as wildlife reserves that are best explored during the
daytime. This is the period when the chances of spotting animals in
their natural habitat increases exponentially. Mozambique’s landscape
also consists of some exotic, smaller islands that are worth visiting. A
daytime itinerary in Mozambique can include any of the following:

**Visiting Niassa Game Reserve**

Located in the northern half of the island, this is among the most
famous wildlife reserves in Africa. Spread over 42,000 sq km, this is
the largest conservation areas in Mozambique and among the biggest in
the world. Its real appeal lies in the natural, forest-like environment
that has been maintained by limiting the commercial activities. The
Niassa Reserve is best suited for folks who are adventurous and enjoy
self-exploration. Even reaching Niassa is not easy. Visitors have to
trek a bit and depend upon a very cumbersome four-wheel drive.

Niassa is among the remotest natural parks in Africa and has a large
number of tuskers. Other animals that can be spotted include the wilder
beast, waterbuck, kudu, impalas, hippos, antelopes and the kudu. The
landscape too is amazingly sculpted, consisting of a large woodland
spread and granite domes that are frequented by the native tribesmen,
called the Mucua.

Booking a day-trip in Niassa isn’t difficult and there are many
packaged deals in the form of safaris that are on offer, throughout the
year. However, the period between April and September is regarded as the
best. These are slightly drier months and the animals tend to surface
more, in search of water and hence, spotting them is easier. A visit to
Niassa can be combined with visiting the Lugenda Wilderness Camp that
can be accessed by undertaking an elephant ride.

**Location: **the reserve is located within Niassa, Mozambique’s
biggest province. Visitors need to reach Cuamba from where the safari
operators arrange for rides headed towards the reserve.

**Visiting Medjumbe Island**

This is among the biggest and the most exotic-looking islands that
make-up the Quirimbas Archipelago, near Mozambique’s northern borders.
Medjumbe Island is popular for its white, sandy beaches and its
uncorrupted, natural terrain. It is suited for travelers who are
searching for an island getaway where privacy is understood. The view
from the island is simply breathtaking, overlooking coral reefs and
pristine, blue waters. Accommodation at this island can be slightly
demanding but day-trips can be easily arranged. Visitors can click the
sun setting in the backdrop of the Indian Ocean or just spend the day,
swinging in a hammock. Medjumbe also offers world-class diving spots
with the availability of rented diving gear and diving instructors.

**Location: **Medjumbe** **lies along the northern stretch of
the popular Quirimbas Archipelago. It is accessible through a flight.
Daily flights towards the island are arranged from the nearby, Pemba
mainland.
