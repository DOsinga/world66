# Public Transport

*Africa > Nigeria > Lagos > Getting Around > Public Transport*

Public transport is virtually the only means for locals to get around. the majority not being able to afford a car. Considering 15 million people still try to get round the city. Still can mean 2 hours getting to/from work.

In order of cheapness.

**Okada's (motorbikes)**

N20 ish for a ride. Most of the drivers must be drunk or high on something. Cheap, plentiful but risky.

**Yellow buses**

Old VW transporters never die. They are shipped to lagos, painted yellow with two blue stripes and put to work transport the populace form point a to point b. Maintenance is non existent and brakes an option. Again not safe. But used by millions daily.

**Taxis**

Again, painted yellow with blue tripes. more expensive than buses, licensed to carry 6 normally. (excluding driver) 2 on front passenger seat and 4 in back. Maintenance a non essential luxury.

**Private car hire**.

Safest means and most expensive (still not too bad) typically N4000 for 4 hours plus N700 for every hour after. found at most hotels. Half the ex Hamburg taxi fleet can be found in Lagos
