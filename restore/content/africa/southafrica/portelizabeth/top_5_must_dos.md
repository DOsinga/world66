# Top 5 Must Do's in Port elizabeth

*Africa > Southafrica > Portelizabeth > Top 5 Must Dos*

Port Elizabeth, South Africa is a little picturesque coastal city
with loads of fun things to do, see, and explore. Here's a list of Top 5
Must Do's, in no particular order.

1. **Whale watching** is a seasonal activity available between
June and December. Southern Right Whales can be seen along the
coastline, but if you want to get a better view there are tours that go
out by boat, and get you within a stones throw. Humpback whales can be
seen in June and July, and in November and December. The Bryde's whale
can be seen off the coast year round.

2. **Humewood Gold Course** is ranked as one of South Africa's
top 10 golf courses in the country, and is one of the world's most difficult
courses. With wide fairways, hard greens, thick bush, lots of animal
life, and an amazing view of Algoa Bay, this course is a must do for
any golf enthusiast.

3. **Seaview Lion Park** is a 30 minute drive from PE, and is a
definite Must Do. You get the opportunity to see lions up close, spot a
rare white lion, and even cuddle and play with a lion cub (additional
price). However, be careful, these lions may be small, sweet, and look
innocent, they are still lions after all!

4. **The Boardwalk**, operated by the same owners of Sun City, is
the heart of Port Elizabeth. You can easily spend half or a whole day
on the boardwalk, and it is fun for all ages. The attractions are
plentiful, and include shopping, dining, movies, bars, pubs, a casino
(fully equipped with slots, blackjack tables, and roulette), and the
outdoor funfair. The Boardwalk is a fun evening activity when all the
lights come on and reflect off the lake. Be sure to bring your camera!

5. **Water sports** are prevelant in Port Elizabeth. Nelson
Mandela Bay offers just about any water sport you possible want to do
or want to see. The city host many sporting events throughout the year
in two of of its internationally recongnised stadiums, St. George's
Park Cricket Oval and Telkom Park Football Stadium. The bay is named
the "watersports capital of Africa" for its near perfect water
conditions for diving, surfing, jet skiing, kite surfing, and
paragliding. The pier at Hobie Beach, is a great spot to watch all of
the above water sports take place on the bay. The Billabong Classic is
hosted annually in PE and draws the world's top surfers for competition.
