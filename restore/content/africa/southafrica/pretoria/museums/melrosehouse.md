# Melrose House

*Africa > Southafrica > Pretoria > Museums > Melrosehouse*

Built in 1886, Melrose House was the home of George Heys, who ran one of the principle stage-coach and transport vehicle lines serving the Transvaal. It is a handsome and well-maintained cross between English Victorian and Cape Dutch styles, lavishly furnished. During the 1899-1902 Anglo-Boer war, both Lord Roberts and Lord Kitchener (the British Commanders) lived here. On 31 May 1902 the Treaty of Vereeniging that ended the war was signed in the dining room. The stained glass, including a window representing Sir Walter Scott's "The Lay of the Last Mistral", is exquisite. The billiard room, the elaborate drawing room, the dining room, the bedrooms, kitchen and conservatory - all retain an atmosphere of the building's historic past.
