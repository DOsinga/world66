# Practical Information in Arusha

*Africa > Tanzania > Arusha > Practical Informat*

If you happen to be looking out for more facts on Arusha, then this **Arusha Practical Information** guide will assist you with that. Arusha, well known all over the world for its bountiful national parks and picturesque views, is a city in the north of Tanzania. Here are some important facts to keep in mind when visiting the beautiful city of Arusha.

**Population and Language**

The population of Arusha as per the 2002 census is 1,288,088. Its population mainly consists of African people and tribes. The rest of its population is made up of Asians, Europeans and Arabs. The main language spoken in Arusha is Kiswahili or Swahili. However, English is used officially as it happens to be the main language for high education, commerce and other administrative purposes.

**Currency**

The currency used in Arusha is the Tanzanian Shilling. It must be kept in mind that when you are exchanging your foreign currency such as dollars into Tanzanian Shillings at a money exchange bureau, make sure you do so using larger denominations as you most definitely get a better exchange rate.

**When to Visit**

The best time to visit Arusha in Tanzania has got to be during the months of October and February. During this period, you will notice that the weather is pleasant and the sky is clear, offering a good view. But, avoid being there in November and December, as it usually rains during this time. July to October is also a great time to be in Arusha. It is a good dry season to view animals, since Arusha's specialty is its national parks.

**Eating Out**

You will never a reason to starve in a place like Arusha. You can choose from African, Indian, Ethiopian and other types of cuisines here. There are many restaurants that offer great meals prepared in the above-mentioned styles. Other than restaurants, a must-visit is Arusha's food market, which happens to be the largest market in the whole of East Africa! Here, you will be able to buy the freshest produce of the land so that you can prepare your own meal. Arusha also houses a good number of coffee houses which serve good breakfasts, snacks and desserts. Fast food is also freely available in Arusha.

**Things to Do**

Arusha is plentiful in things to do. From its amazing national parks complete with wild safaris, to shopping for souvenirs in its colorful markets, right up to learning about Arusha culture, there is much to see and do in this African city. Hiking and trekking around the place is also a must-do, so that you can truly appreciate the beauty of Arusha.
