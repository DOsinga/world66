# Budget Travel Ideas in Dar es salaam

*Africa > Tanzania > Daressalaam > Budget Travel Idea*

The Tanzanian city of Dar es Salaam is a great cultural center. It
offers a good deal of entertainment at a cheap price and is perfect for
anyone traveling on a budget. There are ways to keep those dollars in
your wallet and still have a great time, enjoying all the city has to
offer.

**Beaches**

First and foremost, the beaches of Dar es Salaam are some of the
most beautiful ones in the world, with pristine white sands and
clear, blue water. They offer a close look at the culture of the city and provide visitors with a cheap way to enjoy their time
there. They're just north of Dar es Salaam, accessible by taxi or
rental car.**
**

**Do-It-Yourself Tours**

Touring the city of Dar es Salaam is a fantastic way to get to know
the culture of the people as well as the city as a whole, and save
money at the same time. Professional tours or tour guides can cost
fortunes, but all you need to do it yourself is a map, some stopping
points, the will and a bottle of water. There are many areas of the
city that can be explored, but choosing specific ones to investigate
can help you utilize your time and energy efficiently.

For example,
you can take a walking tour of Dar es Salaam, making an effort to reach
the Askari Monument, the National Museum, the Botanical Gardens, the
State House, Azania Front Lutheran Church and Old Boma in one day.
Setting goals like this and planning out your course ahead of time will
help you get the most out of your time and money while in Dar es Salaam.

**Bike the City**

Renting bicycles for a day and biking around the city is cheap,
provides exercise and allows you to explore the nooks and crannies
that might otherwise have gone unnoticed. You can bike to monuments,
historical architecture sites and more, and still stay on budget.

**Kariakoo Market**

The Kariakoo Market, set in the heart of the African area of Dar es
Salaam, is one of the busiest areas of the city. You can find a
multitude of shops and stalls there. Each of these is covered by a black
parasol and sell some variety of vegetables, exotic fruits, freshly
cooked meat, fresh fish, herbs, spices, coffees, local brews, textiles,
small trinkets and handmade toys of recycled tin and wire. You can
simply look or you can try some of the items up for sale at the Market.
