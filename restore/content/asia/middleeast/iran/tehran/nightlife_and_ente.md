# Nightlife and Entertainment in Tehran

*Asia > Middleeast > Iran > Tehran > Nightlife And Ente*

Due to the strictures of religion, alcohol does not tend to fuel Tehran nightlife, although there is increasingly an underground scene where you can lay your hands on stronger stuff. Generally speaking though, travelers will have to be content with the buzzing restaurants and cafes available.

**The Armenian Club**

Something of a favorite with travelers, this is a Christian-owned establishment that technically means Muslims are not welcome. It is remarkable for many things, chief amongst them this is the place where you will see women without wearing hijabs. Most attractive to many travelers is that you'll be able to get a nice cold alcoholic drink. This establishment isn't easy to find but you have to look out for the yellow light and very small buzzer. Travelers have even reported being invited into the basement to attend a traditional Armenian wedding.

**The Sounds of the Underground**

There is a growing underground party scene in Tehran nightlife involving "slam-fest parties" where people will challenge themselves and each other to drink until they vomit. At such parties canoodling with the opposite sex is acceptable, even encouraged. You must be cautious however as this kind of thing is highly illegal and travelers caught up in it can be subject to large fines or even prosecution. It's better to nurture the right kind of contacts that will get you in to bona fide parties rather than just wander around taking a chance. Trust is very important in the illegal underground Tehran nightlife scene.

**Darband**

If sailing close to illegality isn't your scene then you might consider going to northern Tehran, just above Tajrish Square to a strip of street food stalls and cafes named Darband. The atmosphere is chilled out and Tehrani locals and travelers alike kick back and enjoy kebabs and delicious yoghurt drinks and dips. Great herbal teas are also served along with ghelyoon (water pipes). This aspect of Tehran nightlife doesn't really kick off until around 10 p.m. because Tehranis tend to prefer going out late. After that time, however, you'll find yourself swamped by people from all walks of life, mingling with their friends, wives, families, sampling the delicacies on offer from the various vendors. This is a great - some argue the best - way to enjoy genuine Tehran nightlife.
