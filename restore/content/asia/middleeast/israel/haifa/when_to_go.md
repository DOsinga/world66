# When to Go in Haifa

*Asia > Middleeast > Israel > Haifa > When To Go*

Haifa is a charming old world city that is embossed with a rich cultural heritage. There are stunning mountain ranges and impeccable turquoise waters combined with several top-notch dining and entertainment options. Here is a guide to the best time to visit this highly versatile tourist center.

**Climate
**

Summers in Haifa are not very hot, dry or extreme and the average temperature does not exceed 26 degrees C. Winters are moderate, with temperatures resting at approximately 12 to 15 degrees C. Winters are characterized by frequent rainfall and the period from October to April is the wettest in Haifa. Though the beauty of region is accentuated by the lush rainfall, for visitors who don’t enjoy a wet holiday the best time to visit the city is from May to September. Tourists flock here to savor the year- round pleasant climate, however the best time to visit the city is during the summers when visitors can relax and sunbathe on the idyllic beaches of the town and indulge in enjoyable sightseeing sessions.

**The Haifa International Theater Festival for Children and Youth in Haifa
**

Visitors love to attend this theatrical vaudeville which is held in April every year and is a great showcase of Haifa’s fascinating culture and deep-rooted history. Marvelous performances, thought-provoking subjects and a brilliant execution of the script are all reasons why you will want to plan your visit to the city around this avant-garde theater festival.

**The Haifa International and Children's Film Festival
**

The Haifa International and Children's Film Festival is held in October showcases an amazing collection of films from the Middle Eastern region and the Mediterranean belt. October is a good time to visit Haifa to enjoy a pleasant climate and watch some of the rarest films that sketch the cultural portrait of Israel.

While Haifa is pleasant throughout the year, it is especially worth visiting during the theatrical events and film festivals, when the town becomes a sphere of cultural exchange.
