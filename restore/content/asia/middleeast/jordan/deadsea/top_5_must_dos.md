# Top 5 Must Do's in Dead Sea

*Asia > Middleeast > Jordan > Deadsea > Top 5 Must Dos*

Visiting the Dead Sea is truly a unique experience. There are several **Dead Sea must do's** while on your holiday. Make sure to take your time and enjoy all that the Dead Sea offers. The Dead Sea is said to have waters that heal the body.

**1. Spas**

The Dead Sea is all about health and relaxation. Several resorts offer spa packages that will have you feeling rejuvenated. Whether you are after a basic massage or a complete spa package you will find it at the Dead Sea. The Dead Sea is said to cleanse the skin and purify it. Some of the more popular packages offer access to the Dead Sea pools and complete full body treatments. Spending some time at the Dead Sea spas is sure to offer you a one of a kind experience.

**2. The Beach**

Spending time at the Dead Sea is an unforgettable experience. The Dead Sea is located 400 meters below sea level. The salt level of the dead sea is higher than any where else in the world. Taking a dip in the Dead Sea is something everyone should try at least once. Don't be surprised when you realize you can't swim. Due to the high salt content you will automatically float right to the top of the water. Just lay back and enjoy floating in the Dead Sea.

**3. Hiking**

There are several wonderful hiking trails at the Dead Sea. If you want to truly explore this region then consider a hike. You can also hire a tour guide to assist you in your hike. Most trails are marked well if you do not want to hire a tour guide.

**4. Masada**

Masada is a mountain top fortress that is rich in history. There was a once a group of Jews fighting against Romans on this mountain top. The Jews decided to take their own lives instead of becoming slaves to the Romans. This is a wonderful sight to visit. If you want a breathtaking view hike up at sunrise.

**5. Qumran Caves**

The Qumran Caves consist of eleven caves that are famous for the Dead Sea Scrolls. You will find the oldest manuscript from the Hebrew Bible in these caves. The manuscripts were discovered between the years of 1947 and 1957. Visiting the Qumran Caves should be at the top of your list of things to do.
