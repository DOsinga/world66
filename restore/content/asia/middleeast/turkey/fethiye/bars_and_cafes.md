# Bars and Cafes in Fethiye

*Asia > Middleeast > Turkey > Fethiye > Bars And Cafes*

The city of Fethiye, Turkey has one of the most flourishing
night-lifes of the Eastern European nations. Many visitors doubt such a
claim since there are no night clubs. However, the **Fethiye Bars and Cafes** will keep you entertained. You'll dance the night away in a wide variety of different styles in Fethiye.

**Sobe Tapas and Drinks Bar**

The Sobe Tapas and Drinks bar will dazzle you with the Latin rhythms
that play through the night. Although fun is definitely not prohibited
in this establishment, it is quite a sophisticated place. The upstairs
portion of the bar is a fully functioning restaurant for those who
want to escape the crowds and enjoy a finely cooked meal. An additional
treat is the large picture window where guests can enjoy their dinner
while taking in the majestic view of the harbor outside. The Sobe Tapas
bar offers Salsa lessons in Thursday nights and a local guitarist on
Saturdays playing classical Spanish and Turkish tunes from the 1930s
to the present day.

**Car Cemetery Bar**

If you enjoy a noisy dance hall, then the Car Cemetery Bar is the
place for you. Inside, the younger crowds dance the night away while the
older groups venture outside for some quiet and pleasant company. The
Car Cemetery Bar is a great place and has quite a loyal following. Some
vacationers have been using it as a meeting place since the early 1990s
as a way to meet up with fellow visitors to the area. Another perk to
the Car Cemetery Bar is that it is located behind a Turkish bath.

**Georges Cocktail Bar**

Georges Cocktail Bar is a great place to relax and enjoy the great
weather. The atmosphere is warm and very welcoming, with many visitors
spending large portions of their holidays there. The cocktail prices at
George's range from reasonable to excellent. They are the perfect treat for a hot, sunny Turkish day. The rules of etiquette are quite relaxed
at George's but civility is always appreciated.

**The Ottoman Bar**

A visit to The Ottoman Bar is the perfect way to get to know the
locals. The reason being, the dance floor is so small you're literally
bumping up against the person next to you. The Ottoman Bar is a great, relaxed pub that
allows dancing. The music variety if quieter varied playing everything
from Jazz to Hip Hop. Guests are welcome to gaze at all the Ottoman
regalia on the walls, some of which is for sale. The Ottoman Bar is a
great place for small crowds to meet. Get in before 10 p.m. and you might
get a nice corner table to yourself.
