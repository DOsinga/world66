# Festivals in Izmir

*Asia > Middleeast > Turkey > Izmir > Festivals*

Izmir is the third largest city in Turkey and is popularly
referred to as “the pearl of the Aegean Sea”. Izmir has a long rich history
that goes back almost 3,500 years. Though it is part of Turkey and celebrates
all the festivals that are normally celebrated in Turkey, Izmir also has its
own festivals, namely the Izmir International Fair and the Izmir European Jazz
Festival.

**Izmir International Fair**

Turkey is popular for its tradeshows and the Izmir
International Fair is the oldest tradeshow in the country. The festival
normally takes place during the last week or August or the first week of
September in the huge inner-city Kulturpark. During the fair, several other festival
activities take place. The festival includes various musical and cultural
programs together with the main commercial fair that originally started out in
order to attract people.

**Izmir European Jazz
Festival**

This is a major cultural event that is held early in the
month of March in Izmir. The festival features jazz music artists and groups
from all over Turkey and Europe. The Izmir European Jazz festival was first
started in 1994 by the Izmir Foundation of Culture, Arts and Education. The
festival has seen popular jazz artists and groups like Karlheinz Miklin Trio,
Anna Maria Castelli, Oli Bott, Jean-Francois Giansily and Lighea Trio. Today the
Izmir European Jazz festival is a popular festival that attracts hundreds of
jazz music lovers from all over Europe.

**Izmir Alacati
International Child and Youth Festival**

From the 28th of June to the 3rd of
July Izmir celebrates another popular festival, the Izmir Alacati International
Child and Youth Festival. This festival is organized by the Municipality of
Alaçatı.

**Izmir Fair
International Folk Dances Festival**

The Izmir Fair International Folk Dance Festival was first started
in Istanbul in 1989-1990. Later in 1995 Izmir held the dance festival and
continued to hold the festival to educate the youth in their unique folk
history. The festival showcases to the world the various folk dances that are
unique in Izmir. The festival has resulted in building up a group of about 300
dancers who perform at various places. This is a good time to visit Izmir and
witness these spectacular dances.

**Izmir International
Short Film Festival**

The Izmir International Short Film Festival takes place in the
month of October. The festival is a place where short films made on 16mm and
35mm video and digital format are screened. The festival also includes the “Golden
Cat Award” to the best short film.
