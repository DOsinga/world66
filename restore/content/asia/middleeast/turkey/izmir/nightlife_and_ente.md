# Nightlife and Entertainment in Izmir

*Asia > Middleeast > Turkey > Izmir > Nightlife And Ente*

Izmir is a booming metropolis on the
banks of the Aegean Sea. The third largest city and the second biggest
port in Turkey, Izmir has become a vital hub of trade and a major
tourist destination, bridging the Mediteranean
and the Middle East. Because of its location and its history, Izmir is
less conservative than some of its neighbors to the south and east.
Alcohol is not restricted and there is a vibrant community of bars and
cafes in Izmir. Popular local beverages include Raki, an anise-flavored spirit made from either grapes or figs and wine and beer, including the locally brewed Efes Pilsen. Places to go include the waterfront and the Alsancak district, an upscale neighborhood with some of Izmir's best bars and cafes.

**Alsancak District**

One of the centers of Izmir, the Alsancak, has long been a cultural hub of Izmir. Primarily a residential area, the Alsancak is home to some of the best bars and most exclusive restaurants in the city. The Alsancak
starts at the waterfront, where you can walk along a promenade next to
the Aegean Sea, and stretches inland. In Ottoman times it was known as
the Punta, or point, and was the Greek and European neighborhood of the city. The Izmir Alsancak Stadium, a multi-purpose stadium that is a great place to catch a football match, is also located in the area.

**Ramadan**Ramadan
is a 30-day Islamic holy month that has a big impact on the culture and
nightlife in the Middle East and any other predominantly Muslim
country or city. Ramadan will not necessarily prevent you from
enjoying yourself if you are a non-Muslim tourist in Izmir, but you
should be aware of it so that you can show some consideration to the
locals. Because Ramadan is based on the Muslim lunar calender, it does
not fall on a set time of year according to the western calender, but
you can find out from year to year when it will occur. The main thing
to be aware of if you are in Izmir during Ramadan is to refrain from
eating or drinking in public during daylight hours, as most of the
city's inhabitants are fasting and it is considered rude. In the
evening the fasting is lifted and and special meals and celebrations
commence, but alcohol is still generally considered off-limits. You can
still drink as a tourist, just be aware of where you do it and who you
are around while drinking.
