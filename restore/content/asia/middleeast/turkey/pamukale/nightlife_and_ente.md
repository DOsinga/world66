# Nightlife and Entertainment in Pamukale

*Asia > Middleeast > Turkey > Pamukale > Nightlife And Ente*

**Pamukale nightlife** is on the up and the city is starting to get a reputation for its great bars, cafes and restaurants aside from the reputation it already holds for historical and cultural interest. Foreign visitors are advised to take care in some of Pamukale's bars because they are frequented by sex workers.

**Kocak Hotel**

This fashionable and popular hotel has three different bars catering to all tastes. You can chill out and have a romantic moment in the lobby bar, hustle and bustle in the pool bar and soak up the late night moonlit vibe in the terrace bar. There are also two restaurants in case you get hungry and a meal in either of these will set you back less than $10.

**Kayas**

Located opposite the bus company office in the dead center of the village, Kayas is a standout venue in Pamukale nightlife. You can kick back and relax at the tables in the shaded garden and enjoy cheap drinks at around $2 a pop. There is a disco area in the basement where you can go and dance and freak out but if you want to stay chilled out you can remain in the garden. The food is delicious, cooked on a huge metal barbecue and ranges from Korean to Japanese to Turkish dishes such as sizzling lamb, meatballs, rice, green peppers, fries and chicken.

**Richmond Pamukale Thermal**

Another impressive hotel with welcoming bar, the Richmond Pamukale Thermal is rated as four-star and is located 255 km from Antalya's airport. Patrons can enjoy themselves in the bar and its connected disco, and eat excellent Turkish and foreign cuisine in a choice of several restaurants. Ranking high in Pamukale nightlife, the Richmond is also a great place to stay, offering air-conditioning, mini-bar and satellite TV with all its 315 rooms.

**Random Pleasures of a Night Walk**

Pamukale nightlife need not revolve around drinking and eating. You&rsquo;ll find plenty enough entertainment just walking around this fabulous city at night. Travelers have reported the random pleasure of bumping into a belly dancer performing in the street. Others have reported listening to a chorus of frogs on the streets of Pamukale--this perhaps being what inspired Paul McCartney. Another great bit of sightseeing to be done by night is the Pamukale pools which are gloriously illuminated by all the electric lights of the city once the sun goes down--a truly magical vision by all accounts.
