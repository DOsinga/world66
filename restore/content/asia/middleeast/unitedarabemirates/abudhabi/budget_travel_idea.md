# Budget Travel Ideas

*Asia > Middleeast > Unitedarabemirates > Abudhabi > Budget Travel Idea*

Abu Dhabi is a wealthy area so traveling on a budget is a
challenge. Many hotels are styled with luxury in mind and rooms cost a
great deal. Many restaurants are on the high end as well. However,
you can find accommodations and restaurants with a budget in mind.

**Restaurants**

There are thousands of restaurants in Abu Dhabi that offer just
about any cuisine you desire. The smaller restaurants are cheaper. Go
to the Hamdan area to find some of the best small and family owned
restaurants that won’t cost an arm and a leg to eat at.

**Hotels**

Budget hotels in Abu Dhabi may not offer as many services and they
may not be as luxurious but most of them have impeccable hospitality
and they are clean and neat. Most have satellite television, phones,
attached baths and air conditioning. Some even have restaurants
attached and swimming pools on site.

**Site Seeing**

There are several sites to see in Abu Dhabi that do not have
exorbitant entry fees. The Hili Archaeological Gardens are beautiful
and are in the location of many Bronze and Iron Age sites. Over 200
burials were recovered from an on site tomb with copper tools, beads,
vessels and other items. It is open from 4pm to 10pm weekdays and 10am
to 10pm Fridays and cost is Dh1.

**Beaches**

Corniche beach was getting very old and dilapidated but was
refurbished recently and is now a great place for an individual or
family to take advantage of the sun and sand. There are several coffee
shops and cafes on the beach. A playground is available and there is a
womens and childrens beach. There is a fee for this beach but not
for the main beach.

**Lulu Island**

Lulu Island was man made and is right off the breakwater of Abu Dhabi.
Boats transport tourist and there are buses and a small train that will
transport you around the island. There are two restaurants and a few
coffee shops on the island along with some beaches, sand dunes and a
camel and horse riding track. It is open from 8 to 8 and fee is Dhs15.
Visit Heritage Village at Abu Dhabi to ride a camel on the way to Lulu
Island. Everything is free here unless you purchase some of the crafts
that are displayed.

**Transportation**

To get around Abu Dhabi without spending a lot of money, take the
bus. Each trip is 1 AED and one day passes are 3 AED. You can get
passes from bus stations and grocery stores.

It isn’t impossible to enjoy all that Abu Dhabi has to offer on a budget so get out there and have some fun.
