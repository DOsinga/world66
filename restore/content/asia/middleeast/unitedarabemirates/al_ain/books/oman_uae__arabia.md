# Oman, UAE & Arabian Peninsula

*Asia > Middleeast > Unitedarabemirates > Al Ain > Books > Oman Uae  Arabia*

Author Jenny
Walker has written yet another outstanding travel guide for the Lonely
Planet publishing house. This travel
guide is 7.7 x 4.8 x 1 inches and 596 pages
long.

There are places in this world that you may want to visit on your
own, without relying on tips from those who have gone before you. You
travel to have adventures, and you want them to be your own adventures,
not someone else’s idea of what you should do while in a foreign land.
That’s a wonderful attitude to have, but the modern Middle East is not
the region to have it. These are old cultures, with customs and taboos
that are very different from those of western cultures. That’s why this
guide book is so valuable.

It of course discusses safe places to stay,
exotic cuisines to try and what restaurants are best to try them in,
market areas to be sure to visit as well as areas you may wish to
consider avoiding. But it also gives details that are lacking in many
other guide books. It has subsections dedicated to the cultural history
of the areas, details on the behaviors of children, what different garb
indicates in different areas. Things that are important details which
you could easily trip over if you aren’t aware.

The book also provides
information not just for visitors, but for expatriates living in these
lands. You are traveling to another country to view its culture, not
to find areas of America within a foreign land – but the location of
these familiar enclaves is good information to have.

The guide features
chapters on Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, the United Arab
Emirates and Yemen, with subsections on major cities in each country
and cultural distinctions and details unique to each area. The guide
gives tips on transportation, money, sites to visit and special
considerations for female travelers. It also tells you what sort of
environment to expect on your travels, because this area of the world
is not all sand dunes. Some of the most beautiful beaches in the world
can be found in the Middle East.
