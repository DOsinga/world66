# Fangshan Restaurant

*Asia > Northeastasia > China > Beijing > Eatingout > Fangshanrestaurant*

Make sure to visit this fabulous restaurant after a visit to Beihei Park, the former Imperial Garden right next to the Forbidden City. It's situated on an artificial island you can reach via a bridge or a pagoda-roofed ferry.

It's quite expensive for chinese standards - expect to pay about $ 30 for a twelve course meal. There are some rather exotic choices like cow's face or dromedar's feet, but you can always opt for one of the more conventinal offers. Either way, you'll never find this kind of fare in a normal Chinese Restaurant. It's simply wonderful, either the venison(!) , the abalone or the variety of sweets you get for a dessert.

The food is served by waitresses dressed as Imperial Concubines and it's cooked following the recipes from the Imperial Court Kitchen. There is an english menu and some of the staff even speak a little English.
