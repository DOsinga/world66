# Festivals in Guangzhou

*Asia > Northeastasia > China > Guangzhou > Festivals*

**Guangzhou Festivals** celebrate beliefs
and customs belonging to the people of this area in China. Customs passed down
over generations provide celebrations on a grand scale. Newer customs have been
added over the years in Guangzhou festivals.

**Rourist Art and Guangdong Dun Festivals**

Performances
by folk groups from China and other countries are given at these festivals.
September through October features the festivals. Local delicacies available
for tasting and shopping are offered.

**Flower Fair of Guangzhou**

December
brings the flower fair to an end on the last day of the month right before New
Year’s Day. Flowers line the streets in Binjiang, Jiaoyu, and Dongchuan.
Guangzhou residents transport their flowers to these areas to create the flower
fair.

**Food Festival of Guangzhou**

Dining on
the hundreds of restaurants’ food brings the best variety of Guanghou to this
festival. Cooking tournaments from hundreds of participants offer exciting
dishes during the last day of the festival.

**Dragon Boat Festival**

Tournaments
of the dragon boat in Dongguan and Shunde are held in May annually. Spectators
and visitors by the thousands come out to cheer their favorite competitors.
Creating an energized atmosphere, the sounds of gongs and drums are heard
across the crowds.

**Litchi Festival**

Litchi
fruits are shared while viewing performances of folk dances and songs. Held in
June through July, the competition for the “Litchi Cup” entertains tourists and
locals. Activities offered are photo exhibitions, paintings and other fine
arts, demonstrations, calligraphy and movies.

**Spring Festival of Shantou**

January fun
begins with this cultural festival. Colorful lanterns and streamers decorate
areas all across the city. Exhibitions are given along with trade fairs. Many
tourists from overseas come to celebrate and take part in this festival.

**Liannan Pangu Festival**

Villagers
parade along the villages in the region, carrying images of King Pangu and wife. Drums
and gongs ring out in the streets along the procession. Cakes and rice wine
line the front doors for serving citizens in need. Senior men begin singing
as they follow the parade and offer a one-of-a-kind cultural experience for
tourists.

**Guangzhou Floral Parade of the New Year**

New Year’s
Day brings this well-known festival. Colorful performances entertain many in
the grand opening ceremony. Dozens of floral floats parade through the streets
in Guangzhou. Exquisitely decorated, they tour along the street and offer the
flavor of life in Guangzhou.

**Yangjiang Kite Festival**

Visitors to Guangzhou
during September see colorful kites flying along the hills near this city.
Varying in sizes and shapes, the kites soar above the many participants in this
festival. Kites are self-made and present different colors and patterns.
