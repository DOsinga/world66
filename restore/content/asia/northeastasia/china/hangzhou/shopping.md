# Shopping in Hangzhou

*Asia > Northeastasia > China > Hangzhou > Shopping*

Hangzhou is the commercial and administrative capital of the Chinese Zhejiang province, and **Hangzhou shopping** options cater to the demands of every kind of customer. Hangzhou is primarily famous for its native offerings like China silk and tea. The best among Hangzhou Shopping options include:

**Hangzhou Shopping for Local Crafts**

Qinghefang Street is regarded as the most historic street of Hangzhou. It is paved with many buildings that date back to the Southern Song Dynasty of 12th century. Other important buildings of the Ming dynasty here have now been converted into public offices. This street is the perfect place to shop for affordable antiques and local crafts. The range includes brocades, parasols, Hangzhou fans and authentic Xiaoquan scissors.

**Hangzhou Silk Shopping **

Hangzhou Silk City is a small wholesale location within the main commercial center of Hangzhou. Its silks are renowned throughout Asia. Some of the present-day traders belong to families that started silk trading nearly a thousand years ago! There are more than 600 types of local and blended varieties of silk on offer. Visitors can shop for other fabrics such as cotton garments and those with heavy, embroidered patterns. Among souvenirs-shoppers, silk ties and scarves are the most favored items.

**Location:** the Silky City is situated at 253, Xinhua Road. Visitors can head towards the main intersection of Tiyuchang Road, Fengqi Road or the Xinhua Road. The Silk City is easily accessible from either of these intersections. Those traveling by local buses should seek buses that touch upon route number 11 or 28 to reach the Silk City.

**Hangzhou Shopping for Clothes**

The Sijiqing Clothing Market is the most recommended market for buying clothes. Located along the Hanghai Road, it is among the biggest wholesale markets of Asia. The clothes-on-offer include the latest, international brands and the best of local, Chinese offerings. Due to the wholesale nature of the market, the rates are surprisingly low.

**Hangzhou Tea Shopping **

A particular kind of tea grown in Hangzhou, called the Longjing Tea or the Dragon Well Tea, is regarded among China's best in terms of naturally-flavored teas. This green-colored tea is often retailed as a health drink. Visitors can shop for various other kinds of native teas like the stir-dried tea and roasted-green tea. The ideal location to taste and buy authentic tea varieties is the Meijiawu Village. It is located in the western part of the Lake District that is accessible via a bus-ride.
