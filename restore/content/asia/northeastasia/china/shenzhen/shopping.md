# Shopping in Shenzhen

*Asia > Northeastasia > China > Shenzhen > Shopping*

**Shenzhen shopping **has become very convenient, with an improved version of the local transit railway serving the people. Shenzhen was among the first of Chinese cities that was opened to international trading. Even then, Shenzhen has been able to retain the charm of its native offerings. Previously regarded as a poor cousin of Hong Kong, Shenzhen has quickly developed into a globally-recognized shopping destination.

**Shenzhen Shopping for Jewelery**

Jewelery shopping in Shenzhen is increasingly engaging the attention of the world. Due to some unique tax-reliefs, there is a huge difference in the price of precious and semi-precious stones sold in Shenzhen. As a result, many shoppers head towards Shenzhen for the sole purpose buying popular offerings like the cut-glass jewelery. Rather than shopping at malls, visitors can seek bigger discounts at the numerous, smaller pearl shops also. Backed by a strict licensing and qualitative control, the authenticity of such items is ensured.

**Shenzhen Shopping for Electronics**

Just like Hong Kong, Shenzhen is one of the most prominent electronics hubs of Asia. However, the prices for most electronic items in Shenzhen are more competitive than those in the main commercial centers of Hong Kong. The best market among these is the SEG Computer Market. This market is spread across eight floors, each offering a mind-boggling range of brands. Visitors can buy the latest from world of gadgetry. Another electronics-centric market is the Huaqiang Bei Lu market.

**Shenzhen Shopping for Art**

China took the initiative to introduce its native arts to the global audience by setting-up a series of Artist Villages. Shenzhen has one of the highest concentrations of such artist hubs in China. Visitors can purchase amazingly cheap imitations of nearly every known masterpiece. In fact, with a token advance, some of these imitations can be customized too. The biggest art market in Shenzhen is the Da-Fen Artist Village. It is believed that nearly 6000 artists work here. This art village is located in the Bu Ji neighborhood of the Long Gang District.

**Departmental Stores**

Most visitors need to shop for some basic items like packaged food and other personal stuff, for which a departmental store is ideal. There is a network of Rainbow Stores in Shenzhen. These are preferred by the locals and the international tourist community. The service and overall quality of this departmental store is highly recommended. The main branches are situated at Xinyuan Road, Futian District and Baoan District of Shenzhen. A relatively recent brand, the Maoye Stores are associated with offering the widest range of international, confectionary and cosmetic items. The main branches are situated at Huaqing Road, Heping Road and Shennan Middle Road.
