Green Molly Restaurant & Pub (Gaoxinn District)

Let’s get straight to the point here. It’s good. You should
go there. If you’re hungry or in need of a drink, just use the address/phone
number at the bottom of the page or in the listing and go.

Green Molly Restaurant & Pub (Green Molly’s for short)
is located in the middle of the block on the west side of Gaoxin road, between
Keji and Guanghua roads. You need go behind some buildings and it is sitting
right in the middle with some green windows (back entrance). If you’re coming
from the other side there is a wooden path and a garden along with a sign. From
the HIBC building it is basically north-northwest, and from the large building
just north of the HIBC building (gao ke da sha), it is straight west from its
northern edge. Hope that helps… If that doesn’t get you there, just look around
for a leprechaun named McSomething or other, because that’s the best I can do.

The restaurant focuses primarily on Italian cuisine, but
adds in some dishes from Mexico, Spain, Southeast Asia, the Mediterranean and
America. The pub is a typical Irish/English pub that will make one who is
familiar with this type of establishment instantly feel at home. The restaurant
is on the main floor, the pub is in the basement and its target market is
mostly local Chinese and expats.

NOTES: Here, in no particular order, I’ve compiled
observations from my first couple of trips to the place:

Restaurant Atmosphere

Vivid colors, Mediterranean/Spanish looking interiors,
fountains, can seat about 60, but very cozy, lots of unique “flair items” (I
mean that in a good way), old world music, solid tables and chairs, generally
pleasing to the senses

Restaurant Food & Drink

Carries usual mix of appetizers, soups, salads, pizza and
main courses including seafood and steak. I had a taste of their bruschetta, quesadillas (with salsa/chips), salami pizza
and tuna salad. It was good, but didn’t
blow me away (give it some time). For drinks, they feature a wide variety of
coffee, smoothies, ice cream drinks, tea and some beer and wine choices.

Pub Atmosphere

The basement level pub is decked out with dark wood and
pictures everywhere. It has a really nice horseshoe bar, tables, booths and
great bar chairs. The foot rail is at just the right height. This may seem
unimportant, but it isn’t if you’re sitting at the bar after a long day and
want to rest your legs. There’s a billiard table, foosball table and even air
hockey. Space for about 120 + people. They open around 7, it’s busy some nights
while other nights are pretty laid-back

Pub Drinks

Beers on Tap:

Stella Artois, Boddington’s, Guiness, Hoegaarden, Carlsberg

Beers
(Canned/Bottled)

Includes all those listed above plus:

Corona, Heineken, Leffe, Duvel, Paulaner, Tsing Tao, Newcastle
Brown Ale, Delirium Tremens, Tiger and some special Belgian beers.

*They also have a wide variety of liquors, cocktails and
shooters as well as some non-alcoholic drink choices

Pub: Atmosphere A+, Drink Selection A+,
Service/Entertainment B

Average Price 20-40 rmb per drink
