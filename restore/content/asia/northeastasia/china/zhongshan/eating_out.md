# Eating Out in Zhong Shan

*Asia > Northeastasia > China > Zhongshan > Eating Out*

If you are visiting Zhong Shan, you might be interested in eating at one of the several **Zhong Shan restaurants**. This will give you a chance to enjoy some of the best dishes in this part of the country.

**Zhongshan International Restaurant **

The Zhong Shan International Restaurant located at Jiefang Lu is known for serving the best dim sums in the city. The restaurant is open 24 hour and specializes in dishes such as dim sum, fried squid and shrimp dumplings. You should try the XO turnip cake while you are at this restaurant. The restaurant also serves Indian and Russian dishes. All dishes are reasonable priced to give you total satisfaction when you dine here.

**Maiguangfan Prince Restaurant **

The Maiguangfan Prince Restaurant is located in the Shiqi District of Zhong Shan. The restaurant serves Cantonese, French, Japanese and other international dishes including traditional Chinese food. Popular dishes such as hargao, crispy squid, shaomai, spring rolls and dim sum with oysters filling are hot favorites at this place. The restaurant has comfortable seating and can accommodate up to 1000 guests at a time.

**Shi Qi Lao**

If you want to taste local inexpensive Zhong Shan food, then visit the Shi Qi Lao restaurant. The restaurant is a favorite place among locals and visitors. You might want to try the giant size fried puffs, or the best roast squab dish at this restaurant. Several other dishes on the menu worth trying are the tofu with minced meat, seafood fried rice, fresh pineapple in pineapple buns and snowballs pastries.

**Grace Conqueror Restaurant**

Grace Conqueror Restaurant located in Qianjin. This restaurant is for serving Cantonese style western food. You can also find lot of fusion food here. You can try grilled emu with duck liver, emu kebabs and emu curry. If you are looking for something different, this is the place to go and enjoy a fine meal.

**Century City Food Court **

The Century City Food Court is located in Qingniwa Jie. There are several Chinese, Japanese and Korean food stalls here at the food court. So, if you are looking for variety, this is the place to be. You might also want to try the Malatang dish, which is a soup based dish with several ingredients. You can choose what you want to eat and top it off with chili sauce. You might want to watch out that you do not use too much chili sauce.
