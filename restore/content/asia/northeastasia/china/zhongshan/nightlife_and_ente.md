# Nightlife and Entertainment in Zhong Shan

*Asia > Northeastasia > China > Zhongshan > Nightlife And Ente*

Zhong Shan is a much quieter than the other main cities in China, but there is still plenty of **Zhong Shan nightlife** to chose from. The city is quite beautiful at night as it transforms. For those looking for more than a walk along any one of the beautiful streets or areas, there are several pubs, clubs and other nightspots to visit.

**Popular Nightclubs**

Babyface Nightclub is very popular with tourists and other expatriates. It has many different special events and a particular favorite is DJ night. Come and see the battle of the bands and other festivals. If you like loud music and a good bar, then stop in. The Fragrant Club is another popular choice and it is located across from the Shangri-La Hotel. This is the gathering place for the local businessmen. It has regular karaoke and private karaoke booths.

**Western Style Pubs**

A fantastic pub for westerners missing home is Hans Pub on Jiefang Road. All of the beer offered is brewed on the premises. The food is primarily American and European and all that is missing is a television for viewing football. Jun Yue Lai Beer House offers great beer and is one of the only places that does not play music at full volume. The atmosphere is perfect for quiet conversation. This is a favorite spot for chess players.

**A Great Cup of Coffee**

Plaza Story is one of the most famous coffee houses in the city and is perfect for those looking for a coffee after dinner. The coffee is of the highest quality and you have your choice of a wide range of coffee drinks. There are private booths and hall seats. Occasionally you will have the opportunity to enjoy live guitar performances.

**Enjoy a Movie**

If you are interested in seeing a movie then there are several cinemas around town. In the Yuexiu district you will find Bin Bin Cinema, Children’s Cinema, Mayflower Cinema and the China Plaza Cinema. Other cinemas around the city include Feiyang Cinema, Chang Shou Cinema, Galaxy Cinema, Hua Nan Yiong DU, Qinggong Cinema, Modern Cinema, Sapphire Cinema and Rainbow Cinema.

**Traditional Opera**

You can also attend one of the oldest forms of opera that is still performed, Kunqu Opera. This type of opera dates to about 1300 and has greatly influenced the more modern forms of Chinese opera. The Kunqu Opera is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and if there is an opera being performed, make sure you get tickets.

**Attend a Concert**

There are several concert halls in the city, though one of the most popular is the Forbidden City Concert Hall. Check with your hotel or tourist information about current performances as you can find orchestral, chamber music, jazz, cabaret and more.
