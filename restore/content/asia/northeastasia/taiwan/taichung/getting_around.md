# Getting Around in Taichung

*Asia > Northeastasia > Taiwan > Taichung > Getting Around*

Taichung is one of the largest and most centrally located cities in Taiwan, and the **Taichung transportation** options that you'll have available to you in this city are many. In order to maximize the time that you have to enjoy the cultural sites and attractions of Taichung, research your modes of transportation thoroughly before you arrive.

**Bicycles**

Bicycles remain one of the most common forms of transportation for people in Taichung. The central part of the city is relatively small and accessible by bicycle. Rent a bike from a local store for a small fee; some hotels and other accommodations have bicycles that you can use for free or for an even smaller fee.

**Taxi**

Taxis are the mode of choice for many travelers. Prices are generally reasonable, and this is a faster way of getting around the city, particularly if you're moving between two places that are relatively far away. Be sure that the taxi that you choose is certified and that the driver uses the meter; don't agree to a bargained price, as this is likely more expensive than the meter would read at the end of your journey.

**Buses**

The bus system in Taichung is adequate, but can be very confusing for travelers. It's best to avoid it in most cases.

**Rental Cars**

While it's possible to rent a car in Taichung, this mode of travel is not ideal because many travelers find it very difficult to locate a free parking spot. Don't waste all of your time trying to find a place to park your car when there is a wealth of alternative options instead.
