# Beaches in Bahawalpur

*Asia > Pakistan > Bahawalpur > Beaches*

Any **Bahawalpur beaches** are found along the river. The Sutlej River runs right through the city. While you are enjoying the river, stop in at the ancient cities of Harappa and Uch. These were centers of Indus Valley civilization and Delhi Sultanat. A few other good places to enjoy water and bays are at the national parks. This is mainly an industrial and agricultural city, so it will be necessary to go a bit outside of the city to enjoy the water, sand and sun. Over 2/3 of the area is desert, so make sure you know where you are going.

#### Sutlej River

There are many canals from the river through the southern part of the city. This is the agricultural area, and the canals are used for irrigation. Swimming is spotty, but the canals themselves are very picturesque. This is the longest of the 5 rivers that flow through Punjab. It is also called the Red River. Under the Indus water treaty, all of the water from this river is allocated to India.

#### Lal Sohanra National Park

This is one of the few areas that contain forests, a desert and water. The forest was originally planted to provide materials for the railroads. This park is about 35 kms from the city and surrounds the Desert Branch canal. There are many ponds and lakes in the park. Over 4,500 acres are wet lands. The park is a great place to view wildlife and is particularly known for snakes and birds. The lion safari is a particularly popular tourist draw.

#### Patisar Lake

This lake is in the very center of the national park. This is the best place for bird watching. During the winter, more than 30,000 birds call this home. This lake was originally created to provide irrigation to the arid surroundings. Today, it is the wintering site for birds and contains plenty of lake vegetation.

#### Hakra River

This river only flows during the monsoon, or rainy season. It is quite intermittent and runs from Pakistan to India. Normally the Hakra River in Pakistan is dried out and only a river bed can be seen. In India, this river is called the Ghaggar River. Manu of the ancient settlements were around these 2 rivers, as they were important water sources.

While water is scarce and this area of Pakistan is not a beach haven, there is still plenty of water to enjoy. If you are a bird watcher and want to get out of the cold for the winter, then check out the rivers and lakes for the thousands of bird species in Pakistan.
