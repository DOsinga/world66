# Top 5 Must Do's in Bahawalpur

*Asia > Pakistan > Bahawalpur > Top 5 Must Dos*

Bahawalpur is a city in northern Pakistan located just next to the Indian border, and there are many **Bahawalpur Must Do's** to put on your list when traveling here. This city is packed with history, and is also the safest city in the area. The city is just next to the ancient cities of Harappa and Uch. Once, these cities were a main pillar of the Delhi Sultanate and Indus Valley Civilization. There is so much to see and explore here, but if you take into account this list, you're sure to have yourself a well rounded trip.

**1. ****Derawar Fort**

This is one of the rare fortresses that remained untouched by the hands of time. There are forty bastions, and you can see them from miles away. It is located in the Cholistan Desert, which is continued to the great Thar Desert in India. The fortress was being built in 1733. Close by is the marble Derawar mosque, which was made to be similar to the Red Fort of Delhi. You can also find there a royal necropolis of the Abbasi family.

**2. ****The Noor Mahal**

This castle was made in the neoclassical style, like the castles in Italy in that time. The great ruler Nawab Sadiq Muhammad Khan IV made it for his wife, who never lived there. Most parts are still closed to the public, but it is enough to see just one small part. You can be assured that Indian rulers at that time were extremely rich. The Victorian furniture is still in perfect condition, likely because this building in not open for the public.

**3. ****Mai Jindaan Da Mazaar**

The tomb of the great ruler Mai Jindan is probably the most famous landmark of Pakistan. There are also tombs of many other rulers of Bahawalpur, but this one is the most well known. Decorated with golden leaves, it shines like the sun on a hot summer day.

**4. ****Lal Suhanra**

This national park is one of the few national parks in Pakistan, and it is very interesting for nature lovers and scientists. It is a home to a rare species of chinkara gazelle and more than 160 species of birds. This park is a great example of how forests and the desert can live together; the forest creeps up right next to the sand dunes, and the wild life find a way to manage living together.

**5. ****Channan Pir**

Take a one-hour drive from Bahawalpur and you will get to Channan Pir in the Cholistan desert. Channan Pir is a shrine of a Muslim saint, and it is located just in between Din Gargh and Derawar Forts. For muslims, this is a place of great sanctity, so before you go, there just ask around about the local customs.
