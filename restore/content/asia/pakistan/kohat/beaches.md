# Beaches in Kohat

*Asia > Pakistan > Kohat > Beaches*

There are not many **Kohat beaches** because this section of Pakistan is characterized by desert and mountains. However, if you want to enjoy a day by the water then there are some rivers, streams and man-made lakes near Kohat. This is a famous city by Kohat Pass. Much of the city was built around a British fort, and there are many shrines in town. Take a break from the city and go enjoy some water.

#### Tanda Dam

This is a completely man made reservoir. It is the sight of a wildlife reserve, as many migrating birds stop here during the winter. There is an entire fenced in area that is used for wild animal breeding. This is the main source of water for the region and is a popular recreation spot. Several spots are completely closed in order to maintain rare and extinct birds at the NWFP wildlife department. Besides serving as a recreation area and wildlife park, the dam also provides generates electricity for the nearby city. Several fisheries have set up in the reservoir on the far side from the dam.

#### Kohat Toi

This is a large stream that comes from the Indus River. It actually disappears before the city and then reappears further downstream. There are many other streams that join up with the Kohat Toi. Enjoy some of the walks along the river. However, the stream is prone to dry up, particularly during the dry season.

#### Hab Dam

This dam is another man-made reservoir in an area that contains many stony hills and is particularly dry. This is the 3rd largest dam in Pakistan, as much of the water supply relies on man-made reservoirs. There are not many beaches on the shore because the shores of the reservoir are stony. However, there are plenty of shallow bays and smaller islands to enjoy. Much of the reservoir is unprotected though there is a wildlife sanctuary. This is an excellent spot for bird watching and other wildlife.

#### Khushhal Garh

This is a small village that overlooks the Indus River. This is one of the best places for clear views of the river valley. You will have a bird&rsquo;s eye view of the area. There is a historic bridge that spans the Sindh River. It is more than 120 years old and is used for cars as well as trains. The British built this in 1947, and much of the original structure is still in place. Additionally, this is the boundary between the Punjab and Khyber Pukhtoon Kwa provinces.
