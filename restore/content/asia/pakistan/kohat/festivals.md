# Festivals in Kohat

*Asia > Pakistan > Kohat > Festivals*

Most of the **Kohat Festivals **are of a religious nature. Since Pakistan's population has such a vast religious diversity, mostly revolving around Indian religions, there are many different types of festivals held in Kohat.

**Ramadan**

Ramadan takes place the entire month of September, and is also known as the Islamic month of fasting. Fasting is the act of willingly sustaining from ingesting food or water. Ramadan forbids participants to eat or drink from sunrise to sunset, but families come together at night and celebrate their self control by enjoying a feast. Ramadan is held every year to teach Muslims modesty and self control by forbidding eating, drinking while the sun is up, ill-mannered behavior and sexual activity, and encourages good deeds of its citizens. Ramadan is said to be held in the month of which the Quran was revealed to Muhammad, an Islamic prophet.

**Vaisakhi**

The Vaisakhi Festival is held by the Sikh religion in Kohat, and takes place on the 13th of April. The Vaisakhi Festival is an ancient harvesting festival, held during the time of the year where harvesting season generally starts.

Sikhs celebrate this holiday with festivities such as placing flowers upon shrines in the city, decorating the streets with colorful flags and banners to represent the plants which they harvest, and gather together for group prayer and meditation. Some Sikhs even gather together for praise songs.

**Kohat Picnic**

This is a huge picnic and also a religious gathering of the Muslim people. Held in April, Muslims gather underneath the Regi groves to eat Bushka, Kundi and Indian corn cake, all traditional foods eaten in Kohat. Not much is planned about the Kohat picnic, other than food and prayer.
