# Nightlife and Entertainment in Kohat

*Asia > Pakistan > Kohat > Nightlife And Ente*

As a smaller town, there is not a lot of **Kohat nightlife **and entertainment to choose from in the area. While there are some interesting sights to see by day, this city is primarily known and used as a military town and as such does not cater extensively to tourists, especially in terms of entertainment. Additionally, the North-West Frontier Province (NWFP), in which Kohat is located, is a dry state which makes alcohol difficult to find. If you're used to a typical "western" night out of drinking and dancing, you'll surely have to change gears while staying in Kohat.

#### Pakistan Air Force Establishments

The small amount of entertainment that does exist in Kohat seems to be centered around the PAF, or Pakistan Air Force. There is a PAF Mess Hall, an Officer's Club, and a PAF Cinema that may be open to civilians at certain times. The cinema can be found on the main street of Bannu R

oad only one block from the Officer's Club. Aside from PAF activities, a small number of restaurants and cafes may stay open late enough to enjoy some night-time activity; call in advance for closing times of local establishments.

#### Peshawar

The nearby city of Peshawar offers far more in terms of nightlife and entertainment than neighboring Kohat, though it is also located within the dry state of NWFP and therefore will have primarily alcohol-free establishments. Because Peshawar is a larger city and is more tourist-oriented, there are a variety of restaurants, hotels, and cinemas to attend during the evening. Night life here also extends to a few night clubs, such as Peshawar Nights Lounge, and sometimes the offering of local bands playing uniquely regional music in the evenings.

#### Pearl Continental Hotel

If you're in Peshawar and really feel like splurging, the Pearl Continental Hotel offers travelers a 5-star experience day and night. Their three in-house restaurants serve everything from traditional Pakistani fare including delicious kababs, tikkas, and dumpukht, to fresh Chinese food, to Continental fare including a highly praised buffet breakfast. Serving exclusively Pakistani dishes in a traditional setting, Tatara--one of the three restaurants at Pearl--offers live folk music on the weekends which can be enjoyed with views of the setting sun in their open-air seating.
