# Sights in Kohat

*Asia > Pakistan > Kohat > Sights*

There are a number of **Kohat sights** that you should see when you are going to be traveling into the area. The area is known for the mountain ranges and scenic views that you can enjoy, and there are many cultural sites to visit during your trip.

#### Kohat Pass

The Kohat Pass is a mountain pass located in the North West Frontier that connects Kohat with Peshawar. You can travel the pass by a cart to really experience the beauty of the mountains. There is also the wildlife park area that is located in the pass where you can stop to enjoy the different species of wildlife that are found in the region. You can also travel through the pass to see the tribes that inhabit the area.

#### Darra Adam Khel

The Darra Adam Khel is a narrow stretch of territory that is famous for the production of arms and ammunitions. Many people learn the skills of gun smithing in this area, and it attracts people that want to see the way that some of the products are made. It is also a beautiful area in the region to enjoy Kohat sights.

#### Tanda Dam

The Tanda Dam is near Kohat and the Hangu district. It draws many people to enjoy the recreation and the beauty of the area. There are many areas around the dam to see the forests and the rare animals that are preserved there. There are also areas to relax and enjoy the views, and spot the many animals that are living in the forest areas. This one of the Kohat sights that you want to see while you are traveling.

#### Khushhal Garh

Khushhal Garh is located on the way from Kohat to Islamabad, and it is a small settlement that overlooks the river Indus. The river Indus can provide many views, and you can go to the cliff that provides the beautiful views of the river and the wildlife that is found around the region.

#### The Kohat Tunnel

The Kohat tunnel is a long road tunnel that is located in the north province in Pakistan. It is also known as the Pak Japan Friendship tunnel, and it serves as an alternate route to the pass. This tunnel requires less time to get between Peshawar and also to Kohat, and also offers a number of interesting sites along the way including views of the mountains, rivers and wildlife that you can enjoy easily from the tunnel.
