# Shopping in Murree

*Asia > Pakistan > Murree > Shopping*

The city of Murree, also known as Queen of the Hills, is one of the largest resort towns in Pakistan. **Murree Shopping** includes traditional bazaars that have been around for over a 100 years. The residents of this resort town, a former British military base, provide shopping opportunities for tourists with broad selections of goods. Most of the shopping is located in a town center called the Mall where the visitor can access artisan shops and Western malls.

**Mall Road**

Mall Road, also known as The Mall, is a small stretch of road that contains a variety of stores and restaurants. Prior to 1947, Westerners were limited in what activities they could do on the Mall road. Today all tourists are allowed to explore the strip. Walking down the strip, shoppers will find independent stands that offer clothing, jewelry, and leather items. The items are unique to this special part of Pakistan, where Kashmir is visible from the hills. Part of the fun of going to an international destination is the search for the perfect souvenir and haggling with the store owner on price. The artisans incorporate their local culture into their goods. Shoppers can find unique things to bring back home. While they enjoy their shopping trip, visitors can enjoy local cuisine adding to the experience.

**Millennium Mall**

Murree also has Westernized malls. The most popular one is the Millennium Mall next to the Usmania Restaurant. Patrons can find a variety of luxury goods are available at Millennium Mall. If children are in the group, the parents can leave then at the first floor amusement park while they shop. The stores sell cosmetics, apparel, jewelry and leather goods. The shopper can enjoy a coffee or other snack while browsing through stores like Armani, Victoria&rsquo;s Secret and other Western stores. Western chains like Pizza Hut are in the mall, providing a touch of home for those who are homesick. Shopping at a Western chain in Pakistan can provide shoppers with choices different then what is available to them locally.

Murree has changed a lot from its days as a British. Before 1947 Westerners were limited on what parts of the city they could visit. Today the Mall has been integrated to all visitors so they can find the perfect item to take home.
