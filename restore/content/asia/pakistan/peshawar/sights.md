# Sights in Peshawar

*Asia > Pakistan > Peshawar > Sights*

Amongst **Peshawar sights**, one of the most important one is the Khyber Pass which cuts through the north-eastern part of Safed Koh mountain range. Vale of Peshawar, with its capital Peshawar, lies in the heart of NWFP (North-West Frontier Province) which runs along the border with Afghanistan. Peshawar has always been a frontier town situated near the eastern tip of Khyber Pass.

With a history that dates back to 58 BC as the capital of Gandhara Empire, Peshawar is one of the ancient cities of the country. This city offers a variety of interesting sights, both new and ancient. The city abounds with historic evidence of bygone history, commerce, invasion and wars, and battles.

Some of the main sights in the town and surroundings include:

**Peshawar Museum**

Originally called Victoria Memorial Hall, this century-old museum has some 14,000 items of Gandhara sculptures, coins, weapons, dresses, jewelery, Persian handicrafts, paintings from Mughal Era, and manuscripts and copies of Holy Quran. The Gandhara sculptures and panels of stories of Buddha and his history and stucco sculptures are world famous. Mughal Era paintings, tile and metal artifact's in bronze and silver are unique. The Museum also exhibits of major tribal handicrafts and other art work of NWFP tribes.

**Old City Attractions**

Bazaar-e-Kalan, Chowk Yadgar, Khybar Bazaar, Qissa Khwani Bazaar and Sunehri Masjid are some of the most important places to visit in the old city. Qissa Khwani Bazaar is in the heart of Peshawar thronged by all tribes of NWFP. Bamboo ware, silver ware shops and sweet shops are famous here. Ghanta Ghar (Clock Tower) has a clock presented by Queen Elizabeth, UK. Wazir Bagh, Shahi Bagh and Khalid Bin Waleed Park are popular local gardens.

**Mahabat Khan Mosque**

Built by Mahabat Khan, governor of Peshawar, this mosque attracts visitors due to its splendid architectural beauty.

**Fort Bala-Hissaar**

This fort, as the name suggests, stands on a high mound and is quite spacious inside. This commands an excellent view up to (Shalimar Gardens) Jinnah Park and the surrounding mountains.

**Peshawar University and Islamia College**

These are the educational centers of NWFP and Peshawar city. The university and college offer comprehensive and superior educational opportunities.

**Khyber Pass **

It is located at 1070 meters elevation. You need a government pass to visit this pass and mostly you will get an armed escort as well. There are two highways that wind through the pass – one for road traffic and other for traditional caravans of the local tribes.
