# Top 5 Must Do's in Peshawar

*Asia > Pakistan > Peshawar > Top 5 Must Dos*

Planning a trip to Peshawar can be interesting once you are familiar with the **Peshawar must do&rsquo;s**. There are a lot of fascinating sights to explore and visit. At the same time, you need to remember some important tips while you are at Peshawar. Here is a list of things that you have to put on your checklist:

#### 1. Visit the Khyber Pass

The Khyber Pass is an important historical landmark that you should not miss while you are at Peshawar. You will be in awe once you are briefed on its significance. The Pass was an important gateway to the South Asian sub-continent and was used for trade, invasion, and migration. The Michni check-post is last point in the line of control of Pakistan. A 30-minute journey from here will take you to Afghanistan. The drive along the Khyber Pass is exhilarating and breath-taking while you travel back in time.

Beyond the check-post lies the land of tribal people on either side of the road. You should not wander out alone and should always stay close to the armed personnel accompanying you.

#### 2. Enjoy Photographing the Decorated Trucks

A distinctive beautiful sight in Peshawar is the colorfully decorated trucks and buses. The drivers take pride in making their vehicle look unique and beautiful with the help of artists. The artists paint the trucks with different colors and designs, including birds, animals, and Islamic inscriptions.

Take photographs of the trucks and buses, for which the driver will willingly slow down to smile and pose. Also visit the artists at the edge of town to watch them paint each truck in a distinctive way. They enjoy being watched while they proudly produce each masterpiece.

#### 3. Shop at the Cloth Market

There are a lot of shops that sell traditional, authentic shawls and blankets from the Valleys of Kaghan and Swat. The shawls and blankets are handwoven with red and black wool and have bright borders. You can buy any type of cloth in varying sizes. Remember to bargain for prices. It is the way shopping is done here. You can get good souvenirs in this way. You can also shop at the historical 1000 year old Qissa Khawani Bazaar, one of Peshawar&rsquo;s best known market areas.

#### 4. Travel through Time by Visiting the Fort Bala Hisar

Otherwise known as the &ldquo;elevated fort,&rdquo; this 2500-year-old fort spans across 15 acres in area. It is located in the northwestern corner of the city and stands on a high mound. It gives you a panoramic view of the entire city of Peshawar. On a clear day, you can see the mountains of the Peshawar Valley and even beyond. To take in the breathtaking sight, you need to visit the fort in the early hours of the morning or late evening.

#### 5. Views from Altit Fort

Altit Fort was constructed sometime during the 16th century. It is situated in the Hunza Valley of northern Peshawar. It is surrounded by the Himalayan range. Along with providing an insight into the history of Pakistan, it is also a photographer&rsquo;s haven. The fort gives a mesmerizing view of the Hunza Valley. You will also find a village inside the fort. Long ago, this land was used for cultivation and growing flowering plants. This is done even today and has added to the beauty of the fort.
