# Travel Guide in Peshawar

*Asia > Pakistan > Peshawar > Travel Guide*

Using this **Peshawar Travel Guide** will help you on your trip because the town of Peshawar is different at night than it is during the day. At night the town of Peshawar turns into a danger zone for Westerners, but especially females because of the Afghan refugees who wander through the town at night. Despite the danger that the refugees present Peshawar is still a beautiful place to visit; just make sure you brush up on some of the local customs before going.

**Accommodations**

The town of Peshawar doesn&rsquo;t have very many choices when it comes to hotels, but you can still find a few good ones if you know where to look. The Pearl-Continental is one of the better choices around, but can still be better. The hotel offers you the chance to use a pool and a gym that are in great shape, but the room service can be slow. The biggest drawback is that the hotel is a bit pricey for the area. Rooms start around $140 a night.

**Things To Do**

If you are looking for something to do, one thing that you need to check out is the Smugglers Bazaar, which is located on the outskirts of town. However, tourists are not usually allowed in the far end of the bazaar because of the nature of items that they sell. Make sure you visit the bazaar during the day when it is safe because at night the police are not very helpful. If you are interested in hiking be sure to check out "Shangri-la" or the Baltit Palace.

**Getting Around**

Once you have reached the town of Peshawar, which is best reached by airplane, your best choice for traveling from one end of town to the other is an auto rickshaw, which also happens to be the cheapest method of transportation. Your other choices are to take a taxi in town or the buses. The best part about any transportation in the town is that it is inexpensive because how much you pay depends on what method you use and where you are going.

**Eating Out**

The Rose Hotel has a great restaurant with good prices. The hotel restaurant only serves basic dishes, but you can get a chicken dish for less than $10. If you are interested in trying something local, you will need to try the Chappli Kebab, which is usually made up of peppers, beef or mashed mutton and a few other ingredients. Chappli Kebab is usually served with naan. One of the best places to try this is located in Peshawar, and although it is not named, every driver knows about it. If you just tell them &ldquo;Chappli Kebab, Chawk Fowara,&rdquo; they will drop you off at the right location.

**Nightlife**

The nightlife in the town of Peshawar is practically nonexistent because the Afghan refugees that wander around at night make it unsafe for everybody. If you are looking for something to do in Peshawar during the evening hours, your first choice is to find a hotel that has satellite TV, such as the Rose Hotel. Hotels that have satellite TV allow you to watch English TV. If you don&rsquo;t like the idea of watching TV you can always curl up with a good book or find a restaurant with a street view so you can people-watch.
