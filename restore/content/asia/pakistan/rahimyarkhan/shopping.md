# Shopping in Rahim Yar Khan

*Asia > Pakistan > Rahimyarkhan > Shopping*

There are many things to see and do in the Pakistani city of Rahim Yar Khan, and here are some **Rahim Yar Khan shopping **places:

**Clothing**

With cotton and embroidery being one of the primary cottage industries of the town, it is no surprise that the clothing here is of a very high quality. There are two main services for the average tourists – traditional ethnic clothing and tailor made clothes. The former is available at most of the marketplaces, and generally is a result of the work of the women who stay at home to weave and embroider the costumes that come out of the weaving. These costumes are reflective of the traditional ones that are worn in Pakistan, even today, and consist of colorful cotton dress and flowing shawls or scarves. The latter service, on the other hand, is generally provided by the men – tailors who run shops in the market square.

Tailor made clothes are cheap, so be sure to get some new suits, shirts, trousers or skirts while in Rahim Yar Khan. Some of these shops provide services to send the ready-made items back to your hotel, so convenience is ensured. There are shops located at the main shopping district by the Railway Line – just ask any local and they will point you in the right direction.

**Pottery**

While pottery is not the most convenient item to bring home, be sure to take a look at some of the pieces while in the town. The town has been famous since the olden days for the pottery and clay art pieces by its artisans, and this tradition remains up to this day. Those looking for ornamental pieces to line the walls back at home have come to the right place – oven fired clay plates with elaborate designs on them are made into wall ornaments and placed in a casing for the ease of mounting onto walls. Bring a piece of Pakistan home with you, or simply just take the opportunity to admire the intricacies. Find some of these in markets scattered around the city, and on Shahi Road.

**Confectionery**

Another of Rahim Yar Khan&rsquo;s main industry is the production of sugar and flour, so it is no wonder that the confectionery that comes out of the town is of a very high standard. Indian and Pakistani sweets can be found throughout the streets of the town in little stalls and caf&eacute;s, and should definitely be tasted as they come out fresh from the oil or oven. The jalebi here is particularly of note – flour squiggles that are deep fried in vegetable oil, then drenched in a orange syrup made of brown sugar and consumed hot. Be sure to take care that hygiene standards are up to par, and consume these snacks in a caf&eacute; rather than the side of the road. Other than that, everything here can be addictive! The Jalandhar group of confectioneries includes a sweet mart and a bakery, found at the Old Post Bazaar, and is a good place to start.
