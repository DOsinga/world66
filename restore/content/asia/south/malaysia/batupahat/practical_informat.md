# Practical Information in Batu Pahat

*Asia > South > Malaysia > Batupahat > Practical Informat*

**Batu Pahat Money and Language Tips**

You may find the following **Batu Pahat practical information** very useful if you're planning a trip to the city, as it will give you a better idea on the language and money.

**Demographics**

Batu Pahat finds its place among the 20 largest cities in Malaysia, with a population of more than 300,000 residents. It reaches 400,000 when the number of people from the surrounding metropolitan area is added. The population has colorful ethnics, with 38 percent of the people being Malays, 60 percent being Chinese and the other 2 percent being of Indian origin. That's why a number of languages are spoken and you can use them all for communication. Among these are Mandarin Chinese and Singapore Hokkien, and of course the official Malay language. The Chinese and Indian people prefer to stick to their native languages. English is taught in schools as a secondary language and is widely spoken, but with many Malay loanwords, which sometimes make it very hard to understand.

**Money**

The currency used in Batu Pahat and in the rest of Malaysia is the Malaysian Ringgit. One Malaysian Ringgit is worth about US $0.30. Money can be changed at banks and exchange offices, with the primary exchange currencies being the Australian, American and Singapore dollars. Exchange offices usually give better exchange rates, so prefer them to banks and always double-count the money you get in exchange to avoid frauds. ATMs are also widely used and a major plus is that no banking fees for international cards are charged.
