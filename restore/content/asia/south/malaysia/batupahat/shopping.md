# Shopping in Batu Pahat

*Asia > South > Malaysia > Batupahat > Shopping*

If you&rsquo;re searching for **Batu Pahat shopping** bargains, you&rsquo;ve come to the right place. This small charming district of Johor, Malaysia, is just 3.5 hours away from Kuala Lumpur and 2.5 hours away from Singapore. Renowned for its textile manufacturing industries and a robust agricultural output, the district offers a delightful shopping experience with many products priced lower than in other parts of Malaysia.

**The Summit **

The one-stop-shopping mall offers a complete line of consumer products and houses a supermarket, cinemas, restaurants, and food courts. Through reasonable prices, it attracts both local and foreign consumers to its stores. Aside from Batu Pahat residents, people from Segamat, Yong Peng, Muar, Kluang, Pontian and even Singapore shop in the Summit as well as in the other malls.

**Carrefour and Square One Shopping Malls **

Carrefour is the French retail conglomerate that has 10 super malls in Malaysia as of 2009, the latest of which is in Batu Pahat. The complex is located Taman Flora Utama and houses a hypermarket, clothes stores, electronic shops, restaurants, and specialty stores. Square One is located just beside Carrefour and has the biggest cinema complex–with eight theaters–among the shopping malls in Batu Pahat. It also has a supermarket.

**Batu Pahat Mall**

The newest and biggest mall in Batu Pahat, this shopping complex is located along Jalan Kluang. It is about four km away from the city center. The four-storey complex accommodates a hypermarket, cinemas, bowling lanes, beauty salons and spas, boutique shops, and restaurants. There are also play-time activities for kids, such as train rides and playgrounds. The tenants of the mall typically sell branded and high value goods. Restaurants offer American, Thai, Japanese, Italian, Chinese, and of course, Malay food.

**Pasar Malam **

These well-known Pasar Malam (night markets) are tourist attractions in themselves. Every night, the stalls are located on a different street, with each street corresponding to a day of the week. All sorts of goods are sold here at very affordable prices. Snacks, fruits, vegetables, cooked food, gift items, toys, pants, clothes, bags, shoes, movie discs and ornaments are sold here at a bargain, and you can still haggle to bring the price even lower.

**Jalan Penggaram **

Jalan Penggaram is a street in the district that is lined with colonial-style shops. These shops have become popular because tourists frequent them for their textile goods and clothes, as well as their travel bags and suitcases at fantastically low prices.
