# Travel Guide in Batu Pahat

*Asia > South > Malaysia > Batupahat > Travel Guide*

When visiting the district of Johor, Malaysia for the first time, check this **Batu Pahat travel guide** for tips. Batu Pahat, which denotes chiseled rock in Malay, has a rich history that dates back to the 15th century. The city of less than 600,000 people has a huge manufacturing industry but is mostly known for its textiles. The town is slowly becoming modernized although it tries to keep its old charm.

**Getting In and Around**

You can arrive by buses coming from Johor Bahru. By car, use the North-South Highway, then exit at Ayen (air) Hitam if coming from the north, or Yong Pang if coming from the south. Go west for approximately 40 km, where you would finally reach Batu Pahat. You can get around by bus, car, or taxi. If going by car or taxi, be aware of the ubiquitous one-way roads.

**Accommodations**

The 15-storey Katerina Hotel is the only four-star hotel in this city and is located in the commercial district center. It has 185 rooms, 10 of which are executive suites. Garden Hotel is another popular accommodation and is a walking distance away from prominent food centers and noodle houses. Other notable hotels are Carnival, Landmark, Pelican and Crystal Inn.

**Attractions**

All tourists visit the Dataran Penggaram, the famous monument of a hammer, chisel, and stone. Another attraction is Wet World, which is a 44-acre waterpark that&rsquo;s ideal for family outdoor activities. Built around Merdeka Lake, the park is open in the afternoons on weekdays and for the whole day on weekends and holidays. You won&rsquo;t miss the round-a-bout with a hundred-year-old tree. Then, there&rsquo;s Minyak Beku, the well-known seaside with a lighthouse and a historic well.

**Shopping**

As a manufacturing district, Batu Pahat offers a wide selection of products at great value, so you can shop till you drop and still have money to spend. It has four shopping complexes, namely, The Summit, Carrefour, Square One Shopping Mall, and Batu Pahat Mall. All of these have supermarkets, cinemas, restaurants, and clothing and specialty stores. You can also visit the popular night markets, which open in different streets depending on the day of the week. The stalls offer food, gift items, toys, souvenirs, clothes, and shoes.

**Eating Out **

Batu Pahat offers different kinds of food to suit different palates. Malay, Chinese, and Western food can be found in different parts of the city. The malls house Western fast-food chains. For sumptuous Chinese and Malay food, go to Ah See Wanton Noodles, Hai Kee Square, Minyak Beku, Beef Noodles, or Restoran Ming Choon.
