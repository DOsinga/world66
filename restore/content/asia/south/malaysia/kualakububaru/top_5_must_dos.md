# Top 5 Must Do's in Kuala Kubu Baru

*Asia > South > Malaysia > Kualakububaru > Top 5 Must Dos*

Make a list of **Kuala Kubu Baru Must Do&rsquo;s** while you are visiting as it will keep you relaxed by enjoying a hot spring or playing a round of golf or exhilarate you with treks to the beautiful waterfalls or a white water rafting trip. The possibilities are endless in Kuala Kubu Baru.

#### 1. Visit the Chiling Waterfall

If you are up for a long hike through the woods, a trek to Chiling Falls is worth it. The trail is clearly marked, however you have to cross the river about 5 times before you get to the lowest falls and the pool. As spectacular as this is, there are two more waterfalls higher up, with the highest waterfall a beautifully quiet place for a picnic.

#### 2. Go Water Rafting

For an adventure of a lifetime, take a white water rafting trip down the Chiling River in Kuala Kubu Baru. This 3 hour trip takes you through 9 different rapids and you can view some of the most beautiful scenery in Malaysia. If you are new to rafting, don&rsquo;t be nervous. Each group goes through a safety briefing and instruction on paddling techniques. Each person is also provided with a helmet and other gear. This is one trip you will never forget.

#### 3. Trek through the Jungle

Hiking through the Genting Highlands requires a lot of strength—mental and physical—but is totally worth the trip when can see Kuala Kabu Baru from the top. At the end of your hike, you will witness another of the beautiful waterfalls of the area, Sungai Sendat. Once there, you will find the perfect atmosphere for a swim and a picnic.

#### 4. Soak in the Ula Kalong Hot Spring

Hidden in the center of a palm oil plantation, you will find a small hot spring called Ula Kalong. It is a perfect way to soak your feet, or your whole body, after the long days of rafting or hiking. For a perfect relaxing soak, head to Ula Kalong.

#### 5. Play a Few Rounds of Golf

After spending the day at Fraser&rsquo;s Hills—a colonial station that offers beautiful views of the area—take a few hours and play 18 holes at KKB Golf Course or play 9 holes at Royal Fraser&rsquo;s Golf Club. Both offer challenging courses, scenic views and a variety of wild life to watch. For those who aren&rsquo;t avid golfers, there is also a horse paddock at the end of the course that offers horse riding for children and adults.
