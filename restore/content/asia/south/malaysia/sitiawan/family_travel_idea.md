# Family Travel Ideas in Sitiawan

*Asia > South > Malaysia > Sitiawan > Family Travel Idea*

**Sitiawan family travel **may seem surprising considering that Sitiawan has not always been considered a tourist-laden town,*** ***except for those looking for a feel of provincial life. Where once there were vast colonial houses which were pleasant sights to watch, it seems further settlement has left the houses unprotected and poorly-maintained. There are still many novel experiences to try in Sitiawan, if the family is adventurous enough.

**Visit the Different Plantations in the Area**

Sitiawan is rich in agricultural land, and it&rsquo;s unsurprising to see lots of plantations going to Sitiawan from Kuala Lumpur. If the kids are interested, try to stop by the different plantations with variations of rubber, palm oil, coconuts, vegetable patches and rice paddies.

**Try the Food**

Go on a food trip! Sitiawan&rsquo;s menu is filled with dishes reminiscent of its Huckchou upbringing, a tinge of Chinese with a dash of Malay. Try the Kampung Poh, Sweet and Sour Fish Maw Soup known locally as goroow, Kong Piang Buns, Kompiang and Mou Mou. Douse it down with red rice wine. Try some of the street food, too, such as the Fuzhou fish balls. The version of fish balls is slightly different here, as they're filled with ground pork or minced prawns, quite delicious especially with the special sauce.

**Visit the Town&rsquo;s Oldest Buildings**

You can also visit the Sitiawan Settlement Museum which chronicles the different stages of the life of the first Fuzhou settlers. This was once a 70-year old church that was later converted into a museum. There is also the Chinese Methodist Church and a Chinese School that&rsquo;s about a hundred years old. The school is still used today.

**Bike through the Town**

Sitiawan is an ideal site for some leisurely biking trips with the family. It&rsquo;s relatively near Kuala Lumpur and will be a relaxing ride going to this sleepy town. Most of the attractions in this town are reachable by bike, and getting there via pedal will add to the fun and excitement. Just make sure to bring the proper gear and some extra clothes as well.

**Learn How to Make Kompiang**

Kompiang is a thin biscuit with a fruity and custardy filling. It is a delicacy in Sitiawan and some of its well-known bakeries sell it. If you ask nicely, some of them might even give you a tour and some tips on how to make it. The making of Kompiang is still very natural, all done by hand, and then toasted in a hot stone oven.
