# When to Go in Sitiawan

*Asia > South > Malaysia > Sitiawan > When To Go*

If your travels takes you to Malaysia, the question in your mind may be about **Sitiawan **and** when to go**. Each season brings its own brand of weather, and it is always important when planning your travels to remember what the weather will be like when you arrive at your final destination. **Things to Think About Before Traveling**

Some people enjoy bird watching, while others plan their trip around the weather, or what produce and other foods will be in season. Others choose to travel based on the flora and fauna, so they will make sure to see the sights they desire to see in their peak. Then there are other tourists who plan their trip around the festivals found in Sitiawan.

**Sitiawan Holidays and Festivals**

There are many local traditions and festivals held throughout the year in Sitiawan. On the Fourteenth of June, Trooping the Colour is celebrated. Midsummer's Day is another June holiday, placed on the Twenty-Fourth of the month. Thaipusam is celebrated on January Thirtieth. Chinese New Year is celebrated as well, although the date changes every year.

Ching Ming is on the day of the third moon, which is one hundred and five days after the Winter Solstice. This is one of the bigger holidays, and is also known as the Grave-Sweeping Day as well as Spring Rememberance. The name literally means "clear and bright," which is the time of year when Chinese families celebrate by visiting the graves of their old ancestors and cleaning the area surrounded them. This is meant to show their respect for their dead relatives, and many families leave offering of food and drink for their ancestors to enjoy in their afterlife. This is celebrated all over Malaysia, but some of the most interesting places to see during this time are inside of Sitiawan.
