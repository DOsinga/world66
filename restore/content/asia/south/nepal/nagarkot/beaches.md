# Beaches in Nagarkot

*Asia > South > Nepal > Nagarkot > Beaches*

The nearest one tends to get to **Nagarkot beaches **is the nearby West Rapti River, which runs through the gorgeous scenery of the Himalayas and is fed by rain and subterranean water. The river heads south and then west into the well-known Rapti Dun Valley before turning south-east towards the braod plains of India.

**Messing About on the River**

There is no shortage of things to do on the river. You can enjoy a 30-minute canoe ride during where you will see people washing their clothes on the banks and kids swimming. You may also see crocodiles mooching around in the water. Not something you could do in a typical Nagarkot beaches experience, canoeing is definitely an activity to consider in this part of the world. The canoes are eight-seaters so you and a bunch of friends can get together and have a great time in them. If this mode of transport isn't your thing then you can take a lesiurely boat trip across the river, taking in all the sights only in more comfortable conditions.

**Washing and Elephant**

Nepal, and indeed all of the Indian subcontinent, is famous for its elephants. You can go to the West Rapti River and actually watch these graceful creatures bathing themselves in the cool waters. Elsewhere around the river basin you can see Bengal tigers in captivity, countless free birds and all kinds of other species in the various nature reserves.

**Fancy a Swim?**

The Nagarkot beaches experience wouldn't be complete without the opportunity to go swimming in the river. Locals like to do it before lunch to build up an appetite and cool off for the excellent food served. Do not to stray too far, though because there are elephants that frolick in the water.

**I'm Bushed**

Another great activity for an early morning is a bush walk near the river. It's possible to spot around 500 species of exotic birds, among them the Paradise Flycatcher, the Giant Hornbill and the Lesser Florican. In order to ensure that you see the best sights, you'll be accompanied by a specialist guide.

**All Along the Watchtower**

Perhaps the highlight of a Nagarkot beaches experience of the West Rapti River is taking advantage of Chitwan Jungle Lodge's amazing 22-feet-high watchtower that pokes up through the canopy of the jungle to allow you to look down upon the movements of animals that normally, on foot, you wouldn't be able to get near.
