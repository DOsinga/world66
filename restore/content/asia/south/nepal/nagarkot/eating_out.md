# Eating Out in Nagarkot

*Asia > South > Nepal > Nagarkot > Eating Out*

When visiting the mountaintop town of Nagarkot in Napal, you'll want to sample the local cuisine at **Nagarkot Restaurants.** To avoid problems with microbes that Westerners may not be adapted to, you may choose to eat in hotels and the restaurants attached to them. Restaurants in hotels are aware of the needs of travelers and strives to meet Western standards. They offer food that caters to the tastes of the foreigners—in other words, far less spicy. Here are some restaurants ideas to get you started. **
**

**Country Villa Hotel**

The Country Villa Hotel is one of the newer hotels that has emerged in Nagarkot in the recent years to cater to the growing tourism industry, and serve some of the best breakfasts in the area. The a la carte menu serves up continental classics such as toast, beans and eggs, but the buffet spread is the highlight of breakfast time here. Featuring Indian and Nepalese classics as part of the buffet, the spread is popular among tourists. Be sure to try the chickpea curry and dahl stew, a dish of curried lentils that is popular among locals for breakfast as well.

**Caf&eacute; du Mont**

Those longing for a taste of home in Nagarkot will want to check out the opening of the Caf&eacute; du Mont at the Peaceful Cottage. This little restaurant is located on the Hillstation, and can be a little tough to find for those unfamiliar with the area. Fortunately, the locals are keen to help. The menu is eclectic and packed full of different cuisines, from Nepali and Indian, to Chinese and Italian. As the name suggests, the Italian food here is a good option, with the pizzas and pastas being popular amongst the tourists who are in the area visiting the nearby Mahankal Temple. Compared to elsewhere, the prices here can be a little expensive, but at about US$10 per person, it is still much cheaper compared to home.

**Restaurant Kantipur**

The Restaurant Kantipur is found in the Club Himalayas, a five-star hotel resort that has been a large part of Nagarkot&rsquo;s tourism landscape for some time. Stepping in, the interior d&eacute;cor is beautiful, reminiscent of the ancient Nepalese king&rsquo;s court. The service staff speak wonderful English, and are always happy to explain unfamiliar items in the menu to guests. The kitchen specializes in Indian-inspired Nepalese cuisine, marrying the heavy spice flavors of the former, with the creamy sauces and light dumplings of the latter. Being part of a hotel means that this place is more expensive than normal, but the food is of a good quality and high standard, so it's money well spent.
