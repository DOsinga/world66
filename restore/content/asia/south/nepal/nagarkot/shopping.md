# Shopping in Nagarkot

*Asia > South > Nepal > Nagarkot > Shopping*

Nagarkot is the famous Nepalese mountaintop town, where beautiful views of the city below can be enjoyed together with the fresh mountain air. There are three Cs to remember of Nagarkot and its products, and here are some **Nagarkot Shopping **items.

**Cashmere**

Cashmere and pashmina are considered some of the most popular tourist-purchased items in Nepal these days, and there is no reason why it should not. Both materials are soft and warm, feeling rich and luxurious to touch. Both pashmina and cashmere are made out of the wool of two different types of mountain goats, precious only because each can be found at higher altitudes than normal goats. Pashmina is generally made into scarves or shawls, and dyed into a variety of colors with patterns woven on them to match every outfit. Cashmere, on the other hand, is made into scarves, and remains in the natural color of the goat after being woven. These materials have been considered as precious items to the royal family in the times of the monarchy, and are especially popular in Nagarkot, where the weather gets more chilly than normal.

**Ceramics**

Another item that is popular is the ceramic items in Nagarkot. Ceramics enjoy a long history, dating back from the times of kings and kingdoms. Plates of the farmers and workers were generally made out of ceramics fired a lower temperature, while those of regal statues enjoy plates made out of glazed ceramics, fired at a very high temperature for durability. Tourists now can visit one of Nagarkot&rsquo;s ceramic factories for a chance to see what it was like living in those days – and buying a slice of the past home in the form of plates, mugs or even vases. The ceramics and pottery of Nagarkot can also be seen in the marketplace, where preserved food items are stored in large ceramic vats and left to ferment for the winter.

**Craftwork**

Craftwork in Nepal is exceptional in general, and its finery can be admired in Nagarkot. Some of the finest arts here include traditional paintings, embroidery and wooden carvings of little animals and insects – all of which can be found scattered around the region. Simpler items such as wooden carvings and embroidered bookmarks can be found in the marketplaces, and these can be good souvenir or gift items for those back home. Looking for something a little more expensive and substantial? The woven carpets of Nargakot are world-renowned for being of a high quality, made from pashmina wool and woven by the Nepalese women through traditional handlooms. Each of these take up to several months to complete, and can be expensive, depending on the complexity of the design.
