# Travel Guide in Nagarkot

*Asia > South > Nepal > Nagarkot > Travel Guide*

This **Nagarkot Travel Guide** will offer you information that will help you plan your trip. Nagarkot is a sought-after mountain destination located roughly 32 km from Kathmandu in Nepal. Read on to find out more about this tourist destination

**Getting There**

Nagarkot is considered to be a village resort, and is located close to the valley of Kathmandu. You can fly in to the international airport, which is located 5.6 km from Kathmandu valley. International airlines like Etihad, Bahrain Air, Dragon Air, Air Arabia, and Thai Airways fly into this airport. Domestic carriers like Jet Airways, Kingfisher, and others operate flights to this airport. From the airport, you can travel by bus to Nagarkot. If you are looking for more comfort, then you can hire a car or a tourist taxi from Kathmandu.

**Sightseeing**

Nagarkot is a paradise for nature lovers. You can witness the majestic views of the Himalayan Mountains at this hilltop. During the months between October and March, you can see Mount Everest. The other peaks like Manaslu, Langtang, and Gauri Shankar offer visitors breathtaking views. You can witness the sunrise and sunset of this scenic destination. Visit the village of Tamang and experience the rural lifestyle in Nagarkot. You can go on a fun pony ride in this village or set out for some bird watching, yoga and meditation.

**Accommodation**

Because Nagarkot is a famous tourist destination in Nepal, it presents visitors with several lodging options. Budget travelers and those seeking luxury will both find suitable hotels here. The Nirvana Homes offer the budget-conscious traveler comfortable rooms at an inexpensive rate. In contrast, the Peaceful Cottage is a luxurious hotel, more like a resort, in Nagarkot.

**Getting Around**

This place is best explored by foot. Nagarkot is known for its scenic beauty and walking trails. Your hotel should be able to trace out a walking trail for you. There are also local taxis that you can rent out, but all major tourist spots in Nagarkot are accessible by foot.

**Dining**

You can sample the little-known Nepali cuisine in Nagarkot. The cuisine exhibits strong Tibetan and Indian influences. The most popular dish is called "thali," which comprises rice, lentil curry and vegetable curry. The restaurant in Sherpa Cottage is famous for its food. Sample the Tibetan noodles and the Swiss Rosti at this restaurant. The street food includes momos, roasted corn, and samosas. The streetside masala tea and sugarcane juice are major sources of refreshment here.
