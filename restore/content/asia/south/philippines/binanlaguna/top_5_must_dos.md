# Top 5 Must Do's in Binan Laguna

*Asia > South > Philippines > Binanlaguna > Top 5 Must Dos*

**Binan Laguna must do's **are varied, as there is plenty of family fun and nature to explore. Binan city is a newer city in the Laguna area near Manila. The Philippines is a great place to visit, as they have a rich island culture and a great climate. There are many different type of attractions, so there is something for everyone.

#### 1. Splash City

This is the largest water park in the Philippines, and it is located along the South Super Highway south of Manila in Binan. The water park has 12 different water attractions and was designed based off of the water parks found in the United States. There are 6 different water slides, which include Magellan&rsquo;s drop, King Pilipit, The Dos Sopremos, Twisted Palms, The Big Bam Boo and Taisig Trails. There are also 6 different attractions just for little children. There is also an interactive kiddy pool. All the rides have height and capability restrictions.

#### 2. Hot Springs

The entire area of Laguna contains many hot springs and waterfalls. These different hot springs will normally have a resort built on them. You can enjoy the warm water of a spring, which is soothing and promotes relaxation. One such resort is Hidden Valley Springs, which is in an inactive volcano. Several public hot spring resorts include the Villa Florencia, Villa Mejia resort, Crystal Springs and Makiling water parks.

#### 3. Mt. Makiling

The range of mountains in Mt. Makiling is very popular amoung hikers and mountain climbers. This is a resting volcano, which is covered with lush forests. Climbers can visit Banal na Lugar on Mr. Banahaw, which is supposedly a mystical place to those that make amulets and other faith healers. The slopes of the mountains look like a women in reclining positions and create a very mystical atmosphere.

#### 4. See the Raptors

You can see raptorial birds at the center for the Philippine Raptors. This is an excellent day trip. You can learn all about raptors and see the rescue and rehabilitation that goes on at the center. They also have a breeding center where they breed both nocturnal and daytime raptors.

#### 5. The Underground Cemetery

You may think that this destination is a little sombre, but it is a very historical sight. It was built by Franciscan Missionaries during the 18th century. The walls along the tombstones and the chapel are all covered with intricate designs. You can view many of the old tombstones that are in the brick walls. Supposedly, in this crypt is where the members of the revolutionary secret societies plotted and planned against the Spanish.
