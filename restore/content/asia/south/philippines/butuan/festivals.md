# Festivals in Butuan

*Asia > South > Philippines > Butuan > Festivals*

**Butuan festivals** have always been an intricate part of this Phillippine city's culture. This is a culture which enjoys parties for all sorts of occasions. The major events center around either ethnic or religious reasons, sometimes a little of both.

#### Religious Festivals

The Kahimunan Festival is one of the few festivals celebrated in the winter months. Every third Sunday during the month of January, the city celebrates their patron saint, Santo Nino or baby Jesus. The streets are lined up with dancers, entertainers and musicians. On this day, they ask for baby Jesus to bless their lives.

The city has a patroness named St. Anne who overlooks the Agusan River. They offer her a time of celebration on the last Sunday of July. This festival has dancers performing acts of thanks to her. The streets and venues are filled with traditional music.

#### Secular Festivals

Since this city gained independence, the Adlaw Hong Butuan festival has been celebrated every year. It consists of government and non-government organizations which bring honor to the present and past people who have been an important part of making Butuan a city. Activities include beauty pageants, floats, motorcades and local bands. There is a contest to see who can make the largest sticky rice cake.

A unique celebration in Butuan is called cultural festival/tourism consciousness week. It runs from the last week of July until early August. It is a time of all-out fun and partying. Tourists are encouraged to participate in dancing and singing.
