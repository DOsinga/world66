# Practical Information in Butuan

*Asia > South > Philippines > Butuan > Practical Informat*

This **Butuan Practical Information** guide tells you some important facts you need to keep in mind when traveling to this city located in the Philippines. Many people visit this city to experience its delicious local food, colorful festivals and good shopping.

**Travel Information**

Since Butuan is a multi-lingual city, the different languages spoken include Butuanon, Filipino, Tagalog, Surigaonon and Cebuano. English is also widely spoken. Thus, tourists will not have a problem communicating to locals in Butuan. The official currency of Butuan is the Philippine Peso (PHP). The population of Butuan has been estimated at 300,000 in 2008. With regards to safety, Butuan is a very safe place that is not embroiled in terrorism. Further, the crime rate is very low here. Do check if you passport is valid when traveling to Butuan. Secondly, you might need to apply for a visa to enter the country according to the purpose of your travel. Check with your embassy for these details.

**Climate**

During the months of March to September, Butuan experiences a dry climate, while October to February is considered as the wet season. During the wet season, Butuan experiences frequent rains and sometimes thunderstorms too. So, it is best that you travel to Butuan during the dry period from March through September, so that you can make the best of your trip without having to be stuck indoors.
