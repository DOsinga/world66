# Festivals in Camiguin Island

*Asia > South > Philippines > Camiguinisland > Festivals*

Nestled in the northern coast of the Mindanao region, Camiguin is known for its lovely beaches, hot springs, historical churches and colorful festivals, and every year, hundreds of visitors come to the island to witness famous **Camiguin Island festivals**.

**The Lanzones Festival**

Camiguin is primarily an agricultural province, and its main agricultural product is the small grape-size tropical fruit called lanzones. The Lanzones Festival is held on the third week of October to coincide with the harvest season. The festival starts with the opening of the exhibits and food fair at the municipal Mambajao, the main town of the island. The exhibits highlight the history and culture of the island, while the food fair showcases local cuisine and delicacies.

During the week-long celebration, you can make arrangements with some of the farms to join the local folks in harvesting the lanzones. To experience the culture and tradition of the island, come to the lanzones farms just before sunrise and witness the sacred thanksgiving rituals perform by some of the locals before the start of the harvest. These rituals have been performed by the locals for hundreds of years to thank the &ldquo;spirits&rdquo; and mystical beings in the island for a bountiful harvest.

The festival culminates with a colorful parade and dancing on the streets of Mambajao. The parade and street-dancing usually start late in the morning and end late in the afternoon. The street dancing features dancers from different towns around the island and the neighboring provinces. Visitors can also join the dancers and have fun on the streets. Street parties with lots of food and dancing continue late into the night in some areas of the town.
