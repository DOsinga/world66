# Things to Do in Dagupan City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Dagupancity > Things To Do*

You might be interested in adding these to your **Dagupan City things to do **list. Dagupan is an exciting place in and it is rich with history that dates back to the time when locals traded with the Spanish and the Chinese traders who docked on its shores. Here are a few recommendations.

**Dagupan's Wet Markets**

You can head over to the Malimgas Public Market and buy from the market the famous &ldquo;kape&rdquo;- a freshwater crab which is a local exotic delicacy. Have it cooked for lunch Dagupan-style and enjoy it with calamansi and steamed rice. The end result is worth the effort of going to the market and buying the crab.

**Bonuan Beach **

Take a trip to Bonuan Beach and feel the sea breeze. This is where locals flock to simply have a dip or chill at the beachfront. The experience is more enjoyable if you take Dagupan's famous bangus (milkfish) grilled to perfection and enjoy it while sitting at the beach. You might also want to try out the water sport activities at the beach.

**Tour West Central School**

Take a tour of the Home Economics West Central Elementary School. This school was transformed into a headquarters by General MacArthur when he arrived in Dagupan. At present, the local government is looking into turning it into a museum. You can also visit the Shrine of the Unknown Soldier, which was erected to pay tribute to the soldiers who fought during World War I. You can also take beautiful pictures at the Japan-Philippine Friendship Garden that was built to symbolize the peace that has been established between Japan and the Philippines.

**Visit St. John's Cathedral
**

Take a trip to St. John's Cathedral while you are in Dagupan. This is where the Spanish general surrendered to General Francisco Makabulos, the Filipino Military Strategist during the Philippine Revolution. This is also the place where Jose Rizal's girlfriend, Leonor Rivera, wed a foreign engineer named Henry Kipping.

**Food Trips**

One of the other things you can do in Dagupan is try out the city's unique food selections. If you have a taste for food adventure then head down to Galvan Street and try the pigar-pigar stalls. There you can find the famous pigar-pigar, a local delicacy with a very simple recipe and realize there is a distinct taste to it that can only be found in Dagupan.
