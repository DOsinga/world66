# Nightlife and Entertainment in Koronadal City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Koronadalcity > Nightlife And Ente*

**Koronadal City nightlife** offers a wide variety of things to amuse yourself with. A popular tourist spot within the Philippines is Koronadal City, which is also known as Marbel, and is the capital of South Cotabato province. One census given in 2000 stated the city had a population of 133,786 people that lived in 27,623 houses. The city itself is located at the foothills of the Roxas and Quezon mountain ranges, giving the city the illusion of being a crown jewel set at the bottom of a landlocked crater. The best time to go to Korondal City depends on the types of things you prefer to do. It is a typhoon-free city, so typhoons are not a factor when thinking on travel time. In the months of March and June the weather tends to heat up and it becomes dry, while rains pour in July through October. The time in between November and February is alternatively cool and dry, leaving citizens and tourists wearing light casual clothing.

There are many types of things that are open during the night, such as bars, pubs, discos and there are even certain festivals and recreational activities that happen at night. Even certain hotels offer such things as karaoke rooms and disco bars. One of the most amazing things to see is their night festivals.

**Charter Anniversary**

This is a memorial celebration of the city of Koronadal and is set on October 8. Included in these night fests are beer and food as well as concerts, trade fairs and a celebration much like Mardi Gras.

**Hinugyaw Festival**

This is a week-long merry-making festival which includes beer and food, festive drum beats, concerts provided by celebrities as well as locals, sidewalk bargain shopping, trade exhibits, fun-fairs as well as the Hinugyaw Street Dancing Competition. This is celebrated from January 3 to 30.

**Tinalak Festival**

The city of Koronadal celebrates this on July 18 with the government as the anniversary of South Cotabato. This is also known as the T'nalak Festival.

There are many things to entertain you with in the Koronadal City nightlife, including these festivals and much more. Other things you might find enjoyable are their many pubs and bars, as well as discotheques and many other nightly festivals and fairs.
