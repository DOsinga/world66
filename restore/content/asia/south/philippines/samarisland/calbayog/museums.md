# Museums in Calbayog

*Asia > South > Philippines > Samarisland > Calbayog > Museums*

There are several interesting **Calbayog museums** you should not miss if you are planning to take a trip to Samar Island. Other museums are available in the neighboring areas like Leyte, connected to Samar Island via one of the longest bridges in the Philippines.

#### Samar Archeological Museum and Research Center

The Samar Archeological Museum and Research Center is situated on Christ the King College in Calbayog City. The museum was opened in 1970 and was the first museum on the entire Samar Island. It features numerous collections and exhibits of archeological finds. These artifacts include several notable pieces which can be traced back centuries. For example, there are jars and covers, log coffins, saucers and dishes dated to the 14th century. The Samar Archeological Museum and Research Center is also home to coins, household items, heirlooms and some religious items including chalices and altars.

#### Calbayog Diocesan Museum

The Calbayog Diocesan Museum opened in April 2010 in celebration of the 100th anniversary of Calbayog City. The museum was part of the centennial celebration. The museum is situated in the Calbayog Diocesan Centennial Building on the ground floor.

#### Sto. Ni&ntilde;o Shrine and Heritage Museum

The Sto. Ni&ntilde;o Shrine and Heritage Museum is located on Real Street in Tacloban City in Leyte. The shrine is one of the 29 rest houses built by former Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos. The shrine was built for Imelda Marcos, his wife, who was born in Tacloban. It is one of the most visited destinations in the city because of its association with Imelda Marcos.

The museum features numerous paintings depicting the historical events of Leyte and extravagant furniture and fixtures of the former palace. The entire house is furnished with numerous antique pieces from all over the world, including Austrian mirrors in almost every room. You will also find some of the collections of the Marcos family, like Italian tiles, Chinese jars, carpets from Argentina and chandeliers from the Czech Republic. Explore the entire house and check out the different rooms which are decorated in different themes. One thing the rooms have in common is a signed picture of Ferdinand Marcos. There are some diorama of Imelda Marcos&rsquo; work. The museum is open daily from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. A tour guide is required inside the museum.

#### People&rsquo;s Center and Library

Also located in Tacloban is the People&rsquo;s Center and Library. The center features historical documents about the culture of Samar and Leyte. It has a display of dioramas of the 82 different ethnic groups in the country as well as an extensive collection of humanities books. Aside from the artifacts, the People&rsquo;s Center and Library also has a reading room, gymnasium and social hall.
