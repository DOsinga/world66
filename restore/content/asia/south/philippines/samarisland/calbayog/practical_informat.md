# Practical Information in Calbayog

*Asia > South > Philippines > Samarisland > Calbayog > Practical Informat*

Following is a **Calbayog practical information** guide that highlights important aspects of this Philippines city. It would help tourists to know more about Calbayog and enjoy a better holiday there.

**Area and Population

**Calbayog extends over an area of about 903 square km. This is quite large compared to the total area on which the Samar province stretches. The city is made up of 157 barangays (districts) and is the largest of all the cities in Eastern Visayas. According to a census carried out in 2007, the city&rsquo;s population accounts to 28,912 households and 163,657 people.

**Language

**The people in Calbayog speak different languages including Tagalog, Boholano, Cebuano and English. Some of the Samarenos also speak in the local dialect of Waray, which is also known as Samar-Leyte.

**Currency

**The currency used in Calbayog is Philippines peso, the same currency used all over the country. Its currency code is PHP and conversion rate is 46.33 PHP = $1 approximately.

**Travel Requirements

**Any traveler to Calbayog has to comply with the general Philippine traveling requirements. Tourists should have valid passports, return tickets and visas. American citizens are allowed to stay in Calbayog without a visa, but this is only for a span of 21 days.

**Safety

**Calbayog is a relatively safe and orderly city mainly because of the combined efforts of the AFP and PNP. A lot of support has also been provided by local government units and non-government organizations to combat threat groups and criminality which maintain the levels of peace and normality in the city. However, tourists should always stay safe, and avoid venturing out late in the night alone and in lonely areas.
