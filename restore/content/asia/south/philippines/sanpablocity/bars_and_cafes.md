# Bars and Cafes in San Pablo City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Sanpablocity > Bars And Cafes*

More laidback than the big Filipino metropolises of Manila or Cebu, **San Pablo bars and cafes** will delight travelers of all tastes and persuasions. From rocking live music venues to chilled out little coffee houses, there's something for everyone in this idyllic environment.

**Dub City Cafe**

Boasting an eclectic mix of live and DJed music from modern electronica to 80s pop, and from punk and new wave to 70s rock, this great nightspot sells just as diverse a selection of alcoholic drinks and traditional Filipino food and snacks. Dub City Cafe is billed as an alternative to the somewhat sleazy and cheesy bars that seem to dominate this part of the country. The bar itself is full of interesting pop cultural knick knacks, such as a model of the shark from Jaws and posters of The Godfather, Citizen Kane and Iggy Pop. Aside from the parties that go on to past 1am, Dub City Cafe is a more chilled out venue earlier in the day when it serves tea, coffee and other non-alcoholic beverages.

**Sulyap Gallery Cafe**

Certainly one of the more cultural and intellectual San Pablo bars and cafes, the Sulyap is divided into a 2 story ex-hotel housing an art gallery where you can purchase antiques such as wood carvings, religious icons and wind breakers. There is also a 2 story Spanish-style colonial house wherein is located the cafe, which has a lovely historical vibe to it thanks to the restoration of the furniture and fittings that have taken place. Here, you can feast on beef mechado, a local stewed beef in tomato delicacy, large river prawns cooked in a clay pot, and sugpo, which is prawns cooked in a peanut sauce. Many clients have reported on the excellent ambiance of the place, and the Sulyap is a favorite destination for young couples.

**Clydenjosh**

Located on Lutong Bahay overlooking the gorgeously picturesque Sampaloc Lake, this venue reeks of ambiance. It is built entirely from bamboo and serves local seafood and fish. It is a favourite of travelers and locals alike, offering a quite unique location that simply cannot be matched by other San Pablo City bars and cafes. This is perhaps more of a cafe/eating place than a bar, so don't expect all night drinking sessions or loud music.

**La Pizzeria**

Anyone who has been to the Philippines knows how obsessed the country is with pizza. La Pizzeria in Total Gas Station, Barangay San Rafael is the place not only to eat pizza, but to drink beer and be seen. There are also great pasta dishes and free wi-fi for all patrons.
