# Day Trips in San Pablo City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Sanpablocity > Day Trips*

An ideal way to make **San Pablo City day trips** more interesting is to travel to areas that are away from the bustling metropolis of the city. San Pablo is one of the most commercialized cities in the Laguna province of Philippines. However, it is also among Philippines&rsquo; most historic city and presents plenty of entertaining options. Some of the most popular options for San Pablo City Day Trips have been listed below.

**Biking, Rafting Trips in Sampaloc Lake**

In many travel guides, San Pablo is quoted as the City of Seven Lakes owing to the presence of seven, natural lakes. The Sampaloc Lake is huge, spreading across more than 100 hectares in circumference. It is also the largest of the city&rsquo;s seven lakes. Further, it is least affected by the usual, daytime buzz of San Pablo&rsquo;s commercial activities. Rather than boating in the lake, visitors can rent a bike and ride along the outer circumference of the lake during the early, morning hours. Rental bikes with sidecars are easily available at cheap rates. The biking trips also provide an opportunity to visit the beautiful, green stretch along the outer edge of the city. Here, visitors can bike along dedicated biking paths and get a great view of the outer islands. Another option is to rent a bamboo raft. A bit more tedious than cycling, rafting is more enjoyable too. Visitors can raft for a few hours and head towards the Lake Shore for a brunch at the local eateries here. Most of the rafts are rented from outlets located along the Barangay Wawa. Upon their returns, visitors can view the fishing farms that are packed along the east end of the lake, facing the Banahaw Mountain.

**Visit the Bato **

The Bato Resort provides a unique blend of adventurous and relaxing activities. It sources the icy-cold waters from the nearby, Mount Banahaw. Within the resort, visitors can relax under two, artificially-made waterfalls. Further, a swimming pool can be rented by groups or families. Those who like to explore a bit can look forward to dipping into the underwater grottos or caves present beneath the waterfalls. Some dedicated diving spots have also been created. The entry-fee is very economical.

**Location: **Barangay San Cristobal, near the Calamba, when exiting San Pablo City.

**Explore, Trek the Pandin & Yambo**

The Pandin Lake largely considered as the ideal, picnic spot for families. It is frequented by the citizens of San Pablo over the weekends. Impeccably-maintained, it is situated just about 10 minutes to the northern edge of San Pablo, along the Nagcarlan town. Visitors can hire a jeepney (local cab) from any spot near the cathedral in San Pablo and head towards the Pandin. From here, it is possible to climb along the ridge and head towards the adjoining Yambo Lake. An essential part of any daytrip here should include tasting the Buko Pie that is sold at Collette's Pie. This is the traditional, Filipino-style pastry and most locals believe that the early-morning batch is perhaps the best. Visitors can also undertake a trek to the low-lying hills from the Yambo. These treks are completed within a day and are organized by professional mountaineers.
