# Getting Around in San Pablo City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Sanpablocity > Getting Around*

**San Pablo City Transportation** is as reserved as the city itself. San Pablo City is also called the city of seven lakes. Before reaching San Pablo, the starting point of the journey is always in Manila, Philippines.

**Cars and Buses**

Getting to San Pablo City is easy with the use of cars. You can rent a car if you are an international tourist. You can also opt to ride the public buses, which will cost you around $5 for a one-way trip to San Pablo City, which is way cheaper than renting a car.

**Bicycles **

Once you get to your destination you might want to consider taking a bicycle. Biking around the lake is the best way to see the scenery of the lakes and this is also a good form of exercise.

**Tricycles**

Aside from your typical bicycle you can also rent a more common mode of transportation in the Philippines, a three-wheeled bike which provides extra seating with a sidecar attached to it. Flagging a tricycle to take you to your destination is also an option; this will cost you around 15 cents.

**Float on Sticks**

Floating on sticks is unique since the city will allow you to enjoy time in the lake. This is a 10- to 30-minute tour in Yamboo and Pardin, on top of a bamboo raft. You will want to bring a camera to take photos of the tour around the lake. These bamboo rafts are safe, so there is no need to worry and a round trip ride costs $6.

**In the Air**

If you have extra cash to burn, rent a helicopter and cruise around the city to see San Pablo from the air. This might cost you more than the other forms of transport, but this is one experience you will definitely enjoy. You can also go pooling with this method since a helicopter ride will allow up to 3 or 4 passengers. This way you get to divide the cost among your friends. The price tag of this awesome ride is around $1,000.
