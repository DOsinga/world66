# Practical Information in San Pablo City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Sanpablocity > Practical Informat*

Hopefully this **San Pablo City Practical Information** will give you a better view to the culture and way of life of the local residents. Here are some interesting details about the city which you may find useful.

**Population**

San Pablo City is the fifth biggest city in the province of Laguna, Philippines. It is home to almost 250,000 people, most of them belonging to the Tagalog ethnic group, which is the biggest in the Philippines, being over 20% of the country population. The Tagalogs are very open-minded to Western culture and are heavily influenced by it. They are very polite and respectful people.

**Language**

A number of languages can be heard in San Pablo City. English is widespread, as it is one of the official languages in the Philippines. You can hear traditional Tagalog dialects, as well as the official Filipino language. The official language is actually very similar to the Tagalog, which is the base it was built on. Except for Tagalog, Filipino language borrows many words from Spanish and English.

**Religion**

Most of the people here are Roman Catholics, although there is a significant number of Protestants as well. However, mysticism is still very popular in the area and people's beliefs are strongly influenced by different pagan understandings. For example, in distinction to the traditional Christian religion, the people here believe that after death, the body returns to the four "elements of life": earth, water, fire and air. Secondary burials are frequently practiced as well.

**Economy**

Many of the residents of San Pablo City are agriculturists. This is a very traditional activity for Tagalog people, as they have a certain amount of agricultural experience passed on from the older generations. San Pablo City is a constantly developing eco-tourism destination and some of the local people are involved with that as well. The city is home to showrooms and service centers of many popular Asian car manufacturers.

**Currency
**

Finally, here's some useful currency information. The official local currency is called Philipinne Peso (or Piso). Each Peso has 100 centavos (or sentimos in Filipino). One U.S. dollar is approximately equal to 46 Philippine Pesos. An interesting fact is that the similarity of the 1 peso coin to 1 dirham, 2 &euro; and 25 cent coins has caused many vending machine frauds in the U.A.E., U.S. and Europe.
