# Sights in San Pablo City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Sanpablocity > Sights*

Founded by Cabrera in 1678 San Pablo city, also known as Sampolac, is located in the Laguna region of Philippines. San Pablo earned its city status in 1940. Perched on the slope of Mt. Marayap, San Pablo city is around 150 meters above sea level. The livelihood of the city people mostly depends on copra making. **San Pablo Sights** offer diverse natural beauty as well as typical Philippine lifestyle. Some of the must-see places are mentioned as below:

**AERA Memorial Museum**

AERA Memorial Museum, having been built by Arserio and Rosario in 1923, inherited its name from the acronym of its founders. The museum is an exact replica of the mesmerizing Church of San Francisco at Manila which was completely destroyed during the liberation movement of Philippines. The museum has diverse collection of religious artifacts, oil paintings, full-size dioramas of tribes, traditional attire, antique furniture and a huge collection of coins and currency notes. One of the big halls captures moments of Philippine freedom movement, displaying paintings, suits, dresses and models belonging to that era. The biological section of the memorial this museum features a huge collection of Philippine-African beetles and more than 22,000 butterflies. The outside courtyard is decorated with World War II warfare equipment, including canons, tanks and different type of rifles.

**Villa Escudero**

Situated at the outskirts of San Pablo city, Villa Escudero has immense historical significance in Philippine War of Independence. This region, originally owned by Placido Escudero and his wife Claudia during the late 18th century, was transformed into an 800-hectare coconut plantation. This place was later used to give shelter to revolutionaries during the war against Spanish soldiers and again during the War of Independence against the Americans. Arsenio Escudero created and organized a great guerilla force here which was operated successfully during the War of Independence against the Americans. The beautiful coconut plantation along the highway on the backdrop of Mt. Banahaw has made this place a favorite among the tourists.

**Sampaloc Lake and Other Lakes**

This lake is one of the most visited places in San Pablo. This one-km-long and quite deep lake is a great place for picnic or a nice cool evening walk. The lake is encircled by a nice broad concrete road which is used as the walkway to view the beauty of the lake from all sides. There are many other lakes in and around San Pablo city which are worth a visit. Calibato, Mohicap and Malabunot Lakes are more secluded and less congested than Sampaloc Lake and offer tourists a calmness of nature.
