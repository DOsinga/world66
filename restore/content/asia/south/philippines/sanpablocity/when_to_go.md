# When to Go in San Pablo City

**Climate
**

The local climate consists of two seasons – tag-init and tag-ulan. Tag-init is the hot and dry season from November to April, while Tag-ulan is the cooler rainy season from May to October. Strong monsoon winds rage throughout the year. Depending on the different seasons, temperatures vary from 21 degrees C to 32 degrees C, with the coolest month being January and the warmest – May.

**Coconut Festival
**

Also commonly referred to as the Coco Festival, this is an annual event started in 1996, which takes place in the middle of January each year. The festival lasts one week and celebrates the abundance of coconuts in San Pablo, since they are one of the main sources of income for the local residents. Accompanying the Coconut Festival are different competitions, live music performances and a festival market.

**Mayflower Festival**

The Mayflower Festival (also called Flores de Mayo or Flores de Maria) is another annual event held each May. It has religious roots and honors the Blessed Virgin Mary by offering flowers to her. The flowers are offered by little girls, wearing special clothes. The night before the offering, a parade takes place on the city streets, as well. Similar festivals are held in many other places all around the world, but the many events accompanying this one (like boat races, bike marathons and tricycle competitions) make it really special.

**Festival of Lights
**

Each Christmas, the residents of San Pablo decorate their houses and yards with many colorful lights. That&rsquo;s the reason why the days around Christmas are called a Festival of Lights. The festival itself is just a celebration of Christmas. However, the sight is really marvelous, especially at night, when the whole city shines. The decorations stay for a while after Christmas too, so you have plenty of time to enjoy them.

**October Festival
**

An annual festival held in October, this is nothing more than a large scale trade fair, promoting products of the local residents and testing their skills in different competitions. It is held near the beautiful lake of Sampaloc, which is a very popular place in San Pablo.
