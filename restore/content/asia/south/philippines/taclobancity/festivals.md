# Festivals in Tacloban City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Taclobancity > Festivals*

**Tacloban City festivals** are being celebrated annually, and they are put on to commemorate the city&rsquo;s foundation day or to honor the patron saint of the town. The patron saint is believed to have continuously provided and showered the town with abundant blessings. With that, it is vital to thank the patron saint by having such festivals.

**Pintados Festival**

Pintados Festival is derived from the word &ldquo;pintados&rdquo; which means &ldquo;the painted ones&rdquo;. It is celebrated every 29th day of June. This festival started in the year 1987. The Pintados Festival is distinctive compared to other festivities in the Philippines. The participants in this festival are painted from head to toe. During the celebration, the street is full of people who are fully painted and dancing in the streets. Moreover, the festival also commemorates some parts of the pre-Spanish civilization of the Philippines, like the local epics and the native folk region.

**Sangyaw Festival**

Sangyaw Festival is the other festival celebrated in the city of Tacloban. Sangyaw is drawn from the local word for &ldquo;herald&rdquo;. It is celebrated on the 29th day of June, as with the Pintados Festival celebration. Imelda Marcos, the former Philippine First Lady, conceptualized this festival on the year 1974. However, it was discontinued by the people when the Marcos family was involved in a hstorical controversial issue. In the year 2008, the Sangyaw Festival made its comeback through the decision of the Tacloban City mayor to revive it. Now, the Sangyaw Festival is celebrated along with the Pintados Festival celebration.
