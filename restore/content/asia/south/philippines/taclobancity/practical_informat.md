# Practical Information in Tacloban City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Taclobancity > Practical Informat*

When visiting **Tacloban City, practical information** will help you get the most out of your travels to this Eastern Visayas region of the Philippines. Tacloban city is strategically located on the island of Leyte and is the largest city in this part of Eastern Visayas. The city is well known for its jet-black sandy beaches and other adrenalin inducing extreme sports such as wind surfing, kayaking, rock climbing and the likes. Here is some practical information that will help you plan and execute your vacation.

**Visa Requirements**

The Philippines is one of the few countries in the world that does not require a visa for foreign tourists. As long as your stay in the Philippines does not exceed 21 days and you hold valid tickets for your home journey. As a foreign visitor, you are required to have a passport that is valid for more than 6 months from the date of entry. If you intend to stay more than 21 days in Tacloban City, you can do so after applying for an extension of stay at the Philippine Bureau of Immigration for a minimal fee.

Passport holders of Macau, Hong Kong and Britain are only allowed up to 7 days stay in Tacloban City without a visa. While, citizens of Brazil and Israel can stay as long as 59 days in the city without a visa.

**Currency**

Tacloban city uses the Philippine currency, Philippine Peso (PHP). Where, US$1 is approximately PHP 47.00 as of June 10, 2010.
