# Sights in Tuguegarao City

*Asia > South > Philippines > Tuguegaraocity > Sights*

First-time visitors to Tuguegarao might find it to be too chaotic, particularly when compared to the metropolitan culture of Manila. However, beyond its choked markets and busy commercial buildings, **Tuguegarao City sights **have lots to offer. Tuguegarao is also the capital of Cagayan—a major, commercial province of Philippines. Some of the best Tuguegarao City sights are presented below:

**Popular Churches and Cathedrals**

Among the most popular sites in Tuguegarao is the city&rsquo;s Archdiocese, which is comprised of the Saints Peter Church and Paul Cathedral. The church is reminiscent of the European style of constructing churches. It has a huge belfry that is also seen among 18th-century churches built across Europe. This church dates back to the era of Spanish occupation of Philippines. Not many people know but the Paul Cathedral is also depicted at the Basilica of Rome. It is counted among the most exotically-built cathedrals of south Asia.

Another building of religious significance is the Basilica of Our Lady of Piat. It is also called the Basilica of Our Lady of Visitacion. This basilica is located on the outskirts of Tuguegarao, about 33 km from its northern boundary. This basilica is considered nearly four centuries old. The image of Our Lady here is also referred to as the Black Mary. Among the Christian minorities settled in the outer islands of Philippines, the Piat is regarded as the most sacred place of worship. Another popular church is that of San Jacinto Ermita. This church was built in the early part of the 17th century. The church was partially reconstructed during the Second World War.

**Callao Caves**

These caves are among the few adventurous sites that Tuguegarao offers. The caves are comprised of seven chambers that are nearly impossible to navigate for first-time visitors. The chambers lead into limestone, cave-like formations. Some of the cave-walls have intricate designs. However, none of these patterns are handcrafted—they have been mysteriously formed by the forces of nature, over a period of several centuries. The entrance to the cave itself is a bit of dare, wherein visitors need to climb steeply-inclined, 187 steps. There is a small chapel too inside the Callao Caves complex.

**Buntun Bridge**

This bride is located about 2.5 km from the main city of Tuguegarao. The bridge extends a bit more than 1 km in length. It connects the city of Tuguegarao with other municipalities like the Apayao Province. The Buntun is the second-largest bridge in Philippines. The bridge offers an amazing view of the Cagayan River.

**Other Tuguegarao City Sights**

Visitors can also visit the Hornos. Easily-accessible during the daytime, this public sculpture was built during the 18th century. Another recommended daytime site is the Calvary Hills. Located about 16 km from the central part of Tuguegarao, this site consists of small, rolling hills. The sculptures here depict the suffering of Jesus Christ before his crucifixion.
