# Top 5 Must Do's in Cha Am

*Asia > South > Thailand > Chaam > Top 5 Must Dos*

Most people visit Cha Am for its tranquil beaches. However, there is much more to this beautiful city that its scenic beach-fronts, and the following list of **Cha Am Must Do's** gives an insight into the multitude of things that can be pursued when you visit Cha Am.

**1. Phetchaburi&rsquo;s Wildlife Parks**

This is an ideal daytime trip. Phetchaburi is a little known hamlet, about 30 km from the northern end of Cha Am. Phetchaburi is increasingly getting popular for its wildlife parks. The two most popular parks here are the Phetchaburi National Park and the Kaeng-Krachan National Park. The Phetchaburi National Park is among the largest national parks in Thailand and showcases all the native animals. This park is situated along a historical site—the caves of Khao Wang, and has an old, spooky palace in the background. The Kaeng-Krachan National Park is popular among bird-watchers. It provides an opportunity to view some of the rarest, migratory birds found in Asia.

**2. Wat Cha Am Temple**

This sacred temple is located in the western part of Cha-Am. The temple is a bit hard-to-spot since it is concealed within a rock cave. The temple is truly historic and the image of Buddha inside it is supposed to be many centuries old. Visitors need to climb a long series of steps and some undulating pathways to reach the entrance of the cave. After returning from the temple, visitors can buy some seasonal fruits from the Cha Am Fresh Market.

**Directions:** the temple is accessible from the main beachfront of the city. Visitors can also ask for the Narathip Road or the Phetkasem Road. Upon reaching either of the roads, head towards the main traffic junction. The temple is located just a couple of turnings away. The fresh market is located on the opposite end of the main, Phetkasem Road, in the direction of the train station.

**3. Hat Puk&rsquo;s Sculpture**

This is among the more unusual of Cha Am&rsquo;s many beachfronts. It is also called the Puk Tian beach. The beach is surrounded by many fictional characters, engraved in sand and stone. These characters were created by a popular, native poet and have now become a local legend. The sculptures serve as a unique background for tourists to click photographs.

**Directions:** this beachfront is located near the Cha Am Temple, a few meters from the Bascule Bridge.

**4. Maruekhathayawan Palace**

This famous palace is also called the Mrigadayavan Palace. Unlike some of the other, historic palaces in Thailand, it was built rather recently in 1923. The palace is situated on the boundary, separating Cha Am from Hua Hin. This palace was built as a summer palace for the royal family of Thailand. In order to keep the palace complex cool and prevent the ants from entering it, a unique system of waterways was created. Small streams of water have been sourced through the pillars that circumvent the entire palace. This palace is often referred to as the &lsquo;Palace of Love and Hope&rsquo;. Its most popular section is the huge pavilion made from a rare variety of teak.

**Directions:** the palace is located along the outskirts of Cha Am, about 9 km south of the city&rsquo;s southern border. The palace is open every day, except on Wednesdays.

**Times: **8.30 a.m. to 4.30 p.m.

**5. Picnic at Cha Am Forest Park**

This park is one of the few green covers you will find in the main part of the city. There are some native animals too, including the Thai monkeys and peacocks. The park serves as the perfect resting spot for visitors tired from their exploration of Cha Am. Inside the park, visitors can munch on some affordable, local delicacies. The small restaurant is the perfect family spot with a dedicated karaoke section. The park authorities also rent-out tents to groups looking to picnic throughout the daytime. There is no admission fee and there are plenty of small ponds on the outer edge of the park. Visitors need to get to the park early in the morning since it is difficult to seek entry once the morning batch of tourists starts arriving.

**Directions: **along the southern end of Phetkasem Road, near the Narathip Road junction.
