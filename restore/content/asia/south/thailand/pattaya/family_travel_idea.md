# Family Travel Ideas in Pattaya

*Asia > South > Thailand > Pattaya > Family Travel Idea*

**Pattaya family travel **will take you on many fun, exciting and fantastic adventures. There is, as with any popular tourist resort, the seaside, but in Pattaya, family travel has many more options, and many more attractions.

#### Wildlife Attractions

The Crocodile Farm is Pattaya&rsquo;s number-one family attraction. It has live crocodiles and daring shows. Check out the snake show which is only a short taxi ride from the city center.

The whole family can relax and enjoy some spectacular sights in Nong Nuch Tropical Gardens or visit the giant Golden Buddha statue on Pratumnak Hill. The Golden Buddha has free entry, and Buddha statues do not come any more beautiful than this one. Especially for parents, Pattaya&rsquo;s (almost) best-kept secret is in the huge area of Silver Lake. There is an amazing vineyard and winery, where jazz and blues concerts are held, horse and chariot tours are available and the surrounding scenery will leave you breathless. Family fun can be found in the Royal Garden Plaza on the beachfront, at the scary Ripley&rsquo;s Believe It Or Not Museum.

Other family favorites are Pattaya Elephant Village and the Sriracha Tiger Zoo. Underwater World Theme Park is a must see for those with children, as it is the largest and most modern ocean aquarium in Asia, and it features dozens of species of marine life. Mini Siam and Mini World are also essential attractions to see, with the displays of more than 100 mini models of important Thai historical sites.

#### Splash Out With All The Family

Pattaya Park, at the top of Thrappaya Road just before Jomtien Beach, offers access to the sea and sand of the seaside. Here you will find all the water you want, with swimming pools, whirlpools and gigantic and smaller waterslides. It is a cheap and fun way to keep the family happy. In Pattaya Park, when hunger gets the better of you, visit the revolving restaurant at the top of one of Asia&rsquo;s highest seaside towers with its stunning views as far as the eye can see.

#### **Traveling in Pataya**

If you have no wish to hire a car but would like a bit of independence getting around Pattaya, use the open taxis, called "baht buses," which run to an arranged route all over the city or which can be individually hired to take the whole family on a private tour of Pattaya. They are extremely cheap and a fun way to see the city.
