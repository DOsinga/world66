# Top 5 Must Do's in Hyderabad

*Asia > Southasia > India > Andhrapradesh > Hyderabad > Top 5 Must Dos*

Marking **Hyderabad must do's** on the plan when visiting this
region of [India](/asia/southasia/india)is a must for every traveler. Travel to India is exciting and it
can take weeks to explore the cities. Hyderabad offers a mixture of modern and
ancient learning experiences to explore. Architecture in Hyderabad is
magnificent and provides all day excursions.

**Charminar**

Built in the Islamic architecture style, this edifice has four carved
minarets with four stories each. Walk under this major landmark in the city of
Hyderabad. The detail is romantic as you move closer to the important landmark.
Built for a tribute to Hussain, it marks the end surrounding a plague epidemic
in the 1500s. The surrounding area of Charminar offers rich cultural heritage
and shopping.

**Golconda Fort**

Golconda Fort is a magnificent fort to explore. Easily spend all day
discovering the nooks and intricate details this 13th century structure offers.
Palaces line the structure and the wall expands for over 11 kilometers. Complete
with a light and sound show, this makes a feature of Hyderabad not to miss.

See the highest point at Bala Hissar. Rahban cannon and the water system of
supply are located within the fort as among the major attractions. The tombs of
the Qutub Shahi Kings feature beautiful architecture from Islam, located along
the outer wall of the Golconda Fort. Beautiful expansive gardens encircle the
wall.

**Chowmahalla Palace**

Chowmahalla Palace is not just one palace but four. See the complex that is
open to the public today. Beautifully lit inside the Durbar Hall featuring 19
chandeliers makes a spectacular entrance to this palace. Visit the regal hall to
see the royal seat, known as the Takht-e-Nishan. Domes and arches with Persian
influence line the expanse of this palace.

The Clock Tower houses the Khilwat Clock, which is still ticking to this day.
Council Hall houses rare manuscripts and books that were used by the Nizam in
their day. Exhibitions and treasures now are located in the Council Hall for the
public to see. View the beautiful architecture and decoration of the four
palaces for an experience while in Hyderabad.

**Birla Mandir**

Lord Venkateshwara’s Hindu temple stands magnificently carved from white
marble. Built upon a 280 feet hill named Naubath Bahad, this 13 acre expanse has
the character deities engraved upon the temple walls. Walk up a flight of
extrinsically carved stairs to view the temple. See the Hussain Sagar panoramic
view from the height while visiting this blend of Indian architectures.

**Necklace Road**

Take a break from visiting the monuments and stroll down Necklace Road. Eat
at the local restaurants for traditional [Hyderabad cuisine](/asia/southasia/india/andhrapradesh/hyderabad/eating_out), a blend of hot and
spicy food. View the beautiful lawns that line the road and visit the NTR
Gardens. Sanjivaiah Park is also located here for a walk in nature. At certain
[times of the year](/asia/southasia/india/andhrapradesh/hyderabad/festivals), the People’s Plaza has exhibitions, sports, music, and games
to entertain you.
