# Visiting monastaries near Leh

*Asia > Southasia > India > Jammuandkashmir > Ladakh > Leh > Thingstodo > Visiting Monastari*

**Alchi Gompa**

This extensive buddhist temple and monastic complex contains at least 4 major buddhist temples dating back about 500AD. The main feature are the stunning multicoloured clay statues some 2-3 stories high preserved for over a thousand years. Du-Khang, or temple, that enshrines Mahayana images. Beside it to the east is Sum-tsek, one of the few complete preserved sites not destroyed by the invading muslim armies of the east for the glory of its sculptures, paintings, and unique architectural features. Further east is Lha-khang Soma and to the north-west of the Du-khang are the shrines Lotsawa Lha-khang and Manjushri Lha-khang.

**Likir Gompa**

About 60km from Leh The magnificent gompa at Likir, known as the Klu-kkhyol Gompa, was founded in the 14th century, and was the first in Ladakh known to have been built under the direction of Tibetan monks. It is still an important site and is the home of one of the most senior monks in Ladakh.
