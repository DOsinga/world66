# Top 5 Must Do's in Vaishnodevi

*Asia > Southasia > India > Jammuandkashmir > Vaishnodevi > Top 5 Must Dos*

**Vaishnodevi Must Do's** include activities and sightseeing for
both pilgrims and tourists. Nestled in Trikuta Mountains at a height of
5200 feet above sea level, Vaishnodevi region has awesome natural beauty
and sanctity in the air that fills every visitor here with devotion.

**Trek to the Holy Shrine**

Trip to Vaishnodevi is incomplete without the trek to the Holy Shrine
which is about 13 km from Katra. Devotees can go on foot, pony or
palanquin. There are food arrangements and rest rooms for the pilgrims
convenience throughout the trek. The trek has destinations like
Banganga, Charan Paduka, Ardh Kumari, Bhairon Temple which are must
visit during the pilgrimage.

**Catch on the Aarti**

Aarti of the Goddess is the sacred ceremony performed twice a day,
during early sunrise and immediately after sunset, just outside the Holy
Cave. It is the ritual in which The Godess name is chanted 108 times.
It is considered very auspicious to attend the ceremony during
pilgrimage to Vaishnodevi. The glimpse of the deity remains suspending
during this holy ritual. After aarti the devotees are distributed holy
water and Prasadam.

**Temple Tour in Katra**

Katra located in the foothills of Vaishnodevi, is home to many
ancient temples which are must visit during Vaishnodevi tour. The most
renowned are Shiv Khori which is The Holy Cave of Lord Shiva, Baba
Dhansar Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Dera Baba Banda, a Hindu-Sikh
pilgrimage destination. Pilgrims complete their tour after making visit
in these temples.

**Shop for Souvenirs**

Pilgrims are always interested in taking Souvenirs of their
pilgrimage. Katra Bus Stand has several small souvenir shops that sell
audio cassettes, CDs, shawls, laminated picture of The Goddess, chunri,
bangles and many more items at a very nominal price.

**Taste Pure Vegetarian cuisines**

Pilgrimage destination in India offers strictly vegetarian food.
Therefore Vaishnodevi is no exception. Even the voluntary food
association that distributes foods free of cost to the pilgrims during
the Vaishnodevi trek to The Holy Shrine is strictly vegetarian. Here is a
chance to taste pure vegetarian food right from North Indian, South
Indian to Chinese and other continental dishes.
