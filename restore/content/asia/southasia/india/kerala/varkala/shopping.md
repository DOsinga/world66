# Shopping in Varkala

*Asia > Southasia > India > Kerala > Varkala > Shopping*

**Varkala shopping** is an experience that will fascinate the
traveler. There is no shortage of souvenirs to buy, both unique and the
not so unique. Wherever one looks there are shops and open air markets
to be strolled through and enjoyed. Varkala shopping is anything but
boring. With lively entertainment and the excitement of bazaars and
small shops, Varkala shopping couldn't be more fun and exciting.

**Beach Bazaars**

From the time the shops on the beach open for business in the morning
until they close late in the evening, the beach bazaars are a great
place to shop. Interesting items include artifacts and those made of
coir or carved horns. The entire state of Kerala is well-known for
beautifully and masterfully carved horns. The beach bazaars are
definitely the place to find shopping adventures and delightful
entertainment.

**Seaside Cliffs**

Seaside Cliffs not only provide food and drink but little shops as
well. Aimed more to the tourist, prices in cliff shops may be slightly
higher than on the beach. The visitor can find unique Indian crafts,
clothing and much more in these little shops that are located among the
restaurants and bars along the cliffs. While shopping, enjoy the view, a
brew and some good Indian cuisine. Look for great deals on spices and
coffee, and crawl around the bookstalls for some great reading material.

**Khadi Store**

Khadi Store is jewel of a store that has clothing, hair oils, cooking
oils, food, leather goods and honey. If the visitor is looking for some
great Khadi wear this is the place to find it. The traveler can do a
little grocery shopping as well for a picnic at the beach.

**Tibetan Shops**

Don't over look these special shops. The visitor can find traditional
jewelry and ornaments as well as local attire. "Shop ‘til you drop" and
find treasures that are worth taking home to friends and families or
treasures that are just great decorations for your home. In these little
shops one can find great items from Rajasthan and Kashmir.

Brand names are not in abundance in Varkala. Some bargaining is
allowed and it really is up to the individual. Don't be afraid to haggle
over prices for local handicrafts and other products. Language can be a
barrier, so use sign language or other methods of communication to help
with the bargaining process.
