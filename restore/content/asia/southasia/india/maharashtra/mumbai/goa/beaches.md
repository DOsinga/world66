# Beaches in Goa

*Asia > Southasia > India > Maharashtra > Mumbai > Goa > Beaches*

The area of Goa in India is well known for its many beaches.
All along the west side of Goa are beautiful beaches from secluded places to
relax to crowded places to party. Almost
all beaches have different vendors scattered around for food and drinks. Some hotels and resorts are built right on
the beach for ease of accessibility.
There are many fun things to do on all the beaches of Goa and with the
abundance of different kinds of beaches to choose from. You should be able to
find lots of different activities to fill your beachside vacation.

**Calangute Beach**

Calangute Beach is very popular and busy. There are a lot of visitors to this beach
every year making it a good choice if you’re looking to be around people. Here you can enjoy lots of different sports
activities, food, and entertainment. The
different water sports you can enjoy in Calangute include parasailing, water
skiing and wind surfing.

**Anujuna Beach**

Anjuna is another popular beach more known for its
nightlife. Here, the beach is turned
into a rave party late for celebrations. The beach is also lined with bars and clubs that keep the drinks coming
and the music flowing. You can enjoy
late night parties here or in the day time the beach is nice for sunbathing and
taking long walks. The sands are pretty
and clean as is the waters.

**Baga Beach**

Baga beach is a smaller beach right off of Calangute. The beach is more scenic and a little less
densely populated. Here people can enjoy
some water sports and fishing. It is
also better suited for swimming.
Nightlife here can be pretty entertaining as well with Tito’s, a dance
club right on the beach that travelers frequent.

**Dona Paula Beach**

Dona Paula beach is apparently haunted by Dona Paula de
Menezes. According to the legend the
Viceroy’s daughter took her own life after objections from her family over her
affair with a poor fisherman. Another legend
says that she was pushed off the cliff as punishment for seducing the Count of
Alvor. She is supposed to still be seen
at night coming from the waves with a pearl necklace around her neck. Other than the ghost stories, people visit
this beach for the crystal waters and wide variety of water sports.

There are lots of different beaches to visit in Goa. For more secluded beaches try getting off the
beaten path but always stay safe and listen to the locals for tips.
