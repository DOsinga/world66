# Things to do in Rameshwaram

*Asia > Southasia > India > Tamilnadu > Rameshwaram > Things To Do*

On the island of Rameshwaram, one can find a good deal of **Rameshwaram
Things to Do**. Hordes of tourists come to Rameshwaram as it is a
very famous pilgrimage destination and is known as one of the holiest
places in India. Here is a list of things to do that should not be
missed out on your itinerary when in Rameshwaram.

**Rameshwaram Temple**

A visit to Rameshwaram is definitely not complete without a visit to
the magnificent Rameshwaram Temple. It is regarded as one of the most
sacred sites of the Hindus since this temple houses one of the 12
"Jyotirlingams" in India and is also closely associated with the
legendary Ramayana epic. The architecture of this temple is simply
stupendous and proves to be a treat for photographers.

**Sethu Karai**

Sethu Karai lies around 20 km before the island of Rameshwaram. It is
believed that God Ram built a bridge made from floating stones from
here leading up to Rameshwaram. One should also not miss out on visiting
the famous five faceted Hanuman Temple. The temple also houses statues
of Rama, Sita and Laxman, besides the main Hanuman deity. Located
outside the temple is the mysterious floating stone that according to
history and mythology was used to create the above mentioned bridge.

**Dhanushkodi**

Dhanushkodi is a small village that lies at the southernmost part of
Rameshwaram. There is a lot of historical evidence and myths associated
with Dhanushkodi. In fact, a number of rocks that can be seen in a line
show evidence of a bridge known as Rama's Bridge. Dhanushkodi also has a
very clean and pristine beach, which should not be skipped when
visiting this place.

**Gandhamadhana Parvatham**

This temple is located on the very top of a small hill. Legend has it
that Lord Rama leaped over to Sri Lanka from here. The Gandhamadhana
Parvatham temple sprawls two floors. From the upper level, one is able
to spot the Ramanathaswamy Temple from a distance. The view is also
picturesque and is definitely worth a visit.

**Bathe in Holy Waters**

While in Rameshwaram, don't forget to take a holy dip in the sacred
waters that border the Ramanathaswamy temple. This holy dip is commonly
referred to as Agni Theertham. In fact, on the auspicious day of "Thai
Amavasya", thousands of pilgrims come from all over India to partake in
this holy dip.
