# Getting There

*Asia > Southasia > India > Westbengal > Durgapur > Getting There*

**From Calcutta** starting in the wee hours of the morning (6:00 am
is really early for most Bengalis), you have trains coming to Durgapur
at regular intervals. Frequency slows down a bit in the afternoon
though. So at 6:05 am you have the Shatabdi Express that takes you
non-stop to Durgapur in about 1hr and 45 mins. At 6:10 you have Black
Diamond Express: takes 3 hours (because it travels on the main line).
After that, you have a bunch of long distance trains that stop at
Durgapur: you may have to pay a higher price for the tickets. In the
afternoons you have Mayurakshi Fast passenger leaving at 4:30 pm (not
recommended unless you are a cheap bloke. The daily passengers there
will make your life hell). Right on its heels Coalfield Express,
Asansol Express, Kalka Mail.....

By Road, if you are coming from the Central or Southern part of
Calcutta (Chowringhee and south), use the Vidyasagar Setu to cross over
to Howrah and keep going till you hit Bombay road. Turn right and stay
on Bombay Road to reach Delhi Road (NH2). Take NH2 towards Durgapur
(also known as Durgapur Expressway). I drove mostly at 120 Km/hr, with
two stops (should not need them), you should be home in about 1:45 mins.

From North Calcutta, currently you will have to take the Bally Bridge
(Dunlop,Dakshineswar etc.) and continue on to Kona where you hit
Durgapur Expressway.

**
From North Bengal** (Siliguri, Malda etc) You can avail one of the
nany trains that go to Sealdah. Get down at Burdwan and you will have
to change trains to get back to Durgapur. It is about 45-50 mins train
ride from Burdwan to Durgapur. I have also heard of a train that goes
through Durgapur to Asansol and then turns North. Not sure what it is
though.

The Best bet is by road. Catch the Rocket(yes thats what they call it
there!!!) bus to Durgapur. Stops at a couple of places. Comfortable
journey if you are not sitting in the back seat.

**
From West (Delhi,Patna** etc): With low cost carriers like Air
Deccan, Spice Jet etc., you might wanna fly in to Calcutta from Delhi
and then either drive or ride a train to Durgapur. However, my personal
preference is to ride the Sealdah Rajdhani Express from New Delhi to
Durgapur. Very comfortable journey. Get up in the evening in Delhi and
after a nice nap at night, you are at Durgapur at 7 am. Otherwise,
there are a mutitude of trains: Poorva Exp, Kalka Mail, Toofan(don't
unless you have to) etc.
