# Dhampus-Hiking around Pokhara Travel Guide

*Asia > Southasia > Nepal > Dhampushiking Aro*

Dhampus is a beautiful village in the foothills of the Annapurna
Himalaya. The ridge top at 1,600 m offers close up view of Annapurna and
Manaslu range with layers of green hills towards south. Dhampus offers
glimpse into the typical Nepalese countryside lifestyle and culture. It
still retains charming stone houses with thatch or slate roofing running
down the terraced farms. The village is a gateway to Annapurna
Conservation Area. The largest conservation area of Nepal provides
popular multi-days' trekking to Annapurna base camp, Mustang, Jomsom and
Muktinath and more.
Dhampus has got beautiful family run lodges as
well as luxury lodges to spend night and enjoy the majestic Himalayas.
The village could be accessed by road these days. It is about 26 km from
Pokhara but the last 8 km is winding dirt road up the mountain.
Hiking
is still the best way to enjoy the nature and culture. One can drive to
Dhampus and take a hiking trip to 'Australian camp' in the forest.
'Australian camp' at 2,060 m is a lodge settlements in the forested
ridge above Dhampus. One can also plan 2 nights 3 days hiking with stay
in Australian camp and Astam village.
