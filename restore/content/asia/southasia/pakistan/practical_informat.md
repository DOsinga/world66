Other languages
Nearly all Pakistanis speak mutually related provincial Indo-Iranian languages of the Indo-European family. The most widely spoken is Punjabi, followed by Pashto, Sindhi and Balochi. Other Indo-European languages spoken in Pakistan include [Seraiki](/asia/southasia/pakistan/practical_informat/seraiki), Dari, Hindko, Pothohari, Gujarathi, Shina, Wakhi, Kashmiri, Khowar and many others. In addition, small groups of non-Indo-European languages are also spoken including Brahui which is a Dravidian language and Burushaski which is a language isolate.
