# Getting There

*Asia > Southeastasia > Brunei > Bandarseribegawan > Getting There > Getting There*

Bandar Seri Begawan is the capital of Brunei. It is a small place with fantastic sight seeing opportunities.
By Air
This is the easiest way of getting to Bandar Seri Begawan. The Brunei International Airport serves the entire country, which is very small. Many airlines including Royal Brunei Airlines, Singapore Airlines, Malaysia Airlines, AirAsia, and SilkAir operate flights to this place. The airport is quite close to the city center and one can reach the city center in 10 minutes by taxi from the airport.
By Road
There is a road from Sarawak in Malaysia to Bandar Seri Begawan. You need to cross international borders, so be prepared with your papers. You can either drive down to Bandar Seri Begawan or take a bus to the city. There are buses that run from Sarawak to Bandar Seri Begawan.
By Water
There are speed boats and ferries that operate between Bandar Seri Begawan and Sarawak. Most of these leave only when full, so give yourself plenty of time to reach your destination. The chief ferry terminal in Brunei is the Serasa Ferry Terminal at Muara. There are many ferries every day to/from Labuan and one ferry a day each to/from Lawas and Sundar, both in Sarawak. The ferries are an easy means of transport to this country from Malaysia.
There are no trains to Bandar Seri Begawan. You have to choose between air, road, and boat to reach this city in Brunei. From Malaysia you can travel to this country either by boat or by road.
