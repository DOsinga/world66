# Shopping in Bandar Seri Begawan

*Asia > Southeastasia > Brunei > Bandarseribegawan > Shopping*

Bandar Seri Begawan, being the capital city of Brunei, offers a great
shopping experience. Electronic goods are sold at the same price as in
Singapore. They also offer great deals at the busy shopping malls that
the locals visit. Apart from the Brunei dollar, you can also use the
Singapore dollar in this country.

**The Tamu **

This is the place to go to if you want ethnic artifacts to take home
as gifts. They sell original, locally made baskets and other items.
They also sell imported jewelry.

**Hua Ho Supermarket **

This is where the locals shop. It is a self catering department store
and has almost every conceivable item in it.

**Paul & Elizabeth Book Services**

If you are looking for English language books, this is the place for
you. They stock paperbacks and guides in English.

** Gadong
Shopping Center **

Gadong Shopping Center is a very new and a modern mall consisting of
three floors, one of which is devoted entirely to food and a large
cinema. You will enjoy sitting down to eat in the air conditioned food
court. After resting, you can go back to shopping to your
heart's content in this center that is one of the best in Brunei.

** Yayasan
Sultan Haji Hassanal Bolkiah Complex **

This is the largest shopping center in Brunei. There is ample parking
facility and it's the location of many up market boutiques and shops.
If you want to purchase exclusive, high-end products at steep prices,
this is the place to visit.

**What to Look for**

You should look for elegant earthenware curios, gilded mementos,
trendy yet timeless furniture designs, Chinese urns, wall hangings,
classic brocade upholstery, exotic Indian artifacts, highly coveted
South Asian haute couture pieces and the latest electronics in Brunei.
You will find these things in surprising places – either in a small
store or a glittering department store. Visit the local market for stuff
that you can pick up inexpensively to take as souvenirs of your trip to
Brunei. If you are shopping at the smaller stores, remember that
bargaining is the norm.

One can purchase traditional items such as, "jong sarat" as excellent
souvenirs. These can be obtained at the Arts and Handcrafts Center
which is located off Kota Batu and also at the airport. Getting local
products can be more challenging but also more interesting.
