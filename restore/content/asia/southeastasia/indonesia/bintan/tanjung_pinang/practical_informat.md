# Practical Information in Tanjung Pinang

*Asia > Southeastasia > Indonesia > Bintan > Tanjung Pinang > Practical Informat*

Visiting unfamiliar places like Tanjung Pinang is a great adventure;
however, you will need a few **Tanjung Pinang practical information**
tips to make your journey safe and worth while.

**Climate, Geography and Time Zone**

Tanjung Pinang is situated on the southern part of Bintan Island,
Indonesia. Tanjung Pinang has a tropical climate, and its rainy season is
from October to March. From April to September it is normally dry.

Tanjung Pinang falls in the Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) +7 time zone.
Thus, it is 13 hours ahead of the Eastern Standard Time (EST) time zone.

**Visa and Passport**

If you are visiting Tanjung Pinang, your passport will need at least 6 months validity after your arrival. You
will also be required to have a return ticket and a tourist visa. The
Indonesian government issues visas on arrival, so you will be given a
visa when you arrive in Indonesia.

**Customs**

You are not allowed to carry any deadly weapons, prohibited drugs or
pornographic materials into Indonesia and this applies to all cities in
Indonesia. You are required to declare all expensive and valuable
possessions and register the same at the customs. There are no
limitations on exporting and importing foreign currencies. However, you
are not allowed to import or export more than Rp. 5,000,000, crocodile
skin, ivory or tortoise shells. You are permitted to carry the
following duty free products in limited quantities: 50 cigars, 100gms of
tobacco, 200 cigarettes, 1 liter of alcohol (if you are above
18 years old) and a reasonable quantity of perfume.

**People and Language**

Most people residing in Tanjung Pinang are Muslims so it will be
helpful if you learn or understand several ideas about their religion and culture.
The official language is Bahasa Indonesia although there are few who can
speak English. It will be advisable to carry a pocket translation book because this will be helpful in communicating with the locals.

**Emergency**

In case you encounter any hardship and an accident, you can dial the
following numbers for assistance:
Ambulance: 118 Police: 110 Fire: 113 Search & Rescue: 111/115/151
