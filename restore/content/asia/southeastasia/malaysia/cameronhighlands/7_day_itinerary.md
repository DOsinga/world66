# 7 Day Itinerary in Cameron highlands

*Asia > Southeastasia > Malaysia > Cameronhighlands > 7 Day Itinerary*

Cameron Highlands has everything you could ask for in a holiday destination, especially when you follow this suggested** Cameron Highlands Itinerary**. Whether your family enjoys simple activities such as strolling in gardens and farms, or extreme outdoor adventures like hiking through the jungle, Cameron Highlands has something for you.

**Day 1- Rose Garden**

The beautiful rose garden is considered as the biggest rose-growing centre in Cameron Highlands, with hundreds of varieties of roses blooming. For romantic couples, it is the place to get all the roses you want for your loved one. You can also enjoy a breathtaking view of the Kea Farm from the rose garden.

**Day 2- Cactus Valley**

One of the major tourist attractions in Cameron Highlands is the Cactus Valley. There are several different varieties of cacti that are grown here in the valley. This is a great place visitors can explore and learn about the different cacti species, a few of which are known to be 60 years old.

**Day 3- Butterfly Farm**

Another worthwhile attraction at Cameron Highlands is the Butterfly Farm. Step into this place, and you will feel as though you were in a very colorful world, with a rainbow of butterflies fluttering from one flower to the next. The “Rajah Brooke” is the star butterfly of this place because it is uniquely large with gorgeous black and green wings. A guide will accompany you to explain the various species.

**Day 4- Jungle Trail **

Thousands of visitors visit the Jungle Trail each year. This is a great place to spend the day, surrounded the natural beauty and cool temperature of the trail. Hit the jungles early to avoid afternoon showers and to enjoy the rainforest in the warm sunshine. Of the seven trails, the Parit Waterfall Trail is the best place for a family picnic.

**Day 5- Shopping at Ringlet**

Cameron Highlands shopping is not about the fabulous dresses; rather, it is all about flowers, vegetables, plants, tea and fruits. Preserved insects, wooden handicrafts, and rattan baskets are unique souvenirs that visitors usually buy in various marketplaces, particularly in the town of Ringlet. So go and pick up some of that special tea that you do not get back home.

**Day 6- Galleria Time Tunnel**

Take a trip to the Time Tunnel. This is the local museum where you can learn about the history of Cameron Highlands and take a journey to the past through the many old photographs displayed.

**Day 7- Tea Plantation **

One of the main attractions in Cameron Highlands is the Tea Plantation. You can visit the factories to learn about the tea-making process and enjoy a cup of tea at the café. No trip to Cameron Highlands is complete without a trip to this famous plantation.
