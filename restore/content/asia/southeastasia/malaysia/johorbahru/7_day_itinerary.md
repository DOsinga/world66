# 7 Day Itinerary in Johor Bahru

*Asia > Southeastasia > Malaysia > Johorbahru > 7 Day Itinerary*

Johor Bahru is one of the most popular tourist destinations
in Malaysia. Given its rich history and proximity to Singapore, the city is a
good place to enjoy your holiday. The following is an itinerary you could use
for a week in Johor Bahru.

**Day 1 ****
Sultan Abu Bakar
Royal Palace Museum**

Start your first day by visiting the Sultan Abu Bakar Palace
Museum. This is the former residence of the Sultan of Johor, and although it's still
used occasionally, it is open to the public. The palace has been refurbished on
several occasions but still holds its visitors in awe. It's open
Saturday to Thursday 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.; there is an entrance fee of $7 for
foreigners.**

Day 2****
Johor Bahru Art
Gallery**

Spend a day visiting the Johor Bahru Art Gallery. The
building was built in 1910 and displays several art works, but mostly Malaysian
paintings. Admission to the art gallery is free.**

Day 3****
Saleng Zoo**

A day at the zoo is not only relaxing but educational as well.
Spend a day at this private zoo and enjoy the various animals and species that
are housed here. The zoo is very well maintained and is located close to Senai
Town.**

Day 4****
Tanjung Puteri Resort
Golf Course**

This is one of the largest golf courses in Johor Bahru. The
course is owned by the resort and is one of the best places to enjoy a day
playing golf. The course is professionally designed to give you maximum
pleasure while playing your game.**

Day 5****
Water Sports**

Enjoy a day out with the whole family at Dan’s Dive Base.
This is one of the most popular water sport bases in Johor Bahru. Activities
include scuba diving, snorkeling, sailing and much more. The base is open from
morning to evening.**

Day 6****
Shopping**

Now that you’ve had a chance to visit and enjoy parts of
Johor Bahru, it's time for some shopping. There are several shopping malls in
Johor Bahru, a few examples are: JARO Handicrafts Center, City Square and AEON
Tebrau City. Remember, you can bargain at many places and get some good
discounts.**
**

**Day 7
Endau-Rompin National
Park**

A trip to Johor Bahru is incomplete without visiting the
Endau Rompin National park. The park is the oldest tropical rainforest in the
world and stretches over 488 square km. There are various activities at
the park. It's open Monday to Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
