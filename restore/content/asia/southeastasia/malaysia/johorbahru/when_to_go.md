# When to Go in Johor Bahru

*Asia > Southeastasia > Malaysia > Johorbahru > When To Go*

Johor Bahru can be visited any time of year, but there are benefits to visiting in certain months, depending on your reasons for traveling.

**Climate

**

Johor Bahru has a tropical climate with warm weather and rain all year round. High temperatures average at 86 F and low temperatures in the 70'sF. Humidity is high, and it is recommended to wear clothing that is suited to the heat. The temperature drops at night. The time of year with the most favorable weather conditions is April to October. However, this is also when prices are at their highest and there are more tourists.

**Monsoon Season

**

Monsoon season runs from November until January. During this period Johor Bahru experiences heavy rain. If you travel during this time pack clothing suited to rainy weather, including a raincoat. Benefits to traveling during this season are lower prices and less tourists.

**Peak Season **

Major public holidays can become busy in Johor Bahru, and tourists from surrounding countries tend to visit Malasia at this time. Christmas and New Year are times when Johor Bahru becomes especially crowded. School holidays, which run from June to August, brings many visitors to Jahor Bahru.

**Sale Season

**

For visitors to Johor Bahru that are avid shoppers sale season is July to August. The end of year sale begins in November and continues until January. This is a busy time, where people flock from all over to enjoy the sales, with many tourists from the Middle East.

**
Festivals

**

Johor Bahru has a plentitude of festivals, which are mainly based on religious dates. These festivals are a good way to experience Malaysia's rich cultural history and religious beliefs. The Mooncake and Lantern Festival is held in September and has its origin in China. During this festival Jahor Bahru is adorned with lanterns to celebrate Malaysia's success. Parades are held during this time. Hungry Ghost Festival is a Chinese-based festival held on the 15th of the 7th month, according to the Chinese calender. During this day it is believed that the ghosts of the dead return to earth. Rituals are performed to appease these ghosts. Food offerings are made to the ghosts in homes and temples. Paper cut outs of vehicles and houses are also burnt as an offering. Merdeka Day marks Malaysia's independence and is held on 31st August. Parades fill the streets and there are firework displays. Plays are performed that re-inact Malaysia's history.
