# Nightlife and Entertainment in Kota bharu

*Asia > Southeastasia > Malaysia > Kotabharu > Nightlife And Ente*

The **Kota Bharu nightlife** and entertainment are somethings to
look forward to in Malaysia. Exploring any city or country is great in
the daytime when one wishes to see notable museums, temples and the
general way of life that abounds in the area. However, the nightlife and
entertainment scene of any city is also worth getting to know whenever
one visits a place. It is a chance to get to know and mingle with
locals, taste their local brews and blends of alcohol and simply have
party and unwind. Kota Bharu is a popular spot in Malaysia, noted for
its beaches and its museums. However, let’s take a look at the night and
party scene of the city.
**Understanding the Culture and Religion
**

Before you complain about the lack of nightclubs and bars in the
whole of Kota Bharu, there are a few things you will need to understand
about the city. Kota Bharu, like most of the cities in Malaysia, is
ruled by the PAS, or the Islamic Party of Malaysia. Therefore, socially,
it is deemed very conservative. The majority of Kota Bharu’s
inhabitants and locals are of Muslim faith; therefore, their culture and
religious actions must never be questioned or taken against them.

If you are visiting Kota Bharu and are a non-Muslim female, you don’t
have to dress like one (with a scarf covering your hair); however, it
is only courteous and may be imperative that women and men should wear
clothing that covers the legs and the entire trunk completely. Also,
women are encouraged to cover their arms as well. Muslims pray several
times a day, so be prepared to acknowledge breaks in public places, like
markets during the noted prayer times of Islam.
**Nightlife**

While there are no popular dance clubs in the area, you can still get
a feel of how the nightlife is in Kota Bharu by visiting night markets.
In night markets, you can go from stall to stall and buy local goods
and products. You can also rest and have dinner or a snack there.
**Cafes**

If you would like to enjoy a night out relaxing with a cup of coffee,
look for kopitiams, or cafes. Many of these offer free or paid Wi-Fi. A
popular spot is the riverside Pelangi Mall, where one can enjoy fresh
brewed coffee while admiring the river view.
**Serving Alcohol **

If you are in need of a quick liquor fix, you can head on to some
Chinese restaurants where beer and wine are served only to non-Muslims.

A popular watering hole in Kota Bharu named Restoran, has interiors
like a disco, but people mainly come here to drink more than to dance
and party. Other sites have “Thai drink promoters” who encourage
visitors to try local blends and brew.

Soon Lee Agency is not a bar, but sells wines, spirits and beers.
