# Shopping in Kota bharu

*Asia > Southeastasia > Malaysia > Kotabharu > Shopping*

**Kotabharu shopping** experiences can be enjoyed by all as the
city is bursting with vibrant market life, whether it’s in the morning
or night. Kotabharu crafts are made by hand, and it will be worth
visiting several markets to check out the most stunning pieces of
clothing, gemstones, kites and other things one can take home to
remember the trip. If you have decided to go shopping in the city, here
are some places which are sure to give you a great time.
**Pasar Siti Khadijah**

If you are in for a more traditional feel of shopping in the city,
then you should definitely visit the Pasar Siti Khadijah, otherwise
known as the Kotabharu Central Market. Everything you can think of is
sold here. Many vendors are women. Markets like the Pasar Siti Khadijah
are great for discovering local goods and products as well as having a
taste of local cuisine and sncack sold by sidewalk vendors.
**Nasi Kerabu and Ayan Percik**

These are two notable markets which are frequented by travelers and
locals. Nasi Kerabu exemplifies the hustle and bustle of market life of
Kotabharu. Stalls in this market offer a place to dine for lunch. Ayam
Percik, a night market, is as alive as any daytime market, with
delicious meals to try and native products and goods to buy.
**KBTC (Kota Bharu Trade Center)**

The more modern shopper will appreciate the KBTC because it is the
biggest mall in the city, and it just opened its doors to the public
during August 2009. The KBTC is composed of two buildings which are
linked on the 2nd floor. One of the most popular things travelers like
to buy when visiting the trade center are hand-printed batiks. These are
beautiful clothes with diverse designs, and there are plenty of options
to choose from. Other places to buy batik-designed clothes include the
Citra Batik near the PCB Beach and the town’s KB Mall.
**Other Shopping Sites**

Can’t get enough of Kotabharu shopping? You can continue your
shopping escapade by visiting the KB Mall, Pantai Timur, The Store,
Pelangi Mall, Kota Seri Mutiara, and the newly opened Nirwana Maju.
There’s also a Tesco hypermarket in the main Kotabharu city, which was
launched in 2008. The metropolitan area of Kotabharu is slowly filling
with multi-level shopping centers and malls which are currently being
constructed to replace the more traditional ones. Locals are awaiting
the grand opening of two Mydin Mall branches and a giant hypermarket.
