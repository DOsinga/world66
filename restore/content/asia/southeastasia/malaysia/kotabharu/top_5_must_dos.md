# Top 5 Must Do's in Kota bharu

*Asia > Southeastasia > Malaysia > Kotabharu > Top 5 Must Dos*

A list of **Kotabharu must do's** is not limited to sightseeing.
Kotabharu is a city in the Southeast Asian country of Malaysia. Within
Malaysia, it is in the northeastern part of the Malaysian peninsula. It
is a large city, with a population which is approaching half a million
inhabitants.
**1. Visit Mosques**

Among the most obvious of the Kotabharu must-do’s is to visit some of
the many mosques. The majority of the population in this particular
city is Muslim. The Islamic principles are performed vigilantly by
almost everybody. For example, there are prayer calls which bring the
entire city to a complete stand still while prayer is carried out. It is
also reasonably common to receive a fine for inappropriate dress. In
common with many other mosques, the examples in this city are quite
spectacular but make sure you observe all the regulations and customs
when visiting them.
**2. Go Shopping**

In common with other parts of Southeast Asia. most notably Singapore,
Kotabharu is a good place to go for shopping. Particularly appropriate
places include Pasar Siti Khadijah and Kotabharu Trade Center. The
former is a market where many vendors sell a wide variety of different
wares; the latter is a modern and extremely large shopping mall. The
city is also home to a number of other shopping centers.
**3. Enjoy SCUBA Diving**

Wreck diving is a relatively recent activity in Kotabharu but worthy
of inclusion on a list of Kotabharu must-do’s. The major attraction here
is the IJN Awazisan Maru, which was a Japanese ship that sank during
fighting in the Pacific during War War II. As is the case with many
other good wreck dives, the site itself provides an extremely good
environment for a wide variety of sea life. The warm climate in the area
is another factor that makes diving in the region a good activity.
**4. Taste Buds Enjoyment**

Another worthwhile activity for the tourist who is visiting the town
of Kotabharu is to sample the local cuisine. The area is well-known for
its wide array of good local food. Good quality dishes are available
from a large number of street vendors. The prices tend to be extremely
cheap and make the streets in the area rich with many different scents
and smells. Eating in some of these is another of the Katabharu-must
do’s.
**5. Visit the Old Royal Palace**

A visit to Katabharu cannot be complete without visiting the old
royal palace. The palace has a unique architecture and the sultan still
resides here. Please note that it is not possible to view the palace
inside, but excellent views are available from the outside.
