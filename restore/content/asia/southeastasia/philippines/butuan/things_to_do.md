# Things to do in Butuan

*Asia > Southeastasia > Philippines > Butuan > Things To Do*

If you want to go for exciting and breath-taking adventures, then
check out this **Butuan things to do** list. Butuan City lies in the
Northeast tip of Mindanao Island, in the Southern Philippines. Mindanao
is considered a huge island paradise replete with virgin forests and
pristine lakes, rivers and beaches. Butuan City is no exception, and a
day is not enough to experience the natural beauty of the place.

**Mountain Climbing**

Nature lovers will enjoy hiking on Mount Mayapay, a mountain plateau
that is 2,214 feet above sea level. It boasts of clear springs and
waterfalls where you could take a dip or set up camp. From the heart of
Butuan City, it takes less than an hour to get to the foot of the
mountain. Hire an experienced hiker who is familiar with the forest, and
make sure you have the necessary gear. Climbing Mount Mayapay is worth
the effort.

**Scuba Diving**

Explore the beauty of Butuan Bay’s marine resources, where visibility
reaches 80 feet. The bay has 19 diving sites and they all offer a peek
at the different varieties of fish populating this part of the
Philippines. You could also go snorkeling, and the necessary gear can be
rented from water sports shops near the beaches.

**Kahimunan Festival**

This festival is celebrated annually on the third Sunday of January
in honor of the city’s patron, the Sto. Niño (Holy Child). It is akin to
Mardi Gras, and features a street parade with dancers garbed in
bright-colored costumes, cheered on by numerous spectators. For those
not familiar with Philippine festivals, you would find this ritualistic
celebration quite entertaining and interesting. The parade is
accompanied by drums, trumpets, and gongs, and is a mixture of Islamic,
pagan, and Christian elements.

**Beach Activities**

Relax and enjoy the fine white sand of the beaches along Butuan Bay. A
day at the beach will surely leave you feeling rejuvenated. Let this be
a chance to stretch your legs, and enjoy the soft breeze.
Alternatively, go surfing or windsurfing. The waves are safe and ideal
for kids who want to try these activities. Surfboards can be rented from
beach shops.

**Underground Caves**

Aside from the mountains, beaches, and diving spots, Butuan also
boasts of numerous caves in its vicinity, some of which are underground.
The caves are mostly located in Carmen. They have massive limestone
formations particularly stalactites and stalagmites, and some have
skylights. The chambers have natural crevices through which light seeps,
providing lighting into an otherwise dark space.
