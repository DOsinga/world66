# Top 5 Must Do's in General Santos City

*Asia > Southeastasia > Philippines > Generalsantoscity > Top 5 Must Dos*

General Santos City is attractive in a rather unconventional way. An
acutely urban destination, it's also nice for viewing some great early
morning fishing activity and diving. There are beaches, glitzy shopping
malls and golf clubs alongside elegant administrative buildings and
stately homes. Here are the Top 5 Must Do’s when in this South Cotabato
Province.

**1. Watch Early Morning Fishing**

One of the most arresting visuals to enjoy in the city is that of
the fisherman carrying their humongous catches at the break of dawn at
Fisherport Complex. The dock becomes a throbbing center, with fisherman
seizing and flaunting their catches and parading them to the adjacent
Market 3. If you are lucky, you can spot giant tunas weighing a
staggering 80 kilos on the shoulders of the adept fisher folk. Visitors
can get a firsthand account of how catches are classified, categorized
and put up for sale by visiting the market. The place has now acquired
an iconic status and images representing the city are often ones of the
Fisherport Complex.

**2. Go Scuba Diving**

Visitors can indulge in enjoyable scuba diving sessions at a private
dive resort in South Point. Lemlunay Dive Resort is one of the most
well-maintained and comprehensively equipped dive resorts that hold
regular instructional diving courses and guided sessions. All equipment
and diving gear is available on hire. The food is delicious and the
staff, very amiable. The unobtrusive and pristine natural surroundings
add to a relaxing holiday.

**3. Camp at the Kalaja Karst Area**

The tourist hotpsot of Kalaja Karst, located 9 miles from the
northeast of Barangay Conel, is a quintessential natural reserve
featuring waterfalls, caves, a small water bed with wild flowers and
camping grounds to house nature and adventure purists. Explore the
underground caves with an expert guide and nail a tent on the sprawling
grounds to enjoy sleeping under the ‘Santonian’ night sky.

**4. Enjoy Authentic Filipino Cuisine at Nana Beth**

Nana Beth Fast Food House, nestled on J Catolicao Street opposite the
KCC Mall, is nothing short of an institution for tasty and wholesome
local grub. The menu lists a variety of local dishes that are
affordably priced. Make sure you try their seafood preparations,
especially the mouth-watering fresh tuna salad.

**5. Visit Absekong Waterfalls**

Sitting between the border of General Santos City and
Polomok, The Absekong Waterfalls cascade down the Camp Flores mountain
river along several granite boulders and striking lime-stone rimmed
cliffs. The small puddles and pools surrounding the waterfalls are a
nice sight.

General Santos City features a decent bunch of natural attractions
and activities to help tourists enjoy its rather unusual beauty.
