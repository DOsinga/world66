# When to Go in General Santos City

*Asia > Southeastasia > Philippines > Generalsantoscity > When To Go*

What more exciting place is there to plan a vacation than choosing the Philippines, with your destination for a traveling base at **General Santos City**?** When to go** and when not to go will most likely be high on your list in making your travel decisions. The idea of coming to this section of the world may bring thoughts of severe tsunamis and monsoon rains. However, the city is below the tropical typhoon belt and the mountain ranges withhold tropical depressions. For the most part, the weather is pleasant year round. The enjoyable weather combined with the tropical location are big factors in attracting visitors.

**Summer Season**

If you are coming over to General Santos City for a vacation focusing on fun in the sun, this is when you want to visit. Summertime starts in the month of April, with temperatures rising up to about 38 ℃ . The summer season ends around October. The tropical climate makes it great to get the tan that will have your friends jealous. During this period is when the most travelers come, so be aware that it can get slightly congested. Water sports are the most preferred of all activities available in the city. One of the advantages during the summertime is that barely any rainfall occurs. So if your reason for coming here is just to relax and find some sight-seeing for pleasure, the cool winds that blow off the water make the evenings very cozy. You may find that the nice weather gives you the opportunity to try out all the many restaurants here.**

Winter Season**

General Santos City has a distinctive winter season that separates it from the others. The weather at this time still allows you to do a lot of activities with your family or friends. From November to the month of April is when it is coolest. The average is around 30 ℃, but can lower to 26 ℃ . If you like fishing, this would be the opportune time to set out on a boat. The water temperature doesn't change much, so some scuba diving and cliff diving is still going on. The most attractive benefit about the winter months is that the crowds are noticeably smaller.
