# Top 5 Must Do's in Koronadal City

*Asia > Southeastasia > Philippines > Koronadal City 1 > Top 5 Must Dos*

If you are planning a trip to Southeast Asia and the Philippines,
Koronadal City is a stop you should consider on your vacation and if you
are looking for some **Koronadal City must do’s** there are many
that you can enjoy. The city has many tourist attractions. From scenic
walks and drives to resorts and gardens, the city can offer everyone who
travels there many places to see.
**1. Koronadal City Scenic Attractions**

Some of the most popular attractions in the city are the underground
caves. There are the beautiful waterfalls in the highlands of Koronadal
City that hold majesty and beauty. Also, travelers can enjoy the Siok
Waterfalls, where water falls from a height of 70 feets and creates
pools. These pools are quiet popular among tourists as they are open for
swimming.
**2. Shopping in Koronadal City
**

The city is also well-known for its shopping centers and the
furniture, fabrics and gifts that are available to purchase. The city
has high-end malls as well as the local shops on the side streets near
the market streets.

**3. Enjoy Local Food**

Koronadal City has a number of restaurants which offer excellent
local food. On a visit to the city, you should taste Mindanaoan cuisine.
One of the most famous local food is "balut," or duck egg. You can find
local food on the street-side shops as well high-end malls.
**4. Learn about History**

A visit to any city cannot be complete without knowing about its
history. The best place to know about the city’s history is by visiting
the city museum. You can learn about T’boli Culture at the Cultural
Center Museum. The museum also hosts a special cultural festival. A
number of well-known artists also perform at the Cultural Center.
**5. Enjoy the Environment**

Travelers can also experience the canopy walk that is under the El
Gawel in the city while being able to enjoy the sounds of nature. El
Gawel also has a wildlife sanctuary which attracts a number of tourists.
Tourists can take a jeep tour in the sanctuary and watch a number of
wild animals.
