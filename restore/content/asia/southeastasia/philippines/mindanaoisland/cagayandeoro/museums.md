# Museums in Cagayan de Oro

*Asia > Southeastasia > Philippines > Mindanaoisland > Cagayandeoro > Museums*

Along the northern coastline off the island of Mindanao in the Philippines lies the **Cagayan de Oro Museums**.
The city of Cagayan de Oro, also known as The Gateway to Northern
Mindanao or The City of Whitewater Rafting, offers two museums found
amidst caves, archaeological sites and memorials. Each museum has a
variety of pieces representing the rich history of the original natives
(known as the Huluga settlement) right up to the thriving urban center
it has become.

**Museum of Three Cultures**

The first is the Museum of Three Cultures in the Capitol University
melding archaeological treasures with rare antiquities of three
dominant cultures: Moro, Christian lowland and lumad (the indigenous
people from about 350 A.D.). Brassware, weaponry and musical
instruments all adorned with the trademark Maranao vine patterns can be
found. The crown jewel, however, is the Muslim ceremonial sword
collection representing the great power of the Sultan called the
Danganan. This collection has swords ranging up to fifteen feet in
length with one ten foot blade formerly owned by Sultan Malimala. Over
three hundred years old this spectacular weapon still has the Sultan’s
motif carved in the beak shaped handle.

**Museo del Oro**

The second is the Museo del Oro located on the campus of Xavier
University mostly covering traditional anthropological and folk culture
of Northern Mindanao. On permanent display is the rare collection of
artifacts from a Huluga cave including skeletons, stoneware and Indian
glass beads. In addition, a vast range of preserved historical pieces
representing the Bukidnon and Maranao culture are on display.
