# Top 5 Must Do's in Negros island

*Asia > Southeastasia > Philippines > Negrosisland > Top 5 Must Dos*

**Negros Island Must Do's** list cannot be prepared without
knowing about the place. The Philippines are a beautiful collection of
islands which are located in Southeast Asia. Despite not being rich
people, the islands' inhabitants are commonly understood to be among the
happiest in the world.
**1. Check the Best Beaches - Sipalay**

There are many beautiful beaches in the Philippines and Sipalay
contains some of the best. Sipalay is a remote seaside town with a large
number of beaches which are covered with attractive, clean-looking
white sand. The waters here are also clear and a great place to view
attractive sea life when snorkeling. Look out in particular for Sugar
Beach.
**2. Be Adventurous with SCUBA Diving**

Punta Ballo dive resorts are also located in the Sipalay area of the
Negros Islands. The SCUBA diving here is some of the best in the island
of Negros. The clear water and rich sea life, together with the warm
climate, make it an ideal place to dive. It is known for being a safe
place to dive and is therefore a particularly good place in which to
learn to SCUBA diving or develop your skills if you are an inexperienced
diver.
**3. Visit Dumaguete Town**

This is one of the more attractive towns in the Philippines, making
it one of the popular Negros Island must-do’s. Many of the larger towns
in the Philippines are not especially pleasant places, but Dumaguete is a
notable exception to this rule. The waterside promenade is a
particularly nice area.
**4. Enjoy Mount Kanlaon National Park**

This is a sizable national park, roughly 25,000 hectares in total,
which contains a heavy cover of trees as well as impressive volcanic
scenery. It has a wide variety of bird life and other wildlife making it
popular with nature lovers. It is a particularly good place to try to
see the rare Philippine spotted deer and also the bleeding-heart pigeon.
Hikers also enjoy visiting this particular region although walks can be
restricted because of the active volcano.
**5. Enjoy the History of Silay**

The final activity on the list of Negros Island must-do’s is a visit
to Silay. It is no longer the glamorous place that it once was but is
still worth a visit because of its history. In the 19th century sugar
cane was planted in the region and the area grew rich from this. A
number of ancestral homes were built and it became a fashionable hangout
for artisans. These days are long gone but two of the ancestral homes
remain as museums.
