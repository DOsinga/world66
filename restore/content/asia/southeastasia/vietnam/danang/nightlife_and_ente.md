# Nightlife and Entertainment in Da nang

*Asia > Southeastasia > Vietnam > Danang > Nightlife And Ente*

Although it may not be quite as exciting as that found in other large
cities, there is still plenty of **Da Nang nightlife** to
experience. If you're in the mood to dance or have a beer, then you are
in luck. The city is an important business and trade center, so you will
find options for leisure travelers as well as business travelers. Most
places are not open very late, but a few will stay open until 1 a.m.
Discotheques

Phuong Dong is possibly the most important discotheque in the city.
There is a small dance floor, and you can be assured that you will have
your cocktail in your hand within minutes of ordering. No. 1 Disco is
one of the most upscale discos in the city, although it is quite small.
Another disco at the mouth of the river, New Phuong Dong, is the least
crowded of the discos, but still a lot of fun. If you're staying near
the beach, then the only disco on that side of the city is Festival. In
addition to drinks and dancing, this place also offers dinner and
karaoke.
Pubs and Bars

If you're just looking for a beer, then Tulip Beer and the Czech Beer
Place are excellent. There are many restaurants nearby with great
beers. Red Hot is a bar that is open later than most, as it may close at
1 or 1:30 a.m. Even when the bars close, however, many of the open-air
businesses still serve beer until the wee hours of the morning. The
closing time is not as strictly enforced in Da Nang as in Hanoi, so many
places will stay open after “closing hours.”

If you are an expatriate, then stop by Green Town Bar. English is
spoken at this bar, and you can be sure you will get what you ordered.
They have two large pool tables and a lovely outdoor terrace that has
quite the view. You can get food until 10 p.m., and prices are
affordable. The bamboo bar is another popular ex-pat bar, and there is
always a healthy mix of foreigners and locals there.
Traditional Dance

At Nguyen Hien Dinh you can see local dancers performing traditional
dances of the area. The main dance is called "tuong," but you can also
enjoy watching Peacock, Trinh Tuong and ApSaRa dances. Stop by the
theatre for music performances as well as any plays currently running.
This is a great alternative to the bars or the movies, and one that
should not be missed. If you're in town in March, then go to the Da Nang
festival, as there are many performances to attend during this event.
