# Day Trips in Guam

*Australiaandpacific > Guam > Day Trips*

**Cocos Island**

Off the southern tip of Guam is a small island called Cocos Island.
A ferry will take you to the island from the village of Merizo and will
only cost about $20. This little island might be small but it is
packed with lots of things to do while you are visiting Guam and the
ferry runs all day so you won’t have to worry about getting back when
you want to go. Just be sure to go before sunset when the ferry stops
for the night.

The island was once the site of a famous ship wreck. In 1690 the
Nuestra Senora del Pilar de Zaragosa y Santiago wrecked on the southern
reef of the island. It was coming from Acapulco and going to Manila for
a festival. The bottom of the ship hit the reef and it soon sunk with
all its treasures. On the ship was a shipment of silver swords and
other artifacts. Only one chest of silver was reclaimed and it is
rumored that millions of dollars of Spanish coins and silver is still
somewhere on the bottom of the ocean near the Island. The story adds to
the mystery and romance of this resort island.

On Cocos Island tourists can swim, snorkel, and dive as well as
skiing, and parasailing. The resort rents out reclining chairs so you
can while away the hours on a beautiful beach. There are hiking trails
around the island and you can search for war relics. This island is
full of tall pines and watch large lizards that live here. If you leave
them alone they generally will leave you alone, but they are fun to
watch. A lunch buffet is offered at the resort. If you want a snack you
purchase tickets from the resort and use them in the many shops around
the area.

**Deep Sea Fishing at Tumon Bay or Apra Harbor**

One of the main tourist attractions in Guam is fishing. Take a day
trip out on the ocean to do some deep sea fishing. Tumon Bay and Apra
Harbor are the most popular fishing areas but, Guam is near the
Marianas Trench which is the deepest part of the ocean. There are many
different species of fish that swim and live in the waters. It is
advisable to make reservations for a charter before you want to go
fishing as there is a demand for this activity and if you have never
done deep sea fishing before don’t try to do it on your own. There
aren’t many fishing regulations but there are some and the crew of a
charter will make sure you know what they are. No fishing licenses are
required at any time of the year. Most charters last 3 to 9 hours and
cost anywhere from $350 to $550.

Whether you take advantage of the beach, go diving, or take a deep
sea fishing charter you will enjoy your day trip away from Guam.
