# Sights in Cuba

*Centralamericathecaribbean > Thecaribbean > Cuba > Sights*

Cuba is known all over the world as the country of Fidel Castro. It is also known to the biologists and the geologists as the Galapagos of the Caribbean. More than 20 percent of Cuba is protected for wildlife. Cuba will offer you numerous ecological tours through the forests, lagoons, lakes and beaches. **Cuba Sights** are a delight for any nature lover.

**Havana**

Havana, the capital of Cuba, is the night capital of all of Caribbean. You will find everything that you need as a traveler in Havana. Old Havana will take you back into the era of the 1950s and 1960s with its narrow streets, very old buildings and vintage cars. The plazas, churches and the forts will definitely make you feel your have moved 50 years back in time. The exotic antediluvian beauty of old Havana attracts thousands to visit here every year. You can stop at Calle Tacon market for shopping souvenirs and antique items. You can also bask in the lovely sandy beaches of Havana while sipping into a glass of mojito (Cuban rum cocktail)

**Varadero**

Varadero has less of a Che-Guevara atmosphere and more sophistication than most of the other Cuban cities. This city is famous for its lovely beaches and luxury resorts. This city is also very popular among the people wishing to play golf on the beautiful courses.

**Punta Frances**

Punta Frances has beaches considered as the most blue and cleanest among all the Caribbean beaches. This place, situated to the southwest side of the island, is very popular among the marine biologists. These beaches are good but the sea is even better with its variety of marine life like boa, crocodiles, and sea turtles. The world&rsquo;s smallest bird, popularly known as hamming bird, is found here.

**Baracoa**

Cuba is blessed with unparallel natural beauty and unique ecological balance. Baracoa, located to the far east side of the Cuba, is famous for its lush landscape. Baracoa is also famous as a location where Columbus landed in 16th century. It was also the old capital of the Caribbean Island. Baracoa, now a slow-paced town, carries its authentic Caribbean flavor in its lifestyle, rum and food.

**Santiago de Cuba**

Santiago de Cuba is the second largest city of Cuba in terms of population. Santiago de Cuba is the right place for you to enjoy the lovely beats of Caribbean music. This city kicks back to life during the musical carnival that takes place in July.
