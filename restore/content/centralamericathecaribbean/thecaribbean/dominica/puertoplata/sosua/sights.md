# Sights in Sosua

*Centralamericathecaribbean > Thecaribbean > Dominica > Puertoplata > Sosua > Sights*

The town of Sosua is a great way to experience a lively Caribbean vacation, with its beautiful **Sosua sights.** The Sosua Bay offers magnificent scenery and the pristine and crystal clean ocean allures travelers to swim. Take a walk and visit the dance clubs and restaurants, which make up the lively nightlife of Sosua. Do not forget to visit the souvenir shops for the best handicrafts at a bargain price.

**Museo Judio Sosua**

Museo Judio Sosua is an exceptional sight in Sosua. It chronicles the Jewish way of life in the 1940s, their settlement and immigration in this city. This is a fascinating place to visit; you will be hearing about the injustices done to the Jewish refugees during that time. Visit this place to learn more about the life and hardships by the Jewish settlers.

**Playa Sosua**

Playa Sosua is an amazing beach in Sosua Bay and a known destination for divers because of its rich marine life and colorful coral reefs. The main beach has one kilometer long of golden soft sand and calm waters that is excellent for swimming. From the beach, you can witness the beauty of the mountains and the natural cliffs that surrounds the coastline. Snorkeling is a major sport here, best done offshore, closer to the reef area. You do not need to walk far if you get thirsty because many local people sell drinks and snacks as well as souvenirs.

**El Batey Neighborhood**

El Batey Neighborhood is at the end of Playa Sosua and one of the major tourist attractions in Sosua. Exiled Jews from Eastern Europe founded it during the World War II. It is next to Casa Marina Resort and has a lovely tropical green lawn. It has two prominent structures; one is the wooden synagogue, an interesting structure that hosts marriage ceremonies for Jewish couples. Many Jewish couples from other communities choose to get married in this synagogue and the museum, which documented the life of the Jewish refugees in Sosua. If you are lucky, you can attend or witness the Friday services held in the synagogue once a month. Nowadays, El Batey has no active Jewish community, but is considered part of interesting Sosua sights with its small hotels and resorts.
