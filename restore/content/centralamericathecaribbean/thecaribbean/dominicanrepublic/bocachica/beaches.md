# Beaches in Boca chica

*Centralamericathecaribbean > Thecaribbean > Dominicanrepublic > Bocachica > Beaches*

**Boca Chica Beaches** could very well be one of the largest hidden secrets of the Dominican Republic and all of the Caribbean. Many people have never heard of Boca Chica, a beautiful little town situated on the southern end of the island of Hispaniola, Dominican Republic. Compared to other areas of the Caribbean, Boca Chica beaches are much nicer with calm, clear, shallow waters and soft, bright white sands. Listed below are a few of the more popular beaches in and around Boca Chica that come highly recommended.

**Main Beach**

Boca Chica Beach is also referred to as Main Beach. It's one of the busier and more popular beaches of the area and is lined with a variety of businesses. At the far east end of this beach is the Hamaca Pier and Beach Resort. Parts of this beachfront area are public property (the first 60 meters from the water's edge) and the rest belongs to the Hamaca Beach Resort. Another part of the Main Beach is the Don Juan Beach Resort along with their partnered dive company, Treasured Divers. This centered portion of Boca Chica Beach is definitely the best with another huge pier that stretches out over the turquoise water.

**Los Pinos Beach**

Travel farther west on the island and you'll find the Los Pinos Beach or in English, it's known as The Pines. The beach here is not as nice as the main beach, mainly due to pollution and people not properly cleaning up after themselves. There is also hurricane damage including many bushes, shrubs and trees being uprooted from the harsh winds. Make sure you don't travel to this particular beach without good walking shoes and insect or mosquito repellent.

**La Matica Beach**

Just off the shores of Boca Chica and hidden within the beautiful reef area are a couple of tiny islands you can wade out to and explore. Check out the mangroves and the thousands of herons that call it home. The waters here are shallow so you can either walk around on the sandbar located just below the water's surface, or if you'd rather, take a small boat and float around.

**Playa Andrés Beach**

Head over to Playa Andrés Beach and find a wonderful marina containing several large boats and yachts. There is also an older operating sugar factory here which is still a main source of employment for Boca Chica residents. The beaches on this part of the island are usually less crowded than others and there are always lounge chairs and palm-leaf umbrellas lining the shores for your ultimate comfort.
