# Things To Do in St. Thomas

*Centralamericathecaribbean > Thecaribbean > Virginislands > Stthomas > Things To Do*

Saint Thomas, an island in the Caribbean Sea, is a district of Virgin Islands, and **Saint Thomas things to do** are plenty. The island was first discovered on Christopher Columbus&rsquo;s second voyage to the new world. The influence of the Dutch era can still been seen in and around Saint Thomas. Saint Thomas is serviced by the Cyril E.King airport. Given its accessibility and the plethora of options between the mountains and the beaches, and the rich cultural heritage, Saint Thomas is a tourist paradise. Given below are just some of the Saint Thomas things to do.

**Saint Thomas Beaches**

Saint Thomas has several beaches to match every traveler&rsquo;s requirement. You have beaches which offer you various water sports options like snorkeling, scuba diving, windsurfing, charters, sailing, fishing, kayaking and parasailing if you a looking for an active holiday. There are several beaches that are famous for their world class beach resorts, where you can simply relax and rejuvenate or beaches that are secluded and sparsely populated if you just want some peace and quite and white sand. One of the most famous Saint Thomas beach is the Magen&rsquo;s Bay, which is recognized as the one of the most beautiful beaches in the world. Each beach has something unique to offer.

**Coral World Marine Park and Observatory**

A trip to the Coral World in Saint Thomas is something that you cannot afford to miss. The Coral World can wow all age groups of people with its tropical nature trail, marine gardens and undersea observatory. You can take a moonwalk through the ocean gardens with Star Trek and enjoy the coral reef tank with the exotic marine life. You can also choose to get into the pool with friendly sea lions or just hang around the nature trail watching the exotic sociable birds. If you are up to it, you also get to witness the sharks and the feeding of the sting rays.

**Fort Christian**

Fort Christian, built in 1672 to protect Charlotte Amalie and Saint Thomas against pirates and other rivals, is named after a Danish King and is a National Landmark. If you are the one for history, then this is a must visit place as it is the best place to learn about Caribbean history. The Fort is the oldest standing structure in Saint Thomas overlooking the city Charlotte Amalie. Do not forget to visit the Virgin Island Museum which houses some ancient artifacts and art from the Danish era.
