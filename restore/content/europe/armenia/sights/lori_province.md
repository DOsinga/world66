Tavush Province

Tavush Province is one of Armenia's most beutifull
provinces. Province is covered with vast forest, surrounded with
mountains and rich with history. Several places you should check
out.

Town of Dilijan is nestled in a beutifull forested
area of the province, in one of Armenia' s protected reservations
Dilijan Reservation. The town has restaurants, hotels, bed and
breakfasts and friendly people.

Hagartsin Church complex is located north of
Dilijan. The complex is composed of three standing churches, one
completely ruined one and a dinning complex where Armenian kings
dined. The place is believed to have been a summer residence for
the kings. Parz Lich is another place one should check out if you
are in the vicinity of Dilijan. The name means Clear Lake, but it
is not. The drive to the lake is amazing. It is about 30-40
minute winding road, but the scenary, the smell of the forest, the
sound of the creeks is unforgetable. There are picnic sites
by the lake, you can take hikes, camp and e.t.c. No restaurants
here (not yet), so take your own food. Another place that is
worth your visit is Goshavank Monastery. It is a little north of
Dilijan. A little south west of Dilijan there are two churches
Jukhta Vank (Vank means church) and Matosavank. This two you have
to hike a little to get to them. They are locatd inside the
Dilijan forest (Reservation) and the hike once again is
beuitiful. You can find more information about these places by
visiting www.armenpedia.org or www. cilicia.com and click on the
encyclopedia icon. Another very usefull site (even though some
pages are still under construction) is www.virtualarmenia.am.
Check them out they have pictures, history, directions and e.t.c.
