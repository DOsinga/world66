# When to Go in Yerevan

*Europe > Armenia > Yerevan > When To Go*

The typical climate of Yerevan is continental, with the summers being dry and hot and the winters cold and brief. The reason behind this type of climate is due to the fact that this plain land is encircled by mountains. In the month of August when the summer is at its peak, the temperature touches nearly 40 degree Celsius. Interesting cultural events like film festivals, exhibitions and lectures take place regularly in this performing arts hub. Many film screenings and sporting events like football are also popular. Regular musical events are organized here and high quality orchestra performances as well as chamber music-concerts can be easily heard. Cultural expositions also take place in this colorful and vibrant city. The **Yerevan when to go** guide gives** **visitors detailed information on the overall climate and the best time to visit this exotic locale.

**Climate**

The continental climate in Yerevan is distinctly conspicuous. Although the latitude of this place is same as many other Mediterranean countries, it is quite far from the sea. Hence the temperature reaches high levels bordering 40 degrees Celsius in peak summers. You can plan to visit this place between the months of April to October, when it is not very cold. The humidity remains low throughout the entire year. Rainfall is less and about 350 mm of precipitation occurs annually. There is a dramatic change noticed in winter during the month of January with temperatures dropping to as low as -15 degree Celsius.

**Golden Apricot Yerevan International Film Festival**

The annual Golden Apricot International Film Festival held annually in the month of July showcases the finest masterpieces of world cinema and is a brilliant platform for cross-cultural cinematic dialogue and discussions. The festival screens a medley of independent and mainstream movies by well-known filmmakers and this is indeed a good time to visit Yerevan to get acquainted with an entire treasure-trove of international cinema.

**HIGH FEST International Performing Arts Festival**

The annual festival held in the month of October exhibits an array of performances belonging to various genres of performing arts. There are theatrical renditions, street performances, dances, operas, puppet shows, jazz concerts and numerous other lesser-known albeit interesting variants of performing arts.

**The “One Nation, One Culture” Festival**

This is one of the best times to visit the culturally rich Yerevan. The “One Nation, One Culture” festival is held once in every two years and several Armenian ethnic groups are invited from all over the world to showcase their cultural legacy over a period of 6-7 days. The program comprises of several dance, musical and theatrical performances along with numerous outdoor fetes, a circus and some literary events.

The climate is most favorable for tourists between April to October, when the temperatures are moderate and when some of finest cultural events and festivals greet them.
