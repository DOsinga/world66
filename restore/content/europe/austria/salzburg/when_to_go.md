# When to Go in Salzburg

*Europe > Austria > Salzburg > When To Go*

The best time to visit Salzburg, Austria, especially if you are a
classical music buff, is in the summer time, specifically in July when
the Saltzburg Festival starts.

**The Salzburg Festival**

The Salzburg Festival opens near the end of the month and has been
since 1926. This is an arts festival that is known all over the world.
It continues until the end of August (6 weeks total). People come from
near and far to see the plays, musical performances, and other artistic
entertainment that goes on during the festival. There is always
something to do and something to see during this time of the year. You
will see concerts ranging from symphony music to opera and theater is
performed on the streets of the city at any given time during the day
or night. Many of the rich and famous from Central Europe and all over
the world will congregate in Salzburg to see the sites. You can hear
opera and folk songs in the same day. However, there are other
activities that take advantage of the crowds that the city draws during
this time. There are all kinds of other events including a golf
tournament and other mini festivals near by.

**The Month of July**

There are still other things to do besides attending the festival in
July making this a jam packed month of activities in the area. July 25th
is St. Jacob’s Day. St. Jacob is the patron saint of shepherds and is
honored in Salzburg. There are dance ceremonies and a traditional
wrestling match on Mount Hundsstein where the air is thin and wrestling
is harder. This match has taken place since the middle ages and the
winner gets the title of “Hagmoar” and acknowledgment of other males
in the village.

On Mount Dürrnberg in not to far away Hallein there are salt mines.
The miner’s guild performs the ancient sword dance or “schwerttanz” for
tourists. Also south of Salzburg in Muhr and Zederhause Prangstangen
poles are carried in a ceremony during July. These poles are like May
poles decorated with flowers. Legend says the villagers swore they
would carry the decorated poles around if they were kept safe from the
wrath of grasshoppers. Villagers decorate with over 50,000 flowers and
unmarried young men carry them around. On July 15th they are placed in the church until another festival comes on August 15th.

**International Jazz Festival**

There is an International Jazz Festival in Saalfelden, just south of
Salzburg that lasts for three days in late August. Back in Hallein
there is an International Folk Festival and you can hear European folk
music.
