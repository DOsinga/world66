# Nightlife and Entertainment in Zell am See

*Europe > Austria > Zellamsee > Nightlife And Ente*

Zell am See is an Austrian skiing town known as a popular vacation stop for European tourists, and is famous for its nightlife. It's generally considered a party town for more mature skiers, not the 18-30 club set. Clubs and après-ski in the area are known for their relaxed and unpretentious atmosphere; there are also numerous taverns for those who prefer a quiet night after a day’s skiing. Zell am See has been consistently popular with Europeans, and its nightlife is a big part of the reason why they come back, year after year.

Zell am See offers a wide range of nightlife alternatives for any kind of skier or boarder. for the more adventurous there are several clubs and nightspots, from live music bands to top class discos. For those who want a quiet night after skiing, there are a range of alternative activities such as bowling, or dining out.**5 Clubs in Zell am See **

- **The Resi** – Formerly familiar as the Crazy Daisy pub - in Salzmannstr, is often full of Dutch, British and other European partygoers, enjoying the local bands that play there every day of the week.
- **The Pinzgauer Diele** - An après ski point until 7 in the evening, and then becomes a bar; at 11pm it then becomes a disco.
- **The Bierstadl** - A local watering hole, where 33 kinds of beer can be ordered.
- **The Classics** - Has a live music area, but beware of the smoke!
- **The Gasthof Alpenblick** - A traditional Austrian beer room, not actually in Zell am see, but nearby in Schuttdorf. There are bands and restaurants in the beer room, although it's closed throughout April (until May 5th) and also between the end of November through the Christmas period.
**Other Things to Do in Zell am See**

Most skiers specifically come to enjoy the Zell am See nightlife, but there are also a variety of other things to do at night. These include night skiing (for those who can’t get enough during the day), bowling and visiting the cinema, tobogganing and skating. Dining facilities include the Einkehr, which is a bistro and bar in Shmittenstr, and the Jagawirt, which serves local delicacies such as wild goat, to be followed by the fantastic desserts such as Mandelocken – a traditional almond cake.
