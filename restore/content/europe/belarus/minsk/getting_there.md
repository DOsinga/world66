# Getting There

*Europe > Belarus > Minsk > Getting There*

Belarus is a country in Eastern Europe bordered by Russia, Ukraine, Poland, Lithuania and Latvia, making the capital of Minsk
easily accessible from these surrounding countries. Multiple travel options to this city of 2 million people are available.

**By Train**

Minsk lies at the heart of 2 main railways --the Warsaw-Moscow
railway and the Liepaja-Romany railway. This means that Minsk is easily
accessible from all surrounding areas as well as from Germany, via
the Warsaw-Moscow railway. Trains may be a less expensive option that flying if you take a budget flight into Germany and take a train to
Minsk.

**By Airplane**

Minsk International Airport is served by Belavia, (the Belarusian
airline), Lufthansa, AirBaltic (Latvian airline), LOT (Polish airline)
and the Austrian Airlines Group consisting of numerous airlines.
To determine the best flight arrangements, looking at a multi-airline search engine or call the airlines and
ask about special offers. Some travelers use a discount airline to fly into
one of the surrounding countries and enter Minsk by train or a national airline.

**By Car**

Many major European routes go through Minsk, including the one of
the longest in Europe, the E30. European Routes E28 and E85 and 2 local highways also go
through Minsk.
