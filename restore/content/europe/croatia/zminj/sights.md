# Sights in Zminj

*Europe > Croatia > Zminj > Sights*

**We present you the
Istrian caves: Feštinsko kraljevstvo**

Feštinsko
kraljevstvo is one of the numerous caves in [Istria](/europe/croatia/zminj/sights) which counts
around one thousand of them. The cave is located near Žminj in the
village of Feština.

The name
“kraljevstvo” (kingdom) was given by children. Children’s
imagination, the richness of stalagmite and stalactite shapes which
look like curtains, the particularity of their colours from milky
white to red and brown, truly resemble a fairytale kingdom.

The cave was
discovered accidentally in 1930. Tone Božac, a vineyard owner on
whose land the cave is situated, worked in his vineyard and his peep
fell into a hole. Not realizing the depth of the cave, Tone tried to
cover the hole with soil but it kept falling deeper and deeper into
the ground.

During World War II,
the cave saved his son Josip who hid there while the Germans took and
killed all male inhabitants of the village of Feština.

This cave is proof
of how individuals can do a lot with their effort and good ideas.
With the help of Roman Bošac' daughter and all its families, Istria
gained yet another natural beauty. With the permit issued by the
Ministry of Culture and in collaboration with Natura Histrica and
Silvije Legović’ advice, who went through a similar experience for
the Baredine cave to be open for public, a large project was
initiated. With certain amount of financial aid for the realization
of the project and with significant own investment, the cave was
cleaned up, and its trails and lighting were tended to. It is worth
visiting.

The temperature in the
cave is between 13 and 14 degrees throughout the whole year and you
might even catch a glance of a bat or a small “albino” crab.
