# Eating Out in Ceske budejovice

*Europe > Czechrepublic > Ceskebudejovice > Eating Out*

Czech food is good food and the offerings in Ceske Budejovice are no
exception. Czech cuisine is not light but it is flavorful, plentiful
and interesting to learn about. Ceske Budejovice is also the home to
Budvar beer otherwise known as Budweiser in Europe. Although the name
is the same, Czech Budweiser is vastly different from Anheuser Busch’s
offering. Budvar is a dark lager with a malty taste, wonderful with
many of the Czech dishes mentioned below. As they say in South Bohemia
“dobrou chut” or bon appetit!

**Maly Pivovar**

A wonderful place to get great local food for a reasonable price is
at a “pivnice” or beer pub, sometimes referred to as a “hospada”.
Pivnices have lively and authentic atmospheres and serve up simple yet
delicious food. At the Maly Pivovar beer hall, national and
international cuisine is served. Try Czech classics like fried cheese
or goulash. Freshwater fish, duck, rabbit and chicken are on the menu
as well. There is also a coffee café here with homemade baked goods.

**Modry Dvere “Jazz and Blues Restaurant”**

This hip pub has a lovely outdoor seating area. Local fare is served
here; try the borscht with sour cream and pork or chicken schnitzel
with vegetables and lemon. Familiar favorites like nachos, kebab
skewers and potato pancakes are on the menu as well. Reservations are
accepted and Wednesdays boast live music.

**Grand Hotel Zvon**

The hotel is home to Restaurant Symphony and Pilsner Urquell Original Restaurant.

Restaurant Symphony serves lunch and dinner and is upscale in price
and atmosphere. They serve Austrian and South Bohemian food with
gourmet flair. They are known for their organic beef and veal dishes
that come from their own farm. Be sure to save room for the plum cake
with ice cream.

If a causal evening is more your style, check out “Puor” (Pilsner
Urquell Original Restaurant). Czechs like to have a starter before
their meals so have the garlic soup or onion soup first. Regional fare
that goes well with beer is a specialty here so be sure to try the
house pilsner. Puor has an outdoor terrace if the weather is mild for
your visit.

**Food Stands**

For Czech food on the go, try a “klobasy” or grilled sausage. The
sausage is served with mustard and horseradish on brown bread. Or how
about fried cheese on a bun? Be sure to try the fruit dumplings if your
stand makes them. All items are made to order and served to go!
