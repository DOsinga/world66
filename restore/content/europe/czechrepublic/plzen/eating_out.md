# Eating Out in Plzen

*Europe > Czechrepublic > Plzen > Eating Out*

Plzen, or Pilsen, is the capital of West Bohemia in the Czech
Republic. The city was founded by King Wenceslas II. The cuisine runs
the gambit from German, Italian, Chinese and other nationalities.
Surprisingly, eating out in Plzen will not break the budget.

**Beer and Breweries**

There are many restaurants in Plzen, as it is considered a resort
town. There is something to make everyone happy. Plzen is famous for
its brewery and beer museum. Tourists will find a variety of beers to
choose from, including the local variety, at almost every restaurant.

The brewery is across the street from the public library and the
public park. There is a restaurant inside. The menu is diverse and inexpensive. It has recently been remodeled and restored so the
ambiance is very pleasant. You can get a meal for $5 to $20.

**Palazinki**

Palazinki is a big restaurant and also near the brewery. The
desserts are the best and are called pancakes. They are filled with jam
or fresh fruit and topped with ice cream. Dinners are under $10 and the
dessert is about $2. It is inside the brewery, to the right.

**Restaurace Zumbera**

Restaurace Zumbera has Czech food (which is similar to German
Cuisine). You can get a nice minced meat in potatoes or a schnitzel
seasoned with local herbs. Dinners are less than $10 and it is located
in Old Town.

**San Marino**

San Marino is a wonderful and cozy pizza place. Both young and old
gather to indulge in the Italian food. It is on Raisova Street in
Plzen-Bory. A pizza generally costs less than $10.

**Shanghai**

Shanghai is a Chinese restaurant in Plzen on Klatovaka. It is
actually is in a basement and this makes it hard to find. It is bit
dark and dingy, but the food is great Chinese food. Most plates are $10
or less.

**Plzenka Basta**

Plzenka Basta is another Czech restaurant with many good varieties of
beer. They offer roasted duck with dumplings and cabbage, a traditional
Czech recipe and Pilsen beer.It is near the Central Hotel on the main square, near the Cathedral. You can get dinner for $10 or less.

**Francouzky Restaurant**

Francouzky Restaurant in Hotel Rosso in the central part of the city
is a great place to go. The building has been restored to its 16th century glory. Here they have good wines and a French Restaurant. An American Grill is also in the hotel.

No matter where you turn, you will find beer houses and restaurants just waiting to satisfy your hunger.
