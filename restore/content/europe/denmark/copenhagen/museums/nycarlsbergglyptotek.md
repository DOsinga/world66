# Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek

*Europe > Denmark > Copenhagen > Museums > Nycarlsbergglyptotek*

The Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek has a vast collection of sculpture in which there are over thirty works by the French sculptor Rodin. This makes it the most important Rodin collection outside France. In addition, the museum displays sculptures by Degas, Canova, Rauch and Kai Nielsen and a large collection of antique Greek, Egyptian, Etruscan and Roman sculpture. It collection of paintings may be somewhat smaller but of equal high quality with artists like Géricault, Manet, Monet, Renoir, Degas and Van Gogh. One of the showpieces is a magnificent self-portrait by Cézanne and the Glypotek has no less than 35 works by Paul Gauguin. Appart from this international collection there are works by Danish artists like Eckersberg, Dahl, Købke and Lundbye, but the main attraction is the museum itself. It was build in 1897 by Vilhelm Dahlerup with an addition by Hack Kampmann's. The two buildings are connected by an impressive conservatory, with is the centre of the museum. In 1996, Henning Larsen succeeded in adding an insert in the existing building without even damaging the work of his predecessors. Something probably only a Danish architect could accomplish. Free admission on Wednesdays and Sundays.
