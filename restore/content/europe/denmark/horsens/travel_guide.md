# Travel Guide in Horsens

*Europe > Denmark > Horsens > Travel Guide*

This **Horsens travel guide** will be able to assist you in your tour to the east coast town of Jutland. It is a small town of rich, old culture that dates back to almost the 15th century where classic monasteries and churches were established. Today, it stands as a testament to the fusion of European old folk in the turn of a now modern world. It surely is a place where you can revisit the classic world and have some enjoyable things to do.

**Attractions**

More mature tourists visit Horsens to appreciate the Old World that has been preserved in the structures that stand around the town. Among the must-see places are the Bygholm Castle built in the early 14th century, the Borgergade Church built in the 13th century and the Lichtenbergs Palace, a place built of stone. The Kunstmuseum is another popular tourist destination that houses various contemporary art collections and historical artifacts of Horsens. Most historical sites of the town are visited by artistic tourists who explore Europe&rsquo;s finest art showcases.

**Accommodations**

The most acclaimed among tourist reviews is the Hotel Opus Horsens. If you prefer wide-spaced lodging where you can relax and have more room for other things, then this is the place for you. Although the hotel cost is reasonably more than the common hotels around town, it offers several more facilities you can enjoy such as a fitness center and bigger function rooms available for conferences or meetings of bigger groups. The hotel is very big and new and is accessible to tourists who want to stay online during the duration of their stay.

**Restaurants**

Mackie&rsquo;s restaurant in Horsens offers meals that are tasty and affordable, fit for the tourist who wants to stay on a budget. You can find Mackie&rsquo;s by walking down Graven Road. Prices range from $12 to $17. The dining experience at Horsens goes beyond the food and is much influenced by the restaurant&rsquo;s atmosphere as a whole.

**Transportation**

Horsens town is about 45 to 75 minutes from the international airports. You can take the express trains that run directly into the town from the airport if you do not have private transportation to get you around. You can also travel by ferry if you are coming from neighboring European cities. Taxis, cars and even bicycle rentals are also available. The local bus card can be purchased and is valid for an hour.

**Shopping**

Bytorv Horsens and Bilka are the most popular shopping centers in Horsens that tourists frequently visit.
