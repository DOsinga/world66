# When to Go in Denmark

*Europe > Denmark > When To Go*

If you are planning a family vacation in **Denmark, when to go** is probably what is on your mind. The climate is temperate, with mild, windy winters and cool summers, but sometimes it can be humid and overcast. Temperatures range throughout the year from 8 to 19 degrees Celsius. Prices for accommodations and other amenities are cheaper during late August. You can take advantage of this to avoid the tourist rush after August. Autumn Season is also a good time to visit Denmark, but the countryside scenery is usually dull brown.

**Summer Days**

The best time to visit Denmark is from late June, July and August. Its location on the southernmost part of the Nordic territory causes Denmark to experience long summer days that are cool and comfortable. The sun rises at 4:00 a.m. and sets at 10:00 p.m. The long days give tourists longer time to tour the many sights and museums, attend open concerts, and beach activities. Tourists can venture to the countryside with its scenic, gentle rolling plains of rich green and vast gardens of yellow rapeseed flowers. The Danes celebrate the Sankthansaften or St. John&rsquo;s evening as the longest day in summer.

**Winter Days**

During winter time the days are short, sunrise comes at nine o&rsquo;clock in the morning and sets at around 4:30 p.m. This coincides with Christmas Eve. January and February are the coldest months and the closure of most tourist attractions during this season occurs. People of Denmark actually celebrate the longest and shortest day of the year, which coincides with Midsummer Day and Christmas Eve, respectively.
