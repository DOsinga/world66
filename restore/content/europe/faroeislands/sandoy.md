# Sandoy Travel Guide

*Europe > Faroeislands > Sandoy*

The best resource for sights, hotels, restaurants, bars, what to do and see

This island is connected with Streymoy by a car ferry from near Kirkjubour. It is generally more agricultural and scenically softer than most of the islands, though there are excellent features on the south and east coasts.

As you go into Skopun
on the ferry, you can see a very small island to the west of Sandoy.
This is Djúpaberg and belongs to the farmers of Kirkjubøur, who use
it for grazing.
Crossing the island to
the southern village of Sandur, there are superb views of the most
southerly islands, Skuvoy, beautifully structured Stora Dimun and
Litla Dimun with one farm between them and, in the distance the
larger island of Suduroy. An unclassified roadleads to the
uninhabited but delightful bay of Søltuvik, where we had a walk and
were mobbed by arctic terns. On the way back we lunched near puffin
cliffs.* *
