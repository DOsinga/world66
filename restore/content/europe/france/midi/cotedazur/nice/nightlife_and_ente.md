# Nightlife and Entertainment in Nice

*Europe > France > Midi > Cotedazur > Nice > Nightlife And Ente*

**Nice nightlife** is vibrant and full of options. There is
something for all budgets and tastes. Here are some venues you may want
to look into:

**Old Town vs. Ocean**

Vieux Nice, or the old town, is the hub for bars and clubs. Wayne’s
is one of the most popular stops for visitors under 30. The bar is
frequented by an international crowd. British Bands often play at this
pub. Call (33) 493-134699 for information about performances. An upscale
option in this part of town is L’Odace. Patrons compare it to Paris’
Buddah Bar. It too has Asian-inspired décor and similar beats. A chic
crowd hits the dance floor at this large club. If you want to try
something outside the old town, head to La Suite, an ocean-front venue.
It’s pricey, but if you like international club music and want to
indulge in inventive cocktails; this is the place for you.

**Local Hangouts**

Two bars in Vieux Nice that are loved by locals are Le Bar des
Oiseaux and the Blue Whales. Le Bar des Oiseaux (5 Rue St. Vincent), is
not easy to find. However, it’s worth your trouble if you like jazz.
Don’t expect amazing service or a trendy setting. This place is about a
grabbing a good bite and enjoying great music.

In comparison to
European hot spots like Barcelona, Nice nightlife ends on the early
side. That’s why locals and visitors alike enjoy the Blue Whales. It’s
not far from Wayne’s. This place is an after-hours bar that hosts live
bands. It’s a fun way to end a long night out. Both places are easy on
your pocket.

**Elegant Night Out**

For an elegant alternative to pubs and clubs, try the Nice Opera
House. Front and center tickets for the ballet go for $115 dollars. Call
(33) 492-174000 for information about their programs. You can also try
your luck at Casino Palais de la Mediterranée. This casino is part of a
4-star hotel on Promenade des Anglais.

For a unique experience,
visit Bar le Relais, in Hôtel Negresco. The hotel is a member
of The Leading Hotels of the World and was built in 1912. It, too, faces
the ocean and is located along Promenade des Anglais. The bar features
piano players every night. It has a rare collection of books, a
museum-worthy tapestry and valuable paintings. If you don’t mind the
expense, drop by this English-style bar and experience Nice with style.
