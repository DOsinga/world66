# Getting Around in Southern France

*Europe > France > Midi > Getting Around*

Getting around in southern France is relatively easy. Public
transportation is very reliable, it goes to most destinations and it
doesn’t cost much. All the major cities in Southern France are
connected by train. Once you get to the city of your choice, you can
switch over to the bus system.

**Bus Service**

Bus service in Southern France runs Monday to Saturday on a frequent basis. Most buses do not run on Sundays.

**Rail Service**

Rail service will get you to your destination much faster. Marseille
and Nice are the big transportation hubs in the area, and if you want to
wander outside of Southern France, you can do that easily. Trains are
run by the National Rail Service, and rail passes can be purchased that
allow 3 days of unlimited travel over a 30 day period, or 4 days for
those under 26.

**Rental Cars**

There are many companies in Southern France
that rent cars. You'll need a passport, driver’s license and credit
card to rent, and must be age 21 for some companies and 25 for others.
You may have to display an International Driver’s License for some car
rental companies as well. Car rentals are taxed by the government at
19.6%. Sometimes there is insurance that costs extra, at about $20 to
$30 per day. In Europe, most cars have a standard transmission and it's
seldom that they have anything with an automatic. Speed limits are 81 mph
on expressways and 62 mph on national highways. Country roads are
limited to 56 mph, and in town the speed is 37 mph.

There are
many ways to get where you want to go in Southern France, including
public transportation that's both easy and readily available.
