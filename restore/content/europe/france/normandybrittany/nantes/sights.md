**The Castle of the Dukes of Brittany**

Built in the 13th century, the first ducal castle was demolished in the 15th century to make room for today's building. This is a work of François II, the last duke of the independent Brittany, and his daughter Anne de Bretagne, twice queen of France.

It is a defensive fortress, with seven towers, protecting the residential palace. It was once a Breton residence for kings of France in the 16th and 17th centuries, listed as a historical monument since 1862 and a municipal [museum](/europe/france/normandybrittany/nantes/museums) since 1924.

After a 3-year complete closure, the monument is restored to its full glory. Every corner of the Castle - 5000 m² - is accessible to the public. The walk around the fortified ramparts offers viewpoints of the buildings on the inner courtyard, the moat ditches and the city.
