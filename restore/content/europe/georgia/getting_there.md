# Getting There

*Europe > Georgia > Getting There*

Air Orbi Georgian Airlines (NQ) operates regular flights from Vienna Frankfurt/M Cologne Paris Tel Aviv Ankara Thessaloniki and Istanbul. Aeroflot is often the cheapest option, though Germany Express flies from Dueseldorf directly for 380 Euro's*.*

International airport

Tbilisi airport* *is 19km east of Tbilisi city centre. Taxis and buses run into town. Recently a new airport of western standard has been constructed alongside its Soviet-style predecessor. At present there are few facilities (no restaurants or public telephones) although there is a bar with waitress service and satellite television. Snacks vodka and Georgian wine are served in kiosks outside the old airport building and there are plans to renovate the former complex. In winter there are frequent power failures which the new building may be subject to.

Sea

The main ferry ports are Batumi Poti and Sukhumi. Batumi and Poti provide international connections with the Black Sea ports of Odessa Sochi Trabzon and Istanbul and the Mediterranean ports of Genoa and Piraeus.

Rail

Tbilisi has railway connections with Azerbaijan Armenia (with the line continuing into eastern Turkey) and Iran. War in the break-away region of Abkhazia has severely affected Georgia's rail link with the Russian Federation (see *Rail – Internal* for further information).

Road:

Highways connect Georgia with the Russian Federation in the north via the Caucasian Road Tunnel and the Georgian Military Highway to North Ossetia. Other main routes connect with Turkey Armenia and Azerbaijan. There is a direct bus connection between Tblisi and [Yerevan](/europe/armenia/yerevan) in [Armenia](/europe/armenia)
