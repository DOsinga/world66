# Day Trips in Ulm

*Europe > Germany > Bade > Ulm > Day Trips*

**Ulm Day Trips** help to decipher the significance of Ulm—one of Germany&rsquo;s most influential cities. Ulm is also the administrative center of the Alb-Donau district. Ulm is an epitome of German culture and is largely regarded as a rare German city, where traditions still prevail. Ulm was a city of great, commercial importance during the 14th and 15th centuries because of its location along an important, trading route. It played a major part during the Napoleon era and during Germany&rsquo;s efforts in becoming a world power, during the Second World War. Besides such historical connotations, Ulm is popular for many other reasons that are best discovered as a part of Ulm day trips.

**Unravel Ulm&rsquo;s Old Town**

****The Old Town should be visited for getting a glimpse of Germany during its period of unquestioned industrial might. Visitors can expect tiled lanes and street squares exuding the charm of 19th-century Europe. Further, many parts of Old Town are connected by small bridges and a rudimentary canal system. A must-visit as a part of this trip is the Fishermen's Quarter. This is Old Town&rsquo;s most popular offering. The Quarter is located along the mouth of the river Blau. Just a few steps away lies the ancient-looking Tanners' Quarter.

**Visit Ulm&rsquo;s Fortifications**

During the latter half of the 19th century, Ulm underwent a unique process of strengthening it border. As a result, it was surrounded by several buildings, including forts and a network of small bridges between 1852 and 1868. At present, nearly 40 of these structures are still standing along the circumference of Ulm. Somehow, these fortifications managed to survive the bombings that demolished much of Ulm and nearby cities during World War II. The most well-preserved of these forts is the Oberer Kuhberg. This fort was later used as a concentration camp during the Nazi regime. Now, a memorial has been built here. Visitors need to spend at least half-a-day to explore the fortifications and the remains of Nazi era.
