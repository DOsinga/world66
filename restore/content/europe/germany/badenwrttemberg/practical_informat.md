# Practical Information in Baden-Wurttemberg

*Europe > Germany > Badenwrttemberg > Practical Informat*

**Baden-Wurttemberg Practical Information** is essential not only
to make your trip to Germany more enjoyable but also to enable you to
enjoy some savings. Nowadays budget travel requires more and more
research, so here are some bits of practical information to make your
stay in Baden-Wurttemberg more enjoyable.

**Driving around Baden-Wurttemberg**

Before
leaving your country of origin or residence, it is a good idea to apply
for an international driver's license because it is the most effective
proof of driving ability in Germany and all of the rest of the European
Union. Having an international driver's license will allow you to have a
smoother car rental process and to avoid any surprises when picking up
your car. In North America, visit your local AAA office to learn more
about the application process and fees.

**Driving on German
Highways**

The German autobahns are well known around the
world for their lack of speed limit; however it is a not such a well known
fact that the no speed limit areas are limited by round gray traffic
signs. Make sure to know how they look like to know where and when to
put pedal to the metal. Getting a speeding ticket in Germany is
expensive. Rather being stopped by a cop or patrol man, you will receive
in the mail a photograph showing you speeding and a fine. Don't think
that you can get away from the fine, German authorities are allowed by
law to mail you the fine back home to your home country or just charge
the fine to your car rental fee.

**Saving Money with Hostels and
Hostelling International

**

Youth hostels (Jugendherberge in German) are not limited to
just the youth, all ages are welcomed. There are special group and
student rates so make sure to ask for all prices so that you can decide
what's best for you. Often discounts are also added depending on the
length of your stay or whether you decide or not to share a room.
Breakfast is often included. Having a membership card from the
Hostelling International network will allow you to qualify for the
lowest rates. Application for a membership card within Germany is
limited only to Germans and exchange students with valid student IDs.
Therefore, it is best that you apply for a membership card at your local
Hostelling International office.

In the state of
Baden-Wurttemberg, there are over 20 hotels that are part of the
Hostelling International network. Once you arrive to your hostel, make
sure to ask for the latest version of the network's book for Germany. In
this book you will find all the bars, restaurants, night clubs,
museums, hostels and other places that provide specials and discounts to
members with valid Hostelling International membership cards.
