# Budget Travel Ideas in Ulm

*Europe > Germany > Badenwrttemberg > Ulm > Budget Travel Idea*

**Ulm Budget Travel** is always possible by following just a
couple simple rules. The most important part of planning a trip to
Germany is research and especially when it comes to planning a trip on a tight budget. However most people don't know where to start.
Here are some suggestions to get you started on your planning for your
next budget travel to Ulm.

**Saving Money with the Hostelling International Network**

Hostelling International is a network that operates 4,200 hostels
around the world in more than 80 countries around the world. Having a
membership card from this organization will give you access to special
offers and discounts at bars, restaurants, shops, museums and hostels
not only in Ulm but in all of Europe. It is recommended to apply for a
membership card at the local Hostelling International office in your
country because application within Germany is limited to Germans and
exchange students with a valid student id from a local school or
university.

**Staying in Ulm on a Budget**

If you're visiting Ulm on a tight budget, it is a good idea to stay
at the local youth hostel: the Geschwister Scholl Jugendherberge. This
youth hostel is located at Grimmelfinger 45 and it takes reservations
at +49-731-384455. This hostel holds over 120 people and room rates
start at EUR 20. The lowest rates are possible by having a valid
Hostelling International membership card. There rooms for 2 persons, 4
persons and 6 persons, and breakfast is included. Credit cards are accepted
for payment.

**Using Public Transportation**

If you're planning to use the bus and rail frequently, it is also a good
idea to purchase all-day, weekly or monthly passes. Buying pre-paid
passes will allow you to save money on trips because the passes offer
discounted rates. If you have a Hostelling International card you also
qualify for the lower student rates for passes.

Also, if you are only going to spend a few days in Ulm and are
going to use the bus or rail just a few times, make sure to take the
bus/rail transfers that are available inside some buses and rail
stations. These transfer tickets allow you to get on a bus or use rail transport for
free on your next stop. Don't try to trick the system, previous proof
of ticket purchase is often checked on a random basis.

**Eating on a Budget**

Avoid restaurants and try fixing your own dinners by shopping for
supplies at local discount food retailers such as Lidl and Aldi. All
types of beverages are especially cheap at these 2 places.
