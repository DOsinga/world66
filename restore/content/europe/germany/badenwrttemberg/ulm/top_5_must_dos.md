# Top 5 Must Do's in Ulm

*Europe > Germany > Badenwrttemberg > Ulm > Top 5 Must Dos*

**Ulm must do's** include visiting both religious and historical sites. Ulm is located on the Danube River, and has a rich history and is
a center for science and technology. Albert Einstein was born here in
1879. Ulm has a commercial river port which has contributed to the
areas wealth. In Ulm the old fuses with the new and historical
monuments can be viewed in conjunction with modern constructions.
Visitors can lose themselves down quaint alleys or go for a walk down
the modern promenades along the riverbank.

**Ulm Cathedral**

This magnificent cathedral is a popular tourist attraction in Ulm. The construction of the cathedral was started in 1377 and
completed in 1891. Ulm Cathedral has the highest steeple in the world reaching a height of 161.53 meters. To reach the top you have to climb 768 steps. The
towers, top story and spire offer different views of the cathedral
where you can admire the intricacy of the cathedral design form a
different angle. The spires also offer magnificent views of the city. Inside the
church you can see stained glass windows, choir loft carvings and
paintings. The choir stalls were designed by Jorg Syrlin in 1469. The cathedral
also features a sculpture by Hans Multscher called the Man of Sorrows
which dates back to 1429. There is an admission fee to enter Ulm Cathedral.

**Fisherman'sQuarter**

This area will take you back in time as you walk down the cobblestone
streets and along the footbridges. The Fisherman's Quarter is situated
on the River Blau. Here you can soak in the ambiance of the area as
well as seeing some noteworthy attractions. In the Fishermans Quarter
is the Schiefes Haus which translates to mean crooked house. It is a
house which was built in the 16th century and has now been converted
into a hotel. There is also the Alte Mint which is an example of Renaissance architecture.

**Museum of Bread Culture**

The museum illustrates the history of bread which dates back over the
past 6,000 years. The growth of this museum was made possible by Willy
Eiselen and his son Hermann Eiselen. They founded the museum in 1955
and the first exhibition was held in 1960. The museum is located in the
north of the Old City. It is open every day from 10 until 5. There are more
than 18,000 objects in the museum. The exhibits show the history of
bread as well as breads cultural significance. There is an on site library
which has a good collection of relevant books.

**Wiblingen Monastery**

Located in Wiblingen this monastery is a Benedictine Monastery dating
back to the 11th century. On site there is a church which is an
example of Baroque architecture. Tourists can take guided tours through the church and library. More information about Wiblingen Monastry can be found in the museum which was established in 2006.

**Ulm Museum**

Ulm museum offers insight into the history of the area and it contains
many note worthy artifacts. Beautiful pieces of art reflect the local
culture and the art offers a glimpse into people's everyday lives. The
on site shop is a good place to but souvenirs or an interesting piece
of local art. Refreshments are available at the cafe. There is an
admission fee to enter Ulm Museum.
