# When to Go in Ulm

*Europe > Germany > Badenwrttemberg > Ulm > When To Go*

Ulm is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Germany. The climate plays an important role in the success of a trip to Ulm. So, understanding the different climatic conditions of this place is crucial for planning a perfect holiday. Germany’s climate is predominantly temperate and marine. Although most of Germany falls in the cool temperate zone, the southern portion mostly experiences extremities in the climate. The following **Ulm when to go **guide will help you plan your trip better.

**Summer Season **

The summer months in Germany generally stretch from the end of May to the end of August. During these times, temperatures can go really high, especially in southern parts of the country like Ulm. Thunders and storms are also common aspects of German summers. Even with this kind of weather, summer is usually the preferred time to visit Ulm as the city holds the Free Imperial City celebrations for a week. This celebration mostly takes place during mid-July or the end of July.

**Fall Season
**
This season follows soon after the summer and is a wonderful time to visit the country. A number of other festivals are held during the fall as temperatures drop a little from September to November. These months can also experience rain from time to time. If you are planning a visit during this season, ensure that you carry your umbrella with you.

**Winter Season
**
Germany’s winter season tends to begin near the end of November and continues to the beginning of February. Winters here can be extremely cold, with temperatures usually dropping below freezing point. Regardless of this, the city still draws international tourists, thanks to its exciting holiday season. The place also offers skiing and winter sport possibilities that make it a great place to visit during winter.

**Spring Season
**
After a harsh winter, Germany welcomes spring from March to the end of May. The warm season brings a number of spring fairs and traditional Easter celebrations that are great for visitors. But the rise in temperature also means a rise in hotel and flight prices. It is also at this time of the year that southern places like Ulm witness the Fohn wind, a dry south wind that blows from the Alps throughout southwestern Germany.

The warmer months in Ulm are also the perfect time to climb the Cathedral Tower which has a record height. The climb consists of 768 steps that take tourists to the steeple’s top. On clear sunny days, tourists can get a wonderful view of the Alps from the spire.
