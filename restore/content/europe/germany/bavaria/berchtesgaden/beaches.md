# Beaches in Berchtesgaden

*Europe > Germany > Bavaria > Berchtesgaden > Beaches*

Nestled in the Bavarian Alps between Munich and Salzburg lies a small town that is known more for its regrettable history than its unreserved and unscathed beauty and **Berchtesgaden beaches**. Berchtesgaden&rsquo;s popularity is steadily on the rise as people discover what a truly magnificent and serene place it is. Visitors to this breathtaking city can enjoy a variety of sights like Berchtesgaden Beaches and its lakes.

**K&ouml;nigssee: The Emerald of Germany**

Berchtesgaden lies in a valley surrounded by grandiose mountains, including the Obersalzberg, Unterberg, and Watzmann. The majestic hillsides surrounding the K&ouml;nigssee are covered in lush greens teeming with life during the spring, and gorgeous, warm tones during autumn. K&ouml;nigssee comes to life during the spring, while taking its last breaths before hibernation in the fall, so these are the best two seasons to go.

Long electric-powered boats travel around the lake, providing an excellent form of transportation while allowing visitors to have many photogenic opportunities. On the K&ouml;nigssee&rsquo;s western shore, in front of the immense eastern face of the Watzmann mountain, stands St. Bartholomew&rsquo;s Cathedral (in German, St. Bartholom&auml;), which is accessible through the boats. The ferries are not expensive, and there are options for individuals as well as groups, as well as round trip or one-way tickets. People aboard these boats traditionally play the trumpet while going through the mountainous areas because of the crystal clear echo that resonates through the whole valley.

K&ouml;nigssee has great historical significance; here, Hitler relaxed with his companion, Eva Braun, along with other guests. It is easy to see why he chose this place; K&ouml;nigssee remains remote and wild, untouched except for the nearby town, the citizens of which are hospitable and delightful to converse with. The immaculate, turquoise waters juxtaposed with the lush green forests enchant visitors and encourage them to come back.

**Obersee: The Over Lake**

The Obersee is a short distance from the K&ouml;nigssee and easy to get to via the boat system. Much smaller in size, the Obersee is notorious for its glass-like waters that reflect the surrounding peaks. Not a ripple is seen on Obersee, other than those produced by the various sea creatures or humans. If you are visiting the K&ouml;nigssee, nothing should prevent you from going to the Obersee. Seeing nature in such raw form is a rare treat, and everyone should take advantage of it.

Berchtesgaden is a delightful place for nature lovers, but it is also a magnificent place for anyone to explore.
