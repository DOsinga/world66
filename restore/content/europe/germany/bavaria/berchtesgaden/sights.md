# Sights in Berchtesgaden

*Europe > Germany > Bavaria > Berchtesgaden > Sights*

**Berchtesgaden** sights include historical attractions as well as astounding natural wonders. Berchtesgaden is a charming town with a small population and a rich history. In the town there is still evidence of the Nazi's presence in the area from World
War II. The town is situated in a valley surrounded by mountains which contributes to the area's scenic beauty. Every year the town attracts international tourists.

**
Mountains**

Mount Watzmann is 2713 meters high. It is a well known climbing spot and it's east face in particular is popular. Kehlstein mountain is 1835 meters high with Eagle's Nest situated at an altitude of 1834 meters. The area has breath taking views. It can be reached by hiking or by public transport. The Kehlsteinhaus is located in this area and it is of historical significance because it was originally owned by Hitler. There is an admission fee to enter.

**Berchtesgaden National Park**

Berchtesgaden National Park was founded in 1978. Nestled in the heart of the park is Konigssee. Konigssee
is a clean lake and it is 190 meters deep. Mountains form the
background of this beautiful lake creating good photographic
opportunities. Boat trips on the lake take about 15 minutes but they
may be longer if you have to wait in line first. This is the ideal
setting for outdoor activities such as hiking. Routes are signposted
and along the way you can spot a variety of animals. The routes have
varying lengths and difficulty levels. A renowned hike is from St.Bartholoma to Eiskapelle. Alternatively walk to Malerwinkel which takes approximately 45 minutes. Cycling in the park is possible and if you travel by car there is ample parking space provided.

**Salt Mining Adventure Center**

Salt mining has played an important role in the economy of Berchtesgaden.
The adventure center is fun and informative offering visitors a better
understanding of salt mining. Visitors can tour the site on a mini
train and the train transports guests to a salt mine which is made more
dramatic by light and colors. There is an interesting salt cathedral.
The salt laboratory presents information on salt mining in a fun and interactive way. There is an admission fee. An on site restaurant provides delicious meals and visitors can relax on the rooftop deck.
