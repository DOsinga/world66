# Family Travel Ideas in Garmisch-partenkirchen

*Europe > Germany > Bavaria > Garmischpartenkirchen > Family Travel Idea*

Garmisch and Partenkirchen are twin villages that pack a lot of fun
for families of all sizes and age ranges. Although in the winter, they
represent one of the most popular alpine ski resorts in Germany, in the
summer a family can find plenty of activities to keep them busy and having
fun.
**Winter Fun
**

In the winter, ski slopes are busy and crowded with visitors and
residents alike. You can buy a ski pass from the resort and enjoy all the Alps
have to offer for days. If you have younger children that cannot ski, there are
many sites around the villages that are interesting and fun to visit. The towns
still exude all the Bavarian charm that they were founded on, including some
original structures. There are pedestrian-only streets that take you up toward
St. Martin, an 18th-century parish church. Here you will find many intriguing
artifacts from the 18th-century, such as stuccowork done by Schmutzer, Schmidt,
and Bader, all of whom were Wessobrunn artists.

The Werdenfelser Museum is located inside a building that dates back to
around 1200. There you will find evidence of the region’s history, some of
which dates back over 700 years. After exploring the history of the area in the
Werdenfelser Museum, you can hike to the Zugspitze Mountain which is the
highest mountain in Germany, reaching 9,731 feet at its peak. There are 2
ways up: you can drive a scenic, 75-minute route up the mountain, taking in the
views and winding roads, or you can hop on a cable car that will pull your family up the
slope steadily in 10 minutes where you can catch the cable car at Eibsee, a lake at
the base of the mountain. Eibsee is located about 10 km (6 miles) outside of the
town toward Austria. The price for the cable car ride is less in the winter and includes
the price of ski lift tickets, if you want.
**Summer Sports
**

Garmisch-Partenkirchen is as beautiful in summer as it is in
winter. Your family has many options when it comes to summer fun in Bavaria. With the
mountains so close, you can hike up and take in the expansive views along the
way. If your vacations tend to include more athletic activities, you can rent
mountain bikes and peddle up and down the sloping hills. Miniature golf
courses and summer festivities are great for a younger crowd and are located all
around the villages.

You can horse-back ride around the countryside or take a toboggan ride, depending upon the season. Even small children love the horse-drawn carriage rides offered in the
area.
