# Sights in Garmisch-partenkirchen

*Europe > Germany > Bavaria > Garmischpartenkirchen > Sights*

There are many distinctive sights that can be enjoyed if one’s destination is
to the city of Garmisch-Partenkirchen. Specifically, the sights that
can be enjoyed can be placed into 2 specific categories. The first is the gorgeous scenery that surrounds this beautiful town and the
second is the inspiration of individuals who have left a lasting legacy.

**Scenery**

When it comes to scenery, it doesn’t get much better than being nestled at
the base of the Alps. These majestic mountains beckon the individual to be
inspired and to be renewed with energy.

In addition, the majestic Partnach River flows through
the nearby gorge. This river is a tribute to the drawing power of individuals
who flock to see the cool waters continuing its journe, as they have
for centuries.

During the summer months, the scenic beauty of the landscape
continues to be reflected as many enjoy the hiking paths and trails that meander
at the base of the mountain range. Visitors can further extend
their inspiring view of the landscape by taking advantage of a cogwheel train
trip. This excursion transports the visitor to heights up the
Zugspitze Mountain where more breathtaking views await the relaxed
traveler.

Other trademark Garmisch-Partenkirchen sights include the various
museums that adorn the city. These museums include the Richard Strauss
Institute which gives witness to the creativity and accomplishment of
the composer and musical artist, Richard Strauss, a German
composer who distinguished his musical artistry in the area of operas and music
accompanied by narration.

Another museum that provides a good glimpse into the history of this
Bavarian region is the Werdenfels Museum. This Museum contains the historic collection of items and valued cultural mementos that capture the strength of
the culture of this area. This particular museum was established in 1895 and
honors the rich culture of this specific geographical area in Bavaria.

Another museum is the Aschenbrenner Museum. This museum along
with the Werdenfels Museum contains a superb collection of French and German
dolls that date back to the early 19th and 20th centuries. In addition, this
museum also houses a unique collection of nativity scene pieces.
