# When to Go in Garmisch-partenkirchen

*Europe > Germany > Bavaria > Garmischpartenkirchen > When To Go*

When it comes to planning a trip to **Garmisch-Partenkirchen** it
is important to time one’s travel in accordance with what sort of
activities and specific reasons for going to this unique and enchanting
city.

**The Climate**

Specifically, the altitude of Garmisch-Partenkirchen is 719 meters
or 2,359 feet. At this altitude, even in the summertime, the air can
be cool and therefore would require a sweater or warm clothing to be
worn. In addition, the evening air can be especially cool and
therefore may require a light coat as well to protect the individual
against the cool weather. Therefore, it is important for the traveler
to know that the range of temperature seldom goes, on a average, above
70° Fahrenheit or 20°C and during the winter months hovers around 0 to
10°C or twenty-two 33 degrees Fahrenheit.

**Seasonal Activities**

In addition, when timing a trip to Garmisch-Partenkirchen it is
important to keep in mind what the specific purpose of the excursion. For example, if an individual is going to ski the slopes of the
Bavarian Alps, then obviously they will want to wear warm ski clothing
as well as utilize the various accessories to help optimize the skiing
experience. However, if the excursion is planned during the summer or
fall months and includes the taking in of the beautiful landscape, then
comfortable hiking clothes should be considered when planning or timing
the trip.

**Tourist Season**

Another important consideration to take into account
when timing a trip to Garmisch-Partenkirchen is to consider when are
the heavy travel times and when the off seasons are. Due to the
reality of Garmisch-Partenkirchen providing an exceptional skiing
experience, the heavy times of activity in these twin cities is during
the winter months.

However, because there are certain glaciers that can be skied upon
throughout the year, there still will be skiers who may want to take
advantage of skiing during the off season. Subsequently, the peak
time for a non-skier to visit the city of Garmisch-Partenkirchen would
be from the late spring to the early fall. Specifically, there would
be a number of bargains that could be taken advantage of including
hotel accommodations as well as less expensive shopping.
