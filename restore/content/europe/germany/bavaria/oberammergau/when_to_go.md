# When to Go in Oberammergau

*Europe > Germany > Bavaria > Oberammergau > When To Go*

This Alpine village has a
great deal to offer the visitor during the winter, summer and swing
seasons. In fact, there are reasons to visit Oberammergau throughout the year. High up in
the Alps, filled with Bavarian charm and
architecture, the village is nearly a picture perfect example of a mountainside
retreat.**

Winter**

Oberammergau is a place to visit in the winter if you love winter
sports. Cross-country trails cross the area around the village and there are 2 well-known ski centers nearby. Kolbe and Laber attract large numbers of
skiers who have various levels of skiing ability. Kolbe is the easier ski challenge
of the areas. There are small huts dotting the mountains where skiers can relax
and enjoy refreshments on their trip downhill, adding to the festive atmosphere
of the slopes. People who are not skiers can enjoy the winter in Oberammergau, as well. The
views of the snowy Alps from atop the
chairlift, the good Bavarian food and beer in the village and its welcoming
residents are probably enough to make any visitor feel warm inside and out.
Surprisingly, average temperatures are not as cold as most may imagine and lows
stay in the 20’s in mid-winter.

**Other Times of the Year **

Oberammergau is a wonderful tourist destination even when there
is no snow. Regardless of season, the views of the Alps are
stunning with trees and flowers in bloom. Should a visitor want to attend the
famous Passion Play that is performed every 10 years, a visit any time between
May and October would allow for this, but visitors should realize that while
the play is being performed there are crowds and higher prices for rooms and
for food. The summer is an ideal time to visit because the weather stays
refreshingly cool in the Alps and could be a relief from the heat of villages
and cities that are not as elevated as Oberammergau
is.

The best travel bargains
to Oberammergau
are found in the spring or fall, which are considered the off-season. Room
prices can vary enormously and so can air, train and bus travel during these
times. During these months, as well as during the summer, visitors can take
advantage of the nearby sights such as King Ludwig’s castle, Schloss
Linderhof or Ettal, a baroque monastery a few miles south of the village.
